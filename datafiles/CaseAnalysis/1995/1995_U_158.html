Sarla Mudgal Smt., President, Kalyani and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1302; (1995) SCC (Cr) 569; (1995) 3 SCC 635; AIR 1995 SC 1531; AIR 1995 SCW 2326; 1995 (2) ALT(SC) 50; 1996 (5) Bom.C.R. 272; 1995 (3) CCR 12; 1995 CRLJ 2926; 1996 (1) HLR 659; JT 1995 (4) SC 331; 1995 (2) KLT 45; 1995(3) SCALE 286; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 250; 1995 (2) UJ 764

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Conversion To Islam, Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956.

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25, 26, 27 and 44 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 11, 13 and 15 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 494 - (A) Whether a Hindu husband, married under Hindu law, by embracing Islam, can solemnise a second marriage ? - (B) Whether such a marriage without having first marriage dissolved under law, would be a valid marriage quo first wife who continues to be a Hindu ? - (C) Whether apostate husband would be guilty of offence u/s. 494 of IPC, 1860 ? - Held, a marriage celebrated under a particular personal law cannot be dissolved by application of another personal law to which one of spouses converts and other refuses to do so - Where a marriage takes place under Hindu law parties acquire a status and certain rights by marriage itself under law governing hindu marriage and if one of parties is allowed to dissolve marriage by adopting and enforcing a new personal law, it would tantamount to destroying existing rights of other spouse who continues to be Hindu - Under hindu personal law as it existed prior to its codification in 1955, a hindu marriage continued to subsist even after one of spouses converted to Islam - There was no automatic dissolution of marriage - Position has not changed after coming into force of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 rather it has, become worse, for apostate - Modern hindu law strictly enforces monogamy - A marriage performed under Act cannot be dissolved except on grounds available u/s. 13 of Act - In that situation parties who have solemnised marriage under Act remain married even when husband embraces Islam in pursuit of another wife - A second marriage by an apostate under shelter of conversion to Islam would nevertheless be a marriage in violation of provisions of Act by which he would be continuing to be governed so far as his first marriage under Act is concerned despite his conversion to Islam - Second marriage of an apostate would, therefore be illegal marriage qua his wife who married him under Act and continues to be a Hindu - Marriage solemnised by a hindu husband after embracing Islam may not strictly be a void marriage under Act because he is no longer a Hindu, but fact remains that said marriage would be in violation of Act which strictly professes monogamy but second marriage of a Hindu husband after his conversion to Islam is a void marriage in terms of s. 494, IPC, 1860 - Court request Govt. of India through Prime Minister of country to have a fresh look at art. 44 of Constitution and 'endeavour to secure for citizens a uniform civil code throughout territory of India' - Petitions allowed.
