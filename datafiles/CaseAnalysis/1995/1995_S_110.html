Shankar Balwant Lokhande (Dead) By L.Rs. v Chandrakant Shankar Lokhande And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 393; (1995) 3 SCC 413; AIR 1995 SC 1211; 1995 (4) Bom.C.R. 16; 1995 (2) CCC 41; JT 1995 (3) SC 186; 1995(2) SCALE 318; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 776; 1995 (1) UJ 707

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Stamp Act, 1958.

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - O. 20 r. 18(2) - Bombay Stamp Act, 1958 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 182 - Non judicial stamps - Preliminary decree - Execution proceedings - In the preliminary decree, an order was passed that the first respondent was entitled to 1/6th share and appellants were entitled to 5/6th share in the suit properties - For the final decree the respondent had complied by supplying non judicial stamps to engross and sign the final decree to the extent of his 1/6th share and final decree was passed qua respondent - Appellants had not supplied the non-judicial stamps and no final decree was made qua appellants - Execution proceedings were initiated by appellants but they were rejected - Appellants filed an application to accept the non judicial stamps and pass the final decree and order was passed in favour of appellants - Respondent aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, on the passing of the preliminary decree and not final decree, execution proceedings could be initiated - Held, that a preliminary decree in a partition suit was only a step in the suit which continued until the final decree was passed - After passing of preliminary decree for partition, the decree could not be made effective without a final decree - Final decree made in favour of the first respondent was only partial to the extent of his 1/6th right without any demarcation or division of the properties - Until the rights in the final decree proceedings were worked out qua all and till a final decree in that behalf was made, there was no formal expression of the adjudication conclusively determining the rights of the parties with regard to the properties for partition in terms of the declaration of 1/6th and 5/6th shares of the first respondent and the appellants that entitled them to make an application for execution of the final decree - After final decree was passed and a direction was issued to pay stamped papers for engrossing final decree thereon and the same was duly engrossed on stamped paper, it would have become executable or would have become an instrument duly stamped - Condition precedent was to draw up a final decree and then to engross it on stamped papers of required value - These two acts together constituted final decree, crystallizing the rights of the parties in terms of the preliminary decree - Until then there could be no executable decree as envisaged under O. 20 r. 18(2), attracting residuary art. 182 of the 1908 Act - Appeal allowed.
