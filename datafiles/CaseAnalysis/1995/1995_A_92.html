Dr. Ranbir Singh v Asharfi Lal

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1266; (1995) 6 SCC 580; 1995 (3) CCC 482; JT 1995 (6) SC 668; 1995 (2) RCJ 462; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 534; 1995 (2) RentLR 600; 1995(5) SCALE 470; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Rent Control - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47, r. 27 - Rajsthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 3 - Eviction from rented premises - Title of property is not germane for decision of eviction suit - In a case where a plaintiff institutes a suit for eviction of his tenant based on relationship of landlord and tenant, scope of suit is very much limited in which a question of title cannot be gone into because suit of plaintiff would be dismissed even if he succeeds in proving his title but fails to establish privity of contract of tenancy - In a suit for eviction based on such relationship court has only to decide whether defendant is tenant of plaintiff or not - Defendant denied title of plaintiff in respect of suit premises, furnished a ground for eviction of respondent - Hardship to plaintiff-appellant would be greater than hardship to tenant as need of landlord is greater than that of tenant - Held that denial of title of landlord by tenant and bona fide need of landlord is a good ground for eviction - Impugned judgment and order of High Court is set aside and judgment and decree of Trial Court is restored - Appeal allowed.
