Mangat Mal (Dead) And Anr v Smt. Punni Devi (Dead) And Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1111; (1995) 6 SCC 88; AIR 1996 SC 172; 1995 (3) CCC 548; 1996 (1) DMC 1; 1995 (2) HLR 438; JT 1995 (7) SC 506; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 82; 1995(5) SCALE 199; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Whether widow has a right to sell property, which is given in lieu of maintenance ? - Maintenance necessarily encompass provision for residence - In lieu of a pre-existing right to maintenance and Hindu lady acquires far more than vestige of title - Held, widow to use property she chose for residence for life and she can use in any way she likes - Award and document executed by widow - Clear that award of both property and money was in lieu of her pre-existing right to maintenance - Upon coming into force of Act widow become entitled to sell it.
