Wariyam Singh And Ors v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1249; (1995) 6 SCC 458; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1124; AIR 1996 SC 305; 1996 ALJ 77; 1995 (4) CCR 138; 1995 (4) Crimes 200; JT 1995 (7) SC 117; 1995(5) SCALE 604B; 1995 (3) SCJ 491; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 4, 15 and 28 - TADA Rules, 1987, r. 15 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Confession before police officer - Procedure - Non-sending of confessional statement to Chief Judicial Magistrate for onward transmission to designated court - Whether trial vitiated on ground of irregularity - (B) Accused charged u/ss. 3 and 4 of TADA Act and also u/s. 120B of IPC, 1860 - Trial - Confessional statements relied on by prosecution specifically put to accused at time of his examination u/s. 313, CrPC, 1973 - Whether prejudice caused to accused - Trial, whether vitiated - (C) Evidence Act, 1872 , s. 3 - Interested witness - Credibility - Offence under TADA, 1987 - Witness, a father of deceased, himself sustaining injuries in alleged incident - His presence at place of occurrence, natural - His testimony corroborated with confessional statement of accused - Whether can be rejected on ground of interestedness - (D) s. 15 of TADA, 1987 - Confession - Evidentiary value - Confession proved by police officer recording it - Same also corroborated by testimony of injured witness - Allegations made by accused that confessional statements were fabricated - Whether confession can be rejected merely on basis of such allegations - (E) Recording of confession by police officer in Hindi language - None of accused expressing any difficulty in understanding language in which confessional statement was recorded - Plea raised by one of accused after about 1 and 1/2 years that he could not follow said language - Whether tenable - Held, (A) is only a procedural irregularity Trial not vitiated - (B) No prejudice caused to accused - (C) Confession cannot be rejected - (D) Cannot be rejected - (E) Not tenable - Appeal dismissed.
