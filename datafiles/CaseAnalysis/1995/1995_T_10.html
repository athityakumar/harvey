Biharilal Jaiswal Etc v Commissioner of Income Tax Etc

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 646; (1996) 1 SCC 443; AIR 1995 SCW 4587; 1996 (130) CTR 143; [1996] 217 ITR 746; JT 1995 (8) SC 257; 1995(6) SCALE 508; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 285; [1996] 84 TAXMAN 236; 1996 (2) TLR 165

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Licence for retail sale of country spirit under supply system was obtained by assessee in respect of twenty two out-stall shops - Assessee entered into a partnership with ten other persons to conduct the business under the said licence - Application for grant of registration to the said firm u/ss. 184 and 185 of the 1961 Act was filed - Income Tax Officer (ITO) rejected the application for registration on the ground that the partnership having been formed in violation of cl. VI of the General Licence Conditions Prescribed by the Madhya Pradesh Excise Rules was illegal and could not, therefore, claim registration under the 1961 Act - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner directed ITO to grant registration - Tribunal restored order of ITO - HC rejected applications by assessee u/s. 256(2) of 1961 Act - Whether license agreement could be registered under the 1961 Act - Held, when the law prohibited the entering into a particular partnership agreement, then partnership agreement of that nature and any partnership entered into would be unlawful and void within the meaning of s. 23 of the 1872 Act and no other law, whether State or Central, could recognize such an agreement - Grant of registration under the 1961 Act conferred a substantial benefit upon the partnership firm and its members - There was no reason to extend the benefit to persons who entered into a partnership agreement prohibited by law - Hence, it was bound to be taxed either as an unregistered partnership firm or as an association of persons - Appeal allowed.
