State Of Haryana And Anr v Jagdish Chander

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1421; (1995) SCC (L&S) 539; (1995) 2 SCC 567; AIR 1995 SC 984; AIR 1995 SCW 964; 1995 (29) ATC 385; JT 1995 (2) SC 108; 1996 (2) LLJ 737; 1996 (1) LLN 576; 1995(1) SCALE 378; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 252; 1995 (3) SLJ 122; 1995 (1) SLR 696; 1995 (1) UJ 512

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Punjab Police Rules, 1949, rr. 12 and 21- Discharge - Natural justice - Authority considering record gave finding that police officer is habitual absentee - Negligent and undisciplined - Order passed without an opportunity of hearing - Held, illegal - Is stigma on his career - Disentitles future employment - It is not an order of discharge simpliciter - Reinstatement order by High Court not justified - Authorities directed to hold enquiry.
