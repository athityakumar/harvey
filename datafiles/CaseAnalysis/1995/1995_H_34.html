Gujarat Electricity Board, Thermal Power Station, Ukai, Gujarat v Hind Mazdoor Sabha and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1211; (1995) 5 SCC 27; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1166; AIR 1995 SC 1893; AIR 1995 SCW 2942; 1995 (1) CLR 967; 1995 (71) FLR 102; 1995 (1) GLH 1083; JT 1995 (4) SC 264; 1995 LabIC 2207; 1995 (2) LLJ 790; 1995 (2) LLN 59; 1995(3) SCALE 498; 1995 (3) SLR 712; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 173

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Unloading, Closure, Hours Of Work, Contract Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Central Rules, 1971, Catering, Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and Other Manual Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract, Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - (A) Whether an industrial dispute can be raised for abolition of contract labour system in view of provisions of Act? - (1) If so, who can raise such dispute? - (B) Whether Industrial Tribunal or appropriate Govt. has power to abolish contract labour system? - (1) In case contract labour system is abolished, what is status of erstwhile workmen of contractors? - Held, u/s. 10 of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 it is only appropriate Govt. which has authority to abolish genuine labour contract in accordance with provisions of said Section - No Court including industrial adjudicator had jurisdiction to do so - If contract is sham or not genuine, workmen of so called contractor can raise an industrial dispute for declaring that they were always employees of principal employer and for claiming appropriate service conditions - When such dispute is raised, it is not a dispute for abolition of labour contract and hence provisions of s. 10 will not bar either raising or adjudication of dispute - If labour contract is genuine a composite industrial dispute can still be raised for abolition of contract labour and their absorption - Dispute will have to be raised invariably by direct employees of principal employer - Even after contract labour system is abolished, direct employees of principal employer can raise an industrial dispute for absorption of ex-contractor's workmen - CAs Nos. 5497/95 dismissed - CAs 5498-02 and 5503/95 dismissed - CA 5504/95 allowed.
