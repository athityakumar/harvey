National Federation of Railway Porters, Vendors and Bearers v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1210; (1995) Supp3 SCC 152; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1119; AIR 1995 SC 1617; AIR 1995 SCW 2541; 1995 (2) CLR 214; 1995 (71) FLR 75; JT 1995 (4) SC 568; 1995 LabIC 2062; 1995 (2) LLJ 712; 1995 (2) LLN 634; 1995(3) SCALE 372; 1995 (2) SCJ 258; 1995 (2) SLR 709; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 165; 1995 (2) UJ 338; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1353

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Regularization - Denied - Challenged - Petitioners who were working as Railways Parcel Porters in Railway Stations of Northern Railway, North Eastern Railway and Eastern Railway on contract labour filed writ petitions that they should also be considered as regular employees in terms of the order dt. 15-4-1991 whereby HC direct the respondent, Railway Administration to treat the petitioners as regular Parcel Porters w.e.f. 15-4-1991 and to grant them the same salary which is being paid to regular Parcel Porters.

Held, pursuant to the said order of SC and another similar Order dt. 26-4-1993, a detailed enquiry being held by the Asst. Labour Commissioner (Central), Ministry of Labour, Govt.of India, after affording to all the parties concerned opportunity of participation in it, a report dt. 31-8-1993 August 31,1993 is submitted in which it was stated that the absorption and regularisation of the petitioners in the writ petitions, who could be appointed as permanent Railway Parcel Porters shall be done according to the terms and on such other terms to which they may be subjected to according to the rules or circulars of the railway Board as expeditiously as possible, not being later than six months from today. Petition disposed of.
