Nani Gopal Paul v T. Prasad Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 449; (1995) 3 SCC 579; AIR 1995 SC 1971; AIR 1995 SCW 3078; 1995 (2) BC 263; 1996 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 118; JT 1995 (3) SC 387; 1995(2) SCALE 544; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 521; 1995 (2) UJ 175

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 ; Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o.21, rr. 89 and 90, s.48 - Hypothecated property - Auction sale conducted by Court receiver -Illegalities in sale pointed out by Division Bench of High Court - Application time barred - SC took judicial notice - Held, sale set aside - Court would not remain mute spectator to obvious and manifest illegality committed in conducting Court sales even if application not filed within time prescribed.
