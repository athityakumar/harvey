State of Punjab and Others v Shambhu Nath Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1952; (1996) 1 SCC 296; (1996) SCC (L&S) 302; 1996 (32) ATC 237; JT 1995 (9) SC 136; 1995(6) SCALE 712A; 1996 (1) SLR 33A; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 582

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Reinstatement - Challenged - Appeals filed against order of the DB who directed the reinstatement of the respondent no. 1 pending the criminal prosecution.

Held, since it is now admitted across the Bar that respondent No. 1 had been discharged by the Criminal Court for want of proper sanction, he was reinstated in the service in January, 1988. Consequently, respondent no. 1 is entitled to full salary and allowances for the period during which he was kept under suspension. Appeals disposed of.
