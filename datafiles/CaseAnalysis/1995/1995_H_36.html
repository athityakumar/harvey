Hindustan Lever Limited v Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1069; (1995) 3 SCC 716; 1995 (4) Bom.C.R. 360; JT 1995 (5) SC 603; 1995(3) SCALE 24; 1995 (2) SCJ 224; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 807; 1995 (2) UJ 416

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Construction of property - Value of property - Method of valuation - Municipal Corporation-respondent fixed rateble value of the property constructed by appellant - Appellant objected rateable value fixed by respondent - HC accepted appeal of respondent and enhanced the rateable value - Appeal of appellant was dismissed by HC hence, instant appeal by special leave was filed by appellant - Whether, decision of HC was justified - Held, HC erred in law by not excluding the cost incurred by the appellant for air conditioning, false ceiling, interest on sum deposited by appellant with respondent and interest on total cost of construction therefore, order for the exclusion of the same was passed - The value of wooden partitions was rightly included in calculating rateable value and there was no infirmity in the inclusion of the amount of ground rent in determining the rateable value as it had no application and property tax was already paid hence, appellant was not liable to pay tax on land again - The cost incurred for said items was to be excluded to fix rateable value - Appeal allowed.
