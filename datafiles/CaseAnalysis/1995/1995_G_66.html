State Of Haryana v Gurcharan Singh & Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2174; (1995) Supp2 SCC 637; AIR 1996 SC 106; 1995 (25) ALR 458; JT 1995 (2) SC 345; 1995(1) SCALE 530; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 408; 1996 (Supp) AWC 82

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23 and 25 - Award of compensation for acquisition of land - (A) Whether separate compensation should be awarded granted for land and fruit trees - (B) Multipliers - Whether 8 years and 12 years respectively are suitable multiplier for land with trees and agricultural land - (C) Whether award made by Collector can be reduced by Courts when he committed palpable error of law of awarding separate compassionate of land and fruit bearing trees and had applied multiplier of more than 8 years - Held, compensation is value of acquired land and land includes benefits to arise from land - Sutaible multiplier and under no circumstances multiplier of more than 8 years can be applied to land with trees - Cannot be reduced as it is an offer - Appeal allowed.
