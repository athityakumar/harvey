Additional Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P v Degaon Ganga Reddy G. Ramakrishna and Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1405; (1995) Supp2 SCC 146; AIR 1995 SC 2074; 1995 (125) CTR 130; [1995] 214 ITR 650; JT 1995 (9) SC 623; 1995(2) SCALE 88; 1995 (3) SCJ 123; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 404; [1995] 79 TAXMAN 194

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abkari Act, s. 14 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 75 and 77 - Whether a sub-partnership which is alleged to be illegal as being in violation of s. 14 of Abkari Act, can be registered under IT Act? - In view of clear findings of fact recorded by tribunal, sub-partnerships formed by individual partners of main partnership which were lessees, with some others, merely to finance business of a partner of main firm doing Abkari business and share profits and losses accrued to or received by him from main firm, were not in violation of s. 14 of Abkari Act - For this reason, there is no basis to hold that sub-partnerships were in violation of s. 14 of Abkari Act and, therefore, illegal - Tribunal was right in holding that in facts and circumstances of case, assessee-sub-partnerships being found to be genuine were entitled to be registered under IT Act - Held, High Court has correctly answered question of law referred to it, against revenue and in favour of assessee - Appeals dismissed.
