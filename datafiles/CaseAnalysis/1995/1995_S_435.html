Dhananjay Sharma v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 927; (1995) SCC (Cr) 608; (1995) 3 SCC 757; AIR 1995 SC 1795; AIR 1995 SCW 2815; 1995 (26) ALR 180; 1995 (4) Bom.C.R. 366; 1995 (2) CCR 128; 1995 (2) Crimes 592; JT 1995 (4) SC 483; 1995(3) SCALE 138; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 964; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 203

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c) and 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Habeas corpus petition - Illegal detention - False affidavit filed by police officers - Torture on detenu - And forcing to make false affidavit in Supreme Court to effect that he was not illegally detained by police - The Home Secy. to state Govt. - Not filing counter affidavit to rule nisi issued by Supreme Court - Held, such conduct aggravated their contumacious acts - Deponent is guilty of criminal contempt as filing of affidavits or making false statement.
