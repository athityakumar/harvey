State Of U.P. v M/S. Ram Nath Internationalconst. Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1807; (1996) 1 SCC 18; AIR 1996 SC 782; 1995 (2) ARBLR 577; 1995 (4) CCC 92; 1996 (1) CLT 125; JT 1995 (8) SC 396; 1995(6) SCALE 321; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 99

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - (A) Whether Court can re-assess evidence to find whether arbitrator has committed an error? - Held, it is not open to Court to re-assess evidence to find whether arbitrator has committed any error or to decide question of adequacy of evidence - Court cannot sit on conclusion of arbitrator by re-examining and re appreciating evidence considered by Arbitrator - Award of an Arbitrator can be set aside by a Court only on grounds indicated in s. 30 of Arbitration Act, 1940 - (B) Whether direction of Arbitrator issued beyond arbitration contract is void? - Held, Arbitrator cannot entertain and decide any dispute which has not been referred to it - Direction of Arbitrator must be held to be without jurisdiction - Appeal partly allowed.
