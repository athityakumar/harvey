K. A. Mathai Alias Babu and Another v Kora Bibbikutty and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1540; (1996) 7 SCC 212; (1996) SCC (Cr) 281

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Financier

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 379 r/w s. 114 - Appellants are driver A-1 and registered owner of bus A-2, holding a state carriage permit over it - A-2 sold bus with state carriage permit to complainant, but subject to hire-purchase agreement with financier - Complainant defaulted in payment of installment whereupon vehicle was taken possession of by financier and at that juncture statedly both A-1 and A-2 were present in bus, A-1 having driven it away - Whether appellants are liable to be charged for offence punishable u/s. 379 r/w s.114, IPC? - Held, A-2 could not have passed a better title of bus to complainant than that she had acquired for herself under hire-purchase agreement - Financier took possession of bus from complainant with aid of appellants - It cannot thus be said that appellants, in any way, had committed offence of theft and that too, with requite mens rea and requisite dishonest intention - Assertion of rights and obligations, accruing to appellants under aforesaid two agreements, wiped out any dishonest pretense in that regard from which it could be inferred that they had done so with a guilty intention - Appeal allowed.
