Hindustan Lever Limited v Ashok Vishnu Kate and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1241; (1995) 6 SCC 326; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1385; AIR 1996 SC 285; 1995 (2) CLR 823; JT 1995 (6) SC 625; 1995 LabIC 2714; 1996 (1) LLJ 899; 1996 (1) LLN 173; 1995(5) SCALE 400; 1995 (7) SLR 206; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, ss. 28 and 30 - Jurisdiction of Labour Court - Question involved in the appeal centered round the jurisdiction of the Labour Court functioning under the Act, regarding entertaining of complaints filed u/s. 28(1) of the Act in connection with contemplated discharge or dismissal of the employees alleged to be resorted to by the employer by way of unfair labour practice - Appeal filed by the respondent workmen was allowed by the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the Labour Court had jurisdiction to entertain the complaints - Held, that the Labour Court dealing with the complaints should not lightly interfere with such pending domestic enquiries against the concerned complainants and Labour Court concerned should meticulously scan the allegations in the complaint and if necessary, get the necessary investigation made in the light of such complaint and only when a very strong prime facie case was made out by the complainant appropriate interim orders intercepting such domestic enquiries in exercise of powers u/s. 30(2) of the Act could be passed by the Labour Courts - Where alternative constructions were equally open, that alternative was to be chosen which would be consistent with the smooth working of the system which the Statute purported to be regulating; and that alternative was to be rejected which would introduce uncertainty, friction or confusion into the working of the system - If the alleged unfair labour practice of discharge or dismissal of an employee was to be prevented, it should necessarily contemplate an intervention of the competent Labour Court at a stage prior to the Actual commission of such unfair labour practice - Appeal dismissed.
