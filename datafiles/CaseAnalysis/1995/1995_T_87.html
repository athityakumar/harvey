M/S. Moti Laminates Pvt. Limited Etc. v The Collector Of Central Excise, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K.S. Paripoornan, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1533; (1995) 3 SCC 23; 1995 (57) ECR 1; 1995 (76) E.L.T. 241; JT 1995 (2) SC 324; 1995 (7) RLT 1; 1995(1) SCALE 713; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9, 49 and 173(1) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, item 15A - Levy of duty - Marketability - Appellants manufactured laminated sheets out of various raw materials including paper and other chemicals, namely, phenol, formaldehyde, hexamine etc. - From the process of manufacture, a solution was emerged which was taken out from the vessel in its semi-processed condition and was used without further processing in manufacture of laminated sheets - Since it was not marketed or sold by the appellants and the solution was captively consumed only, the Department did not levy any duty on it - Subsequently, 1944 Rules were amended and the result of which was that even captively consumed goods produced or manufactured became dutiable - Superintendent issued show cause notice to the appellants stating that in view of the amendments in Rules, Phenol, formaldehyde and melamine formaldehyde were liable to duty - Appellants contended that reacting mixture were not only unstable having short life but they were also not marketable in the form they were obtained in intermediate stage in a continuous process - Asst. Collector held that merely because solutions were not stable it did not mean that the resins produced by the appellants were not goods - Collector allowed appeal filed against said order and held that even though impugned products were used for the manufacture of plastic laminated sheets and synthetic resin was formed as an intermediate product but it being unstable in a non-marketable condition the resin mixture could not be considered as excisable under tariff item 15(1) of Tariff - Tribunal held that the intermediate goods produced by the appellants were 'resol' and as 'resol' being mentioned in item 15A of Tariff was covered by the 'chemical nomenclature' and once the product answered the chemical description in the entry, it was exigible to duty - Tribunal also held that since the products manufactured by' appellants, fell under 15-A(1) of Tariff the fact that these were not marketed or sold did not make any difference - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether various goods mentioned in Schedule of Excise tariff were dutiable as such or they would be, 'excisable goods', only when they are marketable or capable of being marketed? - Held, the test of marketability or capable of being marketable applies even to those goods which were mentioned in tariff item - Intermediate resin produced by appellants which were mentioned as 'resols' under tariff item 15A of Tariff not exigible to duty as they were not marketable - Findings of Tribunal that once product manufactured by appellants answered chemical description of product under tariff item 15A of Tariff assessable to duty whether it was marketable or not not well founded - Hence, 'resin' at 'A' stage which chemically known as 'resols' could not be subjected to duty - Appeal allowed.
