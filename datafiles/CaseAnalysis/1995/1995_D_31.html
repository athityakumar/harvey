Dr. G.N. Khajuria And Ors v Delhi Development Authority And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1207; (1995) 5 SCC 762; AIR 1996 SC 253; AIR 1995 SCW 4024; 1996 (63) DLT 775; 1995(5) SCALE 172; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 212; 1995 (2) UJ 656

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Development Act, 1957, s. 8(2)(a) - Delhi Development (Master Plan and Zonal Development Plan) Rules, 1959, r. 4(3)(g) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Lay-out plan for residential colony -Part of land reserved for park - Unauthorised allotment of, to school - (A) Plan indicating space reserved only for school and not for park - Allotment of land to school which was part of park with permanent structure thereon - Plea that dislocation will cause difficulties - (C) Delinquent Officer misusing his power while making such type of allotment - Held, not correct - Liable to be cancelled, also plea cannot be allowed - Supreme Court accordingly directed authorities to make enquiry in matter and submit report - Appeal allowed.
