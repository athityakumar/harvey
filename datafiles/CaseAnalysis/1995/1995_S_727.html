Surjit Singh And Ors. Etc. Etc. v Harbans Singh And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2080; (1995) 6 SCC 50; AIR 1996 SC 135; 1995 (3) AWC 1944; 1995 (3) CCC 413; 1995 (2) HLR 498; JT 1995 (6) SC 415; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; 1995(5) SCALE 212; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 354

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r. 10 - Application for impleadment as parties by assignees in suit for partition of immovable property - During pendency of suit, Trial Court restrained parties from alienating or otherwise transferring any part of property - Assignment was made, despite restraint - (A) Whether it is valid? - Court, in these circumstances has duty, and also right, to treat alienation/ assignment as having not taken place at all for its purposes - Held, assignees had no cause to be impleaded - (B) Delay in justice - Case pending since 1948 - Slow and tardy progress - Further held, Trial Court was directed to dispose of suit before summer vacations of 1996 - Registrar of High Court to supervise progress - Appeal allowed.
