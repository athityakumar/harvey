Jhumman Singh And Ors. v Central Bureau Of Investigation And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 981; (1995) 3 SCC 420; AIR 1995 SC 2083; 1995 (58) DLT 646; JT 1995 (3) SC 360; 1995 (2) RentLR 112; 1995(2) SCALE 546; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 95; 1995 (2) UJ 134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Abuse Of Process Of Law

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Abuse of process of court by possessor of decrees - Maintainability of writ petition u/art. 32 - Fabricated story as to purchase of property and decree of eviction of tenant also got by manipulations - Considered to have tried to over-reach courts and circumvent and defeat ends of justice - Held, petition is maintainable and decree inexecutable - Cost of Rs. 1 lakh imposed - Petition allowed.
