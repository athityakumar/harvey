Gajanan Krishnaji Bapat and Another v Dattaji Raghobaji Meghe and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1697; (1995) 5 SCC 347; AIR 1995 SC 2284; AIR 1995 SCW 3407; JT 1995 (5) SC 410; 1995(4) SCALE 469; 1995 (3) SCJ 321; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 116A, 123(6) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 90 - Corrupt practice - Cogent evidence - Election expenditure - Appellant had filed election petition against the respondent who was the returned candidate on charges that the expenditure incurred by the respondent no 1 was much more than what he had disclosed - The suppressed expenditure involved the election expenditure and not expenditure incurred by political party and social organisations, whose names were given only to conceal the truth of the transactions - Conduct of respondent was liable to hold him guilty for committing corrupt practices and the election was to be declared void - HC passed order that though respondent had not maintained a correct and proper account of the election expenditure, it being within the permissible limit, he was not guilty of commission of corrupt practice - Appellant aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the order of HC dismissing the election petition was justified - Held, that appellants laboured under the wrong impression that once an allegation was made against the returned candidate, their responsibility was over and it was for the returned candidate to prove his innocence - In an election petition, where allegations of corrupt practice were alleged, it was for the election petitioners to prove the charge against the returned candidate beyond a reasonable doubt to the satisfaction of the court - Under the essential principles of election petition, it was incumbent upon the election petitioners to adduce better and cogent evidence, direct or circumstantial, to show that the respondent had incurred or authorised either himself or through his election agent the expenditure in respect of the publication of advertisements in papers like Lokmat, Lokmat Samachar, NavBharat Times, Yugdhardharma, issuance of voter cards, printing of poster of different sizes as alleged in the petition, but no such evidence was produced and the allegations remained unsubstantiated - Appellants had miserably failed to discharge the onus of proving various charges levelled by them against the returned candidate regarding the commission of corrupt practice u/s. 123(6) of the 1951 Act - HC was justified in dismissing the election petition - Appeal dismissed.
