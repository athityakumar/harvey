Prabhash Chand Jain and others v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2360; (1996) 8 SCC 105

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Roster Points

Summary: Service - Appointment - Roster point - Reversion - Entitlement of - State Govt. introduced reservations both in direct recruitment and in promotions in State Employment in Class III and Class IV services - For that purpose, not only it laid down percentage of reservations for different classes, but also introduced roster of 100 points - According to roster points introduced, scheduled caste candidates should be appointed at points 4, 8, 14, 18 and so on up to 100 and backward class candidates at points at 10, 16, 32 and so on - Between 9-2-1979 and 15-7-1987, candidates belonging to General category were appointed by promotion to Head Assistants post in some of departments ignoring claims of scheduled caste candidates who ought to have be appointed - Further, candidates appointed earlier according to roster points in some other departments were reverted - When scheduled caste candidates approached HC against their reversion, Single Judge quashed order of reversion - Petition was filed against further appointment of general category candidate in higher post of Superintendent wherein Single Judge allowed all Petitions and held that petitioners were entitled to be appointed to posts in question according to roster point as introduced by Circular - Decision of Single Judge was upheld by DB of HC in letters patent Appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of Single Judge that petitioners were entitled to be appointed to posts in question according to roster point as introduced by circular was justified - Held, apart from fact that letter did not indicate that it was issued under instructions of State Govt., it gave no basis for specious advice in question which was sought to be tendered by Chief Secretary to Commissioner and Secretary of Social Welfare Department - Therefore, percentage of reservation had to be worked out in relation to number of posts which form cadre strength - Concept of vacancy had no relevance in operating percentage of reservation - Court had explained that when all roster points in cadre were filled, required percentage of reservation in post was achieved - Roster point in cadre could be filled in only when vacancies occur and not otherwise - It was not contention of respondent-employees belonging to reserved category that percentage of reservation was to be calculated dehors number of posts - However, while filling posts, it was vacancies which have to be taken into consideration and that vacancies have to be filled in, according to roster points - Therefore, it was precisely what was sought to be done by circular - Hence, there was no infirmity in decision of HC - Appeals dismissed.
