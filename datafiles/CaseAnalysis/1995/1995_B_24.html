Narayan Vishnu Hendre And Ors v Baburao Savalaram Kothawalesince Deceased By His Heiranant B

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1312; (1995) 6 SCC 608; AIR 1996 SC 368; 1995 (2) BC 597; JT 1995 (7) SC 393; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; 1996 (1) RCR(Rent) 150; 1995(5) SCALE 763

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, ss. 60 and 111 - Tenancy - Implied surrender - Landlord executing mortgage by conditional sale in favour of tenant - No provision in deed for delivery of possession to mortgagor in redemption - Neither any rent was payable nor interest was to be charged during subsistence of mortgage - Whether conduct of parties and deed in question showing intention to surrender tenancy ? - Whether redemption of mortgage would revive tenancy ? - Held, not showing such intention - Redemption of mortgage would revive tenancy - Appeal allowed.
