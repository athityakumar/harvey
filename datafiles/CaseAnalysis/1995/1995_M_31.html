State Level Committee And Anr. v M/S Morgardshammar India Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2134; (1996) 1 SCC 108; AIR 1996 SC 524; 1996 ALJ 137; JT 1995 (8) SC 53; JT 1995 (8) SC 53B; 1995(6) SCALE 306; 1996 (101) STC 1; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - U.P. Sales Tax Act 1948 - Eligibility certificate - New unit - Exemption - Respondent unit had applied for issuance of eligibility certificate u/s. 4-A of the 1948 Act - Petitioner had purchased machinery, which was the respondent-unit - Respondent-unit had not been able to qualify as new unit since a part of the machinery acquired by it for setting up its factory was purchased from another unit which had purchased the said machinery earlier for their own use and so the Divisional Level Committee refused to recognise the respondent unit as a new unit - Review application of the respondent was rejected and W.P. was filed by respondent - HC accepted the W.P. and passed order for enquiry with a direction to re-examine the material on record and to record a categorical finding if the machinery purchased by the petitioner was actually used in any other factory or workshop in India - Committee aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, respondent-unit qualified as a 'new unit' u/s. 4-A of the 1948 Act - Held, that s. 4-A of the 1948 Act provided for exemption from sales tax of goods manufactured by a new unit during the first five-year period - It did not contemplate an enquiry of the nature ordered by the HC - Part of the machinery installed in the respondent-unit was acquired by another unit for use in the factory or workshop proposed to be set up by them and since their project did not materialise, the machinery purchased by them for the said purpose was lying in packed and unused condition and sold to the respondent - Expressions 'already used' and 'acquired for use' could not be interpreted to mean one and the same thing and absurd result of construction had to be avoided - Respondent unit was rightly disqualified from being regarded as a 'new unit' and was not entitled to exemption provided by s. 4-A of the 1948 Act - Appeal dismissed.
