Nand Kishore v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1263; (1995) 6 SCC 614; (1996) SCC (L&S) 57; AIR 1995 SCW 4650; 1995 (31) ATC 787; JT 1995 (7) SC 69; 1996 LabIC 610; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 48; 1995(5) SCALE 582; 1996 (1) SLJ 173; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 16; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Civil Services Rules, Pepsu Service Regulations

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Punjab Civil Service Rules - Retirement - Appellant had joined service in the erstwhile Patiala State - On the formation of Patiala and East Punjab States Union (PEPSU) State he was taken as an Assistant - On the merger of PEPSU with the State of Punjab appellant was integrated as an Assistant in the Punjab Civil Secretariat in the Food Distribution Branch - After having completed ten years qualifying service appellant was compulsorily retired from the service by an order of Governor of Punjab - Representations of the appellant to the Govt. and memorial to the Governor brought the appellant no relief - Appellant filed appeal before the Punjab HC for quashing of the order retiring the appellant compulsorily - Impugned order of compulsory retirement was challenged by appellant on a variety of grounds interalia urging that he was not governed by Rule 5.32 of the Rules but was governed by new pension rules - All the pleas of appellant were repelled - W.P. was dismissed - Hence this appeal - Whether the plea of constructive res judicata was rightly raised against the appellant - Held, View of the Full Bench of the HC was erroneous on first principles - In the question referred to the Full Bench no assumption could be made that a statutory Rule was valid when the court declined to issue a writ of mandamus or its being treated as res judicata for the purpose of the subsequent suit - SC was a competent authority to alter the law when it declares it to be unconstitutional - Appeals allowed.
