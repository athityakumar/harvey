 Sarwan Singh Lamba and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N.P. Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 303; (1995) 4 SCC 546; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1064; AIR 1995 SC 1729; 1995 (30) ATC 585; JT 1995 (5) SC 386; 1995(3) SCALE 457; 1995 (3) SCJ 18; 1995 (3) SLJ 72; 1995 (4) SLR 1; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 427; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 1215

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Administrative Tribunal

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Constitution of Tribunal - Appointment - Petitioners filed petition before HC challenging constitution of State Administrative Tribunal (Tribunal) and appointments of Vice-Chairman and Members of Tribunal on ground that Govt. had not complied with directions of SC to amend the Act and not made appointments after selection by High Powered Selection Committee as directed by SC - HC quashed appointments of respondent Nos. 3 to 7 by impugned judgment dt.29-7-1993 - Hence, instant appeals by said respondents, UOI and Industrial and Labour Bar Association which were later referred to Constitution Bench - Whether mode of selection and appointment of Chairman, Vice-Chairman and Members of the Tribunal as prescribed by amendment of 1987 is valid.

Held, judgment dt.5-5-1987 clearly indicates that direction for setting up a High Powered selection Committee was merely advisory and not mandatory in character. Further, even in the orders passed on review petitions no observation against appointments after consultation with the CJI was made. Further, although it could not be said that the prayer of UOI to introduce the provision to consult the CJI in preference to High Powered Selection Committee was allowed by court, yet it could be perceived that the court itself did not reject the prayer or reiterate the previous suggestion and hence, view expressed in order dt.5-5-1987 stood unaltered. The finding of HC that the appointments of R.P.Kapoor and G.S.Patel were vitiated because their appointments were the result of their own machination could not be upheld. Nor could it be said that their appointments were fraudulent or otherwise vitiated. Appeals disposed of.
