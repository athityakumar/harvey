M. J. Sivani and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1011; (1995) 6 SCC 289; AIR 1995 SC 1770; JT 1995 (4) SC 141; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 38; 1995(3) SCALE 80; 1995 (2) SCJ 620; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Media & Entertainment - Madras City Police Act, 1888, ss. 34, 35 and 39 - Karnataka Police Act, 1964, ss. 31 and 39 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g), 21 and 39 - Bangalore Order 1989, para 3(2)(d) -Whether licence is required to running video games - Held, requirement to obtain licence for running said games applies even when games are running within space of less than 46 1/2 sq mt under rules made u/s. 39 even if area used for purpose of running video games is less than 46 1/2 sq mt - Act of 1964 applies to places where video games are played - Licencee are prohibited from admitting school or college going students during school or college hours - Restriction is in public interest of education of students and therefore, neither unreasonable nor capricious nor violative of art. 21 - Appeal dismissed.
