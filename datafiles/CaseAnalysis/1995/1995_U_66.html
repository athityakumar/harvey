Consumer Education And Research Centre And Others v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1501; (1995) 3 SCC 42; (1995) SCC (L&S) 604; AIR 1995 SC 922; AIR 1995 SCW 759; 1996 (72) FLR 479; JT 1995 (1) SC 636; 1995 LabIC 1368; 1995 (2) LLJ 768; 1996 (2) LLN 1; 1995(1) SCALE 354; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 626; 1995 (1) UJ 648

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Constitution - Labour and Industrial Law - Tort - Constitution, arts. Pre., 21, 32, 38, 39(e), 41, 43, 48A and 226- Factories Act, 1948, ss. 89, sch.1, 112 and 113 - Rights of workmen in Asbestos industries - (A) Right to life - Connotation of expression 'life' with regard to 'Hygienic conditions in work place and leisure' and right to health and medical care - (C) Enforcement and protection of fundamental rights - Applicability of defence of sovereign immunity when remedy of award of compensation available u/arts. 32 and 226 - (D) Vicariously liability of employer for occupational diseases - Workman affected by asbestosis died after cessation of employment - (E) Factories Act - Applicability of Model Rule 123A - Held, state obliged to provide to workmen facilities to reach minimum standard of health, economic security and civilised living - Right to life, includes hygienic conditions in work place and leisure to workman, traditional and cultural heritage of a person and workman has right to health and medical care, both during and after service - Not applicable - Employer liable to pay liquidated damages - Bound by Rules regarding 'safety in the use of asbestos' issued by International Labour Organisation - Petition allowed.
