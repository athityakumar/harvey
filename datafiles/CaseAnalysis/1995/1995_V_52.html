State Of Gujarat v Vora Saiyedbhai Kadarbhai And Ors,

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 581; (1995) 3 SCC 196; AIR 1995 SC 2208; 1995 (1) GLH 1026; 1995 (1) G.L.R. 705; JT 1995 (2) SC 644; 1995(2) SCALE 81; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 470; 1995 (1) UJ 802

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Municipalities & Local Governments; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f) and (g) - Gujarat Rural Debtors Relief Act, 1976, s. 14 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Whether High Court of Gujarat justified in striking down expression 'or an order reducing his debt is made' used in sub-s. (2) of s. 14 of Gujarat Rural Debtors Relief Act, 1976 on ground that it is ultra vires arts. 19(1)(f) and 19(1)(g) of Constitution? - Held, Legislature of Gujarat which had a human problem of saving poverty stricken debtors from clutches of non-institutional creditors, relieving them of their debts to extent found necessary and getting return of their properties from creditors given as security for their debts for eke out their livelihood it was very much justified in introducing provision in sub-s. (2) of s. 14 of Act, which enabled debtors to get back their properties given as security constitutionality of sub-s. (2) of s. 14 of Act is upheld - Appeal allowed.
