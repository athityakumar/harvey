State of Rajasthan v Fateh Chand Soni

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1901; (1996) SCC (L&S) 340; (1996) 1 SCC 562; 1996 (32) ATC 488; JT 1995 (9) SC 523; 1995(7) SCALE 168; 1996 (1) SLR 1; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 559; 1996 (1) Supreme 52

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Rajasthan Police Service Rules, 1954 - Selection - Seniority - Promotion - Election - Appellants and respondents were directly recruited in Police service and promoted to the Senior Scale - Respondent no. 1 was senior to the appellants - Appointment of appellants was on seniority-cum-merit basis - After promotions the seniority scale was fixed - Respondent filed W.P. challenging the seniority list - HC allowed writ and quashed the seniority list - Appellants aggrieved - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, seniority in the Selection Scale was to be fixed on the basis of date of appointment to the Selection Scale or on the basis of seniority in the Senior Scale - Held, that appointment to the Selection Scale of an officer in the Senior Scale in the Service constituted promotion and seniority in the Selection Scale had to be fixed in accordance with r. 33 of the 1954 Rules on the basis of the date of selection - A person selected and appointed as a result of an earlier selection would rank senior to a person who was selected and appointed as a result of a subsequent selection - Appellants were selected and appointed on the basis of earlier selection in 1989 while respondent no. 1 was selected and appointed on the basis of a subsequent selection in 1991, and therefore, appellants were rightfully ranked senior to respondent no. 1 in the Selection Scale - The Selection Scale was a separate scale and not an extension of the Senior Scale - Appointment to the Selection Scale was made by selection on the basis of merit and seniority-cum-merit in accordance with r. 28(a) of the 1954 Rules - Seniority of officers in the Selection Scale of the Service had to be fixed as per r. 33 of the 1954 Rules on the basis of the state of selection - Appeals allowed.
