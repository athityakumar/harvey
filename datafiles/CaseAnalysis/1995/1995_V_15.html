P. Udayani Devi v V.V. Rajeshwara Prasad Rao And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 598; (1995) 3 SCC 252; AIR 1995 SC 1357; 1995 (1) CCC 612; JT 1995 (3) SC 523; 1995(2) SCALE 43; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land and Property - CPC, 1908, s. 115, O. 21, r. 94 - (A) Sale certificate - Boundaries of property sold in execution mentioned in certificate - Name and number of property also given in certificate - Only conclusion possible from sale certificate is that entire property falling within the boundaries was sold - Property comprising of a terraced building, and a building having room on first floor and open land - If property sold can be split into two portions merely on basis of words terraced house used in sale certificate so as to confine sale certificate to only part of the property sold - (B) Scope of revision - Finding about property that has been sold in execution of decree - Held, (A) Cannot be split into - (B) Is finding of fact and not revisable - Appeal allowed.
