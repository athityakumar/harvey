Collector of Central Excise v Messrs H.M.M. Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 561; (1995) Supp3 SCC 322; 1997 (71) ECR 331; 1995 (76) E.L.T. 497; JT 1995 (2) SC 517

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11-A - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether the show cause notice was time barred under the main part of s. 11-A, since it was issued after the expiry period of six months stipulated therein or it falls under the proviso? - In order to attract the proviso it must be shown that the excise duty escaped payment by reason of fraud, collusion or willful misstatement or suppression of fact or contravention of any provision of the Act or of the Rules made thereunder with intent to evade payment of duty - Mere non declaration of the waste/by product in their classification list cannot establish any willful withholding of vital information for the purpose of evasion of excise duty due on the said product - Held, the Addl. Collector did not specifically deal with this contention of the assessee but drew inference that classification list did not make any mention in regard to this waste product it could be inferred that assessee had apparently tried to evade the payment of excise duty - Appeal dismissed.
