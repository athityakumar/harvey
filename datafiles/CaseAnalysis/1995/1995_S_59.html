State of Maharashtra and Another v Sant Joginder Singh Kishan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 375; (1995) Supp2 SCC 475; AIR 1995 SC 2181; JT 1995 (3) SC 21; 1995(2) SCALE 121; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11A, 16, 17, 18 and 23 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act, 1966, ss. 126(2) and 125 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act 37 of 1966 was invoked for acquiring land in question by Regional and Town Planning Board for planned development in State of Maharashtra - Land could be acquired by agreement with owner, or making an application to State Govt. for acquisition - Declaration has to be made within three years from date of publication of notification - Failure to have declaration published within three years entails with prohibition to take further action in acquiring land - U/s. 126(4) declaration is not made within three years or having been made, aforesaid period expired on commencement of Amendment Act, State Govt. has been empowered to make a fresh declaration for acquiring land in manner provided by ss. 126(2) and (3) - (A) Whether award has to be made within two years from date of declaration published u/s. 6 Land Acquisition Act, 1894 i.e., u/s. 126(2) of Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act and this period of limitation must be deemed to have been incorporated in Act? - Held, provision was made in that behalf to put a fetter on exercise of power u/s. 126(2) and simultaneously mitigated - The hardship to be caused to public purpose as well as to private interest of owner of land - While permitting state to exercise its power of eminent domain, owner of land or person interested in land has been relieved of hard ship of payment of compensation as per price prevailing as on date of publication of notification u/s. 125 and directed that market value be determined as on date of publication of fresh declaration u/s. 126(4) - Held, though declaration u/s. 126(2) is not made within three years as prescribed under proviso by necessary inference it would be construed that notification u/s. 125 does not lapse and fresh declaration made u/s. 126(4) is valid - Govt. was then clearly within its power to have, published declaration u/s. 126(2) in official gazette - (B) Whether since award has not been made within two years from date of declaration u/s. 126(2), by operation of s. 11 A, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, notification published u/s. 125 shall be deemed to have been lapsed and, therefore, authorities are devoid of jurisdiction to proceed further? - Held, state legislature amended Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act, 1966 and introduced 3 years limitation for publication of declaration u/s. 126(2) - No attempt was taken by state legislature to amend Act introducing or incorporating s. 11A Land Acquisition Act, 1894 as part of Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act, 1966 - Since legislature had incorporated specific provisions of Land Acquisition Act, 1894, necessary conclusion is that legislature did not intend to apply unspecified provisions of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 to exercise of power under Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act, 1966 - (C) Whether since no separate procedure was prescribed by Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act,1966 for determining compensation, by necessary inference, Land Acquisition Act, 1894 was intended to be applied mutatis mutandis to acquisition under Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act,1966? - Held, compensation need to be determined applying principles in s. 23 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - But, there is a distinction between procedural and substantive provisions of the statute - Determination of compensation by applying appropriate principles is relatable to substantive provision, whereas making of award within a prescribed period is basically procedural - Held, merely because s. 23 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 would apply to acquisition under Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning, Act, 1966, is not enough to hold that what is contained in s. 11-A would also apply - Further, what has been provided in s. 126(4) is clear indication that failure to make award within two years from date of declaration would not render notification published u/s. 125 non-est - Appeals allowed.
