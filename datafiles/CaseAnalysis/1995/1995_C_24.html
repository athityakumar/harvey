Chairman, State Bank of India and Others v Golak Bihari Dehury

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1545; (1996) 7 SCC 63; (1996) SCC (L&S) 458; 1996 (32) ATC 780; 1995 (8) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Promotion - Entitlement of - Penalty to respondent of reduction of basic pay by three stages - Non-consideration for promotion to Middle Management Grade Scale II - Due to debarment policy - Policy prescribes the delinquent officer is not eligible for consideration for promotion till rigor period is over i.e. till pay is restored to level before punishment - Whether the right of the respondent u/art. 16 of the Constitution has been violated by the appellant by non-considering him in promotion on account of debarment policy? - Held, when an employee is held guilty and penalized and is, therefore, not promoted at least till date on which he is penalised, he cannot be said to have been subjected to a further penalty on that account - A denial of promotion in such circumstances is not a penalty but a necessary consequence of his conduct - Action of appellant in not considering respondent for promotion to MMGS-II during currency of penalty of reduction in basic pay that was imposed on him not violative of his right guaranteed under art. 16 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
