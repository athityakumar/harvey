Sheikh Ishaque and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1761; (1995) 3 SCC 392; (1995) SCC (Cr) 534; AIR 1995 SCW 2001; 1995 (2) CCR 48; 1995 (2) Crimes 294; 1995 CRLJ 2682; JT 1995 (5) SC 638; 1995(2) SCALE 271; 1995 (3) SCJ 259; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 692; 1995 (2) UJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154 and 354 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 436 and 34 - Murder - Death sentence - Challenged - Prior enmity between parties - Whether conviction and death sentence on appellants proper? - While appreciating death sentence cases, courts should take into account aggravating circumstances and also mitigating circumstances - S. 354(3) of CrPC, 1973 makes it obligatory cases of conviction for offence punishable with death or with imprisonment for life to assign reason in support of sentence awarded to convict and further ordains that in case Judge awards death penalty 'special reasons' for such sentences shall be stated in judgment - Held, identity and complicity of appellants in crime stands fully established by prosecution witnesses - Prosecution has established case against appellants beyond a reasonable doubt - Conviction for offence u/s. 302/34 IPC, 1860 and ss. 436/34 IPC, 1860 upheld - Further held, neither of two courts below have given any special reasons for awarding sentence of death - Sentence of death imposed upon four appellants is not warrant - Sentence of death commuted to life imprisonment - Appeal dismissed.
