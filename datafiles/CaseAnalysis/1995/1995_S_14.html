The Deputy Director Of Collegiate Education v S. Nagoor Meera

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1581; (1995) 3 SCC 377; (1995) SCC (L&S) 686; AIR 1995 SC 1364; AIR 1995 SCW 1360; 1995 (29) ATC 574; JT 1995 (3) SC 32; 1995 LabIC 1615; 1995(2) SCALE 1; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 308; 1995 (2) SLJ 89; 1995 (2) SLR 379

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 (2) - Show cause notice issued - Challenged - Show cause notice was issued to the respondent calling upon him to show cause why he should not be dismissed from service in view of his conviction by the criminal court - Respondent challenged said show cause notice before Tribunal submitting that inasmuch as the sentence imposed upon him by the criminal court has been suspended by the appellate court, no proceedings can be taken for terminating his services under and with reference to clause (a) of the second proviso to art. 311(2) of the Constitution - Tribunal has quashed the aforesaid show cause notice - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the Tribunal has given yet another reason for quashing the show cause notice, viz., that whereas the conviction of the criminal court was on 4-2-1991, the impugned show cause notice was issued only on 27-10-1993. The appellant has explained that though the respondent had come to know the conviction soon after the judgment of the criminal court there was a doubt whether action can be taken against the respondent in view of the order of the High Court suspending the sentence. It is stated that after obtaining legal advice, the show cause notice was issued. The delay, if it can be called one, in initiating the proceedings has been properly explained and in any event, the delay is not such as to vitiate the action taken. Order of the Tribunal was set aside. Appeal allowed.
