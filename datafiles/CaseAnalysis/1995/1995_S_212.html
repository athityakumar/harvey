Smt. Shakuntalabai And Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 860; (1996) 2 SCC 152; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 541; 1995 (4) CCC 351; JT 1995 (8) SC 501; 1995(6) SCALE 693; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 18 - Whether sale deeds pertaining to lands adjacent to acquired land relevant for determination of its market value when evidence of market value commanded by acquired land itself is available? - Held, in a case where evidence of value of acquired land itself is available on record, it is unnecessary to travel beyond that evidence and consider market value prevailing in adjacent lands; when owner himself has purchased land under acquisition, consideration mentioned in sale deed would form basis to determine market value - Appeal dismissed.
