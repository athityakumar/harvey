Md. Noorul Hoda v Bibi Raifunnisa and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 739; (1996) 7 SCC 767; 1996 (1) CCC 41; 1996 (1) CLT 192; JT 1995 (9) SC 256; 1995(7) SCALE 326; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 110; 1996 (1) Supreme 242

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 31 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 55(1) - Possession and Enjoyment - Binami Land - Partition and Division - Limitation Barred - Respondent filed Suit for partition and division, and preliminary decree was passed, in her favor - Petitioner in possession and enjoyment of binami land, filed suit for declaration, that decrees in suit were illegal, and prayed for perpetual injunction - Courts below hold that preliminary or final decree, could not bind petitioner and suit was filed beyond three years, so barred by limitation under Limitation Act, 1963 - HC also confirmed same - Hence, instant petition - Whether petitioner's title suit was filed within limitation period - Held, If Petitioner seek avoidance of instrument, decree or contract and seek declaration to set aside decrees or cancelled, he was bound to lay suit within three years when he claimed title to property as owner - As, Brother-in-law of petitioner had first known about passing of preliminary decree and final decree, Art. 113 was inapplicable from date, when title deed was filed and preliminary and final decree was passed - Suit was filed beyond three years from date of knowledge of Petitioner and so barred by limitation - Decree of appellate Court and order of HC, were not illegal warranting interference - Appeal dismissed.
