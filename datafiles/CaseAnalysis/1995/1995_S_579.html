Sh. Madan Gopal Garg v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 537; (1995) Supp3 SCC 366; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1235; 1995 (31) ATC 150; 1995 (71) FLR 445; JT 1995 (6) SC 189; 1996 LabIC 950; 1995 (2) LLN 708; 1995(4) SCALE 361; 1995 (3) SLJ 152; 1995 (4) SLR 412; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 815; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1638

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Service - Punjab Food and Supplies Department (State Service Class-II) Rules, 1966, r. 6(1)(a) (g) - Whether question regarding inter se seniority of promotees and direct recruits? - Appointment of the post of Controller - Seniority is governed by Rule which prescribes that for the purpose of seniority service shall be divided into four groups - Whether the promotion of Petitioners as Controllers was within their quota? - Method of appointment - Seniority of the members of service - Expression "posts" has been used while in the proviso to clause the expression "vacancies" is used in the same sense to mean posts in the cadre - Whether quota rule governing appointment on the vacancies in the post of Controller had broken down in view of the promotions in excess of the quota made? - Number of persons after appointment on the post of Controller have been sent on deputation and the appointments were made against temporary vacancies resulting from such officers being sent on deputation - Appointment of the appellant as Controller was in excess of the quota and it continued to be so till 3rd respondent was appointed by direct recruitment - Promotion from the post of Controller to the post of Deputy Director had to be made on the basis of seniority cum merit - Held, appeal dismissed.
