Sat Pal and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1828; (1995) Supp4 SCC 1; (1996) SCC (Cr) 11; 1996 (1) CCR 35; 1995 (4) Crimes 301; 1995(6) SCALE 86

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution, art. 136 - IPC, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Four persons charged with murder - Three convicted and one acquitted by Trial Court - On appeal, High Court affirmed the conviction and sentence recorded by the Trial Court, reversed the acquittal of Sat Pal by convicting and sentencing him to imprisonment for life u/s. 302/34, IPC, 1860 - Present appeal - Held, having carefully gone through the evidence of P W 2 and P W 6 Courts are satisfied that their evidence as against Sat Pal is also cogent and consistent and HC was fully justified in relying upon the same - So far as the other three appellants are concerned both the Learned Courts below, who were entrusted with the duty of investigating into questions of fact have, on a proper discussion and appraisal of the evidence, recorded their findings against them - No interference u/art. 136 required - Appeal dismissed.
