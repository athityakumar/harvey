State Of Maharashtra v Manubhai Pragaji Vashi And Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1146; (1995) 5 SCC 730; AIR 1996 SC 1; JT 1995 (6) SC 119; 1995(4) SCALE 797; 1995 (3) SCJ 610; 1996 (1) SLJ 1; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 733; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1519

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Education Norms, Bar Association, Bar Council, Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 14, 21 and 39 A - Education - Recognised private law colleges - Extension of grant in aid by State - Denial to said colleges and granted to others at same time - Validity - Held, is inherently discriminatory - Violative of mandate under arts. 21 and 39 A - Writ not maintainable claiming relief invoking directive principle of Constitution.
