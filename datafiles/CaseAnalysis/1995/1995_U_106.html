Union of India and Others v C.N. Ponnappan

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 717; (1996) SCC (L&S) 331; (1996) 1 SCC 524; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 243; AIR 1996 SC 764; 1996 (32) ATC 479; JT 1995 (9) SC 175; 1996 LabIC 763; 1996 (1) LLN 420; 1995(7) SCALE 116; 1996 (1) SCJ 135; 1996 (1) SLR 18; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Stenographer

Summary: Service - Constitution, art. 311 - Eligibility criterion for Promotion - Transfer of employee from one unit to other on compassionate grounds - Employee resultantly placed at bottom of seniority list - Service done at place from where he has been transferred being regular service - Whether to be counted as experience for purpose of eligibility for promotion at transferred place - Held, it has to be counted as experience - Appeal dismissed.
