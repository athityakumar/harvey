Nand Kishore Mehra v Sushila Mehra

Supreme Court of India

02 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1998; (1995) 4 SCC 572; AIR 1995 SC 2145; 1995 (26) ALR 440; 1995 (128) CTR 54; [1995] 215 ITR 218; JT 1995 (5) SC 130; 1995 (3) PLR 382; 1995 (2) RentLR 222; 1995(4) SCALE 254; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 797; [1995] 81 TAXMAN 418; 1995 (2) UJ 592

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, ss. 3 (2), 4(1) and (2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 4, O. 11 - Whether prohibition to file a suit or to take up a defiance in respect of a benami transaction imposed by s. 4 of 1988 Act applies to a Benami transaction of purchase of property by a person in name of his wife or unmarried daughter? - Held, since plaintiff is husband who had right to enter into a Benami transaction in matter of purchase of property in name of his wife or unmarried daughter, he is entitled to enforce his rights in properties concerned if he can succeed in showing that he had purchased them Benami in name of his wife - But in view of statutory presumption incorporated in sub-s. (2) of s. 3 of Act, plaintiff can get relief sought in suit only if he can prove that property's concerned had not been purchased for benefit of wife, even if he succeeds in showing that consideration for purchases of properties had been paid by him - Appeal allowed.
