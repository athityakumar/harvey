M/S. Bharat Coking Coal Ltd. v M/S. C.K. Ahuja And Another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2216; (1995) Supp1 SCC 744; 1995 (2) ARBLR 66; JT 1995 (3) SC 132; 1995(1) SCALE 705; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14 (2), 17 and 29 - Reference to arbitration by Court - By consent of parties - Sole arbitrator - Making award rule of court - Notice of award - Filing of award in proper court - Service of copy of award to parties - Time for filing objections to award starts from date of notice - Inspection of award - Barred by limitation - Issuance of second notice to parties at instance of request by counsel cannot be deemed to be date from which limitation starts - Held, Reference to arbitration by Supreme Court - Filing of award and objections in Supreme Court where arbitration originated through reference - In petition of objection it has not been urged that there has been any impropriety in filing award in this Court, accordingly, such objection should not be permitted to be raised at this stage as objection to award has been filed long after period of limitation - Application disposed of.
