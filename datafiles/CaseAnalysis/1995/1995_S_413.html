Ranbir Yadav v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 394; (1995) 4 SCC 392; (1995) SCC (Cr) 728; AIR 1995 SC 1219; AIR 1995 SCW 1980; 1995 (2) CCR 56; 1995 (2) Crimes 161; 1995 CRLJ 2665; JT 1995 (3) SC 228; 1995(2) SCALE 331; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 826; 1995 (2) UJ 212

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 149 and 300 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 154, 161, 162, 194, 216, 326 and 350 - Accused convicted for murder - Appeal - (A) Whether report of incident lodged by prosecution witnesses on next day morning could be taken as FIR - (B) Whether Judge can exercise his judicial discretion for fresh examination - Held, (A) report cannot be taken as FIR - Court may exclude said statement u/s.162 of Code - (B) a Judge can exercise discretion only for further examination and not for fresh examination - Evidence conclusively proved that all accused shared common object of unlawful assembly to commit offences of loot, arson and murder and causing disappearance of evidence of murder - No interference in conviction of accused required - Appeal dismissed.
