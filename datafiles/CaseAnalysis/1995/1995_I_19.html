Union of India and Others v Indian Aluminium Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1026; (1995) Supp2 SCC 465; AIR 1995 SC 1580; 1995 (58) ECR 612; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 268; JT 1995 (5) SC 465; 1995 (8) RLT 421; 1995(3) SCALE 29; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise And Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 56-A - Whether excise duty should be levied on aluminium dross and skimmings removed by assessees between September 1972 and March 1973 ? - Dross and skimmings may contain some small percentage of metal, but dross and skimmings are not metal in same class as waste or scrap - They can be sold, but this does not make them a marketable commodity - Everything which is sold is not necessarily a marketable commodity as known to commerce and which, it may be worthwhile to trade in - Held, Single Judge rightly came to conclusion that proviso to r. 56-A was not applicable as aluminium dross and skimmings are not excisable goods - Appeals dismissed.
