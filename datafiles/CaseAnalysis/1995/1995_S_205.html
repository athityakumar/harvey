Union Of India v Smt. Pratap Kaur (Dead) Through Lrs. And Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1498; (1995) 3 SCC 263; 1995 (25) ALR 461; 1995 (2) CCC 78; JT 1995 (2) SC 569; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 106; 1995 (1) RentLR 588; 1995(2) SCALE 118; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 670; 1996 (Supp) AWC 84

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(i) and 26 - Compensation for acquisition of land - Whether District Judge has power and jurisdiction to award compensation @ Rs. 90,000 acre to area coming within belt of 500 metres as ordered by Division Bench of High Court? - Collector had jurisdiction to determine compensation including belting for purpose of determining market value and correct clerical and arithmetical mistakes committed in making award - HC had exercised appellate power u/s. 54 of Act Appellate power is coextensive with that of Civil Court - HC, while exercising appellate power, could also decide belting Division Bench in LPA while determining compensation had identified belting up to a depth of 500 metres and directed payment of compensation for that land at Rs. 90,000 per acre - When HC exercised appellate power, without any order of remand or calling for a finding, District Judge was devoid of power or jurisdiction to correct any error,that too beyond limitation - Held, with making award Civil Court ceased to have power to alter award except to correct clerical or arithmetical errors - Further held, Civil Court was devoid of jurisdiction and power to pass impugned award or order, as stated by Addl. District Judge and that too after it had made award - Since Addl. District Judge was not called upon to determine compensation after identification of land within belting of 500 metres as determined by Division Bench, order of District Judge is clearly without jurisdiction and power and is a nullity - HC order reversed - Appeal allowed.
