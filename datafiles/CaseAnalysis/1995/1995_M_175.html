Most. Rev. P.M.A. Metropolitan & Ors. v Moran Mar Marthoma & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 June 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1097; (1995) Supp4 SCC 286; AIR 1995 SC 2001; AIR 1995 SCW 3133; 1995 (2) HLR 342; JT 1995 (5) SC 1; 1995(4) SCALE 1; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Aliens

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 9(A), 11 and 9, Expln 1, O 47, r.1, O 1, r.10 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 25 - Places of Worship (Special Provisions) Act, 1991, s. 4(3) - Religious matter - Dispute about religious office of Christians - (A) Civil Court - Jurisdiction - (1) Plea of absence of jurisdiction, when can be raised - (2) Expansive nature of s. 9 of Civil Code - (3) Scope - (4) - Excommunication - (5) Constitution of Malankara Association framed by religious body - Cl enabling every Parish Church irrespective of its size to send 3 delegates - Determining its fairness - (6) Powers of Court, when a particular people say that they believe in spiritual superiority of Patriarch and that it is an article of faith with them - (B) Right to religion - (1) Scope as regard to seeking declaration that Church was episcopal - (2) 'Civil nature' - (C) Places of Worship (Special Provisions) Act - Suit seeking declaration for period prior to Act or for enforcement of right recognized before Act is not barred - Whether suit for declaration that Jacobite Church was autocephalous is barred - (D) Ex-communication - Of Catholico by Patriarch - (E) Review Court - Powers - Court restricting scope of hearing - Findings on issues not allowed to be re-opened - (F) Res judicata - (1) Decision taken in appeal - (2) Applicability - (G) Precedents - Findings given in a case on authenticity of religious canon - (H) Necessary party in suit - (I) Malankara Church - (1) Whether Episcopal - (2) Whether Patriarch can that temporal affairs were governed by Constitution of Malankara Church - (3) Applicability of Constitution of Malankara Church to Knanaya Churches having their acts and conduct - (J) Temporal powers of Syrian Orthodox Church - Catholicos and Patriarch group - Held : (A) Can be raised and entertained at any stage - Content of section widened by use of words 'Civil nature' - Extends to such religious matters which have civil consequences and in absence of any ecclesiastical Courts any religious dispute is cognizable, except in very rare cases where declaration sought may be what constitutes religious rite - Civil Court has jurisdiction to examine - Held unfair, so amendment suggested to be made - Court cannot say 'no; your spiritual superior is Catholicos ' - (B) Includes right to seek declaration that Church was Episcopal, Civil suit for such declaration is maintainable u/s. 9 as scope and ambit of Expln 1 to s. 9 is not restrictive - Religious right is civil in nature - Its infringement is a civil wrong - (C) Not barred - (D) Held was not in accordance with law or religious canons - (E) Can as yet be set aside by review Court - (F) - (1) Appellate Court's decision operate as res judicata and not trial Court's decision - (2)- Matter must have been adjudicated stricto senso in earlier litigation - SC restoring decree of trial Court - Findings of trial Court which formed basis for its decree would become conclusive - (G) Cannot be used as precedent - (H) Declaration cannot be given in absence of Parish Churches - (I) Is episcopal to extent it is so declared in 1934 Constitution - Cannot be denied by Patriarch they having conceded, recognised and affirmed all these things - 1934 Constitution of Malankara Church shall govern them but subject to their own Knanaya Constitution until such time Knanaya Church Samudayam decides otherwise - (J) Their temporal powers over Malankara Churches Stated - Order accordingly.
