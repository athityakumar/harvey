Karnal Improvement Trust, Karnal v Parkash Wanti Smt. (Dead) and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1219; (1995) 5 SCC 159; JT 1995 (5) SC 151; 1995(3) SCALE 487; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure,1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18 and 60 - Punjab Improvement Trust Act, 1922, s. 36 - Composition nature of tribunal for land acquisition - Whether President of tribunal alone can pass award under Act? - Mandatory quorum is three members - Mere fact that President will record evidence, in absence of assessors, or that he is given power to preside over tribunal and to compel presence of witnesses or to secure evidence does not per force minimise or undermine composition of, continuance and functions of assessors as members of tribunal - Presence and participation of each member of tribunal should be mandatory, unless his absence becomes unavoidable and beyond his control - President may be a judicial officer and assessors may be only local men having acquaintance with prevailing prices of land - President has no option but has to associate other members in determining compensation - All three members should be present and should participate at time of enquiry unless unavoidable, hear matter on merits and decision of tribunal, if not unanimous and if there be difference of opinion, be as per majority - Held, President alone could not make award under act - Determination of compensation in disregard thereof renders adjudication void, invalid and inoperative - High Court judgment and tribunal award set aside - Appeal allowed.
