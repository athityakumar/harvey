State of Punjab and Another v Jagir Singh Etc

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1837; (1995) Supp4 SCC 626; 1995 (4) CCC 81; 1996 (1) CLT 39; JT 1995 (9) SC 1; 1995 (2) RentLR 674; 1995(6) SCALE 314; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Land Acquisition Amendment Act, 1984, ss. 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 - Whether High Court has power to award statutory benefits u/ss. 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 while confirming the decree of Reference Court? - Civil Court on reference u/s. 18 or High Court or in some States District Judge exercising appellate power u/s. 54 or civil court u/s. 26, as the case may be, awards compensation in excess of the amount awarded by the Collector, then it gets jurisdiction and power to award additional benefits envisaged in ss. 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 of Act, 1984 - Held, if the HC dismissed the appeal confirming the award of Collector or that of the Civil Court, then it has no jurisdiction and power to award additional statutory amount under the respective provisions as amended under the Amendment Act - HC, therefore, has no power to award the statutory benefits u/ss. 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 while confirming the decree of Reference Court - Appeal allowed.
