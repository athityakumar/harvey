Assistant General Manager, Central Bank of India and Others v Commissioner, Municipal Corporation For The City of Ahmedabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1213; (1995) 4 SCC 696; 1995 (2) CCC 547; JT 1995 (4) SC 310; 1995 (1) RCJ 550; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 263; 1995 (2) RentLR 233; 1995(3) SCALE 561; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963, Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923

Summary: Rent Control - Municipalities & Local Governments - Indirect Tax - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 96 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, ss. 406 and 2(1-A)(ii)(aa) - (A) whether a tenant of a building is entitled to file and maintain an appeal against order assessing property tax? - Bombay Rent Act, s. 10 empowers landlord to pass on burden of increase in property taxes to tenant Municipal Corporations Act, s. 140 makes him liable to pay property taxes in case landlord fails to pay same - Landlord alone cannot file complaint and appeal - It would not be just and equitable to deny that tenant has no right of appeal and right to file complaint - Tenant does possess requisite locus standi to file a complaint - If there is an agreement between landlord and tenant, obligation to discharge and pay property taxes is cast upon tenant - Although this is a private arrangement between parties and cannot form basis of a legal right but it is certainly an additional factor conferring requisite locus standi upon tenant - Held, it is to be disagreed with HC that tenant cannot file an appeal and that it is only owner/landlord of premises who can file an appeal u/s. 406 of Act - Further held where tenant lodges a complaint, he can directly file an appeal u/s. 406 but where he himself has not filed a complaint, he has to file appeal with leave of appellate court - (B) whether s. 2 (1-A) (ii) (aa) of said Act is valid and effective? - A contract for a rent at a rate higher than standard rent is not not enforceable - So far as Municipal Corporations Act is concerned annual rent is actual rent received where standard rent is not fixed u/s. 11 of Bombay Rent Act and it constitutes basis for determining annual letting value, ratable value and property taxes - Said provision is conceived to meet situations where rent put forward as actual rent received is not a genuine plea, i.e., where it is a false plea - A landlord may let out a building at less than market rent for many a reason, e.g., the tenant is a close friend or a close relative or because tenant is a charitable or religious organisation - In such cases, rent actual received is genuinely stipulated one - Further held, rental value of building cannot be fixed higher standard rent under Rent Control Act - Appeal partly allowed.
