Smt. Prem Lata and Another v Messrs Ishar Dass Chaman Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 521; (1995) 2 SCC 145; AIR 1995 SC 714; 1995 (1) ARBLR 321; 1995 (1) BC 636; JT 1995 (1) SC 557; 1995(1) SCALE 145; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 168; 1995 (1) UJ 421

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil - Arbitration - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Petitioners were widow and son of deceased partner of respondent firm, called upon the respondents to render the accounts of the firm - Matter referred to arbitration and s. 20 of Arbitration Act, 1940 invoked - High Court held that ss. 69(1) and (3) exclude the application of s. 20 of the Arbitration Act - Whether suit filed u/s. 20 of the Arbitration Act is maintainable to work out the rights given to the parties u/s. 69(3)(a) of the Partnership Act - Held, yes - No prohibition exists against invoking arbitration clause for rendering accounts of firm - Right to sue for the dissolution of the firm must be interpreted to mean the right to enforce the arbitration clause for resolution of the disputes or for rendition of accounts or any right to realize the property of the dissolved firm - Appeal allowed.
