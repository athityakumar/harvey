Harish Tandon v Additional District Magistrate, Allahabad, U. P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.S. Anand, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1650; (1995) 1 SCC 537; AIR 1995 SC 676; AIR 1995 SCW 453; 1995 ALJ 350; 1995 (25) ALR 184; JT 1995 (1) SC 290; 1995 (13) LCD 898; 1995 (1) RCR(Rent) 217; 1995 (1) RentLR 357; 1995(1) SCALE 65; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control

Keywords: Deemed Vacancy

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, ss. 3(g), 12(2), 12(3) explanation (b), 20(2)(e) and 25 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Eviction - Tenancy rights devolve on original tenant's heirs jointly on his death - One of joint tenants admitted son-in-law as partner in business - Deemed vacancy of premises u/s. 12(2) and (4) of 1972 Act - (A) Whether amounts to sub-letting and eviction can be ordered u/s. 20(2)(e) - (B) One of sons living with tenant (not dependent) and tenant also acquiring any other residential building in same city or town - Whether original tenant would be deemed to have ceased to occupy building within scope of provisions of Act - Held, it amounts to sub-letting within meaning of s. 25 explanation (1) and eviction can be ordered - Cannot be deemed so - Appeal allowed.
