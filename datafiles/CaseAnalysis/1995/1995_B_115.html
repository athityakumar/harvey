 Buta Singh (Dead) By Lrs v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1555; (1995) 5 SCC 283

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Whether decision in Chand Kaur v Union of India raised a conflict which was needed to be resolved by a Constitution Bench of Court? - Held, case of Chand Kaur is per incuriam, inasmuch as, attention of Bench was not invited to a three-Judge Bench decision in case of Scheduled Caste Coop Land Owning Society Ltd. - Subsequent decision in case of Chand Kaur to extent it conflicts with view taken in that case cannot be said to be laying down correct law - Reference disposed to Bench accordingly for disposal in accordance with law laid down in Scheduled Caste Coop Land Owning Society Ltd. - Appeal allowed.
