Faqir (Dead) Through Shyam Deo v Kishori Alias Lalloo and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1061; (1995) 4 SCC 533; AIR 1995 SC 1569; 1995 ALJ 1349; JT 1995 (7) SC 614; 1996 (87) RD 1; 1995(2) SCALE 867; 1995 (2) SCJ 289; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Aboilition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 229-B - Suit for declaration of bhumidhari rights - Whether decree passed by civil court was a nullity as civil court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit for permanent injunction or for alternative relief of possession and that such a suit could be filed only before revenue courts? - Held, suit could be filed only in civil court and not in revenue court - Decree passed by Trial Court, which was upheld by Lower Appellate Court as also in High Court and thereafter by division bench, binding on respondent Faqir against whom suit was filed - Consolidation courts not justified in ignoring those decrees on ground that this court in Chandrika Misir case had laid down that a suit u/s. 209 could be filed only in revenue court and a decree passed by civil court was a nullity - Since suits u/s. 209 of Act were made cognizable by revenue court only with effect from 28-05-1956, suit in Chandrika Misir case which was filed on 05-09-1955 was, therefore, cognizable by civil court and not by revenue court - Further HC, was right in allowing writ petition and quashing orders passed by consolidation authorities - Appeal dismissed.
