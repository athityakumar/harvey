Vinay Chandra Mishra(The Alleged Contemner)(In Re) v Na

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1757; (1995) 2 SCC 584; AIR 1995 SC 2348; AIR 1995 SCW 3488; 1995 (2) CCR 101; 1995 (2) Crimes 381; 1995 CRLJ 3994; 1995 (1) GLH 690; 1995 (2) G.L.R. 992; JT 1995 (2) SC 587; 1995 (1) KarLJ 504; 1995(2) SCALE 200; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 638; 1995 (2) UJ 93

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19, 32, 129, 142, 215 and 226 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2, 12 and 14 - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 35 and 38 - Contempt of High Court committed by Senior Advocate - (A) Powers of Supreme Court to take cognizance - (B) Plea to refer it to larger Bench when as per pre-decided case, Supreme Court u/art. 129 can take cognizance of contempt of HC - (C) Trial procedure for 'In facie curie' contempt - (D) Advocate resented questions of Judge and tried to browbeat, threaten, insult and show disrespect personally to Judge and therby creating scene in court - Calculated acts to obstruct course of justice - (E) Whether power to suspend him from practice or remove his name from roll of State Bar Council is available with Supreme Court - Held, Supreme Court can take cognizance - Plea rejected - Claim for examination of judge or judges before whom contempt is committed held, not sustainable - Further held, guilty of criminal contempt and exemplary punishment for conduct despite of being senior member - Power cannot be said to be not available to Supreme Court constitutionally and being appellate authority u/s. 38 of Advocates Act can impose any punishments mentioned in s. 35 of that Act - Order accordingly.
