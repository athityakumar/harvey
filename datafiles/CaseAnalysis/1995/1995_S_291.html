T.M.A.Pai Foundation And Ors.Etc v State Of Karnataka And Ors.Etc

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1356; (1995) 5 SCC 220; AIR 1995 SC 2431; JT 1995 (6) SC 33; 2002(3) SCALE 213; 1995(4) SCALE 665B; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Education - Karnataka Selection of Candidates for Admission to Engineering, Medical, Dental, Pharmacy and Nursing Courses Rules, 1993 - Fee structure - Instant directions are passed on Interim applications filed but confined to academic year 1995-96 only and limited to medical and dental colleges only.

Held, NRI quota is fixed at 15% for said academic year and in minority educational institutions, orders made on 18-8-1993 would continue to govern and is applicable to all States. Further, in Karnataka, restriction placed by 1995 amendment to Rules would not operate for academic year in question. The admission of Karnataka students against the payment seats is to be made in accordance with the scheme framed in Unnikrishnan case out of the students who appeared in entrance test already held. Further, with regard to fee structure for medical and dental colleges, fee payable by students allotted against free seats is fixed at Rs. 20,000 per annum and fee payable by payment student in respect of colleges with own hospitals, colleges which partly depend upon Govt. hospital and partly upon own facility and colleges which depend wholly upon Govt. hospitals is fixed at Rs. 75,000, 70,000 and Rs.65,000 per annum respectively. Further, for dental colleges, fee is fixed at Rs. 15,000 per annum for free seat and Rs.50,000 per annum for payment seat. Central Govt. should provide subvention of Rs.5,000 per annum for every student admitted in private medical college except for students admitted against NRI quota. Further, issue of uniform system of admissions to private colleges and for equal treatment of all professional colleges irrespective of fact whether they are minority institutions or not involves art. 30 of Constitution and the same could not be considered by instant bench but could be done by only larger bench. In the same way, issue of introduction of only two categories i.e, free seats and NRI seats also could be considered by only larger bench. Further, final adjudication of the various other issues are to be done by larger bench and also scheme framed in Unnikrishnan case also has to be reconsidered or modified by larger bench. Order accordingly.
