T. P. Razak Alias Nagappan Razak v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1588; (1995) Supp4 SCC 256; (1996) SCC (Cr) 57; 1995(2) SCALE 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 41, 42 and 50 - Whether conviction and sentence can be sustained for non-compliance with provisions of s. 50 of Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act? - It is obligatory on part of authorised officer to inform person to be searched, failure to inform person to be searched would amount to non-compliance of s. 50 which is mandatory, it would affect prosecution case and vitiate trial - Held, in view of non-compliance with mandatory provisions of s. 50 of Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, no reliance can be placed on alleged search of person of appellant and alleged recovery from his possession - Conviction u/s. 21 of NDPS Act is set aside - Appeal allowed.
