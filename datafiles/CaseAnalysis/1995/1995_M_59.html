Government of India and Others v Madras Rubber Factory Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 937; (1995) 4 SCC 349; 1995 (58) ECR 385; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 433; JT 1995 (4) SC 512; 1995 (8) RLT 517; 1995(3) SCALE 299; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 1143

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Permissible deductions - Principles governing the interpretation of s. 4 of the 1944 Act before and after the Amendment Act XXII of 1973 were enunciated in Union of India & Ors. v. Bombay Tyre International Ltd. & Ors., 1983 Indlaw SC 452 - SC took up individual cases for disposal on 1-5-1984 - Appeals and petitions were allowed under 'format orders' - Matters were remitted to assessing authorities with a direction to quantify and re-determine the permissible deductions in accordance with the law enunciated in the judgment of Bombay Tyre International (supra) - Other directions were also given with respect to the manner in which the assessing authorities were to proceed in the matter of determining the value - Assistant Collectors accordingly passed orders allowing certain claims for deductions and rejecting certain others - Assessing authorities forwarded the orders of assessment made by them along with the objections filed by the assessees in each case - Revenue too filed certain objections - Bench disposed of the appeals - Revenue filed review petitions contending that the said judgment of the Bench was not in accordance with the judgment in Bombay Tyre International (supra) - Whether the value of an article for the purposes of excise levy must be determined by reference exclusively to the manufacturing cost and the manufacturing profit of the manufacturer or should be represented by the entire wholesale charged by the manufacture - Held, that in cases where the goods were sold in the course of wholesale trade at place or places outside the place of removal, i.e., at depots, the expenses incurred in maintaining and running the said depots could not be deducted from the price - However, cost of transportation along with the cost of insurance on freight could be deducted - Further, the holding of the Bench did not, prevent the assessees from representing their case to the Govt. if they were so advised in this behalf and it was for the Govt. to consider the same in the light of all relevant circumstances - Appeal disposed of.
