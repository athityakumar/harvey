Kartik Malhar v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2272; (1996) SCC (Cr) 188; (1996) 1 SCC 614; 1996 CRLJ 889; JT 1995 (8) SC 425; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 134 - Murder - Single eyewitness - Testimony of - Evidentiary value - Whether conviction can be sustained on testimony of single eye-witness in a murder case? - Held, it is open to courts to record a conviction on basis of statement of a single witness provided evidence of that witness is reliable, unshaken and consistent with case of prosecution - Court will not then insist on corroboration by any other eyewitness particularly as incident might have occurred at a time or place when there was no possibility of any other eyewitness being present - Indeed, courts insist on quality and not on quantity of evidence - Appeal dismissed.
