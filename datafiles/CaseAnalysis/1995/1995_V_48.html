Murlidhar Dayandeo Kesekar v Vishwanath Pandu Barde and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 596; (1995) Supp2 SCC 549; AIR 1995 SCW 2224; 1995 (2) CCC 142; JT 1995 (3) SC 563; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; 1995(2) SCALE 672; 1995 (2) SCJ 553; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 260

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; International; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 46 - Transfer of Property Act, s. 53-A - Universal Declaration of Human Rights, 1948, arts. 1, 3 and 22 - Sale of land belonging to the tribes - Whether the first respondent being a tribal was unable to cultivate the lands and so lawfully entered into the agreement to sell the lands for valuable consideration, subject to permission of the Collector? - Economic empowerment to the poor, Dalits and Tribes, is an integral constitutional scheme of socio-economic democracy - State allots lands to the weaker sections for their economic empowerment - In case the assignee was disqualified or not available, on resumption of such land, the authorities arc enjoined to resume the property and assign to heir or other eligible among the Dalits and Tribes or weaker sections in terms of the policy - Even in respect of private sales of the lands belonging to tribes, statutes prohibit alienation without prior sanction of the competent authority - Held, the authorities rightly refused permission that the assigned land cannot be permitted to be sold or converted to non-agricultural use - Action is in consonance with the constitutional scheme in part IV of the Directive Principles - Thus agreement is void as opposed to public policy - Further the appellant's possession continues to be unlawful and he is not entitled to any improvement made on the lands - Collector is directed to resume the lands immediately and assign the same to the LRs of first respondent, if found eligible or to any other eligible tribal - Appeal dismissed.
