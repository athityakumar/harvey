Vrindavanibai Sambhaji Mane v Ramchandra Vithal Ganeshkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1766; (1995) 5 SCC 215; AIR 1995 SC 2086; AIR 1995 SCW 3226; 1995 (59) DLT 505; 1995 (2) HLR 517; JT 1995 (7) SC 363; 1995(4) SCALE 271; 1995 (2) UJ 360

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Land & Property - Execution of Will - Suspicious circumstances - Deceased-plaintiff, predecessor of respondent nos. 1 to 5, filed suit for declaration that deceased-plaintiff was owner of suit property - Appellant contested suit and claimed title to suit property by virtue of Will left by testatrix in her favour - On trial, Trial Court held that Will is not proved and decreed suit - On appeal, First Appellate Court held that Will was properly proved by appellant and allowed appeal - However, on second appeal before HC, Single Judge concluded that appellant had not dispelled suspicious circumstances surrounding execution of Will and allowed second appeal - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether HC is justified in re-assessing evidence in Second Appeal.

Held, First Appellate Authority examined question of non-disclosure of Will on two earlier occasions and had found that there was nothing suspicious in that regard. Further, there is nothing unnatural about contents of Will as appellant and her husband had throughout looked after testatrix and maintained good relations with her and so, if Will is made leaving properties to appellant, it was only natural. Further, respondent no. 6 who is sister of appellant and also who had good terms with testatrix had not challenged the Will. Furthermore, fact that testatrix made Will at age of fifty could not be considered as suspicious circumstance reflecting on genuineness of Will. Hence, HC was not right in re appraising evidence in Second Appeal and concluding that Will was not genuine. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside and judgment and order of First Appellate Court is restored. Appeal allowed.
