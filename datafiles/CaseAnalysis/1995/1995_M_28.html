National Thermal Powercorporation Limited v M/S Flowmore Private Ltd.And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1127; (1995) 4 SCC 515; AIR 1996 SC 445; AIR 1995 SCW 4306; 1995 (26) ALR 322; 1995 (2) ARBLR 184; 1995 (2) BC 221; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 97; 1995 (59) DLT 333; JT 1995 (5) SC 590; 1995(3) SCALE 545; 1995 (2) SCJ 263; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 29; 1995 (2) UJ 278

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Contract Act, 1872, s. 126 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41 - Encashment of Bank guarantee - Injunction against - Contract for supply of pumps - Supplier furnishing unconditional bank guarantees purchaser payable on demand - Dispute arising - Referring to arbitration - Purchaser not invoking bank guarantee during arbitration - Supplier keeping bank guarantees alive by renewal - Held, not makes conclusion that contractor had precluded himself from encashing bank guarantee during pendency of arbitration - Injunction against invocation of bank guarantees - Not proper - Bank is liable to pay as and when demand is made by beneficiary.
