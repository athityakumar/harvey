Indian Council For Enviro Legal Action and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2354; (2007) 15 SCC 633; JT 1995 (9) SC 427

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pollution by Industries

Summary: Administrative - Environment Wildlife & Animal - Practice & Procedure - Pollution by Industries - Compensation - Entitled to - Petitioner/farmers were not been adequately compensated for loss suffered by them on account of pollution by industries - Hence instant appeal - Held, taking note of fact that in ten villages total loss was to tune of Rs 28.34 lakhs, SC found that contribution from industrialists was to tune of Rs 7,49,936 towards ascertained compensation and out of this amount only sum of Rs 6,54,364 was disbursed to farmers - It appeared that Pollution Control Board had identified as many as twenty two industries responsible for pollution caused by discharge of their effluents into Nakkavagu - They were responsible to compensate farmers - It was duty of State to ensure that this amount was recovered from industries and paid to farmers - State was directed to deposit amount paid by industrialists in first instance with HC - Since there was sufficient delay in matter of compensating farmers SC would not brook further delay - Pollution Control Board had initiated action against defaulting twenty two industries but it was not able to deal with them effectively because these industries had approached HC and their petitions were pending - Order accordingly.
