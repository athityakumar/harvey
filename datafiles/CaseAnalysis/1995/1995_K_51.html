Mohan Lal v Kartar Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2141; (1995) Supp4 SCC 684; JT 1995 (7) SC 573; 1995 (2) RentLR 624; 1995(6) SCALE 27; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Pepsu Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1955, s. 43 - Restoration - Voluntary surrender - Jurisdiction - Appellant was in occupation of the suit land as a tenant and since appellant had not given the original plaintiff his share the Village Panchayat was approached - Compromise was arrived between them and appellant agreed to relinquish possession of land due to inability to pay the rent - Appellant disputed the surrender was not voluntary and claimed restoration of possession u/s. 43 of the Act - Plaintiff's appeal to Commissioner and revision application to Financial Commissioner rejected - W.P. dismissed and order passed that Sub-Divisional magistrate had no jurisdiction to pass an order of eviction u/s. 43 of the Act - Appellant appeal was allowed and plaintiff's suit was dismissed - LPA filed and order passed in favour of plaintiff - Appellant aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the Collector had the jurisdiction to decide of plaintiff was in wrongful or unauthorised possession of the suit land - Held, the Collector had been invested with the power to eject unlawful occupants u/s. 43(1)(b) of the Act - It was his jurisdiction to enquire and decide whether transfer of possession from the applicant to the opponent was invalid or not and the person against whom an application was made was entitled to the use and occupation of the land which he was alleged to be in wrongful or unauthorised possession - HC was not right in allowing the LPA and holding that the civil court had the jurisdiction to consider whether possession of the land by the respondent was lawful or not in view of the compromise and voluntary surrender and that the order passed by the Collector in that behalf was not binding on the Civil Court - Appeal allowed.
