Gen. Manager Tele-Communication and Another v Dr. Madan Mohan Pradhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1077; (1995) Supp4 SCC 268; 1995 (4) CCC 260; JT 1995 (8) SC 193; 1996 (1) RentLR 109; 1995(6) SCALE 387; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 16, 17(1) and 17(4) - Whether High Court justified quashing the notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 in view of declaration having been made after amendment of s. 6? - Supreme Court in the case of Satendra Prasad Jain and Ors. vs. State of U.P. and Ors. 1991 Indlaw SC 1030 had held that once possession has been taken validity of notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 cannot be gone into and s. 11A does not apply - Held, by operation of s. 17(1) even before award could be made, the Land Acquisition Officer is entitled to take possession of the land and he did so; by operation of s. 16, the right, title and interest in the land vested in the Govt. absolutely free from all encumbrances, hence validity of the notifications cannot be gone into - High Court was wholly unjustified in interfering with the acquisition and quashing the notification and declaration - Appeals allowed.
