Union of India and Another v S. S. Ranade

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1056; (1995) 4 SCC 462; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1033; 1995 (30) ATC 559; 1995 (59) DLT 47; 1995 (71) FLR 492; JT 1995 (5) SC 582; 1995 LabIC 2466; 1995 (2) LLN 292; 1995(3) SCALE 65; 1995 (2) SCJ 231; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 773; 1995 (3) SLJ 83; 1995 (2) SLR 718; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1499

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - Border Security Force Act, 1968, s. 2(f) - Border Security Force (Seniority, Promotion And Superannuation of Officers) Rules, 1978, r. 6 - Border Security Force Rules, 1969, r. 9 - Whether a Commandant (Selection Grade) can be considered as holding a 'rank' higher than that of a Commandant for purposes of r. 9 of Border Security Force (Seniority, Promotion and Superannuation of Officers) Rules, 1978? - Held, pay scale may be a good yardstick for measuring rank - But this has no application to selection grade posts - Because creation of a selection grade in same post stands on a very different footing - By its very nature a selection grade provides a higher pay or a higher pay scale in same post - Beneficiary of a selection grade does not thereby occupy a post which is higher in rank than post earlier occupied by him - As pointed out by appellants that if a selection grade is considered as a different post of a higher rank, and a higher age of retirement is prescribed for a selection grade post, that would defeat very purpose of creating such posts - A higher retirement age would add to stagnation at that level and block promotions for others - Appeal allowed.
