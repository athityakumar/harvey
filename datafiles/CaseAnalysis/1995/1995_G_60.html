Gulab Chand v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 957; (1995) SCC (Cr) 552; (1995) 3 SCC 574; AIR 1995 SC 1598; AIR 1995 SCW 2504; 1995 (2) ALT(SC) 159; 1995 (2) CCR 96; 1995 (2) Crimes 188; JT 1995 (5) SC 373; 1995(2) SCALE 623; 1995 (3) SCJ 35; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 27; 1995 (2) UJ 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114, Illustation (a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 394 - Presumption against accused - Murder and robbery - Selling of some ornaments of deceased by accused on very next day of murder - Recovery of stolen property after 3-4 days from accused - No satisfactory explanation by accused - Held, presumption under Evidence Act suggests that accused not only committed murder but committed robbery of ornaments.
