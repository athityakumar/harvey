Jackaran Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2291; (1997) SCC (Cr) 651; AIR 1995 SC 2345; AIR 1995 SCW 3485; 1995 CRLJ 3992

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984 - (A) IPC - Murder by gun shot fired by muffled assailants as per FIR - But in trial, informant stated that he identified accused - Prosecution failing to establish nexus between weapon and accused - (B) Credibility of disclosure statement in absence of signature or thumb expression and non examination of panch witnesses to testify its authenticity - Held, accused entitled to benefit of doubt - Further held, it render such statement unreliable - Appeal allowed.
