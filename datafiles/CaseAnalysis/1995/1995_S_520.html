State of Maharashtra and Another v Sanjay Thakre and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1677; (1995) Supp2 SCC 407; (1995) SCC (L&S) 867; 1995 (30) ATC 132; 1995 (71) FLR 744; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 692; 1995 (2) LLN 659; 1995(2) SCALE 312; 1995 (3) SCJ 101; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 542; 1996 (1) SLJ 62; 1995 (2) SLR 395

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Dispute of inter se seniority between direct recruits and promotees relating to incumbents belonging to cadre of Assistant Conservator of Forests in Maharashtra Forests Service, Class II - Tribunal directed respondents before it to determine inter se seniority by following quota rule - Present appeal - Whether on facts it could be said that quota rule had broken down, which stand of appellants did not find favour with Tribunal and whether could it be said that promotions in present case were not fortuitous? - Held, present was not a case about which it could be said that quota rule had broken down - If quota rule had not broken down, appointment of promotees has to be regarded as in violation of rules - Material placed on record of Tribunal would not permit Court to accept contention on behalf of promotees that their promotions were not fortuitous - There is no force in these appeals - Appeals dismissed.
