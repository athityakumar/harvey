Amrutanjan Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 980; (1996) 9 SCC 413; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Tariff heading 3003.30 - Classification - Whether Amrutanjan Pain Balm Ayurvedic is an ayurvedic product? - Main ingredients were Menthol IF, Camphor IF, Turpentine IP and Methyl Salicylate IF, which were of a synthetic nature - Held, Tribunal would appear to have overlooked fact that same article can have a use both in Ayurveda and in western sciences and be known by different names - Letters IP after article concerned only demonstrates that it is of pharmaceutical quality, as it ought to be, if it is to be used in a medicinal preparation - Tribunal was in error in considering that articles aforestated were synthetic in nature - Balm is an Ayurvedic medicine - Appeal allowed.
