Baby Samuel v Tukaram Laxman Sable and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 519; (1995) Supp4 SCC 215; JT 1995 (6) SC 78; 1995(4) SCALE 684; 1995 (2) UJ 577

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Summary: Constitution - Election - Maharashtra Municipal Corporation Act 1965 - Grounds for removal - Appellant was elected as a Councillor of the Municipal Council - Contesting respondent was also elected as a councillor - On account of a vacancy occurring in the office of the President of Municipality, an election was held to that office wherein the appellant was elected as the President - Prior to becoming the President, appellant was the Chairman of a Committee in-charge of awarding contracts - Notice was issued to the appellant by the Collector calling upon him to show cause why he should not be removed from the officer of the Councillor u/s. 44 of the 1965 Act - Appellant's post as member of the Municipal Council was vacated u/ss. 44(1) and 44(3) of the 1965 Act - Collector convened a meeting to elect the President of the Municipality, whereas the respondent was elected as the President - In exercise of the powers conferred by s. 44(1) of the 1965 Act, Govt. allowed the appeal of the appellant and disqualified the respondent as member of the Municipal Council - Respondent contended that before allowing the appeal he should have been heard and that not doing so vitiated the order - Appellant filed a W.P. for a direction to the authorities to reinstate him in the office of the President of the Municipality as a consequence of the Govt.'s order - Whether the appellant was liable to be reinstated for the post from which he was dismissed - Held, the appellant was removed from the office of the Councillor and at the same time he lost the office of the President of the Municipality - Once the order of removal/disqualification was set aside by the Govt., the appellant was entitled to be put back in the same position which he was in before he was removed - Not only should he be restored to the Councillorship but also to the office of the President - Appeal allowed.
