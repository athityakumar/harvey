Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Degree Of Care

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110-A - (A) Whether amount of Rs.8,57, 352 awarded by High Court to be compensation is justified ? - Held, no - Claimant has been paralysed and has to undergo a lot of physical and mental suffering - (B) What considerations have to be kept in mind while determining amount of compensation ? - Further held, while fixing an amount of compensation payable to a victim of an accident, damages have to be assessed separately as pecuniary damages and special damages - Pecuniary damages are those which are capable of being calculated in terms of money, whereas non-pecuniary damages are those which are incapable of being assessed by arithmetical calculations - Pecuniary damages may include medical attendance, loss of earning of profit up to date of trial, other material loss - Non-pecuniary damages may include damages for mental and physical shock, pain and suffering, damages to compensate for loss of amenities of life, damages for loss of expectation of life, inconvenience, hardship, discomfort, disappointment, frustration and mental stress in life - Object is to compensate such injury 'so far as money can compensate' because it is impossible to equate money with human sufferings - Appeals allowed.
