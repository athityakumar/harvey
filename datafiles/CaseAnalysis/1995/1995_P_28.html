Supreme Court of India

04 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 126; (1995) 3 SCC 583; AIR 1995 SC 1428; 1995 (2) AnWR 10; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 121; 1995 (2) CompLJ 393; 1995 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; 1995 (2) CPR(NC) 11; 1995 (3) CTJ 289; JT 1995 (3) SC 433; 1995(2) SCALE 626; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 174; 1995 (2) UJ 253

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(d) - Whether respondent is a consumer ? - Whether purpose for which a person has bought goods is a 'commercial purpose' within meaning of definition of expression 'consumer' in s. 2(1)(d) of Act ? - Held, it is always a question of fact to be decided in facts and circumstances of each case - A person who buys goods and use them himself, exclusively for purpose of earning his livelihood, by means of self-employment is within definition of expression 'consumer' - Appeal dismissed.
