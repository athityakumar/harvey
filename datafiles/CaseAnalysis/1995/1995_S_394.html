Kummari Veeraiah and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1585; (1995) 4 SCC 136; 1995 (1) RentLR 606; 1995(2) SCALE 278; 1995 (3) SCJ 235; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 379; 1995 (1) UJ 737

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Whether error of law committed in not relying upon sale deeds while determining compensation for land acquired? - In case it is found that documents are genuine and acquired land possessed of same or similar special advantageous features, then reasonable price is required to be determined by giving suitable deduction depending upon extent of land covered by sale transaction and acquired land - When such evidence is adduced, it is required to be considered on touchstone of potentiality of land, human conduct and probabilities - Held, since such evidence was not brought on record, evidence of sale transactions render little assistance to determine market value of acquired lands - Petition dismissed.
