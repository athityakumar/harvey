Salamat Ali and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2348; (1997) SCC (Cr) 842; AIR 1995 SC 1863

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 and 304-B/34 - Appreciation of Evidence - Whether there is clear and cogent evidence to involve parents in demands of dowry made or were they otherwise responsible for inflicting any cruelty on deceased? - Nature of demand indicates proximity to offence of dowry demand - Held, allegations against appellants are general in nature attributed to husband's family - They have been identified because they were members of his family - It is not clear on record as besides them who else were members of family - In absence of clear and pointed evidence it would be unsafe to maintain conviction of parents, on vague allegations that demand of dowry was made by husband's family members - Appellants acquitted - Appeal allowed.
