State Of M.P. v Mahalaxmi Fabric Mills Limited And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 323; (1995) Supp1 SCC 642; AIR 1995 SC 2213; JT 1995 (3) SC 93; 1995(1) SCALE 758; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Mines and Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 9(3) - Constitution, art. 245, sch. 7, List 1, Entry 54 - (A) Whether s. 9(3) is within legislative competence of Parliament - (B) Whether Notification 01-08-1981 issued by Central Govt. u/s. 9(3) ultra vires, illegal and inoperative in law - Held, (A) s. 9(3) is within competence of Parliament and cannot be said to suffering from excessive delegation of legislative power - There are sufficient guidelines from the Act to enable Central Govt. to exercise its delegated legislative function in just and proper manner keeping in view uniform development of minerals throughout country - (B) entire purpose of this exercise is for a legitimate relevant purpose for developing minerals and enabling State which are owners thereof to properly manage mining leases so that minerals can develop on uniform pattern throughout country, it must be held that the impugned Notification cannot be said to be ultra vires - Order accordingly.
