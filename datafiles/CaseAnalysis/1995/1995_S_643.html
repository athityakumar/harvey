Dr Bal Krishna Agarwal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1740; (1995) SCC (L&S) 356; (1995) 1 SCC 614; AIR 1995 SCW 800; 1995 ALJ 454; 1995 (29) ATC 163; 1995 (2) CLR 6; 1995 (70) FLR 1041; JT 1995 (1) SC 471; 1995 LabIC 1396; 1995 (1) LLN 427; 1995(1) SCALE 116; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 148; 1995 (2) SLJ 34; 1995 (1) SLR 686; 1995 (1) UJ 444; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 1055

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, ss. 2, 13, 32 and 68 - (A) Whether High Court was in error in dismissing writ petition, on ground of availability of an alternative remedy u/s. 68 and it had been admitted and was pending in HC for past more than five years? - Held, HC was not right in dismissing writ petition - Question that is raised involves a pure question of law - HC should not have non-suited appellant on ground of availability of an alternative remedy - (B) Whether seniority should be regulated by provisions contained in Statues as they existed on date of appointment or amendments which were made in Statues by Notification D/- 21-02-1985? - Held, promotion to grade of Professor under Personal Promotion Scheme could, not be made prior to 21-02-1985 and it has to be treated to have been made with effect from 21-02-1985 - As per provisions contained in ss. 31-A and 2(14) of Act there is no escape from conclusion that promotion under Personal Promotion Scheme till necessary provisions prescribing length of service and qualifications for such promotion were made in Statutes and since this was done by Notification D/- 21-02-1985, promotion under Personal Promotion Scheme could not be made prior to 21-02-1985 - Promotion of Respondents 4 and 5 to grade of Professor under Personal Promotion Scheme could, therefore, not be made prior to 21-02-1985 - Appellant should have been treated as senior to Respondents 4 and 5 in cadre of Professor in Physics and Executive Council was not justified in placing him junior to said respondents - Appeal allowed.
