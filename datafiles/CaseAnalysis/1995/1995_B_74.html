Bharwad Jakshibhai Nagjibhai and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 318; (1995) 5 SCC 602; (1995) SCC (Cr) 993; AIR 1995 SC 2505; 1995 (3) Crimes 627; 1995 CRLJ 4176; JT 1995 (6) SC 275; 1995(4) SCALE 791; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 23

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 378 - IPC,1860, ss. 149 and 326 - (A) Powers of appellate Court in appeal against acquittal when High Court setting aside acquittal by observing that basic approach of trial Court was erroneous - (B) Unlawful assembly of 50-60 persons - Common object of mob - Medical evidence that injuriesby hard and blunt substance - Accused proved to have used blunt edge - Conviction of accused u/s. 326 r/w s.149 - Held, justified - Held, shared common object to cause grievous hurt and not to commit murder and conviction of accused upheld - Appeal dismissed.
