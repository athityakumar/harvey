Jayalakshmi Traders v Government of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1995

Case Analysis

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 255; 1997 (105) STC 337

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Intellectual Property; Sales Tax

Keywords: Writ appeals and petitions dismissed.

Summary: Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 12A - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, Entry 54, List II, Entry 92-A of List I - Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1979 - Income-Tax Act, 1961 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, r. 18C - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules - Whether provisions contained in s. 12-A of Act, 1959 are unconstitutional and r. 18-C of Rules, 1959 is ultra vires of s. 12-A? - Held, r. 18-C is intended to provide guidelines to assessing authority to determine real consensual price, not to determine prevailing market price - Prevailing market price is only taken as one of factors for determining real consensual value of sale or purchase of goods - Hence, r. 18-C of rules is not ultra vires provisions contained in s. 12-A of Act - Further held, s. 12-A of Act, r. 18-C of rules are valid, are intended only to determine real consensual value of goods sold or purchased as entered in accounts submitted in returns, have provided a machinery, also guideline to assessing authority to satisfy itself as to whether returns submitted by assessee are bona fide, are not intended to evade payment of tax - Appeals dismissed.
