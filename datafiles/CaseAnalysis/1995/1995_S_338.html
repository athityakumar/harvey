Balwinder Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 798; (1995) Supp4 SCC 259; (1996) SCC (Cr) 59; AIR 1996 SC 607; AIR 1995 SCW 4555; 1996 CRLJ 883; JT 1995 (8) SC 81; 1995(6) SCALE 261; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 24 - Failure of prosecution to establish any of circumstantial evidence to prove charge against father for murder of daughters - Issues arise : (A) Validity of conviction - (B) Extrajudicial confession made to relative who lodged complaint with police after three days despite of being in touch with police being a social worker - Held, conviction not sustainable - Confession held, was surrounded by suspicious circumstances - Appeal allowed.
