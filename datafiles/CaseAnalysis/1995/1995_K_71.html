Tej Kaur and Another v Kirpal Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 531; (1995) 5 SCC 119; AIR 1995 SC 1681; 1995 (2) AnWR 4; 1995 (2) CCC 354; 1995 (59) DLT 18; JT 1995 (5) SC 201; 1995 (2) MLJ 81; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 81; 1995(3) SCALE 596; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 98 - Difference of opinion - Confirmation of decree - (A) Whether in absence of majority opinion of DB, confirmation of decree of civil court is valid in law? - Held, when no majority varying or reversing decree appealed from, such decree shall be confirmed - (B) Whether this court can examine case on merits in difference of opinion between Judges? - Further held, court cannot enlarge scope of controversy by itself examining correctness of finding of fact and decide which view of two is correct - (C) Whether Letters Patent power available? - Further held, Letters Patent Court not confined to hearing of appeal by third judge on question of law only on which judges hearing appeal had differed - Such difference could be on question of fact as well - Letters Patent power not available in this case - Relied on Harakh Narain v. Babban, (AIR 1933 Allahabad) - On question of fact in difference of opinion and absence of majority opinion view expressed by court below needs to be given primacy and confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
