P.K. Ghosh, I.A.S. And Ant. v J.G. Rajput

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 804; (1995) 6 SCC 744; AIR 1996 SC 513; 1995 (4) CCC 97; 1996 (1) CLT 51; JT 1995 (8) SC 214; 1995(6) SCALE 257B; 1996 (1) SLR 95; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Respondent's counsel was elevated to Judge in High Court, and same judge heard contempt proceeding between respondent and appellant, opining in favour of respondent - Whether it is appropriate for Judge to rescue himself from hearing petition? - Held, credibility in functioning of justice delivery system and reasonable perception of affected parties are relevant considerations to ensure continuance of public confidence in credibility and impartiality of judiciary, and this is necessary not only for doing justice but also for ensuring that justice is seen to be done - Judge should have rescued himself from hearing this contempt petition, particularly when a specific objection to this effect was taken by appellants in view of respondent's case in contempt petition, wherein impugned order came to be made in his favour - Impugned order is vitiated for this reason alone - Appeal allowed.
