Commissioner of Income Tax, Meerut and Others v Virmani Industries Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1394; (1995) 6 SCC 466; 1995 (129) CTR 189; [1995] 216 ITR 607; JT 1995 (7) SC 322; 1995(5) SCALE 718; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 275; [1995] 83 TAXMAN 343

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Depreciation - Set off - For the A/Y 1956-57, depreciation in the business of the respondent assessee was more than the profits and gains - Respondent assessee claimed that the unabsorbed depreciation to be set off against the profits by highlighting the use of old machinery in new business undertaken by him after a gap of ten years - ITO and AAC rejected assessee's claim - Appellate Tribunal upheld assessee's view - Reference made to HC was in favour of assessee - Revenue aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the unabsorbed depreciation could be allowed to be set off against the profits of the new business - Held, assessee had not claimed for setting off the unabsorbed depreciation against the property income for A/Y 1956-57 but for A/Y 1965-66 when he had commenced another business - If any part of the depreciation allowance remained unabsorbed after being set off against the total income chargeable to tax, it could be carried forward under to the following year and set off against the year's income and so on for succeeding years - If after setting off the unabsorbed depreciation allowance relating to the A/Y 1956-57 against the income for the following A/Ys, any depreciation allowance still remained unabsorbed it could have been set off against the income for the accounting period relevant to the A/Y 1965-66 - Appeal allowed.
