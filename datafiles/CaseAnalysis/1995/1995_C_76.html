Formica India Division, Bombay and another v Collector of Central Excise and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 975; (1995) Supp3 SCC 552; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 511; 1995 (8) RLT 273

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Act, 1934

Summary: Excise - Demand of Duty - Indian Tariff Act, 1934, s. 2-A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 56A - Whether the intermediary product, namely, treated paper and treated cotton fabric is liable to duty under Items 17(2) and 19(III) respectively of the Central excise tariff as in operation at the relevant date? - Appellants contested the correctness of the classification and had, therefore, not paid the duty on the intermediary product - When it was found that they were liable to pay duty on the intermediary product and had not paid the same, but had paid the duty on the end product, they could not ordinarily have complied with the requirements of r. 56 - Appellants should be permitted to avail of the benefit of the notification by complying at this stage with r. 56-A to the satisfaction of the Department - Held, matter remitted to the Collector of Central Excise with a direction to permit the appellants to comply with the requirements of r. 56-A of the Central Excise Rule and claim set-off of the duty payable on the intermediary product by satisfying the Collector that the same was used in the manufacture of the end product on which full duty had already been paid - Appeals allowed.
