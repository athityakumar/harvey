Deva Ram And Another v Ishwar Chand And Another

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1320; (1995) 6 SCC 733; AIR 1996 SC 378; 1996 (27) ALR 53; JT 1995 (7) SC 641; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 88; 1996 (87) RD 73; 1995(6) SCALE 18; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2, r. 2 - (A) Whether cause of action on basis of which previous suit was filed, is identical to cause of action on which subsequent suit giving rise to present appeal, was filed ? - Previous suit was filed for recovery of sale price of land in suit - Subsequent suit was brought by respondents for recovery of possession - Cause of action in subsequent suit was, therefore, entirely different - Held, District Judge as also High Court were correct in rejecting plea raised by appellants with regard to O. 2 , r. 2 of CPC, 1908 - (B) Whether defendant is in possession of suit land as tenant under plaintiff since Samvat 2005 as alleged ? - An issue 5 was framed on status of appellant as to whether they were tenants of land in suit under respondents but in subsequent suit this issue was not raised - Litigating parties and subject matter were same in both suits - Consequently, basic requirement for applicability of rule of res judicata is wanting and in absence of pleadings, in absence of issues and in absence of any finding, it is not open to appellants to invoke rule of res judicata on ground that in earlier suit it was found by Trial Court that appellants were tenants of land in dispute under respondents What they pleaded was that they were in possession since a long time namely from Samvat 2005 and had, therefore, acquired title by adverse possession - Further held, appellant were not tenants of land in suit under respondent since Samvat 2005 - Appeal partly allowed.
