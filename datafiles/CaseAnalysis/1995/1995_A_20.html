Abubakar Abdul Inamdar (Dead)By Lrs. And Others v Harun Abdul Inamdar And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1200; (1995) 5 SCC 612; AIR 1996 SC 112; 1995 (3) CCC 547; JT 1995 (7) SC 179; 1995(5) SCALE 87; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Bombay Merged Territories Miscellaneous Alienations Abolition Act, 1955, s. 4 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 1 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 64 and 65 - Succession to inam land on death of holder, a muslim as per mohammedan law - Lands assigned to his eldest son - (A) On abolition of inam he was regarded as watandar on regrant of properties to him - His brothers and sisters are entitled to laying claims to these lands as co-heirs - (B) Limitation to raised a plea of adverse possession not raised in pleadings - Held, entitled to claim their respective shares in accordance with law of Shariat - No amount of proof can substitute pleadings which are foundation of claim of a litigating party - Appeal dismissed.
