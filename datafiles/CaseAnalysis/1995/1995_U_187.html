Buta Singh (Dead) By Lrs v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1012; (1995) 5 SCC 284; AIR 1995 SC 1945; 1995 (2) RentLR 5; 1995(5) SCALE 392; 1995 (2) SCJ 278; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 148, 149, O. 41, r. 9 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 54 - Deficit Court fee - Payment of - Appeal in Land Acquisition proceedings - Whether a claimant can be allowed to pay deficit court fee upon a subsequent claim for enhanced compensation? - Appellant must have paid proper court fee on Memorandum of Appeal and should have claimed higher compensation - In an appeal u/s. 54 if amount is initially kept low and then depending upon mood of appellant, payment of deficit court fee is sought to be made, it cannot be permitted as such practice would not be conducive and proper for orderly conduct of litigation - As claimants have paid lesser court fee or restricted value of appeals it must be taken that their claims were restricted to amount for which court fee was paid - Adjudication should thus be confined to that amount - Applications for permission to pay deficit court fee dismissed - Order accordingly.
