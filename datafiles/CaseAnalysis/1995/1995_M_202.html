Dr. J.N. Banavalikar v Municipal Corporation Of Delhi And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1739; (1995) Supp4 SCC 89; AIR 1996 SC 326; 1996 (32) ATC 84; 1995 (2) CLR 81; JT 1995 (7) SC 105; 1996 LabIC 215; 1995(5) SCALE 509; 1996 (1) SLJ 128; 1995 (6) SLR 12; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 1; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1955

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution Of India,1950, arts. 226 and 14 - Arbitrariness in action - Public bodies - Doctor under control of Delhi Corporation - Relieved of additional charge - Action challenged by claiming that Medical Superintendent of hospital in question has to be a specialist in tuberculosis and chest diseases - Post of Medical Superintendent, not a promotional post - Held, no rule or administrative policy decision shown favouring claim - Plea is not maintainable - Therefore, selection of Medical Superintendent of hospitals remains a prerogative of corporation.
