Sher Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1322; (1995) 6 SCC 515; (1996) SCC (L&S) 42; AIR 1996 SCW 234; 1995 (31) ATC 746; JT 1995 (8) SC 323; 1996 (3) LLJ 365; 1995(6) SCALE 4; 1995 (5) SLR 654; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Government Policy, Education Norms

Summary: Service - Parity in pay - Entitled to - Whether employed in University of Delhi and its constituent colleges in different grades are entitled to parity in pay scales between professional library staff and teaching staff of University of Delhi and its colleges recognised and established since January, 1961? - Held, Teaching staff has to do some research work, deep study in their respective subjects and to make preparations for daily lectures in classrooms and other academic work while this is not so in case of library staff - Experience of library staff is totally different from one which is required for teaching staff - Working pattern of two sets of employees cannot be said to be identical so as to claim parity between library staff and teaching staff - If Govt. had taken a policy decision to grant parity again with effect from 01-04-1980 and not with retrospective effect from 01-07-1973 when it was disturbed there could be no legitimate grievance for same because Govt. has right to change its policy from time to time, according to administrative exigencies and demands of relevant time - There will be no justification for court to interfere with policy of Govt. merely on ground of change in policy - Courts will not dictate decision of statutory authority in exercise of its discretion and formulation of its policies - Court can only command statutory authority by a Writ of mandamus to perform its duty by exercising discretion according to law - There is no judicial or quasi-judicial duty or any obligation imposed on Govt. to equate library staff with teaching staff, on basis of which enforcement thereof could be claimed by appellants - In such a situation it cannot be said that Govt. did not act fairly or acted mala fide so as to call for any interference by this Court invoking power of judicial review - Appeal dismissed.
