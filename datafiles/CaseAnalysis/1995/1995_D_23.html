Superintendent Of Police (C.B.I.) v Deepak Chowdhary And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1154; (1995) 6 SCC 225; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1095; AIR 1996 SC 186; 1996 (1) CalLT(SC) 14; 1995 (4) CCR 98; 1996 CRLJ 405; 1995 (71) FLR 827; JT 1995 (6) SC 532; 1996 (1) LLJ 731; 1995 (2) LLN 1112; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 37; 1995(5) SCALE 226B; 1996 (1) SLJ 171; 1995 (5) SLR 236; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 818; 1995 (2) UJ 720; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1830

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act 1947, s. 6 - Constitution of India, art. 14 - Validity of sanction for prosecution was in question - Grant of Sanction is an administrative function - Opportunity of hearing to accused before according sanction need not be given - Order quashing sanction on ground that accused was exonerated of charge by disciplinary authority was not proper.
