T.M.A. Pai Foundation and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1291; (1995) 4 SCC 1; AIR 1995 SC 1938; AIR 1995 SCW 2179; 1995 CRLJ 3220; JT 1995 (4) SC 624; 1995(3) SCALE 387; 1995 (4) SLR 12; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 283; 1995 (2) UJ 552

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Reserved seats - Contempt of Court - State Govt. framed rules in which seats reserved for NRI's students was 15% - Increase of seats by respondents from 10% to 15% amounted to contempt of Court hence, the respondents were issued notice to show cause why proceedings of contempt of court should not be initiated against them - On production of documents demanded by Court a notice was issued to show cause why respondents should not be punished - Respondents contended that order of court for reservation of seats was confined to Minority institutions and made an apology to the court - Whether, respondents were liable for contempt of court - Held, the 'unconditional apology' tendered by respondents held them guilty of contempt of court and their conduct was faulty - Constitutional scheme laid that orders of this Court had to be obeyed implicitly and that orders of the Apex Court or any Court should not be trifled with - Appeal disposed of.
