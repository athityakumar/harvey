Sanjiv Datta, Deputy Secretary, Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, New Delhi and Others(In Re) v Na

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1029; (1995) 3 SCC 619; AIR 1995 SCW 2203; 1995 CRLJ 2910; JT 1995 (3) SC 538; 1995(2) SCALE 704; 1995 (2) SCJ 707; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 450; 1995 (2) UJ 786

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contempt of Court - Guilty of contempt - Criminal offence - Petitioner negotiated with respondent no. 1 for telecast of international matches - Course of negotiations lead to filing of W.P. by petitioner - HC directed to telecast the matches - Respondent no. 1 could not obtain permission which was necessary to telecast matches - Petitioner filed instant petition and claimed of contempt of proceedings of court - Notice was issued to respondent to show cause why court should not proceed against him - Whether, the respondents were liable for contempt of court - Held, the respondent no. 2 was guilty of the criminal contempt of the court and was convicted for the offence whereas explanation of respondent no. 3 was accepted and she was discharged of contempt issued to her - Respondent no. 2 was u/art. 129 of the Constitution sentenced to pay a fine and in default to undergo simple imprisonment for one week - The statements made by respondent no. 2 proved a malicious attempt to cast aspersions on and attribute motives to the Court and were not made in ignorance of their consequences nor were they innocent - As a responsible officer of the Govt. the respondent no. 2 ought to have known the serious implications of the statements made by him - Petition disposed of.
