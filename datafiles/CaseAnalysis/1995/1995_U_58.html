Ravi Paul And Ors. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1465; (1995) 3 SCC 300; (1995) SCC (L&S) 670; 1995 (29) ATC 558; JT 1995 (1) SC 579; 1995 LabIC 405; 1995(1) SCALE 261; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 419; 1995 (2) SLJ 205; 1995 (2) SLR 27; 1995 (1) UJ 571

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Border Security Force Act, 1968, s. 141 - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, rr. 105 and 113 - Border Security Force (BSF) (Assistant Commandants) Recruitment (Repeal) Rules, 1973 - Whether Emergency Commissioned Officers (ECOs) and Short Service Commissioned Officers (SSCOs) are entitled to count service rendered in army for fixation of their seniority in Border Security Force? - Held, Emergency Commissioned Officers had joined army in wake of Chinese aggression - In order to attract such officers it was considered necessary to give benefit of service in army for purpose of seniority in Border Security Force - Short Service Commissioned Officers had joined army as a career after emergency resulting from Chinese aggression was over - When they were absorbed to Border Security Force there was a change in policy of Govt. of India and benefit of service in army was not to be given - This condition was expressly mentioned in their letters of appointment - Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Service Commissioned Officers are officers belonging to two different categories and they cannot be regarded as persons similarly situate - Appeal dismissed.
