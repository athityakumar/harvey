Postmaster, Dargamitta H.P.O., Nellore v Raja Prameeelamma (Ms.)

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 897; (1998) 9 SCC 706; 1998 (4) AWC 534; JT 1998 (9) SC 100

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(g) - Respondent purchased six National Savings Certificates on 28-04-1987 - Rate of interest payable w.e.f 01-04-1987 was 11% notified by Govt. of India - Whether District Forum as well as State Commission right in coming to conclusion that Govt. of India/Post Office were bound to pay uncorrected maturity value of certificates? - Erroneous indication of a higher rate of interest and a higher maturity value cannot be deemed to be a deficiency in service with reference to law governing sale of National Savings Certificates - Contract of sale of National Savings Certificates was contrary to terms notified by Govt. of India and this was due to inadvertence of staff - Held, it does not become a contract binding Govt. of India being unlawful and void - As such this is not a case of deficiency in service either in terms of law or in terms of contract as defined in s. 2(1)(g) of Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Appeal allowed.
