Indore Development Authority v Sadhana Agarwal Smt., and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1685; (1995) 3 SCC 1; AIR 1995 SCW 1613; JT 1995 (3) SC 1; 1995(2) SCALE 135; 1995 (3) SCJ 94; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 555; 1995 (2) UJ 146

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973 - Allotment - Possession - Development Authority proposed for a scheme for purchase of flats in multi-storeyed buildings on hire-purchase basis - The plinth area and the deposit amount was specified - Balance payment was to be done in installments and the estimation period for completion of the scheme was 2 years - After a lapse of 2 years, the scheme was modified with respect to allotment of flats, time of completion, area specification as well as payment of installments - Flats were ready for allotment but possession could not be delivered since W.P. in respect of irregular allotment of flats was pending - Price of the flat communicated to the respondents at the time of allotment was highly inflated - Respondent file W.P. - HC allowed the writ and directed the Development to give possession of the flats at the estimated cost - Authority aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the order of the HC against the Development Authority was justifiable - Held, that in the proforma attached to the application for registration, the appellant authority had specified that the price mentioned by them was a probable and estimated cost, the definite price was to be intimated at the time of the allotment - Appellant authority had been informing the respondents and others who had got themselves registered, from time to time regarding the escalation in the cost of the flat - The Authority owed a duty to explain and satisfy the Court, for reasons of the hike in price of the flats - While exercising the writ jurisdiction, it was not needed for the HC to examine every detail of the construction with reference to the cost incurred and it should have been satisfied by the materials on record that the Authority did not act in an arbitrary and erratic manner - Respondents who were already in possession of the flats as per the HC order were directed to make payment of the balance amount along with simple interest at the rate of 6 per cent per annum from the date of obtaining possession of the flat till the balance amount was paid - Appeal allowed.
