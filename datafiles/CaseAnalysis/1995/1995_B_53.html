Indian Council Of Legal Aid And Advice, Etc. Etc v Bar Council Of India And Anr

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1439; (1995) 1 SCC 732; AIR 1995 SC 691; 1995 (1) ALT(SC) 44; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 387; 1995 (1) G.L.R. 848; JT 1995 (1) SC 423; 1995 (1) KLT 311; 1995(1) SCALE 181; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 304; 1995 (1) SLR 562; 1995 (1) UJ 344

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Right To Practise, Enrolment As An Advocate, Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 7, 24, 49(1)(ag) and 49(1)(ah) - Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Chap. 39 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Enrollment of Advocate - Bar Council of India Rules debarring person aged about 45 years of age - Issues are - Vires and constitutionality of Rule - Held, ultra vires - Is discriminatory, arbitrary and unreasonable - Petition allowed.
