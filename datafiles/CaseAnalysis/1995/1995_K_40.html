Mrs. Rupan Deol Bajaj and Another v Kanwar Pal Singh Gill and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1896; (1995) 6 SCC 194; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1059; AIR 1996 SC 309; AIR 1995 SCW 4100; 1995 (4) CCR 156; 1995 (4) Crimes 171; 1996 CRLJ 381; JT 1995 (7) SC 299; 1995(5) SCALE 670; 1995 (3) SCJ 518; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 210, 154 and 482 - Outraging modesty of a lady - (A) Whether quashing of F.I.R. by High Court proper? - Held, no - In absence of finding that allegations made in F.I.R. were absurd and inherently improbable - Quashing of F.I.R. would be illegal - (B) Whether overruling by court of objections raised by appellant in court in recommendation of final discharge by accused in final report was proper? - Reasons for overruling objections were not recorded by court and therefore order accepting police report is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
