State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Modi Distillery and another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Faizan Uddin, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1212; (1995) 5 SCC 753; JT 1995 (6) SC 523; 1995(5) SCALE 59; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 119

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Keywords: U.P. Excise Act, 1910, U.P. Excise Rules, 1910

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 47 - U.P. Excise Act, 1910 - Obscuration - Excise duty - Human consumption - Respondents were manufacturers of Indian made foreign liquor - State being competent, levied excise duty from the respondents - Writs filed by respondents - HC allowed the W.P.s and quashed orders demanding excise duty from them - State aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, judgments and orders dt. 9-3-1979 and 11-10-1979 passed by the HC, were justified - Held, that the power of the State to demand excise duty was limited and not a regulatory measure - Expressions 'alcoholic liquors for human consumption' and 'intoxicating liquor', meant that liquor, which was consumable, in the sense, that it was capable of being taken by human beings, as a beverage or drink - Industrial alcohol, i.e. ethyl alcohol, by itself was not only non-potable but also highly toxic and was used as raw material, in the production of that alcohol, which could be consumed by human beings in its raw form - State was not empowered to levy excise duty on the raw material or input that was in the process of being made into alcoholic liquor for human consumption - Obscuration was the process of adding of caramel to plain spirit and at that stage since it was not ready for human consumption, excise could not be levied - Wastage of liquor after distillation but before dilution and pipeline wastage of liquor during manufacturing but before dilution were not liable to be levied excise duty - Demand of excise duty on the wastage of Indian made foreign liquor, exported outside the state, was justified since it was the ultimate beverage ready for human consumption and so exigible for excise duty - Appeal partly allowed.
