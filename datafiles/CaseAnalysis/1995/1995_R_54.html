State Of M.P. And Anr. v Ram Krishna Balothia And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1660; (1995) 3 SCC 221; (1995) SCC (Cr) 439; AIR 1995 SC 1198; 1995 CRLJ 2076; JT 1995 (2) SC 310; 1995 (1) KLT 535; 1995 MPLJ 303; 1995(1) SCALE 658; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 897; 1995 (1) UJ 514

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Keywords: Forced Labour

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution - Criminal - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention Of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 3 and 18 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 438 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 21 - Offence under Act - Forms distinct class - Cannot be compared with other offences - Anticipatory bail - Not available in offence of atrocity on Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes - (A) Constitutional validity of s. 18 of Act - (B) Whether s.438 of CrPC is considered as essential ingredient of art. 21 - Held, (A) Not violative of arts. 14 and 21 - (B) Anticipatory Bail is statutory right and cannot be considered as essential ingredient of art. 21 - Appeal allowed.
