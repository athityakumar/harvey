Associated Power Co. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Income-Tax

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Faizan Uddin, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 874; (1996) 7 SCC 221; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 174; AIR 1996 SC 894; 1996 (130) CTR 393; [1996] 218 ITR 195; JT 1995 (9) SC 146; 1995(6) SCALE 702; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 721; [1996] 84 TAXMAN 355

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Clear Profit

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 36 and 257 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, Sch. 6, cl. 2(1) - Indian Trusts Act, 1882 - (A) Whether Co. in business of generation and distribution of electricity appropriating sum towards contingency Reserve Account as provided by Electricity (Supply) Act is allowable deduction in 1961 Act? - Held, amount credited to contingencies reserve is not diverted by reason of an overriding obligation or title and, in determining business profits of assessee, it must be taken into account - (B) Doctine of diversion - Further held, doctrine of diversion of income by reason of an overriding title is quite inapposite - Doctrine applies when income is diverted and never reaches in hands of person sought to be assessed - Monies appropriated into contingencies reserve reach electricity co. and are not diverted away from it - Order accordingly.
