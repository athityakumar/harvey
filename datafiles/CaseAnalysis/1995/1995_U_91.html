A. K. Kaul and Another v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1030; (1995) 4 SCC 73; (1995) SCC (L&S) 922; AIR 1995 SC 1403; 1995 (30) ATC 174; 1995 (2) CLR 257; JT 1995 (4) SC 1; 1995 LabIC 1778; 1995 (2) LLN 1; 1995(2) SCALE 755; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 469; 1995 (3) SLJ 1; 1995 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19, 19(2), 74(2) and 311 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 123 and 124 - Whether dismissal of Appellants from service could be held valid? - Held, in a case where material is of such a nature that it requires continued confidentiality in public interest it would be permissible for Court or Tribunal to look into same while permitting non-disclosure to other party to adjudication - Tribunal did not err in looking into record for purpose of determining whether satisfaction has been vitiated for any of reasons mentioned by appellants - The impugned orders for the dismissal of the appellants are not vitiated by mala fides or are based on wholly extraneous or irrelevant grounds - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
