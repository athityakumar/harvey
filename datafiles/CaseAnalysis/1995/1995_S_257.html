R.V. Bhupal Prasad v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1360; (1995) 5 SCC 698; AIR 1996 SC 140; 1995 (3) CCC 417; JT 1995 (6) SC 258; 1995 (2) RCJ 442; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 448; 1995 (2) RentLR 561; 1995(5) SCALE 41; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 658

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment; Rent Control

Keywords: Licences

Summary: Rent Control - Media & Entertainment - A.P. Cinemas (Regulation) Rules, 1970 - Licence granted - Challenged - Respondent No. 3 had demised her property to the appellant for a period of 20 years by lease deed and in furtherance thereof, the appellant has been running the exhibition of Cinematograph films in the said theatre - The lease, by efflux of 20 years, contracted thereunder expired on 31-12-1983 - When the appellant sought renewal of the licence granted, the landlady objected to the renewal - Licensing Authority and the Appellate authority had granted licence but in a writ petition filed by the landlady the Single Judge held that the appellant was not in lawful possession of the theatre and that, therefore, the grant of renewal was not justified in law - DB affirmed said decision - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, after the expiry of the lease, he is treated to be in unlawful possession, since he is required to pay damages for use and occupation at the rate of Rs.100/- per day till he is evicted in due course of law. Thereby it is clear that respondent no. 3 possession was not treated to be juridical possession was not treated to be juridical possession but only unlawful. Respondent No. 3 is required to pay damages for use and occupation. Appeals dismissed.
