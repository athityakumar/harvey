Collector Of Central Excise, Shillong v Wood Craft Products Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 75; (1995) 3 SCC 454; 1995 (57) ECR 417; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 23; JT 1995 (3) SC 207; 1995 (7) RLT 661; 1995(2) SCALE 364; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 797

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, heading 44.08 and 44.10 - Classification - 'Block boards' - Whether 'block boards' are classifiable under heading 4408.90 of CETA, 1985 as claimed by the revenue or under heading 4410.90 of CETA, 1985 as claimed by manufacturers of block boards? - Held, any dispute relating to tariff classification must as far as possible be resolved with reference to nomenclature indicated by HSN unless there be express different intention indicated by CETA, 1985 - Expression 'similar laminated wood' in heading No. 44.08 as it stood from the beginning must be construed to include within it block boards of all kinds so that the amendment in Chapter Note 5 w.e.f. 19-3-1990 and thereafter w.e.f. 1-3-1992 merely clarified and made explicit that which was implicit in the heading throughout - Hence, impugned product classifiable under 4408.90 of CETA, 1985 - Revenue's appeal allowed.
