S.P. Goel v Collector of Stamps, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1107; (1996) 1 SCC 573; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 154; AIR 1996 SC 839; 1996 (1) AnWR 36; 1996 (1) CCC 17; 1996 (1) CPJ 11; 1996 (1) CPJ(Sc) 11; 1995 (3) CPR(NC) 684; 1996 (4) CTJ 177; JT 1995 (9) SC 545; 1995(7) SCALE 174; 1996 (1) SCJ 259; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 340; 1996 (1) Supreme 68

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Registration Act, 1908 - Registration of document - Deficiency of service - Petitioner executed a will in favour of his wife - When the will was presented for registration before Sub-Registrar he stated that the document was conveyance deed which was not stamped and not a will - Petitioner filed a complaint in District Forum against the respondent, for non registration of document even after of payment of fees - Petitioner was not satisfied with compensation ordered by District Forum hence, made an appeal to State Commission who enhanced the compensation - On an revision filed by respondent it was held by the National Forum that District Forum and State Commission had no jurisdiction to adjudicate the claim since petitioner was not consumer - Petitioner filed a W.P. against the order of National Forum and claimed deficiency of service on part of respondent - Whether, there was any deficiency of service on part of respondent - Held, the petitioner or State or District Forum had not recorded refusal of respondent to register document was malicious hence, the matter could not be investigated further - According to s. 86 of 1908 Act, no registering officer shall be liable to any suit, claim or demand by reason of anything in good faith done or refused in his official capacity - According to s. 2(1)(g) of 1986 Act, 'deficiency' means any fault, imperfection, shortcoming or inadequacy in the quality, nature and manner of performance which is required to be maintained by or under any law for the time being in force or has been undertaken to be performed by a person in pursuance of a contract or otherwise in relation to any service - Petition dismissed.
