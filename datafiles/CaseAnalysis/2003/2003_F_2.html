Haryana Tourism Corporation Limited v Fakir Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 845; (2003) SCC (L&S) 36; (2003) 8 SCC 248; AIR 2003 SC 4465; 2003 (99) FLR 821; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 424; 2004 (1) LLJ 195; 2004 (137) PLR 741; 2003(8) SCALE 582; 2003 (6) SLR 439; 2003 (7) SLT 194; 2003 (7) Supreme 278; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 160

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Retrenchment - Appellant-corporation was engaged in the tourism-related activities, started various fast food centres and tourist complexes in different parts of the State and utilised the services of these respondents, who worked on daily wage basis to meet the day-to-day requirements of work - Services of the respondents terminated as the centres were closed due to losses incurred - Labour Court directed reinstatement with 25 per cent back wages - Held, the direction to reinstate them would not be a just and equitable solution at this distance of time - Further held, taking into account the amount of daily wages that were being paid by them, the appellant-corporation can be directed to pay a sum of Rs. 70,000/- to each of these respondents by way of compensation in lieu of reinstatement.
