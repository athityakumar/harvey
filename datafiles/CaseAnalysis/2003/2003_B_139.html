Manorey @ Manohar v Board of Revenue (U.P.) and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 315; (2003) 5 SCC 521; AIR 2003 SC 4102; 2003 (51) ALR 341; 2003 (5) AWC 4441; JT 2003 (3) SC 538; 2003 (94) RD 538; 2003(3) SCALE 584; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 274; 2003 (4) SLT 242; 2003 (3) Supreme 237; 2003 (2) UC 988

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - S. 122B(4F) - maintainability of an application seeking recognition of right under - Held, when the last part of sub-Section (4F) of Section 122B unequivocally provides for the admission of the person satisfying the requisite criteria laid down in the provision as Bhumidhar with non-transferable rights under Section 195, full effect must be given to it. When, once the claim of the applicant is accepted, it is the bounden duty of the concerned revenue authorities to make necessary entries in revenue records to give effect to the statutory mandate. - The lack of specific provision for making an application under the Act is no ground to dismiss the application as not maintainable. The revenue records should naturally fall in line with the rights statutorily recognized.
