Geetu Sapra and Others v B.L. Kapoor Memorial Hospital and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1255; (2005) 10 SCC 549; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1621; 2003 CCC 708; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 207

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Carriers & Transportation - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Accident - Medical negligence - Appellants claim before National Commission for damages - Negligence of hospital and doctors - Dismissed on ground of jurisdiction - Whether National Commission correct in dismissing claim of appellants merely on ground that in view of judgment of SC in Chairman, Thiruvalluvar Transport Corporation v. Consumer Protection Council, 1995 Indlaw SC 1728 they had no jurisdiction? - Held, this judgment has no relevance at all - Claim is based on alleged negligence of hospital and doctors - That is entirely a different cause of action - In respect of such a claim, National Commission would have jurisdiction and it will be open to National Commission whilst considering merits to decide whether appellants can maintain an action under Motor Vehicles Act on basis that death resulted from accident as well as a claim for damages for alleged negligence of hospital and doctors - Impugned order set aside - Matter is remit back to National Commission - Appeal disposed of.
