Collector of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Orient Fabrics Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1054; (2004) 1 SCC 597; AIR 2004 SC 956; 2003 (90) ECC 745; 2003 (111) ECR 769; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 545; JT 2003 (10) SC 282; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 290; 2003(10) SCALE 374; 2003 (7) SLT 416; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 243; 2003 (8) Supreme 515

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Central Act, 1956, Mysore Act, 1957, Bombay Act, 1953, Bombay Act, 1959

Summary: Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957 - Additional duty - Penalty - Whether it is permissible to resort to penalty proceedings or forfeiture of goods for non-payment of additional duty - Held, the rule, if applied, would clearly show the amendment was brought with a view to remedy the defect contained in the unamended provisions of s. 3(3) of the Act. Offences having regard to the provisions contained in art. 20, Constitution of India cannot be given a retrospective effect. Section 3(3) of the Act as amended cannot be said to have any application at all - Further held, the confiscation proceedings taken against the respondents and the penalty imposed upon them were totally without the authority of law and were rightly set aside by the Tribunal.
