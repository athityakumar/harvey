State of Madhya Pradesh v Ghanshyam Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 716; (2003) 8 SCC 13; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1935; AIR 2003 SC 3191; 2003 (2) AllCriC 974; 2003 (4) CCR 26; 2003 (4) Crimes 6; 2004 (1) CRJ 100; 2003 CRLJ 4339; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 129; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 564; 2003(7) SCALE 387; 2003 (5) SLT 419; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 618; 2003 (6) Supreme 649

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 148, 149, 304, 307, 323 - Correctness of judgment rendered by Division Bench of Madhya Pradesh High Court, Gwalior Bench - Long pendency of matter - Held, the High Court has rightly held on the evidence on record that the offence committed by the accused is relatable to s. 304, Part-I and not s. 302 IPC. Taking into account the all relevant aspects of this case, even on a liberal approach, custodial sentence of 6 years would serve the ends of justice - Further held, it cannot be laid down as a rule of universal application that long passage of time in all cases would justify minimal sentence. Long pendency of a matter by itself could not justify lesser sentence.
