The Government Of N.C.T. Of Delhi v Jaspal Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 599; (2003) 10 SCC 586; 2003 (3) CCR 150; 2003 (3) Crimes 335; 2003 (6) CRJ 104; 2003 (3) JCC 1296; JT 2003 (7) SC 302; 2003(6) SCALE 181; 2003 (4) SLT 900; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 335; 2003 (5) Supreme 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Punjab Excise Act

Summary: Official Secrets Act, 1923 - ss. 3, 3(1)(c), 3(2), 5, 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 120B - Possession of secret documents - Held, when solid proof was available as to possession of a restricted document prohibited for the general use of others, no further proof is required. The mode of consideration and method of proof in a case cannot be on the lines of a crime under the provisions of IPC inasmuch as ss. 3(2) and 4 of the Act engrafts the statutory presumptions to be drawn from the facts and that this would make all the difference in the nature of consideration required in respect of offences committed under the Official Secrets Act, 1923 and the criminal conspiracies relating to such offences, be it punishable under s. 120-B, IPC - Further held, the verdict of acquittal recorded by the High Court by way of reversal, suffers patent error of law and perversity of approach, and consequently require to be set aside.
