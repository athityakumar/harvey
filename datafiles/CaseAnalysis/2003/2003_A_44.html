Hardie Trading Limited and Another v Addisons Paint and Chemicals Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 756; (2003) 11 SCC 92; AIR 2003 SC 3377; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 444; 2003 (27) PTC 241; 2003 (3) RAJ 280; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 691; 2003(7) SCALE 451; 2003 (1) SCW 4788; 2003 (5) SLT 616; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 686; 2003 (7) Supreme 206

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Registered User

Summary: [A.] Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999, ss. 102, 12, 18, 2(2)(b), 44, 44(3), 46, 46 (1)(b), 46(1), 46(1)(a), 46(3), 47, 48(2), 56, 69 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, s. 28 - Trademark - 'Spartan' and 'Spartan Velox' - Original trademarks as well as their modified forms have been in use by Hardie, who were registered trademark owners - Trademarks registered in India in 1940 - Collaboration agreement entered into between Hardie and Addisons for a period of 20 years, whereby Addisons would be registered user of trademarks during period of agreement - Hardie agreed to assign its rights in trademarks to Addisons for a sum payable in pound sterling, which consideration was not paid - Addisons informed Hardie that it would discontinue use of Hardie's trade marks - Addisons obtained registration of standing warrior device - Hardie appointed Hansa as registered user of Hardie's trademarks in India - Addisons applied for rectification of Register of Trademarks by deleting Hardie's trademarks - Addisons filed a suit for permanent injunction restraining Hansa from selling paints in containers which were identical to or deceptively similar with Hardie's trademarks or device which Addisons claimed had been used by it - Held, not only was no positive proof of an intention to use device or trademarks adduced by Addisons but evidence shows a conscious abandonment of device in 1971 by issuance of public advertisements - On other hand, taking into consideration continued user of mark since 1979 by Hansa, it should have been assumed that device had over period of so many years become distinctive of Hansa's product and therefore to allow registration of device in Addisons name might deceive public - Further held, appeals must be allowed on short ground that Hardie's trademarks could not have been removed from Register and as long as registration of marks continue in name of Hardie, application for registration of same marks in absence of any plea of bona fide concurrent user u/s. 12 would not arise.
