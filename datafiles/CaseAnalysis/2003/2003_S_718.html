Kaliyaperumal and Another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 665; (2004) 9 SCC 157; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1417; AIR 2003 SC 3828; 2003 (3) CCR 168; 2003 (3) Crimes 374; 2003 (6) CRJ 198; 2003 CRLJ 4321; 2003 (3) JCC 1404; JT 2003 (7) SC 392; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 226; 2003(6) SCALE 707; 2003 (1) SCW 4387; 2003 (5) SLT 1; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1; 2003 (6) Supreme 101; 2003 (2) UC 1365

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113B, 114(a) - Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act, 1983 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986 - IPC, 1860, ss. 304 B, 498 A - Demand for dowry - Suicide - Whether a case u/s. 498 A is made out, even if accusations u/s. 304 B fail - Held, ss 304 B and 498 A, IPC, 1860, cannot be held to be mutually inclusive - These provisions deal with two distinct offences It is true that cruelty is a common essential to both sections and that has to be proved explanation to s 498 A gives meaning of 'cruelty' - In s 304 B, there is no such explanation about meaning of 'cruelty' - But having regard to common background to these offences, it has to be taken that meaning of 'cruelty' or 'harassment' is same as prescribed in explanation to s 498 A under which 'cruelty' by itself amounts to an offence u/s. 304 B, it is 'dowry death' that is punishable and such death should have occurred within seven years of marriage - No such period is mentioned in s 498 A - A person charged and acquitted u/s. 304 B can be convicted u/s. 498 A without that charge being there, if such a case is made out - If case is established, there can be a conviction under both sections.
