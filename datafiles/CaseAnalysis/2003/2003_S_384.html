Tulshidas Kanolkar v State of Goa

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 876; (2003) 8 SCC 590; AIR 2004 SC 978; 2003 (4) Crimes 380; 2003 (3) JCC 1821; JT 2003 (8) SC 447; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 964; 2003(9) SCALE 8; 2003 (1) SCW 6581; 2004 (1) ShimLC 147; 2003 (6) SLT 473; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 978; 2003 (7) Supreme 516

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 376, 506(2) - Rape - Victim, a mentally challenged girl - Case of consent pleaded - Delay in lodging FIR - Convicted by both the Trial Court as well as the High Court - Held, delay per se is not a mitigating circumstance for the accused when accusations of rape are involved - Further held, a mentally challenged girl cannot legally give a consent which would necessarily involve understanding of the effect of such consent. It has to be a conscious and voluntary act.
