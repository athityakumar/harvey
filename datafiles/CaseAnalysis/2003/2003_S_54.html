State of Gujarat v Salimbhai Abdulgaffar Shaikh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 708; (2003) 8 SCC 50; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2006; AIR 2003 SC 3224; 2003 (4) CCR 103; 2003 (3) Crimes 368; 2004 (1) CRJ 487; 2003 CRLJ 4348; 2003 (3) G.L.R. 2378; 2003 (3) JCC 1598; JT 2003 (7) SC 550; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 625; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 488; 2003(7) SCALE 253; 2003 (5) SLT 663; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 414; 2003 (6) Supreme 484

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prevention of Terrorism Act

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 134(1)(c), 134A, 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 143, 147, 148, 149, 153(A), 302, 307, 337, 338, 34, 378(3), 435 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 436, 437, 438, 439, 439(1), 439(2), 482 - Prevention of Terrorism Act, ss. 3(2), 3(3), 34, 34(1), 34(2), 34(4), 4, 49, 49(5) - Railways Act, 1989, ss. 141, 151, 152 - Prevention Of Damage To Public Property Act, 1984, ss. 3, 4 - Bombay Police Act, s. 135(1) - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 20 - To treat certificate by HC as cancelled and appeal as incompetent - Held, settled practice of this Court is that if on face of it, Court is satisfied that HC has not properly exercised discretion under art 134(1)(c), matter may either be remitted or this Court may exercise that discretion itself or treat appeal as one under art 136 - Bail - HC has invoked powers u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 while granting bail to respondents Section 482, CrPC, 1973 saves inherent power of HC - HC possesses inherent powers to be exercised ex debito justice to do real and substantial justice for administration of which alone Courts exist power has to be exercised to prevent abuse of process of Court or to otherwise secure ends of justice - But this power cannot be resorted to if there is a specific provision in Code for redress of grievance of aggrieved party - Further held, there being a specific provision for grant of bail, HC clearly erred in taking recourse to s 482, CrPC, 1973 while enlarging respondents on bail.
