Dharam Pal Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1387; (2007) 15 SCC 770; AIR 2004 SC 611; 2003(3) SCALE 424; 2003 (1) SCW 6158; 2003 (8) SLR 650

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Appellants joined Indian Army in year 1963 - In 1967, he joined Defence Security Corps and was promoted to rank of Havaldar on 01-07-1981 - Appellant became entitled to be considered for promotion to rank of Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) - Appellant was not found fit for promotion - Thereafter, appellant was selected but was not informed that although he had been selected to be promoted but he cannot be given promotion as he had given unwillingness for extension of service as Havaldar - Held, unwillingness to continue as Havaldar beyond 31-05-1988 does not imply unwillingness to be promoted or unwillingness to continue as Junior Commissioned Officer - Fact that appellant's papers for discharge as Havaldar w.e.f. 31-05-1988 had been processed is of no consequence in so far as appellant's entitlement to be promoted as Junior Commissioned Officer after his due selection by screening board, if informed of his slection as Junior Commissioned Officer, appellant would have been fully justified in withdrawing his unwillingness - Further held, appellant was entitled to be considered for promotion in DPC which met in April, 1988 and was, therefore, so considered - Having regard to his ACRs. he was found fit to be promoted - Fact that in earlier two boards he was not found fit, is wholly inconsequential and irrelevant - Directed respondents to grant to him consequential relief, i.e., pensionary benefits w.e.f. May, 1988 treating appellant to be in service as Junior Commissioned Officer - Appeal allowed.
