V.P. Pithupitchai and Anr. v The Special Secretary to the Govt. of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 417; (2003) 9 SCC 534; AIR 2003 SC 2455; JT 2003 (4) SC 321; 2003(4) SCALE 458; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1045; 2003 (1) SCW 2473; 2003 (3) SLT 456; 2003 (3) Supreme 743

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Mines & Minerals - Mines And Minerals (Regulation And Development) Act, 1957 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 246 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1980, r. 69 - Whether sea shells can be termed as 'mineral' within meaning of Act - Whether a seashell is limeshell within meaning of item 28 of Second Schedule to Act because term 'lime' denotes chemical composition of shell and term 'sea' denotes places of its occurrence - Held, seashells may, like bones, contain calcium carbonate, and may also like trees, through a geological process result in a mineral such as limestone - But it cannot be said that a seashell in its original form is a mineral - Further, issue whether seasell is mineral for purposes of Act, was not one, which could be determined without extensive research by technically competent persons State Govt. order does not refer to any report nor has report been brought on record nor its contents disclosed - Therefore, decision of State Govt. cannot be sustained - Also, having regard to fact that Act is a Central Govt's legislation, which occupies field, it is a moot point whether State Govt. was competent to have decided issue as to whether a particular substance was a mineral or not for purpose of Act - Appeals allowed.
