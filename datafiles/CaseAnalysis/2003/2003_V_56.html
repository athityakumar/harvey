Bharat Lal Baranwal v Virendra Kumar Agarwal

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 79; (2003) 2 SCC 343; AIR 2003 SC 1056; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 80; 2003 (50) ALR 622; 2003 (1) ARC 382; 2003 (2) AWC 922; JT 2003 (1) SC 485; 2003 (1) RCJ 63; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 178; 2003(1) SCALE 494; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 659; 2003 (1) SLT 663; 2003 (1) Supreme 806; 2003 (1) UC 518

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - S. 20(2)(d) - whether the tenant-respondent having started using the premises in dispute for a purpose other than the purpose for which it was let out to him without the written consent of the appellant-landlord is liable to be evicted - Held, as the tenant has changed the user of the building from business to manufacturing without the written consent of the landlord before and after the Amendment of the Act, he became liable to be evicted under Section 20(2)(d) of the Act. - High Court thus erred in reversing the well considered judgment of the Revisional Court.
