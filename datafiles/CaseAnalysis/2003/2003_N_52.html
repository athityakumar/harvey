State of Rajasthan v Nathu and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 366; (2003) 5 SCC 537; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1156; 2003 (2) CCR 120; 2003 (3) Crimes 73; 2003 (5) CRJ 477; 2003 (2) JCC 954; JT 2003 (4) SC 496; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 892; 2003(4) SCALE 350; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 967; 2003 (3) SLT 419; 2003 (4) Supreme 127

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - ss. 302, 149 and 141 - whether prosecution has failed to establish common object of unlawful assembly - held, the common object to murder one, Bhima and his family can be gathered from the formidable arms carried by the accused, entering the house by breaking the wall and finding Bhima not there, butchering his children - Further held, if death had been caused in prosecution of common object of unlawful assembly, it is not necessary to record a definite and specific finding as to which particular accused out of the members of the unlawful assembly caused the fatal injury. Once an unlawful assembly has come into existence, each member of the assembly becomes vicariously liable for the criminal act of any other member of the assembly committed in prosecution of the common object of the assembly.
