Officers And Supervisors Of I.D.P.L. v Chairman And M.D. I.D.P.L. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1767; (2003) 6 SCC 490; AIR 2003 SC 2870; 2003 (4) AWC 2780; 2003 (4) ESC 434; JT 2003 (6) SC 68; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 720; 2003 (5) Supreme 157; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1769

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Sick industry - Pay revision - Entitlement - Fifth Pay Commission recommended revision of scales of pay and allowances of the Central Government employees and directed the 3rd respondent Ministry of Industry to revise the pay scales of the employees in sixty-nine public enterprises includes respondent company following CDA pattern - Since respondent did not take any step for implementing the Fifth Pay Commission recommendations, petitioners filed the instant writ petition - Whether the employees of public sector enterprises had any legal right to claim revision of wages that though the industrial undertakings or the companies in which they were working did not have the financial capacity to grant revision in pay scale - Held, since the employees of government companies were not government servants, they had absolutely no legal right to claim that the Government should pay their salary or that the additional expenditure incurred on account of revision of their pay scales should be met by the Government - Being employees of the companies, it was the responsibility of the companies to pay them salary and if the company was sustaining losses continuously over a period and did not have the financial capacity to revise or enhance the pay scale, the petitioners could not claim any legal right to ask for a direction to the Central Government to meet the additional expenditure which may be incurred on account of revision of pay scales - Further, economic capability of the employer played a crucial part in the payment of salary to employees as also its capacity to expand business or earn more profits - 1st respondent company which was engaged in the manufacture of medicines became a sick industrial company for various reasons and was declared as such by the BIFR - It was also not in dispute that the production activities had to be stopped in the two major units and the losses and liabilities were increasing every month and that the payment of three instalments of interim relief of Fifth Pay Commission revision could not also be made due to the threat of industrial unrest - Further, units of the companies had already suspended their operations and as of date no unit was functioning - In view of the said position and keeping in view the huge staff of approximately 6582, the parent Ministry decided to introduce Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) whereby VRS was open for three months with a clear-cut understanding to the employees that if one did not opt for VRS within three months VRS would not be given in future and only retrenchment compensation would be applicable - Further, all the petitioners had already opted for the said VRS and they were likely to be relieved any day after receiving of the funds from the concerned Ministry - It had also been decided that all the employees would be relieved and subsequent decision would be taken by the parent Ministry - Petitioners having applied for VRS, it was not open to them to contend that they were entitled to pay revision - Petition dismissed.
