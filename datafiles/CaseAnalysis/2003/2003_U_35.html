Justice P.Venugopal v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 696; (2003) 7 SCC 726; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1185; AIR 2003 SC 3887; AIR 2003 SCW 4472; 2003 (5) AWC 4555; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 505; 2003(7) SCALE 197; 2003 (6) SLT 358; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 286; 2003 (6) Supreme 550; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2720

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 102(1)(a), 112(3)(iii), 141, 191(1)(a), 217, 217(1), 221, 221(2), 224A - High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, s. 2(1)(c)(i) - High Court Judges (Conditions Of Service) Amendment Act, 1976 - High Court Judge (Salaries and Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, ss. 14, 15, 16 - High Court Judges (Travelling Allowance) Rules, 1956 - Actual Service - Petitioner despite his superannuation as a HC Judge continued to occupy post of Commission of Inquiry - Re-fixing of pension - Whether period during which he served as Commission of Inquiry or as Commissioner of Payments can be taken into consideration for computing pensionary benefits - Held, for purpose of computation of pension, different service of petitioner could have been clubbed in terms of Act 28 of 1954 pension payable to a HC judge would be only for period rendered in that capacity which would constitute charge to consolidated fund of India and services rendered subsequent thereto in terms of order made by a State Govt. would not be charged to consolidated fund - Further held, question as to whether such a person would be entitled to pension from concerned State or not would depend upon statute or terms and conditions of appointment.
