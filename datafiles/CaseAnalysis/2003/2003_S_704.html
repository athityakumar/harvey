Chittar Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 524; (2003) 6 SCC 397; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1377; AIR 2003 SC 3590; 2003 (3) CCR 25; 2003 (3) Crimes 131; 2003 (5) CRJ 741; 2003 CRLJ 3548; 2003 (2) JCC 1189; JT 2003 (7) SC 270; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 857; 2003(5) SCALE 327; 2003 (4) SLT 516; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 633; 2003 (5) Supreme 43; 2003 (2) UC 1168

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - s. 134 - Appreciation of evidence - Whether the accused can be convicted on the sole testimony of a young boy of 15 - Held, there can be no hard and fast rule that the names of all witnesses, more particularly eye-witnesses, should be indicated in the FIR - Further held, it is the quality of evidence of the single witness whose testimony has to be tested on the touchstone of credibility and reliability. If the testimony is found to be reliable, there is no legal impediment to convict the accused on such proof. It is the quality and not the quantity of evidence that is necessary for proving or disproving a fact.
