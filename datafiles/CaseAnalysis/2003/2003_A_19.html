B. Janakiramaiah Chetty v A.K. Parthasarthi and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 324; (2003) 5 SCC 641; AIR 2003 SC 3527; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 79; 2003 (3) AllCJ 1791; 2003 (51) ALR 285; 2003 (4) AWC 3296; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 242; 2003 (1) CLR 704; JT 2003 (3) SC 580; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 186; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 656; 2003 (94) RD 722; 2003(3) SCALE 660; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 369; 2003 (3) SLT 69; 2003 (3) Supreme 262; 2003 (2) UC 1162

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC - Order IX Rule 13, and Explanation to Order XVII Rule 2 - scope and ambit of - Held, the Explanation of Rule 2 confers discretion on the Court to proceed with a case where evidence or a substantial portion of evidence of any party has been already recorded and such party fails to appear on the date to which hearing of the suit has been adjourned. If such is the factual situation, the Court may in its discretion deem as if such party was present. Under Rule 3 the Court may make an order directing that the suit be dismissed when neither party appears when the suit is called on for hearing. Therefore, on the facts it has to be seen in each case as to whether the Explanation was applied by the Court or not.
