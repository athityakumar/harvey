Devinder Singh and Others v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 765; (2003) 11 SCC 488; (2004) SCC (Cr) 185; AIR 2003 SC 3365; 2003 (4) CCR 81; 2003 (4) Crimes 120; 2004 (1) CRJ 186; 2003 CRLJ 4976; 2003 (3) JCC 1577; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 244; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 363; 2003(7) SCALE 580; 2003 (1) SCW 4779; 2003 (2) ShimLC 422; 2003 (5) SLT 561; 2003 (6) Supreme 768; 2004 (1) UC 249

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 376, 380, 457 - Conviction - Rape - Testimony of prosecutrix - Reported the incident to police after six days - Non- examination of witnesses - Medical evidence did not support the case of the prosecution - No test identification parade held - Not naming some persons in the course of deposition, who were earlier named in the FIR - Held, taking into account these features of the prosecution case we are of the view that it is not safe to rely upon the testimony of PW.1.
