Preetam Singh And Ors. v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 955; (2003) 12 SCC 594; JT 2003 (8) SC 427; 2003(9) SCALE 373; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 148, 149, 302, 323, 326 - Material part of the version of the appellants was not accepted by the Trial Court and the High Court - Accused in the counter-case lodged by the appellants were acquitted - Conviction - Criminal liability - Held, appellants 1 to 3 are convicted under s. 304 (Part I) r/w s. 34 and also under s. 148, IPC. The conviction under s. 302 r/w s. 34 is set aside. Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, imprisonment for eight years coupled with the enhancement of fine would be adequate punishment for the main offence they committed under s. 304 Part I - Further held, the period of detention/imprisonment already undergone by the four appellants, at whatever stage it was, shall be setoff against the period of imprisonment to which they are now sentenced.
