Union of India v Rajiv Kumar with Bani Singh

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 519; (2003) 6 SCC 516; (2003) SCC (L&S) 928; AIR 2003 SC 2917; 2003 (4) AWC 3020; 2003 (105) DLT 576; 2003 (4) ESC 596; 2003 (98) FLR 753; JT 2003 (5) SC 617; 2003(5) SCALE 297; 2004 (1) SLJ 1; 2003 (4) SLR 730; 2003 (4) SLT 500; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 597; 2003 (5) Supreme 208; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2059

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Central Civil Appeal Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10(1), r. 10(2), r. 10(3), r. 10(4), r. 10(5)(a), r. 10(5)(b), r. 10(5)(c) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules, 1957 - Scope and ambit of r.10(2) of Rules vis-à-vis other provisions of Rules - Held, order in terms of r.10(2) is not restricted in its point of duration or efficacy to period of actual detention only - It continues to be operative unless modified or revoked under Sub-r 5(c), as provided under sub-r 5(a) - Further held, r 10(5)(b) deals with a situation where a Govt. servant is suspended or is deemed to have suspended and any other disciplinary proceeding is commenced against him during continuance of that suspension irrespective of fact whether earlier suspension was in connection with any disciplinary proceeding or otherwise Rule 10(5)(b) can be pressed into service only when any other disciplinary proceeding is also commenced than one for and during which suspension or deemed suspension was already in force, to meet situation until termination of all such proceedings In contradiction, r 10(5)(a) has application in relation to an order of suspension already made or deemed to have been made It is r 10(5)(a) alone which has application and deemed suspension would continue to be in force till anything has been done under r 10(5)(c) - Similarly, rr 10(3) and 10(4) operate in different fields and merely because a specific provision is made for its continuance, until further orders in them itself due to certain further developments taking place and interposition of orders made by Court or appellate and reviewing authority to meet and get over such specific eventualities, in given circumstances and that does not in any way affect order of suspension deemed to have been made u/r.10(2).
