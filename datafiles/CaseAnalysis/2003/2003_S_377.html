Kuldip Singh v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1086; (2003) 12 SCC 528; AIR 2004 SC 771; 2004 (1) CCR 69; 2004 (1) Crimes 13; 2004 (109) DLT 190; 2004 (1) JCC 63; JT 2003 (10) SC 195; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 292; 2003(10) SCALE 590; 2003 (1) SCW 6953; 2004 (1) SLT 177; 2003 (8) Supreme 624; 2004 (1) UC 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fingerprints

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 302, 380, 460 - Appellants convicted by Trial Court and High Court - Disclosure statement by the accused - Circumstantial evidence - Employment and re-employment of appellant - Held, the mere fact that appellant was once engaged by the deceased would not be a circumstance at all indicating the involvement of the appellant because he was not the only person employed and terminated by the deceased just prior to her death - Further held, the recovery of the property belonging to the deceased is not at all safe or sufficient to base a conviction not only under ss. 302 r/w 34 and ss. 460 r/w 34 IPC, but even for an offence under s. 380 r/w 34, IPC, 1860.
