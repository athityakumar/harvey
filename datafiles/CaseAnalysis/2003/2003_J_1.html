R. Sai Bharathi v J. Jayalalitha and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1495; (2004) 2 SCC 9; (2004) SCC (Cr) 377; AIR 2004 SC 692; 2004 (1) Crimes 43; 2004 CRLJ 286; 2003 (3) JCC 1970; JT 2003 (9) SC 343; 2003(10) SCALE 68; 2003 (1) SCW 6349; 2003 (7) SLT 274; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 85; 2003 (8) Supreme 442

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrongful Gain Or Wrongful Loss, Sale Price, Not For Sale, Private Party, Civil Action, Government Order

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 169, 409, 420 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13, 13(1)(c), 13(1)(d), 13(2) -Sale - Wrongful gain or Wrongful loss - Acquittal - Maintainability - Tamil Nadu Small Industries Corporation Limited(TANSI) was formed by State Govt. which was registered under the 1956 Act as Govt. Company - After transfer some of Govt. Industries units to TANSI incurred losses - Report was sent by TANSI to State Govt. - State Govt. decided by passing G.O. that TANSI should close down 8 units which were incurring losses and explore possibility of disposing properties by inviting offers through advertisements in newspapers - TANSI transferred a part of land and building belonging to TANSI Foundry unit to Tamil Nadu Corporation for Development of Women on 15.5.1987 - Jaya Publications/unregistered partnership firm of accused no.1 and accused no.2 purchased land adjacent to TANSI property in dispute from Hitex Equipment Company - Accused no.1 became Chief Minister and Jaya Publications purchased another adjacent land - Advertisement was published for disposal of TANSAI enameled Wire Units adjacent to TANSI Foundry - Under chairmanship of Chief Minister meeting was held to review performance of TANSI - Decision was taken at meeting is that TANSI to sell properties of its units which were defunct - TANSI Foundry unit was identified as defunct unit and tenders were called through advertisement - Jaya publications, R.R. Industries , SIDCO submitted tenders - TANSI Foundry property was sold to Jaya Publications - N.O.C from Income Tax Dept. was sought with a draft sale agreement which was registered conditionally because value of land and building was less than market value - Private Compliant was lodged before Trial Court - Matter was referred to C.B.C.I.D and investigation was initiated - Trial Court convicted A-1, A-2, A-3 to A-5 u/s. 120-B IPC r/w ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(c) and (d) of 1988 Act and also u/s. 13(2) r/w. ss. 13(1)(c) and 13(2) r/w. s. 13(1)(d) of 1988 Act - Aggrieved by same accused preferred appeals before HC which were allowed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was deliberate sale of land to accused which resulted into wrongful loss to Govt. Company and Wrongful gain to A-1 and A-2 - Held, SC relied upon Vidadala Harinadhababu vs. N.T. Ramarao 1989 Indlaw AP 139 and stated that in law there must be a specific provision prohibiting an act to make it illegal and Code of Conduct prescribed by Govt. under certain notification by itself could not be elevated to level of law - Further, even if Govt. order was traced to have been issued under executive power of State u/art. 162 of Constitution, such a Code would not be enforceable when language used was not in mandatory terms and they were intended to be mere guidelines or instructions to concerned persons in authority - Furthermore, as long as such a Code of Conduct was not enforceable in any court of law and did not even provide what action could possibly be taken in case of breach by the Chief Minister, prohibition contained therein was only having ethical or moral effect and any breach thereof could not be treated to be unlawful or even illegal within the meaning of s. 43 of IPC and to constitute a ground for civil action u/s. 43 IPC, there should be a right in a party which can be enforced - Therefore, SC constrained to hold that offence under aforesaid provision had not been established - Thus, there was nothing in the charge to indicate nor did the prosecution take a specific stand at any stage of the trial that the purchase of TANSI foundry property by A-1 from Govt. would furnish a ground for a particular civil action - Appeals dismissed.
