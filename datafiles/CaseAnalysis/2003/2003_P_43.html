Seth Chand Ratan v Pandit Durga Prasad (D) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 296; (2003) 5 SCC 399; AIR 2003 SC 2736; JT 2003 (3) SC 346; 2003(3) SCALE 457; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 75; 2003 (3) SLT 755; 2003 (3) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Ex-Parte Order

Summary: Trusts and Associations - M.P. Public Trusts Act, 1951, s.8 - Application for registration of Shri Madan Mohan Mandir as a public trust - Subsequently moved application for withdrawal of aforesaid application - Registrar allowed application for withdrawal - Can correction application be filed before Registrar once he gave a finding as to nature of trust? - Held, no, when, the order passed by the Registrar by which it was held that Maheshwari Panchayati Mandir is a public trust and Shri Madan Mohan Mandir and the shops in the precincts thereof were the property of the trust and were being managed by it became final and conclusive, then, the only remedy available to the respondent was to institute a civil suit u/s.8 of the Act for setting aside the said finding It was not open to the Registrar to entertain a correction application and to record that Shri Madan Mohan Mandir is a private trust of the respondent - Further, when the party had statutory remedy of assailing the order passed by the District Court by filing an appeal to the HC itself, he could not bypass the said remedy and take recourse to proceedings under art.226 and 227 of the Constitution - HC committed manifest error of law in entertaining and allowing the writ petition - Appeal allowed.
