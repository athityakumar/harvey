Ramashray Singh v New India Assurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 548; (2003) 10 SCC 664; 2003 (2) ACC 706; AIR 2003 SC 2877; 2003 (4) AWC 2792; [2003] 116 Comp Cas 643; 2003 (98) FLR 976; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 193; JT 2003 (6) SC 97; 2003 (3) LLJ 740; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 134; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 1; 2003(5) SCALE 377; 2003 (1) SCW 3601; 2003 (4) SLT 583; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 666; 2003 (5) Supreme 118

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - ss. 146(1), 147(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 226 -Liability of insurance company to pay compensation on account of death of a khalasi - Held, an insurance policy only covers the person or classes of persons specified it the policy. A comprehensive policy merely means that the loss sustained by such person/persons will be payable up to the insured amount irrespective of the actual loss suffered - Further held, although the appellant's claim under the insurance policy arose under the Workmen's Compensation Act, since the concerned employee was not engaged in the capacity of driver in respect of whom alone premium was paid apart from the passengers, his claim is unsustainable.
