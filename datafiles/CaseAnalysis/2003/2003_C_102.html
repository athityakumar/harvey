M.V. Shankar Bhat and another v Claude Pinto Since (Deceased) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 197; (2003) 4 SCC 86; AIR 2004 SC 636; 2003 (51) ALR 167; 2003 (1) ARC 419; 2003 (2) AWC 1211; 2003 (1) CLR 509; JT 2003 (2) SC 213; 2003 (94) RD 707; 2003(2) SCALE 124; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1212; 2003 (2) SLT 141; 2003 (2) Supreme 62

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, Land Ceiling Act

Summary: Land and Property - Indian Succession Act, ss.211(1), 307(1) - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - s. 20 - Will - Defendant No 1 appointed as sole Executor - Agreement to sell - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case a concluded contract can be said to have been arrived at - Held, a bare perusal of the Will leaves no manner of doubt that although thereby the Original Defendant was given liberty to sell the property in question and distribute the amount received thereby in the manner stated therein but the legatees have also been given an option to partition the property - Once such a desire is expressed before a deed of sale is executed, the Executor had no other option but to consent thereto - Therefore, the agreement of sale in question could not have been specifically enforced and in that view of the matter the question as to whether the Original Defendant No 1 had an absolute right to dispose of the property in question in exercise of his power as an Executor of the Will or not takes a back seat - It is not a fit case where a discretionary jurisdiction of this Court in terms of s.20 of the Specific Relief Act should be exercised - Appeal dismissed.
