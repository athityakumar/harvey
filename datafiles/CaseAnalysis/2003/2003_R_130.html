Rame Gowda (D) by LRs. v M. Varadappa Naidu (D) by LRs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1138; (2004) 1 SCC 769; AIR 2003 SC 4609; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 31; 2004 (54) ALR 725; 2004 (2) CHN 33; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 519; 2003(10) SCALE 950; 2004 (1) SLT 675; 2003 (Supp) RD 299; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 850; 2003 (8) Supreme 928

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Dispute - Demarcation of the boundaries - Possession and enjoyment - Decree of the Trial Court restraining the respondent from interfering with the possession and enjoyment of the suit schedule property upheld by the High Court - Held, the plaintiff as well as the defendant have failed to prove their title. Nevertheless, the plaintiff has been found to be in settled possession of the property - Further held, the Trial Court therefore left the question of title open and proceeded to determine the suit on the basis of possession, protecting the established possession and restraining the attempted interference therewith. The Trial Court and the High Court and the High Court have rightly decided the suit - Further, it is open to the defendant-appellant to file a suit based on his title against the plaintiff-respondent and evict the latter on the former establishing his better right to possess the property.
