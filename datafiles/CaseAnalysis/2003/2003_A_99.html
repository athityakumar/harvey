State Of Rajasthan And Ors. v Anand Prakash Solanki

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 689; (2003) 7 SCC 403; AIR 2003 SC 3849; 2003 (52) ALR 825; 2003 (6) AWC 4686; 2003 CCC 381; 2003 (3) CPJ(Sc) 12; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 98; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 300; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 6; 2003(7) SCALE 108; 2004 (1) SLJ 309; 2003 (5) SLT 456; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 957; 2003 (6) Supreme 532; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 389

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - s. 10 - Whether a president or a member of the district forum, constituted under s. 10, Consumer Protection Act, 1986 can be transferred and, if so, which is the competent authority to transfer them - Held, the scheme of the Act does not prohibit or exclude the exercise of power to transfer the president or members from one district forum to another district forum within the state. Power to transfer vests in the State Government as employer and is available to be exercised on the recommendation of committee contemplated by s. 10(1A) of the Act.
