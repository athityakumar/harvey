Durga Prasad Gupta v State of Rajasthan through C.B.I.

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 795; (2003) 12 SCC 257; 2004 (97) CutLT 489; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 586; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 834; 2003(8) SCALE 107; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act, Explosives Rules, 1983

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 5 - Explosives Act, 1884, s. 9-B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 15, 3(3), 4, 5, 6 - Explosives Rules, 1983, r. 115 - Huge quantity of explosives found from residence of appellant - Appellant had no licence to deal with gelatine or detonators - Conscious possession - Held, once possession is established, person who claims that it was not a conscious possession has to establish it, because how he came to be in possession is within his special knowledge - Further held, possession of such articles in such large quantity by accused has been proved and there is nothing to discard prosecution version that possession was conscious statement recorded is very elaborate and accused has inculpated himself with details like source of purchase of articles which were within his personal and special knowledge - Further held, it is not necessary to go into other question whether past transactions come within umbrella of s 5.
