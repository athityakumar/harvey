Supreme Court of India

07 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o.39 rr. 1, 2, s. 115 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Whether amendment in s.115 affects power and jurisdiction of HC to entertain petitions u/arts.226 and 227 of Constitution - Held, curtailment of revisional jurisdiction of HC does not take away constitutional jurisdiction of HC to issue a writ of certiorari to a civil court nor power of superintendence conferred on HC u/art 227 is taken away or whittled down power exists, untrammelled by amendment in s.115 and is available to be exercised subject to rules of self-discipline and practice, which are well settled - Further held, parameters for exercise of jurisdiction under art.226 or 227 of Constitution cannot be tied down in a straitjacket formula or rigid rules facts and circumstances of a given case may make it more appropriate for HC to exercise self-restraint and not to intervene because error of jurisdiction though committed is yet capable of being taken care of and corrected at a later stage and wrong done, if any, would be set right and rights and equities adjusted in appeal or revision preferred at conclusion of proceedings - Power is there but exercise is discretionary which will be governed solely by dictates of judicial conscience enriched by judicial experience and practical wisdom of judge.
