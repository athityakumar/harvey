K. Anbazhagan v Superintendent of Police and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1001; (2004) 3 SCC 767; (2004) SCC (Cr) 882; AIR 2004 SC 524; 2004 (1) Crimes 261; 2004 CRLJ 583; JT 2003 (9) SC 31; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 720; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 441; 2003(9) SCALE 680; 2003 (1) SCW 6468; 2003 (7) SLT 300; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 610; 2004 (1) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Deem

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 313, 406 - Transfer of petitions to a court of equal and competent jurisdiction in any other state - Held, the circumstances as recited are such as to create reasonable apprehension in the minds of the public at large in general and the petitioner in particular that there is every likelihood of failure of justice - Further held, for the convenience of the parties, the State of Karnataka would be most convenient due to its nearness to Tamil Nadu - Directions issued.
