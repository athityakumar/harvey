M. A. Murthy v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1377; (2003) 7 SCC 517; AIR 2003 SC 3821; 2003 (52) ALR 830; 2003 (185) CTR 194A; JT 2003 (8) SC 292; 2003(7) SCALE 154; 2003 (1) SCW 4377; 2003 (6) SLR 783; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 327; 178 TAXATION 397A

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Prospective over-ruling - Sustainability - Corporation invited applications for recruitment to two posts of Manager (Finance and Accounts) by advertisement - Appellant and respondent no's. 4 and 5 were applicants - Though respondent no.4 was not qualified he was selected- Appellant challenged his selection - Waiting list was prepared and respondent no.5 was placed in waiting list - Application was filed before HC by appellant - HC held that respondent no.4 was ineligible as on date of employment, in public interest selection was to be maintained - Appeal was filed before DB - View of HC was affirmed by DB - HC held selection of respondent no.4 could not be questioned - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, decision in particular case would be prospective in its application by application of doctrine of prospective over-ruling - Doctrine of binding precedent helps in promoting certainty and consistency in judicial decisions and enables organic development of law besides providing assurance to individual as to consequences of transactions forming part of daily affairs - HC erred by holding that judgment which operated on date of selection was operative and not review judgment - All more so when subsequent judgment was by way of Review of first judgment in which case there were no judgments and subsequent judgment rendered on review petitions was judgment rendered, effectively and for all purposes earlier decision had been erased by countenancing review applications -Hence, Impugned judgments of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
