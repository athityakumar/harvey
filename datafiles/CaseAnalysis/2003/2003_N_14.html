N.C. Dhoundial v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1164; (2004) 2 SCC 579; (2004) SCC (Cr) 587; AIR 2004 SC 1272; 2004 (1) AllCriC 710; 2004 (1) AWC 516; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 129; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 636; 2003(10) SCALE 609; 2004 (1) SLT 298; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 674; 2003 (8) Supreme 738

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Socio-Economic

Summary: Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993 - ss. 13, 36 - Prevention of Corruption Act - ss. 13(2), 13(1)(e) - Search - CBI - Interrogation - Illegal detention - Held, in a case where the NHRC proceeds to investigate and inquire into the violation of human rights pursuant to the directions of this Court under art. 32 of the Constitution, the bar contained in s. 36(2) will not apply because in such an event, NHRC does not function under the provisions of the Act but as an 'expert body' aiding the Supreme Court in the discharge of its constitutional power under art. 32.
