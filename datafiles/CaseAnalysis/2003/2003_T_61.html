The State of Maharashtra and another v The Jalgaon Municipal Council and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 273; (2003) 9 SCC 731; AIR 2003 SC 1659; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 709; JT 2003 (5) SC 509; 2003(2) SCALE 84B; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1112; 2003 (2) SLT 362; 2003 (2) Supreme 72

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities and Local Govts - Maharashtra Municipal Council Nagar Panchayat and Industrial Townships Act, 1965 - Proposal for converting the constitution of Jalgaon city from Municipal Council into a Municipal Corporation - (A) Whether any hiatus between abolition of Municipal Council and constitution of Municipal Corporation is violative of Constitution Part IXA? - Held, the said hiatus is an unavoidable event which must take place in the process of conversion of Municipal Council into a Municipal Corporation - (B) The affect of census figures published being called' provision'? - Held, the correctness of the fact that the population had crossed the bench mark of three lakhs provided the requisite foundation for the StateGovt.to take a decision of constitution of a Municipal Corporation - (C) Whether the population of Jalgaon was denied an effective opportunity of raising and hence the principles of natural justice were violated? - Held, neither the principles of natural justice have been violated nor is there any breach committed of the procedural requirements prescribed by s.3(3) r/w s.6 of the MRMC Act as amended by the ordinance - (D) Want of consultation with Municipal Council, effect - Held, consultation must take place at any one stage before the finalisation of the proposal - Final decision was yet to be taken which was stayed by the HC - Judgment of the HC cannot be sustained on any of the grounds upheld by it - Appeals allowed.
