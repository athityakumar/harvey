Maan Singh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.M. Dharmadhikari, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 198; (2003) SCC (L&S) 314; (2003) 3 SCC 464; AIR 2003 SC 1800; 2003 (2) AWC 1581; 2003 (1) CLR 989; 2003 (103) DLT 197; 2003 (3) ESC 282; 2003 (97) FLR 1; JT 2003 (2) SC 514; 2003 LabIC 1176; 2003 (2) LLN 397; 2003(2) SCALE 209; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 129; 2003 (2) SLJ 359; 2003 (2) SLR 607; 2003 (2) SLT 124; 2003 (2) Supreme 247; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3330

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Delhi Police Act, 1978 - S. 21 - departmental enquiry under - absence of appellant from duty without intimation - dismissal from service - appeal - contention that the period of absence having been regularised as 'leave without pay' would automatically set at naught the order of dismissal - held, an order regularising absence from duty subsequent to termination of employment does not have the effect of invalidating termination - when the charge against the appellants in each of these cases is habitual absence for long periods on several occasions unauthorisedly, the view taken by the disciplinary authority is justified.
