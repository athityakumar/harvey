Sukanya Holdings Pvt. Ltd. v Jayesh H. Pandya and another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 326; (2003) 5 SCC 531; AIR 2003 SC 2252; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 75; 2003 (2) ARBLR 43; 2003 (3) AWC 2055; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 413; 2003 (1) CLR 864; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 113; JT 2003 (4) SC 58; 2003 (2) RAJ 32; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 647; 2003(4) SCALE 7; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 558; 2003 (1) SCW 2209; 2003 (3) SLT 194; 2003 (3) Supreme 324

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Extent Of Judicial Intervention

Summary: Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996 - Ss. 5 and 8 - CPC - S. 89 - Held, Arbitration Act does not oust the jurisdiction of the Civil Court to decide the dispute in a case where parties to the Arbitration Agreement do not take appropriate steps as contemplated under sub-sections (1) & (2) of Section 8 of the Act - entire subject matter of the suit should be subject to arbitration agreement - further held, Section 89 CPC cannot be resorted to for interpreting Section 8 of the Act as it stands on a different footing and it would be applicable even in cases where there is no arbitration agreement for referring the dispute for arbitration.
