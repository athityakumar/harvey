Hindustan Copper Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1631; (2004) 12 SCC 408; 2004(1) SCALE 30

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Justifiability, Discriminatory

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Discriminatory - Validity - Justifiability - Practice & Procedure - Respondent company was engaged in extraction of copper ores from captive mines - Mining process and other processes involve blasting of mines and then big pieces of copper ore were carried into dumpers to unit called primary crusher - Primary crusher was not located in captive mines but was located at a distance of 2.53 kilometres away from mines - Appellant, contended in the petition that activities carried on by it did not fall within meaning of adjacent area - It was pleaded that approach of respondents was selective and discriminatory - HC dismissed petitions - Wherther the order passed by HC was just and proper - Held, appellant alleged discrimination and gave certain details, it discharged its initial burden - It was for respondents to show that, on facts of case, no such discrimination was meted out to appellant - Appellant further submitted that because of wrong approach, HC impugned judgment should not be sustained - HC should have considered effect of the word adjacent in construing area, rather than solely depending on connectivity with regard to Activity - Under these circumstances, it was just and appropriate that HC should examine afresh the contentions advanced on behalf of parties, having due regard to materials placed on record and in context of provisions of the Act touching controversy - Hence, HC order was set aside - Appeals allowed.
