Union Of India v Madhusudan Prasad

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 956; (2004) SCC (L&S) 29; (2004) 1 SCC 43; AIR 2004 SC 977; 2004 (4) ESC 641; 2003 (99) FLR 1051; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 231; 2004 (1) LLJ 454; 2004 (1) LLN 432; 2003(9) SCALE 360; 2003 (1) SCW 6579; 2004 (1) SLJ 232; 2004 (1) SLR 103; 2003 (6) SLT 788; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1026; 2003 (7) Supreme 674

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Reinstatement - Back wages - Employee did not report for duty after expiry of leave - Authorities treated him a deserter and issued a warrant of arrest - After imposition of penalty, the Commandant of CRPF issued an order of dismissal from service - Respondent reinstated after ordered by appellate authority - Held, the respondent was removed from service without any enquiry and he was not even given show cause notice prior to his dismissal from service. There was fault on the part of the employer in not following the principle of natural justice - Further held, the court is directed that appropriate order should be passed regarding the back wages.
