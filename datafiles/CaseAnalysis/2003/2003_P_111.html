Commissioner Of Customs (Ep), Mumbai v Prayag Exporters Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1107; (2007) 12 SCC 401; 2003 (110) ECR 265; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 4; 2003 (57) RLT 4

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs – Customs Act,1962,s.112(d) - Prohibited goods - Applicability of provision -Appellant claimed relief u/s.113(d) of the Act - Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal, held that s.113(d) of the Act was not applied for case of appellant - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, Tribunal has arrived at conclusion that it has taken consistent view that s.113(d) of the Act would apply in cases of prohibited goods and would not apply to facts of present case. Admittedly, goods in question are not prohibited for export and no export duty is leviable on the said goods. Thus, no interference is called for with impugned judgment and order. Appeal dismissed.
