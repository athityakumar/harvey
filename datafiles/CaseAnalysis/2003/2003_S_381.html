Communidade of Morombi-O-Pequeno v State of Goa

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1632; (2004) 12 SCC 430

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.4, 23(2), 28 and 23(1A) - Acquisition - Award of compensation - Entitlement to - U/s.4 of Act notification was issued for the purposes of acquiring 6640 sq metres land - Land was being acquired for the purpose of establishing a museum - Out of 6640 sq metres land 1125 sq metres land were paddy land, 2240 sq metres were salt pans and 3320 sq metres were a nallah - Award came to be passed by the Land Acquisition Officer on 21-3-1978 - Land Acquisition Officer fixed a price at Rs 11 per sq metre for paddy land, Rs 10 per sq metre for salt pan and Rs 6 per sq metre for nallah - Land Acquisition Officer also apportioned the compensation on the basis that 1/3 of the compensation, for paddy land, was to be given to the appellants and the rest was to be given to the tenant for the salt pan 4/5 to the appellants and 1/5 to the tenant and for the nallah the entire compensation was given to the appellants - Appellants filed a reference which was disposed of by a judgment dated 27-2-1990 - Before the Reference Court the appellants led the evidence of two witnesses AW1 and AW2 - AW1 was the power-of-attorney holder of the appellants - AW2 was a Civil Engineer who gave evidence of valuation of the acquired land and proved a certificate which showed that the acquired land was within commercial zone - Appellants also relied upon other awards passed in respect of other lands which were acquired - Reference Court considered the entire evidence and noted that it was proved that there was development all around the acquired land - Reference Court took into account the other award which was in respect of acquisition of the land of the appellants itself for the Tourism Department - That land was situated just 200 metres from instant land - On the basis of evidence before it, the Reference Court concluded that the figures given by AW2 were reliable figures - Reference Court accepted those figures and fixed price at Rs.80 per sq metre for paddy land, Rs.60 per sq metre for the salt pan and Rs.20 per sq metre for the nallah - Reference Court apportioned compensation 50:50 between the appellants and the tenant in respect of paddy field - Reference Court awarded solatium u/s.23(2) of Act and interest u/ss. 28 and 23(1A) of Act - Respondents filed an appeal before the HC - HC had, by the impugned judgment, allowed appeal - HC had held that merely because those lands were also paddy land was not sufficient to draw a conclusion that the acquired land should also be awarded at the same rate - Whether HC erred in deciding the compensation for the acquired land - Held, HC had fallen into error in coming to the conclusion that there was no evidence on record in respect of developmental costs - HC had noticed that the award in Land Acquisition Case 'X' had been marked as an exhibit by consent - Reference Court had also called for the record of that case and looked into that record - Reference Court had found that the two lands were comparable - In that award developmental costs were taken into account - Further, HC completely overlooked the evidence of AW2 - Thus a detailed cost analysis for development purposes had been given by the witness of the appellant - HC overlooked the fact that on such aspect the witness was not cross-examined - On that aspect the respondents had led no contrary evidence - Therefore, there was evidence of what the development cost would be - After taking into consideration the development cost and considering the fact that certain portions of the acquired land were paddy lands, salt pans and nallahs, the figures of Rs.80, 60 and 20 were worked out - Appeal disposed of.
