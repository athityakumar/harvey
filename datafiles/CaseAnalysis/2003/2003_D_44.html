Hythro Power Corporation Limited v Delhi Transco Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 566; (2003) 8 SCC 35; AIR 2003 SC 4219; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 125; 2003 (52) ALR 731; 2003 (52) ARBLR 1; 2003 (105) DLT 915; JT 2003 (6) SC 515; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 159; 2003 (2) RAJ 617; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 803; 2003(5) SCALE 590; 2003 (4) SLT 768; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 940; 2003 (5) Supreme 277; 2003 (2) UC 1405

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - ss. 7(4)(b), 11, 16(1) - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 136, 226 - Seeking reference of disputes raised to an arbitral Tribunal - Refused by the High Court in the absence of written arbitration agreement - Held, both the designate of the Chief Justice acting under s. 11 and the High Court in exercise of power under art. 226 acted under a misconception of law and wrongly held that the disputes were not referable to the arbitration. Whether on the facts, an arbitration agreement can be said to have existed by recourse to arbitration clause in NIT, was itself a dispute which deserved to be referred to the arbitral Tribunal in accordance with the arbitration clause.
