Punjab National Bank v Indian Bank and Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 389; (2003) 6 SCC 79; AIR 2003 SC 2284; 2003 (3) AllCJ 1979; 2003 (51) ALR 524; 2003 (3) AWC 2291; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 1; 2003 (104) DLT 477; 2004 (1) DRTC 7; JT 2003 (4) SC 144; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 216; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 98; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 668; 2003 (2) RD 130; 2003(4) SCALE 267; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 836; 2003 (1) SCW 2250; 2003 (3) SLT 269; 2003 (3) Supreme 607

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Recovery Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1987

Summary: CPC - O. 6 r. 17 r/w s. 151 - Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - s. 22 - suit for money decree - dispute relating to refusal to allow amendment of the plaint - held, an amendment would generally not be disallowed except where a time barred claim is sought to be introduced, there too it would be one of the factors for consideration or where it changes the nature of the suit itself or it is malafide or the other party cannot be placed in the same position had the plaint been originally filed correctly, that is to say, the other side has lost right of a valid defence by subsequent amendment. - No such element present in the case in hand so as to disallow the amendment in the plaint.
