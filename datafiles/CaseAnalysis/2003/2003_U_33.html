Smt. Kanta Devi v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1506; (2003) 4 SCC 753; AIR 2003 SC 3428; 2003 (3) AWC 1850; 2003 (2) CGLJ 225; 2003 (2) ESC 185; JT 2003 (3) SC 164; 2003(3) SCALE 235; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 992; 2003 (6) SLR 267; 2003 (2) Supreme 756; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3362

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, Assam General Clauses Act, 1915, Assam Elementary Education Act, 1962, Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1955

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, rr. 7(b), 27, 28 - Order of dismissal - Non-approval by higher authority - Validity - Appellant/Subedar (Inspector) was dismissed from service for mis-conduct - Appellant filed appeal u/r. 28 of the Rules - Appeal was dismissed by the departmental authority - Appellant challenged the order of dismissal before the HC - Single Judge quashed the order of dismissal holding that the order was passed by the Deputy Inspector General of Police (DIG) without the prior approval of Inspector General (IG) - Respondent challenged Single Judge order before DB - DB set aside the Single Judge order on the ground that since the IG was not the appointing authority, the DIG being an officer superior to the appointing authority/Commandant had authority to pass the order of dismissal - Held, appointing authority is the Commandant u/r. 7(b) of the Rules and since the DIG is of higher rank, there is no illegality in the dismissal order passed by him - Just because the IG's approval is required for the purpose of appointment or promotion, the position of the Commandant as the appointing authority is not changed and the IG does not become the appointing authority - Therefore, the conclusion of the DB in holding that the order of dismissal passed by the DIG was legal, does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal disposed of.
