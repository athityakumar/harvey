John Vallamattom and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 538; (2003) 6 SCC 611; AIR 2003 SC 2902; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 51; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2188; 2003 (52) ALR 556; 2003 (4) AWC 2689; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 104; 2003 (2) CLR 578; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 44; JT 2003 (6) SC 37; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 49; 2003 (3) KLT 66; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 691; 2003 (2) RD 467; 2003(5) SCALE 384; 2003 (4) SLT 523; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 638; 2003 (5) Supreme 229

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, United Nations Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, 1966 (ENG), Charitable Uses Act, 1735, Mortmain and Charitable Uses Act, 1888

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 15, 25, 26, 32, 44 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 118 - Indian Succession Act, 1865 - Charitable Uses Act, 1735 - Mortmain and Charitable Uses Act, 1888 - United Nations Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, 1966 (ENG), art. 18 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.92 - Whether restriction imposed by s.118, Indian Succession Act, 1925 in practically preventing members of Christian community from bequeathing property for religious and charitable purposes is a reasonable one - Held, Restriction on person against bequeathing for religious or charitable uses, having nearer relative - Word 'relative', has no application to relationship by marriage, so restrictions u/s.118 does not apply - Near relations for the purpose of s.118 is to be determined according to the Table of Consanguinity, as per s.28 r/w Sch. I of the Act - Pre-constitutional law if found to be unconstitutional would by virtue of Art. 13 be void only from commencement of Constitution - Restriction placed by s.118 is only on Indian Christians - Not based on any intelligible differentia - s. 118 while restricting death bed Will, permits death bed gifts for any purpose - Places restriction on Christian having nephew or niece, but not on Christian having wife - Period of 12 months fixed by s. 118 for classification of testators has no nexus with purpose of Will - s.118 is therefore violative of Art. 14 - art.15 does not have application to s. 118 which restricts a right of a class of citizens in the matter of testamentary disposition who may belong to a particular religion - Rituals and ceremonies that are integral part of religion, are only protected by art. 25 - Disposition of property for religious and charitable uses is not integral part of Christian religion - Restrictions placed on Christians as regards disposition of property for religious or charitable purpose by Will, does not attract arts. 25, 26 - Premises behind art. 44 is that there is no necessary connection between religion and personal law in civilized society - s.118 of Act is anomalous, discriminatory and violative of arts. 14 should be struck down.
