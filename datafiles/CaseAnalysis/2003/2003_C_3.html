The New India Assurance Company Limited v C. Padma and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 737; (2003) 7 SCC 713; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1709; 2003 (3) ACC 319; AIR 2003 SC 4394; 2003 (6) ALD(SC) 94; 2003 (53) ALR 472; 2003 (6) AWC 4636; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 15; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 34; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 273; 2004 (136) PLR 156; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 274; 2003(7) SCALE 445; 2003 (1) SCW 5027; 2003 (5) SLT 557; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 677; 2003 (6) Supreme 701

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 110-A(3), 166(3) - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994, ss. 158(6), 53 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166(3) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6A - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - Claim - Petition filed after 6 years - Tribunal awarded compensation rejecting plea of limitation, based on old Act, raised by appellant - Held, an Act like Motor Vehicles Act is a beneficial legislation aimed at providing relief to victims or their families, if otherwise claim is found genuine - Secondly, it is a self contained Act which prescribes mode of filing application, procedure to be followed and award to be made - Further held, Parliament, in its wisdom, realised grave injustice and injury being caused to heirs and LRs of victims who suffer bodily injuries/die in accidents, by rejecting their claim petitions at threshold on ground of limitation, and purposely deleted s 166(3), which provided period of limitation for filing claim petitions and this being intendment of Legislature to give effective relief to victims and families of motor accidents untrammeled by technicalities of limitation, invoking of art 137, Limitation Act would defeat intendment of Legislature.
