Union of India v Kuldeep Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1386; (2004) 2 SCC 590; (2004) SCC (Cr) 597; AIR 2004 SC 827; 2004 CRLJ 836; JT 2003 (9) SC 584; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 89; 2003(10) SCALE 496; 2003 (1) SCW 7045; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 25(A), 25A, 29, 57, 9(A), 9A - Conviction - Sustainability - Culpable Homicide - Respondent was found guilty of offences punishable u/s. 9A r/w s. 25A and 9Ar/w s. 25A r/w s. 29 of the Act - UOI questioned legality, desirability and proprietary of reducing sentence after conviction as done by HC in impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, when volume of contraband articles was taken note of, it was sufficient for conclusion that quantity of finished product out of it which would have been extracted it would had been nearly 300 kilograms of heroin, and accused would have got about forty kilograms as admitted - HC seemed to have been swayed by age of accused's father, his family problems and more importantly he being not 'habitual offender' - Such considerations were meaningless when fact that accused was in possession of contrabands which would have destroyed health and mental equilibrium of thousands of people was considered - Court was not dealing with accused charged with commission of any minor offence where he being not habitual offender may have some relevance but it was really inconsequential for drug trafficker and smuggler - Hence, reasons given by HC to reduce sentence had no foundation and sentence imposed by Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
