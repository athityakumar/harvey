Union of India & Ors. v A.P. Bajpai & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 28; (2003) SCC (L&S) 182; (2003) 2 SCC 433; AIR 2003 SC 923; 2003 (2) AWC 882; 2003 (19) CLR 620; 2003 (1) ESC 57; 2003 (96) FLR 686; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 71; JT 2003 (1) SC 454; 2003 LabIC 743; 2003 (1) LLJ 847; 2003 (1) LLN 769; 2003(1) SCALE 349; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 429; 2003 (1) SCW 399; 2003 (2) SLJ 202; 2003 (2) SLR 1; 2003 (1) SLT 408; 2003 (1) Supreme 485; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 572

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1965 - Rule 5(1) - respondent appointed as a temporary employee - order or termination simpliciter passed on account of his negligence and dereliction of duties - on appeal Central Administrative Tribunal set aside the order of termination of respondent's services and declared him to be in continued service with all consequential benefits - appeal - Held, the Tribunal committed a serious error in law and facts of the present case in concluding that the order or termination of services of the respondent no.1 involved stigma attached to him. The order of termination of his services was simpliciter without attaching any stigma to the conduct of respondent no.1. The impugned order cannot be sustained and is set aside.
