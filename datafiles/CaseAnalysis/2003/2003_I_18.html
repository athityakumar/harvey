Food Corporation of India v Indian Council of Arbitration and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 522; (2003) 6 SCC 564; AIR 2003 SC 3011; 2003 (52) ALR 570; 2003 (51) ARBLR 692; 2003 (5) AWC 3567; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 170; JT 2003 (5) SC 480; 2003 (2) RAJ 511; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 411; 2003(5) SCALE 276; 2003 (4) SLT 473; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 568; 2003 (5) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, ss.11, 11(6), 16 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Indian Council of Arbitration Rules, - (1) Whether under existing arbitration cl dispute between parties is arbitrable in accordance with I C A Rules - (2) Whether Registrar, I C A has any jurisdiction to direct claimant to get fresh agreement signed - If so, what is effect of failure to obtain such fresh agreement and in such case which will be forum for adjudication of dispute between parties - (3) Whether Registrar, I C A, in asking for new requirement is justified in his decision not to proceed with case further, and also to ask petitioner F C I to seek approval of respondents no 2 and 3 for appointment of a common arbitrator upon F C I for obtaining consent from respondents nos 2 and 3 afresh in this respect - Held, questions relating to relevant scope, meaning, purport and effect of arbitration cl found in agreement between parties concerned and legality or propriety of constitution of Arbitral Tribunal, in view of rr 21, 22, ICA Rules as well as question relating to alleged contradictions or inconsistencies among those provisions are matters which go to jurisdiction of Arbitral Tribunal or as to existence or validity of arbitration agreement itself which, as enjoined u/s. 16 of 1996 Act, falls within jurisdiction of Arbitral Tribunal constituted which has been enabled to adjudicate on such question also before embarking upon an exercise to decide dispute between parties or decide them simultaneously - This is inescapable position which inevitably flows not only from statutory provisions contained in s 16 of 1996 Act, but that such position came to be firmly settled by more than one decision of this Court, including one rendered by Constitution Bench, noticed above.
