Shiv Shakti Coop. Housing Society, Nagpur v M/s. Swaraj Developers and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 406; (2003) 6 SCC 659; AIR 2003 SC 2434; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2133; 2003 (51) ALR 509; 2003 (2) ARC 1; 2003 (3) AWC 2198; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 380; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 38; 2003 ILR(Kar) 3008; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 22; JT 2003 (4) SC 255; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 228; 2003 (2) KLT 503; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 170; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 676; 2003 (2) RD 103; 2003(4) SCALE 241; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 762; 2003 (3) SLT 287; 2003 (3) Supreme 729; 2003 (2) UC 1047

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Civil Procedure Code (Amendment) Act, 1999

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss.96, 100, 115, o.43, r. 1 - Civil Procedure Code (Amendment) Act, 1999, ss.12(i), 32(2)(i) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s.6 - Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976, s.97(3) - Effect of amendment to s. 115 - Scope of appeal and revision - Held, right of appeal is a substantive right, but there is no such substantive right in making an application u/s. 115 - Where there is inherent right in every person to file a suit and for its maintainability it requires no authority of law, but appeal requires so - A plain reading of s.115 as it stands makes it clear that stress is on question whether order in favour of party applying for revision would have given finality to suit or other proceeding - If answer is 'yes' then revision is maintainable - But on contrary, if answer is 'no' then revision is not maintainable - Therefore, if impugned order is interim in nature or does not finally decide lis, revision will not be maintainable - HC were right in conclusion about non-maintainability of revision applications - Appeals dismissed.
