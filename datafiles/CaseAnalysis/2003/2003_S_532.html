Babudas v State of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 378; (2003) 9 SCC 86; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1749; 2003 (2) CCR 116; 2003 (2) Crimes 520; 2003 (5) CRJ 610; 2003 CRLJ 2536; 2003 (2) JCC 973; JT 2003 (4) SC 289; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 871; 2003(4) SCALE 389; 2003 (1) SCW 2483; 2003 (3) SLT 385; 2003 (3) Supreme 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - ss. 302 r/w 34 and 201 r/w 34 - offences under - Evidence Act - s. 114(a) - held, a presumption under Section 114(a) could be drawn only if the factum of recovery is proved beyond reasonable doubt which in this case is not done because the recoveries are highly doubtful. Therefore, on such doubtful recoveries, a presumption as to the guilt of the accused cannot be drawn. Further, in a case of circumstantial evidence, a false alibi set up by the accused would be a link in the chain of circumstances as held by the Supreme Court in the case of Mani Kumar Thapa (supra) but then it cannot be the sole link or the sole circumstance based on which a conviction could be passed. - The prosecution has failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant is responsible for the murder of the deceased.
