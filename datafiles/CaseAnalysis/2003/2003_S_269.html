Javed & Ors v State Of Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 561; (2003) 8 SCC 369; AIR 2003 SC 3057; 2003 (53) ALR 140; 2003 (4) AWC 2920; JT 2003 (6) SC 283; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 793; 2003(5) SCALE 602; 2003 (1) SCW 3892; 2003 (4) SLT 683; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 947; 2003 (5) Supreme 371

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Writ petitions and Civil appeals dismissed

Summary: Haryana Panchayati Raj Act, 1994 - ss. 175(1)(q), 177(1) - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 13, 14, 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 21, 25, 226, 227, 243G, 246 - Haryana Municipal Act, 1973 - s. 13A - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - ss. 123(5), 124(5) - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964 - r. 21 - Vires of the provisions of ss. 175(1)(q) and 177(1), Haryana Panchayati Raj Act, 1994 - Provision disqualifying a person having more than two living children from holding specified offices in Panchayats - Object of the enactment is to popularize Family Welfare/Family Planning Programme - Held, the right to contest an election for any office in Panchayat is neither fundamental nor a common law right. It is the creature of a statute and is obviously subject to qualifications and disqualifications enacted by legislation. A practice does not acquire the sanction of religion simply because it is permitted. Assuming the practice of having more wives than one or procreating more children than one is a practice followed by any community or group of people, the same can be regulated or prohibited by legislation in the interest of public order, morality and health or by any law providing for social welfare and reform which the impugned legislation clearly does - Further held, the challenge to the constitutional validity of ss. 175(1)(q) and 177(1) fails on all the counts. Both the provisions are held intra vires the Constitution. The provisions are salutary and in public interest. All the petitions which challenge the constitutional validity of the abovesaid provisions are held liable to be dismissed.
