Krishnan and Another v State Represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 551; (2003) 7 SCC 56; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1577; AIR 2003 SC 2978; 2003 (3) CCR 53; 2003 (3) Crimes 197; 2003 (6) CRJ 559; 2003 CRLJ 3705; 2003 (2) JCC 1235; JT 2003 (6) SC 151; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 600; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 885; 2003(5) SCALE 484; 2003 (4) SLT 613; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 771; 2003 (5) Supreme 173

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120A, 149, 208, 302, 323, 324, 326, 34, 341 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s.394 - Credibility of ocular evidence - Applicability of s 34 - Held, it would be erroneous to accord undue primacy to hypothetical answers of medical witnesses to exclude eye-witnesses' account which had to be tested independently and not treated as 'variable' keeping medical evidence as 'constant' - Eye witnesses' account would require a careful independent assessment and evaluation for their credibility which should not be adversely prejudged making any other evidence, including medical evidence, as sole touchstone for test of such credibility evidence must be tested for its inherent consistency and inherent probability of story - Consistency with account of other witnesses held to be credit-worthy - Consistency with undisputed facts 'credit' of witnesses - Their performance in witness-box, their power of observation - Then probative value of such evidence becomes eligible to be put into scales for a cumulative evaluation - Further held, applicability of s 34 depends upon facts and circumstances of each case - For applicability of section, it is not necessary that acts of several persons charged with commission of an offence jointly, must be same or identically similar acts may be different in character, but must have been actuated by one and same common intention in order to attract provision.
