State of A.P. and others v D. Dastagiri and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 390; (2003) 5 SCC 373; (2003) SCC (L&S) 708; AIR 2003 SC 2475; 2003 (2) CLR 380; 2003 (3) ESC 291; 2003 (97) FLR 805; JT 2003 (4) SC 273; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 91; 2003 (2) LLN 1101; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 95; 2003(4) SCALE 281; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 877; 2003 (2) SLJ 303; 2003 (3) SLR 454; 2003 (3) SLT 417; 2003 (3) Supreme 605; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1697

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions

Summary: Service - Recruitment - Contention that process of selection was cancelled at last stage, i e, before publishing list of selected candidates on sole ground that State Govt. wanted to introduce prohibition and Govt. felt that there was no need of Excise Constables during imposition of prohibition in State - Held, in absence of publication of select list, selection process was not complete - Even if selection process was complete and assuming that only select list was remained to be published, that does not advance case of respondents for simple reason that even candidates who are selected and whose names find place in select list, do not get vested right to claim appointment based on select list - Further, it was open to State Govt. to take a policy decision either to have prohibition or not to have prohibition in State - When it was open to Govt. to take a policy decision, respondents cannot dub action of Govt. as arbitrary, particularly, when they did not have any right as such to claim appointments - Appeal allowed.
