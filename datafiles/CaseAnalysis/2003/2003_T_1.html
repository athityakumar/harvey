Ashok Kumar Pandey v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1010; (2004) 3 SCC 349; AIR 2004 SC 280; 2003 (4) CCR 465; 2003 (4) Crimes 532; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 116; JT 2003 (9) SC 140; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 940; 2003(9) SCALE 741; 2003 (1) SCW 6105; 2003 (7) SLT 343; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 716; 2003 (8) Supreme 299

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - PIL - Conversion of death sentence to life sentence - Whether violation of fundamental right made out so as to sustain petition u/art. 32 - Held, art 32 empowers this court to issue directions or orders or writs for the enforcement of any of the fundamental rights - The petitioner did not seek to enforce any of his fundamental rights nor did he complain that any of his fundamental right was violated - He sought to enforce the fundamental rights of others, namely, the two condemned convicts who themselves did not complain of their violation - Ordinarily, the aggrieved party which is affected by any order has the right to seek redress by questioning the legality, validity or correctness of the order, unless such party is a minor, an insane person or is suffering from any other disability which the law recognises as sufficient to permit another person, e g next friend, to move the court on his behalf - Further held, neither under the provisions of the Code nor under any other statute is a third party stranger permitted to question the correctness of the conviction and sentence - Petition dismissed.
