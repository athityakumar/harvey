Uday v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 190; (2003) 4 SCC 46; (2003) SCC (Cr) 775; AIR 2003 SC 1639; AIR 2003 SCW 1035; 2003 (1) CCR 376; 2003 (2) Crimes 176; 2003 (4) CRJ 640; 2003 CRLJ 1539; 2003 (1) JCC 506; JT 2003 (2) SC 243; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 565; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 99; 2003(2) SCALE 329; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 231; 2003 (2) SLT 131; 2003 (2) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S. 90 - whether consent given by the prosecutrix to sexual intercourse is voluntary, or whether it was given under a misconception of fact - In a case of this nature two conditions must be fulfilled for the application of Section 90 IPC. Firstly, it must be shown that the consent was given under a misconception of fact. Secondly, it must be proved that the person who obtained the consent knew, or had reason to believe that the consent was given in consequence of such misconception. - Held, all the circumstances lead to the conclusion that the prosecutrix freely, voluntarily, and consciously consented to having sexual intercourse with the appellant, and her consent was not in consequence of any misconception of fact.
