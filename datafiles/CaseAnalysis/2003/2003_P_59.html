Union of India v Paul Manickam and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 828; (2003) 8 SCC 342; (2004) SCC (Cr) 239; AIR 2003 SC 4622; 2003 (4) CCR 184; 2003 (4) Crimes 332; 2003 CRLJ 4561; 2003 (90) ECC 1; 2003 (111) ECR 288; 2003 (162) E.L.T. 6; 2003 (3) JCC 1912; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 503; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 509; 2004 (1) KLT 364; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 927; 2003(8) SCALE 515; 2003 (1) SCW 5423; 2003 (6) SLT 193; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 618; 2003 (7) Supreme 370

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 4(1)(i) - Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992 - Constitution of India, 1950, s. art.22(5) - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992 - Detention - Contraband articles seized from detenue - Representation to President of India - Not disclosed in Writ - Held, in order to invoke jurisdiction under art 32 of Constitution to approach this Court directly, it has to be shown by petitioner as to why HC has not been approached, could not be approached or it is futile to approach HC - Unless satisfactory reasons are indicated in this regard, filing of petition on such matters, directly under art 32 of Constitution is to be discouraged - Further held, President as well as Governor, no doubt are constitutional Heads of respective Govts but day to day administration at respective levels are carried on by Heads of Department-Ministries concerned and designated officers who alone are ultimately responsible and accountable for action taken or to be taken in a given case - If really citizen concerned genuinely and honestly felt or interested in getting an expeditious considerations or disposal of his grievance, he would and should honestly approach really concerned authorities and would not adopt any dubious devices with sole aim of deliberately creating a situation for delay in consideration and cry for relief on his own manipulated ground, by directing his representation to an authority which is not directly immediately concerned with such consideration.
