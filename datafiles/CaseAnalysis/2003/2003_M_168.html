State Of Manipur v Md. Rajaodin

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 661; (2003) 7 SCC 511; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1070; AIR 2003 SC 3794; 2004 (1) AWC 143; 2003 (3) CLR 963; 2004 (1) ESC 1; 2003 (99) FLR 337; JT 2003 (8) SC 57; 2003 LabIC 3148; 2003(6) SCALE 796; 2004 (1) SLJ 247; 2003 (6) SLR 696; 2003 (5) SLT 246; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 107; 2003 (6) Supreme 256; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 221

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Government servant

Summary: Service - Appointment under die-in-harness scheme - Letter issued by the State to the respondent but later denied appointment in view of the ban imposed on appointments and belated claim of the respondent - Held, when the case of the respondent is considered in the panorama of legal principles, the inevitable conclusion is that he was not entitled for appointment. Even after the 1984 scheme came into force, the application was filed after a long lapse of time. He, therefore, had no right much less and legal right to ask for an appointment.
