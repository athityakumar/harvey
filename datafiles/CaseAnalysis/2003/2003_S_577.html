Prabha Shankar Dubey v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1450; (2004) 2 SCC 56; (2004) SCC (Cr) 420; AIR 2004 SC 486; JT 2003 (10) SC 50; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 104; 2003(10) SCALE 420; 2003 (1) SCW 6592; 2004 (1) ShimLC 240; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 50 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Accused apprised that can be taken to a Magistrate or a gazetted officer for search - Opted to be searched by investigating officer - Opium found on their person - Trial Court held testimony of investigating officer to be reliable and that there was full compliance with statutory requirements contained in ss. 42, 50, 55 and 57 of NDPS Act, 1985 - Whether there was compliance with s. 50 of NDPS Act, 1985? - Held, there is no specific form prescribed or intended for conveying information required to be given u/s. 50 - Necessary that accused should be made aware of existence of his right to be searched in presence of one of officers named in s. 50 - Court has to see substance and not form of intimation - Whether requirements of s. 50 have been met has to be decided on facts of each case and there cannot be any sweeping generalization - S. 50 does not involve self-incrimination - Object seems is to ensure fair play and transparency in process of search - That there is no substance in plea that there was non-compliance with requirements of s. 50 of NDPS Act, 1985 - Appeal dismissed.
