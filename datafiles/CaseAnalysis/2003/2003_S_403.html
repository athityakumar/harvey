Ratansinh Dalsukhbhai Nayak v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 882; (2004) SCC (Cr) 7; (2004) 1 SCC 64; AIR 2004 SC 23; 2003 (4) CCR 384; 2003 (4) Crimes 387; 2004 CRLJ 19; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 949; 2003 (3) JCC 1659; JT 2003 (8) SC 53; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 970; 2003(9) SCALE 14; 2003 (1) SCW 5547; 2003 (7) SLT 214; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1030; 2003 (7) Supreme 535

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1871 - s. 118 - Child witness - High Court confirmed the conviction and sentence imposed by the Trial Court - Acceptability of evidence of child witness - Held, a child of tender age can be allowed to testify if he has intellectual capacity to understand questions and give rational answers thereto - Further held, the witness was cross-examined at length and in spite thereof she had described in detail the scenario implicating the accused to be author of the crime. Evidence of the child witness has credibility which reveals a truthful approach and her evidence to put it malady has ring of truthing. There are no exaggerations and she has stuck to her statement made during investigation in all material particulars. That being so, the trial court and the High Court were justified in placing implicit reliance on her testimony.
