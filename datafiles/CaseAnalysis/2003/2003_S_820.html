Uttar Pradesh Public Services Commission v Subhash Chandra Dixit and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 948; (2003) 12 SCC 701; AIR 2004 SC 163; 2003 (53) ALR 815; 2004 (2) AWC 1385; 2004 (1) CLR 276; 2004 (101) FLR 698; JT 2003 (8) SC 531; 2003 LabIC 3712; 2004 (1) LLN 101; 2003(9) SCALE 335; 2003 (1) SCW 5844; 2004 (1) SLR 324; 2003 (7) SLT 688; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 210; 2003 (8) Supreme 375; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 360

Case Digest

Subject: Competition; Constitution

Keywords: Procedural Law, Election Commission, Election Commissioner

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 233, 234, 309, 320, 324 - Uttar Pradesh Public Services Commission Act, 1974, r. 51 - Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission of Procedure & Conduct of Business) Amendment Act, 1976, r. 51 - U P P S C applied a system of scaling of marks awarded by examiners who valued answer papers - Examination system assailed on ground that introduction of scaling of marks was arbitrary and illegal - DB held that application of scaling was not justified nor support by any valid statutory permission - Held, proviso to r 51 is in no way in conflict with r 19, Niyamavali 1951 - Further held, scaling system was adopted with a view to eliminate inconsistency in marking standards of examiners - If any candidate had secured higher marks in written examination, even by applying scaling formula, he would still be benefited - Further, application of scaling formula to examinations in question was neither arbitrary or illegal selection of candidates was done in a better way - Moreover, this formula was adopted by UPPSC after an expert study and in such matters, Court cannot sit in judgment and interfere with same unless it is proved itself was done contrary to rules - Ultimately, agency conducting examination has to be consider as to which method should be preferred and adopted having regard to myriad situations that may arise before them.
