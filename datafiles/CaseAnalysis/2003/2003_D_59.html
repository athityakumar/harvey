Cci Chambers Co-Op. Hsg. Society Ltd. v Development Credit Bank Ltd

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 670; (2003) 7 SCC 233; AIR 2004 SC 184; 2003 (53) ALR 429; 2003 (5) AWC 4505; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 453; 2003 CCC 660; 2004 (1) CLR 8; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 118; 2003 (3) CPJ 9; 2003 (3) CPJ(Sc) 9; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 113; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 181; 2003(7) SCALE 34; 2003 (1) SCW 5887; 2003 (5) SLT 185; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 139; 2003 (6) Supreme 513; 2004 (1) UC 88

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - s. 23 - Bank - Deficiency of service - Bank debited money from the complainants account by honouring cheques with forged signatures - Held, the decision arrived at by the NCDRC is premature. The Commission ought to have issued notice to the respondent and taken its pleadings on record. Only when the pleadings for both parties were available should the Commission have formed an opinion as to the nature and scope of enquiry, i.e. whether the questions arising for decision in the light of the pleadings of the parties required a detailed and complicated investigation into the facts which was incapable of being undertaken in a summary and speedy manner. Then the Commission could have justifiably formed an opinion on the need of driving away the complainant to the Civil Court. Mere complicated nature of the facts and law arising for decision would not be decisive - Matter sent back to the Commission for fresh hearing.
