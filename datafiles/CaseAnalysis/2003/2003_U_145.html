Mithilesh Singh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 209; (2003) SCC (L&S) 271; (2003) 3 SCC 309; AIR 2003 SC 1724; 2003 (2) AWC 1250; 2003 (1) CLR 825; 2004 (4) ESC 647; 2003 (97) FLR 122; JT 2003 (2) SC 264; 2003 LabIC 1152; 2003 (2) LLN 319; 2003(2) SCALE 467; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 377; 2003 (3) SLJ 27; 2003 (2) SLR 620; 2003 (2) SLT 318; 2003 (2) Supreme 302; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 911

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Railway Protection Force Act, 1957 - Railway Protection Force Rules, 1959 - Rule 44 - challenge to punishment from removal from service as awarded by the disciplinary authority and maintained by the Division Bench of the Guwahati High Court - Held, absence from duty without proper intimation is indicated to be a grave offence warranting removal from service. Therefore, mere making an application for leave cannot be construed to be of any consequence in the background of the strict requirement of giving proper intimation. - It is not a sound principle of construction to brush aside word(s) in a statute as being inapposite surplusage; if they can have appropriate application in circumstances conceivably within the contemplation of the statute. The authorities were, therefore, justified in holding that the appellant was guilty of the offence of absence from duty without proper intimation.
