Imdad Ali v Keshav Chand and others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 214; (2003) 4 SCC 635; AIR 2003 SC 1863; 2003 (51) ALR 58; 2003 (2) CGLJ 23; JT 2003 (5) SC 502; 2003 (1) RCJ 171; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 402; 2003(2) SCALE 494; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 259; 2003 (2) SLT 507; 2003 (3) Supreme 276

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - S. 12(3) - whether the heirs of a tenant can be deprived of the benefit of proviso to sub-section (3) of Section 12 of the Act if the heir's father from whom they inherited the tenancy rights had availed of the benefit of proviso of sub-section (3) of Section 12 of the Act - held, a person by reason of inheritance or assignment does not derive any better title than his predecessor, and, thus, the right which the original tenant did not possess cannot be passed on to his successor. Therefore, once the father of the respondents had availed of the benefit of proviso to sub-section (3) of Section 12 of the Act, the said benefit was not available to the respondent-tenants on committing a further default in payment of rent.
