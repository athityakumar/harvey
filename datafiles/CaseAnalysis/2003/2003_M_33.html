Bakhtawar Trust and others v M.D. Narayan and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 471; (2003) 5 SCC 298; AIR 2003 SC 2236; JT 2004 (6) SC 587; 2003(4) SCALE 590; 2003 (3) SLT 793; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 1; 2003 (3) Supreme 800

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Bangalore City Planning Area Zonal Regulations (Amendment & Validation) Act, 1996, (the Act), ss.13, 2, 2(15), 4 - Karnataka Town & Country Planning Act, 1965, ss.13, 13(4), 14, 3(1), 3(3), 4-A, 4-C - Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961 - Mysore Act, 1971, s.23 - Constitutional validity of the Act - Whether Act has removed basis of judgment of HC or it, without amending basis, has purported to nullify judicial decree per se and, therefore, such an Act is ultra vires competence of State Legislature - (I) Held, where a legislature validates an executive action repugnant to statutory provisions declared by a Court of law, what legislature is required to do is first to remove very basis of invalidity and then validate executive action - In order to validate an executive action or any provision of a statute, it is not sufficient for legislature to declare that a judicial pronouncement given by a Court of law would not be binding, as legislature does not possess that power - In instant case, legislature has not merely negated effect of any prior judgment; but it has removed actual basis upon which judgment was based and thereafter validated actions, retrospectively impugned Act is, therefore, constitutionally valid - (II) Further held, where a law is retrospectively amended, consequences of such retrospective amendment are that all actions have to proceed on premise that law, as amended, was always law in force - Appeal allowed.
