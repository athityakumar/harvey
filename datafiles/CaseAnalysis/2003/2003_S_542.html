South Eastern Coalfields Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 866; (2003) 8 SCC 648; AIR 2003 SC 4482; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 135; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 443; 2003(8) SCALE 600; 2003 (1) SCW 5258; 2003 (7) SLT 664; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 651; 2003 (7) Supreme 539

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: [A.] Mines & Minerals - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 31, 64A - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 4, 9(3), 13 - Vires of s 9(3) - Doctrine of restitution - Royalty on coal enhanced - Liability of coalfields to pay interest to State Govt - Liability of consumers/purchasers to pay interest to coalfields - Held, coalfields, i e mining lessees, are bound to pay interest as per terms of mining leases incorporating cl for payment of interest consistently with r 64 A, Mineral Concession Rules - In absence of there being a prohibition either in law or in contract entered into between two parties, there is no reason why coalfields should not be compensated by payment of interest for period for which consumers/purchasers did not pay amount of enhanced royalty which is a constituent part of price of mineral for period for which it remained unpaid It will be a travesty of justice to hold that that though coalfields must pay amount of interest to State but consumers/purchasers in whose hands money was actually withheld be exonerated from liability to pay interest - Further, once doctrine of restitution is attracted, interest is often a normal relief given in restitution Such interest is not controlled by provisions of Interest Act, 1839 or 1978 - Rate of interest reduced.
