Jameel Ahmed and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 416; (2003) 9 SCC 673; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1853; AIR 2004 SC 588; 2003 (2) CCR 139; 2003 (3) Crimes 53; 2003 (5) CRJ 642; JT 2003 (4) SC 294; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 844; 2003(4) SCALE 402; 2003 (1) SCW 6078; 2003 (3) SLT 481; 2003 (3) Supreme 777

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Miscellaneous; Petroleum & Gas; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss.15, 15(1), 15(3), 3(3), 6, 6(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120B, 286 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss.9-B(i)(b), 9B, 9C - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss.4, 5, 6 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss.229, 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss.24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, rr. 15, 15(5) - Appeal agianst conviction and sentence - Held, an acceptable confession of a co-accused can be used as a corroborative piece of evidence in a trial under TADA Act, even to corroborate a confession of another accused in same trial - Prosecution has produced material which could be treated as evidence generally corroborating Ex P-126 which is sufficient to establish guilt of A-5 - However, there is no evidence even indirect, other than solitary statement found in confession of A-2 that A-6 had knowledge that delivery of explosives was for and on behalf of Punjab terrorists - Prosecution having failed to produce any corroborative evidence in addition to confessional statement of A-2 for relying on confession of A-2 to base a conviction on co-accused, it is difficult to uphold conviction of A-6 - Conviction and sentence of A-5 confirmed.
