Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research and another v A.P. Wasan and others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 217; (2003) 5 SCC 321; (2003) SCC (L&S) 651; AIR 2003 SC 1831; 2003 (5) ESC 232; 2003 (98) FLR 479; JT 2003 (2) SC 431; 2003 LabIC 1181; 2003 (2) LLN 1143; 2003(2) SCALE 542; 2003 (2) SLR 688; 2003 (2) SLT 747; 2003 (2) Supreme 452; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1109

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Post-Graduate Institute Of Medical Education And Research, Chandigarh, Act, 1966, Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh, Rules, 1967, Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh Regulations, 1967

Summary: Service - Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh Act, 1966 - Promotion - Whether, in 1980 promotions within the Engineering Department were to be made seniority wise or cadre wise? - Held, the appellant Institute did not rely on the prescribed qualifications for posting persons to different sections - Therefore, the placement in a particular section was fortuitous - Since, all sections of the Engineering Department did not have a Technologist Grade I post, a person's claims of promotion could be effectively curtailed by such fortuitous circumstances - Further held, the posts of Technologists Grade I and II were not described with reference to any particular s.and even in the revised cadre the absence of any description continued - Therefore, there may be a new nomenclature for the post of Technologist Grade I, but this should not deprive him of the right that he had to be considered in respect of the vacancies which existed in 1980 and subsequently in the post of Technologist Grade I or its equivalent - No infirmity in the order of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
