Union of India and Others v Rajesh P.U. Puthuvalnikathu and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 557; (2003) 7 SCC 285; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1048; AIR 2003 SC 4222; 2003 (4) ESC 408; 2003 (98) FLR 980; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 130; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 590; 2003 (3) KLT 392; 2003(5) SCALE 560; 2003 (1) SCW 3916; 2004 (1) SLJ 306; 2003 (6) SLR 590; 2003 (7) SLT 507; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 883; 2003 (5) Supreme 283

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Selection process for appointment already conducted - List of selected candidates cancelled due to irregularities in selection process in valuation of answer sheets - Held, in absence of any specific or categorical finding supported by any concrete and relevant material that widespread infirmities of all pervasive nature, which could be really said to have undermined very process itself in its entirety or as a whole or it was impossible to weed out beneficiaries of one or other of irregularities, or illegalities, there was hardly any justification in law to deny appointment to other selected candidates whose selections were not found to be, in any manner, vitiated for any one or other reasons - Further held, competent authority completely misdirected itself in taking such an extreme and unreasonable decision of canceling entire selections, wholly unwarranted and unnecessary even on factual situation, and totally in excess of nature and gravity of what was at stage, thereby virtually rendering such decision to be irrational appointments shall be made within 60 days from this day, without any further delay.
