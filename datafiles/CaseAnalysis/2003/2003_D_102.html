K.R. Indira v Dr. G. Adinarayana

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 854; (2003) 8 SCC 300; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2002; AIR 2003 SC 4689; 2005 (3) BC 384; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 407; 2003 (4) Crimes 425; 2004 CRLJ 5; 2003 (3) JCC 273; JT 2003 (8) SC 71; 2003 (3) KLT 1042; 2004 (136) PLR 63; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 966; 2003(8) SCALE 730; 2003 (1) SCW 5501; 2003 (6) SLT 330; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 535; 2003 (7) Supreme 741; 2004 (1) UC 394

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Trade

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - ss. 138, 139 - Appeals dismissed by the Trial Court and the High Court on the ground that the common notice was not in accordance with law, more particularly, s. 138(b) - Held, the notice in question is imperfect not because it had any further or additional claims as well, but it did not specifically contain any demand for the payment of the cheque amount, the non-compliance with such a demand only being the incriminating circumstances which expose the drawer for being proceeded against the under s. 138 of the Act - Further held, the ultimate conclusion arrived at by the Trial Court and the High Court do not call for interference.
