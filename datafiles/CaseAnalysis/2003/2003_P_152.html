State of Karnataka v Puttaraja

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1071; (2004) SCC (Cr) 300; (2004) 1 SCC 475; AIR 2004 SC 433; 2003 (4) CCR 461; 2003 (4) Crimes 548; 2004 CRLJ 579; JT 2003 (9) SC 603; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 113; 2003(10) SCALE 325; 2003 (1) SCW 6429; 2003 (7) SLT 329; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 274; 2003 (8) Supreme 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 228-A, 376, 376-A, 376-B, 376-C, 376-D - Rape - High Court reduced the sentence imposed by the Trial Court to 46 days - Held, this is a case where there was no scope for awarding sentence lesser than prescribed minimum and it should have been highest prescribed - Further held, since the State had not questioned the sufficiency of sentence of 5 years imposed by the Trial Court before the High Court, the sentence imposed by the Trial Court is restored.
