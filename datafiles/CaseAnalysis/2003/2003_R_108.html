State v Ram Saran

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1397; (2003) 12 SCC 578; AIR 2004 SC 481; 2004 (101) FLR 447; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 220; JT 2003 (9) SC 578; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 100; 2003(10) SCALE 454; 2003 (1) SCW 6585; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 11, 12, 13, 5, 16(2) and 251 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226, Entry 2 of List I of VII Schedule, Entry 93 of List I - Central Reserve Police Force Act, ss. 10(m), 3(1), 4, 5, 9, 10 and 19(m), rr. 36(a),(b),(e) and (f) - Unauthorised absence of an employee staying beyond sanctioned period of leave - (A) Whether Asst. Commandant of CRPF could have exercised powers of Judicial Magistrate Ist Class and convict and sentence respondent? - U/s. 3(1) of CRPF Act, CRPF is constituted to be an 'armed force' maintained by Central Govt, and thus it is 'any other armed forces of union' under in Entry 2 of List I of VII Schedule to Constitution - Entry 93 of List I enables Parliament to provide for offences against laws with respect to any of matters in List I - S. 16(2) of CRPF Act contains specific 'non obstante' clause that notwithstanding anything contained in CrPC, Central Govt. may invest Commandant or Asst. Commandant with powers of a Magistrate for purpose of inquiring into or trying any offence committed by a member of force and punishable under CRPF Act - Held, that while exercising appellate or revisional jurisdiction under CrPC it is impermissible for any court to decide on vires of provision as done by Sessions Judge and HC - Vires of a provision can only be questioned in a writ proceeding before Constitutional Court - Thus neither Sessions Judge nor HC could have found fault with exercise of jurisdiction by Asst. Commandant in exercising Magisterial powers - Further held, Asst. Commandant was clothed with necessary jurisdiction for trial of matter - (B) What would be an appropriate sentence? - Punishment altered - Fine of two months pay which respondent was drawing at time when proceedings were initiated - Deterrent punishment must be resorted to when such absence is resorted to avoid and evade undertaking a testing or trying venture or deployment essential at any given point of time, and not as a routine in normal course - Appeal allowed.
