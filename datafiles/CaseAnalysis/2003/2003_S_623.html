 Prafulla Kumar Das and Others with Pradip Chandra v State of Orissa and Others with Pramod Chandra

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 829; (2003) 11 SCC 614; (2004) SCC (L&S) 121; AIR 2003 SC 4506; 2003 (99) FLR 858; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 262; JT 2003 (9) SC 477; 2003(8) SCALE 398; 2003 (1) SCW 5212; 2003 (6) SLR 426; 2003 (6) SLT 424; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 301; 2003 (7) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Criteria For Promotion, Writ Petition and Appeal dismissed

Summary: Orissa Administrative Service, Class-II (Appointment of Officers Validation) Amendment Act, 1992 - Orissa Administrative Service, Class-II (Appointment of Officers Validation) Act, 1987 - s. 2 - Validity of s. 2 - Dispute regarding the concept of year of allotment - Held, if a balance is sought to be struck by reason of the impugned legislation, it would not be permissible for this Court to declare it ultra vires only because it may cause some hardship to the petitioners. A mere hardship cannot be a ground for striking down a valid legislation, unless it is held to be suffering from the vice of discrimination or unreasonableness. A valid piece of legislation, can be struck down only if it is found to be ultra vires art. 14 of the Constitution of India and not otherwise - Further held, we uphold the validity of the Orissa Administrative Service, Class-II (Appointment of Officers Validation) Amendment Act, 1999, and particularly s. 2 thereof.
