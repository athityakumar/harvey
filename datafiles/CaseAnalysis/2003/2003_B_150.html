Union of India and Another v British India Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1253; (2003) 9 SCC 505; 2004 (190) CTR 385; [2004] 268 ITR 481; [2004] 140 TAXMAN 357

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Business Profits Tax Rules, r. 4A - Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, ss. 11, 12 and 13 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 50 and 11(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 265 - Whether order of High Court directing refund to respondent-assessee, is sustainable? - Held, s. 50 of 1922 Act, as modified by r. 4A, is a provision of statute which was required to be applied by HC at the time of considering relief, if any - Claim being barred by time, Writ Petition could not have been allowed nor could any relief be granted - Money which was paid as provisional tax became returnable on the date on which right of revenue authorities to make a regular assessment u/s. 12 came to an end - Assuming that there was a right in respondent-assessee to reclaim this money, such a right ought to have been exercised within a period of three years under residuary art. 137 of Limitation Act, 1963, after which even such a right must be held to be barred - There is no doubt that by time assessee asserted his so called right, even this period had expired in respect of assessment years in question - Contention based on art. 265 of Constitution also must, therefore, fail - Contention of respondent must fail for principal reason that respondent's earlier contention that regular assessment was barred by time had succeeded - Basis for such success itself was that respondent's right to claim refund had become time barred u/s. 50 of 1922 Act, as modified by r. 4A - Appeal allowed.
