Rajasthan Financial Corporation v Man Industrial Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1281; (2003) 7 SCC 522; AIR 2003 SC 4273; 2003 (53) ALR 177; 2003 (5) AWC 4281; 2003 (2) CLR 649; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 110; JT 2003 (7) SC 486; 2003 (2) RD 616; 2003(6) SCALE 825; 2003 (6) Supreme 558; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2550

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 114, 115 and 151 - Nine years after execution proceedings application, u/s. 151 CPC, 1908 objecting calculation of interest with half yearly rests - Executing court overruled objections but High Court allowed in revision - Whether HC has exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 by revising/modifying decree? - Held, no substance in the submission that the half yearly rest was to apply only if the rate of interest was to be decided by the appellants - These words clearly applied to both the options - In the clarificatory portion the words on half yearly basis have not been mentioned because the portion is only clarifying how interest was to be calculated - This portion thus does not detract from the fact that the decree is in terms of the compromise deed - Merely because some other minor changes, which appear to be inadvertent changes, have crept in do not also detract from the fact that the decree is in terms of the compromise deed - No uncertainty in the decree - Appeal allowed.
