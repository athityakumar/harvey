Ram Narain Poply v Central Bureau of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.N. Agrawal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 57; (2003) 3 SCC 641; (2003) SCC (Cr) 869; AIR 2003 SC 2748; 2003 (1) CCR 201; 2003 (2) Crimes 312; 2003 (3) CRJ 641; 2003 CRLJ 4801; JT 2003 (1) SC 184; 2003(1) SCALE 171; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 119; 2003 (1) SCW 3119; 2003 (1) SLT 530; 2003 (1) Supreme 537

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Wrongful Gain Or Wrongful Loss, Banking Law, White Collar Crime, Crossed Cheques

Summary: Harshad Mehta's case - Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions In Security) Act, 1992 - IPC - Ss. 120B r/w Ss. 420, 409, 467 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - S. 13(1)(c) r/w S. 13(2) - five accused tried for offences of cheating, criminal breach of trust, forgery by using forged documents, abuse of public offices and dishonest misappropriation of public funds - Held, the offences of mis-appropriation, criminal breach of trust or fraud and forgery are required to be established by the prosecution on the basis of existing criminal jurisprudence, which requires that prosecution has to establish its case beyond reasonable doubt. Serious allegations would not be a ground for convicting the accused unless there is sufficient evidence to connect the accused with the crime. - The prosecution has failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt that accused have committed any offence.
