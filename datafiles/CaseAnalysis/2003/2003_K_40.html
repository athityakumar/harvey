State Bank of India and Ors. v K.P. Narayanan Kutty

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 39; (2003) SCC (L&S) 185; (2003) 2 SCC 449; AIR 2003 SC 1100; 2003 (96) FLR 998; JT 2003 (1) SC 479; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 324; 2003 LabIC 778; 2003 (2) LLJ 1; 2003 (1) LLN 839; 2003(1) SCALE 383; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 391; 2003 (1) SCW 634; 2003 (2) SLJ 36; 2003 (2) SLR 59; 2003 (1) SLT 457; 2003 (1) Supreme 778; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 568

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules, r. 50(3)(ii) - Respondent working as a Manager, Grade-I in appellant Bank - An order of dismissal from service passed against respondent on recommendations of disciplinary authority - On appeal HC allowed Writ accepting contention that no opportunity was given to respondent by disciplinary authority in regard to charges with which findings of Enquiry Officer were not agreed to by disciplinary authority - Held, principles of natural justice have to be read into Rules and delinquent officer will have to be given an opportunity to persuade disciplinary authority to accept favourable conclusion of Enquiry Officer - Difficult to accept contention of appellants that unless it is shown that some prejudice was caused to respondent, order of dismissal could not be set aside by HC - Judgment of SC in case of Punjab National Bank and others vs Kunj Behari Misra [1998 Indlaw SC 2019] followed - No good reason to upset impugned order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
