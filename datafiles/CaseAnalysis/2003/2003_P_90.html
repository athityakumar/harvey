Ramanand Yadav v Prabhu Nath Jha and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 921; (2003) 12 SCC 606; AIR 2004 SC 1053; 2004 CRLJ 640; JT 2003 (8) SC 404; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 469; 2003(9) SCALE 160; 2003 (1) SCW 6731; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 42

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Miscellaneous; Petroleum & Gas

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 149, 302 - Arms Act, 1959 - ss. 25A, 27 - Explosive Substance Act, 1908 - s. 3 - High Court 8 persons convicted and sentenced by the Trial Court - Held, the inevitable conclusion because of the factual and legal panorama is that the High Court was not justified in directing acquittal. The respondents are convicted under s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC, 1860, and are sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life.
