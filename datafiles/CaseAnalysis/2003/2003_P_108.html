Krishna Mohan Kul @ Nani Charan Kul and Another v Pratima Maity and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 729; (2004) 9 SCC 468; AIR 2003 SC 4351; 2004 (54) ALR 144; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 30; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 46; JT 2003 (10) SC 150; 2003(7) SCALE 310; 2003 (1) SCW 5142; 2003 (5) SLT 321; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 496; 2003 (7) Supreme 105

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - s. 100 - Deed of settlement - Suit for declaration and permanent injunction - Executant was more than 100 years of age at the time of alleged execution of the deed suffering from various physical and mental ailments - Validity of the settlement deed - Held, it is always obligatory for the donor/beneficiary under a document to prove due execution of the document in accordance with law, even de hors the reasonableness or otherwise of the transaction, to avail of the benefit or claim rights under the document irrespective of the fact whether such party is the defendant or plaintiff before Court - Further held, in case of an old, illiterate ailing person who is unable to comprehend the nature of the document or the contents thereof, it should be established that there was not mere physical act of the executant involved, but the mental act.
