Commissioner of Income Tax, Bhopal v M/s. Shelly Products and another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 477; (2003) 5 SCC 461; AIR 2003 SC 2532; 2003 (181) CTR 564; [2003] 261 ITR 367; JT 2003 (4) SC 528; 2003(4) SCALE 674; 2003 (3) SLT 698; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 79; 2003 (4) Supreme 192; 175 TAXATION 434; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 271

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.143(3), 144 B, 240, 4(1) - Direct Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 1987, s.240 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 265 - Finance Act - Whether on failure or inability of authorities to frame a regular assessment after earlier assessment is set aside or nullified, tax deposited by an assessee by way of advance tax or self assessment tax, or tax deducted at source is liable to be refunded to assessee, since its retention by revenue would result in breach of art 265 of Constitution of India, which prohibits levy or collection of any tax except by authority of law - Held, liability to pay income tax chargeable u/s. 4(1) of Act does not depend on assessment being made - As soon as Finance Act prescribes rate or rates for any assessment year, liability to pay tax arises and assessee is himself required to compute his total income and pay income tax thereon which involves a process of self-assessment - Since all this is done under authority of law, there is no scope for contending that art 265 is violated - Appeal allowed.
