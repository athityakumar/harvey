Government of Andhra Pradesh and another v Medwin Educational Society and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 971; (2004) 1 SCC 86; AIR 2004 SC 613; 2004 (1) ESC 90; JT 2003 (8) SC 567; 2003(9) SCALE 500; 2003 (1) SCW 6162; 2003 (8) SLR 500; 2004 (1) SLT 12; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 408; 2004 (1) Supreme 412; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 372

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug; Land & Property

Keywords: Delegation Of Power, Charitable or Religious entities

Summary: [A.] Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 10A, 10A(2)(b), 10A(7), 10B, 10B(3), 10C, 64 - Dental Council of India Act - Medical Council of India (Amendment) Act, 1993, s. 33 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956 - Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education Act, 1988, s. 11 - New Medical Colleges Regulations, 1999, reg. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Role of State Govt. in matter of grant of essentiality certificate for establishment of Medical or Dental college, especially in context of operative constitutional, legislative and statutory provisions - Held, HC has committed a manifest error in holding that state has no role to play in matter of identification of location of sites where medical colleges are proposed to be established - While granting an essentiality certificate, particularly having regard to local needs, state, has a positive role to play but same would not mean that State Govt's say is final as ultimately final recommendations have to be made by Medical Council of India and Dental Council of India, as case may be, whereafter final decision has to be taken by Central Govt. - Further held, for purpose of identifying sites art.371 D of Constitution may not have any application - Further, while rejecting an application for grant of essentiality certificate, state must comply with principles of natural justice which would include assigning of sufficient and cogent reasons.
