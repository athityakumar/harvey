Pratima Sinha and others v Shashi Kumar Narain Sinha and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1672; (2004) 13 SCC 599

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 115, O. 14 rr. 1, 2 - Bengal, Agra and Assam Civil Courts Act, 1887, s. 21 - Right of pre emption - Maintainability - Jurisdiction - Suit property was belonged to joint family and compromise decree entered into between one ‘C’ predecessor of respondents and one ‘H’ predecessor of appellants in 1980 dividing joint property amongst themselves - Respondents filed suit before Trial Court against appellants for declaration that appellant nos.1 to 5 had no right to sell suit property in Schedule-I of compromise decree, either to appellant nos. 7 to 9 or any other stranger except to respondents and they claimed right of pre emption as per said deed - Trial Court dismissed suit under O. 14 r. 1 of CPC on ground of maintainability - Respondents filed application u/s. 115 of CPC before HC which set aside decision of Trial Court and remanded matter back to Trial Court - Appellants contended order of HC on ground of jurisdiction - Hence instant appeal.

Held, respondents submitted that SC should in exercise of its powers u/art. 136 of Constitution render substantial justice by upholding order of HC. Appellants submitted that in circumstances of case the appeal would lie only to District Court from order of Trial Court u/s.21 of 1887 Act. Appellants submitted that present issue relating to right of respondents to claim pre-emption could not be said to have been wrongly decided and several decisions were sought to be relied upon in this context. SC is of view that Trial Court should have framed issues arising out of pleadings in accordance with procedure of O.14 r. 1 of CPC. After framing all issues, if Trial Court is of view that any of issues raised would dispose of the suit either wholly or in part then Trial Court can on basis of provisions of O. 14 r. 2 of CPC decide said issue. Trial Court not having followed this procedure, SC does not think that it would be appropriate to uphold decision of Trial Court. SC set aside the decision of HC on ground that revisional application was incompetent. At same time SC set aside order of Trial Court and remands matter back to Trial Court for redeciding the suit strictly in compliance with observations in present judgment. Order accordingly.

Ratio - After framing all issues under O. 14 r. 1 of CPC, if Trial Court is found that any of issues raised would dispose of suit either wholly or in part then Trial Court can on basis of provisions of O. 14 r. 2 of CPC reject said suit on ground of maintainability.
