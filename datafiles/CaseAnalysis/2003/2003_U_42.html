Ram Singh and Others v Union Territory, Chandigarh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 960; (2004) SCC (L&S) 14; (2004) 1 SCC 126; AIR 2004 SC 969; 2004 (1) CLR 81; 2004 (1) ESC 161; 2003 (99) FLR 1064; JT 2003 (8) SC 345; 2004 (1) LLJ 227; 2004 (1) LLN 511; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 160; 2003(9) SCALE 459; 2004 (1) SLJ 395; 2004 (1) SLR 251; 2003 (7) SLT 200; 2003 (8) Supreme 79; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Chandigarh, Disputes Act

Summary: Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - s. 3(q) - Regularisation - Permanent and perennial nature of employment - Appellants employed through contractors for various jobs - Tribunal rejected the petitions by the employees on the ground that the employees engaged through contractor cannot be held to be holders of 'civil post' as defined under s. 3(q), hence the Tribunal has no jurisdiction to grant any relief - Held, the actual nature of relationship concerning a particular employment being essentially a question of fact, it has to be raised and proved before an industrial adjudicator - Further held, without ascertaining through the industrial forum, factual aspects of inter se relationship between the Chandigarh Administration, the contractor and the contract employees, no relief can be granted.
