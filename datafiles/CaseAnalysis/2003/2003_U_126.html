Dayal Singh and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 61; (2003) 2 SCC 593; AIR 2003 SC 1140; 2003 (2) AWC 926; JT 2003 (1) SC 498; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 787; 2003 (1) RD 208; 2003(1) SCALE 499; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 714; 2003 (1) SLT 699; 2003 (1) Supreme 811; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 848

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.23, 23(2), 23A(I), 28A - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 - Applicability of s 28 A of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 in a proceeding under 1952 Act - Held, provisions of Land Acquisition Act are, ex-facie not applicable for determination of compensation under 1952 Act, since mode and manner of compensation payable for acquisition of land under 1952 Act is distinct and different from Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Whether appellants have any legal right to force respondents herein to enter into a fresh agreement replying on or on awards passed by arbitrator in 1987 - Further held, person whose lands were acquired, thus, having entered into an agreement cannot be said to have any legal right which can be enforced in a court of law so as to enable him to obtain an order from Court directing Union of India to reopen agreement, only because by reason of a subsequent award an enhanced amount of compensation has been paid for similar class of lands - If a right to get amount of compensation re-determined is held to be implicit in Act, same for all intent and purport would amount to invoking provisions of Section 28 A of Land Acquisition Act indirectly which cannot be done directly - It is a well-settled principle of law that what cannot be done directly cannot be done indirectly.
