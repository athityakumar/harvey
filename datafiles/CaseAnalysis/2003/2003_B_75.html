D. Saibaba v Bar Council of India and another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 444; (2003) 6 SCC 186; AIR 2003 SC 2502; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2032; 2003 (51) ALR 609; 2003 (3) AWC 2495; 2003 (104) DLT 658; 2003 (4) G.L.R. 3676; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 116; JT 2003 (4) SC 435; 2003 (2) KLT 669; 2003 (2) RD 183; 2003(4) SCALE 546; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1209; 2003 (3) SLT 530; 2003 (4) Supreme 154; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1434

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1935

Summary: [A.] Advocates & Judges - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 38, 48AA - Constitutional validity of s. 48AA - Held, expression 'the date of that order' as occurring in s.48 AA has to be construed as meaning date of communication or knowledge of order of review petition - Where law provides a remedy to a person, provision has to be so construed in case of ambiguity as to make availing of remedy practical and exercise of power conferred on authority meaningful and effective - A construction which would render provision nugatory ought to be avoided - Merely by lapse of sixty days from date of order sought to be reviewed, Bar Council of India or any of its Committees is not divested of its power to exercise review jurisdiction - Challenge to constitutional validity of provision does not survive - Further, Bar Council, if only it had exercised its review jurisdiction, would have formed no opinion other than one of condoning innocuous lapse on part of appellant, a handicapped person, who permitted allotment of STD booth to continue in his name though he had actually discontinued operation of STD booth by himself Bar Council would certainly have taken a sympathetic view and would not have deprived appellant of source of his bread and butter and nipped in bud opportunity of blooming into an independent advocate to an apprentice - Impugned orders of Bar Council set aside enrolment of appellant as an advocate stands restored.
