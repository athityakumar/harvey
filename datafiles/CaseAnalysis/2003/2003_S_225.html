S.R. Ramaraj v Special Court, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 624; (2003) 7 SCC 175; AIR 2003 SC 3039; 2003 (3) CCR 205; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 150; 2003 (4) Crimes 83; 2004 (1) CRJ 525; 2003 CRLJ 3863; 2003(6) SCALE 508; 2003 (5) SLT 52; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 778; 2003 (6) Supreme 508

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Securities Exchange Board of India

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Special Court Trial of Offences Relating To Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992, s. 10(1) - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 182, 183, 191, 192, 193, 199, 200, 209 - Perjury/Contempt of court - Whether defence taken by appellant would amount to contempt of court - Held, in order to expose a person to liability of a prosecution of making false statement there must be a false statement of fact and not a mere pleading made on basis of facts which are themselves not false - Merely because an action or defence can be an abuse of process of court those responsible for its formulation cannot be regarded as committing contempt, but an attempt to deceive court by disguising nature of a claim is contempt If facts leading to a claim or defence are set out, but an inference is drawn thereby stating that stand of plaintiff or defendant is one way or other, it will not amount to contempt, unless it be that facts as pleaded themselves are false - Further held, when appellant tried to explain his case in his evidence, same was shut out on basis that it is hearsay - An officer of bank who had no personal knowledge of transactions in question, and was deposing on basis of material on record, his evidence cannot be from his knowledge and necessarily has to be hearsay.
