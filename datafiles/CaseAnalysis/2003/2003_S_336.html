Jugal Chandra Saikia v State of Assam and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 270; (2003) 4 SCC 59; (2003) SCC (L&S) 394; AIR 2003 SC 1362; 2003 (3) ESC 333; 2003 (97) FLR 328; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 109; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 671; 2003 (2) LLN 402; 2003(3) SCALE 193; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 615; 2003 (2) SLJ 248; 2003 (6) SLR 252; 2003 (3) SLT 76; 2003 (2) Supreme 825; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3370

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - compulsory retirement from service - validity and correctness of - Held, unless it is shown that the order of compulsory retirement was passed arbitrarily and without application of mind or that such information of opinion to retire compulsorily was based on no evidence or that the order of compulsory retirement was totally perverse, the Court cannot interfere. - In the present case no mala fides are attributed. - No good ground to interfere with the order of compulsory retirement.
