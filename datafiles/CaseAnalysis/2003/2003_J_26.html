Illachi Devi (D) by LRs. and Others v Jain Society, Protection of Orphans India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 796; (2003) 8 SCC 413; AIR 2003 SC 3397; 2003 (107) DLT 137; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 428; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 726; 2003(8) SCALE 190; 2003 (1) SCW 4824; 2003 (6) SLT 1; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 62; 2003 (6) Supreme 981

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Indian Companies Act, 1913, Indian Companies Act, 1882, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Public Societies Registration Act, 1350, Indian Companies Act, 1866, West Bengal Society Registration Act, 1961, Friendly Societies Act, 1896

Summary: [A.] Trusts & Associations - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss.218, 223, 236 - Societies Registration Act, 1860, ss. 5,6 - Whether a Society registered under Societies Registration Act, 1860 is entitled to obtain Letter of Administration u/s. 236, Indian Succession Act - Held, HC was not correct in its view that an Administration can be granted in favour of a society registered under Act of 1860 apprehension of HC that in a case of this nature, in event, a Letter of Administration is not granted in favour of beneficiary society, purport of 'Will' will be frustrated is not wholly correct and for grant of Letter of Administration what is necessary is that person duly authorised by Society in accordance with law may file such an application - Further held, it would be open to respondent-society to nominate any of its office-bearer to whom Letter of Administration is granted - Such nominated person may move application for substitution of his name for grant of Letter of Administration If such amendment application is made, HC shall permit this amendment and grant Letter of Administration in favour of person nominated by society for carrying of wishes of testator, which is for benefit of society.
