Union of India and another v International Trading Co. and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 472; (2003) 5 SCC 437; AIR 2003 SC 3983; 2003 (51) ALR 598; 2003 (104) DLT 779; JT 2003 (4) SC 549; 2003(4) SCALE 581; 2003 (3) SLT 556; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 55; 2003 (4) Supreme 114

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; International; Water

Keywords: Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing By Foreign Vessels) Act, 1981, Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing by Foreign Vessels) Rules, 1982

Summary: [A.] Administrative - Maritime Zones Of India (Regulation Of Fishing By Foreign Vessels) Act, 1981 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing by Foreign Vessels) Rules, 1982 - Permits granted under - To obtain on lease and operate Foreign Deep Sea Fishing Vessels - Non-renewal - Change in Govt. policy - HC came to conclusion that there was legitimate expectation or promissory estoppel involved, and that policy decision was contrary to statute - Held, doctrines of promissory estoppel and legitimate expectation cannot come in way of public interest - Indisputably, public interest has to prevail over private interest - Case at hand shows that a conscious policy decision has been taken and there is no statutory compulsion to act contrary - Therefore, HC was not justified in observing that policy decision was contrary to statute and for that reason direction for consideration of application for renewal was necessary - Further held, art.14 of Constitution applies also to matters of Govtal policy and if policy or any action of Govt, even in contractual matters, fails to satisfy test of reasonableness, it would be unconstitutional - In absence of any material to discount legitimacy of policy, respondents have not made out a case for interference - Appeal allowed.
