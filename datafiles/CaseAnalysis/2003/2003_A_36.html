Sayeda Akhtar v Abdulahad

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 547; (2003) 7 SCC 52; AIR 2003 SC 2985; 2003 (52) ALR 728; JT 2003 (6) SC 102; 2003 (2) RCJ 19; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 213; 2003(5) SCALE 429; 2003 (4) SLT 601; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 612; 2003 (5) Supreme 105

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - s. 13 - Suit for eviction on grounds of default in payment of rent and nuisance - Condonation of delay in depositing rent - Held, although the court has the jurisdiction to extend the time for depositing the rent both for the period during which the tenant had defaulted as well as the period subsequent thereto but therefor an application is to be made. The provision requiring an application to be made is indisputably necessary for the purpose of showing sufficient cause as to why such deposit could not be made within the time granted by the Court. The court does not extend time or condone the delay on mere sympathy. It will exercise its discretion judicially and on a finding of existence of sufficient cause.
