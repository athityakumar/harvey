State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Vam Organic Chemicals Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 870; (2004) 1 SCC 225; AIR 2003 SC 4650; 2003 ALJ 2700; JT 2003 (8) SC 1; 2003(8) SCALE 775; 2003 (1) SCW 5463; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 957; 2003 (8) Supreme 165

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Legislative Competence, Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910, Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951, Diesel Oil and Alcohol Taxation (Amendment) Act, 1976, Uttar Pradesh Licences for the Possession of Denatured Spirit and Specially Denatured Spirit Rules, 1976

Summary: U.P. Licences for the Possession of Denatured Spirit and Specially Denatured Spirit Rules, 1976 - r. 3(a) - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Notification dated 13th January 1990 - Licence fee of 15 paise per litre sought to be imposed on the quantity of specially denatured spirit (SDS) obtained from distilleries in Uttar Pradesh - Challenged - High Court held that the additional fee was really a tax in the garb of regulatory fee - Held, in the absence of any correlation between a fee or levy with the cost or expenses administering the regulation, the fee under r. 3 is not a fee at all levied for the purpose of additional regulation or for any service rendered, but is really a tax in the garb of a fee.
