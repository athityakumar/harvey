Mithailal Dalsangar Singh and Others v Annabai Devram Kini and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 758; (2003) 10 SCC 691; AIR 2003 SC 4244; 2003 (53) ALR 297; 2003 (5) AWC 4599; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 363; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 74; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 59; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 262; 2003 (4) MahLJ 721; 2003 (4) MPLJ 362; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 387; 2004 (96) RD 351; 2003(7) SCALE 610; 2003 (5) SLT 546; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 822; 2003 (6) Supreme 796

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 22, r. 9(1), o. 43, r. 1, s. 104 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Abatement - Suit for specific performance - LRs of deceased plaintiff took out chamber summons on Original Side of HC for being brought on record in suit in place of deceased - Defendants took objection to prayer for impleadment submitting that prayer was hopelessly barred by time and that suit had abated - DB held that an order setting aside abatement and bringing on record LRs of deceased plaintiff amounts to 'judgment' within meaning of Letters Patent - Held, LRs being brought on record at any stage of proceedings enures for benefit of entire proceedings application made by defendant-appellants in appeal once allowed would have effect of bringing LRs on record, not only in appeal but also in suit In view of defendants themselves having sought for impleadment of LRs in appeal delay in moving application in suit by LRs, being subsequent in point of time, because meaningless - Further held, an interlocutory order cannot be regarded as a judgment but only those orders would be judgments which decide matters of moment or affect vital and valuable rights of parties, and which work serious injustice to party concerned - A perusal of s 104 r/w O 43, r 1, CPC, 1908, shows that while an appeal is provided against an order refusing to set aside abatement or dismissal of a suit; there is no appeal provided against an order whereby abatement or dismissal of a suit has been set aside - Such an order does not amount to 'judgment' within meaning of Letters Patent.
