Brij Behari Sahai (Dead) Through Lrs. and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1271; (2004) 1 SCC 641; AIR 2003 SC 4930; 2004 (1) ALD(SC) 103; 2004 (54) ALR 114; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 158; 2003(10) SCALE 393; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 302

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18(1) and 54 - Claims for compensation - Determination of Compensation by High Court challenged - By judgment under challenge HC held that claimants had no better interest than that of a tenant holding over - Held, in facts of present case, nazul character of land can be sustained with corresponding rights of perpetual lessee in claimants and their predecessor-interest, subject to payment of periodically revised ground rent - Though as a matter of principle of law, Govt. while acquiring lands in which Govt. also had some or other of interest, need not go for acquiring their interest as well and what is obligated for acquisition is only such of private interest of third parties other than that of Govt., LAO has, while determining market value, indisputably proceed to determine for whole of it, consequently, need for apportionment would inevitably arise - Fixation of apportionment in ratio of 75% in favour of claimants and 25% in favour of State would be just and reasonable and claimants are not bound to pay anything further even by way of capitalization of ground rent payable - Appeal allowed.
