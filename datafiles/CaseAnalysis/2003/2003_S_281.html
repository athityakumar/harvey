Amit @ Ammu v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 608; (2003) 8 SCC 93; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1959; AIR 2003 SC 3131; 2003 (3) CCR 116; 2003 (3) Crimes 318; 2003 (6) CRJ 326; 2003 CRLJ 3873; 2003 (3) JCC 1303; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 423; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 563; 2003(6) SCALE 244; 2003 (4) SLT 828; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 285; 2003 (5) Supreme 576; 2003 (2) UC 1454

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 302, 376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 313, 357 - Murder and rape - Held, though the offence committed by the appellant deserves severe condemnation and is a most heinous crime, but on cumulative facts and circumstances of the case, the case does not fall in the category of rarest of the rare case. Considering the age of the appellant; that he was a student without any record of any previous heinous crime; and also there is no evidence that he will be a danger to the society, if the death penalty is not awarded; and having regard to the totality of the circumstances, the sentence of death penalty is modified to life imprisonment under s. 302, IPC.
