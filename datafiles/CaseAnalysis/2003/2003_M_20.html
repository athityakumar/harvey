State of Himachal Pradesh v M. P. Gupta

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1126; (2004) 2 SCC 349; (2004) SCC (Cr) 539; AIR 2004 SC 730; JT 2003 (10) SC 32; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2003(10) SCALE 522; 2004 (1) ShimLC 231; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 541; 2004 (1) UC 517

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Socio-Economic - Respondent/Chief Conservator of Forests allegedly issued a circular letter advising all the Conservator of Forests to place orders for the supply of barbed wires without obtaining the requisite non-availability certificate from the Controller of Stores - Enquiry Officer found that respondent had committed irregularities with the intention to help the contractor firm - Respondent was charged with the offences punishable u/ss. 120-B, 420 r/w s. 511 IPC and s. 13(1)(d) of 1988 Act - Since s. 197 of the CrPC had no application in the present case, no sanction was accorded for the prosecution of respondent - Respondent challenged the proceedings on the ground of non-sanction for prosecution - Trial Court dismissed respondent's application - Respondent filed appeal before the HC - HC set aside the charges against respondent for the offence u/ss. 467, 468 and 471 IPC, however, charge in respect of other offences u/ss. 420, 120-B IPC and under the provisions of 1988 Act were to be continued - Whether alleged acts of respondent were a part of the official duties and, therefore, a sanction u/s. 197 was mandatory for the purpose of proceeding in the matter and in the absence thereof at the threshold the proceedings were not maintainable - Held, use of the expression, 'official duty' u/s. 197 of CrPC implies that the act or omission must have been done by the public servant in the course of his service and that it should have been in discharge of his duty - S. 197 does not extend its protective cover to every act or omission done by a public servant in service but restricts its scope of operation to only those acts or omissions which are done by a public servant in discharge of official duty - Further, offences u/ss. 406, 409 r/w s. 120-B of IPC is not a condition precedent for launching the prosecution - Sanction u/s. 197 of the CrPC does not require for every offence committed by a public servant nor even every act done by him while he is actually engaged in the performance of his official duties - Ss. 467, 468 and 471 IPC relate to forgery of valuable security for purpose of cheating and using as genuine a forged document, however, it is not part of the duty of a public servant while discharging his official duties to commit forgery of the type covered by the said offences - Want of sanction u/s. 197 of the CrPC is, therefore, no bar for prosecuting the respondent - HC's view cannot be maintained, hence impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
