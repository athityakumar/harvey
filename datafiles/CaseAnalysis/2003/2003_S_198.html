State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Smt. Gulaichi

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 545; (2003) 6 SCC 483; (2003) SCC (L&S) 908; AIR 2003 SC 4209; 2003 (4) AWC 2774; 2003 (3) CLR 168; 2003 (3) ESC 388; 2003 (98) FLR 891; JT 2003 (6) SC 59; 2003 LabIC 2641; 2003 (3) LLN 878; 2003(5) SCALE 432; 2004 (1) SLJ 281; 2003 (5) SLR 93; 2003 (4) SLT 642; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 762; 2003 (5) Supreme 122; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1780

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Service

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Recruitment of Service (Determination of Date of Birth) Rules, 1974 - r. 2 - Uttar Pradesh Recruitment of Service (Determination of Date of Birth) (First Amendment) Rules, 1980 - Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 226 - Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, 1886 - Request for correcting the date of birth a few days before the date of superannuation - Held, in the instant case, the Rules and Amendment Rules referred to clearly indicate the permissible area for correction of date of birth. In view of the specific provisions made, it was not permissible to effect any change. Additionally, the first appellate Court and the High Court seem to have lost sight of the fact that the person who endorsed changes was not authorised to do so.
