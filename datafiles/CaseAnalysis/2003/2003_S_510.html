P.N. Premachandran v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 977; (2004) SCC (L&S) 170; (2004) 1 SCC 245; AIR 2004 SC 255; 2003 (99) FLR 1164; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 558; 2004 (1) KLT 486; 2004 (1) LLN 1174; 2003(9) SCALE 533; 2003 (1) SCW 5969; 2004 (1) SLJ 229; 2004 (1) SLR 12; 2003 (7) SLT 97; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Service; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1958

Summary: Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1958 - r. 17A - Promotion - Respondents temporarily promoted - Appellant fulfilled only the qualification, which has been relaxed by the state - Respondents found fit for regular promotion - Held, the State of Kerala took a conscious decision to the effect that those who have been acting in a higher post for a long time, although on a temporary basis, but were qualified at the time when they were so promoted and found to be eligible by the Departmental Promotion Committee at a later date, should be promoted with retrospective effect. Such exercise of power on the part of the State is not unknown in service jurisprudence - Further held, the appellant was appointed in the year 1984 and was not even qualified to hold for the post in 1964, thus, cannot be permitted to question the promotion of the private respondents.
