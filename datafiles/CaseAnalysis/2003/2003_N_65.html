Sadhana Lodh v National Insurance Company Ltd. and another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 168; (2003) 3 SCC 524; (2003) SCC (Cr) 762; 2003 (1) ACC 332; AIR 2003 SC 1561; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 63; 2003 (2) CHN 150; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 1; JT 2003 (6) SC 126; 2003 (2) KLT 47; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 181; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 772; 2003(1) SCALE 739; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 567; 2003 (2) SLT 118; 2003 (3) Supreme 189; 2003 (1) UC 549

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss.173, 149(2), - Constitution - art. 226 and 227 - CPC - s. 115 - Right of appeal u/s 173 on limited grounds available u/s 149(2) - Can insurer file writ petition when he has ground not availble u/s 149(2) - Right of appeal is a statutory right and where the law provides remedy by filing an appeal on limited grounds, the grounds of challenge cannot be enlarged by filing a petition under art.226/227 of the Constitution on the premise that the insurer has limited grounds available for challenging the award given by the Tribunal - s.149(2) of the Act limits the insurer to file an appeal on those enumerated grounds and the appeal being a product of the statute it is not open to an insurer to take any plea other than those provided u/s.149(2) of the Act - Where remedy for filing a revision before the HC u/s.115 of CPC has been expressly barred by a State enactment, only in such case a petition under art. 227 of the Constitution would lie and not under art.226 of the Constitution - Further, the supervisory jurisdiction conferred on the HCs under art.227 of the Constitution is confined only to see whether an inferior court or Tribunal has proceeded within its parameters and not to correct an error apparent on the face of the record, much less of an error of law - Appeal allowed.
