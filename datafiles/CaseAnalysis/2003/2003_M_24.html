M. S. N. Nadaf Since Dead Through Lrs. and others v Special Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1658; (2004) 13 SCC 75; AIR 2003 SC 3444; 2003(10) SCALE 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 28(1) - Sale deed - Land acquisition - Compensation - Legality - Respondent acquired land pursuant to notification and an award came to be passed fixing compensation - Claimants filed petition and sale deed before Reference Court - Sale was in respect of 4.5 guntas of land at Rs. 13,500/- and sale had taken place few months prior to notification - Reference Court applied deduction of 40% and awarded compensation - On appeal before HC - HC passed impugned judgment - Held, considering that they were developed lands, Court held that normal rate of deduction should be one-third - It was only if there was material on record to show that higher percentage was required to be deducted that there could be higher deduction - There could be no dispute with this proposition - However, if wrong principles had been applied or if judgment in appeal was found to be not right then Court could always interfere - Normal Rule was that one-third should be deducted - HC while deducting up to 60% had given no reasons at all as to why and what was circumstance which required such large deduction - It was therefore, clear that HC had applied wrong principles - HC had erred in deducting to extent of 60% - Under these circumstances, impugned order was set aside and orders of Reference Courts were restored - Interest on solatium was payable - Claimants would also be entitled to all amounts payable to them under law - Appeals disposed of.
