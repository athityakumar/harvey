Union of India and others v M/s. Modiluft Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 469; (2003) 6 SCC 65; AIR 2003 SC 2218; 2003 (3) AllCJ 1998; 2003 (4) AWC 2796; 2003 (104) DLT 938; JT 2003 (6) SC 106; 2003(4) SCALE 553; 2003 (3) SLT 712; 2003 (4) Supreme 161

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Interim order made by HC - Respondents directed to issue a 'No Objection Certificate' and to pay certain sums of money to Customs Authorities - Petitioner directed to obtain certain letter of confirmation confirming that a sum of Rs. 12.5 crores paid by them could be appropriated towards dues of writ petitioners in payment of inland air travel tax - Whether impugned order is an order in equity? - Held, for an order to be an order in equity, it should be equitable to all parties concerned - Impugned order has not taken into consideration interest of Revenue as was expected of court entertaining an appeal against a money decree - In such circumstances, entertaining a petition in its writ jurisdiction to grant a liberal instalment to respondent to pay dues that too only a part of dues, cannot be considered as an interim order made in equity - HC erred in granting impugned relief to respondent, which is in nature of a final relief, which on facts and circumstances of this case, without deciding issues involved in writ, could not have been granted - Impugned order set aside - NOC, which was issued provisionally stands revoked - Appeal allowed.
