Banti @ Guddu v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 908; (2004) SCC (Cr) 294; (2004) 1 SCC 414; AIR 2004 SC 261; 2003 (4) Crimes 415; 2004 CRLJ 372; JT 2003 (8) SC 392; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 911; 2003(9) SCALE 215; 2003 (1) SCW 5977; 2003 (6) SLT 578; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 119; 2003 (7) Supreme 691

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 302 - High Court confirmed the conviction and sentence passed by the Trial Court - Held, there is no reason or ground substantiated on behalf of the appellants to interfere with the concurrent findings recorded by both the courts based on relevant, cogent and trustworthy evidence adduced by the prosecution to prove the guilt of the appellants beyond reasonable doubt.
