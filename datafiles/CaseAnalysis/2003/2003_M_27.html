Liverpool and London S. P. and I Association v M. V. Sea Success I and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1446; (2004) 9 SCC 512; JT 2003 (9) SC 218; 2003(10) SCALE 1; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 851

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Wreck, Marine Insurance, Charter, Voyage, Maritime Lien, Oil Pollution, Salvage, Administration of Justice Act, 1920, Colonial Courts of Admiralty (India) Act, 1891, Inland Vessels Act, 1917, Sister Ship, Multimodal Transportation of Goods Act, 1993, Admiralty Court Act, 1861., Supreme Court of Judicature Act, 1925, Administration of Justice Act, 1956, Admiralty Court Act, 1840, Multi-modal Transportation of Goods (Amendment) Act, 2000, Ship Mortgage Act, 1946

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Admiralty Court Act, 1861 - Supreme Court of Judicature (Consolidation) Act, 1925 - Administration of Justice Act, 1956 - Multi-Modal Transportation of Goods Act, 1993 - Inland Vessels Act, 1917 - Colonial Courts of Admiralty (India) Act, 1891 - Administration of Justice Act, 1920 - Admiralty Court Act, 1840 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Modal Transportation of Goods (Amendment) Act, 2000 - Ship Mortgage Act, 1946 - (A) Whether unpaid insurance premium of P and I Club would come within purview of expression 'necessaries supplied to any ship'? - Held, having regard to changing scenario and keeping in tune with changes in both domestic and international law as also statutes adopted by several countries, a stand, however bold, may have to be taken that unpaid insurance premium of P and I Club would come within purview of expression 'necessaries supplied to any ship' - Other types of insurance, keeping in view existing statutes may not amount to a 'necessary' - In agreement with judgment of Bombay High Court, hold that unpaid insurance premium being a maritime claim would be enforceable in India - (B) Whether letters patent appeal was maintainable? - Further held, cl. 15 of Letters Patent is not a special statute - Only in a case where there exists an express prohibition in matter of maintainability of an intra-court appeal, same may not be held to be maintainable - But in event there does not exist any such prohibition and if order will otherwise be a 'judgment' within meaning of cl. 15 of Letters Patent, an appeal shall be maintainable - Civil Appeal No. 5665 of 2002 allowed - Civil Appeal No. 5666 of 2002 dismissed.
