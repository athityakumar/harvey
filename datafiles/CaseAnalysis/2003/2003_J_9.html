The General Manager, Department of v Jacob S/o Kochuvarkey Kalliath (dead) by Lrs. and

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 299; (2003) 9 SCC 662; AIR 2003 SC 1308; 2003 (4) ALD(SC) 54; JT 2003 (3) SC 522; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 175; 2003 (2) KLT 411; 2003(3) SCALE 549; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 214; 2003 (1) SCW 1716; 2003 (3) SLT 624; 2003 (3) Supreme 694

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Kerala Land Acquisition Act, 1961, Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Lands) Act, 1961

Summary: Land and Property - Kerala Land Acquisition Act, s.11-A - Construction of - Challenge to acquisition proceedings, which had lapsed u/s 11-A - HC held that the acquisition proceedings, initiated to acquire the land in question have lapsed due to delay in passing the award and set aside the proceedings - Setting aside of proceedings by HC whether proper? - Held, the Division Bench seems to have committed a patent error by completely overlooking the mandate contained in s.6(2) that of the various modes of publication envisaged therein, the last of any of the three modes in the series that should be taken to be the date of publication and consequently taken into account for purposes of making the Award as laid down in s.11-A - View taken by the HC not only runs counter to the mandate of law enacted by the Parliament, but opposed to the dicta of the SC - Further, there is nothing to indicate in the order of the HC stipulating or extending the time for passing the Award, that beyond the time so permitted, it cannot be done at all and the authorities are disabled once and for all even to proceed in the matter in accordance with law, if it is so permissible for the authorities under the law governing the matter in issue - Appeal allowed.
