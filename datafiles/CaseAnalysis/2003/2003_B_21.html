Babu Parasu Kaikadi (Dead) by LRs. v Babu (Dead) through LRs.

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1124; (2004) 1 SCC 681; AIR 2004 SC 754; JT 2005 (11) SC 536; 2003(10) SCALE 504; 2003 (1) SCW 6923; 2003 (7) SLT 614; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1153; 2004 (1) Supreme 548

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Tenancy & Agricultural Lands (Vidarbha Region) Act, 1958

Summary: Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 - s. 32(1B) - Interpretation - Tenant - Dispossession - Held, in absence of any registered document having regard to the provisions contained in ss. 17 and 49, Registration Act, no lawful title could pass on the mortgages. Lawful title as well as the legal possession of the land in question therefore remained with the landlord. The so-called mortgagee in the aforementioned circumstances must be help to have merely in permissive possession of the land. Such a possession, on the part of the so-called mortgagee, being not in his own right, the land could have been restored in favour of the appellant.
