Superintendent of Police, C.B.I. and others v Tapan Kr. Singh

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 340; (2003) 6 SCC 175; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1305; AIR 2003 SC 4140; 2003 (2) CCR 47; 2003 (2) Crimes 300; 2003 (5) CRJ 268; 2003 CRLJ 2322; 2003 (2) JCC 843; JT 2003 (4) SC 26; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 880; 2003(3) SCALE 777; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 485; 2003 (3) SLT 147; 2003 (3) Supreme 349; 2003 (2) UC 1100

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Wrong Section

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - S. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e) - offences under - question of validity of the preliminary enquiry and the validity of the search and seizure - Held, the information received by the Superintendent of Police, C.B.I. clearly spells out the offence of criminal misconduct under Section 13 of the Act and the allegation is not vague and bald. - The law does not require the mentioning of all the ingredients of the offence in the First Information Report. It is only after a complete investigation that it may be possible to say that whether any offence is made out on the basis of evidence collected by the investigating agency. - The High Court erred in exercising its revisional jurisdiction to quash the G.D. Entry, the F.I.R. and the investigation undertaken by the Superintendent of Police, C.B.I. in the facts and circumstances of this case.
