Commissioner of Central Excise v Markfed Vanaspati and Allied Industries and another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 335; (2003) 4 SCC 184; AIR 2003 SC 3534; 2003 (108) ECR 1; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 491; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 74; 2003 (55) RLT 745; 2003(3) SCALE 708; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 480; 2003 (3) SLT 192; 2003 (3) Supreme 281

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - whether a good becomes excisable merely because it falls within a tariff item - whether the well settled twin tests of 'manufacture and marketability' cease to apply if a good falls within a tariff entry - held, merely because an item falls in a Tariff Entry it must not be deemed that there is manufacture. The burden to prove that there is manufacture and that what is manufactured is on the revenue. - "Spent earth" was "earth" on which duty has been paid and it remains earth even after the processing. Thus, if duty was to be levied on it again, it would amount to levying double duty on the same product.
