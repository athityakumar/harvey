Commissioner of Income Tax v Indo Nippon Chemicals Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1047; (2003) 11 SCC 452; 2003 (182) CTR 291; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 452; [2003] 261 ITR 275; 176 TAXATION 1; [2003] 130 TAXMAN 179

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss.145, 260A, 4(l) (b) - Excise duty - Excisable goods - Nature of income - Closing stock - Assessees were manufacturing units liable to excise duty under Modvat scheme, they get credit for excise duty already paid on raw-materials purchased by them and utilized in manufacture of excisable goods - When they manufacture the goods and sell them, proportionate part of modvat credit was set off against their excise duty liability - Assessing Officer took the view that Modvat credit that was available should be treated as an income or an advantage in the nature of income and added back the said amount to the income of each of these assesee - Commissioner of Income-tax, in some of cases, agreed with view of Assessing Officer and, in some cases, differed - Tribunal uniformly took the view that Modvat credit could not be added back to the income of assessee - HC taken the several illustrations in charts placed before it by both sides and demonstrated that there are two possible methods of valuation of stock - Whether the value of closing stock of duty paid inputs, work-in-progress and finished goods must necessarily include element of Modvat credit available - Held, manufacturer who manufactures goods would reckon cost of raw material as exclusive of Modvat credit in reckoning cost of 'final product' - Observations not deal with manner of valuation of the unconsumed raw material or work-in-progress in hand - HC correctly appreciated arguments and rendered a judgment which was unexceptionable - No substance in the appeals of Department - Appeal dismissed.
