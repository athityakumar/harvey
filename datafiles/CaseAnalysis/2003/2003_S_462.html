Lallu Manjhi and another v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 3; (2003) 2 SCC 401; (2003) SCC (Cr) 544; AIR 2003 SC 854; 2003 (1) CCR 113; 2003 (1) Crimes 310; 2003 (4) CRJ 588; 2003 CRLJ 914; 2003 (1) JCC 137; JT 2003 (1) SC 1; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 189; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 566; 2003(1) SCALE 14; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2003 (1) SLT 219; 2003 (1) Supreme 146; 2003 (1) UC 699

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S. 147, 148 and 302/149 - assault by unlawful assembly armed with deadly weapons - Held, the genesis or the root cause of the incident is not known. Further, the version of the incident given by the sole eyewitness who is also an interested witness on account of his relationship with the deceased and being inimically disposed against the accused persons is highly exaggerated and not fully corroborated by medical evidence. Also, the version of the incident as given in the Court is substantially in departure from the earlier version as contained and available in the First Information Report. - Therefore, reliance cannot be placed on the sole testimony of PW-9 for the purpose of recording the conviction of all the accused persons. - On the state of evidence available the accused persons could not have been held guilty of the offences charged.
