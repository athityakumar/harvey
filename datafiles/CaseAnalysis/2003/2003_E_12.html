Kailash Nath Gupta v Enquiry Officer, (R.K. Rai), Allahabad Bank and others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 291; (2003) 9 SCC 480; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1137; AIR 2003 SC 1377; 2003 (1) AllCJ 779; 2003 (2) AWC 1509; 2003 (2) CLR 72; 2003 (5) ESC 275; 2003 (97) FLR 556; JT 2003 (3) SC 322; 2003 (2) LLJ 367; 2003 (2) LLN 392; 2003(3) SCALE 428; 2003 (1) SCW 1813; 2003 (3) SLR 1; 2003 (3) SLT 125; 2003 (3) Supreme 318

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Allahabad Bank Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976-Regulations 3 (1), 3 (3) and 24-Employment - Removal-Proportionality of punishment-Interference with quantum of punishment-Power extremely limited-If relevant factors having some bearing on quantum of punishment not taken note of-Certainly Court can direct reconsideration-Or in appropriate case, to shorten litigation, indicate punishment to be awarded-Appellant removed by Bank from service-But reading of various articles of charges showing at most that there is some procedural irregularity in making advances-Which cannot be termed to be negligence to warrant extreme penalty of dismissal from service-These aspects not considered by High Court in proper perspective-Order of High Court set aside-Matter remitted for reconsideration.
