Amar Singh v Balwinder Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 95; (2003) 2 SCC 518; (2003) SCC (Cr) 641; AIR 2003 SC 1164; 2003 (1) CCR 325; 2003 (2) Crimes 186; 2003 (3) CRJ 324; 2003 CRLJ 1282; 2003 (1) JCC 325; JT 2003 (2) SC 1; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 701; 2003(1) SCALE 529; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 754; 2003 (1) SLT 733; 2003 (2) Supreme 155; 2003 (1) UC 586

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: IPC - S. 302 - conviction under - on appeal the High Court set aside the conviction and sentence - appeal - Cr.P.C. - S. 386 - Held, in a case based upon direct eye-witness account the testimony of the eye-witnesses is of paramount importance and if the Appellate Court reverses the finding recorded by the Trial Court and acquits the accused without considering or examining the testimony of the eye-witnesses, it will be a clear infraction of Section 386 Cr.P.C. Further, there is no hard and fast rule that any delay in lodging the FIR would automatically render the prosecution case doubtful. - The judgment and order of the High Court is wholly perverse and illegal inasmuch as it completely failed to consider the testimony of the eye-witnesses and the reasons given for discarding the prosecution case are also unsustainable in law.
