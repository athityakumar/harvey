Bank of Baroda v Sadruddin Hasan Daya and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1170; (2004) 1 SCC 360; AIR 2004 SC 942; 2004 (56) ALR 139; 2004 (2) AWC 1450; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 241; 2004 (97) CutLT 581; 2004 (1) DRTC 17; JT 2005 (6) SC 149; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 298; 2003(10) SCALE 681; 2003 (1) SCW 7223; 2004 (1) SLT 595; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 764; 2004 (1) Supreme 140

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contempt petition - Settlement reached at between the parties with respect to payment - Respondents (defendants) committed default and did not deposit any amount though the first installment - Breach - Held, the position remains that though under the consent decree passed by this Court, the respondents had to deposit the first instalment and the last instalment, but they have not deposited or paid even a single penny. In these circumstances, there is no occasion for showing any leniency in the matter of punishment.
