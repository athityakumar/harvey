Rajni Kumar v Suresh Kumar Malhotra and Anr.

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 293; (2003) 5 SCC 315; AIR 2003 SC 1322; 2003 (4) ALD(SC) 82; 2003 (51) ALR 238; 2003 (1) ARC 539; 2003 (2) AWC 1597; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 213; JT 2003 (3) SC 307; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 776; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 591; 2003(3) SCALE 434; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 66; 2003 (2) SLT 835; 2003 (2) Supreme 910

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC - Rule 4 Order 37 - whether the High Court committed jurisdictional error in declining to set aside the ex parte decree on application of the appellant under Rule 4 of Order 37, on the ground that he failed to disclose facts sufficient to entitle him to defend the suit - held, the High Court was right in accepting existence of special circumstances justifying his not seeking leave of the court to defend, but in declining to grant relief since he had mentioned no circumstances justifying any defence. - No illegality much less jurisdictional error in the order under challenge to warrant interference of this Court.
