Public Services Tribunal Bar Association v State of U.P. & Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 53; (2003) 4 SCC 104; (2003) SCC (L&S) 400; AIR 2003 SC 1115; 2003 (2) AWC 968; 2003 (19) CLR 622; 2003 (1) ESC 65; 2003 (96) FLR 1048; JT 2003 (1) SC 534; 2003 LabIC 781; 2003 (1) LLN 784; 2003(1) SCALE 564; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 666; 2003 (2) SLJ 14; 2003 (2) SLR 343; 2003 (1) SLT 770; 2003 (4) Supreme 316; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 780

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Central Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) Act, 1976, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) (Amendment) Act, 1977, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) (Amendment) Act, 1982, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunals) (Amendment) Act, 1992, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) (Amendment) Ordinance, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) (Amendment) (Second) Ordinance, 1995, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) Amendment Ordinance, 1999, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal (Amendment) Act, 2000, Administrative Tribunals (Amendment) Act

Summary: U.P. Public Services (Tribunal) Act, 1976 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Arts. 14 and 16 - challenge to various amendments made in the U.P. Public Services (Tribunal) Act, 1976 and that the same are violative of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution being arbitrary as they are onerous and work inequitably - Held, the State Legislature was competent to enact the impugned provisions - The provisions enacted are not arbitrary and therefore not violative of arts. 14 and 16 or any other provisions of the Constitution - They are not against the basic structure of the Constitution of India either.
