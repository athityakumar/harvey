State Of Rajasthan v Bhawani & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 580; (2003) 7 SCC 291; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1628; AIR 2003 SC 4230; AIR 2003 SCW 3953; 2003 (3) CCR 86; 2003 (3) Crimes 228; 2003 (6) CRJ 421; 2003 CRLJ 3857; JT 2003 (6) SC 369; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 875; 2003(5) SCALE 595; 2003 (4) SLT 750; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 996; 2003 (5) Supreme 287; 2003 (2) UC 1233

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 148, 302, 307, 448 - Arms Act, 1959 - ss. 3, 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 162, 385, 386 - Eye witnesses account corroborated by medical evidence - Acquittal by High Court - Held, the findings recorded by the High Court on the basis of the site plan prepared by the investigating officer, whereby it discarded the prosecution case, is clearly illegal being based upon inadmissible evidence and has to be set aside - Further held, the judgment of the High Court is wholly illegal and perverse. It is not a case where two views are possible. In fact, on the evidence available on record, the only conclusion that can be drawn is that the prosecution had succeeded in establishing its case beyond any shadow of doubt and accused-respondents are clearly guilty of the charges levelled against them.
