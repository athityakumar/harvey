Union of India and Others v Vipan Kumar Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 532; (2005) 9 SCC 579; 2003 (181) CTR 24; [2003] 260 ITR 1; 174 TAXATION 781; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 59

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Proceedings, Mala Fide

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 120, 124, 131(1), 132(8), 132(9A), 133A, 133B and 142 - Whether High Court rightly invoked principle that a person could not be a judge in his own cause to hold that assessments could not have been carried out in respect of respondents Nos. 2 and 4? - Held, several sections which have been cited by appellants would show that Assessing Officer has, either directly or by virtue of his appointment or authorisation by a superior authority under Act, been given power of gathering information for purposes of assessment - Mode of gathering such information may vary from mere issuance of a notice u/s. 142 to more intrusive method of entry and search envisaged u/ss. 133A and 133B and seizure u/s. 132 - In absence of any challenge to any of these provisions, it was not open to HC to have disabled Assessing Officer from discharging his statutory functions - There is no question of imputing or presuming a bias where action is followed under section - Assessing Officer is required to assess income on basis of facts as found - Such finding may be through any of provisions referred to above - There is nothing inherently unconstitutional in permitting Assessing Officer to gather information and to assess value of information himself - But to hold, as HC has that bias is established only because authorised officer u/s. 132 and Assessing Officer are same person is an incorrect approach - Appeal allowed.
