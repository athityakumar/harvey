Union of India v Tarit Ranjan Das

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 835; (2003) 11 SCC 658; (2004) SCC (L&S) 160; AIR 2004 SC 852; 2004 (1) ESC 147; 2003 (99) FLR 829; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 64; JT 2003 (8) SC 352; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 713; 2003(8) SCALE 350; 2003 (1) SCW 7082; 2004 (1) SLJ 424; 2004 (1) SLR 28; 2003 (6) SLT 768; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 339; 2003 (7) Supreme 612; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 13

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Equal pay - Legality of judgment rendered by Guwahati HC upholding judgment of Central Administrative Tribunal, by which direction was given to pay Stenographers of subordinate offices same scale of pay as paid to Strenographers Grade C in Central Secretariat - Held, Tribunal passed impugned order by reviewing earlier order - A bare reading of two orders shows that order in view application was in complete variation and disregard of earlier order and strong as well as sound reasons contained therein whereby original application was rejected scope for review is rather limited and it is not permissible for forum hearing review application to act as an appellate authority in respect of original order by a fresh and rehearing of matter to facilitate a change of opinion on merits - Tribunal seems to have transgressed its jurisdiction in dealing with review petition as if it was hearing original application - This aspect has also not been noticed by HC - Further held, judgment of Tribunal in review application and of HC confirming it have no leg to stand.
