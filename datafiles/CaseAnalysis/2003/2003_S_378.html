Nazir Khan and Others v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 659; (2003) 8 SCC 461; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2033; AIR 2003 SC 4427; 2003 (3) CCR 173; 2003 (4) Crimes 29; 2003 CRLJ 5021; 2003 (106) DLT 70; 2003 (3) JCC 1456; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 200; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 585; 2003(6) SCALE 652; 2003 (1) SCW 5068; 2003 (5) SLT 14; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 884; 2003 (6) Supreme 234

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Foreigners; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 10, s. 24, s. 25, s. 26, s. 27, s. 28, s. 29, s. 30 - IPC, 1860 ss. 120 B, 121 A, 122, 124 A, 364 A - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss.3, 3(1), 3(5), 3(2)(1), 4, 15 - Foreigners Act, 1946 - s.14 - No corroboration to alleged confessional statement - Held, object and purpose of conspiracy was clear and manner of organising activities to achieve ultimate objective has been amply established - Merely because persons who were kidnapped have not appeared at trial to give evidence on account of unavoidable circumstances, that does not weaken quality/quantity of evidence placed on record - Applying principles which can be culled out from principles set out above to factual scenario, inevitable conclusion is that trial Court was justified in its conclusions by holding accused appellants guilty - When an accused is a participant in a big game planned, he cannot take advantage of being ignorant about finer details applied to give effect to conspiracy hatched - Further held, for purpose of bringing in application of s 3(2)(i), TADA Act, terrorist act should have resulted in death of any person - Only life sentence and not death sentence can be imposed can be imposed in case.
