State of Karnataka v M.V. Manjunathegowda and another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 5; (2003) 2 SCC 188; (2003) SCC (Cr) 467; AIR 2003 SC 809; 2003 (1) CCR 99; 2003 (1) CGLJ 298; 2003 (1) Crimes 289; 2003 (3) CRJ 282; 2003 CRLJ 900; 2003 (1) DMC 201; 2003 (1) JCC 471; 2003 (1) JIC 475; JT 2003 (1) SC 34; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 347; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 543; 2003(1) SCALE 30; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 21; 2003 (1) SLT 159; 2003 (1) Supreme 133; 2003 (1) UC 625

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 -- S 3, 4 and 6 - There is over-whelming evidence against Accused-1 with regard to demand and receipt of part of dowry - Held, no leniency warranted on quantum of punishment. deterrent punishment called for - Ten years R.I. imposed. Judgment of High Court under S 302, IPC, upheld. Held, no circumstantial evidence.
