Director General R.P.F. and others v Ch. Sai Babu

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 80; (2003) 4 SCC 331; (2003) SCC (L&S) 464; AIR 2003 SC 1437; 2003 (2) AWC 986; 2003 (19) CLR 637; 2003 (96) FLR 1004; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 3; JT 2003 (1) SC 557; 2003 LabIC 752; 2003 (1) LLN 809; 2003(1) SCALE 545; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 729; 2003 (2) SLJ 43; 2003 (2) SLR 385; 2003 (1) SLT 691; 2003 (4) Supreme 313; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 765; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 810

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Railway Protection Force Rules, 1987, r. 153 - Punishment of removal from service imposed by disciplinary authority - On appeal learned Single Judge of HC modified impugned order - Appeal - Held, normally, punishment imposed by disciplinary authority should not be disturbed by HC or tribunal except in appropriate cases that too only after reaching a conclusion that punishment imposed is grossly or shockingly disproportionate, after examining all relevant factors including nature of charges proved against, past conduct, penalty imposed earlier, nature of duties assigned having due regard to their sensitiveness, exactness expected of and discipline required to be maintained, and department/establishment in which concerned delinquent person works - Further, normally in cases where it is found that punishment imposed is shockingly disproportionate, HCs or tribunals may remit cases to disciplinary authority for reconsideration on quantum of punishment - Learned Single Judge has not recorded reasons in order to modify punishment imposed - Matter remitted to DB of HC to reconsider case only on quantum of punishment imposed.
