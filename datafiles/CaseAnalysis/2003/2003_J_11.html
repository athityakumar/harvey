State of Haryana v Jagbir Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 807; (2003) 11 SCC 261; (2004) SCC (Cr) 126; AIR 2003 SC 4377; 2003 (4) CCR 178; 2003 (4) Crimes 241; 2004 (1) CRJ 333; 2003 CRLJ 5054; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 393; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 251; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 554; 2003(8) SCALE 221; 2003 (1) SCW 5180; 2003 (6) SLT 137; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 165; 2003 (7) Supreme 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Eye-Witness, Extra-judicial confession, Charge - Sheet, Code of Criminal Procedure, Criminal Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 201, 302, 364, 384 - Respondent caused homicidal death of a child after kidnapping him - High Court reversed the judgment of the trial court by finding them not guilty - Whether the accused persons were responsible for the death and the prosecution was able to prove its claims beyond reasonable doubt - Held, the High Court has analysed the evidence in the present case in great detail to find the evidence to be contradictory and unacceptable in relation to extra judicial confession and alleged recovery - Further held, the court can act on the evidence brought before it. Even though the investigation may not be entirely blemishless, at the same time when the material brought on record is insufficient, the course adopted by the High Court cannot be faulted.
