5 M and T Consultants, Secunderabad v S.Y. Nawab and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 811; (2003) 8 SCC 100; AIR 2003 SC 4942; 2003 (6) ALD(SC) 99; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 170; 2003(8) SCALE 229; 2003 (7) SLT 496; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 187; 2003 (7) Supreme 166

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act, ss. 124, 126, 129A, 148, 420, 421, 422 - Writ of certiorari to call for records of Municipal Corporation of Hyderabad relating to permission granted by Corporation to appellant to erect or display any advertisement/street signs/direction boards/arches on public roads/colonies etc within twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad and permission granted for display thereon to second respondent or to any other person and declare same to be ultra-vires provisions of Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act and art 14 of Constitution of India - Held, a careful and dispassionate assessment and consideration of materials placed on record does not leave any reasonable impression, on peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, that anything obnoxious which require either public criticism or condemnation by courts of law had taken place It is by now well settled that non-floating of tenders or absence of public auction or invitation alone is no sufficient reason to castigate move or an action of a public authority as either arbitrary or unreasonable or amounted to mala fide or improper exercise or improper abuse of power by authority concerned - Further held, case on hand does not constitute or at any rate can by no means said to be outcome of any unreasonable or arbitrary exercise of power so as to warrant interference u/art 226.
