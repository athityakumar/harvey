Dev Singh v Punjab Tourism Development Corporation Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 683; (2003) 8 SCC 9; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1198; AIR 2003 SC 3712; 2003 (5) AWC 4343; 2003 (99) FLR 111; JT 2003 (7) SC 509; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 493; 2003 LabIC 3146; 2003 (3) LLJ 823; 2003 (4) LLN 37; 2003(7) SCALE 122; 2003 (6) SLR 711; 2003 (5) SLT 760; 2003 (6) Supreme 482; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2613

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Punjab Tourism Development Corporation Ltd Service Bye-Laws - Bye-Law 18 - Misplacement of file amounting to misconduct - Disciplinary action - Dismissal - Held, punishment of dismissal for mere misplacement of a file without any ulterior motive is too harsh a punishment which is totally disproportionate to misconduct alleged and same certainly shocks judicial conscience - Hence, having considered basis on which punishment of dismissal was imposed on appellant and facts and circumstances of case, it would be appropriate to modify punishment - On said basis, while upholding finding of misconduct against appellant, appellant be imposed a punishment of withholding of one increment including stoppage at efficiency bar in substitution of punishment of dismissal awarded by disciplinary authority - Further held, appellant will not be entitled to any back wages for period of suspension - However, he will be entitled to subsistence allowance payable up to date of dismissal order.
