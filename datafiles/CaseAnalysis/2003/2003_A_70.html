Collector of Central Excise, Jaipur v Alcobex Metals

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 278; (2003) 4 SCC 630; AIR 2003 SC 3431; 2003 (86) ECC 533; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 241; JT 2003 (3) SC 312; 2003 (55) RLT 377; 2003(3) SCALE 321; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1057; 2003 (3) SLT 122; 2003 (3) Supreme 258

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - S. 11-A - whether show-cause notice dated March 6, 1998 issued by the Deputy Collector, Central Excise, Jaipur, invoking proviso to Section 11-A of the Act is valid in law? - Held, the impugned notice was issued by the Deputy Collector, Central Excise invoking the proviso to Section 11-A for the extended period which postulates satisfaction of the Collector on the question of fraud, collusion or wilful mis-statement or suppression of fact or contravention of any provisions of the Act and the Rules made thereunder for invoking a larger period of five years, i.e., from March 1, 1981 to December 5, 1985. Therefore, it follows that such a notice could have been issued only by the Collector, Central Excise and the impugned notice issued by the Deputy Collector is wholly without jurisdiction. Further, even for shorter period notice based on the grounds contained in the proviso could be issued only by the Collector.
