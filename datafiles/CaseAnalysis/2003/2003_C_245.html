Delhi Farming and Construction (P) Ltd. v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 288; (2003) 5 SCC 36; AIR 2003 SC 1316; 2003 (181) CTR 12; 2003 (104) DLT 216; [2003] 260 ITR 561; JT 2003 (3) SC 267; 2003(3) SCALE 406; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 35; 2003 (3) SLT 31; 2003 (2) Supreme 886; 174 TAXATION 421; [2003] 128 TAXMAN 374

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Distributable Income, Distributable Surplus

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - ss. 104, 45, 47(viii), 2(24)(iv), 2(45) and 109 - (A) Whether on the facts, and in the circumstances of the case the Tribunal was right in law in holding that the capital gains of Rs 7,45,109/- could not be considered for purpose of computing the distributable income of the assessee-company for the purposes of s.104 of the IT Act - Held, the entire amount of capital gains which accrued as a result of acquisition (and hence compulsory transfer) of the agricultural land could not have been subjected to tax u/s.104 of the Act as it was wholly exempted from capital gains and not part of the 'gross income' or the distributable income for the purpose of s.104 of the Act - (B) Whether the Tribunal was right in canceling the orders passed by the Income-Tax Officer, u/s 104 of the Act for the two assessment years 1974-75 and 1975-76? - Held, there cannot be a hard and fast rule that capital gains ought or ought not to be treated as commercial or business profits on which dividends could be distributed It would ultimately depend on the facts and circumstances of each case based upon which the Board of Directors take a commercial decision as to whether dividend should be distributed thereupon or not - Taken against the background of the accumulated losses of the company over several financial years, together with the loss of the only asset of the company, there was nothing unreasonable in the decision of the Board of Directors not to distribute dividends from the compensation awarded but to capitalize it in a reserve account - Tribunal was right in holding that the provisions of s.104 of the Act could not be invoked by the Income-tax Officer in both the assessment year i e 1974-75 and 1975-76 - Appeal allowed.
