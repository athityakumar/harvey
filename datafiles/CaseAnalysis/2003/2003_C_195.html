National Winder v Commissioner of Central Excise, Allahabad

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 455; (2003) 11 SCC 361; 2003 (88) ECC 2; 2003 (110) ECR 268; 2003 (154) E.L.T. 350; 2003 (56) RLT 607; 2003 (4) Supreme 464

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excises And Customs Laws (Amendment) Act, 1991

Summary: Central Excise and Customs Laws (Amendment) Act, 1991 - s. 11B - challenge to the decision of the larger Bench of CEGAT, according to which, a purchaser who claims refund under s. 11B must do so within six months of the date of purchase of the goods, and the purchaser is not entitled to the benefit of the proviso to that section even if the manufacturer has paid the duty under protest - held, the wide language of the proviso shows that it covers not just claims for refund by the manufacturer but also claims for refund by the purchaser. Thus, if duty is paid by a manufacturer under protest, then the limitation of six months will not apply even to a claim for refund by the purchaser - Impugned judgment set aside.
