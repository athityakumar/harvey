Arm Group Enterprises Ltd. v Waldorf Restaurant and others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 305; (2003) 6 SCC 423; AIR 2003 SC 4106; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 59; JT 2003 (3) SC 450; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 594; 2003(3) SCALE 499; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 222; 2003 (3) SLT 425; 2003 (3) Supreme 62

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950, Partnership Act 1932

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Rent Control (temporary provisions) Act, 1950 - Whether the respondent firm can claim status of sub-tenant and seek protection against eviction in execution of the compromise decree obtained against the tenant, under the provision of the Act of 1950 - Held, under the Act of 1950 only the 'tenant inferior to the tenant of first degree' can claim protection as 'direct tenant' under the landlord - Sub-tenant of a 'tenant inferior to the tenant of first degree' does not have any such status or protection under the Act of 1950 - There is neither evidence nor a case set up by either of the parties that the erstwhile proprietor had sub-let the premises to the present firm - The provisions of Act of 1950 do not permit, without consent of the landlord, creation of sub-tenancy by a sub-tenant or in other words 'tenant Inferior to the tenant of first degree' - Division Bench of the HC has wrongly assumed creation of sub-tenancy by the proprietor in favour of the partnership firm - Possession of the leased premises has been left with the firm through its partners who must vacate the premises on extinguishment of the rights of the tenant and the sub-tenant - Appeals allowed.
