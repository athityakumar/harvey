Bondar Singh and others v Nihal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 206; (2003) 4 SCC 161; AIR 2003 SC 1905; 2003 (5) AWC 4366; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 671; 2003 (2) CTC 635; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 131; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 122; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 222; 2003(2) SCALE 589; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 564; 2003 (2) SLT 649; 2003 (2) Supreme 512; 2003 (2) UC 809

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - adverse possession - whether the plaintiffs were in hostile continuous possession of the suit lands by virtue of which they had perfected their title to the land by adverse possession - held, plea of adverse possession raised by the plaintiff clearly established - in the absence of a clear plea regarding sub tenancy the defendants cannot be allowed to build up a case of sub tenancy. - Further, the plaintiffs had become bhoomiswami with respect to the suit land prior to the appointment of the receiver. Therefore, receiver's possession could not be said to be on behalf of the defendants. - Stand taken by the defendants/appellants is totally untenable. - Further since, findings of fact arrived at by the lower appellate court were contrary to evidence on record and, therefore perverse, the High Court was fully justified in setting aside the same resulting in the appeal being allowed and suit being decreed.
