Oil & Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. v SAW Pipes Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 362; (2003) 5 SCC 705; AIR 2003 SC 2629; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 82; 2003 (2) ARBLR 5; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 245; 2003 (3) CompLJ 1; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 148; JT 2003 (4) SC 171; 2003 (2) RAJ 1; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 554; 2003(4) SCALE 92; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 691; 2003 (1) SCW 3041; 2003 (3) SLT 324; 2003 (3) Supreme 449

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Award Compensation, New York Convention of 1958 (ENG), Geneva Convention Act of 1937 (ENG), English Arbitration Act, 1996 (ENG), Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Breach of the contract - Entitlement of damages - Determination of - Held, (1) terms of the contract were required to be taken into consideration before arriving at the conclusion whether the party claiming damages is entitled to the same; (2) if the terms were clear and unambiguous stipulating the liquidated damages in case of the breach of the contract unless it Was held that such estimate of damages/compensation was unreasonable or was by way of penalty, party who had committed the breach was required to pay such compensation and that was what was provided in s. 73 of the Contract Act; (3) S. 74 was to be read along with s. 73 and, therefore, in every case of breach of contract, the person aggrieved by the breach was not required to prove actual loss or damage suffered by him before he could claim a decree - Court was competent to award reasonable compensation in case of breach even if no actual damage was proved to have been suffered in consequence of the breach of a contract; (4) in some contracts, it would be impossible for the Court to assess the compensation arising from breach and if the compensation contemplated was not by way of penalty or unreasonable, Court could award the same if it was genuine pre-estimate by the parties as the measure of reasonable compensation - Appeal allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Court's Jurisdiction u/s. 34 of the Act - Whether the Court would have jurisdiction u/s. 34 of the Act to set aside an award passed by the Arbitral Tribunal which was patently illegal or in contravention of the provisions of the Act or any other substantive law governing the parties or was against the terms of the contract? - Held, Court could set aside the arbitral award u/s. 34(2) of the Act if the party making the application furnishes proof that: (i) a party was under some incapacity, or (ii) the arbitration agreement was not valid under the law to which the parties have subjected it or, failing any indication thereon, under the law for the time being in force; or (iii) the party making the application was not given proper notice of the appointment of an arbitrator or of the arbitral proceedings or was otherwise unable to present his case; or (iv) the arbitral award deals with a dispute not contemplated by or not falling within the terms of the submission to arbitration, or it contains decisions on matters beyond the scope of the submission to arbitration - Appeal allowed.

(C) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Court's Jurisdiction u/s. 34 of the Act - Application for setting aside arbitral award - Held, Court may set aside the award: (1) if the composition of the arbitral tribunal was not in accordance with the agreement of the parties - Also, failing such agreement, the composition of the arbitral tribunal was not in accordance with Part-I of the Act - (2) If the arbitral procedure was not in accordance with: (a) the agreement of the parties, or (b) failing such agreement, the arbitral procedure was not in accordance with Part-I of the Act - However, exception for setting aside the award on the ground of composition of arbitral tribunal or illegality of arbitral procedure was that the agreement should not be in conflict with the provisions of Part-I of the Act from which parties could not derogate - (3) If the award passed by the arbitral tribunal was in contravention of provisions of the Act or any other substantive law governing the parties or was against the terms of the contract - Appeal allowed.

(D) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Application for setting aside arbitral award - Held, award could be set aside if it was against the public policy of India, that is to say, if it was contrary to: (a) fundamental policy of Indian law; (b) the interest of India; or (c) justice or morality, or (d) if it was patently illegal - Appeal allowed.
