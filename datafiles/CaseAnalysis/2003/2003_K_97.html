Sankar Ram and Company v Kasi Naicker and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 568; (2003) 11 SCC 699; AIR 2003 SC 4156; 2003 (52) ALR 607; 2003 (4) AWC 2874; 2003 (3) BC 402; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 56; JT 2003 (6) SC 232; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 162; 2003(5) SCALE 574; 2003 (1) SCW 3732; 2003 (4) SLT 669; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 930; 2003 (5) Supreme 244

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.33(2)(b) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920 - ss. 28, 28(7), 55 - Whether protection provided in proviso to s.55 of Provincial Insolvency Act is available to a bona fide transferee for valuable consideration after presentation of any insolvency petition but before date of passing of order for adjudication without notice of presentation of insolvency petition by or against debtor - Held, order of adjudication and presentation of insolvency petition are two different events essentially referring to two different dates when in same proviso, legislature consciously made a clear statement as to two different dates, they should be given effect to - If intention of proviso to s.55 was not to protect even a bona fide transferee for valuable consideration without notice of presentation of insolvency petition before an order of adjudication was made, legislature could have simply said any transaction taking place after date of presentation of any insolvency petition by or against debtor instead of qualifying transaction that takes place before date of order of adjudication - Further held, a bona fide transferee for valuable consideration without knowledge of presentation of insolvency petition on date of transfer of property is to be protected.
