Union of India v R. Padmanabhan

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 616; (2003) 7 SCC 270; AIR 2003 SC 3274; 2003 (110) ECR 249; 2003 (156) E.L.T. 625; JT 2003 (7) SC 196; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 702; 2003 (3) KLT 655; 2003 (57) RLT 851; 2003(6) SCALE 393; 2003 (5) SLT 179; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 432; 2003 (6) Supreme 154; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2652

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: [A.] Excise - Customs - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Customs Act, 1962 - Gold Control Act, 1968 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Award - Appellant-Govt authorized State Police authorities to effect seizure and investigation of cases under Central Excise and Salt Act, Customs Act, Gold Control Order and Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, to prevent smuggling of gold and other articles through coastal areas of Kerala, as well as in other parts of country with a view to create an incentive generally in matter of detection of such violations, proposed to grant awards to those responsible to assist Govt. in same by being informants as well as Govt. servants - No sanction was made in favour of respondent for reason that he was holding a rank considered higher than that of Assistant Collector/Assistant Director and consequently held not eligible for reward in terms of cl 7 1 of Notification - Held, interpretation placed as though restrictions in cl. 7 will have no relevance to officers other than officers specified of Department of Central Excise/Customs cannot be justified, either on language of Guidelines or on conspectus of Scheme for rewards classification made is between informers on one hand and Govt. servants on other, and not with reference to any particular class of category of Govt. servants alone specification of certain officers are meant to illustrate level and standard of their category/class with particular reference to gradation of offices they hold and granting authority or courts, if need be, have to necessarily arrived at equivalence in other Departments as well, from among other class of Govt. servants, as a whole, serving either under State or Central Govt, and Scheme cannot be mutilated otherwise or moulded to suit consideration of a particular or given case, favourably.
