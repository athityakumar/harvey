P.V. Radhakrishna v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 544; (2003) 6 SCC 443; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1679; AIR 2003 SC 2859; 2003 (3) CCR 47; 2003 (3) Crimes 180; 2003 (6) CRJ 489; 2003 CRLJ 3717; 2003 (2) DMC 220; 2003 (2) JCC 1195; JT 2003 (6) SC 84; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 869; 2003(5) SCALE 438; 2003 (4) SLT 603; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 745; 2003 (5) Supreme 98; 2003 (2) UC 1269

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.32, 60 - Dying declaration - Whether a dying declaration recorded by a police official and not by a magistrate can be considered to be a dying declaration as is understood in law and form sole ground of conviction - Held, if after careful scrutiny, court is satisfied that dying declaration is true and free from any effort to induce deceased to make a false statement, and if it is coherent and consistent, there shall be no legal impediment to make it basis of conviction, even if there is no corroboration - Further held, if person recording dying declaration is satisfied that declarant is in a fit medical condition to make a dying declaration, then such dying declaration will not be invalid solely on ground that is not certified by doctor as to condition of declarant to make dying declaration - Percentage of burns alone would not determine probability or otherwise of making dying declaration - Appeal dismissed.
