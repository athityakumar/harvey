State Of Rajasthan v Raja Ram

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 630; (2003) 8 SCC 180; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1965; AIR 2003 SC 3601; 2003 (3) CCR 198; 2003 (3) Crimes 346; 2003 (6) CRJ 621; 2003 CRLJ 3901; 2003 (3) JCC 1372; JT 2003 (7) SC 399; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 238; 2003(6) SCALE 489; 2003 (5) SLT 45; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 445; 2003 (6) Supreme 11

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302 - Acquittal - Legality of judgment holding the accused as innocent and was entitled to acquittal - Prosecution version rested on circumstantial evidence - Held, circumstantial evidence is not direct to the point in issue but consists of evidence of various other facts which are so closely associated with the fact in issue that taken together they form a chain of circumstances from which the existence of the principal fact can be legally inferred or presumed - Further held, if the evidence relating to extra judicial confession is found credible after being tested on the touchstone of credibility and acceptability, it can solely form the basis of conviction. The non-acceptability by evidence relating to alleged extra-judicial confession, the conclusions of the High Court cannot be said to be one which are unsupportable.
