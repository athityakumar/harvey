Chandra Singh v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 541; (2003) 6 SCC 545; (2003) SCC (L&S) 951; AIR 2003 SC 2889; 2003 (5) ESC 296; 2003 (98) FLR 905; JT 2003 (6) SC 20; 2003 (3) LLN 1186; 2003(5) SCALE 361; 2004 (1) SLJ 401; 2003 (4) SLR 706; 2003 (4) SLT 567; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 674; 2003 (5) Supreme 128; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3210

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955, s.24 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s.22 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 142, 235 - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951, rr. 56, 56A - Rajasthan Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1996, r. 53 - Rajasthan Civil Service (Pension) Rules, 1997, r. 53 - Rajasthan Service (Amendment) Rules, 1998 - Retirement on attaining age of superannuation - Benefit of extension of service up to age of 60 years - Held, in terms of direction of this Court, their cases could not have been reviewed as on said date - Despite same, however, Review Committee of HC considered question as to whether having regard to their performance their service should be extended or not in terms of exception contained in r.56, although amended rules had not come into force - They were found ineligible for extension of their services and recommendations were made by Full Court of Rajasthan HC that they be compulsorily retired - Further held, exception provided for in r.56 of said Rules also is a pointer to fact that said Rules apply to judicial officers - Unless said rules are per se applicable, question of making any exception to applicability thereof would not arise - In other words, exclusionary clause contained in exception points out to applicability of rules and thus it must be held that members of judicial service come with in purview thereof actions on part of HC or State in compulsorily retiring appellants herein were illegal - It is not a fit case where this Court should exercise its discretionary jurisdiction in favour of appellant - HC and State Govt. to pay all retiral benefits to appellants.
