Surya Prakash Jaiswal v Osmania University and another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1578; (2007) 15 SCC 716

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Eligibility, Caste Status

Summary: Service - Appointment - Caste Status - Eligibility - Respondent no. 2 was appointed as lecturer in respondent no. 1-university - Appellant was also an applicant for appointment to the said post but he was not selected - Consequently, respondent no. 2 was not considered for promotion to the post of Reader as he did not belong to the reserved category of BC (B) - It is thereafter that appellant filed petition seeking direction for consideration of his case for appointment as a Lecturer on the ground that the respondent no. 2 was not at all eligible to be considered for appointment in 1988 as a Lecturer as not belonging to the reserved category of BC (B) - HC held that appointment of respondent no. 2 could not be upset by holding an enquiry with regard to her caste status - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it was the Mandal Revenue Officer who was the competent authority to issue a caste status certificate and that the caste status certificate issued to the respondent no. 2 has not been cancelled. It is not right in entertaining the petition after a period of ten years and the petition ought to have been dismissed on the ground of delay and laches. Appeal dismissed.
