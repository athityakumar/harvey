Bhargavan and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1000; (2004) 12 SCC 414; AIR 2004 SC 1058; 2003 (4) CCR 396; 2004 (1) Crimes 412; 2004 CRLJ 646; 2004 (1) JCC 83; JT 2003 (9) SC 513; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 667; 2004 (1) KLT 201; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 452; 2003(9) SCALE 627; 2003 (1) SCW 6745; 2003 (7) SLT 228; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 535; 2004 (2) Supreme 408

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 143, 148, 149, 302 - High Court dismissed the appeals filed by the appellants confirming the conviction and sentences imposed by the Trial Court - Interestedness of witnesses - Common object - Held, relationship is not a factor to affect credibility of a witness. It is more often than not that a relation would not conceal actual culprit and make allegations against an innocent person. Foundation has to be laid if plea of false implication is made - Further held, the 'common object' of an assembly is to be ascertained from the acts and language of the members composing it, and from a consideration of all the surrounding circumstances - Further, the Trial Court and the High Court were justified in convicting the accused and awarding sentences consequentially, and there is no infirmity in the reasons indicated by the Trial Court, the conclusions arrived at by it as affirmed by the High Court, to warrant interference.
