Malkhansingh and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 494; (2003) 5 SCC 746; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1247; AIR 2003 SC 2669; 2003 (3) CCR 1; 2003 (3) Crimes 123; 2003 (6) CRJ 444; 2003 CRLJ 3535; 2003 (2) JCC 1121; JT 2003 (5) SC 323; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 341; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 550; 2003(5) SCALE 152; 2003 (4) SLT 313; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 443; 2003 (4) Supreme 394

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code - ss. 376(2)(G), 506 - Scheduled Castes & Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 - s. 3(1)(x) - Evidence Act - s. 9 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - s. 162 - Rape - Whether the conviction of the appellants can be sustained on the basis of the identification of the appellants by the prosecutrix in court without holding a test identification parade in the course of investigation - Held, what weight must be attached to the evidence of identification in court, which is not preceded by a test identification parade, is a matter for the courts of fact to examine - Further held, the prosecutrix appears to be a witness on whom implicit reliance can be placed and there is no reason why she should falsely identify the appellants as the perpetrators of the crime if they had not actually committed the offence.
