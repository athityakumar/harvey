Executive Engineer, Forest and Fisheries v Sumra Ali Ismail and others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1575; (2007) 15 SCC 540

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Executive Engineer, Forest and Fisheries Development Scheme

Summary: Service - Appointment - Termination of services - Back wages - Appellant was Executive Engineer, Forest and Fisheries Development Scheme - Respondents were appointed on daily wage basis and services of respondents were terminated - Appellants contended before Labour Court that respondents were appointed on work charge basis for fixed period and after completion their services automatically terminated - Labour Court concluded that termination was illegal and six respondents were paid 80% of back wages and respondent no.7 was employed elsewhere so back wages were confined to 20% - Appellant challenged order of Labour Court which was dismissed by Trial Court - Order of Trial Court was challenged before First Appellate Court which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, that Labour Court, after examining evidence on record came to conclusion that Scheme of Fisheries and Port Development had continued and after completion of one project appellant had started another project. Court do not find any merit in contention raised by appellants that services of these respondents were not required in new project. Appellant Department is unnecessarily burdened with payment of back wages and it would cause serious financial burden on Department. Respondents were out of service for a period of nearly 15 years. Respondents are educated persons and they must have been engaged in some employment, at best for some period out of the 15 years they were out of employment. It is just and proper that back wages payable to Respondent nos. 1 to 6 be restricted to 50% of amount already ordered by Labour Court for period they were out of service. As regards back wages paid to respondent no.7 Court find no reason to interfere. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio – When project was continuing, appointment of other employees on temporary basis for new project and termination of services of persons already appointed as daily-wage clerks is illegal.
