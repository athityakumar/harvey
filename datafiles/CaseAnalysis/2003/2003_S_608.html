Kamalakar Nandram Bhavsar and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1383; (2004) 10 SCC 192; AIR 2004 SC 503; 2004 CRLJ 615; JT 2003 (9) SC 570; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 228; 2003(10) SCALE 61; 2003 (1) SCW 6542

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 235, 235(2), 306, 340 -

Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 306, 498(A), 498A - Dying Declaration - Suicide - Harassment - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 306 and 498-A r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court relying on dying declaration by deceased acquitted appellants of all charges - On appeal HC by impugned judgment had set aside judgment of acquittal and convicted appellants for offences punishable u/s. 306 IPC and directed them to undergo RI for 10 years - HC convicted appellants for offence punishable u/s. 498-A IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 3 years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, it was clear from evidence of witnesses that so far as harassment meted out to deceased said prosecution witnesses had consistently spoken about involvement of husband, mother-in-law and sister-in-law - Therefore, Court opined that HC was justified in holding these accused persons responsible for offences punishable u/ss. 306 and 498-A r/w s. 34 IPC because by such harassment meted out to deceased, accused persons had abetted her suicide -With regard to appellant No. 2 no evidence at all was found to hold him guilty of offence therefore it was held that HC had erred - Taking into consideration individual roles played by accused persons in abetting suicide of victim and respective age as well as family circumstances Court modified sentence awarded by HC and maintained direction issued by HC that 80% of fine amount be paid to parents of deceased as compensation - Appeal partly allowed.
