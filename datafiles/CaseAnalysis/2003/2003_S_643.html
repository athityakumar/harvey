Mohinder Singh and Another v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 740; (2004) 12 SCC 311; AIR 2003 SC 4399; 2003 (4) CCR 19; 2003 (4) Crimes 150; 2004 (1) CRJ 84; 2003 CRLJ 5002; 2003 (3) JCC 1943; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 117; 2003(7) SCALE 372; 2003 (1) SCW 5033; 2003 (5) SLT 411; 2003 (6) Supreme 836; 2004 (1) UC 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 148, 149, 201, 302, 323, 324, 326 - Eye witness - Held, having considered the judgments of the two courts below and having independently perused the evidence, the conclusion is that the courts below have misdirected themselves while coming to the conclusion that the prosecution has proved its case beyond reasonable doubt - Further held, the benefit of the acquittal that is being granted to the appellants herein, has to be extended to other accused persons also based on the conclusion that on an evaluation of the evidence led by the prosecution in this case, no conviction of any accused is possible.
