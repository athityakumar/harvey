Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta v M/s. Sharma Chemical Works

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 398; (2003) 5 SCC 60; AIR 2003 SC 2448; 2003 (108) ECR 481; 2003 (154) E.L.T. 328; 2003 (56) RLT 123; 2003(4) SCALE 437; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1027; 2003 (3) SLT 406; 2003 (132) STC 251; 2003 (3) Supreme 634

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicament

Summary: Excise - whether "Bhanphool Oil" is classifiable as a "perfumed hair oil" or as an "Ayurvedic Medicament"- held, the main criteria for determining classification is normally the use it is put to by the customers who use it. The burden of proving that Banphool Oil is understood by the customers as an hair oil was on the revenue. This burden is not discharged as no such proof is adduced. On the contrary it is found that the oil can be used for treatment of headache, eye problem, night blindness, reeling, head weak memory, hysteria, ammenesia, blood pressure, insomnia etc. The dosage required are also set out on the label. The product is registered with Drug Controller and is being manufactured under a drug licence. Further, on the matter being referred to the Drug Controller, it opined that it is an Ayurvedic medicament. - Therefore, the Banphool Oil has been rightly classified as an Ayurvedic medicament under Tariff Item 3003.30
