Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 242; (2003) 4 SCC 200; AIR 2003 SC 1909; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 65; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 587; JT 2003 (2) SC 457; 2003(2) SCALE 618; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 530; 2003 (2) SLT 671; 2003 (2) Supreme 500

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act - Ss. 23 and 28 - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1996 - S. 126(3) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Art. 14 - Whether amendments made to the LA Act by Central Act No. 68 of 1984 to ss. 23 and 28 which confer certain additional monetary benefits to the land holders are to be made applicable to the instant acquisition? - Held, there is nothing in the MRTP Act which precludes adopting the construction that the provision of L A Act, as amended by 1984 Act relating to award of compensation, would apply with full vigour to the acquisition of land under that Act - Further, unless such interpretation is placed on s. 126(3) the acquisition under MRTP Act will be afflicted with the vice of invidious discrimination and palpable arbitrariness hit by art. 14 of the Constitution - Land-holders whose lands are acquired under Chapter VII of MRTP Act cannot be subjected to a disability or disadvantage in the matter of obtaining monetary recompense for the deprivation of land depending upon the nature of public purpose or the authority for whose benefit the land is acquired - Appeal dismissed.
