Narendra Nath Bhalla v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1561; (2007) 15 SCC 775

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: False certificate

Summary: Service - False certificate - Misconduct - Dismissal - Legality - Appellant/employee and was working as Stamp Clerk - On finding certain irregularities and misconduct appellant was suspended - Disciplinary authority on consideration of inquiry report passed dismissal order - Aggrieved appellant filed claim petition before Tribunal and the same was dismissed - Appellant filed petition before DB and the same was dismissed affirming order passed by Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned dismissal order passed against appellant was justified - Held, in light of facts of present case, it was not been shown as to how any prejudice was caused to appellant by mere non-furnishing of inquiry report even when he said that he had no defense to produce - Mere repayment of money does not absolve him of serious charge of misappropriation - Last submission that punishment imposed on appellant was too harsh also did not appeal to SC, regard to serious nature of charges including misappropriation of money and issuing receipt on plain paper and reflecting it in official record as something else - Penalty was modified on peculiar facts of that case - Appeal dismissed.
