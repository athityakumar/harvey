K.T. Venatagiri and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 192; (2003) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2003 SC 1819; 2003(2) SCALE 365; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1081; 2003 (1) SCW 1270; 2003 (4) SLT 273; 2003 (2) Supreme 260

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Karnataka Excise Act, 1965 - Karnataka Excise (Sale of Indian and Foreign Liquors) Rules, 1968 - adjustment of equity - Held, the appellants admittedly took benefit of the interim order passed by the Supreme Court in Khoday's case. They cannot, having regard to the doctrine of 'unjust enrichment' retain the undue advantage derived by it. They must be asked to pay back the amount received either directly or indirectly on account of MSIL. The doctrine of restitution must, thus, be applied in these appeals.
