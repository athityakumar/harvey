Union Of India v B.N. Jha

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 221; (2003) SCC (L&S) 488; (2003) 4 SCC 531; AIR 2003 SC 1416; 2003 (3) AWC 1948; 2003 (3) CalLT(SC) 15; 2003 (2) CLR 134; 2003 (103) DLT 620; 2003 (3) ESC 314; 2003 (97) FLR 1034; JT 2003 (3) SC 201; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 608; 2003 LabIC 2653; 2003 (2) LLN 373; 2003(3) SCALE 8; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 721; 2003 (2) SLJ 395; 2003 (3) SLR 365; 2003 (2) SLT 577; 2003 (2) Supreme 560; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 864

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Border Security Force Rules - Rules 45B and 46 - respondent dismissed from service by an order passed by the General Security Force Court - on appeal the learned Single Judge of the High Court held that there had been a gross violation of Rule 45B and 46 of the Border Security Force Rules. It further held that a very valuable right of the respondent in the form of protection from bias had been denied to him. The Division Bench went through the entire records and arrived at the same finding. - Held, the findings of the learned Single Judge or the Division Bench cannot be said to be perverse or contrary to law.
