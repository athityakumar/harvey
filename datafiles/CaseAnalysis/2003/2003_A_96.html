State of West Bengal v Amritlal Chatterjee

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 709; (2003) 10 SCC 572; AIR 2003 SC 4564; 2003 (52) ARBLR 158; 2003 (6) AWC 4728; 2004 (3) CompLJ 11; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 130; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 308; 2003 (3) RAJ 267; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 252; 2003(7) SCALE 274; 2003 (1) SCW 5339; 2003 (5) SLT 375; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 346; 2003 (7) Supreme 85

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Foreign Award

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - ss. 5, 11, 12 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - s. 21 - Removal of arbitrator and appointment of a new arbitrator - Applicability of s. 21 - Held, if the arbitrator fails in his duty to enter on the reference or make a public ward during the period stipulated under r. 3 of the First Schedule, indisputably a cause of action will arise for his removal or appointment of a new arbitrator in terms of ss. 11 and 12 of the 1940 Act. The words 'commencement of the arbitration proceedings' have not been defined in the 1940 Act. They have to be given their ordinary meaning having regard to the provisions contained in Chapter II thereof - Further held, s. 85(2)(a) of the new Act may have to be construed keeping in view the provisions contained in s. 21 of the new Act.
