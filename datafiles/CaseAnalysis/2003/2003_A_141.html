Central Bureau of Investigation v Ashiq Hussain Faktoo & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 52; (2003) 3 SCC 166; (2003) SCC (Cr) 588; AIR 2003 SC 1441; 2003 (1) CCR 317; 2003 (3) Crimes 79; 2003 (4) CRJ 456; 2003 CRLJ 1292; 2003 (1) JCC 316; JT 2003 (1) SC 565; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 605; 2003(1) SCALE 514; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 733; 2003 (1) SLT 709; 2003 (4) Supreme 147

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - S. 3 - IPC - Ss. 302 r/w 120B - offences under - evidence based on confessional statements - respondents acquitted by the Presiding Officer, TADA Court, Jammu on the ground that the confessional statements were not in conformity with the provisions of law - Held, when the confessional statements had been made before the Superintendent of Police, CBI, voluntarily and at the end of each confessional statements it was also recorded that the confessional statement had been read over and admitted to be correct and required memorandum under Rule 15(3)(b) of the TADA Act also made on each of the confessional statements, then the Designated Court was wrong in holding that the requirement of law were not complied with. - Confessional statements under Section 15 of TADA Act can be used against the Respondents.
