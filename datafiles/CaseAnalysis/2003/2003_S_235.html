St. Johns Teachers Training Institute v Regional Director, National Council for Teacher Education and another

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 177; (2003) 3 SCC 321; AIR 2003 SC 1533; 2003 (2) AWC 1076; 2003 (2) ESC 150; JT 2003 (2) SC 35; 2003(1) SCALE 757; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 975; 2003 (2) SLR 301; 2003 (2) SLT 286; 2003 (2) Supreme 166; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: National Council for Teacher Education Regulations, 1995 - Reg. 5(e) and 5(f) - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - whether Regulations 5(e) and (f) framed by National Council for Teachers Education are ultra vires the provisions of the Act - held, the impugned Regulations do not contravene any one of the conditions inasmuch as Section 32 of the Act clearly empowers the Council to make Regulations generally to carry out the provisions of the Act and thus they come within the scope and purview of the power of the authority framing the Regulations. The Regulations also conform to the provisions of the Act and are not in excess of the authority of the Council as no essential legislative function has been delegated to the State Government. - Therefore, the impugned Regulations are perfectly valid and intra vires the Act.
