Tejumal Bhojwani And Ors v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 672; (2003) 10 SCC 525; AIR 2003 SC 3791; 2003 (53) ALR 426; 2003 (4) AWC 3392; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 194; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 11; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 551; 2003(7) SCALE 38; 2003 (1) SCW 4335; 2003 (5) SLT 138; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 1044; 2003 (6) Supreme 538; 2004 (1) UC 46; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2775

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965 - ss. 28, 32 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - s. 4 - Claimants not satisfied by the compensation awarded by the Land Acquisition Officer for the land, the structure standing on the land as well as the tube well - Whether solaltium and interest should have been awarded as per the Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984; and (2) whether appropriate compensation should have been awarded for structures and tube wells situated on the land concerned - Held, each of the appellants are entitled to solatium @ 30%, interest and additional compensation; (2) the claimants are entitled to separate compensation for land, tube well and structure - Further held, it is true that the deduction for development charges ought to be adequately provided for, but it varies from place to place, area to area and amount of developments which are required to be carried out and thus there cannot be any fixed amount of deduction towards development charges.
