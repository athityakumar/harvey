Dwarka Prasad Agarwal (D) by LRs. and Another v Ramesh Chandra Agarwala and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 489; (2003) 6 SCC 220; AIR 2003 SC 2696; 2003 (4) AWC 2975; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 206; JT 2003 (6) SC 377; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 354; 2003(5) SCALE 131; 2003 (1) SCW 3328; 2003 (4) SLT 404; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 376; 2003 (4) Supreme 497; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2089

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867 ss. 5, 8-B, 8-C- Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, s.145 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9, o. 39, rr. 1, 2 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s.6 - Companies Act, 1956 - (A) Whether HC was justified in issuing a direction that its earlier direction directing Appellate Board to dispose of appeal within three months need not be adhered to, if Ramesh Chander Agarwal files an application for stay of inquiry by District Magistrate during pendency of civil suit? - (B) Whether civil court had any jurisdiction to entertain suit? - Held, (A) when a disputed question as regard right of one partner against other to file a declaration in terms of provisions of Act had arisen for consideration, HC was not correct in issuing a subsequent direction in review petition - Further held, while directing statutory authority to dispose of matter in accordance with law; it does not stand to any reason as to why a party to lis was given such liberty so as to file an application for stay of inquiry by District Magistrate till disposed of civil suit particularly when HC itself was of opinion that suit was not maintainable - Orders of HC are, thus, absolutely contradictory to and inconsistent with each other, and do not stand a moment's scrutiny - (B) dispute between parties was eminently a civil dispute and not a dispute under provisions of Companies Act s. 9 - CPC confers jurisdiction upon civil courts to determine all dispute of civil nature unless same is barred under a statute either expressly or by necessary implication - Appeal allowed.
