Maruti Rama Naik v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 698; (2003) 10 SCC 670; AIR 2003 SC 3884; 2003 (4) Crimes 307; 2004 (1) CRJ 45; 2003 CRLJ 4326; JT 2003 (7) SC 627; 2003(7) SCALE 270; 2003 (1) SCW 4468; 2003 (5) SLT 353; 2003 (7) Supreme 323; 2004 (1) UC 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 147, 148, 302 r/w149, 307 r/w 149, 325 r/w 149 and 337 r/w 149 - Unreliable evidence - Held, taking into account the nature of injuries suffered by this witness and the opportunity investigating officer had to record his statement, the explanation given by the prosecution for delay is not convincing - Further held, the only other piece of evidence relied by the prosecution to support its case would not at all be sufficient to base a conviction without further acceptable corroboration.
