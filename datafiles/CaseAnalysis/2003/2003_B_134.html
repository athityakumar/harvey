Narbada Devi Gupta v Birendra Kumar Jaiswal and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 925; (2003) 8 SCC 745; AIR 2004 SC 175; 2004 (1) ALD(SC) 64; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 205; JT 2003 (8) SC 267; 2004 (136) PLR 405; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 683; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 564; 2003(9) SCALE 210; 2003 (1) SCW 5861; 2003 (6) SLT 527; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 90; 2003 (7) Supreme 664

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Death Of Plaintiff

Summary: Land & Property - Recovery of possession - Admissibility and evidentiary value of the rent receipts - Held, the High Court being the first court of appeal was fully within its powers to re-examine and re-appreciate the documentary and oral evidence. It could come to a conclusion contrary to the one reached by the trial court -Further held, the High Court was fully justified in taking a contrary view as it did and upsetting the judgment of the trial court resulting in dismissal of the suit.
