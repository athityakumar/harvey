Messrs Tafcon Projects (I) Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 801; (2004) 13 SCC 788; AIR 2004 SC 949; 2003 (1) SCW 7269

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 77 - Transaction of Business Rules, 1961, r. 3 - Appointment of 'Event Manager' - Tenders invited from parties with proven credentials in field of organising Tourism related Expositions by Ministry of Tourism - Main criteria for assigning tender to most suitable party was guarantee of highest 'upfront payment' - Appellant chosen as highest bidder and an agreement was entered into - HC set aside appointment of appellant as 'Event Manager' and directed issue of fresh tenders - Held, M/s Tafcon's offer was much better than that of respondent No 3 and its selection cannot be faulted - Besides, respondent No 3 had bid pursuant to tender notice and participated in proceedings before Selection Committee - It cannot now take advantage of any alleged vagueness in tender notice - Further held, respondent No 3 having effectively stopped operation of three years period by reason of writ proceedings before DB of HC, appellant must not be denied benefit, which it would otherwise have got had WP not been filed.
