State of Haryana and Another v Ankur Gupta

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 687; (2003) 7 SCC 704; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1165; AIR 2003 SC 3797; 2003 (4) ESC 619; 2003 (99) FLR 295; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 96; 2003 LabIC 3152; 2004 (136) PLR 66; 2003(7) SCALE 166; 2004 (1) SLJ 507; 2003 (7) SLR 4; 2003 (6) SLT 334; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 333; 2003 (6) Supreme 431

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: [A.] Service -Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14, 16 - Die-in-harness scheme - Compassionate appointment nullified in view of a new policy after respondent had worked for 4 years on ground that surviving parent was in Govt. service - Held, logic of policy can not be undermined - No provision could be shown whereby relaxation is permissible, particularly when policy in this respect was modified on basis of and in implementation of decision of HC - 1970 guidelines do not assist respondent because that was operative at a point of time when policy dated 22/8/1996 notified to be in line with HC's judgment was not operative - Further held, in case respondent applies for a job in Govt. within a period of 2 years and is selected de hors compassionate appointment scheme, question of his having crossed age bar, would not stand on his way and service rendered by him shall be duly considered.
