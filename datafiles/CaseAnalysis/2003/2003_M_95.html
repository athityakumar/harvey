Supreme Court of India

06 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 171; (2003) 4 SCC 147; AIR 2003 SC 1475; 2003 (2) AWC 989; 2003 (103) DLT 20; JT 2003 (1) SC 607; 2003 (1) RCJ 85; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 347; 2003(1) SCALE 722; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 918; 2003 (1) SCW 819; 2003 (2) SLT 1; 2003 (1) Supreme 946

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - whether a decree for ejectment passed by a civil court qua a commercial tenancy in the State of Delhi before the declaration of law by the Supreme Court in Gian Devi Anand vs. Jeevan Kumar, 1985 Suppl. (1) SCR 1, that such a tenancy is heritable, is executable or the judgment-debtors can successfully object to the execution of the decree on the ground that same was passed by a court lacking inherent jurisdiction and therefore inexecutable? - held, the jurisdiction of the civil court to pass the decree for ejectment was barred. A decree passed by a Court having no jurisdiction over the subject matter would be a nullity and the judgment-debtor can object to the execution of such a decree being a nullity and non est. Its invalidity can be set up whenever it is sought to be enforced including the stage of execution of the decree or any other collateral proceedings.
