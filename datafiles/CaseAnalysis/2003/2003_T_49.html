M. Prabhulal v The Assistant Director, Directorate of Revenue

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 762; (2003) 8 SCC 449; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2024; AIR 2003 SC 4311; 2003 (2) CGLJ 342; 2003 (4) Crimes 217; 2004 (1) CRJ 410; 2003 CRLJ 4996; 2003 (89) ECC 697; 2003 (3) JCC 1631; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 459; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 496; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 160; 2003 (58) RLT 717; 2003(7) SCALE 649; 2003 (6) SLT 464; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - ss. 8(c), 8(1), 18, 21, 29, 67 - Conviction primarily based on the statements recorded under s. 67 by officers of Department of Revenue Intelligence - Admissibility of evidence - Non-compliance of s. 42 - Held, the appellants did not make any complaint before the Magistrate before whom they were produced, complaining of any torture or harassment. It is only when their statements were recorded by the trial judge under s. 313, CrPC, 1973, that a vague stand about the torture was taken. Under these circumstances, the confessional statements cannot be held to be involuntary. The statements were voluntarily made and can, thus, be made the basis of appellants' conviction - Further held, s. 42(2) is not applicable when search, seizure etc. is conducted by a Gazetted Officer under ss. 41(2) and (3).
