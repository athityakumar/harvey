Prakash H. Jain v Ms. Marie Fernandes

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 789; (2003) 8 SCC 431; AIR 2003 SC 4591; 2003 (53) ALR 421; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 528; 2003(8) SCALE 29; 2003 (1) SCW 5378; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1011

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act, Andhra Pradesh (Telengana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950, Railway Act 1890, Delhi Rent Control Rules, 1959,

Summary: Rent Control - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, ss.24, 42 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Eviction of tenant and also for compensation on ground that leave/licence has expired by efflux of time - Condonation of delay - Competent authority by an order allowed applications for condonation of delay and also granted leave to defend - Respondent challenged order - HC set aside order - Held, Competent Authority constituted under and for purposes of provisions contained is merely and at best a statutory authority created for a definite purpose and to exercise, no doubt, powers in a quasi-judicial manner but its powers are strictly circumscribed by very statutory provisions which conferred upon it those powers and same could be exercised in manner provided therefor and subject to such conditions and limitations stipulated by very provision of law under which Competent Authority itself has been created - Further held, net result of an application/affidavit with grounds of defence and leave to contest, not having been filed within time as has been stipulated in statute itself as a condition precedent for Competent Authority to proceed further to enquire into merits of defence, Competent Authority is obliged, under constraining influence of compulsion statutorily cast upon it, to pass orders of eviction in manner envisaged in s 43(4)(a) of Act.
