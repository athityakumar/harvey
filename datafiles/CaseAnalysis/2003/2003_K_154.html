Kunju Muhammed @ Khumani & Anr v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 594; (2004) 9 SCC 193; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1425; JT 2003 (7) SC 114; 2003(6) SCALE 254; 2004 (1) UC 74

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 313, 378(3), 379 - Indian Penal code, 1860 - ss. 34, 302, 323, 324 - Judgment of the Sessions Judge, acquitting the accused, reversed by the High Court - Reappreciation of evidence by the High Court in appeal against acquittals - Held, the High Court in the instant case has not come to the conclusion that the finding of the Sessions Court was in any manner perverse or one that cannot be arrived at by a reasonable person. Therefore, assuming another view was possible to be taken on the material on record, the High Court ought not to have substituted its view in place of that of the Sessions Court, and reverse an order of acquittal on such substituted view of its own - Further held, the view taken by the learned Sessions Judge was the only possible view, hence, the High Court ought not to have interfered with the same.
