Harishchandra Hegde v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1425; (2004) 9 SCC 780; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 75; JT 2005 (11) SC 331; 2004(1) SCALE 48; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights; Land & Property

Keywords: Defective Title

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 51 - Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1978, ss. 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether s. 51 of Transfer of Property Act is applicable in cases covered by ss. 4 and 5 of Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1978 - Held, by reason of an order passed u/s. 4 of Act, lands are directed to be restored in event illegalities specified therein are discovered - Consequences contained in s. 5 of Act apply automatically in event an order u/s. 4 of Act is passed - S. 4 of Act contains a non obstante clause - Said provision would, thus, apply notwithstanding anything contained in any agreement or any other Act for time being in force - Act is a special Act whereas Transfer of Property Act is a general Act and in that view of matter also s. 51 of Transfer of Property Act will have no application and consequences contained in s. 5 would prevail - Appeal dismissed.
