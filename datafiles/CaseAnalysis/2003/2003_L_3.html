State Bank of India v L. Kannaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 660; (2003) 10 SCC 499; AIR 2003 SC 3860; 2003 (99) FLR 398; JT 2005 (11) SC 326; 2003 LabIC 2162; 2003 (3) LLJ 1070; 2003 (4) LLN 98; 2003(6) SCALE 603; 2003 (1) SCW 4432; 2003 (6) SLR 8; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 735; 2003 (6) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - State Bank Of India Act, 1955, s. 50 - State Bank of India Employees' Pension Fund Rules - rr.7, 8 - Benefit of pension denied to appellants 1 and 2 on ground of non-completion of 20 years of service in bank and to appellants 3 and 4 on ground that they were originally employed with another bank - Held, circular issued by Govt. of India, does not obligate managements of banks to add military service to service rendered in bank for purpose of pension - Further held, bar under r 8(a) of Rules does not apply to these appellants 3 and 4 - If so, when ex-servicemen were taken out of excluded category by virtue of Circular dated 8/4/1974, and they became eligible to be admitted to benefits of SBI Employees' Provident and Pension Fund, it would be travesty of justice if pension is denied to appellants 3 and 4 merely because they were originally employed by Imperial Bank of India - They should not be driven to a situation in which they would be disabled from availing of benefits either under Imperial Bank Pension Fund or SBI Pension Fund.
