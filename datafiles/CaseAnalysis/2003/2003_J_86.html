United India Insurance Company Limited v Jyotsnaben Sudhirbhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 610; (2003) 7 SCC 212; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1602; 2003 (3) ACC 1; AIR 2003 SC 3127; 2003 (53) ALR 131; [2003] 116 Comp Cas 662; 2003 (3) G.L.R. 2552; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 68; JT 2003 (6) SC 547; 2003(6) SCALE 288; 2003 (1) SCW 4111; 2003 (4) SLT 868; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 380; 2003 (5) Supreme 529; 2004 (1) UC 92

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Tortfeasors

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 149, 149(2), 170, 170(b), 173 - Whether appeal preferred by appellant before HC was maintainable or whether it was barred by provisions of Motor Vehicles Act? - Held, if there is collusion between person making claim and person resisting claim or if person against whom claim is made has failed to contest claim, insurance company can step in and seek permission of Tribunal and make a prayer for getting itself impleaded as a party to proceeding and insurer so impleaded can then contest proceeding on grounds other than grounds enumerated in s.149(2) - This is an enabling provision in event of a collusion between claimant and insured or tortfeaser - Further held, in a situation contemplated by s 170(b), nothing more is required than recording indisputable fact - For failure to do so, appellant shall not suffer prejudice - Therefore, appellant-insurance company was justified in contesting proceedings on grounds other than those enumerated u/s. 149(2), pursuant to permission granted by Court - For same reason, insurance company can be legitimately considered to be 'person aggrieved' within meaning of s 173 of Act.
