Saikou Jabbi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1084; (2004) 2 SCC 186; (2004) SCC (Cr) 481; 2004 (1) CCR 78; 2004 (1) Crimes 112; 2004 (91) ECC 163; 2004 (112) ECR 129; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 801; JT 2003 (9) SC 609; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 96; 2003(10) SCALE 426; 2003 (7) SLT 609; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 456; 2003 (8) Supreme 582

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - ss. 21, 23, 28, 29 - Customs Act, 1962 - s. 135(1)(a)(ii) - Non-compliance of ss. 42(2), 50 - Heroin seized from foreign national - Accused was acquitted of all the charges by a Special Judge but was convicted by the High Court - Held, the High Court was justified in holding that s. 50 has no application, while s. 42(2) had been complied with - Further held, the plea of non-compliance with the requirements of s. 55 is not acceptable as the evidence of the investigating officer about safe custody of the contraband articles have not been challenged or shaken in the cross-examination.
