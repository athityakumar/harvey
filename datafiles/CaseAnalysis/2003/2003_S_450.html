Bhupinder Sharma v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 859; (2003) 8 SCC 551; AIR 2003 SC 4684; 2003 (4) CCR 340; 2003 (4) Crimes 327; 2004 CRLJ 1; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 761; 2003 (3) JCC 1921; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 493; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 960; 2003(8) SCALE 735; 2003 (1) SCW 5493; 2004 (1) ShimLC 150; 2003 (7) SLT 11; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 792; 2003 (7) Supreme 360; 2004 (1) UC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Women & Children - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114-A - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1983 - IPC, 1860, ss.34, 228-A, 342, 376, 376-A, 376-B, 376-C, 376-D - Gang rape - HC enhanced punishment granted by Trial Court from 4 years RI to 10 years RI - Held, in cases of gang rape proof of completed act of rape by each accused on victim is not required statutory intention in introducing expl (1) in relation to s 376(2)(g) appears to have been done with a view to effectively deal with growing menace of gang rape - Further held, in order to exercise discretion of reducing sentence statutory requirement is that Court has to record 'adequate and special reasons' in judgment and not fanciful reasons which would permit Court to impose a sentence less than prescribed minimum reason has not only to be adequate but special - What is adequate and special would depend upon several factors and no strait-jacket formula can be imposed evidence in this case regard is cogent, credible and trustworthy - Since no other just or special reason was given by Trial Court nor could any such be shown as to what were reasons to warrant a lesser sentence, HC was justified in awarding minimum prescribed sentence.
