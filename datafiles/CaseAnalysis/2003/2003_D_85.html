Punit Rai v Dinesh Chaudhary

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 640; (2003) 8 SCC 204; AIR 2003 SC 4355; AIR 2003 SCW 5149; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 243; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 557; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 577; 2003(6) SCALE 551; 2003 (5) SLT 257; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 743; 2003 (6) Supreme 907

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Election

Summary: [A.] Election - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 106, 114, 114(g) - Representation Of People Act, 1950, s. 103 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 162, 166(3), 341, 341(1), 341(2), 342, 342(1), 342(2) - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950 - Elections contested from a reserved constituency as a Scheduled Caste candidate - No proof that he belonged to SC community - Returning officer had initiated criminal proceedings by filing an FIR relating to caste certificate furnished by respondent, being a fabricated document - Burden of proof - Held, question is after having entertaining such a grave doubt so as to decide to criminally prosecute a person still to accept nomination paper runs contrary to normal conduct of a prudent and responsible officer - On above basis, it can well be said without any hesitation that nomination paper of return candidate (respondent) was improperly accepted by Returning Officer - It ought to have been rejected by returning officer in background of above facts and circumstances.
