Indira Jaising v Registrar General, Supreme Court of India and another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 459; (2003) 5 SCC 494; 2003 (3) AllCJ 1813; 2003 (51) ALR 628; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 36; JT 2005 (11) SC 552; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 185; 2003 (3) KLT 198; 2003(4) SCALE 643; 2003 (3) SLT 598; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 108; 2003 (3) Supreme 769

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 124, 127, 217, 32 - Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968 - Petition u/art. 32 for publication of inquiry report made by a Committee consisting of two Chief Justices and a Judge of different HCs in respect of certain allegations of alleged involvement of sitting Judges of HC of Karnataka in certain incidents and also for a direction to any professional and independent investigating agency having expertise to conduct a through investigation into said incident and to submit a report on same to this Court - Held, only course open to parties concerned if they have material is to invoke provisions of art 124 or art 217 of Constitution, as case may be - It is not appropriate for petitioner to approach SC for relief or direction for release of Report, for what Chief Justice of India has done is only to get information from peer Judges of those who are accused and report made to Chief Justice of India is wholly confidential - Further, only source or authority by which Chief Justice of India can exercise this power of inquiry is moral or ethical and not in exercise of powers under any law - Exercise of such power of Chief Justice of India based on moral authority cannot be made subject matter of a writ to disclose a report made to him - Petition dismissed.
