State of Punjab and Others v Manjit Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 766; (2003) 11 SCC 559; (2004) SCC (L&S) 73; AIR 2003 SC 4580; 2003 (6) AWC 4776; 2003 (99) FLR 426; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 343; 2003 LabIC 3679; 2003(7) SCALE 556; 2003 (1) SCW 5362; 2003 (6) SLR 63; 2003 (5) SLT 593; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 856; 2003 (6) Supreme 732

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 16, 16(4), 320, 320(4), 335 - Selection and appointment - Whether it was competent for Commission to resort to screening test with a view to shortlist number of candidates to bring it to ratio of three to five candidates per vacancy and further, whether keeping in view of efficiency required for services in respect of which selection and appointments was to be made, could a written test be held to fix some minimum cut off marks, where process of selection was by interview of eligible candidates belonging to reserve category- Held, it would be a matter of policy to be decided by State Govt. as to what measures, if necessary, may be provided regarding reservations vis-à-vis maintenance of efficiency in services - Where no special qualification or any prescribed standard of efficiency over and above eligibility criteria is provided by Rules or State, it would not be for Commission to impose any extra qualification/standard supposedly for maintaining minimum efficiency which, it thinks, may be necessary - No consultation with commission, in such matters, is envisaged in view of art.320(4) of Constitution - Further held, Commission derives its powers under art.320 of Constitution as well as its limits too Independent and fair working of Commission is of utmost importance - It is also not supposed to function under any pressure of Govt, as submitted on behalf of appellant Commission - But at same time it has to conform to provisions of law and has also to abide by rules and regulations on subject and to take into account policy decisions which are within domain of State Govt. - It cannot impose its own policy decision in a matter beyond its purview - Therefore, stand of appellant Commission in this case that, it being an independent body, is not subservient to any authority or State Govt, hence it is competent for it to lay down minimum efficiency standards including in matters which may fall within purview of art.335 of Constitution, is erroneous.
