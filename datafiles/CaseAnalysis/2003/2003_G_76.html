High Court of Gujarat and another v Gujarat Kishan Mazdoor Panchayat and others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 261; (2003) SCC (L&S) 565; (2003) 4 SCC 712; AIR 2003 SC 1201; 2003 (97) FLR 422; 2003 (3) G.L.R. 2129; JT 2003 (3) SC 50; 2003 LabIC 1521; 2003 (2) LLN 328; 2003(3) SCALE 66; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 799; 2003 (2) SLR 729; 2003 (3) SLT 103; 2003 (2) Supreme 672

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) Act, Draft Recruitment Rules

Summary: Industrial and Labour Laws - Constitution, art. 14, 16 - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 - ss. 10(2) r/w 10(4) - Legality, correctness and validity of the impugned Notification dated 7/12/2000 appointing one, Shri N A Acharya as the President of the Industrial Court at Ahmedabad? - Held, the appointment of Shri N A Acharya as President of the Industrial Court is not in breach of s.10(2) of the B I R Act and also not violative of art.14 and 16 of the constitution - HC failed to appreciate that once a person is appointed as the President of the Industrial Court, he automatically becomes a Member s.10(2) of the B I R Act only envisages that the President is the senior Member of the Industrial Court and that the appointment of President of the Industrial Court is inherent in his appointment as Member of the Industrial Court Further, even in absence of the Draft Rules in terms of Rule 34 of the Recruitment Rules, for the President of Industrial Court appointment can be made by nomination, subject to the nominees holding requisite qualifications - Appeals allowed.
