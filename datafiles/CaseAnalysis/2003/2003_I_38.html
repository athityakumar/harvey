Municipal Corporation of Delhi and others v Intnl. Security & Intelligence Agency Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 178; (2004) 3 SCC 250; AIR 2003 SC 1515; 2003 (50) ARBLR 432; 2003 (102) DLT 922; JT 2003 (2) SC 103; 2003 (1) RAJ 169; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 757; 2003(1) SCALE 771; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 951; 2003 (1) SLT 797; 2003 (1) Supreme 923

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bengal Money Lenders Act, 1940, Hyderabad Jagirdars Debt Settlement Act, 1952, Provincial Insolvency Act, 1907

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - S. 39 - whether in an appeal under Section 39 of the Arbitration Act a respondent has a right to file cross objection and, if so, whether the cross objection must be heard and decided on merits though the appeal by reference to which cross objection has been filed is itself dismissed as not maintainable? - held, if the appeal cannot be heard on merits for the reason that it was no appeal in the eye of law, service of notice in such appeal would not furnish cause for commencement of a new period of limitation for filing appeal in the form of cross objection. The appeal filed in the High Court by the appellants has been rightly held liable to be dismissed as time-barred and is not available to be heard and decided as cross objection in view of the original appeal filed in the High Court by the Respondents herein having failed as incompetent.
