International Coach Builders Limited v Karnataka State Financial Corporation

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1503; (2003) 10 SCC 482; AIR 2003 SC 2012; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 121; 2003 (3) AWC 1794; 2003 (2) BC 161; 2004 (1) DRTC 35; JT 2003 (2) SC 395; 2003(2) SCALE 677; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 631; 2003 (2) Supreme 460; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1022

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Land & Property

Keywords: Secured Creditors, State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, Appeals disposed off, Without Prejudice, Operation Of Law, State Financial Corporation, State Financial Corporations, Privy Council, Companies Act, 1913, So Far As May Be, Industrial Concern, Special Act, Industrial Concerns, Preferential Payments, Solvent Company, Companies Act, 1985, Enforcing, Mortgages And Charges

Summary: Corporate - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether assets of a company under liquidation can be sold by a liquidator only? - Held, if a secured creditor does not opt to stand outside winding up but relinquishes his security and proves his debt in winding up, then there is no doubt that Official Liquidator will come into custody of all assets of Company in liquidation and distribution of assets would have to proceed in accordance with provisions of s. 529-A of Companies Act, in which case secured creditor stands in line as an unsecured creditor - It is only when SFC as a secured creditor opts to stand outside winding up and seeks to realise its security that conflict, if any, can arise - As a result of amendments made by Act of 1985 in Companies Act, 1956, SFCs as secured creditors, must seek leave of Company Court for limited purpose to ensure that paripass charge in favour of workmen is safeguarded by imposition of suitable conditions under supervision of Company Court - If this amounts to impleding their hitherto unimpeded rights, so be it - Appeals disposed of.
