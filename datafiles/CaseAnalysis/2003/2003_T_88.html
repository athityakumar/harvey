Thirumala Tirupati Devasthanams and Another v Thallappaka Anantha Charyulu and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 743; (2003) 8 SCC 134; AIR 2003 SC 3290; 2003 (6) ALD(SC) 72; JT 2004 (6) SC 425; 2003(7) SCALE 352; 2003 (5) SLT 714; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 538; 2003 (6) Supreme 684

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948, Madras Minor Inams (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1963

Summary: Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Inams (Abolition and Conversion into Roytwari) Act, 1956 - s. 14 - Appellants are statutory Devasthanam in control and management of the temple - Maintainability of suit - Whether the High Court could or should have, in exercise of its writ jurisdiction, issued writs or prohibition against the civil Court from proceeding with the suit before it and against the Revenue Divisional Officer, Tirupati from proceeding with the appeal preferred by the Appellants against the order of the Inams Deputy Tahsilar, Chittoor - Held, we set aside the impugned Judgment to the extent that it prohibits the civil court from proceeding with Suit 69 of 1995. We direct the civil Court to frame and decide, as expeditiously as possible and in any case within six months from today, preliminary issues as to maintainability of the suit in view of s. 14 of the said Act and whether the suit is barred on principles of res judicata/estoppel - Directions issued.
