K.A. Abdul Jaleel v T.A. Shahida

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 338; (2003) 4 SCC 166; (2003) SCC (Cr) 810; AIR 2003 SC 2525; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 114; 2003 (51) ALR 402; 2003 (2) AWC 1665; 2003 (4) Bom.C.R. 498; 2003 (1) DMC 765; 2003 (3) G.L.R. 2444; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2003 (4) SC 4; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 339; 2003 (2) KLT 403; 2003 (1) LCD 644; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 202; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 645; 2003(3) SCALE 785; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 498; 2003 (3) SLT 201; 2003 (3) Supreme 369; 2003 (2) UC 1309

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Family Courts Act, 1984 - Explanation (c) to S. 7 - whether the Family Court has jurisdiction to adjudicate upon any question relating to the properties of divorced parties - Held, the jurisdiction of the Family Court extends, inter alia, in relation to properties of spouses or of either of them and would clearly mean the properties claimed by the parties thereto as a spouse of other; irrespective of the claim whether property is claimed during the subsistence of a marriage or otherwise. - The jurisdiction of a court created specially for resolution of disputes of certain kinds should be construed liberally.
