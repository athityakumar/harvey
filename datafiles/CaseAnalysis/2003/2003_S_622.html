State of Orissa and Others v Mangalam Timber Products Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1070; (2004) 1 SCC 139; AIR 2004 SC 297; 2003 (53) ALR 807; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 104; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 173; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 151; 2004 (136) PLR 651; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2003(9) SCALE 578; 2003 (1) SCW 6130; 2003 (7) SLT 132; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 476; 2003 (8) Supreme 65

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Promissory estoppel - Suppy of raw material - Terms and conditions - Revision of terms from back date - Struck down by HC as violative of the principle of promissory estoppel - Unwritten contract - Held, to attract the applicability of the principle of estoppel, it is not necessary that there must be a contract in writing entered into between the parties - Further held, the State Govt.having persuaded the respondent to establish an industry and respondent having acted on the solemn promise of the State Govt, purchased the raw material at a fixed price and also sold its products by pricing the same taking into consideration the price of raw material fixed by the State Govt.and supplied, the State Govt.cannot be permitted to revise the terms for supply of raw material adversely to the interest of the respondent and effective from a back date and place the respondent in a situation which it will not be able to resolve - The respondent could not have revised their price from a back date and recovered it from innumerable consumers to whom their finished products were applied at a fixed price - Appeal dismissed.
