Ramesh Chand Ardawatiya v Anil Panjwani

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1808; (2003) 7 SCC 350; AIR 2003 SC 2508; 2003 (4) ALD(SC) 10; 2003 (51) ALR 699; 2003 (2) ARC 171; 2003 (3) AWC 2511; JT 2003 (4) SC 450; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 26; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 828; 2003(4) SCALE 652; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1149; 2004 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 704; 2003 (4) Supreme 27

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Co-Operative Societies Act, 1965

Summary: (1) Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Section 54-Limitation Act, 1963 - Article 64 - Suit for possession of immovable property, etc.-Suit based on possessory title-Law stated-Title of plot in question vested in N-N entered into contract for sale in favour of plaintiff-He put plaintiff in possession and proved it-Plaintiff being in possession raising boundary wall-Defendant failing to prove any right to possess suit property - Possession of plaintiff invaded upon by defendant otherwise than by due process of law-Plaintiff entitled to declaration that he is owner of property-Decree of courts below modified accordingly.

(2) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VII, Rule 10-Civil suit-Proceeding ex parte-Duty of trial court to examine evidence-Prima facie proof sufficient-Court not bound to frame issues but to frame points for determination.

(3) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VIII, Rule 6A (as per 1976 Amendment)-Counter claim-Purpose-Must necessarily find its place in written statement-Court has discretion to file written statement belatedly-Defendant not filing any written statement thereby forfeiting his right to file it-Trial court fully justified in not entertaining counter claim filed by defendant.

(4) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 9-Rajasthan Co-operative Societies Act, 1965-Sections 75 and 137-Civil suit-Bar to jurisdiction of civil court-Jurisdiction primarily to be determined on averments in plaint-Plaintiff entering into contract of sale in respect of plot allotted to member of Co-operative Society-Defendant trying to dispossess him-Plaintiff filing suit for possession based on possessory title-Whether suit barred by Section 137 of Rajasthan Co-operative Societies Act?-Held, "no"-Plea of bar of jurisdiction not raised before courts below-It cannot be permitted to be raised before Supreme Court.
