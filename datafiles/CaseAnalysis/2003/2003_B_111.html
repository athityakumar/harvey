Bajaj Auto Limited v Bhojane Gopinath D. and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1401; (2004) 9 SCC 488; 2004 (1) CLR 502; 2004 (100) FLR 615; JT 2003 (10) SC 440; 2004 (1) LLJ 821; 2004 (2) LLN 12; 2003(10) SCALE 774; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Trade

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, ss. 2, 3 and 10 - Model Standing Orders, r. 4-C - (A) Unfair Labour Practice - Failure to implement award, settlement or agreement by employer - Held, there is neither any averment nor evidence to show that there was failure on the part of the employer to implement any settlement or agreement - (B) Interim order of High Court - Whether it is an 'award' u/s. 2(b) of the Industrial Dispute Act? - Held, 'award' means an interim of final determination of any industrial dispute or of any question relating thereto by any Labour Court, Industrial Tribunal or National Industrial Tribunal and includes an arbitration award made u/s. 10(A) - The interim order passed by the HC in the writ application cannot be treated to be an award - Interim or final determination by labour court and tribunals, alone would come within the sweep of definition u/s. 2(b) - It cannot be said that the appellant company was in any manner employing unfair labour practice - (C) Whether r. 4-C of Model Standing Orders be applicable to workmen of appellant-company? - Held, workmen be regularized and made permanent in service from date of filing of complaints before Industrial Court - Amendment allowed was not for suitably modifying rules of model standing orders but for deleting them, which is impermissible - R. 4-C incorporated relating to matter set out in item number 10-C of schedule and such deletion by certifying officer is without jurisdiction - Appeal disposed of.
