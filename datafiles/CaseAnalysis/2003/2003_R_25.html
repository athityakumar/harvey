R.V.F. Venkatachala Gounder v Arulmigu Viswesaraswami and V.P. Temple

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1492; JT 2005 (11) SC 574; 2004 (1) UC 365

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Document, Civil Case, Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 34(2) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100, O. 13, r. 4 - Whether High Court was justified in not proceeding to decide issues arising in suit, rather, exercising second appellate jurisdiction by erroneously assuming fact that property belongs to temple while framing substantial questions of law? - A high degree of preponderance of probability proving title to suit property was raised in favour of appellant and courts below rightly concluded burden of proof raised on plaintiff having been discharged while onus shifting on defendant remaining undischarged - Held, HC was bound by findings of fact arrived at by two courts below and should not have entered into exercise of re-appreciating and evaluating evidence, in exercise of its limited jurisdiction u/s. 100, CPC - Appeal allowed.
