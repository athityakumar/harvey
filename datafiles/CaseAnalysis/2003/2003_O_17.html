Oriental Insurance Company limited v Shakuntala Garg and others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1549; (2007) 12 SCC 773

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Award of compensation - Liability of payment - Determination of quantum - Tribunal casted full liability on appellant - Insurance Company to pay compensation - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, New India Assurance Co. Ltd. v. C.M. Jaya, 2002 Indlaw SC 27, observed that Insurance Company's liability is limited i.e. to tune of Rs 50,000 as indicated in policy. However, appellant has accepted its liability to extent of rs 50,000 and that appears to be liability of appellant as could be seen from certified copy of policy being produced. Original policy was not before Claims Tribunal. Thus, award of compensation is modified to fix liability of appellant only to the extent of rs 50,000. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - In accident case liability of Insurance Company has to be fixed as per amount insured in policy.
