T.K. Rangarajan v Government Of Tamil Nadu & Others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 588; (2003) 6 SCC 581; (2003) SCC (L&S) 970; AIR 2003 SC 3032; 2003 (4) AWC 3028; 2003 (3) CLR 505; 2003 (4) ESC 393; 2003 (98) FLR 1009; 2003 (3) G.L.R. 2628; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 1; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 574; 2003 (3) KLT 86; 2003 LabIC 2646; 2003 (3) LLJ 275; 2003 (3) LLN 883; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 41; 2003(6) SCALE 84; 2003(8) SCALE 423; 2004 (1) SLJ 457; 2003 (6) SLR 599; 2003 (4) SLT 782; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 251; 2003 (5) Supreme 256; 2003 (2) UC 1331

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Tamil Nadu Essential Services Maintenance Act, 2002 - Tamil Nadu Government Servants Conduct Rules, 1973 - rr. 22, 22-A - Administrative Tribunal Act, 1985 -Constitution of India - arts. 226, 227, 323-A, 323-B - Termination of employees on strike - Whether High Court can entertain the such petitions in place of the Administrative Tribunal; (2) whether government employees have a fundamental/statutory/equitable/moral right to strike - Held, if thousands of employees are directed to approach the Administrative Tribunal, the Tribunal would not be in a position to render justice to the cause. Hence, because of very exceptional circumstance there is no justifiable reason for the High Court not to entertain the petitions on the grounds of alternative remedy provided under the statute; (2) there is no fundamental/legal/statutory/moral/equitable right to go on strike - Even if the employees are not prepared to agree with what is contended by some leaders who encourage the strikes, they are forced to go on strikes for reasons beyond their control. Therefore, even though the provisions of the Act and the Rules are to be enforced, they are to be enforced after taking into consideration of the situation and the capacity of the employees to resist - Finally, it is made clear that employees who are re-instated in service would take care in future in maintaining discipline as there is no question of having any fundamental, legal or equitable right to go on strike. The employees have to adopt other alternative methods for redressal of their grievances.
