State of Haryana and Others v Sumitra Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1077; (2004) 12 SCC 322; AIR 2004 SC 1050; 2004 (1) CLR 724; 2004 (100) FLR 123; 2003(9) SCALE 779; 2003 (1) SCW 6726; 2004 (1) SLJ 155; 2004 (1) SLR 401; 2003 (7) SLT 511; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 351; 2003 (8) Supreme 511

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Service

Summary: Service - Higher scale of pay for those who possessed qualification of B.A. or B.Ed. or both during their tenure of service - Appellants acquired degree or diploma much prior to their appointment - Held, the circular clearly states that a higher scale of pay would not be admissible to them despite holding a higher qualification having appointed on a lower post such a higher scales of pay was admissible only to such teacher/masters who had enhanced their educational qualification during the course of service. The petitioners, therefore, were not entitled to higher scale of pay.
