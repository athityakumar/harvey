Rajendra Shantaram Todankar v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 4; (2003) 2 SCC 257; (2003) SCC (Cr) 506; AIR 2003 SC 1110; 2003 (1) CCR 144; 2003 (1) Crimes 346; 2003 (5) CRJ 345; 2003 CRLJ 1277; 2003 (1) JCC 147; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 94; JT 2003 (2) SC 95; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 179; 2003(1) SCALE 7; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 10; 2003 (1) SLT 373; 2003 (1) Supreme 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - Ss. 143, 144, 147, 302, 149, 34, 307, 324 and 511 - Whether all the accused persons, as alleged, were involved in the incident, and if so, to what extent? - Held, a mere possibility of the commission of the offence would not necessarily enable the Court to draw an inference that the likelihood of commission of such offence was within the knowledge of every member of the unlawful assembly. - Unless the applicability of Section 149-either clause - is attracted and the Court is convinced, on facts and in law both, of liability capable of being fastened vicariously by reference to either clause of Section 149 of IPC merely because a criminal act was committed by a member of the assembly every other member thereof would not necessarily become liable for such criminal act.
