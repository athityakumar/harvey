Sharda v Dharmpal

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1807; (2003) 4 SCC 493; AIR 2003 SC 3450; AIR 2003 SCW 1950; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2003 (51) ALR 289; 2003 (2) AWC 1534; 2003 (6) Bom.C.R. 396; 2003 (1) DMC 627; 2003 (2) JCC 692; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 98; JT 2003 (3) SC 399; 2003 (2) KLT 243; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 795; 2003(3) SCALE 475B; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 106; 2003 (36) SLT 1; 2003 (2) Supreme 962

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Human Rights Act, 1998, Lunacy, Indian Lunacy Act, Family Law Reform Act, 1987

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 12(1)(b) and 13(1)(iii) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 32, r. 15 - Inherent power of court - Medical Examination - Whether matrimonial court has jurisdiction to direct respondent to undergo medical examination for unsoundness of mind and whether such direction violates personal liberty u/art. 21 of Constitution.

Held, a matrimonial court has the power to order a person to undergo medical test. Passing of such an order by the court would not be in violation of the right to personal liberty u/art. 21 of the Constitution. However, the Court should exercise such a power if the applicant has a strong prima facie case and there is sufficient material before the Court. If despite the order of the court, the respondent refuses to submit himself to medical examination, the court will be entitled to draw an adverse inference against him. Appeal dismissed.
