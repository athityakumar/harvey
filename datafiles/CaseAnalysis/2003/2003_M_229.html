Secunderabad Cantonment Board v Mohammed Mohiuddin and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1420; (2003) 12 SCC 315; AIR 2004 SC 784; 2004 (54) ALR 436; JT 2003 (9) SC 316; 2003(10) SCALE 331; 2003 (1) SCW 6973; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 395; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 882

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constructive

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Cantonment Act, 1924, s. 181(4)(b) - Respondents moved application to Executive Officer Cantonment Board for sanction of lay out in respect of part of land - Application for section of plan was returned to respondents with an objection that they were required to furnish exemption certificate under provisions of Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - (A) What is scope and ambit of s. 181(4)(b) and whether request for sanction of plan was rightfully refused? - Held, it is evident from perusal of s. 181(4)(b) that competent authority dealing with matter, has to see whether there is or not any dispute about land between person applying for sanction of plan and Govt. - In case concerned authority is satisfied about existence of such a dispute in terms of s. 181 of Act, request for sanction of lay out plan is liable to be refused - In circumstances of this case, it would be difficult to say that there would be no bona fide dispute about land between parties - Court find there is enough material, on basis of which an authority could reasonably come to conclusion that there was a dispute relating to land, between applicant and Govt. in respect of which sanction of plan to construct, was applied for - (B) Whether appellants were estopped from raising an objection relating to existence of dispute over land, on basis that no such plea was put forward when map was first returned? - Further held, stage for raising an objection regarding a dispute between Govt. and applicant arises after application is referred to DEO by sanctioning authority in terms of s. 181(3) - Nothing to contrary has been indicated by respondents to show that despite reference of application to DEO u/s. 181(3), Defence Estates Officer had chosen not to file any objection in respect of dispute or claim over land - On basis of above factual aspect, question of failing to raise a plea in earlier proceedings does not arise due to return of first application - Appeals allowed.
