State of Uttar Pradesh v Babu and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 785; (2003) 11 SCC 280; (2004) SCC (Cr) 144; AIR 2003 SC 3408; 2003 (4) Crimes 203; 2004 (1) CRJ 290; 2003 CRLJ 4982; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 379; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 239; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 673; 2003(8) SCALE 48; 2003 (1) SCW 4841; 2003 (6) SLT 271; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1079; 2003 (7) Supreme 53

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal code, 1860 - ss. 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 395 - High Court reversed the judgment of the trial court and acquitted the accused - Held, a bare perusal of the High Court's judgment goes to show that its approach was rather casual and no effort was made to analyse the evidence. It is to be noted that the High Court did not examine the evidence of prosecution witnesses with the required care. Great emphasis was laid by the High Court on the fact that in the site plan place where gaslight was found had not been indicated. The site plan is not substantive evidence. The High Court seems to have proceeded on the basis that omission to indicate the location gaslight in the site plan was fatal - Further held, the conclusion arrived at by the High Court is without reason. Since the High Court has acted on surmises and conjectures, the judgment is indefensible.
