Ahmedabad Education Society v Gilber B. Shah and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1269; (2004) SCC (L&S) 253; (2004) 1 SCC 612; AIR 2004 SC 1167; 2004 (1) CLR 915; 2004 (100) FLR 219; JT 2003 (10) SC 144; 2004 (1) LLN 487; 2003(10) SCALE 516; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 500; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 529

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, s. 14(1)(b) - Bombay Primary Education (Gujarat Amendment) Rules, 1978, Sch. F, r. 34(1) - Respondents are teachers with Appellant Society claiming that they could continue in service till age of 60 as per r. 29 of Leave Rules of Appellant Society contrary to retirement age of 58 years - What is nature of original appointment of Respondents-teachers? - Held, if they were appointed on contractual terms of a definite period, then they could continue in service in accordance with terms of original contract, which, by virtue of r. 29 of Leave Rules, allow them to continue in service till age of 60 - Leave Rules are applicable terms of service in case of Respondents-teachers, and as per original terms and conditions of contract they were appointed up to a particular age, that is, up to age of 60 - Appeal dismissed.
