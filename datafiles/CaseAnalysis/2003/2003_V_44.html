Surendra Kumar Sharma v Vikas Adhikari and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 448; (2003) 5 SCC 12; (2003) SCC (L&S) 600; 2003 (2) CGLJ 113; 2003 (3) ESC 326; 2003 (97) FLR 1169; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 32; JT 2003 (4) SC 564; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 350; 2003 (2) LLJ 1094; 2003(5) SCALE 4; 2003 (3) SLJ 147; 2003 (3) SLR 601; 2003 (4) SLT 309; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 168; 2003 (4) Supreme 61

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service Law - Jawaharlal Nehru Rozgar Yojna or Jawahar Rozgar Yojna - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - ss.2(OO) and 25F - Writ filed for regularisation of services - Appellant employed on daily wages under various schemes - Held, workmen were aware of the fact that the employment was of a temporary nature co-terminus with the scheme itself. Therefore, they could not be said to have been retrenched within the meaning of the Act, so as to be entitled to the relief of reinstatement if the provisions of s.25F of the Act were not complied with - Further held, in case of temporary employment, on completion or abandonment of the project, the employer cannot by a writ of mandamus be directed to continue employing such employees as such a direction would amount to requisition for creation of posts.
