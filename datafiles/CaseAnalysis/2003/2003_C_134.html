Pepsi Foods Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1208; (2005) 9 SCC 28; 111 ECR 776; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 552; JT 2003 (9) SC 595; 2004 (28) PTC 186; 2003 (59) RLT 598; 2003(10) SCALE 196; 2004 (134) STC 344; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Beverage

Summary: Excise - Assessable value - Amounts includible - Whether the royalty amount collected by it from the bottlers for use of the trademark 'lehar' on the soft drink beverages manufactured out of the 'concentrate' sold by the appellant is includible in the assessable value of the concentrates? - Realisation of royalty as important as the realisation of sale price of concentrate from assessee's point of view Bottlers obliged to purchase concentrate from assessee alone, use the trade mark of assessee on bottled beverage in addition to trademark of Pepsico and comply with instructions of assessee in regard to manufacture, sale and distribution of beverages An inextricable bond between the obligation of bottler to purchase concentrate exclusively from assessee and user of trademark of assessee subject to payment of royalty Substratum of agreement regulating terms of dealings between the parties indicate that all of them are integral operations and invoiced price alone was not the sole consideration for sale of concentrate - Held, such royalty amount includible in assessable value Tribunal's order upheld - Appeals dismissed - Appeals Dismissed.
