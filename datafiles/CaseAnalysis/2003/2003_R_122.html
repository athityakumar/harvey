U.P. Drugs & Pharmaceuticals Company Limited v Ramanuj Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 787; (2003) 8 SCC 334; AIR 2003 SC 3337; 2003 (4) AWC 3328; 2004 (4) ESC 635; 2003 (99) FLR 331; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 383; 2003 LabIC 3491; 2003 (3) LLJ 1064; 2003(8) SCALE 73; 2003 (1) SCW 4739; 2004 (1) SLJ 500; 2003 (6) SLR 453; 2003 (6) SLT 285; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1022; 2003 (6) Supreme 857; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2670

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Merit

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - ss. 2(g), 4(k), 6N - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - ss. 2(eee), 25-B - Termination of service - Back wages - None of the workmen had worked for 240 days in the year preceding the date of termination, but in the years prior to the present one they had worked for 240 days - High Court reversed the judgment of the Labour Court holding that under s. 6N r/w s. 2(g), U.P. Act, it is not necessary for the workmen to complete 240 days in the preceding year, and since workmen had completed 240 days in earlier calendar years preceding to 12 months on the date of retrenchment, they were deemed to be in a continuous service and hence their termination in violation of s. 6N of the U.P. Act was illegal - Held, the High Court has rightly concluded that the termination of the respondents was in violation of s. 6N r/w s. 2(g), UP Act - Further held, no interference regarding denial of back wages.
