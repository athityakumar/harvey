Sohan Lal @ Sohan Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 843; (2003) 11 SCC 534; (2004) SCC (Cr) 226; AIR 2003 SC 4466; 2003 (4) Crimes 342; 2003 CRLJ 4569; 2003 (2) DMC 645; 2003 (3) JCC 1869; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 556; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 471; 2003(8) SCALE 568; 2003 (1) SCW 5235; 2003 (6) SLT 476; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 676; 2003 (7) Supreme 386

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 109, 302, 304B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 211(4), 464 - Appeal - Dowry death - Absence of charge framed under ss. 109, 302 - Dying declarations - Held, upon careful consideration of the facts and circumstances of the case, we can safely accept the veracity of the dying declaration made by the deceased, which is also fully corroborated by the other circumstances and not contradicted by her statement recorded under s. 161, CrPC, 1973. No material has been placed to show that the dying declarations were the result of any tutoring or coaching - Further held, once it is held that the dying declaration is credit worthy, there is no doubt that the accusations against the appellants accused Harbans Kaur and accused Kanchan are fully proved.
