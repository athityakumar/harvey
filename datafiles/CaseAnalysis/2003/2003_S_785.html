Salim Akhtar @ Mota v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 334; (2003) 5 SCC 499; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1149; AIR 2003 SC 4076; 2003 ALJ 1414; 2003 (2) CCR 42; 2003 (2) Crimes 289; 2003 (5) CRJ 418; 2003 CRLJ 2302; 2003 (2) JCC 678; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 94; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 67; 2003(3) SCALE 698; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 470; 2003 (3) SLT 130; 2003 (3) Supreme 305

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act - S. 5 - conviction under - appeal - pistol alleged to have been recovered at the pointing out of the appellant - held, mere knowledge of the accused that incriminating articles were kept at certain places does not amount to conscious possession and conviction under Section 5 of TADA - not safe to uphold the conviction of the appellant as the evidence adduced by the prosecution fails to establish the charge against the appellant beyond reasonable doubt.
