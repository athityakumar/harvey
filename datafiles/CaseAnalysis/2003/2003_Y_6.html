Air India Cabin Crew Association with Air India v Yeshawinee Merchant and Others and Air India

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 506; (2003) 6 SCC 277; (2003) SCC (L&S) 840; AIR 2004 SC 187; 2003 (6) Bom.C.R. 568; 2003 (4) ESC 486; 2003 (98) FLR 1044; JT 2003 (5) SC 413; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 479; 2003 LabIC 3727; 2003 (3) LLJ 1; 2003(5) SCALE 208; 2003 (1) SCW 5891; 2003 (4) SLR 683; 2003 (4) SLT 353; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 455; 2003 (5) Supreme 8; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1852

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, Parity, Air Corporation Act, 1994

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 15(1), 16, 16(2), 226, 51-A(e), 77 - Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, ss.15(a), 15(b)(ii), 16, 2(h), 4, 5 - Air Corporations Act, 1953, ss.34, 34(1), 45(2)(b) - Air Corporation Act, 1994 - Industrial Employment Standard Standing Order Act - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - 10 - Whether air hostesses are also entitled to retirement age of 58 years on flying duties at par with flight pursers and other members of cabin crew - Held, early retirement age of 50 years from flying duties for female members of crew with an option to them to accept ground duties beyond 50 years up to age of 58 years being a service condition agreed to and incorporated in a binding agreement or settlement and award reached with employer, same cannot be held to be either arbitrary or discriminatory under art.15 and 16 - It is not a discrimination against females only on ground of sex - Further held, decision in Nergesh Meerza's case was binding on HC - HC was clearly wrong in holding that it had become inapplicable by passage of time - (2) Equal Remuneration Act, 1976 - Further held, term and condition of age of retirement settled in course of industrial adjudication by air hostesses through their associations is a term and condition of their employment fixed in accordance with adjudicatory machinery provided in industrial laws - Section 15, cl (b)(ii), E R Act, 1976, allows special treatment to women in terms and conditions of service relating to retirement - Therefore, early age retirement policy of air hostesses in Air India does not contravene s 5, E R Act, 1976 (3) - Directives issued by Central Govt, therefore, also cannot be held to be in any manner in violation of art.14, 15 and 16 of the Constitution or provisions of Equal Remuneration Act, 1976.
