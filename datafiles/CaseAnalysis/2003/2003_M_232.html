Roop Kumar v Mohan Thedani

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 319; (2003) 6 SCC 595; AIR 2003 SC 2418; AIR 2003 SCW 2425; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 329; 2003 (1) RCJ 157; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 614; 2003(3) SCALE 611; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 292; 2003 (3) SLT 80; 2003 (3) Supreme 296

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - s. 16 - Shops and Commercial Establishments Act - Evidence Act, ss. 91, 92 - Scope, ambit of, Rent Control Act, s.16(2) - Whether relationship between the respondent and the appellant was that of licensor and licensee or it was that of lessor and lessee? - Held, it is a general and most inflexible rule that whenever written instruments are appointed, either by the requirement of law, or by the contract of the parties, to be repositories and memorials of truth, any other evidence is excluded from being used either as a substitute for such instruments, or to contradict or alter them - Written contracts presume deliberation on the part of the contracting parties and it is natural they should be treated with careful consideration by the Courts and with a disinclination to disturb the conditions of matters as embodied in them by the act of the parties - HC has taken note of several instances to conclude that the agreement was one of licence and not of lease - That being the position, the conclusions of the HC are in order and do not warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
