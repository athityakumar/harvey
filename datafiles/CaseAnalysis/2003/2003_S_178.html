State of Uttaranchal and Others v Sidharth Srivastava and Others State of Uttaranchal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1428; (2003) 9 SCC 336; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1092; AIR 2003 SC 4062; 2003 (5) AWC 3580; 2003 (4) ESC 449; JT 2003 (5) SC 393; 2003(5) SCALE 73; 2003 (1) SCW 3019; 2003 (4) SLR 661; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 278; 2003 (4) Supreme 379; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2002

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Anganbari Sevika, MISCELLANEOUS, Uttar Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1961

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000, s. 78(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 323(2) - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994 - Whether selection made by Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission, prior to formation of State of Uttaranchal, is binding on State of Uttaranchal so as to appoint selected candidates to services in State of Uttaranchal having due regard to art. 323(2) of Constitution and s. 78(4) of Uttar Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000? - Held, that a candidate whose name is included in select list does not get indefeasible right for appointment on basis of recommendations made by Public Service Commission, but at same time Govt. has no absolute right to reject recommendations and refuse appointment to candidates recommended without a valid and reasonable ground for not accepting recommendations of UPPSC - Recommendations of UPPSC are not binding to compel State of Uttaranchal to give appointments to candidates recommended by UPPSC - Appeal allowed.
