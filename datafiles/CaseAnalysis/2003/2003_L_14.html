State of Bihar v Lal Krishna Advani and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 750; (2003) 8 SCC 361; AIR 2003 SC 3357; 2003 (6) AWC 4771; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 335; 2003(7) SCALE 524; 2003 (1) SCW 4769; 2003 (5) SLT 681; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 844; 2003 (6) Supreme 762

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - s. 8B - Effect of non-compliance of the principle of natural justice - Respondent objected to certain portions of the report which according to him were objectionable and affected his reputation - Right of an individual to have the safeguard of principles of natural justice - Held, it is thus incumbent upon the Commission to give an opportunity to a person, before any comment is made or opinion is expressed which is likely to prejudicially affect that person. Needless to emphasise that failure to comply with principles of natural justice renders the action non-est as well as the consequences thereof.
