Ramgopal and another v Balaji Mandir Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 155; (2003) 5 SCC 17; AIR 2003 SC 1883; 2003 (51) ALR 246; 2003 (2) CGLJ 188; JT 2003 (2) SC 498; 2003 (1) RCJ 115; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 356; 2003(2) SCALE 340; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 212; 2003 (2) SLT 314; 2003 (2) Supreme 488

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - S. 3(2) - Madhya Pradesh Public Trust Act - whether plaintiff entitled for benefit of exemption notification u/s. 3(2) by proving that it is a religious/charitable trust? - The High Court relying on the decision of the Supreme Court in Betibai and others vs. Nathooram and others (1999) 6 SCC 368) held that the plaintiffs were entitled for a decree as passed by the trial court - held, having not raised by objection as to the maintainability of the suit on the ground that the Exemption Notification did not cover the case and that too in the absence of either admitted or established facts supporting that ground, there is no good or valid reason to differ from the view taken by the High Court in the impugned judgment.
