Modi Entertainment Network and another v W.S.G. Cricket PTE. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 58; (2003) 4 SCC 341; AIR 2003 SC 1177; 2003 (50) ARBLR 533; 2003 (2) AWC 1261; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 754; JT 2003 (1) SC 382; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 98; 2003(1) SCALE 388; 2003 (1) SLT 478; 2003 (1) Supreme 709

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Court, Foreign Judgment, Foreign Jurisdictions

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Anti-suit injunction - Whether DB of HC erred in vacating anti-suit injunction granted by a learned Single Judge restraining respondent from proceeding with action between same parties pending in English Court, forum of their choice - Examination of principles governing grant of an anti-suit injunction by a court of natural jurisdiction against a party to a suit before it restraining him from instituting and/ or prosecuting suit, between same parties, if instituted, in a foreign court of choice of parties - Held, even when appellants had filed suit earlier in point of time in Court of natural forum and respondent brought action in English court which is agreed forum or forum of choice having regard to expressed intention of parties, no good and sufficient reason is made out to grant anti-suit injunction to restrain respondent from prosecuting English action as such an order would clearly be in breach of agreement and court will not, except when proceedings in foreign court of choice result in perpetuating injustice aid a party to commit breach of agreement.
