Kanpur Development Authority v Smt. Sheela Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1254; (2003) 12 SCC 497; AIR 2004 SC 400; 2004 ALJ 22; 2004 (1) AWC 739; 2003 CCC 638; 2004 (1) CPJ 12; JT 2003 (10) SC 18; 2003(10) SCALE 363; 2003 (1) SCW 6791; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 374; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 662

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 195, 340 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 193, 196, 199, 200, 463, 464, 465, 467, 468, 471 - False Evidence - Price Escalation - Allotment - Validity - Three schemes were floated by DA - Applications were invited and Brochure was issued - Respondents were not allotted houses and applications were kept pending for more than 18 years - Houses were to be allotted among valid applicants by lottery further to which applicants had to deposit balance - DA included names of some more applicants after last date - Applicants filed suits in 1981/1982 - Court found fault with DA and decreed suit - Appeals filed by DA against decree passed by Trial court were dismissed - DA increased cost of each house and stated that in case of default name of applicant would not be included in list of lottery - Aggrieved respondents filed petitions - Petitions were admitted and interim orders were issued in spite of which DA again stated that date of lottery had been extended - Lottery was drawn among 108 valid applications - Information of allotment was issued with demand for Rs. 24,000/- towards first 6th monthly installment - HC in petitions stayed demand - DA filed counter affidavit - DB allowed petitions quashing order of DA and directed to deliver possession of houses to respondents on cost fixed in brochure - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, in instant case estimated cost for LIG flat was given out at Rs. 45,000 but by impugned communication appellant informed respondents that actual cost of flat should be Rs. 1,16,000 - Escalation was more than 100% - HC was justified in saying that in such circumstances, DA owed duty to explain and to satisfy Court reasons for such high escalation - Tentative cost of houses was fixed at Rs.48,000/- but final cost was determined at Rs.2,08,000/- - This increase was not mere escalation but it was multiplication by almost four and half times, although escalation could not exceed 10% as was evident from contents of Brochure - DA ran contrary to contents of its own Brochure on which respondents acted adjusting their financial affairs understanding that cost of houses would be fixed in terms of brochure and that too not exceeding 10% of estimated cost fixed initially - Hence, conclusions arrived by HC were justified - Appeals disposed of.
