State of Maharashtra v Kashirao and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 668; (2003) 10 SCC 434; AIR 2003 SC 3901; 2003 (4) Crimes 53; 2004 (1) CRJ 652; 2003 CRLJ 4464; 2003 (3) JCC 1560; JT 2003 (7) SC 437; 2003(6) SCALE 715; 2003 (1) SCW 4492; 2003 (5) SLT 235; 2003 (6) Supreme 293; 2004 (1) UC 153

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss.147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 452 - Applicability of s.149 - Held, when facts are applied, it becomes clear that all accused person are liable in terms of s.149 IPC Looking at nature of injuries, weapons used and manner of assaults, there was no reason to apply s 326 IPC in case of accused-respondent No 1 alone trial Court had rightly convicted accused persons u/s. 302 IPC gruesome nature of attack is amply demonstrated by injuries noticed on body of deceased - Further held, since sentence and fine have been maintained, alteration of conviction notwithstanding no modification of sentence need be made It is true that when two views are possible and if one view has been adopted by Court to either acquit accused or to apply a different provision of law, interference should not be made but when judgment suffers from legal infirmities and application of legal position to factual scenario is unsustainable, interference is not only necessary but also highly desirable.
