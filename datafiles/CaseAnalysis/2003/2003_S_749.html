Messrs Bharat Heavy Electrical Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 523; (2003) 6 SCC 528; AIR 2003 SC 3024; AIR 2003 SCW 3469; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2204; 2003 (4) AWC 2685; 2003 (3) CLR 188; 2004 (4) ESC 593; 2003 (98) FLR 826; JT 2003 (6) SC 14; 2003 LabIC 2630; 2003 (3) LLJ 215; 2003(5) SCALE 321; 2004 (1) SLJ 431; 2003 (4) SLR 679; 2003 (4) SLT 549; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 625; 2003 (5) Supreme 45; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1830; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2596

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - ss. 2(i)(iv), 6-N, 14-A - Contractor Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Services of gardener terminated - Test of control in relation to the work of the respondents-workmen having regard to the definition of 'employer' contained in s. 2(i)(iv) - Held, the definition of 'employer' given in s. 2(i)(iv) is an inclusive definition. If the respondents-workmen as a matter of fact were employed with the appellant to work in their premises, and which fact is found established after removing the mask or facade of make-believe employment under the contractor, the appellant cannot escape its liability - Further held, the other evidence and facts and circumstances of the case were also kept in mind in recording such a finding including a vital fact that the appellant did not produce the records alleging that they were not available which led to drawing adverse inference against them.
