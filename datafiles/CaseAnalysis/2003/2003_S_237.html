Citi Bank N.A. with Canara Bank and Others v Standard Chartered Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 831; (2004) 1 SCC 12; AIR 2003 SC 4630; 2004 (1) BC 211; 2004 (2) Bom.C.R. 540; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 554; JT 2003 (10) SC 597; 2003(8) SCALE 364; 2003 (1) SCW 5434; 2003 (7) SLT 68; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 489; 2003 (7) Supreme 618

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Banking & Finance - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114, 114(g), 114(i) - Special Courts (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss.41, 62, 63 - Large scale irregularities and malpractices committed in transactions in both Govt. and other securities, by some brokers in collusion with employees of various banks and financial institutions - Held, Citi Bank becomes entitled to restitution of total amount paid by it to Standard Chartered Bank (principal and interest) along with interest @ 12% p a from date of receipt of payment by SCB provided it is paid on or before 30 th November, 2003 and in default to pay interest @ 15% p a from date of receipt of payment till it is repaid by Standard Chartered Bank Citi Bank would also be entitled to receive back amount of costs it had paid to Standard Chartered Bank under decree of Special Court but same would not carry any interest - Further held, CMF becomes entitled to restitution of total amount paid by it to Citi Bank (principal and interest) along with interest @ 12% p a from date of payment provided it is paid on or before 5 th December, 2003 and in default to pay interest @ 15% p a from date of payment till it is repaid by Citi Bank - Costs granted.
