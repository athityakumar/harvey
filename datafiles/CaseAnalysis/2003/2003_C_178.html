VIP Industries Ltd. v Commissioner Of Customs & Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1104; (2003) 5 SCC 507; 2003 (88) ECC 232; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 8; 2003 (57) RLT 6; 2003 (4) Supreme 550

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Consideration, Uniform, Consignment Agent, Excisability of goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 2(2), 4 and 11-A - Whether in cases where a manufacturer includes equalised freight in price of goods and sells goods all over country at a uniform price, Department is entitled to compute value by including cost of transportation from factory to depot? - U/s. 4(ia) where price of goods is different for different places of removal, each such price was deemed to be normal price of such goods in relation to 'such place of removal' - Held, in cases where price remains uniform or constant all over country, it does not follow that value for purpose of excise changes merely because definition of term 'place of removal' is extended - Appeals allowed.
