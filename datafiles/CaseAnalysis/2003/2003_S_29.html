Chairman and M.D., B.P.L. Limited v S.P. Gururaja and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 860; (2003) 8 SCC 567; AIR 2003 SC 4536; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 248; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 515; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 14; 2003(8) SCALE 536; 2003 (1) SCW 5298; 2003 (6) SLT 178; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 587; 2003 (8) Supreme 129; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3589

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 162, 166, 21, 226, 25, 26 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, ss. 17, 28(1), 28(4) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 1, r. 8 - PIL - Economic development of a state halted by a PIL - Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board acquired vast tract of land for purpose of allotment to entrepreneurs who intended to set up industries in State of Karnataka - Petitioners granted land for setting up of industries - PIL filed questioning allotment of land - Held, Board and State had not committed any illegality which could have been a subject matter of judicial review - HC committed a manifest error insofar as it failed to take into consideration that delay in this case had defeated equity allotment was made in year 1995 writ application was filed after one year - By that time company had not only took possession of land but also made sufficient investment - Delay of this nature shall have been considered by HC to be of vital importance - Furthermore, HC ought to have taken into consideration factum of resistance in matter from those persons whose lands have been acquired - Only because lands are vested in State upon acquisition thereof, same by itself would not mean that persons whose lands were acquired were not interested in getting allotment locus standi of respondent ought to have been taken into consideration having regard to specific pleas raised in this behalf by appellants herein.
