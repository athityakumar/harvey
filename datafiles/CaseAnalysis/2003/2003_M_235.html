Mohd. Islam v State (NCT) of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1552; (2007) 15 SCC 718

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Reliance on dying declaration - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was charged for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and was tried by Additional Sessions Judge - Additional Sessions Judge found appellant guilty of offence charged and convicted him - Appeal was made by appellant before HC was also dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether conviction was based upon dying declaration - Held, statement made by victim was scribed by Investigation Officer(IO) did not contain any certification by doctor that victim was in fit state of mind and health to make declaration of that nature - Though it was true that Court held that such certification was not always necessary, when statement of that nature was being recorded in hospital if prosecution fails to get such certification document should be very cautiously considered - It would be extremely difficult for Court to accept condition of victim merely on endorsement found in MLC - Even though IO had sufficient opportunity to take assistance of Magistrate to record dying declaration, admittedly no efforts were made to do so and IO had proceeded to do so even without recording his own satisfaction as to condition of victim - Perusal of dying declaration showed from its language and graphic details given therein that it could not be statement of 15-year-old illiterate boy who should be in severe agony and under sedation - Further, spacing of writing in document indicated that there was possibility that thumb mark of victim being taken in blank paper and contents of document inserted thereafter could not be overruled - Thus, prosecution had failed to give satisfactory explanation for non- examination of doctors and it was not safe to rely upon dying declaration to base conviction - Therefore, Courts below erred in placing reliance on dying declaration and to base conviction thereon - Hence, judgments of two Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
