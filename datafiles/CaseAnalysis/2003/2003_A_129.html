State of U.P. v Arun Kumar Gupta

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 19; (2003) 2 SCC 202; (2003) SCC (Cr) 481; AIR 2003 SC 801; 2003 (1) CCR 156; 2003 (1) Crimes 339; 2003 (4) CRJ 396; 2003 CRLJ 894; JT 2003 (1) SC 49; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2003(1) SCALE 76; 2003 (1) SLT 402; 2003 (1) Supreme 175; 2003 (2) UC 737

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - Ss. 302 and 364 - death sentence passed by the trial court and reference made to the High Court for confirmation - the High Court allowed the appeal filed by the respondent while rejected the reference made by the Sessions Judge - appeal - Held, the prosecution has been able to establish only a motive as against the respondent for committing the murder, while most of the circumstances which are otherwise important to be proved in this case have not been established. - The High Court was justified in interfering with the judgment of the Sessions Judge and allowing the appeal.
