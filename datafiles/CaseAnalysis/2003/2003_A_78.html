State of Uttar Pradesh v Allied Constructions

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 596; (2003) 7 SCC 396; AIR 2004 SC 586; 2003 (52) ARBLR 106; 2003 (5) AWC 3457; JT 2003 (7) SC 273; 2003 (3) RAJ 106; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 805; 2003(6) SCALE 265; 2003 (1) SCW 6075; 2003 (4) SLT 873; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 55; 2003 (5) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30, 33 - Claim filed by contractor on account of loss suffered due to flooding of construction site - Award of arbitrator, granting payment, challenged on ground that he has misconducted proceedings, inasmuch as force majeure contained in arbitration agreement disentitled respondent from making any claim which was on account of unprecedented rain - Held, any award made by an arbitrator can be set aside only if one or other term specified in ss.30, 33, Arbitration Act is attracted - It is not a case where it can be said that arbitrator has misconducted proceedings - Further held, s 30 providing for setting aside an award is restrictive in its operation - Unless one or other condition contained in s 30 is satisfied, an award cannot be set aside arbitrator is a Judge chosen by parties and his decision is final Court is precluded from reappraising evidence - Even in a case where award contains reasons, interference therewith would still be not available within jurisdiction of Court unless, of course, reasons are totally perverse or judgment is based on a wrong proposition of law - An error apparent on face of records would not imply closer scrutiny of merits of documents and materials on record - Once it is found that view of arbitrator is a plausible one, Court will refrain itself from interfering.
