James Martin v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1161; (2004) 2 SCC 203; (2004) SCC (Cr) 487; JT 2003 (10) SC 371; 2004 (1) KLT 513; 2004 (2) MahLJ 358; 2004 (2) MPLJ 231; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 306; 2003(10) SCALE 742; 2004 (1) ShimLC 260; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 910

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 96, 99 - Private defence - Bharat bandh - Demand to close mill - Accused fired to stop the activists from entering the compound of the mill - Held, the inevitable conclusion is that the acts done by the accused were in the reasonable limits of exercise of his right of private defence and he was entitled to the protection afforded in law under s. 96, IPC, 1860.
