State of Punjab v Hardan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 770; (2003) 12 SCC 679; AIR 2003 SC 4343; 2003 (4) CCR 170; 2003 (4) Crimes 300; 2004 (1) CRJ 308; 2003 CRLJ 5048; 2003 (3) JCC 1932; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 129; 2003(7) SCALE 675; 2003 (1) SCW 5131; 2003 (6) SLT 123; 2003 (7) Supreme 286

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 109, 302 - Conviction and imprisonment for life - Acquitted by High Court - Testimony of solitary eye witness in absence of corroboration - Unexplained delay in lodging the FIR - Occular evidence not corroborated by medical evidence - Held, the testimony of the witness is also corroborated by material evidence. Even otherwise, after going through the testimony of the witness, it appears that he is quite a natural witness and his testimony is creditworthy and convincing, on which alone the conviction could safely be based - Further held, there is no discrepancy in the ocular evidence of and the medical evidence - Order of the trial court restored.
