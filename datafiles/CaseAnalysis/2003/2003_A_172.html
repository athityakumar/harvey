Union of India and Another v Azadi Bachao Andolan and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 823; (2004) 10 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 1107; 2003 (184) CTR 450; [2003] 263 ITR 706; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 205; 2003(8) SCALE 287; 2003 (1) SCW 5766; 2003 (6) SLT 373; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 222; 2003 (7) Supreme 406; [2003] 132 TAXMAN 373

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; International

Keywords: Finance Act, 1972, Mauritian Income Tax Act, 1995, Mauritius Offshore Business Activities Act, 1992, Economic Crime and Anti Money Laundering Act, 2000, Companies Act, 1984, Stock Exchange Act, 1988, Mauritius and Financial Services Development Act, Income & Corporation Taxes Act, 1970, Income Tax Act (Mauritius), 1995

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 265, 372., 73 - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964, s. 24A - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(1)(b), 10(1)(c), 115A, 116(a), 119, 119(1), 119(2), 245-O, 245R, 245S, 4, 5, 6, 6(b), 73(b), 90, 90(2) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 49A - Finance Act, 1972, s. 90 - Finance Act, 1991, s. 90(2) - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 5A - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(5) - Mauritian Income Tax Act, 1995, ss. 4, 44, 51 - Mauritius Offshore Business Activities Act, 1992, ss. 15, 16, 26, 27, 33 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Economic Crime and Anti Money Laundering Act, 2000 - Companies Act, 1984 - Stock Exchange Act, 1988 - Mauritius and Financial Services Development Act - Income & Corporation Taxes Act, 1970, s. 460 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 21(2) - Double taxation - Whether circular No 789 dated 13/4/2000 issued by CBDT, by which certain instructions were given to Chief Commissioners/Directors of Income-tax with regard to assessment of cases in which Indo-Mauritius Double Taxation Avoidance Convention, 1983, is ultra vires provisions of ss.90, 119, Income Tax Act, 1961 and also, otherwise illegal - Held, contention of respondents proceeds on fallacious premise that liability to taxation is same as payment of tax Liability to taxation is a legal situation; payment of tax is a fiscal fact - For purpose of application of art.4 of DTAC, what is relevant is legal situation, namely, liability to taxation, and not fiscal fact of actual payment of tax - If this were not so, DTAC would not have used words 'liable to taxation', but would have used some appropriate words like 'pays tax' - On language of DTAC, it is not possible to accept contention of respondents that offshore companies incorporated and registered under MOBA are not 'liable to taxation' under Mauritius Income-tax Act - Nor is it possible to accept contention that such companies would not be 'resident' in Mauritius within meaning of art 3 r/w art.4 of DTAC.
