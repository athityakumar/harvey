Kailash Singh v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1694; (2005) 13 SCC 576

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Retirement of service - Recovery of salary - Improper records - Appellant was employee of respondent Board, who joined in year 1968 and worked upto 2000 - Appellant thereafter examined by Medical Board, which showed age of appellant as 65 years - On basis of report it was considered by respondents that effective date of retirement of appellant would be 31-3-1995, as result appellant was treated to have retired on 31-3-1995 and salary for period beyond 31-3-1995 was sought to be recovered from appellant - Appellant filed petition before HC for recovery of salary and post retiral benefits, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, that Court fails to appreciate submission made on behalf of respondents in background of facts. Respondents took work from appellant without any dispute. He would obviously be entitled to his salary and there is no reason to that he should be denied post retiral benefits. Appellant’s total service comes to 32 years. Court has already adverted to fact that medical report has not been placed on record, nor as to that is meant by term ‘average age’ has been explained to Court. In totality of facts and circumstances of appellant’s case, Court finds no good reason to deny those benefits to appellant. In result, order of HC is set aside and it is directed that no recovery of salary paid to appellant for period from 1-4-1995 to 24-4-2000 shall be made, as also submitted on behalf of respondents, and post retiral benefits shall also be worked out accordingly. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When employer did not follow records of service book of employee then he cannot claim to recover salary, which he paid to employee beyond his service period.
