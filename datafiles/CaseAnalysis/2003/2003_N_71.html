State, through Special Cell, New Delhi v Navjot Sandhu @ Afshan Guru and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 473; (2003) 6 SCC 641; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1545; 2003 (2) CCR 186; 2003 (2) Crimes 483; 2003 (5) CRJ 216; 2003 (104) DLT 794; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 570; 2003 (2) JCC 868; JT 2003 (4) SC 605; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 189; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 860; 2003(4) SCALE 629; 2003 (3) SLT 572; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 130; 2003 (4) Supreme 133; 2003 (2) UC 1071

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120, 120B, 121, 121A, 122, 124, 186, 302, 307, 332, 353 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss.3, 4, 5 - Arms Act, 1959, ss.25, 27 - Prevention of Terrorism Act, 2002, ss.18, 34 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss.378(3), 397, 397(2), 397(3), 482 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 41, 42 - Parliament attack case - Respondents applied before Special Judge seeking a direction that intercepted conversation not be used as evidence in trial for proving charge/s under POTA - Special Judge passed an interlocutory order dismissing applications and held that evidence collected by various police officials when case was registered under different provisions of law cannot be washed away merely because provisions of POTA were added - On appeal HC disposed of all Petitions/Applications without mentioning whether it was exercising its power of superintendence u/art. 227 of Constitution of India or its inherent power u/s. 482 of CrPC - Whether, on facts of this case, HC could or should have exercised power under art 227 or jurisdiction u/s. 482? - Held, inherent power is to be used only in cases where there is an abuse of process of Court or where interference is absolutely necessary for securing ends of justice - There was no miscarriage of justice or palpable illegality, which required immediate interference - Neither power under art 227 nor power u/s. 482 enabled HC to correct an error in interpretation even if HC felt that order dated 11 th July 2002 was erroneous - Even if HC did not agree with correctness of that order, HC should have refused to interfere as order could be corrected in appeal u/s. 34 POTA - Appeals allowed.
