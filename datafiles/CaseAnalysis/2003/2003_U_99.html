M/s. Pawan Hans Ltd. v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 333; (2003) 5 SCC 71; AIR 2003 SC 1427; 2003 CCC 632; 4 CLC 434; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 676; 2003 (2) CompLJ 297; 2003 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2003 (3) SC 543; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 181; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 649; 2003(3) SCALE 692; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 443; 2003 (3) SLT 136; 2003 (3) Supreme 242

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP

Keywords: Securities Exchange Act, 1934, Manipulation Of Prices Or Conditions Of Delivery

Summary: Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act - S. 10 - complaint under - Dismissed by the Commission - appeal - Held, to fall under the ambit of 'restrictive trade practice' the conduct of party complained against should be such which may have the effect of preventing, distorting or restricting, competition in any manner which may tend to obstruct flow of capital into the stream of production or may bring about manipulation of prices or conditions of delivery resulting in imposition on the consumers unjustified costs or restrictions. - No such facts have been averred which may be said to have constituted restrictive trade practice on the part of the respondent.
