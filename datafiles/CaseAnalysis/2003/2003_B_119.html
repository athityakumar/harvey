Bidesh Singh v Madhu Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 856; (2003) 11 SCC 448; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 239; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 199; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 500; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 46; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 823; 2003(8) SCALE 727; 2003 (6) SLT 471; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 1074; 2003 (7) Supreme 453

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 82, 83, 86(1), 100(1)(b), 117 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 rr.56(2), 94 A - CPC, 1908, o. 7, r.11, o. 14, r.2 - Election petition dismissed by trial court as well HC - Held, question as to whether election petitioner was estopped and precluded from raising contentions in his election petition as regards validity or otherwise of ballot papers, was a matter which could have been gone into only at trial election petition could have been allowed or dismissed by Tribunal having regard to provisions contained in s 100(1)(e) of Act only after a full-dressed trial and upon giving opportunity to parties to examine themselves and/or their witnesses in support of their respective cases - In such a proceeding, parties could even examine Presiding Officer or other official witnesses as regards contention as to whether said ballot papers were valid or invalid - Further held, keeping in view fact that Tribunal has exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 86 of Act, impugned order is set aside.
