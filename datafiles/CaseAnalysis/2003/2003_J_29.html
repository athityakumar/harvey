Commissioner of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Jalaram Wood Crafts (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 277; (2003) 5 SCC 79; AIR 2003 SC 1414; 2003 (86) ECC 536; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 251; JT 2003 (3) SC 362; 2003 (55) RLT 374; 2003(3) SCALE 328; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1047; 2003 (3) SLT 726; 2003 (2) Supreme 921

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - whether exemption under Notification No. 217/86 for the intermediate products manufactured by the assessee, namely, glue, UF resin and wood veneer etc. used in the manufacture of final products, namely, plywood, veneer, panel flush doors etc., is available to the assessee as the final products were exempted under Notification No. 175/86 - Held, even if the claim of exemption by the assessee in regard to the inputs is based on Notification No. 217/86 and it is not entitled to the same, it hardly makes any difference as the inputs fall within the meaning of 'specified goods' in Notification No. 175/86. Therefore, their clearance value ought to have been excluded while arriving at the aggregate value for the purposes of Notification No. 175/86.
