K. Balakrishnan v K.Kamalam. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1263; (2004) 1 SCC 581; AIR 2004 SC 1257; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 79; 2004 (2) AWC 1462; 2004 (186) CTR 209; JT 2004 (1) SC 572; 2004 (1) KLT 623; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 171; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 513; 2003(10) SCALE 851; 2003 (Supp) RD 595; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 1097; [2004] 135 TAXMAN 48

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 126 and 127 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 11 - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956 - Whether appellant who was minor on date of execution of gift deed D/- 24-09-1945, can be held to have legally accepted property in suit gifted to him and said gift-deed was irrevocable? - Held, s. 126 specifies circumstances under which a gift can be suspended or revoked - S. 127 throws light on question of validity of transfer of property by gift to a minor - It recognises minor's capacity to accept gift without intervention of guardian, if it is possible, or through him - Last part of s. 127, clearly indicates that a minor donee, who can be said to be in law incompetent to contract u/s. 11 of Contract Act is, however, competent to accept a non-onerous gift - Acceptance of an onerous gift, however, cannot bind minor - If he accepts gift during his minority of a property burdened with obligation and on attaining majority does not repudiate but retains it, he would be bound by obligation attached to it - Gift was executed in 1945 - It remained in force for about 25 years during which time donee had attained majority and had not repudiated same - It was, therefore, not competent for donor to have cancelled gift and executed a will in relation to property - Appeal allowed.
