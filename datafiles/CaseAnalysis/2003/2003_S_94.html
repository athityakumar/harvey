Rafique Bibi (D) by LRs. v Sayed Waliuddin (D) by LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 656; (2004) 1 SCC 287; AIR 2003 SC 3789; 2003 (53) ALR 620; 2003 (5) AWC 4596; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 160; 2005 (1) LCD 607; 2003 (2) RCJ 59; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 297; 2003(6) SCALE 785; 2003 (1) SCW 4332; 2003 (5) SLT 127; 2003 (supp) RD 788; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 100; 2003 (6) Supreme 300

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952 s.13(1)(a) - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950 - Tenant defaulted in payment of rent - Whether court before passing decree, should have afforded tenant an opportunity of depositing rent in arrears - Held, it was for tenants to have invited attention of Court by making an application so as to avail an opportunity of wiping out effect of their default, which gave rise to cause of action to respondents, by depositing rent during pendency of suit - That having not been done, tenants-appellants cannot be heard to urge any infirmity in decree - Further held, accepting it at its face value, in eyes of law, challenge seeks to expose a procedural irregularity which may, at best, result in decree being termed as an 'illegal decree', but that in itself would not amount to branding decree as 'without jurisdiction' or 'a nullity' plea, which is sought to be urged in execution proceedings, was available to be raised by tenants before HC in an appeal against decree - Such a plea was not taken before passing of decree and cannot be allowed to be urged during execution proceedings.
