Jammigumpula Sivaiah v Andhra Pradesh State Financial Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1700; (2004) 13 SCC 653

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - State Financial Corporations Act, 1951 - Sale - Validity - Andhra Pradesh State Financial Corporation had lent some amount to the father of respondent Nos. 1 and 3 - Amounts were not repaid - Financial Corporation purported to sell the property to the appellant - Sale was challenged - HC by the impugned judgment set aside the sale on the ground that the guidelines laid down by SC in the case of Mahesh Chandra v. Regional Manager, U.P. Financial Corpn., 1992 Indlaw SC 1069, have not been followed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the procedure followed was neither transparent nor fair. The principles of natural justice have also not been followed. DB has correctly set aside the auction-sale. Appeals dismissed.
