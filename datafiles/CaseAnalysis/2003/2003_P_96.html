Kailash Nath Agarwal and others v Pradeshiya Indust. & INV. Corp of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 188; (2003) 4 SCC 305; AIR 2003 SC 1886; 2003 (2) BC 296; 4 CLC 339; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 4; 2003 (3) CompLJ 103; 2004 (1) DRTC 26; JT 2003 (2) SC 115; 2004 (96) RD 158; 2003(2) SCALE 160; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1159; 2003 (2) SLT 81; 2003 (2) Supreme 207; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1198

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Foreigners

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Public Monies (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Amendment Act, 1993, Sick Industrial Company (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Amendment Act (12 of 1994)

Summary: Sick Industrial Companies' (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - S. 22(1) - scope of protection afforded to guarantees under Section 22(1) of the Act - apparent distinction between the expressions 'proceeding' and 'suit' used in Section 22(1) - held, the deliberate choice of the word 'suit' in the circumstances would indicate that Parliament intended to limit the ambit of the amendment introduced to particular modes for the recovery of money or enforcement of guarantees. - No corresponding reason for widening the scope of the word 'suit' so as to cover proceedings against the guarantor of an industrial company. - Since Section 22(1) only prohibits recovery against the Industrial company, there is no protection afforded to guarantors against recovery proceedings under the U.P. Act.
