Sahebgouda (dead) by Lrs. and others v Ogeppa and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 294; (2003) 6 SCC 151; AIR 2003 SC 2743; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 374; 2003 (3) CalLT(SC) 44; JT 2003 (3) SC 338; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 143; 2003(3) SCALE 446; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 90; 2003 (1) SCW 3088; 2003 (3) SLT 162; 2003 (3) Supreme 13; 2003 (2) UC 846

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - S. 80 - the High Court dismissed the suit filed by the appellants on the ground that the same was barred by Section 80 of the Act - appeal - held, the mere fact that the appellants along with others had taken steps to get the temple and the land annexed to it registered as public trust by moving an application to that effect under Section 18 of the Act before the Assistant Charity Commissioner cannot in any manner oust the jurisdiction of the Civil Court in view of the allegations disclosed in the plaint and the relief claimed therein. The reliefs so claimed do not at all come within the ambit of Section 19 or Section 79 of the Act on which the Deputy or Assistant Charity Commissioner has the jurisdiction to hold an inquiry and give a decision. Therefore, the bar of Section 80 of the Act would not apply. - The view taken by the High Court is clearly erroneous in law and therefore, the judgment and decree passed by it must be set aside.
