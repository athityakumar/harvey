Union of India v Chajju Ram (Dead) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.N. Agrawal, AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 388; (2003) 5 SCC 568; AIR 2003 SC 2339; 2003 (51) ALR 534; 2003 (3) AWC 2333; JT 2003 (4) SC 161; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 682; 2003(4) SCALE 155; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 647; 2003 (3) SLT 262; 2003 (3) Supreme 661; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1797

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Of India Act, 1971, Improvement Trust Act, Central Act, 1968, Kanpur Urban Development Act, Bombay Town Planning Act

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Defence Of India Act, 1971, ss.19, 23, 24, 29, 30, 31, 4, 7(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.23(1), 23(1-A), 23(2), 28, 28A - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984, ss. 23(2), 28 - Improvement Trust Act - Constitutional validity of - Whether absence of any provision for payment of solatium and interest therein for acquisition of land is hit by art.14 of Constitution of India - Whether provisions of s.28 A of Land Acquisition Act are to be read into Act - Held, provision for grant of solatium was inserted in Land Acquisition Act by Parliament having regard to fact that amount of compensation awarded to owner of land is to be determined on basis of value thereof as on date of issuance of notification u/s. 4 of Act - In terms of s.23(2) of Land Acquisition Act, therefore, solatium is paid in addition to amount of market value of land classification sought to be made for determination of amount of compensation for acquisition of land under said Act vis-a-vis Land Acquisition Act is a reasonable and valid one - Said classification is founded on intelligible differentia and has a rational relation with object sought to be achieved by legislation in question - Appeal allowed.
