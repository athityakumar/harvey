Commissioner Of Customs, New Delhi v C.T. Scan Research Centre (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1108; (2003) 11 SCC 25; 2003 (88) ECC 231; 2003 (110) ECR 7; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 3; 2003 (57) RLT 1; 2003 (4) Supreme 485

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 28 - Hospital equipment - Respondent hospital imported goods and cleared them at nil duty - Non compliance with post importation conditions laid down by notification for import of goods at nil rate of duty, namely, free service at least to 40% of outdoor patients - Whether notice was issued beyond permissible limit of five years as per s. 28(1)? - Tribunal held, yes - Held, s. 28(1) is not applicable in view of judgment in case of Jagdish Cancer and Research Centre [2001 Indlaw SC 20060] - Impugned judgment and order passed by tribunal requires to be set aside as there was no question of complying with provisions of s. 28(1) of Customs Act - Appeal allowed.
