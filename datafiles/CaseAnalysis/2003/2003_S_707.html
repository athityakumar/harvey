Gurucharan Kumar and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 18; (2003) SCC (Cr) 675; (2003) 2 SCC 698; AIR 2003 SC 992; 2003 (1) CCR 131; 2003 (1) Crimes 329; 2003 (3) CRJ 355; 2003 CRLJ 1234; 2003 (1) DMC 177; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 35; JT 2003 (1) SC 60; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 199; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 577; 2003(1) SCALE 101; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 60; 2003 (1) SLT 260; 2003 (1) Supreme 165; 2003 (1) UC 528

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - Ss. 304 B and 306 - offences under - appeal - Held, the evidence on record does not suggest that the deceased was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or by her father and mother-in-law for or in connection with any demand for dowry. There is nothing in the suicide note or in the letters which may even remotely suggest that she was ending her life because of the mal-treatment meted out to her by the members of her matrimonial family. - Unfortunately, the High Court has not even referred to the letters and was content to reach its conclusion on the basis of the oral evidence on record. - Appellants entitled to acquittal.
