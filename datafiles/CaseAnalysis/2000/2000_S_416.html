K. K. Patel and Another v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 535; (2000) 6 SCC 195; (2001) SCC (Cr) 200; AIR 2000 SC 3346; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 505; 2000 (3) CCR 1; 2000 (2) Crimes 314; 2000 CRLJ 4592; 2000 (4) G.L.R. 3599; 2000 (2) JCC 523; JT 2000 (7) SC 246; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 863; 2000(4) SCALE 572; 2000 (5) SLT 27; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 312; 2000 (4) Supreme 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161 and 397 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 330 - Whether Sessions Judge has transgressed his jurisdiction in its revisional powers? - High Court held that sessions court exceeded his revisional powers - Held, Sessions Judge rightly exercised his revisional powers and in present case complaint was filed after limitation period as indicated in s. 161 CrPC, 1973 - Complaint was irretrievably barred - Appeal allowed.
