National Insurance Company Ltd. v Behari Lal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 430; (2000) 7 SCC 137; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1319; 2000 (2) ACC 463; AIR 2000 SC 3053; 2000 (41) ALR 65; 2000 (4) AWC 3028; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 104; 2000 (3) G.L.R. 2396; JT 2000 (10) SC 39; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 161; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 445; 2000(6) SCALE 177; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 698; 2001 (1) UC 4B

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 147(2), Proviso, 95(2) - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Point that arises for consideration is the scope and import of the proviso to sub-s. (2) of s.147 of the 1988 Act - Liability of Insurer when statutory limited liability policy taken under Old Act and policy valid for 4 months after New Act but without limits for liability under Old Act - Interpretation of - Held, liability of Insurer becomes unlimited - Appeal dismissed.
