Prashant Kumar Shahi v Ghaziabad Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 196; (2000) 4 SCC 120; 2000 (40) ALR 27; 2000 (2) AWC 1586B; 2000 (1) CCC 84; 2001 (1) CPJ(Sc) 8; JT 2000 (4) SC 607; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 717; 2000(3) SCALE 31; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 896; 2000 (3) Supreme 597

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; MRTP

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices

Summary: Consumer Protection - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 36-A, 36-B(a), 36-D - Possession - Allotment of a plot - Installment - Unfair Trade Practice - Delay - Appellant applied for allotment of a plot under Scheme of "Indrapuram" and paid registration amount of Rs.42,000 - A further sum of Rs.63,000/being reservation amount was paid and plot allotted to him - First installment of Rs.76,125/was paid and later installment were not paid allegedly on ground that respondent/authority had not made any development at site - Possession of plot would be handed over to him - Appellant filed complaint u/ss.36A,36B(a),36D of 1969 Act was registered as Unfair Trade Practice - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent/ authority was responsible for alleged delay - Held, no ground was made out to interfere with order of Commission - No case of unfair trade practices by and on behalf of respondent had been made out - Respondent/authority had failed to deliver possession as per terms of brochure - Allot tee could not be burdened with interest on balance amount not paid by him respondent/authority had proved to be responsible for delay in delivering possession of plot, appellant could not be burdened to pay penal interest for period - Appellant was detained liable to pay amount demanded from him before delivery of possession of plot - Appeal dismissed.
