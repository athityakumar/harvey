Standard Chartered Bank and Another v Custodian and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 276; (2000) 6 SCC 427; AIR 2000 SC 1488; 2001 (1) BC 127; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 314; JT 2000 (4) SC 560; 2000(3) SCALE 310; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 81; 2000 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 60

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Special Court Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities Act, 1992 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Possession - Mal-practices in transactions - Notification - Evidence - Bonus shares - Respondent No.1 /Custodian appointed u/s. 3(2) of Act - Custodian shares and securities, which belonged to respondent no. 2, were in possession of appellant bank - Appellants sought a declaration that no right, title or interest in shares and same did not belong to him on date of notification - Special Court was established u/s.5 of Act to be presided over by HC - Reserve Bank of India noticed large-scale irregularities and mal-practices in transactions in both Govt. and other securities indulged in by some brokers in collusion with employees of various banks and financial institutions - Aggrieved by findings of Special Court in relation to quantum of loss suffered, rights of appellants in regard to bonus shares and dividend and interest which had pledged - Whether appellants were entitled to sell original shares and bonus shares - Held, appellant Bank to lead evidence to prove that it had paid sums of money and did not receive the securities - Special Court on basis of evidence that except for sum of Rs. 280.80 crores, balance claim of appellants stood disproved - Appellant bank had failed to prove loss in excess of Rs. 280.80 crores stands disproved - Bank was entitled to exercise ownership right in respect of shares and to accretions received by appellants - Appellant Bank had suffered loss inasmuch as purchases aggregating Rs.1258 crores were not supported by deliveries with result that securities and shares worth Rs.145 crores had been pledged in favour of appellant bank -Appellant bank's claim of loss of about Rs. 280 crores had upheld and Special Court awarding costs of Rs. 30 lacs could not in any way be regarded as incorrect - Appellants were entitled to sell original shares, rights shares and bonus shares and also to retain dividend and interest accrued on original shares - Appeal disposed of.
