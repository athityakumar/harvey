Charan Singh v Healing Touch Hospital and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.G. Balakrishnan, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2669; (2000) 7 SCC 668; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1444; AIR 2000 SC 3138; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 6; 2000 (41) ALR 433; 2000 (4) AWC 3316; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 323; 2000 (4) CompLJ 213; 2000 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2000 (3) CPR 1; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 26; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 172; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 745; 2000(6) SCALE 431; 2000 (6) SLT 867; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 337; 2000 (6) Supreme 321; 2001 (1) UC 59

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 21 - Relevant factor, for quantification of damages - Complaint against Hospital - Wrong treatment - Claim on basis on salary being drawn - Styling claim as 'unrealistic' etc - Dismissal order after six years - If proper - Held, not proper - Appeal allowed.
