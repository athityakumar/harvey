Amresh Tiwari v Lalta Prasad Dubey and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 703; (2000) 4 SCC 440; (2000) SCC (Cr) 806; AIR 2000 SC 1504; AIR 2000 SCW 1467; 2000 ALJ 1095; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 817; 2000 (2) CCR 92; 2000 (3) Crimes 37; 2000 CRLJ 2226; 2000 (2) JCC 434; JT 2000 (4) SC 466; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 614; 2000(3) SCALE 206; 2000 (4) SLT 78; 2000 (4) Supreme 665

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 145 - (A) Whether proceeding u/s. 145, CrPC, 1973 can be continued during pendency of civil suit regarding same properties? - Held, law on this subject-matter has been settled by decision in case of Ram Sumer Puri Mahant v. State of U.P. 1984 Indlaw SC 15 - When a civil litigation is pending for property wherein question of possession is involved and has been adjudicated, no justification for initiating a parallel criminal proceeding u/s. 145 of CrPC, 1973 - It is only in cases where civil suit is for possession or for declaration of title in respect of same property and where reliefs regarding protection of property concerned can be applied for and granted by civil court that proceedings u/s. 145 should not be allowed to continue - This is because civil court is competent to decide question of title as well as possession between parties and orders of civil court would be binding on Magistrate - Sub-Divisional Magistrate was right in discontinuing proceedings u/s. 145 of CrPC, 1973 - (B) Interim order - Whether binding? - Held, it is settled law that interim orders, even though they may have been confirmed by higher courts, never bind and do not prevent passing of contrary order at stage of final hearing - Appeal allowed.
