S. Saktivel (Dead) By Lrs v M. Venugopal Pillai and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 503; (2000) 7 SCC 104; AIR 2000 SC 2633; 2000 (40) ALR 806; JT 2000 (9) SC 345; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 40; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 93; 2000 (91) RD 615; 2000(5) SCALE 517; 2000 (3) SCJ 212; 2000 (6) SLT 120; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 361; 2000 (5) Supreme 450

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 92, proviso. (4)(C) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Document required by law to be in writing - Property - deed settled by father on his son - To be in writing - Held, parol evidence to substantiate any subsequent arrangement modifying earlier written deed - Cannot be permitted - Trial Court proceeds on basis of agreed fact - Then new plea - In subsequent proceeding - Not allowed.
