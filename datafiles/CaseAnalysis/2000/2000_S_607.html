Masumsha Hasanasha Musalman v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 170; (2000) 3 SCC 557; (2000) SCC (Cr) 722; AIR 2000 SC 1876; 2000 (1) CCR 271; 2000 (1) Crimes 239; 2000 (2) JCC 635; JT 2000 (2) SC 367; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 116; 2000(2) SCALE 70; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1155; 2000 (2) SLT 347; 2000 (1) Supreme 584

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304, Part II - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3(2)(v) - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Fact that accused gave only one blow by sharp edged weapon resulting into death of deceased and presence of other injuries caused to deceased due to scuffle supported by medical evidence only shows that accused had no intention to kill deceased - Further, there was no evidence on record that accused committed alleged offence of causing death of deceased on ground that deceased was member of scheduled caste and scheduled tribe - Held, accused liable to be convicted u/s. 304, Part II and not u/ss. 302 and 3(2)(v) of Act, because there is no evidence at all to effect that accused committed offence alleged against him on ground that deceased is a member of a scheduled caste and scheduled tribe.
