Air India Limited v Cochin International Airport Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 7; (2000) 2 SCC 617; AIR 2000 SC 801; AIR 2000 SCW 351; 2000 (1) ARBLR 554; 2001 (1) CalLT(SC) 41; JT 2000 (1) SC 481; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 72; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 670; 2000(1) SCALE 346; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Ground handling services

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract - Ground handling services - Award - Instant appeals were filed against order passed by DB. Held, Board of Directors having taken tentative decision that there was no point in calling Cambatta thereafter for any negotiation. It may be recalled that Cambatta was recommended over Air India by High Level Committee only because Cambatta's financial rating was found higher. What is significant to note is that even High Level Committee had in its minutes noted that financial rating cannot be sole criterion for taking final decision. Moreover, in a commercial transaction of such a complex nature a lot of balancing work has to be done while weighing all relevant factors and final decision has to be taken after taking an overall view of transaction. It is true that even though Cambatta had called upon CIAL to produce minutes of the meeting of Board of Directors held the same was not made available to Cambatta. But that did not entitle HC to draw any adverse inference. HC had not called upon CIAL to produce those minutes. SC do not think that CIAL did any wrong in taking into consideration fact that Air India is an airline and being a national carrier would be in a position to bring more traffic of Air India and other domestic lines if it was awarded contract. As regards merits of rival offers, SC do not think it proper to look at only financial aspect and hold that CIAL did not accept Cambatta's offer, even though it was better, because it wanted to favor Air India or that it had acted under influence of Air India and Ministry of Civil Aviation. In commercial transaction of complex nature what may appear to be better, on face of it, may not be considered so when an overall view is taken. In such matters Court cannot substitute it's decision for decision of party awarding contract. On basis of material placed on record SC find that CIAL bona fide believed that involving a public sector undertaking and national carrier would, in long run, prove to be more beneficial to CIAL For all these reasons it is not possible to agree with finding of HC that CIAL had acted arbitrarily and unreasonably and was also influenced by extraneous considerations during its decision making process. Judgment of DB is set aside and decision of Single Judge was confirmed. Appeal allowed.
