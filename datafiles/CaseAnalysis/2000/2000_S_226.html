Union of India and Others v Solar Pesticides Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 249; (2000) 2 SCC 705; AIR 2000 SC 862; 2000 (89) ECR 11; 2000 (116) E.L.T. 401; JT 2000 (1) SC 577; 2000 (37) RLT 251; 2000(1) SCALE 423; 2000 (2) SCJ 277; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 629; 2000 (2) SLT 110; 2003 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 430; 2000 (2) Supreme 353

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 27, 28-C and 28-D - Central Excises and Customs Laws (Amendment) Act, 1991 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Customs Notification No 35/81 CE D/- 01-03-1981 - Whether doctrine of unjust enrichment is applicable in respect of raw material imported and consumed in manufacture of a final product? - S. 27 of Act is, in a sense, a complete code by itself, dealing with claim for refund of duty - To claim refund of duty it is immaterial whether goods imported are used by importer himself and duty thereon passed on to purchaser of finished product or that imported goods are sold as such with incidence of tax being passed on to buyer - Held, principle of unjust enrichment incorporated in s. 27 of Act would be applicable in respect of imported raw material and captively consumed in manufacture of a final product - Appeal allowed.
