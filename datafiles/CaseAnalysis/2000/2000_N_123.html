Thimmaiah and Others v Ningamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 423; (2000) 7 SCC 409; AIR 2000 SC 3529B; 2000 (41) ALR 94; JT 2000 (9) SC 516; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 609; 2000(6) SCALE 115; 2000 (3) SCJ 341; 2000 (6) SLT 334; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 653; 2000 (5) Supreme 739

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Coparcener, Succession & Inheritance, Ancestral Immovable Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 4, 6 and 8 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Hindu Law Women's Rights Act, 1933 - Mysore Hindu Law Women's Rights Act, 1933, ss. 3(c) 8(1)(d) and 1(d) - Issue to be decided is share of each of parties in coparcenary properties - 'H' was karta of coparcenary and had two wives 'S' and 'N' - Appellants are children of first wife, 'S' - Respondent 1 is second wife and Respondent 2 is her daughter - Held, under Explanation I to s. 6 of 1956 Act, Court will have to ascertain what shares of parties would be as if 'H' had sought for partition just before his death - Only other coparcener being Appellant 1, partition would have to be effected according to s. 8(1)(a) which provides for partition between a coparcener and his son/sons - U/s. 8(1)(a) female members who could claim a share in coparcenary properties would be 'H' mother, his unmarried daughter (Respondent 2) and widow or unmarried daughters of any predeceased sons or brother - Sole female member entitled to a share u/s. 8(1)(a) is Respondent 2 as nobody else in that category is alive - Appellant being other coparcener would get 1/2 of coparcenary properties on partition - Respondent 2 would get 1/2 her brother's share - Remaining interest would belong to 'H' - U/s. 8 of 1956 Act, each of parties to this appeal is entitled to claim a share in 'H' interest as his Class I heir - On basis of ratio in Gurupad Khandappa Magdum case, 1978 Indlaw SC 232 Respondent 2 would also be entitled to a share in Hiri's interest as an heir on intestacy - Further held, if there were an actual partition of coparcenary properties between 'H' and his son, u/s. 8(1)(a) of Mysore Act, his son, Appellant 1 would get 1/2 share - His wife, Respondent 1, and Appellants 2, 3 and 4 would not get any share in coparcenary property at all - Respondent 2 as unmarried daughter would get a share calculated in terms of s. 8(2)(c) of Mysore Act, namely, 1/4th of share of her brother in addition to her share as heir of 'H' - All appellants as well as both respondents are each entitled to an equal share in 'H' interest as heirs on intestacy - Appeal partly allowed.
