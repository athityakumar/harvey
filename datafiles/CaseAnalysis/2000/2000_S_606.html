Madhukar Bhaskarrao Joshi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2789; (2000) 8 SCC 571; (2001) SCC (Cr) 34; AIR 2001 SC 147; AIR 2000 SCW 4018; 2000 (4) CCR 205; 2000 (4) Crimes 240; 2001 CRLJ 175; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 458; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 705; 2000(7) SCALE 382; 2000 (8) SLT 149; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 475; 2000 (7) Supreme 522

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 20, 7 and 13 - Appellant accepted bribe and was convicted by trial court for one year but High Court while confirming conviction sentenced him to only one day imprisonment - Notice issued by Supreme Court to appellant why his conviction by trial court should not be maintained when HC reduced sentenced to impermissible limits - Once prosecution established that gratification in any form had been paid or accepted by a public servant court is under a legal compulsion to presume that said gratification was paid or accepted as a motive or reward to do or forbear from doing any official act - Held, prosecution is only required to prove that accused was found accepting bribe which is done in present case - Not special reason in this case as for appellant to entitle to get a sentence less than minimum prescribed by law - HC wrongly interfered with trial court conviction order - Order of trial court restored - Appeal dismissed.
