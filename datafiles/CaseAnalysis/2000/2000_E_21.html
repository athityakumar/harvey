Whirlpool of India Limited v Employees' State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.B. Majmudar, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 398; (2000) 3 SCC 185; (2000) SCC (L&S) 326; AIR 2000 SC 1190; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 33; 2000 (2) AWC 1347; JT 2000 (3) SC 147; 2000 (33) LabIC 1023; 2000 (1) LLJ 1101; 2000 (2) LLN 72; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 132; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 145; 2000(2) SCALE 284; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 165; 2000 (2) SLR 15; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1812

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(22) and 75 - Industrial Disputes Act, s. 2(p) - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938, s. 9 A - Appellant under a 'Production Incentive Scheme' pays to its workers production incentive at rates specified in scheme besides normal wages - The respondent Employees' State Insurance Corporation treating said payment as 'wages' issued a demand to appellant for payment of contributions towards Employees' State Insurance Fund - It led to filing of an application u/s. 75 of Act by appellant before Employees' Insurance Court challenging said demand - Whether payments towards production incentive made by appellant to its workers under 'Production Incentive Scheme' falls within scope and ambit of 'wages' as defined in Section 2(22) of Act? - Payment of production incentive, on facts of present case, does not fall either under first part or last part of definition of term 'wages' as defined in Section 2(22) of Act - There has to be actual payment - Held, as payment of production incentive, was made quarterly, does not fall either under first part or last part of definition of term 'wages' as defined in Section 2(22) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
