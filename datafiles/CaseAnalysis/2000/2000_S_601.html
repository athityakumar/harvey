20TH Century Finance Corporation Limited and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, D.P. Mohapatra, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 449; (2000) 6 SCC 12; AIR 2000 SC 2436; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 105; JT 2000 (7) SC 177; 2000(5) SCALE 13; 2000 (5) SLT 459; 2000 (119) STC 182; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 120; 2000 (4) Supreme 534

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952, Goods for Any Purpose Act, 1985

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 4 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.226, 246, 269(3), 286, 366, 366(29-A)(d) - Appellants and petitioners agree to give on lease diverse machinery/equipments subject to terms and conditions stipulated in Master Lease Agreements - According to appellants and petitioners, one transaction of transfer of right to use goods was subjected to sales tax by more than one States - On such a transaction, some States levy tax on appellants and petitioners, merely because goods were found to be located in their States at time of execution of contract which had taken place outside State - Appellants and petitioners challenged validity of legislations - Power of States legislatures to levy sales tax on transfer of right to use any goods envisaged u/art. 366 (29A)(d) of Constitution on the premise that goods put to use were located within their States - Whether State could levy sales tax on transfer of right to use goods merely on basis that goods put to use were located within its State irrespective - Held, definition of sale so far as it relates to transaction of transfer of right to use any goods was concerned in Central Sales Tax Act had not been amended, provisions of s.4 of 1956 Act would not be applicable to such transactions - Transaction of transfer of right to use goods could not be termed as contract of bailment as it was deemed sale within meaning of legal fiction engrafted in art. 366(29A) (d) of Constitution wherein location or delivery of goods to put to use was immaterial - State would not have power to levy sales tax on such transactions - S. 5.E (b) of Act read down effect that it would not be applicable to transaction of transfer of right to use any goods if such deemed sale was an outside sale - Sale in course of import of goods into or export of goods out of territory of India - Appeal disposed of.
