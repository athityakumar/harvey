Ramesh Bhai J. Patel and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2741; (2001) 1 SCC 243; AIR 2001 SC 339; 2000 (164) CTR 648; [2001] 247 ITR 182; JT 2001 (1) SC 77; 2000(8) SCALE 121; 2001 (1) SLT 200; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 424; 2000 (8) Supreme 240; [2001] 114 TAXMAN 236

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Income Tax Rules

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 269 - UA(b) - Income Tax Rules - Correct interpretation of s. 269 - S. 269-UA(b) prescribes how apparent consideration under agreement, is to be determined, and it states that if consideration under agreement is payable on any date or dates falling after date of agreement, value of consideration that is payable after date of agreement shall be deemed to be discounted value of such consideration as on date of agreement - Held, purpose of Chap. XX-C is to determine whether immovable property has been sought to be transferred at an undervaluation - To determine whether there has been an undervaluation, true consideration for transfer has to be determined and, necessarily, it has to be determined as on date of agreement for transfer - Further held, it is clear from latter part of cl. (b), that discounted value of such consideration as on date of such agreement for transfer therein indicates point of time from which period for discounting must be calculated - Appeals dismissed.
