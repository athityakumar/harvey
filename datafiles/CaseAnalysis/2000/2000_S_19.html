Indian Airlines Limited and Others v S. Gopalakrishnan

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2902; (2001) 2 SCC 362; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1036; 2001 (1) CLR 588; JT 2001 (1) SC 28; 2000(8) SCALE 253; 2001 (1) SCJ 400; 2001 (2) SLJ 179; 2000 (8) SLT 805; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 548; 2000 (8) Supreme 279; 2001 (1) UC 283; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 263

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appellant No. 1 called by notice for post of Junior Operator - Requisite qualification for appointment, two years' experience in equipment operating or driving and possess current heavy vehicles driving licence - Whether High Court was justified in holding that action of appellant arbitrary in not appointing respondent on post as he possessed necessary qualification? - When in addition to qualification, experience is prescribed, it would only mean acquiring experience after obtaining necessary qualification and not before obtaining such qualification - Respondent did not possess 5 years of experience as required under relevant rule - Held, when requirement was made very clear that candidate should have experience only after acquiring qualification, view taken by HC to contrary either by learned Single Judge or Division Bench, does not stand to reason - Appeals allowed.
