Commissioner of Income Tax v Karnataka Power Corporation

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 637; (2002) 9 SCC 571; 2000 (162) CTR 249; [2001] 247 ITR 268; JT 2000 (9) SC 629; 2000(6) SCALE 421; 2000 (6) SLT 540; 2000 (8) Supreme 400; [2000] 112 TAXMAN 629

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Cinema Theatre, Civil Appeals dismissed

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Investment allowance - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case Tribunal is right in upholding order of CIT(A) holding that assessee is entitled to investment allowance on generating station building considering it as 'plant'? - Held, question, basically, is a question of fact, and where it is found as a fact that a building has been so planned and constructed as to serve an assessee's special technical requirements, it will qualify to be treated as a plant for purposes of investment allowance - In the instant case there is a finding by fact-finding authority that assessee's generating station building is so constructed as to be an integral part of its generating system - It must, therefore, be held that it is a 'plant' and entitled to investment allowance accordingly - Appeal dismissed.
