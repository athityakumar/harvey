Centre of Public Interest Litigation and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2563; (2000) 8 SCC 606; AIR 2001 SC 80; 2001 (1) ARBLR 319; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 277; 2000 (3) RAJ 380; 2000(7) SCALE 1; 2000 (7) SLT 658; 2000 (7) Supreme 160

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Petroleum & Gas; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Offer - Contract- Justifiability - Respondent no.1/Govt. of India (GOI) took policy decision and decided to develop oil fields on joint venture basis, because of crisis in foreign exchange and lack of resources for development - GOI invited bids and respondent nos. 4 and 5 were shortlisted - GOI along with ONGC entered into Joint Venture Contract with respondent nos. 4 and 5 and Letter of Award (LOA) was issued to said consortium-Aggrieved appellants /writ petitioners filed writ petition before HC challenging Joint Venture Contract which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether award of Joint venture contract to respondent nos. 4 and 5 was arbitrary for collateral consideration and actuated by malafides and criminal investigation was justifiable - Held, Court was unable to accept explanation of CBI and was constrained to observe that statement made in first affidavit as to existence of Part-II file could aptly be described as suggestio falsi and suppressio veri and explanation given in second affidavit of CBI also discloses sad state of affairs prevailing in Organization - Subsequently, CBI had stated before Court that Part II file with which Court was concerned, was destroyed unauthorisedly with ulterior motive by none other than official of CBI in collusion with senior officer of the same Organisation which fact, if true, reflects very poorly on integrity of CBI - Courts had very often relied on said Organisation for assistance by conducting special investigations - Further, reliance of Courts on CBI was based on confidence and reposed in it, instances like the one with which Courts were now confronted were likely to shake Court's confidence in said Organisation - Therefore, it was high time that said Organisation puts its house in order before it was too late - Hence, Court considered all material placed before it, arguments addressed and facts and circumstances of case were satisfied that appellants prayer to direct criminal investigation by appropriate agency was denied - Appeal dismissed.
