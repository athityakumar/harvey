Maharashtra Vikrikar Karamchari Sangathan and another v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 525; (2000) SCC (L&S) 288; (2000) 2 SCC 552; AIR 2000 SC 622; 2000 (1) CLR 487; JT 2000 (1) SC 132; 2000 (33) LabIC 681; 2000 (2) LLN 1; 2000(1) SCALE 105; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 166; 2000 (3) SLJ 1; 2000 (1) SLR 518; 2003 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 409; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 874

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Civil Services (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1982

Summary: Service - Constitution - Maharashtra Civil Services (Regulation Of Seniority) Rules, 1982, rr. 3, 4 and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - How to determine seniority amongst promotees and direct recruits who were promoted and appointed as Sales Tax Inspectors (STIs) during this block? - Held, effect of impugned provisional seniority list D\- 28-04-1987 would be that respondents 2 to 36 who were absorbed personnel and who were promoted as Sales Tax Inspectors after 1981 would be senior to those STIs like first respondent and other similarly situated persons who were promoted after selection prior to September 1980 - It was, therefore, an error on part of Govt. to ignore date of selection and appointment of first respondent and other similarly situated employees to post of STIs and treat them as junior to respondents 2 to 36 who were selected subsequently - If such course is followed, action of Govt. of Maharashtra and Commr. of Sales Tax would be violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
