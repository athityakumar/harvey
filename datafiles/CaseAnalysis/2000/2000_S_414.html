Camilo Vaz v State of Goa

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3536; (2000) 9 SCC 1; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1128; AIR 2000 SC 1374; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 807; 2000 CRLJ 1816; JT 2000 (4) SC 540; 2000(3) SCALE 195; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1088

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 304 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - 17 accused persons including one absconding, were tried for offences u/ss. 302, 307, 326, 325, 143, 144, 140 IPC r/w ss. 149 and 120-B IPC for having committed murder of deceased Sub-Inspector of Police and attempt to murder his two brothers I and V - Trial Court convicted five of them including appellant holding them guilty of murder of S u/s. 302 r/w ss. 120-B and 149 IPC - They were further held guilty for attempt to murder of I and V u/s. 307 r/w ss. 120-B and 149 IPC - They were also held guilty of unlawful assembly u/s. 143 and rioting u/s. 148 r/w ss. 120-B and 149 IPC - For these offences they were respectively sentenced to life imprisonment, 7 years imprisonment and 2 years imprisonment - Five accused had appealed to HC against their conviction and sentence - HC maintained conviction and sentence of appellant u/s. 302 IPC - Other four accused persons were convicted u/s. 326 IPC and their conviction for offence u/ss. 302 and 307 IPC was set aside - Their sentences were reduced to imprisonment they had already undergone - HC also rejected alternate plea of appellant that on facts of case there could be conviction only for offence u/s. 304, Part-I or Part-II IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Order passed by HC was justified - Held, act of appellant in hitting S was done with knowledge that it was likely to cause death but without any intention to cause death or to cause such bodily injury as was likely to cause death - Instant case of appellant would fall u/s. 304 part II IPC - Courts below did not apply their mind to this aspect of matter in proper perspective and they were rather swayed by fact that on account of danda blow by appellant, S died unnatural death - There was no material on record which showed that appellant was bent upon killing S and 'eventually death came out to be result' - Hence, appellant was held to be guilty for offence u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Conviction u/s. 302 IPC was set aside - Appellant was sentenced to undergo Rigorous Imprisonment for seven years - Appeal partly allowed.
