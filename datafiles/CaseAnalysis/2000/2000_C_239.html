K. Govindan and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 721; (2001) 1 SCC 460; AIR 2001 SC 254; 2000 (164) CTR 490; [2001] 247 ITR 192; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 394; 2001 (1) KLT 270; 2000(8) SCALE 47; 2000 (8) SLT 773; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 285; 2000 (8) Supreme 65; [2001] 114 TAXMAN 94

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2, 2(40), 139(8), 147 & 148 - Assessment Year - Interest Payable -Justifiable - Appellant filed return of income in response to notice issued u/s. 148 of theAct - Respondent/assessing authority charged interest u/ss. 139(8) and 271 of the Act - Appellants/assessee filed appeal before Appellate Authority, which held that no interest could be charged u/s. 139(8) of the Act - Appellants filed appeal before Tribunal, which set aside order of Assessing Authority and Appellate Authority - Respondents filed petition before HC, which held that assessing officer could legally charge interest u/s. 139(8) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, position that follows was that while making assessment u/s. 147 in a case where assessee furnishes a return in pursuance of notice served on him u/s. 148 of the Act, provision for charging interest u/s. 139(8) was applicable and it was open to AO to charge interest on assessee in such proceeding - Relied on K. Gopalaswami Mudaliar v Fifth Additional Income Tax Officer, Coimbatore, and Others 1963 Indlaw MAD 122 , National Agricultural Co-Operative Marketing Federation of India Limited v Union of India and Others 1980 Indlaw DEL 104 and Lally Jacob v Income Tax Officer and Others 1992 Indlaw KER 112 - Thus, a first or initial assessment u/s. 147 of the Act was a 'regular assessment' within meaning of s. 139(8) of the Act, had been position of law even before explanation in s.139(8) was added by amendment - In that view of matter explanation merely clarified position taking it beyond pale of doubt - Parliament thought it necessary to add \ explanation with a view to remove doubt raised in certain decisions of different HC in which a contrary view was taken - Thus explanation was merely a clarificatory provision and had application to period of assessment in instant case - Appeal dismissed.
