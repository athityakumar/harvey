P. Sarathy v State Bank Of India

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 534; (2000) 5 SCC 355; (2000) SCC (L&S) 699; AIR 2000 SC 2023; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 68; JT 2000 (7) SC 12; 2000 (2) KLT 771; 2000 (2) LLJ 661; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 108; 2000 (4) SLR 512; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 402

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC,1973, s. 312 - (A) Expenses of witnesses - Diet money - Supreme Court held, proper diet money must be paid immediately to witness not only when he is examined but for every adjourned hearing and he should not be left harassed by subordinate staff of Court - (B) Appellants contented that because the eyewitnesses' account of the involvement of accused was not accepted by either of the courts, therefore their evidence was suspect, is a non sequitur - Held, merely because one portion of the evidence of witnesses is disbelieved does not mean that the courts were bound to reject all of it - (C) IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Murder - Validity of conviction when enmity established, evidences of eye-witnesses corroborated by material evidence - Only minor discrepancies in evidence of investigation officer - Court sustained the conviction of accused - Appeal dismissed.
