Commissioner of Sales Tax, U. P v Lal Kunwa Stone Crusher Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 211; (2000) 3 SCC 525; AIR 2000 SC 1161; 2000 ALJ 868; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 279; JT 2000 (3) SC 204; 2000(2) SCALE 363; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 276; 2000 (3) SLT 66; 2000 (118) STC 287; 2000 (2) Supreme 369

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 2(e - 1) - 'Manufacture' - Stone boulders - Crushing into small stones, dust, gitti - Dealer paying sales tax at time of purchasing stone boulders - Held, not chemically and physically different - Commercial good without change of their identity merely after some process or finishing remain commercially same goods - Cannot be taxed again in a series of sales.
