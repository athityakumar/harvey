Umesh Singh and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 496; (2000) 6 SCC 89; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1026; AIR 2000 SC 2111; 2000 (2) CCR 191; 2000 (3) Crimes 89; 2000 CRLJ 3167; 2000 (2) JCC 468; JT 2000 (6) SC 508; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 14; 2000(4) SCALE 511; 2000 (4) SLT 555; 2000 (5) Supreme 92

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 149 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - All armed with lathis and guns - Taking away paddy from threshing floor - Resistance - Blows and gun shot injuries to deceased - One member of assembly aged 13 years - Common trail - No objection raised during trial - Sentence - Validity - Held, arming with deadly weapons by all indicates their intention - Conviction, no interference - Common trial against child is illegal - Conviction confirmed, however sentence set aside.
