Tarun Prasad Chatterjee v Dinanath Sharma

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2815; (2000) 8 SCC 649; AIR 2001 SC 36; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 522; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 45; 2000(6) SCALE 729; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 634; 2000 (7) SLT 418; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 634; 2000 (7) Supreme 85

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 81(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, r. 11 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 9 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Representation of People (Conduct of Elections and Election Petitions) Rules, 1951 - Whether in computing period of limitation, date of election of returned candidate should be excluded or not? - First day for period of limitation is required to be excluded for convenience of parties and if declaration of result is delayed or is done late in night, candidate or elector would hardly get any time for presentation of election petition - Law comes to rescue to give full forty-five days' period for filing election petition - Any petition presented on date of election of returned candidate would be certainly within period of limitation as it is a presentation on date of election of returned candidate - Held, date of election of returned candidate being 28-11-1998, election petition filed on 12-01-1999 on exclusion of first day from computing period of limitation, was in time and learned single judge rightly dismissed petition filed by appellant - Appeal is without any merits - Appeal dismissed.
