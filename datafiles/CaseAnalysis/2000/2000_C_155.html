Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs, Bhubaneswar, Orissa

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 671; (2000) 3 SCC 472; AIR 2000 SC 1045; 2000 (1) CLC 824; 2000 (89) ECR 1; 2000 (116) E.L.T. 422; JT 2000 (2) SC 155; 2000 (37) RLT 239; 2000(1) SCALE 591; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 876; 2000 (2) SLT 273

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 12, 14, 112, 130E, 156 -

Customs Tariff Act, 1985 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Customs Valuation Rules, 1988, rr .4, 9(1)(e), 9(1)(b) - Project Imports Regulations,1986 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988 - Imports - Excise duty & penalty - Challenged - Appellant imported certain equipments, drawings and engineering documents from Portugal - 3 contracts were were executed for supply of technical documentation ( MD 301) ,for sale of equipments of blast furnace and materials ( MD 302) and umbrella contract covering two agreements - Consignment of MD 301 and first consignment of MD 302 had arrived and cleared by Customs House - After second consignment of MD 302 had arrived and bill of entry filed Department found that contract MD 302 was sub contract and issued show cause notice to appellant - Respondent permitted clearance of goods upon furnishing bank guarantee, extra duty and admitted customs duty - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petition and was allowed by HC directing release of goods subject to furnishing bank guarantee which was complied by appellant and got goods cleared - Appellant filed reply and personal hearing was given by respondent - Respondent issued second show cause notice to appellant and two of its officers and appellants engineering consultant and replies were filed and passed Orders assessing levy of customs duty with penalty - Aggrieved appellant and others filed appeals before Appellate Tribunal and were disposed of by common order holding that three contracts entered into between the seller and appellant were in fact parts of one package and three constituted one composite agreement applying provisions of r.4 and 9(1)(e) of the 1988 Rules and that duty should be confined as found by adjudicating officer and reduced penalty to 4 crores and set aside penalties on other notices - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether Order of Appellate Tribunal justified - Held, Tribunals interpretation of applicability of r. 9(1)(e) of the 1988 Rules was not justified as price paid by appellant for drawings and technical documents forming subject matter of contract DM 301 could by no stretch of imagination fall within meaning of 'an obligation of seller' to third party and there was also no payment made as condition of sale of imported goods - Interpretative notes of r. 4 relied on by Tribunal had been couched in negative form and could not be so read as to mean that those charges which were not covered in cls. (a) to (c) were available to be included in value of imported goods - Appellate Tribunal failed to consider important aspect of undervaluation of blast furnace equipment covered by contract MD 302 which was subject matter of show cause notice and relied by Revenue - Matter remitted back to Tribunal for fresh adjudication to address important aspect of under valuation of blast furnace with necessary directions - Order accordingly.
