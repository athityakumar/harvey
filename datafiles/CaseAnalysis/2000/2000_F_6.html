Gadnis Bhawani Shankar, V v Faleiro Eduardo Martinho

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 575; (2000) 7 SCC 472; AIR 2000 SC 2502; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 5; JT 2000 (8) SC 461; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 14; 2000(5) SCALE 436; 2000 (5) SLT 810; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 77; 2000 (5) Supreme 353

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 82(b) - Election petition - Whether, after withdrawal of his candidature on allegation of corrupt practice, as a voter - S. 82(b) is attracted - Is it necessary to implead him as party respondent u/ s. 82(b)? - Held, s. 82(b) gets attracted to a candidate who allegedly commits a corrupt practice, after withdrawal of his candidature as a voter - Allegations made are allegations of corrupt practice against a nominated candidate - Necessary to implead him as a party - respondent u/s. 82(b) irrespective of fact of withdrawal of candidature and commission of corrupt practice.
