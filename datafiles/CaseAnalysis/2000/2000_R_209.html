Noor Niwas Nursery Public School v Regional Provident Fund Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2943; (2001) 1 SCC 1; (2001) SCC (L&S) 115; AIR 2001 SC 277; 2001 (1) CLR 598; 2000 (88) DLT 841; 2001 (88) FLR 533; JT 2001 (1) SC 157; 2001 (34) LabIC 323; 2001 (1) LLJ 446; 2001 (3) LLN 823; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 22; 2000(8) SCALE 205; 2001 (2) SLJ 199; 2000 (7) SLR 4; 2000 (8) SLT 767; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 478; 2000 (8) Supreme 245; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 647

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Employees provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 2A, 17 - Registrations of Societies Act, 1860 - Establishment of Schools - Applicability of the Act - Entitlement - Appellant/institution was registered Society under the 1860 Act - Said Society runs two schools - Appellant-school's claim was that Francis Girls Higher Secondary School and appellant-school were two different institutions having separate and independent accounts and were managed by two different Managing Committees - Appellant had four employees and it being separate establishment was not covered by provisions of the 1952, Act - Therefore, it was contended that Francis Girls Higher Secondary School and the appellant-school could not be treated as one establishment for purpose of the 1952 Act - Respondent/Provident Fund Commissioner on material held that two institutions constitute one and same establishment and, therefore, order was passed stating that same was covered by the Act - Aggrieved appellant challenged said order before HC which affirmed order of respondent/ Provident Fund Commissioner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether two units were one or distinct had to be considered in light of provisions of s. 2A of the 1952 Act - Held, in instant case, when two units were located adjacent to one another and there were only two teachers it was difficult to believe that Society which runs 30 schools would run separate school consisting of such small number of staff - If unit of appellant/school was not part of unit of Francis Girls Higher Secondary School, Head Clerk, had not been in possession of particulars of appellant/school and could not have furnished such particulars to Inspector when he visited school in connection with grant of code number - Undisputably, two units were run by same Society and had more than 20 employees - Therefore, exemption granted u/s. 17 of the 1952 Act was subject to condition that such exclusion would not apply to appellants unit - When entire establishment was covered by the Act only part of establishment was excluded and condition of exclusion being applicable only to part - Court failed to understand as to how appellant could rely upon said letter to claim non- applicability of the1952 Act on ground that it falls short number of employees - Hence, Court did not find any good reason to interfere with order made by HC affirming view taken by respondent/Provident Fund Commissioner - Appeal dismissed.
