Harnam Singh and Others v Punjab State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2985; (2001) 10 SCC 526; 2001 (88) FLR 282; 2000 (2) LLJ 1413; 2000 (7) SLT 275

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Instant appeal arose out of an order made by the HC quashing the reference made to the Labour Court/Industrial Tribunal in exercise of the powers u/s. 10 of the Act.

Held, HC should have proceeded to examine the original records of the Conciliation Officer and that of the Government to arrive at a decision to find that, when the petitioners have disputed the claim that they were working under them, the Govt. ought to have made a reference to that question. Apart from that, the Govt. has completely ignored the fact that the contesting respondents had invoked the provisions of the Act more than 12 years after the alleged termination of service, when they have not given any explanation whatever, for the inordinate delay. When there is unexplained delay, the Govt. ought to have taken that as a relevant factor. When the Govt. has failed to do so, it is certainly open to exercise jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution. HC though notices that the proceeding arises u/s. 10 of the Act and making of a reference thereto is administrative in character still has examined this matter as if sitting in appeal on the reference made. In fact the scope of the investigation in such a matter is very limited and in this case the two issues could not have been decided except by adjudication before the concerned Competent Authority. In the result SC set aside the order made by the HC and direct the concerned Labour Court/Industrial Tribunal to adjudicate the reference as ordered by the Govt. and while doing so, it shall examine the two questions referred to by us earlier in the course of this order. Appeal allowed.
