Sandeep Singh v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 961; (2002) 10 SCC 549; (2003) SCC (L&S) 800; 2005 (4) SLR 8

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appellants applied for post in Haryana Civil Services for which advertisement had been issued by Public Service Commission - Written examination was held but for some contingencies interviews could not be held, immediately thereafter - Between date of advertisement and till date of holding interview, several other posts in services fell vacant - Commission however, ultimately gave list of those number of vacancies for which initial requisition had been made - Challenged - Held, vacancies available in any particular service till date of interview at least should be filled up from very same examination unless there is any statutory embargo for same - In present case, no statutory embargo has been pointed out - Judgment in 1999 Indlaw SC 1307 should apply to facts and circumstances - Appellants who appeared in competitive examination to be considered for appointment - Appellant entitled to benefit prospectively from date of appointment - Appeals allowed.
