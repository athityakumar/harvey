Hari Singh Mann v Harbhajan Singh Bajwa and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2803; (2001) SCC (Cr) 113; (2001) 1 SCC 169; AIR 2001 SC 43; AIR 2000 SCW 3848; 2000 (4) CCR 188; 2001 (1) CGLJ 150; 2000 (4) Crimes 189; 2001 CRLJ 128; 2001 (1) JCC 53; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 394; 2000 (3) KLT 935; 2001 (1) MahLJ 465; 2001 (127) PLR 150; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 650; 2000(7) SCALE 289; 2000 (7) SLT 693; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 313; 2000 (7) Supreme 401; 2001 (1) UC 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 362 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 182 - Power of Review of an order by the High Court - Whether such power contemplated by CrPC? - Held, HC disposed of a petition-after disposal of main petition, there was no lis pending - Respondent therefore could not have filed any miscellaneous petition - The filing of a miscellaneous petition not referable to any provision of CrPC or rules of court, cannot be resorted to as a substitute of fresh litigation - Directions in case filed by respondents were issued apparently without notice to any of respondents in petition - Merely because Respondent 1 was an Advocate, did not justify issuance of directions at his request without notice of other side - There is no provision in CrPC authorising HC to review its judgment passed either in exercise of its appellate or revisional or original criminal jurisdiction - Such a power cannot be exercised with aid or under cloak of s. 482 of Code - Impugned orders set aside - Appeal allowed.
