New India Assurance Company Limited v Shiv Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2931; (2000) 9 SCC 227; (2000) SCC (L&S) 899; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 2; 2000 (2) AWC 1583; 2000 (85) FLR 595; JT 2000 (4) SC 235; 2000 (1) LLJ 1395; 2000 (2) LLN 952; 2000 (5) Supreme 137; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 1966

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Insurance - Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, s. 4 - A(3) - Compensation - Injury - Falling from insured vehicle - Insurance company liable to pay compensation - Liability to pay interest - Whether Workmen's Compensation Commr. could have held insurance company liable for payment of amount of penalty u/s. 4 - A(3)? - Held, question was considered by Supreme Court in Ved Prakash Garg v Premi Devi and Ors (1998-I-LLJ-363) wherein it was held so far as additional amount of compensation by way of penalty imposed on insured employer by Workmen's Compensation Commr. u/s. 4 - A(3)(b) is concerned, however, insurance company would not remain liable to reimburse said claim and it would be liability of insured employer alone - Further held, appellant - insurance company is not liable to pay amount of penalty as determined by Workmen's Compensation Commr. - Appeal allowed.
