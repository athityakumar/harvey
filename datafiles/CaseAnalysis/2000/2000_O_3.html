Union Bank of India v Official Liquidator H. C. of Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 304; (2000) 5 SCC 274; AIR 2000 SC 3642; 2000 (1) CLC 2176; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 317; 2000 (3) CompLJ 183; JT 2000 (7) SC 86; 2000(4) SCALE 551; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act (SICA), 1985 - Companies Act, s. 446 - Whether the Learned Single Judge has rightly confirmed the auction-sale of the property of M/s. Kolay Biscuits Company Pvt. Ltd. which is the Company under liquidation? - It is the duty of the court to disclose the valuation report to the secured creditors, interested persons and to verify whether the report indicates reasonable market value of the property to be auctioned - Court should verify the statement that sale of the Company on "as-is-where-is basis" will affect 1200 workers and for that proper notice was required to be issued to the secured creditors for whose benefit the property was to be auctioned - There was no material on record for holding that the Company could be revived and the employees would be reinstated in service by giving them re-employment - Held, the Official Liquidator is directed to recover the possession of the property sold and to refund the amount deposited by respondent 2 auction-purchaser - Also directed to resell the property after obtaining fresh valuation report from other reliable expert and after giving a copy of the said valuation report to secured creditors - In the notice for sale reserved price be fixed and due advertisement be published in newspapers having circulation in commercial cities including Delhi, Mumbai and Chennai on the basis of the directions which may be issued by the High Court - The appeal stands disposed of accordingly - The impugned order confirmed in appeal - Appeal is allowed.
