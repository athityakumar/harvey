Tata Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited v Jitendra Prasad Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2922; (2001) 10 SCC 530; 2000 (4) AWC 3327; 2000 (3) CLR 853; 2000 (87) FLR 681; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 222; 2001 (1) LLJ 595; 2010(4) SCALE 483; 2000 (7) SLT 280; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 67

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Certified Standing Orders, O. 24(XIV) - Reinstatement - Whether High Court, in holding that misconduct, attributed to the workmen there is causal connection between misconduct and employment of the workman is not of much significance when such acts have taken place within premises of the factory? - Relied on Glaxo Laboratories v. The Presiding Officer, Labour Court, Meerut and others 1983 Indlaw SC 262 - Held, identical charges were found and would be denial of justice to appellant if alone is singled out for punishment by way of dismissal from service - .
