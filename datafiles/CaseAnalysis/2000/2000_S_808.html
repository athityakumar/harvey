Balbir Kaur and Another v Steel Authority of India Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 323; (2000) 6 SCC 493; (2000) SCC (L&S) 767; AIR 2000 SC 1596; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 72; 2000 (2) CLR 631; JT 2000 (7) SC 135; 2000 LabIC 1900; 2000 (2) LLJ 1; 2000(4) SCALE 670; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1053; 2000 (4) SLR 334; 2000 (4) SLR 567; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2055

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Payment Of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Benefit of compassionate appointment was being taken away as there was an introduction of a new scheme, assuring regular monthly income to disabled employee or dependants of deceased employee - Held, compassionate appointment cannot be negatived on ground of introduction of new scheme as act did not allow taking away of a previous benefit given - Also, terms of scheme compelling deposit of Gratuity and Provident Fund amounts with employer violated s. 4 of Gratuity Act and Provident Fund Act.
