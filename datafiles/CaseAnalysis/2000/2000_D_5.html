Om Prakash Jaiswal v D. K. Mittal and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 163; (2000) 3 SCC 171; AIR 2000 SC 1136; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 69; 2000 ALJ 720; 2000 (2) AWC 1284; 2000 (2) CLT 280; 2000 CRLJ 1700; JT 2000 (2) SC 293; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 104; 2000(2) SCALE 28; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1064; 2000 (2) SLT 363; 2000 (1) Supreme 574

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 20 - (A) Limitation for action for contempt - Held, expression 'initiate any proceedings for contempt' connotes that proceedings are said to be initiated when notice is issued to show as to why contemnor should not be punished and not otherwise - Jurisdiction to initiate proceedings in contempt as also jurisdiction to punish for contempt in spite of a case of contempt having been made out are both discretionary with court - (B) Whether there is a right of filing appeal against refusal to initiate contempt proceedings? - Further held, that an informant does not have a right of filing an appeal u/s. 19 of Act against an order refusing to initiate contempt proceedings or disposing application or petition filed for initiating such proceedings and thus cannot be called an aggrieved party - Appeal allowed.
