Nazira Begum Lashkar and Others v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2794; (2001) 1 SCC 143; (2001) SCC (L&S) 167; AIR 2001 SC 102; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 417; 2001 LabIC 42; 2000(7) SCALE 336B; 2001 (2) SLJ 328; 2000 (5) SLR 777; 2000 (8) SLT 155; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 421; 2000 (7) Supreme 588

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Assam Elementary Education (Provincialisation) Act, 1974

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 136 - Assam Elementary Education (Provincialisation) Rules, 1977, r. 3 - Assam Elementary Education (Provincialisation) Act, 1974, s. 27 - Recruitment - Constitutionality of rejection of appointment as assistant teacher of primary school by State Govt. without issuing notice challenged - Violation of Assam Elementary Education (Provincialisation) Act - Appointments made in favour of appointees indicated clearly that appointment is purely temporary and can be terminated without any notice - Held, before cancellation of appointments, Govt. did issue notice to appointees and it is only after that, termination orders had been issued - Question of issuing any direction for adjustment of equities in favour of appellants would not arise - Appeal dismissed.
