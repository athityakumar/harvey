Jamnalal and Others v Radheshyam

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 274; (2000) 4 SCC 380; JT 2000 (5) SC 1; 2000(4) M.P.H.T. 218; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 462; 2000(3) SCALE 355; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 135; 2000 (4) SLT 429; 2000 (3) Supreme 372

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12 and 13 - Whether High Court was right in holding that as tenant disputed being in arrears of rent and no provisional rent was determined by Trial Court, operation of whole of s. 13(1) was arrested as such tenant could not be met with penal consequences of eviction decree denying him benefit of s. 12(3)? - Held, u/s. 13(1) twin obligations on tenant against whom a suit or proceeding is instituted on any of grounds mentioned in s. 12(1) - Firstly to pay arrears of rent and to pay rent payable after demand notice and secondly to deposit thereafter future rent payable month to month - Two obligations are independent of each other - Compliance with second does not depend upon fulfillment of first obligation - S. 13(1) becomes inoperative only when there is dispute as to rent payable which is sought to be resolved by s. 13(2) - In the present case, there was no dispute regarding rent payable - So operation of s. 13(1) ought not to be arrested - Tenant is not saved from penal consequences of eviction decree denying him benefit of s. 12(3) - Impugned order set aside - Eviction petition allowed - Appeal allowed.
