Collector of Central Excise, Kanpur v Flock (India) Private Limited, C-7, Panki

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 261; (2000) 6 SCC 650; AIR 2000 SC 2484; 2000 (92) ECR 1; 2000 (120) E.L.T. 285; JT 2000 (8) SC 524; 2000 (40) RLT 131; 2000(5) SCALE 445; 2000 (6) SLT 52; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 156; 2000 (5) Supreme 432

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Refund Of Duty

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Respondent was a manufacturer of jute hessian flocked with nylon flocks, filed a classification list in which it was claimed that the said product comes under Tariff Item 22-A - Asst. Collector held the product in question in classifiable under Tariff Item 22-B and not under Tariff Item 22-A and the applicable rate of duty would be 25 % ad valorem - Asst. Collector also expressly stated in the said order that the assessee may prefer an appeal against his order to the Collector (Appeals) - Assessee neither challenged the said order nor did it pay the duty under protest - Subsequently, respondent filed an application claiming refund of the duty paid alleging that the products in question were wrongly classified under Tariff Item 22-B, instead they ought to have been classified under Tariff Item 22-A and that the differential duty should be refunded - Asst. Collector dismissed the claim for refund on the ground that the earlier order of Asst. Collector had attained finality, and therefore, the claim for refund was not maintainable - Collector allowed the appeal filed against said order and remanded the matter to the Asst. Collector with a direction to reconsider the matter on merits including the question whether the goods were classifiable under Tariff Item 22-A or 22-B - Tribunal dismissed appeal filed against order of the Collector - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the Collector as confirmed by the Tribunal could be upheld? - Held, if an order which is appealable under the Act is not challenged then the order is not liable to be questioned and the matter is not to be reopened in a proceeding for refund which, if we may term it so, is in the nature of execution of a decree/order - In the present case, it was specifically mentioned in the order of the Assistant Collector that the assessee may file appeal against the order before the Collector (Appeals) if so advised - Impugned order of the tribunal set aside - Appeal allowed.
