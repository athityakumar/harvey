Pankaj Mehra and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 141; (2000) SCC (Cr) 556; (2000) 2 SCC 756; AIR 2000 SC 1953; 2000 (41) ALR 344; 2000 (1) BC 281; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 321; 2000 (1) CCR 213; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 417; 2000 (1) Crimes 282; 2000 CRLJ 1781; 2000 (1) JCC 178; JT 2000 (2) SC 113; 2000 (2) KLT 338; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 602; 2000(1) SCALE 551; 2000 (2) SCJ 188; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 825; 2000 (2) SLT 168; 2000 (2) Supreme 88

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 6, Companies Act, 1956, s. 536(2) - Dishonour of cheque - Prosecution of Company - Fact that petition for winding up was presented prior to issuance of notice by payee to pay cheque amount - Held, petition for winding up would not extricate it from offence - Word 'void' in s. 536 - Does not mean ab initio void - Winding up proceedings do not make company's debt unenforceable - Disposition of property would take place only after payment is made by banker pursuant thereto.
