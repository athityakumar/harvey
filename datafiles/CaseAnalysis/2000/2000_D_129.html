T. Vijayan and Others v Divisional Railway Manager and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 321; (2000) 4 SCC 20; (2000) SCC (L&S) 444; AIR 2000 SC 1766; 2000 (2) AWC 1589; JT 2000 (4) SC 196; 2000 (33) LabIC 1473; 2000 (2) LLN 965; 2000(3) SCALE 78; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 971; 2000 (3) SLJ 325; 2000 (4) SLR 663; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1565

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Railway Establishment Manual, paras 302 and 216 - Whether question of seniority between direct recruits and promotees would include ad-hoc promotion of promotees? - Held, yes - Ad hoc promotion is permissible pending regular selection - Once ad hoc promotion is found to be permissible under Rules and respondents were promoted on ad hoc basis in exigencies of service, pending regular selection, which, incidentally, took sufficient time as respondents who were on official duty 'online' were not available at one point or at one time to facilitate selection, entire period of ad hoc service will have to be counted towards their seniority, particularly as all respondents were duly selected and their services were also regularised - Appellants were selected in 1988 and were put on two years' training as Apprentices whereafter they were absorbed and were issued separate and individual appointment letters in which, it was clearly mentioned that their seniority was subject to finalisation of selection for promotion which was in progress - Appellants, in this situation, cannot claim seniority over Respondents who had already been appointed on ad hoc basis and were after due selection regularised on those posts - Appeals dismissed.
