Dai-Ichi Karkaria Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 515; (2000) 4 SCC 57; AIR 2000 SC 1741; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 835; 2000 (90) ECR 429; 2000 (119) E.L.T. 516; JT 2000 (4) SC 495; 2000 (38) RLT 787; 2000(3) SCALE 279; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1254; 2000 (5) SLT 101; 2000 (3) Supreme 535

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Notification for exemption - Public interest - Notification to be in force till 10-09-1987 - Subsequent Notification taking out such exemption from 30-12- 1986 till 10-09-1987 and imposing 25% customs duty on ground that such exemption will be misused by private contractors - Petitioners obliged to import goods only for supply to Oil and Natural Gas Commission - License to supply goods to ONGC only - Held, petitioners could not misuse exemption - Notification taking out exemption - Invalid - It was not clear as to why duty was reduced to only 25% by subsequent notification taking out exemption.
