Commissioner of Income Tax v Nagpur Hotel Owners' Association

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 725; (2001) 2 SCC 128; AIR 2001 SC 294; 2001 (165) CTR 1; [2001] 247 ITR 201; JT 2001 (1) SC 113; 2000(8) SCALE 250; 2000 (8) Supreme 275; [2001] 114 TAXMAN 255

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Income From Assessment

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 11(2) - Income Tax Rules, r. 17- Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Assessment Proceedings - Benefit/Exemption - Entitlement - Respondent/assessee filed return and claimed exemption u/s. 11 of the 1961, Act on ground of charitable purpose - Income Tax Officer refused claim on ground that it was not duly registered with appellant/commissioner of Income Tax u/s. 12(a) of the 1961, Act and no notice of accumulation of income as required u/s. 11(2) of the 1961 Act was given - Respondent filed appeal with Appellate Authority, which confirmed order of assessing authority - Tribunal on second appeal held that respondent/assesses objects were charitable and relief could not been refused- Appellant filed appeal before HC, which held that prescription of limitation was illegal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that date of completion of assessment for a/ys 1974-75 and 1975-76, respondent Association did not furnish required information - Hence, said requirement not having been fulfilled for a/ys concerned, Association was not entitled to claim benefit u/s. 11(2)of the 1961 Act for 3 a/ys - Whether order of HC, answering in favour of respondent was justifiable - Held, it was abundantly clear from wordings of s. 11(2) that it was mandatory for person claiming benefit of s. 11 to intimate to assessing authority particulars required, u/r. 17 in Form No.10 of the 1961, Act - If during assessment proceedings AO did not had necessary information, question of excluding such income from assessment did not arise at all - Therefore, it was necessary that assessing authority should had this information at time he completes assessment - In absence of any such information, it would not be possible for assessing authority to give assessee benefit of such exclusion and once assessment was so completed, it would be futile to find fault with assessing authority for having included such income in assessable income of assessee - Further, any claim for giving benefit of s. 11 on basis of information supplied subsequent to completion of assessment would mean that assessment order would had to be reopened - Thus, Act did not contemplate such reopening of assessment - In instant case it was evident from records of case that respondent did not furnish required information till after assessments for relevant years were completed - Thus, HC erred in answering in favour of respondent-assessee - Hence, order of HC was reversed- Appeals allowed.
