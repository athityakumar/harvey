State of Punjab v Vinod Kumar

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3106; (2000) 9 SCC 742; (2001) SCC (Cr) 695; 2001 CRLJ 4938; JT 2000 (7) SC 510

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 364 - Prosecution - Sanction for - (A) Whether sanction is required for prosecuting an offence u/s. 364? - Held, single judge would not have granted a direction more than that though the phraseology used by him is liable to be interpreted in the way the State Govt. entertained apprehension about it - In this case, whether it is open to the High Court to direct State Govt. to grant sanction u/s. 197 of the Code in any given situation - (B) Grant of interim compensation - Held, not express any thing regarding interim compensation awarded as single judge himself made it very clear that such direction was without prejudice to right of any claimant to claim compensation against state or any person who is found responsible for it, in manner provided by law - Question of deciding grant of compensation would arise only after Trial Court enters upon a finding regarding liability to be fixed on delinquent officers - Make it clear that have not in any manner inhibited rights of parties to address arguments at stage of framing charge either for discharge or for framing charge etc - Appeal disposed of.
