New India Assurance Company Limited v R. Srinivasan

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 183; (2000) 3 SCC 242; AIR 2000 SC 941; AIR 2000 SCW 703; 2000 (39) ALR 321; 2000 (2) AWC 1296; 2000 (2) CLT 30; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 522; 2000 CPJ(Sc) 19; JT 2000 (2) SC 475; 2000 (2) KLT 462; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 125; 2000(2) SCALE 131; 2000 (2) SCJ 97; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1228; 2000 (2) SLT 520; 2000 (2) Supreme 70

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Consumer Protection Rules, 1988

Summary: Consumer Protection - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 9, r. 9(1) - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Tamil Nadu Consumer Protection Rules, 1988, rr. 4(8) and 8(8) - Respondent owner of vehicle filed complaint against appellant in respect of damage caused to his vehicle, which was insured with appellant - Claim for damages dismissed in default - Filed a fresh complaint - Whether in view of dismissal of first complaint filed by respondent, a second complaint on same facts and cause of action would not lie and it ought to have been dismissed as not maintainable? - Rules do not provide that if a complaint is dismissed in default by district forum u/r. 4(8) or by State Commission u/r. 8(8) of Rules, a second complaint would not lie - Rule of prohibition contained in O. 9, r.9(1) CPC, 1908 cannot be extended to proceedings before District Forum or State Commission - Fact that case was not decided on merits and was dismissed in default of non - appearance of complainant cannot be overlooked and, therefore, it would be permissible to file second complaint explaining why earlier complaint could not be pursued and was dismissed in default - Court would have inherent power and jurisdiction to restore complaint on good cause being shown for non - appearance of complainant - Appeal dismissed.
