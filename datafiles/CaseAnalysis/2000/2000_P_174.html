National Fertilizers v Puran Chand Nangia

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 27; (2000) 8 SCC 343; AIR 2001 SC 53; 2001 (44) ALR 709; 2000 (3) ARBLR 461; 2000 (1) CLC 1976; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 591; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 153; 2000 (3) RAJ 237; 2000(7) SCALE 174; 2000 (7) SLT 514; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 26; 2000 (7) Supreme 238

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - (A) Whether Arbitrator could have awarded at a flat rate of 50% of extra claim or at 50% of difference between market prices and contract rates? - Held, merely because increase was at a flat rate - No fault with award - (B) Whether Arbitrator clutched at jurisdiction he did not have, by an unreasonable construction of clause '+/- 25%' for purposes of escalation? - Further held, above said clause '+/- 25%' was understood by Arbitrator in a reasonable manner as being applicable to a case where value of sum total of additions and deletions exceeded 25% of contract price - That construction cannot be said to be vitiated by any serious error of law - Appeal dismissed.
