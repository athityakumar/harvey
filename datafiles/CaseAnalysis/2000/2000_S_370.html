Bishwanath Prasad Singh v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2706; (2001) 2 SCC 305; (2001) SCC (L&S) 403; 2001 (2) CLR 281; 2001 (90) FLR 310; JT 2001 (1) SC 161; 2001 (3) LLN 14; 2000(8) SCALE 437; 2001 (1) SCJ 343; 2001 (2) SLJ 339; 2001 (1) SLR 637; 2001 (1) SLT 183; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 718; 2000 (8) Supreme 585

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Orissa Service Code - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(j) - Whether petitioner being a judicial officer could be retired at age of 58 years when same was raised to 60 by Supreme Court and whether High Court was a competent authority to pass order of retirement? - Held, by force of judgment a judicial officer does not acquire a right to continue in service up to extended age of 60 years - It is a benefit subject to an evaluation as to continued utility of a judicial officer, carried out by respective HC before attaining age of 58 years - If HC finds a judicial officer not entitled to benefit of extension he would retire at age of superannuation appointed by service rules - No specific order or communication in that regard is called for either by HC or by Governor of State - Where HC chooses to make communication it would be better advised not to use therein expression 'compulsory retirement' as it creates confusion - Petition dismissed.
