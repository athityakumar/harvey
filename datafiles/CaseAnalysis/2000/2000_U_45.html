A. Sowkath Ali v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 573; (2000) 7 SCC 148; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1304; AIR 2000 SC 2662; AIR 2000 SCW 2828; 2000 (3) CCR 95; 2000 CRLJ 3961; JT 2000 (8) SC 385; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 583; 2000(5) SCALE 372; 2000 (5) SLT 747; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 48; 2000 (5) Supreme 318

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - COFEPOSA Act, s. 3(1) - National Security Act, 1980 - Foreign Exchange - Smmugling - Detention order - (A) Effectof suppression of valid document - Only confessional statements of co-accused placed but his retractions, not placed before detaining authority - Plea that same detaining authority having passed detention order against said co-accused, well aware of retractions - (B) Composite grounds - Only paragraphs narrating facts with details of documents relied on - Question of severability of grounds as provided in s. 5A of the Act - Held, detention illegal - Does not arise as s. 5A applies where detention is based on more than one ground, moreso, when question of severability u/s. 5A was not raised by State in any of counter-affidavit - Petition allowed. .
