A. Abdul Rashid Khan (Dead) and Others v P. A. K. A. Shahul Hamid and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3930; (2000) 10 SCC 636; 2000 (3) AWC 2476; JT 2000 (7) SC 483; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 147; 2001 (129) PLR 324; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 824B; 2001 (2) UC 206

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Indian Evidence Act, s. 92 - (A) Whether on facts and circumstances of this case High Court is right in decreeing suit for specific performance to extent of share of appellants? - Held, yes - Even where any property is held jointly, and once any party to contract has agreed to sell such joint property by agreement, then, even if other co-sharer has not joined, at least to extent of his share, he is bound to execute sale deed - Suit for specific performance to extent of this 5/6th share was rightly decreed by HC - (B) Whether in a suit for specific performance where alternative plea of partition is also raised, HC is right to decree suit for partition? - Further held, how could there be any partition in property, without other co-sharers joining, who are not part of disputed agreement? - When sale deed itself has yet to be executed, his right in property has not yet matured, he cannot claim partition and possession over it - HC was not right in decreeing this alternative prayer of partition - Appeal partly allowed.
