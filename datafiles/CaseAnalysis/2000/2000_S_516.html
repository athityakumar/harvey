T. Thomson v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3726; (2002) 9 SCC 618; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 204; 2000 (3) KLT 612

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Appeals disposed of.

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21 and 25 - (A) Whether evidence required in support of voluntary nature of statements? - Held, record regarding movement of appellants have not been produced in court - (B) Whether there was written authorisation to conduct search in house? - Held, evidence tendered after cross - examination of witness and statement were made voluntarily - There is no statutory requirement that such a record should be produced in court as a matter of course - S. 41(2) requires such authorisation only if officer of gazetted rank (specified in sub-section) wants any other officer subordinate to him to conduct search and if he himself is to conduct search or raid it is unnecessary for him to get any other authorisation from another gazetted officer - Appeal allowed.
