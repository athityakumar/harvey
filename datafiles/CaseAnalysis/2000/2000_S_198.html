Jai Prakash Khadria v Shyam Sunder Agarwalla and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 528; (2000) 6 SCC 598; AIR 2000 SC 2172; 2000 (40) ALR 209; JT 2000 (6) SC 548; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 143; 2000(4) SCALE 584; 2000 (4) SLT 586

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, s. 17 - I - Order granting custody of children final - Maternal and paternal grandfather both claiming custody of their grandchild - Both parties affluent and having love and affection towards child - Evidence shows that paternal grandfather is very attached and has changed his will to bequeath property to grandchild - While on other hand evidence showed that during his stay with maternal grandfather, child had to live all alone with servants - Held, order granting custody of children is not final but are interlocutory in nature and subject to change at any future time upon proof of change of circumstances requiring change of custody but such change in custody must be proved to be in paramount interest of child - Order granting custody to paternal grandfather cannot be faulted - Order made final for 3 years.
