M. Narsinga Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3126; (2001) SCC (Cr) 258; (2001) 1 SCC 691; AIR 2001 SC 318; AIR 2000 SCW 4427; 2001 (42) ALR 738; 2001 (1) CCR 82; 2001 (1) Crimes 79; 2001 CRLJ 515; 2001 (1) JCC 118; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 559; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 95; 2000(8) SCALE 303; 2001 (1) SCJ 300; 2001 (1) SLT 116; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 584; 2000 (8) Supreme 498

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164 and 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 4(1), 7, 13(1)(d), 13(2) and 20 - (A) Whether can a legal presumption be based on a factual presumption? - Presumptions falling under former category are compendiously known as 'factual presumptions' or 'discretionary presumptions' and those falling under latter as 'legal presumptions' or 'compulsory presumptions' - Expression 'shall be presumed' as employed in s. 20(1) of Act, it must have same import of compulsion - Presumption in Law of Evidence is a rule indicating stage of shifting burden of proof - (B) Whether fact that accused had obtained money, presumption under s. 4(1) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 will be attracted or not? - Held, presumption under s. 4(1) of Prevention of Corruption Act is immediately attracted - Presumption is rebuttable - Presumption is not final conclusion to be drawn from other facts - But it will be final if it remains undisturbed - It has to be presumed that accused accepted gratification as a motive or reward for doing or forbearing to do any official act, if condition envisaged in former part of section is satisfied - (C) Whether it is possible to draw any presumption against delinquent public servant u/s. 20 in absence of direct evidence to show that public servant demanded bribery and that same was paid to him? - Further held, legal presumption u/s. 20 is that during trial it should be proved that accused has accepted or agreed to accept any gratification - Direct evidence is one of modes through which a fact can be proved - But that is not only mode envisaged in Evidence Act - Standard required for reaching supposition is that of a prudent man acting in any important matter concerning him - Appeal dismissed.
