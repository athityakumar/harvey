Kantilal Hirji Shah v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3153; (2000) 7 SCC 606; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1427; 2002 CRLJ 261; JT 2000 (7) SC 478; 2000 (7) SLT 488; 2000 (6) Supreme 605

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - FEMA - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Petitioner detained under COFEPOSA Act - Challenged - Representation made to Central Govt. as well as to State Govt. have not been disposed of within a reasonable dispatch - Whether constitutional right of detenu u/art. 22(5) of Constitution has been infringed? - A detenu u/art. 22(5) has a right that his representation should be considered by appropriate authority as expeditiously as possible and there should not be unexplained delay in matter of disposal of representation - When a representation is received in department of concerned authority it is not necessary for authority to whom representation is made, himself to make entry in diary and immediately deal with matter without taking assistance of any other subordinate officers - Subordinate officer calling for comments from any other authority does not, in fact, deal with representation nor does it express any view on representation and acts clerically only to get necessary comments for being considered by persons on whom power to dispose of representation vest - Held, fact that on receipt of representation, Joint Secy. of department called for comments of sponsoring authority immediately and on receipt of same had forwarded to higher authority which was dealt with appropriate authority, would not constitute any infringement of constitutional right of detenu u/art. 22(5) nor it can be said that representation dealt with mechanically without application of mind - Representation dealt with utmost expedition - No delay in matter of disposal of representation - Petition dismissed.
