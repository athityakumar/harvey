Siv Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 491; (2000) 3 SCC 367; AIR 2000 SC 1153; 2000 (89) ECR 209; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 281; JT 2000 (3) SC 133; 2000 (37) RLT 583; 2000(2) SCALE 304; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 231

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3(1) - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether CEGAT had erred in directing that duty of central excise was payable u/s. 3(1) of Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944? - Chapter V-A of Central Excise Rules would not be applicable where Export - Oriented Unit Scheme (EOU) is outside EOU Scheme after unit is debonded - U/r. 100-H, r. 57 - A and other Rules mentioned therein shall not apply to excisable goods produced or manufactured by a 100% export - oriented undertaking - Held, Tribunal was not right in holding that duty is to be leviable in terms of proviso to s. 3(1) of Central Excise Act, 1944 - Appeal allowed.
