Balawwa and Another v Hasanabi and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4047; (2000) 9 SCC 272; JT 2000 (3) SC 600; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 621B; 2008(14) SCALE 552

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, s. 48A - Suit for partition - Tenant claim for title - Order of Tribunal - Civil Court's jurisdiction - Maintainability - Suit for partition was filed - Defendants 1 and 3, who were the widow of the son of the original tenant claimed their exclusive title in respect of the two items of landed properties, on the basis of an order passed by Tribunal u/s. 48-A of the Act - Trial Judge rejecting the claim of the defendants' exclusive right and title, granted a decree for partition - On appeal, Lower Appellate Court affirmed the decree so far as it related to the house property, but reversed the decree so far as it relate to landed properties - On further appeal HC reversed the decree of the Lower Appellate Court and granted the relief of partition - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that Civil Court itself has no jurisdiction in view of the Tribunal created under the land reforms statute - Held, Civil Court could not be said to be ousted of the jurisdiction, in granting the relief sought for - It was too well settled that when a Tribunal was created under a special statute and the jurisdiction of the Civil Court was sought to be ousted under the said statute, it was only in respect of those reliefs which could be granted by the Special Tribunal under the special statute, the jurisdiction of the Civil Court could not be said to be ousted - It was difficult to hold that the Tribunal had the jurisdiction to grant the said relief so as to oust the jurisdiction of the Civil Court - U/s. 48-A of the Act the Tribunal could only grant the relief of declaring the occupancy right in favour of an applicant provided the preconditions for the same were satisfied, namely, that the land was in the possession of the tenant concerned on the relevant date - That being the position and the Tribunal under the Act not having the jurisdiction to grant relief of partition, the civil court itself has the jurisdiction to entertain the suit for partition - Even on equitable consideration, plaintiff could not be denied of a right in the property - In view of the matter, SC was not inclined to interfere with the impugned judgment and decree of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
