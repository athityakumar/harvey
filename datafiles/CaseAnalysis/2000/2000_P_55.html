Jagdish Lal v Parma Nand

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 220; (2000) 5 SCC 44; AIR 2000 SC 1822; JT 2000 (3) SC 580; 2000 (125) PLR 512; 2000 (1) RCJ 635; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 381; 2000(2) SCALE 524; 2000 (3) SCJ 82; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 526; 2000 (3) SLT 201; 2000 (2) Supreme 553

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Change Of Business

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 36 - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 13 - Appeal against eviction - Change of user - Change of business from general merchandise to that of restaurant amounts to change in user but tenant reverted back to his original business very shortly - New business started by tenant in premises let out to him was an allied business, which was ancillary to main business, it will not amount to change of user - Therefore, tenant was allowed to stay in premises but subject to his paying rent at enhanced rate - Other grounds of eviction was also negatived - Held, if tenant is allowed to stay in premises in question as tenant, by paying rent at rate of Rs. 1,500/ - per month than original rent of Rs. 600/ - per month, ends of justice would be better served.
