Life Convict Laxman Naskar v State Of West Bengal & Anr

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 577; (2000) 7 SCC 626; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1431; AIR 2000 SC 2762; AIR 2000 SCW 3060; 2000 (3) CCR 173; 2000 (3) Crimes 217; 2000 CRLJ 4017; JT 2000 (10) SC 29; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 18; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 18B; 2000(6) SCALE 223; 2000 (6) SLT 499; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 62; 2000 (6) Supreme 110

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 53 - Criminal Procedure Code 1973, ss. 432 and 433 A - West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, s. 61(1) - Sentence - Life imprisonment - Premature release - Considerations of - Held, means imprisonment for whole of remaining period of natural life - No indefeasible right of prisoner to be released unconditionally before definite period of 20 years - Age or his change in political views is irrelevant - Potentiality to commit crime and his effect of releasing on society must be considered.
