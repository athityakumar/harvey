Union of India and Others v Pradip Kumar Dey

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2639; (2000) 8 SCC 580; 2001 (1) AWC 176; 2000 (5) SLR 764; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Constitution - Equal pay for equal work - Respondent filed a writ petition on ground that duties performed by respondent as Naik (Radio Operator) were more hazardous than duties performed by personnel with similar qualifications and experience in State services and other organisations - Respondent made his claim on principle of 'equal pay for equal work' - Learned Single Judge dismissed writ petition but Division Bench of High Court allowed appeal and directed appellants to fix up pay of respondent and to revise same if same pay scale has since been revised in order to remove disparity - Sustainability of judgment passed by Single Judge and Division Bench? - Held, Courts should normally leave such matters for wisdom of administration except proven cases of hostile discrimination - But having regard to facts and circumstances of case and position of law stated, Division Bench of HC was not right in granting relief itself, straight away to respondent without examining implications and impact of giving such directions on other cadres - However, rejection of claim of respondent need not be taken as an issue closed once and for all - It is always open to Govt. to consider issue either by making reference to Pay Commission or itself once again as to grant of pay scales to respondent - Judgment and order under appeal are set aside and judgment of learned Single Judge is restored - Appeal allowed.
