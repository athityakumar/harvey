Kusum Ingots and Alloys Limited v Pennar Peterson Securities Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 169; (2000) SCC (Cr) 546; (2000) 2 SCC 745; AIR 2000 SC 954; 2000 (1) BC 300; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 428; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 755; 2000 (2) CompLJ 20; 2000 CRLJ 1464; 2000 (84) DLT 229; JT 2000 (2) SC 390; 2000 (1) LLJ 1649; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 275; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1120

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Companies Act, 1956 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881,s. 138 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986 - Dishonor of cheques - Demanding payment - Stipulated period Restraint order - Post-dated cheques were issued on behalf of company in favour of complainant in course of business - Complainant issued notice to company and/or its Directors stating facts of dishonor of cheques and demanding payment - No payment was made within period of 15 days stipulated under 1881, Act - Payee filed complaint against company and/or its Directors alleging inter-alia that they had committed an offence u/s.138 of 1881 Act - Before cheques were presented in bank or after bank declined to honor cheques drawer company was declared sick under provisions of 1985, Act by Board of Industrial and Financial Reconstruction - HC dismissed the petitions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether company and its Directors could be proceed against for having committed an offence u/s.138 of the 1881, Act - Held, criminal case for alleged offence u/s.138 of the 1881, Act could not be instituted during period in which restraint order passed by BIFR operative cannot be rejected - It would be unjust and unfair and against intent and purpose of statute to hold that Directors should be compelled to face trial in a criminal case - S.22 of the 1985, Act did not create any legal impediment for instituting and proceeding with a criminal case on allegations of an offence u/s. 138 of 1881 Act against a company - Purpose of such an embargo was to preserve assets of company for realization of dues of creditors - Appeals disposed of.
