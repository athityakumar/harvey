State of Haryana v Maruti Udyog Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3058; (2000) 7 SCC 348; AIR 2000 SC 2941; JT 2000 (10) SC 166; 2000(6) SCALE 304; 2000 (6) SLT 639; 2001 (124) STC 285; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 188; 2000 (6) Supreme 211

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, ss. 39, 39(5) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Assessment - Revised - Legality - Respondent Co. for the A/y 1986 87, was assessed to tax and vide orders under the 1973 Act and the Central Act. Respondent was served with a notice proposing to suo motu revise the assessment orders, on the grounds that the orders were illegal and improper. Suo motu revised assessment orders were passed enhancing the gross turnover of the Company by adding excise duty in the turnover returned by it and assessed by the Excise & Taxation Officer, disallowing part of the amount of rebate allowed by the Assessing Authority - Respondent filed writ petition before HC which was allowed vide the judgment impugned - Hence instant appeal - What is the ambit and scope of the words 'unable to pay the whole of the amount of tax assessed' used in proviso to s. 39 of the 1973 Act.

Held, in instant case the prayer was made to grant stay on the ground that 'the petitioner has not collected any additional tax from the customers and is unable to deposit the amount of additional demand created by patently illegal orders'. Respondent Company nowhere mentioned to or referred its inability to pay the amount on account of its alleged financial difficulties or incapacity to make the requisite payment. The legality of the additional demand created could not be made the basis for insisting to entertain the appeal without prior payment, as that would have required the determination on the merits of the appeal. Relying upon the Full Bench judgment of the jurisdictional court in M/s.Emerald International Ltd.'s case, the Tribunal was competent in passing the order which was impugned in the HC. The DB was not justified in ignoring the Full Bench judgment and the judgment of another Bench of coordinate jurisdiction while allowing the writ petition of the Company. The DB even failed to mention the circumstances which justified the passing of the order for allowing the writ petition with direction to the Tribunal for disposal of the appeal on furnishing of the bank guarantee by the Company. Merely because the Tribunal had insisted upon the payment of the amount in terms of proviso to s. 39(5) of the Act, should not have annoyed the court while granting the relief in exercise of its powers u/art. 226 of the Constitution. The impugned order being contrary to settled principles of law cannot be sustained and is accordingly set aside. Appeal disposed of.
