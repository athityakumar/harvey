G. Sagar Suri and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 603; (2000) SCC (Cr) 513; (2000) 2 SCC 636; AIR 2000 SC 754; 2000 ALJ 496; 2000 (1) BC 273; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 694; 2000 (1) CCR 138; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 613; 2000 (1) Crimes 179; 2000 CRLJ 824; 2000 (2) GLH 191; 2000 (1) JCC 158; JT 2000 (1) SC 360; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 707; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 707; 2000(1) SCALE 271; 2000 (2) SCJ 534; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 417; 2000 (1) SLT 499; 2000 (1) Supreme 322

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of FIR - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 420 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Complainant alleged that the respondents took loan from the complainant after making certain false promises but failed to repay the same - Cheques presented for payment by the complainant were dishonoured - Complaints were instituted against the appellants under ss. 406 and 420 of IPC along with s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act - Appellants sought quashing of criminal proceedings instituted against them - Whether the criminal proceedings against the appellant are liable to be quashed - Held, yes - Invoking the jurisdiction of criminal Court for offences under sections 406/420 of IPC is certainly an abuse of the process of law - None of the appellants are directors of co. which allegedly took loan - Prosecution of appellants under s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act will serve the ends of justice - Appeal allowed.
