State of Maharashtra v Damu S/O Gopinath Shinde and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 570; (2000) 6 SCC 269; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1088; AIR 2000 SC 1691; AIR 2000 SCW 1617; 2000 (41) AllCriC 56; 2000 (3) CCR 41; 2000 CRLJ 2301; 2000 (2) JCC 601; JT 2000 (5) SC 575; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 781; 2000(4) SCALE 8; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 880; 2000 (5) SLT 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120(B),302 - Unearthing Treasures - Infant sacrifice - Criminal Conspiracy - Abduction and Murder - Accused abducted and murdered children to unearth hidden treasures - One child managed to escape and that led to arrest of four accused - Accused no.1 made confessions regarding mysterious death of disappeared children -Confession statement of Accused no.1 was held voluntary and true - Accused were charged for abduction and murder of children in order to unearth treasures hidden below and were sentenced to death by Trial Court - HC allowed appeal filed by accused and ordered acquittal of accused on benefit of doubt over confession statement - Aggrieved by order Appellant/State filed appeal - Whether Respondents/Accused were guilty of offence charged - Held, large number of circumstances supported truth of version contained in confession statement and thus confession statement was held voluntary and true - Criminal conspiracy was proved against accused - HC had gone gravely erroneous in side-stepping every findings of prosecution - Children were not abducted and killed for ransom or vengeance but for genuine belief of hidden treasures - Acquittal order passed by HC was set aside - Sentence passed by Trial Court for counts of criminal conspiracy was restored and Accused was convicted and was sentenced to life - Appeal allowed.
