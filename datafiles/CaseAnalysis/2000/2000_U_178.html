T. N. Godavarman Thirumulpad and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, V.N. Khare, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4102; (2007) 15 SCC 273

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: All India Services (Confidential Rolls) Rules, 1970

Summary: (A) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Felling of trees - Regeneration - Concern of State was that they should be permitted to do felling as per working plans while concern of amicus curiae and Central Govt. was that regeneration should take place with regard to lifting ban on felling of trees from land other than Govt. forest land, it was recorded in these minutes that State was revising the Rules for transit of forest produce and felling of trees from private areas - Hence, instant Application - Whether there could be felling without regeneration - Held, it was evident that the felling was far in excess of what would be justified with reference to regeneration - There could not be felling without regeneration because that would over period of time only result in forests vanishing - There had been shortfall with regard to regeneration and as result thereof forest cover was depleting - That shortfall had to be made up and for future such felling had to be done which would ensure that there was at least no further depletion of forest cover, and that targets for increase in forest cover, as contemplated in working plans, were met - In other words, regeneration should be commensurate with felling, and to extent stipulated in working plans, which were approved by Central Govt. - Order accordingly.

(B) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Administrative - Competent authority - Filing of confidential report - Whether there was any authority competent to write confidential report with regard to officer belonging to Forest Department - Held, hierarchy in Indian Forest Service was that above Assistant Conservator of Forests was Divisional Forest Officer, thereafter on promotion person becomes Conservator of Forests, then Chief Conservator of Forests followed by Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and at pinnacle of pyramid was Principal Chief Conservator of Forests - It appears that up to officer of rank of Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests reporting authority had to be immediately superior officer within Forest Department Likewise reviewing authority would be person within same Department - It was only in case of Principal Chief Conservator of Forests that reporting authority would be person other than one belonging to service because there was no one superior to Principal Chief Conservator of Forests within service - As far as Principal Chief Conservator of Forests was concerned, reporting authority would be person who was familiar with work of Principal Chief Conservator of Forests and that would be person to whom he reports and who was superior to him in rank and hierarchy - Order accordingly.
