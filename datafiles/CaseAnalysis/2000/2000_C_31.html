Michael Machado and Another v Central Bureau of Investigation and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 159; (2000) 3 SCC 262; (2000) SCC (Cr) 609; AIR 2000 SC 1127; AIR 2000 SCW 734; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 860; 2000 (1) CCR 298; 2000 (2) Crimes 23; 2000 CRLJ 1706; 2000 (1) JCC 294; JT 2000 (2) SC 531; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 75; 2000(1) SCALE 624; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 981; 2000 (2) SLT 488; 2000 (2) Supreme 326

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319(1), 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B,219, 420,467, 468, 471 - Criminal conspiracy - Evidence - Discretionary power - Jurisdiction - Appellant Nos. 1, 2 was Chief Manager of different branch of Corporation Bank - Complaint was lodged - Allegations that a huge amount, more than half a crore of rupees had been defrauded by certain persons and Bank was put to great loss to extent - Metropolitan Magistrate arraigning 4 persons as accused for offences u/s.120-B, 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC - Order of Magistrate was challenged by concerned investigating officer and HC quashed that order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether action of magistrate in joining appellants as additional accused was legally sustainable - Held, there was reasonable prospect of convicting appellants of offence of criminal conspiracy - Order of Trial court in exercise of s.319 of CrPC had to be interfered with for enabling trial to proceed to its normal culmination - Court was only a discretion as could be proceed against such person - Suspicion was not sufficient to hold that there was reasonable prospect of convicting appellants of offence of criminal conspiracy - There was absolutely no material in evidence so far to proceed against those 2 bank officers - No doubt there was a reference in their evidence to role played by appellants, but such reference was insufficient to make out a case of criminal conspiracy u/s.120B of IPC against appellants - It would be improper to interfere with exercise of his jurisdiction u/s.319(1) of CrPC - Power u/s. 482 of CrPC would interfere with discretionary power of Magistrate passed u/s.319(1) of CrPC - Appeal allowed.
