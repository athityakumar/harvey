Blue Star Employee's Union v Ex Off. Principal Secretary To Government and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2822; (2000) 8 SCC 94; AIR 2000 SC 3110; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 70; 2000(6) SCALE 483; 2001 (7) SLR 345; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 403; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 138

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 ss. 10(2-A), 18(1), 22, 23, 33, 33(1), 33(1)(b) and 33-A - Disputes arisen between the appellant union and respondent No. 2 - Termination of some of the workmen - Reference was made to the Industrial Tribunal - An award made by the Tribunal holding that the termination of services justified and dismissed the complaint - High Court held, that the workmen cannot avail two opportunities for the same relief once on appearing before the Labour Court in individual capacity u/s. 10(2-A) although application was made u/s. 33-A of the Act and second by employees union, therefore, the Government is right in rejecting the application - Held, the Tribunal was justified in dealing with the merits of the dispute - Tribunal has not focused its attention to the first of the question whether there has been any contravention of s. 33 of the Act to enable it to proceed further to decide whether the employee is entitled to any relief under the Act or not - HC not justified in dismissing the writ petitions and in writ appeal upholding that order - Appeal allowed.
