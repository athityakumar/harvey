Malaysian Airlines Systems Bhd (Ii) v Stic Travels Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 31; (2001) 1 SCC 509; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 51; 2001 (2) ALT(SC) 8; 2000 (3) ARBLR 701; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 29; 2001 (1) CompLJ 217; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 313; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 184; 2000 (3) RAJ 436; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 406; 2000(7) SCALE 724; 2000 (8) SLT 737; 2000 (8) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(5) 11(9) - UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules, 1976, art. 6(4) - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, s. 42(1) - Bilateral Agreement - Appointment of Arbitrator - Maintainability - Petitioner company had its Principal place of business at Kualalumpur, Malaysia and had office at New Delhi and it was carrying on business of air-transportation, operation of air flights in and from India under bilateral agreement between Malaysia and India - Respondent had been appointed as General Sales Agent for various countries and that under agreements dt. 15-9-1986 and 11-1-1989, - Therefore, former agreement relating to passengers and latter relating to cargo said agreements were performed in India and cause of action arose in India on account of sale of passenger tickets and airway bills and said, respondent had to pay sum of Rs.96,21,137/- - Hence, instant application before SC for appointment of Arbitrator by petitioner u/s.11(5) of the Act - A) Whether preliminary issues raised by respondent could be decided at stage or be referred to arbitrator - Held, relied on Konkan Railway Corporation Ltd. Vs. Mehul Construction Co. 2000 Indlaw SC 678, M/s Konkan Construction Corporation Ltd. Vs. M/s Rani Construction Pvt. Ltd. 2000 Indlaw SC 29 , Court declined to decided preliminary issues and directed matter straightaway to arbitrator - B) Whether in case of international arbitration agreement, where one of parties was Indian national, it was not permissible to appoint arbitrator of Indian nationality was challenged - Held, relied on Dolphin International Ltd Vs. Ronak Enterprises Inc. 1998 Indlaw SC 1600 , in light of rules in various countries and rulings of Court and also in view of fact that 1996 Act was based on UNCITRAL Model law which in Art. 6(4) only speaks of 'taking into account' nationality as one of factors, Court was of view that word 'may' in s. 11(9) of the Act was not intended to be read as 'must' or 'shall' therefore, while nationality of Arbitration was matter to be kept in view, it did not follow from s. 11(9) that proposed arbitrator was necessarily disqualified because he belongs to nationality of one of parties - Further, word 'may' was not used in sense of 'shall' and said provision was not mandatory - In case party who belongs to nationality other than that of proposed arbitrator, had no objection, Chief Justice of India or his nominee could appoint arbitrator belonging to nationality of one of parties - Hence, Court opined that u/s. 11(9) of the act was not mandatory to appoint arbitrator not belonging to nationality of either parties to dispute and appointed retired Judge of SC as sole arbitrator and remuneration payable to arbitrator should be decided by arbitrator - Order accordingly.
