Vishin N. Khanchandani and Another v Vidya Lachmandas Khanchandani and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 409; (2000) 6 SCC 724; AIR 2000 SC 2747; 2000 (4) AWC 2939; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 626; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 340; [2000] 246 ITR 306; JT 2000 (9) SC 321; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 95; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 415; 2000 (5) Supreme 574

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: National Savings Certificates, Government Savings Certificates Act, 1959

Summary: Banking and Finance - Government Savings Certificates Act, 1959, ss. 6, 7 and 8 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Govt. securities - National Savings Certificate - Interpretation of Statutes - (A) Determination of applicability of non obstante clause - (B) Nominee, if on death of holder of certificate, become entitled to sum due under certificate to exclusion of other legal heirs - Held, determined in consideration of scheme of Act and object and reasons for which Act is made - Does not become entitled - Appeal allowed. .
