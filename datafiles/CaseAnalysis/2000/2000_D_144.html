Union of India and Others v Dudh Nath Prasad

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 541; (2000) 2 SCC 20; (2000) SCC (L&S) 236; AIR 2000 SC 525; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 4; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2000 (1) SC 1; 2000 LabIC 384; 2000 (1) LLN 332; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; 2000(1) SCALE 23; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2000 (3) SLJ 37; 2000 (1) SLR 1; 2000 (1) Supreme 38; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 318

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Whether respondent can be treated as belonging to a scheduled caste? - Held, notwithstanding that parents of respondent lived at one time in a village in District Siwan in State of Bihar and that they owned some property there also, they had shifted to State of West Bengal long ago and had been living there since then - For all intents and purposes, therefore, they shall be treated to be 'ordinarily residing' in State of West Bengal - For State of West Bengal, President, in exercise of his powers u/art. 341(1) r/w art. 366(24) had already declared 'Nuniya' caste as a Scheduled Caste and, therefore, respondent was rightly treated to be a Scheduled Caste candidate and was rightly appointed against a reserved vacancy - Appeal dismissed.
