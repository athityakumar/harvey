N. N. Bhagwati v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3175; 2002 (172) CTR 97; [2001] 247 ITR 206; JT 2000 (10) SC 226; [2001] 115 TAXMAN 139

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: No order as to costs.

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 80AA, 80AB - Retrospective operation - SC are not impressed by submission on behalf of appellant that judgment of SC needs reconsideration by a larger Bench. That judgment notes that ss. 80AA, 80AB of the Act were introduced at one and same point of time and that s. 80AA of the Act was given retrospective operation. It notes that it was held in case of Distributors that s. 80AA of the Act was declaratory of law as it always had been since 1-4-1968. On a parity of reasoning, it was held in judgment in s. 80AB of the Act was also enacted to declare law as it always stood. Whether, therefore, circulars of Board have stated that s. 80AB was prospective is of no relevance and judgment cannot be reconsidered on that ground. Appeal dismissed.
