K. Thimmappa and Others v Chairman, Central Board of Directors, State Bank of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2742; (2001) 2 SCC 259; (2001) SCC (L&S) 374; AIR 2001 SC 467; 2002 (92) FLR 630; JT 2001 (1) SC 347; 2001 (34) LabIC 465; 2001 (1) LLJ 1083; 2001 (1) LLN 814; 2000(8) SCALE 269; 2001 (2) SLJ 317; 2001 (1) SLT 427; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 32 and 226 - State Bank of India Officers (Determination of Terms and Conditions of Service) Order, 1979, Paras 4, 6, 7, 8 and 16 - State Bank of India Act, 1955, s. 43 - (A) Whether in matter of placement of existing officers in new grades and scale in Order, 1979, Para 7 is discriminatory in nature, so far as it deals with Officers Grade I? - Tarsem Lal Gautam v. State Bank of Patiala, 1988 Indlaw SC 272 relied upon - Held, placement of existing officers in new grades, as provided in sch. I, made in para. 7 of Conditions of Service Order, and placement made in respect of officers, Grade I, confirmed on or before 31-12-1972 in Middle Management Grade Scale II and others in Junior Management Grade Scale I, is not hit by art. 14 - (B) Whether putting officers of Grade I, who had not been confirmed on or before 31-12-1972 along with officers of Grade II in Junior Management Grade Scale I, would per se be discriminatory inasmuch as prior to new structurisation of management, promotion was being made from officers, Grade II to officers, Grade I? - Further held, concept of categorisation, placement and fitment in new scale of pay are three different concepts, provided in paras 6, 7 and 8 of Conditions of Order - If this concept is borne in mind and provisions contained in sch I is examined, there is no infirmity in placing officers, Grade I not confirmed till 31-12-1972 in Junior Management Grade Scale I, nor can it be said that it would violate mandate contained in para 6 of Conditions of Service Order - Petitions dismissed.
