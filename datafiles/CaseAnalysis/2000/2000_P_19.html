Special Officer and Competent Authority, Urban Land Ceilings, Hyderabad and Another v P. S. Rao

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 585; (2000) 2 SCC 451; AIR 2000 SC 843; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 48; 2000 (1) CLT 306; JT 2000 (1) SC 393; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 101; 2000 (1) RCJ 216; 2000(1) SCALE 268; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 257; 2000 (1) Supreme 402

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 20(1)(b), 20(1)(b), 21, 22 and 2(1) - Application u/s. 20(1)(b) of the Act - Maintainability of - Whether an application for grant of exemption u/s. 20(1)(b) of Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 is not maintainable once excess land has been declared? - Unless quantum of excess land, after statutory deductions etc. is arrived at, one cannot decide whether to surrender excess land or to, seek exemption either u/s. 20(1)(a) or s. 20(1)(b) or apply u/s. 21 or s. 22 - Definition of words 'to hold' in s. 2(1) cannot be applied in context of s. 20(1)(a) or s. 20(1)(b) - Held, Court does not consider it necessary to interfere with judgment of High Court which held that a s. 20 application is maintainable even if filed after an order of vesting of excess land passed u/s. 10 - Special Leave Petition disposed of.
