Dr. Ram Ashray Yadav,Chairman v Bihar Public Service Commission

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3537; (2000) 4 SCC 309; (2000) SCC (L&S) 670; AIR 2000 SC 1448; JT 2000 (3) SC 540; 2000 (3) LLN 9; 2000(2) SCALE 617; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 688; 2000 (2) SLR 576

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Service - Constitution of India,1950,art. 317 - Complaint - Enquiry - Reference - Validity - Opposition leader of Bihar submitted complaint against appellant to President of India enclosing a list containing 32 specific charges seeking action u/art. 317 (1) of Constitution - On President's notice Governor of Bihar sent reply to President with appellants reply and his comments - President of India made reference to SC and SC had issued notices to parties and granted leave to file their affidavits - Appellants counsel submitted that based on some judgments on same issue matter may sent back to President of India and meantime Attorney General came forward to verify documents if submitted to him - In order to save judicial time of Court and to crystallize issues SC had requested Attorney General to examine entire material including documents to be filed on behalf of appellant and to assist SC - Attorney General held discussions with concerned parties and submitted report to SC regarding various charges to be dealt and not to be dealt - Attorney General initially opined that out of 32 charges only 7 charges required consideration and later after concerned parties filed objections submitted to Court that only charges 3, 9, 12, 13 and 28 merited consideration and enquiry - Accordingly SC had taken up case regarding those charges and heard concerned parties and held charge wise reply- Regarding charge no.3 it was held by SC that it would be unfair to hold that appellant had committed any misbehavior in that selection process in absence of any indictment in his personal official or individual capacity - Regarding charge no.9 it was held by SC that no charge had been established against appellant and hence no misbehaviour could be attributed and charge was not proved - Regarding charge no.12 SC held that appellant had acted as per prevalent practice and procedure till rules were framed and evaluation was neither 'motivated' nor 'mala fide' and system of centralised evaluation introduced by appellant had not been condemned outright either by HC or SC and would not amount to any misbehavior and charge was baseless and not accepted - Regarding charge no.28 SC held that this charge would not require any probe as admittedly amount spent was within sanctioned amount and hence appellant could not be said to be guilty of any misbehavior - All charges of misbehavior were not established and appellant not found guilty - Appellant was relieved from his duties as his term expired and hence no question of reinstatement would lie but directed that appellant should be given all statutory dues eligible under rules - This report was submitted to President of India - Order accordingly.
