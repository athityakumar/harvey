Kans Raj v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, G.B. Pattanaik, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 305; (2000) 5 SCC 207; (2000) SCC (Cr) 935; AIR 2000 SC 2324; 2000 (41) AllCriC 3; 2000 (2) CCR 156; 2000 (2) Crimes 213; 2000 CRLJ 2993; 2000 (1) DMC 645; 2000 (2) JCC 665; JT 2000 (5) SC 223; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 695; 2000(3) SCALE 429; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 662; 2000 (4) SLT 162; 2000 (3) Supreme 554

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Continuous, Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Criminal - Dowry death - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304B and 306 - Deceased wife found dead in her matrimonial home within three years of marriage - On appeal, High Court acquitted respondents of all charges - Present appeal - Expression 'otherwise than under normal circumstances' would mean death not in usual course but apparently under suspicious circumstances, if not caused by burns or bodily injury - Held, prosecution has adduced best available evidence to prove charge against accused - Accused have not given any satisfactory account of even high probability as to how she died - There is a presumption u/s. 113- A of Evidence Act that suicide has been abetted by husband or other relative of husband of deceased - Accused have not been able to rebut that presumption - Also proved that deceased was treated with cruelty on account of dowry - Charges framed against respondent 2 (husband) had been proved by prosecution beyond reasonable doubt - Judgment of HC insofar as it relates to respondent 2 set aside and confirmed so far as it relates to other accused persons - Appeal partly allowed.
