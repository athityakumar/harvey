Kanti Bhadra Shah and Another v State of W.B

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 544; (2000) SCC (Cr) 303; (2000) 1 SCC 722; AIR 2000 SC 522; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 12; 2000 (1) CCR 72; 2000 (1) Crimes 96; 2000 CRLJ 746; 2000 (1) JCC 100; JT 2000 (1) SC 13; 2000 (1) KLT 795; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 407; 2000(1) SCALE 19; 2000 (3) SCJ 77; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 27; 2000 (1) SLT 133; 2000 (1) Supreme 6

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 454, 380 and 120-B - Framing of charge - High Court quashed charges against appellants but directed Metropolitan Magistrate to consider charges afresh - Whether Trial Court can frame charge against accused without giving a reasoned order? - If Trial Court decides to frame charge there is no legal requirement that he should pass an order specifying reasons as to why he opts to do so - Framing of charge itself is prima facie order that Trial Judge has formed opinion, upon considering police report and other documents and after hearing both sides, that there is ground for presuming that accused committed offence concerned Chap. XIX deals with provisions for trial of warrant cases instituted on a police report - Held, it is quite unnecessary to give detailed orders while framing charges - HC quashed order of charge without giving reasons which is not sustainable but at same time directed Metropolitan Magistrate to hear charges afresh - No interference needed - Appeal dismissed.
