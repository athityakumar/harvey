G. B. Pant University of Agriculture and Technology, Pantnagar, Nainital v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 504; (2000) 7 SCC 109; (2000) SCC (L&S) 884; AIR 2000 SC 2695; 2000 ALJ 2420; 2000 (4) AWC 2810; JT 2000 (9) SC 67; 2000 (33) LabIC 2893; 2000 (2) LLJ 1109; 2000 (3) LLN 1065; 2000 (5) SLR 97; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 391; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2200

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural University Act, 1958 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, s. 4(k) - Regularisation of services - Cafeteria workers - Canteen services are no longer looked upon as a mere welfare activity but as an essential requirement where sizeable number of employees work - Residential university having a canteen facility and inmates of hostel not being permitted to have food from outside cannot possibly be said to be a mere welfare service to students - It is a requirement of Regulations framed under Act and thus having statutory sanction and force - Regularisation will undoubtedly bring forth a parity with other employees of university - Principle of corporate jurisprudence is now being imbibed on to industrial jurisprudence and there is a long catena of cases in regard thereto - As regards interpretation, widest possible amplitude shall have to be offered in matter of interpretation of statutory documents under industrial jurisprudence - Draconian concept is no longer available - Regulations lead to unmistakable conclusion that the employees of the Cafeteria cannot but be termed to be the employees of the University - Held, relationship of employer and employee between university and cafeteria staff is established from provisions contained in Act, statutes and Regulations framed thereunder and also by documentary and oral evidence filed before Labour Court - Appeal allowed.
