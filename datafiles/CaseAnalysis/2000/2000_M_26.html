M. C. Mehta v Kamal Nath and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 529; (2000) 6 SCC 213; AIR 2000 SC 1997; JT 2000 (7) SC 19; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 392; 2000(5) SCALE 69; 2000 (5) SLT 36; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 389; 2000 (4) Supreme 391

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Exemplary Damages, Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977, Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Rules, 1978, Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Rules, 1982, Exemplary, Chemical Accidents (Emergency Planning, Preparedness And Response) Rules, 1996, Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Rules, 1975

Summary: Constitution of India,1950, arts. 21, 32 and 226 - 'life' - Hazardous act - Compensation or fine - Liability of - Held, any disturbance of basic environment elements, namely air, water and soil is hazardous to life - Court may direct polluters to pay compensation but not fine to victims.
