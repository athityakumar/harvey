Lakhan Sao v State Of Bihar & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3110; (2000) 9 SCC 82; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1163; AIR 2000 SC 2063; 2000 CRLJ 2959; JT 2000 (5) SC 515; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 812; 2000(4) SCALE 434

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 34 - Murder - Common intention - Deceased on inimical terms with both accused - Identification of accused - Held, fact that either may have his own motive for killing deceased not relevant in determining common intention - Sufficiency of light to carry on work was sufficient to identify also when they were known to each other.
