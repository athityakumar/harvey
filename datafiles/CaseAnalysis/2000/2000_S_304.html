Jagdamba Prasad Shukla v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 698; (2000) 7 SCC 90; (2000) SCC (L&S) 825; AIR 2000 SC 2806; AIR 2000 SCW 3047; 2000 ALJ 2444; 2000 (4) AWC 2982; 2000 (3) CLR 309; 2000 (87) FLR 1; JT 2000 (9) SC 457; 2000 LabIC 3111; 2000 (2) LLJ 1513; 2000(6) SCALE 78; 2000 (5) SLR 164; 2000 (6) SLT 314; 2000 (5) Supreme 650; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2268

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 6 - Constitution, art. 16 - One of the reasons for not appearing in inquiry as intimated to the authorities was the financial crunch on account of non-payment of subsistence allowance and the other was the illness of the appellant - Right of suspended employee (Sub-Inspector) to subsistence allowance - Employee in financial distress due to its non-payment unable to reach place of departmental enquiry - Held, amounts to denial of opportunity to defend in enquiry - Order of removal quashed - Appeal allowed.
