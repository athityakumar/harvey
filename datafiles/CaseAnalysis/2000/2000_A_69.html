Ram Awadh (Dead) By LRs. and Others v Achhaibar Dubey and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 552; (2000) 2 SCC 428; AIR 2000 SC 860; 2000 (39) ALR 365; 2000 (38) ALR 651; 2000 (2) AWC 929; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 63; 2000 (1) CLT 335; JT 2000 (1) SC 535; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 42; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 2; 2000 (91) RD 241; 2000(1) SCALE 415B; 2000 (2) SCJ 342; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 566; 2000 (2) SLT 1; 2000 (1) Supreme 319

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963- Section 16 (c)-Specific performance of contract-Suit therefor-Readiness and willingness of plaintiff-Plea of absence of readiness and willingness of plaintiff-Available to one or other defendant-Open to any defendant to contend and establish that mandatory requirement of Section 16 (c) not complied with-Legal representatives of subsequent purchaser being defendants in suit- Entitled to contend and plead absence of readiness and willingness on part of plaintiff.

The obligation cast by Section 16 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, is upon the Court not to grant specific performance to a plaintiff who has not met the requirements of clauses (a), (b) and (c) thereof. A Court may not, therefore, grant to a plaintiff who has failed to aver and to prove that he has performed or has always been ready and willing to perform his part of the agreement the specific performance whereof he seeks. There is, therefore, no question of the plea being available to one defendant and not to another. It is open to any defendant to contend and establish that the mandatory requirement of Section 16 (c) has not been complied with and it is for the Court to determine whether it has or has not been complied with and, depending upon its conclusion, decree or decline to decree the suit.

(1995) 2 SCC 31, Jugraj Singh v. Labh Singh, overruled. Judgment of Allahabad High Court, reversed.
