Chiranjilal Srilal Goenka (Dead) By Lrs v Jasjit Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2749; (2001) 1 SCC 486; AIR 2001 SC 266; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 88; 2001 (43) ALR 235; 2001 (1) ARBLR 1; 2000 (88) DLT 697; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 418; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 177; 2000 (3) RAJ 472; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 569; 2001 (92) RD 216; 2000(8) SCALE 130; 2000 (8) SLT 603; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 313; 2000 (8) Supreme 137; 2001 (1) UC 276

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Prohibition Act - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Registration Act, 1908 - (A) Whether writing dated 26-1-1961, which spoke of giving one's son in lieu of him adopting property of adopting parents can be considered to be an agreement? - Held, photocopy of letter, presuming that such letter was written at time of giving of adoption, there can be no doubt that it does not reflect any agreement between parties - At most it was only a unilateral offer giving child in adoption - (B) Whether it is an agreement as contemplated by s. 13 Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act of limiting rights of adoptive parents to dispose of property by will? and if so, whether it requires registration? - Held no, because reliance is required to be placed on letter for holding that it restrains from disposing of property by will to be read as a document which limits rights - Award is, on face of it, illegal and erroneous based on misinterpretation of letter as an adoption agreement - Award is inoperative and is set aside - Order accordingly.
