Commissioner of Income Tax v Kanji Shivji and Company

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 604; (2000) 2 SCC 253; (2000) SCC (Cr) 343; AIR 2000 SC 774; 2000 (1) CCR 99; 2000 (1) Crimes 131; 2000 (158) CTR 537; [2000] 242 ITR 124; 2000 (1) JCC 243; JT 2000 (1) SC 350; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 848; 2000(1) SCALE 250; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 365; 2000 (1) SLT 474; 2000 (1) Supreme 261; [2000] 108 TAXMAN 531

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Provision, Introduction

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 40(b) - Provision - Introduction - Held, a Bench of two learned Judges observed that it was difficult to accept proposition that explanation was only clarificatory for reason that if what was contained in said Explanation was already law in force, then giving effect to said Explanation from 01-04-1985 did not make any sense. But Bench immediately noted, 'However, in case before SC, no question of payment of any interest in involved'. Appeal dismissed.
