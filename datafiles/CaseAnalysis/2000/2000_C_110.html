Sounds N. Images v Collector Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3517; (2000) 9 SCC 143; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 538; JT 2000 (4) SC 342; 2000 (38) RLT 242

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Appeal allowed, Bona Fide, Imported, Goods, Value Declared, Similar Goods, Sufficient Evidence, Valuation Of Goods, Imported Into India, Value Of Imported Goods, Quotation, Basis Of Valuation, Appellate Tribunal, Collector, Valuation, Correct Valuation, Fax Message, Condensers

Summary: Customs Act, 1962 - Whether customs authorities were justified in relying on upon fax copy of a quotation issued by a Singapore party to another Singapore party for valuation of goods? - A fax message setting out a quotation by one party in Singapore to another party in Singapore cannot be made basis of valuation of goods imported into India, particularly so when importer's request to be allowed to cross-examine alleged offeror is declined - Held, it is always for customs authorities to establish by methods known to law and in a satisfactory manner that value of imported goods is not what importer says it is and what that value actually is - Appeal allowed.
