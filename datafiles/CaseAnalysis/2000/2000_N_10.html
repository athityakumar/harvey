Essar Constructions v N. P. Rama Krishna Reddy

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 10; (2000) 6 SCC 94; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 29; 2000 (2) ARBLR 625; JT 2000 (5) SC 505; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 130; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 281; 2000(4) SCALE 328; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 17 and 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 41 and 115 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950 - No unanimity among Arbitrators - Whether order of Principal Senior Civil Judge was not an appealable one and could only be revised u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908? - Was ground that there was a total negligence and it is on part of counsel who appeared for State in Trial Court sufficient for condoning delay? - Held, ground was sufficient to excuse delay and to remand matter back to Trial Court for a decision on merits of application u/s. 30 - Appeal dismissed.
