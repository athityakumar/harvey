State Bank of India v Tarun Kumar Banerjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2827; (2000) 8 SCC 12; AIR 2000 SC 3028; 2000 (4) AWC 3304; 2000 (3) CLR 839; JT 2000 (10) SC 571; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 70; 2000(6) SCALE 410; 2000 (5) SLR 261; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 313; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 102

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Domestic enquiry - Validity - Whether tribunal had no power to interfere with the findings on misconduct recorded in domestic enquiry? - Held, in course of adjudication proceedings if tribunal is satisfied that order of discharge or dismissal was not justified, it can reappraise evidence adduced in domestic enquiry and satisfy itself - Award of tribunal is that evidence of three witnesses recorded at enquiry being sufficient to record guilt of respondent 1 - Tribunal could not have placed reliance on same - Single Judge was justified in interfering with same - Appeal allowed.
