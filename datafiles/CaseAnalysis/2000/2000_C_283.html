Comptroller and Auditor General of India and Others v Farid Sattar

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 352; (2000) 4 SCC 13; (2000) SCC (L&S) 440; AIR 2000 SC 1557; AIR 2000 SCW 1374; 2000 (1) CLR 254; JT 2000 (4) SC 374; 2000 (3) LLN 45; 2000(3) SCALE 147; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1067; 2000 (3) SLJ 286; 2000 (2) SLR 636; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1735

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Fundamental Rules, r. 22(1)(a)(3) - Transfer by volition - Respondent applying unilateral transfer in cadre of Accountant - Resigning technically from senior grade of post, as per terms and conditions applicable - Further required to forgo any benefit of passing any departmental examination while working in higher post - Appointed to a post which is lower to post - Consequent Pay Scale - Held, Fundamental Rules contemplates transfer on written request - Lower pay scale has to be fixed as he was considered to be direct recruit in lower post - Pay Scale of higher post not protected.
