Assistant Collector Of Central Excise, Rajamundry v Duncan Agro Industries Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 355; (2000) 7 SCC 53; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1275; AIR 2000 SC 2901; 2000 (3) CCR 116; 2000 (3) Crimes 155; 2000 CRLJ 4035; 2000 (120) E.L.T. 280; 2000 (2) JCC 560; JT 2000 (8) SC 530; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 719; 2000(5) SCALE 454; 2000 (6) SLT 68; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 162; 2000 (5) Supreme 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 24 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Admissibility of evidence - Certain companies engaged in manufacturing cigarettes were prosecuted - Trial Judge acquitted all of them - Appellant filed an appeal before the HC and moved for leave to appeal - Single Judge relied on the view taken by DB in N.S.R. Krishna Prasad vs. Collector of Customs that unless the empowered authority u/s. 108 of 1962 Act administers the caution or the warning embodied u/s. 164(2) of CrPC before recording a statement of confessional nature, from the person summoned, the statement so recorded would be inadmissible in evidence for any purpose - Single Judge excluded all the confessional statements from consideration and declined to grant leave to appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it is necessary to comply with the precautions envisaged in s. 164 of the CrPC when Customs officers record statement u/s. 108 of the 1962 Act.

Held, statement recorded by customs officers u/s. 108 of the 1962 Act is admissible in evidence. The court has to test whether the inculpating portions were made voluntarily or whether it is vitiated on account of any of the premises envisaged in s. 24 of the 1872 Act. Such an exercise can be made only after the appeal is regularised by granting leave to appeal. In the instant case, since leave was declined on a wrong interpretation of law, the impugned order is set aside. Appeal allowed.
