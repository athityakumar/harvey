Nar Singh Pal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 234; (2000) SCC (L&S) 362; (2000) 3 SCC 588; AIR 2000 SC 1401; 2000 (2) AWC 1499; 2000 (2) CLR 15; JT 2000 (3) SC 593; 2000 LabIC 1377; 2000 (1) LLJ 1388; 2000 (2) LLN 407; 2000(2) SCALE 685; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 752; 2000 (3) SLJ 332; 2000 (2) SLR 592; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1298

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311 and 14 - Termination of service - Casual labour in Govt. department - Worked continuously as such for more than 10 years and also acquired 'temporary status' - Prosecuted for criminal offences on allegation of assaulting gate man on duty - Acquitted subsequently - Termination of his services on basis of same incident by paying him retrenchment compensation - Order passed on basis of preliminary enquiry and not on basis of regular departmental enquiry, without issuing a charge sheet or giving opportunity of hearing - Held, being punitive in nature, liable to be set aside - Encashment of cheque by retrenched/terminated employee - Does not debar him from challenging order terminating his services being violative of fundamental rights or constitutional rights.
