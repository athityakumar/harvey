Rajasthan Housing Board v Parvati Devi

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 201; (2000) 6 SCC 104; AIR 2000 SC 1940; 2000 (3) CompLJ 175; 2000 (3) CPJ 9; 2000 (3) CPJ(Sc) 9; JT 2000 (6) SC 237; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 286; 2000(4) SCALE 306; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 934; 2000 (7) SLT 50; 2000 (3) Supreme 729

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; MRTP; Tort

Summary: Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, s. 2(o) - Restrictive trade practice - Delay and demand of additional cost by housing board - Complaint against - Ground, manipulation of services to impose unjustified costs on consumer - No specific allegation that board preventing - Held, question cannot be decided on mere basis that board has represented to give possession within stipulated time and that has not been complied - Practice must have actual or probable effect of preventing, distorting or restricting competition - Ought to be considered on terms of agreement between parties.
