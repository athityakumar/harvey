Gangula Ashok and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 601; (2000) SCC (Cr) 488; (2000) 2 SCC 504; AIR 2000 SC 740; AIR 2000 SCW 279; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 265; 2000 (1) CCR 146; 2000 (1) Crimes 196; 2000 CRLJ 819; 2000 (1) JCC 187; JT 2000 (1) SC 379; 2000 (1) KLT 609; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 101; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 797; 2000(1) SCALE 294; 2000 (2) SCJ 489; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 468; 2000 (1) SLT 529; 2000 (1) Supreme 378

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Human Rights - Indian Penal Code, ss. 201 and 354 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 4(2), 5, 193 and 227 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention Of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 14 - Whether Special Court constituted under Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 can take cognizance of offence without committal of case by Magistrate? - Held, court is essentially a Court of Session - It can only take cognizance of offence when case is committed to it by magistrate in accordance with provisions of CrPC and thus neither charge - sheet nor a complaint can straightaway be filed before such Special Court for offences under Act - Ss. 3 and 4 of Code will not come in aid in order to lift prohibition - Appeal disposed of.
