Lalmuni Devi Smt v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2618; (2001) 2 SCC 17; (2001) SCC (Cr) 275; AIR 2001 SCW 2504; JT 2001 (1) SC 150; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 228; 2000(8) SCALE 432; 2001 (1) SLT 26; 2001 (1) UC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 202 and 203 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 419, 420, 467 and 120 B - Criminal complaint against R2 to R10 was first dismissed by magistrate and then restored by sessions judge and later on quashed by High Court on ground that complaint discloses a civil wrong and not any criminal offence - De facto complainant present appellant - Held, settled law that if complaint discloses a civil claim and also a criminal offence, it is not proper to quash complaint - Merely because a civil claim is maintainable does not mean that criminal complaint cannot be maintained - Further held, in present case, it cannot be stated, at this prima facie stage, that this is a frivolous complaint - HC does not state that on facts no offence is made out - Then merely on ground that it was a civil wrong, criminal prosecution could not have been quashed - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
