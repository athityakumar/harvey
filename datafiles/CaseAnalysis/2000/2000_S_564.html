Mohammed Gazi v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 12; (2000) 4 SCC 342; AIR 2000 SC 1806; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 14; 2000 (40) ALR 14; 2000 (2) ARBLR 207; 2000 (2) CLT 318; JT 2000 (4) SC 55; 2000(4) M.P.H.T. 169; 2000 (1) RAJ 543; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 756; 2000(3) SCALE 6; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 871; 2000 (3) SLT 448; 2000 (4) Supreme 705

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 14 - Equitable relief - Grant of - Appellant participating in tender auction of tendu leaves started again after cancellation of first tender notice - Appellant was declared to be highest bidder - Depositing security money - Appellant could not take benefit of acceptance of his bid by stay order obtained by previous tenderer in writ petition to which appellant was not party - Claiming that since tendu leaves are destroyed he should not be compelled to purchase them and for refund of security deposit - Held, penalty of cut in security amount cannot be imposed - Appellant was not at fault by applying equity clause - Maxims actus curiae neminem gravabit and lex non cogit ad impossibilia apply to appellant's case.
