Mohammed Aynuddin Alias Miyam v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 385; (2000) 7 SCC 72; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1281; 2000 (2) ACC 360; AIR 2000 SC 2511; 2000 (3) Crimes 119; 2000 CRLJ 3508; 2000 (2) JCC 681; JT 2000 (8) SC 317; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 619; 2000 (5) SLT 637; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 15; 2000 (5) Supreme 308

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 A, - Whether death of passenger boarding bus amounts to rash and negligent driving? - Whether onus of proof of rash and negligent driving, depends upon applicability of principal of Res Ipsa loquitor? - Whether rash act is opposed to deliberate act? - Held, without evidence driver cannot be presumed to be negligent - Res Ipsa loquitor is applicable only when nature of accident and present circumstances would reasonably lead to belief that in absence of negligence accident would not have occurred and that thing which caused injury is shown to have been under control of alleged wrong doer - Rash act is opposed to deliberate act.
