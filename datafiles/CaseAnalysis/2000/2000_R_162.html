Ramesh Kumar v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4152; (2009) 17 SCC 594

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & property - Practice & Procedure - Identification of squatting sites - Non-compliance of order - Contempt petition - Applicants were residing in particular area of various zones wherein relevant sites of respondent - Applicants had filed petition for squatting said site before SC, which had directed respondent to make appropriate identification of squatting sites, which had not been complied by them - Applicants had filed applications for execution of earlier order and also contempt petition - Hence instant applications.

Held, that Court direct that respondent through its counsel shall produce minutes of meetings held on various dates for various zones wherein relevant sites were identified for squatting. Copies of minutes will be produced on 4-8-2000 at 2.00 p.m. In meantime, photocopies of minutes will also be furnished by respondent to for applicants latest by 2-8-2000 so that their response may also be available on next date of hearing before Court. For implementation of order, respondent states that he will offer one set of photocopies of minutes to applicants and they has been good enough to say that they will accept same and will make them available to other. Respondent will take instructions in this connection and give response next week. Unnumbered applications in these categories will also be placed/stand adjourned to next week. Respondents states, on instructions, that appropriate identification of squatting sites is already made by Committee which was directed to be constituted by them earlier order of April 2000 and decision is rendered on 16-6-2000. They are permitted to produce copy of this decision of 16-6-2000 along with affidavit. Affidavit will clearly state as to which part of New ‘L’ Market is found to be non-squatting zone and also states that by next week he will file complete site plan indicating in green and red colour which are squatting and non-squatting zones. Contempt Petition at request of applicants adjourned to enable them to produce relevant documentary evidence to show that 39 applicants were eligible squatters since 1992, for supporting their case as put forward in this contempt petition. Order accordingly.
