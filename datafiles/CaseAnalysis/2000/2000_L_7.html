Moolchand Kharati Ram Hospital K. Union v Labour Commissioner and Company

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2977; (2002) 10 SCC 708; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1030; 2001 (1) CLR 29; 2000 (87) FLR 853; JT 2000 (2) SC 204; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 204; 2001 (34) LabIC 2147; 2000 (2) LLJ 1411; 2001 (5) SLR 543; 2000 (7) SLT 204

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Reference - Management filed writ for quashing of reference - Whether Division bench correct in holding learned Single Judge is justified in quashing order of reference? - In view of High Court, relevant considerations have not been taken note of by Govt. and that finding cannot be seriously disputed - In circumstances, HC is justified - However, that would not solve problem of parties - Proper course to be adopted is to direct first respondent to take appropriate steps to make a reference to concerned Tribunal after considering all relevant material on record in case - Such steps be taken within three months from today - Appeals disposed of.
