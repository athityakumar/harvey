Sayed Taher Bawamiya v Joint Secretary To The Government of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.P. Misra, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2842; (2000) 8 SCC 630; (2001) SCC (Cr) 56; 2002 CRLJ 259; 2004(2) SCALE 543

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities(Amendment) Act, s. 3 - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, s. 6(1) - Detention order was passed against detenue but was not served on him for 16 years as he left India and never returned back - With fear of losing his property, detenue field writ petition in High Court - By impugned judgment HC applied ratio of decision of Supreme Court in case of Addl. Secy. to Govt. of India v. Alka Subhash Gadia, 1990 Indlaw SC 636, and came to conclusion that in exercise of his discretion u/art. 226 of Constitution it would not be appropriate to entertain writ petition inasmuch as proposed detenue had not surrendered and detention order had not been served on him - Hence, present appeal - In Alka Subhdsh Gadia case Court held that equitable jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 32 which is discretionary in nature would not be exercised in a case where proposed detenue successfully evades service of order - It was held that courts will interfere at pre-execution stage with detention orders only after they are prima facie satisfied (i) that impugned order is not passed under Act under which it is purported to have been passed, (ii) that it is sought to be executed against a wrong person, (iii) that it is passed for a wrong purpose, (iv) that it is passed on vague, extraneous and irrelevant grounds, or (v) that authority which passed it had no authority to do so - Held, present case does not fall under any of aforesaid five exceptions for court to interfere - Detenu intentionally evading detention orders - Petition dismissed.
