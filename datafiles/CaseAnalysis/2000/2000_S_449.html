Rajiv Kapoor and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3538; (2000) SCC (L&S) 84; (2000) 9 SCC 115; AIR 2000 SC 1476; 2002 (2) CTC 306; JT 2000 (3) SC 635; 2000(2) SCALE 634; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 629; 2000 (2) SLR 603; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1292

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Education - (A) Validity of method and criteria to be followed in matter of selection of candidates for admission to Post Graduate Degree and Diploma courses in Medicine from amongst Haryana Civil Medical Service ('HCMS') candidates for academic session 1997 in Pt B D Sharma Post Graduate Institute of Medical Sciences affiliated to Maharishi Dayanand University - Controversy is limited to admission of 19 and 14 candidates respectively to post Graduate Degree and Diploma courses in Medicine during academic year in question for HCMS candidates and does not include any consideration of candidates either in open merit category or to be filled up on All India basis - Held, prospectus as well as orders of Govt. have to be construed in such a manner that inter se merits of service candidates are properly assessed on basis of their credentials and performance in service and not merely of theoretical knowledge of subject as in case on non-service candidates belonging to other categories - Construction placed by High Court, if accepted may result in discrimination on account of applying different criteria of total marks for open candidates and in service candidates without noticing distinguishing features relevant for purpose of assessment of merit in case of HCMS candidates - Thus court finds no reason or justification to allow any deviation from method of assessment uniformly followed in all previous years for such selection - Further held, merits of HCMS candidates are required to be adjudged in terms of criteria contained in Govt. orders noticed above and selections can be made for admission against reserved seats, as per determination of merit by Selection Committee constituted for purpose - (B) Issue with regard to relief - Further held, though relief is concerned to extent of setting aside judgment of HC and settling principles which should govern selection of candidates for admission to PG Degree/Diploma courses in Medicine in respect of seats reserved for candidates of HCM Services, appellants cannot be granted any relief in their favour - In view of statement made at stage of Special Leave Petition, admission of respondents, however, is not interfered with - Appeals allowed.
