Jagatram Ahuja v Commissioner of Gift Tax, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S.P. Bharucha, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2648; (2000) 8 SCC 249; AIR 2000 SC 3195; 2000 (164) CTR 1; [2000] 246 ITR 609; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 545; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 1; [2000] 113 TAXMAN 459

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Family Property, Goodwill, Transfer Of Property, Partnership Firm, Estate Duty Act, 1953, Gift Tax Act, 1958

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift tax Act, 1958, ss. 2(24) and 2(12) - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 2(15) - Partnership Firm - Dissolution - Distribution of assets among partners inter se - (A) Transaction if amount to 'gift' - (B) 'Transfer of property' and 'Property' as defined under Gift-Tax Act and Estate Duty Act to be interpreted - Held, no, as fact that shares inter se of partners are specific and can be determined at any given point of time, not relevant - Cannot be given same meaning, since aim and object of two legislations is not similar - Order Accordingly.
