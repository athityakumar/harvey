Kadiyala Rama Rao v Gutala Kahna Rao (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 161; (2000) 3 SCC 87; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 53; 2000 (39) ALR 93; 2000 (2) CLT 104; JT 2000 (2) SC 269; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 22; 2000 (91) RD 583; 2000(1) SCALE 696; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1045; 2000 (2) SLT 313; 2000 (2) Supreme 3

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets, Constructive

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 rr. 90, 91, s. 115 - Auction - Highest bidder - Judgment debtor - Petitioner was a stranger auction purchaser of a house property sold in court auction in pursuance of a mortgage decree - Court sale of house property was effected upon payment of 25% of sale price offered by highest bidder - Sale was confirmed upon payment of full purchase price - Respondent filed an application to set aside auction sale - Application rejected - Appellant took delivery of house property - Respondents obtained an interim stay of proceedings upon deposit of half of decretal amount - HC directed respondent to deposit remaining half of decretal amount - Hence, instant appeal - Whether validity of court sale in regard to immovable property was justified - Held, denounce sale on ground of quantum of purchase price - Saleable interest does not come within ambit of O. 21 r.90 of CPC and Judgment-Debtor have not locus standi to apply to Court for setting aside sale - Statute recognizes such a locus standi only in the event of material irregularity or fraud - Law by reason of factum of challenge of sale on ground of sale ability - No saleable interest could be agitated by purchaser only in terms of under O.21 r. 91 of CPC and not by Judgment debtor - Grounds of challenge was specific in provision itself namely, material irregularity or fraud and in absence of any evidence allegation in regard in petition under O.21 r. 90 of CPC - Appeal allowed.
