Ratansingh v Vijaysingh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2731; (2001) 1 SCC 469; AIR 2001 SC 279; 2001 AllCJ 752; 2001 (42) ALR 447; 2001 (1) AWC 403; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 499; 2001 (1) KLT 327; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 189; 2001 (127) PLR 296; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 495; 2000(8) SCALE 214; 2001 (1) SLT 103; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 510; 2000 (8) Supreme 151; 2001 (1) UC 297

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 48 and 115 - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 136 and 182 - Execution of Decree - Limitation Period - Challenged - Appellant/ decree-holder after secured decree on 14-12-1970 by Trial Court and went into slumber and remained for pretty long period - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal before Appellate Court which was dismissed - Aggrieved, respondent filed second appeal before HC which was rejected - As appellant made endeavor to utilize second appeals order to execute Trial Court's decree and filed before Execution Court which resuscitated decree with help of said order - Appellant filed revision before District Court which was reversed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which pointed out that revisional powers of HC u/s. 115 CPC was already exercised by District Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was justifiable - Held, Anandilal & anr. vs. Ram Narain and ors. 1984 Indlaw SC 324, Nagendra Nath Dey and anr. vs. Suresh Chandra Dey and ors. 1932 Indlaw PC 26, relied on - Therefore, decree became enforceable on 1-8-1973 when appellate court passed decree which superseded decree of Trial Court - As no decree was passed HC in appeal - Thus, decree of Appellate Court remained unaffected and enforceability once commenced remained undisturbed for period of 12 years - Further, execution process initiated by appellant long after expiry of 12 years from 1-8-1973 was irretrievably barred- Hence, interference was called for - Appeal dismissed.
