Gouri Sankar Chatterjee and Others v Texmaco Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2939; (2001) 2 SCC 257; (2001) SCC (L&S) 372; 2001 (1) CLR 978; 2001 (89) FLR 694; JT 2001 (1) SC 117; 2002 (35) LabIC 2467; 2001 (1) LLJ 553; 2001 (2) LLN 59; 2000(8) SCALE 407; 2001 (1) SLR 561; 2001 (1) SLT 11; 2000 (8) Supreme 519; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 346

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17-B - Industrial Tribunal held that 92 Badli workmen were entitled to regularisation of services and extension of benefits and status like regular employees and also entitled to have other statutory benefits also from date of their respective initial engagement - Whether award passed justified? - Division Bench of High Court held that Tribunal had enlarged scope of Reference and thereby committed an error of jurisdiction - Main issue whether Badli workmen should be regularised, and for arriving at a just conclusion, Tribunal, evidently, did not put management of first respondent-company on notice - Award passed was set aside - Reference be considered by Industrial Tribunal afresh in accordance with law - Special Leave Petition disposed of.
