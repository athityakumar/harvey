Ishwar Swaroop Sharma v Jagmohan Lal

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2766; (2001) 1 SCC 218; AIR 2001 SC 370; 2001 (43) ALR 326; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 181; 2001 (128) PLR 158; 2000 (2) RCR(Rent) 649; 2000(7) SCALE 607; 2001 (1) SCJ 137; 2000 (8) SLT 453; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 51; 2000 (7) Supreme 662

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 4 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Whether fair rent of shop in question u/s. 4 rightly determined in present case? - For fixing basic rent u/s. 4 only question would be that, was there a subsisting agreement of tenancy under which rent was payable when application for fixation of fair rent was filed and if answer is in affirmative agreed rent must be taken as basic rent and if not, then basic rent is prevailing market rate - Held, even though agreement may have been entered into in 1976 as is admittedly true in this case, but tenancy was continuing until date of application, Rent Controller was obliged to take rate agreed to in 1976 as basic rent under first limb of s. 4(2)(b) and it is only after fair rent is fixed that landlord could seek refixation under second limb subject to limitations provided in Act as rent would then cease to be agreed rent - Appeal dismissed.
