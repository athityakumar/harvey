Rosy and Another v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 557; (2000) 2 SCC 230; (2000) SCC (Cr) 379; AIR 2000 SC 637; 2000 (1) CCR 76; 2000 (1) Crimes 110; 2000 CRLJ 930; 2000 ILR(Kar) 2117; 2000 (1) JCC 228; JT 2000 (1) SC 84; 2000 (1) KLT 494; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 414; 2000(1) SCALE 85; 2000 (3) SCJ 145; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 107; 2000 (1) SLT 299; 2000 (1) Supreme 114

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.313 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.202(2) - Methyl alcohol in arrack - Committal of Case - Evidence recorded - Breach of mandatory provisions - Appellant/Accused charged u/ss. 57-A, 56(b) of Kerala Abkari Act for presence of methyl alcohol in arrack - Magistrate committed case to Sessions Court - Trial commenced and witnesses were examined u/s.313 of CrPC - Appellant/Accused alleged that Magistrate erred in following provisions of s.202(2) of CrPC - Trial Court made reference to HC after findings of breach of mandatory provisions - HC directed Magistrate to conduct fresh enquiry u/s.202(2) of 1973 of CrPC - Aggrieved by order Appellant/Accused filed appeal - Whether Appellant/Accused was entitled to any relief against order passed by HC - Held, Appellant/Accused did not raise issue of committal of case on course of trial - Objections were not raised early stages of trial - Decision on belated objections taking in mind, principles adumbrated in s. 465 of CrPC - Order of HC was set aside and Sessions Court was directed to dispose of case on merits - Appeals disposed of.
