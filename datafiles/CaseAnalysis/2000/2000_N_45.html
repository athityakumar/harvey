Mahavir Singh and Others v Naresh Chandra and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2791; (2001) 1 SCC 309; AIR 2001 SC 134; AIR 2000 SCW 4000; 2000 (41) ALR 765; 2001 (1) ALT(SC) 60; 2001 (1) ARC 154; 2001 (1) AWC 170; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 427; 2001 (127) PLR 496; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 454; 2001 (92) RD 67; 2000(7) SCALE 356; 2000 (8) SLT 163; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 454; 2000 (8) Supreme 13B; 2001 (1) UC 117

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 107, 115, 151 and 107(d), O. 41, r. 27 - Application for adducing additional evidence - Rejected by the Appellate Court - Interference by the High Court - Proprietary - Held, getting documents in question examined to be able to adduce additional evidence - S. 107 requires taking additional evidence in accordance with O. 41, r. 27 only - Circumstances when additional evidence can be adduced - (1) Court from whose decree appeal is preferred has refused to admit evidence which ought to have been admitted - (2) Party seeking to produce additional evidence, establishes that notwithstanding exercise of due diligence, such evidence was not within his knowledge or could not, after exercise of due diligence, be produced by him at time when decree appealed against was passed - (3) Appellate Court requires any document to be produced or any witness to be examined to enable it to pronounce judgment, or for any other substantial cause - Court is not bound under circumstances mentioned under rule to permit additional evidence and parties are not entitled, as of right, to admission of such evidence and matter is entirely in discretion of court to be exercised

judiciously and sparingly - Present case is not covered by any of three situations - The expression 'to enable it to pronounce judgment' means a situation when Appellate Court finds itself unable to pronounce judgment owing to a lacuna or defect in evidence - Mere difficulty in coming to a decision is not sufficient - HC u/s. 115, could not interfere with an order of Appellate Court rejecting permission to adduce additional evidence particularly when whole appeal is not before HC - Appeal allowed.
