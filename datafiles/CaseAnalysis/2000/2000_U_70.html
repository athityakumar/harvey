Ghaziabad Development Authority v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 536; (2000) 6 SCC 113; AIR 2000 SC 2003; 2000 ALJ 1456; 2000 (40) ALR 447; 2000 (3) ARBLR 170; 2000 (3) AWC 2335; 2000 (1) CCC 117; 2000 (3) CompLJ 402; 2000 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2000 (7) SC 256; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; 2000(4) M.P.H.T. 231; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 447; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2047

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract Act, 1872, ss. 73 and 65 - Breach of contract for construction - Mental agony - Unreasonable delay by development authority in completing scheme for allotment of plots - Provision in brochure excluding liability of authority to pay interest in cases of refund of consideration - Held, applies only where claimant was responsible for creating occasions for refund - Mental agony not ordinarily a head of damages in commercial contract - Not permissible therefore.
