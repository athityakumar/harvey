Weston Components Ltd. v Commissioner Of Customs, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 36; (2000) 1 SCC 565; AIR 2000 SC 561; 2000 (1) CLC 886; 2000 (88) ECR 544; 2000 (115) E.L.T. 278; JT 2000 (1) SC 52; 2000(1) SCALE 41A; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 26; 2000 (1) SLT 188; 2000 (1) Supreme 16

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Release of goods - Redemption fine - Appellant contended that, redemption fine could not be imposed because goods were no longer in custody of respondent-authority - Held, goods were released to appellant on an application made by it and on appellant executing a bond. If subsequently it is found that import was not valid or that there was any other irregularity which would entitle customs authorities to confiscate said goods, then mere fact that goods were released on bond being executed would not take away power of authorities to levy redemption fine. Appeal dismissed.
