Commissioner of Income Tax v United Provinces Electric Supply Company

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 733; (2000) 5 SCC 101; 2000 (160) CTR 248; [2000] 244 ITR 764; JT 2000 (4) SC 635; 2000(3) SCALE 377; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 22; [2000] 110 TAXMAN 134

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Profits Chargeable To Tax, Balancing Charge

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 41(2) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 6 - Whether addition u/s. 41(2) of sum received under provisions of Indian Electricity Act, was justified? - Amount was received as compensation which was not finally settled and was an adhoc payment - A business profit u/s. 41(2) is to be taxed in previous year of its receipt - Held, compensation received though adhoc, is a business income and is to taxed in previous year of its receipt - Answered in favour of Revenue - Appeal allowed.
