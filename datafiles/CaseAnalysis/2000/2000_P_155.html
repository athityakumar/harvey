A. P. Pollution Control Board Ii v Prof. M. V. Nayudu (Retd.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2748; (2001) 2 SCC 62; AIR 2000 SCW 4573; 2001 (2) CLC 102; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 322; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 412; 2000(8) SCALE 23; 2000 (8) SLT 786; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 249; 2000 (8) Supreme 318

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Water

Keywords: Sustainable Development, Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977, Prohibition Act, Manufacture, Storage and Import of Hazardous Chemical Rules, 1989

Summary: Water Act, 1974, Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, ss. 28 and 25 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, ss. 2(b), 3(2), 5 and 23 - Prohibition of polluting industries - Exemptions - (A) Whether Govt. orders are referable to delegated authority to impose total prohibition of polluting industries? - In exercise of powers conferred by Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, s. 23, Central Govt. can delegated powers vested in it u/s. 5 of Act to States - Held, Govt. orders are referable to said delegated authority permitting State Govt. to impose 'total prohibition' of polluting industries to be located within 10 km of two reservoirs - (B) Whether GO No. 153 D\-3-7-1997 granting exemption to a particular industry is without statutory backing, arbitrary and violative of art. 21? - Further held, Water Act, 1974 does not enable State to grant exemption to a particular industry within area prohibited for location of polluting industries - Further, GO No. 153 D/-3-7-1997 granting exemption must be held to be without statutory backing and also wholly arbitrary and violative of art. 21 - Appeal allowed.
