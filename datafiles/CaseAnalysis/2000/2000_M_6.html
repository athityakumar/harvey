S.M. Dyechem Limited v Cadbury (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 22; (2000) 5 SCC 573; AIR 2000 SC 2114; AIR 2000 SCW 2172; 2000 (2) ARBLR 498; 2000 (91) ECR 1; 2000 (3) GLR 2548; JT 2000 (7) SC 151; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 96; 2000 (20) PTC 297; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 203; 2000(4) SCALE 713; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 86; 2001 (1) UJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Phonetic Similarity, Rectification Proceedings

Summary: Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, s. 106 - Trade mark - Infringement - Injunction - Power of court - Proof of resemblance - Peculiar script and curve not copied by defendant from common feature - Entitlement - Held, dissimilarities appearing to be more striking than similarity between two marks - Necessary to go into question of 'comparable strength' - No injunction.
