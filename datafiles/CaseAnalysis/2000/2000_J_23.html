Vaneet Jain v Jagjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 131; (2000) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2000 SC 2080; 2000 (91) ECR 32; JT 2000 (6) SC 293; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 507; 2000(4) SCALE 467; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 918; 2000 (5) SLT 78; 2000 (4) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Revisional Jurisdiction

Summary: Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, ss. 13 and 15(6) - Scope of High Court for revisional jurisdiction was in question - Also whether eviction was a bona fide requirement of landlord as he neither had service nor any business - He argued that he required premises to start a business - It was held by HC that since landlord is enrolled with employment exchange, his requirement was not bona fide - Held, it is no doubt true that HC would be justified in interfering with order if legality or propriety of such order demands such interference but HC in its revisional jurisdiction cannot reassess or re - evaluate evidence only to come to a different finding than what has been recorded by lower court - Also, interference by HC with concurrent finding that need was bona fide was not justified.
