State of Andhra Pradesh v A. Sathyanarayana and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3152; (2001) 10 SCC 597; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1033; 2002 CRLJ 265; 2001(2) SCALE 690

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorists and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, ss. 4, 5, 6 and 20-A(1) - Whether a chargesheet under TADA can be filed without prior approval in writing from DSP as envisaged in s. 20-A(1)? - Relied on Kalpnath Rai v. State, nothing in sub-s. A(1) indicates prior approval of District Superintendent of Police should be in writing, what is necessary is fact of approval which is sine qua non for recording information about commission of offence under TADA - Provision intended to operate as a check against police officials of lower ranks commencing investigation into offences under TADA - Serious consequences which such action befalls accused - Check can be effective even if approval of DSP is oral if such an exigency arises-oral approval by itself therefore not illegal - Held, it is not requirement u/s. 20-A(1) to have prior approval only in writing-prior approval is condition precedent for registering a case, but it may be either in writing or oral also, as has been observed by this Court in Kalpnath Rai's case - Therefore, Learned Designated Judge was wholly in error in refusing to register case u/ss. 4 and 5 of TADA - Impugned order of Designated Judge set aside - Appeal disposed of.
