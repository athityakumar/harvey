Veterinary Council of India v Indian Council of Agricultural Research

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, D.P. Wadhwa, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 546; (2000) 1 SCC 750; AIR 2000 SC 545; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 16; JT 2000 (1) SC 41; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 89; 2000(1) SCALE 32; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 43; 2000 (1) SLR 420; 2000 (1) SLT 202; 2000 (1) Supreme 31; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 305

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - All India common entrance examination - Veterinary - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 252 and 77(3) - Indian Veterinary Council Act, 1984, ss. 3, 21(1), 22, 66(1) and 66(2)(n) - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Whether s. 66(1) of Veterinary Council Act, 1984 confers powers to frame regulations to carry out purposes of Act r/w s. 22 of Act? - Held, Veterinary Council of India (VCI) is authorized to frame regulations relating to prescribing standards of veterinary education for granting veterinary qualifications and such an authority must include power to regulate admissions to course so as to maintain 'standards of education' - It is not disputed that s. 22 is a valid piece of legislation enacted by Parliament - Regn. 5(8) has been framed by virtue of powers vested in VCI u/s. 66 of Veterinary Council Act, 1984 r/w s. 22 of Veterinary Council Act - Appeal allowed.
