Prabodh Sagar v Punjab State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 568; (2000) 5 SCC 630; (2000) SCC (L&S) 731; AIR 2000 SC 1684; 2000 (2) CLR 766; JT 2000 (5) SC 378; 2000 (33) LabIC 1848; 2000 (2) LLJ 1089; 2000 (2) LLN 899; 2000(4) SCALE 408; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 866; 2000 (4) SLR 1; 2000 (5) SLT 168; 2000 (4) Supreme 253

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 79 - Punjab State Electricity Board Services (Premature Retirement) Regulations, 1982, Regn. 3(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Service Rules - Senior Accounts Officer - Premature retirement - Reported decline in progress and indulged in litigations repeatedly - Order on recommendation of High Power Committee by Member/Finance and Accounts - Legality - Mala fide, proof - Held, not illegal - No set guidelines are there, it can only be appreciated from facts of each case - Appeal dismissed.
