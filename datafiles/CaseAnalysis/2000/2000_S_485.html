Devi Dass Gopal Krishen Limited and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, B.N. Kirpal, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3072; (2001) 1 SCC 51; AIR 2001 SC 272; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 306; 2000(8) SCALE 55; 2001 (1) SLT 134; 2001 (121) STC 388; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 325; 2000 (8) Supreme 100

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Jammu and Kashmir General Sales Tax Act, 1962, ss. 4(1) and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 142, 310 and 304 - Exemption - Notification - Edible oil in State - S. 4 of Act is charging provision for levy of sales tax in State of Jammu and Kashmir - Exemption from payment of sales tax was granted via Notification of 1991 issued u/s. 5 of 1962 Act to all units manufacturing and carrying on business in edible oil in State - Earlier outside manufacturers had been paying sales tax at rate of 4 per cent as enhanced by means of a notification issued in 1989 - Later on rate of sales tax was further enhanced to 8 per cent by Notification of 1994 - Whether total exemption granted to manufacturers within State was hit by provisions of arts. 301 and 304 as it created tax barriers affecting free-flow of trade and commerce within territory of India? - In present case Court finds that there was no impediment in way of appellant to realise sales tax at full rate - In facts of present case, appellants shall derive no assistance from said decision as well - It is not a case for interference with orders passed by High Court and to issue any such direction to respondents not to realise balance amount of sales tax for reason that it had not been realised by appellants, nor due to fact that exemption granted to local manufacturers was quashed but with effect from a prospective date, viz, 01-04-1997 - Appeal dismissed.
