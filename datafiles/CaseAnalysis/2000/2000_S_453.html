Suneeta Aggarwal v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 507; (2000) SCC (L&S) 313; (2000) 2 SCC 615; AIR 2000 SC 1058; 2000 (1) CLR 825; JT 2000 (2) SC 168; 2000 (2) LLN 51; 2000(1) SCALE 566; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 783; 2000 (3) SLJ 30; 2000 (4) SLR 289; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 806

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Discretionary relief - Denial on account of conduct of petitioner - Vice - Chancellor order disapproving recommendations of Selection Committee to appoint petitioner and directing re - advertisement of post - Challenged - Petition filed after applying for post again - Appearing in interview without protest - Held, conduct of petitioner attracts estoppel for challenging order - No grant of discretionary relief.
