Kumaon Mandal Vikas Nigam Limited v Girja Shankar Pant and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2697; (2001) 1 SCC 182; (2001) SCC (L&S) 189; AIR 2001 SC 24; 2001 (1) AWC 83; 2001 (1) CompLJ 22; 2000 (87) FLR 877; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 206; 2001 LabIC 11; 2001 (1) LLJ 583; 2000(7) SCALE 19; 2000 (8) SLR 769; 2000 (7) SLT 556; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 248; 2000 (7) Supreme 112

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Respondent General Manager (Tourism) dismissed from service - Challenged on grounds of bias and prejudice - Held, test, therefore, is as to whether a mere apprehension of bias or there being a real danger of bias, and it is on this score that surrounding circumstances must and ought to be collated and necessary conclusion drawn therefrom - There is existing a real danger of bias, administrative action cannot be sustained - If on other hand, allegation pertaining to bias is rather fanciful and otherwise to avoid a particular court, tribunal or authority, question of declaring them to be unsustainable would not arise - Requirement is availability of positive and cogent evidence - Further held, action of managing director in matter of withdrawal of authority as noticed above and subsequent introduction of charges in particular, last of charges as noted above and further factum of issuance of an eighteen page letter of termination on self - Same date and within a few hours after pretended hearing was given, cannot but be ascribed to be wholly and totally biased - Appeal dismissed.
