Commissioner of Income Tax v Travancore Titanium Products Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.P. Mohapatra, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 723; (2001) 1 SCC 590; AIR 2001 SC 326; 2000 (164) CTR 651; [2001] 247 ITR 186; JT 2001 (1) SC 71; 2000(8) SCALE 109; 2001 (1) SLT 36; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 461; 2000 (8) Supreme 287; [2001] 114 TAXMAN 249

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Surtax, General Reserve

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964, s. 18 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 256(1) - (A) Whether tribunal is right in law in holding that loan redemption reserve amount to Rs. 1 crore is a reserve and not a provision and is to be included in computation of capital for purpose of surtax? - Held, fact that amount has been set apart for redeeming liabilities makes it obvious that intention is for clearing liabilities and not acquiring an asset and hence it is clear that amount in question cannot be regarded as a 'reserve' but 'provision' - Supreme Court decision in case of Vazir Sultan Tabacco Co Ltd vs CIT 1981 Indlaw SC 391 (SC) discussed - Appeal allowed.
