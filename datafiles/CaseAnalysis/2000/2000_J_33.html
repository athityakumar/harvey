State of Uttar Pradesh v Jaikaran Singh

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3801; (2003) 9 SCC 228; 2003 (3) LLJ 1077

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Disciplinary proceeding - Order of dismissal from service - Writ petition - High Court directed reinstatement and continuity of service of respondent being of opinion that order of dismissal is not commensurate with gravity of alleged misconduct - Held, court in exercise of power u/art. 226 does not interfere with quantum of punishment alone if charges are established against delinquent and there is no lacuna in procedure adopted in departmental proceedings - But at times if court feels that punishment inflicted is grossly unjust and shocks conscience then in appropriate cases court may interfere - Having regard to facts and circumstances of present case and also taking into account fact that respondent had served appellant organisation for about more than 12 years, ends of justice would be met if order of dismissal is altered to one of compulsory retirement - Appeal allowed.
