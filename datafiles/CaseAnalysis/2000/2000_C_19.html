Allahabad Bank v Canara Bank and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 492; (2000) 4 SCC 406; AIR 2000 SC 1535; AIR 2000 SCW 1374; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 83; 2001 (42) ALR 761; 2000 (1) BC 627; 2000 (1) CLC 913; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 64; 2000 (2) CompLJ 170; JT 2000 (4) SC 411; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 36; 2000(3) SCALE 169; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Recovery Officer, Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Companies Act, 1913, Companies (Amendment) Act, 1960, Banking Companies ( Acquisition And Transfer Of Undertakings ) Act, 1980, Industrial Reconstruction Bank of India Act 1995

Summary: Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 17, 18 and 25 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 537, 446 and 442 - Application for recovery of debts - Adjudication of liability of debtor was on Debt Recovery Tribunal - Argued that Civil Courts and Company Court also had jurisdiction over matter - Held, jurisdiction of Tribunal is exclusive and execution of adjudication order passed by Tribunal has to take effect - Jurisdiction of Recovery Officer is exclusive - Jurisdiction of Civil Court or Company Court is ousted.
