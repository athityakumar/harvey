Gaya Din (D) Through Lrs. and Others v Hanuman Prasad (D) Through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2764; (2001) 1 SCC 501; AIR 2001 SC 386; 2001 (43) ALR 226; 2001 (1) AWC 344; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 199; 2001 (92) RD 79; 2000(7) SCALE 613; 2000 (8) SLT 415; 2000 (8) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: United Provinces Agricultural Tenants (Acquisition of Privileges) Act, 1949 (ENG)

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of holdings Act, 1953 - Constitution - United Provinces Agricultural Tenants (Acquisition of Privileges) Act, 1949 (ENG) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Respondents claimed that they and appellants are members of a joint Hindu family and, therefore, their names should also be recorded as co-sharers - Appellants denied existence of joint family and asserted that they were holding khatas in their individual capacity - Issues arise - (A) Whether High Court ought not to have interfered with findings of facts recorded by Deputy Director of Consolidation whose powers u/s. 48 of Consolidation Act are wide enough? - Held, notwithstanding fact that s. 48 has been couched in wide terms, it only permits interference where findings of subordinate authority are perverse in sense that they are not supported by evidence brought on record or they are against law or where they suffer from vice of procedural irregularity - (B) Whether parties were members of joint Hindu family and khatas in question were held for their benefit? - Further held, on basis of fact, Settlement Officer (Consolidation) arrived at conclusion that family was joint and that khatas were maintained in names of members of different branches of family - HC observed that since evidence on record and on appraisal of law, Settlement Officer (Consolidation) did not commit any error apparent on face of record which could have been disturbed by Deputy Director of Consolidation - No illegality in impugned order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
