Sudhakar and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, G.B. Pattanaik, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 359; (2000) 6 SCC 671; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1250; AIR 2000 SC 2602; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 786; 2000 (3) CCR 66; 2000 (3) Crimes 122; 2000 CRLJ 3490; 2000 (2) JCC 657; JT 2000 (8) SC 184; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 383; 2000(5) SCALE 157; 2000 (5) SLT 511; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 507; 2000 (5) Supreme 205

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 306 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Rape victim committing suicide - Suicide after five and half months of incident - Her statement to police although recorded after 11 days of incident - Abetment of suicide - Whether Charge of rape not proved against accused as delaying in lodging FIR, medical examination of prosecutrix, and non - examination of material witnesses and witnesses turning hostile? - Held, statement of prosecutrix not a dying declaration and not in series of circumstances of transaction which resulted in death of deceased - Accused not responsible for abetment of ultimate offence of suicide.
