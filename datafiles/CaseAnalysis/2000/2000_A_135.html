Haldiram Bhujiawala and Another v Anand Kumar Deepak Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 184; (2000) 3 SCC 250; AIR 2000 SC 1287; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 79; 2000 (40) ALR 296; 2000 (1) ARBLR 531; 2000 (2) AWC 1309; 2000 (1) CLT 357; 2000 (84) DLT 301; JT 2000 (2) SC 596; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 159; 2000 (20) PTC 147; 2000 (1) RAJ 452; 2000(2) SCALE 177; 2000 (2) SCJ 75; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1247; 2000 (2) SLT 438; 2000 (2) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Registration of Business Names Act, 1916, Business Names Act, 1985

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11 - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69(2) - Suit for permanent injunction filed by respondents - Appellants filed application for rejection of plaint u/o. 7, r. 11 CPC - (A) Whether s. 69(2) bars a suit by a firm not registered on date of suit where permanent injunction and damages are claimed in respect of a trade mark as a statutory right or by invoking Common Law principles applicable to a passing-off action? - M/s Raptakos Brett and Co. Ltd v. Ganesh Property, 1998 Indlaw SC 1187 followed - Held, suit is not barred by s. 69(2) of Partnership Act if a statutory right or a common law right is being enforced - (B) Whether words 'arising from a contract' in s. 69(2) refer only to a situation where an unregistered firm is enforcing a right arising from a contract entered into by firm with defendant during course of its business or whether bar u/s. 69(2) can be extended to any contract referred to in plaint unconnected with defendant, as source of title to suit property? - Bengal Waterproof Ltd v. Bombay Waterproof of Manufacturing Company and Anr, 1996 Indlaw SC 1881 (SC) followed - Well settled that a passing off action is a common law action based on tort - Further held, suit for perpetual injunction to restrain defendant not to pass-off defendant's good as those of plaintiffs by using plaintiff's trade mark and for damages is an action at common law and is not barred by s. 69(2) - Appeal dismissed.
