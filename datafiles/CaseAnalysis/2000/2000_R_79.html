J. K. Synthetics v Rajasthan Trade Union Kendra and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 115; (2001) 2 SCC 87; (2001) SCC (L&S) 329; AIR 2001 SC 531; 2001 (1) CLR 1058; 2001 (88) FLR 682; JT 2001 (1) SC 363; 2001 LabIC 493; 2001 (1) LLJ 561; 2001 (1) LLN 877; 2000(8) SCALE 329; 2001 (1) SCJ 310; 2000 (6) SLR 39; 2001 (1) SLT 269; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 552; 2000 (8) Supreme 482

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether question of closure was never referred to industrial tribunal and/or that there was no closure of any unit of appellant Co.? - In order to come to conclusion, whether or not retrenchment was justified, industrial tribunal necessarily had to first decide whether there was closure and whether such closure was bonafide - Even though in reference and in pleading term 'closure' may not have been specifically used, what was essential was whether or not there was in fact a closure as understood in industrial law and a permanent discontinuance necessarily means closure - Held, division bench erred in coming to a conclusion that there was no textile section and that there was no closure of textile section - Appeal disposed of.
