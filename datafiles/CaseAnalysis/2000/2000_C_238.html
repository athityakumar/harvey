K. C. P. Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 788; (2000) 6 SCC 665; AIR 2000 SC 2866; 2000 (162) CTR 320; [2000] 245 ITR 421; JT 2000 (9) SC 1; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 302; [2000] 112 TAXMAN 66

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income - tax Act 1961, s. 28 - Levy Sugar Price Equalisation Fund Act 1976, s. 3 - Trading receipt - Excess realisation over and above authorised price on sale of sugar - Should tax be paid on excess amount and if so under which trading receipt? - Argument was that excess amount so recovered has been refunded to sugar equalization fund of Govt. - Held, excess amount recovered constitute trading receipt and liable to be taxed - Head under which trading receipt entered was irrelevant - Fact that excess amount so recovered has been refunded to sugar equalization fund of Govt. is not associated with liability to pay tax.
