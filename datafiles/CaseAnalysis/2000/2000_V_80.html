Vst Industries Limited v Vst Industries Workers' Union and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2625; (2001) SCC (L&S) 227; (2001) 1 SCC 298; AIR 2000 SCW 4566; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 70; 2001 (1) CLR 590; 2001 (88) FLR 548; JT 2001 (1) SC 36; 2001 (1) LLJ 470; 2001 (1) LLN 786; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 64; 2000(8) SCALE 95; 2000 (8) SLT 824; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 438; 2000 (8) Supreme 342; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 265

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, s. 46 - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952 - Petroleum Act, 1934 - Whether the persons employed in a canteen can be treated as employees of the appellant? - The canteen had been working within the premises provided by and with the equipment supplied by the appellant - That the appellant supplies the provisions for the preparation of foodstuffs - That the appellant issues token to the employees, who on production of the same obtain foodstuffs from the canteen - The contributions like ESI, provident fund, etc. had also been categorically specified to be provided by the appellant and ESI code for the permanent establishment and for the present workmen was the same - This clearly indicated that the appellant exercised administrative, financial and disciplinary control over the workmen in question - Held, appeal dismissed.
