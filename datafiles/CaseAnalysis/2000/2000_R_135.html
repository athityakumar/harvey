Roop Singh (Dead) Through Lrs v Ram Singh (Dead) Through Lrs

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 231; (2000) 3 SCC 708; AIR 2000 SC 1485; AIR 2000 SCW 1001; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 18; 2000 (39) ALR 484; 2000 (2) CLT 299; JT 2000 (3) SC 474; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 592; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 427; 2001 (92) RD 374; 2000(2) SCALE 577; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 605; 2000 (3) SLT 459; 2000 (2) Supreme 579

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Adverse Possession

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 64 and 65 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 53-A - Defendant entering into possession of premises as bataidar(tenant) - No cogent and convincing evidence - Showing when possession became adverse and hostile - Concurrent findings of facts by subordinate Court that possession of defendants was permissive - And, that Defendant also failed to prove agreement of sale in his favour - Held, cannot be interfered with by High Court in second appeal.
