Nirmala Devi v Arun Kumar Gupta and others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, D.P. Mohapatra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4107; (2005) 12 SCC 505; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 3; 2000 (39) ALR 546; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 225; JT 2000 (4) SC 229; 2000 (Supp) RD 62

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Execution of Will - Proof of will - Clubbing of cases - Whether probate proceedings can be clubbed with civil suit and can be disposed of by Trial Court itself - Hence instant petition.

Held, that Court has heard appellant as well as respondent no. 1, rest of respondents though served, has not thought it fit to appear and contest proceedings. It has been brought to Court’s notice that settlement is not possible at this stage and now remains question that probate proceedings could be clubbed with suit. Respondent no. 1, submitted that suit is of year 1987 and that despite various orders of HC, it has remained pending and probate proceedings are initiated by appellant in 1997 regarding Will of 1984. Be that as it may, decision in probate proceedings on question of proof of Will, will have direct impact on suit. Only on this short ground and without expressing any opinion on merits of controversy between parties, Court requests Trial Court to make it convenient to dispose of probate proceedings being Probate Case along with civil suit being filed by respondent no. 1 at his earliest convenience and preferably within period of six months from date of receipt of copy of this order at its end. Aforesaid civil suit pending in court of eight Sub-judges, shall therefore, stand transferred to Trial Court and be clubbed with Probate Case which is pending in Trial Court for being tried together. Appeal allowed.
