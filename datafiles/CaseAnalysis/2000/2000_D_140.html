Dr. Parag Gupta v University of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 301; (2000) 5 SCC 684; AIR 2000 SC 2319; JT 2000 (5) SC 345; 2000(4) SCALE 20; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 633; 2000 (2) SLR 788; 2000 (4) SLT 195; 2000 (3) Supreme 691; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1629

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Admission - Migrated - Institutional preference Unfair advantage - Students had qualified for medical degree course got admission under All India quota - Petitioner certainly compete for all India 25% of seats earmarked to be filled up on all India basis from candidates selected and sponsored by Director General of Health Services - Petitioner would have an unfair advantage had secured admission under 15% all India quota, he would became ineligible in State of Tamil Nadu - Candidates would be eligible against 100% of seats 25% all India quota and 75% seats in Delhi University - It would confer unjustified favour and benefits to all such candidates - Whether institutional preference based on any study in an institution fully complies with various directions issued by Court - Held, attaining uniformity in all P.G. courses all over country except to extent indicated - Students who pursued studies in a particular university or State were not invidiously stranded or marooned - India quota should be allowed to participate to compete in their home States where they have their roots, to pursue post graduate studies - Students in home State if at all were put to disadvantage only to a small degree of taking competition with respect to very few students falling in that category of petitioners - There would be uniformity all over country and small disadvantage removed with respect to a small section of student community does not disturb balance and advantage derived achieves uniformity - It would be appropriate for concerned States or other authorities to achieve uniformity by adopting institutional and/or residential preference in terms of decisions - Petitions disposed of.
