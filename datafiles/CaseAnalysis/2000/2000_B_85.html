Marwari Kumhar and Others v Bhagwanpuri Guru Ganeshpuri and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 500; (2000) 6 SCC 735; AIR 2000 SC 2629; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 66; 2000 (41) ALR 19; JT 2000 (9) SC 152; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 184; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 279; 2000 (91) RD 622; 2000(5) SCALE 526; 2000 (6) SLT 111; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 368; 2000 (5) Supreme 486; 2001 (1) UC 8

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice and Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 65(f) and 65(c) - Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 64 and 65 - Land and Property - Possession - (A) Evidence - Proof - Public document - Admissibility of secondary evidence in form of ordinary copy - (B) Limitation - Temple property under dispute - Plaintiff-community held to be owner and respondent only a Pujari - Subsequent suit for possession - Claim of adverse possession but bound by earlier decree - No plea or proof as to how and when his possession became adverse - Held, admissible, when both original and certified copy is lost - Respondent's, claim cannot be accepted - Appeal disposed of.
