Union of India v E. I. D. Parry (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 531; (2000) 2 SCC 223; AIR 2000 SC 831; 2001 (1) CalLT(SC) 5; 2000 (2) CLT 113; JT 2000 (1) SC 505; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 39; 2000 (125) PLR 64; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 668; 2000(1) SCALE 359; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 537; 2000 (1) SLT 602; 2000 (Supp) RD 413; 2000 (1) Supreme 317

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Demurrage, Railway Act 1890

Summary: Railway Act, 1890 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Second appeal - Against decree passed in suit against railways for recovery of excess demurrage charges - Claim based upon Goods Tariff Rules - Not pleaded that said rule was ultra vires Railways Act - No issue framed by Trial Court - High Court held that rules were ultra vires - Held, HC travelled beyond pleadings which was improper so order passed by HC was illegal.
