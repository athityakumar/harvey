State of Punjab and another v V. K. Khanna and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2712; (2001) 2 SCC 330; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1010; AIR 2001 SC 343; 2004 (1) ESC 22; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 349; 2001 (34) LabIC 391; 2001 (127) PLR 262; 2000(7) SCALE 731; 2001 (1) SCJ 439; 2000 (5) SLR 734; 2000 (8) SLT 581; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 200; 2000 (8) Supreme 105; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 280

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Assets dis-Proportionate - Charge sheet - Conduct - Maintainability - Petitioner/State/CM initiated proceedings against respondent No.1 and other senior colleagues alleging that acquisition of assets much beyond known sources of income, grant of Govt. plot to Punjab Cricket Control Board for purposes of Stadium and charge sheet was initiated against respondent No.1 - Aggrieved, respondent No.1 approached Tribunal which dismissed application - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before HC which was allowed and came to conclusion about high - handed, arbitrary and mala-fide approach towards respondent No.1 and both issues dealt affirmatively - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was existing cogent evidence of improper conduct and motive resultantly malafide move on part of appellants against respondent No.1 was challenged - Held, it was well settled in Service Jurisprudence that concerned authority had to apply its mind upon receipt of reply to charge-sheet or show-cause as case should be, as to whether further inquiry was called for - Therefore, Court had dealt with said aspect of matter in detail before judgment thus suffice it to note that further effort on part of respondent No.1 in bringing notice to CM would not had resulted any further development and in that perspective conduct of respondent No.1 could not be faulted in any way - These were instances which HC ascribed to be not in accordance with known principles of law and attributed motive as regards initiation of charge sheet - Thus, Court opined as noticed above malafide intent or biased attitude could not be put on straight jacket formula but depend upon facts and circumstances of each case - Hence, HC orders / judgments could not be faulted - Appeals dismissed.
