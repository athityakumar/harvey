 State of Bihar and Another v Bal Mukund Sah and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, G.B. Pattanaik, V.N. Khare, U.C. Banerjee, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2704; (2000) SCC (L&S) 489; (2000) 4 SCC 640; AIR 2000 SC 1296; JT 2000 (3) SC 221; 2000 LabIC 1389; 2000(2) SCALE 415; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 299; 2000 (2) SLR 448

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Extent Of Laws Made By Parliament, Eligibility - Qualifications, Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s. 4 - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - Appellant/State gave advertisement for filling up vacancies of Additional District Judges, Judges as well as posts in lower judiciary in District Judicial Service of Bihar to have reservations in posts of District Judges for reserved category of candidates and had insisted on proceeding with recruitment as per 1951 Rules, styled as Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951, and u/s. 4 of the Act, which were framed by Governor of Bihar in exercise of powers conferred by proviso to art. 309 r/w. art. 233 of the Constitution and which Rules did not provide for any such reservation - Governor of Bihar issued impugned Ordinance which subsequently became impugned Act by which scheme of 50 percent reservations for reserved category of candidates was directed to be applied while effecting direct recruitment to posts concerned - Appellant/State requested HC to effect recruitment to vacancies on basis of reservation provided by ordinance and said ordinance was challenged by respondents/writ petitioners before HC which was allowed and quashed condition of reservation sought to be imposed by impugned advertisement - Hence, instant appeals -

S.B. Majmudar, J.

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s. 4 - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Applicability - (A) Whether impugned Act of 1991 on its express language covers Judicial Service of Bihar State was challenged - Held, it became obvious that term 'any office of Judiciary of State of Bihar' would naturally include not only ministerial staff but also officers, including Presiding Officers of courts comprised in Judiciary of State - Once that conclusion was reached on express language of relevant provisions of the Act, it could not be held that thrust of s. 4 would not apply to govern reservation for direct recruitment to posts of Presiding Officers in District Courts as well as courts subordinate thereto, as all of them will form part and parcel of Judiciary of State of Bihar and would be treated as holders of offices in State Judiciary - Hence, HC on express provisions of the Act, s. 4 could not apply to govern recruitment to posts in Subordinate Judiciary was first point for determination, therefore, answered in affirmative in favour of appellants and against respondents - Appeals dismissed.

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233, 234, 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s. 4 - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - (B)Whether provisions of impugned Act, especially, s. 4 thereof in its application to Subordinate Judiciary would be ultra vires to arts. 233 and 234 of the Constitution - Held, State Legislature had no role to play while controlling appointments of District Judges u/art. 233 or appointment of Civil Judges to Subordinate Judiciary at grass-root level under District Judiciary and it was only Governor who was entrusted with said task which he had to undertake after consultation with HC and by framing appropriate rules for recruitment to Judiciary at grass-root level as enjoined by Art. 234 and could only act on recommendation by HC for direct recruitment from Bar for being appointed as District Judges as laid down by Art. 233 sub-art. (2) - There was no third method or third authority which could intervene in process, whether legislative authority or executive authority, as case might be - Hence, Such appointments u/s.4 of the Act, obviously would be null and void and violative of Art. 233 (2) - type of bypassing HC would clearly be act of interference with independence of judiciary which was hallmark and bedrock of the Constitutional scheme - Similarly, recruitment at district level judiciary was governed by 1951 Rules framed u/art. 233 r/w.art. 309 of the Constitution - However, direct recruitment as District Judges had to be solely based on appropriate recommendations of suitable candidates by HC - Appeals dismissed.

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s. 4 - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - (C) What appropriate orders could be passed in connection with impugned s. 4 of the Act - Held, therefore, appointments of candidates as per 1955 Rules to posts of Subordinate Judges and Munsiffs were on anvil of consideration and s. 4 of impugned Act did not apply to these recruitments and scheme of reservation of 14 percent for SCs and 10 percent for STs only would apply to such recruitment - Resulted in question of filling up of reserved posts in instant case would remain germane to aforesaid extent of permissible reservation of 24 percent for SC and ST candidates - Therefore, concerned authorities would work out rights of selected candidates for being appointed to these posts governed by 1955, Rules - Hence, modified HC's order and allowed partly - Appeals dismissed.

Pattanaik,J.

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s.4 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Act 11 of 1993 - Judiciary - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - A.) Whether State Legislature could enact law in exercise of their powers u/art. 309 of the Constitution in relation to recruitment and laying down conditions of service of officers belonging to Judicial Services of State was challenged - Held, art. 309 deals with recruitment and conditions of service of persons serving Union or State, particular category of post forming judicial wing had been carved out in Chapter VI in arts. 233 to 235 - When art. 309 itself uses expression subject to provisions of constitution it necessarily means that if in Constitution there was any other provision specifically dealing with topics mentioned in said art. 309, then art.309 would be subject to those provisions of the Constitution - State legislature undoubtedly could make law for regulating conditions of services of officers belonging to judicial wing but could not make law dealing with recruitment to judicial services since field of recruitment to judicial service was carved out in the Constitution itself in Chapter VI under arts. 233 to 236 of the Constitution - Language of art. 233 indicates that entire matter of recruitment to post of District Judge, either by way of direct recruitment or by promotion was left to HC and it was Governor of State who was required to make such appointment in consultation with HC

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4) , 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s. 4 - Bihar Act 3 of 1992 as amended by Bihar Act 11 of 1993 - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - B.) Whether provisions of Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991 as amended by Bihar Act 11 of 1993, providing reservation to extent mentioned in s. 4 would apply to Judicial Services of State - Held, plain and grammatical meaning of words used in S. 4 quoted unequivocally indicates, that it was law relating to recruitment/appointment and as such once, it was held that power of recruitment in respect of Judicial Services was provided for in art.234, State Legislature in garb of making law in consonance with art. 16(4) could not encroach upon art. 234 - So far as Superior Judicial Service was concerned, it was of course true that there had been no provision for reservation - But such provision could always be made by Governor in consultation with HC, bearing in mind mandate of art. 335, namely Maintenance of Efficiency of Administration - Hence, Court opined provisions of Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Sevices for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991 had no application to recruitment of judicial officers in State of Bihar - Appeals dismissed.

Banerjee, J.

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4), 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s.4 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Act 11 of 1993 - Separation of Powers - Judiciary - Legislature - Appointment of Judges - Reservation - Maintainability - Whether Judicial Service comes within the ambit of art. 309 so as to clothe State legislature with power of legislation - Held, Chapter V of the Constitution deals with HC in State, Chapter VI deals with Subordinate Courts, scheme of the Constitution thus, was categorical enough to depict judiciary as specific class by itself being an independent third wing of democratic polity and appointment of district judges though conferred in terms of arts. 233 of the Constitution - Governor of State but in 'Consultation with HC exercising jurisdiction in relation to such State' had been inserted in order to obviate any controversy as to efficiency of officers who were to be promoted to rank of district judge in Higher Judicial Service from post of subordinate Judge - Further, incorporation of arts. 234 and 235 and on plain reading thereof would leave no manner of doubt as to separate categorization of judicial officers exclusive to themselves and their appointment independent of art. 309 -Thus, if art. 309 was subject to be general provision, arts. 233 to 235 ought to be treated as specific provisions for appointment of judicial officers and by reason there for, specific field of legislation thus stands completed and obviously framers of the Constitution had provided arts. 233 to 235, introduced in art.309 - Hence, appointment of district judges, rests with two constitutional functionaries namely, Governor and HC and thus withdrawing same from purview of general power as conferred u/art. 309 - Appeals dismissed.

Sethi, J

Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 (4), 233 to 235, 245, 309 - Bihar Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1955 - Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes Act, 1991, s.4 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1951 - Bihar Act 11 of 1993 - Applicability of Reservation - Appointment of Judges - Challenged - Whether Governor possess legislative power to enact impugned ordinance - Held, relied on B.S. Yadav & Ors, etc.v. State of Haryana & Ors., etc. 1980 Indlaw SC 220 Court considered scope and extent of arts. 235 and 309 of the Constitution and held that power to frame rules regarding the judicial officers vested in Governor and not in HC - Governor possesses legislative power under Constitution was incontrovertible and, therefore, there was nothing unique about Governors power under proviso to art. 309 being in nature of legislative power - U/art.168, Governor was part of legislature of State and to exercise legislative power was given by art.213 to promulgate ordinances when legislature was not in session - Chapter IV of Part VI of the Constitution, in which art.213 occurs, was significant 'Legislative Power of the Governor' - Power of Governor under proviso to art. 309 to make appropriate rules of same kind and it was legislative power - Art. 213, he substitutes for the legislature because legislature was in recess - It was legislative power - Appeals dismissed.
