New India Assurance Company Limited v Rula and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 346; (2000) 3 SCC 42; (2000) SCC (Cr) 601; 2000 (2) ACC 751; AIR 2000 SC 1082; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 75; 2000 (39) ALR 227; 2000 (2) AWC 1358; 2001 (1) CGLJ 201; 2000 (2) CLT 52; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 711; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1219; JT 2000 (3) SC 37; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 167; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 440; 2000 (125) PLR 765; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 391; 2000(2) SCALE 255; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 148; 2000 (2) SLT 535; 2000 (2) Supreme 158

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 2(d) - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64-VB - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 146(1), 147(5) and 149 - Whether in case cheque through which premium was paid was dishonoured, would affect rights of third party and would such third party have a claim against Insurance Company and owner of vehicle? - Held, subsequent cancellation of insurance policy on ground that cheque through which premium was paid was dishonoured, would not affect rights of third party which had accrued on issuance of policy on date on which accident took place - If, on date of accident, there was a policy of insurance in respect of vehicle in question, third party would have a claim against Insurance Company and owner of vehicle would have to be indemnified in respect of claim of that party - Whether premium has been paid or not is not concern of third party who is concerned with fact that there was a policy issued in respect of vehicle involved in accident and it is on basis of this policy that claim can be maintained by third party against insurer? - Appeals dismissed.
