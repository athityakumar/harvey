R. Rathinam v State By Dsp, District Crime Branch, Madurai District, Madurai and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 392; (2000) 2 SCC 391; (2000) SCC (Cr) 958; AIR 2000 SC 1851; AIR 2000 SCW 423; 2000 (1) CCR 203; 2000 (1) Crimes 211; JT 2000 (1) SC 604; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 758; 2000(1) SCALE 458; 2000 (2) SCJ 249; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 718; 2000 (2) SLT 101; 2000 (1) Supreme 431

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 225 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 439 - Petition for cancellation of bail - S. 439 provides that it is power vested in High Court to cancel bail to be invoked either by State or aggrieved party or even suo motu by it - Therefore, any members of public belonging to any profession, who has a concern in matter can move to HC to remind it of need to invoke said power suo motu - If HC considers that there is no need to cancel bail for reasons stated in such petition, it may dismiss petition but not otherwise - Held, it is improper to refuse to look into matter on premise that such a petition filed by advocates who have nothing to do with case is not maintainable in law.
