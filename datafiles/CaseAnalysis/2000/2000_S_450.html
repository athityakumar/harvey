Satpal and Another v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 566; (2000) 5 SCC 170; (2000) SCC (Cr) 920; AIR 2000 SC 1702; 2000 (2) CCR 128; 2000 (2) Crimes 239; 2000 CRLJ 2297; 2000 (2) JCC 571; JT 2000 (5) SC 566; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 757; 2000(4) SCALE 135; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 858; 2000 (4) SLT 408; 2000 (3) Supreme 631

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India,1950, art. 161, Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s. 432 - Pardon - Power of Governor to grant pardon is amenable to judicial review on limited grounds - In present case, order granting Pardon remitting unexpired period of sentence was passed even before accused had surrendered to serve out sentence - Governor shown to have excercised his power with uncanny haste and without application of his mind - Held, mechanically passed order just to allow prisoner to overcome conviction and sentence passed by Court - Order granting pardon was liable to be set aside.
