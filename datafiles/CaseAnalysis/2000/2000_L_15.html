Santakumari and Others v Lakshmi Amma Janaki Amma (D) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 702; (2000) 7 SCC 60; AIR 2000 SC 3009; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 79; 2000 (41) ALR 36; JT 2000 (9) SC 233; 2000 (3) MLJ(SC) 188; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 281; 2000 (91) RD 678; 2000(5) SCALE 531; 2000 (3) SCJ 206; 2000 (6) SLT 157; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 353; 2000 (5) Supreme 538

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, s. 100 - Transfer of Property Act 1882, ss. 50 and 8 - Substantial question of law was raised - Question was whether the transaction was sale or mortgage by conditional sale - Construction of documents - Vendor executing sale to get back property sold in execution - Purchaser on same day executing agreement to sell property to close relative of vendor - Both documents executed and registered simultaneously one after other - Held, it is clear that intention was to reconvey property to vendor - Transaction was mortgage by conditional sale.
