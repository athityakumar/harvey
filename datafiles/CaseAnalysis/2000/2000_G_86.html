T. Sudhakar Prasad v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3071; (2001) SCC (L&S) 263; (2001) 1 SCC 516; AIR 2000 SCW 4611; 2001 (1) CompLJ 270; JT 2001 (1) SC 204; 2001 (3) LLJ 1223; 2001 (1) LLN 829; 2000(8) SCALE 366; 2001 (1) SLT 73; 2001 (121) STC 365; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 610; 2000 (8) Supreme 536; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 588

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 7 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 323A and 323B - (A) Whether Administrative Tribunals under Act have power to punish for their contempt? - (B) Whether s. 17 of Act is being rendered unconstitutional and otiose after decision of Court in 'L Chandra Kumar vs Union of India'? - Administrative Tribunals as established under art. 323-A and Administrative Tribunals Act are an alternative institutional mechanism or authority, designed to be not less effective than High Court, but at same time not to negate judicial review jurisdiction of constitutional courts - Transfer of jurisdiction in specified matters from HC to Administrative Tribunal equates Tribunal with HC in so far as exercise of judicial authority over specified matters is concerned - In L Chandra's case this Court framed three broad issues for its consideration and proceeded to consider constitutional validity of arts. 323-A and 323-B and several provisions of Administrative Tribunals Act - Relevant extract quoted and perused - (A) Constitution Bench in L Chandra's case has not declared provisions of art. 323-A(2)(b) or art. 323-B(3)(d) or s. 17 of Administrative Tribunals Act ultra vires Constitution - There is no anathema in Tribunal exercising jurisdiction of HC and in that sense being supplemental or additional to HC but at same time not enjoying status equivalent to HC and also being subject to judicial review and judicial superintendence of HC - (B) Held, Administrative Tribunals under Administrative Tribunals Act has power to punish for their contempt - Impugned judgement of HC set aside - Appeals disposed of.
