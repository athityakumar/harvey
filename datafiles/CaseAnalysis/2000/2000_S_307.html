State Of West Bengal v Mir Mohammad Omar And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2834; (2000) 8 SCC 382; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1516; AIR 2000 SC 2988; 2000 (41) AllCriC 598; 2000 CRLJ 4047; JT 2000 (9) SC 467; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 147; 2000(6) SCALE 148; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal- IPC, 1860, ss. 300 and 364 - Evidence Act, 1872, s . 45 - CrPC, 1973, s. 156 - Abduction - For murder - Death - (A) Victim repeatedly chased and dragged away - Abductors' words and temper attribute that abduction is for finish off - Same also quoted in FIR by informer without knowledege of death - Whether prove guilt - (B) Identity of corpus delicti - Identified by relatives - Wrong opinion on age and religion (circumcision of penis) by Doctor who first examined - Differed by Docter who conducted postmortem - Plea, identity not established - (C) 'Injury sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death' - Omission to put this question to doctor who conducted post- mortem examination - (D) Murder - Appreciation of evidence - Presumption that accused murdered the victim - (E) Investigation - Irregularity - Remark by HC against IO - Held, accused were guilty of abducting victim in order to murder him - Cannot be allowed - Not enough for Court to reach wrong conclusion - Accused convicted u/s. 300 of IPC - No such particular omission or lacuna referred - Appeal Allowed.
