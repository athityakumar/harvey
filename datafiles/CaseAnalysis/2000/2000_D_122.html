Subedar Singh and Others v Distt. Judge, Mirzapur and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2784; (2001) 1 SCC 37; (2001) SCC (L&S) 141; AIR 2001 SC 201; 2001 (1) AWC 287; 2000 (87) FLR 941; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 628; 2001 LabIC 64; 2001 (1) LLJ 5; 2000(7) SCALE 417; 2000 (5) SLR 792; 2000 (8) SLT 283; 2000 (8) Supreme 6; 2001 (1) UC 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Government of India Act, General Rules (Civil)

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 309 - Government of India Act, ss. 241(1)(b) and 241(2)(b) - General Rules (Civil), r. 269 - Appointment on ad hoc basis - Regularisation - Whether appointment of appellants made as 'paid apprentice' by District Judge, not in consonance with statutory rules be challenged by appointees under regularisation of their services? - Held, when appointment to posts in question is governed by a set of statutory rules, it is unthinkable that District Judge would adopt this extra-constitutional method of appointment and that also, by maneuvering and by deputing copyists to do some other job and replace them by fresh recruits - So-called Regularisation Rules, does not intend to regularise services of illegal and irregular recruits like appellants - No rule, law or regulation, nor even any administrative order had been shown on basis of which appellants could claim right of regularisation - Appeal dismissed.
