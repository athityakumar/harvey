Narmada Bachao Andolan Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3658; (2000) 10 SCC 664; AIR 2000 SC 3751; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 434; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 6; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 94

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Water

Keywords: Water Pollution, Forest Conservation, Constituent Assembly, Environmental Damage, Endangered Species, Environmental Appraisal Committee, Global Warming, The High Courts In The States, Civil Amenities, Sustainable Development, Water Logging, Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Justiciability, Afforestation, Dams, Degradation, Displacement, Environmental Disruption, Environmental Policy, Forest Loss, Greenhouse Effect, Hydroelectric Power, Irrigation, Narmada Valley, Natural Resources, Water Pollution Control, Opinion given accordingly.

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Inter - State Water Disputes Act, 1956, ss. 2(c), 3, 4, 5(1) and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 262 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - PIL - Sardar Sarovar project - Construction of dam - Agitation - Held, when projects are undertaken and hundreds of crores of public money is spent, PIL cannot be permitted to challenge policy decision taken after a lapse of time - Further held, writ being filed in 1994 petitioner who had been agitating against dam since 1986 is guilty of latches in not approaching Court at an earlier point of time - (A) Whether petitioner being third party is competent to question correctness of award of tribunal? - Further held, once award is binding on States, it will not be open to a third party like petitioners to challenge correctness thereof - (B) Displacement of tribals - Further held, displacement of tribals and other persons would not per se result in violation of their fundamental or other rights - Effect is to see that on their rehabilitation at new locations they are better off than what they were - (C) Environmental clearance - Further held, environmental clearance of project was given with proper application of mind - (E) Whether construction of dam would result in an ecological? - Further held, dam is neither a nuclear establishment nor a polluting industry - Construction of a large dam is not cost effective or leads to ecological or environmental degradation - Directions issued for construction of dam, rehabilitation - Order accordingly.
