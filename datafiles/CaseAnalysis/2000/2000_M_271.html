Mohd. Aslam v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3695; (2001) 9 SCC 362; JT 2000 (8) SC 104; 2004(2) SCALE 242

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1973

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 5 and 15 - Indian Penal Code, 1860,s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Arms Act, s. 25 - Appellants (A1 and A7) convicted by designated court - Whether A7 was person who shot dead deceased? - Held, in dying declaration deceased did not mention identity of assailant but mentioned that he was with PW3 and on statement of PW3, A7 was arrested and correctly identified in test identification parade - Evidence of PW3 was further supported by another eye-witness - Safe to conclude that A7 was assailant - Recovery of arms and ammunition effected from house of A1 and nothing was done to disprove it - Appeal disposed of.
