Balbir Singh v Punjab Roadways and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2942; (2001) 1 SCC 133; (2001) SCC (L&S) 165; 2001 (1) CLR 788; 2001 (88) FLR 351; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 602; 2001 (1) LLJ 476; 2001 (2) LLN 118; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; 2000(8) SCALE 180; 2000 (6) SLR 469; 2001 (1) SLT 28; 2000 (8) Supreme 236; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 331

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Petitioner was a conductor in Punjab Roadways - Several orders of punishment of stoppage of increment, with or without cumulative effects were passed against him in June 1978, October 1983, April 1987, November 1990 and May 1991 - Industrial dispute - Tribunal declined to grant relief on ground of delay - Held, whether relief to workman should be denied on ground of delay or it should be appropriately moulded is at discretion of Tribunal depending on facts and circumstances of case - Discretion is to be exercised judicially - High Court on consideration of matter, held that there was no ground to interfere with discretion exercised by Tribunal - No serious illegality which warrants interference - Special leave petition dismissed.
