Life Insurance Corporation of India and Others v Asha Goel Smt. and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2720; (2001) 2 SCC 160; 2001 ACJ 806; AIR 2001 SC 549; 2001 (42) ALR 485; 2001 (2) Bom.C.R. 791; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 79; 2012 (3) CPJ(Sc) 5; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 1990; JT 2001 (1) SC 10; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 347; 2000(8) SCALE 320; 2001 (1) SCJ 291; 2001 (1) SLT 89; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 646; 2000 (8) Supreme 521

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Ailment

Summary: Insurance - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Insurance Act, 1938 - Whether High Court should not ordinarily entertain writ petition filed u/art. 226 of Constitution for mere enforcement of a claim under a contract of insurance? - Held, yes, HC has to consider facts and circumstances of the case, nature of the dispute raised and the nature of the inquiry necessary to be made for determination of the questions raised and other relevant factors before taking a decision whether it should entertain the writ petition or reject it as not maintainable - Appeal disposed of.
