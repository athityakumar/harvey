 Kolhapur Canesugar Works Limited and Another Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal, V.N. Khare, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 39; (2000) 2 SCC 536; AIR 2000 SC 811; 2001 (1) CalLT(SC) 18; 2000 (89) ECR 22; 2000 (119) E.L.T. 257; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2000 (1) SC 453; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 141; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 674; 2000 (37) RLT 1; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Holding Company, Enactment, Defence of India Act, 1962, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1970, Customs,Central Excises and Salt and Central Boards of Revenue(Amendment)Act, 1978

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 3(51) - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1970 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10 and 10 - A - Whether after omission of old rr. 10 and 10 - A and its substitution by new r. 10 by Notification No. 267/77 D/- 06-08-1977 proceedings initiated by notice D/- 27-04-1977 could be continued in law? - If a provision of a statute is unconditionally omitted without a saving clause in favour of pending proceedings, all actions must stop where omission finds them, and if final relief has not been granted before omission goes into effect, it cannot be granted afterwards - R. 10 or r. 10 - A is neither a 'Central Act' nor a 'Regulation' as defined in Act - Hence, s. 6 of Act, 1897 is not applicable in case of omission of a 'Rule' - It did not contain any saving clause for continuance of proceeding initiated under rule which was deleted/omitted - Held, proceeding lapsed after 06-08-1977 and any order passed in proceeding thereafter is to be treated as non-est - Petitions disposed of.
