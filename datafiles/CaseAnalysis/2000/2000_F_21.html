Hernek Singh v Financial Commr., Appeals Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 912; (2001) 9 SCC 480; AIR 2003 SC 2151; JT 2001 (2) SC 232; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 127; 2001(7) SCALE 7; 2001 (7) SLT 125; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 429; 2001 (7) Supreme 703

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Pepsu Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1955, s. 32-E - Punjab Land Reforms Act, 1972, ss. 8, 11 and 28 - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953 - Appellant claims to be legatee of owner of land in question - Respondents 5 to 10 filed application praying for delivery of possession of land in question on ground that same was allotted to them on 22-9-1980 - Allotted - Challenged by appellant - Present appeal - Held, provisions of Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953 and PEPSU Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1955 insofar, as they were inconsistent with provisions of Land Reforms Act were repealed - Surplus area so determined would vest in and can be utilised by State Govt. in accordance with provisions of Land Reforms Act - Vesting of land is pre-requisite of framing of scheme of a particular land and its allotment and without taking of possession, there cannot be a vesting of land in State Govt. - In view of this averment it cannot be legitimately contended that land had vested in State Govt. and therefore allottees had any right in respect of land which is said to have been allotted on 22-09-1980 - Allotment of land in respect of respondents 5 to 10 is, therefore, of no legal sanctity and is bad in law - Appeal allowed.
