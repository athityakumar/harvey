Ragavendra Kumar v Firm Prem Machinery and Company

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 555; (2000) 1 SCC 679; AIR 2000 SC 534; 2000 (38) ALR 458; JT 2000 (1) SC 61; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 50; 2000(1) M.P.H.T. 501; 2000 (1) RCJ 203; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 135; 2000(1) SCALE 49; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 77; 2000 (1) SLT 211; 2000 (1) Supreme 48

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, s. 12(1)(f) - Plaintiff - landlord who is appellant herein filed a suit u/s. 12(1)(f) of Act for eviction of defendant tenant who is respondent herein, on ground of bona fide requirement as he required suit premises for opening a showroom - Trial court came to finding that plaintiff landlord was in bona fide need of disputed premises - Lower appellate court after considering evidence on record upheld above finding of trial court - Whether High Court in second appeal erred in law by setting aside concurrent finding of fact of courts below by reappreciating evidence on record? - Held, in case in hand plaintiff landlord wanted eviction of tenant from suit premises for starting his business as it was suitable and it cannot be faulted - Learned Single Judge also erred in law in holding that lower appellate court wrongly placed onus on defendant tenant - Above view was expressed by appellate court after holding that on preliminary documents and evidence produced before courts below it was evident that disputed shop was required by plaintiff landlord for bona fide need - On going through judgment of lower appellate court we find that appellate court decided appeal on preponderance of evidence, not on basis of burden of proof - In case in hand nothing has been brought to our notice that courts below did not consider relevant evidence on record or approach to matter was wrong - HC in second appeal erred in law by setting aside concurrent findings of facts of courts below by reappreciating entire evidence on record - Appeal allowed.
