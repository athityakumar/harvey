State of Karnataka v Krishnappa

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 237; (2000) 4 SCC 75; (2000) SCC (Cr) 755; AIR 2000 SC 1470; 2000 (2) Crimes 50; 2000 CRLJ 1793; 2000 (1) JCC 372; JT 2000 (3) SC 516; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 459; 2000(2) SCALE 60; 2000(2) SCALE 610; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 791; 2000 (3) SLT 228; 2000 (2) Supreme 611

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 254, 323, 341, 363, 376, 448 and 506 - Reduction in sentence - Justifiability - Trial Court convicted respondent for an offence u/s. 376 of IPC and u/ss. 254, 323, 341, 363, 448 and 506 of IPC - Trial Court found that the respondent himself, a married man with children, at the relevant time aged about 49 years, had in the first place misbehaved with the mother of the prosecutrix in the manner deposed to by her and had also committed rape on the prosecutrix, a little child of 7/8 years of age - Trial Court found that the prosecution had successfully established that respondent had committed various offences alleged against him and convicted him - Trial Court imposed a sentence of 10 years RI and a fine - On appeal, DB of HC, confirmed the conviction - However, HC held that it was a case 'for showing leniency' to the respondent (accused) in the matter of punishment because the accused was '49 years of age' and 'at the time of occurrence, he had an old mother, wife and children to look after - Sentence for the offence u/s. 376 of IPC was reduced from 10 years RI to 4 years RI - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified, in the facts and circumstances of the case, to reduce the sentence from 10 years RI to 4 years RI - Held, legislative mandate was to impose sentence, for the offence of rape on a girl under 12 years of age, for a term which should not be less than 10 years, but it could extend to life and also to fine - Though in exceptional cases 'for special and adequate reasons' sentence of less than 10 years RI could also be awarded - Further, measure of punishment in a case of rape could not depend upon the social status of the victim or the accused - It should depend upon the conduct of the accused, the state and age of the sexually assaulted female and the gravity of the criminal act - In the instant case, the Trial Court gave sufficient and cogent reasons for imposing the sentence of 10 years RI for the offence u/s. 376 of IPC on the respondent - Approach of the HC, was casual and inappropriate - There were no good reasons given by the HC to reduce the sentence - HC exhibited lack of sensitivity towards the victim of rape and the society by reducing the substantive sentence - Hence, sentence of 4 years RI, as imposed by the HC, enhanced to 10 years RI - Order accordingly.
