Kshetriya Kisan Gramin Bank v D. B. Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2775; (2001) 1 SCC 353; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1000; AIR 2001 SC 168; 2001 (1) AWC 279; 2001 (88) FLR 28; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 596; 2001 LabIC 56; 2001 (1) LLJ 10; 2001 (1) LLN 24; 2000(7) SCALE 489; 2000 (3) SCJ 242; 2000 (5) SLR 770; 2000 (8) SLT 269; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 638; 2000 (8) Supreme 29

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, ss. 3 and 17(1) - Appellant regional rural bank, established u/s. 3 - (A) Parity in scale - Violation of art. 14 - Discrimination - Held, even a single institution can form a class by itself and while deciding question of violation of art. 14 what is necessary is that there must be a nexus between basis of classification and object of such classification - Tribunal did not apply principle of 'equal pay for equal work' and on other hand applied principle of parity with officers of respective sponsor banks - Tribunal having specifically held that employees of regional rural banks will be entitled to claim parity with officers and other employees of sponsor banks ultimate decision of Equation Committee on basis of such parity, cannot be held to be discriminatory nor can it be held to be violative of art. 14 - (B) Equation made by Equation Committee - Whether proper? - Held, no infirmity with equation made by Equation Committee on basis of pay structure of employees of respective sponsor banks and same is in consonance with directions of tribunal as well as second proviso to sub-s. (1) of s. 17 - (C) What extent HC would be justified in exercise of its extraordinary jurisdiction u/art. 226 to interfere with findings of an expert body like Equation Committee? - Held, Equation Committee consisting of specialised personnel having examined relevant data and having made equation with their expertise same could not have been interfered with by HC, particularly when neither there has been any allegation of malice or extraneous consideration nor any materials on that score were there before Court - Appeal allowed.
