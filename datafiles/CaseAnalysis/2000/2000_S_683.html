Gura Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2738; (2001) 2 SCC 205; (2001) SCC (Cr) 323; AIR 2001 SC 330; AIR 2000 SCW 4439; 2001 (1) CCR 12; 2001 (1) Crimes 34; 2001 CRLJ 487; 2001 (1) JCC 64; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 528; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 122; 2000(8) SCALE 147; 2000 (8) SLT 656; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 408; 2000 (8) Supreme 402; 2001 (1) UC 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Criminal Procedure Act, 1865

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 25, 26, 142 and 154 - Murder - Extra-judicial confession - Appellant murdered his father and later on made extra judicial confessions to some witnesses - FIR was lodged and appellant arrested - Weapon of offence and blood stained chaddar recovered at instance of appellant - Trial Court convicted appellant and appeal against conviction dismissed by High Court - Held, extra judicial confessions can be made basis of conviction even if uncorroborated if it inspires confidence - Prosecution proved case beyond reasonable doubt - Appeal dismissed.
