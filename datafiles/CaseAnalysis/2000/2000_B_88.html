R. N. Dey and Others v Bhagyabati Pramanik and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 278; (2000) 4 SCC 400; 2000 (2) AWC 1600; 2000 (2) CLT 148; JT 2000 (4) SC 629; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 727; 2000(3) SCALE 365; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 172; 2000 (4) SLT 20; 2000 (3) Supreme 363; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1754

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Contempt - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Estates Acquisition Act, 1953 - (A) Whether contempt proceedings warranted against officers of State Govt. for not making payment of compensation to claimants in terms of Court's order? - (B) Appeal against same whether maintainable? - Held, (A) Contempt cannot be used for execution of decree or implementation of an order for which alternative remedy in law is provided for - Under coercion of contempt proceeding, appellants cannot be directed to pay compensation amount which they are disputing by asserting that decree was obtained by claimants by suppressing material fact and by fraud - In land acquisition cases when a decree is passed, State was in position of a judgment - Debtor and court should not normally lend help to a party who refuses to take legally provided steps for executing decree - Facts of this case are grossly inadequate and contempt proceedings were not required to be initiated at all - (B) Exercise of jurisdiction to punish for contempt commences with initiation of a proceeding for contempt and if order is passed not discharging rule issued in contempt proceedings, it would be an order or decision in exercise of its jurisdiction to punish for contempt and against such order, appeal would be maintainable - Appeal allowed.
