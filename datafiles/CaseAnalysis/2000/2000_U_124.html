Krishna Mohan Shukla v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 819; (2000) 2 SCC 690; JT 2000 (1) SC 447; 2000(4) M.P.H.T. 158; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 350; 2000(1) SCALE 340; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 384

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 136, 226, 227 - Bhopal Gas Tragedy - Relief Tribunal - Compensation - Arbitrary Functioning - Petitioner alleged defective medical categorization, preparation of illegal compensation and categorization list and holding of Lok-adalats which were illegal - Petitioner further alleged that Deputy Commissioners adopted and formulated their own yardsticks, defective medical facilities and non-functioning of hospitals - Whether Petitioner was entitled to any relief in WP filed - Held, orders passed by Deputy Commissioner was to be appealed with Welfare Commissioner who was a sitting HC Judge - Petitioner further had right of Judicial review provided by arts.226, 227 of Constitution - Petitioners proper remedy was to approach HC rather than SC u/arts. 32 or 136 of Constitution - Allegations regarding lack of medical facilities and non-functioning of hospitals were converted into WP - Petitioner was permitted to withdraw petition with liberty to file fresh petition with all necessary details - All matters were transferred to HC to be treated as Petitions u/arts. 226, 227 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
