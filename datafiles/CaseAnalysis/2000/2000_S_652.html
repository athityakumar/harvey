Praveen Singh v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2787; (2000) 8 SCC 633; AIR 2001 SC 152; 2001 (1) AWC 293; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 526; 2001 LabIC 47; 2000(7) SCALE 452; 2001 (2) SLJ 275; 2001 (1) SLR 229; 2000 (8) SLT 286; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 490; 2000 (7) Supreme 464; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 533

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Development and Panchayat Class II (Service) Rules, 1974

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 136 - Punjab Development and Panchayat Class II (Service) Rules, 1974 - Selection - Shortlisting of candidates - Constitutionality of Punjab Development and Panchayat Class II (Service) Rules challenged - Relied on J P Kulshrestha (Dr) v Chancellor, Allahabad University 1980 Indlaw SC 309 - Held, selection arbitrary and unreasonable - Public Service Commission directed to take both written and viva voce in consideration for effective appointment - Further held, appointment made shall continue subject to further result declared by Public Service Commission for Block dev and Panchayat Officers - Appeal allowed.
