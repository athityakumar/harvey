 State of Maharashtra v Milind and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2635; (2001) 1 SCC 4; (2001) SCC (L&S) 117; AIR 2001 SC 393; AIR 2000 SCW 4303; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 620; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 213; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 29; 2000(7) SCALE 628; 2000 (6) SLR 301; 2000 (8) SLT 525; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 65; 2000 (8) Supreme 429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights

Summary: Constitution,of India, 1950, arts. 136, 162, 256, 341, 342 and 339(2) - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - (A) Whether 'Halba-Koshti' caste is a sub-tribe within the meaning of Entry 19 (Halba/Halbi) of the STs Order relating to the State of Maharashtra, even though it is not specifically mentioned as such? - Held, committee conducted inquiry and appellate authority examined relevant materials shows that 'Kosthi' has no identity with 'Halba' the ST under entry 19 of Presidential order - (B) Whether it is permissible to hold inquiry and let in evidence to decide or declare that any tribe or tribal community or part of or group within any tribe or tribal community is included in the general name even though it is not specifically mentioned in the entry concerned in the Constitution (STs) Order, 1950? - Relied on Parsram v. Shivchand 1968 Indlaw SC 335, Basavalingappa 1964 Indlaw SC 332 and Bhaiya Lal 1965 Indlaw SC 385 - Held, no inquiry is permissible and no evidence let in to find out within scope and meaning of entry concerned in the presidential order when it is not specifically included - Further held, not open to state Govt. or courts or tribunals or any other authority to modify, amend or alter the list of STs specified in the notification issued u/cl. 342(1) - Appeal allowed without any costs.
