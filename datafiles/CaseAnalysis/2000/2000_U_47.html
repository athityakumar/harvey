Saurashtra Cement And Chemical Industries Limited And Anr v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2811; (2001) 1 SCC 91; AIR 2001 SC 8; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 1899; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 160; 2000(7) SCALE 230; 2000 (7) SLT 612; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 44; 2000 (7) Supreme 220B

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952 - Madras Panchayats Act, 1958 - Orissa Rural Employment, Education and Production Act, 1992 - Whether Union Legislature have powers under Entry 54 of List I to enact such a law which denudes right of State Legislature to levy tax on mineral rights under Entry 50 of List II? - Held, effect of reading two entries together is clear jurisdiction of sate legislature under Entry 23 is subject to limitation imposed by latter part of said entry - If Parliament by its law has declared that regulation and development of mines should in public interest be under control of Union, to extent of such declaration jurisdiction of State Legislature is excluded - In other words, if a Central Act has been passed which contains a declaration by Parliament as required by Entry 54, and if said declaration covers field occupied by impugned Act, impugned Act would be ultra vires, not because of any repugnance between two statutes but because state legislature had no jurisdiction to pass law - Appeal dismissed.
