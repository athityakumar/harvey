P. P. Unnikrishnan and Another v Puttiyottil Alikutty and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2657; (2000) 8 SCC 131; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1460; AIR 2000 SC 2952; AIR 2000 SCW 3223; 2000 CRLJ 4041; JT 2000 (10) SC 116; 2000 (3) KLT 483; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 25; 2000(6) SCALE 276; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 142; 2001 (1) UC 15A

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bombay Police Act., Kerala Police Act, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Bombay Police Act, s. 161(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 197, 482, 473, 467 and 197 - Indian penal code, 1860, ss. 218, 325, 342, 330, 506 - Kerala Police Act, s. 64 - Police Act, 1861, s. 36 - Private complaint against appellants - Appellants objected to the cognizance taken by the magistrate in view of s. 64 of Kerala Police Act which bars taking cognizance after 6 months - Held, s. 64 is enacted to protect public servant sand police officers from frivolous prosecutions but in the present case there is no nexus between official duty and offences alleged - Appellants cannot be afforded the benefit of s. 64 of Kerala Police Act - Appeal dismissed.
