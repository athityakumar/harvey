Food Corporation of India v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2751; (2001) 1 SCC 291; AIR 2001 SC 250; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 376; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 409; 2000(8) SCALE 12; 2000 (8) SLT 757; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 276; 2000 (8) Supreme 423

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss. 51 to 68 - Whether order D/- 5-12-1991 amending assessment list in respect of its property by Municipal Committee, Sangrur in State of Punjab, valid and legal? - While vesting power in committee to amend an assessment list, legislature has taken care to specify circumstances in and grounds on which such amendment may be made, it has also laid down manner in which such amendment or revision of assessment list is to be made - Care has also been taken to comply with principle of natural justice by making provision for giving notice to person who is likely to be affected by proposed amendment giving him not less than a month's time to tender objection, if any, to committee and allowing him an opportunity of being heard in support of objections raised - Notice to affected person mandated in section is not an empty formality, it is meant for a purpose - A vague and unspecific notice will not provide reasonable opportunity to noticee to file objection meeting reasons/grounds on which amendment of assessment list is proposed to be made - Such a notice cannot be taken to be complying with statutory requirement - Held, on a perusal of notice issued to corporation, which is on record, it is evident that notice is vague and lacks particulars - It neither states reason for/or ground on which amendment is proposed to be made nor does it indicate any material on basis of which revision as stated in notice is proposed to be made - Such a notice not only does not comply with statutory requirements, it also defeats very purpose of statutory provisions - Order passed by assessing officer and confirmed by Appellate Authority and State Govt. are clearly unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
