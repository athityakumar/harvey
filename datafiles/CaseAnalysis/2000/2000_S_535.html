K. V. Chacko Alias Kunju v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3129; (2001) 9 SCC 277; (2002) SCC (Cr) 547; AIR 2001 SC 537; 2001 (1) Crimes 48; 2001 CRLJ 713; 2001 (1) JCC 39; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 615; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 139; 2000(8) SCALE 194; 2000 (8) Supreme 415; 2001 (1) UC 249

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 332, 324, 302, 397 and 201 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Appellant sentenced to death by High Court - Appellant murdered whole family and burnt them so as to show make it look like accident - Whether case of prosecution based on circumstantial evidence proved so as to warrant death sentence? - Circumstantial evidence in order to sustain conviction must also be complete and incapable of explanation of any other hypothesis than that of guilt of accused - Circumstantial evidence should not only be consistent with guilt of accused but should be inconsistent with his innocence - Held, test laid down in 'Gambhir vs State of Maharashtra', 1982 Indlaw SC 27 not proved which applies to circumstantial evidence cases - Many inconsistencies in prosecution case - Prosecution has failed to establish circumstances relied by it to establish guilt of appellant - Conviction and death sentence set-aside - Appeal allowed.
