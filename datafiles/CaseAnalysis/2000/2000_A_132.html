Amritlal and Others v Union Government Through Secretary, Ministry of Finance and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3136; (2001) SCC (Cr) 147; (2001) 1 SCC 341; AIR 2000 SC 3675; 2001 CRLJ 474; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 178; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 81; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 450; 2001 (1) UC 142

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8/18 - Detention order - Validity of - Whether detention orders passed by High Court was justified? - Held, Re Binod Singh v. District Magistrate, Dhanbad, (1986 Indlaw SC 784), it was held that if a person is in custody and there is no imminent possibility of his being released there from, power of detention should not ordinarily be exercised - There must be cogent materials before officer passing detention order that detenu is likely to be released on bail - The inference must be drawn from available material on record - Requirement as noticed in Binod Singh's case that there is 'likelihood of petitioners being released on bail' that however is not available in reasonings as provided by concerned officer - Reasoning available is 'likelihood of his moving an application for bail' which is different from 'likelihood to be released on bail' - This reasoning, not sufficient compliance with requirements as laid down - The emphasis that before passing detention order concerned authority must satisfy himself of likelihood of petitioner being released on bail and that satisfaction ought to be reached on cogent material - Available cogent material is likelihood of having a bail application moved in matter but not obtaining a bail order - Therefore order of detention quashed - Appeals disposed of.
