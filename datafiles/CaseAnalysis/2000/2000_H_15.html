Liaq Ahmed and Others v Habeeb-Ur-Rehman

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 314; (2000) 5 SCC 708; AIR 2000 SC 2470; JT 2000 (5) SC 611; 2000 (1) RCJ 609; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 484; 2000(4) SCALE 100; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 800; 2000 (4) SLT 148; 2000 (3) Supreme 626

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(e) - Petition for Eviction - Leave to contest - Application by tenant - In present case landlord claiming to be owner on basis of a sale deed - The claim of landlord was resisted by appellants on ground that impugned property is vested in Custodian of Enemy Properties for India which could not be alienated or sold to respondent - Landlord - But, Rent Controller negatived plea of appellants as taking property as non - evacuee property - Held, that Rent Controller failed to see distinction between Evacuee Property Act under which order D/- 30-03-1954 was passed and Enemy Property Act, 1968 regarding which letter was issued intimating that property, subject matter of litigation, had vested in Custodian of Enemy Properties for India - Question as to whether property had actually vested or not, consequence of its vesting or non - vesting and authenticity of sale deed relied upon by respondent, were questions which could be determined only at trial after appellants were granted leave to contest claim of respondent - landlord - Controller was obliged to grant leave and after giving opportunities to parties, adjudicate rival claims.
