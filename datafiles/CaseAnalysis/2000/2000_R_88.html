United India Insurance Company Limited v Rajendra Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 210; (2000) 3 SCC 581; (2000) SCC (Cr) 726; 2000 (1) ACC 484; AIR 2000 SC 1165; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 60; 2000 ALJ 849; 2001 (42) ALR 451; 2000 (2) AWC 1349; 2000 (2) CLT 25; [2000] 100 Comp Cas 705; JT 2000 (3) SC 151; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 181; 2000 (125) PLR 455; 2000 (125) PLR 787; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 483; 2000(2) SCALE 343; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 264; 2000 (3) SLT 1; 2000 (2) Supreme 294

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151, 152 and 153 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appellant insurance Co. - Rank fraud had been played by two claimants who wangled two separate awards from a Motor Accident Claims Tribunal for a bulk sum - Neither tribunal nor High Court of Allahabad, before which insurance Co. approached for annulling awards, opened door but expressed helplessness even to look into matter and hence insurance Co. has filed these appeals - (A) Whether a court has power to recall its own order? - Held, no court or tribunal can be regarded as powerless to recall its own order if it is convinced that order was wangled through fraud or misrepresentation of such a dimension as would affect very basis of claim - (B) Whether insurance Co. should be afforded an opportunity to substantiate their allegation of fraud? - Further held, claim must be allowed to be resisted, on ground of fraud now alleged by insurance Co. If no opportunity is afforded to insurance Co. to substantiate their contentions it might certainly lead to a serious miscarriage of justice - Appeal allowed.
