Kunal Nanda v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 284; (2000) 5 SCC 362; (2000) SCC (L&S) 705; AIR 2000 SC 2076; 2000 (2) CLR 774; JT 2000 (6) SC 574; 2000 LabIC 1992; 2000 (3) LLN 515; 2000(4) SCALE 34; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 214; 2000 (3) SLJ 336; 2000 (4) SLR 609; 2000 (5) SLT 148; 2000 (4) Supreme 388; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2171

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 136 - Appellant deputationist claiming absorption in Borrowing Dept. - False representation as to his educational qualification - Plea, he is a service candidate and therefore need not be graduate for absorption - Held, is not tenable - No vested right to deputationist - Making false representation by itself sufficient to deny his candidature.
