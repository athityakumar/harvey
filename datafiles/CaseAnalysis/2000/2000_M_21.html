R. Keshava v M. B. Prakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2723; (2001) 2 SCC 145; (2001) SCC (Cr) 289; AIR 2001 SC 301; 2001 (1) CCR 39; 2001 (1) Crimes 67; 2001 CRLJ 497; JT 2001 (1) SC 183; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 489; 2000(8) SCALE 336; 2001 (1) SLT 41; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 596; 2000 (8) Supreme 473

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Fema; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Person Concerned, Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities (Second Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Customs - Constitution - FEMA - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 3(1)(i) and 3(1) (iii) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Act, 1974, s. 3 - Smuggled Goods - Custom Duty - Detention - Challenged - Detnue was carried smuggled goods and said goods was confiscated by Respondent/Customs authority and detained detenu u/ss. 3(1)(i) and 3(1)(iii) of the 1962 Act and kept in custody in Central Prison and grounds of detention were served upon detenu in jail - Detenu was also informed that he could file representation against detention order to State Govt. or Central Govt. - Detenu made representation to Advisory Board and admittedly did not make any representation either to Govt. of India or State Govt. or any other authority and did not even request Advisory Board or jail authorities to forward his representation to any of Govts. or authority - Appellant filed writ petition on behalf of detune before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether detention made by respondent was in violation of Constitutional provisions and HC 's order was challenged - Held, detenu did not avail opportunity of making representation to appropriate Govt. or confirming authority and chose to address representation to Advisory Board alone - Therefore, appropriate Govt. was justified in confirming order of detention on perusal of record and documents excluding representation made by detenu to Advisory Board - For alleged failure of appropriate Govt, order of detention of appropriate Govt. was neither rendered Constitutional nor illegal - Thus, Court, satisfied that order of confirmation was passed by appropriate Govt. after perusal of whole record available with it and such power was not mechanically exercised as alleged and order of detention and its confirmation appears to had been based upon subjective satisfaction arrived at by objective considerations with reference to all record pertaining to matters relating to circumstances warranting detention of detenu - Hence, Court did not found any error of law or jurisdiction in order of HC , detaining authority and confirming authority - Appeal dismissed.
