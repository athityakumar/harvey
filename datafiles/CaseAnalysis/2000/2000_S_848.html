Vadivelu v Sundaram and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2816; (2000) 8 SCC 355; AIR 2000 SC 3230; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 408; 2000(6) SCALE 719; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 642; 2001 (1) UC 94

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Tamil Nadu Panchayats (Elections) Rules, 1995, r. 122 - Irregularities in Counting of Votes - Appellant filed an election petition u/r. 122 of 1995 Rules challenging election of 1st respondent on ground of certain irregularities committed while counting of votes was made - Learned Single Judge held that Election Tribunal was not justified in ordering re-count of votes as appellant had not made out a prima facie ground for re-counting - Further he held, election petition is bereft of any material facts and only vague allegations have been made and appointment of Commr. for re-counting of votes was illegal and incorrect and, therefore, without jurisdiction - Revision petition was allowed - Sustainability of judgment passed by Single Judge? - Appellant-election petitioner could not make out a case for recount of votes - He filed application for re-count before Returning Officer only after declaration of result and that was rightly rejected by Returning Officer - Held, appellant had no case that illegality or irregularity, if any, committed had materially affected result of election - Taking all aspects into consideration, Courts are of view that ld Single Judge was perfectly justified in holding that Election Tribunal erred in appointing a Commr. and ordering the re-count of votes - Further held, when there is error of jurisdiction or flagrant violation of law laid down by apex Court, by exercising revisional powers, court can set aside order passed by Tribunal to do justice between parties - Illegality committed by Election Tribunal has been corrected by revisional order - No merit in case - Appeal dismissed.
