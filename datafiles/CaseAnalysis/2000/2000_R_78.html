Rajasthan State Warehousing Corporation v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3662; (2000) 3 SCC 126; AIR 2000 SC 972; 2000 (159) CTR 132; [2000] 242 ITR 450; JT 2000 (2) SC 373; 2000(2) SCALE 44; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1113; [2000] 109 TAXMAN 145

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Firm, Assessment, Reference, Counsel, Assessee, Order, Revenue, Charge, Court, Income Tax Act, 1922, State Government, Business, Gains, Rent, Will

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 256(1) - Charges for procurement - Indivisible Business - Expenditure - Deductions - Appellant derived its income from interest letting out warehouses and administrative charges for procurement of food grains and claimed deduction of expenditure - Respondent allowed expenditure allocated to taxable income and disallowed rest of it was referable to non-taxable income - HC affirmed action - Hence, instant appeal - Whether all ventures carried on by assessee was constituted as one indivisible business - Held, income from various ventures earned in course of one and indivisible business - Apportionment of expenditure and allowing deduction of only that proportion of it was referable to taxable income was unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
