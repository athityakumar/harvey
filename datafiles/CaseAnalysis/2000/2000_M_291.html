Monarch Infrastructure Private Limited v Commissioner, Ulhasnagar Municipal Corporation

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2846; (2000) 5 SCC 287; AIR 2000 SC 2272; 2000 (2) ARBLR 638; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 1; JT 2000 (6) SC 491; 2000(4) SCALE 446; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1159

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution, art. 14 - Govt. Contract - Collection of Octroi duty - Tenders invited - Stipulation, earnest money should be deposited by DD/ PO or cash - Rejection of offer as photocopy of DD duly notarised submitted - (A) Alteration in eligibility condition after receiving offers - (B) Rejection, if proper - (C) Receipt of offers - Earlier tender process set aside for waiver of eligibility condition - Authority while re-tendering altogether deleting that eligibility condition - Restoration of condition sought for - Held, entire process of tender has to be carried out afresh - Rejection of his offer, proper - Futher held that it would be naive to accept his plea that DD was misplaced in haste and confusion - Court refused to intercede to restore the condition - Appeal disposed of. .
