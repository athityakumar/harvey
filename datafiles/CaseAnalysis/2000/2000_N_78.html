Canara Bank and Others v National Thermal Power Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2693; (2001) 1 SCC 43; 2001 (1) BC 613; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 97; 2001 (72) CompLJ 224; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 491; 2000(8) SCALE 139; 2000 (8) SLT 696; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 387; 2000 (8) Supreme 210

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Body Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1971 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 - Mutual Fund Purchase by Govt. undertaking - Disputed arose and proceeding initiated in Company Law Board - High Court referred matter to High Powered Committee - Whether HC was justified in referring the matter to High Powered Committee? - Held, HC was not right in referring the alleged disputes to the High Powered Committee with the aid of the judgment in ONGC's case, (1995 (4) SCC 541) - Public undertakings should not spend Govt. money in frivolous litigation - It was under an obligation to give a finding with regard to the directions given by the Board to pay the redemption amount to the appellants - The trustees of the trust constituted by the Canara Bank as settler for the benefit of numerous units holders cannot be termed and styled as Govt. company or public sector undertaking - The dispute raised by the respondents with the appellant was imaginary and even prima facie not real - Further, the Board in its order had dealt with all aspects of the matter and rightly concluded that the ONGC judgment was not applicable in the facts and circumstances of the present case - Set aside the judgments of the HC and restoring the orders of the Board - Appeal allowed.
