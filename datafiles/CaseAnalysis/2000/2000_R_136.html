State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Ram Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 521; (2000) 5 SCC 88; (2000) SCC (Cr) 886; AIR 2000 SC 870; 2000 (1) CCR 167; 2000 (1) Crimes 245; 2000 CRLJ 1401; 2000 (1) JCC 299; JT 2000 (1) SC 518; 2000(1) M.P.H.T. 558; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 784; 2000(1) SCALE 396; 2000 (3) SCJ 373; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 579; 2000 (1) SLT 622; 2000 (1) Supreme 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 , ss. 5A(1), 5(e)(1) - Sources of income - Illegal - Search and seizure - Investigation - Corrupt practices - Respondent/Sub Inspector had acquired properties disproportionate sources of income - On verification it was found that respondent had earned movable and immovable properties allegedly much more disproportionate sources of income during check period - Entire search and seizure made by Special Police Establishment was illegal, malafide and without any basis - HC held that allegations made in complaint do not constitute a cognizable offence for commencing a lawful investigation and granted relief as prayed for by petitioner - HC quashing FIR as not being legally and factually sustainable in law - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any valid order of Superintendent of Police permitting appellant to investigate offence falling u/s.5(e)(1) of Act - Held, there was absolutely no reason given by SP in directing SHO to investigate, order of SP was directly in violation of dictum of law - Validity of objection as to violation of s.5(4) of Act - Nature and extent of violation and pass appropriate orders for such investigation wholly or partly, and by such officer as appropriate with reference to requirements of s. 5-A of Act - Superintendent of Police to direct authorization to order investigation - Investigation to have not been investigated by an authorized officer being not sustainable in law in accordance with provisions of law - HC should not have liberally construed provisions of Act in favour of accused resulting in closure of trial of serious charges made against respondents in relation to commission of offences punishable under an Act legislated to curb illegal and corrupt practices of public officers - Appeal allowed.
