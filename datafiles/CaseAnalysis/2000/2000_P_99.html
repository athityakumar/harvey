State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Pradeep Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 540; (2000) 7 SCC 372; 2000 (41) ALR 218; 2000 (4) AWC 3201; JT 2000 (10) SC 349; 2000 (3) KLT 598; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 4; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 730; 2000(6) SCALE 347; 2000 (6) SLT 675; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 235; 2000 (6) Supreme 368; 2001 (1) UC 136B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 41, r. 3-A - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - A suit was filed by respondents against State for a decree of declaration of their title and consequential injunction - Suit was dismissed on ground of want of jurisdiction to entertain suit - Plaintiffs filed an appeal before District Court against dismissal and District Judge reversed decision of Trial Court - Appellants filed a second appeal before High Court challenging judgment and decree passed by District Judge - HC held that an appeal filed out of time unaccompanied by an application to condone delay is liable to be axed down at threshold and and rejected appeal on sole ground that delayed appeal was presented without accompanying an application to condone delay - What is consequence if such an appeal is not accompanied by an application mentioned in O. 41, sub-r. (1) of r. 3-A? - Held, it must be noted that Code indicates in immediately preceding rule that consequence of not complying with requirements in r. 1 would include rejection of memorandum of appeal - Even so, another option is given to court by said rule and that is to return memorandum of appeal to appellant for amending it within a specified time - It is to be noted that there is no such rule prescribing for rejection of memorandum of appeal in a case where appeal is not accompanied by an application for condoning delay - If memorandum of appeal is filed in such appeal without an accompanying application to condone delay consequence cannot be fatal - Court can regard in such a case that there was no valid presentation of appeal - In turn, it means that if appellant subsequently files an application to condone delay before appeal is rejected same should be taken up along with already filed memorandum of appeal - Only then court can treat appeal as lawfully presented - Object of enacting r. 3-A in O. 41 of Code seems to be twofold - (1) to inform appellant himself who filed a time-barred appeal that it would not be entertained unless it is accompanied by an application explaining delay,(2) to communicate to respondent a message that it may not be necessary for him to get ready to meet grounds taken up in memorandum of appeal because court has to deal with application for condonation of delay as a condition precedent - Barring above objects, it cannot find out from rule that it is intended to operate as un-remediably or irredeemably fatal against appellant if memorandum is not accompanied by any such application at first instance - Deficiency is a curable defect, and if required application is filed subsequently appeal can be treated as presented in accordance with requirement contained in r. 3-A , O. 41 of CPC,1908 - Appeal allowed.
