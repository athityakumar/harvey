Collector Of Central Excise, Indore. v M/S.Hindustan Lever Ltd., Chhindwara.

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 204; (2000) 6 SCC 614; AIR 2000 SC 2907; 2000 (92) ECR 5; 2000 (120) E.L.T. 3; JT 2000 (8) SC 445; 2000 (39) RLT 885; 2000(5) SCALE 425; 2000 (6) SLT 15; 2000 (5) Supreme 375

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35 - CPC, 1908, s. 11 - Execise Duty - Powers of Supreme Court - (A) Claim for deduction - Appeal allowed by Tribunal and matter remanded back to original authority which decided it against claimant - Parties reapproaching the Tribunalwhich allowed the claim , appeal against to SC - Whether SC is bound by finding of Tribunal in its earlier remand order - (B) Claim for ' damage discount' - Plea, agreement allowing and trade practice - Authorities not going into impact of clauses of agreement as to the trade practice - It raises question of fact - SC not bound - Matter remanded back to original authority for decision afresh - Appeal disposed of.
