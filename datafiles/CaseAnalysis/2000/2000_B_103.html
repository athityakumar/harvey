Sakhahari Parwatrao Karahale and Another v Bhimashankar Parwatrao Karahale

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4072; (2002) 9 SCC 608

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Partition - Suit for injunction - Appellant/defendant and respondent/plaintiff were brothers - Respondent alleged that there was partition took place between family members hence filed suit for perpetual injunction restraining appellant from obstructing his possession of suit land before Trial Court - Trial Court dismissed suit on ground that there was no family partition - On appeal, First Appellate Court upheld order of Trial Court and another appeal before HC which reversed order of Lower Courts - Hence instant appeal.

Held, that Court finds, HC committed error in allowing second appeal by setting aside concurrent findings recorded by both Lower Courts. HC held, even in absence of any partition, there was family arrangement under which respondents came in possession of suit property. This finding of family arrangement is new case taken by HC. Apart from partition, Court does not find any pleading by respondents of such family arrangement. Not only there is no pleading, but any issues, no adjudication by any of two Lower Courts. In absence of this, once finding is recorded there was no partition between members of joint family, each member of joint family is co-sharer and possession of one is possession of all. Even if there be exclusive possession of respondent before partition, it would be possession on behalf of all. It therefore cannot disentitle appellant's claim against suit property. In view of this it is inconceivable that injunction could be granted against another co-sharer of joint Hindu property as has been done in appellant’s case. Findings recorded by HC, are unsustainable in law. Court quashes impugned order of HC and confirms findings recorded by both Lower Courts. Appeal allowed.
