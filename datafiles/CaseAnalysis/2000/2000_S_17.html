A. Venkatasubbiah Naidu v S. Challappan and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2598; (2000) 7 SCC 695; AIR 2000 SC 3032; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 32; 2000 (41) ALR 369; 2001 (43) ALR 848; 2000 (4) AWC 3311; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 327; 2000 (4) CompLJ 283; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 687; JT 2000 (10) SC 599; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 75; 2001 (127) PLR 96; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 396; 2000(6) SCALE 398; 2000 (6) SLT 767; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 303; 2000 (6) Supreme 269; 2001 (1) UC 55

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, rr. 1, 2, 3A - Temporary injunction - Vacate - Alternative Remedies - Challenged - Appellant was lease holder of suit schedule property on mortgage and he was in continuous possession of property as tenant - Appellants/plaintiff filed suit for permanent injunction restraining defendant Nos.1 to 5 from dispossessing him - Along with institution of suit appellant moved application under O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 CPC to pass ad interim injunction restraining respondent Nos. 1 to 5 or their men or agents, or their representatives or any person claiming through them or under them from evicting petitioner from suit property other than by due process of law - Trial Court passed ex-parte order and directed R1 to R5 were restrained from evicting appellant/plaintiff from suit property, except under due process of law - Aggrieved Respondents/defendants filed revision petition before HC which set aside Trial Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC should have entertained petition u/art.227 of Constitution when party had two other alternative remedies- Held, when respondents had two remedies statutorily available to them - First was that respondents could have approached Trial Court for vacating, if not for any modification, of interim ex-parte order passed - Second was that appeal could had been preferred by respondents against said order - It was open to respondents to opt either of two remedies - Therefore, there was no hurdle could be put against exercise of Constitutional powers of HC it was well-recognized principle which gained judicial recognition that HC should direct party to avail himself of such remedies one or other before he resorts to Constitutional remedy - Thus, Single Judge need not had entertained revision petition at all and party affected by interim ex parte order should had been directed to resort to one of other remedies - Further, that as it might, now it was idle to embark on that aspect as HC had chosen to entertain revision petition - In light of direction issued by HC that Trial Court should pass final orders on interlocutory application filed by appellant/plaintiff on merits and in accordance with law - Hence, Court, further, add that till such orders were passed by Trial Court, status-quo as it prevailed immediately preceding institution of suit would be maintained by parties - Appeal disposed of.
