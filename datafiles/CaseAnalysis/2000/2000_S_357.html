Hridaya Ranjan Prasad Verma and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 248; (2000) 4 SCC 168; (2000) SCC (Cr) 786; AIR 2000 SC 2341; AIR 2000 SCW 2077; 2000 (2) CCR 62; 2000 (2) Crimes 72; 2000 CRLJ 2983; 2000 (1) JCC 362; JT 2000 (3) SC 604; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 484; 2000(2) SCALE 694; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 859; 2000 (3) SLT 526; 2000 (3) Supreme 13

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 415 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Breach of contract and offence of cheating - Distinction between - Agreement for sale of property entered into by accused on behalf of himself and his brothers with complainant - Complaint for cheating was filed against accused by complainant - Purchaser on ground that accused did not disclose that one of his brothers had filed partition suit which was pending - Could not establish that there was any dishonest/fraudulent intention on part of accused for not disclosing above information which is gist of offence of cheating - Held, High Court was not justified in declining to quash criminal proceeding against accused.
