Kalloomal Tapeswari Prasad v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 162; (1982) SCC (Tax) 73; (1982) 1 SCC 447; AIR 1982 SC 760; 1982 (26) CTR 415; [1982] 133 ITR 690; 1982(1) SCALE 11; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 9; 1982 TAXLR 314; [1982] 8 TAXMAN 5; 1982 UJ 751

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Immovable Property - Partial partition - Double Taxation - Avoidance Agreement - Assessee was a Hindu undivided family governed by Mitakshara school of law - Assessee was deriving income from various sources such as income from property, income from money lending business, income from speculation business and cloth business etc - There was a partial partition amongst family members - Partial partition was accepted and acted upon by Income-tax Department where cloth business was treated as business of a firm consisting of most of the coparceners as partners - Income Tax Officer rejected the claim of partial partition - Lower Courts found that it was not possible to divide properties physically into ten shares referred to above was not tenable and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether claim of partial partition was made liable to be included in computation of assessee's income - Held, subject-matter of partial partition would continue to be treated as belonging to family and its income would continue to be included in its total income until such a finding was recorded - Court did not consider that such a plea was available to assessee because acceptance of such a plea would lead to nullification - U/s. 171 holding that a partial partition had taken place Hindu undivided family should be deemed for purposes of Act to be owner of property which was subject matter of partition and also recipient of income from such property - Assessment should be made as such and tax assessed can be recovered as provided in Act - Appeals disposed of.
