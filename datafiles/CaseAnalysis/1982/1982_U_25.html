 D. S. Nakara and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Baharul Islam, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Y.V. Chandrachud, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 158; (1983) SCC (L&S) 145; (1983) 1 SCC 305; AIR 1983 SC 130; 1983 (47) FLR 42; 1983 LabIC 1; 1983 (1) LLJ 104; 1983 (1) LLN 289; 1982(2) SCALE 1213; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 165; 1983 (1) SLJ 131; 1983 (2) SLR 246; 1983 UJ 217

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension, Liberalised Pension Scheme, Kerala Building Tax Act, 1961, Ips (Regulation Of Seniority) Rules, 1954

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 34 - (A) Whether petitioners entitled to receive superannuation or retiring pension under Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972 ('1972 Rules', for short) form a class as a whole? - By Memorandum of Ministry of Defence bearing No. 8/40725/AG/PS 4-C/1816/AD (Pension) /Services D/- 28-09-1979, liberalised pension formula introduced for Govt. servants governed by 1972 Rules was extended to armed forces personnel subject to limitations set out in memorandum with a condition that new rules of pension would be effective from 01-04-1979 and may be applicable to all service officer who become/became non-effective on or after that date - Held, liberalised pension formula shall be applicable prospectively to those who retired on or after 31-03-1970 in case of Govt. servants covered by 1972 Rules and in respect of defence personnel those who became/become non-effective on or after 01-04-1979 - Consequently those who retired prior to specified date would be entitled to benefits of liberalised pension formula - (B)Whether the date of retirement is a relevant consideration for eligibility when a revised formula for computation of pension is ushered in and made effective from a specified date? - Date of retirement of each employee remains as it is - Average emoluments have to be worked out keeping in view emoluments drawn by him before retirement but in accordance with principles of liberalised pension scheme - Two features which make liberalised scheme more attractive is redefining of average emoluments in r. 34, and introduction of slab system simultaneously raising ceiling - Pension will have to be recomputed with effect from date from which liberalised pension scheme came into force i.e. 31-03-1979 - There is no question of fresh commutation of pension of pensioners who retired prior to 31-03-1979 and have already availed to benefit of commutation - It is not open to them to get that benefit at this late date because commutation has to be availed of within specified time limit from date of actual retirement may be some marginal retirees may earn benefit - Further held, Ex P-2,are unconstitutional and are struck down with this specification that date mentioned therein will be relevant as being one from which liberalised pension scheme becomes operative to all pensioners governed by 1972 Rules irrespective of date of retirement - (C)Whether differential treatment to pensioners related to date of retirement qua revised formula for computation of pension attract art.14 of Constitution and element of discrimination liable to be declared unconstitutional as being violative of art. 14 ? - Old men who retired when emoluments were comparatively low and are exposed to vagaries of continuously rising prices, falling value of rupee consequent upon inflationary inputs, we are satisfied that by introducing an arbitrary eligibility criteria : 'being in service and retiring subsequent to specified date' for being eligible for liberalised pension scheme and thereby dividing a homogeneous class, classification being not based on any discernible rational principle and having been found wholly unrelated to objects sought to be achieved by grant of liberalised pension and eligibility criteria devised being thoroughly arbitrary - Further held, eligibility for liberalised pension scheme of 'being in service in specified date and retiring subsequent to that date' in impugned memoranda, Exhibits P-1 and P-2, violates art. 14 and in unconstitutional and is truck down - Petition disposed of.
