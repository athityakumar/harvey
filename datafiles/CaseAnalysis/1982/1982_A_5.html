Agencia Commercial Internationalltd. and Others v Custodian of The Branches of Banconacional Ultramarino

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Baharul Islam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 132; (1982) 2 SCC 482; AIR 1982 SC 1268; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 1; 1982 (3) CompLJ 117; 1982(1) SCALE 575; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 16; 1982 UJ 561

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Goa, Daman and Diu (Administration) Act, 1962, Goa Daman and Diu (Banks reconstruction) Regulation 1962, Power of Custodian of bank, Right to sue for recovery of debts

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Goa, Daman and Diu (Banks Reconstruction) Regulation, 1962, ss. 5(I), 8(1) - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 81 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 16 - Power of Custodian of bank - Right to sue for recovery of debts - Maintainability - Appellants filed instant appeals arising out of suits for recovery of loans made to appellants at various branches of respondent (bank) - Trial Court decreed suits against appellants (principal debtor) but dismissed against the guarantors - Against the judgment of Trial Court, appeals were filed before Addl. Judicial Commr., which were dismissed holding that Regulation effected a reconstruction of branches in Goa, Daman and Diu of respondent bank, that rights and obligations of branches referred to in Regulation should be understood to mean the rights acquired and obligations undertaken by respondent through those branches and thus Custodian was entitled to maintain suits and sue for realisation of debts arising out of transactions entered into through those branches - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that loans were granted by head office of respondent bank, and not by branches and that Custodian was not entitled to sue for recovery of loans granted by head office - Further contended that Commr. erred in not determining whether appellants were entitled to credit for adjustments claimed by them in loan accounts - Whether Custodian of respondent bank was entitled to sue for recovery of debts covered by branches of bank - Held, it was indisputable as a general proposition that a body corporate and its branches were not distinct and separate entities from each other, that the branches constitute mere components through which corporate entity expresses itself and that all transactions entered into ostensibly with the branches were in legal reality transactions with corporate body, and it was with the corporate body, that a person should deal directly - Although loan agreements might have entered into with respondent bank, the branches were authorised by head office to give effect to those agreements, and accordingly branch concerned embarked upon the execution of agreements and the working out of transactions - Entire business involved in those transactions and dealings was effected by branch concerned, and it was only when occasion strictly so required that branch made reference to head office for authority to amend or enlarge the scope of operation - Transaction and business nonetheless remained throughout those of branch, and that was fully affirmed by existence and operation of the loan accounts in the books of branch, by the pledge or hypothecation of goods in almost all cases in favour of branch and by overall nature and character of the transaction as an ordinary banking transaction falling within the normal business of a branch - Entire purpose of the Regulation was to reconstruct by operation of statute the closed branches of respondent bank and to constitute them into a Bank and to work out existing transactions and square up all pending business with a view to ultimately winding-up the affairs of branches - Transactions under consideration in instant appeals fall within the scope of Regulation and the Custodian was fully entitled to sue for the recovery of debts covered by loan agreements - It was within the competence of Court to base its decree on the books of account of branches in Goa and on other evidence which could be produced - It was not necessary for Custodian, indeed it was not possible, to produce the Promissory Notes and Bills of Exchange - Resort to provisions of O. 7 r. 16 of CPC and s. 81 of the Act could not be justified inasmuch as the cases fall to be determined under the Regulation and Portuguese law which continued in force in Goa - Even in respect of Portuguese law, provisions in Portuguese Commercial Code and Portuguese Uniform Law, it stood superseded by reason of express provisions contained in s. 8(1) of the Regulation - No indemnity could be reasonably required of Custodian when it was proved to the satisfaction of Court that the document was removed to Portugal or to any of territories under Portuguese control - Regulation vested entire right in Custodian to recover the debt and no further right was left in anyone else; debts were regarded as properties and assets of branches, and all rights in respect of them stood transferred to and vested in Custodian by virtue of s. 5(I) of the Regulation - It was not possible to conceive that it would be open to head office of respondent bank to sue the debtors for recovery of debts - Trial Court was directed to take up suits again solely for purpose of examining the validity of claims to set-off made by appellants in those suits - Appeals dismissed.
