State of Maharashtra v Narayan Shamrao Puranik and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 241; (1982) 2 SCC 440; AIR 1982 SC 1198; 1982(1) SCALE 443; 1982 UJ 368

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960

Summary: Advocates & Judges - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 41 and 51 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 3 and 4 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 14 - Whether opinion of Chief Justice to appoint seat of High Court of a new State at a place other than principal seat under sub-section (3) of s. 51 of Act amounts to colourable exercise of power ? - Held, when there is continued existence of sub-section (1) of s. 51 of Act in relation to principal seat of HC for a new state, a fortiori, there is, to an equal degree, continued existence of provisions contained in sub-sections (2) and (3) of s. 51 for establishment of a permanent bench or benches of HC of a new state by President or appointment by Chief Justice of sittings of Judges and Division Courts of HC at any other place or places for that state, that is, at places other than place where principal seat of HC of a new State, is located - Provisions of sub-section (2) and (3) of s. 51 of Act are supplemental or incidental to provisions made by Parliament u/art. 3 and 4 of Constitution - Art. 3 of Constitution enables Parliament to make a law for formation of a new State-Act is a law u/art. 3 for Reorganisation of State - Art. 4 of Constitution provides that law referred to in art. 3 may contain 'such supplementary, incidental and consequential provisions, as Parliament may deem necessary' - Act is a permanent piece of legislation on statute book - S. 14 of General Clauses Act, provides that, where, by any Central Act or Regulation, any power is conferred, then unless a different intention appears, that power may be exercised from time to time as occasion arises - Power to appoint sittings of Judges and Division Courts of HC for a new State at places other than place of principal seat, is in unquestioned domain of Chief Justice - Only condition being that he must act with approval of Governor - It is basically an internal matter pertaining to HC Chief Justice is master of roster - Chief Justice has full power, authority and jurisdiction in matter of allocation of business of HC which flows not only from provision contained in sub-section (3) of s. 51 of Act - Opinion of Chief Justice to appoint seat of HC of a new state at a place other than principal seat under sub-s. (3) of s. 51 of Act must therefore normally prevail because it is for more convenient transaction of judicial business - There is no territorial bifurcation of Bombay HC merely because Chief Justice by impugned order issued under sub-s. (3) of s. 51 of Act directed that Judges and Division Courts shall also sit at Aurangabad Chief Justice acted with best of intentions within scope of his powers - No substance in HC's conclusion that notification issued by Chief Justice is a colourable exercise of power - Petition dismissed.
