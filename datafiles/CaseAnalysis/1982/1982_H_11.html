Jaya Mala v Home Secretary, Government of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 37; (1982) SCC (Cr) 502; (1982) 2 SCC 538; AIR 1982 SC 1297; 1982 AllCriC 101; 1982 CRLJ 1777; 1982 CRLR 441; 1982(1) SCALE 558; 1982 UJ 859

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, art. 22(5) - J. and K. Public Safety Act, 1978, s. 8 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Detention - (A) Grounds - Minor infraction of law - Whether activity prejudicial to the maintenance of public order - Every minor infraction of law having a penal sanction cannot be upgraded to the height of an activity prejudicial to the maintenance of public order - (B) Age - Proof - Doctor's opinion - Radiological test - Margin of error in age - Extent - (C) Detention of a minor school going boy aged 17 years for infraction of law on two occasions - Held, such infraction of law, by itself, cannot be a ground for detention - Margin of error in age ascertained by radiological examination is two years on either side - Held, was wholly unwarranted in the facts and circumstances of the case - Petition Allowed.
