Mayur Panabhai Shah v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 6; (1982) 2 SCC 396; (1982) SCC (Cr) 442; AIR 1983 SC 66; 1982 CRLJ 1972; 1982 CRLR 557; 1982(1) SCALE 523

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Held, not case which should have been summarily rejected by Single Judge - Even where doctor deposed in court, his evidence was to be appreciated like evidence of any other witness - There was no irrebuttable presumption that doctor was always witness of truth - Order of Single Judge set aside and appeal remanded to HC - Appeal allowed.
