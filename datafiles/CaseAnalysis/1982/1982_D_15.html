Smt. Kalloo and Others v Dhakadevi and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 133; (1982) 1 SCC 633; AIR 1982 SC 813; 1982 (8) ALR 323; 1982 (2) RCJ 359; 1982 (1) RCR(Rent) 402; 1982 (1) RentLR 298; 1982(1) SCALE 177; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 201; 1982 UJ 251

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Compromise, Eviction decree, Emerging of fresh contract

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Eviction decree - Compromise - Emerging of fresh contract - Legality - Predecessor of respondents (decree-holder) filed a suit for eviction of the predecessor of appellants (judgment-debtors) and obtained a decree for eviction from a shop - Decree-holder filed a petition for execution of decree for eviction in executing court and during pendency of execution proceeding, parties filed a compromise petition that half of shop has to be vacated and its possession delivered to decree-holder, as stated in compromise petition - Thereafter, a fresh lease was created in favour of judgment-debtor in respect of remaining half of shop - It was submitted that decree was not executable - Executing Court upheld the objection holding that compromise dt.21-3-1968 amounted to a fresh lease - An appeal was filed before District Judge, who set aside the order of Executing Court - In a second appeal, HC upheld the order of District Judge, and dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal -Whether decree has been extinguished as a result of compromise and a fresh contract was emerged - Held, compromise petition shows that judgment-debtor had already vacated half of shop and given its possession' and time was granted till 31-12-1972 for vacating and delivering possession of the other half of shop as the judgment- debtor wanted time till then lest his business will be ruined by vacating the shop in hurry - That clearly shows that intention of parties was not to create a fresh lease in respect of half of shop in possession of the judgment-debtor, but to help the judgment-debtor find out, not in a hurry; alternative accommodation for his shop so that his established business was not 'ruined' and time for vacating the half of the room was given till 31-12-1972 - And that was also how the compromise petition was understood by Executing Court when it recorded in para 3 of its order 'The judgment-debtor to give vacant possession of shop to decree-holder by 31-12-1972 according to the compromise'- Intention of parties clearly was not to extinguish the decree for eviction but to create a mode of its full discharge - Appeal dismissed.
