Mohd. Abdul Hafeez v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 63; (1983) SCC (Cr) 139; (1983) 1 SCC 143; AIR 1983 SC 367; 1983 CRLR 26; 1982(2) SCALE 1064; 1983 UJ 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 392 - Robbery - Conviction of - Confession to the Police - Admissibility of - Held, if evidence otherwise confessional in character is admissible u/s. 27 of Indian Evidence Act, 1872, it is obligatory upon Investigating Officer to state and record who gave information - When he is dealing with more than one accused, what words were used by him so that a recovery pursuant to information received may be connected to person giving information so as to provide incriminating evidence against persons - Evidence against present appellant and it is not sufficient to connect appellant with crime and charge is not brought home to appellant - Appeal allowed.
