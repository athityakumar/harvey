Jugal Kishore Paliwal v S. Sat Jit Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 240; (1984) 1 SCC 358

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Whether High Court was right in refusing to go into merits of case on ground that appeal was not maintainable? - Amendment of written statement was sought at time of framing issues and it vitally affects right of parties and seeks to work some injustice to plaintiff, it merits serious consideration by Appellate Court on question whether or not amendment should be allowed - Held, it would certainly not be a purely interlocutory order against which no appeal before LPA bench would be maintainable - Case sent back to division bench for admitting appeal and disposing it off, according to law on merits - Appeal allowed.
