K. Rajendran and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 197; (1982) SCC (L&S) 208; (1982) 2 SCC 273; AIR 1982 SC 1107; 1982 LabIC 876; 1982 (2) LLJ 259; 1982 (2) LLN 26; 1983 (1) MLJ(SC) 19B; 1982(1) SCALE 342; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 628; 1982 (1) SLJ 604; 1982 (2) SLR 196; 1982 UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Andhra Pradesh and Madras (Alteration of Boundaries) Act, 1959, Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, University of Agricultural Sciences Act, 1963

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g), 226, 310 and 311 - Tamil Nadu Abolition of Posts of Part-time Village Officers Act, 1981 - Pre Constitutional validity of Act - Village offices in State of Tamil Nadu were abolished - (A)Whether Tamil Nadu Abolition of Posts of Part-time Village Officers Act is violative of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution ? - Held, Tamil Nadu Abolition of Posts of Part-time Village Officers Act (3 of 1981) does not violate art. 19(1)(g), as it does not affect right of any of incumbents of posts to carry on any occupation of their choice even though they may not be able to stick on to posts which they were holding - (B) Whether Administrative Reforms Commission was justified in abolition and reorganisation of village service? - The posts of village officers which were governed by Madras Act, 1894, Madras Act, 1895 and Board's Standing orders were feudalistic in character and appointments to those posts were governed by law of primogeniture, family in which applicant was born, village in which he was born and fact whether he owned any property in village or not - These factors are alien to modern administrative service and are clearly opposed to arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Further held, Administrative Reforms Commission were justified in recommending their abolition and reorganisation of village service - (C)Whether appointment of village officers under Act of 1981 and Rules thereunder are violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - Further held, even though village officers appointed after 16-12-1970 are in a way different from village officials appointed prior to that date, they too cannot be equated with new village administrative officers who will be appointed under Act of 1981 and Rules made thereunder - It cannot, therefore, be said that art. 14 of Constitution has been violated in abolishing posts held by those appointed after 16-12-1970 - Petitions dismissed.
