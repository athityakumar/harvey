Sat Pal Gupta and Another v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 123; (1982) SCC (Cr) 327; (1982) 1 SCC 610; AIR 1982 SC 798; 1982 CRLR 83; 1982 CrLR(SC) 83; 1982(1) SCALE 89; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 196; 1982 UJ 178

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Edible

Summary: Administrative - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 ss. 2(a)(v) , 3 - Haryana Rice Bran (Distribution and Price) Control Order, 1967 - Power conferred - Regulating sale or supply - Challenged - In exercise of power u/s. 3 of the Act delegated by Central govt. to State Govt., promulgated 1967 Order which provided that no dealer or owner of rice mill should sell or offer to sell or supply rice bran save against permit granted by the Director, Food and Supplies, or the District Magistrate or any other officer authorised by the Director in that behalf - Aggrieved appellants/petitioners filed writ before HC challenged Cl.3 of said Order, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether power conferred u/s. 3 of the Act cannot be exercised by State for purpose of regulating its sale or supply.

Held, any stuff which is commonly used as food by the generality of living beings is foodstuff and it is not legitimate to restrict the meaning of that word to things which are used as food by human beings. Therefore, animal kingdom is not any the less important in the cosmic scheme than the human empire and it is a distortion to say that it is a matter of little or no concern to the State whether the cattle and the poultry get their due ration of the means of their subsistence. Further, cattle feed and poultry feed are food to the cattle and the poultry, and therefore they are foodstuffs. Thus, rice bran being a foodstuff within the meaning u/s. 2(a)(v) of the Act, it is an essential commodity and the power conferred u/s. 3 of the Act can be used to regulate its production, sale or supply. Hence, it is held that rice bran, being a foodstuff, is an essential commodity. Appeal dismissed.
