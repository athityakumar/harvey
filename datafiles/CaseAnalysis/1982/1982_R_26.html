V.T. Khanzode and Others v Reserve Bank of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 201; (1982) 2 SCC 7; (1982) SCC (L&S) 147; AIR 1982 SC 917; 1982 (44) FLR 317; 1982 LabIC 1217; 1982 (1) LLJ 465; 1982 (1) LLN 564; 1982(1) SCALE 316; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 411; 1982 (1) SLJ 466; 1982 (1) SLR 731

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reserve Bank Of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, arts. 14, 16 and 32 - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, ss. 7(2), 58, 58(1) and 58(2) - Reserve Bank of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948 - Introduction by Reserve Bank of India of a common seniority and inter-group mobility amongst different grades of officers which adversely affected their seniority - Issues arise (1) Whether the Reserve Bank of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948 are statutory in character - Held, s. 58(1) confers power on Directors to make regulations for all necessary or convenient matters to give effect to the provisions of the Act - Manifestly necessary to provide for the service conditions of the Bank's staff in order to give effect to the provisions of the Act - (2) Whether Bank can provide for conditions of service of its staff by administrative circulars - Held, Yes - If the staff regulations are not framed under section 58(1), Central Board can issue administrative circulars regulating the service conditions of the staff - Powers provided under section 7(2) apply - (3) Whether the impugned circular and seniority list offend against the provisions of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Held, No - Adjustments in the administrative affairs of the Bank are a necessary sequel to the growing demands of new situations which are bound to arise in any developing economy - Inter-group mobility and common seniority are solutions to the conflicting demands of officers belonging to different groups - Appeals dismissed.
