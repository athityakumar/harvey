Kishan Chand Mangal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 53; (1982) 3 SCC 466; (1983) SCC (Cr) 92; AIR 1982 SC 1511; 1983 CRLJ 1; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Bribery - Corrupt practices - Conviction of - Circumstantial evidence - Reliability of - Held, a fact may be proved either by direct testimony or by circumstances evidence - Sanctity of word made dependent upon office held or wealth acquired is a nauseating phenomenon - Truth is neither monopoly nor preserve of affluent or of highly placed persons - In a country where renunciation is worshipped and grandeur and wild display of wealth frowned upon, if would be travesty of truth if persons coming from humble origin and belonging to officewise, wealthwise lower strata of society are to be disbelieved or rejected as unworthy of belief solely on ground of their humble position in society - Charge is brought home to the accused and he has been rightly convicted and sentence awarded being minimum, no case is made out for interfering with same - Appeal dismissed.
