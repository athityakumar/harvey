Ashok Kumar v Delhi Administration And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 31; (1982) 2 SCC 403; (1982) SCC (Cr) 451; 1982 (19) ACC 262; AIR 1982 SC 1143; 1982 AllCriC 262; 1982 CRLJ 1191; 1982(1) SCALE 459; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 707; 1982 UJ 484

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, art. 22(5) - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3(2) and 8 - Detention - (A) Grounds of detention supplied two days late -Whethe detention order becomes invalid in the light of expression 'as soon as may be' - (B) Whether Order specifing period of detention is mandatory - (C) 'Public order' and 'law and order' - Distinction - Potentiality of act to disturb even tempo of community life - Prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Preventive measure even involving hardship upon individual - Whether penal - Held, detention order is not rendered invalid and expression means ' as soon as practicable' - Not mandatory - Not in nature of punishment - Petition Dismissed.
