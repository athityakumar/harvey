D. Ramaswami v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 173; (1982) SCC (L&S) 115; (1982) 1 SCC 510; AIR 1982 SC 793; 1982 (44) FLR 247; 1982 LabIC 443; 1982 (1) LLJ 349; 1982 (1) LLN 230; 1983 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1982(1) SCALE 92; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 75; 1982 (1) SLJ 194; 1982 (1) SLR 690; 1982 UJ 154

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Service - Tamil Nadu Special Rules for Commercial Taxes Service, rr. 2(b) and 36(b)(i) - Compulsory retirement - Validity of - Whether order of Govt. retiring appellant from service is correct ? - Previous history of a Govt. servant should be completely ignored, once he is promoted - Sometimes, past events may help to assess present conduct - But when there is nothing in present conduct casting any doubt on wisdom of promotion, there is no justification for needless digging into past - There was an adverse entry in the confidential file of the appellant in 1969 - The basis of the entry was knocked out by the order D/- 29-11-1974 of the Govt., and the effect of the entry was blotted out by the promotion of the appellant as Deputy Commr. - After his promotion as Deputy Commr. there was no entry in the service Book to his discredit or hinting even remotely that he had outlived his utility as a Govt. servant - If there was some entry, not wholly favorable to the appellant after his promotion, one might hark back to similar or like entries in the past, read them all in conjunction and conclude that the time had arrived for the Govt. servant to quit Govt. service - But, with nothing of the sort, it is indeed odd to retire a Govt. servant a few months after promoting him to a Selection post - In the present case, the court made a vain search in service record of the appellant to find something adverse to the appellant apart from the 1969 entry - Appellant will be reinstated in service and paid arrears of salary due to him under rules - Appeal allowed.
