Umesh Chandra v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 198; (1982) SCC (L&S) 200; (1982) 2 SCC 202; (1982) SCC (Cr) 396; AIR 1982 SC 1057; 1982 CRLJ 994; 1982 CrLR(SC) 259; 1982(1) SCALE 335; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 583; 1982 UJ 426

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 35, 73 and 74 - Rajasthan Children Act, 1970, ss. 3 and 6 - (A) Admissibility of public document - Two documents of two different public schools showing same age of child - (B) Pendency of proceedings against child at time of commencement of Act - Court should proceed with but is debarred from passing sentence - (A) Held, admissible u/s. 35 as ss. 73 and 74 would be irrelevant - (B) Date of applicability of Act is date on which offence takes place and not date of trial - Appeal Allowed.
