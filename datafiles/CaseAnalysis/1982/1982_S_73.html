Ibrahim Ahmad Batti v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 190; (1982) 3 SCC 440; (1983) SCC (Cr) 66; AIR 1982 SC 1500; 1983 (1) Crimes 275; 1982 CRLR 465; 1982 CrLR(SC) 465; 1982(2) SCALE 888; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution, art. 22 (5) - Customs Act, 1962 - Appeal against detention - Detenu was not furnished with the materials incorporated in the grounds by reference, necessary for detention - Couldn't make effective representation - Held, he should be set free and directed that all documents must be furnished within five days ordinarily and in the exceptional circumstances and for reasons to be recorded in writing not later than 15 days from the date of his detention and secondly, all such material must be furnished to him in a script or language which he understands and failure to do either of the two things would amount to a breach of the two duties cast on the detaining authority u/art. 22 (5) of the Constitution - Petition allowed.
