Vijay Kumar v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 115; (1982) 2 SCC 43; (1982) SCC (Cr) 348; AIR 1982 SC 1023; 1982 CRLJ 988; 1982 CrLR(SC) 245; 1982 MLJ 471; 1982(1) SCALE 240; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 522; 1982 UJ 382

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Jammu and Kashmir Public Safety Act, 1978, s. 8 - Detention u/s. 8 of Jammu and Kashmir Public Safety Act, 1978 - Detenu already arrested and in jail on suspicion that he had committed offences under Enemy Agents Ordinance National Security Act, 1980 - Communication of grounds and consideration of detenu's representation - Whether order of District Magistrate suffers from non-application of mind as date on which he passed impugned order of detention dated 1-07-1981, detenu had already long before been arrested and was locked up in jail? - If detenu is already in jail charged with a serious offence, he is thereby prevented from acting in a manner prejudicial to security of state - Detaining authority must disclose awareness of fact that person against whom an order of preventive detention is being made is to knowledge of authority already in jail and yet for compelling reasons a preventive detention order needs to be made - Held, there is nothing to indicate awareness of detaining authority that detenu was already in jail and yet impugned order is required to be made - Held, this clearly exhibits non-application of mind and would result in invalidation of order -(B) Whether slackness in opportunity to make a representation against detention order to Govt. would be denial of protection conferred by National Security Act, 1980? - As soon as detenu is deprived of his personal liberty, statute makes it obligatory on authorities concerned to afford him an earliest opportunity to represent his side of case and which inheres corresponding obligation on authority to consider same - Word 'earliest' which qualifies opportunity must equally qualify corresponding obligation of State to deal with representation, if and when made, as expeditiously as possible - Further held, any slackness in this behalf, not properly explained would be denial of protection conferred by statute and would result in invalidation of order - (C) Whether order of detention in instant case can be invalidated on ground contravention of s. 13 of Act of 1980? - In instant case delay apart from being inordinate was not explained on any convincing ground - Further held, representation was not dealt with as early as possible or as expeditiously as possible and therefore, there would be contravention of s. 13 of Act of 1980 which would result in invalidation of order - Order accordingly.
