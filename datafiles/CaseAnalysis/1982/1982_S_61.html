Devi Lal Mahto v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 139; (1982) 3 SCC 328; (1983) SCC (Cr) 37; AIR 1982 SC 1548; 1983 (1) Crimes 146; 1982 CRLJ 2363; 1982 CrLR(SC) 485; 1982(2) SCALE 910; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 630; 1982 UJ 894

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Writ of habeas corpus - Order of preventive detention - Validity of - Held, one can envisage a hypothetical case in which a preventive detention order may have to be made against a person already deprived of his personal liberty by being confined or detained in jail - In such a situation detaining authority must show awareness of this fact that person against whom detention order is proposed to be made is already in jail and is incapable of acting in a manner prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Yet for reasons which may appeal to District Magistrate on which his subjective satisfaction is grounded a preventive detention order is required to be made - This awareness must appear either in order or in affidavit justifying impugned detention order when challenged - The order of preventive detention having been mechanically made and suffering from vice of non-application of mind is vitiated - Quashed and set aside the order of detention - Order accordingly.
