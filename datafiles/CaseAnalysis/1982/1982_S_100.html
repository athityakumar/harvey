Mohanlal Gangaram Gehani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 14; (1982) SCC (Cr) 334; (1982) 1 SCC 700; (1983) SCC (Cr) 29; AIR 1982 SC 839; 1983 (1) Crimes 83; 1982 CRLJ 630B; 1982 CrLR(SC) 77; 1982 MLJ 431; 1982(1) SCALE 143; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 277

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 323 and 326 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 145 and 164 - Appeal against conviction u/s. 326 - Appellant pleaded prior enmity, reason of being implicated - Time of registration of FIR not correct - Appellant not known to injured - Test identification parade not conducted - Name of appellant not mentioned in Registrar's note - Held, where statement made by a person or witness is contradicted not by his own statement but by statement of another prosecution witness, question of application of s. 145 does not arise - Statement made by P.W. 11 was, therefore, an admission of a prosecution witness and if it was inconsistent with statement made by another prosecution witness, there was no question of application of s. 145 which did not apply to such a case in terms - Further held, case not proved beyond reasonable doubt - There is absolutely no legal evidence on basis of which appellant could be convicted - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
