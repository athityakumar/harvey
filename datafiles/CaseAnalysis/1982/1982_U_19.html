Wing Commander J. Kumar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, A.D. Koshal, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 125; (1982) 2 SCC 116; (1982) SCC (L&S) 177; AIR 1982 SC 1064; 1982 LabIC 1586; 1982 (2) LLN 1; 1982(1) SCALE 327; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 453; 1982 (1) SLJ 452; 1982 (1) SLR 715; 1982 UJ 313

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Air Force

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 309 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Office Memorandum No. Pers/18601/RD. Sel. Bd/7971/D (R&D) D\- 23-11-1979 - Seniority - Fixation of - Rules relating to the procedure for intake of service officers in the R and D Organisation - Promulgated ny President under proviso to art. 309 - Validity u/arts. 14 and 16 - Held, since appellant had been permanently seconded to R and D Organisation in 1971, long prior to promulgation of impugned rules, his rights regarding seniority and promotions cannot, in any way, be effected by provisions of new rule - Lateral induction of officers holding ranks above substantive major/equivalent and assigning of seniority to such subsequent inductees by applying provisions of r. 16 amounts to deprivation of vested rights of persons like appellant who had joined Organisation earlier and it is, therefore, illegal and unwarranted - A statement contained in a statute or statutory rule of factual background leading up to enactment has ordinarily to be accepted and acted upon by court as wholly correct - The service conditions pertaining to seniority are liable to alteration by subsequent changes that may be introduced in rules and except to extent of protecting promotions that have already been earned under previous rules, revised rules will operate to govern seniority and future promotion prospects of all persons in concerned service - There is a selection only in a very limited sense that suitability of concerned officer is adjudged before he is taken, but what is important to note is that in intake of officers into R and D Organisation - There is no comparative evaluation of merits of other personnel occupying same rank or possessing like experience in Defence Services - The rule-making authority has adopted as base for reckoning seniority highest common factor applicable in respect of time scale promotions in three services, namely rank of Major/equivalent and thereby ensured to service officers seconded to R and D Organisation a just and equitable treatment - High Court was perfectly right in upholding validity of impugned rule and in rejecting challenge raised by petitioner-appellant against selections, inductions and promotions made in R and D Organisation on basis of said rule - Appeal dismissed.
