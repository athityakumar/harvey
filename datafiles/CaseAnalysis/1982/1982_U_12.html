Dr. Asim Kumar Bose v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 153; (1983) SCC (L&S) 185; (1983) 1 SCC 345; AIR 1983 SC 509; 1983 (46) FLR 224; 1983 (2) LabIC 370; 1983 (1) LLN 503; 1982(2) SCALE 1299; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 16; 1983 (1) SLJ 203; 1982 (3) SLR 97; 1983 UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Research, Medical Council, Central Health Service Rules, 1963

Summary: Service - Central Health Service Rules, 1963, rr. 8(2) and 8(2A) - Appointment - Promotion - Eligibility - Entitled to - Appellant was appointed as a Lecturer in Radiology in M College, a post placed in Category 'E' of Central Health Service - Appellant acquired requisite teaching experience of an Associate Professor of Radiology as well as acquired higher academic qualification - Appellant was conferred degree of Doctor of Philosophy (Medicine) in Radiotherapy by C University - Faculty of Medical Sciences appointed the petitioner as a Supervisor for post-graduate students for M.D. (Radiotherapy) course of study - Appellant was not only holding post of Radiologist in I Hospital, but was also actively associated with teaching under-graduate and post-graduate students as an Associate Professor of Radiology of M College for MD, DMRT and MBBS. courses of studies - GOI promoted and appointed Dr. K, Lecturer in Radiology in M College as Associate Professor of Radiotherapy ignoring claim of the appellant - Aggrieved, appellant preferred writ petition - HC held that appellant admittedly holds high academic and professional qualifications and had also good teaching experience to his credit, rejected writ petition on its construction of the Rules - HC observed that recruitment rules required that requisite experience should be experience gained while working in a medical college or in a teaching institution i.e. as a teacher in a teaching department - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was eligible for appointment to post of Professor of Radiotherapy in M College or whether appellant fulfilled the conditions laid down in rr. 8 (2) and 2 (A) of the Rules - Held, provisions contained in r. 8 (2A) of the Rules to Second Schedule should be interpreted in a broad and liberal sense as it would otherwise work great injustice to persons in Specialists Grade II like appellant who, while holding a non-clinical post in a teaching hospital like I Hospital, had been actually teaching students of M College to which it was affiliated - Contention that position which appellant enjoys as Radiologist-cum-Associate Professor of Radiology (ex- officio) in I Hospital was similar to that of honorary Professor or Associate Professor in W Hospital and mere designation of appellant as such did not give him a right to hold post of Associate Professor of Radiology, could not prevail - It was not for Court to give appellant promotion or make his appointment to post of Professor of Radiotherapy - Court could only on a true construction of r. 8 (2A) of the Rules to the Second Schedule determined the question of his eligibility for such promotion or appointment - If appellant was eligible to hold post of Professor of Radiotherapy, he could always apply irrespective of fact whether or not he was in line of promotion - It was for Union Public Service Commission to advertise post of Professor of Radiotherapy and everyone who satisfied the required qualifications could make an application - That was because Commission undoubtedly had power to relax any of qualifications - Judgment and order of HC was set aside and impugned order passed by GOI, Ministry of Health & Family Planning, Dept. of Health dt.23-2-1974 was quashed - Appellant had acquired requisite teaching experience as envisaged by r. 8 (2A) to Second Schedule of the Rules, and was eligible to be considered for appointment to post of Associate Professor of Radiotherapy in M college which had fallen vacant in 1973 - Direction was issued to UPSC to re-advertise post of Professor of Radiology in M College which had fallen vacant during pendency of the appeal and call appellant for an interview for being considered appointment to that post - Appeal allowed.
