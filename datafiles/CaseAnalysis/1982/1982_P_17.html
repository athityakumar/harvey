Precision Steel and Engineering Works and Another v Prem Deva Niranjan Deva Tayal

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 279; (1982) 3 SCC 270; AIR 1982 SC 1518; 1982 (2) RCJ 643; 1982 (2) RCR(Rent) 544; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e) and 25(B) - Whether leave to contest petition ought or ought not to be granted? - Held, when a petition is brought before Controller u/s. 14(1)(e) a summons has to be issued to tenant and when summons is served tenant cannot straightway proceed to contest petition for eviction from premises but either he must surrender possession or seek leave to contest petition - While seeking leave he must file an affidavit setting out grounds on which he seeks to contest application for eviction - Then comes s. 25 B(5) which provides that Controller is under a statutory duty to grant leave if affidavit filed by tenant discloses such facts as would disentitle landlord from obtaining an order for recovery of possession of premises on ground mentioned in s. 14(1)(e) - While examining question whether leave to defend ought or ought not to be granted limited jurisdiction which Controller enjoys is prescribed within well defined limits and he cannot get into a sort of a trial by affidavits preferring one set to other and thus, concluding trial itself - Browsing through affidavit if there emerges averment of facts which in a trial, if believed, would nonsuit landlord, leave ought to be granted - Order set aside - Leave to appeal granted - Appeal allowed.
