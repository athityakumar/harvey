Jogdhayan v Babu Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 146; (1983) 1 SCC 26; AIR 1983 SC 57; 1983 (9) ALR 90; 1982(2) SCALE 1061; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 844; 1983 UJ 15

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Agreement of sale - Suit for pre-emption and possession - Partial payment of consideration - Effect - Appellant filed an execution case before Executing Court to get possession of suit land - Executing Court held that short deposit of 25 paise was due to bona fide mistake on part of degree holder and overruled objection of judgment/debtor, taking view that in interest of justice default on part of decree-holder should be condoned - Judgment-debtor preferred appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court set aside order of Executing Court - Appellant filed second execution appeal before Single Judge and same was dismissed - Appellant prayed for leave to appeal under Letters Patent and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether first execution Appellate Court was justified in holding that amount directed to be deposited was not deposited as it fell short by 25 paise - Held, under O. 20, r. 14 of CPC, plaintiff decree-holder, in order to get delivery of possession of land, had to fulfill two conditions that he had to deposit in Court purchase money together with cost, if any, decreed against him and deposit must be made on or before date fixed by Court - Admitted position was that appellant deposited entire amount of purchase money together with costs decreed against him, less 25 paise within time fixed by Court and 25 paise too was deposited, but beyond time - Executing Court held that short deposit was due to bona fide mistake, while executing Appellate Court held that it was not due to any bona fide mistake, but it was default and thereby executing Appellate Court deprived decree-holder of legitimate fruits of decree he obtained in all Courts - Finding of first executing Appellate Court that non-deposit could not be due to any bona fide mistake, was absolutely untenable for reason that while appellant had deposited in total Rs. 17,936.00 from time to time as directed by Courts, there was absolutely no reason as to why he would not have deposited 25 paise, unless it was due to mistake - This was pre-eminently case in which first execution Appellate Court ought to have exercised its discretionary powers u/s. 148 of CPC and accepted delayed deposit of 25 paise, 85 was done by original executing Court - Orders passed by HC as well as first execution Appellate Court was set aside and restore order of original executing Court - Appeal allowed.
