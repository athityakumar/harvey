Lt. Col. Prithi Pal Singh Bedi etc. v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Y.V. Chandrachud, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 193; (1982) 3 SCC 140; (1982) SCC (Cr) 642; AIR 1982 SC 1413; 1983 CRLJ 647; 1982 CrLR(SC) 523; 1983 (1) LLN 9; 1982(1) SCALE 676; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 393; 1982 (2) SLJ 582; 1983 (1) SLR 365; 1982 UJ 695

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Court Martial, Legal Advisor, Attorney-General

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution - Army Act, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Army Rules, 1954 - Confidential reports - Requirement - Violation - Omission - Petitioner/Commanding officer on part of his duty had to write interim confidential reports of officers subordinate to him - Petitioner was charged u/s.45 of 1950 Act and called upon to face trial by General Court Martial - Petitioner contends that order issued by Court Martial was not in legality - Hence instant petitions - Whether order issued by Court Marital was justified - Held, it was not possible to accept that law prescribed for trial of offences by court martial must satisfy requirement of art. 21 of Constitution - In application to Armed Forces art.21 was modified by enactment of procedure in 1950 Act - Composition of Court-Martial was provided by r.40 of 1954 Rules and there was nothing to show that r.40 had violated - It was clear that in absence of request from petitioner as-required by r.2S, he failed to comply with rr. 22, 23 and 24 and would not vitiate trial by General Court-Martial - Hence, failure to comply with requirements of rr. 22, 23 and 24 petitioner inaction or omission would had no impact on order convened by Court-Martial - Court Martial Appellate Court had power to determine question necessary to be determined in order to do justice - Petitions disposed of.
