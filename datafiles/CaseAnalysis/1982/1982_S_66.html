Miss Veena Sethi v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 57; (1982) SCC (Cr) 511; (1982) 2 SCC 583; AIR 1983 SC 339; 1982 BLJ 616; 1983 CRLJ 675; 1982 CRLR 303; 1982(1) SCALE 793; 1982 UJ 543

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Release of prisoners - Detainees were insane but later declared sane - Jail authorities not taking proper steps to release them - Prisoners were detained for period ranging from 19 years to 37 years - Some were convicted, some acquitted and trials were pending against some of them - No proper medical check-ups - Held, they should be released because even if they are proved guilty period they have undergone would exceed maximum imprisonment that they might be awarded - Order accordingly.
