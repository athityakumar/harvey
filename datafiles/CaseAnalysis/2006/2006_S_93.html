Ravikant S Patil v Sarvabhouma S. Bagali

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1253; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 417; (2007) 1 SCC 673; 2007(2) ALL MR 443; 2007 (1) ALT 55; 2007 (2) CTC 349; JT 2006 (10) SC 578; 2007 (1) KarLJ 398; 2007 (1) KLT 685; 2006(12) SCALE 295; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1156

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Date Of Nomination

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191(1)(e) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 374, 389 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 218, 366, 376, 392 and 466 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 7(b), 8(1), 8(3), 8(4), 36(2)(a), 100 and 116A - Election of appellant was challenged by respondent on ground that appellant was not qualified to contest election - It was alleged that on date of filing of nominations and on date of declaration of results, appellant was disqualified for in view of ss. 8(1), 8(3) and 8(4) of Representation of People Act, 1951, as he had been convicted for an offence punishable u/ss. 366 and 376 of IPC, 1860 - Whether appellant was rightly disqualified to contest election? - Appellant's stand of being qualified to contest election was not either on basis of subsequent acquittal or on basis of stay of execution of sentence, but based on stay of conviction - Held, order granting stay of conviction is not rule but is an exception to be resorted to in rare cases depending upon facts of a case - Such power should be exercised only in exceptional circumstances where failure to stay conviction, would lead to injustice and irreversible consequences - Where execution of sentence is stayed, conviction continues to operate - But where conviction itself is stayed, effect is that conviction will not be operative from date of stay - An order of stay, of course, does not render conviction non-existent, but only non-operative - Be that as it may - In present case, an application was filed specifically seeking stay of order of conviction - Held, decision of High Court that appellant was disqualified as on date of nomination and that his nomination was improperly accepted cannot be sustained - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
