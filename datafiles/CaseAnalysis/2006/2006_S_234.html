Munna Chanda v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 67; (2006) 3 SCC 752; AIR 2006 SC 3555; 2006 CRLJ 1632; 2006 (1) JCC 457; JT 2006 (3) SC 366; 2006 (4) SCJ 147; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 467

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, ss.34, 302, 149 - No direct evidence and eye witness to occurrence - Whether all accused would be convicted with aid of s.149? - Held, it is essential to prove that person sought to be charged with an offence with aid of s.149 was a member of unlawful assembly at time offence was committed - Appellants herein were not armed with weapons - As it has not been established that appellants were members of an unlawful assembly, they could not have been convicted for commission of an offence under ss 302/149 - Appeals allowed.
