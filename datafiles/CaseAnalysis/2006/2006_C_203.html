S. A. Builders Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals) and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1075; (2007) 15 SCC 147; [2007] 289 ITR 26; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 360A; 2006(14) SCALE 94; 2006 (8) SCJ 628; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1077; [2007] 158 TAXMAN 230

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax - Section 32AB, Income Tax Act, 1961 - Civil construction - Appellant-assessee claimed deduction under s. 32AB - Tribunal rejected the claim of the assessee on the ground that the assessee was engaged in the business of civil construction and was not carrying on any manufacturing activity and secondly, that the claim was not based on facts on record - Assessee did not place any material to show how the assessee is entitled to such deduction - Held, assessee not entitled to the deduction.
