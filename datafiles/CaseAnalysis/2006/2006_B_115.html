Mcdermott International Inc v Burn Standard Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1268; (2006) 11 SCC 181; 2006 (5) ALD(SC) 84; 2007 (1) CalLT(SC) 36; 2007 (3) CompLJ 213; JT 2006 (11) SC 376; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 456; 2006(6) SCALE 220; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1992

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2, 31 and 34 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961 - Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1992 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - ONGC appointed contractors to fulfill substantial portions of its off-shore construction requirements - Burn Standard Company Limited (for short "BSCL") - Contracts contained arbitration agreements - BSCL and Mcdermott International Inc. (for short "MII") entered into Technical Collaboration Agreement - Disputes arose - Arbitration clause invoked - S. 34 of the Act praying for setting aside the final award - Changes in 1996 Act - Public Policy - Supervisory jurisdiction - Delay and disruption - Method of computation of damages - Determination of actual loss - Indirect and consequential loss measurement - Interest - Held, it is evident that as the parties did not intend that time was to be of the essence of the contract on the expiry whereof the contract became voidable but by reason thereof the parties shall never be deprived of damages - Held, the additional award, in our opinion, is not vitiated in law - We may state that both the partial award and the final award are subject matter of challenge u/s. 34 of the Act - In this case, given the long lapse of time, it will be in furtherance of justice to reduce to 7% - As regards certain other contentions, in view of the fact that the same relate to pure questions of fact and appreciation of evidence, we do not think it necessary to advert to the said contentions in the present case - Allowed in part - Award of the Learned Arbitrator modified.
