Employees State Insurance Corporation v Santhakumar

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1000; (2007) 1 SCC 584; 2007 (5) AWC 5215; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 132; JT 2006 (10) SC 549; 2007 (1) KLT 133; 2007 (2) LLJ 3; 2007 (147) PLR 191; 2006(12) SCALE 171; 2006 (7) SCJ 219; 2007 (2) SLR 225; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 212

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Arrears Of Land Revenue, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Andhra Pradesh Assigned Lands (Prohibition of Transfers) Act, 1977, Employees State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950

Summary: Insurance - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 40, 45A, 45B, 45C, 54, 55, 75, 77 and 77 (I-A) - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 - Andhra Pradesh Assigned Lands (Prohibition of Transfers) Act, 1977 - Employees State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950 - Whether judgment of full bench of the Kerala High Court, which held that the limitation prescribed u/s. 77 restricting the claim for a period of five years clearly indicated by fact that the contribution for a period of more than five years cannot be claimed by Corporation - No, court held, s. 77 of the Act relates to commencement of proceedings before the E.S.I. Court - The proviso to sub-s. 77 of Act cannot independently give any meaning without reference to the main provision, namely, s. 77 of Act, therefore, the proviso to cl.(b) of s. 77(lA) of the Act, fixing the period of five years for the claim made by corporation, will apply only in respect of claim made by the corporation before the E.S.I. Court and to no other proceedings - Appeals disposed of.
