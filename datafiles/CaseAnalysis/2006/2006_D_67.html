Mohan Raj v Dimbeswari Saikia & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 753; (2007) 15 SCC 115; AIR 2007 SC 232; 2007 CRLJ 52; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 139; 2006(11) SCALE 330; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure (Assam Amendment) Act, 1983 - Criminal Procedure Code (Assam Repealing) Act, 1986, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Assam General Clauses Act, 1915, s. 6 - Interpretation and application of provisions of CrPC, 1973 as amended by State of Assam by CrPC (Assam Amendment) Act, 1983 and CrPC (Assam Repealing) Act, 1986 falls for consideration - HC opined that once sanction had been granted by State of Assam, there was no bar in proceeding with complaint case - Appeal against - Held, Once such a certificate is given, Court has no other option but to dismiss complaint - Accused derives a vested or accrued right as soon as said certificate is issued - Appeal allowed.
