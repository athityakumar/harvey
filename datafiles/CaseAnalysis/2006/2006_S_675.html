Union of India and Others v Subedar Devassy

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1334; (2006) 1 SCC 613; AIR 2006 SC 909; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 30; 2006 (62) ALR 801; 2006 (2) AWC 1350; 2006 CRLJ 971; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 103; JT 2006 (1) SC 382; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 446; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 702; 2006(1) SCALE 334; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 303; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 745

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solicitor General, Contumacious Conduct

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 12, 15 - contempt proceedings - Instant appeal was filed against order passed by HC - Held, if any party concerned is aggrieved by order which in its opinion is wrong or against rules or its implementation is neither practicable nor feasible, it should always either approach Court that passed order or invoke jurisdiction of Appellate Court. Rightness or wrongness of order cannot be urged in contempt proceedings. Right or wrong, order has to be obeyed. Flouting an order of Court would render party liable for contempt. While dealing with an application for contempt Court cannot traverse beyond order, non-compliance with which is alleged. In other words, it cannot say what should not have been done or what should have been done. It cannot traverse beyond order. It cannot test correctness or otherwise of order or give additional direction or delete any direction. That would be exercising review jurisdiction while dealing with an application for initiation of contempt proceedings. The same would be impermissible and indefensible. SC notice that pursuant to direction given by HC, exercise directed to be undertaken was in fact undertaken. The respondent was given promotion and in meantime he has retired. That being so, it is not necessary to go into correctness of direction given, except clarifying position in law. Appeal Disposed of.
