State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Yogesh Chandra Dubey and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 476; (2006) 8 SCC 67; 2006 (6) AWC 5594; JT 2006 (8) SC 595; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 932; 2006(9) SCALE 73; 2006 (3) ShimLC 284; 2006 (5) SLR 834; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 858; 2007 (1) UC 74

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 12 - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 41 - Madhya Pradesh Public Health Engineering Department (Non-Gazetted) Service (Conditions of Service and Recruitment) Rules, 1976 - Whether respondents, who were engaged on daily wages, are entitled to claim minimum of pay scale attached to post in which they had been working with applicable allowances? - Held, only because a person is given a particular status, same would not mean that his other terms and conditions of service would not be governed by contract of employment or other statute(s) operating in field - As respondents did not hold any post, in our opinion, they are not entitled to any scale of pay - Appeal allowed.
