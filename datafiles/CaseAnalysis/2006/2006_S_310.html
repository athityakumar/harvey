State of Andhra Pradesh v S. Rayappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 46; (2006) 4 SCC 512; AIR 2006 SC 3709; 2006 CRLJ 1616; 2006 (1) JCC 396; JT 2006 (2) SC 392; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 966; 2006(2) SCALE 321; 2006 (3) SCJ 668; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 144, 148, 149, 302 - Conviction by trial Court - HC acquitted all accused - Appeal against acquittal - HC doubted presence of PW1 and PW 2 - Held, in view of direct evidence of prosecution witnesses regarding presence of PW 2 at spot at time of inquest, report finding recorded by HC is clearly perverse - Testimony of a witness otherwise inspiring confidence cannot be discarded on ground that he being a relation of deceased is an interested witness - Discrepancy, which does not materially affect prosecution case, does not create any infirmities - Order of Trial Court convicting respondents is restored - Appeals allowed.
