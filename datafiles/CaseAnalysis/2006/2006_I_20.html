Indian Airlines Limited v Prabha D. Kanan

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1331; (2006) 1 SCC 67; AIR 2007 SC 548; 2006 (7) AWC 7499; JT 2006 (10) SC 334; 2007 (2) LLJ 113; 2006(12) SCALE 58; 2007 (7) SCJ 270; 2007 (1) SLR 813; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1027; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 423

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Airlines (Flying Crew) Service Regulations, Central Inland Water Transport Corporation Ltd. Service Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1979, Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Service - Customs Act, 1962, s. 114 (i) - Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, regn. 13 - Termination - Reinstatement - Full Wages - Justifiability - Respondent was terminated from services u/reg. 13 of 1994, Act for her involvement in racket of dealing in foreign exchange - Tribunal exonerated respondent and imposed penalties u/s. 114 (i) of the Act - Respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC held regulation 13 as ultra vires - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that HC committed a manifest error in holding regn. 13 to be unconstitutional - Held, in instant case regn. 13 was invoked when termination of services was effected by reason of some act on part of respondent, which does not amount to misconduct - Evidently, there was no provision for appeal since decision was taken by highest authority of corporate entity - However, in absence of appeal, employee was not remediless as power of judicial review would be applicable - Thus, while exercising power under judicial review, HC had to delve deeper into matter and require deeper scrutiny - Doctrine of unreasonableness had now given way to doctrine of proportionality - Relied on Ajit Kumar Nag v G.M.(P.J.)Indian Oil Corporation Limited Haldia and Others 2005 Indlaw SC 585 ,held regn. 13 as intra vires - Hence, penalty imposed by Tribunal was not sustainable and interest of justice would be sub-served if nature of relief granted by HC was upheld - Appeals partly allowed.
