Kishori Lal v Sales Officer, District Land Development Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 444; (2006) 7 SCC 496; 2006 (4) BC 527; 2007 (1) CGLJ 323; JT 2006 (8) SC 200; 2006(8) SCALE 521; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 274; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2907

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Banking & Finance - Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 - Land Development Bank Act, s. 18(2)(b) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 226, 227 - Madhya Pradesh Sahakari Bhoomi Vikas Bank Rules, 1967, r. 15(d) - Appellant obtained a sum of Rs. 6, 473.69 by way of loan from said Co-operative Society - Failed to repay amount - Lands mortgaged to Bank sold - Sale certificate issued in name of auction purchaser - Plea that appellant having been found to be a minor, contract was void and no notice served to him - Held, it is evident that there has been no proper service of notice upon appellant - HC did not arrive at a finding that there was a valid service of notice - HC committed an error in interfering with findings of fact arrived at by Board of Revenue - Appellant should be directed to deposit entire auction money with interest thereupon @6% per annum - On such deposit being made, auction shall stand set aside and possession of property shall be restored to appellant - Appeal allowed.
