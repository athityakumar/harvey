Messrs Sneh Enterprises v Commissioner of Customs, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 480; (2006) 7 SCC 714; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 7; JT 2006 (8) SC 587; 2006(9) SCALE 95; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Anti-Dumping; Customs

Keywords: Importation

Summary: [A.] Anti-Dumping - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, ss. 2, 3(6), 9A - Finance (No. 2) Act, 2004, s. 26 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 15(1), 15(A), 3(6) - Antidumping duty - Held, Anti-dumping duty would be payable in respect of goods which have already entered Indian Territory and are warehoused - Goods were cleared by Customs Authorities without imposing any duty - It was at a later date duties were sought to be imposed, wherefor a show cause notice was issued - In absence of a statute, contract between parties would not be superceded - Appeal allowed.
