M.R. Kudva v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 958; (2007) 2 SCC 772; AIR 2007 SC 568; 2007 CRLJ 763; 2007 (1) JCC 529; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 868; 2006(14) SCALE 118; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1142

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 120B/420, 468, 471; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1) - Conviction and sentence - Application before High Court under ss. 482/427 of Code of Criminal Procedure, praying, inter alia, therein that sentences imposed upon in both the cases be directed to run concurrently dismissed - Appeal against - Held, High Court could not have exercised its inherent jurisdiction in a case of this nature as it had not exercised such jurisdiction while passing the judgments in appeal - S. 482 of the Code was not an appropriate remedy having regard to the fact that neither the Trial Judge, nor the High Court while passing the judgments of conviction and sentence indicated that the sentences passed against the appellant in both the cases shall run concurrently - Appeal dismissed.
