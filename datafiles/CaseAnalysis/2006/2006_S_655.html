Shiva Nath Prasad and Another v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 34; (2006) 2 SCC 757; AIR 2006 SC 1181; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 76; 2006 CRLJ 1258; 2006 (127) DLT 198; 2006 (1) JCC 269; JT 2006 (2) SC 172; 2006(2) SCALE 217; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 98; 2006 (1) UC 655

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 120B, 204, 405, 406, 417, 420 - CrPC, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91, 92 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 3 - HC refused quashing of process by Chief Judicial Magistrate in respect of alleged offences - Appeals against - Held, allegation is that beneficial interest (enforceable not against assets but against trustees) is dishonestly misappropriated by accused - Complaint is about dishonestly setting up personal title to trust property - Credentiality of complainant at this stage is not relevant - Complaint should not be dismissed at initial stage on ground of alleged malafides of complainant - Appeals dismissed.
