Kamal Kumar Dutta and Another v Ruby General Hospital Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 375; (2006) 7 SCC 613; JT 2006 (7) SC 333; 2006(7) SCALE 668; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 462

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Articles Of Association, Directorship, Status Quo, Debenture Holder, Fiduciary Duty, Civil Procedure Code, Code of Civil Procedure, CPC

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398, 402 - CPC, 1908, ss. 100A, 4(1) - Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002 - Alleging various acts and oppression and mis-management in affairs of company before CLB - CLB given some directions - Finding that final decision was taken in absence of main promoter of company ie, appellant and this reflects complete lack of probity on part of Directors in passing such a resolution - Appeal before Single Judge - Finding that appellants have failed to make out a case u/s. 397 of Act for winding up of company on ground that it was just and equitable that the company should be wound up - Appeal against - Whether appeal against order of learned Single Judge lies before DB under Letters Patent or not? - Whether acts of company were oppressive to any member or members justifying winding up as just and equitable - Held, where appeal has been decided from an original order by a single Judge, no further appeal shall lie to DB of same HC - Act of oppression by Board of Directors proved - Confirmed order and direction of CLB - Appeals allowed.
