Utha Moidu Haji v Kuningarath Kunhabdulla and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1286; (2007) 14 SCC 792; 2007 (5) AWC 4965; 2006(14) SCALE 156; 2006 (7) SCJ 248; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 886

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 6(1), 7, 8 and 27, arts. 59, 60, 60(a) and 65 - Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 91 and 114 - Adverse possession - Limitation - Applicability of - Whether provisions of art. 60 of Sch. appended to Limitation Act and other provisions of Limitation Act have any applicability? - If plaintiff is in possession of a property, he may file a suit for declaration that deed is not binding upon him but if he is not in possession thereof, even under a void transaction, right by way of adverse possession may be claimed - Thus, it is not correct to contend that provisions of Limitation Act would have no application at all in event transaction is held to be void - Held, each provision laid down in law of limitation is applicable to legal proceedings instituted by litigant - Appeal allowed.
