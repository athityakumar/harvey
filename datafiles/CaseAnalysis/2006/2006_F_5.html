U.T. of Dadra and Haveli and Another v Fatehsinh Mohansinh Chauhan

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 376; (2006) 7 SCC 529; 2006 CRLJ 4636; 2006 (3) JCC 1363; JT 2006 (7) SC 419; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 113; 2006(8) SCALE 36; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 522

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 161, 190(1)(b), 207, 311, 313, 397, 401, 482 - CrPC, 1898, s. 540 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114(g), 165 - CPC, 1908, o. 16, r. 14 - Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 25, 29 - Application for purpose of summoning Collector, Dadra and Nagar Haveli, Silvassa to rebut plea of alibi taken by respondent allowed - HC set aside order of Sessions Judge holding that grant of application inevitably result in permitting prosecution to fill in lacuna in prosecution case - Whether order passed by Sessions Judge comes within parameters of s.311 CrPC, which confers power on Court to summon a material witness or examine a person present in Court? - Held, calling a witness or re-examining a witness already examined for purpose of finding out truth in order to enable Court to arrive at a just decision of case cannot be dubbed as 'filling in a lacuna in prosecution case' unless facts and circumstances of case make it apparent that exercise of power by Court would result in causing serious prejudice to accused resulting in miscarriage of justice - It was for Sessions Judge to decide whether for just and fair decision of case, evidence of Collector is necessary or not and he having come to a conclusion that evidence of Collector was necessary for just and fair decision of case, order passed by him could not have been set aside by HC on ground that it would amount to filling in lacuna in prosecution case - Appeal allowed.
