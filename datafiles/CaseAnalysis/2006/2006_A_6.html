S. Venugopal v A. Karruppusami and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 230; (2006) 4 SCC 507; AIR 2006 SC 1930; 2006 (63) ALR 471; 2006 (2) CTC 615; JT 2006 (5) SC 218; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 29; 2006 (3) RLW 2199; 2006(3) SCALE 656

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960 - (A)whether there was a bona fide personal need of landlord for doing jewellery business? - Held, landlord has clearly stated that he needed one shop for carrying on his own jewellery business and premises in question were located in a commercial area in city of Coimbatore - Reading deposition as a whole, it would appear that landlord, in categorical terms, has repeatedly asserted that he needed premises in question for running his own business in jewellery - There was a bona fide personal need - (B) whether building was or was not in a dilapidated condition? - Further held, question as to whether building was or was not in a dilapidated condition is not relevant because s. 14(l)(b) does not contemplate that building sought to be demolished must necessarily be in a dilapidated condition - Even if a building is not in a dilapidated condition, it may be demolished for purpose of erecting a new building on same site - Landlord realises that if he demolishes old structure and erects a new multi-storied building, he will get a much better return of his investment - Landlord established his case that he required premises for its demolition and erection of a new building on same site - Appeal allowed.
