Epuru Sudhakar and Another v Government of A.P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 681; (2006) 8 SCC 161; AIR 2006 SC 3385; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 877; JT 2006 (9) SC 72; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1555; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 616; 2006(10) SCALE 98; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 161, 32 and 72 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 302 and 304(1) - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 432 and 433 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 14 and 21 - Challenge to order passed by Govt. of Andhra Pradesh, whereby respondent No 2 was granted remission of unexpired period of about seven years imprisonment - Conviction u/s. 109 of IPC, 1860 and custodial sentence of 10 years' rigorous imprisonment was imposed - Power for judicial review - Held, if it comes to knowledge of Govt. that pardon has been obtained on basis of manifest mistake or patent misrepresentation or fraud, same can be rescinded or cancelled - Collector does not appear to have made any independent enquiry on his own - Report shows as to how extraneous materials which had no relevance formed foundation of his report - Irrelevant and extraneous materials entered into decision making process, thereby vitiating it - Petitions partly allowed.
