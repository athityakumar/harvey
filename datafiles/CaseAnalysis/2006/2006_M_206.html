B.S.N.L. and Others v Messrs Subash Chandra Kanchan and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 494; (2006) 8 SCC 279; AIR 2006 SC 3335; 2007 (1) AWC 71; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 133; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1828; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 499; 2006(9) SCALE 217; 2006 (7) SCJ 139; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 93; 2007 (1) UC 22

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Advocates & Judges - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Contract entered by parties for work of construction of 9 Nos Type-V quarters - Application u/s. 11 by respondent - Appointed arbitrator, recording that counsel for opposite party has no objection - Prayer for modification - Plea that, no objection against appointment of Shri B C Bhattacharya is not correct and in fact he had objection - HC refused to recall its order - Held, apart from failure on part of Managing Director of Appellant to appoint an arbitrator within specified time, Appellants evidently waived their right under arbitration agreement - A concession made by such an advocate is binding on party whom he represents - Appeal dismissed.
