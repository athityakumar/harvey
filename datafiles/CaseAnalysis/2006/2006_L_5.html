L.K. Verma v H.M.T. Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 29; (2006) 2 SCC 269; AIR 2006 SC 975; 2006 (5) AWC 4807; JT 2006 (2) SC 99; 2006(2) SCALE 90; 2006 (2) SCJ 137; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 959; 2006 (2) SLR 369; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 747

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Factories (Safety Officers) Rules, 1984

Summary: [A.] Service - Factories Act, 1948, s. 40B - Uttar Pradesh Factories (Safety Officers) Rules, 1984, rr. 14, 14(3), 8 - Misconduct - Suspension from service - Appeal against - Held, in departmental proceedings, Appellant, herein did not deny or dispute that he had used indecent language and also abused officer - Verbal abuse has been held to be sufficient for inflicting a punishment of dismissal - No case is made out for interfering with impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
