Canara Bank and Others v Swapan Kumar Pani and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 418; (2006) 3 SCC 251; AIR 2006 SC 1297; 2006 (5) AWC 5153; 2006 (3) CTC 203; 2006 (2) ESC 141; JT 2006 (3) SC 472; 2006(2) SCALE 604; 2006 (3) SCJ 302; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 631; 2006 (2) SLR 832; 2006 (2) Supreme 343; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1239

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, s. 19 - Canara Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline &Appeal) Regulations, 1976, regns. 18, 21(3), 24, 6 - Canara Bank Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976, regns. 3(1), 24 - First respondent working as Accountant - Misconduct - Charge sheet quashed but denied back wages - Held, HC, however, committed an error in granting liberty to bank to initiate a fresh inquiry - If HC was of opinion that new materials purported to have been found were not sufficient for initiation of enquiry in question, on what basis liberty was given to bank to initiate a fresh inquiry, more so when misconduct, if any, was committed as far back as in year 1985 - Appeals disposed of.
