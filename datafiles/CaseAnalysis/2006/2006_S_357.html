G.M. Tank v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 219; (2006) 5 SCC 446; AIR 2006 SC 2129; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2348; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 309; JT 2006 (11) SC 36; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 143; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 251; 2006 (3) RLW 2480; 2006(5) SCALE 582; 2006 (4) SCJ 13; 2006 (4) SLR 10; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(e), 5(2) - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 329, 338, 353 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Gujarat Civil Services Conduct Rules, 1971, r. 3(1) - Allegation that appellant had illegally accumulated excess income by way of gratification - Order of dismissal - HC confirmed dismissal - Appeal against - Whether in case of no evidence, employee can be dismissed from service? - Whether acquittal, absolutely on merits amounting to clear exoneration of appellant by Special Court under the Act does ipso facto absolve appellant from liability under disciplinary jurisdiction when charges leveled against appellant in departmental proceedings and criminal proceedings are grounded on same set of facts, charges, circumstances and evidence? - Held, there is no iota of evidence against appellant to hold that appellant is guilty of having illegally accumulated excess income by way of gratification - Respondent failed to prove charges leveled against appellant - Facts and evidence in department as well as criminal proceedings were same without there being any iota of difference - Appeal allowed.
