State of Karnataka and Another v Shreyas Papers Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 7; (2006) 1 SCC 615; AIR 2006 SC 865; 2006 (1) CTC 290; JT 2006 (1) SC 180; 2006(1) SCALE 160; 2006 (2) SCJ 40; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 235; 2006 (144) STC 331

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, ss. 29, 29(1), 29(2) - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 13, 13(2)(i), 15, 15(1), 2(f-2) - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 100 - Whether purchaser of assets of a concern sold by a State Financial Corporation, in exercise of its powers u/s. 29 of 1951 Act would be liable under 1957 Act, for arrears of sales tax of concern whose assets have been transferred? - Under what circumstances does a charge created on a property become unenforceable against a transferee of such a property? - Whether a completely novel relief, not argued/claimed before HC or decided by impugned judgment, may be claimed before this Court? - Held, First Respondent was a purchaser for value without notice of sales tax arrears of Defaulting Company or consequent charge on property - Property in hands of First Respondent was free of charge and it is not open to appellants to enforce liabilities of Defaulting Company in this manner against First Respondent - First Respondent was not liable for tax arrears of Defaulting Company - Appeals dismissed.
