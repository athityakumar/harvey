State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Rajkumar Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 75; (2006) 3 SCC 330; 2006 (2) AllCJ 1116; 2006 (2) AWC 1452; 2006 (2) ESC 230; JT 2006 (3) SC 167; 2006(2) SCALE 785; 2006 (3) SCJ 713; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 877; 2006 (3) SLR 236; 2006 (2) Supreme 448; 2006 (2) UC 713; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1433

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 320, 323(2) - Uttar Pradesh Hill Sub-cadre Rules, 1992 - Uttar Pradesh Public Works Department Subordinate Engineering Rules, 1951, r. 12 - Uttaranchal issued the order not to appoint the selected candidates mentioning two reasons that (1) new reservation policy of State of Uttaranchal is different from that of U P and (2) practical and legal difficulties 'in such a situation' in giving appointments to candidates recommended by UPPSC - HC took view that recommendations made by UPPSC of erstwhile State of U P were binding on new State of Uttaranchal - Appeal against - Held, all posts advertised for plain cadre have been filled up - State has rightly taken stand that there was no scope for appointing non-official respondents - art.14 does not envisage negative equality, and if State committed mistake it cannot be forced to perpetuate same mistake - Appeal allowed.
