Appasaheb Peerappa Chandgade v Devendra Peerappa Chandgade and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1055; (2007) 1 SCC 521; AIR 2007 SC 218; 2007 (1) AWC 204; 2007 (1) KarLJ 177; 2006(11) SCALE 184

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Family Business

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 - Partition - Joint Hindu family property - Burden of proof - Whether properties for which a decree was passed by trial court was joint Hindu family property or those were self-acquired property of defendants and their children? - Held, so far legal proposition is concerned, there is no gainsaying that whenever a suit for partition and determination of share and possession thereof is filed, then initial burden is on plaintiff to show that entire property was a Joint Hindu Family property and after initial discharge of burden, it shifts on defendants to show that property claimed by them was not purchased out of joint family nucleus and it was purchased independent of them but where it is established that family possessed some joint property which from its nature and relative value may have formed nucleus from which property in question may have been acquired, burden shifts to party alleging self-acquisition to establish affirmatively that property was acquired without aid of joint family property - Therefore, so far as proposition of law is concerned, initial burden is on person who claims that it was joint family property but after initial discharge of burden, it shifts to party who claims that property has been purchased by him through his own source and not from joint family nucleus - Defendant-respondents herein have failed to prove that properties mentioned in aforesaid transactions were acquired by them as self-acquired property, judgment under appeal cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
