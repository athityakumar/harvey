N. Srinivasa Rao v Spl. Court Under A.P. Land Grabbing (Prohibition) and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 612; (2006) 4 SCC 214; AIR 2006 SC 3691; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 10; 2006(3) SCALE 386; 2006 (4) SCJ 328; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Right To Work

Summary: Land & Property - Hyderabad Tenancy and Agricultural Land Act, 1950 - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Land Grabbing Act, 1982 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Specific Relief Act, 1968 - Constitution of India, 1950 - One Kaneez Fatima Begum was former owner of lands - One Uppari Ramaiah, who was her tenant purchased 14 acres and 6 guntas of land from Kaneez Fatima Begum - Prior to execution of said deed, Uppari Ramaiah is purported to have sold an extent of 20, 086 square yards from out of total area measuring 14 acres and 6 guntas to one Mir Riyasat Ali by a sale deed - Said Mir Riyasat Ali sold 8, 866 square yards to Smt P Neelakan-teswaramma and to one Chandra Ramalingaiah - Whether actions arising out of dispute raised by heirs of Uppari Rammaiah can be said to attract provisions of A P Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982? - Held, admittedly, transferees from Mir Riyasat Ali and Chandra Ramalingaiah as also P Neelakanteswaramma have been in possession of properties in dispute and at no point of time had their possession been disturbed - Attempts by heirs of Uppari Ramaiah to dispossess said transferees could at best be said to be an attempt to gain possession of lands without actually obtaining possession thereof, which would not constitute an act of land grabbing within meaning of A P Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - In order to constitute an act of land grabbing, an attempt to dispossess must be followed by actual dispossession which would then constitute land grabbing so as to attract penal provisions of 1982 Act - Appeals disposed of.
