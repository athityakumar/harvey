National Highways Authority of India and Another v Bumihiway Ddb Limited (JV) and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 520; (2006) 10 SCC 763; 2007 (1) AWC 133; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 879; 2006(9) SCALE 564; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 586

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(3), 11(4), 11(6), 11(7), 11(8), 15(1)(a), 15(2), 34, 37, 9 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 227 - HC substituted another arbitrator in place of arbitrator appointed in agreement -What is scope of jurisdiction of Court on resignation of an arbitrator considering a specific mandate and mechanism u/s. 15(2) and cl. 67.3 of Contract? - Whether on resignation of one of arbitrators, statutory provision that comes into play is s. 15(2) or s. 11(6)? - Whether an Arbitration clause, which is a sacrosanct clause, can be rewritten by appointment of a judicial arbitrator when no qualification thereof is provided in agreement? - Whether consent given by one of parties is enough for clause to be re-written? - Held, HC failed to appreciate that u/s. 15(2) of Act on termination of mandate of Presiding Arbitrator, two nominated arbitrators were first required to reach a consensus and on their failure to arrive at a consensus only respondent No 2 was authorized to make appointment - Appeal allowed.
