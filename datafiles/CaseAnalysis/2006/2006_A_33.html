Indochem Electronic and Another v Additional Collector of Customs, A.P

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 372; (2006) 3 SCC 721; 2006 (63) ALR 305; 2006 (2) AWC 1744; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 142; 2006 (6) CCC 91; 2006 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2006 (3) CTC 74; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 92; JT 2006 (3) SC 174; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 237; 2006 (143) PLR 143; 2006(2) SCALE 565; 2006 (3) SCJ 724; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 584

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(e), 2(g), 12(3) and 14 - Whether appellant can be made liable even after expiry of warranty? - Appellant itself, it may be inferred that it voluntarily undertook to meet requirements of respondent relating to mal-functioning etc. of said system despite expiry of period of warranty - For all intent and purport, period of warranty, thus, stood extended - Held, as defects in system including manufacturing defects, if any, were found not only during period of warranty but also during extended period, and as appellant itself undertook to attend to complaints received in that behalf, it is too late for it now to contend that in view of fact that period of contract or warranty expired, it had no liability therefor - Appeal dismissed.
