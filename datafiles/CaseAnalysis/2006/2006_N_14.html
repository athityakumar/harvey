Nagar Nigam, Meerut v Al Faheem Meat Exports Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1290; (2006) 13 SCC 382; 2007 (2) AWC 1366; JT 2007 (1) SC 484; 2007(1) SCALE 88; 2006 (8) SCJ 702; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 354; 2007 (1) UC 597; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 398

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Adhiniyam, 1959

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 136 - Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Adhiniyam, 1959 - Notification issued - Contract to grant license to run slaughter house - State of U.P. rejected proposal of municipal corp. - (A) Whether High Court is justified in fixing terms and conditions of Contract for giving tender of slaughter house which in turn violates art. 14 of constitution? - Relied on Association of Registration Plates v. Union of India and Ors. 2004 Indlaw SC 1031 - Held, HC erred in interfering with the terms and conditions of contract - (B) Whether notification issued by municipal corp. suffers from any arbitrariness and malfunctions? - Held, not arbitrary and HC not justified in interference with notification and health of inhabitants of locality to be taken in consideration which is fundamental right u/art. 21 - Further held, State to look at matter and to consider feasibility of grant of license to run slaughter house on the condition that plant should be modernized by licensee - Appeal allowed without any costs.
