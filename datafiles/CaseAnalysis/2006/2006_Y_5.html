Yedla Srinivasa Rao v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1420; (2006) 11 SCC 615; 2006 (3) JCC 1623; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 474; 2006(9) SCALE 692; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 375, 376, 376(2), 417, 90 - Misconception of fact - Sexual intercourse - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused committed forcible sexual intercourse with prosecutrix/PW1 against her will and consent and accused promised that he would marry her subsequently accused were not agreeing for marriage - PW1 filed case against accused u/ss. 376,417 IPC before Trial Court - Trial Court after recording all necessary evidence, acquitted accused for offence u/ss. 417, 376 IPC - Aggrieved by order, appeal was preferred before HC - HC found testimony of PW1 being truthful that she had sexual intercourse with accused with promise to marry attracts category of offence punishable u/ss. 417, 376 IPC and further found that in view of statement of prosecutrix, accused gave promise which was false right from beginning and under misconception of fact prosecutrix submitted to lust of accused - Therefore, HC found accused guilty u/ss. 417, 376 IPC and sentenced accused to undergo rigorous imprisonment for period of seven years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, Deelip Singh Alias Dilip Kumar v. State of Bihar, 2004 Indlaw SC 999, relied on consent which had been obtained by accused was not voluntary one which was given by her under misconception of fact that accused would marry her but this was not consent in law - This was more evident from testimony of PW1 as well as PW6 who was functioning as Panchayat where the accused admitted that he had committed sexual intercourse and promised to marry her but he absconded despite the promise made before Panchayat, that shows that accused had no intention to marry her right from beginning and committed sexual intercourse totally under misconception of fact by prosecutrix that he would marry her - Therefore, order of HC was satisfied by SC and conviction and sentence awarded to appellant was correct - Appeal dismissed.
