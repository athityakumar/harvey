A.P. Public Service Commission v K. Sudharshan Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 640; (2006) 5 SCC 505; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1160; AIR 2006 SC 2540; 2006 (7) AWC 7177; JT 2006 (6) SC 145; 2006(6) SCALE 412; 2006 (5) SCJ 635; 2006 (5) SLR 97; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 188; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2372

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Whether respondents had acted in a manner contrary to law by giving advantage of weightage marks while preparing merit list for preparation of seniority list? - Held, if this Court had intended that weightage given to concerned candidates was not to be counted towards their position in merit list, it would have said so explicitly - On other hand, while mentioning fact of their appointment on strength of such weightage this Court went on to say that such candidates would not be adversely affected by judgment - In other words, decision rendered in judgment would not adversely affect their existing service conditions - Appeal allowed.
