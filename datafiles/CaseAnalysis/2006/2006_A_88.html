Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited v Anil and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1330; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 432; (2007) 1 SCC 610; JT 2006 (10) SC 297; 2006(11) SCALE 567; 2007 (7) SCJ 201; 2007 (1) SLR 258; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes, Individual Dispute, Immediate Employer

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2-A, 4-K, 6-N and 2(k) - Whether the High Court rightly directed reinstatement of the respondents 1 to 14 and held that the termination of services of respondents by his employers from 01-12-88 was not justified? - Respondents 1 to 14 were not recruited directly in BHEL/appellant company - Respondents had never applied for job in BHEL - The appointment letters appear to have been given by the contractor - BHEL has its own waiting list of workmen, who claimed permanency/ regularization - They were not before the Labour Court - The impugned judgment of the HC is set aside by directing BHEL/appellant to re-employ respondents 1 to 14 directly or through its contractor - This order will not preclude the workmen/respondent from raising an industrial dispute claiming status of direct workmen of the company after joining the recognized union/ concerned union in the appellant company - Held, appeal is disposed of.
