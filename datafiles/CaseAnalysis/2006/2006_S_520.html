Sham Shankar Kankaria v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 456; (2006) 13 SCC 165; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 663; 2006 (3) JCC 1523; JT 2006 (8) SC 538; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 239; 2006(8) SCALE 760; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 709

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Bombay Police Act, 1951, s. 135 - IPC, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 149, 302, 304, 34, 342 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Appeal against conviction - Held, this is a case where basis of conviction of accused is dying declaration - There is no material to show that dying declaration was result of product of imagination, tutoring or prompting - Same appears to have been made by deceased voluntarily - It is trustworthy and has credibility - Prosecution has clearly established that vital blow was given by accused No 1 - Taking into account surroundings facts and nature of weapon allegedly used, correct provision for conviction would be s. 304 Part I - Custodial sentence of 10 years - Conviction u/s.342 as well as sentence are maintained - Appeal partly allowed.
