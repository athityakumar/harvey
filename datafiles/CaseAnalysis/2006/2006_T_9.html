T. N. Godavarman Thirumalpad v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1614; (2006) 5 SCC 28; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 503; 2006(4) SCALE 232; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 1046

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land & Property - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Forest Act, 1927 - Plant - Approval - Validity - State Govt. issued a circular in compliance with directions issued in T.N. Godavarman Thirumulpad (1) v. Union of India 1996 INDLAW SC 2014 for purpose of identification of forest - CEC submitted its report wherein it was concluded that land allotted to Maruti Clean Coal and Power Limited (Maruti) was a forest land and, therefore, prior approval of Central Govt.. under the FC Act was necessary before allowing setting up of coal washery plant by Maruti - State of Chhattisgarh and Maruti disputed that land was part of forest - However, CEC in second report concluded that land allotted to Maruti was not a forest land - Thereafter, various applications and also a letter were filed by Bilaspur Environment Society filing therewith a report of Regional Remote Sensing Services Centre to challenge conclusion contained in second report about land not being forest land and seeking to rely upon settlement record - CEC was directed to again examine entire matter and report - Third report reiterated conclusions and recommendations made in second report that land allotment to Maruti was not of forest land - Question was raised by applicant being concerned about adverse effect on environment of area as a result of grant of lease of forest land for non-forest activities in violation of law - Applicant claimed that undue favour and patronage was extended to Maruti for establishment of coal washery plant in respect of land which was a forest land by wrongly showing in various revenue records - Whether land leased by State of Chhattisgarh to for setting up of coal washery was a part of forest land or not? - Held, second report, considered in detail several notifications, old settlement maps and guidelines of State Govt.. in respect of orange area proposals - It showed that non-forest land could also be included in said proposals and various other documents and for reaching the conclusion that the land in question was not a forest land, in fact, said land was of 'N' and not Village 'R' and that this area was not formally merged and made part of Village 'R' in settlement (bandobast) carried out during 1928-29 - There was no reason to take a view different than one taken by CEC in its second report - Conclusions reached and recommendations made in second report deserve to be accepted - Recommendations of CEC as contained in second and third reports accepted - Applications dismissed.
