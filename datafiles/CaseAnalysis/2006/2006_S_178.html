Punjab State Civil Supplies Corp. Limited v Sikander Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 599; (2006) 3 SCC 736; AIR 2006 SC 1438; 2006 (2) AllCJ 979; 2006 (2) ESC 122; JT 2006 (3) SC 457; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 972; 2006 (143) PLR 206; 2006(2) SCALE 670; 2006 (3) SCJ 742; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 694; 2006 (2) SLR 818; 2006 (2) Supreme 654; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1231

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Companies Act, 1956 - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - CCS (Pension) Rules, 1972 - - Respondents working as Inspector and Field Officer/Supervisor - Shortage of 4513 bags of wheat - Departmental proceedings - Appellate authority directed his reinstatement subject to his depositing remaining 400 bags of wheat - Not reinstated - High Court directed his reinstatement - Upheld in the Supreme Court while relief of backwages was denied - Held, HC failed to consider that the question of negligence in a departmental proceedings and a suit for recovery of money must be viewed differently - Impugned judgment set aside accordingly and the matter is remitted to the HC for consideration of the matter afresh in accordance with law.
