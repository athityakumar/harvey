Mullaperiyar Environmental Protection Forum v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 69; (2006) 3 SCC 643; AIR 2006 SC 1428; JT 2006 (3) SC 31; 2006(2) SCALE 680; 2006 (3) SCJ 196; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Kerala Forest Act, 1961

Summary: [A.] Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 108 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 262, 3, 363, 4 - Kerala Forest Act, 1961, s. 85 - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 11 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Issue relates to safety of Mullaperiyar dam if water level is raised beyond its present level of 136 ft - Whether s 108 of States Reorganisation Act, 1956 is unconstitutional? - Whether jurisdiction of this Court is barred in view of art.262 r/w s 11 of Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956? - Whether art 363 of Constitution bars jurisdiction of this Court? - Whether disputes are liable to be referred to Arbitration? - Whether raising of water level of reservoir from 136 ft to 142 ft would result in jeopardising safety of people and also degradation of environment? - Held, power of Parliament to make law under art 3 and 4 is plenary and traverse over all legislative subjects as are necessary for effectuating a proper reorganisation of States - Art 363 has no applicability to ordinary agreements such as lease agreements, agreements for use of land and water, construction works - There is no report to suggest that safety of dam would be jeopardized if water level is raised for present to 142 ft - Matters are disposed of by permitting water level of Mullaperiyar dam being raised to 142 ft and by permitting further strengthening of dam - Petition disposed of.
