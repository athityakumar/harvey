Banshi Dhar v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 875; (2007) 1 SCC 324; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 86; 2007 (1) RLW 770; 2006(11) SCALE 199; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 78

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Service - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Appellant dismissed from service - Reached his age of superannuation - Acquitted in the criminal proceeding - Questioning the correctness of the judgment of the Learned Single Judge as also the Division Bench of High Court denying him back wages - Departmental proceedings, however, could not be held as on the date of passing of the judgment of acquittal, he had already reached his age of superannuation - Held, we are of the opinion that it is not a fit case, having regard to the fact that the appellant has been paid the retiral benefits, where we should interfere with the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
