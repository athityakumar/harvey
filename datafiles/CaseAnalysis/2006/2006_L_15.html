State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Lalit Kumar Verma

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1432; (2007) 1 SCC 575; AIR 2007 SC 528; 2006 (7) AWC 7409; 2006(1) SCALE 642; 2006(12) SCALE 642; 2007 (2) SLR 74; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Revised Pay Rules, 1998

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-F - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960 - Regularisation - Respondent was appointed on daily wages - Recruitment was not made in terms of the statutory rules and no offer of appointment was issued - Respondent filed an application before the Labour Court purported to be for his classification in permanent category of workman - Labour Court was of view that having worked continuously for a period of more than six months, respondent acquired a right for classification in the category of permanent clerk - He was directed to be classified on the permanent basis as clerk - Writ petition filed thereagainst by the appellants was dismissed - Present appeal - Held, as per Clause 2 of the Standard Standing Orders a permanent employee is one who has completed six months satisfactory service in a clear vacancy in one or more posts whether as a probationer or otherwise, or a person whose name has been entered in the muster roll and who is given a ticket of permanent employee - A workman, would be entitled to classification of permanent or temporary employee, if the conditions precedent therefor are satisfied - In the present case, respondent was not appointed against a clear vacancy - He was not appointed in a permanent post or placed on probation - He had also not been given a ticket of permanent employee - Working on daily wages alone would not entitle him to the status of a permanent employee - Labour Court, Industrial Tribunal as also the HC, therefore, was not correct in directing regularisation of service of the respondent - Impugned judgments set aside - Appeal allowed.
