Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Mohammed Narsullah Khan

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 21; (2006) SCC (L&S) 316; (2006) 2 SCC 373; AIR 2006 SC 1214; 2006 (1) CLR 778; 2006 (1) ESC 42; JT 2006 (2) SC 82; 2006(2) SCALE 12; 2006 (2) SCJ 81; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 911; 2006 (2) SLR 319; 2006 (1) Supreme 569; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 769

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 309 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Order of dismissal - HC directed that respondent be reinstated into service forthwith with all back wages and all attendant benefits, which he could have received, had he not been dismissed from service - Appeal against - Held, HC re-appreciated evidence while acting as an Appellate Authority - Apart from re-appreciating evidence, which is not permissible in law, HC also fell in grave error by directing Govt. Pleader and counsel for respondent herein to again view cassettes - Its jurisdiction is circumscribed and confined to correct errors of law or procedural error, if any, resulting in manifest miscarriage of justice or violation of principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
