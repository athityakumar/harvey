Surinder Prasad Tiwari v Uttar Pradesh Rajya Krishi Utpadan Mandi Parishad and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 475; (2006) 7 SCC 684; 2006 (10) ADJ 222; 2006 (6) AWC 5583; JT 2006 (8) SC 504; 2006(9) SCALE 101; 2006 (5) SLR 631; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 828; 2006 (8) Supreme 29

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Fixed Term Appointment

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16, 226, 309, 335 - Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act, 1959 - Regularization in public employment - Appellant was appointed by Rajya Krishi Utpadan Mandi Parishad, U P, for a period of three months on contractual basis - Plea thet appellant has been working continuously for 14 years and was entitled to be regularized in service - Held, courts cannot countenance appointments to public office which have been made against constitutional scheme - It would be improper for courts to give directions for regularization of services of person who is working either as daily-wager, ad hoc employee, probationer, temporary or contractual employee, not appointed following procedure laid down under art.14, 16 and 309 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
