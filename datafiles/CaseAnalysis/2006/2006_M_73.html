MRF Limited, Kottayam v Assistant Commissioner (Assessment) Sales Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 510; (2006) 8 SCC 702; 2006(9) SCALE 420; 2008 (12) S.T.R. 206; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Withdrawal, Employees Provident Fund Act, Overriding Public Interest, State Sales Tax Act, Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1968

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss. 10, 25, 45A, 78A - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1968, s. 10 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 301, 304 - Tax exemption and eligibility certificate granted to appellant - Assistant Commissioner (Assessment) issued notice proposing to levy purchase tax on footing that exemption under SRO No 1729/93 dated 3/11/1993 was not available with effect from 15/1/1998 by reason of amendment by SRO No 38/98 - Held, it is stated in cl (2) of SRO 1092/99 that industrial unit which had commenced production before 1 st day of January, 2000 shall continue to enjoy concession for full period covered by order of exemption/deferment - MRF made a huge investment in State of Kerala under a promise held to it that it would be granted exemption from payment of sales tax for a period of seven years - It is not open to or permissible for State Govt. to deprive MRF of benefit of tax exemption in respect of its substantial investment in expansion in respect of compound rubber when State Govt. had enjoyed benefit from investment made by MRF - Impugned action is highly unfair, unreasonable, arbitrary and, therefore, same is violative of art.14 - Mandamus is issued restraining respondents from taking any proceedings against MRF Ltd contrary to or inconsistent with eligibility certificate and exemption order - Appeal allowed.
