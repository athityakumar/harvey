K.H. Siraj v High Court of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 236; (2006) 6 SCC 395; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1345; AIR 2006 SC 2339; JT 2006 (11) SC 424; 2006(6) SCALE 167; 2006 (4) SCJ 504; 2007 (3) SLR 1; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Election procedure and process

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 226, 234, 235, 30, 335, 341(1), 342(1) - Kerala Public Services Act, 1968, s. 2(1) - Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, rr. 14, 15, 16, 17 - Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, rr. 10(3), 7(i) - Kerala High Court Rules, r. 148 - Post of Munsiff-Magistrate in Kerala Judicial Services in pay scale of Rs. 2500 -4000 - Prescription of minimum eligibility - Single Judge declared that decision to fill up candidates appearing in S. Nos 60, 62, 64, 66, 68 and 70 in select list from open merit candidates as illegal and restrained HC of Kerala and State of Kerala from filling up above posts from select list - DB held that selections and appointments made were regular in all respects - Appeal against - Held, it is open for HC to prescribe bench marks for written test and oral test in order to achieve purpose of getting best available talent - There is nothing in Rules barring such a procedure from being adopted - Even assuming that r.7 did not prescribe any particular minimum, it was open to HC to supplement Rule with a view to implement them by prescribing relevant standards in advertisement for selection - Appeals dismissed.
