Sheela Devi and Others v Lal Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1033; (2006) 8 SCC 581; 2006 (65) ALR 463; 2006 (4) AWC 3859; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 140; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 797; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 912; 2006(10) SCALE 75; 2006 (Supp) RD 419; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 874; [2006] 157 TAXMAN 527

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Female Heirs, Death Of a Coparcener, Tenancy Rights, Property Of Individual, Devolution, Sole Surviving Coparcener, Agricultural Holding, Obstructed Heritage, Paternal Ancestor, Paternal Uncle, Son Of a Predeceased Son, Son's Son's Son, Step-Mother, Hindu Succession Act, 2005

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 6 and 8 - Hindu Succession Act, 2005 - Hindu Law - Mitakshara school - Coparcenary property - Succession opened in 1989 - B died in year 1989 leaving behind two sons - L born in 1938 and S born in 1956 - Whether provisions of s. 8 of Act would apply to facts of present case or law as applicable prior to enforcement of 1956 Act would apply? - Held, succession having opened in 1989, evidently, provisions of Amendment Act, 2005 would have no application - Sub-s. (1) of s. 6 of Act governs law relating to succession on death of a coparcener in event heirs are only male descendants - But, proviso appended to sub-s. (1) of s. 6 of Act creates an exception - First son of B, L, was, thus, a coparcener - S. 6 is exception to general rules - It was, therefore, obligatory on part of Plaintiffs-Respondents to show that apart from L, S will also derive benefit thereof - So far as Second son S is concerned, no evidence has been brought on records to show that he was born prior to coming into force of Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Thus, it was half share in property of B, which would devolve upon all his heirs and LRs. as at least one of his sons was born prior to coming into force of Act - Except to aforementioned extent, courts below are correct in applying provisions of s. 6 of Act and holding that s. 8 thereof will have no application - Appeal partly allowed.
