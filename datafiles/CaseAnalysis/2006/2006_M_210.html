State of Goa v Messrs Western Builders

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 291; (2006) 6 SCC 239; AIR 2006 SC 2525; 2006 (64) ALR 787; 2006 (4) AWC 3346; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 119; 2008 (5) CompLJ 83; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 257; JT 2006 (6) SC 125; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 97; 2007 (1) MPLJ 7; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 475; 2006(6) SCALE 574; 2006 (6) SCJ 205; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 288

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Limitation - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 14, 29(2), 5 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 37(1b), 43, (the Act) - Application for setting aside of award - Applicability of s.14 of Limitation Act - The Act does not expressly exclude the application of s. 14 - Any prohibitory provision has to be construed strictly - The underlying purpose of the Act is to expedite the resolution of commercial issues for the sake of economic progress - S. 43 of the Act applies the Limitation Act as a whole - It is only by virtue of s. 29(2) of the Limitation Act, that its operation is excluded to that extent of the area which is covered under the Act - If two Acts can be read harmoniously without doing violation to the words used therein, then there is no prohibition in doing so - Appeals allowed.
