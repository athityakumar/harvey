Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi v D.C.M. Textiles

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 155; (2006) 9 SCC 349; 2006 (195) E.L.T. 129

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4(iv)(D)(ii) - Whether Tribunal erred in holding that commission paid by respondent to its dealers was deductible as a trade discount for purpose of computing assessable value of cotton yarn? - Discount allowed in trade, if established under agreements or under terms of sale or by established practice, allowance and nature of discount being known at or prior to removal of goods, then same shall amount to a trade discount provided sale is from 'principal to principal basis' - Held, agreement entered into between respondent and dealers was on 'principal to principal basis' and it was an absolute sale made by respondent in favour of dealers - Tribunal was correct in its holding - Appeal dismissed.
