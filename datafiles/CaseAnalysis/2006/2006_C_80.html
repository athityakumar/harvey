Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Baroda

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 587; (2007) 13 SCC 803; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Exemption - Notification No. 75/84 - Appellant engaged in refining of crude petroleum which leads to manufacture of various petroleum products - Whether appellant is entitled to benefit of exemption notification No. 75/84 in terms of entry 34 r/w clarificatory circular? - Low Sulphur Heavy Stocks (LSHS) is used by appellant as fuel in a thermal power plant located within refinery area for generating electricity which in turn is captively consumed for production of various petroleum products - Entry No. 34 of notification No. 75/84 r/w clarificatory circular clearly spells out that appellant would be entitled to benefit of exemption on LSHS to extent it is used in thermal power plant located within refinery area for generating electricity which in turn is used in process of manufacture of petroleum products - Held, appellant would be entitled to benefit of exemption notification No. 75/84 in terms of entry 34 r/w clarificatory circular - Further observed, exemption shall not be available in respect of LSHS used in generation of electricity which in turn is not used in process and manufacture of petroleum products within refinery area - Order accordingly.
