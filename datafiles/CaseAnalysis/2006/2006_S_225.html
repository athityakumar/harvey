Harihar Nath and Others v State Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 426; (2006) 4 SCC 457; 2006 (63) ALR 790; 2006 (2) BC 538; 2006 (3) CTC 520; JT 2006 (4) SC 241; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 224; 2006 (143) PLR 638; 2006(4) SCALE 43; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, s. 446(1) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 3, 5 and 15(2), art. 137 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 16 and 22 - Applicability of art. 137 of Limitation Act, 1963 to a petition u/s. 446(1) of Companies Act, 1956 seeking leave of Company Court to proceed with a pending suit - Held, object of s. 446 of Act is not to cancel, nullify or abate any claim against company - U/s. 446(1), winding up court only decides about forum where suit to be tried and disposed of - Application u/s. 446(1) filed before Company Court seeking leave to proceed with a pending suit or proceeding, is an 'interlocutory application' with reference to pending suit/proceeding - Art. 137 is intended to apply to applications for enforcement of a claim or adjudication of a right or liability in a Court - An application for leave to proceed with a pending suit or proceeding not being such an application for any relief, will not attract art. 137 - When winding up is stayed, a suit against company (filed before winding up order) which stood stayed u/s. 446(1) could be proceeded with, even though leave had not been obtained to proceed with suit - Order of winding up does not create any 'right' to file an application u/s. 446(1) of Act - Nor does any right 'accrue' to a plaintiff/petitioner in a suit/proceeding to file an application u/s. 446(1), when an order of winding up is passed - Right to apply for leave accrues, not because of order of winding up, but because suit/proceeding is stayed - Right to apply for grant of leave u/s. 446(1) accrues every moment suit remains stayed - As long as suit/proceeding (filed before order of winding up) remains stayed, an application for leave can be filed - Therefore, application filed on 11-8-1995 was in time and not barred by limitation, even if art. 137 is applied - Deletion of condition imposed by Company Court while granting leave - Appeal dismissed.
