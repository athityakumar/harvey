M/S. Peacock Plywood Pvt. Ltd. v The Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 979; (2006) 12 SCC 673; 2007 (1) ACC 47; 2007 (1) CCC 323; JT 2007 (1) SC 191; 2007 (145) PLR 668; 2006(14) SCALE 300; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Constructive Total Loss, Navy Act, 1957, Actual Total Loss, Constructive Total, Marine Insurance Policy, Notice Of Abandonment, Peril

Summary: Marine Insurance Act, 1963 - Interpretation of a policy of marine insurance entered into by and between the parties herein covering goods in transit - Whether the suit was barred by limitation?; Whether the policy of insurance was an all risk policy?; Whether the policy covered constructive total loss?;Whether the exclusion clauses in the policy are applicable in the facts of this case so as to repudiate the claim of Appellant? - Held, only because the expression "without prejudice" was mentioned, the same, by itself was not sufficient and would not curtail the right of the insured to which it was otherwise entitled to - An insurance policy is to be construed in its entirety - A marine insurance policy does not come to an end only because the ship became stranded at a port - Division Bench of High Court committed an error in holding that the insurance policy stood terminated after June/ July, 1988 in terms of clause 9 of the policy when the contract of carriage had terminated on account of the unseaworthiness of the ship - Appeal allowed.
