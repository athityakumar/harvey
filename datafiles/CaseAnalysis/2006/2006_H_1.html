Divisional Controller, North-East Karnataka Road Transport Corporation v H. Amaresh

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 831; (2006) 6 SCC 187; AIR 2006 SC 2730; 2006 (6) AWC 6214; JT 2006 (6) SC 428; 2006 (5) KarLJ 513; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 610; 2007 (1) RLW 67; 2006(7) SCALE 163; 2006 (5) SLR 721; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 585; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2240

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(4) - Whether the Division Bench, rightly affirmed the findings of the Labour Court and of the Learned Single Judge with regard to reinstatement of the appellant who was alleged of misconduct and disrupting the services of a public transport system? - High Court and the Labour Court failed to consider all the urgent evidence and documents produced by the corporation/appellant and miserably erred by not considering that the respondent was in a drunken condition when there was no denial on the part of the workmen to that effect - When an employee is found guilty of pilferage or of misappropriating a corporation's funds, there is nothing wrong in awarding a punishment of dismissal - Even short remittance amounts to misconduct and, therefore, applying the rulings of this court, the impugned order ought not to have been passed by the Division Bench ordering reinstatement - Possession of excess sum of money on the part of the respondent, a fact proved, is itself a misconduct - The impugned order is ex facie illegal and contrary to the principles laid down by the various decisions of this court - Held, respondent has no legal right to continue in service any further - Appellant/corporation is directed to immediately discharge the respondent from service - Held, orders passed by the Labour Court is set aside - Appeal allowed.
