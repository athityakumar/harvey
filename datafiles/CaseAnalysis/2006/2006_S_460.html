K.K. Bhalla v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 11; (2006) 3 SCC 581; AIR 2006 SC 898; JT 2006 (1) SC 300; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 134; 2006(1) SCALE 238; 2006 (1) SCJ 729; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Press

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, ss. 3, 49, 53, 58, 72, 85, 87(1)(c)(iii) - Madhya Pradesh Town Improvement Trusts Act, 1960, ss. 51, 67, 68, 71(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14, 166 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh, Vikasit Bhoomiyo, Griho, Bhavano tatha anya sanrachnaon ka Vyayan Niyam, 1975, rr. 19, 20, 3, 4, 5 - Questioning allotment of lands measuring 20000 sq feet and 8000 sq feet in favour of private parties - Plea that such allotment having been made for industrial purpose, was in contravention of Master Plan drawn - HC held that grants were made for public purposes which State was empowered to do - Appeal against - Held, allotment of land to newspaper industries or other societies was not a decision taken by appropriate Ministry - When State has framed rules and adopted a procedure for disposal of land, both State and Jabalpur Development Authority were bound thereby - They could not have taken any action contrary thereto or inconsistent therewith - State had no power to interfere with day to day functioning of Development Authority - Authority is directed to consider matter in accordance with law - Appeals allowed.
