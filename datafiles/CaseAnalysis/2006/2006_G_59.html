Tea Auction Limited v Grace Hill Tea Industry and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1243; (2006) 12 SCC 104; AIR 2007 SC 67; 2006 (6) ALD(SC) 76; 2006 (65) ALR 305; 2007 (1) ALT 6; 2006 (4) AWC 3915; 2007 (1) CTC 81; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1688; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 487; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 412; 2007 (1) RLW 490; 2006(9) SCALE 223; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 163; 2007 (1) UC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security, Adjourned Hearing

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13 - Imposition of condition - Scope of - An ex-parte decree passed for non-appearance of defendant No. 1 - On application of defendant, decree was recalled on condition of furnishing entire suit amount either in form of bank guarantee or cash within a period of three months - Appeal before High Court against order was allowed as imposing a condition which was too harsh - Whether the Order of HC is valid? - Held, while setting aside ex-parte decree court may also consider question as to whether defendant should be put on terms - But such conditions should not be unreasonable or harshly excessive - Upon unreasonable or harsh conditions being imposed, Appellate Court would have power to interfere therewith - Expression 'or otherwise' is required to be construed widely - Appeal allowed.
