Rishikesh Sharma v Saroj Sharma

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 994; (2007) 2 SCC 263; 2007 (66) ALR 312; 2007 (1) ALT 1; 2007 (1) AWC 760; 2006 (5) CTC 703; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 239; 2007 (2) MPLJ 319; 2007 (147) PLR 397; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 315; 2006(12) SCALE 282; 2006 (3) ShimLC 389; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 1; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 245

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 28 - Appellant filed petition for decree of dissolution of marriage on ground of mental cruelty and desertion by respondent - District Judge dismissed petition of appellant - On appeal, High Court also dismissed appeal - Whether HC right in dismissing appeal of appellant? - Marriage is irretrievably broken down with no possibility of parties living together again - HC was not justified in refusing to exercise its jurisdiction in favour of appellant - Held, it will not be possible for parties to live together so there is no purpose in compelling both parties to live together - Decree of dissolution of marriage in favour of husband - Appeal allowed.
