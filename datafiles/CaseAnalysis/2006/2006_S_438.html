K.C. Skaria v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 576; (2006) 2 SCC 285; AIR 2006 SC 811; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 10; 2006 (1) AWC 940; JT 2006 (1) SC 287; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 124; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 460; 2006(1) SCALE 204; 2006 (2) SCJ 347; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 274

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 149 - Court Fee Act, s. 4 - Whether the defendants committed breach of contractual obligation and if so, what is the amount due to the plaintiff as damages for breach of contractual obligations? - Held, the Department committed breach by causing delays on various counts, thereby preventing the appellant from completing the work, though the amount due towards the work done was Rs.5,33,560/- as the appellant had claimed only a sum of Rs. 2 lacs on that count and paid court fee thereon, the appellant was entitled to a decree for Rs. 2 lacs only towards value of work done - we set aside the order of HC and restore the decree granted by the Trial Court for Rs. 2 lakhs plus Rs. 1,000/- with interest thereon as per the decree of the Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
