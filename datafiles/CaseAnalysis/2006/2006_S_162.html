Purushottam and Another v Shivraj Fine Art Litho Works and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1336; (2007) 15 SCC 58; 2007 (66) ALR 672; 2007 (1) AWC 878; JT 2007 (4) SC 564; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 623; 2007 (146) PLR 305; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 108; 2006(12) SCALE 232; 2007 (7) SCJ 772; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Contract - Unregistered Partnership - Claim of Amount Due - Justifiability - Appellant/plaintiff filed suit claiming outstanding amount with interest against respondent/defendant no. 1 to 9 - Trial Court decreed amount - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and held that suit was not maintainable in view of provision of s. 69(2) of the 1931 Act, being appellant/plaintiff unregistered firm - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that once registration was granted, even though after filing of suit, suit should be held to be maintainable as from date on which registration was granted subject to law of limitation - Further, appellant in his personal capacity could sue respondent firm for amount in question, if firm of which he was a partner was for reason of non-registration unable to maintain a suit and s. 69(2) of the 1932 Act was not attracted to a case where contract in question was not with unregistered firm - Whether judgment of HC was justifiable - Held, in instant case contract was entered into with respondent firm by erstwhile proprietor of concern - Partnership firm came into existence later - Amount claimed in suit were due to proprietor who carried on his proprietary business in name and style of 'Dinesh Paper Mart' - When he entered into partnership with others, he contributed to partnership by way of his contribution to capital, all assets and liabilities of his erstwhile proprietary concern - Thus, though partnership firm, which was unregistered, became entitled to enforce contractual obligation of defendant firm which it owed to proprietor, contract was not one entered into by unregistered firm with a third party, nor was it one entered into by unregistered firm in course of its business dealings with defendants - Thus, bar of s. 69(2) of the 1932 Act could not apply to suit filed by appellants - Hence, judgment and decree of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
