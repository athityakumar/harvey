Mathew Oommen v Suseela Mathew

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 9; (2006) 1 SCC 519; AIR 2006 SC 786; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 22; 2006 (62) ALR 484; 2006 (1) AWC 790; 2006 (1) CTC 300; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 111; JT 2006 (1) SC 225; 2006 (2) KarLJ 80; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 458; 2006 (100) RD 366; 2006 (2) RLW 1062; 2006(1) SCALE 75; 2006 (1) SCJ 661

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Travancore Christian Succession Act, 1917 - Validity of a will executed by a lawyer - Appellant-son is beneficiary - Application for grant of Letters of Administration with respect to Will - Objection by daughter of testator on ground that it was a fabricated will - Trial court accepted will but HC rejected same - Appeal against - Held, nothing abnormal or unnatural in document - There is nothing unnatural in a senior advocate of advance age to ask his junior advocate to write down something which he would like to be written - Will valid - Appeal allowed.
