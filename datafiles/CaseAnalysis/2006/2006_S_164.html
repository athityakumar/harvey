Messrs Bay Berry Apartments Private Limited and v Shobha and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 700; (2006) 13 SCC 737; AIR 2007 SC 226; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 92; 2006 (65) ALR 789; 2007 (1) AWC 266; 2007 (1) CHN 68; JT 2006 (9) SC 404; 2007 (102) RD 91; 2006(10) SCALE 596; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 738

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Family & Personal - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 136, 49, 59 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 4, 4(1), 6, 8 - Hindu Law of Inheritance (Amendment) Act, 1929 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 119 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 7 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 41 - Suit for declaring that Plaintiffs are also lawful heirs entitled to bequests under Will executed by their grand-father as lineal heirs of First Defendant - Suit decreed - Appeal against - Whether plaintiffs were 'heirs' within meaning of term as expressed in Will? - Held, Plaintiffs who are daughters of original defendant No 1, in law was not entitled to inherit their father's share in properties but for provisions of Hindu Succession Act, which brought statutory change - By reason of s 8, they became heirs of their father in terms whereof sister's share is equal to that of brothers - No case has been made out for exercise of discretionary jurisdiction u/art.136 - Appeal dismissed.
