Board of Trustees, Visakhapatnam Port Trust and Others v T.S.N. Raju and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 471; (2006) 7 SCC 664; JT 2006 (8) SC 486; 2006(9) SCALE 55; 2006 (5) SLR 601; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service; Water; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Service - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Scheme of voluntary retirement - Benefit not granted to respondents because they have attained cut off age of 58 years before their cases could be considered - Single Judge directed Port Trust to consider and accept voluntary retirement scheme of respondents - Appeal against - Held, request of employees seeking voluntary retirement was not to take effect until and unless it was accepted in writing by Port Trust Authorities - Port Trust Authorities had absolute discretion whether to accept or reject request of employee seeking voluntary retirement under scheme - VRS was not a proposal or an offer but merely an invitation to treat and applications filed by employees constituted an offer - Appeals allowed.
