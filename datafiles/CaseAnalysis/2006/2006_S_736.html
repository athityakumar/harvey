Commissioner of Police and Others v Syed Hussian

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 574; (2006) 3 SCC 173; (2006) SCC (L&S) 503; AIR 2006 SC 1246; 2006 (5) AWC 4882; 2006 (2) CGLJ 75; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 207; JT 2006 (2) SC 332; 2006(2) SCALE 301; 2006 (2) SCJ 244; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 805; 2006 (2) SLR 630

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 14 - Misconduct - Dismissal - Respondent a police constable - Stood surety for one person who was accused in 32 cases involving snatching of goods from other persons - Alleged misconduct admitted - Departmental proceedings initiated - Inquiry - Verdict of guilt passed and respondent was directed to be removed from service - Original application - Dismissed - Writ petition filed before High Court - HC upheld findings given by inquiry officer but interfered with quantum of punishment on ground of clean service record - HC's interference with award of punishment, whether justified or whether doctrine of proportionality to be invoked in facts of present case or whether exercise of power of judicial review called for? - Doctrine of proportionality has to be applied in appropriate case as depth of judicial review will depend on facts and circumstances of each case - Position of trust held by respondent and his duty towards upholding rule of law and thereby to aid prosecution in getting guilty punished, noted by court and factual position of his being aiding or abetting accused in fleeing from justice pressed upon - Finding of fact of disciplinary authority as to accused being a hardened criminal and in fact had jumped bail once - All goes to show that disciplinary authority cannot be said to have committed an error in imposing punishment of removal - Held, HC's interference with award of punishment of removal, not justified - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
