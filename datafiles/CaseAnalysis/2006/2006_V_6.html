V. Venkata Subbarao v State Represented By Inspector Of Police, A.P.

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 952; (2006) 13 SCC 305; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 175; AIR 2007 SC 489; 2007 CRLJ 754; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 253; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 519; 2006(14) SCALE 125; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988 - Demand made by a Mandal Revenue Officer to cut and remove casurina trees - Held, prosecution witnesses even did not know in which village the M.R.O., Surveyor and Revenue Inspector had their respective residences. A short intervention made by the appellant was purported to be in relation to the quantum of amount - Why M.R.O., who had made a demand, on whose behalf the appellant had accepted the amount, had escaped prosecution has not been explained - Appeal allowed.
