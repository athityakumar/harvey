Mayar (H.K.) Limited and Others v Owners and Parties, Vessel M.V. Fortune Express and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 580; (2006) 3 SCC 100; AIR 2006 SC 1828; 2006 (3) BC 156; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 344; JT 2006 (10) SC 48; 2006(1) SCALE 30; 2006 (2) SCJ 492; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 860

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Colonial Courts Of Admiralty Act, 1890, Hague Rules, 1924

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11, O. 6, r. 210 - Indian Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1925 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Colonial Courts of Admiralty Act, 1890 - Hague Rules, 1924, arts. 1(c) and 3(6) - (A) Whether Calcutta court had jurisdiction to try suit? - Held, it is apparent that court has found that Calcutta Court has jurisdiction to try proceedings except when forum selection cl excludes jurisdiction of court - Court has also found that law of Singapore is not known - Case of defendant carrier/owner of ship, of exclusion of Calcutta Court, is solely based on exclusion clause which conferred jurisdiction on court where defendant has principal place of business which according to court has to be determined only after sufficient material is placed before court - (1) How is principal place of business to be decided? - Further held, it appears that principal place of business would be where governing power of corporation is exercised or place of a corporation's Chief Executive Offices, which is typically viewed as nerve center or place designated as principal place of business of corporation in its incorporation under various statutes - Therefore, to arrive at a finding as to which is principal place of business parties would be required to place relevant material before court - (B) Whether it can be held that arrest of ship was obtained by plaintiffs suppressing material facts which could warrant stay of suit by court? - Further held, defendants have not asserted dismissal of suit on ground of jurisdiction of court at outset when letter of intention was furnished by Punjab National Bank on their behalf nor at time of furnishing bank guarantee and waited till 07-07-2001 to file an application - From reading of Admiralty Rules, it appears that it is a usual and common practice to issue warrant of arrest if affidavit filed u/r. 4 contains all particulars required - Appeal allowed.
