S. Sudershan Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1266; (2006) 10 SCC 163; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 503; AIR 2006 SC 2716; 2006 CRLJ 4033; 2006 (3) JCC 1357; JT 2006 (7) SC 237; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 764; 2006(7) SCALE 244; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Duty Of Court

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Conviction based on uncorroborated testimony of PW 3 who was closely related to deceased, whether correct - Held, relationship is not a factor to affect credibility of a witness - It is more often than not that a relation would not conceal actual culprit and make allegations against an innocent person - Foundation has to be laid if plea of false implication is made - In such cases, court has to adopt a careful approach and analyse evidence to find out whether it is cogent and credible - It is duty of court to separate grain from chaff - Where chaff can be separated from grain, it would be open to court to convict an accused notwithstanding fact that evidence of some of witnesses has been found to be deficient - (B) Maxim 'falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' - Further held, it has not received general acceptance nor has this maxim come to occupy status of rule of law - Doctrine merely involves question of weight of evidence which a court may apply in a given set of circumstances, but it is not what may be called 'a mandatory rule of evidence' - (C) Whether non-mention about source of light in FIR is fatal to prosecution case? - It is well settled that FIR is not an encyclopedia of facts concerning crime merely because of minutest details of occurrence were not mentioned in FIR same cannot make prosecution case doubtful - It is not necessary that minutest details should be stated in FIR - It is sufficient if a broad picture is presented and FIR contains broad features - Further held, non mention in FIR about source of light is really non consequential - Therefore, mere absence of indication about source of light does not in any way affect prosecution version - Appeal dismissed.
