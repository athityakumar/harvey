R.S. Garg v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 801; (2006) 7 SCC 405; (2006) 6 SCC 430; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1388; AIR 2006 SC 2912; 2006 (6) AWC 6186; JT 2006 (7) SC 1; 2007 (2) MLJ 315; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 315; 2006 (7) SCJ 193; 2006 (5) SLR 348; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Affirmative Action

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 14 and 16-U. P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994-Section 3-U. P. Inspector of Boilers and Factories Service Rules, 1980-Rules 5 (iii), 16 and 28-U. P. Labour Department (Factories and Boilers Division) Officers Service (Second Amendment) Rules, 1992 (1992 Rules)-Employment-Promotion-Validity - Appellant appointed as Inspector re-designated as Assistant Director of Factories on 3.1.1972-Third respondent so appointed on 17.1.1987-Appointment of appellant in terms of 1992 Rules indicating selection through Public Service Commission-Third respondent appointed purely on ad hoc basis till selection of regular candidate by Commission and joining post or till such time his services required-Appellant confirmed on 13.5.1978-Respondent appointed by State of U.P. as Assistant Director of Fisheries on regular basis by order dated 15.11.1995 on probation for two years-One temporary post of Dy. Director of Factories (Chemical) converted into post of Dy. Director of Factories (Admn.)-Third respondent belonging to Scheduled Caste promoted on said converted post-Seniority list published on 28.4.1989 showing appellant at Sl. No. 6-Then third respondent out of reckoning as his appointment was on ad hoc basis-His services regularised only in 1995 without referring matter to Commission-But State cannot make rules or issue executive instructions by way of regularisation of service-It would violate rules made under Article 309-And be opposed to constitutional scheme of equality in Articles 14 and 16-Rule 5 (iii) after 1992 amendment provides for 5 years services as substantively appointed Assistant Director of Fisheries for promotion to post of Deputy Director (Admn.)-At relevant time rule provided for selection through Commission-Same having not been done, appointment of third respondent void ab initio-No question of regularisation of his services-Appointment de hors rules to render it illegal and not irregular-Being of Scheduled Caste is not undue hardship for relaxation under Rule 28-Hardship caused to other employees belonging to same category and being senior to him-Action of promoting third respondent in undue haste-Smacks of mala fide-Relaxation permissible only in consultation with Commission-But Commission ignored-Entire approach of authorities was only for achieving private interest and not public interest-Their action suffered from vice of malice in law-No relaxation for promotion could be granted under Reservation Act-Five years' experience from date of substantive appointment being essential qualification-No relaxation could be given to third respondent to promote him in 1997-Chief Minister and Principal Secretary did not have authority to make relaxation-Case pointing out how illegalities are committed by State causing deprivation of legitimate right of promotion of more meritorious and senior candidates-Impugned judgment set aside-Appellant entitled to cost of Rs. 50,000 to be borne equally by State and third respondent.
