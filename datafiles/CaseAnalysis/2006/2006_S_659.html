Aloke Nath Dutta and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1278; (2007) 12 SCC 230; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 264; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 468; 2006(13) SCALE 467; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 662

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Martial, Police Record, Mitigating Circumstance, Jail Manual, Extra Judicial Confession, Police Torture, Horse Racing, Exact Words, Civil Liberty, Greed, Notions

Summary: Criminal - Death sentence - Confession - Retracted confession - Evidentiary value - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 302 and 201 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 164, 311 and 313 - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Appellants alleged to have entered into a conspiracy to commit murder of deceased at first floor of premises - (A) Confession Generally - Confession ordinarily is admissible in evidence - It is a relevant fact - It can be acted upon - Confession may under certain circumstances and subject to law laid down by superior judiciary from time to time form basis for conviction - For said purpose court has to satisfy itself in regard to, (i) voluntariness of confession (ii) truthfulness of confession (iii) corroboration - Confession which would otherwise be within special knowledge of accused may itself be not sufficient to raise a presumption that confession is a truthful one - Main features of a confession are required to be verified - If it is not done, no conviction can be based only on sole basis thereof - (B) Retracted Judicial Confession - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 2, 25, 26 and 30 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 162(1) and 164 - Constitution, 1950, art. 20(3) - Evidentiary value of retracted confession - Held, retracted confession of a co-accused cannot be relied upon for purpose of finding corroboration for retracted confession of an accused - Value of a retracted confession is now well-known - Court must be satisfied that confession at first instance is true and voluntary - Caution and prudence in accepting a retracted confession is an ordinary rule - Whatever be terminology used, one rule is almost certain that no judgment of conviction shall be passed on an uncorroborated retracted confession - Court shall consider materials on record objectively in regard to reasons for retraction - It must arrive at a finding that confession was truthful and voluntary- Merit of confession being voluntariness and truthfulness, same, in no circumstances, should be compromised - (C) Whether sentence of death imposed by Sessions Court justified? - Analysis of the evidences is required to be done keeping in view the factual matrix involved herein - Evidence must be scrutinised more closely - When a statute provides for death penalty, so long as same is not ultra vires, application thereof cannot be altogether eliminated - Death penalty can be awarded only if in opinion of court, case answers description of rarest of rare cases - What would constitute a rarest of rare cases must be determined in fact situation obtaining in each case? - Because case involved offences under same provision, same by itself may not be a ground to lay down any uniform criteria for awarding death penalty or a lesser penalty as several factors therefor are required to be taken into consideration - Held, sentencing indisputably is a part of criminal jurisprudence - More importantly, in death penalty references sentencing issues requires serious deliberation - Cases of this nature warrant objective evaluation of indicia and legal thresholds - In instant case, HC not attempted to do that exercise and come to an abrupt conclusion about case being not covered by rarest of rare category - Appropriate to remit matter to HC to consider matter afresh and take decision as to appropriate sentence - Order accordingly.
