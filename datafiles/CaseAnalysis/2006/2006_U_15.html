U.P.S.R.T.C v Mitthu Singh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1019; (2006) 7 SCC 180; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1590; AIR 2006 SC 3018; 2006 (6) AWC 6405; JT 2006 (8) SC 151; 2007 (1) RLW 280; 2006(8) SCALE 109; 2006 (8) SCJ 86; 2006 (5) SLR 613; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 672; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2914

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Continuity In Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of back wages - Entitled to - Payment of back wages is a discretionary power which has to be exercised by a Court/Tribunal keeping in view facts in their entirety and neither straight jacket formula can be evolved nor a rule of universal application can be laid down in such cases - Entitlement of a workman to get reinstatement does not necessarily result in payment of back wages which would be independent of reinstatement - While dealing with prayer of back wages, factual scenario and principles of justice, equality and good conscience have to be kept in view by appropriate Court/Tribunal - Held, record clearly reflects that services of respondent-workman were never found to be satisfactory - In fact, before more than 30 years, his services were terminated but he was taken back by giving a chance to improve - Unfortunately, however, respondent-workman did not utilise it - Even prior to three incidents in question, at several times, respondent-workman was warned - It was, therefore, not a fit case to grant back wages - Labour Court and High Court were not right in granting said prayer - Appeal partly allowed.
