Amrit Lal v Shiv Narain Gupta

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1641; (2010) 15 SCC 510

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 15(7) - Eviction - Compromise deed - Jurisdiction - Justifiability of order - Respondent-landlord initiated proceedings for eviction of appellant-tenant as there was some dispute as to rate of rent at which rent was payable in respect of premises - There was compromise deed accompanied by leas deed - In pendency of application filed by landlord, tenant moved application for amendment in written statement which was rejected by Trial Court and by same order, allowed application filed by landlord seeking direction for striking out defence u/s. 15(7) of the Act - Two appeals preferred by tenant was allowed by Rent Controlling Authority - As against petition filed by landlord was allowed by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC had jurisdiction or exceeded in exercise of jurisdiction in entertaining petition u/art. 227 of the Constitution and setting aside order of Appellate Authority and restoring that of Trial Court - Held, prayer for amendment in written statement was concerned, it was clear that compromise was promptly disputed by landlord submitting that same was void being outcome of fraud and coercion played upon him - Trial Court felt convinced by plea of landlord and therefore refused to take compromise on record - Therefore, HC had rightly formed it's opinion that amendment sought was based on event that too by passing order in which objection of landlord upheld by Appellate Authority and in face of prayer of landlord seeking striking out of defence of tenant was belated and malafide and should not have been allowed by Appellate Authority - Hence, it could not be said that HC did not have jurisdiction or exceeded in exercise of jurisdiction in entertaining petition u/art. 227 of the Constitution and setting aside order of Appellate Authority and restoring that of Trial Court - Appeals dismissed.
