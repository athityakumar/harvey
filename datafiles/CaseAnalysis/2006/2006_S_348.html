Raj Kumar Alias Raju Yadav Alias Raj Kumar Yadav v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1596; (2006) 9 SCC 589; 2007 (5) RCR(Criminal) 41A

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s.376 – Rape – Conviction - Reduction in sentence - Appellant was, charged for commission of offence rape - Trial Court convicted appellant and sentenced him to undergo 7 years' imprisonment – Appellant filed appeal before HC who affirmed conviction and sentence awarded to appellant – Hence, instant Petition – Whether appellant is entitled to get reduced sentence.

Held, appellant has already undergone approximately three-and-a-half years' incarceration. Further, there was delay of three days in lodging FIR and fact that doctor-PW6, who examined victim, in her testimony has deposed that she did not find any confirmatory evidence of rape on victim. Therefore, it is appropriate to reduce sentence awarded to appellant to period already undergone. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio – Court shall reduce sentence of accused person in absence of corroborative evidence.
