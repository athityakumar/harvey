R.D. Nagpal v Vijay Dutt and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1317; (2006) 12 SCC 498; (2011) 12 SCC 498; 2007 (1) CCC 197; 2006 (3) CPJ(Sc) 7; 2006 (144) PLR 72

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 33 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Consumer Protection Rules, 1987, rr. 2(b), 14(1) and 14(3) - Whether National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission correct in refusing authorized representative of appellant to cross-examine complainant? - Held, r. 14(3) of Consumer Protection Rules, 1987 allows parties or their agent to appear before National Commission - Given wide definition of word 'agent', there was no reason, if commission was otherwise satisfied that Dr K was authorised on behalf of appellant, to refuse to allow Dr K to represent appellant before it and to cross-examine complainant - Further, s. 33 makes it clear that advocates alone will be entitled to practice before any court or before any authority, etc. 'except as otherwise provided in Act or in any other law for time being in force' - Consumer Protection Act r/w Rules would be a law for time being in force - Therefore, appeal allowed and Dr K or any other duly authorized agent of appellant permitted to represent appellant before commission in pending proceedings - Such authorization should be in writing - Appeal allowed.
