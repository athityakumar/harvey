Commissioner of Central Excise, Raipur v Hira Cement

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 161; (2006) 2 SCC 439; 131 ECR 148; 2006 (194) E.L.T. 257; JT 2006 (2) SC 369; 2006 (72) RLT 831; 2006(2) SCALE 134; 2006 (2) SCJ 569; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1077; 2006 (145) STC 264; 2006 (8) S.T.R. 96

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4 and 351(b) - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether respondent was entitled to claim benefit of SSI exemption? - If manufacturer uses some brand of its own, it would be entitled to said exemption, but it would not be, for one reason or other, it had been using brand of another - Held, having regard to definition of brand name as contained in Explanation IX to notification D/- 28-2-1993, matter requires fresh consideration by Tribunal upon taking into consideration fact of matter - Impugned judgment is set aside and matter is remitted to Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
