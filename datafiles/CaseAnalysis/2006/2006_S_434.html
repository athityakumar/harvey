Siddarama and Others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 500; (2006) 10 SCC 673; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 72; AIR 2006 SC 3265; 2007 (2) ALT 72; 2006 CRLJ 4622; 2006 (3) JCC 1688; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 304; 2006(9) SCALE 346; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 149, 307, 326, 341 - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 - Appellants were sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 8 years and to pay a fine of Rs. 500/- - Appeal against - Plea that sentence is highly disproportionate to nature of offence committed - Held, offence committed is certainly gruesome but State has not questioned alteration of conviction from s. 307 r/w s. 149 to s. 326 r/w s. 149 - Reduced custodial sentence to five years but enhance fine in respect of each appellant to Rs. 20, 000/- Appeal dismissed.
