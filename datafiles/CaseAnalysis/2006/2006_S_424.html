U.P. Raghavendra Acharya and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 240; (2006) 9 SCC 630; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1948; AIR 2006 SC 2145; JT 2006 (6) SC 416; 2006(6) SCALE 23; 2006 (7) SCJ 270; 2006 (5) SLR 706; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 582

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Ex Gratia Payment, Computation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Karnataka Civil Services Rules, r. 296 - Matter relating to fixation of pension on basis of UGC pay scales - Cut-off date for applicability of revised rates of pension to staff of Govt. Aided Colleges challenged - Whether appellants having been given benefit of revised pay scales wef 01-01-1996, could have been deprived of retiral benefits calculated with effect therefrom? - Held, appellants had been enjoying benefit of revised scales of pay - Recommendations have been made by Central Govt. as also UGC to State of Karnataka to extend benefits of Pay Revision Committee in their favour - Pay in their case had been revised in 1986 whereas pay of employees of State of Karnataka was revised in 1993 - Benefits of recommendations of Pay Revision Committee w e f 1/1/1996, thus could not have been denied to appellants - Appeals allowed.
