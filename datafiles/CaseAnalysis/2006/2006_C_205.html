Mercantile Bank Limited, Bombay v Commissioner of Income Tax Bombay City-III

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 193; (2006) 5 SCC 221; AIR 2006 SC 3667; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 568; 2006 (202) CTR 457; [2006] 283 ITR 84; JT 2006 (5) SC 344; 2006(5) SCALE 244; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 628; [2006] 153 TAXMAN 97

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 119(1), 36(1)(vii), 36(2), 40A (6), 40A(5), 40A(5)(c)(i) - Whether appellant is liable to be taxed in respect of interest on doubtful advances credited to interest suspense account? - When an employee ceases to be in employment during previous year, is employer entitled to deduction at rate of Rs. 5, 000/- pm as long as employee was in employment and again up to limit of Rs. 60, 000/- when employee retires? - Held, only one limit is prescribed for deduction on account of salary whether paid to an employee in service or a retired employee in any one previous year - Appeal partly allowed.
