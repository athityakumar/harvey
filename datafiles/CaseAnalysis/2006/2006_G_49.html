Associated Cement Companies Limited v Government of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 96; (2006) 1 SCC 597; AIR 2006 SC 928; JT 2006 (1) SC 107; 2006(1) SCALE 147; 2006 (1) SCJ 691; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 149; 2006 (144) STC 342

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Packing Material, Tax Payable, Sale Price, Rate Of Tax, Rate, Tax Levied, Packing Materials, Distinction, Government of Ireland Act, 1920

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Finance Act, 1994 - Govt. of Ireland Act, 1920 - Whether same commodity i.e., cement could not be subjected to different rates of taxation depending on whether cement and packing material are sold separately? - Held, legislature distinguished between two categories of sale of cement recorded by dealer as in these two categories there is considerable variation in turnover base - In category of transactions falling in cl. (a) Entry 18 taxable turnover includes value of cement and value of packing material - Category of transactions falling under cl. (b) taxable turnover includes value of cement only - It does not include value of packing material - Discrimination does not arise for any dealer because dealer can avail any one of option available in cls. (a) and (b) - If dealer sells cement along with packing material and sale price includes value of packing material he continues to pay tax at previous rate, i.e., 16% - If dealer opts to sell packing material and cement separately he has to pay tax at higher rate i.e., 20% on cement only - He can exercise one of two options and pay tax accordingly - Appeal dismissed.
