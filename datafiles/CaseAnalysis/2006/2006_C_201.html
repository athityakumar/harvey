Commissioner of Customs, New Delhi v Messrs Prodelin India Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 453; (2006) 10 SCC 280; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 13; JT 2006 (8) SC 615; 2006(8) SCALE 748; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 14 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 2(2)(i), 2(2)(iv) - Respondent-Company i e, M/s Prodelin India (P) Ltd was set up under an agreement between M/s Prodelin Corporation U S A and one Mr Ashok Mago of New Delhi for marketing facility for promotion and selling VSAT Antennas, accessories and other communication equipments, assembly of equipments, testing, servicing - Loading of 10% in invoice value of goods imported holding that their foreign collaborator are related persons covered by rr.2(2)(i), 2(2)(iv) - Appeal by respondent allowed - Held, there is nothing on record to show that any technical fee was being charged in respect of imported goods or pre-import function - Department has not brought any evidence on record to show that relationship between respondent and M/s PC USA has influenced price or value of imported goods - No evidence brought by appellant that their relationship did influence price of imported components - Appeal dismissed.
