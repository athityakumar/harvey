South Eastern Coalfields Limited v Commissioner, Customs and Central Excise, Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 363; (2006) 6 SCC 340; 2006 (200) E.L.T. 357; JT 2006 (7) SC 121; 2006(7) SCALE 464; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: [A.] Excise - Mines Act, 1952, ss. 2(J)(viii), 82 - Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957, s. 3(3) - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 5A(1) - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2(m) - Whether workshop in question can be said to be situated within precincts of mine? - Tribunal rejected assessee's claim for exemption on ground that since mine was registered under Factories Act, it cannot have benefit of exemption in Notification No 63/95-CE since as per s.2(m) of Factories Act a factory means any premises including precincts thereof but does not include a mine - Held, a mine under Factories Act has nothing to do with assessee's claim for exemption under Central Excise Act - Present case is covered by Central Excise Act and has nothing to do with Factories Act - Workshop which is in an area in environs of a mine and is existing solely for purpose connected with mine and under same management, is obviously directly serving mining operations - Appeal allowed.
