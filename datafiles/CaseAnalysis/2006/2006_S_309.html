State of Andhra Pradesh v S. Narasimha Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 383; (2006) 5 SCC 683; AIR 2006 SC 2924; 2006 CRLJ 4090; 2006 (2) JCC 1098; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 46; JT 2006 (6) SC 358; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 558; 2006(7) SCALE 97; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 120B, 381, 411 - CrPC, 1973, s. 394 - CrPC, 1898, s. 431 - Conviction set aside - Appeal pending - A-3 died - Applicant filed present appeal stating that on death of said respondent appeal abated so far he is concerned - Held, every appeal against conviction abates on death of accused except an appeal from a sentence of fine - Appeal filed by State of Andhra Pradesh so far it questions correctness of judgment in Criminal Revision stands abated on death of respondent (A-3) - Application disposed of.
