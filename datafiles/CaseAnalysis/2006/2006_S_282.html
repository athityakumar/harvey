Gagan Kanojia and Another v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 852; (2006) 13 SCC 516; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 109; 2007 (66) ALR 172; 2007 (1) JCC 321; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 222; 2006(12) SCALE 479; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fingerprints

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 364 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 366 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 6, 8 - Kidnapping and murdering two children - Sentenced to death - HC while upholding judgment and conviction opined that case cannot be said to be a rarest of rare one meriting award of death penalty - Appeal against - Held, keeping in view circumstantial evidences, which have been brought on records, satisfied that all links in chain are complete and evidences led by prosecution point out only to one conclusion, that is, guilt of appellants - Appeals dismissed.
