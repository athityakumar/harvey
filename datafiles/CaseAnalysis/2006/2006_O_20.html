Suresh Pathrella v Oriental Bank of Commerce

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 726; (2006) 10 SCC 572; AIR 2007 SC 199; 2006 (6) AWC 5423; 2006 (134) DLT 516; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 72; 2006(10) SCALE 362; 2007 (4) SLR 295; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 564

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420 - Oriental Bank of Commerce Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1982, reg. 3(1) - Imputation of misconduct against appellant - Removal from service - Held, it is a case of loss of confidence in officer by bank - In such a situation, it would be a futile exercise of judicial review to embark upon decision of disciplinary authority removing officer from service, preceded by an enquiry, and to direct bank to take back officer in whom bank has lost confidence, unless decision to remove officer is tainted with malafide, or in violation of principles of natural justice and prejudice to officer is made out - No such case is made out - Appeal dismissed.
