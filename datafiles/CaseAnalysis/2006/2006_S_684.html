Faquir Chand and Another v Sudesh Kumari

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1389; (2006) 12 SCC 146; 2006 (65) ALR 457; 2006 (4) AWC 3843; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 25; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3338; 2007 (145) PLR 16; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 64; 2006(9) SCALE 554; 2006 (Supp) RD 474; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Civil Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 16, 16(c) - Sale deed - Absence of readiness and willingness - Specific performance - Alternative prayer for damages - Succeed - Respondent/plaintiff filed suit for specific performance or in alternative for damages - All three courts below, on consideration of entire materials placed, decreed suit for specific performance - Appellant/defendant contended that in absence of pleading to readiness and willingness to execute sale deed, suit for specific performance could not be decreed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled for specific performance - Held, compliance with readiness and willingness had to be in spirit and substance and not in letter and form, continuous readiness willingness could very well be seen in instant case from conduct of plaintiff as whole -Thereafter, appellant notice to respondent herein, on dt. 7-1-1988 and it was also not in dispute that respondent herein had deposited entire sale consideration into Court on dt. 18-5-1999, he was also present in Registrar's office for registration of document - However, appellant was not present at Registrar's Office , careful perusal of pleadings, would only show that respondent was ever ready and willing to perform his part of obligation under agreement and decree passed by HC confirming decree of lower Courts did not suffer from any infirmities - Therefore, appellant was to be suitably compensated and direct respondent to pay appellant sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- and if amount was paid within time as stipulated, appellant should execute sale deed in favour of respondent without any further delay - Appeal disposed of.
