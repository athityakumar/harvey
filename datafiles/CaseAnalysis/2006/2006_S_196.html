Groupe Chimique Tunisien Sa v Southern Petrochemicals Industries Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1417; (2006) 5 SCC 275; AIR 2006 SC 2422; JT 2006 (11) SC 198; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 440; 2006(6) SCALE 295; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 954

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 7(5) - Recovery of due amounts - Nature of purchase - Petitioner was company incorporated under laws of Tunisia and respondent placed purchase orders on petitioner for supply of various quantities of Phosphoric Acid - All purchase orders stated that all other terms and conditions were as per FAI terms (Fertilizer Association of India Terms and Conditions for Sale and Purchase of Phosphoric Acid) - Cl. 15 of FAI terms provided for settlement of disputes by arbitration - Petitioner effected supplies in pursuance of purchase orders and raised invoice for such supplies - Respondent failed to pay invoice amounts, and went on seeking extension of time for making payment on ground of financial difficulties - Petitioner filed suit in Amman Court of First Instance, Jordan for recovery of amounts due - Amman Court of First Instance, dismissed petitioner's case on ground of lack of jurisdiction and Amman Court of Appeal was also dismissed same - Petitioner issued statutory notice demanding payment of amount due with interest - On respondent's failure to pay, petitioner filed petition for winding up in Company, which was pending - Petitioner also issued another notice informing respondent that nonpayment of amounts due by respondent could be settled by arbitration and also informed respondent that if respondent failed to comply, appropriate proceedings could be initiated - Hence, instant Petition - Whether arbitration agreement had to be decided with reference to contract documents - Held, purchase orders placed by respondent on petitioner were contracts between parties and they were subject to FAI terms which contain arbitration clause specifically provided that where there was reference in contract to document containing arbitration clause, such reference constituted arbitration agreement, if contract was in writing and reference was such as to make that arbitration clause part of contract - Thus case squarely fell u/s. 7(5) of the Act and there was arbitration agreement between parties as per cl. 15 of FAI terms - Further purchase orders were contracts and invoice was document which was prepared with reference to supplies made under contract - When contract incorporated arbitration agreement by reference, invoice need not contain provision for arbitration - Petitioner had proceeded on basis that arbitration agreement existed between parties - However limitation was issue that could be considered and decided by Arbitration Tribunal - Petition allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.10 - Appointment of arbitrator - Whether respondent had lost its right to appoint its nominee to Arbitral Tribunal in view of its failure to comply with demand of petitioner to appoint arbitrator within 30 days from date of receipt of notice - Held, it was apparent that respondent did not appoint Arbitrator as it was under bona fide impression that there could not be arbitration - S. 10 of the Act provided that number of Arbitrators could not be 'even' - Arbitration clause provided that dispute could be referred to two Arbitrators and in event of Arbitrators not agreeing then umpire to be appointed by Arbitrators in writing before proceeding to reference - Having regard to s. 10 of the Act, Arbitral Tribunal could consist of three Arbitrators - Thus, respondent was allowed to appoint arbitrator - Petition allowed.
