Union of India and Others v Kali Dass Batish and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 6; (2006) 1 SCC 779; AIR 2006 SC 789; 2006 (5) AWC 4573; 2006 (1) ESC 48; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 105; JT 2006 (1) SC 170; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 184; 2006 (72) RLT 616; 2006(1) SCALE 190; 2006 (2) SCJ 1; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 261; 2006 (2) SLR 48; 2006 (1) Supreme 187; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 1050

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Administrative - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, ss. 14, 4, 6(3), 6(3A), 6(7), 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 323A - What is scope of 'judicial review' in an order for appointment of a member of Central Administrative Tribunal made in consultation with Chief Justice of India? - Held, if Parliament has reposed faith in Chief Justice of India as paterfamilias of judicial hierarchy in this Country, it is not open for anyone to contend that Chief Justice of India might have given his concurrence without application of mind or without calling for necessary inputs - Mere inclusion of a candidate's name in selection list gave him no right, and if there was no right, there could be no occasion to maintain a Writ for enforcement of a non-existing right - Where candidates were duly qualified and eligible for posts against which they were appointed, and all of them had been appointed after consultation with Chief Justice of India, there was no violation of any law or procdure in their appointments - Appeals disposed of.
