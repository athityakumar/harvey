Jasbir Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1239; JT 2006 (9) SC 35; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3038; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 136; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 217; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 469/467, 468/218, 120B - Bail granted by Sessions Judge - Administrative Judge held that while passing the order of bail on 6.5.2003, Sessions Judge had not discussed the matter on merit and set aside the order - Appeal against - What is the width and amplitude of the power of superintendence over subordinate courts and whether it authorizes the Inspecting Judges to transact any judicial work, which is in the domain of the subordinate courts? - Held, even if any application for bail is received by the Inspecting Judge, the proper course is to send the application to the concerned court to pass appropriate orders - If the learned Inspecting Judge passes any order in such matter, he would only be usurping the powers of the courts authorized to pass such orders - Appeal disposed of.
