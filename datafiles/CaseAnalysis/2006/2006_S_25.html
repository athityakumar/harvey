State of Jammu and Kashmir v S.Mohan Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 82; (2006) 9 SCC 272; AIR 2006 SC 1410; 2006 CRLJ 1691; 2006 (1) JCC 474; JT 2006 (3) SC 452; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 414; 2006(3) SCALE 156; 2006 (4) SCJ 461

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Respondents were convicted and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life and to pay a fine of Rs. 1, 000/- By trial court - HC acquitted both respondents - Appeal against acquittal - Held, HC was not justified in disbelieving them on sole ground that they were interested persons - In a murder trial, merely because a witness is interested or inimical, his evidence cannot be discarded unless same is otherwise found to be not trustworthy - Order of acquittal rendered by HC suffers from vice of perversity and is liable to be set aside - Appeal disposed of.
