Messrs Crawford Bayley and Company and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 292; (2006) 6 SCC 25; AIR 2006 SC 2544; 2006 (64) ALR 603; 2006 (4) AWC 3312; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 785; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 195; JT 2006 (6) SC 217; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 452; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 133; 2006(6) SCALE 541; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 240

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 166, 226, 254(2), 77 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971, (the 1971 Act), s. 3 - Challenge to appointment of Estate Officer - Amended proviso providing that an officer of a Statutory Authority shall only be appointed as an Estate Officer in respect of the public premises alleged to be violative of doctrine 'no man can be a Judge in his own cause' - 2nd proviso to s.3 (a) of the 1971 Act not violative of the doctrine - Said doctrine comes into play only when it is shown that officer concerned has personal bias or personal interest or that he has already taken a decision which he may be interested in supporting the same - For the applicability of the principle 'no man can be Judge in his own cause', proof of personal bias is essential - Provisions of Central Act viz. Public Premises Act, 1971 will prevail over State Act viz. Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 2000 in view of art.254 - Appeals dismissed.
