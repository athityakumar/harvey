K.K. Parmar and Others v H.C. of Gujarat Through Registrar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 652; (2006) 5 SCC 789; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1201; AIR 2006 SC 3559; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2445; JT 2006 (6) SC 509; 2006(5) SCALE 691; 2006 (6) SCJ 37; 2006 (5) SLR 127; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Due

Summary: Service - Due - Promotion - Entitled to - Instants appeals were filed against order passed by HC.

Held, 60 marks were fixed for written test and 20 marks for oral, no mark whatsoever was allotted towards past performance. An endeavour has been made by counsel for respondents to contend that as appellants were aware that no marks had been allotted in regard to past performance but despite same, they, having taken part in examination, were estopped and precluded from questioning same. SC do not agree. Same, however, may not by itself be sufficient to set aside entire selection process. Records have not been placed before SC. Appellants might not have obtained requisite marks for passing examination either in written test or at oral test or both. If any of appellants, failed to obtain qualifying marks fixed in terms of rules, 40 marks question as to whether their past performance was otherwise better than candidates who had been selected would take a back seat. However, only such candidates who had not only passed both written and oral tests, their past performances were required to be taken into consideration. There was no reason as to why same had been kept out of consideration by Selection Committee. If a selection is based on merit and suitability, seniority may have to be given due weight but it would only be one of the several factors affecting assessment of merit as comparative experience in service should be. Out of 29 Assistants, 4 have already retired and 17 have already been promoted. Only the cases of 8 Assistants are pending promotion. Cases of those 8 Assistants who had not been promoted, should be directed to be considered afresh. Appeals allowed.
