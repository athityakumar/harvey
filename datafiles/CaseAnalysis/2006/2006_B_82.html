Union of India and Others v Bikash Kuanar

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 677; (2006) 8 SCC 192; 2006 (4) ESC 492; JT 2006 (12) SC 578; 2006(10) SCALE 86; 2006 (3) ShimLC 293; 2006 (7) Supreme 643; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 192

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Respondent's appointment cancelled - DB held that administrative instructions have no statutory force, therefore, these cannot be enforced and respondent's appointment could not be legally cancelled - Appeal against - Held, respondent was recruited not only on basis of marks obtained by him in matriculation examination but also upon consideration of various other criteria necessary therefore - Only because one had obtained higher marks in matriculation examination, same by itself should not have been a ground for cancelling order of recruitment passed in favour of respondent - When a Selection Committee recommends selection of a person, same cannot be presumed to have been done in a mechanical manner in absence of any allegation of favouritism or bias - Appeal dismissed.
