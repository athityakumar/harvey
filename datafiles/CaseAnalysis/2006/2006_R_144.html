Harigovind Yadav v Rewa Sidhi Gramin Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 205; (2006) 6 SCC 145; AIR 2006 SC 3596; 2006 (7) AWC 6889; JT 2006 (5) SC 601; 2006(5) SCALE 524; 2006 (6) SCJ 381; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment & Promotion of Officers and other employees) Rules, 1988

Summary: [A.] Service - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, s. 17, s. 29 - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment & Promotion of Officers and other employees) Rules, 1988 - Promotion policy - Promotion on basis of seniority cum merit - Promotion denied on ground that as per promotion policy only those who got 10 marks and above in interview, were eligible for promotion; and that appellant who had secured only 9 marks in interview was thus not eligible for promotion - Held, procedure is not one of ascertaining minimum necessary merit and then promoting candidates with minimum merit in accordance with seniority, but assessing comparative merit by drawing up a merit list, assessment being with reference to marks secured for seniority, performance, postings at rural/difficult places and interview - Fact that appellant had failed to secure minimum marks in interview, is not relevant as entire procedure adopted by bank (of which interview is a part) is found to be vitiated and not in consonance with principle of seniority cum merit - Directed first respondent bank to promote appellant as a Field Supervisor - Appeal allowed.
