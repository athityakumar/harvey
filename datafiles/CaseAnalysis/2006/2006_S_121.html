Chief Administrator Puda and Another v Shabnam Virk

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 370; (2006) 4 SCC 74; AIR 2006 SC 1758; 2006 (63) ALR 351; 2006 (2) AWC 1711B; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 919; 2006 (9) CCC 185; 2006 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2006 (3) SC 563; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 881; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 275; 2006 (3) RLW 1970; 2006(3) SCALE 347; 2006 (3) SCJ 720; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 221

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Land & Property - Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 21(b) - Scheme for allotment of flat - Commission held that as delay in handing over possession was clearly established and reasons in price escalation of house was not proved or established, respondent was entitled to get house at Rs. 6.3 lacs instead of Rs. 7.44 lacs as demanded by appellants - Challenged - Respondent herself had accepted in undertaking that she accepted allotment of house and undertook to abide by all terms and conditions of allotment letter - It is not in dispute that in allotment letter figure as demanded has been reflected - That being so respondent was liable to pay amount as stipulated in allotment letter - Held, National Commission was clearly in error granting relief to respondent - Appeals allowed.
