San-A Tradubg Company Limited v I.C. Textiles Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1341; (2012) 7 SCC 192; 2007 (1) DRTC 1; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 154

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: U.P. Public Moneys (Recovery of dues) Act, 1972

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 17(3), 22 - Appointment of arbitrator - Legality - Instant applications were filed by applicant to seek appointment of an arbitrator in view of inability expressed by Mr. Manabu Nonoguchi to act as an arbitrator.

Held, SC viewed that s. 22 of 1985 Act does not debar arbitration proceedings under the 1996 Act. The objections to arbitration proceedings raised by respondent fail for aforesaid reasons and are rejected. Mr. Manabu Nonoguchi was appointed as an arbitrator by parties. Normally, parties are best judge for deciding as to who will be person capable and competent to adjudicate disputes raised considering his experience, knowledge and competence in particular trade or business to which disputes relate and taking these factors into account parties have appointed Mr. Manabu Nonoguchi as an Arbitrator, in case a dispute arises between parties. Unfortunately, for some reason, named arbitrator refused to act as an arbitrator. During pendency of these arbitration applications, I.A. supported by affidavit was been filed stating that named arbitrator is ready and willing to take up arbitration. Considering this fact, it would be appropriate if Mr. Manabu Nonoguchi, is appointed as an Arbitrator to adjudicate upon disputes arising between parties. SC accordingly, appoint him as Arbitrator. Petition disposed of.
