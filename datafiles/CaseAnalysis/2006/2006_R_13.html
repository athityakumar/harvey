Sabitha Ramamurthy and Another v R.B.S. Channabasavaradhya

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 502; (2006) 10 SCC 581; AIR 2006 SC 3086; 2007 (2) BC 210; 2006 CRLJ 4602; 2006 (3) JCC (NI) 247; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 138; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 295; 2007 (1) RLW 467; 2006(9) SCALE 212; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 200, 482 - Appellants filed an application u/s. 482 of CrPC praying for quashing of processes issued against them - Held, complainant is bound to make statements on oath as to how offence has been committed and how accused persons are responsible therefore - Complaint petitions does not meet statutory requirements, sworn statement of witness made by son of Respondent herein, does not contain any statement that Appellants were in charge of business of company - Appeals allowed.
