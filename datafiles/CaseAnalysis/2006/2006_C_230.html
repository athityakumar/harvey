 Confederation of Ex-Servicemen Associations And Others v Union of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, K.G. Balakrishnan, S.H. Kapadia, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1415; (2006) 8 SCC 399; AIR 2006 SC 2945; JT 2006 (8) SC 547; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 427; 2006(8) SCALE 399; 2006 (8) SLR 1; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: West Bengal Special Courts Act, 1950, Ex-servicemen Contributory Health Scheme, 2002

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 19, 21, 32 - Ex-Servicemen Contributory Health Scheme, 2002 - Ex-servicemen Associations - More medical facilities were provided to ex-army personnel - Succeed - Hence, instant petition was filed by petitioner-Confederation of ex-serviceman Associations for appropriate direction to respondent-Union of India to recognize right of full and free medicare of ex-servicemen, their families and dependants treating such right as one of fundamental rights guaranteed under Constitution of India - Further prayer was also made to extend such medicare for all diseases including serious and terminal diseases, even if treatment for those diseases was not available at Military Hospitals - Respondents contended that free medical service to all its employees in-service or out-of service was never held to be fundamental right guaranteed by Constitution and even if there were some observations to that effect, they were either 'obiter dicta' or 'passing observations' and did not lay down correct law and every State has limited financial means and resources, financial capacity and available means, it has to undertake its obligations of providing social services including medical facilities to its employees in- service or retired - Held, right to medical aid was fundamental right of all citizens including ex-servicemen guaranteed by art. 21 of Constitution, framing of scheme for ex-servicemen and asking them to pay 'one time contribution' neither violates Part III nor it was inconsistent with Part IV of Constitution - Govt. of India, Ministry of Defense, should consider, without it being treated as precedent, question of granting waiver of contribution required to be made under ECHS by ex- servicemen of category with which concerned, i.e., those who have retired prior to 1st January, 1996, having regard to contribution that might have been made by them in service of nation and particularly considering that they, while in service, were not making any payment so as to enjoy benefit of medical care - Alternatively, Govt. could also consider making payment on behalf of those who might be interested in availing benefits under ECHS - Therefore, safely be presumed that Govt. had no difficulty in waiving/paying contribution as 'one time measure' on behalf of ex- defense personnel who retired prior to January 1, 1996 and wish to avail benefits of ECHS - Petition disposed of.
