Hari Shankar Singhania and Others v Gaur Hari Singhania and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 229; (2006) 4 SCC 658; AIR 2006 SC 2488; 2007 (5) AWC 5017; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 10; 2006 (2) CTC 597; JT 2006 (4) SC 251; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 243; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 454; 2006(4) SCALE 74; 2006 (3) SCJ 511; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 726; 2006 (2) UC 1236

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Right to apply accrues - (A) Whether Bombay High Court was correct in observing while dismissing appeal of appellants was that, an oral application for condonation of delay will not be entertained in Court of law according to laws present in our judicial system? - Held, condonation of delay needs to be asked for only if there is a delay in fling a suit and in fact situation of this case, there is no delay in filing of arbitration suit as observed earlier and suit for arbitration filed by appellants is within time prescribed u/art. 137 of Limitation Act, 19630 - (B) why dispute needs to be settled? - Further held, already matter is pending adjudication from 1987 onwards, respondent Nos. 1-9 are admittedly in possession and enjoyment of valuable immovable properties depriving valuable rights of appellants other respondent Nos. 10-20 and 24 - It would not allow respondent Nos. 1-9 to drag proceedings any further - Parties have to settle their disputes one day or other - Time has now come to nominate a single Arbitrator as provided u/cl. 13 of agreement - (C) whether HC erred in coming to conclusion that because no distribution of property had been made till 29/2/1988, it was indicative of fact that there were disputes and differences between parties? - Further held, it has failed to appreciate that merely because parties did not take steps for distribution of immovable properties it did not automatically follow that disputes and differences had arisen between them in this regard - In fact, from correspondence on record, it is clear that parties were making efforts to complete distribution of immovable properties as per terms of agreement between them - Appeal allowed.
