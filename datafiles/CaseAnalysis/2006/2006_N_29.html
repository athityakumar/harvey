Commissioner of Police, New Delhi v Narender Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 625; (2006) 4 SCC 265; (2006) SCC (L&S) 686; AIR 2006 SC 1800; 2006 (128) DLT 801; 2006 (2) JCC 724; JT 2006 (4) SC 328; 2006 (3) RLW 2042; 2006(4) SCALE 161; 2006 (5) SCJ 11; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 872; 2006 (3) SLR 31; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1544; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1545

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26, 24 and 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Delhi Police (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1987, r. 12 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 308 and 34 - Theft of armoury - Dismissal - Without holding any enquiry in terms of proviso appended to art. 311(2) of Constitution - Validity of - Whether even in a disciplinary proceeding provisions contained in s. 26 of Evidence Act would be attracted? - Held, standard of proof required in recording a finding of conviction in a criminal case and in a departmental proceeding are distinct and different - Whereas in a criminal case, it is essential to prove a charge beyond all reasonable doubt, in a departmental proceeding preponderance of probability would serve purpose - If an employee has been acquitted of a criminal charge, same by itself would not be a ground not to initiate a departmental proceeding against him or to drop same in event an order of acquittal is passed - Policy underlying behind ss. 25 and 26 is to make it a substantive rule of law that confessions whenever and wherever made to police, or while in custody of police to any person whomsoever unless made in immediate presence of a magistrate, shall be presumed to have been obtained under circumstances mentioned in s. 24 and, therefore, inadmissible, except so far as is provided by s. 27 of Act - If provisions of Evidence Act are not attracted in a departmental proceeding, a fortiori ss. 25 and 26 shall not apply - Appeal allowed.
