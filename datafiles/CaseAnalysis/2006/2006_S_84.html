Sarat Chandra Mishra & Ors. v State Of Orissa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 2; (2006) 1 SCC 638; AIR 2006 SC 861; 2006 (2) ESC 81; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 336; JT 2006 (1) SC 190; 2006(1) SCALE 9; 2006 (1) SCJ 414; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 116; 2006 (1) Supreme 252; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 1057

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Orissa Ministerial Service (Method of Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Lower Division Assistant in Office of Department of Secretariat) Rules, 1951 - Validity of Gradation List of 1984 - Two sets of applications were filed before Tribunal at first instance; one questioning legality of Common Gradation List of year 01/01/1984; and another questioning legality of circular letter dated 21/02/1989 and consequent publication of Gradation List dated 22/08/1990 - Judgment not questioned - Held, judgment and order of Tribunal attained finality - No distinct cause of action is shown - Principle of res judicata apply in instant case - This Court cannot exercise its jurisdiction u/art.142 in violation of statutory provisions and that too at this distant time so as to unsettle a settled thing - Appeal dismissed.
