Minu Kumari and Another v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 142; (2006) 4 SCC 359; AIR 2006 SC 1937; 2006 CRLJ 2468; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2013; 2006 (2) JCC 769; JT 2006 (4) SC 569; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 271; 2006(4) SCALE 329; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 1086; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 156(3), 169, 170, 173, 178, 190, 190(1)(c), 200, 202, 362, 482 - IPC, 1860, ss. 323, 34, 341, 435 - Power of CJM to recall or review his order - Appellants questioned correctness of the order by filing a petition under s. 482 of the Code - HC dismissed the petition holding that Subordinate Court could not have recalled its own order u/s. 362 of Code on pretext that there was correction of clerical and arithmetical errors - Appeal against - Held, this is a case when cognizance was taken, summons were issued by mistake and names of appellants were also mentioned in order - Since police have not found any material against appellants, CJM without following procedure could not have directed issuance of summons so far as they are concerned - As noted by CJM later that was a mistake and, therefore, he had ordered to strike of names of appellants - HC was not justified in rejecting application in terms of s.482 - Appeal allowed.
