Sarup Singh Gupta v S. Jagdish Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 232; (2006) 4 SCC 205; AIR 2006 SC 1734; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 73; 2006 (63) ALR 468; 2006 (2) AWC 1925; 2006 (2) CTC 658; 2006 (128) DLT 534; 2006 (4) G.L.R. 3440; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 277; JT 2006 (4) SC 277; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 414; 2006 (143) PLR 287; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 560; 2006 (1) RCR(Rent) 421; 2006 (3) RLW 1868; 2006(4) SCALE 14; 2006 (3) SCJ 398; 2006 (2) UC 1215

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106 and 113 - Eviction proceedings - Two quit notices were given - Whether acceptance of rent pursuant to eviction proceedings by itself constitute an intention to treat lease as subsisting? - Held, mere acceptance of rent did not by itself constitute an act of nature as stated in s. 113 showing intention to treat lease as subsisting - It cannot be said that mere acceptance of rent amounts to waiver of notice to quit unless there be any other evidence to prove or establish that landlord so intended - Appeal dismissed.
