Ewanlangki-E-Rymbai v Jaintia Hills District Council And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1460; (2006) 4 SCC 748; AIR 2006 SC 1589; JT 2006 (4) SC 139; 2006(3) SCALE 562; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Assam Autonomous Districts (Constitution of District Councils) Rules, 1951, United Khasi Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Appointment and Succession of Chiefs and Headmen) Act, 1959, Chotanagar Tenancy Act, 1908, Jowai Autonomous District Act, 1967

Summary: Constitution - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 16, 25, 26 and 244(2) - United Khasi Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Appointment and Succession of Chiefs and Headmen) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Constitutional validity of s. 3 of the Act, 1959 - Writ petitions were filed challenging Notice dated September 4, 2001 that announced the programme for the conduct of election for Dolloi in the Elaka Jowai and notice that provided that only the members of the class mentioned therein could contest the aforesaid election and thereby the persons belonging to the Christian faith were excluded from contesting the said election - Writ petitions also challenged that constitutional validity of s. 3 of the Act, 1959 which provides that subject to the provisions of the Act and the Rules made thereunder all elections and appointments of Chiefs and Headmen shall be in accordance with the existing customs prevailing in the Elaka concerned - It was contended that exclusion of Christians from contesting the election was in violation of arts. 14, 15 and 16 of the Constitution since they were excluded only on the ground of religion - It was further submitted that two persons could be called upon to perform those duties, one performing the administrative duties and the other the religious functions - HC held that a custom prevailed in the Elaka Jowai which on account of its long practice and by common consent acquired the status of a governing rule for election and appointment of Dolloi to perform both administrative and religious functions and therefore there cannot be two Dollois one performing the administrative functions and the other performing the religious functions - HC further held that there was no breach of arts. 14, 15 and 16 of the Constitution and in fact it protected the rights guaranteed u/arts. 25 and 26 of the Constitution - Present appeals - Whether exclusion of Christians from contesting election for the post of Dolloi in Elaka Jowai is discriminatory and in breach of arts. 14 to 16 of the Constitution of India since their exclusion is merely on the ground of religion? - Held, Jowai District was an autonomous District to which the provisions of Sixth Schedule of the Constitution apply in view of the provisions of art. 244(2) of the Constitution - It was the tribal custom of the Elaka that the Dolloi of the Elaka Jowai must perform both the administrative and religious functions of his office - By long standing custom, the Dolloi must perform both administrative and religious functions, and such duties cannot be bifurcated by appointing one other to perform the religious functions only - Hence, by excluding Christians from contesting the post of Dolloi, arts. 14, 15 and 16 were not violated - Exclusion was justified by good reason, since admittedly the religious duties of a Dolloi of Elaka Jowai cannot be performed by a Christian - Thus the ground for exclusion of Christians is not solely the ground of religion, but on account of the admitted fact that a Christian cannot perform the religious functions attached to the office of Dolloi - Reason cannot be said to be either unreasonable or arbitrary - Hence, s. 3(1) of the Act, 1959 as also the Notifications impugned in the writ petitions cannot be struck down on the ground of violation of arts. 14, 15 and 16 of the Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
