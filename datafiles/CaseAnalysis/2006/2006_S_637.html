Mohd. Sartaj and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1436; (2006) 2 SCC 315; AIR 2006 SC 3492; 2006 (1) AWC 950; 2006 (1) ESC 56; JT 2006 (1) SC 331; 2006(1) SCALE 265; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 377; 2006 (2) SLR 99; 2006 (1) Supreme 262; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 719

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Basic Education Act, 1972, U.P. Industrial Disputes Rules, U.P. Basic (Teachers) Services Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Basic (Teachers) Services Rules, 1981 - Cancellation of appointment - Appellants appointed as teachers even though they lacked the requisite qualification at the time of initial appointment - However, within a very short span of their appointment, respondents cancelled their appointment on the ground that they did not possess the prescribed qualification - Appellants filed writ petition against said cancellation - HC stayed the order of cancellation of appointment - In the mean time appellants acquired BTC qualification - HC dismissed the writ petition filed by the appellants and held that appointment of appellants was improper and directed respondent to re-advertise for the posts - Appellants filed appeals against HC's order submitting that appointments were cancelled without issuing any prior notice or giving opportunity of being heard and hence the order of dismissal issued is violative of principles of natural justice - Appellants also submitted that they are having higher qualifications than the prescribed ones and hence even if they acquired the prescribed qualifications at later stage, the selection and appointment was perfectly lawful, just and proper - Whether appellants' submissions could be accepted and HC's order could be set aside? - Held, appellants were not qualified as they did not possess the prescribed qualification - In view of the basic lack of qualifications, they could not have been appointed nor their appointment could have been continued - Hence the appellants did not hold any right over the post and therefore no hearing was required before the cancellation of their services - Moreover, cancellation order has been issued within a very short span of time giving no probability for any legitimate expectation to the appellants regarding continuation of their service - Appeals dismissed.
