Sanjiv Kumar v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1537; (2009) 16 SCC 487

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B and 498-A - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Dowry death - Cruelty - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 304-B and 498-A of IPC - Conviction of the appellant was based on the presumption drawn u/s. 113-B of Evidence Act against him - On appeal, conviction was affirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the prosecution proved its case beyond reasonable doubt? - Held, u/s. 304-B of IPC the prosecution was required to establish that the death was caused otherwise than under normal circumstances, that such death took place within seven years of the marriage, and that it was shown that soon before her death the woman was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or any relative of her husband for or in connection with any demand for dowry - If these facts were established by the prosecution, the presumption u/s. 113-B of Evidence Act, 1872, arises and the court should presume that such person who had subjected the woman to cruelty or harassment in connection with any demand for dowry shall be presumed to have caused the dowry death - Presumption that arises in such cases may be rebutted by the accused - In the instant case, there was no direct evidence to prove that he had administered any poisonous substance to the deceased - Defence set up by the appellant was probable and sufficient to rebut the presumption against him - Appellant should be given benefit of doubt - Appellant was acquitted of the charges levelled against him - Appeal allowed.
