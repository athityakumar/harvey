Romesh Kumar Sharma v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 354; (2006) 6 SCC 510; 2006 (3) AWC 2955; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 80; JT 2006 (7) SC 209; 2006(7) SCALE 471; 2006 (5) SLR 407; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: [A.] Service - Army Act, 1950, ss. 20(1), 20(3)(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 14, 17 - Appellant working as Havildar/Clerk (GD) in Ladakh Scouts - Espionage activities - Dismissal from service - Challenged order of dismissal on ground that same was illegal, unconstitutional, improper, malafide and violative of r.17 and art.14 and 21 and that no opportunity of being heard had been afforded to him to explain his conduct - Held, u/r.17 Chief of Army Staff and other officers are competent to order dismissal or removal without complying with procedure set out in main part of Rule after certifying that it is not expedient or reasonably practicable to comply with provisions so set out - Chief of Army Staff had followed requisite procedure and certificate as contemplated in proviso to r.17 has been given - Appeals dismissed.
