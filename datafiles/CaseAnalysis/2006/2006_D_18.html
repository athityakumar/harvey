Uttar Pradesh Gram Panchayat Adhikari Sangh and Others v Daya Ram Saroj and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1282; (2007) 2 SCC 138; 2007 (1) ADJ 299; 2007 (2) AllCJ 817; 2007 (2) AWC 1289; JT 2007 (1) SC 597; 2007 (102) RD 393; 2006(13) SCALE 442; 2007 (3) SCJ 66; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 581; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1969

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1947, ss. 25 & 25-A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 37, 40, 243G & 309 - Parent Deputation - Justification - Employees of 8 departments were deputed to Gram Panchayat as multi-purpose workers - G.O. dt 20-7-2004 was issued for repatriation of employees of 3 departments to their Parent Departments - Writ Petition was filed challenging order which was dismissed by Single Judge - Appeal against order of Single Judge Bench was dismissed by DB holding that transferred employees remained Govt. Servants and retained their lien on posts in their original departments and they could always be repatriated - Several similar Writ Petitions were also filed challenging G.O. dt 20-7-2004, which were dismissed - Another G.O dt 19-7-2005 was issued to re-transfer all tube-well operators and repatriate tube well operators as well as part-time operators of irrigation department to their parent departments - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of DB was justifiable - Held, 73rd amendment was brought to give effect to directive principles of state policy, art.40 which could not be said to qualify as basic feature of constitution - It was enabling provision and state was empowered either to eliminate, modify or cancel by exercising power under enabling provisions, which was further subject to conditions as might be specified - Thus, it was for state legislature to consider legal conditions and make law accordingly - Hence, judgment and order of DB was not sustainable in law- G.O orders dt. 8-9-2005 and 25-1-2006 were restored - Appeals disposed of.
