Syndicate Bank and Others v Venkatesh Gururao Kurati

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 22; (2006) 3 SCC 150; AIR 2006 SC 3542; 2006 (5) AWC 4875; 2006 (1) CLR 783; JT 2006 (2) SC 73; 2006 (2) KarLJ 205; 2006(2) SCALE 101; 2006 (2) SCJ 156; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 920; 2006 (2) SLR 273; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 775

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Written Statement Of Defence

Summary: [A.] Service - Administrative - Syndicate Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline & Appeal) Regulations, 1976, regn. 6 - Syndicate Bank Officer Employees' (Conduct) Regulations, 1976, regn. 3(1) - Removal from service - Whether non-supply of documents, which did not form part of charge sheet and were not relied upon by prosecution prejudice delinquent officer resulting in vitiating enquiry proceedings? - Held, non-supply of documents on which Enquiry Officer does not rely during course of enquiry does not create any prejudice to delinquent - To sustain allegation of violation of principles of natural justice, one must establish that prejudice has been caused to him for non-observance of principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
