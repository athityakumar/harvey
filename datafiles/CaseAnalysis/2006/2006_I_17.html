Inderpreet Singh Kahlon and others v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 536; (2006) 11 SCC 356; AIR 2006 SC 2571; 2006 (5) AWC 5021; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 251; JT 2006 (5) SC 352; 2006 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2338; 2006(5) SCALE 273; 2007 (6) SLR 287; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 772

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab General Sales Tax (First Amendment) Rules, 1970, Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) (Class-I) Rules, 1976

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 311, 315, 320 - CrPC, 1973, s. 164 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2), s. 7 - Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) (Class-I) Rules, 1976, rr. 11, 17, 8, 8(1), 8(2), 8(3), 8(5) - Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1970 - Selection made by Punjab Public Service Commission during 1998 to 2001 cancelled - Mass cheating and appointments made in violation of eligibility criteria - Held, only because wards of its sitting Judges obtained employment, same by itself would not give rise to a presumption that everything was not well in selection making process - With a view to segregate between tainted with non-tainted and that in interest of justice HC should be requested to constitute two independent scrutiny committees one relating to executive officers and other relating to judicial officers - Matters are remitted to HC for consideration of matters afresh - Appeals partly allowed.
