Nisar Khan @ Guddu and Others v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 593; (2006) 9 SCC 386; 2006 (1) JCC 259; JT 2006 (2) SC 262; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 818; 2006(2) SCALE 25; 2006 (3) SCJ 401; 2006 (1) UC 577

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149 and 309 - High Court confirmed order of Trial Court convicting five appellants u/ss. 149 and 302/149 IPC - Present appeal - It is now well settled principle of law that recovery pursuant to disclosure statement made by accused u/s. 27 of Evidence Act is admissible in evidence - Held, it is evidence on record that accused were arrested and pursuant to a disclosure statement made by them, arms were recovered - In facts and circumstances of this case no infirmities in well reasoned concurrent finding recorded by two courts below - Appeals dismissed.
