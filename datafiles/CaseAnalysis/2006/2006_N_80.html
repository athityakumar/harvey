North Eastern Karnataka R.T. Corporation v Ashappa

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 243; (2006) 5 SCC 137; AIR 2006 SC 2164; 2006 (7) AWC 6781; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 193; 2006 (3) RLW 2495; 2006(6) SCALE 89; 2006 (5) SCJ 501; 2006 (8) SLR 645; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Service -Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Misconduct of unauthorised absence from duty - Dismissal from service - Absent from duty for 129 days - HC found that absenting oneself from duty can be treated to be a minor misconduct - Appeal against - Held, remaining absent for a long time cannot be said to be a minor misconduct - He was found not only to have remained absent for a period of more than three years, his leave records were seen and it was found that he remained unauthorisedly absent on several occasions - Appeal allowed.
