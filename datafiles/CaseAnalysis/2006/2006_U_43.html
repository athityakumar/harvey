M.C. Mehta v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 130; (2006) 11 SCC 582; JT 2006 (11) SC 621; 2006(4) SCALE 246; 2006 (5) SCJ 42

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Environmental Damage

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900, s. 4, s. 5 - Mining - Whether mining activity carried out in Villages Khori Jamalpur and Sirohi in District Faridabad in Haryana are in violation of orders passed by this Court on 06-05-2002? - Held, it does not appear that area in question falls under any category of prohibition for carrying out mining activity - But another aspect that remains to be examined is about impact of mining in villages in question on environment - Merely on basis of photographs or plying of large number of trucks per day, a direction can not be made for stopping mining activity - Monitoring Committee constituted in terms of directions in M C Mehta's case is directed to inspect mining activity being carried on in 75.05 hectares in village Khori Jamalpur and in 50.568 hectares in village Sirohi in Faridabad district and report impact - Report be filed in 3 months - Order accordingly.
