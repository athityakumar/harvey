Arun Kumar Nayak v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 503; (2006) 8 SCC 111; 2006 (6) AWC 6064; 2006 (4) ESC 484; JT 2006 (12) SC 372; 2006(9) SCALE 401; 2006 (6) SLR 16; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 404; 2006 (7) Supreme 323

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Controversy relates to appointment of Extra Departmental Sub Post Master - Selection process - Tribunal directed respondent department to conduct a selection process afresh and consider all applications on merit, received both from Employment Exchange and candidates who submitted applications pursuant to public Notification - HC confirmed appointment of 4 th respondent - Appeal against - Held, rationale behind such direction is consistent with sound public policy that wider opportunity of notice of vacancy by wider publication, so that adequate choices are made available and best candidates would be selected and appointed to subserve public interest better - Appeal allowed.
