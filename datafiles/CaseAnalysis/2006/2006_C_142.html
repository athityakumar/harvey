Boving Fouress Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chennai

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 785; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Notification No. 205/88CE - Notification Nos. 5/98CE and 5/99 - Whether assessee entitled to benefit under Notification Nos. 205/88 and 5/98-CE? - If no appeal is filed against an earlier order or earlier appeal involving identical issue was not pressed by revenue, revenue is not entitled to press other appeals involving same question - Held, point involved in present case is identical to point involved in Sulzer's case and department having accepted principle laid down therein,same has attained finality, department cannot be permitted to take a different stand in present appeals - Assessee is held to be entitled to benefit of Notifications in question - Impugned orders are set aside - Appeals allowed.
