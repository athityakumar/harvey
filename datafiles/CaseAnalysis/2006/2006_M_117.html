Punjab National Bank and Others v Manjeet Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1423; (2006) 8 SCC 647; AIR 2007 SC 262; 2006 (6) AWC 6085; JT 2006 (9) SC 247; 2007 (2) MPLJ 242; 2006(9) SCALE 710; 2007 (1) SLR 408; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 18(3)(d) - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Scheduled banks - Initiated scheme - Service of deposit collectors - Entitled commission - Succeed - Appellant was nationalized bank, some schedule banks initiated schemes were introduced for mobilizing small savings from public - Respondents were deposit collectors in Pygmy Deposit Scheme floated by bank, under agreement, they were entitled to commission at rate of 3.5% of collection made by them - Industrial dispute was raised by such deposit collectors of various banks pursuant where to Central Govt. by notification on 3-10-1980 which was 'Whether demand of Commission Agents or as case might be Deposit Collectors employed in Banks that they were entitled to pay scale and other service conditions admissible to regular clerical employees of these banks was justified, if not to what relief workers concerned entitled to and from which date, for adjudication' before Industrial Tribunal - By an award made on 22-12-1988, Tribunal opined that deposit collectors were workmen and as such reference was maintainable - Petition filed by appellant before HC, which was dismissed and held, notices were issued to Mini Deposit Collectors for recovery of amounts paid to them from dt. of judgment of HC i.e., 1-4-2001 - Hence, instant appeal - Respondent contended that petitioners being not parties to award, award was not binding upon them, opportunity been given to them, they could have shown that appellant could not take different stand vis-a-vis other nationalized banks - Whether order of HC was succeed - Held, Viveka Nand Sethi v. Chairman, J & K Bank Ltd and Ors., 2005 Indlaw SC 363, relied on - Court would not insist on compliance of principles of natural justice in view of binding nature of award, their application would be limited to situation where factual position or legal implication arising there under was disputed and not where it was not in dispute or could not be disputed - If only one conclusion was possible, writ would not issue only because there was violation of principles of natural justice - Hence, appellant Bank had no other option but to implement award, if it did not, its action could be held to be penal and reliance placed by respondents on letter of Bank dt. 26-5-2001 was also misplaced - Therefore, circular letter did not state that principles of natural justice were required to be complied with, as on date of issuance of circular letter they had not received necessary guidelines, temporary measure was proposed to be taken and judgment could not be sustained which was set aside - Appeal allowed.
