Sub Divisional Officer (P), Uhbvnl v Dharam Pal

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 763; (2006) 12 SCC 222; AIR 2007 SC 1214; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 106; 2007 (66) ALR 320; 2007 (5) AWC 5240; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 839; 2007 (1) CCC 306; 2007 (1) CompLJ 248; 2007 (1) CPJ(Sc) 16; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 292; 2007 (2) MLJ 260; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 260; 2006(12) SCALE 465; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1175

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2l - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 15, 26(6) - Indian Electricity Rules, 1956, r. 57 - Charge of tampering - Demand - District forum held that demand was illegal and instead reference ought to have been made in terms of s.26(6) - Appeal against - Held, s.26(6) can have no application in a case where a meter has become completely non-functional on account of any reason whatsoever - District Forum, State Commission and Commission were not justified in holding that a reference in terms of Section 26(6) of Act was called for - Appeal allowed.
