Noble Resources Limited v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 529; (2006) 10 SCC 236; AIR 2007 SC 119; 2006 (6) AWC 5444; 2006(9) SCALE 181; 2006 (Supp) RD 654; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 226, 299 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Whether a Writ Petition is maintainable in contractual matter? - Held, a specific performance of contract would not be enforced by issuing a writ of or in nature of mandamus, particularly when keeping in view provisions of Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Damage may be an adequate remedy for breach of contract - Although approach of HC was not entirely correct, its ultimate decision to refuse to exercise its discretionary jurisdiction cannot be faulted with - Appeal dismissed.
