Settu and Others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 407; (2006) 10 SCC 549; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 147; AIR 2006 SC 2986; 2006 CRLJ 3889; JT 2006 (7) SC 524; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 38; 2006(8) SCALE 382; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 299, 299(b), 300, 300(2), 300(3), 302, 304, 323, 324, 326, 34 - Appellant Nos 1 and 2 were convicted for offence punishable u/s.302 - Appellant No 3 was convicted for offence punishable u/s.302 r/w s.34 - Appellant no 2 further found guilty of offence punishable u/s. 324 - Held, it will be sufficient to say that cl (4) of Section 300 would be applicable where knowledge of offender as to probability of death of a person or persons in general as distinguished from a particular person or persons - Being caused from his imminently dangerous act approximates to a practical certainty - Conviction of A 1 confirmed - Taking note of weapon used by appellants and place where injuries were inflicted, A 2 has to be convicted u/s. 304 Part I - A 3 is to be convicted in terms of s.326 - Conviction of A 2 in terms of s. 324 is in order - Appeal disposed of.
