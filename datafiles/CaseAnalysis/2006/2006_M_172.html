Messrs H.P.L. Chemicals Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 164; (2006) 5 SCC 208; 2006 (197) E.L.T. 324; JT 2006 (5) SC 101; 2006(4) SCALE 479; 2006 (4) SCJ 643; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Soap, Classification Of Goods

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Companies Act, 1956 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9(2) - Classification of Denatured Salt - Commissioner (Appeals) classified 'Denatured Salt' under Chapter Heading 25 01 and Tribunal classified it under Chapter Heading No 38 24 - Appeal against - Held, goods in question admittedly contain 53 6% Sodium Chloride and their essential character is derived by Sodium Chloride, which is salt Since in present case salt is unfit for human consumption, same would be classifiable as 'Denatured Salt' under specific Heading No 25 01 and not under Heading 38 23 which is a residuary Heading - If Department intends to classify goods under a particular heading or sub-heading different from that claimed by assessee, Department has to adduce proper evidence and discharge burden of proof - It would fall under specific Heading 25 01 - Appeals allowed.
