Jagat Ram v Varinder Prakash

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 602; (2006) 4 SCC 482; AIR 2006 SC 1786; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 21; 2006 (63) ALR 474; 2006 (2) AWC 1923; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 293; JT 2006 (4) SC 342; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 141; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 429; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 121; 2006 (3) RLW 2208; 2006(3) SCALE 554; 2006 (3) SCJ 812; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 65 - Whether suit is barred by limitation u/art. 65? - Held, yes - Article itself provides that possession of defendant shall be deemed to become adverse only when female dies - Thus, there is no scope for argument that limitation does not run from date on which Hindu female died and that it would start running from some other date - Suit should have been filed by plaintiff within 12 years of death of Hindu female - Appeal dismissed.
