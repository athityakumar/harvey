Dagdu v President, Anandrao Naik Shikshan Prasarak Mandal and others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1586; (2006) 9 SCC 782

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Boards Act, 1965, Maharashtra Employees'of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Employees'of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977, ss. 2(20), 9 - Termination - Jurisdiction - Appellant filed application before Labour Court against his termination from services as Headmaster in 1997 which was transferred to School Tribunal - Tribunal directed respondent to reinstate appellant into services with back wages - Aggrieved, respondent filed petition before HC which was allowed on the ground of jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 9 of the Act gave jurisdiction to the Tribunal to entertain appeals from employees in a private school and the words 'private school' had been defined in s. 2(20) of the Act as meaning a recognised school established or administered by a management - Boards referred to the definition of the word 'reognised' mean Boards which dealt with education at levels other than the level at which the ashram school of which the appellant was the Headmaster operates - Since it was not recognised, therefore, it was not a 'private school' within the meaning of s. 2(20) of the Act - Consequently, Tribunal would not have the jurisdiction to entertain an appeal of appellant since he was not an employee of a private school - If such an appeal was filed, the officer concerned would dispose of the same after hearing the appellant and other affected parties within a period of 4 months from the filing of the appeal - Respondents had fairly conceded that since the appellant was pursuing his remedy in a wrong forum the issue of limitation will not be raised by them - Appeals disposed of.
