Jagdish Mandal v State Of Orissa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1285; (2007) 14 SCC 517; 2006(14) SCALE 224; 2006 (8) SCJ 359; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 606

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Tenders - Awarding Contract - Judicial Review - Justifiability - Respondent no,2 invited tenders for construction and tender of respondent no.5 was considered - Complaint was made against respondent no.5 alleging that he defrauded department - Contract was awarded to appellants - Respondent no.5 filed writ petition before HC seeking direction to respondent no. 1 to 4 not to award contract to appellant - HC quashed agreement between appellant no.1 and department and directed to reconsider case of respondent no.5 - Further it also set aside decision of committee regarding second contract, which was awarded to appellant no.2- Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that scope of judicial review of tender processes and award of contracts was limited only to cases involving material violation of terms relating to scrutiny and acceptance of tenders or decision was vitiated by arbitrariness/irrationality/mala fides or favortisim - Further, HC ought not had interfered with contracts awarded to appellants - Whether judgment of HC was justifiable - Held, Judicial review of administrative action was intended to prevent arbitrariness, irrationality, unreasonableness, bias and mala fides - Its purpose was to check whether choice or decision was made 'lawfully' and not to check whether choice or decision was 'sound' - Principles of equity and natural justice stay at a distance - If decision relating to award of contract was bona fide and was in public interest, Courts would not, in exercise of power of judicial review, interfere even if a procedural aberration or error in assessment or prejudice to a tenderer, was made out - Power of judicial review would not be permitted to be invoked to protect private interest at cost of public interest, or to decide contractual disputes - Tenderer or contractor with a grievance could always seek damages in a Civil Court - In instant case, there were good and adequate reasons for committee to reject lowest tenders of respondent no.5 in both cases - HC was not justified to interfere with contracts awarded to appellants - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
