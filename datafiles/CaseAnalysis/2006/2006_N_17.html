B. S. N. Joshi and Sons Limited v Nair Coal Services Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1426; (2006) 11 SCC 548; AIR 2007 SC 437; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 744; JT 2006 (10) SC 131; 2006(11) SCALE 526; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 11

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Inviting tender - Essentials terms of contract - Failed to fulfill conditions - Succeed - Notice inviting tender was issued by coal liaisoning, quality and quantity supervision for its Thermal Power Station - Appellant as also respondent nos. 1, 4 and 5 submitted their tenders, tender of appellant herein was accepted by MAHAGENCO - Appellant failed to fulfill essential qualifications as contained in notice inviting tender - Writ petition was filed by respondent no.1 before HC, which was allowed and quashing order awarding contract in favor of appellant and declared appellant was defaulter, appellant did not fulfill condition of handling quantity of 5 million metric tones in financial year 2004-04 - Hence, they also demonstrate that Authority floating tender must insist upon compliance of essential conditions of eligibility and was not entitled to deviate from insistence on strict compliance of such essential conditions of eligibility, however, in case of ancillary or subsidiary condition, it was open to Authority to deviate there from - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there had been consistent practice in past that contracts were awarded for one year or two years, as case may be, and in view of fact that contracts were not awarded on financial year or calendar year basis, deviation was permissible - Whether appellant was entitled to get contract and whether appellant was defaulter - Held, having regard to fact that huge public money was involved, public sector undertaking in view of principles of good corporate governance may accept such tenders which was economically beneficial to it and it might be true that essential terms of contract were required to be fulfilled - Hence, If party failed or neglected to comply with requisite conditions which were essential for consideration of its case by employer, it could not supply details at latter stage or quote lower rate upon ascertaining rate quoted by others - Employer has power of relaxation must be found out not only from terms of notice inviting tender but also general practice prevailing and court may consider practice prevailing in past - Keeping in view particular object, if in effect and substance it was found that offer made by one of bidders substantially satisfies requirements of conditions of notice inviting tender, employer may be said to have general power of relaxation in that behalf - Therefore, respondents who had formed cartel have successfully obtained contract after judgment of HC and award of such contract although was subject to decision of this appeal, SC could not ignore fact that if appellant was permitted to take over forthwith, supply of coal to Thermal Power Station mighty be affected and intend to give another opportunity to MAHAGENCO - Appeal allowed.
