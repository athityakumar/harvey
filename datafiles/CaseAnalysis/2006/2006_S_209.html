Standard Chartered Bank v Andhra Bank Financial Services Limited, and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Y.K. Sabharwal, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 826; (2006) 6 SCC 94; AIR 2006 SC 3626; JT 2006 (11) SC 456; 2006(5) SCALE 384; 2006 (7) SCJ 459; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Capital Market

Keywords: Interrogatories, Bonds, Government Companies

Summary: Banking & Finance - Capital Market - Special Court Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities Act,1992,s. 10 - Companies Act,1956,ss. 2(12), 108(1), 111, 620(l)(a) - Specific Relief Act, 1963,s. 34 - Specific Relief Act,1877,s. 42 - Transfer of Property Act,1882, s. 137 - Code of Civil Procedure,1908,s. 11 - Indian Evidence Act,1872,ss. 114, 165 - Security bonds - Disputes regarding ownership - Counter cases - Validity - NPCL issued two series of bonds 9% tax free bonds and 17% taxable bonds and Andhra Bank Financial Services Ltd (ABFSL) purchased bonds of Rs. 100 crores i.e Rs. 50 crores in 9% tax free bonds and Rs. 50 crores in 17% taxable bonds and NPCL issued letter of allotment (LOA) confirming purchase of suit bonds - Same day ABFSL/respondent sold them to appellant and issued cost memo and appellant issued pay order dt. 26-2-1992 for Rs. 42,52,50,000 on which ABFSL issued Banker's Receipt (BR) acknowledging receipt of amount - ABFSL/respondent sent letter asking appellant to return BR in lieu of original LOA in respect of suit bonds enclosed with letter - After securities scam broke out in April/May 1992 appellant investigated its records and found only xerox copy of LOA instead of original and immediately informed NPCL/respondent that though ABFSL/respondent mentioned original LOA was sent but infact only xerox copy was enclosed and requested to make note that original LOA was missed in its records and requested due caution and to issue duplicate allotment letter - ABFCL/respondent sent letter to NPCL/respondent confirming sale of suit bonds to appellant had no objection to issue duplicate LOA - 'X' was declared as 'notified person' and FIR was filed against him for scam of misappropriation of funds - CMF submitted transfer deed with original LOA to NPCL seeking transfer and registration of suit bonds in its name as purchased them for valid consideration - NPCL/respondent informed same to appellant and also informed CMF/respondent about request of appellant to issue duplicate LOA - NPCL/respondent informed appellant that due to dispute of ownership of suit bonds it could not take action - Appellant filed declaration suit in Bombay HC and was transferred to Special Court - CMF/respondent filed petition before Company Law Board and was transferred to Special Court - Special Court dismissed petition of CMF holding that it had no right title and interest in suit bonds - Aggrieved CMF filed appeal in SC - SCB/appellant had withdrawn claim against CMF/appellant and so suit of appellant was dismissed by Special Court for non joinder holding that CMF was not added as necessary party - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal in SC - SC had allowed both appeals and remanded both cases to Special Court for being tried de novo - Special Court dismissed appellant/SCB suit and allowed petition of CMF/respondent - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeals - As per facts and circumstances who would be entitled to suit bonds - Held, SC in Vasudev Ramchandra Shelat v. Pranlal Jayanand Thaker and Ors. 1974 Indlaw SC 205 held that title of any person acquiring property would depend upon the antecedent title of person from whom the property is acquired - Appellant validly acquired title to suit bonds from ABFSL/respondent who acquaird same from original issuer NPCL/respondent on 26-2-1992 as rightly observed by Special Court - Appellants title arised from antecedent right of ownership of its transferor/ABFSL/respondent about whose title there was no dispute at all - Suit bonds were nothing but debentures and so actionable claim and act between transferor and transferee was sufficient to convey all rights of ownership - Janakiraman Committee with experts was appointed by RBI to investigate securities scam and tarred both banks for fudging their accounts - Where there was defect in title of antecedent transferor then transferee got no title - PW.2 employee of ABFSL/respondent denied that any such transaction had taken place by which the suit bonds were sold by ABFSL to CMF through broker 'X' and there was no cross-examination on this aspect - CML/respondent at no point of time sought to obtain cost memo for this transaction - No evidence was led by CMF as to which employee of CMF had transacted this deal - CMF had filed affidavit of 'Y' as its witness having knowledge of this transaction but 'Y' was not examined - Pleadings of CMF on the issue of consideration appear to be most confusing and shifty - Only witness of CMF frankly admitted that she had no personal knowledge of the suit transaction whatsoever - No documents were produced by CMF to show transaction between itself and ABFSL with broker 'X' to show it purchased suit bonds as owner -- CMF/respondent could never be said to be purchaser for value as there was no evidence to show that any consideration was paid by CMF for acquisition of suit bonds - - Material evidence on record showed that appellant had purchased suit bonds from NPCL/respondent for valid consideration and original LOA with transfer deed was handed over to appellant and its missing from its records was subject matter of FIR and triable by criminal court - Impugned judgments of Special Court in Special Court were set aside - Appeals allowed.
