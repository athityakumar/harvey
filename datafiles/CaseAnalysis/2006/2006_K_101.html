National Insurance Company Limited v Kusum Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 100; (2006) 4 SCC 250; 2006 (2) ACC 19; AIR 2006 SC 3440; 2006 (63) ALR 817; 2006 (2) AWC 1918; 2006 (2) CPJ(Sc) 8; 2006 (2) CTC 347; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2224; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 20; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 288; JT 2006 (4) SC 9; 2006 (143) PLR 246; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 313; 2006 (3) RLW 1863; 2006(3) SCALE 519; 2006 (3) SCJ 657; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 387

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance

Keywords: Quantum Of Damages

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 10, 149(2), 163A, 166, 170(2), 3, 4, 5 - Vehicle driven by a Khalasi who had a driving licence for driving a Light Motor Vehicle met with an accident resulting death of a 12 years old girl - Liability of insurance company - Trial Court held company liable - Plea of violation of terms and conditions of contract of insurance - Held, appellant was not liable to pay claimed amount as driver was not possessing a valid licence and HC was in error in holding otherwise - But in peculiar facts and circumstances of case declined to interfere with impugned award - Directed that Appellant may recover amount from owner - Appeal dismissed.
