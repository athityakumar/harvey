N. Khosla v Rajlakshmi (Dead) and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 78; (2006) 3 SCC 605; AIR 2006 SC 1249; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 119; 2006 (63) ALR 534; 2006 (2) AWC 1911; JT 2006 (3) SC 200; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 448; 2006 (144) PLR 651; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 389; 2006 (1) RCR(Rent) 369; 2006(3) SCALE 95; 2006 (3) SCJ 500; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 906; 2006 (Supp) RD 382

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Indian Registration Act, 1877, Indian Registration Act, 1866

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 14 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17(1)(b) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 22, r. 4 - Gift deed - Whether with abatement of appeal in respect of deceased, whole appeal qua other respondents abated or not? - Whether award of Arbitrator purports or operates to create, declare, assign, limit or extinguish in praesenti or in future any right, title or interest of value of one hundred rupees and upwards to or in immovable property which requires registration u/s. 17 (1)(b)? - Held, if interests of co-defendants are separate, as in case of co- Owners, suit will abate only as regards particular interest of deceased party - s.17 (1)(b) speaks of creating rights or extinguishing rights in praesenti or in future - Any right created or extinguished in past is conspicuously absent - Award of Arbitrator clearly delineated pre-existing facts, on basis of which award was passed - Award of Arbitrator is made Rule of Court - Appeal allowed.
