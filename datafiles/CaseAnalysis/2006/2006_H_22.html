Messrs Maharashtra State Seeds Corporation Limited v Haridas and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 595; (2006) SCC (L&S) 587; (2006) 3 SCC 690; AIR 2006 SC 1480; 2006 (5) AWC 5137; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 834; JT 2006 (3) SC 23; 2006(2) SCALE 584; 2006 (3) SCJ 491; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 613; 2006 (2) SLR 689

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Employment-Dismissal-Validity -Whether Enquiry Officer has jurisdiction to recommend any punishment to be imposed on respondent by disciplinary authority? -Held, “no” - Whether when disciplinary authority acted on recommendation of Enquiry Officer, it could have corrected its mistake apparent on face of record?-Held, “yes”-Authority committed no illegality in issuing second show cause notice-Principles of natural justice complied with-Respondent not only defalcated huge amount-But also misappropriate some bags of seeds-Hence, improper for High Court to interfere with quantum of punishment-Grounds for judicial review by High Court limited-Impugned judgment set aside.
