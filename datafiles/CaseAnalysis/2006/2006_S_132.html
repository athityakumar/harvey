Chief Engineer, Ranjit Sagar Dam and Anr v Sham Lal

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 279; (2006) 9 SCC 124; AIR 2006 SC 2682; 2006 (7) AWC 7160; JT 2006 (6) SC 50; 2006(6) SCALE 388; 2006 (5) SCJ 457; 2006 (4) SLR 708; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 80; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 2015

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Whether employer has to prove that concerned workman had not worked for 240 days or more in year immediately preceding date of termination? - Held, provisions of Evidence Act in terms do not apply to proceedings u/s. 10 of Industrial Disputes Act - Burden of proof is on claimant to show that he had worked for 240 days in a given year - This burden is discharged only upon workman stepping in witness box and adducing cogent evidence, both oral and documentary - Appeal allowed.
