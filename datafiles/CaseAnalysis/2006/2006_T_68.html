Sathi Vijay Kumar v Tota Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1277; (2006) 13 SCC 353; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1806; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 732; 2006(14) SCALE 199; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non Joinder, Ballot Papers, Verification Of Pleadings, Contesting Candidate, House Of The People, Disqualifications For Membership, Allotment Of Symbols, Reserved Symbol, Non Joinder Of Parties, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Any Other Candidate, Contents Of Petition, Corrupt Practice, Electoral Rolls, Free Symbol, Presentation Of Nomination Paper And Requirement For a Valid Nomination, State Legislative Assemblies, Trial Of Election Petitions, Representation of People (Amendment) Act, 1996, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952

Summary: Election - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 16, O. 7, r. 11 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83 and 123(7) - Conduct of the Election Rules, 1961, r. 54A - Striking out certain paras from the election petition of the appellant - Rejection of the averment of returned candidate (respondent no. 1) regarding non-joinder of Bijender Singh - (A)Whether these actions of the High Court is valid? - Held, HC rightly held that non-joinder of Brijinder Singh as party respondent could not be held to be fatal for election petitions as Brijinder Singh was a 'substitute' candidate set up by same political party which had set up Respondent No. 1 - Striking of certain paras from election petition - S. 83 of Act not being exhaustive O. 6, r. 16 could be invoked to strike out those pleadings which did not disclose any cause of action - (B) Deletion of 586 voters from voters' list - Allegation of - Whether such plea is incomplete or vague? - Held, HC was wrong in observing that allegation regarding deletion of 586 voters from voters' list was vague and that allegation of antedating election rolls, was incomplete and vague - While alleging that names of certain electors were wrongly deleted all particulars relating to excluded electors were placed in schedule with election petition - (C) Corrupt practice - Held, allegation in election petition were clear that first respondent was guilty of corrupt practice of obtaining assistance of a Gazetted Officer, who was a Returning Officer as well as Electoral Registration Officer - Material facts and full particulars as required by s. 83 r/w s. 123(7) set out - HC was wrong in deleting para 13(a) - Wrongful acceptance of nomination paper of Harish Kumar and Harnek Singh - HC was not right in deleting para 11 on ground that petitioner had not disclosed that those voters who voted for Harish Kumar and Harnek Singh were voters of petitioner and improper acceptance of nomination papers of two candidates had materially affected result of election - HC was not wrong in striking off para 17 - When Returning Officer passed order that 20 out of 22 postal ballot papers did not contain requisite declaration u/r. 54A, they were liable to be rejected not contrary to law - Civil Appeal no. 4093/2004 partly allowed and order passed by HC ordering deletion of paras 11, 12 and 13(a) of election petition set aside - Direction of HC deleting para 17 is confirmed - Appeal partly allowed - Civil Appeal Nos. 5999-6000/2004 dismissed.
