Senthamilselvi v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1375; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 50; (2006) 5 SCC 676; 2006 CRLJ 4605; 2006 (2) JCC 955; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 314; 2006(6) SCALE 462; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Order of detention - Delay in disposal of representation - Application for bail - Sustainability - Order of detention passed in respect of detenue, which was questioned by his mother/appellant by filing Habeas Corpus Petition before HC and prayed that there was delay in disposal of representation and detenue had not filed any application for bail, therefore detaining authority had committed error in holding that there was imminent possibility of his coming out on jail - Further, detaining authority had relied upon confessional statement of co-accused without supplying copy there of - HC held that petition was filed even before order of rejection was served and that being so detenue could not make grievance that, State had not explained position as to how his representation was dealt with - Hence, there was no substance in plea that confessional statement of co-accused was relied upon, but copy thereof was not supplied and grounds of detention merely refer to confession by co-accused, that did not form foundation for detention and other hand it appears that detenue himself made confession and that was main factor on which order of detention was founded - Therefore, HC did not find any substance in said submission and dismissed petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Order of HC was succeeded - Held, relied on in Rajesh Gulati v. Govt, of NCT of Delhi and Anr., 2002 Indlaw SC 1653 - HC was justified, in coming to conclusion that though reference was made to co- accused's statement that was not relied upon for the purpose of detention - Hence, detenu might be released on bail could not be ipsi-dixit of detaining authority and on basis of materials before him, detaining authority came to conclusion that there was likelihood of detenu being released on bail and that was his subjective satisfaction based on materials and normally, such satisfaction was not to be interfered with - Appeal dismissed.
