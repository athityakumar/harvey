Dinesh @ Buddha v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 70; (2006) 3 SCC 771; AIR 2006 SC 1267; 2006 CRLJ 1679; 2006 (1) JCC 452; JT 2006 (3) SC 66; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 132; 2006(2) SCALE 734; 2006 (4) SCJ 211; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3(2)(v) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 375, 376(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1983 - Appellant was sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life and to pay a fine of Rs. 1, 000/- - Appeal against - Held, no evidence has been led to establish requirement of s 3(2)(v) - In absence of evidence to that effect, s 3(2)(v) has no application - Sentence is reduced to 10 years - Appeal dismissed.
