Daulat Singh Surana & Others v First Land Acquisition Collector & Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 760; (2007) 1 SCC 641; AIR 2007 SC 471; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 1; 2007 (1) AWC 524; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 55; JT 2007 (1) SC 24; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 260; 2006(11) SCALE 482; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1076

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provision) Act, 1947 - West Bengal Land (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1948, ss. 17(4), 3(1), 4(1a), 5(3) - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 37, 39 - Appellant aggrieved by Notification u/s. 4 and declaration u/s. 6 - Held, power of compulsory acquisition as described by term 'eminent domain' can be exercised only in interest and for welfare of people - Concept of public purpose should include matters, such as, safety, security, health, welfare and prosperity of community or public at large - State of West Bengal has been using premises in question for more than six decades for safety and security of people by having an office of Deputy Commissioner of Police - It cannot be said that premises was not required by State Govt. for interest and welfare of people or there was no public purpose involved in acquiring premises in question - No reason to interfere with well-reasoned judgment passed by DB - Appeal dismissed.
