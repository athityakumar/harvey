Assistant Engineer, C.A.D., Kota v Dhan Kunwar

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1468; (2006) 5 SCC 481; AIR 2006 SC 2670; 2006 (7) AWC 7183; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 254; JT 2006 (6) SC 142; 2006(6) SCALE 571; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 274; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2369

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rajasthan Public Works Department (Buildings and Roads) including Gardens, Irrigation, Water- Works and Ayurvedic Departments, Work-charged Employees Service Rules, 1964

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Rajasthan Public Works Department (Building and Roads) including Gardens, Irrigation, Water-Works and Ayurvedic Departments, Work-charged Employees Service Rules, 1964, r. 26 - Termination - The respondent (workman) was appointed as work-charged employee on temporary basis - The respondent was declared quasi-permanent in service and worked upto 30.5.1983 - Subsequently, the Appellant terminated her service after paying one month's salary in terms of r. 26 of 1964 Rules - After about 8 years dispute was raised by the respondent before Labour Court u/s. 10(1) of 1947 - The Appellant contended that the department in which respondent was working was abolished - The Labour Court held that though the claim was delayed, yet the respondent was not to be denied the benefits - The Labour Court further directed 30% back wages to the appellant, along with direction for reinstatement - The Single Judge dismissed the appeal filed against said order and held that merely because the claim was raised after about 8 years, that did not disentitle the respondent to get relief and the Labour Court was justified in awarding only 30% back wages - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether lower courts rightly allowed the claim of the appellant? - Held, the SC in Nedungadi Bank Ltd. vs . K.P. Madhavankutty and Ors. 2000 INDLAW SC 123, held that the law did not prescribe any time-limit for the appropriate Government to exercise its powers u/s. 10 of the Act - It was not that this power could be exercised at any point of time and to revive matters which had since settled - Hence, following said judgment, Labour Court should not have granted relief - Unfortunately, the Single Judge and the DB did not consider the issues in their proper perspective and arrived at abrupt conclusions without even indicating justifiable reasons - Order of reinstatement was set aside - Appeal allowed.
