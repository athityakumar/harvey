Bhupinder Singh & Ors v Jarnail Singh & Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1465; (2006) 6 SCC 277; AIR 2006 SC 2622; 2006 CRLJ 3621; 2006 (3) JCC 1415; JT 2006 (6) SC 619; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2684; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 677; 2006(7) SCALE 79; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Borstal Act, 1926

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167 - Dowry death - Delay in submitting the challan/charge-sheet - Regular Bail - Grant of - Appellants (husband and in-laws) allegedly caused the death of deceased/wife - Appellants were arrested in pursuance of lodging of an FIR alleging an offence u/s. 304(B) IPC - Appellants sought bail before the Magistrate Court - Appellants contended that since the challan/charge-sheet was not filed in time, they were entitled to bail in terms of s. 167(2)(a)(ii) CrPC - Magistrate Court rejected appellant's bail application holding that the challan was presented in court prior to the completion of 90 days of filing of FIR and therefore, it was presented within the prescribed period - Appellants filed appeal before the Sessions Court - Sessions Court granted bail to appellants holding that the challan should be filed within 60 days and not within 90 days - Respondent/De facto complainant challenged the Sessions Court order before the HC - HC set aside the bail of appellants holding that period during which the challan has to be filed is 90 days and not 60 days as held by the Sessions Court - Hence the present appeal - (A) In a case involving offence punishable u/s. 304(B) IPC, is the period for filing challan 90 days or 60 days - Held, period of 90 days is applicable to cases where the investigation relates to the three categories of offences which are punishable with (i) death, (ii) imprisonment for life; or (iii) imprisonment for a term of not less than ten years - In case of s. 304(B) IPC, the range varies between 7 years and imprisonment for life - What should be the adequate punishment in a given case has to be decided by the Court on the basis of the facts and circumstances involved in the particular case - Where minimum and maximum sentences are prescribed both are imposable depending on the facts of the cases - It is for the Court, after recording conviction, to impose appropriate sentence and it cannot, therefore, be accepted that only the minimum sentence is imposable and not the maximum sentence - Merely because minimum sentence is provided that does not mean that the sentence imposable is only the minimum sentence - Therefore, HC's view in the impugned order that permissible period of filing of challan was 90 days is the correct view - (B) Does mere filing of challan without relevant documents satisfy the requirement of filing the challan within a stipulated period for the purpose of s. 167(2)(a) - Held, challan which the appellants called an incomplete challan was in fact a completed report of the kind which s. 173(1)(a) of the CrPC contemplates - Said report did contain the name of the accused and the nature of the offence - Police report can only be what is prescribed in s. 173(2) there would be sufficient compliance if what is required to be mentioned by the statute has been set down in the report - Magistrate Court took proper cognizance of the offence on the basis of such report - Allegation of filing of the defective challan was really of no consequence - Since all the relevant documents were before the Court before expiry of 90 days period, grievance of the appellant is sans merit - Appeal dismissed.
