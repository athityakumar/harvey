District Rehabilitation Officer & Ors v Jay Kishore Maity & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 752; (2006) 12 SCC 380; AIR 2007 SC 290; 2006 (7) AWC 7636; JT 2006 (10) SC 276; 2007 (2) LLJ 401; 2006(11) SCALE 545; 2007 (1) SLR 333; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1011

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Scheme for Rehabilitation of disabled people - Employees filed Original Applications contending that they being employees of Central Govt, terms and conditions of services applicable to Central Govt. should apply in their case also - Allowed - Held, employees do not become employees of Central Govt. only because project was conceived by it or it used to give directions from time to time - Central Administrative Tribunal opined that Respondents are employees of Central Govt. - No reason has been assigned therefore - No analysis of available materials was made - Concerned employees who have affirmed affidavits showing inclination to serve any project under Central Govt, may be absorbed by it - Appeals disposed of.
