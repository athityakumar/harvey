Naveen Kohli v Neelu Kohli

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, A.K. Mathur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 598; (2006) 4 SCC 558; AIR 2006 SC 1675; 2006 (63) ALR 313; 2006 (2) AWC 1057; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 240; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2182; 2006 (1) JCC 520; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 128; JT 2006 (3) SC 491; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 241; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 290; 2006 (3) RLW 1892; 2006(3) SCALE 252; 2006 (3) SCJ 673; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Companies Act, 1956 - Divorce and Matrimonial Causes Amendment Act, 1920 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Dissolution of marriage - False FIR registered against husband u/ss. 420/467/468 and 471 of IPC - Husband and wife have been living separately for more than 10 years - Large number of criminal and civil proceedings initiated against each other - Cruelty - Whether in facts and circumstances of this case concept of Irretrievable Breakdown of Marriage is applicable? - Held, Irretrievable breakdown of marriage is not a ground for divorce under Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Because of change of circumstances and for covering a large number of cases where marriages are virtually dead and unless this concept is pressed into services, divorce cannot be granted - Ultimately, it is for Legislature whether to include irretrievable breakdown of marriage as a ground of divorce or not but in our considered opinion Legislature must consider irretrievable breakdown of marriage as a ground for grant of divorce under Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - High Court ought to have examined facts of case and its impact - In instant case, following cases were filed by respondent against appellant 1 - Parties filed a large number of cases against each other - Even at this stage, respondent does not want divorce by mutual consent - From analysis and evaluation of entire evidence, it is clear that respondent has resolved to live in agony only to make life a miserable hell for appellant as well - This type of adamant and callous attitude, in context of facts of this case, leaves no manner of doubt that respondent is bent upon treating appellant with mental cruelty - It is abundantly clear that marriage between parties had broken down irretrievably and there is no chance of their coming together, or living together again - In view of fact that parties have been living separately for more than 10 years and a very large number of aforementioned criminal and civil proceedings have been initiated by respondent against appellant and some proceedings have been initiated by appellant against respondent, matrimonial bond between parties is beyond repair - A marriage between parties is only in name - Marriage has been wrecked beyond hope of salvage, public interest and interest of all concerned lies in recognition of fact and to declare defunct de jure what is already defunct de facto - To keep sham is obviously conducive to immorality and potentially more prejudicial to public interest than a dissolution of marriage bond - HC was not justified in setting aside order of Trial Court - On consideration of totality of facts, this Court would like to recommend Union of India to seriously consider bringing an amendment in Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 to incorporate irretrievable breakdown of marriage as a ground for grant of divorce - A copy of this judgment be sent to Secretary, Ministry of Law and Justice, Department of Legal Affairs, Govt. of India for taking appropriate steps - Appeal disposed of.
