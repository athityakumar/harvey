Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi v Carrier Aircon Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 294; (2006) 5 SCC 596; 2006 (199) E.L.T. 577; JT 2006 (6) SC 181; 2006(6) SCALE 564; 2006 (7) SCJ 448; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11 A and 11 C - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether chillers manufactured by respondent are classifiable under Chapter Heading 84.18 of Schedule to Central Tariff Act as claimed by them or under Chap Heading 84.15 as contended by revenue? - Held, only because 90% of chillers manufactured by respondent are used in air-conditioning system cannot be basis for classification of chillers as parts of air-Conditioning system classifiable under heading 84.15 - Chillers in domestic and international trade parlance are known as refrigerating equipment - No merit in these appeals - Appeals dismissed.
