State of Maharashtra and Others v Nagpur Distillers, Nagpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 653; (2006) 5 SCC 112; AIR 2006 SC 1987; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 200; 2006 (2) JCC 738; JT 2006 (5) SC 336; 2006(5) SCALE 77; 2006 (6) SCJ 372; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Maharashtra Distillation of Spirit and Manufacture of Potable Liquor Rules, 1966 - Interim order granting stay of demand - Respondent no. 1 was a partnership firm engaged in the business of manufacture and sale of Indian Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL) and holder of a wholesale licence under the State Govt. in Form PLL as per the 1966 Rules that were made under the 1949 Act - Respondent no. 1 did not own a distillery and was not manufacturing rectified spirit and extra neutral alcohol, which it required for manufacture of IMFL, but had to purchase rectified spirit and extra neutral alcohol from distilleries owned by others - Bombay Rectified Spirit (Transport in Bond) Rules 1951 were made applicable for rectified spirit under which issue of a transport pass was required for the transport of rectified spirit from the distillery to the factory of the user subject to payment of the fee under the 1951 Rules - Respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 2, a partner therof, filed a petition challenging the notification dt. 12-7-1999, impugning r. 5 of the 1951 Rules and the fee imposed on them under those Rules - Respondents also sought an interim order of stay of the demand pending disposal of the petition - HC granted an interim order staying the recovery of the fee on the strength of SC decision in Vam Organic Chemicals Limited, where SC granted interim orders of stay as demanded by a similarly situated licensee - Hence, instant appeal by the State and office of the State Excise Department - Whether the interim order granting stay of demand was proper - Held, that decision in Vam Organic Chemicals Limited may be distinguishable from cases where the licensee himself did not manufacture the rectified spirit - In the instant case, licensee himself did not manufacture the rectified spirit - Rectified spirit was not manufactured by the respondent no. 1 and such spirit was not being used captively in its own premises for manufacture of IMFL - HC was not justified in passing an unconditional interim order of stay as sought for by the respondents - HC should have paid a little more attention to the interests of the State and the consequences arising out of its order staying the payment of the fee merely on an undertaking by the licensee to pay it in case at a future point of time he was found liable to pay the same - To ensure balance the equities and afford protection to the interests of the State and the interests of the licensee, licensee was to pay 50 per cent of the license fee payable and that it was required to give an undertaking to pay the balance 50 per cent in case ultimately the W.P. was decided against it - Appeal allowed.
