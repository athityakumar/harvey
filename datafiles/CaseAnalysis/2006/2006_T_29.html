Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v T.M. Krishnaveni and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 827; (2006) 7 SCC 365; 2007 (1) AWC 117; JT 2006 (7) SC 582; 2006(8) SCALE 1; 2006 (7) SCJ 73; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Case of parties before High Court was that as on 17-02-1976, T Chinna Seetharamaiah was mentally incapacitated - His wife on behalf of her husband, for herself, two major sons, namely, and minor son, filed joint declaration of their shares to land u/s. 6 of 1976 Act on 15-09-1976 - Competent authority on 23-07-1979 issued a Draft Statement u/s. 8(1) and Notice u/s. 8(3) of Act, determining surplus area after giving one unit to T Chinna Seetharamaiah, his wife and minor son and one unit each to two major sons - Daughters were held not entitled to any share in property - All declarants, except daughters of T Chinna Seetharamaiah, filed a joint petition on 23-07-1979 stating that they have no objection for surrender of excess land as determined by competent authority - Learned Single Judge directed reopening of entire declarations on basis of claims made by respondents including claim of eldest married daughter who had not filed declaration under Act for getting her share in land on basis of family arrangement dated 13-11-1970 - Division Bench of HC dismissed Writ Appeals of appellants upholding judgment and order of learned Single Judge - (A) Whether judgment and order of Division Bench is sustainable? - Held, as deceased T Chinna Seetharamaiah and his wife T Rama Tulsamma and their three sons did not file any objection as envisaged under Act, in principle, it must be accepted that they had no objection in respect of their shares of land having vested in State Govt. - Proceedings initiated and completed by competent authority could not be found to be arbitrary, perverse or illegal on facts of case or in violation of provisions of Act and/or Rules framed thereunder - (B) Whether daughters acquired any right under family arrangement dated 23/07/1979? - Further held, agreement was a valid and legal settlement by which some land was given by father to his daughters at time of their marriages under age-old custom known as 'Pasupu Kumkuma' conferring an absolute title of daughters to property - Contention of learned senior counsel for appellants that Smt P Rukmini and Smt M Krishnaveni, for first time, have raised claim of their shares to land on basis of alleged family settlement in writ petitions filed by them, does not merit acceptance - Appellants are directed to hold an independent inquiry in terms of provisions of Act and Rules framed thereunder into claims of declarants Smt M Krishnaveni and Smt P Rukmini - Appeal dismissed.
