D. Vinod Shivappa v Nanda Belljappa

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 658; (2006) 6 SCC 456; AIR 2006 SC 2179; 2007 (66) ALR 871; 2006 (3) BC 465; 2006 CRLJ 2897; 2006 (2) JCC (NI) 161; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 377; JT 2006 (11) SC 187; 2006 (5) KarLJ 32; 2006 (143) PLR 787; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 50; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 145; 2006 (3) RLW 2528; 2006(6) SCALE 277; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 1008

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques, Legal Notice

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Cheque issued appellant returned with endorsement - Notice send to appellants to make payments - Returned unserved - Appellant filed an application u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 before the High Court - Dismissed - (A) Whether notice issued by the complainant is sufficient and it is deemed served? - Complainant not admitted in the complaint that notice had not been served within the meaning of s. 138 - (B) Whether in appellant could pray for quashing of the proceedings u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973? - Drawer of the cheque makes himself liable for prosecution u/s. 138 if he fails to make the payment within fifteen days of the receipt of the notice given by the drawee - With a view to avoid unnecessary prosecution of an honest drawer of a cheque, or to give an opportunity to the drawer to make amends, the proviso to s. 138 provides that after dishonor of the cheque, the payee or the holder of the cheque in due course must give a written notice to the drawer to make good the payment - If in case of non availability of drawer the law is understood to mean that there has been no service of notice, it would completely defeat the very purpose of the Act - Once the complainant, the payee of the cheque, issues notice to the drawer of the cheque, the cause of action to file a complaint arises on the expiry of the period prescribed for payment by the drawer of the cheque - If the complainant is able to prove that the drawer of the cheque knew about the notice and deliberately evaded service and got a false endorsement made only to defeat the process of law, the Court shall presume service of notice - This, however, is a matter of evidence and proof - Held, it would be premature at the stage of issuance of process to move the HC for quashing of the proceeding u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 - In such a case the HC ought not to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 - Essentially a question of fact, and that must be considered in the light of the evidence on record - Appeal dismissed.
