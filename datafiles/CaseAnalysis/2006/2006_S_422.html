S.N. Chandrashekar and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 40; (2006) 3 SCC 208; AIR 2006 SC 1204; JT 2006 (2) SC 202; 2006 (2) KarLJ 305; 2006(2) SCALE 248; 2006 (2) SCJ 655; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1039

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961 - Conversion of site for residential purpose for use of a (commercial) vegetarian restaurant - Bangalore Development Authority held that conversion was in public interest and permitted conversion of use from dwelling to commercial (restaurant) - Writ dismissed holding that as Appellants did not file any objection, they were not entitled to any relief - Appeal against - Held, Planning Authority allowed change of user in violation of Zoning Regulations - Respondent No 6 can utilize premises held by him within purview of permissible user as contained in Zoning Regulations as had invested a heavy amount - Appeal allowed.
