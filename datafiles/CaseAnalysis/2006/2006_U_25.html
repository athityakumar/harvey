 Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others v Umadevi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, Y.K. Sabharwal, Arun Kumar, G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 125; (2006) 4 SCC 1; AIR 2006 SC 1806; 2006 (5) AWC 5325; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 36; JT 2006 (4) SC 420; 2006 (4) KarLJ 29; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 326; 2006(4) SCALE 197; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 953; 2006 (3) SLR 1; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1880

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Ad Hoc Appointment, Forced Labour

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts.12, 14, 141, 142, 16, 162, 21, 226, 309, 32, 39(a) - National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 - Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act, 1959 - Matter of regularization of ad hoc employees - Conflict relates to right, if any, of employees appointed by State or by its instrumentalities on a temporary basis or on daily wages or casually, to approach HC for issue of a writ of mandamus directing that they be made permanent in appropriate posts, work of which they were otherwise doing - Held, unless appointment is in terms of relevant rules and after a proper competition among qualified persons, same would not confer any right on appointee - Temporary employee could not claim to be made permanent on expiry of his term of appointment - Merely because a temporary employee or a casual wage worker is continued for a time beyond term of his appointment, he would not be entitled to be absorbed in regular service or made permanent, if original appointment was not made by following a due process of selection as envisaged by relevant rules - HCs should not ordinarily issue directions for absorption, regularization, or permanent continuance unless recruitment itself was made regularly and in terms of constitutional scheme - Theory of legitimate expectation cannot be successfully advanced by temporary, contractual or casual employees - Appeals disposed of.
