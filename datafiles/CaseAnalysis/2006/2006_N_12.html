Indian Oil Corporation v NEPC India Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 430; (2006) 6 SCC 736; AIR 2006 SC 2780; 2006 (2) JCC 1190; JT 2006 (6) SC 474; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 740; 2006(7) SCALE 286; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973,ss. 200, 250, 378, 403, 482 - Aircraft Act,1934, s. 8 - Indian Penal Code,1860,ss.378, 403, 405, 415, 425 - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002,s. 2(n) - Contract - Material supplied - Non payment - Civil and criminal proceedings - Challenged - Appellant entered into contracts with respondents for supply of aviation turbine fuel and aviation lubricants and both respondents were liable to pay nearly 19 crores as on 2007 to appellant - Respondent no.1 hypothecated its two Fokker aircrafts and respondent no.2 had hypothecated its aircraft to appellant by separate hypothecation deeds to secure dues - Tripartite agreement was also entered among parties setting out mode of payment of dues and recovery in event of default - Appellant filed suit against respondent no.1 in HC and HC had granted interim relief of temporary injunction in favour of appellant - Appellant also filed similar suit in trial Court for injunction - Appellant also filed 2 complaints against respondent no.1 and its directors u/s. 200 CrPC - Respondents filed petitions u/s. 482 CrPC in HC for quashing complaints filed by appellant and HC by common judgment allowed both petitions and quashed two complaints holding that provisions in complaint would not attract criminal offence - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, appellant had invoked both civil and criminal jurisdiction by filing civil suits and complaint u/ss. 378,403,405,415 and 425 IPC - SC in several judgments laid down principles relating to exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC to quash complaints and criminal proceedings - Remedies under commercial transaction or contractual dispute include both civil and criminal proceedings - nature and scope of civil proceedings were different from criminal proceeding - Criminal proceedings could not be quashed merely on ground that civil remedy was available or had been availed - Test to be followed was whether allegations in complaint would disclose criminal offence or not - Merely as appellant had availed civil remedy would not debar appellant to take criminal proceedings or to consider that appellant had estopped from seeking such criminal remedy - Hypothecation means creating security without delivery of title or possession and both ownership and possession would remain in debtor - Creditor would have equitable charge over property and was given right to take possession and sell hypothecated movables to recover his dues - Appellants complaint lacks ingredients of ss. 378,403 and 405 CrPC and had ingredients of ss. 415 and 425 IPC - HC was not justified in quashing complaints/criminal proceedings in entirety as complaint had ingredients u/ss. 415 and 425 IPC - Order of HC was quashed and set aside in respect of ss,415 and 425 IPC - Directed that Trial Courts should proceed with trial u/ss.415 and 425 IPC - Appeals partly allowed.
