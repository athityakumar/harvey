Kirtibhai Madhavlal Joshi and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1575; (2006) 4 SCC 680; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 1850; 2006 (2) JCC 959; 2007 (5) RCR(Criminal) 67

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bail Application, Financing to terrorism, Charged

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Terrorism Act, 2002, ss. 3(3), 22(3), 49 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Financing to terrorism - Charged - Bail application - Entitled to - Appellants A1 to A3 firm was used to receive huge amounts from Dubai through hawala transactions and with full knowledge of fact that money received by them and handed over to recipients was used for terrorist activities - Appellants case was considered by Review Committee - Review Committee gave adverse report against appellants - Accused were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 3(3), 22(3) of the Act and some offence under IPC by HC - HC rejected appellants' bail application - Hence, instant appeal - Held, provisions of s. 49(7) of the Act provided that where Public Prosecutor opposed application of accused for release on bail, no person accused of offence punishable under this Act or any rule made thereunder should be released on bail until Court was satisfied that there were grounds for believing that he was not guilty of committing said offence - However, proviso, as interpreted by SC in People's Union for Civil Liberties v. Union of India 2003 Indlaw SC 1399 provided that after expiry of period of 1 year from date of detention of accused for offence under the Act, provisions of subsection (6) should apply, which means that after expiry of 1 year of detention, accused should be released on bail under ordinary law without applying rigour of s. 49(7) of the Act - Confessions of appellants before SC and reports of Review Committees as material and provisions of s. 49 of the Act, case was made out for grant of bail to appellants - Accordingly, it was directed that appellants should be released on bail - Appeals allowed.
