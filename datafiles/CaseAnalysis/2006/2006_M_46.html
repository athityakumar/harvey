Oil & Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. v M/S Nippon Steel Corporation Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 745; (2007) 2 SCC 382; AIR 2007 SC 327; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 56; 2007 (1) AWC 648; JT 2006 (10) SC 238; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 73; 2006(12) SCALE 211; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 596

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Filing Of Award

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 14(2) - Companies Act, 1956 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 119, 119(b), 158 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 3 r. 5 - Bombay High Court (Original Side) Rules, r. 786 - Arbitration petition - Dismissed - Whether HC was justified in extending principle of constructive notice ignoring express stipulations of s.14(2)? - Whether HC was justified in ignoring fundamental difference between two expressions i e date of service of notice and date of knowledge of award? - Whether HC was justified in overlooking legislative intent in framing art.119(b) by incorporating expression 'the date of service of notice'? - Whether in view of exhaustive nature of art.119(b), HC was justified in importing principle embodied in O III r 5 CPC, 1908 - Whether HC has failed to appreciate significance of expression 'the Court shall thereupon give notice to parties of filing of award' occurring in s.14(2)? - Whether HC was justified in overlooking that presumption of constructive notice can be drawn only against party whom counsel is representing at time of performing said act? - Filing of award - Notice to party - Party, appellant did not instruct their counsel to file award before Court - Award was filed by counsel at instance of Arbitrator - Knowledge of said lawyer, who was counsel of appellant in arbitration proceedings about filing of award - Is not notice, either actual or constructive to appellant - Counsel acting as representative of arbitrator -And not as representative of appellant, a party - Presumption under O.3, R.5, not applicable - Limitation -Date of service of notice of filing of award before Court - Is relevant date for computation of period of limitation - Period of limitation cannot be computed from date of knowledge of award - Application for setting aside award - Limitation - Computation - Letter by Prothonotary and Senior Master of High Court - Directing appellant's counsel in arbitration proceeding to furnish detailed address of parties for purpose of serving them notice of filing award - Is not notice of filing of award to appellant, party - Court ought to serve notice separately - Period of limitation for application for setting aside award, cannot be computed from date when said counsel furnished addresses of parties - Doctrine of constructive notice -Cannot be extended to acts that are performed at instance of third party - Held, application for setting aside the award not barred by limitation.
