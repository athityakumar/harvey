Dhaneswar Mahakud and Others v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 630; (2006) 9 SCC 307; AIR 2006 SC 1727; 2006 CRLJ 2113; 2006 (2) JCC 676; JT 2006 (5) SC 144; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 860; 2006(4) SCALE 170; 2006 (5) SCJ 387; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 149 and 323 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Six accused of causing death of two persons - Conviction - On appeal High Court acquitted two and convicted other accused u/ss. 302 and 323 but did not find them guilty u/ss. 148 and 149 - Appeal - Whether in absence of a charge u/s. 34, IPC appellants can be convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 only? - To convict accused of an independent charge u/s. 302, IPC, it is necessary that injuries inflicted by each individual taken in isolation, were sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death of deceased persons - If injuries caused are sufficient in ordinary course of nature and they have been caused in furtherance of common intention, then each and every individual propagating common intention can be convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC, although Appellants not been charged u/s. 34, IPC and charged u/s. 149 IPC along with s. 302, IPC - Though there is substantial difference between two Sections, they also to some extent overlap - If common object does not necessarily involve a common intention, then substitution of s. 34 for s. 149 might result in prejudice to accused and ought not, therefore, to be permitted - But if it does involve a common intention then substitution of s. 34 for s. 149 must be held to be a formal matter - Non-applicability of s.149 is, therefore, no bar in convicting appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC - Held, that nature of injuries sustained by deceased clearly indicates intention of accused-appellants to do away with deceased - Evidence of witnesses along with injuries reported brings out common intention of accused-appellants - Accused-appellants would not be prejudiced merely because charge was framed u/s. 302 r/w s. 149, IPC and not u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC - Appeal dismissed.
