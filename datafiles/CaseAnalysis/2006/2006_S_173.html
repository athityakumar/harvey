Chairman, Sebi v Shriram Mutual Fund and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 237; (2006) 5 SCC 361; AIR 2006 SC 2287; 2006 (3) BC 392; JT 2006 (11) SC 164; 2006(6) SCALE 154; 2006 (4) SCJ 439; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 833

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market; Exchange Control & FDI; Sales Tax; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: CAPITAL MARKETS, federal structure, federalism

Summary: [A.] Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities And Exchange Board Of India Act, 1992, ss. 12-B, 15(I), 15-E, 15-I, 15-J, 15-Z, 15A, 15B, 15C, 15D(b) - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996, regn. 25(7)(a), ss. 139(1), 15-H, 276-C - Failure to comply with regn. 25 (7)(a) with regard to routing of transactions through associate brokers - SAT held that penalty to be imposed for failure to perform a statutory obligation is a matter of discretion - Whether once it is conclusively established that Mutual Fund has violated terms of Certificate of Registration and statutory Regulations imposition of penalty becomes a sine qua non of violation? - Held, penalty is attracted as soon as contravention of statutory obligation as contemplated by Act and Regulation is established and hence intention of parties committing such violation becomes wholly irrelevant - Unless language of statute indicates need to establish presence of mens rea, it is wholly unnecessary to ascertain whether such a violation was intentional or not - Once contravention is established then penalty is to follow - Appeals allowed.
