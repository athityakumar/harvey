Committee of Management Kanya Junior High School v Sachiv, U.P. Basic Shiksha Parishad Allahabad

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 458; (2006) 11 SCC 92; AIR 2006 SC 2974; 2006 (6) AWC 5798; JT 2006 (7) SC 562; 2006(8) SCALE 222; 2006 (7) SLR 24; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Harijan Temple Entry Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act 1972, Uttar Pradesh Recognized Basic Schools (Junior High Schools) (Recruitment and Conditions of Services of Teacher) Rules, 1978

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30(1) - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act 1972 - Uttar Pradesh Recognized Basic Schools (Junior High Schools) (Recruitment and Conditions of Services of Teacher) Rules, 1978, rr. 15, 16 - Allahabad High Court Rules, r. 10 - Respondent working as a teacher - Order of termination - Single Judge held that minority status could be granted only by U P Basic Shiksha Parishad and since it was not granted to appellant institution by said Parishad appellant institution could not terminate services of respondent no 3 without prior permission from District Basic Education Officer - Appeal against - Held, State at one point of time accepted school in question as having been established and administered by Jain community which is a minority community in State of Uttar Pradesh - Prior approval of District Basic Education Officer was not necessary before terminating services of a teacher - As appellant's institution was recognised as a minority institution, HC was not correct in interfering in manner it did - Appeal allowed.
