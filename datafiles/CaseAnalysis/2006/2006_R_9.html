Govindammal v R. Perumal Chettiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 710; (2006) 11 SCC 600; AIR 2007 SC 204; 2007 (2) ALT 91; 2007 (1) AWC 761; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 139; JT 2006 (10) SC 121; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 569; 2006(11) SCALE 452

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Coparcener

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for partition and separate possession and also claimed for rendition of accounts - Whether plaintiff is entitled to 1/3 rd share in properties or not? - Held, a co-sharer, as is well settled, becomes a constructive trustee of other co-sharer and right of a person or his predecessors-interest is deemed to have been protected by trustees - Just because plaintiff gave a notice and she did not pursue same, on that basis no adverse inference can be drawn and she cannot be ousted on that count by way of adverse possession - Appeals disposed of.
