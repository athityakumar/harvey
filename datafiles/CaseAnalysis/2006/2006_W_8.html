Management, Mettur Beardsell Limited v Workmen of Mettur Beardsell Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 177; (2006) 9 SCC 488; AIR 2006 SC 2056; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 237; JT 2006 (5) SC 295; 2006(4) SCALE 612; 2006 (6) SCJ 259; 2006 (4) SLR 25; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(b), 12(3), 25FF, 25FFF, 33(C)(2) - Transfer of undertaking - Consent of individual employee not a pre-requisite for transfer - Not obtaining consent cannot be a ground to invalidate transfer - Textile division of appellant hired off - Appellant became partner of newly formed subsidiary - Employees of textile division absorbed as part of Integrated Textile Division with unaltered terms and conditions of work covering salary, wages, benefits, retrenchment and retirement - Such a partial transfer of a division of undertaking is outside the ambit of s.25FF - Employees cannot claim retrenchment compensation u/S.25FF - Fraud alleged on grounds that only 27 employees were targeted for transfer - Not established in the absence of any specific material and evidence - Appeals disposed of.
