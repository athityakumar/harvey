Ranjit Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 116; (2006) 4 SCC 153; AIR 2006 SC 3685; 2006 (2) AllCJ 1241; 2006 (5) AWC 5274; 2006 (129) DLT 405; 2006 (2) ESC 224; JT 2006 (4) SC 376; 2006(4) SCALE 154; 2006 (4) SCJ 710; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 885; 2006 (3) SLR 82; 2006 (3) Supreme 363; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1538

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964, rr. 18(3), 3(1)(i)(ii) - Appellant allegedly possessing assets disproportionate to his known source of income - Enquiry Officer exonerated him from charges - Disciplinary Authority, however, differed with findings of Enquiry Officer directed dismissal of Appellant from services - WP dismissed - Appeal against - Held, apart from complying with principles of natural justice it was obligatory on part of Disciplinary Authority, in absence of any show cause filed by Appellant, to analyse materials on records afresh - Matter is remitted to Disciplinary Authority for consideration of matter afresh.
