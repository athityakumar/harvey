Pardeep Kumar v Union Administration, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 400; (2006) 10 SCC 608; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 41; AIR 2006 SC 2992; 2007 (2) ALT 56; 2006 CRLJ 3894; 2006 (3) JCC 1347; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 73; JT 2006 (7) SC 457; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2724; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 34; 2007 (1) RLW 285; 2006(8) SCALE 68; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 109, 34, 366, 368, 376, 376(2)(g) - Appeal against conviction - Held, mere presence at a place is insufficient to show that there was a prior concert or meeting of mind or plan formed suddenly at time of commission of offence by accused-appellant with other accused persons for commission of rape on prosecutrix - Accused- Appellant is entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
