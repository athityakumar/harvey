State of Assam and Another v Messrs Abhinandan Trading Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1339; (2007) 11 SCC 704; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2006 (10) SC 356; 2006(12) SCALE 86; 2007 (7) SCJ 790; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Assam Excise Rules, 1945

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Excise - Administrative - Assam Excise Rules, 1945, r. 91 - Whether Division Bench of High Court erred in holding that entire process of granting contract has been vitiated resulting in an arbitrary decision? - Unless, it can be shown that said procedure had been misused to favour any particular individual, it would not be proper for court to express any opinion as to procedure Govt. should adopt except to say that whatever procedure is adopted should be open, fair and transparent - Held, that there is scope of concept of 'viability range' being misused to favour a particular applicant - In order to avoid future controversy, Govt. of Assam may adopt some other procedure for granting similar contracts in future which is open and transparent - Appeals allowed.
