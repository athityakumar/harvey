Sadhu Singh v Gurdwara Sahib Narike and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 788; (2006) 8 SCC 75; AIR 2006 SC 3282; 2006 (65) ALR 639; 2007 (1) ALT 12; 2006 (4) AWC 3865; 2006 (4) CTC 773; JT 2006 (8) SC 525; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; 2007 (145) PLR 3; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 468; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 754; 2006 (Supp) RD 757; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 799; 2007 (1) UC 51

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Women & Children

Keywords: Testamentary Succession

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 8 and 14 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 18, 21, 22, 27 and 28 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 39 - R's self-acquired property - R executed a Will - Will's genuinity doubted - R's widow gifted property to Gurdwara - Whether under will of R, widow took only a life estate and properties were to vest in appellant and his brother? - Held, thus on reconciling various clauses in Will and destination for properties that testator had in mind, Court has no hesitation in coming to conclusion that apparent absolute estate in favour of widow has to be cut down to a life estate so as to accommodate estate conferred on nephews - Thus understood, it has necessarily to be held, as was held by First Appellate Court, that I was not competent to gift away properties in favour of Gurdwara as she had done - Even if gift were to be treated as valid, donee thereunder cannot resist claim for eviction by legatees under Will, nephews of R, on cessation of life estate of widow - Admittedly, that life estate has ceased and once it is found that plaintiff has acquired a title to property as a legatee under Will, he would be entitled for and on behalf of himself and his brother to recover possession of property from Gurdwara in view of death of I - Appeal allowed.
