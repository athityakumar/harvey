FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

In Re : News Item Published In Hindustan Times Titled "And Quiet Flows The Maily Yamuna"

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1622; (2009) 17 SCC 720

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Water

Keywords: Water Pollution, Encroachment, Removal of encroachment

Summary: Water - Administrative - Water pollution - Encroachment - Removal of encroachment - Central Pollution Control Board filed affidavit stating that water quality gets polluted and affected at a point when it enters Delhi - Affidavit gave details of 22 rounds of monitoring at various places in Delhi and stated that none of drains were meeting criteria for one or other indicator parameters - Main cause of water pollution was massive encroachment on bank of Yamuna river - DB, seized of matter and issued direction for removal of encroachment and constituted Committee to monitor - Committee was directed to take up in right earnestness and on day-to-day basis task of removing encroachments up to 300 meters from both sides of river Yamuna - It should not be doubted that if Delhi residents have to be provided with 'C' category water, it was necessary, apart from other measures to remove encroachment on bank of river as also on bank of drains - Frequent use of large number of parks in city for marriage and other functions were bound to degrade environment and utility of parks as recreation for public - Held, in view of interest of society it was permitted only 50% per cent of parks to be used for marriages etc - It was claimed, that granting permission for use of 70 parks for aforesaid purposes - Total number of parks was 146 - It was implicit in that even temporary user of fifty per cent parks for marriages, etc. should be stopped altogether in phased manner and that regulatory authority would ensure that user did not cause any inconvenience or nuisance to others - MCD had spelt out 16 conditions on which parks were let out for holding functions - Nothing was placed on record either by MCD or by DDA as to what action, if any was taken against anyone in last 10 years who might have not properly used parks, violated rules and caused inconvenience - Applicant had placed on record certain photographs showing construction of big pandal as placement of generator in DDA park - There was nothing on record as to how compliance with conditions was ensured and who were officers to ensure compliance - SC directed DDA as well as MCD to file affidavits of its Vice-Chairman and Commissioner to place on record steps taken to stop user in phased manner- DDA should specifically place on record its stand as to whether green area should be used for holding of such functions which, prima facie, would be contrary to user permissible in master plan - Affidavits should also state whether any additional community halls had been constructed and/or planned to be constructed to facilitate holding of functions of marriages, etc - Order accordingly.
