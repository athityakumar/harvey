Trimukh Maroti Kirkan v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 680; (2006) 10 SCC 681; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 80; 2007 CRLJ 20; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 293; JT 2006 (9) SC 50; 2006(10) SCALE 190; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 364, 498-A - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 174, 313 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(a) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Appellant was convicted and sentenced to imprisonment for life and a fine of Rs. 2, 000/- - Appeal against conviction - Held, there is no eye-witness of occurrence and case of prosecution rests on circumstantial evidence - Further held, where an offence like murder is committed in secrecy inside a house, initial burden to establish case would undoubtedly be upon prosecution, but nature and amount of evidence to be led by it to establish charge cannot be of same degree as is required in other cases of circumstantial evidence - Burden would be of a comparatively lighter character - Circumstances enumerated above unerringly point to guilt of accused and they are inconsistent with his innocence - Appeal dismissed.
