Jay Engineering Works Limited v Industry Facilitation Council and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 488; (2006) 8 SCC 677; AIR 2006 SC 3252; 2006 (6) AWC 5454; 2007 (1) DRTC 446; 2006(9) SCALE 285; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Rehabilitation Scheme, Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, Financial Institutions Act, 1993, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 15, 20(4), 22, 25, 32(1) - Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, s. 6(2) - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 433 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993 - Appellant declared a sick unit - Respondent filed a claim petition - Amount of Rs. 10, 92, 253. 00 awarded - Bank account of Appellant was attached - Held, if liabilities of Appellant are covered by Scheme framed u/s. 22 of 985 Act, HC was clearly in error in coming to conclusion that provisions thereof are not attracted only because debt had been incurred after Company was declared to be a sick one - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
