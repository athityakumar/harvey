Union Of India & Ors v M. Mathivanan

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1500; (2006) 6 SCC 57; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1271; AIR 2006 SC 2326; 2006 (7) AWC 7149; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 50; JT 2006 (6) SC 56; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 305; 2006(6) SCALE 466; 2006(6) SCALE 473; 2006 (4) SLR 727; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 30; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 2008

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Benefit of Time Bound Promotion Scheme - Entitlement to - Respondent was enrolled as Reserve Training Pool Postal Assistant and was appointed initially as Postal Assistant in the year 1981 - In 1983, he volunteered for enrollment in Army Postal Services - A Time Bound Promotion Scheme was formulated by the authorities that provided placing of officers in the 'next higher grade' who had completed sixteen years of service in Group 'C' and 'D' - In 1999, the respondent made an application for granting benefit of Time Bound Promotion Scheme as he had completed sixteen years - Respondent was not included in the Time Bound Promotion Scheme - On appeal, Central Administrative Tribunal held that respondent services ought to have been considered from 1983 and since he had completed sixteen years in 1999, he was entitled to the benefit of Time Bound Promotion Scheme - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Held, for placing of an officer in the 'next higher grade' what was relevant and material was that such official belonging to basic grades in Group 'C' and 'D' must have completed "sixteen years of service in that Grade" - The Tribunal, therefore, rightly held that basic eligibility condition for being placed in the next higher grade was that the officer must have completed sixteen years of service in the basic grade in Group 'C' and Group 'D' - Respondent was, therefore, entitled to the benefit of 'next higher grade' under paragraph 1 of Time Bound Promotion Scheme and the action of the authorities in not granting the said benefit was illegal and contrary to law - Appeal dismissed.
