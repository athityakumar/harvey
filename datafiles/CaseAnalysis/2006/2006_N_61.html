Messrs Shobika Attire v New India Assurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 495; (2006) 8 SCC 35; AIR 2006 SC 3261; 2006 (65) ALR 347; 2007 (1) ALT 4; 2006 (4) AWC 3903; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 22; 2007 (1) CCC 284; 2006 (4) CPJ(Sc) 3; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 330; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 465; 2006(9) SCALE 293; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 23 - Damages of stock during rioting- Claim for payment of compensation amounting to Rs. 2.20 crores - Held, appellants had discharged initial burden regarding destruction, damage of showroom and stocks therein by fire and riot in support of claim under insurance policy and it was for insurance company to disprove such claim with evidence, if any - Insurance company, despite report of investigator, failed to establish that claim of appellants was not justified and was not covered by policy of insurance - Appeal allowed.
