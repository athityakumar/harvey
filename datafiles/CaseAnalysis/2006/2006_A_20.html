A.P.S.R.T.C. and Others v G. Srinivas Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 62; (2006) 3 SCC 674; AIR 2006 SC 1465; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 227; JT 2006 (3) SC 189; 2006(2) SCALE 539; 2006 (3) SCJ 132; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 494; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1438

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, s. 10(1) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Circular dated 1/9/1988 containing guidelines for absorption of persons employed on casual basis - Regularisation of contract labours - HC held that there was direct relationship of master and servant between principal employer (Corporation) and contract labour (Respondents) and, therefore, respondents were entitled to absorption - Appeal against - Held, HC could not, in exercise of its jurisdiction under art.226, direct absorption of respondents, on ground that work for which respondents were engaged as contract labour, was perennial in nature - If respondents want relief of absorption, they will have to approach Industrial Tribunal/Court and establish that contract labour system was only a ruse/camouflage to avoid labour law benefits to them - Respondents were not also entitled to relief of absorption/regularization on basis of Circular dated 1/9/1988, as it specifically excluded contract labour - Appeal allowed.
