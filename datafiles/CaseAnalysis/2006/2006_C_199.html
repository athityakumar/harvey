Messrs Ispat Industries Limited v Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 565; (2006) 12 SCC 583; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 767; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 561; 2006(9) SCALE 652; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Delivery At The Time And Place Of Importation, Customs Valuation Rule, 1988

Summary: [A.] Customs - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 14, 18, 8(a) - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 20 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Customs Valuation Rule, 1988, rr. 9, 9(2)(a) - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988 - Whether transportation charges for use of barges for carrying cargo from mother vessel which anchored at BFL to Dharmatar jetty where goods were unloaded are to be added to calculate assessable value for purpose of duty? - Held, unloading of goods from mother ship at BFL was valid, since it was done in accordance with ss.33, 34 - Freight upto Dharamtar jetty had been paid by buyer - Fact that mother ship could not come upto Dharamtar Jetty is an extraordinary situation (due to lack of draft) and hence any extra transportation charge to meet this situation cannot be added to value of goods - Appeals disposed of.
