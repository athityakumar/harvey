State of Kerala v Kurissum Moottil Antony

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1256; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 403; (2007) 1 SCC 627; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1828; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 535; JT 2006 (10) SC 370; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 172; 2006(12) SCALE 94; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self-Inflicted

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 377 - Unnatural offences - Sexual offence - Trail Court convicted respondent for having committed unnatural offences on minor girl - On appeal High Court acquitted respondent from charges and held that there is absence of corroboration and alleged suppression of report purported to have been given before FIR in question was lodged - Whether HC right in acquitting respondent from charges u/s. 377 IPC, 1860? - Accused cannot cling to fossil formula and insist on corroborative evidence, even if taken as a whole, case spoken to by victim strikes judicial mind as probable - Judicial response to human rights cannot be blunted by legal jugglery - Just as witness who has sustained an injury, which is not shown or believed to be self-inflicted, is best witness in sense that he is least likely to exculpate real offender, evidence of victim of sex offence is entitled to great weight, absence of corroboration notwithstanding - In cross-examination no material inconsistency has surfaced except some minor ones which are but natural - Held, HC clearly lost sight of these factors and has directed acquittal on untenable grounds - Appeal allowed.
