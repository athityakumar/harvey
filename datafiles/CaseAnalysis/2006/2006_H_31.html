New India Assurance Co. Ltd v Harshadbhai Amrutbhai Modhiya and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 303; (2006) 5 SCC 192; 2006 (2) ACC 539; 2008 (1) ACC 149; AIR 2006 SC 1926; 2006 (7) AWC 6680; 2006 (3) CTC 166; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2246; 2006 (3) JhrCR (SC)110; JT 2006 (5) SC 228; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 156; 2006 (143) PLR 727; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 814; 2006 (3) RLW 2142; 2006(5) SCALE 70; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Employer's Liability

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Insurance - Carriers & Tranportation - Insurance Act, 1938 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 4-A(3) - Insurance Claim - Award - Liability of Insurer - Sustainability - Heirs and legal representatives of deceased filed application for grant of compensation before Court , claiming sum of Rs. 2,25,220/- - Court awarded sum of Rs. 2,25,220/- with 9% interest from date of filing of application till realization in favour of claimants - Appeal against award of compensation was filed by appellant, which had been dismissed - Appellant contended that having regard to contract of insurance, appellant was not liable to pay any interest on awarded sum - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interest was payable by insurer while indemnifying insured amount of compensation awarded against him under the 1923 Act was sustainable - Held, s. 3 of the 1923 Act provided for employer's liability to pay compensation in event workman suffered personal injury by accident arising out of and in course of his employment - By provisions of the 1923 Act, employer was not statutorily liable to enter into contract of insurance, however, contract of insurance was entered into by and between employer and insurer, insurer should be liable to indemnify employer - In Ved Prakash Garg vs Premi Devi and Others 1997 Indlaw SC 414, SC held that in terms of contract of insurance entered into by and between employer and insurer under provisions of the 1988 Act, which would also apply in given case to claim under provisions of the 1923 Act - Where statute did not provide for compulsory insurance or extent thereof, it would bear repetition to state, parties were free to choose their own terms of contract - In P.J. Narayan v. Union of India and Ors., 2003 Indlaw SC 1627, it was held that in absence of any statute to that effect, insurance Company should not be forced by Courts to take on liabilities - By contract of insurance, insurer had made itself liable to reimburse insured if during period of insurance any employee in his immediate service sustained personal injury by accident or disease arising out of and in course of employment by insured in business - Hence, appellant was not liable for interest, however, employer should be liable to pay amount of interest to claimant - Appeal allowed.
