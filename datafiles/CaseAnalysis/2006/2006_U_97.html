Anjan Kumar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 47; (2006) 3 SCC 257; AIR 2006 SC 1177; 2006 (5) AWC 4885; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 538; 2006 (1) CTC 558; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 218; JT 2006 (2) SC 385; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 2006(2) SCALE 327; 2006 (2) SCJ 472; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 209; 2006 (2) SLR 487

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 15(2), 15(4), 164), 17, 21, 46 - Whether offshoot of tribal woman married to non-tribal husband could claim status of Scheduled Tribe and on basis of which Scheduled Tribe certificate could be given? - Held, condition precedent for granting tribe certificate being that one must suffer disabilities wherefrom one belongs - Offshoots of wedlock of a tribal woman married to a non-tribal husband - Forward Class (Kayastha in present case) cannot claim Scheduled Tribe status reason of being such offshoot was brought up in atmosphere of Forward Class and he is not subjected to any disability - Appeal dismissed.
