 M. Nagaraj and Others v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Y.K. Sabharwal, K.G. Balakrishnan, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 722; (2006) 8 SCC 212; AIR 2007 SC 71; 2006 (4) AWC 4054; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 936; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 147; JT 2006 (9) SC 191; 2006(10) SCALE 301; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 336; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 129

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Affirmative Action, Freedom Of Press

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 16(4A), 17, 19, 25, 32, 335, 356, 368, 38, 46 - Constitution (Eighty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 2001 - Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995 - Constitution (Eighty-First Amendment) Act, 2000 - Constitution (Eighty-Second Amendment) Act, 2000 - Width and amplitude of right to equal opportunity in public employment, in context of reservation - Whether by virtue of impugned constitutional amendments, power of Parliament is so enlarged so as to obliterate any or all of constitutional limitations and requirements? - Constitution must be construed in wide and liberal manner so that constitutional provision does not get fossilized but remains flexible enough to meet newly emerging problems and challenges- Part 3 does not confer fundamental rights but confirms their existence and give them protection - Amendment should not destroy constitutional identity - In the field of reservation in public employment, limit on Govt.'s power is placed by Art. 335 which provides to maintain efficiency in service - 'Backwardness' and 'inadequacy' of representation are two limits on its application - Held, upheld constitutional validity of Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995, Constitution (Eighty-First Amendment) Act, 2000, Constitution (Eighty-Second Amendment) Act, 2000 and Constitution (Eighty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 2001 - Order accordingly.
