Prem Singh and Others v Birbal and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 191; (2006) 5 SCC 353; AIR 2006 SC 3608; 2006 (64) ALR 162; 2006 (3) AWC 2512; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 332; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 270; JT 2006 (5) SC 311; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 37; 2007 (1) MPLJ 1; 2006 (143) PLR 647; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 381; 2006 (101) RD 260; 2006 (3) RLW 2522; 2006(5) SCALE 191; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 59, s. 27 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 31 - Registration Act, 1908 - Whether provision of art.59 would be attracted in a suit filed for setting aside a Sale deed?- Held, if a deed was executed by plaintiff when he was a minor and it was void he could either file suit within 12 years of deed or within 3 years of attaining majority - Plaintiff did not either sue within 12 years of deed or within 3 years of attaining majority - Suit was rightly held to be barred by limitation by trial court - Appellate Court and HC were not right in law in holding that suit was not barred by limitation - Appeal allowed.
