Union of India and Another v Manik Lal Banerjee

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 346; (2006) 9 SCC 643; AIR 2006 SC 2844; 2006 (4) AWC 3255; 2006 (4) ESC 429; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 190; JT 2006 (6) SC 590; 2006(7) SCALE 386; 2006 (7) SCJ 246; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 66; 2006 (5) Supreme 813

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, ss. 2(a)(i), 2(e), 2(f) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14 - Indian Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 4 - Railway Services (Pension) Rules, 1993, rr. 15(4)(ii), 70 - Respondent was paid 16 months emoluments comprising basic salary and 20% dearness allowance towards Death-Cum-Retirement Gratuity - Another person, Pritam Singh, similarly situated, claimed and obtained benefits of gratuity in terms of provisions contained in 1972 Act in terms whereof the element of dearness allowance was calculated at rate of 125% of basic salary - Appellant opined that respondent was not governed by provisions of 1972 Act but by provisions of 1993 Rules - HC held that 1993 Rules do not make an employee of Railways disentitled to benefit of gratuity under 1972 Act - Appeal against - It is well-settled that a decision is an authority for what it decides and not what can logically be deduced therefrom - Decision in Pritam Singh's case having indisputably not taken into consideration, the exclusionary clause contained in s. 2(e) of the 1972 Act cannot be held to be an authority for the proposition that despite the provisions of the 1993 Rules, the 1972 Act would apply in the case of the railway servants - Held, interpretation clause contained in s. 2(e) takes out from purview of said Act, a person who holds inter alia post under Central Govt. and whose terms and conditions of service are governed by an Act or Rules providing for payment of gratuity - If a decision has been rendered without taking into account statutory provision, same cannot be considered to be a binding precedent - Appeal allowed.
