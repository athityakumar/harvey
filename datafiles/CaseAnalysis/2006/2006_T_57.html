Col. (Retd.) B.J. Akkara v The Govt. Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1025; (2006) 11 SCC 709; 2007 (207) E.L.T. 3; JT 2006 (9) SC 125; 2006(10) SCALE 206; 2008 (11) S.T.R. 305; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 58; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 227

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Defence Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 14 - Retired Employees - Revised Scale - Pensionary Benefits - Entitlement - Circular was issued by respondent stepped up pension to 50% of the minimum pay in revised scale of pay introduced w.e.f from 1-1-1996 of rank held by such pensioner, where his pension was less - Further another circular was issued to recalculate pension by excluding NPA from basic pay and recovery of excess payment made - Petitioners filed writ petitions in different HC, which were transferred to SC - Hence, instant transfer petitions - Whether circular issued for clarification by respondent was justifiable - Held, Whenever the government revises the pay scales, a fitment exercise takes place as per the principle of fitment (formula) provided in the rules governing the revision of pay so that the 'basic pay' in the old scale is converted into a 'basic pay' in the revised pay scale - In instant case circular dt. 7-6-1999 intended by way of benefit to all pensioners, was minimum pension in 1996 revised scale pay - NPA has no relevance for stepping up pension under circular dt 7-6-1999 - Therefore, it became necessary to issue clarification that it was impermissible to again add NPA to minimum pay in revised pay scale for purpose of stepping up pension - Further, petitioners retired with same rank, they were not of 'same class' or 'homogeneous group' - Employer could validly fix cut-off date for introducing any new pension/retirement scheme or for discontinuance of any existing scheme - Such relief, restraining recovery back of excess payment, was granted by courts not because of any right in employees, but in equity, in exercise of judicial discretion, to relieve employees, from hardship that will be caused if recovery was implemented - Petitions dismissed.
