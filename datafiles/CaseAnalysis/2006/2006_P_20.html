Sheila B. Das v P.R. Sugasree

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 52; (2006) 3 SCC 62; AIR 2006 SC 1343; 2006 (63) ALR 509; 2006 (2) AWC 1245; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 524; 2006 (1) JCC 407; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 210; JT 2006 (2) SC 482; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 294; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 758; 2006(2) SCALE 388; 2006 (3) SCJ 547; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 342; 2006 (2) UC 794

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Women & Children - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, ss. 17(3), 25, 7 - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, s. 6 - Special Marriage Act, 1954 - Custody of a minor child - HC granted custody to father - Appeal against - Held, from discussion with minor, it has been able to gather that though she has no animosity as such towards her mother, she would prefer to be with father with whom she felt more comfortable - Child appears to be happy in respondent's company and has also been doing consistently well in school - Respondent appears to be financially stable and is not also disqualified in any way from being guardian of minor child - Appeal disposed of.
