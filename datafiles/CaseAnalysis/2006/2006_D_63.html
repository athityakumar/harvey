Dildar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 926; (2006) 10 SCC 531; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 129; AIR 2006 SC 3084; 2006 CRLJ 3914; 2006 (3) JCC 1339; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 192; 2006(8) SCALE 370; 2006 (7) SCJ 893

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.376, 506 - Rape - Conviction - Delay in lodging of FIR - Sustainability - Appellant was drawing teacher in Govt. school where prosecutrix was studying in class VIII - When prosecutrix had gone to school for coaching in science practical, appellant took her to class room and raped her - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss.376, 506 of IPC - HC in Appeal upheld conviction of appellant - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether concurrent findings of fact recorded by Trial Court and HC could be interfered with - Held, fact could not be overlooked that in sexual offences delay in lodging of FIR could be due to variety of reasons particularly reluctance of prosecutrix or her family members to go to police and complain about incident which concerned reputation of prosecutrix and honour of her family - Thus, delay in lodging FIR could not be used as ritualistic formula for doubting prosecution cases and discarding same - Prosecutrix was minor below age of 16 years - Prosecutrix did not report incident to anyone either on first occasion or on second and when stage was reached when she could not keep it secret since her mother compelled her to disclose true facts - Medical evidence did not disclose any feature which would conclusively prove commission of rape - However, medical examination after about three months of occurrence would hardly furnish any corroboration to case of prosecutrix - Further defence of appellant was vague - Therefore, no fault was found with judgment of HC in reaching conclusion that prosecutrix was speaking truth and appellant was guilty of offences alleged against him - Hence, no reason was found to interfere with concurrent findings of fact recorded by trial Court and HC - Appeal dismissed.
