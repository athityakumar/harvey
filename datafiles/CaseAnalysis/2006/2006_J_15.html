Managing Director, Orix Auto Finance (India) Limited v Jagmander Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 42; (2006) 2 SCC 598; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 33; 2006 (62) ALR 783; 2006 (2) AWC 1174; 2006 (2) BC 108; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 543; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 45; 2006 (1) CTC 670; 2006 (127) DLT 278; 2006 (1) JCC 373; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 72; JT 2006 (2) SC 344; 2006 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 680; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 756B; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 386; 2006(2) SCALE 297; 2006 (2) SCJ 614; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 169

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Financier

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 39, rr. 1, 3, s. 115 - Hire Purchase Agreement - Total financed amount of Rs. 9, 24, 000/- in 33 monthly installments of Rs. 28, 000/- - Each Default in making payment of monthly installments - Repossession of truck by financer - Civil Judge directed appellant to release vehicle subject to deposit of balance of installments along with interest amounting to Rs. 1, 61, 504/- - Appeal against - Contention that trial Court did not take note of fact that according to appellant arrears were much higher than defaulted installments - Held, directed respondent no 1-hirer to pay appellant a sum of Rs. 1, 50, 000/- In addition to amount already deposited - Appeal partly allowed.
