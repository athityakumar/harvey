J. Srinivasa Rao v Govt. Of Andhra Pradesh And Another.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1498; (2006) 12 SCC 607; 2006(13) SCALE 27; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963, M.P. Motor Vehicles Taxation Act

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963, s. 3 - Levy of tax on Motor Vehicles - State enacted the Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1963 to consolidate and amend the law relating to levy of a tax on motor vehicles in the State - In the Schedule appended to the Act, the rate of tax for Maxi Cab permitted to carry more than six passengers but not more than 12 passengers was prescribed at Rs. 1,000/- - Vide amendement dt. 27.04.1997, the rate of tax was modified - Appellant filed writ petition questioning the validity of said notification - Said petition was dismissed by the HC holding that in all other entries of the Schedule, tax was levied on seat basis, harmonious reading of the provisions thereof would lead to the conclusion that rate of tax prescribed in the Schedule of Act is valid in law - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, Section 3 of the Act provides for a charging section stating that the tax shall be levied on every motor vehicle used or kept for use in the State at the rates specified in the First Schedule - The levy of tax, therefore, was on the motor vehicles - Its rates may vary having regard to the use or category of the vehicle - Maxi Cabs although come within the purview of the definition of 'contract carriage', but the rate of tax therefor has differently been provided for in the statute itself - When the rate of tax is provided under a statute, construction thereof applying the principles of noscitur a sociis and ejusdem generis would not apply - The rate of tax was fixed at Rs. 1,000/- - The tax was not to be calculated on passenger basis - Moreover, once the rate of tax was fixed and the same had been realized, any Notification enhancing the rate thereof cannot be permitted to transgress the statutory limits provided for in the proviso appended to s. 3 of the Act - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
