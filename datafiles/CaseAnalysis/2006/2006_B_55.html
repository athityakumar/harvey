Musaraf Hossain Khan v Bhagheeratha Engineering Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 148; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 98; (2006) 3 SCC 658; AIR 2006 SC 1288; 2006 (2) AWC 1749; 2006 (2) BC 515; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 98; 2006 CRLJ 1683; 2006 (2) CTC 57; 2006 (2) JCC 113; JT 2006 (3) SC 80; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 189; 2006 (143) PLR 213; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 137; 2006 (3) RLW 1789; 2006(2) SCALE 592; 2006 (3) SCJ 604; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 595; 2006 (1) UC 424

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 177, 178, 219 and 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Several cheques of diverse sums issued by respondent had been dishonoured - Chief Judicial Magistrate took cognizance of offence - Respondents filed a Writ Petition in High Court - HC granted interim stay - Whether HC had jurisdiction in matter? - HC should not ordinarily interfere with an order taking cognizance passed by a competent Court of law except in a proper case - Only such HC within whose jurisdiction order of Subordinate Court has been passed, would have jurisdiction to entertain an application u/art. 227 unless it is established that earlier cause of action arose within jurisdiction thereof - Perusal of complaint petition shows entire cause of action arose within jurisdiction of district courts and in that view of matter it is that Court which will have jurisdiction to take congnizance of offence - Even if s. 178, CrPC is attracted, court of Chief Judicial Magistrate will alone have jurisdiction in matter - Sending of cheques from Ernakulam or respondents having an office at that place did not form an integral part of 'cause of action' for which complaint petition was filed by appellant and cognizance of offence u/s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 was taken by Chief Judicial Magistrate - Object of provision of s. 138 is that for proper and smooth functioning of business transaction in particular, use of cheques as negotiable instruments would primarily depend upon integrity and honesty of parties - Held, for purpose of proving afore mentioned ingredients of offence u/s. 138 appellant was required to prove facts constituting cause of action none of which arose within jurisdiction of HC - Appeal allowed.
