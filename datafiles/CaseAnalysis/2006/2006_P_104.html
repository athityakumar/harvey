Jasbir Singh v Punjab and Sind Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 889; (2007) 1 SCC 566; 2006 (7) AWC 7617; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 884; 2006(11) SCALE 204; 2007 (1) SLR 569; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 409 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11-A - Appellant appointed as peon in respondent Bank - Embezzlement of fund - Departmental proceeding - Criminal case was also initiated u/s. 409/201 of IPC - Acquitted in criminal case - Purported confession which he had allegedly made was found to have been done under undue coercion - Despite acquittal, departmental proceedings continued - Ex parte report that charges against appellant proved - High Court dismissed appeal - Held, judgments of both Civil Court and Criminal Court established that appellant was treated very unfairly and unreasonably - For all intent and purport, a criminal case was foisted upon him - A confession, according to learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, was extracted from him by bank officers in a very cruel manner - It is not a case where back wages should be denied - Respondent Bank has tried to proceed against appellant both in a civil proceedings as well as in a criminal proceedings and at both independent forums, it failed - In a case of this nature HC should have applied its mind to fact of matter with reference to materials brought on records, HC failed to do so - Impugned orders and judgments cannot be sustained and are set aside - Appellant is directed to be reinstated with back wages, continuity of service and other consequential benefits - Appeal allowed.
