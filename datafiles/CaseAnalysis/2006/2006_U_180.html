Union of India v Messrs Millenium Mumbai Broadcast Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 795; (2006) 10 SCC 510; AIR 2006 SC 2751; 2006 (3) AWC 2128; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 694; 2006(5) SCALE 44; 2006 (6) SCJ 555; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Telecom; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Condition Precedent

Summary: Telecom - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 14 - Whether Tribunal exceeded in its power in directing that respondents herein are entitled to migrate from fixed licence fee regime to revenue sharing? - How cl. 9 of the agreement can be given effect to would depend on the construction of terms and conditions of the licence - By reason of cl. 1.2, as contained in the Sch. C appended thereto, the licensor had been conferred with a power to revoke the licence as also encash and forfeit the bank guarantee without any notice - The expression 'and' occurring in between the words 'right to revoke the licence' and encash and 'forfeit the bank guarantee' must be read as two separate clauses - The same cannot be read as conjunctive in view of the fact that it is admitted that the revocation of licence is not permissible without service of 30 days' notice - What was, therefore, permissible is that the licensor in terms of the said condition of licence may encash and forfeit the bank guarantee furnished by the licensee without giving any notice - The same would evidently mean that for the purpose of encashment and forfeiture of the bank guarantee, no separate notice is required to be given in the event any cause of action arises therefore - Held, Tribunal proceeded on basis that licence having not been validly revoked and same continued to be operative - Further, Tribunal, in terms of s. 14 of Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act has a wide power and hence its direction to treat respondent to be entitled to benefit of change in policy decision as regard payment of different mode of licence as other existing licence holders is well within law - Appeal dismissed.
