Kallu @ Masih and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 4; (2006) 10 SCC 313; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 546; AIR 2006 SC 831; 2006 CRLJ 799; 2006 (1) JCC 163; JT 2006 (2) SC 631; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 427; 2006 (2) RLW 1146; 2006(1) SCALE 100; 2006 (3) SCJ 141; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross-Complaint

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 307, 323, 324, 326 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - CrPC, 1973, s. 378(3) - Acquittal by trial Court - Appeal by state partly allowed by HC and convicted appellants - Appeal against conviction - Held, trial court unreasonably disbelieved evidence of six eye-witnesses on insufficient grounds - HC has also assigned reasons for interfering with acquittal - HC interfered rightly - Mere fact that several accused were acquitted and only four are convicted, does not enable four who are found guilty to contend that s.149 is inapplicable - Appeal dismissed.
