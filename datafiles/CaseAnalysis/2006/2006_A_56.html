State of Karnataka and Another v All India Manufacturers Organisation and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 554; (2006) 4 SCC 683; AIR 2006 SC 1846; JT 2006 (11) SC 337; 2006 (4) KarLJ 369; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 596; 2006(4) SCALE 398; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Frivolous

Summary: Corporate - Commercial & Contract - Companies Act, 1956 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 28(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 340 and 195 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 300A and 12 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Bangalore-Mysore Infrastructure Corridor Project - Land acquisition for - (A) whether there was fraud and misrepresentation on part of Nandi Enterprises? - Mala fides, if any, appears to be on part of state Govt. for political reasons - State Govt. had specifically been made aware of CAA and fact that members of Consortium had transferred their rights to Nandi - Change of mind on part of state Govt. came about co-incidentally or otherwise with a change of Govt. - Held, Nandi submitted a draft of FWA to state Govt. which was considered by core committee - FWA was approved by state Govt. and came to be signed - Thus, plea of fraud and misrepresentation was clearly an after thought - (B) whether dispute between State and Nandi is barred by principle of resjudicata, constructive resjudicata or estoppel arising from judgment and proceedings in Somashekar Reddy's case? - Main purpose of doctrine is that once a matter has been determined in a former proceeding, it should not be open to parties to re-agitate matter again and again - S. 11 of CPC, 1908 recognises this principle and forbids a court from trying any suit or issue, which is res judicata - As long as litigation is bona fide, a judgment in a previous PIL would be a judgment in rent and bars any member of public from coming forward before court and raising any connected issue - In principle that Somashekar Reddy, as a PIL, could bar present litigation - Explanations III and IV to s. 11 CPC, 1908 - Issue of excess land was specifically raised in Somashekar Reddy's case - Judgment also contains copious references to issue of land - Further held, res judicata applies - (C) Excess land - Permitting argument on excess land to be heard again to scuttle a project of this magnitude for public benefit would encourage dishonest politically motivated litigation and permit - Neither interim report nor final report of expert committee identified excess land but in fact, left it for KIAD Board - Further held, there was no question of land being acquired for a purpose other than a public purpose or there being any contravention of art. 300A - (D) Whether relief granted was wholly beyond jurisdiction of High Court u/art. 226? - HC said that there is no scope for raising frivolous and mala fide objections for ulterior purposes - When state's acts of omission or commission are tainted with extreme arbitrariness and with mala fides, it is certainly subject to interference by Constitutional Courts in this country - Further held, that no exception could be taken to relief granted in judgment of HC impugned - All that HC has done is to reaffirm and require state Govt. and its instrumentalities, as "State" under Constitution, to act without arbitrariness and mala fides, especially in matter of land acquisition - (E) Writ Petition by landowners - Judge took view that acquisition of 60% of land by state Govt, was valid, while acquisition of remaining 40% was invalid and to that extent acquisition was quashed - State Govt, KIAD Board and Nandi filed appeal - (1) whether there was non-service of notice? - Further held, that unable to accept contention that notices were not served on appellants as required u/s. 28(1) of KTAD Act - (2) whether notice was vague? - Further held, that purpose indicated in notifications is sufficiently precise and is not affected by vice of vagueness as alleged - (3) whether land was acquired for public purpose? - Project is an integrated infrastructure development project and not merely a highway project - Various changes brought about to KIAD Act, also reflect intention of state's Legislature to provide for land acquisition for project - Appeals dismissed.
