M. Gurudas and Others v Rasaranjan and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1003; (2006) 8 SCC 367; AIR 2006 SC 3275; 2006 (65) ALR 331; 2006 (4) AWC 3907; 2006 (6) KarLJ 337; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 2006 (144) PLR 724; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 341; 2006 (101) RD 822; 2006(9) SCALE 275; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 103

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: Adopted Daughter

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 8 - Adoption - (A) Whether adoption in question is valid? - Held, to prove valid adoption actual giving and taking ceremony to be established - Performance of 'datta homam' was imperative - Prima facie Nirmala, mother of appellants herein, was not validly adopted daughter of Obalappa - In that case she would inherit only property which fell to share of Kadarappa, her natural father, on partition - As daughter of Kadarappa she can claim interest only in his share - Exactly would be Nirmala's share to be found by Trial Court - (B) Whether injunction is grantable in the instant case? - Held, while considering an application for injunction courts have to consider whether party seeking it has made out a prima facie case in its favour, whether balance of convenience lies in its favour and if plaintiffs might suffer irreparable injury if prayer for injunction is refused - Contention of plaintiffs must be bona fide - Question sought to be tried must be a serious question and not only on a mere triable issue - (C) Whether plaintiffs have prevaricated their stand from stage to stage? - Held,courts below have not applied their mind as regards balance of convenience and irreparable injury which may be suffered by appellants - What if plaintiffs' suit is to be dismissed or if their share is found only to be l/64th? - Prima facie their share is not more than l/8th in suit properties - Appeals partly allowed.
