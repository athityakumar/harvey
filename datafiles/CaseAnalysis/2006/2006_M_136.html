Mayuram Subramanian Srinivasan and Others v C.B.I

Supreme Court of India

16 June 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 803; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 83; (2006) 5 SCC 752; AIR 2006 SC 2449; 2006 CRLJ 3285; 2006 (2) JCC 1007; JT 2006 (6) SC 1; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 260; 2006(6) SCALE 483; 2006 (7) SCJ 23; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arijit Pasayat, J

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 21 r. 13A - Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transaction in Securities) Act 1992, ss. 10, 9(4) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, - Bail bond - Substantive sentence - Exemption from surrender - Special Court suspended substantive sentence passed against accused for that purpose each of accused executed fresh RR Bond - Time was granted for execution of bond - Appellant stated that Rules had no application to instant case, as there was special provision u/s. 9(4) of the Act authorizing Court to regulate its procedure, adopt such procedure as it may deem fit consistent with principles of natural justice and in exercise of that power operation of sentence was suspended - Appellant was not surrendered - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether accused who preferred criminal appeal should surrender or seek exemption from surrender - Held, Criminal Appeal could not be posted unless proof of surrender was furnished by appellant who was convicted - Requirements of O. 21 r. 13A of the Rules was mandatory and had complied with except when order was passed for exemption from surrendering - There was no application made for exemption from surrendering - Special Court had granted protection for some periods by suspending sentence - It was discretion of Court whether to extend that protection - But that would be subject to provisions of O. 21 r. 13A of the Rules - It was directed that appeals should be posted only after appellants surrender and proof of surrender was filed - Order accordingly.

ALTAMAS KABIR, J.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, r. 13A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - Statutory Provision - Exemption from surrender - Whether accused who preferred criminal appeal should surrender or seek exemption from surrender - Held, statutory provisions of s. 389 (3) CrPC had overriding effect over the Rules and hence once bail was granted to convicted person by trial court, Court could not insist that he should surrender to sentence in terms of r. 13A of the Rules before his appeal could be registered - S. 389(3) of CrPC empowered trial Court to release convicted person on bail for such period as will afford him sufficient time to present appeal and obtain orders of appellate Court u/s. 389 (1) of CrPC and it was only for such period that sentence of imprisonment should be deemed to be suspended - Provisions of s. 389 of CrPC and that of the Rules were independent provisions and would be considered on their own standing - Appeals could be posted only after appellants surrender and proof of surrender was filed - Order accordingly
