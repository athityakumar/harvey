State Of Uttaranchal v Rajesh Kumar Gupta

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1464; (2007) 1 SCC 355; 2006 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 178; JT 2006 (10) SC 8; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 974; 2007 (1) RLW 774; 2006(11) SCALE 391; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8, 37 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Rules, 1985, Sch. I - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, Sch. G, H - Bail - Entitlement - Respondent/Ayurvedic Doctor allegedly issued an advertisement in various news papers claiming that the medicines prepared by him would cure epilepsy - Police raided respondent's clinic and recovered unlabelled tablets containing psychotropic substances making the unsuspecting patients addicted to the drugs - Respondent was arrested and was put into jail - Respondent filed an application for bail, which was dismissed by the Trial Court, however, allowed by the HC - Hence the present appeal by appellant/State - Whether the respondent entitled to get bail - Held, medicines seized from respondent's clinic would come within the purview of Schedules G and H of the Drugs and Cosmetics Act - Further, seized medicines were mentioned in Entries 69 and 36 of the 1985 Act respectively, whereas none of them found place in the Schedule I appended to the 1985 Rules - If the seized drugs do not find place in Schedule I appended to the Rules, the provisions of s. 8 of the 1985 Act containing penal clause would have no application - In view of the fact that all the drugs being allopathic drugs mentioned in Schedules G and H of the Drugs and Cosmetics Act indisputably are used for medicinal purposes - Once the drugs are said to be used for medicinal purposes, it cannot be denied that they are acknowledged to be the drugs which would come within the purview of description of the expression 'medicinal purposes' - Further, s. 37 of the 1985 Act, which relates to the offences in relation to commercial quantity must be construed in a pragmatic manner and it cannot be construed in such a way so as to negate the right of party to obtain bail which is otherwise a valuable right for all practical purposes - Thus, s. 37 of the 1985 Act would prima facie has no application in view of the exception contained in s. 8 thereof r/w the Rules - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
