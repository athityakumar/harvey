Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Munimahesh Patel

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1320; (2006) 7 SCC 655; 2006 (65) ALR 316; 2006 (4) AWC 3921; 2007 (1) CCC 314; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 73; 2006(9) SCALE 167; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 30; 2006 (3) UC 2021

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: False Claim, Accident Policy

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Janata Personal Accident Policy - Insured died on account of an accident - Complaint filed alleging deficiency in service on part of appellant - Dismissed - On appeal, commission held that though there may have been some information given which has no relation with actual state of affairs, yet factum of accident resulting in death and policy was not in dispute and, therefore, claim of complainant was to be allowed - Present appeal - Whether issues involving factual questions can be adjudicated upon by consumer forums? - Nature of proceedings before commission are essentially in summary nature - Factual position was required to be established by documents - Commission was required to examine whether in view of disputed facts it would exercise jurisdiction - State Commission was right in its view that complex factual position requires that matter should be examined by an appropriate court of law and not by commission - Held, commission having accepted that there was wrong declaration of nature of occupation of person insured, should not have granted relief in manner done - Appeal allowed.
