Chairman And M.D., Bharat Pet. Corpn. Limited And Others v T.K. Raju

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1461; (2006) 3 SCC 143; AIR 2006 SC 3504; 2006 (5) AWC 5132; JT 2006 (2) SC 624; 2006(2) SCALE 553; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 519; 2006 (3) SLR 220

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Management Staff Conduct, Discipline and Appeal Rules

Summary: Service - Conduct, Discipline and Appeal Rules for the Management Staff, rr. 4 and 22 of Part II r/w. Clauses 4, 6, 20, 22, 31 and 37 of Rule A of Part III - Dismissal from service - Respondent was a Senior Sales Officer (LPG) in the Sales Department of the appellants - Appellants received complaints against the respondent alleging financial irregularities on the part of the respondent - Charges were framed against the respondent in terms of rr. 4 and 22 of Rules - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against the respondent and he was found guilty - Punishment of dismissal from service was imposed - Appeal filed against said order was dismissed - On appeal, HC held that the quantum of punishment is disproportionate to the charges of misconduct levelled against him and as such remitted the matter back to the appellate authority for imposing appropriate punishment - DB confirmed the finding of guilt and remitted the matter to the Appellate Authority to take decision to impose penalty except the penalty of dismissal or removal from service - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the DB could be upheld? - Held, respondent admittedly was not only charged under clause (4) of Part III of the Rules, he was also charged for various other misconducts enumerated in different clauses of Part II thereof - HC, therefore, was not justified in proceeding with the matter on the premise that some of the charges against the respondent had been framed only in terms of clause (4) of Part II of the Rules - It is also not correct that 2 of the 8 charges have not been found to be proved - The charges levelled against the respondent must be considered on a holistic basis - By reason of such an action, the respondent had put the company in an embarrassing sitution - It might have lost its image - Hence, there was reason for the appellants to believe that by such an action on the part of the respondent the appellant's image has been tarnished - It cannot be said that the quantum of punishment was wholly disproportionate to the charges levelled against the respondent - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
