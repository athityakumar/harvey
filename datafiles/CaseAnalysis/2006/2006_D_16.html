H.P. Pyarejan v Dasappa(Dead) By L.Rs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 36; (2006) 2 SCC 496; AIR 2006 SC 1144; 2006 (62) ALR 788; 2006 (2) AWC 1134; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 668; 2006 (2) CTC 778; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 202; JT 2006 (2) SC 228; 2006 (2) KarLJ 239; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 646; 2006(2) SCALE 241; 2006 (3) SCJ 107; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - CPC, 1908, s. 100 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 16(1)(c), 16(c) - Suit for specific performance - Trial Court and First Appellate Court held that plaintiff had failed to establish that he was ready and willing to perform his part of contract - HC reversed finding - Appeal against - Held, no question was even formulated by HC regarding correctness or otherwise of findings of facts recorded by Trial Court - Not discussed as to in what way requirement of s. 16(c) regarding proof of readiness and willingness of plaintiff to perform his part of contract was fulfilled - Appeal allowed.
