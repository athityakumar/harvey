M.D. Bhadra Shahakari S.K. Niyamita v President, Chitradurga Mazdoor Sangh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 876; (2006) 8 SCC 552; 2006 (7) AWC 7619; 2006(10) SCALE 614; 2007 (1) SLR 583; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 212

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Inaction of Management and implementation of settlement - Petition filed by District Mazdoor Sangh calling in question inaction of Management in implementing settlement produced and for a consequent direction to Management to implement said settlement - Petition allowed - Appeal filed by Managing Director, Bhadra Shahakari SK Niyamita against President, Mazdoor Sangh and Ors against judgment of HC - (A) Maintainability of said Writ Petition? - Held, Court have Petition filed by respondent-Union against co-operative sugar factory is not maintainable - Therefore answered issue of maintainability of said Petition in favour of appellant - Management - (B) Whether it would be possible for appellant to pay back wages to respondent-workmen and extent thereof? - Considering critical financial situation of appellant sugar factory, direction issued for payment of 10% back wages which comes to Rs. 9.52 lacs if now ordered would meet ends of justice - Therefore, in interest of justice and in interest of workers, direction issued to appellant-Management to pay a sum of Rs. 9.52 lacs by way of back wages - Further Court held, said amount shall be distributed among 51 workmen as per their dues - Since reinstatement has been ordered by HC, workers will not be disturbed except in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
