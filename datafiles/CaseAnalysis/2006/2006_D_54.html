Dharam & Others v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 929; (2007) 15 SCC 241; AIR 2007 SC 397; 2007 CRLJ 791; JT 2007 (1) SC 299; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1028; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1057; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 291; 2006(13) SCALE 280; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 323 and 324/149 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether or not the appellants had assaulted the deceased and his party in exercise of the right of private defence? - Held, right of private defence is a defensive right - It is neither a right of aggression nor of reprisal - There is no right of private defence where there is no apprehension of danger - Burden of establishing the plea of self defence is on the accused but it is not as onerous as the one that lies on the prosecution - Being members of the aggressors' party none of the appellants can claim right of self-defence - Appellants have failed to establish that they were exercising right of private defence - Having regard to the nature of the injuries sustained by both the closely related parties, fatal injury was not inflicted with the intention to cause death or an injury likely to cause death of the deceased - Offence committed by appellants would fall within the ambit of s. 304 Part-II - Sentence of rigorous imprisonment for seven years - Appeal partly allowed.
