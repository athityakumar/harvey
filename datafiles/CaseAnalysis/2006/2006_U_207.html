Hasham Abbas Sayyad v Usman Abbas Sayyad & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 961; (2007) 2 SCC 355; AIR 2007 SC 1077; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 59; 2007 (66) ALR 637; 2007 (2) AWC 1426; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 324; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 15; JT 2007 (1) SC 616; 2007 (2) MPLJ 294; 2006(14) SCALE 131; 2006 (Supp) RD 563; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, s. 54 - Whether the property in suit could be put on auction sale without initiating a formal final decree proceeding? - Held, it is the final decree proceeding which would be executable in nature - Without drawing a final decree proceeding, the court could not have put the property on auction sale - Suit property is a residential house - Auction sale was wholly illegal - Appellant is directed to deposit a sum of Rs.18 lakhs - Appeal allowed.
