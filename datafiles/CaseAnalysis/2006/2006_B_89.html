Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Birendra Bhandari

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1393; (2006) 10 SCC 211; AIR 2006 SC 3220; 2006 (6) AWC 6041; 2006(9) SCALE 642; 2007 (2) SLR 450; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 690

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 33C (2) - Payment of arrears - Difference of salary - Implementation of recommendations - Succeed - Claim petition was filed by respondent before Labour Court u/s. 33C(2) of the Act, for payment of arrears relating to difference of salary, leave encashment, arrears of dearness allowance arising out of implementation of recommendation of 5th pay Commission - Labour Court accepted prayer and directed payment - Aggrieved, appellant filed petition before HC, contended that application in terms of s. 33C(2) was misconceived but HC dismissed petition and upholding order of Labour Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Labour Court was succeed - Held, relied on in State of U.P. and Anr. V. Brijpal Singh, 2005 Indlaw SC 1248, right or benefit accrued to workman as specific question of relief granted was confined only to reinstatement without stating anything more as to back wages - Hence, that relief should be deemed to have been denied, for what was claimed but not granted necessarily gets denied in judicial or quasi-judicial proceeding - Therefore, HC ought not to have presumed that award of Labour Court for grant of back wages was implied in relief of reinstatement or that award of reinstatement itself conferred right for claim of back wages - Appeal allowed.
