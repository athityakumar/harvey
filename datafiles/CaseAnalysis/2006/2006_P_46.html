State of Karnataka and Another v Pastor P. Raju

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 368; (2006) 6 SCC 728; AIR 2006 SC 2825; 2006 CRLJ 4045; JT 2006 (7) SC 165; 2006 (5) KarLJ 337; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 859; 2006(7) SCALE 503; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 167, s. 173, s. 190, s. 196(1-A), s. 436, s. 482 - IPC, 1860, ss. 153-B, 505(3) - Initiation of criminal proceedings against respondent u/s. 153-B IPC were quashed by HC - HC held that as investigating agency had not obtained previous sanction of Central Govt. or of State Govt. or of District Magistrate as required by s.196(1-A) CrPC, initiation of criminal proceedings against respondent is bad in law - Appeal against - Whether passing of an order of remand would amount to taking of cognizance of offence? - Held, neither any complaint had been filed nor any police report had been submitted nor any information had been given by any person other than police officer before Magistrate competent to take cognizance of offence - An order remanding an accused to judicial custody does not amount to taking cognizance of an offence - S.196(1-A) CrPC can have no application at all and HC clearly erred in quashing proceedings on ground that previous sanction of Central Govt. or of State Govt. or of District Magistrate had not been obtained - Appeal allowed.
