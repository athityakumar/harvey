Rajiv Ranjan Singh 'Lalan' and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 987; (2006) 6 SCC 613; 2006 (6) ALD(SC) 103; 2006 (205) CTR 53; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 89; JT 2006 (8) SC 328; 2006(8) SCALE 161; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 742; [2006] 156 TAXMAN 512

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Interest And Penalty, Political Party, Appeal To High Court

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Public Interest Litigation - Socio-Economic - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 19 - Public Interest Litigation filed by petitioners against respondents No.4 and 5 alleging they were involved in large-scale defalcation of public funds and falsification of accounts amounting to hundreds of crores of rupees in the Govt. Department - Proceedings were initiated against them and charge sheet filed under Prevention of Corruption Act - Whether such proceedings are sustainable against the respondents? - Held, no - Criminal litigation was exclusively between state and concerned respondents - No person can interfere by way of public interest litigation as it would hamper course of justice and prejudice the accused denying a fair trial - Petitioners could not produce any documents to show that the allegations made in the original petitions were true - Petitions dismissed.
