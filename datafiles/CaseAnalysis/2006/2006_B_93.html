Everest Industries Ltd. And Anr v Board Of Trustees For The Port Of Calcutta And Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1424; (2006) 10 SCC 327; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1865; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 733; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 467; 2006(9) SCALE 706; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, s. 56 - Lease deed - Renewal clause - Increased rate of rent - Maintainability - By indenture of lease dt. 15-3-1938, appellant was given lease plot of land for period of 30 years w.e.f. dt.1-2-1938 and it contained renewal clause but increased rate of rent which would not exceed 50% of rent for period immediately preceding - Another deed of lease was executed by respondents in favour of appellants for period of 10 years with effect from 1-2-1958 - By notification dt.19-9-1996, Rent Schedule of land, inter alia, was altered and notification was issued purported to be in terms of s. 56 of the Act - Appellant filed petition before HC and contended that reason of notification, quantum of rent had been increased by more than 52% and as they were required to pay huge amount by way of premium and security, same was violative of art. 14 of Constitution of India being arbitrary in nature, further contention of appellants was that notification was ultra vires provisions of the Act - Single Judge dismissed petition and held, there was no renewal clause in expired lease, it reasonably expected that after expiry of lease in question same would be renewed for further period upon reasonable increase in rent since on earlier occasion after expiry of earlier leases, expired lease was executed - Aggrieved by order, appeal was preferred by appellants before DB - DB exercised its discretionary jurisdiction in matter of grant of interim relief, relied on in Jamshed Hormusji Wadia v. Board of Trustees, Port of Mumbai & Anr., 2004 Indlaw SC 5, by order dt. 12-5-2005, while issuing notice, stayed only demand of premium, pursuant to direction appellant no. 1 had paid 80% of current rate, as directed by HC and it also furnished bank guarantees as directed, it was not in dispute that except for premium, all other directions of HC have since been complied - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB committed serious error in passing Judgment in so far as it failed to take into consideration that no premium was payable under Deed of Lease therefore, on basis of notification, which has no statutory backing, was per se illegal - Whether order of DB was succeed - Held, major part of directions issued by HC was complied with - Further, only payment which was required to be made by appellant was 50% of amount of premium and furnishing of Bank Guarantee in respect of rest 50% - Hence, respondents were held not to be entitled to entire increased rent or amount of premium, payment of amount deposited towards security in terms of notification dt.19-9-1996 as also demand, as contained in letter dt.13-7-2001 by respondent, should be subject to condition that in event respondents were found liable to get refund of any amount, same should carry interest @12% per annum - Appeal disposed of.
