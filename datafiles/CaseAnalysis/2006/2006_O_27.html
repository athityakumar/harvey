Oswal Woolen Mills Ltd. v Oswal Woolen Mills Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1507; (2006) 13 SCC 719; 2007 (1) AWC 294; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 283; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1449; 2006(5) SCALE 21; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 46, 49 - Imposition of surcharge - Legality - Appellant Mill availed electric connection for a load of 6664 KW from respondent Board by way of supply at 11 KV - Respondent, by Circular dated 21-1-1991, decided levy surcharge of 17% on consumers who availed load exceeding 5000KW - Respondent demanded surcharge from appellant - Respondent, by letter dated 13-5-1992, demanded appellant to show place of installation of 66 KV sub-station, failing which a penalty at 17% would be levied - Appellant challenged demand before Trial Court - Trial Court and Appellate Court held respondent Board could levy surcharge only with effect from 13-5-1992 - Whether HC was correct in holding that in view of fact matter relating to payment of surcharge was governed by circulars, which having not been superseded by notification dated 21-1-1991 and 3-5-1992 impugned demand was valid in law - Held, surcharge by way of additional rate or penalty could be levied only in terms of a tariff notification - Such a power, therefore, could be exercised by respondent Board only in exercise of its statutory power and not by reason of an executive power - In terms of a circular letter issued by respondent Board, therefore, neither any surcharge nor any penalty could be levied - In year 1991, circular letter dated 21-1-1991 was followed by tariff notification issued in terms of ss. 46 and 49 of Act - Subsequent circular letter dated 3-5-1991 was, however, not followed by any notification making tariff applicable with retrospective effect - Respondent Board, therefore, could levy surcharge only in terms of notification and not by reason of any circular letter - Impugned notification clearly stated that 17% surcharge should be leviable for all Arc furnace load consumers which were being given supply at 11 KV, HC clearly fell in error in arriving at finding that by reason of said notification, circular letters dated 21-1-1991 and 3-5-1991 were not superseded - Respondent Board being a statutory authority, its power to issue bills for consumption of electricity would be governed solely by tariff notification - HC, therefore, was clearly wrong in arriving at finding that earlier notifications dated 21-1-1991 and 3-5-1991 were not superseded - HC failed to pose unto itself correct question, namely, as to whether after issuance of tariff notification, Board could levy any surcharge at 17% on tariff on those consumers who did not have Arc furnace - HC, therefore, misdirected itself in law in passing impugned order and hence set aside - Appeal disposed of.
