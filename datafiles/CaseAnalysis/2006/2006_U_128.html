Steel Authority of India Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 525; (2006) 12 SCC 233; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 630; AIR 2006 SC 3229; 2006 (6) AWC 5574; 2006 (111) FLR 483; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 36; 2006(9) SCALE 597; 2006 (6) SLR 8; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1)(c) - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 58 - Demand of absorption as permanent employees - Held, 1970 Act not only provides for regulation of contract labour but also abolition thereof - Relationship of employer and employee is essentially a question of fact - Determination of said question would depend upon a large number of factors - Ordinarily, a writ court would not go into such a question - Neither Labour Court nor writ court could determine question as to whether contract labour should be abolished or not, same being within exclusive domain of Appropriate Govt. - Appeal allowed.
