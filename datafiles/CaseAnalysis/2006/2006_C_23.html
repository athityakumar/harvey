Rajesh Ranjan Yadav @ Pappu Yadav v Cbi Through Its Director

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 766; (2007) 1 SCC 70; 2007 CRLJ 304; 2007 (1) JCC 726; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 166; 2006(11) SCALE 551; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302, 34, 437(1)(i), 439 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Bail application rejected - Appeal against - Held, while it is true that art.21 is of great importance because it enshrines fundamental right to individual liberty, but at same time a balance has to be struck between right to individual liberty and interest of society - FIR itself shows that it is a triple murder case, and incident was committed in broad day light with sophisticated weapons - Wholly inappropriate to grant bail when not only investigation is over but even trial is partly over, and allegations against appellant are serious - Appeal dismissed.
