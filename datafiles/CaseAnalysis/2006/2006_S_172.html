V.C. Banaras Hindu University and Others v Shrikant

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 819; (2006) 1 SCC 42; AIR 2006 SC 2304; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 102; 2006 (7) AWC 6875; JT 2006 (11) SC 578; 2006(6) SCALE 66; 2006 (7) SCJ 396; 2006 (4) SLR 769; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 536; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2671

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 14, 21, 162, 311 - Banaras Hindu University Act,1915 - Leave recommended by HOD - Alleged misconduct and terminated - Validity - Respondent and his wife were employees of appellant/University - Respondents wife got Commonwealth Fellowship in United Kingdom and hence respondent and his wife submitted leave letter and forwarded with recommendations by Director/ HOD - Respondent handed over charge to 'X' and leave of respondent's wife was sanctioned - Respondent and his wife left for United Kingdom without express sanction of leave and without permission of appellant - Appellant issued show cause notice to respondent alleging misconduct and terminated respondent - After receiving office memo of termination respondent came back to India and submitted joining report and was not accepted by Appellant/University stating that his services were abandoned - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition in HC and HC had directed appellant to consider respondents representation sympathetically and passed stay orders - Appellant confirmed earlier orders of termination - Again respondent filed second writ petition and was dismissed by HC holding that respondent could avail alternative remedy by making representation to Executive Council of University - Respondent made representation to Executive Council but was not heard till 2 years and so filed contempt petition in HC and was partly allowed directing reinstatement without back wages - Aggrieved both parties filed instant cross appeals - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, matters related to appointment and disciplinary action were governed by statute/ordinance - Executive Council or Vice Chancellor or any other authority who were creatures of Statutes must act within four corners and were required to follow procedure laid down for initiation of disciplinary proceeding against employee - Executive Council had not made any amendment to existing ordinance relating to abandonment of service and had not been inserted in ordinance - Hence implementing new punishment of abandonment by Appellant/University on respondent was illegal and ultra vires - SC in DDA and Ors. v. Joginder S. Monga and Ors, 2003 Indlaw SC 1519 held that when conflict arises between statute and executive instruction indisputably statute would prevail over executive - Departmental proceedings could be initiated by Executive council alone and not by Vice chancellor - Statute and Ordinance postulate that order of termination could be passed only by Executive Council with two-third of Members present and voted in support thereto - Vice Chancellor could not have initiated any proceeding and imposed any punishment on respondent - Principles of natural justice were not followed by University - Appeals disposed of.
