Budh Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 635; (2006) 9 SCC 731; AIR 2006 SC 2500; 2006 CRLJ 2886; 2006 (2) JCC 921; JT 2006 (11) SC 503; 2006(5) SCALE 666; 2006 (5) SCJ 480; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 715

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302 and 307 - Appellants charged for offence u/ss. 148, 302 and 307/149 and u/s. 27 of Arms Act, 1959 and acquitted by Trial Court but convicted by High Court - Whether HC right in convicting appellant? - Ante mortem injuries found on dead body of deceased clearly belied statements made by prime witness 1, 2 and 3 - Prime witness 2 related to complainant - Appellant No. 6 was not carrying any arm - Land belonging to prime witness 2 and 3 were half kilometer away and they did not had any field near deceased - Held, order of HC set aside as prosecution of appellants could not be sustained on materials brought on record by prosecution - Appeal allowed.
