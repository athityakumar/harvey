State of Madhya Pradesh v Kedar Yadav

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 866; (2009) 17 SCC 280; 2006(12) SCALE 629; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 852

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - Trial court found respondent guilty and had imposed sentence of ten years rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs.1, 000/- - Single Judge while upholding the conviction reduced the sentence to the period already undergone which was about 1 year and three months - Appeal against - Held, it is sufficient to justify a conviction under s. 307 if there is present an intent coupled with some overt act in execution thereof - It is not essential that bodily injury capable of causing death should have been inflicted - An accused charged under s. 307 cannot be acquitted merely because the injuries inflicted on the victim were in the nature of a simple hurt - High Court was not justified in reducing the sentence to the period already undergone - Respondent shall undergo custodial sentence for three years - Appeal partly allowed.
