Chandrika Singh (Dead) By LRs. and Another v Sarjug Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1288; (2006) 12 SCC 49; 2007 (1) AWC 668; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 182; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 891; 2006(13) SCALE 408; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Married Women, Suit For Possession, In Lieu Of Maintenance, Hindu Women's Rights To Property Act, Hindu Married Women's Rights to Separate Residence and Maintenance Act, 1946

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Gift Deed - Acquisition of Land - Justifiability - 'X' executed gift deed in respect of certain properties in favor of appellants, which was acquired by Collector under 1894, Act - Compensation was paid to respondent - Appellants filed application u/s. 30 of 1894, Act , where reference was made - Reference Court passed judgment and decree - First Appellate Court reversed order of Reference Court - Respondents filed appeal before HC - HC held that gift deed was not valid and affirmed order of Reference Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC committed manifest error in interfering with finding of fact in respect of property in question - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, while exercising its Jurisdiction u/s. 100 of Code, HC was required to formulate a substantial question of law in relation to a finding of fact - HC exercise limited jurisdiction in that behalf - Ordinarily unless there exists sufficient and cogent reasons, findings of fact arrived at by Courts below were binding on HC - In instant case, once it was found that 'X' was possessed of land in question in lieu of her right of maintenance, s.14(1) of the 1956 Act would be clearly attracted as she became absolute owner of property and competent to execute deed of sale in year 1960 - Thus, HC committed manifest error in reversing judgment of Appellate Court - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
