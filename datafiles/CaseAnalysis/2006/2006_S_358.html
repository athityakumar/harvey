Rameshchandra Nandlal Parikh v State of Gujarat and Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 163; (2006) 1 SCC 732; AIR 2006 SC 915; 2006 (2) BC 178; 2006 CRLJ 964; 2006 (194) E.L.T. 140; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1365; 2006 (1) JCC 230; JT 2006 (1) SC 326; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 675; 2006 (2) RLW 1090; 2006(1) SCALE 254; 2006 (3) SCJ 242

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 1984, Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985

Summary: Criminal - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 18, 24 and 35A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154, 155, 157, 162, 169, 170 and 173 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 405, 406, 408, 409, 420, 467 and 471 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 156(3) - Petitioner was Managing Director of bank - Due to inability of bank to repay its depositors, there was public outcry, and public interest litigation was filed before High Court - Bank also filed a criminal complaint against petitioner and several others, for different offences under IPC, 1860 and s. 35 A of 1949 Act - Petitioner was arrested and remanded to police custody - Petitioner filed series of criminal miscellaneous applications to quash investigation being carried out against him - HC dismissed all applications - Whether HC was right in dismissing applications of petitioners? - In the facts of the present case, the complaints which were filed in respect of malfeasance and misfeasance within the jurisdiction of the Ahmedabad Police, were not in respect of the same cognizable offence or the same occurrence giving rise to one or more cognizable offences, nor were they alleged to have been committed in the course of the same transaction or the same occurrence as the ones alleged in First C.R. No. 67/2001 - Petitioner contended that several FIRs were filed in respect of same incident and claimed prohibition of FIR on ground that some other FIR had been filed against petitioner - Held, FIRs. which were under challenge were regarding independent and distinct offences - Other FIRs could not be prohibited on ground that some other FIR had been filed against petitioner in respect of other allegations made against petitioner - Petitioner cannot be entitled to be released on bail - Moreover, the HC was correctly cognizant of limitations while exercising its powers u/s. 482 of the CrPC, 1973 which should not in any event, be exercised lightly - Reading the impugned judgment of the HC as a whole, there is no scope for interference - The HC was justified in declining to exercise its powers u/s. 482 of the CrPC, 1973 and in refusing to interfere with the orders passed by the learned Chief Metropolitan Magistrate - Finally, considering the nature of the allegations involved and the facts and circumstances of the present case, this court also not inclined to exercise its extraordinary powers u/art. 136 of the Constitution to interfere - Order of HC justified - Petitions dismissed.
