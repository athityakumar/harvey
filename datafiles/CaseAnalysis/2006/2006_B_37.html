Sankar Dastidar v Banjula Dastidar and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 901; (2006) 13 SCC 470; AIR 2007 SC 514; 2007 (1) AWC 625; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 234; 2007 (147) PLR 232; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 413; 2006(13) SCALE 598; 2007 (3) SCJ 759; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963 - Suit by Respondent No. 1 against appellant inter alia for declaration of title in regard to residential house - What would be the period of limitation for raising a counter claim in respect of wrongful detention of goods? - Held, for specific movable property lost or acquired by theft, or dishonest misappropriation or conversion; knowledge as regards possession of the party shall be the starting point of limitation in terms of art. 68 - Time from which the period begins to run would be when the property is wrongfully taken or injured or when the detainer's possession becomes unlawful - Appeal allowed.
