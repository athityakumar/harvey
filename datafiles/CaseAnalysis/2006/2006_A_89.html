Jayrajbhai Jayantibhai Patel v Anilbhai Jayantibhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 481; (2006) 8 SCC 200; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 850; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 306; 2006(9) SCALE 147; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 884

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act, Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963, Gujarat Municipalities (President and Vice-President) Election Rules, 1964

Summary: [A.] Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963, s. 32 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Gujarat Municipalities (President and Vice-President) Election Rules, 1964, rr. 3, 4 - Election of Appellant as President of Anand Municipality has been set aside and respondent has been declared as elected President - Whether HC was justified in exercising its power of judicial review and setting aside election of appellant? - Held, detention of two councillors, a few minutes before election meeting was a relevant factor which ought to have been taken into account by Presiding Officer to decide whether to continue with election or to postpone it and call meeting on some other day in terms of r.10 - Failure to do so not only offends against procedural propriety, it makes his decision to go ahead with election meeting perverse and irrational, a facet of unreasonableness, warranting interference u/art.226 - HC not committed any error of law and/or jurisdiction in setting aside election of appellant - Order declaring respondent as President of Anand Municipality is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
