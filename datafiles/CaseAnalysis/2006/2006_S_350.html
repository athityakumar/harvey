Rohit Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1007; (2006) 12 SCC 734; AIR 2007 SC 10; 2006 (65) ALR 890; 2007 (1) AWC 1; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 383; JT 2006 (9) SC 145; 2007 (146) PLR 790; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 674; 2006(10) SCALE 258; 2006 (8) SCJ 129; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 278; 2007 (1) UC 193

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues, Bihar Land Reforms Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 80 and 105(1), O. 1 r. 10, O. 8 rr. 6 A and 6 E - Forest Act, 1927, s. 29 - Suit for declaration of the title and recovery of possession - Vested forest property - Counter claim - Maintainability - Whether counter-claim is maintainable in the instant case? - Held, claim cannot be made after issues have been framed and evidence is closed - After evidence was closed, there was no occasion for impleading interveners - Claim could be filed even after written statement is filed but that does not mean that a counter-claim can be raised after issues are framed and evidence is closed - Entertaining so-called counter-claim of Defendants No. 3 to 17 by Trial Court, after framing of issues for trial, was clearly illegal and without jurisdiction - 'Counter-claim' filed by Defendants No. 3 to 17 not maintainable - What was not treated by court as counter-claim cannot be expected to be answered by plaintiffs and co-defendants and no decree can be passed only on basis that no answer was filed to 'counter-claim' by other defendants - Trial Court clearly found that there was no evidence on side of Defendant No. 3 to 17 in support their claim of possession but still granted a decree on ground of alleged default of Defendant No. 1 and 2 and Defendants No. 18 to 20 in not filing an answer to counter-claim made by Defendants No. 3 to 17 - Whole procedure adopted and decree thus granted were unsustainable - Claim is to be directed against plaintiff and may only incidentally seek certain relief against co-defendants - By filing a counter-claim as against co-defendants litigation cannot be converted into sort of an interpleader suit - Their whole case was directed against Defendants 1 and 2 in suit and claim was put forward against State and were challenging claim of State that land involved was a notified forest in possession of State - Such a 'counter-claim' should not have been entertained by Trial Court - Decree on counter-claim vacated - Order of remand passed by HC, set aside - Appeal dismissed.
