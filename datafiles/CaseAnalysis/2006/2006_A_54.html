All India ITDC Workers Union and Others v ITDC and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 887; (2006) 10 SCC 66; AIR 2007 SC 301; 2006 (7) AWC 7525; JT 2006 (9) SC 585; 2006(11) SCALE 375; 2007 (1) SLR 787; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Jawaharlal Nehru University Act, 1966, Manipur University Act, 1980

Summary: Corporate - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India,1950, arts. 14,16,21,311 - Companies Act,1956, ss.154, 617 - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 - Disinvestment policy - Property sold - Challenged - As per disinvestment plan of Union Govt. respondent proposed to sell his hotel and created respondent no.4/company and through it being sold to respondent no.5 for meager amount - Appellants who were employees of respondent no.1 challenged on different grounds disputing selling of hotel and to implement Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) etc by filing writ petition in Allahabad HC and was transferred to SC - Another writ petition was filed by 8 persons in Delhi HC against respondents challenging proposed transfer of their services from respondent No.1 to respondent No.3 /newly incorporated company - Delhi HC had transferred that writ petition to SC - As both writ petitions were tried together - Whether grievance of petitioners was justified - Held, disinvestment was policy decision of Central Govt.- Decision of Central Govt. to disinvest property was policy decision and not in any manner contrary to law of land - SC in several judgments held that policy decision should be least interfered in judicial review and that Govt. employees had no absolute right u/arts. 14, 21 and 311 of the 1950 Act - Petitioners were not Govt. servants and were merely employees of respondent no.1 public sector undertaking - Scheme was formulated and it was evident that all employees were to be retained as stipulated in transfer documents on same terms and conditions of service for 1 year and were entitled to payment of gratuity and provident fund and so apprehension of petitioners was baseless and liable to be rejected - SC in Balco Employees Union (Regd.) vs. Union of India and Others, 2001 Indlaw SC 20366 held that employees of company registered under 1956 Act did not have any vested right to continue to enjoy status of employee of an instrumentality of State - Intending to promote scheme of disinvestment Govt. issued advertisement to outright sale of 6 hotels and long term lease for 2 hotels and property of respondent No.1 was demerged in name of the new company/respondent no.4 with approval of Company Law Board an all existing employees were shifted to new company which was also subsidiary company of respondent no.1 - Tenders were invited and accepted highest bidding - Respondent nos. 4 and 5 were under no obligation to float VRS scheme because VRS had to be given only when company retrenches its regular employees - Company was ready to continue with its employees with same terms and conditions and employees were unwilling to continue and hence could not compel management to introduce VRS scheme - Decision taken by Cabinet Committee on Disinvestment to divest each property individually and Govt. acted in fair and transparent process took advise of experts issued tenders and sold to highest bidder show that sold for highest price - On directions of SC dept. of company affairs gave public notice heard parties and approved scheme of agreement and demerger was complete and shares of respondent no.4 were sold to respondent no.5 by share purchase agreement - Informed that 8 petitioners who had challenged policy decision of Central Govt. had resigned their jobs and joined some other service which was not disputed or denied by their counsel -Policy decision taken by Central Govt. could not be interfered at instance of employees - Petitions dismissed .
