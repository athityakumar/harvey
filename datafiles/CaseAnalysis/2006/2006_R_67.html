Ram Biraji Devi and Another v Umesh Kumar Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 212; (2006) 6 SCC 669; AIR 2006 SC 2035; 2006 CRLJ 2782; 2006 (2) JCC 903; JT 2006 (11) SC 133; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 308; 2006(5) SCALE 638; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 482 - IPC, 1860, ss. 120-B, 406, 419, 420 - Oral agreement to sell plot - Some amount given as consideration - Failure to sell property - Order of taking of cognizance of offences u/s. ss.406, 419, 420, 120-B IPC - Petition for quashing of cognizance taken by Magistrate dismissed by HC - Appeal against - Held, averments of complaint and statements of complainant and his witnesses recorded by Magistrate would amount to civil liability inter se parties and no criminal liability can be attributed to appellants on basis of material on record - Appeal allowed.
