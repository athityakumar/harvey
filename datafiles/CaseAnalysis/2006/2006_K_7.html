G.L. Vijain v K. Shankar

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 995; (2006) 13 SCC 136; AIR 2007 SC 1103; 2007 (66) ALR 168; 2007 (5) AWC 4846; 2006 (5) CTC 796; 2007 (1) KLT 273; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 630; 2007 (146) PLR 564; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 195B; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 668; 2006(12) SCALE 649; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supplemental Proceedings

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, s. 25 - Fixation of fair rent by Appellate Authority - Revisional jurisdiction of High Court u/s. 25 of Act - Whether HC should impose conditions as has been done by impugned judgment while admitting revision petition? - Held, though HC having plenary jurisdiction has incidental or ancillary power - Revisional jurisdiction cannot be curtailed by imposing condition while admitting a revisional application in name of 'incidental, ancillary or inherent power'- Court's power to impose condition for entertaining an application must be provided for under statute itself - HC can exercise its inherent jurisdiction in appropriate cases - Revisional jurisdiction, however, in effect and substance is an appellate jurisdiction - Conditions can be imposed only while considering question of grant of stay - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained and that part of order whereby conditions have been imposed for admission of revision application is set aside - Appeal allowed.
