Union of India and Another v K.G. Soni

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 421; (2006) 6 SCC 794; 2006 (3) AWC 2931; 2006 (3) ESC 373; JT 2006 (7) SC 509; 2006(8) SCALE 49; 2006 (7) SLR 1; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 560; 2006 (6) Supreme 389; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2452

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 21(2) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 27 - Punishment of compulsory retirement imposed on respondent - HC held that punishment was disproportionate to alleged misconduct and Appellate Authority was directed to consider matter afresh with regard to quantum of punishment - Appeal against - Held, unless punishment imposed by Disciplinary Authority or Appellate Authority shocks conscience of Court/Tribunal, there is no scope for interference - HC has not kept correct position in view - It has not even indicated as to why punishment was considered disproportionate and why it considered misconduct to be not serious - Appeal allowed.
