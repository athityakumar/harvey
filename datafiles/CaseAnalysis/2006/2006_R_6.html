R. Janakiraman v State of Tamil Nadu, Through CBI, SPE, Madras

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 8; (2006) 1 SCC 697; AIR 2006 SC 1106; 2006 CRLJ 1232; 2006 (1) JCC 209; JT 2006 (1) SC 147; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 432; 2006(1) SCALE 167; 2006 (3) SCJ 226; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 212

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(e), 5(2) - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 313, 423(1)(b) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 131 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91, 92 - Assets beyond known sources of income - Appellant found guilty and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for one year and pay a fine of Rs. 1, 000/- - Appeal against conviction - Held, explanation as to how he obtained said money is clearly unacceptable - Persons from whom he allegedly borrowed money, Rs. 2, 50, 000/-, have denied having lent said amount - No error found in concurrent findings of trial court and HC that appellant had not borrowed said amount from PW-11 and PW-15 and that same was part of ill-gotten money acquired as illegal gratification - Appeal dismissed.
