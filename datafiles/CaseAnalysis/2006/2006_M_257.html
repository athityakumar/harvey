Muklesh Ali v State of Assam and Anr

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 286; (2006) 5 SCC 485; AIR 2006 SC 2672; JT 2006 (6) SC 111; 2006(6) SCALE 500; 2006 (5) SLR 77; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 228

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Assam Service (Discipline & Appeal) Rules, 1984, rr. 26, 27 - Disciplinary Proceedings - Suspension - Reinstatement - Sustainability - Appellant was suspended from service by respondent no.2 - By Notification dt. 12-12-1994, appellant was reinstated in his service - On 29.7.1997, in pursuance of order of SC, appellant was served with memo to show cause containing as many as five charges along with statement of allegations - After Enquiry, Enquiry Officer found that appellant was not guilty of offence of felling of trees - Subsequently, SC directed State to submit Action Taken Report in consequent to earlier order of SC and action taken against concerned officials, who were found responsible for significant felling of trees - Respondent no.2, by Memo dt. 20-10-2001, directed appellant to submit written statement as to need of intimation of decision to appellant vide order no. FRE.79/98/139 dt. 1-11-2000 would not be reviewed asking appellant to submit his written statement within ten days - Appellant filed writ petition in HC, challenging validity and correctness of notice dt. 20-10-2001 by - HC dismissed writ petition - DB dismissed writ appeal filed by appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether second memo issued by respondent no. 2, in pursuance of SC order, to appellant to submit written statement was sustainable - Held, disciplinary proceedings were initiated against appellant and appellant was placed under suspension and later was reinstated in service - In response to first memo, appellant submitted his written statement as well as additional written statement - HC was not justified in holding that respondents had power to review u/rr. 26 and 27 of the Rules, particularly, when review was sought to be done in pursuance of SC's order dt. 15-1-1998 and 12-5-2001 - Single Judge and DB completely misinterpreted and misread paragraphs 27 and 12 of dt. 15-1-1998 and 12-5-2001 respectively passed, in coming to conclusion that case of appellant was covered by aforesaid two orders of SC - Plea as to exercise of review power u/rr. 26 and 27 of the Rules was not taken either before Single Judge or before DB, moreover, no written plea or any oral argument was advanced in regard - In Indian Bank vs. ABS Marine Products Pvt. Ltd. 2006(4) SCALE 423 2006 Indlaw SC 150, it was held that Courts should be careful to ascertain and follow ratio decendendi, and not relief given on special facts, exercising power u/art. 142 - DB was not justified in upholding action of respondents on ground that State had exercised power u/r. 27 of the Rules - Hence, judgment passed by DB, affirming judgment of Single Judge was not valid in law and against directions issued by SC dt. 15-1-1998 and 12-5-2001 - Appeal allowed.
