State of Jharkhand and others v Tata Cummins Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 99; (2006) 4 SCC 57; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 129; JT 2006 (4) SC 1; 2006(3) SCALE 499; 2006 (4) SCJ 97; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 443; 2006 (145) STC 340

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bihar Finance Act, 1981

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Bihar Finance Act, 1981 - Benefit of Industrial Policy, 1995 - Pre-conditions for grant of benefit of Industrial Policy, 1995 under notifications was that proprietor/partner/holding company must have its exclusive ownership of holding company - DB of HC declared that Tata Cummins Ltd is entitled to benefit of Industrial Policy directed to adjust refundable amount of Rs. 54.5 crores towards sales tax dues from assessee for accounting year commencing on and from 1/4/2004 - Held, object of insisting on ownership of building or lease was to ensure that industry was bona fide investor - State Govt. has renewed Head lease in favour of TISCO who in turn has sub-leased a portion thereof to TELCO, which has 50% interest in joint venture, namely, Tata Cummins Ltd - Industry entitled to benefit of tax exemptions - Appeals disposed of.
