M/S Benara Valves Ltd. & Ors v Commissioner Of Central Excise & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 847; (2006) 13 SCC 347; 2006 (204) E.L.T. 513; 2006(12) SCALE 303; 2006 (8) SCJ 791; 2008 (12) S.T.R. 104; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 341; [2009] 20 VST 297

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11AC, 35F - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 2001 - Questioning correctness of order passed by Customs Excise and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal dealing with applications filed for staying recovery of duty and penalty imposed pending disposal of appeals - Held, it is for Tribunal to impose such conditions as are deemed proper to safeguard interest of revenue - Tribunal while dealing with application has to consider materials to be placed by assessee relating to undue hardship and also to stipulate condition as required to safeguard interest of revenue - Considering nature of dispute and difficulties highlighted by appellants seeking dispensation of deposit, directed that appeals shall now be heard without requiring further deposit, if appeals are free from other defects - Appeals disposed of.
