Sankaran Moitra v Sadhna Das and another

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 145; (2006) 4 SCC 584; AIR 2006 SC 1599; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2304; 2006 (2) JCC 689; JT 2006 (4) SC 34; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 389; 2006(3) SCALE 414; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 109, 114, 120-B, 120B, 148, 149, 201, 302, 304, 336, 34, 506(II) - CrPC, 1973, ss. 197, 210, 482 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 136 - Deceased beaten to death by police on polling day - Claim of protection of s.197(1) - Whether appellant was acting in his official capacity while alleged offence was committed or was performing a duty in his capacity as a police officer which led to offence complained of? - Held, it was part of duty of appellant to prevent any breach of law and maintain order on polling day or to prevent blocking of voters or prevent what has come to be known as booth capturing - It would be an offence committed during course of performance of his duty by appellant and it would attract s.197 - Quashed complaint only on ground of want of sanction u/s. 197(1) - Appeal allowed.
