Kartar Rolling Mills v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 253; (2006) 4 SCC 772; 2006 (197) E.L.T. 151; 2008 (9) S.T.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 L(b) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9(2) and 57 F(2) - Whether appellants were covered by Notification Nos. 214/86 and entitled to exemption from payment of excise duty? - Tribunal had specifically held that products manufactured by appellants were marketable and therefore eligible to levy of excise duty - Held, appellants failed to bring any evidence on record to prove that supplier of raw materials had supplied materials to them under provisions of Notification No. 214/86 - Conditions laid in notification for its applicability were not satisfied - Finding recorded by Tribunal and authorities below on this point is a finding of fact which cannot be interfered with in absence of any material to contrary - No merit in these appeals - Appeal dismissed.
