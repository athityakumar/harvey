Sadashiv Dada Patil v Purshottam Onkar Patil (D) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1425; (2006) 11 SCC 161; JT 2006 (9) SC 64; 2006(10) SCALE 21; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Revenue Patels (Abolition of Office) Act, 1962, Bombay Tenancy & Agricultural Lands Act, 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy & Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 2(2), 2(6), 31, 32, 32-G, 32-O - Bombay Hereditary Offices Act, 1874 - Maharashtra Revenue Patels (Abolition of Office) Act, 1962, ss. 3, 8 - Tenanted land - Re-grant - Desire to pay purchase price - Succeed - Respondent/tenant served notice on his landlord/appellant expressing his desire to pay purchase price of land in terms of s. 32-G of the 1948 Act - But Tehsildar by order dt. 31-1-1984 opined that tenancy commenced on date of re-grant, tenant ought to have given notice within one year from date thereof as contemplated u/s. 32-O thereof and as such notice had not been given, proceedings were dropped - Aggrieved by this appeal was filed by respondent, appellate authority opined that provisions of s. 32-O of the Act of 1948 were not applicable and tenant was entitled to purchase tenanted land in terms of s. 32-G thereof - Aggrieved by this appellant filed revision application, which was allowed - Respondent questioning legality and validity of order by filed petition before DB of HC - DB by order dt. 22-11-2002, held relied on in S. Sundaram Pillai, Etc v V.R. Pattabiraman Etc 1985 Indlaw SC 443, provision of s. 32-O were not applicable and appellant did not exercise remedy of eviction of tenant available under the Act of 1874 he was neither entitled for re-grant of land in question under the Act of 1874 nor was he entitled to seek possession thereof - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB committed serious error in relying upon provisions of the 1874 Act, which had no application in present case and HC also committed manifest error in relying upon S. Sundaram Pillai, Etc v V.R. Pattabiraman Etc 1985 Indlaw SC 443 to hold that provisions of s. 32-O were not applicable - Whether order of HC was succeed - Held, statutory right was conferred upon tenant and right was created to fulfill object that tiller should become owner but thereby landlord was not to be deprived of price of land - Hence, s. 32-O of the Tenancy Act would not be attracted, only because proviso appended to Section 8 of the 1962 Act provides for new date, for said purpose, it was not necessary to make any amendment in the 1948 Act in view of fact that relevant provisions of 1948 Act were made part of the 1962 Act - Therefore, it was not case where 1948 Act was required to be made applicable with retrospective effect, as proviso appended to s. 8 of the 1962 Act was to be read in light of s. 32-G and s. 32-O of the 1948 Act, Proviso appended to s. 8 of the 1962 Act has limited role to play - Therefore, SC agree with findings of HC - Appeal dismissed.
