S.R. Batra and Another v Taruna Batra

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 993; (2007) 3 SCC 169; AIR 2007 SC 1118; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 66; 2006 (67) ALR 175; 2007 (3) ALT 1; 2007 (3) CTC 219; 2007 (136) DLT 1; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 69; 2007 (146) PLR 425; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 378; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 403; 2006(13) SCALE 652; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1206

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Matrimonial Homes Act, 1967

Summary: Women & Children - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, s. 2(s) - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Matrimonial Home Act, 1967 - Whether 'shared household' includes house purchased and belonging to mother-in-law? - Wife is only entitled to claim a right to residence in a shared household, and a 'shared household' would only mean house belonging to or taken on rent by husband, or house which belongs to joint family of which husband is a member - Held, property in question in present case neither belongs to husband nor was it taken on rent by him nor is it a joint family property of which husband is a member, it is exclusive property of appellant No. 2, that is, her mother in law - Hence it cannot be called a 'shared household' - Appeal allowed.
