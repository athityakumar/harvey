Punjab and Sind Bank v Allahabad Bank and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 103; (2006) 4 SCC 780; 2006 (2) BC 271; 2006 (199) E.L.T. 3; JT 2006 (4) SC 161; 2006 (143) PLR 283; 2006(3) SCALE 557; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Suit filed by respondent no 1-Bank against appellant-Bank - Application by Appellant-Bank for dismissal of suit on ground that modalities indicated in ONGC-I case were not followed - Order of Single Judge that there was no need to adopt procedure indicated by this Court in Oil and Natural Gas Commission case (1991 Indlaw SC 17) - Appeal against - Held, view expressed by Division Bench of HC is confusing and patently shows that ratio of various decisions has not been understood in proper perspective - To say that decision in ONGC-I case was of an administrative nature though a judicial order, shows non-application of mind - Any order passed in a judicial proceeding, can by no stretch of imagination be described as one of administrative nature - Remitted matter to HC for fresh consideration keeping in view modalities and principles set out by this Court in various decisions - Appeal partly allowed.
