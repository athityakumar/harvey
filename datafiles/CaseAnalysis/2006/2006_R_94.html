Mst. Sugani v Rameshwar Das and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 306; (2006) 1 SCC 587; 2006 (4) ADJ 687; AIR 2006 SC 2172; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 41; 2006 (63) ALR 772; 2006 (3) AWC 2392; 2006 (3) CTC 108; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 218; JT 2006 (11) SC 268; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 131; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 319; 2006(4) SCALE 491; 2006 (Supp) RD 371; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 235

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 110 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - (A)whether High Court was justified in exercise of second appellate jurisdiction u/s. 100 CPC in reversing judgment and decree passed by Trial Court as affirmed by first Appellate Court? - No issue was framed as regards alleged non-fulfillment of requirement of s. 16(c) of Act, strangely HC upset factual findings recorded by trial court and first appellate court holding that requirements of s. 16(c) of 1963 Act were not fulfilled - Held, where from a given set of circumstances two inferences are possible, one drawn by lower Appellate Court is binding on HC in Second Appeal, adopting any other approach is not permissible - (B) whether a litigant should be allowed to raise a point of law as substantial question in second appeal which has not been pleaded or is found to be arising between parties in absence of any factual format? - Further held, a litigant should not be allowed to raise that question as a substantial question of law in Second Appeal - Mere appreciation of facts, documentary evidence or meaning of entries and contents of document cannot be held to be raising a substantial question of law - Appeal allowed.
