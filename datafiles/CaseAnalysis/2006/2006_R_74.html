Jaipur Development Authority v Ram Sahai and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 882; (2006) 11 SCC 684; 2006 (6) AWC 5409; JT 2006 (9) SC 520; 2006(11) SCALE 95; 2007 (2) SLR 666; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes rules, 1958, r. 77 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25G and 25H, 25F and 11A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - Whether the High Court was justified in directing the reinstatement of service of workman/respondent? - If not then to what relief and amount the workman/respondent is entitled to receive? - Provisions of ss. 25G and 25H of the Act do not require that the workman should have been in continuous employment within the meaning of s. 25B before being retrenched - There had been a violation of ss. 25G and 25H of the Act, but the Labour Court should not have passed an Award of re-instatement with entire back wages - This Court has held that the jurisdiction u/s. 11A must be exercised judiciously - The workman must be employed by a State within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution, having regard to the doctrine of public employment - Respondent had not regularly served appellant, the job was not of perennial nature - There was nothing to show that when services were terminated any person who was junior, had been retained - It would not be proper to direct reinstatement with back wages - Held, sum of Rs.75,000/- is awarded to respondent by way of compensation - Held, appeal is allowed in part.
