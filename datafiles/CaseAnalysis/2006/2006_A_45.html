Ajay Goswami v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 953; (2007) 1 SCC 143; AIR 2007 SC 493; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 70; 2007 (5) AWC 4741; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 632; 2006(14) SCALE 317; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Morality, Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986

Summary: Petition involves a substantial question of law and public importance on the fundamental right of the citizens, regarding the freedom of speech and expression as enshrined under art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution of India - grievance is that the freedom of speech and expression enjoyed by the newspaper industry is not keeping balance with the protection of children from harmful and disturbing materials - Held, in view of availability of sufficient safeguards in terms of various legislations, norms and rules and regulations to protect the society in general and children, in particular, from obscene and prurient contents, writ at the instance of the petitioner is not maintainable - Any steps to ban publishing of certain news pieces or pictures would fetter the independence of free press which is one of the hallmarks of our democratic setup - Petitioner has failed to establish the need and requirement to curtail the freedom of speech and expression - Petition dismissed.
