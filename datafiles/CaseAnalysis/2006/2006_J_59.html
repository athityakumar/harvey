D. Anuradha v Joint Secretary & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1506; (2006) 5 SCC 142; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 432; AIR 2006 SC 3661; 2006 (2) CalLT(SC) 115; 2006 CRLJ 2784; 2006 (2) JCC 784; JT 2006 (5) SC 91; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 905; 2006(4) SCALE 470; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Preventive detention - Legality - Order of detention was passed against detenue by the authorities u/s. 3(1) of the COFEPOSA Act - It was alleged that the detenue was engaged in transactions in violation of the provisions of FERA - Order was challenged before HC - HC held that the detention order was legal - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Delay in considering the representations of detenue - Held, in the instant case, detention was not liable to be quashed on the ground that one out of the five representations was not disposed of in time - The delay was satisfactorily explained and the failure to get the translated copy of the representation was an unavoidable delay - (B) It was contended that the COFEPOSA Advisory Board was not supplied with the materials as contemplated u/s. 8 of the COFEPOSA Act and thus there was no proper reference to COFEPOSA Advisory Board - Held, the delay of only one week in sending some of the relevant records may not by itself make the whole reference illegal and vitiated - Hence, the fact that merely because some of the materials were not sent along with the reference, would not vitiate the proceedings - Order of detention was rightly passed - Appeal dismissed.
