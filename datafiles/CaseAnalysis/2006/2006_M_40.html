M.V. Bijlani v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 122; (2006) 5 SCC 88; AIR 2006 SC 3475; 2006 (5) AWC 5279; JT 2006 (4) SC 469; 2006(4) SCALE 146; 2006 (4) SCJ 788; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 896; 2006 (3) SLR 105

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Allegation that appellant failed and/ or neglected to maintain a register known as ACE-8 Register and failed to supervise working of line and utilisation of copper wire while rules require personnel supervision and accountability of said wire and showed misleading entries on bills of transportation for transportation of material - Dismissal from service - Held, evidences recorded by Enquiry Officer and inferences drawn by him were not commensurate with charges - Enquiry Officer rejected relevant testimony of witnesses only on basis of surmises and conjectures - He cannot enquire into allegations with which delinquent officer had not been charged with - Orders of disciplinary authority as also appellate authority which are based on Enquiry Report, thus, cannot be sustained - Directed appellant to be reinstated in service with 50% back wages - Appeal allowed.
