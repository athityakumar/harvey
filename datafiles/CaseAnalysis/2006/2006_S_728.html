Ajendraprasadji N. Pande and Another v Swami Keshavprakeshdasji N. and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1289; (2006) 12 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC 806; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 93; 2007 (7) AWC 6704; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 1872; JT 2007 (1) SC 579; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 481; 2006(13) SCALE 525; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Boycott Of Courts, Re-Examination, Filing Of Application

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 - Amendment of pleadings - Scope of O. 6 r. 17 - Whether the amendment of written statement is allowable? - Held, no facts are pleaded nor any grounds are raised in amendment application, to even remotely contend that despite exercise of due diligence, these matters could not be raised earlier - Grant of amendment at this belated stage when deposition and evidence of three witnesses is already over and documentary evidence is already tendered would cause serious prejudice to respondents - Appellants' application praying for recasting of issues was denied and was never challenged - Amendment also seeks to introduce a totally new and inconsistent case - Not seeking of appropriate amendment at appropriate stage in manner envisaged by law disentitles appellants to any relief - Appeal dismissed.
