Jyothi Ademma v Plant Engineer, Nellore and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 316; (2006) 5 SCC 513; 2006 (3) ACC 356; AIR 2006 SC 2830; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 333; JT 2006 (6) SC 300; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 154; 2006(7) SCALE 28; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 400; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2381

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 30 - Appellant's husband died at work spot - Claiming compensation of Rs. 1, 00, 000/ - Commissioner for Workmen's Compensation awarded a sum of Rs. 61, 236/- - HC found that nature of job which deceased workman was doing could not have caused any stress and strain and, therefore, death due to heart attack can not be said to have been caused by any accident - Appeal against - Held, deceased was suffering from chest disease and was previously being treated for such disease - Doctor had clearly opined that there was no scope for any stress or strain in his duties - In view of factual findings recorded HC's judgment does not suffer from any infirmity - Appeal disposed of.
