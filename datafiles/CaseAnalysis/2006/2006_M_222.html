Morgan Securities And Credit Pvt. Ltd. v Modi Rubber Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 976; (2006) 12 SCC 642; AIR 2007 SC 683; 2007 (1) AWC 946; 2007 (2) AWC 1683; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 113; 2007 (2) CompLJ 401; 2006 (135) DLT 679; 2007 (1) DRTC 428; JT 2007 (1) SC 432; 2007 (2) MLJ 663; 2007 (146) PLR 442; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 665; 2006(14) SCALE 267; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1022

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Amendment Act, 1993, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Repeal Act, 2003, Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction Regulations, 1987

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996; Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Whether provisions of 1996 Act would prevail over the provisions of 'SICA'? - Held, once the arbitral award having the force of a decree is put into execution, s. 22(1) of SICA would come on its way from being enforced - Provisions of SICA, it will bear repetition to state, have been made to seek to achieve a higher goal and, thus, the provisions of SICA would be applicable, despite s. 5 of the 1996 Act - Appeal dismissed.
