Gunwantbhai Mulchand Shah and Others v Anton Elis Farel and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 77; (2006) 3 SCC 634; AIR 2006 SC 1556; 2006 (2) AWC 1475; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 81; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 178; JT 2006 (3) SC 212; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 399; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 251; 2006(3) SCALE 82; 2006 (3) SCJ 538; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 886; 2006 (Supp) RD 115

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Limitation - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 14 r. 2, s. 9A - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 113, 54, s. 27 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 46 - Suit for specific performance of agreement of sale and for a perpetual injunction - Dismissed as barred by limitation - Decision confirmed by first appellate court and HC - Appeal against - No time for performance was fixed in agreement for sale - Held, in a case where no time for performance was fixed, court had to find date on which plaintiff had notice that performance was refused and on finding that date, to see whether suit was filed within three years thereof - Trial court should have insisted on parties leading evidence, on this question or court ought to have postponed consideration of issue of limitation along with other issues arising in suit, after a trial - Suit as regards prayer for perpetual injunction to protect possession of plaintiff over suit property on claim that predecessor of plaintiffs was put in possession of property pursuant to agreement for sale, on a subsequent date, could not have been held to be not maintainable on any ground - Suit is remanded to trial court for a proper trial of all issues - Appeal allowed.
