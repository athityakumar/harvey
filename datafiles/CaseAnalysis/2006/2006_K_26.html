Kailash v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1421; (2006) 11 SCC 420; JT 2006 (9) SC 278; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 229; 2006(9) SCALE 681; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 102, 300, 302, 304, 323, 324, 34, 506, 96, 99 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - All three accused persons armed with axe, lathi and musal came to decease house and appellant herein was said to have been given blow by axe on head of decease, case was instituted against accused persons u/ss. 324 and 506 IPC before Trial Court - In post mortem examination only one injury was found to have been suffered by the deceased and offence was altered to u/s. 302 IPC before Trial Court given conviction to appellant - Aggrieved by same appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant was sustainable - Held, in Virsa Singh v State of Punjab 1958 Indlaw SC 82, relied on - Further, intention of accused was limited to infliction of bodily injury sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature and did not extend to intention of causing death, offence would be murder - Hence, it would be sufficient where knowledge of offender as to probability of death of person or persons in general as distinguished from particular person or persons being caused from his imminently dangerous act, approximates to practical certainty, such knowledge on part of offender must be of highest degree of probability, act having been committed by offender without any excuse for incurring risk of causing death or such injury as aforesaid - Therefore, appellant could not be held to be guilty of commission of offence u/s. 302 but u/s. 304, Part II IPC, appellant was, in peculiar facts and circumstances of case, sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for period of seven years - Appeal allowed.
