Maharashtra State Mining Corporation v Sunil

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 227; (2006) 5 SCC 96; AIR 2006 SC 1923; 2006 (7) AWC 6669; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 745; [2008] 142 Comp Cas 421; 2007 (4) CompLJ 227; 2006(5) SCALE 17; 2007 (1) SLR 525

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Plea that order of dismissal was passed by Managing Director of appellant, who had no authority or power to do so, as same was vested in Board of Directors of appellant - WP allowed and HC held that this defect could not be rectified subsequently by resolution of Board of Directors - Appeal against - Held, it was entirely wrong in holding that an invalid act cannot be subsequently 'rectified' by ratification of competent authority - Ratification by definition means making valid of an act already done - Since order of Managing Director had been ratified by Board of Directors such ratification related back to date of order and validated it - Appeal allowed.
