Ashwin S. Mehta and Another v Custodian and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 572; (2006) 2 SCC 385; AIR 2006 SC 795; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 329; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 255; JT 2006 (1) SC 229; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 220; 2006(1) SCALE 33; 2006 (2) SCJ 113; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Special Court Trial of Offences Relating To Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992, ss. 3, 4, 11, 12 and 13 - (A)whether appellants being not involved in offences in transactions in securities could have been proceeded against in terms of provisions of Act? - Held, there cannot be any doubt whatsoever that they being notified persons, all their properties would be deemed to be automatically attached as a consequent thereto - For said purpose, it is not necessary that they should be accused of commission of an offence as such - (B) whether individual liabilities of appellants ought to have been separately considered by special court as not a part of Harshad Mehta Group? - Further held, question need not be determined at present advised, in view of fact that appropriate applications in this behalf are pending consideration before learned special court - (C) whether tax liabilities could not have been held to be due as order of assessments did not become final and binding? - Further held, orders, of assessment have been set aside - If orders of assessment have been set aside liabilities of appellants have to be worked out on basis of new orders of assessment - So long, such orders of assessment are not passed by competent assessing authorities, it cannot be said that appellants are liable to pay a huge amount by way of income tax dues on basis of such orders of assessment which have since been set aside - (D)whether commercial properties could have been sold in auction? - Further held, sale of commercial properties has never been seriously contested by appellants - In fact one of contentions raised on behalf of appellants had been that if commercial properties are sold, there would be no need to sale residential properties - (E) whether residential properties should have been released from attachment? - Further held, liabilities of appellants are required to be considered afresh by learned judge, special court - Appeals allowed.
