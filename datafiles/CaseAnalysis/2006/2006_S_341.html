Rakesh Kumar Mishra v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 589; (2006) 1 SCC (Cr) 432; (2006) 1 SCC 557; AIR 2006 SC 820; 2006 CRLJ 808; 2006 (1) JCC 549; JT 2006 (1) SC 1; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 456; 2006 (2) RLW 979; 2006(1) SCALE 15; 2006 (3) SCJ 114; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 124; 2006 (1) UC 602

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 197, 197(1), 197(2), 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120(B), 190, 342, 389, 469, 471 - Cognizance - Protection - Claim - Availability - Appellant filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC seeking quashing of order of cognizance of offences punishable u/ss. 342, 389, 469, 471 and 120(B) IPC, taken by Judicial Magistrate, on complaint filed by respondent no. 2 - Before HC, appellant contended that in absence of sanction as contemplated u/s. 197 CrPC, proceeding should not be continued - HC dismissed petition by holding that since requirement of s. 100(4) CrPC were not followed, while search was conducted in respondent no. 2's premises, provisions of s. 197 CrPC were not applicable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether protection u/s. 197 CrPC was available for appellant against taking cognizance of offences punishable u/ss. 342, 389, 469, 471 and 120(B) IPC - Held, protection given u/s. 197 was to protect responsible public servants against institution of possibly vexatious criminal proceedings for offences alleged to have been committed by them while public servants were acting or purporting to act as public servants - S. 197(1) CrPC provided that when any person who was public servant not removable from his office save by or with sanction of State was accused of any offence alleged to have been committed by him while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty, no Court should take cognizance of such offence except with previous sanction (a) in case of person who was employed or, as case might be, was at time of commission of alleged offence employed, in connection with affairs of State and (b) in case of person who was employed or, as case might be, was at time of commission of alleged offence employed, in connection with affairs of State - HC was not justified in holding that s. 197 was not applicable to facts of instant case - In instant case, therefore s. 197 CrPC had clear application- events, which allegedly took place after 11-7-1996 on which emphasis was laid by respondent no. 2 had really no relevance for issue under consideration - Hence, order of Magistrate taking cognizance was set aside - Appeal allowed.
