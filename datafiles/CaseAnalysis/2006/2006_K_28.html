Sanghamitra Ghosh v Kajal Kumar Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 775; (2007) 2 SCC 220; 2007 (66) ALR 155; 2007 (5) AWC 4850; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 55; 2007 (1) JCC 373; JT 2006 (10) SC 288; 2007 (148) PLR 76; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 845; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(a), 13(1)(b), 13-B, 9 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, s. 25 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Matrimonial suit for restitution of conjugal rights by husband - Transfer petition by wife - Prayer to grant a decree of divorce by mutual consent - Held, marriage between parties has irretrievably broken down because of incompatibility of temperament - Matrimonial bond between parties is beyond repair - Compromise filed - Directed that cases pending between parties, as enumerated in preceding paragraphs, are disposed of in view of settlement between parties - Order accordingly.
