(1) Workmen of Bhurkunda Colliery of Messrs v (1) Management of Bhurkunda Colliery of Messrs

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 17; (2006) 3 SCC 297; 2006 (5) AWC 4814; 2006 (1) CLR 635; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 123; JT 2006 (2) SC 1; 2006 (1) LLJ 842; 2006(1) SCALE 595; 2006 (2) SCJ 12; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 851; 2006 (2) SLR 386; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 784

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 19 - Main concept of regularization in Indian Industrial jurisprudence is the subject-matter - Demand that 125 Civil Engineering Workers should be regularized - Tribunal awarded that only 74 of them could justify their claim and directed to enlist those 74 workers as casual workmen and regularized after they have fulfilled condition of attendance - High Court upheld award - Appeal against - Held, no reason to interfere with findings of fact arrived at by Tribunal - Appeals disposed of.
