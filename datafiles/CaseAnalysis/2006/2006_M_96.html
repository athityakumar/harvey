Deputy Director Land Acquisition v Malla Atchinaidu and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1380; (2006) 12 SCC 87; AIR 2007 SC 740; 2007 (67) ALR 510; 2007 (5) AWC 4969; JT 2007 (3) SC 18; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 894; 2006(14) SCALE 1; 2006 (Supp) RD 546; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 13A,17(4), 18, 23(1a), 23(2), 4(1), 54, 9(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115, 151, 152 - Acquiring land - Compensation - Justifiability - Land was acquired u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act, for provision of house sites for weaker sections by appellant - On dt. 31-7-1982, Land Acquisition Officer issued notice u/s. 9(1) of the ??? Act directing for conducting award enquiry and on dt. 3-2-1983, gave award granting compensation of Rs. 11, 500/- per acre to respondents and also valued certain palmyrah trees at Rs. 2000/-(Rs. 10/- for big Palmyrah tree, Rs. 5/- for small Palmyrah tree and Rs. 50/- for cashew trees) - However respondents not satisfied with compensation awarded by Land Acquisition Officer, matter was referred to Civil Court u/s. 18 of the ??? Act - Trial Court passed order where by compensation amount was enhanced to Rs. 55,000/- per acre and compensation with regard to palmyrah trees was enhanced to Rs. 100/- for big tree and Rs. 50/- for small tree and Rs. 200/- for cashew tree - However, respondent filed I.A. No. 70/91 in O.P.No.21/87 contending that there were 10,000 big palmyrah trees and 4,500 small palmyrah trees and that order of Sub-Judge contained typographical error - Trial Court in his order rejected application of respondents - Aggrieved by order of Trial Court, respondents filed revision petition before HC - On dt. 31-12-1993, Single Judge allowed revision and directed that order of Trial Court be amended - Appellants filed appeal before DB - DB dismissed appeal of appellants and enhanced compensation to Rs. 1, 50,000/- per acre relying on registered sale deed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Order of Single Judge was right in law in upholding plea of respondents/claimants that their grievance was amenable for correction u/s. 152 CPC and Whether Single Judge was right in entertaining respondent's Revision u/s. 115 CPC, more so, matter was seized by DB and thus pre-empting adjudication by latter - Held, Single Judge had erred in law in upholding plea of claimants that their grievance was amenable for correction u/s.152 CPC and that Single Judge was not right in entertaining respondents' revision u/s. 115 CPC more so, when matter was seized by DB and thus preempting adjudication by latter - Hence, order passed by Single Judge of HC revision petition dt. 31-12-1993 and allow appeal filed by appellant insofar as quantum of compensation awarded for trees pursuant to order passed by HC to amend number of trees - Therefore, respondents would be entitled for payment of compensation only with reference to number of trees as ordered by Sub-Judge and not as ordered by the order in revision petition in regard to total number of trees and value and direct appellant to pay balance compensation and solatium, interest etc - Appeal disposed of.
