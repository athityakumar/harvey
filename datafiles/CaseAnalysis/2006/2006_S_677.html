M.P. Wakf Board v Subhan Shah (D) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 886; (2006) 10 SCC 696; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 86; 2007 (1) AWC 8; 2006(11) SCALE 71; 2006 (3) ShimLC 382; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Women & Children

Summary: Land & Property - Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954 - Wakf Act, 1995 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960 - Erstwhile Maharaja Holkar Darbar, a Hindu king granted Mafi Inayat Land to Dargah - Allegedly, land in question was held to be not forming a part of Wakf in terms of provisions of Wakf Act, 1954 - One Munna Bai filed an application u/s. 25 of 1954 Act for registering Dargah as a Wakf - On or about 18-03-1968, properties were declared as Wakf property and Dargah was registered as Wakf - A suit thereafter was filed in Civil Court for a declaration that orders passed by Board are null and void and for recovery of possession of suit property inter alia contending that order registering Dargah as Wakf was vitiated in law - Both Board and private parties aggrieved thereby filed revision applications before High Court - HC dismissed both applications - Whether HC committed an illegality in passing impugned order? - Held, property was dedicated to Dargah, if any, a long time back - An application for registration of said property as a Wakf property in terms of s. 25 of 1954 Act, therefore, could have been filed only within period specified thereunder, viz.,nine months from date of coming into force of said Act - At no stage appellant was informed that Central Govt. proposed to exercise its suo moto power and asked him to show cause against exercise of such a power - Failure of Central Govt. to do so, in our opinion, vitiates impugned order - If nature of dedication of property does not constitute a Wakf within meaning of provisions of Act, it must be proved that it became a Wakf by reason of long user - No such finding has been arrived at - Matter requires fresh consideration at hands of Tribunal - Appeals disposed of.
