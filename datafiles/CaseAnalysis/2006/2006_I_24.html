Indian Drugs & Pharmaceuticals Ltd. v Workman, Indian Drugs & Pharmaceuticals Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 769; (2007) 1 SCC 408; 2007 (6) CompLJ 219; JT 2006 (10) SC 216; 2006(12) SCALE 1; 2007 (1) SLR 388; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 73

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Appellant a Public Sector Undertaking - Dispute relates to ten concerned employees who were appointed as casual workers on daily rate basis for reason that they were dependants of employees dying in harness - Plea that while holding that respondents were not entitled to regularization, it directed that company shall continue such employees in its services till their superannuation and they shall be paid wages like regular employees of company - Held, if court/tribunal directs that a daily rate or ad hoc or casual employee should be continued in service till date of superannuation, it is impliedly regularizing such an employee, which cannot be done - Appellant is a sick company which has been running on huge losses for many years, and is practically closed down - There are no vacancies on which respondents could have been appointed - Appeal allowed.
