State Bank of Bikaner and Jaipur v Om Prakash Sharma

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 242; (2006) 5 SCC 123; 2006 (7) AWC 7025; JT 2006 (11) SC 286; 2006(6) SCALE 107; 2006(69) SCALE 111; 2006 (5) SLR 61; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25H - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, r. 77 - Dispute regards violation of s.25H referred to Labour Court - Labour Court set aside termination - HC opined that if reference in question referred only to Section 25H of Act, same would not mean that tribunal was debarred from going into other illegalities committed under Act - Appeal against - Held, jurisdiction of Labour Court emanated from order of reference - It could not have passed an order going beyond terms of reference - If Labour Court exceeds its jurisdiction, Award must be held to be suffering from a jurisdictional error - HC fell in error in refusing to exercise its jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
