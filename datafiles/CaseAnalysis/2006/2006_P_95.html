Raghunath Rai Bareja And Another v Punjab National Bank And Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 996; (2007) 2 SCC 230; 2007 (2) ALT 114; 2007 (1) AWC 507; 2007 (1) CompLJ 108; 2007 (5) CTC 642; 2007 (1) DRTC 285; JT 2007 (1) SC 542; 2007 (2) MPLJ 306; 2006(13) SCALE 511; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appointed Day, Entertain And Decide, Time Barred, Ambiguous

Summary: Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 17, 18, 24, 31 - Suit for recovery of Rs. 14, 53, 577/- with pendente lite and future interest decreed - Execution proceedings - High Court by impugned judgment transferred execution proceeding to the Debt Recovery Tribunal - Appeal against - Held, final decree was passed and became enforceable on 15.1.1987, the period of limitation for filing an execution application expired on 15.1.1999 - S. 31 states that only suits or other proceeding pending before the Court immediately before the establishment of the Tribunal under the Act, stand transferred to the Tribunal - Since, admittedly the Tribunal in the present case, had been established in 1993, and no proceeding was pending before it on the date when it was established, no transfer could take place - Impugned order clearly illegal and without jurisdiction - Moreover the claim is time-barred - Appeals allowed.
