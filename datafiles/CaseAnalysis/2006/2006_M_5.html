Prabhakaran and Others v M. Azhagiripillai (Dead), By LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 183; (2006) 4 SCC 484; AIR 2006 SC 1567; 2006 (2) AWC 1899; 2006 (2) CTC 412; JT 2006 (5) SC 57; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 272; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 428; 2006 (1) RCR(Rent) 381; 2006 (3) RLW 2324; 2006(3) SCALE 235; 2006 (4) SCJ 80; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 28; 2006 (Supp) RD 720

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, - Tamil Nadu Debt Relief Act, 1979,s. 9 (5) - One Manickam Pillai mortgaged his property in favour of Krishna Pillai under a usufructuary mortgage deed D/-7-9-1935 - Mortgagee (Krishna Pillai) assigned said mortgage in favour of one Soundararaja Iyenger - He died leaving him surviving his widow and son (defendants 1 and 2 in suit) - Mortgagor Manickam Pillai died - Thukkaram, his son and Yasodai Ammal, his daughter settled their interest in right of redemption to Sakkubai, his daughter - Before such gift/settlement, they had issued a notice for redemption of mortgage - Defendants 1 and 2 sent a reply that they were not in possession of suit property and one Azhagiri Pillai (third defendant in suit) was in possession of property - (A) Whether assignment of usufructuary mortgage by Krishna Pillai in favour of Soundararaja Iyenger under deed D/-7-9-1935 amounted to an 'acknowledgment' u/s. 18 of Limitation Act, 1963, thereby enabling plaintiffs to compute a fresh period of limitation for suit for redemption, from date of such acknowledgment? - Held, deed states that assignee (Soundararaaja Iyenger) was entitled to receive all amounts as per original mortgage - Statement that assignee is entitled to receive amount as per original mortgage is an assertion of right of mortgagee against mortgagor under mortgage, and consequently, an admission of subsistence of mortgage and of jural relationship between mortgagee and mortgagor - It is an acknowledgment u/s. 18 of Act - When said deed of assignment was executed on 12-02-1954, mortgage D/-07-09-1935 was subsisting, as period of limitation at that time, was 60 years - In view of admission of jural relationship contained in assignment deed, operating as an acknowledgment of liability, a fresh period of limitation started from 12-02-1954 - Suit filed in year 1981 was clearly within limitation - (B) Whether mortgage debt under deed of mortgage dated 07-09-1935 stood discharged u/s. 9 of Tamil Nadu Debt Relief Act, 1979? - Further held,s. 9(5) of Debt Relief Act applies as mortgage transaction does not fall under any of exceptions enumerated in s. 4 of said Act - As mortgagee and his successors were in possession of mortgaged property ever since 07-09-1935, that is, for more than 10 years as on date when Act came into force, said mortgage debt stood wholly discharged with effect from 14-07-1978 - Appeal allowed.
