S. C. Saxena v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1588; (2006) 9 SCC 583

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Legality, Unauthorized absence

Summary: Service - Unauthorized absence - Compulsory retirement - Legality - Appellant was employed as in Intelligence Bureau - Appellant remained absent to work on account of illness - Appellant was served with charge-sheet alleging unauthorized absence from duty against him for entire period - Enquiry officer submitted his report - Disciplinary authority ordered compulsory retirement for misconduct of unauthorized absence - Appellant filed application before Tribunal and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal and HC was justified - Held, enquiry officer rightly said that charge could not be fully established because evidence showed that appellant could not be said to have remained absent unauthorizedly - Enquiry officer took cognizance of fact and disciplinary authority noticed it - This was not situation where disciplinary authority was disagreeing with findings of enquiry officer, which would have required following of some procedure prescribed under Rules - Appellant had sufficient leave to his credit and, therefore, respondents should have taken liberal view in matter and permitted him to resume duty when he attempted to resume in year 1992 by sanctioning leave that was available to his credit - SC found no reason to interfere with order made by Tribunal and HC - Appeal dismissed.
