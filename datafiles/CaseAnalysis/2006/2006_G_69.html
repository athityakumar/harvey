Anil Rishi v Gurbaksh Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 192; (2006) 5 SCC 558; AIR 2006 SC 1971; 2006 (64) ALR 178; 2006 (3) AWC 2656; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 205; JT 2006 (11) SC 521; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1455; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 33; 2006 (143) PLR 775; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 347; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 60; 2006 (101) RD 265; 2006 (3) RLW 2210; 2006(5) SCALE 153; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 659

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 111 - Suit for declaration by respondent alleging that sale deed dated 26-03-1991 was a forged, fabricated and was a void document - Trial Court framed issue as whether sale deed D/- 26-03-1991 is forged and fabricated as prayed for - On request of respondent issue reframed as whether alleged sale deed D/- 26/3/1991 is a valid and genuine document - Appeal against - Held, ordinarily, burden of proof would be on party who asserts affirmative of issue and it rests, after evidence is gone into, upon party against whom, at time question arises, judgment would be given, if no further evidence were to be adduced by either side - Order reframing issue is set aside thus reviving issue originally framed - Appeal allowed.
