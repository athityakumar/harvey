 Amar Nath Dogra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 530; AIR 1963 SC 424; 1962 (64) PLR 895; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Notice served u/s. 80 - Appellant filed suit for permanent injuction restraining state balance of licence-fees - Suit was withdrawn by appellant with liberty to file fresh suit - Suit filed by appellant but no fresh notice was given - Whether suit of appellant was bad in law as no fresh notice was given to respondent? - Suit dismissed hence present appeal - If notice satisfied requirements of law in respect of second suit then there was no necessity for further notice before filing subsequent suit - Provisions of s. 80 is mandatory - Held, a radical difference between state of circumstances when impugned notice was issued and when plaint was filed - No compliance of s. 80 - So entire claim in suit fails - Appeal dismissed.
