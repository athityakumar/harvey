Pratap Singh and Another v Gurbaksh Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 559; AIR 1962 SC 1172; 1962 CRLJ 262; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 838

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 3 - Departmental proceedings - Deducted - Illegal - Contempt of Court - Appellants both public servants, initiated departmental proceedings against respondent, another public servant, for having sued Govt. for a declaration that a certain sum of money was being illegally deducted from his salary - Respondent brought suit before exhausting all his departmental remedies as required by an official circular - Grievances arising out of a Govt. Servant's employment conditions of service - Respondent complained to HC that two appellants had committed contempt of court punishable u/s.3 of 1952 Act - HC detained that appellants were clearly guilty of contempt but ends of justice would be sufficiently directed to abandon departmental proceedings and warned against complying with instructions - Whether appellants committed contempt of Court - Held, amount to gross contempt of Court and convicted members of Special Committee for it - Departmental enquiry against respondent did not tend to interfere with course of justice - Appellants alleged conduct committed contempt of Court - Appellant was conducting enquiry under orders of respondent directed enquiry under orders from Govt. - Appeals disposed of.
