Inder Lal v Lal Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 248; AIR 1962 SC 1156; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 114; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Statement Of Fact, Electorate, Circulation, Private Character

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 101(b), 123(4) and 153(4) - Whether circulation of false statements amounts to corrupt practice? - Circulation of false statements about private or personal character of candidate during period preceding elections is likely to work against freedom of election itself in as much as effect created by false statements cannot be met by denials in proper time and so Constituency has to be protected against circulation of such false statements which are likely to effect voting of electors - Dissemination of false statements about personal character of a candidate thus constitutes a corrupt practice - Appeal allowed.
