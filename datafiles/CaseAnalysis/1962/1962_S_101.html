 Travancore Rubber and Tea Company Limited and Another v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1964 SC 572; 1963 (6) FLR 266; 1963 (1) ITJ 180; [1963] 48 ITR 102; 1963 KerLJ 351; 1963 (1) KerLR 293; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1961 - Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950, s. 5 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 59(3) - Whether provisions of Explanation 2 to s. 5 are discriminatory against agricultural income from rubber plantations? - Held, State Legislature has full powers to tax such income as income within expression 'agricultural income' as defined in Agricultural Income Tax Act, definition being in conformity with definition of 'agricultural income' in Income Tax Act - It is for State Legislature to provide such deductions from such income as it considers fit - S. 5 of Agricultural Income Tax Act makes provisions for deductions considered necessary by Legislature - State Legislature was competent to enact Explanation 2 to s. 5 and thereby provide for non-deduction of expenses incurred in upkeep or maintenance of immature plants from which no income has been derived in accounting year - Nothing discriminatory in provisions of Explanation 2 to s. 5 - It is applicable to agricultural income derived from all crops except tea - Provision for computation of agricultural income from tea plantations has to be different and is to be found in rules made u/s. 59(3) of Income Tax Act for determining proportions of agricultural income and income from business in entire income from sale of tea - Difference in provisions for computation of agricultural income from tea plantations and from rubber plantations is therefore based on good reasons - Provisions of Explanation 2 are not discriminatory against agricultural income from rubber plantations - Petitions dismissed.
