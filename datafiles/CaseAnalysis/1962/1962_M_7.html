National, Union of Commercial Employees and Another v M. R. Meher, Industrial Tribunal, Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 222; AIR 1962 SC 1080; 1962 (4) FLR 323; 1962 (1) LLJ 241; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solicitors

Summary: Labour &; Industrial Law - Advocates & Judges - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(j), 2 (n)Cl (VI) and 12(5) - (A) Whether a Solicitor's Firm can be termed as an 'Industry' u/s. 2(j) of Industrial Disputes Act? - Held, concept of industry postulates partnership between capital and labour or between employer and his employees - Co-operation between capital and labour or between employer and his employees must be direct and must be essential - Service rendered by solicitor functioning either individually or working together with partners, is service which is essentially individual - It depends upon professional equipment, knowledge and efficiency of solicitor concerned - Solicitor's firm cannot be referred as an 'Industry' - Appeal dismissed.
