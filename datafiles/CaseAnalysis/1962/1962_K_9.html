 Ramkishore Lal v Kamal Narain

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 459; AIR 1963 SC 890; 1963 JabLJ 523; 1963 MahLJ 428; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 417; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities and Local Govts. - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 92 - (A )Deed construction - Ascertainment of intention of parties -(A) Whether by award embodying partition that took place between a joint Hindu family, an absolute dedication of village T was made in favour of temple? - Held, Intention expressed by words 'malik' or 'Milkiyat'- Village T was dedicated absolutely to temple of Shri Ramchandra Swamy - (B) Whether village was given in full proprietorship to R with only a charge on it to meet expenses of temple? - Further held, Intention to give R possession and management of village for benefit of temple - Course of conduct of parties is of no relevance for construction of a document which itself unambiguous - (C) What points to be considered while ascertaining intention of parties to instrument? - Further held, court must consider document as a whole - All words to be construed in their ordinary natural sense - Take into account circumstances under which particular words were used - Rule of harmonious construction to be followed - Appeal allowed - Case sent back to High Court for proper disposal.
