Budhu Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 189; 1963 CRLJ 698; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Foreigners

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 471, 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 , s. 195 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Forgery - Application for Compensation - Displaced person - Fabricated document - Enquiry - Sustainability - Appellant was a displaced person who made an application before assistant settlement officer who was functioning under the 1954 Act - Appellant submitted an attested copy of his verified claim which was later found to be a fabricated document with help of enquiry - Appellant was convicted by Additional session judge u/ss. 471, 420 of IPC - On appeal the sessions judge upheld the sentence and set aside the fine - On revision decision of session judge was affirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Assistant Settlement Officer was deemed to be a court within the meaning of s. 195 (1) (e) of CrPC and therefore prosecution was incompetent in absence of a complaint by Assistant Settlement Officer - It was also contended that document submitted was only a copy and thus there could not be an offence under s. 471 of IPC, even if it was accepted along with its enclosures and was filed before Assistant Settlement Officer- it was also contended that there was no evidence to prove that application filed was made by appellant himself or on his behalf- It was not necessary that Assistant Settlement Officer in instant case when acting under the 1954 Act could be deemed to be court within the meaning of s. 195 (1) (c) of CrPC -Assumption was made for instant case that he is a court to which s. 195 (1) (c) of CrPC applied - Since document produced was not original forged document but was a copy of it - It was clear from language of that section that it was only when forged document was produced in court that complaint by that court was necessary - it was held that s. 195 (1) (c) of CrPC refers only to the document alleged to be forged and not to a copy of it and therefore the absence of a complaint from a court where copies of forged documents were produced or use of it was no bar to trial for an offence of forgery - Any court before which a copy of a document was produced would not not really be in a position to express any opinion on genuineness of original - It was suggested that a forged document might at least be said to be given in evidence - Even though a copy of secondary evidence of the contents of a document might have been produced the forged document itself would not thus be given in evidence - Thus, in instant case an attested copy would serve the purpose of forgery and production of such a copy would amount to use of original forged document as genuine - Therefore, appellant was convicted- Appeal Dismissed.
