 Kameshwar Prasad and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 230; AIR 1962 SC 1166; 1966 (33) FLR 194; 1963 (42) ILR(Pat) 259; 1962 (1) LLJ 294; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 369; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Constitutionality

Summary: Constitution - Service - Bihar Government Servants' Conduct Rules, 1956, r. 4 A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a)(b), 19(2) and 132 - Any form of demonstration - Prohibited by r. 4 A - Validity of - Held, if rule were so framed as to single out those types of demonstration which were likely to lead to a disturbance of public tranquility or which would fall under other limiting criteria specified in art. 19(2) validity of rule could have been sustained - R. 4 A in form in which it presently stands prohibiting any form of demonstrations is violative of appellants' rights u/art. 19(1)(a) and (b) and should therefore be stuck down - The rule in so far as it prohibits a strike, cannot be struck down since there is no fundamental right to resort to a strike - Appeal partly allowed.
