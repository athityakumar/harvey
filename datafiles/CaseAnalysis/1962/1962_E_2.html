Collector of Customs, Calcutta v East India Commercial Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 514; AIR 1963 SC 1124; 1984 ECR 132; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Keywords: Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Practice & Procedure - (A) Whether High Court can issue writ if it has no territorial jurisdiction? - Held, when once an order of an original authority is taken in appeal to appellate authority which is located beyond territorial jurisdiction of HC, it is order of latter authority which is operative order after appeal is disposed of - HC cannot issue a writ against appellate authority for want of territorial jurisdiction it would not be open to it to issue a writ to original authority which may be within its territorial jurisdiction once appeal is disposed of - It may be that appellate authority has merely confirmed order of original authority and dismissed appeal - (B) What is procedure adopted once an appeal is made ? - Further held, appellate authority can do one of three things, namely (i) it may reverse order under appeal (ii) it may modify that order, and (iii) it may merely dismiss appeal and thus confirm order without any modification - In all these three cases after appellate authority has disposed of appeal, operative order is order of appellate authority whether it has reversed original order or modified it or confirmed it - (C) Whether order of original authority is merged in order of appellate authority whatsoever its decision ?- Further held, it does not - Appellate order of confirmation is quite as efficacious as an operative order as an appellate order of reversal or modification - Petition dismissed.
