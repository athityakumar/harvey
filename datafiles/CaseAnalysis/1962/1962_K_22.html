 Kedar Nath Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 562; AIR 1962 SC 955; 1962 CRLJ 103; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 40; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Comments, Disposition, Constitutionality, Sedition, Prize Competitions Act, 1955

Summary: Criminal - Doctrine of Harmonious Construction - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 124 A and 505 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(a) - Whether ss. 124 A and 505 IPC have become void in view of provisions of art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution - Held, No - Fundamental right guaranteed by art. 19(1)(a) of freedom of speech and expression is not an absolute right - Section penalizes any spoken or written words, signs or visible representations, which bring or attempt to incite hatred and disaffection towards Govt. and jeopardize security of State and public order - But strong words used for criticism or disapproval of measures of Govt. with intent to their improvement by lawful means would not come within section - Comments expressing disapprobation of actions of Govt., without exciting feelings to cause public disorder by acts of violence, would not be penal - Disloyalty to Govt. established is not same as commenting in strong terms about measures of Govt., or its agencies - Further held, if certain provisions of law construed in one way would make them consistent with Constitution, and another interpretation would render them unconstitutional, Courts would lean in favour of former construction - Explanations to section make it clear that criticism of public measures or comments on Govt. action, however strongly worded, would be within reasonable limits and would be consistent with fundamental right of freedom of speech and expression - These provisions would not exceed bounds of reasonable restrictions on right of freedom of speech and expression - It is clear, therefore, that cl.(2) of art. 19 clearly saves section from vice of unconstitutionality - Appeal No. 169 of 1957 dismissed - Appeals Nos 124 to 126 of 1958 allowed.
