Nedunuri Kameswaramma v Sampati Subba Rao

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 169; AIR 1963 SC 884; 1963 (2) AnWR 49; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 208

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Zamindar

Summary: Land & Property - Suit filed by appellant for ejectment of respondent from 480 acres of jeroyti land and for mesne profits - Respondent denied that land was jeroyti land, alleged that it was part of a Dharmila inam land - High Court considered whether land in question was a Sarvadumbala Dharmila inam or a Karnikam service inam, and came to conclusion that two courts below were wrong in holding that it was a Karnikam service inam? - (A) Whether suit should be dismissed on ground of want of proper pleas by appellant in answer to written statement? - Held, since parties went to trial fully knowing rival case and led all evidence not only in support of their contentions but in refutation of those of other side, it cannot be said that absence of an issue was fatal to case, or that there was that mistrial which vitiates proceedings? - (B) Whether decision that this was not a Karnikam service inam is proper in circumstances of this case? - Decision in this case, therefore, depends upon documents produced by two parties in proof of their own contentions - From an analysis of documents, it will appear that right from 1903 to 1925 this land was treated as held on karnam service inam liable to be resumed by Zamindar - Other documents show that it was, in fact, so resumed and a jeroyti patta was given, and in all subsequent documents, it is described as jeroyti land - It is quite clear that documents on side of appellant established that this was a Karnikam service inam, and action of Zamindar in resuming it as such, which again has a presumption of correctness attaching to it, clearly established appellant's case - Appeal allowed.
