R. K. Dalmia v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 534; AIR 1962 SC 1821; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 699; 1962 CRLJ 805; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 22, 114, 120 B, 176, 405, 409 and 477A - Appeals by special leave - Against convictions u/ss. 120-B r/w 409 IPC, 1860 and of some u/s. 477 A r/w s. 110, IPC - A criminal conspiracy to do and cause to be done illegal acts viz, criminal breach of trust of funds of company in capacity of Chairman and Principal Office for purposes of meeting losses suffered by appellant in forward transaction (of speculation) in shares and for other purposes not connected with affairs of Company, illegal purpose of diverting funds - By false entries showing defalcated funds were invested in Govt. Securities and conspiracy to dishonestly misappropriate and withdrawing said funds from current account - Court mainly dealt with following issue : (A) Illegalities in procedure - Whether Delhi Court had no territorial jurisdiction to try offences of criminal breach of trust committed at Bombay - Held, following decision in Purushottam Das Dalmia's case 1961 Indlaw SC 473, Delhi court had jurisdiction to try appellant (Chokhani) of offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 as offence was alleged to have been committed in pursuance of criminal conspiracy - (B) Whether due to substantive charge of offence u/s. 409 (against Dalmia) offended against provisions of s. 233 of Code, whole trial was bad - Further held, if it be complained that charge framed u/s. 409 is vague because it does not specifically state one particular mode in which offence was committed, vagueness of charge will not make trial illegal, especial when no prejudice is caused to accused and no contention has been raised that Dalmia was prejudiced by form of charge - (C) Whether funds of Company in Bank, which were alleged to have been misappropriated were not 'property' within meaning of s. 405 and 409, IPC - Held, no good reason to restrict meaning of word 'property' to movable property only when it is used without any qualification in s. 405 or in other - (D) And if they were property, whether appellant (Dalmia ) did not have dominion over them - Further held, as Chairman and Principal Officer of Bharat Insurance Company, he had power, on behalf of company, to operate on those - (E) Whether Director was not an 'agent' within meaning of s. 409 IPC, as only that person could be such agent who professionally carried on business of agency - Further held, agents within meaning of s. 409 IPC - (F) Whether confessional statement made by appellant (Dalmia) was not admissible in evidence - Made in presence of a Chartered Accountant - Further held, statement (Exhibit P 10) is a voluntary statement and is admissible in evidence - (G) If confessional statement was not in admissible in evidence in view of s. 24 of Evidence Act, it was inadmissible in view of provisions of cl. (3) of art. 20 of Constitution - Further held, not inadmissible as it was not made at a time when he was accused of an offence, as is necessary for application of that clause

- (H) Secretary-cum-Chief Accountant denies that he was a party to fabrication of false accounts and vouchers in furtherance of interests of conspiracy - Charged u/s. 120 B r/w s. 409 IPC and s. 477A - Held to have been in - Further Court declined to specifically deal with ancillary contentions regarding question of onus of proof, erred in their approach to evidence, adequate corroboration of evidence, knowledge of impugned transactions in that form - And declined to reduce sentence - Appeals dismissed.
