 State of Assam and Others v Padma Ram Borah

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 572; AIR 1965 SC 473

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Fundamental Rules of State of Assam, r. 56 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 241(2) - Superannuation - Order of suspension around a week before due date of superannuation - Extension of service on public ground - Power to extend - Respondent, a Superintendent in office of Excise Commr. - Whether actions on part of appellant-State extending service of respondent for purpose of departmental proceeding, proper and lawful? - Govt. servant can be retained after age of superannuation on public grounds after satisfying conditions contained in FR 56 - Effect of order D/- 06-01-1961 was that service of respondent would come to an end on 31-03-1961 unless departmental proceedings were disposed of at a date earlier than 31-03-1961 - Admitted fact, that departmental proceedings were not concluded before 31-03-1961 - Clear effect of Order D/- 06-01-1961 was that service of respondent came to an end on 31-03-1961 - Service came to an end not because retirement was automatic but because State Govt. had itself fixed date up to which service of respondent would be retained - State Govt. made no further order before 31-03-1961, but about a month thereafter passed an order on 09-05-1961 extending service of respondent for a further period of three months with effect from 01-04-1961 - State Govt. had no jurisdiction to pass such an order on 09-05-1961 - State Govt. could not by unilateral action create a fresh contract of service to take effect from 01-04-1961 - If State Govt. wished to continue service of respondent for a further period, State Govt. should have issued a notification before 31-03-1961 - Held, action of State Govt. not proper - Appeal dismissed.
