Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North v Lakhiram Ramdas

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1967 SC 338; [1962] 44 ITR 726

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1) (a) - Question of Fact and Law - Respondent, who was the assessee, carried on a cloth business - Assessee's income was derived from his cloth business carried on at different places - Separate sets of accounts were maintained for the head office as well as for the two branches - After the completion of the said assessment, the Income-tax Officer concerned received information that the assessee had purchased a draft for certain amount from the Bank and the draft was deposited in a different branch of the bank - In consequence and on the basis of this information, the Income-tax Officer initiated a proceeding u/s. 34(1)(a) of the 1922 Act against the assessee - There was an appeal to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner who confirmed the order of the Income-tax Officer - Then, there was an appeal to the Tribunal - The Tribunal allowed the appeal - Appellant then moved the Tribunal u/s. 66(1) of the Income-tax Act and asked the Tribunal to state a case to HC on the question of law, which arose out of the Tribunal's order - The Tribunal rejected the application u/s. 66(1) - The appellant then moved HC u/s. 66(2) of the 1922 Act - That application, was dismissed by HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of HC was justified - Held, the question whether the assessee had or had not failed to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment was a question of fact - No question of law arose in this case and HC rightly rejected the petition u/s. 66(2) of the 1922 Act - Appeals dismissed.
