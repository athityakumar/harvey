 Messrs Tulsidas Khimji v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 406; AIR 1963 SC 1007; 1962 (4) FLR 453; 1962 (1) LLJ 435; 1964 (1) SCJ 300; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 675

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 14 and 26A - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. 18, r. 1 - (A) Whether income tax deduction can be on basis of notional basis ? - Held, though income-tax deduction has to be made on a notional basis, notional basis must have relevance to law of income tax in respect of firms - Income Tax cannot be deducted at 7 annas in a rupee because that will be on basis of a tax on a corporation - Tax cannot also be deducted on basis of firm being unregistered which income tax authorities are entitled to do in certain circumstances - A major portion of profits cannot be set apart on such a highly notional basis - (B) Whether calculating deduction on actual figures of profits of each partner separately is reasonable ? - Further held, it is reasonable because figures are known and tax of each constituent member of firm can be easily calculated on basis of his share - Firm is not a legal person within meaning of IDA - It is partners of firm

who are employers - That fact has to be taken into account in considering question of income tax, even as in other matters like remuneration, etc, ie, amount of tax payable by each partner, qua business of firm, irrespective of their other sources of income or loss, because notional is quite different from actual, though not wholly disassociated from it - (C) Whether Supreme Court is a regular court of appeal from industrial tribunal? - Further held, Supreme Court does not function as a regular Court of Appeal from Industrial Tribunal - Its function is merely to see that law is being properly administered, in accordance with well settled rules of natural justice - Though comparing sum allowed by way of remuneration to partners to wage bill for year in question amount fixed by tribunal errs on side of inadequacy, Supreme Court will not embark upon a fruitless task of determining a figure which will not have any substratum of solid facts and figures to support its conclusion - (D) What are condition for payment of customary bonus ?- Further held, four so called conditions namely, (1) that payment has been made over an unbroken series of years, (2) that it has been so made for a sufficiently long period; (3) that payment has been made at a uniform rate throughout, and (4) lastly, that it has been paid even in years of loss, and did not depend upon earning of profits, are not really in nature of conditions precedent but are circumstances which have been taken into account - (F) Whether case can be made out during arguments in absence of case not being made out in statement of case? - Further held, if a certain case is not made out in statement of case Supreme Court will not allow case to be made out at time of arguments - Supreme Court has to be very strict in enforcing rules of pleading, as laid down in rules of Supreme Court bearing on question of statement of case of parties - Supreme Court would not ordinarily permit any laxity in matter of pleadings in Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
