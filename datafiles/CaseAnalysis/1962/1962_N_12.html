Gurbaksh Singh v Nikka Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 264; AIR 1963 SC 1917; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 55

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 100 and 101 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 41 - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887, ss. 44 and 37 - (A) Whether findings of lower appellate Court bind High Court in Second appeal? - Held, yes, binds but if finding not based on evidence, it is not binding - (B) Who has onus to show he is ostensible owner of property? - Further held, s. 41 is an exception to general rule that a person cannot confer a better than he has - Onus certainly is on transferee to show that transferor was ostensible owner of property and that he had, after taking reasonable care to ascertain that transferor had power to make transfer, acted in good faith - But where purchaser had knowledge that title of his transferor was in dispute and he had taken a risk in purchasing same it is not possible to hold that he had purchased property in good faiths - (C) Punjab Land Revenue Act - Whether vendor admission correctness of new entry or agreement to have old entry corrected after parting with his interest in property, can bind purchaser? - Held, no, it cannot bind - But where u/s. 37 of Act, Revenue Officer makes entry on facts proved before him and not on basis of admission alone, a presumption of correctness of entry can be taken - Appeal dismissed.
