State of West Bengal v Hemant Kumar Bhattacharjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 385; AIR 1966 SC 1061; 1966 CRLJ 805; 1963 MLJ 613; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 542; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 409 - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949, ss. 4(1), 12 - Notification - Allotment - Jurisdiction -Three respondents allegedly committed offences and were charged in September, 1950 - Though 12 years passed by since then no step was taken beyond issue of notices to them - Such delay was caused by conflicting views which were entertained from time to time about Court having jurisdiction to try respondents, whether it was Court of Chief Presidency Magistrate, Calcutta, or judge of Special Court constituted under the 1949 Act - Impugned judgment of HC held that judge of Special Court had no jurisdiction to proceed with trial but that Chief Presidency Magistrate before whom charge-sheet in respect of offences alleged against respondents was laid had alone jurisdiction to try instant case - Hence, instant appeal by State Govt. - Appellant contended that on construction of relevant statutes, it was special judge who had jurisdiction to try the case - Held, State Government could still allot a case which deals with same offence, arising out of identical facts against same accused to a Special judge would be a patent infringement of terms of s. 12 of the Act and in derogation of protection which that provision was meant to confer - Further, it was not in dispute that case allotted to special Court related to same occurrence, charged same accused with substantially same offences as were involved in proceedings in case before Chief Presidency Magistrate - Appellant therefore would gain no advantage by fresh allotment in July, 1957 or earlier allotments - Order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
