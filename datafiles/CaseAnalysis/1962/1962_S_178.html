State of West Bengal v S. N. Basak

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1963 SC 447; 1963 CRLJ 341; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 154, 156, 439 and 561A - (A) Whether investigation into a cognizable offence can be interfered with when no charge has been laid? - Held, it cannot - S. 154 deals with information in cognizable offences and s. 156 with investigation into such offences and under these sections police has statutory right to investigate into circumstances of any alleged cognizable offence without authority from a Magistrate - This statutory power of police to investigate cannot be interfered with exercise of power under s. 439 or under inherent power of court under s. 561A, when there was no case pending at time excepting that person against whom investigation was started had appeared before court, had surrendered and had been admitted to bail - Appeal allowed.
