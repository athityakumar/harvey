 Cement Marketing Company of India Private Limited and Another v State of Mysore and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 97; AIR 1963 SC 980; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 777; 1963 (14) STC 175

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(2) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 3 - Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 27 - (A) When can a transaction be said to be inter state transaction? - Held, tests which have been laid down to bring a sale within inter - State sales are that transaction must involve movement of goods across border - Transactions are inter State in which as a direct result of such sales goods are actually delivered for consumption in another State - A contract of sale must involve transport of goods from one State to another under contract of sale - Appeal partly allowed.
