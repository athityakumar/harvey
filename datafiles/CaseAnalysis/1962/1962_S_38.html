Commissioner of Income Tax v Shapoorji Pallonji Mistry

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 412; AIR 1962 SC 1086; 2001 (170) CTR 431; [1962] 44 ITR 891; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 171

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 34 - Escaped Assessment - Enhancing Assessment - Material Evidence - Interpretation - Appellant assessed income of respondent which escaped assessment - Respondent challenged action of appellant in adding escaped Income without any convincing material evidence - HC ordered against appellant that escaped income shall not be included in assessment without concrete evidence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 31(3) of the Act favoring respondent in assessing escaped income Valid - Held, provisions of ss. 34 and 33B of the Act by which escaped income can be brought to tax there was reason to think that view expressed uniformly about limits of powers of Appellate Assistant Commissioner to enhance assessment was accepted by legislature as true exposition of words of section - Income tax Act amended several times in last 37 years but no amendment of s. 31(3) of the Act was undertaken to nullify rulings referred - Further, s. 31 could not be differently interrupted - Appeal dismissed.
