Padma Vithoba Chakkayya v Mohd. Multani

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 506; AIR 1963 SC 70; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 269; 1962 (2) KerLR 340; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 229

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17 and 49 - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 144 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 11 - (A) Whether Supreme Court can interfere u/art. 136 if there are concurrent findings of fact by two subsequent lower court? - Held, it was concurrent finding of two subsequent court that plaintiff had attained majority more then three years prior to suit - Supreme Court would not interfere when there is no sufficient reason to differ from concurrent finding of two lower courts - (B) Whether endorsement of cancellation on back of unregistered sale deed registered extinguish title of vendee u/ss. 17 and 49 of Registration Act, 1908? - Further held, endorsement does not extinguish title of vendee and is not admissible in evidence except to show character of possession of vendee - (C) Whether mortgagee by an unilateral act change character of possession of his possession?- Further held, mortgagee cannot by unilateral act change character of his possession - When a person gets into possession of properties as mortgagee, he cannot by any unilateral act or declaration of his prescribe for a title by adverse possession against mortgagor, because in law his possession is that of mortgagor - But if mortgagor and mortgagee subsequently enter into a transaction under which mortgagee is to hold properties thereafter not as a mortgagee but as owner that would be sufficient to start adverse possession against mortgagor if transaction is for any reason inoperative under law - If owner of mortgaged property is a minor at time of entering into transaction of sale with mortgagee, possession of mortgagee which is lawful at its inception cannot become adverse to minor who is in law incapable of giving consent to arrangement entered into with mortgagee - Appeal allowed.
