Bhoju Mandal v Debnath Bhagat

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 382; AIR 1963 SC 1906; 1963 (1) KerLR 391; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 82; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Mortgage By Conditional Sale

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58 - (A) What is the distinction between mortgage by conditional sale and sale with condition of repurchase u/s. 58 of Act? - Held, in a mortgage by conditional sale relationship of debtor and creditor subsists and right to redeem remains with debtor - A sale with a condition of repurchase is an out and out sale whereby owner transfers all his rights in property to purchaser reserving a personal right of repurchase - A document can be determined by ascertaining intention of parties on a consideration of contents of a document and other relevant circumstances - For ascertaining intention of parties under one document a decision on a construction of terms of another document cannot ordinarily afford any guidance unless terms are exactly similar to each other - Appeal dismissed.
