 Khandige Sham Bhat and Another v Agricultural Income Tax Officer, Kasaragod, and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 350; AIR 1963 SC 591; [1963] 48 ITR 21; 1963 KerLJ 196; 1963 (1) SCJ 140; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Kerala Agricultural Income-tax Act, 1950, s.2A - Agricultural lands - Assessment - Infringe - Constitutionally void - Petition filed by different parties were directed against Agricultural Income-tax Officer for a declaration that s.2A of 1950 Act as amended by Kerala Act, 11 of 1959 was constitutionally void and for quashing orders of assessment made by respondent No.1 pursuant to provision agricultural lands of petitioner's family were situate - Petitioner sought to set aside that assessment on ground that section offends art.14 of Constitution and therefore assessment was bad - Whether doctrine of classification could be infringe u/art.14 of Constitution - Held, hold that it was unreasonable and therefore, liable to be struck down - It cannot infringe art.14 of Constitution - Income of assessees that accrued was not taxable - Both cases income that accrued was liable to tax - Crops assessees in Malabar area harvested cannot be a ground for holding that law made an unjust discrimination between persons belonging to same class - Impugned section was a temporary provision intended to apply only for one year to tide over a difficult situation brought about by reorganization of States - Petition dismissed.
