 B. M. Ramaswamy v B. M. Krishnamurthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1963 SC 458; 1964 (2) SCJ 268; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Govts. - Karnataka Village Panchayats And Local Boards Act, 1959, ss. 9, 10 and 13 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 23, 24 and 30 - Karnataka Panchayats and Taluk Boards Election Rules, 1959, r. 3 - Representation of People (Preparation of Electoral Rolls) Rules, 1956, rr. 26 and 27 - (A) Whether an illegal act done by electoral officer in his official capacity can be questioned in a civil court? - Held, it cannot be - Terms of s. 30 of Representation of People Act, 1951 are clear and action of electoral registration officer in including name of person in electoral roll, though illegal, cannot be questioned in a civil court - It could be rectified only in manner prescribed by law, i.e., by preferring an appeal under r. 27 of Rules or by resorting to any other appropriate remedy - Appeal dismissed.
