 Mahendra Lal Jaini v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 1963 SC 1019; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 912

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13(1) and 32 - Uttar Pradesh Land Tenures (Regulation of Transfers) Act, 1952 - (A) Whether doctrine of eclipse applies to post-constitutional laws? - Held, it did not - Constitutionality of a post-constitution legislation such as U P Act 15 of 1952 must be judged on basis of Constitution as it was on date legislation was passed, subject to any retrospective amendment of Constitution - It cannot be judged on basis of Constitution as it stood on date of writ petition challenging validity of such legislation - Doctrine of eclipse would apply to pre-Constitution laws which are governed by art. 13(1) and would not apply to post - Constitution laws which are governed by art. 13(2) - Words 'to extent of' in art. 13 do not import any idea of time - They only import idea that law may be void either wholly or in part and that only such portions will be void as are inconsistent with Part III or have contravened Part III and no more - (B) Whether it is necessary to include name of all persons whose similar right have been violated? - Further held, it is not necessary - If a person has a right to maintain a petition u/art. 32, fact that he has not made another person who would have equal right with him to maintain petition, even a party to petition, would not result in his petition being thrown out on that ground alone - This cannot be a ground to deny him relief in matter of enforcing his fundamental right - Principle applicable to suits for possession, namely that all co-owners must join in a suit to recover property unless law otherwise provides, can have no application by way of analogy to a petition u/art. 32, which is not a suit for possession - (C) Whether subsequent modification of a notification already published in gazette is effective and valid?- Further held, no - Modification is required to be notified in gazette as well if it is to be effective - Appeal partly allowed.
