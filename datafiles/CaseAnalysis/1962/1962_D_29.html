 Shanti Prasad Jain v Director of Enforcement

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 522; AIR 1962 SC 1764; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 231; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 23(1)(a) and 23 D - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 1 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Banker and Customer - Deposit by German firms in German Bank - In name of an Indian resident - Condition that money was to be repaid only towards future supplies of machinery by firms - Prohibition of transactions in foreign exchange by resident in India u/s 4(1) - Questions raised - (A) Constitutionality of s. 23(1)(a) and 23 D of Act - (B) Applicability of Evidence Act to proceedings under FERA and scope of r.3(5) u/s. 27 in light of 24 A of Act - (C) Nature of proceedings u/s. 23 D of Act - (D) Nature of relationship between banker and customer - (E) Liability of bank considering meaning of contingent debts, s. 4(1), s. 23(1)(a) - (F) Materiality of fact, that transaction takes place when such person is in foreign country, for applicability of s. 4(1) - Held, does not offend art.14 and Power of transfer u/s. 23 D is not unguided or arbitrary - Applies only to proceedings in court and r.3(5) has not effect of rendering admissible evidence which was irrelevant or inadmissible under Evidence Act - Proceedings are quasi-criminal in nature and prosecutor has to make out beyond all reasonable doubt that there has been a violation of law - Relationship created is that of debtor, except when there is special arrangement, and irrespective of fact that who deposit amount - Bank is stake-holder and not debtor or trustee, there is no lending within s. 4(1), so penalty u/s. 23(1) (a) cannot be imposed - Fact immaterial - Appeal No. 319 of 1961 allowed ; Appeal No. 320 of 1961 dismissed.
