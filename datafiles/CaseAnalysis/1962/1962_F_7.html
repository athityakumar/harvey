French Motor Car Company, Limited v Workmen

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 291; AIR 1963 SC 1327; 1963 (6) FLR 80; 1962 (2) LLJ 744; 1964 (1) SCJ 335; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 16; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 16

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - S. 10 Industrial Disputes Act No. XIV of 1947 - The Employees Provident Funds Act,(No. XIX of 1952) - (A) Whether the wages should be increased when the wage scale had been revised few years back? - Held, the wage structure can be altered but the wages of dissimilar and different units cannot be compared - (B) What is the system of prevailing dearness allowance? - Held, the system of dearness allowance prevailing before that agreement of 1954 was to be applied - (C) What should be the rate of provident fund contribution in the company? - Held, that the rate of provident fund contribution should remain at 6 1/4 per centum of the gross earnings (basic pay plus dearness allowance) instead of 8 1/3 per centum - Appeal partly allowed and partly dismissed.
