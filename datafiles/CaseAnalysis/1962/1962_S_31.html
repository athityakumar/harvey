Chairman of The Municipal Commissioners of Howrah v Shalimar Wood Products and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 156; AIR 1962 SC 1691; 1962 CRLJ 636; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, ss. 488, 386 - Bengal Municipal Act, 1932, ss.540, 541 - West Bengal Fire Services Act, 1950, s.38 - License - Unauthorized usage of land - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/complainant was chairman of Municipal Committee and respondent was a company which was carrying on manufacture of bobbins, card pine, shuttles etc which included storage of wood and timer at a warehouse - Magistrate passed an order and held that applicability of s. 386 of 1923 Act remains unimpaired wherein in rest of premises was used as a factory or for other purposes and thus respondent was convicted under s. 488 of 1923 Act and was sentenced to a fine of Rs. 250 - Additional Sessions Judge held that s. 38 of 1950 Act does not repeal all 3 clauses of s. 386 but partially repeals a. 386(3) which deals with levy of fees and therefore a license under s. 386(1) would still have to be taken but as premises had already been licensed as a warehouse respondent could not be required to pay any fees under a.386(3) of the 1923 Act- Levy of fees twice over in respect of same premises was prohibited and not that license was not required - sentence of fine was therefore reduced from Rs. 250 to Rs. 10 only - Against Trail court's order appellant took a revision to HC - HC held that where premises were licensed as a warehouse under 1950 Act but a portion of it was used as a workshop Municipal Committee had no longer power to levy any fees for granting license in respect of premises even though there would have been a liability to take out a license - Appellant contended that a. 38 of 1923 Act did not apply to Municipality in instant case because it was governed neither by Calcutta Municipal Act nor by Bengal Municipal Act but by Calcutta Municipal Act- HC held that a. 38 of 1923 Act was applicable to Municipality in instant case - Revision was therefore dismissed and rule was discharged- Against HC's order appellant appealed to SC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 38 of 1950 Act could not affect operation of s. 386 of 1923 Act as it was extended to Municipality in instant case - Held, although a. 38 of 1950 Act extends to whole of Bengal and s. 386 of 1923 Act which applies to Corporation of Calcutta yet had not affected operation of s. 386 of former Act as modified and extended to Municipality of Howrah - Language of s. 386 had been modified to make it appropriate in its application to Municipality of Howrah and for that purpose in place of word 'corporation ' word "'Commissioners" had been substituted - Thus modified it was not a. 386 of Calcutta Municipal Act but a different section - Therefore what s.38 of 1923 Act repeals is s.386 of 1923 and not s. 386 of that as modified and was applied to Municipality of Howrah - Therefore ,contention of appellant was well founded and s. 38 of 1950 Act does not repeal s. 386 as modified and as applicable to Municipality of Howrah -Thus, impugned order of HC was set aside and respondent was convicted of offences charged - Appeal allowed.
