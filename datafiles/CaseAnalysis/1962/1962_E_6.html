 Prem Chand Garg v Excise Commissioner, U.P., Allahabad

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 1963 SC 996; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 142 and 145 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. 35, r. 12 - (A) Whether an order calling upon petitioner to furnish security would retard assertion or vindication of his fundamental right u/art. 32? - Held, yes, it will- Since it would impose a financial obligation on petitioner and if he is not able to comply with it his petition would fail - Order would in some cases effectively bar, and in all cases amount to a hindrance in further progress of petition and cannot be said to aid a fair hearing of petition like order prescribing manner in which paper books have to be prepared, or other steps in connection with petition have to be taken - (B) Whether such order be justified on ground that powers conferred upon Supreme Court u/art. 142(l) of Constitution are wide and cannot be controlled by art. 32? - Further held, a fair construction of art. 142(1) Supreme Court has no power to circumscribe fundamental right guaranteed u/art. 32 - An order which court can make u/art. 142 in order to do complete justice between parties, must not only be consistent with fundamental rights guaranteed by Constitution but it cannot even be inconsistent with substantive provisions of relevant statutory laws - It would not be possible to hold that art. 142 (1) confers upon Supreme Court powers which can contravene provisions of art. 32 - (C) Whether such orders can be made u/art. 145? - Further held, rule providing for making of an order for furnishing security in cases of petitions u/art. 32, where court is satisfied that in case petition fails, petitioner may not be able to pay costs of respondent, cannot also be made u/art. 145(1)(f) - Even assuming that expression 'costs of and incidental to any proceedings in court' used in (f) may cover an order of security if an order for security amounts to a contravention of art. 32, there would be no power to make such a rule u/art. 145(1)(f) - Petition allowed.
