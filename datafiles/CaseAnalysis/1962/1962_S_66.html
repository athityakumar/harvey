Provincial Transport Service v State Industrial Court

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 418; AIR 1963 SC 114; 1962 (5) FLR 294; 1962 (2) LLJ 360; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Central Provinces and Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947, ss. 16, 30 and 31 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Whether dismissed employee was correct in claiming that in absence of an enquiry his dismissal was invalid? - Management produced before Assistant Labour Commissioner papers showing evidence that was claimed to have been recorded during such enquiry - While it would certainly have been better if Enquiring Officer had also put his signature on paper containing statements, that omission cannot possibly be a ground for thinking that he did not hold enquiry - Held, conclusion of Asst. Labour Commr. that 'there are sufficient ground to doubt whether an enquiry was really made' must therefore be held to be perverse - Decision of Asst. Labour Commr. that no enquiry had been held by management amounts therefore to a clear error in law - There was a proper enquiry held against this employee and management dismissed him on finding on that enquiry that two charges against him had been fully proved, and that there was no reason to think that management acted mala fide - Appeal allowed.
