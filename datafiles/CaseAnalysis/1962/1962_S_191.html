Surajnath Ahir and Others v Prithinath Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1963 SC 454; 1963 (2) SCJ 140; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Plaintiffs-respondents sued appellants for recovery of possession of disputed lands and mesne profits as family of defendants did not have any raiyat interest in disputed lands except rehan interest under rehan deed dated 03-07-1906, and that subsequent to redemption of that deed, they had no right to remain in possession and occupation of disputed lands - (A) Whether record of rights supported case of defendants that they were qaimi raiyats? - Held, defendants were in possession of land in suit only as mortgagees as held by Court below and they had no right to possession after mortgage had been redeemed - (B) Whether sale deed of 1915 in favour of respondents did not include land in suit and plaintiffs are estopped from taking any action against defendants who had been in possession for long? - Further held, on a review of sale deed and as held by High Court, land in suit which is included in milkiat share was not excepted for sale - Only property excluded from sale was chaukidari chakran land - Long possession of appellants therefore does not estop respondents from recovering possession from them - (C) Whether suit is barred by limitation? - Further held, suit was instituted within 12 years of redemption of mortgage deed and is not therefore barred by limitation - (D) Whether plaintiffs-respondents had no subsisting title to evict appellants in view of provisions of Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950? - Further held, land in suit cannot deemed to be settled with respondents by State in accordance with provisions of s. 6 of Act - In absence of any such settlement, no rights over land in suit remained in respondents after date of vesting, all their rights having vested in State by virtue of s. 3(1) of Act - Respondents lost their right to recover possession from appellants, even if they were trespassers, on their estate vesting in State, by virtue of ss. 3 and 4 of Act and thereafter, they had no subsisting right to recover possession from appellants - Right to possession now vests in State - Appeal allowed.
