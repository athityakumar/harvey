Honorary Secretary, South India Millowners Association and Others v Secretary, Coimbatore District Textile Workers' Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1962 SC 1221; 1962 (4) FLR 262; 1962 (1) LLJ 223; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 926

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Printing, Rehabilitation, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Available Surplus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25E - (A) Which factors are to be taken into account in order to determine life of machinery for rehabilitation in order to arrive at surplus available bonus ? - Held, it is to be determined in light of evidence adduced by parties - Supreme Court would not be justified in interfering with conclusion of tribunal that said life can be reasonably estimated at 25 years - Once probable or estimated life of machinery is determined, there is no scope for making any additions to number of years thus determined on any extraneous considerations , as to financial position of employer or availability of machinery - If amount awarded for rehabilitation for any given year is not utilised for that purpose, same may be taken into account next year - Tribunal was in error in making further additions to estimated life of textile machinery - Divisor must be adopted on basis of finding that 25 year was estimated life of machinery and no more - It would not be right to insist that an employer who purchases second hand machinery must rehabilitate it by purchasing second hand machinery in turn - It would be obviously unreasonable and unjust - (B) How to determine whether two undertakings by same company are one unit for sake of determining bonus? - Further held, to be determined in light of facts in each case - Relevant factors - Unity of ownership and management - Geographical location - Functional integrity are an important factor - Appeal allowed.
