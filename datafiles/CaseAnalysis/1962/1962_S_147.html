Narain Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 254; 1963 ALJ 330; 1964 CRLJ 730; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 342 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 304 - Narain Singh and his three nephews were tried for offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC, 1860 in furtherance of their common intention caused the death of one Bachan Singh by making a murderous assault on him - in the evening of when Narain Singh and his three nephews were irrigating their field, Bachan Singh diverted the flow of water into his own filed - a quarrel broke between them - appellants attacked deceased with a kaholi, caused him serious injuries - At the time of the assault nephews were armed with a spear, a Kaholi, and a stick - Sessions Judge acquitted three nephews and convicted Narain Singh u/s. 304 Part II IPC, 1860 five years rigorous imprisonment - Appeal to High Court - HC agreed with Sessions Court that evidence was insufficient to establish the case for the prosecution, reduced the sentence, 3 years RI - Special leave Narain Singh has appealed to this Court - Held, the prosecution case, did not establish affirmatively that Narain Singh had done any act which rendered him liable for the offence of murder - His responsibility, arose only out of the plea raised by him: if it amounted to confession of guilt the court could convict him relying upon that plea, but if it amounted to admission of facts and raised a plea of justification, the court could not proceed to deal with the case, evidence did not warrant the plea of justification - Courts below were in error in convicting Narain Singh u/s. 304 Part II IPC, 1860 - We set aside the conviction, Narain Singh be acquitted - Appeal allowed.
