S. S. Munna Lal v S. S. Rajkumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 547; AIR 1962 SC 1493; 1963 JabLJ 210; 1962 MPLJ 977; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 418; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Womens' Right to Property Act, 1937 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14, 15 and 16 - Dispute between parties arose in a suit for partition of joint family property - Saheblal son of Munnalal filed Suit for a decree of partition and separate possession of his 1/12 th share in joint family property - Padamchand had died before enactment of Hindu Womens' Right to Property Act, 1937, and his widow, Bhuribai, could not claim by virtue of that Act a share in property of family - Widow and her son Rajkumar, pleaded that Rajkumar, as a son adopted by Bhuribai to Padam Chand became a coparcener in joint family and entitled to a share in property and accretions thereto - It was submitted by contesting defendants and Bhuribai had no authority express or implied from her husband Padam Chand to adopt a son and that adoption of Rajkumar as a son without such authority was invalid - (A) Whether Trial Court was in error in delegating to a Commr. judicial function, such as, ascertainment of property to be divided and effecting partition? - Held, Commr. was merely called upon to make proposals for partition, on which parties would be heard, and Court would adjudicate upon such proposals in light of decree, and contentions of parties - By so authorising Commr., Trial Court did not abdicate its functions to Commr. - (B) Whether in absence of express authority from her husband, Bhuribai could not adopt a son? - Further held, custom that a sonless Jain widow was competent to adopt a son to her husband without his permission or consent of his kinsmen, has been sufficiently established - This custom governs parties, because custom has become a part of law applicable to Jains in India (except in Madras and Punjab) by a long and uninterrupted course of acceptance - (C) Whether interest of Khilonabai, grandmother of Munnalal and Ramchand under preliminary decree became her absolute property by virtue of s. 14 of Hindu Succession Act, 1956 and on her death it devolved upon her grandsons? - Further held, s. 16 which prescribes order of succession and manner of distribution among heirs of a Hindu female provides by Rule - Among heirs specified in s. 15(1), those in one entry shall be preferred to those in any succeeding entry, and those included in same entry shall take simultaneously - If share declared by preliminary decree in favour of Khilonabai is property possessed by her at date of her death, it should devolve upon her grandsons, to exclusion of Rajkumar adopted son of Padam Chand - Appeal partly allowed.
