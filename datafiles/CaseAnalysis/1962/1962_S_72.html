 Roop Chand v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 274; AIR 1963 SC 1503; 1963 (65) PLR 576; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 539

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948, s. 42 - Whether order made u/s. 42 was without jurisdiction and affects fundamental rights of petitioner? - Power to hear appeal was delegated to Assistant Director u/s. 41(1) - An agent exercises no powers of his own but only powers of his principal therefore, an order passed by an officer on delegation to him u/s. 41(1) of power of Govt. u/s. 21(4), is for purposes of Act, an order of Govt. - East Punjab Holdings Act, 1948 does not empower State Govt. to interfere with an order passed by an officer to whom power to hear appeals has been delegated - Change in allotment may affect right of number of persons which cannot be adjusted in appeal - Held, whole scheme of consolidation may fail as there would be no remedy in civil court - Appeal dismissed.
