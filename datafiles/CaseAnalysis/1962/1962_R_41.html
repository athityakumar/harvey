 Dahya Lal and Others v Rasul Mohammed Abdul Rahim

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1964 SC 1320; 1963 (65) BomLR 328; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Tenancy & Agricultural Land Act, 1948, s. 29 - Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1947 - Mortgage - Redemption - Eviction - Restoration of possession - Validity - By deed dt. 24-7-1891, ancestors of appellants mortgaged land admeasuring 11 acres and 20 gunthas to one Umiyashanker with possession shortly after mortgage - Mortgagee inducted tenant/respondent on land - Appellants as owners of equity redemption applied to court constituted under the 1947 Act for adjustment of debt due under deed dt. 24-7-1891 and for redemption of land mortgaged - Award was made by compromise between parties that Rs. 3,000 were due to mortgagee under said deed and land in dispute was in possession of tenant of mortgagee and that mortgagor had right to take possession of land from tenant - In execution of award, tenant was evicted - Respondent made application u/s. 29 of the 1948 Act for restoration of possession - Mahalkari rejected application and the same was confirmed in appeal by District Deputy Collector - On further appeal, it was confirmed by Tribunal - Respondent filed petition before HC, which ordered that possession of land be restored to him - Hence, instant appeal- Whether respondent was entitled to possession - Held, tenant of mortgagee in possession if inducted on the land in ordinary course of management under authority derived from mortgagor and so long as mortgage subsists, even under ordinary law he was not liable to be evicted by mortgagor - It appeared that Legislature by restricting exclusion to mortgagees in possession from law of deemed tenants intended that tenant lawfully inducted by mortgagee should on redemption of mortgage be deemed to be tenant of mortgagor - Therefore, HC was right in holding that respondent was entitled to claim protection of the 1948 Act as deemed tenant - Appeal dismissed.
