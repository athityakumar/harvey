Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Manilal Dhanji

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 331; AIR 1963 SC 433; 1963 CRLJ 494; [1962] 44 ITR 876; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 902

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income -Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1922, s. 16(3)(b) - (A) Whether an assessee can be taxed on income from a trust fund for benefit of his minor child u/s. 16(3)(b)? - Held, an assessee can only be taxed on income from a trust fund for benefit of his minor child, provided that in year of account, minor child derive some benefit under trust deed - Either he receives income, or income accrues to him, or he has a beneficial interest in income in relevant year of account - But if no income accrues, or no benefit is derived and there is no income at all (so far as minor child is concerned), then it is not consistent with scheme of s. 16 that income or benefit which is nonexistent so far as minor child is concerned, will be included in income of his father - (B) Whether settler intended to impose a trust by direction or whether direction was only motive of gift? - Further held, Court does regard as binding and obligatory and does enforce a direction or trust in favour of third parties if such a binding obligation can be clearly ascertained from document - (C) Whether beneficiary trustee is a trustee within meaning of s. 41? - Further held, in respect of sum received, by assessee, assessee was a trustee, within meaning of s. 41 of Income Tax Act, appointed under a trust declared by a duly executed instrument in writing and as such trustee he had right to contend that his assessment in respect of money received by him not as beneficiary but as a trustee could only be made under first proviso to s. 41 - Appeal dismissed.
