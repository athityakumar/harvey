 Commssioner of Income Tax, Madras and Another v S.V. Angidi Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1962 SC 970; [1962] 44 ITR 739; 1963 (2) SCJ 81; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 640

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 28 and 44 - (A) Whether imposition of penalty u/s. 28 after dissolution of firm is illegal? - Held, it is not illegal - Penalty provisions under s. 28 are in event of default contemplated by cl. (a),(b) or (c), applicable in course of assessment of a registered firm - If a registered firm is exposed to liability of paying penalty, by committing any of defaults contemplated by cl. (a),(b) or (c), by virtue of s. 44, notwithstanding dissolution of firm assessment proceedings are liable to be continued against registered firm, as if it has not been dissolved - (B) Whether ITO has power u/s. 28 to impose penalty in course of assessment of a firm even if firm stood at date of order dissolved by death of one of its partners? - Further held, yes it has - S. 44 sets up machinery for assessing tax liability of a firm which has discontinued its business - Expression 'assessment' in different section of chap. IV is not used merely in sense of computation of income - When s. 44 declares that partners or members of firm shall be jointly and severally liable to assessment, it refers to liability to computation of income under s. 23 as well as application of procedure for declaration and imposition of tax liability and machinery for enforcement thereof - (C) Whether a firm is a person? - Held, a firm is manifestly a body of individuals and would therefore fall within definition of 'person' contained in s. 2 (a) r/w s. 3(42) of General Clauses Act, and may be exposed to an order for payment of penalty in circumstances set out in cls. (a), (b) and (c) of s. 28 of Income Tax Act - To imposition of a penalty, liability to pay tax by person against whom penalty is sought to be imposed is not a condition precedent - (D) Whether penalty can be imposed only when ITO is satisfied with proceedings? - Further held, power to impose penalty under s. 28 depends upon satisfaction of ITO in course of proceedings under Act - It cannot be exercised if he is not satisfied about existence of conditions specified in cl. (a), (b) or (c) before proceedings are concluded - Proceeding to levy penalty has, however, not to be commence by ITO before completion of assessment proceedings by ITO - Satisfaction before conclusion of proceeding under Act and not issue of a notice or initiation of any step for imposing penalty is a condition for exercise of jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
