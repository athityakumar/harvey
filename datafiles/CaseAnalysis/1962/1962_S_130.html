 M. R. Balaji and Others v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 272; AIR 1963 SC 649; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 15(4) and 46 - Whether provision of reservation of seats for advancement of socially and educationally backward classes of citizens in State of Mysore u/art. 15(4) of Constitution is valid? - Object of making a special provision for advancement of castes or communities, there specified, is to carry out directive principle enshrined in art. 46 - Held, art. 15(4) is an enabling provision and it does not impose an obligation, but merely leaves it to discretion of appropriate Govt. to take suitable action, if necessary - Petitions allowed.
