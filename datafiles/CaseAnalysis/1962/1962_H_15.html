Workmen of Hindusthan Motors v Hindusthan Motors and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 68; 1962 (5) FLR 350; 1962 (2) LLJ 352

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Grading & scaling - Determination - (A) Whether tribunal justified in not applying grades and scales as awarded by Third Major Engineering Tribunal? - Unexplained why respondent failed in executing scheme - Financial condition of respondent company is satisfactory - Held, grades and scales of pay as provided by Scheme of Third Major Engieering Tribunal to be applied - (B) Whether omitted to provide a sliding scale for allowance by Tribunal, is justifiable? - Further held, no proper reason has been given in award - Sliding scale to be introduced to neutralise increase of cost of living - (C) Whether tribunal erred in not applying scheme of production bonus? - No demand was made in written statement for production - No case for extension of incentive scheme was made - Further held, not possible to give appellants any relief in this matter - Appeal partly allowed.
