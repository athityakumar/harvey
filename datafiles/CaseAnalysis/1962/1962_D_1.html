 Bhopal Sugar Industries Limited, Madhya Pradesh v D. P. Dube, Sales Tax Officer, Bhopal Region

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 386; AIR 1964 SC 1037; 1963 MahLJ 493; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 488; 1963 (14) STC 406

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Madhya Bharat Sales of Motor Spirit Taxation Act, 1953 - Whether High Court had erred in holding appellant liable to pay sales tax in respect of motor spirits and lubricants consumed for its vehicles? - Held, HC was in error in proceeding to decide petition on a ground which was not set up in affidavit of Sales Tax Officer - Levy and collection of sales tax on motor spirits and lubricants consumed by Company cannot be regarded as illegal unless it is found that goods were in ownership of Company, and question whether goods consumed belonged to Company cannot be decided by this Court - It will of course be open to Company in an appeal properly filed before taxing authorities to contend that under terms of agreement with Caltex (India) Ltd., the Company is owner of goods received by it and that on that account consumption of those goods by it for its own vehicles did not amount to sale - Appeal dismissed.
