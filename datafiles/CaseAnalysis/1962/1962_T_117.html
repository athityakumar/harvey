 Trust Mai Lachmi Sialkoti Bradri v Chairman, Amritsar Improvement Trust

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1963 SC 976; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Punjab Development of Damaged Areas Act, 1951, Punjab Damaged Areas Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Arbitration & ADR - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 A - (A)Whether arbitrator can be considered as a tribunal?- Held, fact that arbitrator under s. 10 A is not exactly in same position as a probate arbitrator, does not mean that he is a Tribunal under art. 136 - Not invested with State's inherent judicial power - Appointed by parties and power to decide dispute between parties who appoint him is derived by him from agreement of parties and from no other source - His appointment once made by parties is recognised by s. 10 A and after his appointment he is clothed with certain powers and has thus, no doubt, some of trappings of a court, does not mean that power of adjudication which he is exercising is derived from State - Character of an adjudicating body is not satisfied - From decision of an arbitrator under s. 10, a special leave to appeal to Supreme Court under art. 136 (1) cannot be granted - From mere fact that a writ under art. 226 may lie against award pronounced by an arbitrator acting under s. l0 A it cannot be concluded that such an arbitrator is a 'tribunal ' within art. 136 (1) because art. 226 is in a sense wider than art. 136 - (B) Whether a scheme framed u/s. 3 of Punjab Development and Damaged Areas Act, 1951, in respect of area, which was not declared to be 'damaged' area under 1951 or 1949 Acts, is without jurisdiction?- Further held, yes, it is - Fact that area was declared to be 'damaged area' under temporary 1947 Act is of no avail - It cannot be contended that validity of Scheme cannot be challenged by reason of provisions of s. 5 (4) of 1951 Act which imparted a conclusive effect as to legality of scheme which had received approval of Govt. and had been published under s. 5 (3) of Act - There is total lack of jurisdiction on part of Improvement Trust or Govt. to frame a scheme for this area - Appeal dismissed.
