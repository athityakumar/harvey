 State of West Bengal v S. K. Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 373; AIR 1963 SC 255; 1963 CRLJ 252; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 111

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance, 1944, ss. 12(1) and 13(3) - Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance, 1943, s. 10 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20(1) - Indian Penal Code, s. 53 - (A) Whether it is necessary for court to record a finding as to amount of money procured u/s. 12(1) of Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance, 1944 ? - Held, s. 12(1) requires court to record a finding as to amount of money or value of other property procured by accused before it by means of offence for which he is being tried - It is not necessary that court when it gives a finding as to amount of money or value of other property procured by means of offence should say in so many words in passing order that it is making that finding on a representation under s. 12(1) - (B) Whether finding given for purpose of s. 10 in determining amount of money or value of other property procured by offender be taken as a finding under s. 12(1) of 1944 Ordinance? - Further held, there is no reason why finding given for purpose of s. 10 should not also be taken as a finding under s. 12(1) of 1944 Ordinance - Result of two findings is exactly same, only difference being that under s. 10 of 1943 ordinance, Court may do this suo moto, while under s. 12(1) of 1944 ordinance it has to be done on representation made by prosecution - Result in either case is that a finding as to amount of money or value of property procured by offender by means of other offence is given - (C) Whether forfeiture u/s. 13(3) of 1944 ordinance requires final judgment of criminal Court as to finding of money or value of property in pursuance of s. 12?- Further held, s. 13(3) requires that it should be in final judgment of criminal Court as to finding of amount of money or value of property in pursuance of s. 12 - So long as criminal Court trying an offender has given a finding as to amount of money or value of other property procured by means of offence in judgment, that is sufficient compliance with s. 12(1) and requirement therein that it should be on representation of prosecution is a mere formality - (D) Whether criminal court u/s. 12(1) of ordinance of 1944 will determine value of property other than that procured by offence? - Further held, where property attached under s. 3 of 1944 ordinance is property purchased with embezzled money or something like that, criminal court will not determine value of such property - Only amount of money procured by means of offence or value of property procured by means of offence has to be determined by criminal Court - (E) Whether forfeiture provided in s. 13(3) in case of offences is a punishment u/art. 20 of constitution? - Further held, it is not - S. 13(3) is a speedier method of realising Govt. money or property as compared to a suit which Govt. could bring for realising money or property and is not punishment or penalty within meaning of art. 20(1) - (F) Whether forfeiture by District Judge under s. 13(3) be equated to forfeiture of property which is provided in s. 53 of IPC? - Further held, a sentence of fine imposed u/s. 10 of 1943 ordinance as amended in 1945, has nothing to do with amount to be forfeited u/s. 13(3) of 1944 ordinance and simply because that fine was imposed after taking into account amount embezzled would make no difference so far as s. 13(3) is concerned - Appeal allowed.
