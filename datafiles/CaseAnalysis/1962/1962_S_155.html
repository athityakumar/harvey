Pyare Lal Bhargava v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 280; AIR 1963 SC 1094; 1963 ALJ 458; 1963 CRLJ 178; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 689; 1988 (32) TTJ 282

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Wrongful

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 24 - (A) What is meaning of word appears in u/s. 24 of Evidence Act? - Held, a confession would be irrelevant if it should appear to court to have been caused by any inducement, threat or promise - Appropriate meaning of word 'appears' is 'seems' - It imports a lesser degree of probability than proof - Standard of a prudent man is not completely displaced, but stringent rule of proof is relaxed - On evidence and circumstances in a particular case it may appear to court that there was a threat, inducement or promise, though said fact is not strictly proved - It is not possible or advisable to lay down an inflexible standard for guidance of courts - (B) What is meaning of threat u/s. 24? - Further held, threat etc must proceed from person in authority and threat etc must cause reasonable belief in mind of confessing accused that he would get advantage etc - (C) Whether Chief Secretary of State Govt. is an authority within meaning of s. 24? - Further held, yes, he is - (D) Whether corroboration is necessary in case of retracted confession? - Further held, it is necessary - A retracted confession may form legal basis of a conviction if court is satisfied that it was trustworthy and was voluntarily made - A court shall not base a conviction on such a confession without corroboration - (E) Whether temporarily deprivation of property from owners possession amounts to offence u/s. 378? - Further held, yes, it does - Not in legal possession of file - To commit theft one need not take movable property permanently out of possession of another with intention not to return it to him - A temporary period of deprivation or dispossession of property of another causes loss to other - Appeal dismissed.
