 Ladli Prasad Jaiswal v Karnal Distillery Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 435; AIR 1963 SC 1279; 1963 (2) ALT(SC) 228; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 593; 1964 (2) SCJ 12; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 270

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Examination Of Accounts, Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Companies Act - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(a) and art. 133(1)(b) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o. 6, r. 4 - Appeal to courts competence of court to decide matter- (A) Whether a 'Court immediately below' includes a Judge of High Court trying a proceeding in exercise of original jurisdiction? - A Court subordinate to HC is a Court Subject to superintendence of HC, whereas a Court immediately below is Court from whose decision appeal has been filed - cl. 10 of Letters Patent (Lahore) confers an unqualified right of appeal to HC from judgment of a single Judge - Held, expression, 'Court immediately below' must mean Court from decision of which an appeal has been filed to HC, whether such court is a single Judge of HC, or a court Subject to superintendence of HC - (B)Whether when judgment of single Judge is reversed, an appeal preferred to Supreme Court with certificate granted by HC under art. 133(l)(a) and (b) is competent? - Further held, where judgment of HC delivered in a Letters Patent appeal filed against appellate judgment of a single Judge of same HC in a suit brought under provisions of Companies Act, involves some Claim or question respecting property of value exceeding twenty thousand rupees and judgment of since Judge is reversed, an appeal preferred to Supreme Court with certificate granted by HC under art. 133(l)(a) and (b) is competent- (C) Whether HC was justified in deciding that plaintiff failed to prove that resolutions were not obtained by exercise of undue influence? - Further held, decisions of District Court and Division Bench of HC given against plaintiff, suffered from serious infirmities in that they wrongly placed onus of proof upon plaintiff, and reached a conclusion that plaintiff failed to prove that resolutions were not obtained by exercise of undue influence- Appeal allowed.
