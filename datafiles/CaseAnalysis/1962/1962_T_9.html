Seth Banarsi Das v Cane Commissioner and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 388; AIR 1963 SC 1417; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 760; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Election

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1940, ss. 8, 9 and 10 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - United Provinces Sugar Factories Control Rules, 1938, rr. 23(1) and 23(2) - United Provinces Sugar Factories Control Act, 1938, s. 18(2) - (A) Whether it is mandatory to follow a given provision of law at all times? - Held, whole scope and purpose of statute under consideration must be regarded and general rule is, that an absolute enactment must be obeyed or fulfilled exactly, but it is sufficient if a directory enactment be obeyed or fulfilled substantially - (B) Whether an arbitration clause in an agreement is enforceable only if agreement is signed by parties? - Held, arbitration clause in agreement is enforceable, if agreed to, even without signature of party as it is settled law that to constitute an arbitration agreement in writing - It is not necessary that it should be signed by parties and it is sufficient if terms are reduced to writing and agreement of parties thereto is established - (C) Whether a slight variation in form would render an agreement invalid? - Held, where conditions set out have all been incorporated in agreement and where provision of law is directory, failure to execute same in prescribed form will not invalidate it - (D) Whether u/r. 23 of Act Cane Commr. is given arbitrary power to discriminate between one case and another in as much as he can decide one case himself and refer another to arbitration and whether rule thus offends equal protection clause contained in art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, where there are two procedures one for every one and other if disputants voluntarily agree to follow it, there can be no discrimination because discrimination can only be found to exist if election is with someone else who can exercise his will arbitrarily normal mode is decision by Cane Commr. with a possibility of arbitration by agreement of parties - Thus rule cannot be said to be offending art. 14 because Cane Commr. may himself decide a dispute - Appeal dismissed.
