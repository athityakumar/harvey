 A. V. Thomas and Company Limited v Deputy Commissioner Of agricultural Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 307; AIR 1964 SC 569; 1963 KLT 1064; 1964 (1) SCJ 373; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 608; 1963 (14) STC 363; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 286 - Sales were effected outside state - Imposition of sales tax - Constitutionality u/art. 286(1) - Whether sale was 'outside' or 'inside sale'? - Held, Explanation to art. 286(1)(a) explains what a sale outside State is - According to that Explanation a fiction is created as between two States, one where goods are delivered for consumption in that State and other where title in goods passes and former is treated as situs of taxable event to exclusion of latter - Therefore where Explanation applies difficulty about situs is resolved but in a case like present one difficulty still remains because explanation does not operate in sense that rival States claiming to tax same taxable event are not States of delivery for consumption in that State and those where title in goods passes - Reffered Indian Copper Corporation Ltd v State of Bihar ([1961] 2 S C R 2761961 (2) SCR 2761960 Indlaw SC 199, 286,) - Where property in goods passed within a State as a direct result of sale, sale transaction is not outside State for purpose of art. 286(1)(a) unless Explanation operates - Sale was an 'outside sale' and cannot be said to be an 'inside sale' qua Travancore Cochin because title passed at Fort Cochin which is in State of Madras - Money was paid there and delivery order was also received there even though actual physical delivery of goods was made at a Willingdon Island in State of Tranvancore Cochin - Fiction created by Explanation to, art. 286(1)(a) is inapplicable because there was no delivery as a direct result of sale for purpose of consumption in any particular State - Question of goods which were exported out of India from Willingdon Island - It cannot be said that there was a sale inside 'State of Travancore Cochin because same considerations will apply - Appeal allowed.
