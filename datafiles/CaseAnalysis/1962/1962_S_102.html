 Karimtharuvi Tea Estates Limited, Kottayam and Another v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 474; AIR 1963 SC 760; [1963] 48 ITR 83; 1963 KerLJ 344; 1963 (1) KerLR 283; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Agriculture & Agri Products - Income Tax Act, s. 59, rr. 24 and 25 - Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1961, ss. 2 explanation 2 and s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366 - (A)Whether any state legislature can determine taxes on agricultural income from tea plantations as its own discretion - Held, power of state legislature - For determining taxes on agricultural income - Limited by r. 24 of Income-Tax Rules, 1922 - (1) How is income from tea derived - Further held, agricultural income is derived - Partly from land by agriculture and partly from business - Therefore proportions of both are considered for entire income - Hence u/s. 59 r/w r. 23 and r. 24 of Indian Income Tax Rules, 1922, such income is calculated - (B) Whether deductions can be allowed for expenses incurred for maintenance of immature tea plantations as allowed under Income Tax Act, 1922 - Further held, money spent for maintenance of immature plants is running expenditure , not capital expenditure - Calculated in accounting year - Therefore no deduction is permissible - (1)Whether state legislature can enact a legislation in contravention to Income Tax Act? - Further held, provisions under Income Tax Act and its rules - Controls provisions of Agricultural Income Tax Act - Enacted by state legislature - Petition allowed.
