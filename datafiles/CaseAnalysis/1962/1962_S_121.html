Hafiz Din Mohammad Haji Abdulla v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 108; 1962 (13) STC 292

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Water; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - CP and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 23 - Agreement of sale - Assessees are bidi merchants at Kamptee, despatched bidis to merchants at diverse places in India, each merchant executing an agreement in form of a letter addressed to assessees - Whether said agreement creates a relation of principals and agent or vendors and purchaser between assessees and merchant to whom bidis were despatched? - As per agreement between parties, goods are to be sold at a price fixed by supplier, prices are liable to be altered at instance of supplier, person to whom goods are supplied is to receive a fixed remuneration for his exertion, and is liable to remit price only after sale is effected - Held, relation is of principal and agent and not of vendors and purchaser - Appeal allowed.
