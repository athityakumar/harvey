Oudh Sugar Mills Ltd v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 569; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finding

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 83 r/w r. 226 - Alleged that Appellant had not kept correct accounts of amount of mixed juice which went into production of sugar - Collector of Central Excise ordered Appellant to pay additional duty on 11,606 maunds of sugar - Further, penalty of Rs. 2,000/- Imposed for not maintaining accounts correctly - Held, finding that 11,606 maunds of sugar were not accounted for by appellant has been arrived at without any tangible evidence and is based only on inferences involving unwarranted assumptions - Finding thus vitiated by an error of law - Further held, registers were not properly maintained as required by r. 83 is also an inferential finding based upon calculations made by Assistant Chemical Examiner - Appellant not liable to pay additional duty - Order accordingly.
