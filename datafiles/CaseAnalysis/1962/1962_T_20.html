 Jia Lal v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 507; AIR 1962 SC 1781; 1962 AllCriR 431; 1963 CRLJ 1; 1962 PLR 1051; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Indian Arms Act, 1878, ss. 19(f), 20 and 29 - Whether s. 29 of Act is unconstitutional and void as contravening art. 14, in that it requires sanction for prosecution for offences under Act, when they are committed in some areas but not in others? - Held, the fact that s. 29 is a procedural and not a substantive enactment is sufficient to turn the scale heavily in favour of the State - On a consideration of the scheme of the Act, and it provisions, s. 29 is severable from the other portions of the Act, and that its invalidity does not affect the validity of s. 19 - Further, it cannot be said that the State had launched prosecutions under the Act, some with, and others without sanction, and that was discrimination hit by art. 14 - Appeals dismissed.
