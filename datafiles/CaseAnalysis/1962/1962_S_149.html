 Sukhbans Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1962 SC 1711; 1962 ALJ 883; 1963 (1) LLJ 671; 1962 (64) PLR 1008; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 416

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850 - Govenment of India Act, 1919, s. 96-B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Punjab Civil Services (Exclusive Branch) Rules, 1930, rr. 5, 22, 23 and 24 - Whether order of reversion to previous appointment amounts to violation of art. 311? - Held, only reasonable inference which can be drawn from facts is that, Govt. wanted to punish appellant for what it thought was misconduct on his part and therefore, reverted him - Omission of Govt. to give reasons for reversion does not make action any less a punishment but as requirements of art. 311(2) were not fulfilled, as they ought to have been, Govt. wanted to give reversion appearance of an act done in ordinary course entailing no penal consequences - Circumstances clearly show that action of Govt. was malafide and reversion was by way of punishment for misconduct without complying with provisions of art. 311(2) - Appeal allowed.
