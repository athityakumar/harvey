 State of Rajasthan v Mst. Vidhyawati and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 557; AIR 1962 SC 933; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 70; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 989

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution -Tort - Constitution of India, 1950, art.300 - Extent of vicarious liability of Govt. for tortious acts of its employees, acting in course of their employment - (A) whether jeep car, rash and negligent driving of which led to claim in suit was being maintained 'in exercise of sovereign power' and not as part of any commercial activity of State? - View of HC upheld - Held, State liable - State is in no better position in so far as it supplies cars and keeps drivers for its civil service - Not a sovereign purpose for official duties - (B) Whether vicarious liability of state dwells in art.300? - Further held, it is not correct to argue that provisions of art.300 are wholly out of way for determining liability of appellant State - S. 32 of Govt. of India Act, 1915 - S. 32 has specific reference to 'remedies', and has provided that remedies against Secretary of State in Council shall be same as against East Indian Company, if Govt. of India Act of 1858, and Govt. of India Act, 1915, had not been passed - It will thus be seen that by chain of enactments, beginning with Act of 1858 and ending with Constitution apply Govt. of a State to same extent, as they applied to East India Company - Extent of liability of East India Co. - Peninsular and Oriental Steam Navigation Co. v Secretary of State for India [Decided in 1861 and reported in Newspaper 'Englishman' of 23-10-1861, relied on - That Secretary of State for India was subject to same liabilities as those which previously attached to East India Co. - East India Co. itself could not have claimed any such immunity as was available to be sovereign - No distinction to be made out between liability under contract and other wrongs - India under constitution is a welfare stare - Functions not confine to maintenance of law and order - But also as employer-functions - Position of Govt. of India akin to that of East India Co. - Sovereign - (C) Whether u/art. 300 of Constitution, State of Rajasthan, was not liable as corresponding Indian State would not have been liable if case had arisen before Constitution came into force? - Further held, state of Rajasthan has not shown that its predecessor was not liable by any rule of positive enactment or common law - Appeal dismissed.
