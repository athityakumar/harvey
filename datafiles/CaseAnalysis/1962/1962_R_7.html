R. Viswanathan v Rukn-Ul-Mulk Syed Abdul Wajid

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1963 SC 1; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Foreign Country, Charities, Competency

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 15(5) and 115 - Whether High Court was in error in disposing of case as though application in revision made to HC was an application u/s. 115, CPC when application was really an application u/s. 15(5) of Act? - Held, it is manifest that scope of s. 15(5) of Act is not same as scope of s. 115, CPC - Scope of s. 15(5) of Act is wider and is not confined to questions of jurisdiction only - But even if learned Judge of HC was in error in treating application as one u/s. 115, CPC, fact still remains that he affirmed findings of learned District Judge and one of these findings was that landlord did not require building to carry out repair work which was suggested by Municipal Committee - Municipal Committee had suggested a very simple work of repair, namely, filling up of doorway in pillar so that pillar might be one solid wall to support screen - It has not been seriously disputed before us that such repairs could be easily carried out without necessity of asking respondent to vacate building - Appeal dismissed.
