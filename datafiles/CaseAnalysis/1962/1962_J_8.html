Jai Development v State of Punjab (And Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 197; AIR 1963 SC 612; 1963 CRLJ 495; 1964 (2) SCJ 272; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 99, 100, 109, 148, 149, 302 and 326 - Unlawful assembly, common object to commit offence of rioting while armed with deadly weapons - Two appellants with four others alleged to have committed offences u/ss. 148, 302 and 326 r/w s. 149 IPC - Charges against two, not proved beyond reasonable doubt, so acquitted - Five accused persons convicted of offence u/s. 326/149 - High Court set aside conviction of three accused persons and convicted appellants u/s. 302, IPC - Whether appellants could be said, to have, had a right of private defence? - Held, both victims were at a long distance from appellants when they were shot dead is conclusion of HC, no reason to interfere with it - If, at time when two appellants used their rifles against their respective victims standing at considerable distances from them, all villagers had run away, there was obviously no threat continuing and so, right of private defence had clearly and unambiguously come to an end - HC was right in holding that appellants guilty of murder u/s. 302 IPC - Ends of justice would be met if sentence of death imposed on two appellants set aside and instead, an order is passed directing that they should suffer imprisonment for life - Conviction confirmed - Sentence reduced - Order accordingly.
