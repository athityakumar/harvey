Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore v Mysore Sugar Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 345; AIR 1967 SC 723; [1962] 46 ITR 649; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(Xi), 10(2)(Xv) - Assessment to Tax - Loss of Capital - Revenue Expenditure - Respondent Company was doing forward arrangement for next year's crop and paying an amount in advance out of the price so that growing of the crop may not suffer due to want of funds - Appellant/revenue assessed Income and tax to be paid on treating as revenue expenditure - HC on appeal treated as loss of capital and decided in favor of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether money which was given up represented a loss of capital or must be treated as a revenue expenditure - Held, amount so far as assessee Company was concerned represented current expenditure towards purchase of sugarcane and it makes no difference that sugarcane thus purchased was grown by with seedling, fertilizer and money taken on account from assessee Company - Assessee Company was doing no more than making a forward arrangement for next year's crop and paying an amount in advance out of the price so that growing of the crop may not suffer due to want of funds in hands of the growers - Hence, money which was given up represented a loss of capital and not subjected to tax - Appeal dismissed.
