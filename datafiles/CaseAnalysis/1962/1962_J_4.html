Jabalpur Electric Supply Company v Sambhu Prasad Srivastava and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1966 SC 1288; 1962 (5) FLR 191; 1962 (2) LLJ 216; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Reinstatement - Validity - Whether power to take disciplinary action against an employee, under standing orders of company be legally delegated to any of its officers? - Held, it was not Resident Engineer in his capacity as Resident Engineer that made order of discharge - Clearly in making order of discharge he was acting on basis of power of attorney executed in his favour on 26-06-1957 - Under cl. 10 of power of attorney he had power 'subject to standing orders from time to time given by Company to appoint, dismiss, suspend or terminate services of any of employees of Company at Jabalpur' - Power of Company under cl. 19 of standing orders to dismiss or suspend and its power under cl. 20 to discharge an employee are both covered by cl. 10 of power of attorney - If there is nothing in law to prevent these powers being delegated to Resident Engineer, there could be no escape from conclusion that exercise of power u/cl. 20 in present case by Resident Engineer amounted in law to an exercise of power by Company itself - Disagreeing with High Court, and courts below that order of discharge made by Resident Engineer was in exercise of power validly delegated to him and that there, has been no breach of standing orders by such action - Petition allowed.
