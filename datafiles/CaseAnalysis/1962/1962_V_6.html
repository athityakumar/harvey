Venkata Reddi and Others v Pothi Reddi

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 306; AIR 1963 SC 992; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 126; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 616; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Presidency

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 2(2), 97, O. 20, r. 18 - (A) When can a decision of court be said to be final? - Held, a decision is said to be final when, so far as Court rendering it is concerned, it is unalterable except by resort to such provisions of CPC as permit its reversal, modification or amendment - A final decision would operate as res judicata between parties if it is not sought to be modified or reversed by preferring an appeal or a revision or a review application as is permitted by Code - (B) Whether a preliminary decree in a mortgage suit or a partition suit, is conclusive decree? - Further held, it is not conclusive - Legislature in its wisdom has thought that suits of certain types should be decided in stages and though suit in such cases can be regarded as fully and completely decided only after a final decree is made, decision of court arrived at earlier stage also has a finality attached to it s. 97 - Further proceedings are required to be taken for procuring relief to which a party is held entitled by a decision is no test for not regarding that decision as final - Appeal allowed.
