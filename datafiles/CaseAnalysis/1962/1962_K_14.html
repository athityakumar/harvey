Kanji Manji v Trustees of The Port of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1963 SC 468; 1962 (64) BomLR 258; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 461; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Houses & Rents - Bombay Rents, Hotel & Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1947, ss. 4(1) and 4(4)(a) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106 and 111- (A) Whether provisions of Rent Control Act come in way if Govt. or a local authority wants to evict a person from land? - Held, it does not - S. 4(a) of Bombay Act, 1953, was enacted to cut down operation of words 'any premises belonging to Govt. or a local authority by excluding only buildings which were occupied by sub tenants even though buildings belonged to Govt. or continued to belong to it - S. 4(b) excluded s. 15, which prohibited sub-letting of buildings by a tenant - Any action by Govt. or local authority in respect of land falls to be governed by 4(1) and not 4(a), and 4(1), put case in relation to land, entirely out of Rent Control Act - Suit for ejectment does not have to be filed in court of Small Causes, as required by Rent Control Act but in City Civil Court - Appeal dismissed.
