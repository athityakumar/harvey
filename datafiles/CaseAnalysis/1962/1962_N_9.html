 Nehru Motor Transport Co-Operative Society Limited, and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 450; AIR 1963 SC 1098; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 68(D)(2) - (A) Whether approval of draft scheme is subject to review by state Govt.? - Held, no review of an approval once given by State Govt. or by officer appointed in that behalf though he may be entitled to correct any clerical mistakes or inadvertent slips that may have crept in his order - U/s. 68 D(2) a draft scheme has to be considered as a whole and all objections to it have to be decided before it can be approved by State Govt. or by officer appointed in that behalf - Act does not envisage approving of a part of scheme once and putting it into effect and leaving another part unapproved and left over for enforcement later - (B) Whether s. 68 C violates art. 14 of Constitution?- Further held, it does not - U/s. 68 C it is open to take over any area or route to complete or partial exclusion of other persons - It was open to State Govt. to take over certain route only and exclude those only who may by plying completely on that route, leaving out of scheme those who covered route only partially - Unless it can be shown that others who are similarly situated have not been excluded from scheme there can be no question of discrimination - Petition dismissed.
