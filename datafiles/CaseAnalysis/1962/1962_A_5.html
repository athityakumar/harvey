 State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Abdeali

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 421; AIR 1963 SC 1237; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 704; 1962 (13) STC 931

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950, s. 3 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 304(a) - Exemption from tax under notification D/ 28-01-1956 - Respondent carried on business of importing and selling different types of footwear, claimed exemption from sales tax on sale of hand-made shoes, chappals and other types of footwear whose sale price did not exceed Rs. 12-8-0 per pair - Exemption claimed was denied by Sales tax officer on ground that condition laid down in notification was not fulfilled, and as respondent was an importer and dealer of footwear and not manufacturer or a member of family of manufacturer, he was not entitled to claim any exemption - On appeal, High Court held that exemption applies to hand-made shoes, chappals etc, whether made within or outside State if other conditions mentioned in notification are satisfied - Whether said notification would be discriminatory in nature and would contravene provisions of art. 304(a) of Constitution? - Exemption by itself creates no discrimination between footwear manufactured or produced in State and imported from outside - Even a small manufacturer in State must fulfil conditions laid down by notification in question before he can claim exemption from tax - Held, respondent can claim exemption only if interpretation put by HC on notification D/-28-01-1956, is accepted as correct - Further held, if that interpretation is not correct, then this appeal must be allowed even if notification is bad by reason of provisions of art. 304(a) of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
