 Mohan Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1963 SC 174; 1962 AllCriR 448; 1963 CRLJ 100; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 848; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 141,147, 149 and 302 - Whether acquittal of two co-accused leaves it open to prosecution to rely upon s. 149 against appellants? - Essential condition of unlawful assembly that its membership must be five or more, as soon as two co-accused were acquitted, membership of assembly was reduced from five to three and that made s. 141 inapplicable which inevitably leads to result that s. 149 cannot be invoked against appellants - If both charge and evidence are confined to persons named in charge and out of persons so named two or more are acquitted leaving before court less than five persons to be tried, then s. 149 cannot be invoked - Held, conviction of appellants altered into one u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Conviction and sentence for offence u/s. 147 set aside and appellants are ordered to be acquitted in respect of that offence - Order accordingly.
