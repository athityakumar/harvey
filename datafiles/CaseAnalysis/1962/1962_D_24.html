Chavalier I. I. Iyyappan and Another v Dharmodayam Company

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 536; AIR 1966 SC 1017; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Easements Act, 1882, ss. 60 and 62 - Whether appellant entitled to recover the money from the company under the trust deed? - The directors are trustees of the company's money and property, agents in the transaction which they enter into on behalf of the company, the trustees of assets which have come into their hands, or which are under their control - Held, the offer and the acceptance of the terms of the trust deed being wholly different from what has now been executed by the appellant, it is not possible for a Court in equity to accept the new trust as a bar to the respondent's claim for possession - High Court rightly held appellant only entitled for compensation for the structure erected - Appeal dismissed.
