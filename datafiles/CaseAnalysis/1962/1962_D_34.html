 Dr. Indramani Pyarelal Gupta v W. R. Nathu and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 319; AIR 1963 SC 274; 1963 (65) BomLR 378; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Concurrence, Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904, Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952

Summary: Socio-Economic - Agriculture & Agri products - Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Municipalities & Govts - Practice & Procedure - Socio - Economic - Trade - Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952, ss. 4, 3, 11, 12 and 28 - (A) What is nature of power that is conferred on Forward Markets Commission u/s. 4(F) of Act? - Further held, no limitation in relation to regulation of forward trading in goods which Act is designed - No common positive thread running through cls. (a) to (e) of section such as would bring them under one genus and negatively they do not expressly include any administrative or executive functions, that itself might be a reason why expression 'other' occurring in cl. (f) should receive construction that it is intended to comprehend such a function - Where Court is concerned with a question whether it is legally competent to vest a particular power in a statutory body, proper rule of interpretation would be that unless nature of power is such as to be incompatible with purpose for which body is created, or unless particular power is contra-indicated by any specific provision of enactment bringing body into existence, any power which would further provisions of Act could be legally conferred on it - (B) What is meaning of 'By' an Act u/s. 12 and 'under Act' u/s. 4(f) of Act? - Further held, means by a provision directly enacted in statute in question and which is gatherable from its express language or by necessary implication there from - signify bye laws made by a Subordinate law-making authority which is empowered to do so by parent Act - Distinction is thus between what is directly done by enactment and what is done indirectly by rule - Making authorities which are vested with powers in that behalf by Act - There is some little tautology in use of expression 'as may be prescribed' after comprehensive reference to powers conferred 'under Act', but in order merely to avoid redundancy Court cannot adopt a rule of construction which cuts down amplitude of words used except of course, to avoid redundancy - Normally and in their ordinary signification, words 'under Act' would include both 'rules' framed u/s. 28 as well as 'Bye-Laws' u/ss. 11 or 12, reference to 'rules' might be eliminated as tautologous since they have been specifically provided by words that follow - (C)Whether bye Laws vesting authorities external to Association power to interfere with forward dealings were within scope of bye law making powers u/ss. 11 and 12 of Act? - Further held, yes - Bye law 52AA of bye laws of East India Cotton Association made by Central Govt. in exercise of powers conferred on them u/s. 12 and published in Gazette of India - Extraordinary dated 21-1956 is one for regulation and control of forward contracts - Association can also made by laws - (D) Whether Supreme Court can pronounce upon new point u/s. 136? - Further held, on point not raised in lower Courts but having heard arguments on it Supreme Court pronounced upon it - (E) Whether there is limitation upon powers of Association u/art. 64 ? - Further held, no - Limitation is imposed only on powers of Board - (E) Whether a bye law can have retrospective operation? - A statute which could validly enact a law with retrospective effect could in express terms validily confer upon a ruler authority a power to make a rule or frame a bye law having retrospective operation - Where contract entered into by parties purported to be one under bye-laws for time being in force any change in bye-laws would seem to be contemplated and provided for by contract itself, so that it might not be correct to speak of new bye-law as affecting any accrued rights under a contract - Appeal dismissed.
