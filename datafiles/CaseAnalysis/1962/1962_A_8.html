Abinash Chandra Bose v Bimal Chandra Bose

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 147; AIR 1963 SC 316; 1963 CRLJ 261; 1964 (2) SCJ 285; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 564

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 423(1)(a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - (A) Whether order for retrial can be passed by High Court for giving second opportunity to complainant to adduce evidence merely on basis that case is between client and its counsel?- Held, no, it cannot - In absence of anything to show that Magistrate had refused to give an opportunity to complainant to adduce necessary evidence HC was not justified to do - Accused should not be placed on trial for same offence more than once except in very exceptional circumstances - This case is not an exceptional circumstance - Appeal allowed.
