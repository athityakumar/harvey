R. Ramachandran Ayyar v Ramalingam Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 446; AIR 1963 SC 302; 1963 ALJ 67; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 86; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - (A) Whether s. 100(1)(c) would be applicable if appreciation of evidence made by lower Appellate Court is patently erroneous and can be said to introduce a substantial error or defect in procedure? - Held, no, it cannot - Since s. 100(1)(c) refers to an error or defect connected with procedure and not an error or defect in appreciation of evidence - But if Appellate Court placed onus on a wrong party and its finding of fact is result or fails to consider an issue which had been tried and found upon by trial Court and proceeds to reverse trial Court's decision without consideration of such an issue, or allows a new point of fact to be raised for first time before it, or permits a party to adopt a new plea of fact, or makes out a new case for party, then there would be defect in procedure - (B) Whether High Court can interfere with finding of fact recorded by lower appellate Court merely because judgment of lower Appellate Court was not as elaborate as that of Trial Judge, or because some of reason given by Trial Judge had not been expressly reversed by lower Appellate Court? - Further held, HC is not justified - A finding of fact which is not supported by any evidence can be questioned u/s. 100 - But it does not mean that wherever HC thinks that evidence accepted, by lower Appellate Court could not have been reasonably accepted HC would be justified in interfering with decision of lower Appellate Court - Appeal allowed.
