 Mohanlal Chunilal Kothari v Tribhovan Haribhai Tamboli

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1963 SC 358; 1964 MahLJ 269; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s.88(1) - Appellants were plaintiff-landlords, and respondents were tenants in possession of certain lands which came under Act on 01-08-1949 - Appellants claim that respondents had become trespassers on service of notice in March 1950, with effect from beginning of new agricultural section in May 1951 - Notification was issued by Bombay Govt. on 24-04-1951 which had effect of putting lands in question out of operation of Bombay Tenancy Act - When matter was still pending in Court of Appeal, judgment of lower Appellate Court dated 27-09-1954, notification cancelling previous notification was issued - Whether respondents were protected tenants under Bombay Tenancy Act? - Held, under provisions of s. 3 A(1) of Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939, as amended, a tenant would be deemed to be a protected tenant from 01-08-1950, and that vested right could not be affected by notification aforesaid - Further, when judgment of lower Appellate Court was rendered, position in fact and law was that there was no notification under clause (d) of s. 88(1) in operation so as to make land in question immune from benefits conferred by Tenancy Law - Appeal dismissed.
