Indian Cable Company, Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 49; 1962 (4) FLR 444; 1962 (1) LLJ 409; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 589; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 13(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k) and 25G - Retrenchment - Validity - Legal right to get employment in other branches - Whether retrenchment of workmen of Ambala branch of Indian Cable Company Ltd. is justified and legal under provisions of s. 25 G and whether seniority of workmen in all branches of company was pooled for purpose of effecting retrenchment? - Branches are located in different places and there is also a lack of functional integrality - Each branch is a separate industrial establishment - Held, dispute of respondents is an industrial dispute as defined in s. 2(k), as that has been raised by majority of workmen of Ambala branch, which is an industrial establishment - But as establishment has been closed and closure itself to not impugned as bad on ground that it is colourable and not bona fide, s. 25G has no application, respondents therefore are not entitled to any relief under that section - Appeal allowed.
