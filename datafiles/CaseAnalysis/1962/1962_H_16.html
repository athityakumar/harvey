Hoshiar Singh v Gurbachan Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 552; AIR 1962 SC 1089; 1962 CRLJ 236; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 127; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land allotted to Budh Singh, a displaced person, was, it was stated by appellants, forcibly occupied by respondent Gurbachan Singh - On 09-05-1958, appellant No. 1 made an order that Budh Singh and other allottees like him would be given possession of land, allotted to them - Petitions were put up before learned Chief Justice on 16-05-1958, when he issued an order staying delivery of possession till 19-05-1958 - On 19-05-1958, Division Bench extended operation of stay order until 23-05-1958 Appellants contend that a notice of second order was not officially communicated to appellants till 21-05-1958 Respondent contends that on 20-05-1958, two appellants were informed by certain interested persons that an extension of stay order up to 23-05-1958, had been granted by High Court - However, appellants formally dispossessed respondent and handed over possession of land to Budh Singh - HC instead of committing two appellants for contempt of court merely administered a warning to them and directed them to pay costs of respondent - (A) Whether HC was wrong in finding that two appellants had wilfully disobeyed order of HC? - Held, if stand of appellants was that they did not know of order made by HC on 19-05-1958 such a point was not urged in HC - On affidavits made available to Court, only reasonable inference is that both appellants were informed that HC had granted an extension of stay order in all cases - (B) Whether in absence of official communication or service, proceeding of contempt must fail? - Further held, in order to justify committal for breach of a prohibitive order it is not necessary that order should have been served upon party against whom it has been granted, if it be proved that he had notice of order aliunde, as by telegram, or newspaper report, or otherwise, and knew that it was intended to be enforced - Court do not, however, think that appellants in present case had any real justification for doubting authenticity of order made by HC, even though order had not been officially communicated to them - Appeal dismissed.
