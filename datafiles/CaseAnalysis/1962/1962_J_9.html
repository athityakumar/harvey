 Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Janabha Muhammad Hussain Nachiar Ammal

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 451; AIR 1963 SC 1401; [1963] 49 ITR 80; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 137

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Assessment Proceedings - Initiation of Proceedings - Period of Limitation - Proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act were initiated with the issue of a notice dt.25-7-1949 - Assessee's contention was that initiation of proceedings was invalid as the department's right to revive assessment was governed by old s. 34 where period of limitation prescribed was only four years in the case of a failure to file a return and this period having expired dt.31-3-1947 and HC ordered the proceedings against respondent invalid - Hence, instant appeal - Whether u/s. 34 of the principal Act as amended in 1948 applies to notice-issued and assessment order valid - Held, notices and assessment orders in respect of years ending before dt.1-4-1948 in proceedings commenced after dt.8-4-1948 shall comply with provisions of s. 34 as amended in 1948 - Notice and an assessment order valid u/s. 34 of the Act as amended in 1948 would be valid even if time prescribed in respect of them by s. 34 of the Act as it stood before 1948 amendment expired - S. 34 of the principal Act as amended in 1948 applies to the notice-issued and the assessment order made in this case. Both of them are valid u/s. 34 of the Act as so amended - Appeal allowed.
