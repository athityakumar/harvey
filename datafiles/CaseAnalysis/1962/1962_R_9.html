Radhakrishnadas v Kaluram

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 531; AIR 1967 SC 574; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 10; 1962 (2) KerLR 412; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 648

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Gorelal, acting for himself and as guardian of his minor son Balramdas, appellant No. 2 and Radhakrishnadas, appellant No. 1, describing himself as a major executed a sale deed in favour of respondents for a consideration of Rs. 50, 000/- It was further stated in sale deed that this amount was required for performing marriages of appellant No. 1 Radhakrishnadas and Gorelal's daughter Ramjibai, who were both stated to be majors - Appellants contended that since income of family was Rs. 7000/- per year, considerable savings could be made out of it after defraying expenses of family - There was, therefore, no necessity for executing sale deed - (A) Whether there was any legal necessity for sale? - Held, what alienee is required to establish is legal necessity for transaction and that it is not necessary for him to show that every bit of consideration which he advanced was actually applied for meeting family necessity - (B) Whether transaction cannot bind interests of appellants? - Further held, fact that that sale deed had been executed also by his father who was manager of family, makes transaction binding upon him just as it is admittedly binding upon his brother, second appellant, who was then a minor - It is clear that Rs. 45, 000/- Out of consideration of Rs. 50, 000/- were required for family purposes - Even where such a transaction has been entered into solely by a manager it would be deemed to be on behalf of family and binding on it - Appeal dismissed.
