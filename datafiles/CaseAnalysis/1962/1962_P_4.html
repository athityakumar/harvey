 P. V. Raghava Reddi and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 122; AIR 1962 SC 977; [1962] 44 ITR 720; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 81; 1962 (2) SCJ 263; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 596

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.4(1)(a) - Non-resident Company - Assessment - Deposit Account - Statutory Agent - Assessee firm/appellant instructed by Japanese company to pay amount according to terms of agreement and assessee firm credited amount to non- resident Japanese company - Income-tax authorities treated assessee firm as statutory agent of Japanese Company and assessed tax on amounts - Tribunal ordered against respondent and on appeal order passed in favor of revenue - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sum credited to non- resident company's account in books of assessee chargeable to assessee u/ s. 4 (1)(a) of the Act - Held, character of money changed from a debt to a deposit in such same way as if it was credited in a Bank to the account of Company - Amount on terms of agreement to have been received by Japanese Company and this attracts the application of s.(4)(1)(a) of the Act and subjected to taxation - Appeal dismissed.
