Bhaiya Punjalal Bhagwanddin v Dave Bhagwatprasad Prabhuprasad

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 503; AIR 1963 SC 120; 1963 (2) SCJ 441; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 27 - Whether it is a condition precedent for institution of a suit by a landlord for recovery of possession from a tenant who has been in arrears of rent that there had been first a determination of contractual tenancy? - Held, s. 12(1) of Act provides that a landlord shall not be entitled to recovery of possession of any premises so long as tenant pays, or is ready, wiling to pay, amount of standard rent, permitted increases, if any, observes, performs other conditions of tenancy, in so far as they are consistent with provisions of Act - It creates a restriction on landlord's right to recovery of possession - When landlord will have such a right is not provided by it - Ordinarily, landlord will have a right to recover possession from tenant when tenancy had determined - Provisions of this section therefore will operate against landlord after determination of tenancy by any of modes referred to in s. 3 of Transfer of property Act - What this section of Act provides is that even after determination of tenancy, a landlord will not be entitled to recover possession, though a right to recover possession gets vested in him, so long as tenant complies with what he is required to do by this section - It is this extra protection given by this section which will be useful to tenant after his tenancy has determined - Section does not create a new right in landlord to evict tenant when tenant does not pay his rent - It does not say so, and therefore, it is clear that a landlord's right to evict tenant for default in payment of rent will arise only after tenancy is determined and continued possession of tenant is not account of contractual terms but on account of statutory right conferred on him to continue in possession so long as he complies with what sub-section 1 requires of him - Landlord is restricted from evicting tenant till tenant does not do what he is required to do for peaceful possession under sub-s. (1) of s. 12 - Where a tenant is in possession under a lease from landlord, he is not to be evicted for a clause which would give rise to a suit for recovery of possession u/s. 12 if his tenancy has not been determined already - It follows that whenever a tenant acts in a way which would remove bar on landlord's right to evict him it is necessary for landlord to serve him with a notice determining his tenancy and also serve him with a notice under sub-s. (2) of s. 12 of Act - S. 12 of Act is differently worded and cannot therefore be said to be a complete Code in itself - There is nothing in it which overrides provisions of Transfer of Property Act - So along as contractual tenancy continues, a landlord cannot sue for recovery of possession even if s. 12 of Act does not bar institution of such a suit, and that in order to take advantage of this provision of Act he must first determine tenancy in accordance with provisions of Transfer of Property Act - Appeal dismissed.
