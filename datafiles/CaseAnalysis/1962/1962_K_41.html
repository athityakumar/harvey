Kurapati Venkata Mallayya and Another v Thondepu Ramaswami and Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 438; AIR 1964 SC 818; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 110; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 995; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 503 - Receiver appointed for realization of debts due to respondent firm - Receiver instituted suit in his own name against appellant - Appellant contended receiver has no right to insitute suit and he has not been authorise by court so suit is untenable - High Court held that Receiver was entitled to institute suit having been authorised by court to collect debts - Held, receiver is appointed for benefit of all concerned, and he is representative of court, and of all parties - Court can only administer through a receiver - Receiver function cannot be limited merely to preservation of property - It is open to a court to confer upon him power to take such steps including instituting suits in interest of parties - Appeal partly allowed.
