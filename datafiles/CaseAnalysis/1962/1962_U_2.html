Udai Bhan v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 560; AIR 1962 SC 1116; 1962 CRLJ 251; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 380 and 451 - Whether appellant could be tried in 1955 under IPC for offences committed by him in 1948 when state, in which offences were committed, was not a part of Dominion of India and whether State IPC did contain any provision corresponding to Sections of IPC under which appellant has been convicted? - Held, yes - In 1948, when offences were committed by appellant, law in State of Jhabua was IPC and that law was continued by Ordinance of Rajpramukh and Repealing Act and any penalty incurred in respect of any offence committed in 1948 is punishable under IPC as applied to Jhabua State - Appellant can be tried under IPC, 1860, ss. 380 and 457 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26 and 27 - Whether appellants conviction u/ss. 457 and 380 of IPC was not sustainable? - A discovery of a fact includes object found, place from which it is produced and knowledge of accused as to its existence - Held, no - Conviction of appellant was proper as discovery of stolen articles amounted to discovery of facts - Appellant did not make any confessional statement and his handing over stolen articles did not amount to confessional statement so as to be inadmissible in evidence u/ss. 25 and 26 of Act - S. 27 does not nullify ban imposed by s. 26 in regard to confessions made by persons in police custody but because there is guarantee of truthfulness from fact discovered, that part of statement which distinctly relates to discovery of fact whether confessional or not, is allowed to be given in evidence - Appeal dismissed.
