Prabhu v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1963 SC 1113; 1963 CRLJ 182; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26 and 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Incriminating statement made by accused to police officer - Statement not leading to discovery of fact - It is not admissible in evidence - (A)Whether circumstantial evidences can lead to he conviction of accused? - Held, that if statement of accused was excluded from evidence, circumstantial evidence was not complete to lead to conviction of accused for murder - (B)Whether an incriminating statement to police is valid as an evidence? - Further held, incriminating statements made to a police officer are hit by ss. 25 and 26 of Evidence Act - Appeal allowed.
