Mst. Kharbuja Kuer v Jangbahadur Rai

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 532; AIR 1963 SC 1203; 1963 BLJR 892; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Pardhanashin lady - Execution of deed - Whether burden of proof rest upon pardanashin lady to prove fraud? - Held, in Indian pardhanashin ladies have been given a special protection in view of social conditions of times - Burden of proof shall always rest upon person who seeks to sustain a transaction entered into with a pardanashin lady to establish that said document was executed by her after clearly understanding nature of transaction - It should be established that it was not only her physical act but also her mental act - Burden can be discharged not only by providing that document was explained to her and that she understood it, but also by other evidence, direct and circumstantial - (B) Whether findings of fact given by said courts were binding on HC u/s. 100 of CPC? - Further held, finding of facts of both courts below is supported by evidence, and there is no permissible ground for interference with it in second appeal - Appeal allowed.
