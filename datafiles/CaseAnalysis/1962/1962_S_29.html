Upper Doab Sugar Mills Limited v Shahdara (Delhi) Saharanpur Light Railway Company

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, J.L. Kapur, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 521; AIR 1963 SC 217; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 333

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Carriers & Transportation - Administrative - Railways Act, 1890, ss. 41(1)(b), 41(1)(c), 46 B, 26 and 39 -(A)Whether Railway Rates Tribunal had jurisdiction to entertain or try complaints as regards reasonableness or otherwise of rates and charges made prior to institution of complaint u/s. 39? - Held, it had not - Cannot order refund - Question of refund could arise only after a decision that charges made were more than what was reasonable - Complainant can ask only for a declaration that rate or charge is unreasonable and it is only this declaratory relief which Tribunal is authorized to give - Cannot give a consequential relief - (B) Whether an order for payment of costs which may be made by Tribunal could u/s. 46 B be transmitted to a civil court and executed by a civil court as if it were a decree?- Further held, yes, it can be - Even if no other order passes, which may require execution, s. 46 B will serve its purpose in connection with orders for costs - If under law, apart from Railways Act a consignor is entitled to obtain relief against unreasonable charges which he has paid in past, s. 26 will not stand in his way - Appeal dismissed.
