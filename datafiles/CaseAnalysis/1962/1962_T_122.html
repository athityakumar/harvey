State of West Bengal v Tulsidas Mundhra

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 284; 1964 CRLJ 443; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 173, 207A, 208, 342, 362 and 540 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 409 and 477A - Whether provisions of s. 540 apply to a case tried by Magistrate u/s. 207A? - Held, conclusion of Magistrate was that application made by respondent was vexatious, intended merely to delay proceedings - In view of reasons given by Magistrate in rejecting application of respondent, it is very difficult to sustain view taken by High Court that Magistrate was inclined to hold that s. 540 did not apply to proceedings in present case - HC also referred that accused persons have not been examined u/s. 342 CrPC, 1973 and it has apparently asked Magistrate to examine accused persons - Relevant provisions of s. 207A(6) provides that Magistrate can examine accused if he thinks it necessary to do so - According to HC failure to examine accused persons u/s. 342 did not amount to a material irregularity and could not justify reversal of order passed by learned Magistrate - Order passed by HC set aside - Order of learned Magistrate restored - Appeal allowed.
