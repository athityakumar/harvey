C. Vasantlal and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 344; [1962] 45 ITR 206

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66(2) - Income Assessment - Question of Fact - Question of Law - Jurisdiction - Respondent/revenue assessed income of appellant and on appeal to Tribunal it found total income of assessee based on materials available before it - Appellant appealed to HC which decided against appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was right in rejecting application of appellant filed u/s. 66 of the Act - Held, yes - Evidence on which conclusion of Appellate Tribunal could be founded and question which fell to be determined by it was purely one of fact - Tribunal arrived at conclusion with materials available with it which was pure question of fact - Jurisdiction of HC u/s. 66 of the was merely advisory and HC was right in rejecting application of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
