Prithi Nath Singh and Others v Suraj Ahir and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 457; AIR 1963 SC 1041; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 302

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 58(d), 60 and 62 -(A) Whether mortgagor has any right in land after money borrowed is taken back? - Held, when mortgage money is paid by mortgagor to mortgagee, there does not remain any debt due from mortgagor to mortgagee - Mortgage can no longer continue after mortgage money has been paid - Definition of usufructuary mortgage itself leads to conclusion that authority given to mortgagee to remain in possession of mortgaged property ceases when mortgage money has been paid up - If mortgage money has been received by mortgagee and thereafter he refuses to perform acts which he is found to do u/s. 60, mortgagor can enforce his right to get back mortgage document, possession of mortgaged property and reconveyance of that property through court - Right to demand mortgagee to do certain things on payment of mortgage money is different from enforcing demands subsequent to payment of money - Petition dismissed.
