Radhakishan v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 270; AIR 1963 SC 822; 1963 ALJ 397; 1963 CRLJ 809; 1963 (2) LLJ 667; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Criminal - Indian Post Office Act, 1898, s. 52 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 103 and 165 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 467 and 471 - Whether High Court was right in setting aside acquittal of respondent and convicting him u/s. 52 of Act and penalising him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for a period of one year in each case? - Prosecution failed to prove that letters were in exclusive possession of appellant - No presumption can, therefore, be drawn that appellant had secreted them from mere fact that they were found in almirah which, at best, may be regarded as being in joint possession of appellant and his father - Inference of joint possession would not be legitimate - Conviction and sentences passed against appellant is set aside - Appeals allowed.
