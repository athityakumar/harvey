U. B. Dutt and and Company Private, Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1963 SC 411; 1962 (4) FLR 247; 1962 (1) LLJ 374; 1964 (2) SCJ 241; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & industrial law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 3 - (A) Whether Industrial Court has a right to inquire into causes that lead to termination of service of an employee?- Held, yes it has - Company has power to determine services of workman by giving notice Under Standing Orders, r. 18(a) - Colorable exercise of power - Industrial Court has even in such a case, a right to inquire into causes that lead to termination of service - (B) Whether action taken u/r. 18 of standing order of company requires bona fide reasons?- Further held, requirement of bona fides is essential - Where termination of service is capricious, arbitrary or unnecessarily harsh on part of employer judged by normal standards of a reasonable man that may be cogent evidence of victimisation or unfair labour practice - An enquiry is not held by Company and action is taken under s. 18(a) - Employer can defend action under s. 18(a) by leading evidence before Tribunal to show that there was in fact a misconduct and therefore action taken under r. 18(a) was bona fide and was not colourable exercise of power under that rule - Appeal dismissed.
