Kanwal Lal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 483; AIR 1963 SC 1317; 1963 CRLJ 345; 1964 (1) SCJ 320; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Administration - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 499, Exceptions 8 and 9 - Punjab Gram Panchayat Act, 1953, ss. 38 and 42- (A) Whether District Panchayat officer had any lawful authority over R? - Held, prostitution not an offence u/IPC, 1860 - Keeping of bawdy house is not offence under sch IA of Punjab Gram Panchayat act, 1953 - Only Panchayat have criminal Jurisdiction - District panchayat officer had no lawful authority over R - (B) Whether application made by accused to District Panchayat Officer regarding bawdy house in locality is maintainable under exception 8 and 9 of s. 499 of IPC, 1860? - Further held, keeping bawdy house is not an offence within sch IA to Punjab Gram Panchayat Act, 1953 - Exception 9 posits that person to whom communication is made has an interest in protecting person making accusation - District Panchayat office had no interest in communication made by accused - Application not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
