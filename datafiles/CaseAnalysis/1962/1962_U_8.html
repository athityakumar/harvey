Banwari Lal Jhunjhunwala and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 462; AIR 1963 SC 1620; 1963 CRLJ 529; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 338; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distinct Offence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 120 B and 71- Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 233 and 179 - (A) What does expression 'every distinct offense' in s. 223 indicates? - Held, it has a different content from expression 'every offence' or 'each offense' - Separate charge is required for every distinct offence and not necessarily for each separate offence - 'Distinct' means 'not identical', 'two things not being same' - Not interrelated offence - (B) Whether a court can try offences committed beyond its territorial jurisdiction? - Further held, yes, in case of conspiracy Court is competent to try accused for all offences committed in pursuance of that conspiracy irrespective of fact that any or all other offences were not committed within its territorial jurisdiction - (C) Whether court is competent to amend charge of accused as sanction given for prosecution of accused did not state that he had abused his position for his personal gain? - Further held, yes, prosecution can still lead evidence to effect that sanction given for prosecution was based on facts which referred to his obtaining money for himself - Also prosecution can urge that it was a matter of inference from alleged conduct of accused that he obtained benefit for himself - Appeal Nos. 113 and 114 Dismissed; Appeal No. 190 Allowed.
