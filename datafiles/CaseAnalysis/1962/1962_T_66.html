Sunder Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 1962 SC 1211; 1962 CRLJ 290; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Procedure Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149 and 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 423(1)(a) -The three appellants, Sunder Singh and his sons Lal Singh and Gurmukh Singh along with one Rachhpal Singh were tried before learned Addl. Sessions Judge, Karnal with having committed offence of murdering Malook Singh, Anup Singh and Darbara Singh - Prosecution case was that these three murders were committed by four accused persons in furtherance of their common intention - Evidence adduced against Rachhpal Singh left room for doubt - Benefit of doubt, he was acquitted - (A) What is the effect on prosecution if out of persons charged of same offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC, 1860 some are prosecuted and one is acquitted? - Held, Failure of prosecution to prove that one of them took part in commission of offence - Does not introduce any infirmity in its case against remaining accused at all - Appeal partly allowed.
