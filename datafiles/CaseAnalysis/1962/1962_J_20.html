 State of Punjab v Joginder Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 467; AIR 1963 SC 913; 1964 (1) SCJ 627; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 169; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 169

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) class III rules, 1961, rr. 2(d) and 2(e) - Whether discrimination between municipal board teacher and state Govt. teacher violated art. 14 of Constitution ? - Held, order does not violate art. 14 - By order d/- 27-09-1957 which came into effect from 01-10-1957 teachers in erstwhile board schools became employees of Govt. but there was no integration of two cadres either expressly or by necessary implication - It would follow that it was not Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) class III rules that created two distinct cadres but that they existed independently of rules - Two services started as independent Services - If they were distinct services, there was no question of inter se seniority between members of two services, nor of any comparison between two in matter of promotion for founding an argument based upon arts. 14 or 16(1) - They started dissimilarly and they continued dissimilarly and any dissimilarity in their treatment would not be a denial of equal opportunity - Appeal allowed.
