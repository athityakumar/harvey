Gursahai Saigal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 1963 SC 1062; [1963] 48 ITR 1; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 893

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax, Advance Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 18A - (A) Whether interest can be calculated u/s. 18A if assess has failed to submit estimate? - Held, assessee can be charged with interest u/s. (8), calculating amount by applying machinery of calculation laid down in Sub-s. (6), even though he has not submitted an estimate of his income and paid tax as required u/s. (3) of s. 18A - (B) Whether calculation of interest be made u/s. 18A(6) if assessee fails to submit tax? - Further held, s. 18A(8) by its terms applies to a case where no payment of tax has been made and, therefore, there is no first day of January of a financial year in which tax was paid, from which day calculation of interest has to commence - s. 18A(8) intended to, did in clear terms impose interest - Not necessary to resort to any equitable rule of construction or to alter meaning of language used or to add to or vary it in order, It in substance says that in calculating amount of interest machinery of calculation laid down in sub s. (6) shall be applied - Sub s. (6) which says that interest would be calculated from 1st day of January in financial year in which tax was paid obviously cannot literally be applied to a case where no tax has been paid - Sub s.(6) being only a provision which lays down machinery for calculation of tax rule of literal construction which applied only to a taxing provision should not be applied to it - (C) Whether calculation of interest be made u/s. 18A(6) if assessee fails to submit tax? - Sub s. (6) which says that interest would be calculated from 1st day of January in financial year in which tax was paid obviously cannot literally be applied to a case where no tax has been paid - Appeals dismissed.
