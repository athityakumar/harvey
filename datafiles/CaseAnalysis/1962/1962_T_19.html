Jetmull Bhojraj v Darjeeling Himalayan Railway Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1962 SC 1879; 1963 (2) ALT(SC) 80; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 89; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 832

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross Objection, Carriage By Rail

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Limitation Act, Art. 30 - Railway Act 1890 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 15 (2) - On 07-06-1946, 169 bales delivered to appellant to whom the Railway Receipt had been endorsed - Correspondence thereafter ensued for remaining 90 bales - Recovery of damages for negligence of railways in carrying goods - Held, two reasons why this appeal should fail, first the suit was barred by limitations by art. 30 of the Limitation Act which provides for a suit against a carrier for compensation for injuring goods, within one year from date of injury and a further period of two months, being the time requisite for the notice under s. 80 of the CPC, 1908 to the benefit of which the appellant was entitled u/s. 15 (2) of the Limitation Act, the suit was clearly out of time - No 'notice under s. 77 of the Railways Act, 1890, had been given - Decree of the High Court set aside - Appeal allowed in part.
