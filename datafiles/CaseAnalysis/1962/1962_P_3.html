 P. V. Godbole v Jagannath Fakirchand

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 314; AIR 1963 SC 1399; [1963] 49 ITR 88; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 130

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1953

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 34(1), 34(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 132(1), 133(1)(b) - Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1953 - A/Ys 1944-45, 1945-46, 1946-47 - Assessee - Partnership - Notice for returns - Legality - Respondent/assessee filed petition in HC challenging jurisdiction of IO to issue notice u/s. 34(1) of the 1922 Act in respect of A/ys 1944-45,1945-46, 1946-47 pursuant to direction given by Appellate Assistant Commissioner (AAC) in appeal of another assessee that income was income of partnership of which respondent and other assessee were partners - HC held that notice was illegal - Hence instant appeal by revenue - Whether second proviso to s. 34(3) of the 1922 Act as amended in 1953 could save impugned proceedings - Held, second proviso to s. 34(3) of the 1922 Act was invalid as it was offending art. 14 of Constitution and would afford no protection to revenue authorities - Further, impugned notice was issued in consequence of order u/s. 31 of the 1922 Act in proceeding to which assessee was not party - S.C. Prashar, Income Tax Officer v. Vasantsen Dwarkadas, 1962 Indlaw SC 313, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
