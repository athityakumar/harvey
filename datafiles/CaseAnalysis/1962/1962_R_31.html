 Rama Krishna Ramanath v Janpad Sabha, Gondia

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 244; AIR 1962 SC 1073; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 70; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 143(2) - Central Provinces and Berar Local Self Government Act, 1920 - Central Provinces and Berar Local Government Act, 1948 - Terminal tax - Levy - Legality - Appellant was carrying on business of manufacturing and selling bidis - In course of business, appellant exported bidis to various places outside territorial limits of Janpad Sabha, Gondia - Sabha demanded and collected taxes when export took place from railway stations within its territorial jurisdiction - Between 26-1-1950 and 30-6-1952, respondent Sabha collected tax totalling Rs. 3,818/15/3 - Appellant sought refund of sum of tax from respondent while contending that from the date constitution came into force, imposition and collection of terminal tax by respondent was illegal as right to levy terminal tax was vested exclusively in Union Govt under entry 89 of First List to 7th schedule - When demand was not met, appellant served notice on the sabha in consequence of which sabha discontinued collection of tax but refused to refund tax already collected - Appellant instituted suit praying for decree for the sum of Rs. 3,818/15/3 and thereafter this along with several similar suits including three by appellants in other three appeals were withdrawn to HC under art. 228 for deciding substantial question of law as to interpretation of constitution and Govt of India Act as to whether levy of tax by respondent was lawful or not - HC dismissed suits - Hence instant appeals - Whether levy of tax by respondent was lawful - Held, entry 13 of the Provincial Legislative List II to schedule VII of the 1935 Act empowered legislature to enact 1948 Act - Further, merely because legislature was empowered under this entry to constitute local authorities and vest them with powers and jurisdiction it would not follow that these local bodies could be vested with authority to levy any and every tax for the purpose of raising revenue for the purposes of local administration - They could be validly authorised to raise only those taxes which Province could raise under and by virtue of the relevant entries in Provincial Legislative List - This was on principle that Province could not authorise local bodies created by it to impose taxes which it itself could not directly levy for purposes of Provincial Govt - But its application in particular instances would be controlled by express constitutional provision modifying the same - Case on hand was s. 143(2) of the 1935 Act - If in exercise of such limited power, Province desired to discontinue tax and effected repeal of relevant statute, repeal would be effective - For instance, Provincial Legislature desired continuance of tax but considered rate too High and wished it to be reduced and passed enactment for that purpose, it should not be that legislation was incompetent and that State Govt should permit local authority to levy tax at the same rate - If such legislation was enacted to achieve reduction of rate of duty, its legislative competence should obviously be traceable to power contained in words 'may continue to be levied' in s. 143(2) of 1935 Act - It would follow that in exercise of such limited legislative power, Provincial Legislature would also have right to legislate for continuance of tax provided, if of course, other conditions of s. 143(2) of the 1935 Act were satisfied - Thus, if there was limited legislative power in the Province to enact law with reference to tax levy so as to continue it, validity of the 1949 Act which manifested legislative intent to continue the tax without any break, legal continuity being established by retrospective operation of provision, should be upheld - Appeals dismissed.
