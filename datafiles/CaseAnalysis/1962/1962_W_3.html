Wenger and Company and Others v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1964 SC 864; 1963 (6) FLR 303; 1963 (2) LLJ 403; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 862; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract & Commercial - Delhi Shops and Establishments Act, 1954 - (A) Whether industrial establishments owned by same management constitute separate units or one establishment? - Held, establishments in question have shown uniform prosperity and all of them can be characterized as established concerns - (B) What would be wages structure of employees in case of hotels? - Further held, tribunal has fixed Rs. 35 as a flat rate for dearness allowance and has provided for appropriate deductions from said amount - (C) Whether tips should be taken into account while paying dearness allowance to waiters? - Further held, a minimum amount would be taken into account in determining quantum of dearness allowances at a flat rate - Appeals partly allowed.
