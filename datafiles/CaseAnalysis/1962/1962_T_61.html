 Shivdev Singh v State of Punjab (And Connected Petition)

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 195; AIR 1963 SC 365; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 426

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Improvements

Summary: Land & Property - Petitioners, landowners in village Dhamo Majra - Running an agricultural farm on a mechanised scale - Appeal against law enacted for fixing permissible limit of land which can be owned or hold by any person as landowner or tenant under his personal cultivation - Pepsu Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1955 as amended by Pepsu Act, 1956, ss. 32 and 52 - Pepsu Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Rules, r. 31 - (A) Whether in evolving marking system as provided in r. 31 commission's discretion has been fettered and its independent judgment made illusory? - So long as marking system takes into account what is required under s. 32 K(1)(iv) in order to claim exemption from ceiling it cannot be said that marking stem that has been evolved is something beyond what was contemplated by legislature - It has full discretion to evaluate various features set out in sch. B items I to IX and has full power to give such marks as it thinks fit - Held, it cannot therefore, be said that by providing marking system sch. B rule has in any way fettered discretion and judgment of this commission, and affected its independence, system behind sch. B is that half total number of marks is provided for first three conditions and other half is provided for yields - (B)Whether marking system which has been evolved in Sch. B is in any way foreign to purposes of Act or in any way fails to carry out object behind s. 32 K(1) (iv)? - It cannot said that allotment of half total number of marks to yields in sch. B is in any manner contrary to intention of legislature - Held, marking system which has been evolved in sch. B is not in any way foreign to purposes of Act or in any way fails to carry out object behind s. 32-K(1)(iv) - Marking system only gives guidance to Commission in task assigned to it by s. 32-P(4)(c) - (C) Whether at time of revision Govt. is bound to take advice of Commission and act accordingly? - Ss. 32 P(4) and (5) lay down that State Govt. will be advised by Commission with regard to exemption under s. 32 K and advice of Commission would be binding on State Govt. - Further held, But though r. 31(3) does not specifically provide for consultation with Commission at time of revision that rule must be read subject to s. 32 P(4) and even at time of revision Govt. is bound to take advice of Commission and is bound to act accordingly (D) Whether Pepsu Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Rules, 1956, r. 31(3) is ultra vires of Act of 1955 ? - There is nothing in s. 32 K or any other provision of Act of 1955 to suggest that once a farm is taken out of provision of s. 32 A of Act of 1955 by application in s. 32 K that exemption is open to revision thereafter - s. 32 K lays down that, provisions of s. 32 A will not apply to efficiently managed farm etc. - There is nothing in Act which gives power to State Govt. to subject a farm to which s. 32 A does not apply in view of s. 32 K to provisions of s. 32 A later - Further held, Once farm as it was on October 30, 1956, gets benefit of s. 32 K(1)(iv), such a provision cannot be made by a rule, for in that case rule would be going beyond purview of Act and would be ultra vires - Rule 31(3) must be struck down as ultra vires of Act - (E) Whether burden of proof lied on party alleging prove malafides on part of Govt. ?- Further held, burden of proving that yield fixed in sch. C of r. 31 has been deliberately fixed so high as to be unattainable with object of making provision of s. 32 K(1)(iv) nugatory and of establishing mala fides of State Govt. is on party alleging same to be so - (F) Whether r. 31(4)(b) is ultra vires provisions of s. 32 K(1)(iv) of Act of 1955? - There is nothing in r. 31 which gives a discretion to Commission when applying proviso to r. 31(4)(b) to take into account factors like natural calamities, pests, locusts, excessive rain, floods, draught etc in deciding yield - Further held, quality of land is one factor which should be properly taken into account by Commission but as proviso to r. 31(4)(b), stands, Commission is bound to apply sch. C on a mathematical basis without consideration of other factors - It therefore, goes beyond provisions of s. 32 K - Proviso therefore, must be struck down as going beyond rule-making power inasmuch as it is ultra vires provisions of s. 32 K(1)(iv) - Appeal allowed.
