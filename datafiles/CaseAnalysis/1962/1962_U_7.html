 Union Territory of Tripura, Agartala v Gopal Chander Dutta Choudhury

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 266; AIR 1963 SC 601; 1963 CRLJ 491; 1963 (6) FLR 330; 1963 (2) LLJ 633; 1964 (2) SCJ 293; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Termination Simpliciter

Summary: Service - Central Services (Temporary Services) Rules, 1949, r. 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 309 and 311 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 31(1) and 33 - Whether Superintendent of Police passed an order in truth one of dismissal for misconduct, negligence, inefficiency or like cause or he enforced contractual right of State to terminate employment of respondent who was a temporary employee? - Order in terms merely terminates service of respondent- it was not preceded by any enquiry for ascertaining whether respondent was guilty of any misdemeanor, misconduct, negligence, inefficiency or a similar cause - Held, State is not prohibited by Constitution from reserving a right by terms of employment to terminate services of a public servant, and if in bona fide enforcement of that right employment is terminated protection of art. 311 of Constitution will not avail him, because such a termination does not amount to dismissal or removal from service - Before terminating employment of a public servant sanction of Court is not necessary - Order of termination of employment operates proprietors and is not made justifiable - Validity of such an order may be challenged only on ground that constitutional protection prescribed by art. 311 and rules made u/art. 309 was denied to public servant concerned - Appeal allowed.
