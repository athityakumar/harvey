V. T. S. Chandarasekhara Mudaliar (Died) and v Kulandaivelu Mudaliar and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 518; AIR 1963 SC 185; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Law - (A) Whether a Dravida Hindu widow can adopt a son exercising a Dower? - Held, yes she can - Hindu law recognizes her not merely as an agent of her husband but, to use felicitous Hindu metaphor, as his surviving half - Not bound to make an adoption and cannot be compelled to do so - But in absence of any specific authorisation by her husband, her power, subject only to assent of sapinda - Role of sapinda is only that of advisers - Where last male holder is a divided member of family, his widow can make an adoption with consent of a remoter sapinda if a nearer sapinda or sapinda improperly refused to give consent to adoption - (B) What is essence of adoption and on what matter validity of adoption is judged? - Further held, substitution of a son of deceased for spiritual reasons - Devolution of property is mere accessory to it - Validity of an adoption has to be judged by spiritual rather than temporal considerations and devolution of property is only of secondary importance - (C) Whether consent of sapinda is required for adoption by widow?- Further held, consent is necessary - It is a sufficient guarantee against any capricious action by widow in taking a boy in adoption - (D) What are powers of sapinda while giving consent to an adoption by widow? - Further held, he cannot refuse to give consent on basis that widow is preferring non sapinda over sapinda - He should ask himself two questions (i) whether proposed adoption would achieve object for which it was intended, and (ii) whether boy selected was duly qualified - He can raise objection on ground that boy belongs to a different caste, or is married, or is an idiot, or is suffering from an incurable disease, or he is notoriously a bad character, for in such cases he would not be suitable to continue line - Sapindaship is not a legal qualification or vice versa - (E) Whether High Court can issue a limited certificate u/o. 16 r.4 and O 18 r.3 (2) of Supreme Court Rules, 1950, and allow to raise a plea before Supreme Court? - Further held, it cannot but they assume that under certain circumstances it can do so - art.133 of Constitution does not empower High Court to limit certificate to any particular point - If decree of HC is one of affirmance, HC certifies that appeal involves a substantial question of law - Where certificate is given u/art. 133 entire appeal is before Supreme Court - But it does not follow from this legal position that Supreme Court should allow appellants to raise a plea before it if they had failed to do so before HC - Appeal dismissed.
