 Burmah Shell Oil Storage and Distributing Company India Limited v Belgaum Borough Municipality

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 295; AIR 1963 SC 906; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 216; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, sch 7, List 2, Entry 52, List 1, Entry 89 - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, s. 73(1)(iv) - (A) What is meaning of word consumption in sch 7, list 2, entry 52 of constitution? - Held, word consumption in its primary sense means act of consuming and in ordinary parlance means use of an article in a way which destroys, wastes or uses up that article - But in some legal contexts, word 'consumption' has a wider meaning - It is not necessary that by act of consumption commodity must be destroyed or used up - (B) Whether company was liable to pay octroi tax on goods brought into local area? - Further held, it was liable to pay tax - Goods have been brought in for purposes of consumption when a person brings them either for his own use or consumption, or to put them in way of others in area, who are to use and consume - Act of sale is merely means for putting goods in way of use or consumption or use - So long as goods are brought inside area for sale within area to an ultimate consumer, it makes no difference that consumer does not consume them in area but takes them out for consumption elsewhere - Word 'therein' does not mean that all act of consumption must take place in area of municipality - It is sufficient if goods are brought inside area to be delivered to ultimate consumer in that area because taxable event is entry of goods which are meant to reach an ultimate user or consumer in area - Appeal allowed.
