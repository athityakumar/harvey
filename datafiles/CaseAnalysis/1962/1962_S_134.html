 Kumar Bimal Chandra Sinha v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1962 SC 1912; 1963 (29) CutLT 169; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951

Summary: Land & Property - Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951, ss. 2, 5, 6 and 26 - Orissa Tenancy Act, 1913 - Whether appellant's raiyati interests in lands and in buildings standing on those lands have been affected by abolition of his interest as proprietors and whether state authorities had legally taken possession of those? - Reading two definitions, 'Estate' and 'Intemediary' together, position in law is that 'estate' includes interest, by whatever name called, of all persons, who hold some right in land between state at apex and raiyat at base and Act is intended to abolish all intermediaries and rent receivers and to establish direct relationship between state, in which all such interests vest after abolition under Act, and tillers of soil - Appellants hold those properties with buildings not as proprietors as such, but as rayats - Held, rent payable by appellants as raiyats in respect of disputed lands would form part of assets which have to be included in gross assets in determining compensation, but that does not mean that interests of raiyats also have become vested in State as a result of notification u/s. 3, r/w s. 5, therefore, appellant's raiyati interests in lands and in buildings standing on those lands have not been affected by abolition of his interest as proprietors and that state authorities had illegally taken possession - Appeal allowed.
