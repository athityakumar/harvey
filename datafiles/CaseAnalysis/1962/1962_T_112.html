Shri Raja Durga Singh of Solan v Tholu

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 510; AIR 1963 SC 361; 1962 (64) PLR 837; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 9, 100 and 101- Punjab Tenancy Act,1887, ss. 7(3), 44 and 77(3) - Evidence Act, 1872, s.35 - (A) Whether legislature barred suits from cognizance of a civil Court where there was dispute between parties that a person cultivating land was a tenant or not u/s. 7? - Held, barred only cases where there was no dispute - A suit for possession and mesne profits against defendants who claimed to be occupancy tenants but whose status as tenants was denied by plaintiff - Is not barred from cognizance of civil court u/s. 73(3) r/w proviso 1 - (B) Whether finding as to status of defendant is finding of fact can be set aside in second appeal in an ejectment suit? - Further held, it can be set aside - Though for determining question documentary evidence falls to be considered, finding on question is no less a finding of fact than may have been case if evidence to be considered is merely oral - (C) When there is conflict between entries in recorded of rights, which entry would prevail?- Further held, later entry which must prevail - An issue of law does not arise merely because documents which are not instruments of title or otherwise direct foundation of rights but are merely historical documents, have to be construed - Appeal allowed.
