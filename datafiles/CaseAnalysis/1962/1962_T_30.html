Income Tax Officer, Madras v S. K.Habibullah, Madras

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1962 SC 918; [1962] 44 ITR 809; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 107; 1963 (1) SCJ 535; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 716

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 35 - Assessment - Rectification of assessment - Revision Order - Show Cause Notice - Respondent who was a partner in firm was assessed by Appellant/revenue for the years 1946-47 and 1947-48 and later appellant issued notices to show cause why assessments of assessee should not be rectified under s. 35 of the Act - Appellant revised assessment of assessee - Respondent applied to Commissioner of Income-tax praying for revision of orders who found s. 35 was properly invoked for rectification of assessments and HC found no justification to invoke provision - Hence, instant appeal - Whether relying upon s. 35 of the Act an Income tax Officer may rectify assessment of a person who was a partner of a firm when the assessment of the firm is completed before dt. 1-4-1952 - Held, power to rectify assessment of a partner consequent upon assessment of firm of which he was partner by including or correcting his share of profit or loss can therefore be exercised only in Case of assessment of firm made on or after dt. 1-4-1952 - Income-tax officer has no jurisdiction u/s. 35 of the Act to rectify assessment of a partner of a firm consequent upon assessment or reassessment of firm disclosing an error made before dt. 1-4-1952 - Appeals dismissed.
