Neta Ram v Jiwan Lal

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1963 SC 499; 1962 (2) SCJ 270; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, Patiala and East Punjab States Union Urban Rent Restriction ordinance

Summary: Rent Control - Houses and Rents - Patiala and East Punjab States Union Urban Rent restriction Ordinance, 2006 BK, s. 13(3) (a) (iii), s. 13(3)(b) - (A) Whether it is necessary for landlord to satisfy Rent Controller about genuineness of his claim before he can evict tenant from land? - Held, before a landlord can obtain an order for ejectment of his tenant on ground of his requirement for reconstruction of a house, he must satisfy Rent Controller about genuineness of his claim - This can only be established by looking at all surrounding circumstances such as condition of building - Possibility of its being put to a more profitable use after construction, means of landlord - It is not enough that landlord comes forward and says that he entertains a particular intention, however strongly, said to be entertained by him - S. 13(3)(b) of Pepsu Urban Rent Restriction Ordinance speaks not of bona-fides of landlord, but says on other hand, that claim of landlord that he requires building for reconstruction and re-erection must be bona fide, that is to say honest in circumstances - Appeal allowed.
