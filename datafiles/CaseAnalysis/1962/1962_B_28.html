 State of Orissa and Others v Bidyabhushan Mohapatra

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1963 SC 779; 1963 (1) LLJ 239; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 648

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227, 309 - Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850 - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, R. 55 - Disciplinary Proceeding - Illegal Gratification - Dismissal - Unlawful Discrimination - Invalidation of Proceedings - Respondent was a permanent non-gazette employee of appellant/State - Information was received by appellant State of that respondent was habitually receiving illegal gratification and possessed property totally disproportionate to his income - Tribunal hold that there was reliable evidence to support charge of corruption and possession disproportionate to his income and recommended that respondent be dismissed from service - After consulting Public Service Commission, Governor directed for dismissal of respondent from service - Respondent appealed to HC u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution, for quashing order of dismissal and entire disciplinary proceeding - HC hold that findings of Tribunal were vitiated as it had failed to observe rules of natural justice and quashed order of dismissal - Hence instant appeal - Whether on basis of charges, respondent would maintain punishment of dismissal or else a lesser punishment would suffice and Whether HC was right in holding that findings of Tribunal - Held, no right of appeal against order passed imposing penalty upon a public servant, but same could not be regarded as a ground sustaining unlawful discrimination infringing protection u/art. 14 of Constitution - Court hold that an enquiry directing procedure under Act, 1850 and not u/r. 55 of Rules did not result in any discrimination, leading to invalidation of proceedings against public servant under the Act, 1850 - Absence of proof of any prejudice to respondent/public servant, mere adoption of one procedure in preference to another permissible procedure would not justify an inference of unlawful discrimination - Court viewed that HC had no power and erred directing Governor to reconsider order of dismissal and there had been violation of rules of natural justice - Order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
