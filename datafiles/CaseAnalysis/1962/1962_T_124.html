 Orient Weaving Mills Private Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1963 SC 98; 1963 (29) CutLT 115; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 311; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 481; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Keywords: Society

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944, s. 37(2)(xvii) - Notification 74 of 1959 D/- 31-07-1959 and 70 of 1960 D/- 30-04-1960 - Companies Act, 1913 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g), 43 and 245 - Central Excise - Exemption of duty - On cotton fabrics produced on power looms - To co-operative societies of weavers - Notification - Constitutional validity of s. 37(2)(xvii) of 1944 Act and r. 8(1)of rules challenged - Rules alleged to be suffering from vice of excessive delegation of power - Validity of notification in light of arts. 14, 19(1)f) and (g) of Constitution - Held, no excessive delegation of power to exempt - No violation of constitutional provisions - Appeal dismissed.
