 Waverly Jute Mills Company Limited v Raymon and Company (India) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 419; AIR 1963 SC 90; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 209

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Madras District Municipalities Act, 1920, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Agriculture & Agri Products - Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, Sch. 7, List 1, Entry 48, List 2, Entry 26, List 3, Entry 7 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 2(a) and 33 - Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952 - (A) Application of general maxim Generalia specialibis non derogant - Held, wherever there are two entries in a schedule out of which one has general character and other specific has a specific character, former is construed in a manner so that latter is excluded - (B) Whether Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act being a legislation on Forward Contracts must be held to fall within exclusive competence of Union under entry 48 in List I? - Further held, yes - Word 'market' in entry 48 'Future Markets' of List 1 of Seventh Schedule has reference only to business and not to any location - It would be a legislation on Future Markets within meaning of entry 48 in List 1 of seventh schedule - Though a law on Forward Contracts would also be a law with respect to Trade and commerce coming under entry 26 of List II entry 48 in List I being a specific one must be excluded from general entry 26 in List II - (C) When can an arbitration proceeding be said to be without jurisdiction? - Further held, an agreement for arbitration is base upon on which jurisdiction of arbitrators to act rest, where such jurisdiction is not in existence while entering duties proceedings are deemed to be without jurisdiction - Effect of lack of initial jurisdiction in arbitrators on proceedings - Proceedings are void - It is not cured by appearance of parties in proceedings - Parties can enter into fresh agreement to refer to arbitration - An agreement would be effective and operative only when it is separate from and independent of contract which is impugned as illegal - Dispute relating to validity of contract to be decided by court and not by arbitrators - If a contract is illegal and void, an arbitration clause which is one of terms thereof, must also perish along with it - A dispute relating to validity of contract is in such cases for court and not for arbitrators to decide - Appeal allowed.
