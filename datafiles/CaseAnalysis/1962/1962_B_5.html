 Bachhittar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 541; AIR 1963 SC 395; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 713; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 166 and 311(2) - Rules of Business of Govt. of PEPSU, rr. 8, 25, 28(1), 34 - (A) Dispute regarding order of Revenue Minister of PEPSU - Held, merely writing something on file does not amount to an order - Order of State Govt. has to be expressed in name of Governor and then it has to be communicated to person who would be affected by that order before State and that person can be bound by that order - Until order is communicated to person affected by it, it would be open to Council of Ministers to consider matter over and over again and, therefore, till its communication order cannot be regarded as anything more than provisional in character - (B) Whether Chief Minister of Punjab was competent to deal with matter inasmuch as it pertained to portfolio of Revenue Minister? - Further held, order passed by Chief Minister, even though it is on a matter pertaining to portfolio of Revenue Minister, will be deemed to be an order of Council of Ministers - Appeal dismissed.
