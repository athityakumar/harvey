 Jaswant Sugar Mills, Limited, Meerut v Lakshmi Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1963 SC 677; 1965 (10) FLR 179; 1963 (1) LLJ 524; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 2(c)(iii) - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Dismissal - Sabotage and slow-down strike - Conciliation officer granted permission in respect of 11 workmen, remaining fifty-two workmen in his view were mere 'passive participants' in go-slow campaign - Company preferred an appeal to Labour Appellate Tribunal, Lucknow, against direction of conciliation officer refusing to grant permission to dismiss fifty-two workmen, but appeal was rejected - Whether conciliation officer functioning u/cls. 29 is an industrial Tribunal? - Because in view of Appellate Tribunal conciliation officer was not an 'authority' within meaning of s. 2 (c) (iii) of Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act - Held, a conciliation officer functioning u/cl. 29 is not an industrial tribunal constituted under 1947 Act - Appeals dismissed.
