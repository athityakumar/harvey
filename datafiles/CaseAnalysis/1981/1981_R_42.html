Corporation Of The City Of Nagpur Civil Lines, Nagpur & Anr. v Ramchandra S/O Gurunath Modak & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 216; (1981) SCC (L&S) 455; (1981) 2 SCC 714; AIR 1984 SC 626; 1981 (43) FLR 78; 1984 LabIC 179; 1981 (2) LLJ 6; 1981(1) SCALE 503; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 22; 1981 (2) SLR 274; 1981 UJ 316

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Service - Municipalities & Local Governments - Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948 - Suspension - Validity - It was case of respondents that suspension was ordered in connection with departmental inquiry relating to 2 accidents, which occurred during construction of stadium, which was being looked after by respondents - Complaint u/s. 304A IPC was filed against respondents - On appeal, Appellate Authority dismissed said appeal - Aggrieved by that. Respondents filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed petition and quashed order of suspension on the ground that order of suspension pending departmental inquiry was by Municipal Commissioner, who was not competent to suspend respondents pending departmental inquiry - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that u/s. 59 (3) of the Act, Municipal Commissioner was the competent authority to suspend respondents pending a departmental inquiry - Held, merely because accused was acquitted, the power of authority concerned to continue departmental inquiry was not taken away nor was its direction in any way fettered - However, as quite some time had elapsed since departmental inquiry had started authority concerned would take into consideration this factor in coming to the conclusion if it was really worthwhile to continue departmental inquiry in event of acquittal of respondents - If, however, authority felt that there was sufficient evidence and good grounds to proceed with inquiry, it could certainly do so - In case respondents were acquitted SC direct that order of suspension should be revoked and respondents would be reinstated and allowed full salary thereafter even though authority chose to proceed with inquiry - Order of HC was quashed - Appeal allowed.
