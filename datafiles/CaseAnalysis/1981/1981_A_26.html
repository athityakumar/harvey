Ajitsingh Thakursingh and Another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 428; (1981) SCC (Cr) 184; (1981) 1 SCC 495; AIR 1981 SC 733; 1981 CRLJ 293; 1981 CRLR 167; 1981 MLJ 357; 1981(1) SCALE 54; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Kirpan, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 326 - Murder - High Court condoned delay in filing appeal - HC quashed order of acquittal of appellant passed by Trial Court - Whether HC right in condoning delay in filing appeal and disposing the appeal on merits? - Held, HC erred in condoning the delay - No merits in appeal filed before HC - Trial Court found that evidence was untrustworthy and accused was given benefit of doubt - Eye-witnesses were found hostile to accused even prior to incident - No independent witness produced in support of case - HC has omitted to consider that integrity of evidence alone can ensure whether accused are guilty - HC erred in interfering with judgment of Trial Court - Order of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
