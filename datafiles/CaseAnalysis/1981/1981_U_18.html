Akhilesh Prasad v Union Territory of Mizoram

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 424; (1981) 2 SCC 150; (1981) SCC (Cr) 337; AIR 1981 SC 806; 1981 CRLJ 407; 1981 CRLR 271; 1981(1) SCALE 292; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Defence and Security Forces -Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, s.3 (1) -CrPC,1973, s.197(2)- Constitution, Sch.7, List 1, Entry 2 -Whether Central Reserve Police Force is included in expression "Armed Forces of the Union" u/s.197(2)? -The contents of Entry 2 in List I of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India clearly envisages armed forces other than the three well known forces of State namely the naval, military and air forces. s.3 (1) of the Central Reserve Police Force Act also makes it clear that the Central Reserve Police Force is a part of the "Armed Forces of the Union" -Held, Just because the Central Reserve Police Force is a reserve force it does not follow that it is not a regularly operating force.It squarely falls within the expression "Armed Forces of the Union" as used in S. 197 (2) of the Act of 1949 -Appeal allowed.
