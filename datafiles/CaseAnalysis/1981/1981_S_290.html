Dudh Nath Pandey v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 107; (1981) 2 SCC 166; (1981) SCC (Cr) 379; AIR 1981 SC 911; 1981 ALJ 228; 1981 CRLJ 618; 1981 CrLR(SC) 353; 1981(1) SCALE 285; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant convicted for offence u/s. 302 and was sentenced to death - Appeal - (A) Plea of alibi - Held, plea of alibi postulates physical impossibility of presence of accused at scene of offence by reason of his presence at another place - Plea can therefore succeed only if it is shown that accused was so far away at relevant time that he could not be present at place where crime was committed - Evidence of defence witnesses, accepting it at its face value, is consistent with appellant's presence at Naini factory and at scene of offence at relevant time - (B) Question of sentence - Held, since a part of crucial event has been screened from Court's scrutiny and possibility of an altercation between appellant and deceased cannot reasonably be excluded, it unsafe to sentence appellant to extreme penalty - Further held, view of HC and Sessions Court correct - Conviction of appellant u/s. 302 confirmed - Sentence of death imposed upon set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
