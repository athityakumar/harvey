Rameshchandra Kachardas Porwal and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Pathak, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 423; (1981) 2 SCC 722; AIR 1981 SC 1127; 1981(1) SCALE 334; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Trade; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Markets, Agricultural Produce Marketing, Maharashtra Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Act, 1963, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Maharashtra Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Rules 1967

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Trade - Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960 - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939, s.4A(2) - Petitioners/traders involved in business of commodities in B - State in exercise of powers conferred by s.4A(2) of 1939 Act, by notification declared B as one of principal market yards for market area - Director issued circular that it was not permissible for anyone to carry on trade in agricultural commodities outside G market yard - On W.P filed by petitioners HC postponed commencement of function of Principal Market from time to time - Petitioners contended that no provision in Act that trader could be compelled to principal market established - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioners entitled to continue business in old market - Held, neither G market nor T market had convenience or facility or was ready for use on date of notification as Principal Market for concerned market area - Material on record was clear that reasonable conveniences and facilities were available in both markets - Traders had attempted to avoid moving into new markets and they had successful in stalling notifications from becoming effective for quite number of years - Old markets had existed from ancient days and it had become necessary to establish modern market yards with conveniences and facilities - Notifications for new market yards cancelled and old markets allowed to function for some time - Traders should be provided sufficient time to enable them to prepare themselves to move into new market - Confusion was not on arbitrary part of Govt. but was result of desire to accommodate traders - Petitions disposed of.
