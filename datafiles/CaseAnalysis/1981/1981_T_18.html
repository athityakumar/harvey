Firestone Tyre and Rubber Company of India v Workmen Employed Represented By Firestone

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 234; (1981) 3 SCC 451; (1981) SCC (L&S) 515; AIR 1981 SC 1626; 1981 (43) FLR 258; 1981 LabIC 1110; 1981 (2) LLJ 218; 1981 (2) LLN 291; 1981(3) SCALE 1075; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 20; 1981 (2) SLJ 422; 1981 (2) SLR 714; 1981 UJ 624

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Laws - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.10(4), 15 - Dismissal - Reference - Jurisdiction of Labour Court - Charge-sheets vague and not served on employees - Tribunal held, cannot travel outside terms of reference - Tribunal can allow employer to adduce evidence and ask the parties to lead evidence - Appeal partly allowed and matter remitted to Industrial Tribunal.
