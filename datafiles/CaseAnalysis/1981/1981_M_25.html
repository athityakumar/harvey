Indu Bala Bose & Ors. v Manindra Chandra Bose & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 324; (1982) 1 SCC 20; AIR 1982 SC 133; 1981(3) SCALE 1766; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1188; 1982 UJ 7

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Will - Probate - Refusal - challenged - One 'R'/testator had executed Will and plaintiff/respondent nos. 1 and 2 were executors of Will - Plaintiff nos. 1 & 2 filed application for probate of Will before Trial court and appellants/defendants contended that executor was not in physical or mental condition to execute Will - Trial court dismissed suit and refused to grant probate of Will on ground that there were certain doubts and suspicions about condition of testator's mind - Aggrieved plaintiffs filed application before HC which set aside decree of Trial court and granted probate of Will - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there is no evidence that executor did not have the mental capacity to execute the Will and D.W. 2 who visited executor during his illness, and D.W. 1, doctor who deposed by perusing the prescriptions, did not depose that executor was in coma or had lost his mental faculty. Thus, court could not find any suspicious circumstance surrounding the execution of the Will and rule, as observed by the Privy Council, is that 'where a will is charged with suspicion, the rules enjoin a reasonable septicism, not as obdurate persistence in disbelief. They do not demand from the judge, even in circumstances of grave suspicion, a resolute and impenetrable incredulity. He is never required to close his mind to the truth.' Hence, Trial court was wrong in holding that the circumstances in question were suspicious and the HC was fully justified in setting aside the judgment of the Trial court. Appeal dismissed.
