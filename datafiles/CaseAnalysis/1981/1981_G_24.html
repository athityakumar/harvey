Guda Vijayalakshmi v Guda Ramchandra Sekhara Sastry

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 417; (1981) SCC (Cr) 574; (1981) 2 SCC 646; AIR 1981 SC 1143; 1981 (1) DMC 330; 1981 HLR 312; 1981(1) SCALE 794; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 223; 1981 UJ 695

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil procedure - Code of Civil Procedure Act, 1976 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Divorce maintenance - Transfer - Jurisdiction - Incorporation - Petitioner/wife filed suit for maintenance against her husband/respondent - On receipt of notice of suit, respondent filed divorce u/s. 13 of 1955 Act - Petitioner u/s. 25 of 1908 Act sought to get husband's suit transferred to E was allowed by HC - Hence instant transfer petition - Whether petitioner entitled to transfer petition u/s. 25 of 1908 Act - Held, s. 25 of Code indicates that wide jurisdiction and powers had conferred to transfer suit, appeal or other proceeding from HC or Civil Court for ends of justice - Court enjoy power and jurisdiction under provisions of transferring suit, unless power and jurisdiction were taken away by statute - Hence, s. 21 of 1955 Act could not be construed to exclude jurisdiction conferred on Court u/s. 25 of 1908 Act - Jurisdiction conferred by s.25 of 1908 Act was not affected by s.21 of 1955 Act - Hence, Court enjoys power and jurisdiction to entertain application u/s. 25 of 1908 Act and ss. 21 , 21A of 1955 Act do not exclude - Power u/s. 25 of 1908 Act as s. 25 came into force only after ss. 21, 21A had incorporated in 1955 Act - Preliminary objection rejected - Order accordingly.
