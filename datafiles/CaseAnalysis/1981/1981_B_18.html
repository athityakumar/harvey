C.I.T. Bombay v Bar Council Of Maharashtrabar Council Of Indiabar Council Of

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 300; (1981) 3 SCC 308; AIR 1981 SC 1462; 1981 (22) CTR 106; [1981] 130 ITR 28; 1981(1) SCALE 679; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 542; 1981 TAXLR 889; [1981] 6 TAXMAN 1; 1981 UJ 507

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss.10 (23A),11, 2(15) - Assessment - Securities - Enrollment fees - Notification - Exemption - Charitable purpose - Respondent/assessee-Bar Council was body corporate established under 1961 Act - During accounting periods relevant to A/Y assessee derived income from securities and other income by way of enrollment fees - IO subjected to tax income from both sources for all three years - Appellate Assistant Commissioner negatived exemption claimed u/s.10(23A) in absence of Central Govt. notification according approval to association and with regard to claim for exemption in respect of interest on securities - Tribunal concluded that assessee/Council was entitled to exemption u/s.10(23A) in respect of its income by way of enrollment fees - Whether assessee/Council would be exempt from tax liability u/s.11 of 1961 Act - Held, assessee-Council would be exempt from any tax liability u/s.11 of Act - Securities required to exempted from tax liability by assessee/Council on trust wholly for charitable purpose for advancement of an object of general public utility - Securities for educational purpose or for any other charitable purpose then exemption u/s.11 would be admissible to extent available under law - S.11 was relatively wider in its scope and ambit that while s.10 (23A) granted absolute exemption in respect of particular types of income s.11 imposed certain conditions for exemption but such exemption was available for all sources and there was nothing inherently improbable or inconceivable about two provisions operating simultaneously and as such claim for exemption u/s.11 was available to assessee-Council provided it satisfied all the requirements of that provision - Appeals disposed of.
