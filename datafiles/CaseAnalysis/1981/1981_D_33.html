Dr. S.P. Kapoor Etc. v State Of Himachal Pradesh & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 285; (1981) 4 SCC 716; (1982) SCC (L&S) 14; AIR 1981 SC 2181; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Himachal Pradesh Health Services Rules, 1974, rr. 9, 9(4) and 10(n)(iii) - Appointment - Inter se seniority - Entitled to - Petitioners were appointed as Deputy Director of Health Services on regular basis - However, appointment of respondent Nos. 2 and 4 as Deputy Directors of Health Services was contrary to provisions of the Rules - Respondent Nos. 2 and 4 were appointed in disregard of rights of petitioners - Petitioners preferred petitions before HC - HC held that petitioners were specifically appointed on ad hoc basis and that, therefore, petitioners could not claim right to post of Deputy Directors of Health Services or to seniority on basis of their ad hoc appointments, though they could add period of such appointment in matter of experience for promotion and confirmation in view of addition of notice to r. 9 of the Rules - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, inter se seniority had to be determined only in accordance with r. 10 (n) (iii) of the Rules - R. 9(4) of Rules lays down that super-time Grade II (General) posts should be filled by promotion of HPHS-Grade I officers with not less than 7 years of service in category or Specialists' Grade officers with not less than 5 years of service in that category - Selection would had to be on recommendation of a Departmental Promotion Committee on basis of merit-cum-seniority of officers concerned above on 50:50 basis keeping in view nature of duties of post - Contention of appellant was that Departmental Promotion Committee should not had started rotation to post of Director of Health Services with a Specialist - However, contention of Govt. before HC was that r. 9 of the Rules was silent on question as to category with which roster should be started and, therefore, State Govt. decided to supplement the Rule by starting roster with category of Specialists had regard to fact that senior-most Specialist officer available on date of Departmental Promotion Committee meeting on 3-11-1979 had put in greater length of qualifying service than senior-most HPHS (Grade I) officer - HC expressed view that Specialists had an advantage for their category starting roster by senior most of Specialists had put in more number of years of qualifying service than HPHS (Grade I) officers - HC was justified in taking view and that Govt. was right in getting roster started with Specialists instead of HPHS (Grade I) officers - Respondents were directed to decide the matter of selection of Deputy Directors and Directors of Health Services - Order accordingly.
