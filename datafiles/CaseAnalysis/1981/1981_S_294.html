Janeshwar Das Aggarwal v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Baharul Islam, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 544; (1981) 3 SCC 10; (1981) SCC (Cr) 616; AIR 1981 SC 1646; 1981 ALJ 887; 1981 CRLR 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Misappropriation of goods - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants filed instant appeals by special leave against the judgment of the HC by which the conviction of the appellant u/s. 409 IPC on three counts was affirmed -

Held, before a conviction under Section 409, Indian Penal Code can be recorded, the prosecution must prove two essential facts : (1) the factum of entrustment, and (2) the factum of misappropriation of the entrusted articles. Even if it be assumed that entrustment was proved in this case, there is absolutely no evidence to show, either direct or circumstantial, that the appellant had misappropriated any of the articles in the godowns. As the godowns were open and accessible to all and sundry, the possibility of the goods having been pilfered or stolen away by others cannot be excluded. HC was, therefore, clearly wrong in holding that as the appellant has not given any explanation for the shortage, he must be presumed to have misappropriated the articles kept in the godowns. In the present state of evidence, no such legal inference could be drawn. Even the two Chowkidars who were in physical charge of the godowns and not been examined to prove who caused the shortage. In view of this unsatisfactory nature of the evidence, it is impossible for us to sustain the conviction of the appellant. Conviction and the sentence passed upon the appellant are set aside and the appellant is acquitted of the charge framed against him. Appeal Allowed.
