State of Karnataka v Pratap Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 279; (1981) 2 SCC 335; (1981) SCC (Cr) 453; AIR 1981 SC 872; [1981] 51 Comp Cas 198; 1981 CRLJ 595; 1981 CrLR(SC) 225; [1981] 128 ITR 573; 1981(1) SCALE 529; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Fema; Health & Drug

Summary: Health & Drug - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, ss. 18(a)(ii), 18(c), 18 A and 34 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 23 C - Drugs Control Act, 1950, ss. 27(a)(ii), 27(a)(i) and 28 - Conviction under the provisions of Drugs and Cosmetics Act and Drugs Control Act - Complicity of partners - What does expression a person in charge and responsible for conduct of affairs of a company mean? - Held, the second respondent was sought to be made liable on the ground that he along with the first respondent was in charge of the conduct of the business of the firm - Word 'company' includes a firm or other association and same test must apply to a director in-charge and a partner of a firm in charge of a business - It seems to us that in context a person 'in charge' must mean that person should be in over all control of day to day business of company or firm - The evidence in the present case shows that it was respondent No. 1 and not respondent No. 2 who was in over all control of the day to day business of the firm - The second respondent is not liable to be convicted merely because he had the right to participate in the business of the firm under the terms of the Partnership Deed - Appeal dismissed.
