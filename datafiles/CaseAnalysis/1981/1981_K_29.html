Jeewanti Pandey v Kishan Chandra Pandey

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 318; (1981) 4 SCC 517; (1981) SCC (Cr) 853; AIR 1982 SC 3; 1981 ALJ 1341; 1981 (7) ALR 603; 1981 CrLR(SC) 652; 1981 (2) DMC 256; 1982 HLR 130; 1981(3) SCALE 1641; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1003; 1982 UJ 57

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Parties to marriage originally belonged to a village within territorial jurisdiction of District Judge, Almora - Marriage took place in New Delhi and wife resided with her uncle in Delhi ever since marriage - Husband being employed also resident of Delhi - Petition for nullity of marriage under s. 12 of Hindu Marriage Act filed by husband in Court of District Judge, Almora on ground that parties were residents of a village within territorial jurisdiction of that Court - Meaning of residence - Held, no jurisdiction of District Judge to try petition - In order to give jurisdiction on ground of residence, something more than a temporary stay is required - Residence must be more or less of a permanent character and of such a nature that Court in which resident is sued should be his natural forum - Judgement of High court Set aside.
