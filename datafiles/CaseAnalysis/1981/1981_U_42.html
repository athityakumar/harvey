Bhagwan Dass Jain v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 276; (1981) SCC (Tax) 84; (1981) 2 SCC 135; AIR 1981 SC 907; 1981 (21) CTR 339; [1981] 128 ITR 315; 1981(1) SCALE 28; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 808; 1981 TAXLR 664; [1981] 5 TAXMAN 7; 1981 UJ 169

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income-tax Act, 1961 -Constitution of India, 1950 - Tax on building falling under Entry 49 of List II of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution - Sustainability - Petitioner/assessee filed writ petition before HC and contended that inclusion of any amount u/s. 23(2) of the Act in his income was unconstitutional as there could be no income at all in such a case accruing to him in the true sense of that term, liability that was sought to be imposed under the Act in respect of his residential house was, therefore, in its pith and substance a tax on building falling under Entry 49 of List II of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution and hence Parliament could not impose said liability under a law made in exercise of its legislative power under Entry 82 of List I of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution which authorised it only to levy taxes on income other than agricultural income - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether it was open to Income-tax Officer while computing liability of assessee to tax under the Act to include in income of assessee any amount calculated in accordance with s.23 (2) of the Act in respect of a house in occupation of assessee for the purposes of his own residence - Held, computation of income for purposes of levy of income tax in accordance with s. 23(2) of the Act was justifiable under Entry 82 of List I of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution - Government of India Act, 1935 was enacted when Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 was in force - S. 9 of Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 provided for levy of income tax on basis of bona fide annual value of property even when it was in occupation of assessee for purposes of his own residence - It would not be wrong to construe the word 'income' in Entry 82 as including all items which were taxable under contemporaneous law relating to tax on incomes which was in force at the time when Constitution was enacted - Even in its ordinary economic sense, expression 'income' included not merely what was received or what comes in by exploiting the use of a property but also what one saves by using it oneself - That which could be converted into income could be reasonably regarded as giving rise to income - Tax levied under the Act was on income (though computed in an artificial way) from house property in the above sense and not on house property - Entry 49 of List II of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution was not, therefore, attracted - Levy in question squarely falls under Entry 82 of List I of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution - Petition dismissed.
