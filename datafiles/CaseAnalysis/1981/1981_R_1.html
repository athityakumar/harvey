Union of India and Others v R. C. Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Baharul Islam, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 209; (1981) 2 SCC 308; (1981) SCC (L&S) 323; AIR 1981 SC 951; 1981 (42) FLR 348; 1981 LabIC 498; 1981 (1) LLJ 402; 1981 (1) LLN 569; 1981(1) SCALE 320; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 854; 1981 UJ 197

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Payment of bonus, Stoppage of bonus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Municipalities and Local Governments - Payment of bonus - Stoppage of bonus - Entitled to - Respondents were employees of Delhi Development Authorityb (DDA) and were paid bonus 10 years prior to 1973-74 - However, it was stopped thereafter on advice of Ministry of Law - Respondents challenged the stoppage of bonus before HC by wat of writ petition, which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DDA was a 'local authority' and was bound to pay bonus to respondents under the 1965 Act - Held, proper and careful scrutiny of s. 3(31) of the 1897 Act suggested that an authority in order to be a local Authority, should be of like nature and character as a Municipal Committee, District Board or Body of Port Commissioners, possessing, therefore, many, if not all, of distinctive attributes and characteristics of a Municipal Committee, District Board, or Body of Port Commissioners - But, possessing one essential feature, namely, that it was legally entitled to or entrusted by Govt. with, control and management of a municipal or local fund - In instant case, DDA was constituted for specific purpose of 'the development of Delhi according to plan' - Planned development of towns was a Governmental function which was traditionally entrusted by various Municipal Acts in different States to municipal bodies - With growing specialisation, along with growth of titanic metropolitan complexes, legislatures had felt need for creation of separate town-planning or development authorities for individual cities - DDA was one such - It was an authority, to which was entrusted by Statute a Governmental function ordinarily entrusted to municipal bodies - However, activities of Authority were limited to local area of Union Territory of Delhi - HC assumed that DDA had extra-territorial powers extending to peripheral areas in adjoining States - There is no basis in Statute for assumption made by HC - DDA enjoys a considerable degree of autonomy, as was seen from summary of the provisions of the 1897 Act - Powers of Central Govt. over DDA were usual supervisory powers which every State Govt. exercises over municipalities, district boards etc. - Such powers of supervision did not made municipalities disautonomous and mere satellites - DDA was constituted for performing one of several functions which a local authority might perform - Local authorities performing other functions were referred to as 'local authorities' in the 1897 Act by which DDA was created - While DDA was referred to as Authority, was no ground for holding that DDA was not a 'local authority' as defined by s. 3(31) of the 1897 Act - DDA was endowed with all usual attributes and characteristics of a 'local authority' and there was no reason to hold that it was not a 'local authority' - DDA was a Local Authority and therefore, provision of the 1965 Act were not attracted - However, direction was issued to DDA to consider the instant matter - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
