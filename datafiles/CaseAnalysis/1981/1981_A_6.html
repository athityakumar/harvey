Abdul Karim Allarakha v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 468; (1982) 3 SCC 227; AIR 1982 SC 61

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, s. 18 - Award - Enhancement - Land Acquisition Officer awarded a total compensation of Rs 6, 92, 317.34 - On a reference u/s. 18 of the 1953 Act made by the Collector Civil Judge, reduced the amount of compensation for the land to Rs 40 per sq. yd. - On appeal, HC enhanced the compensation amount to Rs 42 per sq. yd. and further granted an amount of Rs 5000 as compensation for the electric fittings and fixtures - Hence instant appeal - Whether competency of the reference made by the Collector u/s. 18 of the Act, 1953, at the instance of the State Govt., for reduction of the amount of compensation awarded by him and second, as to whether the Collector by his award having made an offer on behalf of the State Govt., could have made a reference u/s. 18 of the Act at all -

Held, No doubt, the award made by the Land Acquisition Officer is in the nature of an offer, but it is subject to the right of the State Govt. to ask for a reference u/s. 18 of the Act. U/s. 18 of the Act, one of the questions that may be referred by the Collector for the determination of the court is as to the objection to the amount of compensation awarded. If the State Govt. is dissatisfied with the award made by the Land Acquisition Officer, it may apply for a reference u/s. 18 for reduction of the amount of compensation. The reference made by the Collector u/s. 18 of the Act as to the amount of compensation was, therefore, competent. Both the points must, therefore, be answered in favour of the respondent. HC has enhanced the amount of compensation awarded for the land to Rs 42 per sq. yd. as against Rs 40 per sq. yd. awarded by Civil Judge. Order accordingly.
