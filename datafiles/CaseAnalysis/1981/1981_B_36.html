Bibi Rahmani Khatoon and Others v Harkoo Gope and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 409; (1981) 3 SCC 173; AIR 1981 SC 1450; 1981(1) SCALE 739; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Abatement, Effect Of

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Consolidation of Holdings and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 4 - Appellant filed a suit in Court of Addl. Subordinate Judge for declaration of their title and for recovery of possession of agricultural lands comprised in two holdings bearing khata nos. 458 and 459 - Defendants in suit were respondents and three others defendants Nos 5, 6 and 7 - Defendant no 7 claiming an interest in khata no 458 on basis of a sale deed by one Deonandan Singh who was defendant 5 in trial court - It must be made distinctly clear that Brahmadeo claimed interest in khata no 458 while present respondents claimed interest in khata no 459 only - Trial court decreed suit declaring that plaintiffs were owners of both khatas - Appeal in court of District Judge as per his judgment and decree dismissed appeal and affirmed decree of trial court - Present respondents alone preferred Second Appeal in HC and neither defendant 7 who died pending appeal before District Court and whose LRs were not impleaded, nor anyone claiming under him either came to be substituted in appeal pending in District Court nor any of them preferred appeal to HC - Hrkoo Gope and three others who claimed interest in khata no 459 only, preferred second appeal - An affidavit was filed by appellants drawing attention of Court to a notification u/s. 3 of Act and further intimating to Court that village in which disputed khatas were situated was taken up for consolidation of holdings and, therefore, appeal pending in HC would abate in view of provision contained in s. 4 of Act - HC accepted submission and disposed of appeal by its order - Whether the findings of HC is correct in law? - Held, HC was right in not only holding that second appeal pending before it abated but also judgment and decree of trial court - Since defendant no 7 claimed separate, specific and exclusive right to khata no 458, on his death his LRs ought to have been substituted - No one was substituted on his behalf - Obviously, therefore, appeal preferred by him abated - It may also be made clear that LR of him have not preferred second appeal - Second Appeal was preferred by present respondents who claimed interest in khata no 459 only - Accordingly, when appeal preferred by present respondents abated, it only abated with reference to khata no 459 and in no case it would have any impact on title of present appellants which became established under a decree of trial court which became final on appeal of Brahmadeo having abated before notification u/s. 3, and it could not at all be dealt with by HC - Appeal partly allowed.
