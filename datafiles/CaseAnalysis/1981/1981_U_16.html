 R.K. Garg Etc. Etc. v Union Of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 372; AIR 1981 SC 2138; 1981(3) SCALE 1601; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 947

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Special Bearer Bonds (Immunities and Exemptions) ordinance, 1981 - Special Bearer Bonds (Immunities and Exemptions) Act, 1981 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Through the instant appeal appellant challenged the Constitutional validity of the ordinance and the 1981 Act on the ground that, it was violative of the equality clause contained in art. 14 of the Constitution and on the ground that the President had no power u/art. 123 of the Constitution to issue the ordinance and the ordinance was therefore ultra vires and void - Whether, the 1981 Act was violative of the Constitution as alleged by the appellant - Held, instant court had considered the provisions of the impugned Act and the object of the Act that, and to the provisions of the art. 14 of the Constitution - It was clear from the provisions of the 1981 Act that the advantages which the tax-evaders derived from the immunities provided by the Act were not available to those who had acquired the Bonds with 'white' money and the Act promises anonymity and security for tax-evaders - Instant court reached to the conclusion that the ordinance and the Act were invalid on the ground that they infringed art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal allowed.
