Khatri And Ors. Etc. v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 96; (1981) 2 SCC 493; (1981) SCC (Cr) 503; AIR 1981 SC 1068; 1981 BBCJ 124; 1981 BLJR 425; 1981 CRLJ 597; 1981 MLJ 456; 1981(1) SCALE 531; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 145; 1981 UJ 924

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 32 and 226 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 162 and 172 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32, 35, 122, 123, 124, 126, 129 and 145 - Applicability of CrPC in Writ Petition - (A) Whether CrPC,1973 is applicable to all criminal cases being investigated into? - Held, it is apparent from s. 4 of CrPC that provisions of CrPC are applicable to an offence under IPC,1860 or under any other law is being investigated, inquired into, tried or otherwise dealt with - (B) Whether production of reports submitted by police officer after investigation is barred by either ss. 162 and 172 of CrPC, 1973? - Held, production of reports submitted by police officer after investigation is not barred by either s. 162 or s. 172 of CrPC, 1973 - Where a writ petition u/art. 32 was filed by under-trials for enforcement of their fundamental right u/art. 21 on allegation that they were blinded by police officer either at time of their arrest or after their arrest, whilst in police custody production of report submitted by police officer to State Govt. and correspondence exchanged by police officer or notings on files made by them in inquiry ordered by State Govt. into alleged offences, would not be barred by s. 162 or s. 172 of CrPC - (C) Whether proceedings in writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950 can be either an 'inquiry' or 'trial' for an offence? - Further held, writ petition filed u/art. 32 to enforce fundamental right guaranteed u/art. 21 is neither an 'inquiry' nor a 'trial' for an offence nor is Supreme Court hearing writ petition a criminal Court nor are petitioners, accused or their agents as contemplated by s. 172, CrPC, 1973 so far as offences arising out of their blinding are concerned - Order accordingly.
