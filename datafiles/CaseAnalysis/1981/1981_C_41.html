Kshitish Chandra Bose v Commissioner of Ranchi

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 228; (1981) 2 SCC 103; AIR 1981 SC 707; 1981 (1) RCR(Rent) 633; 1981(1) SCALE 521; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 764; 1981 UJ 137

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration of his title and adverse possession - Suit decreed in favour of plaintiff - On appeal, High Court remanded case to Trial Court for a decision only on question of title - Plaintiff's case that he had acquired title by prescription was concerned, it was finally decided against him - Challenged - (A) Whether HC was justified in remanding case? - Held, question of title legally erroneous as HC exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC, 1908 by reversing pure finding of fact given by Trial Court - Finding of HC on question of adverse possession given without at all considering materials and evidence on basis of which courts had concurrently found that plaintiff had acquired title be adverse possession - Order of remand was an interlocutory judgment which did not terminate proceedings and so correctness thereof can be challenged in an appeal from final orders - HC had no jurisdiction after reversing concurrent findings of fact of Courts on question of adverse possession to remand case to Addl. Judicial Commr. on question of title which also was concluded by concurrent findings of fact arrived at by two courts - (B) Adverse possession - Proof - Held, law requires that possession must be open and without any attempt at concealment and it is not necessary that possession must be so effective so as to bring it to specific knowledge of owner - Such a requirement may be insisted on where an ouster of title is pleaded but that is not case here - If a person asserts a hostile title even to a tank which, as claimed by municipality, belonged to it and despite hostile assertion of title no steps were taken by owner to evict trespasser, his title by prescription would be complete after thirty years - Appeal allowed.
