Bhaiya Ramanuj Pratap Deo v Lalu Maheshanuj Pratap Deo and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 478; (1981) 4 SCC 613; AIR 1981 SC 1937; 1981(3) SCALE 1425; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Coparcenary Property, Mesne Profits

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Chota Nagpur Encumbered Estates Act, 1876, s. 12A - Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 - Deceased was the holder of the impartible estate, whose succession was governed by the rule of lineal primogeniture, where the eldest male member of the eldest line was to succeed to the estate while the junior members of the family were entitled only to maintenance grants subject to resumption on extinction of an heir in the male line of the eldest branch - Dispute arose between the parties, and a petition was filed, decided in favour of the defendant - Hence, the present appeal - Whether there was fraud and fabrication in the kharposh deed claimed by the defenadant - Held, that by the khorposh deed deceased holder of the impartible estate, had in fact given to the defendant in khorposh eight villages but the defendant did not acquire any interest in the said land on the basis of the khorposh deed as the same was against the provisions of s. 12A of the 1876 Act and the 1908 Act; that the disputed estate was an impartible estate governed by the rule of lineal primogeniture but it ceased to be so after the enforcement of the 1956 Act, and since the holder of the impartible estate, died after the Act came into force the succession to the estate would be governed by survivorship as contemplated by s. 6 of the 1956 Act - Appeal disposed of.
