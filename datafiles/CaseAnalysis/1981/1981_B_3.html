State of Karnataka v B. Raghurama Shetty Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 294; (1981) SCC (Tax) 134; (1981) 2 SCC 564; AIR 1981 SC 1206; 1981(1) SCALE 571; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 280; 1981 (47) STC 369; 1981 UJ 361

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax; VAT

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1)(a) - Karnataka Sales Tax Act 1957, s. 6(i) - Whether assessee are liable to pay purchase tax u/s.(i) on turnover consisting of price paid by them for purchasing paddy for purpose of converting it into rice for sale, in their respective rice mills? - Paddy and rice are two distinct commodities and that milling of paddy involves a manufacturing process - Held, assessees had consumed paddy in manufacture of rice which was a different commercial commodity for sale, case of assessees squarely falls u/s. 6(i) - Turnover in question in each case is held to be taxable u/s. 6(i) - Appeal allowed.
