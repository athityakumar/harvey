Ashok Kumar & Ors. v Additional District Judge, Nainital & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 261; (1981) 1 SCC 427; AIR 1981 SC 771; 1981 ALJ 74; 1981 (7) ALR 129; 1981 (1) RCR(Rent) 446; 1981 (1) RentLR 531; 1981(1) SCALE 5; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 504; 1981 UJ 76

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s.16 - Delivery of possession of property - Not granted - Entitled to - Premises were rented out to tenant/respondent No.1 on an annual rent of Rs. 14,000 and the allotment of Hotel X to respondent No.1 - Thereafter, respondent No.1 defaulted in the payment of rent resulting in a suit filed by the appellants for his eviction - That suit was filed on 9-4-72 for ejectment and for recovery of arrears amounting to Rs. 26,743, due up to 4-6-1971 - On 12-3-1973 the tenant was asked to furnish security for arrears which he failed to do and an application by the tenant for extension of time for furnishing security was also rejected by the District Judge - Landlord/appellant in anticipation of the premises falling vacant filed an application before the Rent Control and Eviction Officer u/s.16 of Act for release of the building in question - On this application, Eviction Officer directed the Inspector to submit a report on the question as to whether or not the premises had fallen vacant - Eviction Officer decided in favour of appellant - However, due to the confusion created by respondent No.3 District Judge did not give delivery of possession to appellant - Whether appellant was eligible to be granted the possession of vacant portion - Held, as a decree for ejectment was under contemplation, it was open to the appellant to have moved the District Magistrate for notifying the vacancy u/s.16(1) (a) of the Act - SC had pointed out that the premises did fall vacant subsequently and the delivery of possession was also given to the landlord in pursuance of the decree of ejectment passed by the civil court - Respondent No. 3 appeared to have made a futile attempt to make confusion worse confounded by representing that the premises were not vacant when he knew full well that the delivery of possession was given to the landlord in his presence and he had later filed a suit for setting aside the decree which was dismissed - Thus, it appeared from the record that respondent No. 3 never came in possession of the premises in question but tried to defeat or delay the decree passed by the civil court in favour of the appellant by various subterfuges and pretexts - However, as the appellate authority had accepted the appeal filed by respondent No. 3, appellants were compelled to take the matter to HC by way of a writ petition - HC, however, dismissed the writ petition mainly on the ground that before notifying the vacancy, Eviction Officer did not hear respondent No. 3 - HC did not appear to have considered the history of the case and the various proceedings leading to the eviction of respondent No.1 and to the fact that respondent No. 3 had absolutely no claim or right to the property - At no stage could respondent No.3 prove that either he was a sub-tenant or a partner of respondent No.1 - Respondent No.3's attempt to get the decree, passed in favour of the appellant, set aside failed - In these circumstances, therefore, there was no question of his being heard by the Eviction Officer after the possession was delivered to the landlord and the vacancy was notified - If at all, such a hearing would have been futile and would have ultimately led to the same result - In these circumstances, SC was of the opinion that the HC erred in law in dismissing the writ petition and upholding the judgment of the District Judge remanding the matter to the Eviction Officer - SC, therefore, allowed the appeal, set aside the judgment of the HC as also that of the District Judge (the appellate authority) and restore the order of the Eviction Officer releasing the accommodation in favour of the appellant - Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972
