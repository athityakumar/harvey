Sewakram Sobhani v R. K. Karanjiya Chief Editor, Weekly Blitz and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 14; (1981) 3 SCC 208; (1981) SCC (Cr) 698; AIR 1981 SC 1514; 1981 CRLJ 894; 1981(1) SCALE 851; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Abortion, Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 499 and 500 - Quashing prosecution of respondent - Respondent guilty u/s. 500 of IPC for publication of a defamatory news-item - Protected under Ninth Exception to s. 499 of Code - (A) Whether journalist enjoy any special privilege have greater freedom than others to make any imputations or allegations, sufficient to ruin reputation of a citizen? - Held, journalists do not enjoy any special privilege, and have no greater freedom than others to make any imputations or allegations, sufficient to ruin reputation of a citizen - Even truth of an allegation does not permit a justification under First Exception unless it is proved to be in public good -(B) Whether High Court was justifies in quashing prosecution on ground that accused (Editor) was entitled to protection u/s. 499 Exception 9 of IPC, 1860? - Though prayer was not for quashing entire proceedings that is a technical defect of which no serious notice need be taken in an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution - Questions of 'good faith' and 'public good' etc., are questions of facts to be decided after regular trial and should not have been answered at stage when even accused was not examined - Further held, that HC was correct in it's place exercising power u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 and appellant should prefer Civil Court for remedy - Appeal allowed.
