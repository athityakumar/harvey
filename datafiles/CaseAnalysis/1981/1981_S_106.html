Syed Israr Masood, Forest Contractor, Ret Ghat v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, A.D. Koshal, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 194; (1981) 4 SCC 289; (1982) 2 SCC (Cr) 377; AIR 1981 SC 2010B; 1981(3) SCALE 1591; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 894; 1982 UJ 51

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Entitlement of, Auction sale to cut trees, Suit for recovery of deposited money

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Auction sale to cut trees - Breach of contract - Suit for recovery of deposited money - Entitlement of - Appellant (plaintiff) filed a suit for recovery of money from respondent (State) by way of refund of first installment of sale price deposited by appellant with the Forest Dept. pursuant to an auction of 2 forest coupes, together with damages alleged to have sustained on account of breach of contract by respondent - Trial Court decreed suit but rejected appellants' claim for damages - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before HC, which was partly allowed holding that appellant did not proved that number of trees which were actually available for extraction in 2 coupes, according to proper markings, was less than the number of trees which was covered by assurance given to appellant at the time of auction - Further held that Govt. had no longer the right to claim from appellant the balance of sale consideration represented by second and third installments and that decree for permanent injunction granted by Trial Court did not, called for any interference - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, admittedly, auction sale was of right to cut trees which were marked and numbered in entire area covered by 2 coupes in question - Details of quantity and quality of timber available for cutting in respective coupes were announced by concerned Range officers at the time of auction and it was on the basis of said information that participants in auction were invited to bid - Trial Court and HC concurrently found that an assurance was given by Dept. at the time of auction that 2 coupes contained the specified quantity of timber of different varieties and girth - Evidence clearly show that there was a large number of trees of different varieties situated on the bank of a 'Nalla' in coupe no. 2 'B' and they had hammer marked and serially numbered for cutting and removal - At the time of auction sale, Dept. had treated those trees as being available for extraction by contractor and it was on that basis that the particulars regarding the total quantity of timber belonging to different species available for cutting in 2 coupes were announced to bidders - Appellant in his evidence, as PW2, stated that there were about 300 teak trees in area forming the bank of Nalla and that the value of those trees would amount to between Rs.10,000 and Rs.12,000 - Though 3 officers of Dept. were examined, and testimony given by appellant was not controverted by them - Trial Court was perfectly right in its view that, while condition no. 3 of sale notice, would operate to prevent the contractor from claiming any damages or compensation from Govt. on the ground that the details of quantity of forest produce were subsequently found to be incorrect, it would not preclude him from repudiating the contract on its being found that there was substantial variance between the particulars furnished at the time of auction regarding quantity and quality of timber that would be available for extraction in concerned coupes - It was clearly established by evidence in instant case that a very substantial quantity of timber standing on the bank of 'Nalla' was marked for extraction and numbered and the auction sale was held on the basis that the highest bidder would be entitled to fell and remove all those trees - But by the time coupes were allowed to be inspected by the auction- purchaser, that area was declared to be 'reserved', with the result that there was a complete prohibition against the felling of any timber therefrom - That was substantially altered the very foundation of contract and hence it was perfectly open to appellant to repudiate the contract and claim a refund of amount deposited by him as a part payment of purchase price - Impugned judgment and decree passed by HC were set aside and those of Trial Court were restored - Appeal allowed.
