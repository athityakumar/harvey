Randhir Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 94; (1981) 4 SCC 484; (1981) SCC (Cr) 856; AIR 1982 SC 55; 1982 CRLJ 195; 1981 CRLR 543; 1981(3) SCALE 1584; 1981 UJ 850

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300, 302 and 304 - A young college going boy accused of offence of murder - Act consequent to some altercation between father of accused and deceased - Only one injury inflicted by accused - Weapon was not carried by accused in advance - Death occurred nearly after six days - Held, under s. 300(3),IPC, intention to inflict that particular bodily injury which in ordinary course of nature was sufficient to cause death must be proved - In circumstances, accused guilty of offence under s. 304 Part II, IPC, 1860 - Conviction altered from s. 302 to s. 304 Part II, IPC - Appeal allowed.
