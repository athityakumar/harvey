Dev Raj Dogra And Ors. v Gyan Chand Jain And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 271; (1981) 2 SCC 675; AIR 1981 SC 981; 1981 (7) ALR 412; 1981 (29) BLJR 412; 1981 (1) RentLR 642; 1981(1) SCALE 663; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 174; 1981 UJ 641

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 rr. 95, 96, s. 151 - Auction purchaser - Claim of physical possession of leased property - Entitlement - 'D'/Mortgagor mortgaged premises in question along with various other properties with Mortgagee/Bank on 28-6-1960 by deposit of title deeds - Mortgagee/bank filed suit to recover its dues on mortgage - Trial Court decreed suit by consent of parties in favour of mortgagee for sum of Rs. 479177.49 - After decree was passed, it appeared that judgement-debtor/mortgagor leased out different portions of premises to appellants - Judgment debtor/mortgagor failed to make payment of decreetal dues in terms of the provisions contained in consent decree and premises in question resold by public auction on 28-10-1971 - Respondent no.1/applicant was highest bidder and was declared to be purchaser of premises and the sale in his favour was confirmed on 6-8-1973 - Respondent no.1/auction purchaser filed application in HC under O. 21 rr. 95 and 96 and also s.151 of CPC for physical possession of entire property - Single Judge passed an order of physical possession of said portions in favour of respondent no. 1- On appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent No. 1/applicant was entitled to recover actual physical possession of portions in occupation of appellants as lessees, the leases in respect of which were created after decree in mortgage suit by consent between parties were passed, in application made by respondent no. 1 under O.27 rr. 95 and 96 of CPC - Held, in instant case an outside auction purchaser/respondent no.1 was seeking recovery of physical possession of property purchased by him at auction from appellants who were in possession of different portions of said premises as tenants of said portions - Auction purchaser/respondent no. 1 was not the mortgagee and he was no party to the suit in which compromise decree was passed - Auction-purchaser/respondent no. 1 derived his right to obtain possession only after sale in his favour had become absolute and sale certificate was obtained by him - Mode and manner of obtaining such possession were regulated by rr. 95 and 96 of CPC - Auction purchaser/respondent no. 1 had applied for obtaining possession u/r. 95 which provided for actual possession and also u/r. 96 which provided for symbolic possession - In view of provisions contained in r. 95, respondent no. 1/auction purchaser was not entitled to recover physical possession of portions in occupation of appellants as tenants - Appellants were not judgment-debtors, they were not in occupation of property on behalf of judgment-debtor and they were also not claiming to be in occupation under a title created by judgment-debtor subsequently to any attachment of property - There was no question of any attachment in instant case - Appellants were in occupation of respective portions as tenants and they claimed to occupy the same as such - In instant proceeding, auction-purchaser who was outsider and was not a party to suit resulting in compromise decree in execution of which property was put up for sale, was not entitled to recover physical possession from appellants in view of provisions contained in O. 21 r. 95, and auction-purchaser should be held to be entitled to symbolic possession in terms of provisions contained in O.21 r. 96 in respect of portions in occupation of appellants - Appeal allowed.
