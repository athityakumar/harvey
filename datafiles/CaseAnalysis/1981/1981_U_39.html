Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited v Union of India and Two Others Cases

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 349; (1981) 1 SCC 664; AIR 1981 SC 818; [1981] 51 Comp Cas 210; 1982 (1) CompLJ 309; 1981 (42) FLR 225; 1981(1) SCALE 90; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Trade; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Majority View

Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, s. 18AA - Applicability of principles of natural justice - Government of India in exercise of its power under Clause (a) of Sub-s. (1) of S. 18AA of the IDR Act, passed an order whereby it authorised National Textile Corporation Limited (Authorised person) to take over the management of the whole of the industrial undertakings (appellants company) - Writ petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution was filed to challenge the validity of the Government Order - Question before HC was that whether a prior hearing was necessary to be given to the persons affected before the order u/s. 18AA was passed - Writ petition was partly allowed, the challenge to the validity of the impugned order failed and to that extent the petition was dismissed - Petition was allowed in so far as it seeks to protect from the impugned order the corporate entity of the company, the corporate entity of the subsidiary and its assets - Respondents were restrained from in any manner interfering with the corporate entity, the assets and property which were outside the impugned order - Instant appeals - (A) Whether, as a matter of law, it was necessary, in accordance with the rules of natural justice, to give a hearing to the owner of an undertaking before issuing a notified order, or enforcing a decision of its takeover u/s. 18AA? - Held, before taking action u/s. 18-A(1)(b), the Central Government had to form an opinion on the basis of the investigation conducted u/s. 15, in regard to the existence of the objective fact, namely : that the industrial undertaking was being managed in a manner highly detrimental to the scheduled industry concerned or to public interest; while u/s. 18-AA(1)(a) the Government had to satisfy itself that the persons incharge of the undertaking have brought about a situation likely to cause fall in production, by committing any of the three kinds of acts specified in that provision - Hence, from a plain reading of s. 18AA, it was clear that it did not expressly in unmistakable and unequivocal terms exclude the application of the audi alteram partem rule at the pre-decisional stage - It was not reasonably possible to construe S. 18AA(1) as universally excluding, either expressly or by inevitable intendment, the application of the audi alteram partem rule of natural justice at the pre-takeover stage, regardless of the facts and circumstances of the particular case - In the circumstances of the instant case, in order to ensure fairplay in action it was imperative for the Government to comply substantially with this fundamental rule of prior hearing before passing the impugned order - (B) Effect of the non-observance of fundamental principle of fairplay - Whether non-observance of the audi alteram partem rule, which in the quest of justice under the rule of law, was considered universally and most spontaneously acceptable principle, render an administrative decision having civil consequences, void or violable? - Held, in the facts and circumstances of the instant case, there was a non-compliance with such implied requirement of the audi alteram partem rule of natural justice at the pre-decisional stage - Impugned order therefore, could be struck down as invalid - However, in view of commitment made by the Solicitor General that a hearing would be afforded to the appellant Company if it approaches the Central Government for cancellation of the impugned order not quashed - Appeals disposed of.

Minority View

Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, s. 18AA - Applicability of principles of natural justice - Whether, as a matter of law, it was necessary, in accordance with the rules of natural justice, to give a hearing to the owner of an undertaking before issuing a notified order, or enforcing a decision of its takeover u/s. 18AA? - Held, where, there was a provision in the statute itself for revocation of the order by the very authority making the decision, it was unnecessary to insist upon a pre-decisional observance of natural justice - Question must be considered by regard to the terms of the statute and by an examination, on the terms of the statute, whether it was possible, practicable and desirable to observe pre-decisional natural justice and whether a post decisional review or reconsideration as provided by the statute itself was not a sufficient substitutes - S. 18AA refers to 'Power to take over industrial undertakings without investigation under certain circumstances' - S. 18AA contemplated a situation where the disaster had occurred and action was necessary to restore normalcy - Restoration of production where production had stopped in a key industry or industrial undertaking was as important and urgent, in the public interest, as prevention of a situation where production may be affected - Necessity for immediate action by the Central Government, contemplated by Parliament, was definitely indicative of the exclusion of natural justice - Principles of natural justice were not attracted to the situations contemplated by S. 18AA - Order accordingly.
