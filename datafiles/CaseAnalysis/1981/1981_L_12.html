Bhaichand Ratanshi v Laxmishanker Tribhoyan

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 244; (1981) 3 SCC 502; AIR 1981 SC 1690; 1981 (22) GLR 1063; 1982 (1) RCJ 242; 1981 (2) RCR(Rent) 594; 1981(3) SCALE 1114; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 153; 1981 UJ 588

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 13(1)(g), 13(2) - Eviction - Bonafide requirement - Validity - Appellant/plaintiff/landlord filed suit for eviction of respondent/tenant/defendant from suit premises on ground that he reasonably and bona fide required suit premises for starting his business - Trial Court as well as District Judge on appeal, decreed suit and upheld plaintiff's claim u/s. 13(1)(g) of the Act - On revision before HC, HC dismissed revision and reversed findings of Courts below - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and decree of HC - Held, merely because defendant who was aged and infirm and stranger as his licensee and under an arrangement was paying a fixed amount to defendant by way of maintenance did not imply that passing of a decree u/s. 13(1)(g) of the Act would cause greater hardship to defendant than to plaintiff - Further, HC failed to appreciate that perhaps old age and infirmity might have been relevant considerations in judging issue of greater hardship u/s. 13(2) of the Act if defendant were himself to carry on business from suit premises and not where, he had admittedly parted with possession in favour of a stranger -Thus, it was clearly in error in spelling out a new case for defendant of so-called arrangement between himself and a stranger, for which there was no foundation in pleadings and which could not in law be pleaded in answer to plaintiff's claim u/s. 13(1)(g) of the Act - That apart, during pendency of appeal defendant died, question of greater hardship u/s. 13(2) of the Act did not arise - Impugned judgment and decree of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
