Union of India and Others v M. B. Patnaik and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 277; (1981) 2 SCC 159; (1981) SCC (L&S) 296; AIR 1981 SC 858; 1982 (42) FLR 252; 1981 LabIC 313; 1981 (1) LLJ 453; 1981 (2) LLN 1; 1981(1) SCALE 297; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 817; 1981 (1) SLJ 400; 1981 (1) SLR 377; 1981 UJ 204

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Disciplinary proceedings - Validity of - Whether disciplinary proceeding relating to first charge, which led to removal of all seven persons from service is valid? - It is not at all necessary that enquiry which had been held in part by more than one enquiry officers should be continued by same enquiry officers until end - Post which members of Inquiry Committee held originally might have been ceased to exist at a later stage, or one or more of members of Inquiry Committee may no longer be available either on account of retirement or due to any other cause - For that reason, it could not be held that enquiry could not be continued at all - There could be no valid objection to supplementary enquiry being continued by very two individuals, even after they had ceased to hold their respective offices which they held at time of original enquiry - Held, no good foundation has been laid for plea of malafides, bias or prejudice by enquiry officers - Appeals dismissed.
