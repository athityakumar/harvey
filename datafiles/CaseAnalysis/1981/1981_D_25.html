Wasi Uddin Ahmed v District Magistrate, Aligarh, U.P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 103; (1981) 4 SCC 521; (1982) SCC (Cr) 4; AIR 1981 SC 2166; 1981 CRLJ 1825; 1981 CRLR 625; 1981(3) SCALE 1629

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Aligarh Muslim University Act, 1920

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 22(5) and 32 - National Security Act, 1980 - S. 8 - Detenu was detained to prevent him from acting in a manner prejudicial to public order - Detention challenged on ground that proper opportunity to make representation was denied to the detenue - Whether the order of detention was vitiated on ground that detenue was not able to make representation against detention? - Held, no - Detenue admitted before Advisory Board that detention order and necessary documents served upon him - No prejudice caused to detenue - Detention was to prevent detenu from acting in a manner detrimental to public peace - Order of detention justified - Petition dismissed.
