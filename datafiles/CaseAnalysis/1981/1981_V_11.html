Vidya Charan Shukla v Purshottam Lal Kaushik

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 199; (1981) 2 SCC 84; AIR 1981 SC 547; 1981(1) SCALE 159; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 637

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 8(2), 36(2)(a), 67A, 100(1)(d)(i), 100(1)(a), 100(1)(d) and 100(1)(i) - Constitution of India, art. 102(1)(e) - Election - (A) Disqualification - Enquiry u/cls. (a) and (d) (i), of s. 100(1) of the Act - Focal point of time - (B) Validity of acceptance of nomination - Returned candidate disqualified on date of scrutiny of nomination papers due to sentence - Acquittal, during pendency of election petition before pronouncement of judgment - Held, it is the date of declaration of election result in the former while it is date of scrutiny of nomination papers in the latter - Disqualification is wiped out from date of scrutiny - Acceptance of his nomination, not open to challenge

Appeal allowed.
