Kake Singh Alias Surendra Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 16; (1981) Supp SCC 25; (1981) SCC (Cr) 645; AIR 1982 SC 1021; 1982 CRLJ 986; 1982 CRLR 8

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 Part II, 326 - Culpable homicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was found guilty of offence u/s. 302 of IPC by Trial Court and was sentenced to death - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment convicted appellant u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and was sentenced to undergo RI for 10 years - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, solitary evidence against the appellant consists of the dying declaration alleged to have been made by the deceased before Head Constable (PW8). Court find that the dying declaration presents suspicious features. The HC had given the certificate mainly on the ground that having regard to the statement of the deceased, the only offence that could have been made out would be one u/s. 326 of IPC and not that under Section 304, Part II, Indian Penal Code, 1860 if the charge u/s. 302 failed. It is not necessary for Court to go into this question because in view of the fact that the dying declaration cannot be accepted, the appellant is entitled to an acquittal. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside and appellant is acquitted of the charges framed against him. Appeal allowed.
