Tata Engineering & Locomotive Co. Ltd v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 388; (1981) 4 SCC 627; (1982) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1981 SC 2163; 1981 (43) FLR 354; 1982 LabIC 1; 1981 (2) LLJ 429; 1982 (1) LLN 28; 1981(3) SCALE 1587; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 929; 1982 (1) SLJ 64; 1981 UJ 919

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(p) and 18(1) - Appeal against award deciding reference u/s/10 (1)(d) requiring adjudication of demands raised by the workmen - Industrial Dispute - Settlement among Company and Unions by vast majority but minority refused to - (A) Onus of Proof, forged or fictitious signature not proved by challenging Union - (B) Validity of settlement challenged - Held, assertion by each signatory to declaration that he was member of Union entering settlement has to be taken at its face value and presumed to be correct - Settlement does not cease to be just and fair - Appeal allowed.
