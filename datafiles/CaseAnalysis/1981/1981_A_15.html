Francis Coralie Mullin v Administrator, Union Territory of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 117; (1981) SCC (Cr) 212; (1981) 1 SCC 608; AIR 1981 SC 746; [1982] 52 Comp Cas 554; 1981 CRLJ 306; 1981 CrLR(SC) 437; 1981 MLJ 331; 1981(1) SCALE 9; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 516

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Legal Adviser

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Rights of Prisoners - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 22 and 32 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange & Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act - S. 3 - Petitioner, a British national arrested u/s. 3 of COFEPOSA - Petitioner was allowed to meet her advocate and family members while she was imprisoned - Petitioner challenged the restrictions placed upon her as arbitrary and unreasonable and violative of arts. 14 and 21 of the Constitution - Whether the restrictions placed upon the detenue were violative of provisions of fundamental rights under arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution? - Detenue entitled to interviews and meetings with family and her advocate with permission of Prison Authorities - Petition Allowed.
