State Of Tamil Nadu v Hind Stone Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 223; (1981) 2 SCC 205; AIR 1981 SC 711; 1981(1) SCALE 237; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 742; 1981 UJ 408

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral! Concession rules 1959

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 298 and 301 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation And Development) Act, 1957, ss. 13(2)(f), 15(3), 17 and 18 - Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules 1959, rr. 8A, 8B and 8C - Whether r. 8C is vires and did it apply to grant of renewals of lease at all? - Held, yes - In case of lands in which right to minerals belongs to private owners and those owners seek permission to quarry black granite, applications will have to be dealt with under relevant rules in s. III of Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules - R. 8C does not impose a general ban on quarrying black granite but only imposes a bar on grant of leases of quarrying black granite - Appeals and SLPs which arise out of applications for grant of permission to quarry black granite in Patta lands belonging to applicants themselves, have to be dismissed - R. 8C has been made by state Govt. pursuant to power conferred upon it by s. 15 of Act - A statutory rule is to be treated as part of statute and as effective - Rules made under statute must be treated for all purposes of construction or obligation exactly as if they were in Act and are to be of same effect as if contained in Act and are to be judicially noticed for all purposes of construction or obligation - Appeals partly allowed.
