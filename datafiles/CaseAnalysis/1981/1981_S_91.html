Kirit Kumar Chaman Lal Kundaliya v State Of Gujarat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 585; (1981) 2 SCC 436; (1981) SCC (Cr) 471; AIR 1981 SC 1621; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 107; 1981 CRLJ 1267; 1981 CrLR(SC) 263; 1981 (22) GLR 1067; 1981(1) SCALE 223; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 718

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 32 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Preventive detention - Validity of - Whether a detention in absence of supply of relevant documents related to charges can be considered as valid? - Held, question as to whether or not documents demanded by detenu were relevant was decided not by Minister who was detaining authority but by Secretary - Secondly, detenu made a representation which was rejected not by detaining authority but by Secy. - Once documents are referred to in grounds of detention it becomes bounden duty of detaining authority to supply same to detenu as part of grounds or pari passu grounds of detention - If there is undue delay in furnishing statement and documents referred to in grounds of detention right to make effective representation is denied detention cannot be said to be according to procedure prescribed by law - Petition allowed.
