Barendra Prasad Ray & Ors. v Income Tax Officer 'A' Ward Foreign Section and

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.S. Pathak, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 309; (1981) SCC (Tax) 149; (1981) 2 SCC 693; AIR 1981 SC 1047; 1981 (22) CTR 157; [1981] 129 ITR 295; 1981(1) SCALE 949; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 387; 1981 TAXLR 657; [1981] 6 TAXMAN 19; 1981 UJ 873

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 9(1), 14, 2(13) and 163(1) - Income Tax liability on fees paid to English Counsel What is 'Business connection' within meaning of u/s. 9 (1) - English solicitors and Indian solicitors - For German Firm - Non-resident English Counsel engaged by English solicitors - Arguing in India along with Counsel engaged by Indian solicitors for - Correspondence among - Inference of 'Business connection' - 'Business' includes 'profession' also - Indian solicitors are liable for tax on fees paid to English Counsel - Appeal Dismissed.
