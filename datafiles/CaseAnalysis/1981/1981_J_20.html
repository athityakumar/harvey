Jethabhai Khatau & Co v Luxmi Narayan Cotton Mills Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 314; (1981) 3 SCC 61; AIR 1981 SC 1201; 1981(1) SCALE 800; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 449; 1981 UJ 447

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Recovery of amount - Decree of attachment - Appointment of receivers - Appellant filed suit against company on original side of HC to recover certain amount due against respondent - Administrator on behalf of respondent no.1 company entered into compromise with appellant and after obtaining leave of Court to settle matter, invited consent decree by which company was held liable and directed to pay amount - Appellant obtained leave to execute decree by attachment of funds lying in hands of respondent no.3 receiver - Single Judge directed receiver to pay amount to decree-holder - Interim order was confirmed by DB - Hence, instant Petition.

Held, it is incontrovertible that the judgment-debtor has in its fixed deposit with Bank a certain sum. Respondent no.3 was once a receiver. Respondents say that he has been removed and respondent nos.5 and 6 claim to be appointed as joint receivers. The date of appointment is not made clear but the interim order leaves no room for doubt that till that date respondent no.3 was not discharged as receiver. There seems to be some apparent collusion between the company on one hand and the joint receivers in not complying with the Court's order, even though action for contempt was avoided by giving an unconditional undertaking to carry out that order. Thus, proper thing to do would be to set aside the interim stay order as also the order confirming the interim order but in order to ensure the resultant justice it proper is to give certain directions, while restoring status quo ante in the event the appeals filed by respondent nos.1 and 2 are allowed or any specific positive direction is given by the court in this behalf. Hence, order made by the DB is liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.
