Ahura Chemical Products Pvt. Ltd. v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 220; (1981) 4 SCC 277; AIR 1981 SC 1782; 1982 ECR 625; 1981 (8) E.L.T. 613; 1981(3) SCALE 1365; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 621; 1981 TAXLR 2835; 1981 UJ 729

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 16 - Purchase from open market - Rejection of claim of exemption of excise duty - Legality - It was case of appellant that he was engaged in manufacture of various process chemicals required for Textile industry - Appellant purchased total quantity of 1,64,500 kgs. of organic surface active agents from 'I'/company - Said organic surface active agents sold to appellant was not subjected to levy of excise duty inasmuch as said supplier/'I' was eligible for exemption from payment of excise duty on account of the fact that goods were manufactured by it without aid of power - Emulsifiers/wetting out agents, etc. intended for use in any industrial process were exempted from levy of duty under Entry 15AA of First Schedule to the Act, subject to certain conditions, by Notification dt.20-1-1968 issued by Govt. of India - Show cause notice dt.6-8-1974 was issued to appellant on ground that appellant escaped duty in respect of emulsifiers/ wetting out agents manufactured by appellant - Appellant contended that he was entitled to benefit of exemption from levy of duty in respect of manufactured products under Notification dt.20-1-1968 inasmuch as surface active agents used in manufacture of emulsifiers/wetting out agents were purchased by appellant from open market after 20-1-1968 - Assistant Collector rejected said contention of appellant as conditions prescribed in said Notification were not fulfilled - On appeal, Appellate Collector of Central Excise uphold order of Assistant Collector and held that purchase of raw-material effected by appellant from another company could not be regarded as 'purchased from open market', hence, appellant was ineligible to claim benefit of exemption - On revision, Govt. of India rejected said revision and held that 'purchases made from particular manufacturer, production from whose factory was exempt from payment of duty, being manufactured without aid of power, could not be treated at par with purchases made from open market' - Hence instant appeal - Whether transactions of purchase effected by appellant from another company could be regarded as purchases 'from the open market' - Held, in determining eligibility of a person for benefit of exemption conferred by Notification on basis of fulfillment of second condition, it was wholly irrelevant to enquire whether duty of excise was already paid in respect of surface active agents purchased and utilised as raw material for manufacture of emulsifiers/wetting out agents - Further, it was open to every person desirous of purchasing surface active agents to place orders with manufacturing Company, and obtain supply on payment of price at prevailing rate - Sales by said Company were not to a limited class only - Hence, purchases of surface active agents effected by appellant from another company had to be treated as purchases made 'from the open market' - Denial to appellant of the benefit of exemption provided for by Notification was, therefore, clearly illegal - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
