Hindustan Brown Boveri Ltd. Etc. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 307; (1981) 2 SCC 743; AIR 1981 SC 1055; 1981(1) SCALE 607; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 435; 1981 (47) STC 376; 1981 TAXLR 2956; 1981 UJ 547

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Commission Agent, Processing, Gujarat Sales Tax Rules 1970

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969, s. 2(33) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Charges on purchase tax - Legality - Appellant/Hindustan Brown Boveri Ltd., company manufactured some goods which were sold to certain electrical undertaking in State of Gujarat against 'C' forms in order to claim exemption from payment of tax on said sales under the Act - On coming to know of said sales, Sales Tax officer/assessing authority/respondent passed order under Act levied purchase tax u/s. 16 of 1969 Act on raw materials purchased be appellant on ground that undertaking given in Form No. 19 had been violated - Appellant filed appeals before Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax and Gujarat Sales Tax Tribunal against said levy were unsuccessful - Tribunal referred matter to HC - HC passed order in favor of respondent/revenue - Hence instant appeals - Whether sales tax should be levied on goods which were not included in Schedule I - Held, there was no substance in grounds urged by appellants for reason that present case was governed by definition of expression 'taxable goods' in s. 2(33) of the Act - It was interesting to note that 1959, Act which was in force in State of Gujarat before Act came into force and which was repealed by s. 88 of the 1969, Act contained definition of expression 'taxable goods' in s. 2(33) of 1969 Act - Definition of 'taxable goods' in that Act did not refer to sales exempted under s. 41 of the Act - One had to wait till disposal of goods by dealer to find out whether goods were taxable goods or not in view of definition of said expression which takes away goods sold under circumstances attracting s. 49 of the Act from scope of meaning of that expression - Possibility of any liability arising u/s. 50 of the Act on breach of any condition imposed by s. 49 or notification issued thereunder would absolve appellants of their liability to pay tax u/s. 16 of 1969 Act - Any such levy made u/s. 50 of the Act had not been shown to have any effect in law on liability of appellants u/s. 16 of 1969 Act - Scheme of the Act appeared to be that sales tax should be levied on goods which were not included in Schedule I at least once inside State in series of sales and purchases even though they might have been converted into manufactured goods and that was why Form No. 19 required purchaser to state that goods would be used by him as raw or processing materials or as consumable stores in manufacture of taxable goods for sale by him inside State and s. 16 of the 1969 Act provides that where any dealer had purchased any taxable goods under certificate given by him u/s. 12 or 13 of the Act and contrary to such certificate goods were used for another purpose or were not resold or dispatched in manner and within period certified or on re-sales in course of inter-State trade or commerce, of goods so purchased no tax under 1956 Act was actually payable by him on account of any deductions admissible under any of provisions of that Act, then such dealer should be liable to pay tax on purchase price of goods purchased under such certificate - Deliberate alteration of definition of 'taxable goods' in the Act also was attributable to said intention of State legislature - Appeals dismissed.
