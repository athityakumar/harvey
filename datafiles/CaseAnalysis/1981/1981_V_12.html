Vijay Kumar Moti Lal v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 554; (1981) 2 SCC 719; AIR 1981 SC 1632

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Acquired land, Potentiality, Determination of compensation

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Hyderabad Land Acquisition Act - Acquired land - Potentiality - Determination of compensation - Land in dispute was developing town and in neighborhood of plot in dispute grain market was built, several other municipal plots was also sold out and constructions like shops came up - HC found that land in question had great building potentiality.

Held, HC pointed out that the evidence of 'X' had not been challenged on behalf of the State. Therefore, this was a very reasonable and adequate basis for determining the compensation of the land in dispute. There is absolutely no warrant for the HC for upholding the rate of Rs. 2 per square yard which was grossly inadequate having regard to the locality; the character of the land; the building potentiality, the coming up of the various constructions and such other factors and more particularly when the HC itself found that the evidence of the President of the Municipality remained unchallenged and was not contested by the State. The judgment of the HC was clearly wrong and did not follow the correct principles in determining the proper compensation to which the appellant was entitled for the land in dispute. The compensation enhanced from Rs. 2 per square yard to Rs. 10 per square yard. Appeal allowed.
