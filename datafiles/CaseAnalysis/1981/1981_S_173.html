Southern Pharmaceuticals and Chemicals Trichur and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 396; (1981) SCC (Tax) 320; (1981) 4 SCC 391; AIR 1981 SC 1863; 1981(3) SCALE 1376; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 519; 1981 TAXLR 2838

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Enactment, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, Medicinal And Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Rules, 1956

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Excise - Health & Drug - Indirect Tax - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Rules, 1956 - - Abkari Amendment) Act, 1967, - s. 12A,B, 14,68-,Art. 19(1) (g)- Whether the State Legislature has no power to enact the law relating to medicinal and toilet preparations as the topic of legislation is within the exclusive domain of Parliament .?- It is a matter of common knowledge that preparations are always likely to be misused as a substitute for alcoholic beverages and, therefore, the restriction imposed is a reasonable restriction - There is demand for the appellants's medicinal preparations not only in the State, but throughout the country and to limit the quantity to be manufactured, taking into account the requirements of the State alone, is but an abridgment on the freedom of inter-State trade and commerce - s. 12A has such effecti this case - The Commissioner shall have regard to the total requirements for consumption and use in th e State, while fixing the quantity of the medicinal preparations to be manufactured - Challenge with regard to Art. 301 does not arise as, the Bill was reserved for the assent of the President, and is, therefore, protected by Art. 304(b) of the Constitution - It is not disputed that the provisions are regulatory in nature and they impose reasonable restrictions on the freedom of trade. - Held, the Appeal and the Special Leave Petition must fail and is dismissed.
