Lalappa Lingappa and Others v Laxmi Vishnu Textile Mills Limited, Sholapur

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 275; (1981) 2 SCC 238; (1981) SCC (L&S) 316; AIR 1981 SC 852; 1981 (42) FLR 258; 1981 LabIC 307; 1981 (1) LLJ 308; 1981 (1) LLN 347; 1981(1) SCALE 268; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 796; 1981 (1) SLJ 420; 1981 UJ 225

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 2(c) - (A) Whether permanent employees are entitled to payment of gratuity u/s. 4(1) of Act for years in which they remained absent without leave for a number of days in a year and had actually worked for less than 240 days, due to absence without leave? - If it was contemplated by Explanation I that it was sufficient that there should be a subsisting contract of employment, then it was not necessary for legislature to use words 'actually employed' - It is not permissible to attribute redundancy to legislature to defeat purpose of enacting Explanation - Expression 'actually employed' in Explanation I to s. 2 (c) of Act must, in context in which it appear s, mean 'actually worked' - Held, permanent employees were not entitled to payment of gratuity u/sub-s. (1) of s. 4 of Act for years in which they remained absent without leave and had 'actually' worked for less than 240 days in a year - (B) Whether badli employees are entitled to such gratuity on becoming permanent employees, for badli period in respect of years in which there was no work allotted to them due to their failure to report to duty? - Held, Management has got a separate register for badli employees and that those who need work and when they call at gate of mills for work, such number of them are employed by mills to fill up vacancies of permanent operatives or probationers who are absent on a particular day either on account of illness or for any other cause - Report of Badli Labour Enquiry Committee, Cotton Textile Industry, 1967, no doubt shows that badli employees are an integral part of textile industry and that they enjoy most of benefits of permanent employees, but there may not be any continuity of service as observed by this Court in Delhi Cloth Mills' case - Badli employees are nothing but substitutes, they are like 'spare men' who are not 'employed' while waiting for a job - Further held, badli employees are not covered by substantive part of definition of 'continuous service' in s. 2(c), but came within Explanation I and, therefore, are not entitled to payment of gratuity for badli period, i.e. in respect of years in which there was no work allotted to them due to their failure to report to duty - Appeal dismissed.
