Commissioner Of Income Tax, New Delhi v Federation Of Indian Chambers Of Commerce & Industries, Newd

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 295; (1981) 3 SCC 156; AIR 1981 SC 1408; 1982 (2) CompLJ 451; 1981 (22) CTR 124; [1981] 130 ITR 186; 1981(1) SCALE 637; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 489; 1981 TAXLR 1062; [1981] 6 TAXMAN 7; 1981 UJ 540

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Trusts and Associations - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.2(15) and 11(1)(a) - Assessee holding of Trade Fair and Conference for pourpose of trust - Interpretation of expression 'not involving the carrying on of any activity for profit' u/s. 2 (15) of the Act - Held, it is wrong to think that all springs of charity in India will dry up if true effect is given to s. 2 (15) - But following the majority view, activities held to be charitable - Order accordingly.
