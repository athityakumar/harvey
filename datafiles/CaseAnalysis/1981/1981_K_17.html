Mangal Sen v Kanchhid Mal

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 255; (1981) 4 SCC 117; AIR 1981 SC 1726; 1981 ALJ 1028; 1981 (7) ALR 573; 1981 (2) RentLR 475; 1981(3) SCALE 1242; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 331; 1981 UJ 743

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - U. P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, 2(2)(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.115 - Eviction - On non payment of rent - maintainability - Respondent was the owner of a shop building town which he had let out to the appellant on a month to month tenancy basis - Suit for ejectment was filed by the respondent in the Court of Small Causes (Civil Judge) praying for eviction of the appellant from the shop u/s. 2(2) (a) Act on the ground that the tenant was in arrears of rent for not less than four months commencing from 9-4-1972 and had failed to pay the same to the landlord within one month from the date of service upon him of a notice of demand (October 19, 1972) - It was alleged in the plaint that the agreed rent of the shop was Rs. 100/per month and that the tenant had kept the rent in arrears from 9-4-1972 onwards despite notice having been served on him on 1910-1972 demanding payment of arrears of rent and determining the tenancy - Appellant pleaded in defence that the rent was only Rs. 90/per month, that he had not committed any default in payment of the same and hence the suit for ejectment was not maintainable - Trial court denied the plaintiff the relief of ejectment and decreed the suit only for recovery of arrears of rent - However, District Judge allowed the Revision Petition and granted the plaintiff a decree for ejectment u/s. 20 (2) (a) of the Act - Thereafter, appellant took up the matter in further revision before HC u/s. 115 CPC - HC confirmed the findings of the District Judge and dismissed the Revision Petition - Hence instant petition - Whether allowing of eviction petition of respondent was sustainable - Held, SC was satisfied that the District Court was perfectly right in its view that there had not been any conduct on the part of the plaintiff which would constitute a waiver by him of the demand for surrender of possession made as per the notice which was served on the tenant - As rightly observed by the District Court, appellant had not put forward any plea of waiver in the written statement filed by him before Trial Court and the absence of any specific pleading in that behalf, Trial Court was not really called upon to go into the question of waiver - Further, it being the specific case put forward by appellant himself that no amount whatever had been paid by the appellant to the sales-tax authorities on behalf of the plaintiff and that the respondent was not agreeable to make any endorsement on the Rent Deed adjusting the proposed payment of sales-tax against the arrears of rent, SC failed to see how it could be said that there had been any waiver by respondent of the demand for surrender of possession already made by him as per the notice dt. 9-10-1972 - Finding rendered by Trial Court that the effect of the notice had been effaced by the subsequent conduct on the part of the landlord which amounted to a waiver was manifestly illegal and perverse and it was rightly set aside by the District Judge - Appeal dismissed.
