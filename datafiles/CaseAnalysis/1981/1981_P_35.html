Smt. Sooraj Devi v Pyare Lal and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 135; (1981) SCC (Cr) 188; (1981) 1 SCC 500; AIR 1981 SC 736; 1981 CRLJ 296; 1981 CrLR(SC) 174; 1981 MLJ 392; 1981(1) SCALE 46; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 485; 1981 UJ 262

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 362 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 448 - House trespass - Appeal by special leave against an order of HC dismissing an application for 'clarification' of an earlier order made in criminal proceeding - Whether HC was right in refusing to entertain review application on the ground that it had no power to review its order? - Inherent power of Court cannot be exercised for doing that which is specifically prohibited by CrPC - Prohibition in s. 362 against Court altering or reviewing its judgment is subject to 'what is otherwise provided by CrPC or by any other law for the time being in force' - Those words, however, refer to those provisions only where Court has been expressly authorised by CrPC or other law to alter or review its judgment - Held, HC rightly dismissed review application as it has no power to alter judgment except clerical or arithmetical errors - S. 482, CrPC cannot be invoked in such cases which have been specifically prohibited by CrPC - Appeal dismissed.
