Eskayef Limited v Collector Of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 445; (1990) 4 SCC 680; 1990 (31) ECR 1; 1990 (49) E.L.T. 649; JT 1990 (4) SC 85; 1990(2) SCALE 497; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 442; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Medicinal And Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 - Whether products, Bifuran Supplement, Neftin50 and Neftin-200, manufactured by appellant, are chargeable to excise duty as 'patent or proprietary medicines' under Item 14-E of First Schedule to Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 or said products are exempted from excise duty under Notification No 6/84 D/- 15-02-1984, as animal feed supplement? - Once it is found that Neftin-200 are medicines for use for treatment and prevention of aliments in poultry they have to be regarded as patent and proprietary medicines chargeable to excise duty under Item 14-E - Held, appellant cannot obtain an exemption in disregard of law by invoking right to equality before law and equal protection of laws guaranteed u/art. 14 of Constitution - No substance in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
