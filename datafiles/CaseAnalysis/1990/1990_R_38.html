Tara Chand and Another v Ram Prasad

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 212; (1990) 3 SCC 526; JT 1990 (3) SC 17; 1990 (1) RCJ 673; 1990 (2) RCR(Rent) 158; 1990 (2) RentLR 72; 1990 SCALE 190; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 7; 1990 (2) UJ 157

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Family Business, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, ss. 13(1), 13(1)(a) to (k) - Eviction - Bonafide requirement - Demised premises - Validity - Respondent/landlord filed suit for eviction of appellants/tenant from suit premises on ground that on death of tenant, appellants had no right to continue in occupation of demised premises - Trial Court dismissed suit on ground that appellants paid rent after determination of lease and respondent accepted same, so appellants was a tenant holding over - On appeal, Appellate Court confirmed judgment and decree of Trial Court - On further appeal, HC allowed appeal and reversed decrees of Courts below - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and decree of HC - Held, appellants enjoyed status as a statutory tenant of premises even after determination of tenancy - However, jural relationship of landlord and tenant between respondent and appellants under the Act was not snapped off - Thus, heritable property or interest in lease hold right in tenancy continued to subsist in tenant - Appellants by virtue of intestate succession being Class I heirs, succeeded to heritable interest in lease hold right of a demised premises thereby, stepped into shoes of tenant - Therefore, appellants continued to remain in possession as on date of suit as statutory tenants and were entitled to protection of their continuance as a statutory tenant under the Act - Hence, appellants succeeded to heritable interest in lease-hold right in demised premises governed by the Act subject to rights and limitations prescribed under the Act and also subject to the rights in favour of respondent created u/s. 13(1)(a) to (k) and other provisions of the Act - Thus, appellants were entitled to remain in possession of demised shop in question till appellants were duly evicted in accordance with provisions of the Act - Impugned decree and judgment of HC was reversed and that of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
