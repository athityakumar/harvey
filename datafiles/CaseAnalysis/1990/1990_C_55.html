Akbar Badrudin Jiwani v Collector Of Customs, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 704; (1990) 2 SCC 203; (1990) SCC (Cr) 291; AIR 1990 SC 1579; 1990 CRLR 472; 1990 CrLR(SC) 472; 1990 (27) ECC 69; 1990 (28) ECR 145; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 161; JT 1990 (1) SC 256; 1990(1) SCALE 176; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 369; 1990 (1) UJ 514

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Collector Of Customs, Samples, Sea Customs Act 1962, Tariff Act 1934

Summary: Customs Act - Indirect Tax - Imports (Control) Order, 1955, sch. 1, App 1-B, item No. 25.15 - Import and Export Policy for 1988-1991, Part B, Appendix 2, Entry 62 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 112 and 125 - (A) Marble - Construction of term - Light of imports (Control) Order and Import and Export Policy - (B) Penalty/fine - Imposition - Requisite mens rea has to be established - Assessee importing goods under bona fide belief that they are importable under open General License - Tribunal recording finding as to bona fides of assessee - Held, (A) Terms are used in scientific or technical sense, commercial nomenclature or understanding in trade cannot be applied in construing it - (B) Imposition of heavy fine and penalty in such circumstances is not warranted - Appeal allowed.
