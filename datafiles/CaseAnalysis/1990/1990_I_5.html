Inderjit Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 1991 SC 1674; 1991 CRLJ 2191

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 34 - Charge of Murder - Circumstantial evidence relied by prosecution - (1) Appellants took away deceased from his house, who thereafter was never seen alive (2) Dead body of deceased found on bank of canal identified as that of deceased (3) Denial of appellants to have not taken away deceased for bird-shooting (4) Suggestion as to why not appellants could be falsely implicated in case - Held, circumstances not sufficient to establish guilt of accused - Prosecution must establish all circumstances by independent evidence which must form a complete chain in proof of guilt beyond all reasonable doubts and consistent only with guilt of accused - Sole circumstance that deceased was last seen in company of accused is not sufficient to convict accused - Other circumstances not incriminating - Convictions and sentences set aside - Appeal allowed.
