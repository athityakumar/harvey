Jute Corporation of India Officers' Association and Others v Jute Corporation of India Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 213; (1990) 3 SCC 436; (1991) SCC (L&S) 58; 1990 (61) FLR 21; JT 1990 (2) SC 255; 1990 (1) LLN 1023; 1990(1) SCALE 1; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 1006; 1991 (3) SLR 6; 1990 (2) UJ 139

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Implementation of the recommendations of the High Power Committee - The court directed the Central Govt. to constitute a High Power Pay Committee to go into various aspects relating to pay scales and other incidental matters including interim relief to the said employees, viz. the employees governed by the Central Govt. pattern of pay scales and dearness allowance. - The employees insisted that the said allowance should be given from 01-01-1986 whereas the government contended that it can properly be implemented only w.e.f. 01-01- 1989, since the Report was of 02-11-1988 - Petitions, transferred cases and all interlocutory applications, civil miscellaneous petitions and contempt petition are disposed of accordingly
