Mohinder Sain Garg and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 567; (1991) SCC (L&S) 555; (1991) 1 SCC 662; 1991 (16) ATC 495; 1991 (1) CLR 254; JT 1990 (4) SC 704; 1991 (2) LLN 31; 1990(2) SCALE 1014; 1991 (1) SLR 546; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 108; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 710

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Ex-servicemen, Effectiveness, Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, Rajasthan State And Subordinate Services (Direct Recruitment By Combined)

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Excise and Taxation Inspectors - Selection of candidates - Viva voce test - Unconstitutional - Respondents published advertisement regarding post of Excise and Taxation Inspectors - 54 posts were called for and 1200 candidates were interviewed - Appellants alleged that marks for viva voce test were very high - Appellants alleged mala fide intentions of selection committee by calling 1200 candidates for 54 posts - Whether Respondents were entitled to relief of being selected and appointed as Excise and Taxation Inspectors - Held, many candidates in General Category had secured lesser marks than Appellants No.1,3,4 in written examinations held - It was held prudent and safe to follow percentage adopted by UPSC in case of selection of IAS - Appellants No. 1,3,4 were stated to have chances of being selected had viva voce test marks been reduced to 15 percent - Appellants no. 2,5,6 had no chances of being selected even if percentage of viva voce marks were reduced to 15 percent - Appeals filed by Appellants No. 1,3,4 were allowed and Appeals filed by Appellants no. 2,5,6 were dismissed - Respondents were directed to take appropriate steps to appoint Appellants No.1,3,4 for suitable posts - Appeals partly allowed.
