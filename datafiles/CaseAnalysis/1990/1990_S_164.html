Baldev Raj v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 14; (1991) Supp1 SCC 14; (1991) SCC (Cr) 659; AIR 1991 SC 37; 1990 CRLJ 2643; JT 1990 (4) SC 524; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Appellant murdered his wife - Appellant gave extra-judicial confession in presence of witnesses, produced incriminating articles - Held, extra judicial confession if made voluntarily can be accepted - Circumstances proved are conclusive of guilt of appellant and incapable of being explained on any other reasonable hypothesis - Appeal dismissed.
