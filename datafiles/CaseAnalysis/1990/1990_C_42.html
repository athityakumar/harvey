Star Enterprises Etc. Etc. v City And Industrial Development Corporationof Maharashtra Lt

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 822; (1990) 3 SCC 280; 1990 (2) CCC 245; [1991] 72 Comp Cas 1; 1990 (2) CompLJ 138; JT 1990 (2) SC 401; 1990 (2) KLT 37; 1990 (2) PLR 264; 1990(1) SCALE 44; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 826; 1990 (1) UJ 728

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, s. 617 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 12, 14 and 226 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 113(3)(A), 159, regn. 4 - (A) Whether conduct of respondent, is arbitrary, unconstitutional and contrary to Rule of Law? - Activity in question is commercial and and there is nothing wrong in respondent who is engaged in trading activity to act, as it considers appropriate for purpose of protecting its business interest - Held, respondents acted bona fide and in refusal to accept highest offer of appellant in regard to specific plots has been actuated by consideration of looking for better offers for specific plot in economic interests of respondent - (1) Whether when highest offer in response to an invitation is rejected, public authority is required to provide reasons for such action? - Further held, when highest offers of type in question are rejected, reasons sufficient to indicate stand of appropriate authority should be made available and ordinarily same should be communicated to concerned parties unless there by any specific justification not to do so - Appeal dismissed.
