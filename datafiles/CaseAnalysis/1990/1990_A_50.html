Wander Limited and Another v Antox India P. Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 820; (1990) Supp SCC 727; 1990 (2) ARBLR 399; 1990 (3) CCC 240; 1990 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1991 (11) PTC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Drugs and Cosmetics Rules

Summary: Intellectual Property - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 48 and 49 - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, r. 69-A - An injunction restraining appellants from passing off their medicinal product 'Cal-De-Ce' as that of respondent-plaintiff - Respondent claimed to have acquired a right in that trademark by continuous user - Temporary injunction was refused by Learned Single Judge - But in appeals, preferred against refusal, Division Bench granted temporary injunction in appeal - (A) Limitations on powers of Appellate Court to substitute its own discretion in an appeal preferred against a discretionary order of Single Judge - Held, an appeal against exercise of discretion is said to be an appeal on principle - Appellate Court will not reassess material and seek to reach a conclusion different from one reached by court below, if reached by applying discretion reasonably and in a judicial manner reasonably possible on material - (B) Whether passing-off action is based on asserted right to a trademark said to have been acquired by continuous user? - Held, not registered user u/ss. 48 and 49 of Trade and Merchandise Marks Act nor was it a manufacture carried out in pursuance of a loan - In a passing-off action this would be relevant only in a negative way - Positively, plaintiff must establish a prior user of his own - Appeals allowed.
