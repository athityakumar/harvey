Dr. V.L. Chandra And Ors. Etc. v All India Institute Of Medical Sciences And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 764; (1990) 3 SCC 38; (1990) SCC (L&S) 435; AIR 1990 SC 1670; 1990 (13) ATC 571; 1990 (60) FLR 726; JT 1990 (1) SC 515; 1990 LabIC 1368; 1990 (2) LLJ 29; 1990(1) SCALE 534; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 104; 1990 (2) SLR 430; 1990 (1) UJ 600; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 685

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956

Summary: Education - All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956, ss. 13 and 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - (A) Institute - Object - To carry on research in continuous way to improve medical knowledge - Building up a team of researchers for keeping up tempo - Interference of court sought for - (B) Service matter - Researchers in All India Institute of Medical Sciences - Appointment project-wise - Engagement however continuing for over a decade - Termination of service for absence of research project with Institute - Institute having no scope to employ researchers - Direction sought from Supreme Court - Held, (A) Direction issued to institute to frame scheme in that regard in co-ordination with Health Ministry and ICMR and project wise appointment of researchers only in special cases - (B) Supreme Court directed ICMR to offer adequate employment to researchers within two months - Order accordingly.
