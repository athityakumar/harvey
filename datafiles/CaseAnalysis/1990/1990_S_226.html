B. N. Singh and Others v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 840; (1990) 2 SCC 154; (1990) SCC (Cr) 283; AIR 1990 SC 1628; 1990 (1) Crimes 640; 1990 CRLJ 1601; 1990 CRLR 194; 1990 (1) GLH 256; JT 1990 (1) SC 164; 1990(1) SCALE 129; 1990 (1) UJ 617

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 300 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Offence of murder - Appreciation of evidence - (A) Interested witnesses - One of accused found to be falsely implicated by them - Version of witnesses improbable in view of injuries found all over body of deceased - Whether accused entitled to be acquitted - (B) Corroboration - Medical evidence, panchanama of scene of occurrence and fact that complaint was lodged within 20 minutes - Held, (A) Accused entitled to be acquitted as evidence of witnesses not trustworthy - (B) Do not and cannot corroborate version of witness as to complicity of accused as these circumstances at most may establish place and time of occurrence and nature of weapons used - Appeal allowed.
