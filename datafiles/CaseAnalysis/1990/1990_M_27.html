Union Of India And Ors. v M/S. Wood Papers Ltd. And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 809; (1990) 4 SCC 256; (1990) SCC (Tax) 422; AIR 1991 SC 2049; 1990 (28) ECC 182; 1991 (33) ECR 235; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 500; JT 1991 (1) SC 151; 1990 SCALE 61; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 659; 1991 (83) STC 251; 1990 (1) UJ 717

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Soap, Central Excise Rules 1944 (contd)

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 - Question regarding interpretation of exemption notification - Interpretation of words 'commencing production' coming in exemption notification - Held, rule of strict as well as liberal construction both are required to be invoked at different stages while interpreting exemption provision - When question is whether a subject falls in notification or in exemption cl then it being in nature of exception is to be construed strictly and against subject but once doubt about applicability is lifted and subject falls in notification then full play should be given to it and it calls for wider and liberal construction - Words 'commencing production' given in notification would mean commencing of production of goods by factory which was not existing on relevant date and they do not refer to commencement of production of specified exempted categories of paper - Appeal allowed.
