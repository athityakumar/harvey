Sanjeev Kumar Aggarwal v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 785; (1990) 3 SCC 309; (1990) SCC (Cr) 473; AIR 1990 SC 1202; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 719; 1990 CRLJ 1238; 1990 CrLR(SC) 292; JT 1990 (2) SC 62; 1990(1) SCALE 666; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 318

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) -

Detention under - Detenu already in jail - Detaining authority, aware of this fact - Recoreded circumstances for its satisfaction that detenu was likely to continue smuggling - Whether detention order, liable to be quashed on ground that detenu was in jail - Delay in serving order - Record Showing that it took quite sometime for translating documents to Hindi and Gurumukhi - Whether there were reasons for delay in serving detention order - Held, not liable to be quashed on that ground - There were valid and sufficient reasons for delay - Appeal dismissed.
