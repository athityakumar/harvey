Bhoolchand And Anr. v Kay Pee Cee Investments And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 490; (1991) 1 SCC 343; AIR 1991 SC 2053; AIR 1991 SCW 545; JT 1991 (1) SC 186; 1990 (2) RCJ 568; 1990 (2) RCR(Rent) 694; 1991 (1) RentLR 177; 1990(2) SCALE 711; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 251; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 251; 1991 (1) UJ 40

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, ss. 21 and 50 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115 and 107(2) - Eviction proceedings - Question regarding power of revision of High Court u/s. 50 of Act of 1961 - Whether HC can interfere with finding of Trial Court holding that bona fide requirement landlord not proved on ground of absence of documentary evidence revision? - Power of revision u/s. 50 is not narrow as in s. 115 of CPC, 1908 - HC in a revision u/s. 50 is required to satisfy itself not only as to legality of impugned order but also of its correctness - HC in a revision u/s. 50 is required to examine correctness of not only findings on questions of law but also on questions of fact - No finding by Trial Court as to existence of any material document in possession of landlord - HC justified in interfering with finding of fact even though based on credibility of witnesses - Considering premises held by other members of family while assessing bona fide need of landlord-firm is infirmity warranting interference in revision - Appeal dismissed.
