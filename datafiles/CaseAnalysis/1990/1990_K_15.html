Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Madras v K.R. Sadayappan

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 478; (1990) 4 SCC 1; (1990) SCC (Tax) 396; AIR 1992 SC 591; 1990 (86) CTR 120; [1990] 185 ITR 49; JT 1990 (3) SC 199; 1990(2) SCALE 89; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 255; [1990] 51 TAXMAN 304; 1992 (2) TLR 333

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 271(1)(c) and 256 - Concealment of income - Penalty - Tribunal though accepting that there was falsity of explanation given by assessee, setting aside penalty - Presumption of concealment of income by assessee arises - Held, no explanation on this account given - As no rebuttal on part of assessee was given so decision of tribunal was not justified - Question of law was liable to be referred to High Court - Appeal allowed.
