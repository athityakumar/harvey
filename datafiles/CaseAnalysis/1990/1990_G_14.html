Managing Director J and K Handicrafts Jammu v Good Luck Carpets

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 664; (1990) 4 SCC 740; AIR 1990 SC 864

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Jammu and Kashmir Arbitration Act, 2002, s. 20 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - (A) Whether arbitrator can allow claims which were neither contemplated by incentive scheme nor by agreement where under reference was made? - Held, having perused scheme and agreement for limited purpose - Arbitrator had no jurisdiction to make award except with regard to items referred in agreement, award with regard to other items being without jurisdiction cannot be sustained in proceeding for making said award rule of court - (B) Whether arbitrator has any jurisdiction to award interest either for period prior to date of institution of proceedings of pendente life and future? - Executive Engineer (Irrigation) v Abhaduta Jena (1987 Indlaw SC 554) followed, said decision lays down certain exceptions to general rule that an arbitrator cannot award interest, one of such exceptions in s. 34 of CPC r/w Interest Act of 1978 which includes an arbitrator within definition of 'court', neither Interest Act of 1839 nor Interest Act of 1978 applies to State of Jammu and Kashmir - Further held, arbitrator not competent to award any interest - Appeal partly allowed.
