Collector of Central Excise, Calcutta v Berger Paints India Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 751; (1990) SCC (Tax) 289; (1990) 2 SCC 349; AIR 1990 SC 1276; 1990 CRLR 371; 1990 CrLR(SC) 371; 1990 (41) DLT 186; 1990 (28) ECC 4; 1990 (29) ECR 433; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 210; JT 1990 (3) SC 133; 1990 SCALE 35B; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 1027; 1990 (1) UJ 666

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Socio-Economic

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1982,r. 9(2) - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L(b) - Tribunal dismissed appeal on ground that provisions of rule 9(2) of Rules had not been complied with - Tribunal was of opinion that purpose and spirit of r. 9(2) was to ensure that appeal was authorised by Collector to be filed - Tribunal was of opinion that there was nothing in rules to justify acceptance of kind of general authorisation or notesheet orders which authorised filing of appeal without referring to a specified officer as being in consonance with r. 9(2) of Rules - Challenged - Rules are to ensure that there was an application of mind to points in respect of which question for filing an appeal arose and that appeal was duly authorised by Collector, and was filed by person authorised by Collector in order to ensure that frivolous and unnecessary appeals are not filed - In present context and in view of terms of purpose intended to be served, appeal was competent and was duly filed in compliance with procedure as enjoined by rules - It has to be borne in mind that rules framed therein were to carry out purposes of Act - Language of relevant Section and rules do not warrant such a strained construction - Held, Tribunal was in error in dismissing appeal on ground that it did - Matter remanded to Tribunal - Appeal disposed of.
