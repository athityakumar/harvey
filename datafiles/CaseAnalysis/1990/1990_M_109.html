U. P. State Road Transport Corporation v Muniruddin

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 373; (1990) 4 SCC 464; (1991) SCC (L&S) 214; 1991 (16) ATC 444; 1990 (61) FLR 656; JT 1990 (3) SC 494; 1990 (2) LLN 712; 1990(2) SCALE 250; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 777; 1990 (3) SLJ 144; 1991 (3) SLR 39; 1990 (2) UJ 496; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1964; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 788

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Retirement benefits - Entitlement - Respondent was a bus conductor in Road Transport Corporation - A trap was laid against him and disciplinary enquiry was initiated and dismissal order was passed on 23-3-1965 - Respondent filed a civil suit questioning same - Trial Court dismissed suit - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal - On appeal, HC allowed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Corporation contended that where failure to produce carbon copies of some of the documents did not cause any prejudice and that at any rate it was question of fact, HC erred in interfering in second appeal - Held, SC found at more than one place that Trial Court had observed that employee could have insisted on production of these documents - HC no doubt had considered this aspect in detail and in doing so had referred to the contents of various documents - From this alone it could not be said that HC had not kept in view scope of second appeal - Since employee was throughout pleading that he did not make erasures or any other false entry, it naturally became necessary to see whether they were also found in carbon copies - From this point of view HC considered various figures and entries in originals in which such erasures and entries were alleged to have been made by employee and eventually observed that entire enquiry was based on some of these documents and if a carbon copy of way-bills were shown, authority might well have been convinced that charge levelled against him was not correct, and that, therefore the non-supply of these documents had caused prejudice - Hence HC had not committed any error which warranted interference u/art. 136 of Constitution - Admittedly respondent had already reached age of superannuation some years ago - During these years respondent should have sought some employment or other hand counsel also could not seriously dispute the same - Taking into consideration all these aspects including that respondent would have been entitled for some retirement benefits, SC award Rs. 35, 000 and directed appellant to pay this amount to respondent within 2 months from today - Appeal disposed of.
