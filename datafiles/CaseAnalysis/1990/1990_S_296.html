Jaswant Singh v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 593; (1991) SCC (L&S) 282; (1991) 1 SCC 362; AIR 1991 SC 385; 1991 (15) ATC 729; 1991 (1) CLR 1; 1991 (62) FLR 137; JT 1990 (4) SC 554; 1991 LabIC 258; 1991 (1) LLN 13; 1990(2) SCALE 1152; 1991 (1) SLR 180; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 354; 1991 (1) UJ 276; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 959

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 133 and proviso 311(2) - (A) Dismissal - Dispensation with departmental enquiry - Nature of satisfaction of concerned authority - (B) Remand - Appeal against dismissal of writ petition in limine by High Court - Held, (A) Subjective satisfaction of concerned authority must be fortified by independent material - Decision by Supreme Court on legal aspect only as decision on factual aspect found to be unnecessary - (B) Supreme Court declined to remit case to HC after lapse of seven - Eight years - Appeal allowed.
