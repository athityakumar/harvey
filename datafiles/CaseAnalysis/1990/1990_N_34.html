Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay v New Standard Engineering Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 306; (1991) 1 SCC 611; AIR 1991 SC 1362; 1991 (4) Bom.C.R. 403; JT 1991 (1) SC 174; 1990(2) SCALE 1236; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 478; 1991 (1) UJ 253

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Act, 1888

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 154 - Whether the High Court has rightly directed the Corporation to re-assess the rateable value of the building taking into account only the actual rentals recoverable from each of the tenants? - The restrictions in respect of rent cannot be whittled down by allowing the Corporation to choose its own mode or method of assessing the rateable value - There is no disagreement on the finding that there was no consultation with the Corporation in respect of the Scheme in question - The property tax is the major source of corporation revenue, consultation is a matter of importance and consequence to the Corporation - Its right to recover normal rates of property tax would be deprived of if the Govt. prepares a scheme for subsidised housing without consultation - The right to be consulted in opposition to a claim or proposal which will adversely affect its financial interests is to be regarded as mandatory - Consultation with the Corporation must be there on the essential points , it must enable the Corporation to consider the pros and cons of the question as to the concessional rate of property tax - Held, The appeal is allowed.
