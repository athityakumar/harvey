Keshav Chandra Joshi and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 270; (1992) Supp1 SCC 272; (1993) SCC (L&S) 694; AIR 1991 SC 284; 1993 (24) ATC 545; 1991 LabIC 216; 1990(2) SCALE 951; 1991 (2) SLJ 42; 1992 (8) SLR 636; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Forest Service Rules 1952 r 3(b) Appendix B

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 309 and 311 - Uttar Pradesh Forest Service Rules, 1952, rr. 3(4), 5 and 27 - Computation of seniority - Ad hoc appointment not according to Rules and was made as stop gap arrangement - Period of officiation in such post - Whether can be considered for computing seniority? - Appointment - Relaxation of requirements of conditions of service by Governor u/r. 27 - Whether r. 5(a), (b) and Appendix 'A', 'B' would be subject to r. 27 - Held, seniority would be counted only from date of substantive appointment - Cannot be considered - R. 5(a), (b) and Appendix 'A', 'B' are basic rules of recruitment and would not be subject to r. 27 - Petition allowed.
