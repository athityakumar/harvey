Union of India and Others v Dev Raj Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 495; (1991) 1 SCC 63; AIR 1991 SC 93; JT 1990 (4) SC 176; 1990(2) SCALE 794; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 300; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 300; 1991 (1) UJ 68

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Land Acquisition

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 7 and 20(1) - (A) What constitutes an application for permission to convert user of land? - Held, respondents wanted to know from Land and Development Officer were terms and conditions for construction of a commercial building on land and charges that would have to be paid for same - This can hardly be called an application for permission to construct a commercial building on land - It is no more than an enquiry - Letter did not constitute an application for permission to use land for commercial purposes - Absence of a prescribed form does not make letter required application - Least that is expected in an application for purpose is a request by all lessees to permit change of user of land showing readiness and willingness to abide by terms and conditions for such conversion of user - Letter of 15-02-1978 was not an application made for change of user of land - It was for first time on 27-02-1981 that a proper application was made for purpose - (B) From which date conversion charges are leviable? - Held, in absence of anything else on record, it will have to be held that date with reference to which conversion charges have to be counted is 27-02-1981 - Authority has calculated additional premium with reference to 27-05-1981 on footing that outer limit for granting permission was three months from date of receipt of application - There is no justification for authority to hold thus, for they are expected to process application as early as possible and not to wait till end of three months - Unless there are valid reasons for them to do so or delay is caused on account of an omission or commission on part of applicants, it is not proper to take end of three months as date with reference to which conversion charges should be calculated - In present case it makes no difference whether charges are calculated with reference to 27-02-1981 or May 1981 - Further held, additional premium should be calculated by appellants on basis of rate which was prevalent as on 27-02-1981 which is date of application made for change of user - Interest should be charged on such additional premium w.e.f. 12-04-1984 since a period of three months from date of notice, viz, 12-02-1984 was available to Respondent to make payment of additional premium - Appeal allowed.
