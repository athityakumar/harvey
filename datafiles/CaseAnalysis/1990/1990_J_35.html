Jogendra Lal Saha v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 272; (1991) Supp2 SCC 654; AIR 1991 SC 1148

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930, Bihar Public Demand Recovery Act 1914

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Indirect Tax - Bihar Public Demand Recovery Act 1914 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 82 and 83 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Whether the High Court rightly initiated proceedings against the appellant under the Bihar Public Demands Recovery Act, 1914, for recovery of the two installments on the requisition of the forest officer after adjustment of the security deposit and the price obtained at a re-auction and upheld the action? - The fact that the third installment had not fallen due by the date, the question of including that amount in the certificate did not arise - The proceeding was non-est in law - The State's dues have got to be recovered on the admitted facts and if the certificate proceeding is quashed on technical grounds ,the interest of the State would be prejudiced - Ss. 82 and 83 of the Indian Forest Act, 1927 makes it clear that the liability of the appellant shall be confined to two installments which were due for the second and third years in terms of the contract after setting off the security deposit and the amounts that have been recovered being Rs. 2200 and Rs 28, 000 respectively - The Certificate Officer before proceeding further shall apply his mind to the facts and if necessary hear the certificate debtor, ascertain the exact due, make an appropriate amendment in the certificate as required , and proceed to recover the amount that he finds is still recoverable under law - The appellant shall not be burdened with any claim of interest or other charges until the Certificate Officer modifies the certificate - Appeal is dismissed .
