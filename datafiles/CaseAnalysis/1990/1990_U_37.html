D.V. Kapoor v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 286; (1990) 4 SCC 314; (1990) SCC (L&S) 696; AIR 1990 SC 1923; 1990 (16) ALR 943; 1990 (14) ATC 906; 1990 (2) CLR 383; 1990 (61) FLR 429; JT 1990 (3) SC 403; 1990 LabIC 1522; 1990(2) SCALE 175; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 697; 1990 (3) SLR 5; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1970

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Civil Services Conduct Rules 1964

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Central Civil Services Pension Rules, 1972, r. 9 - Civil Services Conduct Rules, 1964, rr. 3(1)(ii) and 3(1)(iii) - Disciplinary proceeding initiated u/rr. 3(1)(ii) and 3(1)(iii) of 1964 Rules - Delinquent allowed to retire voluntarily - (A) Whether Authorities can continue proceeding - Right of employee to pension is statutory - (B) Withholding of pension - Powers of President - Hedged with conditions precedent - That pensioner committed grave misconduct or negligence - Held, (A) Authorities have power to continue proceeding - (B) Finding of misconduct or negligence must precede withholding of pension - Appeal allowed.
