Mostt. Simrikhia v Smt. Dolley Mukherjee @ Smt. Chhabimukherjee And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 686; (1990) SCC (Cr) 327; (1990) 2 SCC 437; AIR 1990 SC 1605; 1990 (2) Crimes 271; 1990 CRLJ 1599; 1990 CrLR(SC) 258; JT 1990 (3) SC 79; 1990(1) SCALE 455; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 788

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction of HC

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 452, 323 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 202, 192(2) - Jurisdiction of HC - Complaint was filed by appellant against respondent accused u/ss. 452, 323 IPC - Magistrate had transferred the case without taking cognizance of the offence - Petition before HC - Petition was dismissed - Respondents again filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC before HC alleging, inter alia, that the record of the proceedings on close scrutiny would indicate that the case had not been taken cognizance of before transfer - Single Judge accepted the case of the respondents and quashed the proceedings by impugned order - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, there had definite finding that the complaint was taken cognizance of by Magistrate before he transferred the proceedings u/s. 192(2) CrPC for enquiry u/s. 202 CrPC. The only ground on which relief was claimed is the alleged irregularity in the transfer of the proceedings. It was not open to the parties to re-agitate the question by fresh application nor was the court empowered u/s. 482 CrPC to reconsider the matter. The inherent jurisdiction of HC cannot be invoked to override bar of review u/s. 362. It is clearly stated in Smt. Sooraj Devi v Pyare Lal and Another 1981 Indlaw SC 135 that inherent power of the court cannot be exercised for doing that which is specifically prohibited by the Code. The law is therefore clear that inherent power cannot be exercised for doing that which cannot be done on account of the bar under other provisions of the Code. The court is not empowered to review its own decision under the purported exercise of inherent power. SC found that impugned order in instant case is in effect one reviewing the earlier order on reconsideration of the same materials. The HC has grievously erred in doing so. Even on merits, SC does not find any compelling reasons to quash proceedings at that stage. Thus, order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
