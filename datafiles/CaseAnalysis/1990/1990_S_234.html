Dhan Singh and Others v State of Haryana Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 246; (1991) Supp2 SCC 190; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1179; AIR 1991 SC 1047; 1991 (17) ATC 317; 1991 (62) FLR 131; JT 1990 (4) SC 735; 1991 LabIC 683; 1991 (2) LLN 8; 1990(2) SCALE 1216; 1991 (1) SLR 200; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 423; 1991 (1) UJ 267

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Punjab Govt. National Emergency (Concession) Rules, 1965, r. 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309, 311 and 352 - (A) Military service - Benefits of increments, seniority and pension - Persons enrolled during emergency but released from military service on compassionate ground - Amendment to r. 4 denying benefit to them under rule, already struck down as violative of arts. 14 and 16 by Supreme Court - Whether such persons were entitled to benefits u/ r. 4 - (B) 'Military service' - Amendment of definition - Benefits of military service not available to those who joined before proclamation of emergency - Whether discriminatory and violative of art. 14 - (C) Civil services - Seniority - Ex-army personnel re-employed in State service - Period of service in army after lifting of emergency - Could it count for determining his seniority - Held, entitled to benefits - Not discriminatory and violative of art. 14 on that ground - Could not count for determining seniority - Appeal partly allowed.
