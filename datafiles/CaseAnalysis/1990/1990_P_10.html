State Of Andhra Pradesh v P.V. Pavithran

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 649; (1990) 2 SCC 340; (1991) SCC (Cr) 7; AIR 1990 SC 1266; 1990 (2) Crimes 40; 1990 CRLJ 1306; 1990 CrLR(SC) 251; JT 1990 (2) SC 43; 1990(1) SCALE 418; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154 and 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2) and 5(1)(c) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) First Information Report - Quashing of - Ground - Inordinate delay on part of investigating agency in completing investigation - (B) Bribery - F I R filed against Govt. servant - Allowed to retire peacefully on attaining age of superannuation - F I R and consequent proceedings quashed by High Court - 50 days thereafter Govt. according sanction for prosecution of Govt. servant - Whether interference by HC is required? - Held, (A) Cannot always be a ground for quashing F I R - Order of HC quashing F I R could not be interfered with - Appeal dismissed.
