State of Uttar Pradesh v Motiram and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 697; (1990) 4 SCC 389; (1990) SCC (Cr) 585; AIR 1990 SC 1709; 1990 (2) Crimes 705; 1990 CRLJ 1710; 1990 CrLR(SC) 450; JT 1990 (2) SC 358; 1990(1) SCALE 152; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 939; 1990 (2) UJ 544

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147, 109, 120B, 307 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - There was deep rooted enmity and simmering feelings between 2 groups due to various incidents happened among them - On account of deep-rooted animosity between parties, first 2 accused and accused No. 27 hatched a conspiracy with some of their associates to murder 'P' and his men, when they were expected to be Engaged for cutting standing sugar cane - In pursuance of this conspiracy these 3 accused traveled without tickets by 2 Dn. Mail Train running between Khora Bhar and Gauri Bazar which stations lie on Gorakhpur-Bhatni line - As these 3 accused who traveled as ticketless passengers did not pay charges due from them to TTE/Accused No. 40, they as pre-planned, were apprehended and produced before Railway Magistrate on 13-1-1974 who convicted them and sentenced them to imprisonment till January1974 - Occurrence in question took place and all 13 deceased persons accompanied by 2 injured persons PWs 3 and 24 and few others went for cutting the sugar cane crops raised by one of deceased and his family members -Culprits chased these 13 deceased persons ruthlessly and indiscriminately attacked them - All 13 persons instantaneously succumbed to their injuries - Trial Court acquitted 25 accused persons out of the total of 41 accused and convicted rest of 16 accused under various provisions of IPC and 1959 Act - On appeals, HC disposed of these appeals inclusive of State appeal and referred case by common impugned judgment - HC allowed appeals filed by convicted accused and set aside convictions - Hence instant appeals - Whether instant appeals were maintainable - Held, there was a deliberate false implication of accused Nos. 1, 2 and 27 to whom overt acts were attributed - In fact, HC had gone in great depth into facts and circumstances of the case and rightly concluded that prosecution had miserably failed in establishing guilt of accused except Accused no. 36 - In spite of best efforts and great deal of pondering over matter, SC was unable to disagree with conclusion arrived at by HC in rejecting testimony of these witnesses whose evidence lacks guarantee to inspire confidence especially when major portion of evidence was manifestly false and patently incredible - No doubt, circumstances attending case, namely, conduct of accused no. 1, 2 and 27 voluntarily getting themselves arrested by accused no. 40 created suspicion against them - However, suspicion by itself strong it might be was not sufficient to take place of legal proof and warrant finding of guilt of these 3 accused - It was pertinent to note that even charge framed by Trial Court reads as if all accused inclusive of accused no. 1, 2 and 27 formed themselves into an unlawful assembly on 14-1-1974 in village Bali in prosecution of common object of committing murder of 13 deceased persons and disposing of dead bodies - In such a situation, could it be said that there was justification for convicting rest of accused barring accused Nos. 1, 2 and 27 for specific acts attributed to them by witnesses whose evidence was tainted with patent falsehood - Entire evidence was nothing but a coloured version with concocted story and exaggerated account mixed with falsehood - Appeals dismissed.
