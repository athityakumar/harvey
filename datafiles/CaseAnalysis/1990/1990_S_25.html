S.V. Sivaswami Servai v Hafez Motor Transport (Firm) And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 372; (1990) 4 SCC 459; AIR 1991 SC 911; 1990 (3) CCC 569; JT 1990 (3) SC 518; 1990(2) SCALE 275; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s. 47(3) - Grant of stage carriage permit to appellant, on ground that respondent no. 1 had already been granted one permit in same sitting, challenged - Held, grant of a permit is to be made primarily with object of serving interests of general public and it cannot be treated as a dispute relating to grant of a permit between rival claimants only - It is not a matter which can be decided merely on basis of an agreement between two rivals - Question of grant of permit is to be decided primarily by RTA having regard primarily to interests of general public and other prescribed relevant factors - U/s. 47(3) RTA is first required to determine number of stage carriages for route and then to grant permits according to that determination as made - Grant of any permit in excess thereof was not permissible without first making a fresh determination and increasing number, if necessary - Matter is remanded to RTA for a fresh consideration and decision of claim of all applicants for grant of permit on merits in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
