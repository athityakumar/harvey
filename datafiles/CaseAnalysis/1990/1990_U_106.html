Ramakant Shripad Sinai Advalpalkar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 530; (1991) Supp2 SCC 733; (1992) SCC (L&S) 115; AIR 1991 SC 1145; 1992 (6) SLR 290

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Administrative - Service - Goa, Daman and Diu (Absorbed Employees) Act, 1965 - Service of Upper Division Clerk - Duties of Treasurer - Liberation of Territory - Determination of Equivalence - Appellant joined services as Aspirante/Upper Division Clerk in Portugese Govt. - Appellant was asked to perform duties of Treasurer at an allowance of Rs.100/- per month as Treasurer post was vacant - After liberation of territories Appellant assailed for determination of equivalence - Appellant's claim was rejected by judicial Commissioner - Hence instant Appeal - Whether Appellant was entitled to higher rank under Govt. of India - Held, arrangement contemplated did not amount to promotion of Appellant as Treasurer as Appellant also held substantive lower post - Appellant continued to be “absorbed employee” only in relation to post held immediately prior to20-12-1961 - Appellant was only in charge of Treasurer Post as Appellant had no right, equities or expectations to be built upon -' In-charge' arrangement was not recognition of or was necessarily based on seniority - Appeal dismissed.
