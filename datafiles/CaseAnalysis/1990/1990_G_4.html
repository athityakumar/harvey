State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v G. S. Dall and Flour Mills

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K.N. Saikia, Ranganath Misra, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 454; (1992) Supp1 SCC 150; AIR 1991 SC 772; [1991] 187 ITR 478; JT 1990 (4) SC 430; 1990(2) SCALE 756; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 590; 1991 (80) STC 138; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Permissibility

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Exemption - Permissibility - Madhya Pradesh Sales Tax 1958, s. 12 - Assesses claim for exemption from sales tax for certain periods in question was accepted by HC in case of G.S. Dhall & Flour Mills - Subsequently Full Bench HC in case of Jagadamba Industries disapproved the view taken by DB in G.S. Dhall & Flour Mills case and petitions were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether s. 12 of the Act enabled State Govt. to grant exemption from levy of sales tax in certain circumstances - Held, s. 12 of the Act was clear that, while notification under it should be prospective or retrospective, only prospective operation should be given to notification rescinding exemption granted earlier - If notification was interpreted as done by it or even held it to be ambiguous there was no scope for assessee to invoke the doctrine of promissory estoppel - In other cases covered by Full Bench decision mere fact that exemption was initially granted and then revoked would be insufficient to find the claim of estoppel particularly when it had been found that assesses started production after 12-1-1983 and claimed exemption very much later - Terms of notification were clear and envisage no denial of exemption to traditional industries - Full Bench had there pointed out that even if it could be said that two interpretations of notification were equally plausible, assesses in those cases had set up industries after explicit instructions of 12-1-1983 were made public and thus took deliberate risk - Hence, on the scope of notification Full Bench decision which should be treated as overruled - It was concluded that G.S. Dhall and Flour Mills case laid down correct law and not Full Bench - State Govt. had not intended exemption to be availed of by certain categories of industries - But it had failed to achieve purpose on account of wide language in which it couched exemption notification - Appeals disposed of.
