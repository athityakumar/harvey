Mahabir Auto Stores And Ors. v Indian Oil Corporation And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 831; (1990) 3 SCC 752; AIR 1990 SC 1031; [1990] 69 Comp Cas 746; JT 1990 (1) SC 363; 1990(1) SCALE 410; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 818; 1990 (2) SLR 69; 1990 (2) UJ 62

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Trade - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 298 - State - Power to enter into or not to enter into contract with individuals - Exercise of, must be governed by rule of law and informed by reason - Firm - Sale and distribution of lubricants for 18 years - Abrupt stoppage of supply of lubricants to firm by Indian Oil Corporation - Without notice or intimation - Constitutionality - Held, action of corporation is arbitrary - Order accordingly.
