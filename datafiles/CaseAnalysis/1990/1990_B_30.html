Sanatan Gauda v Berhampur University And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 691; (1990) 3 SCC 23; (1990) SCC (L&S) 423; AIR 1990 SC 1075; 1990 (70) CLT 241; JT 1990 (2) SC 57; 1990(1) SCALE 647; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 273; 1991 (3) SLR 29; 1990 (1) UJ 661

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Behrampur University Regulations

Summary: Education - Berhampur University Act, 1966, s. 23 - Regulations u/chap. VIII, regn. 1 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Admission to Law course - Eligibility - No requirement of any particular marks for PG students - Candidate passing M A Examination with 36% in aggregate - (B) Candidate admitted to law course - Permitted to appear in pre-law and intermediate Law examinations - Admitted to final year course - Refusal to declare results - Ground, ineligibility to be admitted to law course - Applicability of rule of estoppel - Held, is duty qualified - Barred by estoppel - Appeal allowed.
