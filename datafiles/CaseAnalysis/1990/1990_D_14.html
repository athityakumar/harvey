 Marri Chandra Shekhar Rao v Dean, Geth G. S. Medical College and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray, K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 695; (1990) 3 SCC 130; 1990 (14) ATC 671; 1990 (41) DLT 239; JT 1990 (2) SC 285; 1990 SCALE 3; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 843; 1990 (3) SLJ 18; 1990 (2) UJ 117; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 932

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Affirmative Action

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 15, 29, 32, 341 and 342 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Amendment) Order Bill, 1967 - (A) Whether one who is recognised as a ST in State of his origin and birth continues to have benefits or privileges or rights in state of migration or where later goes and what expression 'in relation to that State' in conjunction with 'for purposes of this Constitution' in art.341 seeks to convey? - Words 'for purposes of this Constitution' must mean that a SC so designated must have right u/arts. 14, 19(1)(d), 19(1)(e) and 19(1)(f) in as much as these are applicable to him in his area where he migrates or where he goes; expression 'in relation to that State' would become nugatory if in all States special privileges or rights granted to Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes are carried forward and it will also be inconsistent with whole purpose of scheme of reservation - Held, treating determination u/arts. 341 and 342 to be valid for all over country would be in negation to very purpose and scheme and language of arts. 341 r/w art. 15(4); construction that reservation made in respect of SC or ST of that State is determined to be entitled to all privileges and rights under Constitution in that State would be most correct way of reading, consistent with language, purpose and scheme of Constitution - (B) Whether those SC and ST students who get protection of being classed as SC or ST in States of origin are entitled to some protection so that they may continue or pursue their education in case of involuntary transfer by their families ? - Held, where migration from one State to another is involuntary, by force of circumstances either of employment or of profession, in such cases if students or persons apply in migrated State where without affecting prejudicially rights of Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes in those States or areas, any facility or protection for continuance of study or admission can be given to one who has or migrated then some consideration is desirable to be made on that ground - (C) Whether petitioner is entitled to admission to Medical college? - Held, boy's educational prospects should not be jeopardized since he has progressed to a certain extent and disqualifying him at this stage or this year on ground that he is not entitled to protection of SC or ST, would not confer any commensurate benefit to SC or ST in Maharashtra or for that matter on anybody else, therefore though petitioner is not entitled to be admitted to Medical College on basis that he belonged to ST in Andhra Pradesh but his continuance in college will depend upon consideration indicated herein before - Petition disposed of.
