Dr. Yogesh Bhardwaj v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 804; (1990) 3 SCC 355; AIR 1991 SC 356; 1990 ALR 476; 1990 (1) AWC 720; JT 1990 (2) SC 236; 1990(1) SCALE 808; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 666; 1990 (2) SLR 758; 1990 (2) UJ 228; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1975

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act 1974

Summary: Education - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1974, s. 26(5) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 5 - (A) Notification D/- 19-08-1983 u/cls. (2) and (4)(a), (b) - 'Domiciled' within meaning of cl. (4)(a) and 'bona fide resident' in cl. (4)(b) - Student, if covered under - Student nominated by another State for a course, coming to U. P. and residing there for more than five years - (B) International Law (Private) - Acquisition of domicile of origin - Change of domicile - Burden of proof - Held, his residence does not acquire attributes of domicile, however, he comes within meaning of expression 'bona fide resident' in cl. (4)(b) - It is on him who asserts it, domicile of choice is combination of residence and intention - Appeal allowed.
