D. B. Raju v H. J. Kantharaj and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 317; (1990) 4 SCC 178; JT 1999 (3) SC 148; 1990(2) SCALE 1; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 336; 1990 (2) UJ 477; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2049

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Karnataka Zila Parishads, Taluk Panchayat Samithis, Mandal Panchayat and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1983, Karnataka Zilla Parishads, Taluk Panchayat, Mandal Panchayats and Nyaya Panchayats (Conduct of Election) Rules 1985

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Zilla Parishads, Taluk Panchayat Samithis, Mandal Panchayats and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1983 - Assembly election - Nomination of members - Inclusion of nominations - Legality - Election in question related to 'C' Constituency comprising 121 Mandal Panchayats - Appellant alleged that a decision was taken by 'C' Zilla Parishad to nominate 2 members from each Mandal Panchayat, that was total number of 242 members - However, 242 members were duly nominated in time to be included in the electoral roll under the 1983 Act - Respondent No. 1/election petitioner denied the nomination included in electoral roll - Respondent No.1 preferred writ petition before HC - Respondent No. 1 contended that inclusion of names of nominated members in electoral roll took place after period for nomination was over and they were not included in electoral roll in eye of law - HC set aside the election of appellant to State Legislative Council and directed the recount of votes after excluding those of 242 nominated members - However, election was held by adopting the 'single transferable vote method - Aggrieved, appellant preferred instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, difference between an Act of a legislature and a subordinate was the Acts of the legislature were passed by accredited representatives of people who in theory could be trusted to see that their constituents know what had been done, and that was done only after debates took place which were open to public - But instant case was different with delegated legislation and, if might add, also in case of orders passed by authorities like that in present appeal - Mode of publication could vary but there should be reasonable publication of some sort - It was hold that in eye of law electoral roll in question was not modified by inclusion of names of nominated members - Impugned order passed by HC was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
