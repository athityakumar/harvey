Milak Brothers v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 427; (1991) Supp1 SCC 71; AIR 1990 SC 2256; 1991 CRLR 195; 1991 CrLR(SC) 195; 1991 (32) ECR 6; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 204; JT 1990 (4) SC 508; 1990(2) SCALE 727; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 141; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 141; 1991 (1) UJ 193

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Cess Act, 1940, Tariff Act, 1934

Summary: Civil - Customs - Tariffs - Customs Act, 1962 - Tariff Act, 1934 - Appellants were engaged in the process of processed peanuts which were exported to a series of process to improve quality and appearance - Appellant contended that the product exported by it was processed peanuts and that it did not fall under the tariff entries - Whether the goods exported by the appellants are liable to export duty? - Held, yes - Product exported has not ceased to be groundnut - No ambiguity in the interpretation of the tariff entry - Tariff entry covers groundnut and there is no justification for confining it any one variety alone - End product sold is still groundnut - Appeals dismissed.
