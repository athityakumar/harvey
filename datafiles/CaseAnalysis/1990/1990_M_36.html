State Of Maharashtra v Mahadeo Deoman Rai Alias Kalal And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, K. Jagannatha Shetty, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 791; (1990) 3 SCC 579; JT 1990 (3) SC 48; 1990 SCALE 104; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 533; 1990 (1) UJ 703

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - In 1955 respondent purchased land in question from one Patwardhan and in 1957 obtained permission to construct a building thereon - However, no construction was made and in March 1962, a notification u/s. 4 of Land Acquisition Act was issued for purpose of establishing a Tonga Stand - Respondent made a fresh application for permission to make construction - He was told not to do so on ground that land was reserved for road widening under a Town planning Scheme - He however started construction work and when prevented from so doing, filed a suit in civil court inter alia claiming damages - Soon thereafter a resolution was passed by Municipal Council on 13-02-1967 whereby a decision was taken to accord permission to respondent as asked for - Aforesaid resolution was thereafter rescinded by Municipal Council - Whether resolution was passed by way of a compromise between parties, and acting upon same respondent withdrew his suit which he had earlier filed in civil court, and, therefore, Municipal Council cannot be permitted to wriggle out of situation? - Held, fact that respondent has withdrawn his suit for damages could not by itself indicate that municipality was bound by said Resolution - Municipality was equally answerable to State Govt. which restrained respondent from proceedings with construction and appointed a high power Committee to go into entire matter - There is no scope for application of principles of res judicata - Question as to whether a particular scheme framed in exercise of statutory provisions is in public interest or not has to be determined according to need of time and a final decision for all times to come cannot be taken - State or a body like Municipal Council entrusted with a public duty to look after requirement of community has to assess situation from time to time and take necessary decision periodically - Resolution dated 13-02-1967 was not binding on Municipal Council so as to disable it to take a different decision later - Appeal allowed.
