Othayoth Lekshmy Amma and Another v Neelachinkuniyil Govindan Nair and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 792; (1990) 3 SCC 374; JT 1990 (3) SC 230; 1990 SCALE 196; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 539; 1990 (2) UJ 242

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 66 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, s. 13-B - Appellants filed execution application u/s. 13-B of Act for restoration of possessions - Respondent 3 (court auction purchaser) and his assignees respondent 1 and 2 contended that appellants have no interest in properties - Appellants attacked contending that respondents 1 and 2 were benamidar of decree-holder - Trial Court held respondents 1 and 2 are not bona fide purchasers for consideration - Ultimately High Court affirmed decree of lower Appellate Court and dismissed second appeal - (A) Whether respondents 1 and 2 are bona fide purchasers of scheduled land in dispute for adequate consideration entitled to benefit of proviso of s. 13-B (1)? - Held, evidence and circumstances of case coupled with evidence on record do establish that respondents 1 and 2 are not bona fide purchasers for consideration - Therefore, respondents 1 and 2 are not entitled to benefit of proviso to s. 13-B(1) of Act - (B) Whether appellants are entitled to benefit of s. 13-B(1) of Act? - Held, yes - For invoking benefit of s. 13-B(1), person entitled to restoration of possession of his holding should within a period of 6 months from commencement of Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969 deposit purchase money together with interest at rate of 6 per cent per annum in court and apply to court for setting aside sale and for restoration of possession of his holding - S. 13-B(1) in view of the said intervening cause cannot be availed of since the respondent 3, though, got possession earlier by the auction purchase was dispossessed by the subsequent event of trespass by the appellants and got possession by instituting the suit O.S. No. 6 of 1966 - Appeal allowed.
