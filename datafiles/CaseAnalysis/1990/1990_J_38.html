State of Punjab and Others v Joginder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 770; (1990) SCC (Cr) 419; (1990) 2 SCC 661; AIR 1990 SC 1396; 1990 (2) Crimes 260; 1990 CRLJ 1464; 1990 CrLR(SC) 273; JT 1990 (2) SC 323; 1990(1) SCALE 610; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 147; 1990 (2) ShimLC 118

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interpretation

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Paras 516-B, 631 - Interpretation - Validity - Appellants filed instant appeals seeking interpretation of paras 516-B and 631 of the Manual for Superintendence and Management of Jails in State wherein preface to Manual shows that those paragraphs of the Manual against which a black line appears were in substance, either quotations from law or from the Rules having force of law - HC held that para 516-B, as amended by executive instructions of 1971 and 1976, could not override statutory rule contained in para 631 read with note appended thereto - whether impugned judgment and order of HC was valid - Held, according to preface only those paras which were black lined had statutory character - Further, Note in question was not so black lined where note was intended to be given statutory character it was black lined - Secondly, source of para 516-B and Note was very same Resolution No. 159-167 of Govt. - It was difficult to believe that same resolution was intended to be a mere executive instruction in one part of Manual and was intended to be conferred a statutory character in another part of same Manual - Thirdly, marginal note to Note in question in terms refers to para 516-B which means it was merely a reproduction of latter para - Thus, if Note was intended to be conferred a statutory character, it would have been black lined in keeping with scheme of the Manual - Lastly Note at the foot of para was by way of a reminder that notwithstanding duration fixed under said rule, para 516-B requires that cases of such prisoners should be submitted on expiry of duration fixed under para 516-B - Therefore, Note was neither an integral part of para 631 nor did it have statutory flavour as held by HC - Appeals allowed.
