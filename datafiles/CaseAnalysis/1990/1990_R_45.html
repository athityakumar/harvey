Coal Mines Provident Fund Commissioner v Ramesh Chander Jha

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 682; (1990) SCC (L&S) 185; (1990) 1 SCC 589; AIR 1990 SC 648; 1990 (60) FLR 347; JT 1990 (1) SC 115; 1990 LabIC 577; 1990 (1) LLN 291; 1990(1) SCALE 96; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 181; 1990 (1) UJ 387

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Coal Mines Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act 1948 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 2(17) - Employee's Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 - Coal Mines Provident Fund Commr. - Public officer u/s. 2(17)(h) - Definition of - Whether Coal Mines Provident Fund Commr. is a 'Public officer' as defined in s. 2(17) of CPC? - Held, word 'service' in s. 2(17)(h) must necessarily mean something more than being merely subject to orders of Govt. or control of Govt. - To serve means 'to perform function; do what is required for' - The Commr. appointed by Govt. performs functions as envisaged in Act and scheme thereunder - When he is actually acting in capacity of Provident Fund Commr., he does not cease to be an officer in service of Govt. - Provident Fund Commr. holds office of Commr. on appointment by Govt. by virtue of his office - His services are temporarily placed at disposal of Board - He does not therefore, cease to be an officer in service of Govt. - Payment of his pay out of Fund does not alter his status as Govt. employee - The courts below have erred in holding that Coal Mines Provident Fund Commr. is not a public officer within meaning of term in s. 2(17)(h) of CPC - Appeal allowed.
