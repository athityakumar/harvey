Government of Andhra Pradesh and Another v M. Hayagreev Sarma

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 835; (1990) SCC (L&S) 542; (1990) 2 SCC 682; 1990 (13) ATC 713; 1990 (60) FLR 837; JT 1990 (2) SC 138; 1990 (2) LLJ 158; 1990(1) SCALE 746; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 366; 1990 (2) SLR 742; 1990 (1) UJ 731

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Births, Deaths and Marriage registration Act 1886, AP Public Employment (Recording and Alteration of Date of Birth) Rules 1984

Summary: Service - Births, Deaths and Marriage Registration Act, 1886, s. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Recording and Alteration of Date of Birth) Rules, 1984, rr. 4 and 5 - (A) Whether alteration of respondent's date of birth was permissible after enforcement of 1984 Rules when respondent's earlier application has already been rejected before enforcement of 1984 Rules? - R. 4 laid down a solitary principle to prohibit reopening of question of correction of date of birth which may have become final prior to enforcement of 1984 Rules - Held, since question of alteration of respondent's date of birth had been made on basis of school certificate and his application for alteration had already been rejected in 1968, he was not entitled to claim alteration of his date of birth after enforcement of 1984 Rules - (B) Whether, r. 5 was repugnant to s. 9 of Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, 1886? - In substance, r. 5 lays down that pending applications of employees for alteration of their date of birth shall be decided on basis of age as recorded in school and college records; however, if date of birth recorded in service book at time of entry of an employee is not based on school or college records, r. 5 does not operate as a bar to consideration of other relevant materials in determining date of birth of employee - Question of repugnancy cannot arise if State makes law in exercise of its legislative powers in respect of an entry specified in List II of Seventh Schedule, even though it may incidentally trench upon a law made by Union in respect of a matter referable to an entry in Union List of Seventh Schedule - Held, Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, 1886 is a central law, referable to Union List and s. 9 merely related to admissibility of documents, it does not seek to regulate conditions of service of a State employee - Further held, r. 5 and s. 9 operate in different areas and there is no question of conflict in two provisions - Appeal allowed.
