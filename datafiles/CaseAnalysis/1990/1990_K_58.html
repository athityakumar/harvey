Suryakumar Govindjee v Krishnammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 817; (1990) 4 SCC 343; JT 1990 (3) SC 343; 1991 (1) MLJ(SC) 21; 1990 (1) RCJ 707; 1990 (2) RCR(Rent) 230; 1990 (2) RentLR 334; 1990 SCALE 77; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 782; 1990 (2) UJ 259

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 2(2), 10(2)(vii), 10(3)(a)(i) and 14(1)(b) - Madras City Tenants Protection Act, 1922 - (A) Whether demised premises constitute a 'building' within meaning of s. 2(2) of Rent Control Act? - Held, since Act applies to residential and non residential building alike expression hut cannot to be restricted only to huts or cottages intended to be lived in; it will also take in any shed hut or other curd or third class construction consisting of an enclosure made of mud or by poles supporting a tin or asbestos roof that can be put to use for any purpose residential or non residential in same manner as any other first class construction, hence, Kaichalai is a structure which falls within purview of definition in s. 2(2) - (B) Whether lease in present case within scope of Rent Control Act? - Kaichalai was a material structure let out as such to lease for non residential purposes and which with necessary permission could also have been used for residential purposes; what lessor intended was a lease of both land and building - Held, this was a composite lease with a composite purpose and it is difficult to break up integrity of lease as one of land alone or or building alone, therefore, this letting would come in within scope of Rent Control Act - (C) What does word 'separately' in s. 2(2) connotes? - Held, word is intended to emphasis that for purposes of Act a building means any unit comprising whole or part of a building that is separately let out and it does not mean that composite leases of land and building would not be covered by it; further 'appurtenant' land is not just restricted to land which, on a consideration of circumstances, a court may consider necessary or imperative for its enjoyment and it should be construed as comprehending land which parties considered appropriate to let along with building - (D) Whether applicability of s. 2(2) is restricted to cases of letting of building and appurtenant lands only? - Held, where a person leases a building together with land, it seems impermissible in absence of clear intention spelt out in deed, to dissect lease as (a) of building and appurtenant land covered by Rent Control Act and (b) of land alone governed by other relevant statutory provisions - Appeal dismissed.
