P.N. Veeti Narayani v Pathumma Beevi And Another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 439; (1990) 4 SCC 672; AIR 1991 SC 720; 1992 (20) ALR 673; 1991 (2) BC 328; 1990 (3) CCC 368; JT 1991 (5) SC 420; 1990(2) SCALE 481; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 433; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Executor, Substantive Law, Debt Laws

Summary: Family & Personal - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 18, 19 and 20 - Heirs of Muslim under Mohammedan law, who died intestate, has individual liability as to debts - Acknowledgment by one heir - Whether fact that he was in possession of estate, makes him liable for entire debt? - Held, would be confined to himself and cannot be extended to other co-heirs - He would not be liable for entire debt - Appeal dismissed.
