Indira Bai v Nand Kishore

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 240; (1990) 4 SCC 668; AIR 1991 SC 1055; 1990 (3) CCC 308; JT 1990 (4) SC 163; 1990(2) SCALE 445; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 349; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Keywords: Rajasthan Pre-emption Act, 1966

Summary: Civil Procedure - Preemption - Estoppel - Rajasthan Pre-emption Act, 1966, s. 9 - Respondent exercised his right of pre-emption after appellant had fulfilled all procedural formalities to convey his property - Whether respondent can claim his right of pre-emption? - Right of pre-emption is a weak right and it can be defeated by estoppel - Even in Muslim law which is genesis of this right, as it was unknown to Hindu law and was brought in wake of Mohammedan rule, it is settled that right of pre-emption is lost by estoppel and acquiescence - Estoppel is a rule of equity flowing out of fairness striking on behaviour deficient in good faith - It operates as a check on spurious conduct by preventing inducer from taking advantage and assailing forfeiture already accomplished - It is invoked and applied to aid law in administration of justice - But for it great many injustice may have been perpetrated - Held, no - Conduct of respondent does not entitle him to claim pre-emption - He himself assisted appellant in conducting conveyance of property - Respondent precluded from basing his claim on pre-emption - Appeal allowed.
