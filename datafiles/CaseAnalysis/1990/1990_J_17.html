G. B. Mahajan and Others v Jalgaon Municipal Council and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 438; (1991) 3 SCC 91; AIR 1991 SC 1153; JT 1993 (3) SC 605; 1991(1) SCALE 378; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Maharashtra Municipalities Act, 1965, ss. 272(1) and 92 - Project for development of real estate sponsored by Town Municipal Council, Jalgaon - Appellants who entertained serious misgiving as to legal permissibility, economic soundness and propriety of policy of this venture petitioned to Collector of district under provisions of Maharashtra Municipalities Act, (MM Act), 1965 to suspend resolutions of Municipal Council undertaking project and that agreement for its execution - (A) Whether mode of financing of project is unconventional and does not accord with any recognised or accepted norms of functioning and financial discipline of governmental bodies? - Held, a project, otherwise legal, does not become any less permissible by reason along that local authority, instead of executing project itself, had entered into an agreement with a developer for its financing and execution - Question is not whether it is unconventional by standard of extant practices, but whether there was something in law rendering it impermissible - Essentially matters of economic policy which lack adjudicative disposition, unless they violate constitutional or legal limits on power or have demonstrable pejorative environmental implications or amount to clear abuse of power - In context of expanding exigencies of urban planning it will be difficult for court to say that a particular policy option was better than another - Held, that project is not ultra vires of powers of Municipal Council - (B) Whether present transaction amounts to lease? - S. 272(1) were intended to and did meet a special situation pertaining to municipal markets while s. 92 dealt with transfer or lease of other properties - Possible to fit power exercised by municipal authority into s. 272(1), MM Act - Appellants have not been able to establish that essential elements of transaction are such that s. 92 of Act is violated - Held, it would be unduly restrictive of statutory powers of local authority if a provision enabling establishment of markets and disposal of occupancy rights therein are hedged in by restrictions not found in statute - (C) Whether resolutions of Municipal Council touching approval of project and its execution are vitiated by 'unreasonableness' and 'arbitrariness'? - Held, different contexts in which operative of 'reasonableness' as test of validity operates must be kept distinguished - 'Reasonableness' in administrative law must, therefore, distinguish between proper use and improper abuse of power - While it is true that principles of judicial review apply to exercise by a Govt. body of its contractual powers, inherent imitations on scope of inquiry are themselves a part of those principles - Further held, material placed before court falls far short of what law requires to justify interference - Charge of arbitrariness cannot be upheld - Appeals dismissed.
