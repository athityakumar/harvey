A.M. Mathur v Pramod Kumar Gupta

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 763; (1990) 2 SCC 533; AIR 1990 SC 1737; JT 1990 (1) SC 545; 1990(1) SCALE 527; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 110; 1990 (1) UJ 595

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 33, 114 and 151, O 20, r. 1 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 353, 354 and 482 - Expunging of remarks - Strictures against Advocate General - Propriety - Review petition dismissed not on merits but for want of jurisdiction - Observation therein that Advocate General obtained judgment in earlier case by fraud - Whether liable to be expunged - Held, judicial restraint and discipline necessary for administration of justice - Observation, unjustified and unwarranted and liable to be expunged - Appeal allowed.
