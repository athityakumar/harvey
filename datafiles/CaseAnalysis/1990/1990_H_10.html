Sudhoo v Hajilal Mohd. Biri Works and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 262; (1990) 4 SCC 37; (1990) SCC (L&S) 563; AIR 1990 SC 1971; 1990 (61) FLR 419; JT 1990 (3) SC 399; 1990 LabIC 1538; 1990 (2) LLJ 598; 1990 (2) LLN 645; 1990(2) SCALE 178; 1990 (3) SLR 2; 1990 (2) UJ 504; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 1343

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Beedi And Cigar Workers (Conditions of Employment) Act, 1966, s. 31(2) - Termination of service of workman - Appeal filed u/s. 31(2) alleging his termination without notice - Authority directing employer to pay arrears and compensation - (A) Interference of High Court with order in writ petition - Employee prevented from doing work by employer - (B) Whether appeal is maintainable u/s. 31(2) before prescribed authority against such action? - (C) Whether order in writing by employer is not necessary - Held, (A) Findings of prescribed authority were based on evidence and HC was wrong in interfering with those findings? - (B) Appeal is maintainable - (C) Not necessary - Appeal allowed.
