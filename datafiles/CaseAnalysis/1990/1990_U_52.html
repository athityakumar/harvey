S. Harcharan Sinoh v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Ranganath Misra, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 1011; (1990) 4 SCC 647; AIR 1991 SC 945; 1990 (2) ARBLR 243; 1990 (3) CCC 201; JT 1990 (3) SC 692; 1990(2) SCALE 413; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 76; 1990 (2) ShimLC 336; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 76; 1990 (2) UJ 673

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Award - Setting aside of - Arbitration agreement or deed of submission not requiring arbitrator to give reasons -Whether arbitral award could be questioned on basis of error on face of it - Contractor claiming payment at enhanced rate for additional work required to be done by him - Held, award cannot be questioned on ground of error on face of award, as it contained no reasons - Arbitrator not accepting claim in full but partly allowing said claim.
