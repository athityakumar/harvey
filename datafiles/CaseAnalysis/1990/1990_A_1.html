Malayammal And Ors. v A Malayalam Pillai And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, N.D. Ojha, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 963; (1991) Supp2 SCC 579; 1990 (3) CCC 689; JT 1990 (4) SC 321; 1990(2) SCALE 740; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 235; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 235

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Endowment - Validity -Permanent dedication of properties for performance of annual ceremonies of testator is equally valid - Annual shradha or anniversary is certainly a religious rite - It is common among Hindu testators to make provisions in their wills for celebration or performance of such anniversaries of themselves or their ancestors - Held, trust in respect of properties for construction of samadhi with raising flower garden and lighting up would not be valid as it is not recognised under Hindu law - Neither plaintiffs nor defendants will be entitled to it under terms of will - Further held, endowment and directions as to application of property for construction of matam and performance of ceremonies and pooja would be valid since they are religious and charitable in nature - Appeal allowed.
