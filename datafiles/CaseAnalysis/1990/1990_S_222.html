Shambhoo Missir and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 348; (1990) 4 SCC 17; (1990) SCC (Cr) 518; AIR 1991 SC 315; 1990 (2) Crimes 749; 1991 CRLJ 395; 1990 CRLR 506; JT 1990 (3) SC 290; 1990(2) SCALE 118; 1990 (2) UJ 579

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 386 - Appeal against acquittal - High Court failed to deal with important aspect of evidence highlighted by trial Court and strengthening defence version that accused had been involved by obliging witnesses and unfair investigation - Court also not dealt with other circumstances relied on by Trial Court to negative prosecution case - No reasons have been given to negative them or show them to be improper, unjustified or unreasonable - Whether order of HC interfering with order of acquittal liable to be set aside? - Held, order of HC was liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
