Nityananda Kar and Another v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Ramaswamy, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 431; (1991) Supp2 SCC 516; AIR 1991 SC 1134; 1992 (19) ATC 236; 1991 (62) FLR 145; JT 1990 (4) SC 418; 1991 LabIC 782; 1990 (2) LLN 1086; 1990(2) SCALE 976; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 644; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 644

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32, 311 and 309, Proviso - Orissa Administrative Service Class II (Appointment by Promotion, Transfer and Selection) Regulations, 1959, Regn. 1 - Orissa Administrative Service Class II (Appointment of Officers Validation) Act, 1987, s. 3(2)(a) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Precedents - Administrative service - Seniority - Challenge as to year of allotment - Earlier decision of High Court upholding year of allotment - Decision becoming final - Held, as decision rendered after careful consideration - Cannot be challenged in Supreme Court by way of civil appeal or writ petition - Validation Act permitting deemed promotes to be placed above direct recruits of same year in gradation list - Is violative of art. 14, as no reasonable classification exists.
