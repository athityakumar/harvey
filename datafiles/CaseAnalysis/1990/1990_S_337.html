Vashisht Narain Karwaria v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 41; (1990) SCC (Cr) 372; (1990) 2 SCC 629; AIR 1990 SC 1272; 1990 ALJ 296; 1990 (1) AWC 558; 1990 (2) Crimes 175; 1990 CRLJ 1311; 1990 CrLR(SC) 302; JT 1990 (1) SC 566; 1990(1) SCALE 604; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 212

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss. 307 and 506 - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3 and 5 - Habeas Corpus - Appellant detained for preventing him from acting prejudicial to public order - Writ petition of appellant dismissed by High Court - Hence, present appeal - Held, irrelevant and extraneous material put before detaining authority while detaining appellant - Appellant denied of his right of effective representation - Detention order is suffering from vice of consideration of extraneous materials vitiating validity of order - Detention order set aside - Appeal allowed.
