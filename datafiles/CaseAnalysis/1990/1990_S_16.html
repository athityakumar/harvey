Ayub Alias Pappurkhan Nawabkhan Pathan v S. N. Sinha and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 382; (1990) 4 SCC 552; (1990) SCC (Cr) 664; AIR 1990 SC 2069; 1990 CRLJ 2232; 1990 CrLR(SC) 605; JT 1990 (3) SC 530; 1990(2) SCALE 273; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 927

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-Social Activities Act, 1985, s. 3(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 307 and 451 - Detenue was detained under above Act - Detenu and his associates armed with deadly weapons committed offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 307 and 451 of IPC, 1860 - Whether detention of appellant was justified? - Held, no - Non-application of mind by detaining authority - Appellant not a habitual offender or dangerous person - Solitary incident not sufficient to conclude that detenu was habitually committing or attempting to commit or abetting commission of offences - Appeal allowed.
