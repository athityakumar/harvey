M. Mohamed Sulthan v Joint Secretary To Government of India, Finance Department and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 465; (1991) SCC (Cr) 104; (1991) 1 SCC 144; AIR 1990 SC 2222; 1990 (3) Crimes 450; 1990 CRLJ 2473; 1990 CRLR 691; 1990 (31) ECR 187; JT 1990 (4) SC 41; 1990(2) SCALE 629

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments, Past Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3 and 3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Preventive detention - (A) Subjective satisfaction - (1) Material documents - Detaining authority considering order granting bail subject to conditions - Application for relaxation of conditions, not considered - Non-supply of copies thereof - Effect - (2) Solitary incident - Smuggling of gold - Detenu preparing himself for it by obtaining passport in false name - Acquiring skill to conceal large quantity of gold in his body - Inference - (C) Excluding time taken in communicating only six days taken - Delay in consideration of representation, whether - Held, (A) Not material documents, so would not deprive detenu of his right of making representation - (B) Detenu is likely to indulge in smuggling of goods in future - (C) Same cannot be regarded as unduly long - Petition dismissed.
