Amulya Chandra Kalita v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 656; (1991) SCC (L&S) 145; (1991) 1 SCC 181; 1990 (16) ALR 487; 1990 (14) ATC 911; 1990 (1) CalLT(SC) 47; 1990 (1) CLR 668; 1990 (60) FLR 719; JT 1990 (1) SC 558; 1990 (1) LLJ 523; 1990 (2) LLN 1; 1991 (3) SLR 58

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Administrative - Advocates & Judges - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985,ss. 5(1)and 5(2) - Whether Administrative Member of Central Administrative Tribunal could alone decide case in face of Supreme Court decision in S P Sampath Kumar v Union of India 1986 Indlaw SC 193? - Held, Parliament enacted Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, providing for adjudication of such disputes and complaints through Tribunals established u/s. 4 of said Act - S. 5(1) sets out constitution of Tribunal and adds that 'the jurisdiction, powers and authority of Tribunal may be exercised by benches thereof' - S. 5(2) provides that 'a bench shall consist of one Judicial Member and Administrative Member' - No provision to contrary is shown - It is, therefore, statutorily recognised that every bench of Tribunal must consist of a Judicial Member and an administrative Member - It is, therefore, obvious that Administrative Member alone could not have heard and decided matter - Appeal disposed of.
