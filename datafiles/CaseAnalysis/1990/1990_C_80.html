Keshavji Ravji And Co. Etc. Etc. v Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 825; (1990) 2 SCC 231; (1990) SCC (Tax) 268; AIR 1991 SC 1806; AIR 1991 SCW 1845; 1990 (1) CompLJ 374; 1990 (82) CTR 123; [1990] 183 ITR 1; JT 1990 (1) SC 235; 1990(1) SCALE 207; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 243; [1990] 49 TAXMAN 87; 1991 (2) TLR 669

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Income & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 40(b) - Income Tax - Firm - (A) Interest paid to partner - Disallowance of interest paid by partner to firm had to be deducted - (B) Interpretation of taxing statutes - (1) Legislative intent - Resort to interpretative process in order to unfold it - When allowed - (2) Not intrusion into right of private property - Object of - (3) Explanation added to statutory provision - Determination of purpose and intentions - (C) Whether Explanation to s. 40(b) introduced in 1984 is prospective or explains position already existing - Held, (A) Only net amount is liable to disallowance - (B) Not permissible when language of statute is unambiguous - Object is equitable distribution of burden of community to sustain social services - Are determined by its own words - (C) Notes on clauses in Taxation Laws (Amendment) Bill, 1984 show prospective effect - Express prospective operation and effectuation of 'Explanation' might, perhaps, be factor necessarily detracting - However, Supreme Court felt it unnecessary to examine this question any further - Appeal allowed.
