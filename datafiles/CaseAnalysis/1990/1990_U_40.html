Dharmendra Suganchand Chelawat & Suganchand Kanhaiyyalal v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.C. Ray, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 73; (1990) SCC (Cr) 249; (1990) 1 SCC 746; AIR 1990 SC 1196; 1990 (1) Crimes 634; 1990 CRLJ 1232; 1990 CrLR(SC) 214; 1990 (28) ECC 12; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 181; JT 1990 (1) SC 184; 1990(1) SCALE 146; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Prevention of Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1988, s. 3 - Narcotic Drugs, Psychotropic substances Act, 1985 - In what circumstances an order for preventive detention may be passed against a person who is already in custody? - Held, order for detention can be validly passed against a person in custody subject to laid down grounds - Grounds of detention show detaining authority aware of appellants in custody on date of passing of order of detention - Order for detention not sustainable - Set aside.
