Hundraj Kanyalal Sajnani Etc. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.C. Ray, K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 747; (1990) Supp SCC 577; AIR 1990 SC 1106; 1991 (15) ATC 771; JT 1990 (2) SC 145; 1990 LabIC 890; 1990(1) SCALE 492; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 994; 1991 (2) SLJ 174; 1990 (2) SLR 400; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 571

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Service - Constitution - Income Tax Officers (Class I) Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1961, ss. 116, 117 and 118 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14, 16, 39(d), 303 and 311 - Income Tax Officers (Class I) Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1973, r. 3 - Income Tax Officer - (A) Recruitment - Rules framed as per Supreme Court's directions in earlier case conclusion on basis of material before it that quota for recruitment of direct recruits and promotees had broken down - Instant petition alleging that contrary material showing that promotees were short of their quota not produced by Govt. - However, material produced by petitioners not supporting allegation - (B) Classification in different grades - Constitutionality - Held, (A) Seniority Rules of 1973 were validly framed, legislature was competent both under unamended and amended (1987) Provisions - (B) Distinction is based on statute itself - Appeal dismissed.
