Indian Metals and Ferro Alloys Limited, Cuttack v Collector of Central Excise, Bhubaneshwar

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 583; (1991) Supp1 SCC 125; AIR 1991 SC 1028; 1990 (26) ECC 324; 1991 (33) ECR 11; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 165; JT 1990 (4) SC 763; 1990(2) SCALE 1109; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 329; 1991 (1) UJ 302

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, ss. 3, chapter 1, item, 26 AA and 68 - Pipes and tubes - Residuary Item - Poles manufactured out of iron pipes and tubes by processes of forging, drawing etc - Whether cease to be pipes and tubes ? - Unless department can establish that goods in question can, by no conceivable process of reasoning, be brought under any of specific items mentioned in tariff, resort cannot be had to residuary item - It failed to satisfy this condition - Held, do not cease to be pipes and tubes - Poles fall under item 26 AA - Appeal allowed.
