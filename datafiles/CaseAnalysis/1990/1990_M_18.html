M.B. Majumdar v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.M. Punchhi, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 390; (1990) 4 SCC 501; (1991) SCC (L&S) 233; AIR 1990 SC 2263; 1990 (14) ATC 804; JT 1990 (3) SC 655; 1991 (2) LLJ 585; 1991 (1) LLN 30; 1990(2) SCALE 345; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 946; 1991 (2) SLJ 79; 1990 (5) SLR 1; 1990 (2) UJ 709; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: High Court Judges, Central Administrative Tribunal (Salaries and Allowances and Conditions of Service of Chairman, Vice-chairman and Members) Rules 1985

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, ss. 4, 8 and 10 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 14, 39(d), 32 and 226 - Central Administrative Tribunal (Salaries and Allowances and Conditions of Service of Chairman, Vice-Chairman and Members) Rules, 1985, r. 3 - (A) Administrative Tribunals - Not to be equated with High Court in all respects - Not deemed HC - Members of Tribunal, whether entitled to claim equality with HC Judges or Vice-chairman of Tribunal as regards pay and age of superannuation? - (B) Writ Petition - Relief - Direction to amend provisions of Act or Rules - If can be granted - Held, (A) Cannot claim - (B) Cannot be granted - Petition dismissed.
