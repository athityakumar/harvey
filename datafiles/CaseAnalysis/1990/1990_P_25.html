Periyar And Pareekanni Rubbers Limited v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 989; (1991) 4 SCC 195; AIR 1990 SC 2192; JT 1991 (1) SC 450; 1990(2) SCALE 525; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 362; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Travancore Land Acquisition Regulation, 1089, s. 25(3) -(A) Whether High Court is justified in reversing awards and decrees of Civil Court? - Held, in determining market value court has to eschew arbitrary fixation keeping in view settled principles of law in evaluating market value in compulsory acquisition on hypothesis of a willing vendor and a willing vendee - Underlying principle to fix a fair market value with reference to comparable sale is to reduce element of speculation - In a comparable sale features are : (1) it must be within a reasonable time of date of notification, (2) it should be a bona fide transaction; (3) it should be a sale of land acquired or land adjacent to land acquired and (4) it should possess similar advantages - Cumulative effect of all facts and circumstances should be taken into consideration in arriving at a reasonable and fair market value - It is well settled law that amount awarded by Land Acquisition Collector forms an offer and that it is for claimants to adduce relevant and material evidence to establish that acquired lands are capable of fetching higher market value and amount offered by Land Acquisition Collector was inadequate and he proceeded on a wrong premise or principle - Ex. P 10 also would not form a base to fix market value - It is undoubtedly true that HC did not accept evidence adduced by State - Judgment of HC is confirmed - (B) Whether claimants are entitled to severance charges and injurious effects on remaining lands of claimant? - Held, HC has thoroughly considered evidence of all witnesses and reports of Commrs. - HC is well justified in arriving at finding that appellant has not expended any money for either constructing any boundary walls, culverts, bridges or roads etc. - Value of lands of appellant has not been injuriously affected due to acquisition - No damage due to severance was caused - Further held, Under these circumstances appellant is not entitled to compensation in this regard - (C) Whether appellant is entitled to payment of interest on solatium? - Held, s. 25(3) contemplates payment of interests on solatium to recompensate owner of land for loss of user of land from date of taking possession till date of payment into court - Word compensation has been advisedly used by legislature - Appellant is entitled to interest on solatium - Appeal allowed.
