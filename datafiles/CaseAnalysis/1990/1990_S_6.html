Patasibai and Others v Ratanlal

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 676; (1990) 2 SCC 42; 1990 (1) CCC 445; JT 1990 (3) SC 68; 1990(1) SCALE 279; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11, O. 23 r. 3 A - Amendment of Plaint - Triable Issue - Cause of Action - Rejection of Plaint - (A) Whether suit barred by O. 23 r. 3 A and res judicata? - Held, suit did not raise a triable issue nor disclosed any cause of action - Suit not maintainable - No necessity to go into issue of bar of suit raised u/r. 3 A of O. 23 - (B) Whether misdescription of names has mad the consent decree null? - Held, no - Names later on corrected with permission of court and decree was passed against existing entity - Respondents has not disputed validity of consent decree on ground of fraud, coercion or misrepresentation - Respondents failed to show that averments in plaint disclose a cause of action giving rise to a triable issue - Hence, plaint liable to be rejected u/O. 7 r. 11(a) - (C) Whether amendments to plaint allowed after adjudication ending with dismissal of Second Appeal? - Further held, amendment of plaint sought on earlier grounds already adjudicated and has become final could not be allowed - Appeal allowed.
