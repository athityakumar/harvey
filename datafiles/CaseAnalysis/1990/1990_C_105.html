Mahabir Cold Storage v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 311; (1991) Supp1 SCC 402; AIR 1991 SC 1357; 1991 (91) CTR 89; [1991] 188 ITR 91; JT 1990 (4) SC 754; 1990(2) SCALE 1226; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 469; [1991] 56 TAXMAN 42

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Assessable Entity, Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Limitation Act, 1963 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 33 - Firm consisting of two partners carrying on business at two places - New firm constituted with third partner carrying on business at one of two places of business - Machinery installed by old firm at place where newly constituted firm carried on business - Development rebate - Held, that assessee new firm was not entitled to development rebate u/s. 33(1) - There was no reconstitution of original firm - Thus it is clear that assessee new firm was a new identity under Act - Appeal dismissed.
