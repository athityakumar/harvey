Veerattalingam And Others v Ramesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 452; (1991) 1 SCC 489; AIR 1990 SC 2201; JT 1991 (5) SC 425; 1991 (1) MLJ(SC) 58; 1992 RD 184; 1990(2) SCALE 550; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 507; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Suit for Partition

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for partition - Maintainability - Plaintiffs filed suit for partition- Suit was contest by defendants - Trial court rejected the plea based on the rule against perpetuity - Appeal before HC - HC confirmed the findings of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was maintainable.

Held, there is no manner of doubt, and it is not denied by any party that neither the sons nor the grand-sons of the testatrix got any life estate in the properties. It is the agreed case of the parties that as soon as plaintiffs and defendants no. 5 to 14 become major they are entitled to get the property absolutely without waiting for the death of their respective fathers or grand-fathers. Therefore, it is interpreted that the will without being influenced by the meaning given to the will in the reported case. The devolution of the property under the will takes place on the plaintiffs and defendants No. 5 to 14 for the first time 'under equal shares'. Admittedly, plaintiff no.3 has also attained majority during the pendency of the instant appeal and has, therefore, become entitled to a share in the properties now. Thus, suit is decreed in favour of plaintiffs, their share being one thirteenth each. The plea that the disposition under the will was hit by the rule against perpetuity was rejected by Trial Court on the ground that sons of the testatrix, namely, the first defendant and plaintiff's witness no. 2 as also their respective sons, defendants no. 2 to 4 are alive. Thus, view of Trial Court appears to be correct, and does not require reconsideration at this stage. Appeal party allowed.
