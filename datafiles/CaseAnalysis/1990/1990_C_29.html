State of Maharashtra and another v Chandraprakash Kewal Chand Jain

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 661; (1990) SCC (Cr) 210; (1990) 1 SCC 550; AIR 1990 SC 658; 1990 (1) AWC 325; 1990 (2) Bom.C.R. 630; 1990 (1) Crimes 724; 1990 CRLJ 889; 1990 CrLR(SC) 328; JT 1990 (1) SC 61; 1990(1) SCALE 33; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 115

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Degree Of Care

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113, 114, illu.(b) and 118 - Rape - Accused a Police Officer - Evidence of prosecutrix - Corroboration, not necessary - Standard of proof to be expected by Court - Sentence - Police Officer committed rape on a young girl in her late teens - No room for sympathy or pity - Punishment must in such cases be exemplary - Whether sentence of 5 yrs Rigorous Imprisonment and fine of Rs. 1000/- Was proper - Held, prosecutrix of sex-offence, competent witness u/s. 118 of Evidence Act - Degree of care in evaluating her evidence same as an injured complainant - Court alive to fact that she is interested in outcome of charged, is entitled to base a conviction on her evidence - Very rarely direct evidence of a person other than prosecutrix is available - Punishment, held proper - Appeal allowed.
