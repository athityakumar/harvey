Shainda Hasan v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 811; (1990) 3 SCC 48; (1990) SCC (L&S) 432; AIR 1990 SC 1381; 1990 (13) ATC 577; 1990 (1) AWC 695; 1990 (60) FLR 877; JT 1990 (2) SC 178; 1990 LabIC 1227; 1990 (2) LLJ 603; 1990 (2) LLN 268; 1990(1) SCALE 815; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 699; 1990 (2) SLJ 93; 1990 (3) SLR 10B; 1990 (1) UJ 746; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 750; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2058

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Education - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 30, 30(1), 136 and 226 - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 31(11) - Religious minority institution - Appointment of Principal - Relaxed the qualification - University declined to approve - Challenged the decision of the University - High Court rejected - (A) Whether Selection Committee was not justified in relaxing the qualification without reserving that right to itself in the advertisement? - Court held, HC has rightly held the relaxation granted by the Selection Committee to be arbitrary - In absence of statutory rules providing power or relaxation, the advertisement must indicate that the Selection Committee/appointing authority has the power to relax the qualifications - (B) Whether qualification 'working knowledge of Urdu' was unjust? - Held, 'working knowledge of Urdu' not agree with HC that the said qualification is unjust - Further held, college being a Muslim minority institution prescribing the said qualification for the post of Principal, is in conformity with the object of establishing the institution - Appeal disposed of.
