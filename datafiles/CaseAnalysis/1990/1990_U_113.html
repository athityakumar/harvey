 Shri Sitaram Sugar Company Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty, K.N. Saikia, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 740; (1990) 3 SCC 223; AIR 1990 SC 1277; 1990 (2) CompLJ 18; JT 1990 (1) SC 462; 1990(1) SCALE 475; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Quantity, Fixing Prices

Summary: Corporate - Socio-Economic - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3(3C) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Prices of levy sugar - Fixation of prices by Central Government - (A) Economic policy - Judicial review - Grouping of sugar factories on basis of their geographical location - (B) Orders duly published in official gazette - If legislative - (C) Legislative action - Rule of natural justice - Applicability - (D) Expression 'having regard to' u/s. (3 C) - If mandatory - Held, (A) Is a policy decision based on exhaustive expert conclusions and do not attract power of judicial review - (B) Can be characterized as legislative - (C) Not applicable to such order - (D) Not strictly mandatory - Petition dismissed.
