State of Tamil Nadu and Others v Nellai Cotton Mills Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 756; (1990) SCC (L&S) 334; (1990) 2 SCC 518; 1990 (1) CLR 640; 1990 (2) CompLJ 174; JT 1990 (2) SC 19; 1991 (1) LLJ 35; 1990 (1) LLN 685; 1990 (2) MLJ(SC) 5; 1990(1) SCALE 633; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 33; 1990 (2) SLR 794

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Tamil Nadu Industrial Establishment (Conferment of Permanent Status to Workmen) Act, 1981, s. 3 - Amending Act 44 of 1985 - (A) Whether legislature by amending principal Act with retrospective effect and also validating acts done and proceedings taken under principal Act, has accepted High Court's judgment holding portion in sub-s. (2) of s. 3 as unconstitutional? - After statute has been judicially interpreted in a certain way and if legislature by taking note of judgment amended statute appropriately so as to give it a different meaning from one asserted by courts, or not giving any different meaning from view taken by court, it may be argued with some justification that legislature has expressly or by implication ratified judicial interpretation - Held, in instant case, legislature has expressly taken note of HC verdict and removed practical difficulties caused thereby in implementing provisions of Act, by appropriate amendments and no provision was inserted to rewrite and validate portion which was struck down by HC, therefore, legislature has accepted judgment of HC to extent indicated - (B) Whether HC rightly held part of s. 3 of Act as constitutionally invalid? - Held, HC rightly held that word 'non-employment' in s. 3 would include retrenchment as well and a person whose services have been terminated or discharged albeit illegal cannot at all be said to be a person in service, much less in continuous service, therefore, period of non-employment or period after discharge cannot be counted for purpose of giving continuity of service; if discharge is set aside and workman is reinstated by process known to law workman automatically gets continuity of service and no special provision is necessary for such purposes - Appeal dismissed.
