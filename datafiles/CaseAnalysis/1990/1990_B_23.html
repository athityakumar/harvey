State of Madhya Pradesh v Bani Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 57; (1990) Supp SCC 738; AIR 1990 SC 1308; 1990 CRLJ 1315; JT 1990 (2) SC 54; 1990 (2) LLJ 529; 1990 (1) LLN 780; 1990 (2) SLR 798

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, ss. 14 and 19 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311 - (A) Disciplinary proceedings - Delay and laches - Department aware of involvement of officer in alleged irregularities - Inordinate delay in issuing charge memo, not properly explained - Proceedings initiated after 12 years - Maintainability - (B) Promotion - Deferring consideration - By Screening Committee - Ground, complaints about integrity of officer - Legality - (C) Powers of tribunal - Promotion - Denial of - Tribunal giving relief of retrospective promotion on overall consideration of entire facts and circumstances of case - (D) Application u/s. 19 - Representations to Govt. - Delay in filing - Dismissal - Justification - Held, (A) Liable to be quashed - (B) Illegal - (C) No interference - Not a ground for dismissal of application - Appeals dismissed.
