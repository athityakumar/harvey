Income Tax Officer, Callcut v Smt. N.K. Sarada Thampatty

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, K. Jagannatha Shetty, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 441; (1991) 2 SCC 737; AIR 1991 SC 2035; 1990 (89) CTR 154; 1991 (2) HLR 673; [1991] 187 ITR 696; JT 1990 (4) SC 358; 1990(2) SCALE 701; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 473; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 473; [1990] 53 TAXMAN 78; 1991 (2) TLR 755

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 171 - Hindu Undivided Family - Partition - Preliminary decree for partition - Under Hindu Law members of a joint family may agree to partition of joint family property by private settlement, agreement, arbitration or through Court's decree - Held, members of family may also agree to share income from property according to their respective share - In all such eventualities joint status of family may be disrupted but such disruption of family status is not recognised by legislature for purposes of Income tax - Appeal allowed.
