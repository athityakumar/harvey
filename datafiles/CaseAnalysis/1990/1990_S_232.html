S. D. Soni v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 637; (1992) SCC (Cr) 331; (1992) Supp1 SCC 567; AIR 1991 SC 917; 1991 (2) Crimes 4; 1991 CRLJ 330; 1991 CrLR(SC) 114; 1993 (1) DMC 212; 1991 (1) HLR 472; JT 1991 (1) SC 1; 1990(2) SCALE 1342; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Parents, Poisoning, Vomiting, Poison

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 302, 304 Part II, 196, 498 A - Murder - Deceased was wife of appellant - Appellant convicted for murder by Trial Court but High Court altered conviction to u/s. 304 Part II - Present appeal - Injuries found on body of deceased - Circumstantial evidence - Whether deceased committed suicide or was murdered by appellant husband? - Held, defence of suicide as pleaded by appellant completely false and concocted - Appellant may not have intention to cause death but his act is done with knowledge that it can cause death - HC view correct, offence falls u/s. 304 Part II - Appeals dismissed.
