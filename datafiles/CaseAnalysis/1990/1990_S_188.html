Ashok Kumar v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 545; (1991) SCC (Cr) 126; (1991) 1 SCC 166; AIR 1990 SC 2134; 1990 (2) AWC 1551; 1991 (1) Crimes 116; 1990 CRLJ 2276; 1990 CRLR 633; 1990 CrLR(SC) 633; JT 1990 (4) SC 149; 1990(2) SCALE 464; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 401; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 378, 386(1) and 300 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - (A) Appeal against acquittal - Interference on ground of mere probability or possibility - Perversity or misreading of evidence involved - (B) Dowry death - Inference of - Smell of kerosene coming from body of deceased - (C) Dowry killing - Remedy - (D) Dying declaration - Death by burning - Entries in injury report construed as dying declaration - Entry in bed head ticket that deceased complained of misbehaviour by brother in law - Corroborated by entry in injury report - By identifying accused as brother- Authenticity - Held, (A) Interference is imperative - (B) It is case of murder and possibility of accident ruled out - (C) Social ostracism of boy afterwards in matter of marriage suggested - (D) Authenticity was not open to challenge - Appeal dismissed.
