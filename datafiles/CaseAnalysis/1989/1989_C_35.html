Bhor Industries Limited, Bombay v Collector Of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 753; (1989) SCC (Tax) 98; (1989) 1 SCC 602; AIR 1989 SC 1153; 1989 CRLR 351; 1989 CrLR(SC) 351; 1989 (21) ECR 273; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 280; [1990] 184 ITR 129; JT 1989 (1) SC 450; 1989(1) SCALE 226; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 382; 1989 (73) STC 145; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 92; 1989 (1) UJ 503

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Leather, Kiln Gas

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 2(d), 2(f) and 3 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10A and 173 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Excisability - Crude PVC films - Appellant was a manufacturer of Crude PVC films for the purpose of use in final products such as leather cloth and laminated jute mattings and PVC tapes both insulation and adhesive - Appellant filed a classification list in respect of impugned product used for lamination with jute and for tapes claiming that the said PVC films were non-excisable on the ground that the same were non-marketable intermediate products used exclusively for captive consumption - Said classification was approved by the Asst. Collector - Subsequently, Asst. Collector issued show cause notice to the assessee to show cause as to why the impugned products should not be re-classified under item no. 15A(2) of Tariff Schedule and appropriate duty not recovered u/r. 10 of 1944 Rules - Asst. Collector confirmed the show-cause notice and also confirmed the demand - Appellate Collector dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal also confirmed the order of the Appellate Collector and held that the impugned goods fell under Tariff Item No. 15A(2) of Tariff and were dutiable in the intermediate list - Hence, present appeal - Whether crude PVC film is dutiable? - Held, simply because a certain article falls within the Schedule it would not be dutiable under excise law if the said article is not 'goods' known to the market - Marketability, therefore, is an essential ingredient in order to be dutiable under the Schedule to the Central Tariff Act, 1985 - In the instant case, the Crude PVC films as produced by the appellant were not known in the market and could not be sold in the market and were therefore, not capable of being marketable - Appeal allowed.
