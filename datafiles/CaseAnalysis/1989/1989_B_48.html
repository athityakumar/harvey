 I.J. Rao, Assistant Collector Of Customs And Ors. v Bibhuti Bhushan Bagh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah, M.H. Kania, M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 715; (1989) 3 SCC 202; (1989) SCC (Cr) 528; AIR 1989 SC 1884; 1989 CRLR 548; 1989 CrLR(SC) 548; 1989 (24) ECR 1; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 338; JT 1989 (2) SC 531; 1989(1) SCALE 1431; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 110(2), Proviso - Custom Duty - Confiscation of goods - Proposal for extension of time fixed for completion of investigation - Notice must be issued to person from whom goods are seized before expiry of six months - Service of notice before expiry of period becoming impossible - Held, Collector can grant extension and afford post decisional hearing - Appeal allowed.
