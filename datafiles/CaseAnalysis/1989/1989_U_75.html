Builders' Association of India and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 557; (1989) SCC (Tax) 317; (1989) 2 SCC 645; AIR 1989 SC 1371; 1989 (2) ARBLR 356; 1989 (2) CompLJ 1; JT 1989 (2) SC 47; 1989(1) SCALE 770; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 320; 1989 (73) STC 370

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Building Contract, Declared Goods, Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Tax Levied, Collected, Building Contractor

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian sales of goods Act, 1930 - Central sales tax Act, 1956 - Levy of sales tax - Transfer of property in goods involved in execution of works contract - Held, assessing authorities did not attempt to ascertain whether sales of goods involved in a execution or Works contract had taken place in favour of person who had assigned contract outside state in which Works contract was being executed - No attempt to find out whether any part of goods so used in a Works contract had been imported from abroad on account of person who had assigned contract or whether any part of goods, such as, iron and steel etc which were declared goods, had already suffered Sales tax at an earlier point in State - No determination on whether tax on such goods exceeded limit prescribed by s. 15 of Central Sales tax Act, 1956 - Exemptions under state sales tax laws was also not considered - Such levy is also subject to restrictions and conditions mentioned in art. 286 - Expression 'a tax on sale or purchase of goods' in art. 286 includes transfer of goods referred to in sub-cl. (b) of cl. (29-A) of art. 366 which is deemed to be a sale of goods - Tax leviable thereon would be subject to terms of cl. (1) of art. 286 - Restrictions mentioned in cl. (2) of art. 286 of Constitution - Parliament may by law formulate principles for determining when a sale or purchase of goods takes place - Sub-cl. (a) of cl. (3) of art. 286 is generally applicable to all sales including transfer, supply or delivery of goods which are deemed to be sales u/cl. (29-A) of art. 366 of Constitution - Therefore, state needs to exercise due care in certain cases of 'building activity' - Relates to a basic substantial necessity - Would merit separate treatment determined by legislative wisdom.
