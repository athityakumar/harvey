N. D. M. C v Statesman Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 278; (1989) Supp2 SCC 547; AIR 1990 SC 383; JT 1989 (4) SC 207; 1989(2) SCALE 877B; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 591; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delhi Urban Art Commission Act, 1973, Delhi Fire Prevention And Fire Safety Act, 1986, Statutory Building Bye-taws for Union Territory of New Delhi (Promulgated under Punjab Municipal Act 1911)

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 - Delhi Urban Art Commission Act, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Delhi Fire Prevention and Fire Safety Act, 1986 - Statutory Building Bye-taws for Union Territory of New Delhi (Promulgated under Punjab Municipal Act 1911) - Revised plan for construction - Dismissal order - Legality - Respondent No. 1 was a publisher of Newspapers, holds a lease in perpetuity from Govt. Respondent No. 1 obtained permission from Land Development Officer, to erect a high-rise building and obtained, sanction from NDMC/appellant of its building plans - Respondent No. 1 submitted revised plans incorporating therein substantial changes in plans necessitated - Appellant forwarded revised plans to Delhi Urban Art Commission/DUAC - DUAC did not promptly scrutinize the plans but engaged itself in some correspondence with appellant - Appellant rejected the plans - Respondents No. 1 & 2 filed writ petition before HC, during pendency of proceedings in HC the DUAC decided to approve plans and gave clearance - HC was persuaded that appellants disinclination to accord sanction to plan was unjustified and directed appellant to convey its formal sanction of building plans and release the same - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was right in passing impugned order - Held, it was for this reason that though in view of findings recorded on various contentions, order of HC required to be and was set aside however, SC kept this appeal pending for such final orders and directions as might become necessary to be issued - SC permitted respondent No. 1 and 2 to effect such rectifications to plans in regard to Refuge Area as might be necessary in light of observations in this order - Refuge-areas could be located in each of the floors separately, provided that it could be shown to satisfaction of appellant that such measure would better promote fire safety in building and, provided further, that they were located on external walls 'by cantilever projection or in any other manner' abutting vacant space from which rescue operations were rendered possible - If such rectifications to plans were made and submitted within 3 weeks from today, appellant would consider and decide question of according sanction to plans in light of observations in this order without insisting upon any fresh clearance from DUAC or Chief Fire Officer - Appeal was kept pending and be taken-up for final disposal after submission of report from appellant - If respondents No. 1 and 2 were aggrieved by such fresh decision of appellant, those grievances should be considered in further proceedings in appeal - SC permitted respondents, at their option to commence construction work according to plans submitted by them, on condition that they file written undertaking before appellant that construction would be at risk of respondents No. 1 & 2 and it would not progress beyond a height of 15 metres and in event of an ultimate rejection of plans, they would have no claim against appellant for any loss occasioned to respondent No. 1 and 2 - Order accordingly.
