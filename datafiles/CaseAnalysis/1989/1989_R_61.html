Bank of Baroda v Rednam Nagachaya Devi

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Kuldip Singh, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 217; (1989) 4 SCC 470; AIR 1989 SC 2105; [1990] 68 Comp Cas 165; JT 1989 (3) SC 597; 1989(2) SCALE 489; 1989 (2) UJ 590

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Usurious Loans Act, 1918, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Agriculturists Relief Act 1938

Summary: Banking and Finance - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Agriculturists Relief Act 1938, s. 4(e) - Constitution, arts. 14, 226 and 245 - Usurious Loans Act, 1918 - Suit for recovery of loan was filed by a Nationalised Bank against an agriculturist - Plea challenging s. 4(e) of 1938 Act as discriminatory was not raised before the High Court in second appeal - Burden lies on person who impeaches to make specific, clear and unambiguous allegations and give sufficient particulars - Questions arise - (A) Whether the statutory provision u/s. 4(e), is arbitrary in the light of arts. 14, 226 and 245 of Constitution - (B) Whether nationalised banks are entitled to exemption u/s. 4(e) - Held, in the absence of specific plea court declined to decide the matter on its own - Provisions of 1938 Act granting relief are not applicable - Matter Remitted To High Court.
