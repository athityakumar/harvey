Collector Of Central Excise, Baroda v Ambalal Sarabhai Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 253; (1989) 4 SCC 112; (1989) SCC (Tax) 584; AIR 1990 SC 59; 1989 CRLR 662; 1989 CrLR(SC) 662; 1989 (24) ECC 193; 1989 (24) ECR 433; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 214; [1990] 185 ITR 87; JT 1989 (3) SC 341; 1989(2) SCALE 255; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 784; 1990 (77) STC 190; 1989 (2) UJ 537

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3, ch. 1, item 1 E - Marketable goods - Levy of excise duty - Production of starch hydrolysate - By applying process of hydrolysis to starch - Product never marketed and not capable of being marketed as highly unstable character - Product was not known in market as glucose in one form or other - Held, not dutiable - Appeal dismissed.
