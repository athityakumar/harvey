Gouranga Chakraborty v State Of Tripura And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 560; (1989) 3 SCC 314; (1989) SCC (L&S) 474; AIR 1989 SC 1321; 1989 (11) ATC 130; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 86; 1989(1) SCALE 793; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 271; 1991 (3) SLR 88; 1989 (2) UJ 252

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Absence Without Leave, Border Security Force Act, 1968.

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Border Security Force Act, 1968, ss. 19, 11(2), 11(4) and 48 - Border Security Force Rules, 1969, r. 177 - Violation of natural justice - Appellant, constable in Border Security Force - Absent from duty without sufficient cause - Terminated from service - Validity when commandant asking him to submit his explanation against proposed punishment - Held, that punishment was according to rules and it was appellant who did not avail opportunity and didn't file any show cause to said notice - Appeal dismissed.
