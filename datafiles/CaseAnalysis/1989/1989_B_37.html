Municipal Board, Bareilly v Bharat Oil Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 148; (1990) 1 SCC 311; AIR 1990 SC 548; 1990 ALR 248; 1990 (1) AWC 51; JT 1989 (4) SC 453; 1989(2) SCALE 1269; 1990 (78) STC 453; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 376; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 209

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Municipalities and Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, s. 59 - Uttar Pradesh Municipal Account Code, 1925, r. 131 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 128, 135, 153, 296 and 300 - Whether Municipal Board had authority to impose octroi on mineral oil in view of the proviso r. 131 of Octroi Rules contained in the U.P. Municipal Account Code, 1925? - Held, 1963 Rules had been framed u/s. 296 of the Act in supersession of the existing rules after publication by the State Government, in the gazette as provided u/s. 300 and therefore r. 131 in 1925 Rule ceased to have any operation in respect of the matters dealt with therein so far as Municipality is concerned, therefore appellant Board had authority to levy octroi on mineral oils - Appeals allowed.
