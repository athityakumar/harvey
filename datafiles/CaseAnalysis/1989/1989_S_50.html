Shantilal Rampuria And Ors. v Vega Trading Corporation And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 382; (1989) 3 SCC 552; AIR 1989 SC 1819; 1990 (1) CalLT(SC) 41; JT 1989 (3) SC 301; 1989 (2) RCJ 305; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 268; 1989(2) SCALE 250; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 632; 1989 (2) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 13(1)(a), 14 and 16 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Land and Property - Subtenancy - General authority to induct not written, so landlord entitled to evict tenant - Dismissal of previous suit instituted prior to Act, on ground that sub-tenants were illegally inducted - Subsequent suit filed after Act by both pre and post Act inducted tenants - Eviction on ground of violation of s. 13(1)(a) of 1956 Act inter alia pleaded - (A) Aplicability of 'Res judicata' - (B) Validity of creation of sub-tenants - With ancillary questions of non-joinder of sub-tenants as parties and plea of service of notice to landlord - Earlier suit would not operate as res judicata since parties are not same and question of violation of provisions of 1956 Act not involved in earlier suit - General authority given by landlord to tenant to induct sub-tenants not sufficient in law - Suit would not fail for non-impleading them - It is open to sub-tenant to raise plea - Appeal allowed.
