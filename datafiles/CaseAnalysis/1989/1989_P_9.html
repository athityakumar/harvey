Abdul Khader Rowther v P. K. Sara Bai and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 208; (1989) 4 SCC 313; AIR 1990 SC 682; 1989 (15) ALR 656; 1990 (1) CCC 316; JT 1989 (3) SC 527; 1989(2) SCALE 1464; 1989 (2) UJ 696

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 ,O. 6, r. 4, sch. 1 - Pleadings, Forms 47 and 48 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(1)(c) - (A) New plea - Suit for specific performance - Issue regarding mortgage by conditional sale decided against plaintiff by Trial Court - Finding not challenged in appeal filed by respondent - Whether contention can be allowed to be raised in appeal before Supreme Court? - (B) Specific performance of covenant for reconveyance - Pleadings and evidence - No pleadings that plaintiff was willing to specifically perform his part as required by Forms 47 and 48 of sch. 1 - Whether equitable relief could not be granted? - Held,(A) Contention could not be allowed to be raised in appeal before Supreme Court - (B) Could not be granted - Appeal dismissed.
