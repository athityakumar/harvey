 Charan Lal Sahu Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, A.M. Ahmadi, K.N. Saikia, K.N. Singh, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 137; (1990) 1 SCC 613; AIR 1990 SC 1480; 1990 (1) CompLJ 125; JT 1989 (4) SC 582; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 597

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Bhopal Gas Leak Disaster (Processing Of Claims) Act, 1985, Industries (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 1984

Summary: Constitution - Liability of Sate - Bhopal Gas Leak Disaster (Processing of Claims) Act, 1985 - ss. 3, 4, 6 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Art. 21 - Bhopal Gas Leak Disaster (Processing of Claims) Act, 1985 provided locus standi to government to file suit on behalf of victims - Act further replaced Civil Court of competent jurisdiction by an Officer appointed by the Central Government - Whether Bhopal Gas Leak Disaster (Processing of Claims) Act, 1985 violated fundamental rights of the victims and was constitutionally invalid - Held, no - The Act conferred powers upon the government to act alongwith the victims - Rights of the victims to seek compensation is not curtailed in any manner - Art. 14 provides for differential treatment of special class of citizens and victims can be treated differently - Victims were financially not competent to indulge in legal battle against the big corporations and Government can act on their behalf - Disposed accordingly.
