Kashiram Yadav And Anr. v Oriental Fire And Gen. Insurance Co. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 256; (1989) 4 SCC 128; (1989) SCC (Cr) 702; 1990 (1) ACC 1; 1989 (2) ACJ 1078; AIR 1989 SC 2002; 1989 (15) ALR 613; 1989 (3) CCC 64; [1989] 66 Comp Cas 814; 1989 CrLR(SC) 761; JT 1989 (3) SC 504; 1989(2) SCALE 343; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 811; 1989 (2) UJ 659

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Licensed Driver

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 92 and 96 - Appeal as to liability of insurer to indemnify owner to satisfy judgment against him - Owner of vehicle ordered to compensate for rash and negligent act of an unlicensed driver resulting in death - Court held that s. 962(b) provides that insurer is not liable to satisfy judgments against person insured if there has been breach of a specified condition of policy - One of conditions of policy specified u/cl. (ii) is that vehicle should not be driven by any person who is not duly licensed - Appeal dismissed.
