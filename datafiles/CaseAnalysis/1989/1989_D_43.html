Union of India and Others v Dr. S. Krishna Murthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 248; (1989) 4 SCC 689; (1990) SCC (L&S) 23; 1989 (11) ATC 892; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 263; 1989(2) SCALE 769; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 275; 1990 (1) SLJ 67; 1989 (4) SLR 472

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Executive Instructions, Year Of Allotment, Released Emergency Commissioned Officers And Short Service Commissioned, All India Services (Conditions of Service Residuary Matters) Rules, 1960, Indian Forest Service (Initial Recruitment) Regulations, 1966, Ips (Regulation Of Seniority) Rules, 1954, Indian Police Service (Regulation of seniority) Rules 1954, All India Services Act 1954, All India Services (Amendment) Act 1975, Indian Forest Service (Regulation of seniority) Rules 1968, Indian Police Service (Appointment By Competitive Examination) Regulations, 1955

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 , arts. 14 and 16 - All India Services Act, 1954 - All India Services (Amendment) Act, 1975, s. 3 - All India Services (Conditions of Service Residuary Matters) Rules, 1960 - Indian Police Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1954 - Indian Forest Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules 1968, r. 3(2) (d) - Indian Police Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules 1954, rr. 3(2) (d), 3(3)(c) and (d) - Released Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Service Commissioned - Whether respondents even though recruited before appellants could be made junior in seniority to appellants who had served as Emergency Commissioned Officers (ECO's) and Short Commissioned officers(SSCO's)? - Respondents were appointed to the Indian Forest Service in 1966, but their year of allotment is '1964 1/2', that is to say, long before service came into existence - If it is possible in case of respondents, it is failed to understand why it is not possible in case of other recruits including Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Commissioned Officers - Year of allotment of respondents has been granted to them on basis of certain principles, as contained in r. 3 of Indian Forest Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1968 - Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Commissioned Officers are, however, governed by impugned rules - Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Commissioned Officers have been classified into a distinct and separate class and that since such classification is reasonable, no objection can be taken to year of allotment given to them in accordance with impugned rules - No invidious discrimination has been made between Emergency Commissioned Officers and Short Commissioned Officers on one hand and respondents on other, both in regard to Indian Forest Service and Indian Police Service - No illegality has been committed by Govt. in framing impugned rules with retrospective effect - Appeal allowed.
