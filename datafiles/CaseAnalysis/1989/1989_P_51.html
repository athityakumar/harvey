Pramod Mahto and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 590; (1989) Supp2 SCC 672; (1990) SCC (Cr) 206; AIR 1989 SC 1475; 1989 (2) AWC 1176; 1989 CRLJ 1479; 1989 CRLR 506; JT 1989 (3) SC 494; 1989(1) SCALE 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Offence of rape - (A) Plea that case was foisted on accused due to communal feelings - (B) Sentence - Accused playing leading role - Maximum sentence imposed on him - Interference sought - Held, (a) even if communal feelings had run high, it is inconceivable that an unmarried girl and two married women would go to extent of staking their reputation and future in order to falsely set up a case of rape on them for sake of communal interests - (B) Supreme Court declined to modify it, but maximum sentence of other accused reduced to minimum sentence as there were no special circumstances to call for such punishment - Appeal partly allowed.
