Mafatlal Fine Spinning and Manufacturing Company v Collector Of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 396; (1989) 2 SCC 446; AIR 1989 SC 784; 1989 CRLR 258; 1989 CrLR(SC) 258; 1989 (20) ECC 7; 1989 (21) ECR 2; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 218; JT 1989 (1) SC 160; 1989(1) SCALE 121; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 204; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 78; 1989 (1) UJ 374

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Appellant engaged in manufacture of cotton fabrics - Opted under r. 49-A for facility of payment of duty of exercise payable on yarn to be deferred until clearance of cotton fabrics manufactured - Rule 49-A prescribes differential rates of interest on deferred yarn-duty - Whether interest rate should be 1 1/2 per cent or 3 per cent? - Depends upon whether cotton fabrics cleared are 'Grey' (unprocessed) or they are cleared after 'processing' - Whether processes render 'Grey' fabric, a 'processed' fabric within meaning of r. 49-A(2)? - Appellate Tribunal held that 'calendering' and 'shearing' are 'finishing processes' and render 'Grey' fabrics to cease to be 'unprocessed' to attract interest at 3 per cent - Held, test applied by Appellate Tribunal not appropriate under r. 49-A - 'Grey' fabric, in present case does not become new and commercially different commodity and ceases to be 'grey' cloth - No justification to take out of r. 49-A(1)(b).
