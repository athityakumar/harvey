State of Assam v Bhelu Sheikh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 677; (1989) Supp2 SCC 1; (1989) SCC (Cr) 643; AIR 1989 SC 1097; 1989 (1) Crimes 689; 1989 CRLJ 879; 1989 CRLR 335; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 54; 1989(1) SCALE 554

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 32 - Offence of murder - Appreciation of evidence - No clear and cogent evidence that accused had caused injuries - Statement of one of deceased not recorded by Magistrate even though he was alive for nine days after occurrence - Injuries on person of accused not explained - Acquittal of accused - Validity - Held, proper - Appeal dismissed.
