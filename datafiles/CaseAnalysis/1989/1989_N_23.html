V. K. Rama Rao and Others v National Bank For Agriculture and Rural Development Through Its Managing Director

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, K. Ramaswamy, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 344; (1990) Supp SCC 100; (1990) SCC (L&S) 619; 1991 (15) ATC 840; 1990 (1) BankCLR 430; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 394; 1989(2) SCALE 1407; 1990 (2) SLJ 78; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 501

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: National Bank For Agriculture and Rural Development Act, 1981, Nabard

Summary: Service - Fitment benefits - Equality of treatment - Discriminatory - Petitioners who were employees of respondent No.1/bank filed instant writ petition seeking for a direction to bank to give them fitment benefits which were given to their counter parts who were in bank's service prior to 1-2-1984 - Whether petitioners were entitled to fitment benefits as given to old employees by bank - Held, chart as applied was uniform one and was designed to adjust old salaries in new scale - In a large organisation with a large number of employees involved in exercise, a few marginal cases of excessive benefits could not be relied on to invalidate entire chart for no adjustment chart in such cases could be free from some defects - Further, salaries of old employees could not be brought on to new or revised pay scales by giving them fitment increments as was done in instant case - However, in such a case to refuse to fit salaries of old employees in new scales of pay by denying them necessary fitment or adjustment increments was to deny them equality of treatment - Thus, that amounts to ignoring their past service and to treating them on par with new entrants which would be unjust in itself - Therefore, adjustment increments granted to old employees on such occasions automatically achieve dual purpose of rewarding them for their past service and of adjusting their salaries in new scale - Hence, adjustment fitment and increments were not discriminatory but eminently just and valid - Petition dismissed.
