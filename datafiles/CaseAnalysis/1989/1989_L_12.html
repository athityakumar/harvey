Karnal Leather Karamchari Sanghatan (Regd.) v Liberty Footwear Company (Regd.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 214; (1989) 4 SCC 448; (1990) SCC (L&S) 60; AIR 1990 SC 247; 1989 (2) CLR 531; 1989 (59) FLR 547; JT 1989 (3) SC 537; 1990 LabIC 301; 1989 (2) LLJ 550; 1989 (2) LLN 507; 1989(2) SCALE 460; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 1065; 1990 (1) SLJ 108; 1989 (2) UJ 636; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 60

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Central) ) Rules 1957

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Arbitration & ADR - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, ss. 10-A(3), (4), 17(1), 17-A, 18(2), 18(3) - Non publication of arbitration agreement - Arbitral award - Enforceable - Validity - Respondent No. 1/firm had a serious dispute with the workers - Workers union complained that the management had illegally terminated more than 200 workers - Dispute however, remained unsettled and the workers went on strike which took a violent turn - Parties entered into an agreement containing the terms of settlement of their dispute - It was mutually agreed that a committee consisting of 5 persons, 2 from the management and 2 from the union with the Deputy Commissioner, as the President should be constituted, they would be the arbitrators to determine the said dispute - Committee of arbitrators passed its award directing the management to reinstate in all 159 workmen - Management did not reinstate the workers - It challenged the validity of the award by way of writ petition in HC - Single Judge of HC who considered the matter did not examine all the contentions urged by the management - HC accepted the writ petition only on the effect of non-publication of agreement in the Gazette, HC expressed the view that the requirement of s. 10-A (3) of the Act was mandatory and its non-compliance would vitiate the award - HC quashed the award and directed the State Govt. to publish the agreement in Gazette - Appellants without preferring Letters Patent Appeal before HC against the judgment of Single Judge had directly appealed to SC - (A) Whether non-publication of the arbitration agreement as required u/s. 10-A(3) of the Act rendered the arbitral award invalid and unenforceable - Held, s. 10-A (4) empowered the arbitrator to investigate and adjudicate upon the industrial dispute referred to him under the arbitration agreement - Arbitrator should submit an award signed by him - If there were more than one arbitrator, all of them should sign the award - Award should be submitted to the appropriate Govt. - It was also to be published like any other award under the Act in accordance with the provisions of s. 17(1) of the Act - S. 17-A of the Act provided that an award (including an arbitration award) should become enforceable on the expiry of 30 days from the date of its publication - S. 18(2) of the Act made an arbitration award which had become enforceable, binding on the parties to the agreement - S. 18(3) of the Act goes a step further - In a case where notification was issued s. 10-A(3-A) of the Act the arbitration award would be binding on all parties to the dispute as well as on all other persons summoned to appear in the proceedings as parties to the dispute - Such an award would also bind the successors or assigns of the employer and all present and future workmen employed in the establishment - (B) Whether publication of arbitration agreement was obligatory and if so, when it should be published or Whether it was necessary to publish the agreement within the time prescribed u/s. 10-A(3) of the Act and what would be the consequences of delayed publication - Held, r. 7 stated that the arbitration agreement should be made in form C and delivered personally or forwarded by registered post to the Secretary to the Ministry of Labour and Chief Labour Commissioner etc. - It should be accompanied by the consent, in writing, of the arbitrator or arbitrators - S. 10-A (3) of the Act also required that a copy of the agreement should be forwarded to appropriate Govt. and the appropriate Govt. should, within one month from the date of receipt of such copy publish it in the Official Gazette - At both the places it might be noted that the legislature had used the word 'shall' - In SC's opinion, little doubt about obligation to publish the agreement in the Official Gazette - Appellant also did not dispute said proposition - Appeal disposed of.
