Ram Gajadhar Nishad v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 496; (1990) 2 SCC 486; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2080

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Conditions of tender form

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Conditions of tender form - Non-compliance - Rejection - Justification - Tenders were invited from parties to give their offers for lease of right of the collection of toll of ferry - Appellant and respondent no. 4 filed tender forms - Tender of respondent no. 4 was accepted and tender of appellant was not opened on the ground that appellant had not complied with conditions of tender - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that certificates were more than 3 months old and he had applied before Tehsildar for renewal and or issuance of solvency certificate - Held, it was quite clear that tender form that was submitted by appellant did not fulfill all requirements of the tender notice - Commissioner by its order held that appellant did not comply with mandatory condition, filing of solvency certificate and character certificate within the last date of submission of tender, so the Commissioner did not open the tender form submitted by appellant on that ground - Appeal dismissed.
