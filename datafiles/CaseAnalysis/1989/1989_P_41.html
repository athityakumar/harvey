Rashidmiya @ Chhava Ahmedmiya Shaik v Police Commissioner, Ahmedabad And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 623; (1989) 3 SCC 321; (1989) SCC (Cr) 559; AIR 1989 SC 1703; 1989 CRLR 482; 1989 (22) ECR 483; JT 1989 (2) SC 323; 1989(1) SCALE 1592; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Criminal - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-Social Activities Act, 1985, ss. 2(c), 2(b) and 3(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 212 and 307 - Constitution of India, art. 22(5) - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Arms Act, s. 25 - Petitioner in case was detained on grounds of indulging in criminal and anti-social activities in Ahmedabad city by indulging in bootlegging activities, always armed with dangerous weapons like bombs, cartridges etc - Cases were filed in this regard - Detenu was also alleged to be a member of gang of Abdul Latif Abdul Wahab Shaikh entered into a conspiracy to spread an atmosphere of fear and terror among residents of that area and also a sense of insecurity among people - Whether solitary instances are sufficient to bring a person within ambit of 'dangerous person' as defined in s. 2(c) of Act of 1985? - Solitary incident would hardly be sufficient to conclude that detenu was habitually committing or attempting to commit or abetting commission of offences - General and vague allegations made in grounds of detention that detenu was taking active part in communal riots and entered into conspiracy to spread an atmosphere of terror being a member of gang of Abdul Latif Abdul Wahab Shaikh in absence of any specific instance or registration of any case thereof, cannot be construed an offences falling under any of above three chapters of IPC or Chapter V of Arms Act enumerated u/s. 2(c) of Act of 1985 so as to characterise detenu as a 'dangerous person' - (B) Whether impugned order can be sustained on other ground that detenu is a 'bootlegger'? - Order could be sustained, provided there are materials to show that bootlegging activities of detenu affected adversely or were likely to affect maintenance of public order - A conjoint reading of s. 2(b) and s. 3(4) of Act of 1985 with explanation annexed thereto clearly spells out that in order to clamp an order of detention upon a 'bootlegger' u/s. 3 of Act, detaining authority must not only be satisfied that person is a bootlegger within meaning of s. 2(b) but also that activities of said bootlegger affect adversely or are likely to affect adversely maintenance of public order - Held, vague allegations in grounds of detention that detenu is main member of gang of Abdul Latif Abdul Wahab Shaikh indulging in bootlegging activities and that detenu is taking active part in such dangerous activities, are not sufficient for holding that his activities affected adversely or were likely to affect adversely maintenance of public order in compliance with s. 3(4) of Act that activities of detenu have caused harm - (C)Whether accused is liable to be detained on grounds of bootlegging? - Further held, it may be that petitioner is a bootlegger within meaning of s. 2(b) of Act, but merely because he is a bootlegger he cannot be preventively detained under provisions of Act unless, as laid down in s. 3(4) of Act, his activities as a bootlegger affect adversely or are likely to affect adversely maintenance of public order - Petition allowed.
