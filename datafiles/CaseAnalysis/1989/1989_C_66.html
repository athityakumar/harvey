Sundarjas Kanyalal Bhatija and Others v Collector, Thane, Maharashtra, and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 758; (1989) 3 SCC 396; AIR 1991 SC 1893; AIR 1991 SCW 2122; 1990 (2) Bom.C.R. 82; [1990] 68 Comp Cas 20; 1989 (25) ECR 129; [1990] 183 ITR 130; JT 1989 (3) SC 57; 1989(2) SCALE 7; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 405; 1990 (77) STC 347; 1991 TAXLR 230; 1989 (2) UJ 485

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether State has a wide discretion in selection of areas for constituting a Corporation and court cannot interfere with such discretion? - Held, function of Govt. in establishing a Corporation under Act is neither executive nor administrative - It is a legislative process - No judicial duty is laid on Govt. in discharge of statutory duties - In present case, Govt. did publish proposal by a draft notification and also considered representations received - It was only thereafter that a decision was taken to exclude Ulhasnagar for time being - That decision became final when it was notified u/s. 3(2) - Court cannot sit in judgment over such decision - It cannot lay down norms for exercise of that power - It cannot substitute even 'its juster will for theirs' - Equally, rule issued by High Court to hear parties is untenable - Govt., in exercise of its powers u/s. 3, is not subject to rules of natural justice any more than is Legislature itself - Rules of natural justice are not applicable to legislative action, plenary or subordinate - Procedural requirement of hearing is not implied in exercise of legislative powers unless hearing was expressly prescribed - HC, therefore, was in error in directing Govt. to hear parties who are not entitled to be heard under law - Appeals allowed.
