 S.S. Rathore v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, B.C. Ray, E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza, K.N. Singh, S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 163; (1989) 4 SCC 582; (1990) SCC (L&S) 150; AIR 1990 SC 10; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 790; JT 1989 (3) SC 530; 1989(2) SCALE 510; 1989 (5) SLR 779; [1989] S1 S.C.R. 43; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 43; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 74

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, Sch. 1, art. 58 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Whether suit was within limitation? - If original order of punishment is taken as date when cause of action first accrues for purposes of art. 58, great hardship is bound to result - On one side, claim would not be maintainable if laid before exhaustion of remedies, on other, if departmental remedy though availed is not finalised within period of limitation, cause of action would no more be justifiable having become barred by limitation - Held, it would be appropriate that cause of action first arises when remedies available to public servant under relevant Service Rules as to redressal are disposed of - Only when appeal or representation provided by law is disposed of, cause of action shall first accrued and where such order is not made, on expiry of six months from date when appeal was filed or representation was made, right to sue shall first accrued - Suit was within time - Appeal allowed.
