Kiran Bedi v Committee of Inquiry and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, E.S. Venkataramiah, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 901; (1989) 1 SCC 494; AIR 1989 SC 714; 1989 (1) CCC 302; 1989 CRLJ 903; 1989 (37) DLT 217; JT 1989 (1) SC 21; 1989(1) SCALE 10; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 20; 1989 (1) UJ 340

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 346 - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952, ss. 10, 10A, 4, 5, 5(4), 5A, 6, 8, 8A, 8B, 8C, s 9 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 178, 179 - Commissions of Inquiry (Central) Rules, 1972 - Burden Of Proof - Contempt Of Court - Commission Of Inquiry - Maintainability - Confrontation between lawyers and police sparked off from alleged incident of lawyer being apprehended by students of College and being handed over to police on accusation of committing offence within campus of College - Case was filed before Committee on behalf of HC Bar Association,said lawyer was brought by police in handcuffs for production before Metropolitan Magistrate - Lawyers protested against handcuffing but their protest was ignored by police officials - Metropolitan Magistrate discharged lawyer directed Commissioner of Police to take action against guilty police officials - Lawyers went on strike - It was stated that petitioner made statement in Press conference justifying Action of police and criticised order of Magistrate in discharging thief and that in order to express their deep concern and anguish group of lawyers went to meet petitioner - Police took to lathi charge on lawyers at orders of Petitioner - In said statement of case it had further been asserted that while indefinite strike and agitation of lawyers demanding judicial inquiry into incident of lathi charge and suspension of Petitioner continued, statement of case was also filed by Commissioner of Police, attaching thereto affidavits of 25 police officers including affidavit of petitioner- Court directed that provisions of ss. 4, 5, 5-A, 6, 8, 8-A, 8-B. 8-C, 9, 10 and 10-A of the 1952 Act and Rules made u/s. 12 thereof should be applied to Committee - Petitioner filed Writ petition orders passed by Committee sought to be quashed- Hence, instant petitions - Whether orders passed by Committee were justified - Held, Committee held that petitioners were not covered by s. 8B of the 1952 Act and compelling to enter witness box on dates in question was discriminatory and orders directing complaint being filed against petitioners were illegal - It was that case involved infringement of arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution - Power of Court to pass order in exercise of its jurisdiction u/arts. 32 and 142 of Constitution should not be seriously doubted in regard to special facts and circumstances of case - On orders directing filing of complaints being held to be invalid consequential complaints and proceedings included orders of Magistrate issuing summons could not survive and it was in this view of matter that by order of 1988 Court had quashed - In instant case, if petitioners were compelled to face prosecution in spite of finding that orders directing complaint to be filed against were illegal it would cause prejudice - Order accordingly.
