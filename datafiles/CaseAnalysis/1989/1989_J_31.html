R. L. Gupta and Others v Jupitor General Insurance Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 305; (1990) SCC (Cr) 140; (1990) 1 SCC 356; 1990 (1) ACJ 280; 1990 CRLR 571B

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Assessment of compensation made by Tribunal that in regard to death of two persons has been determined at Rs. 8000 each while for others a fair compensation had been given - But relying upon provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, liability of insurer had been fixed at Rs. 50, 000 - Whether claim of compensation in respect of death of two persons is correctly determined at Rs. 8000? - Held, in peculiar facts of case we do not think technicality of law should be permitted to stand in way and a fair compensation should be paid in respect of two deaths - Court assess compensation for each of them at Rs. 20, 000 in absence of any specific evidence - This is keeping in view quantum of no fault liability now provided by statute prospectively - Interest shall be paid at rate of 12 per cent from date of application till date of payment - Appeal disposed of.
