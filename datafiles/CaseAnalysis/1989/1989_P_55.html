Brij Lal and another v Prem Chand And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 585; (1989) Supp2 SCC 680; (1991) SCC (Cr) 394; AIR 1989 SC 1661; 1989 (2) Crimes 192; 1989 CRLR 493; 1989 (2) DMC 3; 1989 (2) HLR 126; JT 1989 (3) SC 1; 1989(1) SCALE 1076; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 612

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Parents, Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 (Uttar Pradesh 1 of 1951)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Criminal Penal Code, 1973, s. 313 - Accused used to demand money from deceased wife, quarrelling with her every day over payment of money - Deceased on fateful day set fire to herself - Accused could be said to have instigated her to commit suicide - Whether accused is to be held liable u/s. 306 IPC, 1860 ?- Seen that committing of suicide by Veena Rani was due to accused's instigation - It is not a case where Veena Rani had wanted to commit suicide for reasons of her own and accused had facilitated her in commission of suicide - Held, conviction of appellant u/s. 306 IPC, 1860 has to be restored - Appeal Allowed.
