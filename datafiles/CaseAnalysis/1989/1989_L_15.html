Lt. Colonel K. D. Gupta v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 65; (1989) Supp1 SCC 416; (1989) SCC (L&S) 448; AIR 1989 SC 1393; 1989 CRLR 592; 1989 LabIC 1364; 1989 (2) LLJ 143; 1989(1) SCALE 879; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 370; 1989 (3) SLJ 114; 1989 (2) SLR 424; 1989 (2) UJ 371

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Lower medical characterization - Denial of promotion - Compensation - Determination of - Contempt proceeding against respondents on allegation that directions contained in judgment of this court, D/- 20-04-1988 in Civil Appeal No. 1702 of 1987 (1988-II-LLJ-144) have not been complied with - Whether petitioner has to be compensated and what should be its measure? - Held, petitioner has, of course, advanced tall claims by contending that he has suffered physical and mental torture, loss of reputation and of social acceptance and financial loss - What promotions petitioner would otherwise have earned would be a matter of speculation and cannot be ascertained at this stage for lack of appropriate decisive criteria 'Petitioner' grievance that he suffered in dignity and humiliation as a result of being looked down upon by his batch mates, friends and relatives has perhaps been sufficiently met by appellate judgment which has declared that his lower medical categorisation was unjustified and petitioner continued to be shape-I without break from 1977 - A total compensation of Rs. four lakhs would meet ends of justice - This would obviously mean that petitioner would not be entitled to any other claim on these heads but it is clear that he would be entitled to all other service benefits which an officer of Lt Colonel's rank, which petitioner admittedly holds, would be entitled to - This judgment should serve petitioner in vindication of his stand and to dispel clouds cast on his physical and mental health by purported lower medical characterisation and obviously in event of his being considered for reemployment after retirement his suitability would be considered on basis of his service records and judgment of court - Contempt proceedings disposed of.
