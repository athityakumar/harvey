Pt. Parmanand Katara v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 47; (1989) 4 SCC 286; (1989) SCC (Cr) 721; AIR 1989 SC 2039; 1989 ALJ 1111; 1989 (15) ALR 595; 1990 (2) Bom.C.R. 96; 1990 CRLJ 671; JT 1989 (3) SC 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.21 and 32 - Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33(m) - Code of Medical Ethics, cls. 10 and 13 - Public interest litigation - Medical Profession - Professional ethics - Injured citizen brought for medical treatment - Operation of legal impediments - Held, Should instantaneously be given medical aid and thereafter procedural criminal law should be allowed to operate - There is no legal impediment for doctor to attend to such cases immediately - Order accordingly.
