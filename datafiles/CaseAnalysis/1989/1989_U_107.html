Prof. C.D Tase v University Of Bombay And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 472; (1989) Supp1 SCC 273; (1989) SCC (L&S) 370; AIR 1989 SC 829; 1989 (1) CLR 538; 1989 (58) FLR 503; JT 1989 (1) SC 364; 1989 LabIC 993; 1995 (3) LLJ 714; 1989 (1) LLN 572; 1989(1) SCALE 432; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 736; 1989 (2) SLJ 107; 1989 (1) SLR 784; 1989 (1) UJ 562

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: University Grants Commission

Summary: Education - Constitution - Bombay University Act, 1974, s. 42 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 133 - Pay scale - Placement of lecturer - Failure of college to forward his name to University - Although he was entitled to higher pay scale - Denial of higher pay scale to him on that basis by University - Constitutionality - Held, inequitable and discriminatory - Supreme Court directed University to pay within 3 months difference in salary to lecturer as well as to other similarly placed persons even though they were not parties before Supreme Court - Appeal allowed.
