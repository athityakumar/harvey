Firm Ganpat Ram Rajkumar v Kalu Ram And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 240; (1989) Supp2 SCC 418; AIR 1989 SC 2285; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 258; 1990 (1) RCJ 101; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 589; 1990 (1) RentLR 451; 1989(2) SCALE 692; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 223; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(b)and 20 - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 13 - Contempt of Courts - (A) Civil contempt - Order of eviction against a firm confirmed by Supreme Court - Firm not furnished undertaking given as directed nor vacated premises - Sons and grandsons of partners obtained temporary injunction - A partner restrained ejection of plaintiffs - (B) Limitation - Eviction Order - Contempt consisting of act of not giving possession by force of order of temporary injunction obtained in another suit - Failure to give possession if it amounts to contempt - Held, order of Supreme Court cannot be defeated and premises should be vacated by firm - Further held,in facts of case,their was a continuing wrong to which s. 20 was not applicable - Order accordingly.
