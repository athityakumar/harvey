Commissioner Of Income Tax, Calcutta v Prahaladrai Agarwala

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 598; (1989) Supp2 SCC 279; AIR 1990 SC 270; 1989 (77) CTR 67; [1989] 177 ITR 398; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 139; 1989(1) SCALE 1063; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 737; [1989] 44 TAXMAN 435; 1990 (2) TLR 146

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 64(1)(iii) - Whether inclusion share of profit of assessee's wife in income of assessee proper? - Gift by assessee to his wife - Assessee's wife become partner in firm - Admission of assessee's wife to partnership was not in consequence of gift - Held, connection between income of share of profits and gifts by assessee to his wife too remote - Therefore, inclusion of share of profit of assessee's wife in total income of assessee not proper - Appeal dismissed.
