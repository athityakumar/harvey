State of Uttar Pradesh v Chet Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 529; (1989) SCC (Cr) 388; (1989) 2 SCC 425; AIR 1989 SC 1543; 1989 (2) Crimes 13; 1989 CRLJ 1785; 1989 CRLR 314; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 65; 1989(1) SCALE 755

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Dying declaration - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973,s. 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss. 34, 302, 307 and 394 - Constitution of India 1950,art. 136 - Dispute regarding to land between deceased and respondents - Dying declaration of deceased gave details of assault as well motive - Whether conviction of respondents was justified? - Held, yes - Deceased fully conscious when she made her statement to police as to who her assailants were and how occurrence took place - It merited acceptance as dying declaration and to be accorded high degree of value - Medical evidence corroborates dying declaration - Testimony of prosecution witness inspire confidence - Conviction of respondents restored - Appeal allowed.
