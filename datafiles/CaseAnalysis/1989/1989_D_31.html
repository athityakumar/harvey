Francis John v Director Of Education And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 325; (1989) Supp2 SCC 598; (1990) SCC (L&S) 105; AIR 1990 SC 423; 1991 (17) ATC 182; 1990 (60) FLR 65; JT 1989 (4) SC 402; 1990 LabIC 376; 1989(2) SCALE 1142; 1990 (1) SLR 291; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 252; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 420

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Maintainability of writ petition - Termination of service - Appellant was Head Master in a school that was receiving grant-in-aid - Disciplinary proceedings started against him - Results of Dispute Settlement Committee approved by Director of Education which resulted in termination of service - Writ petition against termination order was rejected in limine by High Court - Held, writ petition is maintainable since HC has not gone into merits of case - Case remanded to HC - Directed to hear writ petition on merits in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
