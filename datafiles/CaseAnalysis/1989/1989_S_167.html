Padala Veera Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, M.M. Dutt, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 31; (1989) Supp2 SCC 706; (1991) SCC (Cr) 407; AIR 1990 SC 79; 1989 BBCJ 121; 1990 CRLJ 605; 1990 CRLR 1; JT 1989 (4) SC 223; 1989(2) SCALE 906; 1990 (1) UJ 137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 Indian Evidence Act 1872, s. 3 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence was to be considered - Prosecution established that death of deceased was due to forcible administration of poison although there was no direct evidence to support them - Held, accused entitled to acquittal because of failure of prosecution to prove his guilt decisively as strong suspicion against him cannot be regarded as legal proof.
