J. Ranga Swamy v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 352; (1990) SCC (L&S) 76; (1990) 1 SCC 288; AIR 1990 SC 535; 1991 (16) ATC 525; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 426; 1990 LabIC 296; 1990 (1) LLJ 526; 1989(2) SCALE 1405; 1991 (3) SLR 86; 1990 (1) UJ 424

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 32 and 136 - Petitions before Administrative Tribunal - Requested tribunal to declare the action of state in isolating and framing the rules for the post of Lecturer in Radiological Physics and converting the non-gazetted post held by the respondent 4 as gazetted and in appointing respondent 4 to the said post - Tribunal disposed of petitions with certain directions - Review petition dismissed by the tribunal - Civil appeal as well as writ petition dismissed by Apex Court - Again review petitions also dismissed - Petitioner filed applications for directions in the civil appeal, writ petition and review petitions - Objection raised by office to the maintainability of these applications - Whether these applications are maintainable - Court held, all the points which the petitioner urged regarding the constitutionality of the government orders in question as well as the appointment of respondent instead of petitioner to the post in question had been urged before the bench, which heard the civil appeal and writ petitions, therefore, matters were disposed of after a consideration of all the point urged by the petitioner and the mere fact that the order does not discuss the contentions or give reasons cannot entitle the petitioner to have what is, virtually, a second review, therefore,applications are not maintainable - Applications dismissed.
