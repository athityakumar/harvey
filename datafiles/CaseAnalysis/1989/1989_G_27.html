N. Meera Rani v Government Of Tamil Nadu And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.D. Ojha, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 199; (1989) 4 SCC 418; (1989) SCC (Cr) 732; AIR 1989 SC 2027; 1989 (3) Crimes 173; 1989 CRLJ 2190; 1989 CrLR(SC) 697; 1989 (24) ECR 651; JT 1989 (3) SC 478; 1989(2) SCALE 363; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3, 3(2)and 5 - Appellant-petitioner was wife of detenu who was detained under Act - This preventive detention of detenu was challenged in High Court praying for issuance of a writ of habeas corpus but HC dismissed writ petition - Detenu was already detained in a bank dacoity case and question before court was that when detenu was already in custody, being arrested in connection with Bank dacoity, could there be any reasonable basis for making detention order and serving it on detenu during his custody ? - Held, court quashed detention order confirmed by State Govt. as detention order r/w. its annexure indicated that detaining authority were awareness of fact of detenu's jail custody at time of making of detention order - Further held, detention order to be invalid for this reason alone - Appeal allowed.
