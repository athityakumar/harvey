Shakuntalabai And Anr v L.V. Kulkarni And Anr

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 819; (1989) 2 SCC 526; AIR 1989 SC 1359; 1989 (2) CCC 49; 1989 (1) DMC 536; 1989 (2) HLR 1; JT 1989 (1) SC 607; 1989(1) SCALE 737; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman, Lingayat

Summary: Women & Children - Family & Personal - Hindu Law - Marriage - Custom - Panchamsale sub-sect of Lingayat Community - Ancient and unbroken custom of dissolution of earlier marriage and performance of second marriage in Udiki form recognised by community - Wife proved dissolution of her former marriage and her subsequent marriage in customary form with 'G' - Lived as husband and wife, with 'G', until his death - Enjoyed family pension as being nominated as wife - Accepted by community as wife of 'G' - Legality of marriage - Held, her marriage with second husband could not be declared illegal - Appeal allowed.
