 Tinsukhia Electric Supply Company Limited v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 576; (1989) 3 SCC 709; AIR 1990 SC 123; 1989 (2) CompLJ 377; JT 1989 (2) SC 217; 1989(1) SCALE 1006; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 544; [1989] 45 TAXMAN 29; 1989 (2) UJ 712

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Electricity Act, 1910, Electricity (Assam Amendment) Act 1973, Tinsukhia and Dibrugarh Electric Supply Undertakings (Acquisition) Act 1973

Summary: Civil - Electricity & energy - Indian Electricity (Assam Amendment) Act, 1973, s. 2 - Tinsukhia & Dibrugarh Electric Supply Undertakings (Acquisition) Act, 1973, ss. 6, 7, 9, 10, 11(3) and 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 39(b) and 213 - Petitioner companies were acquired by, and stood vested in, the Govt. by way of the enactments - Whether the declaration u/art. 31C of the Constitution is justiciable and open to judicial review and the extent of that judicial review - Whether the provisions of the impugned legislation have any reasonable and direct nexus to the principles in art. 39(b) of the Constitution - Held, yes - Distribution does not mean that property of one should be taken over and distributed to others - Legislative measure is one of nationalisation of the undertakings and the law is eligible for and entitled to the protection of art. 31C of the Constitution - Impugned legislations are not colourable legislations and have no reasonable nexus with arts. 31(b) and (c) of the Constitution - object of the legislations have a direct and reasonable nexus with the objective of distributing the material resources so as to sub serve the common good - Electric energy generated by the petitioner companies constitutes material resources of the community within art. 39(b) of Constitution - Impugned legislations are sustainable - Petitions dismissed.
