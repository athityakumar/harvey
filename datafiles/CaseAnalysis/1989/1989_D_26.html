Stree Atyachar Virodhi Parishad and Another v Dilip Nathumal Chordia And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 672; (1989) SCC (Cr) 285; (1989) 1 SCC 715; 1989 (1) AWC 309; 1989 (1) Bom.C.R. 633; 1989 (1) Crimes 443; 1989 CRLR 292; 1989 (1) DMC 390; 1989 (1) HLR 635; JT 1989 (1) SC 247; 1989 MahLJ 462; 1989(1) SCALE 330; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 560; 1989 (1) UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Criminal - Dowry death - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 227 and 228 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 306 and 34 - Death of a newly married girl - Elder brother and father of husband - (A) Question regarding charge framed by trial court against brother-in-law 'D' of deceased - Held, it is not obligatory for judge at that state of trial to consider in any detail and weigh in a sensitive balance whether facts, if proved, would be incompatible with innocence of accused or not ? - Standard of test and judgment which is to be finally applied before recording a finding regarding guilt or otherwise of accused is not exactly to be applied at stage of deciding matter u/ss. 227 or 228 of CrPC - But at initial stage if there is a strong suspicion which leads court to think that there is ground for presuming that accused has committed an offence then it is not open to court to say that there is no sufficient ground for proceeding against accused - In instant case trial court has considered every material on record in support of charge framed - Trial court has also given reasons why a charge u/s. 302 IPC is warranted against - even though police charge-sheeted him u/s. 306 IPC - (B) Whether it is necessary to put - also on trial with material on record? - It was argued that - being manager of family ought to have taken care of deceased and without his convince, none would have demanded dowry and put her on fire - It is true that it is his obligation as manager of family to protect daughter-in-law and safeguard her rights - He has failed to perform his moral obligation; but that by itself without anything more is not sufficient to frame a charge against him - Order accordingly.
