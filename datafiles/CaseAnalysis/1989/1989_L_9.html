Laxmi Narayan Nayak v Ramratan Chaturvedi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 355; (1990) 2 SCC 173; AIR 1991 SC 2001; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 438; 1989(2) SCALE 1439; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(c), 123(7), 100(1)(b) and 123 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 173(a) - (A) Validity of rejection of nomination paper when candidate sent letter to returning officer to appoint some authorised officer so that he could subscribe oath - As he was unable to move because suffering from bronchitis - (B) Proof of corrupt practice - (1) Strict proof necessary - Inconsistent and vague evidence - No evidence that candidate made any promise of gratification to any elector or obtained in return promise from voter - (2) Proof of charge of obtaining or procuring assistance of Govt. servants in service for furtherance of prospects of election - Material contradictions in evidence of witnesses, pleadings and name of Govt. servant - No acceptable evidence that Govt. servant distributed money - Held, not improper, as no serious ailment disabling to subscribe oath, and nothing to show that officer was biased - Further held, that element of bargaining was absent and that therefore, appellant had failed to establish charge of corruption - Further held, appellant had miserably failed to establish allegations of corrupt practices within mischief of s. 123(7) - Appeal dismissed.
