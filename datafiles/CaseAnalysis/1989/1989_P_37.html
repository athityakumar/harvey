Union Of India And Ors v Playworld Electronics Pvt. Ltd. And Anr

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 851; (1989) 3 SCC 181; (1989) SCC (Tax) 379; AIR 1990 SC 202; [1990] 68 Comp Cas 582; 1989 (41) E.L.T. 368; [1990] 184 ITR 308; JT 1989 (3) SC 11; 1989(1) SCALE 1186; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 1023

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss.3, 4(1)(a), sch.1, Item 33A, Item 37AA - Constitution, art.265 - Eligibility as to applicability of concessional rate of duty as provided under Notfn. 358/77CE - Assessee selling its unbranded manufactured products solely to one Co. and not in open market - Said Co. in turn selling those products in open market under its own trade mark - Whether assessee eligible for concessional rate of duty - Facts found by authorities did not established evasion of tax -Held, concessional rate of duty could not be denied to assessee - Appeal dismissed.
