Municipal Council, Jodhpur v Parekh Automobiles Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.H. Kania, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 135; (1990) SCC (Tax) 193; (1990) 1 SCC 367; 1990 (1) CompLJ 1; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 309; 1989(2) SCALE 1349; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 49; 1990 (1) UJ 209

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Rajasthan Municipalities Act, 1959

Summary: Indirect Tax - Municipalities & Local Governments - Rajasthan Municipal Octroi Rules, 1962, rr. 6, 9, 9(2), 13, 13(4) - Tax on diesel - Levy octroi tax - Legality - Respondents filed writ petitions for direction restraining appellants from realizing any tax on diesel, which was supplied to appellant by Indian Oil Corporation(IOC) - Respondents further prayed that appellants be ordered to refund the octroi tax - Respondents alleged that appellant had no jurisdiction to levy octroi tax on goods brought within municipal limits but not sold, consumed or used therein and subsequently exported outside said limits - Single Judge allowed the writ petition and restrained appellant from charging or realising octroi on goods brought within municipal limits by IOC - Appellants filed appeals before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent No. 2 had not made declaration as required by r. 9 of the Rules and that under u/r. 13(4) of the Rules, the goods exported were only - Whether the goods brought within Municipal limit by IOC were liable to payment of octroi because of non-compliance with procedure in rr. 6 and 9 of the Rules - Held, HC was fully justified in holding that terms of rr. 6 and 9 of the Rules had no relevance to payment of duty in cases covered by current account facility envisaged u/r. 13 of the Rules - HC was right in holding that instant case could not be brought within terms of proviso to r. 9(2) of the Rules on basis of a deemed consumption, use or sale within Municipal limits - In instant case as IOC had sold goods within Municipal limits, and subsequent transport was though effected by IOC, was really on behalf of dealer goods so transported and entered in transport passes of IOC should be excluded from deduction u/r. 13(4) of the Rules - Instant case was governed by terms of r. 13 and IOC was entitled to go on paying octroi duty on basis of goods brought by it within Municipality less goods transported outside Municipality even where transport outside Municipality might be in pursuance of a sale within Municipality so long as such sale was in pursuance of an intention that goods should be consumed or used outside Municipal limits - HC rightly held that octroi charged on IOC in respect of impugned sales was not justified - Appeals dismissed.
