Marathwada University v Seshrao Balwant Rao Chavan

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 579; (1989) 3 SCC 132; (1989) SCC (L&S) 436; AIR 1989 SC 1582; JT 1989 (2) SC 276; 1989 LabIC 1532; 1989 (2) LLJ 161; 1989 (2) LLN 7; 1989(1) SCALE 996; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 454; 1989 (3) SLJ 50; 1989 (2) UJ 403

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Principal And Agent

Summary: Service - Marathwada University Act, 1974 - Power - Delegation of - Legality - (A) Whether delegation of Executive Council's disciplinary power to Vice-Chancellor is valid? - Held, resolution which was not in conformity with statutory requirement could not confer power on Vice-Chancellor to take action against respondent - By power delegated under resolution, Vice-Chancellor could either accept or reject report with intimation to Executive Council - Vice-Chancellor could not have taken any other action and indeed, he was not authorised to take any other action - (B) Whether decision taken by Executive Council in its meeting held on 26-12-1985/27-12-1985 can be legally ratified? - Held, principles of ratification, apparently do not have any application with regard to exercise of powers conferred under statutory provisions - Statutory authority cannot travel beyond power conferred and any action without power has no legal validity - It is ab initio void and cannot be ratified - Principles of ratification governing transactions of a Co. where general body is repository of all powers cannot be extended to present case - Further, ratification by Executive Council could be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
