P.M. Paul v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 389; (1989) Supp1 SCC 368; AIR 1989 SC 1034; 1989 (2) ARBLR 215; 1989 (1) CCC 433; JT 1989 (1) SC 299; 1989(1) SCALE 221; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 115; 1989 (1) UJ 399

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitrator allowing escalation in contract amount - Does not commit any misconduct award could not be set aside on that ground - Escalation in contract amount by arbitrator - No misconduct - Can only be set aside under s. 30 of Arbitration Act - Award of an arbitrator/umpire can be set aside, inter alia, if he has misconducted himself or proceeding - Adjudicating upon a matter which is not subject matter of adjudication, is a legal misconduct for arbitrator - Held, arbitrator had not misconducted himself in awarding amount of compensation - Cannot be said that if work was not completed within stipulated time party has got a right only for extension of time - On failure, contractor can claim difference in prices.
