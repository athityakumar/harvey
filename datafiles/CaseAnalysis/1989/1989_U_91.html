Rup Diamonds and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 910; (1989) 2 SCC 356; AIR 1989 SC 674; JT 1989 (1) SC 7; 1989(1) SCALE 1; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Import-Export Policy, 1982-83 - Delay - Petitioners-Recognized Export House were granted six imprest-licences for the import of uncut and unset diamonds with the obligation to fulfill certain export commitment for the export, out of India, of cut and polished diamonds of the FOB value, stipulated in each of the imprest-licences - Petitioners sought revalidation and endorsement for OGL items in year 1986 after a lapse of several years from the completion of their export obligations - Such request was declined by Joint Chief Controller of Imports and Exports on grounds of inordinate delay in seeking revalidation and endorsement , and on merits and permissibility of the claim - Petitioners filed writ petition challenging said order contending that their claims are similar to two other Export House and their writ petitions were allowed by HC - Petitioners also submitted that Special Leave Petitions of the Government against said decision being dismissed by the SC - Whether petitioners submissions could be accepted and writ petition filed by the petitioners could be allowed? - Held, petitioners are re-agitating claims which they had not pursued for several years - Petitioners were not vigilant but were content to be dormant and chose to sit on the fence till somebody else's case came to be decided - Their case cannot be considered on the anology of one where a law had been declared unconstitutional and void by a Court, so as to enable persons to recover monies paid under the compulsion of a law later so declared void - There is also an unexplained, inordinate delay in preferring this writ petition which is brought after almost an year after the first rejection - Apart altogether from the merits of the other grounds for rejection, the inordinate delay in preferring the claim before the authorities as also the delay in filing the writ petition before SC should, by themselves, persuade us to decline to interfere in this matter - Writ petition dismissed.
