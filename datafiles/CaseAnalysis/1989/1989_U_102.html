Frick India Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, S. Ranganathan, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 850; (1990) SCC (Tax) 185; (1990) 1 SCC 400; AIR 1990 SC 689; 1990 (41) DLT 337; 1990 (27) ECC 8; 1990 (29) ECR 145; 1990 (48) E.L.T. 627; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 432; 1989(2) SCALE 1417; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Refund - Tariff description -Assessment - Validity - Appellants filed refund applications on ground that parts of refrigerating and air-conditioning appliances which they had removed under gate passes were not excisable goods falling under Tariff Item No. 29A(3) - Assistant Collector of Customs rejected both applications holding that assessment was made correctly - On appeal, Collector of Customs and Central Excise dismissed appeals - Appellants filed writ petition in HC challenging order of Appellate Court - Single Judge dismissed writ petition holding that goods supplied were parts of a refrigerating and air-conditioning appliances, and that they would fall within purview of Tariff Item 29A(3) - On further appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether manufacturer supplied refrigerating or air-conditioning appliances as a complete unit or not for levy of duty on parts specified in Item 29A(3) - Held, Entry 29A adverts to goods which would fall within one or other of the 3 classifications specified therein - Further, description of each category of goods was concerned tariff description was 'parts of refrigerating and air-conditioning appliances and machinery' - However, it could not be read words manufactured for sale' in entry No. 3 by drawing on theory of 'Taking colour' which had no application in a case like instant one - If SC inject those words it would be re-writing that section and would be legislating which could not be done - Hence, complete plants which were covered by items (1) and (2) could not be considered as parts on machinery and such complete plants would not be classifiable under Item 29A(3) - Appeal dismissed.
