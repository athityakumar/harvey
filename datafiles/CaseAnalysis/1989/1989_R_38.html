Dr. Subramaniam Swamy v Ramakrishna Hegde

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 268; (1990) 1 SCC 4; AIR 1990 SC 113; 1989 (15) ALR 773; 1989 (3) CCC 307; JT 1989 (4) SC 131; 1989(2) SCALE 860; 1990 (1) ShimLC 222; [1989] SUPP1 S.C.R. 469

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 25 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 36 B - Petition for transfer of suit by defendant on ground that he would be handicapped in his defence in case suit is tried in Bombay - Held, paramount consideration for transfer of suit is that justice according to law is to be done and mere inconvenience and dominus litis are secondary - No prejudice would be caused to plaintiff - Ends of justice demand that suit be transferred - Petition allowed.
