Union Of India And Ors. v Hind Lamp Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 439; (1989) Supp2 SCC 332; AIR 1989 SC 1733; 1989 CRLR 554; 1989 CrLR(SC) 554; 1989 (21) ECC 182; 1989 (23) ECR 1; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 161; JT 1989 (2) SC 390; 1989(1) SCALE 1485; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Foreign Company, Trade Marks

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Respondent company engaged in manufacture of electric lamps, fluorescent lamps and miniature lamps - Five shareholders of company, one was Indian Company and four were foreign companies - Customer companies - Manufacturing company sold its entire output of products exclusively to said customer companies - Sale of goods was on principal to principal basis and to a shareholding company (Indian Company) and so-called to associate companies of foreign shareholding companies - Goods were supplied to customers in their brand name - No extra commercial consideration as same prices were charged from all customers - How valuation of goods for purpose of levy of excise duty under CESA is to be effected? - Held, prices at which manufacturing company sold its products to customer companies, would be value for levy of excise duty and not price at which customer companies sold these to wholesale dealers and others - Appeal dismissed.
