Imperial Bank of India Pensioners' Association By Its Secretary, C. L. Kapoor and Others v State Bank of India By Its Chairman and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 484; (1989) Supp1 SCC 236; (1989) SCC (L&S) 355; AIR 1989 SC 1049; 1989 (58) FLR 573; JT 1989 (1) SC 383; 1989 LabIC 1242; 1989 (2) LLJ 567; 1989 (1) LLN 527; 1989(1) SCALE 450; 1989 (2) SLJ 64; 1989 (1) SLR 788; 1989 (1) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax

Keywords: IBI Employees Pension and Gratuity Fund Rules and Regulations

Summary: Service - State Bank of India Act, 1955 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Imperial Bank of India Employees Pension and Gratuity Fund Rules and Regulations - Discrimination between India based employees and London based employees - Fixation of 50% of average substantive salary as maximum limit - Effect of changing date - Held, London based employees constitute a class by themselves so no discrimination in same class - Fixation not improper - Changing effective date without giving reasons is also not proper - Petition partly allowed.
