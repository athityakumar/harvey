Snow White Industrial Corporation, Madras v Collector Of Central Excise, Madras

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 848; (1989) 3 SCC 351; (1989) SCC (Tax) 431; AIR 1989 SC 1555; 1989 (22) ECC 287; 1989 (23) ECR 169; 1989 (41) E.L.T. 360; JT 1989 (2) SC 410; 1989(1) SCALE 1328; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 782; 1989 (2) UJ 163

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Principal And Agent, Sole Selling Agents, Selling Agency, Central Excises and Salt (Second Amendment) Act, 1973

Summary: Excise - Contract & Commercial - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(c) - Contract Act, 1872, s. 182 - Excise Duty - Levy of - assessable value - Manufacturer entering into agreement with company - Alleged as be agreement for sale of goods - Finding that agreement for sole selling agency and not for outright sale - First sale of goods was by company to customers of market - Whether price of sale be assessable value u/s. 4 of Act? - Held, price of that sale would be assessable value u/s. 4 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
