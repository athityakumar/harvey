Gujarat Travancore Agency, Cochin v Commissioner Of Income-Tax,Kerala, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 734; (1989) 3 SCC 52; (1989) SCC (Tax) 389; (1989) SCC (Cr) 509; AIR 1989 SC 1671; 1989 (77) CTR 174; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 350; [1989] 177 ITR 455; JT 1989 (2) SC 446; 1989(1) SCALE 1275; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 1000; [1989] 44 TAXMAN 278

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Challenged, Penalties

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 271(1)(a) - Penalties - Challenged - Appellant filed instant appeals against the order of HC - Whether Tribunal is justified in law in cancelling the penalties levied u/s. 271(1)(a) of the Act for the A/y 1965- 66 and 1966- 67.

Held, proceeding u/s. 271(1)(a) of the Act, however, it seems that the intention of the legislature is to emphasise the fact of loss of Revenue and to provide a remedy for such loss, although no doubt an element of coercion is present in the penalty. In this connection the terms in which the penalty falls to be measured is significant. Unless there is something in the language of the statute indicating the need of establish the element of mens rea it is generally sufficient to prove that a default in complying with the statute has occurred. Thus, there is nothing in s. 271(1)(a) of the Act which requires that mens rea must be proved before penalty can be levied under that provision. Accordingly, SC held that the element of mens rea was not required to be proved in the proceedings taken by the Income tax Officer u/s. 271(1)(a) of the Act against the assessee for the A/y 1965- 66 and 1966- 67. Appeal dismissed.
