Income Tax Officer, Azamgarh and Another v Mewalal Dwarka Prasad and another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 748; (1989) SCC (Tax) 266; (1989) 2 SCC 279; AIR 1989 SC 1088; 1989 (76) CTR 40; [1989] 176 ITR 529; JT 1989 (1) SC 239; 1989(1) SCALE 349; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 604; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 40

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 147, 148 and 149 - Re-opening of Assessment - Notice - (A) Alleged escapement of income not resulting from failure to disclose all material facts - Notice issued after four years - Not indicating whether it is u/cl. (a) or (b) of s. 147 of Act - (B) Notice found to be valid for one item of income - Whether Court can question validity regarding remaining items? - Held, (A) s. 149(1)(b) could be invoked and notice being issued after limitation of four years would be invalid - (B) Cannot question - Appeal allowed.
