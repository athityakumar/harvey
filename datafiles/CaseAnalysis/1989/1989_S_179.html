Allauddin Mian and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 577; (1989) 3 SCC 5; (1989) SCC (Cr) 490; 1989 ACC 400; AIR 1989 SC 1456; 1989 (2) AWC 911; 1989 (2) Crimes 266; 1989 CRLJ 1466; 1989 CrLR(SC) 471; JT 1989 (2) SC 171; 1989(1) SCALE 945; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 114 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302 and 452 - There were six accused in all, first two convicted for murder trespass and rioting with deadly weapon and rest for unlawful assembly - Prosecution case was that all of accused together constituted an unlawful assembly, common object of which was to kill Baharan Mian and in pursuance of said object accused caused death of Sahana Khatoon aged about seven years and another accused caused death of Chand Tara aged about seven months - Above action by accused was corroborated by eye witness and medical evidence - Held, court convicted first two accused for murder and sentenced them for imprisonment for life and rest were acquitted only if they have already served out their sentences - Appeal partly allowed.
