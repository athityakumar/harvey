Dandasi Sahu v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 334; (1990) 1 SCC 214; AIR 1990 SC 1128; 1990 ALR 219; 1990 (1) ARBLR 5; 1989 (3) CCC 525; 1990 (70) CLT 256; JT 1989 (4) SC 466; 1989(2) SCALE 1234; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Challenged, Arbitral Award

Summary: Arbitrator & ADR - Interest Act, 1978 - Arbitral award - Challenged - Appellant made claim against respondent before Executive Engineer, for sum of Rs.2,81,745 with interest at 18% from the date of receiving of the claim book till payment - Appellant invoked cl. 23 of the agreement and s. 8 of the Act and requested the Chief Engineer to nominate an arbitrator - Arbitrator awarded lump sum of Rs.25,00,156 together with interest at 9% - State filed application before Trial Court - Trial court overruled the objections of State and the award was made a decree of the court - Single Judge disallowed the interest from date of decree till realization - Appeal before HC by - HC set aside the award and matter was remitted back to Arbitration Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was maintainable.

Held, original claim made before arbitrator, the value of the work not paid was stated as Rs.3,87,796, The supplemental claim made before the arbitrator amounted to Rs.8,27,857. Thus the total value of the work not paid, according to appellant, was Rs. 12, 15,653. As against this claim the arbitrator has awarded lump sum of Rs.25,00,156. It is true that the appellant has asked for payment of interest and including interest his claim was Rs.31,44,437. In view taken from State Of Orissa v Niranjan Swain 1989 Indlaw SC 254 it has been held that where a reference to arbitration was made prior to the commencement of the Act, the arbitrator is not empowered to grant interest for the period upto the date of submission of the claim or the period during which the dispute was pending before the arbitrator. It was further held that where the award granted a lump sum amount it shall deem to have included the interest also if interest had been claimed before the arbitrator and the inclusion of such interest rendered that part of award invalid. If Court exclude the interest portion then there could be no doubt that award of Rs.25,00,156 suffers from the vice of giving disproportionately high amount. State had disputed major part of the claim in their counter statements before the arbitrator and SC held that the award suffers not merely on the ground that it included interest. Thus, there was nothing wrong in approach of HC and that this is not a fit case for interference. Appeal dismissed.
