State Of Punjab v Sukhpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 42; (1990) SCC (Cr) 1; (1990) 1 SCC 35; AIR 1990 SC 231; 1990 CRLJ 584; 1990 (40) DLT 18; JT 1989 (4) SC 95; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 420; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: COFEPOSA

Summary: Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3, 9, 10 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Registration of criminal prosecution against detenu is not a condition precedent for preventive detention by way of anticipatory and precautionary action - Preventive detention - Validity of - Detenu informed about non-acceptance of his representation after two months - Report of advisory board submitted without hearing detenu and without affording him opportunity to produce witnesses - Held, detention vitiated and illegal - Appeal dismissed.
