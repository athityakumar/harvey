 M. Jhangir Bhatusha and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Natarajan, S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 627; (1989) Supp2 SCC 201; AIR 1989 SC 1713; 1989 CRLR 598; 1989 CrLR(SC) 598; 1989 (23) ECC 301; 1989 (23) ECR 163; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 344; JT 1989 (2) SC 465; 1989(1) SCALE 1458; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 356; 1989 (2) UJ 377

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1962, Sea Custom Tariff Act 1975

Summary: Customs - Indirect Taxes - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3 - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 58 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Exemption from duty - Granted to State Trading Corporation and not to private importers - Reasons set out in exemption orders not irrelevant or unreasonable - Exemption granted in public interest - Validity - Held, proper - Appeal and petition dismissed.
