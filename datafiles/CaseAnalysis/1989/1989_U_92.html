Smt. Bimla Rani v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 242; (1989) 4 SCC 509; (1989) SCC (Cr) 756; 1989 (3) Crimes 578; 1989 CRLR 770; 1989 CrLR(SC) 770; JT 1989 (3) SC 737; 1989(2) SCALE 660; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 241; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3 - Habeas corpus - Petitioner detained to prevent him from acting prejudicial to public order - Held, activities of petitioner tend to harm public order - Even a single activity is enough for breach of public order - There were cogent reasons apparent on fact of record justifying order of detention - Detention order justified - Petition dismissed.
