Kayastha Pathshala, Allahabad and Another v Rajendra Prasad And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 191; (1989) Supp2 SCC 732; (1990) SCC (L&S) 86; AIR 1990 SC 415; 1990 (1) AWC 402; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 381; 1989(2) SCALE 1394; 1990 (1) SLR 820; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 450; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Service - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 14(1)(b) - Uttar Pradesh High Schools and Intermediate Colleges (Payment of Salaries of Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1971, s. 10 - Reinstatement of teacher - Teaching since only one or two years in education institution - Suspension - Several litigations between parties - Employee out of teaching for about 25

years - Held, reinstatement of such employee not justifiable and salary of three years on account of damages would be sufficient - Further, that damages would be paid by management and not by Govt. - Appeal allowed.
