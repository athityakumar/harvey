Jaishri Engineering Co. (P) Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 432; (1989) SCC (Tax) 278; (1989) 2 SCC 439; AIR 1989 SC 1218; 1989 CRLR 416; 1989 CrLR(SC) 416; 1989 (21) ECC 116; 1989 (21) ECR 177; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 214; JT 1989 (1) SC 500; 1989(1) SCALE 602; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 870; 1989 (2) UJ 52

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 9, 11A and 35L - Appeal u/s. 35L of Act against order passed by Customs, Exercise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal - Issue was whether goods were classifiable under Tariff Item 52 or Tariff Item 68 of erstwhile First Schedule to Act - Deliberate suppression of item by assessee established - Court upheld decision of tribunal classifying goods under Tariff Item 52 of Central Excise Tariff and in upholding demand of duty for a period beyond six months as contemplated by s. 11A of Act - Tribunal duly gave benefit of exemption notification in respect of goods which had been exported and reduced penalty for deliberate suppression of item from Rs. 1 lakh to Rs. 50,000 - Appeal dismissed.
