Jaswant Kaur v Major Harpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 711; (1989) 3 SCC 572; JT 1990 (1) SC 252; 1990 (1) MLJ(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal – Practice & Procedure - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14, 14(1) and 14(2) - Will - Acquisition of property - Possession - Before the Act came into force, husband of appellant gave to her one half of his property under Will - Will imposed certain restrictions on right of alienation - Appellant continued to be in possession of property till the Act came into force and thereafter she disposed of part of property which was challenged by respondent by way of suit - Hence instant appeal.

Held, s.14 of Act was aimed at removing restrictions or limitations on right of female Hindu to enjoy, as full owner, property possessed by her so long as her possession was traceable to lawful origin, that if she has vestige of a title. It makes no difference that property was acquired by inheritance or devise or at partition or in lieu of maintenance or arrears of maintenance or by gift or by her own skill or exertion or by purchase or by prescription or in any other manner whatsoever. Settled law that if female Hindu acquires property under written instrument or decree of court and where such acquisition was not traceable to any antecedent right then s.14(2) of Act alone would be attracted and where antecedent right was traceable, document in nature of will was of no consequence and case would be covered by provisions contained in s.14 (1) of Act. No justification in maintained judgment passed by HC. Judgment passed by HC was set aside and suit filed by respondent was dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Very right to receive maintenance was sufficient title to enable ripening of possession into full ownership if party was in possession of property in lieu of maintenance.
