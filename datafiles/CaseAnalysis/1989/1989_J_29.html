Food Corporation Of India v Joginderpal Mohinderpal

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 498; (1989) 2 SCC 347; AIR 1989 SC 1263; 1989 (2) ARBLR 159; 1989 CRLR 263; 1989 (20) ECC 15; 1991 (32) ECR 343; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 226; JT 1989 (2) SC 89; 1989(1) SCALE 664; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act,1940 - No exhaustive definition of misconduct - Award may be set aside for error of law appearing on the face of it - Jurisdiction is not to be lightly exercised - Award can also be set aside if, inter alia, the arbitrator has misconducted himself or the proceedings- Setting aside of award - Arbitrator on interpretation of arbitration clause arriving at conclusion - Construction by arbitrator was conceivable and possible-Arbitrator has chosen to make a speaking award -Held, not a misconduct on the part of an arbitrator to come to an erroneous decision, whether or not his findings of fact are supported by evidence - Court has no jurisdiction to modify award given by Arbitrator-The order of the arbitrator was a fair order after considering all the records and the conclusion arrived at by him is a plausible conclusion - Court has no jurisdiction to interfere or modify the award.
