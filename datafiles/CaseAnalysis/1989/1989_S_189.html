Goodyear India Limited and Others v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 269; (1990) 2 SCC 71; (1990) SCC (Tax) 223; AIR 1990 SC 781; 1990 (1) CompLJ 23; 1990 (27) ECR 5; [1991] 188 ITR 403; JT 1989 (4) SC 229; 1989(2) SCALE 982; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 510; 1990 (76) STC 71; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 510

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1983

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution Act, 1982 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1983 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Registered dealer - Assessment - Notification - Raw material - Appellant/petitioner was engaged in manufacture and sale of automobile tyres and tubes - Appellant was registered dealer both under Haryana Act and 1956 Act, had submitted his quarterly returns and paid sales-tax in accordance with law - Assessing and revenue Authorities imposed purchase tax u/s. 9 of Act - Petitioner filed petitions in HC were decided in favour of appellant - In exercise of powers conferred by ss. 9 , 15(1) of 1973 Act Governor directs that rate of tax payable by all dealers in respect of purchases of goods was liable to u/s. 18 of 1973 Act - B and petitioner filed petitions in HC challenging assessment - HC quashed notification and set aside assessment orders - Hence instant appeals - Whether assessment orders set aside by HC was justified - Held, s. 9 of 1973 Act and s.13AA of 1959 Act purport only to levy purchase tax - State. competence to impose tax should not be upheld - It was one thing to levy purchase tax where character and class of goods in respect of tax levied was described in particular manner - There were efforts at sales tax avoidance by sending goods manufactured in State, out of raw materials purchased inside to other States by way of consignments rather than by way of sales attracting tax - Situation lends force to submission of assessee that States, unable to tax exodus directly, attempted to do so indirectly by linking levy ostensibly to purchases - Hence, HC was justified to set aside assessment orders - Appeals disposed of.
