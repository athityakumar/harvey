Collector Of Central Excise, Hyderabad v Chemphar Drugs And Liniments, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 755; (1989) 2 SCC 127; (1989) SCC (Tax) 245; AIR 1989 SC 832; 1989 CRLR 323; 1989 CrLR(SC) 323; 1989 (21) ECC 66; 1989 (21) ECR 182; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 276; [1990] 184 ITR 224; JT 1989 (1) SC 417; 1989(1) SCALE 436; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 711; 1989 (1) UJ 539

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10(1)(a) and 173Q - Demand - Exemption - Notification No. 80 of 1980 dt. 19.06.1980 - Respondent cleared patent and proprietary (P and P) medicines of Rs. 55,80.01/- without payment of duty, availing of the benefit of exemption notification no. 80 of 1980 - Under notification nos. 80 of 1980 the manufacturer would not be eligible for exemption in respect of clearances of patent or proprietary medicines, who manufactures excisable goods failing under more than one item of the First Schedule of the Act, and the aggregate value of the clearances of all such excisable goods by the manufacturer or on his behalf cleared for home consumption from one or more factories had exceeded Rs. 20 lakhs - Respondent furnished particulars of only the value of P and P medicines manufactured and cleared it and did not furnish the particulars of the value of the goods cleared under tariff item No. 68 and also not file any declaration under notification no. 11 of 1978 - Show cause notice issued to the respondent to explain as to why excise duty in respect of patent and proprietary medicines manufactured and cleared by it should not be demanded under proviso (a) to r. 10(1) of 1944 Rules and why penalty should not be imposed on it u/r. 173Q of Rules, 1944 for having cleared the goods without payment of duty - Collector held that the respondents are ineligible for the benefit of the said notification - Respondent filed appeal against said order submitting that the demand for the period beyond six months from the receipt of show-cause notice was time-barred inasmuch as there was no suppression or misstatement of facts by the respondent with a view to evade payment of duty - Tribunal held that the demand raised on this for a period beyond six months was not maintainable - Hence, present appeal - Whether Tribunal was legally justified in restricting the demand of duty to six months prior to the date of issue of show-cause notice? - Held, in order to make the demand for duty sustainable beyond a period of six months and up to a period of 5 years in view of the proviso to s. 11A of the Act, it has to be established that the duty of excise has not been levied or paid or short-levied or short-paid, or erroneously refunded by reason of either fraud or collusion or wilful misstatement or suppression of facts, or contravention of any provision of the Act or the Rules made thereunder, with intent to evade payment of duty - Some positive action more than mere failure or inaction on part of manufacturer or deliberate withholding of information is necessary - Hence, claim had to be limited for a period of six months as the Tribunal did - Appeal dismissed.
