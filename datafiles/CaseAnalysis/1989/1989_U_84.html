Messrs Kesho Ram and Company and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 446; (1989) 3 SCC 151; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 203; 1989 (1) RCJ 545; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 425; 1989 (2) RentLR 149; 1989(1) SCALE 1421; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 3 and 13 - Validity of s. 3 of Act and Notification No. 3205-LD-74/3614, D/- 24-09-74 issued thereunder by Chief Commr., Union Territory of Chandigarh, granting exemption from s. 13 to buildings constructed in urban area of Chandigarh for a period of five years have been challenged - (A) Whether s. 3 is unconstitutional delegating essential legislative function to Central Govt. without laying down any guidelines for exercise of power of exemption? - Held, s. 3 does not suffer from vice of constitutional infirmity, it is a valid provision - Examining object and purpose of exemption granted by notification - Restrictions on landlords' resulted in shortage of accommodation because those who had money and capacity to build new houses were discouraged from investing money in constructing buildings because of restrictions placed by rent control legislations - Legislature made provision for granting exemption to newly constructed buildings for certain number of years from operation of restrictions of rent control legislations - These steps were taken to meet acute scarcity of accommodation and to encourage landlords to construct buildings - (B) Whether impugned notification enlarges period of exemption for an indefinite period and tends to amend s. 13 and is contrary to object and purpose of Act? - Notification has taken care to make exemption effective by providing that exemption shall be available to building even if decree is is passed after expiry of period of five years, provided suit is instituted during period of exemption - S. 3 provides for granting exemption from provisions of Act and is an exception to s. 13 and therefore two provisions need not be consistent in their effect - (C) Whether notification is discriminatory as tenants of newly constructed buildings have been denied protection of Act? - Classification is founded on intelligible differentia which has a rational nexus with object of Act - It does not practice any invidious discrimination between two classes of tenants, classification is reasonable and it does not violate art. 14 - Appeals dismissed.
