Dalpat Abasaheb Solunke and Others v Dr. B. S. Mahajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 172; (1990) SCC (L&S) 80; (1990) 1 SCC 305; AIR 1990 SC 434; 1991 (16) ATC 528; 1990 (1) CLR 3; 1990 (60) FLR 172; JT 1989 (4) SC 487; 1990 (2) LLJ 470; 1990 (1) SLR 849; 1990 (1) UJ 328; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 528

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 133 - University - Appointment - (A) Setting aside of - Ground, delay in filing vacancy - (B) Selection Committee - Decision on comparative merits of candidate by it - Challenge as to - Powers u/art. 133 - (C) Experts in committee from teaching faculty - Disqualifications - (D) Plea that constitution of Selection Committee was defective - Raised for first time in appeal - Held, (A) Not a ground for setting aside appointment - (B) Cannot be interfered with by Court - (C) Not disqualified on ground that they have to interview their own ex-students - (D) Cannot be raised for first time in appeal - Appeal allowed.
