Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v P. Doraiswamy Chetty

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 296; (1990) Supp SCC 83; 1990 (86) CTR 192; [1990] 183 ITR 559; [1990] 52 TAXMAN 346

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Finance Act, 1979 - Income Tax Act,1961 - Assessment - Set off - Treatment of losses - Liability - For assessment year 1968-1969, assessee filed return declaring a loss of Rs. 30,945 which was arrived at by including assessee's own share of Rs. 15,473 from business of firm and share of loss of Rs. 15,472 of assessee's wife in same firm - Assessee claimed that not only his share of the loss but also that of loss of his wife in firm should be carried forward to subsequent years for being set off against his future business income - Income Tax Officer stated that where assessee's wife made income that such income would become includible in total income of assessee u/s. 64(1)(i) of Act where there was only loss in case of wife such loss could not be set off against or added to income or loss of assessee - On appeal Appellate Assistant Commissioner upheld the order of Income Tax Officer and dismissed the appeal - In further appeal before Income Tax Appellate Tribunal, assessee succeeded - Revenue made reference to HC - Whether assessee entitled to carry forward his share and also share of loss of his wife from firm and whether expression "income" in s. 64(1) includes "loss" - Held, share income of wife and minor children included in assessee's total income u/s. 16(3) of Act should be regarded as business income derived from business carried on by assessee - Assessee entitled to set off his loss carried forward from previous years - Spouse u/s.64(1)(i) led to treatment of losses which may fall to share of spouse from partnership - On general principles, income from membership in firm would include loss and s.(1) (i) does not warrant contrary construction - Liability to assessment cannot alternate from year to year between individual and spouse depending on profit or loss - Reference answered.
