A.R. Krishnamurthy And Anr v C.I.T. Madras

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 750; (1989) 1 SCC 754; AIR 1989 SC 1055; 1989 (76) CTR 18; [1989] 176 ITR 417; JT 1989 (1) SC 243; 1989(1) SCALE 345; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 596; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 30

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(14) and 45 - Income Tax - Capital gains - Mining lease - Land purchased by assessee - Mining lease to excavate clay from that land granted - Right to grant lease derived from ownership - Cost of acquisition of right to grant mining lease is computable - Applicability of s. 45 of Act - Held, existence of nexus between 'cost of acquisition' and 'grant of lease' - S. 45, attracted - Appeal dismissed.
