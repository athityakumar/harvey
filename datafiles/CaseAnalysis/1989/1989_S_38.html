R. B. Shreeram Durga Prasad and another v Settlement Commission (Income Tax and Wealth Tax) and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 727; (1989) SCC (Tax) 124; (1989) 1 SCC 628; AIR 1989 SC 1038; 1989 (1) CompLJ 302; 1989 (75) CTR 187; [1989] 176 ITR 169; JT 1989 (1) SC 234; 1989(1) SCALE 247; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 335; 1989 TAXLR 389; [1989] 43 TAXMAN 34

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 245 D(1-A) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income Tax - Settlement of case - Application for - Rejection due to Commr. 's objections - Without being heard - (A) Amendment empowering commission to overrule Commissioner's objections, coming into force - Effect of - (B) Assessee making submissions on objections - No clear opportunity to demonstrate that Commr. was not justified in making objections and commission should not accept objections given - Held, (A) Order rejecting application is nullity - (B) Application would be pending and governed by amended provision - Case remanded - Appeal allowed.
