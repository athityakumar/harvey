Asif Hameed and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Jagannatha Shetty, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 506; (1989) Supp2 SCC 364; AIR 1989 SC 1899; 1989(1) SCALE 1547; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 19; 1989 (3) SLR 735

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Selection process - Legality - HC directed the State Govt. in an earlier case Jyotshana Sharma and Ors., v. State of Jammu & Kashmir(Jyotshna Sharma case) to entrust selection process of two medical colleges to a statutory independent body and till that was done State Govt. should entrust process of selection to such a body which was to be free from executive influence - Selection was not held by competent authority as constituted by order of HC - Hence, State and selected candidates preferred instant appeal challenging the judgment of HC - However, unselected candidates also filed instant contempt applications - Whether HC had competence to issue directions to State Govt. to constitute 'Statutory Body' for selections to medical colleges - Held, HC directions for constituting 'Statutory Independent Body' obviously mean that State legislature should enact a Law in that respect - Constitution had laid-down elaborate procedure for legislature to act thereunder - Legislature was supreme in its own sphere under Constitution - It was solely for legislature to consider as to when and in respect of what subject matter, laws were to be enacted - No directions in that regard could be issued to legislature by Courts - HC was, therefore, patently in error in issuing directions in Jyotshana Sharma's case and reiterating same in judgment under appeal - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeals filed by State and selected candidates were allowed - Appeals filed by unselected candidates were dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
