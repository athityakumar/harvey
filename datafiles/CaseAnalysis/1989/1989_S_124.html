Gujarat Steel Tubes Limited Etc. v State Of Kerala And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 619; (1989) 3 SCC 127; (1989) SCC (Tax) 376; AIR 1990 SC 1779; 1989 (23) ECC 3; 1989 (23) ECR 161; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 513; JT 1989 (2) SC 474; 1989 (2) KLT 9; 1989(1) SCALE 1483; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 210; 1989 (74) STC 176; 1989 (2) UJ 307

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Challenged, Galvanised iron pipes

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 14(iv)(xi) - Galvanised iron pipes - Additional tax - Challenged - Appellants company was engaged in manufacture and sale of steel tubes and pipes, both black and galvanized - Appellant contended that galvanised iron pipes manufactured by it are 'declared goods' and are not liable to additional sales tax as well as surcharge - Assessing authority taxed the turnover of galvanised iron pipes at 4% and also assessed and additional tax and surcharge treating the galvanised iron pipes as goods falling under Entry 46 of the First Schedule to the Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether galvanised iron pipes and tubes were commercially different commodity from steel tubes mentioned in s. 14(iv)(xi) of the Act.

Held, purpose of galvanising a pipe is merely to make it weatherproof. It remains steel tube. By being put through the process of galvanising it is made rust-proof. Neither its structure nor function is altered. As commercial item it is not different from steel tube. That galvanisation is done on steel tubes or pipes as a protective measure only was the basis of the decision of the HC Associated Mechanical Industries v Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, Bangalore 1986 Indlaw KAR 42. Merely because the steel tube has been galvanised does not mean that it ceases to be a steel tube. The limited purpose of galvanisation does not, bring a new commodity into existence. Thus, galvanised pipes are steel tubes within the meaning of s. 14(iv)(xi) of the Act. The view taken by HC is erroneous. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside and the orders of the tax authorities in each case are set aside. The Sales Tax Officer will now proceed to re-assess the appellant in accordance with law and the observations contained in the judgment. Appeal allowed.
