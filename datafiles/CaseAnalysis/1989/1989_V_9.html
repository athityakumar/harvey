Smt. Chandrakantaben, Wife of Jayantilal Bapalal Modi and another v Vadilal Bapalal Modi And Others.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 552; (1989) 2 SCC 630; AIR 1989 SC 1269; 1989 (15) ALR 817; JT 1989 (4) SC 115; 1989(1) SCALE 802; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114(e) and 61 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 64 and 65 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 182 and 188 - (A) Evidence Act - (1) Title Proof - Revenue entry - Property in question having large value - No registered instrument of transfer was executed - Revenue entry describing claimant as occupant only - (2) Account books - Entries - Nobody taking responsibility of supporting correctness of entries - Reason as to non-production of supporting documents not given - (B) Limitation Act - Adverse possession - (1) Possession of joint family property by way of gift - Mutation of her name - Description of disputed property as belonging to her on printed rent receipts given to tenants - Evidence reliable and supported by important circumstances - Exclusive possession for 14 years - Maintainability of her claim - (2) Whether actual physical possession by claimant is necessary ? - Considering fact that property in question was in possession of tenants - (C) Joint possession - Claim by agent - Maintainability - Merely because he is collecting rent from tenants - Held, (A) Only possession can be presumed and not as to title - Account books liable to be rejected - (B) Maintainable - Would be of no consequence - (C) He could not be treated in joint possession - Appeal allowed.
