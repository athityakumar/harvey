Madan Lal Anand Etc v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 845; (1990) SCC (Cr) 51; (1990) 1 SCC 81; AIR 1990 SC 176; 1990 (1) Crimes 251; 1990 CRLJ 659; 1990 CrLR(SC) 69; 1990 (25) ECC 277; 1990 (29) ECR 289; 1990 (45) E.L.T. 204; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 295; 1989(2) SCALE 970; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 733; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1975, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Validity of order of detention, main issue - Considering ancillary points - (A) Smuggling, if - Goods imported in violation of condition of advance licence - (B) Subjective satisfaction - Supply of copies of civil miscellaneous applications filed in civil revision but not of revision petition, on which detaining authority relied - Not prejudiced - Plea as to non application of mind - (C) Confessional statement - Recorded by collector u/s. 108 of Customs Act - Reference to, by detaining authority - Subsequent retraction by detenu - Not placed before detaining authority - Detention order passed separately on each of grounds - (D) Non filing of affidavit by detaining authority - Effect - (E) Representation - Delay in disposal explained - No laches or negligence - (F) Import of goods, duty free under licence - Selling in market at huge profit but detriment to national economy - Whether detention on this ground is legal? - Held, Detention order was proper - (A) Smuggling, provisions of s. 110(o) of Customs Act violated - (B) Plea not tenable - (C) Therefore, even excluding inadmissible ground relating to confessional statement order of detention would not be illegal - (D) Not fatal in all circumstances - (E) Order of detention cannot be rendered invalid on ground of delay - (F) Order of detention passed against detenu, not illegal - Appeal and Petition dismissed.
