Sham Sundar And Ors. v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 197; (1989) 4 SCC 630; (1989) SCC (Cr) 783; AIR 1989 SC 1982; 1989 (2) AWC 1121; [1990] 67 Comp Cas 1; 1989 (3) Crimes 347; 1989 CRLJ 2201; 1989 CrLR(SC) 725; JT 1989 (3) SC 523; 1989(2) SCALE 446; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 886

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Haryana Rice Procurement (Levy) Order 1979

Summary: Socio Economic - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 7 and 10 - Haryana Rice Procurement (Levy) Order, 1979 - Appellants, rice mill owners convicted for contravention of provisions of 1979 order r/w s. 7 of Act - Offence by firm and partners - Defence rested their submission on s. 10 of Act - Court held that partner who was at relevant time incharge of business will only be convicted as it is common that some of partners of a firm may not even be knowing of what is going on day to day in firm i.e. sleeping partners - Appeal allowed.
