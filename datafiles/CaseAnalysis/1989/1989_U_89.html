Ramesh Birch and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 593; (1989) Supp1 SCC 430; AIR 1990 SC 560; JT 1989 (2) SC 483; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 79; 1989 (2) RentLR 164; 1989(1) SCALE 1489; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 629

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delegated Legislation, Law In Force, Existing Laws, Constitution & Amendments, Conditional Legislation

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 141 and 246 - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, s. 87 - (A) Precedents - Clear conclusion by majority of judges in a case arising out of Legislation framed on advice of Supreme Court reference - Different judges gave different reasonings - Whether such conclusion can be ignored? - (B) Legislative power - Delegation of - Conferment power on executive to extend any law in any part of country to Union Territory of Chandigarh after independence - Validity - (C) Central Govt., Notification D/- 15-12-1986 - Extension of provisions of Punjab Amended Rent Act, 1985 to Union Territory of Chandigarh by notification - Principal Act made applicable earlier - Whether notification is invalid because of it? - Held, (A) it cannot be ignored - (B) Valid even on 'policy and guideline theory' - Context and purpose of Act justify it -(C) Notification is not invalid because of it - Provisions introduced not inconsistent with principal Act - Appeal dismissed.
