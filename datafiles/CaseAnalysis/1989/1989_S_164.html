Mir Mohd. Omar And Ors. v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 681; (1989) 4 SCC 436; (1989) SCC (Cr) 750; AIR 1989 SC 1785; 1989 (2) AWC 1330; 1990 (2) CalLT(SC) 11; 1989 (2) Crimes 698; 1989 CRLJ 2070; 1989 CrLR(SC) 646; JT 1989 (3) SC 316; 1989(2) SCALE 292; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 278, 311 and 313 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Sessions trial - Recorded evidence - Power of Sessions Court to make correction - Not intended to permit witness to resile from his statement - Correction slips filed much after recording of evidence of witness, also unsigned - Refusal to correct substantive part of evidence - (B) Re-examination of witness - Prosecution evidence closed - Accused examined u/s. 313 - Prosecution at no stage moving trial Court for recalling witness for further examination - Revision - Giving liberty to prosecution to recall witness and ordering deletion of entire examination u/s. 313 by High Court is - Validity - (C) Examination of accused - Counsel of accused to be consulted by Court on nature of circumstances or type of questions to be put to accused, need of - (D) Constitution - Procedure - Order of Supreme Court - Communication - Held, (A) Refusal held, proper - (B) Grant of liberty and order, not proper - (C) No need, however prosecution can bring it to notice of court if any incriminating circumstance is not put to accused - (D) Help of courier service taken - Appeal allowed.
