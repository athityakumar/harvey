Surinder Kumar v Union Territory, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 676; (1989) 2 SCC 217; (1989) SCC (Cr) 348; AIR 1989 SC 1094; 1989 (1) AWC 543; 1989 (1) Crimes 658; 1989 CRLJ 883; 1989 CrLR(SC) 625; JT 1989 (1) SC 505; 1989(1) SCALE 563; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 941; 1989 (2) UJ 23

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code 1860, ss. 300, Exception 4 and 304 Part I - Murder in sudden quarrel between accused who caused injuries to deceased - Accused did not act in a cruel manner - Held, he is entitled to benefit of Exception 4 to s. 300 and can be convicted under s. 304, Part I.
