Arjun Khiamal Makhijani and Others v Jamnadas Tuliani and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 691; (1989) 3 SCC 257; AIR 1989 SC 1599; JT 1989 (2) SC 567; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 76; 1989(1) SCALE 1527

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 ss. 12 (3), 15 (2) and 29 - Maharashtra Act, 1987 - Suit for eviction - On the grounds of default in payment of rent and change of user - Later, added another ground of subletting - Writ petitions before High Court - Dismissed - During pendency of WPs, Bombay Rent Act was amended by Maharashtra Act 18 of 1987 - Amendments came into force with effect from 01-10-1987 - Attempt was made by defendants to rely upon s. 11 of Amending Act - Sub-s. (3) of s. 12 of Principal Act was substituted - Tenants claimed advantage of amending provisions and pleaded that it had retrospective effect - Default in payment of rent if paid as envisaged in new provision, therefore, could not any longer form basis for claiming eviction - Reliance was also placed on s. 25 of Amending Act which contained savings clause and provided that a case would not be deemed to have been finally disposed of, if in relation to that suit or proceeding, any appeal or proceeding was pending - Regarding defendant 6's WP, HC held that defendant 6 was not in actual possession on particular date and this was necessary u/s.15 (2) of Act and he at all came to possession with effect from a later date - Whether order of HC is valid in law? - Held, in context of provisions of Rent Act, as it then stood, the tenant had to plead and prove in court that position of sub-tenancy became lawful - Tenant has to prove consent received by him for creation of sub-tenancy - Such consent of landlord is most crucial prerequisite and if such a prerequisite is not pleaded in written statement, person claiming lawful sub-tenancy is out of court - Amendment became operative from 01-10-1987 - Default related to a period beyond a score of years in past - On facts as appearing on record, even if amendment was retrospective tenants were not entitled to benefit not being strictly covered by what is envisaged in amendment - Bombay HC has taken view that provision is not retrospective and special leave petitions against such judgments have been dismissed by Supreme Court - Creation of sub-tenancy without landlord's consent was prohibited under Act at time defendant 6 claims to have got into property - Defendant 6, as found by HC, was not in actual possession of garage on 01-02-1973 - That being so, benefit of s. 15 (2) of Act, as amended, is not available to him - Defendant 6's claim to sub-tenancy, therefore, has not rightly been accepted - Appeals dismissed.
