Wallace Flour Mills Company Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, Bombay,Division Iii.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 250; (1989) 4 SCC 592; (1990) SCC (Tax) 10; 1990 (26) ECR 169; 1989 (44) E.L.T. 598; [1990] 186 ITR 440; JT 1989 (4) SC 184; 1989(2) SCALE 804; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 311; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 311; 1990 (1) UJ 93

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Removal of pre-budget stock of goods - Contention that goods in question were not liable to duty until 28-02-1987 and had been made dutiable with effect from 1-03-1987 and hence, eligible for duty free clearance - Rejected on ground that products in question were excisable though exempted from duty - Whether duty is required to be paid on clearance of goods which were exempt on date of manufacture under a notification but which was not in force on date of removal? - Even though taxable event is manufacture or production of an excisable article, duty can be levied and collected at a later date for administrative convenience - Held, Tribunal was right in rejecting appellant's plea on ground that taxable event is fact of manufacture or production of an excisable article, payment of duty is related to date of removal of such article from factory - Appeal dismissed.
