Prithi Chand v State Of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 399; (1989) SCC (Cr) 206; (1989) 1 SCC 432; AIR 1989 SC 702; 1989 (1) AWC 392; 1989 (1) Crimes 384; 1989 CRLJ 841; 1989 CrLR(SC) 235; JT 1989 (1) SC 106; 1989(1) SCALE 74; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32 and 62 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 375 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 154 - Appellant in case is accused of rape of one eleven year old girl -The delay in filing FIR - Proof of penetration and admissibility of carbon copy of medical certificate submitted in Court was in question - Court held that delay in filing FIR is no delay as they waited for return of prosecutrix's father - Absence of hymen with edges torn and profuse bleeding from vagina where accused was a strong robust man and doctor opining that vagina admitted one finger with difficulty proved penetration and carbon copy of certificate is admissible in view of s. 32 being primary evidence plus within explanation of s. 62 of evidence act - Appeal dismissed.
