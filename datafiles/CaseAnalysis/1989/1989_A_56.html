Atul Mathur, Divisional Sales Manager Ofjenson And Nicholson v Atul Kalra And Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 828; (1989) 4 SCC 514; (1989) SCC (Cr) 761; [1990] 68 Comp Cas 324; 1989 (3) CompLJ 127; 1990 (3) Crimes 735; 1989 CRLR 651; 1989 CrLR(SC) 651; JT 1989 (3) SC 350; 1989(2) SCALE 239; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, s. 630 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 465 and 482 Mr. Atul Mathur is 'X', is Power of attorney holder - The power of attorney was executed just before complaint was filed and it was stated in complaint that X was filing complaint on behalf of company and he was duly authorised to do so - Whether High Court had erred in law in setting aside judgments of courts below in a matter arising u/s. 630 of Companies Act in exercise of its powers u/s. 482, CrPC? - HC was not right in construing power of attorney as conferring only special powers and not general powers on X - Held, there was no substance in contention of respondent 1 that complaint suffered from a material irregularity not curable u/s. 465 CrPC - Judgment of HC was not sustainable - Thus, judgment of HC was set aside and judgments of Addl. Chief Metropolitan Magistrate and Addl. Sessions Judge was restored - Appeal allowed.
