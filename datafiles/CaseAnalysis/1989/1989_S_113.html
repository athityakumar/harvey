Takhatry Shivadattray Mankad v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 621; (1989) Supp2 SCC 110; AIR 1989 SC 1843; 1990 (12) ATC 692; 1989 (59) FLR 824; JT 1989 (3) SC 33; 1989 LabIC 1930; 1994 (3) LLJ 1122; 1989(1) SCALE 1244; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 214; 1989 (3) SLJ 97; 1989 (2) UJ 342

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Saurashtra Covenanting State Servants (Superannuation Age) Rules, 1955, Bombay Civil Services Rules 1959, Junagardh State Pension and Parwashi Allowances Rules 1932

Summary: Service - Junagadh State Pension and Parwashi Allowances Rules, 1932, r. 241 A - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, rr. 188, 189 and 189 A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Retirement - (A) At age of superannuation only in accordance with decision of Supreme Court - Subsequent finding in enquiries that employee's services were not satisfactory - Reduction in his pension and gratuity by Govt. - Validity - (B) Proposed reduction - Ground, rendering of unsatisfactory service - Show cause notice - Employee failed to avail opportunity to disprove allegations - Order reducing pension and gratuity Legality - (C) Disciplinary enquiry - Dropped - Since become infructuous - Consideration of charges which were subject-matter of enquiry - Only for determining quantum of employee's pension and gratuity - Held, (A) was proper - (B) Not illegal, employee not entitled to benefit of cl. (b)(ii) of proviso to r. 189, as proceedings were instituted long before his retirement - (C) Not illegal - Appeal dismissed.
