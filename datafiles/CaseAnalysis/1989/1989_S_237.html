Video Electronics Private Limited and Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 356; (1990) 3 SCC 87; (1990) SCC (Tax) 327; AIR 1990 SC 820; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 457; 1989(2) SCALE 1483; 1990 (77) STC 82; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 731; 1990 (1) UJ 482

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 304 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(5) - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 4A - Notifications - Tax exemption - Concessional rates - Constitutionality - Petitioners were carrying on business of selling cinematographic films and other equipments in State - State issued two notifications u/s. 4A of the 1948 Act and u/s. 8(5) of the 1956 Act exempting new units of manufacturers in respect of various goods for different periods ranging from 3 to 7 years from payment of any sales tax - Hence, instant writ petitions - Whether impugned notifications issued by respondents were valid - Held, in case an exemption was granted applying same rate resulting tax might be somewhat higher but that did not contravene equality clause contemplated by art. 304 of Constitution - If incentives in form of subsidies or grant were given to any part of units of a State so that it might come out of its limping or infancy to compete as equals with others, that did not and could not contravene spirit and letter of Part XIII of Constitution - However, that was permissible only if there was a valid reason, i.e., if there were justifiable and rational reasons for differentiation - Hence, concessional rate was introduced and was not discriminatory - State Govt. as well as interveners, other States being comparatively under-developed in electronic industry, it could not be said that there was violation of either Part XIII of Constitution or art. 14 of Constitution - Petitions dismissed.
