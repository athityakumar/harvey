Hindustan Oil Mills Limited and Another v Special Deputy Collector

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 806; (1990) 1 SCC 59; AIR 1990 SC 731; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 283; 1989(2) SCALE 838; 1990 (1) UJ 178

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Acquisition of land - Grant of compensation - Justifiability - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act dt.26-4-1961 published in gazette on 11-5-1961 for acquisition of dry lands in survey No. 135/1 belonging to 'S', 13-20-18 acres - Subsequently 'S' sold 10 acres out of this survey no. to the company to enable the latter to establish a lubricating oil plant - Hence, it was common ground that 10 acres belonged to company and 6 acres 35 1/2 guntas to 'S' - Total extent of land acquired under notification was revised from 18-36-30 acres to 21-11-30 acres, difference being entirely attributable to increase in the area acquired in survey No. 135/1 from 13-20-18 acres to 15-35-18 acres - Lands were acquired in due course in pursuance of above notifications and an award was made by Land Acquisition Collector - Thereafter reference was made to District Judge, who modified award in some respects - On appeals, HC made further changes - Hence instant appeals - Appellants contended that they were awarded compensation on basis of market value of lands as on 11-5-1961, the first of notifications - Compensation payable should be determined on basis of notification published on 28-2-1963 - Further contended that HC had finally determined compensation, in respect of lands belonging to company, at Rs. 5 sq. yard and in respect of lands belonging to 'S' at Rs. 4 Per sq. yard - Appellant also contended that extent of land on which godown stands and land appurtenant thereto could not be taken to be in excess of one acre - Held, it was only notification dt.28-2-1963 that could be taken to be effective notification u/s. 4 of the Act - In first place, this was notification on which reliance was placed in first place, this was notification on which reliance was placed in notice u/s. 4 r/w s. 5-A issued on 28-2-1963 - There was nothing to show that government intended to acquire entirety of the survey No. 135/1 - Thus notification of 11-5-1961 was defective in material respects - Second notification of 22-6- 1962 did not carry matters further as it only gave split up of waste land and buildings sites within area of 13-20-18 acres mentioned in earlier notification - Even third notification did not say that it was entirety of survey No. 135/1 that was proposed to be acquired but it made a distinction between land belonging to 'S' and the land belonging to company - It transpired only from award that full extent of survey No. 135/1 was taken as 15-35-18 acres - It was only in this notification for first time that land proposed to be acquired were defined with sufficient precision or clearness and, hence, it was this notification alone that could be taken as effective notification for purposes of computing market value - On a proper construction of the notifications, that the real and effective notification in respect of lands presently in question in survey No. 135/1, was only notification dt.28-2-1963 - Award needed to be modified and compensation had to be fixed with regard to the market value of lands in question as on 28-2-1963 - Further, extent of free land and the market value of godown along with land on which it stands, SC was in agreement with appellant that extent of land on which godown stands and land appurtenant thereto could not be taken to be in excess of one acre - Building occupied only 2000 sq. yards and even making provision for a fairly wide roadway for access to trucks and lorries to the main road from godown, the total extent of such land referable to godown could not be taken as more than one acre - Estimate of 4 acres by the Collector and even 2 acres by the District Judge and HC were rather excessive - Therefore, out of 10 acres belonging to company, 9 acres should be paid for as land and only one acre should be included as part of godown - However, evaluation of godown itself, there was no error in HC's conclusion - Rent derived from godown was Rs. 1000 p.m. - Order of HC was fully justified in taking the net annual rent at Rs. 10,000 - Therefore, no error in the computation by HC so far as the value of the godown was concerned - Appeals partly allowed.
