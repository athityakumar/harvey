Union Of India Etc. v Parma Nanda

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 20; (1989) 2 SCC 177; (1989) SCC (L&S) 303; AIR 1989 SC 1185; 1989 (10) ATC 30; 1989 (2) CLR 1; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 320; 1989 (58) FLR 934; JT 1989 (2) SC 132; 1989 LabIC 1338; 1989 (2) LLJ 57; 1989 (1) LLN 800; 1989(1) SCALE 606; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 19; 1989 (2) SLR 410; 1989 (2) UJ 97

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Service - Misconduct - Whether Tribunal could interfere with penalty awarded by competent authority on ground that it is excessive or disproportionate to misconduct proved? - If there has been an enquiry consistent with rules and in accordance with principles of natural justice, what punishment would meet ends of justice is a matter exclusively within jurisdiction of competent authority - Adequacy of penalty unless it is mala fide is certainly not a matter for Tribunal to concern with - Held, order of Tribunal imposing a lesser penalty on respondent cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
