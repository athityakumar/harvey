Vij Resins Private Limited and Another v State Of Jammu And Kashmir And Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 832; (1989) 3 SCC 115; AIR 1989 SC 1629; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 214B; 1989(1) SCALE 1303; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Jammu and Kashmir Extraction of Resin Act, 1986, ss. 3 and 4 - Constitution, (as applicable to Jammu and Kashmir ) arts. 19(1)(f), 31(2), 31(5) and 31(6) - (A) Forests - Right to exploit or utilize, under Govt. orders or contracts - Is property - To be protected u/arts. 19(I)(f) and art. 31(2) of Constitution - Taking away such right without providing for compensation by 1986 Act - Constitutionality - (B) Evidence Act - Estoppel against legislature - Companies invited by State Govt. to set up industries, by assuring raw material supply - They invested - Factories were ready and in a position to utilise raw material - Legislation enacted to obliterate Companies' rights and enabling State to get out of commitments - Held, violative of art. 31(2), so entire Act struck down - Circumstances gave rise to a fact situation of estoppel - Petition allowed.
