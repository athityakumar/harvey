State Of Maharashtra v Jagannath Achyut Karandikar

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 675; (1989) Supp1 SCC 393; (1989) SCC (L&S) 417; AIR 1989 SC 1133; 1989 (10) ATC 593; 1989 (58) FLR 635; JT 1989 (1) SC 520; 1989 LabIC 1237; 1989 (1) LLJ 441; 1989 (1) LLN 557; 1989(1) SCALE 566; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 947; 1990 (1) SLJ 146; 1989 (2) SLR 31; 1989 (1) UJ 679

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Maharashtra Government Subordinate service Rules 1962

Summary: Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226, 309 and 311- (A) Executive instruction - May supplement but not supplant statutory rules - Whether Govt. can restrict operation of statutory rules by issuing executive instruction - (B) Promotion - Departmental examination - To be passed within stipulated period - Failure to hold examination, for several years - Whether person who has not exhausted all his chances, is entitled seniority - Held, cannot - Such person could not be denied of his seniority as it is unreasonable and arbitrary to penalise such person for default of Govt. to hold examination every year - Appeal allowed.
