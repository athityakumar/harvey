Aslam Ahmed Zahir Ahmed Shaik v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 566; (1989) 3 SCC 277; (1989) SCC (Cr) 554; AIR 1989 SC 1403; [1990] 67 Comp Cas 335; 1989 (2) Crimes 111; 1989 CRLJ 1447; 1989 CRLR 447; 1989 CrLR(SC) 447; 1989 (22) ECC 308; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 330; JT 1989 (2) SC 34; 1989(1) SCALE 840; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 415

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Exchange Control & FDI; Fema

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 136 - Appellant was indulging in receiving and making payments in India unauthorisedly and order of detention was passed against him - Order of detention was upheld by High Court - Undue delay caused by Central Govt. in disposing of representation of detenu in violation of art. 22(5) of Constitution - Whether detention of appellant was justified? - Held, no - Delay of 40 days caused - Appellant handed over representation to Superintendent who left same unattended for 7 days and forwarded same to Govt. later - No satisfactory explanation given for delay caused by Superintendent - Violation of valuable right of representation u/art. 22(5), hampering speedy consideration of representation - Continued detention of appellant is illegal and constitutionally impermissible - Appeal allowed.
