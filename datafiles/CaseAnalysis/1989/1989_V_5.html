State of Andhra Pradesh and others v V. Sadanandam and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 May 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 911; (1989) SCC (L&S) 511; (1989) Supp1 SCC 574; AIR 1989 SC 2060; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 232; 1989(1) SCALE 1464; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Parliament & Legislature; Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Organisation of Local Cadres and Regulation of Direct Recruitment) Order, 1975, Andhra Pradesh Treasury and Accounts Subordinate Service Rules, 1963

Summary: (A) Service - Parliament & Legislature - Administrative - Andhra Pradesh Treasury and Accounts Subordinate Service Rules, 1963, r. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - State Government amended r. 3 of the Rules in order to give effect to an order issued by the President on 18-10-1975 - Seven persons belonging to category 5 of Branch II of the State Treasury and Accounts Subordinate Service presented a representation petition before the State Administrative Tribunal for declaring r. 3 of the 1963 Rules ultra vires, in so far as it made provision for promotion of Clerks of the Directorate of Treasuries and Accounts and Assistants of the Finance Department of the Secretariat to the posts of Head Accountants and Sub Treasury Officers, in violation of para 5(1) of the Presidential Order issued on 18-10-1975 - Petitioners alleged that with the promulgation of the Presidential Order on 18-10-1975, the posts of Head Accountants and Sub Treasury Officers had become zonal posts and as such the zone would be the unit for recruitment, appointment, discharge, seniority, promotion and transfer - Petitioners asserted that the Service rules issued u/art. 309 of the Constitution, as they existed at the time of the Presidential Order did not conform to the local cadres created under the Presidential Order and hence the State Government had issued G.O.P. No. 728 General Administration S.P.W.A. Department dated 1-11-1975 amending r. 3 suitable to the Service Rules in each service - Petitioners contended that U .D. Accountants of the Feeder Sources of the zone alone were eligible for consideration in that particular zone for promotion to the rank of Head Accountant and Sub Treasury Officer and not the personnel from other zones including U.D. Accountants of the Directorate of Treasuries and Accounts and Assistants of the Finance Department of the Secretariat - Consequently, Full Bench of the Tribunal held that r. 3 of the 1963 Rules which specified various categories of posts without reference to zone as feeder posts for the purpose of promotion to the posts in question were inconsistent with para 5(1) of the Presidential Order dt. 18-10-1975 - State Government, subsequently, by G.O. Ms. No. 196 dated 17-6-83, gave retrospective effect to the amended Rule with effect from 18-10-1975 - Validity of the retrospective operation of amended rule was challenged before the Tribunal in set of another two representative petitions - Tribunal held that the judgment of the Full Bench did not afford scope to the State Government to pass a G.O. in conflict with para 5(1) of the Presidential Order and further, the impugned G.O. Ms. No. 196 did not set out under which part of para 5(2) of the Presidential Order the G.O. was issued and therefore amended G.O. Ms. No. 196 cannot be upheld - Hence the present appeal by the State challenging the order of Tribunal - Whether order of the Tribunal is correct - Held, Tribunal clearly erred in striking down the amended r. 3 - Tribunal failed to construe para 5(2) of the Presidential Order in its proper perspective and give full effect to the powers conferred thereunder on the State Government to make provisions contrary to the scheme of local cadres prescribed under para 5(1) thereof - Words of sub-para (2) of para 5 of Presidential Order viz. 'nothing in this order shall prevent the State Government from making provision for' sets out the overriding powers given to the State Government - Such over-riding powers were given to the State Government in express terms in recognition of the principle that public interest and administrative exigencies had precedence over the promotional interests of the members belonging to local cadres and zones - Since para 5(2) also forms a part of the Presidential Order, it forms part of the scheme envisaged for creating local cadres and zones - Tribunal was, therefore, in error in taking the view that if the State Government was to exercise its powers under para 5(2) and make provision for promotion of UD Assistants in the Directorate and Assistant Section Officers in the Secretariat to be transferred to posts in Zones I to IV, it would be the very negation of the creation of cadres and zones under para 5(1) and it would be destructive of the scheme underlying the Presidential Order dt. 18-10-1975 - Further, Full Bench was concerned with the old r. 3 which was framed long before the Presidential Order was issued - In order to make the provisions of the old Rule to have currency even after the Presidential Order was passed, the Government issued G.O. Ms. No. 728 on 1-11-75 - However, the Full Bench was of the view that the G.O. Ms. No. 728 did not conform to the requirements of para 5(2) of the Presidential Order and therefore the Full Bench held the old Rule cannot have operative force 'without any specific provision being made in the Rules in pursuance of the authority given to the State Government under para 5(2) of the Presidential Order' - In such circumstances, in order to affirm the Full Bench order, the Government had issued G.O. Ms. No. 196 dated 17-6-83 to make the amended rule being in force during the currency of Presidential order and thereby, so as to make the Rule conform to the requirements of para 5(2) of the Presidential Order - Tribunal failed to realise the said position and had therefore committed the error of holding that the view taken by the Full Bench with reference to the old Rule would continue to hold good even with reference to the amended Rule - Therefore, r.3 of the 1963 Rules is declared as intra vires and impugned order of Tribunal set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Service - Administrative - Andhra Pradesh Treasury and Accounts Subordinate Service Rules, 1963, r. 3 - Matters relating to transfer and promotion - Whether Tribunal is justified in striking down the inter-zonal transfer of personnel working in the government departments - Held, Tribunal held that there was no justification for personnel belonging to other zones being transferred on promotion to offices in other zones - In drawing such conclusions, the Tribunal travelled beyond the limits of its jurisdiction - Mode of recruitment and the category from which the recruitment to a service should be made are all matters which are exclusively within the domain of the Executive - Judicial bodies cannot sit in judgment over the wisdom of the Executive in choosing the mode of recruitment or the categories from which the recruitment should be made as they are matters of policy decision falling exclusively within the purview of the Executive - Question of filling up of posts by persons belonging to other local categories or zones is a matter of administrative necessity and exigency - When the Rules provide for such transfers being effected and when the transfers are not assailed on the ground of arbitrariness or discrimination, the policy of transfer adopted by the Government cannot be struck down by Tribunals or Court of law - Appeal allowed.
