 T. C. Basappa v T. Nagappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1954 SC 440; 1954 SCJ 695; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Expenses, Return Of Election Expenses, Procure

Summary: Election -Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81(2)(b), 88 and 67 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 132(1), 133(1)(c) and 226 - (A) On what grounds could High Court, in exercise of its powers u/art. 226 grant a writ of certiorari to quash adjudication of Election Tribunal ? - Held, HC has power to issue writs u/art. 226 in grave cases where subordinate tribunals or bodies or officers act wholly without jurisdiction, or in excess of it, or in violation of principles of natural justice, or refuse to exercise a jurisdiction vested in them, or there is an error apparent on face of record, and such act, omission, error or excess has resulted in manifest injustice - Jurisdiction of HC is not wide or extensive enough to convert itself into a Court of Appeal - (B) Whether such ground exist in present case and are HC's findings on that point proper, which should not be disturbed in appeal ? - Further Further held, no - Findings of HC are not proper and cannot be supported - Writ issued by HC is vacated - Appeal allowed.
