 Shyam Lal v The State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 30; AIR 1954 SC 369; 1954 (2) LLJ 139; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 730; 1954 SCJ 493; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules, r. 55 - Whether order of compulsory retirement of appellant is illegal as he was not given a chance to show cause against action proposed? - Compulsory retirement does not amount to dismissal or removal and, therefore, does not attract provisions of art. 311 of Constitution or of r. 55, therefore, order of President cannot be challenged on ground that appellant had not been afforded full opportunity of 'showing cause against action sought to be taken in regard to him' - Appeal dismissed.
