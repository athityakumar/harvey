 Virendra Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 1954 SC 447; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 369; 1954 SCJ 705; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 415

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rights Of Citizenship

Summary: Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Independence Act - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 31(1) and 19(f) - (A) Whether the contractual obligation of the conquered state towards individuals to be undertaken by the conquering state? - Art. 1(1) of Constitution of India, 1950 sets out India shall be a Union of States including territories in which disputed lands situate - (B) Whether the states have the right to set aside the grants in ordinary Courts of the land or can it deprive the party of properties by legislative process? - Held, no opinion and arts. 31(1) and 19(f) of the Constitution, 1950 attracted - Writ petition allowed.
