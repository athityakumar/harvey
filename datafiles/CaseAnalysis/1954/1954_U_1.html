Gopal Singh and Others v Ujagar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1954 SC 579; 1954 SCJ 562; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - They claim to be presumptive reversioners to one Harnam Singh - Who owned property in dispute - After Harnam Singh's death, his daughter Mst Biro, second defendant, gifted plaint properties, excluding a small house, to her sons who have been grouped together as first defendant - Plaintiffs contend that property is ancestral and that daughter got only a life estate, so they sue for a declaration that gift will not affect their reversionary rights - (A) Whether daughter, 2nd defendant, only succeeded as a limited heir and that after her reversion will go to father's heirs in usual way? - Held, that is not Punjab custom among tribe to which parties belong, namely agricultural Jats - Case is one in which daughter succeeded and all authorities produced before us indicate that in that event her sons will exclude collaterals - (B) Whether retention by Mst Biro of house would prevent an acceleration of estate? - Further Further held, fact that she gave away all her property to her sons, bar this house, including property to which she had an absolute right, is relevant to show that her intention was to efface herself completely - Retention of this, particularly in these circumstances when sons already live there with her, would not invalidate surrender - Court agree with HC that gift operated to accelerate succession - Plaintiffs are no longer reversioners even if they would otherwise have been entitled to succeed on failure of daughter's sons and their line - Appeal dismissed.
