 In Re Hira Lal Dixit And Two Others. v Na

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1954 SC 743; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 720; 1954 SCJ 846; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 677

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Act, 1950

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt - Policy of nationalising road transport - Pursuant to policy State of UP under Uttar Pradesh Road Transport Act, 1950 issued notifications specifying routes on which it will operate exclusively and for other various allied matters - Spate of litigation by transporters - Present petition with two appeals and numerous similar applications u/art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950, came to be posted on cause list for a particular date for final disposal - Proceedings continued till fourth day from initial date - On second day printed leaflet in Hindi language were distributed allegedly at court premises, purported to be written by respondent HL Dixit - Leaflet contained a graphic account of harassment and indignity said to have been meted out to writer by state officers and then State Minister of Transport in connection with cancellation and eventual restoration of licence of respondent in respect of a passenger bus - Second paragraph of leaflet as translated in English contained a passage which is as follows: 'The public has full and firm faith in Supreme Court but sources that are in know say that Govt. acts with partiality in matter of appointment of those Hon'ble Judges as Ambassadors, Governors, High Commrs., etc, who give judgments against Govt. but this has so far not made any difference in firmness and justice of Hon'ble Judges' - Upon such offending paragraph of leaflet, court issued rule as to why respondents should not be proceeded against for contempt of present Court - Whether conduct of distributing leaflets, and content of leaflet, in facts and circumstances of case amounted to making hindrance or obstructing due course of administration of justice in courts? - Act or publication which scandalises court itself amounts to making such hindrance, but in present case there has been no scandalisation of court - Fact of distribution of leaflet in court premises stands proved - Other relevant facts as to author of leaflet was himself a petitioner of case and distribution was made exactly at time when matters were posted on cause list and were actually being heard, noted - Impugned passage perused and its effect given - Passage when read as a whole amounted to 'Govt disfavours Judges who give decisions against it but favours those judges with high appointments who decide in its favour: that although this is calculated to tempt judges to give judgments in favour of Govt. it has so far not made any difference in firmness and justice of Judges' - Word 'so far' is significant - Purpose of writing passage, its timing and place, noted and deliberated upon - Hidden warning existed in passage that although judges have 'so far' remained firm and resisted temptation of deciding cases in favour of Govt. in expectation of getting high appointments, nevertheless, if they decide in favour of Govt. on this occasion knowledgeable people will know that they had succumbed to temptation and had given judgment in favour of Govt. in expectation of future reward in shape of high appointments of kind mentioned in passage - Object was to affect minds of judges and to deflect them from strict performance of their duties - Held, offending passage and time and place of its publication certainly tended to hinder or obstruct due administration of justice and is a contempt of court - Gross committal of contempt of Court - Punishment of imprisonment for a fortnight, awarded - Rule made absolute.
