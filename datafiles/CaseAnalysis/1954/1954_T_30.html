 Raj Rajendra Malojirao Shitole v State of Madhya Bharat and others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 21; AIR 1954 SC 259; 1954 SCJ 274; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 748

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Madhya Bharat Abolition of Jagirs Act, 1951, ss. 3, 3(1), 4(1) and 4(1)(g) - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 385, 382, 379, 3, 32 and 226 - (A) What is intent and purpose of arts. 379, 382 and 385 of Constitution - Held, intent was to give recognition to and invest powers conferred by Constitution in those bodies or authorities or Houses of Legislature which were actually functioning before 26-01-1950 - This was done to provide definiteness and avoid disputes during interim period - Body actually functioning immediately before commencement of Constitution would be body that would exercise powers and perform duties conferred by provisions of Constitution on body in Constitution - Procedure was founded upon considerations of policy and necessity - Fully justified - (B) Whether a defect in formation of interim legislative assembly of Madhya Bharat invalidated Madhya Bharat Abolition of Jagirs Act, 1951? - Further held, assembly was invested by provisions of Constitution - Even if assembly was not formed in strict compliance with provisions indicated in sch. 4 of covenant its defective formation does not affect constitutionality of Madhya Bharat Abolition of Jagirs Act, 1951 - Constitution has given recognition to and conferred powers on, assembly under art. 385 - Appeal dismissed.
