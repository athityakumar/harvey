 Dinabandhu Sahu v Jadumoni Mangaraj and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1954 SC 411; 1954 SCJ 605; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o. 6 r. 15(2) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 83(1), 85, 90(4), 117 and 123 - Limitation Act, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether Election Commission had sufficient ground for excusing delay, and whether it could be done otherwise than by personal appearance of petitioner ? - Held, yes - Election Commission can condone delay - Delay can be condoned suo-motu - (B) Whether in case of defects in verification of petition, it is liable to be dismissed by Election Commission or, can Election Commission permit it to be amended ? - Further held, no express power on Election Commission to permit amendment of verification - It is discretion of EC to either dismiss petition for defective verification or not - Verification allowed - Appeal dismissed.
