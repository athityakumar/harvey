 Himmatlal Harilal Mehtav v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 1954 SC 403; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 690; 1954 SCJ 445; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1122; 1954 (5) STC 115

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(g), explanation 2 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 286, 301 and 304 - (A) Validity of s. 2(g), explanation 2 of 1947, Act - (B) Writ of Mandamus u/art. 226 of Constitution - Threat of using coercive machinery against applicant - Another remedy under impugned Act - Held, threat under provisions declared to be ultra vires - Mandamus can issue - Alternative remedy inadequate - Appeal allowed.
