 Sales Tax Officer, Pilibhit v Messrs. Budh Prakash Jai Prakash

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1954 SC 459; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 124; 1954 SCJ 573; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 243; 1954 (5) STC 193

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Trade

Keywords: Supply Of Goods

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Trade - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2 and 3B - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - (A) Whether imposition of sales tax is valid on an agreement to sell? - Held, property passes only on sale and not agreement to sell - It was used in legislation both England and India - It authorises imposition of a tax only when there is a completed sale involving transfer of title, and not when there is a mere agreement to sell - State Legislature cannot by, enlarging definition of 'sale' as including forward contracts, arrogate to itself a power which is not conferred upon it by Constitution - Definition of sale in s. 2(h) of Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 was declared ultra vires - (B) Whether damages for breach of a contract of sale are liable to be assessed to Sales Tax? - Further held, position therefore is that a liability to be assessed to sales tax can arise only if there is a completed sale under which price is paid or is payable and not when there is only an agreement to sell or a breach of such contract - Imposition of tax on damages for breach of contract is contrary to principles - Appeal dismissed.
