Pandurang, and Others v State of Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 173; AIR 1955 SC 216; 1955 CRLJ 572; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1083

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 377, 378 - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 34, 302, 326 - Three appellants were prosecuted for murder - Convicted and sentenced to death u/s. 302 - Held, three sets of committal proceedings, usual questioning by police and then proceedings in Sessions Court, it is not surprising that these simple rustics got confused and not remembered in minute detail and exactly what they had said from stage to stage, major part of their story hangs together remarkably well despite many attempts to trip them in cross-examination in various Courts - Two eye-witnesses gave substantially the same version of what they saw of assault and they can be relied upon - Medical evidence shows that injury that caused death was one on neck, all eye-witnesses agreed that 'B' was responsible - Conviction u/s. 302 upheld - 'T' caused fatal injury therefore, his conviction u/s. 302 justified - 'P' case falls u/s. 326, conviction u/s. 302 set aside - Sentences of death converted to transportation for life because of difference of opinion in High Court - Order accordingly.
