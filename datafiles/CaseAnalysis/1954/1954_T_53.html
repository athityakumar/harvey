 Durga Shankar Mehta v Thakur Raghuraj Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1954 SC 520; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 385; 1954 SCJ 723; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 267

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 66, 81, 100(1)(c), 100(2)(c) and 105 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 173, and 329(b) - Elected candidate under 25 years of age - Not qualified to be elected to a seat in Legislative Assembly of a State u/art. 173 - Acceptance of nomination by Returning Officer amounted to an improper acceptance of nomination - Whether present case comes within purview of s. 100(1)(c) of Act or s. 100(2)(c)? - Held, s. 100(2)(c) applies. - It amounts to holding an election without complying with provisions of Constitution - Fundamental disability of candidate to stand for election - When a candidate appears to be properly qualified on face of electoral roll and nomination paper and no objection is raised thereto, Returning Officer has no alternative but to accept nomination - Election of candidate in question is only to be declared as void - Appeal partly allowed.
