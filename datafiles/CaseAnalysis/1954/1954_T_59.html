 Vice-Chancellor, Utkaluniversity and Others v S. K. Ghosh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1954 SC 217; 1954 SCJ 252; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Whether resolution to cancel examination and reconduct same is valid despite lack of notice to one member of syndicate regarding listing of matter during meetings - Held, High Court right, in invalidating resolutions and issuing mandamus directing syndicate to take steps for publication of results - (B) Whether HC right in conducting itself as a court of appeal while ruling u/art. 226 for mandamus - Further held, need for notice is not inflexible - It depends on facts - Thus, if a person who was not noticed appears at meeting and waives irregularity, defect is cured; so also when a person is too far away to be reached in time to enable him to communicate with Committee before meeting: sending of a notice is then excused - Relied on, King v Pulsford(108 E R 1073), La Compagnie De Mayville v Whitley([1896] 1 Ch 7881896 (1) Ch 788 ), and Parker and Cooper Ltd v Reading([1926] 1 Ch 9751926 (1) Ch 975) - Substance is more important than form and if there is substantial compliance with spirit and substance of law - An unessential defect in form should not defeat what is otherwise a proper and valid resolution - Thus, HC wrong in holding resolution invalid - HC unjustified in conducting itself as court of appeal - Discretion of authorities entrusted by law cannot be substituted by that of HC - Appeal allowed.
