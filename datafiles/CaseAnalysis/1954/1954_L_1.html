 Jumuna Prasad Mukhariya and Others v Lachhi Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1954 SC 686; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 711; 1954 SCJ 835; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a) and 245(1) - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(5) - (A)Whether Supreme Court as a court of appeal has jurisdiction to consider conclusions of fact - Held, under law decision of tribunal is meant to be final - Supreme Court will only interfere when there is some glaring error which has resulted in a substantial miscarriage of justice - In a special appeal against decision of an Election Tribunal Supreme Court, as a court of appeal will not examine conclusions of fact arrived at by Tribunal - (B) Whether systematic appeal of a class of voters amounts to a corrupt practice - Further held, systematic appeal for votes on basis of caste alone constitutes a minor corrupt practice u/s. 124(5) -(1) Whether issuing of leaflet and poster not bearing name of printer on them amounts to an illegal practice - Further held, this is a question of fact - In this case, it amounts to illegal practice - Supreme Court will not interfere with findings of Election Tribunal in special appeal - (2) Whether election of a candidate held for illegal practices is void - Further held, election of such a candidate is void u/s. 100(2)(b) - It is not necessary to prove that result of election was materially affected - All that needs to be proved is that a corrupt practice has been committed -(3) Whether an election procured by a minor corrupt and illegal practice void? - Further held, it is not necessary to enquire whether election has been materially affected - Use of illegal practice is a reason sufficient in itself for declaring election void -(C) Whether ss. 123(5) and 124(5), Representation of People Act, 1951 are ultra vires art. 19(1)(a)? - Further held, these sections do not interfere with a citizen's fundamental right to freedom of speech - They merely prescribe conditions which must be observed if one wants to enter Parliament - Right to stand as a candidate and contest an election is a statutory right guided by conditions laid down by statute - To be elected members of Parliament is not a fundamental right - These sections are not ultra vires of art. 19(1)(a) - (D) Whether declaration u/s. 101(b) is mandatory ? - Further held, declaration must be made - However, tribunal should not enter into speculation - Appeal partly allowed.
