 Waryam Singh and Another v Amarnath and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1954 SC 215; [1954] S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13(2)(i) - Govt. of India Act, 1915, s. 107 - Appellants, tenants of a certain shop premises - Appellants fell into arrears with the payments of rents due for the years 1948 and 1949 and the respondents made applications to the Rent Controller for eviction - Whether Judicial Commr. of Himachal Pradesh was correct in exercise of powers u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution of India, 1950 setting aside orders of subordinate courts refusing to make an order of ejectment against tenants in order to keep subordinate courts within bounds of authority? - Held, art. 227 (1) r/w art. 241 of the Constitution of India, 1950 confers on Court of Judicial Commr. power of superintendence over such Tribunals - Preponderance of judicial opinion in India was that s. 107 of the Act of 1915 which was similar in terms to s. 15 of High Courts Act, 1861, gave a power of judicial superintendence to High Court apart from and independently of provisions of other laws conferring revisional jurisdiction on HC - S. 224(2) of 1935 Act has been omitted from art. 227 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Judicial superintendence has therefore been restored - This power of superintendence conferred by art. 227 of the Constitution of India, 1950 is to be exercised most sparingly and only in appropriate cases in order to keep Subordinate Courts within bounds of their authority and not for correcting mere errors - 'Dalmia Jain ays Ltd v Sukumar Mukherjee', AIR 1951 Cal 193, 1951 AIR(Cal) 193(SB) relied on - Its a case which called for an interference by Court of Judicial Commr. and it acted quite properly in doing so - Appeal dismissed.
