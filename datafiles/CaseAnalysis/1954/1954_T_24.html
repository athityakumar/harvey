Kalidas Dhanjibhai v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 10; AIR 1955 SC 62; 1955 (57) BomLR 702; 1955 CRLJ 193; 1954 (2) LLJ 694; 1955 SCJ 150; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 887

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Burden Of Proof - Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948, rr. (6), 18(5), ss. 2(27), 5, 52 (f), 7, 7(3) - Construction - Validity - Appellant was owner of small establishment - Appellant employs three workers - No buying or selling was done on premises - Small fine of Rs. 50 (Rs. 25 on each of two counts) had been imposed for couple of breaches u/s. 52 (f) of the Act r/w r.18(5) and (6) of Rules framed under the Act - Question was whether concern of this nature was 'shop' within meaning of s. 2(27) of the Act - Magistrate held that it was not and so acquitted - HC on appeal against acquittal, held it was and convicted - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, appellant applied for registration under Act and in statement made u/s. 7 of the Act called his establishment 'workshop' and described nature of his business as 'factory' - HC considered that this imported admission that his establishment was 'shop' because of use of word 'shop' in 'workshop' - Thatmight have raised inference of fact against appellant had nothing else been known but when facts were fully set out and admitted appellant's opinion about legal effect of those facts was of no consequence in construing section - Placing of burden of proof on appellant was wrong, in criminal case, because of admission - HC also adverted to fact that though appellant's concern was registered as 'shop' he made no protest and did not have recourse to s. 7(3) of the Act - Hence, s. 7(3)of the Act had no application - Appeal allowed.
