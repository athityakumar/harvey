 Ratilal Panachand Gandhi v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1954 SC 388; 1954 (56) BomLR 1184; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 718; 1954 SCJ 480; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1055

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 92 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25, 26, 132(1) and 226 - Bombay Public Trust Act 1950, ss. 3, 9, 10, 11, 18, 32, 33, 34, 35, 37, 44, 47, 48, 50, 55, 56, 57, 58, 60, 62, and 66 - Parsi Public Trusts Registration Act, 1936 - Question on constitutional validity of the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950? - Held, s. 44 relates to the appointment of the Charity Commr. as a trustee of any public trust by the court without any reservation in regard to religious institutions like temples and Maths is unconstitutional, and must be held to be void - The provisions of cls. (3) to (6) of s. 47 to the extent that they relate to the appointment of the Charity Commr. as a trustee of a religious trust like temple and Math are invalid - S. 50 is a substitute for s. 92 CPC, 1908 and relates to suits in connection with public trusts - No question of infraction of any fundamental right - S. 58 is not ultra vires of the State Legislature, it is not a tax but a fee which comes within the purview of entry 47 of List III in sch.7 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Appeals Allowed.
