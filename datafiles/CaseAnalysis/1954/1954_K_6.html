Kapur Singh v State of Pepsu

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1956 SC 654; 1956 CRLJ 1265

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Sentencing - Death sentence - Appellant inflicted as many as 18 injuries on arms and legs of deceased with a gandasa - Out of all injuries which were thus inflicted none was inflicted on a vital part of body - Held, fact that no injury was inflicted on any vital part of body of deceased goes to show in circumstances of this case that intention of appellant was not to kill deceased outright - Proper section under which appellant should have been convicted was s. 304(1) and not s. 302 - Appeal allowed.
