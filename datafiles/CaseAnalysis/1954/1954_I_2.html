 India United Mills Limited v Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 162; AIR 1955 SC 79; [1955] 27 ITR 20; 1955 SCJ 36; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, ss. 15, 26(3) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 (1) - Revised Assessment - Excess Profits Tax - Chargeable Accounting Period - Bad Debts - Business Income or Loss - Appellant/Company/Assesses, applied for relief u/s. 26(3) of Act, in proceedings for assessment of excess profits - On an application made through Excess Profits Tax Officer, Respondent/Central Board of Revenue was satisfied that computation in accordance with provisions of buildings, plant or machinery which would not be required for purposes of business after termination of present hostilities - Respondent directed that such allowances should be made in respect of circumstances, in computing profits of chargeable accounting period, such allowance to be inclusive of all depreciation allowable for excess profits tax purpose during chargeable accounting period as it thinks just - Notices were issued u/s. 15 of Act, and for reopening assessment - Lower Authority, revised assessments for periods and found that there were no grounds for granting relief to appellants u/s. 26(3) of Act - Order was confirmed on appeal by Appellate Assistant Commissioner, but was reversed by Appellate Tribunal -Whether revised assessments for chargeable accounting periods were liable to be cancelled as Excess Profits Tax Officer had erred in invoking S. 15 of Act and excessive relief could be granted - Held, Excess Profits Tax Officer had, no power to revise assessment for the accounting periods - Appellant obtained relief u/s. 26(3) of the Act for buildings, plant and machinery would not be fit for use after war - As appellant continued to use machinery in business after termination of war, basis of relief granted disappeared, and assessable profits for chargeable accounting periods was bad and not subjected of excessive relief - When Excess Profits Tax Officer found that appellant to whom relief had been granted u/s. 26(3) had utilized buildings, plant or machinery in business after termination of war, he was entitled to proceed u/s. 15 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
