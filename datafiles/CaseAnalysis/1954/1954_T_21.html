 Budhan Choudhry and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 4; AIR 1955 SC 191; 1955 ALJ 309; 1955 CRLJ 371; 1955 SCJ 163; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 28, 30 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143 and 366 - Abducted Persons (Recovery And Restoration) Act, 1949 - (A) What is test for determining reasonable classification u/art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, there must be a nexus between basis of classification and object of Act under consideration - (B) Whether protection under art. 14 is applicable only to substantive law? - Further held, art. 14 condemns discrimination not only by a substantive law but also by a law of procedure - (C) Whether s. 30 of CrPC, 1898 is violative of art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Further held, section only authorises State Govt. to invest certain Magistrate with power to try all offences not punishable with death and this authority can be exercised only in specified places - No discrimination in section itself - Appeal dismissed.
