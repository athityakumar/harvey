Chhote Khan, Deceased, Represented By His Son, Harmat, and Others v Mal Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1954 SC 575; 1954 SCJ 577; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 60

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Punjab Land Revenue Act,1887

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887, s. 117 - Possession of Land - Declaration - Right to Claim Partition - Settlement Deed - Appellants/plaintiffs claimed a declaration along with respondents/defendants Nos. 17 to 19 were owners in possession of land - Respondents Nos. 1 to 16 had no right to claim partition of land and they were entitled only to land possessed by them without payment of land revenue - Respondents/defendants denied that any agreement or condition restricting their right to partition was binding after expiry of term of Settlement - It could not operate as a bar to their claim to partition - Assistant Collector tried suit u/s. 117 of 1887 Act and decreed claim - Respondents/defendants were entitled only to get produce of land in their possession without payment of land revenue and had no interest in remaining land - Decree was reversed on appeal, by HC holding that respondents were entitled to 4/5th share as proprietors, and original agreement were binding so long as Settlements and after that, agreement was not binding on respondent/defendants - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondents were entitled to 4/5th share as proprietors, and original agreement was binding on parties - Held, partition was a right incident to ownership of property and once respondents were hold as co-owners, their right to partition could not be resisted - Settlements ceased to operate and which were made under orders of Collector had no value, when respondents did not agree to its being incorporated - Respondents were joint owners and not mere cultivators who were not entitled to claim partition of property - Regarding settlement, Court found that appellants alone could get land partitioned in five equal shares but descendants of other four co-sharers - Suit had no substance and was rightly rejected by HC, on ground that possession was under an arrangement between co-sharers and no question of adverse possession could arise - Appeal dismissed.
