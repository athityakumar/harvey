 M Y Shareef And Another v The Hon'ble Judges Of The Nagpur High Court

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1955 SC 19; 1955 CRLJ 133; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 13; 1955 SCJ 54; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Municipalities & Local Governments - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Contempt of Court - Counsel signing applications or pleadings containing matter scandalizing Court - (A) Whether apology asked for by an advocate or a counsel is a defence u/s. 4 of Contempt of Courts Act, 1952? - There cannot be both justification and an apology, two are incompatible - Held, an apology is not a weapon of defense to purge guilty of their offence; nor is it intended to operate as a universal panacea, but it is intended to be evidence of real contriteness - (B) Whether Supreme Court has a power to interfere with discretion of High Court u/art. 136 of Constitution? - Held, that it is not practice of Supreme Court in special leave cases and in exercise of its overriding powers to interfere with a matter which rests in discretion of HC - Except in very exceptional cases - Appeal allowed.
