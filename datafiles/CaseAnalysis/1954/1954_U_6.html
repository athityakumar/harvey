 S. A. Venkataraman v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1954 SC 375; 1954 CRLJ 993; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 702; 1954 SCJ 461; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Prohibition Act - Indian Civil Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20(2) and 311(2) - Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850, s. 3 - Civil Services Rules, r. 49 - Whether there has been a violation of fundamental right of petitioner u/art. 20(2) of Constitution which would justify issue of a writ for enforcement of same? - Held, in order to enable a citizen to invoke protection of art. 20(2) of Constitution, there must have been both prosecution and punishment in respect of same offence - At close of enquiry, Commr. has to submit a report to Govt. regarding his finding on each one of charges made - This is a mere expression of opinion and it lacks both finality and authoritativeness which are essential tests of a judicial pronouncement - Opinion is not even binding on Govt. - In an enquiry under Public Servants (Inquiries) Act of 1850, there is neither any question of investigating an offence in sense of an act or omission punishable by any law for time being in force, nor is there any question of imposing punishment prescribed by law which makes that act or omission an offence - No violation of art. 20(2) - Petition dismissed.
