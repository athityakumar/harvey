 Hari Vishnu Kamath v Ahmad Ishaque and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 212; AIR 1955 SC 233; 1972 CRLJ 643; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 157; 1955 SCJ 267; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1104

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Constitution - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97, 100(2)(c) - Rules, rr. 23, 28, 47(1)(a),(b),(c),(d), 47(4) and 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 and 329(b) - Election result between appellant and respondent No. 1 (R1) with a margin of 174 votes - Election petition by appellant - Ground, that in two of polling stations ballot papers did not have distinguishing marks prescribed u/r. 28 and that by reason of their improper reception, result of election had been materially affected, and thus votes counted in favour of R1 were liable to be rejected u/r. 47(1)(c) - Election Tribunal (ET) held that r. 47(1)(c) was mandatory and ballot papers in question found in box of R1 bearing wrong mark should not have been counted and further it held that result of election had not been materially affected by such erroneous reception of votes, and thus petition is to be dismissed - Writ petition preferred by appellant before High Court - (A) Whether art. 329(b) imposes limitation on HC's power u/art. 226? - Held, no - On a plain reading of art. 329(b), what is prohibited therein is 'initiation' of proceedings for settings aside an election otherwise than by an election petition presented to such authority and in such manner as provided therein - A suit for setting aside an election would be barred under this provision - (B) Whether, on presuming that there is jurisdiction u/art. 226 to HC to issue 'certiorari' against ET, HC is incapable of exercising it for reason that under Act, 1951? - Tribunal is an 'ad hoc' body set up for determination of a particular election petition, that it becomes functus officio' when it pronounces its decision, and that thereafter there is no authority in existence to which writ could be issued - Writ is directed to body or officer whose determination is to be reviewed, or 'to any other person having custody of record or other papers to be certified' - Held, application of appellant for a writ of 'certiorari' and for other reliefs was maintainable u/art. 226 and 227 of Constitution of India, 1950 - (C) In facts of case whether there are proper grounds for issue of 'certiorari'? - (1) Proposition of law as to when writ of 'certiorari' - Firstly, for correcting errors of jurisdiction, secondly when Tribunal acts in violation of principle of natural justice, thirdly, Court issuing such writ exercise supervisory and not appellate jurisdiction - Thus Court will not review findings of fact reached by Inferior Court or Tribunal, even if they be erroneous - (2) Whether a writ can be issued, when decision of inferior Court or Tribunal is erroneous in law? - Yes, but it is essential that it should be something more than a mere error; it must be one which must be manifest on face of record - (3) What is error on face of record? - It cannot be defined precisely or exhaustively, there being an element of indefiniteness inherent in its very nature, and it must be left to be determined judicially on facts of each case - (4) Finding of Tribunal as to that r. 47(1)(c) is mandatory and it has been contravened, upheld - (5) Scope of enquiry u/s. 100(2)(c) - Two cumulative conditions, firstly inter alia improper reception or refusal of a vote or reception of vote which is void and secondly that as a consequence thereof result of election had been materially affected - Held, certiorari lies in facts of case, as ET had jurisdiction to decide whether on a construction of s. 100(2)(c) it could go into fact of breach of r. 23, and if it committed an error, it was an error in exercise of its jurisdiction and not in assumption thereof, but error is manifest on face of record, and calls for interference in 'certiorari' - (C) Whether on rejecting election of R1, appellant is to be declared elected? - Held, no - Combined effect of ss. 97 and 100(2)(c) is that there is no valid election - (D) What order to be passed? - To quash decision of ET and remove it out of way by 'certiorari' u/art. 226 and to set aside election of R1 in exercise of powers conferred u/art. 227 - Whole election set aside - Fresh election to be conducted - Appeal allowed.
