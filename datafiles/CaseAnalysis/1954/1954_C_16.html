E. D. Sassoon and Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay Cit. and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1954 SC 470; [1954] 26 ITR 27; 1954 SCJ 771; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(1) - Transfer of Property Act,1882 - Agreement - Commission - Income - Escaped assessment - Appellants were Managing Agents of Sassoon under Agreements - Shareholders of respective companies to agreements for transfer was duly obtained and Managing Agencies were ultimately transferred to respective transferees - Accounts of Managing Agency commission payable by respective Companies to Managing Agents for year 1943 were made up in year 1944 and Messrs - Income-tax Officer and Excess Profits Tax Officer discovered that amounts of Managing Agency commission earned by Sassoons prior to dates of respective transfers were not brought to tax and issued notices u/s.34 of 1961 Act and s.15 of 1940 Act, upon Sassoons on ground that their income from Managing Agency had escaped assessment - Sassoons Income Tax Officer and Excess Profits Tax Officer determined those sums as their escaped incomes and assessed them accordingly - Hence instant appeal - Whether income accrued in share by appellant was taxable income - Held, continuous and successive functioning by both assignor and assignee under Managing Agency Agreement was effective source of year's income - Income accrued on completion of year and was joint income of both assignor and assignee - Prior assignments in course of year operated as assignments of that future right to a share of income - Share in that income accrued to Sassoons on completion of year remained taxable income of Sassoons and they were rightly taxed - Appeal disposed of.
