Bhagat Ram v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1954 SC 621; 1954 CRLJ 1645

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 342 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409 and 420 - Appeal against a common judgment of High Court - Appellant convicted by First Class Magistrate, at two separate trials - Convictions confirmed by Session Judge on appeal, and by HC in revision - Circumstantial evidence - Whether conviction is justified? - Held, in cases where evidence is of circumstantial nature guilt to be drawn should be fully established and all facts so established should be consistent only with hypothesis of guilt of accused - Hanumant v State of Madhya Pradesh relied on - Conviction set aside - Offences charged are set aside - Appeals allowed.
