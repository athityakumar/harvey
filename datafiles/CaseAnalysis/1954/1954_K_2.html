 K.S. Rashid and Son v Income Tax Investigation Commission and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 20; AIR 1954 SC 207; [1954] 25 ITR 167; 1954 SCJ 264; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 738

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India,1950, arts. 133(1), 225, 226 ,227 - Taxation of Income(Investigation Commission) Act,1947,ss.5, 5(3),9 - Partnership - Dissolution - Evasion of payment - Discretionary jurisdiction - Partnership firm was dissolved on death of a partner and new firm was started bearing same name, with two surviving partners - Central Govt. referred cases of this firm to Income Tax Investigation Commission for enquiry on evasion of payment of income-tax - Appellants complaint, that contrary to provisions of Act respondent extended his investigations to period up to date of income-tax assessment - Petition embodying complaint made to authorized official but no order was passed - Appellants claimed that proceedings before Commission might be revised u/art.277 of Constitution - Four miscellaneous petitions filed on behalf of appellants for writ of prohibition was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants entitled to relief u/art.226 of Constitution - Held, appellants had already availed themselves of remedy provided for in s.8(5) of 1947 Act and that reference had made to HC in terms of that provision which was awaiting decision - Remedy provided in art.226 of Constitution was discretionary remedy - Hence, it would not be proper to allow appellants to invoke discretionary jurisdiction u/art.226 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
