Hem Singh and Another v Harnam Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1954 SC 581; 1954 SCJ 566; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Customary Law of Gurdaspur - Adoption - Gill Tats of village Gillanwali, District Gurdaspur (Punjab) - Whether under Customary Law applicable to Gill Jats of village Gillanwali, H could only adopt a near collateral and G being a distant collateral his adoption was invalid? - Under Hindu Law, adoption is primarily a religious act intended to confer spiritual benefit on adopter and some of rules have, therefore, been held to be mandatory and compliance with them regarded as a condition of validity of adoption - Held, under Customary Law in Punjab, adoption is secular in character, object being to appoint an heir and rules relating to ceremonies and to preferences in selection have to be held to be directory and adoptions made in disregard of them are not invalid - Appeal dismissed.
