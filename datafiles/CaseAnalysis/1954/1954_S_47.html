Ramkishan Mithanlal Sharma and Others v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 172; AIR 1955 SC 104; 1955 CRLJ 196; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 66; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Criminal - Bombay City Police Act 1902, s. 63 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 162, 167, 423 and 537 - Indian Evidence Act, ss. 27 and 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 397 and 396 - Appellants convicted u/s. 397 r/w s. 395 of IPC, 1860 with having committed dacoity and used deadly weapons at time of committing same and were also charged u/s. 396 of IPC, 1860 with having committed murder at same time and place and in course of same transaction while committing said dacoity - Challenged - (A) Whether evidence in regard to test identification parades (TIP) held at instance of police and under their active supervision was hit by s. 162 of CrPC, 1898? - S. 167(2) could only apply to those rights, privileges, obligations or liabilities already acquired, accrued or incurred under Bombay City Police Act 4 of 1902 before date of its repeal - Test identification parades in regard to accused 1 and 2 were all held prior to 01-08-1951 and no question could therefore arise as to provisions of s. 162 of CrPC, 1898 being applicable to evidence in regard to those parades - TIP in regard to accused 4 however were held after 01-08-1951 between 16th January and 22nd January 1952 - These parades are held by police in course of their investigation for purpose of enabling witnesses to identify properties which are subject-matter of offence or to identify persons who are concerned in offence - They are not held merely for purpose of identifying property or persons irrespective of their connection with offence - Further held, whole of identification parades were directed and supervised by police officers and Panch witnesses took a minor part in same and were there only for purpose of guaranteeing that requirements of law in regard to holding of identification parades were satisfied - Under these circumstances that statement, if any, involved in process of identification were statements made by identifiers to Panch witnesses and not to police officers as otherwise it will be easy for police officers to circumvent provisions of s. 162 by formally asking Panch witnesses to be present and contending that statements, if any, made by identifies were to Punch witnesses and not to themselves - TIP in regard to accused 4 attracted operation of s. 162 and evidence of identification at those parades was inadmissible against accused 4 - (B) Whether statement of police officer that it was 'at instance of' or 'in consequence of certain statement by' accused that certain discoveries were made was hit by s. 27 of Indian Evidence Act? - Held on a bare reading of terms of section it appears that what is allowed to be proved is information or such part thereof as relates distinctly to fact thereby discovered - Information would consist of a statement made by accused to police officer and police officer is obviously precluded from proving information or part thereof unless it comes within four corners of section - If police officer wants to prove information or a part thereof, Court would have to consider whether it relates distinctly to fact thereby discovered and allow proof thereof only if that condition was satisfied - If, however, police Officer does not want to prove information or any part thereof, s. 27 does not come into operation at all - Evidence of police officer would no doubt go to show that accused knew of existence of fact discovered in consequence of information given by him - But that would not necessarily show his direct connection with offence - It would merely be a link in chain of evidence which taken along with other pieces of evidence might go to establish his connection therewith - Further held, verdict of jury against accused 1 and 2 is concerned same was not vitiated either by admission of inadmissible evidence or by any misdirection or non-direction - As regards accused 4 however there has been an error of law in admitting evidence of TIP in regard to him - Order accordingly.
