 Dewan Bahadur Seth Gopal Das Mohta v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1955 SC 1; [1954] 26 ITR 722; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 19; 1955 SCJ 60; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indirect Tax - Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, s. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 31, 32 and 19(1)(f) - Whether petition u/art. 32 alleging that entire proceedings under Act were wholly illegal, ultra vires, void and unconstitutional, is sustainable? - Held, whatever tax petitioner has already paid, or whatever is still recoverable from him, is being recovered on basis of settlement proposed by him and accepted by Central Govt. - Because of his request for a settlement no assessment was made against him by following whole of procedure of Act - In this situation unless and until petitioner can establish that his consent was improperly procured and that he is not bound thereby he cannot complain that any of his fundamental rights has been contravened for which he can claim relief u/art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950 - Petition dismissed.
