Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Mysore Chromite Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1955 SC 98; [1955] 27 ITR 128; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 98; 1955 SCJ 185; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 4 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 23 and 25 - Sale at unascertained or future goods - Buyer outside India - Assessment of tax - (A)What is the distinction between s. 23 and s. 25 of Sale of Goods Act, 1930?- Held, that s. 23 of Sale of Goods Act lays down that where there is a contract for sale of unascertained or future goods by description and goods of that description and in a deliverable state are unconditionally appropriated to contract, either by seller with assent of buyer or by buyer with assent of seller property, in goods thereupon passes to buyer - Requirement of section is not only that there shall be appropriation of goods to contract but that such appropriation must be made unconditionally where as in s.25 of that Act which provides that where there is a contract for sale of specific goods or where goods are subsequently appropriated to contract, seller may, by terms of contract or appropriation, reserve right of disposal of goods until certain conditions are fulfilled - (B) Whether in present case, sale and profits derived by assessee took place outside British India? - Held that in London, where bill of lading was handed over to buyers' bank against acceptance of relative bill of exchange; that sales took place outside British India - Profits derived from such sales arose outside British India - Appeal dismissed.
