 Rajnarain Singh v Chairman, Patna Administration Committee, Patna, and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 133; AIR 1954 SC 569; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 344; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Repeal

Summary: Administrative - Municipalities & Local Governments - Bengal Municipal Act, 1884 - Patna Administration Act, 1915, ss. 3(1)(f), 5 and 6(b) - Patna Administration (Amendment) Act, 1928, s. 4 - Bihar and Orissa Municipal Act, 1922, ss. 4, 5 and 6 - Appellant and members of an association resided in an area which was originally outside municipal limits of Patna and was not liable to municipal taxation - Area was brought within municipal limits vide Notification D/- 18-04-1951 and subjected to municipal taxation - Appellant and members of association were called to pay taxes for respective period - Challenged - Act 1915 had not created any Municipality but it empowered Local Govt. to create new Municipality by extending provisions of Bengal Act to it - Local Govt. exercised its power and created municipality for Patna capital, by name 'Patna Administration Committee', which Court categorises as 'Patna Administration' - As Bengal Act was not as such applicable to Patna administration but only through exercise of power by Local Govt. u/s. 3 of Act 1915, it remained in that form - Local Govt. was not empowered to apply any other Act except Bengal Act to Patna administration, so for purpose of applying Act, 1922, Legislature passed Amendment Act, 1928 so that Local Govt. may now apply provision of Act, 1922 also - Local Govt. exercised its power - In year 1931 Governor repealed all actions done vide notifications issued under Bengal Act and 1922 Act and instead applied certain sections of Act, 1922 and certain modified sections of Act, 1922 to Patna administration - Thus in effect fresh Municipal Code was made applicable to Patna administration - Constitution came into force - As capital of province was witnessing expansion, Patna village was sought to be brought into jurisdiction of Patna administration(capital) - Impugned notification was issued u/s. 3(1)(f) of Act, 1915 - Essential legislative function consists in determination or choosing of legislative policy and of formally enacting that policy into a binding rule of conduct - To alter essential character of an Act or to change it in material particulars is to legislate, and that, namely power to legislate, all authorities are agreed, cannot be delegated by a Legislature which is not unfettered - Modification can be done by executive authority either in existing or future laws but not in any essential feature - Modification cannot include change of policy - Policy u/ss. 3, 4 and 5 of 1922, Act, which was extended to Patna administration and Patna Village exercising power u/s. 3(1)(f) of Act, 1915 - Policy was, no municipality competent to tax shall be thrust upon a locality without giving its inhabitants a chance of being heard and of being given an opportunity to object - Not only could an entire enactment with modification be extended but also a part of one - Held, notification so far as picking and modifying section(s) of 1922 Act is concerned, upheld as intra vires - But so far as policy underlying Act, 1922 is concerned, it was held to be ultra vires, as 'notification of 23-04-1951 does, effect a radical change in policy of Act, therefore, it travels beyond authority which, s. 3(1)(f) confers and consequently it is ultra vires' - Held further, s. 3(1)(f) of Act, 1915 is intra vires - Appeal allowed.
