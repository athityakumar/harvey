Biswabhusan Naik v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1954 SC 359; 1954 CRLJ 1002; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 79; 1954 SCJ 537; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Socio-Economic - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 6, 5(3) and 4(1)(a) - (A) What are essential requirements of sanction under s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947?- Held, it is not necessary for sanction under Prevention of Corruption Act to be in any particular form or in writing or for it to set out facts in respect of which it is given - Facts set out were found to exist and explanation of accused rejected as unsatisfactory - Attracted s. 5(3) - The Court was bound to presume that accused was guilty u/s. 5(2) - No particulars need be set out in charge as offence u/s. 5(1)(a) does not consist of individual acts of bribe taking as in s. 161 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 but is of general character - Accused had not been prejudiced because he knew everything that was being urged against him and led evidence to refute facts on which prosecution relied - Appeal allowed.
