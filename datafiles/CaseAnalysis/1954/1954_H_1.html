H. N. Rishbud and Inder Singh v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1955 SC 196; 1955 CRLJ 526; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 173; 1955 SCJ 283; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Service - Prevention of Corruption (Second Amendment) Act, 1952, s. 5(2), (4), 5-A - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 5, 5(2), 54, 154, 162, 168, 170, 173, 190, 193, 195 to 199, 537, 551, 155, 156(1), (2) and (3), sch 11, item 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 161, 165, 165-A and 420 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 3, 4 and 5(1) to (5) - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - ss. 7 and 5(2) - (A) Whether provision of 1947 Act which provides investigation into offences specified u/s. 5(4) to be conducted by a police officer of rank not lower than a Deputy Superintendent of Police without specific order of Magistrate is directory or mandatory? - When a statutory provision enjoins that investigation shall be made by a police officer of not less than a certain rank, unless specifically empowered by a Magistrate in that behalf, notwithstanding anything to contrary in CrPC, 1898 it is clearly implicit therein that investigation should be conducted by officer of appropriate rank - Policy apparently underlying it is reasonably clear that said provision must be taken to be mandatory - Investigation conducted in violation thereof bears stamp of illegality - (B) Whether invalid investigation effects competence and jurisdiction of trial court? - If cognizance is in fact taken, on a police report vitiated by breach of a mandatory provision relating to investigation, there can be no doubt that result of trial which follows it cannot be set aside unless illegality in investigation can be shown to have brought about a miscarriage of justice - Held, it is not possible to say that cognizance on an invalid police report is prohibited and is therefore a nullity - Thus, an illegality committed in course of investigation does not affect competence and jurisdiction of Court for trial - Criminal Appeals Nos. 96 and 97 of 1954 allowed and Criminal Appeal No. 95 of 1954 dismissed.
