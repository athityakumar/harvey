Rao Shiv Bahadur Singh and Another v State of Vindhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 157; AIR 1954 SC 322; 1954 CRLJ 910; 1954 SCJ 362; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1098

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 161, 465 and 466 - Appellant no. 1, Minister of Industries - Appellant no. 2 was Secy to Govt. of Commerce and Industries Deptt - Appellant no. 1 charged and convicted u/s 120- Appellant no. 2 u/ss. 120-B and 161 - Acquittal - On appeal, both of them convicted - Appeal - Supreme Court observed that, Magistrates will not be employed by police authorities in manner it was done by Special Police Establishment in this case - Independence of judiciary is a priceless treasure to be cherished and safeguarded at all costs against predatory activities of this character and it is of essence that public confidence in independence of judiciary should not be undermined by any such tactics adopted by executive authorities - Court, therefore, eliminated whole of evidence given by Addl. District Magistrate - Held, guilt of appellant no. 1 proved relying solely on testimony of two independent witnesses - Appeal of appellant no. 1 dismissed and appeal of appellant no. 2 allowed.
