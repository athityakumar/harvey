 Suraj Mall Mohta and Company v A. V. Visvanatha Sastri And another

Supreme Court of India

28 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 48; AIR 1954 SC 545; [1954] 26 ITR 1; 1954 SCJ 611; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 448

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure -Evidence Act, 1891- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Companies Act, 1913 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, ss. 5(1), 5(4), 6, 7 and 8 - Whether certain sections of Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act have become void from date of commencement of Constitution by reason of contravening guarantee of equal protection of laws and of equality before law, or whether impugned provisions of Act are based on a valid classification which is rational in view of objects of Act? -Whether s. 6(5) of Act offends against art. 20 (3) of Constitution? - Whether procedure prescribed by Act XXX of 1947 for discovering concealed profits of those who have evaded payment of taxation on their income is substantially different and prejudicial to assessees than procedure prescribed in Indian Income-tax Act by s. 34 - Art. 14 assures to everyone same rules of evidence and modes of procedure in similar circumstances and this principle does not mean that every law must have universal application for all persons who are not by nature, attainment or circumstance, in same position - State can by classification determine who should be regarded as

a class for purposes of legislation and in relation to a law enacted on a particular subject, but classification permissible must be based on some real and substantial distinction bearing a just and reasonable relation to objects sought to be attained and cannot be made arbitrarily and without any substantial basis - If persons dealt with by impugned Act are deprived of substantial and valuable privileges which they would otherwise have if they were dealt with under Indian Income-Tax Act, in that situation it is no defence to say that discriminatory procedure also advances course of justice - Matter has to be judged from point of view of ordinary reasonable man and not from point of view of Govt. - Held, s. 5(4) and procedure prescribed by impugned Act in so far as affects persons proceeded against under that sub-section being a piece of discriminatory legislation offends against provisions of art. 14 of Constitution and is thus void and unenforceable - Appropriate writ be issued.
