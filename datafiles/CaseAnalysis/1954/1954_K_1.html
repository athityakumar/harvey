 K. N. Guruswamy v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 45; AIR 1954 SC 592; [1955] S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: regulating the the sales of Excise Privileges.

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1) and 32 - Mysore Excise Act, 1901, ss. 16, r. I 1, r. II 8 - Regulating sales of Excise Privileges, r.10 - (A)Whether there lies a writ in case of improper cancellation of liquor license under Mysore Excise Act, 1901 - Held,rules for liquor licensing in State of Mysore do not tie down authorities to method of auction and tender - They have discretion under r. I 1 and can act 'otherwise' - However, due notice and publicity should be given of 'otherwise' method in a Govt. notification - Cancellation, though irregular, was proper - A right to license does not accrue from mere fact that contract had been knocked down in his favour - Thus, a writ cannot be maintained - (B) Whether it is necessary to give same meaning to same expression while interpreting a statutory provision? - Held, unless there is anything to indicate contrary, same word appearing in same section of same set of rules must be given same meaning - Appeal dismissed.
