Manilal Mohanlal Shah and Others v Sardar Sayed Ahmed Sayed Mahmad and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1954 SC 349; 1955 (57) BomLR 10; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; 1954 SCJ 509; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. XXI rr. 72, 84 and 85 - Execution of decree - Whether failure to make deposit under o. XXI, rr. 84 and 85, is only a material irregularity in sale which can only be set aside u/r. 90 or whether it is wholly void? - Held, r. 72 is directory, not mandatory - Provision regarding deposit of 25 per cent by purchaser other than decree-holder is mandatory as language of rule suggests - Provisions leave no doubt that unless deposit and payment are made as required by mandatory provisions of rules, there is no sale in eye of law right to own and possess property accrues to defaulting purchaser - Appeal dismissed.
