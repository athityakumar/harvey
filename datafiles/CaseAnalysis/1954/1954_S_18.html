Pandit Chunchun Jha v Sheikh Ebadat Ali and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1954 SC 345; 1954 ALJ 546; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 708; 1954 SCJ 469; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Mortgage By Conditional Sale

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58 - Bihar Tenancy Act, 1885, s. 40 - Whether given transaction is a mortgage by conditional sale or a sale outright with a condition of repurchase? - It is clear from amended s. 58 of Act that if sale and agreement to repurchase are embodied in separate documents, then transaction cannot be a mortgage whether documents are contemporaneously executed or not, but mere fact that there is only one document does not necessarily mean that it must be a mortgage and cannot be a sale; if condition of repurchase is embodied in document that effects or purports to effect sale, then it is a matter for construction which was meant; first point is to see whether there is an 'ostensible sale', i.e. a transaction which takes outward form of a sale, for essence of a mortgage by conditional sale is that though in substance it is a mortgage, it is couched in form of a sale with certain conditions attached - Held, on a fair construction, cl.(6) means that if money is paid within 2 years then possession will revert to executants with result that title which is already in them will continue to reside there and necessary consequence of that is that ostensible sale becomes void; similarly, cl.(7) can only mean that if money is not paid, then sale shall become absolute, therefore, matter falls squarely within ambit of s. 58(c); further, surrounding circumstances show that there was a relationship of debtor and creditor between parties existing at date of transaction, bulk of consideration went in satisfaction of mortgage of 06-05-1927, hence, seeing that deed takes form of a mortgage by conditional sale u/s. 58(c), it is legitimate to infer that relationship of debtor and creditor was intended to continue and deed is a mortgage by conditional sale u/s. 58(c) - Appeal allowed.
