 Mahant Sri Jagannath Ramanuj Das and Another v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 26; AIR 1954 SC 400; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 591; 1954 SCJ 329; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1046

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Family & Personal; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution -Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 25, 26, 27 and 32 - Government of India Act 1935, sch. 7 Entry 34 of List 11 - Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act 1951, s. 58 - Orissa Hindu Religious Endowments Act 1939, ss. 11, 14, 20, 28, 38, 39, 41, 46, 47, 49 and 50 - Grounds for validity of Act 1939 was challenged on specific grounds - Whether Act of 1939 was ultra vires Orissa legislature and for other consequential reliefs and whether hit by art. 27 of Constitution ? - It is secular administration of religious institutions that legislature seeks to control and object, as enunciated in Act, is to ensure that endowments attached to religious institutions are properly administered and their income is duly appropriated for purposes for which they were founded or exist - Held, as there is no question of favouring any particular religion or religious denomination, art. 27 not applicable - Settling of a scheme in regard to a religious institution by an executive officer without intervention of any judicial tribunal amounts to an unreasonable restriction hence, ss. 38 and 39 of Act must be held invalid - Thus, only sections of Act, which are invalid, are ss. 38 and 39 and proviso to s. 46 of Act of 1939 - Case no. I of 1950 dismissed - Application allowed.
