 V. M. Syed Mohammad and Company v State of Andhra

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 63; AIR 1954 SC 314; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 619; 1954 SCJ 390; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1117; 1954 (5) STC 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Taxes - Constitution - Madras General Sales Tax Act,, 1939, ss. 5(vi), r. 16(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, Entry 48 and 54, List II - (A) Interpretation of Constitution of India, Pre. ; Sch 7, List 2, Entry 54 - Held, intention of constituent assembly cannot be a guide for ascertaining intention of a totally different body, namely, British Parliament, in enacting Entry 48 in List II of Seventh Sch. to Govt. of India Act, 1935 - (B) Whether imposition of tax on purchasers under Madras Act, 1939 was valid? - Further held, Entry 48 in List II of Seventh Sch. to Govt. of India Act, on a proper construction, was wide enough to cover a law imposing tax on purchaser of goods as well and constituents - Constitutionality of Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, cannot, therefore, be impugned on ground that Provincial Legislature had no power under Govt. of India Act of 1935 to enact a law imposing a tax on purchasers - (C) Whether separate classification of Tax on purchasers of hides and skins imposed by Act of 1939 is violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Further held, art. 14 does not forbid classification for legislative purposes - Such classification should be based on some differentia having a reasonable relation to object and purpose of law in question - Validity of legislative classification is always presumed - Separate classification of purchasers of hides and skins not violative of art. 14 - (D) Whether r. 16(5) of Madras Turnover and Assessment Rules (1939) is valid - Further held, rule contravenes s. 5 (vi) of Act and is therefore ultra-vires - Sub-rule is however severable and does not affect validity of other rules - Appeal dismissed.
