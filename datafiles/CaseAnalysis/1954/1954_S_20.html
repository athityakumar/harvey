Shreekantiah Ramayya Munipalli and Another v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1955 SC 287; 1955 CRLJ 857; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 197 and 537 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 r/w s. 34, 109, 120-B, 197 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Criminal breach of trust by a public servant - Whether an offence u/s. 409 would have to be tried under that Act, or whether it could also be tried in ordinary way by ordinary Courts? - Held, a choice is conferred on some authority to choose whether any given accused should be tried in a special Court with a special procedure and be subject to a lesser punishment u/s. 5(2) or whether he should be tried in ordinary way u/s. 409, IPC with risk of a higher punishment - There must be some sort of preliminary planning which may or may not be at the scene of crime and which may have taken place long beforehand, but there must be added to it, an element of physical presence at scene of occurrence coupled with actual participation which can be of a passive character provided that it is done with an intention of assisting in furtherance of common intention and there is a readiness to play part in pre-arranged plan when time comes to act - Officers in position of accused do not commit illegal acts like this and expose themselves to prosecution and possible disgrace, unless they are prompted by some strong motive, usually self-interest - Though a conviction can be based on evidence which does not disclose a motive if facts proved justify such a course, yet it would ordinarily be unsafe to convict in a case like the present one in the absence of proof indicating an adequate reason for criminal behaviour on part of the accused - Two convictions quashed.
