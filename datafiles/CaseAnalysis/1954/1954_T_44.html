 P. Joseph John v State of Travancore-Cochin

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1955 SC 160; 1956 (1) LLJ 235; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 134; 1955 SCJ 221; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1011

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Travancore Public Servants (Enquiries) Act, 1122, Travancore Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1132

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 320 - Service - Charges - Removal - Justifiability - Petitioner was serving in erstwhile Travancore state and promoted to Executive Engineer - Govt. had received complaints about conduct and dealings against petitioner and after enquiry order was passed for removal from service and debarred from reappointment - Petitioner filed application for reconsideration of said order, which was rejected - Aggrieved petitioner filed application before High Court against said order which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether petitioner was given opportunity to show cause against the action proposed to be taken against him was in substantial compliance with the provisions of the art. 320 of Constitution.

Held, rules of natural justice were fully observed during the enquiry and petitioner had the fullest opportunity to put in his defence both before the Enquiry Commissioner and against the action proposed to be taken against him. It was by reason of his own default that he failed to avail himself of the second opportunity. Petitioner was put in a belated review but such a review is not provided for under the rules and it was not necessary to consult the Public Service Commission at that stage. Hence, petitions are not within the contemplation of the Constutional provisions. Appeal dismissed.
