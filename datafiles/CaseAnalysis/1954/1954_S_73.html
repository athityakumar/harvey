Madan Mohan Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1954 SC 637; 1954 CRLJ 1656

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Socio-Economic - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Whether there was any proper or valid sanction obtained for prosecution of accused in present case, as is required u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act and consequently initiation of proceeding was illegal and without jurisdiction? - Held, no proper or valid sanction obtained for prosecution of accused in present case, as is required u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act and consequently initiation of proceeding illegal and without jurisdiction - High Court's approach to case has not been proper - A public officer has no right to demand any bribe; but when he is hauled up before a criminal court to answer a charge of having taken illegal gratification, question whether any motive for payment or acceptance of bribe at all existed is certainly a relevant and a material fact for consideration - Further held, finding of Magistrate quite reasonable and supported by facts and HC did not apply principles which it was incumbent upon to keep in mind when dealing with a judgment of acquittal and reasons given by it to overrule estimate of evidence made by Trial Court which heard and recorded are inadequate and flimsy - Appeal allowed.
