 Laxmanappa Hanumantappa Jamkhandi v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 72; (1996) 1 SCC 110; AIR 1955 SC 3; [1954] 26 ITR 754; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 94; 1955 SCJ 590; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31(1), 32, 19(1)(f) and 265 - Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, ss. 5(1) and 8(2) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Assessment Years 1926-27, 1940-41, 1941-42 and 1943-44 - Enforcement of fundamental rights u/arts. 31(1) and 19(1)(f) - Petitioner, along with his brothers, used to carry on the business of toddy and liquor vendors - One of the brothers used to run a bus service and dealt in cotton and money-lending also - All the brothers owned extensive properties, both agricultural and non-agricultural - Prior to assessment year 1926-27, base of assessment of income tax was Hindu undivided family upto 1946 - As the case of partition set up by them was incorrect an assessment u/s. 34 of IT Act, 1922 was made on the four brothers jointly, treating them as an association of persons, for the year 1942-43 - Similar assessment proceedings were taken against them in respect of other mentioned years - Justification of proceedings adopted u/s. 34? - From the facts stated it is plain that the proceedings taken under the impugned Act of 1947 concluded so far as the Investigation Commission is concerned in Sept, 1952, more than two years before the present petition was presented in apex Court - Assessment orders under the Income-tax Act, 1922 itself were made against the petitioner in Nov, 1953 - Held, in these circumstances, Courts are of the opinion that petitioners is entitled to no relief under the provisions of art. 32 - Further held, right conferred by art. 265 is not a right conferred by Part III of the Constitution of India, 1950, it could not be enforced u/art. 32 - Petition dismissed.
