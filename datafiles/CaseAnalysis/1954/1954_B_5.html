 Rananjaya Singh v Baijnath Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1954 SC 749; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 714; 1954 SCJ 838; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 671

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 123(7), 124 and rr. 117 and 118 - Employment made by a candidate of election - Ambit of corrupt practices - (A) Whether appellant in circumstances of this case be held to be guilty of any corrupt practice u/s. 123(7) of Act of 1951 ? - In eye of law these extra persons were in employment of father of appellant and paid by father and they were neither employed nor paid by appellant - Held, appellant cannot in circumstances of this case be held to be guilty of any corrupt practice u/s. 123(7) of Act of 1951 as alleged against him - (B) Whether appellant is guilty of offence u/s. 124(4) of Act of 1951? - Further held, appellant not having incurred any expenditure over and above what was shown by him in his return of election expenses, he cannot be said to have concealed such expenditure and, therefore, he cannot be held to have been guilty of any minor corrupt practice u/s. 124(4) of Act of 1951 - Appeal allowed.
