Muir Mills Company, Limited v Suti Mills Mazdoor Union, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1955 SC 170; 1955 (1) LLJ 1; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 127; 1955 SCJ 214; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 991

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Dispute regarding the workers' claim for bonus-appellant company had suffered a trading loss as result of various factors including indiscipline on part of the workers - (A) Whether workers are entitled to payment of a bonus in spite of employer having worked at a loss during year? - The claim for bonus can be made by the employees only if as a result of the joint contribution of capital and labour the industrial concern has earned profits - Held, if in any particular year the working of the industrial concern has resulted in loss there is no basis nor justification for a demand for bonus - (B) Whether workers have any right, title or interest in reserves and undistributed profits of previous years? - The workers not being members of the company would also not have any right, title and interest in the reserves or the undistributed profits which would from part of the assets of the company - Held, workers had already received their full bonus in previous year - No further claim for payment of bonus out of those reserves or undistributed profits can therefore survive - (C) Whether the Supreme Court has jurisdiction to entertain this appeal? - Held,the Industrial Tribunals were tribunals within the meaning of art. 136 of the Constitution, 1950 and art. 136 has vested in the Supreme Court exceptional and overriding power to interfere where it reaches the conclusion that a person has been dealt with arbitrarily or that a Court or tribunal within the territory of India has not given a fair deal to litigant - Appeal allowed.
