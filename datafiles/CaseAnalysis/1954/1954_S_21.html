 Shri Audh Behari Singh v Gajadhar Jaipuria and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1954 SC 417; 1954 SCJ 590; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Whether burden and benefit of a right of pre-emption are incidents annexed to lands belonging respectively to vendor and pre-emptor or is right merely one of re-purchase, which a neighbour can enforce personally against vendee in whom title to property has already vested by sale? - Held, law of pre-emption imposes a limitation or disability upon ownership of a property to extent that it restricts owner's unfettered right of sale an compels him to sell property to his co-sharer or neighbour as case may be - Benefit as well as burden of right of pre-emption run with land and can be enforced by or against owner of land for time being although right of pre-emptor does not amount to an interest in land itself Law of pre-emption creates a right which attaches to property and on that footing only it can be enforced against purchaser - Pre-emption is stated to be a purely personal law even when it rests on custom - It is no incident of property and right which it creates is enforceable only against persons who belong to a particular religious community or fulfill description of being natives of a particular district - A local custom of pre-emption exists in city of Banaras and right attaches at least to all house properties situated within it and no incident of such custom is proved which would make right available only between persons who are either natives of Banaras or are domiciled therein - Appeal allowed.
