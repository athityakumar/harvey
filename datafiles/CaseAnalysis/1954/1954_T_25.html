 Suleman Issa v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 62; AIR 1954 SC 312; 1954 CRLJ 881; 1954 SCJ 387; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Telecom

Keywords: Police Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Public Securities Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 517 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 109 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Sea Customs Act - Public Securities Act - Bombay District Police Act, s. 61E - (A) Whether confiscation order u/s. 517 of CrPC, 1898 was justified - Held, power of confiscation with court u/s. 517 should not be used without considering facts of a case - The s. 61-E, Bombay District Police Act, 1890 does not by itself empower court to impose penalty of confiscation - Confiscation is not only mode of disposal u/s. 517 - It is open to court to order property to be delivered to person claiming to be entitled to its possession - Appeal allowed.
