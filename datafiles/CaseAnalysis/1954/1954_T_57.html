 Tolaram Relumal and Another v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1954 SC 496; 1954 (56) BomLR 1206; 1954 CRLJ 1333; 1954 SCJ 547; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1947, ss. 18(1), 5, 6 and 18(3) - Offence of receiving money as 'pugree' - (A) Whether receipt of a sum of money by a person who enters into an executory contract to grant a lease of a building under construction falls within mischief of s. 18(1) of Act ? - Held, money must be received by landlord in respect of grant of a lease and section would not cover an executory agreement to grant a lease - Grant of tenancy means grant of new or initial tenancy; renewal of tenancy means grant of tenancy after its termination; and continuance seems to contemplate continuance of a tenancy which is existing - An instrument is usually construed as a lease if it contains words of present demise - Oral agreement made between parties did not constitute a lease but it amounted to an agreement to grant a lease in future - (B) Whether s. 18(1) makes punishable receipt of money at a moment of time when lease had not come into existence, and when there was a possibility that contemplated lease might never come into existence? - Further held, provisions of s. 18(1) are penal in nature and it is a well settled rule of construction of penal statutes that if two possible and reasonable constructions can be put upon a penal provision, court must lean towards that construction which exempts subject from penalty rather than one which imposes penalty -It is not competent to court to stretch meaning of an expression used by Legislature in order to carry out intention of legislature - High Court laid undue emphasis on words 'in respect of' in context of section - It is difficult to hold that any relationship of landlord and tenant comes into existence on execution of an agreement executory in nature or that expression 'premium' can be oppositely used in connection with receipt of money on occasion of execution of such an agreement - It may well be that if a lease actually comes into existence then any receipt of money which has a nexus with that lease may fall within mischief of s. 18(1) - S. 18(3) was added to section by Act 42 of 1951, subsequent to agreement in question, and for purposes of this case s. 18(1) should ordinarily be read as it stood in Act, at time offence is alleged to have been committed - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
