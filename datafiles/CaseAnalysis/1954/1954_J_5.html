 Jagan Nath v Jaswant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 19; AIR 1954 SC 210; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 480; 1954 SCJ 257; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 892

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 1, rr. 9,10 and 13 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136, 226, and 227 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 81, 82, 83 and 117 - (A) Whether non-compliance with provisions of s. 82 is fatal to petition ? Held, No - It is not fatal - Matter is to be determined in accordance with rules of CPC, 1908 which have been made expressly applicable - (B) Whether non-joinder of a party is a fatal defect ? - Further held, no - It is merely a directory provision - Decree can be passed so far as parties actually on record are concerned - Provided, party omitted is not a necessary party that in his absence no relief could be given at all, even as regards parties actually on record - (C) Should Election Commission decide whether parties to petition have been properly impleaded ? - Matter is to be determined by Election Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
