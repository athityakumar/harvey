Mahadeo Prasad v State of W.B

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 1954 SC 724; 1954 CRLJ 1806

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Appellant convicted u/s. 420 and sentenced for one year rigorous imprisonment - Appeal - Appellant knew that he had taken delivery of ingots without payment of cash against delivery and only way in which he would get away from criminal liability was to arrive at a settlement with complainant - Held, whole of conduct of appellant is sufficient to hold that at time when he took delivery of 25 ingots of tin, appellant had no intention whatsoever to pay but merely promised to pay cash against delivery in order to induce complainant to part with goods - Appellant was, therefore, rightly convicted of offence u/s. 420 - Appeal dismissed.
