Sitaji and Others v Bijendra Narain Choudhary and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1954 SC 601

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 33, 32(2) and (6) - Appeal and a cross appeal for possession and mesne profits - Whether plaintiffs to be next reversioners? - Held, it is business of the Panjikars to collect this evidence about pedigrees and presumably they endeavour to collect correct information because what they put down about one man will affect a whole family and families of those who marry into it - There are checks and counter checks as information pours in from different members and branches of family - They would consequently fall into disrepute if their books contained glaring inaccuracies - Evidence of 5th plaintiff, taken with Panjis, Exs. 4, 4(a) 4(b), 4(d) and 4(e), is sufficient to afford a good foundation for decision of two Courts of fact - Appeal dismissed.
