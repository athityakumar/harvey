State of Bombay v Bhanji Munji and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1955 SC 41; 1955 (57) BomLR 595; 1955 SCJ 10; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f) and 31(2) - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, ss. 6(4) and 5(1)(a) - Governor of Bombay issued orders requisitioning the premises of the three respondents -Constitutional validity of the orders -(A) Whether orders u/ss. 6(4)(a) and 5(1) of Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948 requisitioning premises are 'ultra vires' arts. 19(1)(f) and 31(2) of Constitution? - Held, because restrictions imposed on right to hold, acquire and dispose of property are neither reasonable nor in interests of general public and, secondly, because Act does not require that there should be a public purpose - (B) Whether tenants to have a right u/art. 19(1)(f)? - Held, art. 19(1)(f) of the Constitution of India, 1950 does not apply to tenants - 'State of West Bengal v Subodh Gopal Bose', AIR 1954 SC 92, 1954 AIR(SC) 92, 1953 Indlaw SC 50 - 'Dwarkadas Shrinivas of Bombay v Sholapur Spinning and Weaving Co Ltd ', AIR 1954 SC l19, 1954 AIR(SCL) 19(b) relied on - Arts. 19(1)(f) and 31 deal with different subjects and cover different fields - Art. 19(1)(f) r/w cl. (5) postulates existence of property which can be enjoyed and over which rights can be exercised because otherwise reasonable restrictions contemplated by cl. (5) could not be brought into play if there is no property which can be acquired held or disposed of, no restriction can be placed on exercise of right to acquire, hold and dispose of it- Appeal allowed.
