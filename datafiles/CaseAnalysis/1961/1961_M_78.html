 Mst. Jadao Bahuji v Municipal Committee, Khandwa and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1961 SC 1486; 1962 (2) SCJ 518; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 633

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Government of India Act 1935, Khandwa Municipality (Validation Of Tax) Act, 1941

Summary: Constitution - Government of India Act, 1935 ss. 93, - Municipality (Validation of Tax) Act, 1941 - Khandwa Ginning and Pressing Cotton Tax Validating Act, 1938 - Validation of Act by governor - Constitutionality - Municipal Committee resolved to impose tax on trade of ginning and pressing cotton by means of steam or mechanical process and after sundry procedure notification was published on 25-11-1922 in Central Provinces and Berar Gazette, imposing tax Certain traders including appellant, affected by tax, filed suits seeking injunction against Committee on ground that tax was invalid and illegal and their suit were accordingly dismissed - Meantime Provincial Legislature had been dissolved, and Governor had assumed all powers of Provincial Legislature u/s. 93 of the Act of 1935 and with assent of Governor- General enacted second Validating Act intituled the Act of 1941 which received assent by Governor-General and was published in C. P. and Berar Gazette on 11-7-1941 and the Act of 1938 was hereby repealed - Provincial Govt. framed rule which shortly stated, provided for recovery of IV amount by way of execution application made to very Court which executed decree - Committee deposited decretal amount in Court, which was withdrawn by appellant on furnishing security on 7-8-1947 Committee filed its application under rule for execution of decree, objections were raised by appellant, but were disallowed, and Committee realised amount of tax from surety - Appellant filed appeal before HC and HC held that by second Validating Act which was passed after 31-3-1939 limit of Rs. 50 per annum imposed by second sub-section of s. 142-A of the Act 1941 was exceeded, 'and that Act was thus ultra vires but Governor and C J was of contrary opinion and matter was placed before another bench who agreed with C. J. and appeal was dismissed - Hence Instant appeal - Appellant raised many objections, but instant Court concerned with one only, viz., that Act was ultra vires Provincial Legislature and consequently Governor, being repugnant to a. 142-A, which was introduced in Act of 1935, and which imposed a limit of Rs. 50 on taxes on professions, trades and callings after 31-3-1939 - whether power to pass retrospective and validating law was taken away by enactment of s. 142-A of the Act of 1941 and amendment of Entry in the Act of 1935 was justified - Held, it was of no doubt that if a law was passed after amendment and sought to impose taxes on professions etc., for any period after 31-3-1939 it had to conform to limit prescribed by s. 142A (2) of the Act of 1941 prohibition in second sub-section operated to circumscribe legislative power by putting a date-line after which a tax in excess of Rs. 50 per annum per person for a period after date-line could not be collect-id unless it came within proviso. But neither sub-s. (2) nor proviso speaks of a period prior to 31-3-1939 sub-section speaks only of "total amount payable...... after thirty- first day of March, 1939" - These words are important they create a limit on amount leviable as tax for a period after that date but if a law was passed validating another which imposed a tax for a period prior to date indicated, it would be taxing professions etc., in excess of Rs. 50 not after 31-3-1939 but before it neither Entry nor section either directly or indirectly prohibited this, nor did they create any limit for prior period - Hence Validating Act, though passed in 1941, can be read only as affecting period for which there was no limit if sub-section said that tax shall not be payable in excess of Rs. 50 without indicating period or date, argument would have some support, but it puts in a date, and operation of prohibition was confined to period after that date - Validating Act, being thus completely within powers of Governor and could remove retrospectively defect in earlier Act though it reimposed tax from date of earlier Act, it took care to impose tax for period ending with 31-3-1938 and impugned Act did not need support of proviso, because it did not fall within ban of second sub-section Thus Act of 1941 was within powers of Governor, and was a valid piece of legislation - Appeal dismissed.
