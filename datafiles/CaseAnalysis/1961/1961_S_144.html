Purushottamdas Dalmia v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 1961 SC 1589; 1961 CRLJ 728; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 137; 1961 (2) SCJ 563; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Keywords: Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Criminal Law - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 471 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 177 - Appellant applied for a licence for importing art silk yarn - Provisional licence was not confirmed within two months as required - Appellant's friend told him that he could get licence validated by one Rajan - Appellant handed over licenses to Rajan through friend - Licences containing alleged forged endorsements later returned to appellant - Jury returned a verdict of 'guilty' against appellant on charge of conspiracy u/s. 120-B r/w s. 471 - (A) Whether Court having jurisdiction to try offence of conspiracy can try an offence constituted by such overt acts which are committed beyond its jurisdiction? - Overruled decision of Calcutta High Court in Jiban Banerjee v State which answers above question in negative - Accepting this would mean that either prosecution is forced to give up its right of prosecuting accused for commission of those overt acts or that both prosecution and accused are put to unnecessary trouble - Held, provisions of CrPC should be construed to give jurisdiction of Court trying offence of criminal conspiracy to try all overt acts committed in pursuance of that conspiracy - Territorial jurisdiction is provided just as a matter of convenience - Further, Calcutta Court had jurisdiction to try appellant of offences u/s. 471 r/w s. 466 even though those offences, in pursuance of conspiracy, were committed at Madras - (B) Whether appellant was charged with two conspiracies in alternative, which is unwarranted by law? - Further held, this is not correct interpretation of charge of conspiracy framed against appellant - Conspiracy was one and it being doubtful what facts proved would establish about nature of offences to be committed by conspirators, charge illustrated offence in this form - Appeal dismissed.
