 General Manager, Southern Railway v Rangachari

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1962 SC 36; 1970 (2) LLJ 289; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 71; 1961 (2) SCJ 424; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 586

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16(1), 16(2), 16(4), 335 - Reservation of posts - Disputed - Validity - Respondent was working under appellant/General Manager, Southern Railway, filed petition before HC and writ of mandamus had issued restraining appellants, from giving effect to impugned circular issued by Railway Board on 27-4-1959, ordering reservation of selection posts in Class III of railway service in favour of members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes and in particular reservation of selection posts among Court Inspectors in Class III one of which was held by respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, it was true that in providing for reservation of appointments or posts u/art. 16(4) of Constitution State had to take into consideration claims of members of backward classes consistently with maintenance of efficiency of administration - It might not be forgotten that efficiency of administration was of such paramount importance that it would be unwise and impermissible to make any reservation at cost of efficiency of administration - That undoubtedly was effect of art. 335 - Reservation of appointments or posts might theoretically and conceivably mean some impairment of efficiency but risk involved in sacrificing efficiency of administration might always be borne in mind when any State sets about making provision for reservation of appointments or posts - It was also true that reservation which could be made u/art. 16(4) was intended merely to give adequate representation to backward communities - It could not be used for creating monopolies or for unduly or illegitimately disturbing legitimate interests of other employees - In exercising powers u/art. 16(4) of Constitution problem of adequate representation of backward class of citizens might be fairly and objectively considered and an attempt might always be made to strike reasonable balance between claims of backward classes and claims of other employees as well as important consideration of efficiency of administration but, in instant case, as SC had already seen challenge to validity of impugned circulars was based on assumption that said circulars were outside art. 16(4) of Constitution because posts referred to in said Article was posts outside cadre of services and in any case, do not include selection posts - Since, in SC opinion, that assumption was not well founded SC might hold that impugned circulars were not unconstitutional - In result HC order was reversed and respondents application for writ was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
