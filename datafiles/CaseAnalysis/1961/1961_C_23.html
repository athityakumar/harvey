Commissioner of Income Tax New Delhi v Messrs Chuni Lal Moonga Ram

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 458; AIR 1962 SC 1272; [1961] 43 ITR 1; 1961 (2) SCJ 544; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Income-tax Act, 1922 - In the instant case, two appeals were decided by the instant court, first was arisen out of the assessment of income-tax and other one out of assessment of excess profits tax - Respondent-firm entered into certain transactions called hedge outside the state from where they were running their business and claimed that it had incurred losses to non-residents in said transactions and requested that those losses should be taken into consideration in determining its income - Income-tax authorities disallowed claim on ground that if transactions would have resulted in profits, it would have been exempt from tax in terms of s. 14(2)(c) of the Act, the proviso to s. 24(1) of the 1922 Act, was a bar to adjustment of losses - Tribunal allowed deduction claimed on footing that it was not possible to split up transactions - Hence instant appeal - Whether, on the facts and circumstances of the case and in the view of the provisions of both the Acts, appeals of the appellant should be allowed - Held, appellant itself agreed that the decision of the earlier court was correct in the case of first appeal, hence, that appeal was subsequently dismissed - However in the case of second appeal, which was related to the excess profits tax, instant court held, that, failed to consider the third proviso to s. 5 of the 1940 Act while deciding in favor of the respondent - And hence, the appeal related to the excess profits tax was allowed in the purview of the third proviso to s. 5 of the 1940 Act - Appeals disposed of.
