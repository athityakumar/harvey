 Gordon Woodroffee Leather Manufacturing Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1962 SC 1361; [1962] 44 ITR 551; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 58; 1962 (2) SCJ 143; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Applicability of s. 10(2)(xv) to a gratuity paid by appellant to one of its officers on his retirement from service - Amount was paid voluntarily in recognition of long and faithful service of employee - Payment was not for facilitating carrying on of business of appellant - Amount was not paid in pursuance of any scheme of payment of gratuity - Held, amount claimed is not a deductible item u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Appeal dismissed.
