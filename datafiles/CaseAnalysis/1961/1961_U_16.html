 Glass Chatons Importers and Users' Association v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1961 SC 1514; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 115; 1962 (2) SCJ 213; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs/Constitution - Indirect Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Import of glass chatons could be made only on licences granted by licensing authorities - No application for issue of license made by Petitioners - Therefore, no license issued - Licenses issued in favour of the respondent - Application u/art. 32 for protection of fundamental rights u/arts. 19(1)(f) and (g), arts. 31 and 14 made - Whether Respondents were given preference over the Petitioners for issue of licence? - Petitioner did not apply for licenses only - Though it was open for them to apply for licenses - Held, no question of being discriminated against - Whether the canalization through special or specialized agencies or channels in the interests of the general public? - Held, the burden on the person challenging that the Govt. of the country is not right in its estimate of the effects of a policy as regards imports in the general interests of the public will be very heavy indeed and when the Govt. decides in respect of any particular commodity that its import should be by a selected channel or through selected agencies, the Court would proceed on the assumption that that decision is in the interests of the general public unless the contrary is clearly shown - Therefore, petitioners not entitled to any relief u/art. 32.
