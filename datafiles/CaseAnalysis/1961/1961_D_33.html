 Dr. Zafar Ali Shah and Others v Assistant Custodian of Evacuee Property, Jhansi

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1967 SC 106; 1962 (1) SCJ 677; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 749

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, ss. 7 and 8 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f) - Whether order declaring two houses of petitioner to be evacuee property violates fundamental rights u/art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, it is well established and not disputed, that no property of any person can be declared to be evacuee, property unless that person had first been given a notice u/s. 7 of Act - Ebrahim Aboobaker v Tek Chand Dolwani relied on - No such notice had been issued to petitioners - Their interest in houses could not have vested in custodian - Acts of custodian in so far as they deprive petitioners of their property cannot be upheld - Petition allowed.
