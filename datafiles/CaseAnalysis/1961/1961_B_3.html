Saibal Kumar Gupta and Others v B. K. Sen and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 508; AIR 1963 SC 633; 1961 CRLJ 749; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 460

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Service - Civil Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 497 - Complaint was filed against respondent (Commissioner of Calcutta Corporation) u/s. 497 of IPC, 1860 - Magistrate after examining numerous witnesses declined to frame a charge and discharged him - On revision, Sessions Judge directed further enquiry - Magistrate while holding further enquiry, as directed, allowed prosecution to tender further evidence - High Court stayed further proceedings - Case of respondent was that action of appellants as members of Special Committee amounted to gross contempt of HC as well as of court of trial - Appellants convicted for contempt of court and each of them was sentenced to pay a fine of Rs.500 - Hence present appeal by special leave - Whether conduct of appellants as members of Special Committee amounted to contempt of court? - Held, circumstances do not establish that Special Committee had constituted itself as a court of parallel enquiry to look into matters in issue in s. 497 case against respondent or which were in issue in pending proceedings in HC - Special Committee had embarked upon an enquiry on directions of Corporation in order to discover malpractice on part of Corporation's servants - Malpractices on part of a servant of Corporation would presumably include making unworthy appointments - Ascertainment of motive for appointments would be merely incidental to main purpose of enquiry - It would be difficult to conclude therefrom that Special Committee were holding a parallel enquiry on matters pending decision by a court of law and that thereby their action tended to interfere with course of justice - Appellants at no time intended to interfere with course of justice and their conduct did not tend to interfere with course of justice - Appellants had been careful in making no comments on any proceedings pending in a court of law or issues arising out of them - In these circumstances, offence of contempt of court by appellants has not been established - Appeal allowed.
