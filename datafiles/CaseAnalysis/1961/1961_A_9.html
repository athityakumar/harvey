 State of Andhra Pradesh v Abdul Khader

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1961 SC 1467; 1962 MLJ 80; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 65; 1961 (63) PLR 751; 1962 (1) SCJ 100; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners; International

Summary: Foreigner - Foreigners Act, 1946, ss. 3(2)(c), 2(a) and 14 - Citizenship Act, 1955, s. 9(2) - Was respondent a foreigner so as to justify making of order against him and convicting him for non-compliance of order u/s. 3(2)(c) of Foreigners Act, 1946? - Held, evidence on record showed that respondent went to Pakistan just before he arrived in India and thus stayed there for a short time, further evidence showed that he owned a shop in Adoni in Hyderabad, India and he always had his family there - Finding, he had never migrated to Pakistan, as a short visit there would not amount to migrating to that country - (i) Question arise as to that whether passport obtained by him from Pakistan would be a evidence that he renounced Indian citizenship and acquired Pakistan's citizenship? - (i) Finding, such evidence would be of no use in instant case in view of s. 9(2) of Citizenship Act, 1955 under which a Court cannot decide such issue, as Central Govt. is authorized to take decision on it and it has not decided on it - Court laid down that respondent has discharged onus that lay on him u/s. 9 of Foreigners Act, 1946 to prove that he was not a foreigner, by proving that he was born and domiciled in India prior to 26-1-1950, when Constitution of India, 1950 came into force and thereby had become an Indian citizen under art. 5(a) of Constitution of India, 1950 - Respondent has further proved that he had never migrated to Pakistan - Held, respondent was not a foreigner and no order could be made against him u/s. 3(l)(c) of Foreigners Act, 1946 - Appeal dismissed.
