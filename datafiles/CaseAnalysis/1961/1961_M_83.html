 State of Orissa and Another v Murlidhar Jena

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 516; AIR 1963 SC 404

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Excise Department - Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1951 - Corruption charges - Dismissal - Respondent, Senior Superintendent of Excise - Three charges framed, firstly respondent had put himself under an obligation to an excise vendor by his name by receiving from him cash and gifts in kind from time to time, secondly respondent had purchased an Austin Car at concessional rate from one who were interested in shop of Excise vendor from whom respondent received gifts, thirdly some gold ornaments were prepared for him by said excise vendor through a goldsmith - Preliminary investigation - Enquiry by Tribunal - Writ to High Court - Admitted - Finding given by HC that order of dismissal was based on no evidence at all - Whether finding proper? - Charges framed, findings recorded by Tribunal, grounds for findings, materials on record relied upon, looked into - Held, Tribunal's decision and findings supported by evidence - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
