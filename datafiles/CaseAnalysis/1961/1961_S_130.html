 Jagannath Prasad Sharma v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1961 SC 1245; 1961 (2) LLJ 166; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Police Act, Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1947, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850, Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceeding (Administrative Tribunal) Rules 1947, Bihar and Orissa Subordinate Services Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1935

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132 and 311 - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 96 B, 240, 241 and 243 - Police Act, ss. 7 and 46 (2) - Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850 - Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules, s. 55 - Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1947, rr. 3 and 4 - Whether proceedings before tribunal were vitiated? - Held, there is no substance in any of pleas raised by appellant relating to regularity of proceedings of Tribunal - It may be pertinent to note that even though appellant challenged before High Court regularity of proceedings of Tribunal, no argument was, it appears, advanced before HC in support thereof - Appeal dismissed.
