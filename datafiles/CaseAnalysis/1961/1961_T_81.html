State of Punjab v Barkat Ram

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1962 SC 276; 1962 CRLJ 217; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, Police Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Opium Act, 1878, Bombay Abkari Act, 1878, Land Customs Act, 1924, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bengal Excise Act

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26 and 27 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 167 to 193 - Land Customs Act, s. 9(1) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 23(1) - Police Act, 1861 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 and 228 - Sea Customs (Amendment) Act, 1955 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 186 and 15(2) - Conviction order of respondents are set aside by High Court on ground that confessional statements made to customs officers were in admissible in evidence - Whether customs officers were police officers within s. 25 of 1872 Act? - Duties of Customs officers are very different from those of police officers - Customs officer possessing certain powers, which may have similarity with those of police officers, for purpose of detecting smuggling of goods and persons responsible for it - Customs officers are empowered to take evidence and record statements - Respondents can be convicted on basis of statements made to customs officers - Custom officers have powers and they also discharge functions of police officers - Customs officer is police officer qua his police functions - Held, if confession made by respondent to Customs officers was excluded, there would be no other evidence on which conviction could be sustained - HC right in setting aside conviction of respondent - Appeal dismissed.
