V. R. Sejbramanyam v B. Thayappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1966 SC 1034; 1961 KLT 37; 1961 MWN 794; 1961 (2) SCJ 191; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - A written agreement D/- 01-10-1942, modified on 06-10-1942, respondent to construct for appellant on plot six shops, an out-house and a garage at rates specified in agreement - Municipality of Bangalore did not sanction plan proposed by appellant - Plan altered, shops were deleted, area of out-house was increased, and a puja room on ground floor and an extra room on first floor were added to plan and sanctioned - Dispute arose - Held, if a party to a contract has rendered service to the other not intending to do so gratuitously and the other person has obtained some benefit, the former is entitled to compensation for the value of the services rendered by him - Evidently, the respondent made additional constructions to the building and they were not done gratuitously - Respondent was therefore entitled to receive compensation for the work done which was not covered by the agreement - The respondent claimed under an oral agreement compensation at prevailing market rates for work done by him, even if he failed to prove an express agreement in that behalf, the court may still award him compensation u/s. 70 of the Contract Act, 1872 - By awarding a decree for compensation under the Statute and not under the oral contract pleaded, there was in the circumstances of this case no substantial departure from the claim made by the respondent - Claim for compensation for loss suffered due to defective work done by respondent, suit dismissed by High Court and it is not open to appellant thereafter to seek to reagitate same question in companion suits when no appeal has been preferred against decree and no plea of equitable set off has been raised in written statements in companion suits, no substance in any of contentions raised - Appeal dismissed.
