 Bishan Das and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 474; AIR 1961 SC 1570; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trust & Associations - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 31- Appellant was dispossessed from dharamshala by respondent and was deprived from management and control of dharamshala - High Court rejected matter on ground of disputed questions of facts - Held, Dharamshala constructed by their family funds of appellant on Govt. land with Govt. permission - Action of Govt. in taking law into their hands and dispossessing petitioners by display of force, exhibits a callous disregard of normal requirements of rule of law - Appellant cannot be dispossessed irrespective of whether it was a trust, public or private - Application allowed.
