 Dr. Rai Shivendra Bahadur v Governing Body of The Nalanda College

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 358; AIR 1962 SC 1210; 1962 (4) FLR 507; 1962 (1) LLJ 247; 1962 (2) SCJ 208; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - University of Bihar Act, s. 20 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Mr. D.P. Srivastava who was a Govt. servant was appointed Principal of Nalanda University but Govt. withdrew him later - Allegedly an extraordinary meeting of governing body of college was held, appellant was appointed its Principal and University were informed of this appointment as required by University Statutes and he took charge of his office - Thereafter, there was a change in Constitution of governing body -In their resolution President, respondent No. 2, appointed respondent No. 4 as Principal of College - Appellant was asked to hand over charge to the new appointee -The petitioner thereupon filed a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution challenging validity of appointment of respondent No. 4 -(A)Whether petitioner having no legal right can ask for issuing writ petition i.e., mandamus? - Held, where petitioner has not shown that he has any right entitling him to get an order for appointment or reinstatement, he cannot ask for writ to issue against governing body - Appeal dismissed.
