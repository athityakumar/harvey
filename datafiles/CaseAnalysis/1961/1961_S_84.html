 State of Bombay v Abraham (F. A.)

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1962 SC 794; 1962 (64) BomLR 575; 1963 (6) FLR 148; 1963 (2) LLJ 422; 1962 (2) SCJ 168; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Order reverting respondent from rank of officiating Deputy Superintendent of Police to rank of inspector of police - Whether order reverting respondent from his post, illegal and void? - Held, reversion has not in any way affected respondent so far as his condition and prospect of service are concerned - Govt. had right to consider suitability of respondent to hold position to which he had been appointed to officiate and was entitled for that purpose to make inquiries about his suitability - This is all that Govt. did in this case - Enquiry was concerned with ascertaining suitability of respondent for higher rank and was not a punishment - Reversion was not by way of punishment because he had no right to continue in higher post or to benefits arising from it - Appeal allowed.
