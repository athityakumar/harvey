Kashinathsa Yamosa Kabadi v Narsingsa Bhaskarsa Kabadi

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1961 SC 1077; 1961 (63) BomLR 659; 1962 (2) SCJ 597; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 792

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Arbitration & ADR - Constitution - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Registration Act, 1908 - Hindu undivided property - Partition - Arbitration - Suit for partition - Revocation - Maintainability - Appellants and respondents/ parties in instant case were members of a joint Hindu family which possessed both moveable and immovable properties - They appointed panchas to determine shares and to divide property among them - Panchas determined properties and reduced it to writing which was accepted and signed by respondents and appellants - Later on, panchas divided a lot of movable and immovable properties among them and gave possession of shares which was recorded in 'partition books' and were signed by panchas and appellants and respondents - Panchas left a few properties undivided for which Godkhindi was appointed for division of remaining properties with consent of parties - Godkhindi also failed to divide remaining properties and divided only a few - one of the parties served a notice cancelling authority of Panchas and filed for suit of remaining properties - Upon application for revocation by plaintiff for reference trial court cancelled arbitration as one of the panchas was unwilling to proceed with division - Another party filed a suit for partition of all properties contending that division made by panchas was not binding as award was not made a rule of court and reference had been revoked because award was not registered - Hence, instant appeals - Held, that divisions made by panchas were binding and only remaining properties were to be divided - Thereafter, by division of family assets which was accepted by parties and by taking into possession of their shares by parties properties came under individual ownerships to whom they were allotted and in respect of remaining properties they became tenants-in-common - Proceedings taken up by panchas were not revoked by order of trial court as they had been accepted and acted upon by parties - Where an award made in arbitration out of court was voluntarily accepted and acted upon by parties and a suit was thereafter filed by one of the parties ignoring acts done in pursuance of acceptance of award, defence that suit was not maintainable was not founded on plea that there is an award which barred the suit but that parties have by mutual agreement settled dispute and that agreement and subsequent actions of parties were binding - Such a plea was not barred by s. 32 of the Arbitration Act - Records made by Panchas were documents which merely acknowledged partitions already made and thus were not required to be registered - Appeals dismissed.
