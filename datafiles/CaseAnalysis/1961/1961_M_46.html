Messrs Murlidhar Chiranjilal v Messrs Harishchandra Dwarkadas and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 250; AIR 1962 SC 366; 1962 (1) SCJ 462; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Impossible Of Performance, Measure Of Damages

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 73 - Quantum of Damages - Contract between traders which is covered by words 'which naturally arose in usual course of things from such breach' appearing in s. 73 - Liability to pay damages to be decided - Held, this is a simple case of purchase of goods for resale anywhere and therefore measure of damages has to be calculated as they would naturally arise in usual course of things from such breach - Respondent had to prove market rate at Kanpur on date of breach for similar goods and that would fix amount of damages, in case that rate had gone above contract rate on date of breach - As respondent had failed to prove rate for similar canvas in Kanpur on date of breach it is not entitled to any damages in circumstances - Appeal allowed.
