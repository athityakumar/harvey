 Gian Chand and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1962 SC 496; 1962 CRLJ 485; 1984 ECR 105; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1365; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 364; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Smuggling of goods - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were convicted of an offence punishable u/s. 167 (81) of the Act for 'having acquired possession of smuggled gold and for carrying, keeping and concealing the said gold with intent to defraud the Govt. knowing that the gold had been smuggled into India from a foreign country and that no duty had been paid thereon' and were sentenced to terms of imprisonment - Revision petition preferred from the decision of lower appellate court, to the HC was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, appellant's revision petition was rightly dismissed by the HC - Held, HC failed to consider the fact while dismissing the revision petition that, lower appellate court had upheld the conviction of the appellants by an independent finding that the prosecution had positively established that the goods were smuggled and that the appellants had knowingly done the acts referred to in s. 167(81) of the Act with which they were charged and hence, in the view of the conclusion instant court had directed the HC that revision petition should be disposed of considering discussed aspect - Order of the dismissal of revision petition by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
