 Gian Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1962 SC 219; 1962 (1) SCJ 641; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 515

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Government servant

Summary: Service - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 16 - Indian Independence Act, s. 18(3) - Punjab Land Revenue Act,1887, s. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.309, 133, 311, 367 and 372 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 241 - Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, r. 6 - Punjab Civil Services Rules, rr. 2.14, 14.9 - Whether order of dismissal was passed by an incompetent authority? - Held, condition of service of civil servants having remained unaltered even after Govt. of India Act, 1935 was brought into operation by virtue of Commencement and Transitory Provisions Order 1936, and Adaptation of Indian Laws Order having made express provision saving rules as well as authority granted under rules to Financial Commr., order of Financial Commr. dismissing appellant from service was not unauthorised - Appeal dismissed.
