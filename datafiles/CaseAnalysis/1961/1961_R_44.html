Rangilal Choudhury v Dahu Sao and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 1962 SC 1248; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Substantial Character

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 33 (1), 33 (4)and 36 (4) - (A) Whether Returning officer is correct in rejecting nomination paper without giving an opportunity to candidate to correct information on nomination paper? - Held, s. 33 (4) of Act - It is the duty of returning officer to satisfy himself that names and electoral roll numbers of candidate and his proposer entered in nomination paper are same as those entered in electoral roll - It gives him power to permit removal if there is any defect in this connection - Failure of returning officer to give this opportunity - Defect was of an insubstantial character and would not result in rejection of nomination paper - Appeal dismissed.
