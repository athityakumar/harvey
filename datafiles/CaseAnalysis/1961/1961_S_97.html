 Messrs George Oakes Private Limited v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 38; AIR 1962 SC 1037; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 570; 1961 (12) STC 476

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Collected, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Madras General Sales Tax (Definition of Turnover and Validation of Assessments) Act, 1954, Bihar Sales Tax (Definition of Turnover and Validation of Assessments) Act, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 2(i), 2(h), 3, 8-B and 12-B - Constitution of India, 1953, art. 286 - Madras General Sales Tax (Definition of Turnover and Validation of Assessments) Act, 1954, ss. 2, 3 - Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956 - Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939 - Whether Madras General Sales Tax (Definition of Turnover and Validation of Assessments) Act is ultra vires Constitution? - True effect of s. 8-B and Turnover and Assessment Rules is that (a) a registered dealer is enabled to pass on tax, (b) an unregistered dealer cannot do so, and (c) amount collected by way of tax is to be shown separately, for it has to be paid over to Govt. - In express terms s. 2 states that tax shall be deemed to have formed part of turnover and obliterates distinction, if any, between 'tax' and 'turnover' for limited period during which impugned Act operates - What is taxable is not each transaction of sale but total turnover of dealer, computed in accordance with provisions of Act and Rules - Held, impugned Act is intra vires - Appeal dismissed.
