 State Of Uttar Pradesh v Kunwar Sri Trivikram Narain Singh.

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1963 SC 799; 1962 (2) SCJ 613; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Land Revenue Act, 1901, s. 78 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 73 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1951, ss. 3 (8), 4 and 6 (b) - Whether right to receive allowance under arrangement of year 1838 was extinguished as a consequence of vesting of 'Sudpore Bhettree' parganas in State of Uttar Pradesh u/s. 4? - U/s. 3 (8) of Act, an 'estate' is an area included under one entry in registers described in cls. (a) to (d) of Land Revenue Act if grant or confirmation of title is in respect of a right or privilege to land in an estate or its revenue, it must determine u/cl. (b) of s. 6 but a right to receive an allowance which is granted in consideration of extinction of a right to land or land revenue does not, by force of cl .(b) determine - Held, of 12 mahals respondent was a proprietor, land of mahals was 'estate' within meaning of s. 3 (8) and by s. 4, right of respondent in that estate stood vested in and transferred to State, and right to remission could not be converted into a positive right to receive amount thereof, notwithstanding extinction of his right in those 12 mahal; allowance granted as consideration for abandonment of right to 166 mahal, has not quality of land or land revenue, its quantum only was measured by equating it with a fourth share in net revenue of a part of land, subject matter of suit in which arrangement for payment of allowance was made; right to receive allowance of Rs. 30, 612-8-0 from Govt. under arrangement cannot, in absence of an express provision to that effect, be called 'an area included under one entry in any of registers' described in various clauses and HC rightly held that right of respondent to receive Rs. 36,330 per annum was not an 'estate' within meaning of Act - Appeal dismissed.
