 Union of India v Ghaus Mohammad

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1961 SC 1526; 1961 AllCriR 346; 1961 CRLJ 703; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 73; 1961 (63) PLR 838; 1962 (1) SCJ 119; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners; International

Summary: Criminal - Foreigners - Constitution - Foreigners Act, 1946, ss. 3 (2) (c), 8 and 9 - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss.9 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Order u/s. 3(2)(c) asking respondent to leave India - Challenged - Whether respondent was foreigner or Indian citizen? - Onus falls on person to prove that he is an Indian citizen - Held, question whether respondent is a foreigner is a question of fact and where there is a great deal of dispute on question which would require a detailed examination of evidence a proceeding u/art. 226 of Constitution would not be appropriate for a decision of question - Question is best decided by a suit - Order accordingly.
