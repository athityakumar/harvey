Delhi Administration v Ram Singh

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1962 SC 63; 1961 ALJ 805; 1962 CRLJ 106; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 694

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Criminal

Keywords: Police Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Prevention of Corruption Act

Summary: Socio-Economic - Human Rights - Women & Children - Practice & Procedure - Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Act, 1956, ss. 13(1), 13(3) and 2(i) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 5(2), 156 and 551 - Whether expression 'dealing with offence' includes power of investigation? - Held, expression 'dealing with offences' in s. 13(1) is of wide import and will include any Act which police has to do in connection with offences under Act - Expression 'function in relation to offences' in s. 13(3) also includes functions connected with investigation of offences - There is no reason to exclude such functions from functions contemplated by sub-s. (3) - It is thus clear that special police officer is competent to investigate and that he and his assistant police officers are only persons competent to investigate offences under the Act and that police officers not specially appointed as special police officers cannot investigate offences under Act even though they are cognizable offences - Appeal dismissed.
