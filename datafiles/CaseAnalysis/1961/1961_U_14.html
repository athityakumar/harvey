 Union of India v Pandurang Kashinath More

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 519; AIR 1962 SC 630; 1961 (3) FLR 323; 1961 (2) LLJ 427

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 311 - Respondent was put under detention under Bombay Public Security Measures Act - Order of suspension stated that respondent was not entitled to any subsistence allowance during period of suspension - Manager passed in order terminating service and he was given one month's pay in lieu of notice - High Court held that art. 16 of Constitution of India, 1950, had been violated and allowed appeal and made declaration sought by respondent - Whether the order of HC is valid in law? - Held, if respondent is not allowed to raise a case of discrimination, then no question of violation of art. l6 would arise - Likewise, no such question would arise if respondent is permitted to make that case for he failed to establish it on evidence - Appeal allowed.
