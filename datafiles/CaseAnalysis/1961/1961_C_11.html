 N. Masthan Sahib v Chief Commissioner, Pondicherry

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 415; AIR 1962 SC 797; 1964 (1) SCJ 212; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 981

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 138(1) - Whether Pondicherry is not part of territory of India, with consequence that Chief Commissioner whose order is impugned in appeal is not 'a Court or tribunal in territory of India' and hence, this Court would have no jurisdiction in absence of any legislation by Parliament u/art. 138(1), and civil appeal would have to be dismissed as incompetent? - Held, powers of this Court u/art. 32 of Constitution are not circumscribed by any territorial limitation - It extends not merely over every authority within territory of India but also those functioning outside provided that such authorities are under control of Govt. of India - In view of circumstances of case, Court direct that question whether Pondicherry is comprised within territory of India or not and what is extent of jurisdiction exercised by Union Govt. over said territory shall be forwarded to Union of India, under seal of this Court for submission of their answers - Appeal disposed of.
