Magiti Sasamal v Pandab Bissoi

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1962 SC 547; 1962 (28) CutLT 85; 1962 KLT 119; 1962 (1) SCJ 636; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Orissa Tenants Protection Act, 1948

Summary: Rent Control - Orissa Tenants Protection Act, 1948, s. 7 (1) - (A) Whether civil courts or revenue court have jurisdiction to decide question of existence of relationship of landlord and tenant between parties u/s. 7(1) of Act? - Held, only civil court has jurisdiction and not revenue court - Legislature intends that disputes contemplated by provisions should be tried, not by ordinary civil courts, but by tribunals specially designated by it, and so in dealing with scope and effect of jurisdiction of such tribunals relevant words used in section should receive not a narrow but a liberal construction - If a statute purports to exclude ordinary jurisdiction of civil courts it must do so either by express terms or by use of such terms as would necessarily lead to inference of such exclusion - S. 7(1) provides exclusive jurisdiction of Collector to try five categories of disputes that may arise between landlord, and tenant - Unreasonable to hold that a dispute about status of tenant also falls within purview of said section - Appeal allowed.
