 Roshan Lal Mehra v Ishwar Das

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1962 SC 646; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 947

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Completion Of Construction, Punjab Courts Act, New Delhi House Rent Control Order, 1939

Summary: Constitution - Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer-Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947, s. 7 A, sch. IV - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 226 - (A) Does prescribing different procedures for fixation of standard rent violate guarantee of equal protection guaranteed by art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, amended Delhi and Ajmer Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947 laid down standard rent of newly constructed premises to be fixed by Rent Controller - Prescribing different procedures for fixation of standard rent is not violative of art. 14 of Constitution - (B) Is it a violation of natural justice if ex-parte proceedings take place after repeated notifications are given to landlord to appear in court? - Further held, no - Landlord not appearing in spite of repeated notices and adjournments - No violation of rules of natural justice - Appeal Allowed.
