Devidas and Others v Shrishailappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 1961 SC 1277; 1962 (64) BomLR 82; 1962 (1) SCJ 557; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 896

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Mortgage deed - Challenged - Defendant no. 1, executed deed of simple mortgage in favour of defendant no. 9, to secure repayment of Rs. 1, 73, 000, Rs. 76, 700, out of which were received in cash and balance amounts which defendant no. 9, agreed to pay to defendant no. 1 creditors - In insolvency Application before First Class Subordinate Judge, defendant no. 1 was adjudicated an insolvent and receivers were appointed by Insolvency Court to administer his estate and receivers applied for declaration that mortgage deed, was in fraud of creditors and was accordingly void - Assistant Judge, in Appeal from order of Insolvency Court held that defendant no. 9, was entitled out of mortgage amount to recover Rs. 45, 700, as secured debt and Rs. 31, 000, as unsecured debt - In meanwhile creditor of defendant no. 1 filed Suit No. 84 of 1932 against defendant no. 9, in court of First Class Subordinate, for decree of Rs. 8, 000, which was decreed ex-parte against defendant no. 9, and claim against other family members was either withdrawn or rejected - Appellants/defendant nos. 4A to 4D filed suit before trial court which was decreed against them - Aggrieved defendant nos. 4A to 4C appealed to HC was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was sustainable - Held, when mortgage deed was executed by defendant no. 1, defendant no. 9, was manager of joint family - In Suit No. 84 of 1932, filed by creditor it was alleged that defendant no. 9, was manager of joint family consisting of himself and branches of Shivappa and Basavanappa and that mortgage transaction was for benefit of joint family and that Raohappa had entered into that transaction for and on behalf of joint family and in that suit defendant no. 9, alone was declared liable to pay Rs. 8, 000 - Partition of year 1930, was supported by evidence which had remained unchallenged - Evidence of defendant nos. 9 and 5, that partition took place also had remained uncontradicted - Question which calls for consideration was whether at partition, mortgagee right under deed executed by defendant no. 1, was kept undivided - Defendant no. 5, in his evidence stated that an equal division was made of lands according to income and that defendant no. 9, was not given smaller share in lands and also stated that houses were divided in equal shares and outstandings in money-lending business except two bonds mortgage bond executed by defendant no. 1, and one Desai were kept undivided - He denied suggestion that mortgage debt due from defendant no. 1, was allotted exclusively to defendant no. 9 - Defendant no. 9, in his evidence also stated that mortgage bond was kept undivided between three branches and that it was not true that it was allotted to his shares at partition - Appellant/defendant no. 4A, had evidently no personal knowledge about partition or terms thereof his statement that defendant no. 9, had told him at time when defendant no. 4, offered his bid at court auction that mortgage bond was allotted exclusively to defendant no. 9's share could not in circumstances of case be true and was rightly believed by trial court and HC - On an analysis of various entries on record of rights relating to lands held by three branches, trial Judge held that plaintiff's father had received at partition, lands admeasuring 203 acres 18 gunthas assessed at Rs. 233-10, defendant nos. 5 to 8 had received 127 - Order accordingly.
