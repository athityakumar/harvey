Garment Cleaning Works v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1962 SC 673; 1961 (2) FLR 534; 1961 (1) LLJ 513; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 711

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 12(5) and 21 - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952, r. 71 - (A) Whether continuous service of 15 yrs is necessary to claim gratuity scheme ? - Held, continuous service of employee for more than 15 yrs - Not a rule of universal application - Gratuity scheme where 10 years' minimum service is prescribed - If employee resigns after 10 years' service - Cannot be said to be improper - (B) What is the effect of dismissal due to misconduct on claims for gratuity?- Further held, gratuity not paid to employee gratuitously or as a matter of boon - If misconduct of employee caused financial loss - Employee to compensate employer for financial loss caused - If any balance from gratuity claimable by employee remains, that is paid to him - Appeal dismissed.
