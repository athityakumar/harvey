Union of India v Ram Kanwar and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 1962 SC 247; 1962 (2) SCJ 670; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, Government of India Act, Requisitioned Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947, Delhi Premises (Requisition and Eviction) Act, 1947, Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Delhi Premises (Requisition and Eviction)Act, 1947 - Govt. of India Act - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Requisition of Land(Continuance of Powers)Act, 1947 - One Babu Ram was owner of Flat No. 5 in New Delhi; respondents 1 to 6 are his sons and widow - By an order dated 14-04-1943, Govt.of India requisitioned said flat u/r.75-A (1) of Defence of India Rules - Period of requisition was extended from time to time, and finally by an order dated 02-04-1946, flat was requisitioned from 15-04-1946, until further orders of Central Govt. - Babu Ram requested Govt. from time to time to de-requisition said flat for his personal use - Govt. of India rejected his request on ground that on surrender by officers of Indian National Airways it would be required for allotment to Central Govt. officers - Flat was afterwards given to present respondent No.7, Triveni Kala Sangam - On 04-11-1952, respondent No.1 again requested Govt. to de-requisition flat mainly on ground that said flat was not in use by officers of Central Govt. but was put in possession of Triveni Kala Sangam which was a private dance and music school - (A) What is the period of limitation prescribed for an appeal against an order of a Single Judge of Punjab High Court to a division bench of same HC? - Held, while u/art. 151 of Limitation Act a period of 20 days is prescribed for preferring an appeal from an order of High Court of Punjab in exercise of its original jurisdiction, u/r. 4 of HC rules for an appeal u/cl. 10 of letters patent a period of limitation of 30 days is provided - r. 4 is made by HC in exercise of legislative power conferred upon said HC u/cl. 27 of letters patent - Limitation Act itself saves operation of said rule - With result that r. 4 applies to such an appeal, whereas art. 151 of Limitation Act will govern appeals not covered by r. 4 or appeals from orders made by other HCs in exercise of their original jurisdiction, if no rule similar to r.4 is made by said HC or HCs - Since r. 4 is invoked in this case, appeal was well within time - (B) Whether respondents are now legally entitled to ask Central Govt. to de-requisition said premises under 1952 Act? - Further held, u/s.24(1) of 1952 Act,1947 Act was repealed - Under sub-section(2) thereof, it was provided that on commencement of Act properties which were subject to requisition under provisions of earlier Act shall be deemed to be property requisitioned u/s. 3 of Act and that all provisions of Act shall apply accordingly - But fiction created by s. 24(2) of Act would operate only upon requisition already made - Fiction could not validate any illegal act of Govt. - On facts of case, it is manifest that flat was not used for any of the purposes for which it was requisitioned for a number of years - If so, it must be held that purpose for which property was requisitioned ceased to exist and respondents have acquired a right to be put in possession thereof - Appeal dismissed.
