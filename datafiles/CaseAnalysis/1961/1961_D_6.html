 Daryao and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1961 SC 1457; 1962 (2) MLJ 16; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; 1962 (1) SCJ 702; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Res Judicata - Whether dismissal of a writ petition filed by a party for obtaining an appropriate writ creates a bar of res judicata against a similar petition filed in this Court u/art. 32 on same or similar facts and praying for same or similar writ? - Held, right given to citizen to move this Court by a petition u/art. 32 and claim an appropriate writ against unconstitutional infringement of his fundamental rights itself is a matter of fundamental right - Doctrine of res judicata is not a technical doctrine applicable only to records - Binding character of judgments pronounced by courts of competent jurisdiction is itself an essential part of rule of law - An original petition for a writ u/art. 32 cannot take place of an appeal against order passed by High Court in petition filed before it u/art. 226 - Scope of writs, orders or directions which High Court can issue in appropriate cases u/art. 226 is concurrent with scope of similar writs, orders or directions which may be issued by this Court u/art. 32 - A writ petition filed by a party u/art. 226 is considered on merits as and contested matter, and is dismissed decision thus pronounced would continue to bind parties unless it is otherwise modified or reversed by appeal or other appropriate proceedings permissible under Constitution - It would not be open to a party to ignore said judgment and move this Court u/art. 32 by an original petition made on same facts and for obtaining same or similar orders or writs - If petition filed in HC u/art. 226 is dismissed not on merits but because of laches of party applying for writ or because it is held that party had an alternative remedy available to it, then dismissal of writ petition would not constitute a bar to a subsequent petition u/art. 32 except in cases where and if facts thus found by HC may themselves be relevant even u/art. 32 - If a writ petition is dismissed in limine and an order is pronounced in that behalf, whether or not dismissal would constitute a bar would depend upon nature of order - If order is on merits it would be a bar - If order shows that dismissal was for reason that petitioner was guilty of laches or that he had an alternative remedy it would not be a bar, except in cases which we have already indicated - If petition is dismissed in limine without passing a speaking order then such dismissal cannot be treated as creating a bar of res judicata - If petition is dismissed as withdrawn it cannot be a bar to a subsequent petition u/art. 32, because in such a case there has been no decision on merits by Court - Petition dismissed.
