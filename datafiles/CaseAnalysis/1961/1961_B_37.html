Bombay Union of Journalists and Others v The Hindu Bombay, and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 427; AIR 1963 SC 318; 1961 (3) FLR 466; 1961 (2) LLJ 436; 1962 (2) SCJ 107; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 893

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Working Journalists, Individual Dispute

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(k) - Whether a dispute between an employer and a single employee can be an industrial dispute u/s. 2(k)? - Held, no it cannot - But it may become one if it is taken up by union or a number of workmen under same employer - They must themselves be directly and substantially interested in dispute - Test is whether at date of reference dispute was taken up or supported by union of workmen of employer against whom dispute is raised by an individual workman or by an appreciable number of workmen - Just as subsequent withdrawal of support will not take away jurisdiction of an Industrial tribunal, subsequent support will not convert what was an individual dispute at time of reference into an industrial dispute - Appeal dismissed.
