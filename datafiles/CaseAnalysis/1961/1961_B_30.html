 Bidi, Bidi Leave's and Tobacco Merchants Association v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 419; AIR 1962 SC 486; 1962 (4) FLR 71; 1961 (2) LLJ 663; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 381; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Minimum Wages

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether it was necessary to revise the minimum wages prescribed by the earlier notification and the mode of determining those wages? - The minimum rates of wages had been fixed on industry cum-regionwise basis and that cls. 3 to 7 were intended to make the fixation of minimum rates of wages - The Act was passed in 1948 in order to provide for fixing minimum rates of wages in certain employments - It is common-ground that employment in any tobacco (including bidi making) manufactory is a schedule employment under the schedule of the Act - s. 2(h) defines wages and it prescribes intra alia, that wages means all, remuneration capable of being expressed in terms of money which would, if the terms of the contract of employment, express or implied, were fulfilled be payable to a person employed in respect of his employment or of work done in such employment, and includes house-rent allowance - s. 5 provides for the procedure for fixing and revising minimum wages - Cls. 1 and 2 clearly fall within the purview of the power conferred on the respondent because they do no more than prescribe the minimum rates of wages as therein specified; but cls. 3 to 7 clearly and unambiguously purport to deal with the terms of the contract between the parties other than that relating to the remuneration - The respondent strenuously contends that cls. 1 and 2 of the notification which have prescribed the minimum rates of wages per 1000 bidis would become ineffective unless cls. 3 to 7 supplement them - Therefore, the Act has made a specific provision for the enforcement and implementation of the minimum rates of wages prescribed by notifications - Held, he present notification purports to ignore the said cls. 1 and 2 of the notification have prescribed the revised minimum rates of wages - Appeal dismissed.
