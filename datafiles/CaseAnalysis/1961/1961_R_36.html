 State of Bihar v Rameshwar Pratap Narain Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 370; AIR 1961 SC 1649; 1962 BLJR 185; 1961 (40) ILR(Pat) 996; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 382; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Bihar Land Reforms Amendment Act, 1959 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31, 31 A and 32 - Whether Bihar Land Reforms Amendment Act is void on ground that it violates arts. 31, 19 and 14 of Constitution ? - Held, impugned provisions of amending Act is a law providing for acquisition by state of rights in an 'estate' within meaning of art. 31 A and consequently, even if it is assumed that they are inconsistent with or take away or abridge any of rights conferred by arts. 14, 19 and 31, they are not void on that ground - State's appeals allowed - Petitions dismissed.
