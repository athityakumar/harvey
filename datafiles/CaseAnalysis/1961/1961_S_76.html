State of Bihar v Lt. Col. K. S. R. Swami

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 195; AIR 1966 SC 1847; 1962 (1) SCJ 607; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Private Forests Act, 1948, Bihar Land Reforms, 1952, Forest Act, 1927

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Conrol - Bihar Land Reforms, 1952 - Bihar Private Forests Act, 1948, ss. 14, 16, 19 and 30 - Indian Forest Act, 1927 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether petitioner's right to land had ceased u/s. 19 of Bihar Private Forests Act? - Held, considering background of provisions in Act for claims to be made u/s. 16, for enquiry into these by Forest Officer and thereafter, for appeals from decision of Forest Officer, after completion of all of which only 'the final notification constituting private forest 'a private protected forest' can be made - A notification under proviso of s. 30 can however be made at any time after notification u/s. 14 has issued - There will be no extinction of rights u/s. 19, if person claiming rights, satisfies Forest Settlement Officer that he had sufficient cause for not preferring claim within period fixed u/s. 16 - Question of thus satisfying Forest Settlement Officer can clearly not arise before period fixed u/s. 16 has expired - All this clearly shows that extinction of rights u/s. 19 can take place only after final notification u/s. 30 has been published - (B) Whether order passed by Forest Officer on basis of r. 4 of Bihar Protected Forests Rules should prevail over permission granted by Collector u/r. 8? - Further held, reasonable way of reading rr. 1 to 4 and r. 8 appears to us to be that rr. 1 to 4 apply to cutting or removal of trees where in spite of such cutting forest would continue to be a forest but cutting of trees which is necessary for clearing land for cultivation or any other purpose is not controlled by rr. 1, 2, 3 or 4 but only by r. 8 - When tenant on land asked for permission to clear land for cultivation and it was this clearing which involved cutting and removal of trees, rr. 1 to 4 had no application and r. 8 under which Collector acted on applied - Permission given in present case by Collector was therefore, in accordance with law and neither Forest Officer nor Govt. had any authority in law to interfere with that permission - Appeal dismissed.
