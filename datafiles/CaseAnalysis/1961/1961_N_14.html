 All India Bank Employee's Association v National Industrial Tribunal and Others (And

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 441; AIR 1962 SC 171; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 414; 1961 (3) FLR 307; 1961 (2) LLJ 385; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(c) - Banking Companies Act, 1949, s. 34A - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Banking Company - Exempt by s. 34A from disclosing secret reserves or provision made for bad and doubtful debts in proceedings under Industrial Disputes Act - Issues are - (A) Scope of 'freedom of association' enshrined in Constitution with regard to Labour Unions, collective bargaining and strike - (B) Constitutional validity of s. 34A of 1949 Act - Held, right guaranteed u/art. 19(l)(c) does not extend to concomitant right to effective collective bargaining or to strike and are restrictive - No contravention of arts. 19(1)(c) and 14 - Appeal dismissed.
