 Chandrakant Krishnarao Pradhan and Another v Collector of Customs, Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 445; AIR 1962 SC 204; 1983 ECR 2183; 1962 (2) MLJ 127; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 127; 1962 (2) SCJ 507; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Customs - Constitution - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 202(2) and 9 - Custom House Agents Licencing Rules, 1960, rr. 10(c) and 11 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 265 - (A) Whether Chief Customs Authority can make rules other then u/s. 202(2)? - Held, yes - u/s. 9, also it can make rule if there be need - Agents must carry out certain provisions of Act, a rule can be made in exercise of two powers together - When a rule or rules are pointed out which sub serve neither special purpose of section nor general purposes of Act that they can be successfully questioned - (B) Whether r. 10(c) and r. 11 of Custom House Agents Licensing Rules are constitutional? - Held, r. 10(c) confers a discretion on Customs Collector to reject an unsuitable candidate - An unreasonable restraint upon right of Custom House Agents to carry on their avocation - R. 11 prescribes a renewal fee of Rs. 50 - Is invalid in as much as it has provided not for a fee out for a tax - It does not offend arts. 14 and 19 of Constitution nor do they go beyond rule making power conferred by s. 202(2) - Petition allowed.
