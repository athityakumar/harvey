Senairam Doongarmall v Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1961 SC 1579; [1961] 42 ITR 392; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, s.52 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Indian Contract Act, s.206 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Assessment - Admissible expenditure - Compensation - Profits and gains - Assessment was made u/s. 23(3)and(4) of Income Tax Act for assessment year 1946-1947 - Income tax Officer excluded the sum paid on account of repairs and treated whole of amount as income taxable under provisions of Income tax Act, after deduction of admissible expenditure - Appeals filed by appellants to Appellate Assistant Commissioner against both assessments were unsuccessful - On appeal, Income Tax Appellate Tribunal opines that receipts represented revenue but on account of use and occupation of premises requisitioned and computed not compensation attributable to such use and occupation at 20 per cent of total receipts in both years and if receipts included income from tea estate inclined to apply r. 24 as first Income tax Officer - HC answered against appellants and dismissed the petitions - Whether payment could be treated as income from property u/s. 9 of Income Tax Act and whether appellants were entitled to benefit of rr. 23 and 24 of Income Tax Rules - Held, Act divides sources of income, profits and gains under various heads in s. 6 - Business dealt with u/s. 10 and primary condition of application of section was that tax payable by assessee under head profits and gains of business in respect of business carried on by him - Where assessee does not carry on business at all, section cannot be made applicable and compensation that he receives cannot bear character of profits of business - When payment was made to compensate assessee no doubt measure was out turn of tea manufactured but that has little relevance - Assessee was not compensated for loss or destruction of or injury to capital asset buildings were taken for time being, but injury was not fixed capital as to business as whole - Entire structure of business was affected that no business was left or done in two years - Requisition did not involve buying of tea either as raw material or even as finished product and once its held that this was not profit at all rr. 23 and 24 of Indian Income tax Rules could not apply and no apportioning amount as laid down in r. 24 - Whole of amount received by assessee was not assessable - Appeal allowed.
