 Custodian of Evacuee Property, Bangalore v Khan Saheb Abdul Shukoor, Etc

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 499; AIR 1961 SC 1087; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - The Mysore Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1949 provided for the appointment of a Custodian of Evacuee Property for the State of Mysore and other officers subordinate to him for the purpose of administering evacuee property in that State - S. 5 laid down that all evacuee property situate in Mysore would vest in-the custodian and as such the Custodian issued a notification by which he declared the properties of the two respondents as evacuee properties which had vested in him, as the respondents had become evacuees - Two claims were filed u/s. 8 of the 1949 Act separately by the two respondents - These claims were investigated by the Deputy Custodian who dismissed the same declaring that the, properties were evacuee properties - Matter was taken upto the custodian who contended that there was not sufficient evidence to prove the respondents as evacuees and consequently the properties in question could not be treated as evacuee properties - The Custodian General gave notices to the respondents u/s. 27 of the 1950 Act in respect of the order of the Custodian and asked them to show cause why the said order of the Custodian be not revised - Appellant contended that the HC was in error when it held that the Custodian-General had no power to set aside the order u/s. 27 of the 1950 Act - In the second place, his contention was that the HC was in error in holding that an appeal lay to it from the order of the Custodian - Whether the Custodian General had jurisdiction to set aside the order of u/s. 27 of the 1950 Act - Held, the Custodian General had the power u/s. 27 of the 1950 Act to call for the record of the proceeding in which the order was passed and consider its legality or propriety and pass such order in relation thereto as he thought fit - Appeals allowed.
