 Hota Venkata Surya Sivarama Sastry v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1967 SC 71; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 120; 1962 (1) SCJ 252; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 244 - Madras Scheduled Areas Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Regulation, 1951 - Madras Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948 - Notification D/- 15-08-1950 - Taking over the estates - (A) Gangole 'A' and 'C' estates - Applicability of Abolition Act - Held, Abolition Act, 1951 did not had it own force - Nor Government of India Act, 1935, render the Act applicable to that area - Abolition Act, 1951, applicable to only a part of Gangole 'A' and 'C' area - There were portions of each estates outside the purview and operation of Act - (B) Gangole estate - Applicability of Madras Permanent Settlement Regulation XXV of 1802 - Held, the parent estate of Gangole estate is a permanent settled estate - Where pattern of sanads and kabuliyats issued under the Madras Permanent-Settlement Regulation - (C) Scheme of taking over - Held, State Govt. having power to take over the entirety of an estate chose - If it takes over some defined portions, the notification liable to be challenged - Such taking over not contemplated by the scheme of enactment - (D) Notification D/- 15-08-1950 - Validity of - Held, the Abolition Act, 1951 was applicable in the estates in question - Effective in law to vest the portion to which it related in the State Govt. - Regulation IV of 1951 made the Abolition Act, 1951 extended to other portions of estates - Notification valid - Appeal dismissed.
