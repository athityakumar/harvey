State of Andhra Pradesh v Thadi Narayana

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1962 SC 240; 1962 CRLJ 207; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 77; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Implied, Alter The Finding, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Murder and Robbery - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 423(1)(b) and 439 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 392 and 411 - In Court of Sessions, Visakhapatnam Division, respondent Thadi Narayana was charged at instance of appellant State of Andhra Pradesh with having committed offences punishable u/s. 302 and s. 392 of IPC, 1860 - Case against her was that on 27-12-1956 at about night meal-time at Gangacholapenta she, committed murder of a minor girl K. Sriramulamma by stabbing her with a knife and thus rendered herself 'liable to be punished u/s. 302 - It was also alleged against her that, in course of same transaction she had robbed said victim of her four pairs of gold Konakammulu and a pair of gold Alakalu and thereby committed the offence of robbery u/s. 392 - Trial judge found that charges against respondent u/ss. 302 and 392 had not been proved beyond a reasonable doubt, and so he acquitted her of said offences - But observed that respondent has committed a offence u/s. 411, IPC, 1860 and sentenced her to undergo rigorous imprisonment for period of two years - Respondent preferred a jail appeal in High Court of Andhra Pradesh - In result conviction and sentence of accused u/s. 411 was set aside and case was remanded to Trial Court for retrial on charges already framed against her - Whether an accused is tried by a Sessions Court on charges of murder and robbery, and Sessions Court acquits accused of those charges and convicts her only of an offence u/s. 411 IPC, 1860 and accused appeals to HC against conviction and sentence but State Govt. does not appeal against acquittal of accused on charges of murder and robbery, is it open to HC to set aside conviction and sentence u/s.411 IPC, 1860 and order accused to be retried on charges of murder and robbery? - (B) When in pursuance of order of HC, Sessions Court again frames charges u/ss. 302 and 392 IPC, 1860 against accused, is it or is it not open to accused to plead statutory bar of autrefois acquit u/s. 403 CrPC, 1898? - Held, answer given by full bench to first, question is that except in exercise of revisional powers u/s. 439 of CrPC, 1898 subject to limitations prescribed therein it is not open to HC to order a retrial on charges on which accused was acquitted by trial court in an appeal by accused against his conviction, though it is empowered to reverse conviction and order a retrial on that charge alone - On second question Full Bench held that it was open to accused to plead bar of autrefois acquit u/s. 403 notwithstanding order of HC unless there is an adjudication on acquittal by HC either u/s. 423(1) (a) or s. 439 of CrPC, 1898 - Revisional application preferred by respondent was allowed, her plea u/s. 403 was upheld and it was ordered that retrial of respondent for offences u/ss. 302 and 392 of IPC, 1860 cannot be proceeded - Criminal Appeal No. 222 of 1959 dismissed.
