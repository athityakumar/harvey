 Pandit Kishan Lal v Ganpat Ram Khosla and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 256; AIR 1961 SC 1554; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - One Ganpat Ram Khosla was Sales Manager of company, which was, since year 1934, tenant for business purposes of a shop belonging to appellant - On 30-08-1954, Company addressed a letter to appellant intimating that it desired to close down its office and Mr Khosla will be carrying on Sewing Machine business in your shop in his personal capacity - Appellant informed Company that unless vacant possession was delivered to him tenancy could not be validly determined - But Company delivered possession of shop to Khosla on 01-09-1954 - Appellant applied u/s. 13 of Act for an order against Khosla and Co. - Whether by unilateral action on its part, Company could require appellant to treat Khosla as his tenant? - Held, a tenancy except where it is at will, may be terminated only on expiry of period of notice of a specified duration under contract, custom or statute governing premises in question - A tenant does not absolve himself from obligations of his tenancy by intimating that as from a particular date will cease to be in occupation under landlord and that some one else whom landlord is not willing to accept will be tenant - Unless possession is delivered to landlord before expiry of period of requisite notice, tenant continues to hold premises during period as tenant - Therefore, by merely assigning rights, tenancy of Company did not come to an end - Company did not surrender its rights to appellant; it sought to transfer its rights to Khosla - Company admittedly did not serve notice as required by law, nor did appellant agree to accept unilateral determination of tenancy by company - Being an assignee of tenancy rights of Company was as much liable to be sued in court of Controller as company for an order in ejectment - Appeal allowed.
