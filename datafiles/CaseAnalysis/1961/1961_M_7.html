 M/S. Harinagar Sugar Mills Ltd. v Shyam Sundar Jhunjhunwala And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 372; AIR 1961 SC 1669; [1961] 31 Comp Cas 387; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Indian Evidence Act, Mineral Concession Rules

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 111 (3)and 155 (3) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 101 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Transfer of Shares - Discretionary power to refuse registration - Directors refused - Directors with 'capricious or mala fide conduct' but only with arbitrary refusal, without stating any reasons - Order made by Central Govt. in appeal u/s 111 (3) - (A) Nature of order and proceedings in court and before Central Govt. - (B) Constitution, art. 136 - Meaning 'Court or tribunal' - Held, order is one made by 'tribunal', in proceedings burden of proof is on appellant and tribunal should record reasons - 'Courts' is used to designate those tribunals which are set up in an organised State for administration of justice and a tribunal which is a part of ordinary hierarchy of Courts of Civil judicature, what distinguish them is that courts have 'an air of detachment' - Matter remitted to High Court.
