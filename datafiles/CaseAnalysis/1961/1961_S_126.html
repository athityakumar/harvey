Deep Chand v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 483; AIR 1961 SC 1527; 1961 CRLJ 705; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1962 (2) SCJ 655; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 662

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Abduction - Indian Penal Code, ss. 347, 365 and 386 - Criminal Procedure Code, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Appellants conviction upheld by High Court and sentence also enhanced - Conviction challenged - (A)Whether evidence given by Magistrate is admissible u/s. 9 of Evidence Act? - Held, yes - Evidence given by magistrate is admissible u/s. 9 but statements recorded by Magistrate in contravention of s. 164 of CrPC are inadmissible sections to be construed harmoniously - Further held, Magistrate not precluded from giving witness regarding certain fact where no statutory provision expressly prohibits him - Re-appreciation of evidence by HC justified - (B) Whether enhancement of sentence by HC justified? - Further held, when Sessions Judge gave such a disproportionately lenient sentences, it was duty of HC to rectify such an obvious error - HC rightly enhanced sentence imposed on appellant - Appeal dismissed.
