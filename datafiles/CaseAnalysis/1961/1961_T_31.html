Dooars Tea Company, Limited v Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 518; AIR 1962 SC 186; [1962] 44 ITR 6; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bengal Agricultural Income-tax Act, 1944, ss. 2(1)(b)(i) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Appellant submitted return for agricultural income and Agricultural Income-tax Officer, however, did not accepted the correctness of the said return and increased the amount - That increased amount included a sum which represented the market value of the appellants agricultural income from bamboos, thatching grass and fuel timber - Appellant alleged that the production from bamboos, thatching grass and fuel timber were used for the business purpose - Tribunal made reference of the instant case to HC, and questions put before HC were answered of favor of revenue - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the bamboo, thatch, fuel, etc. grown by appellant and utilized for its own benefits in its business, was agricultural income within the meaning of the 1944 Act and if that was so, whether, such income be computed u/r. 4 of the rules framed under the 1944 Act - Held, the bamboo, thatch, fuel, etc. grown by appellant and utilized for its own benefits in its business, was agricultural income within the meaning of the 1944 Act since, the words in s. 2(1)(b)(i) of the 1944 Act were, wide, plain and unambiguous and they could not be construed to exclude agricultural produce used by the appellant for its business - And since, construction of r. 4(2) of the rules framed under the 1944 Act was the one which was directly and rightly supported the instant case as to the true scope and effect of s. 2(1)(b)(1) of the 1944 Act, the said income was computed u/r. 4(2) of the rules framed under the 1944 Act - Appeal dismissed.
