The State v Captain Jagjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1962 SC 253; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 421; 1962 CRLJ 215; 1962 (1) SCJ 408; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Official Secrets Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 498(1) and 496 - Official Secrets Act, 1923, ss. 3 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B - Bail - Accused along with two others charged with offence for conspiracy and offences u/s. 3 and 5 of Act - Case against three persons was that they in conspiracy had passed on official secrets to a foreign agency - Held, that for granting bail to an accused gravity of offence has to be looked into even though it has wide powers u/s. 498 of code - Order accordingly.
