 Messrs Ashok Leyland Limited v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1961 SC 1433; 1962 (1) MLJ 78; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 78; 1962 (1) SCJ 165; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 607; 1961 (12) STC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 22 (2) - Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 (2) - Assessee delivering motor vehicles to dealers in other states - State Govt. imposed sales tax claiming sale to have taken place in state - Whether s. 2 of Act, 1956, applies to transactions in question? - Provisions of s. 2 provides that no law of a state imposing tax on sales shall be deemed to be invalid merely because such sales are in course of inter-state trade or commerce - Effect of this provision is merely to liberate state laws from fetter placed on them by art. 286 (2) and to enable such laws to operate on their own terms - Held, sub-s. (2) of new s. 22 of Act, 1939 makes it quite clear that section does not affect liability to tax of any sale or purchase under any other provision of Act, 1939 - After removal of fetter of art. 286 (2), Act, 1939 operating on its own terms makes transactions in question liable to tax, and new s. 22 makes no difference to that position - Act, 1956 applies and assessment on transactions in question cannot now be challenged on ground alleged by assessee - Appeal dismissed.
