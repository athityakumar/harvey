 Orient Paper Mills Limited v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1961 SC 1438; 1961 (2) SCJ 610; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 549; 1961 (12) STC 357

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Recovery Of Tax, Collected, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 14 and 14 A - Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19 (1) (f) and 286 (1) (a) - Whether appellant is entitled to refund in view of judgement of this court in case of State of Bombay and Another v. United Motors (India) Ltd. and Others? - Claim of assesses must fail because of retrospective amendment of Act by Orissa Legislature and introduction of s. 14 A which provides that refund of tax which dealer was not liable to pay can only be claimed by person from whom dealer has actually realised it whether as sales-tax or otherwise - Held, appellant not entitled to refund - Appeals No.'s 273 and 274 of 1960 dismissed and Appeals No.'s 275 to 277 of 1960 allowed.
