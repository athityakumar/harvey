 George Oakes Private Limited and Others v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 47; AIR 1962 SC 1352; 1962 (13) STC 98

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, ss. 3(1)(a) and 3(1)(b) - Madras General Sales Tax (Definition of Turnover and Validation of Assessments) Act, 1954 - Deputy Commercial Tax Officer included in total turnover tax chargeable, and then levied tax on turnover relating to goods liable to additional tax, at rate of 9 pies, and in remaining turnover, at 3 pies for every rupee -Whether additional tax can be included in turnover relating to special goods and resultant sum taxed at 6 pies for every rupee? - Tradesman pays tax at rate of 3 pies for every rupee on all goods and an additional tax of 6 pies on every rupee of turnover relating to certain classes of goods; But, though he pays tax on tax charged by him in price, tax at different rates goes into different turnovers, and there is no additional tax at 6 pies on those goods on which such tax is not imposed by Act - Held, High Court was right in setting aside order of Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal, and restoring order of Deputy Commercial Tax Officer - Appeal dismissed.
