 P. J. Irani v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 1961 SC 1731; 1962 (1) AnWR 92; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 92; 1962 (1) SCJ 194; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Tort

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1949, s.13 - Whether power to exempt u/s. 13 of Madras Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1949, offends art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, power conferred by s.13 of Act on State Govt. to exempt 'buildings or class of buildings' from operation of Act is constitutionally valid - Legislation was enacted for achieving three purposes: (1) regulation of letting; (2) control of rents and (3) prevention of unreasonable eviction of tenants from residential and non-residential buildings - Entire basis for upholding constitutional validity of s. 13 of Act and considering that it did not offend equal protection of law guaranteed by art. 14 of Constitution was that discretion or power conferred upon Govt. was not unguided, uncanalised or arbitrary, but that it had to be exercised in accordance with policy and object of enactment gatherable from preamble as well as its operative provisions - Mere fact that tenant continued in possession after termination of tenancy is by itself no ground why he should be evicted from premises, because it is very policy of Act to protect right of tenants to continue in possession of premises after termination of their term because of great difficulty of their obtaining alternative accommodation - A lessee of reversion stands in same position as a landlord and cannot have any higher rights, nor can appellant derive any assistance from fact that second respondent declined to be a lessee for any term longer than seven years when that option was offered to him by High Court - Appeal dismissed.
