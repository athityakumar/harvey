Imperial Tobacco Company of India Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 523; AIR 1962 SC 1348; 1961 (3) FLR 524; 1961 (2) LLJ 414

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Negligence - Standing Order - Workman responsible for the maintenance of leave registers denied his responsibility with respect to the charges of wilfully making false entries and consequently an inquiry was made against him - After the inquiry was closed, the branch manager passed the order against that workman - Reference was made to the labour court by the Govt. - In the circumstances the labour court held that the inquiry which resulted in dismissal was not a valid inquiry as required by the Standing Orders of appellant - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the workman was responsible for the charges - Held, that based on the entries made by the workman, the sort of negligence which had occurred had happened for the first time - In the circumstances there was no justification for interfering with the order of reinstatement - Appeal dismissed.
