Kundan Lal Rallaram v Custodian, Evacuee Property, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 528; AIR 1961 SC 1316; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 85

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 118 - Presumption of law - Rebuttal - Burden of proof - Presumption of law u/s. 118 of NI Act, 1881 - Transaction of exchange entered into by one Nathuram and another Abdul with respect to properties in India and Pakistan at time of partition - Execution of promissory note by Nathuram in favour of Abdul in respect of one amount which was payable - Endorsement by Abdul in favour of appellant - It was stated that endorsement was made in consideration of transfer of stock-in-trade of appellant's business - Appellant made a demand on Nathuram, which was denied by him - Declaration of property of Abdul as 'Evacuee's property' and promissory note was also declared to be an evacuee property, under Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Deputy Custodian under Evacuee Act, 1950 held endorsement of promissory note to be not bonafide or for valuable consideration - Whether rebuttal of presumption of law u/s. 118 of NI Act, 1881 by Deputy Custodian based on any evidence, direct or circumstantial? - Special rule of evidence u/s. 118 of NI Act, 1881 and 'burden of proof' looked into and deliberated upon - Held, yes proposition of law enunciated by Custodian-General is correct, and on basis of relevant legal evidence he had rebutted presumption - Appeal dismissed.
