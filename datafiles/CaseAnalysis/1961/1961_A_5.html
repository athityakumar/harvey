Raja Ram Mahadev Paranjype and Others v Aba Maruti Mali and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1962 SC 753; 1962 (64) BomLR 569; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 5, 14, 25(1) and 29 - Whether for purposes of s. 25(2) a tenant is to be considered as having failed to pay rent for any year in respect of which he had been granted relief u/s 25(1)? - Possession of lands held by tenants - Tenants entitled to relief against forfeiture on equitable principles - Equity does not operate to annul a statute - Inspite of relief granted u/s 25(1), tenant remains a tenant who made default in paying rent for year within period specified in s. 14 and that is tenant mentioned in s. 25 (2) - Words 'as he deems fit' do not bestow a power to make any order on considerations dehors statute which authorities consider best according to their notions of justice - Provisions of s. 25 (2) does not require three successive years of default but it is satisfied where tenant has been in default for any three years - Nothing in s. 25 (2) to justify view that in such a case year of default cannot be taken into account in computing three years there mentioned - Appeal allowed.
