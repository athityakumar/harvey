Mahanth Ramswaroop Das v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 86; AIR 1961 SC 1147; [1961] 42 ITR 770; 1962 (1) SCJ 415; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 405

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Estate

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes -Bihar Agricultural Income-tax Act 1948, s. 28(3) - Assessment year -Agricultural income - Tax Liability - Maintainability - Appellant was Mahant of Asthal Estate in Bihar -Court Receiver was appointed to manage estate - Receiver handed over charge of estate to appellant who submitted a return of income of estate to Agricultural Income-tax Officer - Agricultural Income tax Officer assessed and ordered appellant to pay Rs. 20,290- Appeals against the order of assessment preferred to Commissioner of Agricultural Income-tax and Board of Agricultural Income-tax were unsuccessful- Hence, instant appeal- Whether petitioner could have been legally assessed for income of Estate in when Estate was in the hand of Receiver? - Held, - SC held that liability to pay tax was charged on agricultural income of every person -Income though collected by receiver was income of the appellant - Receiver was deemed to be assessee and was liable to pay tax which did not absolve appellant from obligation to pay agricultural income-tax - When property was in possession of Receiver taxing authorities proceeded against owner of income and assessed tax against him - connotation of" person " undoubtedly included a receiver, trustee, common manager, administrator or executor, and by such inclusion, it was open to taxing authorities to assess tax against any such persons and income in hand of owner was not exempted from liability to assessment of tax - Appeal dismissed.
