 Chhotabhai Jethabhai Patel and Company v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1962 SC 1006; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 118; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Provisional Collection of Taxes Act, 1931 - Finance Act, 1951 - Excisability of goods - Appellants were tobacco merchants and manufacturers of biris - They own private warehouses licensed u/r. 1944 Rules - Bill was introduced - Cl. 7 of the Bill made provision for the amendment of the 1944 Act by way of alteration of duties on 'tobacco manufactured and un manufactured' - A declaration u/s. 3 of the 1931 Act was made in respect of the provision for imposing the duties on tobacco u/cl. 7 of the bill - Appellants paid the excise duty at the rates imposed u/cl. 7 of the bill and obtained clearance certificates in regard to the tobacco moved out from their warehouses - Consequential provisions were enacted in s. 7 (2) of the 1951 Act - In pursuance of s. 7 (2) a demand was made upon the appellants for the payment of the duty payable by them - Appellants filed a petition u/art. 226 of the constitution in the HC urging that the retrospective operation given to s. 7(1) by sub-s. (2) thereof was illegal, ultra vires and unconstitutional - HC repelled all the contentions disputing the legislative competence and the constitutionality of the legislation contained in s. 7(2) of the 1951 Act but upheld the objection to the adequacy of the procedure for recovery based on the limited scope of r. 10 of the 1944 rules - Further and a fresh demand was made for the payment of the duty in terms of s. 7(2)(b) of the 1951 Act - On petition filed in the HC by the appellants challenging the validity of this demand which was rejected - Hence this appeal - Whether HC was right in rejecting the petition of the appellant - Held, contention of infringement of the appellants' right under Art. 19(1)(f) was unsound - It was for department in its discretion to select anything for taxation or to exclude it - The challenge to the legality of the tax in dispute was not based and was unsustainable on the ground of specific limitation or prohibition on Parliamentary power but has been raised on the ground of the infringement of an article containing the principles of the State's power of control - Appeals dismissed.
