 A. V. Venkateswaran, Collector of Customs, Bombay v Ramchand Sobhraj Wadhwani and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 138; AIR 1961 SC 1506; 1962 (64) BomLR 386; 1983 ECR 2151; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1327; 1962 (1) MLJ 83; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 83; 1962 (1) SCJ 170; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 753

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Tariff Act, 1934, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, Indian Tariff (Amendment) Act, 1954, Finance Act, 1949

Summary: Customs - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Indian Tariff (Amendment) Act, 1954 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Sea Customs Act, 1878 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Tariff Act, 1934 - Whether High Court should have rejected writ petition of respondent in limine because he had not exhausted all statutory remedies open to him for having his grievance redressed? - Rule that party who applies for issue of a high prerogative writ should, before he approaches Court, have exhausted other remedies open to him under the law, is not one which bars jurisdiction of High Court to entertain petition or to deal with it, but is rather a rule which courts have laid down for exercise of their discretion - Held, HC has not exercised its discretion improperly in entertaining writ application or granting relief prayed for by respondent and that no care for interference by us in an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution has been made out - Appeal dismissed.
