Donald Miranda and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City Ii

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 267; AIR 1961 SC 1233; [1961] 42 ITR 166; 1962 (1) SCJ 112; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.25(4) - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Excess Profits Tax Ordinance, 1943, s.2 - Income - Excess profit - Deposit - Repayment - Exempted from tax - Firm carrying business of wine merchants, was registered under Income Tax Act,1922 - Firm dissolved on 1945 and Private limited company succeeded firm's business - Firm taxed to excess profits tax under 1940 Act - Firm became entitled to repayment of portion of excess profits tax but submitted that amount refunded was business profit and exempt from tax u/s.25(4) of 1922 Act - Income Tax Officer rejected submission and appellants was assessed to income-tax and super tax and balance was confirmed - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal assumed that sum refunded was income from business and exempt from Income Tax u/s.25(4)of 1922 Act - HC assumed that amount refunded was income from other sources taxable u/s.12 of 1922 Act and appellants were not entitled to benefit of s. 25(4)of 1922 Act - Whether repayment of excess profits tax was income of appellant and whether amount was liable to taxation - Held, it appeared that amount of excess profits tax was allowable deduction for purpose of computation of business profits of assessee u/s.12(1) of 1940 Act and when it or portion of it was refunded it was treated as income of assessee - Amount refunded not lose its character had before deposit and it was erroneous view to take that income was assessable u/s.12 of 1922 Act and not u/s.10 of 1942 Act but if falling u/s.10 appellants were entitled to get benefit of s.25(4)of 1922 Act and amount was not liable to taxation - Appeals disposed of.
