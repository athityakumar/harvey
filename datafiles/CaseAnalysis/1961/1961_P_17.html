Pratap Chand v Ram Narayan and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 109; 1962 (1) SCJ 158; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property -Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - One Ramchandar Jat originally owned Annas 10/8 share in Mauza Tamalawadi - Ramchandar executed a simple mortgage deed on 27-07-1920, in favour of Seth Ram Jiwan and two minors Ram Narain and Radhey Sham - Plaintiffs-respondents are representatives of mortgagees - On 27-08-1926, defendant-appellant purchased Annas -/5/4 share belonging to other share holders in village - Appellant brought a suit against Ramchandar who was lambardar of village for profits, in which decree was passed against Ramchandar - In execution of that decree appellant purchased entire Annas -/10/8 share of Ramchandar in village about year 1932 - In consequence, appellant became owner of entire village subject to mortgage of respondents on Annas -/5/4 share therein - On July 27, 1932, respondents sued Ramchandar on basis of their mortgage-deed and a preliminary decree for sale was passed in March, 1937 - By year 1940 respondents were owners of Annas -/5/4 share in village while appellant was owner of Annas -/10/8 share - Appellant was also a lambardar - There were certain other lands which were nominally recorded as Muafi Khairati in name of Ramchandar's mother but were actually in possession of Ramchandar - It appears that Ramchandar was ejected from these lands also and they came into, possession of appellant - Further appellant as a lambardar came into possession of certain other lands by surrender or otherwise - Respondents filed a suit for partition claiming half share in lands of Ramchandar and his mother which came into possession of appellant - They also claimed a share in other lands which came into possession of appellant as lambardar - Whether mortgage deed of 1920 which is basis of title of respondents did not include said plots, and sale certificate also did not include these plots, though some other plots were mentioned therein and hence, respondents were not entitled to these plots as accession to mortgage? - Held, what was mortgaged was entire half share of Ramchandar in -/10/8 share which he owned in village - It is true that sir is not specifically mentioned in mortgage but as mortgage was of entire -/5/4 share out of -/10/8 share it will include (unless there is a specific exclusion of sir) area of sir also pertaining to share mortgaged - Coming to sale certificate we find that it certifies that respondents had purchased -/5/4 share in village with abadi, khudkashat, chhotaghas and all rights pertaining to share - It is true that khudkashat and chhotaghas are specifically mentioned in sale certificate but words all rights pertaining to share appearing in sale certificate would include such proprietary rights in sir land as belonged to share mentioned in sale certificate - When Ramchandar's ex-proprietary rights came to an end and land came into possession of appellant and became khudkashat, mortgage would cover this khudkashat land to extent of mortgagees' share therein - Therefore, High Court that on ex-proprietary tenancy being extinguished, sir land which would otherwise have remained in exclusive possession of Ramchandar as an ex-proprietary tenant became an accession to mortgaged property and respondents would be entitled to half of it on their purchasing 5/4 share in execution of decree on mortgage - Appeal dismissed.
