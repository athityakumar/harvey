Radhakrishna Sivadutta Rai and Others v Tayeballi Dawoodbeai

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 401; AIR 1962 SC 538; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 81; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, s. 230 - (A) Whether bought and sold notes showing a material variation taken together can be relied upon for purpose of proving terms of contract? - Held, it cannot be - Established commercial usage, if there is no variation or disparity in bought and sold notes, bought and sold notes issued by brokers constitute terms of contract between parties for whom brokers act - Where bought and sold notes clearly, indicate that appellant was acting for disclosed principal, fact that he (the agent) did not add relevant description in his signature or used word 'we' in operative portion of letters would not materially alter fact spoken to by notes that appellant was acting on behalf of disclosed principal - Letters, like confirmation slips are and must be presumed to be consistent with notes and so it would be unreasonable to attach undue importance to signature and to use of relevant works 'we' and 'you' - Appeal dismissed.
