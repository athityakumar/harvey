Additional Settlement Commissioner v Yeshwant Madhao Mahajan

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 328; AIR 1966 SC 1971; 1967 MahLJ 216; 1962 (2) SCJ 159; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 710; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights Act, 1951 - States Reorganization Act, 1956 - Berar Land Revenue Code, 1928, - Lease Deed - Home farm - Undivided share - Acquisition of rights - Compensation - Validity - In 1947 X executed a sale deed with Y for an undivided half share of Land in his village - On same day X executed a Lease deed for 5 years in respect of same land for cultivation - When the 1951 Act came into force compensation officer enquired about assessment of compensation - Y claimed to retain possession of the half share in all fallow lands in village which had been leased for cultivation to X by stating that those lands were 'home farm' -Claim was rejected by Compensation Officer and was confirmed by Additional Settlement Commissioner - HC quashed the order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the lands in instant case could be regarded as 'home-farm'? - Held, in respect of an undivided interest in land, superior holder was not entitled to benefit of s. 4 of the 1951 Act because land in instant case was not a 'holding' and could not be regarded as 'Home- farm' - Part of a holding or an undivided interest in a " holding" in Berar could also have been 'home farm' land if it otherwise fulfilled requirement of s.2(g) (3) (i) to (iii) of the 1951 Act - land was already granted in lease for cultivation which had to be retained in possession of proprietor by virtue of s. 4(2) of the 1951 Act - Grant of lease for cultivation proved an intention on part of proprietor that land was to be converted into for agricultural purposes and default on part of lessee to cultivate land would not deprive proprietor of benefit granted to him by statute - Therefore, order of HC was upheld holding that words of s. 2(g)(3)(ii) of 1951 Act were explicit and survey number which was lying fallow was held by lessee from superior holder was within definition of 'home-farm' - Appeal Dismissed.
