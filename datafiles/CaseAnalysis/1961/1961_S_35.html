 Shri Madhav Laxman Vaikunthe v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 378; AIR 1962 SC 8; 1961 (3) FLR 365; 1962 (1) SCJ 134; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 886

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Government of India Act 1935, misa, Indian Limitation Act

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Government Of India, Act, 1935, s. 240 (3) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 102 - Reversion its meaning - Civil Judge, Senior Division, held in favour of appellant but High Court in appeal held to contrary - (A) Whether a public servant officiating in a higher post but reverted to his substantive rank as a result of an adverse finding against him in a departmental enquiry for misconduct, can be said to have been reduced in rank within meaning of s. 240 (3) of 1935 Act? - It has been found that requirements of s. 240 (3) of 1935 Act, corresponding to art. 311 (2) had not been fully complied with - His reversion in rank, therefore, was in violation of constitutional guarantee - In view of these considerations it must be held that HC was not right in holding against appellant that his reversion was not a punishment contemplated by s. 240 (3) of 1935 Act - Thus, decision of HC reversed and that of Trial Court is restored - (B) Whether salary was not included within term 'wages'? - Held, relied on Tarachand's case Indlaw Old Reference(1947) F C R 89 - In Court's opinion, no good reasons have been adduced before court for not following aforesaid decision of Federal Court - Appeal partly allowed.
