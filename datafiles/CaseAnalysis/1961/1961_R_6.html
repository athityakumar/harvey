Andhra Bank Limited v R. Srinivasan and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 439; AIR 1962 SC 232; 1963 (1) AnWR 14; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 14; 1962 (2) SCJ 676; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Court, Foreign Judgment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O.21, r. 22 and O. 22, r. 4 - Suit based on foreign decree obtained by appellant and against respondent by Hyderabad Court - Amount due to appellant, so present appeal - Legal Representatives of deceased were nonresident foreigners - Respondent contended that Hyderabad court had no jurisdiction as they are foreigners therefore decree passed by City Civil Courts was without jurisdiction - Whether City Civil Court of Hyderabad had jurisdiction to try suit and that respondents were bound by decrees or not? - Rule of private international

law is that all personal actions must be filed in courts of country where defendant resides - Rule of private international law has to be applied in time at which suit is instituted - Held, Hyderabad Court had jurisdiction to try suits when suit filed - Nothing in rule of private international law to suggest that said jurisdiction automatically came to an end when defendants died leaving his Legal Representatives persons who were non-resident foreigners - Judgements pronounced by City Civil Court are judgements by court of competent jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
