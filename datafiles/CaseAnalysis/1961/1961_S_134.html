 Kalyan Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 216; AIR 1962 SC 1183; 1962 ALJ 523; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 76

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 68 C, 68 D and 68 F - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether u/s. 68 C of Motor Vehicles Act, State Transport Undertaking has to form its opinion and prepare a scheme for nationalisation, but in present cases State Govt. initiated schemes and therefore, schemes were not validly made? - Held, though functions of different bodies are clearly demarcated in case of undertakings run by corporations, there is overlapping in case of an undertaking run by a State Govt. - Statutory authority, namely, State transport undertaking, has to form an opinion within meaning of s. 68 C of Act, and opinion must necessarily be that of State Govt. which runs it - If State Govt. running an undertaking forms an opinion, it can legitimately be said that statutory authority i.e., the State transport undertaking, has formed opinion - Opinion was formed by State transport undertaking within meaning of s. 68 C of Act, and that there was nothing illegal in manner of initiation of said scheme - (B) Whether scheme is invalid as neither objection to proposed scheme were heard nor were they approved by State Govt. as they should be u/s. 68 D of Motor Vehicles Act? - Further held, there is no doubt that scheme has been duly published u/s. 68 D(3) and if objection to invalidity of scheme on ground that objection were not heard by an authority competent in that behalf cannot be permitted to be raised in this Court for first time during course of arguments, statutory consequences prescribed by s. 68 F must ensue - Appeal dismissed.
