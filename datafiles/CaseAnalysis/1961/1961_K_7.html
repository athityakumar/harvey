 Badri Narayan Singh v Kamdeo Prasad Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1962 SC 338; 1962 BLJR 446; 1962 (41) ILR(Pat) 534; 1962 (1) MLJ 39; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 39; 1962 (1) SCJ 63; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 374(4) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Whether second appeal was filed beyond period of limitation and was not maintainable and that when it was dismissed as not maintainable first appeal would we barred by principle of res judicata? - Held, existence of one finding and one decision simply contemplates presence of common points in all appeals and absence of any different point in those appeals, and consequently, of one decision on those common points in all appeals - Question of res judicata arises only when there are two suits and even when there are two suits it has been held that a decision given simultaneously cannot be a decision in former suit - When there is only one suit, question of res judicata does not arise at all - Decision of High Court in two appeals, though stated in one judgment, really amounted to two decisions and not to one decision common to both appeals - Appeal dismissed.
