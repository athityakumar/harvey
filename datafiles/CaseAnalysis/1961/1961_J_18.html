Uppalapati Veera Venkata Satyanarayanaraju and Another v Josyula Hanumayamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 426; AIR 1967 SC 174; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 81; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 35; 1962 (2) SCJ 115; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 910

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Possessory title over the property - Determination of - Held, High Court was right in holding that there was no finding by Subordinate Judge on a crucial issue - Where person in whose favour attornment had been made has no title, a mere paper attornment would not be enough to establish, as against third parties, possession of person in whose favour attornment has been made and it will still have to be shown that possession was effective in sense that person who attorned also paid rent voluntarily or under a decree to person in whose favour he made attornment - Trial court had given a finding in favour of first respondent in that respect which Subordinate Judge overlooked - HC did have jurisdiction to remand case to Subordinate Judge for a finding on the issue - Appeal dismissed.
