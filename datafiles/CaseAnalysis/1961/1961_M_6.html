M. V. Joshi v M. U. Shimpi and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 112; AIR 1961 SC 1494; 1962 (64) BomLR 158; 1961 CRLJ 696; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 145; 1961 (2) SCJ 571; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 986

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 ss. 16(1), 7(1) - Adulteration - Conviction - Justifiability - Appellant, dealer in butter was running shop at Thane - On 27-06-1957 Food inspector/respondent no.1 visited shop of appellant and purchased butter and later he notified his intention of getting it analysed/tested - Based on analyst report, respondent no.1 filed complaint against appellant for offence u/ss. 16, 7(1) of the Act - Trial court acquitted appellant holding that respondent no.1 failed to prove that butter was purchased from shop of appellant and same was sent for testing - Respondent no.1 filed appeal before High court which set aside order of acquittal and convicted appellant under the Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there was a conflict of view, in High court as regards the question whether butter made from curd would be butter within the meaning of the rule. Indeed, it was brought to our notice, that on 16- 04-1960, Central Government made another rule amending rule A-11.05 by inserting the word " curd " in the definition of butter and the amended definition ' butter means the product prepared exclusively from milk, cream or curd of cow or buffalo...'. This must have been made to clarify the position in view of the conflicting decisions. Therefore, sentence of fine would meet the ends, of justice. Hence, set aside the sentence of two months' rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs 250/and instead sentence the appellant to pay a fine of Rs. 500/-. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio: The Act was passed to make provisions for the prevention of adulteration of food.
