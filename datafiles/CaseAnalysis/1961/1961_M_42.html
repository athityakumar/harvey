Messrs Dhanrajamal Gobindram v Messrs Shamji Kalidas and Company

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 342; AIR 1961 SC 1285; 1962 (64) BomLR 169; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 1020

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Contract Act, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 20, 46, 47, 7, 12, 32, 33, 36 and 37 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 - Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Indian Contract Act, s. 29 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Sale of Goods Act - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - (A) Whether in view of invalidity of contract, on grounds that agreement was in contravention of 'import policy of Govt. of India' and Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 as a whole, arbitration clause in agreement was not binding? - Held, contract is saved by s. 21 - Contract was not void for illegality - (B) Whether agreement was void because of vagueness and uncertainty of phrase 'subject to usual force majeure clause'? - According to s. 29 of Indian Contract Act, 'Agreements', meaning of which is not certain or capable of being made certain are void - Further held, applying these tests to present case and in light of provisions of s. 29 of Indian Contract Act, it is clear that clause impugned is capable of being made certain and definite - Appeal dismissed.
