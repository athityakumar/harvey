Management of Messrs May and Baker (India) v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 513; AIR 1967 SC 678; 1961 (2) FLR 594; 1961 (2) LLJ 94

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s.) and 25-F - Delhi Shops and Embellishments Act, 1954, s. 22(1)(b)(i) - Dispute between company and its workmen - (A) Medical facilities - Held, Tribunal has fixed ceiling and no reason to disagree with view taken by tribunal in this behalf - (B) Leave facilities - Privilege leave - Further held, s. 22(1)(b)(i) provides that privilege leave admissible may be accumulated up to maximum period of thirty days - Not open to tribunal to allow accumulation of leave up to twelve weeks - (C) Working hours - Further held, tribunal has reduced working hours by half an hour each day - No justification for this reduction - Existing working hours which are well within hours of work prescribed under Delhi Shops and Establishments Act will continue - (D) Uniform - Further Further held, present practice is that uniform is not allowed to be taken home - It seems that practice in this respect varies in different concerns - (E) Order of reinstatement - Company's case is that at time M was discharged definition of word 'workman' u/s. 2(s) of Industrial Disputes Act did not include employees like him who was a representative - If nature of duties is manual or clerical then person must be held to be a workman - Further held, nature of duties assigned to him not any clerical or manual work - Not a workmen - (F) Retrenchment compensation - Further held, s. 25-F came into force on 24-10-1953, while services of I were terminated on 30-09-1953, therefore not entitled to compensation - Though tribunal was wrong in holding that s. 25-F applied, no reason to interfere with order, as industrial tribunals use to give retrenchment compensation even before s. 25-F was enacted and that Section merely standardized practice which was generally prevalent - Under scheme in force in company at relevant time, gratuity could only be awarded to an employee who had been in service for five years - I was not in service for that period - Tribunal cannot compel company to grant gratuity against scheme of gratuity in force - Appeals disposed of.
