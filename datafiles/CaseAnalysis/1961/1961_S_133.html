 Harnam Das v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 364; AIR 1961 SC 1662; 1961 CRLJ 815; 1961 (2) ILR(All) 437; 1962 (1) MLJ 112; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 112; 1962 (1) SCJ 223; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 487

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Religious Feelings

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 99A and 99D - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 124A, 153A and 295 A - Code Civil Procedure, 1908 - The appellant was author of two books in Hindi called Sikh Mat Khandan Part 1 and Bhoomika Nazam Sikh Mat Khandan which he had published in April, 1953 - On July 30, 1953, Govt. of Uttar Pradesh, respondent in this appeal, made an order u/s. 99 A of that Code forfeiting these books which were thereupon seized and taken away - Order that is challenged in present appeal - The High Court then proceeded to examine books for itself and found that their contents were 'obnoxious and highly objectionable' and dismissed application observing that appellant had 'entirely failed to show that books did not contain matters which promoted feelings of enmity and hatred between different classes, or which did not (sic) insult or attempt to insult religion or religious beliefs of Sikhs - Whether HC was right in rejecting argument that order of forfeiture should be set aside on ground that grounds of Govt's opinion were not stated in Govt. notification as required by s. 99 A? - Held, it seems clear that duty cast by s. 99 D on judges of HC is not to see whether in a particular case grounds stated by Govt. for forming its opinion are correct, but to see whether opinion formed was correct - To perform this duty one and only way is to examine document which in Govt's opinion contains matter complained of argument that HC is not in a position to perform this duty u/s.99 D satisfactorily in absence of a statement by Govt. of grounds of its opinion appears to be therefore wholly unsound - Judges of HC of Allahabad felt no difficulty in coming to a conclusion on question before them even though Govt. had not stated grounds of its opinion - Appeal dismissed.
