Gopal Das Sindhi and Others v State of Assam and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 527; AIR 1961 SC 986; 1961 CRLJ 39; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 217

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 156(3), 439 and 561-A - Constitution Of India, 1953 art. 227 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 448 - (A) Whether Magistrate acted without jurisdiction in directing police to register a case investigate and submit a charge-sheet, if warranted? - Held, complaint disclosing cognizable justifies Magistrate sending complaint, u/s. 156(3) to police for investigation, on other hand, there may be occasions when Magistrate may exercise his discretion and take cognizance of a cognizable offence - (B) Whether Magistrate acted illegally in framing a charge u/s.448 under provisions of s. 251-A as provisions of said section applied only to warrant cases whereas s. 448 of IPC was triable as a summons case? - Further held, after amendment of CrPC an offence u/s. 448 is triable as a summons case and Magistrate adopted procedure prescribed for a case triable as a warrant case, this irregularity does not vitiate proceedings and is curable by provisions of s. 537, as no prejudice to accused is established - (C) Whether case against appellants should be quashed as dispute was of a civil nature - Further held, not the stage to express any opinion on third issue - Appeal dismissed.
