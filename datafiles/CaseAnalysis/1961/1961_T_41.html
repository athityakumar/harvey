Dr. H. S. Rikhy and Others v New Delhi Municipal Committee

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1962 SC 554; 1962 (64) PLR 699; 1962 (1) SCJ 612; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, ss. 2(j), 2(c) 2(g), 38 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss. 18, 46, 18 and 47 - (A) Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act - Whether definition of 'Tenant' include licensee under Act? - Held, it does not - Act is not intended to be so comprehensive as to include within its sweep not only tenants properly so called, but also licensees - While defining the term 'premises', has excluded a room in a hotel or a lodging house from its definition - (B) Whether use of term 'rent' in receipts passed by a Municipality to occupiers of shops in market can preclude landlord from pleading that there is no relationship of landlord and tenant? - Further held, no, it cannot - 'Rent' may be used in legal sense of recompense paid by tenant to landlord for exclusive possession of premises occupied by him, can be used in generic sense - In legal sense only be reserved on a demise of immovable property - Act applies only to that species of 'letting' by which relationship of landlord and tenant is created, that is to say, by which an interest in property, however limited in duration, is created - (C) Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 - What are formalities of contract between municipality and tenants under Act? - Further held, s. 18 of Act contains authorisation in favour of Municipal Committee to enter into contracts, to transfer property belonging to it - Contract has to satisfy conditions of s. 46 (2), if value or amount thereof exceeds Rs. 500 - Transfer binding - An instrument in writing, executed by President or vice President and at least two other members of Committee, and execution by them should have been attested by Secretary - Condition not fulfilled not binding - Provisions are mandatory and are not merely directory - Definitions of 'landlord', 'premises' and 'tenant' in s. 2 of Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, postulate relationship of landlord and tenant which can come into existence only by a transfer of interest in immovable property, in pursuance of a contract - (D)Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 - Whether Municipal Committee is estopped by its conduct from challenging enforceability of contract even though there is no contract in writing as required by s. 47? - Where a statute makes a specific provision that a body corporate has to act in a particular manner, and in no other, that provision of law being mandatory and not directory, has to be strictly followed - Appeal dismissed.
