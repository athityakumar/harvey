Seth Hiralal Patni v Sri Kali Nath

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1962 SC 199; 1961 (2) ILR(All) 481; 1961 (2) KerLR 555; 1961 (2) MLJ 157; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 157; 1961 (2) SCJ 592; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Decrees and Orders Validating Act, 1936

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 21 - Decrees and Orders Validating Act, 1936 - Appeal arose from execution proceedings, taken by decree holder where appellant, as defendant in action, challenges territorial jurisdiction of Bombay High Court on original side - Whether suit filed u/cl. 12 of Letters Patent was outside territorial jurisdiction? - Held, objection as to local jurisdiction of a court does not stand on same footing as an objection to competence of a Court to try a case, competence of a Court to try a case goes to very root of jurisdiction, and where it is lacking, it is a case of inherent lack of jurisdiction - Objection as to local jurisdiction of a Court can be waived and having consented to have controversy between parties resolved by reference to arbitration through court, defendant deprived himself of right to question authority of Court, therefore, defendant is estopped from challenging jurisdiction of HC - Appeal dismissed.
