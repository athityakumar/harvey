Hari Shankar v Rao Girdhari Lal Chowdhury

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 157; AIR 1963 SC 698; 1962 (1) KerLR 389; 1962 (64) PLR 1097; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 933

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, s. 35 - Revisional powers of High Court - Whether High Court in exercise of its revisional power is entitled to re-assess value of evidence and to substitute its own conclusions of fact in place of those reached by Court below? - Held, s. 35 is undoubtedly worded in general terms, but it does not create right to have case reheard - A right of appeal carries with it a right of rehearing on law as well as on fact - Power to hear a revision is generally given to a superior Court so that it may satisfy itself that a particular case has been decided according to law - U/s. 115 of CPC HC's power are limited to see whether in a case decided, there has been an assumption of jurisdiction where none existed, or a refusal of jurisdiction where it did, or there has been material irregularity or illegality in exercise of that jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
