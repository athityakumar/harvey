The Management Of Marina Hotel v The Woremen

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 322; AIR 1962 SC 1258; 1961 (3) FLR 266; 1961 (2) LLJ 431; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Delhi Shops and Establishments Act, 1954 s. 22 - Industrial Award - Justification - Dispute between workmen-respondents and appellant-hotel was referred to Tribunal for adjudication which was awarded in favour of respondents - Hence instant appeal - Matters under dispute comprised of number of items but under instant appeal SC was concerned with only few points - Whether award of Tribunal was justified in following points (1) Bonus for years 1953-1954 & 1954-1955 (2) Causal-Cum-Sick leaves (3) Scale of pay & Dearness allowances - Held, appellant contented with regards to bonus that as workmen get share in service-charges and also some amount by way of tips from customers no bonus can be awarded to them, Tribunal correctly stated that amounts received through distribution of service charges and tips are quite inadequate to bring wages to level of a living wage besides, all workmen of appellant share in distribution of service charges and thus stand on same footing so far as distribution of bonus from available surplus, if any, was concerned as to available surplus for year 1953-54 & 1954-1955 taking into account prior charges three months' bonus would be justified however Tribunal and appellant both did not prepared chart in accordance with Full Bench formula to work out available surplus going thru evidence on record by way of balance sheet and flow charts of subsequent years Tribunal was justified in awarding three months bonus in instant case - Contentions of appellant was justified with regards to Sick Leave as Tribunal had gone beyond scope of s. 22 of the Act 1954 in granting 15 days Sick leaves and it was accordingly reduced to 12 days as provided under Act of 1954 - Appellant with regards to Scale of pay stated that his hotel falls under B category hotel and Tribunal had awarded scale pay similar to A category hotel SC attention was also drawn to statement of Secretary Hotel Workers' Union on behalf of respondents who stated that workers placed under appellant hotel was category A, which included almost all hotels in New D and other evidence shows that there was no reasons to disregard with view of Tribunal that appellant had capacity to pay scales of pay fixed by Tribunal it was true that profits had gone down since 1954-55 even so there was no reason to hold that 'Tribunal was wrong in view that hotel would be able to bear increase in wage-bill due to introduction of those scales of pay thus award of Tribunal was justified and dearness allowance fixed by Tribunal was in accordance with present scale where workmen were demanding Rs. 35, but Tribunal had fixed Rs. 20 per month and has 'provided that where a workman takes his meals at hotel amount will be reduced by Rs. 15; but where he lives in accommodation provided by hotel but does not take his meals there amount will be reduced by Rs. 5; further where he both lives and takes his meals in hotel there will be no dearness allowance paid to him - Thus modification in matter of Sickness leaves as indicated above view absorbed by Tribunal was fully justified - Appeal dismissed.
