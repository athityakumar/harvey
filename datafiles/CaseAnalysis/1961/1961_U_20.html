S. N. Dutt v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 487; AIR 1961 SC 1449; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 188; 1961 (1) KerLR 543; 1962 (1) MLJ 69; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 69; 1962 (1) SCJ 115; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Appellant claimed that he suffered heavy loss due to misconduct and carelessness on part of Bengal and Assam Railway administration - Appellant claimed damages - Notices were given u/s. 80 to secretary respondent - Respondent contended that notices were defective - Subordinate Judge and High Court held that notices were defected, therefore, suit was rightly rejected - Whether notices in question were in confirmity with s. 80 of CPC? - Held, provisions of s. 80 were imperative and must be strictly complied with - Appeal dismissed.
