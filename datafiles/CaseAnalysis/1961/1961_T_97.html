 Tarachand Damu Sutar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 461; AIR 1962 SC 130; 1962 CRLJ 196; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 775

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - (A) What does word 'Acquittal' means u/art. 134(1)(a)? - Held, trial must have ended in a complete acquittal - Where an accused has been acquitted of charge of murder and has been convicted of a lesser offence - (B) Whether conviction u/s. 302, IPC based solely on dying declaration is correct? - Further held, yes it is - A dying declaration is not to be believed merely, because no possible reason can be given for accusing accused falsely - Can only be believed if there are no grounds for doubting it at all - Appeal dismissed.
