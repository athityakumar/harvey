Rani Purnima Devi and Another v Kumar Khagendra Narayan Dev and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 1962 SC 567; 1962 (2) MLJ 7; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; 1962 (1) SCJ 725; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal Law - Succession Act, 1925, s. 63-(A) Succession Act -(A) Whether it is sufficient for a will to be registered to dispel all suspicion regarding it where suspicion exists, without submitting evidence of registration to a close examination? - Held, it is not - If evidence reveals that registration was brought home to testator - He admitted its execution and signed it in token thereof, registration will dispel doubt as to genuineness of Will - But if evidence show that officer registering Will did not read it over to testator or did not bring home to him that he was admitting execution of a will or did not satisfy himself in some other way that testator knew that it was a Will execution of which he was admitting, fact that Will was registered would not be of much value - Registration may take place without executants really knowing what he was registering - As propounder was unable to dispel suspicious circumstances - No letters of administration in his favour could be granted on basis of it - (B) Constitution - Whether Supreme Court can allow counsel for parties to go into entire evidence when there is some difference in approach of two Judges composing Bench of High Court existing? - Further held, yes, it is allowed - So that Supreme Court might be able to judge whether HC was right in its conclusion that fact of registration of Will had dispelled all suspicions - Appeal allowed.
