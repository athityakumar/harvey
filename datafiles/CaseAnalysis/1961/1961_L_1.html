L. Janakirama Iyer v P. M. Nilakanta Iyer

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 398; AIR 1962 SC 633; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 206; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 206

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Property - Administration - Trust deed - Payment of interest - Defendants 1 to 6 brought suit in Sub Court for administration of trust created for account from trustees and for recovery of trust properties - Preliminary decree for account was passed by Sub Court - Claim was not granted - Decree gave rise to three appeals before HC - Appeals were heard together and common judgment was delivered - HC confirmed ending of trial court and remanded suit - Defendants 1 to 6 filed petition for withdrawal of litigation - Petition was allowed and suit filed was dismissed - Before defendants 1 to 6 were allowed to withdraw litigation, suit was filed by three plaintiffs who were creditors of defendants 1 to 6 and who purported to act on behalf of general body of creditors - Leave was granted to plaintiff - Trial judge rejected plaintiffs' claim for account, but passed decree declaring that properties described in schedules mentioned, continued to be impressed with trust imposed upon them by trustees - Decree gave rise to three appeals before HC - Subject to modifications made in regard to payment of interest, rest of decree passed by trial court had been confirmed and dismissed all three appeals - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was correct in finding that all alienations were effected for inadequate consideration and rejecting plea of res judicata and limitation - Held, argument was misconceived because neither judgment proceeded on assumption that whole of agricultural property was double crop land - Question about value of property had been determined on evidence, documentary and oral, led in case, and both Courts had found in favour of plaintiffs and against alienees - Appeals dismissed.
