Dr. Gopal Dass Verma v Dr. S. K. Bhardwaj and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1963 SC 337; 1961 (2) SCJ 616; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Keywords: New Delhi House Rent Control Order, 1939

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, ss. 13 (1) (e), 2 (g) and 13 (1) (h) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether landlord would be entitled to eject tenant he has given consent for professional use of a substantial part of premises when premises are let for residential purposes u/s 13(1)(e)? - Held, no, he cannot even if he proves that he needs premises bona fide for his personal use - Premises have by their user ceased to be premises let for residential purposes alone - (1) Whether Act would be applicable to cases where landlord fails to prove tenancies are created for essential purposes u/s 13 (1) (g)? - Further held, yes, u/s 13 (1) (e) he would be entitled to eject tenant once it is shown that tenant has acquired another suitable residence - If premises from which ejectment is sought are used not only for residence but also for profession, then it is unreasonable to hold that tenancy which has been created or used both for residence and profession can be successfully terminated merely by showing that tenant has acquired a suitable residence but not suitable for profession - (B) What does word 'premises' refer to in s. 2 (g) of Act? - Further held, it refers to residence, commerce and any other purpose which necessarily must include residence and commerce combined - Any other purpose refers to charity too - (C) Whether new pleas which need further evidence allowed to be raised in appeals u/art. 136 of Constitution? - Further held, it is not allowed - Appeal dismissed.
