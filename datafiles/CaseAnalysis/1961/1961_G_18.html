 Gopal Vinayak Godse v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 227; AIR 1961 SC 600; 1961 CRLJ 736; 1962 (1) SCJ 423; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Miscellaneous; Petroleum & Gas

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 401 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4(b), 5 and 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 53, 53 A, 55, 58, 109 and 302 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Prison Act, 1894, s. 59, rr. 934, 934 (c), 937(c), 1419, 1419(c) and 1447(2) - (A) Whether a sentence of transportation for life or imprisonment for life, prima facie be treated as transportation or imprisonment for whole of remaining period of convicted person's natural life? - Held, remissions of sentence of transportation for life is ordinarily equated with a definite period, but it is only for that particular purpose and not for any other purpose - As sentence of transportation for life or its prison equivalent, life imprisonment, is one of indefinite duration, remissions so earned do not in practice help such a convict as it is not possible to predicate time of his death - A prisoner sentenced to life imprisonment is bound in law to serve life, term in prison - (B) Whether there is any provision of law whereunder a sentence for life imprisonment, without any formal remission by appropriate Govt., can be automatically treated as one for a definite period? - Question of remission is exclusively within province of appropriate Govt; and in this case it is admitted that, though appropriate Govt. made certain remissions u/s. 401 of CrPC, 1898 it did not remit entire sentence - Petitioner has not yet acquired any right to release - (C) Whether under relevant statutory provisions, an accused who was sentenced to transportation for life, could legally be imprisoned in one of jails in India; and if so, what was term for which he could be so imprisoned? - Held, Rules under Prisons Act do not substitute a lesser sentence for a sentence of transportation for life - Before Act XXVI of 1955 a sentence of transportation for life could be undergone by a prisoner by way of rigorous imprisonment for life in a designated prison in India - After said Act, such a convict shall be dealt with in same manner as one sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for same term - Petition is dismissed.
