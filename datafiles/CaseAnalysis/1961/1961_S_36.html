 Shri Mahadeo Paikaji Kolhe Yavatmal v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 253; AIR 1961 SC 1517; 1961 (2) SCJ 589; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1958 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1954, s. 146 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 A(2)(a) - Word 'estate' - (A) Whether lands belonging to petitioners comes within term 'estate' used in art. 31A so that Act is said to be applicable to lands and saved by art.31A? - Held, Act in question in substance was intended to extend to Vidarbha region and Cutch area which had then become a part of Greater Bi-Lingual State of Bombay - Art. 31 A(2)(a) provides, inter alia, that expression 'estate' shall in relation to any local area have same meaning as that expression or its local equivalent has in existing law relating to land tenures in force in that area - Existing local law was MP Code - (1) Whether lands of petitioners can be said to be an 'estate' within meaning of Code? - Provisions of impugned Act, perused - Held, thus reading relevant definitions along with provisions of s. 146 of Code, it would follow that land in possession of Bhumiswami who is a tenure-holder is in substance all estate - It is true that word 'estate' as such has not been employed in Code, but it must be borne in mind that art. 31A(2)(a) refers not only to estate but also to its local equivalent - Petitioners hold lands under State and they pay land revenue for the lands thus, held by them -Therefore, there is no difficulty in holding that under existing law relating to land tenures, lands held by them fall within class of local equivalents of word 'estate' as contemplated by art. 31A(2)(a) - Lands belonging to petitioners comes within term 'estate' used in art. 31A - Petitions dismissed.
