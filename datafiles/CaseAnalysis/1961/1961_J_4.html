Jaisri Sahu v Rajdewan Dubey and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 466; AIR 1962 SC 83; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 258; 1962 (1) SCJ 578; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 558

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - (A) Whether a Hindu widow succeeds as an heir to her husband's property after his death? - Held, ownership in properties - Both legal and beneficial vests in her - Fully represents Estate - Not accountable to anyone - (B) What status of reversioners in reference with deceased's estate? - Further held, interest of reversioner - Only spes Successions - (C) When there is a mortgage subsisting on property, whether widow could sell it in discharge of it? - Further held, there being no absolute prohibition against her effecting a sale - If income from property has increased in value - Where there is necessity for a transfer, restriction imposed by Hindu law on her power to alienate ceases to operate - (D) What practice is to be followed when there is a conflict among decisions of Benches of same High Court - Further held, bench hearing case to refer matter to a full bench in view of conflicting authorities without taking upon itself to decide whether it should follow one bench decision or other - Appeal allowed.
