Lalji Haridas and Another v Income Tax Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 453; [1961] 43 ITR 387

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-tax Act, s. 23(2) - Whether or not proceedings against appellant is without jurisdiction? - Held, it is significant that case for appellant is that order of transfer passed by Central Board on 30/09/1953 was illegal and unauthorised and validity of said order is not sought to be supported even by respondent so that if said order was invalid there can be no doubt that subsequent order passed by CIT (Central), Bombay, assigning case to ITO, s. IV (Central), Bombay, would itself be invalid and it is obvious that this latter order has been passed by Commr. in pursuance of authority conferred on him by earlier order passed by Central Board itself; sequence of orders leaves no doubt on point and besides, it is not disputed that CIT (Central), Bombay, would otherwise have no jurisdiction to deal with income-tax proceedings pending; it is patent that transfer of case file to Bombay by ITO, Jamnagar, was in law wholly invalid and unauthorised, therefore, it would be idle for appellant to contend that proceedings had been validly transferred to any Income-tax Officer in Bombay and that being so, it follows that proceedings are properly pending before first respondent - (B) Whether ITO has no jurisdiction to assess appellant, Chhotalal for income in question on ground that proposed assessment would be in nature of a precautionary or protective assessment which concept is not recognized by Act? - Further held, ex parte order relied upon by appellant was passed against appellant's brother Lalji holding that Lalji was liable to pay tax on amount of income in question, but said order has been subsequently set aside, and fresh proceedings against Lalji have been initiated, however, in fairness to appellants, directed that proceedings taken against both appellants should continue and should be dealt with expeditiously having regard to fact that matter is fairly old - Appeals dismissed.
