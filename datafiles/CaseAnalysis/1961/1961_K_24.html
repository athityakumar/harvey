Mrs. Dossibai N. B. Jeejeebhoy v Khemchand Gorumal and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1966 SC 1939; 1962 (1) SCJ 598; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 928

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Part II - Open land - Jurisdiction of city civil court - Appellant brought four suits in City Civil Courts, Bombay, for recovery of arrears of rent in respect of premises - Lessee taking lease of open land for purpose of constructing on it buildings intended to be used for residence or for business is 'letting for residence' - Words, 'let for residence, education, business, trade or storage' are wide enough to include a letting for achievement of these purposes with construction of buildings - It is clear under law that City Civil Court, Bombay, would have no jurisdiction to try these suits if provisions of Part II of Act applied to permises in suits - Held, lease was taken with a view to construct buildings thereon for residential, business, industrial or office purposes - Premises let are therefore, 'premises' to which u/s 6(1) provision of Part II of Act, apply - Trial Court and High Court were therefore, right in holding that City Civil Court, Bombay, had no jurisdiction to try suits - Appeal dismissed.
