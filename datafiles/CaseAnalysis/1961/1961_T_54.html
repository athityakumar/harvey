 Carl Still G. m. b. H. and Another v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 66; AIR 1961 SC 1615; 1962 (41) ILR(Pat) 27; 1962 (2) SCJ 32; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 81; 1961 (12) STC 449

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Liability To Tax, Sales Tax Authorities

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947,ss.2(b), 2(c), 2(d), 2(g), 2(h), 2(1), 4, 5, 9(1)and 13 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.286(1)(b) - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930,s. 9 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - (A) Whether on construction of agreement dated December 19, 1953, it could be held that there was a sale by appellants of materials used in construction works, apart from execution of those works? - Clause 15 of agreement is clear that no sale of materials was intended, because it expressly provides that if they were destroyed by fire, tempest or otherwise, loss would fall not on owner, which must be result if property is taken to have been absolutely transferred to it, but on contractor - Section 9 lays down that where there is a contract of sale of movable but price is not mentioned, it has to be fixed either in manner provided in agreement or by having regard to course of dealings between parties, and where that is not possible, buyer has to pay seller a reasonable price - Held, agreement dated December 19, 1953, being a contract for construction of works, one and indivisible, respondents have no right to impose a tax on materials supplied in execution of that contract on footing that such supply is a sale - (B) Whether appellants could agitate this matter in Supreme Court? - If proceedings relate to any extent to sales otherwise than under contract, then enquiry with respect to them must proceed before authorities under Act and application u/art 226 must fail - Further held, High Court was right in declining to issue writ prayed for - Appeals allowed.
