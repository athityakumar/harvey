 Banwarilal Agarwalla v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 526; AIR 1961 SC 849; 1961 CRLJ 12; 1961 (3) FLR 253; 1961 (2) LLJ 140; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Mines Act, 1952, ss. 6, 59(3) and 76 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Coal Mines Regulations, 1957 - Mines Act, 1923, s. 10 - (A) Whether s. 76 of Act, 1952, in pursuance of which appellant, who was not himself owner of colliery company, but only one of directors and shareholders has been prosecuted, is void as it violates art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, issue is based on assumption that word 'any one' in s. 76 means only 'one of directors, and only one of shareholders' however it has been decided that word 'any one' should be interpreted there as 'every one' - Thus, u/s. 76 every one of shareholders of a pvt. company owning mine, and every one of directors of a public company owning mine is liable to prosecution - No question of violation of art. 14 therefore arises - (B) Whether Regulations, 1957, are invalid having been framed in contravention of s. 59(3) of Act, 1952? - Further held, no general rule can be laid down for deciding whether any particular provision in a statute is mandatory, meaning thereby that non-observance thereof involves consequence of invalidity or only directory, i.e., a direction non-observance of which does not entail consequence of invalidity, whatever other consequences may occur - Provisions in s. 59(3) of Act, 1952, are mandatory - Appeal allowed.
