 Trimbak Damodhar Raipurkar v Assaram Hiraman Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 359; AIR 1966 SC 1758; 1962 (64) BomLR 565; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 700; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Tenancy Act, Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939 - Bombay Tenancy Act - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Respondents had executed a rent note in respect of these lands in favour of appellant on 05-02-1943 - Period of five years for which rent note was executed was statutorily extended to ten years as a result of s. 23 (1) (b) of Bombay Tenancy Act - During subsistence of tenancy statutorily extended Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act LXVII of 1948 came into force - Appellant gave a notice to respondents calling upon them to deliver possession at end of period - Before notice could be enforced, Bombay Act XXXIII of 1952 came into operation - On 04-04-1953 appellant instituted present tenancy proceedings - Whether appellant is entitled to eject respondents even without complying with statutory requirement as to valid notice prescribed by amending Act XXXIII of 1952? - Held, there can be no doubt that as a result of amending Act of 1952, expiration of lease did not take place on 31-03-1953 as had been anticipated by appellant - In order to put an end to tenancy thus statutorily safeguarded, appellant has to follow course prescribed by amending statute and give a valid notice as required by said statute - Just as appellant could not have complained against extension of original period of five years to ten years by Act XXIX of 1939 so he cannot complain against further extensions statutorily granted to respondents by s. 5 (1) of amending Act XXXIII of 1952 - Right of appellant to eject respondents would arise only on termination of tenancy and in present case it would have been available to him on 31-03-1953 if statutory provision had not in meanwhile extended life of tenancy - Appeal dismissed.
