Khan Bahadur Shapoor Fredoom Mazda v Durga Prosad Chamaria and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 494; AIR 1961 SC 1236; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 34,r. 1 - Limitation Act, 1908, s. 19 - Mortgage - Acknowledgement of debt - Whether letter in question amounts to a valid acknowledgment u/s. 19 of Limitation Act? - S. 19 is a mere acknowledgment of liability in respect of right in question it need not be accompanied by a promise to pay either expressly or even by implication - Effect of words used in a particular document must inevitably depend upon context in which words are used - Acknowledgment for liability u/s. 19 need not be express but may be implied from facts and circumstances - Held, letter acknowledges existence of jural relationship as mortgagee and mortgagor and amounts to a clear acknowledgment u/s. 19 of Limitation Act - Appeal dismissed.
