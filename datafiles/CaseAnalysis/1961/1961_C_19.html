K. A. Ramachar And Another v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 199; AIR 1961 SC 1059; 1972 CTR 106; [1961] 42 ITR 25; 1962 (2) SCJ 504; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Deeds of settlement - Share of profits - Assessment - Overriding title - Law of partnership - A was one of five partners of firm hold six-anna share in profits and loss of partnership - On 22-9-1947, A executed three deeds of settlement in favour of his wife, married adult daughter and minor daughter he assigned fourth share of profits of firm payable to him for period of 8 years vesting right in them to receive share of profits absolutely and exclusively and declaring settlements to be irrevocable - For assessment year 1947-1948 profits due to A amounted was credited to account of A and transferred to accounts of each of three disponees - Assessee claimed that these amounts could not be included in his total income for purposes of assessment being excluded by reason of third proviso of s. 16(1) (c) of 1961 Act - HC concluded that s. 16(1)(c) did not apply and third proviso was not attracted and income accrued to assessee in first instance been applied for payments under deeds - Appellants contended that what had been assigned was actionable claim and profits were diverted before they accrued to disponer - Whether inclusion in the assessee's total income of the profits settled by him on his wife and two daughters is justified in law - Held, as per referred case, by overriding title income was diverted in such way as never to become income of assessee - Under law of partnership, partner and partner alone entitled to profits - Assessee merely allowed payment to his wife and daughters to constitute valid discharge in favour of firm but what was paid in law a portion of his profits or his income - Amounts were first credited in Khata of A and then under his directions were transferred from his Khata to those of his wife and daughters - Dispositions in law and portions of income of A after income had accrued to him and tax was payable by him at point of accrual - Profits were diverted by overriding title before they accrued to A - Appeals disposed of.
