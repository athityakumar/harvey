Purshottam Lal Dhawan v Dewan Chaman Lal and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1961 SC 1371; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1908, s. 5 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 27, 56, 12, 31(5)and 14(6) - Administration of Evacuee Property (Central) Rules, 1950, rr. 14 and 31(5) - (A) Revision application to Custodian General - Limitation for filing - Held,r.31(5) provides that a revision petition to Custodian General 'shall ordinarily be made within sixty days of 38 order sought to be revised' - This rule is only a rule of guidance and not one of limitation and in law a revision can be entertained even after sixty days if Custodian General in his discretion thinks fit to entertain it - Revision application was not barred by time - (B) Powers of Custodian General in cancellation of allotment in revision - Further held, u/r. 14(6) Custodian could not, after 22-07-1952, cancel an allotment except under certain specified circumstances; but second proviso to r. 14 (6) permitted Custodian General, in exercise of his powers of revision u/s.27 Administration of Evacuee Property Act, to cancel an allotment made by a lower authority on or before 22-07-1952 - Further held, Custodian General had power to cancel allotment made on 2-12-1949 - Appeal dismissed.
