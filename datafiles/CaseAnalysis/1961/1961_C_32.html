 Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Scindia Steam Navigation

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 283; AIR 1961 SC 1633; [1961] 42 ITR 589; 1962 (2) SCJ 322; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 788

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (Majority Order)

Venkatarama Aiyar, J. for S. K. Das, J. L. Kapur, M. Hidayatullah, JJ and for himself

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66, 66(1), 66(2), 66(4) and 66(5) - Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1946 - A/y 1946-47 - Jurisdiction - Respondents, owners of a steamship, received compensation from the Government as owners of a requisitioned steamship lost in enemy action - The original cost of the ship was Rs. 24,95,016/- and its written down value at the commencement of the year of account was Rs. 15,68,484/- - The difference between the cost price and the written-done value, was Rs. 9,26,532/- - As the total compensation received exceeded the cost price, the respondents were assessed to tax under the fourth proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) of Act, which was inserted into the Act by the Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1946 that came into force on 4.05.1946 - Respondents filed an application u/s. 66(1) of the Act for referring the question to the court that whether the amount of Rs. 9,26,532/- was liable to be included in the total income of the respondents for the a/y 1946-47 when said income was received not in the year of account but in the previous year and the fourth proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) of Act had no application at the material date - Tribunal referred the question to the HC who held that that the compensation amount was not liable to charge as the proviso in question was not in force on the material date - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that it was not open to HC to go into question as to applicability of proviso to s. 10 (2) of the Act as the same was neither raised before Tribunal nor considered by it - Whether the HC in answering a reference u/s. 66 (1) of the Act could decide a question not raised or argued before the Tribunal? - Held, a question of law may have more than one aspect and s. 66(1) of the Act does not contemplate that each aspect of a question was by itself a distinct question - It only requires that the question of law which was referred to the Court must have been in issue before the Tribunal - It does not further require that the reference should be limited to those aspects of the question which had been argued before the Tribunal - In the instant case, the question referred to the HC was wide enough to cover the contention raised by the respondent and the HC was right in holding that the fourth proviso to s. 10(2)(Vii) of the Act, not being retrospective in operation, had no application - Revenue's appeal dismissed.

(Minority Order)

J. C. Shah, J

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66, 66(1), 66(2), 66(4) and 66(5) - Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1946 - A/y 1946-47 - Jurisdiction - Whether the HC in answering a reference u/s. 66 (1) of the Act could decide a question not raised or argued before the Tribunal? - Held, s. 66 of the Act does not contemplate that the question which Tribunal may refer, or which the HC Court may call upon the Tribunal to refer, must be one that was raised and argued before the Tribunal at the hearing - Section 66 of the Act does not specifically impose such a restriction nor was it implied - Under s. 66(5) of the Act, the court has to record its opinion on the questions arising out of the order of the Tribunal and not on the arguments advanced before it - In the instant case, the HC, on the question arising out of the order of the Tribunal and referred to it, had jurisdiction to decide that the proviso which made the amount taxable was not in operation at the material date - Appeal dismissed.
