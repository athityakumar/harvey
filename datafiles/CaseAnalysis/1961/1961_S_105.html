 Abdul Gafoor v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 264; AIR 1961 SC 1556; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 68-C, 68-D(1) and 68-F(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Whether STA can grant without undertaking form Regional Transport Authority and without publishing and notice to applicants regarding issue of permit u/s. 68-F(1) and RTA performs quasi judicial function u/s. 68-F(1) is valid and justified? - Relied on Srinivasa Reddy and Ors v State of Mysore and Ors (1959 Indlaw SC 98) - Held, Regional Transport Authority functions as quasi judicial authority when dealing in application u/s. 68-F(1) and can reject application u/s. 68-F(2) failed - Further held, Regional Transport Authority while dealing with application of State Transport Authority undertaking u/s. 68-F(1) act in a ministerial capacity so, contention that no permit can be granted to State Transport Authority undertaking until application for permit is duly published and notices given to petitioner of these application is unsound - Petition dismissed.
