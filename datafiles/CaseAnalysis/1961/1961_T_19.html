 Ranjit Singh v Commissioner of Income Tax, U. P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 254; AIR 1962 SC 92; [1961] 42 ITR 761; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 966

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 19(1)(g), 31 - Excess Profits Tax Act,1940 - Indian Income Tax Act,1922 - Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Demand notice - Settlement - Recovery procedure - Discriminatory and violative - On 30-5-1948, Secretary of Commission issued notice to petitioner to furnish list of businesses or concerns in which petitioner was interested and to produce account books, registers etc. relating thereto - Petitioner complied with notice - Authorized Official commenced investigation and submitted report u/s. 8-A(1) of Act - Order was passed u/s. 8-A (2) of Act stated that demand notice be served immediately by Income Tax Officer u/s. 29 of 1922 Act, on petitioner in accordance with terms and conditions of settlement and all such other proceedings under 1922 Act or under any other law as may be necessary with view to enforce payment of demand and terms and conditions of settlement - Petitioner unable to make full payment within stipulated as result whole amount outstanding at time became immediately payable by petitioner - Petitioner filed petition challenging legality of demand notice and subsequent proceedings taken in pursuance of notice - Proceedings taken against petitioner in pursuance of order u/s.8-A(2) are violative of guarantee of equal protection of laws u/art.14 of Constitution -Discriminatory procedure continued even after coming into force of Constitution - Whether recovery procedure taken against petitioner before dt. 26-1-1950 was discriminatory with regard to validity of proceedings - Held, petitioner does not really belong to larger class of persons characterized as debtors of Govt. and he belongs to special class who had evaded payment of Income tax and entered into settlement to pay amount due as income tax or arrear of income tax and for this class of persons procedure laid down in s.8-A (2) was same and no discrimination was made in favour of or against any member of same class -Settlement itself was vitiated by discriminatory procedure adopted by Commission - Everything was concluded before dt. 26-1-1950, when Constitution came into force including issuance of notice of demand - No merit - Petition dismissed.
