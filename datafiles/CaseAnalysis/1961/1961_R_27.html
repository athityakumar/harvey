 Ram Saran Lall and Others v Mst. Domini Kuer and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 365; AIR 1961 SC 1747; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 47 and 61 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3 and 54 - Law of pre-emption - Whether first demand was made before or after sale was completed? - Held, s. 47 of Registration Act does not say when a sale would be deemed to be complete - It only permits a document when registered to operate from a certain date, which may be earlier than date when it was registered - It has nothing to do with completion of registration and therefore nothing to do with completion of a sale, when instrument is one of sale - A sale which is admittedly not completed until registration of instrument of sale is completed, cannot be said to have been completed earlier because by virtue of s. 47 instrument by which it is effected, after it has been registered, commences to operate from an earlier date - It is not necessary to discuss question of construction of instrument of sale in this case, that is, to decide whether on its proper reading transfer was intended to take immediate effect on its execution or later on after balance of purchase money had been paid - Further held, time when sale becomes complete so as to entitle pre-emptor to perform talabs should be determined by application of principle of intention laid down in s.47 of Registration Act - To determine time from which sale should be held to be complete, intention of parties would be crucial and only test which has to be gathered by reference to document itself read in light of surrounding circumstances, with however a proviso that if document were clear and its terms explicit, no evidence to contradict them would be admissible - In face of recitals, convenants and stipulations which clearly express intention of parties that deed should have effect from date of its execution and therefore it argument that it could be postponed to a later date or either date when registration was complete under or when on registration receipt having been handed over to vendee is hardly tenable - Appeal dismissed.
