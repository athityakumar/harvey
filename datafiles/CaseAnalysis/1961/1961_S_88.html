 Board of Trustees, Ayurvedic Andunani Tibia College v State of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 399; AIR 1962 SC 458; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 156; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Tibbia College Act, 1952

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 92 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31, 32, 239 and 240 - Societies Registration Act, 1860, ss. 1, 2, 5, 6,7, 8, 9, 20, 21 and 22 - Tibbia College Act, 1952, ss. 3 and 9 - Suit in court of senior Subordinate Judge s. 92 of CPC, 1908 against Secy. and 31 members of Board, application made for appointment of a receiver - Suit was pending, Delhi State Legislature passed an Act called Tibbia College Act, 1952 - (A) Constitutional validity of Act challenged - Held, on registration under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 old Board did not become a corporation is sense of being incorporated within meaning entry 44 of List I, it remained an unincorporated society, legislation while creating new Board has given it a corporate statue, but has confined its powers and duties to college, pharmaceutical institute and laboratory in Delhi and while giving new Board rights, powers and privileges of old Board has limited them to such purposes for which new Board is constituted and therefore,legislation falls under entry 32 of List II, so far as dissolution of old Board is concerned, under second part of entry and so far as incorporation of new Board is concerned, under its first part - Legislation well within legislative competence and power of Delhi State Legislature - (B) Whether art. 14 is violated? - Held, no - Legislation does not fall u/art. 31(2) and cannot be judged by tests laid down there in as to cl. (1) of art. 31 there is no question of any violation of that clause if law by which transference of management has been made is valid law - Petition dismissed.
