 B. K. Wadeyar v B. K. Wadeyar

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 404; AIR 1961 SC 311; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 924

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, ss. 10 (b) and 11 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 286 (1) (b)- (A) Whether property in goods passed on shipment or at some point of time before shipment? - When export has been defined in Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947, as 'taking out of India by land, sea, or air, export in Export Control Order, cannot be held to have commenced till at least ship carrying goods has left port, though it may in some contexts be more correct to say that it does not commence till ship has passed beyond territorial waters - Held, goods remained sellers property till goods had been brought and loaded on board ship and so sales were exempted from tax u/art. 286 (1) (b) of Constitution - (B) Whether 'a person' in s. 8 (b) should be interpreted to include a registered dealer or anybody else? - Further held, scheme of Legislature clearly is that where intention as declared has not been carried out, purchase tax should be levied - Courts below have rightly interpreted words 'a person' in s. 10 (b) of Bombay Sales Tax Act as a'registered dealer' and that purchasing dealers have rightly been assessed to purchase tax u/s. 10(b)- Appeals dismissed.
