 Kailash Chandra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 384; AIR 1961 SC 1346; 1961 (3) FLR 379; 1961 (2) LLJ 639; 1961 (2) SCJ 675; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 374

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Railway Establishment Code, rr. 2046/2 and 2046 (2)(a) - Appellant,clerk, in East Indian Railways was compulsorily retired from service w.e.f 30-06-1948, on attaining age of 55 years - Whether appellant had right to be retained in service till age of 60 years? - Correct interpretation of r. 2046(2) (a) is that a railway ministerial servant falling within this clause may be compulsorily retired on attaining age of 55 but appropriate authority has option to continue him in service, subject to condition that servant continues to be efficient but authority is not bound to retain him even if a servant continues to be efficient - Held, High Court was right in holding that this rule gave appellant no right to continue in service beyond age of 55 - There was a reasonable classification of ministerial servants who had been retired u/r. 2046(2)(a) on attaining age of 55 into two classes - There is, therefore, no denial of equal protection of laws guaranteed by art. 14 - Appeal dismissed.
