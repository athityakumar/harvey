 Jagannath and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1962 SC 148; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 304; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether excise tariff imposed by cl. (6) in entry 4(1) in sch. 1 to Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 is valid in law? - Though Rustica varieties of tobacco are generally not known to be used for biris, when they are cured in broken leaf grades they can be used with admixture with biri tobacco like Pandharpuri tobacco for imparting strength to biri mixtures - Held, grievance made by petitioners that tobacco in which they deal can never be used for biris is not well founded - Distinction between tobacco falling u/cls. (5) and (6), according to report of Committee, is so clear and unambiguous and its relation to object intended by imposition of tariff is so clearly reasonable that attack against its validity on ground of unconstitutional discrimination cannot be upheld - Petition dismissed.
