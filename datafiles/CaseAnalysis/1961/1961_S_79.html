 Guru Datta Sharma v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 406; AIR 1961 SC 1684; 1962 (41) ILR(Pat) 47; 1962 (1) SCJ 382; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 292

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property; Socio-Economic

Summary: Land & Property - Government of India Act, 1935 - Bihar Private Forests Act, 1948 - Bihar State Management of Estates and Tenures Act, 1949 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Whether main operative provisions of Bihar Private Forests Act, both as originally enacted in 1946 as well as when re-enacted in 1948, were unconstitutional as contravening requirements of s. 299 (2) of Government of India Act, 1935? - Held, reference may be made to legislative practice which preceded Government of India Act, 1935, as having relevance to understanding of scope and ambit of entry - Situation, therefore, demanded that there should be a large extension of grounds upon which such private forests would be taken over for better management by state officials as compared with Chapter V of Indian Forests Act, 1927 - Correlation between Bihar Act III of 1946 now impugned and Indian Forests Act, 1927 is brought out in long title of former - Impugned Act was therefore, an Act supplementary to or rather a complement of Indian Forests Act of 1927 and is clearly covered by Entry 'Forests' in item 22 of State Legislative List - Extract from judgment of Viscount Simonds affords a sufficient answer to a submission that right of landholder to possession was itself a right of property and as this had been taken over it constituted an acquisition within constitutional provision - There is no scope for contention that imposition, so to speak, of a compulsory Governmental agency for purpose of managing forest with liability imposed to account for income as laid down by statute is an 'acquisition' of property itself within s. 299 (2) of Government of India Act, 1935 - Impugned enactments were validly enacted and are not obnoxious to provisions of Government of India Act, 1935 - Appeal dismissed.
