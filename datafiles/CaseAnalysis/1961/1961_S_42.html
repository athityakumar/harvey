River Steam Navigation Company, Lt.D v Shyam Sundar Tea Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1962 SC 1276; 1961 (2) AnWR 160; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 160; 1961 (2) SCJ 5954; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Carrier, Common Carrier, Common Carriers

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Carriers Act, 1865 - Whether appellants were common carriers of goods between Dillibari Ghat and Calcutta? - Held, mere fact that appellants are engaged in transport of goods from certain places on their Steamer Service to other places does not necessarily justify conclusion that whatever carriage they may undertake elsewhere is also done as a common carrier - It is therefore necessary to examine nature of public profession made by appellants with regard to carriage of goods from Dillibari Ghat - If, as evidence appears to establish, companies were ready to supply boats whenever requested, without picking and choosing, that would be sufficient public profession to act as a common carrier - Nor is fact that there was no fixed rate for carriage of goods from Dillibari to Desangmukh of any assistance to appellants contention that they were not common carriers, for law does not require that a common carrier must have one and same rate for all goods - On a consideration of entire evidence therefore that appellants did profess by their conduct, even if not by any public notice, that they would carry goods indiscriminately for all those who ask for such carriage from Dillibari to various places on their main steamer route - They were thus common carriers in respect of carriage of plaintiff's goods from Dillibari - Appeal dismissed.
