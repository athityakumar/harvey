Shankar Balaji Waje v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 347; AIR 1962 SC 517; 1962 CRLJ 497; 1962 (1) LLJ 119; 1962 (2) SCJ 426; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 249; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contract Of Employment, University

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 2 (1), 79 (1) and 80 (1) - (A)Whether 'Bidi Roller' comes under definition of 'worker' within s. 2 (1) of Factories Act, 1948? - Held, No - Employment is contract of service between employer and employee where under employee agrees to serve employer subject to his control and supervision - Persons would be workers who work regularly at factory and are paid for work turned out during their regular employment on basis of work done - Bidi workers are not covered u/s. 2 (1) of Factories Act, 1948 - (B) Do provisions of ss. 79 and 80 of Factories Act, 1948 apply to Bidi workers? - Further held, Bidi workers come and go at place of work according to their sweet will - No basis for calculating daily average of workers total full time earnings - Provisions of ss. 79 and 80 about leave and leave wages do not apply in case of such workers - Appeal allowed.
