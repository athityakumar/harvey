N. Kasturi v D. Ponnammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 495; AIR 1961 SC 1302; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 174; 1962 (1) SCJ 471; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 955

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - This appeal raises a short question about construction of a Will executed by testator, Diraviyam Pillai - Cl. 12 of Will provided that in event testator or his wife die without adopting a boy, appellant and one Kalyanasundaram shall get whole of his properties - Whether appellant gets any right u/cl. 12 of Will which would justify his claim for a declaration and other appropriate reliefs made by him in present suit? - Held, cl. 11 deals with position which would have arisen if an adoption had been made by widow of testator, whereas cl. 12 deals with position which would arise where no adoption is made - When no adoption is made and until it is so made there is a vested right in respect of half properties in appellant which right no doubt may be defeated if an adoption is subsequently made - It would be plainly inconsistent with all rules of construction to take cl. 12 by itself and isolate it from rest of Will - We are satisfied that reading cls. 11 and 12 together High Court was right in holding that cl. 12 was intended to operate at time of death of testator and not later and that appellant would get an interest under cl. 12 only if widow of testator pre-deceased testator and there is no adoption by testator before his death - If that be so, appellant cannot claim any right or title on strength of cl. 12 because at relevant time it was not intended to be operative at all - In circumstances appellant's rights are provided for by cl. 11 alone, and those rights cannot come into existence unless and until he is adopted by respondent 1 - Appeal dismissed.
