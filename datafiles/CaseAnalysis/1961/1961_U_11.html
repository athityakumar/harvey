Issardas Daulat Ram and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 420; AIR 1966 SC 1867; 1966 CRLJ 1507; 1983 ECR 2227; 1962 (2) SCJ 286; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 358

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Importation, Prohibition Act, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Customs - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Prohibition Act - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136(1) - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 167(8), 178(A) and 182 - Whether order of Collector of Customs holding appellant's gold seized at refinery to be smuggled gold so as to be liable to confiscation u/s. 167(8) of Sea Customs Act is vitiated by any error such as to call for interference u/art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, there were undoubtedly relevant pieces of evidence which bore on question regarding character of gold, whether it was licit or illicit - It is not right absence of material before Collector to justify finding recorded in para. 6 we have set out earlier - Appeal dismissed.
