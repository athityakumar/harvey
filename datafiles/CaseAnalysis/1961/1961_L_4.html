Lakshman Singh Kothari v Smt. Rup Kanwar

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 490; AIR 1961 SC 1378; 1962 (2) MLJ 172; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 112; 1962 (2) SCJ 472; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Son claiming adoption - Suit for declaration against father - Father denied adoption - What are ingredients of giving and taking ceremony and requirements of a valid adoption? - Documentary evidence, clearly establish, no ceremony of adoption had taken place, though boy was taken to house of S with a view to take him in adoption either after he was admitted in Gurukul or after his education Gurukul was completed - Oral evidence also consistent with documentary evidence P.Ws. speak of custom in community to which parties belong to effect that in that community consent of person giving in adoption and person taking in adoption and going of adopted son from his original family to live in adoptive family were preliminary steps to a valid adoption - No attempt has been made in any of courts below to sustain adoption on alleged custom and, therefore, court does not propose to consider evidence relating to alleged custom - Held, under Hindu Law, whether among regenerate caste or among Sudras, there cannot be a valid adoption unless adoptive boy is transferred from one family to another and that can be done only by ceremony of giving and taking - Object of corporeal giving and receiving in adoption is obviously to secure due publicity - To achieve this object it is essential to have a formal ceremony - No particular form is prescribed for ceremony, but law requires that natural parent shall hand over adoptive boy and adoptive parent shall receive him - Nature of ceremony may vary depending upon circumstances of each case - But a ceremony there shall be, and giving and taking shall be part of it - Documents and oral evidence in case establish that adoptive father did not delegate his power to give boy in adoption to M to H and that M did not receive boy as a part of ceremony of adoption, but only received him with a view to send him to Gurukul - Hence, ceremony of giving and taking, which is very essential for validity of an adoption, had not taken place in this case - Appeal dismissed.
