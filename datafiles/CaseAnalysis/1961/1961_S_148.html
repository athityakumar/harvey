 State of Punjab v Suraj Parkash Kapur, Etc

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.L. Kapur, Raghubar Dayal, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 464; AIR 1963 SC 507; 1962 (1) SCJ 108; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 711

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948, ss. 14 and 15 - (A) When can one approach High Court u/art. 226? - Held, existence of a right and infringement thereof are foundation of exercise of jurisdiction of HC under art. 226 of Constitution - Right that can be enforced under art. 226 of Constitution must ordinarily be personal or individual right of applicant - Notwithstanding a notification u/s. 12 of Act of 1954, an evacuee has a valuable right in property allotted to him and said right is entitled to protection of constituted authorities and courts - (B) Whether any person can be deprived of any property u/s. 15 of East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948 - Further held, there is no provision in Act empowering Consolidation Officer to deprive a person of any part of his property without allotting to him property of equal value or paying him compensation if he is allotted a holding of less market value than that of his original holding - Appeal dismissed.
