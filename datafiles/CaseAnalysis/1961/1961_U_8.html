 Messrs Burrakur Coal Company, Limited v Union of India and Others (And Connected Petition)

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 502; AIR 1961 SC 954; 1962 (2) SCJ 216; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Colliery

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 4, 5, 6, 6(1), 7, 8, 13 and 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1), 31 A(1) and 31(2) - Whether law which provided for prospecting for coal and acquisition and also discussed meaning of expressions 'unworked land' 'to undertake any operation in land' with respect to Act, 1957 is ultravires? - Held, expression 'unworked land', according to Act is that land which was not being worked at time of notification issued under Act and includes dormant mines - Mineral cannot be regarded as separate tenement except perhaps in a case of a trespass and, therefore, there is no question of law providing for a separate compensation for them - Apart from that if minerals have become a separate tenement then present Act may not apply to such a tenement at all - Coal contained in two collieries in question is not held by respective petitioners as a tenement separate from surface - In circumstances, challenge to validity of Act on ground that it offends art. 31(2) of Constitution fails - Other expression refers to undertaking of an operation on land not for first time only but at resumption of an operation which had been abandoned or discontinued - Petitions dismissed.
