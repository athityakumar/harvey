Sriratnavaramaraja v Smt.Vimla

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1961 SC 1299; 1961 KLT 67; 1962 MLJ 36; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 1962 (1) SCJ 60; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 1015

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Court-Fees Act

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Court-fees Act - Suits Valuation Act, 1887 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Plaintiff claimed that on death of her father, she became entitled to properties in suit but defendant wrongfully possessed himself of those properties - Trial court raised an issue about adequacy of court-fee, paid by plaintiff - On 13-02-1957, defendant applied for appointment of a Commr. to value properties - In revision, High Court substantially confirmed order passed by Subordinate Judge except as to an institution described as 'Nelliyadi Beedu', in respect of which HC directed trial court to determine whether institution was 'extra commercial' after giving an opportunity to both parties - Whether HC erred in entertaining revision application? - Held,Court fees Act was enacted to collect revenue for the benefit of State and not to arm a contesting party with a weapon of defence to obstruct trial of an action - It is not appreciated what grievance defendant can make by seeking to invoke revisional jurisdiction of HC on question whether plaintiff has paid adequate court-fee on his plaint - Whether proper court-fee is paid on a plaint is primarily a question between plaintiff and State - Anxiety of Legislature to collect court-fee due from litigant is manifest from detailed provisions made in ch. III of Act, but those provisions do not arm defendant with a weapon of technicality to obstruct progress of suit by approaching HC in revision, against an order determining court-fee payable - HC grievously erred in entertaining revision applications on questions of court-fee at the instance of defendant, when no question of jurisdiction was involved - Appeal dismissed.
