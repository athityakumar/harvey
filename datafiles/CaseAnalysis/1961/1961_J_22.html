 Jyoti Pershad v Administrator For The Union Territory of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1961 SC 1602; 1962 (2) SCJ 58; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Decreeholder

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Slum Areas (Improvement & Clearance) Act, 1956 - Whether s. 19 and particularly sub-s. 3 of Slum Areas Act was, in so far as it enabled competent authority to withhold permission to those who had obtained decrees for eviction from executing their decrees, unconstitutional? - Held, restrictions imposed on right of landlord to evict have a reasonable and rational connection with object sought to be achieved by Act, ie, the ultimate elimination of slums with protection to slum-dwellers from being meanwhile thrown out on streets - Criteria for determining degree of restriction on right to hold property which would be considered reasonable, are by no means fixed or static, but must obviously vary from age to age and be related to adjustments necessary to solve problems which communities face from time to time - Ban imposed on evictions is temporary - If 'competent authority' oversteps limits of his powers or ignores policy behind Act and acts contrary to its declared intention, appellate authority could be invoked to step in and correct error - It would therefore be a provision for doubly safeguarding that policy of Act is carried out and not ignored in each and every case that comes up before 'the competent authority' - Taken in conjunction with provisions of Delhi Development Act, 1957, which makes provision for design of a Master Plan for city which, if executed, is likely to greatly reduce, if not to eliminate, slums altogether, it would be seen that power to restrain eviction u/s. 19 of Act is one which would not last for ever but to a limited period, though this could not naturally be defined by reference to fixed dates - There is enough guidance to competent authority in use of his discretion u/s. 19(1) of Act and reject contention that s. 19 is obnoxious to equal protection of laws guaranteed by art. 14 ,Constitution Of India, 1950 - Petitions dismissed.
