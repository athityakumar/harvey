 Bhaiyalal Shukla v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 48; AIR 1962 SC 981; 1962 (13) STC 236; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: part c States (Miscellaneous Laws) Repealing Act, 1951, Government of Part C States Act, 1951, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Vindhya Pradesh Laws (Validating) Act, 1952, Constitution (Amendment of the First and Fourth Schedules) Order, 1950

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Central Provinces & Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 119 - Delhi Laws Act, 1912 - Part C States (Miscellaneous Laws) Repealing Act, 1951 - Vindhya Pradesh Laws (Validating) Act, 1952 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Constitution (Amendment of First and Fourth Schedules) Order, 1950 - Validity of sales tax law in Vindhya Pradesh - United State of Vindhya Pradesh was formed by Rulers of States in Baghelkhand and Bundhelkhand, who agreed to unite into common State, with Maharaja of Rewa as Rajpramukh - By Covenant, it was provided that until a Constitution for United State was formed, legislative authority of United State would vest in Rajpramukh, and he was authorised to make and promulgate Ordinances for peace and good Govt. of United State or any part thereof - Rajpramukh, in exercise of his powers drawn from Covenant, promulgated Vindhya Pradesh Sales Tax Ordinance, 2 of 1949, for levy of a tax on sale of goods in Vindhya Pradesh - Central Govt. by a Notification extended to State of Vindhya Pradesh to Central Provinces and Act, 1947 as in force for time being in State of Madhya Pradesh, subject to certain modifications necessitated by application of Act to this new area and also added a new section 'Repeal and saving' to Madhya Pradesh Act - Whether Act, 1947, can be said to have been extended for first time by Vindhya Pradesh Legislature in 1952 when it passed Act, 1952, to exclusion of order contained in Notification or whether Act continued to be in force in Vindhya Pradesh even before, and all that Vindhya Pradesh Act did was to remove any doubts about its validity? - Held, laws in different portions of new State of Madhya Pradesh were enacted by different Legislatures, and u/s 119 of States Reorganisation Act, all laws in force are to continue until repealed or altered by appropriate Legislature - Further held, sales tax law in Vindhya Pradesh was validly enacted, and it brought its validity with it u/s 119 of States Reorganisation Act, when it became a part of State of Madhya Pradesh - Different laws in different parts of Madhya Pradesh could be sustained - Petition dismissed.
