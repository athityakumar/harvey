Central Talkies Limited, Kanpur v Dwarka Prasad

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 525; AIR 1961 SC 606; 1961 CRLJ 740; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - United Provinces (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1946, ss. 2(d) and 3 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 1(2)and 10 - Ejectment - Persona designata - Whether suit was incompetent because permission of District Magistrate as required by s. 3 had not been obtained? - Additional District Magistrate appointed by Notification is competent and invested with all powers of District Magistrate to deal with an application under Eviction Act for permission to file a civil suit without special authorisation from District Magistrate - S 10(2) of CrPC being a special provision, sub-sec. (2) of s. 1 is excluded, and an Additional District Magistrate must be regarded as possessing powers under any other law including Eviction Act - By act of transferring case to Additional District Magistrate, District Magistrate must be deemed to have authorised him to exercise his powers u/s. 3 of Eviction Act - District Magistrate is not persona disgnata - Appeal dismissed.
