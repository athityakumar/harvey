 R. L. Arora v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 414; AIR 1962 SC 764; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 268; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 23; 1963 (1) SCJ 33; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 149

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 40, 41 and 42 - Notification issued providing collector to take possession of any waste or arable land forming part of land on question - Appellant filed petition in High Court for quashing notification - Inquiry ordered by Govt. - Fresh notification issued - Appellant filed another petition for quashing fresh notification on ground that notification was invalid as not complied with provisions of s. 40(1)(b) of 1894 Act - HC dismissed petition - Whether acquisition is for a work which is useful to public u/s. 40(1)(b) and which public is entitled to use in accordance with fifth term to be entered in agreement u/s. 41? - For acquiring land previous consent of Govt. has to be obtained and agreement u/s. 41 has to be executed - Held, land should be acquired only when work to be constructed is directly useful to public and public shall be entitled to use work as such for its own benefit in accordance with terms of agreement u/s. 42 - Appeal dismissed.
