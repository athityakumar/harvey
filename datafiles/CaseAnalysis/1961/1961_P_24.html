 Purushothaman Nambudiri v The State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1962 SC 694; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 180; 1962 (1) SCJ 477; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 753

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Intermediary, Legislative Procedure, Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, Titles, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Kerala Agrarian Relations Act, 1961, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, Punjab Land Revenue Act,1887, Bihar Land Reforms Amendment Act, Bihar Land Reforms, 1950, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act 1956

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31 - Kerala Agrarian Relations Act, 1961, ss. 1 to 40, 57, 58, 60, 74 to 79, 81 to 95 - Estate - Validity of Act - Petitioner owns about 1, 250 acres of land in Kerala, which was originally situated in State of Cochin - Notification was issued by respondent that directed implementation of ss. 1 to 40, 57, 58, 60, 74 to 79 as well as ss. 81 to 95 of the 1960 Act from date of notification - Petitioner contended that notification issued under the 1960 Act was ultra vires, unconstitutional and illegal and he wanted an appropriate writ or order to be issued quashing said notification - Petitioner claimed declaration that the 1960 Act was ultra vires and unconstitutional and prayed for writ of certiorari or other appropriate writ, order or direction against respondent, that restrained it from implementing provisions of the 1960 Act - Petitioner contended that material provisions of the 1960 Act contravened fundamental rights guaranteed by arts. 14, 19(1)(f), 31 of Constitution Hence, instant petition - Whether petitioner's properties were 'estate' within meaning of art. 31 A(2)(a) of Constitution alternatively if the 1960 Act was protected u/art. 31A(1) (a) of Constitution or it was inconsistent with arts. 14, 19, 31 - Held, whole or part of properties with which petitioner was concerned was outside purview of 'estate' as described by art. 31A(2) (a) of Constitution, thus challenge to validity of the 1960 Act on merits was to be considered - Petitioner owned about 900 acres of land which were classified as Pandaravaka holdings and about 350 acres which were described as Puravaka holdings -Pandaravaka Verumpattom were regarded as local equivalent of estate u/ art. 31A(2)(a) of Constitution - Position with regard to Puravaka lands was still more clear - Lands held by petitioner were estate under art. 31 A(2)(a) of Constitution, and so the Act in so far as it operated against holdings of petitioner was protected u/art. 31A(1)(a) of Constitution and so it was not open to petitioner to challenge its validity on ground that its material provisions offended against arts. 14, 19, 31 of Constitution - Although agricultural property were expressly not described as estate by local law, some properties in areas constituted estate u/art 31A(2)(a) of Constitution depending upon attributes and essential features of it - Interests of petitioner in lands held by him on Puravaka tenure were within art. 31A because they were lands covered by definition of 'estate' - However, Pandaravaka Verumpattom lands were clearly not 'estate' and interests of petitioner in them had not amounted to 'an interest in an estate' within sub-cl. art. 31A(2)(b) of Constituttion - Validity of impugned Act in relation to Pandaravaka lands was to be considered with reference to arts. 14, 19, 31 of Constitution - Impugned Act was constitutionally invalid and not applicable to Pandaravaka Verumpattom lands of petitioner and petitioner was not entitled to any relief as regarded his other properties - Petition dismissed.
