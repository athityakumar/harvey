Ved Mitter Gill v Union Territory Administration, Chandigarh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 224; (2015) 8 SCC 86; AIR 2015 SC 1796; JT 2015 (3) SC 393; 2015(4) SCALE 234; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 73; 2015 (3) SLR 739

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Escape, Jail Manual, Communicate

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311(2)(a), 311(2)(b) - Dismissal - Appellants as well as petitioners were holding various posts in Model Jail, Burail, Chandigarh were dismissed from service as four under trials had escaped from jail by digging an underground tunnel - Whether appellant and petitioners were rightly dismissed from service.

Held, responsibility of all the jail inmates (safe custody of all prisoners) rested on appellant's shoulders, and the petitioners herein, who assisted him in the same. Had the appellant and the petitioners, performed their duties diligently, there could not have been any possibility, of the escape. Competent authority was justified in concluding, that the four prisoners could never have escaped, if the appellant and the petitioners, had diligently discharged the duties assigned to them. It would not have been reasonably practicable, to hold a departmental proceeding against the appellant/petitioners, as junior jail officers were not likely to come forward to depose against him, for fear of earning his wrath in future. Thus, conditions for dismissal as stipulated in cl. (b) of the second proviso to art. 311(2) of the Constitution were satisfied. Appeal dismissed.
