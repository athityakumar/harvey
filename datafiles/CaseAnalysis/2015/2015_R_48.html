Jai Mahal Hotels Private Limited v Rajkumar Devraj and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 834; (2016) 1 SCC 423; 2015(6) ALL MR 410; 2015 (113) ALR 732; 2016 (1) AWC 176; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 1; [2015] 193 Comp Cas 214; 2015 (4) CompLJ 353; JT 2015 (8) SC 561; 2015 (7) MLJ 736; 2016 (130) RD 459; 2015(10) SCALE 14; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 323; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 39

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Succession, Rectification, Transfer Of Shares

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 10F, 111, 111(7) - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 370, 381 - Transfer of Shares - Rectification in Share Register - Challenged - 'X' held shares in appellant/ hotels and executed will in favour of his mother, 'Y' who jointly with respondent nos. 1-2 obtained succession certificate - However, respondents representation claiming transfer of share on basis of transfer deed executed by 'Y', succession certificate issued by Lower Court was rejected by Company Law Board - HC allowed appeal of respondents, thereby directed rectification in share register of a company - Hence, instant appeals - Whether exercise of jurisdiction by HC was beyond scope of s. 111 of the Act.

Held, there is a thin line in appreciating the scope of jurisdiction of the Company Court/Company Law Board. The jurisdiction is exclusive if the matter truly relates to rectification but if the issue is alien to rectification, such matter may not be within the exclusive jurisdiction of the Company Court/Company Law Board. In instant case, respondents had the succession certificate in its favour apart from the transfer deed from 'Y' who admittedly inherited rights from 'X'. Thus, the respondents derived rights from the 'Y' by documents executed by her in her lifetime and conveyed to the Company. Even if the Will of 'Y' is not taken into account, for purposes of issue of rectification, the documents executed by 'Y' clearly entitled the respondents to have the rectification made. Therefore, even in summary jurisdiction, the CLB had no justification to reject the claim of the respondents. The High Court rightly reversed the said order. Hence, appeals devoid of any merits and the same are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
