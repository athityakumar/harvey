Sharad Kumar Sanghi v Sangita Rane

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 263; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2405; 2015 (2) JCC 1277; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1924; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 120; 2015(3) SCALE 126; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vicarious Liability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 482 - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Quashing of Proceedings - Appellant was managing director of company and was engaged in business of automobile sale, finance and shipping etc. - Respondent-complainant purchased diesel vehicle from Bhopal branch of company - Respondent faced difficulty with the vehicle and eventually he came to know that a different engine number was made in the invoice that was issued to him than the engine that was put in the chasis - Respondent filed a complaint u/s. 200 of the CrPC alleging that company being represented by the Managing Director had suppressed the information and deliberately cheated the respondent - Appellant filed application u/s. 482 of the CrPC before the HC for quashing of proceedings against him which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, respondent's initial statement would reflect, the allegations are against the company, but the company has not been made arrayed as a party. Therefore, the allegations have to be restricted to the appellant. Allegations are vague and in fact, principally the allegations are against the company. There is no specific allegation against the appellant. HC should have quashed proceedings initiated against the appellant and that having not been done, the order is sensitively vulnerable and accordingly set aside. Thus, criminal proceedings initiated by the respondent against the appellant were quashed. Appeal allowed.
