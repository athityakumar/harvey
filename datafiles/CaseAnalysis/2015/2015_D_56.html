Dharampal Satyapal Limited v Deputy Commissioner of Central Excise, Gauhati and others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 364; (2015) 8 SCC 519; 2015 (320) E.L.T. 3; JT 2015 (5) SC 634; 2015(6) SCALE 612; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Principle Of Natural Justice, Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957, Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978

Summary: (A) Excise - Administrative - Finance Act, 2003 , s. 154 - Order passed by lower court - Principle of Res judicata - Determination - Respondent in view of development of industries introduced policy in region making it tax free zone for period of ten years giving incentives - However, another Notification was issued whereby exemption of central excise was withdrawn in respect of goods related to tobacco and pan masala, hence duty was charged to appellant industry - Lower court dismissed petition of appellant, however, DB reversed order of lower court and held that principal of promissory estoppel shall apply - Further, vide s. 154 of Act, withdrawal of benefit was effected from retrospective effect - Recovery order was passed by respondent from appellant, which was benefit that had been drawn by appellant for period November 1999 till February 2001 in terms of notification - Further, Tribunal passed order against appellant for deposit of recovery amount - Further, review petition was dismissed by HC - Whether order of lower Court at pre-deposit stage can operate as res judicata on merits.

Held, lower Court while dismissing petition, dealt with these issues, which touched upon merits of main issue, however, that observations which were made by lower Court on issues raised were only prima facie in nature and prime focus of judgment rested on core issue, namely, whether direction of Commissioner (Appeals) directing appellant to make deposit of amount as pre-condition for hearing of appeal was sustainable or not and petition was dismissed affirming said order. Therefore, any observations made by lower court which were tentative in nature, could not be taken into consideration by DB in impugned judgment, thereby dismissing Reference, invoking principle of res judicata. Order of lower court dismissing petition was challenged before DB and DB passed interim orders in appeals not to dismiss appeals preferred by appellant for non-deposit of duty. Hence, appeals were heard and appellant even partly succeeded. After order of Commissioner (Appeals) deciding appeals partly in favour of appellant, appeals which were pending before DB had become infructuous and disposed of as such without going into merit of order passed by lower Court, hence, order of lower Court could not be treated as res judicata. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Excise - Administrative - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Recovery Proceedings - Failure to issue notice - Justification - Whether recovery proceedings can be initiated without show-cause notice under s. 11A of Act, which is mandatory.

Held, issuance of show cause notice is basic requirement, hence, there was requirement of issuance of show-cause notice by Deputy Commissioner before passing order of recovery, irrespective of fact whether s. 11A of Act is attracted in instant case or not. However, SC held that even after notice is issued, appellant cannot take any plea to retain said amount on any ground whatsoever as it is bound by dicta in earlier judgment passed by SC. Further, with respect to quantification of amount is concerned, it is not disputed at all, hence, issuance of notice would be empty formality. Further, SC held that non-issuance of notice before sending communication has not resulted in any prejudice to appellant and it may not be feasible to direct respondents to take fresh action after issuing notice as that would be mere formality. Appeals dismissed.
