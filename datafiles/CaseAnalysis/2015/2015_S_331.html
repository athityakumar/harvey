Taramani Parakh v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 191; 2015 CRLJ 2031; 2015 (2) JabLJ 1; JT 2015 (3) SC 185; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 83; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 445; 2015(3) SCALE 616; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 672

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of proceedings - On complaint made by appellant alleging harassment demanding dowry, criminal proceedings were initiated against respondent no. 2-husband and his parents - However, on petition by respondents u/s. 482 of CrPC, said criminal proceedings were quashed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether criminal proceedings against respondents could be quashed.

Held, in complaint filed by appellant, there are allegations against respondent no. 2 and his parents for harassing her which forced her to leave matrimonial home. Even now, appellant continues to be separated from matrimonial home as she apprehended lack of security and safety and proper environment in matrimonial home. Further, question whether appellant has in fact been harassed and treated with cruelty is a matter of trial but at instant stage, it could not be said that no case is made out. Hence, quashing of proceedings before the trial is not permissible. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - If the allegations are absurd or do not make out any case or if it could be held that there is abuse of process of law, then proceedings could be quashed but if there is triable case, then Court should not go into reliability or otherwise of the version or the counter version.
