Vijay Mallya v Enforcement Directorate, Min. of Finance

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 482; (2015) 8 SCC 799; AIR 2015 SC 2726; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3271; 2015 CRLJ 3802; 2016 (332) E.L.T. 28; 2015 (3) JCC 2085; JT 2015 (6) SC 337; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 335; 2015(8) SCALE 1; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 709

Case Digest

Subject: Fema; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Fema - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 8(1), 9(1)(c), 40, 47(1), 47(2), 56 - No Response to Summons - Framing of charges - Justification - Appellant entered into agreement with company for advertisement of brand name on racing cars providing for fee payable - However, requisite permission of RBI was not taken which was in violation of provisions of ss. 47(1) & (2), 9(1)(c) and 8(1) of Act - Further, approval was later sought from Finance Ministry for payment on which was rejected - Appellant was summoned by Enforcement Directorate u/s. 40 of Act - Further, since appellant failed to appear in response to summons issued more than once, complaint u/s. 56 of Act was filed - Trial Court summoned appellant and framed charge against him u/s. 56 of Act - HC passed order against appellant and held that framing of composite charge could not be treated to have caused prejudice so as to vitiate proceedings - Further, with respect to three defaults made by appellant, proceedings were not liable to be interfered with as appellant could contest matter before Trial Court itself in first instance - Whether non-compliance to summons by appellant was deliberate which required punishment u/s. 56 of Act.

Held, fact that adjudicating officer chose to drop proceedings against appellant does not absolve appellant of criminal liability incurred by him by virtue of operation of s. 40 read with s. 56 of Act. Further, offence u/s. 56 read with s. 40 of Act is independent offence. SC held that if factual allegations contained in charge are to be proved eventually at trial of criminal case, appellant is still liable for punishment notwithstanding fact that presence of appellant was required by adjudicating officer in connection with enquiry into certain alleged violations of various provisions of Act, but at subsequent stage adjudicating officer opined that there was either insufficient or no material to proceed against appellant for alleged violations of Act, is immaterial. SC held that entire approach adopted by appellant is sheer abuse of process of law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Failure to punish accused who evades process of law and thereby commits independent offence on ground that he is found to be not guilty of substantive offence would be destructive of law and order, apart from being against public interest.
