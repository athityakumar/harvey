B. P. L. Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Cochin-II Commissionerate

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 324; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 556; JT 2015 (4) SC 426; 2015(6) SCALE 68

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 35 L(b) - Exemption - Disallowed - Appellant was engaged in manufacture of excisable goods - Appellant filed classification declaration from time to time and classified items and claimed exemption under Notification - Revenue took view that Defibrillators were not eligible to benefit of exemption Notifications - Show-cause notice was issued whereunder demand duty with respect to Defibrillators manufactured was proposed as duty in show-cause notice - Commissioner confirmed demand and imposed equivalent penalty u/s. 11AC of Act as well as further penalty - Appellant filed Appeal before Tribunal, issue was referred to Third Member, President of Tribunal who passed order disallowing benefit of notification to Defibrillator - Whether Tribunal erred in disallowing benefit of Notifications to appellant.

Held, originally those D.C. Defibrillators which were meant for both internal as well as external use and also pacemakers and their accessories etc. were eligible for exemption. What is omitted is not only external use but also accessories of these D.C. Defibrillators. It is not disputed by the appellant that their Defibrillators are primarily meant for external use. It is also admitted case that the defibrillator manufactured by the appellant is not implantable internally in the human body. Simply because it can be used internally as well but not without the paddles and paddle is optional accessory, it is difficult to hold that conditions contained in the exemption Notifications are satisfied. Reasoning and rational given by the Tribunal in coming to the conclusion that the goods of the appellant would not qualify the description contained in Notification was approved. It is trite that strict interpretation is to be given to the exemption notifications and it is upon the assessee to approve that he fulfills all the conditions of eligibility under such Notifications. No doubt there is difference between the 'actual use' and 'intended for use' and even when a product is not actually used but is capable of being used, it would be treated as the product 'for use'. Defibrillator when sold without paddle is not capable of being used internally and paddle is only sold as optional accessory. Majority opinion of the Tribunal is correct in law. Appeals dismissed.
