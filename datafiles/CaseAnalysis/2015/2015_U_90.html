P. Suseela and others Etc. v University Grants Commission and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 190; (2015) 8 SCC 129; AIR 2015 SC 1976; 2015(3) ALL MR 981; 2015 (3) AWC 2252; JT 2015 (4) SC 120; 2015 (3) MLJ 734; 2015(3) SCALE 726; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 643; 2015 (3) SLR 664

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: University Grants Commission Act, 1956, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 2005, University Grants Commission Regulations (Minimum Qualifications Required for the Appointment And Career Advancement of Teachers in Universities and Institutions affiliated to it) (the third Amendment) Regulation, 2009

Summary: Education - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 20, 26 - University Grants Commission Regulations (Minimum Qualifications Required for the Appointment and Career Advancement of Teachers in Universities and Institutions affiliated to it) (the third amendment) Regulation, 2009 - Regulation mandated NET/SLET to be minimum eligibility condition for recruitment and appointment of lecturers in universities/colleges/institutions and the same was challenged in before various HCs but said regulation was upheld by HCs - However, HC of Allahabad held that said regulations were issued pursuant to directions of Central Govt., which were issued outside powers conferred by UGC Act and so, eligibility conditions would not apply to M.Phil and Ph.d degrees awarded prior to 31-12-2009 - Hence, instant appeal by UOI against said judgment of HC of Allahabad and other appeals by M.Phil degree holders and Ph.D degree holders who have not been appointed as Assistant Professors in any University or College or Institutions - Whether impugned regulation is sustainable.

Held, both the directions dt.12-11-2008 and 30-3-2010 are directions made pertaining to questions of policy relating to national purposes in as much as, they were based on the Mungekar Committee report. Furthermore, Central Govt. is of opinion that common uniform nationwide test should be minimum eligibility condition for recruitment for the appointment of Lecturer and Asst. Professors in Universities or Colleges or Institutions since M.Phil degrees or Ph.d degrees are granted by different Universities and Institutions having differing standards of excellence. The object sought to be achieved by said directions is that all lecturers in universities, colleges, institutions governed by UGC Act should have certain minimum standard of excellence before they are appointed as such. Further, said directions are not only made in exercise of powers u/s. 20 of the Act but are made to provide for coordination and determination of standards which lies at the very core of the UGC Act. Hence, any regulation made u/s. 26 should conform to directions issued by Central Govt. u/s. 20 of the Act. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - S. 26 of the Act, enables Commission to make regulations which conform to s.20 of the Act. Further, u/s. 20 of the Act, Central Govt. is given the power to give directions on questions of policy relating to national purposes which will guide the Commission in the discharge of its functions under the Act.
