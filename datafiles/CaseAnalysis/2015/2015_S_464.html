Neeru Yadav v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 663; AIR 2015 SC 3703; 2015 CRLJ 4862; 2015 (1) JCC 2668; JT 2015 (9) SC 61; 2015(10) SCALE 234; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cancellation Of Bail

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 147, 148, 149, 302, s. 307, 34, 394, 411, 454, 506 - History Sheeter - Grant of bail - Justification - Complainant lodged FIR against accused persons for murder of his brother - Charge sheet was filed u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 394, 411, 454, 506, 120B read with s. 34 of IPC - Further, Trial Court rejected application of bail filed by respondent no.2 accused - However, HC held that considering nature of allegation, severity of punishment and period of detention, it is fit case for bail and bail application was allowed - Whether impugned order passed by HC granting bail to accused person is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, apart from present case, there are seven cases pending against respondent no.2. Hence, SC held that respondent no.2 is history-sheeter and is involved in heinous offences. Having stated facts and noting nature of involvement of accused in crimes in question, it can be held that respondent no. 2 is 'history sheeter'. SC held that HC has totally ignored criminal antecedents of accused. History-sheeter involved in nature of crimes are not minor offences so that he is not to be retained in custody, but crimes are of heinous nature and such crimes and it cannot be regarded as jejune. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. SC held that if respondent no.2 is at large, he shall be taken into custody forthwith; and if he is still in custody because of certain other cases, he shall not be admitted to bail in connection with present case. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to grant bail to offender who is history sheeter and is engaged in heinous crime.
