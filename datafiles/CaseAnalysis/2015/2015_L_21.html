Asger Ibrahim Amin v Life Insurance Corporation of India

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 705; 2015 (6) AWC 5829; JT 2015 (9) SC 329; 2015(10) SCALE 639; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 865; 2015 (6) SLR 731

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement, Termination Of Service, Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, Competent Authority, Pension Scheme, Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960, Life Insurance Corporation of India Class III and Class IV Employees (Revision of Terms and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1985, Life Insurance corporation of India (employees) Pension Rules, 1995, General Insurance (Termination, Superannuation and Retirement of Officers and Development Staff) Scheme, 1976, Employees' Pension Scheme, 1995

Summary: Service - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Employees) Pension Rules, 1995, r. 31 - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960 - Resignation - Eligibility to claim pension - Determination - Appellant joined services of respondent Corporation on 30-6-1967 on post of Assistant Administrative Officer (Chartered Accountant) at age of twenty seven and worked for 23 years and 7 months in Corporation before tendering his resignation on 28-1-1991, owing to personal reasons - Resignation of appellant was allowed by respondent Corporation and period to serve three months' notice was waived - Further, Central Govt. notified Regulation and Rules which, though notified on 28-6-1995, were given retrospective effect from 1-11-1993 - Pension Rules stated that resignation from service would lead to forfeiture of benefits of entire service including eligibility for pension - Further, respondent in view of Pension Rules rejected claim of appellant for pension on ground that appellant having resigned from service, was not eligible to claim pension under Pension Rules - Appeal filed by HC is dismissed by HC and LPA was also dismissed on grounds of delay - Whether appellant is entitled to claim pension even though he resigned from service of his own volition and taking into consideration limitation period.

Held, with respect to claim being time barred, SC held that such claims recur month to month and would not stand extinguished on application of laws of prescription, merely because legal remedy pertaining to time barred part of it has become unavailable. Further, with respect to appellant being entitled to pension after his retirement, SC held that appellant ought not to be deprived of pension benefits merely because he styled his termination of services as 'resignation' or because there was no provision to retire voluntarily at that time. Further, objective of Pension Rule is to extend benefits to class of people to tide over crisis and vicissitudes of old age, and if there are some inconsistencies between statutory provisions and avowed objective of statute so as to discriminate between beneficiaries within class, end of justice obligate to palliate differences between two and reconcile them as far as possible. Hence, so-called resignation tendered by appellant was after satisfactorily serving period of 20 years ordinarily qualifying or enabling voluntary retirement. Furthermore, while there was no compulsion to do so, waiver of three months notice period was granted by respondent Corporation. Hence, termination of services of appellant, was voluntary retirement within ambit of r. 31 of Rules Appellant is entitled for pension, provided he fulfills condition of refunding of entire amount of Corporation's contribution to Provident Fund along with interest accrued thereon as provided in Rules. Further, delay in approaching court, SC limited benefits of arrears of pension payable to appellant to three years preceding date of petition filed before HC. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Pension benefits cannot be denied to employee who has resigned from service where period of service qualify for voluntary retirement.
