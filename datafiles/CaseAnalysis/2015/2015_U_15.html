DM Wayanad Institute of Medical Sciences and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 490; (2016) 2 SCC 315; AIR 2015 SC 2940; 2015 (5) AWC 4440; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 235; JT 2015 (6) SC 493; 2015(8) SCALE 146; 2016(8) SCALE 146; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 81; 2015 (5) SLR 685

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Violation of fundamental rights, Permission to medical colleges

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art.32 - Permission to medical colleges - Violation of Fundamental Rights - Instant writ petitions by petitioners challenging refusal of Medical Council of India (MCI) to recommend renewal of permission for admitting students in MBBS course of petitioner institutes for academic year 2015-16 and consequent refusal of Union Govt. to renew such permission - Whether petitioners could invoke jurisdiction of SC u/art. 32 of Constitution.

Held, petitioners challenged the decision of MCI and Central Govt. refusing to grant permission or renewal to carry on their courses for academic session 2015-16 and said decisions were based on the inspection reports submitted by the teams of MCI. However, the jurisdiction of MCI or Central Govt. to grant or refuse to grant permission has not been challenged. Hence, it is well within the jurisdiction of MCI which is statutory body to take a decision based on the inspection of the college to satisfy itself the compliance of various provisions of the acts, rules, and regulations. Further, u/art. 32 of Constitution, SC is not supposed to go into finding of facts recorded by authorities and to come to a different conclusion. Furthermore, even though petitioners have a right to establish institutions for imparting medical and technical education, such right is not a fundamental right and even as per judgments of Constitution Bench of SC, rights claimed by petitioners are not fundamental rights, and hence, the same could not be agitated directly before SC u/art. 32 of Constitution and so, petitions are dismissed. However, petitioners could approach appropriate forum for redressal of their grievances. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - The SC u/art. 32 of Constitution does not interfere with an administrative order where the Constitutionality of the statute or the order made thereunder is not challenged on the ground of contravention of Fundamental Rights.
