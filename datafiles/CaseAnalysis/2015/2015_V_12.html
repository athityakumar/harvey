State of Karnataka TR. SEC. HSG. & URB. and another v Vasavadatta Cement and another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 2015 SC 1397; JT 2015 (2) SC 403; 2015 (3) KarLJ 1; 2015 (2) MLJ 350; 2015(2) SCALE 421; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 399; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 97

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Karnataka Municipalities Act, the Act, the 'Act', Karnataka Municipalities Act, 1961

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Municipalities Act,1961, s.9 - Posting of notification - Determination of - Govt. initially by draft Notification, proposed to alter existing limits of Council for inclusion of land in question within municipal limits of town municipality, inviting objections and suggestions to proposal from persons likely to be affected therein - It was followed by notification issued by Governor, altering existing limits of Council - Single Judge dismissed writ petition, challenging notification - DB allowed appeal and set aside order passed by Single Judge - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there is proper compliance of posting notification at requisite places as stated in s.9 of the Act.

Held, s.9 of the Act stipulates that whenever it is proposed to add or exclude from smaller urban area any inhabited area, it shall be duty of municipal council to cause a copy of such proclamation to be posted up in conspicuous places in such area. Phrase 'such area' means inhabited area which is proposed to be added or excluded from smaller urban area. On perusal of original record, it was found that two notifications are same but there is a substantial difference in last paragraph which mentions places where copies of notification were to be posted. In 1st notification, it is shown that notice to be posted at four places. Other notification shows that direction has been issued to post notification at nine places and additional five places include premises of respondent no.1. Second notification is signed in green ink by some other officer. Prima facie it appears that notification containing nine conspicuous places wherein it was to be notified, signed in green ink by some officer has been prepared subsequently. Thus, a detailed inquiry is required and State Govt. is directed to inquire into matter. Appeal allowed.
