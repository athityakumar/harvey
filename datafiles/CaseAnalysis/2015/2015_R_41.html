Rajendra Shankar Shukla and others etc. v State of Chhattisgarh and others etc.

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 503; (2015) 10 SCC 400; AIR 2015 SC 3147; JT 2015 (7) SC 104; 2015 (7) MLJ 578; 2015(8) SCALE 384; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 723

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Local Authority, Madhya Pradesh Zila Yojana Samiti Adhiniyam, 1995, M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, Chhattisgarh Zila Yojna Samiti Act, 1995, Chhattisgarh Town Planning Act, 1973

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, ss. 14, 17, 50 - Acquisition for township development - Authority to pass scheme - Determination - Appellants are landowners of land in question - Respondent no.2 planned township development scheme for which land to be acquired was increased and land in question was subsequently acquired - Further, few land owners failed to agree with scheme and filed petitions, however, petitions were dismissed by HC - Further, appeal was dismissed by DB on ground that same were devoid of merit - Whether Township Scheme provide authority to Director of respondent to formulate Town Development Scheme and is it in contravention to 73rd and 74th Amendments to Constitution.

Held, as per Part IX and Part IX-A of Constitution, zonal plan has to be framed by democratic institutions as prescribed under its provisions. However, respondent no. 2 has framed Town Development Scheme without consulting or taking into account views of Panchayat and District Planning Committee which are constitutionally authorized to undertake task of framing Scheme. Further, SC held that Chief Executive Officer of respondent no. 2 is not permitted to unilaterally prepare development scheme resulting reconstitution of land without taking into consideration opinion and suggestions of democratically elected bodies such as District Planning Committee and Officer of Town and Country Planning Department, as mentioned in Act. Further, as per evidence on record Chief Executive Officer of respondent No. formulated Town Development Scheme without taking opinion of local committees which are constitutionally authorized to make suggestions in matter of Town Development Scheme under amended provisions of s. 50(5) of Act. Hence, SC held that Master Plan so prepared is in clear contravention of s. 14 of Act read along with s. 17 of Act. Further, SC held that there is total lack of application of mind by respondent no. 1- State Govt. in not taking into consideration all relevant aspects while declaring Township Scheme and finance Scheme proposed by respondent no. 2. Further, initial approval of Scheme was on 25-1-2008 and approval to add 1900 acres of land to scheme dt. 10-8-2009 was granted by State Govt. without any application of mind and objective consideration by respondent no. 1 which fact is expressly clear as said proposed scheme was neither in accordance with Development Plan nor did any Zonal Plan which existed at material point of time. Appeals allowed

(B) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 50(1) - Formation of scheme - Subsequent acquisition - Justification - Whether Town Development Scheme is formulated as per provision mentioned in s. 50(1) of 1973 Act and subsequent alteration of land acquired, is in consonance with provisions of Act.

Held, respondent No. 2 has not put any document on record, either before HC nor SC which shows any assessment of need or requirement for town expansion conducted by it prior to proposing Township Scheme. Further, frequent changes in extent of land acquired for Township Scheme by respondent no.2 is very strong indicator of fact that there is no rationale behind proposal of said Scheme. Respondent No.2 had proposed area of Township scheme to be 900 acres on 31-7-2006, 1100 acres on 14-11-2006, 394 acres on 3-6-2008 and eventually 2300 acres on 20-7-2009, without assigning reasons for coming to such conclusions in expanding area to scheme. Hence, there is clear non-application of mind on part of State Govt. behind increase in sanctioned area of Scheme. Further, respondent no. 2 without any resolution of Board, on its own motion, addressed letter dt. 31-7-2006 and approached State Govt. for change of land use because it had to propose township in other villages. Under provisions of 1973 Act, development plan/ Raipur Master Prevised 2021 that is prevailing, respondent no. 2 and State Govt. gave primacy to Scheme and sought changes in master plan to suit scheme which is impermissible in law. Further, finding recorded by HC in its judgment in this regard that no finality can be attached to master plan is erroneous finding. Hence, SC held that scheme was not prepared in accordance with s. 50 of Act and Scheme is ultra vires to 1973 Act. Appeals allowed.

(C) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 20 Formation of scheme - Failure to have zonal plan - Justification - Whether Development Scheme framed by respondent no.2 , in absence of zonal plan, is legal and valid.

Held, in absence of zonal plan in place, respondent no. 2 has skipped legal mandate in place for preparation of Township Scheme. Further, object and purpose of Act itself foresees that zonal plan is necessary for implementation of Town Development Scheme. SC held that Township scheme has been prepared in absence of Zonal Plan. It is not possible to define utilization of land under Town Development Scheme unless Zonal Plan formulated by local authority describes with sufficient particularity details for which broadly indicated use of land in Development Plan may be put. Respondent no.2 is not permitted to either usurp or bypass power vested with local authorities for preparing town development scheme in absence of zoning plan merely on ground that local authority did not exercise its constitutional power in preparing zonal plan following direction of respondent no. 1 u/s. 20 of Act. Further, Master Plan shows that it does not set out detailed land use with sufficient particulars. Therefore, framing of Zonal Plan by local authority in laying out detailed plan of land use with sufficient particulars is sine qua non under provisions of Act. Hence, SC held that respondent no.2 could not have formulated scheme without Zoning Plan there in place.

(D) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 50(5) - Reconstitution of plots - Justification - Whether Act authorizes Town Planning and Development Authority to reconstitute plots and change land use apart from public utility.

Held, Board conferred power upon respondent no. 2 to make provision for reconstitution and not for reconstruction per se. In any event, such power to make provision for reconstitution is limited to certain specified public purposes, which does not include general housing scheme. Further, there is conspicuous absence of any empowering mechanism under Act for above purpose and no authority has been vested under provision of Act applicable to State, to adjust rights of parties in land. Further, SC held that functioning of Committee u/s. 50(5) of Act of is dissatisfactory and required process to be followed afresh. Further, committee rejected objections submitted by appellants without providing any reasons for same and not even providing any hearing opportunities to put forth their objections before said Committee. Hence, recommendations of Committee did not carry any weight. Hence, action of State Govt. is vitiated in law and liable to be set aside. Raghbir Singh Sehrawat v. State of Haryana, 2012, 1 SCC 792 2011 Indlaw SC 805, relied on SC held that reconstitution of plot for purpose of town development scheme is permissible for public purpose only and that too by following legal procedure of publication by authority in gazette about its intent to acquire land. In absence of same, and also when purpose for reconstitution of land is not for public purpose, such reconstitution of land is impermissible under Act. Hence, respondent no.2 could not have reconstituted plot for any other purpose other than public purpose. Appeals allowed.

(E) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Act, 1976, s. 40(3)(jj)(a) - M.P. (C.G.) Nagar Thatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 50(6)(vi) - Awarding of compensation - Return of land - Justification - Whether proposal of respondent no.2 to return 35% of area of land taken away from appellants is legally permissible.

Held, since compensation for acquisition of land need to be reasonable and adequate in interest of justice, Kesavananda Bharati Sripadagalvaru And Ors v State Of Kerala And Anr, 1973 Indlaw SC 537, relied on SC held that returning of 35% of land in lieu of acquisition is constitutionally impermissible. This is also because 'development' which occurs due to implementation of Town Development Scheme accrues benefit to everyone. In same way, appellants whose land has been acquired and proposed to be developed, would have gained from development, if at all, as member of community gaining from town development scheme and not in his individual capacity. Further, 35% figure was arrived at by respondent no. 2 while allocating reconstituted land to appellants, without any valid form of calculation arrived at by respondents. Even u/s. 40(3)(jj)(a) of 1976 Act, maximum permissible contribution of land by land owner cannot exceed 50%. Hence, in absence of any reasonable procedure arrived at by respondents, taking 65% of area of plot as development contribution is wholly unfair and arbitrary, and is also impermissible as per s. 50 (6)(vi) of 1973 Act. Hence, SC held that respondents were not justified in returning only 35% of reconstituted plots and retaining 65% for different purposes mentioned by them. Appeals allowed.

(F) Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Approval from authority - Environmental Compliance - Determination - Whether respondents ensured compliance with EIA clearance procedure from competent authority before planning Township Scheme.

Held, respondent no.2 submitted its application on 17-6-2010 for EIA approval for 2300 acres of township. On 25-1-2011, EC clearance/EIA approval was granted by State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA) to respondent no.2 which is not authority to give such clearance as per O.M dt. 13-1-2010 since same has to be granted by MoEF. Further, due to change in scope of project, respondent no. 2 was required to seek sanction for project from Central Govt. and same has not been done. Hence, scheme has also failed to obtain environmental clearance requirement which is mandatory requirement in law for initiating any project by respondent no. 2 faulty town development scheme prepared through incompetent authorities with violation of legal and environmental procedure cannot be reason for deprivation of constitutional rights of appellants. Hence, impugned orders passed by lower courts is set aside and prayer of appellants is allowed by quashing acquisition of their land of villages which were included subsequently in scheme. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Acquisition of land by authority is liable to be set aside when the same is not in accordance with law and procedures laid down.
