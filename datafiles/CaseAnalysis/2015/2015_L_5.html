H. Lakshmaiah Reddy and others v L. Venkatesh Reddy

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 284; AIR 2015 SC 2499; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 179; 2015(4) ALL MR 914; 2015 (111) ALR 238; 2015 (5) ALT(SC) 1; 2015 (3) AWC 2884; 2015 (2) CGLJ 420; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 88; JT 2015 (4) SC 284; 2015 (4) MLJ 99; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 997; 2015 (128) RD 641; 2015(5) SCALE 349; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 938; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 133

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 15 - Mutation - Declaration of Title - Suit property belonged to deceased-wife of appellant no. 1 and mother of respondent - After death of deceased, mutation in respect to suit property was effected in the name of respondent - Appellant no. 1 married another woman and appellant nos. 2 to 5 were their children - Appellants denied the ownership of the plaintiff in the suit property and respondent herein filed the suit against the appellants seeking for the relief of declaration of his title to the suit property which was dismissed - Lower Appellate Court held that respondent and appellant no. 1 being class-I heirs of deceased were entitled to half share each in the suit property and decreed the suit in part - HC decreed the suit in full as prayed for by respondent - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, assumption on part of HC that as result of mutation, appellant no. 1 divested himself of title and possession of half share in suit property was wrong. Mutation entries do not convey or extinguish any title and those entries are relevant only for the purpose of collection of land revenue. HC misdirected itself and impugned judgement of HC is set aside. Decree of the lower appellate court is restored. Appeals allowed.
