Star Industries v Commissioner of Customs (Imports), Raigad

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 689; (2016) 2 SCC 362; 2015 (324) E.L.T. 656; 2015(10) SCALE 529; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Adjudicating Authority, Imported Goods, Confiscation, Seized, India

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Custom Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3(1) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Ore Concentrate - Manufacture - Assessee regularly claimed benefit of Notification no.4/2006-CE on imports of Ore Concentrate and Customs Department extended benefit, whereby, no CVD was levied u/s.3(1) of the Act - However, basing on intelligence reports that assessee misdeclared goods and sought benefit of exemption under said Notification, goods of assessee were seized and later show cause notice was issued and demand was raised for differential duty - Later, order was passed by adjudicating authority confirming demand raised and on appeal, Tribunal vide impugned order concurred with opinion of adjudicating authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Ore Concentrate imported by assessee is eligible for complete exemption from payment of additional duty of custom i.e., countervailing duty(CVD) in addition to normal custom duty.

Held, with the insertion of Note 4 in Chapter 26 of 1985 Act, process of converting ores into concentrates would amount to 'manufacture'. Hence, Notification no.4/2006-CE which exempts only ores would not include within itself 'concentrates' and also because of the reason that after the insertion of Note 4, concentrate is to be treated as a different product than ores, in law for the purposes of products of Chapter 26. Hence, Tribunal had rightly arrived at conclusion that by virtue of Note 4, concentrate has to be necessarily treated as different from ores which is deemed as manufactured product after Molybdenum Ores underwent the process of roasting and when same is considered as manufacture, becomes liable for Central Excise levy. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Exemption notifications are to be given strict interpretation and even if two views were possible, the view that favours the Revenue had to be preferred while interpreting the same.
