Union of India and others v A. K. Behl, AVSM, PHS etc.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R.K. Agrawal, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 673; (2015) 9 SCC 256; AIR 2015 SC 2929; 2016(1) ALL MR 456; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, Armed Forces, Corps, Electricity (Supply) (H.P. Amendment) Ordinance, 1990

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Retirement age - Rules framed by Govt. - Justification - Provisions are made by Govt. in providing tenure for those holding rank of Lt. General and equivalent in AFMS subject to condition that in case officer completes his two years tenure before he attains 60 years of age he shall continue till that age - Respondent no.1 born on 17-9-1954 was commissioned in Army Medical Corps on 1-3-1976 and was under provisions of existing policy due to retire on 30-9-2014 on attaining age of 60 years after tenure of two years which he completed on 31-3-2014 - Further, respondent no.2 born on 24-9-1954 was also due to retire on 30-9-2014 - Respondent filed compliant before authority contending policy of Govt. prescribing tenure linked age of retirement for officers of rank of Lt. General and equivalent in Armed Forces Medical Services is ultra vires - Compliant filed by respondent was rejected by Competent Authority - However, Tribunal allowed applications filed by respondents and held that fixation of two year tenure is ultra vires and accordingly set aside clause with direction that all Lt. Generals and their equivalent in AFMS will retire only upon completion of 61 years of age - Whether 'Tenure Clause' applicable to AFMS officers of rank of Lt. General and its equivalent and to Director General Armed Force Medical Services (DGAFMS) is constitutionally valid.

Held, there is nothing wrong in stipulating of tenure of two years conditionally, specially when unconditional prescription had resulted in anomaly, where officer who because of his merit picked-up rank of Lt. General or equivalent early in life had to go out upon his completing period of two years in that rank, even before he completed age of 60 years which incidentally is age at which other similar ranks in army and services, retire. Further, SC held that tenure rule if allowed to have unhindered application may have taken retirement age of those who picked-up rank around time they were 60 years old to 62 years. Govt. in its wisdom did not approve of such scenario and made tenure rule subject to condition that officer holding rank of Lt. General or equivalent in AFMS must demit office at age of 61 regardless whether he has completed his two years tenure. SC held that Tribunal committed manifest error in holding that there was actually no tenure prescribed for those serving in rank of Lt. Generals and equivalent. It is one thing to say that officer will continue till age of 60 years even after he completes two years tenure but entirely different thing to say that there is no tenure at all. Tenure rule being conditional, same could and is being applied on uniform basis in all cases without exception. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Fixation of retirement age by Govt. cannot be challenged when same is applied on uniform basis and without being prejudicial to interest of others.
