International Advanced Research Centre For Powder Metallurgy and New Materials (ARCI) and others v Nimra Cerglass Technics Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 832; (2016) 1 SCC 348; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4101; JT 2015 (8) SC 536B; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 13; 2015(10) SCALE 136; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Contract, Criminal Prosecution, Cheating, Dishonest, Production, Fabrication, Criminal Liability, False Representation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 405, 415, 418, 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 197, 482 - Cheating - Denial of Quashing of Proceedings - Challenged - Respondent/complainant company dealing with manufacturing of scientific devices and equipments entered into agreement with appellant nos. 2-3 for transfer of technology - Further, on failure to achieve targeted specification of end product, complaint was filed, which was closed on basis of final report that dispute was purely civil nature - However, Magistrate took cognizance of offence in protest petition of respondent and issued summons order - HC dismissed petition of appellant seeking quashing of proceedings - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in order to constitute an offence of cheating, the intention to deceive should be in existence at the time when the inducement was made. It is necessary to show that a person had fraudulent or dishonest intention at the time of making the promise, to say that he committed an act of cheating. A mere failure to keep up promise subsequently cannot be presumed as an act leading to cheating. Further, while exercising inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC, it is not for the High Court to appreciate the evidence and its truthfulness or sufficiency inasmuch as it is the function of the trial court. High Court's inherent powers, be it, civil or criminal matters, is designed to achieve a salutary public purpose and that a court proceeding ought not to be permitted to degenerate into a weapon of harassment or persecution. If the averments in the complaint do not constitute an offence, the court would be justified in quashing the proceedings in the interest of justice. In instant case, Appellants No.2 and 3 who were the Associate Director and Director of ARCI respectively were only monitoring the progress of the project cannot be said to have committed the offence of cheating and allegation in the complaint do not constitute the offence alleged and continuation of the criminal proceeding is not just and proper and in the interest of the justice. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and criminal proceedings against appellant nos.1-3 is quashed. Appeal allowed.
