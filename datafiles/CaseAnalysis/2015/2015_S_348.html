Indra Vijay Alok v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 594; (2016) 1 SCC 709; AIR 2015 SC 3681; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4097; 2015 CRLJ 4545; 2015 (3) JabLJ 173; JT 2015 (8) SC 56; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 119; 2015(9) SCALE 393; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 288

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Conviction - Testimony of witnesses - Complainant made complaint to authorities over alleged demand of Rs.1,900 by appellant for carrying out mutation in revenue records and later, trap was laid and appellant was arrested - Upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court found accused guilty and passed sentence of imprisonment - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal before HC whereupon HC after re-appreciation of evidence, concurred with conviction and sentence recorded by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction and sentence recorded by Trial Court and as affirmed by HC could be upheld.

Held, statement of complainant gets corroborated by statement of DSP as well as by Deputy Collector. As such, Trial Court and HC have not committed any error of law in relying on testimony of said three witnesses as trustworthy, against the statement of panch witness-Sahir. Further, merely for the reason that panch witness-Sahir has not supported prosecution case, the ring of truth in the prosecution case is not shaken, particularly, when statements of remaining witnesses are credible and trustworthy. Hence, there is no illegality in appreciation of evidence by Trial Court and HC and so, impugned order could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.
