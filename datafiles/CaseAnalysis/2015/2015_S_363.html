Yakub Abdul Razak Memon v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 547; (2015) 9 SCC 552; 2015 (1) JCC 2556; JT 2015 (6) SC 626; 2015(8) SCALE 354; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Mercy Petition, Death Sentence, Attorney, Mercy, Prisoner

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 72 - Rejection of mercy petition - Stay over execution of death warrant - Justification - Death penalty was imposed on petitioner for which execution warrant was issued on 30-4-2015 and execution date of death penalty was on 30-7-2015 - Further, mercy petition filed by petitioner before President u/art. 72 is rejected - Petitioner contended that when mercy petition is rejected, there has to be minimum period of 14 days between its rejection being communicated to petitioner and his family and scheduled date of execution - Whether execution of death penalty imposed on petitioner should be stayed.

Held, death warrant was issued on 30-4-2015 which was admittedly received by petitioner on 13-7-2015 and date of its execution is 30-7-2015. Further, petitioner had not submitted any representation invoking authority of President u/art. 72 of Constitution. However, it is not in dispute that his brother had submitted and petitioner does not disown submission of petition by his brother on his behalf. Petitioner had communicated to Superintendent, Central Jail, on 7-8-2013, informing him about receipt of petition by office of President of India so as to pursue same. Further, said mercy petition as has been indicated earlier stood rejected on 11-4-2014 and petitioner did not think it appropriate to challenge rejection of mercy petition by President of India. SC held that mercy petition was preferred on 6-8-2013 and prior to that, review petition was dismissed by circulation on 30-7-2013 by two-Judge Bench of SC which had decided appeal on 21-3-2013. Since petitioner had not filed curative petition, he was entitled to seek reopening of review petition. Further, his review petition was heard by three-Judge Bench in open Court. After rejection of said review petition on 9-4-2015, he filed curative petition on 22-5-2015 which also got dismissed on 21-7-2015. Further, petitioner submitted mercy petition to Governor, which was received on 22-7-2015. He also submitted another mercy petition to President of India which was received by President of India at 2.00 p.m. on 29-7-2015, however, both these mercy petitions have been rejected. SC held that petitioner was tried for bomb blast case and stood convicted in year 2007. Almost 22 years have passed since 1993 when incident occurred. SC held that when first mercy petition was rejected on 11-4-2014, there was sufficient time available to petitioner to make arrangement for his family members to meet him in prison and make necessary worldly arrangements, however, petitioner chose not to challenge petition. Hence, SC held failed to agree that present mercy petition was preferred by petitioner for first time and, therefore, 14 days' time should be granted so that he can do needful as per law. Hence, it is not justified to stay execution of death warrant. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Execution of death warrant cannot be challenged when procedures provided under Constitution are duly followed.
