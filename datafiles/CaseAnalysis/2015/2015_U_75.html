Union of India v Manjeet Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 360; AIR 2015 SC 2114; 2015 (4) AWC 3650; JT 2015 (5) SC 255; 2015 (4) MLJ 617; 2015(6) SCALE 276; 2016(6) SCALE 276; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 192; 2015 (5) SLR 327; 2015 (3) UPLBEC 1753

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Army Rules, 1954

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Rules, 1954, r. 14 - Alleged physical disability - Removal from Service - Failure to grant disability pension - Respondent joined Army service after undergoing medical examinations, however, respondent while on training fell unconscious and was treated for his ailment in hospital - Medical Board lowered his medical category - Further, he was invalided from service on being adjudged unsuitable, by invaliding Medical Board which assessed his disability percentage as 20% qua first disability; and 20% for second disability; summing upto 40% for both diseases and was denied disability pension - HC held that invaliding Medical Board having failed to record reasons that disease could not be detected on medical examination at time of entry in service and that same could not have aggravated during course of his employment and it was not in compliance of Regulations and respondent was entitled for disability pension, hence, order passed by appellant authority was set aside - Order passed by lower Court was affirmed by DB - Whether order passed by lower Court in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, adverting inter alia to r. 14(b) of Rules, SC held that reasons, that diseases could not be detected on medical examination prior to acceptance in service, ought to have been obligatorily recorded by Medical Board sans whereof, respondent would be entitled to benefit of statutory inference that same had been contracted during service or have been aggravated thereby. Further, there is no reason forthcoming in proceedings of Medical Board, as to why his disabilities eventually adjudged to be constitutional or genetic in nature had escaped notice of authorities concerned at time of his acceptance for Army service. On comprehensive consideration of regulation, rules and general principles as applicable, service profile of respondent and proceedings of Medical Board, SC held that respondent was wrongly denied benefit of disability pension. His tenure albeit short, during which he had to be frequently hospitalized does not irrefutably rule out possibility, in absence of any reason recorded by Medical Board that disability even assumed to be constitutional or genetic, had not been induced or aggravated by arduous military conditions. SC held that requirement of recording reasons is not contingent on duration of Army service of member thereof and is instead of peremptory nature, failing which decision to board him out would be vitiated by inexcusable infraction of relevant statutory provisions. Further, burden to establish disability would lie heavily upon employer, for otherwise Rules raise presumption that deterioration in health of member of service was on account of Army service or had been aggravated by it. Hence, order passed by lower Court in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Authority cannot withhold disability pension to applicant who is removed from service due to physical disability which was detected after date of his appointment.
