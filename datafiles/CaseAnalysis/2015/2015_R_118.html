Ajithkumar P. and another v Remin K. R. and another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 730; 2016(1) ALL MR 469; 2015 (147) FLR 877; JT 2015 (9) SC 322; 2015(10) SCALE 684; 2015 (6) SLR 724

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Examination, Examinations, Public Service Commission, Relaxation, Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, Reservation

Summary: Service - Kerala Public Service Commission Rules, rr. 2(g), 4 - Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, r. 14(e) - Alleged Relaxation of Cut-off marks - Selection invalid - Commission issued notification inviting applications from qualified candidates for appointment to posts of Sub-Inspector - Commission initially opined that 2000, out of total 42000, applicants could be short-listed to appear for r. 3 examination process - However, Commission reached tentative conclusion that subjecting only those 2000 candidates for r. 3 examinations may not yield enough candidates to fill up vacancies belonging to various reserved categories, hence, Commission decided to permit some more candidates belonging to various reserved categories - Further, 657 candidates became eligible to appear in r. 3 examinations - It is alleged that decision of Commission to relax cut-off marks with respect to reserved category candidates is not in accordance with law - Further, Commission conducted r. 3 examinations, selected 838 candidates and published 'ranked list' - Further, Tribunal and HC passed order against appellant and held that they were not eligible to participate in r. 3 examination and r. 3 examinations does not confer any right on them to claim open category posts and they are entitled to compete only for those posts which are reserved in favour of respective class to which each of appellants belongs - Hence, present appeal.

Held, conclusion reached by HC is erroneous. Preliminary examination for shortlisting candidates who would be eligible to take r. 3 examinations has no statutory basis. Neither State Rules nor Rules of Procedure contemplate such preliminary examination. R. 14(e) of Commission Rules and r. 4 of Rules of Procedure relied upon by HC refer to 'ranked list' - a defined expression u/r. 2(g) of Rules of Procedure. Such 'ranked-list' is prepared only pursuant to the r. 3 examinations. Further, preliminary screening test is outside purview of r. 3 examinations. Hence, Rules can have no application in context of preparation of 'shortlist' pursuant to preliminary examination. Hence, impugned order passed by HC and Tribunal is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Preliminary screening of candidate done by commission cannot be challenged when Rules laid down has no application on such selection.
