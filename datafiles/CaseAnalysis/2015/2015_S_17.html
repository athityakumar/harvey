Chairman & Managing Director, TNHB and another v S. Saraswathy and others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 354; (2015) 8 SCC 723; AIR 2015 SC 2315; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 33; 2015 (111) ALR 507; 2015 (4) AWC 3601; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 171; JT 2015 (5) SC 562; 2016 (1) MahLJ 648; 2015 (5) MLJ 248; 2016 (1) MPLJ 253; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 540; 2015 (129) RD 155; 2015(6) SCALE 229; 2016(6) SCALE 229; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 11(1), 23(1) - Acquisition of land - Issuance of No-objection Certificate - Justification - State Govt. initiated land acquisition proceedings to acquire land under question for housing scheme - Notification u/s. 4 of Act was issued on 23.10.1975 and published followed by declaration u/s. 6 of Act issued and published - Two batches of petitions were filed by petitioner alleging constitutional validity of ss. 11(1) and 23(1) of Act and contended that compensation determined as on date of publication of notification u/s. 4 of Act was inequitable and arbitrary and second batch laid assault to notifications published under Act in their entirety - HC allowed petition filed by one party granting relief of quashing of declaration, however, notification issued u/s. 4 of Act was left intact, and it declined relief to those petitioner, who acquired ownership of land under acquisition after issuance of notification u/s. 4 of Act - Further, despite acquisition of suit land having been completed in all respects, petitioner illegally divided suit land into twelve plots and sold them in year 1987 to respondents vide registered Sale Deeds, after over three years of vesting of land into State - Respondent filed petitions seeking protection of their possession and enjoyment over suit land - HC vide order allowed petitions of respondents, believing that SC had quashed acquisition proceedings in totality and directed Govt. to issue no objection certificate to respondent - Whether order passed by lower Court in favor of respondent is in accordance with law.

Held, unless declaration u/s. 6 of Act or notification u/s. 4 of Act is not explicitly quashed in toto or in its wholeness by Court, benefits of relief granted by Court would be effective only qua parties before it. SC held that at time of second batch of appeal came to be decided, three year limitation period to publish declaration u/s. 6 of Act had already expired, making it impossible for Govt. to complete fresh process culminating in another declaration; and it was for this reason that acquisition was quashed by Court. SC held that respondents including their vendor, cannot be permitted to take any advantage of orders passed by SC in second batch of appeal. SC held that relief of quashing of declaration u/s. 6 of Act was expressly limited to some while being plainly denied to others, signifying thereby, that declaration u/s. 6 was left untouched in other cases. Further, in second batch of appeal, SC annulled notification issued u/s. 4 on premises of limitation which mean that rest of acquisition proceedings was left untouched by SC in second batch of appeal. Further, respondents had purchased suit land after award had been passed and possession of land had been taken by State, they could not have acquired any rights against State. Further, petitioner of first batch did not bring down acquisition proceedings qua his land, but on contrary, by accepting compensation, had manifested his acceptance of award. Hence, once land stood vested in State u/s. 16 of Act, petitioner of first batch and his vendees, i.e. respondents, could not have created and engineered rights or interests in property against State, except right of seeking and receiving enhanced compensation. Hence, impugned order passed by lower Court is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Once land is acquired by State, no rights are vested with parties against State and they cannot claim title to property.
