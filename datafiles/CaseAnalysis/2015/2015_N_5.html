N. Sudhir and others v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 47; (2015) 6 SCC 685; 2015(2) ALL MR 899; JT 2015 (1) SC 215; 2015(1) SCALE 323; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 884; 2015 (2) SLR 38; 2015 (4) UPLBEC 2601

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Inter Se Seniority, All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, Madras University Act, 1923, Tamil Nadu Private Colleges (Regulation) Act, 1976, MCI Regulations, Post-graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, Kerala Medical Officers' Admission to Postgraduate Courses under Service Quota Act, 2008

Summary: Education - Constitution - Kerala Medical Officers' Admission to Postgraduate Courses under Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Medical Council of India Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000 - Validity of S. 5(4) of the Act - Petitioners filed petitions challenging the constitutional validity of s. 5(4) of the Act in so far as it provides that 'admission to post-graduate in-service quota shall be only on the basis of seniority' - Petitioners submitted that the State legislature could not enact a law that would make selection for admission to the post-graduate courses dependent solely on the seniority of the in-service candidates without prescribing the minimum conditions of eligibility for the candidates concerned - Further submitted that the said piece of legislation violated the regulations framed by the Medical Council of India the authority competent to do so - HC agreed in principle that admission to post-graduate courses can be made only on the basis of inter se seniority provided the candidates appear in the common entrance examination and qualify - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC was right in holding that inasmuch as the provisions of s. 5(4) of the Act provides a basis for selection of candidates different from the one stipulated by the MCI Regulations it was beyond the legislative competence of the State Legislature. Having said that the HC adopted a reconciliatory approach when it directed that seniority of the in-service candidates will continue to play a role provided the candidates concerned have appeared in the common entrance test and secured the minimum percentage of marks stipulated by the Regulations. The HC was, not correct in making that declaration. A meritorious in-service candidate cannot be denied admission only because he has an eligible senior above him though lower in merit. It is now fairly well settled that merit and merit alone can be the basis of admission among candidates belonging to any given category. In service candidates belong to one category. Their inter-se merit cannot be overlooked only to promote seniority which has no place in the scheme of MCI Regulations. That does not mean that merit based admissions to in-service candidates cannot take into account the service rendered by such candidates in rural areas. Weightage for such service is permissible while determining the merit of the candidates in terms of the third proviso to regn. 9 of Regulations. Regn. 9 of Regulations remains as the only effective and permissible basis for granting admission to in-service candidates provisions of s. 5(4) of the Act notwithstanding. That being so, admissions can and ought to be made only on the basis of inter se merit of the candidates determined in terms of the said principle which gives no weightage to seniority simplicitor. Appeals dismissed.
