Balu @ Bala Subramaniam and another v State (U.T. of Pondicherry)

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 720; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4537; 2016 CRLJ 176; 2016 (1) JCC 419; JT 2015 (9) SC 387; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 371; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 180; 2015(11) SCALE 635; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Intention, Injured Witness, Overt Act, Attack, Constructive Liability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 326, 34 - Alleged Murder - Common Intention - Determination - There was dispute between parties with respect to chit transaction, however, there was settlement talks between parties - It is alleged that accused nos. 1 and 2 murdered deceased victim - Further, PW2 was attacked and he sustained injury on his left hand - Further, accused no.1 and 3 chased PW3 and accused no.2 assaulted and inflicted cut injuries on head of PW3 - Further, accused persons also inflicted cut injury on PW4 - FIR was lodged against accused persons u/ss. 147, 148, 307 read with s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court held that prosecution has established guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt and convicted all five accused u/ss. 148, 302 read with s. 149 of IPC - However, HC modified conviction and party allowed criminal allowed - Hence, present appeal.

Held, consistent version of PWs 1 and 5 establish presence of appellants and that they attacked victim with sticks. Presence of appellants and that they were armed with sticks is also substantiated by evidence of injured witness PW2. Findings recorded by courts below that appellants attacked victim with sticks are unassailable. However, SC held that facts and circumstances of case show that attack was not premeditated one nor was there prior concert. Initially settlement talks were on and fight started only when accused party was informed by their person that other person was cut by complainant party and thus incident arose suddenly. Further, SC held that way occurrence took place as depicted by prosecution, there could not have been common intention between accused. Further, appellants were not armed and admittedly they are said to have removed sticks from bullock cart standing nearby and on exhortation by accused No. 1, appellants have attacked deceased victim. There may be similar intention in minds of assailants to attack; but it cannot be said that appellants have acted in furtherance of common intention to attract constructive liability u/s. 34 of IPC. Further, SC held that though HC has modified conviction from s. 302 read with s. 149 IPC as s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC, HC has not recorded any finding as to how appellants shared common intention to establish their constructive liability to sustain conviction u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC. Hence, conviction of appellants u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC cannot be sustained and same is modified as conviction u/s. 325 of IPC and sentence is modified to period of imprisonment already undergone. Further, no specific overt act is attributed to appellants in attacking prosecution witnesses 2, 4 and 5. As appellants have not shared or acted in furtherance of common intention in attack of witnesses, hence, conviction of appellants as modified by HC u/s. 326 read with s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 324 read with s. 34 of IPC cannot be sustained and same is set aside. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - For conviction of an offence read with s. 34 of IPC, it is necessary that there should be a finding as to the common intention of the participants.
