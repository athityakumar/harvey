State of West Bengal and others v R.K.B.K. Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 604; (2015) 10 SCC 369; AIR 2015 SC 3411; 2015 (4) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2015 (8) SC 175; 2015 (7) MLJ 105; 2015(9) SCALE 550; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 309

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Essential Commodities Act, 1955

Summary: (A) Administrative - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - West Bengal Kerosene Oil Control Order, 1968, s. 3(e) - Excess quantity of oil delivered - Action taken by authority - Jurisdiction of Authority - Respondent no,1 was granted licence for carrying on business of superior kerosene oil as agent by Joint Director of Consumer Goods, in accordance with Control Order - Further, physical inspection was carried out by Inspector and it was complained that excess quantity of superior kerosene was delivered in excess - Show cause was issued to respondent no.1 by Sub Divisional Controller, Food and Supplies (SCFS), however, SCFC forwarded entire record to District Controller, who in turn sent entire case records to Director of Consumer Goods for appropriate decision - Director of Consumer Goods issued show cause notice - Respondent no.1 contended that after licence is issued to agent by Office of Director, District Magistrate having jurisdiction or any officer authorised by him, is alone empowered to look into functioning of said agency and to give directions to him and/or initiate action against concerned agent - Director imposed penalty on respondent no.1 and reduced allocation of kerosene - Lower Court held that impugned order passed by Director of Consumer Goods is without jurisdiction and directed to approach alternative forum - Further, impugned order stood extinguished by expiration of time limit - However, DB reversed order passed by lower court - Whether Director of Consumer Goods, Food and Supplies Department has jurisdiction to take action against respondent no.1.

Held, intent of Control Order is to totally prohibit creation of any kind of situation which will frustrate proper distribution of kerosene oil. It would be incongruous to hold that even when licence of agent at State level is granted and issued by Director, District Magistrate, as defined in paragraph 3(e) of Control Order, in exercise of concurrent jurisdiction can suspend or cancel State level licence. SC held that as per s. 21 of Act, power to issue notification/ order/rules/bye-laws, etc. includes power to amend/ vary or rescind. Further, it is indicative that generally unless statute or rule provides to contrary, either expressly or impliedly, issuing or appointing authority would also exercise right to cancel or suspend licence. SC held that expression 'District Magistrate having jurisdiction' reflects legislative intent that District Magistrate having jurisdiction under paragraph 9 of Control Order would be same District Magistrate or authority which has power to grant licence to dealer under paragraph 6. Hence, SC has no hesitation in holding that it is Director alone who could have issued show cause notice under paragraph 9 and has authority and jurisdiction to pass order in terms of paragraph 9 of Control Order. Earlier notice issued by SCFS has to be regarded at best show cause notice to ascertain and affirm facts alleged and it ensured response and reply from respondent no.1. Further, said notice by SCFS could not have culminated in order under paragraph 9, for he has no authority and jurisdiction to pass order suspending or cancelling licence. Therefore, matter was rightly referred to Director for action, if required, in terms of paragraph 9 of Control Order. Appeal allowed.

(B) Administrative - West Bengal Kerosene Oil Control Order, 1968 - Communication of impugned order - Validity of impugned order - Determination - Whether order has to be passed and communicated within 30 days under Control Order

Held, order passed by authority comes into effect when it is communicated. Order passed in file in case of this nature would not be effective order, for it is adverse to interest of dealer or agent. SC held that words used in paragraph 10 of Control Order are 'date of order' and in scheme of Control Order, order comes into effect from date of receipt by agent or dealer. Once that becomes decision, commencement of limitation of 30 days for purpose of paragraph 10 of Control Order would be date when order is effective. Appeal can be filed once order is communicated and effective. Hence, SC held that HC has erroneously interpreted paragraph 9 and 10 of Control Order and that is why it has arrived at erroneous conclusion. Hence, as SC held that Director alone has jurisdiction to pass order, said order remains valid order and can be challenged in appeal under paragraph 10 and appellate authority would be State Govt. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Unless order is communicated to concerned party with in limitation period, same cannot be held as valid order.
