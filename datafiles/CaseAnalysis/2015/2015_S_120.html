AG v Shiv Kumar Yadav and another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 619; (2016) 2 SCC 402; AIR 2015 SC 3501; 2015 CRLJ 4640; JT 2015 (8) SC 224; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3470; 2015 (4) RLW 3271; 2015(9) SCALE 649; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 455

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Right, Advocates Act, 1961, Attorney, Legal Aid

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 311, 313 - Statement recorded of accused - Recalling of witness - Justification - FIR was filed on 6-12-2014 against respondent accused alleging that accused committed rape on victim - Further charge sheet was filed on 24-12-2014 - Since accused was not represented by counsel, he was provided legal aid counsel and thereafter on 2-1-2015, accused engaged his private counsel in place of legal aid counsel - Witnesses were duly cross-examined by counsel engaged by accused and statement of accused u/s. 313 of CrPC was recorded on 3-2-2015 - Further, on 4-2-2015, application for recall of prosecutrix PW2 and formal witness PW 23 who booked cab was made, but same was rejected - Thereafter on 9-2- 2015, accused engaged another counsel, who filed another application u/s. 311 of CrPC for recall of all 8 prosecution witnesses which was dismissed by Trial Court, however, it was allowed by HC vide impugned order on 4-3-2015 - Whether recall of witnesses, at stage when statement of accused u/s. 313 of CrPC has been recorded, could be allowed on plea that defence counsel was not competent and had not effectively cross-examined witnesses.

Held, not single specific reason has been assigned by HC as to how in present case recall of 13 witnesses was necessary as directed in impugned order. No fault has been found with reasoning of order of Trial Court. HC rejected on merits only two reasons pressed before it that trial was hurried and counsel was not competent. In face of rejecting these grounds, without considering hardship to witnesses, undue delay in trial, and without any other cogent reason, allowing recall merely on observation that it is only accused who will suffer by delay as he was in custody could, in circumstances, be hardly accepted as valid or serving ends of justice. It is not only matter of delay but also of harassment for witnesses to be recalled which could not be justified on ground that accused was in custody and that he would only suffer by prolonging of proceedings. Mere observation that recall was necessary 'for ensuring fair trial' is not enough unless there are tangible reasons to show how fair trial suffered without recall. Recall is not matter of course and discretion given to court has to be exercised judiciously to prevent failure of justice and not arbitrarily. While party is even permitted to correct its bona fide error and may be entitled to further opportunity even when such opportunity may be sought without any fault on part of opposite party, plea for recall for advancing justice has to be bona fide and has to be balanced carefully with other relevant considerations including uncalled for hardship to witnesses and uncalled for delay in trial. Having regard to these considerations, SC found no ground to justify recall of witnesses already examined. Further, expeditious trial in heinous offence as is alleged is in interests of justice. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and application for recall is dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Recalling of witness should be bonafide and should not cause hardship to victim and accused.
