Secretary, Minor Irrigation Department and R.E.S. v Narendra Kumar Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 253; 2015 (110) ALR 336; 2015 (3) AWC 2568; 2015 (145) FLR 779; JT 2015 (4) SC 17; 2015 (3) MLJ 616; 2015(4) SCALE 569; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 742; 2015 (3) SLR 685; 2015 (2) UPLBEC 1161

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc, Uttar Pradesh Regularisation of Ad Hoc Appointments (on posts within the purview of the Public Service Commission) (Second Amendment) Rules, 1989, Uttar Pradesh Regularisation of Ad hoc Appointments (on posts within the purview of the Public Service Commission) Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Regularisation of Ad hoc Appointments (on posts within the purview of the Public Service Commission) Rules, 1979 - Benefit of Past Service - Entitlement - Petitioner who was first appointed as Work Engineer by Department was later on appointed on temporary post of Assistant Engineer in regular scale on ad hoc basis - Petitioner filed petition with grievance that in seniority list, his name was placed at Serial No.274 showing his service w.e.f. 14-12-1989 - In doing so, he was not given benefit of past service, while such benefit was given to other similarly placed persons - HC held that petitioner was not entitled to count adhoc service for his seniority - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, scheme of the working of the Rules in the Department shows that right from 1979, the Department has been making direct recruitment after due selection and by applying the 1979 Rules which rules have been extended from time to time to subsequent recruitments, services were regularized. In such circumstances, when the rules provide that such adhoc appointments have to be regularized and seniority counted from the date of appointment, the petitioner could not be deprived of the past service rendered by him from 12-6-1985 till the date of regularization. It is not a case of appointments made without due selection or without vacancy or without qualification or in violation of rules. Appointment of the petitioner was not dehors the rules nor by way of stop gap arrangement. The rules had the effect of treating the appointment as a regular appointment from initial date of appointment. Applying the principle laid down in Direct Recruit Class II Engineering Officers' Association, petitioner is entitled to count service from 12-6-1985. Appeal allowed.
