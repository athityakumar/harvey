Noorunissa Begum v Brij Kishore Sanghi

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 406; AIR 2015 SC 2322; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 155; 2015 (2) RCR(Rent) 327; 2015(3) SCALE 211; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 246

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Decree For Eviction, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, States Reorganisation Act, 1956, Completion Of Construction, Madras Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1949, Andhra State Act, 1953, Hyderabad House (Rent, Eviction and Lease) Control Act, 1954, Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control (Amendment) Act, 2005, Assam Non-Agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act

Summary: (A) Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control (Amendment) Act, 2005, s. 32(c) - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 26, 32 - Exemption of certain buildings - Amendment having prospective application - Determination - S. 32 of Act, as it stood on date of enactment exempted buildings owned by Govt. and buildings constructed on and after 26-8-1957 from purview of Act. However, after declaration of s. 32(b) of Act as unconstitutional - Further, Govt. issued order in 1983 whereby it exempted certain buildings whose rent was more that Rs. 1000 from operation of provisions of Act. Further, s. 32 of Act was amended by Amendment Act, 2005 and Act was made inapplicable to buildings rent of which as on date of commencement of Amendment Act, 2005, exceeds Rs.3,500/- p.m. in areas covered by Municipal Corporations in State and Rs.2,000/- p.m. in other areas - On amendment of s. 32 of Act, tenants of buildings who were exempted under Govt. notification against whom eviction cases or appeal or revision or execution cases were pending before various courts approached HC contended in view of amended s. 32 of Act, pending suits cannot be adjudicated by civil courts and decrees already passed cannot be executed because courts which passed decrees will be deemed to have become 'coram non judice' - Larger Bench vide impugned order held that Amendment Act, 2005 to be prospective - Further, it held that cl.(c) of Govt. order id reluctant - Whether s. 32 of Act has prospective operation.

Held, after amendment of s. 32 by Amendment Act, 2005, tenants of building, rent of which is Rs.3,500/- p.m. or below in areas covered by Municipal Corporations and tenants of building, rent of which is Rs.2,000/- p.m. or below in other areas, only remain as protected tenants. In effect, rest of tenants of any building rent of which exceeds Rs.3,500/- p.m. in area covered by Municipal Corporation and Rs.2,000/- p.m. in other areas, no more remain tenant within meaning of Act and Act is not applicable to them. Further, since, 2005, class of tenants of building whose rent exceeds Rs.3,500/- p.m. in Municipal Corporation areas and Rs.2,000/-p.m. in other areas, lost their right of protection under Act in view of amended s. 32(c) of Act and tenants of any building constructed or substantially renovated, either before or after commencement of Act for period of fifteen years from date of completion of such construction or substantial renovation, no more remain 'tenant' within meaning of Act and they also lost their right of protection in view of amended s. 32(b). However, position with regard to building belonging to Govt. remained same. Hence, part of s. 32 i.e. s. 32(a) and (c) is prospective and other part i.e. S. 32(b) is retrospective. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Grant of exemption to buildings - Clause held reluctant - Determination - Whether clause of govt. order which is declared reluctant by larger Bench is in accordance with law.

Held, exemption granted by State Govt. u/s. 26 of Act by Govt. order as overriding effect over rest of provisions of the Act. Further, buildings whose rents are upto Rs.3,500/- in Municipal areas and Rs.2,000/- in other areas were already covered by Act and after amendment it continues to be covered by Act but the tenants of buildings, rent of which is more than Rs.1,000/- and does not exceed Rs.3,500/- in Municipal area or Rs.2,000/- in other area even after amendment of S. 32 cannot claim protection in view of exemption granted u/s. 26 of the Act. SC held that Ss. 26 and s. 32 of Act operate in two different fields. S. 32 of Act relates to non applicability of Act to a class of building whereas S. 26 deals with power of State to exempt building or class of buildings to which Act is applicable. Further, there is no clash between s. 26 and s. 32 of Act, as they operate in two different fields, hence, question of overriding of one over another does not arise. Further, SC held that Cl. (a) of Govt. order has become redundant, however, cl. (b) of Govt. order still holds good. Further, suit, appeal, revision application or execution case which are pending for determination under General Law are not affected by amended s. 32 of Act and will continue to be decided in accordance with General Law. SC held that order of HC with respect to prospective operation of s. 32(c) is upheld and its effects on pending proceeding. However, finding of majority decision in regard to cl. (b) of Govt. order declaring said part of the Govt. order as redundant is declared bad in law and is set aside. Appeal disposed of.
