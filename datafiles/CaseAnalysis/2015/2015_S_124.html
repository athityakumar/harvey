Shree Bhagwati Steel Rolling Mills v Commissioner of Central Excise and another

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 802; (2016) 3 SCC 643; 2015 (326) E.L.T. 209; JT 2015 (12) SC 355; 2015 (8) MLJ 613; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 644; 2015(12) SCALE 686; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Rules, 1944, Central Excise Act, Compounded Levy, General Clauses Act, 1897, Enactment, Defence of India Rules, 1962, Induction Furnace Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997, Central Excise Act, 1994

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 3A, 37 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 96ZO, 96ZP and 96ZQ - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - Delayed payment of tax - Imposition of penalty - Appellant opted for compounded levy scheme u/r.96ZP of the Rules, which was introduced by s.3A of the Act and later, surrendered its registration after expiry of lease - However, appellant was issued notice demanding interest for delayed payment of duty and appellant approached HC wherein it was held that on omission of s.3A, liability of the assessee was not wiped out - Hence, appeal by assessee - Further, civil applications were filed before HC challenging constitutional validity of rr. 96ZO, 96ZP and 96ZQ as they prescribed imposition of penalty equal to amount of duty outstanding without any discretion to reduce the same depending upon the time taken to deposit the duty - HC struck down said rules on basis that not only were they ultra vires the Act but they were arbitrary and unreasonable and hence, violative of arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Hence, appeals by revenue - Whether instant batch of appeals could be allowed.

Held, as s.3A of the Act that provided for a separate scheme for availing facilities under a compound levy scheme had not provided for the levying of interest, rr. 96ZO, 96ZP and 96ZQ of the Rules, could not do so and hence, impugned judgment is set aside. Further, penalty could only be levied by authority of statutory law, and s. 37 of the Act had not expressly authorized the Govt. to levy penalty higher than Rs.5000 and so, imposition of a mandatory penalty equal to the amount of duty not being by statute makes rr.96ZO, 96ZP and 96ZQ without authority of law. Hence, said rules are struck down insofar as they impose mandatory penalty equivalent to the amount of duty since said provisions are violative of arts.14, 19(1)(g) and are ultra vires of the Act. Further, load capacity of an induction furnace unit is certainly relevant material referred to in r.3(2) to determine the capacity of the furnace installed. Further, it is not necessary to state such load capacity in terms for it to be included in r.3(2). Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and further, Chartered Engineer Certificate dealing with the sanctioned electrical load for a furnace is a relevant consideration which could be looked at in the absence of other factors mentioned in r.3. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per decision of SC, interest could be levied and charged on delayed payment of tax only if the statute that levies and charges the tax makes a substantive provision in that behalf.
