Muneer Enterprises v Ramgad Minerals and Mining Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 342; (2015) 5 SCC 366; AIR 2015 SC 1834; 2015(3) SCALE 431; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 551

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Mining Operations, Forest (Conservation) Rules, 2003, Food Safety and standards (Amendment) Rules, 2014

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 - Surrender of Lease - Subsequent Transfer of lease - Justification - Original licensee was granted miming lease in question, however, it had surrendered lease on later date - However, after surrendering of lease it is stated that original licensee transferred lease in favor of respondent no.1 - State Govt. passed orders allowing application for transfer of mining lease as applied for by original licensee in favour of respondent no.1 - HC set aside order passed by State Govt. - Further, DB set aside order of lower court and restored transfer of mining lease Hence, present appeal - Whether order of transfer passed by DB in favor of licensee is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, original licensee surrendered land prior to 16-4-1999 and remaining land was surrendered through its letter dt. 27-3-2001 and that in pursuance of said decision, it informed Director of Mines and Geology to determine lease either on expiry of twelve months or on any day earlier to that and in response to said desire expressed by original licensee, Director of Mines and Geology also responded by directing licensee to surrender lease book and mining plan and then subsequently also collected whatever arrears which were due and payable by licensee as on 31-1-2002. Hence, leasehold rights of licensee had come to end by 31-1-2002. Further, SC held that when licensee was not carrying its operation and desired to surrender its lease, there was no reason for State Govt. to wait for period of 12 months should lapse from 27-3-2001 for surrender to come into effect. Further, SC held that when once such surrender had come into effect, it must be stated that there was no scope for licensee to resile from said surrender and contend that it still had right to transact for any other purpose including for effecting any transfer in favour of anyone much less in favour of respondent no.1. Further, SC held that no specific provision in Rules that there was is mandatory requirement of delivery of possession as stipulated. Further, SC held that licensee committed serious violation in regard to compliance of s. 2 of Act at time of first renewal in year 1983/86 itself and in any event, by refusing to comply with conditions imposed in order dt. 24-12-1997, said violation would strike at very root of claim for transfer of dead lease. Hence, there was no scope at all for State Govt. to consider application made by licensee for transferring of its mining lease in favour of respondent no.1. Hence, order passed by DB in transferring lease is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Lease once surrender in favor of authority cannot be transferred to third party as original licensee fails to have rights in lease property.
