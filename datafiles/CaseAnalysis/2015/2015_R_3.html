R. Dineshkumar @ Deena v State Represented by Inspector of Police and others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 187; (2015) 7 SCC 497; AIR 2015 SC 1816; 2015 CRLJ 2362; JT 2015 (3) SC 197; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1361; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 188; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 297; 2015 (3) RLW 2431; 2015(3) SCALE 598; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 605

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pardon, Challenged, Prosecution Witness, Sea Customs Act, 1878, Denial, Same Transaction, Joint Trial, Indian Oaths Act, 1873, Seeking summon as additional accused

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319, 306, 307 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 132 - Seeking summon as additional accused - Denial - Challenged - Appellant accused along with other accused allegedly committed murder of deceased - Appellant filed petition before Trial Court seeking to summon respondent no.2 PW64 as additional accused to be tried together with rest of the accused - Petition was dismissed by Trial Court - Revision before HC - HC dismissed revision petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether Sessions Court is obliged to summon PW64 as an additional accused exercising the power u/s. 319 CrPC.

Held, proviso to s. 132 of the Act is facet of the rule against self incrimination and the same is statutory immunity against self incrimination which deserves the most liberal construction. Therefore, no prosecution can be launched against the maker of a statement falling within the sweep of s. 132 of the Act on the basis of the 'answer' given by a person while deposing as a 'witness before a Court. Thus, HC rightly refused to summon PW64 as an accused to be tried along with appellant and others. The HC recorded the conclusion that the examination of PW64 as prosecution witness without securing pardon u/s. 306 CrPC is illegal, if PW64 is a party to the conspiracy along with A2 and A3 without assigning any reason in support of such a conclusion. Thus, in view of the fact s. 307 CrPC authorizes even a Court conducting trial to tender pardon to such a person and ends of justice in instant case would be met by directing Trial Court to grant pardon in favour of PW64 after following appropriate procedure of law and record his evidence afresh. Appeal disposed of.
