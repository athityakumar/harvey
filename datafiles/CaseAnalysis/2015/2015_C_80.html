Coal Handlers Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise Range Kolkata- I

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 325; JT 2015 (7) SC 580; 2015 (4) MLJ 115; 2015(6) SCALE 58; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 860; 2015 (38) S.T.R. 897; [2015] 80 VST 465

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax

Summary: Service Tax - Finance Act, 1994, ss. 65(25), 65(48)(j), 65(19), 69 - Services as agent - Taxability - Appellants provide certain services as Agents under contracts signed with their respective Principals - Appellant filed application for registration, Certification of Registration was granted - Appellant field application surrendering Registration Certificate on ground that services rendered by them were not covered by s. 65(25) of the Act and same was rejected by Superintendent of Central Excise (SCE) - Appellant's appeal was also dismissed - Tribunal also dismissed appeal by impugned order - Hence, instant appeals - Whether services provided by appellants would label them as Clearing & Forwarding Agents making them liable to service tax in accordance with the Act.

Held, in order to qualify as a C & F Agent, such a person is to be found to be engaged in providing any service connected with 'clearing and forwarding operations'. It would essentially include getting the coal cleared as an agent on behalf of the principal from the supplier of the coal and thereafter dispatching/ forwarding the said coal to different destinations as per the instructions of the principal. None of the activities are performed by the appellant. There is no role of the appellant in getting the coal cleared from the collieries/ supplier of the coal. The goods are not under any legal detention from which they need to be freed by the appellant. The destination is the factories of the principal itself where the coal is to be delivered by the coal company as per pre-determined/agreed covenants between them. There is no occasion for companies to instruct the appellant to dispatch/forward the goods to a particular destination which is already fixed as per the contract. The appellant does not even undertake any loading operation. The primary job of the appellant, as per the contract, is of supervising and liaisoning with the coal company as well as the Railways to see that the material required is loaded as per the schedule. The services rendered by the appellant would not qualify as C & F Agent within the meaning of s. 65(25) of the Act. Impugned orders passed by the Tribunal are set aside by quashing the demand of service tax made from the appellants. Appeals allowed.
