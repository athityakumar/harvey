M. Narayan v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 998; 2015 (2) JCC 1396; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 226

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Suicide, Cruelty, Harassment, Marriage, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Version, Dead Body, Oath, Informant, Dowry, Matrimonial, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Valuable Security, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 304(B), 30 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2, 3, 4, 6 - Dowry Death - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly harassed and drove deceased/wife to commit suicide - Charge sheet was filed against appellant u/ss. 304B, 498A IPC and u/ss. 3, 4 and 6 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted accused of charges - On appeal, HC convicted accused u/ss. 498A and 304B IPC and u/ss. 3, 4 and 6 of the Act and was sentenced for 7 years of imprisonment for the offence u/s. 304B IPC, for the offence u/s. 498A IPC accused was sentenced to 3 years, 5 years imprisonment for the offence u/s. 3 of the Act and 6 months imprisonment for the offence u/s. 4 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, prosecution was able to prove the culpability of appellant vis-a-vis the charges beyond any reasonable doubt. The evidence of prosecution witnesses on that regard was evidently coherent, consistent and compact. The materials on record though indicate that deceased had lost her mother at a young age and was denied the love and affection of her father who married for the second time and was also a little sensitive and self-centered, there was nothing supervening to suggest that she did suffer from any mental imbalance or eccentricity so as to probabilise any act of self-elimination without any compelling reason. On the other hand, the evidence on record demonstrates in emphatic terms that she had been complaining of appellant's persistent and hurtful demand for dowry by way of an additional amount of Rs.50,000/- and her pitiable condition, being subjected to continuous and ruthless harassment and ill-treatment resulting in severe physical and mental torture. The gravamen of testimony of PWs 2, 3 and 10 bearing on the essential facts constituting the ingredients of offences with which appellant was charged has remained unshaken in their cross-examination. Minor and stray inconsistencies in their narration, does not destroy the substratum of their version which otherwise do wholly furnish the required materials to constitute pre-requisites for the offences u/ss. 304B, 498A IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act. The view adopted by Trial Court having regard to the gamut of evidence adduced by prosecution was not a possible one. SC affirm the conviction and sentence against accused as recorded by HC. Appeal dismissed.
