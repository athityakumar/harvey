Kerala Bar Hotels Association and another v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

29 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 914; AIR 2016 SC 163; 2016(2) ALL MR 481; 2016 (2) AWC 1226; 2016 (2) CTC 192; JT 2015 (12) SC 626; 2016 (1) KLT 134; 2016 (1) MLJ 202; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 681; 2016(1) SCALE 70; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Equal Protection, Privileges, Punjab Excise Act, 1914, Abkari Act, 1077, Foreign Liquor Rules, American Constitution, Irish Constitution, Prohibition Act, 1950, Liquor Licence (Second Amendment) Rules, Abkari Rules

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14,19(1)(g),47 - Abkari Act, 1077, s. 15(c) - Abkari Rules, r. 13(3) - Prohibition of liquor - Permission to Five Star hotels - Petitioners, which include, Two Star, Three Star, Four Star and Heritage hotels, challenged Abkari Policy for year 2014-15, and also amendments to Foreign Liquor Rules before HC - Single Judge of HC held that policy was violative of art.14 of Constitution and struck down policy but carved out exception vis-à-vis eligibility of Four Star and Heritage category hotels to grant of FL-3 license, finding their exclusion to be arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Aggrieved, both Hotels and State preferred appeals before HC - DB of HC reversed findings of Singe Judge and observed that the impugned policy is in consonance with art. 47 of Constitution that provides that the State has to regard the raising of nutrition and the standard of living of its people and the improvement of public health as among its primary duties, in particular endeavoring to bring about prohibition and dismissed appeals filed by Two Star, Three Star and unclassified hotels and allowed appeal filed by State and consequently, only Five Star Hotels in State are permitted to serve alcohol in their bars - Hence, instant appeals by petitioners - Whether Abkari policy is arbitrary and violative of provisions of Constitution.

Held, least amount of sale of alcohol occurs in Five Star hotels, which sale includes guest orders in room-service. There is also little scope for cavil as the guests in Five Star hotels are usually of a mature age and they do not visit hotels with the sole purpose of consuming alcohol. Further, prices of alcohol in Five Star Hotels is usually prohibitively high, and the same acts as deterrent to individuals going in for binge or even causal drinking. Hence, there is no arbitrariness either in classification or in the unique treatment given by State to hotels possessing Five Star rating. Further, there is no illegality or irrationality with the intention of the State to clamp down on public consumption of alcohol and also State duly considered recommendations of One Man Commission Report and incorporated a number of them. However, as it is only obligation on State to consider the Report and not incorporate in its entirety, no legal requirement was transgressed and so, policy could not be written off as arbitrary or procedurally unsound. Further, business in potable liquor is in the nature of res extra commercium and as such is subject to more stringent restrictions than any other trade or business. Hence, while the ground of art.19(1)(g) could be raised, still, it could not be said that the qualification on that right is unreasonable. Further, as r. 13(3) postulates that special measures for promotion of tourisms could be ordained by the State, it could not be said that Rule violates s.15C of the Act. Hence, impugned judgment of DB of HC is upheld. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - The Court could only interfere if the policy framed is absolutely capricious or not informed by reasons or totally arbitrary and founded ipse dixit offending the basic requirement of art.14 of Constitution. Further, art. 47 of Constitution places responsibility on every State Govt. to at least contain if not curtail consumption of alcohol and how the policy for the same is to be implemented, modified, adapted or restructured is the province of the State Govt. and not of the judiciary.
