Sanjaysinh Ramrao Chavan v Dattatray Gulabrao Phalke and others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 30; (2015) 3 SCC 123; 2015 CRLJ 1259; 2015 (2) JCC 930; JT 2015 (1) SC 365; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 822; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 308; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 570; 2015(1) SCALE 457; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 130

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Judicial Notice

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 12 and 13 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 190 and 204 - Cognizance of offence - Jurisdiction of HC - Complaint was filed by respondent No. 1 against appellant stating that the he had to pay an amount of Rs.75,000/ by way of bribe for getting a certificate for non-agricultural use of his land - Magistrate, having seen the records and having heard the parties, held that no offence was made out against the appellant under the provisions of the Act so as to prosecute him - On appeal, HC directed the Director General of Police to forward the request for sanction for prosecution to the competent authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in setting aside the Magistrate's order accepting closure report submitted by the police. .

Held, cognizance is a process where the court takes judicial notice of an offence so as to initiate proceedings in respect of the alleged violation of law. The whole purpose of taking cognizance of an offence u/s. 190(1)(b) CrPC is to commence proceedings under Chap. 16 of the CrPC by issuing process u/s. 204 CrPC to the accused involved in the case. Once the legal requirements to constitute the alleged offence qua one of the accused are lacking, there is no point in taking cognizance and proceeding further as against him. Further, once the prosecution is of the view that no case is made out so as to prosecute an accused, unless the court finds otherwise, there is no point in making a request for sanction for prosecution. If the prosecution is simply vexatious, sanction for prosecution is not to be granted. That is one of the main considerations to be borne in mind by the competent authority while considering whether the sanction is to be granted or not. In the instant case, HC exceeded in its jurisdiction in substituting its views and that too without any legal basis. The impugned order is hence set aside. Appeal allowed.
