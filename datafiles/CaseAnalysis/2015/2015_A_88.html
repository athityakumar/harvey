State of Madhya Pradesh v Ashok and others etc.

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 467; 2015 (3) CGLJ 456; 2015 CRLJ 3973; 2015 (2) JabLJ 433; 2015 (1) JCC 2228; JT 2015 (6) SC 23; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 740; 2015(7) SCALE 324; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 1073

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Enmity

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Victim while coming from his duty was burnt alive by accused by pouring petrol - Further, victim disclosed to PWs 4, 15 that he was set afire in by accused persons - Dying Declaration was recorded by Executive Magistrate and victim succumbed to injuries -Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 302, 148 of IPC and respondent was found guilty u/ss. 149, 302 of IPC - However, HC found that case of prosecution was fully established as against two accused, however, benefit of doubt was given to respondents on premise that they had reached spot after commission of offence and as such charge of formation of unlawful assembly by them was not established - Hence, present appeal.

Held, statement leading to registration of crime and statement recorded by Executive Magistrate are dying declarations by deceased victim. Both these statements are consistent and name present respondents and state role played by them in surrounding deceased and giving cries that he be beaten and should not be left. Hence, it is impossible to accept that these respondents arrived at scene of occurrence after crime was completed. Their role is that of participants in crime who did not allow deceased to escape by encircling him. SC held that finding rendered by HC is against record. Further, both statements clearly referred to presence of PW13. It was PW13 who immediately ran home and intimated fact that deceased was set afire, to inmates of house. Consequently PWs 4, 15 arrived at scene of occurrence. Further, in his testimony PW13 stated that while deceased was burning, respondent threw burning tyre upon him and original accused threw sword at him. Post mortem clearly shows incised injury in back suffered by deceased, which completely supports such assertion. SC accepted presence of said PW13 completely established and accept him to be eye witness to occurrence and HC has also not disbelieved testimony of PW13. Further, even if one of them was ready with sword, that is clearly indicative of level of preparedness on their part and that they can be said to be members of unlawful assembly. It was crime which was committed by all of them guided by same purpose, acting in concert achieving result that was desired. Intent of entire assembly was clear, eloquently established by their presence, preparedness and participation. Hence, acquittal order passed by HC is set aside and conviction order passed by Trial Court is restored. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused based on statement of witness and dying declaration is justified and in accordance with law.
