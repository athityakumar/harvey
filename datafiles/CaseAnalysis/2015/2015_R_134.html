Royal Medical Trust (Registered) and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Anil R. Dave, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 638; (2015) 10 SCC 19; AIR 2015 SC 3300; JT 2015 (8) SC 14; 2015(9) SCALE 68; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College, Medical Council Of India, Dentists Act, 1948, Dental Council of India (Establishment of New Dental Colleges, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity in Dental Colleges) Regulations, 2006

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 10A - Approval for college - Application filed after due date - Determination - Petitioners are colleges which filed applications for seeking permission for establishment of new colleges, colleges seeking increase in intake of seats for admissions of students to first year and Colleges seeking renewal of permission, who have already received permission in previous years either for establishing new Medical College or for increasing intake capacity of existing Medical College - However, after conducting inspection of respective Medical Colleges, respondent Medical Council of India (MCI) found infirmities or inadequacies in infrastructure, facilities and faculty - Further, applicants claimed that they had rectified shortcomings and asked for compliance verification, however, Central Govt. and/or MCI refused to undertake any fresh inspection for verification, for want of adequate time - In few cases HC directed respondent to conduct fresh inspection, however in few cases HC failed to grant relief to petitioner - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Scheme u/s. 10A of Act may itself contemplate stage wise achievement of annual targets and requirements of reasonable opportunity to be afforded not only at initial stage but also in cases of subsequent renewal and further that opportunity must be afforded at both stages namely by MCI and by Central Govt., Schedule under Regulations must accommodate and provide for adequate time limits to take care of such eventualities. Schedule which was brought in force by way of amendment dt. 21-9-2012 does not provide for such stage wise consideration. It simply gives four stages without indicating any time limits to ensure grant of such reasonable opportunity in case decisions of disapproval are taken against applicants. It also does not speak of any compliance verification. SC held that inspections were undertaken in and around April/May 2014 and letters of disapproval were sent by Central Govt. on or about 15th July2014. Though compliance was reported, no verification in that behalf was undertaken. SC held that Central Govt. is statutorily empowered to modify Schedule in respect of class or category of applicants, for reasons to be recorded in writing, however, since deadline for making admissions was over and there was no formal permission to establish new Medical Colleges or to increase intake capacity in respect of existing Colleges, applicants in Categories I and II were not considered fit for grant of any interim relief. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. MCI and Central Govt. are directed to discharge their functions in accord with concerned Regulations and Statute and in keeping with observations made hereinabove. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - Application filed after due date for approvals from college authority cannot be entertained and same is liable to be set aside.
