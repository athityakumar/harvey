State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Anand Mohan and another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 479; (2015) 9 SCC 35; 2015 CRLJ 3968; JT 2015 (6) SC 212; 2015 (5) MLJ 721; 2015(7) SCALE 648; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 352; 2015 (5) SLR 126

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Employed, Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013, Madhya Pradesh Works (Allotment) Rules

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 201, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Madhya Pradesh Works (Allotment) Rules - Criminal Conspiracy - Competent Authority to grant sanction - Determination - Respondent no.1 was Executive Engineer, and respondent no.2 was Assistant Engineer with Bhopal Development Authority (BDA) and said authority for construction work for which tenders were invited and work order was given in favour of one party - Further, construction was completed and given to Board - It is alleged that respondents, in connivance with other accused, entered into criminal conspiracy in connection with construction work, and got prepared forged note-sheet, pursuant to which excess payment was paid to contractor- Crime was registered in respect of offences punishable u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120B and 201 of IPC, and u/s.13 (1) (d) read with s. 13 (2) of Act against respondents and other accused - Further, authority sought previous sanction necessary for prosecution of respondents from Administrative Department of State Govt., however same was declined - However, appellant no. 2, Secretary, Department of Law and Legislative Affairs, State Govt. granted necessary sanction to prosecute respondents - HC held that appellant No. 2, i.e. Secretary, Department of Law and Legislative Affairs was not Competent Authority to grant sanction - Whether authority in question was competent authority to grant sanction to prosecute respondents.

Held, order dt. 3-2-1988 published in Official Gazette, whereby Madhya Pradesh Works (Allotment) Rules were amended and Chief Minister of Madhya Pradesh vide order dt. 8-2-1988 delegated power to grant sanction for prosecution of public servants to Law Secretary of Madhya Pradesh Law Department, it is evident that power to grant sanction for prosecution, already existed with Department of Law and Legislative Affairs, since February, 1988. Circular letter dt. 28-2-1998 does not confer any new power and it only clarifies that Department of Law and Justice is competent authority not only in respect of investigations made by Lokayukta Organization, but also State Economic Offences Investigation Wing. Power with appellant No.2 to grant sanction is, in fact, conferred by rule as amended vide notification dt. 3-2-1988 published in Official Gazette. After such amendment in rule whereby power to grant sanction was delegated to Department of Law and Justice, it cannot said that Administrative Department had power to decline sanction as it has done vide its order dt. 10-7-1997. Further, from sanction granted by Law Department, it is evident that authority has examined material on record before granting sanction. Hence, SC held that HC has erred in law in allowing petition filed by respondents seeking quashing of sanction granted by appellant no.2, Secretary, Department of Law and Legislative Affairs, State Govt. SC found no infirmity as to competence of appellant No.2 to grant sanction. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Power granted to authority to grant sanction cannot be challenged if same is provided by legislature.
