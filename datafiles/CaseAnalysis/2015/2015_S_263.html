Vijay Shankar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 727; AIR 2015 SC 3696; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4950; 2015 CRLJ 4774; 2015 (1) JCC 2444; JT 2015 (8) SC 216; 2015(8) SCALE 517; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Liquor, Panchayat, Clothes, Distance, Improbable

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 449 - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Appellant accused allegedly assaulted deceased and inflicted injuries - Based on statement of deceased's brother, PW 10, FIR was lodged against appellant - Trial Court held that prosecution has established guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt and convicted appellant u/ss. 302, 449 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment and imposed fine - Further, conviction order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, with respect to motive of appellant, SC held that allegation of previous enmity between appellant and deceased family has not been proved. Excepting PW10, no other independent witness was examined to prove that deceased had rebuked appellant and serious doubts arise as to motive suggested and alleged previous enmity. Hence, in absence of convincing evidence as to motive makes court to be circumspect in matter of assessment of evidence and this aspect was not kept in view by HC and Trial Court. Further, with respect to last seen theory, SC held that Trial Court and HC based conviction of appellant mainly on last seen theory relying on evidence of PW 11 who allegedly saw appellant on night of incident saw appellant coming out of room of deceased. However, SC held that considering evidence of PW11 and improbabilities, evidence of PW11 neither inspires confidence nor does it lead to conclusion that appellant was last seen with deceased. Further, it is quite improbable that farm servants who were sleeping in adjoining room where deceased was sleeping never heard noise and that none of servants came to rescue of deceased which raises serious doubts about prosecution case. Further, with respect to extra judicial confession made by appellant accused to PW 12, SC held that when PW 12 was only member of Gram Panchayat and not person of influence with police, it is doubtful that appellant accused had approached him making extra-judicial confession and requested him to save him. Further, SC held that PW 12 cannot be said to be independent witness. Further, SC held that extra-judicial confession made to PW 12 is highly doubtful, hence, much weight cannot be attached to alleged recovery of bloodstained clothes and knife. Hence, SC held that circumstances relied upon by prosecution are not established by convincing evidence and they do not form complete chain pointing to guilt of appellant. Hence, conviction recorded by courts below is not supported by credible evidence and prosecution has failed to establish guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt and benefit of doubt is to be given to appellant. Impugned order passed by HC and Trial court is set aside and appellant is acquitted of charges. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Conviction order passed against accused cannot be sustained when prosecution has failed to prove guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
