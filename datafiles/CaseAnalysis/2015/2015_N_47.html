Quantum Securities Private Limited and others v New Delhi Television Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 441; (2015) 10 SCC 602; AIR 2015 SC 3699; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 124; 2015 (112) ALR 506; 2015 (5) ALT(SC) 56; 2015 (4) AWC 4112; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 63; JT 2015 (6) SC 115; 2016 (3) MahLJ 668; 2015 (5) MLJ 479; 2016 (2) MPLJ 538; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 793; 2015 (129) RD 553; 2015(7) SCALE 329; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 140; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 47

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 3A - Contempt proceedings - Disposal of main suit - Respondent filed civil suit before HC against appellants and inter alia prayed HC to restrain appellants from writing defamatory letters against respondent, and also sought appellants to issue unconditional corrigendum apart from payment of damages - Single Judge of HC granted ex parte ad-interim relief vide order dt.6-8-2013 permanently restraining appellants from writing defamatory letters - Later, respondent filed contempt petition aggrieved by alleged communication made by or/and on behalf of appellants in violation of said interim order and Single Judge issued notice to appellants vide order dt.26-2-2014 - However, during pendency of said contempt petition, respondent filed additional affidavit alleging fresh contempt by appellants of interim order and again notice was issued to appellants vide order dt.31-10-2014 - Aggrieved by said two notices dt.26-2-2014 and 31-10-2014, instant appeals were filed.

Held, there is no justification on part of parties to keep main Notice of Motion pending and prosecute its off-shoot proceedings in preference to main case. Further, when admittedly order dt.6-8-2013 was an ex parte one then in such circumstances, no sooner the appellants entered appearance in civil suit and filed their pleadings in reply to Notice of Motion, then Court should have disposed of Notice of Motion on merits in light of mandate contained in O.39 r. 3A of CPC. However, Court had not disposed of Notice of Motion despite appellants entering appearance as back as 21-8-2013 in main suit and completed their pleading on 5-9-2013 and because of which, ex parte ad-interim order dt.6-8-2013 remained in operation. Further, it is in interest of parties to the Lis to get main case decided first on its merits as far as possible rather than to pursue their off-shoot proceedings on merits by keeping the main case undecided and more so, when decision in main case would have bearing over pending off-shoot proceedings. Furthermore, when issue on merits is seized of by original court in civil suit and rights of the parties are still not decided on merits then it is not proper for SC to probe into the facts and record any finding on any of the issues arising out of collateral proceedings such as the instant one else observations made by instant Court would prejudice to parties while prosecuting their case before original court on merits. Hence, in interest of both parties, recording of findings on various issues arising including on legal issues are deferred and Single Judge of HC is requested to dispose of Civil Suit and Notice of Motion on merits and until the same, contempt proceedings are stayed and after disposal of Notice of Motion, contempt proceedings could be decided in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per O. 39 r. 3A of CPC, Court should make endeavor to finally dispose of application within 30 days from the date on which the ex parte injunction was granted.
