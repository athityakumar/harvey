Devi Multiplex and others v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 357; (2015) 9 SCC 132; AIR 2015 SC 2348; JT 2015 (5) SC 19; 2015 (5) MLJ 115; 2015(6) SCALE 317; 2016(6) SCALE 317; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 1; [2016] 94 VST 95

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Gujarat Entertainments Tax Act, 1977

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Scheme for promotion of industry - Extension of time - Refusal to grant certificate - State Govt. had lodged scheme to provide incentives and concessions to industries for promoting and development of tourism industry - Appellant applied for scheme and completed various procedure - However, due to earthquake and communal riot, appellant was unable to commence operation, however, appellant was granted extension to complete its projects - Authority refused to grant eligibility certificate to appellant due to delay in commencement of operation - HC affirmed order passed by authority, hence, incentives and concessions were not granted to appellant - Whether appellant was eligible for grant of eligibility certificate.

Held, scheme promised various incentives on fulfillment of various conditions by industries. Further, it also promised initial period of two years for going operational in first instance, extendable by further period of two years subject to satisfactory progress to be found by State Level Committee. Even thereafter, Unit could still approach State Govt. for further extension. This was part of core of Scheme, which invited investment in tourism units promising tax holiday as stated above. Based on such representation, various units including that of appellants having come forward and altered their position, State Govt. would certainly be bound by principles of Promissory Estoppel. State Govt. was thus estopped from going back on promise so made in Scheme and could not have curtailed period and opportunity specifically made available within which project could be completed so as to avail benefits under Scheme.

Hence, appellants were entitled to have full benefit and advantage of Scheme and curtailment of period and opportunity available of Scheme by subsequent order was bad and ineffective. Further, progress of appellant's project was affected by earthquake and riot in State and extension sort by appellant was supported by relevant documents and relevant certificate from Chartered Accountant. Hence, order passed by HC holding in so far as it held that operative period of Scheme came to end on 30-11-2000 and that there could be no further extension of time limit is set aside. Appeal allowed.
