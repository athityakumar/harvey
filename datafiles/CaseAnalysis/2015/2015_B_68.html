Yellapu Uma Maheswari and another v Buddha Jagadheeswararao and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 680; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 40; 2015 (113) ALR 769; 2015 (6) AWC 6019; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 178; JT 2015 (12) SC 592; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3768; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 765; 2016 (130) RD 479; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1877, Registration Act, 1866, Registration Act, 1871

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, ss.17, 49 - Admissibility of unregistered documents in evidence - Appellant no. 1 filed chief examination affidavit and sought to mark Exhibits in suit filed against him and others by respondent no. 1 for relief of partition claiming 1/4th share in suit schedule properties - However, respondent no. 1 raised objection with regard to admissibility of 2 exhibits, i.e., Deed of Memorandum dt.5-6-1975, witnessing earlier partition effected between respondent no. 1 and appellant no. 1 (Exhibit B-21) and agreement dt.4-6-1975 entered by respondent no.1, appellant no.1 and Later Mahalakshmamma (Exhibit B-22) - Both Trial Court and HC upheld objection raised by respondent no. 1 on ground that both documents require stamp duty and as said documents are unregistered and unstamped documents, they were not admissible in evidence - Further, Trial Court also gave specific finding that said exhibits are not admissible for collateral purpose also - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Exhibits B21 and B22 are not admissible in evidence as they are unregistered documents.

Held, in both Exhibits B-21 and B-22, there was relinquishment of right in respect of immovable property through a document which is compulsorily registerable document and if the same is not registered, becomes an inadmissible document as envisaged u/s.49 of the Act. Hence, Exhibits B-21 and B-22 fall within the ambit of s.17(i)(b) of the Act and so, are compulsorily registerable documents and the same are inadmissible in evidence for the propose of proving the factum of partition between the parties. However, said documents could be used for collateral purpose and hence, if appellants want to mark them for collateral purpose, then they could do the same on payment of stamp duty along with penalty and Trial Court could mark said documents for collateral propose subject to proof and relevance. Hence, appeal partly allowed by holding that Exhibits B-21 and B-22 are admissible in evidence for collateral purpose subject to payment of stamp duty, penalty, proof and relevancy. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - S.17(1)(b) of the Act, mandates that any document which has the effect of creating and taking away the rights in respect of an immovable property should be registered and s. 49 of the Act imposes bar on the admissibility of an unregistered document and deals with the documents that are required to be registered u/s. 17 of the Act.
