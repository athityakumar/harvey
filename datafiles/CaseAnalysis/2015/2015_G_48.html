Commissioner of Central Excise, Bhavnagar v Gujarat Maritime Board, Jafrabad

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 528; JT 2015 (6) SC 423; 2015 (6) MLJ 630; 2015(8) SCALE 127; 2016(8) SCALE 127; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 187; 2015 (39) S.T.R. 529; [2015] 83 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service Tax

Keywords: Gujarat Maritime Board Act, 1981

Summary: Service Tax - Indirect Tax - Gujarat Maritime Board Act, 1981, s. 35 - Service Rendered - Payment of Service Tax by assessee - Determination - Respondent Board entered into agreement with company whereby licence was granted to company to construct and use jetty for landing of goods - Further, service tax was payable on wharfage charges by respondent collected by them from their licensee i.e. company under taxable category of port services - Revenue authorities initiated investigation against for respondent under-valuation and short payment of service tax and show cause notice was issued for payment of service tax - Commissioner held that wharfage, is squarely covered under head port services and amount of rebate/concession granted in wharfage charges amounting to 80% allowed to licensee should be included for purposes of calculation of service tax. Further, amount that was demanded on account of lease rent for waterfront usage was also confirmed, together with interest and penalty, which was imposed on assessee - However, Tribunal reversed order passed by Commissioner holding that no service at all was rendered by respondent Board in relation to any vessel, hence, no amount was payable by way of service tax - Further, 80% of charges levied not being of nature of payment for services rendered would equally render payment bad in law - Whether any service is rendered by respondent Board or by any person authorized by Board in relation to vessel or goods.

Held, agreement makes it clear that it is duty of licensee, i.e. company to maintain jetty in good order and condition during tenure of agreement. Further, it is company that is to provide all services at or around jetty including dredging, navigation, water supply etc. Hence, it clear that during currency of agreement it is not respondent Board but Licensee i.e. company who keeps said jetty in such condition that it is capable of enabling vessels to berth alongside it to load and unload goods. Hence, no service is rendered by respondent Board to licencee company under agreement. Further, rebate in wharfage charges of 80% is condition imposed statutorily u/s. 35 of Act and to say that it is in nature of lease rent or licence fee, would not be correct inasmuch as separate licence fee is payable under agreement. Further, jetty is primarily meant for loading and unloading goods belonging to particular private party that repair and maintenance expenses are to be borne by private party and not by respondent Board, hence, there is no service, rendered by respondent Board to company licensee. Further, on reading of agreement that it is respondent Board itself that charges or recovers wharfage charges from licensee company and does not authorize company to recover such charges from other persons. Hence, no service is rendered by port or by any person authorized by such port, hence, very first condition for levy of service tax is absent on facts of present case. Further with respect to direct berthing facilities provided for captive cargo is concerned, lease rent charged for use of waterfront also does not include any service in relation to vessel or goods and cannot be described as 'port service'. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Assessee is not liable to pay service tax when no service is provided by assessee to other party.
