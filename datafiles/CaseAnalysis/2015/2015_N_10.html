Sai Kripa Mangal Karyalaya and others v Nagpur Municipal Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 91; 2016(1) ALL MR 501; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 549; JT 2015 (2) SC 360; 2015 (2) MLJ 494; 2015(2) SCALE 313; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 1082; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948, Nagpur Improvement Trust Act, Illegal structures, Sanctioned layout

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, s. 2(15) - Illegal structures - Sanctioned layout - Legality - Appellant filed instant appeal against impugned judgment of HC in a petition filed by respondent nos. 5 (member of respondent no. 4 Public Trust) and respondent no. 6, member of locality, in public interest, alleging mismanagement by the Trust in leasing lands to appellants - HC held that building permits granted by respondent (Corporation) to the land belonging to Trust are unauthorized and illegal and buildings put up pursuant to such building permits are liable to be demolished - Appellant contended that a personal grudge of respondent no. 5 was clear as he was claiming to be a member of Trust which was not accepted by the Trust and that petitioners having not approached the Court with clean hands and it being a private dispute between respondent no. 5 and Trust the said writ petition was not maintainable.

Held, fact clearly shows that petition filed by respondent nos. 5 and 6 was not bona fide but it was filed only on account of personal disputes between the parties i.e. inter se between the members of the respondent no.4 (Trust) indirectly affecting the appellants (tenants). Trust in its reply before the High HC supported the appellants but before SC their counsel supported the case of respondent nos. 5 and 6. In absence of original coloured plan the true copy of the plan cannot be relied upon to hold any construction illegal in view of the fact that the Corporation which sanctioned building plan supposed to have gone through the original plan. In absence of the sanctioned plan, HC was not justified in deciding the disputed question of fact as to whether the building was constructed in accordance with Town Planning Scheme. The HC ought to have looked into the sanctioned plan to find out whether it was for office (Karyalaya) or for residential or for commercial purpose for coming to a definite conclusion. Further, there being a long delay of 8 years in filing the writ petition and in absence of challenge to the plan sanctioned by Corporation, the HC was not justified in entertaining the so called PIL filed by persons who had personal dispute with Trust. Impugned judgment passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
