Praveenbhai S. Khambhayata v United India Insurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 112; 2015 (2) ACC 48; 2015 ACJ 936; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 17; 2015 (2) AWC 1482; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 518; 2015 (145) FLR 66; 2015 (2) G.L.R. 1127; JT 2015 (2) SC 458; 2015 (2) LLJ 57; 2015 (1) OLR 731; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 114; 2015 (2) RLW 1488; 2015(2) SCALE 481; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Entitled to, Motor Accident Claim

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Insurance - Employee's [Workmen's] Compensation Act, 1923, ss. 4A (3)(a), 4A (3)(b) - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Entitled to - Deceased met with accident and succumbed to injuries - Respondents 2 to 4 claimants were parents and wife of deceased - Respondents filed claim petition before Commissioner - Commissioner held that appellant owner of the vehicle and respondent no. 5 was liable to pay the compensation - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, as per s. 4A(3)(a) of the Act, where any employer commits default in paying compensation due under Act within one month from date it fell due, Commissioner shall direct employer to pay simple interest not exceeding maximum of lending rates of any scheduled bank as may be specified by Central Govt. As per s. 4A (3)(b), in addition to the amount of arrears and interest thereon, Commissioner shall direct employer to pay further sum not exceeding 50% of such amount by way of penalty. Claimants are entitled to statutory interest and penalty not exceeding 50% of the amount of compensation. Commissioner for Workmen's Compensation has awarded only 6% interest and 10% penalty as against the statutory entitlement of the dependents of the deceased employee in terms of s. 4A(3) of the Act. Regard to the passage of time and in interest of justice, in our considered view, statutory rate of penalty i.e. 15% is to be ordered in addition to statutory interest - Appellant has deposited Rs.3,25,365/- i.e. principal amount with Commissioner on 18-2-2014. The matter was listed before SC Lok Adalat on 6-12-2014 wherein appellant was directed to deposit balance amount. Respondent no. 1 insurance company shall deposit balance compensation being 15% penalty and interest. On such deposit, same shall be disbursed to respondent's no. 2 to 4. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
