Munna Lal Jain and another v Vipin Kumar Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Anil R. Dave, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 376; (2015) 6 SCC 347; 2015 (2) ACC 806; 2015 ACJ 1985; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 114; 2015(4) ALL MR 436; 2015 (4) AWC 3845; 2015 (4) Bom.C.R. 72; 2015 (3) CGLJ 93; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 203; JT 2015 (5) SC 1; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2526; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 447; 2015 (3) RLW 2021; 2015(6) SCALE 522; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 207; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 113

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Accident - Death - Enhancement of Compensation - Appellants son aged 30 years died in motor accident and was self - employed as Pandit - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.6,59,000.00 including loss of dependency to tune of Rs.6,24,000.00 with interest @ 7.5 per cent from date of institution of petition - Further, HC enhanced compensation and fixed it at Rs.12,61,800 with interest as ordered by Tribunal - However, dissatisfied with amount awarded by HC, appellant filed present appeal - Whether appellant is entitled to enhanced compensation -

Held, in present case there is no exceptional circumstances or compelling reasons for deviation on basis of evidence and therefore deduction of 50% towards personal and living expenses is not to be disturbed. Further, multiplier, in case of age of deceased between 26 to 30 years is 17. There is no dispute or grievance on fixation of monthly income as Rs. 12,000 by HC, hence, appellants are entitled to compensation of Rs.18,36,000 towards loss of dependency. Further, there shall be no change on amounts awarded by HC on other heads or on rate of interest. Order passed by HC is modified and amount of compensation is enhanced by SC. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - There is no straight jacket formula for grant of compensation in case of death in accidents and amount of compensation depends on facts and circumstances of each case.
