Rajni Kant Ojha v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 711; 2015(10) SCALE 780; 2016 (2) SLR 488

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Inter Se Seniority, Surplus, Gradation List, Supply Inspector Cadre Recruitment Rules, 1993

Summary: Service - Supply Inspector Cadre Recruitment Rules, 1993, r. 18 - Determination of seniority - Merit List - Appellants and private respondents were appointed to their respective departments, through common written test conducted on 1-3-1982 by BSSSB - However, when private respondents were declared surplus from post of Assistant Consolidation Officers in Revenue and Land Reforms determent, they were temporarily accommodated as Supply Inspectors in Food, Civil and Commerce Department - Later, private respondents approached HC claiming seniority on basis of merit determined by BSSSB in common written test - HC allowed petition and so, appellants filed appeals before DB of HC but the same were declined and hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in arriving at conclusion that private respondents were entitled to determination of their seniority in the Food, Supply and Commerce Department, on basis of their merit, in the process of selection held in furtherance of written test conducted on 1-3-1982.

Held, even though originally appellants and private respondents were direct recruits from common process of selection, yet, they were engaged in different cadres of different departments. After the appointment of private respondents as Assistant Consolidation Officers, in the Revenue and Land Reforms Department, they ceased to have any nexus with the Supply Inspectors appointed to the food Supply and Commerce department. Having been so appointed, there remained no connectivity between the two, insofar as the issue of seniority is concerned. Hence, private respondents could not claim any benefit for determination of their seniority under 1993 Rules. Further, condition expressed in letters of appointment of private respondents when they temporary accommodated in Food, Supply and Commerce department that their seniority would be determined from date of their joining the posts of Supply Inspectors was never assailed by private respondents and so, same is binding on them. Hence, seniority of private respondents in Food, Supply and Commerce Department would be regulated on the basis of their appointment in the said department and not on the basis of their merit, determined by BSSSB, at the time of their selection. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned orders of HC are set aside. Further, State Govt. is directed to re-determine seniority of appellants and private respondents based on date of joining on post of Supply Inspectors. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - R. 18 of 1993 Rules could be invoked only if rival parties were direct recruits to the same cadre in the same department.
