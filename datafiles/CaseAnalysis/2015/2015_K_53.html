Lisamma Antony and another v Karthiyayani and another

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 386; 2015(4) ALL MR 979; 2015 (3) AWC 2704; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2121; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 575; 2015(3) SCALE 836; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Framing of substantial question of law - Remand of matter to lower court - Justification - Plaintiff stated that she and her two children were absolute owners of property, which they purchased vide Sale Deed and are in possession of property - Further, it is stated that defendant held tenancy right of previous owners with respect to certain portion of land and defendants were in possession of said part - Further, boundary between property in question and that of defendants is well demarcated, however, on date of incident defendants attempted to demolish existing fence - Trial Court held that property in suit is owned by plaintiffs and restrained defendants by decree of permanent injunction from trespassing in land into property in question - Order passed by Trial Court is affirmed by appellant court - However, HC disposed of appeal filed by defendant and framed substantial question of law with respect to document which relates to identity of property claimed by defendants and remanded matter to Trial Court - Hence, present appeal.

Held, question formulated by HC cannot be termed to be question of law, much less substantial question of law and it is nothing but question of fact. Merely for reason that on appreciation of evidence another view could have been taken, it cannot be said that HC can assume jurisdiction by terming such question as substantial question of law. Further, HC simply re-appreciated evidence on record and allowed second appeal and remanded matter to Trial Court. SC held that suit was not disposed of on any preliminary issue by Trial Court and second appellate court should have restrained itself from remanding case to Trial Court. Remanding case for re-appreciation of evidence and fresh decision is harassment of litigant and unnecessary delay in final disposal of lis, shakes faith of litigants in court. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and judgment and decree as modified by first appellate court shall stand affirmed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to remand matter to lower Court when there is no substantial question of law on appreciation of evidence by higher court.
