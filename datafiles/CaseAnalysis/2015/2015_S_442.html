Mehboob Ali and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 733; 2016 (1) JCC 216; JT 2015 (9) SC 512; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3989; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 377; 2015(12) SCALE 67; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Confessional Statement, Memos, Dealing, Discovery, Admissible

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26, 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 489B, 489C - Confessions Inadmissible - Discovery of facts - Determination - FIR was registered against accused persons on ground that they were engaged in dealing with fake currency notes - Further, due to identification of other accused persons and confessions of appellant, various accused persons were arrested - Trial Court convicted appellant accused no.1 for commission of offence u/s. 489C for 3 years' RI, for s. 489B read with s. 120B IPC of IPC five years' RI and fine of Rs.1000/- each; in default to further undergo one month simple imprisonment - Further, other appellant accused were convicted and sentenced u/s. 489B read with s. 120B IPC for 5 years' RI and fine of Rs.1,000/-; in default to suffer one month simple imprisonment - Conviction order passed by Trial Court is affirmed by HC - Appellant accused nos. 2 and 3 contended that confessional statement of accused recorded u/s. 27 of Act was not admissible as there is no recovery of currency notes from their possession - Hence, present appeal.

Held, on basis of information furnished by accused no.2 and 3, accused no.5 was arrested. Further, fact that accused no. 3 was dealing with forged currency notes was not to knowledge of police. SC held that statement of both accused has led to discovery of fact and arrest of co-accused not known to police. They identified him and ultimately statements have led to unearthing racket of use of fake currency notes. Hence, information furnished by accused no. 2 and 3 vide information memos is clearly admissible which has led to identification and arrest of accused no. 5. SC held that statement of accused persons has led to discovery of fact proving complicity of other accused persons and entire chain of circumstances clearly makes out that accused acted in conspiracy as found by Trial court and HC . Hence, statements of accused persons leading to discovery of fact are admissible as per provisions contained in s. 27 of Act which carves out exception to general provisions about inadmissibility of confession made under police custody contained in ss. 25 and 26 of Act. Hence, judgment passed by Trial Court and HC is confirmed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Confessions made by accused persons are admissible as per s. 27 of Act when same leads to discovery of new facts and arrest of co-accused.
