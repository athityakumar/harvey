State of Himachal Pradesh and others v Ashwani Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 796; 2015 (6) AWC 6224; JT 2015 (11) SC 230; 2016 (1) MLJ 223; 2016 (133) RD 390; 2015(12) SCALE 619; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 142

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Mortgagee, Himachal Pradesh Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1972, Himachal Pradesh Ceiling on Land Holdings Rules, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Himachal Pradesh Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1972, s. 4 - Land holdings of family - Surplus land - Dev Raj, predecessor-in-interest of respondents was issued notice in form C-V wherein land belonging to him was proposed to be declared as surplus under the Act - Aggrieved, writ petition was filed before HC wherein Collector was directed to determine surplus area and Collector passed order holding that landowner wife, and sons be excluded from holding of landowner and all members of family holding land continue to enjoy rights therein to extent of determined permissible area - Later, on revision, Financial Commissioner remanded case to Collector - On remand, Collector passed order holding that Dev Raj and that of his family on appointed day i.e., 24-1-1971, landowner is entitled for two units of land as permissible area - On appeal, Divisional Commissioner held that s.4(6) is very explicit under which total land held by family members has to be considered - On revision, said order was upheld and so, successor-in-interest of Dev Raj filed writ petition before HC challenging orders passed by Revenue Authorities - DB of HC allowed writ petition and quashed orders of Revenue Authorities - Hence, instant appeal - Whether family of husband, wife, one adult son and three minor children, though everybody was holding the land on 24-1-1971, could hold more than two units of permissible area.

Held, even if respondents were holding property in their respective individual capacity a as person, land held by them will be taken into account for the purpose of calculating the permissible area. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is contrary to law, facts on record, and findings recorded therein could not be sustained and so, same is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per s. 4 of the Act, landowner could be a family, capable of holding property, consisting of husband, wife and three minor children and, permissible area which a family consisting of husband, wife and up to three minor children could hold would be to the extent provided therein. Further, every adult son would be treated as a separate unit and is entitled to land up to extent permissible to a family u/s. 4(1) subject to condition that aggregate land of the family and that of the separate units put together could not exceed twice the area permissible under said section. Further, u/s. 4(6), where a person is a member of the family, the land held by such person together with the land held by all the members of the family will be taken into account for the purpose of calculating the permissible area.
