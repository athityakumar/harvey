Daljit Singh Grewal v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 641; (2015) 9 SCC 680; AIR 2016 SC 1260; JT 2015 (11) SC 12; 2015(9) SCALE 142; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 533; 2016 (1) SLR 391

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Adverse Remarks, Executive Instructions, Home Guard Act, Punjab Home Guard Class-II Rules, 1988, Punjab Home Guard Class-I Rules, 1988

Summary: Service - Grading as average in ACR - Failure to grant promotion - Justification - Appellant joined Department as District Commander in year 1993 - Work was appreciated by his senior, however, for period 1-7-1999 to 31-3-2000, appellant received ACR which rated his performance as 'average' - Further, on 30-6-2001, appellant became eligible for promotion to post of after completion of 8 years of service however, same was denied due to ACR - Various representations filed by appellant was denied by respondent - Lower court passed order in favor of appellant and adverse remarks recorded against appellant in ACR for period 1-4-1999 to 31-3-2000 were expunged and all consequential benefits were granted to appellant - However, respondent no.4 failed to grant promotion to appellant - Further, appellant's performance was shown as 'average' in ACR for period 01.04.2001 to 31.03.2002 which was graded by respondent No.4 and appellant was denied promotion - HC dismissed review petition filed by appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, perusal of ACR for period 2000-2001 reveals that though general remarks stated that appellant is very good which is also is agreed by respondent no. 4, however, appellant was assessed to be 'average' officer without assigning any reason. Further, perusal of copy of ACR for period 2003-2004 reflects true picture of injustice that has been perpetrated against appellant. ACR has been written by respondent no. 4 who was Reporting Authority as Divisional Commandant. Further, same officer also happened to be Final Accepting Authority as Commandant General. Further, in said year also performance of appellant had been graded as 'average' clearly reveals malafide intention of respondent nos.1-4 in deliberately denying promotion to appellant to post in question. Further, judgment and decree passed in favour of appellant by lower Court has not been implemented by respondent Nos. 4 and 5, despite having attained finality, which clearly reflects fact that respondent No.4 was not fair in considering him for promotion. Further, appellant has fulfilled requirement of seniority-cum-merit by securing 14 marks, as per Instructions in relation to all aspects entered in ACR, however, he was not granted promotion. Further, SC held that if calculation of marks made by respondents on various aspects in ACR of appellant is believed to be true, then also he has achieve required benchmark. Hence, impugned order passed by respondent and affirmed by HC is set aside. Further, respondent nos. 1 to 5 are directed to reconsider claim of appellant. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Failure to grant promotion due to malafide intention of authority is unjustified and liable to be quashed.
