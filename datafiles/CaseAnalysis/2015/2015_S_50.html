Sanjeev Kumar Gupta and others v State of U. P. (Now State of Uttarakhand)

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 340; JT 2015 (4) SC 529; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 760; 2015(6) SCALE 176

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prosecution Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Appellant accused threatened victims to withdraw their names from election of College, however, on their refusal, accused assaulted victim with arms victims suffered injuries and one of victim was died due to injuries - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 read with s. 149 of IPC and sentencing them to life imprisonment and to pay fine - Order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction order passed against appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, testimonies of prosecution witnesses are consistent, on whole, and minor discrepancies are such that those will not weaken prosecution case. Further, prosecution witnesses have established presence and participation of all accused in offence. Medical examination has gone further to strengthen their testimonies. Statement of PW1 gets corroborated by injury report prepared by doctor PW 8. Further, another eyewitness PW 3 has supported version given by PW1 and he has named all seven accused with respect to their presence at cycle stand and has also supported PW1 with respect to their individual roles played in assaulting deceased and PW1. With respect to question of presence of seven accused persons and individual role played by them, SC held that there is no inconsistency in statements of prosecution witnesses. Further, with respect to inconsistency with statement given by PW 1 in F.I.R and statement given in Court, SC held that it is not be fatal to prosecution case as there is possibility of post incident trauma and shock which might have been caused to injured eye witness. Further, failure to recover weapon used by accused appellants and recovery of blood stained shirt after six days of date of incident would not be fatal to prosecution case. SC held that accused persons, at time of incident were members of unlawful assembly because their common object was to threaten and prevent deceased and other persons from contesting College elections and common intention to might have arisen on spur of moment. Hence, order passed by Trial Court and HC is confirmed by SC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In criminal case, guilt of accused should be established beyond reasonable doubt by prosecution.
