Narendra Kumar Amin v CBI and others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 34; (2015) 3 SCC 417; AIR 2015 SC 1002; 2015 CRLJ 1334; 2015 (3) JCC 1730; JT 2015 (2) SC 233; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 209; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 566B; 2015(1) SCALE 427

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167 (2), 173 (2) - Default bail - Entitled to - Appellant accused was involved in fake encounter death in Ishrat Jahan - Respondent investigating agency failed to submit police report within 90 days - Appellant sought default bail - HC, by impugned order, dismissed appellant's application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, charge sheet was filed on 3-7-2013 that is 90th day. The particulars to be furnished in the police report are complied with in the instant case. Therefore, filing of the police report as required u/s. 173 (2) is within 90 days in the instant case. Therefore, the HC is right in rejecting the prayer of default bail u/s. 167 (2) of CrPC. Further, upon the filing of the police report, cognizance was taken by Magistrate on 3-7-2013. However, the said order remains unchallenged by the appellant. Therefore, it is not open for him to turn around and contend that cognizance was not taken by the Magistrate on 3-7-2013. Further, filing of police report containing the particulars as mentioned u/s. 173 (2) CrPC amounted to completion of filing of the report before the Magistrate, cognizance is taken and registered the same. Hence, the impugned order does not call for any interference. Appeal dismissed.
