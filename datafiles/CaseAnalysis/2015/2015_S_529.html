Subrata Chattoraj and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 938; (2016) 2 SCC 1; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 432; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Transfer of case involving scam to CBI - Lack of manpower - Directions for recruitment - Cases involving chit fund deposits across several States in eastern part of country involved several companies, scam which involved huge amount - Further, more than 25 lakhs claims were lodged by depositors before Commissions of Inquiry set up by States of Odisha and West Bengal - Further, scam had inter-state ramifications apart from international money laundering dimensions involving several persons in public life - PIL was filed by respondent for transferring of large number of other cases to Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) - HC disposed of petition with direction to Central Govt. to issue notification transferring all chit fund cases registered in State of Assam to CBI with direction to CBI to investigate said cases - CBI filed application praying for modification of order passed by HC on ground that CBI is small organization with limited material and human resources and pre-occupied with Coal Scam and 2G Scam cases entrusted to it - Further, transfer of large number of cases relating to Chit fund scam in State of Assam, Odisha and West Bengal will, according to CBI, bring it under tremendous pressure which, hence, CBI cannot handle with existing manpower and other infrastructure at its disposal - Hence, instant application.

Held, affidavit is filed by CBI stating there is absence of comprehensive review of cadre strength ever since organization came to be established. Further, data furnished by CBI shows that creation of vacancies and filling up of such vacancies has suffered primarily because of lackadaisical attitude of departments concerned towards matter of significant public importance directly related to administration of justice and rule of law. Hence, SC issued direction that Central Govt. shall within two months take steps for comprehensive revision of cadre strength of CBI. Further, revision of cadre strength when ordered shall be completed within period of four months from date Govt. issues necessary directions. Further, pending comprehensive revision of cadre strength, Central Govt. shall take immediate steps at appropriate level to fill up available vacancies within existing cadre. SC held that Govt. attends to and processes on urgent basis all proposals currently pending at different levels of hierarchy. Further, State Govt. shall forward panel of 12 Deputy Superintendants of Police, 8 Inspectors, 20 Sub-Inspectors and 40 Constables to enable CBI to select from out of said panel, 6 Deputy Superintendants of Police, 4 Inspectors, 10 Sub-Inspectors and 20 Constables for deputation to CBI and same shall be done within four weeks if not already done by State Govt. in which case CBI shall expedite process of selecting personnel to be taken on deputation to mitigate its current manpower requirement. Application disposed of.

Ratio - In order to investigate scam cases it is necessary that agency appointed should have adequate manpower, hence, Court is justified to pass directions for increasing ability of such agency.
