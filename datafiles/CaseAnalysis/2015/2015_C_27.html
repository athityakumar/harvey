Adani Power Limited v Central Electricity Regulatory Commission

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R. K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 412; 2015 (3) AWC 2890; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 1050; 2015(4) SCALE 376

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Electricity Act, 2003

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 22 - Condonation of delay - Appellant entered into power purchase agreement (PPA) with respondent nos. 2 and 3, and another PPA with respondent no. 4 whereby appellant agreed to supply power to them - Later, appellant filed petition before Central Commission to effect that it could not supply power at rates agreed as per said PPAs and sought various reliefs on grounds of inter alia, 'Force Majeure' and 'Change of Law' - However, Central Commission rejected said two grounds and accepted third submission on directions for constitution of Committee - Aggrieved, respondent nos. 2 and 3 preferred appeal before Tribunal along with application to permit them to participate in the proceedings of said Committee without prejudice to their contentions in appeal and appellant filed cross objections against rejection of grounds of force majeure and change of law - While so, Central Commission passed order quantifying compensatory tariff and aggrieved by said order, respondents filed appeals and were pending - Tribunal dismissed cross objections as not maintainable and hence, appellant preferred appeal before Tribunal and also filed application for condonation of delay in preferring appeal - Appellate Tribunal declined to condone delay of 481 days in preferring appeal and hence, instant appeal.

Held, the present Court is not required to go into the question of the applicability of O. 41 r. 22 of CPC as decision of Appellate Tribunal to reject cross-objections of the appellant has become final and no appeal against said order is pending. Further, question of condonation of delay in preferring appeal also need not be examined in view of submission of appellant and as long as appellant does not seek declaration that appellant is relieved of obligation to perform the contracts i.e., PPA in question, appellant is entitled to argue, in pending appeals, any proposition of law, whether, 'force majeure' or 'change of law' in support of order quantifying compensatory tariff, so long as such argument is based on facts already pleaded before Central Commission. Appeal disposed of.
