S. Syed Mohideen v P. Sulochana Bai

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 521; (2016) 2 SCC 683; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 321; 2016 (66) PTC 1; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 810; 2015(7) SCALE 136

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Trademark

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999, ss. 18, 19, 25(1), 27, 28, 34 - Trademark - Passing Off - Permanent Injunction - Respondent was a registered owner of trademark 'Iruttukadai Halwa' - Respondent filed suit for permanent injunction against appellant who was also selling halwa under the trade name 'Iruttukadai Halwa' - Trial Court decreed the suit - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, conjoint reading of ss. 34, 27 and 28 of the 1999 Act would show that the rights of prior user are recognized superior than that of the registration and even the registered proprietor cannot disturb / interfere with the rights of prior user. In view of plethora of evidences, respondent has been able to establish that the trade mark 'Iruttukadai Halwa' has been used of by her/her predecessors since the year 1900. Business in the name of 'Iruttukadai Halwa' is carried on by respondent's family and it has become a household name which is associated with the respondent/her family. Halwa sold by the respondent's shop as 'Iruttukadai Halwa' is not only famous with the consumers living in Tirunelveli, but is also famous with the consumers living in other parts of India and outside. Respondent shall also be entitled to cost which is quantified at Rs. 50,000. Appeal dismissed.
