Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board v Indraprastha Gas Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 461; (2015) 9 SCC 209; AIR 2015 SC 2978; 2015(7) SCALE 288; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 215

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Petroleum & Gas

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board Act, 2006, Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (Authorizing Entities to Lay, Build, Operate or Expand City or Local Natural Gas Distribution Networks) Regulations, 2008, Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (Determination of Network Tariff for City or Local Natural Gas Distribution Networks and Compression Charge for CNG) Regulations, 2008

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Administrative - Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board Act, 2006, ss. 11, 22, 61 - Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (Determination of Network Tariff for City or Local Natural Gas Distribution Networks and Compression Charge for CNG) Regulations, 2008 - Fixation of Tariff by Board - Jurisdiction of Board - Determination - Board u/s. 22 of Act determined network tariff and compression charges for CNG in respect of network of petitioner and directed petitioner to recover said network tariff and compression charges - Petitioner contended that Board does not have power to direct petitioner, while charging its consumers, to disclose network tariff and compression charges and also to fix said network tariff and compression charges in any particular manner - DB held that Board is not empowered to fix or regulate maximum retail price at which gas is to be sold by entities to consumers - Further, Board is not empowered to fix any component of network tariff or compression charge for entity having its own distribution network. HC opined that that provisions of Regulations as far as it is construed to empower Board to fix tariff is unsustainable and order passed by Board is quashed - Whether Board has power to fix or regulate maximum retail price at which gas is to be sold by entities to consumers.

Held, Board has not been conferred such power as per s. 11 of Act and that is legislative intent. S. 61 of Act enables Board to frame Regulations to carry out purposes of Act and certain specific aspects have been mentioned therein. S. 61 of Act has to be read in context of statutory scheme. SC held that Act does not confer any such power on Board and expression 'subject to' used in s. 22 of Act makes it conditional one. It has to yield to other provisions of Act. Power to fix tariff has not been given to Board. Hence, Board cannot frame Regulation which will cover area pertaining to determination of network tariff for city or local gas distribution network and compression charge for CNG. As entire Regulation centres around said subject, said Regulation deserves to be declared ultra vires, and we do so. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Authority cannot exercise power or pass such order which are not granted to them by statute.
