Jaswal Neco Limited v Commissioner of Customs, Visakhapatnam

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 536; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 561; JT 2015 (7) SC 88; 2015(8) SCALE 484; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Provisional, Additional Duty Of Customs, Tariff Value, Such Goods, Finance Act, 1963, Finance Act, 2009

Summary: (A) Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, s. 18 - Failure to pay duty - Interest levied - Justification - Appellant is engaged in manufacture of pig iron and imported goods in question - Further, appellant claimed exemption from payment of special customs duty, special additional duty, antidumping duty on basis of notification issued - However, appellant failed to fulfill condition provided under notification to claim notification, hence show cause notice was issued demanding duty - Appellant made payment towards all duties except duty towards antidumping duty - Commissioner demanded duty and imposed penalty - Further, Tribunal partly allowed appeal by remanding matter to original authority to calculate duty, interest, and penalty - Further, interest was levied on customs duties that are due from appellant - Appellant contended that assessment is only provisional and under Act itself there was no provision for collection of interest for period in dispute as s. 18 was amended to include such a provision only prospectively with effect from 2006 - Whether appellant is liable to pay interest on customs duties that are due.

Held, notification dt. 19-5-2002 makes it clear that interest at rate of 24% p.a. is only liable to be paid if at time of clearance of imported materials importer executes bond in which such interest is stated to be payable. Further, bond id executed in present case, however, it says nothing about any interest that is payable in case conditions of notification of 1997 are not met. Hence, it is clear that no interest is payable on any of customs duties that are due from appellant. Further, provisional assessment had been made in 1998 and final assessment only on 4-11-2004 by Commissioner. Both these dates being prior to 13-7-2006, no interest is chargeable u/s. 18 of Act, for period in question. Appeal allowed.

(B) Customs - Imposition of anti-dumping duty while imposing custom duty - Justification - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, ss. 3(2), 3A(2) - Finance Act, 1963, s. 23 - Customs Tariff (Identification, Assessment and Collection of Anti-Dumping Duty on Dumped Articles and For Determination of Injury) Rules, 1995 - Whether Anti-dumping duty can be included in calculating special customs duty and special additional duty.

Held, very words 'as addition to, and in same manner as' used in s. 3(2) and 3A(2) of 1975 Act have been used in s. 23 of 1963 act when what was sought to be levied was only surcharge. By way of contrast, s. 24(3) when it levies different duty -regulatory duty of customs - uses expression 'in addition'. Hence, what is referred to in s. 3(2) and 3A(2) of 1975 Act is only surcharge or additional duty of customs. Further, from reading of Rules it is clear that Anti-dumping duty apart from being separate levy from levy of customs duty is also levied in completely different manner from that of customs duty. Further, after 2002, ss. 3(2) and 3A(2) of 1975 Act have been amended w.e.f. 1-3-2002 so as to expressly not include Anti-dumping duty. Further, with respect to penalty, SC held that appellant has not diverted goods meant for export to domestic tariff area. Further, market considerations made it difficult for appellant to fulfill its export obligations, hence, penalty imposed is ought to be set aside. Impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Duty can be calculated and imposed on assessee when same is provided by statute.
