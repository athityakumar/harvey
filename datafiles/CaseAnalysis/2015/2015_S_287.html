K. B. Ramachandra Raje Urs (Dead) By Lrs and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 900; (2016) 3 SCC 422; 2016(1) ALL MR 997; 2016 (2) AWC 1452; JT 2015 (12) SC 487; 2016 (2) KarLJ 77; 2016 (1) MLJ 484; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition, MUNICIPALITIES & LOCAL GOVERNMENTS, City of Mysore Improvement Act, 1903, Karnataka Regularisation of Unauthorised Construction In Urban Areas Act, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - City of Mysore Improvement Act, 1903, ss. 16(1), 17, 23(4) - Acquisition of land - Alleged Illegality - Determination - Appellant filed petition before HC challenging preliminary notification dt. 21-6-1985 issued u/s. 16(1) of Act for proposing to acquire of land in question for improvement of city - Appellant alleged that acquisition of land in question was illegal - Lower court passed order holding that acquisition was illegal, however, DB vide impugned order reversed order passed by lower Court - Appellant contended that there was no scheme prepared and reference to availability of scheme for inspection in preliminary notification dt. 21-6-1985 as published in Gazette on 30-3- 1987 is hollow declaration - Hence, present appeal.

Held, requirement of existence of plan/development scheme prior to publication of preliminary notification u/s. 16(1) of Act is mandatory requirement. However, such mandatory requirement has not been followed. Further, there is no material to show that question of modifications in scheme were duly considered in light of objections received and that scheme was sent to State Govt. for sanction as required u/s. 17 of Act. Further, Govt. approval to resolution of Board to handover 55 acres of land to respondent No.28 i.e. Society on 28-5-1988 and handing over of possession of such land on 26-9-1988 is also contrary to specific provisions contained in ss. 23(4) of Act inasmuch as aforesaid provision of Act contemplates vesting of land in Govt. after award is passed and compensation is paid and only on such vesting of land in Govt. same can be transferred to Board. Further, SC held that it would be just, fair and equitable to direct that land vacant as on today and all such lands under encroachments, after being made free therefrom, may be retained by development authority for developmental works in consonance with objects of Act and owner thereof be entitled to compensation in terms of directions that follow. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Acquisition of land which fails to comply with mandatory provisions of statute is illegal and not in accordance with law.
