Gurjit Singh alias Gora and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 165; (2015) 4 SCC 380; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1633; 2015 CRLJ 1955; JT 2015 (5) SC 472; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 763; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 838; 2015(3) SCALE 337; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Defence Version

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Dispute arose in family with respect to property and application for demarcation of property, however, accused brother were not in agreement with course adopted by complainants - Further, heated arguments took place between appellant accused and deceased and as result of which deceased was attacked with weapons by accused and his family - Order of acquittal was passed by Trial Court in favor of accused - However, HC convicted appellant accused u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, there is clear evidence that accused party comprised of appellant and his wife and sons who were armed with weapons . Further, there are two injuries made by 'kassis'; on back of head and other on face of deceased. Eye-witnesses accounts of PW13 and PW14, who were undoubtedly present, in no uncertain terms reveals that deceased was attacked by accused party and gave blow with weapon to deceased. Further, second blow was given on right side of face of deceased. SC held that Trial Court has come up with inference, which is different even from defence version. And considered it appropriate to accept judgment of HC. Further, SC also agreed with judgment of HC that injury on appellant accused is self-inflicted, in all likelihood and inordinate delay in seeking medical attention raises many questions. Further, appellant accused also refused to undergo radiological examination of injuries and did not tell doctor as to how and why he got injuries. SC held appellant's conduct appears to be wholly unnatural and it is not possible to accept defence version. Hence, order of conviction and sentence as recorded by HC is upheld and order of acquittal passed by Trial Court is set aside. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Acquittal of accused is not justified when evidence on record proves guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
