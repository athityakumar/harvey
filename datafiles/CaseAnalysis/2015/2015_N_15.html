Nanjappa v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 529; AIR 2015 SC 3060; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3318; 2015 CRLJ 4012; 2015 (1) JCC 2409; JT 2015 (6) SC 473; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3054; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 744; 2015 (4) RLW 2746; 2015(8) SCALE 171; 2016(8) SCALE 171; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Second Trial, Employed, Panchayat Raj Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13, 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 7- Invalid Sanction - Conviction - Justification - Appellant was working as Bill Collector in Gram Panchayat and it was alleged that appellant demanded bribe from complainant for which raid was conducted and appellant was caught red handed - FIR was lodged against appellant - However, Trial Court acquitted appellant on ground that prosecution failed to prove charges framed against appellant - Further, Trial Court held that sanction for prosecution of appellant had not been granted by competent authority, hence not in accordance with s. 19 of Act - HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and held that since validity of sanction order was not questioned at appropriate stage, appellant was not entitled to raise same at conclusion of trial - HC convicted appellant u/ss. 7 and 13 read with s. 13(2) of Act - Whether impugned order passed by HC against appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, absence of valid previous sanction required u/s. 19 of Act, Trial court was not competent to take cognizance of offence alleged against appellant. SC held that Trial Court not only entertained contention urged on behalf of accused about invalidity of order of sanction but found that authority issuing said order was incompetent to grant sanction. Trial Court held that authority who had issued sanction was not competent to do so, fact which has not been disputed before HC or SC - SC held that only error which Trial Court committed was that, having held sanction to be invalid, it should have discharged accused rather than recording order of acquittal on merit of case. Further, HC has not correctly appreciated legal position regarding need for sanction or effect of its invalidity. It has simply glossed over subject, by holding that question should have been raised at earlier stage. SC held that HC did not realise that issue was not being raised before it for first time but had been successfully urged before Trial Court. Further, with respect to fresh proceedings, SC held that that incident in question occurred on 24-3-1998 and appellant was, at that point of time, around 38 years old, however, appellant is today senior citizen. SC found no compelling reason for directing fresh trial at this distant point of time in case of this nature involving bribe of Rs.500/-, for which appellant has already suffered ignominy of trial, conviction and jail term no matter for short while. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In case sanction is regarded to be invalid, Court should not decide case on merits.
