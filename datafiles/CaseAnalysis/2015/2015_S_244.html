Deepak v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 171; (2015) 4 SCC 762; 2015 (2) CalLT(SC) 29; 2015 CRLJ 2049; JT 2015 (3) SC 28; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 246; 2015(3) SCALE 414; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conviction, Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1983, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 506, 120B - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed rape on prosecutrix who was a minor - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 376, 506, 120B IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeal before HC - HC upheld the conviction and sentence of accused - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by Courts below were sustainable.

Held, on careful analysis of statement of prosecutrix has created an impression on minds that she is reliable and truthful witness and her testimony suffers no infirmity or blemish whatsoever. Even the medical evidence supports the commission of sexual violence on her and this need not elaborate on issue any more in the light of concurrent finding of the courts below having been recorded against accused holding in clear terms that sign of commission of rape on her by accused stood proved by medical evidence beyond reasonable doubt. Accused had not disputed the factum of commission of sexual intercourse by him on prosecutrix because as taken note of, the accused only defence was that since, prosecutrix had consented to commission of the sexual act, no offence of rape was made out against him. Thus, SC upheld the finding of commission of rape by accused on the prosecutrix, which was rightly recorded by two courts below. In regard to sentence, once the offence u/s. 376 IPC is proved then minimum sentence is 7 years, which may extend to imprisonment for life and the fine. Hence, there is no merit in instant appeal. Appeal dismissed.
