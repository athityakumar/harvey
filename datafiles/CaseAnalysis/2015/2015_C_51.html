S. Krishnamoorthy v Chellammal

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 392; AIR 2015 SC 3182; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3672; 2015 (3) BC 427; 2015 CRLJ 4043; 2015 (2) JCC (NI) 135; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 590; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 513; 2016 (1) RLW 333; 2015(4) SCALE 371; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of Cheque - Criminal Proceedings - Respondent borrowed certain sum from appellant and issued a post-dated cheque towards repayment of the loan - Cheque on presentation was dishonoured for insufficiency of funds - Criminal proceedings were initiated against respondent in respect of offence punishable u/s. 138 of the 1881 Act - Respondent contended that loan was taken by mortgaging house in favour of brother of appellant - Respondent further contended that brother of appellant, instead of getting the mortgage deed executed, obtained an agreement of sale from two persons with false and incorrect recitals - HC quashed criminal proceedings against respondent by exercising the powers u/s. 482 of the CrPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, defence of the respondent before the HC, is nothing but absolutely factual in nature, which is neither admitted by the appellant nor apparent on the face of the record. Such type of disputed factual defences could have been appreciated only by the trial court, after the parties led their evidence. In our opinion, the HC committed grave error of law in examining the allegations and counter allegations which are disputed and factual in nature in a proceeding u/s. 482 of the CrPC. Appeal allowed.
