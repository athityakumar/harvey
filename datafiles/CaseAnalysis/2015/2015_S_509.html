Dilip K. Basu v State of West Bengal and others

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 855; (2015) 8 SCC 744; AIR 2015 SC 2887; 2015 (3) CalLT(SC) 89; 2015 CRLJ 4170; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 199; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 814

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights

Keywords: Assembly, Human Dignity, Denial, Interrogation, Recommendation, Violence, Access, Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, Directory, Visitors, Protection Of Human Rights Act, 1993, Protection of Human Rights Ordinance, 1993, Protection of Human Rights Act, 1963, Protection of Human Rights Bill, 1993

Summary: Human Rights - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 30 - Custodian Torture - Protection of Human Rights - Recommendations - There was growing incidence of torture and deaths in police custody which resulted in violation of human rights - Suitable directions are sought by SC with respect to setting-up of State Human Rights Commissions in States of Delhi, Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Meghalaya, Tripura and Nagaland, where such Commissions were not set-up even after two decades had passed since enactment of Act - Hence, present petition.

Held, power of State Govts. to set-up State Human Rights Commission in their respective areas is not power simpliciter but power coupled with duty to exercise such power especially when it is not case of anyone of defaulting States that there is no violation of human rights in their territorial limits. Further, SC held that very purpose of setting up of State Human Rights Commission gets defeated if vacancies that occur from time to time are not promptly filled up and Commission kept functional at all times. SC held that there is hardly any explanation for failure of State to take immediate steps for filling-up of vacancies wherever they have occurred. Further, SC held that denial of access to mechanism conceptualised under Act by reason of non filling up of vacancies directly affects rights of citizens and becomes non functional. Hence, all vacancies against post of Chairperson and Members of State Human Rights Commission shall be filled up by concerned State Govts. Within period of three months. Further, with respect to recommendations concerning Constitution of Human Rights Court in different districts in terms of s. 30 of Act, SC held State Govt. shall take appropriate action in terms of s. 30 of Act, in regard to setting up/specifying Human Rights Courts. With respect to installation of CCTV cameras in police stations and prisons, with view to checking human rights abuse, all States have in their affidavits supported recommendation for installation of CCTV cameras in Police Stations and prisons. In some of States, steps appear to have already been initiated in that direction. However, SC did not consider it necessary to issue direction for installation of CCTV cameras in all police stations and State Govts. may consider taking appropriate decision and appropriate action wherever it is considered feasible to install CCTV cameras in police stations. Further, with respect to cameras in prison, SC held that it will help long way in preventing violation of human rights of those incarcerating in jails. It will also help authorities in maintaining proper discipline among inmates and taking corrective measures wherever abuses are noticed. Further, with respect to appointment of non-official visitors to prisons and police stations for making random and surprise inspection to check violation of human rights, SC held that there is no real harm or danger in appointment of non-official visitors to prisons and police stations provided visitors who are so appointed do not interfere with ongoing investigations. Further, State Govt. may take appropriate action keeping in view provisions of Prison Manuals and Police Acts and Rules applicable to each State. Further, SC held that there is no reason why appropriate proceedings cannot be initiated in cases where enquiry establishes culpability of those in whose custody victim dies or suffers any injuries or torture. Further, with respect to deployment of women constables, SC held that States concerned would consider desirability of posting women constables in police stations wherever it is found that over period of past two years women were detained in connection with any criminal case or investigation. Petition disposed of.
