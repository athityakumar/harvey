Krishnamoorthy v Sivakumar and others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 126; (2015) 3 SCC 467; AIR 2015 SC 1921; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 62; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 210; JT 2015 (2) SC 273; 2015 (2) MLJ 49; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 921; 2015(2) SCALE 1; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 987

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Constituency, Result Of The Election, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Disqualified, Nomination Paper, Candidate, Rejected, Electorate, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, Haryana Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, Purity, Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, Sanctity, Uttar Pradesh Controls of Goondas Act, 1970, Representation of People Amendment Ordinance, 2002

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Tamil Nadu Panchayats Act, 1994 - Representation of People's Act, 1951 - Undue influence - Corrupt practice - Appellant was elected as President of Panchayat but validity of said election was challenged before Tribunal on ground that appellant filed false declaration suppressing details of criminal cases pending trial against him and hence, his nomination deserved to be rejected by Returning Officer and that election was unsustainable - Tribunal held election as null and void and ordered for re-election and hence the same was challenged in revision before HC - HC opined that non-disclosure of full and complete information relating to implication in criminal cases amounted to an attempt to interfere with the free exercise of electoral right which would fall within 'undue influence' and consequently 'corrupt practice' u/s. 259(1)(b) read with s. 260(2) of 1994 Act and upheld election as null and void - Hence instant appeal - Whether non-disclosure of criminal antecedents would tantamount to undue influence, which is facet of corrupt practice, as per s. 123(2) of 1951 Act.

Held, while filing nomination form, if requisite information, relating to criminal antecedents are not given, then there is an attempt to suppress, effort to misguide and keep the people in dark and said attempt undeniable and undisputedly is undue influence and hence, amounts to corrupt practice. Further, if a candidate gives all particulars and still secures the votes, then that is an informed, advised and free exercise of right by electorate. Hence, there is a distinction between a disqualification and corrupt practice. In an election petition, the election petitioner is required to assert about the cases in which the successful candidate is involved as per the rules and how there has been non-disclosure in the affidavit. Once that is established, it would amount to corrupt practice and it has to be determined in an election petition by Tribunal. In instant case, election was sought assailed on many grounds. The factum of suppression of cases relating to embezzlement has been established. Hence, HC is justified in declaring that election as null and void on ground of corrupt practice. Hence, appeal dismissed with costs. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Information that is required to be given u/ss. 259 and 260 of 1994 Act makes the provisions contained u/s. 123 of 1951 Act applicable. Further, as per decisions of SC, voter has a fundamental right to know about the candidates contesting the elections as that is essential and necessary concomitant for free and fair election. The voter is entitled to make choice after coming to know the antecedents of a candidate a requisite for making informed choice.
