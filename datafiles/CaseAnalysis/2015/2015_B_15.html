Banwari Lal (Dead) by Lrs. and another v Balbir Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 993; (2016) 1 SCC 607; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 316; 2016 (6) MahLJ 1; 2016 (4) MPLJ 248

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Second Appeal, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.1 r. 10, O. 22 rr. 11, 3, 4, 9 - Suit Land - Order Challenged - Trial Court dismissed suit of respondent/plaintiff filed for declaration and permanent injunction - First Appellate Court set aside order of Trial Court and remanded matter back to Trial Court- Trial Court once again dismissed suit of plaintiff and First Appellate Court set aside order of Trial Court - HC dismissed second appeal of defendant on ground of failure to bring legal representatives on record, irrespective of application filed by legal representatives of deceased/defendant - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the rules of procedure under O. 22 of CPC are designed to advance justice and should be so interpreted as not to make them penal statutes for punishing erring parties. On sufficient cause, delay in bringing the legal representatives of the deceased party on record should be condoned. Procedure is meant only to facilitate the administration of justice and not to defeat the same. The dismissal of the second appeal by the HC does not constitute a sound and reasonable exercise of its powers and the impugned order cannot be sustained. Hence, applications for condonation of delay is allowed and legal representatives of appellant No.2 are ordered to be brought on record. Further, second appeal is ordered to be restored. Order accordingly.
