Ahmed Shah and another v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 14; (2015) 3 SCC 93; 2015 CRLJ 1398; JT 2015 (1) SC 590; 2015(1) SCALE 222; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Spear

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148, 149, 302, 304 and 307 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted all the accused persons u/ss. 148, 307/149 and 302/149 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction of the appellants u/ss. 302/34 of IPC and u/ss. 307/149 of IPC and, all other accused were acquitted of the charges u/ss. 307/149 and 302/149 of IPC and they were convicted u/s. 148 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - It was argued that the instant case was a one of free fight and since individual liability of the accused persons could not be ascertained and the appellants could not have been convicted u/ss. 302/34 of IPC and ss. 307/34 IPC.

Held, dispute between the parties pertains to the land-14 bighas. The possession of the land was with the accused and that the complainant party armed with gun went to the field to take forcible possession of the property. There seems to be mutual provocation and aggravation as the complainant party went to take possession of the land, there appears to be scuffle between the parties. There was no previous deliberation or pre-meditation and the incident is a result of sudden fight. During the course of scuffle, the appellants inflicted injuries on the deceased and other accused inflicted injuries on X and PW8. Hence, considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the instant case cannot be said to be a case punishable u/s. 302 of IPC but a case falling under Exception 4 to s. 300 of IPC. Since the appellants inflicted injuries on the neck and scalp of deceased with the intention of causing death and the act of the accused-appellants is punishable u/s. 304 Part I of IPC. Further, HC has analyzed the evidence and observed that the evidence is omnibus and generalized and that no specific overt act is attributed to the remaining accused. HC cannot be said to have misdirected itself in acquitting other accused. Appeals disposed of.
