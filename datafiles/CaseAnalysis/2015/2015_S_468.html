State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Anil Kumar Sharma and another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 500; (2015) 6 SCC 716; 2016 CRLJ 213; 2015 (1) JCC 2241; JT 2015 (5) SC 451; 2015(6) SCALE 590; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subordinate, Advocate General, Doctrine

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 207, 209 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 209 - Delay in trial proceedings - Issuance of direction by HC - Jurisdiction - FIR was lodged u/s. 209 of IPC against respondent No. 1 clerk - Respondent filed petition for quashing of FIR - However, HC made observations relating as to why trials get prolonged due to various factors, including non-arrest of accused by police while submitting reports u/s. 173 (2) of CrPC - Further, HC on its own motion, referred to procedural issues relating to criminal trials and directed police authorities to get installed photocopying machines at police stations for preparing copies required to be supplied u/s. 207 of CrPC - HC passed impugned order wherein it started monitoring process of expediting criminal trials in State and asked State to file compliance report - Appellants contended that due to general directions issued by HC to Trial Courts not to accept reports u/s. 173 of CrPC unless accused are produced, thousands of cases got held up in which reports could not be filed in courts - Whether HC exceeded its jurisdiction is issuing impugned order with respect to filing of compliance report.

Held, HC has clearly erred in law in treating petition which was filed for quashing of FIR, and had become infructuous, as PIL and issued directions, without there being sufficient data and material before it to pass directions. There is no requirement u/s. 173 of CrPC for Investigating Officer to produce accused along with charge-sheet. SC held that HC failed to take into consideration that where there are several accused and only some of them could be arrested and remanded to judicial custody, and others are on bail, how all of them can be produced together by police. Further, HC should have realized that trial of under-trial prisoners cannot be allowed to be delayed, for want of presence of accused absconding in case. Further, State submitted that all arrangements have been made for preparation of copies of papers before charge sheet is filed so that they can be served on accused persons. It is also accepted that all efforts have to be made to apprehend accused persons so that trial can be expedited. Further, there is no mandate in s. 207 of CrPC which requires police to prepare copies of all papers before charge sheet is filed. SC held that HC has exceeded its jurisdiction in asking State Authorities to file compliance report, in matter of dispensing with requirement of committal of cases by Magistrate to Trial Courts as provided in s. 209 of CrPC. Further, Central Govt. was not even party in petition in which direction was issued to get amended CrPC. Hence, impugned order passed by HC giving directions to State authority is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - HC cannot exceed its jurisdiction in issuing directions to State and other authorities as such power is not granted to HC by statute.
