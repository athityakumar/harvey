Dulu Devi v State of Assam and others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 703; AIR 2016 SC 2152; 2015(6) ALL MR 973; 2016 (148) FLR 204; JT 2015 (9) SC 341; 2015(10) SCALE 405; 2015 (6) SLR 742; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 107

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Regularisation

Summary: Service - Termination from Service - Failure to communication termination order - Justification - Appellant was first appointed as Assistant Teacher in 1976, however, thought appellant was rendering continuous service as Assistant Teacher for more than 10 years, she was not paid her salary - Further, HC vide order directed respondent authority to pay salary to appellant - Appellant was paid all arrears of her salary and other allowances till August, 2007 - Further, appellant was given charge of head-mistress, however, respondent authority submitted report enclosing therewith list of 193 teachers which included name of appellant who had been appointed in 1989 but were subsequently terminated, still drawing their salaries - Hence, respondent authority vide impugned order stopped salary of 193 teachers including appellant - Appellant filed appeal before HC seeking direction to respondents to allow her to continue in service as Headmistress in- charge of School; for regularisation of her service and for payment of regular salary to her for service being rendered which was dismissed by HC - Appellant contended that she had never been terminated from her service and that no order of termination had ever been served upon her - Hence, present appeal.

Held, HC in impugned order further noted that letter of termination was neither issued nor services of appellant were terminated. Further, HC assuming that services of appellant were terminated, refused to grant relief and dismissed writ petition. SC held that approach of HC is not in accordance with law. Services of teacher who has been working for last 25 years shall not be assumed to have been terminated and deprived of from her legitimate claim. Further, State of Punjab vs. Amar Singh Harika, 1966 Indlaw SC 14, followed where it was held that mere passing of order of dismissal or termination would not be effective unless it is published and communicated to officer concerned, SC in present case held that in background of facts of present case, particularly, continued service of appellant for last 25 years, impugned order passed by HC cannot be sustained in law and same is set aside. Appeal allowed.
