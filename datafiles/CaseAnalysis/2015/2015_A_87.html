Shreya Vidyarthi v Ashok Vidyarthi and others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 883; AIR 2016 SC 139; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 73; 2016 (115) ALR 457; 2016 (3) ALT(SC) 14; 2016 (2) AWC 1363; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 45; JT 2015 (12) SC 477; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 22; 2016 (1) MLJ 449; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 668; 2016 (2) RLW 1099; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 187

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Karta, Joint Family, Estate Duty Act, 1953, Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005, Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Suit Premises - Decree of Partition Challenged - Respondent/plaintiff filed suit seeking permanent injunction, decree of partition and separation of shares by metes and bounds against appellants/defendants nos. 1, 2 & 8 - Trial Court dismissed suit and First Appellate Court decreed suit ex-parte, thereby respondent was entitled to ¾ share and appellant/defendant no.8, entitled to remaining 1/4th share of suit property - Further, recall application against HC order was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, a Hindu Widow is not a coparcener in the HUF of her husband and, therefore, cannot act as Karta of the HUF after the death of her husband the two expressions i.e. Karta and Manager may be understood to be not synonymous and the expression 'Manager' may be understood as denoting a role distinct from that of the Karta. Hypothetically, in the case of HUF where the male adult coparcener has died and there is no male coparcener surviving or where the sole male coparcener is a minor. In such a situation obviously the HUF does not come to an end. The mother of the male coparcener can act as the legal guardian of the minor and also look after his role as the Karta in her capacity as his (minor's) legal guardian. In instant case, defendant no.1 in her capacity as the step mother of the respondent/plaintiff therefore, consistent with the legal position which recognizes a Hindu Widow acting as the Manager of the HUF in her capacity as the guardian of the sole surviving minor male coparcener. Further, the suit property was a joint family property, the respondent-plaintiff was found entitled to seek partition thereof and on that basis the apportionment of shares in the suit property between the plaintiff and the contesting defendant no.8/appellant was rightly made by the High Court in accordance with the reliefs sought in the suit. Hence, impugned order passed by HC does not warrant any interference and appeals are devoid of any merits. Appeals dismissed.
