Raj Singh and another v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Amitava Roy, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 292; (2015) 6 SCC 268; 2015 (111) ALR 241; 2015 CRLJ 2803; JT 2015 (4) SC 287; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 594; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 847; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 268; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Good Faith, Life Imprisonment, Harm, Private Defence, Right Of Private Defence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 , 300, 302, 304, 323, 324, 302, 307 506 - Alleged assault and murder - Conviction - Right of private defence - Complainant alleged that he and his brother was assaulted by appellant accused who attacked them with arms and weapons - Further, due to assault, brother of complainant died and complainant received various injuries - Charge sheet was filed u/ss. 323, 324, 302, 307 and 506 read with s. 34 of IPC - Appellant accused contended that he acted in private defence - Trial Court vid judgment held that appellant accused exceeded right of private defence and convicted appellant- u/s. 304 Part-1 IPC and acquitted other alleged appellant - Further, in revision, HC converted conviction of appellant u/s. 304 Part 1 IPC to S. 302 of IPC and maintained sentence of life imprisonment imposed on him - Hence, present appeal.

Held, HC had righty observed that accused have also not examined any witnesses to substantiate their plea, hence, Trial court was not right in holding that place of occurrence was house of accused and that complainant party were aggressors. Further, it was rightly held that investigating officer had helped appellant by changing place of occurrence to make it appear that complainant party were aggressors. Further, from evidence on record , it is clear neither any firearm was used by complainant and others nor any such firearm was found in their possession. Further, with respect to injuries received by appellant accuse, doctor opined that said injuries are possible by fall. SC held that considering nature of injuries and other material on record, complainant party have not exceeded their right of private defence and caused harm that was necessary for purpose of private defence. Hence order passed by lower Court convicting appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law. Order passed by first judge of bench is agreed by second judge of bench and he that opined that once it was held that accused and others were aggressors and that incident had taken place in house of complainant and not at house of said appellants as alleged by them, there is no room for appellants to claim benefit of Exception 2 to s. 300 of IPC - Hence, order passed by HC convicting appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - To succeed in plea of private defence, party has to prove that he exercised right of private defence in his favour and this right extended to extent of causing death.
