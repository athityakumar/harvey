Bridgestone India Private Limited v Inderpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 792; (2016) 2 SCC 75; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 871; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 38; [2016] 194 Comp Cas 138; 2016 (2) CompLJ 392; 2016 CRLJ 553; 2016 (1) DRTC 349; 2016 (3) JabLJ 277; 2016 (1) JCC (NI) 4; JT 2015 (12) SC 217; 2016 (1) KLT 845; 2016 (4) MahLJ 911; 2016 (3) MPLJ 269; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 320; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 171; 2015(13) SCALE 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appearance, Territorial Jurisdiction, Cheque, Proviso, Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Ordinance, 2015, Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Second Ordinance, 2015

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 177, 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 142A - Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Ordinance, 2015 - Territorial jurisdiction - Dishonor of cheques - Appellant initiated proceedings u/s. 138 of the Act before Judicial Magistrate, Indore, against accused-respondent as cheque issued by respondent was dishonored - Accused-respondent preferred application u/s. 177 of CrPC challenging territorial jurisdiction of Judicial Magistrate, Indore, but the same was declined by Judicial Magistrate, Indore - Aggrieved, accused-respondent filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC assailing orders of Judicial Magistrate, Indore - HC disposed of petition by remitting case to Judicial Magistrate, Indore, requiring him to pass fresh order and Judicial Magistrate, Indore, again passed order holding that he had territorial jurisdiction - Accused-respondent again approached HC wherein impugned order was passed by HC holding that jurisdiction lay only before the Court wherein the original drawee bank was located - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC could be upheld.

Held, s. 142(2)(a), amended through the Ordinance, vests jurisdiction for initiating proceedings for the offence u/s. 138 of the Act, inter alia in the territorial jurisdiction of the Court, where the cheque is delivered for collection (through an account of the branch of the bank where the payee or holder in due course maintains an account.) Further, judgment rendered by SC in DashrathRupsinghRathod v. State of Maharashtra and antoher, 2014 INDLAW SC 494, would not stand in the way of appellant, insofar as the territorial jurisdiction for initiating proceedings emerging from dishonor of the cheque. Hence, as cheque drawn on UBI Chandigarh, was presented for encashment at IDBI Bank, Indore, which intimated its dishonor to the appellant on 4-8-2006, Judicial Magistrate, Indore, would have territorial jurisdiction to take cognizance of proceedings initiated by appellant u/s. 138 of the Act, after promulgation of Ordinance. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside and parties are directed to appear before Judicial Magistrate, Indore. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - With reference to an offence u/s. 138 of the Act, the place where a cheque is delivered for collection i.e., the branch of the bank of the payee or holder in due course, where the drawee maintains an account, would be determinative of the place of territorial jurisdiction.
