Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore v Micro Labs Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 865; [2016] 380 ITR 1; 2015(13) SCALE 460; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1154; [2016] 237 TAXMAN 74

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Goods Or Merchandise, Exports, Finance Act, 1990, Such Profits, Gross Total Income, Finance (No.2) Act, 1991, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 2005

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 80HHC, 80IA, 80IA(1), 80IA(9), 80IB - Deduction claimed twice over same profits - Justification - Assessee having several industrial units with different activities or different businesses - Further, assessee being also in business of export, had also claimed and was allowed deduction u/s. 80HHC of Act - It is stated that inspite of fact that assessee had claimed deduction in respect of provisions of s. 80-IB of Act, assessee had also claimed deduction u/s. 80HHC of Act with respect to same profits - AO had allowed deductions u/s. 80HHC of Act without considering fact that assessee had also claimed and was allowed deduction under provisions of s. 80-IB of Act - Further, Commissioner directed AO to revise assessment order - Further, Tribunal dismissed appeal filed by assessee - However, HC allowed appeal and passed order in favor of assessee - Whether impugned order passed by lower Court in favor of assessee is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, by first judge of bench that intention of legislature is very clear to effect that if assessee claims any deduction under provisions of ss. 80-IA or/and 80-IB of Act, he cannot claim deduction to extent to such profits and gains which had been claimed and allowed under provisions of s. 80HHC of Act, because s. 80HHC of Act is included in heading 'C' of Chapter VIA of Act. SC held that HC was in error while permitting assessee to get benefit in respect of s. 80HHC as it did not take into account fact that profits in respect of which deduction was allowed u/s. 80HHC had also been previously allowed u/s. 80-IB of Act. Hence, SC reversed order passed by HC. However, order passed first judge of bench is not concurred by second judge of bench on ground that s. 80AB and s. 80IA(9) of Act have to be harmoniously construed and read together. Further, second limb of s. 9 to s. 80IA has been enacted to prevent cascading effect of deductions u/s. 80IA and 80HHC of Act. There was already cap or upper limit stipulated in s. 80IA(2) of Act that deductions cannot exceed gross total income of assessee. However, s. 9 to s. 80IA stipulates that in no case deduction shall exceed profits and gains of such eligible business of undertaking and enterprise. Further, said provision does not make reference to gross total income but it refers to profits and gains of such eligible business of undertaking and enterprise. Second judge held that two provisions operate independently. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. In view of difference of opinion, matters are referred to larger Bench. Matter referred to larger Bench. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Deductions in respect of same profits can be claimed by assessee taking into consideration intention and object of legislation.
