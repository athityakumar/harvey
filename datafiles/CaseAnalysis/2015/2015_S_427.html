State of Rajasthan v Daud Khan

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 783; (2016) 2 SCC 607; 2016 CRLJ 165; 2016 (1) JCC 282; JT 2015 (10) SC 622; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 502; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 123; 2015(12) SCALE 316

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Special Report, Revolver, Bullet, Pistol, Distance, Shooting, Forthwith

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304, 34 - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - It is stated that deceased accompanied by his friend i.e. accused no 3 and other person had gone to restaurant - Further, accused nos. 1 and 2 came and brandished knife and fired upon deceased which resulted in death of deceased - FIR was lodged against all three accused persons - Trial Court convicted accused no.1 for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and s. 3 read with s. 25 of Act. Further, accused no.2 was convicted of offence punishable u/s. 3 read with s. 25 of Act but was found not guilty of offence u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC - However, accused no.3 was found not guilty of any offence - Further, HC vie impugned order held that accused no.1 was not guilty of offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC but was guilty of offence punishable under first part of s. 304 of IPC and his conviction u/s. 3 read with s. 25 of Act was maintained - Further, with respect to conviction of accused no.2 , his conviction under Act was confirmed but sentence was reduced - Further, HC dismissed appeal filed by State against acquittal of accused no.2 of offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and complete acquittal of accused no.3 - Whether impugned order passed by impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to delay in lodging FIR, SC held that incident is stated to have occurred at about 9.30 pm. FIR was lodged at about 10.30 pm, hence, there is hardly any 'delay' in lodging of FIR. Further, as there was no delay in lodging FIR, hence question of its manipulation does not arise. Further, officer in charge of police station, PW 21 was not asked any question about delay in sending special report to Magistrate, hence, no adverse inference can be drawn against prosecution nor it can be held that delay in receipt of special report by Magistrate is fatal to case of prosecution. Further, with respect to FSL Report, SC held that although FSL report was inconclusive in sense that it could not be stated whether extracted bullet could be 'definitely' linked to recovered weapon, but there was no doubt that extracted bullet was capable of being fired from recovered gun. Hence, there was no mismatch between bullet and gun. Further, there is no doubt both from medical and ocular evidence that accused no.1 had shot with gun. Forensic evidence shows that bullet extracted from body of accused was capable of being fired from recovered gun. Further, SC held that nobody questioned gun-shot was fired from close distance, but there was no blackening of deceased skin possibly due to his apparel. Further, nobody question that medical experts testified spillage of blood from entry wound is not inevitable and so it is possible that deceased's blood was not found between place of incident and place where he collapsed. However, blood was found where deceased collapsed. Further, SC held that five witnesses have testified to events that took place on day of incident. SC found no reason to disbelieve any of them, particularly since they have all given consistent statement of events. Hence, impugned order passed by HC convicting accused is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Conviction of accused is upheld when prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
