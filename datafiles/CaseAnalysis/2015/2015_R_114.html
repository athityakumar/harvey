Regional Deputy Director v Zavaray S. Poonawala and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 430; (2015) 7 SCC 347; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 583; 2015(5) SCALE 184; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Endangered Species, Wildlife, Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972

Summary: Administrative - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Customs Act, 1962, s. 124 - Import of protected and prohibited species - Respondent no. 1 wanted to import trophy of stuffed leopard and permission was granted by Director General Foreign Trade (DGFT) in form of license subject to conditions to obtain clearance and certificate from DGFT and Convention of International Trade on Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) authorities - However, on receipt of communication from CITES, DGFT issued show cause notice u/s. 124 of the Act for confiscation of trophy - Hence, respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC and HC vide impugned judgment held that authorities concerned had given permission to respondent no. 1 and further, CITES had no locus to entertain application or to reject it and allowed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC is sustainable.

Held, permissions granted by DGFT and Chief Wildlife Warden were conditional and subject to approval of CITES which had not given any permission and which in fact passed order specifically refusing the permission. Thus, conditions mentioned in the approval granted by DGFT and Chief Wildlife Warden, were not met by respondent no. 1 and in the absence thereof it could not be treated that there were any proper or valid approval or permission given by DGFT or by the Chief Wildlife Warden which could enable respondent no. 1 to import said item into the country. Further, with regard to jurisdiction of CITES, HC looked into the function of Management Authority alone and error is committed by glossing over the function of Scientific Authority. Matter has to be placed before Scientific Authority which has to form an opinion as to whether the import will or will not be detrimental to the survival of the species involved. Hence, judgment of HC is not sustainable and the same is set aside. Further, orders of CITES and show cause notice given by Customs Authority were also set aside. Further, respondent no. 1 is permitted to apply to Scientific Authority for necessary permission. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Before import of any specimen of species included in Appendix I, prior import permit of Scientific Authority and Management Authority is required and before such a permit is given, opinion of Scientific Authority and Management Authority is required. Further, Scientific Authority has to look into issue whether import is not detrimental to the survival of the species involved and the proposed recipient is suitably equipped of house and care for it and Management Authority is to satisfy itself that the specimen is not to be used for primary commercial purposes.
