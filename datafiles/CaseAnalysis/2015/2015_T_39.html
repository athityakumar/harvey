Kanhsingh and another v Tukaram and others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 27; 2017 (2) ACC 615; 2015 ACJ 594; 2015 (109) ALR 710; 2015 (1) AWC 973; JT 2015 (1) SC 319; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 220; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 613B; 2015 (2) RLW 1572; 2015(1) SCALE 366; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 28

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166 and 173 - Enhancement of compensation - X, son of appellants met with an accident and succumbed to his injuries during the course of treatment - Appellants filed a claim petition u/s. 166 of Act for a compensation of Rs. 27,85,000/- - Tribunal partly allowed the claim petition by awarding a total sum of Rs.12,10,014/- - HC partly allowed the appeal filed against said order and disposed of the same with an enhancement of Rs.2,00,000/- - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, deceased was 27 years of age, working with HDFC as the Manager earning Rs.1,81,860/- per annum (i.e. Rs.15,155/- p.m.) and there were definite chances of his further promotion and consequent increase in salary by way of periodical revision of the salary on the basis of cost of living Index prevalent in the area if he would alive and worked in the bank. Therefore, adding 50% under the head of future prospects to the annual income of the deceased the total loss of income comes to Rs.2,72,790/- per annum. Deducting 1/3rd [Rs.81,837] towards personal expenses since the appellants are the parents of the deceased, loss of dependency comes to 1,63,674 X 11(appropriate multiplier as per the age of the parent) Rs. 18,00,414/-. Tribunal and the HC have further erred in law in awarding only Rs.2,000/- towards funeral expenses instead of Rs.25,000/-. Under the heads of loss of love and affection, SC award Rs.1,00,000/- to the appellants towards the same. Further, SC award Rs.5,00,190/- towards medical expenses incurred towards medical treatment. The total compensation payable to the appellants by the respondent will be Rs. 24,25,604/- with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. from the date of filing of the application till the date of payment to the appellants. Appeal allowed.
