Indian Performing Rights Society Limited v Sanjay Dalia and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 464; (2015) 10 SCC 161; AIR 2015 SC 3479; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 65; 2015 (4) AWC 4035; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 18; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 296; 2015 (5) MLJ 446; 2015 (63) PTC 1; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 213; 2015 (4) RLW 2789; 2015(7) SCALE 574; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 210

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Domestic Violence and Matrimonial Proceedings Act, 1976

Summary: Intellectual Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 20 - Copyright Act, 1957, s. 62 - Trade Marks Act, 1999, s. 134 - IP rignt - Infringement of - Plaintiff filed suit praying for relief against defendant no.1 so as to prevent infringement of rights of appellant without obtaining licence - Defendant owns cinema halls in Maharashtra and Mumbai where infringement is alleged and entire cause of action, as alleged in plaint, has arisen in Mumbai, Maharashtra - However, suit is filed by plaintiff in Delhi as Branch Office of plaintiff is situated at Delhi and plaintiff is carrying on business at Delhi, however, plaintiff's Head Office is situated at Mumbai - Defendant raised objection with respect to territorial jurisdiction of court at Delhi - Lower Court and DB held upheld objection and held that suit should have been in court at Mumbai - Hence, present appeal.

Held, considering language of s. 62 of 1957 Act and s. 134 of 1999 Act, additional forum has been provided by including District Court within whose limits plaintiff actually and voluntarily resides or carries on business or personally works for gain. Object of provisions was to enable plaintiff to institute suit at place where he or they resided or carried on business, not to enable them to drag defendant further away from such place. Further, expression 'notwithstanding anything' contained in CPC does not oust applicability of provisions of s. 20 of CPC and it is clear that additional remedy has been provided to plaintiff so as to file suit where he is residing or carrying on business etc., as case may be. S. 62 of 1957 Act enabled plaintiff or any of them to file suit at places provided in the section, however, if they were residing or carrying on business or personally worked for gain already at such place, where cause of action has arisen, wholly or in part, said provisions have not provided additional remedy to them to file suit at different place and said provisions never intended to operate in that field. SC held that principal place of business is, admittedly, in Mumbai and cause of action has also arisen in Mumbai. Hence, provisions of s. 62 of 1957 Act and s. 134 of 1999 Act cannot be interpreted in manner so as to confer jurisdiction on Delhi court to entertain such suits. Delhi court would have no territorial jurisdiction to entertain it. Hence SC found no reason to interfere with order passed by HC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - If aggrieved party, is residing or carrying on business etc. at place where cause of action, wholly or in part, has also arisen, he has to file suit at that place.
