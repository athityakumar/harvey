State Bank of Hyderabad v Rabo Bank

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 654; (2015) 10 SCC 521; AIR 2015 SC 3820; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 140; 2015(6) ALL MR 922; 2015 (113) ALR 752; 2016 (2) ALT(SC) 37; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 517; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 113; JT 2015 (9) SC 68; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4602; 2016 (3) MahLJ 645; 2015 (7) MLJ 370; 2016 (2) MPLJ 544; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 968; 2016 (130) RD 472; 2015(10) SCALE 308; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Attorney

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37, r. 3 - Trial Issues of adjudication - Leave to defend suit - Justification - Respondent sent bill of exchange to appellant bank for acceptance - However, appellant Bank did not remit amount even after expiry of due date on ground that manner and mode in which transactions took place was not in ordinary course of business and acceptance given by its branch appears to be in total disregard to prevailing procedure in Banks - Further, various correspondences were exchanged between parties - Respondent filed Summary Suit - Trial Court filed liability on appellant and made absolute summons for judgment awarding interest - Further, order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by DB - Further, appellant filed appeal by way of special leave - Whether Courts below were right in decreeing Summary Suit without granting relief of leave to defend to appellant as envisaged under O. 37 r. 3 of CPC.

Held, leave to defend summons for judgment shall always be granted to defendant when there is triable issue as to meaning or correctness of documents on which claim is based or alleged facts are of such nature which entitle defendant to interrogate or cross-examine plaintiff or his witnesses. SC held that acting at requests of representatives from Indian clients of respondent's constituent, Chief Manager had induced some officers of appellant Bank who were In-charge of Foreign Exchange Department to issue tested telex messages of co-acceptance. Further, charge sheet further alleges that these officers were not authorized to issue such co-acceptances and motive behind their illegal and unauthorized action was to enable constituent of respondent to get their bills discounted by jeopardizing interests of appellant Bank. Further, substantial revelations of appellant in affidavit coupled with views expressed by DB makes it clear that there are certain triable issues for adjudication and appellant is entitled to defend suit. Appellate side of HC ought to have taken into consideration factual matrix of case before recording its finding. Taking into consideration totality of facts and circumstances of case, SC opined that appellant has made out prima facie case of triable issues in suit which needs to be adjudicated. Hence, defendant is entitled to grant of unconditional leave to defend suit. Hence, judgment and decree passed by lower Court is set aside. Appellant is granted unconditional leave to defend summons for Judgment in Summary Suit. Lower Court has to deal all issues raised by parties afresh and any observation made by SC while dealing with appeal should not be construed as expression of SC. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Leave to defend suit should be granted when there are certain triable issues for adjudication.
