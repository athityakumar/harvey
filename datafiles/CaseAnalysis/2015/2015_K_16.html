K. S. Ravindran v Branch Manager New India Assurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 328; (2015) 7 SCC 222; AIR 2015 SC 2369; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 28; 2015 (145) FLR 998; JT 2015 (4) SC 489; 2015(6) SCALE 131; 2015 (4) SLR 647

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: General Insurance (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1975, Development Staff Scheme, 1976

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Development Staff Scheme, 1976 - General Insurance (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1975 - Unauthorised absence - Termination - Reinstatement - Appellant was terminated from service for his unauthorised absence and poor business performance - Appellant raised industrial dispute - Labour Court passed award holding that action of management in terminating services of appellant was justified and did not suffer from any illegality - Writ Petition was allowed by Single Judge and directed respondent-Company to reinstate appellant in his post with back-wages - DB partly allowed appeal of respondent holding that order of reinstatement passed by disciplinary authority was modified into one of "stoppage of increment for with cumulative effect" and direction of Single Judge to pay back-wages to appellant was set aside - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB of HC erred in denying back-wages to appellant as quantified by Single Judge.

Held, it is clear that the order of termination against the appellant on the ground that he failed to achieve the target fixed on him by the respondent for the particular year is erroneous. It was also rightly held by the single Judge that neither the respondent nor the Labour Court, have taken into consideration the recommendation of the Branch Manager of the respondent and the explanation given by the appellant in his representation challenging the order of termination passed against him. Single Judge has rightly appreciated the facts and circumstances of the case on hand and passed an order quashing the award of the Labour Court and directed the respondent to reinstate the appellant with all consequential benefits. DB has erred in modifying the order passed by the single Judge into one of stoppage of increment with cumulative effect and set aside the direction of the single Judge directing the respondent to pay back-wages to the appellant.

Impugned judgment and order of the DB of the HC is set aside. Respondent is directed to reinstate the appellant in his post and pay him back-wages. Appeal allowed.
