Sunil Khergade v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 558; 2015 CRLJ 4365; JT 2015 (7) SC 235; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 766; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 112; 2015(8) SCALE 710; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 - Alleged Murder - Reduction in sentence - Justification - Appellant and his brother had fight with deceased's family over sharing of common open space - It is alleged that accused caused fatal injury to deceased with knife - Appellant along with his younger brother was convicted u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC and sentenced to undergo life imprisonment by Trial Court - Order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction order passed by HC against appellants accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, FIR need not necessarily contain each and every particular injury sustained by deceased. It needs to contain only some information about crime and some information about manner in which offence has been committed. It is not required to contain minute details of whole crime. Further, in present case FIR, was prepared on basis of statement given by PW1 father of deceased and to him, it is not number of injuries sustained what mattered but death resulting from stab injury. It has also come in evidence that deceased had been inflicted with three injuries by appellant and fatal injury is one which pierced heart of deceased. SC held that it has been established in evidence that deceased and other members of family were wholly unarmed, deceased had come to his village only in morning of fatal day, appellant and his younger brother, who is co-accused, both were in possession of arms, appellant had fetched knife which had wooden handle and 17 centimeter long blade portion with which fatal injury was caused on left side of chest of deceased. Hence, it is not case where appellant is entitled to alteration of sentence from s. 302 of IPC to s. 304 Part II of IPC. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Sentence imposed on accused cannot be reduced when prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
