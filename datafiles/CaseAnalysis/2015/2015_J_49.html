Joshi Technologies International Inc. v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 365; (2015) 7 SCC 728; [2015] 374 ITR 322; JT 2015 (5) SC 372; 2015 (4) MLJ 739; 2015(6) SCALE 632; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 1042; [2015] 232 TAXMAN 201

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Signing, Admissibility, Assam Land & Revenue Regulation

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Contract & Commercial - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 42, 42(1)(b), 42(1)(c) - Deduction disallowed - Absence of stipulation in agreement - Justification - Appellant entered into two contracts with respondent in year 1992 relating to exploration of certain oil fields - Appellant filed income tax returns and claimed benefit of s. 42 of Act - Income Tax Authorities extended benefit of granting deductions u/s. 42 of Act from year 2001- 2002 onwards when appellant commenced commercial production, however, in A/Y. 2005-2006, AO denied grant of benefit on ground that no such provisions was made in agreements which were signed between Central Govt. and appellant - Further, as no clarification was received by authority, AO disallowed claim for deduction u/s. 42(1)(b) and 42(1)(c) of Act - Further, order of AO was affirmed by HC - Whether appellant is entitled to avail benefit granted u/s. 42 of Act.

Held, conditions mentioned in s. 42 of Act are not fulfilled. In two production sharing contracts (PSCs), no provision is made for making admissible allowances to assessee. It is obvious that AO could not have granted these allowances/deductions to assessee in absence of such stipulations, mandatory requirement, in PSCs. Further, appellant is conscious of this position. Hence, appellant is not entitled to avail benefit granted u/s. 42 of Act. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Contract & Commercial - Execution of contract - Stipulation related to allowance - Aid of other agreement - Whether Model Production Sharing Contract (MPSC) can be read as part of and incorporated in PSCs.

Held, intention expressed in PSC's are just contrary. It is rather made crystal clear in agreement that agreement is sole repository of terms on which it is signed and nothing else would be looked into for this purpose. Further, intention behind clauses of agreement is more than apparent, namely, not to look into any other document or correspondence which took place between parties prior to signing of this agreement. Further, understanding between parties is to be ignored as well. Hence, it is impermissible for appellant to take aid of MPSC or clauses contained therein while construing terms of PSCs. Hence, it is not open to Income Tax Authorities to go beyond stipulations contained in PSCs while making assessment and had to exclusively remain within provisions of agreement. Hence, AO had no option but to deny benefit of deductions/allowances claimed by appellant in its income tax returns filed for A/Y. 2005-2006. Appeal dismissed.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 42 - Intention to grant benefit of deduction - Determination - Whether there was any intention between contracting parties for giving benefit of deductions u/s. 42 of Act.

Held, question of any intention to contrary between parties does not arise because clause of agreement specifically supersedes any understanding between parties prior to effective date of this contract. Further, SC held that when in law no such deduction was permissible as per PSCs in present form, even if such deduction was given wrongly in earlier years that would not amount to wrong act on part of Income Tax Authorities, hence, it would not ensure to benefit of appellant in A/Y. in question as well. Further, with respect to letters written by authority to appellant seeking its clarification, SC held that respondent has accepted that intention between parties was to give benefit of allowances u/s. 42 of Act to appellant and there was request to authority to give its nod for amending contract by incorporating such provision which was allegedly left out inadvertently. Hence, SC held that approach of HC in dealing with this aspect may not be entirely correct ad respondent no. 1 neither disowned those letters nor tried to explain away those letters, however, it is only PSCs signed between parties, which can be looked into. Further, SC held that it is not fit case where HC should have exercised discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Hence, SC held that appellant is not entitled to claim benefit u/s. 42 of Act. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Benefit of deduction cannot be availed by party when there is no stipulation with respect to same mentioned in agreement entered by parties.
