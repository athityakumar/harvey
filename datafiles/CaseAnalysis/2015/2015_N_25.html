Sumer Builders Private Limited v Narendra Gorani

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 735; (2016) 2 SCC 582; 2016(3) ALL MR 431; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 706; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 183; 2015 (4) CompLJ 382; 2016 (4) CTC 777; JT 2015 (11) SC 32; 2016 (5) MahLJ 289; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 102; 2015(10) SCALE 752; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specific Performance, Arbitration Clause

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Land Dispute - Territorial Jurisdiction to entertain claim - Determination - Parties entered in agreement for development of land of respondent - Respondent handed over vacant possession of land to enable appellant to start development work - However, dispute arose with regard to payment and respondent terminated MoU, forfeited security deposit and invoked arbitration clause - Further, appellant filed application u/s. 9 of Act before HC of Bombay for grant of leave asserting that courts at Bombay have territorial jurisdiction, and accordingly leave was granted - Further, Lower Court revoked leave on ground that disputes in development agreement are closely connected with land, possession of which is disputed by parties and suit is suit for land, hence, it should be in Indore where land is situated - Order passed by lower Court is confirmed by DB - Appellant contended that relief sought inasmuch as it was money claim and when amount was paid at Mumbai, HC of Mumbai had jurisdiction - Whether impugned order passed by lower Courts with respect to territorial jurisdiction is in accordance with law.

Held, on scrutiny of agreement and MoU it is clear that development agreement had created certain interests in land in favour of appellant. Assertions made in application along with relief clause when read in entirety, it becomes clear that core dispute pertains to possession of land, for appellant claims to be in exclusive possession and respondent, per contra, has asseverated that it had taken over possession. Hence, there has to be determination as regards possession and issue of direction for recovery of possession. Hence, conclusion arrived at by DB on basis of scrutiny of documents that dispute is embedded with regard to possession of land because fundamental claim pertains to certain constructed space on land, hence, it would conceptually fall within conception of 'suit for land' appearing in Clause Letters Patent is unexceptionable. SC held that it is difficult to accept submission that it is money claim, hence, Bombay HC would also have territorial jurisdiction. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Disputes in development agreement are closely connected with land, possession of which is disputed by parties, hence, suit for same should be filed where land is situated.
