State of Karnataka etc. v Pro Lab and others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 61; (2015) 8 SCC 557; AIR 2015 SC 1098; 2015 (321) E.L.T. 366; JT 2015 (2) SC 81; 2015(1) SCALE 796; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 808; [2015] 78 VST 451

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Deemed Sale, Building Contract, Government of India Act, 1935, Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, Transfer Of Property In Goods, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Supply Of Goods, Processing, Photographer, Karnataka State Laws Act, 2004, Photograph service, Imposition of sales tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, sch. VI, Entry 25 - Karnataka State Laws Act, 2004, s. 2(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366 - Photograph service - Imposition of sales tax - Legality - Entry 25 of sch. VI to 1957 Act was inserted in 1957 Act by an amendment through 2004 Act which came into effect from 1-7-1989, thereby providing levy of tax for processing and supply of photographs, photo prints and photo negatives - That amendment was challenged before HC by respondents - HC by impugned judgment declared the said amendment as unconstitutional - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the Entry 25 of sch. VI to the Act was Constitutionally valid.

Held, SC specifically commented that after 46th Amendment, State is empowered to levy sales tax on the material used even in those contracts where 'the dominant intention of the contract is the rendering of a service, which will amount to a Works Contract'. It is amply clear SC was of the firm view that two Judges Bench judgment in Rainbow Colour Lab's case, 2000 Indlaw SC 605, did not lay down the correct law as it referred to pre 46th Amendment judgments in arriving at its conclusions which had lost their validity. A three member Bench of SC in M/s Larsen Toubro and another v. State of Karnataka and another, 2013 Indlaw SC 637, stated that ACC Ltd. v. Commissioner of Customs, 2001 Indlaw SC 451, had expressly over-ruled Rainbow Colour Lab and Another v. State of Madhya Pradesh and others, 2000 Indlaw SC 605, while holding that dominant intention test was no longer good test after 46th Constitutional Amendment. The argument of respondent that ACC Ltd. case did not over-rule Rainbow Colour Lab's case is, therefore, clearly misconceived. The obvious conclusion would be that Entry 25 of sch. VI to 1957 Act which makes that part of processing and supplying of photographs, photo prints and photo negatives, which have 'goods' component exigible to sales tax is constitutionally valid. Respondent argued that the processing of photographs etc. was essentially a service, wherein the cost of paper, chemical or other material used in processing and developing photographs, photo prints etc. was negligible. This argument, however, is founded on dominant intention theory which has been repeatedly rejected by SC as no more valid in view of 46th Amendment to the Constitution. Entry 25 was inserted for the first time by amendment of 1957 Act with effect from 1-7-1989. This amendment was post 46th Constitutional Amendment. However, the HC of Karnataka declared the said Entry to be unconstitutional and the SLP was also dismissed. Undoubtedly, it was because of the judgment in Rainbow Colour Lab 2000 Indlaw SC 605, which judgment was declared as not a good law in ACC Ltd. case (which position is repeated in BSNL as well as M/s Larsen and Toubro cases 2013 Indlaw SC 637). Thus, the very basis on which Entry 25 of sch. VI was declared as unconstitutional, has been found to be erroneous. In such circumstances, the legislature will be justified in enacting the law from the date when such a law was passed originally and that date is 1-7-1989 in the instant case. Keeping in mind the fact that on the basis of this amendment, there have been assessments made by the assessing authorities. This was admitted by the respondents. HC in the impugned judgment has not dealt with the mater in its correct perspective. The reason given by the HC in invalidating Entry 25 is that this provision was already held unconstitutional by the said HC in Keshoram's case 1999 Indlaw KAR 254 against which the SLP was also dismissed and in view of that decision, it was not permissible for the legislature to re-enact the said Entry by applying a different legal principle. This was clearly an erroneous approach to deal with the issue and the judgment of the HC is clearly unsustainable. The HC did not even deal with various facets of the issue in their correct perspective, in the light of subsequent judgments of SC with specific rulings that Rainbow Colour Lab 2000 Indlaw SC 605 is no longer a good law. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside and petitions filed by respondents in the HC are dismissed holding that Entry 25 of sch. VI of 1957 Ac is constitutionally valid. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Subject to Constitutional restrictions, a power to legislate includes a power to legislate prospectively as well as retrospectively.
