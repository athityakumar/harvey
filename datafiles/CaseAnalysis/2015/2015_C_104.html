Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai-IV v Fitrite Packers, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 679; 2015 (324) E.L.T. 625; JT 2015 (9) SC 554; 2015(10) SCALE 513; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 851

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Manufacturing process - Transformation of original article - Adjudicating authority issued orders holding that printing of GI paper would be treated as manufacture, and as such, assessee was liable to pay excise duty thereon - However, Tribunal held that printing is only incidental and primary use of GI printing paper roll is for wrapping which is not changed by process of printing and that process of printing of GI paper does not amount to manufacture - Hence, instant appeal by revenue - Whether printing on duty paid GI paper would amount to manufacture.

Held, even though the paper in-question was meant for wrapping and said end use remained the same even after printing, yet, after the printing of logo and name of the specific product of 'Parle' thereupon, the end use was confined to only that particular and specific product of the said particular company/customer. The printing, therefore, is not merely a value addition but has transformed from general wrapping paper to special wrapping paper and in that sense, the end use has positively been changed as a result of printing process undertaken by assessee. Hence, process of said particular kind of printing has resulted into a product, i.e., paper with distinct character and use of its own which it did not bear earlier. The 'test of no commercial user without further process' is satisfied in instant case and so, appeal is allowed and order of Tribunal is set aside and Order-in-Original passed by Adjudicating Authority is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - To bring the process within the definition of 'manufacture' u/s.2(f) of the Act, it is essential that there should be a transformation by which something new and different comes into being, i.e., there emerge an article which has distinctive name, character or use.
