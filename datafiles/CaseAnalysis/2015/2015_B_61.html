Cricket Association of Bihar and another v Board of Control for Cricket in India and another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 44; (2015) 3 SCC 251; AIR 2015 SC 3194; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 111; 2015 (3) CompLJ 451; JT 2015 (1) SC 526; 2015 (1) MLJ 711; 2015(1) SCALE 608; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Rules Of Evidence, Conflict Of Interest, Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, Tamil Nadu Registration of Societies Act, 1975, BCCI Regulations, Amendment of Rules, Indian Finance Corporation Act, 1948, IPL Anti-Corruption Code, IPL Operational Rules, IPL Regulations, IPL spot fixing and betting, Violation of Operational Rules, Duty of overseeing the tournament

Summary: (A)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - BCCI Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - IPL Operational Rules, rr. 2.2, 2.3 - IPL Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - Tamil Nadu Registration of Societies Act 1975 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 226 - IPL spot fixing and betting - Violation of Operational Rules - Legality - Respondent (BCCI) was a Society registered under 1975 Act, respondent no. 2 was President of the BCCI besides being the Vice-Chairman and Managing Director of respondent no. 3 (India Cements Limited) - BCCI taken a decision to launch Indian Premier League (IPL) to be run by a Committee constituted by general body of BCCI to be called IPL Governing Council - Respondent no. 2 was elected as Secretary of BCCI in a General Body Meeting - In the same meeting regn. 6.2.4 of the IPL Regulations was amended to exclude from its operation events like IPL and Champions' League twenty-20 - On allegations of spot fixing/betting FIR was registered and son-in-law of respondent no. 2 'GN' was allegedly involved in spot fixing/betting - Appellant (association) filed PIL before HC, for a declaration that appointment of 2-member Probe Commission by BCCI was ultra vires of the Rules and Regulations of BCCI and for a mandamus directing constitution of a panel comprising retired Judges to hold an enquiry against among others 'GN' for his involvement in betting and spot fixing - Further appellant sought to terminate the franchise agreement entered into between BCCI and Chennai Super Kings and Rajasthan Royals and to institution of disciplinary proceedings against respondent no. 2 and his suspension and restraining him from contesting the election for the post of President BCCI - HC by impugned judgment, partly allowed petition declaring probe commission set up by BCCI was not valid and was in violation of IPL Operational Rules - Aggrieved appellant and BCCI filed instant appeals -Whether BCCI was 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 of Constitution and if it was not, whether it was amenable to writ jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 of Constitution.

Held, it is common ground that BCCI has a complete sway over the game of cricket in this country. It regulates and controls the game to the exclusion of all others. It formulates rules, regulations norms and standards covering all aspect of the game. It enjoys the power of choosing the members of the national team and the umpires. It exercises the power of disqualifying players which may at times put an end to the sporting career of a person. It spends crores of rupees on building and maintaining infrastructure like stadia, running of cricket academies and Supporting State Associations. It frames pension schemes and incurs expenditure on coaches, trainers etc. It sells broadcast and telecast rights and collects admission fee to venues where the matches are played. All these activities are undertaken with the tacit concurrence of the State Govt. and the GOI who are not only fully aware but supportive of the activities of the BCCI. The State has not chosen to bring any law or taken any other step that would either deprive or dilute the BCCI's monopoly in the field of cricket. The functions of the BCCI are clearly public functions, which, till such time the State intervenes to takeover the same, remain in the nature of public functions, no matter discharged by a society registered under 1975 Act. If the Govt. not only allows an autonomous/private body to discharge functions which it could in law takeover or regulate but even lends its assistance to such a non-Govt. body to undertake such functions which by their very nature are public functions, it cannot be said that the functions are not public functions or that the entity discharging the same is not answerable on the standards generally applicable to judicial review of State action. BCCI may not be State u/art. 12 of Constitution but is certainly amenable to writ jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Appeals disposed of.

(B)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - IPL Operational Rules, rr. 2.2, 2.3 - IPL spot fixing and betting by team official - Legality - Whether 'GM', son-in-law of respondent no. 2 and 'RK' of Rajasthan Royals team were 'team officials' of their respective IPL teams.

Held, Probe Committee, constituted by SC order dt.8-10-2013, has correctly appreciated the facts as emerging from the documents and the depositions of witnesses recorded by it and rightly come to the conclusion that 'GM' was a team official of CSK. That is so especially when India Cements Ltd. who owns the team made a candid admission before Court that 'GM' was indeed a team official within the meaning of that expression under the Rules. Court, therefore, see no real, much less compelling reason, for Court to disagree or reverse the finding recorded by the Probe Committee on that aspect. Probe Committee held that 'GM' was indulging in betting. The material assembled in the course of the investigation by the Probe Committee provides a reasonably safe basis for holding that the accusations made against 'GM' stood established on a preponderance of probabilities. The finding is by no means without basis or perverse to call for our interference with the same. Probe Committee found 'RK' to be a team official of Rajasthan Royals. Even according to the concessions made by counsel appearing for 'RK' Jaipur IPL Cricket Private Ltd. he was a duly accredited team official. Admittedly, 'RK' was heard by the Committee before concluding that he had indulged in betting. So long as Probe Committee has conducted the proceedings in consonance with the principles of natural justice, the Committee's finding that 'RK' was a team official of Rajasthan Royals and that he had indulged in betting cannot be faulted. Appeals disposed of.

(C)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - BCCI President - Conflict of interest - Legality - Whether allegations of cover up, leveled against respondent no. 2 stand proved.

Held, allegation that an effort was made to suppress facts before the Probe Committee or that 'GM' was shown only as a cricket enthusiast whereas he was a team official, may, at best, raise a suspicion against respondent no. 2 but suspicion can hardly be taken as proof to hold him guilty of the alleged cover up. Court cannot, therefore, with any amount of certainty, say that the charge of attempted cover up leveled against respondent no. 2 stands proved. Appeals disposed of.

(D)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - BCCI Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - IPL Operational Rules, rr. 2.2, 2.3 - IPL Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - IPL administrators - Commercial interest - Legality - Whether regn. 6.2.4 to the extent it permits administrators to have commercial interest in the IPL, Champions League and Twenty-20 events was legally bad.

Held, there is no manner of doubt that the investment made by India Cements Ltd. is a business investment no matter in a sporting activity. To the extent the business investment has come from India Cements Ltd. promoted by respondent no. 2 and his family, India Cements and everyone connected with it as shareholders acquire a business/commercial interest in the IPL events organised by BCCI. Whether or not a player who is an 'administrator' by reason of an existing or earlier assignment held by him can acquire or hold a commercial interest in any BCCI event, will depend upon the nature of the interest that such person has acquired and whether the same is purely professional or has any commercial element to it. Beyond that Court do not propose to say anything at this stage. Amendment to r. 6.2.4 of BCCI Regulations permitting Administrators of BCCI to acquire or hold commercial interests in BCCI like IPL, champions league and T-20 held to be bad. Appeals disposed of.

(E)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - IPL Operational Rules, rr. 2.2, 2.3 - IPL Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - Violation of Operational Rules - Duty of overseeing the tournament - Legality - Whether allegations leveled against 'SR', Chief Operating Officer IPL, stand proved? if so, to what effect.

Held, the report submitted by the investigating team and the Probe Committee do not indict Chief Operating Officer IPL in clear words. The observations made regarding his role and conduct simply give rise to a serious suspicion about his involvement in the betting affairs of the team owners/officials apart from suggesting that having received information about betting activities in connection with IPL matches, he remained totally inert in the matter instead of taking suitable action warranted under the circumstances. Court propose to issue appropriate directions regarding further investigation and probe into the activities and conduct of 'SR', Chief Operating Officer IPL. The three-member Committee, shall examine the role of 'SR' Raman with or without further investigation, into his activities, and if found guilty, impose a suitable punishment upon him on behalf of BCCI. Appeals disposed of.

(F)Constitution - Trusts & Associations - IPL Operational Rules, rr. 2.2, 2.3 - IPL Regulations, regn. 6.2.4 - Amendment of rules - Legality - What orders and directions need be passed.

Held, amendment to rule 6.2.4 whereby the words 'excluding events like IPL or Champions League Twenty 20', were added to the said rule is hereby declared void and ineffective. Impugned judgment and order of HC in is resultantly set aside and PIL is allowed. The quantum of punishment to be imposed on 'GM' and 'RK' as also their respective franchisees/teams/owners of the teams shall be determined by a Committee comprising former Chief Justice of India, as Chairman and two former Judge of SC as Members. The Committee shall, before taking a final view on the quantum of punishment to be awarded, issue notice to all those likely to be affected and provide to them a hearing in the matter. Appeals disposed of.
