Chandra Babu @ Moses v State, through Inspector of Police and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 474; (2015) 8 SCC 774; AIR 2015 SC 3566; 2015 CRLJ 4538; 2015 (5) CTC 214; 2015 (1) JCC 2400; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2518; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 597; 2015 (4) RLW 2844; 2015(7) SCALE 529; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 1002

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Protest

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 307, 323, 324, 341 - Order of re-investigation - Re-investigation by other agency - Justification - Appellant filed FIR u/ss. 147, 148, 341, 324, 323 and 307 of IPC alleging assault by accused persons due to business rivalry - final report was filed stating that case was mistake of fact which was accepted by Trial Court - Protest petition was filed by appellant to re-open case and file fresh report which was rejected - Further, after remitting matter, it was held by Trial Court that investigation by Inspector had been conducted in biased manner and ordered re- investigation by other agency - However, order passed by Trial Court was rejected by HC - Whether order of Lower court to direct re-investigation of case of appellant by other agency is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Magistrate can disagree with police report and take cognizance and issue process and summons to accused. Hence, Magistrate has jurisdiction to ignore opinion expressed by investigating officer and independently apply his mind to facts that have emerged from investigation. Magistrate has basically directed for further investigation and said part of order cannot be found fault with, but eloquent one, he could not have directed another investigating agency to investigate as that would not be within sphere of further investigation and, in any case, he does not have jurisdiction to direct reinvestigation by another agency. Hence, it is directed that investigating agency that had investigated shall carry on further investigation and such investigation shall be supervised by concerned Superintendent of Police and after investigation, report shall be submitted before Magistrate who shall deal with same in accordance with law. Hence, order passed by HC is set aside except where it has held that learned Magistrate could not have allowed another agency to investigate. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Magistrate has authority to re-investigate case, however, it has no authority to direct re-investigation by another agency.
