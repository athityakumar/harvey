National Highways Authority of India v ITD Cementation India Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 297; 2015(5) ALL MR 484; 2015 (111) ALR 490; 2015(5) SCALE 554; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Karnataka Mine and Minerals Concession Rules, 1994, UP State Rules, 1963

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Arbitrator & ADR - Change in legislation - Increase in royalty fees - Claim for increased rate - Contact was awarded to respondent by appellant on 17-10-2001 for execution of work of widening of lanes and rehabilitation of existing two lane carriageway - Further, State Govt. by issuing notification increased Royalty charges on raw material - Respondent requested appellant to increase price of being paid to respondent, however same was rejected on ground that increase in royalty charge cannot be paid separately as same was already considered under Price Adjustment formula being paid for general materials under clause - However, during course of audit, payment made by appellant towards increase in Royalty charges was considered to be irregular by Govt. auditors on ground that no such separate payment was required to be made under contract as payment was already made under Price Adjustment formula for price increase and matter was place before Tribunal - Tribunal held that respondent had incurred additional cost because of change in rates of royalty fee pursuant to change in legislation and that said increase in rates had not been taken into account in indexing of any inputs to price adjustment formula in general materials, hence, respondent would be entitled to be paid additional cost incurred by him - Order passed by Tribunal was affirmed by HC - Whether respondent was entitled to be paid additional cost incurred by him due to change in legislation.

Held, provision for cost escalation based on agreed Price Adjustment Formulae and Compensation for additional cost resulting from subsequent legislation are two separate & specific stipulations, and claimant is entitled to be compensated for any additional cost caused to it provided same shall not have already been taken into account in indexing of any inputs to Price Adjustment Formulae in accordance with provisions of contract. SC held that construction of terms of contract is primarily for arbitrator to decide. He is entitled to take view which he holds to be correct one after considering material before him and after interpreting provisions of contract and court while considering challenge to arbitral award does not sit in appeal over findings and decisions unless arbitrator construes contract in such way that no fair minded or reasonable person could do. Hence, SC held that construction of terms of contract by Tribunal is completely consistent with principles laid down by SC. Hence, order passed by HC confirming order of Tribunal is justified and in accordance with law. SC directed that money deposited by respondent was allowed to be withdrawn on furnishing of Bank Guarantees. Bank guarantees so furnished stand discharged. Further, with respect to claim of reimbursement of cess, SC held that while quoting initial rates and prices, it would not have been in contemplation of party as to framework of any revision in rates of royalty at future date and clause in agreement can be said to have covered such eventualities. Hence, order passed by Tribunal and HC in favor of respondent for reimbursement of cess is justified. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957- Payment of royalty on ordinary earth - Issuance of Notification - Claim of refund - Respondent was demanded to pay royalty on ordinary earth which was paid by respondent - Further, Central Govt. vide notification dt. 3-2-2000 declared ordinary earth as minor mineral - Tribunal held that that after the commencement of Act it was not within the powers of State Govt. to have framed State Rules and consequently such Rules were not binding on Contractor and imposition of royalty by State Govt. vide notification dt. 20-3-2001, being after Central Government's notification dt. 3-2-2000, created a liability to pay royalty and since liability was validly created after contract was entered into, matter was covered under contract - Order passes by Tribunal is confirmed by HC - Whether respondent is entitled to refund of royalty on ordinary earth.

Held, State Govt. was levying royalty on ordinary earth and this situation was obtaining on such date. If State Govt. lacked power to levy and collect such royalty prior to notification dt. 3-2-2000 whereby ordinary earth was brought under definition of minor mineral, such ground may certainly entitle party to lay requisite challenge before appropriate forum. However, for purposes of contract such levy being existing levy must be deemed to have been part of rates or prices quoted. Further, by notification dt. 20-3-2001, same rate was maintained and as such there was no change arising due to any subsequent legislation. Hence, matter was outside scope of contract. Tribunal ought to have confined itself to terms of Contract and see if there was any variation for purposes of clause and it went beyond its powers in holding that existing levy as on date contract was entered into was without any authority in law and as such imposition by notification dt. 20-3-2001 created liability for first time. Hence, SC held that Tribunal went beyond scope of contract and it clearly exceeded its jurisdiction. Hence, order passed by Tribunal is set aside. Appellant is entitled to encash that bank guarantee to recover sum that was deposited.

Ratio - Agreement entered by parties during execution of contract determines rights and liabilities of parties.
