Chennai Properties & Investments Limited, Chennai v Commissioner of Income Tax Central III, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 313; 2015(6) SCALE 9; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 413; [2015] 231 TAXMAN 336

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Rental income - Appellant company, main objective, was to acquire the properties in the city of Chennai and to let out those properties - Assessee had rented out such properties and the rental income received therefrom was shown as income from business in the return filed by the assessee - Assessing officer refuse to tax the same as business income and held that it would be treated as income from house property and taxed the same accordingly under that Head - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed - Tribunal upheld said order - Appeal filed before HC was allowed - HC held that the income derived by letting out of the properties would not be income from business but could be assessed only income from house property - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the income derived by the company from letting out this property is to be treated as income from business or it is to be treated as rental income from house property.

Held, main object of the appellant company is to acquire and hold the properties and to let out those properties as well as make advances upon the security of lands and buildings or other properties or any interest therein. Thus, there is no other income of the assessee except the income from letting out of these two properties. Hence, letting of the properties is in fact is the business of the assessee. The assessee therefore, rightly disclosed the income under the Head Income from Business. It cannot be treated as 'income from the house property'.

Appeals disposed of.
