Saroj Kumar v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 572; AIR 2015 SC 3668; 2015 (5) AWC 4353; 2015 (147) FLR 10; JT 2015 (7) SC 267; 2015 (6) MLJ 622; 2015(9) SCALE 35; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 171; 2015 (5) SLR 482

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Promotion - Downgrading of marks - Appellant approached Tribunal with grievance that ignoring his candidature, juniors to him were promoted and later, in pursuance of orders of Tribunal, respondent authorities communicated annual confidential reports (ACRs) to appellant and later, appellant submitted representation to respondent authorities against downgrading of remarks but the same was rejected and ACRs were upheld - Appellant again approached Tribunal challenging rejection of representation by respondent authorities and Tribunal again reemitted matter back to respondent authorities - Aggrieved, respondent authorities filed petition before HC but the same was dismissed - However, respondents after considering matter afresh, again found no substance in representation and rejected the same vide order dt.23-3-2011 - Appellant again approached Tribunal challenging order dt.23-3-2011 passed by authorities and Tribunal allowed application and set aside impugned order therein with direction to respondent to reconstitute Review DPC and reconsider case of appellant for promotion - Aggrieved, respondents approached HC and HC vide impugned order, allowed the same - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned order of HC could be upheld.

Held, HC has rightly taken note of the fact that on conclusion of second round of litigation neither there was direction by the Tribunal nor by HC to ignore the entries in question of promotion of the appellant from the date when his juniors were promoted. In instant round of litigation, Tribunal erred in directing authorities to consider the case of the appellant for promotion from the date when his juniors were promoted, ignoring the remarks, which had been communicated after first round of litigation. Further, after ACRs have been communicated and representation has been rejected, Tribunal should not have treated the remarks as not communicated. Hence, impugned order of HC is upheld. Appeal dismissed.
