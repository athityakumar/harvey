Rajbala and others v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 853; (2016) 2 SCC 445; AIR 2016 SC 33; 2016 (1) AWC 775; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4169; 2016 (1) MLJ 351; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 371; 2015(13) SCALE 424; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 1106

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Citizenship, Arbitrariness, Election Commission, Advocates Act, 1961, State Legislature, Scheduled Castes, Local Authorities, Government of India Act, 1935, Elections, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, Panchayats, Constitutionality, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Haryana Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, representation of the People Act, 1950, Presidency-Towns Insolvency Act, 1909, Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, Representation of the People (Third Amendment) Act, 2002, Kerala Education Bill, 1957, Haryana Panchayat Raj (Amendment) Ordinance, 2015, Haryana Panchayati Raj (Amendment) Act, 2015

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Haryana Panchayati Raj (Amendment) Act, 2015 - Restriction placed by legislature - Alleged Violation of rights - Determination - Impugned Act in question was enacted to bring then existing law governing Panchayats in State in tune with Constitution as amended by 73rd amendment - Further, by impugned Act , five categories of person was rendered incapable of contesting elections for any one of elected offices under Act - Further, respondent no.2 issued notification specifying election schedule for Panchayats of Haryana - Petitioner contended that restrictions placed by impugned Act is not justified and is arbitrary in nature - Further, it is alleged that impugned Act violates rights of citizen under Constitution - Whether impugned Act is constitutionally valid.

Held, People'S Union Of Civil Liberties (P.U.C.L.) & Anr. v Union Of India & Anr. 2003 Indlaw SC 290, relied on SC held that every citizen has Constitutional right to elect and to be elected to either Parliament or State legislatures. Further, right to contest for seat in either of two bodies is subject to certain Constitutional restrictions and could be restricted further only by law made by Parliament. Further, SC held that right to vote and right to contest at election to Panchayat are Constitutional rights subsequent to introduction of Part IX of Constitution. SC held that both rights can be regulated by appropriate Legislature directly. Parliament can indirectly curtail only right to contest by prescribing disqualifications for membership of Legislature of State. Further, with respect to violation of art. 14 of Constitution, SC held that courts do not undertake task of declaring piece of legislation un-constitutional on ground that legislation is 'arbitrary' since such exercise implies value judgment and courts do not examine wisdom of legislative choices unless legislation is otherwise violative of some specific provision of Constitution. Further, to undertake such examination would amount to virtually importing doctrine of 'substantive due process'. A.K. Gopalan v The State Of Madras.Union Of India: Intervener, 1950 Indlaw SC 42 relied on, SC held that it is not permissible Courts to declare statute un-constitutional on ground that it is 'arbitrary'. Further, with respect to educational qualification imposed by impugned Act, SC held that impugned provision creates two classes of voters i.e. those who are qualified by virtue of their educational accomplishment to contest elections to Panchayats and those who are not. SC held that proclaimed object of such classification is to ensure that those who seek election to Panchayats have some basic education which enables them to more effectively discharge various duties which befall elected representatives of Panchayats. SC held that object sought to be achieved cannot be said to be irrational or illegal or unconnected with scheme and purpose of ACT or provisions of Part IX of Constitution. Hence, prescription of educational qualification is not irrelevant for better administration of Panchayats. Further, classification cannot be said either based on no intelligible differentia unreasonable or without reasonable nexus with object sought to be achieved. Further, with respect to disqualification with respect to persons who are in arrears of amounts to cooperative bodies, SC held that Constitution makers recognised indebtedness as factor which is incompatible in certain circumstances with right to hold elected office under Constitution. Hence, there is no illegality if legislature declares people who are indebted to cooperative bodies or in arrears of electricity bills to be ineligible to become elected representatives of people in Panchayats. Further, it is held that insolvency is field over which both Parliament as well as legislatures of State have legislative competence concurrently to make laws. Further, with respect to disqualification of person from contesting election to Panchayat if such person has no functional toilet at his place of residence, SC held that those who aspire to get elected to those civic bodies and administer them must set example for others. Further, if legislature stipulates that those who are not following basic norms of hygiene are ineligible to become administrators of civic body and disqualifies them as class from seeking election to civic body, such policy, can neither be said to create class based on unintelligible criteria nor can such classification be said to be unconnected with object sought to be achieved by ACT. Order passed by first judge is confirmed by second judge of bench. Hence, impugned Act is constitutionally valid. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Right to contest for seat is subject to certain Constitutional restrictions and could be restricted further only by law made by Parliament, hence, Act providing restriction cannot be challenged.
