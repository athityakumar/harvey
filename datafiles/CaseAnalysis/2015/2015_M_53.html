Makhan Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 476; 2015 CRLJ 3282; 2015 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 100; JT 2015 (4) SC 222; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2149; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 631; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 834; 2015(5) SCALE 430; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 50 - Seizure of drugs from vehicle - Conviction - Justification - On day of incident, police officials during patrolling saw vehicle driven by appellant - Further, during search of vehicle, police found three bags of drugs lying in vehicle - Charge sheet was filed u/s. 15 of Act - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 15 of Act which was affirmed by HC and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for ten years and to pay fine - Whether conviction of appellant by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, prosecution misdirected itself by unnecessarily focusing on s. 50 of Act, when fact is that recovery has been made not from person of appellant but from vehicle which was allegedly driven by appellant, hence, s. 50 of Act had no application at all. Prosecution ought to have endeavoured to prove whether appellant had some nexus with seized vehicle. SC held that though police has seized vehicle, prosecution has not adduced any evidence either by examining neighbours or others to bring home point that appellant was owner or possessor of vehicle. PW6 admitted in his cross examination that signature or thumb impression of appellant was not obtained on recovery memo. SC held that courts below erred in attributing to appellant onus to prove that wherefrom vehicle had come, especially when ownership/ possession of vehicle has not been proved by prosecution. SC held that since vehicle was searched and contraband was seized from vehicle, compliance with s. 50 of Act was not required. In absence of independent evidence connecting appellant with vehicle, mere compliance with s. 50 of Act by itself would not be sufficient to establish guilt of appellant. SC held that when independent witnesses PW1 and DW2 have not supported prosecution case and recovery of contraband has not been satisfactorily proved, conviction of appellant u/s. 15 of Act cannot be sustained. Hence, conviction of appellant and sentence imposed on him is set aside and appeal is allowed. Fine amount if paid, is ordered to be refunded to appellant. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In cases under Act, since punishment is severe, hence, strict proof is required for proving search, seizure and recovery.
