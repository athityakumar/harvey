State of West Bengal and another v Calcutta Mineral Supply Company Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 327; (2015) 8 SCC 655; 2015 (2) CalLT(SC) 38; 2015 (4) MLJ 499; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 715; 2015(6) SCALE 150; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Garden, Leases, Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, Salami, West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953, West Bengal Land Reforms Act, West Bengal Estates Acquisition Rules, 1954, WBEA Act

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 4, 5, 59, 6(3) - West Bengal Land Reforms Act, s. 14Z - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Rules, 1954 - Lease period - Payment of Salami - DB of HC allowed writ petitions preferred by respondents against decision of Tribunal dismissing their original applications moved against respective order passed by Government resuming lands held by them - HC disposed of three writ petitions primarily observing that although facts were different there were certain communions of identity within question to be answered and certain common principles of law were involved in writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, HC has misconstrued and misinterpreted the relevant provisions contained in the Rules viz-a-viz the condition of renewal as contained in cl. 16(a) of the lease deed. The HC has committed error of law in holding that the amendment brought about could not be given retrospective effect to affect the right of the lessee/transferee stepping into the shoes of the transferee/lessee to obtain further renewal of lease for a further period of 30 years and to successive renewals for similar periods. The HC is not correct in law in holding that the amended clause would have retrospective operation. Indisputably, the renewal of lease is a fresh grant where the principal lease executed between the parties containing a clause that the lease shall have to be renewed by giving a fresh grant in accordance with the said clause. In the instant case, as per cl. 16(a) of the earlier lease deed, the lease is to be renewed for a further period of 30 years but subject to the rules and the terms and conditions of the lease and also such other terms and conditions as the State Government may from time to time consider it necessary to impose and include in such renewed lease. Cl. 16(a) further provides that additional terms and conditions that may be considered necessary by the State Government be included but the same shall not be inconsistent with the law renewing such lease and shall not have retrospective effect.

Admittedly, before the expiry of the lease in question in 1998, the respondent/transferee stepped into the shoes of the original lessee in the year 1990. Therefore, the respondent shall not be liable to pay salami during the unexpired period of lease up to 1998.

The State Government has rightly not made any claim for salami for the unexpired period of lease, but for the fresh renewal of lease after 1998 which is a fresh grant. The demand of salami by State Government for according sanction for renewal of lease cannot and shall not by any stretch of imagination be held to be retrospective. Respondent is liable to pay salami which is one of the conditions of the Rules for the purpose of renewal of lease. The demand made by the Collector is fully justified. It is also not in dispute that at all point of time the respondent-company was holding the land of factory within the ceiling limit as provided under the 1953 Act and the Act. HC rightly set aside order dt. 29-7-2011 passed by the Special Secretary upholding the notice issued by the Sub-Divisional, Land and Land Reforms Officer. Appeals disposed of
