Vikram Singh @ Vicky and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R.K. Agrawal, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 617; (2015) 9 SCC 502; AIR 2015 SC 3577; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3715; 2015 CRLJ 4500; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 85; 2016 (1) RLW 148; 2015(9) SCALE 183; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kidnapping, Life Imprisonment, Capital Punishment, Jurisprudence, Assembly, Death Sentence, Rarest Of Rare, Ransom, Imprisonment For Life, Interfere, Distinct, Association, Criminal Law (Amendment) Bill, 1992, Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Bill, 1994, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1993

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 129, 142, 226 - Death penalty - Kidnap - Appellants were tried, convicted and sentenced to death for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 364A of IPC and same was affirmed by HC and also by SC - However, appellants again approached SC through writ petition but the same was withdrawn and then moved HC praying inter alia for mandamus striking down s. 364A of IPC and order restraining execution of death sentence awarded to them - DB of HC dismissed said petition and hence, instant appeal - Whether petition is maintainable.

Held, contention of appellants that s. 364A was not attracted in instant case was examined and rejected in appeal filed by them by SC and so long as that rejection holds the field, there is no room for SC or any other court to take a contrary view. Hence, writ petition filed by appellants to the extent the same sought to urge that s. 364A was not attracted to their case was, thus, not maintainable in law. Further, the only remedy which appellants could resort to in terms of the view taken by SC Rupa Ashok Hurra's case is by invoking inherent powers of SC u/art.129 and 142 of Constitution for recall, reversal or modification of the order passed by SC in criminal appeal filed by appellants. A writ petition before HC for that relief was clearly untenable in law. Further, language employed in s. 364A is wide enough to cover even cases where the demand for ransom is made not as a part of any terrorist act but also for monetary gain from a private individual. Hence, appeal is dismissed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - If against final judgment of SC, a remedy was not available u/art.32 of Constitution the same would also not be available u/art. 226.
