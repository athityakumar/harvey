State of Madhya Pradesh v Madanlal

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 432; (2015) 7 SCC 681; AIR 2015 SC 3003; 2015 CRLJ 3976; 2015 (3) JabLJ 295; 2015 (1) JCC 2497; JT 2015 (6) SC 8; 2015 (3) KLT 125; 2015 (3) RLW 2168; 2016 (1) RLW 301; 2015(7) SCALE 261; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 998

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 376(2)(f) - Alleged Rape Committed - Conversion of conviction to assault - Justification - Prosecutrix minor girl in search of her mother met accused who committed rape on prosecutrix - FIR was lodged by prosecutrix mother - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 376(2)(f) r/w s. 511 of IPC - However, HC has converted offence to one u/s. 354 of IPC and confined sentence to period of custody already undergone - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Trial Court has scanned evidence and arrived at conclusion that prosecution had been able to bring home charge on base of credible evidence. Further, HC has not at all referred to evidence that has been adduced during trial and has only stated in his judgment that prosecution has examined so many witnesses and filed nine documents. SC held that approach adopted by HC does not satisfy requirement of exercise of appellate jurisdiction. Further, HC has been influenced by compromise that has been entered into between accused and parents of victim as victim was minor, however, trial Judge had rejected compromise application on ground that offence was not compoundable. SC held that in case of rape or attempt of rape, conception of compromise come into picture. Hence, matter has to be remitted to HC for reappraisal of evidence and for fresh decision. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Reversal of order or conversion of offence by higher court should be based on evidence in favor of accused.
