All Kerala Online Lottery Dealers Association v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, H.L. Dattu, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 764; (2016) 2 SCC 161; JT 2015 (11) SC 190; 2015 (8) MLJ 207; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 345; 2015(12) SCALE 419; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Legislative Power, Gambling, Lotteries (Regulation) Act, 1998, Kerala Paper Lotteries (Regulation) Rules, 2005, Lotteries (Regulation) Rules, 2010, British Act, Kerala State Lotteries (Regulation) (Repeal) Rules, 2005, Kerala State Lotteries (Regulation) Rules, 2003, Sikkim Online Network Lottery Rules, 2001, Sikkim Online Network Lottery Rules

Summary: Socio-Economic - Administrative - Lotteries (Regulation) Act, 1998, ss. 4,5,12 - Prohibition of online lottery - Discrimination - Determination - Respondent State vide notification prohibited sale of all computerized and online lottery tickets marketed and operated through vending machines, terminals, electronic machines and tickets sold through internet in respondent State with immediate effect and declared that State shall be free zone from online and internet lotteries - Further, respondent State decided to prohibit sale of all lotteries organized, conducted or promoted by State and by every other State Govt. in respondent State - However, respondent State permitted sale of paper lotteries organized, conducted or promoted by every State Govt. - It is alleged by appellant Association that respondent State vide impugned notification discriminated between paper lotteries and online lotteries - Lower Court dismissed petition - Further, DB vide order dismissed appeal - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Messrs B. R. Enterprises and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others, 1999 Indlaw SC 1635, referred to SC held that State Govt. can separately ban sale of online lotteries as online lottery is particular class of lottery, different and distinct from paper lotteries conduct of certain types of lottery trade in country, malpractices thereof and their impact on poorer sections of society have been under scrutiny of Govt. for quite some time. Further, provisions of Act clearly demonstrate that even though all types of lotteries are meant to be regulated by said Act, online lotteries were not under contemplation of Central Govt. at time when Act came into force. Further, SC held that if Govt. takes decision in public interest to prohibit online lotteries, SC should not interfere with said decision unless there are compelling grounds. Further, SC held that virtue of provisions contained in s. 12 of Act, Centre has delegated its power to legislate with regard to lotteries to States and further that there is specific delegation with regard to ban of lotteries of other States by virtue of provisions contained in s. 5 of Act. Further, delegation of legislative power of principal to delegatee would not amount to abdication of legislative power by Centre and it would not be without any guidelines and would be sustainable in law if concerned State may ban lottery in its own State and of other States as well. Hence, SC held that respondent State may prohibit particular kind of lottery from its own State, it can prohibit sale of such lottery from any other State as it is within its power conferred on it u/s. 5 of Act. Further, SC held that State Govt. can organize, conduct or promote lottery subject to conditions mentioned in s. 4 of Act and if there is any violation of conditions mentioned in s. 4 of Act, it would be always open to State Govt. to prohibit such lottery and that would be within legislative power vested with State u/s. 5 of Act as in that case State would be only complying with provisions of Act made by Parliament. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Petitions dismissed.

Ratio - State Govt. is justified to prohibit particular type of lottery in view of public interest and to reduce gambling.
