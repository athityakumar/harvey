Union of India and others v 3989606 P, EX-Naik Vijay Kumar

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 579; (2015) 10 SCC 460; AIR 2015 SC 3425; 2015 (5) AWC 5405; 2015 (147) FLR 403; JT 2015 (7) SC 573; 2015(9) SCALE 289; 2016 (1) SLR 406

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Disability Pension, Armed Forces, Entitlement Rules for Casualty Pensionary Awards, 1982, Entitlement of

Summary: Defense and Security Forces - Disability pension - Entitlement of - Respondent was enrolled in Indian Army - Respondent fell down and sustained multiple injuries - Respondent was assessed 60% of disability - Respondent was invalidated from service after completion of 17 years of service - Respondent was paid monetary benefits due and payable to him and also other pensionary benefits - Respondent claimed disability pension, which was rejected - Appeal before Appellate Authority - Appeal was dismissed - Respondent filed application before Tribunal - Application was allowed by Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent was entitled for disability pension.

Held, when the opinion of the assessment by the Board is recommendatory in nature and is subject to acceptance by the Pension Sanctioning Authority, the opinion of the Medical Board by itself cannot confer right upon the respondent to claim disability pension. Further, after accident the respondent was treated in the military hospital for three to four months and he was placed in low medical category. The respondent went for six weeks sick leave and reported back for review and invalidated from service. After the accident when the respondent was not actually performing military service, the opinion of the Medical Board 'aggravated due to stress and strain of military service' does not appear to be in proper perspective. After the accident, when the respondent was not actually performing his duties and therefore disability cannot be attributed to military service nor can it be said to have been aggravated due to stress and strain of military service. Thus, it is clear that the injury suffered by the respondent has no causal connection with the military service. The Tribunal failed to appreciate that the accident resulting in injury to the respondent was not even remotely connected to his military duty and it falls in the domain of an entirely private act and therefore the impugned orders cannot be sustained. Hence, impugned order of the Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.
