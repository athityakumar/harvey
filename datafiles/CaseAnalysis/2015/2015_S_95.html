K. Anbazhagan v Selvi J. Jayalalitha and another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 268; (2015) 6 SCC 86; (2015) 6 SCC 156; JT 2015 (4) SC 159; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 740; 2015(5) SCALE 125; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 355

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subordinate, Old Code, Criminal Justice, Civil Case, Advocate General, Government Advocate, Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1978, Developments, Intervene, Karnataka Law Officers (Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1977, Obedience

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 24,25,25A, 301(1) - Appointment of Special Public Prosecutor at trial stage - Authority to appear in appeals - In view of allegations of amassing assets disproportionate to their known sources of income, criminal proceedings were initiated against accused and her associates and Special Courts were constituted by State Govt. for trial of cases against accused persons - While so, after change of Govt. at State, appellant moved transfer petitions in SC seeking transfer of cases pending at court in Chennai to any competent court in any other State and SC allowed transfer petitions and transferred said cases to Karnataka with various directions, inter alia, to constitute Special Court and appoint senior lawyer as Public Prosecutor to conduct transferred cases against accused persons - In pursuance of said directions of SC, State appointed Mr. B.V. Acharya as Public Prosecutor but as he resigned, State issued another notification appointing respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor - Appellant filed objections on appointment of respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor and submitted representations to Govt. and HC with request to remove him and later filed writ petitions before HC challenging appointment of respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor - Pending writ petition, Govt. withdrew respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor and aggrieved by the same, accused persons filed writ petition in SC - Later, SC heard writ petitions and held that order removing respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor is mala fide and not sustainable in law and quashed the same - While so, by judgment dt.27-9-2014, Special Court convicted all accused persons - Aggrieved, accused persons filed appeals before HC challenging conviction - While so, Govt. of Tamil Nadu passed order authorizing Directorate of Vigilance and Anti-Corruption to engage services of respondent no. 5 before HC in matters arising out of conviction of accused persons - Hence, appellant made representation to Govt. of Karnataka to appoint senior lawyer to contest appeals filed by accused persons and later filed writ petition before HC seeking direction to State to appoint any other senior lawyer in appeals of accused persons - However, Single Judge of HC inter alia held that directions of SC issued earlier regarding appointment of Special Public Prosecutor were confined to procedure to be followed in trial and that appeals could be proceeded notwithstanding challenges to appointment of respondent no. 5 - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal before HC wherein by impugned judgment HC held inter alia that respondent no. 5 had authority to not only appear in case before Special Court but also in appeal arising out of it - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether respondent no. 5, appointed as Special Public Prosecutor in trial of case was entitled to represent the prosecution in appeals filed by accused persons against their conviction.

Held, as per Notification appointing respondent no. 5 as Special Public Prosecutor, respondent no. 5 was given authority to represent the prosecution only before Special Court and not in HC and notification appointing respondent no. 5 as Special Public prosecutor only for the trial and not for subsequent proceedings correctly reflected intent of SC. Further, respondent no. 5 has no authority to per se conduct appeals in HC on behalf of prosecution as he was not appointed as Public Prosecutor or Special Public Prosecutor for HC u/s. 24(1) f0f CrPC. Further, respondent no. 5, having been appointed as Special Public Prosecutor for a specific case pertaining to accused persons before Special Court was answerable in all respects to Deputy Director of Prosecution in terms of s. 25-A(6) of CrPC and his authorization was limited only to that case before Special Court and the same precluded him from appearing on behalf of prosecution in appeals filed by accused persons in HC. Respondent no. 5 required specific authorization which would have then made him subordinate to Director of Prosecution and not continued his subordination to Deputy Director of Prosecution. Hence, as the prosecution was not represented by authorized person, hearing of appeals in HC were vitiated. Appeal is allowed and HC is directed to hear appeals afresh with the prosecution represented by a Public Prosecutor appointed u/s. 24(1) of CrPC or a Special Public Prosecutor appointed by State u/s. 24(8) of the Code. Further, another appeal challenging dismissal of application filed by appellant seeking permission to intervene in pending appeals filed by accused and assist Special Public Prosecutor is dismissed since s. 301(2) of CrPC does not postulate filing of any written submissions. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - ss. 24(1) and 24(3) of CrPC limit jurisdiction or the area of operation or the authority or the orbit of Public Prosecutor to HC (s. 24(1) of CrPC) or the district (s. 24(3) of CrPC). Further, s. 301(1) of CrPC enforces the 'jurisdictional' or 'operational' limit and enables the Public Prosecutor and Assistant Public Prosecutor to appear and plead without written authority only within their 'jurisdictional' or 'operational' limit, provided the Public Prosecutor or the Assistant Public Prosecutor is in charge of that case.
