Managing Director, K. S. R. T. C and another v New India Assurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 745; (2016) 2 SCC 382; 2015 (4) ACC 644; 2015 ACJ 2849; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 166; 2016 (1) AWC 316; 2016 (3) CalLT(SC) 24; JT 2015 (9) SC 486; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4609; 2016 (5) MahLJ 305; 2016 (3) MPLJ 567; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 938; 2016 (4) RLW 2885; 2015(12) SCALE 52; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Bodily Injury, Contractual Liability, Financier, Policy Of Insurance, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, Hypothecation, Registered Owner, Payment Of Compensation, Liability To Pay Compensation, Certificate Of Insurance And The Policy

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 2 (30) - Registered owner - Liability to make payment - Accident was caused by the bus which was driven under the control of KSRTC - Said bus was owned by respondent no. 2, insured by the Insurance Co. - Tribunal allowed the claim petition preferred by the claimants and awarded a sum of Rs.4,09,000/- with interest @ 6% p.a. - As per the agreement between KSRTC and the owner of the bus, the liability was fastened upon the owner and the insurer of the vehicle jointly and severally to make the payment of compensation, not on KSRTC - HC allowed the appeal filed by the insurer and held that the liability to make the payment of compensation was that of KSRTC alone - Hence, instant appeal - Whether in the wake of lease agreement entered into by registered owner with KSRTC, the registered owner and insurer along with KSRTC can be fastened with the liability to make payment to the claimants and whether KSRTC can recover the amount from registered owner and its entitlement to seek indemnification from insurer?

Held, SC in HDFC Bank Limited v. Reshma & Ors. 2014 Indlaw SC 816 held that the insurer cannot escape the liability, when ownership changes due to the hypothecation agreement. In the case of hire also, it cannot escape the liability, even if the ownership changes. Even though, KSRTC is treated as owner u/s. 2(30) of Act, the registered owner continues to remain liable as per terms and conditions of lease agreement lawfully entered into with KSRTC. In the present case, registered owner, insurer as well as KSRTC would be liable to make the payment of compensation jointly and severally to the claimants and the KSRTC in terms of the lease agreement entered into with the registered owner would be entitled to recover the amount paid to the claimants from the owner as stipulated in the agreement or from the insurer. Appeals allowed.
