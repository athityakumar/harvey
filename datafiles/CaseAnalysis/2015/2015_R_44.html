Rajinder Kumar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 67; (2015) 4 SCC 215; 2015 CRLJ 1560; 2015 (2) JCC 1049; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 155; 2015(1) SCALE 354; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Poisoning

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant had murdered deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 498A and 304B of IPC - On appeal, HC set aside the conviction and sentence of the appellant u/s 498A of IPC and upheld conviction and sentence u/s 304B of IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the death of deceased occurred within 7 years of her marriage. It is not disputed that her death is not under normal circumstances. Statements of the complainant and PW-13 are specific about the harassment of the deceased by the appellant in connection with the demand of dowry. It is also evident from the evidence on record that she was meted out with such harassment soon before her death. Hence, prosecution was successful in proving the ingredients of s. 304-B of IPC. The Trial Court rightly presumed that the appellant has caused the dowry death of the victim and the HC rightly upheld the conviction and sentence. Appeal dismissed.
