SEBI Through Its Chairman v Roofit Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 772; 2016(1) ALL MR 928; 2016 (1) BC 422; [2016] 194 Comp Cas 186; 2016 (1) CompLJ 1; JT 2015 (12) SC 229; 2015 (8) MLJ 864; 2015(12) SCALE 642; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Securities and Exchange Board of India (Amendment) Act, 2002, Mines Act, 1952, Securities Laws (Amendment) Act, 2014

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 15A, 15A(a), 15J - Failure to comply with summons issued - Reduction of Penalty - Justification - Appellant having noticed allegations of share-price rigging by respondent, initiated investigation into shareholder pattern of respondent and price manipulation thereof - Appellant issued summons to respondent requiring it to procure and produce certain documents and also for submitting additional information - Further, since summons were still not complied with and information required was not provided by respondent, Adjudicating Officer was appointed to conduct enquiry - Further, in terms of s. 15A(a) of Act, penalty of Rs. 1 crore was imposed on respondent and in connected penalty of Rs. 75 lakhs was imposed on each of various Respondent companies - Further, Tribunal held that given that business of respondent had come to dormancy, there would be no point in imposing high penalties which would remain paper orders, hence, penalty was reduced to Rs. 60,000 and quantum of penalty in connected appeals was also reduced for same reasons, from Rs. 75 lakh to Rs. 15,000 in five cases and Rs. 60,000 in one case - Further, review application filed by appellant was dismissed - Whether Tribunal erred in reducing penalty imposed by Adjudicating Officer.

Held, penalty imposed by Adjudicating Officer should not have been reduced on wholly extraneous grounds not mentioned in s. 15J of Act. Further, SC held that initial summons to respondent was dt. 23-7-2002. From this date onwards, there was obligation on respondent to produce documents and information sought by appellant, but it failed to do so, even until imposition of penalty by Adjudicating Officer on 29-3-2004. Instead, respondent sought extensions of time vide three letters. After third letter, appellant sent reminder letter dt. 5-9-2002. Further, appellant had declined request for further extension of time beyond 16-9-2002. Respondent had failed to furnish information by that date, resulting in penalty u/s. 15A of Act becoming applicable. Hence, penalty prior to amendment to s. 15A of Act would be applicable, i.e. Rs. 1.5 lakhs. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Penalty imposed by authority cannot be reduced when assessee has failed to comply with summons issued and reduction is not in accordance with law.
