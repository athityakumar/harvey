Eshwarappa v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 530; AIR 2015 SC 3037; 2015 CRLJ 4034; JT 2015 (6) SC 462; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 591; 2015(8) SCALE 159; 2016(8) SCALE 159; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 498A - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Deceased and appellant accused got married on 20-3-2003 and from said wedlock, a child was born - It was alleged that appellant accused had illicit relationship with other female due to which deceased and appellant accused had quarrels - Further, it was alleged that dowry was demanded by accused - Further, on fateful date it was stated that deceased went to field of accused to take money from accused for treatment of child, however, later her death body was found - FIR was registered against accused appellant - Trial Court convicted appellant husband u/ss. 498A, 302 and 201 of IPC - Further order passed by Trial Court is affirmed by HC - Whether conviction of appellant accused by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Trial Court and HC have both concurrently held material facts to have been fully established. Further, material placed on record collaborated with testimony of witness. Further, Trial Court and HC rightly rejected story of suicide by deceased because death in case at hand occurred because of strangulation/constriction force around neck leading to asphyxia and shock as observed by doctor which is possible not necessarily by hanging. Further, if death had occurred because of hanging, she would have been discovered by witnesses in hanging position. Further, it is nobody's case that she was carrying rope with herself when she was seen going towards field and there was no immediate provocation for deceased to take step to commit suicide. SC held that in totality of circumstances and having regard to nature of evidence which courts below have found credible on all material aspects of prosecution case, there is no compelling reason to interfere with view taken by Trial Court as affirmed by HC, however, SC set aside conviction order passed by lower Court with respect to s. 498A of IPC. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Conviction order passed by lower court based on evidence on record and testimony of witness is justified and in accordance with law.
