Narayan Laxman Patil v Gala Construction Company Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 926; 2015(6) ALL MR 928; 2016 (2) ALT(SC) 44; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 19; JT 2015 (9) SC 346; 2016 (130) RD 159; 2015(10) SCALE 559; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Validity, Mutation entry

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, ss. 148, 149, 150 - Mutation Entry - Validity - Disputed land which belonged to owner was in possession of appellant alongwith six other persons who used to cultivate paddy crop on said land - Mutation Entry was made recording name of appellant alongwith six other persons in 'other rights' column - Respondent no. 1 and owner of disputed land entered into agreement for sale in regard with said land - Appellate authority cancelled mutation entry made in respect of appellant and six other persons - Whether mutation entry in 'other rights' column of Record of Rights was valid.

Held, in order to exercise right u/s. 148 read with ss. 149, 150 of the 1966 Code for entering one's name in Record of Rights, applicant has to be holder, occupant, owner, mortgagee or tenant. Appellant has to prove his title of possession by pointing out that it was lawful and if such requirement could not be proved, mutation entry is required to be cancelled. Since appellant and others never claimed that they had any title to said land, entering their names in 'Record of Rights' was not at all justified and, therefore, mutation entry could not have been made in said record. Appeal dismissed.
