K. P. Manu v Chairman, Scrutiny Committee for Verification of Community Certificate

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 147; (2015) 4 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 1402; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 155; 2015(3) ALL MR 443; JT 2015 (2) SC 499; 2015 (2) KLT 237; 2015 (2) MLJ 460; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 168; 2015(3) SCALE 1; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976, Kerala (Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Regulation of Issue of Community Certificates Act, 1996, Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, Statement of Objects and Reasons accompanying the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Bill, 1989

Summary: Administrative - Kerala (Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Regulation of Issue of Community Certificates Act, 1996 - Reconversion - Acceptance by community - Appellant great grandfather though belonged to Hindu Pulaya Community, his son who was appellant grandfather converted to Christianity and also Appellant father married Christian - However, appellant at age of 24 converted himself to Hindu religion and changed his name - On basis of conversion, appellant applied for caste certificate from Sangham and same was granted and later, Tehsildar also issued caste certificate - However, on basis of complaint made against issuance of caste certificate to appellant, Scrutiny Committee constituted under the Act, conducted enquiry and held that appellant does not belong to Hindu Pulaya caste - On basis of said report, State Govt. directed respondent no.2-employer to remove appellant from service and recover amount from him - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC but the same was dismissed holding that appellant who was born to Christian parents and professed Christian faith is not entitled to claim that he is Hindu-Pulaya - On appeal, DB of HC vide impugned judgment and order accepted report of Scrutiny Committee constituted under the Act and cancelled caste certificate granted in favour of appellant by Tehsildar - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant is entitled for caste certificate as Hindu Pulaya.

Held, the community which is a recognized organization by State Govt. has granted the certificate in categorical terms in favour of appellant. It is the community which has the final says as far as acceptance is concerned, for it accepts the person, on reconversion, and takes him within its fold. Hence, appellant after reconversion had come within the fold of the community and thereby became a member of Scheduled Caste. Further, acceptance of community is in continuum. Hence, impugned judgment of HC, findings of Scrutiny Committee and orders passed by State Govt. and respondent no. 2 are set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Three things that need to be established by person who claims to be a beneficiary of caste certificate are firstly, there should be absolutely clear cut proof that he belongs to that caste that has been recognized by Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950, secondly, there has been reconversion to original religion to which the parents and earlier generations had belonged, and lastly there has to be evidence establishing the acceptance by community. Further, even if one of three is not substantiated, the recognition would not be possible.
