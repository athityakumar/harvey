D. T. Virupakshappa v C. Subash

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 299; AIR 2015 SC 2022; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2434; 2015 CRLJ 2589; 2015 (3) JCC 1766; JT 2015 (4) SC 311; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 637; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 1001; 2015(5) SCALE 573B; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324, 326, 341, 120, 114, 506, 149, 482 - Alleged offence by Govt. Servant - Process initiated without sanction - Justification - Complainant is filed against appellant police officer alleging that appellant during investigation of criminal case assaulted respondent in order to extract some information with regard to death of one person, and in that connection, respondent was detained in police station for some time - Trial Court registered case against appellant u/ss. 323, 324, 326, 341, 120, 114, 506 read with s. 149 of IPC - Appellant moved HC u/s. 482 of CrPC which was declined by impugned order - Appellant contended that Magistrate could not have taken cognizance of alleged offence and issued process to appellant without sanction from State Govt. u/s. 197 of CrPC, and that on that sole ground, HC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, allegation in present case is that appellant exceeded in exercising his power during investigation of criminal case , hence, alleged conduct has essential connection with discharge of official duty of appellant. SC held that u/s. 197 of CrPC, in case, Govt. servant is accused of offence, which is alleged to have been committed by him while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty, previous sanction is necessary. Hence, Magistrate could not have taken cognizance of case without previous sanction of State Govt. and HC missed this crucial point in impugned order. Hence, order passed by HC is set aside along with proceedings initiated by Trial Court taking cognizance and issuing process to appellant. Further, case such sanction is obtained and same is produced before Magistrate, Magistrate may proceed further in case in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Process against Govt. servant for alleged offence which is committed by him while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty cannot be initiated unless sanction is obtained from State Govt.
