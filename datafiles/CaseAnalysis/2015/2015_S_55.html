Food Corporation of India v Sankar Ghosh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 481; (2015) 9 SCC 104; AIR 2015 SC 3555; 2015 (146) FLR 991; JT 2015 (6) SC 100; 2015 (3) LLJ 429; 2015(7) SCALE 644; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 345; 2015 (5) SLR 93

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Dusting, Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971, FCI Recruitment Rules, 1971

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Claim of regularization of post - Filing of additional documents - Determination - Appellant Corporation awarded contract to Co-operative Society for execution of various operational works - Respondents took part in such operational works as 'Analyser', 'Picker' and 'Dusting Operators' - Corporation stated that respondents worked under contractor, however, respondents claimed that they were engaged directly by appellant Corporation as casual workers - Tribunal directed appellant Corporation to regularize respondents in post of Dusting Operators on basis of doctrine of 'equal pay for equal work' - Further, lower court dismissed petition filed by appellant - Further, appeal was allowed and impugned order passed was set aside - Hence, services of respondents were disengaged by appellant Corporation - Further in view of order passed by SC, appellant Corporation has filed affidavit along with various annexures - Whether impugned order passed by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, SC proposed to go into merits of rival contentions raised by parties in additional affidavits and documents filed pursuant to order of SC. SC failed to express any opinion on merits of matter, SC set aside impugned order passed by HC and matter is remitted back to HC for consideration of matter afresh in light of fresh material adduced by parties. DB shall afford opportunity to both parties and consider matter afresh in accordance with law. Matter remanded to lower Court. Order accordingly.

Ratio - In order to pass order in favor of party, it is necessary that evidence and additional documents filed should be taken into consideration.
