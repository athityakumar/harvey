TATA Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited v Director (Research) for and on behalf of Deepak Khanna and others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 609; (2015) 10 SCC 734; 2015(6) ALL MR 491; 2015 (6) AWC 5448; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 42; 2015 (4) CompLJ 161; 2015 (4) CPJ(Sc) 6; JT 2015 (8) SC 198; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 56; 2015(9) SCALE 604; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices, Trade Practice, Preliminary Investigation

Summary: MRTP - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 36A(1)(i), 36A(1)(ii), 36A(1)(iii), 36A(1)(vi), 36D(1) - Alleged Unfair Trade Practice - Order passed against manufacture - Justification - Appellant manufacturing company introduced new car for which appellant invited prospective customers to book car through dealers, however, booking amount demanded by appellant was quite high and close to estimated price finally payable which would include excise duty, sales tax and transportation charges - Initial allotment was done by appellant - However, three complainants filed compliant on grounds that demanded amount exceeded basic price of car if cess, taxes and transportation cost were left out, hence, appellant had indulged in Unfair Trade Practice (UTP) by demanding excessive amount for bookings of cars and by including likely taxes, cess and transportation cost - Commission passed impugned order by which it directed appellant to cease and desist from continuing with practices complained of and not to repeat same in future - Whether impugned order passed by Commission against appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, SC found no material or even allegation in Preliminary Investigation Report (PIR) which could satisfy any of four unfair trade practices covered by various clauses such as clause (i), (ii), (iv) and (vi) of s. 36-A(1) of Act. Perusal of notice of enquiry dt. 25-9-2000 reveals that no doubt copy of PIR was enclosed but notice made it clear itself that Commission came to considered opinion that Director (Research) had found appellant indulging in unfair trade practices falling precisely and only under clauses (i), (ii), (iv) and (vi) of ss. 36A(1) of Act. SC held that scrutiny of judgment under appeal discloses that Commission failed to keep in mind precise allegations against appellant with view to find out whether facts could satisfy definition of Unfair Trade Practices as alleged against appellant in Notice of Enquiry. Commission was apparently misled by PIR Report also which claimed to deal with reply received from appellant in course of preliminary enquiry but patently failed even to notice stipulation as regards payment of interest on booking amount although this fact was obvious from terms and conditions of booking and was reportedly relied upon by appellant in its reply even at stage of preliminary investigation. Commission noticed relevant facts including provision for interest while narrating facts, but failed to take note of this crucial aspect while discussing relevant materials for purpose of arriving at its conclusions. SC held that conclusion of Commission is based only upon subjective considerations of fairness and do not pass objective test of law as per precise definitions under s. 36A of Act. SC held that allegations and materials against appellant did not make out case of unfair trade practice against appellant. Nor there is any scope to pass order u/s. 36-D(1) of Act when no case of any unfair trade practice is made out. Hence, impugned order passed by Commissioner is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Order desisting manufacturer from continuing production cannot be passed when there is no material against manufacture proving that he is engaged in unfair trade practice.
