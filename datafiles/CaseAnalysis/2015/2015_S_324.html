State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Hitkishore Goswami

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 107; 2015(2) ALL MR 989; 2015 (2) CGLJ 61; 2015 (145) FLR 70; 2015 (1) JabLJ 407; JT 2015 (2) SC 410; 2015 (3) MLJ 221; 2015(2) SCALE 428; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 202; 2015 (3) SLR 346

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Retiral Benefits

Summary: Service - Computation of pensionary benefits - Reckoning of past service - Respondent was working as Lecturer in Govt. School - Respondent was selected for the post of Lecturer in Degree College - Respondent resigned from Govt. School to enable him to join in Degree College - However, while calculating respondent's pensionery benefits, appellant department did not consider his earlier service in School - Respondent filed writ petition - Single Judge directed appellant to count respondent's previous service for pensionery purpose - Appellant challenged Single Judge order before DB - DB dismissed appellant's writ appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, respondent was not entitled to claim the benefit of his past services which he rendered as Lecturer in Govt. Schools while determining his qualifying service for fixing his pension etc. The respondent having voluntarily tendered his resignation from the said service without there being any condition much less a condition to enable him to claim any kind of its benefit in the event of his joining other services with the State, no benefit of such past services was available to the respondent. The effect of tendering the resignation by the respondent - may be for any reason was that the relationship between the parties insofar as that particular employment was concerned got severed for all purposes leaving no benefit to remain in respondent's favour. Further, in order to claim continuity in the service for claiming any benefit arising therefrom, it was necessary for the respondent to have shown any specific rule or condition recognizing such right in his favour. The respondent, however, was not able to show any such rule or/and condition in his favour. Therefore, courts below erred in directing the State to give benefit to the respondent of his services which he had rendered in Govt. Schools for fixing his pension without properly examining the effect of his tendering resignation on the issue raised in the writ petition. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - A right to claim pension is governed by the statue. An employee has, therefore, no right to claim any benefit in relation to pension dehores the statute.
