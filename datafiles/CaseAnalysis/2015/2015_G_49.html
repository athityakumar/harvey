Ashapura Mine-Chem Limited v Gujarat Mineral Development Corporation

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 281; (2015) 8 SCC 193; 2015(4) ALL MR 449; 2015 (5) AWC 4679; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 123; 2015 (2) CompLJ 417; 2015 (3) CTC 541; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2844; 2016 (2) MahLJ 4; 2015 (3) MLJ 838; 2016 (1) MPLJ 571; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 614; 2015(5) SCALE 379; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 880; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 136

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Arbitration clause - Termination of agreement - Appellant entered into Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with respondent agreeing to constitute joint venture with respondent and another company QTX for setting up alumina plant and respondent agreed to supply bauxite to proposed plant - However, disputes arose between appellant and respondent and appellant issued legal notice to invoke cl.27 of MoU to appoint Arbitrator for amicable settlement of disputes - Respondent had not concurred for appointment of Arbitrator and so, appellant approached HC filing application u/s. 11 of the Act and sought appointment of Arbitrator - However, by impugned order, HC rejected appellant's application and hence, instant appeal - Whether arbitration clause contained in MoU ceases to exist with termination of MoU.

Held, as per cl. 27 of MoU, in the event of failure of an amicable settlement at the bilateral level relating to a dispute or difference arising between appellant and the respondent to be reached as contained in cl. 26 of MoU, then such unresolved dispute or difference concerning or arising from MoU, its implementation, breach or termination whatsoever including any difference or dispute as to the interpretation of any of the terms of MoU is referable to sole Arbitrator appointed by appellant and the respondent. Hence, irrespective of fact whether MoU fructified into full-fledged agreement, having regard to non-fulfillment of any of the conditions or failure of compliance of any requirement by either of parties stipulated in other clauses of MoU, specific agreement has been entered into by appellant and the respondent u/cl. 27 to refer such controversies as between the parties to the sole arbitrator by consensus. Hence, when consensus was not reached as between the parties for making the reference, eventually it will be open for either of parties to invoke s. 11 of the Act and seek for reference of dispute for arbitration. As per sequence of events occurred between 17-8-2007 and 25-4-2011, both parties were at variance with reference to various terms and conditions contained in MoU and consequently there was every right in either of parties to seek for amicable settlement in first instance as specified u/cl. 26 of MoU. Further, as per materials on record, appellant vide letter dt.11-7-2011 addressed to respondent expressed desire to amicably resolve dispute at bilateral level. Since there was no response from respondent, appellant caused legal notice on 7-12-2012 by invocation of cl. 27 of MoU for appointment of Arbitrator and suggested name of retired HC Judge and sought for concurrence of respondent. However, since respondent not inclined to agree for reference, appellant exercised option of moving HC by filing application u/s. 11 of the Act. Hence, as cl. 27 of MoU is valid arbitration agreement, appellant was fully entitled to invoke said agreement and seek reference to arbitrator. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and former Judge of HC is appointed as sole Arbitrator to adjudicate disputes arose between parties. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Arbitration clause in agreement continues to be enforceable notwithstanding a declaration of contract being null and void.
