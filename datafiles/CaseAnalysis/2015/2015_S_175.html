Kanaklata v State of (NCT) of Delhi and others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 81; (2015) 6 SCC 617; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1247; 2015 (109) ALR 706; JT 2015 (2) SC 27; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1219; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 609; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 897; 2015 (127) RD 567; 2015(2) SCALE 182; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presiding Officer

Summary: T. S. Thakur, J. and Adarsh Kumar Goel, J.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities Act), 1989, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323 and 354 - Transfer of case - FIR was registered for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 323/354 of the IPC and ss. 3(i) (X) (XI) (XV) of the Act - Additional Sessions Judge heard the parties on the question of framing of charges and discharged the accused persons for the offences under the Act - Revision petition filed thereagainst was allowed - Complainant expressed an apprehension about the fairness of the approach which the Trial Court may adopt in view of the findings recorded in the order of discharge earlier passed by her - Application for transfer of case was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the ordinary course if an order passed by the Court is set aside the observations and findings recorded therein also get obliterated for all intents and purposes. However, there may still be situations where the nature of the observations made by the court concerned create a reasonable apprehension in the mind of the litigant that the Court has so committed itself to a given approach or thought process that it may not be possible for it to retrace its steps to take a fair and non-partisan view in the matter. In the instant case, despite the safeguards provided by the HC's observations, the apprehension of the complainant continues to subsist. Apprehension is not wholly misconceived nor can it be dubbed as forum shopping in disguise. Transfer of the case directed to another Court. Appeal allowed.

R. Banumathi, J.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities Act), 1989, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323 and 354 - Transfer of case.

Held, in considering the transfer application, it is to be kept in mind that whether a litigant could reasonably apprehend a bias attributable to a presiding Judge. Mere presumptions or possible assumptions are not sufficient for transfer of a case. Only on good and sufficient grounds a transfer can be ordered. In the instant case, appellant has not made out any sufficient ground for transfer and the request for transfer is bereft of merits. The HC has rightly dismissed the transfer petition and same does not warrant any interference by this Court. Appeal dismissed.
