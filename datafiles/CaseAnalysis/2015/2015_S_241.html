Daulat Ram @ Daulti v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 344; 2015 CRLJ 2639; 2015 (3) JCC 1587; JT 2015 (5) SC 145; 2015(3) SCALE 740; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 1001

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Skull

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Justification - PW-1 complainant informed Inspector that his elder son deceased was missing - Further, it was stated that deceased had gone on request of appellant with him and PW2, disclosed him that in previous night deceased was seen having liquor with appellant - PW9 stated PW 1 that he had seen appellant throwing dead body into dry well near tube well and later PW 1 recovered body of his elder son - Complainant contended that appellant and co - accused had killed his elder son - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 read with s. 34 and u/s. 201 of IPC - Further, co-accused had already been convicted by Trial court, vide its judgment and order - Order passed by Trial court is affirmed by HC with respect to appellant accused , however, co-accused were acquitted by HC from charge of offence punishable u/s. 302/34 of IPC but their conviction and sentence u/s. 201 of IPC was not interfered with - Hence, present appeal.

Held, with respect to role of appellant accused in murder of deceased, SC held that it cannot be said that complete chain of circumstances is proved to hold him guilty of commission of murder of deceased or as to fact that he caused disappearance of evidence by throwing dead body of deceased into well. There is nothing on record to suggest that appellant had any enmity with deceased or that he had any motive to commit crime. No doubt, motive is not required to be proved for commission of crime but in case of circumstantial evidence, it cannot be altogether ignored where other accused with similar role have been acquitted, from charges of murder.

Further, other witnesses got examined on behalf of prosecution have given evidence of formal nature which is not incriminatory against appellant. Hence, SC held that chain of circumstances brought on record is not complete to prove charge of offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC against appellant as such, conviction and sentence recorded by courts below against him is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In criminal case, guilt of accused has to be established beyond reasonable doubt by prosecution.
