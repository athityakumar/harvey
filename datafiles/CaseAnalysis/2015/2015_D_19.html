Padmakumari and others v Dasayyan and others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 269; (2015) 8 SCC 695; 2015 (5) ALT(SC) 7; 2015 (4) AWC 3354; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 94; 2015 (6) CTC 545; 2016 (2) MahLJ 307; 2016 (1) MPLJ 589; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 972; 2015(5) SCALE 409; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 56; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 176

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c), 19(b) - Stipulation of time - Essence of contract - Defendant nos. 1 to 11 entered into agreement of sale in favour of plaintiff in respect of suit schedule property and plaintiff paid Rs.2,000 as advance out of total sale consideration of Rs.63,000 - However, plaintiff had not paid balance amount within 9 months as agreed upon and so, defendant nos. 1 to 11 entered into agreement of sale in respect of part of suit schedule property with defendant no. 12 to 15 - Later, plaintiff filed suit against defendants demanding execution of sale deed and Trial Court passed decree of specific performance in favour of plaintiff - Aggrieved, defendant nos. 12 to 15 filed appeal before HC and HC concurred with findings of Trial Court and hence, instant appeal challenging judgment and decree of HC - Whether findings of Trial Court and HC are valid.

Held, taking measurement of property would not arise before plaintiff performs his part of contract regarding the balance consideration within the period stipulated in the agreement. Plaintiff had not paid balance amount within stipulated time and notice was issued by plaintiff only after one year, hence, plaintiff had not adhered to time which is stipulated to pay the balance consideration amount to defendant nos. 1 to 11 which is very important legal aspect which was required to be considered by Trial Court and HC at the time of determining rights of parties and pass impugned judgment. However, Courts ignored the same, and hence findings of Trial Court and HC were contrary to terms and conditions of the agreement, pleadings and evidence on record. Further, plaintiff has not complied with legal requirement which is mandatory as provided u/s. 16(c) of the Act and so, concurrent finding of fact recorded by HC is erroneous in law and is liable to be set aside. Furthermore, defendant nos. 12 to 15 before entering into agreement with defendant nos. 1 to 11 made proper verification from competent authority to purchase the part of the suit schedule property and got the agreement of sale executed in their favour and later got sale deed registered by paying sale consideration amount. Hence, defendant nos. 12 to 15, being bona fide purchasers as per s. 19(b) of the Act, the specific performance of contract could not be enforced against them. Hence, impugned judgments and decree of HC and Trial Court are set aside and suit dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per s. 19(b) of the Act, specific performance of contract could not be enforced against transferee who purchases property for value and pays amount in good faith without notice of original contract.
