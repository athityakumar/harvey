S. R. Sukumar v S. Sunaad Raghuram

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 451; (2015) 9 SCC 609; AIR 2015 SC 2757; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2898; 2015 CRLJ 3829; 2015 (3) JCC 2157; JT 2015 (6) SC 72; 2015 (3) KLT 382; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 758; 2015 (3) RLW 2237; 2015(7) SCALE 404; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Curable, Oath, Judicial Notice

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Amendment of Complaint - Cognizance of offence - Respondent filed complaint against appellant and his mother on allegation of committing offences punishable u/ss.120-B, 499 and 500 IPC - Later, respondent moved application seeking amendment of complaint and Trial Court allowed said amendment on 24-5-2007 and later took cognizance of offence and directed issuance of process to appellant vide Order dt.21-6-2007 - Aggrieved by said order, appellant approached HC for quashing of proceedings on ground that there was no provision in CrPC for amendment of complaint - However, vide impugned order, HC dismissed petition filed by appellant and hence, instant appeal - Whether complaint filed u/s. 200 CrPC could be amended.

Held, even though proposed amendment of complaint was not formal amendment, but a substantial one, amendment application was allowed by Magistrate, since, firstly, no cognizance was taken of the complaint before disposal of amendment application; secondly, since summons was yet to be ordered to be issued to the accused, no prejudice would be caused to the accused; thirdly amendment did not change the original nature of the complaint being one for defamation; fourthly the publication of poem being in nature of subsequent event created a new cause of action in favour of respondent which could have been prosecuted by respondent by filing a separate complaint and hence, to avoid multiplicity of proceedings, the Trial Court allowed the amendment application. Hence, HC rightly upheld order of Trial Court allowing amendment of complaint and so, impugned order of HC is upheld and Trial Court is directed to take up the matter and dispose the same in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - If the amendment of complaint sought to be made relates to a simple infirmity curable by means of formal application for amendment and by allowing such amendment, no prejudice could be caused to other side, then, notwithstanding the fact that there is no enabling provision in the Code for entertaining such amendment, the Court could permit such an amendment to be made.
