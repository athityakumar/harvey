Neeta W/o Kallappa Kadolkar and others Etc. v Div. Manager, MSRTC, Kolhapur

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 19; (2015) 3 SCC 590; 2015 ACJ 598; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 50; 2016(1) ALL MR 508; 2015 (110) ALR 226; 2015 (1) AWC 969; JT 2015 (1) SC 354; 2015 (5) KarLJ 40; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 223; 2015 (6) MahLJ 19; 2015 (4) MPLJ 418; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 625; 2015(1) SCALE 369; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 45

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Motor accident - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - Appellants (claimants) filed a claim petitions before Tribunal, seeking compensation on account of death of both deceased in a motor accident - Tribunal by common judgment awarded compensation of Rs.7,68,000/- and Rs.7,88,000/- respectively, with interest at the rate of 8% p.a. by taking the monthly income of both deceased at Rs.4,500/- p.m - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which were party allowed by re-assessing the monthly income of both the deceased at Rs.6000/- p.m - Hence, instant appeals for enhancement of compensation - Appellants contended that quantum of compensation awarded by Courts below was not just and reasonable and therefore the same was required to be enhanced on the basis of legal evidence on record.

Held, evidence on record remained unchallenged by the respondent and there is no rebuttal evidence adduced by the respondent disputing the claim of the appellants. Thus, the Tribunal and the HC have committed an error, both on facts and in law in not taking the correct monthly income of both the deceased for computation of loss of dependency, keeping in view the fact that they were carpenters which is the skilled job. Therefore, the monthly income of the deceased taken by the Tribunal and the HC for determination of loss of dependency is erroneous, as it is not in accordance with the guiding factors laid down by SC in the catena of cases to arrive at the just monthly income earned by both the deceased in the absence of documentary evidence. Therefore, the same is liable to be set aside and it has to be properly determined by taking the gross income of both the deceased. The Tribunal and the HC even in the absence of the salary slip/certificate ought to have taken the monthly salary of both the deceased at Rs.12,000/- p.m., the Act notification, wherein, the State of Karnataka on the basis of the said notification for the relevant period, had fixed the minimum wage of the carpenters in their report, which is a skilled job in the Zone-II and the deceased were working in the aforesaid Zone, at Belgaum District, during the relevant period of their death. Further, it should have been noted by both the Tribunal and the Appellate Court that the minimum wages fixed in the notification is not fair wage and therefore, they could have taken the monthly salary on the basis of real wages that were being paid in the absence of documentary proof on the basis of speculation. They should have taken the reasonable monthly income of the deceased for the purpose of computation of just and reasonable compensation in favour of the appellants. Thus, the annual income of both the deceased would be Rs.1,44,000/- each. Thus, the total compensation payable to the claimants of deceased, by respondent will be Rs.21,53,000/- and Rs.23,03,000/- respectively, with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. Appeals allowed.
