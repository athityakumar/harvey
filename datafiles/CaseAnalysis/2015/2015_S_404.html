Karnail Kaur and others v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 48; (2015) 3 SCC 206; AIR 2015 SC 2041; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 47; 2015 (109) ALR 894; 2015 (2) AWC 1963; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 407; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 162; 2015 (1) CTC 551; JT 2015 (1) SC 377; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1190; 2015 (4) MahLJ 616; 2015 (3) MPLJ 333; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 786; 2015 (127) RD 786; 2015(1) SCALE 598

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition proceedings - Quashing of notification - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act,2013, s.24(2) - State through Secretary, issued notification for purpose of setting up residential urban estate in area of revenue estate of village - Objections were raised against same by appellants but were not decided by Land Acquisition Officer and notification was published - Petition was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, physical possession of land belonging to appellants have neither been taken by respondents nor compensation paid to them even though award was passed on dt.6-8-2007, and more than five years have lapsed prior to date on which Act of 2013 came into force. Therefore, conditions mentioned in s.24(2) of 2013 Act are satisfied for allowing plea of appellants that land acquisition proceedings are deemed to have lapsed in terms of s.24(2) of the Act. Further, amendment would not be applicable to case on hand for reason that appeals were pending much prior to ordinance and also applications u/s. 24(2) of 2013 Act were filed prior to amendment to s .24(2) of 2013 Act by Ordinance and same were heard and reserved for orders on dt.28-10-2014 and therefore Ordinance in so far as insertion of proviso to above section by way of amendment is prospective. Hence, acquisition proceedings in respect of appellants' land have lapsed. Thus, land acquisition proceedings notification in so far as lands of appellants are concerned is also quashed. Appeal disposed of.
