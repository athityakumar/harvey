Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 903; (2015) 7 SCC 649; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 217; [2015] 376 ITR 306; 2015(7) SCALE 367; [2015] 233 TAXMAN 495

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Foreign Company, Plant And Machinery, Chargeable To Tax, Fees For Technical Services, Hire, Royalty, Technical Services, Mines Act, 1952, Oilfields (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948, Petroleum and Natural Gas Rules, 1959, Oil Fields (Development and Regulation) Act, 1948

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 44BB, 44D - Payment to foreign company for mining work - Taxability - Determination - Appellant and non resident/foreign company had entered into agreement by which non-resident company had agreed to make available supervisory staff and personnel having experience and expertise for operation and management of drilling rigs for A/Y 1985-1986 and other drilling rig for A/Y 1986-1987 - Appellant has been assessed in representative capacity on behalf of different foreign companies with whom it had executed separate agreements for services to be rendered by such companies in connection with prospecting, extraction or production of mineral oils by appellant - AO held that assessments should be made u/s. 44D of Act and not s. 44BB of Act - Appellant Commissioner and Tribunal disagreed with view of AO - HC vide impugned order overturned view taken by Appellate Commissioner and Tribunal and held payments made to be liable for assessment u/s. 44D of Act - Whether amounts paid by appellant to non-resident assessees for providing various services is chargeable to tax as 'fees for technical services' u/s. 44D of Act.

Held, proximity of works contemplated under agreement, executed with non-resident assessee or foreign company, with mining activity or mining operations that would be crucial for determination of question whether payments made under such agreement to non-resident assessee or foreign company is to be assessed u/s. 44BB or s. 44D of Act. Further, authority has issued circular on 22-10-1990 to effect that mining operations and expressions 'mining projects' or 'like projects' occurring in Explanation 2 to s. 9(1) of Act would cover rendering of service like imparting of training and carrying out drilling operations for exploration of and extraction of oil and natural gas and hence payments made under such agreement to non-resident/foreign company would be chargeable to tax under provisions of s. 44BB of Act and not s. 44D of Act. Further, SC held that pith and substance of each of contracts is inextricably connected with prospecting, extraction or production of mineral oil and dominant purpose of each of such agreement is for prospecting, extraction or production of mineral oils though there may be certain ancillary works contemplated thereunder. Hence, payments made by appellant and received by non-resident assessees or foreign companies under said contracts is more appropriately assessable under provisions of s. 44BB of Act and not s. 44D of Act. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and order passed by Appellant commissioner as affirmed by Tribunal is restored. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Assessee should be assessed in accordance with provisions laid down in statute and circulars issued by authority from time to time.
