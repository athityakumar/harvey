Madras Institute of Development Studies and another v Dr. K. Sivasubramaniyan and others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 574; (2016) 1 SCC 454; AIR 2015 SC 3643; 2015 (5) AWC 4356; 2015 (147) FLR 856; JT 2015 (7) SC 350; 2015(9) SCALE 62; 2015 (5) SLR 486

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Madras Institute of Development Studies (MIDS) is governed by its Faculty Recruitment Rules, 2001

Summary: Service - Education - Selection process - Writ petitioner challenged before HC, decision of Executive Council of Institute approving appointment of various persons and respondent nos. 2 to 4 for post of Associate Professors - Single Judge of HC dismissed writ petition on ground that writ petition was not maintainable against Institute and also that there is no discrepancy with regard to qualification mentioned in advertisement and service rules - Aggrieved, writ petitioner filed appeal before DB of HC which allowed appeal and reversed order of Single Judge and quashed order dt.14-8-2006 approving appointment of respondents - Hence, instant appeals - Whether selection process is vitiated in law.

Held, short-listing of the candidates was done by the Director in consultation with the Chairman and also Senior Professors. Further, Committee constituted for the purpose of selection consists of eminent Scientists, Professor of Economic Studies and Planning and other members. The integrity of said members of the Committee has not been doubted by writ petitioner. Further, candidates so selected possessed all requisite qualifications and experience and, so, their appointment could not be questioned on the ground of lack of qualification and experience. Hence, HC ought not to have interfered with decision of the Institute in appointing respondent nos. 2 to 4 on the post of Associate Professor. Further, writ petitioner, without raising any objection to alleged variations in the contents of the advertisement and the Rules, submitted application and participated in the selection process by appearing before the Committee of experts and only after he was not selected for appointment, turned around and challenged the very selection process. Hence, in view of law laid down by SC in various decisions, DB of HC committed grave error in law by passing the impugned judgment reversing the order passed by Single Judge. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned judgment and order of DB of HC is set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Decision of Academic Authorities about the suitability of a candidate to be appointed as Associate Processor in a research institute could not normally be examined by the HC under its writ jurisdiction. Further, HC should not grant any relief to such petitioners who appear at examinations without protest and when not succeed in said examinations, file petitions challenging examination.
