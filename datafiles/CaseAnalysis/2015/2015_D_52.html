Devidas Ramachandra Tuljapurkar v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 361; (2015) 6 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 2612; 2015 CRLJ 3492; JT 2015 (5) SC 268; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 51; 2015(6) SCALE 356; 2016(6) SCALE 356; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisprudence, Modesty, Decency, Spirit, Censor, Freedom Of Speech, Human Dignity, Public Order, Escape, Press Council Act, 1978, Morality, Developments, Distinct, Struggle, Consciousness, Publisher, Notions, Obscene, Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986, Vulgar, Advocacy, Association, Communications Decency Act, 1996, Obscene Publication Act, 1959, the 1959 Act

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.153A,B,34,292 - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s.482 - Constitution of India,1950, arts.19(1)(a),(2) - Obscene poem - Unconditional apology - Quashing of charges - Complaint was lodged relating to publication of disputed poem meant for private circulation amongst members of All India Bank Association Union - Crime was registered for offences punishable u/ss.153A,B r/w s.34 of IPC - Charge sheet was filed for said offences along with s.292 of IPC against appellant, publisher and printer and author - On application for discharge Magistrate by order allowed discharge u/ss.153A,B of IPC but declined u/s.292 of IPC - On revision, same was dismissed by Trial Court - On application to HC u/s.482 of CrPC same was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of framing of charge u/s.292 of IPC is sustainable, regard being has to law pertaining to charge and HC has correctly applied principle.

Held, freedom of speech and expression u/art.19(1)(a) of the Constitution is not absolute in view of art.19(2) of the Constitution and by bringing in historically respected personality to arena of s.292 of IPC, neither new offence is created nor is ingredient interpreted. However, view of HC pertaining to framing of charge u/s.292 of IPC cannot be flawed. Appellant had published disputed poem which had already been recited in 1980 and was earlier published on dt.2-10-1986 by others. Appellant has published poem only in 1994. But immediately after coming to know about reactions of certain employees, he tendered unconditional apology in next issue of 'Bulletin'. Further, once appellant has tendered unconditional apology even before inception of proceedings and almost more than two decades have passed, charge framed against him as well as printer as he had printed as desired by the publisher, are liable to be quashed. Trial is open in regard to author who is co-accused and facing trial u/s.292 IPC. Thus, order of framing of charge u/s.292 of IPC is not sustainable in regard to publisher and printer and HC has not correctly applied principle. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - By bringing in historically respected personality to arena of s.292 of IPC, neither new offence is created nor is ingredient for offence under said section is interpreted.
