Krishna Bhatacharjee v Sarathi Choudhury and another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 770; (2016) 2 SCC 705; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 17; 2016 (1) CGLJ 105; 2016 CRLJ 330; 2016 (1) JCC 31; JT 2015 (11) SC 132; 2015 (4) KLT 999; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4200; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 623; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 151; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 152; 2015(12) SCALE 521; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 52

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Adultery, Omission, Aggrieved Person, Gift, Dealing, Violence, Continuing Offence, Member, Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Rules, 2006

Summary: Women & Children - Family & Personal - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 2(a), 12 - Judicial Separation - Claim for obtaining Stridhan - Rejection - Appellant and respondent was married in year 2005, however, it is stated that there was dowry demand by respondent husband and his relatives - However, due to failure to fulfill such demand, appellant was driven out of her matrimonial house - Respondent husband filed petition seeking judicial separation before Family Court - Appellant filed application before Trial Court for obtaining her Stridhan back from her husband, however, Trial Court dismissed suit of appellant wife on ground that claim preferred u/s. 12 of Act was not entertainable as she had ceased to be 'aggrieved person' u/s. 2(a) of Act and further that claim as put forth was barred by limitation - Further, appellant Court concurred findings of lower Court - Further, HC failed to interfere with order passed by lower Courts - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Hirachand Srinivas Managaonkar v Sunanda, 2001 Indlaw SC 20230, relied on, SC held that it is clear that there is distinction between decree for divorce and decree of judicial separation; in former, there is severance of status and parties do not remain as husband and wife, whereas in later, relationship between husband and wife continues and legal relationship continues as it has not been snapped. Hence, finding recorded by courts below which have been concurred by HC that parties having been judicial separated, appellant wife has ceased to be 'aggrieved person' is wholly unsustainable. Further, with respect to issue of limitation, SC held that There can be no dispute that wife can file suit for realization of stridhan but it does not debar her to lodge criminal complaint for criminal breach of trust. Further, SC held that concept of 'continuing offence' gets attracted from date of deprivation of stridhan, for neither husband nor any other family members can have any right over stridhan and they remain custodians. For purpose of Act, she can submit application to Protection Officer for one or more of reliefs under Act. SC held that in present case, wife had submitted application on 22-5-2010 and said authority had forwarded same on 1-6-2010 and in application, appellant wife had mentioned that husband had stopped payment of monthly maintenance from January 2010, hence, she had been compelled to file application for stridhan. SC held that having regard to said concept of 'continuing offence' and demands made, application was not barred by limitation and courts below and HC had fallen into error by dismissing application being barred by limitation. Hence, order passed by lower Courts is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - So long as status of aggrieved person remains and stridhan remains in custody of husband, wife can always put forth her claim u/s. 12 of Act .
