State of Maharashtra v Ramlal Devappa Rathod and others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 707; 2016 CRLJ 340; 2016 (1) JCC 294; JT 2015 (9) SC 188; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 133; 2015(10) SCALE 347; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Common Object, Assembly, Single Witness, Overt Act, Unlawful Assembly, Identity, Clothes, Fracture, Attack

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 326, 395, 427, 435, 436, 452 - Alleged Murder - Acquittal - Justification - It is stated that due to dispute over land , deceased victim was assaulted by respondent accused persons which resulted in death of victim - FIR was registered against 34 accused respondents - Trial Court respondents accused nos. 1, 2, 3, 7, 10, 12, 29 and 30 guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 452 read with s. 149 IPC while acquitted other respondents - However, HC vide impugned order acquitted respondents of charges against them on ground that entire case rested on sole testimony of PW12 and if according to her she had followed assailants and had tried to save her husband, possibility of her suffering any injury could not be ruled out but no such injuries were reported - Whether impugned order passed by HC acquitting respondent accused persons is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, deposition of PW12 shows that while her husband was being pursued and assaulted, her attention was focussed in so far as her husband was concerned, which is quite natural. All stages and acts at each stage are corroborated by other material on record. Further, fact that incident started at temple, then shifted next to well and then to cluster of houses of other persons is well-supported by blood stains found at these places as disclosed in spot panchnama as also by damage caused to houses. SC held that material on record including medical evidence thus lends complete support to version as unfolded through deposition of PW12. Further, SC held that intensity of incident where entire village stood against deceased, had impact on witnesses who turned hostile one after other. Further, though there are no physical injuries on her person but this by itself is no ground to reject her testimony. Further, pursuant to disclosure statements made by A1, A2 , A3, A29 and A30, certain weapons with blood stains were recovered immediately on day after incident. Further, PW 18 in his testimony deposed that such recoveries were made pursuant to disclosure statements of accused. Hence, SC held that deposition of PW12 is devoid of any exaggeration, completely trustworthy and reliable. Her deposition is well-supported by medical evidence and other material on record including destruction and damage to houses of other people. Hence, though she is sole witness, her evidence is completely reliable and trustworthy. Further, SC held that rule of prudence cannot mean that in every case of mob violence there must be more than one eye- witness. Hence, Trial Court was perfectly right and justified in relying upon testimony of sole witness PW12 and HC completely erred in applying test laid down in earlier case. Hence, SC held that view taken by HC being completely erroneous and unsustainable; hence, order passed by HC is set aside. However, out of eight accused found guilty by Trial Court, going by testimony of PW12, only six of them i.e. accused nos. A1, A2, A3, A12, A29 and A30 had caused final assault on deceased which resulted in his death. Other two accused, according to witness had set house of on fire and had not participated in final assault. Hence, benefit of doubt is granted to them and their acquittal is confirmed. However as regards other six accused, they having pursued, taken out victim by lifting him from house and thereafter assaulted him in field adjacent to house, case of prosecution as against them stands completely proved. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - It is not justified to acquit offenders in criminal case when guilt of accused it proved by prosecution beyond reasonable doubt.
