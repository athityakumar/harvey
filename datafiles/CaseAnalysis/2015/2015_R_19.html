Raj Bala v State of Haryana and others Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 570; (2016) 1 SCC 463; AIR 2015 SC 3142; 2015 CRLJ 4360; JT 2015 (7) SC 274; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 108; 2016 (1) RLW 683; 2015(9) SCALE 25; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Quantum of sentence - Respondent nos. 2 to 4 were put on trial for offence punishable u/s. 306 of IPC and Trial Court on perusal of material on record, held respondent nos. 2 to 4 guilty of offence punishable u/s. 306 of IPC - However, Trial Court taking lenient view on quantum of sentence, passed sentence of three years to respondents nos. 2 to 4 - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeal before HC which affirmed conviction but reduced sentence to period already undergone by respondents i.e., 4 months and 20 days - While so, informant also preferred revision before HC for conversion of criminal offence but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether reduction of sentence by HC is justified.

Held, once the offence u/s. 306 of IPC is proved, there should have been adequate and appropriate punishment. However, Single Judge of HC while dealing with appeal preferred by respondents remained quite unmindful and unconcerned to the obvious and, hence, the reduction of sentence by HC to the period already undergone is set aside. Further, even though mitigating factors that have been highlighted by Trial Court are absolutely non-mitigating factors and totally inconsequential for imposing sentence of three years, yet, as there was no appeal either by State or by persons aggrieved, sentence imposed by Trial Court is restored. Hence, appeal challenging reduction of sentence by HC is allowed and judgment of conviction and order of sentence by Trial Court is restored. Further, appeal challenging order passed in revision by HC is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Prime objective of criminal law is the imposition of adequate, just and proportional punishment that commensurate with the gravity, nature of the crime and manner in which the offence is committed keeping in mind the social interest and the conscience of the society.
