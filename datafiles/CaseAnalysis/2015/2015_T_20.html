Tarabai v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 36; (2015) 3 SCC 530; 2015 CRLJ 1326; JT 2015 (1) SC 331; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 743; 2015(1) SCALE 521

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 34 and 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113A - Dowry death - Conviction - Prosecution alleged that accused no. 1 (Mother-in-law) and accused No. 2 (sister- in-law) ill-treated deceased for not bring any cash, gold and new clothes in marriage - Further alleged that accused nos. 1 and 2 murdered deceased by burnt her - Trial Court convicted accused Nos. 1 and 2 for the offences punishable u/ss. 498A and 304-B r/w s. 34 of the IPC - HC dismissed the appeal in respect of the accused No.1 (appellant) and allowed the appeal in respect of accused No.2 by acquitting her of the charges - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, submission of the appellant that since benefit of doubt was given to accused No. 2, by the HC, on parity the same benefit should be extended to the accused No. 1 by acquitting her has no substance for the reason that there was enough evidence to prove the complicity of the appellant in commission of offence whereas the prosecution failed to adduce any evidence to prove the complicity of accused No.2. It has come in evidence that soon after the marriage, the accused No. 1 started making demand of gold, cash and clothes etc. from the deceased coupled with beating and ill-treating her for not satisfying the demands made by her. A young girl in early twenties ending her life with 100 % burns within 8 months of her marriage due to ill treatment, beating and demands made by mother-in-law cannot be over-looked to show sympathy towards the appellant. The Courts below have awarded 7 years' simple imprisonment to the appellant. The appellant should feel fortune to suffer only 7 years because having regard to the nature of commission of the offence and her complicity in the offence, it could have been even more than what has been awarded. Appeal dismissed.
