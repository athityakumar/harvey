K. V. S. Ram v Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 2015 SC 998; 2015 (2) AWC 1565; 2015 (144) FLR 994; JT 2015 (1) SC 252; 2015 (1) LLJ 257; 2015(1) SCALE 436; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 26; 2015 (2) SLR 738; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 23

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination from service, Superintendence u/arts. 226, 227 of Constitution

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Termination from service - Superintendence u/arts. 226, 227 of Constitution - Legality - Appellant (workman) was charged on allegation that he had secured appointment by producing a false transfer certificate - Disciplinary authority passed order imposing punishment of dismissal from service - Aggrieved appellant raised an industrial dispute - Labour Court directed the respondent (Corporation) to reinstate appellant in his original post with continuity of service but without backwages - Aggrieved respondent filed a petition before HC and Single Judge allowed the same - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed on the ground that charges were serious in nature - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that similar cases of other workmen who produced bogus certificate, were reinstated in service withholding of few increments with cumulative effect.

Held, in exercise of its power of superintendence u/art. 227 of Constitution, the HC can interfere with the order of the Tribunal, only, when there has been a patent perversity in the orders of Tribunal and Courts subordinate to it or where there has been gross and manifest failure of justice or the basic principles of natural justice have been flouted. When Labour Court has exercised its discretion keeping in view the facts of the case and the cases of similarly situated workmen, the HC ought not to have interfered with the exercise of discretion by the Labour Court. Once the Labour Court has exercised the discretion judicially, the HC can interfere with the award, only if it is satisfied that the award of the Labour Court is vitiated by any fundamental flaws. Court does not find that the award passed by the Labour Court suffers from any such flaws. While interfering with the award of the Labour Court, the HC did not keep in view the parameters laid down by this Court for exercise of jurisdiction by the HC u/arts. 226 and/or 227 of Constitution and the impugned judgment cannot be sustained. Appeal allowed.
