New India Assurance Company Limited v Hilli Multipurpose Cold Storage Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Vikramajit Sen, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 860; AIR 2016 SC 86; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 90; 2016 (114) ALR 184; 2016 (2) ALT(SC) 32; 2016 (1) AWC 594; 2016 (1) CompLJ 369; 2016 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2016 (1) CTC 810; JT 2015 (12) SC 39; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 28; 2016 (1) MLJ 196; 2016 (1) OLR 499; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 367; 2015(13) SCALE 352; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 199

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: District Forum

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8 r. 1 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 12, 13, 13(2)(a) - Complaint filed before Consumer Forum - Time to file reply - Upon receipt of complaint by District Forum, if complaint was admitted u/s. 12 of Act, copy of complaint was to be served upon opposite party and as per provisions of s. 13 of Act, opposite party had to give his version of case within period of 30 days from date of receipt of copy of complaint - Further, as per s. 13(2)(a), District Forum may extend period, not exceeding 15 days, to opposite party for giving his version - Further, in earlier judgment passed in 2002, it was held that by SC that in no case period beyond 45 days can be granted to opposite party for filing its version of case - However, in other judgment passed in 2005, considering provisions of O.8 r. 1 of CPC pertaining to grant of time or additional time for filing written statement or reply, in interest of justice, SC held that provisions of O. 8 r. 1 of CPC are not mandatory but directory in nature and therefore, in interest of justice, further time for filing reply can be granted, if circumstances are such that require grant of further time for filing reply - Whether in case under provisions of Act, where complaint has been filed and opposite party has not filed its version to case within 30 days or within extended period of 45 days, version given by opposite party can be accepted.

Held, Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others vs. Shrinath Chaturvedi, 2002 Indlaw SC 1757, relied on, SC held that judgment delivered in that case holds field, hence, District Forum can grant further period of 15 days to opposite party for filing his version or reply and not beyond that. Further, SC held that law laid down in Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others v Shrinath Chaturvedi, 2002 Indlaw SC 1757, was decided in 2002, whereas Kailash v Nanhku and other, 2005 Indlaw SC 269, was decided in 2005. Further, while deciding case of Kailash v Nanhku and others, 2005 Indlaw SC 269, SC ought to have respected view expressed in Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others v Shrinath Chaturvedi, 2002 Indlaw SC 1757 as judgment delivered in case of Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others v Shrinath Chaturvedi, 2002 Indlaw SC 1757 was earlier in point of time and was passed by larger Bench. Hence, law laid down by three-Judge Bench of SC in case of Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others v Shrinath Chaturvedi, 2002 Indlaw SC 1757 would prevail. Reference answered. Order accordingly.
