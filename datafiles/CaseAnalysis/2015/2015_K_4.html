K. Anbazhagan v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 298; (2015) 6 SCC 158; JT 2015 (4) SC 314; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 493; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 875; 2015(5) SCALE 577; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 469

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subordinate, Advocate General, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Karnataka Law Officers (Appointments and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1977

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 24(1), 24(8) 301(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1), 13(2) - Transfer of Case - Appointment of Public Prosecutor - Jurisdiction to appear in appeal - Appellant no. 5 was elected ad Chief Minister of State of Tamil Nadu, however, various allegations pertaining to amassing assets disproportionate to known sources of income, criminal proceedings were initiated against her and her associates - Appellants were charge sheeted for offences punishable u/s. 120B of IPC read with ss. 13(1) and 13(2) of Act - Appellant preferred petitions seeking transfer of case from State of Tamil Nadu to a court of equal and competent jurisdiction in any other State which was allowed and matter was transferred to State of Karnataka - Public Prosecutor was appointed to conduct case against accused persons, however, he resigned and respondent no.4 was appointed as Special Public Prosecutor vide notification issued by State of Tamil Nadu - Further, appeal was filed by appellant accused in HC and in that appeal respondent no.4 appeared as Public Prosecutor before HC - Different views emerged with respect to jurisdiction of respondent no. 4 to appear as Public Prosecutor in appeal, hence mater is placed before Court - Whether respondent no.4 was authorised to represent case of prosecution in HC in appeals filed by accused persons against their conviction by Trial court.

Held, s. 24(8) of CrPC confers power on State Govt. to appoint Special Public Prosecutor for any case or class of cases. Further, if word 'case' is given meaning to include appeal, it will be denuding power of appointing authority. Further, if Govt. by notification appoints eligible person clearly stating that he shall conduct trial as well as pursue appeal arising out of it, there will be no difficulty. SC held that Public Prosecutor who is appointed to conduct case before Trial Court cannot be deemed to be appointed for purpose of appeal arising therefrom solely because of language employed in s. 301(1) of CrPC. Further, State of Tamil Nadu has no authority to appoint respondent no.4, as Public Prosecutor to argue appeal and it is State of Karnataka which is sole prosecuting agency and it was alone authorized to appoint Public Prosecutor. Further, appointment of respondent no.4, as Public Prosecutor for trial did not make him eligible to prosecute appeal on behalf of prosecuting agency before HC. SC held that appointment of Public Prosecutor, as envisaged u/s. 24(1) of CrPC in HC is different than appointment of Public Prosecutor for District Courts; and that notification appointing respondent no.4 did not enable him to represent State of Karnataka in appeal. Hence, SC held that respondent no.4 respondent was not appointed by State of Karnataka to argue appeals before HC and he could not have appeared in appeal. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Annulment of appointment of Public Prosecutor - Re-hearing of Case - Determination - Whether annulment of appointment of Public Prosecutor would entail de novo hearing of appeal.

Held, in pursuance of order passed by HC, appeal has been heard on day to day basis. Further, Court has already heard appeal and is in process of preparation of judgment. SC held that there is no justification for rehearing of appeal as matter has been heard at length and reserved for verdict. SC allowed submission of written notes by appellants and respondent which shall be considered by lower Court. Further, written note of submissions, if any, by respondent no.4 shall not be considered by lower Court. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Power is conferred upon State Govt. to appoint persons to be Public Prosecutor to appear in trial and in appeal and in absence of notification, Public Prosecutor in trial cannot appear as public prosecutor in appeal unless appointed by State Govt.
