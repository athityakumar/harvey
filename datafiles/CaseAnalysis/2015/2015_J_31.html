Jeles Education Society and others v R. T. Bhitale

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 797; 2016 (148) FLR 207; JT 2015 (11) SC 356; 2015(12) SCALE 253; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 878; 2016 (1) SLR 730

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Backward Classes, Vacancy, Permanent, Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Services) Regulation Act, 1977, Maharashtra Private School Employees (Condition of Service) Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Private School Employees (Conditions of Services) Rules, 1981, rr. 9(9)(a), 42(3) - Temporary appointment against reserved post - HC collectively disposed off writ petitions filed by appellant-Society and by respondent by affirming orders passed by Tribunal dt.26-6-1992 holding, that appellant was inducted as a permanent employee, and his services were dispensed with in violation of statutory rules and that School Management was not justified in requiring respondent to tender his resignation u/r. 42(3) of the Rules, on account of the fact that he was a permanent employee, and not a temporary employee - Hence, instant appeals by appellant-Society- Whether respondent was liable to be treated as a permanent employee.

Held, respondent did not fulfill desired qualifications for occupying the permanent vacancy created by Raut, inasmuch as he did not possess the educational qualification of Sanskrit. On account of his not possessing the qualification of Sanskrit, respondent was clearly not eligible for filling up the vacancy created by Raut, on a permanent basis. Further, even though respondent was found to be most meritorious candidate, out of those who had responded to the advertisement dt.4-12-1987, yet, as respondent did not possess the qualifications stipulated for the advertised vacancy, it was well within the right of the appellant-society, to offer him temporary appointment till the end of the academic year, u/r.9(9)(a). Furthermore, u/r. 9(9)(a) of the Rules, candidates could only be appointed on temporary basis, or on a year to year basis, when none of the backward class candidates is found suitable. Accordingly, when the respondent was appointed temporarily on 7-12-1987 his appointment was in conformity and in consonance with r. 9(9)(a) of the Rules. Hence, termination of respondent services on 30-4-1988 was not only in consonance with his appointment order dt.7-12-1987, but was also in conformity with the statutory rules. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside.

Ratio - U/r. 9(9)(a) of the Rules, if out of the candidates who had applied for the advertised post, a backward class candidate though not fulfilling the qualifications stipulated for the post, was found to be the most meritorious, then, he could be appointed against the advertised vacancy on temporary basis.
