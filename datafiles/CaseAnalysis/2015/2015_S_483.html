Sunil Kumar Verma and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 864; (2016) 1 SCC 397; 2015 (147) FLR 864; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 566

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Retrenchment, MISCELLANEOUS, Corporations, Uttar Pradesh Absorption of Retrenched Employees of Government or Public Corporations in Government Service Rules, 1991, U.P. Absorption of Retrenched Employees of the State Government/Public Sector Corporation in Government Service (Recession) Rules, 2003

Summary: Service - U.P. Absorption of Retrenched Employees of the State Government/Public Sector Corporation in Government Service (Recession) Rules, 2003 - Uttar Pradesh Absorption of Retrenched Employees of Government or Public Corporations in Government Service Rules, 1991 - Absorbtion in service - Failure to grant benefit - Justification - Respondent Corporation was wound up on 8-1-2-1999 - Further, 1991 Rules was applicable to State - Appellant and others filed petition seeking absorption under 1991 Rules in question - Lower court held that Rules were rescinded on 8-4-2003 and much prior to that employees had represented and respondents were required to consider their rights for absorption within two months and regard being had to rule position, it could safely be held that rights of employees had crystallized much before Rules were rescinded - Hence, respondents were directed to absorb petitioners - Order passed by lower court was confirmed by DB and SC - Further, petitions based on similar facts which were preferred in year 2001 were still pending before HC - However, in case of petitioner, DB failed to grant relief to petitioner - Hence, present appeal.

Held, petitioner who had approached HC in 2004, did not stand on better footing than present appellants. Lower court had treated him as retrenched employee on similar facts. Further, there had already been interpretation of 2003 Rules by lower Court which has been affirmed up to SC. Hence, DB could not have entered into analysis of ratio of earlier judgment and discussion on binding precedents. SC held that it is absolutely inappropriate on part of HC to go in search of ratio of judgment rendered by lower court on earlier occasion, when controversy was put to rest by SC. Decision rendered by SC inter se parties was required to be followed in same fact situation. SC held that when factual matrix was absolutely luminescent and did not require any kind of surgical dissection, there was no necessity to take different view. Hence, SC found no reason that appellants should not reap benefits of absorption and, accordingly, it is directed that they shall be absorbed by State Govt. as per their seniority and be given benefit of increments. Further, cause of justice would be best sub-served if each of appellant is paid 40% of back wages, and it is so directed. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Similar benefits is to be granted to party to suit where facts are same and benefit is allowed by court.
