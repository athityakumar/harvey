Bhupal Singh and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 250; (2015) 5 SCC 801; AIR 2015 SC 2073; 2015 (110) ALR 207; 2015 (3) AWC 2369; 2015 (2) CalLT(SC) 18; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 79; JT 2015 (3) SC 567; 2016 (1) MahLJ 15; 2015 (3) MLJ 746; 2016 (1) MPLJ 56; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 634; 2015 (128) RD 125; 2015(4) SCALE 394; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 975; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 11

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 23 - Fair market value of land - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4 and acquired large chunk of lands belonging to appellants and others and declaration was published u/s. 6 of the Act - Later, Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) passed award fixing compensation at Rs.16.52 per sq. yard as fair market value of acquired land payable to appellants - Aggrieved, appellants sought reference to Civil Court for re-determination of compensation and reference Court enhanced rate of compensation to Rs.22 per sq. yard - However, appellants filed appeals before HC challenging award passed by Reference Court - DB of HC partly allowed appeals by enhancing compensation at Rs.50 per sq. yard. - However, not satisfied with award of HC, instant appeals were preferred by appellants seeking enhanced award - Whether HC is justified in awarding compensation at Rs. 50 per sq. yard.

Held, HC having rightly concluded that fair market value of land in question on date of acquisition was Rs.63 per sq. yard, there was no justification on the part of HC to have then reduced it to any rate less than Rs.63 sq. yard. Further, in view of findings recorded by lower courts in relation to location of land, its potentiality, surroundings, rate of adjacent land determined by Courts, the condition of the acquired underdeveloped lands, the expenditure required to develop the acquired land to start the activities, per cent of deductions to be made, its proximity to the various places in nearby town, and lastly, fact that appellants failed to file any sale deed of any parcel of land sold in near proximity of acquired land, the fair market value of the lands in question as on date of acquisition could reasonably worked out to Rs.63 per sq. yard. Hence, LAO is directed to calculate compensation payable to appellants at Rs. 63 per sq. yard and impugned judgment and orders are modified to such extent and appeals partly allowed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per decisions of SC, inter alia, when acquired land is large chunk of undeveloped land having potential and was acquired for residential purpose then while determining the fair market value of the lands on the date of acquisition, the appropriate deductions are also required to be made.
