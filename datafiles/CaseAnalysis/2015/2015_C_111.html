Poonam Spark (Private) Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 508; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 413; JT 2015 (7) SC 64; 2015(8) SCALE 332

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Job work - Manufacture of new product - Payment of Excise duty - Company imported various components of water purifier and supplied same to appellant - Job work was assigned to appellant for assembly of water purifier on behalf of company - Revenue stated that work carried out by appellant, namely, assemble of components resulted into new product having different name and character and it amounted to 'manufacture' as per s. 2(f) of Act, hence, appellant was liable to pay excise duty - Adjudicating Authority passed order thereby confirming demand raised in show cause notice and penalty was also imposed on appellant - Further, appeal filed by appellant is dismissed which was affirmed by Tribunal - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Tribunal has recorded finding that company supplied materials to appellant and appellants undertake job of assembling all items received from comp on base plate and thus brings into existence new and commercially different commodity known as Water Purification & Filteration System. SC held that it is on this basis, finding of fact is arrived at by all three Authorities that activity undertaken by appellant amounts to 'manufacture' within meaning of s. 2(f) of Act, since end result of process or activity resulted in new and different commercial product. Hence, on basis of aforesaid findings which are concurrent findings of all Courts below, correct legal principle has been applied. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Job of assembling when amounts to production of new product would come under ambit of manufacture and is liable to pay excise duty.
