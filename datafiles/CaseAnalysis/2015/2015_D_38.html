Construction and Design Services v Delhi Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 2015 SC 1282; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 22; 2015 (109) ALR 684; 2015 (2) AWC 1659; 2015 (1) CGLJ 455; 2015 (3) CTC 182; JT 2015 (2) SC 60; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1060; 2015 (2) MLJ 250; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 445; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 878; 2015(2) SCALE 48; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 341; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 115

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 73 and 74 - Breach of contract - Compensation - Respondent (DDA) awarded a contract to the appellant for constructing a sewerage pumping station - Appellant failed to complete the same, the contract was terminated - Respondent levied compensation of Rs.20,86,446/- for delay in execution of the project by an order of penalty - On failure of the appellant to respond to the said order, the respondent filed suit for recovery of the said amount with interest - Single Judge dismissed the suit holding that the respondent had not treated the time fixed for performance of the contract as of essence and the compensation stipulated in cl. 2 of the agreement was in the nature of penalty - On appeal, DB reversed the view taken by the Single Judge and held that delay in a contract of construction of a public utility service could itself be a ground for compensation without proving the actual loss - Appellant filed a review petition which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, delay in executing the work resulted in loss for which the respondent was entitled to reasonable compensation. Evidence of precise amount of loss may not be possible but in absence of any evidence by the party committing breach that no loss was suffered by the party complaining of breach, the Court has to proceed on guess work as to the quantum of compensation to be allowed in the given circumstances. Since the respondent also could have led evidence to show the extent of higher amount paid for the work got done or produce any other specific material but it did not do so, it will be fair to award half of the amount claimed as reasonable compensation. Decree granted by the DB is modified to the effect that the respondent is entitled to half of the amount claimed with rate of interest as awarded by the DB. Out of the amount deposited in SC, the respondent will be entitled to withdraw the said decretal amount and the appellant will be entitled to take back the remaining. Appeals disposed of
