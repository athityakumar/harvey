TATA Iron and Steel Company Limited v District Transport Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 828; AIR 2016 SC 989; 2015(11) SCALE 570; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 325

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, Bihar and Orissa Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1930

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Bihar and Orissa Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1930, ss. 2(j), 6, 6(1A) - Capativity Use of Vehicles - Additional Motor Vehicles Tax - Challenged - Appellant/company used most of vehicles for business purposes and were adapted to be used for carriage of passengers and goods - However, respondent/ adjudicating authority held that vehicles were used for reward in return for work or service and vehicles tax was liable to be paid - HC dismissed writ petition filed by company - Hence, instant appeal - whether Adjudicating Authority was correct in holding that use of vehicles in the manner mentioned above amounts to using for 'reward'.

Held, s. 2(j) of 1930 Act, states that a motor vehicle falling under the definition of 'Public Service Motor Vehicle' has to satisfy the following conditions:(1) It is used or adapted to be used for the carriage of passengers and goods; (2) Such use has to be for 'hire' or 'reward'. Further, the expression 'hire' or 'reward' would mean that the vehicle must be run for the benefit of another. When the owner of the vehicle uses the vehicle himself but for the benefit of another person i.e. third person and receives some charges for the said use, that may fulfill the requirement of 'reward'. In instant case, most of the vehicles are used by appellant for business purposes such as, used for carrying the children of the employees from their residence to schools and back. Hence, that would not be treated as using the vehicle for public or third party and does not fulfill second condition as required u/s. 2(j) of the Act. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
