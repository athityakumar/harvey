Parisons Agrotech (Private) Limited and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 616; (2015) 9 SCC 657; AIR 2015 SC 3335; 2015 (323) E.L.T. 3; 2015(9) SCALE 165; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Keywords: FEMA, Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992, Director General

Summary: Trade - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992 - Prohibition on import of palm oil - Appellants filed writ petitions challenging validity of notifications issued by Central Govt. prohibiting import of palm oil through all ports of State - However, Single Judge dismissed said petitions and on appeal, DB of HC also dismissed appeals and hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned notifications are sustainable.

Held, there is a sufficient public good sought to be achieved by laying down the exception banning the imports of crude palm oil through ports in the State. Hence, impugned judgment of HC upholding notifications are sustainable and so, appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - U/s. 3(2) of the Act, Govt. could make such exceptions as it deems fit while issuing the Notifications or the Order in prohibiting, restricting or regulating import or export of goods etc. Further, in such process, it could restrict the import of particular goods through particular ports or disallow the import through specified ports; however, such action could not be arbitrary or irrational and should be backed by sound reasons.
