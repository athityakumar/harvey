Cochin Shipyard Limited v Apeejay Shipping Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 789; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 156; 2016 (2) ALT(SC) 17; JT 2015 (10) SC 661; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4406; 2015 (8) MLJ 376; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 230; 2015(12) SCALE 370B; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 994

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Legal Misconduct

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30, 33 - Misconduct of arbitrator - Examination of witness - Appellant moved Court for passing decree in terms of award passed by Arbitrator and respondent filed petition u/ss. 30 and 33 of the Act to set aside award of arbitrator - While so, during pendency of petition, respondent filed application seeking permission to examine Arbitrator and GM of respondent as witnesses - Civil Court rejected said application and aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC though concurred with views expressed by Civil Court on examination of Arbitrator, yet, granted liberty to respondent to produce other available evidence to substantiate its claim and also granted liberty to summon entire record including the orders passed in the course of arbitral proceeding and accordingly, modified orders of Civil Court - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether on basis of allegations of legal misconduct, HC could have allowed examination of witness.

Held, person urging ground of legal misconduct has to satisfy the court from the records of the arbitral proceedings that there has been a legal misconduct on the part of the arbitrator as a consequence of which the award gets vitiated. However, examination of any witness in court to substantiate a stance of legal misconduct on the part of the arbitrator is impermissible. Hence, respondent is at liberty to establish ground of legal misconduct from the arbitral proceedings and Civil Court could requisition records from Arbitrator, and respondent would be at liberty to advance arguments for pressing the factum of misconduct from said records. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Legal misconduct has to be manifest or palpable from the proceedings before the Arbitrator and the question of adducing any kind of oral evidence to substantiate the plea or stand or stance does not arise. Evidence could not be adduced in court to substantiate the challenge on the score of legal misconduct.
