Shabnam v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 515; (2015) 6 SCC 702; AIR 2015 SC 3648; 2015 CRLJ 3274; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 350; 2015(7) SCALE 1; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Capital Punishment, Right To Life, Decency, Death Sentence, Human Dignity, Mercy, Prisoner, Aggravating

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 136, 137 - Execution of death warrants - Failure to avail remedy - Justification - Accused were convicted for committing murder of seven persons who were members of accused family - Trial Court awarded death penalty to both accused which was confirmed by HC - Further, appeal of accused were dismissed by SC thereby confirming conviction and sentence of death imposed on them - It is alleged that death warrants have been issued by Trial Court with in six days of dismissal of appeals which are impermissible inasmuch as various remedies which are available to convicts, even after dismissal of appeals by SC are still open and yet to be exercised by them - Whether execution of death warrants is illegal and contrary to provisions of Constitution.

Held, convicts have not exhausted their judicial and administrative remedies, which are still open to them even if their appeals in highest Court have failed affirming imposition of death penalty. Those appeals were filed via route of art. 136 of Constitution. However, law gives such persons another chance, namely, to seek review of orders so passed, by means of filing of review petition. It is to provided u/art. 137 of Constitution. Further, right to file mercy petitions to Governor of State and to President of India also remains in tact. Hence, SC held that till time limitation period for filing review petition and thereafter reasonable time for filing mercy petition has not lapsed, issuing of death warrants would be violative of art. 21 of Constitution. SC held condemned prisoners also have right to dignity and execution of death sentence cannot be carried out in arbitrary, hurried and secret manner without allowing convicts to exhaust all legal remedies. Further, execution of death penalty should be carried out according to procedure laid down under law. Hence, SC held that death warrants were signed by Trial Court in haste, without waiting for exhaustion of remedies on part of convicts, same are hereby quashed and set aside. Petitions allowed.

Ratio - Death penalty cannot be executed by Court till time limitation period for availing remedies provided under Constitution is not lapsed.
