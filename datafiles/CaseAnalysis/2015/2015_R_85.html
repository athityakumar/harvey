State of Rajasthan v Ramesh

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 769; 2016 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; 2016 (1) JCC 353; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 635; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 175; 2015(12) SCALE 550; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Station House Officer, Hanging, Autopsy, Provocation, Asphyxia

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 300, 302, 304 - Alleged culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Acquittal - Justification - Telephonic information was given that respondent's daughter has committed suicide by hanging - FIR was registered relating to offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - Trial Court convicted respondent accused u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - However, DB vide impugned order has set aside conviction order passed against respondent by Trial Court holding that chain of circumstances as against convict was not complete to come to conclusion that accused respondent committed murder of his daughter - Whether impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, through medico legal evidence on record, SC held that it was not case where view could have been taken that deceased died of hanging. Further, there was no reason to disagree with opinion given by PW 8 doctor that deceased had died of asphyxia as result of pressure over neck. Though PW-10 minor daughter of accused has stated that her elder sister's body was found hanging, however, witness was got declared hostile by prosecution, and Trial Court rightly disbelieved her statement, for reason that after losing her elder sister, she was not in position to lose her father. Further, scrutinizing evidence on record of PW 9 and PW 4, on record, SC held that it is proved beyond reasonable doubt on record that when accused saw his daughter talking to PW 9 he got suddenly provoked and lost his power of self-control, slapped her, took her inside house, and caused death of his daughter by strangulation and throttling. Hence, SC held that going through medical evidence and oral testimony of witnesses, it is held that prosecution has successfully proved charge of culpable homicide not amounting to murder punishable u/s. 304 Part I against accused respondent. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Respondent is convicted u/s. 304 Part I IPC and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for ten years. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Acquittal order passed in favor of accused is not justified when prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
