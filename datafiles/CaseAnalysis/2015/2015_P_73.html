Prof. N. K. Ganguly v CBI New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 800; (2016) 2 SCC 143; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 2016 CRLJ 371; 2016 (1) JCC 243; 2016 (3) JCC 1523; JT 2015 (11) SC 113; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 605; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 98; 2015(12) SCALE 500; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 192

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheating, Conspiracy, CBI, Summons, Official Duty, Search Warrant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Previous sanction of Govt. - Quashing of proceedings - Criminal case was registered u/s. 120-B of IPC read with ss.13(1)(d) and 13(2) of the Act, against appellants in matter relating to alleged unauthorized and illegal transfer of plots from ICPO to ICPO-ICMR Housing Society - Later, Special Judge vide order dt.8-11-2012 taken cognizance and issued summons to appellants to face trial for said offences - Aggrieved, appellants filed applications before HC for quashing of proceedings but vide common judgment, HC dismissed applications - Hence, instant appeals - Whether previous sanction of Central Govt. is required to prosecute appellants.

Held, as allegations made against appellants in the final report filed by respondent alleged that offences were committed by them in discharge of their official duty, it was essential for the Special Judge to correctly decide as to whether previous sanction from Central Govt. u/s. 197 of CrPC was required to be taken by respondent, before taking cognizance and passing an order issuing summons to the appellant for their presence. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and proceedings taking cognizance and issuing summons to appellants are quashed in absence of previous sanction obtained from Central Govt. to prosecute the appellants as required u/s. 197 of CrPC. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - For the purpose of obtaining previous sanction from the appropriate Govt. u/s.197 of CrPC, it is imperative that the alleged offence is committed in discharge of official duty by the accused.
