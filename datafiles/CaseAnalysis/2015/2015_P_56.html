Prasad Shrikant Purohit v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 266; (2015) 7 SCC 440; AIR 2015 SC 2514; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2853; 2015 CRLJ 2739; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 555; 2015(5) SCALE 79; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 575

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Order, Judicial Notice, Commitment, Special Rules, Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Maharashtra Control of Organized Crime Act, 1999 - Continuing unlawful activity - On 21-11-2003, there was bomb explosion at Parbhani and later at Jalna - In case pertaining to Parbhani, charge sheet was filed on 7-9-2006 against accused no. 1 and supplementary chargesheet was filed against four accused persons - In case of Jalna, charge sheet was filed against accused no. 1 on 30-9-2006 and two supplementary charge sheets were filed on 7-1-2008 and 14-1-2008 agasint four additional accused persons and against five accused persons - While so, on 13-11-2008, supplementary charge sheet was field against appellant-Rakesh in Parbhani case and supplementary charge sheet was filed against him in Jalna case on 15-11-2008 - While so, as there was bomb blast at Malegaon on 29-9-2008, charge sheet was filed against appellants on 20-11-2008, under the Act and sanction was also granted u/s. 23(2) of the Act - Instant appeals were concerning application of the Act - Whether the Act is applicable to instant case and consequently, rejection of bail by Trial Court and as confirmed by HC is justified.

Held, in respect of accused no. 7, he has been charge-sheeted in Parbhani, Jalna and Malegaon bomb blast and as per charge-sheet filed dagaisnt him, in Malegaon his direct involvement has been alleged. Further, accused no. 7 had nexus with the member of an 'organized crime syndicate' and also had very nexus with the offence in the nature of an 'organized crime' of the two earlier cases, Parbhani and Jalna and also direct involvement in Malegaon bomb ballast. Hence, accused no. 7 activity and involvement in all three bomb blasts disclose nexus with crime and also with the other accused involved in the crime and thereby the satisfaction of definition of 'continuing unlawful activity' of an 'organized crime' on behalf of an 'organized crime syndicate' is satisfactorily shown. Hence, by virtue of s. 21(4) of the Act, accused no. 7 is not entitled for the grant of bail and he does not fall within the excepted category of ss. 21(4)(a) or 21(4)(b). However, in case of other accused-appellants, their involvement is with reference to only Malegaon bomb blast and they were not proceeded against for offences relating to Parbhani and Jalna and also it could not be ascertained as to the involvement of appellants either by way of nexus with any accused who is a member of an 'organized crime syndicate' or such nexus with the offence of an 'organized crime' pertaining to Parbhani and Jalna. Hence, in view of absence of any material to disclose any nexus with accused of an 'organized crime syndicate' or with the offence in nature of an 'organized crime, in Parbhani and Jalna, bail applications of appellants other than accused no, 7 could be considered by Special Court. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - If in the preceding 10 years from the date of their continuing unlawful activity if more than one charge-sheet has been filed before a competent Court which had taken cognizance of such offence which would result in imposition of a punishment of three years or more, undertaken by a person individually or jointly either as a member of an 'organized crime syndicate' or on its behalf, such crime if falls within the definition of 'organized crime', the invocation of the Act would be the resultant position. Further, cognizance is of an offence and not of the offender, and it does not involve any formal action and as soon as the Magistrate applies his judicial mind to the suspected commission of offence, cognizance takes place.
