Dharam Chand v Chairman, New Delhi Municipal Council and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 506; (2015) 10 SCC 612; AIR 2015 SC 2819; 2015 (5) AWC 5412; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 176; JT 2015 (7) SC 67; 2016 (3) MahLJ 661; 2015 (6) MLJ 702; 2016 (2) MPLJ 552; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 368; 2015(8) SCALE 295; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950 - Relocation for security reasons - Fundamental Right to carry business - Determination - Appellant stated that he was allotted area opposite SC for operating tea staff in year 1999 and since then he was operating same, however, in year 2011 order was issued by Enforcement Department for temporary suspension of various Tehbazari holders, including appellant, for security reasons - Lower court held that order of relocation was issued due to security reasons, which was in public interest and aforesaid terms could never be said to be illegal or unconstitutional and matters of security must be left to wisdom and decision of police - Order passed by lower court is affirmed by DB on grounds that appellant's relocation due to security reasons was in terms of letter dt. 20-5-1999 which had granted Tehbazari rights to him - Whether order passed by lower Courts is in accordance with law.

Held, Deputy Commissioner (Security) produced before SC confidential folder containing many messages received by authority giving threat of exploding bomb blasts in different places. SC held that notwithstanding constitutional right of citizen to carry on business but such right is subject to certain restrictions. It cannot be disputed that there are certain areas which may be required to keep free of such types of kiosks for security reasons. Court cannot direct administration to allow such kiosk even if there is threat to safety and security. Further, appellant has right to earn his livelihood, but on other hand there is serious issue of safety and security of premises near SC, hence, SC has to balance between two. Purpose involving general interest of community as opposed to interest of individual directly or indirectly has to be balanced. Merely because of contention of appellant and respondents that after bomb blasts took place in Delhi HC compound in 2011, no such incident happened till date, it cannot be presumed that such incident will not happen in near future. Court cannot assume and presume that there is no threat to safety and security of SC and its vicinity and allow appellant to continue said business. Hence, order passed by lower Courts is justified and requires no interference. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Fundamental rights granted to citizen under constitution are subject to certain restrictions which include public interest and security.
