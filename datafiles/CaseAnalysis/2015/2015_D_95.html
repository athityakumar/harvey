National Textile Corporation (MN) Limited v Durga Trading Company and others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 177; AIR 2015 SC 2202; 2015 (113) ALR 17; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 788; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 496; 2016 (130) RD 105; 2015(2) SCALE 464; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1972, Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1995, Textile Undertakings (Taking Over of Management) Act, 1983, Legality, Removal of movable structures/fixtures

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971, s. 5A, 4(2) (b) r/w s. 7(1)(3), 2(g) - Textile Undertakings (Taking Over of Management) Act, 1983 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, 53A - Textile Undertakings (Nationalization) Act, 1995, s. 5(1) - Removal of movable structures/fixtures - Legality - Respondent no.1 filed petition before HC challenging the proceedings initiated by appellant against u/s. 5A for removal of movable structures/fixtures and u/s. 4(2) (b) r/w s. 7(1) and (3) of the Act in respect of subject premises - HC allowed the said petition by impugned judgment and order - Hence instant appeal - Whether proceedings initiated by appellant before Estate Officer against respondent no.1 under the Act should continue or appellant should be relegated to prefer suit before the civil court as held by HC.

Held, detailed tabular chart was presented before the Court to demonstrate that the Real Estate Division was part and parcel of textile undertaking. In the said chart it was mentioned that of the remaining plots, there were certain old godowns of the textile mill and they were sold by petitioners (SSML) to charitable trust of tantias in 1974-75 for setting off loans taken from the trust for textile business. The aforesaid chart produced by one of parties before SC was though noticed but no finding has been given by SC that the property in question was sold by the textile undertaking prior to commencement of 1983 Act. On the other hand if show that the land in question was point of the textile mills. A suit for specific performance was filed by respondent no.1 before HC against SSML 25 years after unregistered agreement to sell, thereby, acknowledging that there was no registered document of title with respondent no.1. The said suit is still pending. Thus, it is clear that all other rights and interests in or arising out of such property as were existing immediately before the appointed day in the ownership, possession, power or control of the textile company in relation to the said undertaking vested with the Central Govt. and by virtue of s. 3(2) of the 1995 Act stood transferred to, and vested in, the National Textile Corporation. Liability if any of the owner of textile undertaking that's SSML of any period to appointed day is liability of such owner (SSML) and can be enforceable against him and not against the Central Govt. or National Textile Corporation in view of s. 5(1) of the 1995 Act. Therefore respondent no.1 cannot derive any advantage against the Central Govt. or the National Textile Corporation on the ground of pendency of suit against the owner (SSML). SC viewed that the DB failed to analyze the provisions correctly and wrongly presumed that the property in question has been sold to the Textile Undertaking prior to the commencement of the 1983 Act. The Court wrongly relied on s. 53A of the 1882 Act to hold that respondent no.1 has valid defence available under the said provision and hence erred in holding that respondent no. 1 is an authorized occupant within the meaning of s. 2(g) of the 1971 Act. Hence, the impugned judgment passed by DB is set aside and upheld notices issued u/ss. 4 and 7 of the 1971 Act. Appeal allowed.
