Inspector of Police and another v Battenapatla Venkata Ratnam and another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 2015 SC 2403; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2064; 2015 (2) CGLJ 272; 2015 CRLJ 2942; 2015 (2) JCC 1390; JT 2015 (4) SC 147; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 328; 2015 (4) RLW 3021; 2015(5) SCALE 253; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 468, 477A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Sanction - Quashing of Proceedings - Complainant-District Registrar lodged complaint against respondents that while they were working as Sub-Registrars they conspired with stamp vendors and document writers and other staff to gain monetary benefit and thereby cheated the public and the Government - HC quashed the proceedings against respondents on the sole ground that there was no sanction u/s. 197 CrPC - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, alleged indulgence of the officers in cheating, fabrication of records or misappropriation cannot be said to be in discharge of their official duty. Their official duty is not to fabricate records or permit evasion of payment of duty and cause loss to the Revenue. Unfortunately, the HC missed these crucial aspects. Trial Court has correctly taken the view that if at all the said view of sanction is to be considered, it could be done at the stage of trial only. Appeals allowed.
