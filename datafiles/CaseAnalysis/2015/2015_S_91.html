Videocon International Limited v Securities and Exchange Board of India

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 16; (2015) 4 SCC 33; AIR 2015 SC 1042; 2015 (1) BC 443; [2015] 188 Comp Cas 566; 2015 (1) CompLJ 465; JT 2015 (1) SC 286; 2015(1) SCALE 293; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Mandate, Person Aggrieved, Controller, Securities Appellate Tribunal, Deletion, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Amendment) Act, 2002, Australian Commonwealth Judiciary Act, 1903, Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Amendment) Ordinance, 2002, Central Provinces of Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, s. 15Z - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Amendment) Act, 2002, ss. 27 and 32 - Amendment of statute - Maintainability of appeal before HC - Whether an order passed by the Securities Appellate Tribunal before 29-10-2002 would be appealable under the unamended provision of s. 15Z of the 1992 Act before the HC.

Held, amendment of a statute, which is not retrospective in operation, does not affect pending proceedings, except where the amending provision expressly or by necessary intendment provides otherwise. Pending proceedings are to continue as if the unamended provision is still in force. Amendment to s. 15Z of the 1992 Act, contemplates a mere change of forum of the second appellate remedy. Despite the aforesaid, it just and appropriate, in the facts and circumstances of the present case, to delve on the above subject as well. In dealing with the submission advanced at the hands of the appellant, on the subject of forum, SC will fictionally presume, that the amendment to s. 15Z by the 2002 Act had no effect on the second appellate remedy made available to the parties, and further that, the amendment merely alters the forum of the second appeal, from the HC (under the unamended provision), to the SC (consequent upon the amendment). All such appeals as had been filed by the Board, prior to 29-10-2002, would have to be accepted as vested, and must be adjudicated accordingly. All appeals preferred by the respondent, before the HC, were maintainable. Appeal dismissed.
