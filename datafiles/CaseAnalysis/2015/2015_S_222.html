Satish Kumar Jayanti Lal Dabgar v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 162; (2015) 7 SCC 359; 2015 CRLJ 1959; 2016 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2015 (3) SC 48; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 754; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 230; 2015(3) SCALE 344; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 375, 376 - Alleged marriage with minor girl - Conviction - Mercy Plea - Complaint was filed stating that prosecutrix was kidnapped by appellant - Further, appellant surrendered himself and investigation revealed sufficient evidence against appellant - Trial Court convicted for offences u/ss. 363, 366, 376 of IPC and was sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for committing offences - HC confirmed conviction, however, it modified sentence by reducing it to rigorous imprisonment for period of 4 years instead of 7 years for offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC - Appellant pleaded that entire episode was result of love affair between appellant and prosecutrix and every act between them was consensual - Further, even prosecutrix was married and had one child and, therefore, was happily settled in her matrimonial home, hence, appellant should be accorded sympathetic treatment by reducing sentence imposed upon him - Whether conviction order passed by HC was justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Trial Court based on based on evidence of register of birth and death held that prosecutrix was a minor girl on the date of incident, hence, clause sixthly of s. 375 of IPC would get attracted making her consent for sexual intercourse as immaterial and inconsequential. SC held that even when there is consent of girl below 16 years, other partner in sexual act is treated as criminal who has committed offence of rape and law leaves no choice to him and he cannot plead that act was consensual. SC held that case has to be treated where appellant has committed rape of minor girl which is regarded as heinous crime. Such act of sexual assault has to be abhorred and if consent of minor is treated as mitigating circumstance, it may lead to disastrous consequences. SC held that merely because appellant has now married hardly becomes mitigating circumstance. Likewise, appellant cannot plead that prosecutrix is also married and having child, hence, appellant should be leniently treated. It is not case where appellant has married prosecutrix. Further, HC has already reduced sentence from seven years rigorous imprisonment to 4 years u/s. 376 of IPC. Hence, appellant is not entitled to any further mercy. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Mercy Plea or plea to reduce sentence would be considered based on mitigating circumstances, nature of offence, age of victim and other factors.
