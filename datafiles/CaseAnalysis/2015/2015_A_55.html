Amrutlal Liladharbhai Kotak and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 143; (2015) 4 SCC 452; AIR 2015 SC 1355; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1656; 2015 CRLJ 1951; 2015 (2) JCC 1148; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1205; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 715; 2015(2) SCALE 688; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 304A, 306, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Abetment of suicide - Presumption as to guilt of husband - Deceased was found hanging to fan in house and criminal complaint against appellants were lodged for the same - On trial, appellants were convicted for offences punishable u/ss. 498A, 304B and 306 read with s. 114 of IPC by the Trial Court and the same was upheld by HC on appeal - Hence, instant appeal on ground that prosecution failed to substantiate guilt of appellants u/ss. 306 and 304B of IPC - Whether impugned judgments could be upheld.

Held, as per version of PWs, there is a reasonable apprehension of crime committed by appellants. Further, reasonable apprehension could be raised, for that accused committed crime u/s. 304B of IPC and presumption could be raised u/s. 113B of the Act, since seven years of marriage had not been completed and also it was established from version of PWs that there was demand for dowry and deceased was being mentally harassed. Hence, impugned judgments of HC or Trial Court could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per s. 113B of the Act, when a woman commits suicide within a period of seven years from the date of marriage then presumption arises that such suicide has been abetted by her husband or by such relative of her husband.
