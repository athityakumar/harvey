ONGC Limited v Petroleum Coal Labour Union and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 286; (2015) 6 SCC 494; AIR 2015 SC 2210; 2015(4) ALL MR 476; 2015 (146) FLR 443; JT 2015 (4) SC 247; 2015 (2) LLJ 257; 2015(5) SCALE 353; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, Oil and Natural Gas Commission Recruitment and Promotion Regulations, 1980

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 25T, 25U - Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Act, 1946 - Regularisation of Services - Entitlement - Appellant-Corporation employed concerned workmen some of whom are members of the respondent-Union, as security guards and security supervisors through contractors - Services were utilised by the appellant through the Co-operative Society to meet its requirements and for the time period for which required, thus dispensing with intermediary contractors - Concerned workmen who were engaged through contractors and those who were members of the Co-operative Society became employees of the appellant on temporary basis and raised an industrial dispute claiming regularisation of their services in the appellant Corporation - HC held that concerned workmen were entitled for regularisation and that their appointment cannot be stated to be illegal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, workmen have clearly completed more than 240 days of services subsequent to the memorandum of appointment issued by the appellant Corporation in the year 1988 in a period of twelve calendar months, therefore, they are entitled for regularisation of their services into permanent posts of the appellant Corporation as per the 1947 Act as well as the Certified Standing Orders of the appellant Corporation. Appellant is bound by law to take its decision to regularise the services of the concerned workmen as regular employees as provided u/cl. 2(ii) of the Certified Standing Orders after their completion of 240 days of service in a calendar year as they have acquired valid statutory right. Appeal dismissed.
