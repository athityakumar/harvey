Jitendra Khimshankar Trivedi and others v Kasam Daud Kumbhar and others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor accident claim - Reduction of compensation by HC - Justifiability - Deceased met with an accident and succumbed to injuries due to rash and negligent driving of respondent No. 1 - Appellant claimants filed petition before Tribunal seeking compensation - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.2,24,000/- On appeal, HC reduced compensation to Rs.2,09,400/- Hence, instant appeal by claimants.

Held, in order to award just and reasonable compensation, income of the deceased is taken as Rs.3000/- per month. Deducting 1/3rd for personal expenses contribution of the deceased and the family is calculated at Rs.2,000/- per month. At the time of her death deceased was aged about 22 years, proper multiplier to be adopted is 18. Adopting multiplier of 18, total loss of dependency is calculated at Rs.4,32,000/- (Rs.2000 x 12 x 18). With respect to the award of compensation under conventional heads, Tribunal has awarded Rs.5,000/- towards loss of estate and Rs.3,000/- towards funeral expenses totaling Rs.8,000/-. The HC has awarded conventional damages of Rs.15,000/- i.e. Rs.10,000/- towards loss of estate and Rs.5,000/- towards funeral expenses. The courts below have not awarded any compensation towards loss of consortium and towards love and affection. Further, Rs.1,00,000/- is awarded towards loss of consortium and Rs.1,00,000/- towards loss of love and affection to the minor children. Towards loss of estate and funeral expenses, award of compensation of Rs.15,000/- awarded by the HC is maintained. Thus, the claimants are entitled to a total compensation of Rs.6,47,000/-. As against the award passed by the Tribunal even though the claimants have not preferred any appeal and even though the claimants have then prayed for compensation of Rs.2,96,480/-, for doing complete justice to the parties, exercising jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution, it appropriate to award enhanced compensation of Rs. 6,47,000/ to the claimants. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is modified. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - As against the award passed by the tribunal even though the claimants have not filed any appeal, as it is obligatory on the part of courts/tribunals to award just and reasonable compensation, it is appropriate to increase the compensation.
