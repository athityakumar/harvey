TATA Steel Limited v State of Jharkhand and others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 840; 2015(10) SCALE 35

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Government Grants Act, 1895, Failure, Bihar Reorganisation Act, 2000, Bihar and Orissa Municipal Act, 1922, Bihar Restriction of Uses of Land Act, 1948, Bihar Industrial Areas Development Authority Act, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Industrial Areas Development Authority Act, 1974, ss. 2(b), 2(e)(7), 2(f)(8), 3, 3(1), 3(2)(13),4(1)(6), 6(2a), 6(2b)(17), 9 - Government Grants Act, 1895, s. 25 - Grant of Lease of Land - Termination of Grant - Challenged - Appellant/ company was granted lease of 350 acres of land for specific purpose by Governor of state of Bihar - Further, respondent no.1 was successor in interest of state of Bihar, respondent no.2/development authority was created by virtue of the Act - However, respondent no.3/Director of Authority passed order cancelling allotment of land of 100 acres of land out of 150 acres of land lying vacant in possession of appellant - HC dismissed writ petition filed by company - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was liable to be evicted from a portion of land on ground that portion of the land was not physically utilised for the specified purpose.

Held, the rights, privileges and obligations of any grantee of the Government would be completely regulated by the terms of the grant, even if such terms are inconsistent with the provisions of any other law. Cl. (xiv) of the lease agreement, stipulates that in the event of the appellant failing to "use the land for the specified purpose within a period of one year from the date of the lease" (GRANT-I), the same may be terminated and the appellant be evicted from the lands without notice. However, it only contemplates the termination of the GRANT-I in the event of the failure on the part of the appellant to use the land for the specified purpose and eviction of the appellant from the land. The language of the clause is significant. It can only be invoked in the case of total failure to utilise the land for the specified purpose. Cl. (xiv) read with the right of the appellant u/cl. (v) to sell a part of the land covered by the GRANT-I which is no longer required by him, can lead to only one conclusion that it was never intended by the GRANT-I that every inch of the land must be utilised for the purpose of the establishment of the industry. Any other construction of cl. (xiv) would simply render the cl. (v) meaningless and destructive of the right created there under in favour of the appellant. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and order of respondent no.3 is quashed. Appeal allowed.
