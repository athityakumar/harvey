Sonu Gupta v Deepak Gupta and others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Anil R. Dave, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 93; (2015) 3 SCC 424; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1192; 2015 (2) JCC 969; JT 2015 (2) SC 258; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1397; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 598; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 32; 2015 (2) RLW 1544; 2015(2) SCALE 295; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 1069

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Making false documents, Evaluation of merit of evidence for interfering with summoning order

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 464, 468, 471 - Making false documents - Evaluation of merit of evidence for interfering with summoning order - Legality - Appellant (complainant/wife) lodged compliant against respondent (A1 to 9) for alleged offence u/ss. 464, 468, 471 of IPC - Trial Court issued summons against accused persons after finding a prima facie case on the basis of complaint petition, statement of appellant as well as records of CID investigation - A1 to 8 preferred one set of criminal revision and A9 preferred another criminal revision before Revisional Court, which were dismissed upholding summoning order in respect of A1 to 5 but set it aside in respect of A6 to 8 and A9 - Aggrieved by two orders appellant preferred revision petitions whereas A1 to 5 also preferred a petition before HC - HC by common judgment dismissed both criminal revision petitions and allowed petition of A1 to 5 by setting aside the summoning order of Trial Court and directed the appellant to appear before Trial Court for adducing further evidence to support her allegation in complaint petition - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, specific case of the appellant that FIR was registered on an undated photocopy of a petition attributed to the appellant but not bearing her original signature could not have been rejected by Trial Court at the instant stage especially in view of the report of investigation by the CID which was also called for and there being no dispute that the FIR was registered only on the basis of a photocopy on which the signature is not in original and hence HC grossly erred in exercise of its jurisdiction by directing the appellant to lead further evidence and produce the original documents to show forgery. If the FIR is admittedly on the basis of only a photocopy of a document allegedly brought into existence by the accused persons, the HC erred in directing the appellant to produce the original and get the signatures compared. HC fell into error of evaluating the merits of the defence case and other submissions advanced on behalf of the accused which were not appropriate for consideration at the stage of taking cognizance and issuing summons. Order of summoning passed by Trial Court is restored. Impugned orders passed by Revisional Court and HC are set aside. Appeals allowed.
