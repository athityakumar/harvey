Md. Ali @ Guddu v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 161; (2015) 7 SCC 272; 2015 CRLJ 1967; 2015 (2) JCC 1327; JT 2015 (3) SC 35; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1927; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 707; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 206; 2015(3) SCALE 274; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 416

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Separation of Judicial and Executive Functions (Extension) and the Code of Criminal Procedure (Provision for Uniformity) Act, 1958

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 368, 376 - Alleged Kidnapping of Minor Child - Conviction - Justification - PW2 mother filed complainant alleging that her minor daughter PW1 was kidnapped by appellant accused - Further, after investigation victim was found and statement was recorded - Charge sheet was filed against eight accused against u/ss. 363, 366, 368 and 376 of IPC - Trial Court held that prosecution had been able to establish charges against four accused persons for offences u/ss. 366, 368 and 376 of I.P.C., but had failed to bring home charges against other accused persons and on that basis convicted and imposed sentence as has been stated hereinbefore - Order passed by Trial Court was affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, conclusions arrived at by HC are totally unsupportable on basis of evidence on record. SC held that victim was missing from house on 22-11-1996, however, mother of victim lodged FIR on 3-12-1996 almost after expiry of eleven days alleging factum of kidnapping by accused persons. Further, no explanation has been offered for such delay. SC held that Trial Judge has adverted to this facet on unacceptable backdrop by referring to principle that prosecutrix suffered from trauma and constraint of social stigma. SC held that prosecutrix at that time was nowhere on scene and it is mother who was required to inform police about missing of her grown up daughter. Further, persons who were accompanying brother of victim have also not been examined by prosecution, hence, manner of recovery of prosecutrix from house of remains mystery. Further, prosecutrix has deposed that she was taken from one place to other and remained at various houses for almost two months and only explanation given by her is that she was threatened by accused persons. It is not in her testimony that she was confined to one place. Further, medical evidence gains significance, for examining doctor has categorically deposed that there are no injuries on private parts. SC held that delay in FIR, non- examination of witnesses, testimony of prosecutrix, associated circumstances and medical evidence, leave mark of doubt to treat testimony of prosecutrix as so natural and truthful to inspire confidence. Hence, order of conviction passed by Trial Court and confirmed by HC is no justified and in accordance with law and are set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused is not maintainable and justified when same is not based on documentary or circumstantial evidence.
