Baleshwar Dayal Jaiswal v Bank of India and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 537; (2016) 1 SCC 444; AIR 2015 SC 2881; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 162; 2015 (112) ALR 645; 2015 (5) AWC 4361; 2015 (4) BC 182; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; [2015] 192 Comp Cas 74; 2015 (3) CompLJ 530; 2015 (4) CTC 809; 2015 (2) DRTC 310; 2015 (2) DRTC 457; JT 2015 (7) SC 318; 2015 (3) KLT 652; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2831; 2016 (4) MahLJ 733; 2015 (6) MLJ 95; 2016 (3) MPLJ 260; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 801; 2015 (129) RD 587; 2015(8) SCALE 509; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Condonation

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 29(2) - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, s. 20(3) - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 18(1), 18(2) - Condonation of Delay - Authority of Tribunal - Determination - Appellant contended that Appellant Tribunal under Act has power to condone delay in filing appeal u/s. 18(1) of Act - Conflict view was taken by HC's - Whether Appellate Tribunal under Act has power to condone delay in filing appeal u/s. 18(1) of 2002 Act.

Held, bare perusal of s. 18(2) of 2002 Act makes it clear that Appellate Tribunal under has to dispose of appeal in accordance with provisions of 1993 Act . Further, provisions of 1993 Act stand incorporated in 2002 for disposal of anappeal. Once it is so, SC is unable to discern any reason as to why Appellate Tribunal cannot entertain an appeal beyond prescribed period even on being satisfied that there is sufficient cause for not filing such appeal within that period. Even if power of condonation of delay by virtue of S. 29(2) of 1963 Act were held not to be applicable, proviso to s. 20(3) of 1993 act is applicable by virtue of s. 18(2) of 2002 Act. Further, unless scheme of statute expressly excludes power of condonation, there is no reason to deny such power to Appellate Tribunal when statutory scheme so warrants. Hence, SC held that delay in filing anappeal u/s. 18 (1) of 2002 Act can be condoned by Appellate Tribunal under proviso to s. 20(3) of 1993 Act read with s. 18 (2) of 2002 Act. Hence, appeal filed by Bank is dismissed and appeals filed by borrowers are allowed. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside and matter is remanded to Appellate Tribunal for being dealt with afresh in accordance with law. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Appellant Tribunal has authority to condone delay when same is provided by statue.
