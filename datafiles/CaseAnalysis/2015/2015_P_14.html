Padmashree Dr. D.Y. Patil Medical College v Medical Council of India and another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 606; (2015) 10 SCC 51; AIR 2015 SC 3320; JT 2015 (8) SC 37; 2015(9) SCALE 428; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 686; 2015 (6) SLR 268

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College, Opening of a New or Higher Course of Study or Training (including Post-graduate Course of Study or Training) and Increase of Admission Capacity in any Course of Study or Training (including a Postgraduate Course of Study or Training) Regulations, 2000

Summary: Education - Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 1999 - Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training (including Post-graduate Course of Study or Training) and Increase of Admission Capacity in any Course of Study or Training (including Postgraduate Course of Study or Training) Regulations, 2000 - Increase in admission capacity - Rejection of application - Justification - Petitioner claimed that it had filed application for increase in intake capacity on 30-8-2014 - Further, essentiality certificate could not be submitted by petitioner as it had not been issued to it by State Govt. and it was issued on 3-9-2014 and was submitted to Central Govt. on 5-9-2014 - Central Govt. rejected application of petitioner on ground that essentiality certificate was not filed with application form - Lower Court passed order in favour of petitioner condoning delay and allowed to increase seats for course from 150 to 250 from academic session 2015-2016 - However, order passed by Lower Court was reversed by HC and held that no direction can be issued due to time schedule.

Held, compliance with requirements under Act and Regulations being mandatory, in absence of their compliance, no permission can be granted by Central Government for increase in admission capacity in any course in any medical college. SC held that there being no compliance with requirements under Act, Central Govt. was justified in refusing permission for one-time increase in admission capacity in medical colleges. SC held that application was admittedly incomplete when it was filed as essentiality certificate issued by State Govt. was not enclosed along with application form due to which application came to be rejected and delay has taken place for which petitioner has to blame itself. SC held that time schedule is required to be strictly observed. Hence, it would not be appropriate to issue any direction for consideration of petitioner's case for ongoing academic session 2015-2016 in which inspection is yet to be made. It is too to direct inspection for session 2015-16 as all dates fixed in time schedule are over and fixation of time schedule has purpose behind it and from particular date session has to commence and part of seats to be filled by competitive examination held on all-India basis. Any relaxation in time schedule would make holding of examinations on all India basis farce and several complications would arise. Hence, SC directed hat application which has been submitted by college for academic session 2015-2016 be considered for next academic session, subject to fulfillment of other requisite formalities, and thereafter authority shall conduct inspection well-in-time as per time schedule fixed under Regulations of 1999. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Permission to increase admission capacity cannot be granted unless institutions comply with provisions of Act and Regulations.
