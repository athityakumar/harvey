State of Orissa v Samantary Construction Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 676; 2015 (6) AWC 5441; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 714; 2015(9) SCALE 685; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 840

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitrator & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.34 - Value of machinery - Hire charges - Respondent-company was awarded contract by appellant-State but the same was terminated as work executed even after extension of time was only 47.67% - Respondent-Company raised claim of Rs.6.99 Crore towards dues and as dispute arose, matter was referred to arbitration wherein arbitrator upheld claim of respondent-company to extent of Rs.4.45 Crore and also allowed counter claim of appellant-State to extent of Rs.1.37 Crore - Appellant-State filed objections u/s. 34 of the Act to award including award of Rs.3 Crore towards hire charges of machinery and other equipments - District Judge held that arbitrator committed gross illegality in awarding amount of Rs.3 Crore and accepted objection and held that claimed amount was Rs.68,44,332 - However, HC reversed said view of District Judge and observed that claim was not limited to Rs. 68,44,332 - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-State - Whether award passed by Arbitrator is valid.

Held, arbitrator ought to have ascertained the total value of the machinery as any person in dealing with claim on account of loss caused by wrongful seizure of machinery or equipment, would enquire the total value thereof. Further, the hire charges could, at times furnish the basis for determining the compensation but such determination could not normally exceed the price of the equipment as the loss caused could not normally be more than the price of the equipment itself. Hence, there is non application of mind in awarding the amount of Rs.3 Crore towards hire charges and as the claim could not be upheld beyond the price even if depreciation was not considered, claim of the respondent-company is assessed at Rs.1.25 Crore and award is modified accordingly and award is upheld in all other respects. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - In considering an objection to the award, the Court does not sit in appeal over the decision on merits. However, patent error or perversity could certainly provide basis for interference.
