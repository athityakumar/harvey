Ramanlal and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 370; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2463; 2015 (3) JCC 1905; JT 2015 (5) SC 77; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 111; 2015(6) SCALE 560; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Defence Version

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 323, 207 - Acquittal of accused - Unlawful assembly - Dispute arose due to trespassing of fields of victim - Abusive responses were given by accused and victims were attacked by group of people - Victims were assaulted and serious injuries were inflicted and one of victim scrambled to death - FIR was filed u/ss. 148,149, 323 and 207 of IPC against appellant accused - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 of IPC - However, HC vide impugned order acquitted few accused, while convicted other accused - Appellant contended that acquittal of 6 out of 10 accused persons, charge that appellants constituted unlawful assembly cannot be sustained.

Held, s. 149 of IPC are no longer available to prosecution for convicting appellants whose number is reduced to 4 consequent upon acquittal of remaining accused persons. In present case, prosecution had named all those constituting unlawful assembly, but, only four of those named were eventually convicted, thereby reducing number to less than five. There is no evidence to suggest that any one, apart from persons named in charge-sheet were members of unlawful assembly, but, were either not available or remained unidentified. Such being position, conviction of appellants with help of s. 149 of IPC does not appear to be legally sustainable. Appeals partly allowed.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 304 - Death caused by injury - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Determination - Whether injury accused by accused to deceased amounted to murder.

Held, keeping in view nature of injury, vital part of body on which same was inflicted and weapon used by accused appellant and medical evidence, that said injury was sufficient in ordinary course to cause death, culpable homicide would, in case at hand, tantamount to murder, however, incident was without any pre-meditation and sudden fight upon sudden quarrel. Injuries upon deceased were inflicted in heat of passion and without appellant taking any undue advantage or acting in cruel or unusual manner. Hence, offence committed by author of injury is not murder but culpable homicide not amounting to murder punishable u/s. 304 of IPC. Further, even when act may not have been committed with intention of causing death, same was intended to cause such bodily injury as was likely to cause death, within meaning of s. 304 Part I of IPC. Hence, conviction of appellants u/s. 302 read with s. 149 of IPC and sentence of imprisonment for life awarded to them is set aside. Appeals partly allowed.

Ratio - Conviction of offender for murder is not sustainable when injury cased to by accused is in wake of passion and is not pre-planned.
