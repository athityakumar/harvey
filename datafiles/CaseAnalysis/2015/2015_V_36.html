Zonal General Manager, Ircon International Limited v Vinay Heavy Equipments

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 336; 2015 (111) ALR 248; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 223; 2015 (4) MLJ 110; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 467; 2015(6) SCALE 103; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 938

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Sub contractor - Cancellation of Contract - Failure to reimburse unpaid amount - Appellant availed contract from original party and further entered in contract with respondent for construction of road - Respondent completed approximately more than half of work under 2 subcontracts but thereafter ceased work on both - Appellant cancelled subcontracts, and managed completion of work by engaging other agencies and paid amount to respondent for work done, however, claimed unpaid balance as arrears due to it by appellant, and resorted to arbitration - Appellant also claimed amount from original contractor which was allowed by arbitrator - Arbitrator passed award in favor of respondent - However, appellant having availed amount from original contractor failed to reimburse respondent for unpaid amount - Appeal filed by appellant was dismissed by lower Courts and unpaid amount was granted to respondent - Whether appellant was liable to pay unpaid amount to respondent due to cancellation of contract.

Held, lower Court rightly upheld arbitrator's repudiation of applicability of 'back-to-back' principle to issue of liability of payment affirming that appellant as Employer was primarily liable to respondent. Further, SC held that appellant had obtained favourable award in Arbitration with original contractor by substantially relying on adverse award passed in its arbitration with respondent; on other hand, it appealed before Single Judge against adverse award which had substantially been premise and reason for appellant's success against original contractor. SC held that earlier award must be presupposed to be valid, when validity of later award has not been disproved or rebutted. Hence, award in favour of appellant is positively valid, and its unsettlement uninviting prospect. Further, there was nothing which suggests that original contract with appellant provided for 'back to back' subcontracts whereby original contractor would be directly answerable for payment claims raised by contractors. SC held that appellant, stands bound and bonded by legal consequences of this initial admission made by it before arbitrator. Hence, order passed by arbitrator and lower Court is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Sub-contractors are bound to reimbursed amount which are due to party after they have received amount from original contractor.
