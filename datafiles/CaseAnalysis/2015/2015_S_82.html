Umrala Gram Panchayat v Secretary, Municipal Employees Union and others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 236; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 211; 2015 (145) FLR 688; 2015 (3) G.L.R. 2197; JT 2015 (3) SC 366; 2015 (2) LLJ 403; 2015(4) SCALE 334; 2015 (3) SLR 705

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Gujarat Panchayat Act, 1993

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Permanent Employees - Workmen of the Panchayat were appointed to the post of safai kamdars of the appellant-Panchayat and have served for many years - Workmen, however, were considered as daily wage workers and were therefore, not being paid benefits such as pay and allowances etc. as are being paid to the permanent safai kamdars of the appellant- Panchayat - Labour Court by its Award held that the workmen are to be made permanent employees as safai kamdars in the appellant - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, in the light of the facts and circumstances of the present case, it is held that the services of the concerned workmen are permanent in nature, since they have worked for more than 240 days in a calendar year from the date of their initial appointment, which is clear from the evidence on record. Therefore, not making their services permanent by the appellant is erroneous and also amounts to error in law. Hence, the same cannot be allowed to sustain in law. Appellant was directed to treat the services of the concerned workmen as permanent employees, after five years of their initial appointment as daily wage workmen till they attain the age of superannuation for the purpose of granting terminal benefits to them. Appellant is further directed to pay the regular pay-scale as per the revised pay scale fixed to the post of permanent safai kamdars for a total period of 15 years to the concerned workmen and the legal representatives of the deceased workmen. Appeals dismissed.
