Balu S/o Onkar Pund and others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 70; (2015) 3 SCC 409; AIR 2015 SC 949; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1209; JT 2015 (2) SC 36; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 143; 2015 (3) RLW 1962; 2015(2) SCALE 147; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part-I, 302, 147, 148, 323, 324, 436, 440, 448, 506 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were charged u/ss. 302, 147, 148, 323, 324, 436, 440, 448, 506 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court found appellants guilty of offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo life imprisonment - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment confirmed the conviction awarded by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Trial Court and HC were justified in convicting the appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC considering the genesis of occurrence and the facts in entirety or they should convicted u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC.

Held, from the evidence of the prosecution, Count find that instant case is a case where the appellants should have been convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC instead of s. 302 of IPC. Neither there was any motive and nor any intention on the part of any of the appellants to eliminate deceased. There was no enmity of any kind with deceased in person with any of the appellants. The appellants had gone there to take possession of the cattle shed and not with an intention to kill any member of the family of PW3. If at all, if there was some kind of animosity or jealousy then it was towards A1 whose panel had won the election. Deceased had nothing to do with election because she never contested the election. Despite the appellants armed with weapons, none of them inflicted any injury or gave blow to deceased but single blow was inflicted only on PW3, who fortunately survived. Deceased died due to sustaining of burn injuries, which she suffered because the appellants ablazed the cattle shed by pouring kerosene on it. If the appellants had not ablazed the cattle shed then the incident of death of deceased would not have occurred. It was a fight on a spur of moment between the two male groups on the issue of taking possession of cattle shed with no intention to kill any one and lastly, in the absence of any overt act attributed to any of the appellants towards deceased for inflicting any injury to her, the appellants could not have been convicted for an offence of committing murder of deceased so as to attract the rigour of s. 302 of IPC and instead they should have been convicted for an offence of culpable homicide not amounting to murder u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC. Appellants are convicted u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC instead of s. 302 of IPC and are sentenced to undergo RI for 7 years. Appeals partly allowed.
