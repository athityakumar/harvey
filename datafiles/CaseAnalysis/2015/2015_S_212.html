Jogendrasinhji Vijaysinghji v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 472; (2015) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 3623; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 370; 2015 (3) KLT 240; 2015 (5) MLJ 592; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 801; 2015(7) SCALE 495; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Writ Of Certiorari, Pronounced

Summary: (A) Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, art. 227 - Proceeding from Civil Court before DB- Maintainability of LPA - Determination - Appellants challenged legal substantiality of judgment and order passed by HC in bunch of Letters Patent Appeals (LPA) preferred u/cl. 15 of Letters Patent wherein it is held that once petition is u/art. 227 of the Constitution and while entertaining such petition u/art. 227 of Constitution if court allows petition by setting aside order impugned, then against such order no LPA would lie - DB referred to larger Bench in view of conflict in two decisions - Whether order passed by civil court is only amenable to be scrutinized by HC in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 227 of Constitution.

Held, order passed by civil court is only amenable to be scrutinized by HC in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 227 of Constitution which is different from art. 226 of Constitution and Radhey Shyam v Chhabi Nath & Ors., 2015 Indlaw SC 157, relied on no writ can be issued against order passed by civil court, hence, no letters patent appeal would be maintainable in respect of order passed by lower court dealing with order arising out of proceeding from Civil Court would not lie before DB. Further, maintainability of letters patent appeal before DB would depend upon pleadings in writ petition, nature and character of order passed by lower court, type of directions issued regard being had to jurisdictional perspectives in Constitutional context. Further, DB would be required to scrutinize whether facts of case justify assertions made in petition to invoke jurisdiction under both articles and relief prayed on that foundation. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Impleadment Tribunal as party - Justification - Lower Court held that even if application is described as one not only u/art. 226 of Constitution, but also u/art. 227 of Constitution, Court or tribunal whose order is sought to be quashed, if not arrayed as party, application would not be maintainable as one of relief of certiorari, in absence of concerned tribunal or Court as party, cannot be granted - Whether it is necessary to implead Tribunal as party .

Held, authorities or Tribunals, who in law are entitled to defend orders passed by them, are necessary parties and if they are not arrayed as parties, petition can be treated to be not maintainable or court may grant liberty to implead them as parties in exercise of its discretion. There are tribunals which are not at all required to defend their own order, and in that case such tribunals need not be arrayed as parties. Hence, in such situation, superior court, that is HC, even if required to call for records, District Judge need not be party. Hence, when Tribunal or authority is required to defend its own order, it is to be made party failing which proceeding before HC would be regarded as not maintainable. Matter is remanded to HC to be heard by DB in accordance with principles laid down in present judgment. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - No writ can be issued against order passed by civil court, hence, no letters patent appeal would be maintainable. Further, Tribunal being or not being party in writ petition is not determinative of maintainability of letters patent appeal.
