Surinder Pal Kaur and another v Satpal and another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 414; AIR 2015 SC 2739; 2015 (109) ALR 717; 2015 CRLJ 3821; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1163; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 915; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 920; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 136; 2015(1) SCALE 376; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1963

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 145, 145(1) and 146(1) - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 13 - Eviction - Default in payment of rent - Appellants (landlords) filed application for ejectment against respondents alleging that respondents failed to make payment of rent w.e.f. 1-6-1987, and committed default for 25 months - Respondents filed written statement disputing the relationship of landlord and tenant between the parties and, as such, the appellants have no right, title and interest in the property - Rent Controller allowed said application for ejectment of the appellants - Appellate Court allowed the appeal filed against said order and set aside the order of the Rent Controller - Appellate Court held that appellant no. 1 was an employee of management committee of a temple and after his removal from the post of Manager, he had no right and authority to collect the rent from the respondents - HC dismissed the appeal filed against said order and held that the property was under attachment on the date of filing of petition u/s. 13 of Act - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC has erred in law in dismissing the revision merely for the reason that the property was under attachment on the date of filing of petition u/s. 13 of Act, and the Receiver was appointed in respect of the said property. It is nobody's case that the respondents were not in possession of the property in question as tenants on said date. Impugned order is set aside. The matter is remitted to the HC for fresh adjudication under revisional jurisdiction. Appeal allowed.
