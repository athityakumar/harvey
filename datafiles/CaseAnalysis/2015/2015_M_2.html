M. D., Frozen Foods Exports Private Limited and others v Hero Fincorp Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sanjay Kishan Kaul, Rohinton Fali Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 1004; 2017 (5) AWC 4968

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, In Addition To, Reserve Bank Of India Act, 1934, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Arbitration, Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Derogation, Bankruptcy Act, 1890, Conveyancing of Law and Property Act, 1892

Summary: Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002-Sections 13 (2) and 2 (1)-Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Section 37-Recovery of debts-Arbitration proceedings-Interim order restraining appellants from creating any third party interest over properties confirmed by Arbitrator allowing application to substitute order of status quo parties with name of appellants-Act 2002 applied to all claims alive to time when it was brought into force-Thus, qua the respondent or other N.B.F.Cs. it was to be applicable similarly from date when it was so made applicable to them-Provisions of Act 2002 as opined became applicable qua all debts owing and live when Act became applicable to respondent in terms of parameters contended by counsel for respondent-Appeal being completely devoid of merit and being only an endeavour to prolong ultimate "date of judgment" for appellants to meet their obligation dismissed with costs.
