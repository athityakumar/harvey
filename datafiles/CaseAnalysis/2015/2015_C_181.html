Chief Controlling Revenue Authority v Costal Gujarat Power Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 549; (2015) 10 SCC 700; 2015(5) ALL MR 952; 2015 (112) ALR 467; 2015 (3) G.L.R. 2306; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 794; 2015 (129) RD 371; 2015(8) SCALE 656B; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Stamp (Gujarat Second Amendment) Rules, 2007

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Gujarat Stamp Act, 1958, ss. 6, 33, 39 - Bombay Stamp (Gujarat Second Amendment) Rules, 2007 - Execution of Mortgage Agreement - Alleged Evasion of stamp duty - Determination - Respondent Company in need to financial assistance for starting project secured assistance from few lenders - Lenders i.e. financial institutions, which were thirteen in number, formed consortium as trust and executed security trustee agreement (STA) inter se appointing one banker as lead trustee - Respondent executed deed with lead trustee mortgaging its assets and said deed was presented for registration which was registered - However, Collector held that respondent was liable to pay deficit stamp duty on ground that respondent cannot evade payment of stamp duty by forming consortium - Petition filed by respondent was dismissed, however, HC held that State is not entitled to recover any additional stamp duty and stamp duty is payable on instruments and not on transactions - Further, merely because intended effect was achieved by executing one single document as against different sets of documents, such fact would not enable State authorities to claim stamp duty - Whether impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, instrument of mortgage came into existence only after separate loan agreements were executed by borrower with lenders with regard to separate loan advanced by those lenders to respondent borrower. Further, from trustee document it appears that altogether 13 banks lent money to mortgagor, details of which have been described in schedule and for repayment of money, borrower entered into separate loan agreements with 13 financial institutions. Had this borrower entered into separate mortgage deed with these financial institutions in order to secure loan there would have been separate document for distinct transactions. SC held that on proper construction of indenture of mortgage it can safely be regarded as 13 distinct transactions which falls u/s. 5 of Act. Member, Board of Revenue v Arthur Paul Benthall, 1955 Indlaw SC 82, relied on and on perusal ss. 33, 39, art. 6 and 6(b) of Act and Rules and circular dt. 2-4-2007, SC held that HC has committed serious error of law in interpreting provisions of ss. 5 and 6 of Act. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside and respondent is liable to pay deficit stamp duty together with interest as directed by revenue authorities. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Clubbing of transaction in order to evade stamp duty is not justified and assessee is liable to pay deficit stamp duty to authority
