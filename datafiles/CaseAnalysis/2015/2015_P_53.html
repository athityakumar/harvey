Bengal Ambuja Housing Development Limited and another v Pramila Sanfui and others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 650; (2016) 1 SCC 743; AIR 2015 SC 3729; 2015 (6) AWC 6440; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 57; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 625; 2016 (4) MahLJ 54; 2015 (7) MLJ 93; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 601; 2016 (1) RLW 461; 2015(10) SCALE 69; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 65

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Lis Pendens, Particulars, West Bengal Housing Board Act, 1972, West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953, West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, West Bengal Acquisition of Estates Act, 1953, West Bengal Estates Acquisition, Act 1953

Summary: Land & Property - Temporary injunction - Appellant-Housing Board acquired ownership of property by way of five registered conveyance deeds from successors-in-interest of late Gangadas Pal - Hence, plaintiffs-respondents filed petition praying compliance with order of temporary injunction passed by Trial Court in respect of property in dispute - Subordinate Judge passed orders directing SP to see that consent order of temporary injunction granted was maintained by parties - Aggrieved, appellant-Bengal Ambuja filed application before HC but the same was dismissed holding that appellant-Housing Board purchased suit property lis pendens, and that no permission was taken from Court - Aggrieved, appellant-Bengal Ambuja filed SLP before SC but the same was dismissed as withdrawn by granting liberty to file application before HC - Appellant-Bengal Ambuja filed review application before HC but the same was dismissed and hence, instant appeals - Whether the appeals filed by appellant-Housing Board are maintainable as SLP filed by appellant-Bengal Ambuja was dismissed.

Held, order of temporary injunction passed in the original suit proceedings in respect of property in dispute without impleading either vendors of appellant-Housing Board or the heirs of late Gangadas Pal to the original suit proceedings could not be said to have a binding effect on appellant-Housing Board. Hence, appeals are maintainable. Further, amendment of plaint to include suit property of heirs of Late Gangadas Pal was done in pursuance of orders of Subordinate judge, wherein land in question that was sold to appellant-Housing Board was added to schedule of suit lands but the same is erroneous in law and hence, liable to be set aside as said order is not binding on the appellant. Hence, order of temporary injunction passed in favour of plaintiffs-respondents is set aside in so far as it relates to the property of the appellant-Housing Board is concerned which property was included by way of an amendment to the plaint and appeals are allowed. Appeals disposed of.
