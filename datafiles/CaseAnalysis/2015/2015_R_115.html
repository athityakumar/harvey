Zuari Cement Limited v Regional Director E.S.I.C. Hyderabad and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 449; (2015) 7 SCC 690; AIR 2015 SC 2764; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 25; 2015 (4) AWC 3881; 2015 (146) FLR 897; JT 2015 (6) SC 1; 2015 (3) KLT 700; 2015 (3) LLJ 257; 2016 (1) MahLJ 617; 2016 (1) MPLJ 313; 2015(7) SCALE 397; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 474; 2015 (5) SLR 3

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: ESI, Principal Employer, Esi Corporation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 74, 75, 87 - Exemption from applicability of the Act - Appellant factory was granted exemption from operation of the Act by various orders by State Govt. for period from 1-3-1986 to 31-3-1993 but later, rejected claim for exemption for period from 1-4-1993 to 31-3-2001 and issued notices demanding amount towards contributions for said period - Aggrieved, appellant approached HC by filing various writ petitions and HC disposed of writ petitions by holding that ESI court has jurisdiction to decide all issues and questions including applicability of the Act and so, appellant approached ESI Court wherein ESI Court granted future exemption to appellant from coverage of ESI Scheme and set aside impugned demand notices for period between 1993 to 2001 - Aggrieved, ESI Corporation filed appeal before HC and vide impugned judgment, HC held that ESI Court does not have power to grant exemption u/s. 75(1)(g) of the Act and allowed appeals - Hence, instant appeals assailing said orders of HC - Whether ESI court has jurisdiction to grant exemption to appellant from applicability of the Act.

Held, as per scheme of the Act, the power to grant exemption is a plenary power given to an appropriate govt. It follows that the ESI Court constituted u/s. 74 of the Act has no jurisdiction to take up the question of grant of exemption. The Court constituted u/s. 74 pf the Act could not decide such matters including the validity of an exemption notification. Hence, as the ESI Court had no jurisdiction to consider the question of grant of exemption, order passed by the ESI Court granting exemption and consequently setting aside the demand notices is non-est. Hence, HC had rightly set aside the order of ESI Court and impugned judgement of HC is sustainable and so, all appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - When the statute prescribed the procedure for grant or refusal of exemption from the operation of the Act, it is to be done in that manner and not in any other manner. Further, as per s. 87 of the Act, only the appropriate govt. has the power to grant exemption to a factory or establishment or class of factories or establishments from the operation of the Act.
