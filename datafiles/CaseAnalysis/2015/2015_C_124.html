M. P. Steel Corporation v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 288; (2015) 7 SCC 58; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 373; JT 2015 (8) SC 413; 2015 (2) KLT 996; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3266; 2016 (1) MahLJ 505; 2015 (4) MLJ 225; 2016 (1) MPLJ 279; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 965; 2015(5) SCALE 505; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 291; [2015] 80 VST 402

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Good Faith, Civil Proceedings, Vessel, U.P. Sales Tax Act, Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishment Act

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Alleged filing of appeal after limitation period - Condonation of delay - Justification - Dispute arose between parties with respect to encashment of bank guarantee vide order of Collector - Appellant authority set aside order of Collector, however, HC passed order in favor of department and dismissed order of lower Court holding that Tribunal had no jurisdiction to pass order - Appeal filed by assessee was dismissed contending that it was passed beyond limitation period - Appellant filed application for condonation of delay for filing appeal against lower Court which was dismissed - Appellant contended that entire period starting from 25-3-1992 up till 12-3-2003 ought to be excluded by applying s. 14 of Act Limitation Act - Whether delay in filing appeal by assessee can be condoned.

Held, abortive appeal had been filed against orders passed in March- April, 1992. Further, present appeal was filed u/s. 128 of Act, and before its amendment in 2001, it provided maximum period of 180 days within which appeal could be filed. Time began to run on 3-4-1992 u/s. 128 pre amendment when appellant received order of Superintendent of Customs intimating it about order passed by Collector of Customs on 25-3-1992. SC held that on principles contained in s. 14 of Act, time taken in prosecuting abortive proceeding would have to be excluded as appellant was prosecuting bona fide with due diligence appeal before appellant authority which was allowed in its favour by appellant authority on 23-6-1998. Department preferred appeal against said order sometime in year 2000 which appeal was decided in their favour by HC only on 12-3-2003 by which appellant authority order was set aside on ground that Tribunal had no jurisdiction to entertain such appeal. SC held that time taken from 12-3-2003 to 23-5-2003, on which date present appeal was filed before appellant authority would be within period of 180 days provided by pre amended s. 128 of Act, when added to time taken between 3-4-1992 and 22-6-1992. Further, order that is challenged in present case was passed before 2001 and right of appeal within period of 180 days (which includes discretionary period of 90 days) from date of said order was right which vested in appellant. SC held that shadow was cast by abortive appeal from 1992 right up to 2003 and this shadow was lifted when it became clear that proceeding filed in1992 was proceeding before wrong forum. Vested right of appeal within period of 180 days had not yet got over. Upon lifting of shadow, certain residuary period within which proper appeal could be filed still remained and that period would continue to be within period of 180 days notwithstanding amendment made in 2001 as otherwise right to appeal itself would vanish given shorter period of limitation provided by s. 128 after 2001. Hence, order passed by lower authority is set aside and matter is remanded to appellant authority. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is justified to condone delay in filing appeal when appellant was prosecuting bona fide with due diligence appeal before appellant authority within limitation period, hence, applicant should be put in same position as he was when he started abortive proceeding.
