Ravinder Kaur v Anil Kumar

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 277; (2015) 8 SCC 286; AIR 2015 SC 2447; 2015 CRLJ 2839; 2015 (3) JCC 1569; JT 2015 (5) SC 189; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2148; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2015 (4) RLW 3155; 2015(5) SCALE 417; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 551

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 s. 13 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 s. 320 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 493, 494, 495, 496, 420, 506, 120B - Ex parte Decree of Divorce - Compounding of Offences - Respondent husband filed petition seeking divorce from the appellant wife and in the absence of appellant, an ex-parte decree of divorce was granted to the respondent - Appellant preferred an application, for setting aside the said ex- parte decree and the same was allowed - Matrimonial ties between the appellant and the respondent came to be restored, as if the marital relationship had never ceased - Respondent had continued the sexual relationship with the appellant for the period when the ex-parte decree of divorce was passed till he married another woman - Appellant filed complaint accusing the respondent of offences u/ss. 493, 494, 495, 496, 420, 506 read with s. 120-B of the IPC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, charge against the respondent is not made out u/s. 493 of the IPC, because the respondent could not have deceived the appellant of the existence of a 'lawful marriage', when a lawful marriage indeed existed between the parties, during the period under reference. In regard to s.494 of the IPC is concerned, same is compoundable and appellant had no objection to the compounding of the offence. After consideration of facts and circumstances of case, best course for the parties is to settle their dispute amicably and s. 320 of the CrPC is an avenue available to the parties, for such resolution. Appellant was directed to compounding of complaint made by the appellant with reference to s.494 of the IPC. Respondent was directed to pay sum of Rs.5 lakhs, as compensation to the appellant. Appeal disposed of.
