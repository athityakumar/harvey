Standard Chartered Bank v Andhra Bank Financial Services Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 652; (2016) 1 SCC 207; AIR 2015 SC 3530; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 97; 2015 (4) BC 251; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 563; [2015] 193 Comp Cas 163; 2015 (4) CompLJ 1; JT 2015 (8) SC 60; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 542; 2015(9) SCALE 370

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 91(a) - Suit for compensation - Limitation - Petitioner filed suit against respondent for the recovery of the principal amount of Rs.48,02,50,000/- representing the consideration paid by petitioner to respondent against the transaction of purchase of 17% NPCL bonds of the face value of Rs. 50 crores - On 20-10-1995, Petitioner filed an application for amendment of the suit and to include X and Y as party respondents and to file claim against the said respondents in the alternative to the claim preferred by petitioner against respondent - Special Court, declined to grant any relief to the petitioner on the ground that the suit was barred by limitation - Special Court came to the conclusion that the period of limitation for institution of the suit started running on 18-3-1992 as the petitioner could have possibly found out about the conversion of the bonds was 23-5-1992 as that was the date on which X had himself informed the petitioner of the conversion of the suit bonds during a meeting - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the provision of art. 91(a) of Act could apply to the instant case.

Held, the revelation made by X during the meeting held on 23-5-1992, did not give the petitioner the knowledge requisite for the purpose of art. 91(a) of the Act. The revelation in contention made by X was vague and he gave evasive responses after the same and thus, it is not reasonable to expect the petitioner to believe the same and initiate legal proceedings on the basis of the said statement. On examination of all the dates alternative dates prior to 7-11-1992 proposed by the respondents, where knowledge could be said to be imputed to the petitioner for institution of suit against the respondents. The period of limitation would start running only on 7-11-1992. The finding of the Special Court that the institution of the suit of the petitioner against respondents is barred by limitation is set aside. Suit claim with interest and costs is allowed by allowing these appeals. Respondents are directed to pay the adjudged principal sum of Rs.50,18,61,250/- along with interest at the rate of 6% per annum from 10-1-1996 till the date of realisation with suit costs throughout for having converted the suit bonds. Order accordingly
