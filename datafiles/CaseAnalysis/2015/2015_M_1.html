HMT Watches Limited v M. A. Abida and another

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 209; 2015 (2) BC 267; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 246; 2015 (3) CGLJ 596; 2015 (2) CompLJ 449; 2015 CRLJ 2408; 2015 (2) CTC 446; JT 2015 (3) SC 216; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1594; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 205; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 497; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 366; 2015 (3) RLW 1983; 2015(3) SCALE 832; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 719; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 8

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal complaints - Jurisdiction of HC - Appellant filed twenty criminal complaints against respondent-accused stating that around 57 cheques issued by respondent were dishonoured by banks as 'payment stopped by drawer' and even after issuance of notice to make payment of amount mentioned in said cheques, no payment was made - While so, respondent-accused filed criminal complaints challenging proceedings initiated against him and HC accepting plea of respondent-accused, quashed said criminal complaint cases - Hence, instant appeals by appellant - Whether HC was justified in quashing criminal complaint cases filed against respondent-accused.

Held, respondent-accused challenged proceedings of criminal complaint cases before HC, taking factual defenses. Whether cheques were given as security or not, or whether there was outstanding liability or not, were questions of fact which could be determined only by the Trial Court after recording evidence of the parties. Hence, HC should not have expressed its view on disputed questions of fact in petition filed u/s. 482 of CrPC, to come to conclusion that offence is not made out and HC erred in law in going into factual aspects of matter which were not admitted between parties. Further, HC also erred in its observation that s. 138(b) of the Act was not complied even though respondent-accused admitted that he replied the notice issued by appellant. Further, HC could not have examined in its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC, fact as to whether signatory of demand notice was authorized by appellant or not, when such plea was refuted by appellant. Hence, HC committed grave error of law in quashing criminal complaints filed by appellant in respect of offence punishable u/s. 138 of the Act, in exercise of powers u/s. 482 of CrPC by accepting factual defenses of respondent-accused which were disputed ones. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned orders of HC were quashed and Trial Court is directed to proceed with trial in criminal complaint cases. Appeals disposed of.
