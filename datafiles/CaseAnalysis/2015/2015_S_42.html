Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi-IV v Sandan Vikas (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.K. Agrawal, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 462; (2015) 9 SCC 701; 2015 (321) E.L.T. 353; JT 2015 (6) SC 439; 2015(7) SCALE 206; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exemption Notification, Finance Bill, 1990

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Payment of duty in accordance with Notification - Reconsideration of decision - Determination - Appellant paid ad valorem excise duty at 65% on car air-conditioning unit without gas compressors and 40% ad valorem duty on gas compressors - However, revenue is that in terms of Explanation 2 read with r. 2(a) and Section Note 4 to Section XVI, held that goods manufactured would be covered by serial no.3 of aforesaid Notification and they were chargeable under same - Sanden Vikas (India) Ltd. v. C.C.E., New Delhi, 2003 Indlaw SC 220, relied on Tribunal opined that issue raised by revenue is covered by said decision wherein it is held that Explanation to mean that it has effect of taking away automotive gas compressor (with or without magnetic clutch) from out of car air-conditioning kit, hence, appeal was sans merit and did not warrant any interference - Appellant contended that view expressed by DB that car air-conditioning kit minus automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch will remain in description of goods against Item No. 8 and that excluded part of kit, namely, 'automotive gas compressor' will cease to be part of Item No.8 and would be liable to duty separately is not correct conclusion and it requires to be reconsidered - Further, it is contended that in view of express language of Explanation (2) which excluded car air-conditioning kits which contained automotive gas compressor, with or without magnetic clutch, from purview of Item No.8 of Notification, entire kit would stand excluded from scope of Item No.8 and thereby said air-conditioning kit which continues to be meant for providing air conditioning in cars would then revert to Item No. 3 which has been expressly held to cover car air-conditioning kits - Hence, present appeal.

Held, if manufacturer sells kit and automotive gas compressor as one unit of transaction, it will get out of serial no.8. If manufacturer sells kit and automotive gas compressor separately by different invoice or by separate pricing, there is no reason for exclusion of air-conditioning kit from serial no.8 because there are two transactions and kit is charged as per serial no.8 and compressor is charged as per serial no.1. Further, one can buy automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch with kit, and both can also be purchased separately from different manufacturers. Lower Court held that air-conditioning kit minus automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch will remain in description of goods against item no.8 of Notification and that excluded part of kit, namely, automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch will cease to be part of item no.8 and will be liable to duty separately. Hence, DB has quite categorically stated that if air-conditioning kit does not contain automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch, duty is paid as per item no.8 and if it contains automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch, it will not come under item no.8. Hence, ratio laid down in said decision cannot be found to be erroneous but as matter of clarification, SC held that if kit and compressor are sold in singular invoice or in one pricing, it will go out of item no.8 and duty will be paid separately, but if there are two invoices for separate pricing, air-conditioning kit would come under serial no.8 and automotive gas compressor with or without magnetic clutch will be liable to duty separately. Further, with respect to present case, SC held that respondent-assessee has sold kit and compressor separately and that position having been accepted by Tribunal, hence, there is no error in order passed by authorities and Tribunal. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Duty on manufactured goods will be levied as per notification issued and explanation issued by legislature in that behalf.
