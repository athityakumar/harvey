Chanderi Devi and another v Jaspal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 378; 2015 (2) ACC 705; 2015 ACJ 1612; 2015 (3) AWC 3065; JT 2015 (4) SC 74; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2156; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 699; 2015(4) SCALE 390; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 234

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Enhancement of compensation-Death case-Deceased aged 32 years at time of death was employed as Indian Cook in restaurant of Germany-High Court enhanced amount of compensation to Rs. 17,10,000 from Rs. 2,00,000 as awarded by Tribunal-On considering facts and circumstances of case and plausibly estimating as to how much cook of similar nature as deceased would have earned in India in year 2006, it would be just and reasonable to ascertain income of deceased at time of his death at Rs. 15,000 p.m. instead of Rs. 8,333 p.m.-Loss of dependency computed at Rs. 25,92,000-Rs. 1,00,000 awarded towards loss of estate to appellant-wife-Rs. 25,000 towards funeral expenses and Rs. 1,00,000 towards loss of consortium awarded to appellant-wife-Rs. 1,00,000 awarded to appellant-minor towards loss of love and affection of her father (deceased)-Appellants entitled to compensation of Rs. 29,17,000 alongwith interest @ 9% p.a.-Compensation amount enhanced by sum of Rs. 12,67,000-Appeal allowed.
