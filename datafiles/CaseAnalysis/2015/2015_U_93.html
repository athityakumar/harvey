Union of India and others v Uttam Steel Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 439; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 598; JT 2015 (7) SC 484; 2015(6) SCALE 39; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Excisable Goods, Central Excise Rules, 1944

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11B - Central Excise Rules, 1944 , r. 12 - Claim of rebate - Alleged Time Barred - Rejection - Respondent was engaged in manufacture and export of steel products and goods were shipped on board on 25-5-1999 and 10-6-1999 respectively in two lots - Further, as per law prevailing at relevant time, respondent had to file claims for rebate within six months from date of shipment i.e. on or before 20-11-1999 and 10-12-1999 respectively, however, claims for rebate on both counts were filed only on 28-12-1999 beyond period of six months u/s. 11B of Act as it stood at relevant time -Lower authority rejected claim for rebate on ground that they were time barred - Further, s. 11B of Act was amended on 12-5-2000 where period of six months was substituted by period of one year - Appellant authority allowed respondent's appeal holding that extended period of one year was available to respondent, period prescribed for limitation being procedural law and, therefore, retrospective in nature - Central Govt. vide order held that extended period of limitation of one year was not available to assessee - However, HC vide impugned order allowed claim of rebate in favor of assessee - Whether assessee is entitld to claim rebate.

Held, effect of amendment of s. 11B of Act on 12-5-2000 is that all claims for rebate pending on this date would be governed by period of one year from date of shipment and not six months. However, it is subject to rider that claim for rebate should not be made beyond original period of six months. SC held that in present case since claims for rebate were made beyond original period of six months, respondents cannot avail of extended period of one year on subsequent amendment to s. 11B of Act. Mafatlal Industries Ltd. v. Union of India, 1996 Indlaw SC 4128, relied on by SC it is held that such claims for rebate can only be made u/s. 11B of Act within period of limitation stated therefor. Hence, argument based on r. 12 of Rules would have to be discarded as it is not open to subordinate legislation to dispense with requirements of s. 11B of Act. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Rebate claim is not maintainable if same is not made within time specified under statute.
