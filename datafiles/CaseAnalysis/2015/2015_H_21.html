Pegasus Assets Reconstruction Private Limited v Haryana Concast Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

29 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 909; (2016) 4 SCC 47; AIR 2016 SC 494; 2016 (115) ALR 464; 2016 (1) AWC 854; 2016 (1) BC 389; [2016] 194 Comp Cas 310; 2016 (2) CompLJ 273; 2016 (2) CTC 173; 2016 (1) DRTC 323; JT 2016 (1) SC 57; 2017 (1) MahLJ 1; 2016 (1) MLJ 569; 2017 (1) MPLJ 25; 2016 (131) RD 578; 2016(1) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Liquidation, Private Limited, State Financial Corporation, Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, s. 529A - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 13, 17, 18 - Sale of secured asset by secured creditor under 2002 Act - Power of Official Liquidator (OL) - Determination - Companies in present was wound up- Secured creditor received recovery certificate from Tribunal against company - The Company Judge allowed registered securitization company to proceed under 2002 Act for enforcing its security but in view of orders passed earlier in winding up proceedings Company Judge laid down certain terms and conditions for permitting registered securitization company to stay outside winding up proceedings and bring about sale of secured assets u/s. 13 of 2002 Act - Different views are taken by HC with respect to power of secured creditor under 2002 Act for sale of secured asset and power of Company court and OL - Whether Company Court, directly or through OL can exercise any control in respect of sale of secured asset by secured creditor in exercise of powers available to such creditor under 2002 Act - Hence, present appeal.

Held, no order is required by Company Judge for association of OL in order to protect interest of workers and to realize their dues. Sufficient provisions have been made for this purpose under 2002 Act and Rules framed thereunder. Further, in capacity of borrower OL is not satisfied with decisions or steps taken by secured creditor or authorized officer, at appropriate stage it has sufficient opportunity to avail right of appeal u/s. 17 of 2002 Act before Tribunal. Further, there is right of further appeal u/s. 18 of 2002 Act before Tribunal. Further, required provisions of 1956 Act have been incorporated in 2002 Act for harmonizing 2002 Act with 1956 Act in respect of dues of workmen and their protection u/s. 529A of 1956 Act. Hence, in view of specific provisions under 2002 Act, there is no reason as to take recourse to any provisions of 1956 Act and permit interference in proceedings under 2002 Act either by Company Judge or OL. Hence, view expressed by Punjab and Haryana HC is not justified and is set aside. Further, SC held that confirmed sale in present case as there is no illegality or irregularity established against conduct of auction and once it is found that offer amount was fair offer in competitive bid conducted fairly and offer has been accepted and sale confirmed, it would not be proper for SC to undermine value of such auction sale conducted not only by secured creditor but also by OL who was permitted to be associated with whole process of finding out of valuation as well as conduct of sale. Hence, SC confirmed with judgment and order of Delhi HC by holding that powers under 1956 Act cannot be exercised by Company Judge to interfere with proceedings by secured creditor to realize its secured interests as per provisions of 2002 Act. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Company judge or OL cannot exercise its power or interfere in sale of secured assets by secured creditors under 2002 Act.
