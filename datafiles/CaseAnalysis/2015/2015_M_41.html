A. N. Sachdeva (Dead) by Lrs. and others v Maharshi Dayanand University, Rohtak and another

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 541; (2015) 10 SCC 117; JT 2015 (7) SC 173; 2015 (6) MLJ 719; 2015(8) SCALE 597; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 796; 2015 (5) SLR 611

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Computation Of, Retiral Benefits, Pension Rules, Ex-servicemen, Liberalised Pension Scheme, Age-Limit, M.D. University Pension Scheme, 1997

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - M.D. University Pension Scheme, 1997, r. 4(vii) - Service rendered not qualified for purpose of pension - Alleged Discrimination - Justification - Appellants worked in Punjab University and Kurukshetra University and later was promoted and transferred to respondent University - Further, appellants are receiving pension after their retirement from respondent University, however, it is confined to services rendered by them in same university and services of appellants in Punjab University/Kurukshetra University have not been counted as qualifying service for purpose of pension by respondent University - DB vide impugned order held dismissed application and in view of r. 4(vii) of Rules, services rendered by appellants in Punjab University/Kurukshetra University cannot be counted - Whether services rendered by appellants in Kurukshetra University/Punjab University is qualifying service for purpose of pension and can be added to services rendered by them in respondent University.

Held, vide memorandum dt. 24-12-2001 issued by State Govt., pension scheme was modified inasmuch as State Government has agreed for counting services of employees of Punjab University/Kurukshetra University on retirement from respondent University as qualifying service. Further, after rendering services in Punjab University/Kurukshetra University, appellant employees had been directly appointed on very next day in respondent University. Earlier, employees of Punjab University were allocated to Kurukshetra University and it is not in dispute that present respondent University used to be regional centre of Kurukshetra University prior to its establishment as full-fledged University. Further, considering principles enunciated under arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution, and that benefit is not ex gratia payment but payment in recognition of past service, discrimination could not have been made between those employees who have been absorbed/allocated are entitled to count their services as qualifying service for purpose of pension and not those who have been appointed directly. Further, it is case of upward revision of benefit and classification which is sought to be created of not extending benefit to persons appointed directly and by fixing cut-off date cannot be said to be intelligible one; same is discriminatory, hence, appellants would be entitled for benefit from date decision has been taken on 24-12-2001 to compute previous service rendered in Punjab University/Kurukshetra University as qualifying service. Further, in other case of respondent university, SC failed to interfere with decision of HC in favor of employees. Hence, appellants are entitled for benefit of counting services rendered in Punjab University/Kurukshetra University as qualifying service for purpose of pension subject to fulfillment of conditions specified in memorandum dt. 24-12-2001 etc. Impugned order passed by lower Court is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Creation of two different class for grant of benefit should be based on reasonable classification.
