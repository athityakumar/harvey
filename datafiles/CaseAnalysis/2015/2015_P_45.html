Prabhu Dayal Khandelwal v Chairman, U. P. S. C. and others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 856; AIR 2015 SC 3057; 2015(5) ALL MR 910; 2015 (5) SLR 663

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Failure in communication of ACR - Promotion denied - Justification - Appellant was to be considered for promotion from post of Commissioner of Income Tax to that of Chief Commissioner of Income Tax - However, it is stated that appellant failed to satisfy benchmark mentioned in guidelines, hence, he was not considered fit for promotion - Appellant contended that un-communicated Annual Confidential Reports could not be taken into consideration, to defeat his claim for promotion - Tribunal allowed claim of appellant which - However, HC vide impugned order reversed order passed by Tribunal - Hence, present appeal.

Held, with respect to non-consideration of claim of appellant, SC held that impugned order passed by HC, deserves to be set aside, inasmuch as, claim of appellant could not be ignored by taking into consideration, un-communicated Annual Confidential Reports for years 1995-1996, 1996-1997 and 1998-1999, wherein appellant was assessed as 'good'. In absence of aforesaid entries, it is apparent, that remaining entries of appellant being "very good", he would be entitled to be considered fit for promotion, to post of Chief Commissioner of Income Tax, on basis of then prevailing guidelines, and remaining valid Annual Confidential Reports. Further, with respect to representations filed by appellant against reports for years 1995-1996, 1996-1997 and 1998-1999 need to be taken to their logical conclusion, SC held that since almost two decades have passed by since Annual Confidential Reports were recorded, it would be too late in day to require authorities to adjudicate upon representations made by appellant as against un- communicated Annual Confidential Reports. Hence, respondents are directed to reconsider claim of promotion of appellant, to post of Chief Commissioner of Income Tax, for vacancies which arose during years 2000-2001 and 2001-2002 on basis of communicated reports for years 1997-1998 and 1999-2000. Further, appellant is found to be entitled for promotion to post of Chief Commissioner of Income Tax, he shall be promoted to said post, with effect from date of his entitlement. In such eventuality, he shall also be entitled to all arrears of salary, as would have been payable to him, if he had been promoted as Chief Commissioner of Income Tax at right time. Simultaneously, he would be entitled to revision of his retiral benefits. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Non communication of ACR to public servant is arbitrary and is violative of art. 14 of Constitution.
