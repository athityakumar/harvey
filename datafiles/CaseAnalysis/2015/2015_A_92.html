Delhi Diocesan Trust Association v Ashwani Kumar

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 778; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 144; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 47; JT 2015 (9) SC 35; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 709; 2015 (2) RCR(Rent) 421

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Unauthorized occupation of premises - Eviction - Justification - Appellant Association filed suit for mandatory injunction against respondent with pleading that appellant is owner of land in question - Further, it is stated that land was given to respondent's grandmother on license - Further, when respondent's grandmother failed to deliver possession of land to appellant , license in her favour was terminated by getting served notice on her - Hence, appellant stated that occupation of land thereafter, by respondent is unauthorized - Trial Court passed order in favor of appellant - Further, HC vide order reversed order passed by Trial Court - Further, second appeal filed by appellant was dismissed - Hence, present appeal.

Held, respondent has not pleaded that lease was created independently in his favour. Further, Trial court after going through evidence on record found that PW6 to whom defendant claims to have paid rent has supported case of appellant. Further, SC held that respondent failed to prove that he was tenant in open land as pleaded by him. Further, first appellant Court also found that respondent failed to prove himself to be statutory tenant as pleaded by him. Hence, SC held that first appellate court has erred in not accepting finding of Trial Court, and reversing same. HC has also erred in ignoring title of appellant in view of fact that DW 1 has admitted in cross examination that local church was merged with respondent. Hence, impugned order passed by first appellate court and HC is set aside and decree passed by Trial Court is restored. Respondent is directed to vacate land in question within period of two months. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - It is justified to evict tenant prom premises when he fails to prove that he is statutory tenant.
