Ashwani Kumar @ Ashu and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 271; (2015) 6 SCC 308; 2015 CRLJ 2543; 2015 (2) JCC 1156; JT 2015 (4) SC 399; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 609; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 74; 2015(5) SCALE 265; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 1039

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Benefit Of Doubt, Fax, Kirpan

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 302, 367, 120B - Refusal to Identification parade - Charge-sheet was filed against accused for committing murder of deceased and causing injuries to PW15-husband and Trial Court held that prosecution successfully proved its case against seven accused persons including appellants - On appeal, HC gave benefit of doubt to three accused persons and acquitted them but confirmed conviction and sentence of appellants and hence, instant appeals by appellants - Whether conviction of accused-Ashwani Kumar, accused-Anil Kumar and accused-Joginder Singh could be upheld.

Held, prosecution had made the witness available for test identification but accused-Ashwani Kumar and accused-Anil Kumar had refused to participate in the test. Further, extra judicial confession deposed by PW7 and the incident which had happened at dhaba as spoken by PW5 and PW6 corroborate evidence against said accused. Further, fact that photograph of deceased was recovered pursuant to disclosure statement by accused-Ashwani Kumar is another circumstance and said photograph was recovered from Farm that was under control of accused-Anil Kumar. The description of deceased on backside of photograph and refusal on part of accused-Ashwani Kumar to give his specimen hand writing leads to adverse inference against said accused. Furthermore, recovery of weapon-Kirpan which according to doctor could have resulted in the injuries suffered by victim and her PW15-husband and the blood-stained seat cover are other circumstances lending complete corroboration. The communication by accused-Ashwani Kumar and accused-Anil Kumar with number in Canada which itself was source for the fax-message is another circumstance and all said circumstances stand proved and clearly point in the direction of guilt of accused-Ashwani Kumar and accused-Anil Kumar and additionally lend complete support to the testimony of and identification by PW15-husband. Hence, Trial Court and HC were justified in finding accused-Ashwani Kumar and accused-Anil Kumar guilty of offences u/ss. 364/307 and 302 of IPC. Furthermore, with regard to conviction of accused-Joginder Singh, tempo used by accused-Ashwani Kumar was under control of accused-Joginder Singh and also there was no explanation offered by accused-Joginder Singh for telephonic conversations between him and accused-Ashwani Kumar and accused-Anil Kumar just before and soon after the incident which are extremely crucial and also as per extra Judicial confession of PW7, parents of deceased had given money through accused-Joginder Singh. Hence in such circumstances, findings of Trial Court and HC as to guilt of accused-Joginder Singh u/ss. 364, 302 and 367 read with s. 120B of IPC is justified. However, with regard to accused-Darshan Singh, prosecution only produced record of telephonic conversations and said communications were from landline number that stands in name of brother of accused-Darshan Singh and there is no evidence on record that said landline number was under exclusive control of Darshan Singh. Further, daughter of accused-Darshan Singh is married with son of maternal uncle of deceased and so, conversations with number in Canada are explainable and so, benefit of doubt is given to accused-Darshan Singh and acquitted. Hence, appeals of accused-Ashwani Kumar, accused-Joginder Singh and accused-Anil Kumar are dismissed affirming orders of conviction and sentence passed against them and appeal of accused-Darshan Singh is allowed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - If the witness is trustworthy and reliable, the mere fact that no test identification parade was conducted would not be a reason to discard the evidence of the wtiness.
