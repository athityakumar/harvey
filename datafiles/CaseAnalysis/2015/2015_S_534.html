Chintaman Namdev Patil (dead) v Sukhdev Namdev Patil and another

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 777; (2016) 1 SCC 681; AIR 2015 SC 3773; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 136; 2015 (113) ALR 729; 2016 (1) ALT(SC) 20; 2015 (6) AWC 5915; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 48; 2016 (4) CTC 108; JT 2015 (9) SC 32; 2016 (4) MahLJ 20; 2016 (3) MPLJ 18; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 878; 2016 (130) RD 456; 2015(10) SCALE 229; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 823; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 250

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Order passed by lower court - Sustainability - Appellant sought declaration that he is owner of suit land in question - Appellant also sought injunction against respondents restraining them from interfering in his possession - Trial court dismissed suit of appellant - Further, first appellate Court allowed appeal and decreed appellant's suit by granting decree as prayed by him - HC admitted second appeal of respondent on two substantial questions of law arising in case - Further, HC vide impugned order allowed second appeal and in consequence dismissed appellant's suit - Hence, present appeal.

Held, on perusal of judgment it clearly shows that HC neither set out case of parties from their pleadings properly nor mentioned findings recorded by Trial Court and nor of first appellate court. Further, HC did not examine case in context of legal provisions governing issues and nor dealt with any submissions urged by parties much less to record categorical finding on questions framed. SC held that it was legally obligatory upon HC to properly set out case of parties, findings recorded by Trial Court and first Appellate Court, arguments of parties on questions of law framed and then answer questions framed in light of law applicable to controversy involved by giving its reasoning. Hence, manner in which HC decided appeal does not satisfy requirement under law. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and matter is remanded to HC for fresh consideration. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Order passed by lower Court without framing substantial question of law, arguments of both parties, reason of passing order is not justified and liable to be set aside.
