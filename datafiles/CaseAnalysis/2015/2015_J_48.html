Rajan v Joseph and others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Kurian Joseph, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 242; (2015) 8 SCC 436; 2015 ACJ 2151; AIR 2015 SC 2359; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2044; 2015 CRLJ 2951; JT 2015 (3) SC 565; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1930; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 245; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 542; 2015(4) SCALE 597; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Machine

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Quashing of Proceedings - Justifiability - Deceased who was working as maid in house of respondents died due to electric shock allegedly sustained by her while working on washing machine - Appellant who was husband of deceased filed complaint against respondents - HC quashed proceedings initiated against respondents - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, after due inquiry, Electric Inspector found that there is no possibility of current leakage in the washing machine. It was also reported that by mistake deceased might have tried to turn on and off the switch with wet hands and at that time she might have come into contact with the live portion behind the plug and died due to electric shock. As seen from the certificate issued from Hospital, the respondents immediately rushed to the hospital to save the life of the deceased and she was declared dead by the Doctor CW3. Considering the materials on record, no offence u/s. 304A IPC is made out. Although no rash or negligent act is noticed on the part of the respondents, the fact remains that the deceased was doing the household work for the respondents, and working as per the instructions of the respondents at the relevant time. Thus, respondents were liable to compensate deceased for the said work. Appeal disposed of.
