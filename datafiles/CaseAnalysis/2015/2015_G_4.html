Commissioner of Income Tax-I, Coimbatore v G. R. Govindarajulu and Sons

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 858; [2015] 378 ITR 1; 2015(10) SCALE 207; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Government securities

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 11, 11(1)(a) - Money spent for Charitable purpose - Deduction claimed - Justification - Respondent assessee is Public Charitable Trust and it filed its return for A/Y. 1994-1995 declaring 'nil' taxable income - Assessee has mentioned gross income for year in sum of Rs. 99,41,221/- - Further, from said income, assessee has spent Rs. 47,27,533/- for objects of Trust - Further, in return it was also stated that it was setting apart sum of Rs. 32 Lacs to be spent for charitable purposes in following year - Hence, assessee claimed that it was entitled to have deduction of entire amount and for purpose of taxation income was 'nil' u/c. 11 of Act - AO found that assessee had actually spent sum of Rs.47,27,533/ and deduction to that effect was given by AO, however, with respect to assessee set apart sum of Rs.32 Lacs, AO denied deduction on ground that no option for this purpose was exercised by assessee before filing of return - Further, appellant authority allowed appeal of assessee which was confirmed by Tribunal and HC - Whether impugned order passed by lower court is in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to exercising option in return filed by assessee is concerned, SC held that HC and lower authorities below are right in their approach. Further, law does not mention any specific mode of exercising option and said option has to be exercised before filing of return. SC held that if option is exercised when return is filed, that would be treated as in conformity and comply with provisions contained in s. 11 of Act. However, SC held that as per provisions of s. 11(1)(a) of Act, amount which is actually applied for and spent towards objects of Trust is to be allowed. Actual expenditure which was made for Rs. 47,27,533/- was allowed by AO. Further, s. 11(1)(a) of Act also entitled assessee to set apart further amount if not spent in same year and option is exercised in that behalf. However, appellant authority has gone wrong as he has ignored provision which entitled assessee to exercise such option only to extent of 25%. SC held that in present case, assessee had exercised option of setting apart amount of Rs.32 lacs which was more than 25%. Total income was Rs. 99,41,221/- and 25% thereof would be Rs.24,85,305/-. Hence, entire amount of Rs. 32 lacs could not have been allowed as directed. SC held that this aspect has not been noticed by HC and no further amount could be allowed as deduction and entire income cannot be treated as exempted from income tax. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and AO is directed to recompute taxable income in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Assessee is entitled for deduction for amount spent on charitable purpose in accordance with law laid down under statute.
