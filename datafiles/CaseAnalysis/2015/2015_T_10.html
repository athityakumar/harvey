State of Tamil Nadu and another v TVL. South Indian Sugar Mills Assn. and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 565; 2015(8) SCALE 699; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, Tamil Nadu Distillery Rules

Summary: Excise - Tamil Nadu Distillery Rules, r. 5-A - Administrative service fee on Industrial alcohol manufactured by Sundry distilleries - State Govt. amended r. 5-A of the Rules and increased administrative service fee to Rs.1 per bulk litre for industrial alcohol produced by sundry distilleries located in State - Writ petitioners challenged the same before HC contending that said exercise of increasing administrative service fee had to be meticulously calculated on premise of quid pro quo - Single Judge of HC observed that increased demand of Rs.1 per bulk litre of industrial alcohol would amount to effecting increased in recovery from 1/3rd to 2/3rd of total expenses incurred by Excise Department which would ceased to be based on principle of quid pro quo and dismissed writ petitions, making it legal for State to impose and collect only 50 paise per bulk litre - Aggrieved, State preferred appeals but DB of HC concurred with decision of Single Judge of HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeals could be allowed.

Held, as per figures noted by DB of HC, there was only expenditure of Rs.93.2 lakh against which there was an estimated collection of administrative fee at rate of 50 paise per bulk litre aggregating to 11.73 crore, which would undoubtedly cover expenses over and above those incurred by State only on its establishments/offices in respective distilleries. Hence, there is no scope to interfere with impugned judgment of DB of HC. Further, State Govt. failed to furnish credible details of expenditure which, according to it, related to administrative or regulatory or service expenses. Further, even though there was no justification for State Govt. or its Excise Department to collect charges at the rate of Rs.1 after it had been quashed by Single Judge, yet, in view of absence of diligence by writ petitioners from seeking timely variations or modification of Orders passed by SC, prayer for directions for refund of collection of charges over and above 50 paise per bulk litre are rejected. Appeals dismissed with costs. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - So long as expense are incurred by State Govt. in ensuring that industrial alcohol is not used as potable alcohol, recovery thereof would be permissible.
