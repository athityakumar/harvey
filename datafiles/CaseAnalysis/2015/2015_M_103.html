M.C.D. and another v Mehrasons Jewellers Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 551; (2015) 9 SCC 719; AIR 2015 SC 3121; 2015(5) ALL MR 911; JT 2015 (7) SC 218; 2015(8) SCALE 753; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, Ahmedabad Municipal Act

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, s. 116E - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Incorporation of Bye-laws - Determination of rateable value of land - Application of SC judgments - Appellant Municipal Corporation published bye laws in question and had taken upon itself determination of rateable value of lands and buildings according to principles laid down therein - However, in two other decisions in question, SC held that notwithstanding advent of 1994 bye-laws, 'annual value' has still to be determined on principles laid down in these two judgments - Appellant Corporation contended that once Corporation lays down its own bye-laws, principles laid down in two SC judgments no longer apply, as they were applied in situations where appellant Corporation did not itself lay down how annual value was to be determined - Hence, present appeal.

Held, both judgment's in question were judgments dealing with situation where 1952 Act applied to premises governed by said Act. Further, present appeal refers to A/Y's post 1994 and are said to be in factual scenario where after amendment of 1988 to 1952 Act, 1952 Act does not apply either for reason that rent fixed is more than Rs.3,500/- p.m. or that property has been newly constructed and is exempt from its provisions for period of 10 years. Government Servant Cooperative House Building Society Limited & Others v. Union of India & Others, 1998 Indlaw SC 978, relied on SC held that decision in question is not correctly decided as cases where Municipal Corporation of particular State itself lays down as to how annual value is to be determined. Hence, assessments made after 1994 bye-laws came into existence, such assessments shall be governed by these byelaws alone and principles laid down in cases in question would have no relevance in such situation. Further, with respect to other decision where it is decided that assessee is empowered to ask for decision on basis of annual value of property, SC held that under 3rd proviso of s. 169 of Act, appeal pending before District Judge is to be transferred compulsorily to Taxation Tribunal (after it is set up) if applicant requests for disposal of appeal on basis of annual value. Hence, even at appellate stage applicant can opt to apply for new unit area method provided for in s. 116E so that his property tax assessment may be decided in accordance with said method even though it pertains to assessment year prior to 2003. Further, second proviso to s. 169 of Act would apply in cases where, after Taxation Tribunal is set up, there is no request by any applicant to determine his case on basis of annual value. In such cases also, Tribunal once set up may take up appeal of such person with approval of earlier appellate authority, hence order in question passed by DB cannot be faulted. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Bye laws published by authority will apply to property matters over judgments passed and principles laid down by SC.
