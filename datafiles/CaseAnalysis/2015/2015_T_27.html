Dhropadabai and others v Technocraft Toolings

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 388; 2015 (2) ACC 381; 2015 ACJ 1751; AIR 2015 SC 2307; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 185; 2015 (3) AWC 2675; 2015 (146) FLR 1; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 336; 2015 (4) RLW 3179; 2015(4) SCALE 165; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 921; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 190

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employee's [Workmen's] Compensation Act, 1923 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2(14) - Death of employee - Failure to grant compensation - Justification - Appellants, legal heirs of deceased employee filed application for grant of compensation under 1923 Act on grounds that appellant no.1 is wife and other respondents were minor children of deceased-employee, who had suffered chest pain at work place and was immediately taken to Hospital where he was declared dead - Labour Court held that there was no justification to deny compensation under 1923 Act solely because employee was insured person under 1948 Act and compensation was awarded to his legal heirs - Further, HC allowed appeal of respondent and held that that legal heirs would not be entitled to get compensation under 1923 Act as he was insured person - Whether appellants are entitled to compensation from authorities under 1923 Act.

Held, based on earlier judgment, it is clear that once employee is 'insured person' u/s. 2(14) of 1948 Act, neither he nor his dependents would be entitled to get any compensation or damages from employer under 1923 Act. Further, plain language used in Act clearly conveys so, hence, order passed by HC cannot be interfered. However, SC held though respondent is getting benefits under 1948 Act, yet amount that has already been withdrawn by legal heirs of deceased-employee, should be recovered by employer by way of deducting periodical sum that is paid to family members of deceased employee. SC held that cause of justice should be best sub-served as appellants have been fighting litigation since decade. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Legal heirs of deceased would not be entitled to get compensation under 1923 Act as he was insured person under 1948 Act.
