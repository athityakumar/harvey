Madhya Pradesh Housing and Infrastructure Development Board and others v B. S. S. Parihar and others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 907; JT 2015 (9) SC 127; 2015(8) SCALE 100; 2016(8) SCALE 100; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Administrator, Societies, Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, Madhya Pradesh Co-Operative Societies Rules, 1962, Madhya Pradesh Griha Nirman Mandal Adhiniyam, 1972, Madhya Pradesh Preparation and Revision of Market Value Guidelines Rules, 2000, Madhya Pradesh Housing Board Accounts Rules, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Allotment of plot - Price escalation - Justification - Advertisement was published in local newspaper regarding Housing Scheme which was floated by appellant Board for allotment of houses in question for employees of State Govt. and State Public Sector Undertaking - Appellant Board held draw of lots for allotment of said houses in dispute and successful applicants were notified by appellant-Board in 2007 - Further, appellant Board constituted Price Fixation Committee for fixation of rational price for said houses and prices was fixed - However, at time of delivery of house in question, appellant Board has increased price of plot and demanded money from respondent - Commissioner of appellant-Board vide order rejected representations of allottees - However, lower court allowed petition of respondent and directed appellant Board to fix price of land as it existed on date of issuance of allotment letter and consequently quashed land price determined by appellant-Board which was based on guidelines of Collector - DB upheld findings of lower court thereby quashing enhanced/final demand raised by appellant Board - Hence, present appeal.

Held, contentions of respondents that once appellant Board has made allotment of said plot of land, it is debarred from raising cost of construction or claiming enhanced prices for said land, is wholly untenable in law in view of clauses contained in advertisement published in newspaper which read that cost of houses shown in this advertisement are totally provisional and final fixation of price will be done after completion of Scheme. Hence, allottees will have to pay difference between tentative cost and final sale price of land which is based on fixation of final cost of land, within stipulated time. Hence, allotment of said plot of land in favour of respondent-allottees is only provisional in nature and same would be subject to final fixation of price of land that will be done after completion of Scheme as said clause is binding upon respondent- allottees. Further, u/s. 50 of Act, appellant Board is empowered to retain, lease, sell, exchange or otherwise dispose of any land, building or other property vesting with it, situated in area comprised in any housing Scheme or in any adjoining area. SC held that final sale price which is fixed and intimated to allottees is in accordance with provisions of Act and Rules and clause of advertisement which is binding on respondent allottees. Hence, HC has committed error in law by quashing demand notice of appellant Board for payment of final sale price and allowing petitions of respondent allottees without considering terms and conditions of advertisement and statutory provisions of Act and Rules towards fixation of cost of land. Hence, impugned order passed by lower court is quashed. SC held that determination of final cost of land in dispute must be in consonance with doctrine of proportionality but not on basis of market price, i.e. fixed by Committee for determination of guidance value of immovable property in District which would be arbitrary, unreasonable and unfair. Further, in order to maintain balance, SC modified demand notice served upon respondent allottees and fixed cost of developed plots for year 2009 at Rs.16,500/-. Further, same may be revised by adding 10% to provisional cost every year upto date of demand made upon said amount which is payable by respondent allottees. Further, interest at rate of 9% p.a. may be added on such enhanced revised value amount from date of demand till date of payment in modification of demands to aforesaid extent from respondent allottees. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Authority is justified to raise price of plot allotted as prices provided in adverstisement is provisional, however, increase in price should be reasonable.
