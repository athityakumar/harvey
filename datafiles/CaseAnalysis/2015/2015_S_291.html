Sri Malaprabha Co-Oprative Sugar Factory Limited v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 618; 2015 (5) KarLJ 513; 2015(9) SCALE 125; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 837

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Karnataka Excise (Manufacture and Bottling of Arrack) Rules, 1987

Summary: Excise - Fixation of price of rectified spirit - Respondent-State while fixing price of rectified spirit uniformly at Rs.6 per litre, indicated that captive distilleries would be entitled to receive only Rs.5 per litre and balance Rs.1 per litre would be receivable by Respondent-State - While so, appellant received entire sum at Rs.6 per litre on supplies of rectified spirit made by it and when demand of Rs.13,32,000 was made by Superintendent of Excise, filed writ petition before HC and judgment of Single Judge in said writ petition was upheld by DB of HC and hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether appeal could be allowed.

Held, appellant had full knowledge of the fact that it had been permitted to supply rectified spirit to third parties who are engaged in business of production of arrack on the condition that of the general fixed price of Rs.6 per litre, Rs.1 per litre would have to be made over to Respondent-State. Hence, appeal is liable to be dismissed. Appeal dismissed.
