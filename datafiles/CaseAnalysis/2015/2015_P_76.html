Commissioner of Customs (Import), Mumbai v Pundrick Ravindra Trivedi and others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 870; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 812; 2015(9) SCALE 18

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importer, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Statements, India

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Goods imported in parts - Evasion of duty - Three consignments of imported goods landed at Port - Bills of Entries for clearance of these consignments were filed and it was declared that consignments contained various electronic components/parts - Further, goods were supplied by same supplier and had arrived on same vessel and were also shipped on board on same day - Further, bills of lading for three consignments were also issued on same day - Further, invoice raised by suppliers in respect of all three consignments were again issued on same day - Further, after investigation it was found that systems was assembled from parts imported vide Bills of Entries - Show cause notice was issued against respondent no. 1 to 3 for payment of differential duty and payment of penalty - Commissioner vide order confirmed payment of differential duty and penalty - However, Tribunal vide impugned order passed order in favor of respondent and order passed by Commissioner was set aside - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Tribunal failed to take into consideration that technicians from office of companies were called who had inspected goods in question and were able to assemble systems from parts imported by respondent and make it functional and this was accomplished in presence of panchas. Further, it was found that all parts, down to last screw, were imported to make complete sets. There was no rebuttal by respondents to aforesaid clinching evidence. On contrary, there are clear admissions by respondents, alleged sole proprietors of these two firms, that they are small time agents and it is respondent who had opened said two import firms in their names and back accounts were also opened by respondent. Further, they were paid money every month for signing various import documents and they did not know anything about import of electronic goods. They also stated that they have no knowledge of DGFT/IEC code etc; that they had never visited office premises of said firms which had been opened in their name; that they had not met concerned clearing agent at any time. Further, none of three persons retracted their statements which were recorded u/s. 108 of Act. SC held that material placed by Department clearly proves that inadequate and wrong descriptions were given in import documents which gave right to Revenue to correctly assess goods. Further, transaction value, as disclosed by importers, was doubtful and was rightly rejected by adjudicating authority. Further, even declaration given in Bills of Entries showing goods as 'parts/components' was erroneous and, therefore, rightly rejected by Commissioner. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is justified to impose duty and penalty upon assessee when there is willful evasion of duty under law.
