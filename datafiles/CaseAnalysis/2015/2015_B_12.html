Election Commission of India v Bajrang Bahadur Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 2016 SC 2301; 2015 (6) AWC 6191; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 144; 2015 (3) MLJ 848; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 795; 2015(5) SCALE 225; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Citizenship, Constituency, Obligation, Disqualified, Maharashtra Municipalities Act, Purity

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 192, 192(1) - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 150(1), 9A - Election - Disqualification - Petitioner in Transferred case contested in general elections to the UP Legislative Assembly from 315 Pharenda Assembly Constituency and was declared elected - Governor of Uttar Pradesh made a declaration in exercise of the authority conferred u/art. 192 of the Constitution that the petitioner incurred the disqualification stipulated u/s. 9A of the 1951 Act - Governor declared respondent no. 1 in SLP and petitioner, disqualified from the membership of Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly - Petitioner filed application and HC passed interim order staying the election process to fill vacancy to the above-mentioned constituency - Aggrieved Commission moved SLP and interim suspension of the impugned order of the HC was granted - Whether the HC was acting within its jurisdiction when it intercepted the election process after the issuance of a notification u/s. 150(1) of the 1951 Act.

Held, authority and jurisdiction of the HCs u/art. 226 of the Constitution to adjudicate the disputes which are brought before them is a grant of the Constitution, though such authority and jurisdiction have well known limitations. There is always a possibility in a given case that the decision of the Governor could be held to be unsustainable. In the eventuality of such a conclusion by the HC, the Legislator who is unseated consequent upon decision of the Governor u/art. 192 of the Constitution is entitled to continue as a Member of the Legislature if the tenure to which he is elected still survives. In the meanwhile if a bye-election were to be held to fill up the vacancy arising as a consequence of the decision of the Governor and in such an election if a person other than unseated legislator gets elected, there would be a very anomalous situation of two persons validly elected to the same seat in the Legislature. Interim order granted by the HC is perfectly justified. Petition disposed of.
