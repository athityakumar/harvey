Union of India and another v S. N. Maity and another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 46; (2015) 4 SCC 164; AIR 2015 SC 1008; 2015 (3) AWC 2243; JT 2015 (1) SC 64; 2015(1) SCALE 85; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 580; 2015 (2) SLR 248

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Repatriation, Reversion, Transfer on deputation, Orissa Lokpal and Lokayuktas (Repeal) Ordinance, 1992, Orissa Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 1970, Orissa Lokpal (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1984, Order of repatriation

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Transfer on deputation - Order of repatriation - Justifiability - Respondent was appointed as Controller General of Patents, Designs and Trade Marks on transfer on deputation by appellant (UOI) - Respondent filed an application before Tribunal against his repatriation to his parent dept., which was dismissed by Tribunal - Aggrieved respondent filed petition before HC u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution - HC by impugned order held that appointment was not a case of simplicter deputation; that employer did not have the prerogative to get him repatriated to his parent dept. as the controversy fundamentally related to appointment and the source of appointment i.e. deputation on transfer; that the principles inhered u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution were violated and directed to be reinstated respondent in the post - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that it was the prerogative of the employer to recall respondent to parent dept. without assigning any reason before the term of 5 years was over as such a rider was postulated in the order of appointment.

Held, it is not a case of simple transfer. It is not a situation where one can say that it is a transfer on deputation as against an equivalent post from one cadre to another or one dept. to another. It is not a deputation from a Govt. dept. to a Govt. Corporation or one Govt. to the other. There is no cavil over the fact that the post falls in a different category and the respondent no. 1 had gone through the whole gamut of selection. The notification of appointment makes it absolutely clear that it is a tenure posting and the fixed tenure is 5 years unless it is curtailed. But, a pregnant one, this curtailment cannot be done in an arbitrary or capricious manner. There has to have some rationale. Merely because the words 'until further orders' are used, it would not confer allowance on the employer to act with caprice. Impugned communication dt.17-1-2005 by the Under Secretary to GOI is absolutely silent on any aspect and it has curtailed the tenure of posting without any justifiable reason. Therefore, the judgment passed by the HC lancinating the said order cannot really be found fault with. In instant case, the appellant should not suffer the loss of salary, but if Court direct for respondent's reinstatement as the HC has done, it will create an anomalous situation. It would be not apt at this juncture and, therefore, the cause of justice would be best subserved if he is allowed to get the entire salary that was payable to him for the post in question for the balance period, that is, 5 years minus the period he had actually served and drawn salary. The balance amount shall be paid with interest @ 9% p.a. within 3 months hence. The normal rule relating to pay scale has to apply to avoid any kind of piquant and uncalled for situation. Appeal disposed of.
