Kirshna Texport & Capital Markets Limited v Ila A. Agrawal and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 329; (2015) 8 SCC 28; AIR 2015 SC 2091; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2414; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 544; [2015] 190 Comp Cas 241; 2015 (2) CompLJ 431; 2015 CRLJ 2847; 2016 (1) DRTC 360; 2015 (3) JCC (NI) 226; JT 2015 (5) SC 177; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2101; 2016 (1) MahLJ 582; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 708; 2016 (1) MPLJ 270; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 341; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 949; 2015 (3) RLW 2200; 2015(6) SCALE 95; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 284; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 159

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141, 142(b) - Dishonour of cheque - Notice - Cheque drawn by Company on Bank in favour of appellant was returned with endorsement "funds insufficient" - Appellant filed Complaint against Company, Chairman and Managing Director of Company and 11 other directors including respondent nos. 1 and 2 - Magistrate convicted Company but acquitted respondent nos. 1 and 2 of offence punishable u/s. 138 of the Act - Hence, instant application - Whether notice u/s. 138 of the Act is mandatorily required to be sent to directors of Company before complaint could be filed against such directors along with Company.

Held, the notice u/s. 138 of the Act is required to be given to "the drawer" of the cheque so as to give the drawer an opportunity to make the payment and escape the penal consequences. No other person is contemplated by s. 138 of the Act as being entitled to be issued such notice. There is nothing in s. 138 of the Act which may even remotely suggest issuance of notice to anyone other than the drawer. The persons who are in charge of the affairs of the Company and running its affairs must naturally be aware of the notice of demand u/s. 138 of the Act issued to such Company. It is precisely for this reason that no notice is additionally contemplated to be given to such directors. If it is their case that the offence was committed without their knowledge or that they had exercised due diligence to prevent such commission, it would be a matter of defence to be considered at the appropriate stage in the trial and certainly not at the stage of notice u/s. 138 of the Act. S. 138 of the Act does not admit of any necessity or scope for reading into it the requirement that the directors of the Company in question must also be issued individual notices u/s. 138 of the Act. Therefore neither on literal construction nor on the touch stone of purposive construction such requirement could or ought to be read into s. 138 of the Act. The order passed by the HC is set aside. The matter remitted back to the HC for fresh consideration. Appeal allowed.
