Kamlesh Aggarwal v Narain Singh Dabbas and another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 127; 2016 (117) ALR 691; 2015 (4) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 58; 2015(2) SCALE 410; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 133; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 169

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code,1973, ss. 262, 251 - Civil Procedure Code,1908, O.11 r.32 - Constitution of India,1950, art.142 - Allotment of plot - Execution of sale deed - Non-compliance of order - Appellant filed complaint before District Consumer Forum against respondent for not allotting plot in her name and cancelled membership of appellant in default of payment by her - District Forum accepted complaint and directed respondent to allot plot - Since, there was non-compliance of order, appellant filed execution petition before District Forum to execute order which was allowed - Appeal filed by respondents was allowed by State Commission and order of District Forum set aside - Appellant filed first appeal before National Commission which was dismissed holding that there was no provision in the Act regarding filing of second appeal - Hence, instant Appeals.

Held, appellant, who is consumer, has been litigating matter before District Forum, State Commission and National Commission for last 17 years to get her legitimate right of getting sale deed registered in respect of allotted site. Therefore, there is proper ground to exercise power u/art.142 of the Constitution for reason that State Commission has erred in not remanding case to District Forum, after it has found fault with order of District Forum in convicting and sentencing respondents for not following procedure as provided under CrPC with direction to District Forum to follow procedure u/s.262 r/w Chapter 20, s.251 of CrPC to initiate penal action against respondents for non compliance of statutory provisions. Further, O.11 r/w. r.32 of CPC are applicable for execution of order of District Forum and to give effect to order passed by it on complaint as same will be in nature of decree as defined under CPC as procedure contemplated under O.11 r/w. r.32 of CPC which is substantial procedural right of appellant and same can be invoked by her as decree holder. However, alternative remedy is also available to appellant to take penal action against respondent. Hence, she is at liberty to avail said remedy also if she wants to get decree executed by District Forum as same has attained finality in her favour. Appeals disposed of.
