Wipro Limited v Assistant Collector of Customs and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 282; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 177; JT 2015 (5) SC 484; 2015 (5) MLJ 232; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 478; 2015(5) SCALE 390; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 640

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Place Of Importation, Delivery At The Time And Place Of Importation, Rate Of Exchange, Handling Charges, Such Goods, Ascertainable

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 14, 14(1), 14(1)(A), 156 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, r. 9(2) - Free on board value of goods - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC challenging validity of r.9(2) proviso (II-i) of the Rules contending that notional fixation of handling charges with addition of 1% of free on board value of goods, irrespective of nature of goods, size of cargo, was in total disregard to total handling charges, even when such actual handling charges could be ascertained and hence addition was totally irrational, arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of Constitution and ultravires of ss. 14(1) and 14(1)(A) of the Act - HC dismissed petitions and appeals preferred against were also dismissed and hence, instant appeal - Whether r.9(2) proviso (II-i) of the Rules is sustainable.

Held, amended proviso introduced fiction as far as addition of cost of loading, unloading and handling charges is concerned even in those cases where actual cost paid on such an account is available and ascertainable. Further, when actual charges paid are available and ascertainable, introducing fiction for arriving at the purported cost of loading, unloading and handling charges is clearly arbitrary with no nexus with the objective sought to be achieved and on contrary, goes against objective of s. 14 to accept the actual cost paid or payable and even in the absence thereof to arrive at the cost which is most proximate to the actual cost. Addition of 1% of free on board value is thus, arbitrary and irrational and violative of art.14 of Constitution. Hence, r. 9(2) proviso (ii) is unsustainable and bad in law and so, judgment of HC is set aside. Appeals allowed.
