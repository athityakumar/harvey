Brij Bihari Singh v Bihar State Financial Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 790; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 23; 2016 (1) AWC 666; 2016 (148) FLR 197; JT 2015 (11) SC 126; 2015 (4) LLJ 513; 2015(12) SCALE 514; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 51; 2016 (2) SLR 705

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Competent Authority, Reasonable Opportunity, Hearing, Enquiry Report, Bihar State Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1965

Summary: Service - Bihar State Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1965, regns. 39, 40 - Disciplinary proceedings - Right of appeal - On directions of State Govt., appellant was put under suspension and disciplinary proceedings were initiated by Disciplinary Authority on various charges of misconduct - Later, on recommendation of MD, Board of Directors passed order of dismissal of appellant from service - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC assailing said orders of dismissal but the same was dismissed by Single judge of HC and on appeal, DB of HC upheld orders of Single Judge and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether disciplinary proceedings are vitiated in law.

Held, disciplinary proceeding was conducted in gross violation of regn. 39 of Regulations inasmuch as no reasonable opportunity was given to appellant to place his case in defence. Further, Disciplinary Authority, instead of exercising power as Disciplinary Authority imposing punishment, referred his recommendations to appellate authority, Board of Directors, for taking a decision and Board of Directors exercised the power of Disciplinary Authority and imposed punishment of dismissal thereby deprived the appellant from moving the appellate authority against the said order. Such exercise of power is wholly arbitrary and discriminatory. Further, procedure adopted by respondents in removing the appellant from service is erroneous and suffers from serious discrimination and bias. Further, the Enquiry Officer conducted the enquiry without following the procedure and without giving sufficient opportunity to appellant to place his case. Enquiry is also vitiated in law. Hence, orders passed by Board of Directors and impugned judgments of HC are set aside and matter is sent back to Disciplinary Authority to proceed from stage of enquiry afresh and pass reasoned order after giving full opportunity of hearing to appellant. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Regulations impose duty on Authority to give personal hearing to delinquent and also right of appeal is provided by regn.40 of Regulations against any order passed by competent authority.
