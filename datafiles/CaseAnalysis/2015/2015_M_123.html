Molugu Mahipal Reddy and others v M. Pradeep Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 939; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 172; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 923; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Right To Information Act, 2005, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4, s. 48 - Illegal acquisition of land - Failure to consider vital aspects of case - Maintainability - Allegedly land in question was acquired by respondent authority by illegal ways which was causing injury to third parties - PIL was filed by aggrieved party - DB vide order dt. 22-5-2014, allowed PIL and gave direction to respondents no.1 to 3 in implement orders passed by HC in earlier proceedings - Whether impugned order passed by DB giving direction to respondents is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, HC has failed to examine vital question relating to petition being bonafide and in public interest. Further, whether land in question was Govt. land or privately owned land, was matter on which any finding by HC was bound to adversely affect and prejudice appellants. Further, appellants were not, arrayed as parties either in PIL or in earlier rounds of litigation even when they claim title to property in question on basis of properly registered instruments of sale in their favor. Further, whether notification issued by Govt. u/s. 48 of Act as early as in year 1972 could be assailed by public activist years after same was issued, is also required to be considered by HC. Further, HC failed to examine that notifications u/ss. 17, 9 of Act was issued. SC held that several issues have been raised that need to be suitably and articulately formulated and addressed by HC before it can issue any direction on subject. SC held that HC acted in hasty manner which resulted in miscarriage of justice and order passed is unsustainable. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and PIL is remitted back to HC for fresh disposal in accordance with law, after impleading appellants as parties to same and giving them opportunity of being heard in matter. Matter remanded to lower court. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Order passed by lower Court without taking into consideration vital aspect of case is not justified and is liable to be set aside.
