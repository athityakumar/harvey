Bhanuben and another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 621; (2015) 10 SCC 390; AIR 2015 SC 3405; 2015 CRLJ 4664; 2016 (2) G.L.R. 1206; 2015 (1) JCC 2617; JT 2015 (8) SC 262; 2016 (1) RLW 406; 2015(9) SCALE 716; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 306, 498A - Alleged Dowry demand - Suicide - Conviction - Appellant nos. 1 and 2 i.e. mother-in-law and sister-in-law respectively and husband of deceased tortured deceased within 2 years of marriage - It is alleged that dowry was demanded by appellant accused - Further, deceased was frequently driven out of her matrimonial home and was forced to stay at other place - Deceased had filed complaint against accused and filed maintenance application - However, after settlement and compromise deceased was brought back to appellants house, however, deceased was beaten and tortured by accused persons - Further, it is alleged that deceased consumed poison and committed suicide - Trial Court convicted appellant accused persons u/ss. 498A and 306 r/w s. 114 of IPC, however, accused persons were acquitted for offences punishable u/s. 304B read with s. 114 of IPC and s. 4 of Act - Order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction of accused person by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, it is undisputed fact that deceased was frequently taking refuge at other place and her paternal relatives had pacified her many times and she was sent back to her in laws' home after compromise which is proved by deposition of PW 6 paternal uncle of deceased before Trial Court and PW7 wife of PW6 and PW9 brother of deceased. Same has also been further corroborated by PW19 social worker who is working where deceased use to stay. Further, deceased had filed FIR against accused u/ss. 498A, 506(2), 114 of IPC and ss. 3 and 7 of Act and had also filed maintenance application. Further, witnesses have also stated that deceased was regularly taunted and mentally and physically harassed by accused and she had complained about same to witnesses. Further, independent witnesses who are neigbours of accused also deposed before Trial Court that they had witnessed heated exchanges and quarrel between accused and deceased which has been corroborated by PW 8 in his testimony before Trial Court. Further, another independent witness, PW10, has also deposed before Trial Court that deceased had sat out of her house for 2 consecutive days as accused had locked up their house and gone somewhere and came back only when they received call from their neighbours about deceased sitting there. It has also been noticed that deceased had spent several days sleeping at odd places like, empty buses, etc. as she had nowhere else to go and had also asked help from PW 12 thereby claiming that her husband and in-laws were trying to kill her and that is why she had run away from their home. SC held that there is no other material evidence or fact brought before SC to show that deposition of witness is unreliable - Hence, SC held that prosecution has established by adducing cogent and convincing evidence that deceased had been tortured by her in-laws and her husband, hence, ingredients to constitute offence u/s. 498A of IPC have been fully satisfied in against appellants. Further, with respect to accused had abetted in commission of suicide of deceased as provided u/s. 306 of IPC, SC held that same cannot be proved with conclusive evidence in light of fact that deceased in her dying declaration had clearly stated that she had consumed poisonous tablets by mistake and same was further corroborated by doctor who had examined her and had declared her as stable and conscious enough to give reasonable statement. Further, accused were present inside house at time deceased consumed poisonous tablet and they had taken her to hospital as soon as they realized that she was in critical condition. Thus, even though cruelty aspect meted on deceased has been proved beyond all reasonable doubt, it cannot be said that same had led her to commit suicide or that accused had abetted in commission of same. SC held that if accused had any hand in her death, same would have been revealed in dying declaration of deceased. Hence, prosecution has failed to prove beyond all reasonable doubt that accused had abetted deceased in commission of suicide as provided under provision of s. 306 of IPC. Hence, convicted and sentence for offence punishable u/s. 498A of IPC passed by HC is upheld, however, conviction and sentence for offence punishable u/s. 306 read with s. 114 of IPC is set aside. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Conviction of accused for dowry death should be based on evidence on record and proved ill-treatment and torture by accused persons.
