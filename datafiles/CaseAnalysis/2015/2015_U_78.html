Bharti Airtel Limited and others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 363; AIR 2015 SC 2583; 2015 (3) CompLJ 342; JT 2016 (1) SC 388; 2015 (4) MLJ 340; 2015(6) SCALE 479; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Migration, Indian Wireless Telegraphy Act, 1933

Summary: Telecom - Failure to renew license - Auction of license - Justification - Appellant is licensee holding licence granted under Act for specified region - Further, grant of licence and allotment of spectrum by respondent pursuant to two press releases issued on 10-1-2008 became subject matter of litigation before SC and vide judgment in 2G case set aside all licences granted pursuant to press releases - Further, respondent took steps to conduct auction with respect to licenses - Appellant licensee sought extension/ renewal of license , however, no action was taken by Govt. - Further, HC disposed petitions filed by appellant and directed Govt. to dispose of applications of writ petitioners within stipulated time frame, however, application of licensee was rejected by Govt. - Tribunal dismissed petition of appellant - Whether appellant licensee are entitled for extension/renewal of license.

Held, appellant licensee have no automatic right of renewal/extension on expiry of original tenure of license. Contract only provided for extension of period of license at sole discretion of licensor subject to condition that licensee makes application seeking extension during 19th year of currency of license and all licensee did make such application. However, under contract neither licensor nor licensee has right to insist that other party should continue with contract even if such other party is not willing to continue except on such terms and conditions on which other party may desire to continue. Such terms and conditions obviously include terms and conditions regarding economic stipulations subject to which either of parties is willing to be in contract. Further, SC held that licensee are not compelled to pay any specific tariffs fixed by licensor for availing the right to use the spectrum. If price for securing allocation of spectrum is likely to go up because of procedure of auctioning to have access to spectrum, it goes up because of market forces. Further, SC in earlier case held that findings of 2G case were limited to the case of spectrum. Further, it is held that Court respects the mandate and wisdom of executive in matter of choosing most suitable method of distribution of natural resources. Further, SC held that it is clearly matter of economic policy entailing intricate economic choice and Court lacks necessary expertise to make such choice. SC held that in matter of disposal of spectrum, auction is only 'permissible and intra vires method for disposal'. Hence, submission of licensee is required to be rejected. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Auction of license is economic policy and interference of Court is decisions passed by expert authority is not justified.
