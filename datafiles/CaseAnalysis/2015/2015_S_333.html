Vikram Cement and another v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 380; AIR 2015 SC 2397; 2015(5) ALL MR 946; 2015 (4) AWC 3411; 2015(4) SCALE 533; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 497; [2015] 80 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Sthaniya Kshetra Me Mal Ke Pravesh Par Kar Adhiniyam, 1976

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitutiton - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Madhya Pradesh Sthaniya Kshetra Me Mal Ke Pravesh Par Kar Adhiniyam, 1976 - Claim of refund - Notification in question violative of fundamental rights - Determination - Appellant Nos. 1 and 2 industrial units carries on manufacture and sale of cement for which it requires raw material - Appellants had been paying entry tax for entry of these goods in territory of State under Act - Further, in year 1999, respondent no.1 issued notification by which it reduced rate of entry tax on raw material in question - Notification in question remained in force for limited period, that is from 1-5-1997 to 30-9-1997, however, entry tax rate before dates in question remained unchanged - Further, notification explanation stated that amount which is already paid by dealer at higher rate shall not be refunded - Appellants contended that though they had paid entry tax at higher rate, which was now reduced vide aforesaid Notification, they became entitled to get refund of excess amount paid, but were still deprived of that refund because of explanation, hence, explanation attached to notification is violative of art. 14 of Constitution - HC dismissed petition holding that similar issue was already considered in earlier case - Whether explanation in question infringed fundamental right of appellant.

Held, notification in question results in invidious discrimination towards those who have paid tax at higher rate, like appellants, when compared with that category of persons who were defaulters and have now been allowed to pay tax at lower rate for relevant period. Hence, it results to situation where two categories of tax payers who are made to pay tax at different rates, even though they are identically situated. There is no basis for creating these two classes and there is no rationale behind it which would have any causal connection with objective sought to be achieved. Further, respondent failed to explain or show from any material on record which authorities to provide Explanation in question, hence, it becomes apparent that there is no objective behind such Explanation appended to notification which is sought to be achieved, except that Govt., after collecting tax from those who had paid at higher rate, did not intend to refund same. Hence, SC held that Explanation is highly discriminatory in nature. Further, in order to determine as to whether particular dealer is in fact entitled to refund or not, Govt. can go into issue of unjust enrichment while considering his application for refund and that would depend on facts of each case. It cannot be presumed that burden was passed on to buyers by these dealers, hence, they are not entitled to refund. Hence, SC held that impugned Explanations in Notifications are un constitutional and the same are quashed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Creation of classes should be based on reasonable classification and should not create discrimination being violative of fundamental right of citizen.
