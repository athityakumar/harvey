Ramchander v Ananta

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 139; 2015(3) ALL MR 493; 2015 (110) ALR 512; 2015 (3) ALT(SC) 38; 2015 (2) AWC 1953; JT 2015 (3) SC 192; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1905; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 1; 2015(2) SCALE 634; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 255; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 116

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(ia), 13(1)(ib) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 498-A - Cruelty - Desertion - Appellant-husband filed suit for divorce on ground of cruelty and desertion u/ss. 13(1)(ia) and 13(1)(ib) of the Act against respondent-wife and Trial Court upon consideration of evidence, granted decree of divorce holding that appellant-husband proved ground of cruelty and desertion against respondent-wife - However, on appeal, HC set aside said decree of divorce and allowed appeal by respondent-wife - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant-husband proved cruelty and desertion by respondent-wife.

Held, with regard to allegation of frequent shifting of residence due to quarrels by respondent-wife with landlords and neighbors, neither family members of appellant-husband nor landlords or neighbors of tenanted premises were examined, and as rightly held by lower courts, there is no evidence adduced by appellant-husband to substantiate said allegation. Further, with regard to cruelty meted out due to allegation made by respondent-wife in case filed u/s. 498-A of IPC against appellant-husband, said case was withdrawn by respondent-wife and continued to live with appellant-husband. The aberration on part of respondent-wife has been condoned by appellant-husband by resuming cohabitation and they continued to live together till date of alleged separation in 2003. Further, with regard to cruelty alleged by appellant-husband due to allegation by wife that he was involved in extra marital affair, lower courts concurrently found that respondent-wife had not substantiated said allegation. Mere failure to prove such allegation would not entitle appellant-husband to decree of divorce as rightly held by HC and conduct of wife that was complained is not so grave and weighty to be treated to be more serious than ordinary wear and tear of married life. Further, ground of desertion alleged is also not established. Hence, contention of appellant-husband that marriage has irretrievably broken down could not be accepted. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Instances of cruelty are not to be taken in isolation but to take the cumulative effect of the facts and circumstances emerging from the evidence on record and then draw fair inference whether plaintiff was subjected to mental cruelty due to conduct of other spouse.
