Commissioner of Income Tax, Gauhati v Sati Oil Udyog Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 387; (2015) 7 SCC 304; AIR 2015 SC 3244; [2015] 372 ITR 746; JT 2015 (6) SC 157; 2015 (3) MLJ 362; 2015(4) SCALE 142; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 1099; [2015] 230 TAXMAN 521

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Additional Income, Finance Act, 1993, Income Tax Act, 1995

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 143(1A) - Retrospective effect of section - Alleged Arbitrary - Respondent in its annual return for A/y's 1989-1990 and 1991-1992 showed loss - Vide Finance Act of 1993, s. 143 (1A)(a) of Act was substituted with retrospective effect from 1-4-1989 - Further, by assessment order dt. 14-12-1992, AO levied additional tax u/s. 143 (1A) for two A/Y's in question in manner provided in section - Respondent filed petition declaring provisions of s. 143 (1A) as ultra vires - Lower Court held that upheld s. 143 (1A) of Act as amended in 1993 prospectively but held that insofar as it operated with effect from 1989 on losses made by companies, section is arbitrary and unreasonable and would, therefore, have to be struck down - Order passed by lower court is confirmed by DB - Hence, present appeal.

Held, object of s. 143(1A) of Act is prevention of evasion of tax. By introduction of s. 143 (1A), persons who have filed returns in which they have sought to evade tax properly payable by them is meant to have deterrent effect and hefty amount of 20% as additional income tax is payable on difference between what is declared in return and what is assessed to tax. Further, even on reading of s. 143 1(a) which is referred to in s. 143 (1A), loss is envisaged as being declared in return made u/s. 139 of Act. Hence, retrospective amendment made in 1993 would only be clarificatory of position that existed in 1989 itself. Further, SC held that s. 143 (1A) of Act can only be invoked where it is found on facts that lesser amount stated in return filed by assessee is result of attempt to evade tax lawfully payable by assessee. Burden of proving that assessee has so attempted to evade tax is on revenue which may be discharged by revenue by establishing facts and circumstances from which reasonable inference can be drawn that assessee has, in fact, attempted to evade tax lawfully payable by it. Hence, subject to aforesaid construction of s. 143 (1A) of Act, SC upheld retrospective clarificatory amendment of said Section. Judgments of lower Courts are set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Retrospective application of section in question is valid when same is not arbitrary and unreasonable.
