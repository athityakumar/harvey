Rajdeep Sardesai v State of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 514; (2015) 8 SCC 239; AIR 2015 SC 2182; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3250; JT 2015 (5) SC 87; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 735; 2015(6) SCALE 598; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867, Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, All India Services (Conduct) Rules, 1968

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 199, 199(4), 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 499, 500, 501, 502 - All India Services (Conduct) Rules, 1968 - Alleged defamation - Prosecuting of accused - Quashing of criminal proceedings - News item on various dates in year 2007, allegedly making false implication against respondent no.2 Additional Commissioner of Police with regard to encounter case was published by appellants in respective publications and was telecast on news channel - Representation was given by respondent no.2 to State Govt. seeking previous sanction u/s. 199(4)(b) of CrPC for prosecution of appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 120B, 499, 500, 501, 502 of IPC which was granted - Trial Court has taken cognizance of offences alleged against appellants and passed orders summoning them to appear before Court for further proceedings in respective cases - HC vide impugned order allowed one petition and dismissed all other criminal petitions holding that news telecast in electronic media and other news items published in various newspapers of appellants per se are integrally connected with official discharge of duties of respondent no.2 and held that whether same amounts to defamatory, libel or scandalous statements is matter that has to be decided on evidence to be adduced by parties and in absence of any privilege to broadcaster, appellants cannot claim to quash criminal proceedings initiated against them and there was no merit to quash criminal proceedings against appellants - Hence, present appeal.

Held, sanction was accorded by State Govt. under relevant Government order in favour of respondent no.2. Further, according to s. 199 of CrPC read with Rules it is not necessary for State Govt. to separately issue sanction order against each one of appellants, when they are all responsible for telecasting and publishing said news item in electronic and print media and also when names of said electronic and print media have already been mentioned in said sanction order. Hence, there is no merit in contention urged on behalf of appellants that their names have not been specifically mentioned in said sanction order. Further, SC held that State Govt. in exercise of its administrative powers appreciated facts of matter, rightly applied its mind and accorded sanction u/s. 199(4) of CrPC in favour of respondent no.2 to initiate criminal proceedings against appellants. Further, said sanction was accorded by State Govt. after appreciating that statements telecast/published by appellants in electronic and print media and statement given by appellant in appeal Urdu Daily on basis of which news is published by its Editor, which are all statements defaming respondent no.2 while he was discharging his public function as public servant. Hence, impugned order passed by HC in rejecting petitions for quashing initiation of criminal proceedings against appellants u/s. 482 of CrPC is perfectly legal and valid, same does not call for interference by SC n exercise of its appellate jurisdiction as there is no substantial question of law framed in appeals nor is there any miscarriage of justice for appellants to interfere with at this stage. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - It is not justified to quash criminal proceedings when authority has passed sanction order in favor of aggrieved party in accordance with law.
