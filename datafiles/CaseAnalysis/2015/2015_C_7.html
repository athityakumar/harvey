Chaitanya Prakash Audichya v C.B.I.

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 468; (2015) 7 SCC 720; JT 2015 (6) SC 631; 2015(7) SCALE 268

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bribe, Raiding Party

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 20, 7 - Illegal Gratification - Conviction - Justification - PW1 firm was undertaking construction work for governmental agencies and was awarded contracts - PW1 required license from competent authority for which application was submitted - Appellant failed to process application of PW1 and alleged demanded amount towards illegal gratification for issuance of license to PW1 - Compliant was filed by PW1 and raiding party laid down trap where appellant was caught red handed accepting illegal gratification - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 7, 13(1)(d) read with . 13(2) of Act - Order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction of appellant by lower court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, complaint preferred itself disclosed that money was demanded and that complainant was asked to make payment by 30-5-2003 itself. Versions of PWs 1, 2 are completely consistent establishing basic ingredients of demand and acceptance. Tainted currency notes were found on person of appellant and explanation given by him soon after incident through his letter dt. 10-6-2003 is completely different from theory put forth while appellant examined himself as DW2. SC held that demand and acceptance stood fully established and presumption invocable u/s. 20 of Act also stood unrebutted. Hence, conviction order passed by lower Courts is confirmed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused from acceptance of illegal gratification is justified when basic ingredients of demand and acceptance is established.
