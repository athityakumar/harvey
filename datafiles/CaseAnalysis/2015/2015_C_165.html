S. N. Wadiyar (dead) Through Lr v Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 710; [2015] 378 ITR 9; JT 2015 (8) SC 455; 2015(10) SCALE 82; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 1059; [2015] 234 TAXMAN 881

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Valuation Date, Net Wealth, Land Appurtenant, Wealth-Tax Act, 1957, Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961, Direct Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 1989, Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1901, Urban Land Ceiling Act, 1962

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth-tax Act, 1957, s.20 - Urban Land Ceiling Act, 1962 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, s. 6(1) - Compensation - Land ceiling - Assessee applied to Settlement Commission for settlement of dispute regarding valuation of property and said applications were disposed of by Settlement Commission laying down norms for valuation of property - While so, assessee filed statement u/s. 6(1) of 1976 Act and later filed application before State Govt. u/s. 20 of 1957 Act for exemption of his lands under 1976 Act - Competent Authority passed orders dt.27-7-1989 determining vacant land in excess of ceiling limits and also observed inter alia that as per s. 11(6) of 1976 Act, maximum compensation that could be received by assessee was Rs.2 Lakh - Assessee challenged said order of Competent Authority before Tribunal and also challenged orders of Wealth Tax Officer passed under 1957 Act fixing value of land for different A/Ys before Commissioner(Appeals) - Commissioner (Appeals) passed orders accepting that urban land appurtenant to property be valued at Rs.2,00,000 - However, aggrieved by orders of Commissioner (Appeals), both assessee and Revenue filed appeals before Tribunal wherein common judgment was passed directing vacant land to be valued at Rs.2 Lakh - Assailing orders of Tribunal, Commissioner of Wealth Tax sought reference before HC - HC vide impugned order held that although prohibition and restriction contained in 1976 Act had effect of decreasing value of Property, still value of land could not be maximum compensation that is payable under provisions of 1976 Act and answered reference against assessee - Hence, instant appeals - Whether for purposes of 1957 Act, market value of vacant land belonging to assessee should be taken at price which is maximum compensation payable to assessee under 1962 Act.

Held, when the asset is under the clutches of 1976 Act and in respect of the said asset/vacant land, the Competent Authority under the 1976 Act had already determined the maximum compensation of Rs.2 Lakh, such property would not fetch more than Rs.2 Lakh as the assumed buyer knows that the moment property is taken over by Govt., he will receive the compensation of Rs.2 Lakh only. Hence, such asset would fetch if sold in the open market on the valuation date would not be more than 2 Lakh. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Tax Officer has to form an opinion about the estimated price if the asset were to be sold in the assumed market and the estimated price would be the one which an assumed willing purchaser would pay for it and on such reckoning, the asset has to be valued in the ordinary way.
