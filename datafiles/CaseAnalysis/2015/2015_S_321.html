Periyar and Pareekanni Rubbers Limited v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 622; (2016) 1 SCC 294; 2015 (113) ALR 271; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 1; JT 2015 (8) SC 315; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 119; 2016 (130) RD 243; 2015(9) SCALE 732; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Amendment and validation) Act, 1967, Disinclination

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23, 23(1), 23(1A), 23(2), 24, 26, 26(1), 28, 31, 34 - Compensation for land acquired - Date for payment interest on solatium - Determination - Awards were passed by Acquisition Officer awarding compensation, on market value of land which were partly based on capitalization method of yielding rubber trees for planted area and partly based on value of bare land on which there were no yielding rubber plantation. Awards included solatium and interest on compensation including solatium - Further, Reference Court vide order enhanced compensation partly based on capitalization method of yielding rubber trees for planted area and partly based on value of bare land on which there were no rubber trees and held that claimant is entitled to get 30% solatium, 12% additional market value from date of notification i.e., 10-10-1978 till date of award passed against it and they are also entitled to get 9% interest for first one year from date of dispossession and thereafter at 15% till realization of compensation awarded in favour of claimant/decree holder - Further, Execution Court passed order fixing balance amount payable by State Govt. after excluding solatium on that portion of market value of acquired land based on capitalization method of yielding rubber trees for planted area - Further, HC vide impugned order confirmed that solatium is payable by State Govt. for enhanced compensation awarded for market value of entire land, however, HC awarded interest on solatium w.e.f. 19-9-2001, date of judgment delivered by Constitution Bench in earlier case instead of from date of acquisition of land of appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, s. 34 read with s. 31 and term 'sum' u/s. 28 of Act that award includes not only sum as is determined u/s. 23(1) of Act but also amounts payable u/s. 23(1A) of Act and s. 23(2) of Act. . Further, SC held that enhanced compensation includes solatium @ 30% as provided under s. 23(2) of Act. Hence, claimant/decree holder is entitled for interest on solatium component which is part of compensation payable by State government to claimant. Further, SC held that HC was right in holding that claimant is entitled for interest not only in respect of land but also with respect to trees standing on land of which market value is determined by Reference Court. Further, with respect to date of payment of interest on solatium, Sunder v Union of India 2001 Indlaw, SC 67, relied on SC held judgment rendered by Constitution Bench is binding precedent on question of payment of legislative statutory interest payable on solatium u/ss. 23(1A), 28 and 34 of Act which cannot be deprived to claimant/decree holder by the court. As said judgment is binding upon State Govt. it cannot contend that it is not liable from date as provided under provisions of Act. Hence, impugned judgment and order with regard to awarding interest payable on solatium w.e.f. 19-9-2001 is vitiated in law, hence, that portion of impugned judgment and order is set aside. Respondent State Govt. is directed to pay interest as provided u/s. 23(1A) of Act on solatium component of award u/s. 23(2) of Act in reference award in earlier decisions and interest payable u/ss. 28 and 34 of Act. Further, respondent-State Govt. is further directed to compute same with reference to compensation awarded by Reference Court from date when claimant decree holder is entitled strictly in accordance with provisions of Act including solatium. However, second judge of bench disagreed with order passed by first judge and confirmed order passed by HC. Any contrary view can be taken only by larger Bench. Hence, it is appropriate that matter is placed before Bench of 3-Judges. Matter remanded to larger bench. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Compensation for land acquired will include interest on solatium and date for payment of interest on solatium should be fixed in accordance with provisions of Act.
