Surendra Kumar and others v Greater Noida Industrial Development Authority and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 448; 2015 (4) AWC 4108; 2015 (3) CGLJ 347; 2015 (146) FLR 994; JT 2015 (6) SC 95; 2015 (5) MLJ 474; 2015(7) SCALE 386; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 970; 2015 (5) SLR 114

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization of contractual employees - Appellants were initially appointed on contractual basis and later, services of appellants and other similarly situated contractual employees were regularized pursuant to policy decision taken by respondents and as approved by State Govt. and were appointed on post of Asst. Manager - However, after joining said posts, appellants filed claim petition before Tribunal and sought regularization with effect from date on which advertisement was issued for appointment to posts of Asst. Manager - Tribunal allowed claim petition and directed authorities to consider appellants' claim and so, respondents filed writ petition before HC wherein impugned judgment was passed quashing orders of Tribunal - Further, HC also quashed appointments of appellants as ex-facie illegal - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether policy decision extending the benefit of regularization to contractual employees against 60% vacant posts is deemed to regularize the services of appellants from the retrospective date, i.e., 20-11-2002, when the said posts were first advertised.

Held, even though advertisement dt.20-11-2002 indicated that there were vacancies, the policy of regularization of contractual employees was approved by State Govt. only on 5-3-2008. The appellants were appointed only pursuant to the policy decision of the respondents for regularization of contractual employees and thus, the appellants could not seek for regularization with retrospective effect from when the advertisement was issued, because at that time regularization policy was not in vogue. By policy of regularization, it was intended to give the benefit only from the date of appointment. The appellants having accepted the appointment orders dt.6-8-2010 and also joined the post, could not turn around and claim regularization with retrospective effect. Further, since appellants continued in service not by order of Court or Tribunal but by the decision of the respondents, and were regularized as per policy decision taken by respondent no.1, and approved by State Govt., HC was not right in quashing the appointment of the appellants as the same were never in question before HC. Hence, judgment of HC quashing appointment of appellants is set aside and plea of appellants for regularization with retrospective effect is declined. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - As per exception carved out by Constitution Bench of SC, in Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others v. Uma Devi and Others, 2006 INDLAW SC 125, UOI/State Govts./their instrumentalities should take steps to regularize the services of such irregular employees who have worked for more than ten years.
