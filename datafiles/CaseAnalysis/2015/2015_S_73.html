State of Uttar Pradesh v Satveer and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 519; (2015) 9 SCC 44; AIR 2015 SC 2406; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3281; 2015 (1) JCC 2517; JT 2015 (6) SC 125; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 341; 2015(7) SCALE 340; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Alleged Murder - Acquittal - Justification - PW1 complained that respondent accused took his nephew in their house and committed murder of victim deceased - FIR was lodged against respondent - Trial Court convicted respondents u/s. 302 read with s. 34 of IPC - Further, Trial Court found case to be rarest of rare warranting extreme punishment of death penalty, hence, imposed death penalty on respondents subject to confirmation by HC - However, reversed order passed by Trial Court and acquitted respondents - Whether acquittal order passed by HC in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, apart from testimony of PW2 eye witness nothing has been placed on record by prosecution which could lend corroboration to his own presence and content of his version. Further, no reason has been given why PW2 and his friend were sitting on bench outside clinic of doctor. Neither doctor nor his friend were examined. Further, place from where he allegedly witnessed occurrence is not natural place where either witness resides or carries on any vocation and reason for his being there is not placed on record. Further, none from house of deceased was examined nor did PW1 throw any light as to when deceased left house and in whose company was he playing. Neither has prosecution given names of those children nor has anybody else been examined to say that he had seen children playing at place in question. There is nothing on record which could corroborate that deceased was actually present with other children. Further, none of villagers were examined. As regards version of PW2 about incident, manner in which it statedly occurred, involvement of respondents whether all or some of them, there is nothing on record which could possibly allow to test veracity of version of sole witness. Further, no material evidence is available to corroborate with version of PW2. Further, no blood stained clothes were recovered from any of respondents though they were supposed to be authors of crime. Hence, SC held that material on record falls short and respondents are entitled to benefit of doubt. Impugned order passed by HC is dismissed. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In case of conviction in criminal cases, it is necessary that prosecution should prove guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
