Pradip Nanjee Gala v Sales Tax Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 302; JT 2015 (4) SC 502; 2015 (4) MLJ 605; 2015(5) SCALE 695; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 141; [2015] 80 VST 375

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 45 - Bombay Sales Tax Rules, 1959 - Remission of tax - Appellant was partner in assessee-Firm and assessing authority carried out assessments and confirmed demand under the Act and appeals preferred by appellant against said orders were dismissed by First Appellate Authority - Aggrieved, appellant approached Tribunal and contending that State Minister for Finance accepted offer of settlement and in view of same, Commissioner of Sales Tax issued letter quantifying amount due and payable by assessee-Firm for relevant assessment years on basis of partnership deed - However, revenue denied existence of such settlement and Tribunal refused to adjudicate upon settlement issue and if appellant was relieved of his obligation in view of said settlement - Aggrieved, appellant approached HC vide writ petition but the same was dismissed holding that appellant was liable for payment of tax under the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Revenue could resile from settlement entered into with the assessee on basis of which appellant already paid and settled dues under the Act.

Held, there is no provision under the Act empowering the State Govt. or the Commissioner to enter into a settlement with an individual partner regarding his liability in respect of the dues payable by the assessee-Firm. Further, the Rules relevant to exercise of power of remission by the Commissioner under the Act also does not provide any condition with respect to remission of sales tax under the Act by entering into any settlement, more so a settlement for the payment of individual liability of partners under the partnership deed. Hence, in the absence of any specific provision contained in the Act or the Rules, there could be no settlement with an individual partner so as to discharge him from his obligation to pay the sales tax dues payable by the assessee-Firm. The settlement, if any, reached between the appellant and the State Govt. for part payment of tax liability by the partner of an assessee-Firm would not fall under the four corners of the Act or the Rules as claimed by appellant. Hence, judgment and order of HC is confirmed and appeal is dismissed with costs. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per s. 45 of the Act, Commissioner alone is vested with the power of remission of tax which could be exercised only in accordance with such circumstances and conditions as prescribed by State Govt. under the Rules.
