Nawal Kishore Mishra and others v High Court of Judicature at Allahabad Through its Registrar General and others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 110; (2015) 5 SCC 479; AIR 2015 SC 1332; JT 2015 (2) SC 576; 2015 (3) MLJ 231; 2015(2) SCALE 506; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 107; 2015 (3) SLR 481

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, Bihar Judicial Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1955, Bihar Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services (For Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1991, Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1995

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975, r. 8 - Direct recruitment from In service candidates - Locus Standi - HC invited applications for direct recruitment for filling up vacancies in Higher Judicial Services - However, appointment were made in unfilled reserve vacancies by way of promotion from 'in service candidates' by application of r. 8(2) of the Rules - Appellants, who were unsuccessful candidates, filed writ petitions before HC challenging appointment from 'in service candidates', which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants had locus standi to challenge appointments made.

Held, since larger issue as to entitlement of appellants as successful candidates belonging to 'direct recruit' source to seek appointment to unfilled posts of that very source i.e., direct recruit, though belonging to reserved category, merits consideration, appellants challenge to filling up posts as made by HC by adopting the procedure prescribed u/r. 8(2) of the Rules could be validly raised as point of challenge. Hence, appellants had every locus to challenge the appointment made by HC by invoking r. 8(2) of the Rules. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, s. 3(1) - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975, r. 7 - Rule of reservation - Whether HC could have validly adopted the Act by relying upon r. 7 of the Rules.

Held, after the emergence of the Act, the application of r. 7 of the Rules could be only by way of adopting the statutory prescription contained in s. 3(1) of the Act. Hence, HC was well justified in applying the extent of reservation prescribed in the Act by invoking existing r. 7 of the Rules. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Advocates & Judges - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, s. 3(1) - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975, rr. 7 and 8- Adoption of rule of reservation - Whether HC adopted the Act, or any other order of Govt. providing for reservation validly.

Held, in view of Resolution passed by Selection and Appointment Committee constituted by HC, and since proceedings of Full Court pursuant to direction of Chief Justice dt.31-3-2009, approved said resolution Selection and Appointment Committee, as per Rules of the Courts, reading of said resolution of Selection and appointment Committee and resolution of Full court together would constitute a valid adoption as contemplated u/r. 7 of the Rules. Hence, HC adopted rule of reservation as per the Act which was well within the prescription contained in r. 7 of the Rules. Further, course adopted by HC is also in consonance with the various principles laid down by Constitution Bench in State of Bihar v. BAL Mukund Sah 2000 INDLAW SC 2704. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Advocates & Judges - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, s. 3(1) - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975, r. 8(2) - Direct recruitment - Vacancy in reserved category - Whether HC is justified in filling up unfilled posts by way of promotion of 'in service candidates' instead of considering successful candidates.

Held, when the posts were reserved for SC, ST, then filling up of those posts from the general category candidates would seriously affect the rule of reservation, as once the posts of direct recruit are filled up from other category candidates even the carrying forward of those vacancies as provided u/r. 8(2) proviso, could not be operated upon. On contrary, by applying r. 8(2) in the event of vacancies remaining due to non-available of the candidates of the reserved category and such vacancies were filled up by the 'in service candidates' by resorting to promotion, the proviso could be conveniently operated upon by carrying forward those vacancies in the future years in the direct recruit source and by maintaining rule of reservation to the extent it could not be filled up in the relevant recruitment years. Hence, HC by adopting the Act, adopted rule of reservation to the full extent provided for and as prescribed u/s. 3(1) of the Act and that in respect of any unfilled vacancies of that category, HC rightly resorted to prescription contained in r. 8(2) by resorting to filling up of such vacancies by special recruitment in that year as directed by instant Court and in the absence of not getting such vacancies filled up, then by resorting to such filling up by promotion of 'in service candidates' and also by applying the r. 8(2) proviso and thereby carry forward those vacancies in future years of recruitment. Hence, action of HC is justified. However, as r. 8(2) proviso specifically mandates that while fixing number of vacancies to be allotted to the quota of direct recruitment at the next recruitment, it should be raised accordingly. Hence, without disturbing promotions already made by HC, directions are made to HC to provide number of vacancies which remained unfilled in year 2009 in reserved category of direct recruit source by adding that number of vacancies in the recruitment to be made in the future years until such number of vacancies of unfilled reserved category pertaining to year 2009 are filled and appeals are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
