Mohd. Akbar v Ashok Sahu and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 150; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 44; 2015 (110) ALR 220; 2015 (3) AWC 2671; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 54; JT 2015 (3) SC 311; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 222; 2015(3) SCALE 107; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 26

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 86(7) - Disposal of election petition - Appellant filed election petition challenging election of respondent no. 1 on various grounds including commission of certain corrupt practices - Instant appeals were filed complaining that HC had not disposed of election petition as mandated by s. 86(7) of the Act.

Held, even after 15 months after election, trial of instant election petition has not yet commenced. Hence, CJ is requested to take necessary steps for disposal of election petition. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s. 86(7) of the Act, election petition should be tried and concluded within six months.
