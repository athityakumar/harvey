C. Sukumaran v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 59; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1200; 2015 CRLJ 1715; 2015 (2) JCC 1322; JT 2015 (1) SC 487; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1407; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 159; 2015(1) SCALE 751; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 152

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 and 13 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Challenged - Special Judge held the appellant guilty of the offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - On appeal, HC held that the conviction of the appellant u/s. 7 of the Act was not warranted as the essential element of demand of illegal gratification by the appellant, from the complainant, was not proved - However, the HC held that there was a strong evidence against the appellant u/s. 13(1)(d) of the Act to show his culpability - Hence, the appeal of the appellant was partly allowed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it has been rightly held by the HC, there is no demand for the illegal gratification on the part of the appellant u/s. 7 of the Act. Therefore, the question of acceptance of illegal gratification from the complainant under the provision of s. 13(1)(d) of the Act also does not arise. However, HC erroneously affirmed the conviction for the alleged offence u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act, although as per law, demand by the appellant u/s. 7 of the Act, should have been proved to sustain the charge u/s. 13(1)(d) of the Act. Further, none of the prosecution witnesses have deposed in the case that the appellant was the person who had demanded and accepted the bribe from the complainant and since PW2 has materially turned hostile, therefore, neither the demand aspect nor the acceptance of the bribe money can be verified from any other witnesses of the prosecution. The prosecution has failed to satisfy beyond all reasonable doubt that the charge levelled against the appellant is proved. Since, the charge against the appellant is not proved, the conviction and sentence imposed upon the appellant by the HC u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act is set aside. Appeal allowed.
