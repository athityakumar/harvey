Sakharam v State of Madhya Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 573; (2015) 10 SCC 557; AIR 2015 SC 3139; 2015 CRLJ 4369; 2015 (3) JabLJ 197; 2015 (1) JCC 2689; JT 2015 (8) SC 115B; 2016 (1) RLW 369; 2015(9) SCALE 41B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 320, 325, 307 - Conviction - Grievous hurt - Complaint was lodged against accused persons for assaulting victims and upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court held that prosecution has established guilt of accused persons beyond reasonable doubt and convicted accused persons and passed sentence - Aggrieved, accused-Tularam and appellant preferred appeals before HC and vide impugned judgment, HC partly allowed appeal of appellant setting aside his conviction u/s. 302 of IPC and modified his conviction u/s. 307 of IPC as conviction u/s. 325 of IPC - However, conviction of accused-Tularam u/s. 302 of IPC was confirmed - Hence instant appeal by appellant assailing conviction u/s. 325 of IPC and sentence of imprisonment - Whether conviction of appellant u/s. 325 of IPC could be upheld.

Held, PW2 sustained fracture or dislocation of the bone which clearly falls in the category of grievous hurt as expressly mentioned in s.320(7) of IPC. Even though PW15-Doctor was not questioned about the nature of injuries, fracture of the frontal bone would bring the offence within the definition of 'grievous hurt'. Hence, heaving regard to the nature of injuries and the X-ray report, HC rightly convicted appellant u/s. 325 of IPC and same could not be modified. However, as the occurrence was sudden fight and in a fit of passion the appellant inflicted injuries on PW2, sentence of imprisonment of seven years imposed is excessive and the same is reduced to three years. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - To make out the offence of voluntarily causing grievous hurt, there should be specific hurt voluntarily inflicted and coming within the eight kinds of hurt enumerated in s. 320 of IPC.
