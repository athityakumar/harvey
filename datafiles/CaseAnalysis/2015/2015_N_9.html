Nagaraja Rao v Central Bureau of Investigation Respondent

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 419; (2015) 4 SCC 302; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2039; 2015 CRLJ 1458; 2015 (2) JCC 940; JT 2015 (1) SC 322; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 915; 2015(1) SCALE 475; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 424

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Indian Post Office Act, 1898, Gold (Control) Act, Two Offences

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 71, 381, 419 - Indian Post Office Act, 1898, s. 52 - Sentences to run concurrently - Appellant was accused of committing theft of registered insured parcel containing gold jewellery while on duty in post office and case was registered against appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 381 and 419 of IPC read with s. 52 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted appellant for charge u/s. 419 of IPC but convicted for offence u/s. 381 of IPC and s. 52 of the Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal which was dismissed confirming conviction and sentences awarded and revision before HC was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal challenging sentences awarded to appellant - Whether sentences awarded under IPC and the Act were to run 'concurrently'.

Held, sentences awarded to appellant could be directed to run 'concurrently' because, firstly, the case out of which appeal arose related to year 1993 and pending for long period of 21 years; secondly, two sentences which were imposed on appellant, arose out of one offence of theft punishable u/s. 381 of IPC tried in one trial; thirdly, provisions of s. 52 of the Act were required to be invoked against appellant because he was the postal employee; fourthly, the gold jewellery that was stolen was long recovered and also handed over to person concerned; fifthly, appellant was already dismissed from services due to impugned conviction and lastly, appellant was suffering from heart ailment since long. Hence, both sentences awarded to appellant were to run 'concurrently'. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - When an accused is convicted in one case under different counts of offences and sentenced to different terms of imprisonment under each such count, all such sentences were to run concurrently.
