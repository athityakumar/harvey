Working Friends Cooperative House, Building Society Limited v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 706; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 46; 2015 (113) ALR 678; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 138; JT 2015 (9) SC 357; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3451; 2016 (130) RD 419; 2015(10) SCALE 632; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 1096

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Compulsory Acquisition, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement (Amendment) Ordinance, 2014

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement (Amendment) Ordinance, 2014 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(2) - Acquisition of Land - Failure to pay compensation - Acquisition lapsed - Notification was issued by State Govt. u/s. 4 of 1894 Act proposing to acquire land in question which was followed by notification u/s. 6 of 1894 Act - Further, due to compulsory acquisition of land, compensation was awarded to appellant, however, compensation was deposited in Treasury - Further, compensation was enhanced and was deposited in Reference Court - Further, HC dismissed petition filed by appellant which challenged land acquisition notifications - Appellant challenged order of HC before SC and during pendency of appeal, Parliament enacted 2013 Act which came into force w.e.f. 1-1-2014 and as result appellant moved direction stating that acquisition proceedings by which appellant's land was acquired had lapsed as compensation was not paid to appellant - Whether compulsory acquisition of appellant's land under 1894 Act lapses in view of provisions of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act.

Held, appellant had accrued right which must be recognized by s. 24(2) of 2013 Act. Ordinance which purported to take away such accrued right would have to be treated as prospective unless legislative intent was clearly to give it retrospective effect. Further, SC held that initial compensation was neither paid to appellant nor was it deposited in Reference Court and it was admittedly deposited in Govt. Treasury of State. Further, deposit made only after Act came into force and was with view to get over provisions of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act. Further, even deposit of compensation amount in Reference Court on 26-6-2014 does not come to aid of appellant under any circumstances and cannot be taken as 'deemed payment'. Hence, SC held that acquisition proceedings in so far as appellant is concerned lapsed with enactment of 2014 Act. Impugned judgment and order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is justified to quash acquisition proceedings when compensation is not paid to land owner or deposited in Reference Court u/s. 24 of 2013 Act.
