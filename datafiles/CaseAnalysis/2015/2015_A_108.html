Sunil Haribhau Kale v Avinash Gulabrao Mardikar and others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 120; 2015(2) ALL MR 926; JT 2015 (2) SC 453; 2015 (2) MLJ 501; 2015(2) SCALE 610; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Maharashtra Local Authority Members' Disqualification Act, 1986, Maharashtra Local Authority Members Disqualification Rules, 1987

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Local Authority Members' Disqualification Act, 1986, s. 2(1) - Maharashtra Local Authority Members Disqualification Rules, 1987 - Political alliance - Change of group leader - Respondent No. 1 was elected as group leader for a political alliance (group) - Divisional Commissioner (DC) approved alliance and group leader - However, one constituent in alliance sought to appoint appellant as new group leader in the place of respondent No. 1 - DC approved appellant's appointment - Respondent No. 1 challenged DC order before HC - HC allowed respondent No.1's writ petition and quashed appellant's appointment - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, once a group is formed and duly recognized by DC, it becomes a municipal party in terms of s. 2(i) of the Act. The group leader of the group can be changed only by the group and not by one of the political parties, big or small, belonging to the group. Once the birth of a leader in a group is by way of election by the group, the group leader thus elected cannot be replaced otherwise than through the very same process of the election in the group, in the absence of any rules to the contra. The constituent in question has 17 members in the group. That does not mean that the said party can impose a group leader in the group. Imposition of a group leader otherwise than by the democratic process cuts at the roots of the democracy and certainly it is in violation of the Rules. It is always open to the original political parties to have their respective leaders in the group. However, as far as group leader is concerned, he has to be elected by the group. Thus, view taken by the HC was agreed with. Appeal dismissed.
