State, represented by Inspector of Police, 'Q' Branch C.I.D., Tirunelveli Range, Tamil Nadu v Mariya Anton Vijay and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 470; (2015) 9 SCC 294; 2015 (3) JCC 2091; JT 2015 (6) SC 180; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 461; 2015(7) SCALE 460; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vessel, Piracy, Revolver, Territorial Waters, Continental Shelf, Exclusive Economic Zone and Other Maritime Zones Act, 1976, Arms Rules, 1962, Motor Spirit and High Speed Diesel (Regulation of Supply, Distribution and Prevention of Malpractices) Order 1990, Merchant Shipping Rules

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959. ss. 25(1B)(a), 25(1B)(f) - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 3(2)(d), 3(b), 7(1)(a)(ii) - Motor Spirit and High Speed Diesel (Regulation of Supply, Distribution and Prevention of Malpractices) Order, 1990 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397, 482 - Vessel containing ammunition without license - Order of acquittal - Justification - Coast Guard received information that one vessel which was located within territorial seawaters of India and unidentified boat was suspected to have sailed to Vessel for doing some kind of illegal activities on vessel with connivance of crew members on vessel - Further, after inspection, crew members admitted that they do possess and were carrying with them arms and ammunitions on vessel - Authority found that vessel was carrying huge quantity of arms/ammunition without any valid authorization and documentation by crew members - FIR was registered u/s. 25 (1B) (a) and (f) of 1959 Act read with ss. 3(b) and 7 (1) (a) (ii) of 1955 Act read with s. 2(m) (5) of Order - However, HC quashed charge sheet filed against all accused persons insofar as it related to offences punishable under 1959 Act - However, HC upheld filing of charge sheet against A-3 and A-38 for their prosecution in relation to offences punishable for violating Control Order, 2005 punishable u/s. 3 (ii) (d) read with s. 7 (1)(a)(ii) 1955 Act holding that prima facie case against these accused for commission of offences under 1955 Act is made out - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, it is apparent from mere reading of impugned order that entire approach of HC while deciding petition was illegal and erroneous. Further, instead of considering prima facie case, HC appreciated and weighed materials on record for coming to conclusion that charge sheet against respondents could not have been filed and if filed no charges could have been framed against respondents on basis of such charge sheet, for facing trial. Further, HC while deciding matters virtually acted as appellate Court as if he was hearing appeals arising out of final order and proceeded to examine each and every issue mentioned in charge sheet by appreciating material on record and applying relevant provisions of various Acts, Rules and international treaties governing controversy. SC held that this approach of HC while hearing case u/s. 482 of CrPC was wholly unwarranted, illegal, hence, cannot be upheld. Further, reasons given by HC were not reasons which could be made basis to invoke inherent jurisdiction of HC. Further, very fact that huge quantity of arms and ammunition were recovered from possession and control of crew members from vessel and further during investigation, crew members were unable to satisfy their legal possession over such arms/ammunition with them by not being able to produce any evidence such as licenses, certificates etc. it was sufficient to attract provisions of 1959 Act for initiating prosecution of accused for commission of offences punishable under 1959 Act. Further, some material questions were framed which had bearing over issues involved in case, however, none if questions were capable of being answered without aid of evidence to be adduced by parties, by mere reading of FIR, Final report, charge sheet, for first time by HC in exercise of its inherent jurisdiction. Further, HC held that this was not case falling in category of rare case requiring interference of HC by invoking powers u/ss. 397 or/and s. 482 of CrPC. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and Trial Court is directed to proceed with case and decide same on merits in accordance with law. Appeals allowed
