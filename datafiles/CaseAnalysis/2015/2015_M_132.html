Kandivali Cooperative Industrial Estate and others v Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 2015 SC 1434; 2015(3) ALL MR 481; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 676; JT 2015 (2) SC 126; 2015 (2) MLJ 365; 2015(2) SCALE 55; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 989

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Trade

Summary: Trade - Municipalities & Local Governments - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 - Increase of Trade Refuse Charges (TRC) - TRC were revised by the Respondent vide a circular dt. 14-1-2008 by almost 300% of the trade licence fees - Appellants and several other parties made representations and preferred writ petitions urging reconsideration of the rates - HC dismissed the writ petitions preferred by the appellants challenging the Circular and the respective entries made in the schedule appended thereto issued by the Respondent - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, increase of trade refuse charge by 10 per cent every year irrespective of the actual escalation or reduction in costs involved or the nature of business carried on by the Licensee etc. violates principles of reasonableness as well as natural justice. Accordingly, respondent was directed not to recover increased trade refuse charge at the rate of 10 per cent w.e.f. 2009. The actual increase can be ascertained and realized in future but not without giving reasonable opportunity of hearing to the licensee or the persons liable to pay the said increased charges. Appeals disposed of.
