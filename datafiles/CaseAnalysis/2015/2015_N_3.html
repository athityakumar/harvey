P. R. Yelumalai v N. M. Ravi

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 393; (2015) 9 SCC 52; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 2015 (110) ALR 503; 2016 (115) ALR 380; 2015 (3) AWC 2686; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 319; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 132; 2015 (2) CTC 559; JT 2015 (3) SC 382; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2155; 2016 (2) MahLJ 483; 2015 (3) MLJ 321; 2016 (2) MPLJ 85; 2015 (1) OLR 1051; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 585; 2015 (128) RD 403; 2016 (130) RD 180; 2015(4) SCALE 322; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 163; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 6

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Civil Courts Act, 1964

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Failure to grant extension of time to pay consideration - Remand of case to lower court - Justification - Sale deed was executed between appellant buyer and respondent seller for which part payment was made - Suit for specific performance was issued in favor of appellant and time was specified within which amount of consideration was to be paid to respondent - However, appellant buyer failed to deposit amount within specified period and extended time which was granted by Trial Court - Further, appellant filed Memo for issue of Receipt Order for depositing said amount, however, respondent seller was not served with copy of Memo and was not notified with regard to alleged deposit - Further, respondent seller sold property in question to other buyer under registered sale deed - Execution petition filed by appellant buyer was dismissed which is affirmed by HC - Further, application filed by appellant for extension of time was rejected by lower court - Further, matter was remanded by HC to Trial Court formulating questions for disposal afresh in accordance with law - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Trial Court has discussed full merits of application and given finding that there is no evidence to show that plaintiff had made any effort to deposit amount at end of specified date. Further, SC held that application was dismissed on its merits and not merely on technical grounds. Further, appellant buyer had even failed to make deposit through mode of payment as required by decree. SC since appellant buyer failed to comply with terms of decree, suit stood dismissed as order passing decree was peremptory order. In present case, there is rather reverse situation wherein Trial Court has dismissed application for extension of time giving due reasons. Hence, appellant buyer has clearly defaulted on time of depositing as well as mode of payment. Decree was self-operative and suit stood dismissed for non-compliance of decree. Further, appellant buyer also failed to make out case for condonation of delay. Hence, questions formulated by HC in order of remand are not required to be answered by Trial Court. Consequently, appeal filed by appellant Buyer is dismissed and appeal filed by respondent seller is allowed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - It is not required to remand case to lower Court when case is fully discussed by lower courts on merits.
