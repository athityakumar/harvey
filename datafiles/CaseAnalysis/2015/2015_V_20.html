Vennangot Anuradha Samir v Vennangot Mohandas Samir

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 763; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 51; 2016 (114) ALR 706; 2016 (1) AWC 506; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 250; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 61; 2016 (1) CTC 249; 2016 (4) G.L.R. 3329; 2016 (1) JCC 10; JT 2015 (12) SC 97B; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 5; 2015 (8) MLJ 760; 2016 (1) OLR 457; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 316; 2015(13) SCALE 140; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 276; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 205

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(1a), 13B - Wife suffering from disease - Valid Consent - Divorce on mutual consent - Respondent-husband filed suit for dissolution of marriage by decree of divorce u/s. 13(1)(1a) of Act on ground that petitioner wife after solemnization of marriage had committed various acts of cruelty - Further, in settlement agreement, respondent husband agreed to pay money towards full and final settlement as alimony, maintenance for past and future or any other claim of petitioner and such amount would be paid to petitioner at time of passing of decree of divorce by mutual consent - Further, application was filed with prayer to treat divorce petition pending before Family Court as application u/s. 13B of Act and treat present application as second motion and grant divorce by way of mutual consent - Further, in application it was mentioned that petitioner wife is suffering life threatening disease i.e. cancer and urgently requires funds for her medical treatment and also that she has to depend on herself for proper care - Whether Court would be justified in granting decree for divorce on basis of settlement when wife is suffering with cancer and is in need of money for her treatment and can that be consideration for dissolution of marriage.

Held, from records it is evident that petitioner needs sufficient amount of money for treatment of cancer. Hence, it cannot be ruled out that in order to save her life by getting money, she agreed for settlement of dissolution of marriage. Further, SC held that it cannot be disputed that after marriage law enjoins corresponding duty on husband to look after her comforts and not only to provide her food and clothes but to protect her from all calamities and to take care of her health and safety. Further, from perusal of facts of case and development which has taken place in present case, it seems that petitioner wife agreed for divorce by mutual consent on condition that respondent husband will pay her Rs.12,50,000/- as full and final settlement. SC held that petitioner wife is suffering from such disease which has compelled her to agree for mutual consent divorce. SC held that fact that petitioner wife is ready for mutual consent divorce after knowing about her medical condition raises suspicion as to whether consent obtained from petitioner wife is free as required by law for granting decree of divorce by mutual consent. SC held that it is duty of respondent husband to take care of health and safety of petitioner wife. SC held that in present case, by settlement agreement respondent husband is promising to do something which he is already duty bound, is not valid consideration for settlement. SC directed respondent husband shall pay sum of Rs. Five Lacs (Rs.5,00,000/-) out of Rs.12,50,000/- to petitioner wife immediately for her treatment and meeting other medical expenses. Further, after petitioner is fully cured from disease or within six months whichever is earlier, Family Court where divorce petition is ordered to be transferred, shall take up case along with fresh application that may be filed by parties u/s. 13B for divorce by mutual consent. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - In case of divorce on mutual consent, it is necessary that consent of both parties is free as required under law.
