Ch. Narasimha Rao and others v Land Acquisition Officer, Eluru and others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 961; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 139; 2016 (114) ALR 714; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 796; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 74; JT 2015 (12) SC 70; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 953; 2016 (131) RD 150; 2015(13) SCALE 406

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Requirement of, Denial of reference application

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 18(1) - Denial of reference applications - Requirement of - Appellant filed instant appeal against the order of HC, who rejected the reference application on the ground that the reference applications u/s. 18(1) of the Act had not been filed within the time prescribed under the said section - Whether Reference Applications filed by appellants u/s. 18 of the Act was beyond statutory period.

Held, acquisition proceedings had been finally confirmed by the judgment delivered by SC on 15-9-2011. Till the said judgment was delivered by SC, there was no acquisition of the land and therefore, there was no need for the appellants to file any application u/s. 18(1) of the Act. Upon knowing the fact that the lands had been acquired by virtue of the judgment dt. 15-9-2011 delivered by SC, the appellants filed the applications u/s. 18(1) of the Act on 17-10-2011. In the aforestated circumstances, applications filed u/s. 18(1) of the Act should not have been rejected as they were filed immediately after pronouncement of the judgment by SC. Thus, the judgment delivered by HC was set aside and direct that without going into the issue of delay or limitation, the applications filed by the appellants u/s. 18(1) of the Act shall be entertained in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.
