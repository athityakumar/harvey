Dr. (Mrs.) Vijaya Manohar Arbat v Kashi Rao Rajaram Sawai And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28592; (1987) 2 SCC 278; (1987) SCC (Cr) 354; AIR 1987 SC 1100; 1987 BBCJ 138; 1987 (1) Bom.C.R. 629; 1987 (1) Crimes 713; 1987 CRLJ 977; 1987 CRLR 281; 1987 (11) ECC 416; JT 1987 (3) SC 46; 1987(1) SCALE 379; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 331; 1988 (1) ShimLC 133

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 125(1)(d) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 8 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 13(1) - Whether married daughter is liable to maintain her father under s. 125(1)(d, CrPC? - Held, married daughter is liable to maintain her parent - Word 'his' does not exclude the parents claiming maintenance from their daughter - Purpose of such enactment is to enforce social obligation and there is no reason why daughters should be excluded from such obligation to maintain their parents - Appeal dismissed.
