State Of Uttar Pradesh And Ors. v Kasturi Lal Har Lal

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28739; (1988) Supp SCC 302; JT 1987 (3) SC 234; 1987(2) SCALE 191; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 756; 1987 (67) STC 154

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Registered Dealer, Sales Tax Law, Collected, Subsequent Sale, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Sales Tax - Interstate sale - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 2(a), 2(b), 3(b), 6, 8, 7, 9(1) and 10 - Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Whether respondent liable to State of UP for payment of Central Sales Tax in respect of transactions of sale of coal? - S. 6(2) stipulates that no such subsequent sale shall be exempt from tax under this sub-section unless dealer effecting sale furnishes to prescribed authority in prescribed manner and within prescribed time, a certificate as mentioned therein - In this case, subsequent sale, if there be any, in State of UP did not occasion movement of goods; it is therefore, not subject to inter-state sales tax - Held, Sales Tax Officer in UP was not appropriate authority either to impose or collect duty on inter- Appeal dismissed.
