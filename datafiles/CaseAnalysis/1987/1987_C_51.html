Prahlad Singh v Col. Sukhdev Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28065; (1987) 1 SCC 727; AIR 1987 SC 1145; 1987 (1) CCC 813; 1987 (29) E.L.T. 490; JT 1987 (1) SC 541; 1987(1) SCALE 423; 1987 (1) UJ 406

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, s.13 - CPC, 1908, s.11 - Res judicata - Setting aside of ex-parte decree in eviction petition on the finding that landlord had agreed to withdraw petition - He could have questioned the finding before the appellate authority and the High Court in the appeals preferred by the tenant but he did not choose to do so - Continuance of eviction petition thereafter by disregarding the finding recorded in proceedings for setting aside of ex-parte decree - Legality - Held, is illegal - He could not do so as he had earlier thought it prudent not to enter the witness box though he put the question in issue in the proceeding to set aside the ex-parte order by contesting the statement of the tenant - Appeal allowed.
