Hurmat Bibi and Others v Prodosh Kumar Bajpayee and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28181; (1988) Supp SCC 507

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Partnership Act 1932

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - Partition Act, s.4 - Right of pre-emption of stranger purchaser - Stranger purchasers, respondents in this appeal, filed a suit in Court of Civil Judge, Banaras for partition of property namely premises called 'Rock House' at Darjeeling and a plot land in city of Banaras - At time of preliminary decree Mohd Hanif and Mohd Amin who were defendants 2 and 4 in partition suit made an application u/s. 4 of Partition Act to buy up shares of plaintiffs in Rock House - Their prayer was allowed by Court and Court valued plaintiffs' share in that house would be sold to Mohd Hanif and Mohd Amin provided they deposited aforesaid amount in Court within three months - Subsequently final decree was passed in suit on basis of report and map preferred by Commr. appointed in case - This decree was transferred to Darjeeling court by Banaras court for division and delivery of possession of Rock House, to respective shareholders according to final decree - When matter was thus pending, LRs of original defendants made an application u/s. 4 of Partition Act for buying up shares of plaintiffs in Rock House - This application was allowed by Subordinate Judge, Darjeeling - Further, a civil revision petition was filed - Whether a party in whose favour an order for pre-emption u/s. 4 had been passed and who had failed to avail himself of opportunity that was given to him, can still maintain a fresh application for same purpose u/s. 4 of Act? - On a plain reading of provision of s. 4 it is clear and evident that when an application is made by co-sharer for buying up share of stranger purchaser in a dwelling house belonging to undivided family, court shall make valuation of such share in manner it thinks just and proper and direct sale of such share to shareholder and may give all necessary directions in that behalf - Object of s. 4 is to enable members of a family to buy out a stranger transferee from one of member, who seeks partition of dwelling house - S. 4 was enacted having in mind consideration of corporate property existing as rule with special reference to joint property and social desire to preserve unity of such property - It was also enacted with a view to maintain indivisibility and integrity of property - Provisions of s. 4 do not create any bar for making a second application to buy shares of a stranger purchaser provided at time of making such application property has not been divided by metes and bounds and possession of share of stranger purchaser has not been delivered to him - Appeal allowed.
