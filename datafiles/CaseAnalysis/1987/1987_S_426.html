P.K. Dixit And Ors. v Statf Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28768; (1987) 4 SCC 621; (1988) SCC (L&S) 11; AIR 1988 SC 260; 1987 (5) ATC 123; 1987 (55) FLR 833; JT 1987 (4) SC 55; 1988 LabIC 371; 1988 (1) LLN 33; 1987(2) SCALE 706; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 398; 1987 (5) SLR 695

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Service - Constitution, arts. 233 and 309 - U.P. Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, rr. 8 Proviso, 22, 23 and 26 Proviso - Writ petitions challenging inter se seniority of the officers in the higher judicial service coming from two sources; (A) promoted from the judicial service and (B) others who are directly recruited from the Bar - Fixation of seniority among: Civil and Sessions Judges who were working on 8-5-1974 and secondly among promotes with three years' period of continuous officiating - Held, seniority has to be reckoned from date on which vacancy was available for their confirmation - This principle applies only to promotions made after rules came into force and not to promotions made before coming into force of Rules - Petitions partly allowed.
