Kansa Behera v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28085; (1987) 3 SCC 480; (1987) SCC (Cr) 601; AIR 1987 SC 1507; 1987 CRLJ 1857; JT 1987 (2) SC 193B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - First information report registered against unknown assailant - Circumstantial evidence that each one of circumstances had to be established beyond doubt and all circumstances put together must lead to only one inference of guilt of accused - Held, only case against appellant was that he was seen with deceased on evening before day of incident - On that circumstance alone inference of guilt could not be drawn especially in circumstances of case where, one another accused person was from whom an instrument of offence was recovered and who had a grudge against deceased had been let off - Courts below wrong in convicting appellant on these facts - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
