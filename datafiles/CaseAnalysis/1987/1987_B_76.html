Ram Sarup Gupta (Dead) By Lrs. v Bishun Narain Inter College And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28351; (1987) 2 SCC 555; AIR 1987 SC 1242; JT 1987 (2) SC 76; 1987(1) SCALE 700; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 805; 1987 (2) UJ 162

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Indian Easements Act, 1882 - CPC, 1908 - Easements Act, 1882, s.60(b) - (A) Whether the respondents in their written statement have raised the necessary pleading that the licence was irrevocable as contemplated by s.60(b) of the 1882 Act and, if so, is there any evidence on record to support that plea - Settled law is that no party should be permitted to travel beyond its pleading - Supreme Court held, that once it is found that in spite of deficiency in the pleadings parties knew the case and they proceeded to trial on those issues by producing evidence, in that event it would not be open to a party to raise the question of absence of pleadings in appeal - (B) Revocation of irrevocable licence of premises and land attached thereto for running school - When school made constructions on land - But licencee sell of land to discharge debts by revoking licence - Permissibility - Held, it is not desirable to place undue emphasis on form rather than substance of pleadings should be considered - Held, the licence being irrevocable, the licenser or the transferee from him could not revoke it or evict school - Appeal dismissed.
