Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation v Ramanbhai Prabhatbhai & Another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28458; (1987) 3 SCC 234; (1987) SCC (Cr) 482; 1987 (2) ACJ 561; AIR 1987 SC 1690; 1987 (2) CCC 194; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 609; 1987 CrLR(SC) 510; JT 1987 (2) SC 384; 1987(1) SCALE 1027; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 404

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: English Fatal Accidents Act, 1846 (ENG)

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 92, 110A Proviso and 110B - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - English Fatal Accidents Act, 1846 (ENG) - Accident - Death - (A) Determination of effect of provision in s. 92-A enabling claimant to recover prescribed sum without proof of negligence of driver or owner - (B) Whether LR applying for compensation for death must be the persons contemplated by s.1-A of Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - (C) Whether a brother of a person who is killed in a motor vehicle accident can claim compensation in a proceeding instituted before a Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal - Held, it modifies substantive law to that extent and makes departure from common law principal requiring claimant to prove negligence on part of driver or owner - Need not be - Can maintain it if he is a legal representative of the deceased - Petition dismissed.
