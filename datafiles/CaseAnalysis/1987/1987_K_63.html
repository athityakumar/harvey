Skandia Insurance Co. Ltd v Kokilaben Chandravadan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28829; (1987) 2 SCC 654; 1987 ACC 413; 1987 (1) ACJ 411; AIR 1987 SC 1184; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 138; 1987 CRLR 400; JT 1987 (2) SC 43; 1987(1) SCALE 648; 1987 SCJ 411; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 84, 94 and 96(1)(2)(b)(ii) - Motor vehicle accident - Liability of insurer to satisfy Award when there was a breach of exclusion clause in policy - Clause puts a prohibition as to t driving by person other than one holding driving licence - Whether absolve insurer of his liability - Held, it does not absolve - He has to establish that insured himself was guilty of committing breach of promise in contract of insurance - Appeal dismissed.
