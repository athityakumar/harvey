Hari Nath and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28066; (1988) 1 SCC 14; AIR 1988 SC 345; 1988 (1) AWC 374; 1987 (3) Crimes 667; 1988 CRLJ 422; 1988 CrLR(SC) 55; JT 1987 (4) SC 316; 1987(2) SCALE 980; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 9 and 114 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154 and 386 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Test identification - Evidentiary value - When (A) One of eye-witnesses identifying accused could be expected to have known some of accused as residents of villages in close vicinity and students of same institutions, a statable probability - Effect of failure to state identity of accused in first information report - (B) Parade held after unexplained delay of 4 months - (C) Appeal against conviction by co-accused - Conviction set aside - Findings interdependent and inextricably integrated - Validity of conviction of non-appealing accused - Held, it raises reasonable doubt about complicity of accused in crime - Benefit of such delay enures to accused - Further held, conviction cannot sustained - Appeals allowed.
