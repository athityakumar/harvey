K. Madhavan and Another v Union Of India And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28845; (1987) SCC (L&S) 496; (1987) 4 SCC 566; AIR 1987 SC 2291; 1987 (5) ATC 91; 1988 (56) FLR 18; JT 1987 (4) SC 43; 1988 LabIC 26; 1987(2) SCALE 727; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 421; 1987 (5) SLR 725

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - Special Police Establishments (Executive Staff) Recruitment Rules, 1963 - CBI (Deputy Director General of Police / Deputy Director) Recruitment Rules, 1975 - Service Rules - Appointment - Seniority - (A) Appointment to - (1) Post of SP in CBI Eligibility - (2) Post of DIG - Eight years service as SP on a regular basis - 'On a regular basis', meaning of - (C) Post of SP in CBI - Quota rule - Appointment of Deputationist alleged to be to post meant for direct recruits - No evidence to substantiate allegation - (B) Seniority - Fixation - (1) Respondent, an incumbents to post of SP shown to be retrospectively appointed - Placed above regular appointees, petitioners in seniority list - No material to justify it - (2) Petitioner regularly promoted to post of Deputy Commandant in BSF - Joined CBI on deputation as SP - Preparation of seniority list - Period, to be considered - (3) Computation - Transfer of Govt. servant from one department to another Govt. department - Effect on seniority - (C) Person holding a post of Deputy Commandant in BSF at time of his deputation as SP in CBI - Legality of appointment - Held, not required to be in rank of DSP in CBI for period of 8 years including a period of two years on probation - Further held, would mean appointment to post on a regular basis in contradiction to appointment on ad hoc or stop-gap or purely temporary basis - Appointment could not be said to be illegal - (B) Further held, seniority list would be liable to be quashed - Period from date of promotion of petitioner to post of Deputy Commandant in BSF up to his joining CBI on deputation should be taken into consideration, as post of Deputy Commandant in BSF was equivalent to grade of SP in CBI - Cannot wipe out his length of service in post from which he has been transferred - (C) No illegality in appointment as post Deputy Commandant in BSF was equivalent to post of SP in CBI - Petition allowed.
