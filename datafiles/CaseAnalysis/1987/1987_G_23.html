Ghulam Abbas and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28492; (1988) Supp SCC 426; (1988) SCC (Cr) 856; 1988 CRLR 761; JT 1988 (1) SC 5B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 25 and 26 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 107, r/w ss. 110 and 144 -Justification of appropriate proceedings taken by District Magistrate u/s. 107 r/w s. 110 of CrPC, 1973 against both rival communities i.e., Sunnis and Shias communities - Held, maintenance of law and order is a function of District Magistrate and District Magistrate reasonably with a view to avoid any possible breach of peace would take necessary steps well in advance for purpose of maintaining public order which would be in larger interests of society - Exercise of fundamental rights u/arts. 25 and 26 is not an absolute right but must yield or give way to maintenance of public order as laid down by Apex Court in Gulam Abbas v State of U P ((1982) 1 SCC 711982 (1) SCC 711981 Indlaw SC 39 : 1982 SCC (Cri) 821982 SCC(Cri) 821981 Indlaw SC 39 : (1982) 1 SCR 10771982 (1) SCR 10771981 Indlaw SC 39) - Further, principles are well settled and it is but for District Magistrate to exercise his powers in consonance with provisions of s. 144 of CrPC - Petitions disposed of.
