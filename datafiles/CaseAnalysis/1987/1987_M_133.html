Dr. Dinesh Kumar and Others v Motilal Nehru Medical College, Allahabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28418; (1987) 4 SCC 459; JT 1987 (3) SC 658; 1987(2) SCALE 656; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 351; 1988 (1) UJ 238

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Fixed percentage of admission in medical faculties - Finalisation of programme relating to selection examination - (A) Structuring postgraduate courses - Held, all universities and institutions shall take timely steps to bring about such amendments as may be necessary to bring statutes, regulations, and rules obtaining in their respective institutions in accord with this direction before end of 1991 so that there may be no scope for raising of any dispute in regard to matter - Uniform pattern has to be implemented for 1993 - (B) Whether diploma being a qualification for admission in post-graduate course as prevailing in State of Tamil Nadu is necessary ? - Further held, system now prevailing in State of Tamil Nadu regarding diploma until 1992 is allowed - From 1993 there would be no necessity for diploma to be a qualification for admission to post- (C) Provision in regard to super-specialities like MD and other higher degrees - Further held, there is no necessary to make any directions in regard to admissions therefore at present and until necessity arises it is not appropriate that court should consider that question - Appeal partly allowed.
