Manmohanlal and Others v Income Tax Officer, Ward E, City Circle, Cuttack

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28872; (1987) Supp SCC 760; 1987 (2) CCC 1082; 1987 (66) CTR 58; 1988 (34) E.L.T. 419; [1987] 168 ITR 616; JT 1987 (4) SC 68; 1987(2) SCALE 661

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tax Recovery Officer

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 226(4) - Whether court, where application u/s. 226 (4) has been made, has authority to decide amount claimed by ITO is a valid debt due from assessee? - An application u/s. 226 (4) is made by ITO to a court where money is lying to credit of assessee in default - Any tax can be recovered from an assessee only when it becomes a debt due from him - Held, it is open to court where application u/s. 226 (4) is made, to get satisfied that tax is actually due from assessee and to decide objections, if any, raised by assessee and if it upholds objections it cannot permit recovery of tax claimed - Appeal disposed of.
