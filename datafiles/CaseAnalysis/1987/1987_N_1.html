G.C. Gupta & Ors. v N K. Pandey & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28880; (1988) SCC (L&S) 260; (1988) 1 SCC 316; AIR 1988 SC 654; 1987 (5) ATC 329; JT 1987 (4) SC 593; 1988 LabIC 522; 1988 (1) LLN 339; 1987(1) SCALE 1120; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 185; 1987 (3) SLJ 1; 1988 (7) SLR 706

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - United Provinces Service of Engineers (Buildings and Roads Branch) Class II Rules, 1936, rr. 5(i), 3(b), 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Inordinate delay - Seniority - Respondents were initially appointed as temporary Assistant engineers subject to final approval of Govt. by Chief Engineer - Appointments were subsequently approved by Govt. between 1948 and 1949 in accordance with the provisions of r. 5(i) of the Rules - Thereafter on 20-01-1950 Govt. in consultation with Public Service Commission confirmed provisional appointments of respondents as temporary Assistant Engineers and these respondents had also passed requisite tests held by Govt. - They were confirmed vide Gazette - Date of confirmation of all respondents was fixed as 1.4.1956 - Appellant Nos. 3 to 5, 7 were appointed between 1951 and 1952 as temporary Assistant Engineers against guaranteed posts on probation and after competition of probationary period they were confirmed in 1955 vide Gazette Notification - Seniority of appellant Nos. 1 to 4 was fixed earlier from 1.4.1955 whereas seniority of Respondent Nos. 1 to 12 was fixed below them from 1956 treating date of confirmation in service as date of their becoming member of service - Aggrieved by order of confirmation of appellant Nos. 2 to 7, respondent no. 4 made representation to Govt. for re-determination of confirmation and consequential determination of seniority of respondents vis-a-vis appellants - No steps were taken to consider representations and to re-determine date of confirmation of respondents, thus W.P. was filed - Petition was dismissed by order on sole ground that petition was highly belated and respondents were guilty of laches and delay in challenging impugned notification - Special appeal was filed, but was dismissed - It was observed that though respondents could not be permitted to question propriety of confirmation orders, they were entitled to claim seniority over appellants as per rules regulating service of engineers - Later, Govt. by their order rejected all representations against fixation of seniority as permanent Assistant Engineers - Hence W.P. was filed that claimed relief of re-determination of their seniority in accordance with rules as there had been no determination of inter se seniority of respondents and appellants according to r. 23 of the said Rules - W.P., was unsuccessful - W.P. and Special Appeal were dismissed on ground of inordinate delay and laches in moving Court for redress against order of confirmation of respondents - W.P., was unsuccessful - W.P. and Special Appeal were dismissed on ground of inordinate delay and laches in moving Court for redress against order of confirmation of respondents - W.P. was partly allowed in nature of certiorari, it quashed gradation or seniority list and further directed issuance of writ of mandamus commanding State Govt. to prepare a fresh seniority list in accordance with law in light of its observations made in judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether determination of seniority between respondents who were appointed as temporary Assistant Engineers and subsequently made permanent therein and appellants who were appointed on probation in permanent posts of Assistant Engineers and made permanent after expiry of period of probation and thereafter their provisional appointment as temporary was correct - Held, Seniority of employees in service was determined from date when an employee became member of service being appointed substantively to post in cadre of service, no matter whether said post was permanent or temporary - Seniority of appellants remained unchallenged for 14 years - Improper exercise of jurisdiction on part of the HC to have interfered with impugned seniority list after 30 years - Further, Division Bench to need not have interfered with impugned orders and given direction to State Govt. to re-determine the inter-se seniority between appellants and respondents u/r. 23 of the Rules afresh, with advertence to observations made by it - Well-established principle that there were no specific rules for determination of seniority in service, length of continuous service was yardstick for determining seniority of members of service - Date of order of appointment to the service determined seniority - Inordinate delay was not merely factor for Court to refuse appropriate relief but was relevant consideration not to unsettle settled things - However, respondent nos. 1-12 were disentitled to any relief u/art. 226 of Constitution due to inordinate delay and laches - Appellants by reason of their seniority and record of service, reached higher echelons of service - As against that, eleven of 12 respondents have since retired, thus only one of them was left - Open to State Govt. to reconsider case of remaining respondent in light of settled principles that temporary Assistant Engineers on absorption were entitled to benefit of their seniority from date from which their services were regularised i.e. date from which they became 'Members of Service' within meaning of r. 3(b) of the Rules - It was open to Govt. to grant him necessary relief, if he was found suitable for promotion to higher post, without disturbing appointment, promotion and confirmation of appellants, by creation of supernumerary post - Judgment and order passed by HC quashing seniority list was set aside without any order as to costs - Appeal allowed.
