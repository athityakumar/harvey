N.C. Dalwadi v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28764; (1987) SCC (L&S) 299; (1987) 3 SCC 611; AIR 1987 SC 1933; 1987 (4) ATC 261; 1987 (55) FLR 572; JT 1987 (3) SC 152; 1987 LabIC 1638; 1987 (2) LLN 431; 1987(2) SCALE 107; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 640; 1987 (3) SLJ 134; 1987 (4) SLR 505; 1987 (2) UJ 453

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, r. 161(1)(a) first proviso, r. 161(1)(c)(ii)(1) - Exemption u/r. 161(1)(c)(ii)(1) from compulsory retirement of superintending engineers is not limited to those appointed substantively - Whether appellant, who was officiating as superintending engineers in Gujarat service of engineers, class I could be retired by State Govt. on date on which he attained age of 55 years on ground that he was merely officiating in that post and had not 'attained to rank of a Superintending Engineer' within meaning of r. 161(1)(c)(ii)(1) of Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959? - Order of compulsory retirement of appellant purporting to be under first proviso to r. 161(1)(a) of rules struck down as arbitrary - Appellant shall be deemed to have retired from service on attaining his normal age of superannuation of 58 years - Appeal allowed.
