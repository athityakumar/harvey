Gulshan and Another v Zila Parishad and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28784; (1987) Supp SCC 619; JT 1987 (4) SC 334; 1987(2) SCALE 1466; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 538; 1988 (1) UJ 311

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Socio-Economic - Carcass utilization - Traditional - Illiteracy - Implementation - Bye-law provides that right to trade in carcass utilization in rural area of respective Z should be put to public auction- Single Bench struck down latter part of bye-law provided for framing out of privilege of utilization and disposal of carcass of dead animals, for maintaining health, safety and convenience of inhabitants of rural areas - Division Bench referred matter to Full Bench and was declared that no person should store bones of dead animals or shall skin carcass or shall cure and dye skins or shall prepare leather goods in rural areas except he had obtained license - Petitioners were traditionally engaged in work of skinning, tanning and preparing articles of leather goods - Court passed order directing State. to frame Model Scheme for Carcass Utilization at village panchayat level - State. convened meeting with regard to formulation of Model Scheme and to organize one industrial co-operative society of skin citrine and leather craft industry each at block level in districts - Hence instant appeals - Whether State policy could be implemented effectively in all area - Held, contract system envisaged by bye-law framed by the different Z in State had virtually abandoned - State. proposes to replace system of auction by system of licensing, giving preferential right to co-operative societies consisting of members of traditional occupation, for disposal of carcass of dead animals - Owing to their illiteracy, penury, and social disadvantages they lack of will and ability to organize themselves - Social welfare department of State. Should made effort to bring members of traditional occupation within fold of co-operative societies - It would be open to Z as purely transitory measure and with prior concurrence of State. to arrange for carcass utilization by auction - Policy decision could not be implemented effectively in all area, and Z continue present contract system subject to variation necessary till co-operative societies were formed in that area - Appeals disposed of.
