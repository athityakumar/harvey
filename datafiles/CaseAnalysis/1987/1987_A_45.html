Amar Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28075; (1987) SCC (Cr) 232; (1987) 1 SCC 679; AIR 1987 SC 826; 1987 CRLJ 706; 1987 CRLR 225; JT 1987 (1) SC 465; 1987 SCALE 374; 1987 (1) UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 141, 148, 149 and 302 - Appeal by four petitioners against judgment of Punjab and Haryana High Court convicting them under above sections - Seven accused were part of unlawful assembly which led to death of deceased - Three acquitted by lower court - Testimony of sole eye-witness not corroborated by medical evidence - Held, conviction of remaining four accused u/ss. 148 and 149 cannot be sustained as three already acquitted - Entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
