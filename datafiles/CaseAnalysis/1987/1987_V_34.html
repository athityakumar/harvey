Beopar Sahayak (P) Limited And Ors. v Vishwa Nath And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28762; (1987) SCC (Cr) 641; (1987) 3 SCC 693; AIR 1987 SC 2111; 1987 ALJ 1434; JT 1987 (3) SC 76; 1987 (2) RentLR 714; 1987(2) SCALE 27; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 496; 1987 (2) UJ 388

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s.3(e) - Prescribed Authority - Tahsildars appointed as First Class Magistrates - Whether the order of release passed by the Prescribed Authority under the 1972 Act is a null and void order because the Prescribed Authority had no jurisdiction to pass the order as he did not possess the requisite qualification for being appointed as such Authority - Considering individual communication of notification and applicability of de facto doctrine - Held, all Tahsildars have been conferred powers of Magistrate Class I, it is to be presumed that they can exercised it for trail of criminal cases and experience as u/s. 3(e) is not required ; de facto doctrine applies and legality and validity of order passed by him cannot be challenged - Appeal dismissed.
