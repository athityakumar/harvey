Thamma Venkata Subbamma (decd.) (by Legal v Thamma Rattamma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28821; (1987) 3 SCC 294; AIR 1987 SC 1775; 1987 (2) CCC 332; 1987 (2) HLR 190; [1987] 168 ITR 760; JT 1987 (2) SC 440B; 1987(3) SCALE 1000; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 236; 1988 (1) ShimLC 61; 1987 (2) UJ 268

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Family and Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, art.141 - (A) Hindu Law - Mitakshara School - Coparcenary - (1) Whether a gift by a coparcener of his undivided coparcenary interest to another coparcener is void or not - (2) What is the essence of coparcenary - (B) Whether courts can change a law prevailing in particular area for decades without any change thought legislature - Ostensible gift held valid, consent of other coparceners is immaterial - Community of interest and unity of possession are essence of coparcenary - Courts should not upset such law except under compelling circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
