State Bank of India and Others v Yogendra Kumar Srivastava and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28737; (1987) 3 SCC 10; (1987) SCC (L&S) 141; AIR 1987 SC 1399; 1987 (3) ATC 733; 1987 (1) BankCLR 168; 1987 (55) FLR 154; JT 1987 (2) SC 477; 1987 LabIC 995; 1988 (1) LLJ 41; 1987 (2) LLN 432; 1987(3) SCALE 937; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 115; 1987 (2) SLJ 81; 1987 (2) SLR 732

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules ,1975 - Seniority - Entitlement - Respondent-Bank had 2 categories of officers Grade-I and Grade-II - Function performed by Officers Grade-I and Grade-II were similar in nature and there was little difference in pay scales - Officers Federation of respondent was demanding abolition of distinction between 2 Grades - Govt. appointed a Committee who held that there should not be any distinction between Officers Grade-I and Grade-II - Before the order was made and published petitioner-Probationary Officers/ Trainee Officers were appointed by respondent in Grade-I - These petitioners were fitted to Junior Management Grade-Scale-I after the order was passed - Some of them filed petitions before Allahabad HC and some before Delhi HC - Allahabad HC allowed petitions and Delhi HC dismissed petitions - Hence instant appeal and petitions by respondent and petitioners - Whether the orders of both the HC Courts were justified - Held, from telex and telegraph messages, it indicated that petitioners who joined after implementation of recommendations of Committee, they would be governed by revised terms of service and salary scales - After appointment of petitioners they were fitted to Junior Management Grade: Scale-I and given higher start - On the day petitioners were appointed they were admittedly seniors to Officers Grade-II - Seniority of petitioners had been taken away by giving retrospective operation to order - Respondent accepted demand of Officers Federation and distinction was removed - In these circumstances, it was apparent that petitioners being Officers Grade-I, were of the same status and position as Officers Grade-II - Officers Grade-II were appointed much earlier than petitioners, who were petitioners in HC - It was pointed out that everybody would be considered for promotion from Junior Management Grade to Middle Management Grade - Thus, even though petitioners were placed below Officers Grade-II, they would be allowed to appear at written test, one of modes prescribed for promotion along with others included Officers Grade II, provided they complete 6 years of service in Grade-I - Petitioners were fitted to higher scale of pay in the Scale-I of the Junior Management Grade, although they were not formally included in that Grade - In these circumstances, it should not be said that petitioners have been prejudiced by order - Bank should take immediate steps for applying the order to petitioners - Impugned judgment of Allahabad HC was set aside and that of Delhi HC was affirmed - Appeal allowed.
