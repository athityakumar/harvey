Dhartipakar Madan Lal Agarwal v Rajiv Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28459; (1987) Supp SCC 93; AIR 1987 SC 1577; 1987 (2) CCC 369; JT 1987 (2) SC 402; 1987(1) SCALE 1086; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 369; 1987 (2) UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People (Amendment) Act, 1974, s.77 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s.116A - Government of India Act, 1935, s.175(3) - CPC, 1908, O. 6 r. 16 , O. 7 r. 11 , s.123(1)(a) - Election petition - Corrupt practices - Appeal against -(A) During the pendency of appeal against dismissal of impugned election, the candidate got elected in subsequent election - Effect on maintainability of petition -Held, relief of setting aside election infructuous however charges of corrupt practice have to be investigated to determine disqualification from contesting election - And desirability of amending law to prescribe time limit for inquiry into allegations of corrupt practice pointed out - (B) Permissibility of amendment of election petition by insertion of new grounds after limitation period for filing petition - Held, not allowed - (C) Non disclosure of cause of action in petition - Maintainability - Held, Court can reject petition under O. 6 r.11 of CPC, 1908 even before filing of written statement - (D) Petitioner insisting on arguing petition only after being allowed to put on crown - Permissibility - Permission refused; Court observed that it would be wholly obnoxious to judicial propriety to allow litigants to appear in Court wearing a crown to argue the case, Court cannot be converted into a dramatic or theatrical stage - (E) Whether the following amounts to corrupt practice: (1) Grounds of challenge - Alleged obstruction by police in filing nomination paper by wearing only a 'langot' and further allegation that police were shadowing him and two policemen always kept Company with him - (2) Allegation that food was being given to workers of returned candidate at some place - (3) Practice of undue influence - Allegations that mother of returned candidate who was Prime Minister toured with the elected candidates and appealed to voters to vote for candidate - (4) Allegation that on polling day a lady went to polling booth with one person and accompanying person affixed stamp on ballot paper and returned with her - (5) Undue influence - Allegation as to distribution of ' batashas ' and drinking water to voters - No allegation that distribution was with consent of returned candidate or that candidate spent money over it or that said action influenced voters or that it materially affected result of election - (6) Allegations that elected candidate's workers helped voters to cast their votes in favour of elected candidate - Averments do not amount to any corrupt practice - (7) Allegations that petitioner did not appoint any counting agent but number of persons acted as counting agents of returned candidate in unauthorised manner and complaints made by petitioner were not considered - (8) Incurring or authorising expenditure beyond prescribed limit and of not keeping account of expenditure - Expenditure by sympathisers or friends - (90) Bribery - Gift to voters -Completion of some developmental activity in progress, during election period - (10) Bribery and undue influence - Statement of promise by candidate, his workers and his mother who was leader of party besides being Prime Minister that candidate if elected, constituency would be developed - (11) Hiring or procuring of vehicle for free conveyance of voters - All the three ingredients must be specifically pleaded - No pleading regarding hiring or procuring of vehicle by elected candidate or his election agent - (12) Procuring assistance from Government servants - Allegations that certain persons helped voters to cast votes and certain persons cast votes 100 to 200 times Sand their signatures were not taken - (13) Allegation of failure to remove posters and propaganda material displayed within 100 meters - Allegations do not make out any charge of corrupt practice - (C) They do not make out any ground u/s.100 - It does not make out any case of corrupt practice - Appeal is legitimate and does not constitute corrupt practice of undue influence - These facts do not constitute any corrupt practice and further, in view of magnitude of difference of votes, not materially affected - No corrupt practice is made out - They relate to irregularities and illegalities and would be relevant if there was further allegation that it materially, affected result of election , moreover, when term of candidate under election in question had already expired, allegations do not survive - They do not make out any case of commission of corrupt practice - Is not expenditure by candidate or his election agent - Does not amount to any gift or promise to voters - No corrupt practice of bribery or undue influence -Corrupt practice of procuring or hiring of vehicle is not made out - Case of corrupt practice contemplated by s.123 (7) not made out - It could be a ground u/s.100 (1) (d) (iv) for setting aside election on ground of election being materially affected - Order accordingly.
