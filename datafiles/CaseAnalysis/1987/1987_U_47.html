Tekraj Vasandi v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28433; (1988) 1 SCC 236; (1988) SCC (L&S) 300; AIR 1988 SC 469; 1988 (1) CCC 241; JT 1987 (4) SC 621; 1988 LabIC 961; 1988 (1) LLJ 341; 1988 (1) LLN 892; 1987(2) SCALE 1278; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 260; 1988 (3) SLR 560

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Indian Independence Act, 1947

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16 and 311 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 3 - State - Institute of Constitutional and Parliamentary Studies - (A) Whether institute registered under Societies Registration Act comes under 'other authorities' u/art. 12 of Constitution - (B) Effect of setting aside of dismissal of employee and restoring proceedings to stage of enquiry - Held, (A) Institute is neither an agency nor instrumentality of state so as to come within purview of 'other authorities' in art. 12 - (B) Employee shall deemed to have been restored to service - Suspension which had merged into dismissal cannot continue after dismissal order is vacated and hence, he would be entitled to salary for past period - Order accordingly.
