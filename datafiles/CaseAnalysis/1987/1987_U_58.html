Paradise Printers And Ors. A v Union Territory Of Chandigarh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28228; (1988) 1 SCC 440; AIR 1988 SC 354; 1988 (1) CompLJ 27; JT 1987 (4) SC 553; 1988 (1) PLR 400; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 32; 1987(2) SCALE 1235; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 157; 1988 (1) UJ 365

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Particulars, Capital of Punjab (Development and regulation) Act 1952, Chandigarh Leasehold of Sites and Buildings Rules 1973

Summary: Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act 1952, s. 3 - Chandigarh Leasehold of Sites and Buildings Rules 1973, r. 8 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Application for allotment of site in industrial area under 1952 Act - Filed with requisite earnest money and premium - Allotments delayed as new policy was adopted by authority - Applications considered for smaller industrial area in phase II instead of originally proposed phase I - And moreover without any statutory provision higher premium demanded - (A) Whether the applicants have to pay a higher premium - (B) Whether the applicant can acquire right to get transfer of site until there is official communication as required u/r. 8(3) - (C) Whether the use of the word 'shall' in r. 8(3) is makes the allotment mandatory - (D) When asked for higher premium - Whether applicants can invoke doctrine of estoppel alleging that they made heavy investment on machineries in the light of s.115 of the Evidence Act - (E) Whether the revised policy is arbitrary or discriminatory in the perview of arts.14 and 16 - Held, applicants could not be required to pay at a higher premium - Applicant cannot acquire the right before official communication - The right of every applicant u/r. 8(3) is only the right to have his application considered and not mandatory - Applicants cannot invoke doctrine of estoppel - Revised policy not found to be arbitrary or discriminatory - Appeal dismissed.
