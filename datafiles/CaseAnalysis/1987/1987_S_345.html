Balwant Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28706; (1987) 2 SCC 27; (1987) SCC (Cr) 249; AIR 1987 SC 1080; 1987 BLJR 491; 1987 (1) Crimes 910; 1987 CRLJ 971; 1987 CRLR 187; 1987(1) SCALE 279; 1987 (1) UJ 492

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 156 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376(1)(g) - Prosecutrix was a girl of 19/20 years of age - Prosecution story was that prosecutrix was raped by four accused persons, one after other - Police disbelieved prosecution story and treating case as cancelled - Medical report supported version of prosecutrix - Report of chemical examiner withheld by police - Defence taken by accused that they had been falsely implicated as they had enmity with father of prosecutrix - Held, evidence of prosecutrix that she was raped by accused persons one after other has been supported by other evidence - No importance be given to fact that police was not satisfied with prosecution case - Cannot be said that resistance offered must inflict injury on body of victim - Contention of appellants rejected.
