Seriyal Udayar v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28081; (1987) 2 SCC 359; AIR 1987 SC 1289; 1987 (2) Crimes 57; 1987 CRLJ 1058; 1987 CRLR 22; JT 1987 (1) SC 789; 1987(1) SCALE 589; 1987 (1) UJ 675

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 100, 302 and 447 - Prosecution for offence u/ss. 302 and 447 IPC, 1860 - Right of private defence - Whether appellant is entitled to be acquitted on grounds of private defence? - Though right of private defence of accused appellant was not established still material produced in cross-examination and circumstances indicated that incident might have happened in a manner in which it was suggested by accused-appellant - Held, on facts and circumstances that it could not be said that prosecution had established offence against appellant beyond reasonable doubt and therefore, he is entitled to be acquitted - Appeal allowed.
