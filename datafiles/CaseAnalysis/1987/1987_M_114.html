Conscientious Group v Mohammed Yunus and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28093; (1987) 3 SCC 89; (1987) SCC (Cr) 465; AIR 1987 SC 1451; 1987 (2) Crimes 724; 1987 CRLJ 1182; 1987 CRLR 321; JT 1987 (3) SC 377; 1987(1) SCALE 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 15 - Supreme Court permitted withdrawal of contempt petition with liberty to review petition on obtaining consent or in case refusal of consent was on any irrelevant ground - Subsequent petition under prayer to revive a contempt petition which had been previously withdrawn by him - Petition filed without complying with requirement of obtaining written consent of Attorney General or Solicitor General - Whether contempt petition of petitioner is entitled for revival of petition? - Elaborate reasons have been stated by learned Solicitor General in support of his conclusion that it would not be in public interest to give his consent to initiation of contempt proceedings - Held, grounds stated by Solicitor General for declining to grant consent cannot be said to be irrelevant in eye of law, nor can view expressed by Solicitor General be characterised as arbitrary, illegal or unreasonable - In circumstances it has to be held that petitioner is not entitled to succeed in his prayer for revival of contempt petition since neither of conditions precedent specified in order D/- 19-12-1986 for maintainability of prayer for revival can be said to be fulfilled - Petition dismissed.
