Tirath Ram Gupta v Gurbachan Singh And Anr,

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28664; (1987) 1 SCC 712; AIR 1987 SC 770; JT 1987 (1) SC 365; 1987 (2) RCJ 369; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 242; 1987 (1) RentLR 168; 1987(1) SCALE 255; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 190; 1987 (1) UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949

Summary: Rent Control - CPC, 1908 - Transfer of property Act, 1882 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s.13(2) - Tenanacy - Sub-letting - Created before coming into force of Act - Eviction of sub-lessee - (A) Ordered u/s.13(2) of the Act, permissibility - (B) Sub-lease - Legality - (1) Allegation that lease deed prohibited creation of sub-tenancy - Lease deed not produced - (2) Valid sub-lease created before coming into force of Act - Subsequent termination thereof by landlord - (C) Ejectment - (1) Decree against both tenant and sub-tenant - Appeal against, who can file - (2) Surrender of portion of premises given in possession of sub-tenant to landlord by tenant - Effect - (A) Eviction of sub-lessee cannot be ordered u/s.13(2) - (B) Presumption is that valid lease deed had been created and it contained contract to the contrary in so far as tenant's right to sub-lease is concerned - Sub-tenancy is not rendered unlawful thereby - (C) Latter alone can file appeal - Landlord cannot seek possession of portion on basis of surrender - Appeal dismissed.
