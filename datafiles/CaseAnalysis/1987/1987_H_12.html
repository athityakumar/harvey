Hardeep Singh v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28367; (1987) Supp SCC 295; (1988) SCC (L&S) 157; 1988 (56) FLR 65; JT 1987 (3) SC 417; 1987 (2) LLN 918; 1987(2) SCALE 431; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 1138; 1988 (1) SLJ 207; 1987 (4) SLR 576; 1987 (2) UJ 742

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Punjab Police Rules, 1934, rr. 12.21, 16.24(IX)(b) - Termination - Charges not served - Reinstatement - Legality -Petitioner/constable filed instant writ petition challenging order issued by respondent/Commandant on ground that impugned order of removal from service was in effect a penal order and same was made without complying with requirements of art. 311(2) of Constitution as well as r. 16.24(ix)(b) of the Rules was wholly arbitrary, illegal and unwarranted and petitioner to be reinstated in service - Whether impugned order passed by respondents was valid - Held, impugned order casts a stigma on service career of petitioner and order was made by way of punishment, petitioner was entitled to protection afforded by provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution as well as by provisions of r. 16.24(IX)(b) of the Rules - Petitioner was not served with any charges of misconduct in discharge of his duties as a police constable nor had he ever been asked to show cause against said charges - Thus, order of removal from service was made because of his union activities namely participating in call for expressing protest of association for improvement in service conditions by abstaining from taking meals in Mess though petitioner like other members of association performed his duties on that day and did not abstain from duty - Hence, impugned order undoubtedly tantamounts to dismissal from service by reason of misconduct of petitioner in discharge of official duties as police constable - Ajit Singh and Others v State of Haryana and Others, 1984 Indlaw SC 366, followed - impugned order of discharge of petitioner from State Police Force u/r. 12.21 of the Rules passed by Commandant was quashed - Respondents were directed that petitioner be reinstated in service with 50% back wages from date of termination of his service till date of his reinstatement - Petition allowed.
