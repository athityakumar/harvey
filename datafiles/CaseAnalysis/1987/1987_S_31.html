Samar Singh v Kedar Nath Alias K. N. Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28337; (1987) Supp SCC 663; AIR 1987 SC 1926; JT 1987 (3) SC 165; 1987(2) SCALE 135; 1987 (2) UJ 548

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Civil Procedure - Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 34 and 36 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11 - (A) Whether High Court was within its jurisdiction to entertain an application u/O. 7, r.11 after settlement of issues? - Question whether HC has power to reject an election petition summarily under O. 7, r. 11 is no longer res integra as controversy has been set at rest by Supreme Court in Azhar Hussain v Rajiv Gandhi - If an election petition does not disclose cause of action, it can be dismissed summarily at threshold under O. 7, r. 11 - If an election petition can be summarily rejected at threshold of proceeding there is no reason why same cannot be rejected at any stage of subsequent proceeding - (B) Whether appellant's nomination paper had been accepted improperly by Returning Officer which materially affected result of election? and whether Returning Officer acted in violation of proviso to s. 34 of Act as he failed to correct entries in appellant's nomination paper and list of contesting candidates with regard to petitioner's name, resulting in outcome of election being materially affected? - Returning Officer examined appellant's nomination paper, and found that name and electoral roll number were same as entered in electoral roll - In these circumstances Returning Officer had no authority in law to permit correction of any entry in electoral roll or in nomination paper - Returning Officer rightly accepted appellant's nomination paper and appellant's grievance that Returning Officer failed to exercise jurisdiction under proviso to s. 34 is misconceived - Once nomination paper was accepted and on scrutiny was found to be valid and list of contesting candidates was prepared as per s. 36, Returning Officer had no jurisdiction to make any correction therein - Appeal dismissed.
