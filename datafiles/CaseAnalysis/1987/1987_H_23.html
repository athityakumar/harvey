Harsharan Verma v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28312; (1987) Supp SCC 310; AIR 1987 SC 1969; 1987 (2) CCC 400; JT 1987 (3) SC 62; 1987(2) SCALE 25; 1987 (2) UJ 308

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 75 and 88 - Appeal by special leave - (A) Appointment of minister in central cabinet - But not member of either House of Parliament - Ceasing to be Minister - But court's practice is not to undertake interpretation of Constitution unless there is live issue before them - (B) Legality of appointment of non-member of Parliament as minister - Held, since question of condonation of delay in filing special leave petition was involved in case question was decided on merit - It is not illegal and he can hold office up to a period of six months - Application dismissed.
