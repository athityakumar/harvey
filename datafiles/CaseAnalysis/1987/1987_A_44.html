Om Prakash v Amar Singh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28695; (1987) 1 SCC 458; AIR 1987 SC 617; 1987 ALJ 184; 1987 (1) CCC 417; JT 1987 (1) SC 199; 1987 (1) RCJ 629; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 326; 1987 (1) RentLR 502; 1987(1) SCALE 35; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 968; 1987 (1) UJ 442

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Cantonment Rent Control Act 1952

Summary: Civil Procedure - U.P. Cantonments (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1952, s.14(c) - Material alteration - Means substantial change in character, form and structure of building without destroying its identity - (A) Question whether construction materially altered accommodation - High Court in second appeal interfered with the findings of fact recorded by the lower courts on the question - (B) Construction whether of permanent or temporary nature - Mixed question of fact and law and hence High Court committed error in interfering with the findings of the first appeal court - Held, is relevant consideration - Appeal allowed.
