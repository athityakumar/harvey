Rishabh Kumar and Sons v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28255; (1987) Supp SCC 306; (1988) SCC (Tax) 19; AIR 1987 SC 1576; 1987(1) SCALE 1147; 1987 (66) STC 222; 1987 (1) UJ 705

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, s.3A - Sales Tax on sale of weights and measures - High Court holding commodity not covered by certain entry in notification - Appellate Authority put the assessee to notice and on the basis of the decision of the High Court held that the sales were not covered by the notifications u/s.3A and were liable to tax at the general rate - Whether clarification orders passed by authority support that commodity is covered by notification - Orders of authorities held, cannot be taken aid of to urge that commodity is covered by notification - In the special facts and circumstances of this case, the assessee should not be made to pay the amount however Supreme Court indicate that this shall not be cited as a precedent - Appeal disposed of.
