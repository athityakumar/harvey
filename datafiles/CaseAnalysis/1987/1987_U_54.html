Union Of India v Nihar Kanta Sen And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28243; (1987) 3 SCC 465; AIR 1987 SC 1713; 1987 (1) CalLT(SC) 38; 1987 (2) CCC 387; JT 1987 (2) SC 555; 1987(1) SCALE 1015; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1108; 1987 (2) UJ 310

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Land and Property - West Bengal Estate Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 2(1), 3, 4(1), 5, 6 - Defence of India Rules, 1939, r. 75-A - Recurring compensation - Award of - Whether HC committed error in awarding recurring compensation to claimants for period beyond April 15, 1955, as claimants ceased to have any right, title or interest in property in dispute, as same vested in State with effect from April 15, 1955 under provisions of West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953? - Held, HC proceeded that land could be settled for mooram extraction to a willing party at an annual rent payable to claimants at rate of Rs. 5000 - On these findings HC awarded a sum of Rs. 5000 as recurring annual compensation to claimants - There is no evidence on record to show that mooram was available over entire area of 160 acres - There is further no evidence to show that claimants had let out right to excavate mooram to any one or that they had been deriving any recurring income each year - In absence of any such evidence, no recurring compensation could be granted to claimants - Recurring compensation is granted to make good loss which owner may suffer - If owner fails to prove recurring annual loss, he could not be entitled to recurring compensation for requisitioned property - HC has held that coloured clay was available in requisitioned land, which could be used for industrial purposes and for which Nirode Babu intended to set up a factory - HC had no evidence before it with regard either to area or to quality, or quantity of coloured clay available in requisitioned land - Modify order of HC to extent that claimants are entitled to a sum of Rs. 2, 00, 000 as terminal compensation and also to a sum of Rs. 25, 650 as recurring compensation, in respect of Sal trees and agricultural land etc, per annum with effect from October 1, 1942 to April 15 1955 - Appeal partly allowed.
