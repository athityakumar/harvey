Sitaram Jivyabhai Gavali v Ramjibhai Potiyabhai Mahala and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28195; (1987) 2 SCC 262; AIR 1987 SC 1293; 1987 (3) ATC 515; JT 1987 (1) SC 767; 1987 LabIC 969; 1987(1) SCALE 608; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 635; 1987 (2) SLR 279; 1987 (2) UJ 133

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Civil Services Rules, Central Civil Services (Temporary service) Rules 1965

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 10 and 100 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 102(1)(a) and 309 - Holding of 'office of profit' under Govt. as a disqualification for election - Appellant, a temporary Govt. servant resigned before date of scrutiny of nomination papers to be effective from date of submission of resignation letter along with notice pay of one month - Held, that appellant ceased to be a Govt. servant before he submitted his nomination paper on facts that resignation was one under conditions stated in appointment letter and became effective before date of scrutiny of nomination papers - Appeal allowed.
