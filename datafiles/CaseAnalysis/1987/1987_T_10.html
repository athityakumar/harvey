Prativa Devi v T. V. Krishnan

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, G.L. Oza, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28497; (1996) 5 SCC 353; JT 1987 (1) SC 764B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e) and 25-B(8) - Whether High Court was justified in facts and circumstances of case in interfering with order of Rent Controller allowing application made by appellant -landlady u/s. 14(1)(e)? - Rent Controller had kept legal principles in view and on an objective determination had come to a definite conclusion that need of appellant of demised premises for her residential use was bona fide and that she did not have any alternative accommodation available for that purpose within meaning of s. 14(1)(e) - Appellant had established her bona fide requirement of demised premises for her personal use and occupation, which finding was based on a proper appreciation of evidence in light of surrounding circumstances - HC erred in laying down that test is availability of alternative accommodation and not legal right to such occupation in adjudging bona fides of claim of landlord - Held, although revisional power of HC u/s. 25-B(8) may not be as narrow as u/s. 115, there was no ground on which legality and propriety of order of Rent Controller could be successfully assailed - Appeal allowed.
