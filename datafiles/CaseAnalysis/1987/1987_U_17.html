Bakshi Sardari Lal (Dead) Through Lrsand Ors. Etc. v Union Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28345; (1987) 4 SCC 114; (1987) SCC (L&S) 381; AIR 1987 SC 2106; 1987 (4) ATC 660; 1987 (2) CCC 723; 1987 (55) FLR 427; JT 1987 (3) SC 180; 1987 LabIC 1681; 1988 (1) LLJ 251; 1987 (2) LLN 665; 1987(2) SCALE 147; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 704; 1987 (3) SLJ 147; 1987 (5) SLR 283; 1987 (2) UJ 517

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Service matter - Dismissal of Govt. servant after dispensation of enquiry u/cl. (2) of second proviso to art. 311(2) - Satisfaction of President - (A) Satisfaction reached by President upon aid and advice of Council of Ministers Effect - (B) Reasons, recorded in writing but not communicated to aggrieved - (C) Second order of dismissal after reinstatement after order of Supreme Court - Need of leave of Court when first order was quashed on account of non-compliance of requirements of law - (D) Home Minister stating in Lok Sabha that cases of dismissed employees, Sub-Inspector, Head Constables and Constables would be considered for payment of compensatory allowance - No response to enquiry as to what was in view of Govt. at time of making statement - First order of dismissal was passed twenty years ago and some of them even died - Held, same is not vitiated - Satisfaction is not vitiated for non-communication - Leave of Supreme Court not necessary for making fresh order in exercise of disciplinary jurisdiction after removing defects - Further held, that situation would be met in a just way if instead of paying recurring allowance lump sum payment is made - Supreme Court directed payment of Rs. 60,000/-, 50,000/- and Rs. 40,000/- To each Sub-Inspector, Head Constable and Constable respectively - Appeals dismissed.
