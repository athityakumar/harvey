Bhagwan Dass and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28346; (1987) 4 SCC 634; (1988) SCC (L&S) 24; AIR 1987 SC 2049; 1987 (5) ATC 136; 1987 (2) CCC 708; JT 1987 (3) SC 206; 1987 LabIC 1662; 1988 (1) LLN 1; 1987(2) SCALE 160; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 714; 1988 (1) ShimLC 76; 1987 (3) SLJ 93; 1987 (4) SLR 517; 1987 (2) UJ 556

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 and 139(d) - (A) Service Conditions - Employment whether part-time or full-time - Supervisors under adult education scheme required to stay for whole day in village and to visit I E C in day and A E C in night - And to go on tour, to remain at headquarter once a week from 9-30 a m to 4 p m - (B) Denial of equal pay for equal work to persons doing similar work - Ground : (1) Mode of recruitment was different - (2) Appointment under temporary scheme - (C) Absorption in regular service - Entitlement - Supervisors - Appointed each time for 6 months after giving break of day or two - In context of a temporary scheme - Held, (A) Supervisors are not part-time but whole-time functionaries - Cannot be denied pay equal to that of supervisor appointed on regular basis - (B) Further held, cannot be denied - Not a ground to deny equal pay - (C) Not entitled to be absorbed as regular employees on a permanent basis from date of their initial appointment - However, entitled to pay on basis of length of service computed from date of their appointment by disregarding breaks which have been given for a limited purpose - Order accordingly.
