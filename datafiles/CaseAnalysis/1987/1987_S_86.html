State of Karnataka and Others v Shree Rameshwara Rice Mills, Thirthahalli and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28854; (1987) 2 SCC 160; AIR 1987 SC 1359; 1987 (2) ARBLR 144; 1987 (1) BankCLR 13; JT 1987 (1) SC 578; 1987(1) SCALE 448; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 398; 1987 (2) UJ 85

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Trade

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950 - Revenue Recovery Act, 1890 - Whether term that for any breach of condition of contract, contractor shall be liable to pay to Govt. such damages as assessed by Govt. - Damages for breach of conditions amounts to money due under contract - Held, interests of justice and equity require that where a party to a contract disputes committing of any breach of conditions adjudication should be by an independent person or body and not by other party to contract - Damages can be recovered as land revenue.
