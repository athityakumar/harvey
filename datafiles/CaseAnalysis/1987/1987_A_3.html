A. K. Subbaiah and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28389; (1987) 4 SCC 557; (1987) SCC (Cr) 768; 1987 (3) Crimes 399; JT 1987 (3) SC 435; 1987(2) SCALE 451; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 1128; 1988 (1) ShimLC 137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 397, 401, 199 and 204 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 500 - Defamation - Complaint case by State Govt. - Trial Court took cognizance of matter but on revision petition by accused persons High Court deleted names of two respondents - Hence, present Special Leave Petition - Whether HC correct in deleting parties from revision although prayer sought was entirely different? - When the issue of process is challenged in the revision petition before the HC what the High Court is expected to see is as to whether complaint and papers accompanying the complaint prima facie indicate that an offence is made out - Question about anyone else being instrumental in getting the prosecution launched or questions which are foreign are not to be considered in a revision when the issue of process is being challenged and therefore the further question as to whether the party against whom an allegation is made is or is not a necessary party in the proceedings also is of no avail - Held, parties deleted by HC were not parties in lower court and question raised was regarding process being issued by Trial Court and nothing else - HC correct in deleting some parties - Appeal dismissed.
