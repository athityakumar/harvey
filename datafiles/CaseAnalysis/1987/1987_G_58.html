Budhwanti And Anr. v Gulab Chand Prasad

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28651; (1987) 2 SCC 153; AIR 1987 SC 1484; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 297; 1987(1) SCALE 501; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 534; 1987 (2) UJ 22

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Rent Control - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, s. ss.3 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art 136 - Tenancy - Eviction on grounds of default in payment of rent and bona fide requirement of the leased premises for the business needs of the landlord (A) Whether it is permissible to interfere with finding as to bonafide requirement in second appeal - (B) If comparison of hardships of both parties while determination of bona fide requirement of landlord, can be made - (C) Whether plea for adjustment of rent paid in excess of agreed rent can be allowed. Held, permissible when finding is vitiated by application of wrong tests or is based on conjectures - It is not contemplated by the Act - Not permissible unless compulsion or exploitation of tenant in paying extra rent is proved and doctrine of 'in pari delicto' will not be attracted when there is no element of compulsion or exploitation and both parties have by consensus contravened the provisions of law for their mutual advantage - Appeal dismissed.
