State Of Haryana v P. C. Wadhawa, Ips, Inspector General of Police

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28239; (1987) 2 SCC 602; AIR 1987 SC 1201; 1987 (3) ATC 690; 1987 LabIC 901; 1987 (1) LLJ 529; 1987 (2) LLN 27; 1987(1) SCALE 799; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1030; 1987 SLJ 162; 1987 (2) SLR 393; 1987 (2) UJ 233

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: All India Services (Confidential Rolls) Rules, 1970, Punjab Police Service Rules 1934, All India Services (Confidential Rolls) rules 1970, Business of the Haryana Government (Allocation) Rules 1974

Summary: Service - All India Services (Confidential Rolls) rules 1970 - All India Services (Confidential Rolls) Rules, 1970 - Business of the Haryana Government (Allocation) Rules 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Police Act, 1861 - Punjab Police Service Rules 1934 - All India Services (Confidential Rolls) Rules, 1970 - Service Rules - Writing of a confidential report by Home Secretary of State on work and conduct of I.G. of Police, Haryana i.e head of Police Deptt. - Writ potion against, challenging the authority to write such report - Not proper and Division Bench of the High Court was therefore, perfectly justified in quashing the confidential report written by the then Home Secretary on the work and conduct - (B) Adverse remarks therein were duly accepted by the competent authority under 1970 Rules - Inordinate delay in communicating adverse remarks - Validity - Held, not proper - Substantial Compliance of provisions of rr. 5, 6, 6-A and 7of 1970 Rules is sufficient - Appeal dismissed.

.
