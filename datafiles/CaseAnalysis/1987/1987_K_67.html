State Of Bihar Etc. Etc. v Kripalu Shanker Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28751; (1987) 3 SCC 34; (1987) SCC (Cr) 442; AIR 1987 SC 1554; 1987 BLJ 250; 1987 (2) CCC 144; 1987 CRLJ 1860; 1987 CrLR(SC) 521; JT 1987 (3) SC 49; 1987(1) SCALE 1070; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 1; 1987 (2) SLJ 96

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss.2(b) and 2(c) - Scope of definition of 'Contempt' - Govt. officers made notings in internal files (privileged document) which do not amount to formal order u/art. 166 of the Constitution - Expressing independent opinion against Court orders in such notings - Disclosure of privileged document in Court - High Court held some of the officers of the guilty solely on the basis of the views expressed by them in the files, which were not, in fact, accepted by the Govt. and which were only at the stage of suggestions and views - Held, they cannot be used for purpose other than that relating to the case - Cannot be made basis for action in contempt - Appeal allowed.
