Dipak Kumar Biswas v Director Of Public Instruction And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28672; (1987) 2 SCC 252; AIR 1987 SC 1422; 1987 (3) ATC 505; 1987 (54) FLR 447; JT 1987 (1) SC 631; 1987 LabIC 1012; 1987 (1) LLJ 516; 1987(1) SCALE 544B; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 572; 1987 (3) SLJ 215; 1987 (3) SLR 65; 1987 (1) UJ 715

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Continuance In Service

Summary: Service - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Termination - Appointment of lecturer subject to approval of Director of Public Instruction - Principal assured that it was a formality - Approval wrongly refused - Suit for permanent injunction and for a declaration that he continues to be in service with benefits - Held, since college was not a statutory body action of Director of Public Instruction was not in contravention of any statutory provisions or regulations or procedural rules, still appellant is liable for compensation and directed respondent to pay 3 years salary and allowances - Appeal allowed.
