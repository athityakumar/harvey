Delhi Cloth And General Mills Limited v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28363; (1988) 1 SCC 86; AIR 1987 SC 2414; 1987 (2) CCC 983; 1987 (3) CompLJ 133; JT 1987 (4) SC 35; 1987(2) SCALE 715; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 383; 1988 (1) UJ 207

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Railway Act 1890

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Railways Act, 1890, ss. 28 and 40(1)(b) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Railways - Carriage of commodity - Surplus income - Different rates for Naptha and crude oil - Naptha being dangerous, need special care but not crude oil - Only on getting assurance as to Rate of freight, from railway - The company established a factory at a particular place - Railway charging higher rate - The Questions arise under Railways Act 1890 - (A) As to proof of undue preference or advantage given by Railway administration u/s 28 - (B) Whether reasonable freight is charged u/s. 40(1)(b) - (C)Whether damage, prejudice or detriment to promisee or action on the basis of assurance is required to be proved for invoking estoppel u/s 115 of the Evidence Act 1872 - (D) Whether representation by the Railways is sufficient for estoppel - Held, charges u/s. 28 not proved - Surplus cannot by itself be basis for judging reasonableness of freight, thereby demand of parity in freights unjustified - For applicability of promissory estoppel u/s. 115, the alteration of position by promisee is only requirement - Representation made must be clear, unambiguous and not tentative or uncertain hence no estoppel - Appeal dismissed.
