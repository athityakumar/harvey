Commissioner Of Income-Tax,U.P., Lucknow v British India Corpn. Ltd., Kanpur.

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28813; (1987) 2 SCC 96; AIR 1987 SC 798; 1987 (2) BankCLR 436; 1987 (60) CTR 54; [1987] 165 ITR 51; JT 1987 (1) SC 328; 1987(1) SCALE 221; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 133; 1987 TAXLR 465; [1987] 30 TAXMAN 546; 1987 (1) UJ 480

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(2)(xv) - Agreement with foreign company for supply of technical know-how - Assessee to appoint nominee of foreign company as distributor in India and also to pay to establishment expenses - Whether the expenditure was a capital expenditure deductible u/s.10(2)(xv) of the Act? - Held, sum payable to nominee is revenue expenditure - Appeal dismissed.
