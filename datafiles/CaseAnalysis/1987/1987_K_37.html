Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation v Secretary, Karnataka State Transport Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, B.C. Ray, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28569; (1987) Supp SCC 728; AIR 1987 SC 714; JT 1987 (1) SC 422; 1987(1) SCALE 346; 1987 (1) UJ 374

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Interstate route - Permit - Grant of - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 62(1)(c) and 68 FF - State Govt. of Karnataka framed a Kolar Pocket Scheme is in regard to intra-state routes under Chapter IV-A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Respondent 2 applied for grant of a temporary permit u/s. 62(1)(c) of Act - Rejected - Grant of permit to appellant - High Court set aside order granting permit in favour of appellant and directed that a temporary permit be granted to respondent 2 - Present appeal - Intra-state route covered under scheme and inter-state route in question overlap for a major portion principle indicated in s. 68-FF as accepted by Constitution Bench judgment would bar grant of a permit less it is in accordance with law and provisions of scheme - Temporary permit obtained by respondent 2 was not an existing permit when revised scheme came into force in 1980 - Therefore, it would not be saved by provision in scheme and grant of a temporary permit would not be in accordance with statutory requirements of s. 68 - Held, HC wrong in directing grant of a temporary permit to respondent but temporary permit that was granted by HC has long lapsed - From material on record it transpires that there is need for extra service on inter-state route -Direction issued - Appeal allowed.
