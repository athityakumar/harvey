Daya Shankar v High Court of Allahabad and Others Through

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28355; (1987) 3 SCC 1; (1987) SCC (L&S) 132; AIR 1987 SC 1469; 1987 (3) ATC 723; 1987 (2) CCC 174; 1987 (55) FLR 43; JT 1987 (2) SC 381; 1987 LabIC 1025; 1987 (2) LLJ 356; 1987 (2) LLN 392; 1987(1) SCALE 1024; 1987 (3) SLJ 131; 1987 (2) SLR 717; 1987 (1) UJ 708

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Civil Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Judicial Officers Protection Act, s. 1 - Termination - Validity - Removal of Petitioner, a Munsif from Judicial Service who was found copying in LLM examination and High Court recommended Govt. to remove him from service upholding findings of Inquiry Officer who found him guilty and Governor removed him from services - Held, Judicial Officers must have only one standard of rectitude, honesty and integrity and they cannot act even remotely unworthy of office they occupy - Petition dismissed.
