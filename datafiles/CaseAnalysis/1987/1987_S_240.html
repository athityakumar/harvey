Sachidananda Pandey v State Of West Bengal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28720; (1987) 2 SCC 295; AIR 1987 SC 1109; 1987 (1) BankCLR 403; 1987 (1) CompLJ 211; JT 1987 (1) SC 425; 1987(1) SCALE 311; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 223; 1987 (1) UJ 641

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Trade

Keywords: Migration, Bengal Public Parks Act 1904

Summary: Tort - Environment, Wildlife and Animal - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Water (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1974 - CPC, 1908, O.6, r.17 - Social action litigation for closure of hazardous industrial units - Oleum gas leak during pendency of petition for closer of certain units of a company - Questions of seminal importance and high constitutional significance raised - (A) Whether SC can entertain applications for compensation on behalf victims of gas leak despite failure to amend petition to include claim for compensation - No claim for compensation originally made in the writ petition - (B) Infringement of fundamental right of large number of persons - Whether SC can award relief in the writ petition - (C) Whether the letter addressed to only one justice of Court can be entertained u/art. 32 - (D) Whether a private corporation like Shriram (which is owned by a public company limited by shares and which is engaged in an industry vital to public interest) would fall within the scope and ambit of art.12 - (E) Tort - Provisions of arts. 21 and 32 - Liability of industry engaged in inherently hazardous activity to compensate for harm caused to all affected persons - Determination of mode of Compensation - (F) Precedents - Reference to law as it prevails in England or in any other country, if - Held, failure to amend petition immaterial as application for compensation are for enforcement of the fundamental right to life enshrined in art. 21 and while dealing with such applications, a hyper-technical approach which would defeat the ends of justice could not be adopted; court while dealing with an application for enforcement of a fundamental right must look at the substance and not the form - Supreme Court can award remedial relief of compensation in the writ petition itself - And it would not be right to reject a letter merely on the ground that it is not addressed to the Court or to the Chief Justice and his companion Judges; And the Court should not adopt a rigid stance that no letters will be entertained unless they are supported by an affidavit - Court declined to go into the issue due to time and other constraints - Compensation must be correlated to the magnitude and capacity of the enterprise because such Compensation must have a deterrent effect - Supreme Court cannot allow its judicial thinking to be constricted by reference to law as it prevails in England or in any other country - Matter disposed of.
