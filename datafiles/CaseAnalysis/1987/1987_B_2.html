Abdul Latif Abdul Wahab Sheikh v B. K. Jha and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28095; (1987) 2 SCC 22; (1987) SCC (Cr) 244; AIR 1987 SC 725; 1987 CRLJ 700; 1987 CrLR(SC) 289; JT 1987 (1) SC 397; 1987(1) SCALE 275; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 203

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 22(4) - Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985, s. 15 - Petitioner detained once but later released - Detained for second time - Detention order assailed for non-compliance of statutory and constitutional compliance - Whether order of subsequent detention was justified? - If report of Advisory Board is not made within three months of date of detention, detention becomes illegal notwithstanding that it is within three months from date of the second order of detention - Held, no - Although subsequent detention was made in compliance of mandate laid down in art. 22(4), but such order could not be passed until Advisory Board gives its opinion on previous order of detention - Detention quashed - Appeal allowed.
