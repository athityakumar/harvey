Central Wines and Others v Special Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28699; (1987) SCC (Tax) 184; (1987) 2 SCC 371; AIR 1987 SC 611; JT 1987 (1) SC 127; 1988 (32) KarLJ 19; 1987(1) SCALE 15; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 945; 1987 (65) STC 48; 1987 (1) UJ 448

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax; Socio-Economic

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, s.2(s) - Madras Prohibition Act, 1937 Turnover - Whether the amount collected by the seller from the buyer which comprises of the two components the actual sale price and the sales tax is a part of the 'turnover' and comes within the expression 'any other sum charged by the dealer whatever be the description, name or object thereof' occurring in the definition in s.2(s) of 1957 Act - Held, sales tax component included in sale price is includable in making aggregate for purpose of turnover - Appeal dismissed.
