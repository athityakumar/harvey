Orient Transport Co. Gulabra And Another. v Jaya Bharat Credit And Investment Co., Ltd.,And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28304; (1987) 4 SCC 421; AIR 1987 SC 2289; 1988 (2) ARBLR 142; 1988 (1) CCC 1082; JT 1987 (3) SC 575; 1987(2) SCALE 581; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 47; 1988 (1) UJ 191

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Suit challenging validity of a contract not barred by s. 32 merely because it contains arbitration clause - Whether right u/s. 9, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 is taken away by s. 32 - Held, right u/s. 9, CPC, 1908 is not taken away by s. 32 - Ss. 9 and 32 of Act does not contemplate case of a suit challenging validity of a contract merely because it contains an arbitration clause - Appeal allowed,judgment and order of the High Court and the courts below set aside
