State Of U.P. v Hari Shankar Tewari

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28078; (1987) SCC (Cr) 388; (1987) 2 SCC 490; AIR 1987 SC 998; 1987 (1) Crimes 729; 1987 CRLJ 840; 1987 CRLR 340; 1987 CrLR(SC) 340; JT 1987 (1) SC 563; 1987(1) SCALE 462; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 426

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Whether a matter relates to public order or law and order? - Held, it depends on facts of each case - Question whether grounds furnished in support of order of detention related to public order or not was not gone into as detenu had suffered detention for a major part of period covered by order before he was released by High Court - The detention in normal course would have lapsed more than eighteen months before hearing of appeal - Appeal dismissed.
