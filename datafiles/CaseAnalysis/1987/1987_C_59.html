Jain Engineering Co. v Collector Of Customs, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28877; (1987) 4 SCC 492; AIR 1987 SC 2279; 1988 (1) CCC 1078; 1987 CRLR 657; 1987 CrLR(SC) 657; 1987 (13) ECR 273; 1987 (32) E.L.T. 3; JT 1987 (3) SC 596; 1987(2) SCALE 584; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 220; 1988 (1) UJ 163

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Custom

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Notification granting exemption of internal combustion piston engine - (A) Whether only engine falling under Heading No. 84.06 of Customs Tariff Act are exempted - (B) Scope of term 'parts' in para. 2 of column (2) in Table annexed to notification - (C) Whether bushings and bearings of such engine are same part - Inclusion of such parts under another heading of Tariff Act - Held, it applies not only to such engine but also to parts of such engine - Refers to few parts also - They are distinct and separate parts even though functions of both may be same - Appeal allowed.
