Hindustan Steel Works Construction Limited v C. Rajasekhar Rao

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28840; (1987) 4 SCC 93; 1987 (2) ARBLR 200; JT 1987 (3) SC 239; 1987(2) SCALE 218; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 653; 1987 (2) UJ 468

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Constitution of India, 1950 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 28 - (A) Whether period from date of his entering upon references had expired, and umpire became functus officio, (1) Whether power to extend period of passing award was vested in court alone u/s. 28 of Arbitration Act? - Held, extend time for giving award and award will be deemed to have been given in time - (B) What is a speaking award? - Held, an award as a whole needs reasons given for purpose of making award, otherwise it is not a speaking award at all - Award needs to speak as to why umpire has awarded as he did - It needs to speak mind of umpire - Application disposed of.
