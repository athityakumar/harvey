Commissioner Of Income Tax, v Vikram Cotton Mills Ltd

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28807; (1988) Supp SCC 442; (1988) SCC (Tax) 456; AIR 1988 SC 460; 1988 (67) CTR 169; 1988 (69) CTR 259; [1988] 169 ITR 597; JT 1987 (4) SC 708; 1987(2) SCALE 1403; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 389; 1988 TAXLR 833; [1988] 36 TAXMAN 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land and Property - Income tax Act, 1922, ss. 10 and 12 - Income tax Act ,1961 ss. 28 -Income from lease - Limited company 's income is assessed under the head business income - Certain crises conditionsaris - In order to tide over, the company suspended its business for a for a temporary period - And leased out its assets - Under which particular head the income of company from lease rent would be assessable to tax - Held, such income will be assessable under the head ' profits and gains of business' - Appeal dismissed.
