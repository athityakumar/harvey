Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Shah Sadiq and Sons

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28815; (1987) SCC (Tax) 270; (1987) 3 SCC 516; AIR 1987 SC 1217; 1987 (2) BankCLR 213; 1987 (61) CTR 269; [1987] 166 ITR 102; JT 1987 (2) SC 157; 1987(1) SCALE 816; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 942; 1987 TAXLR 2089; [1987] 31 TAXMAN 498

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 75 (2) - Loss in speculation business - Assessment - Set off - Carry forward - Assessee suffered loss in speculation business was carried forward for adjustment against speculation profits of future years - In assessment proceedings , assessee claimed certain sum as loss set off against speculation profit - Income Tax Officer(IO) rejected assessee's claim - On further appeal, Tribunal declared that right to carry forward losses relating to a/ys 1960-61 and 1961-62 was governed by 1922 Act and s. 75(2) of 1961 Act was applicable to that assessment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to set off speculation losses suffered in assessment years against speculation profits of the previous year - Held, assessee entitled to carry forward losses of speculation business and set off such losses against profits made from that business in future years - Fight of carrying forward and set off accrued to assessee - Right had accrued and continued to capable of being enforced notwithstanding repeal of statute under accrued unless repealing statute took away such right expressly or by necessary implication - Appeal dismissed.
