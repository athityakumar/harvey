Tara Devi v Sri Thakur Radha Krishna Maharaj

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28427; (1987) 4 SCC 69; AIR 1987 SC 2085; JT 1987 (3) SC 258; 1987(2) SCALE 232; 1987 (2) UJ 329

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Suit for declaration

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Suit for declaration - Maintainability - Respondent plaintiff filed suit for declaration and recovery of possession with mesne profits that pattas executed by X in the name of defendant were illegal, ineffective and not binding on plaintiff - Suit was decreed by Trial Court - Revision petition was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant petition - Whether the order recorded by HC was maintainable.

Held, that the valuation is arbitrary, unreasonable and the plaint has been demonstratively undervalued, the court can examine the valuation and can revise the same. The plaintiff has valued the leasehold interest on the basis of the rent. Such valuation, as has been rightly held by Courts below, is reasonable and the same is not demonstratively arbitrary nor there has been any deliberate underestimation of the reliefs. There was no reason to grant special leave to appeal asked for in petition as order passed in said revision is unexceptionable. Petition dismissed.
