Municipal Corporation Of Greater Bombay A v Dr. Hakimwadi Tenants Association And Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28799; (1988) Supp SCC 55; AIR 1988 SC 233; 1988 (1) Bom.C.R. 578; JT 1987 (4) SC 448; 1988 MahLJ 1; 1988 MLJ 1; 1987(2) SCALE 1133; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 21

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land and Property - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 126 and 127 - Land Acquisition Act , 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Whether the period of six months specified in s. 127 of the Act is to be reckoned from the date of service of the purchase notice by the owner on the Planning Authority, or the date on which the requisite information of particulars furnished by the owner - Failure by Authority to take steps for acquisition of land within six months - Held, reservation has lapsed, acquisition upon such reservation is bad and the delay in filing the petition, such as it is, can make no difference to this position in law - Appeal dismissed.
