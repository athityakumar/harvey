P.D. Aggarwal And Ors. v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 June 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28357; (1987) SCC (L&S) 310; (1987) 3 SCC 622; AIR 1987 SC 1676; 1987 (4) ATC 272; 1987 (55) FLR 404; JT 1987 (2) SC 606; 1987 LabIC 1307; 1987 (2) LLN 876; 1987(1) SCALE 1283; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 427; 1987 (3) SLJ 113; 1987 (4) SLR 134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - U.P. Service of Engineers (Buildings and Roads Branch) Class II Rules, 1936, rr.3 (c), 5, 6 and 23 - Uttar Pradesh Service of Engineers (Buildings and roads Branch) Class H (Amendment) Rules 1971 - Service Rules Amendment - Appointment substantively to temporary post - Seniority - (A) Determination of Constitutionality of amended r. 23 as amended by the Amendment Rules of 1971 - (B) Temporary Asstt. Challenging seniority list for 1971 by way of writ petition - Direct appointees pleaded undue delay and laches - (C) Whether Rules are ammendable of superseded by office memorandum - Held, is arbitrary and rr.3 (c), 5, 6 are also violative of arts. 14, 16 of Constitution - Plea has no merit; Asstt. Engineers appointed to temporary posts prior to the amendment of Rules are entitled to have their seniority reckoned from the date of appointment - Rules are statutory and cannot be amended or superseded by office memorandum which is nothing else but administrative order - Appeal dismissed.
