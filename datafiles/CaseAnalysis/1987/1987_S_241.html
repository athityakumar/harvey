Kan Singh and Others v State Transport Appellate Tribunal And Others, Etc

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28787; (1987) Supp SCC 671; AIR 1988 SC 18; 1987 (2) ALT 316; 1988 CRLR 21; JT 1987 (4) SC 185; 1987(2) SCALE 838; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 47(1H) and 58(2) - Principles of natural justice flouted - Seperate applications filed by private and state transport corporations for renewal of inter state permit - Applications of private companies was rejected unheard by regional transport authority (RTA) - Court held that principle of natural justice 'audi alterm partem' was violated as the petitioners were not given a fair chance and it is clearly the duty of the RTA to consider the evidence placed by both the parties, allow each party an opportunity to rebut the material placed by the other and then arrive at its decision - Appeal allowed.
