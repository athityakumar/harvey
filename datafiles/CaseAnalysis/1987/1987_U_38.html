Yogender Pal Singh And Others v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28122; (1987) 1 SCC 631; AIR 1987 SC 1015; 1987 (3) ATC 28; 1987 (1) CCC 745; 1987 (54) FLR 225; JT 1987 (1) SC 227; 1987 LabIC 707; 1987 (1) LLJ 337; 1987 (1) LLN 749; 1987(1) SCALE 175; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 49; 1988 (1) SLJ 7; 1987 (1) SLR 379; 1987 (1) UJ 569

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Delhi Police (Appointment and Recruitment) Rules, 1980, Punjab Police Service Rules 1934, Delhi Police (Appointment and Recruitment) Rules 1980

Summary: Service - Delhi Police (Appointment And Recruitment) Rules, 1980 - Delhi Police Act, 1978 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Recruitment of constables - Rule granting preference in favour of sons and near relatives of persons serving in Police service - The rr. 12.14 and 12.15 of 1934 Rules repealed from date of promulgation of 1980 Rules - But r.32 of 1980 Rules has no effect of reviving back these rules - (A) Determination of its effect - (B) Whether DCP has power as to relaxation in qualifications - (C) Constitutionality of the Rule also challenged - Held, sons and wards of Delhi policemen cannot now claim preference in matter of recruitment - Can be made by Administrator i.e. Lt. Governor of UT of Delhi and not by DCP - Rule is unconstitutional - However, make it clear that this judgment shall not affect the appointments of sons or wards of police officers already made by relaxing the Rules and they shall remain undisturbed - Appeal dismissed.

.
