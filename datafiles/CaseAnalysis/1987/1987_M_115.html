Mohan Lal (Dead) By Lrs v Kartar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28643; (1987) Supp SCC 485

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Pepsu Tenancy And Agricultural Act, s. 43(1) - Tenants alleged that first respondent was in wrongful or unauthorised possession of lands previously occupied by them - Also alleged, but in order that jurisdiction of collector to hold a summary enquiry and to pass order complained of may be attracted, it was further necessary to establish that u/s. 43(1)(b) person in wrongly or unauthorised possession was not entitled to use and occupation of lands under provisions of Act - Held, collector has been invested with power to eject unlawful occupants u/s. 43(1)(b) - Certainly, no express provision providing that a person in unlawful occupation will not have a right to continue in possession is necessary - By very nature of his occupation being found to be unlawful, he has no legal right to continue in occupation of land - It is implicit in s. 43(1)(b) that an unauthorised or unlawful occupant has no right under said Tenancy Act to remain in possession - In view of fact that provisions constitute a complete code and are specially enacted to protect tenants without obliging them to resort to time-and-money-consuming civil suit, decision in Ardaman Singh case, 1968 Indlaw SC 328 requires reconsideration - Order accordingly.
