V. Bhagat v Mrs. D. Bhagat

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28597; (1987) Supp SCC 318; 1987(1) SCALE 576A

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, s. 13 - Divorce - Adultery, cruelty and desertion - Striking out pleadings by High Court - Whether appellant is aggrieved by striking out of pleadings and whether he would be prejudiced in supporting his prayer for relief of divorce? - Held, though application is unnecessarily long and more descriptive than necessary for purposes of pleading, to analyse pleadings at this stage to find out which portion should be kept and which portion deleted is an exercise which from one angle is unnecessary and from other may indeed by prejudicial - Therefore, it is direct that all paragraphs in plaint shall stand restored and impugned order of HC shall stand vacated - Respondent allowed a month's time to file written statement - Further held, scheme of Hindu Marriage Act indicates legislative policy that cases under it have to be expeditiously disposed of - Therefore, learned Chief Justice of Delhi High Court requested to nominate a learned Judge to take up matter and dispose it of in accordance with law as expeditiously as possible - Appeal disposed of.
