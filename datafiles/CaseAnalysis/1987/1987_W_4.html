Workers Of M/S Rohtas Industries Ltd v M/S Rohtas Industries Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28750; (1987) SCC (L&S) 124; (1987) 2 SCC 588; 1987 (1) CCC 1062; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 872; 1987 (2) CompLJ 125; 1987 (55) FLR 340; JT 1987 (2) SC 283; 1987 (2) LLJ 1; 1987 (2) LLN 8; 1987(1) SCALE 894; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1216; 1987 (2) UJ 318

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Bihar Relief Undertakings (Special Provisions) Act, 1981 - Is liability of industry for payment to workers enforceable in light of other claims laid? - Held, it would be just and proper that stock lying be sold and out of sale proceeds workers be paid their dues upto date of closure so that at least they will get something for subsistance - So far as pledge and priority of financial institutions are concerned, they have other sufficient securities and properties of Co. and if stock of finished products are sold to meet basic requirements of workers, their interests would not be in jeopardy - If loss of amount of Rs. 91,77,000 comes in way of scheme of re-starting of industry, Govt. would find funds to save situation and help early revival of Company - Petition disposed of.
