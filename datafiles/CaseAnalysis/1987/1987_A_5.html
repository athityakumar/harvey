Begum Subanu Alias Saira Banu and Another v A. M. Abdul Gafoor

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28163; (1987) 2 SCC 285; (1987) SCC (Cr) 300; AIR 1987 SC 1103; 1987 CRLJ 980; 1987 CRLR 357; 1987 CrLR(SC) 357; 1987 (1) DMC 506; JT 1987 (2) SC 55; 1987(1) SCALE 672; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Maintenance - Whether a Muslim wife whose husband has married again or taken a mistress is entitled to claim maintenance from her husband? - Held, irrespective of husband's right under his personal law to take more than one wife, his first wife would be entitled to claim maintenance and separate residence if he takes a second wife - Two kinds of matrimonial injury to a wife, viz, by husband either marrying again or taking a mistress - Explanation places a second wife and a mistress on same footing and does not make any differentiation between them on basis of their status under matrimonial law - It will undoubtedly lead to a strange situation if it were to be held that a wife will be entitled to refuse to live with her husband if he has taken a mistress but she cannot refuse likewise if he has married a second wife - Otherwise, it will lead to discriminatory treatment between wives whose husbands have lawfully married again and wives whose husband have taken mistresses - Appeal allowed.
