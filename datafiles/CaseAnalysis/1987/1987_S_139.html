Vinod Kumar Arora v Smt. Surjit Kaur

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28320; (1987) 3 SCC 711; AIR 1987 SC 2179; JT 1987 (3) SC 106; 1987 (2) RCJ 261; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 156; 1987 (2) RentLR 660; 1987(2) SCALE 60; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 552; 1987 (2) UJ 734

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: EP Urban Rent Restriction Act 1949

Summary: Rent control - (A) Code Procedure Code, 1908 - (1) When interference with concurrent finding of fact in revisional jurisdiction is justified - (2) Effect of variation between pleadings and proof - (B) East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 - (1) Effect of conversion of residential premises into non-residential premises without written consent of rent controller - (2) Plea of composite nature of tenancy given up during enquiry by tenant - Effect - (A) Held, justified, when findings suffer from inherent defects - Pleadings of parties form foundation of their case and it is not open to them to give up case set out in pleadings and propound a new and different case - (B) Tenant cannot get over embargo placed by s. 11 - Not permissible - Appeal dismissed.
