Union Of India v A.R. Shinde And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28244; (1987) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1987 SC 1004; 1987 (3) ATC 206; 1987 (54) FLR 470; JT 1987 (1) SC 487; 1987 LabIC 701; 1987 (1) LLN 763; 1987(1) SCALE 397; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 339; 1987 (2) SLJ 156; 1987 (2) SLR 375; 1987 (1) UJ 632

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Service - All India Radio (Recruitment of Director General, All India Radio) Rules, 1963, sch. col.10 and sch. Col. 2 - Violation of appointment rules - Recruitment for the post of director at AIR - Only through promotion, deputation and direct recruitment - On date, no candidate eligible for promotion - Finally, a person was appointed on deputation for six months and later extended for 2 years - Respondent on due date was short of eligibility criterea - Respondent contended that selection was not according to rules - Held, the initial appointment for six months by transfer on deputation could not be challenged by the respondent as not having completed 3 years service in the post of Addl. Director General and even on the date on which the proposal for extension of initial appointment was mooted for consideration none was eligible for being appointed by promotion - The very fact that extension was made two years shows that there was anxiety to fill up the vacancy ultimately by promotion which was the first preferential mode of appointment, if possible - Appeal allowed.
