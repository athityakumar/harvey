Sarwan Kumar Onkar Nath v Subhas Kumar Agarwalla

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28846; (1987) 4 SCC 546; AIR 1987 SC 2302; JT 1987 (4) SC 64; 1988 (1) RCJ 193; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 502; 1987 (3) RentLR 388; 1987(2) SCALE 723; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 414; 1988 (1) UJ 20

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bihar Building (Lease Rent and Eviction) Control Act 1977

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947 - Eviction of tenant on ground of non-payment of rent of 2 months - When two months rent paid in advance at time of taking lease whereas only one month's advance permissible u/s. 3 of Act - Held, eviction of tenant for default in payment of rent for two months without adjusting advance paid is illegal - Appeal allowed.
