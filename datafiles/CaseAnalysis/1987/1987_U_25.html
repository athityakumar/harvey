Ajmer Singh Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28268; (1987) 3 SCC 340; (1987) SCC (Cr) 499; AIR 1987 SC 1646; 1987 (2) Crimes 433; 1987 CRLJ 1877; 1987 CrLR(SC) 395; JT 1987 (2) SC 290; 1987(1) SCALE 953; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 84; 1987 (2) UJ 219

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Remissions

Summary: Criminal - Defence and Security Forces - CrPC, 1973, ss. 5 and 428 - Army Act, Chap. VI ss.34 to 68, chap. XIII ss.166 to 176, chap. XIV 179 to 190 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134A - Grant of benefit of the provision for set off - Determination of applicability of s. 428 of CrPC, 1973 to persons sentenced to undergo imprisonment by General Court-martial under the Army Act - Divergence of views between different High Court on this question - Distinction between 'trial by a court to which this code applies' and by a Court-martial - Held, have no applicability whatever in the respect of persons convicted and sentenced by Court-martial - Appeals dismissed.
