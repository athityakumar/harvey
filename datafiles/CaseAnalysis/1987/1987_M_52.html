Dr. Smt. Kuntesh Gupta v Management Of Hindu Kanya Mahavidyalaya, Sitapur (U.P) Andors

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28780; (1987) SCC (L&S) 491; (1987) 4 SCC 525; AIR 1987 SC 2186; 1987 (5) ATC 86; 1988 (1) CCC 29; 1987 (13) ECR 944; 1988 (56) FLR 44; JT 1987 (3) SC 670; 1987 LabIC 1901; 1987 (2) LLN 922; 1987(2) SCALE 667; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 357; 1987 (5) SLR 643; 1988 (1) UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Authorised Controller, Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act 1973

Summary: Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 68 - Dismissal - Review - Power of - Whether High Court was justified in dismissing writ petition of appellant on ground of availability of an alternative remedy? - Held, it is well established that an alternative remedy is not an absolute bar to maintainability of a writ petition - When an authority has acted wholly without jurisdiction, HC should not refuse to exercise its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution on ground of existence of an alternative remedy - In instant case, Vice-Chancellor had no power of review and exercise of such a power by her was absolutely without jurisdiction - Indeed, order passed by Vice-Chancellor on review was a nullity, such an order could surely be challenged before HC by a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution and, HC was not justified in dismissing writ petition on ground that an alternative remedy was available to appellant u/s. 68 of U.P. State Universities Act - As impugned order of Vice-Chancellor is a nullity, it would be a useless formality to send matter back to HC for disposal of writ petition on merits - Quash impugned order of Vice-Chancellor D/- 7-03-1987 and direct reinstatement of appellant forthwith to post of Principal of Institution - The judgment of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
