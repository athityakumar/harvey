Venkatlal G. Pittie And Anr v Bright Bros. (Pvt.) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28326; (1987) 3 SCC 558; AIR 1987 SC 1939; JT 1987 (3) SC 139; 1987 (2) RCJ 457; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 531; 1987 (2) RentLR 519; 1987(2) SCALE 115; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 593; 1987 (2) UJ 725

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13(1)(b) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 108(p) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Interference - Construction of permanent structures by tenant on demised premises in violation of s. 13(1)(b) of 1947 Act and s. 108(p) of 1882 Act - Finding as to, by Courts based on consideration of relevant factors and not perverse - Question raised in appeals is the jurisdiction of the High Court to interfere with the findings of this nature u/art. 227 - Held, findings cannot be interfered with; as tests laid down have not been transgressed by the Court of Small Causes and although a different view might have been taken but that is no ground which would justify the High Court to interfere - Appeal allowed.
