Sarup Chand v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28568; (1987) SCC (Cr) 385; (1987) 2 SCC 486; AIR 1987 SC 1441; 1987 (1) Crimes 818; 1987 CRLJ 1180; 1987 CRLR 242; JT 1987 (1) SC 592; 1987(1) SCALE 477; 1987 (1) UJ 687

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Appellant who patwari of village demanded money from complainant for providing certain documents relating to property of complainant - Charge of bribery against appellant - Whether conviction of appellants is proper? - Held, yes - No previous ill-will between complainant and appellant - Currency notes paid as bribe recovered from appellant - Conviction is proper - Sentence reduced to already undergone - Appeal dismissed.
