Subash Shiv Shankar v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28887; (1987) 3 SCC 331; (1987) SCC (Cr) 573; 1987 ACC 507; AIR 1987 SC 1222; 1987 AllCriC 507; 1987 CRLJ 991; 1987 CrLR(SC) 368; 1987(1) SCALE 838; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss.34, 300 and 302 - Evidence Act - Croc, 1973, s.154 - Murder by repeatedly stabbing deceased with knives and causing fatal injuries to him - Motive to settle scores with deceased for taking away machine without paying repair charges, a few days ago - (A) Evidentiary value of test identification parade - When witnesses identified the accused in first ID parade but in subsequent parade held after 4 months of the witnesses was able to identify him - Absence of his description in FIR and statements - His conviction is based solely with reference to his identification at ID parade - Held, he has to be given the benefit of doubt and acquitted in the light of our finding - (B) Whether inference of Ante-dating of FIR can be drawn - When FIR sent to Court not containing Magistrate's endorsements as to time of its receipt - Head constable that the special report was dispatched to Magistrate at the time of its receipt and the General Diary containing entry to that effect - Held no material to warrant an inference that Exhibit Kha 1 had been given later but ante-dated to cover up the delay in making the report - (C) Whether acquittal can be ordered - When acquittal of co-accused for want of proof of their identity but it was on his instigation that attack was launched - And eye-witnesses also knowing him - Held, he cannot escape conviction u/s. 302 r/w s.34 IPC when his participation with three other assailants in the attack has been established beyond reasonable doubt - Crl. Appeal No. 287/78 dismissed and Crl. Appeal No. 288/78 allowed.
