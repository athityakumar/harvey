B.K. Srinivasan And Another Etc. Etc. v State Of Karnataka And Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28527; (1987) 1 SCC 658; AIR 1987 SC 1059; JT 1987 (1) SC 180; 1987(1) SCALE 142; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 1054; 1987 (1) UJ 657

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Trade

Summary: Women and Children - Constitution of India, 1950,s.art 134A - CrPC, 1973, s.125 - Muslim Women (Protection Of Rights On Divorce) Act, 1986, s.3(b) - Maintenance - Determination of validity of refusal of maintenance to child on the ground of legitimacy - Child had been conceived even before marriage - Alleged suppression of the fact of her being enceinte at the time of the marriage - Child born within 7 months' time after marriage - Facts not conclusive that child is not legitimate - Possibility of access before marriage cannot be ruled - (B) Quashing of order of maintenance by HC - Whether, in an application u/s.482 CrPC the High Court can interfere with concurrent findings rendered by the courts below - Refusal of maintenance to child held, was improper - Held, proper course for the High Court, even if entitled to interfere, should have been to sustain the order of maintenance and direct the husband to seek an appropriate declaration in the civil court, after a full-fledged trial, that the child was not born to him and as such he is not legally liable to maintain it ; As proceedings u/s.125 CrPC, are of a summary nature and are intended to enable destitute wives and children, the latter whether they are legitimate or illegitimate, to get maintenance in a speedy manner - Appeal allowed.
