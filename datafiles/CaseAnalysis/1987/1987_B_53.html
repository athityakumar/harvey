Bhagat Ram Sharma v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28795; (1988) Supp SCC 30; (1988) SCC (L&S) 404; AIR 1988 SC 740; 1988 (6) ATC 783; JT 1987 (4) SC 476; 1987(2) SCALE 1097; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 1034; 1987 (5) SLR 713

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Punjab Re-organisation Act, 1966, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970, Himachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly (Allowances and Pension of Members) Act 1971, Punjab State of Legislature Members (Pension and Medical Facilities) regulation Act 1977, Punjab State Public Service Commission (Condition of Service) Regulations 1958

Summary: Service - Constitution, art. 226 - Himachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly (Allowances and Pension of Members) Act 1971 - Punjab Re-organisation Act, 1966 - Punjab State of Legislature Members (Pension and Medical Facilities) regulation Act 1977 - Punjab State Public Service Commission (Condition of Service) Regulations 1958 - State Of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - (A) Territorial jurisdiction - Writ Petition u/art. 226 against State of Punjab - Pension claimed, as ex.M.L.A. and/or Retired Member of State Public Service Commission - Concerned Legislative Constituency transferred to state of H.P. - No relief claimed against that State - (B) Regn. 8(3) of the Punjab State Public Service Commissions (Conditions of Service) Regulations, 1958 (as substituted in 1972) - Retrospectivity - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - 'Repeal' and 'Amendment' - Distinction between - Writ petition liable to be rejected as no cause of action arose in Punjab - Mere use of word 'substitution' does not imply that Regn. 8(3) relates back to 10-08-1972, the appointed day - Provision as substituted is not retrospectiveand operates from 10- 08-1972, the date of its enforcement - Appeal dismissed.
