Isha Steel Treatment, Bombay v Association of Engineering Workers, Bombay and

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28280; (1987) SCC (L&S) 91; (1987) 2 SCC 203; AIR 1987 SC 1478; 1987 (1) CCC 928; 1987 (54) FLR 454; JT 1987 (1) SC 548; 1987 LabIC 1028; 1987 (1) LLJ 427; 1987 (1) LLN 741; 1987(1) SCALE 442; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 414; 1988 (1) SLJ 68; 1987 (2) UJ 75

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25G and 25FFF - Retrenchment or closure - Firm carrying business of metal processing - Had two units with seperate functioning - Closing of one unit - Respondents contended that two units had functional integrality - Held, two units were not really integral as the other unit continued to function normally not withstanding closure of one of the two units and as such it could not be said that the other unit could not function after the closure of the unit - Appeal allowed.
