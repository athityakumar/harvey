Life Insurance Corporation Of India Andanother Etc. v S.S. Srivastava And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28276; (1988) Supp SCC 1; (1988) SCC (L&S) 375; AIR 1987 SC 1527; 1987 (54) FLR 750; JT 1987 (2) SC 529; 1987 LabIC 1039; 1987 (2) LLJ 414; 1987(1) SCALE 975; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 180; 1987 (5) SLR 188; 1987 (2) UJ 681

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Life Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1956, Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulation 1960, Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulation 1956

Summary: Service - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulation 1960 - Service Rules - Retirement Age - Constitutionality - Officers from insurance department released to join corporation by government - Fixation of their reirement age at 60 as per negotiations - Issue is - Grounds (A) Discrimination in fixation - With direct recuits recaruits and promotees whose whose retirement age is fixed at 58 - (B) Reasonableness and judicial notice of - (C) Age of superannuation - Fixation of different ages of retirement for Class III and, Class IV employees on one hand and Classes I and II Officers on other - (D) Discrimination - Retirement age for Classes III and IV employee joining service after 01-09-1956 and for Classes I and II Officers, same, i.e. 58 years when Class III employee was promoted as Class I Officer - Retirement age for Class III and Class IV employees raised to 60 subsequent to promotion of employee - (E) Classification of officers for purpose of retirement age between employees transferred from undertakings whose business was taken over and those appointed after 01-09-1956, i.e. appointed day - Held, not open to challenge - By itself cannot be considered to be arbitrary and court can take judicial notice of different ages of retirement in several services - By itself not violative of Arts. 14 and 16 - Employee cannot, after having accepted promotion and enjoyed benefits as such officer, complain of discrimination on ground that he would have benefit of retirement at 60 if he had remained in Class III - Not unreasonable and Regn. 19 (2) is not violative of Arts. 14 and 16 -
