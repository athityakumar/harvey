Election Commission Of India v Shivaji And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28429; (1988) 1 SCC 277; AIR 1988 SC 61; 1988 (1) CCC 418; JT 1987 (4) SC 298; 1987(2) SCALE 996; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 878

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election - Constitution, arts.327, 329(b) and 226 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 81 -(A) Election - Meaning of - For considering HC's jurisdiction u/art. 226 to entertain petition challenging election - (B) Writ Petition - Costs - Parties challenging notification fixing calendar of events for election to Legislative Council - HC interfering with election process once by postponing last date for withdrawal of candidatures and, secondly, by staying poll by entertaining review petition - Held, term 'election' in art.329(b) connotes entire process culminating in a candidate being elected and HC's jurisdiction is taken away - Held, entire proceedings amounted to abuse of process of court - Petitioners saddled with costs - Appeal allowed.
