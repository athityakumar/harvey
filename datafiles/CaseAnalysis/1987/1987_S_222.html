Hem Lall Bhandari v State Of Sikkim And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28124; (1987) 2 SCC 9; (1987) SCC (Cr) 262; AIR 1987 SC 762; 1987 (1) Crimes 393; 1987 CRLJ 718; 1987 CrLR(SC) 275; JT 1987 (1) SC 315; 1987(1) SCALE 188; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 78

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - National Security Act, 1980 - - Curtailment of personal liberty of person practising 'law'- (A) Whether the order of detention u/s. 3 is liable to be quashed on the ground that the mandatory requirements, u/s. 8(1) of the Act have not been complied with - Delay in communication of grounds of detention and absence of exceptional circumstances or satisfactory explanation - (B) Allegations of mala fides, against person placed in high position unsupported by acceptable evidence - Effect of his omission to file counter-affidavit - (A) Held, mandate enacted in the section is a safety valve for a citizen who is robbed of his liberty and to disable the authorities from manipulating the grounds of detention - Detention liable to be quashed (B) Omission is immaterial - Petition disposed of.
