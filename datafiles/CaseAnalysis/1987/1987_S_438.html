Salonah Tea Company Ltd v Superintendent Of Taxes Nowgong And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28826; (1988) SCC (Tax) 99; (1988) 1 SCC 401; AIR 1990 SC 772; 1988 (2) CompLJ 25; 1988 (67) CTR 45; 1988 (33) E.L.T. 249; [1988] 173 ITR 42; JT 1987 (4) SC 714; 1987(2) SCALE 1435; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 474; 1988 (69) STC 290

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Assam Taxation (on Goods Carried by Road or Inland Waterways) Act, 1961, ss. 7, 9 and 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 23, art. 113 - Refund of tax - Payment made under mistake of law - Competency of writ court to order refund - Assessment made and tax recovered without jurisdiction - Immediately after decision by High Court that recovery of tax was illegal, assessee filed writ petition - Whether entitled to refund? - Held, entitled to refund - Writ court competent to order refund - Appeals allowed.
