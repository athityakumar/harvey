State of Karnataka v Shivalingaiah Alias Handigidda

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28146; (1988) Supp SCC 533; (1988) SCC (Cr) 881; AIR 1988 SC 115; 1988 CRLJ 394; 1988 CRLR 734; JT 1987 (2) SC 226

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 304, 320, 325 and 323 - Respondent, accused of causing death by squeezing testicles resulting in instantaneous death and all this incident occurred in spur of moment - State challenging decision of High Court convicting him u/s. 323 and not u/s. 302 of IPC, 1860 - Held, act of accused in instant case would not amount to culpable homicide amounting to murder or not amounting to murder but act of squeezing testicles is dangerous to human life and it can lead to cardiac arrest, such an act would clearly be covered by s. 320 of IPC, 1860 and, therefore, amounts to grievous hurt punishable u/s. 325 of IPC, 1860 - Appeal allowed.
