Raj Kumar Dey And Others v Tarapada Dey And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28869; (1987) 4 SCC 398; AIR 1987 SC 2195; 1988 (2) ARBLR 151; 1987 (1) CalLT(SC) 58; 1987 (2) CCC 1074; JT 1987 (3) SC 555; 1987(2) SCALE 551; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 118; 1988 (1) UJ 241

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Indian Regulation Act 1908

Summary: Registration Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Award remained in custody of court up to certain date due to injunction and thereafter, due to court's refusal to return for purposes of registration is to be included - Held, as arbitrators could not take back award from custody of court to take any further steps for its registration entire period during which award remained in custody of court should be excluded and it could not be said that they failed to get award registered as law required i.e. within period of four months.
