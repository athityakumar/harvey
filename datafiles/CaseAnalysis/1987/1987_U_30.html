Messrs D. Navinchandra and Company, Bombay and v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28234; (1987) 3 SCC 66; AIR 1987 SC 1794; 1987 (13) ECC 1; 1987 (11) ECR 824; 1987 (29) E.L.T. 492; JT 1987 (2) SC 141; 1987(1) SCALE 790; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 989

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Trade - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s.3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art 141 - (A) Canalised item - Export House Certificates and additional licences granted to diamond exporters - They would be entitled to import items which were canalised or not if the import policy prevailing at the time of import permitted them to import items falling under such category - Determination of permissibility as to import ignoring canalisation process - (B) Evidence - Applicability of promissory estoppel - Held, not permissible- The doctrine of promissory estoppel is applicable against the Govt. in the exercise of its governmental, public or executive functions and the doctrine of executive necessity or freedom of future executive action cannot be invoked to defeat the applicability of the doctrine of promissory estoppel - Petition dismissed.
