Bank Of India v Vijay Transport And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28472; (1988) Supp SCC 47; AIR 1988 SC 151; [1988] 63 Comp Cas 428; JT 1987 (4) SC 389; 1987(2) SCALE 1028; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 961; 1988 (1) UJ 91

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Keywords: Cash Credit, Madras Agriculturists Debt Relief Act, 1938, Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Agriculturists Relief Act 1938

Summary: Banking and Finance - A.P. (Andhra Area) Agriculturists Relief Act,1938, s. 4(e) - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, ss. 1 and 3 - Constitution, art.136 - Interpretation of Statutes - Suit by nationalised bank for recovery of balance of three principal amounts severally advanced to a firm under CC A/c - Such banks are bound by s. 4(e) of 1938 Act - (A) Whether scaling down of debts due to Bank as provided by the ss. 4(e) and 13 of the A.P. act 1938, is necessary in the light of s. 3(27) (as amended by A.O. 1937) of the General Clauses Act ,1897 - (B) Whether a new Plea be allowed to be raised in the arguments for the first time before the SC, considering art.136 - (C) Validity of 1970 Act - Held, scaling down of debts is not necessary - Plea not allowed to be raised, as it involved question of fact to be pleaded and proved - The Act held to be quite legal and valid - Appeal allowed.
