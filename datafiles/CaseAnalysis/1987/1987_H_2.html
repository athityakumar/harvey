Shalimar Tar Products Limited v H.C. Sharma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28506; (1988) SCC (Cr) 60; (1988) 1 SCC 70; AIR 1988 SC 145; [1988] 64 Comp Cas 55; 1988 (3) CompLJ 232; 1988 (1) HLR 169; JT 1987 (4) SC 440; 1988 (1) RCJ 483; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 671; 1987 (3) RentLR 725; 1987(2) SCALE 1114; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 1023; 1988 (1) UJ 345

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(b), 16(2) and 16(3) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, s. 105 - Whether there was any sub-letting, if so was same with consent in writing of landlord? - Consent contemplated by s. 14(1), proviso (b) is a direct consent to a contemplated assignment to a particular assignee - Consent enjoined by bargain between parties in this case must be in writing and must be to specific subletting - Absence of word 'previous' in sub-s. (2) shows that it was not the intention of legislature that consent in writing should be obtained before sub-letting, it was necessary for tenant to obtain consent in writing to sub-letting

premises - Consent must be to specific sub-letting and must be in writing - Mere permission or acquiescence will not do - Held, exclusive possession was given to sub-lessee and tenant had transferred right to possess in that portion - If that is so, there was inevitable breach of covenant - There was no consent in writing on part of landlord to such sub-letting - It is clear that sub-letting was done without consent in writing of landlord - High Court was right in upholding order of Rent Control Tribunal and directing eviction of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
