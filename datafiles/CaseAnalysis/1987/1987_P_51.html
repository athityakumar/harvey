General Secretary, Bihar State Road Transport Corporation, Patna v Presiding Officer, Industrial Tribunal, Patna and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1987

Case Analysis

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28107; JT 1988 (1) SC 29; 1988 (2) LLJ 109

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Regularization

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Regularization - Justifiability - Appellant filed instant appeal - Whether it would be justified to make regular those employees who completed service for 240 days.

Held, following the decision in Daily Rated Casual Labour Employed under P And T Department Thr v Union Of India And Others 1987 Indlaw SC 28447; U.P. Income-Tax Department Contingent Paid State welfare Asso v Union Of India And Ors. 1987 Indlaw SC 28225, respondent was directed to prepare reasonable scheme for regularisation of the casual labourers who have been working for more than one year. Scheme will be prepared within 8 months from today. Respondent was directed to pay with effect from 1-1-1987 salary and allowances to the casual labourers at the rates equal to the minimum pay in the pay-scale of regularly employed persons in the corresponding cadres of the Corporation. All arrears payable pursuant to instant order shall be paid within four months from today. Appeal disposed of.
