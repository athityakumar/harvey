Union Of India And Ors. v N. Hargopal And Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28231; (1987) SCC (L&S) 227; (1987) 3 SCC 308; AIR 1987 SC 1227; 1987 (4) ATC 51; 1987 (55) FLR 602; JT 1987 (2) SC 182; 1987 LabIC 915; 1987 (1) LLJ 545; 1987 (2) LLN 20; 1987(1) SCALE 753; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 910; 1988 (1) SLJ 59; 1988 (1) SLR 5B; 1987 (2) UJ 41

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act, 1959 - (A) Whether an 'establishment in the public sector' or an 'establishment in the private sector' as defined in the 1959 Act may make appointments to posts to which the Act applies, of persons not sponsored by the Employment Exchanges - Act does not oblige any employer to employ those persons only which were sponsored by employment exchanges - (B) Whether the Act covers govt. establishments also - Held, Act is applicable to govt. establishments - Whether the instructions issued by the govt. that in the case of govt. departments the field of choice should, in the first instance, be restricted to candidates sponsored by the Employment Exchanges offend Articles 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Does not offend arts. 14 and 16 - Appeals and Petition disposed of.
