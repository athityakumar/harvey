S. B. Patwardhan and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 64; (1977) SCC (L&S) 391; (1977) 3 SCC 399; AIR 1977 SC 2051; 1977 LabIC 1367; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 775; 1977 SLJ 457; 1977 (2) SLR 235

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Indian Railway Establishment Manual

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14, 16 - Question of seniority in service - Whether departmental promotees and direct recruits appointed as Deputy Engineers in Engineering Services of Govts of Maharashtra and Gujarat belong to same class so that they must be treated with an even hand or whether they belong to different classes or categories and can justifiably be treated unequally? - Crux of grievance is not that they are denied promotion but that they are discriminated against in matter of seniority in comparison with direct recruits - If officiating Deputy Engineers belong to Class II cadre as much as direct recruits do and if quota system cannot operate upon their respective confirmation in that cadre, is there any valid basis for applying different standards to members of two group for determining their seniority - Though drawn from two different sources, direct recruits and promotees constitute a single integrated cadre, discharging identical functions, bear similar responsibilities and acquire an equal amount of experience in their respective assignments - Yet r. 8(iii) provides that probationers recruited during any year shall be senior to promotees confirmed in that year - Had there been some intelligible differentia bearing nexus with efficiency in public services, such classification might have been sustainable - Instead, r. 8(iii) leaves seniority to depend upon mer.e accident of confir.mation which intur.n depends neither on efficiency of incumbent nor on availability of substantive vacancies - That is impermissible u/arts. 14 and 16 - R 8(iii) rejects test of merit altogether - Thus r.8(iii) must be struck down as being unconstitutional - Civil Appeal No. 1113 of 1974 allowed - Civil Appeal No 286 of 1974 dismissed - Civil Appeal No 287 of 1974 dismissed - Civil Appeal No 242 of 1974 partly allowed - Civil Appeal No 285 of 1974 dismissed.
