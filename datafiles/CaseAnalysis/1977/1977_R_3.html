R. K. Malhotra, I.T.O.Group Circle Ii(1), Ahmedabad v Kastur Bhai Lalbhai (H.U.F.)

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 267; (1977) 3 SCC 519; AIR 1977 SC 2129; 1977 CTR 336; [1977] 109 ITR 537; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 289; 1977 UJ 559

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Municipal Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Estate Duty Act, 1953 - Income tax Act, 1961 - s.147,147(b) - Assessment - Deduction - Notice - Reassessment - Respondent/assessee was Hindu undivided family owning two house properties - Appellant/Income-tax Officer treated properties of respondent as self occupied properties - Respondent claimed deduction of municipal taxes u/s. 23 (2) of 1961 Act was allowed - Order of assessment was made by appellant and after lapse of over 3 years issued letter called upon respondent to show cause that deduction was wrongly allowed - Respondent replied that appellant was not competent to reopen assessment u/s. 147 and municipal taxes were validly allowed as deduction - Not satisfied with explanation, appellant issued notice to reassessment - Respondent filed W.P u/art.226 of constitution was allowed and notice was quashed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to reopen assessment u/s. 147(b) of 1961 Act - Held, during assessment, appellant was erroneously thought that assessee was entitled to deduction of municipal taxes - Audit Department , pointed out to appellant that on true interpretation of s. 23(2), deduction of municipal taxes in respect of self occupied properties was not admissible - On receipt of intimation from Audit Department, appellant issued notice u/s. 148 of 1961 Act - According to s. 147(b) Income tax Officer conferred to assess or reassess income that had escaped assessment - Hence, appellant was entitled to reopen assessment u/s. 147(b) of 1961 Act - Appeal allowed.
