State of West Bengal v Bejoy Kumar Bose and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 394; (1978) SCC (Cr) 78; (1978) 1 SCC 173; AIR 1978 SC 188; 1978 CRLJ 138; 1978 MLJ 250; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 200 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 420, 379, 466, 468 and 471 - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, ss. 4(2) and 5(1) - Complaint filed against accused persons - Cognizance taken by Special Court - Charge framed against accused persons - High Court quashed trial in its revisional jurisdiction - Whether special judge rightly took cognizance without examining complainant u/s. 200 , 1898? - U/s. 4(2) of Act, allotment by State Govt. to Special Judge of a case involving of scheduled offences vests necessary jurisdiction in Special Judge to proceed to trial and is, therefore, equivalent to that Court's taking cognizance of offence - S. 200, CrPC, 1898 in terms, comes into play after taking cognizance of offence by Magistrate - There is nothing in s. 5(1) of Act even after amendment in 1960 to compel Special Judge to comply with provisions of s. 200, CrPC, 1898 - Held, objection of accused persons on score of invalidity of cognizance taken by Special Judge clearly untenable - If Special Judge is satisfied on perusal of complaint that an offence under Act is clearly made out, no need to examine complainant u/s. 200 CrPC, 1898 and then issue process - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
