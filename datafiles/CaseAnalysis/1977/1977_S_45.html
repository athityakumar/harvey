Bihar Eastern Gangetic Fishermenco-Operative Society Limited v Sipahi Singh & Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.K. Goswami, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 49; (1977) 4 SCC 145; AIR 1977 SC 2149; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 375; 1977 UJ 586

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Government of India Act 1935, Indian Regulation Act 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 70 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(26) - Transfer of Property Act - Indian Registration Act, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950, Art. 299 - Issue - (A) If the writ petition filed by respondent No. 1 was maintainable? - (B) Whether the doctrine of promissory estoppel applied to the case? - (C) Was the writ of mandamus rightly issued by the HC? - Art. 299 requires that a contract made in the exercise of the executive power of the Union or of a State must satisfy three conditions (i) it must be expressed to be made by the President or by the Governor of the State; (ii) it must be executed on behalf of the President or the Governor and (iii) its execution must be by such person and in such manner as the President or Governor may direct or authorise - Failure to comply with these conditions nullifies the contract and renders it void and unenforceable - Respondent No. 1 could not invoke the doctrine of promissory estoppel as he neither deposited the amount required for execution of the lease nor was any 'Parwana' issued to him and the High Court rejected his plea that after the receipt of the Government order, he invested large amounts of money in purchasing boats etc. and had to enter into contracts with large number of employees whose services were needed for the Jalkar - There cannot be any estoppel against the, Government in exercise of its sovereign legislative and executive functions - For mandamus to issue compelling the authorities to do something, it must be shown that there is a statute which imposes a legal duty and the aggrieved party has a legal right under the statute to enforce its performance
