Union of India v Central India Machinery Manufacturing Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 406; (1977) SCC (Tax) 376; (1977) 2 SCC 847; AIR 1977 SC 1537; 1977 (6) CTR 220; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 437; 1977 (40) STC 246

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Contract & Commercial - Whether contract between UOI and Central India Machinery Manufacturing Co. Ltd. (Wagon and Structural Division) Bharatpur for manufacture and supply of wagons was contract of sale or a work contract and whether appellant liable to pay sales tax? - Contract for manufacture and supply of 258 BG Bogie covered BCX type wagons - This is largely question of fact, depending on terms of contract - No consolidated contract deed formally executed, but there are documents including correspondence between parties which embody terms and conditions - Held, conditions embodied in contract documents read as a whole, clearly show that property in materials purchased by company with assistance of Railway/Govt. does not pass to Railway - Substantially all raw materials required for construction of wagons before their used belong to Company and not to President/Railway Board - Contract was one of sale, and not of agency - Further held, appellant after having taken delivery of goods, was owner of goods and if it consumed same for its own purposes is was not doing so as agent but as owner which it was fully entitled to do - In this view of matter, quantities of petrol consumed by appellant for its own purposes would not constitute a sale so as to be exigible to sales tax - Use of Hispeedol and petrol by appellant for its own purposes is not exigible to sales tax and proceedings for imposing sales tax on appellant hereby quashed - Appeals allowed.
