Pushpa Devi v Commissioner Of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 97; (1977) 4 SCC 184; AIR 1977 SC 2230; 1977 CTR 348; 1977 HLR 598; [1977] 109 ITR 730; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 329; 1977 TAXLR 1396; 1977 UJ 580

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958,s 26(1) - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937,s.3(2) - Income-tax Act,1961,s 261 - Joint Hindu family - Separate property - Gift - Income - Appellant was a member of a joint Hindu family - Appellant in her individual capacity entered into a partners with her father-in-law - Firm owned two houses 'X' and 'Y' and separate profit and loss accounts used to be drawn up - Sum of Rs. 67,284.57 stood to the credit of appellant in books of 'X' - Hindu undivided family paid advance tax on the amount and filed its return in respect of that income - Appellant did not include that income in her return for her one-third share in the income of 'X' was assessable - Appellate Tribunal hold that Hindu female who was a member of undivided family could not, by an unequivocal expression of intention, impress her separate property with the character of joint family property no restriction could be placed on a female's fight to abandon her exclusive interest in favour of joint family - HC found that right of blending could be exercised only by a coparcener and since appellant, though a member of joint family was not a coparcener, she could not throw her separate property into the joint family stock - Whether Hindu female had right to blend her separate property with joint family property and whether Income of gifted property was liable to tax - Held, well settled that a Hindu coparcenary was a much narrower body than the joint family and it includes only those persons who acquire by birth an interest in the joint or coparcenary property - Hindu female was not a coparcener- Fundamental notions governing a Hindu joint family that a female member of joint family cannot blend her separate property, even if she was an absolute owner with the joint family property - Income of Rs.21,544 from 'X' was not assessable in hands of the Hindu undivided family on the basis that appellant had blended it with the joint family property - Appellant must be deemed to have made a gift to undivided family of which she was a member - Income of the property gifted to Hindu undivided family will be liable to be brought to tax in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
