A. P. M. Mayankutty and another v Secretary and another

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 18; (1977) SCC (L&S) 274; (1977) 2 SCC 360; 1977 LabIC 551; 1977 (1) LLJ 437; 1978 (1) LLN 37; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 937; 1977 SLJ 206; 1977 (1) SLR 488; 1977 UJ 254

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - LegislationsReferred :. Madras State and Subordinate Services Rules, r. 10(a)(i)(1) - Whether services rendered by the appellants u/r. 10(a)(i)(1) were treated by the Govt. as permanent? - Held, that Govt. assigned artificial dates for fixing the commencement of appellant probationary periods but such dates, though anterior to the dates of their actual selection by the PSC, were quite subsequent to the dates of their initial appointment - This shows that the services rendered by the appellants u/r. 10(a)(i)(1) were treated by the Govt. which appointed them as a matter of stop-gap, emergency of fortuitous arrangements - Cl. (v) of r. 10(a) provides that a person appointed u/cl. (i) shall not be regarded as a probationer, that he is not entitled by reason only of such appointment to any preferential claim to future appointment to the service and that the services shall be liable to be terminated at any time without notice and without assigning any reason - Appeal dismissed.
