Tata Engineering & Locomotive Co. Ltd., Bombay v The Registrar Of The Restrictive Tradeagreement, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 152; (1977) 2 SCC 55; AIR 1977 SC 973; [1977] 47 Comp Cas 520; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Tort

Summary: MRTP Act, 1969, ss. 2(o), 10(a)(iii), 33(1) and 38(1) - Monopolies and Restrictive Trades Commission Regulation, 1974, Regn. 55 - Definition of restrictive trade practice is exhaustive and not inclusive - The primary question is - Whether an agreement with dealers allocating territories within which they can sell products of the company constitute a 'restrictive trade practice' - Ancilliary issues are - (A) Contents of an application u/s. 10 (a) (iii) of the Act - (B) Considerations while determining whether a trade practice is restrictive, are to be stated - (C) Whether the categories mentioned in u/ss.33 (1) are statutory examples of restrictive trade practices in the light of s. 2(o) - (D) Whether agreement allocating particular territory for operation by dealer, are registerable u/ss. 2(o) and 38(1) - (E) Is an agreement for exclusive dealings , restrictive in nature - Agreement does not amount to a restriction in competition - And - Necessity of statement of facts under Reg. 55 - Considerations stated - Not statutory examples - Registrable only if found restrictive u/s. 2 (o) - Held not restrictive trade practice and also not prejudicial to public interest - Appeal Allowed.
