Narendra Madivalapa Kheni v Manikrao Patil & Ors

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 93; (1977) 4 SCC 16; AIR 1977 SC 2171; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 193

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 s. 123(7) - Illegitimate votes - Declared elected - Challenged - Appellant and respondent no.1 filed their nominations in conformity with law and after polling , appellant was declared elected - Respondent no.1 found illegitimate votes casted in favour of appellant and electors were ineligible to figure on electoral roll but had been surreptitiously introduced therein by collusion, fraud and other improper machinations in which returned candidate and returning officer were collaborative actors - Respondent no.1 filed petition before HC to set aside poll verdict u/s. 123(7) of the Act which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the result of election has been materially affected.

Held, that corrupt practice, u/s. 123(7) of the Act, has not been established, but officials must realize and to the highest in administration that the proviso u/s. 123(7) of the Act does not authorise, out-of-the-way doings which are irregular and a wrong does not become right if the law slurs over it. Finding by HC that an influential candidate had interfered with officials to adulterate an electoral roll is vacated and warn that the civil services have a high commitment to the rule of law, regardless of covert commands and indirect importunities of bosses inside and outside government. The courage and probity of the hierarchical election machinery and its engineers, even when handsome temptation entices or huffy higher power browbeats, is the guarantee of electoral purity. Thus, such aspersions against public servants affect the integrity and morale of the services but where the easy virtue of an election official or political power-wielder has distorted the assembly-line operations, he will suffer one day. Hence, direct the HC to send for the ballot papers and pick out the 16 ballots relating to the Board members, examine them without exposing the identity of the persons who have voted and to whom they have voted and record a tally excluding these 16 tainted votes from the respective candidates. If the resultant-balance-sheet shows that the appellant has polled less valid votes than the respondent no.1, his election will be set aside and the respondent no.1 declared duly elected and if, on the other hand, despite these deletions the appellant scores over the respondent no.1, his return will be maintained. Appeal disposed of.
