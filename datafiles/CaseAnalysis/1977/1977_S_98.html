Mohammad Giasuddin v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 83; (1977) 3 SCC 287; (1977) SCC (Cr) 496; AIR 1977 SC 1926; 1977 CRLJ 1557; 1977 CrLR(SC) 285; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 153; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: White Collar Crime

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 248 (2) - Age of appellant 28 years old with two University degrees - Appellant along with another accused, deceived desperate unemployed young men, by false pretences that they would secure jobs for them - Found guilty under s. 420, IPC - Sentenced to 3 years R I - Parents, brother and sisters dependant on him - Scope of s. 248(2), CrPC, 1973 in award of sentence - Provision under s. 248 (2), CrPC, 1973, provides opportunity to both parties to bring to notice of court facts and circumstances which will help personalize sentence from a reformative angle - Rule of thumb sentences of rigorous imprisonment or other are too insensitive to highly delicate and subtle operation expected of a sentencing judge - State has to rehabilitate rather than avenge - Opportunity contemplated by s. 248 (2), CrPC has a penological significance of far-reaching import, which had not been considered by Courts below - Sentence reduced on humanist grounds - Appeal partly allowed.
