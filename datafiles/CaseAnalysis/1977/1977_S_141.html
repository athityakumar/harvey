Dagdu and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 74; (1977) 3 SCC 68; (1977) SCC (Cr) 421; AIR 1977 SC 1579; 1977 CRLJ 1206; 1977 CrLR(SC) 261; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 164(3), 235(2), 360, 554 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 29, 133, 114 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 302 - (A) Appreciation of evidence of accomplice - Confessional statements made by accused - Duty of Court - Whether requires corroboration? - Held, no reliance can be placed on evidence of an accomplice unless that evidence is corroborated in material particulars, by which is meant that there has to be some independent evidence tending to incriminate particular accused in commission of crime - Rule of corroboration must be present to mind of Judge and that corroboration may be dispensed with only if peculiar circumstances of a case make it safe to dispense with it - (B) Effect of failure in compliance with s. 164(3),CrPC and High Court - Circulars - Whether renders confessions admissible? - Further held, failure to comply with s. 164(3), CrPC or with HC Circulars will not render confessions inadmissible in evidence - Relevancy and admissibility of evidence have to be determined in accordance with provisions of Indian Evidence Act- (c) Whether under s. 235(2), CrPC, hearing on question of sentence is mandatory? - Further held, imperative language of s. 235(2), CrPC, provides that after recording finding of guilt and order of conviction, Court is under an obligation to hear accused on question of sentence unless it releases him on probation of goods conduct or after admonition under s. 360, CrPC - (D) Power of Appellate court in case of non-compliance by Trial Court - Further held, whenever an appeal Court finds that mandate of s. 235 (2) for a hearing on sentence had not been complied with, it, at once, becomes duty of appeal court to offer to accused an adequate opportunity to produce before it whatever materials he chooses in whatever reasonable way possible - Appeals disposed of - Order accordingly.
