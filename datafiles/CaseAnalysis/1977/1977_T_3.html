Bhaba Nanda Sarma and Others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.K. Goswami, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 130; (1977) 4 SCC 396; (1977) SCC (Cr) 602; AIR 1977 SC 2252; 1977 CRLJ 1930; 1977 CrLR(SC) 472; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 714; 1977 UJ 725

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s.114 - Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 38 and 302 - Murder case - (A) Evidence - Adverse inference - Prosecution failed to explain injuries on person of one of the accused - Whether benefit of doubt can be claimed - (B) Common intention - (1) Proof - (2) Applicability of s. 38 when victim assaulted by three accused - Held, accused not entitled to benefit of doubt on ground that the prosecution had failed to explain injuries on person of one of the accused - It must be established beyond any shadow of doubt that the criminal act was done by several persons in furthernance of the common intention of all - On facts and circumstances of the case held that liability of the two accused was established with the aid of s. 34 of IPC, 1860 - Appeal Partly Allowed.
