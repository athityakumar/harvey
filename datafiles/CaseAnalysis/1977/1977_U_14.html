 State of Rajasthan and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta, N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 272; (1977) 3 SCC 592; AIR 1977 SC 1361; 1978 (1) SCJ 78; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971, Constitution (Thirty-Eighth Amendment) Act, 1975

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32,131, 356 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Representation of the People Act, 1951- Unconstitutional - Limitations - Ultra vires - Discretion - Defendant/Union Govt. issued letters to 6 States. to dissolve Legislative Assembly or impose Presidential Rule u/art. 356 of Constitution - Plaintiffs/States. contended letter issued by Govt./Home Minister was unconstitutional, illegal and ultra vires Constitution as art. 356 of Constitution contain express limitations on functions of Governor which were to be exercised by President u/art. 356(1) (a) of Constitution - Plaintiffs claimed for declaration that they were not legally obliged to comply with directive contained in letter - Plaintiffs initiated suits and petitions for permanent and interim injunction - Preliminary objections were taken to maintainability of suits - Whether letter issued by defendant was illegal and ultra vires constitution - Held, Constitution was wedded to democratic pattern of Govt. - If State acts in undemocratic fashion, it could not be said Govt. of State was carried on in accordance with provisions of Constitution - It was clear that art. 356 of Constitution appears to had prima facie satisfied and allegations of plaintiffs and petitioners was not tenable - Court could not substitute its discretion for that of President nor to play role of advisor and it was beyond judicial review - Hence, art. 356 of Constitution did not contain express or implied limitations on functions of Governor which were to be exercised by President u/art. 356(1) (a) of Constitution - Petitions disposed of.
