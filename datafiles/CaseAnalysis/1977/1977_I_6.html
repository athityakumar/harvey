State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Indian Hume Pipe Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 40; (1977) 2 SCC 724; AIR 1977 SC 1132; 1977 (6) CTR 171; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 120; 1977 (39) STC 355; 1977 TAXLR 1871; 1977 UJ 219

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Notification No. ST 1363/X 1045/1960 D/- 5-4-1961 - Hume pipes "sanitary fittings" - (A)Whether or not hume pipes which are subject-matter of present case amount of "sanitary fittings" as contemplated by a notification issued by Govt. under Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act? - Held, as there was absolutely no material before Sales Tax Officer to show that any of hume pipes manufactured and sold by respondent were meant for use in lavatories, urinals or bathrooms and, in fact, material was used entirely other way, Sales Tax Officer was not at all justified in holding that they were sanitary fittings - (B) Whether High Court was competent to entertain present writ petition where alternative remedy is available? - Held, there is no rule of law that HC should not entertain a WP where an alternative remedy is available to a party - Further held, HC ought not to have entertained WP and should have allowed assessee to avail of the remedies provided to him under U.P. Sales Tax Act, particularly when questions of fact had to be determined - Appeal dismissed.
