Santuram Khudai v Kimatrai Printers and Processors Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 22; (1978) SCC (L&S) 38; (1978) 1 SCC 162; AIR 1978 SC 202; 1978 (36) FLR 82; 1978 LabIC 1; 1978 (1) LLJ 174; 1978 (1) LLN 315; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 387; 1978 UJ 13

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946, ss. 27 A, 32, 33, 33 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art.227 - Whether appellant has the locus standi to act and appear before the proceedings under the Act? - Held, provisions of Act are designed to emphasize that if labour in an industry is organised under Act - Union which can appear and do all acts and agitate matters in its representative capacity for labour - If the Union does choose to appear or act then no individual employee is competent to appear and present his point of view - S. 27 A provides that no employee shall be allowed to appear or act in any proceeding under Act, except through representative of employees, only exceptions to this being provisions of ss. 32 and 33 - Neither appellant nor his other co-employees had any locus standi to appear or act as individual employees in the proceedings initiated by respondent No 1 in which respondent No 2 which representative-union in industry in local area had right to appear and act as their representative of employees in industry and did appear or act as such - Appeal dismissed.
