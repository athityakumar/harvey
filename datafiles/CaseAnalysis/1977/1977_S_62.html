Gurcharan Singh and Others v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 410; (1978) SCC (Cr) 41; (1978) 1 SCC 118; AIR 1978 SC 179; 1978 CRLJ 129; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 358; 1978 TAXLR 129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 439(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B r/w s. 302 - Criminal conspiracy to - Murder a person in police custody - Held, grave apprehension of witnesses being tampered with by accused persons on account of their position and influence which they wielded over witnesses - S. 439 of new code confers special powers on HC or Court of Session regarding bail and court may direct any person who has been released on bail to be arrested and committed to custody - At stage of investigation unless there are some materials to justify an officer or court to believe that there are no reasonable ground for believing that person accused of or suspected of commission of such an offence has been guilty of same, there is a ban imposed u/s 437(1), CrPC against granting of bail - If to either officer in charge of police station or to court there appear to be reasonable grounds to believe that accused has been guilty of such an offence there will no question of court or officer granting bail to him - Principle underlying s. 437 is towards granting of bail except in cases where there appear to be reasonable grounds for believing that accused has been guilty of an offence punishable with death or imprisonment for life and also when there are other valid reasons to justify refusal of bail - Appeal dismissed.
