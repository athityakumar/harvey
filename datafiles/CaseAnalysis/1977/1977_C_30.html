Travancore-Cochin Chemicals Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Hans Raj Khanna, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 223; (1977) 2 SCC 20; (1977) SCC (Tax) 258; AIR 1977 SC 991; 1977 CTR 148; [1977] 106 ITR 900; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 715; 1977 UJ 136

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Capital expenditure - Laying of roads - Assessee sought to deduct amount spent on laying road, which was shared along with Govt. and other companies, from its total income treating the same as revenue expenditure but ITO and appellate authority disallowed the same - However, Tribunal held that assessee was entitled for deduction as revenue expenditure and later referred matter to HC - On reference, HC held that amount contributed was capital expenditure and hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether money contributed by assessee for construction of new road to improve transport facilities in area it is located, is capital expenditure or revenue expenditure.

Held, by having the new road constructed for the improvement of transport facilities, assessee acquired an enduring advantage for its business. Hence, expenditure incurred by the assessee was of a capital nature. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - When expenditure is made with view to bringing into existence asset or advantage for enduring benefit of trade, then such expenditure should be attributed to capital.
