Heckett Engineering Company v Workmen

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 118; (1977) 4 SCC 377; (1977) SCC (L&S) 552; AIR 1977 SC 2257; 1977 CRLJ 1843; 1977 (35) FLR 422; 1977 LabIC 1843; 1978 (1) LLJ 23; 1978 (1) LLN 5; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 693; 1978 SLJ 194; 1977 UJ 706

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, items 1 and 2 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 16 - Industrial Dispute - Interpretation of Statutes - Dismissal - Appeal by special leave - Findings by tribunal on evidence on record - (A) Interference - (B) Competency of Plant Manager, u/cl. 5(b) r/w cl. 7(d) of Standing Order - (C) Construction of provision - (D) Power to appoint implies power to dismiss - Held, no interference - Competent - Should not violate phraseology in provision - Firmly established that power to terminate service is a necessary adjunct of power of appointment and is exercised as an incident to or consequence of that power - Appeal allowed.
