Chairman, Board of Mining Examination and Another v Ramjee

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 181; (1977) SCC (L&S) 226; (1977) 2 SCC 256; AIR 1977 SC 965; 1977 (34) FLR 381; 1980 (1) LLN 284; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 904; 1977 UJ 184

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Coal Mines Regulations, 1957, regn. 26 - Respondent was a shot-firer in a colliery and being a risky, technical job, had to possess a certificate for it - He handed over an explosive to an unskilled hand who fired it, an accident occurred and one person was injured -(A) Whether cancellation of shot-firing certificate was in violation of regn. 26? - Held, Board's power is independent and is ignited by report of Regional Inspector - There is an overall duty of over sight vested in Board to enforce observance of rules of safety - To invalidate Board's order because Regional Inspector did not suspend certificate is a fallacy - (B) Recommendation by Regional Inspector for cancellation without suspension of concerned officer - Held, assuming it to be necessary, here respondent has, in form of an appeal against report of Regional Inspector, sent his explanation to Chairman of Board - He has thus been heard and compliance with regn. 26, in circumstances, is complete - Recommendations are not binding but are merely raw material for consideration - (C) Principle of natural justice - Unnatural expansion of natural justice, without reference to administrative realities and other factors of a given case, can be exasperating - Held, where there is no surrender of judgment by Board to recommending Regional Inspector, there is no contravention of canons of natural justice - Order accordingly.
