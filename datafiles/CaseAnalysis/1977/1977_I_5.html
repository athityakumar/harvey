Board Of Trustee Of The Port Of Bombay v Indian Goods Supplying Co

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, M. Hameedullah Beg, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 60; (1977) 2 SCC 649; AIR 1977 SC 1622; AIR 1995 SCW 1802; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 343; 1977 TAXLR 2026; 1977 UJ 302

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Water

Keywords: Charterparty

Summary: Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879, ss. 43, 43A and 43B, 43(a), 43B(1), 43B(1A) - Port Trust - Demurrage - Scale of rates - Force of law - Claim by Port Trust for delay in clearing goods not due to negligence of importer - D. O. Letter by Central Govt. to Bombay Port Trust - (A) Maintainability of claim - (B) Interpretation of Letter - (C) Meaning of 'Free days' u/s. III under the Scale - Held, caim cannot be resisted in absence of evidence - Lannguage indicated that Govt. wanted the Port Trust to consider her proposal and nothing further - Only omit sunday, holidays and days on which the assessment cannot be taken up and not include entire completion period - Appeal Allowed.
