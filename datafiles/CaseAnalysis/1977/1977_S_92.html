Palaniappa Gounder v State Of Tamil Nadu & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 190; (1977) SCC (Cr) 397; (1977) 2 SCC 634; AIR 1977 SC 1323; 1977 CRLJ 992; 1977 CrLR(SC) 193; 1978 (1) MLJ 13; 1978 (1) MLJ(SC) 13; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 132; 1977 UJ 339

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss.302, 392 - CrPC, 1973, ss.357(1)(c), 482 - Whether propriety and legality of fine imposed by HC is justified ? - Held, no - Amount of fine imposed is excessive - Legitimacy is not to be confused with propriety and fact that Court possesses a certain power does not mean that it must always exercise it - Though there is power to combine a sentence of death with a sentence of fine, that power in sparingly exercised because sentence of death is an extreme penalty to impose and adding to that grave penalty, a sentence of fine is hardly calculated to serve any social purpose - Apart from fact that even compensation was not fixed on any reliable data, HC put cart before horse in leaving propriety of fine to depend upon amount of compensation - Appeal partly allowed.
