T. Arivandandam v T. V. Satyapal and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 112; (1977) 4 SCC 467; AIR 1977 SC 2421; 1977 (3) ALR 704; 1978 (1) RCJ 33; 1978 (1) RentLR 91; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 742; 1977 UJ 697

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 35A, O. 7, r. 11 - Practice and Procedure - Rejection of plaint - False or vexatious claims - Duty of court and counsel - Held, Trial Court to see that the ground is fulfilled and should insist imperatively on examining the party at the first hearing so that bogus litigation can be shot down at the earliest stage - The counsel, as an officer of justice, can also contribute to the cause of justice by screening wholly fraudulent and frivolous litigation and by not collaborating in shady actions - Petition Dismissed
