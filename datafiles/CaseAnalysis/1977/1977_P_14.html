Pratap Misra and Others v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 358; (1977) 3 SCC 41; (1977) SCC (Cr) 447; AIR 1977 SC 1307; 1977 CRLJ 817; 1977 CRLR 233

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Abortion

Summary: Criminal - Rape - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Indian Penal Code, 1896, ss. 376, 452, 342 and 325 - Whether Sessions Judge as also High Court have completely overlooked some striking facts and glaring defects appearing in prosecution evidence which have vitiated findings of fact? - Held, courts below tried to examine possibility which was clearly suggested by evidence of prosecution itself that one or more of appellants may have had sexual intercourse with prosecutrix not against her will but with her consent and connivance of her husband PW 2 - Completely losing sight of fact that in a criminal case accused was not bound by his pleading and it was open to accused to prove his defence even from admission made by prosecution witnesses or circumstances proved in case - HC has not considered this aspect at all - Once case of rape which is closely connected with case of assault by A-2 on PW 4 is rejected, then fundamental part of case fails, and it would be unsafe to rely on allegation of assault divorced from circumstances on which prosecution relies - Prosecution has failed to bring home any of charges against appellants - Appeals allowed.
