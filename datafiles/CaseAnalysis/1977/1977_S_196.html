Madhya Pradesh State Road Transportcorporation, Bairagarh, B v Sudhakar & Ors. Etc

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 73; (1977) 3 SCC 64; 1977 ACJ 290; AIR 1977 SC 1189; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - (A) Assessment of damage - Compensation - Accident - Death of earning wife - Held, method of assessing damages, usually followed in England, is to calculate net pecuniary loss upon an annual basis and to arrive at total award by multiplying figure assessed as amount of annual dependency by number of years benefit was expected to last, taking into consideration imponderable factors in fixing either multiplier or multiplicand - Further held, in assessing damages certain other factors have to be taken note of, such as uncertainties of life and fact of accelerate d payment that husband would be getting a lump sum payment which but for his wife's death would have been available to him in driblets over a number of years - Thus amount assessed has to be reduced taking into account these imponderable factors - Husband may not be dependant on wife's income but basis of assessing damages would be similar - Upon consideration of a number of factors including expected earning span, fact that husband was not dependent on wife's earning and that he remarried within 11 months, amount arrived at by Tribunal found to be justified - (B) Accident - Victim boy aged about 4 years coming from well-to-do family - Held, possibility was there of deformity being removed by surgical operation when boy grew up to be 16 years, other possibility cannot be altogether ruled out - Sum awarded by HC need not be interfered with - Order accordingly.
