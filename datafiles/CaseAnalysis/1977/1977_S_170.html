State of Rajasthan, Jaipur v Balchand @ Baliay

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 105; (1977) 4 SCC 308; (1977) SCC (Cr) 594; AIR 1977 SC 2447; 1978 CRLJ 195; 1977 CrLR(SC) 476; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 535; 1977 UJ 650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bail - Entitlement of - Petitioner (accused) filed instant application for bail, having surrendered after leave was granted to the State to file an appeal against acquittal by the HC - Whether petitioner was entitled for bail.

Held, circumstances and the social milieu do not militate against the petitioner being granted bail at this stage and any possibility of the abscondence or evasion or other abuse can be taken care of by a direction that the petitioner will report himself before the police station once every fortnight. Therefore, petitioner will be released on bail after furnishing a bond of his own and one surety for Rs. 5,000/- to the satisfaction of the Trial court. Hence, petitioner is directed for furnishing of one surety for Rs. 5,000/-. Application allowed.
