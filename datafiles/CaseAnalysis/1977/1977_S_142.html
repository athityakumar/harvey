Eknath Shankarrao Mukkawar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 126; (1977) 3 SCC 25; (1977) SCC (Cr) 410; AIR 1977 SC 1177; 1977 (3) ALR 422; 1977 CRLJ 964; 1977 CrLR(SC) 352; 1977 MLJ 421; 1977 (2) SCJ 117; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Prevention Of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 2(i)(1), 16(1) and 16(1)(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 377 - Charge against appellant was that he sold chili powder which was adulterated inasmuch as percentage of total cash was more than permissible limit - Conviction of appellant u/s. 16(1)(a)(i) r/w 2(i)(1) and 7(i) of Act - Appeal against order of acquittal of father of appellant and against inadequacy of sentence awarded to appellant - High Court enhanced sentence to six months' simple imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1000 in default simple imprisonment for two months - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC was justified in a case of this nature to interfere with sentence when Trial Court had given adequate reasons for imposing lesser sentence? - HC power of enhancement of sentence under revisional jurisdiction - CrPC, 1973, has not abolished HC power of enhancement of sentence by exercising revisional jurisdiction, suo motu - Provision for appeal against inadequacy of sentence by State Govt. or Central Govt. does not lead to such a conclusion - In an appeal u/s. 377 CrPC, HC may interfere with sentence if no reasons for awarding a lesser sentence are recorded by Magistrate - If reasons recorded by Magistrate are irrelevant, extraneous, without materials and grossly inadequate, HC will' be justified in enhancing sentence - Magistrate are not so grossly inadequate that HC was right in interfering with sentence in this petty case - (B) Award of lesser sentence by Magistrate - Validity of - Held, Prevention of Food Adulteration Act provides that when conviction is u/s. 16(1)(a)(i) for selling an adulterated article coming within definition of s. 2(i)(1) - Magistrate had jurisdiction under first proviso to s. 16(1) to award less than minimum sentence in this case by recording adequate and special reasons - (C) Precedents - Decision of Co-ordinate court - Binding effect - Held, when there is a decision of a co-ordinate court, it is open to Judge to differ from it but in that case only judicial alternative is to refer it to a larger bench and not to dispose off appeal by taking a contrary view - Appeal allowed.
