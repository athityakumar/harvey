Bir Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 416; (1977) 4 SCC 420; (1977) SCC (Cr) 640; AIR 1978 SC 59; 1978 ALJ 365; 1978 CRLJ 177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 378 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 307 - Enlargement of Supreme Court Jurisdiction Act - Murder - Appellants charged u/ss. 302/34, 307/34, 302 and 307 - Trial court after considering evidence led before it came to conclusion that prosecution has failed to prove case against appellants beyond reasonable doubt and accordingly acquitted appellants of all charges framed against them - On appeal, High Court reversed order of acquittal - Whether HC justified in reversing order of acquittal? - Witnesses highly interested - Timing of FIR doubtful - Rewriting found on general diary - Injuries on prosecution witness doubtful - No blood found at place of occurrence - Held, taking overall picture HC not justified in reversing acquittal order - Appeal allowed.
