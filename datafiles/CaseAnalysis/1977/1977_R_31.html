Rangaswami, The Textile Commissioner v Sugar Textile Mills Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 158; (1977) SCC (Cr) 386; (1977) 2 SCC 578; AIR 1977 SC 1516; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 825; 1977 UJ 605

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Summary: Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Cotton Textiles (Control) Order, 1948, cl. 20 - (A) Direction to manufacturer u/cl. 20 - nature of specification of period for which it will remain in operation - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of words 'may' when construes as 'must' or 'shall' - Held, is is obligatory - Word 'may' is capable of manning 'must' or 'shall' in the light of the context and where a discretion is conferred upon a public authority coupled with an obligation, the word 'may' which denotes discretion should be construed to mean a command - Appeal Dismissed.
