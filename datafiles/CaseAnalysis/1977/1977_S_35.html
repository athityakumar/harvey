Vice-Chancellor, Jammu University and another v Shri Dushiant Kumar Rampal

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 359; (1977) SCC (L&S) 329; (1977) 2 SCC 616; AIR 1977 SC 1146; 1977 (2) LabIC 1; 1977 (2) LLJ 1; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 43; 1977 SLJ 318; 1977 UJ 591

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Jammu and Kashmir University Act, 1965, ss. 48(2) and 51 - Jammu and Kashmir Universities Act, 1969, ss. 20, 51, 52, 13(4), 13(6), 52(1) and 52(4) - Suspension - Validity of - Whether Order of suspension made by Vice-Chancellor is a valid order or it suffered from any legal infirmities? - Respondent is governed by sub-s. (1) and not sub-s. (4) of s. 52 - If there is anything in Act of 1969 which was inconsistent with conferment of power of interim suspension on Vice-Chancellor, Statute 24(ii) could not be approved by Chancellor, because no Statute can be made which is in conflict with any provision of Act - General principle is that an employer can suspend an employee of his pending an enquiry into his misconduct and only question that can arise in such a suspension will relate to payment of his wages during period of such a suspension - Held, suspension was ultimately upheld by Supreme Court and it was held that respondent was not legally entitled to anything more than subsistence allowance actually paid to him, matter will be left to Chancellor to determine in his sole and absolute discretion as to whether any additional amount at all, and if so, what amount, may be paid to respondent for period of suspension ex gratia without any liability on part of University - Appeal allowed.
