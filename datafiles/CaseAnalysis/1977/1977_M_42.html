Pushpabai Purshottam Udeshi and others v Messrs Ranjit Ginning and Pressing Company Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 334; (1977) 2 SCC 745; 1977 ACJ 343; AIR 1977 SC 1735; 1977 (2) SCJ 442; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 372

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Municipalities & Local Governments - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 95(a) and 95(b)(i) - Whether the accident occurred due to the rash and negligent driving of the Manager of the company and whether the incident took place during the course of the employment of the driver? - There is no dispute that the car was under the management of the company's manager and that from the facts disclosed by PW 1 if the driver had used proper care in the ordinary course of things the car could not have dashed, the driver was acting in the course of his employment and the owner is liable - The car could not have gone to the rights extremity and dashed with such violence on the tree if the driver had exercised reasonable care and caution - It is for the respondents to prove that the incident did not take place due to their negligence, which they have not even attempted to do - Evidence on record is convincing to prove the case of rash and negligent driving set up by the claimants - Held, set aside the finding of the High Court that the act was not committed in the course of employment or under the authority of the master - Held, appellants decreed a favour to the extent of Rs. 27, 500 against the respondents out of which the liability of the insurance company will be restricted to Rs. 15, 000 - The appeal is allowed .
