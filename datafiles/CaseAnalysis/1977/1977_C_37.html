Smt. Tarulata Shyam and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 204; (1977) 3 SCC 305; (1977) SCC (Tax) 445; AIR 1977 SC 1802; 1977 CTR 275; [1977] 108 ITR 345; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 697; 1977 TAXLR 989; 1977 UJ 450

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6A)(e), 12(IB), 16(2) - Advances and Loans - Deemed Dividend - ITO treated Rs.2,72,703 as net dividend income in hands of assessee within meaning of s. 2(6A)(e) of the Act and grossed up amount u/s. 16(2) of the Act and gave credit for tax - On second appeal before Tribunal having failed with appeal before appellate authority, Tribunal dismissed appeal but later made reference to HC - HC held that tax was attracted at point time when said loan was borrowed by shareholder and it was immaterial whether loan was repaid before end of accounting year or not and hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether any payment by Company by way of advance or loan to shareholder is deemed dividend even if that advance or loan is subsequently repaid fully during relevant previous year in which it was taken.

Held, after deducting opening credit balance and some other items credited to assessee account, ITO found that in the previous year the assessee share-holder had received a net payment of Rs. 2,72,703 by way of loan or advance from the Company. The Company's business is not money lending and it could not be said that the loans had been advanced by the Company in the ordinary course of its business. Further, all conditions except fifth condition which is not applicable to instant case, are factually exist in instant case and so, all factual conditions for raising statutory fiction created by ss. 2(6A)(e) and 12(IB) are satisfied. Further, as the taxability of income is related to its receipt or accrual in previous year, the moment a dividend is received whether it is actual dividend declared by company or is deemed dividend, income taxable under residuary head, 'income from other sources', arises. The charge being on accrual or receipt the statutory fiction created by s. 2(6A) (e) and s. 12(IB) would come into operation at the time of the payment by way of advance or loan, provided other conditions are satisfied. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - S. 12(IB) could be invoked against shareholder if five conditions are satisfied, i.e, firstly, company should not be one in which public are not substantially interested within meaning of s. 23A in year in which loan was advanced, secondly, borrower should be shareholder as on date of loan advancement, thirdly, company possessed accumulated profit at date of loan, fourthly, loan should not have been advanced by company in ordinary course of business, and lastly loan should remain outstanding in relation to A/Y 1955-56.
