Krishan Murari Lal Sehgal v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 21; (1977) SCC (L&S) 312; (1977) 2 SCC 587; AIR 1977 SC 1233; 1977 LabIC 554; 1977 (1) LLJ 442; 1977 (2) LLN 15; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 956; 1977 SLJ 312; 1977 (1) SLR 498

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, art. 311(1) - Patiala and East Punjab States Union Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1953, rr. 4 and 6 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115(7) - Punjab Financial Commissioner's Office (State Services Class III) Rules, 1957 - (A) Violation of art. 311(1) of Constitution - Reorganisation of States - Transfer of assistant in Patiala and East Punjab States Union Secretariat to Punjab - Under 1953 Rules , appointing authority and punishing authority was the State Government which, under Pepsu General Clauses Act meant the Raj Pramukh - Dismissal order by Financial Commissioner (Revenue) a subordinate to the Governor, the Co-ordinate Authority - (B) 'Previous approval' - Subsequent ratification - The 1957 Rules promulgated on 28-02-1957 - Circular D/-11-5-1957 if amoumts to previous approval - Held, is invalid being in violation of art. 311(1), and liable to be struck down - Held, circular cannot amount to previous approval - Appeal Allowed.
