Union of India and Others v Bhanudas Krishna Gawde and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 283; (1977) SCC (Cr) 208; (1977) 1 SCC 834; AIR 1977 SC 1027; 1977 CRLJ 785; 1977 CrLR(SC) 94; 1978 MLJ 18; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Fema

Keywords: Emergency Provisions, Defence of India Act, 1962, Prison Act, 1894, Punjab Communist Detenus Rules, 1950, Constitution (Thirty-Eighth Amendment) Act, 1975, Defence of India Ordinance, 1962

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3, 5 and 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 21, 352, 358 and 359 - Whether in view of orders issued by President u/art. 359(1), petitions challenging executive action taken during emergency on ground that it is arbitrary or unlawful is maintainable? - Held, no - Art. 359(1) empowers the President to suspend the right of an individual to move any court for enforcement of such of the rights conferred by Part III of the Constitution as may be specified by the President in his order - In other words, while art. 358 proporio vigore suspends the fundamental rights guaranteed by art. 19 of the Constitution thus enabling the State during the period the proclamation of emergency is in operation to make laws in violation of art. 19 of the Constitution, 1950 and to take Executive action under those laws despite the fact that those laws constitute an infringement of the rights conferred by arts. 19 and 359(1) of the Constitution, 1950 does not suspend any fundamental right of its own force but authorises the President to deprive an individual of his right to approach any court for enforcement of any or all of the rights conferred by Part III of the Constitution - Further, right of any person to challenge any executive action taken during emergency on ground that it was arbitrary or unlawful has be negatived - Effect of Presidential Order suspending enforcement of fundamental right amounts to bar locus standi of any person to move court on ground of violation of a fundamental right - It is not open to anyone either to challenge validity of any law or any Executive action on ground of violation of a fundamental right specified in Presidential Order promulgated u/art. 359(1) of Constitution, 1950 - While a Proclamation of Emergency is in operation Presidential Order u/art. 359(1) can suspend enforcement of any or all fundamental rights - Art. 359(1) also suspends any pending proceedings for enforcement of such fundamental right or rights - Appeals dismissed.
