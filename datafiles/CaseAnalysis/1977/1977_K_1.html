K. K. Shrivastava and Others v Bhupendra Kumar Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 347; (1977) 2 SCC 494; AIR 1977 SC 1703; 1977 UJ 344

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Rules of Bar Council of Madhya Pradesh, r. 31 - Bar council election dispute - What High Court has overlooked that period of limitation prescribed by rules is 15 days and if WPs are to be entertained long afterwards it will stultify statutory provision? - Held, there is no foundation whatever for thinking that where challenge is to an 'entire election' then writ jurisdiction springs into action - On other hand circumstances of this case convince the Supreme Court that exercise of power u/art. 226 may be described as mis-exercise - HC fell into an error in entertaining petitions - Tribunal created by Bar Council takes note of fact that as considerable lapse of time has already occurred it must now move quickly to hear and dispose of election disputes - Appeals allowed.
