Juggilal Kamlapat v Pratapmal Rameshwar

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.C. Gupta, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 257; (1978) 1 SCC 69; AIR 1978 SC 389; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Written Contracts

Summary: Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 2(4), 18 and 23 - Evidence Act, 1972, s. 92 - Custom and usage - Sale of goods - As a custom, the pucca delivery orders , delivered as the documentory title of non-specific goods - Questions arise - Whether pucca delivery orders are documents of title - Does the title passes u/s. 23, by delivery of such orders - Question as to estoppel - Custom, if can be pleaded as an answer to s. 92, Evidence Act - Held, puccca delivery orders were not documents of title under 1930 Act - Title could not pass until the goods were ascertained in view of s. 18 - Precluded from raising the question of estoppel - Custom proved in one case should not be utilised to found facts in another - Appeal Dismissed.
