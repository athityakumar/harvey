Union Of India And Ors v Gujarat Woollen Felt Mills

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 87; (1977) SCC (Tax) 399; (1977) 2 SCC 870; AIR 1977 SC 1548; 1977 (1) E.L.T. 24; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 472

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, sch. 1, entry 21, Pre. - Excise Duty - (A) Woollen fabrics - Whether non-woven felts manufactured from woollen fibres by machine pressing and used for filtration in heavy industries are woollen fabric - (B) Interpretation of items in schedule - Rule as to - Held, are not 'woollen fabrics'' within Entry 21 - Held rule is that 'resort should be had not to the scientific or the technical meaning of such terms but to their popular meaning or the meaning attached to them by those dealing in them, that is to say, to their commercial sense' - Appeal Dismissed.
