Hiralal Mallick v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 266; (1977) 4 SCC 44; (1977) SCC (Cr) 538; AIR 1977 SC 2236; 1977 CRLJ 1921; 1977 CrLR(SC) 395; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Probable Consequence

Summary: Women & Children - Criminal - Indian Penal Code , ss. 83, 326 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Bihar Children Act, 1970 - Children Act, 1960- Whether presumption that child between age of ten and fourteen is doli incapax is rebuttable? - Held, child under 14 years is presumed not to have reached age of discretion and hence to be doli incapax - But presumption may be rebutted by strong and pregnant evidence of a mischievous discretion - For capacity to commit crime, do evil and contract guilt, is not so much measured by years and days as by strength of delinquent's understanding and judgment - No evidence as to whether he was under twelve, as conditioned by s. 83, IPC, was adduced and no attention to feeble understanding or youthful frolic was addressed - Prima facie inference of intent to endanger life of deceased with a sharp weapon stood unrebutted - Conviction u/s. 326, IPC, sustained - Appeal dismissed.
