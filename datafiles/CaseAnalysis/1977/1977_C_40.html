Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Andhra Pradesh v Trustees of H. E. H. Nizam's Family (Remainder Wealth) Trust

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 229; (1977) 3 SCC 362; (1977) SCC (Tax) 457; AIR 1977 SC 2103; 1977 CTR 306; [1977] 108 ITR 555; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Trust deed - Individual - By a deed of trust, Nizam of Hyderabad and Berar created family trust - Detailed provisions were made in deed regarding disposition of different units allocated to various beneficiaries - Beneficiaries specified in second schedule of deed appealed against their assessments of wealth tax on ground that each of them was entitled only to life interest in corpus of units allocated to them and they could not be assessed in respect of entire value of corpus - Contention was accepted by AAC - AAC set aside assessments and directed WTO to make fresh assessments - Result of making fresh assessments was that, value of remainder wealth escaped tax - WTO said remainder wealth should be assessed u/s. 21(4) the Act - Assessees appealed to AAC, which was accepted - In Tribunal, appeals filed by Revenue were dismissed - HC held that trustees were liable to be assessed as individual u/s. 3 of the 1957 Act - It was not permissible to tax trustees u/s. 3 of the 1957 Act ignoring provisions of s. 21 of that Act - HC held s. 21(1) of the 1957 Act was applicable - HC said Tribunal was not right in holding that deed created one single indivisible trust but there were several separate trusts created in favour of each of relatives mentioned in second schedule - Whether trustees were liable to be assessed u/s. 3 of the 1957 Act in the status of an individual and whether s. 3 should be considered as subject to s. 21 of the 1957 Act - Held, on a combined reading of ss. 3 and 21 of the 1957 Act assessment was to be made in accordance with provisions of s. 21 - Every case of assessment on trustee was to fall u/s. 21 and he could not be assessed apart from and without reference to provisions of s. 21 - First part of issue was answered in favour of Revenue - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Trust deed - Applicability of provision - Whether remainder wealth should be assessed in respect of each of several units or groups of units allocated in favour of beneficiaries u/s. 21 (4) or u/s. 21(1) of the 1957 Act - Held, remainder in respect of each set of unit or units allocated to respective relative specified in second schedule was liable to be assessed in hands of assessees u/s. 21(1) of the 1957 Act in same manner and to same extent as each beneficiary in respect of his determinate and known share in such remainder - Applicability of s. 21(4) in assessment of remainder was plainly excluded - Appeals dismissed.
