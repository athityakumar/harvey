Chander Kali Bai and Others v Shri Jagdish Singh Thakur and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 303; (1977) 4 SCC 402; AIR 1977 SC 2262; 1978 (1) RCJ 17; 1977 (2) RCR(Rent) 604; 1978 (1) RentLR 11; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 625; 1977 UJ 667

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, s. 12(1)(f) - Appeal from suit for eviction on ground of bona fide personal necessity - (A) New plea that business for which accommodation was required by plaintiff was not 'his business' within meaning of s. 12(1)(f) of Act - Held, where a claim has never been made in defence presented no amount of evidence can be looked into upon a plea which was never put forward - (B) Whether rented shop in which business was carried on should have been held to be an accommodation 'of his own in his occupation' within meaning of second part of s. 12(1)(f)? - Under Act, mere occupation of another premises is not sufficient - It must be his own or belong to landlord and he must be in occupation of same - Held, tenanted shop in mere occupation of plaintiff was not sufficient to deny him a decree for eviction against his tenant u/s. 12(1)(f) - (C) Whether decree for damages could be awarded from date of termination of contractual tenancy? - Held, decree for damages could be awarded only from date when an eviction decree was passed until delivery of vacant possession and not from termination of contractual tenancy - In absence of a decree of eviction the person in occupation of the accommodation continues to be a tenant and is not liable to pay any damages as his occupation is not unauthorised or wrongful even after the termination of the contractual tenancy - Appeal allowed.
