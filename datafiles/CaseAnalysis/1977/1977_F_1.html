India Pipe Fitting Company v Fakruddin M.A. Baker and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 67; (1977) 4 SCC 587; AIR 1978 SC 45; 1978 (1) RCJ 41; 1978 (1) RCR(Rent) 472; 1978 (1) RentLR 328; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 797; 1977 UJ 742

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Challenged - Respondent no.1 was landlord and appellant became tenant by purchasing goodwill and tenancy rights from original tenant 'X' - Respondent no.1 instituted suit in Trial court for eviction of appellant which was dismissed and appellate court affirmed order of trial court and dismissed appeal - Aggrieved respondent no.1 filed appeal before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent no.1 requirement is bona fide and reasonable as has been concurrently found by lower courts against respondent no.1 by appreciating entire evidence.

Held, after examining the reasons given by both the courts it is not possible to hold that the conclusions are 'perverse' or even that these are against the weight of evidence on record. It is a case of reasonably possible factual appreciation of the entire evidence and circumstances brought on the record - Therefore, HC arrogated to itself the powers of a court of appeal, which it did not possess under the law and has exceeded its writ jurisdiction. Hence, appeal is allowed and order of HC is set aside and order of Trial court and appellate court is restored.
