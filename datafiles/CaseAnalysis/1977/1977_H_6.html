Govind Prasad Chaturvedi v Hari Dutt Shastri And Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 159; (1977) 2 SCC 539; AIR 1977 SC 1005; 1977 (3) ALR 193; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 877

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Specific performance of contract - Failed to prove essence of time - Effect - Appellant/plaintiff and respondents/defendants entered into an agreement for sale of suit property on 24-3-1964 - Respondents failed to execute sale deed as agreed - Aggrieved by that, appellant filed suit for specific performance of said contract - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of appellant - On appeal, HC held that even if time was not held to be of essence of contract it was of opinion that appellant was not entitled to decree for specific performance as he had failed to prove that he was ready to perform his part of contract - HC granted certificate u/art. 133(1) (a) of the Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC was in error in holding that time was of the essence of contract and that HC's finding that appellant was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract while respondents were always ready to perform their part of contract was opposed to oral and documentary evidence and probabilities of case - (A) Whether time was of the essence of contract - Held, SC was satisfied that HC was in error in allowing respondents to raise this question in absence of specific pleadings or issues raised before Trial Court and when the case of time being the essence of contract was not put forward by respondents in Trial Court - Apart from the absence of pleadings SC did not find any basis for the plea of respondents that time was of essence of contract - (B) Whether appellant was always ready and willing to perform his part of contract - Held, parties were suspecting each other and nothing would have been achieved by appellant by going in person and requesting respondents to execute sale deed - In fact respondents set up story that appellant approached respondents and stated that he was not able to perform his part of contract within stipulated time - This evidence could not be accepted taking into account relationship between parties - On considered evidence and correspondence between parties and it had no hesitation in accepting the conclusion reached by Trial Court that appellant was always ready and willing to perform his part of contract and that respondents were evading their responsibility - Respondents failed to establish that time was of essence of contract and that appellant had succeeded in establishing that he was always ready and willing to perform his part of contract and, respondents evaded their responsibility - Judgment of HC was set aside and order of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
