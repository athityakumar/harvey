Delhi Cloth and General Mills Limited v Shambhu Nath Mukherjee and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 9; (1977) 4 SCC 415; (1978) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1978 SC 8; 1977 (35) FLR 353; 1977 LabIC 1695; 1978 (1) LLJ 1; 1978 (1) LLN 1; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 591; 1977 SLJ 628; 1977 UJ 673

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Retrenchment - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25F, 2A, 10(1)(c), 2(k), 10, 2(oo) and 12(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, 14 and 144A - Whether under provisions of Standing Orders workman can be automatically struck off rolls by management? - S. 10 of Act as being invalid on ground of violation of art. 14 of Constitution - Ratio decidendi of Niemla Textile Finishing Mills is applicable while dealing with objection u/art. 14 of Constitution in respect of present reference u/s. 10(1)(c) of Act - Held, that striking off name of workman from rolls by management is termination of his service - Such termination of service is retrenchment within meaning of s. 2(oo) of Act - There is nothing to show that provisions of s. 25F(a) and (b) were complied with by management in this case - Provisions of s. 25F(a), proviso apart, and (b) are mandatory - Any order of retrenchment, in violation of these two peremptory conditions precedent, is invalid - Appeal dismissed.
