Siddu Venkappa Devadiga v Smt. Rangu S. Devadiga and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 387; (1977) 3 SCC 532; AIR 1977 SC 890; 1977 UJ 101

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether it was permissible for High Court to interfere with finding of Trial Court by setting up a plea which had never been taken in plaint? - Power of Appellate Court does not extend to making out a new case for a party - No issue as to nature of business being 'benami' or otherwise was raised by either party - Decision of a case cannot be based on grounds outside plea of parties and that it is case pleaded which has to be found - Held, HC went wrong in ignoring this basic principle of law, and in making out an entirely new case which was not pleaded and was not subject-matter of trial - Appeal allowed.
