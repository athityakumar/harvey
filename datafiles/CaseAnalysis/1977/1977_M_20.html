Mangal Oram and Others v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 149; (1977) 2 SCC 46; AIR 1977 SC 1456; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 666; 1977 UJ 177

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Orissa Development of Industries, Irrigation, Agriculture, Capital Construction and Resettlement of Displaced Persons (Land Acquisition) Act, 1948, ss. 3(1), 5(1), 2(c) and 2 - Whether State Govt. is competent to acquire land in question under Act for establishment of a steel plant? - Held, validity of acquisition of this land is that fourteen years after acquisition of land, railway authorities for whom land was acquired have transferred 3 .21 acres of land to Notified Area Committee, Rourkela - According to affidavit filed on behalf of respondents, above mentioned area is sought to be transferred to Notified Area Committee because Notified Area Committee is appropriate body to construct and maintain link road, bus and taxi stands and shops surrounding railway station - There is no principle of law by which a valid, compulsory acquisition stands voided because long later requiring authority diverts it to a public purpose other than one stated in declaration - Appeals dismissed.
