State Of Bihar v Ramesh Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 50; (1977) 4 SCC 39; (1977) SCC (Cr) 533; AIR 1977 SC 2018; 1977 (3) ALR 443; 1977 CRLJ 1606; 1977 CrLR(SC) 375; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 257; 1977 UJ 788

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 ss. 209, 226, 227, 228 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 302, 201 - Charged - No sufficient ground - Discharged - Respondent/accused was charged for offence u/ss. 302, 201 of IPC and trial was conducted u/s. 209 of CrPC - Respondent contended during trial that there was no sufficient ground for proceeding with trial and could be discharged u/s. 227 of CrPC which was allowed and discharged - Appellant/State filed Revision before HC against said order , which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the respondent will be able to prove his defence at the final stage of the trial.

Held, u/s. 226 of CrPC while opening the case for the prosecution the prosecutor has got to describe the charge against the accused and state by what evidence he proposes to prove the guilt of the accused. The Judge has to pass thereafter an order either u/s. 227 or s. 228 of CrPC and if the Judge consider that there is no sufficient ground for proceeding against the accused, he shall discharge the accused and record his reasons for so doing. The standard of test and judgment which is to be finally applied before recording a finding regarding the guilt or otherwise of the accused is not exactly to be applied at the stage of deciding the matter u/ss. 227 or 228 of CrPC. Therefore, deceased had died an unnatural death and there were burn injuries on her person does not seem to be in doubt or dispute that assault on deceased by the respondent a day prior to the occurrence is perhaps sought to be proved by the evidence of the informant, and it seems, he would also try to say, rightly or wrongly, that at the time of the said assault the respondent had given her a threat to kill her. Hence, impugned orders of the HC and Trial court are set aside and direct that appropriate charge or charges will be framed against the respondent and the trial shall proceed further in accordance with the law. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Unless, the court finds that the evidence led before him is self-contradictory, or intrinsically untrustworthy, process cannot be refused if that evidence makes out a prima facie case.
