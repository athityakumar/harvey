Umedbhai Jadavbhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 138; (1978) SCC (Cr) 108; (1978) 1 SCC 228; AIR 1978 SC 424; 1978 CRLJ 489; 1978 CrLR(SC) 72; 1978 (19) GLR 268; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 378 Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 -Appeal against acquittal (A)Whether High Court would ordinarily interfere with Trial Court's conclusion and appreciation of evidence in an appeal against acquittal ? - Held, in an appeal against acquittal, HC would not ordinarily interfere with Trial Court's conclusion unless there are compelling reasons to do so, on account of manifest errors of law or of fact resulting in miscarriage of justice - HC would give due importance to opinion of Sessions Judge if same were arrived at after proper appreciation of evidence - This rule will not be applicable where Sessions Judge has made an absolutely wrong assumption of a very material and clinching aspect in circumstance of case - Appeal dismissed.
