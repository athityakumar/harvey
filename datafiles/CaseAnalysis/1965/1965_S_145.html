 Ajaib Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1965 SC 1619; 1965 CRLJ 553; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 845

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence of India Act, 1962, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 10(2) - Defence of India Act, 1962, r. 30(1)(b) - Appellant was detained u/r. 30(1)(b) of Defence of India Rules by an order passed by Shri Lal Singh on 30-06-1964 - That order was passed by Shri Lal Singh as District Magistrate of Amritsar - (A) Whether Shri Lal Singh was District Magistrate of Amritsar on 30-06-1964? - (1) Whether if he was not District Magistrate on that date, could he as Addl. District Magistrate, exercise power of detention and that would depend upon whether an Addl. District Magistrate is of same rank as District Magistrate or below him in rank? - Held, Shri Lal Singh was not appointed as District Magistrate - An Addl. District Magistrate is below rank of a District Magistrate and cannot be said to be of same rank as District Magistrate - S. 10(2) of code which shows that an Addl. District Magistrate need not necessarily be conferred with all powers of District Magistrate under code or any other law for time being in force - He can be an Addl. District Magistrate though, he may be exercising only some of powers of District Magistrate - Clearly, therefore, an Addl. District Magistrate must be an officer below rank of District Magistrate - Further sub-s. (3) of s. 10 bears this out - That sub-section says, that for certain purposes, Addl. District Magistrate shall be deemed to be subordinate to District Magistrate - Therefore, even if Addl. District Magistrate is invested with all powers of a District Magistrate under code or under any other law for time being in force he is still below District Magistrate for certain purposes mentioned in s. 10(3) of Code - Besides there is only one District Magistrate in a district and all other magistrates, whether they be Magistrates first class or even Addl. District Magistrates must obviously be below him in rank - Order of detention passed by Shri Lall Singh on 30-06-1964, when he was not District Magistrate of Amritsar but only an Addl. District Magistrate is not in accordance with Act and rules and must be set aside - Petition allowed.
