Nirmala Bala Ghose and Another v Balai Chand Ghose and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1965 SC 1874; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 550

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Hindu Law - Family & Personal - Land & Property - (A) Whether a deed of dedication of properties to deities is absolute or partial? - Held, it must be settled by a conspectus of all provisions of deed - Where property is wholly dedicated to worship of idol without reserving any beneficial interest to settler, his descendants or other persons, dedication is complete - If intention of deed is to create a charge in favor of deity and residue vests in settler, dedication is partial - Court has to ascertain intention of settler, and for that purpose to take into consideration all terms of deed - If it appears that after endowing property in favor of a religious institution or a deity, surplus is either expressly or by implication retained with settler or given to his heirs, a partial dedication may readily be inferred - (B) Whether endowment is made bad if there is a reasonable provision for remuneration, maintenance and residence of shebaits? - Further held, no, it is not - Possession and management of property must be entrusted to a shebait or manager, and nomination of settler himself and his heirs with reasonable remuneration out of endowed property with right of residence in property will not invalidate endowment - Provision for benefit of persons other than shebait, if it infringes rule against perpetuities or accumulation or rules against impermissible restrictions, may not be valid, but that does not affect validity of endowment - In case of an absolute dedication, if direction for accumulation is found invalid, benefit of income will ensure for benefit of deity without restriction and income will not revert to settler - A provision entitling shebait to use land belonging to deity at specially low rates may not by itself amount to an impermissible reservation by settler - (C) Whether u/O. 44, r.33 appellate court can reopen decrees which have become final merely, because appellate Court does not agree with opinion of Court appealed from? - Further held, no, it cannot - O.44 ,r.33 has to be applied with discretion, and to cases where interference in favor of appellant necessitates interference also with a, decree which has by acceptance or acquiescence become final; so as to enable court to adjust rights of parties - If appellate court, in adjusting right claimed by appellant, it is found necessary to grant relief to a person who has not appealed, power u/O.41, r.33 may properly be invoked - Appeal dismissed.
