 B. H. Aswathanarayan Singh and Others v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 460; AIR 1965 SC 1848; 1965 (2) SCJ 824; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 46, 48, 68A, 68C, 68D, 68D(3) and 68E - State Transport Undertaking (Mysore) Rules, 1960, rr. 3(e), 3(f) and 12 - (A) Whether nature of service refers to class of service u/s. 68?- Held, it does refer - No substantial difference between two referred in s. 68 - (B) Whether requirements of s. 68C would be satisfied when scheme indicates both minimum and maximum number of vehicles and trips on each route requirements of s. 68C would be satisfied? - Further held, it is - It would be permissible and legitimate to refer to provisions in ss. 46 and 48 in order to find out what particulars of nature of services proposed to be rendered have to be given u/s. 68C - When s. 68C provides for giving particulars of nature of services proposed to be rendered, such details should be given as are necessary to enable objectors to make their objections - So, need not be in form of precise number of vehicles and trips - (C) Whether existence of some defect in draft scheme would be fatal u/s. 68D? - Further held, no - If approved scheme emerges after objections have been heard and decided u/s. 68D is in accordance with requirements of s. 68C - (D) Whether proviso of s. 68D(3) applicable to two termini of route are in same state or not? - Further held, If termini are in same state although routes are different it does not include interstate route - But even if a part of scheme covers roads which continue beyond state and connect with other states, it would include inter state - (E) Who has authority to hear objection u/s. 68D? - Further held, state Govt. - U/r. 8 Chief Minister has authority - Minister in charge of transport can also be nominated by Govt. under Act - Appeal dismissed.
