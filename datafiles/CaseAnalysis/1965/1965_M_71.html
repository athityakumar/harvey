Mongibai Hariram v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 240; AIR 1966 SC 882; 1966 (68) BomLR 781; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 322

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Decree-Holder, Ejectment Suit, Charitable or Religious entities

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 6, 13 and 28 - (A) Whether premises were not vacant as contemplated by Act and therefore could not have been requisitioned? - 'Premises' is defined in s.4(3) and means any building or part of a building let or intended to be let separately; expression 'let or intended to be let separately' governs only 'part of a building' and cannot have any reasonable meaning with reference to a building as there is no question of its being intended to be let separately, it is to be let or not to be let; hence all buildings, irrespective of fact whether they were let or were intended to be let at time Act came into force came within expression 'premises' and therefore could be requisitioned by Govt. if requirements of ss. 5 and 6 were satisfied - From date of enforcement of Act, every building comes within expression 'premises' and that a part of a building comes within expression if it is let or if it is intended to, be let separately on that date and room no. 26, which had been let, was 'premises' within meaning of that term in Act - (B) Whether or not premises was vacant when requisitioned? - Held, Govt. can requisition premises which are vacant; sub-section(2) of s. 6 requires landlord to give notice of vacancy of premises within a week of its falling vacant and if such notice is received and Govt. requisitions building within a month of receiving notice, no question about vacancy of premises can arise for determination and if no such intimation is given by landlord, proviso to cl.(a) of sub-section(4) of s. 6 requires State Govt. to make such enquiry as it deems fit and make a declaration in order of requisition itself that premises were vacant or had become, vacant on or after date of notification u/s.ub-s (1) of s. 6; such a declaration is made conclusive evidence of premises being vacant or having become vacant - Further held, such a declaration is made in requisition order dt. 10/09/1956 requisitioning premises in suit; also premises became vacant in view of sub-s (1) of s. 6 and explanation to s. 6 when tenant ceased to occupy it due to eviction in execution of decree secured by appellant - (C) Whether requisition order was made malafidely? - Further held, there is no allegation that State Govt. is in any way interested in earlier tenant in whose favour allotment order was made after requisitioning of premises; circumstances that earlier tenant had not paid rent to landlords-appellants, delayed execution of ejectment decree and applied for allotment of house on day following ejectment, do not, in law, make requisition order mala fide when order was not made on account of any animus against appellants or for a purpose for which requisition could not have been made - (D) Whether or not Act was inapplicable to such ejected person on basis of provisions of s. 17 of Rent Act? - Further held, s. 17(1) empowers court to order landlord to reallot premises to tenant who had been evicted therefrom in case landlord does not occupy premises within a period of 1 month or if landlord reallots premises to another person within a year of tenant's eviction; if Act provides by s.6 that landlord cannot occupy premises which had become vacant on eviction of tenant within a month of receipt of intimation of vacancy by State Govt., there is no conflict between that provision and discretionary power vested in Court u/s 17(1) as court, undoubtedly, cannot exercise such a discretionary power when another enactment by its language provides for landlord's not occupying premises for a period in excess of a month - There is not real conflict between provisions of s. 6 of Act and provisions of s. 13 or 17 of Rent Act; fields of operation of two Acts are different - Appeal dismissed.
