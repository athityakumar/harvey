Calcutta Tramways Company Limited v Corporation of Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 387; AIR 1965 SC 1728; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 354

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Arbitration & ADR - Calcutta Tramways Act, 1951, s.5(1) - (A) Whether proviso to s.5 of Calcutta Tramways Act saves reference to arbitration with respect to sums to be paid to Calcutta Corporation ? - Held, yes, proviso to s.5 saves from operation of substantive section sums payable under any agreements to any such bodies mentioned therein - In respect of sums payable to Calcutta Corporation, Corporation would still continue to be a party to said agreements for said purpose - Relying on arbitration clause of agreements can refer disputes arising in respect of sums payable in terms of said agreements to arbitration - Substantive right to payment of rent and procedural one to have any dispute arising in respect of that right referred to arbitration embodied in agreements are interconnected and are not severable - Appeal dismissed.
