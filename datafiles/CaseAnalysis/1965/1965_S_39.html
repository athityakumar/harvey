Sheikh Gulfan and Others v Sanat Kumar Ganguli

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 114; AIR 1965 SC 1839; 1965 (2) SCJ 839; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, s.30(c) - Calcutta Improvement Act, 1911, ss. 2(1 a), 25, 81, 132, 78 A and 122 - (A) Interpretation of Statute? - Held, do not rely on bare dictionary meaning of words - It becomes essential to have regard to subject matter of statute, context in which words occur and object which it is intended to achieve - If two constructions are reasonably possible, court should prefer construction which helps to carry out beneficent purpose and does not unduly expand area or scope of exception - (B) What is object of s.30(c) and other clauses of s. 30 of Act ? - Further held, to help thika tenants - If an exception is provided, provision prescribing exception and creating a bar to application of Act to certain cases must, be strictly construed - Other clauses of s. 30 clearly indicate that it is only lands vested in Govt. or other special bodies or authorities that are excepted from application of Act - U/s. 30(c) it is land which must be required, and not any fee or charges that may, be levied against it - Land must be one about which it could be said that it was necessary for carrying out a particular provision of Improvement Act - Necessity must be established for carrying out provisions of Improvement Act and not policy of said provisions or object which they are intended to achieve - (C) Whether u/s. 81 Board can acquire more land? - Further held, board can acquire more land than is absolutely necessary for purpose of scheme as such, and may later dispose of superfluous land - Though it would not be consistent with fair construction of s. 30(c) to hold that because land in question can be made liable to pay betterment fee and betterment fee thus realised from land serves purpose of s. 122 of Act, land itself is required for carrying out provisions of s. 122 - (D) Why a betterment fee is levied by board u/s. 78A? - Further held, because its value is increased as a result of improvement scheme - U/s. 25 of Improvement Act, 1911 landlord may apply for enhancing rent payable by thika tenants to him - But a landlord should not get additional benefit of exemption from application of provision of Thika Tenancy Act which give protection to tenants - Appeal allowed.
