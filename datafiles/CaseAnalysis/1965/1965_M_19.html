Ambika Prasad Thakur and Others v Maharaj Kumar Kamal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 269; AIR 1966 SC 605; 1966 BLJR 147; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Bengal Tenancy Act, 1885 - Whether if a thing or a state of things is shown to exist, an inference of its continuity within a reasonably proximate time both forwards and backwards may be drawn? - Presumption of future continuance is noticed in Illustration(d) to s. 114 of Indian Evidence Act - In appropriate cases, an inference of continuity of a thing or state of things backwards may also be drawn though on this point section does not give a separate illustration - Rule of continuity of presumption has been recognised in Anangamanjari Chowdhrani v Tripura Soondari Chowdhrani ((1887) L R 14 I A 101, 110 - However, presumption of continuity weakens with passage of time, how far presumption may be drawn both backwards and forwards depends upon nature of thing and surrounding circumstances - Held, HC rightly refused to draw inference from state of things during 1892 to 1909 - Appeal dismissed.
