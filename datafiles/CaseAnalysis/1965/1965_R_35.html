Bhanwar Lal and Another v Regional Settlement Commissioner, Jaipur, Cum-Custodian of

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1965 SC 1885; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Evacuee Interest Separation Act, 1951, s. 10 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 7(1) - Notice to mortgagee could not be served as he died long before - Notice fixed at conspicuous place in village - No objections - (A) Whether order declaring land to be evacuee property can affect mortgagee rights? - Held, no - It cannot- Although non-issue of notice to them was of no consequence and they could not therefore challenge validity of order on ground of want of notice - U/s. 10 of Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act, 1951, competent officer can take all necessary measures for purpose of separating interest of evacuees from those of claimants - It is only after such separation that evacuee interest gets vested in custodian free from all encumbrances - Unless proper action is not taken to separate interest of evacuees and those who claim, to be mortgagees, custodian cannot take any action against those claiming to be mortgagees or their tenants who are said to be in possession of property - Appeal partly allowed.
