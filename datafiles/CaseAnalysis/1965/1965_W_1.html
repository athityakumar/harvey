Union of India v Watkins Mayor and Company

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 493; AIR 1966 SC 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 61 and 120 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 80 - Appeals under art. 133(1)(a) of Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether storage charges granted at Rs. 300 per month by High Court were justified? - Held, yes - At time of inspection weight of iron sheets stored was 498 tons, though quantity of iron sheets originally stored was 600 tons - HC has also taken into consideration additional services rendered by plaintiff in looking after iron sheets during period of storage - (B) Whether suit was governed by art. 61 of Limitation Act, 1908? - Held, no - Transaction of bailment between parties was a single and indivisible transaction and claim of compensation made by plaintiff cannot be split up into different items for applying bar of limitation - Provisions of art. 61 of Limitation Act, 1908, do not apply to present case - (C) Whether plaintiff was entitled to a decree for interest to extent of Rs. 2,974. 20? - Held, u/s. 80 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Court may award interest at rate of 6 percent per annum, when no rate of interest is specified in promissory note or bill of exchange - It is well established that interest may be awarded for period prior to date of institution of suit if there is an agreement for payment of interest at fixed rate or if interest is payable by usage of trade having force of law, or under provisions of any substantive law entitling plaintiff to recover interest - Appeal No. 43 of 1963 allowed and Appeal No. 44 of 1963 dismissed.
