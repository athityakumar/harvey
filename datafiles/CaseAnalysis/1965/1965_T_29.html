Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v D. R. Singh

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1966 SC 288; 1965 (11) FLR 106; 1965 (2) LLJ 122; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.33(2) - An industrial concern, filed an application u/s. 33(2)(b) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 before Industrial Tribunal asking for its approval of action which it proposed to take against its employee - 'Workman' for applicability u/s. 33(2), must be a workman concerned in main industrial disputes; if he was not so concerned, s. 33(2) would not apply - (A) Whether tribunal was in error in not dealing with preliminary point as to whether s. 33 applied to facts of this case? -In instant case, even if appellant took view that workman against whom it was taking action was not a workman concerned with main industrial disputes, it would be justified in refusing to take risk of deciding said point for itself - It would be legitimate for an employer like appellant to make an application u/s. 33 of Act of 1947 without prejudice to his case that s.33 did not apply - Held, that tribunal committed an error in not dealing with preliminary point as to applicability of s. 33 to facts of this case- (B) Whether appellant is right in contending that respondent is not a 'workman concerned with such disputes' within meaning of s. 32(2)? - Held, appellant was right in contending that respondent is not a workman - Appeal dismissed.
