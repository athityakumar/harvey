Nawab Usmanali Khan v Sagarmal

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1965 SC 1798; 1965 JabLJ 1028; 1965 MahLJ 776; 1965 MPLJ 864; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 14 and 17 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 86(1), 87 B and 141 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 291 and 363 - (A) Whether an award is to be registered when it is stating only existing facts? - Held, where an award dated merely an existing fact - Award did not either create or of its own force declare any interest in any immovable property and did not therefore come within purview of s. 17 - Not required to be registered - (B) Whether passing of an order or decree on an award is a suit? - Further held, passing of a judgment and decree on an award is not a suit - Parties to whom notice of filing of award is given u/s. 14(2) cannot be regarded as 'sued in any' court - (C)Whether arbitration against former Indian state is a suit? -Further held, ruler of any former Indian state cannot sued in any court except with consent of Central Govt. - A proceeding u/s. 14 r/w s. 17 of Arbitration Act, 1940, for passing of a judgment and decree on an award is not a suit - Institution of arbitration proceeding against ruler of a former Indian state is not therefore barred by s.86(1) r/w s. 87B, CPC, 1908 - (D) Whether ruler of a former Indian state can claim immunity from legal proceedings? - Further held, ruler of a former Indian state cannot claim immunity from proceedings other than suit - (E)Whether political pension can be enforced in a court? -Further held, guarantee given by Govt. of India was in nature of a treaty obligation with sovereign rulers of Indian states - Cannot be enforced by action in municipal courts - Sanction is political and not legal - Appeal allowed.
