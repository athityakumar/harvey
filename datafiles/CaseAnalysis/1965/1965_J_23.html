State of Maharashtra v Jugamander Lal

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 432; AIR 1966 SC 940; 1976 (2) ALT(SC) 140; 1966 (68) BomLR 671; 1966 CRLJ 707; 1966 MahLJ 573; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Brothel

Summary: Criminal - Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Act, 1956, s. 3(1) - Whether it is obligatory upon court which convicts a person of an offence u/s. 3(1) to pass a sentence of imprisonment where conviction is, in respect of a first offence, for a term not less than one year and not merely to a sentence of fine? - Held, word 'punishable' has been used in s. 3(1), it is impossible to construe it as giving any discretion to court in matter of determining nature of sentences to be passed in respect of a contravention of provision - By using expression 'shall be punishable' Legislature has made it clear that offender shall not escape penal consequences - Appeal disposed of.
