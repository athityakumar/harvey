South Asia Industries Private Limited v S. Sarup Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1966 SC 346; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 829

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14 (1), 17 and 25 - Respondent let out premises to Allen Berry who transferred lease to appellant, thereafter respondents made application to Controller for an order of recovery of possession from appellant - Controller made order in favour of respondent and High Court declined to set aside order - Whether s. 14(1) of 1958 Act, puts a complete ban on recovery of possession from all tenants? - Held, where tenant becomes extinct without leaving any successor on whom tenancy devolves, an order can be made against a person who took an assignment of lease from tenant before it became extinct, and that an order for recovery of possession can be made against assignee alone, for that would enable object of statute which was to enable the landlord to recover possession to be achieved - Appeal dismissed.
