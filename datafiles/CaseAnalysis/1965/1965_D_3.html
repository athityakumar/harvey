A. K. Gupta and Sons v Damodar Valley Corporation

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 266; AIR 1967 SC 96; 1966 BLJR 340; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Civil Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 42 - Dispute between parties related to clause of contract between them - Appellant sought leave of High Court to amend plaint by adding a claim based on same contract, which was refused - Whether amendment seeking to introduce a claim based on same cause of action be allowed? - Held, since amendment sought interpretation of a clause in contract only for a decision of rights of parties under it and for no other purpose, and claim sought to be made by amendment, was mentioned in plaint in stating valuation of suit for purpose of jurisdiction, and that punishing of mistakes is, of course, not administration of justice - Appeal allowed.
