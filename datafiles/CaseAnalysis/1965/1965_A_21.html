 Joint Chief Controller of I. and E., Madras v Aminchand Mutha

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1966 SC 478; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 273; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 67; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Any Goods, Import Licence, Licensing Authority, Administrative Instructions

Summary: Customs - Red Book for period January-June, 1957 - Instruction 71 and 72 - Partnership firm known as Nainmull Juthmull had 3 partners - Firm dissolved - Had quota for import of certain things - Quota to be divided amongst partners on dissolution - Application for division of quota made to Chief Controller of Imports - Thereafter, application for license made to Joint Chief Controller of Imports - For grant of license, approval of Chief Controller with respect to division of quota rights of dissolved firm necessary - Whether approval to division of quota between partners of dissolved firm granted by Chief Controller must take effect from date of agreement of between them? - Held, Chief Controller is bound to divide quota of a firm consisting of partners which has been dissolved in accordance with provisions of agreement between partners provided necessary evidence has been produced before him, as required by Instruction 72 in that behalf - Such being nature of proceeding before Chief Controller, it follows that when he gives approval to division of quota between partners of a dissolved firm in accordance with agreement between them, approval must take effect from date of agreement between partners - If such approval by Chief Controller were not to date back to date of agreement, it would mean that partners who were otherwise entitled to approval under Instructions 71 and 72 might lose advantage that they would have before licensing authority by delay in approval by Chief Controller - Appeal dismissed.
