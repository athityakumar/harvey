Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v S. N. Modak

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1966 SC 380; 1965 (11) FLR 61; 1965 (2) LLJ 128; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Corporate - Indirect Tax - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Whether as a result of pendency of an industrial dispute, between an employer and his employees, employer is required to apply for approval of dismissal of his employee u/s. 33 does such an application survive if main industrial dispute is meanwhile finally decided ? - Application of appellant can in a sense, be treated as an incidental proceeding; but it is a separate proceeding all same, and will be governed by provisions of s.33(2)(b) as such - Held, it cannot be said a proceeding which validly commences by way of an application made by employer u/s. 33(2)(b) should automatically come to an end because main dispute has in meanwhile been decided - Appeal dismissed.
