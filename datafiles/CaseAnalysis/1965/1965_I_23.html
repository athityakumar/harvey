Ward, Lucknow v Bachu Lal Kapoor

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 1966 SC 1148; [1966] 60 ITR 74; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2, 3, 14, 22, 25A and 34 - Validity of notice u/s. 34 to the respondent being karta of Hindu undivided family - Application u/s. 25A that the family has been partitioned, by the respondent was accepted in the earlier year - For the earlier assessment years and even in the relevant assessment year, the members of the family were assessed as individuals - Notice was issued under implication that the family in fact continued to be a joint Hindu family and its income escaped assessment - Challenged - If a Hindu undivided family exists, u/s. 3 the ITO has to assess it in respect of its income and members of the family cannot separately be assessed - ITO has jurisdiction to initiate proceedings u/s. 34 if the conditions laid down therein are complied with, against a person on the ground that the income, though it has been assessed in the hands of another, has escaped assessment in his hands - Held, notice in question was valid - Further held, High Court went wrong in holding that the ITO had no jurisdiction to initiate proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act against the respondent as the karta of a HUF - Case remanded to HC - Appeal allowed.
