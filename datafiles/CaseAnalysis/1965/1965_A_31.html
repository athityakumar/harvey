Raja Bhanupratap Singh v Assistant Custodian Evacuee Property U.P

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1966 SC 245; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 304

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws, Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 7, 17, 10(2)(n), 10(2)(m) and 56 - Administration of Evacuee Property Rules, r. 22 - Whether custodian is entitled to entertain claim of holder of a money decree against evacuee for satisfaction of his dues out of assets vested in custodian by s. 7 of Act? - Words used in cl. (n) of s. 10(2) empowering custodian to pay to 'any other person' any sums of money out of funds in his possession are not restricted to persons who are members of family of evacuee, they include other persons as well who are entitled to receive money from evacuee, decree of a civil or revenue court or an order of any other authority is, it must be observed, not decisive of validity or admissibility of claim against evacuee property, it is for custodian to determine whether claimant is entitled to receive sum of money claimed by him out of funds in his possession, he has to form his 'opinion' on this question, and in forming his opinion he must act judicially and not arbitrarily - Held, where a claim is made by a person who claims to be a creditor of evacuee and he satisfies custodian that he is entitled to any sum of money, then normally custodian would be justified in discharging obligations of evacuee out of funds in his possession - Appeal allowed.
