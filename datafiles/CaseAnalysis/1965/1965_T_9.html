Madhya Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Regional Transport Authority, Raipur

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 463; AIR 1966 SC 156; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 46, 54, 62, 62(c) and 62(d) - Granting of temprory permit - (A) Whether words 'in any case'' in s. 62 means that under no circumstances a temporary permit can be granted on any route for more than a total period of 4 months ? - Held, words 'in any case'' in s. 62 does not mean that under no circumstances a temporary permit can be granted on any route for more than a total period of 4 months is not correct section means that at any one time Regional Transport Authority is not permitted to issue to any person a temporary permit for a period exceeding 4 months - (B) Whether Regional Transport Authority granting a temporary permit for a second time and total duration of two periods is more than 4 months would invalidate permit? - Where formalities u/s. 57 are not completed within a period of 4 months, Regional Transport Authority can grant a second temporary permit in order to meet temporary need - Further held, where no abuse of power is alleged or shown, mere fact that he has granted a temporary permit for a second time and total duration of two periods is more than 4 months, would not invalidate second permit - Appeal allowed.
