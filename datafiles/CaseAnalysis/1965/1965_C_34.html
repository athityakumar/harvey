Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhra v H. E. H. Mir Osman Ali Khan

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 239; AIR 1966 SC 1260; [1966] 59 ITR 666; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 96; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 296

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Government securities, Code of Civil Procedure, Immunity, Finance Act, 1950, International Law, Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, Part B States (Taxation Concession) Order 1950

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 372 - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(14 A), 3 and 66(1) - Assessee was an ex-ruler - Whether assessee was immune to taxation under Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, in respect of any part of his income? - Held, it is manifest that after 26-01-1950, assessee ceased to be a ruling chief and he was, therefore, liable to assessment under Act, and his right to exemption, if any, under international law during accounting year was irrelevant to question of taxation under Act, as said law ceased to apply to him during assessment year - Appeal partly allowed.
