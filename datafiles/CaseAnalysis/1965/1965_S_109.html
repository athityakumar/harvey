 Jaora Sugar Mills Private Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1966 SC 416; 1966 JabLJ 323; 1966 MahLJ 279; 1966 MPLJ 295; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961, s. 3 - Madhya Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1958, ss. 3, 23, 21 and 5 - Madhya Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Rules, 1959, rr. 60, 63, 45 and 47 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 248, 245(1) and 266, Sch.7, List 1, Entry 97 - Validity of provisions of Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961- Legislative competence - Colourable legislation - Conformity with art. 226 of Constitution - (A) Whether provisions of s. 3 of Act of 1961 is valid on ground of legislative competence ? It cannot be disputed that Parliament is competent to make a law in respect of cesses in question, to apply provisions of such a law to different states, and to make them retrospective in operation - Held, provisions of s. 3 of Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961, s. 3 are valid - (B)Whether provisions of s. 3 of Act of 1961 is valid on ground of colourable legislation? - When a challenge is made to validity of an Act on ground that it is a colourable legislation, what has to be proved to satisfaction of court is that though Act ostensibly is within legislative competence of legislature in question, in substance and in reality it covers a field which is outside its legislative competence - Parliament has exercised its undoubted legislative competence to provide for recovery of specified cesses and commissions in respective state areas from dates and in manner indicated by it - Further held, provisions of s. 3 of Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961 are valid- (C) Whether cess recovered under Act of 1661 by State not having been dealt with, in accordance with art. 266 is a ground for invalidating s. 3 of Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961? - Validity of Act must be judged in light of legislative competence of legislature which passes Act and in certain cases, by reference to question as to whether fundamental rights of citizens have been improperly contravened, or other considerations which may be relevant in that behalf - Further held, validity of s. 3 of Cess Act cannot be questioned on ground that cesses recovered under it are not dealt with, in accordance with provisions of art. 266 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
