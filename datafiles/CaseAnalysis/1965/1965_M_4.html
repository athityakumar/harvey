 Chairman Madappa v M. N. Mahanthadevaru and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 250; AIR 1966 SC 878; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 92(1) and 92(1)(f) - A muth in village Davanur - Suit was brought in 1942 u/s. 92 of CPC, 1908, for framing a scheme for management of muth - (A) Whether any individual can file a suit u/s 92(1) of CPC, 1908 against a trust? - Management of Muth - Main purpose of s. 92(1) is, to give protection to public trusts of a charitable or religious nature from being subjected to harassment by suits being filed against them - Held, suits u/s. 92(1) of CPC, 1908 can only be filed either by Advocate General or two or more persons having an interest in trust with consent in writing of Advocate General - Object is that before Advocate General files a suit or gives his consent for filing a suit, he would satisfy himself that there is a prima facie case either of breach of trust or of necessity for obtaining directions of Court - (B) Whether s. 92(1)(f) can be read to limit power of trustees to manage trust property? - Held, s. 92(1)(f) of CPC, 1908 was not meant to limit in any way Power of trustees or managers to manage trust property to best advantage of trust, in its interest - And if necessary, even to let, sell, mortgage or exchange such property - There can be no bar to a provision being made in a scheme for directions by court in that behalf - Appeal dismissed.
