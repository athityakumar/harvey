 Ram Dial and Others v State of Punjab With connected Writ Petition

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1965 SC 1518; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 858

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govt. - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss .14 (e), 16 (1) and 24(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appellants election to Municipal Committee - Constitutional validity of s. 14 (e) of Act of 1911 - (A)Whether when State Govt. intends to remove a person in public interest for any reasons given u/cls. 16(1)(a) to (g) of it should take action u/s 14(e) or 16(1) of Act of 1911? - The removal of a member contemplated in s. 16(1) for reasons in cls.a to g thereof, as their content shows, is in public interest and proviso to s. 16(1) provides for a hearing in manner indicated therein - S. 14(e) of Act of 1911 which also provides for removal in public interest makes no provision for hearing member to be removed - All reasons given in cls. a to g are in public interest and, therefore, even if state Govt. intends to remove a person for any reasons given in cls. a to g it can take action u/s. 14(e) and thus circumvent provisions contained in proviso to s. 16(1) for hearing - (B)Whether s. 14 of Act of 1911 is violative of art. 14 of Constitution ? There is no guidance in Act for determining what matters, though not in public interest, may yet be capable of being deemed to be in public interest by State Govt. - In circumstances it must be held that power which conferred upon State Govt. being unguided is unconstitutional- Held, s. 14 in so far as it confers power on State Govt. to require a seat of a member of a committee to be vacated for any reason which it may deem to affect public interest is violative of art. 14 of Constitution and, therefore, unconstitutional- Appeal allowed.
