Commissioner v Chari and Chari Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1966 SC 54; [1965] 57 ITR 400; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Agency, Managing Agent

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - A/y 1952-1953 - Claimed deduction - Disallowed - Challenged - Assessee/respondent (Company) was carried on business in hides and skins, minerals, tobacco and other commodities, and also acted as managing agents for X Company and for two other Companies - Assessee was appointed as its agent by Central Govt. and agreed to pay assessee with commission on all purchases - Thereafter, assessee passed resolution placing his director in 'special charge' and agreed to pay him 30 percent of net profit as remuneration - Under contract with Govt. Rs. 1,38,454, became due to assessee as commission in account year ending 31-3-1952 - After providing Rs. 41,473, for expenses, 30 percent of balance being Rs. 29,094, was paid to director as commission and was claimed in A/y 1952-1953 as permissible deduction before IO who allowed only 10 percent of net profit for services rendered by director and disallowed Rs. 19,796, out of amount claimed - Assessee's agreement with X Company as managing agency was terminated and assessee was paid Rs. 17,346, as compensation and that amount was taken into account by IO in computing income of assessee for A/y ending 1952 - Aggrieved assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and to Tribunal challenging disallowance were unsuccessful - Tribunal referred two questions before HC, who answered both questions in negative - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, it might be observed that it was for revenue to establish that particular receipt was income liable to tax, and beyond stating that assessee was managing agent of X Company and of two other Companies, about their relative importance qua managing agency of X Company and whether by reason of extinction of managing agency of X Company any enduring asset was lost to assessee or its trading organization was adversely affected - IO observed that 'assessee's business of Managing Agency as such had not come to an end', and assessee still continues as 'managing agents of other companies' - Even after surrender of one of agencies, assessee carries on business as before, its structure not being affected' and therefore 'receipt was to be considered as revenue with that view Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Tribunal agreed - But in absence of evidence as to what effect determination of managing agency of X Company had upon business of assessee, mere circumstance that assessee had managing agencies of two other companies without more would not bring ease within Kelsal Parsons and Company v. Commissioners of Inland Revenue 21 T.C. 608 - In Kettlewell Bullen and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta 1964 Indlaw SC 352, SC pointed out that ordinarily compensation for loss of office or agency was regarded as capital receipt, but rule was subject to exception that payment received even for termination of agency agreement, where agency was one of many which assessee holds, and termination of agency does not impair profit-making structure of assessee, but was within framework of business, it being necessary incident of business that existing agencies might be terminated, and fresh agencies might be taken, was revenue and not capital - Kelsal Parsons and Company's case 21 T.C. 608, falls within exception to ordinary rule, and circumstances which brought case of assessee within exception might be clearly established - Hence, HC was of opinion that compensation received for taking over X Company was capital receipt not liable to be taxed, and on materials placed before SC was unable to disagree with HC on that question - Appeal dismissed.
