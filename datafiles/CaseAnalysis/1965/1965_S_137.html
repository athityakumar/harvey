Master Construction Company Private Limited v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 81; AIR 1966 SC 1047; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 99; 1966 (17) STC 360

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice and Procedure - Constitution of India, 1953 arts. 136 and 226 - Oriss Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947, r. 83 - (A) Whether appellant could not file appeal unless it had exhausted remedy u/art. 226? - Held, art. 136 confers a discretionary appellate jurisdiction on Supreme court against any order passed by any tribunal in territory of India - Said jurisdiction is not subject to any condition that party who seeks special leave of Supreme Court to appeal from such order, has to exhaust all his other remedies - Existence of a statutory remedy to such a party may persuade Supreme Court not to give leave to appeal to party - Further held, in present case, Act does not provide for a further remedy against order made by Commr. in revision - No justification to dismiss appeal on ground that appellant has not exhausted all his remedies - (B) Jurisdiction of Commr. u/r. 83 - Further held, jurisdiction of Commr. under this rule is limited and is confined only to correction of mistakes or omissions mentioned therein - On a perusal of orders passed by Commr., it is clear that he reviewed his previous order which was passed on merits mainly on two grounds - Both of them are question of limitation as well as question of construction of appellate orders and impact of those orders on amounts paid towards tax before assessments were arguable questions of fact and law - Wrong conclusion, if any, arrived at by Commr. in his earlier order, because of fact that arguments were not advanced before him, cannot be said to be errors apparent on face of record arising or occurring from an accidental slip or omission - Indeed Commr. re-heard arguments and came to a conclusion different from that which he arrived on earlier occasion - This is not permissible u/r. 83 of Rules - Order of Commr. is set aside, except in regard to items (b) and (e) mentioned in para 7 of his order - Appeal partly allowed.
