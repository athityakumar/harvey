Poona Electric Supply Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 138; AIR 1966 SC 30; [1965] 57 ITR 521; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 3 - Income Tax Act, s. 10(1) - (A) Whether amount returned or returnable by licensee to consumers as rebate is deductible for ascertaining taxable income from business u/ss. 10(1) or 10(2)(xv) of Income Tax Act? - Income tax is tax on real income - Real profits can be ascertained only by making permissible deductions - Distinction between deductions made for ascertaining profits and distributions made out of profits - In a given case, whether outgoings fall in one or other of heads is a question of fact to be found on relevant circumstances, having regard to business principles - Also distinction between real and statutory profits - Two profits are for different purposes, one is for commercial and tax purposes and other is for statutory purposes in order to maintain a reasonable level of rates - Held, amounts credited to 'Consumers Benefit Reserve Account' did not form part of asessee's real profits - So, to arrive at taxable income of assessee, such rebates have to be deducted from its total income - In this view, court did not express opinion on question whether said amounts would be allowable deductions u/s. 10(2)(xv) of Income Tax Act - (B) Whether amounts so reserved for future payment deductible in computing income, profits or gains from assessee's business ? - Further held, where an assessee maintained accounts on mercantile basis, accrued liability and estimated expenditure could be deducted from income of accounting year, in which liability accrued - Appeals allowed.
