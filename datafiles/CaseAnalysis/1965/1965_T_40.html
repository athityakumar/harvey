The Commissioner Of Income Tax, Punjab v The Lahore Electric Supply Co.

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1966 SC 843; [1966] 60 ITR 1; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 10, 10(2)(xv), 10(2)(vii), 10(2), 13 and 66(1) - Respondent is a Company incorporated in 1912 - Company acquired several licenses for supply of electricity to Lahore city - All these licenses either terminated or disposed of by 1942 - Govt. of then Province of Punjab acquired Co.'s undertaking - Company received from Govt. a part of money payable to it in respect of - In its assessment to income-tax for years 1948-1949 and 1949-1950 Company claimed deduction of various amounts u/s 10(2)(xv) of Income-tax Act, 1922, on basis that it had been carrying on business in accounting years concerned - (A) Whether on facts found it could be said that Company had been carrying on business in two accounting years? - Since 1942 only business of Co. was to work Lahore electric supply licence - It stopped that business on 05-09-1946 when undertaking was taken over by Govt. - Thereafter, during accounting years concerned, namely, 1947-48 and 1948-49 it had not started any other business - Held,the mere fact that Co. had not gone into liquidation would not establish that it had intention to do business - If it were not so, then in case of all trading companies it had to be held that they were always doing business - Therefore, Co. had ceased to carry on business -Appeal allowed.
