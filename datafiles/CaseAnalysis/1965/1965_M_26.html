Mamidi Venkata Satyanarayana Manikyala Rao and Another v Mandela Narasimhaswami and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 1966 SC 470; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 29; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian limitation Act, 1908, art. 144 - Appellant filed an appeal suit asking for partition of joint family property and for removing defendant from possession - High Court allowed appeal filed by defendant on ground that suit by barred by time - (A) Whether suit having been brought within twelve years of date of symbolical possession, was barred u/art. 144 of Limitation Act ? - In order that suit may be barred under article defendant must have been uninterrupted possession for twelve years before date of suit - By delivery of symbolical possession under order of 06-11-1939, adverse possession of defendants was interrupted - Held, suit having been brought within twelve years of date of symbolic possession, it was not barred under that article u/art. 144 of Limitation Act - (B) When would possession of purchaser of a coparcener's undivided interest in joint family property date? - It is well settled that purchaser of a coparcener's undivided interest in joint family property is not entitled to possession of what he has purchased his only right is to sue for partition of property and ask for allotment to him of that which on partition might be found to fall to share of coparcener whose share he had purchased - Held, purchaser's only right is to sue for partition of property and ask for allotment to him of that which on partition might be found to fall to share of coparcener whose share he had purchased - His right to possession would date from period when a specific allotment was made in his favour - Appeal allowed.
