Bhaurao Shankar Lokhande and Another v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 84; AIR 1965 SC 1564; 1965 CRLJ 544; 1965 MLJ 561; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 92; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 837

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 3 and 17 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 494 - (A) Whether s. 494 will apply when marriage is not solemnized?- Held, s. 494 will apply when marriage comes within 'solemnized marriage' - 'Solemnize' means ' to celebrate marriage with proper ceremonies and in due form ' - Mere going through certain ceremonies with intention of marriage will not make ceremonies one as prescribed by law or approved by custom - Expression 'whoever marries' in s. 494, IPC, 1860 must mean 'whoever marries validly' or 'whoever marries and whose marriage is a valid one' - (B) Whether second marriage u/s. 17 of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 becomes void in all cases ? - Further held, s. 17 of HMA makes marriage between two Hindus void if two conditions are satisfied : (i) marriage is solemnized after commencement of Act, and (ii) at date of such marriage, either party had a spouse living - It is, therefore, essential, for purpose of s. 17 of Act, that marriage to which s. 494, IPC, 1860 applies on account of provisions of Act, should have been celebrated with proper ceremonies and in due form - Bare fact of a man and a woman living as husband and wife does not, at any rate, normally give them status of husband and wife - Appeal allowed.
