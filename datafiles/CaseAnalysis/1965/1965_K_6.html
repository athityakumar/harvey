 Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax, Kerala v Kailas Rubber and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 355; [1966] 60 ITR 435

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950, s.2 - Purchase - Yield - Computation of agricultural income - Sale Proceeds - Capital receipt - An appeal by special leave against order of HC in Income-tax Reference - Facts leading to that appeal might be briefly stated - Respondent was a company which owned a rubber estate purchased by it along with plantation - Estate included rubber trees which, at time of purchase, were in a condition of producing latex - Rubber trees were sold by company after they ceased to yield latex any further - In computing its agricultural income for accounting year ending 28-2-1959, relevant to assessment year 1959-60, for purposes of Act, Commissioner by his order included sum of Rs. 8, 532.50 representing sale proceeds of rubber trees - Respondent appealed to Commissioner of Income-tax and Sales Tax objecting to inclusion of amount in computing its agricultural income - Appellant's contention was that sum of Rs. 8,532.50 was in nature of a revenue receipt and had to be included in computing agricultural income - Contention of respondent was sale proceeds of rubber trees - By order Commissioner rejected contention of respondent - Hence instant appeal - Whether sale proceeds of unyielding trees purchased many years back as yielding trees were capital receipts - Held, agricultural income in s. 2 did not include proceeds from sale of rubber trees in an estate which were utilized for purpose of deriving income in shape of latex - There was enough evidence in record justifying HC's conclusion that rubber trees formed part of capital assets of respondent - Respondent did not grow rubber trees for purpose of selling them - In course of time rubber trees became old and unyielding - When trees were no longer productive of latex, respondent felled them and sold them - Appellate Tribunal and HC were right in holding sale proceeds of trees should be treated as capital receipt and not taxable as agricultural income - Appeal dismissed.
