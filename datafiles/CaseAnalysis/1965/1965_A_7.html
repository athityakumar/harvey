Achuthan Nair v Chinnamu Amma and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 448; AIR 1966 SC 411; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 85; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Tarwad

Summary: Family & Personal - Marumakkattayam Law - Suit against tawazhi represented by its manager and others - Tarwad ownership of property - Acquisition in name of junior member - (A) Whether said property is property of tavazhi or is self acquired property of first respondent and her son, present appellant? - Held, property in question jointly acquired in name of Karnavati of tawazhi and her son who was de facto manager of tawazhi was tawazhi property and not self-acquired property of so - Tavazhi has properties yielding appreciable income from and out of which said property could have been purchased - Said property was tavazhi property - (B) Whether principles applicable to self-acquired property in Hindu law are applicable to property in Marumakkattayam law? - Held, as per Hindu Law When it is proved or admitted that a family possessed sufficient nucleus with aid of which member might have made acquisition, law rises a presumption that it is a joint family property and onus is shifted to individual member to establish that property was acquired by him without aid of said nucleus - Further held, principle applied property in Hindu Law is not applied to acquisition of properties in name of junior member of a tarwad (anandravan) under Marumakkathayam law - There is no presumption either way and question has to be decided on facts of each case - Appeal dismissed.
