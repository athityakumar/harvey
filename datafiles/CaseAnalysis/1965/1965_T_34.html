Ahmedabad Mill Owners' Association Etc v Textile Labour Association

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 191; AIR 1966 SC 497; 1965 (11) FLR 337; 1966 (1) LLJ 1; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross-Examination, Fair Wage, Court Of Enquiry, Financial Capacity

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Trade Disputes Act, 1929 - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 73 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Whether the reference u/s. 73 of the Act is invalid, if the requirements of s. 42 of the Act are not met? - Held, it is true that the power conferred on the State Govt. to make a reference is not absolute or unqualified - It can be exercised only, if one of the conditions specified by s. 73 is satisfied - Once the State Govt. comes to the conclusion that, atleast one of the conditions is satisfied, its power to make a reference is not limited by conditions in s. 42 - (B) Whether the appellants are justified in contending that, the Industrial Court erred in over-ruling their contention, that the new survey suffered from inadequacy of the sample size? - Held, if the quality of investigation has improved, and the method of working out the sample survey has made progress, then it would not be correct to say that because the size of the sample in the present case was smaller as compared to the size of the sample last taken, the inadequacy of the size introduces an infirmity in the investigation itself - Appeal dismissed.
