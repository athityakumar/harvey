 Ram Kishore Sen And Others v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1966 SC 644; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, Government of India Act 1935, Constitution (Ninth Amendment) Act, 1960

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Constitution (Ninth Amendment) Act, 1960 - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Government of India Act, 1935 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constiution of India, 1950, arts. 3 and 368 - (A) Whether a law of Parliament relatable to art. 3 of the Constitution is sufficient for implementation of agreements of Exchange of Enclaves or is an amendment of the Constitution in accordance with art. 368 necessary for the purpose, in addition or in the alternative? - When it is contended that the division had to be made by a strict mathematical line, the fact that the provision made no reference to any tangential planes or geometrical lines has to be taken into account - Held, a law of Parliament relatable to art. 368 of the Constitution is competent and necessary - A law of Parliament relatable to both art. 368 and art. 3 would be necessary only if Parliament chooses first to pass a law amending art. 3 - (B) Whether when the provision says, that the division shall be made horizontal, it only means that it was not to be vertical; it had to be according to the latitude and not according to the longitude? - Held, the power to cede a part of the national territory and the power to acquire additional territory were the inherent attributes of sovereignty; and if any part of the national territory was intended to be ceded, a law relating to art. 3 alone would not be enough unless appropriate action was taken by the Indian Parliament under art. 368.
