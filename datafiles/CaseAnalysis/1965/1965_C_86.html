John Mowlem and Company Limited v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Orissa

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 71; 1967 (19) STC 59

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Appellants constructing tunnels, surge shafts and pipe tunnels etc. for Govt. of Madras - Sales Tax Officer treating the appellants as "dealer" and assessed turnover to tax - Issue (A): Whether the contract for tunnel-holding between the appellant and the Govt. of Madras is not composite and indivisible, but contains a separate and distinct contract for the supply of material, viz., plant as such, and the value of the plant being liable for the sales tax? - Held, the High Court was right in holding that there was a severable contract relating to the plant, machinery and equipment from the works contract - Issue (B): Whether the contract entered into with the appellant by the Govt. of Madras for tunnel-holding is in the nature of a contract of agency where the goods are Govt. property from the inception, and there is no passing of title in them, at any time, from the company to the Govt.? - Held (B): It is not possible to raise an inference from the clause, "the final accounting for the plant, and its passing into the hands of the purchaser will take place" in para 8 of the contract, that till it was delivered at the site of the Machkund Dam, the appellants were the owners of the plant. The clause deals merely with the obligations undertaken by the appellants for "transportation and insurance" of the plant, and not with the passing of property in the plant from the appellants to the State of Madras.
