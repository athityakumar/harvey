 Union of India v Sukumar Pyne

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 273; AIR 1966 SC 1206; 1966 CRLJ 946; 1966 (2) SCJ 227; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Summary: Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 23(1)(a) and 23 D(As substituted and added respectively vide amendment Act, 1957) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 20(2) - Proceedings for contravention of s. 23(1) - Before amendment in 1957, there lied only criminal proceedings before a court and after amendment, provision was made for adjudication proceedings as well - Contravention u/s. 23(1) committed before amendment - Adjudication proceedings sought to be commenced with respect to such contravention - Whether adjudication proceedings valid on touchstone of art. 20 of Constitution? - Held, yes - There is no principle underlying art. 20 which makes a right to any course of procedure a vested right - In matter of procedure it is not necessary that there should be a special provision to indicate that new procedural law is retrospective - Appeal allowed.
