 Management Utkal Machinery Limited v Workmen, Miss Shanti Patnaik

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1966 SC 1051; 1966 (12) FLR 45; 1966 (1) LLJ 398; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether termination of services of respondent by management is legal and justified and if not, what relief she is entitled to? - In absence of any Standing Order unsatisfactory work of an employee may be treated as misconduct and when respondent was discharged according to management for unsatisfactory work it should be taken that her discharge was tantamount to punishment for an alleged misconduct - Held, Labour Court found that there was no proof of alleged misconduct on part of respondent and there was no justification for terminating her services and in face of complete absence of evidence in regard to unsatisfactory work of respondent discharge of respondent from service was mala fide - View taken by Labour Court is correct - Further held, no special circumstances for 'awarding compensation to two years' salary - Having regard to these considerations amount of compensation awarded by Labour Court should be reduced - Appeal partly allowed.
