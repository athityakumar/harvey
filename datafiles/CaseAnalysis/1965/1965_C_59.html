Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P., Nagpur and Bhandara v Seth Govindram Sugar Mills

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 284; AIR 1966 SC 24; [1965] 57 ITR 510; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 488

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 42 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16(1)(b) - Deed of partnership executed between two persons representing their joint families - One partner died - Under partnership deed, legal heirs of deceased partner to become partner - Firm applied for registration before ITO which was denied on ground that partnership was dissolved on death of one partner and no new partnership was formed - Whether the High Court rightly held that the partnership business was carried on by representatives after death of one partner - S. 42 of Partnership Act, 1932 operates only in case of partnership firm having more than two partners - Held, in the present case, the partnership was immediately dissolved on the death of partner and no partnership constituted between legal heirs - Appeals dismissed.
