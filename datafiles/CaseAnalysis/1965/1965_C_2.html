State of Kerala v C. Velukutty

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 309; [1966] 60 ITR 239; 1966 (17) STC 465

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Travancore Cochin General Sales Tax Act, s. 12(2)(b) - Best judgment assessment - Sales tax assessments - Surprise inspection athead office - Transactions disclosed in secret books were 135% of turnover recorded in regular accounts and on that basis added 135% to turnover disclosed in regular books of said office - Applied same percentage in regard to assessment of turnover of branch office - On appeal, High Court set aside assessment order on ground that assessment was based on conjecture and rejcted plea of State that matter should be remanded for a fresh assessment - Whether HC was wrong in holding that best judgment assessment was capricious? - U/s. 12(2)(b) of Act, power is conferred on assessing authority in circumstances mentioned thereunder to assess dealer to best of his judgment - Though there is an element of guess-work in a 'best judgment assessment', it shall not be a wild one, but shall have a reasonable nexus to available material and circumstances of each case - Held, there was no material before assessing authority relevant to assessment and impugned assessments were arbitrarily made by applying a ratio between disclosed and concealed turnover in one shop to another shop of assessee - HC was right in setting aside assessments orders - Appeal dismissed.
