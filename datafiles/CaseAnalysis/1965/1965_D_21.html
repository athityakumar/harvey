 State of Orissa v Durga Charan Das

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, P. Satyanarayana Raju, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 19; AIR 1966 SC 1547; 1967 (1) LLJ 396; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 907

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Protection Rules, 1936, r. 1936 - Appointment - Promotions - Reversion - Pension - Challenge - Respondent was promoted various post during his service and he was reverted to his substantive post and later, he was again promoted to officiate as Registrar in Supply Department - With various promotions and ultimately he was confirmed as Registrar Eventually, he retired from service - At time of his retirement, dispute arose regarding pension fixed by appellant/State, to which respondent made various claims and appellant rejected prayers made by respondent - Respondent filed petition before HC and he claimed appropriate remedy in which HC held that in regard to alternative claim made by respondent and HC directed appellant to re-fix pension payable to respondent - Hence, instant Appeal - Held, there could be no doubt that interpretation placed by Joint Public Service Commission on Protection Rules, or opinion expressed by Governor, though perhaps relevant, could not had material bearing on construction of Rule in question when matter reached Court - However, if promotion to selection post was outside terms of r. 6 of the Rules, it would be difficult to entertain claim made by respondent on basis that his junior had been promoted to post of Registrar and confirmed as such on same date on which he was promoted - As promotion to selection post depends upon several relevant factors, it was unreasonable and impracticable to determine question by reference to another enquiry as to when officers junior to respondent were promoted - Further, appellant was anxious to have decision from SC on his point, as case would serve as test case and may be relied upon as precedent by several public servants who belong to category of respondent - Therefore, order passed by HC was set aside and petition filed by respondent was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
