Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh Etc v Messrs Straw Products Limited, Bhopal

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 209; AIR 1966 SC 1113; 1966 (1) ITJ 518; [1966] 60 ITR 156; 1966 (1) SCJ 610; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Taxation Laws (Merged States Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1949 - Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) (Amendment) Order, 1962 - Whether on the facts of the case and having regard to para 2 of the Taxation Laws Order and cl. 8 of the agreement made on 20-09-1938, between the assessee and the State of Bhopal, the correct basis for computing the written down value of the depreciable assets is the one which is adopted by the ITO or the one adopted by the Appellate Asst. Commr.? - We cannot agree that the expression "actually allowed under any laws or rules of a merged State" occurring in para 2 of the Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1949, meant allowable under the provisions of the said laws or rules, the expression "actually allowed" is unambiguous and connotes the idea that the allowance was actually given effect to - If it was intended to include any allowances which are not actually allowed then the Central Govt. would have added a deeming provision - Held, the question framed by the Appellate Tribunal is wide enough to include a discussion of the amendments made retrospectively in the Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1949 - Appeal allowed
