 Bishambar Nath Kohli and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 251; AIR 1966 SC 573; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - Administration Of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - Displaced Persons (Compensation And Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Administration Of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 27 and 58(3) - Whether in a given case, Custodian-General may entertain a petition against an order passed by a subordinate authority, notwithstanding gross delay in instituting proceeding is a matter within his discretion, especially when titles of persons other than those directly concerned in order sought to be revised, have intervened? - It is difficult to escape conclusion that order would be subject to appellate and revisional jurisdiction of authorities who have appellate or revisional power by virtue of provisions conferring those powers and which must also be deemed to have been in force at date when impugned order was passed - Held, Custodian-General has authority to entertain a petition in such a case but principles of natural justice must be followed, if not, proceedings must fail - Appeal allowed.
