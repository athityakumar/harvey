First Income Tax Officer, Salem v Short Brothers Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 358; AIR 1967 SC 81; [1966] 60 ITR 83; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 84

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art.226 - Finance Act, 1955 - Finance Act, 1956 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 18, 2(6A)(c) - Resolution - Liquidation - Assessment - Capital appreciation - Accumulated profits - Capital gains - Respondent sold its coffee estates and other assets and by resolution, it was resolved that it be voluntarily wound up and liquidators be appointed to administer its affairs - Out of proceeds realized by sale of its assets, liquidators of Company distributed Rs. 8,50,000 to shareholders - Liquidators submitted that amount distributed to shareholders was capital appreciation realized by sale of agricultural lands and buildings of Company and was not liable to tax and that in any event amounts distributed represented current profits of year in which it was resolved that Company be wound up and were on that account not dividend within meaning of s. 2(6A)(c) of 1922 Act - HC would not entertain a petition in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution when party claiming relief had an alternative remedy which was adequate and efficacious - Hence instant appeal - Whether capital gains arising from sale of capital assets was within income tax officer's authority - Held, capital gains u/s. 12B were chargeable in respect of any profits arising from transfer of capital assets and capital assets did not include lands from which income derived was agricultural income, Profits derived by transfer of lands from which income, derived was agricultural income would not be chargeable on a combined reading of ss. 12B , 2 (4A) of 1922 Act under head capital gains - Expression accumulated profits did not include capital gains arising within excepted periods vide Explanation to s. 2 (6A) - Accumulated profits were profits which were so regarded in commercial practice and capital gains as defined in 1922 Act - Realization of appreciated value of assets in commercial practice was regarded as realization of capital rise and not of profits of business - Appreciation in value of capital assets was included in capital gains, distribution by liquidator of rise in capital value would not be deemed dividend for purpose of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
