 Kamala Mills Limited v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 295; AIR 1965 SC 1942; [1965] 57 ITR 643; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 64; 1965 (16) STC 613

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Taxes - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, ss. 20, 21, 22, 22B - Special sales tax on sales outside the State - Claim for refund - Institution of civil suit - Maintainability - Appellant/Mills sold its goods within and outside the respondent/State - Sales Tax Authority levied general sales tax on the sales within the State and special sales tax on the sales outside the State - Appellant filed an Original Suit before the HC seeking refund of the special tax paid for their sales outside the State - Respondents contended that the Court had no jurisdiction to entertain the suit and s. 20 of the Act created a bar against the institution of the present suit, and the suit should, therefore, be dismissed on that preliminary ground - Single Judge dismissed the suit holding that s. 20 of the Act was a bar to the institution of the suit for refund and on that view, he upheld the plea raised by the respondent - Appellant filed Letters Patent Appeal - DB dismissed appellant's appeal confirming the Single Judge order - Whether s. 20 creates any bar against the institution of civil suit for claiming the refund - Held, all questions pertaining to the liability of the dealers to pay assessment in respect of their transactions are expressly left to be decided by the appropriate authorities under the Act as matters falling within their jurisdiction - Whole activity of assessment beginning with the filing of the return and ending with an order of assessment, falls within the jurisdiction of the appropriate authority and no part of it can be said to constitute a collateral activity not specifically and expressly included in the jurisdiction of the appropriate authority as such - S. 21 of the Act provides for the remedy of an appeal and s. 22 of the Act provides for a revisional remedy before the appropriate authority and if the appellant had pursued a remedy available to it u/ss. 21 or 22 r/w s. 22B, its case would have been considered by the appropriate authority and the validity of the grounds set up by it for the refund of the tax in question would have been legally examined - S. 20 constitute an absolute bar against the institution of the present suit - Appropriate authorities dealt with the matter in regard to the taxability of the impugned transactions in accordance with the provisions of the Act and in consequence, tax in question was recovered on the basis that the said transactions were taxable under the Act - Transactions in question fell within the purview of the charging section cannot be said to be without jurisdiction or a nullity and the assessment based even on an erroneous conclusion of the assessment authority would fall u/ s. 20 of the Act - Therefore, present suit is non-maintainable as per the provisions of s. 20 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.
