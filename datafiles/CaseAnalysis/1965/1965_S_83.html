 Singareni Collieries Company Limited v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 246; AIR 1966 SC 563; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 110; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 190; 1966 (17) STC 197

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Colliery Control Order, 1945

Summary: Sales Tax - Colliery Control Order, 1945 - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 286, 286(1) and 286(2) - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950 - Whether appellant-company is liable to pay sales tax assessed under Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950 on price of coal supplied to allottees outside taxing State pursuant to directions of Coal Commr. issued under Colliery Control Order, 1945? - Held, company which has its registered office at Hyderabad in former Part 'B' State of Hyderabad, and now in State of AP, carried on business of mining coal from its collieries and supplying it to consumers within and outside State of Hyderabad - Appeals relate to three financial years 1954-55, 1955-56 and 1956-57, during which coal was a controlled commodity, and its disposal and use could be made only under orders issued by appropriate authority under Colliery Control Order, 1945 - Constitution with a view to prevent imposition of manifold taxes on same transaction of sale imposed by art. 286, restrictions on levy of sale and purchase taxes on certain classes of transactions - Art. 286, thus imposed qua sales four bans upon legislative power of States - Cl.(2) State was prohibited from imposing tax on sale of goods where such sale took place in course of inter-State trade or commerce - But ban could be removed by legislation made by Parliament - Held, coal, in appeals under review was transported from colliery of company to consumers outside taxing State, as a result of covenant or incident of contract of sale and therefore, sale must be regarded as an inter-State sale and not liable to be taxed under Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950 - High Court was in error in holding that turnover of company in which coal was loaded in railway wagons for conveyance to places outside taxing State was taxable under Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act - Order passed by HC set aside - Order accordingly.
