 Mangaldas Raghavji Ruparel & Anr. v The State Of Maharashtra & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 166; AIR 1966 SC 128; 1966 CRLJ 106; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 894

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Health & Drug - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss.16(1)(a)(ii), 11, 2(1) and 7(v) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.537(b) and 182 - Purchase for analysis - (A) Whether transaction like present for analysis amounts to a sale? - Held, s.2(xiii), specifically includes within its ambit a sale for analysis - Transaction like present do amount to sale - (B) Whether Trial Court had jurisdiction to try appellant as he had not committed any offence within its jurisdiction? - Further held, where offence is committed partly in one local area and partly in another or where an offence is a continuing one and continues to be committed in more local areas than one or where it consists of several acts done in different local areas, it may be inquired into or tried by a court having jurisdiction over any of such local areas - Trial Court had jurisdiction to try appellant - (C) Whether wrong place of trial would vitiate same? - Further held, mere fact that proceedings were taken in a wrong place would not vitiate trial unless it appears that this has occasioned a failure of justice - Appeal dismissed.
