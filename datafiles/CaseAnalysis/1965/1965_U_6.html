 Hari Krishna Bhargav v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 272; AIR 1966 SC 619; 1966 (1) ITJ 77; [1966] 59 ITR 243; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Annuity, Compulsory Deposit Scheme Act, 1963

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Compulsory Deposit Scheme Act, 1963 - Income Tax Act, 1961, Chap.XXII-A - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Whether Chap.XXII-A of Income Tax Act(IT Act) is unconstitutional and is otherwise violative of fundamental right guaranteed by art. 14? - Scheme of Chap.XXII-A is that certain classes of taxpayers in comparatively higher income groups are required to make out of their total income deposits at specified rates on adjusted total income, with Central Govt. - Amount so deposited is made returnable with interest in ten annual instalments - Income Tax Act, 1961 and Compulsory Deposit Scheme Act, are separate statutes, but that does not mean that Parliament has no power to incorporate it within Income Tax Act, 1961, if it so desired - If Annuity Deposit Act were enacted as a separate Act, several provisions requiring references to Income Tax Act, 1961 and conferment of power upon authorities constituted under Income Tax Act, 1961 would have had to be duplicated - To avoid repetition and cross references Legislature has thought it proper to enact within Income Tax Act, 1961 - Art. 14 guarantees equality before law, and equal protection of laws - But, thereby power of Legislature to make a reasonable classification of persons, objects or transactions for attaining certain objectives is not excluded - If a classification is based on some real and substantial distinction, bearing a just and reasonable relation to objects sought to be achieved, it is valid - Held, Chap.XXII-A is valid - Petition dismissed.
