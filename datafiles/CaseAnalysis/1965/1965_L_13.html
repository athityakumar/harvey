 Ajoy Kumar Mukherjee v Local Board Of Barpeta.

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1965 SC 1561; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Assam Local Self Government Act 1953

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Assam Local Self Government Act, 1953, s. 62 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, item-49 of List II of sch. VII - Local Boards - Levy of annual tax for use of any land for purpose of holding markets - Constitutionality thereof - Appellant, a landholder, wherein, he holds market since 1936 - In 1953-54, Local Board under whose jurisdiction land in question lied, issued notice to appellant to take out licence and for payment of licence fees - (A) Legislative competence of State of Assam to tax markets, challenged - Held, proposition settled as to, that use to which land is put can be taken into account in imposing a tax on it within meaning of entry 49 of List II - For annual value of land, which can certainly be taken into account in imposing a tax for purpose of this entry would necessarily depend upon use to which land is put - Held, tax is on land, though incidence depends upon use of land as a market, thus imposition thereof within competence of State Legislature under entry 49 of List II - No facts and evidence in support, thereof, produced by appellant for alleged violation of art. 14 - Allegations raised also vague - Further held, violation of art. 14 not made out - Appeal dismissed.
