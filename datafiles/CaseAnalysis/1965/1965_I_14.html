 S. A. L. Narayan Row and Another v Ishwarlal Bhagwandas and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 297

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23, 35 and 18A(6) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 132(1) and 133(1)(c) - (A) 'Civil proceeding' within meaning of art. 133 - Held, there is no ground for restricting expression 'civil proceeding' only to those proceedings which arise out of civil suits or proceedings which are tried as civil suits, nor is there any rational basis for excluding from its purview proceedings instituted and tried in High Court in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226, where aggrieved party seeks relief against infringement of civil rights by authorities purporting to act in exercise of powers conferred upon them by revenue statutes - (B) Whether HC was right in setting aside order which was passed by Commr. without considering proviso to s. 18 A(6)? - Held, by virtue of retrospective amendment in s. 18A(6) order which was made by ITO on date of assessment and which was plainly inconsistent with terms of s. as it then stood became one which he was competent to pass in exercise of his power - Case of assessee did fall within terms of r. 48(1) and ITO must in law be bound to consider whether he was entitled to reduction or waiver of interest under fifth proviso - Matter was brought before Commr. of Income Tax and it is unfortunate that Commr. in considering matter u/s. 33A assumed that amending Act had no retrospective operation and rejected claim of assessee on ground that at date when order of assessment was made, amendment had not come into operation - In so holding, Commr. committed an error of law apparent on face of record - HC was therefore right in setting aside order which was passed by Commr. without considering proviso to s. 18 A(6) which was clearly applicable to case of assessee and in light of r. 48 which was enacted in pursuance of that proviso - Appeal dismissed.
