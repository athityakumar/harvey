Tek Bahadur Bhujil v Debi Singh Bhujil and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 494; AIR 1966 SC 292

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Respondent instituted a suit for partition of his half share in property as well as his half share of business for separate possession of his such half share by metes and bounds - Appellant contested suit on various grounds, including one that all properties were self-acquired properties - Trial Court decreed suit against appellant holding that properties in suit were joint properties and that there had been a division of family properties with separate possession and enjoyment by parties - Appellant filed revision to High Court - Respondent relied on a family agreement in support of alleged division of property - Appellant contended that said agreement is not a family arrangement and if it does amount to a family arrangement, it required registration - Contention of appellant about agreement, being not a record of a family arrangement is based on several grounds - Respondent 1 was a uterine brother of appellant and respondent 2 and therefore could not be a member of their family and there could not be a family arrangement between members of family and a non-member and secondly, there was no dispute that two businesses belonged to respondent No. 2 - Whether these contentions are sustainable? - Held, there had been a family arrangement between appellant and respondent No. 1 and that agreement is a record of that family arrangement - Next contention is that agreement required registration and is of no effect as it was not registered under Indian Registration Act - This agreement did not require registration even if Registration Act was in force in that area at time of its execution - Agreement does not recognize that any of brothers had sole and absolute title to any of properties dealt with by them - On other hand recitals in document indicate that three brothers considered property to be joint property of all of them - Appeal dismissed.
