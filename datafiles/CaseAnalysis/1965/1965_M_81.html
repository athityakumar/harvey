 Bharat Kala Bhandar Private Limited v Municipal Committee, Dhamangaon

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 283; AIR 1966 SC 249; 1967 (69) BomLR 69; 1965 (2) ITJ 657; [1966] 59 ITR 73; 1966 MahLJ 185; 1966 MPLJ 201; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [Mudholkar J.

(A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Civil Procedure - Central Provinces Municipalities Act, 1922, ss.66(1)(b), 48 - Levy of excess tax - Recovery - Appellant was private limited company which owned ginning factory - Notified Area Committee imposed tax, u/s. 66(1)(b) of the Act - Certain rules were framed by Govt. for assessment and collection of tax - Those rules were amended by Local Govt. - Municipal Committee which had replaced Notified Area Committee proposed to raise tax but eventually dropped proposal - Appellant and other factory owners instituted suits for recovery from Municipal Committee of excess tax paid by them within 3 years of dates of respective suits - During pendency of suit, petition was filed by appellant against alleged enhancement of tax which was allowed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether suit by assessee to recover amount paid by him in excess of Constitutional limit would be in respect of matter 'purported to be done' under the Act and provisions of s. 48 of the Act would apply to it - Held, general principle was that if any public or private body charged with execution of Statute honestly intended to put law in motion and not unreasonably believed in existence of facts which, if existed, would justify his acting and acts accordingly then his conduct would be in pursuance of Statute and would be protected - Committee had jurisdiction to recover amount up to constitutional limit - However, it could not fairly be contended on its behalf that merely because of that, recovery of amount in excess of Constitutional limit by it was only irregular or at worst illegal - Thus, where power existed to assess and recover tax up to particular limit and assessment or recovery of anything above that amount was prohibited, assessment or recovery of amount in excess was wholly without jurisdiction and therefore, s. 48 of the Act would not apply - Thus, Statute under which action was purported to be taken could afford no protection to municipal committee - Hence, Municipal Committee could not be said to be acting 'under the Act' when it did that which was expressly prohibited by Legislature - Appeal allowed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Central Provinces Municipalities Act, 1922, ss.48, 85(2) - Recovery of tax - Bar of suit - Maintainability - Whether provisions of s. 85(2) of the Act barred suit for recovery of tax wrongfully recovered by Municipal Committee - Held, Court should lean in favour of construing law in such way as not to take away right and render illusory protection afforded by Constitution - No machinery was provided by the Act for obtaining refund of tax assessed and recovered in excess of Constitutional limit and that machinery actually provided by the Act was not adequate for enabling assessee to challenge effectively constitutionality or legality of assessment or levy of tax by Municipality or to recover from it what was realized under invalid law - Thus, it could not be inferred that jurisdiction of Civil Court was barred - Appellant had claimed in Suit only repayment of amount recovered by Municipal Committee in excess of that which under Constitution, it was competent to recover from appellant - Further, in view of decision of HC, Trial Court in suit had to ascertain only amount which was payable to appellant and whether suit was barred u/ss. 48, 85(2) of the Act - Further question raised in suit was as to jurisdiction of AO to proceed against assessee and levy on or collect from him amount in excess of that permitted by Constitution - Thus, matter would be entirely out of bar of provision of s.85(2) of the Act - Hence, appellant's suit was not barred - Appeal allowed.

Raghubar Dayal J.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Central Provinces Municipalities Act, 1922,ss.48(1),84(3) - Assessment of tax - Recovery of amount - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Whether respondent's collecting amount in excess of amount was ' act done or purported to be done under the Act' within meaning of s. 48(1) of the Act and suit filed by appellant was barred by s. 84(3) of the Act - Held, suit filed by appellant was covered by provisions of sub-sec.(1) of s. 48 of the Act - However, condition precedent for institution of said suit was not satisfied - Further u/s. 84(3) of the Act, assessment of tax or liability of person assessed or taxed could not be objected to in any manner or before any authority other than what was provided in the Act - Correctness of assessment could not be questioned by any other manner and questioning by instituting suit in Civil Court would be one such other manner - Suit filed by appellant was suit for modification of taxes assessed and for consequential order decreeing repayment of amount held to have been collected in excess of amount so modified - Thus, as per s. 84(3) of the Act, exclusive jurisdiction to determine correctness of amount assessed was given to authorities mentioned in s.83 of the Act - Therefore, suit filed by appellant was barred from cognizance of Civil Court - Further Committee's overlooking constitutional provisions in exercise of its jurisdiction to assess tax would not make its assessment of tax without jurisdiction but would only show that Committee made mistake of law in course of exercise of its jurisdiction - It could be inferred that when Committee made assessment of tax on appellant, assessment was founded on Activity of Committee acting as assessing authority and fact that it overlooked constitutional requirement was immaterial in determining whether assessment was made under the Act - Expression 'made under the Act' had no relation to accuracy of assessment in point of law - Hence, assessment made on appellant was assessment made under the Act and that of, illegal collection with respect to amount in excess was act done under the Act - Appeal dismissed.
