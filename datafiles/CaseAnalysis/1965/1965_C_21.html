Kedarnath Jute Manufacturing Company v Commercial Tax Officer, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1966 SC 12; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 626; 1965 (16) STC 607

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Registered Dealer, Declaration Form, Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, ss. 5(2) (a) (ii) and 21 A- Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1941, r. 27 A - 'On demand' expression - Implication of - Interpretation of s. 5(2)(a) (ii) of Act - Appellant claiming exemption u/s. 5(2)(a) (i) and (ii) of Act- Declaration forms to be furnished - (A)Whether a dealer is entitled to exemption u/s. 5(2)(a)(ii) of Act of 1941 if he does not furnish declaration in prescribed form? - In Order to prevent fraud and facilitate administrative efficiency, exemption u/s. 5(2)(a)(ii) of Act of 1941, is made subject to a condition that person claiming same shall furnish a declaration form in manner prescribed under section - Held, A dealer is not entitled to exemption under substantive part of s. 5(2)(a)(ii) of Act of 1941 if he does not furnish declaration in prescribed form - (B) Whether expression on demand in r. 27-A(3),(4) of Rules of 1941 make furnishing of declaratory forms directory? - R. 27-A(3),(4) of Rules of 1941 provide only safeguards against abuse of declaration forms by purchasing dealers; they do not enable selling dealer to either directly apply or to compel purchasing dealers to apply for duplicate forms; they do not also enjoin appropriate authority to give selling dealer a duplicate form to replace lost one Further held, expression 'On demand' in R. 27 A only fixes point of time when declaration forms are to be produced; it does not make furnishing of declaratory forms directory, otherwise rule would be inconsistent with section - Appeal dismissed.
