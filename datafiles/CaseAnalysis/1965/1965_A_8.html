Aghnoo Nagesia v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1966 SC 119; 1966 CRLJ 100; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss.162 and 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29 and 30 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302 and 304A - Murder - FIR lodged by appellant himself - No eye-witness - Whether statement of appellant in FIR is admissible evidence for conviction? - S. 27 applies only to information received from a person accused of an offence in custody of a police officer - Now, Sub-Inspector stated that he arrested appellant after he gave first information report leading to discovery - Prima facie, therefore, appellant was not in custody of a police officer when he gave report, unless it can be said that he was in constructive custody - On question whether a person directly giving to a police officer information which may be used as evidence against him may be deemed to have submitted himself to custody of police officer within meaning of s. 27, there is conflict of opinion - Held, for purposes of case, court shall assume that appellant was constructively in police custody and therefore information contained in first information report leading to discovery of dead bodies and tangi is admissible in evidence - Entire evidence against appellant then consists of fact that appellant gave information as to place where dead bodies were lying and as to place where he concealed tan, discovery of dead bodies and tangi in consequence of information, discovery of a blood-stained chadar from appellant's house and fact that he had gone to place of occurrence on morning of 11-08-1963 - This evidence is not sufficient to convict appellant of offenses u/s. 302 of IPC, 1860 - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
