 Municipal Board, Hapur v Raghuvendra Kripal and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 255; AIR 1966 SC 693; 1966 ALT 205; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 94(3), 128, 128(1)(x), 135(2) and 135(3) - Municipal Board passed a special resolution imposing water tax in Hapur - Notification by Govt. of U.P. was published in Uttar Pradesh Gazette u/s. 135(2) of U.P. Municipalities Act, 1916 D/- 11-12-1956 notifying resolution - Challenged - (A) Whether tax was illegal as it was imposed in contravention of provisions of Municipalities Act? - Held, s. 128 of Municipalities Act confers on municipalities in Uttar Pradesh power to levy taxes and enumerates kinds of taxes - Water tax on annual value of building or land or both is one of tax that can be levied - (B) Resolution of appellant-Board framing proposal was not published in a local paper of Hapur printed in Hindi - Non-compliance of s. 94(3) - Whether this omission renders imposition of tax illegal? - Held, it is duty of Govt. to see that various steps laid down for imposition of tax are followed, before it notifies resolution Govt. satisfies itself about requirements - Complaint is of a breach of one of provisions which can only be regarded as directory - Once Govt. condones departure, decision of Govt. is rightly made final by making notification conclusive evidence of compliance with requirements of Act - First part of s. 131(3) requiring publication of proposals was mandatory and second part which required that publication should be in manner required by s. 94(3) was only directory - Imposition would have protection of s. 135(3) and tax must be deemed to be imposed according to procedure laid down in Act - (C) Whether s. 135(3) of Act was ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 because it created a bar against proof and left no remedy to tax payers, thereby making a discrimination between them and other litigants? - Held, tax payers in municipality are allowed under Municipalities Act to object to proposal for tax and rules and to have their objections considered - Rules, objections and orders and has approved of proposal, a special resolution is passed by Municipal Board and a notification is issued - It cannot be said that sub-s. (3) of s. 135 which leads to conclusion that imposition of tax is according to Municipalities Act is discriminatory because it only concludes objections against procedure followed in imposition of tax - Sub-section only shuts out further enquiry and makes notification final - There is no exercise of a judicial function - (D) Whether there has been excessive delegation, inasmuch as State Govt. has been given power to condone breaches of Act and thus to set at naught Act itself? - Power to tax is conferred on State Legislature but is exercised by local authority under control of State Govt. - Selection of tax for imposition in a Municipal area is by legislative will, expressed in s. 128 - Neither Municipal Board, nor Govt. can go outside list of taxes therein included - Procedure for imposition of tax is also laid down by Legislature for Municipal Board to follow and State Govt. is there to ensure due observance of that procedure - Provisions, such as they are, are best means of achieving consultation of local population and close scrutiny of actions of their representatives in imposing tax - Notification which issues is given finality by voice of Legislature - This task is delegated to appellant-Board which is representative body of local population on whom tax is levied - There was no excessive delegation or a conferral of legislative functions on appellant Board or State Govt. - Judgment of High Court under appeal must be set aside - Appeal allowed.
