Sales Tax Officer, Jodhpur And Another v M/S. Shiv Ratan G. Mohatta

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1966 SC 142; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 71; 1965 (16) STC 599

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Taxability

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, s. 2(f) - Whether High Court is right in going into disputed questions of fact? - Held, Sales Tax Officer had not dealt with question at all, and it is not function of HC u/art. 226, in taxing matters, to constitute itself into an original authority or an appellate authority to determine questions relating to taxability of a particular turnover- Proper order in circumstances of this case would have been to quash order of assessment and send case back to Sales Tax Officer to dispose of it according to law - If any facts are not found by Sales Tax Officer, they would be found by appellate authority and it is not function of a HC to find facts - Order of HC set aside - Matter remitted back to sales tax officer - To be decided untrammelled by views expressed by HC - Appeal allowed.
