State of Madhya Pradesh(Now Maharashtra) v Haji Hasan Dada

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 235; AIR 1966 SC 905; 1966 JabLJ 858; 1966 MahLJ 929; 1966 MPLJ 833; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 854; 1966 (17) STC 343

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946

Summary: Indirect Tax - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - States Reorganization Act, 1956 - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax, 1947, ss. 13 and 23 - Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 13 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, s. 13 - Indian Income Tax Act 1986, s. 66(3) - Whether application is maintainable u/s. 13 of Act in those cases where assessment order is not set aside by appropriate proceedings?- Held, application was not maintainable u/s. 13 as originally framed as section implies that refund may be granted only of amount which is not lawfully due and it is for Asst. Commr. to determine whether a certain amount is lawfully due or not, in making order of assessment or re-assessment - Appeal allowed.
