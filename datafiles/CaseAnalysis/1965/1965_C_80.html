Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v Gangadhar Banerjee and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 374; AIR 1965 SC 1977; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 620; [1965] 57 ITR 176; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Undistributed

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act 1922, s.23 A - Section refers to accounting profits - Considering significance of words 'having regard to' - (A) How to determine 'Reasonableness dividend' - (B) Determination of commercial profits - (C) Determination of profits when balance sheet is not final - (D) On whom does burden of proof lies - Held, no decisive tests but on facts of case, any larger dividend than that declared by Co. could not reasonably be distributed - Tax actually assessed can be deducted - Parties can produce other evidence for determination - Burden is on revenue to prove that the conditions laid down thereunder were satisfied before the order was made - Appeal dismissed.
