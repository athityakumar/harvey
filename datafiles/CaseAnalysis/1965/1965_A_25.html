Anda and Others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 489; AIR 1966 SC 148; 1966 CRLJ 171

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300 and 302 - Appellants convicted u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 - Whether conviction for murder justifiable? - When a number of persons participate in commission of a criminal act responsibility may be individual, that is to say, that each person may be guilty of a different offence or all of them may be liable for total result produced - This depends on the intention and knowledge of participants - Held, in present case, accused were present at spot by previous arrangement - Injuries were not on a vital part of body and no weapon was used which can be described as specially dangerous - It cannot, therefore, be said safely that there was an intention to cause death within first clause of s.300 - Number of injuries show that every one joined in beating him - Looking at injuries caused in furtherance of common intention of all it is clear that injuries intended to be caused were sufficient to cause his death in ordinary course of nature even if it cannot be said that his death was intended - This is sufficient to bring the case within the third cl. of s. 300 - It cannot be said that offence was not murder but only culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Appeal dismissed.
