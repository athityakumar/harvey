 Morvi Mercantile Bank Limited and Another v Union of India, Through The General Manager, Central Railway, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 1965 SC 1954; 1966 (68) BomLR 61; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 629; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Documents Of Title

Summary: Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 137, 148, 172 and 178 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 2 (4) and 137 - Indian Railways Act, 1890, ss. 73 and 74-E - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 30(2) and 53(1) - (A) Whether owner of a railway receipt can pledge receipt? - Held, u/s. 178 of Indian Contract Act, s. 137 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 2(4) of sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Railway receipts are documents of title and therefore goods covering them can be pledged by transfer of documents - (B) Whether a mercantile agent has same authority and limitation to pledge as owner ? - Further held, yes, u/s. 178 of Indian Contract Act, 1872 a mercantile agent can have possession of documents of title with consent of owner whereby he has same authority to pledge documents - (C) Whether a person can confer on another higher title than he possesses? - Further held, no - According to ss.30(2) and, 53(1) of Sale of Goods Act, 1930 a person cannot confer on another a higher title than he possesses - Appeal dismissed.
