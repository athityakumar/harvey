 K. Anandan Nambiar and Another v Chief Secretary, Government of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1966 SC 657; 1966 CRLJ 586; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Anganbari Sevika, Constitutional Rights, Defence of India Rules, 1962, Defence of India Act, 1962, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Service - Defence of India Act, 1962, s. 40 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 352, 105 and 102 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 30 - Issues Raised - (A) Whether a member of a legislative body cannot be detained by an order u/r. 30(1)(b) of the Defence of India Rules, in light of the various articles of Constitution? - The argument, that so long as, the member of Parliament has not incurred any disqualification, he is entitled to exercise his rights as such member, cannot be accepted - Held, the true constitutional position, therefore, is that so far as a valid order of detention is concerned, a member of Parliament can claim no special status higher than that of an ordinary citizen and is as much liable to be arrested and detained under it as any other citizen - (B) Whether an order of detention issued by the President u/art. 359 is in accordance with art. 77? - Held, art. 77(2), which refers to orders and other instruments made and executed in the name of the President is wide enough to include the present order - Appeal fails
