 Jawaharmal v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 257; AIR 1966 SC 764; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Finance Act, 1961, Finance Act, 1963, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Bihar Taxation On Passengers And Goods (Carried By Public Service Motor Vehicles), Rajasthan Passengers and Goods Taxation (Amendment and Validation) Act 1964

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Bihar Taxation On Passengers and Goods (Carried By Public Service Motor Vehicles) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Rajasthan Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1959, s. 2 - Rajasthan Passengers and Goods Taxation (Amendment and Validation) Act 1964 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 255 - Whether Legislature can validate an earlier Act which is invalid by reason of non-compliance with art.255 by itself declaring by statutory provision that failure to comply with art. 255 can be cured by its own enactment, even if said enactment received assent of President? - Legislature appears to have clearly assumed that it can by itself cure infirmity resulting from non-compliance with art. 255 and all that it has to do in such a case is to obtain assent of President to its own view about its power to cure such an infirmity - Held, Legislature is incompetent to declare that failure to comply with art. 255 is of no consequence and, with respect, assent of President to such declaration also does not serve purpose which subsequent assent by President can serve u/art. 255 - Petition partly allowed.
