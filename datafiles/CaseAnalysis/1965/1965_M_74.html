Ishverlal Thakorelal Almaula (Deceased) After Him His Heirs and Legal Representatives v Motibhai Nagjibhai

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 498; AIR 1966 SC 459; 1965 (67) BomLR 645; 1966 (7) G.L.R. 233; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Agriculture & Agri Products - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 2, 5, 14 and 31 - Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939 - Mamlatdars' Courts Act, 1906 - Bombay Act, 1952 - Bombay Act, 1956, s. 43 C - Bombay General Clauses Act, 1904, s. 7 - (A) Whether rights of respondent in land conferred or recognised by virtue of Bombay Act, 1948 were extinguished on the enactment of Bombay Act, 1952, and by amendments made by Act of 1956 and those rights were not restored to respondent so as to prejudice appellant's claim to evict him? - Held, Bombay High Court had held in case of Maganbhai Jethabhai's case, [60 Bom LR 1383 (FB)] that proviso to s. 43-C affords protection to tenant if tenant had protection of Act of 1948 as originally enacted, notwithstanding that protection was taken away by Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 1952 and that protection of proviso to s. 43-C must be given to tenant even in cases where it is claimed in a suit filed before amendment was enacted, if suit is not finally disposed of and Court agrees with Bombay HC on both questions decided in said case - (B) Whether Civil Court was competent in suit filed by appellant to grant a decree for possession of land held by respondent? - Legislature has clearly expressed itself that issues required under Act 67 of 1948 to be decided by a Revenue Court, even if arising in a civil suit must be decided by Revenue Court and not by Civil Court -Held, District Judge's finding that Civil Court had no jurisdiction to hear suit and ultimate order passed by him dismissing plaintiff's suit are not strictly accurate; if appellant desired that questions relating to tenancy and its determination which arose in suit be tried by Mamlatdar as a Revenue Court, which that authority alone was competent to decide, District judge should have referred to Revenue Court those questions for determination and should not have proceeded to dispose of suit - Appeal remanded - By Act of 1948 rights of protected tenants acquired under Bombay Act of 1939 were, notwithstanding the repeal of that Act, preserved right to claim that every contractual tenancy is statutorily extended for a period of ten years, right to claim that tenancy may not be determined otherwise than in circumstances mentioned in s. 14, and in case of protected tenants subject to restrictions imposed by s. 34 right not be deprived of possession otherwise than by an order u/s 29 (2), were rights vested in respondent before Act of 1952 was enacted were and these and other rights were restored to tenants retrospectively from date on which Act 33 of 1952 was enacted by virtue of express provision contained in proviso to s.43 - Legislature having restored rights originally granted under Act 67 of 1948 with retrospective operation from date on which Act 33 of 1952 was enacted, a person sued, before Act 13 of 1956 was brought into force, could in a pending suit set up defence that he is entitled to rights of a tenant or a protected tenant - But order passed by District Judge dismissing suit cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
