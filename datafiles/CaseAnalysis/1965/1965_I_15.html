 Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay and Another v Ishwarlal Bhagwandas and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 158; AIR 1965 SC 1818; 1966 (68) BomLR 85; 1965 (2) ITJ 264; [1965] 57 ITR 149; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132, 133, 134 and 136 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 18-A and 23-B - Income Tax officer served notice to assessee for paying penal interest, order was confirmed by Commissioner and was quashed in appeal in High Court, however, HC granted certificate for appeal in Supreme Court - Whether HC has power u/art. 133 of Constitution to certify a proposed appeal against an order in proceeding not being 'civil proceeding', commenced by a petition for issue of a writ u/art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, expression 'civil proceeding' is not defined in Constitution, nor in General Clauses Act, and expression covers all proceedings in which a party asserts existence of a civil right conferred by civil law or by statute, and claims relief for breach thereof, and therefore, a proceeding for relief against infringement of civil right of a person is a civil proceeding even if infringement be in purported enforcement of a taxing statute - Appeal dismissed.
