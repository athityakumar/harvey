Thayyil Mammo and Another v Kottiath Ramunni and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 495; AIR 1966 SC 337

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Agriculture & Agri Products - Rent Control - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, ss. 2, 13, 95 and 132 - Malabar Tenancy Act, 1929, ss. 31 and 23 - Kerala Stay of Eviction Proceedings Act, 1957 - Kerala Agrarian Relations Act, 1960 - Whether contesting defendants are kanomdars and, therefore, tenants within meaning of Kerala Land Reform Act? - A registered instrument styled a release deed releasing right, title and interest of executant in any property in favour of releasee for valuable consideration may operate as a conveyance, if document clearly discloses an intention to effect a transfer - Held, Ex. B-2 clearly discloses an intention to transfer all rights of Baithan to defendants 1 to 5, and though the word 'surrender' is used and deed is styled as a release deed, it operates as an assignment, therefore, konam right under Ex. A-3 were duly vested in defendants 1 to 5, and they became kanomdars, and consequently, they are protected from eviction under Kerala Land Reforms Act - Appeal dismissed.
