 Brundaban Nayak v Election Commission Of India And Another.

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1965 SC 1892; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Commission, Dismissed

Summary: Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 190(3), 191(1), 192(1) and 192(2) - (A) Who can raise question of disqualification of legislator u/art. 192(1) of Constitution ? - Held, along with members of lLegislative Assembly, an ordinary citizen or voter can raise question in form of a complaint to Governor - Manner in which it can be raised is not relevant for purpose of its application - (B) Who can decide questions raised u/art. 192(1) of Constitution and hold enquiry u/art. 192(2)? - Further held, it is Governor - But it must be in accordance with opinion of Election Commission - Although some other authority can receive complaint and after initial investigation, send it on or refer it to Governor for his decision - Court cannot decide - When Governor receives complaint, and he forwards same to Election Commission, it can be assumed that Election Commission should proceed to try complaint before giving its opinion - Jurisdiction of Election Commission to hold enquiry is not affected merely because copies of complaint and order of reference have not been sent along with such notice - Complaints made u/art.192(1) must be disposed of as expeditiously as possible - (C) What is remedy when any member of Legislative Assembly is disqualified u/art. 191(1)? - Further held, they cannot continue to be a member of Legislative Assembly of a state and have to vacate seat - Obligation to vacate his seat as a result of his subsequent disqualification having been imposed by Constitution itself by art. 190(3)(a), any citizen can make a complaint to Governor alleging that such member of Legislative Assembly has incurred one of disqualifications mentioned in art. 191(1) and should vacate his seat - Appeal dismissed.
