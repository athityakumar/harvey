Karpagathachi And Ors. v Nagarathinathachi

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1965 SC 1752; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 61; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Family & Personal - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 6(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 96, O.41, r. 1 - Hindu Law - (A) Whether co-widows inheriting property as joint tenants can enforce an absolute partition?- Held, they cannot - Under Hindu law as it stood in 1924, two widows were entitled to enforce a partition of those properties so that each could separately possess and enjoy portion allotted to her - Neither could, without consent of other, enforce an absolute partition of estate so as to destroy right of survivorship - (B) Whether such an arrangement is repugnant to s. 6(a) of TPA, 1882 - Further held, it is not - Interest of each widow in properties, inherited by her was property, and this property together with incidental right of survivorship could be lawfully transferred - S.6(a) of TPA, 1882 prohibits transfer of bare chance of surviving widow taking entire estate as next heir of her husband on death of co-widow, but it does not prohibit transfer by widow of her present interest in properties inherited by her together with incidental right of survivorship - Each could agree to relinquish her right of survivorship in portion allotted to other - (C) Whether a new plea can be raised in appeal?- Further held, it cannot - In High Court, appellants raised contention for first time that two partition lists were required to be registered - This new contention could not be raised for first time in appeal - (D) Whether claimant has to establish that right of survivorship is extinguished ? - Further held, party asserting that partition had effect of extinguishing right of survivorship has to establish it - Once it is established he is discharged of such an onus - Appeal allowed.
