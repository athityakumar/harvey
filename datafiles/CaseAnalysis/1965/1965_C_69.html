T. S. Srinivasan v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1966 SC 984; [1966] 60 ITR 36; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 27; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Family Property, Assessable Entity

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.3 - Hindu undivided family - Income from sources - Existence of birth of son - Assessable entity - Appellant/assessee was Karta of a Hindu undivided family - Partial partition of family took place and 150 shares of Rs.1,000 each in private limited company were divided equally among coparceners - Assessee got 25 shares of value of Rs.25,000 - Assessee was service manager of company and also received substantial remuneration - Assessee claimed that income from all sources except salary should assessed in hands of Hindu undivided family consisting of himself and his son/V come into existence in or about March 1952 when V was conceived - Income Tax Officer(IO) thought that an unborn son would not come into picture therefore IO recognized family only from date of birth of child i.e.,11-12-1952 - Appellate Assistant Commissioner uphold view of IO - Appellate Tribunal referred question before HC that whether assessment of income of assessee other than salary in hands of assessee as an individual and not as a Hindu undivided family till 11-12-1952 for assessment year 1953-54 was valid - HC answered question against assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether doctrine of Hindu Law could applied for a Hindu undivided family as an assessable entity - Held, extremely difficult to reconcile doctrine of Hindu Law with provisions of 1922 Act - Not justified in introducing uncertainties and anomalies in working of Act by introducing doctrine for purpose of s.3 of Act - Not able to satisfy that any rights of son affected by not recognising his existence for purposes of s.3 of Act till he was actually born - Income tax was a liability and it could not have intention of legislature to impose a liability on persons yet unborn - Even if a Hindu undivided family was in existence towards end of accounting year, whole income received or accrued in the accounting year did not became assessable income of Hindu undivided family - When income and profits arose, they belonged to assessee as no Hindu undivided family was in existence - Position could not displaced by birth of son brought into existence of a Hindu undivided family - Agree with HC that answer to question must be in favour of revenue - Appeal dismissed.
