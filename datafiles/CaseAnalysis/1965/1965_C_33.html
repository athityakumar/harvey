H.E.H. Nizam's Religious Endowment Trusthyderabad v Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhra Pradesh, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 1966 SC 1007; [1966] 59 ITR 582; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 163; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 384

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4 - Income Tax Act - The Nizam of Hyderabad created a trust under which he settled certain securities of the face value of Rs.40 lakhs for the objective of the trust - Under the trust deed, three trustees were appointed, including the settlor - It further decided that income of the trust would be spent on the religious and charitable purpose mentioned in the deed - It was decided and declared that in all matters wherein the Trustees have a discretionary power, the votes of the majority of the Trustees for the time being voting in the matter shall prevail and be binding on the minority as well as on those Trustees who may not have voted and if the Trustees shall be equally divided in opinion, the matter shall during the life-time of the Settlor be decided according to the opinion of the Settlor and after his death according to the opinion of the Trustee most senior in age for the time being - It is important to notice that under the deed, no power was conferred on the trustees during the life time of the settlor to set apart and allocate the accumulated income or a part of it from the trust properties for any one or more of the objects - For the assessment years, 1952-53 and 1953-54 the Trustees were assessed to income tax on the income during the relevant previous years arising from the said trust property - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal, Hyderabad, concurrently held, that the assessee was not entitled to the exemption under the said section - At the instance of the assessee, the following question was referred to the High Court u/s. 66(1) of the Act - 'Whether the income arising from property settled upon trust under the deed of settlement, D/- 14-09-1950, or any part thereof is exempt from tax u/s. 4(3)(i) of the Indian Income Tax Act, 1922'? - The said desire does not amount to setting apart by the Trustees of the whole or a part of the income from the trust for purposes within the taxable territories - Indeed, cl. 3(d) of the trust deed indicates that the Trustees have no power to set apart or accumulate the income for any of the purposes mentioned in the trust deed till after the death of the settlor - We cannot, therefore, hold on the material placed before us, that the Trustees have set apart the accumulated income for purposes within the taxable territories - For the aforesaid reasons, we hold, that the answer given by the HC to the question referred to it by the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal is correct - Held, the appeals fail and are dismissed with costs
