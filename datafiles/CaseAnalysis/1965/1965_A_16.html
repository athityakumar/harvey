 State of Kerala v Aluminium Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 482; 1965 KLT 517; 1965 (16) STC 689

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 286(1) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 72 - Refund of sales tax - Respondent submitted returns for period from 30-05-1950 to 31-03-1951, and deposited necessary sales tax, contended that by mistake it did not claim exemption in connection with sales made to State of Punjab and asked for refund - Whether under said circumstances respondent could be entitled to refund? - Where tax is levied by mistake of law it is ordinarily duty of state to refund tax - If refund is not made, remedy through court is open subject to same restrictions and also to period of limitation - Held, papers will be sent to Agricultural Income-tax and Rural Sales Tax Officer, Quilon for a finding on question whether writ petition was within three years of date on which mistake first became known to respondent so that a suit on that date for refund would not be barred under Limitation Act, 1908 - Matter remitted to authority.
