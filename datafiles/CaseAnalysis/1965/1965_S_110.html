 Messrs Anwar Khan Mehboob and Company v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 392; AIR 1966 SC 1637; 1966 JabLJ 874; 1966 MPLJ 756; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Petitioner is a partnership firm which manufactures and sells, bidis, under name and style of Anwarkhan Mehboob and Co., Jabalpur - In 1948, petitioner firm, with a view to securing a supply of tendu leaves over years acquired for a term of 25 years, right to pluck and carry away tendu leaves from plants in ninety-nine villages in former Imlai Estate from Malguzar Raja Raghuraj Singh - Period of 25 years was to run from 1948 to 1973 - Document which was not executed was executed by Raja - It was brief document and all that it says is that tendu leaves in 99 villages have been 'sold' for 25 years for a consideration of Rs.9,000 per year which must be paid after each tendu leaf crop is over but before expiry of three months, that only leaves should be plucked and that no bushes should be cut down - Whether by provisions of Adhiniyam any right to property as such is being offended? - Held, purchase of tendu leaves must now be in accordance with Adhiniyam - Since, there is no right to property before leaves are plucked, no such rights can be said to be invaded by Adhiniyam - It cannot be said either by reason of any rule of res judicata or on analogy that petitioner firm is entitled to invoke art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950 when it possesses no right of property in leaves - It has only a contract in its favour and that is not a right of property - No doubt Adhiniyam indirectly overreaches decision of Supreme Court - But, in any event, it is open to State Legislature provided it passes a valid law - To that effect law is not challenged as invalid and it must therefore apply to petitioner firm, as to any other person - Petitioner firm cannot take shelter of explanation 1 till it buys leaves from Govt. under Adhiniyam and Niyamavali - Petition dismissed.
