Lekhraj Satramdas, Lalvani v Deputy Custodian-Cum-Managing Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 458; AIR 1966 SC 334; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226, Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act Of 1954 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 16 - Administration Of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 10(2)(b) - Proprietors of two firms migrated to Pakistan and both these firms became vested in Custodian of Evacuee Properties for State of Madras under 1950 Act - Later on Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 was passed - There was an advertisement published in Press for sale of aforesaid evacuee properties appellant applied to Chief Settlement Commr. for stopping sale of two concerns - (A) Whether appellant was lawfully removed from management of business by order of respondent no 1 D/-18-12-1959? - Deputy Custodian of Evacuee Property has power to terminate management - Held, when an authority passes an order which is within its competence, it cannot fail merely because it purports to be made under a wrong provision if it can be shown to be within its power under any other rule, and validity of order should be judged on a consideration of its substance and not of its form - (B) Whether appellant is entitled to move High Court for grant of writ of mandamus? - A writ of mandamus may be granted only in a case where there is a statutory duty imposed upon officer concerned and there is a failure on part of that officer to discharge that statutory obligation - Held, appointment of a person as a Manager by Custodian by virtue of his power u/s. 10(2)(b) of Administration of Evacuee Property Act is contractual in nature and there is no statutory obligation as between latter and former - Thus former is not entitled to move HC for grant of a writ in nature of mandamus u/art. 226 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
