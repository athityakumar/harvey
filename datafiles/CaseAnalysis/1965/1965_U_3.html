Madhya Pradesh Industries Ltd. v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1966 SC 671; 1966 MahLJ 249; 1966 MPLJ 256; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, 1956- Mines & Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 5, 8(2),10 and 17 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Lease of mines granted through public application - Mines reserved for exploitation by public sector by Maharashtra Govt. - Central Govt. rejected revision - (A) Whether order of Central Govt. for rejection of revision is valid or not? - Held, order by Central Govt. bad, because no reasons given for rejecting revision - Condition of giving reasons attached to order by Govt. when functions judicially as a tribunal and in a small number of matters - Central Govt's order is vitiated - (B) Whether whole making order, Govt. has followed principles of natural justice ? - Further held, order bad because non compliance natural justice - Opportunity of written representation need not be a personal hearing - (C) Whether order of Central Govt. is illegal because of it ignored Order made by State Govt. granting lease to appellant? - Further held, appellant has to compete with others who were not earlier in field - It has certainly an opportunity to apply for lease - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
