Kalooram Govindram v Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P., Nagpur and Bhandara

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 296; AIR 1966 SC 4; [1965] 57 ITR 335; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(2)(Vi) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Depreciation allowance - Private auction - Bid - Sale - Appellant was a Hindu undivided family carrying business in two branches - As per partition decree, each item of property put up for sale by bidding - Among those, sugar factory knocked down in favour of G After all items of property sold to one or other of the parties - G and his family members continued to run the factory - Income-tax Officer, assessed the appellant in respect of the income from said factory - Appellant claimed depreciation as provided u/s.10(2)(vi) - Income-tax Officer and Appellate Assistant Commissioner rejected the claim - On a further appeal, Appellate Tribunal held that so far as 10/16th share in factory which belonged to appellant , cost was original cost and so far as 6/16th share of Bs branch ,original cost was amount for which appellant's branch purchased 6/16th share of other branch - HC found that depreciation allowance should be computed on the basis of the original cost to larger joint family and not on the basis of the valuation at which assessee took over the assets - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee purchased his own share in the private auction - Held, no - Assessee does not purchase his own share at valuation put by him at the private auction, he merely purchases the share of the other sharer at a valuation made on bid offered by him - Depreciation allowance should be computed on basis of 10/16th share of original cost to the joint family of the assets which are admissible to depreciation allowance plus an appropriate amount attributable to the assets admissible to depreciation allowance on the footing that the value of 6/16th share of B in factory was Rs.12,75,000/- Order of HC modified - Appeal allowed.
