Shri Bhagwan and Another v Ram Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 1965 SC 1767; 1965 ALJ 353; 1966 (2) SCJ 295; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control Of Rent And Eviction

1947, ss. 7 and 3 - (A) Whether nature of proceedings taken before State Govt. u/s. 7-F and character of jurisdiction and power conferred on State Govt. is quasi-judicial or administrative?; Whether State Govt. decided question and exercised its jurisdiction without complying with principles of natural justice? - Held, It is ends of justice which determine nature of order which State Govt. would pass u/s. 7-F, and in securing ends of justice, State Govt. cannot but apply principles of natural justice and offer a reasonable opportunity to both parties while it exercises its jurisdiction u/s. 7-F - Jurisdiction conferred on District Magistrate to deal with rights of parties is of such a character that principles of natural justice cannot be excluded from proceedings before him - Provisions unambiguously suggest that proceedings before District Magistrate as well as before Commr. are quasi-judicial in character - In exercising their respective powers u/ss. 3(2) and 3(3), appropriate authorities have to consider matter in a quasi-judicial manner, and are expected to follow principles of natural justice before reaching their conclusions - (B) Whether learned single judge was appropriate in taking upon himself to consider question as to whether earlier decisions of division benches of High Court needed to be reconsidered and revised? - Held, considerations of judicial propriety and decorum require that if a learned single Judge hearing a matter is inclined to take view that earlier decisions of HC, whether of a division benches or of a single judge, need to be reconsidered, he should not embark upon that enquiry sitting as a single judge, but should refer matter to a division bench or, in a proper case, place relevant papers before Chief Justice to enable him to constitute a larger bench to examine question - Appeal dismissed.
