 Mohd. Idris and Others v Sat Narain

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 430; AIR 1966 SC 1499; 1966 ALJ 870; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 15

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r. 10 - U P Agriculturist Relief Act,s. 12 - Transfer of Property Act,, s.83 - Suit for ejectment by respondent on ground of mortgage satisfied from usufruct of land - Appellants contended that they have become 'sirdars' under U. P. Z. A. Act and L. R. Act, 1950 so suit not maintainable in Munsif Court - Munsif held suit maintainable and order ejectment - Appeal dismissed by District Court and High Court - Suit filed in 1952 and U P Agriculturist Relief Act repealed by Abolition Act in 1953 - New law took away jurisdiction of Munsif Court and gave it to Asst. Collector - No provision incorporated in Abolition Act to effect that cases pending prior to date of coming into existence of Act were to stand transferred to Assistant Collector for disposal - S.6 of General Clausesl Act is applicable to pending litigation - Held, proceedings before Munsif were with jurisdiction because they were not affected by passing of Abolition Act or amending Act, 1953, regard being had to provisions of s.6 of U P General Clauses Act in first instance and more so in view of provisions of s.23 of Amending Act, 1956 which came before proceedings between parties had finally terminated - Appeal dismissed.
