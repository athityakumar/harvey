Godde Venkateswara Rao v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 245; AIR 1966 SC 828; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 37; 1966 (2) SCJ 270; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Samithis and Zilla Parishads Act (35 of 1959)

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Samithis and Zilla Parishads Act, 1959 - Review - Resolutions - Cancellation - Before the Act came into force, the Primary Health Centre was inaugurated at Dharmajigudem presumably subject to the condition that the said village would comply with the conditions laid down by the administrative directions, governing the location of a Centre - One of the important conditions was that the village seeking to have the Centre should give 2 acres of land free and 50% cash contribution which would not be less than Rs. 10,000 - The said amount was not paid by Dharmajigudem village with the result the condition was not complied with - Appellant who was the representative of the village filed an application before the HC under Art. 226 of the Constitution for quashing the said order of the Govt. - Application was dismissed by the HC and on appeal it was confirmed by DB - Hence this Appeal - Whether Andhra Pradesh HC was right in refusing its extraordinary discretionary power - Held, Primary Health Centre was not permanently located at Dharmajigudem - Representatives of the said village did not comply with the necessary conditions for such location - Both the orders of the Govt. were not legally passed - The HC rightly refused to exercise its extraordinary discretionary power - Appeal dismissed.
