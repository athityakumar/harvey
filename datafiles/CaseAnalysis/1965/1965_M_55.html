Commissioner of Wealth Tax (Central)CALCUTTA v Messrs Standard Vacuum Oil Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 390; AIR 1966 SC 995; [1966] 59 ITR 569; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 317

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 2(m) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 18 A - Demands in respect of payment of tax u/s. 18 A of Indian Income Tax Act were made against respondent company, M/S. Standard Vacuum Oil Co. Ltd, for two years ending on 31-12-1956 and 31-12-1957, by notices of demand D/- 28-05 1956 and 31-05-1957, respectively - Final installment of amount of Rs.47,69,653 for each of two years was outstanding on respective valuation daters - Assessee claimed that demand for such tax should be allowed as deduction in determining net wealth of assessee under Wealth Tax Act - Appellate Tribunal held that this sum should be deducted from total computation of wealth, if said amount was outstanding for less than a year - Whether amounts directed to be paid by notices of demand D/- 28-05-1956 and 31-05-1957 are 'debts owed' by assessee within s. 2(m) on respective valuation dates? - Held, a debt is owed when an order u/s. 18 A(1) is passed and a notice of demand sent amount mentioned in notice begins to be owe till a new figure is substituted by action of assessee - On valuation dates in these appeals, assessee had not taken any action u/s. 18 A(2) and consequently amounts mentioned in notices of demand were debts owed within s. 2(m) of Wealth Tax Act on valuation dates - Agree with High Court, that answer to question referred to it should be in favour of assessee - Appeal dismissed.
