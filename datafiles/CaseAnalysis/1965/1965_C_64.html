Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Messrs. Best and Company

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 228; AIR 1966 SC 1325; [1966] 60 ITR 11; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 134; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - (A) Whether referred amounts were assessable u/s. 10? - Amounts were received on termination of agencies - Such termination of agencies was a part of normal course of its business - Amounts were received not only in lieu of giving up of agencies but also for accepting a restricting covenant for a specific period - Part of amount received attributable to restrictive covenant was a capital receipt - Apportionment of amount to be made on reasonable basis by concerned authorities - (B)Whether that part of compensation attributable to restrictive covenant is a capital receipt or a revenue receipt? - Held, that part of compensation attributable to restrictive covenant was a capital receipt and hence not assessable to tax - (C)Whether compensation paid is severable? - If compensation paid was in respect of two distinct matters, one taking character of a capital receipt and other of a revenue receipt, no principle which prevents apportionment of income between two matters - Further held, in present case apportionment of compensation has to be made on a reasonable basis between loss of agency in usual course of business and restrictive covenant - Only such part of sums as is attributable to loss of agency is assessable u/s 10 of Act for assessment years 1951-52 and 1952-53 - Appeal partly allowed.
