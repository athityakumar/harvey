Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Manmohandas

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 225; AIR 1966 SC 798; [1966] 59 ITR 699; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Set Off, Profession, Carry Forward

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 7, 10 and 12 - (A) Whether on a true interpretation of deed of agreement, appointing assessee as Treasurer of Bank, income earned by assessee from his activities as such Treasurer fell to be computed u/s. 10 of Act or s. 7 or s. 12 of Income Tax Act ? - Fact that assessee held an office is not decisive of question whether remuneration earned by him was as a servant of Allahabad Bank - Receipt of remuneration for holding an office does not necessarily give rise to a relationship of master and servant - Taking into consideration nature of duties performed, and obligations undertaken, together with right to remuneration subject to compensation for loss arising to Bank from his own acts and omissions or of servants introduced by him into business of Bank, assessee may be regarded as following a vocation - Held, remuneration must therefore be computed u/s. 10 of Income Tax Act - (B) Whether assessee could claim a set off of loss suffered by him in preceding year against profits in year under consideration, having failed to prefer an appeal against refusal by ITO making assessment for previous year to allow assessee to carry forward loss u/s. 24(2) of Income Tax Act? - Further held, loss of profit suffered in that vocation in any year may be carried forward to next year and be set off against profit of succeeding year - Appeal dismissed.
