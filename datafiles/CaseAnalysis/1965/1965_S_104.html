 Karimtharuvi Tea Estates Limited v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 357; AIR 1966 SC 1385; 1966 (1) ITJ 572; [1966] 60 ITR 262; 1966 KerLJ 661; 1966 KLT 488; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 93

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950 - Kerala Surcharge on Taxes Act, 1957 - Appellant was assessed to agricultural income tax, and surcharge at 5 per cent was levied - Appellant objected to this on the ground that law applicable to assessment for 1957-58 was the law in force on 01-04-1957, and the Surcharge Act, which did not have retrospective effect, only came into force on 01-09-1957 - Held, it is well-settled that the Income Tax Act, as it stands amended on the first day of April of any financial year must apply to assessments of that year - Surcharge Act not being retrospective by express intendment or necessary implication, cannot be made applicable from 01-04-1957, as the Act came into force on September 1, of that year - Since the Surcharge Act was not the law in force on 01-04-1957, no surcharge could be levied under the said Act against the appellant in the assessment year 1957-58 - Appeal allowed.
