R. Jeevaratnam v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 438; AIR 1966 SC 951; 1967 (14) FLR 285; 1967 (1) LLJ 393; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 67; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 204; 1967 SLR 657

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Disciplinary proceedings - Dismissal - Suit for declaration - Decree null and void - Legality - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against appellant of illegal gratification - Appellant was placed under suspension and relieved of his duties - Tribunal ordered appellant dismissal of service - Appellant instituted the suit asking for a declaration that the order dismissing him from service was illegal and void - Trial Court dismissed the suit, and, the said decree was affirmed on appeal by HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, appellant had reasonable opportunity to defend himself against the charges - Order dt. 17-10-1950 directed that appellant be dismissed from service with effect from the date of his suspension, from 20-5-1949 - In substance, this order directed that (1) the appellant be dismissed, and (2) the dismissal do operate retrospectively as from 20-51949 - The two parts, of said composite order were separable - First part of the order, operates as a dismissal of appellant as from 17-10-1950 - Invalidity of the second part of the order, assuming this part to be invalid, does not affect the first part of the order - Order of dismissal as from 17-10-1950 was valid and effective - Appellant was lawfully dismissed, and he was not entitled to claim that he was still in service - An order of dismissal with retrospective effect was, in substance, an order of dismissal as from the date of the order with the superadded direction that the order should operate retrospectively as from san anterior date - Two parts of the order were clearly severable - Assuming that the second part of the order was invalid, there was no reason why the first part of the order should not be given the fullest effect The Court could not pass a new order of dismissal, but surely it could give effect to the valid and severable part of the order - Appeal dismissed.
