Bhagwan Das v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1966 SC 1869; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 511

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953 - Whether appellant is a small landowner within meaning of s. 9(1)(i) of Act? - Held, on a combined reading of ss. 2, 3, 4, 5, 5 A, 5 B, 5 C, 10 A, 19 A and 19 D, if a person is then found to be a small landowner, he continues to be so for purpose of Act, until he acquires more land, and on taking into account value of land in terms of standard acres on date of acquisition, he is found to be a big landowner - If he is a small landowner at commencement of Act, his status is not altered by reason of improvements in value of his land or re-allotment of land on compulsory consolidation of holdings -The appellant did not acquire any land after commencement of Act - His status as a small landowner was not altered by reason of subsequent improvements or re-allotments of land on compulsory consolidation of holdings - On date of application for eviction, he, therefore, continued to be a small landowner - Appeal allowed.
