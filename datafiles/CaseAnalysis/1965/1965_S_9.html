S. P. Jain v Kalinga Tubes Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 86; AIR 1965 SC 1535; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 351; 1965 (1) CompLJ 193; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Capital Issue

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397 and 398 - Company - Oppression - Two groups of shareholders - Financial troubles - Finance supplied by third party on terms of allotted no of shares equal to held by existing groups - Company not party to agreement and also agreement not made part of Articles of Association of company - Fresh issue of shares - Issues are - Is it a case of oppression within scope and applicability of s. 397 of Companies Act, 1956 ? - Whether agreement is binding in case of fresh issue and new shares can be allotted to non-existing share-holders who are not benamidars or stooges of existing two groups? - Whether slight delay in payment of full value of new shares issued is a case of prejudice to interest of Company in light of s. 398 of Act? - No case of oppression - Agreement is not binding in case of fresh issue - No case of prejudice to interest of Company - Appeal dismissed.
