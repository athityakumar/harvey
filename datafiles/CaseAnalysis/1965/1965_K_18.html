Harbhajan Singh v Karam Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 1966 SC 641; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bengal Land Revenue Sales Act, 1859, East Punjab (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act 1951

Summary: Land & Property - East Punjab (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act 1951, s. 42 - Whether Director, Consolidation of Holdings, had power to review his previous order and whether his subsequent order D/- 29-08-1958 u/s 42 of Act is legally valid? - There is no provision in Act granting express power of review to State Govt. with regard to an order made u/s. 42 of Act - Held, in absence of any such express power, it is manifest that Director, Consolidation of Holdings, cannot review his previous order d/-29-08-1958 dismissing application of appellant u/s. 42 of Act and therefore order d/-29-08-1958 is ultra vires and without jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
