South Asia Industries Private Limited v S. B. Sarup Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 398; AIR 1965 SC 1442; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Delhi Rent Control Act, Police Act, Calcutta Improvement Act, 1911, Government of India Act

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 39 and 43 - Govt. of India Act, 1915, ss. 107 and 108 -(A) Whether an appeal lies under cl. 10 of Letters Patent for High Court of Lahore, to a Division Bench of Punjab HC against a judgment passed by a single judge of said HC in a second appeal u/s 39 of Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958? - Held, it does not lie - Expression 'appeal' in s. 39 of Act does not take in a Letters Patent appeal under cl. 10 of Letters Patent - Appeal to HC will be regulated by practice and procedure obtaining in HC - Under s.108 of Govt. of India Act, 1915, an appeal u/s 39 of Act will be heard by a single Judge - Any judgment made by single Judge in said appeal will, u/cl. 10 of Letters Patent, be subject to an appeal to that court - If order made by a single judge is a judgment and if appropriate legislature u/cl. 3 of Letters Patent has expressly or by necessary implication, not taken away right of appeal, an appeal shall lie from judgment of a single judge u/cl.10 of Letters Patent to HC - (B) Whether right of appeal conferred by cl.10 of Letters Patent, Lahore, has been taken away by a law made by appropriate legislature ?- Further held, appropriate legislature can take away that right - Expression 'final' in Part II of s. 43 of Act puts an end to a further appeal and words 'shall not be called in question in any original suit, application or execution proceeding' bar collateral proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
