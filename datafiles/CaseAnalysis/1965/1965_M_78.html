Commissioner of Income Tax v Mugneeram Bangur and Company

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1966 SC 50; 1966 CRLJ 129; [1965] 57 ITR 299; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Land and Income Tax Act, 1916 - Allotment of shares - Sale of valuable stock in trade - Part of slump price - Taxable profit - Respondent/vendors were a firm carried on business of land development - Partners of firm agreed to sell all business of firm to vendee promoted by partners of firm - Consideration of Rs.34,99,300 paid by allotment of 17,500 Redeemable Preference shares of Rs.100 each and 17,493 Ordinary shares of Rs. 100 each - Vendors received shares of face value of Rs.34,99,300 for assets transferred to company - Income Tax Officer(IO) found that Rs.2,50,000 was charged by vendors as a lump sum amount of profits on sale of valuable stock in trade and not goodwill as alleged - Appellate Assistant Commissioner observed that Rs.2,50,000 was value of the goodwill and profit was capital gain not liable to tax - Commissioner of Taxes assessed appellant upon it for income tax under 1916 Act - Appellate Tribunal opined profits arose out of sale was taxable income - Tribunal referred question to HC that whether sale of whole business concern was included in taxable profit of Rs.2,50,000 - HC decided that even if value of stock in trade taken over by assessee was greater than figure shown in agreement for sale there was no profit could be taxed - Hence instant appeal - Whether any part of slump price was taxable as income - Held, price paid by purchase was not on consideration of goodwill of vendors but upon taking over entire going concern and paid consideration not in money but by allotment of shares - Surplus was out of sale of business as a whole including stock in trade of assessee firm - Amount of Rs. 2,50,000 was excess value of lands sold along with other assets - Income Tax Department not entitled to took mere book keeping entries as evidence of any profit in matter - Sale was sale of whole concern and no part of slump price was attributable to cost of land - No part of slump price was taxable - Answer the referred question in negative - Appeal dismissed.
