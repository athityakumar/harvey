P. C. Gulati v Lajya Ram Kapur and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1966 SC 595; 1966 CRLJ 465; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cognizance of an offence, Cognizable offence

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 , ss. 193, 291, 480, 485, and 526 - Whether the High Court can transfer a case pending in the Court of a Magistrate to the Court of the Addl. Session Judge? - S. 500 of Cr.P.C, 1898 deals with that on a complaint of Public Prosecutor without case being committed to it for trial - The language of s. 526(1)(ii) must be restricted so as to be consistent with and be harmonious with the requirements of s. 193 of the CrPC, 1898 - Held, the High Court had no power to transfer the criminal proceedings in the present case from the Court of the Sub Divisional Magistrate to the Court of the Addl. Sessions Judge without a proper order of commitment being made - Appeal dismissed.
