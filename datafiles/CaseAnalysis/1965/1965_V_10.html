Eramma v Verrupanna and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 217; AIR 1966 SC 1879; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Immovable Property, Coparcenary Property, Limited Estate, Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce, women and children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Whether s. 14(1) can be attracted in case of a Hindu female, who is in possession of property of last male holder on date of commencement of Act, when she is only a trespasser without any right to property? - Held, when a male Hindu dies after commencement of this Act, having at time of his death an interest in a Mitakshara coparcenary property, his interest in property shall devolve by survivorship upon surviving members of coparcenary and not in accordance with this Act - Property includes movable and immovable property acquired by a female Hindu by inheritance or devise, or at a partition, or in lieu of maintenance or arrears of maintenance, or by gift from any person - It applies only to coparcenary property of male Hindu holder who dies after commencement of Act - Appellant was admittedly in possession of half properties of her husband, Eran Gowda, after he died in the year 1341 F and by virtue of s. 14 of Hindu Succession Act, 1956, she became full owner of properties and respondents 1 and 2 cannot, therefore, proceed with execution case - Provisions of s. 14(1)cannot be attracted in case of a Hindu female who is in possession of property of last male holder on date of commencement of Act, when she is only a trespasser without any right to property - Appeal dismissed.
