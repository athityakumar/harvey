Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore v Shah Mohandas Sadhuram

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1966 SC 15; [1965] 57 ITR 415; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income tax & Direct Tax - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66 A(2) and 26 A - Partnership Act 1932, s. 30 - Minor admitted to partnership - Contract entered into by guardian on behalf of minor - Registration of Partnership u/s. 26-A of Income Tax Act (A) Whether a partnership deed can be regarded as invalid for making a minor a partner? - Held, as long as a partnership deed does not make a minor full partner, a partnership deed cannot be regarded as invalid, because a guardian has purported to contract on behalf of a minor, if contract is for purposes mentioned in s.30 of Partnership Act, 1932 - (B) Whether in instant case firm should have been registered u/s. 26A of Income Tax Act, 1922 ? - On a reasonable interpretation of partnership deed conclusion reached that it conferred only benefits of partnership on two minors and that did not make them full partners - Further held, firm should have been registered u/s. 26 - Appeal dismissed.
