Janki Ram Bahadur Ram v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1965 SC 1898; [1965] 57 ITR 21; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Adventure In The Nature Of Trade

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10 - Purchase of property - Sale of property - Taxable income - Adventure in nature of trade - Appellant carried on business as a dealer in iron scrap and hardware - Company were owners of a jute pressing factory installed on a piece of land belonged to Company - On 21-1-1941 Company leased out to R the jute pressing factory together with machinery standing on land owned by Company for ten months - R failed to vacate and deliver up possession of premises after expiry of period of lease and company instituted suit against R - By an agreement dt.31-10-1942 appellant agreed to purchase all rights of Company in factory and appurtenant premises for Rs.2,45,000 - On 12-6-1943 appellant agreed to sell to P the property purchased from Company for Rs. 4,73,364/3/6 free from all encumbrances - Appellant substituted as plaintiff in suit filed by Company against R and obtained possession of factory premises - Income Tax Officer(IO) brought to tax in hands of appellant Rs.2,24,864 as profit arising out of sale of property to P - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal partially modified order and reduced total income by Rs.7,000 - Tribunal referred question to HC that whether Tribunal was right in holding that surplus of Rs.2,35,211 received by assessee as a result of sale of jute press arose out of an adventure in nature of trade and was rightly assessed to tax - HC answered question in affirmative - Hence instant appeal - Whether profit made by appellant by sale of property to P was taxable u/s.10 of 1922 Act - Held, s. 10 of Act makes profits and gains of business, profession or vocation carried on by an assessee taxable - Profit motive in entering a transaction was not decisive for an accretion to capital did not became taxable income merely because an asset was acquired in expectation that it might sold at profit - Purchase of property by appellant was an isolated transaction not related to business of appellant - Tribunal and HC were in error in holding that right of Company was not sold to appellant in lands in properties - Conveyance by Company to appellant was not on record - There was nothing to show that appellant desired to convert property to some other use - No brokers were employed for entering into a transaction of sale - Property purchased was not such that an inference that adventure in nature of trade must have intended by appellant - Answer given by HC in respect of question submitted by Tribunal discharged and record a negative answer - Appeal allowed.
