Gurdeep Singh Alias Deep v State (Delhi Admn.)

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 727; (2000) SCC (Cr) 449; (2000) 1 SCC 498; AIR 1999 SC 3646; 1999 (4) CCR 36; 1999 (4) Crimes 243; 1999 CRLJ 4573; 1999 (81) DLT 717; JT 1999 (7) SC 191; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 238; 1999(6) SCALE 20; 2000 (2) SCJ 358; 1999 (8) SLT 39; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 693; 1999 (8) Supreme 641

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 326, 324 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s.15 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, s.5 - Bomb blast - Murder - Confession - Conviction - Bomb exploded near apartments killed three persons and injured two persons - Appellant/terrorist was arrested - Appellant was interrogated by Superintendent of Police - Appellant made his confessional statement u/s.15 of 1987 Act - Appellant admitted his complicity and involvement in bomb explosion - Appellant was charged u/ss.302, 326, 324 of IPC and s.5 of 1908 Act - Appellant challenged conviction u/ss.302, 324 of IPC and s.5(a)(b) of 1908 Act by Designated Court - Whether confession made by appellant u/s.15 of 1987 Act was voluntary - Held, confession was made by appellant before Superintendent of Police after followed procedure prescribed under 1987 Act and explained to appellant that he was not bound to make a confession and if he would make, it could be used against him - Conviction of confession was based on maxim 'habemus optimum testem, confitentem reum' meant that confession of accused was best evidence against him - Confession recorded by police officer not below rank of Superintendent of Police was admissible in evidence - Confessional statement of appellant was voluntarily and truthfully made by him did not require any further corroboration - Judgment passed by designated court was just and proper - Conviction and sentence of Appellant confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
