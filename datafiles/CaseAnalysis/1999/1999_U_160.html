United India Insurance Company Limited and others v Roshan Lal Oil Mills Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2218; (2000) 10 SCC 19

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64UM(2) - Fire insurance policy - Claim of self-spontaneous combustion - Survey report - Repudiation of claim - In consideration of fire insurance policy, respondent claimed that mustard seed stored in silo had been affected due to self-spontaneous combustion - On receipt of information, appellant appointed Joint surveyors who submitted their report in which they indicated that though spontaneous combustion had taken place, it had not resulted in 'fire' - Therefore, appellant, repudiated claim of respondent on ground that damage to stock was not covered within ambit of insurance policy - Claim petition filed by respondent was allowed by National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether survey report on basis of which claim of respondent was repudiated by appellant was not considered by Commission - Held, appellant had appointed joint surveyors in terms of s. 64-UM(2) of the Act - Joint surveyors report had been placed on record in which detailed account of factors on basis of which joint surveyors had came to conclusion that there was no loss or damage caused on account of fire, was given and it was on basis that claim was not found entertainable, which was important document which was placed before Commission, however, Commission, curiously, had not considered report - Since claim of respondent was repudiated by appellant on basis of joint survey report, Commission was not justified in awarding insurance amount to respondent without adverting itself to contents of joint survey report specially factors enumerated therein - Hence, non-consideration of important document had resulted in serious miscarriage of justice and vitiates judgment passed by Commission - Appeal allowed.
