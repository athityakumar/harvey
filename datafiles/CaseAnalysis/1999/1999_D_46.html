Pratap Singh Alias Babu Ram and Another v Deputy Director of Consolidation, Mainpuri and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 742; (2000) 4 SCC 614; 2000 (2) CLT 55; JT 1999 (10) SC 494; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 479; 2000 (91) RD 494; 2000(2) SCALE 62; 2000 (2) SLT 642; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 150; 2000 (2) Supreme 132

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 73 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 14 and 209 - Uttar Pradesh Hereditary Tenancy Act, 1939 - (A) Whether possession of mortgagee whose rights have been extinguished u/s. 14(1) of Abolition Act is, on or after date of vesting, per se, adverse or permissive? - (B) Does period of limitation for a suit u/s. 209 of Abolition Act commence to run from date of vesting or on date of demand for possession? - Held, (A) s. 14(1) purports to abolish all rights of mortgagee in possession of an estate or a share therein with effect from date of vesting - S. 18 provides that even if land was in cultivatory possession of mortgagee, on date of vesting, it would be treated, fictionally, 'sir' or 'khudkasht' of mortgagor, provided land, on date of mortgage, was 'sir' or 'khudkasht' of mortgagor - The overall effect of s. 14 (1) and (2) is that rights of a mortgagee come to an end with effect from date of vesting and mortgagor becomes 'bhumidhar' of that land u/s. 18 of Act - Further held, (B) a bare reading of words of Entry 30 in Appendix III, relating to suits u/s. 209 of Act, makes it clear that period of limitation would not run from date of vesting, as character of mortgagee's possession remains 'permissive' and does not become 'adverse' to interest of mortgagor who after acquiring 'bhumidhari' rights u/s. 18, may still allow mortgagee to continue in possession - If on being asked to deliver possession, mortgagee refuses or declines to do so, it would give rise to a cause of action on date on which possession is refused to be delivered and consequently period of three years would start running from that date - Appeal dismissed.
