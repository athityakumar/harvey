State of Maharashtra and Others v Kumari Tanuja

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 951; (1999) 2 SCC 462; AIR 1999 SC 791; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 586; JT 1999 (1) SC 234; 1999(1) SCALE 203; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 315; 1999 (1) SLT 411; 1999 (1) Supreme 281

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Reservation category - Reservation denied - Challenged - Validity - Respondent belonged to Hindu Bawa community included in Nomadic tribe which had reservation category - But Caste Scrutiny Committee and Addl. Commissioner by their Orders held that respondent was not entitled for reservation under nomadic tribe category - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition in HC and challenged above orders and also State Govt. resolution dt.1-4-1987 as beyond executive powers - DB referred case to Full Bench as there were conflicting judgments on questions involved - Writ petition was partly allowed by Full Bench holding that if all Govt. resolutions were read together with historical background it was clear that in Sindh which was once part of Bombay Presidency 'Bawa' community was included as Nomadic tribe in various Govt. resolutions issued from time to time and that Govt. resolution dt.1-4-1987 was beyond executive power of State - On factual aspects Full Bench viewed that petitioner should establish mutual affinity amongst tribal communities specified in Schedule before appropriate authorities and accordingly remanded matter to Scrutiny Committee for reconsidering on merits - Aggrieved State Govt. filed instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, it was rightly noticed by HC that various resolutions and notifications issued by State Govt. from time to time showed that Bawa community without reference to any region was included as synonyms of Bairagi/Gosavi and was notified as Nomadic tribe - Bawas were treated as belonged to Nomadic tribe before and after separation of State - By subsequent resolution Bombay Govt. continued to show Bairagi and Gosavi communities with their synonyms as Nomadic tribes without any regional restrictions - Based on above facts from various Govt.circulars HC was justified in holding that Bawa community from Sindh were entitled to benefits reserved for Nomadic tribe as per Govt. resolution dt. 21-11-1961 - HC had rightly held and settled law was that State Govt. while purporting to clarify position could not enter upon judicial power and could not set aside binding judgment of court by issuing executive fiat - SC in Municipal Corporation of Ahmedabad etc. v. The New Shrock Spinning & Weaving Co. Ltd & etc, 1970 Indlaw SC 498 and in Madan Mohan Pathak v. Union of India, 1978 Indlaw SC 152 and in State of Haryana & Ors. v. Kamal Cooperative Farmers' Society Ltd. & Ors., 1993 Indlaw SC 1061 held that if Executive or Legislature wants to render judicial decision ineffective it could only do so by removing or altering or neutralising legal basis of judgment which was sought to be made ineffective and that neither Legislature nor Executive had powers to simply declare earlier decisions of courts as invalid or not binding - Findings of HC were justified - Appeals dismissed.
