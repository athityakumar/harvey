Gramophone Company of India Limited v Collector of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 90; (2000) 1 SCC 549; 1999 (85) ECR 817; 1999 (114) E.L.T. 770; JT 1999 (9) SC 275; 1999 (35) RLT 902

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Customs Act, 1962 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Whether activity in which appellant is engaged amounts to a process necessary for manufacture or production of a commodity or its activity is designed merely to offer services of any description? - Moment there is transformation into a new commodity commercially known as a distinct and separate commodity having its own character, use and name, whether it be result of one process or several processes, manufacture takes place - Transformation of goods into a new and different article should be such that in commercial world it is known as another and different article - Held, pre-recorded audio cassettes are certainly goods known in market as distinct and different from blank audio cassettes - Appellant is indulging in a mass production of such pre - recorded audio cassettes - It is a manufacturing activity - Order accordingly.
