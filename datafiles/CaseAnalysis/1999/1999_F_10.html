P. A. Ahammed Ibrahim v Food Corporation of India

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 30; (1999) 7 SCC 39; AIR 1999 SC 3033; 1999 (3) ARBLR 263; JT 1999 (6) SC 382; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151, O. 6, r. 17 - (A) Application for filing arbitration agreement - It is a stage prior to institution of suit and is not suit - Application for referring dispute to arbitrator - Whether can be treated as plaint as contemplated under CPC? - (B) Amendment of plaint - Application for - Before applying provisions u/O. 6, r. 17 there must be institution of suit - Any application filed under provisions of different statutes cannot be treated as a suit or plaint unless otherwise provided in said Act - Amendment in facts would introduce totally new cause of action and change nature of suit - It would also introduce a totally different case which is inconsistent with prayer made in application for referring dispute to arbitrator - Likely to cause prejudice to appellant as it was sought 11 years after cause of action arose - Amendment cannot be allowed - S. 151, CPC could not be applied in such cases - Held, ( A) s. 20(2) no doubt provides that application for filing arbitration agreement shall be in writing and shall be numbered and registered as a suit, it nowhere provides that application filed for referring dispute to arbitrator is to be treated as a plaint as contemplated under CPC, hence, it cannot be considered to be a plaint - Appeal allowed.
