Vijayan and another v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 959; (1999) 3 SCC 54; (1999) SCC (Cr) 378; AIR 1999 SC 1086; 1999 (38) AllCriC 485; 1999 (1) CCR 102; 1999 (1) Crimes 92; 1999 CRLJ 1638; 1999 (1) JCC 177; JT 1999 (1) SC 499; 1999 (1) KLT 760; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 824; 1999(1) SCALE 479; 1999 (1) SCJ 480; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 659; 1999 (2) SLT 174; 1999 (2) Supreme 76

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B and 300 - Arms Act, 1959 - Murder conviction based on circumstantial evidence - Identification not reliable and dying declaration not referring to accused - Moreover no evidence connecting bullets recovered from dead body to bullets recovered on basis of accused's statement - (A) Validity of conviction - (B) Proof of conspiracy - Conviction held, not proper - Is difficult to establish by direct evidence, however, there should be material showing connection between alleged conspiracy and act done pursuant to that conspiracy - Appeal allowed.
