Jai Bhagwan and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 391; (1999) 3 SCC 102; (1999) SCC (Cr) 388; AIR 1999 SC 1083; 1999 (38) AllCriC 475; 1999 (1) CCR 74; 1999 (1) Crimes 98; 1999 CRLJ 1634; 1999 (1) JCC 171; JT 1999 (1) SC 386; 1999 (2) KLT 28; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 845; 1999(1) SCALE 372; 1999 (2) SLT 8; 1999 (2) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 103, 104, 302, 307 and 441 - Murder - Murderous assault - Self defence - Right of self defence extended only to causing of harm not death - Post mortem certificate proved that death was caused due to haemorrhage and shock as a result of injuries on head and face - Both injuries are on vital parts of body - Common intention - S. 34 will be attracted as essentially it involves vicarious liability but if participation of accused in crime is proved and a common intention is absent, then cannot be invoked - Held, direct evidence of a common intention has to be inferred from facts and circumstances of each case - Appeal partly allowed.
