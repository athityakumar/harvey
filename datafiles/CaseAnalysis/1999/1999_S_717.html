Syed Dastagir v T. R. Gopalakrishna Setty

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 818; (1999) 6 SCC 337; AIR 1999 SC 3029; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land and Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Allowing discretionary relief - On the basis of the plea of readiness and willingness - Whether plea of plaintiff is sufficient to conform to requirement of s. 16(c)? - Held, language in s. 16(c) does not require any specific phraseology but only that plaintiff must aver that he has performed or has always been and is willing to perform his part of contract - So compliance of 'readiness and willingness' has to be in spirit and substance and not in letter and form - So to insist for a mechanical production of exact words of a statute is to insist for form rather than essence - So absence of form cannot dissolve an essence if already pleaded - S. 16(c) makes it obligatory to a plaintiff seeking enforcement of specific performance that he must not only come with clean hands but there should be a plea that he has performed or has been and is ready and willing to perform his part of obligation - Unless this is there, s. 16(c) creates a bar to grant of this discretionary relief - For this it is not necessary to plea by any specific words, if through any words it reveals readiness and willingness of plaintiff to perform his part of obligation then it cannot be said there is non-compliance of said section - Readiness and willingness cannot be treated as a strait-jacket formula - This has to be determined from entirety of facts and circumstances relevant to intention and conduct of party concerned - Pleading as made by plaintiff not only shows his readiness and willingness to perform his part of obligation under contract but by tendering total amount shows he has performed his part of obligation - Such a plea to be a plea of readiness and willingness as required u/s. 16(c) - Appeal allowed.
