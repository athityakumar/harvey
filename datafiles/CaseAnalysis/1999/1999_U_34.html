Arosan Enterprises Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1324; (1999) 9 SCC 449; (2001) SCC (Cr) 467; AIR 1999 SC 3804; 1999 (3) ARBLR 310; 1999 (81) DLT 825; JT 1999 (7) SC 122; 1999 (3) RAJ 297; 1999(6) SCALE 46; 1999 (8) SLT 104; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 621; 1999 (9) Supreme 427

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Promisee

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Issues raised are - (A) Whether time was essence of contract? - Held, extension given by arbitrator ought to be categorical in nature rather than being vague or in anvil of presumptions - When parties themselves extent time of contract they provide a future date for performance of contract and parties may also agree to abandonment of previous contract - In absence of any date of completion mentioned in contract Courts will find that parties themselves by their conduct have given a go by to original term of contract as regards time being essence of contract - (B) Whether contract becomes voidable when parties does not intent to make time an essence of contract? - Held, if it was not intention of parties that time should be of essence of contract, contract does not become voidable by failure to do such thing at or before specified time - Promisee is entitled to compensation from promisor for any loss occasioned to him by such failure - When contract itself provides for extension of time, same cannot be termed to be essence of contract - Default in such a case does not make contract voidable either - It becomes voidable provided matter in issue can be brought within ambit of first paragraph of s. 55 - It is only in that event that Govt. would be entitled to claim damages and not otherwise - (C) Whether compensation can be claimed in case of voidable contract? - Held that a contract voidable on account of promisor's failure to perform his promise at time agreed, promisee accept performance of such promise at any time other than that agreed - Promisee cannot claim compensation for any loss occasioned by non-performance of promise at time agreed, unless, at time of such acceptance, he gives notice to promisor of his intention to do so - (D) Whether failure to reply amounts to denial of rights of buyer? - Held, failure to speak would forfeit all rights of buyer in terms of agreement - It will not jeopardize sellers interest neither same would authorise buyer to cancel contract when there has been repeated requests for acting in terms of agreement between parties - (E) Damages - Liability to pay - Held, seller is not liable in damages for non-delivery, and buyer is liable in damages for non-acceptance if seller has not been instructed by buyer as to manner in which delivery has to be made - Further held that if there has been 'fraud in transaction' bank could dishonour beneficiary's demand for payment - Bank must pay if documents are in order and terms of credit are satisfied - Bank could not, by merely stating that there was not-supply of goods by appellant, use words 'fraud or misrepresentation' for purposes of coming under exception - (G) Whether non reasoned award of arbitrator allows a court to interfere with award? - Held, Court cannot substitute its evaluation and come to conclusion that Arbitrator had acted contrary to bargain between parties - If view of Arbitrator is a possible view award or reasoning contained therein cannot be examined - In event of there being no reasons in award, question of interference of Court would not arise at all - U/s. 30 reappriciation of award is not permissible - Appeal allowed.
