K. Ramakrishnan Unnithan v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1008; (1999) 3 SCC 309; (1999) SCC (Cr) 410; AIR 1999 SC 1428; 1999 (38) AllCriC 650; 1999 (2) CCR 41; 1999 (2) Crimes 19; 1999 CRLJ 2101; 1999 (1) JCC 223; JT 1999 (2) SC 235; 1999 (2) KLT 129; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 219; 1999(2) SCALE 128; 1999 (2) SCJ 435; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1222; 1999 (3) SLT 99; 1999 (3) Supreme 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, Ss. 324, 341, 449, 324 and 302 r/w.34 - Murder - Conviction - Intention - Acquittal - Appellant and his son stood charged for offences u/ss. 449, 341, 324 and 302 r/w. 34 of 1860 Code for having wrongfully restrained PW1 and for causing murder of deceased/father of PW1 - Sessions Judge, failed to establish charges beyond reasonable doubt and therefore, appellants were entitled to be acquitted - On appeal by respondent/State, HC affirmed order of acquittal passed by Sessions Judge for appellant No. 2/son, but reversed order of acquittal of appellant no.1/father and convicted him u/s. 302 and 324 of 1860 Code, and was sentenced to imprisonment for life - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant's blow on deceased was with intention of causing murder of deceased and had to be convicted u/s. 302 of 1860 Code - Held, Court opined that reasons adduced by trial Court for discarding their testimony were not at all sound and evaluation of evidence was manifestly erroneous - HC had exceeded its limit in interfering with an order or acquittal passed by Sessions Judge - Thus it was established beyond reasonable doubt that appellant had given severe blow either with intention of causing murder of deceased or with requisite knowledge that death would be inevitable result - Hence, Court hold that appellant did not commit offence u/s. 302 but under part II of s.304 of IPC - Court accordingly, set aside conviction of appellant u/s. 302 of IPC and instead, convicted him u/s. 304 Part II of IPC - As accused/appellant had already undergone sentence of about four years Court sentenced him to period already undergone - Appellant's conviction u/s. 324 of IPC remains unaltered, no separate sentence was awarded - Appeal disposed of.
