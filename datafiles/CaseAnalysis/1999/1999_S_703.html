State of Maharashtra v Suresh

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 768; (2000) SCC (Cr) 263; (2000) 1 SCC 471; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 736; 1999 (4) CCR 344; 2000 (1) Crimes 1; 2000 (1) JCC 93; JT 1999 (9) SC 513; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 149; 1999(7) SCALE 386; 2000 (1) SLT 6; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 215; 1999 (10) Supreme 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Rape - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 302 - Accused abducted, raped and killed a four year old girl - No doubt that victim was kidnapped from her house and she was raped and killed on same day - Whether crime was committed by accused? - Held, yes accused visited house of victim and after he left house it was realised that victim also disappeared - Prosecution witnesses, who were examined, saw accused and child together and thereafter walking along road - Accused after his arrest disclosed that dead body of child was concealed in farm - Offence of rape and murder of child proved beyond reasonable doubt - Conviction restored - Order accordingly.
