Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti Through Its Secretary v Arvind Chaubey and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2026; (2002) 9 SCC 549; 2003 (1) LLJ 507

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Respondent 1 who was a peon working on daily wages since year 1983 onwards was removed from service - He was ordered to be reinstated with continuity of service and back wages as a regular employee - Said decision of Labour Court was confirmed by High Court in writ petition as well as in review proceedings - In view of fact that Respondent 1 is found to be continuing for more than 16 years, it is too late for appellant to contend before us that he should be treated as a temporary peon - Hence, on these peculiar facts and circumstances of case, no relief could be granted to appellant - Appellant contended that Mandi Samiti is not an 'Industry' - HC has held against appellant on ground that such a contention was not canvassed before Industrial Tribunal - Once appellant did not raise such a contention before Industrial Tribunal, it was not available to be canvassed higher up - Held, no relief could be granted to appellant - Appellant Mandi Samiti is directed to comply with order of Labour Court as confirmed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
