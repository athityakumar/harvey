Ramanbhai Naranbhai Patel and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 855; (2000) SCC (Cr) 113; (2000) 1 SCC 358; AIR 1999 SCW 4770; 1999 (4) CCR 320; 2000 (1) Crimes 59; 1999 CRLJ 5013; 2000 (2) G.L.R. 1009; 2000 (1) JCC 144; JT 1999 (9) SC 319; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 93; 1999(7) SCALE 258; 2000 (1) SCJ 253; 1999 (10) SLT 245; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 41; 1999 (10) Supreme 76

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 326, 324, 323, 342, 452 and 149 - Murder - Eye witness - Dying declaration - Appellants convicted by Trial Court - Conviction confirmed by High Court - Whether conviction of appellants proper? - Held, no reason for eye-witness to falsely implicate appellants - Dying declaration given by deceased fully corroborated by evidence of other witnesses - Case proved beyond shadow of doubt - Appeal dismissed.
