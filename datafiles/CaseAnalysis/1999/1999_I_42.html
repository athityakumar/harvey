High Court of Punjab and Haryana Through R. G v Ishwar Chand Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 290; (1999) 4 SCC 579; (1999) SCC (L&S) 881; AIR 1999 SC 1677; 1999 (2) CLR 30; 1999 (3) ESC 1759; JT 1999 (3) SC 266; 1999 LabIC 1823; 1999 (3) LLN 28; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 834; 1999 (4) Supreme 299; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1097

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 311 - Disciplinary proceedings - Premature retirement - Maintainability - Respondent joined superior judicial service of State of Haryana after his selection by appellant/HC - Respondent's service were terminated wef 30-12-1986 and he was reinstated on 9-6-1988 - Further, respondent was placed under suspension w.e.f 3-10-1992 in contemplation of disciplinary proceedings initiated against him which were still pending - State Govt. issued letter dt. 10-5-1996 retiring respondent from service - Respondent challenged his pre-mature retirement, DB set aside order of pre-mature retirement of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment of DB was justified - Held, from resolutions of Full Court of 12-12-1995 and 11-1-1996 it was apparent that respondent was retired while under suspension - Further, it appeared that HC on its administrative side decided to keep disciplinary proceedings against respondent pending for purpose of imposing cut on his retiral benefits - Therefore, conclusion was obvious that action of HC in retiring respondent was based on allegation of misconduct, which was subject matter of inquiry before Judge of HC and which appeared to SC to be basis for recording of adverse remarks by HC in ACR of officer for year 1991-92 - Thus, HC on its judicial side was correct in setting aside order compulsorily retiring respondent and allowing writ petition of respondent to extent mentioned in impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
