Balbir v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 788; (2000) SCC (Cr) 160; (2000) 1 SCC 285; AIR 2000 SC 11; 1999 (4) CCR 209; 1999 (4) Crimes 289; 2000 CRLJ 169; 2000 (1) JCC 50; JT 1999 (8) SC 403; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 839; 1999(6) SCALE 600; 2000 (1) SCJ 1; 1999 (9) SLT 7; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 120; 1999 (9) Supreme 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Joint Trial

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Joint trial - Two cases in respect of murder of one person - Two trials committed before Sessions Court - But for fact that murdered person was same, diametrically divergent stories put against both the accused - (A) Procedure to be followed - Held, in such cases most appropriate procedure to be followed by a Sessions Judge should be that two trials were separately conducted one after other by same court before same Judge and judgments in both cases were separately pronounced on same day - No doubt Sessions Judge should take care that he would confine his judgment in one case only to evidence adduced in that particular case - If more than one Public Prosecutors are available at same station, it is advisable that Public Prosecutor who prosecuted one case should avoid prosecuting other case - (B) Conviction challenged - Held, court should have been absolutely certain that the police version was false, fabricated and accentuated by a sinister motive in order to dumb a thoroughly innocent teenager to very serious criminal charge of murder - At any rate, court should have been absolutely certain that police officials and real culprits have entered into a criminal conspiracy to rescue real murderer - Unless court reaches such a degree of satisfaction a Criminal Court cannot afford to conclude that prosecution instituted on a private complaint succeeded in proving beyond all reasonable doubt that it was this accused who had committed the murder - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
