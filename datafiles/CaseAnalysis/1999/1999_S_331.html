Leela Ram (Dead) Through Duli Chand v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 564; (1999) 9 SCC 525; (2000) SCC (Cr) 222; AIR 1999 SC 3717; 1999 (4) CCR 165; 1999 (4) Crimes 233; 1999 (2) JCC 624; JT 1999 (8) SC 274; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 588; 1999(6) SCALE 477; 1999 (3) SCJ 604; 1999 (8) SLT 518; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 435; 1999 (8) Supreme 631

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Former Statement

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, (1973), s. 156 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3, Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Whether any irregularity or illegality during investigation be a ground to reject prosecution case? - Whether discrepancy in evidence of eye-witnesses can affect credibility of evidence witness? - Held, any irregularity or illegality during investigation cannot be said to be fatal to prosecution case and fully corroborated evidence of eye-witnesses cannot be rejected on ground that they were interested witnesses and therefore acquittal of accused by relying upon some minor contradictions in evidence is liable to be set aside.
