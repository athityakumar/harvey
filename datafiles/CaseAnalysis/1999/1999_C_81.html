Belapur Sugar and Allied Industries Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Aurangabad

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1071; (1999) 4 SCC 103; AIR 1999 SC 1692; 1999 (82) ECR 209; 1999 (108) E.L.T. 9; JT 1999 (3) SC 237; 1999 (32) RLT 223; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 635

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules 1944, r. 8(1) - Notification Nos. 132 dated 21-4-1982 and 193 dated 11-6-1982 - Sugar factories - Exemption granted by Notification No. 132 to factories making excess production during lean period of production - Factories having no production for preceding 3 years however excluded - Subsequently this exclusionary clause substituted by notification No. 193 - Substituted clause specifically conferring exemption for entire period covered by earlier notification even on factories having nil production for preceding 3 years - Held, view that exemption granted under Notification No. 193 was available only from date Notification No. 193 was passed was not proper - Plea, since assessee has paid duty he could not claim exemption - Not tenable.
