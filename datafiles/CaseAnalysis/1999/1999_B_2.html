Delhi Financial Corporation v B. B. Behel

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1015; (1999) 3 SCC 298; AIR 1999 SC 2358; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 116; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 8; JT 1999 (2) SC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Banking & Finance - Financial Corporations Act, ss. 29 and 31 - Whether appellant is entitled to claim compound interest? - Held, it is far beyond power of court to compel a creditor to forego part of its claim of interest on ground of hardship to a debtor - In financial transactions, such adjustments should be left to parties to settle matter in best interest or exigencies of business - Appellant is a statutory financial institution which carries on its activities by borrowing amounts so a direction of such a nature will upset its financial equilibrium and land it in a financial crisis making it non-viable - However, on peculiar facts of this case, only relief which fit to grant to respondent during period from 01-07-1986 to 30-06-1993 is to condone default in repaying amount - Consequently, interest at reduced rate of 13.5 per cent per annum would be payable during said period - Appeal allowed
