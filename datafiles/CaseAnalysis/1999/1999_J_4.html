Union of India v J. R. Dhiman

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1313; (1999) 6 SCC 403; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1183; JT 1999 (10) SC 241; 2000 (1) SLR 25; 2000 (1) SLT 297

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Central Administrative (Procedure) Rules, 1987, r. 24 - Scope and applicability - It is not permissible at all for tribunal to direct revisional authority to pass such a punishment alone and any higher punishment could not be imposed - Held, order made by tribunal is that it could not have passed an order other than what had been passed earlier, namely, one of a penalty reducing time scale of pay of two stages for a period of two years and certain other directions - Quashed order of tribunal - Appeal allowed.
