A. Robert v United Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 677; (1999) 8 SCC 226; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1415; AIR 1999 SC 2977; 1999 (4) AWC 3357; JT 1999 (6) SC 273; 2000 (124) PLR 396

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110A - Whether statutory liability of Insurance Co. is required to be fully foisted on respondent? - Held, yes - Looking at injuries which are permanent in nature suffered by claimant and which have a permanent adverse effect on his future healthy life including sexual life, an additional amount was required to be granted to claimant on head of pain, shock and suffering - However, respondent Insurance Co.'s statutory liability is confined to Rs. 1,50,000 - Therefore, award is confined to Rs. 1,50,000 which will include compensation already allowed on all remaining items of expenditure on nourishment, medical treatment, travelling expenses and for actual injuries and disablement suffered by claimant - Appeal allowed.
