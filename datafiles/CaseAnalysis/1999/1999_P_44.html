Sree Jain Swetambar Terapanthi Vid (S) v Phundan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 390; (1999) 2 SCC 377; AIR 1999 SC 2322; 1999 (35) ALR 484; JT 1999 (1) SC 380; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 38; 1999(1) SCALE 366; 1999 (1) SCJ 598; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 498; 2000 (1) SLJ 260; 1999 (2) SLR 8; 1999 (2) SLT 94; 2000 (Supp) RD 597; 1999 (2) Supreme 5

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Appeal against injunction order - Powers of appellate Court to set aside injunction order - Scope - Interference by Supreme Court - Discretionary jurisdiction - Held, where High Court has granted some relief by way of social justice or on equitable grounds without violating right of other parties, though in law such relief was not permissible, Supreme Court would not interfere in its discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 136 if order under appeal advances cause of justice and if it is just and equitable so to do - In an adversarial litigation relief has to be granted to parties based on their pleadings - No relief should be granted in interlocutory proceedings beyond scope of suit - Held, court is not inclined to continue administration of society/trust by joint administrators pending disposal of appeal by HC - Order under appeal is set aside and case is remitted to HC for disposal of appeal filed by respondents 1 and 2 afresh in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
