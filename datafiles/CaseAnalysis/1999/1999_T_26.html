Raj Deo Sharma (Ii) v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 741; (1999) 7 SCC 604; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1324; AIR 1999 SC 3524; AIR 1999 SCW 3522; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 184; 1999 (4) Crimes 52; 1999 CRLJ 4541; 1999 (81) DLT 660; 2000 (1) G.L.R. 605; JT 1999 (7) SC 317; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Clarification order - Instant petition is filed only for clarification of the directions of SC dt. 8-10-98 in appeal - Only prayer by the petitioner was to clarify the main judgment, in order that the Subordinate Courts understand the directions contained therein in the proper perspective and carry out the same in letter and spirit.

Held, in the judgment in the main appeal in the instant case, the court has not fixed any time limit for the conclusion of trial. The court has only laid down guidelines for closing the prosecution in certain circumstances. There is a difference between fixing a time limit for the disposal of a trial and fixing time limit for the prosecution to complete its evidence. A perusal of the guidelines contained in the main judgment would themselves show that there is no hard and fast rule applicable to every case, irrespective of facts and circumstances thereof. If the delay is not due to any fault of the prosecution, it is open to the prosecution to place the relevant facts before the court and seek further time for producing its evidence. The judgment in the appeal cannot be understood as punishing the prosecution and preventing the same from adducing evidence even when it is not responsible for the delay. Order accordingly.
