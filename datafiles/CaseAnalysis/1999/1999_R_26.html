Allahabad Bank, Calcutta v Radha Krishna Maity and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 766; (1999) 6 SCC 755; AIR 1999 SC 3426; 1999 (37) ALR 438; [1999] 98 Comp Cas 264; JT 1999 (6) SC 527; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 52; 2000 (125) PLR 1; 1999(5) SCALE 433; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Shall Not Be Bound, Debts Recovery Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1993

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 19(6) and 22 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, r. 1 - (A) Power of Debt Recovery Tribunal to pass Interim Order - Whether not limited to stay or injunction enumerated in sub-cl. (6) of s. 19 - (B) Where pending a suit filed by Bank before Tribunal for recovery of monies from respondents - Whether granting an injunction order restraining respondents from recovering any money from another company within powers of Tribunal? - Held, (A) Power of Debt Recovery Tribunal to pass Interim order not limited to stay or injunction - (B) Tribunal even has wider powers than CPC - Granting injunction order is within powers of Tribunal - However, has to observe principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
