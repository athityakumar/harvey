Dr. Uma Agrawal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1019; (1999) 3 SCC 438; (1999) SCC (L&S) 742; AIR 1999 SC 1212; 1999 (2) AWC 1486; 1999 (2) CLR 156; 1999 (3) ESC 1697; 1999 (82) FLR 72; JT 1999 (2) SC 359; 1999 LabIC 1174; 1999 (1) LLJ 1335; 1999 (2) LLN 651; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 29; 1999(2) SCALE 153; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 42; 1999 (3) SLJ 212; 1999 (2) SLR 22; 1999 (3) SLT 138; 1999 (3) Supreme 138; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1006

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Retirement benefits - Delay in dispersal - Interest thereon - Entitlement - Whether respondents are entitled to pay interest to appellant on account of delayed payment of her retrial benefits? - Held, this case is a clear example of departmental delay which is not excusable - Petitioner retired on 30-04-1993 amounts due to petitioner were cooperated and payments were made only during 1997-98 - It was only after 12-02-1996 when respondents started work by sending a special messenger to various places where petitioner had worked - This exercise was to be completed long before retirement of petitioner - Matter should not go back for computation of interest, instead, on facts of case, interest is payable to petitioner at Rs. 1 lakh within two months from today - Petition disposed of
