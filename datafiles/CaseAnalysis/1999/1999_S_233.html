State Of Tamil Nadu And Another v Board Of Trustee Of The Port Of Madras

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2259; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Trader, Food Corporations Act, 1964, Profit Motive, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Bengal Finance(Sales Tax) Act, 1941, M.P.General Sales Tax Act, 1959, CP & Berar Sales Tax Act, U.P.Sales Tax Act, 1948, W.B.Cement Control Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, ss. 37, 39, 42 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Establishment of port - Issuance of notice - Sustainability - Commercial Tax Officer issued notice calling upon Port Trust/respondent to register itself as dealer - Port Trust/respondent filed petition contending that 1959 Act did not apply to it and that notices issued to them were without jurisdiction - Single Judge of HC dismissed petition - Port Trust made appeal to DB which was allowed and notices were quashed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Port Trust was established by statute to 'carry on business' - Held, incidental or ancillary activities of sales were not 'business' and onus of proof of independent intention to do 'business' in that connected, incidental and ancillary sales would rest on department - However, if main activities were 'business' then sales in connection with or incidental or ancillary thereto need not be intended as business or commercial activity - Port Trusts were not established for carrying on business - Port Trust's activities and services in some detail only with view to show that port trust was not constituted by Parliament to 'carry on business' - In view of requirements of ss. 37, 39, 42 of the Act, ships came to ports and carry goods outside or bring goods inside our country - Therefore, it was clear that Port Trust was not involved in any activity of 'carrying on business' - Hence, case set up by State in show cause notice, it was not necessary to drive respondent - Port Trust to remedy under the Act - Appeal dismissed.
