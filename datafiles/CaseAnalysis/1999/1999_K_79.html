Commissioner of Income Tax v Kasturi and Sons Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1189; (1999) 3 SCC 346; AIR 1999 SC 1275; 1999 (153) CTR 1; [1999] 237 ITR 24; JT 1999 (2) SC 272; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1207; [1999] 103 TAXMAN 342; 1999 (2) TLR 425

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 41(2) - Contract Act, 1872, s. 126 - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 2(6 B) - Money payable on destruction of business asset - Taxability - Asset covered under contract of insurance - Terms of contract giving option to insurer to replace asset insured - On exercise of option to replace asset - Nature of contract gets altered to contract of reinstatement - Held, does not remain a contract for insurance which is contract for money - Non applicability of s. 41(2) therefore when there is replacement of asset on destruction/damage.
