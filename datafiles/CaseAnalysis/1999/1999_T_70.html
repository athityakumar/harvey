The State Of J & K & Anr. v Dev Dutt Pandit

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 31; (1999) 7 SCC 339; AIR 1999 SC 3196; 1999 (3) ARBLR 374; JT 1999 (6) SC 283; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration - Arbitration Act, 1940 - ss. 20, 30 and 33 - Respondent failed to complete the work granted under the contract and only half of the work was done - Contractor claimed a sum of money much higher then contract - Arbitrator made an award - Award file in court for making it rule of Court which was allowed - Whether the award of the Arbitrator was sustainable and properly made rule of Court? - Held, yes - Arbitrator rightly rejected the inflated claims made - Arbitration is considered important alternative disputes redressal process and its purpose would be hampered by unnecessary objections - Award being made rule of Court was justified - Disposed accordingly.
