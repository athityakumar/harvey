W. B. Essential Commodities Supply Corporation v Swadesh Agro Farming and Storage Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1516; (1999) 8 SCC 315; 1999 (3) ACR 340; AIR 1999 SC 3421; 1999 (37) ALR 340; 1999 (4) AWC 2768; JT 1999 (6) SC 599; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 44; 1999(5) SCALE 504; 2000 (Supp) RD 433; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r.11 and O. 20, r.6 A, - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 136, 5 and 12(2) - Execution of decree - Limitation - Whether starting point is date of decree and not date on which decree is actually drawn and signed? - Plea that due to court taking more than a year to draw up and sign decree, period of limitation of 12 years, available to party, is cut short so starting point of limitation has to be computed from date of signing of decree to avert hardship and prejudice to him - Held, decree becomes enforceable moment judgment is delivered - Period of limitation u/art. 136 of Limitation Act runs from date of decree and not from date when decree is actually drawn up and signed by Judge - Plea could not be accepted - Appeal dismissed.
