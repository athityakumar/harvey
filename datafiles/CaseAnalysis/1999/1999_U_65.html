All India Federation of Central Excise v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 973; (1999) 3 SCC 384; (1999) SCC (L&S) 690; AIR 1999 SC 1204; 1999 (81) ECR 369; 1999 (107) E.L.T. 3; 1999 (81) FLR 830; JT 1999 (1) SC 632; 1999 LabIC 1315; 1999 (2) LLN 631; 1999(1) SCALE 612; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 809; 2000 (1) SLJ 24; 1999 (2) SLR 329; 1999 (2) SLT 325; 1999 (2) Supreme 322

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Customs and Central Excise Service Group A (Amendment) Rules, 1998 - Proposals - Promotion - Recruitment - Adhoc basis - Feeder categories for promotion to posts in Group A services constituting Indian Customs and Central Excise (Group A) Service were Superintendents of Central Excise, Group B which consists of all promotees from lower cadres - Promotee from feeder-cadres of Customs Examiners in ratio of 50:50 - In respect of promotion to Group A posts from all three feeder groups (a), (b) and (c) it was agreed that proposals of Govt. of India - Under those proposals a new quota rule of 6:1:2 was to apply to three feeder channels in Group B for promotion to Group A - Court observed that proposals were just fair and equitable - Exercise was limited to promotee quota of 50% of Group A posts from three feeder channels because remaining 50% in Group A was to be filled by direct recruitment - Whether promotion was conducted on an adhoc basis - Held, there was no merit that promoted category of Group A officers, there should always be 6 promotees from Excise Superintendents, Group B for every 9 posts in Group A - UPSC for promotion to Group A, adhoc promotions have been made to Group A and members from first feeder category Superintendents of Excise Group B were not promoted - There have been excess promotions to Group A from petitioners category of excise Superintendents Group B and, therefore more officers from other two feeder channels have been promoted on an adhoc basis - Respondents shown adequate justification for not following ratio while making adhoc promotions - Appeal disposed of.
