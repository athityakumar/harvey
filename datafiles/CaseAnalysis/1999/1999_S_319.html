Amanulla Khan Kudeatalla Khan Pathan v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 June 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 566; (1999) 5 SCC 613; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1014; AIR 1999 SC 2197; 1999 CRLJ 3504; 2000 (4) G.L.R. 3623; JT 1999 (4) SC 455; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Tort

Summary: Arms Act, 1959 - Gujarat Prevention of Anti - Social Activities Act, 1985, ss. 2(c) and 3(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Dangerous person - Preventive detention - Single criminal case registered - Grounds of detention clearly mentioned three incidents which indicated repeated and persistent activity of detenu - Incidents considered by detaining authority - Activities of detenu whether amounts to breach of public order - Held, satisfaction of detaining authority that detenu habitually commits offence and is dangerous person cannot be said to be based on solitary incidence - Not vitiated - Disturbance caused by such activity amounts not only to breach of law and order but amounts to breach of public order - Order of detention, not vitiated.
