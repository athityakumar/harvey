Shivajirao B. Patil Kawekar v Vilasrao D. Deshmukh

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 507; (2000) 1 SCC 398; AIR 2000 SC 341; 2000 (1) CLT 55; JT 1999 (9) SC 448; 1999(7) SCALE 310; 1999 (10) SLT 222; 1999 (10) Supreme 129

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Circulation

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 100(1)(d), 116 A, 123(3A) and 123(4) - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, rr. 25 and 94A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 16 - Whether appellant had consented for publication of derogatory articles against respondent? - An inference as to publication having been made with consent of appellant cannot be drawn merely because journalist has been alleged to be canvasser of appellant - From statement of journalist it is clear that he as a journalist and owner of a newspaper, was, of his own, collecting information - Publication was neither sponsored by nor made with express consent of appellant - There are no facts and circumstances brought on record to draw an inference as to existence of even implied consent of appellant in publication - Respondent has failed to establish charge of corrupt practice against appellant - Judgment has proceeded more on 'assumptions' and 'surmises' than on any established facts - Appeal allowed.
