Gujarat Agro Industries Company Limited v Municipal Corporation of The City of Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1551; (1999) 4 SCC 468; AIR 1999 SC 1818; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 62; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 546; JT 1999 (3) SC 259; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 895

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, s. 406(2)(e), proviso - Rateable value or property tax - Appeal against - Maintainability of - Precondition of deposit of disputed tax - Proviso to s. 406(2)(e) limiting powers of Judge to dispense with condition of deposit only up to 25% of disputed tax - Held, cannot be said that such a law violates art. 14 of Constitution - If statute does not create any right of appeal, no appeal can be filed - For maintainability of an appeal there must be authority of law - Not arbitrary.
