T. Sivasubramaniam and Others v Kasinath Pujari and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 682; (1999) 7 SCC 275; AIR 1999 SC 3190; 1999 (37) ALR 752; JT 1999 (6) SC 405; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10(3)(a)(i) and 25 - Eviction of tenants on ground of bona fide requirement - Landlord desirous of living separately from his father but no other compelling reasons pleaded - Eviction decree set aside in revision - (A) if mere desire can be ground for eviction - (B) Setting aside of decree, if proper - Mere desire cannot constitute need and cannot be ground for eviction - Being contrary to law, setting aside proper - Appeal dismissed.
