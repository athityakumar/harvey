Velamuri Venkata Sivaprasad (Dead) By Lrs v Kothuri Venkateswarlu (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, M. Srinivasan, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 846; (2000) 2 SCC 139; AIR 2000 SC 434; 2000 (1) ALD(SC) 21; 1999 (3) CalLT(SC) 90; 2000 (1) DMC 1; JT 1999 (9) SC 242; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 485; 1999(7) SCALE 224; 1999 (10) SLT 40; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 522; 1999 (10) Supreme 38

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce, Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Law - Succession - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Hindu Widow's Remarriage Act, 1856, s. 2 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madras Hindu (Bigamy, Prevention and Divorce) Act (6 of 1949), s. 2 - (A) Whether remarriage of a widow prior to Hindu Succession Act, 1956 would divest her of even limited ownership of her deceased husband's property, having due regard to provisions of s. 2 of Hindu Widow's Remarriage Act, 1856? - Held, s. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act, in light of explanation thereto, clearly indicates that if a Hindu widow is given in lieu of her pre-existing right of maintenance, any property with limited interest, said interest would mature into full ownership u/s. 14(1) of Act - It has further to be kept in view that under will, first wife's preexisting right of maintenance was recognised and in recognition of very same right by compromise decree she was given limited interest to recover maintenance from earmarked properties mentioned in same decree - It has, therefore, to be held in light of judgment in Tulasamma case (1977 Indlaw SC 281) that if her right of maintenance had survived till coming into operation of s. 14(1) of Act, then her limited interest in properties concerned over which said right was exercised as per consent terms could have matured u/s. 14(1) of Act, and to that extent Division Bench judgment upsetting view of learned Single Judge cannot be found fault with - However, in light of admitted fact on record, on remarriage of widow in 1953, her right to claim maintenance from ex-husband's properties got extinguished prior to 1956 - Succession Act of 1956 obviously is prospective in operation and in event of a divestation prior to 1956, question of applicability of s. 14(1) would not arise since on date when it applied, there was already a remarriage disentitling widow to inherit property of deceased husband - Act of 1856 had its full play on date of remarriage itself, as such Succession Act could not confer on widow who has already remarried, any right in terms of s. 14(1) of Act of 1956 - Succession Act has transformed a limited ownership to an absolute ownership but it cannot be made applicable in event of there being a factum of pre-divestation of estate as a limited owner - If there existed a limited estate or interest for widow, it could become absolute but if she had no such limited estate or interest in lieu of her right of maintenance from out of deceased husband's estate, there would be no occasion to get such non-existing limited right converted into full ownership right - (B) Whether disqualification of inheritance, if any, by reason of remarriage would stand obliterated by reason of provisions of Madras Hindu (Bigamy Prevention and Divorce) Act, 1949? - Further held, Act of 1949 being penal in nature, was introduced in statute - book to prohibit bigamous marriages and to provide for a right of divorce on certain grounds as mentioned therein - Statutory prohibition cannot be treated to be in aid of conferment of right, it is a prohibitory statute and not a conferring statute - In contextual facts, doctrine of sincerity has its due application - Lakshmamma cannot take advantage of her own immoral conduct and illegality to confer upon herself a right to continue to get maintenance from properties of her deceased husband under consent decree - In contextual facts of matter under consideration however, and since factual situation of remarriage of Lakshmamma in year 1953 stands proved, it has to be held that s. 2 of Hindu Widow's Remarriage Act, 1856 gets attracted - As a result thereof, defendant 1's right to get maintenance from her deceased husband's property came to an end on her civil death qua her ex-husband's estate latest by 1953 - Hence, there was no subsisting legal right of maintenance available to defendant 1 qua her deceased husband's estate in any of his properties nor was there a subsisting limited interest of hers in any of those properties which get matured into full ownership u/s. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act when it came into force - Widow divests herself of any right to deceased's properties by reason of s. 2 of Act of 1856, but in event widow is married to a person with a spouse living, same tantamounts to no marriage and resultantly entitlement under general law would be available to widow - Appeal allowed.
