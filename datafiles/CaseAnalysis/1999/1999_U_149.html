Indra Sawhney v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, D.P. Wadhwa, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 262; (2000) SCC (L&S) 1; (2000) 1 SCC 168; AIR 2000 SC 498; AIR 1999 SCW 4661; 2000 (1) ALD(SC) 54; JT 1999 (9) SC 557; 2000 (33) LabIC 27; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 229; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 455

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Kerala State Backward Classes (Reservation of Appointments or Posts in Services under State) Act, 1995, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - 'Creamy Layer' - Identification of - Backward Classes in State of Kerala - Implementation of law declared and directions issued in Indra Sawhney v. UOI, (1992 Indlaw SC 735 ) - Committee appointed - Part I of report says even if statutory reservation in favour of any backward class is not satisfied or there is over representation, same will not be a justification for giving benefit of reservation u/art. 16(4) in favour of affluent part of other Backward Classes - Part 11 of report deals with criteria fixed for identifying 'creamy layer' in Backward Classes - Other Backward Classes coming up on merit basis were to be excluded from 40% reservations of other Backward Classes - Exclusion of creamy layer to apply in Govt. and Public Sector, Govt. Company and Autonomous Bodies - Held, guidelines and criteria fixed by committee to be applicable to these communities and sub-castes also - Justice Joseph Committee's Report accepted in toto subject to addition of communities and sub-castes as pointed out in affidavit of State - Applications and petitions disposed of.
