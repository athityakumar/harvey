R. R. Naidu v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2205; (2000) 10 SCC 141

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 21(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Removal from service - Limitation - Challenged - Appellant filed suit against his removal from service u/s. 80 CPC - Matter went up to HC - HC directed to implead District Judge and Registrar - Later, Trial Court dismissed suit as barred by limitation - HC affirmed Trial Court order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, if there is delay in impleading the District Judge and the Registrar of the HC it can be condoned under the proviso of s. 21(1) of the Act. Further, it was necessary for the HC to call for the original record relating to the removal of the plaintiff and find out the circumstances immediately preceding the order of the removal to find out whether the removal was based upon any categorical findings arrived at in order to find out his guilt. Hence, matter is remitted to HC for decision on the question as to whether the order of removal was punitive or not - Appeal allowed.
