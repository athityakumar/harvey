J. Jermons v Aliammal

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1248; (1999) 7 SCC 382; AIR 1999 SC 3041; 1999 (156) CTR 31; JT 1999 (6) SC 26; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 71; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 467; 1999 (2) TLR 848

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 ss. 220(2), 222, 222(1)(a), 226(3), 226(3)(Viii) - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960 - Prohibitory order - Willful default - Eviction order - Validity - Appellant/tenant took suit premises on rent from respondents/landlords - On 6-3-1979, appellant was served with prohibitory order by Tax Recovery Officer, Income Tax Department, which was followed by another order issued by same authority u/s. 226(3) of the 1961 Act on 18-1-1988 - From date of service of prohibitory order appellant stopped payment of monthly rent to respondents, but on receiving notice on 18-1-1988, appellant paid rent for entire period to Tax Recovery Officer - Respondents filed application for eviction of appellant before Rent Controller u/ss. 10(2)(i) and 10(3)(a)(iii) of the 1960 Act on ground that appellant had committed wilful default in payment of rent and that respondent required premises for his own use and occupation - Application was dismissed on 30-4-1991 - Respondents filed appeal before Appellate Authority which only found ground of wilful default in payment of rent against appellant and accordingly eviction of appellant was ordered on 12-4-1993 - Against that order both appellant and respondents filed Revisions before HC, which confirmed order of eviction by common order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, it was true that there was no direction in prohibitory order to appellant not to pay future rent to landlord from that date, but tenant believed that, by virtue of prohibitory order of Tax Recovery Officer, appellant was directed not to pay rent to landlord who was also injuncted from receiving rent until further orders of that authority - SC was satisfied that there was reasonable basis for tenant to assume that he was prohibited from paying rent and it was also strengthened by fact that during this period landlord also did not make any demand and therefore, this could not entail in appellant's eviction from suit premises - Further, it was not case of landlord that he paid tax due to concerned Income tax Authority and informed same to tenant, nor was there any material to effect that during said period authorities withdrew notice - Therefore, non-payment of rent regularly to respondents after receipt of notice and while prohibitory order was in force, would not give any cause of action to respondents/landlord to file eviction petition against appellant on ground of wilful default in payment of rent - Thus, payment of rent by the tenant to the Tax Recovery Officer instead of to the landlord was indeed payment not only in discharge of his contractual obligation and statutory obligation under the 1960 Act but also under said provision of the 1961 Act - In such case no landlord could be heard to say that though tenant had paid rent in compliance with notice of attachment and notice u/s. 266(3) of the 1961 Act towards discharge of income tax due by him covered by certificate issued u/s. 222 of the 1961 Act, yet such payment would have to be ignored for purpose of the 1960 Act and tenant would have to be treated as wilful defaulter - Therefore, SC set aside impugned order of HC - Appeal allowed.
