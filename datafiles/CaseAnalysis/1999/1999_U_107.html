Punjab Communications Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1548; (1999) 4 SCC 727; AIR 1999 SC 1801; JT 1999 (3) SC 331; 1999(3) SCALE 149; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 1033

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Administrative - Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Tenders Cancellation - Validity - Asian Development Bank (ADB) agreed to grant soft loan to UOI/respondent no. 1 for funding digital wireless telecom facility project to identified villages in Eastern UP - Dept. of Tele-communications invited tender on 9-10-1996 - Technical Evaluation Committee (TEC) short-listed appellant/Punjab Communications Ltd and BEL on 7-7-1997 - High Level Committee submitted its report stating that there were 2 'deviations' in tenders submitted by appellant as noticed by TEC in respect of required specifications - Therefore, impugned order was passed cancelling tenders on 16-12-1997 - Appellant then filed writ petition on 6-1-1998 in HC of Punjab and Haryana, which dismissed writ petition on 27-2-1998 - Review application was filed but that was also dismissed on 19-3-1998 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether cancellation of tenders was proper - Held, though main judgment in writ petition was rendered by HC on 27-2-1998, events which had taken place during pendency of these appeals had changed complexion of case - Therefore, review of facts and subsequent events showed that issues which were live when writ petition of appellant was pending in HC had now lost all their relevance and entire tender was based on ADB loan, if ADB loan itself had now stood withdrawn, there was now no possibility of ADB loan project for Eastern UP being started or completed - It would well nigh?? be impossible to issue any directions to UOI to seek renewal of lapsed loan nor to issue any directions to continue project for Eastern UP on basis of ADB loan - Further, it would be noticed that at one stage when ADB loan lapsed, Govt. took decision to go ahead with project on its own funds, but later it thought that scheme regarding telephones in rural areas should cover not only villages in Eastern UP but also in other backward rural areas in other states - Thus, statistics given in counter-affidavits of UOI showed that there were other states in country where percentage of telephones was far less than what it was in eastern UP - Therefore said facts were reason for change in policy of govt. and for giving up notification calling for bids for Eastern UP - Therefore, such change in policy could not be said to be irrational or perverse - Appeal dismissed.
