Govindan v Subramaniam and others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2228; (2000) 9 SCC 510

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Legality

Summary: Civil Procedure - Locus standi - Legality - Appellant filed instant appeal against the order of HC - Whether HC in impugned judgment was justified in taking the view that Defendant 2, who was one of the appellants in the second appeal had no locus standi to maintain the same.

Held, observation of HC that the appellant's vendors have not filed any appeal would therefore not come in the way. It is obvious that once they had sold out the disputed properties to appellant-Defendant 2, they would not be interested in prosecuting the proceedings any further and the real interest would be of only Defendant 2, appellant herein to try to show that his vendors had legal title to convey the property in question. SC finds that HC while dealing with legal position has held that the appeal filed by appellant to be not maintainable. It is pertinent to note that HC has framed substantial question of law as mentioned in the judgment. This question is not answered at all on the ground that the appellant had no locus standi to file the appeal. Thus, impugned judgment and order are set aside. The second appeal is restored to the file of HC with request to redecide the same on the substantial question of law which has been framed and answer the same on merits after hearing the parties. Appeal allowed.
