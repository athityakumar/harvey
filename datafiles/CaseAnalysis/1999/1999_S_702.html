Secretary, H.S.E.B v Suresh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1040; (1999) 3 SCC 601; (1999) SCC (L&S) 765; AIR 1999 SC 1160; JT 1999 (2) SC 435; 1999 LabIC 1323; 1999 (1) LLJ 1086; 1999 (2) LLN 612; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 238; 1999 (2) SLR 1; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1087; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1186

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Intermediary, Equality

Summary: Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, s. 10 - Contract Labour - Absorption in service - Electricity Board - Work to maintain cleanliness and hygiene - Overall control of workings and administration of contract labour with Board - Board neither registered nor contractor licensed - Held, contract system a mere camouflage which could be easily pierced and employer employee relationship between Board and employee easily visualised - Work not of seasonal nature - Employees having engagement of more than 240 days cannot therefore be denied absorption.
