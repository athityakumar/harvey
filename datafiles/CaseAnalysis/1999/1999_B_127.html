Boddu Narayanamma v Venkatarama Aluminium Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 734; (1999) 7 SCC 589; AIR 1999 SC 3549; 1999 (6) ALD(SC) 6; JT 1999 (7) SC 364; 2000 (1) RCJ 147; 1999(6) SCALE 122; 2000 (2) SCJ 236; 1999 (9) SLT 317; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 20; 1999 (8) Supreme 87

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 2(iii), 10(3)(a) and 10(3)(a)(i)(b) - Appellant is landlady and respondents are tenants - Eviction from demised building on ground of bona fide requirement for personal residence of their family - Whether a petition by a landlord seeking eviction of a tenant u/s. 10(3)(a)(i)(b) of Act from demised building, let out under a composite lease for residential as well as non-residential purposes, is maintainable? - Combined reading of s. 2(iii) and s. 10(3)(a) of Act indicates that when a residential building or a non-residential building or parts thereof are let separately for residential and/or non-residential purposes, provisions of s. 10(3)(a), namely, sub-cl. (i) in case of a residential building and sub-cls. (ii) and (iii) in case of a non-residential building, can be invoked and on requirements thereof being satisfied a landlord can seek eviction of a tenant therefrom - Nothing in Act to suggest that right of a landlord like appellant to recover possession of demised building from respondent tenant is taken away either expressly or by necessary implication - Once it is held that a building having both residential and non-residential portions which are let out together under a composite lease is a building within meaning of that term and within ambit of Act, such a building has to fall within one or other category, namely, residential or non-residential - On such determination, suit/petition of landlord has to be decided u/sub-cl. (i) or sub-cls. (ii) or (iii) of cl. (a) of s. 10(3) of A.P Act, as case may be - Held, requirement for residence of appellant is bona fide and petition of appellant filed u/s. 10(3)(a)(i)(b) is maintainable, therefore appellant is entitled to a direction to respondents to put appellant landlord in possession of demised building - Appeal allowed.
