State of Himachal Pradesh v Jailal and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 694; (1999) 7 SCC 280; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1184; AIR 1999 SC 3318; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 15; 1999 CRLJ 4294; JT 1999 (6) SC 548; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 318

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Quantity

Summary: Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 420 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Criminal conspiracy - Scheme by State Govt. to purchase diseased affected apples from its growers and to destroy them - Allegation that some growers had cheated State - Evidence of expert as to fruit bearing capacity of orchards in question belonging to said growers - Held, expert visited orchards only in succeeding year after apple season of that year was over - Neither made any scientific study or research in such assessment nor he undertook such job earlier - Report was based on surmises and conjectures and runs counter to expert evidence led by parties - Was clearly unreliable and inadequate to establish charges against accused - Acquittal of accused - No interference.
