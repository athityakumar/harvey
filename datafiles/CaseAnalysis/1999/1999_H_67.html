Hyderabad Karnataka Education Society v Registrar of Societies and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 577; (2000) 1 SCC 566; AIR 2000 SC 301; JT 1999 (9) SC 482; 1999(7) SCALE 361; 1999 (10) SLT 408; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 161; 1999 (10) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Karnataka Societies Registration Act, 1960, ss. 2(b), 5, 6, 9, 10, 11, 18, 19, 21, 22, and 23, 25, rr. 7A and 7B- (A) Whether r. 7-A and the earlier r. 7 are violative, of s. 2(b) of the Act? - Held, the impugned r. 7-A and its predecessor r. 7, which is in pari materia, do not offend s. 2(b) in any manner, answered in favour of the appellant Society - (B) Whether the aforesaid rules are harsh and unconscionable? - Held, the rule is very harsh in its operation and, therefore, it is arbitrary - (C) Whether the consequential directions issued by High Court are justified in law? - Held, the impugned judgment of HC cannot be sustained, is set aside - Interim orders stand vacated - Appeals allowed.
