Dattatraya Laxman Kamble v Abdul Rasul Moulali Kotkunde and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1118; (1999) 4 SCC 1; AIR 1999 SC 2226; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 543; JT 1999 (2) SC 283; 1999 (2) MahLJ 793; 1999 (2) RCJ 1; 1999 (1) RCR(Rent) 508; 1999(3) SCALE 46; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 912; 1999 (4) SLT 196; 1999 (4) Supreme 350

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13(1)(g) - Eviction of tenant on ground of 'Reasonably and bona fide required by landlord' i.e. for starting business - Interpretation or determination of - Possessing know-how for doing business, if amount to requirement? - Held, it means really genuine requirement from any reasonable standard, but not on standard of dire need - Further held, that it is not a requirement - Appeal allowed.
