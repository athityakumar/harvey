Madan Mohan Choudhary v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 445; (1999) 3 SCC 396; (1999) SCC (L&S) 700; AIR 1999 SC 1018; 1999 (1) BLJR 630; 1999 (1) CLR 80; 1999 (1) CLR 800; 1999 (1) ESC 799; 1999 (81) FLR 712; JT 1999 (1) SC 459; 1999 (2) LLJ 229; 1999 (2) LLN 4; 1999(1) SCALE 444; 1999 (1) SCJ 449; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 596; 2000 (1) SLJ 87; 1999 (1) SLR 718; 1999 (2) SLT 100; 1999 (1) Supreme 448

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Bihar Service Code, 1952, r. 74 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 233, 234, 235 and 311 - Subordinate judiciary - Compulsory retirement of Addl. District and Sessions Judge - Ground, granting anticipatory bail in a case under s. 307 Indian Penal Code - Held, cannot be made basis for his Compulsory retirement - Particularly when there was a cross case - Order might be wrong but it was an order passed on judicial side in all bona fides - Consideration of uncommunicated adverse remark - Does not vitiate action of compulsory retirement if such remark is recorded in normal course - Remarks for 3 years recorded at one go and that too after standing committee of HC has formed opinion to retire Sessions Judge - Cannot be said to be recorded in 'normal course' - Order based on such remarks liable to be set aside.
