Mahabir Prasad Jain v Ganga Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 532; (1999) 8 SCC 274; AIR 1999 SC 3873; 1999 (37) ALR 742; 1999 (82) DLT 36; JT 1999 (7) SC 639; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 97; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 415

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 45 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Suit for possession - (A) Whether High Court failed in its duty, in non - considering evidence adduced in light of findings of Trial Court as to failure to prove tenancy in suit for possession? - (B) Entitlement to get equitable relief under Specific Relief Act when respondent is making inconsistent allegations and abusing process of Court - (C) Whether grant of additional relief not claimed is beyond scope of s. 6 of 1963 Act? - Held, exclusive possession would not give rise to presumption of tenancy so HC failed in its duty - Not entitled to get any equitable relief under Specific Relief Act - Order of Trial Court and HC liable to be set aside as it was beyond scope of s. 6 - Appeal allowed.
