Kondiba Dagdu Kadam v Savitribai Sopan Gujar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1093; (1999) 3 SCC 722; AIR 1999 SC 2213; AIR 1999 SCW 2740; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 57; 1999 (36) ALR 218; 1999 (2) AWC 1608; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 532; 1999 (3) CLT 62; 1999 (2) CTC 468; JT 1999 (3) SC 163; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 105; 1999(2) SCALE 633; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 728; 1999 (4) SLT 163; 2000 (Supp) RD 265; 1999 (4) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Second appeal - Substantial question of law - What can be termed as - Held, relief cannot be granted merely on equitable grounds - Concurrent finding of facts erroneous - Cannot be interfered with - No Court has power to add to or enlarge those grounds - Where it is found that first Appellate Court has assumed jurisdiction which did not vest in it, same can be adjudicated in second appeal, treating it as substantial question of law.
