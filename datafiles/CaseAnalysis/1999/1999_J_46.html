A. Mohammed Farook v Joint Secretary To G. O. I. and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 490; (2000) 2 SCC 360; (2000) SCC (Cr) 411; 2000 (2) CCR 89; JT 1999 (10) SC 290; 1999(7) SCALE 274; 2000 (4) SLT 59

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities, Act, 1975, s. 3(1) - Detention order passed u/s. 3(1) against petitioner - Legality of - Detaining authority must explain satisfactorily inordinate delay in executing detention order otherwise subjective satisfaction gets vitiated - Held, only explanation given by detaining authority as regards delay of 40 days in executing detention order is that despite their efforts petitioner could not be located at his residence or in his office and therefore order could not be executed immediately - In absence of any satisfactory explanation explaining delay of 40 days, detention order must stand vitiated by reason of non-execution thereof within a reasonable time - Detention order quashed - Petition allowed.
