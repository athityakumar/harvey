State of Himachal Pradesh v P. D. Attri and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 426; (1999) 3 SCC 217; (1999) SCC (L&S) 671; AIR 1999 SCW 4897; 1999 (1) CLR 795; 1999 (1) ESC 787; JT 1999 (1) SC 441; 1999 (2) LLN 654; 1999(1) SCALE 409; 1999 (1) SCJ 434; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 587; 2000 (1) SLJ 125; 1999 (1) SLR 641; 1999 (2) SLT 77; 1999 (2) Supreme 57

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Punjab And Haryana High Court Establishment (Appointment And Conditions

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 231, 229(2) r/w. 231 - Punjab & Haryana High Court Establishment (Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1973 - Himachal Pradesh High Court Officers and Servants (Salaries, Leave, Allowance and Pension) Rules, 1971 - Notification - Re-designation - Scale of Pay - Revision - Respondents/employees of HC were governed by Rules, 1971, working as Senior Translators and Junior Translators, as per notification after receiving approval of President of India u/art. 229(2) r/w. 231 of Constitution - As a result, posts of Senior Translators were re-designated as Revisers' and equated with posts of 'Superintendent' - Appellant/State as per "policy and practice" had been adopting same pay-scales for employees of HC, as sanctioned from time to time for employees of Punjab & Haryana HC and it may even follow to grant pay-scales, but was certainly not bound to follow - Chief Justice of appellant /State HC made recommendations to Governor to re-designate/equate posts of Senior Translators and Junior Translators in appellant's HC, to those in Punjab & Haryana HC, no decision was communicated to respondent - Respondents/employees approached HC, which allowed same - Whether appellant/State had to follow every change brought in States of Punjab & Haryana in regard to Rules and regulations applicable to employees in States of Punjab & Haryana - Held, Court felt that once Chief Justice, in interest of HC administration, had taken a progressive step specially to ameliorate service conditions of officers and staff working under him - Appellant/State Govt. would hardly raise any objection to sanction of creation of posts or fixation of salary payable for that post or recommendation for revision of scale of pay - Recommendations of appellant/ State Chief Justice of HC should be considered by State Govt. - Judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
