Dr. Preeti Srivastava and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 758; (1999) 7 SCC 120; AIR 1999 SC 2894; AIR 1999 SCW 2795; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 34; 1999 ALJ 1947; 1999 (4) AWC 2853; JT 1999 (5) SC 498; 1999(4) SCALE 579; 1999 (4) SLR 687; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Andhra University Act 1926

Summary: Education - Health & Drug - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Service - Andhra University Act, 1926 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Whether apart from providing reservation for admission to the postgraduate courses in Engineering and Medicine for special category candidates, it is open to the State to prescribe different admission criteria, in the sense of prescribing different minimum qualifying marks, for special 'Category candidates seeking admission under the reserved category.'? - The State of Uttar Pradesh has prescribed a Postgraduate Medical Entrance Examination for admission to postgraduate degree/diploma courses in Medicine - Therefore, consideration of national interest and the interests of the community or society as a whole cannot be ignored in determining the reasonableness of a special provision u/art. 15(4) - Held, we are concerned with special provision u/art. 15(4) made at the stage of admission to the postgraduate medical courses, providing for lesser qualifying marks or no qualifying marks for the members of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes for admission to the postgraduate medical courses - Emphasis placed on the fact that since all the candidates finally appear and pass in the same examination, standards are maintained - Appeal dismissed.
