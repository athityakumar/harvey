Rajendra Kumar Sitaram Pande and Others v Uttam and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 427; (1999) 3 SCC 134; (1999) SCC (Cr) 393; AIR 1999 SC 1028; AIR 1999 SCW 606; 1999 (38) AllCriC 438; 1999 (1) CCR 82; 1999 (1) Crimes 88; 1999 CRLJ 1620; 1999 (1) JCC 140; JT 1999 (1) SC 426; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 800; 1999(1) SCALE 402; 1999 (1) SCJ 445; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 580; 1999 (2) SLT 35; 1999 (2) Supreme 63

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 204, 397(2) and 482 - Indian Penal Code, s. 500 - Order issuing process against accused - (A) Maintainability - Whether order directing issuance of process is an 'interlocutory order' within meaning of s. 397(2) which bars exercise of revisional jurisdiction? - (B) Allegation - Accused made false imputations - Departmental enquiry initiated Complainant found guilty - Whether allegations in complaint of accused covered by exception 8 to s. 499 of IPC? - Held, (A) expression 'interlocutory order' in s. 397(2) - Restricted sense - Merely denotes orders of purely interim or temporary nature which do not decide or touch important rights or liabilities of parties - (B) Order directing issuance of process is not purely interlocutory - No case of defamation made out - Order issuing process against accused, quashed - Appeal allowed.
