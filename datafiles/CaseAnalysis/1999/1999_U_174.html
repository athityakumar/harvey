A. K. Singh and Others v Uttarakhand Jan Morcha and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 540; (1999) 4 SCC 476; (1999) SCC (Cr) 580; AIR 1999 SC 2193; AIR 1999 SCW 2216; 1999 ALJ 1638; 1999 (39) AllCriC 262; 1999 (3) AWC 2408; 1999 CRLJ 3500; JT 1999 (4) SC 414; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 624

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 226-Writ petition-Confrontation of police with Uttarakhand rallysts-Resulting in lot of blood-shed including loss of many lives, infliction of injuries on persons, outraging modesty of women ranging to ravishments-Judges of Allahabad High Court should have avoided making observations concerning matters pending consideration by subordinate courts-Judges directed Government to pay compensation of various sums without trial and without even considering evidence which may be adduced in cases-Direction for payment of compensation-Clearly unsustainable and liable to be set aside-However, if any sum disbursed to claimants-State will not recover same-If any person has not made claim-It will be open to him/her to approach competent court for compensation in accordance with law-Direction for fund allotment for development of hilly regions of U. P.-Judges not indicating how Government should make up such huge amounts- Direction cannot stand judicial scrutiny-And liable to be set aside.
