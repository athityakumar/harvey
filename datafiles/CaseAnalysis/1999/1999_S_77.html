K. Bhaskaran v Sankaran Vaidhyan Balan and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 755; (1999) 7 SCC 510; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1284; AIR 1999 SC 3762; 1999 (39) AllCriC 844; 2005 (3) BC 158; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 178; 1999 (3) Crimes 212; 1999 CRLJ 4606; JT 1999 (7) SC 558; 1999 (3) KLT 440; 2000 (124) PLR 113; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 271; 1999 (8) Supreme 608

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Unclaimed

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Ss. 118, 138, 139 and 177 - (1) Whether the cause of action has arisen as the notice sent by the appellant to the accused was returned as "unclaimed"? - When a notice is returned by the sendee as unclaimed such date would be the commencing date in reckoning the period of 15 days - Such reckoning would be without prejudice to the right of the drawer of the cheque to show that he had no knowledge that the notice was brought to his address - The appellant never attempted to discharge the burden to rebut the presumption - Failure on the part of the drawer to pay the amount should be within 15 days "of the receipt" of the said notice - Giving is a process of which receipt is the accomplishment, it is for the payee to perform the former process by sending the notice to the drawer at the correct address - Held, the accused guilty of the offence - (2) Whether the High Court has rightly imposed a sentence of imprisonment for 6 months and a fine of Rs. one lakh on the appellant? - Sentence and award of compensation must be considered by the trial court - Magistrate shall hear the prosecution and the accused if the complainant and the accused settle their disputes regarding this cheque - It can be taken into consideration in determining the extent or quantum of sentence - Even if the trial was before a Court of a Magistrate of the First Class the Court has power to award compensation to be paid to the complainant - Held, conviction of the offence u/s. 138 is upheld - The sentence awarded by the High Court is set aside.
