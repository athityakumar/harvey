Jalandhar Improvement Trust v Sampuran Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 907; (1999) 3 SCC 494; AIR 1999 SC 1347; 1999 (2) CLT 135; JT 1999 (2) SC 598; 1999(2) SCALE 415; 1999 (3) SLT 306; 1999 (4) Supreme 270

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Equitable Estoppel

Summary: Punjab Development Of Damaged Areas Act, 1951, s. 73 - Jullundur Improvement Land Disposal Rules, 1954, r. 2 (b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Preferential allotment of plot - Benefit of, is available to person who qualifies to be locally displaced person under Rule - Held, mere fact that similarly situated persons were allotted plots - No ground to claim equal treatment if allotments were made wrongly in favour of those persons - Wrongly allotted plots may become liable for cancellation - That apart, acceptance of initial deposit towards consideration - Cannot protect illegal allotment by applying principle of promissory/equitable estoppel - There is no estoppel against law.
