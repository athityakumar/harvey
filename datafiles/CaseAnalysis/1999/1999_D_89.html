Utkal University v Dr. Nrishingha Charan Sarangi and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1161; (1999) 2 SCC 193; (1999) SCC (L&S) 541; AIR 1999 SC 943; 1999 (1) CLR 633; JT 1999 (1) SC 101; 1999 (2) LLN 40; 1999(1) SCALE 92; 1999 (2) SCJ 94; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 19; 2000 (1) SLJ 61; 1999 (1) SLT 205; 1999 (1) Supreme 160; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 543

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Editor

Summary: Utkal University Act, 1966, s. 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 16 and 14 - Whether appointment of reader be invalidated on ground that claimant did not possess qualification of D Litt degree at time of filing application for said post but before date of interview? - Whether petitioner has locus standi to file petition? - Whether selection committee was biased in favour of selected candidate? - Held, there is no reason to hold that claimant would have been selected if additional marks were given to him for said qualification - Hence, said appointment of reader and selection of candidate cannot be invalidated on that ground - No legal injury to petitioner disclosed as there was no reason for holding that petitioner would have been selected even if all his contentions in writ petition were accepted - Petitioner has no locus standi to file petition - It cannot be held that Selection Committee was biased in favour of selected candidate.
