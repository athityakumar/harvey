State through CBI, Delhi v Gian Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, G.B. Pattanaik, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 697; (1999) 9 SCC 312; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1512; AIR 1999 SC 3450; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 34; 1999 (3) CCR 238; 1999 CRLJ 4315; 1999 (81) DLT 556; 1999 (2) JCC 493; JT 1999 (6) SC 575; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 193; 1999(5) SCALE 497; 1999 (7) SLT 432; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 382; 1999 (8) Supreme 44

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Prevention Act, s. 3(1)(2) - (A) Scope - Accused persons firing revolvers towards podium wherefrom deceased leader was to address public meeting - Their main intention was to administer a terror or shock wave to people at large - Deceased leader was not only target of shooters, though he was one of principal targets - Whether accused liable to be convicted for offence u/s. 3(1)? - (B) Sentence - Punishment provided u/s. 3(2) of 1985 Act superseded by provisions under TADA Act 1987 - Extreme penalty of death diluted to alternative option of imprisonment for life - Whether benefit of said benevolent provision can be extended to accused who committed offence during subsistence of 1985 TADA Act? - Held, (A) said act of accused would fall within ambit of s. 3(1) - Sentence as for offence u/s. 3(2) of TADA 1985 is altered to imprisonment for life - (B) said legislative benevolence can be extended to accused who awaits judicial verdict regarding sentence - Appeal allowed.
