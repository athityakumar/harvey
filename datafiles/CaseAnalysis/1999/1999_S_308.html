Jai Karan v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 748; (1999) 8 SCC 161; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1385; AIR 1999 SC 3512; 1999 CRLJ 4529; 1999 (81) DLT 859; JT 1999 (7) SC 514; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Dying declaration - Independent piece of evidence can be acted upon without corroboration if true and reliable - Doctor alleged to have admitted deceased in hospital and recorded her statement - But neither read over it to deceased nor get it signed or attested due to her unfit condition - In facts and circumstances of case held that accused not be convicted solely on basis of said statement of deceased - Appeal allowed.
