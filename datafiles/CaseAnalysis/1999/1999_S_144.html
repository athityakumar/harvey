High Court of Judicature At Bombay Though Its Registrar v Shashikant S. Patil and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 798; (2000) SCC (L&S) 144; (2000) 1 SCC 416; AIR 2000 SC 22; 2000 (1) AWC 99; 1999 (2) CLR 115; JT 1999 (8) SC 493; 2000 (1) LLN 317; 2000 (2) SLJ 98; 1999 (5) SLR 615; 2000 (7) SLR 322; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 205; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 158

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Miscellaneous - Jurisdiction of High Court u/art. 226 with respective to disciplinary proceedings - Held, Division Bench of HC has not approached question from correct angle which is evident when Bench said that it is imperative for Disciplinary Committee to discuss materials in detail and contest conclusions of enquiry officer - If a judicial officer 'tips scales of justice its, rippling effect would be disastrous and deleterious' - When a constitutional function is exercised by administrative side of HC any judicial review thereon should be made with great care and circumspection, but confining strictly to parameters set by this Court - Appeal allowed.
