Saij Gram Panchayat v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 940; (1999) 2 SCC 366; AIR 1999 SC 826; AIR 1999 SCW 492; JT 1999 (1) SC 223; 1999(1) SCALE 176; 1999 (1) SCJ 344; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 263; 1999 (1) SLT 338; 1999 (1) Supreme 248

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution (Seventy-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1992 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Gujarat Industrial Development Act, 1962, s.16 - Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963, 264A - Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, s. 9(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Notification - Industrial area - Consolidated tax - Principles of natural justice - Notification was issued by State Govt. u/s.2(g) of 1962 Act declaring certain lands of village - Notification u/s.16 of 1962 Act declaring Industrial area as notified area u/s. 264A of 1963 Act - Govt. resolution whereby l/3rd of amount recovered as consolidated tax by proposed notified area authority used for benefit of gram Panchayats - HC for quashing impugned notifications of Govt. resolution - Notification issued u/s.9(2) of 1961 Act, excluding notified area from gram panchayat was also contrary to Parts IX and IXA of Constitution - Hence notification was illegal and void - Hence, instant appeals - Whether notification issued u/s.9(2) of 1961 Act, excluding notified area from gram panchayat contrary to Parts IX and IXA of Constitution - Held, inclusion of an area governed by a Gram Panchayat within a notified area would certainly involve civil consequences - Denial of such an opportunity was not in consonance with scheme of rule of law governing our society - There had been a long drawn out exchange of objections over issuing of a notification u/s.16 of 1962 Act with exclusion of area from panchayat area u/s.9(2) of 1961 Act - Gram panchayat from out of taxes collected from notified areas which were removed from jurisdiction of gram Panchayats, therefore, appellants could not complain of any violation of principles of natural justice - Appeal disposed of.
