Haryana Telecom Limited v Sterlite Industries (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1986; (1999) 5 SCC 688; AIR 1999 SC 2354; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 31; 1999 (37) ALR 99; 1999 (3) AWC 2436; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 245; [1999] 97 Comp Cas 683; 1999 (3) CompLJ 161; JT 1999 (4) SC 545; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Keywords: 6. For the aforesaid reasons this petition is dismissed in limine.

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Section 8 (1)-Reference to arbitration-Scope of Section 8 (1)- Only that dispute or matter referable which arbitrator competent or empowered to decide-Matter of winding up of company pending before High Court-It cannot be referred to arbitration-High Court right in rejecting application.

The power to order winding up of a company is contained under the Companies Act and is conferred on the Court. An arbitrator, notwithstanding any agreement between the parties, would have no Jurisdiction to order winding up of a company.
