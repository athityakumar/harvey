Jangir Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2220; (2000) 10 SCC 261

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 325/149 - Murder and rioting - Acquittal - Reversal by HC - Appellants allegedly rioted and murdered the deceased - Trial Court acquitted appellants - However, HC convicted appellants u/ss. 302, 325 r/w 149 and u/s. 148 IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, PW10 wife of deceased was a witness to the occurrence and if so she was able to see the assailants whose identity she knew even earlier. The incident happened at 4.00 p.m. and hence there can be no possible contention that there was paucity of light for PW10 to identify the assailants. The fact that PW10 has so many injuries would probabilise her version that when she found her husband in great danger being attacked by the assailants she threw herself into a vortex for rescuing her husband from being killed like that. Such an instinctive protective reaction on the part of a wife is a highly probable conduct in the given situation. That accounts for the large number of injuries which she sustained at the spot itself. Testimony of PW10 has been corroborated in all material particulars by the evidence of PW12 and PW13 to whom she had narrated the incident as soon as possible. Such evidence is admissible u/s. 157 of the Evidence Act. Hence, no interference on conviction and sentence by HC. Appeals dismissed.
