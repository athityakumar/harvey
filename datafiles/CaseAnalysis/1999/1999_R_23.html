T. N. Electricity Board v R. Veerasamy and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, Sujata V. Manohar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1034; (1999) 3 SCC 414; (1999) SCC (L&S) 717; AIR 1999 SC 1768; 1999 (2) ESC 1030; 1999 (82) FLR 174; JT 1999 (2) SC 429; 1999 LabIC 2086; 1999 (2) LLJ 783; 1999 (3) LLN 7; 1999(2) SCALE 276; 1999 (2) SCJ 428; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 221; 1999 (3) SLJ 163; 1999 (2) SLR 182; 1999 (3) SLT 206; 1999 (3) Supreme 289; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1180

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Employees' Deposit Linked Insurance

Summary: Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 79 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Pension scheme - Introduced prospectively from 1-7- 1986 - Legality - Employees retiring before 1 - Workmen, who had retired after receiving all benefits available under Contributory Provident Fund Scheme, cease to be employees of appellant - Board w.e.f. date of their retirement - Held, they form a separate class - Cannot compel Board to extend benefit of such scheme with retrospective effect - No illegality.
