Thyssen Stahlunion Gmbh v Steel Authority of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 25; (1999) 9 SCC 334; AIR 1999 SC 3923; 1999 (3) ARBLR 532; 2000 (1) CLC 139; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 383; JT 1999 (8) SC 66; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 25; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Curial Law, Different Intention, Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Arbitration Act, Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955, Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, New York Convention Awards (ENG), UNCITRAL Conciliation Rules

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 48 and 85(2)(a) - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 17 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, ss. 6 and 7 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937, s. 1 - (A) Whether enforcement of arbitral award, made under old Act, after coming into force of new Act, is possible under old Act ? Held, yes - Enforcement is under provisions of old Act as s. 85(2)(a) of new Act saves operation of old Act - It is not necessary that for right to accrue that legal proceedings must be pending when new Act comes into force - Court cannot adopt a construction which would lead to such anomalous situations were party seeking to have award set aside find himself without any remedy - It would be provisions of old Act that would apply to enforcement of award in such a case - (B) Whether agreement referred to under s. 85(2)(a) of new Act for purposes of applicability of new Act can be entered into even before new Act comes into force? - Parties can agree to applicability of new Act even before new Act comes into force and when old Act is still holding field - Nothing in language of s. 85(2)(a) which bars parties from so agreeing - But not where proceedings had not commenced - (C) Whether foreign award arbitral proceedings that had commenced in U.K. prior to commencement of new Act, but given after date of commencement of new Act of 1996 can be enforced under new Act of 1996 ? - Further held, ss. 85(2)(a) of new Act cannot save operation of Foreign Awards Act, 1961 - It is not possible to agree that s. 85(2)(a) of new Arbitration Act would keep that Act alive for purpose of enforcement of a foreign award given after date of commencement of new Act though arbitral proceedings in foreign land had commenced prior to that - Appeals disposed of.
