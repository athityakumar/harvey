Union of India v Ram Samujh and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 680; (1999) 9 SCC 429; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1522; 1999 (4) CCR 89; 1999 (4) Crimes 134; JT 1999 (6) SC 397; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 93; 1999(5) SCALE 276; 1999 (8) SLT 275; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 76; 1999 (7) Supreme 578

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Bail - Grant of - Principles - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 37 - Whether order passed by High Court granting bail to respondent No. 1 requires to be set aside on ground that HC ignored provisions of s. 37 of NDPS Act, 1985 as well as law laid down by Supreme Court? - Bail can be granted in case where there are reasonable grounds for believing that accused is not guilty of such offence and that he is not likely to commit any offence while on bail - Provision makes offences under Act cognizable and non-bailable - Limitations on granting of bail are in addition to limitations under CrPC, 1973 - In case, persons who are dealing in narcotic drugs are instruments in causing death or in inflicting death blow to number of innocent young victims, who are vulnerable; they would continue their nefarious activities of trafficking and/or dealing in intoxicants clandestinely as in Durand Didier v Chief Secretary, Union Territory of Goa, 1989 Indlaw SC 212 - Parliament has provided that person accused of offences under the NDPS Act, 1985 should not be released on bail during trial unless mandatory conditions provided in s. 37, namely (i) there are reasonable grounds for believing that accused is not guilty of such offence; and (ii) that he is not likely to commit while on bail are satisfied - Held, HC has not given any justifiable reason for not abiding by the aforesaid mandate while ordering the release of the respondent accused on bail - Appeal allowed.
