Sukhar v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 463; (1999) 9 SCC 507; (2000) SCC (Cr) 419; AIR 1999 SC 3883; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 52; 1999 (4) CCR 85; 1999 (4) Crimes 191; 2000 CRLJ 29; 1999 (2) JCC 606; JT 1999 (7) SC 537; 1999(6) SCALE 280; 1999 (8) SLT 270; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 314; 1999 (8) Supreme 568

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 161 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 6 and 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - (A) Statement given by injured to investigation officer and also FIR lodged by him - Admissibility as : (1) Evidence u/s. 33 of Evidence Act - (2) Dying declaration u/s. 32 of Evidence Act - (3) Rule of res gestae - Statement that on hearing sound of firing he went to scene and found injured lying on ground and he told him as to who had fired shot - (B) Validity of conviction of accused on unreliable and shaky evidence of witness - Held, not admissible - Is not admissible as dying declaration - Being part of same transaction, it is admissible u/s. 6 - Further held, cannot be sustained.
