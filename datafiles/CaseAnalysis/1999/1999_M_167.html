Mulavani Kannan and Another v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2047; (2002) 10 SCC 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian penal code, 1860, ss.302, 149 and 326 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Four appellants i.e., A1, A2, A4 and A5 who alone were convicted by the Trial Court out of thirteen persons arrayed before it - But Trial Court restricted the conviction to s.326 r/w. s.149 of IPC and sentenced each of them to rigorous imprisonment for five years - Four appellants as well as the State had filed appeals and the HC allowed the State appeal by escalating the offence to s.302 IPC as against A2 and A5 and sentenced them to imprisonment for life - Conviction and sentence passed on A1 and A4 were maintained without interference - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of A2 and A5 for murder was maintainable - Held, Trial Court declined to place reliance on the evidence of PW1 solely because the doctor who examined PW1 did not notice any external injury on his person - But the fact remained that PW1 was admitted in the hospital on complaint that he was subjected to beating - Perhaps no visible injury would have been sustained by deceased - Trial Court did not consider the implication of the first information statement which PW1 lodged with the authorities very soon after the occurrence - That statement had a great corroborative value so far as testimony of an eyewitness was concerned - SC agreed with the HC that the evidence of PW1, should not have been lightly sidelined - Testimony of PW2 normally went with PW 1 because both of them were standing at proximal distance between each other - A2 and A5 then adopted an alternative contention that the offence should not have been escalated to s.302 IPC - A2 and A5 invited SC's attention to the injuries described in the post-mortem certificates issued by the doctor who conducted the autopsy on the dead body of the deceased - SC agreed with the contention of A2 and A5 that none of the injuries could be termed as individually sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death - Doctor concerned also did not say that any of those injuries would be of that type - But those wounds were likely to cause death as blood vessels on the legs were cut - SC therefore agreed with A2 and A5 that the offence could not be a first-degree murder, but it could be altered to a second-degree murder i.e. s.304 Part I IPC - SC therefore altered the conviction as for A2 and A5 to the offence u/s.304 Part I IPC - Regarding A1 and A4, they were convicted for the offence u/s.326 IPC r/w. s.149 IPC by considering some of the injuries which could be termed as grievous hurt - Taking into account the fact that 17 years have lapsed thereafter, SC was inclined to reduce the sentence as for A1 and A4 to imprisonment for the period they had already undergone - Appeal disposed of.
