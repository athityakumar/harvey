Umapati Choudhary v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Sujata V. Manohar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 552; (1999) 4 SCC 659; (1999) SCC (L&S) 902; AIR 1999 SC 1948; 1999 (2) CLR 33; 1999 (2) ESC 1587; 1999 (82) FLR 797; JT 1999 (3) SC 627; 1999 LabIC 1979; 1999 (3) LLN 385; 1999(3) SCALE 687; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 752; 1999 (3) SLJ 304; 1999 (2) SLR 606; 1999 (5) SLT 483; 1999 (5) Supreme 422

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Bihar Sanskrit Education Board Act, 1982, s. 5 - Permanent absorption - Deputationist appellant deputed as Controller of Examinations to Education Board - In public interest - And with consent of parent department and borrowing department - University as parent department and Education Board as borrowing department - Sincerity of Deputationist considered by borrowing department for permanent absorption - Parent department consenting - Held, can be treated as employee of borrowing department.
