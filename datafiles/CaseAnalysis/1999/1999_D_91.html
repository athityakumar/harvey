Dr. Narayan Sharma and Another v Dr. Pankaj Kr. Lehkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 505; (2000) 1 SCC 44; AIR 2000 SC 72; 2000 (1) CLT 225; JT 1999 (8) SC 612; 1999(7) SCALE 96; 2000 (1) SCJ 315; 2000 (1) SLT 151; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 364; 1999 (9) Supreme 114

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Constitution - Assam Medical Colleges - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 166, 15(1), 15(4) and 29(2) - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, rr. 4, 5 and 7 - Assam Medical Colleges (Regulation of Admission to Post-Graduate Courses) Rules, 1994 - (A) To what extent is state competent to make special provision which could be u/art. 15(4)? - Held, interest of society at large would be served by promoting advancement of weaker sections in society, that art. 15(4) authorizes special provision to be made and that if a provision which is in nature of an exception completely excludes rest of society that is clearly outside scope of article - (B) Whether provisions contained in rr. 4(ii), 4(iii), 4(iv), 5(i) and 8(vii) of Assam Medical Colleges (Regulation of Admission to Post - Graduate Courses) Rules, 1994 are valid? - Further held, (1) Sub-rules (ii) and (iii) of r. 4 of rules are valid and constitutional;(2) Sub-rule (iv) is unconstitutional and void; (3) R. 5(i) is unconstitutional and not valid insofar as it exempts candidates referred to in sub- rules (ii) and (iv) of r. 4 from appearing in entrance examination; (4) R. 8(vii) and proviso are valid - Appeal allowed.
