Pon Adithan v Deputy Director, Narcotics Control Bureau, Madras

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 585; (1999) 6 SCC 1; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1051; AIR 1999 SC 2355; AIR 1999 SCW 2457; 1999 CRLJ 3663; JT 1999 (4) SC 540; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 897

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s. 313 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act,1985, ss. 8 (C ) ,21 , 50(1) - Held with heroin - Convicted - Sustainability - Appellant/accused was searched and having found 150 gms of heroin in his possession respondent arrested him and in course appellant had given confession statement agreeing crime - Prosecution had examined witnesses and Trial Court believing evidence of witnesses and confession statement of accused passed conviction Orders - Aggrieved appellant/accused filed appeal and was dismissed by HC holding that evidence of PW.1 was reliable and sufficient to sustain conviction of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, PW 1 had recorded confession statement only after informing accused of his rights and hence could be relied upon - Oral testimony of witnesses was corroborated by confession statement of accused - Accused plea that confession statement was not made voluntarily was not accepted by SC holding that accused had never complained of it to Magistrate and also did not inform what was nature of threat and pressure given to him but only on suggestion during trial for first time this plea was raised when recording of statement u/s. 313 of CrPC and hence could not be considered as genuine - SC upheld lower Courts finding that confessional statement was made voluntarily by appellant and therefore could be used against him - Chemical tests also proved material seized was heroin and hence Courts below had rightly convicted accused/appellant - Appeal dismissed.
