Sri Bhagwan Samardha Sreepada Vallabha Venkata Vishwanadadha Maharaj v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1656; (1999) 5 SCC 740; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1047; AIR 1999 SC 2332; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 248; 1999 CRLJ 3661; JT 1999 (4) SC 537; 1999 (3) RCR(Rent) 587; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 870

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 415 and 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1974, s. 173(8) - Cheating - Fraudulent Representation - Accused represented to have divine healing powers through his touches, particularly of chronic diseases - Complainant induced and gave money to accused but did not get desired result - Whether constitute offence of cheating? - Re-investigation - Whether a Magistrate can give order without giving reasonable opportunity of being heard? - Held, allegations of complainant about fraudulent activities constitute an offence of cheating - Can be ordered by Magistrate without affording opportunity of hearing to accused even after receipt of first report of police - Nothing in s. 173(8) to suggest that Court is obliged to hear accused before any such direction is made.
