Rajendra Prasad v Narcotic Cell Through Its Officer In Charge, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 865; (1999) 6 SCC 110; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1062; AIR 1999 SC 2292; 1999 (3) Crimes 106; 1999 CRLJ 3529; JT 1999 (4) SC 496; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Re-examination of prosecution witnesses - Cannot be permitted merely for filling up lacuna in prosecution evidence - (A) Whether mistakes or laches in conducting case by public prosecutor can be understood to mean lacuna in prosecution case? - (B) Whether Court can exercise power of re-summoning any witness if once that power was exercised? - Held, (A) An oversight in management of prosecution cannot be treated as irreparable lacuna - (B) If proper evidence was not adduced or a relevant material was not brought on record due to any inadvertence, power of Court to summon or even recall any witness at any stage of case is plenary - Appeal dismissed.
