State of Himachal Pradesh v Jeet Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 995; (1999) 4 SCC 370; (1999) SCC (Cr) 539; AIR 1999 SC 1293; 1999 (38) AllCriC 550; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 75; 1999 CRLJ 2025; JT 1999 (2) SC 99; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1033; 1999 (3) Supreme 230

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 309 and 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - (A) Whether death due to smothering or suicide? - Held, Doctors opinion that it could be of smothering even without referring to chemical examiners report - Injuries found on legs - High Court wrong in agreeing with cause of death as suicide - (B)Whether recovery of article from a place which is open and accessible to others would vitiate evidence based on incriminating article - Further held, no - There is nothing in s. 27 of Evidence Act which renders statement of Accused inadmissible if recovery of articles was made from any place which is 'open or accessible to others - Crucial question is whether it was ordinarily visible to others - Whether inculpatory statement of accused is admissible in evidence - Further held, yes - Statements given to police officer are admissible because section uses words - 'Whether it amounts to confession or not' - Whether motive of accused in commission of crime would be fatal to prosecution case? - Held, no, It is a sound principle to remember that every criminal act was done with a motive but its corollary is not that no criminal offence would have been committed if prosecution has failed to prove precise motive of accused to commit it - (D) Whether HC was right in acquitting accused of murder? - Further held, No, HC order set aside - Accused had opportunity to administer poison - Inference of doctors - Report of chemical analyser, that poison would have worked fatally in victim - Accused having opportunity to administer poison when he was in same room with deceased on fateful night - Appeal allowed.
