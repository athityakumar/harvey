H. D. Revanna v G. Puttaswamy Gowda and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 928; (1999) 2 SCC 217; AIR 1999 SC 768; JT 1999 (1) SC 126; 1999(1) SCALE 112; 1999 (2) SCJ 100; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 198; 1999 (1) SLT 245; 1999 (1) Supreme 183

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r.63 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.6, r.16, O.7, r.11 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss.81, 82, 83, 86, 117 - Recounting of votes - Undue influence - Violation of rules - Appellant was declared as elected for 133 Assembly Constituency in Karnataka State - Allegations in Election Petition filed by respondent no.1 were that after completion of counting by Returning Officer, request made by Appellant for recount was entertained by official was unduly influenced by father of appellant - Appellant filed applications for summary dismissal of Election Petition for non-compliance with certain statutory provisions which are mandatory - Applications filed by appellant dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether recounting ordered by Returning Officer was unduly influenced by father of appellant - Held, s.86 of Act provided for dismissal of election petition for non-compliance of ss.81, 82 and 117 of Act - Non-compliance of s.83 of Act did not lead to dismissal u/s.86 of Act - Non-compliance of s.83 of Act lead to dismissal of petition only if matter fell within scope of O.6, r.16 or O.7, r.11 of CPC - Respondent No.1 was leading by four votes and in order to make appellant to win election, recounting was ordered - In course of such recounting, several illegalities were committed - Large number of ballot papers were liable to be rejected were counted as valid votes in favour of appellant - Recounting ordered by Returning Officer was in violation of provisions of r.63 of Rules and Officer was unduly influenced by father of appellant - Appeals disposed of.
