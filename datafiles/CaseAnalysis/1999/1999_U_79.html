Ferro Alloys Corporation Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1020; (1999) 4 SCC 149; AIR 1999 SC 1236; 1999 (2) CLT 217; JT 1999 (2) SC 288; 1999(2) SCALE 576; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 49; 1999 (3) SLT 405; 1999 (3) Supreme 171

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 8(3) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Res judicata - Renewal of mining lease but confining it to only half area - Writ Petition filed by TISCO and High Court directing Central Govt. to reconsider - Sharma committee appointed - Personal hearing of rivals (FACOR also ) - Recommendations - Order confirmed by Supreme Court in SLP filed by TISCO - Writ petition filed by FACOR challenging order of Committee - Grounds, assessment of need of FACOR for chrome was under estimated - (A) If Barred - (B) Constructive res judicata - Plea of bar, when can be invoked inter se respondents - (C) Evidence - Estoppel and acquiescence - FACOR itself got assessment - Not challenge order of Court accepting report of Committee - Nor raised dispute inter se amongst other respondents - Challenging as to assessment - Held, not barred by principles of res judicata or constructive res judicata, as decision of HC in TISCO petition cannot be said to be an express decision on vexed question as to assessment of need of appellant (FACOR) - When plea was required to be raised by contesting Respondents to meet claim of appellant in such proceedings - Further held, by its own conduct waived dispute and acquiesced said assessment, Govt. acted upon its non contentious attitude and acted upon representation and FACOR estopped from challenging assessment - Order accordingly.
