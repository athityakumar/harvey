Ajit Chopra v Sadhu Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 487; (2000) 1 SCC 114; AIR 2000 SC 212; 1999 (37) ALR 794; 2000 (1) CLT 191; JT 1999 (8) SC 594; 2000 (124) PLR 19; 2000 (91) RD 85; 1999(7) SCALE 33; 2000 (1) SCJ 335; 1999 (9) SLT 378; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 60; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 340; 1999 (9) Supreme 297

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 11 and 47 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Suit for eviction decreed - Order remaining unexecuted - Sale of suit property - Vendee filing fresh suit for eviction - (A) Did limitation start against appellant (vendee) when he purchased from vendor or when Rent Appellate Authority in earlier case ordered eviction in favour of vendor? - (B) Does tenant continue to be so even after landlord's appeal from suit for eviction is allowed? - Held, second suit neither barred by s. 47 nor by s. 11 - Relationship as tenant continues throughout proceedings before Rent Controller, during pendency of appeal, till statutory revision under Act is disposed of - Relationship as tenant continues till eviction order is passed by appellate or statutory revisional authority and does not come to be in adverse possession - Defendant continued to be tenant vis-a-vis vendor and premises even after plaintiff's purchase - High Court erred in treating present suit as one 'virtually' for execution of order of eviction passed in earlier case - Ban u/s. 47 cannot apply - If suit for possession is decreed and decree-holder gets possession and thereafter there is fresh dispossession, fresh suit is maintainable for ejectment, because fresh trespass creates a fresh cause of action - Relied on Amina v. Ahmed Bin Chayabba (AIR 1949 Mad 815); Kutti Ali v. Chindan (ILR (1900) 23 Mad 629) - Appeal allowed.
