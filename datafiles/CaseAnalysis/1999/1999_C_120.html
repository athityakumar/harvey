Kiran Spinning Mills v Collector of Customs

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 74; (2000) 10 SCC 228; AIR 2000 SC 3448; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 753; 1999 (35) RLT 208

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s.3 - Additional Duty of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Ordinance, 1978 - Import of articles - Levy of additional duty - Appellants had imported acrylic polyster fiber - Imported articles were placed in bonded warehouse after they had landed in India - In terms of the Ordinance articles were charged with additional duty to excise under the Act - Articles which were imported by appellants were cleared from bonded warehouse after 4-10-1978 - Customs Authorities demanded additional duty at rate of 10 per cent under the Ordinance - Appellants paid amount demanded under protest and filed application for refund of amount - Appellants were unsuccessful before Authorities and Tribunal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant was liable to be levied of additional duty u/s. 3 of the Act - Held, taxable event occurred when customs barrier was crossed - In case of goods which were in warehouse customs barriers would be crossed when they were sought to be taken out of customs and brought to mass of goods in country - As on that day when goods were so removed additional duty of excise under the Ordinance was payable on goods manufactured after 4-10-1978 - Import would be completed only when goods were to cross customs barriers and that was time when import duty had to be paid and that was what had been termed - Taxable event being day of crossing of customs barrier and not on date when goods had landed in India or had entered territorial waters - Hence, on date of taxable event additional duty of excise was leviable under the Ordinance and additional duty u/s. 3 of the Act was rightly demanded from appellants - Appeal dismissed.
