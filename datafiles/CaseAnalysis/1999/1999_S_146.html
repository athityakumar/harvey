Shetty's Construction Company Private Limited v Konkan Railway Corporation Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1507; (1999) 8 SCC 604; AIR 2000 SC 122; 1999 (3) ARBLR 676; JT 1999 (8) SC 177

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940 ss. 8, 20 - Direction for appointment of arbitrators - Validity - Appellant/contractor entered into 4 different contracts with respondent/Corporation in connection with laying of broad-gauge railway line - All 4 contracts were terminated by respondent authorities - After termination of contracts and handing over incomplete work of contracts by respondent authorities to other contractors, appellant contractor raised certain disputes arising out of working of said contracts - Appellant contractor demanded reference to arbitration pursuant to terms of contractual agreement, respondent authorities did not comply with said demand and, on contrary, wrongly rejected claims put forward by appellant contractor - Appellant contractor filed before HC of Bombay u/s. 8 r/w s. 20 of the 1940 Act - Single Judge directed respondent authorities to comply with procedure of appointment of arbitrators as per contracts agreed to between parties - However, HC did not accept further prayer of appellant that arbitration should be entrusted to independent arbitrators - Directions were accepted by respondent authorities by not challenging said orders partially granting prayers of appellant - However, appellant contractor in search of appointment of independent arbitrators filed original side appeals before DB - DB confirmed directions of HC and appeals were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned directions of HC were justified - Held, it could not be said that respondent authorities had failed to carry out their contractual obligations of appointing arbitrators under agreement even though validly called upon by appellant contractor to do so and therefore had missed bus and consequently the Court could appoint independent arbitrators, if it thought fit - Consequently, it should be held that in instant 4 cases, HC was perfectly justified in relegating both parties to procedure of arbitration as laid down under contracts binding on them - Therefore, final orders passed by Single Judge in all 4 suits and as confirmed by DB remain well sustained on record - However, SC noted that rightly or wrongly appellant contractor had simmering grievance since 1992 when his 4 contracts were terminated giving rise to diverse claims which though rejected by respondent authorities, were required to be adjudicated upon by arbitrators as envisaged by parties under contracts - As years had rolled by, it would be appropriate to direct respondent authorities to comply with orders passed by Single Judge as confirmed by DB and also by SC at earliest - Procedure laid down by cl. 63.3.2 r/w cl. 63.3(b) should be carried out by respondent authorities within 4 weeks of receipt of copy of this order at their end and thereafter appellant contractor should also carry out his obligations under relevant clauses and 2 arbitrators to be appointed by Chairman-cum-Managing Directors see to it that arbitration proceedings were completed at earliest and not later than 4 months from date on which they enter upon reference -

Thereafter, if there was any dispute between them on any point or points, then umpire might be appointed by them within 4 weeks of emergence of such dispute - Umpire so appointed should carry out his exercise as envisaged by relevant clauses of agreement within period of 2 months of being seized of matter - Thus, latest within 8 months or so, both sides might come to know where they stood in connection with disputes in question - Subject to aforesaid directions, all these 4 appeals stood dismissed -

Appeals disposed of.
