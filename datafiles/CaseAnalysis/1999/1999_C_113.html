Steel Authority of India v Collector of Customs, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 59; (2001) 9 SCC 198; 2000 (88) ECR 764; 2000 (115) E.L.T. 42; 2000 (36) RLT 369; 2000 (1) SLT 319

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Benefit of an exemption notification - Said notification gave such exemption to refractory bricks and there was a trade notice issued by Bombay Customs House for information and guidance of importers, clearance agents - Tribunal declined to take trade notice into account on ground that it could not enlarge scope of said notification - Whether Tribunal is bound by trade notices? - Held, Supreme Court has consistently held that authorities are bound by trade notices that they issue and cannot argue to contrary - It was, therefore, not open to revenue to contend that for said notification did not exempt refractory blocks - The trade notice issued by one Customs House must bind all Customs authorities and, if it is erroneous, it should be withdrawn or amended, which in instant case, admittedly, has not been done - Appellants entitled to refund - Appeal allowed.
