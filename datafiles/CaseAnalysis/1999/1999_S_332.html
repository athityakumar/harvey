M. S. Ahlawat v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 794; (2000) SCC (Cr) 193; (2000) 1 SCC 278; AIR 2000 SC 168; 2000 (2) CalLT(SC) 12; 1999 (4) CCR 235; 2000 CRLJ 388; 2000 (1) JCC 40; JT 1999 (8) SC 530; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 718; 1999(6) SCALE 648; 2000 (1) SCJ 402; 1999 (9) SLT 149; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 160; 1999 (9) Supreme 21

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973,ss.195 - IndianPenalCode,1860,ss.193,340- Constitution of India, 1950,Arts.129,142 - Conviction - Jurisdiction Challenged - Validity - Acting on report of petitioner that his signature was forged in affidavits SC directed detailed inquiry to be made by District Judge (DJ) and DJ submitted enquiry report holding that petitioner was not responsible - Based on that report SC directed CBI enquiry and CBI submitted its report - SC acting on CBI report issued notices to head constable SI ASI and petitioner - Petitioner was convicted by SC for offences of forgery and contempt of Court u/s. 193 IPC and u/art. 129 of Constitution - Review petition filed by petitioner was dismissed summarily - Petitioner had undergone sentences - Instant writ filed by petitioner challenging his conviction u/s. 193 IPC only - Whether writ petition was maintainable - Held, chapter XI of IPC deals with false evidence u/s. 193 IPC and offences related to documents used in court and only court to which offence was committed may initiate proceedings - Provisions of s. 195 Cr.P.C. were mandatory and no court had jurisdiction to take cognizance offences mentioned therein without complaint in writing - Settled law was that every incorrect or false statement does not make it incumbent upon court to order prosecution but to exercise judicial discretion in larger interest of administration of justice - SC in Supreme Court Bar Association v. Union of India & Anr,1998 Indlaw SC 688 held that u/art. 142 of Constitution SC could not altogether ignore substantive provisions of statute and pass orders concerning issue which could be settled only through mechanism prescribed in another statute - SC in Mohan Singh v. Late Amar Singh through L.Rs, 1998 Indlaw SC 616 dealing with forged document filed in Court directed Registrar of SC to file complaint before appropriate court and set criminal law in motion - SC could not have assumed jurisdiction by issuing notice proposing conviction for forgery punishable u/s. 193 IPC without following procedure prescribed u/ss. 195 and 340 Cr.P.C - Primarily SC do not exercise any original criminal jurisdiction in relation to offences arising u/s. 193 IPC and secondly seriousness of the charge arising u/s. 193 IPC requires elaborate inquiry and trial by competent criminal court - Summary inquiry by merely issuing show cause notice and considering affidavits or inquiry reports would not tantamount to procedure provided under Cr.P.C - Order made by SC convicting petitioner u/s. 193 IPC was therefore without jurisdiction and without following due procedure prescribed under law - To perpetuate error was no virtue but to correct it was compulsion of judicial conscience - No proceedings were directed u/s.340 Cr.P.C. as petitioner had already undergone sentence imposed - Petition allowed.
