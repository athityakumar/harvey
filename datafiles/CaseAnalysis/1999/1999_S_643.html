Commissioner Of Income Tax, Karnataka v Sterling Foods, Mangalore

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1201; (1999) 4 SCC 98; AIR 1999 SC 2036; 1999 (153) CTR 439; 1999 (83) ECR 481; 1999 (108) E.L.T. 3; [1999] 237 ITR 579; JT 1999 (3) SC 227; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 699; [1999] 104 TAXMAN 204; 1999 (2) TLR 647

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80HH - Whether assessee is entitled to deduction under s. 80HH? - Assessee has a business of processing and exporting sea food - Import entitlements granted to him under export promotion scheme of Govt. - Earning from sale of import entitlements - No direct nexus with business of assessee - Sale consideration therefrom cannot create profit and gain derived from assessee's industrial undertaking - Held, assessee deriving income from sale of import entitlements which has no connection with his main business of processing and exporting sea food is not entitled to deduction under said provision.
