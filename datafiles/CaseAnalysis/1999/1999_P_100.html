State of Kerala and another v Puttumana Illath Jathavedan Namboodiri

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 429; (1999) SCC (Cr) 275; (1999) 2 SCC 452; AIR 1999 SC 981; 1999 (1) CCR 92; 1999 (1) Crimes 62; 1999 CRLJ 1443; 1999 (1) JCC 119; JT 1999 (1) SC 456; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 808; 1999(1) SCALE 418; 1999 (1) SCJ 431; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 575; 1999 (2) SLT 83; 1999 (1) Supreme 461

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 401 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 408,468 and 477 A - Powers of High Court - Whether HC has power of revision against conviction? - Accused alleged to have manipulated official record and misappropriated amount - Held, conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court is proper, while interference by HC with conviction of accused by re - appreciating oral evidence is not proper.
