A. P. Aggarwal v Government of Nct of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 830; (2000) SCC (L&S) 206; (2000) 1 SCC 600; AIR 2000 SC 205; JT 1999 (9) SC 125; 2000 (1) LLN 8; 2000 (2) SLJ 123; 2000 (1) SLR 382; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 443

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Delhi Sales Tax Act, 1975, ss. 13 and 13(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appointment - Rejection of name - Whether rejection of appellant's name for appointment as Member, Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal without reason is arbitrary and unconstitutional? - Held, appellant is only other person in panel of names selected by Select Committee and nothing has been brought out against him by Govt. - Office memorandum was issued with a view to fill up vacancy as soon as practicable - All conditions set out in office memorandum issued by Central Govt. D/- 14-05-1987 are fulfilled - Rejection of appellant's name without any reason is arbitrary and unconstitutional - Initiation of fresh process of selection is not valid and it is hereby quashed - Appeal allowed.
