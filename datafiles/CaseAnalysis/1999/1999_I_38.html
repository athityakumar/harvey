Umar Abdul Sakoor Sorathia v Intelligence Officer, Narcotic Control Bureau

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 561; (2000) 1 SCC 138; (2000) SCC (Cr) 200; AIR 1999 SC 2562; 1999 (37) ALR 100; 1999 (3) CCR 173; 1999 (3) Crimes 204; 1999 CRLJ 3972; 1999 (66) ECC 8; 1999 (84) ECR 291; 1999 (2) JCC 388; JT 1999 (5) SC 394; 1999 (3) RCR(Criminal) 658; 1999(4) SCALE 497; 1999 (6) SLT 411; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 113; 1999 (6) Supreme 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Probable Consequence

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 23 and 29 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Investigation - Seizure of contraband in foreign country - Communication by foreign police stating that 'no material has been thus far collected to connect appellant with contraband consignment' - Whether such view of foreign police can foreclose investigating force in India from arriving at right conclusion? - (B) Identification by photo during Investigation - Whether admissible in evidence? - Whether at stage of framing of charge Court can take into account fact that during investigation witness identified person in photo as one whom he saw at relevant time? - (C) Framing of charge - Seizure of contraband - Accused, a diplomatic outfit earlier - Investigation revealing that accused conspired with other four accused already charge sheeted - Accused was alleged to be Chairman of clearing agency company which took possession of consignment - Whether Court can frame charge against accused u/s. 29 r/w s. 23? - Held, (A) view of foreign police cannot foreclose investigating force in India from arriving at right conclusion nor even police authorities in those countries themselves from taking different view subsequently - (B) Identification by photo is inadmissible as substantive evidence - At stage of framing of charge, Court is not disabled from considering prospect of such a witness correctly identifying appellant during trial - (C) It cannot be said that in circumstance Court cannot frame charge against accused u/s. 29 r/w s. 23 - Appeal dismissed.
