Balraj Taneja and Another v Sunil Madan and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 619; (1999) 8 SCC 396; AIR 1999 SC 3381; 1999 (6) ALD(SC) 21; 2000 (40) ALR 494; 1999 (81) DLT 779; JT 1999 (6) SC 473; 1999 (90) RD 600; 1999(5) SCALE 400; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 2(9), O. 8, r. 10, O. 40, r. 4(2) - (A) when can the suit be decreed - Written statement not filed - Serious disputed question of fact involved - Two different versions are set out in plaint itself - Whether suit decreed merely on grounds of failure of defendants in filing written statement legal - (B) 'Judgment' - What constitutes - If definition of judgment u/s. 2(9) of Code - Not applicable to judgment passed by Delhi High Court in its original jurisdiction wherein proceedings are regulated by Delhi HC Act, whether it is absolved of its obligation to write a judgment - Held, (A) It is a matter of court's satisfaction that there is no fact which need be proved on account of deemed admission - Suit decreed merely on grounds of failure in filing written statement illegal - (B) Judgment means process of reasoning by which a judge decides a case in favor of one party and against other - Delhi HC is not absolved of its obligation to write a judgment as understood in common parlance - Appeal allowed.
