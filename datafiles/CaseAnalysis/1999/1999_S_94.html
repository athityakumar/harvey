New India Assurance Company v Satpal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 493; (2000) SCC (Cr) 130; (2000) 1 SCC 237; 2000 (1) ACC 1; 2000 (1) ACC 573; 2000 ACJ 1; AIR 2000 SC 235; AIR 1999 SCW 4337; 2000 (1) AWC 454; 2000 (1) BLJR 1; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 258; JT 1999 (9) SC 416; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 115; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 340; 2000 (1) MPLJ 339; 2000 (124) PLR 464; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 274; 1999(7) SCALE 300; 1999 (10) SLT 175; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 149; 1999 (10) Supreme 87

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 95(1) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether deceased was a gratuitous passenger in truck and hence no liability can be fastened with insurer? - Held, as per proviso to s. 95(1) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 (the old Act) when r/w its cl. (ii) it is clear that policy of insurance shall not be required to cover liability in respect of death of or bodily injury to persons who were gratuitous passengers of that vehicle - As u/s. 95 risk to a passenger in a vehicle who is not carried for hire or reward is not required to be insured, plea of counsel for insurance Co. will have to be accepted and insurance Co. held not liable under requirements of Motor Vehicles Act - U/s. 147 of new Act, policy must be a policy which insures person or classes of persons specified in policy to extent specified in sub-section (2) - Under new Act an insurance policy covering third-party risk is not required to exclude gratuitous passengers in a vehicle, no matter that vehicle is of any type or class - Decisions rendered under old Act vis-a-vis gratuitous passengers are of no avail while considering liability of insurance Co. in respect of any accident which occurred or would occur after new Act came into force - Appeal dismissed.
