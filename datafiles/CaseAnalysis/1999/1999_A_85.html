Bharat Barrel and Drum Manufacturing Company v Amin Chand Payrelal

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 966; (1999) 3 SCC 35; AIR 1999 SC 1008; 1999 (36) ALR 7; 1999 (2) AWC 1266; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 1; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 10; [1999] 97 Comp Cas 786; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 118 - Promissory note - Presumption as to consideration - Burden of proof - Promissory note allegedly executed as a collateral security and not for 'value received' as mentioned therein - Defendant failing to prove non-existence of consideration - Held, onus cannot be shifted on plaintiff - Claim made by plaintiff has to be allowed even if evidence adduced by plaintiff is found to be unbelievable in rebuttal of defendant's case.
