State of Andhra Pradesh v K. V. L. Narasimha Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 353; (1999) 4 SCC 181; (1999) SCC (L&S) 841; AIR 1999 SC 2255; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 49; JT 1999 (3) SC 205; 1999 (3) LLN 16; 1999 (2) SLR 352

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14, 16 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Hyderabad Civil Services Regulations rr. 26(aa), 40(aa) - Seniority list - Service rules - Applicability - Respondents were originally appointed as Munsif Magistrates in erstwhile state of Hyderabad and after formation of state of Andhra Pradesh their services were intergraded with state of AP - Certain litigations arose as to norms to be adopted for fixing seniority and ultimately HC and concerned officers did not actually work in higher post and they had no right to claim monetary benefits - HC in writ petition concluded that provisions of Fundamental rr. 26 or 40 of the Regulations had no application to cases falling within ambit of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Rules were applicable to respondents - Held, wrong was committed in unduly dealing finalsation of seniority and giving promotions thereto and hence denial of monetary benefits to them would be arbitrary in violation of arts. 14, 16 of Constitution - Further, as matter of fact, rules of erstwhile State regarding seniority were not applicable in new state as allottees were governed by the Act and seniority was finalised therein, even so, SC did not see that there was any impediment to frame new rules affecting conditions of service of such allottees but in conformity with the Act - Thus, surely new rules could not be brushed aside by saying that they were not applicable to cases coming under the Act, there was no contention either in HC or before SC that they were formed in contravention of the Act - SC failed to see as to why the Rules were not applicable to respondents as held by HC - Appeal allowed.
