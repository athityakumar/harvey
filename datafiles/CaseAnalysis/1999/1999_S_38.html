S. Hanumantha Rao v S. Ramani

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1042; (1999) 3 SCC 620; AIR 1999 SC 1318; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 16; 1999 (2) AWC 1502; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 85; 1999 (3) CalLT(SC) 8; 1999 (3) CLT 54; 1999 (1) DMC 628; JT 1999 (2) SC 451; 1999(2) SCALE 338; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 296; 1999 (3) SLT 318; 1999 (3) Supreme 358

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1)(ia) - Hindu Marriage - If following amounts to mental cruelty - (A) Removal of Mangalsutra by wife at instance of her husband - (B) No reply received of letters sent to husband - She preserved copies without any intention to use as evidence - Husband, filed all letters in court - Retention of letters by wife - (C) Representation made by parents of wife to Women's Protection Cell for reconciliation - Held, none of circumstances amounted to mental cruelty on husband - Particularly in absence of evidence to show that neither husband nor his family were harassed by Cell - Appeal dismissed.
