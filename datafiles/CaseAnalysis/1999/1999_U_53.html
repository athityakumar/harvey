Union of India and Others v Himmat Singh Chahar

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 863; (1999) 4 SCC 521; (1999) SCC (Cr) 596; AIR 1999 SC 1980; 1999 CRLJ 2894; JT 1999 (3) SC 631; 1999 (3) LLN 421; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service; Water

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Navy Act, 1957, s. 77(a) - Whether High Court in exercising its power of judicial review crosses jurisdiction when it re appreciates evidence and substitutes finding of guilt recorded by authority in Court martial proceedings - Held, HC does overstep its jurisdiction, when it reappreciates evidence and gives a finding other than that given by Competent Authority in Court martial proceedings.
