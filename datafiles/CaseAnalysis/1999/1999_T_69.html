Ram Kumar Agarwal and Another v Thawar Das (Dead) Through Lrs

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 658; (1999) 7 SCC 303; AIR 1999 SC 3248; 1999 (37) ALR 230; 1999 (4) AWC 3341; JT 1999 (6) SC 179; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 63; 2000 (Supp) RD 526; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, s. 53-A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Specific performance of contract - Willingness to perform - Interference by High Court - Legality of - Whether judgment and decree passed by HC is sustainable in law? - Judgment of HC suffers from serious infirmities - It also suffers from vice of exercise of such jurisdiction as did not vest in HC under law - U/s. 100 CPC, 1908 (as amended in 1976) jurisdiction of HC to interfere with judgments of courts below is confined to hearing on substantial questions of law - Interference with finding of fact by HC is not warranted if it involves reappreciation of evidence - HC did not frame any substantial questions of law as contemplated by sub-section (5) of s. 100 CPC, 1908 - It has not even discussed any evidence - No basic finding of fact recorded by courts below has been reversed much less any reason assigned for taking a view to contrary, still finding on question of readiness and willingness to perform contract which is a mixed question of law and fact has been upset - Plea u/s. 53-A of Transfer of Property Act which again involves a mixed question of law and fact has been allowed to be urged and upheld by HC - Judgment of HC wholly unsustainable in law - Judgment and decree passed by HC are set aside and instead judgment and decree passed by Trial court and as affirmed by District Judge are restored - Appeals allowed.
