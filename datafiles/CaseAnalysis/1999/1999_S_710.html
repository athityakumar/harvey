Sudershan Devi and Another v Sushila Devi and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 756; (1999) 8 SCC 31; AIR 1999 SC 3688; 1999 ALJ 2394; 1999 (37) ALR 496; 1999 (4) AWC 3484; JT 1999 (7) SC 500; 1999 (10) SLT 93; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction Act, 1972, s. 20(4) - Deposit of arrears of rent at first hearing of case - Expression hearing in group of words as per explanation - Due date of deposit of arrears of rent - Summons served by method of substituted service - Not accompanied by copy of plaint - Tenant filed application seeking copy of plaint - Application allowed - Fresh date of filing written statement and fresh date for first hearing given - Held, word hearing means date on which Court proposes to apply its mind to determine points in controversy and to frame issue, if necessary - And, not date fixed for filing written statement - Fresh date for first hearing so given and not initial date fixed in summons, is due date for deposit of arrears of rent.
