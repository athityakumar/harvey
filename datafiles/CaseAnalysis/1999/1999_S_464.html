 Mathuram Agrawal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1295; (1999) 8 SCC 667; AIR 2000 SC 109; JT 1999 (8) SC 505; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 71; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, ss. 127A(1), 127A(2) and 127A(2)(b) - Levy and collection of property tax in respect of buildings owned by appellant - (A) When several items of properties (houses, buildings or lands) within municipality, annual letting value of each of which does not exceed Rs. 1800 per annum, are owned by one person, then, is owner liable to pay property tax for such properties? - Held, on a fair reading of proviso to s. 127-A(2)(b) it is clear that in respect of any building or land whose letting value is less than Rs. 1800 which is owned by a person who owns any other building or land in same municipality, annual letting value of such building or land shall be deemed to be aggregate annual letting value of all buildings or lands owned by him in municipality - Provision also makes it clear that this exception is meant for purpose of cl. (b) of sub-s. (2) - It follows, therefore, that exemption to levy u/sub-s. (1) of s. 127A will not be available in a situation to which proviso applies - (B) Whether sub-s. (1) and proviso to sub-s. (2)(b) should be construed together and annual letting values of all buildings owned by a person to be taken together for determining amount to be paid as tax in respect of each building? - Held, construction placed on said provision does not flow from plain language of provision - Proviso requires exempted property to be subjected to tax and for purpose of valuing that property alone value of other properties is to be taken into consideration - But, if in doing so, said property becomes taxable, Act does not provide at what rate it would be taxable - One cannot determine rateable value of small property by aggregating and adding value of other properties, and arrive at a figure which is more than possibly value of property itself - Moreover, what rate of tax is to be applied to such a property is also not indicated - Further held, proviso to cl. (b) of sub-s. (2) of s. 127-A of Act being contrary to charging section is struck down as ultra vires - Appeal allowed.
