R. S. Saini v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 692; (1999) 8 SCC 90; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1424; AIR 1999 SC 3579; JT 1999 (6) SC 507; 1999 (2) LLJ 1415; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 173

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, s. 22 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133, 226 and 14 - Appeal - Consideration - Appeal against order of disciplinary authority removing President of Municipal Council - Findings of disciplinary authority - Scope - Natural justice - Violation - President of Municipal Council was removed from office by Disciplinary authority - Guilty of charges framed against him - Enquiring authority has based its conclusions on materials available on record - Held, findings assailed on ground of malice and violation of natural justice rule - Considering fact that order impugned removes an elected office bearer from office otherwise than by process of election and nature of grounds raised, Court considered facts elaborately.
