Ravinder Kumar Sharma v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 700; (1999) 7 SCC 435; AIR 1999 SC 3571; AIR 1999 SCW 3578; 1999 (6) ALD(SC) 38; 1999 (37) ALR 453; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; 1999 (81) DLT 795; JT 1999 (6) SC 565; 2000 (125) PLR 165; 1999(5) SCALE 467; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 86; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic; Tort

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement, Abuse Of Power, Malice

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 22 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 81 - Arrest of appellants - Validity - Grounds based on newspaper reporting that movement of control order is withdrawn - Wireless message issued by State Govt. to all Deputy Commr. and SDOs denying newspaper reporting - Police Officer acting upon wireless message - Suit for damages for malicious prosecution, abuse of power and unauthorised action - Held, not without reasonable or probable cause - Newspaper report cannot be treated as proof of facts - Respondent can question adverse finding without filing cross-objection - Filing of cross - objection is optional and not mandatory.
