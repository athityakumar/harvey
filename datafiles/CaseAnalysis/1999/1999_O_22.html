Ashok Gangadhar Maratha v Oriental Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 202; (1999) 6 SCC 620; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1170; 2000 ACJ 319; AIR 1999 SC 3181; 1999 (4) AWC 2791; 2000 (2) Bom.C.R. 149; 1999 (4) CompLJ 125; 1999 (3) CPJ 5; JT 1999 (6) SC 423; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 105; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 437; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 202

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2(21), 2(14) and 147 - Determination of liability of insurer in regard to accident of vehicle weighing 5920 kilos for plying which permit, neither pleaded nor produced - Irrespective of design which cannot be held as transport vehicle u/s. 66 of Act - No effective valid licence amounting to breach of policy - Held, insurer cannot escape liability on ground of breach of policy by insured - Appeal allowed.
