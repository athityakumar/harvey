Coronation Spinning India v Commissioner of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 149; 1999 (66) ECC 288; 1999 (85) ECR 832; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Limitation - Dismissal of appeal - Appeal of appellant before CEGAT was dismissed without any adjudication on merits as being barred by time by delay of 86 days - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant claims that other appeals raising an identical issue have been heard and dated merits in favour of appellant and it is only this appeal which has been dismissed on ground of limitation resulting into gross injustice to appellant. Having heard the parties, Court opines that Tribunal ought to have heard and disposed of appeal on merits condoning the delay in filing appeal. Appeal is allowed and impugned order is set aside. Tribunal shall hear and dispose of appeal on merits. Appeal allowed.
