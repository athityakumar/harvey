Sampuran Singh and Others v Niranjan Kaur Smt. and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 974; (1999) 2 SCC 679; AIR 1999 SC 1047; 1999 (35) ALR 557; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 49; 1999 (2) CLT 49; JT 1999 (2) SC 14; 1999(1) SCALE 607; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 841; 1999 (2) SLT 317; 2000 (Supp) RD 367; 1999 (2) Supreme 210

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 18 and 61 - Acknowledgment - When to be made? - Oral mortgage in year 1893 - Filing of suit for redemption in year 1980 - Limitation period - Computation of - Held, acknowledgement should be made during subsistence of period of limitation - Acknowledgement of liability after expiry of limitation period for filing suit - Does not revive Period of limitation - Limitation period starts from very first date of valid mortgage, unless any restrain in mortgage deed is provided - Hence, period of limitation would start from very date valid mortgage is said to have been executed - Period of limitation of 60 years would start from very date of oral mortgage that would be from March, 1893.
