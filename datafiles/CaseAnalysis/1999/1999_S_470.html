Kalema Tumba v State of Maharashtra and Anr

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 125; (1999) 8 SCC 257; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1422; AIR 2000 SC 402; AIR 1999 SCW 4544; 1999 (37) ALR 645; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 545; 1999 (3) CalLT(SC) 55; 2000 CRLJ 507; 2000 (88) ECR 766; 2000 (115) E.L.T. 38; JT 1999 (8) SC 293; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 670

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108, 135(i)(a) and 135(i)(ii) - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c), 21, 23, 28 and 50 - (A) Whether evidence would become inadmissible because appellant was not told, before search by officers of Narcotic Control Bureau that he had a right to be searched in presence of a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate? - Held, only when a person of an accused is to be searched that he is required to be informed about his right to be examined in presence of a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate - (B) Whether Chemical Analyser rejected because it was a cryptic report without any details of test? - Further held, appellant had himself admitted in his statement u/s. 108 of Customs Act that it was heroin - Officers of Narcotic Control Bureau also had tested substance found from appellant - (C) Whether reliance should have been placed upon statement recorded u/s. 108 of Customs Act as it was not made by appellant voluntarily and he did not know what was written in it when he had signed it as he did not know English? - Further held, evidence does not support contention that appellant did not know English or that statement was not voluntary - Appeal dismissed.
