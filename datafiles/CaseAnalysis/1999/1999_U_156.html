State of Haryana v Unique Farmaid Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1366; (1999) 8 SCC 190; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1404; AIR 2000 SCW 1985; 2000 CRLJ 2962; JT 1999 (8) SC 162; 2000 (124) PLR 290; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Insecticides Act, 1968, ss. 21, 22, 24 and 29 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Sample of insecticides were misbranded - High Court quashed proceedings against persons on ground that day on which accused persons were summoned they had lost their right of getting samples re-analysed - Present appeal - Respondents have been deprived of their valuable right to have sample tested from Central Insecticides Laboratory u/s. 24(4) of Act, 1968 - Held, respondents have been deprived of that right, thus, prejudicing them in their defence - HC right in quashing proceedings as it would have amounted to abuse of process of law - HC order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
