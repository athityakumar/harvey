Hindustan Construction Company Limited and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 29; (1999) 8 SCC 436; AIR 1999 SC 3710; 1999 (3) ARBLR 510; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 297; JT 1999 (8) SC 142; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: misa

Summary: Banking - Practice & Procedure - Petitioner furnished a bank guarantees to the respondent - Respondent invoked the bank guarantees as the petitioner failed to complete the contract - High Court restrained the respondent from invoking the bank guarantee - Whether the bank was justified in invoking the bank guarantee - Held, no - Respondents were not possessed of sufficient funds for completion of the work - No lapse on part of the respondents - Bank guarantee constitutes a separate, distinct and independent contract - Bank guarantee is independent of the contract between petitioners and respondents - Disposed accordingly.
