Ashwani Kumar Mishra v P. Muniam Babu and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1180; (1999) 4 SCC 22; (1999) SCC (Cr) 502; 1999 (5) ACC 505; AIR 1999 SC 2260; 1999 (2) AWC 1526; 1999 (3) CLT 6; JT 1999 (2) SC 619; 1999(2) SCALE 479; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 518; 1999 (3) SLT 447; 1999 (4) Supreme 24

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Accidental claim - Quantum of compensation - Appellant, 23 years of age, met with an accident and received severe injuries causing damages to his spinal cord - Appellant remained under treatment for about 90 days and became permanently disabled - Appellant claimed Rs. 63,00,9i19.15 from the owner, driver and the insurer of the vehicle for injuries suffered by him in the motor accident - Motor Accident Claim Tribunal (Tribunal) held that the appellant was travelling as an agent of the construction firm when he met with the accident and awarded him a compensation of Rs. 1,64,037 - Appeals filed thereagainst were disposed by the impugned judgment holding the appellant entitled to Rs. 2.25.000 as compensation payable with interest at the rate of 12 per cent per annum - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the appellant has been awarded Rs. 94,037 as expenses incurred on medical care, Rs. 20,000 for special diet and expenses for attendant during treatment. For his becoming permanently disabled and paraplegic on account of the injury and damages caused to his spinal cord, the appellant, who admittedly has been held permanently incapacitated has been granted only Rs. 1,00,000. The appellant was right in claiming his income at Rs, 2,000 per month while working with his father at the time of the accident and even if we apply the multiplier of 16, he is entitled to the claim of Rs. 3,84,000 on account of loss of expectation to life besides disappointment, frustration and mental stress particularly when he has to keep a permanent attendant to look after him in his rest of life. The appellant is entitled to a total sum of Rs. 4,98,000 which is round up to Rs. 5,00,000 inclusive of costs of litigation. Appeal allowed.
