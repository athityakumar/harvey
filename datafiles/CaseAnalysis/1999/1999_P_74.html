Sardul Singh v Pritam Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1301; (1999) SCC (Cr) 445; (1999) 3 SCC 522; AIR 1999 SC 1704; 1999 (36) ALR 1; 1999 (2) CLT 141; JT 1999 (2) SC 498; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 76; 1999(2) SCALE 343; 1999 (2) SCJ 6; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 22; 1999 (3) SLT 325; 2000 (Supp) RD 283; 1999 (3) Supreme 364; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1262

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961 - Professional misconduct - Suspension - Practice Continued - Admonition Order - Bar Council of Delhi hold that first respondent was guilty of professional misconduct and suspended him from practice for a period of three years - Appellant filed a complaint before Delhi Bar Council that notwithstanding order of suspension - First respondent continued to practice in violation of order of Bar Council of India and even after issue of notification, he had committed fresh misconduct, which was liable to be punished - Bar Council of Delhi passed an order holding that, respondent continued practice during period of suspension and he had concealed his earlier conviction by a criminal court for an offence under Act and was sentenced to six months imprisonment - First respondent filed review application u/s. 44 of Act before Bar Council of India, which restored order of admonition passed by Delhi Bar Council - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent continued practice during period of suspension and restoration of order of admonition passed by Delhi Bar Council was maintainable - Held, respondent was not permissible to continue to practice during period of prohibition - Respondent had been practicing under a different name, could not be viewed lightly and guilty could not be let off hook with warning and reprimand - Hence, Court allow appeal setting aside order of Bar Council of Delhi and respondent was suspended from practice for a further period of 3 years, as misconduct of respondent was very grave - Bar Council of India had not given enough seriousness, regarding misconduct of violating order and practicing in a different name throughout relevant period - Court directed for a fresh order of prohibition from practicing of respondent for further three years - Appeal allowed.
