Capt.M. Paul Anthony .. v Bharat Gold Mines Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 346; (1999) 3 SCC 679; (1999) SCC (L&S) 810; AIR 1999 SC 1416; AIR 1999 SCW 1098; 1999 (2) AWC 1579; 1999 (1) CLR 103; 1999 (1) CLR 1032; 1999 (2) ESC 1009; 1999 (82) FLR 627; JT 1999 (2) SC 456; 1999 (3) KarLJ 472; 1999 (2) KLT 17; 1999 LabIC 1565; 1999 (1) LLJ 1094; 1999 (2) LLN 640; 1999(2) SCALE 363; 1999 (2) SCJ 358; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 257; 1999 (3) SLJ 152; 1999 (2) SLR 338; 1999 (3) SLT 381; 1999 (3) Supreme 376; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1280

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 21, 309 - Misconduct - Suspension - Reinstatement - Maintainability - Appellant was appointed as security officer with respondent/employer - Superintendent of Police raided residential premises of appellant and had recovered mining sponge gold ball of 'gold bearing sand' and criminal case was launched against accused - On same set of facts departmental proceedings were initiated against appellant as 'recovery' was treated to be 'misconduct' - Accused was ultimately found guilty of charges and was dismissed from service - Subsequently, judgment in criminal case was pronounced and appellant was acquitted - This judgment was communicated by appellant to respondents with request that he might be reinstated, but his request was rejected - Order of dismissal passed by respondents was challenged in departmental appeal which was rejected by Appellate Authority - Appellant approached HC through Writ Petition, which was allowed by Single Judge and directed reinstatement of appellant - HC gave liberty to respondents to initiate fresh proceedings against appellant after perusing judgment passed in criminal case - In appeal DB set aside judgment in letters patent appeal filed by respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, appellant was not provided any subsistence allowance during period of suspension and adjournment prayed for by him on account of his illness, duly supported by medical certificates, was refused resulting in ex-parte proceedings against him - Appellant was punished in total violation of principles of natural justice and he was literally not afforded any opportunity of hearing - Further, there was yet another reason for discarding whole of case of respondents, criminal case as also departmental proceedings were based on identical set of facts, namely, 'the raid conducted at the appellant's residence and recovery of incriminating articles there from' - Findings recorded by Inquiry Officer, copy of which was placed before SC, indicate that charges framed against appellant were sought to be proved by Police Officers and Panch witnesses, who had raided house of appellant and had effected recovery - They were only witnesses examined by Inquiry Officer and Inquiry Officer, relying upon their statements, came to conclusion that charges were established against appellant - Same witnesses were examined in criminal case but Court, on consideration of entire evidence, came to conclusion that no search was conducted nor was any recovery made from residence of appellant, whole case of prosecution was thrown out and appellant was acquitted - Therefore, where appellant was acquitted by judicial pronouncement with finding that 'raid and recovery' at residence of appellant were not proved, it would be unjust, unfair and rather oppressive to allow findings recorded at ex- parte departmental proceedings, to stand - Thus, in peculiar circumstances of case, specially having regard to fact that appellant was undergoing this agony since 1985 despite having been acquitted by criminal court in 1987, SC would not direct any fresh departmental inquiry to be instituted against him on same set of facts - Appellant should be reinstated forthwith on post of security officer and should also be paid entire arrears of salary, together with all allowances from date of suspension till his reinstatement - Appeal allowed.
