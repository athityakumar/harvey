Paper Products Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 103; (1999) 7 SCC 84; AIR 1999 SC 3341; AIR 1999 SCW 3337; 2001 (165) CTR 693; 1999 (66) ECC 36; 1999 (84) ECR 284; 1999 (112) E.L.T. 765; [2001] 247 ITR 128; JT 1999 (6) SC 185; 1999 (34) RLT 365; 1999(5) SCALE 172; [2001] 115 TAXMAN 147

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 37 B - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1986, Chap. 49, Chap. 39 - Whether directions issued by Central Board of Custom and Excise are binding on the department? - All three products of appellant are to be treated as products of printing industry and not that of packaging industry - Issued show cause notice - Central board by subsequent circular treated appellant's product that of packaging industry - Challenge - Held, department cannot challenge circulars even on basis that they are inconsistent with statutory provisions - Demand notice issued by Department in view of subsequent circular, ab initio bad.
