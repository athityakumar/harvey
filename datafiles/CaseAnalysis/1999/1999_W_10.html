Aristo Pharmaceuticals Limited v Wockhardt Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 39; (2000) 10 SCC 93; AIR 2000 SC 3624; 1999 ARBLR 449; 2001 (21) PTC 139; 2000 RAJ 92; 1999(7) SCALE 617; 2000 (1) SLT 426

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Intellectual Property - Arbitration and ADR- Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, s. 29 - Trademark - Violation - Injunction suit - Whether in the suit for injunction filed by the respondent alleging violation of its trade mark the Division Bench of the High Court should have reversed the finding of the Single Judge and granted temporary injunction - Manufacturing and selling - Drug called 'spasmo proxyvon' and 'spasmo-flexon' - Plea 'spasmo' has been used for several drugs since long before and after respondent got its trade mark registered - Facts alleged, not exactly admitted by the respondent - Necessary to allow parties to lead evidence - Ex-Parte Injuction, if can be granted -Held, cannot be granted - Appeal allowed.
