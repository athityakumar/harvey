Jagdish Rai and Brothers v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1017; (1999) 3 SCC 257; AIR 1999 SC 1258; 1999 ALJ 976; 1999 (36) ALR 35; 1999 (1) ARBLR 696; JT 1999 (2) SC 268

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 29 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Claim for interest on arbitral award, raised for first time only in appeal against decree - Claimant disentitled to interest upto date of decree - Interest, u/s. 34 CPC, 1908 is a matter of procedure and ought to be granted in all money decrees - So refused, from date of decree to date of its realisation - Validity - Held, incorrect - Such interest could be awarded and decree could be corrected when application in that regard had been filed in appeal - Appeal allowed.
