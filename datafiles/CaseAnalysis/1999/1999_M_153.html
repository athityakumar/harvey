Krishna Mohini (Ms) v Mohinder Nath Sofat

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 789; (2000) 1 SCC 145; AIR 2000 SC 317; 2000 (1) CLT 67; JT 1999 (8) SC 379; 1999(6) SCALE 616; 1999 (3) SCJ 638; 1999 (10) SLT 362; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 76; 1999 (9) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: Election - Government of Union Territories Act, 1963 - Representation of the People Act, 1951,ss.100(1)(b) , 100(1)(c) (i) , 116 A, 29 A, 33, 36 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 324 - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961,rr. 4, 5, 10 - Election - Set aside - Challenged - Validity - Legislative assembly elections were declared and respondents and appellant and some others filed their nomination papers - Returning Officer(RO) rejected nomination papers of 'X' and 'Y' as substitute candidates - 7 persons were in fray and elections were held and appellant was declared elected - Appellant won by 26 votes on her nearest rival respondent - Respondent/petitioner filed election petition in HC challenging u/ ss.100(1)(b) and 100(1)(c) (i) of the 1951 Act alleging improper rejection of nomination forms of 'X' and 'Y' - HC on consideration of evidence allowed election petition holding that rejection of 'X' and 'Y' nomination papers was illegal and election of returned candidate was setaside - Hence instant appeal by returned candidate /appellant - Whether Judgment of HC was justified - Held, any person may be nominated as candidate in election u/s. 32 of the 1951 Act if he was duly qualified under the Constitution - Nomination papers of candidates could not be rejected unless suffered disqualifications mentioned in s. 36(2) of the 1952 Act and if no grounds of disqualifications his nomination had to be accepted and his name to be included in validly nominated candidates list - SC in In N.T. Veluswami Thevar v. Raja Nainar and Ors,1958 Indlaw SC 90 held that there could be no rejection of nomination paper outside s. 36(2) of the 1952 Act - Nomination paper subscribed to by ten proposers being electors of that Constituency could not be rejected even on error or omission of symbol - Both 'X' and 'Y' were set up by unrecognised political parties and their nomination papers were subscribed each by ten electors of Constituency as proposers and hence RO could have accepted their nomination forms as none of them were disqualified and nomination forms also did not suffer from any other infirmity - RO was not right in rejecting nomination papers of 'X and 'Y' as rightly held by HC - Illegal rejection of nomination was by itself good ground for setting aside election without considering result of election having been materially affected - Hira Singh Pal v. Madan Lal, 968 Indlaw SC 242 followed - HC another finding that nomination papers of 'Z' were illegally accepted by RO was set aside giving due weight to evidence of RO and his assistnat holding that being public oficers no obvious reason to lie and held that nomination paper of 'Z' was rightly accepted by RO - HC judgment declaring election void as materially affected by illegal rejection of nomination papers was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
