R. P. Moidutty v P. T. Kunju Mohammad and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 749; (2000) 1 SCC 481; AIR 2000 SC 388; JT 1999 (7) SC 457; 1999(6) SCALE 204; 1999 (3) SCJ 396; 1999 (9) SLT 30; 1999 (8) Supreme 372

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 15 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 81(3), 83 116-A, 123(3) and (3-A) - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, r. 94-A - Corrupt practices on contents of video cassette with objectionable and inflammatory speeches, offending ss. 123(3) and (3-A) of Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election Judge of High Court dismissed petition - Whether it is valid? - Held, Affidavit filed by petitioner in election petition as required by r. 94-A does not satisfy requirement of proviso to s. 83(1) of Act and Form 25 appended to rules, neither verification in petition nor affidavit gives any indication of source of information of petitioner as to such facts as were not in his own knowledge, therefore petition should have been rejected at threshold for non-compliance with mandatory provisions of law as to pleadings - Appeal dismissed.
