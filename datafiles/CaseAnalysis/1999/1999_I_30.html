Commissioner of Sales Tax v Industrial Coal Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 371; (1999) 2 SCC 607; AIR 1999 SC 1324; 1999 ALJ 977; JT 1999 (2) SC 6; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 871; 1999 (114) STC 365

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Sales Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 4 A - Exemption from sales tax for specified period - Grant of - To industrial unit having capital investment below Rs. 3 lakhs fulfilling prescribed conditions - Capital investment subsequently increases on account of shifting of unit to its own premises - Held, exemption granted will not cease to operate - Neither section nor notification contains any such condition - That exemption once granted would cease to operate if capital investment of unit exceeds Rs. 3 lakhs subsequently.
