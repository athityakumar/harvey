Sube Singh Bahmani and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K. Venkataswami, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 718; (1999) 8 SCC 213; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1453; JT 1999 (7) SC 53; 2000 (5) SLR 783; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class Ii) Rules, 1963, Punjab Financial Commission Class Iii Rules, 1957

Summary: (A)Service - Administrative - Punjab Financial Commission Officers governed by ( Group B) Service Rules, 1986 ,rr.9(3),11 - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class III ) Rules, 1952, rr.6,6(3),9 (c ) - Haryana Secretariat Service(Group-A) Rules, 1979 - Punjab Financial Commission Class III Rules,1957 ,rr.7(2),11( c) - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class II) Rules, 1963 ,rr.8,8(3),10(1) - Disputes arose between general candidates and reserved candidates regarding seniority -Appellant /reserved candidate was promoted as Superintendent and was disputed by respondents/general candidates by filing writ petition which was allowed by HC following Ajit Singh No.1 - Hence instant appeal by appellant/reserved candidate - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, in Class II promotions were based on seniority cum merit and not on seniority alone - In Class III posts all appointments whether by transfer or promotion were strictly by selection and seniority determined by seniority in appointment from which they were promoted but for Assistants seniority would be determined from order of their promotion - There was roster to implement reservation for promotion appellant was promoted as Superintendent on 6-1-1988 whereas respondents were promoted as Deputy Superintendents on 16-11-1988 , 1989, 1992, 1995 much after appellant and not even reached level of Dy.Superintendents when appellant was promoted to Superintendent - State Govt. informed in counter that due to disputes raised by reserved candidates date of promotion of appellant was modified from 6-1-1988 to 10-4-1989 vide GO dt.19-2-1990 - As per this modification respondent no.2 had reached level of Dy.Superintendent on 16 -11-1988 before appellant was promoted as Superintendent long before Ajit Singh No.1 and as stated by SC in Ajit singh No.II, it does not matter whether the general candidate reaches the level of Dy. Superintendent before or after Ajit Singh No.1 - Hence respondent no.2 should be considered senior to appellant at level of Dy.Superintendent - Other respondents would have no claim against appellant as none of them reached level of Dy.Superintendent before 10 -4-1989 - Directed to consider respondent no.2 seniority at level of Dy.Superintendent and to consider his promotion as Superintendent with that of appellant interse at level of Superintendent - Appeals disposed of .

(B) Service - Administrative - Punjab Financial Commission Officers governed by ( Group B) Service Rules, 1986 ,rr.9(3),11 - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class III ) Rules, 1952, rr.6,6(3),9 (c ) - Haryana Secretariat Service(Group-A) Rules, 1979 - Punjab Financial Commission Class III Rules,1957 ,rr.7(2),11( c) - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class II) Rules, 1963 ,rr.8,8(3),10(1) - Promotions - Disputed - Validity - Respondents 2 to 4/general candidates filed writ petition challenging promotions of appellants and was allowed by HC following Ajit Singh No.1 - Hence instant appeal by appellants/reserved candidates - Whether Judgment of HC was justified - Held, when appellant became Superintendent on 11-2-1985 none of respondents/general candidates reached level of Dy.Supdt - Hence respondents/general candidates could have no claim against appellant - Other reserved candidates/Dy.Superintendents senior to appellant should become Superintendents even before appellant and directions issued for their promotion to superintendent at par with appellant - Respondents/general candidates became Dy Superintendents much before respondents/reserved candidates 'X' , 'Y' and 'Z' and hence 4 writ petitioners/general candidates had rightful claim of seniority over them and even if they were promoted earlier they had to be treated as juniors to respondents /general candidates - Seniority of general candidates at level of Dy.Superintendents was to be refixed - Directed to review seniority of these four general candidates - Ajit Singh No.II had to be implemented - State Govt. had issued Circular dt.9-2-1979 that reserved candidates would not count their seniority on promotion at roster point which was reiterated by elaborate Circular dt.10-1-1997 issued after Ajit Singh Januja and Others v State of Punjab and Others , 1996 Indlaw SC 3762 (Ajit Singh No.1) - SC held that Circulars issued by State Govt. were in accordance to correct legal position - Appeals disposed of .

( C ) Service - Administrative - Punjab Financial Commission Officers governed by ( Group B) Service Rules, 1986 ,rr.9(3),11 - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class III ) Rules, 1952, rr.6,6(3),9 (c ) - Haryana Secretariat Service(Group-A) Rules, 1979 - Punjab Financial Commission Class III Rules,1957 ,rr.7(2),11( c) - Punjab Civil Secretariat (State Service, Class II) Rules, 1963 ,rr.8,8(3),10(1) - Seniority - Disputed - Validity - Appellants were reserved candidates and respondent nos. 2 to 8 were general candidates - Writ petition filed by general candidates was allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Promotions from post of Clerk to Assistant and Assistant to Dy. Superintendent were by way of roster as far as appellants were concerned - Appellants were promoted as Dy. Superintendents on 23-6-1995 and 23-11-1995 and respondent nos. 2 to 5/general candidates who were senior to them as Assistant were all promoted as Dy.Superintendents on 8-2-1996 - Kamal Kanta was promoted as Superintendent in on 18-3-1996 whereas respondents 2 to 5 were promoted as Superintendent in June and October and December 1996 - Respondent nos. 2 to 5 had to be treated as seniors to appellants at level of Dy.Superintendent - Hence seniority at level of Deputy Superintendents had to be refixed - Promotion and seniority of appellants as Superintendent be reviewed vis- a-vis respondent no. 2 to 5 - Respondents 6 to 8/general candidates could have no claim against appellant as they did not get promotion as Dy.Superintendents before appellant was promoted as Superintendent - All these three appeals would be governed by Ajit Singh Januja and Others(1) v State of Punjab and Others 1996 Indlaw SC 3762 and Ajit Singh and Others (II) v State of Punjab and Others,1999 Indlaw SC 725 and R. K. Sabharwal and Others v State of Punjab and Others, 1995 Indlaw SC 1756 - Appeals disposed of.
