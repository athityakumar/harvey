K. Mohanan v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2097; (2000) 10 SCC 222

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Possession of contraband, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 50, 31 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused was allegedly in possession on contraband articles - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/s. 21 of the Act - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - HC confirmed the conviction - Hence instant appeal -Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable - Held, if accused, who was subjected to search was merely asked whether he required to be searched in presence of Gazetted officer or Magistrate it should not be treated as communicating to him that he had right under law to be searched so - What PW1 done in instant case was to seek opinion of accused whether he wanted it or not - If he was told that he had right under law to have it searched what would have been answer given by accused should not be gauged by Court at this distance of time - This was particularly so when main defence adopted by accused at all stages was that s. 50 of the Act was not complied with - SC, therefore, held that there was non-compliance with s. 50 of the Act and consequently evidence of search spoken to by PW1 should not be acted upon in absence of any other independent evidence to show that accused was in possession of contraband article - Hence SC was in view that s. 31 was inapplicable as against accused - Therefore conviction and sentence was set aside - Appeal allowed.
