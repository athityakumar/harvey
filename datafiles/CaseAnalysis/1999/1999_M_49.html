 Union of India and Others v M. V. Valliappan and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1207; (1999) 6 SCC 259; AIR 1999 SC 2526; 1999 (155) CTR 193; [1999] 238 ITR 1027; JT 1999 (5) SC 134; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1146; [1999] 105 TAXMAN 605; 1999 (2) TLR 799

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 171(9), 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245, sch. 7, list 1, entry 82 - Hindu Undivided Family - Partial partition de-recognition by s. 171(9) - Not beyond legislative competence - Hardship not ground to assail legislative competence-recognition of partial partition - Validity - Held, s. 171(9) does not entrench upon ss. 4 and 5 - Not arbitrary and violative of art. 14 - Is valid - Assessment after partial partition - S. 20-A de-recognising partial partition - Not unconstitutional.
