State of Kerala v Aravind Ramakant Modawdakar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 760; (1999) 7 SCC 400; AIR 1999 SC 2970; JT 1999 (5) SC 588

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Kerala Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1976, Kerala Finance Act, 1994

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Kerala Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1976, s. 22, ch. 1, item 4(1)(f) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Contract carriages - Reduction of tax liability in favour of vehicles covered by intra - State contract permit - Not discriminatory qua vehicles covered by inter-State permits - Classification is made for purpose of granting exemption u/s. 22 of Act - Exemption/reduction is in 'Public interest' - Whether taxing statute violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Held, in many factual ways these vehicles covered by two different permits do form separate and distinct class - So long as this classification is not arbitrary or unreasonable, courts will not interfere - Appeal allowed.
