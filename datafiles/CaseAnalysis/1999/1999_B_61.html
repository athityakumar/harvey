V. Sudeer v Bar Council of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 454; (1999) 3 SCC 176; AIR 1999 SC 1167; JT 1999 (2) SC 141; 1999 (2) KLT 73; 1999(2) SCALE 32; 1999 (2) SCJ 177; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 1048; 1999 (1) SLR 732; 1999 (2) SLT 539; 1999 (2) Supreme 460; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1517

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Right To Practise, Solicitor General, Enrolment, Legal Education, State Bar Councils, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Bar Council of India Rules, 1975

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961 - Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899 - Bar Council of India Training Rules, 1995 - Whether the Bar Council of India Training Rules, 1995 are competent to train the entrants of the legal profession within the competence of the Bar Council of India or are ultra vires its rule-making powers under the Advocates Act, 1961 and in the alternative, whether these Rules are unreasonable and arbitrary and hence violative of art. 14 of the Constitution of India? - Held, yes - In Indian Council of Legal Aid & Advice v. Bar Council of India ((1995) 1 SCC 7321995 while Shri Rao, Learned Senior Counsel for the Bar Council of India submitted the decision while interpreting the provisions of s. 49(1) of the Act was rendered per in curiam as it had not noticed the decision of the Constitution Bench of this court in Lily Isabel Thomas, Re (AIR 1964 SC 8551964 AIR(SC) as well as the express provisions of s. 24(3)(d) of the Act - Mr. Rao submitted that the impugned Rules were legal and valid and were properly framed u/s. 7 r/w s. 24(3)(d) and ss. 49(1) and (2) of the Act - Bar Council of India can promote legal education by laying down standards of such education in consultation with the respective universities in India.
