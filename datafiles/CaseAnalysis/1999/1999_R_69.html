Trisuns Chemical Industry v Rajesh Agarwal and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 728; (1999) 8 SCC 686; (2000) SCC (Cr) 47; AIR 1999 SC 3499; AIR 1999 SCW 3492; 1999 CRLJ 4325; JT 1999 (6) SC 618; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 179, 190 and 482 - (A) Inherent powers - Quashment of FIR or a complaint in exercise of - Whether can be ordered merely because, arbitration is a remedy for affording relief to party? - (B) Inherent powers - Whether quashing of complaint on grounds that Judicial Magistrate First Class has no territorial jurisdiction to take cognizance of offence alleged and issue process proper? - Held, (A)quashing of FIR or a complaint in exercise of inherent powers of High COurt should be limited to very extreme exceptions - Arbitration is not an effective substitute for a criminal prosecution - (B) Order is improper and also premature - Quashed - Appeal disposed of.
