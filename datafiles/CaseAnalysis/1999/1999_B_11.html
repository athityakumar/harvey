Superintending Engineer and Others v B. Subba Reddy

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 15; (1999) 4 SCC 423; AIR 1999 SC 1747; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 38; 1999 (2) ARBLR 304; JT 1999 (3) SC 311; 1999 (2) MahLJ 858; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Court fees Act

Summary: Arbitration - Interest - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 20, 39 and 41 - Award of interest @ 18% per annum was reduced to 12% per annum by the Trial Court - Respondent did not file any appeal challenging the same - Whether the cross objections filed by them are sustainable - Held, no - If no right of cross-objection is given under s. 39 of the Act, it cannot be read into s. 41 of the Act - Filing of cross objections is procedural in nature - Cross objections are substantially an appeal by nature - Respondent challenges findings without preferring an appeal which cannot be allowed - Appeal partly allowed.
