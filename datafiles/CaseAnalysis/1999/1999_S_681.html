Arumugham (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v Sundarambal and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1124; (1999) 4 SCC 350; AIR 1999 SC 2216; 1999 (36) ALR 751; JT 1999 (4) SC 464; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 127; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 950; 2000 (Supp) RD 342

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101 and 104 - (A) Second appeal - Reversal of judgment of First Appellate Court - If allowed, on ground that it had not come to grips with reasonings of Trial Court - (B) Both parties adduced oral as well as documentary evidence - Significance of question of burden of proof - Held, not permissible - Question becomes insignificant - Appeal allowed.
