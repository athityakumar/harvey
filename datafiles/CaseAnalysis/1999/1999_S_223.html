Mohinder Singh v State Government of Nct of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2045; (2002) 10 SCC 189

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.307 - Explosive Substances Act, 1884, s.4A - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant no.1 was accused under the 1987 Act - Appellant No.1 was convicted by Trial Court u/s.307 of IPC, s.5 of 1987 Act and s.4-A of 1884 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant relying on evidence of PW5 was maintainable - Held, it was not disputed that the only act for which the appellant No.1 stood convicted was the hurling of the hand grenade from the roof of the house of appellant No.2 - In between that house and the house of PW5 there was another house - Conviction of the appellant No.1 would stand or fall depending upon the reliability of the evidence of PW 5 because his son PW7 admitted in his evidence that he did not see the actual act of the appellant throwing the hand grenade, all that he knew was that his father told him that such a thing was done by the appellant no.1 - When conviction was to be based on the evidence of a single eyewitness, the testimony of that eyewitness should be wholly reliable - In instant case PW5 said that he saw the appellant No.1 throwing the hand grenade, but in cross-examination PW5 said that before the hand grenade was thrown there was a quarrel with appellant No.1 and then PW5 had gone inside his house and bolted the door of the house - Upon the arrival of the police PW 5 was asked to go out of the room and he did so - PW 5 further said that when he came out of the room he saw some brickbats and a hand grenade lying in the courtyard - Much reasoning was not necessary to highlight the insufficiency of the evidence of PW5 to reach a conclusion that the prosecution had proved beyond all reasonable doubts that it was the appellant who had thrown the hand grenade which fell in the courtyard - It could either be an allegation which the aggrieved PW 5 made against the appellant or it might be an impression which he formed in his mind, or it might be something which others would have made him to believe - There was no other corroboration - What PW7 had deposed was that his father PW5 revealed to him that he witnessed the throwing of hand grenade while peeping through the space of the bolted door - Even PW5 did not say in court that he saw the incident through such a narrow space - It was apparently incredible - There was no other evidence to connect the appellant with the hand grenade - In view of the paucity of evidence, SC found it difficult to sustain the conviction of the offences passed by Trial Court on the appellant No.1 - Appeal allowed.
