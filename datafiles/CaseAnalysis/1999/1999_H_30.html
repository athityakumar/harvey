Jeet Mohinder Singh v Harminder Singh Jassi

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 787; (1999) 9 SCC 386; AIR 2000 SC 256; 2000 (1) CLT 165; JT 1999 (8) SC 432; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 1999(6) SCALE 703; 2000 (1) SCJ 150; 1999 (9) SLT 267; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 33; 1999 (10) Supreme 99

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Different Constituency

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(b), (d)(ii), 2(a) 116 A, 123(1), 123(2), 123(3), 123(3A)and 123(6) - Corrupt practices - Legislative Assembly elections in State of Punjab - Whether appellant rightly contended that, nomination paper filed by respondent was invalid and it should be rejected and respondent is also guilty of various acts of commission and omission which amounted to corrupt practices? - No material brought on record to infer result of election having been materially affected - Requisite consent of returned candidate or his election agent is a constituent part of corrupt practices under sub-ss. (3) and (3A) of s. 123 and an ingredient of ground u/s. 100(1)(b) - Such consent has to be pleaded and proved - Implied consent of candidate cannot be read into provision - Whenever requirement is of consent, it must be free consent given by giver of consent of his own volition - It must be specifically alleged and positively proved - In this case there are neither such pleadings nor proof as to alleged violence having been committed by respondent - Held, none of findings arrived at by Learned Judge negativing proof of alleged corrupt practices by respondent is liable to be interfered - Appeal dismissed.
