Secunderabad Hyderabad Hotel Owners' Association and Others v Hyderabad Municipal Corporation, Hyderabad and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 917; (1999) 2 SCC 274; AIR 1999 SC 635; JT 1999 (1) SC 75; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act, 1955 ,ss. 521 (1)(e)(ii) ,622 ,622 (2) ,197 , 230 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - License fee - Disputed - Validity - License fee for different trades were specified by respondent in 1972 and were revised in 1981 which was challenged by appellant category by filing writ petition in HC but was dismissed by Single Judge - Appeal was dismissed by DB holding that respondent was justified as increased for rendering of services and upheld levy as fee and not tax - Aggrieved petitioners/appellants filed instant appeals - Again respondent increased licence fees after discussions with appellants/ petitioner category and was challenged by petitioners by filing writ petition in SC - Both appeals and writ petition were tried together by SC as common questions of law involved - Whether action of respondent was justified - Held, u/s. 230 of the 1955 Act respondent had powers to fix special rates of conservancy tax in respect of hotels, clubs or other large premises and licence fee could not be termed as tax - License fee may either be regulatory or compensatory - Respondent had obligation to ensure cleanliness and removal of garbage and maintenance of hygiene in its jurisdiction by sanitation etc for benefit of general public - SC in Commissioner, Hindu Religious Endowments, Madras v. Sri Laxshmindra Thirtha Swamiar of Sri Shirpur Mutt,1954 Indlaw SC 33 made clear distinction between tax and fee - SC in Indian Mica & Micanite Industries Ltd. v. State of Bihar & Ors,1971 Indlaw SC 665 considered fee which was charged for services rendered - License fees charged was not just for services rendered but also towards regulatory fee for monitoring activities of licensees to ensure that they comply with terms and conditions of licence - SC had made distinction between regulatory and compensatory fees in P. Kannadasan & Ors. v. State of Tamilnadu & Ors,1996 Indlaw SC 2915 - Expenditure of respondent under various heads between 1981 and 1992 had more than doubled and hence first increase in 1981 after 1972 and second increase after 10 years in 1992 was justified and not excessive and were co-related with cost increase in providing services - Respondent had reduced license fee based on compromise after several discussions with traders joint action committee and revised rates were implemented after approved by Standing Committee and General Body of respondent - License fee charged was regulatory cum compensatory fee and it was not tax - Judgment of HC was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
