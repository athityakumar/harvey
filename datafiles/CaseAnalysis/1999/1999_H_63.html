Shiv Kumar v Hukam Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 679; (1999) 7 SCC 467; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1277; JT 1999 (6) SC 385; 1999(5) SCALE 294; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prosecution - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 225 and 301(2) - Conduct of prosecution by pleader of private person - Appellant is aggrieved because a counsel engaged by him was not allowed by High Court to conduct prosecution in spite of obtaining a consent from Public Prosecutor concerned - Whether prosecution can be conducted by a counsel engaged by a private party and not Public Prosecutor? - Held, no - Scheme of CrPC, 1973 -Legislative intention is manifestly clear that prosecution in a Sessions Court cannot be conducted by anyone other than Public Prosecutor - Legislature reminds State that policy must strictly conform to fairness in trial of an accused in a Sessions Court - Expected attitude of Public Prosecutor while conducting prosecution must be couched in fairness not only to court and to investigating agencies but to accused as well - Legislature imposes curb on a counsel engaged by any private party - Role of such counsel is limited to acting under directions of Public Prosecutor - Prosecution by a pleader of private party may deteriorate into a means for wreaking private vengeance - Ends of justice require that every trial is conducted in a fair and impartial manner - Appeal dismissed.
