Harendra H. Mehta and Others v Mukesh H. Mehta and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 21; (1999) 5 SCC 108; AIR 1999 SC 2054; 1999 (3) ARBLR 1; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 543; [1999] 97 Comp Cas 265; 1999 (156) CTR 201; [1999] 238 ITR 158; JT 1999 (4) SC 50; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 562; [1999] 106 TAXMAN 1; 1999 (2) TLR 650

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Foreign Judgment, Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Settlement Agreement, Civil Practice Law Rules

Summary: Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, ss. 2, 4 and 7 - Foreign award - Enforcement of - Parties having joint venture and properties in India and USA - Conflict arising between parties - Parties appointed arbitrator to make division of jointly held assets and business - Arbitration agreement made, proceedings held and award passed in USA - Award passed is Foreign award - Differences that arose between parties were brothers, were out of legal relationships and certainly of commercial nature under Indian laws - Held, enforcement in India - Not barred by fact that judgment on basis of award has been obtained in country of its origin.
