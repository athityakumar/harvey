Lokmat Newspapers Private Limited v Shankarprasad

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 587; (1999) 6 SCC 275; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1090; AIR 1999 SC 2423; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 81; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 322; JT 1999 (4) SC 546; 1999 (3) LLN 538; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 907; 1999 (4) SLR 248

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Printing, Closure, Failure Report, Conciliator, Rationalisation, Standardisation

Summary: Letters Patent (Bom), cl. 15 - Letters patent appeal - Tenability - Order of single judge in writ petition u/arts. 226 and 227 - Basic averments for invoking such jurisdiction pleaded in petition - Petition dismissed - Observation, courts below had appreciated contentions and rejected petitioners complaint - Held, does not mean that single judge was not inclined to interfere u/art. 227 only - Writ appeal maintainable.
