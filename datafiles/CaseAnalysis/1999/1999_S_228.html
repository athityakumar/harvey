Rammi Alias Rameshwar v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1512; (1999) 8 SCC 649; (2000) SCC (Cr) 26; AIR 1999 SC 3544; 1999 CRLJ 4561; JT 1999 (7) SC 247; 2000(1) M.P.H.T. 153; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Former Statement

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 27, 138 and 155 - Witness - Post event conduct - Recovery of weapons - Admissibility of information received from accused which led to recovery of articles - Material discrepancy in evidence of eye-witness and that of investigating officer regarding time when police took accused in custody-Re- examination of witness - Scope - Credibility of witness - Is variation in statement of witness sufficient to discredit statement? - Held, credibility of witness - Mere variations falling in narration of incident by witnesses - Not sufficient to discredit their testimony - When eye - witness is examined at length it is quite possible for him to make some discrepancies - No true witness can possibly escape from making some discrepant details - An untrue witness who is well tutored can successfully make his testimony totally non-discrepant - Appeal dismissed.
