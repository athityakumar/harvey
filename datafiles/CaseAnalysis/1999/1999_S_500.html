Ajit Kumar Rath v State of Orissa and Other

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 481; (1999) 9 SCC 596; AIR 2000 SC 85; 2000 (1) CLR 29; JT 1999 (8) SC 578; 1999 LabIC 3842; 2000 (2) LLN 879; 1999(7) SCALE 22; 2000 (2) SLJ 108; 2000 (1) SLR 622; 1999 (9) SLT 571; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 302; 1999 (9) Supreme 321

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Civil Procedure - Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Administrative Tribunal Act, s. 22(3)(f) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 114, O. 47 - Orissa Service of Engineer Rules, 1941, rr. 6 , 16(a), and 26 - (A) Whether the appellant and respondent No. 12 having been promoted in 1972 would rank senior to the respondents who were appointed as Asst. Engineers by direct recruitment in the same year? - R. 26 underwent an amendment in 1967 which specifically provided that if the posts of Asst. Engineers were filled up in a particular year both by direct recruitment as also by promotion, those promoted would rank senior to those who were directly recruited; this amendment was made with retrospective effect from 01-01-1972, the appellant was promoted on a regular, though provisional basis pending concurrence from the Orissa Public Service Commission; the promotion having been made in accordance with the Rules, the entire period of ad hoc service beginning from 1972 to 1976, when the appellant was appointed on a regular basis on the concurrence of the Commission, would have to be counted towards the seniority of the appellant vis-a-vis the contesting respondents - Held, in terms of r. 26, the appellant, who was promoted in 1972, in which year direct recruitments of respondent Nos. 2 to 11 were also made, shall rank senior to respondent Nos. 2 to 11, the Tribunal rightly invoked the principles laid down in Direct Recruit Class-II Engg. Officers Association's case and held that appellant and respondent No. 12 were senior to the respondents - (B) Whether the respondents are entitled to reckon their seniority from 1970 and 1971 as they were appointed against the vacancies of 1970 and 1971? - Held, the law on this question has already been explained in the case of Jagdish Ch. Patnaik and others v. State of Orissa and Ors. [1998 Indlaw SC 759] wherein it was categorically held that the appointment does not relate back to the date of vacancy; the plea is wholly unfounded - (C) Whether the Tribunal could review its own judgment merely because there was a judgment in which a contrary view was taken which was not earlier noticed by the Tribunal? - Provision of r. 22(3)(f) indicate that the power of review available to the Tribunal is the same as has been given to a court u/s. 114 r/w O. 47, CPC, the power is not absolute and is hedged in by the restrictions indicated in O. 47, a review cannot be claimed or asked for merely for a fresh hearing or arguments or correction of an erroneous view taken earlier, i.e, the power of review can be exercised only for correction of a patent error of law or fact which stares in the face without any elaborate argument being needed for establishing it, the expression "any other sufficient reason" used in O. 47 r. 1 means a reason sufficiently analogous to those specified in the r. 31 - Held, any other attempt, except an attempt to correct an apparent error or an attempt not based on any ground set out in O. 47, would amount to an abuse of the liberty given to the Tribunal under the Act to review its judgment - Appeal allowed.
