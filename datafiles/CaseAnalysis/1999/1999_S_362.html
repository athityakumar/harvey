Om Prakash v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 972; (1999) 3 SCC 19; (1999) SCC (Cr) 334; AIR 1999 SC 1332; AIR 1999 SCW 1029; 1999 (1) CCR 115; 1999 CRLJ 2044; 1999 (1) JCC 182; JT 1999 (1) SC 599; 1999 (2) KLT 21; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 200; 1999(1) SCALE 570; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 794; 1999 (2) SLT 286; 1999 (2) Supreme 227

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Seven of a family murdered - Sentence - Whether case called for imposition of death punishment as it came within category of rarest of rare? - Held, no - It is a crime committed by accused who had a cause to feel aggrieved for injustice meted out to his family members at hands of family of other party who according to him were strong enough physically as well as economically and having influence with authority which was required to protect him and his family - May be that his mind got derailed of track and went astray or beyond control because of extreme mental disturbances for constant harassment and disputes - There is no reason to believe that he cannot be reformed or rehabilitated and that he is likely to continue criminal acts of violence as would constitute a continuing threat to society - Accused was working in B.S.F. as a disciplined member of Armed Forces aged about 23 at relevant time, having no criminal antecedents - Appeal allowed.
