Kalu Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1997; (2000) SCC (Cr) 86; (2000) 10 SCC 324; AIR 2000 SC 3630

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161 and 302 - Whether Trial Court as well as High Court correct in convicting appellant? - Held, accused thought of inflicting burns to deceased and to frighten her but situation slipped out of his control and it went to fatal extent - Very probably appellant would not have anticipated that act done by him would have escalated to such a proportion that she might die - If he had ever intended her to die he would not have alerted his senses to bring water in an effort to rescue her - Conviction altered from s. 302, IPC to s. 304, Part II, IPC with 7 years RI - Appeal disposed of.
