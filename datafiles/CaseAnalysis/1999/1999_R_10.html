H. P. State Electricity Board v R. J. Shah and Company

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1309; (1999) 4 SCC 214; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 68; 1999 (2) ARBLR 316; JT 1999 (3) SC 151; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 53; 1999 (2) RAJ 163; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Non speaking award - In award no reference to contract or any of its clause made - Distinction between an error within jurisdiction of arbitrator and an error in excess of jurisdiction of arbitrator - (A) Whether award can be set aside, if so whether award is in excess of jurisdiction? - Held, by purporting to construe contract court could not take upon itself burden of saying that award was contrary to contract and as such arbitrators had acted beyond their jurisdiction - When arbitrator is required to construe a contract then merely because another view may be possible court would not be justified in construing contract in a different manner and then to set aside award by observing that arbitrator has exceeded jurisdiction in making award - Issue before arbitrators, related to interpretation of terms of contract - Said contract was being read by parties differently, decision thereon, even if it be erroneous, cannot be said to be without jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
