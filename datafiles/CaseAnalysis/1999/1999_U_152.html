Laghu Udyog Bharati v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 101; (1999) 6 SCC 418; AIR 1999 SC 2596; 1999 (156) CTR 270; 1999 (156) CTR 770; 1999 (84) ECR 53; 1999 (112) E.L.T. 365; 1999 (33) RLT 911; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1199; 1999 (115) STC 616; 2006 (2) S.T.R. 276; 1999 TAXLR 828; [1999] 105 TAXMAN 630; 1999 (2) TLR 828

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Telephone, Tour, Cab, Mandap

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Finance Act, 1994, ss. 65, 66 and 94 - Service Tax Rules, 1994, rr. 2(d)(xii) and 2(xvii) - Person responsible for collection of Service tax - Tax rr. 2(xii) and (xvii) making client of clearing and forwarding agents and customer of goods transporter responsible - Rules are in conflict with ss. 65 and 66 of Act - Held, liable to be quashed - Tax collected from such client or customer liable to be refunded - Petition dismissed.
