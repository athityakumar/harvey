Anil Hada v Indian Acrylic Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 853; (2000) 1 SCC 1; (2001) SCC (Cr) 174; AIR 2000 SC 145; AIR 1999 SCW 4228; 2000 (38) ALR 273; 2006 (1) BC 143; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 391; 1999 (4) CCR 285; 2000 (1) CLC 91; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 36; 2000 (1) CompLJ 7; 2000 (1) Crimes 26; 2000 CRLJ 373; 2000 (1) CTC 94; 2000 (1) JCC 1; JT 1999 (9) SC 223; 2000 (1) KLT 141; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1; 1999(7) SCALE 209; 2000 (1) SCJ 217; 1999 (10) SLT 1; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 6; 1999 (9) Supreme 484

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Fiction, Rebuttal

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Cheques issued and dishonoured for insufficiency of funds - Winding-up proceedings ordered by court on company - Proceedings against directors of company - Held, normally an offence can be committed by human beings who are natural persons - Such offence can be tried according to procedure established by law - But there are offences which could be attributed to juristic persons also - If the drawer of a cheque happens to be a juristic person like a body corporate it can be prosecuted for the offence u/s. 138 - If prosecution proceedings against company were not taken or could not be continued, it is no bar for proceeding against the other persons falling within the purview of sub-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 141 - Appeal dismissed.
