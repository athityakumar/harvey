United Bank of India v Debts Recovery Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1408; (1999) 4 SCC 69; 1999 (2) ACJ 1383; AIR 1999 SC 1381; 1999 (2) AWC 1513; 2000 (1) BC 662; 1999 (3) CalLT(SC) 18; [1999] 96 Comp Cas 602; JT 1999 (2) SC 574; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 80; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 2(9) and 17 - Whether expression 'debt' is wide enough to include claim of an undetermined sum? - Held, yes, expression 'debt' has to be given widest amplitude to mean any liability which is alleged as due from any person by a bank during course of any business activity undertaken by bank either in cash or otherwise, whether secured or unsecured, whether payable under a decree or order of any court or otherwise and legally recoverable on date of application - Whether matter has to be tried by tribunal established under Act? - Further held, yes - Entire averments made in plaint has to be looked into while deciding whether claim can be adjudicated upon by tribunal constituted under Act - Claim in question made by plaintiff Bank is essentially one for recovery of a debt due to it from defendants it is tribunal which has exclusive jurisdiction to decide dispute and not ordinary Civil Court - Appeal allowed.
