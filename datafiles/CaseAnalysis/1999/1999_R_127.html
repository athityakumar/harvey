Dadarao and Another v Ramrao and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 491; (1999) 8 SCC 416; 1999 (37) ALR 789; 2000 (2) Bom.C.R. 15; JT 1999 (8) SC 608; 2000 (Supp) RD 456; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Appellant 1 herein is one of heirs of Balwantrao Ganpatrao Pande, who owned three acres of agricultural land and had entered into an agreement D/- 24-04-1969 with one Tukaram Devsarkar - Respondents herein are successorsinterest of Tukaram - Agreement provided that Pande would sell 3 acres of land for Rs. 2000 and received Rs. 1000 towards same - Further, it was provided that in case sale deed was not executed, in addition to earnest money, an amount of Rs. 500 shall be given and no sale deed will be executed - No sale deed, as contemplated by aforesaid agreement, was executed by Pande - Tukaram filed a suit for specific performance - Whether respondents are entitled to a decree of specific performance? - Held, agreement itself provides as to what is to happen if either seller refuses to sell or purchaser refuses to buy - Agreement provides that in addition to earnest money of Rs. 1000 a sum of Rs. 500 was to be given back to Tukaram Devsarkar and that 'no sale deed will be executed' - If agreement had not stipulated as to what is to happen in event of sale not going through, then perhaps plaintiff could have asked Court for a decree of specific performance - Lower appellate court and HC erred in coming to conclusion that successors-interest of Tukaram Devsarkar were in any way entitled to a decree of specific performance - Appeal allowed.
