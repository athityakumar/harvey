Ishwar Dass Jain (Dead) Through Lrs v Sohan Lal (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 854; (2000) 1 SCC 434; AIR 2000 SC 426; AIR 1999 SCW 4573; 2000 (1) ALD(SC) 40; 2000 (39) ALR 756; 2000 (1) CLT 45; JT 1999 (9) SC 305; 2000 (125) PLR 56; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 168; 1999(7) SCALE 277; 2000 (1) SCJ 197; 1999 (10) SLT 209; 2000 (Supp) RD 18; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 24; 1999 (10) Supreme 27

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bankers Books Evidence Act, 1891 - Code Of Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Registration Act, 1908 - Suit filed by appellants for redemption of mortgage and for possession - Suit dismissed on the ground that notwithstanding the mortgage deed, the real relationship between the parties was as landlord and tenant and that the defendant could not be evicted except under the rent control law - Issues arise - (A) Whether the High Court can interfere u/s. 100, CPC (as mentioned in 1976) with the findings of fact arrived at by the Lower Appellate Court? - There are two situations in which interference with findings of fact is permissible - (a) when material or relevant evidence is not considered which, if considered would have led to an opposite conclusion - (b) there a finding has been arrived at by the Appellate Court by placing reliance on inadmissible evidence which if it was omitted, an opposite conclusion was possible - In either of the above situations, a substantial question of law can arise which would entitle the court to interfere with the findings - (B) Whether on the facts of the case, the mortgage was proved by the plaintiff by production of a certified copy of the deed ? - Held, the defendant admitted the execution of the mortgage - The plaintiff could therefore not be faulted for not examining any of the attestors - Hence the mortgage stood proved by the certified copy - (C) Whether oral evidence is admissible u/s. 92(1) of the Evidence Act could be a bar for proving a document to be a sham document? - Held, oral evidence is admissible to show that the document executed was never intended to operate as an agreement but that some other agreement altogether, not recorded in the document, was entered into between the parties - The plea and evidence of collateral security offered by the defendant appears to us not to fit into a situation where the plaintiff has executed the mortgage - The reason mentioned and evidence given by the defendant as to why a sham document was executed falls to the ground - (D) Whether the Lower Courts had omitted vital evidence in that Exs. D-2 to D-5 could not be treated as account books for purposes of s. 34 of the Evidence Act and were not admissible - Held, the courts below, in our view, failed to notice that no account book or books were ever produced by the defendant in the Court - Exs. D-2 to D-5 filed into court were only "extracts" of the defendant's account books - S. 34 cannot attach to private extracts of alleged account books where the original accounts are not filed into court - (E) Whether the mortgagee who got possession of the entire property under the deed of mortgage could be permitted to deny the title of the mortgagor either wholly or partly? - Held, the defendant's title was a derivative title as mortgagee - Having come into possession of the whole property as a mortgagee from the plaintiff, treating the plaintiff as full owner it was not open to the defendant to question the title of the plaintiff - Suit decreed for redemption - Appeal allowed.
