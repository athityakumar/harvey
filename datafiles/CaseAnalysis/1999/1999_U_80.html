Garden Silk Mills Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, R.P. Sethi, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 754; (1999) 8 SCC 744; AIR 2000 SC 33; 1999 (85) ECR 1; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 358; 2000 (2) G.L.R. 1863; JT 1999 (7) SC 522; 1999 (34) RLT 833; 1999 (10) SLT 8; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(23), 2(27), 12 and 14 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Whether while assessing customs duty payable in respect of imported goods, Customs Authorities can add/include landing charges in arriving at value of those goods? - S. 14(1)(a) clearly indicates that it is not price stated in CIF contract which alone is to be accepted as being value of such goods for purpose of s. 14 of Act - For purpose of arriving at value at which goods are delivered to buyer at time and place of import to India, concept of value as understood in s. 14 of Act necessarily requires landing charges to be included in value - Held, landing charges were rightly taken into consideration in determining assessable value of imported goods for purposes of s. 14(1)(a) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
