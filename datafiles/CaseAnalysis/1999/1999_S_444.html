Dwaraka Das v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 35; (1999) 3 SCC 500; AIR 1999 SC 1031; 1999 (35) ALR 472; 1999 (3) ARBLR 291; 1999 (2) CLT 26; JT 1999 (1) SC 375; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; 1999 (1) RAJ 466; 1999(1) SCALE 376; 1999 (1) SCJ 538; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 524; 1999 (2) SLT 69; 2000 (Supp) RD 586; 1999 (1) Supreme 429

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 152 - 'Accidental omission' - Suit for breach of contract claiming damages - Decree passed - Court awarding only future interest despite prayer of plaintiff for grant of interest from date of alleged breach - Held, impliedly does mean that Court rejected claim for pendente lite interest - Not accidental omission - Prayer for award of pendente lite interest on such ground - Cannot be allowed.
