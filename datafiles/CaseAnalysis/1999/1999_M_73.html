Maharaja Chintamani Saran Nath Shahdeo v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 574; (1999) 8 SCC 16; AIR 1999 SC 3609; JT 1999 (8) SC 45; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, Bihar Land Reforms (Validation And Amendment) Act, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms (Validation and Amendment) Act, 1974, s. 6 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 25(4) - Limit of rate of compensation - Determination of - Tribunal held that maximum limit of rate of compensation would be twenty times u/s. 25(4)(b) of old Act - As per s. 6 of Amending Act of 1974, the rate would be three times of net income from royalties - Whether Amending Act of 1974 has got retrospective effect and thereby appellant entitled the enhanced compensation u/s. 6 of the Amended Act? - Held, applying golden rule of construction as stated by the Court in Garikapati Veeraya v. N. Subbiah Choudhary (1957 Indlaw SC 160), in amending Act there was nothing to show that Act would have retrospective effect - As 'the essential idea of a legal system is that current law should govern current activities' - Rate of compensation shall have to be determined in accordance with provisions of Act which was in force at time compensation was payable i.e. unamended sub-s. (4) of s. 25 of Act would apply - Moreover, amending Act affects substantive right of appellant, therefore, it would have prospective operation - There is also no express or implied provision in amending Act to indicate that Act will have retrospective effect - Amending Act would apply prospectively - It was an agreement between appellant and State as per cl. (a) of sub-s. (4) of s. 25 of Act - Under sub-section in absence of an agreement question of referring dispute to a tribunal would arise - Further, as in present appeal there was a clear agreement between parties, appellant forfeited his right for asking matter to be referred u/s. 25(4)(b) to a tribunal for arbitration and question of granting compensation at more than ten times could not arise - Appeal dismissed.
