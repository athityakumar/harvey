Baburam v C. C. Jacob and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 274; (1999) 3 SCC 362; (1999) SCC (Cr) 433; (1999) SCC (L&S) 682; AIR 1999 SC 1845; JT 1999 (2) SC 256; 1999 LabIC 2084; 1999 (2) LLJ 983; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 27; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 1378

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Promotion to post of Superintendent of Customs - By filing up a vacancy reserved for Scheduled Caste - Legality of - Whether promotion of appellant by Departmental Promotion Committee (DPC) to the post of Superintendent of Customs in a vacancy reserved for Scheduled Castes, is valid? - Held, DPC considered suitability of candidates eligible for promotion to four vacancies which arose during 1993-94 in cadre of Superintendent of Customs (Preventive) from post of Preventive Officers in which proceedings of DPC appellant was chosen to be promoted against a reserved vacancy earmarked for Scheduled Castes - Appellants herein came to be promoted on 26/6/1994 as against a reserved vacancy which arose on 1/6/1994 - Quota rule laid down by Supreme Court in R. K. Sabharwal and Ors. v. State of Punjab and Ors. (1995 I CLR 719 S.C.), wherein it was held that such rule would operate prospectively - Prospective declaration of law is a device innovated by Apex Court to avoid reopening of settled issues and to prevent multiplicity of proceedings - Therefore, subordinate forums which are legally bound to apply declaration of law made by this Court are also duty-bound to apply such dictum, to cases which would arise in future only - In the instant case, both decisions of the DPC as well as the appointing authority being prior to the judgment in Sabharwal's case - Tribunal was in error in applying that decision - Appeal allowed.
