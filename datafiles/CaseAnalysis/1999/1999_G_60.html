V. V. S. Sugars v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 374; (1999) 4 SCC 192; AIR 1999 SC 2124; AIR 1999 SCW 1871; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 46; JT 1999 (3) SC 420; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 925; 1999 (114) STC 47

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation Of Supply And Purchase) Act, 1961, Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Rules, 1961

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961, ss. 4, 5 and 21(3-D) - Agriculture - Exclusion of substantive provision u/s. 21(5) by s. 21(3 D)(a) while levying interest on arrears of tax on purchase of sugarcane - Determination of time limit for applicability of exclusion - Held, not limited only to crushing season of 75-76 rather operate without any time limit - There being no provision, interest could not be levied on arrears of tax that applied to purchases of sugarcane made subsequent to date of commencement of amending Act - Appeals allowed.
