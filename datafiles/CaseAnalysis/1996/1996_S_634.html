Ram Sanjiwan Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2863; (1996) 8 SCC 552; (1996) SCC (Cr) 701; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 96; AIR 1996 SC 3265; 1996 (2) Crimes 162; 1996 CRLJ 2528; JT 1996 (4) SC 502; 1996(4) SCALE 63; 1996 (4) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Summary: Education - University - Admission - Eligibility - Reservation - Common entrance - Appellant no. 1 was a society registered under the J & K Society Registration Act with the primary object being to promote medical education by establishing medical colleges, hospitals and child welfare centers - Proposal was submitted to the State Govt. for setting up a medical college and a private hospital in Jammu, the State Govt. had allotted 356 kanals of land for the proposed medical college and charitable hospital - A further allotment of 250 kanals of land was made by the State Govt. - Further the State Govt. recommended to the Jammu University for grant of affiliation to the proposed medical college to be set up by the society and also requested the Medical Council of India to grant approval/recognition to the said medical college - Inspection was made by the representatives of the Medical Council of India in respect of infrastructure including the teaching facilities and in due course temporary recognition of the institution was granted - Appellant no. 1 had approached the State Govt. to issue necessary guidelines and to frame rules to regulate the admissions to the medical college established by the Society - State Govt. even after repeated requests had failed to issue any guidelines - State Govt. had issued an advertisement applications from eligible candidates for appearing at the entrance test for admission to the two Govt.s medical colleges but it had not invited applications for the admission into the medical college of appellant no. 1 - Some students seeking admission in the medical college of appellant no. 1 filed a W.P. before the HC of Jammu & Kashmir making a grievance in respect of failure on the part of the State Govt. to frame necessary rules/guidelines to regulate admission to the medical college of appellant no. 1 which had deprived them of the opportunity of seeking admission - All necessary records and information were placed before the HC inspite of the several opportunities being given to the State Govt. no affidavit on their behalf was filed - Learned single Judge of the HC had constituted a Committee and issued directions for admission in appellant no. 1's college - Subsequently an appeal was filed by the State Govt. against the order passed by the single Judge of the HC, the matter was placed before the DB of the HC - DB of the HC set aside the order of the learned single Judge - Hence the instant appeal before the SC by the appellant no. 1 against the order passed by DB of the HC - Whether the DB of the HC was justified to interfere with the directions given by the learned single Judge and whether the directions issued by DB would affect the students whose admissions had been done on the basis of the directions of the committee appointed by the single Judge - Held the claim of made by appellant no. 1 and as well as applicants who were admitted in the said medical college, that their studies commenced and as such on equitable grounds they should be allowed to pursue their studies could not be held valid as the admission criteria was not according to law - State Govt. had failed to issue guidelines for admission to appellant no. 1's college but only on equitable grounds, a procedure which was not sanctioned by law could not be approved only to mitigate the hardship of such candidates who have sought admissions in the medical college of the society that is appellant no. 1 - Also guidelines issued by DB were not according to law and thus they could not be enforced - A common entrance test was to be held for admissions - Also held that due to the aforesaid conditions it was not possible to complete the admission in the said medical college of appellant no. 1 for the session which had already commenced and that a fresh advertisement was to be issued for the next academic session in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
