Ganapathi National Middle School v M. Durai Kannan (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2166; (1996) 6 SCC 464; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 385; AIR 1996 SC 2803; 1996 (3) CCC 343; JT 1996 (9) SC 80; 1996(6) SCALE 36; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 356; 1996 (6) Supreme 549; 1997 (1) UJ 108

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Recognised Private School! (Regulation) Act 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6 - Aided institution - Instrumentality of State - Appellant established aided institution in property belonging to landlord and on application by landlord, eviction order was passed - Appellant approached Govt. for acquisition of land under the Act and thus notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 of the Act came to be passed by Govt. - Respondents challenged said notification and declaration before HC, which allowed the same and quashed notification - On appeal, DB of HC confirmed the same in limini - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acquisition of property in question is for public purpose.

Held, right of education is fundamental right to every child but State could not impart education by itself and agency through which it organizes imparting education is recognized private institutions according to its procedure. SC recognized the need for the continuance of the educational institution in the property in question and State had taken action to acquire the land at the expense of the State to provide the education to the middle school going children. Further, entire expenditure for acquisition of said property is being met from the public funds. Hence, it is clearly a case of public purpose and HC was wholly wrong in its conclusion that public purpose is not served in acquiring the land but benefits the private individuals. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Educational institution receiving aid from State is an instrumentality or education agency of the State imparting education on behalf of the State which is a fundamental right of the citizens.
