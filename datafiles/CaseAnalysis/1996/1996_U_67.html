M.C. Mehta v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4092; (1996) 4 SCC 351; (1996) 8 SCC 462; AIR 1996 SC 1977; 1996 CLT 93; JT 1996 (5) SC 209; 1996(4) SCALE 422; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 806; 1996 (4) Supreme 535

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Development Act, 1957, s. 11A(2) - Land acquisition - Amendment of Master plan - Master Plan was approved by Central Govt. u/s IIA(2) of the Act which specifically provided that hazardous heavy industries were not permitted to operate in city and existing industrial units falling in these categories were shifted/relocated - Development authority examined question regarding utilization of land made available as result of relocation/shifting of industries - Committee agreed with reuse of vacated land as suggested by Board - Several industrial units submitted their objections/suggestions - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there was necessity to amend master plan in consideration of use of land - Held, provisions of master plan was statutory and binding - Land which would become available on account of shifting/relocation of industries could only be used for making up deficiency as per needs of community, based on norms given in Master Plan - When Master Plan permitted use of land only to meet community-needs, it was means that land had surrendered and dedicated to community - It was necessary to make suitable provision for owner to enable him to meet expenses of relocating/shifting industry - Directed that on percentage of land owners should be entitled to one and half time of permissible under Master Plan - Land surrendered by owner was to be used for development of greenbelt and open spaces also to be developed in accordance with user permitted under Master Plan - Thus, it was not necessary to amend Master Plan - Order accordingly.
