Depot Manager, A. P. S. R. T. Corporation v Ramulu and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Venkataswami, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2038; (1997) 11 SCC 319

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - As a result of an accident property of appellant was damaged and this damage was estimated at Rs. 500, in addition, delinquent driver was subjected to a departmental enquiry on allegation that he had been guilty of rash and negligent driving - Division Bench modified order of learned Single Judge holding that delinquent driver was entitled to full back wages - Whether Division Bench has erred in its decision? - Held, driver had caused pecuniary loss to appellant estimated to be Rs. 500 - It was this pecuniary loss which was ordered to be recovered from delinquent - In addition to that, delinquent was punished for misconduct and it is that punishment with which labour court interfered and so also did learned Judge of High Court - Only order that survived was reimbursement of loss occasioned to appellant on account of act of delinquent driver - It is true that has been shown to be a penalty under regn. 8(v) of Regulations - But penalty for act of negligence was removal from service - Explanation to regn. 8, however, enumerates various penalties which are not to be treated as penalties and one of them is as cl. (5), which says that penalty of recovering loss caused to management under regn. (I)(v) shall not preclude management from imposing any other penalty - HC was, therefore, wrong in thinking that this was a case of double jeopardy - Appeal allowed.
