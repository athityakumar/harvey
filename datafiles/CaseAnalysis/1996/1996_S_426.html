State of Bihar v Sri Rajendra Agrawalla

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4034; (1996) 8 SCC 164; (1996) SCC (Cr) 628; AIR 1996 SCW 591; 1996 (1) Crimes 21; 1996 CRLJ 1372; JT 1996 (1) SC 601; 1996 (4) KarLJ 59; 1996(1) SCALE 394; 1996 (2) SCJ 76; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 414 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Inherent powers - Cognizance of offence - Appreciation of evidence - Held, if charge-sheet and FIR filed in case in hand are examined and impugned order of High Court is tested, conclusion becomes irresistible that HC exceeded its jurisdiction by trying to appreciate evidence and coming to a conclusion that no offence is made out - HC wholly unjustified in invoking its inherent power u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 to quash cognizance taken inasmuch as allegation in FIR and material referred to in charge-sheet do make out an offence u/s. 414 of IPC, 1860 so far as respondent is concerned - Impugned order of HC quashed - Appeal allowed.
