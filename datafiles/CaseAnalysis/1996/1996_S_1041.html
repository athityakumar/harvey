Anugrah Narain Singh and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2528; (1996) 6 SCC 303; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 477; JT 1996 (8) SC 733; 1996(7) SCALE 56; 1996 (3) SCJ 335; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 719; 1997 (1) Supreme 310; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 388

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Muncipal Corporation Adhiniyam, s. 32 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 243-ZGm and 243-Q - (A) Whether in terms of art. 243-ZG of the Constitution, 1950, there is complete and absolute bar in considering any matter relating to municipal election on any ground whatsoever after the publication of the notification for holding municipal election? - (B) Whether under the art. 243-ZG of the Constitution, 1950, writ jurisdiction of the court, could invoked to stall the election process? - (C) Whether court should intervene even when the elections were imminent? - No - (D) Whether the survey made by the State Govt. to ascertain the figures of persons belonging to Backward Classes was lawful or not can be raised in any Court? - No, held - The Court on no account should have directed postponement of the elections by the impugned judgment and order D/- 13-11-1995 - On 11-10-1995, the notification for holding the municipal elections was issued - 16-10-1995 to 20-10-1995 was the period during which the nomination papers could be filed - 24-10-1995 was the last date for withdrawal of nomination papers - Voting was to take place between 17-11-1995 to 20-11-1995 - The writ petition was filed as late as 26-10-1995 on the allegation that there were defects in the electoral rolls, delimitation of constituencies and reservation of seats - A similar writ petition moved before the Lucknow Bench of the Allahabad High Court - Dismissed by the Court on 18-10-1995 - One week before the voting was scheduled to commence, the Court decided to intervene in the matter regardless of the repeated warnings given by this Court in a number of earlier decisions - The Court should not intervene even when the elections were imminent - Here, the election was well under way - As the elections have already been held under the interim order passed by this Court, no further direction in this regard is necessary - Appeal allowed.
