New India Assurance Company Limited v Kusumanchi Kameshwara Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1992; (1997) 9 SCC 179; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 343; 1997 (1) CLT 180; JT 1996 (10) SC 779; 1996(8) SCALE 757; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 280; 1997 (2) Supreme 555

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Guarantee Bond, Debt Laws

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Guarantee bond - Dissolution deed - Liability - Respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 2 entered Deed - Respondent no. 2 agreed and undertook to pay to respondent no. 1 sum of Rs. 168,499.32 being amount settled to be due to respondent no. 1, and also agreed to furnish guarantee bond from appellant for due payment of Rs. 1,68,499.32 - Accordingly appellant agreed to execute guarantee bond in favour of appellant for amount of Rs. 1,25,000/- on certain terms - Respondent no. 1 contended that as respondent no. 2 failed to perform terms of agreement respondent no. 1 demanded guarantee amount of Rs. 1,25,000 from appellant by registered notice - As it was not complied with, respondent no. 1 filed suit for recovery of Rs. 1,25,000/- against appellant and respondent no. 2 - Appellant contended that allegation that it had executed guarantee bond under which it agreed to pay Rs. 1,25,000/- to respondent no. 1 or such lesser amount as had been demanded by respondent no. 1 on failure of respondent no. 2 was not true - Trial Judge passed impugned order which respondent no. 1 challenged in appeal - Division Bench found that surety bond covered liability undertaken by Dissolution Deed and as that liability was not discharged by respondent no. 2, respondent no. 1 was entitled to decree also against appellant and accordingly decreed suit also against appellant - Decree against appellant resulted in appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant had given any guarantee to meet liability of respondent no. 2 qua respondent no. 1 arising from Deed of Dissolution and whether guarantee bond covered obligations of respondent no. 2 qua respondent no. 1 as flowing from Dissolution Deed - Held, guarantee bond had nothing to do with the liability of respondent no. 2 under Dissolution Deed and that liability was not secured and no guarantee was given by predecessor-in-interest qua that liability of respondent no. 2 pursuant to said bond - It was not stated that respondent no. 2, as retiring partner had undertaken liability under Dissolution Deed to pay amount that had fallen due to respondent no. 1 from Deed from respondent no. 2 when firm was dissolved - It was found that appellant or its predecessor had not given any guarantee to cover liability of respondent no. 2 to extent of Rs. 1,25,000/- flowing from Dissolution Deed - Hence, judgment and decree passed by Division Bench of HC against appellant was quashed and set aside and suit of respondent no. 1 was quashed and set aside and suit of respondent no. 1 against appellant was dismissed and decree of dismissal of suit against appellant as passed by Trial Court was restored - Pending appeal, amount was deposited by appellant in Trial Court , if that amount had been withdrawn by respondent no. 1, it was to be refunded to appellant, who was entitled to withdraw said amount along with total accrued interest on such invested amount - No order as to costs - Appeal allowed.
