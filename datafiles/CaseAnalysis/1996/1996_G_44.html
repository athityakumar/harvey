Commissioner of Income Tax v Gemini Pictures Circuit Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3497; (1996) 4 SCC 216; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 421; AIR 1996 SC 1522; 1996 (132) CTR 256; [1996] 220 ITR 43; JT 1996 (3) SC 665; 1996(3) SCALE 197; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1008; 1996 (3) Supreme 330; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 594; 1996 (2) TLR 449

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 45 - (A) Whether, lands sold during year of account was not 'agricultural land in India' during year of assessment and, hence, not liable to be excluded from definition of words 'capital asset'? - (B) Whether surplus realised on sale of land in year of account is not exempt from capital? - Held, no other conclusion is reasonably possible in facts of present case - All three authorities under Act too arrived at same conclusion - Thus, court finds their conclusion wholly unsustainable and unacceptable - Therefore, judgment of High Court is set aside and two questions referred u/s. 256(1) are answered in favour of Revenue and against assessee - Appeal allowed.
