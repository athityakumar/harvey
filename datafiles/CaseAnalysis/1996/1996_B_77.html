Basant Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3107; (1998) 8 SCC 589; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1548

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 125(2) - Constitutional validity of s. 125(2) of CrPC - Application of respondent - wife u/s. 125 of CrPC - Maintenance was granted to her at rate of Rs. 500 per month from date of application - Order was challenged before High Court by appellant - husband as being contrary to provisions of s. 125(2) of CrPC - Statutory provisions are to be assumed to be constitutional, that constitutionality is to be considered only where absolutely necessary, that a statute cannot be struck down unless notice has been given to Attorney General in case of a Central statute, as here, or Advocate General in case of a State statute - Held, it would be in consonance with justice that entire judgment and order that is impugned should be set aside and criminal revision application restored to file of HC for being decided afresh without reference to impugned judgment - Judgment set aside insofar as it holds that s. 125(2) is unconstitutional - Appeal allowed.
