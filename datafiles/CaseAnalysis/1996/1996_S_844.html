Anil Alias Andya Sadashiv Nandoskar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3267; (1996) SCC (Cr) 356; (1996) 2 SCC 589; AIR 1996 SC 2943; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 491; JT 1996 (3) SC 120; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Rule Of Law, Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 5 and 19 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Appellant convicted by designated judge u/s. 5 - Challenged - Appellant a wanted criminal - Unlicensed country made pistol and cartridges recovered from him - To hold an accused guilty of an offence u/s. 5 of TADA, 1987 prosecution is required to prove satisfactorily that accused was in conscious possession, unauthorisedly, in a notified area, of any arm and ammunition of specified description - Held, evidence of the police officials cannot be termed as interested if it inspires confidence - In present case, police evidence is clear and unshaken - Prosecution satisfactorily established case against appellant - No merits in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
