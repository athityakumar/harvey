Smt Sarla Dixit And Anr. v Balwant Yadav And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 622; (1996) 3 SCC 179; 2004 (1) ACC 396; 1996 ACJ 581; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 13; AIR 1996 SC 1274; 1996 (2) CCC 1; JT 1996 (3) SC 252; 1996 (2) MLJ 55; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; 1996(2) SCALE 802; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 30; 1996 (3) Supreme 72; 1996 (2) UJ 110

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s.110B - Contributory negligence - Collision between truck and scooter - Happened at middle of inter section of two roads - Truck driver novice and without licence - Not able to stop truck there and then after accident - Stopping it only after traveling 70 feet distance - Held, indicating high speed of truck - Scooterist having crossed half of inter-section it cannot be said that truck driver could not have seen him - No contributory negligence - Driver of truck alone guilty of negligence - Multiplier of 15 is proper for determination of compensation.
