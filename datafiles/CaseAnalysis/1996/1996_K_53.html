State of West Bengal and Another v Kailash Chandra Kapur and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2003; (1997) 2 SCC 387; AIR 1997 SC 1348; 1997 (4) CLT 2; JT 1996 (11) SC 515; 1997(1) SCALE 184; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 398; 1997 (1) Supreme 324

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Contract Act, s. 23 - Lease of Govt. land - Testamentary disposition of lease by lessee in favour of a stranger to his family - (A) Whether leasehold interest of tenant can be bequeathed by testamentary disposition? - (B) Whether landlord is not bound by it? - (C) Whether bequest of Govt. land in favour of stranger is illegal as it was against public policy? - (A) Held, may be bequeathed by testamentary disposition - (B) Landlord however not bound by it - (C) it cannot be held to be illegal though it was against public policy as object of assignment of Govt. in favour of lessee was to provide him right of residence - Appeal dismissed.
