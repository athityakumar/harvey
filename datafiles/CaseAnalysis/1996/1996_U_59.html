Surjeet Singh Chhabra v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1400; (1997) SCC (Cr) 272; (1997) 1 SCC 508; AIR 1997 SC 2560; 1997 (34) AllCriC 154; 1997 (68) ECR 29; 1997 (89) E.L.T. 646; JT 1996 (10) SC 239; 1996 (17) RLT 331; 1996(8) SCALE 287; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 111 - Constitution, art.14 - Customs Duty - Confiscation of gold jewellery - Availability of opportunity to accused to cross examine Panch witnesses - When accused confessing that he purchased gold and converted it into article of jewellery and brought it - Not violative of principle of natural justice and moreover accused retracting confession - Plea of article being exempted - Held, immaterial as confession though retracted, binds accused - Held, accused, in view of admission , accused is not entitled to benefit of exemption - Petition dismissed.
