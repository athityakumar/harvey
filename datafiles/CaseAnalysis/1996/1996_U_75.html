M. C. Mehta [Calcutta Tanneries Matter] v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1556; (1997) 2 SCC 411; 1997 (1) CLT 85; JT 1997 (1) SC 221; 1996(9) SCALE 397; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Degradation, Water (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Amendment Act, 1988, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Tanneries

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25B - Relocation of tanneries - Control on pollution - After enactment of Act, more than one hundred tanneries applied for consent to State Pollution Control Board - On non-construction of effluent treatment plant, State Board issued show-cause notices asking why factory may not be directed to be closed for not putting up right type of effluent treatment plant as per order - More than 280 tanneries submitted undertaking whereby Court agreed with opinion of Board order for relocation of tanneries - Hence, instant Petition - Whether, the impugned order of court in imposing penalty on tanneries and removal of those tanneries is legally sustainable.

Held, on review of proposed CETP schemes for tannery wastewater management and after consultants with inspection team notes that proposed schemes are neither scientifically sound, nor can be constructed on existing locations without interfering with normal life of residents areas. Further, proposed CETP schemes are not capable of treating wastewater laden with high total dissolved solids, chromium and nitrogenous constituents. Thus, proposed CETP designs connote control pollution and odour in totality at tannery. However, proposed designs have little scientific basis, and do not consider industry specific requirements of effective wastewater treatment in tannery clusters. Technical Report by surveyor indicating that new leather complex does not fall within area of wetland has also been attached along with affidavit of Principal Secretary and site plan enclosed with affidavit clearly shows that leather complex is outside boundary of wetland. No material to contrary has been place on record by Calcutta tanneries. Therefore, monitoring done by Court towards relocation of Calcutta tanneries steps to facilitate relocation has been undertaken. Hence, tanneries which are not closed must relocate and closure order with effect from 30-9-1997 shall be unconditional. Petition disposed of. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - One who pollutes the environment must pay to reverse the damage caused by his acts.
