Indian Shaving Products Limited v Board of Industrial and Financial Reconstruction

Supreme Court of India

03 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3495; (1996) 1 SCC 683; AIR 1996 SC 960; 1996 BC 478; [1996] 87 Comp Cas 589; [1996] 218 ITR 140; JT 1996 (1) SC 1; 1996(1) SCALE 57; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 31; 1996 (2) TLR 229

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 72A - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 32(2) - Amalgamation of sick industrial Co. with another Co. - Impugned order of appellate authority upheld order of BIFR by which benefit of s. 72A was not extended to appellant upon amalgamation - Held, conditions for sanctioning a scheme u/s. 18 of said Act being same as those required for a declaration u/s. 72A of Income Tax Act, BIFR could not have sanctioned scheme of amalgamation of sharp edge with appellant but declined to make declaration u/s. 72A with regard to that amalgamation - Thus, order under appeal as also order of BIFR declining to make a declaration u/s. 72A in respect of amalgamation with appellant - Appeal allowed.
