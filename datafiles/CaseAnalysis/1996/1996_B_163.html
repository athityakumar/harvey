Deputy Commissioner v Board of Trustees of the Visakhapatnam Port Trust

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4145; (2003) 10 SCC 247

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Tax assessment - Liability - Notice was given by appellant calling upon the respondents to show cause why it should not be subjected to tax in respect of A/y 1970-71 to 1974-75 - Appellant approached the HC straight away by way of a writ petition contending that since it was not a 'dealer' as defined in the Act, it was not liable to pay sales tax - DB of HC upheld the said contention - Whether the Board of Trustees of Port Trust were liable to pay sales tax on the sale of unserviceable material or surplus material as well as on supply of some other goods to ships and others - Held, having considered the reasoning in SC judgments, HC was of the opinion that these matters ought to be placed before a Bench comprising three Judges for an authoritative pronouncement on the said question which was frequently arising in several matters all over the country - Papers might accordingly be placed before Chief Justice of India for orders regarding posting of these matters before a Bench of three Judges - District Controller of Stores, N. Rly. v. Asstt. Commercial Taxation Officer, 1975 Indlaw SC 83, State of Gujarat v. Raipur Manufacturing Co. Ltd. 1966 Indlaw SC 297 and State of T.N. v. Burmah Shell Oil Storage and Distributing Co. of India Ltd.1972 Indlaw SC 205, relied on - Order accordingly.
