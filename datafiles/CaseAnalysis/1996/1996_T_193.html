Hindustan Steelworks Construction Limited v Tarapore and Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3600; (1996) 5 SCC 34; AIR 1996 SC 2268; AIR 1996 SCW 2861; 1996 ARBLR 484; 1996 (3) CCC 145; [1996] 87 Comp Cas 344; JT 1996 (6) SC 295; 1996(5) SCALE 186; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Corporate

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, s. 126 - (A) bank guarantee - Nature of - Unconditional bank guarantee - Bank's obligation - If absolute - (1) Whether there is distinction between guarantee for due performance of works contract or towards security deposit? - (2) Whether obligation of Bank remains same? - (3) How to be discharged - Manner - (B) Bank guarantee - Unconditional - Interference by court - Whether cases of fraud or where irretrievable injustice would be caused, if guarantee is allowed to be encashed, exist? - Held, (A) bank's obligation is absolute - (1) No distinction can be drawn - (2) It remains same - (3) Has to be discharged in manner provided in bank guarantee - (B) Such exceptional cases must exist - Appeals allowed.
