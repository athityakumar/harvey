Commissioner of Income Tax v Lakshmi Vilas Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3506; (1996) 8 SCC 458; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 535; AIR 1996 SC 2060; 1996 (1) BC 630; 1996 (133) CTR 139; [1996] 220 ITR 305; JT 1996 (5) SC 141; 1996(4) SCALE 275; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 522; 1996 (4) Supreme 126; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 231

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 28 - Assessment Year 1964-65 and 1965-66 - Whether, appellate tribunal was right in law in holding that certain sum of (Rs. 1,69,996 and Rs. 62,563) in relevant assessment years received as deposits in first instance and forfeited at a later stage, was not income of assessee liable to tax but that assessee was entitled to take them into account in arriving at cost of securities acquired by assessee when these sums were forfeited? - High Court answered question referred to it in affirmative and against department - Held, forfeited amount was bank's income made in course of its banking business and had to be assessed accordingly in year in which it became bank's money - Accrual of income cannot be deferred by adjusting deposit amount against cost of securities - It may have utilised deposits although there is no finding of fact to that effect, as part payment of price of securities - But after its forfeiture, deposit amount became property of bank - Money that was utilised for purchase of securities was bank's money - No question of reduction of cost of securities by adjustment of deposit amount can arise in facts of this case - Thus judgment of HC set aside - Answered negative and in favour of Revenue - Appeal allowed.
