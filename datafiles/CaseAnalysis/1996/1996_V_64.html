Union of India and Others v Vipinchandra Hiralal Shah

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1786; (1996) 6 SCC 721; (1997) SCC (L&S) 41; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 114; 1996 (74) FLR 2752; JT 1996 (9) SC 686; 1997 (1) LLN 1; 1996(7) SCALE 803; 1997 (1) SLJ 69; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 750; 1996 (8) Supreme 127; 1997 (1) UJ 41

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: IAS (Appointment and Promotion) regulations 1955, IAS (Recruitment) Rules 1954

Summary: Administrative - Constitution, - IAS (Appointment and Promotion) regulations, 1955, regns 3 and 5 - IAS (Recruitment) Rules, 1954, r. 9 - Whether it is permissible to club vacancies of a number of years while preparing selection list for promotion to Indian Administrative Service ? Held, Tribunal held that such clubbing of vacancies is not permissible and that separate select lists should be prepared by Selection Committee for each year - Judgment of Tribunal upheld to effect that respondent is entitled to seek fresh consideration on basis that selection should be made for vacancies occurring in each year separately - Directions of Tribunal substituted - Appeal disposed of.
