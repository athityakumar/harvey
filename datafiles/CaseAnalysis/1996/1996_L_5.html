Secretary To Government, Prohibition and Excise Department v L. Srinivasan

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 561; (1996) 3 SCC 157; (1996) SCC (L&S) 686; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 433; 1996 (33) ATC 745; 1996 (1) CLR 752; 1996 (73) FLR 1247; JT 1996 (3) SC 202; 1996 (2) LLJ 245; 1996 (1) LLN 448; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 54; 1996(2) SCALE 411; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 737; 1996 (2) SLR 291; 1996 (2) Supreme 509; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 1918

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Suspension - Offences of embezzlement and fabrication of false records - Tribunal had set aside the departmental enquiry and quashed the charge on the ground of delay in initiation of disciplinary proceedings - Held, Administrative Tribunal has committed grossest error in its exercise of the judicial review - Member had exceeded his power of judicial review in quashing the suspension order and charges even at the threshold - Appeal allowed.
