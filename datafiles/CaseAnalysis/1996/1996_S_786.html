State of Madhya Pradesh v Dhirendra Kumar

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1633; (1997) SCC (Cr) 54; (1997) 1 SCC 93; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 91; AIR 1997 SC 318; 1997 (1) AWC 225; 1997 (1) CCR 58; 1996 (4) Crimes 195; JT 1996 (10) SC 93; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 617; 1996(8) SCALE 233; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 443; 1996 (8) Supreme 272

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act (1 of 1872), Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - (A) Evidence Act (1 of 1872)9 - Motive - Evidence as to - Reliability of - Accused a tenant in house of father-in-law of deceased - Prosecution alleging that fact of accused, having evil eye on deceased led to killing - Family of deceased coming to know about said fact from deceased herself - She reporting said fact to her mother-in-law and mother - law in turn, reporting to her husband who asked accused to vacate premises - Though mother - law stated that she spoke about it to her husband fifteen days before occurrence, father-in- -law stating as to his wife stating it to him 7 - 8 days before occurrence - Statement of father-in-law that he had asked accused to vacate house not challenged in cross-examination - Whether said little discrepancy about number of days warrants discarding otherwise consistent evidence of witnesses? - Whether omission of mother-in-law to tell during investigation as to her having asked her husband to get house vacated is enough to disbelieve her statement? - Whether motive was proved? - (B) Evidence Act, s. 3 - Murder - Appreciation of evidence - Eye witness - Reliability - Mother of deceased and neighbours gathering after firing of shot and before accused escaped - Accused having revolver and threatening to kill all members of family of deceased - Whether failure of persons present to apprehend accused causes dent to evidence of mother? - (C) Evidence Act, s. 32 - Dying declaration - Mother-in-law of deceased after hearing cries of deceased in a position to reach upstairs within 5-6 minutes - Autopsy surgeon opining as to deceased being able to speak for 10-15 minutes - Whether High Court's view that deceased being not in a position to talk could not have made dying declaration tenable - (D) CrPC, s. 154 - FIR - Value of - Facts deposed to by witnesses not stated in FIR - Whether some omission in FIR requires disbelieving of evidence - Non-mention of fact of making of dying declaration - Whether statement of witnesses present at that point can be disbelieved as to giving of it especially when some received corroboration by recovery of revolver from accused - Fact deposed to by witness forming material part of prosecution case - Said fact not mentioned in FIR - Whether same may be borne in mind while deciding about credibility of evidence given by witness? - (E) Evidence Act, s. 57 - Taking of judicial notice - Judicial notice can be taken of fact that many a time prescribed registers are not available and, so, they are kept in non - prescribed way - Many a time even a case diary is not kept in prescribed way - (F) Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Sentence - Mitigating circumstances - Death sentence awarded by Trial Court - Accused however acquitted by High Court in 1982 - Accused having enjoyed acquittal - Whether death sentence could be imposed? - (A) Said little discrepancy does not warrant discarding otherwise consistent evidence - Not enough to disbelieve her statement - Motive held, was proved - (B) Failure of persons present to apprehend accused cannot cause dent to evidence of mother as nobody could have risked his life - (C) Not tenable - (D) Some omission in FIR does not require disbelieving of evidence - Statement of witnesses cannot be disbelieved - Credibility of evidence by witness affected if fact deposed is not mentioned in FIR - (E) Judicial notice taken of fact that prescribed registers and even a case diary kept in non - prescribed way - (F) Could not be imposed despite murder deserving death sentence because of acquittal enjoyed by convict after HC's judgment

Appeal Allowed.
