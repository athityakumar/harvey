N Re: Suo Motu Contempt Petition No 10 Of 1996 In Writ Petition Civil 239 Of 1993 v Na

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service; Tort

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Court - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Issuance of contempt notices in writ petition in case Khedut Mazdoor Chetna Sangath v State of MP - Khedut Mazdoor Chetna Sangath, a registered trade union of tribals - Working for upliftment of the tribals - Union in agitation against construction of Sardar Sarovar Dam on river Narmada on the ground that the construction of the Dam would be prejudicial to the interests of the tribals residing in the catchment area of the Dam since their lands would be submerged in water and they would be displaced - Arrests made against agitators - Handcuffing and parading in street of undertial prisoners while commuting between jail and court - Relied on Prem Shankar Shukla v Delhi Administration; Sunil Gupta v State of MP ; Baradakanta Mishra, Ex-Commissioner of Endowments v Bhimsen Dixit' - A prima facie case is made out for taking contempt action against persons responsible - Show cause notice issued - Regulation 465 of MP Police Regulations, requires an express authorization from the Magistrate/Jail Officer for the purpose of taking him to court from jail and from jail to court - Admittedly, no such authorisation was obtained in this case - Regulations undisputedly violated - Question is, though handcuffing of the members of the Sangath who were undertrial prisoners was not justified and was in clear disregard of the law laid by this court, whether the said actions of the contemners in handcuffing, constitute contempt of this court? - Court took cases of contemners No. 1 to 5 first - Contempt of court is disobedience to the court, by acting in opposition to the authority, justice and dignity thereof - If signifies a willful disregard or disobedience of the court's order; it also signifies such conduct as tends to bring the authority of the court and the administration of law into disrepute - Willful disregard or disobedience of the court's order presupposes awareness of the order that has been disregarded or disobeyed - Affidavit of contemner 1 to 5 stating that they were not aware of law laid down in Prem Shankar's case and in Sunil Gupta's case - Held, action to punish for contempt, not called for - Disapproval of court, in unauthorised handcuffing and violation of provision of Police Regulations, recorded and direction issued for a note regarding such disapproval of their conduct to be placed in the personal file of all contemners - Further directions issued to Chief Secy., M P Govt. to ensure that suitable steps are taken to amend the MP Police Regulations in the light of the law laid down by this court in Prem Shankar Shukla's case and proper guidelines are issued for the guidance of the police personnel in this regard - Contempt proceedings disposed of accordingly - With direction for sending the copy of order to Govt. of MP - It is not disputed that no orders were obtained from the concerned Magistrate with regard to handcuffing of the prisoners before taking them to court from jail and to the jail from the court - Handcuffing of the members of the Sangath who were under trial prisoners, was, therefore, not justified and was in clear disregard of the law laid down by this court in the decisions referred to above - A serious lepse on the part of the contemner in the discharge of his duties as a judicial officer who is expected to ensure that the basic human rights of the citizens are not violated - Keeping in view that the contemner is a young judicial officer, it is refrained from imposing punishment on him - Recording disapproval of his conduct and direct that a note of this disapproval by this Court shall be kept in the personal file of the contemner - Contempt notices issued against the contemners are discharged subject to the directions regarding disapproval of the conduct of contemners Nos 1 to 5 and 7 and directions regarding placing the note of the said disapproval in the personal files of all of them - Petition disposed of.
