State Bank of India and Others v Kashinath Kher and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3344; (1996) 8 SCC 762; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1117; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 270; AIR 1996 SC 1328; JT 1996 (2) SC 569; 1996 (2) LLJ 654; 1996 (2) LLN 1281; 1996(2) SCALE 423; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 324; 1996 (2) SLR 282; 1996 (2) Supreme 544; 1996 (1) UJ 691; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 638

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - State Bank of India Act, 1955, s. 43 - State Bank of India Officers (Determination of Terms and Conditions of Service) Order 1979, r. 17 - Promotion - Bank officers - Eligibility criteria - Exposure to peculiar type of work and work experience in rural branches - Many officers have no such exposure/experience because of non-availability of posts without their fault - Decision to promote them as and when they fulfilled eligibility criteria preserving their seniority - Held, not unreasonable.
