The Scheduled Castes And Scheduledtribes Officers Welfare Co v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2446; (1997) SCC (L&S) 194; (1997) 1 SCC 701; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 249; AIR 1997 SC 1451; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 596; 1997 LabIC 423; 1997 (4) LLN 88; 1996(8) SCALE 22; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 544

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 309 - Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995 - Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955 - Promotions - Merit - Colorable exercise of power - Respondent/Medical Health Dept. in deviation of general principle of seniority, subject to grounds of unfitness, introduced merit and ability to fill up post of Chief Medical officer, without obtaining prior approval - All appointments and promotions were made u/art. 309 of Constitution on ad hoc basis without statutory rules and procedure - Petitioner/Association sought to quash memo and for declaration that promotions made within cadre should be on basis of seniority subject to rejection on ground of unfitness - Whether appointments and promotion made on adhoc basis were in abuse of colorable exercise of power - Held, Officer from General category were promoted, when general candidates were not available for promotion - Appointments and promotions was in abuse of colorable exercise of power creating disharmony in policy and resorting to victimization of eligible officers - Mala fide exercise of power by respondent - Court had no hesitation to quash offending memo issued by respondents and they were directed to consider cases of petitioners if found eligible, to promote them or any of other eligible officers - Petition allowed.
