Sita Ram Bansal and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1974; (1997) 9 SCC 250; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1245; AIR 1997 SC 2341; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 44; 1997 (2) LLJ 681; 1997 (2) LLN 1005; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Dispute regarding cut-off date for entitlement of pensionary benefits - Petitioners are non-provincialised employees working in notified Municipal Committees - Govt. introduced pension scheme applicable to All India Gazetted Officers and Punjab Civil Services Officers working in municipalities w.e.f. 1-4-1990 and subsequently, extended to non-provincialised employees - Petitions filed on ground that prescription of a cut-off date was arbitrary and denying them benefit of pensionary scheme is violative of art. 14 - Held, date bears rationality, namely, scheme for first time was introduced on that date - All those employees who retired prior to that date were treated as a class and those employees either in service or retiring on and after that date have been treated as a separate class and scheme was extended to it - Thus, there is no illegality in introducing cut-off date, nor does it violate art. 14 - Petitions dismissed.
