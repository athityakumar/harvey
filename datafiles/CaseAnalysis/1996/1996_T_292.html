Sri Samir Sobhan Sanyal v Tracks Trade Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 720; (1996) 4 SCC 144; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 553; AIR 1996 SC 2102; AIR 1996 SCW 2539; 1996 (3) CCC 65; JT 1996 (5) SC 74; 1997 (1) RCJ 101; 1996 (2) RCR(Rent) 82; 1996(4) SCALE 266; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 397; 1996 (5) Supreme 237; 1996 (2) UJ 321

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution of India, art.21; Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s.47, o.21, rr.98 and 99 - Dispossession of tenant without due process of law - Agreement to sale - Transaction entered into with knowledge that tenant was in possession of premises - Vendee obtaining decree for possession - Execution of - No independent eviction suit against tenant - Nor tenant impleaded as party to suit - Tenant filed suit regarding his right to remain in possession - Meanwhile dispossession of tenant by transferee - Held, owner without due process of law - Illegal.
