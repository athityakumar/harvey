State Bank of India Scheduled Caste / Tribe Employees Welfare Association v State Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2845; (1996) 4 SCC 119; (1996) SCC (L&S) 911; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 442; AIR 1996 SC 1838; JT 1996 (4) SC 547; 1996 LabIC 1612; 1996 (1) LLN 814; 1996(3) SCALE 799; 1996 (2) SLJ 19; 1996 (2) SLR 682; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 719; 1996 (4) Supreme 295

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16(4) - Reservation policy - Validity of - Whether reservation policy framed by State Bank of India reserving certain posts for employees belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes on promotion at stage of from 'Award Cadre' to Junior Management Grade Cadre is fair and constitution? - Held, any relaxation granted in favour of Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe employees in respect of service norms is a matter of policy - Undoubtedly, art. 16(4) enables Govt. to make reservations for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes either at initial stage of recruitment or at stage of promotion - Reservation has been made by State Bank of India at promotional stage in manner which is set out in two circulars - These circulars have been applied at relevant times - Circular of 28-07-1983 gave a further relaxation in service norms exceeding relaxation prescribed in first circular of 03-04-1978 - As a result, service norms for reserved category could be relaxed to 5 years of service - Circular also gave power to relax service norms even further, but with sanction of Central Office of State Bank of India - This is not a relaxation which can be applied retrospectively - It becomes available from date when it is granted and is not a matter of right - It would, therefore, be very difficult to relate back relief on basis of second circular of 28-07-1983 to years 1978 to 1983 - Reserved vacancies in promotional posts during this period, if not filled, lapsed after three years as per scheme framed under earlier circular of 3 - Provision for relaxation under circular of 28-07-1983 thus adequately safeguards interests of Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe employees and provides a fair opportunity to them for filling up reserved vacancies as they arise - Appeal and writ petition dismissed.
