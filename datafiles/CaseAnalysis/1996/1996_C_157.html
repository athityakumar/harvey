J. K. Synthetics Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4044; (1996) 6 SCC 92; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 152; AIR 1996 SC 3527; 1997 (142) CTR 521; 1996 (66) ECR 417; 1996 (86) E.L.T. 472; JT 1996 (7) SC 674; 1996 (16) RLT 214; 1996(6) SCALE 299; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 295; 1996 (6) Supreme 632

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Customs Act, 1962, s. 156 - Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1982, r. 41, 20 and 21 - Powers of Tribunal to pass ex parte order in appeal - Tribunal can set aside on sufficient cause being shown - Rule 20 of CEGAT deals with cases where appellant has defaulted - Rule 21 of CEGAT gives power to hear ex parte appeals - Rule 41 gives CEGAT wide powers to prevent abuse of its process or, most significantly, to secure ends of justice - Held, CEGAT has, power to set aside an order passed ex parte against respondent before it is found that respondent had been unable to appear due to sufficient cause - Appeal allowed.
