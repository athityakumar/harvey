Gangabai Gopaldas Mohata v Fulchand and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1560; (1997) 10 SCC 387; AIR 1997 SC 1812; AIR 1997 SCW 1162; JT 1997 (1) SC 343; 1997(1) SCALE 1; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 21, r. 2, O. 39, r. 147, O. 21, r. 16 - Maharashtra Municipalities (Sale of Distrained or Attached Movable and Immovable Property) Rules, 1967, r. 8(2) - Municipal Council attached right of appellant in very same property for arrears of municipal tax claimed from appellant - Municipal Council put property to public auction - On application by Appellant Court by its order D/- 24-02-1984 held that sale of buildings of plaintiff might be held by defendant but it should not be confirmed before 15 days after sale and if within period of one month plaintiff deposits entire amount in court, sale will be cancelled and if said amount is not deposited, then sale may be confirmed - Municipal Council thereupon conducted public sale on 27-02-1984 in which present respondent 1 was highest bidder - Appellant deposited 1/4th of bid amount on same day and balance on 9-11-1990 - However, appellant deposited an amount of Rs. 50,000 in court on 13-3-1984 which is more than arrears of tax amount claimed by Municipal Council - Chief Officer of Municipal Council thereupon issued a sale certificate in favour of respondent 1 - Execution court took view that sale certificate issued by Municipal Council was not in accordance with law and hence respondent 1 cannot be substituted as transferee of decree-holder - (A) Whether Execution Court has jurisdiction and what is consequence of order D/- 24-2-1984? - Held, if a person approaches execution court claiming that he is representative of decree-holder's interest in decree and decree-holder disputes it, execution court has to resolve dispute for proceeding with execution of decree - Respondent 1 claims to be a transferee of decree-holder's interest by virtue of sale conducted by Municipal Council - But legal consequence of order of Civil Court D/- 24-2-1984 was that, on depositing tax arrears within time stipulated in order, sale would go off - Merely because suit was subsequently dismissed legal effect of court's direction contained in interlocutory order D/- 24-2-1984 would not become a nullity - Whatever happened during pendency of suit and what all actions were taken pursuant to any orders passed by court during interregnum would remain a reality unless court itself had disturbed or modified them - (B) Would respondent have got rights of appellant by bidding in a sale which was conditioned by court through order D/- 24-2-1984? - Held, sale should have gone off very day appellant had deposited amount as per directions of court - Neither Chief Officer nor even Municipal Council had authority to bypass order of Civil Court by confirming sale on a later day without even obtaining permission from court - (C) Whether balance amount should have been paid by respondent within 15 days? - Held, rr. 8(2) and (3) and r. 13 of Maharashtra Municipalities Act would unmistakably show that requirement to pay balance 75 per cent within 15 days is mandatory - Neither Chief Officer nor even Municipal Council has power to relax or even condone delay and accept a delayed payment - If any officer accepted money after period fixed under sub-r. (2), action of officer has no support of law and no legal effect would flow therefrom - Sale consequently becomes void - Here sale was conducted on 27-2-1984 - Bidder who made his initial deposit of Rs. 25,000 did not pay balance within period of 15 days i.e. 13 - Instead payment was made only on 9-11-1990 which was 6 years 8 months and 12 days after sale - Sale of 27-2-1984 would, therefore, stand annulled - Further, respondent did not legally acquire interest of decree-holder in property - Respondent is, therefore, not a transferee 'by operation of law' as envisaged under O. 21, r. 16 of Code - Appeal dismissed.
