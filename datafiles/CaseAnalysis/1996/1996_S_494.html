National Council For Cement Andbuilding Materials v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 555; (1996) 3 SCC 206; (1996) SCC (L&S) 687; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 594; 1996 (2) CLR 234; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 508; 1996 (2) LLJ 125; 1996 (2) LLN 49; 1996(2) SCALE 371; 1996 (2) SCJ 346; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 689; 1996 (2) SLR 482; 1996 (2) Supreme 562; 1996 (1) UJ 719

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, s. 10(4) - Industrial employment - Preliminary objection raised in the written statement - It was not an 'Industry' therefore, no reference could be made to the Tribunal - Tribunal directed that the preliminary issues as well as the other main issues be considered later on - (A) Whether the appalling situation created by such preliminary issues which take long years to settle as the decision of the Tribunal on the preliminary issue is immediately challenged in one or the other forum including the High Court and proceedings in the reference are stayed which continue to lie dormant till, the matter relating to the preliminary issue is finally disposed of? - Held, the grievance that the Industrial Tribunal, having once decided to hear the matter as a preliminary issue, could not change its mind and decide to hear that issue along with other issues on merits - Appeal dismissed.
