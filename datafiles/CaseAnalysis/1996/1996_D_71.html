K. Sankaran Nair (Dead) Through Lrs v Devaki Amma Malathy Amma and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2483; (1996) 11 SCC 428; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 669; JT 1996 (8) SC 664; 1996(7) SCALE 292; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 839; 1996 (7) Supreme 282

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Improvements, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, ss. 6-C and 74 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976 - Whether original appellant-defendant 2 was entitled to again claim benefit of deemed tenancy as per s. 6-C of Act? - Held, as per provisions of s. 74 of Act no tenancies could be created after 01-04-1964 and thus there was total bar to creation of such tenancies - Consequently appellant's claim to be a tenant of land as per registered lease deed dated 10-01-1969 had stood repelled up to this court - In order to infuse life in such void leases s. 6-C was brought on Statute - Book by legislature - It is of course true that s. 6-C starts with a non obstante clause and recites that notwithstanding anything contained in s. 74, or in any contract, or in any judgment, decree or order of any court or other authority, any person in occupation at commencement of Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969, of land of another person on basis of a lease deed executed after 1964 shall be deemed to be a tenant if he satisfies conditions (a) and (b) mentioned in said section - For applicability of this section person concerned who claims deemed tenancy status must be in occupation at commencement of Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969, that is, on 1970 - It is now well settled that legislature cannot overrule any judicial decision without removing substratum or foundation of that judgment by a retrospective amendment of legal provision concerned - Legislature in its wisdom did not think it fit, while bringing on Statute - Book s. 6-C from 07-1979, to either give it retrospective effect from 1970, or to delete s. 74 retrospectively at least from 1970 if not from an earlier date of 01-04-1964 when parent Act itself was brought on Statute-Book - Consequently non obstante clause introduced in s. 6-C for bypassing final judgments, decrees or orders of any court against any person remained in realm of an abortive or an incompetent exercise on part of legislature - It must be held that s. 6-C could not be pressed into service by original appellant for displacing binding judgments rendered by tribunal, High Court and this court in earlier tenancy proceedings wherein his claim for tenancy of suit land came to be repelled and those judgments have become final and binding and were not in any way legally displaced by any competent piece of legislation by Kerala Legislature - Those judgments remained fully operative against appellant and consequently on principle of res judicata appellant could not once again reagitate question about his tenancy - Appeal dismissed.
