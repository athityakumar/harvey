Secretary To Government and Others v A. C.J. Britto

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1554; (1997) 3 SCC 387; (1997) SCC (L&S) 813; AIR 1997 SC 1393; 1997 (2) CalLT(SC) 86; 1997 (2) ESC 1028; JT 1996 (11) SC 525; 1997 LabIC 1528; 1997 (2) LLJ 388; 1997(1) SCALE 21; 1997 (1) SLR 732; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 441; 1997 (1) Supreme 467

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950 - Tamil Nadu Police Subordinate Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, r. 3(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Respondent, sub-inspector of police transferred and posted at another place dismissed from service - Respondent was continuously absent from service on ground of health problems - Deputy superintendent of police directed respondent to appear before medical board - Held, reasons for non- appearance given by respondent was false excuse showing his intention to wilfully flout order - Act of respondent in disobeying legitimate order of his superior amounts to misconduct - Copies of certain documents which delinquent wanted to pursue was not relevant to charge therefore it cannot be said he was deprived of reasonable opportunity of defending himself - Appeal allowed.
