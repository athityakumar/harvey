R. C. Jain v High Court of Patna and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2329; (1996) 10 SCC 5; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 744; AIR 1996 SCW 3714; 1996 (4) CLT 2; JT 1996 (7) SC 442; 1996(6) SCALE 144; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 179; 1996 (6) Supreme 420

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Extension of service - Appeal - Posting of case - Appellant's service was not extended beyond 58 years by HC - Special Leave Petition (SLP) filed by the appellant alleged that the Letters Patent Appeal (LPA) was filed without the permission of the Chief Justice of the HC and Registrar General and certain other officers of the Registry of the HC were inimically disposed towards him - LPA was based on the misrepresentation contained in the notes of Registrar (Inspection) - An erroneous impression was given to the Chief Justice that the appellant filed the LPA against the decision of the Single Judge - Alleged that certain directions were given by the Chief Justice which had resulted in manifest injustice to him - Whether, an erroneous impression was given to the Chief Justice that the appellant had filed the LPA against the decision of the Single Judge which had resulted the Chief Justice to give certain directions in the matter - Held, that Chief justice had directed the Registry to file an LPA through the Registrar against the judgment of the Single Judge - Registrar (Inspection), by office note, sought direction of the Chief Justice regarding the posting of the case - Chief Justice was aware at that time that LPA was filed against the judgment of the Single Judge as per his orders and that earlier he had directed the posting of the case before the DB and further direction was sought since the constitution of the Benches had changed - Chief Justice of HC had directed the filing of the LPA and the subsequent inadvertent and mistaken noting of the Registrar (Inspection) had nothing to do with the direction of the Chief Justice in posting the case before the Bench - Allegations made in the SLP regarding the then Registrar General and also the officers of the Registry of HC were absolutely unwarranted and unjustified - Appeal was filed and the hearing of the same from time to time by the different DBs of the HC and the disposal thereof had been done in the normal and routine way, and therefore they were not open to any attack in the SLP - Appeal dismissed.
