Ajit Singh Januja and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3762; (1996) SCC (L&S) 540; (1996) 2 SCC 715; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 597; AIR 1996 SC 1189; AIR 1996 SCW 1196; 1996 (33) ATC 239; 1996 (1) CLR 96; JT 1996 (2) SC 727; 1996 LabIC 1030; 1996 (2) LLJ 154; 1996 (2) LLN 65; 1996(2) SCALE 526; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 125; 1996 (2) SLR 71; 1996 (2) Supreme 436; 1996 (2) UJ 81; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 658

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Consideration Of Seniority

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 335 - Promotion - General category post in higher grade - Scheduled Castes/Backward Classes candidate appointed/promoted to lower grade post on basis of reservation and system of roster - Held, cannot claim promotion to higher grade general post on basis of seniority in lower grade achieved because of accelerated promotion/appointment - Roster does not survive once prescribed percentage of reserved posts are filled.
