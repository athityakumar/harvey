Collector of Customs, Calcutta v Tin Plate Company of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2113; (1997) 10 SCC 538; 1996 (87) E.L.T. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 15, 28 and 46 - Commercial - (A) Whether in the case of suppression of facts the proviso would be attracted and the period of limitation would stand extended to five years? - (B) Whether those two demand notices were within the time? - Yes, held - The proviso to s. 28(1) extends the period of limitation to five years in certain cases of collusion, willful misstatement or suppression of facts by the assessee - The two demand notices were concerned they were beyond the period of limitation prescribed by s. 28(1)(b) of the Customs Act, the Deputy Collector of Customs issued a notice D/- 22-7-1985 in which for the first time the allegation in regard to suppression of facts came to be made - On the basis of that notice, it was stated that the two demand notices were within time - If action is contemplated u/s. 28(l)(h), it is incumbent on the part of the Department to serve the assessee a notice requiring him to show cause why he should not pay the amount specified therein - This requirement is in keeping with the principle of natural justice - Indisputably, no show cause notice was issued to the assessee before the two demand notices were issued - The element of deliberate or conscious omission had not been established and, therefore, even on that count it did not uphold the department's contention - Appeal dismissed.
