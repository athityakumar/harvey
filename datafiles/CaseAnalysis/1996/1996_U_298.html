Union of India v S. S. Uppal and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4084; (1996) 2 SCC 168; (1996) SCC (L&S) 438; AIR 1996 SC 2340; 1996 (32) ATC 668; 1996 (72) FLR 549; JT 1996 (1) SC 258; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 230; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 991

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - All India Services Act, 1951 - Indian Administrative Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1987 - Seniority - Assignment of 'year of allotment' - Whether amended rule or earlier rule determine seniority on basis of year of allotment ? - Seniority has to be determined by rules in force on date of appointment to IAS - Fixation of seniority in IAS follows appointment to service - Year of Allotment in IAS will have to be determined according to provisions of seniority rules which are in force at time of his appointment - Date of occurrence of vacancy has really no relevance for purpose of fixation of seniority in IAS - Held, respondent's seniority to be determined on date of appointment - Central Govt. is competent to amend seniority rules from time to time keeping in view exigencies of administration - Further held, mere inclusion of name in panel does not confer any right of appointment - State Govt. and officer concerned had to go through some formalities - No ordinate delay - No infraction of any rule or violation of any instruction issued by Govt. in determining seniority - Appeal allowed.
