Tahir v State (Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4085; (1996) 3 SCC 338; (1996) SCC (Cr) 515; AIR 1996 SC 3079; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 386; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 5 - Arms Act, s. 27 - Appellant convicted u/s. 5 TADA - Conviction recorded on evidence of police officials - Appeal - No rule of law or evidence which lays down that conviction cannot be recorded on evidence of police officials, if found reliable, unless corroborated by some independent evidence - Where evidence of police officials, after careful scrutiny, inspires confidence and is found to be trustworthy and reliable, it can form basis of conviction and absence of some independent witness of locality to lend corroboration to their evidence does not in any way affect creditworthiness of prosecution case - Held, no infirmity attaches to testimony of police officials, merely because they belong to police force - Evidence of police officials are trustworthy witnesses and their evidence suffers from no infirmity whatsoever - Further held, in Sanjay Dutt v State of Maharashtra 1994 Indlaw SC 1008 it has been laid that mere conscious possession of an unlicensed fire arm, which answers description of an arm under Arms Act, without any licence, in a Notified Area attracts punishment u/s. 5 of TADA and an accused shall, notwithstanding anything contained in any other law for time being in force - In view of established facts on record, law laid down Sanjay Dutt's case, fully applies to case of appellant - Conviction of appellant well merited - Appeal dismissed.
