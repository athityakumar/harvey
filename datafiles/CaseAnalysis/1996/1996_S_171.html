Sarvinder Singh v Dalip Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2093; (1996) 5 SCC 539; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 546; 1996 (4) CCC 20; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 429; 1996(6) SCALE 59; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 271; 1996 (7) Supreme 210

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, r. 1, O. 1, r. 10 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Respondents claimed right, title and interest pursuant to registered sale deeds said to have been executed by defendants-heirs pending suit - Whether respondents are said to be necessary party to suit? - Foundation for exclusive right, title and interest in property, subject matter of suit, is founded upon registered Will executed by mother of appellant - Held, foundation for relief of declaration in second suit is registered Will executed in favour of appellant - Respondents indisputably cannot challenge legality or validity of Will - Respondents cannot be said to be either necessary or proper parties to suit in which primary relief was found on basis of registered Will executed by appellant's mother - Further held, defendants in suit were prohibited by operation of s. 52 to deal with property and except with order or authority of court - Authority or order of court had not been obtained for alienation of those properties - Therefore, alienation would be hit by doctrine of lis pendens by operation of s. 52 - Under these circumstances, respondents cannot be considered to be either necessary or proper parties to suit - Appeal allowed - Petition under O. 1, r. 10 CPC dismissed.
