State of Madhya Pradesh v Jaora Sugar Mills Limited and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1356; (1997) 9 SCC 207; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 554; AIR 1997 SC 600; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 567; 1996(8) SCALE 88; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Sugarcane Control Order, 1966, rr. 3(3), 3(1), 3(3 A), 5 A, 2(g), 2(i) and 2(j) - M.P. Sugarcane [Regulation of Supply and Purchase] Act, 1959, ss. 21, 12, 15, 16, 17, 13, 19, 20, 23, 26 and 27 - Essential Commodities Act, 1957, ss. 3(3)(c) and 7 - Final price of sugarcane fixed - Though sugarcane was supplied by cane-growers, since theirs amounts could not be paid, Appellant-Govt. resorted to s. 21 of M.P. Sugarcane Act to enforce liability by recovering same as arrears of land revenue - The respondents came to challenged demands by filing WPs - (A) Whether there is an agreement for final price of sugarcane for relevant period and if so whether it is in consonance with Order? - Held, as regards fixation of price, field undoubtedly is occupied by Order - Despite fixation of minimum price u/r. 3(1), by agreement between sugarcane grower and purchaser of sugarcane, they would be at liberty to agree to sell or purchase sugarcane at a higher price than what was fixed by Central Govt. - Only for postponement of payment beyond 14 days there should be an agreement in writing between parties obviously with concurrence of Central Govt. or authorised authority in that behalf - Thus there is no statutory prohibition in that behalf to pay higher price - Thus, it could be seen that prior to coming into force of r. 3 A, minimum price fixed by Central Govt. u/r. 3(1) and additional price fixed u/r. 5 A, it was within domain of contract between sugarcane growers and factories who could agree to pay price higher than minimum price fixed under Order - R. 3(2) prohibits purchase or sale or agreement in that behalf, for bargain to pay price lesser than minimum price fixed by Central Govt. - In other words, sugarcane growers should not be compelled to sell sugarcane at a price lesser than was prescribed by Order - Held, there was no statutory prohibition at relevant time to agree to pay higher price than was fixed under order - (B) Whether such a higher price has been agreed to be paid to sugarcane growers, when contract has come into existence between respondents and canegrowers? - Held, there was consensus ad idem to pay higher price of sugarcane than minimum price fixed by Central Govt. - There was no prohibition for oral agreement between growers and owners through service of Cane Commr., a statutory authority to effect such agreement - Sugarcane crushing year has been defined under Order itself and during season Price fixed by Central Govt. was treated by State Govt. to be tentative price, subject to agreements between parties and final price was agreed as contracted by parties - (C) Whether it is a compulsive price and whether State Govt. had entered into such a contract? - Held, Cane Commr. is the statutory authority under M.P. Sugarcane Act and Order to regulate fixation of zone for supply of sugarcane to respective factories and for regulation of supply of sugarcane to factories covered under M.P. Sugarcane Act - Statutory authority has obligation to ensure proper price of sugarcane supplied by sugarcane growers - Thus, Govt. has to ensure meeting of growers and occupiers of factories and their Association - Thereafter final price of sugarcane was fixed, parties orally agreed thereto and proceedings culminate into a concluded gentlemen contract - Thus, it would be seen that M.P. Sugarcane Act regulates recovery as arrears of land revenue - Accordingly, demand has been made for payment of amount - Demands issued against respondents are in accordance with provisions of M.P. Sugarcane Act and they are liable to pay same - (D) Whether respondent is also liable to pay interest for delayed payment? - Held, it is seen that under Order and M.P. Sugarcane Act there is power to impose interest not exceeding 15% - 14% and odd was interest levied on delayed payment - In view of agreement, as upheld earlier, in addition to minimum price, therefore, liability has arisen under Order for payment of value of sugarcane supplied by growers - On account of default in payment thereof, in terms of r. 3(3), since it was not paid, by operation of s. 20 of M.P. Sugarcane Act, they are entitled to recover same as arrears of land revenue - Parties would always be at liberty to agree for payment of higher price than minimum price fixed by Central Govt. and contract will be novation of minimum price fixed by Central Govt. u/r. 3(1) of Order - Respondent is liable to pay interest on delayed payment under M.P. Sugarcane Act r/w order - Appeal allowed.
