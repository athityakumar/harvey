Krishnan Kakkanth v Government of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1845; (1997) 9 SCC 495; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 278; AIR 1997 SC 128; AIR 1996 SCW 4288; 1997 (1) CLT 203; JT 1996 (9) SC 489; 1997 (1) KLT 388; 1996(7) SCALE 647; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 487

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Constitutional validity of the circular dt. 19-5-1995 issued by the Secretary to the Govt., directing for distribution of pumpsets under Comprehensive Coconut Development Programme and other similar schemes of the Agriculture Department and to streamline the implementation of the schemes specifying specific roles and responsibilities for different agencies involved, was challenged - HC upheld the validity of the impugned circular - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the impugned circular was invalid and illegal - Held, that the contention that the impugned circular had resulted in blacklisting of the private dealers of pumpsets without even giving them an opportunity of being heard could not be accepted - It could not be reasonably contended that if the Govt. selected a dealer as its approved dealer, the same could mean that all the other dealers had been blacklisted - Question of blacklisting did not arise because it was nobody's case that all other dealers were previously approved dealers of the Govt. but by the impugned circular, they had been suddenly stripped of such status without affording them an opportunity of being heard - Unless the policy decision was demonstrably capricious or arbitrary and not informed by any reason whatsoever or it suffered from the vice of discrimination or infringed any statute or provisions of the Constitution, the policy decision could not be struck down - It should be borne in mind that except for the limited purpose of testing a public policy in the context of illegality and unconstitutionality, courts should avoid 'embarking on uncharted ocean of public policy' - Appeal dismissed.
