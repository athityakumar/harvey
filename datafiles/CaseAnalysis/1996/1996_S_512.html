T.M.A. Pai Foundation And Ors. Etc. v State Of Karnataka And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1061; (1996) 5 SCC 8; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 218; AIR 1996 SC 2652; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 420; 1996(5) SCALE 666; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 436; 1996 (6) Supreme 36

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Education - Fee structure in professional colleges - SC passed Order dt.10-5-1996 stating that Order dt.11-8-1995 with respect to Academic Year 1995-96 would continue to apply for next academic year, 1996-97 as well - Even then, many Interlocutory Applications were filed by various medical and engineering colleges and others seeking variety of directions - Hence, instant orders which would be in addition to, in continuation of and in clarification of earlier orders including Order dt.11-8-1995, as extended by Order dt.10-5-1996.

Held, firstly, fee fixed for each of Academic Years 1993-94, 1994-95 and 1995-96 would be confined to that respective year only. Secondly, fee structure and all other directions provided in Order dt.11-8-1995 applicable for Academic Year 1995-96, would also apply to and continue to apply for Academic Year 1996-97. Further, Order dt.10-5-1996 would apply to professional colleges in all States. Fourthly, w.e.f. Academic Year 1995-96 free seat students would pay fee prescribed in Order dt.11-8-1995 even though students had been admitted during Academic Year 1993-94 or 1994-95. Fifthly, there would be no change in fee for engineering colleges. Further, with regard to 35% payment seats in medical colleges in State of Karnataka, said seats should be filled in same proportion as between Karnataka and non-Karnataka students as specified for Academic Year 1995-96. Applications disposed of.
