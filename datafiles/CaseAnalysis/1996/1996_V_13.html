Food Corporation of India and Another v V. P. Bhatia

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2136; (1998) 9 SCC 131; (1998) SCC (L&S) 466; 1999 (1) CLR 25; 1999 (81) FLR 22; JT 1998 (8) SC 16B; 1999 (3) LLJ 733

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Undue Delay

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Undue delay in commencement of disciplinary proceedings - Held, as per direction in Vigilance Manual no fault can be found with appellant - Corporation in waiting for investigation report of CBI - High Court was in error in holding that appellant - Corporation need not have waited for report of CBI and should have started disciplinary proceedings straightaway - Having regard to alleged misconduct and fact that large number of documents and statements of witnesses had to be looked into it cannot be said that period of slightly more than one year taken in serving charge - Memos after recommendations of Central Vigilance Commission is unduly long - Appeal allowed.
