Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay v Industrial Development Investment Company Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2162; (1996) 11 SCC 501; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 761; AIR 1997 SC 482; 1996 (3) CCC 398; JT 1996 (8) SC 16; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 49; 1996(6) SCALE 379; 1996 (3) SCJ 186; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 551; 1996 (7) Supreme 26

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Planning Act, 1966, s. 126 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 12 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, s. 126 - Notification for acquisition of land issued - Land acquired - Award passed - Possession taken - Challenge to notification u/s. 126 by interested person - (A) When should be made - (B) Whether notification issued u/s. 4 and declaration u/s. 6 can be challenged by tenant when landlord himself had accepted award and claimed compensation - (C) Writ petition against acquisition after passing of award whether liable to be dismissed on ground of delay and laches - Held, (A) should be made before acquisition becomes final and conclusive u/s. 12(1) of Act of 1894 - (B) It cannot be challenged by tenant - (C) Writ petition liable to be dismissed - Appeal allowed.
