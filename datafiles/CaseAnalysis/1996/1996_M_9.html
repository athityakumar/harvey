Commissioner of Income Tax, A.P v B. Posetty and Company

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1638; (1996) 11 SCC 11; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 225; 1996 (136) CTR 250; [1996] 223 ITR 333; JT 1996 (10) SC 100; 1996(8) SCALE 131; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 452; 1996 (8) Supreme 264; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 117

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Respondent formed a sub partnership on condition that they would provide requisite finance on allotting certain share to them out of respondent's income in main firm - Sub partnership filed an application for registration for A/Y 1966-67 - Income-tax officer refused registration to sub partnership, on the ground that sub partnership contravened provisions of s. 14 of the Act - HC decided that the sub partnership was entitled to registration - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the sub partnership contravened the provisions of s. 14 of the Act - Held, that the HC was justified in law in answering the question in favor of the assessee, that the business of sub partnership was not same as that of main partnership and all insignia of a valid partnership were present, so it was valid and entitled to registration - Inhibition contained in s. 14 of the Act, would apply only in a case, where the lessee declared any person as its partner - S. 14, of the Act, was not attracted, nor was there any basis to hold that there was any contravention of provisions of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
