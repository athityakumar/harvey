Ravindra Trimbak Chouthmal v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 600; (1996) 4 SCC 148; (1996) SCC (Cr) 608; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 108; 1996 (3) Bom.C.R. 684; 1996 (1) CCR 168; 1996 (1) Crimes 137; JT 1996 (5) SC 336; 1996 (4) KarLJ 645; 1996(2) SCALE 368; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 1009; 1996 (2) Supreme 272

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Dowry death - Death Sentence - Rarest of rare cases - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120 B, 201, 302, 304 B, 316 and 498 A - Deceased was wife of appellant - Appellant and his parents took deceased to Mumbai and at that time she was carrying child for 8 months - Appellant and his parents murdered the deceased and cut her body into 9 pieces - Her severed head was recovered at the pointing out by father of the appellant and the remaining body parts were also recovered - Whether the murder qualify as rarest of rare cases and death penalty was justified? - Held, no - Dowry deaths have ceased to belong to the species of killing - Death sentence commuted to imprisonment for life - Appeal partly allowed.
