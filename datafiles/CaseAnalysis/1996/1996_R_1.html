Anokhe Lal v Anokhe Lal

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1577; (1996) 6 SCC 730; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 469; AIR 1997 SC 257; 1997 (1) AWC 337; JT 1996 (10) SC 266; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 125; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 106; 1996(8) SCALE 121; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 308; 1996 (8) Supreme 75; 1997 (1) UJ 57

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 30, r. 4, O. 1, r. 10, r. 4(1), r. 4(2), O. 1, r. 10 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 45 - Impleading third party involving de novo trial should not normally be allowed - Eviction suit against firm - Son of deceased partner seeking impleadment - Revisional Court aware that suit is already decreed - Held, that there was no need or occasion to pass an order for impleading a person as a new party since suit was not pending when High Court took up revision - Revision should only have been dismissed as infructuous.
