Shatrughna Prasad Sinha v ajbhau Surajmal Rathi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2527; (1996) 6 SCC 263; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1310; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 25; AIR 1996 SCW 4030; 1996 (4) CCR 155; 1996 (4) Crimes 27; 1997 CRLJ 212; JT 1997 (10) SC 469; 1996(6) SCALE 769; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 771; 1996 (7) Supreme 157

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Religious Feelings, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200 and 190 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 295A, 500 and 34 - Appellant defamed respondent's community and also outraged their religious feeling - Magistrate issued notice to appellant for appearance - Whether Magistrate is correct in holding that complaint discloses offence u/s. 500 IPC? - It is settled legal position that a court has to read complaint as a whole and find out whether allegations disclosed constitute an offence u/s. 499 triable by Magistrate - Held, Magistrate prima facie came to conclusion that allegations might come within definition of defamation and hence took cognizance - Not feasible to quash complaint at this stage - Appeal partly allowed.
