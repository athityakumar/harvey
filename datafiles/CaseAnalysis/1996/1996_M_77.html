M. Vijaya Laxmi v G. Goverdhan Reddy

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2795; (1997) 11 SCC 358; 1996(3) SCALE 65; 1996 (2) UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106, 110 and 111(a) - Appeal by landlord in a suit for eviction - Whether notice of termination of tenancy given by appellant does not entitle him to seek possession of property? - Tenancy was for a fixed period of 11 months and it came to an end by efflux of time on expiry of agreement - No notice for terminating tenancy u/s. 106 was required for purpose of filing suit after expiry of agreement - Held, respondent cannot be treated as a tenant holding over in view of express indication given by appellant by his notice that he did not propose to continue tenancy of respondent - Merely because appellant had given a notice terminating tenancy it would not mean that appellant was not entitled to seek possession of property after tenancy had come to an end by efflux of time u/s. 111(a) - Even if it is held that notice was not a valid notice u/s. 106 it could be regarded as a notice indicating that tenancy would not be continued after term of tenancy comes to an end - Appeal allowed.
