Pannalal Bansilal Pitti and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Anothe

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3084; (1996) 2 SCC 498; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 684; AIR 1996 SC 1023; 1996 (1) HLR 176; JT 1996 (1) SC 516; 1996 (4) KarLJ 252; 1996(1) SCALE 405; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 603; 1996 (1) Supreme 545; 1996 (1) UJ 265

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987, ss. 16, 15, 17, 29(5) and 144 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 25, 25(1), 25(2), 26, 15(1) and 245 - Constitutionality/Validity of Endowments - Considering : (A) Enactment of 1987 Act in light of bringing uniform law for all - (B) Abolition of hereditary rights in trusteeship - (C) Whether religious freedom is denied to Hindus? - (D) Making founder of institution or endowment or members of his family chairperson of board would effectuate object of institution/endowment and encourage establishment of such institution in future - Held, not violative of arts. 14 and 15 for absence of law in this regard for other religions - Abolition held, not violative of art. 25(1) - Freedom not denied to Hindus, majority in population - Requirement of board being headed by founder or his family member has to be read in ss. 17 and 29(5) of Act - Petition disposed of.
