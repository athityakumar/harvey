Balbir Singh v State

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4179

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 5, 19 - Arms Act, 1959 - Recovery of live cartridge - Rifle with live cartridges were recovered from appellant and on completion of investigation, appellant was tried for offence u/s. 5 of 1987 Act and convicted and sentenced - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant could be upheld.

Held, there is ample evidence on record to show that appellant was in conscious possession of rifle which weapon answered description of an arm under 1959 Act as per the report of CFSL. Appellant had no licence for such weapon and was thus in an unauthorized possession of the same. Further there is no dispute that the recovery was made from the area which was declared notified area. Hence, all ingredients essential for proving of an offence u/s. 5 of 1987 Act stand established and so, conviction is well merited. However, sentence awarded is reduced to 6 years. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - As per decision of Constitution Bench in Sanjay Dutt v. State, 1994 INDLAW SC 1008, to convict an accused u/s. 5 of 1987 Act, prosecution is required to prove that accused was in conscious possession unauthorizedly, in a notified area of any of the arms and ammunition or of bombs, dynamite or other explosives substances. Further, nexus with any terrorist or disruptive activity is not required to be proved by prosecution, in view of the statutory presumption and conviction would be valid on strength of presumption.
