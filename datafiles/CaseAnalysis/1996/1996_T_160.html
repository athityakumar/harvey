Director General, Est & Anr. v T. Abdul Razak

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3944; (1996) 4 SCC 708; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1061; AIR 1996 SC 2292; 1996 (2) CLR 61; JT 1996 (6) SC 502; 1996 LabIC 2037; 1996 (2) LLJ 765; 1996 (2) LLN 506; 1996(5) SCALE 113; 1996 (3) SLJ 71; 1996 (4) SLR 508; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 80; 1996 (5) Supreme 394; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2820

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1950, ss. 94-A and 97(1) - Employees' State Insurance Rules, 1950, r. 16 (2) - Employees' State Insurance Corporation (Staff and Conditions of Service) Regulations, 1959, regns. 11, 12(2) and 13(1) - Delegation of authority - Validity of - Whether Director General has the power to delegate his authority to others? - Employees' State Insurance Corporation is a statutory corporation established under provisions of Employees' State Insurance Act - S. 97(1) empowers Corporation to make regulations and for carrying into effect provisions of Act - Powers of Director General: (a) institute disciplinary proceeding against any employee, (b) direct a disciplinary authority to institute disciplinary proceeding against any employee on whom that disciplinary authority is competent to impose under the regulations any of penalties specified in regn. 11 - R. 16(2) to extent empowering him to delegate said powers and duties, is invalid - R. 16(2) and resolution of Standing Committee, to extent they empower Director General to delegate powers or duties delegated to him under any resolution of Corporation or Standing Committee referable to s. 94-A are invalid but rest of said Rule and resolution are valid - Power of delegation u/r. 16(2) can be divided into two parts - One relating to delegation of powers or duties under Rules or Regulations and other relating to powers and duties under any resolution of Corporation or Standing Committee - In so far as power or duties under Rules or Regulations are concerned, conferment on Director General power to delegate same is not violative of principle of sub-delegation - Because said powers and duties are in nature of independent statutory powers conferred on Director General under Rules or Regulations - No infirmity can, therefore, be found either in r. 16(2) or in resolution of Standing Committee empowering Director General to delegate any of his powers or duties under Rules or Regulations - Position is, however, different in respect of powers and duties conferred on Director General under any resolution of Corporation or Standing Committee - Conferment of such powers or duties under a resolution of Corporation or Standing Committee could be by way of delegation of powers of Corporation or Standing Committee u/s. 94-A of Act and empowering Director General to further delegate said powers or duties would amount to sub-delegation of a power delegated to him which is impermissible - Thus r. 16(2) and resolution of Standing Committee to extent they empower Director General to further delegate powers or duties delegated to him by Corporation or Standing Committee under a resolution referable to s. 94-A, have to be held to be invalid - Petition dismissed.
