State of Andhra Pradesh v Gangula Satya Murthy

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1901; (1997) 1 SCC 272; (1997) SCC (Cr) 325; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 516; AIR 1997 SC 1588; 1996 (4) CCR 261; 1996 (4) Crimes 203; 1997 CRLJ 774; JT 1996 (10) SC 550; 1996(8) SCALE 391; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 808; 1996 (8) Supreme 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26 and 114 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 375 - Evidence - Rape - Murder - (A) Consideration of appreciation of evidence, admissibility of extra-judicial confession, bar of s. 26 of Act and presumption to infer offence of murder - (B) Validity of acquittal from charges of rape on ground of absence of resistance challenged - In light of facts like, injury on right side of posterior labia minora, letter by victim, extra-judicial confession, fresh tear on vaginal wall and sexual intercourse soon followed by throttling - But absence of hymen so High Court observed that there was no evidence that accused alone had intercourse - Evidence appreciated - Held, much should not have been made of disparity, possibility of making an error in recording the time a.m. for p.m., but at any rate, it is not proper to jettison otherwise sturdy piece of evidence of extra - judicial confession on such a rickety premise - Bar not attracted - Definitely adverse to accused - Observation not proper without any basis for such assumption, victim has every right to refuse sexual intercourse with accused - Appeal allowed.
