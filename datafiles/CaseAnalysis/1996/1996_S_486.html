Dr. Bipin Shantilal Panchal v State Of Gujrat

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1158; (1996) SCC (Cr) 200; (1996) 1 SCC 718; 1996 (33) ACC 126; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 426; AIR 1996 SC 2897; AIR 1996 SCW 734; 1996 (3) CCR 103; 1996 (1) Crimes 9; 1996 CRLJ 1652; JT 1996 (1) SC 111; 1996 (1) RCR(Rent) 505; 1996(1) SCALE 142; 1996 (2) SCJ 198; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 193; 1996 (1) Supreme 279

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code 1974, s.167(2) Proviso ; Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act 1985, s.37 - Grant of bail was in question - S.37 of Act does not exclude application of proviso to s.167 (2) - Failure of prosecution to file charge-sheet within prescribed time under s.167(2) Proviso does not create indefeasible right on accused to exercise it at any time - Charge sheet was filed and accused was in custody on basis of order of remand - Held, accused could not be released on bail because chargesheet was not submitted within statutory period.
