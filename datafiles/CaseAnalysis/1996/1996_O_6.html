State of Uttar Pradesh v O. P. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3314; (1996) SCC (Cr) 497; (1996) 7 SCC 705; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 111; AIR 1996 SC 2983; AIR 1996 SCW 1229; 1996 ALJ 601; 1996 (28) ALR 79; 1996 (2) AWC 919; 1996 (1) CCR 171; 1996 CRLJ 1878; JT 1996 (2) SC 488; 1996(2) SCALE 356; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 236; 1996 (2) Supreme 501

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Quantity, Edible

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of FIR - Offence under s. 3/7 of Essential Commodities Act - FIR specifically mentions storage of oil without licence - Quashing on ground of absence of ingredients constituting offence - Accused dealer within meaning of cl. 2(g) of order, 1996 and cl. 2(f) of order, 1977 - Held, quashing not proper.
