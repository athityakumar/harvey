Collector of Customs (Preventive), Ahmedabad v Essar Gujarat Limited, Surat

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1896; (1997) 9 SCC 738; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 706; 1997 (68) ECR 386; 1996 (88) E.L.T. 609; JT 1996 (10) SC 426; 1996 (17) RLT 587; 1996(8) SCALE 396; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 757; 1996 (8) Supreme 402

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988

Summary: Customs - Contract & Commercial - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, r. 9 - Customs Act, ss. 14, 46 and 50 - (A) Whether licence fees paid to Midrex International B V, Zurich, (Midrex) should be added to invoice value of plant bought by respondent? - Held, licence fees paid to Midrex will have to be added to price of plant to arrive at transaction value of plant as obtaining a licence from Midrex was a precondition of sale - (B) Whether cost of technical services paid to Midrex should be added to invoice value of plant bought by respondent? - Further held, as all these services were to be rendered under process licence agreement with Midrex, amount payable to Midrex as part of process licence fee has to be included in value of plant - (C) Whether engineering and consultancy fee paid to Midrex should be added to invoice value of plant bought by respondent? - Further held, expenditure incurred for dismantling plant including engineering and consultancy services for making it ready for delivery is to be added to value of plant - Appeal disposed of.
