Shiv Sagar Tiwari v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3689; (1997) 1 SCC 444; AIR 1997 SC 2725; 1997 (1) CLT 110; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 127; 1996(9) SCALE 680; 1997 (1) SCJ 4; 1996 (3) SLR 609; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 925; 1997 (1) Supreme 61

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Merit

Summary: Administrative - Fundamental Rules - Supplementary Rules - Allotment of Govt. Residences (General Pool in Delhi) Rules, 1963 - Allotment of Govt. houses - Court constituted a Committee - Court determines questions by this Judgment by considering various objections to Report of Committee - (A) Who should face eviction? - It would be all those whose names find place in Categories IV, VI, IX, X, XI and such of Category VII who had not become actually entitled to in - turn allotment by date(s) respective reports were submitted - Those IAS, IPS and IFS and other officers who are occupying General Pool quarters, despite being eligible for quarters in Tenure Pool, would also be evicted - Including a new list would be drawn up on basis of this judgment - (B) How much licence fee should be charged from those out-of-turn allottees who would become liable to eviction on account of their illegal occupation of quarters in question? - After an allotment has been cancelled or is deemed to be cancelled under any provision of rules, officer a concerned becomes liable to pay damages for house on occupation for residence etc as determined by Govt. from time to time, or twice licence fee he was paying whichever is higher - Committee's Report para. 6.3 would determine quantum of liscense fee - Any allottee in Categories IV, IX and XI is due for retirement in next one year from 27-09-1996, he may be exempted from eviction in view of exceptional hardship that is likely to be faced by him and his family - This would apply to others also as stated above - It is, however, made clear that they would have to pay enhanced licence fee for period of their occupation as per this judgment - Eligibility for in-turn allotment as on date is to be determined by Estate Officer within six weeks an out-turn allottee is an ineligible person because he has not become eligible as per rules governing allotment - Turn allottee is required to pay damages - (1) What should be done in case of those Govt. employees who had occupied quarters on out-turn basis, but who are not required to be evicted by now? - (2) To put it differently, if they are not required to be evicted, how much licence fee they should be asked to pay? - These allottees should also be treated for purpose of charging of licence fee in same way as those who would be required to be evicted by force of this judgment - (C) How should those who were denied allotment, despite same having become due as per Rules, be compensated? - Additional licence fee (over and above normal licence fee) which would be collected in terms of this judgment from out-turn allottees, has to be used to compensate those employees who were illegally and wrongly denied their allotments which had become due - (D) Should there be any out-turn allotment? - If answer be in affirmative, how should it be regulated and what should be its limit? - The concerned ministries/departments would frame appropriate rules relating to out-turn allotment and duly notify same - 5 percent would be put - Turn allotment for each category - (E) Should private citizens (to wit, journalists, freedom fighters, artists and social workers) be accommodated in Govt. quarters? - If so, on what terms? - 5 percent out-turn allotment permitted to freedom fighters - 5 percent discretionary quota to artists and if there is any vacancy in present incumbent place it on general pool - 5 percent for social workers and if there is any vacancy, place it on general pool - Freelancer journalist is not entitled press quota.
