University Grants Commission v Sadhana Chaudhary and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3861; (1996) 10 SCC 536; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1431; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 682; 1997 (1) ESC 187; JT 1996 (9) SC 234; 1997 (2) LLJ 272; 1996(7) SCALE 211; 1996 (6) SLR 701; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 392; 1996 (8) Supreme 168; 1996 (2) UJ 676

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 12(d), 26(1)(e), 2(1), 2(f) and 3 - University Grants Commission Regulations, 1991 - University Grants Commission Regulations, 1982 - (A) Whether cut off date (December 31, 1993) prescribed in 1991 Regulations, as amended, can be held to be arbitrary? - Held, settled law, choice of a date as a basis for classification cannot always be dubbed as arbitrary even if no particular reason is forthcoming for choice unless it is shown to be capricious or whimsical in circumstances - Decision of legislature or its delegate must be accepted unless it is very wide of reasonable mark - (B) Whether exemption from clearing Eligibility Test or equivalent examination under Circulars and Notification is valid? - Held, object underlying grant of exemption, to mitigate hardship to candidates who had registered for Ph.D degree or had joined course for M. Phil degree on basis of minimum qualifications prescribed under 1982 Regulations - Exemption granted with condition of passing M. Phil examination or have submitted Ph.D thesis by a particular date - SLP (C) No. 27375 of 1995 dismissed - SLP (C) No. 16220 of 1995 partly allowed.
