Nathuni Yadav and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1562; (1998) 9 SCC 238; (1998) SCC (Cr) 992; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 385; AIR 1997 SC 1808; AIR 1997 SCW 1158; 1997 (1) BLJR 547; 1997 (1) Crimes 81; JT 1997 (1) SC 406; 1997(1) SCALE 56; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 905; 1997 (1) Supreme 490

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Murder - Appeal challenging conviction and sentence for murder - Eye-witness well acquainted with physiognomy of each of assailants - Neighbours rushing to scene deposing that victims mentioning names of accused and stating them to be assailants who shot at victims - Evidence may not be substantive but has utility at trial - Motive for criminal act need not necessarily be proportionally grave to do grave crimes - It is quite possible that emotion impelling to do crime would remain undiscoverable - Sometimes motive established may appear to be weak one - Conviction and sentence appropriate - Appeal dismissed.
