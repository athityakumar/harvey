Union Of India & Ors. v Jaswant Rai Kochhar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3351; (1996) 3 SCC 491; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 396; AIR 1996 SC 1352; 1996 (2) CCC 37; 1996 (2) CLT 70; JT 1996 (3) SC 671; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 80; 1996 (1) RentLR 417; 1996(3) SCALE 119; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 206; 1996 (3) Supreme 212; 1996 (2) UJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Acquisition - Challenged - Notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was published acquiring the land for housing scheme - Same was challenged on the ground that the appellant had proposed to use the land for the district center i.e., commercial purpose - Single Judge allowed the writ petition and quashed the notification holding that the property acquired was for housing scheme which cannot be used for commercial purpose, namely, District Center - On appeal, DB confirmed the same - Thus appeals by special leave.

Held, notification u/s. 4(1) of Act could not have been quashed on the ground that the land is sought to be used for District Center, namely, for commercial purpose. It is obvious that the lands acquired for a public purpose should serve only the public purpose of providing facilities of commercial purpose, namely, District Center as conceded by the counsel in fairness to be a public purpose. The notification u/s. 4(1) of Act cannot be quashed on the ground of change of user. HC was wholly wrong in quashing the notification on the ground of change of user. Appeals allowed.
