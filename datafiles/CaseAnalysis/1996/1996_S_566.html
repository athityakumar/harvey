State Through Anti-Corruption Bureau, Government of Maharashtra, Bombay v Krishanchand Khushalchand Jagtiani

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4028; (1996) 4 SCC 472; (1996) SCC (Cr) 755; AIR 1996 SC 1910; 1996 (2) Crimes 228; 1996 CRLJ 2510; JT 1996 (4) SC 495; 1996(4) SCALE 57; 1996 (2) SLR 893; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Sanction - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5 and 6 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 83 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161 and 165 - Illegal gratification - Respondent caught accepting bribe on a trap laid - Respondent raised objection regarding sanction granted by Municipal Commr. without obtaining previous approval of standing committee of corporation - Objection was overruled by Special Judge but allowed by High Court - Hence, present appeal - Object of s. 6(1)(c) is that there should be no unnecessary harassment of public servant - If authority competent to remove such public servant accords previous sanction, such prosecution can be instituted and proceeded with - Previous sanction for prosecution contemplated by s. 6(1)(c) of Act could have been accorded and was validly accorded by Commr. acting by himself and that said previous sanction is not invalid or ineffective in law on ground that it was not preceded by previous approval of Standing Committee - Held, previous sanction was validly granted by commr. and it was not necessary that commr. should have obtained previous approval of standing committee - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
