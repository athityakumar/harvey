State Of Karnataka v S. M. Kotrayya and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2098; (1996) 6 SCC 267; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1488; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 5; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 483; 1997 (1) LLJ 640; 1997 (1) LLN 40; 1996(7) SCALE 179; 1996 (3) SCJ 295; 1996 (6) SLR 664; 1996 (7) Supreme 512

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 21 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether the Tribunal has power to condone the delay in the absence of any explanation as to why application could not be filed within six months? - (B) Whether the Tribunal has power to condone the delay after the expiry of the period prescribed in sub-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 21? - Held - The explanation offered was that they came to know of the relief granted by the Tribunal in August 1989 and that they filed the petition immediately thereafter - That is not a proper explanation at all - Appeal allowed.
