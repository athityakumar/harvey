State Bank of India Staff Association and Another v State Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 629; (1996) 4 SCC 378; (1996) SCC (L&S) 949; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 20; AIR 1996 SC 1685; AIR 1996 SCW 1764; 1996 ALJ 757; 1996 (1) CLR 798; 1996 (74) FLR 2037; JT 1996 (4) SC 100; 1996 (2) LLJ 288; 1996 (2) LLN 277; 1996(3) SCALE 249; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1104; 1996 (3) Supreme 319; 1996 (2) UJ 426; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2885

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Trade Unions Act, 1926 - Staff Association Rules - Election of General Secretary - Triennial Meeting - Instant appeal by appellants was filed challenging order passed by DB of HC dismissing writ petition filed by appellants on ground that the same was not maintainable as earlier writ petition was dismissed as withdrawn without permission to file fresh petition for same relief - Further, instant writ petition was filed by appellants seeking quashing of impugned letter dt.3-5-1995 issued by respondent no. 3 to appellant no. 2 stating that in view of advise received from Central Office, Management would not negotiate with appellant no. 2 on any matter of Union/Association as he had already retired from Bank on 31-1-1995 - Whether impugned communication could be upheld.

Held, appellant no. 2 was ordinary member of Staff association within the meaning of r.5 of the Rules. Being such ordinary member, he was elected as General Secretary of Staff Association in triennial meeting held on 16-10-1994. However, appellant no. 2 was retired from service on 31-1-1995. He was not elected as an honorary or a temporary member in any Special Meeting of the General Council or of the Committees convened for that purpose any time after his retirement. As a consequence, in view of r. 9 of the Rules, appellant no. 2 could not legitimately claim his continuance as an ordinary member and General Secretary of the Union after his retirement from the service of the Bank. Further, as appellant no. 2 ceased to be an ordinary member on his retirement and since he was not elected as honorary member at the triennial or a Special Meeting of the General Council, etc. as contemplated in r.6 of the Rules, he neither remained as ordinary member or as honorary member of the Association. Hence, appellant no. 2 could not claim right to negotiate with management as representative of the Union. Hence, impugned letter is upheld and petition dismissed. Further, triennial meeting held on 16-10-1994 in which appellant no. 2 was elected as General Secretary was not a valid meeting since said meeting was called much after prescribed period of 9 months as contained in r. 32(a) and no approval of Registrar of the Trade Union was obtained for calling said meeting on 16-10-1994. Hence, appeal fails. Order accordingly.

Ratio - As per r. 9(a) of the Rules, member of Central Committee/Central Working Committee/Circle Committee/Unit Committee would forthwith cease to be such member if he is ceased to be an ordinary/honorary member. Further, as per provisions of 1947 Act, the representatives of the workman have to be chosen only from amongst the workmen already engaged in the establishment and not an outsider or an ex-workman of the establishment concerned or any other person.
