State of Haryana v Rajinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 613; (1996) 8 SCC 77; (1996) SCC (Cr) 534; AIR 1996 SC 2978; 1996 (1) Crimes 153; 1996 CRLJ 1875; JT 1996 (3) SC 396; 1996 (4) KarLJ 460; 1996(2) SCALE 488

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Lathi - Acquittal - Judicial confession -Postmortem report - State felt aggrieved at the acquittal of the respondent by the HC on appeal preferred by him against the judgment of the Sessions Judge by which he was found guilty, along with two others, u/ss. 302/34 IPC and 498-A - Though the HC had acquitted all the three convicts, notice was issued by this Court only against the respondent - Whether the Court accepted the defence version that a false case not instituted on 31st March - the death being on 27th - on which date the FIR of the present case was filed, stating about causing of death of deceased on failure to meet the demand of dowry - Held, as per the post-mortem report of the deceased it was contended that injury was not caused by a lathi blow because it was between the genital organ and anus - Though P.Ws.2 and 5, who were the parents of the deceased, had mentioned about this demand, the judges were inclined to place reliance on this piece of evidence as stated by the brother of the deceased, about there being no ill will between the two families - It was because of this that the parents had first accepted the death due to accident, as mentioned in the inquest report, which was prepared by police which came to the scene pursuant to the information given by the respondent himself - Thus, the Court accepted the defence version that a false case not instituted on 31st March - the death being on 27th - on which date the FIR of the present case was filed, stating about causing of death of deceased on failure to meet the demand of dowry - Appeal dismissed.
