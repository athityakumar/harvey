D. K. Basu and another v State of West Bengal and another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1546; (1997) SCC (Cr) 92; (1997) 1 SCC 416; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 180; AIR 1997 SC 610; AIR 1997 SCW 233; 1997 (1) CCR 81; 1996 (4) Crimes 233; 1997 CRLJ 743; 1997 JIC 967; JT 1997 (1) SC 1; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 284; 1996 (8) Supreme 581

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; International; Tort

Keywords: Custodial Death, Constitutional Safeguards, Death In Police Custody, Destruction Of Evidence, communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Constitution - Human Rights - Universal Declaration of Human Rights - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22(1), 32 and 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 330 - Custodial death - (A) Whether any form of torture or cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment fall within inhibition of art. 21? - (B) Whether failure to comply with requirement issued by Supreme Court to be followed in all cases of arrest or detention till legal provisions are made in that behalf as preventive measures shall render concerned official liable? - (C) Whether citizen entitled to receive compensation from State in cases of torture to arrestee? - (D) Whether punishment u/s. 330 of Code inadequate? - (E) Whether amendment of relevant provisions of law to protect interest of arrested persons in cases relating to economic offences, offences needed? - Held, (A) any form of torture or cruel treatment fall within inhibition of art. 21, whether it occurs during investigation or interrogation - Held, (B) concerned officials shall be held liable for departmental action and also liable to be punished for contempt of Court - Further held, (C) citizen are entitled to receive compensation and quantum of compensation would depend on peculiar facts of each case - Held, (D) punishment u/s. 330 of Code inadequate - Further held, (E) Amendment to relevant provisions is a genuine need - Order accordingly.
