Govind Das v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2672; (1997) 11 SCC 361

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Disciplinary proceedings - Charges of misconduct - Defalcation of funds - Appellant acquitted in criminal proceedings - Whether once appellant has been acquitted in criminal case, order for termination of his services should have been set aside? - Held, acquittal of appellant is based on view that charges are not proved beyond a reasonable doubt - Since standard of proof required to prove a charge of misconduct in departmental proceedings is not same as that required to prove a criminal charge, acquittal of appellant in criminal case, in these circumstances, could not, be made basis for setting aside order for termination of services of appellant passed in disciplinary proceedings on basis of evidence adduced in departmental inquiry conducted in charges levelled against appellant - Appeal dismissed.
