Mohd. Alam Khan v Narcotics Control Bureau Andanother

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3275; (1996) 9 SCC 462; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1062; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 81; AIR 1996 SC 3033; 1996 (1) CCR 190; 1996 CRLJ 2001; JT 1996 (2) SC 636; 1996(2) SCALE 276; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction And Sentence

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c), 22 and 66 - (A) Search and seizure - Ownership and possession of premises by accused from which contraband articles were seized not established - Whether accused is to be acquitted? - (B) Documentary evidence - Admissibility - Admission of accused during course of his interrogation - Can it be made admissible in evidence - Said document having not been seized from custody of accused - If admissible by invoking s. 66 - Held, (A) Entitled to be acquitted - (B) Cannot be made admissible in evidence - Was not admissible by invoking s. 66 - Appeal allowed.
