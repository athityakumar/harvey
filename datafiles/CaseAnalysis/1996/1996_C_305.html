Common Cause" A Registered Society Through Its Director v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3970; (1996) 4 SCC 33; (1996) SCC (Cr) 589; 1996 (1) ACC 648; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 170; AIR 1996 SC 1619; AIR 1996 SCW 2279; 1996 (4) Bom.C.R. 519; 1997 (2) CalLT(SC) 8; 1996 (2) CCR 180; 1996 (2) CLR 987; 1996 (2) Crimes 114; 1996 CRLJ 2380; 1996 (2) DMC 49; 1996 JCC 503; JT 1996 (4) SC 701; 1996(4) SCALE 127; 1996 (3) SCJ 41; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 196; 1996 (4) Supreme 37; 1996 (2) UJ 263

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 437 - Directions sought with respect to cases pending in criminal courts all over country - Pendency of criminal cases in case of minor offences punishable for not more than three years or even less with or without fine - Held, in a majority of these cases, whether instituted by police or private complainants, the accused belong to the poorer sections of the society, who are unable to afford competent legal advice - Instances have also come before courts where the accused, who are in jail, are not brought to the court on every date of hearing and for that reason also the cases undergo several adjournments - It appears essential to issue appropriate directions to protect and effectuate the right to life and liberty of the citizens guaranteed by art. 21 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - It is also necessary to ensure that these criminal prosecutions do not operate as engines of oppression - Directions issued to criminal courts to release on bail or on personal bond executed by accused subject to condition u/s. 437, CrPC, 1973 - Appropriate action to be taken in accordance with directions and directions applicable to not only but also to cases instituted thereafter - Writ Petition disposed of.
