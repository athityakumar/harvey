Narendra Kumar and Another v Yarenissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3379; (1998) 9 SCC 202; (1999) SCC (Cr) 245; 1997 (1) ACC 341; 1998 ACJ 244; 1999 (1) AWC 754; JT 1998 (7) SC 445

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Tortfeasors

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Tort - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 95 and 96(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether High Court taking the view that a joint appeal by the insurance company and the owner or driver of the offending vehicle is not competent in its entirety or the view that the appeal of the owner or driver of vehicle would be competent though not a joint appeal i.e., insurance company appeal alone may be incompetent is valid and justified? - Held, in case of event of joint appeal by insurer and owner of offending vehicle if an award has been made against tortfeasors as well as insurer even though an appeal filed by insurer is not competent not be dismissed and tortfeasors cam proceed with appeal after cause title is suitably amended by deleting name of insurer - Appeal dismissed and disposed of.
