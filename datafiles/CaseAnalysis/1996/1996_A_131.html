K. V. Muthu v Angamuthu Ammal

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2056; (1997) 2 SCC 53; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 250; AIR 1997 SC 628; AIR 1997 SCW 253; JT 1996 (11) SC 246; 1996 (2) RCJ 562; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 497; 1997 (1) RentLR 183; 1996(9) SCALE 375; 1997 (1) SCJ 48; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 188; 1997 (1) Supreme 13; 1997 (1) UJ 308

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Tamil Nadu Building (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, S. 2(6A) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(57) - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Foster son - Eviction application - Bonafide need - Appellant was tenant in respect of non-residential building - Respondent was new owner of building - Respondent filed application for eviction of appellant on ground inter alia of personal need and wilful default - Premises occupied by appellant were said to be required for carrying on the lime-shell business of respondent and her son AB - Appellant contested application and raised plea that AB was not natural son of respondent and consequently he was not be a 'member of the family' within meaning of S. 2(6A) of the 1960 Act - Petition contended that appellant was not liable to be evicted as he had not committed wilful default in payment of rent - Rent Controller found that AB was son therefore, petition was maintainable for eviction of appellant from premises in question on ground of bona fide need - Appellate authority reversed decision of Rent Controller and held that AB was merely foster son, therefore, was not member of family - Respondent challenged decision in HC, matter was referred to Division Bench and it was held that petition filed by respondent for eviction of appellant from premises in question for bona fide need of herself and that of her 'Foster Son' was maintainable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether 'Foster Son' would be 'member of family' in relation to landlord within meaning of S. 2(6A) of the 1960 Act - Held, s. 3(57) of the 1897 Act defines "Son" - Definitions indicated that 'Foster Child' need not be real legitimate child of person who brought him up, and was essentially child of another person but was nursed, reared and brought up by another person as his own son - AB was related to appellant's husband by blood and was his heir not only under old Mitakshara Law (as brother's son) but also under the 1956, as class-Il heir - He was brought up by appellant and her husband and was living all along with them - He acted as devoted son to appellant's husband and helped him in all possible ways including business which he carried on - Appellant's husband acted as devoted father to AB in whose favour he executed will - Facts and circumstances indicated that AB was clearly member of family within meaning of S. 2(6A) of the 1960 Act and consequently, accordingly application was maintainable - No infirmity in judgment passed by Madras HC - Costs was quantified at Rs. 15, 000 - Appeal dismissed.
