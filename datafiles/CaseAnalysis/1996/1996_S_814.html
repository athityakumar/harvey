A. A. Mulla and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1797; (1996) 11 SCC 606; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 737; AIR 1997 SC 1441; 1996 (4) CCR 276; 1996 (4) Crimes 125; 1997 CRLJ 353; JT 1996 (9) SC 551; 1996(7) SCALE 840; 1996 (7) Supreme 697

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Second Trial

Summary: Service - Criminal - Gold Control Act, 1968 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 403(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20(1) and 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 5 - Customs Act - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Bar of second trial - Accused tried for offences u/s. 409 of IPC, 1860 and s. 5 of Act of 1988 on ground that accused, belonging in Customs Department retained nine gold bars from bars recovered - Acquitted - Second trial for offences under Customs Act and Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 initiated after obtaining sanction - Whether second trial barred? - Not only ingredients of offences in two trials are different but factual foundations of offences in previous and second trial are different - Held, second trial not barred - Offences committed by accused are serious economic offences - Second trial cannot be quashed on ground of delay - Petition dismissed.
