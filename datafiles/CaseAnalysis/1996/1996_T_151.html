Sant Ram and Company v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1914; (1997) 1 SCC 147; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 400; AIR 1997 SC 2557; 1997 (1) ARBLR 209; 1996 (4) CCC 292; 1997 (1) CLT 155; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 95; 1996(8) SCALE 722; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 931; 1996 (8) Supreme 695; 1997 (1) UJ 184

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41(b) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Appellant entered into a contract with respondent-State - Dispute - Referred to arbitrator - Pending arbitration, respondent sought to adjust amounts due to appellant in another contract - Appellant filed an application in District Court under O. 39, r. 1, CPC, 1908 r/w s. 41(b) and Second Schedule of Arbitration Act, 1940 for ad interim injunction restraining respondent from adjusting same - Held, to avail of remedy under provisions of CPC, when an application for injunction u/s. 41(b) r/w Second Schedule is filed, Court shall have, pending proceedings for purpose of and in relation to arbitration proceedings availed of through process of Court, same power of making orders in respect of any matters set out in Second Sch. as it has for purpose of and in relation to any proceedings before Court - Initiation of pendency of any proceedings in Court in relation to arbitration proceedings would, therefore, be a precondition for exercise of power by Civil Court under Second Schedule of Act - Held, u/cl. (50) of contract, appellant has expressly agreed for adjustment of amount pending with respondent in respect of claim under dispute or any other contract with Department - Appeal dismissed.
