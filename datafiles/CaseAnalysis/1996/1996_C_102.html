M/S. Ranadey Micronutrients v Collector Of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 821; (1996) 10 SCC 387; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 86; AIR 1997 SC 69; 1996 (87) E.L.T. 19; 1996 (16) RLT 501; 1996(6) SCALE 702; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 755; 1997 (5) Supreme 405

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 37B - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, heading 31.05 and 38.08 - Classification - Micronutrients - Circular dt. 20.06.1990 issued by Central Board of Excise and Customs stated that micronutrients to be classified under heading 38.05 of Tariff as 'plant growth regulators' - However, Board vide later circular dt. 21.11.1994 classified the micronutrients under heading 31.05 of the Tariff as 'other fertilizers' - Revenue contended that circular dt. 21.11.1994 was contrary to terms of statue - Held, orders instructions and directions u/s. 37B of the Act were to be issued when Central Board of Excise and Custom considered it necessary or expedient to do so to achieve uniformity in classification of excisable goods and levy of Excise duty thereon - Central Excise officers are obliged to observe and follow these orders, instructions and directions - The orders, instructions and directions may not relate to a particular assessment or case or interfere with the appellate functions of a Collector - It was not open to Revenue to raise contention against some binding circular issued by Central Board of Excise and Custom - Appeal allowed.
