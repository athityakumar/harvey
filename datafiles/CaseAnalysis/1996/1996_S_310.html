The Secretary,Ministry Of Works And Housinggovernment Of India v Shri Mohinder Singh Jagdev And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2289; (1996) 6 SCC 229; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 738; 1996 (4) CLT 186; 1996 (74) FLR 2382; JT 1996 (8) SC 46; 1997 (1) LLJ 549; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 59; 1996(6) SCALE 397; 1996 (5) SLR 647; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 732; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 736; 1996 (7) Supreme 162

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Central Government Services Rules 1949

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 127, 420, 468 - Central Government Services Rules 1949 - r. 5 - False certificates - Services terminated - Limitation - Respondent was appointed as Section officer in Central Public Works Department - Executive Engineer discovered appointment was based on false certificates - Report u/ss. 127, 420, 468 of the IPC resulted in his suspension - Thereafter, order u/r. 5 of the Rules terminated respondent's services - Respondent, after his acquittal by criminal court filed suit that sought declaration that termination of his service was wrong, unconstitutional, that he was deemed to have continued in service, thus entitled to Rs.84,000/- by way salary and damages for expenses incurred by him to defend criminal cases etc. - Trial Judge held that termination order was unconstitutional, as it lacked compliance of art. 311(2) of Constitution, dismissed suit as barred by limitation - Division Bench held that suit was not barred by limitation as suit was filed after rejection of his application for reinstatement and consequent to acquittal by criminal court, therefore, it was within limitation - Suit was decreed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appeal had been competently laid, whether delay had been properly explained in filing special leave petition, and whether suit was barred by limitation - Held, nomenclature given in cause title as Secretary instead of Union of India, was not conclusive as Secretary representing Govt. of India had filed appeal obviously on behalf of Union of India - Explanation offered by appellant was well acceptable and was accepted - Accordingly, delay was not bar to consider matter on merits was condoned - Dismissal order was foundation for cause of action - After dismissal of Department's appeal, suit was laid - Accordingly, suit was filed within limitation - Appellant was entitled to terminate services of its respondent in terms of order of appointment which conferred power to take action in terms thereof - Respondent was temporary - R. 5 of the Rules clearly empowered termination of services of temporary servant in terms of order of appointment - Gurdev Singh's case 1991 Indlaw SC 224 (supra), relied on - Court agreed with ratio therein - Hence, HC wrongly applied principle of dismissal followed by conviction for misconduct and acquittal thereof - Judgement and order of Division Bench was set aside, but without costs - Appeal allowed.
