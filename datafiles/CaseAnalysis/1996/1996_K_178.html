State of Rajasthan v Kunji Raman

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2053; (1997) 2 SCC 517; (1997) SCC (L&S) 559; AIR 1997 SC 693; 1997 (2) AWC 939; 1997 (1) ESC 528; 1997 (75) FLR 545; JT 1997 (1) SC 144; 1997 LabIC 574; 1997 (2) LLJ 781; 1997 (1) LLN 139; 1996(9) SCALE 384; 1997 (2) SLR 201; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 255; 1997 (1) UJ 347

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951 - Rajasthan Service (Concessions on Project) Rules, 1962, rr. 2(b),(d),4(2),(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Whether High Court is correct by striking down rr. 2(b) and (d) of 1962 Project Rules and rr. 4(2) and (4) of 1975 Project Rules also (as 1975 Project Rules had replaced 1962 Project Rules) as violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution? - Held, employees engaged in work-charged establishments are concerned, not only their recruitment and service conditions but nature of work and duties to be performed by them are not same as those of employees of regular establishment - A regular establishment and a work-charged establishment are two separate types of establishments and persons employed on those establishments thus form two separate and distinct classes - For that reason, if separate set of rules are framed for persons engaged in work-charged establishment and general rules applicable to persons working on regular establishment are not made applicable to them, it cannot be said that they are treated in an arbitrary and discriminatory manner by Govt. - It is well settled that Govt. has power to frame different rules for different classes of employees - HC committed an error in declaring rr. 2(b) and (d) of Project Rules, 1962 and rr. 4(2) and (4) of Project Rules, 1975 as ultra vires arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution, 1950 - Civil Appeal No. 652 of 1993 allowed and Civil Appeal No. 653 of 1993 dismissed.
