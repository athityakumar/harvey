Star Wire (India) Limited v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2482; (1996) 11 SCC 698; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 172; 1996 (4) CLT 269; JT 1996 (9) SC 429; 1996(7) SCALE 632; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 6; 1997 (5) Supreme 410

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 16-(A) Whether subsequent purchaser entitled to challenge notification u/s. 4(1)? - Any encumbrance created by erstwhile owner of land after publication of notification u/s. 4(1) does not bind State if possession of land is already taken over, after award came to be passed; land stood vested in State free from all encumbrances u/s. 16 - Held, petitioner having purchased property covered by notification u/s. 4(1) after it was published, therefore, its title is a void title and it has no right to challenge acquisition proceedings much less award - (B) Whether petition barred on ground of latches? - Further held, as observed by court in case of Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay v Industrial Development and Investment Co.(P) Ltd [1996 Indlaw SC 2162] person who approaches court belatedly will be told that laches, close gates of court for him to question legality of notification u/s. 4(1), declaration u/s. 6 and award of Collector u/s. 11 - Petition dismissed.
