Munishwar Dutt Pandey v Ramjeet Tiwari And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1215; (1997) 3 SCC 599; (1997) SCC (L&S) 867; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 311; AIR 1997 SC 1571; 1997 (29) ALR 321; 1997 (1) AWC 553; JT 1996 (10) SC 795; 1997 LabIC 349; 1996(8) SCALE 736; 1996 (6) SLR 279; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 439; 1997 (5) Supreme 595; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 199

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Election Commission, Election Commissioner

Summary: Service - U.P. Secondary Education Services Commission and Selection Boards Act, 1982, ss. 18 and 33-A - Ad hoc Principal - Amendment of section - Regularised - Appellant appointed as ad hoc Principal - Approval was given for appointment u/s. 18 of the Act and appointment was purely temporary and would last till candidate selected by Board was available - Procedure started for selection of regular Principal and names of appellant and contesting respondent was sent and had called for interview - Contesting respondent was appointed as Principal and he took over charge from appellant - In meantime, s. 33-A of the Act got amended - Placing reliance on amended provisions of s. 33-A, appellant moved HC by way of W.P. contending that his ad hoc appointment as Principal had stood statutorily regularised - He, by separate W.P., challenged process of selection undertaken by Board - W.P. filed by appellant was allowed by Single Judge - Respondent challenged order by filing Special Appeal before DB of HC - DB allowed appeal and set aside judgment of Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether appointment of Principal was statutorily regularized by amended provision of s. 33-A of the Act - Held, order of DB of HC in Special Appeal was quashed and set aside and order passed by Single Judge was restored - Appellant's W.P. would accordingly stood allowed - As a consequence, order of status quo granted would stood vacated - Appellant was held entitled to function as full-fledged Principal of College subject to his probation being declared by Management - Appeal allowed.
