Chief Executive Officer and Vice-Chairman, Gujarat Maritime Board v Haji Daud Harun Abu and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1912; (1996) 11 SCC 23; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 453; [1997] 89 Comp Cas 710; 1997 (1) CompLJ 73; 1996 (3) CPJ(Sc) 20; 1996 (4) CTJ 945; JT 1996 (11) SC 43; 1996(8) SCALE 608; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Consumer - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 13(4), 13(5), 13(6), 14(1), 21, 22 and 24 - Rival claims - Power of the National Consumer Commission for adjudication - Whether Commission has power to adjudicate rival claims and decide dispute? - Held, the Act defines the expressions 'complainant' and 'consumer' as also the expression 'consumer dispute' and that s. 24 invests the orders of the Commission with finality - The jurisdiction of the Commission to entertain and decide complaints necessarily means that where plurality of persons claim the same relief, simultaneously disputing each other's right to claim the said relief, the Commission has the necessary power to adjudicate the rival claims and decide the said dispute also - This power flows from and is incidental and ancillary to the substantive power conferred by s. 2(a)(i) r/w. s. 22 which applies sub-ss. (4), (5) and (6) of s. 13 to the National Commission as well - It is well settled that where a substantive power is conferred upon a Court or Tribunal, all incidental and ancillary powers necessary for an effective exercise of the substantive power have to be inferred - Relied on Khyerbari Tea Co. Ltd. v. State of Assam (1963 Indlaw SC 361) - The impugned orders of the Commission are set aside and the matter is remitted to the Commission for a fresh disposal of the matter according to law - Appeal allowed.
