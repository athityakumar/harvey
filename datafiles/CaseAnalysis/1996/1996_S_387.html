Saraswati Devi v Smt. Shanti Devi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1893; (1997) 1 SCC 122; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 15; AIR 1997 SC 347; JT 1996 (11) SC 23; 1996(8) SCALE 357; 1996 (8) Supreme 512; 1997 UJ 399

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 243-T - Haryana Municipal Election Rules, 1978, r. 70(4) - Haryana Municipal Act, 1973, ss. 10(5), 278 and 18 - Appellant as well as respondent 1 are Scheduled Caste women - Elections were held to Loharu Municipal Committee - Loharu Municipal Area is divided into 11 wards - Appellant was elected from ward no. 5 reserved for Scheduled Caste women while respondent 1 was declared elected from ward no. 11 which was reserved for General women - Local Govt. declared that seat of President, Municipal Committee shall be filled up from amongst members belonging to Scheduled Caste category - Respondent 1 contended that as she was also an elected member belonging to Scheduled Caste women category, she was entitled to contest for Presidentship of municipality - Local Govt. vide its order clarified that where there is a single number of Scheduled Caste category office of President is to be filled up from amongst members belonging to Scheduled Caste category, such single member belonging to Scheduled Caste category shall be deemed to have been elected as President of such municipality - Respondent 1 filed a writ petition in High Court for quashing Govt. order - Allowed - Whether Govt. order taking view that appellant is sole candidate for post of President, Municipal Committee and there is no question of any contest between appellant and respondent 1 for said post, is justified in law or not? - Held, as per art. 243-T(4) offices of chairpersons in municipalities shall be reserved for SC, ST and women in such manner as legislature by an enactment may provide - Such chairmanships of municipalities would be made available by rotation - Therefore, category of women as contra-distinguished from category of SC and ST would form a separate electoral division for purpose of elections to posts of chairpersons of such municipalities - On a combined reading of art. 243-T, ss. 10(5) and 18 of Haryana Municipal Act and r. 70(4) of Election Rules, it becomes clear that Parliament as well as Legislature have enacted these provisions in order to provide for reservation of office of President for of members of SC, ST, Backward Classes and women in rotation - S. 10(5) and r. 70(4) shows that offices of Presidents are to be filled from amongst members belonging to different categories by rotation and by lots - Therefore, respondent 1 is a member who is elected on seat earmarked for General category women cannot be said to be a member elected on a seat reserved for SC women - Very concept of rotation presupposes that for contest of Presidentship once a reservation is made for members elected from a particular category only those members can contest for Presidentship - Appellant is sole candidate elected on seat reserved for SC women - As respondent 1 and appellant did not belong to same category of candidates elected on seats reserved for SC women even though both were women and belonged to SC they represented separate electoral wards - Appellant was sole candidate from ward reserved for SC women - Thus she was sole contestant for said post and was rightly held to be entitled to be President - Appeal allowed.
