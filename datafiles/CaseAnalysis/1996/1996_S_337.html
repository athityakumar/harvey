Singhai Lal Chand Jain(Dead) v Rashtriya Swayam Sewak Sangh,Panna And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 556; (1996) 3 SCC 149; AIR 1996 SC 1211; JT 1996 (3) SC 64; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 130; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, S. 11, O. 1 R. 8 - Temporary Occupation - Eviction From Premises - Res Judicata - Delivery of Possession - Suit before District Court for eviction of Respondents was filed and claimed title and for eviction of respondent's office bearers from premise as it had no authority to stay in premises - Appellant's requested for temporary occupation of respondents of premises for office purposes till alternative site was secured - Later respondents failed to vacate premises - Trial Court upheld plea of respondent and dismissed suit - HC allowed appeal and decreed suit for ejectment - Executing Court and HC upheld objection and dismissed petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether dismissal of suit by HC was correct in law and decree operate as a Res Judicata - Held, s.11 of Code, 1908 envisaged principle of Res Judicata , i.e., no court should try any suit or issue in which matter directly and substantially in issue had been directly in former suit between same parties, claim, litigating under same title, suit had been heard and finally decided by such Court - As respondents claimed under same title in previous suit and thereby they were bound by decree - Doctrine of Res Judicata prohibited members of respondent/Sangh to obstruct execution of decree - Since, decree of ejectment bind every member of respondent and appellant was entitled to had decree executed and possession to be taken by him - Respondents were directed to deliver vacant possession of premises to appellant - Appeal allowed.
