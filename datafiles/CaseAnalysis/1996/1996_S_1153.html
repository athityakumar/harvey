Kiran Bala v Surinder Kumar

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2604; (1996) 4 SCC 372; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 90; AIR 1996 SC 2094; 1996 (2) CCC 313; 1996 (3) CLT 9; 1996 (1) HLR 590; JT 1996 (5) SC 610; 1996(4) SCALE 440; 1996 (2) SCJ 542; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 277; 1996 (4) Supreme 313

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 60(1)(ccc) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Appeal arises against an order of in limine dismissal of a revision petition - Exemption from attachment and sale of residential house of judgment debtor - Finding that transfer was hit by provisions of s. 53 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882, being a fraudulent transfer as same had been effected to defeat and delay claim of creditor-plaintiff - Still judgment-debtor is entitled to protection u/s. 60(i)(ccc) - As a result of earlier decision, judgment debtor got a negative declaration that she continued to be owner in possession of house - Effect - Held, legal consequence is that she would be entitled to claim exemption from attachment or sale u/cl. (ccc) of s. 60(1) i.e., its result is, that said house cannot be attached or put to sale in execution of decree - Appeal allowed.
