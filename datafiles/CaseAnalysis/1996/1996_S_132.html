Kesar Singh and Others v Sadhu

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3162; (1996) 7 SCC 711; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 381; 1996 (2) CLT 104; 1996 (1) HLR 433; JT 1996 (2) SC 334; 1996 (1) RentLR 573; 1996(2) SCALE 177; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1017; 1996 (2) Supreme 302; 1996 (1) UJ 766

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Custom (Power to Contest) Act, 1920 - Respondent filed a suit in 1978 for recovery of possession of land from appellants on basis of a declaratory decree obtained by one Nathu in year 1924 as a collateral on basis of custom - Appellant took plea that since customary right had been taken away by an amendment made later, decree passed by trial court is a nullity - Whether appellants were entitled to raise plea of nullity? - Held, custom was confirmed and regulated by Punjab Laws Act and principal Act and it was done away with by Amendment Act - No statute was passed on basis of custom itself so as to transform custom itself into a higher statutory right - Therefore, either before or after custom has been done away with by Amendment Act, rights of parties under Hindu law remain unaffected and will provide rule of decision where alienations are contested under Hindu law - View of learned Judge that appellant is not entitled to raise executability of decree in execution is not correct since Amendment Act was applicable at time of passing decree by appellant court and above decree, therefore, is a nullity - Appeal allowed.
