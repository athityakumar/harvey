Director General of Posts and Others v B. Ravindran and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1705; (1997) SCC (L&S) 455; (1997) 1 SCC 641; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 686; 1997 (75) FLR 105; JT 1996 (10) SC 228; 1996(8) SCALE 279; 1997 (1) SLR 147; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 638; 1997 (5) Supreme 648

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1919, s. 96 B(4) - Civil Service Regulations, arts. 521 and 526 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 313 - Whether an ex - serviceman, who after his retirement before attaining age of 55 is re-employed in civil service, while getting his pay fixed, is entitled to an advance increment only if his pay plus pension equivalent of gratuity is less than last pay drawn at time of retirement? - Subsequent orders issued in 1978 and 1983 were supplementary in nature and did have a binding force - Under these circumstances, Govt. could not have, under guise of a clarificatory order, taken away right which had accrued to such re-employed pensioners with retrospective effect by declaring that while considering hardship last pay drawn at time of retirement was to be compared with initial pay plus pension whether ignorable or not - Held, 1985 clarificatory instructions were not only inconsistent with relevant provisions of Civil Service Regulations and 1978 and 1983 orders but its effect was to supersede said provision and orders - Tribunal was right in holding said instructions insofar as they directed to take into consideration ignorable part of pension also while considering hardship invalid and without any authority of law - Appeals dismissed.
