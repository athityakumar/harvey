P.S.Rajya v The State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2967; (1996) 9 SCC 1; (1996) SCC (Cr) 897; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 627; 1996 (2) Crimes 234; JT 1996 (6) SC 480; 1996(4) SCALE 344; 1996 (2) SCJ 705; 1996 (6) SLR 328; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 631; 1996 (4) Supreme 455

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Departmental enquiry - CBI - Criminal proceedings - Preliminary enquiry - Public service - Appellant was appointed as Inspector in the Income Tax Service in 1961 and the appellant's wife was a teacher - The appellant's wife was allotted on long lease a plot in 1980 for a sum of Rs. 20,000 by the Steel Authority of India Limited (SAIL) - As per terms and conditions imposed by SAIL, shops in the ground floor and residence at first floor were constructed by the appellant with his earnings as well as the earnings of his wife - Construction was strictly under the supervision and on the drawings supplied freely by SAIL township authority - Building was valued by SAIL township engineer at Rs. 4.75 lakhs - Appellant meanwhile got a promotion and in the course of his duties the appellant had impounded the books of accounts of certain business people who then complained to the local Congress Party M.P. and who in turn complained about the appellant to the Minister of Finance with a request to transfer the appellant and order a CBI inquiry - Following which the appellant's house and office were raided and nothing of importance was found but the charge sheet filed showed total assets of Rs. 9,88,093.91 whereas the estimated savings for the period were Rs. 6,30,000 and on that basis it was alleged that the assets were disproportionate to the extent of Rs. 3,57,439 - Appellant's house was valued at Rs. 7,69,300 as against the earlier valuation by the Income tax Department at Rs. 4.67 lakhs - Appellant aggrieved by the above charge that was taken into cognizance by the Special Judge challenged the same at the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC - HC allowed the petition and remitted the matter back to the Special Judge directing him to get a preliminary enquiry conducted by higher authority of the appellant or do it himself before taking cognizance of the matter - Special Judge took cognizance of the matter without undertaking a preliminary enquiry and also disregarded the enquiry by the appellant's department by which the appellant had been exonerated - On appeal against the cognizance by Special Judge the HC dismissed the application - Hence the instant appeal before the SC by the appellant challenging the cognizance by Special Judge - Whether prosecution against the appellant u/s. 5(2) read with s. 5(1)(e) of the 1947 Act was on valid grounds and whether the valuation of appellant's house by the CBI at Rs. 7,69,300 was accurate - Held, both the engineers who prepared the valuation report for income tax purposes also prepared the report for the CBI and there was no indication in the subsequent report as to why there was a difference in the value of the property - Value estimated by the income tax authority for Rs. 4.67 lakhs and even the valuation given by the Engineer and the Govt. approved valuer were very near to that figure, the reasonable value of that property was taken as 4.75 lakhs assessed by the Engineer on detailed estimate basis - Value estimated by CBI in the charge sheet was hence held to be inaccurate - Also the proceedings under 1947 Act were not on valid grounds as the report of the Central Vigilance Commission, exonerating the appellant of the same charge in departmental proceedings was not given due regard and thus no criminal proceedings initiated against the appellant could be pursued - Appeal allowed.
