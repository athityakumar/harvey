Watkins Mayor (Agrico) Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3507; 1996 (132) CTR 495; [1996] 219 ITR 563; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 689; 1996 (2) TLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 37, 256 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment year - Method of accounting - Change in method - Single integrated activity - Validity in law - Appellant was public limited company and for assessment year 1964-1965, assessee changed its method of arriving at net income - Taxable income from tea business by deducting 10 per cent of total profits from tea business on account of managing agency commission - For assessment year 1968-1969, it adopted different method again - Income Tax Officer rejected the change in method - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner upheld assessee's claim - Revenue appealed to Tribunal - Tribunal ordered that method of accounting adopted by assessee until assessment year 1964-65 was proper and proper allocation of managing agency commission was called for in proportion to expenditure incurred on those activities - Matter was remitted to Income Tax Officer and assessee applied for reference u/s. 256(1)of Act - HC agreed with Tribunal and answered in favour of Revenue and against assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether decision of Appellate Tribunal for assessment year of assessee's activity in various lines of activity constitute one single integrated activity or business was valid in law - Held, one or two circumstances among several circumstances relied upon are not relevant finding of fact recorded by Tribunal cannot be interfered with if there are other relevant circumstances which sustain finding - Appellant was banking company which invested in course of its business large sum in securities including securities interest from which was exempt from tax - While computing business income of assessee, profits and losses on purchase and sale of all such securities were duly taken into account - Appeals dismissed.
