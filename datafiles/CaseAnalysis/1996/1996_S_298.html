Shri Chander Chinar Bada Akhara Udasin Society and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 845; (1996) 5 SCC 732; AIR 1997 SC 399; AIR 1996 SCW 3778; JT 1996 (8) SC 89; 1996(6) SCALE 570; 1996 (3) SLR 375; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Medical colleges - Admission - Eligibility - Appellant, a registered society with the primary object to promote medical education by establishing medical colleges, hospitals and child welfare centers - A proposal was submitted to the State Govt. for setting up a medical college and a private hospital in Jammu - State Govt. had allotted 356 kanals of land for the proposed medical college and charitable hospital - A further allotment of 250 kanals of land was made by the State Govt. - Further the State Govt. recommended to the Jammu University for grant of affiliation to the proposed medical college to be set up by the society and also requested the Medical Council of India to grant approval/recognition to the said medical college - Inspection was made by the representatives of the Medical Council of India in respect of infrastructure including the teaching facilities and in due course temporary recognition of the institution was granted - Appellant had approached the State Govt. to issue necessary guidelines and to frame rules to regulate the admissions to the medical college established by the Society - State Govt. even after repeated requests failed to issue any guidelines - State Govt. had issued an advertisement inviting applications from eligible candidates for appearing at the entrance test for admission to the two Govt. medical colleges but it had not invited applications for the admission into Appellant's medical college - Some students seeking admission in the medical college of the Appellant filed a W.P. before the HC of Jammu & Kashmir making a grievance in respect of failure on the part of the State Govt. to frame necessary rules/guidelines to regulate admission to the medical college of the Society which had deprived them of the opportunity of seeking admission - All necessary records and information were placed before the HC - Inspite of the several opportunities being given to the State Govt. no affidavit on their behalf was filed - HC had constituted a Committee and issued directions for admission in the appellant's college - Subsequently, an appeal was filed by the State Govt. against the order passed by the HC, and matter was placed before the DB of the HC - DB of the HC set aside the order of the learned single Judge - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the DB of the HC was justified to interfere with the directions given by the learned single Judge and whether the directions issued by DB would affect the students whose admissions had been done on the basis of the directions of the committee appointed by the single Judge - Held, claim made by appellant as well as applicants who were admitted in the said medical college, that on equitable grounds they should be allowed to pursue their studies since it had already commenced could not be held valid - Admission criteria was not according to law - State Govt. had failed to issue guidelines for admission to appellant's medical college but only on equitable grounds, a procedure which was not sanctioned by law could not be approved only to mitigate the hardship of such candidates who had sought admissions in the medical college of the appellant - However, guidelines issued by DB were not approved and could not be enforced - A common entrance test was to be held for admissions - Also, it was not possible to complete the admission in the said medical college of the appellant for the session which had already commenced and that a fresh advertisement was to be issued for the next academic session in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
