Hari Shankar and another v State of U. P. and another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2870; (1996) 9 SCC 40; (1996) SCC (Cr) 913; JT 1996 (4) SC 740; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 34 and 302 - Whether ocular version of eyewitnesses could be accepted in sustaining conviction of appellant? - It is settled law that if genesis of motive of occurrence was not proved ocular testimony of witnesses as to occurrence could not be discarded only by reason of absence of motive, if otherwise evidence was worthy of reliance - Held, testimony of eyewitnesses is consistent with medical evidence and no fault could be found in same so as to render it doubtful or in any case unreliable - Conviction and sentence of appellant is well founded and there are absolutely no reasons or any grounds to interfere with same - Appeal dismissed.
