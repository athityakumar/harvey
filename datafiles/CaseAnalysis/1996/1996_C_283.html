Sundaram Clayton Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3487; (1996) 9 SCC 10; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 665; 1996 (133) CTR 151; [1996] 220 ITR 281; JT 1996 (5) SC 348; 1996(4) SCALE 246; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 253; 1996 (4) Supreme 711; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 187

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax, Bonus Shares, Debenture Holder, Computation of Capital, Computation of Capital Income, Computation of Capital Reserves

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, s. 2 - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964, Sch. II, rr. 1(iv), 1(v) and 3 - Whether assessee-company is entitled to benefit contemplated by second part of r. 3 of Sch. II to Surtax Act, 1964? - Held, by issuing bonus shares in assessment year in question there had only been a conversion of reserves into fully paid bonus shares, which conversion did not add up to capital or reserve base which was not there on first day of previous year - Before r. 3 of Surtax Act, 1964, can be made applicable, an increase in capital base as computed u/r. 1 has to be shown to have taken place - In order that r. 3 could apply capital base of company, as computed in accordance with r. 1 of Sch. II to Surtax Act, 1964, must have increased during previous year and such increase should be on account of increase of paid-up share capital or issue of debentures referred to in cl. (iv) or borrowing of any moneys referred to in cl. (v) of r. 1 - Unless these conditions are satisfied, there would be no occasion for assessee-company to get benefit contemplated by second part of r. 3 of Sch. II to Surtax Act, 1964 - Appeals dismissed.
