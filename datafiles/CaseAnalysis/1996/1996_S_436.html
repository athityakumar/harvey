Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of India Ltd. v Srinivas Agencies and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 591; (1996) 4 SCC 165; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 255; 1996 (2) CompLJ 421; 1996(2) SCALE 774; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1913 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 446, 537 and 529(1),(2)(A) - Whether the secured creditors to realize their debts from the assets of a company which is under winding-up or has been wound up, by approaching for other than the Company Court, is valid? - Held, in a particular case the secured creditor who has approached the Civil Court happens to be one who has lent huge amount - In such a situation on approach being made by such creditor, would duly take note of this fact by the Company Court and should like to grant leave required by s. 446(1) and by the same token refuse to transfer the proceeding to his Court - The Company Court shall also apprise itself about the fact whether dues of workmen are outstanding and if so, extent of the same - It would be seen whether after the assets of the company are allowed to be used to satisfy the debt of the secured creditor, it would be possible to satisfy the workmen's dues pari passu - Appeal disposed of.
