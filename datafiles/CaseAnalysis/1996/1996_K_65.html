Union of India and Others v Kameshwar Prasad

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1690; (1997) 11 SCC 650

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Posts and Telegraphs Extra Departmental Agents (Conduct and Service) Rules, 1964, r. 9(3) - Whether an Extra Departmental Agent in Posts and Telegraphs Department of Govt. of India who was 'Put-off Duty' on account of pendency of departmental proceedings against him is entitled to allowances for period of 'Put-off Duty' on his being exonerated in departmental proceedings? - Held, r. 9(3) of Rules prescribes that 'an employee shall not be entitled to any allowance for period for which he is kept off duty' - Said provision does not envisage an exception in matter of payment of allowances for period employee was kept off duty if employee is exonerated in departmental proceedings - Tribunal was not justified in directing payment of allowances to respondents for period they were kept off duty - Impugned judgments of Tribunal set aside - Appeal allowed.
