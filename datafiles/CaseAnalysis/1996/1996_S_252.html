State of Orissa v Sharat Chandra Sahu and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3741; (1996) 6 SCC 435; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1387; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 849; AIR 1997 SC 1; 1996 (4) CCR 211; 1996 (4) Crimes 212; 1996(7) SCALE 588; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 304; 1996 (8) Supreme 806

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 155(4) and 198(1), Proviso, Cl. (c) - Quashment of complaint of non-cognizable nature - Two offences, one cognizable another non-cognizable - Offences relating to marriage - Quashment ground, non-filing of complaint by aggrieved party - Held, not proper - Under s. 155 police can investigate non-cognizable offence also as cognizable offence irrespective of fact as to who filed it.
