Commissioner of Income Tax and Others v G. Parthasarathy Naidu and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3523; (1998) 8 SCC 487; 1999 (155) CTR 180; [1999] 236 ITR 350; [1999] 104 TAXMAN 197

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether two separate partnership firms having same partners can be treated as same entity for purpose of assessment of income tax? - Intention of partners is of paramount importance - Firms constituted under separate deeds of partnership - Relationship of partners is founded on agreement executed - Held, in present case, partners intended to form two distinct partnership firms - Reference made to K Kelukutty case 1985 Indlaw SC 138 - Appeal allowed.
