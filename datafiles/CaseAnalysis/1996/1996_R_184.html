Ranganath Parmeshwar Panditrao Mali and Another v Eknath Gajanan Kulkarni and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3383; (1996) 7 SCC 681; AIR 1996 SC 1290; 1996 (4) Bom.C.R. 640; [1997] 223 ITR 601; JT 1996 (1) SC 293; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 112 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Evidence Act - Presumption of valid marriage in order to determine status of sons born there from as regarding succession - Fact of living together and marriage proved by evidence on record and also admitted by adversary - Ignoring it, High Court gave finding that such presumption would arise only if factum of marriage is proved - Interference sought from Supreme Court - Held, finding not proper and liable to be interfered with u/art. 136 of Constitution - And sons are legal heirs of property of husband and succeed to property - Appeal allowed.
