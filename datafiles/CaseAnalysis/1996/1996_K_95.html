State of Punjab and Another v Kashmir Singh

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2163; (1996) 10 SCC 356; (1997) SCC (L&S) 88; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 312; JT 1996 (8) SC 498; 1996 (5) SLR 303; 1996 (7) Supreme 2; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 19

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Indian Penal Code, s. 304 A - FIR issued against respondent u/s. 304 A IPC, 1860 - Disciplinary enquiry on charge of causing loss to Corporation by driving bus rashly and negligently - Criminal court acquitted respondent - Fresh disciplinary enquiry ordered - Respondent found guilty - On appeal, order of dismissal was declared illegal and respondent was held entitled to all benefits flowing from such declaration - Whether order of dismissal of respondent from service on basis of report of enquiry officer was void and illegal? - Held, enquiry report and order imposing punishment not available - Thus, basis of judgment of first Appellate court becomes indefensible - Copy of judgment, order of criminal court acquitting respondent, proceedings ordering fresh enquiry and reasons on which fresh disciplinary enquiry was ordered, not shown - Matters require further examination - Appeal allowed.
