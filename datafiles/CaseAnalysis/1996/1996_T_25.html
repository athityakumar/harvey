Messrs Escorts Farms (Ramgarh) Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2506; (1996) 6 SCC 357; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 613; 1996 (136) CTR 434; [1996] 222 ITR 509; 1996(7) SCALE 221; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 18; 1996 (8) Supreme 239; [1996] 88 TAXMAN 453; 1996 (2) TLR 966

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1964, Finance Act, 1966

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Finance Act, 1964 - Finance Act, 1966 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 45 - (A) Whether the assessee was justified in taking the value of the shares at their original cost under s. 45 of the Income Tax Act, 1961? - (B) How the cost of the acquisition of the original shares should be determined for the purposes of capital gains tax? - (C) How the cost of acquisition of the original shares is to be determined when bonus shares are issued subsequently? - Whether the assessee is an investor or dealer in shares and shares sold are original shares or bonus shares? - Held, the principle so laid down is one of the general application - The character of the owner of the shares as an 'investor' or as a 'dealer' is of no consequence - There is no 'dichotomy' as to whether the shares are held by an 'investor' or 'dealer' in shares - The assessee had opted to take the cost of acquisition as provided by the relevant statute, i.e., statutory cost of acquisition and thus substituting the market value as on 01-01-1954 in the place of actual cost of acquisition, and only in such a case, the subsequent issue of bonus shares cannot affect the issue - Appeals dismissed.
