State of Punjab and Others v Dr Harbhajan Singh Greasy

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3054; (1996) 9 SCC 322; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1248; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 404; JT 1996 (5) SC 403; 1996 (2) LLJ 636; 1996(4) SCALE 195; 1996 (3) SCJ 125; 1996 (4) SLR 30; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 288; 1996 (5) Supreme 187; 1996 (2) UJ 237; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 2041

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Respondent charged for being absent from duty in emergency of attending to flood victims - Charge of dereliction of duty - Admission of having private practice - Disciplinary authority removed respondent from service - High Court allowed writ petition and directed reinstatement with consequential benefits - Held, when enquiry was found to be faulty, it could not be proper to direct reinstatement with consequential benefits - Matter requires to be remitted to disciplinary authority to follow procedure from stage at which fault was pointed out and to take action according to law - Pending enquiry, delinquent must be deemed to be under suspension - Consequential benefits would depend upon result of enquiry and order passed thereon - Respondent is not entitled to back wages - Appeal allowed.
