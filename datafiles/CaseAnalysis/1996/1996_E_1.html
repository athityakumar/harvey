Visakhapatnam Dock Labour Board v E. Atchanna and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3067; (1996) 2 SCC 484; (1996) SCC (L&S) 526; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 353; 1996 (33) ATC 224; 1996 (1) CLR 598; JT 1996 (3) SC 6; 1996 (2) LLJ 95; 1996 (2) LLN 85; 1996(1) SCALE 731; 1996 (2) SCJ 299; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1126; 1996 (1) SLR 797; 1996 (2) Supreme 90; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 1470

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Superannuation - Date of birth - Correction of - Interlocutory stage - Directions sought - Held,

the appellant had issued a circular D/- 10-07-1987 and it was initiated to all concerned after Govt. of India had issued the notification D/- 30-11-1979 prescribing the procedure to be followed for change of date of birth - It is not in dispute that the appellant is a Central Govt. undertaking and that the said notification which is incorporated as Note. 5 to Fundamental Rule 56(m) applies to the respondents - Therefore, for alteration of their dates of birth the respondent were required to the steps within 5 years from the date of coming into force of the said notification - Even after the circular was issued by the appellant, the respondents did not approach the appellant within reasonable time - The respondents had sought alteration on the basis of the certificates which did not provide irrefutable proof as regards their correct dates of birth, more particularly for the reasons pointed out by the Single Judge - Further, the request for referring the respondents to the Medical Board was refused by the appellant - That prayer was also rejected by the Single Judge, whether that should have been done or not is itself in issue in the appeals - The impugned directions given at an interlocutory stage are very likely to cause serious prejudice to the appellant's case - Appeal allowed.
