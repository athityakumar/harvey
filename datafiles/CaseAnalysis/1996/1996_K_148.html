Krishan Gopal Sharma and Another v Government of N.C.T. of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3922; (1996) 4 SCC 513; 1996 (2) Crimes 218; JT 1996 (5) SC 102; 1996(4) SCALE 378; 1996 (2) SCJ 563; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 472

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Arbitrary And Capricious

Summary: Socio-Economic - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7 and 16 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, r. 47 - Adulteration of pan masala and mouth freshener - Conviction for not following standard saccharin content of pan masala and mouth freshener - Violation of r. 47 - Validity of - Held, even if the complaint is accepted to be correct, the only offence committed by the appellants amounts to technical violation of the mandate of r. 47 for adding saccharin to the extent of 2000 and 2450 p.p.m. in the Chutki Pan Masala and Mouth Freshener - Such addition of saccharin cannot be held to be injurious to health because, considering later findings on research and analysis on the effect of saccharin on human system, addition of saccharin to the extent of 8000 p.p.m. in pan masala has been allowed by amending r. 47 - The articles sold are not alleged to be injurious to health and such allegations, even if made, cannot be accepted - There is no allegation that any other injurious substance was added to the articles sold making them potentially health hazards - It is also not the case that pan masala and mouth freshener were of inferior quality and substandard - In a case like the present one, the offence committed is on account of technical violation of r. 47 - It should be emphasized that strict adherence to Prevention of Food Adulteration Act and Rules framed there under should be insisted and enforced for safeguarding the interest of consumers of articles of food - In the special facts of the present cases, a deterrent punishment of imprisonment is not called for and imposition of fine will meet the ends of justice - The criminal cases were initiated on the basis of samples taken in 1987 - The accused-appellants have already faced the ordeal of criminal trials for a number of years - In such circumstances, further agony of criminal trials need not be prolonged - Fine imposed - Appeals disposed of .
