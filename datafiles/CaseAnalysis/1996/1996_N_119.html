Dharma v Nirmal Singh Alias Bittu and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3301; (1996) SCC (Cr) 444; (1996) 7 SCC 471; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 943; AIR 1996 SC 1136; 1996 (1) CCR 139; 1996 (1) Crimes 83; 1996 CRLJ 1631; JT 1996 (4) SC 608; 1996(1) SCALE 677; 1996 (2) SCJ 259; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 115; 1996 (1) Supreme 780

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 142 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 401(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 375, 376, 511, 300 and 302 - Appeal against acquittal - Are powers of Supreme Court circumscribed by any limitation - Offence was of attempt to rape and murder - Testimony of sole eye-witness corroborated by medical evidence - Extra judicial confession made before Sarpanch was also reliable - Recovery of weapon connecting him with crime - Fact of accused absconding also proved - Held, powers of Supreme Court not limited - Accused was guilty of committing offence of attempt to rape and murder making acquittal out of question while life imprisonment was to be awarded - It was not rarest of rare cases so death penalty could not be awarded.
