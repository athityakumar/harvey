Delhi Science Forum and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 574; (1996) 2 SCC 405; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 1065; AIR 1996 SC 1356; AIR 1996 SCW 953; 1996 (1) CCC 235; 1996 (1) CLT 540; 1996 (2) CompLJ 1; JT 1996 (2) SC 295; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 118; 1996(2) SCALE 218; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 767; 1996 (2) Supreme 182

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: National Telecom Policy

Summary: Constitution of India, arts.14 and 32, Telegraph Act (13 of 1885), s.4(1) - Writ Petition - Policies adopted by parliament - Infringing art.14 - Tenders invited for telephone service - New telecom policy encourage private sector - Improve quality & coverage - Govt. company debarred from bidding - Ulterior motive - Govt. companies ignored and discriminated - Held, Telecom Authority is free to restrict the number of the service areas for which one Company can be licensed and Govt. companies are not ignored or discriminated.
