Union of India v Sube Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2352; (1997) 9 SCC 69; 1997 (29) ALR 139; JT 1996 (10) SC 529; 2000 (Supp) RD 388; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23(2) and 28 - Whether High Court has jurisdiction to entertain application for enhancement after Amendment Act 68 of 1984 came into force? - Since Appellate Court has no power to amend decree and grant enhanced compensation by way of solatium and interest u/s. 23(2) and proviso to s. 28 of Act, as amended by Act of 1984, it is a question of jurisdiction of court - Since courts have no jurisdiction, it is settled legal position that it is a nullity and it can be raised at any stage - Held, HC committed no error of law - So order is valid in law - Award of solatium @ 30% u/s. 23(2) of Act and interest 9% for one year from date of taking possession and 15% thereafter till date of deposit under proviso to s. 28 stands set aside and original order of HC D/-24-7-1984 stands restored - Appeals allowed.
