Income Tax Officer (Iii), Circle-I, Salem and Another v Arunagiri Cheitiar

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2882; (1996) 9 SCC 33; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 174; AIR 1996 SC 2160; 1996 (134) CTR 167; [1996] 220 ITR 232; JT 1996 (5) SC 254; 1996(4) SCALE 501; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 461; 1996 (5) Supreme 202; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 330; 1996 (2) TLR 528

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body, Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Civil Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 189(3) - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 25 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 50(1) - Assessment years 1962-63 and 1963-64 - Whether an erstwhile partner is liable to pay tax arrears due from partnership firm pertaining to period when he was a partner? - Held, every person who was, during previous year, a partner of a firm, and LR of any such person who is deceased, shall be jointly and severally liable along with firm for amount of tax, penalty or other sum payable by firm for assessment year to which such previous year is relevant, and all provisions of this Act, so far as may be, shall apply to assessment of such tax or imposition or levy of such penalty or other sum - Appeals allowed.
