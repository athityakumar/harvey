Girdhari Parmanand Vadhava v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2466; (1996) 11 SCC 179; (1997) SCC (Cr) 159; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 29; AIR 1996 SCW 3957; 1996 (4) CCR 27; 1996 (4) Crimes 77; JT 1996 (8) SC 473; 1996(7) SCALE 103; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 631; 1996 (7) Supreme 416

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 19 and 3(2)(i) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 364, 368, 385, 302/120-B and 140 - Issue - (A) Meaning of 'act of terror' and terrorist activity - A crime even if perpetrated with extreme brutality may not constitute 'terrorist activity' within meaning of s. 3(1) - For constituting 'terrorist activity' u/s. 3(1) of TADA, activity must be intended to strike terror in people or a section of people or bring about other consequences referred to in said s. 3(1) - Terrorist activity is not confined to unlawful activity or crime committed against an individual or individuals but it aims at bringing about terror in minds of people or section of people disturbing public order, public peace and tranquillity, social and communal harmony, disturbing or destabilising public administration and threatening security and integrity of country - It is impact of crime and its fallout on society and potentiality of such crime in producing fear in minds of people or a section of people, which makes a crime, a terrorist activity u/s. 3(1) of TADA - Held, intention to strike terror in minds of people can be reasonably inferred in present case because of declaration made by one of the person that if their demand was not met, extreme consequence of killing of an innocent person would be resorted to - In order to send such message to society, it was decided that deceased would be killed and he was killed for giving effect to intended threat to people - If an innocent boy is killed only because demand for ransom amount was not met by family members, such killing cannot but send a shockwave and bring about terror in minds of people of locality - Conviction upheld - Enhancement of sentence disallowed as it is not rarest of rare case - Appeal dismissed.
