Election Commission Of India And Another v Dr. Subramanian Swamy And Another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.N. Kirpal, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2843; (1996) 4 SCC 104; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 750; AIR 1996 SC 1810; 1996 (2) CCC 132; 1996 (2) CLT 310; JT 1996 (4) SC 463; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 65; 1996(3) SCALE 734; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 637; 1996 (3) Supreme 679

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Chief Election Commissioner And Other Election Commissioners (Conditions Of Service) Act, 1991

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 192(2) and 324 - Election Commission - Opinion of - Disqualification of being Member of legislative Assembly - Petition preferred to State Governor - Governor forwarded petition to Election Commission for its opinion - Allegation by MLA of bias against Chief Election Commr. - Plea, it must be of all those who constitute that body or not at all - Held, plea is narrow reading of provisions - Doctrine of necessity applies.
