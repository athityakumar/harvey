Poonam Verma v Ashwin Patel And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2999; (1996) 4 SCC 332; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 634; AIR 1996 SC 2111; 1996 (2) CCC 322; 1997 (2) CompLJ 1; 1996 (2) CPJ 1; 1996 (4) CTJ 465; JT 1996 (5) SC 1; 1996(4) SCALE 364; 1996 (2) SCJ 623; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 671; 1996 (4) Supreme 328

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Human Rights

Keywords: Homoeopathy Central Council Act, 1973, Antibiotics

Summary: Medical Council Act 1956, s.15(3) ; Consumer Protection Act 1986, s.14 ; Bombay Homeopathic and Biochemic Practitioners Act 1960, s.23 ; Maharashtra Medical Council Act 1965, s.16 - Negligence by Doctor - Doctor registered as medical practioner, entitled to practise in Homeopathy only, prescribed allopathic medicine - Death of patient took place - Held, doctor is guilty of negligence per se and therefore, liable to pay damages.
