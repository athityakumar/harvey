Bengal Waterproof Limited v Bombay Waterproof Mnfg. Company

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4090; (1997) 1 SCC 99; AIR 1997 SC 1398; 1996 ARBLR 335; 1997 (1) AWC 324; 1997 (2) BLJR 1728; JT 1996 (10) SC 822; 1997 (17) PTC 98; 1996(8) SCALE 369; [1996] 8 S.C.R. 695; 1998 (Supp) RD 302; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 695; 1997 (1) UJ 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 2, r. 2 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 22 - Whether, second suit of the plaintiff is barred by O. 2, r. 2(3), CPC, 1908 as on merits the High Court held defendants's trademark phonetically and visually resembled that of plaintiff's, and there being no cross objections or cross Special Leave Petitions by the respondents? - Gurbux Singh v. Bhooralal 1964 Indlaw SC 67 followed - Defendants not produced on the record of the Trial Court, the pleadings in the first suit - Held, complete bar against the defendants from raising the bar of O. 2, r. 2 and sub-r. (3) against the plaintiff in the present case - Further court held, in cases of continuous causes of action or recurring causes of action, bar of O. 2, r. 2 and sub-r. (3) cannot be invoked - It would be probable in this regard to invoke s. 22 of Limitation Act - Appeal allowed.
