Alankar Granites Industries and Others v P.G.R. Scindia, Mla and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3794; (1996) 7 SCC 416; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 595; JT 1996 (1) SC 320; 1996 (4) KarLJ 40; 1996(1) SCALE 373; 1996 (1) SCJ 633; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 721; 1996 (1) Supreme 425

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1969, rr. 3, 3A and 66 - Govt. of Karnataka issued an order D/- 18-6-1991 for grant of quarry leases for black, pink and multi-coloured granite u/r. 3 contrary to prohibition contained in r. 3-A - Challenged in High Court in writ petitions - Allowed by learned Single Judge and those grants were quashed - Writ appeals dismissed - Hence, present appeals by special leave - Whether in present case r. 66 was available to make relaxation notwithstanding express prohibition contained in r. 3-A? - Held, a plain reading of Govt. order D/- 18-6-1991 leaves no doubt that it was power only u/r. 3 which was exercised for making these grants and this conclusion was reached on basis of legal opinion obtained by Govt. for this purpose - R. 66 requires Govt. to form its opinion that public interest requires grant of quarrying leases on such terms and conditions other than those prescribed in these Rules, as Govt. may, by order, specify - This requirement precedes making of grant on terms and conditions specified for purpose and it cannot be subsequent to grant for purpose of justifying grant without prior satisfaction to this effect leading to relaxation of Rules - Moreover, by its very nature, power of relaxation is to be exercised separately in each individual case, which too has not been done in case of these 203 leases - No basis to uphold grant of any of these 203 quarry leases u/r. 66, particularly, when that Rule was not even resorted to by Govt. for making any of these grants - Further held, prohibition in r. 3-A did exist at time of making impugned grants and, therefore, these grants having been made against said prohibition were rightly held to be invalid and do not require any further consideration - Judgment of HC holding these grants to be invalid does not suffer from any infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
