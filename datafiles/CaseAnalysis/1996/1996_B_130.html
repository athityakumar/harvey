State of Bihar and Others v Bihar Chambers of Commerce and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3811; (1996) 9 SCC 136; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 186; AIR 1996 SC 2344; JT 1996 (2) SC 53; 1996(1) SCALE 760; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 184; 1996 (103) STC 1; 1996 (2) Supreme 44

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas for Consumption, Use or Sale therein Act, 1993, s. 3 - Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 249 to 253, 255, 301 and 304(b) - Levy of tax - Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957 - (A) Whether impugned tax imposed under Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas for Consumption, Use or Sale therein Act, 1993, has been established to be compensatory or whether it can be treated as a regulatory measure? - Held, State does provide several facilities to trade including laying and maintenance of roads, waterways and markets, etc. - As a matter of fact, since levy is by State, therefore facilities provided by State also have to look into for ascertaining whether State has established compensatory character of tax - On this basis, it must be held that State has established that impugned tax is compensatory in nature - (B) Whether it is saved by virtue of provision contained in art. 304(b) r/w art. 255 of Constitution? - Held, in absence of any material to contrary, averment of State accepted and it is held that requirement of prior consent has been satisfied in case of impugned Act - (C) Whether impugned tax constitutes a reasonable restriction and whether it is imposed in public interest? - Held, no extra burden is cast by impugned Act insofar as four out of six commodities mentioned in its Schedule are concerned - These facts are not disputed by anyone - So far as tobacco products are concerned, there is an additional levy of three per cent by virtue of impugned Act and to that extent it may be said to impede freedom guaranteed by art. 301 - Extraordinary high level of excise duties on tobacco is meant precisely to discourage its consumption - It is not possible to say that addition of three per cent is either an unreasonable restriction on freedom of trade and commerce or that it is not required in public interest - (D) Whether Bihar Legislature is deprived of its legislative competence to enact impugned Act on account of enactment of ADE Act and/or because State of Bihar is getting a portion of taxes levied and collected under ADE Act? - Held, unable to say that by agreeing to take a share in proceeds of additional duties of excise, States of Bihar has deprived itself of its power to levy entry tax under and by virtue of Entry 52 in List II in Seventh Schedule to Constitution - ADE Act does not affect legislative competence of State Legislature to make a law with reference to any of entries in List II - (E) Whether impugned enactment is outside purview of Entry 52 in List II of Seventh Schedule to Constitution and, therefore, beyond legislative competence of Bihar Legislature? - Held, impugned Act is also not relatable to any of arts. 249 to 253 which are in nature of exceptions to normal rule that Parliament can make no law with respect to entries in List II - If so, it follows that State Legislatures are not denuded or deprived of their power to make a law either with reference to Entry 52 or with reference to Entry 54 in List II - That power remains untouched and unaffected - Appeal disposed of.
