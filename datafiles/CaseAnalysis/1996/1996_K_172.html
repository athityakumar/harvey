Kuldip Singh v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2418; (1996) 10 SCC 659; AIR 1997 SC 79; 1997 (1) AWC 90; JT 1996 (8) SC 491; 1997 (1) LLJ 131; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 339; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 20

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2), Proviso - Police Act, 1861 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25 and 26 - Police services - Disciplinary enquiry - Confession before police or while in police custody - Disciplinary authority can act upon it (if true and voluntary) - Holding of disciplinary enquiry against delinquent Police Head Constable having links with terrorists, reasonably not practicable - Dismissal order challenged - Held, Supreme Court would not examine question whether confession was voluntary and true - Appeal dismissed.
