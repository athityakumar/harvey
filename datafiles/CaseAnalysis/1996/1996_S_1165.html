Biswa Rajan Sahoo & Ors. v Sushanta Kumar Dinda & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 464; (1996) 5 SCC 365; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1179; AIR 1996 SC 2552; 1996 (2) CLR 617; 1996 (74) FLR 2737; JT 1996 (6) SC 515; 1996 LabIC 2253; 1996 (2) LLJ 763; 1996 (2) LLN 863; 1996(5) SCALE 297; 1996 (3) SLJ 62; 1996 (5) SLR 172; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether the notice was required to be issued to the persons affected and whether they needed to be heard? - Held, lack of integrity and sincerity in the selection process - Tribunal was right in not issuing notice to the persons who are said to have been selected and appointed - Procedure adopted are in breach of the rules offending arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Appeals dismissed.
