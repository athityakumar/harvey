Kirtikant D. Vadodaria v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4125; (1996) 4 SCC 479; (1996) SCC (Cr) 762; 1996 (4) Bom.C.R. 197; JT 1996 (6) SC 244; 1996 (2) KLT 415; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 45

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 125 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(20) - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act 1956, s. 18(1) - Whether a 'stepmother' can claim maintenance from stepson or not, having regard to aims and objects of s. 125 of CrPC, 1973? - Provisions in s. 125 provide a speedy remedy to those women, children and destitute parents who are in distress - Dominant purpose behind benevolent provisions contained in s. 125 clearly is that wife, child and parents should not be left in a helpless state of distress, destitution and starvation - A childless stepmother may claim maintenance from her stepson provided she is a widow or her husband, if living, is also incapable of supporting and maintaining her - Held, 'stepmother' respondent 2 has got 5 natural born sons who are all major and at least 3 of them are well-to-do and capable of maintaining their mother - Husband of respondent 2 also possessed of sufficient means and property besides monthly income that he derives from business of snuff enabling him to maintain and support his second wife - Respondent 2 proceeded against her stepson with a view to punish and cause harassment to appellant, which is wholly unjustified - Respondent 2 is not entitled to claim any maintenance from stepson, appellant herein - Orders of High Court and courts below are set aside - Appeal allowed.
