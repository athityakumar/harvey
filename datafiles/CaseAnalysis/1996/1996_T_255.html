Ramesh Babulal Doshi v The State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2602; (1996) 9 SCC 225; (1996) SCC (Cr) 972; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 205; AIR 1996 SC 2035; AIR 2000 SCW 1430; 1996 (3) CCR 16; 1996 (2) Crimes 194; 1996 CRLJ 2867; JT 1996 (6) SC 79; 1996(4) SCALE 185; 1996 (3) SCJ 46; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 265; 1996 (4) Supreme 167

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 386 - Powers of appellate Court in appeal against acquittal - High Court reappraising evidence without considering Trial Court's findings - Especially when Lower Court had given clear reason for disbelieving evidence - Held, HC would not be justified while reversing acquittal by reappraising evidence and not even considering findings of Trial Court or demonstrating that they were not sustainable at all - Appeal allowed.
