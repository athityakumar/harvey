All India State Bankofficers Federation and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1276; (1997) 9 SCC 151; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1004; 1997 (75) FLR 288; JT 1996 (8) SC 550; 1997 (1) LLJ 419; 1997 (1) LLN 543; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 255; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 131

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Banking & Finance - Service - Banking Regulation Act,1949 - State Bank of India Act, 1955 - Eligibility norms - Respondent bank was established by 1955 Act and according to s. 17 of the said Act its Management was entrusted to the Central Board constituted under the Act - Bank framed State Bank of India Service Rules, 1975 which dealt with various grades and scales of officers, conduct rules, salary, seniority, promotion - In exercise of the powers, the Central Board of the Bank had been determining the policy for promotion to various grades of scales of officers - Central Board approved a promotion policy under which assignment appraisals and interviews were made the two components of the selection procedure - According to the petitioners, zone of consideration from amongst the eligible officers had been varying from time to time - Central Board approved a modification in the promotion policy in terms of which officers with two years service in Senior Management Grade Scale V were to be eligible for promotion to Top Executive Grade Scale VI and officers with two years service in Top Executive Grade Scale VI were to be eligible for promotion to Top Executive Grade Scale VII - Central Board of the Bank in its meeting made two modifications in the then existing policy - As a result of the modification qualifying marks for performance appraisal had been changed and the manner in which the performance had been appraised had also undergone a change from time to time - Petitioner contended that it was unreasonable not to limit the zone of consideration to three to four times the number of vacancies - It was then contended that the change in the policy was motivated with a view to benefit respondents nos. 4 and 5 who were working as Chief Officer and as Private Secretary to the chairman of the Bank at Central Office, Bombay - It was alleged that on account of the positions so held they wielded a lot of power in determining the personal policies of the bank - Whether the change in the policy rules of the bank was arbitrary and without any reason - Held, bank was a business organisation and it must identify the best available talent in the organisation for holding challenging assignments in the top executive grades - There was nothing wrong if the bank devised a policy defining the eligibility norms on a realistic basis and devised a system whereby the best available talent would be chosen to man the critical positions - Keeping this objective in view, the changes in the promotion policy had been made - It was always for the employer to see how to promote and utilise the best talent available in the organisation - The changes so made could not be regarded as arbitrary and the Court could not sit in appeal and decide as to what was good for the institution - Petition dismissed.
