Binay Kumar Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3712; (1997) 1 SCC 283; (1997) SCC (Cr) 333; AIR 1997 SC 322; AIR 1997 SCW 78; 1996 (4) CCR 253; 1997 CRLJ 362; JT 1996 (10) SC 79; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 620; 1996(7) SCALE 910; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 134, 145 and 155(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 149 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134 - (A) Criminal appeal - Accused convicted by Trial Court - Not filing appeal before High Court - Appeal against conviction filed by him before Supreme Court - Whether maintainable? - (B) Evidence of witness - Impeachment - Scope - Non compliance with procedure prescribed in s. 145 of 1872 Act - Attention of witness was not drawn to recorded statement attributed to him - Whether credit of witness can be impeached by proof of any statement which is inconsistent with any part of his evidence in court - Whether evidence of witness cannot be rejected? - (C) Evidence Act, s. 3 Identification of accused - Massacre - Drawing hiatus between injured witnesses and non - injured witnesses as for capacity of identify assailants while in action - Whether proper? - (D) Evidence Act, s. 11 - Plea of Alibi - Strict proof is required for establishing plea of alibi - Finding of fact disbelieving plea of alibi based on weight and sturdy reasons - Interference u/art. 136 whether proper? - (E) Evidence Act, s. 3 - Appreciation of evidence of eye - witnesses - Concurrent finding regarding reliability of evidence of those witnesses - Whether reappreciation on ground of some discrepancies permissible? - (F) Number of witnesses - Whether testimony of single witness sufficient? - In case large size of unlawful assembly, whether single witness sufficient for identification of accused - Held, (A) not maintainable - (B) Not unless his attention is called to those parts of it which are to be used for purpose of contradicting him - Evidence of witness cannot be rejected - (C) Not proper - (D) No interference - (E) Reappreciation not permissible as discrepancies were not material or serious - (F) Even a testimony of single witness is sufficient - In case large size assembly, court can insist on at least two reliable witnesses - Appeal dismissed.
