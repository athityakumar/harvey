Union of India and Others v A. Sanyasi Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3490; (1996) 3 SCC 465; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 401; AIR 1996 SC 1219; AIR 1996 SCW 1251; 1996 (132) CTR 81; [1996] 219 ITR 330; JT 1996 (2) SC 425; 1996(2) SCALE 280; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 570; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 321; 1996 (2) TLR 357

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Classification For Taxation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Constitutionality of provisions of ss. 28, 44AC and 206C of Income Tax Act, 1961, facilitating collection of tax due from specified traders - Adoption of purchase price as measure of tax - Held, provision not violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Part of s. 44AC which dispenses with regular assessment is unreasonable - Appeal partly allowed.
