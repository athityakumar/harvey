Babu Singh Bains and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2539; (1996) 6 SCC 565; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 882; AIR 1997 SC 116; JT 1996 (9) SC 371; 1996 (2) RentLR 421; 1996(7) SCALE 548; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Public Premises Act, s. 5 - Chandigarh (Sale of Sites and Buildings) (Amendment) Rules, 1979, r. 11-D - Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1952, ss. 8-A and 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 14, 136, 226 and 19(1)(f) - Appellant having had allotment of freehold plot had constructed a double-storeyed house - Used it as Guest House, contrary to regulations - Notice of resumption u/s. 8-A of Act was given to appellant - Became final - Notice issued to appellant for taking possession of land - Resumption order as well as order of eviction became final and conclusive against appellant - Appellant then filed an application after 13 years u/r. 11-D of Rules - (A) Whether appellant is entitled to avail of remedy u/art. 32? - Held, Court had permitted appellant to convince this Court as to invalidity of resumption order passed u/s. 8-A but he could not point out any invalidity nor did he raise any contention as regards validity of s. 8A - Therefore, principle of contention res judicata stands fast in his way to raise same contention once over - (B) Whether s. 8-A, Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1952 valid? - Held, once statute occupied field and gave power to resume land or building subject to conditions mentioned therein, general principles of resumption and other principles considered therein absolutely remained no more relevant - S. 8-A is valid in law and, therefore, not violative of art. 14 since it has prescribed an inbuilt procedure of giving an opportunity and right to adduce evidence and consideration thereof by Estate Officer before resumption and to pass a reasoned order in support thereof - Order is also subject to an appeal before Chief Administrator - If permissible, within circumscribed parameters, appellate order may be subject to judicial review u/art. 226 - Therefore, resumption u/s. 8-A is not only in case of non-payment of dues under sale but for violation of conditions of sale, rules, regulations and other relevant conditions applicable in that behalf - Therefore, no invalidity in s. 8 - (C) Whether appellant's application u/r. 11-D Chandigarh (Sale of Sites and Buildings) (Amendment) Rules, 1979 has not been considered in proper perspective by Estate Officer and whether any illegality has been committed by HC by not interfering therewith? - Held, considering reasoning given by Estate Officer in his order and conclusion reached by HC, no illegality in refusing to condone delay - R. 11-D is only an enabling provision to make an application - Estate Officer has discretion to re-grant land or building which stood already resumed and vested in State - Cannot arbitrarily reject petition, and has to give reasons in support thereof - Not given any proper explanation for inordinate delay of 13 years in not making application - Appeals and writ petition dismissed.
