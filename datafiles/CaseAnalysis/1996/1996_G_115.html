The State Of Punjab v Gurmit Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1087; (1996) SCC (Cr) 316; (1996) 2 SCC 384; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 492; AIR 1996 SC 1393; AIR 1996 SCW 998; 1996 (4) CCR 134; 1996 (1) Crimes 37; 1996 CRLJ 1728; JT 1996 (1) SC 298; 1996 (4) KarLJ 132; 1996(1) SCALE 309; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 532; 1996 (1) Supreme 485

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 376, 376A, 376B, 376C - CrPC, 1973, ss. 154, 156, 327(2)(3) and 354 - (A) Testimony of prosecutrix - Reliability - Investigating agency not conducting investigation properly or was negligent - Whether can be a ground to discredit testimony of prosecutrix - (B) Sexual offences - Delay in lodging FIR - Whether material - (C) Testimony of prosecutrix - Appreciation of - After committing rape, accused persons leaving prosecutrix near school - Prosecutrix not disclosed the incident to lady teachers or girl students - Narrating only to mother on reaching home - (1) Whether her conduct was natural - (2) Whether her testimony could be disbelieved on ground the she did not complain to lady teachers or girl friends - (3) Testimony of prosecutrix, victim in cases of sexual offences - Evidentiary value - Corroboration, when necessary - (D) Trial of rape case - (1) Observation by Court that probably the prosecutrix is a girl of loose moral character - Propriety - (2) Rape - Compensation - Convicts sentenced to 5 years R. I. and fine Rs. 5000/- - Whether compensation be awarded - Effect on victim - Appreciation of evidence - Court's duty - (3) Rape - Cross-examination of prosecutrix - Scope - (4) Sexual offence - In-camera trials - Amended provisions of s. 327 CrPC - Trial by lady judges - Whether anonimity of victim to be maintained - Held, (A) Cannot be a ground - (B) Not material when properly explained - (C) (1) Her conduct was natural - (2)Her testimony could not be disbelieved on this ground - (3) Unless there are compelling reasons which necessitate corroboration, the Courts should act on her testimony alone to convict - (D) The inference based on no evidence lacks sobriety expected of a Judge - The Courts must use self-restraint while recording such findings - Even in cases, where there is material on the record to show that the victim was habituated to sexual intercourse, no such inference is permissible from that circumstance alone - (1) Not awarded since fine is already imposed -

(2) Must deal with such cases with utmost sensitivity - Should examine the broader probabilities and not get swayed by minor contradictions or insignificant discrepancies in prosecutrix' statement, not of a fatal nature, to throw out an otherwise reliable prosecution case - ( 3) Courts' duty to see that prosecutrix is not unnecessarily harassed and humiliated - It must effectively control the recording of evidence, while the victim is cross-examined - (4) Sub-s.(2), s.273 casts a duty on the Court to conduct the trial of rape cases etc. invariably 'in camera' - Wherever available, suggested - Must be maintained - Petition allowed.
