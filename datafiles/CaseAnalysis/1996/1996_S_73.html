 State (Through Cbi/NEW Delhi) v S. J. Choudhary

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, G.N. Ray, N.P. Singh, Faizan Uddin, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 224; (1996) SCC (Cr) 336; (1996) 2 SCC 428; 1996 (33) ACC 258; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 1056; AIR 1996 SC 1491; 1996 (1) CCR 156; 1996 (1) Crimes 96; 1996 CRLJ 1713; 1996 (64) ECR 185; JT 1996 (2) SC 186B; 1996 (1) KLT 454; 1996(2) SCALE 37; 1996 (2) SCJ 327; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 556; 1996 (2) Supreme 150

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Evidence Act

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Expert evidence - Meaning of - Word 'science' occurring in s. 45 - Whether includes experts in typewriters? - Whether typewriting falls within meaning of word 'handwriting' - Whether opinion of a typewriter expert is admissible in evidence u/s. 45, 1872 Act? - Held, includes typewriters experts - Falls within meaning of 'handwriting' - Is admissible in evidence - Appeal allowed.
