State of Rajasthan Etc v Rajasthan Agricultural Input Dealers Association, Etc

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4081; (1996) 5 SCC 479; AIR 1996 SC 2179; JT 1996 (6) SC 217B; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 265

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Seeds, Rajasthan Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Rajasthan Agricultural Product Markets Act, 1961, s. 40 - Agricultural produce - Definition - Seeds business - Commercial product - Requirement of licence - Appellants/state Govt. demanded and required respondents to obtain licenses for engaging in trade of purchase and sale of seeds - Respondents filed petitions before HC claiming that they were engaged in seeds business and seeds should not be termed as agricultural produce for purpose of the 1961 Act and its schedule - DB allowed petitions and was of the view that when food grains of particular varieties were treated and subjected to chemical process for preservation, those grains would become commercial known as 'seeds' - Hence, instant appeals by State Govt. and its ally Krishi Upaj Mandi Samiti which were heard together - Whether seeds would come under category of agricultural produce requiring respondents to obtain licence - Held, undoubtedly true that foodgrains per se could be used as seeds for being sown and achieving germination, but in that form they retain dual utility of being foodgrains as well as seeds - By process of coating and applying insecticides, other chemicals and poisonous substances to foodgrain meant to be utilised as seeds, one of its basic character, its consumption as food by human beings or animals or for extraction for like purpose, would get irretrievably lost and such processed seeds would become commodity distinct from foodgrains as commonly understood - That distinction was borne in mind by HC in allowing petition of respondents, and in this courts view rightly - It should not be spelt out that words relating to foodgrains would automatically include seeds also - Schedule was not meant to be filled by inference - What was meant to contain therein should be explicit and categoric - Nothing would prevent State Govt to add suitable words therein to convey that foodgrains, as processed for seeds, would also be agricultural produce within meaning of expression 'or otherwise' occurring in s. 2 (1)(i) of the 1961 Act - Since no. such exercise had been taken, State Govt should not be permitted to achieve indirectly which it could have achieved directly, by being specific in that regard - HC rightly rejected such contention raised before it by appellants - No merit in these appeals - Appeals dismissed.
