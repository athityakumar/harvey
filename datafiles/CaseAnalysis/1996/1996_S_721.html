Shree Mahavir Oil Mills and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2012; (1996) 11 SCC 39; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 165; JT 1996 (10) SC 837; 1996(8) SCALE 595; 1997 (104) STC 148; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 356; 1996 (8) Supreme 316

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951

Summary: Sales Tax - Jammu and Kashmir General Sales Tax Act, 1962, s. 5 - Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 142, 301 and 304 - Whether exemption of all edible oil industries in state from payment of sales tax unconditionally amounts to discrimination prohibited by arts. 301 and 304 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, art. 301 speaks of freedom and art. 304(a) speaks of uniform taxation of both imported goods and locally produced goods by states - Keeping said aspect in view, invoke our power u/art. 142 of Constitution and mould relief to suit exigencies of situation - Declare that exemption granted by Notification No. SRO 93 of 1991 to local manufacturers/producers of edible oil is violative of provisions contained in arts. 301 and 304(a) - At same time, the court direct that: (a) appellants shall not be entitled to claim any amounts by way of refund or otherwise by virtue of or, as a consequence of, declaration contained herein and; (b) that declaration of invalidity of impugned notification shall take effect on and from 01-04-1997 - Till that date, i.e., up to and inclusive of 31-03-1997, impugned notification shall continue to be effective and operative - Appeal allowed.
