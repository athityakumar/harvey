K. C. P. Limited v Presiding Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.M. Ahmadi, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 369; (1996) 10 SCC 446; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1410; AIR 1997 SC 2334; JT 1996 (8) SC 707; 1997 (1) LLJ 308; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; 1996 (5) SLR 730; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 18 - After dismissal of workmen, dispute raised by union on behalf of all dismissed workmen - Settlement between management and union - Workmen given a choice either to accept reinstatement without back wages or lump sum amount with other monetary benefits - Whether settlement binding on all workmen? - Held, settlement not unjust and unfair and is binding on all workmen who were dismissed - Order refusing to pass award in terms of such settlement and to continue reference in respect of workmen who were not accepting settlement was illegal - Appeal allowed.
