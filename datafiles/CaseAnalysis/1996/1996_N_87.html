Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation v Nawab Khan Gulab Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1838; (1997) 11 SCC 121; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 183; AIR 1997 SC 152; AIR 1996 SCW 4315; JT 1996 (10) SC 485; 1996(7) SCALE 770; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; International; Land & Property

Keywords: Fundamental Duties

Summary: Human Rights - Municipalities & Local Governments - Universal Declaration Of Human Rights - Constitution - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 63(i)(19) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 21 - Natural justice - Removal of encroachment by Municipality - (A) Scope and contents of requirement of following of principles of natural justice - (B) During removal of encroachment corporation gives 21 days notice to encroachers - Whether it is necessary to afford hearing to encroachers before taking action for ejectment? - (C) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 41 - Whether it is duty of State to prevent constant efflux of rural people to urban areas to check consequential growth of slums and encroachments? - (D) Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 38, 39 and 46 - Whether it is duty and authority of Municipal Corporation to implement directions to enforce schemes in planned manner by annual budgets to provide right to residence to poor? - (E) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Scope of removal of encroachment - Grounds for maintainability of encroachers case - Whether plea that intervention of court would aid impetus to encroachers to abuse judicial process tenable - (F) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether it is necessary that one should be provided with an alternative accommodation at expense of State before ejectment in case of removal of encroachment? - Held, (A) deprivation of life or personal liberty must be according to procedure, that is fair and reasonable - (B) Not necessary - Corporation/Municipality have statutory duty to have encroachments removed - Service of notice by personal service or through affixture on hutment duly attested by two independent panchas - (C) It is for Constitutional functionaries to provide continuous means of employment, educational and medical facilities in rural area - (D) It is duty of Municipal Corporation - (E) It would be no ground - Plea is untenable - Court is to consider as to what would be appropriate procedure required to be adopted in given facts and circumstances - (F) No rule - In all cases it may not be necessary - It is likely to result in abuse of judicial process - Appeal allowed.
