Mohd. Kavi Mohamad Amin v Fatmabai Ibrahim

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2325; (1997) 6 SCC 71

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 84-C - Appellant by two registered sale deeds purchased from respondent, Survey Plot Nos 53 and 372/1 + 2 - On a suo motu enquiry u/s. 84-C of Act, Mamlatdar held that sales in question were invalid as appellant was not an agriculturist belonging to State of Gujarat - Whether impugned order can be set aside on ground that suo motu power has not been exercised within a reasonable time? - Held, s. 84-C of Act does not prescribe any time for initiation of proceeding - In present case transfer took place as early as in year 1972 and suo motu enquiry was started by Mamlatdar in September 1973 - Court satisfied that in facts and circumstances of present case, suo motu power u/s. 84-C of Act was not exercised by Mamlatdar within a reasonable time - Appeal allowed.
