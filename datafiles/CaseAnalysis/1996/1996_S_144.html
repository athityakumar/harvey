Union Of India & Anr. v Samar Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1688; (1996) 10 SCC 555; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1443; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 814; 1997 (1) ESC 94; 1997 (75) FLR 493; JT 1996 (9) SC 184; 1996(7) SCALE 460; 1997 (1) SLJ 56; 1996 (6) SLR 732; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 253; 1996 (7) Supreme 410; 1996 (2) UJ 723

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Appointment - Validity of - Whether Tribunal has declared that action of appellants in omitting name of respondent from panel prepared for appointment to post of Secretary to Govt. of India or equivalent post without proper consideration of his case is arbitrary, unsustainable and void and has directed appellants to consider suitability of respondent for empanelment and appointment on post of Secretary to Govt. of India or equivalent post afresh? - Held, as is evident from para 14 of Central Staffing Scheme record is one of matters which has to be taken into consideration by Special Committee while making selection - Apart from record there are other matters that have to be considered, namely, merit, competence, leadership and flair for participating in policy-making process and need of Central Govt. which is paramount consideration - Court is unable to hold that since performance of respondent after his promotion as Additional Secretary had been found to be excellent and outstanding, non-inclusion of his name from panel by Special Committee must lead to inference that there was no proper consideration of merit and suitability of respondent for empanelment by Special Committee - Directions given by Tribunal in impugned judgment cannot be upheld and have to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
