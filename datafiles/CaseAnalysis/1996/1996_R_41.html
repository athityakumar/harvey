Rafiq Khan v Laxmi Narayan Sharma

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Venkataswami, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2688; (1997) 2 SCC 228

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, ss. 33 and 36 - Whether Learned Judge of High Court rightly took view that rejection of nomination form was neither illegal nor improper and that election petition was wholly misconceived and unsustainable? - Responsibility of producing documentary evidence of registration as elector in a different constituency rests entirely on candidate - All errors in regard to electoral roll numbers of candidate and proposer in nomination paper do not constitute defects of a substantial character, they would not be defects of sustained character only if at time of scrutiny Returning Officer either by himself or with assistant of candidate or his proper or any other person is able to find out correct serial numbers of candidate and proposer by reference to correct part number of electoral roll - Held, whether defect is of substantial character or not, would depend on facts of each case - Unless defect is one which can be per se noticed and corrected at stage of s. 33(4) or later at stage of s. 36(4) without need to refer to various other documents same cannot be said to be of a non-substantial character - In instant case also defect as to number could have been said to be not of a substantial character if appellant had shown that name of proposer appeared on very same sheet at Serial No. 138 instead of 136 i.e., only two steps away - Since there is no evidence to suggest that name of proposer appeared on sheet at Serial No. 138 instead of 136 in electoral roll, it is difficult to find fault with rejection of nomination paper by Returning Officer - Held, no merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
