Indian Bank v M/S Satyam Fibres (India} Pvt.Ltd

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2393; (1996) 5 SCC 550; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 112; AIR 1996 SC 2592; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 149; 1997 (2) CPR 187; 1996 (4) CTJ 756; JT 1996 (7) SC 135; 1996(5) SCALE 722; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 464; 1997 (5) Supreme 485

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2, sub-s. 13 - Export Supply - Bills of exchange drawn - Letter by supplier to his bank - Not directing to write to foreign bank to deliver documents to buyer only on co-acceptance - Subsequent letter containing such direction found to be forged - Whether Indian bank liable for negligence in non-indication of requirement as to co-acceptance by foreign bank in letter forwarding bills? - Held, bank could not be said to have acted negligently.
