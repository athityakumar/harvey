U.P. Jal Nigam, Lucknowthrough Its Chairman and v Messrs Kalra Properties Private Limited, Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1099; (1996) 3 SCC 124; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 680; AIR 1996 SC 1170; 1996 ALJ 523; 1996 (1) CCC 133; JT 1996 (1) SC 354; 1996 (4) KarLJ 64; 1996 (1) RentLR 539; 1996(1) SCALE 389; 1996 (1) SCJ 554; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 683; 1996 (1) Supreme 448; 1996 (1) UJ 549

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 11, 17 and 23 - Land acquisition - Encumbrance - Respondent had purchased acquired land by sale deed for a total consideration of Rs.60,000 - Petition was filed for mandamus commanding the appellants to pay compensation in respect of the land on the basis of the market value fixed by the District Magistrate - District Magistrate issued mandamus and directed that the compensation should be paid to the respondents in accordance with the market value assessed by the Collector with all consequential benefits of solatium and interest under the Act - Appellants contended that after the judgment, it had come to light that in respect of the lands, the market value as per the guidelines issued by the Govt. was determined for stamp duty at Rs. 80/ per square yard and the respondent himself had purchased the land for Rs. 60,000 - It was contended that determination of the compensation by the Collector at Rs. 200/ per square foot was an obvious error apparent on the face of the record and the directions issued by the DB were vitiated by manifest error of law - It was alleged that the market value determined by the Collector on the basis of basic valuation was illegal as the basic valuation would form basis for determination of the compensation u/s. 23(1) and that the Collector was wrong in determining the compensation u/s. 23(1) of the Act on the basis of prevailing rates as per basic valuation circulars - Whether respondent was entitled to compensation and, if so, at what rate - Held, that it was a settled law that after the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was published in the Gazette any encumbrance created by the owner did not bind the Govt. and the purchaser did not acquire any title to the property - In the instant case, notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was published, possession was taken as urgency was acute and that pumping station house was to be constructed to drain out flood water - Land stood vested in the State u/s. 17(2) of the Act free from all encumbrances as once possession was taken, by operation of s. 17(2) of the Act, the land vested in the State free from all encumbrances unless a notification u/s. 48(1) of the Act was published in the Gazette withdrawing from the acquisition - S. 11A of the Act did not apply and the acquisition did not lapse - Notification u/s. 4(1) and the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act remained valid - Respondent had purchased the land after the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was published, its sale was void against the State and it acquired no right, title of the land - The original owner had the right to the compensation u/s. 23(1) of the Act - Though the respondent acquired no title to the land, he would be entitled to step into the shoes of the owner and claim payment of the compensation, but only according to the provisions of the 1984 Act - District Collector had assessed the market value at Rs.200 per square foot and there was no illegality in the order of the DB in directing payment of the compensation and also the consequential solatium and interest - It was settled law that the price prevailing as on the date of the publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was the price to which the owner or person who had an interest in the land was entitled - Hence, respondent as a person interested in the compensation, would be entitled to claim compensation at 6 per cent interest from the date of taking possession - Appeal allowed.
