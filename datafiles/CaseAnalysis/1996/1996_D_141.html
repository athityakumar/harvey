K. John Koshy and Others v Dr Tarakeshwar Prasad Shaw

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Venkataswami, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3297; (1998) 8 SCC 624

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Ministry of Health and Family Planning - Postgraduate Training and Research Department - Seats reserved for outside candidates - (A) Whether court could refuse to take up a matter when it is urgent and party's career is at stake and refuse to pass an appropriate order on merits even when advocates are on a strike? - Held, answer must obviously be in negative because to do so would tantamount to court becoming privy to strike - Court is under an obligation to hear and decide cases brought before it and cannot shirk that obligation on ground that advocates are on strike - (B) Whether order of 13-3-1995 for vacating/modifying interim orders of division bench can be sustained on merits? - Further held, order is not a speaking order, in that, no reasons are given in support of order - Court could certainly pass an interim order if merits of case so demand and to contend otherwise is clearly misconceived - Appeal disposed of.
