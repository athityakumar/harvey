Sultana Begum v Prem Chand Jain

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1760; (1997) 1 SCC 373; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 469; AIR 1997 SC 1006; 1996 (4) CCC 226; 1997 (1) CLT 219; JT 1996 (11) SC 1; 1997 (1) RentLR 1; 1996(9) SCALE 55; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 707; 1996 (8) Supreme 482; 1997 (1) UJ 227

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Decree For Restitution Of Conjugal Rights

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47 and O. 21, r. 2(3) - Compromise of rights by parties amounting to adjustment of decree was not reported to executing court - Whether court can proceed to execute decree? - Held, though it is open to parties to adjust or compromise their rights under decree but if it amounts to adjustment of decree, it must be reported to court whose duty it is to execute decree so that court whose duty it is to execute decree so that court may record or certify same - If it is not done, court before whom execution proceedings are initiated will proceed to execute decree - (B) Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Eviction decree - Tenant pleaded that possession already delivered to landlord's attorney who permitted him to continue in possession as licensee - Further held, decree for eviction could not be said to be superseded by such agreement - Right to exclusive possession is basic feature of tenancy created by lease - Intention of parties clearly was not to extinguish decree for eviction but to create only a license allowing tenant to stay in premises for a while - Appeal allowed.
