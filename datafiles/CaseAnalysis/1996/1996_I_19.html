Marshal Sons and Company (India) Limited v Income Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3608; (1997) 2 SCC 302; AIR 1997 SC 1763; [1997] 88 Comp Cas 528; 1997 (138) CTR 1; [1997] 223 ITR 809; JT 1996 (10) SC 740; 1996(8) SCALE 564; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 216; [1996] 89 TAXMAN 619; 1997 (2) TLR 277

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 139(2) and 142(1) - Companies Act, 1956, s. 394 - Amalgamation of companies - Schemes for - Specifying date of enforce - Court just sanctioning scheme without modification - Providing subsidiary Co. shall be deemed to have carried on business from date of transfer, on behalf of holding company - Notice to subsidiary Co. for filing returns - Held, is unwarranted in law.
