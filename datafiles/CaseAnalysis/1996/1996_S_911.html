 New Delhi Municipal Council v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, A.S. Anand, B.L. Hansaria, B.N. Kirpal, K.S. Paripoornan, S.C. Agrawal, S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1552; (1997) 7 SCC 339; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 309; AIR 1997 SC 2847; AIR 1997 SCW 2851; 1997 (1) CLT 457; JT 1997 (1) SC 40; 1996(9) SCALE 613; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 472; 1997 (4) Supreme 90

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, Government Of National Capital Territory Of Delhi Act, 1991, Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970, Constitution (Twelfth Amendment) Act, 1962, Government of Union Territories Act, 1963, Constitution (Seventy-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1992, Constitution (Sixty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1991, Constitution (Fourteenth Amendment) Act, 1962, Delhi Laws Act, 1915, North-Eastern Areas (Reorganisation) Act, 1971, Constitution (Tenth Amendment) Act, 1961, State Of Arunachal Pradesh Act, 1986, State Of Mizoram Act, 1986, Income Tax Act, 1912, Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1956

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 133, 239, 246(4) and 289(1), sch. 7, list 1, entries 24, 25, 47, 49 and 52 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, s. 61 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, s. 113 - New Delhi Municipal Council Act, 1994, s. 60 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(58) - Constitutionality of property tax levied under Punjab Municipal Act, Delhi Municipal Corporation Act and New Delhi Municipal Corporation Act - (A) If levy comes with in scope of 'union taxation' - (B) Determination of exemption regarding State property - (C) If entries 49, 24, 25, 47 and 52 in union list were only instances kept in contemplation by framers at time of drafting of art. 289(1) - (D) If union territories can avail privileges available to states - (E) Applicability of definition of state u/s. 3(58) of General Clauses Act to art. 246(4) - Held, constitute 'union taxation' within meaning of art. 289 (1) of constitution - Authorities to determine nature of use as State property for purpose of Govt. is exempt while for trade or business is taxable - It cannot be said so - This will not enable them to avail of privileges - Inapplicable - Order accordingly.
