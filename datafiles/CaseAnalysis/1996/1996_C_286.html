Veecumsees, Madras v Commissioner of Income-tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2860; (1996) 9 SCC 25; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 509; AIR 1996 SC 3292; 1996 (133) CTR 500; [1996] 220 ITR 185; JT 1997 (10) SC 394; 1996(4) SCALE 323; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 60; 1996 (4) Supreme 582; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 243

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 36(1)(iii) - (A) Whether, provisions of s. 36(1)(iii) of IT Act, 1961, Appellate Tribunal was right in holding that interest attributable to loans borrowed by assessee firm for purpose of construction of Safire Theatre should be allowed under head 'business' especially when theatre complex was sold as a going concern on 31-7-1965 and business of exhibition of cinematographic films stopped on and from 31-7-1965? - Fact that revenue had during years when assessee carried on business of cinematographic films permitted as a deduction u/s. 36(1)(iii) interest on loans obtained by assessee for purpose of constructing said theatre shows that at time when loans were obtained said theatre was a part of business of assessee - It was interest on these loans, borrowed for purpose of business of assessee, which was being paid in years in question and thus, tribunal was, right in concluding that such interest had to be treated as a deduction u/s. 36(1)(iii) - Loans had been obtained for purposes of assessee's business - (B) Whether conclusion of Appellate Tribunal that business carried on by assessee as jewellers and in running of cinema theatre, restaurant, etc., are composite is based on valid materials and is a reasonable view to take on facts and in circumstances of case? - Held, answered in affirmative and in favour of assessee - Appeal allowed.
