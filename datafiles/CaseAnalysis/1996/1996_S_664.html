Hukum Chand and Others Etc v State of Haryana and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3465; (1996) 5 SCC 164; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 427; AIR 1996 SC 3275; 1996 (4) CLT 289; JT 1996 (4) SC 328; 1996(3) SCALE 669; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1087; 1996 (4) Supreme 571

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 28-A(1) - Whether the Land Acquisition Officer was right in refusing to award the compensation to the appellants @ Rs. 135/- per sq.yd. Pursuant to the order passed by the High Court in RFA No. 1326/79. Section 28-A (1) of the Act.

Held, in this case, admittedly the appellants have availed the remedy of reference u/s. 18 of Act and had the compensation enhanced. Thereafter, they did not pursue appellate remedy u/s. 54 of Act to the HC for further enhanced compensation but some of the claimants pursued the appellate remedy and had further enhanced the compensation at Rs. 135/- per sq.yd. Having not availed of the remedy u/s. 54 of Act, the appellants are not entitled to make an application u/s. 28-A (1) of Act to seek the same benefit of the enhanced compensation. The remedy u/s. 28-A (1) of Act is available to only when the compensation was enhanced u/s. 26 of Act award and judgment in part III of the Act and the same is not available when it was enhanced u/s. 54 of the Act. Appellants are, therefore, not entitled to make the applications u/s. 28-A of Act further enhancement. The Land Acquisition Officer and the HC have rightly refused to grant the relief of enhanced compensation on par with other claimants. Appeals dismissed.
