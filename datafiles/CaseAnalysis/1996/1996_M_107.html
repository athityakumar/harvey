Union Of India. v M/S. G.S. Atwal And Co.(Asansole)

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 588; (1996) 3 SCC 568; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 332; AIR 1996 SC 2965; 1996 (1) ARBLR 400; 1996 (1) CCC 308; 1996 (1) CLT 524; JT 1996 (2) SC 607; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 34; 1996(2) SCALE 447; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 940; 1996 (2) Supreme 620

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Respondent had entered into an agreement in 1968-69 for excavation of feeder canal from RD.68.00 to RD.97.00 - During the course of the execution of the work certain disputes had arisen between the respondent and the appellant - Disputes were referred from time to time to arbitration - Arbitrator awarded by a non-speaking award, a sum of Rs.35,72,550/- with interest at 15% per annum from July 1, 1976 or the date of the payment of decree whichever was earlier - Asst. District Judge set aside the award passed by the Arbitrator - On appeal, HC set aside the order of the civil Court holding that there was no error apparent on the face of the award warranting setting aside of the award - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, arbitrators went out of their way to declare that whatever amount in addition to Rs.3,500/- was found due from respondent No.1 upon the bahikhata account was remitted having regard to his labour and poverty and the whole unspecified amount found due against respondent No.2 was remitted in full in view of his labour and poverty.

Arbitrator had misdirected himself and committed legal misconduct in making the award vitiating the entire award itself. It is difficult to decide as to what extent each of the claims was accepted or rejected. The order and judgment of the HC is set aside and that of the Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed
