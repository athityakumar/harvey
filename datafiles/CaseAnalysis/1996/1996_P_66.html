Bihar State Electricity Board and Others v Parmeshwar Kumar Agarwala, Etc

Supreme Court of India

27 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4083; (1996) 4 SCC 686; AIR 1996 SC 2214; JT 1996 (5) SC 555; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Central Electricity Board

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 26 and 37 - Indian Electricity Rules, 1956, r. 27 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 46 and 49 - Electricity theft - High tension industrial consumer and low tension industrial consumer - Notification by appellant/Board as to special mode of assessment at 30% of load factor for low tension consumers and 45% of load factor for high tension consumers -(A) Whether Board had power to issue such notification? - Agreement - Terms and conditions of agreement and terms of notification - Cl. 14 of agreement deals with alteration of various energy charges and states that on charges being altered, tariff shall become realisable accordingly - (B) Whether notification can be justified under clause? - Held, no - Present case is not a case of alteration of charges mentioned in such clause - It really seeks to introduce an unknown mode of charging for energy, which is not visualised by agreement entered into between parties - Provisions perused, terms of agreement and notification looked for - Notification without authority of law - Appeal dismissed.
