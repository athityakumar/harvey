Abdur Rahman and Others v Athifa Begum and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2385; (1996) 6 SCC 62; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 373; 1996 (3) CCC 394A; 1996 (4) CLT 16; 1996(6) SCALE 505; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 391; 1996 (6) Supreme 753

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 41, r.17 - Whether High Court could have dismissed appeal on merits in absence of appellants' counsel? - HC recorded that all relevant aspects of matter have been taken into account in order to hold that there was no available ground for interference with decision of Trial Court - Held, this was an exercise against which HC should have been well advised not to indulge in at stage of O. 41, r. 17, CPC - Appeal allowed.
