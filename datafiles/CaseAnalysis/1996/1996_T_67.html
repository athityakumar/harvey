Manipur Tea Company Private Limited v Collector of Hailakandi

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2034; (1997) 9 SCC 673; AIR 1997 SC 1779; JT 1996 (11) SC 535; 1997(1) SCALE 221; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 108; 1997 (2) Supreme 743

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Land Acquisition Officer without examining any witness relied on the sales statistics which formed basis for his award and rejected three sale deeds Exs. 17(1) to 17(3) proved on behalf of appellant - The Reference Court and High Court also relied on the same sale statistics for reaching conclusion - Whether, it is error of law? - Held, it is duty of court to assess evidence adduced by claimants and determine compensation on touchstone of prudent purchaser in open market, i.e., whether he would offer market value at rates proposed by court - Evidence has to be put to test whether sale deed or evidence adduced would offer market value higher than that which has been determined by Land Acquisition Officer - Compensation awarded by Land Acquisition Officer is an offer that binds Govt. but it is not conclusive - HC and Reference Court, therefore, correctly applied test and did not accept three sale deeds produced by appellant in determining compensation which relate to agricultural land, not tea garden or estate - Having rejected sale deeds relied on by appellant to do justice to respondent, they relied on sale statistics relied by Land Acquisition Officer - (B) Reference Court having noticed that in three months an area of 90 cottahs 11 chitaks was sold on 21/5/1979 @ Rs. 2539. 68 per bigha and 18 cottahs 13 chitaks of land was sold on 7/3/1980 @ Rs. 1268. 83 per bigha and a further area of 1 bigha 4 cottahs and 4 chitaks was sold @ Rs.4948. 45 per bigha on 3-10-1981, which would show that there was a gradual rise in prices, fixing escalation charges at Rs. 270 per month instead of Rs. 367 - Whether, the principle adopted by court is correct in law? - Held, it was required to be proved that there was really an increase in value of land - As a matter of fact, it has to be established that there is gradual increase, every month, in value of land of that area and, therefore, when compulsory acquisition was made, appellant was entitled to higher compensation - Further held, there is no illegality committed by courts below in granting escalation at Rs. 270 per bush - (C) Whether, instead of adding severance charges awarded by Reference Court, HC has reduced compensation, therefore, it committed an error of law? - Held, question is confined to extent of expenditure - Compensation has to be awarded for such severance - It is stated by claimants that they were required to put up fencing for protecting tea estate and also drainage channel - It is seen that HC has proceeded on that premise and it is not a case of parties that on account of acquisition of land, tea estate is exposed to public and public have access into tea estate, only railway tracks would pass through estate and, therefore, setting up of fencing or of drainage to protect tea estate may not be necessary - Further held, suffice it to state that HC having found that appellant was required to set up a fencing and drainage channel, an amount of Rs. 2, 36, 000 as estimated, would be sufficient to meet expenditure - It being an estimate made by appellant, there is no error of law warranting interference - (d) Whether, by operation of proviso to s. 28 of Act, claimants would be entitled to interest for one year from date of taking possession @ 9% per annum and for balance period @ 15% per annum on enhanced compensation? - Held, the Reference Court and HC have proceeded on principle that court has discretion to award interest @ 15% or less and on facts, Court found that 9% would be reasonable rate of interest - Further held, under these circumstances, though word 'may' has been used in proviso to s. 28 of Act, it has to be construed as 'shall' and, therefore, claimants would be entitled to interest at rate of 9% on enhanced compensation for one year and thereafter @ 15% till date of deposit in court - Appeal allowed.
