Punjab State Electricity Board v Messrs Guru Nanak Cold Storage and Ice Factory, Mahilpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3058; (1996) 5 SCC 411; AIR 1996 SC 2684; 1996 (2) ARBLR 285; 1996 (3) CCC 204; 1996 (3) CLT 191; JT 1996 (6) SC 678; 1996(5) SCALE 487; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 534; 1996 (6) Supreme 11; 1996 (2) UJ 586

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Civil Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Recovery of arrears - Appellant filed suit for recovery of arrears - Respondent called upon the appellant to refer the dispute to an arbitration - Appellant filed suit for declaration and also for permanent injunction restraining the appointment of an arbitrator to adjudicate the dispute raised in the notice - Civil Suit was dismissed which was confirmed in appeal - HC dismissed the appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 52 of the 1910 Act or 76(2) of 1948 Act r/w s. 19(1) of 1910 Act has no application to dispute. So, s. 52 of the 1910 Act does not get attracted; and equally s. 76(2) of the 1948 Act. Therefore, it is not arbitrable under the statute. The arbitrator cannot decide that question. Appeal allowed.
