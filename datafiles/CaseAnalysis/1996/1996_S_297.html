Major Yogendera Narain Yadav Etc v Shri Bindeshwar Prasad and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3702; (1997) 2 SCC 150; (1997) SCC (L&S) 319; JT 1996 (11) SC 555; 1997 (1) LLN 575; 1997(1) SCALE 193; 1997 (1) SLR 765; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Consideration Of Seniority

Summary: Service - Bihar Engineering Class I Service Rules, 1939, rr. 17 and 24 - Seniority - Entitlement - Appointment to a temporary post, though in cadre, is not a substantive appointment as contemplated by rule - Substantive appointment to permanent post gives right to a substantive status in post on permanent basis - R. 17 prescribes procedure for promotion to post of Executive Engineer with a condition that merit should be consideration and seniority also may be taken into consideration - Due weight is given only to merit and where merit and ability are approximately equal, seniority also could be considered - Held, circular of Govt. of Bihar, giving benefit of past service as a demobilised military officer, respondents has no right to claim seniority over appellant - Though respondents are entitled to other benefits by virtue of their temporary appointment, for purpose of inter se seniority their seniority would be considered only from date of entry into service as permanent Asst. Engineers - Since respondents entered into service much later to appellant, they cannot claim any seniority over appellant - Appeal allowed.
