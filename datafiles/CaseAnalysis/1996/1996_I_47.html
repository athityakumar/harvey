U. P. Rajkiya Nirman Nigam Limited v Indure Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3362; (1996) 2 SCC 667; AIR 1996 SC 1373; 1996 (1) ARBLR 236; 1996 (2) AWC 772; JT 1996 (2) SC 322; 1996(2) SCALE 247; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 386; 1996 (2) UJ 48

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Acceptance Of Proposal

Summary: Arbitration Act of 1940, s. 2(a) - Indian Contract Act of 1872, ss. 7, 32 and 33 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - Civil appeal - Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board tender for work contract - Offer by one party - Second party accepted with material alterations - No communication from first party regarding any objection to counter offer - Submission of unilateral tender amounted to acceptance of counter offer - Concluded contract - Whether respondent could rely on arbitration clause? - Held, no acceptance of counter offer - No consensus ad idem on material terms - No concluded contract - Contract not entered into, so no arbitration agreement - Existence or validity - Can be decided only by court not arbitrators.
