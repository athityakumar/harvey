Ujjain Vikas Pradhikaran v Tarachand and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3056; (1996) 5 SCC 574; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 92; AIR 1996 SC 2777; 1996 (3) CCC 320; 1996 (3) CLT 226; JT 1996 (7) SC 206; 1996(5) SCALE 685; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 531; 1996 (6) Supreme 488; 1996 (2) UJ 547

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Acquisition - Compensation - Enhanced - Challenged - Possession of the respondent's land was taken on 19-5-1979 - Land Acquisition Officer awarded compensation @ Rs.27,500/- per hectare - On reference, the District Judge enhanced the compensation to Rs.50,000/- per hectare together with solatium and interest @ 15% and 6% respectively. On appeal, the HC enhanced the compensation to Rs.1,25,000/- per hectare which worked out @ Rs.26,125/- per bigha - HC also enhanced the solatium and interest respectively at 30% and 9% from the date of taking possession for one year and 15% thereafter - Appellant contended that the respondents having confined their claim to enhancement of the compensation to Rs.20,000/- per bigha in the memorandum in grounds filed in the HC, the HC was clearly in error in awarding compensation @ Rs.26,125/- per bigha, i.e., at Rs.1,25,000/- per hectare - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the party having limited the compensation to Rs.20,000/- per bigha in the memorandum of appeal filed in the HC, it ' would be obvious that the respondents claimed that they were entitled to the maximum of the compensation @ Rs.20,000.- per bigha. Thereby the Court was precluded to award compensation beyond the amount claimed by the party and award in excess thereof would be obviously illegal. The power of the Court would be confined to the difference of the amount awarded by the reference Court and the amount claimed in the memorandum of the appeal but not in excess thereof. HC was clearly in error in awarding compensation in excess of the amount claimed by the respondents. Appeal allowed.
