State Of Andhra Pradesh v Nagoti Venkataramana

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2307; (1996) 6 SCC 409; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 212; 1997 (1) ARBLR 303; 1997 (1) AWC 25; 1996 (4) CCR 42; 1996 (67) ECR 244; JT 1996 (8) SC 282; 1996 (16) PTC 634; 1996(6) SCALE 417; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 812; 1996 (7) Supreme 13

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Cinematograph Films

Summary: Copyright Act, 1957, ss. 52-A, 63 and 68-A - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Cinematograph Act, 1952 - (A) Whether identification of the owner of the copyright is a precondition for violation of the provisions of s. 63 or s. 68-A? - Held, yes - (B) Whether the prosecution has failed to establish the identification of the owner can be entitled for benefit of doubt? - Held, yes - (C) Whether video film is a cinematograph? - Held, yes - It is settled view that video tapes come within the expression 'cinematograph' - Held, strict construction is required to be adopted and if any real doubt arises, necessarily the reasonable benefit of doubt would be extended to the accused - The identification of the owner being an essential element to prove the offence of infringement of copyright, the prosecution has failed to establish the same - In construction of the penal statute strict construction should be adopted and in that perspective the benefit of doubt given by the High Court is well justified and does not warrant interference - The absence thereof does not constitute lack of essential element of infringement of copyright - If the particulars on video films etc. as mandated u/s. 52-A do not find place, it would be infringement of copyright - instead of imposing a sentence of imprisonment, sentenced fine of Rs. 10,000/- - Appeal allowed.
