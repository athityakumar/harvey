Regional Provident Fund Commissioner v Naraini Udyog and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2952; (1996) 5 SCC 522; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1284; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 730; 1996 (3) CLT 182; 1997 (75) FLR 480; 1996 (2) LLJ 1063; 1996 (2) LLN 904; 1996(5) SCALE 500; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 202; 1996 (6) Supreme 52; 1996 (2) UJ 518

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, s. 7-A - Whether functional unity and integrality between two firms show that they are a single establishment or not? - Held, no - Provisions of Act which is a beneficial legislation to provide healthy security to workmen - In ultimate analysis employer gets maximum out-turn of his production by ensuring health insurance to its employees which is fundamental right to latter - High Court's order is unjustifiable - Appeals allowed.
