S A Ramachandran v S. Neelavathy

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1565; (1997) 1 SCC 767; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 427; AIR 1997 SC 1735; JT 1997 (1) SC 279; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 231; 1997 (1) RentLR 88; 1996(9) SCALE 714; 1997 (1) SCJ 166; 1997 (1) Supreme 347

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tamil Nadu City Tenants Protection Act, 1922, ss. 9 and 11 - Suit filed for ejectment of tenant without notice - Tenant filed application u/s. 9 for directions to owner to sell property in question - Application rejected - Thereafter, tenant pleading that suit was liable to be dismissed for want of notice - (A) Whether prior notice contemplated u/s. 11 necessary? - (B) Whether tenant waived his right to object illegality in institution of suit by filing application u/s. 9? - (C) Whether suit liable to be dismissed? - Held, (A) prior notice is necessary - (B) tenant cannot be said to have waived his right to object - (C) suit liable to be dismissed - Appeal allowed.
