All India Institute of Medical Sciences Employees' Union (Regd.) Through Its President v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1808; (1996) 11 SCC 582; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 4; 1996 (4) Crimes 180; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 57; 1996(8) SCALE 383; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 138; 1996 (8) Supreme 142; 1997 (1) UJ 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Proceedings - Mandamus - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154, 190 and 200 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Criminal proceedings instituted against former Director of AIIMS under s. 409 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Petitioner contended that despite putting all information before the concerned authorities about the offences committed by the Director, no action was taken - Writ petition filed u/art. 226 of the Constitution which was not allowed - Whether the institution of proceedings against the respondent are justified? - Held, no - The offence complained was cognizable and procedure laid down in CrPC, 1973 has to be followed - The procedure for investigation, charges and committal has been provided by law - No procedure followed by the petitioner in the present case - Petitioner cannot directly approach the High Court - HC was justified in refusing to entertain the writ petition - Appeal dismissed.
