Narashimaha Murthy v Susheelabai And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3932; (1996) 3 SCC 644; AIR 1996 SC 1826; JT 1996 (4) SC 300; 1997 (6) KarLJ 254; 1996(3) SCALE 625; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Land & Property - Prohibition Act - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 23 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 13(2) and 44 - Partition Act, s. 4(1) - (A) Dwelling house - Postponement of right of female heir to claim partition - Words 'the male heirs choose to divide their respective shares' - Whether mean that at least two such male heirs must exist and decide not to partition dwelling house - Whether provision applies even if intestate leaves behind single male heir - (B) Dwelling house - Connotation - Means house inhabited by one or some members of intestates family - Whether can include a tenanted house - (C) Dwelling house of intestate - Daughter's right to residence - Whether qualifications prescribed by proviso to s. 23 applies to all kinds of daughters - Whether she is entitled to a right of residence in dwelling - house so long as (1) unmarried - (2) a deserted wife - (3) a separated wife - and (4) a widow? - Held, (A) language in plural with reference to male heirs would have to be read in singular with aid of provisions of General Clauses Act - Provision applies - (B) Cannot include a tenanted house - (C) proviso covers cases of all kinds of daughters, by employment of words 'where such female heir is a daughter' and not 'where such female heir is daughter' - Appeal dismissed.
