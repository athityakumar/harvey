Inder Singh and Another v Financial Commissioner, Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1741; (1997) 11 SCC 206; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 633; JT 1996 (10) SC 374; 1996(8) SCALE 327; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 421; 1997 (1) UJ 421

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property -- Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887, s. 2 (k) - Tenancy Agricultural Lands Act, 1955 - Patiala Punjab State Union Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1953, ss 7-A(a)(b) (1), 20, 22 (a) to (c) of sub-section (2) of Section 22 - Continuous Possession - Ownership Rights - Ejectment - Personal Cultivation - Appellants/tenants were in possession of land their application u/s. 22 of Act, 1955 was rejected on ground that they did not have possession for 12 years preceding commencement of President's Act, which was confirmed by all authorities including HC - Subsequent, application for conferment of ownership rights based on tenancy from respondents was also dismissed - Meanwhile, landlord filed an application for reservation of land for personal cultivation which was dismissed - On appeal, Respondents/ Financial Commissioner also confirmed same order of dismissal - Division Bench set aside order of authorities on ground of res judicata - Appellants were aggrieved - Hence instant appeal - Whether Appellant/tenant liable to ejectment , right to purchase proprietary rights of Respondent/land holder were available and application for reservation of land for personal cultivation could be allowed - Held, Appellant/tenant was not liable to ejectment and right to purchase proprietary rights of a land holder by such a tenant was subject to provisions of ss.20 and 22 of Act - Respondent/landlord should reserve land hold by tenant, for his personal cultivation as conditional precedent, in accordance with Chapter II of act - Appellants were not liable to ejectment by termination of tenancy hold by them u/s.7-A(1)(a) of Act ,as respondent was in excess of 30 standard acres of land - Therefore, benefit to purchase proprietary right u/s. 22 of Act was not available to Respondent - Appellants thereby, acquired right to purchase proprietary interest of land hold by them as tenant - Court set aside judgment of HC - Appeal allowed.
