A. Suresh v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1930; (1997) 1 SCC 319; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 144; AIR 1997 SC 1889; 1997 (1) CLT 190; JT 1996 (10) SC 542; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 23; 1996(8) SCALE 493; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 947; 1997 (2) Supreme 56

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Freedom Of Press

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tamil Nadu Entertainment Tax Act, 1939 - W.P.s were filed challenging the validity of the Amendment Act of the 1939 Act - Hence, the present petition - Whether the Amendment Act was legally valid - Held, that the rate of entertainment tax levied upon the cable television at the same level and on the same par as the entertainment tax levied upon cinema theatres was neither excessive nor unreasonable - Appellants, with a view to promote their business, were showing programmes, designed to cater to base instincts and vulgar tests - Appellants were incorrectly comparing themselves with Doordarshan, which was a govt. organization, which was supposed to act in furtherance of public interest, and not a business carried on for profit - Petitions dismissed.
