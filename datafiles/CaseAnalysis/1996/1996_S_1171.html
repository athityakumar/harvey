Delhi Administration Through Its Chief Secretary v Sushil Kumar

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2134; (1996) 11 SCC 605; (1997) SCC (L&S) 492; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 669; AIR 1997 SC 1802; 1997 (1) ESC 179; 1997 (75) FLR 42; JT 1996 (10) SC 34; 1996(8) SCALE 11; 1997 (1) SLR 123; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 199; 1997 (1) UJ 159

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 79(c) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 3 - Entitlement to back wages - Petitioner terminated from on ground of conviction for criminal offence - Reinstatement in service on acquittal - Whether petitioner entitled to back wages? - Held, petitioner not entitled to back wages as petitioner was disabled from rendering service on account of his conviction for criminal offence and not on account of any disciplinary action taken by employer which was unsustainable in law - Petition dismissed.
