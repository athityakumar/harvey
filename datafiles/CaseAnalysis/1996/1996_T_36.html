Disciplinary Authority-Cum-Regional Manager and Others v Nikunja Bihari Patnaik

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3778; (1996) 9 SCC 69; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1194; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 41; 1996 (1) CLR 991; 1996 (73) FLR 1252; JT 1996 (4) SC 457; 1996 (2) LLJ 379; 1996 (2) LLN 92; 1996(3) SCALE 711; 1996 (2) SLR 728; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 314; 1996 (3) Supreme 710; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2862

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Advances

Summary: Service - Central Bank of India Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) regn. 1976, regns. 3 and 24 - Misconduct - Termination - Validity - Whether bank has actually incurred any loss on account of acts of respondent means charge of misconduct is not established? - No - Regn. 3 requires every officer/employee of bank to take all possible steps to protect interests of bank and to discharge his duties with utmost integrity, honesty, devotion and diligence and to do nothing which is unbecoming of a bank officer - It requires officer/employee to maintain good conduct and discipline and to act to best of his judgment in performance of his official duties or in exercise of powers conferred upon him - Breach of regn. 3 is 'misconduct' within meaning of regn. 24 - Held, very act of acting beyond authority that too a course of conduct spread over a sufficiently long period and involving innumerable instances is by itself a misconduct even if it does not attribute any loss to bank - Termination on ground of misconduct is valid - Appeal allowed.
