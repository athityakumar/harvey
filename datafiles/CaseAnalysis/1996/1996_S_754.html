Indian Aluminium Company and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3200; (1996) 7 SCC 637; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 137; AIR 1996 SC 1431; 1996 (64) ECR 193; JT 1996 (2) SC 85; 1996(1) SCALE 780; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 23; 1996 (4) Supreme 640

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Finance Act, 1964, Finance Act, 1978, Constitutionality, Karnataka State Civil Services (Regulation of Promotion, Pay and Pension) Act, 1973, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Madras Land Revenue Surcharge Act, 1954, Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961, Professions Tax Limitation Act, 1941, Kerala Luxury Tax On Tobacco (Validation) Act, 1964, Kerala Essential Articles Control Act, 1986, Essential Articles (Control) Act, 1963, Madras Land Revenue (Additional Surcharge) Act, 1955, Cochin Tobacco Act, 1909

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indirect Tax - Pith and Substance - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246(3) - Kerala Electricity Surcharge (Levy and Collection) Act, 1989, ss. 3 and 11 - Constitutional validity of Kerala Electricity Surcharge (Levy and Collection) Act, 1989 - Challenged - Whether the Kerala Electricity Surcharge (Levy and Collection) Act, 1989 is unconstitutional - Held, no - Words sale or consumption in entry 53 of the State list and the Act in exercise of the power u/ art. 246(3) of the Constitution, 1950, would receive wide interpretation so as to sustain the constitutionality of the Act - Controversy as regards vires of legislation must be resolved in favour of the legislative body - Liberal construction upon the relevant legislative entry - Word supply should receive liberal interpretation to include sale or consumption of electricity envisaged in Entry 53 of the State List - Appeal dismissed.
