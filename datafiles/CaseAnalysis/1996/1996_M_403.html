Mrs. Joyce Primrose Prestor v Vera Marie Vas and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 870; (1996) 9 SCC 324; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 737; 1996 (2) CCC 228; 1996 (2) CLT 353; JT 1996 (4) SC 333; 1996(3) SCALE 596; 1998 (Supp) RD 79; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 268; 1996 (4) Supreme 179

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Indian Succession Act, 1925,ss.58, 63, 276, 291 - Indian Evidence Act,1872,s. 68 - Will - Verification of signature - - Validity - Appellant and respondent nos. 3 and 4 were sisters and daughters of testatrix who had executed Will infavour of them and died subsequently - Appellant called upon executors to take out probate but was not favoured - Hence appellant/ plaintiff filed suit for grant of Letters of Administration of estate of deceased based on Will - Trial Court decreed suit - Aggrieved respondents/defendant no. 3 and 4 filed appeal in HC - DB by impugned judgment allowed appeal holding that Will was shrouded in suspicion and propounder had failed to satisfy judicial conscience dispelling all doubts that will Ex.P-1 was legally and properly executed by testatrix - Hence instant appeal - Whether finding of DB was justified - Held, it was admitted by contesting party respondent/defendant no.3 that signature and entire Will was in handwriting of testatrix and hence 'holograph Will' and that testatrix was active till her last day - Presumptions were different in normal will and holograph will - HC of Calcutta in Ajit Chandra Majumdar v. Akhil Chandra Majumdar,1959 Indlaw CAL 148 stated about holograph will that whole of this Will was written in hand by testator himself and handwriting was clear and firm and law makes great presumption in favour of genuineness of holograph Will for very good reason that mind of testator in physically writing out his own Will was more apparent in holograph will than where his signature alone appears to either in typed script or in script written by somebody else - In instant case writing of Will and signature of testator were admitted by contesting party and will was properly attested as per law and no suspicious circumstance appears on face of will Ex.P1 - Constitution Bench of SC Shashi Kumar Banerjee and Others v Subodh Kumar Banerjee rep by LR's and Others, 1963 Indlaw SC 414 held that cases of 'holograph Will' which were admittedly in handwriting of testator were special cases which would require different approach in considering evidence and to find whether will had been duly executed and attested - As stated by SC in Shashi Kumar Banerjee's v Subodh Kumar Banerjee rep by LR's and Others 1963, Indlaw SC 414 it was in this background evidence in case including that of attesting witnesses should have been examined and what was required was only to formally prove will and very little evidence to prove due execution and attestation of will was alone called for which was totally ignored by HC - SC in several cases held that appellate courts should give weight of importance to findings of trial Court who had occasion to watch demeanor of witnesses and assess their credibility - Order of HC was set aside being totally erroneous - Judgment of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
