Gentela Vijayavardhan Rao and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2361; (1996) 6 SCC 241; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1290; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 352; AIR 1996 SC 2791; 1997 (2) CCR 119; 1996 (3) Crimes 197; 1996 CRLJ 4151; JT 1996 (7) SC 491; 1996(6) SCALE 231; 1996 (3) SCJ 247; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 273; 1996 (6) Supreme 356

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - (A) Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 6, 32 and 157 - Hearsay evidence - Res gestae - Magistrate recording statements of victims of fire set up to bus by accused after interval between acts and recording - Whether statement can be used as substantive evidence as maker of statement not dying? - (B) Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Death sentence - Mitigating circumstances - Facts that accused were young and prime motive was property - If constitute mitigating circumstances - Held, cannot be used - Do not constitute mitigating circumstances and death sentence is warranted - Appeal dismissed.
