J. Jose Dhanapaul v S. Thomas and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 568; (1996) 3 SCC 587; (1996) SCC (L&S) 792; 1996 (73) FLR 1297; JT 1996 (3) SC 197; 1996 (2) LLJ 646; 1996 (1) LLN 456; 1996(2) SCALE 73; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 757; 1996 (2) SLR 65; 1996 (2) Supreme 515; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 1685

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appellant was not a party to the impugned order - Without being impleaded as a party, appointment of respondent was annulled by the impugned order - Held, Tribunal therefore has committed grave error of law in upsetting his appointment when he was not made a party - Appeal allowed.
