Nawab Mir Barkat Ali Khan Bahadur v Controller of Estate Duty

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1695; (1996) 10 SCC 685; [1996] 222 ITR 612; JT 1996 (9) SC 202; [1996] 89 TAXMAN 102

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Estate Duty Act, 1953, ss. 5, 9, 9(1), 36, 44, 44(1), 45, 46 and 46(1) - (A) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, sum of Rs 1,56,971 relating to Estate of late Mazharunnisa Begum is includable in estate of deceased as passing u/s. 5 of Estate Duty Act, 1953? - Held, High Court rightly came to conclusion that Mazharunnisa Begum was wife of late Nizam and consequently amount relating to her estate passed on to Nizam after her death and, therefore, was rightly includable in estate of late Nizam - Question answered against appellant and in favour of respondent - (B) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, sum of Rs 8,23,697 representing amount spent on construction of quarters of dependents and Khanazadas is includable in estate of deceased u/s. 9 of Estate Duty Act, 1953? - Held, HC referred to evidence on record and concluded that even though Khanazadas had right of occupation and buildings were given to them for that purpose no liability was attached to them towards cost of construction and that liability was discharged by late Nizam by meeting cost and, therefore, to that extent this would be taken as an extinguishment at expense of deceased Nizam of a debt or other right and, therefore, it amounted to disposition of property by late Nizam within two years of his death and consequently under fiction of s. 9 of Act property is deemed to have passed on his death - (C) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, for purposes of determining u/s. 36 of Estate Duty Act, 1953 principal value of estate passing on death of deceased, amount of estate duty payable is liable to be taken into account and principal value of estate should be reduced accordingly or whether amount could be deducted as a 'debt' u/s. 44 of Estate Duty Act, 1953? - Held, this issue is covered against appellant by a decision of Supreme Court in case of P. Leelavathamma v. CED, [1991 Indlaw SC 512] - Answered against appellant and in favour of respondent - (D) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, claim for allowance of sums as debts due was hit by limitation? - S. 46 seeks to impose limitations for excluding from allowances and deductions available u/s. 44 of Act amounts of debts incurred by deceased as mentioned in cl. (a) of s. 44 under circumstances mentioned in s. 46 - HC therefore, was not justified in treating these amounts as debts due from Nizam to beneficiaries concerned and in invoking s. 46(1) in that connection - It could not be said that claim for allowances of aforesaid sums was hit by limitations imposed by s. 46 of Act - (E) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, sum of Rs 5,01,460 being value of properties in occupation of Sahebzadas and Sahebzadis was includable in hands of deceased as property passing? - Held, there is no clear evidence on record to show that aforesaid amount represented value of properties which were occupied by way of full ownership by said Sahebzadas and Sahebzadis, said amount was rightly included in estate of deceased - Not possible to take any view contrary to one taken by HC as well as by authorities below - Appeal partly allowed.
