Reckitt and Colman of India Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 209; (1997) 10 SCC 379; 1997 (68) ECR 770; 1996 (88) E.L.T. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, Item 1B, Sch. 1 - Whether Robinsons Patent Barley was a 'preparation with a basis of starch' and, therefore, outside purview of exemption granted by an exemption notification D/- 01-03-1970, issued u/r. 8(1) (whereby Central Govt. had exempted prepared or preserved foods falling under Item 1B of First Schedule to Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944)? - Held, finding of Tribunal improper - Product comes within exemption - Having come to conclusion that said product was not a preparation based on starch, Tribunal should have allowed appeal - It was beyond competence of Tribunal to make out in favour of Revenue a case which Revenue had never canvassed and which appellants had never been required to meet - Appeal allowed.
