Escorts Limited v Presiding Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1619; (1997) 11 SCC 521; (1998) SCC (L&S) 235

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (ooo), 25-F and 25-G - Termination - Validity of - Whether Labour Court was in error in holding that it constituted retrenchment and was protected by ss. 25F and 25-G of Act ? - Held, termination of services of workman does not constitute retrenchment in view of cl. (bb) in s. 2(oo) of Act cl. (bb) excludes from ambit of expression 'retrenchment' as defined in main part of s. 2(oo) 'termination of services of workman as a result of non-renewal of contract of employment between employer and workman concerned on its expiry or of such contract being terminated under a stipulation in that behalf contained therein - Services of workman were terminated as per terms of contract of employment, it does not amount to retrenchment u/s. 2(oo) of Act and Labour Court was in error in holding that it constituted retrenchment and was protected by s. 25-G of Act - Appeal allowed.
