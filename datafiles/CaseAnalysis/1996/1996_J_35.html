Jai Jai Ram And Others v The U.P. State Road Transportcorporation, Lucknow And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3940; (1996) 4 SCC 727; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1071; AIR 1996 SC 2289; 1996 (74) FLR 2016; JT 1996 (6) SC 463; 1996 LabIC 2034; 1996 (2) LLJ 729; 1996 (2) LLN 465; 1996(5) SCALE 131; 1996 (3) SLJ 15; 1996 (5) SLR 141; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 413; 1996 (2) UJ 540; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 1803

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - (A) Disciplinary proceedings Competent authority - Delinquent and officer initiating action against him both Govt. servants sent on deputation to Corporation - Officer initiating action not subordinate to rank of appointing authority of delinquent - Plea that officer was not Govt. servant as per definition given in r. 9(7-B) - Whether tenable? - Whether action initiated by officer valid? - Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, r. 9(7 B) - (B) Disciplinary proceedings - Any other authority so long as it is not subordinate in rank or grade to authority by which delinquent Govt. servant was appointed - Whether can initiate disciplinary proceedings - (C) Whether there is requirement that authority which takes action must continue to have power of making appointment to civil service or on a civil post under Union or a State - Held, (A) Without substance as definition of Govt. servant given in rule is not relevant for purposes of art. 311 - Action initiated by officer valid - (B) Can initiate disciplinary proceedings - (C) There is no such requirement - Appeal dismissed.
