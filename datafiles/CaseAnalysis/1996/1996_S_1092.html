Cooch Behar Contractors' Association and Others v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 837; (1996) 10 SCC 380; JT 1996 (8) SC 343; 1996 (103) STC 477; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 57

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Contractors - Constitutional validity - Appellants were contractors - By insertion of s. 6D of the Act all transfer of property in goods involved in execution of a works contract was deemed to be a sale of those goods by person making transfer and purchase of those goods by the person to whom such transfer was made - Revenue sought to assess appellants u/s. 6D of the Act and appellants aggrieved moved HC challenged initially constitutionality of s. 6D of the Act - Subsequently cases were transferred to West Bengal Taxation Tribunal - Tribunal in judgment repelled and dismissed case - Whether 46th Amendment of the Constitution inserting cl. 29A in art. 366 was constitutionally valid - Held, goods used in execution of works contract stand transferred from contractor to contractee at time goods were incorporated in construction - There was a sale which was liable to tax - Conclusion made by Tribunal was correct - There was no substance in the argument regarding payment of royalty - No interference u/art. 136 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
