Kundanbhai Dulabhai Shaikh and another v Distt. Magistrate, Ahmedabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3216; (1996) 3 SCC 194; (1996) SCC (Cr) 470; 1996 (33) ACC 319; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 612; AIR 1996 SC 2998; 1996 (2) AWC 781; 1996 (1) CCR 199; 1996 CRLJ 1981; JT 1996 (2) SC 532; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 479; 1996 (2) Supreme 33

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Prevention of Black Marketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980, s. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - (A) Preventive detention under Act - Plea that right to make representation against is not constitutional right on ground of non - specification of authorities in art. 22(5) to whom same is to be made - Whether sustainable - (B) Representation against preventive detention - Delay in disposal - Order of detention is fatally affected - In one case, representation could not be disposed of as Central Govt. was not furnished comments by state Govt. in spite of telegrams and reminders, for over a month - In another case, representation remaining with state Govt. for about one month and when sent to Central Govt., comments were not furnished for a month - Whether guarantee of early disposal of representation was violated? - Order of detention - Held, (A) Plea, not sustainable - (B) In both cases, guarantee of early disposal of representation was violated - Order of detention quashed - Petition allowed.
