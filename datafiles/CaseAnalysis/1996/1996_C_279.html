Smith Kline and French (India) Limited and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2810; (1996) 8 SCC 579; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 22; 1996 (132) CTR 500; [1996] 219 ITR 581; JT 1996 (4) SC 231; 1996(3) SCALE 562; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 385; 1996 (3) Supreme 716; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 683

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Distributable Income, Education Cess

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Deduction - Surtax liability - Assessee claimed deduction of Rs. 76,777/- being surtax liability - Income Tax Officer disallowed said deduction - Appellate Assistant Commissioner allowed the assessee's claim but on further appeal by the Revenues, the Tribunal upheld the Revenues' contention and disallowed the claim for deduction on the basis of s. 40(a)(ii) of Act - HC had affirmed the view taken by the Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Rs. 76,777/- being the surtax liability is to be allowed as a deduction in computing the total income of the assessee for the assessment year 1976-77.

Held, surtax is essentially levied on the business profits of the company computed in accordance with the provisions of the Act. Merely because certain further deductions [adjustments] are provided by the Surtax Act from the said profits, it cannot be said that the surtax is not levied upon the profits determined or computed in accordance with the provisions of the Act. S. 4 of the Surtax Act read with the definition of 'chargeable profits' and the First Schedule make the position abundantly clear. Appeals dismissed.
