O. Bharathan v K. Sudhakaran and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3311; (1996) 2 SCC 704; AIR 1996 SC 1140; JT 1996 (2) SC 384; 1996 (1) KLT 466; 1996(1) SCALE 688; 1996 (1) Supreme 757; 1996 (1) UJ 307

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100 and 62 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 73 and 45 - Election petition - Reception of votes - Improper - Void or invalid votes - Witnesses showed evidence of voters admitted to have voted two times - Or must be deemed to have voted two times in view of similarity of signatures on two counter foils alleged to be related to said witnesses - Whether comparing of disputed signatures on counter foils by High Court judge without aid of expert or person conversant with disputed signatures is legal? - No - It is not in conformity with spirit of s. 73, of Evidence Act - Appeal allowed.
