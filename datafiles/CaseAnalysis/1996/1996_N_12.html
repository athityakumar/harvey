Tamil Nadu Electricity Board and Another v N. Raju Reddiar and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 9; (1996) 4 SCC 551; AIR 1996 SC 2025; 1996 (2) ARBLR 210; 1996 (2) CCC 433; JT 1996 (6) SC 14; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1996(4) SCALE 180; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Suit filed for recovery of amount - (A) Whether plaintiffs entitled to claim multi slab basis or single slab basis - (B) Whether conduct of some of Superintending Engineers in passing some of bills on multi slab basis can be pleaded as an estoppel against defendants and can form basis of plaintiffs' case? - Once contract is reduced to writing it is not open to parties to seek to prove its terms and it is only when written contract does not contain whole of agreement between parties and there is any ambiguity then oral evidence is permissible to prove other conditions which also must not be inconsistent with written contract - Passing of some bills by some of Superintending Engineers on multi slab basis is not determinative of terms and conditions of contract particularly when contract is a written one - Held, Division Bench of High Court wholly erred in law in allowing plaintiffs' appeal and granting decree accepting plaintiffs' claim that rate has to be paid on multi slab basis - Appeal allowed.
