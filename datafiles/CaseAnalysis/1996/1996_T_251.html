Assam Madhyamik Sikshak Arukarmachari Santha, Nagaon v The State Of Assam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2867; (1996) 9 SCC 186; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1208; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 185; AIR 1996 SC 2257; 1996 (73) FLR 1793; JT 1996 (5) SC 613; 1996(4) SCALE 650; 1996 (2) SLJ 61; 1996 (5) SLR 182B; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 116; 1996 (4) Supreme 783

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Assam Secondary Education (Provincialisation) Act, 1977

Summary: Assam Secondary Education (Provincialisation) Act, 1977, s. 4(3) - Aided institutions taken over by Govt. - Option given to teachers for superannuation at age of 58 years with pensionary benefits or at age of 60 years with C.P.F - No rules regarding this - Resolution by Govt. for teachers who had remained in service after appointed day and were yet to complete age of 60 years to treat them as Govt. teachers entitled to pension on attaining age of 58 years - Claim, same benefit should be given to teachers continued on appointed day and remained in service till attaining 60 years - Held, direction to re-deposit C.P.F withdrawn by them.
