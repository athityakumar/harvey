Jaipur Development Authority v Mahavir Housing Coop. Society, Jaipur and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2433; (1996) 11 SCC 229; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 578; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 406; 1996(7) SCALE 404; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 491; 1997 (5) Supreme 254; 1996 (2) UJ 733

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 - Rajasthan Urban Improvement Act, 1959 - Land Acquisition Officer determined compensation at rate of Rs. 5000 per bigha to respondent - Jai Ambe Cooperative Housing Society and Rs. 7500 per bigha to respondent - Mahavir Housing Cooperative Society - On reference, Civil Judge enhanced compensation at rate of Rs. 40,000 per bigha - As regards award in favour of Mahavir Housing Cooperative Society, no appeal was filed - But in execution an objection has been raised regarding additional amount awarded u/s. 23(1-A) which was negatived - What would be reasonable compensation which claimants are capable to get? - Held, claimants being subsequent purchasers cannot have a higher right than that original owner himself had - They cannot set up any title to property on basis of sale deeds and consideration but may be entitled to compensation obviously getting into shoes of claimant - Entire process has gone on in collusion - Having regard to facts and circumstances, two times more than what was granted by Land Acquisition Officer would be just compensation in given circumstances of case - Appeals allowed.
