Sadasivam v K. Doraisamy

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3815; (1996) 8 SCC 624; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 355; AIR 1996 SC 1724; 1996 (1) HLR 428; JT 1996 (2) SC 400; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; 1996(2) SCALE 89; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 and 65 - (A) Whether exclusive possession by a coparcener is adverse to other coparceners - (B) Alienation under Hindu Law - Father executing sale deed in favour of near relative without consideration and parties never intended to act upon it - Validity of deed - Held, not adverse to other coparceners, unless such possession is exercised by ousting others - It is sham and invalid - Appeal allowed.
