 New Delhi Municipal Committee v State of Punjab Etc

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Agrawal, J.S. Verma, B.N. Kirpal, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1512; (1997) 7 SCC 399

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Emergency Provisions, Government Of National Capital Territory Of Delhi Act, 1991, Constitutionality, Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970, Constitution (Twelfth Amendment) Act, 1962, Constitution (Sixty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1991, Constitution (Fourteenth Amendment) Act, 1962, Delhi Laws Act, 1915, Constitution (Tenth Amendment) Act, 1961, State Of Mizoram Act, 1986

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246(4), 289(1) and (2) - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 155 - Whether the properties owned by the States which are situated within Union Territories are exempt from paying property taxes? - Definition of 'State' provided in s. 3(58) of the General clauses Act, which declares that the word 'State' would include 'Union Territory', is inapplicable to art. 246(4) - Term 'Union Taxation' used in art. 289(1) will ordinarily mean 'all taxes leviable by the Union' and it includes within its ambit taxes on property levied within Union Territories - Held, the States can avail of the exemption provided in art. 289(1) in respect of their properties situated with in Union Territories - Property taxes levied by municipalities within Union Territories are properly within the ambit of the exemption provided in art. 289(1) and the States can avail of the exemption - Appeal dismissed.
