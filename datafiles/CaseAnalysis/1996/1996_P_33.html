R.Puthunainar Alhithan Etc v P.H. Pandian and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 688; (1996) 3 SCC 624; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 267; AIR 1996 SC 1599; 1996 (2) CLT 142; JT 1996 (4) SC 146; 1996(3) SCALE 317; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 932; 1996 (3) Supreme 476

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Expenditure - Election - Candidate - Corrupt practice - Appellant was elected by an election to the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly from the Assembly Constituency No.220 - Appellant's election was challenged by respondent no. 1, unsuccessful candidate - Appellant had declared the election expenditure as Rs. 36,350/- whereas the appellant had accepted that he had used the vehicle bearing registration No.TN-72 1909 and had incurred an expenditure towards the running of that vehicle during the election campaign of Rs. 15,875/- and he had also admitted that he had used another vehicle, bearing registration No.TNH-555 - Respondent no. 1 alleged that appellant had exceeded the limit prescribed under the 1951 Act, and therefore, the appellant's election was void - HC held that as the appellant had admitted to using two vehicles for the election but expenditure was recorded for one vehicle only and on being told the appellant failed to produce records - Thus it was held that the appellant had committed corrupt practice under ss. 123(6) and 77 of the 1951 Act and his election was declared as void - Hence, the instant petition - Whether the finding of the HC that the appellant had committed corrupt practice under s. 123(6) of the 1951 Act was sustainable in law - Held, that the prescribed expenditure was Rs. 50,000/- and as per s. 123(6) of the 1951 Act incurring or authorizing of expenditure in contravention of s. 77 of that Act was deemed to be corrupt practice for the purpose of the 1951 Act - Appellant had admitted in his written statement that he had used two vehicles for the purpose of election the vehicle bearing registration No.TN-72 1909 and for which he had incurred an expenditure of Rs. 15,875/- during the election campaign and he had also admitted that he had used another vehicle, bearing registration No.TNH-555 whose expenditure was not submitted - Appellant failed to give complete record of his expenses after reasonable opportunity was provided - Hence, appellant had deliberately suppressed the material fact of the user of the vehicle and the expenditure incurred for its use - Further, u/s. 77 of the 1951 Act it was necessary for the candidate/election agent to maintain a regular account of the expenditure incurred in connection with the election and a statement in that behalf was required to be filed before the Collector - Respondent had issued a notice to the appellant calling upon him to produce the expenditure account which he had failed to produce - Appellant had deliberately concealed the expenditure records and that the appellant had committed corrupt practice u/s. 123(6) of the 1951 Act - Declaration of the result of the election of the appellant as void was not vitiated by any error of law warranting interference - Appeal dismissed.
