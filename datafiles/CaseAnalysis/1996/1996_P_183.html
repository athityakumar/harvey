Hindustan Paper Corporation v Purnendu Chakrobarty and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1825; (1996) 11 SCC 404; (1997) SCC (L&S) 244; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 122; JT 1996 (10) SC 1; 1997 (2) LLJ 704; 1996(8) SCALE 96; 1996 (6) SLR 14; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 159; 1996 (8) Supreme 12; 1997 (1) UJ 369

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Loss Of Lien

Summary: Service - Unauthorised absent - Hindustan Paper Corporation Conduct, Discipline and Appeal Rules, rr. 23 (vi)(E) and 25 - (A) Unauthorised absence from duty - Failure to give satisfactory explanation and medical certificate - Effect - Employees should avail the leave with prior sanction and extend the same only after the earlier one was sanctioned - Application filed by the employee not only in proper form and not supported by Medical certificate - Held, the context that the appellant-Corporation invoked r. 23(vi)(E) and terminate the employee thereof, is justifiable - (B) R. 23(vi)(E) - Validity - Held, rule read in consonance with the principles of natural justice - Rule is not arbitrary or unreasonable or ultra vires of art. 14 of the - Before taking action under the rule, opportunity should be given to employee - If the cause shown by the employee is good and acceptable, no action would be taken - Clause of the rule is neither unreasonable nor violative of art. 16 of the Constitution - (C) Unauthorised absence from duty - Failure to give satisfactory explanation supported by medical certificate - Invoking r. 23(vi)(E) by employer - Penalty by way of loss of lien to the employee - Allegation of principles of natural justice have not been complied with - As per r. 23(vi)(E) unauthorized absent or overstay beyond the sanctioned period of leave granted or subsequently extended for more than 8 consecutive days would result in loss of lien - In the present case, employee unauthorisedly absent for more than 6 months - Employer sought explanation - Employee has not properly given explanation - Held, principles of natural justice complied with - No enquiry as contemplated under r. 25 is required - Appeal disposed of.
