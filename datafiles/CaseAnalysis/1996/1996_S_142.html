Messrs Marikar Motors Limited v Sales Tax Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 770; (1996) 3 SCC 263; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 392; [1996] 101 ITR 377; JT 1996 (2) SC 169; 1996(2) SCALE 120; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 217; 1996 (101) STC 377; 1996 (2) Supreme 204

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Sale Price, Producer Of Sugar

Summary: Sales Tax - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Sugarcane (Control) Order, 1966 - (A) Determination of price - On a perusal of the relevant provisions of the Sugar-cane (Control) Order, 1966, particularly cls. 3 and 5-A it is clear that the total price of sugar-cane fixed thereunder is the aggregate of the minimum cane price fixed u/cl. 3 and the additional cane price fixed u/cl. 5-A - Under the Statute there is no liability of the purchaser to pay to the grower any amount in excess of this aggregate amount - For the purpose of determining the price of sugar-cane for computation of the purchase tax, the only significant amount is the aggregate of the minimum price fixed u/cl. 3 and the additional cane price fixed u/cl. 5-A, unless a higher price is paid to the grower by agreement between the purchaser and grower - Unless a clear finding to that effect is recorded, the amount paid by the purchaser in excess of the aggregate of the minimum price fixed u/cl. 3 and the additional price fixed u/cl. 5-A, as a part of the amount paid as advance prior to fixation of the additional price u/cl. 5-A, cannot be treated automatically as a part of the total price of sugarcane - (B) In a Hire Purchase Agreement, when does the sale come about? - Coming into being of the sale is a question of fact and it takes place when the hirer exercises the option - Held, sales takes place when the hirer exercised his option - Matters remitted to the HC.
