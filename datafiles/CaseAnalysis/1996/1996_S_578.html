A. S. Narayana Deekshitulu v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2727; (1996) 9 SCC 548; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 135; AIR 1996 SC 1765; JT 1996 (3) SC 482; 1996(2) SCALE 911; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 543; 1996 (5) Supreme 293

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25 and 26 - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1966, ss. 34, 35, 37, 39 and 144 - Archak (Priest) - Abolition of hereditary right to appointment - Constitutionality - Held, hereditary right to appointment is not essential part of religion - Not violative of fundamental rights - Qualifications prescribed by Act - Not arbitrary, unjust and unfair - Right to a share in collections, Prasadam as per custom/usage also stands abolished.
