Smt. Kilasho Devi Burman and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3505; (1996) 7 SCC 613; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 337; AIR 1996 SC 3114; 1996 (132) CTR 173; [1996] 219 ITR 214; JT 1996 (2) SC 555; 1996(1) SCALE 801; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 301; 1996 (2) Supreme 41; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 346; 1996 (2) TLR 458

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 256(2) - Scope of Reference - (A) Can High Court go behind facts found by tribunal? - HC in a reference under taxation statutes exercises advisory jurisdiction in regard to question of law - (B) When can it go into facts? - Is it when it has before it a question that asks, whether tribunals has, upon evidence on record before it, come to a conclusion which is perverse - Can HC look at evidence that was not before tribunal? - Held, (A) Cannot go behind those facts - (B) Only when tribunal has reached a perverse conclusion, for this is question of law - (C) Cannot look at such evidence - Appeal allowed.
