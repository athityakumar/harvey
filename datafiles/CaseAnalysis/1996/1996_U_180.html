Anuj Kumar Dey and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1997; (1997) SCC (L&S) 153; (1997) 1 SCC 366; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 298; 1997 (66) DLT 592; 1997 (75) FLR 173; JT 1996 (10) SC 679; 1996(8) SCALE 659; 1997 (2) SLJ 63; 1997 (1) SLR 220; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 269; 1996 (8) Supreme 605

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Enrolled, Indian Navy, Naval Service, Navy (Pension) Regulations, 1964, Navy Act, 1957

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Navy Act, 1957, ss. 12 and 17(4) - Navy (Pension) Regulations, 1964, regns. 261 and 79 - Whether appellant can be regarded as a 'sailor' during period of Artificer Apprentice? - Regn. 261 clearly lays down that recruitment of sailors shall be made through boy entry, artificer apprentice entry and direct entry as officer - S. 12 lays down that where a person after his enrollment has for a period of three months from date of such enrollment been in receipt of pay as sailor, he shall be deemed to have been duly enrolled - Held, appellant was enrolled as an Artificer Apprentice, he was a sailor and was serving Navy as a sailor - (B) Whether appellant could be said to have been in service of Navy during period of training and on this bases entitle to pension? - Appellant was enrolled as a sailor, took oath as a sailor and drew salary as a sailor and was in service of Navy as a sailor during period of undergoing training as Artificer Apprentice - Regn. 79 lays down that all service from date of enrollment or advancement to rank of ordinary seaman or equivalent to date of discharge shall qualify for pension or gratuity - Statutory certificate given u/s. 17(4) of Navy Act state full period of service in Indian Navy this period of service is more than qualifying period of 15 years - Further held, appellant even during period of training working as Artificer Apprentice was in service of Navy, entitled to get pension accordingly - Appeal allowed.
