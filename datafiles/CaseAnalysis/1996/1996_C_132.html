Hindustan Polymers Company Limited and Others v Collector of Central Excise, Guntur

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1548; (1997) 11 SCC 302; 1999 (106) E.L.T. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Practice and Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether excise duty should be chargeable at coloured stage of polystyrene when it was cleared from factory after colouring or at uncoloured stage, as it was captively consumed in factory in their colouring plant? - Held, Tribunal should not, in this case, have passed an order which proceeded upon a basis that is altogether different from that of demand made upon appellants - That is not 'moulding' relief - Demand that was made upon appellants was under Tariff Item 68 and it proceeded upon basis that there was a process of manufacture of coloured polystyrene from C uncoloured polystyrene - Having come to a conclusion against Revenue on these counts, appropriate order for Tribunal to have passed was to have set aside demand and left it open to Revenue to proceed against appellants, as permissible under law - Appellants would then have had opportunity of meeting precise case made out by Revenue - Appeal allowed.
