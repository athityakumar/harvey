Naramadaben Maganlal Thakker v Pranjivandas Maganlal Thakker and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2529; (1997) 2 SCC 255; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 647; 1997 (30) ALR 119; 1996 (4) CCC 90; 1996 (4) CLT 174; JT 1996 (9) SC 273; 1996(7) SCALE 334; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 780; 1996 (7) Supreme 654

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 122 and 123 - Owner of properties executed a gift deed in favour of respondent, later cancelled said gift and two days before his death executed a Will in favour of appellant - Whether gift in question had become complete u/s. 123 of Transfer of Property Act? - Held, gift deed conferred only limited right upon respondent-donee, and that donor had executed a conditional gift deed and retained possession and enjoyment of property during lifetime, and recitals in cancellation deed is consistent with recitals in gift deed, therefore deed is duly cancelled - Appeal dismissed.
