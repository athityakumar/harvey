Commissloner Of Income Tax,Shilong v Jai Prakash Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3154; (1996) 3 SCC 525; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 118; AIR 1996 SC 1303; 1996 (132) CTR 262; [1996] 219 ITR 737; JT 1996 (3) SC 356; 1996(2) SCALE 832; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 377; 1996 (3) Supreme 61; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 407

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(29), 142 (1), 143 (2) and 159 - CPC, 1908, s. 2(11) - A.Y. 1965-66, 1966- 67 and 1967-68 - Notice to legal representatives - After the death of the assessee returns relating to his income filed by his eldest son - Legality of assessment orders passed after service of notice on eldest son except other legal representatives ? - Held, principle that emerges from the decision of HC is that an omission to serve or any defect in the service of notices provided by procedural provisions does not efface or erase the liability to pay tax where such liability is created by distinct substantive provisions - Any such omission or defect may render the order made irregular - depending upon the natu re of the provision not complied with- but certainly not void or illegal - Appeals allowed.
