Tripura State Cooperative Union Limited and Another v Bimal Kanti Bhattacharjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2836; (1997) 10 SCC 368; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1697

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularisation - Whether direction given by High Court for regularisation of respondent 1 on post of photographer, justified? - Diploma granted to respondent 1 was only with regard to photography and had no connection with Technology of Printing - Respondent 1 discontinued his studies in second year preparatory course in 1981-82 and neither passed preparatory course nor passed five years' diploma course - Respondent 1 made incorrect statements about his qualifications - No post of photographer in Cooperative Union - Respondent 1 discharging work of clerk - As a result of direction given by HC for regularisation, Cooperative Union would be required to create a post of photographer for purpose - Such a direction was not permissible - Held, since there is no post of photographer, principle of equal pay for equal work cannot be applied - Appeal allowed.
