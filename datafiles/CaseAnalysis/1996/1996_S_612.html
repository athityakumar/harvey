Al-Karim Educational Trust and Another v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2641; (1996) 8 SCC 330; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 367; AIR 1996 SC 1469; JT 1996 (2) SC 662; 1996(2) SCALE 474; 1996 (2) SLR 53; 1996 (2) Supreme 477; 1996 (1) UJ 540; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 630

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, arts. 226 and 133 - Bihar State Universities Act, 1976, s. 21 - Education - Medical course - Affiliation - Grant of - Ordinarily decided by State Govt. after consultation with Medical Council - College by minority community - Temporary affiliation - Only minor deficiencies remaining to be complied with - Held, insistence on absolute adherence to requirements - Not proper when institution has begun to function - Unreasonable withholding of decision by State Govt. - Supreme Court constrains to exercise jurisdiction.
