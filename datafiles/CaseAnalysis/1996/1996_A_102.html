Allahabad Bank Officers' Association and Another v Allahabad Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3927; (1996) 4 SCC 504; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1037; AIR 1996 SC 2030; 1996 (73) FLR 1636; JT 1996 (5) SC 275; 1996 LabIC 1730; 1996 (2) LLJ 519; 1996 (2) LLN 469; 1996(4) SCALE 150; 1996 (2) SCJ 483; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 172; 1996 (2) UJ 328; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 2012

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Allahabad Bank Officers Employees (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976 - Whether the order of compulsory retirement casts a stigma on appellant 2? - Order of compulsory retirement passed in terms of Regn. 19 - Power to compulsorily retire a govt. servant is distinct from power to punish him for misconduct - Compulsorily retired govt. servant does not loose any part of the benefit earned during service Compulsory retirement differs both from dismissal and removal as it involves no penal consequences - Held, appeal dismissed.
