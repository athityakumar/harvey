I. Vikheshe Sema v Hokishe Sema

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2584; (1996) 4 SCC 53; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 274; AIR 1996 SC 1842; AIR 1996 SCW 2151; JT 1996 (6) SC 100; 1996(4) SCALE 93; 1996 (2) SCJ 552; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 157; 1996 (2) UJ 444

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 62(3)(4) and 100(1)(c) - (A) Void votes - Inclusion of name of voter at more than one place - Whether makes his vote void? - Whether voting by such person at both places is necessary to make it void? -

(B) Election petition - Ground, polling of large number of void votes - Polling of void votes in large number proved - Held, (A) Does not by itself make his vote void - Necessary to make it void - (B) Court should identify void votes, exclude them and recount valid votes before concluding that election has been materially affected - Appeal allowed.
