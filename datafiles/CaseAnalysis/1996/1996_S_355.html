Smt. Anuradha Mukherjee and Others Etc v Union of India and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3403; (1996) 9 SCC 59; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1187; 1996 (1) CLR 789; 1996 (73) FLR 1237; JT 1996 (3) SC 576; 1996(3) SCALE 135; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 276; 1996 (2) SLR 625; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 1395

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Emoluments

Summary: Service - Emoluments - Corporate - Direct recruits - Junior - Appellants were graduates appointed as Lower Division Clerks Grade II on different dates - Railway Administration restructured the ministerial cadres in the Railways, determined their gradewise percentage of the posts and made distribution - Forty percent of the vacancies in the Clerks category were earmarked for Clerks [Grade II] by Railway Board's letter - Subsequently it was revised by another Board's letter on another date - Whether there was proper selection of graduate clerks by the Railway administration - Held, it was seen that graduate Clerks though appointed as Grade II after 1-10-1980 by process of selection through open competitive examination or limited recruitment by departmental examination were upgraded under the aforesaid rules, they would not get the promotion with effect from the pro forma date of 1-10-1980 but only from the date of their actual appointment as Grade II Clerks, nationally as Grade I Clerks since their appointments were after 1-10-1980 - Inter se seniority of the twenty percent direct recruits on the one hand and limited recruitment graduate Grade II Clerks and promotees on the other, should be determined in accordance with para 302 of the Railway Establishment - They could get seniority not from the date of their initial appointment but from the date on which they were actually selected and appointed in accordance with the rules and their appointment and seniority would take effect from the date of selection after due completion of the process and they would be junior to in-service as well as direct recruit-candidates - It would be clear that the directions in various letters of the Board should be worked out in the above manner and the seniority should be determined accordingly - Appeals disposed of.
