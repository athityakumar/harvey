Govt. Of Tamil Nadu And Ors. v S. Vel Raj

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1558; (1997) SCC (L&S) 689; (1997) 2 SCC 708; AIR 1997 SC 1900; 1997 (2) CalLT(SC) 93; 1997 (2) ESC 1022; JT 1997 (1) SC 349; 1997 (2) LLN 26; 1996(9) SCALE 728; 1996 (6) SLR 358; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 452; 1997 (1) Supreme 387

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution, arts. 309 and 311 - T.N. Police Subordinate Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, r. 1 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s.15 - Service Rules - Misconduct - Police Constable found in drunken condition and in 'Mufti' while on duty - Departmental enquiry - Punishment of compulsory retirement - As enhanced by appellate authority - Tribunal quashing the order - Ground, evidence was inconsistent and misconduct not proved - (A ) Validity of Quashing it - (B) Charge memo in the disciplinary proceedings issued by DSP who was not appointing authority - Validity - (C) If punishment can be considered excessive - Erroneous, tribunal transgressed its jurisdiction in re-examining evidence as if it was an appellate authority - Not invalid - No - Appeal allowed. .
