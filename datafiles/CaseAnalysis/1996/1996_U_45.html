Mrs Anil Katiyar v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1832; (1997) 1 SCC 280; (1997) SCC (L&S) 728; AIR 1997 SC 2656; 1997 (1) CLR 378; JT 1996 (10) SC 768; 1997 (1) LLN 46; 1996(8) SCALE 57; 1997 (1) SLJ 145; 1997 (1) SLR 153

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14 - Constitution, art. 14 - Jurisdiction of Tribunal - Selection of candidate to promotional post - Question about gradation of candidate in confidential reports - Observation by Tribunal that 'outstanding grade given to candidate did not flow from various parameters given and reports entered therein' - (A) Whether liable to be set aside - (B) Whether tribunal could sit in judgment over selection made by Departmental Promotion Committee - Held, (A) liable to be set aside - (B) Could not sit in judgment, unless selection is assailed as being vitiated by mala fides or as arbitrary - Appeal disposed of.
