Raghubir Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3134; (1996) 9 SCC 233; (1996) SCC (Cr) 980; AIR 1996 SC 3051; 1996 (1) Crimes 181; JT 1996 (4) SC 1; 1996(2) SCALE 689; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Self Defence

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 149, 300 and 325 - Offence of murder and causing grievous hurt - Accused persons armed with weapons forming unlawful assembly - Attacking one person - Two of them inflicting fatal blows on person intervening, causing his death - Injury on vital parts of body of deceased found to be intentional and not accidental - FIR containing details - Evidence of injured eye-witness corroborated by medical evidence - (A) Whether accused inflicting blows on deceased intervenor liable to be convicted for murder - (B) Other accused causing grievous hurt by forming unlawful assembly - Whether to be convicted - Held, (A) Liable to be convicted for murder - (B) Other accused convicted for causing grievous hurt by forming unlawful assembly - Appeal dismissed.
