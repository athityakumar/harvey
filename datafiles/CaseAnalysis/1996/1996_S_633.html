Ram Nath Mahto v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2988; (1996) 8 SCC 630; (1996) SCC (Cr) 726; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 563; AIR 1996 SC 2511; 1996 (2) CCR 35; 1996 CRLJ 3585; JT 1997 (10) SC 478; 1996(3) SCALE 441; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 163; 1996 (3) Supreme 567

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 396 - Dacoity with murder - Identification parade by Magistrate - Prosecution witness identified accused - But denied to identify in Trial Court by fear of accused - Held, oral testimony of Magistrate on issue is substantive evidence - More so, there is remark of Trial Court upon staring on witness by accused on record - Conviction based upon above is proper.
