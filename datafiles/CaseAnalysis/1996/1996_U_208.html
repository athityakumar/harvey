Indian Council For Enviro-Legal Action, Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1073; (1996) 3 SCC 212; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 544; AIR 1996 SC 1446; JT 1996 (2) SC 196; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 503

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Tort; Water

Keywords: Act Of God

Summary: Water - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Hazardous Wastes (Management and Handling) Rules, 1989 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 - PIL - Environmental pollution - Highly toxic effluents from private companies polluting environment as whole - Enormous damage to water, cattle, plants and to area - (A) Writ against private companies - Maintainability - Held, writ petition is not really for issuance of appropriate writ, order or directions against the respondents but is directed against the UOI, Govt. of Rajasthan and R.P.C.B to compel them to perform their statutory duties enjoined by the Acts on the ground that their failure to carry out their statutory duties is seriously undermining the right to life guaranteed by art. 21 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - An industry is established without obtaining the requisite permission and clearances and running in blatant disregard of law to the detriment of life and liberty of the citizens living in the vicinity, cannot be suggested with any modicum of reasonableness that Supreme Court has no power to intervene and protect the fundamental right to life and liberty of the citizens of this country - (B) Report of Experts - Reliability of - Held, experts prepared the report without any bias - Reports called by Supreme Court and several orders passed on the basis of those reports - Objection raised against it after several years is not tenable - (C) Power of Central Govt. to take remedial measures - Held, Central Govt. is empowered to take all measures and issue all such directions - The said powers will include giving directions for the removal of sludge, for undertaking remedial measures and also the power to impose the cost of remedial measures on the offending industry and utilizes the amount so recovered for carrying out remedial measures - Directions issued.
