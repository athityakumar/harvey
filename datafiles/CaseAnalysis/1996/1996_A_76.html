State of Punjab and Others v Ajit Singh

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3276; (1997) 11 SCC 368

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Respondent was suspended from service - Respondent filed petition challenging order of suspension before High Court, which was allowed - Present appeal - Held, no ground to interfere with judgment of HC insofar as quashing order of suspension is concerned - However, HC was in error in setting aside charge sheet that was served on respondent in disciplinary proceedings - In doing so HC had gone into merits of allegations on which charge sheet was based and even though charges had yet to be proved by evidence to be adduced in disciplinary proceedings - Allegations are based on documents which would have been produced as evidence to prove charges in disciplinary proceedings, till such evidence was produced it could not be said that charges contained in charge sheet were without any basis whatsoever - Impugned order of HC insofar as they relate to quashing of charge sheet are set aside - Appeal allowed.
