Shri Heikham Surchandra Singh and Others Etc v Representative of "LIOS" Kakching, Manipur

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1715; (1997) 2 SCC 523; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 53; 1996 (4) CCC 195; 1997 (1) CLT 294; JT 1996 (10) SC 442; 1996(8) SCALE 407; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 371; 1996 (8) Supreme 229

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(2), 17, 46, 340, 341, 341(1), 341(2) and 366(24) - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Commission Report - Entitlement of Status - Issue of Caste Certificates - Notification Quashed - State Govt. accepted Commission's recommendations and issued directions for issue of caste certificates to Respondents/persons, not only for their social backwardness but also their educational backwardness as laid down in Constitution - High Court, hold that impugned order of Govt. was not sustainable in the light of Presidential Order, however, directed that till appropriate legislation was passed, Respondents were not entitled to status of Scheduled Castes - Appellants submitted that HC was not right in quashing notification issued by Govt. - Hence instant appeal - Whether Respondents were Schedules Tribes and entitled to claim status as Scheduled Castes - Held, Respondents were subject to disabilities mentioned by Commissioners and accepted by statutory Commission under arts. 17, 15(2) and 46 of Constitution - Commission had pointed out that Govt. which issued social status certificate to Respondents, as Scheduled Caste was without any enquiry and misuse of power to claim false social status - HC was right that Respondents would be entitled to status of Scheduled Castes, before issuance of certificate of status, competent officer should enquire if applicant is subjected to disabilities of Scheduled Castes u/art. 341(2) of Constitution - Court hold that, HC was not right in concluding that though certificates issued by Commissioner were valid, that would be subject to law made by Parliament - State Govt. was directed to furnish report submitted by Commission, to President, for appropriate action by Central Govt. by laying before Parliament to effect necessary amendment u/art. 341(2) of Constitution - Appeals disposed of.
