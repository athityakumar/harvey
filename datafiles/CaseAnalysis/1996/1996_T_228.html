State Bank Of Patiala, Patiala v The Commissioner Of Income-Tax,Patiala

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, N.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3237; (1996) 3 SCC 513; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 1; AIR 1996 SC 1525; 1996 (132) CTR 273; [1996] 219 ITR 706; JT 1996 (3) SC 317; 1996(2) SCALE 871; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 359; 1996 (3) Supreme 146; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 416; 1996 (2) TLR 451

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, Companies (Profits) Sur-tax Act of 1964, sch.1, r.1(xi) (b), sch.2, r.1 (iii) - Bank transfered amount in balance sheet as reserves ad-hoc- when no anticipated liability - Neither any amount written off nor any deduction claimed during relavent assessment years - Assessee treated it as reserves and not as provisions to meet liability etc.- Whether such reserves qualifying for tax relief - Held, a banking company not bound to anticipate on the date of preparation of balance sheet all or any of its debts -Such amount kept amounts to Reserves and available for relief as Reserves.
