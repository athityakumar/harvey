Cantonment Board, Mathura v Krishna Bricks and Lime Factory

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2555; (1996) 6 SCC 72; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 80; 1997 (1) AWC 197; JT 1996 (8) SC 180; 1996(6) SCALE 510; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 135; 1996 (6) Supreme 688; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 53

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: State Laws, Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, Constitution (Sixtieth Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 276(2) - Cantonments Act, 1924 - Ss. 3 and 60 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 142-A - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916 ,s. 128(1)(ii) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) Whether the ceiling prescribed by art. 276(2) of the Constitution, 1950, shall also be applicable to the Board which has been established under the Cantonments Act, 1924? - (B) Whether the provisions of art. 276 shall be applicable, be examined by construing the scope of s. 60 of the Cantonments Act? - (C) Whether u/s. 60 of the Cantonments Act, 1924, while imposing a profession Tax, art. 276(2) can be violated? - Held, no - S. 60 of the Cantonments Act, 1924 prescribes two conditions for imposition of tax by the Board - Firstly, that there must be a previous sanction of the Central Govt. and secondly that only such tax can be imposed within any cantonment which under any enactment for the time being in force may be imposed in any municipality in the State wherein such cantonment is situated - The term 'impose' should, therefore, be taken to have been used in wider sense in s. 60 and that being so, the respondent cannot collect taxes in contravention of State laws - Appeal dismissed.
