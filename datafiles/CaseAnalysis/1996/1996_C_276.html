National Thermal Power Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1625; (1997) 7 SCC 489; 1999 (157) CTR 249; 1998 (99) E.L.T. 200; [1998] 229 ITR 383; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 358

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 254 - What is extent of jurisdiction of tribunal to examine a question of law which arises from facts as found by authorities below and having a bearing on tax liability of assessee? - Held, tribunal has jurisdiction to examine a question of law which arises from facts as found by authorities below and having a bearing on tax liability of assessee - Appellate Tribunal may, after giving both parties to appeal an opportunity of being heard, pass such orders thereon as it thinks fit u/s. 254 - Power of tribunal in dealing with appeals is thus expressed in widest possible terms - Purpose of assessment proceedings before taxing authorities is to assess correctly tax liability of an assessee in accordance with law - Assessee as well as department have a right to file an appeal/cross-objections before tribunal - Order accordingly.
