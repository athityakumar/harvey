M/S.Poothundu Plantations Private Ltd. v Agricultural Income Tax Officer,Chittoor, Kerala State, And

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2204; (1996) 9 SCC 499; AIR 1996 SC 3427; 1996 (134) CTR 593; 1996 (66) ECR 224; 2004 (178) E.L.T. 16; [1996] 221 ITR 557; JT 1996 (6) SC 601; 1996 (2) KLT 499; 1996(5) SCALE 384; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 567; 1996 (5) Supreme 618; [1996] 87 TAXMAN 286; 1996 (2) TLR 705

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24 - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950, ss. 12 and 36 - Whether an Agricultural ITO can rectify order passed by his predecessor in office, on the ground that assessment order was passed by wrongly construing s. 12 of the 1950 Act ? - Held, s. 24 of the 1922 Act and s. 12 of 1950 Act are not identically worded - It was not a rectifiable error as the matter had been referred to a larger bench for decision - The scope of the two Acts and the content of the two sections are materially different - Error committed by the officer was not an error apparent on the record - Appeal allowed.
