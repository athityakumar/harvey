Kesoram Reyon (A Unit of Messrs Kesoram Industries Limited) v Collector of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1030; (1996) 5 SCC 576; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 475; 1996 (3) CLT 330; 1996 (66) ECR 201; 1996 (86) E.L.T. 464; JT 1996 (7) SC 519; 1996 (16) RLT 97; 1996(6) SCALE 123; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 77; 1996 (6) Supreme 394

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Excise - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 15(1), 46, 59, 61( 1)(b) and 72 - Issue - (A) What should be the rate at which customs duty is to be levied on goods that remain in a bonded warehouse beyond the permitted period? - The consequence of non-removal of warehoused good within the permitted period or the permitted extension is, by virtue of the terms of s. 72, that the date on which it comes to end is the date relevant for determining the rate of duty - When the duty is in fact demanded is not relevant - Held (A) - Appeal dismissed with costs.
