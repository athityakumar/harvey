All India Federation Of Central Excise Etc v The Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3720; (1997) SCC (L&S) 159; (1997) 1 SCC 520; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 179; JT 1997 (10) SC 630; 1996(8) SCALE 546; 1997 (2) SLJ 69; 1997 (6) Supreme 197

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Service - Indian Customs and Central Excise Service Group 'A' Rules, 1987, rr. 18(2), 19(2) and 20 - Whether r. 18(2) acts as impediment in determination of seniority of Group 'B' Officers of different feeder cadres in their quota of promotion to entry grade of Asst. Collector/Senior Superintendent in Indian Customs and Central Excise Service Group 'A' ? - Inter se seniority between direct recruit appraisers and promotees is concerned, that should be finalised in light of judgment of this Court in Gaya Baksh Yadav case, 1996 Indlaw SC 2936 - Held, proposal as modified is just, fair and equitable and accordingly UOI directed to amend impugned Rules so far as Group 'A' Service is concerned and it would, therefore, be of necessity that Govt. should rearrange their inter se seniority and promotions of respective direct recruits and promotees within their quota and consequential promotions in further higher services, their seniority be arranged accordingly - Petitions disposed of.
