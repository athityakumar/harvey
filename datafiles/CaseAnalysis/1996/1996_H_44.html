State of Uttar Pradesh v Harish Chandra

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 157; (1996) 9 SCC 309; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1240; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 37; AIR 1996 SC 2173; 1996 (3) AWC 1284; 1996 (2) CCC 212; 1997 (69) ECR 243; 1996 (85) E.L.T. 209; JT 1996 (4) SC 414; 1996 LabIC 1843; 1996 (2) LLJ 627; 1996(3) SCALE 730; 1996 (2) SLR 723; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 260; 1996 (4) Supreme 14; 1996 (3) UPLBEC 1808

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Subordinate Officers Clerical Staff (Direct Recruitment) Rules, 1985, rr. 23 and 26 - Where High Court justified in issuing mandamus to make recruitment of respondents after expiry of said list, list under Recruitment Rules having force only for a period of one year from date of selection? - Held, duty that may be enjoined by mandamus may be one imposed by Constitution or a Statute or by Rules or orders having force of law but no mandamus can be issued to direct Govt. to refrain from enforcing provisions of law or to do something which is contrary to law - U/r. 26 of Recruitment Rules HC was wrong to issue mandamus - Appeal allowed.
