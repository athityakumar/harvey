Shrijee Sales Corporation and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1561; (1997) 3 SCC 398; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 421; 1997 (1) CLT 38; 1997 (89) E.L.T. 452; JT 1996 (11) SC 648; 1997(1) SCALE 117; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 888; 1997 (1) Supreme 352

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Notification No 205/F - No 355/141/80 - Cus I D/- 16-10-1980 - Notification No 66 - Cus D/- 15-3-1979 - (A) Whether plea of estoppel can be taken in a case involving public interest? - Determination of applicability of promissory estoppel against public authority/Govt. hinges upon balance of equity or 'public interest' - It is Court which has to determine whether Govt. should be held exempt from liability of 'promise' or 'representation' - Held, supervening public interest in present case - Govt. justified in withdrawing exemption without notice - Appeal dismissed.
