Rameshwarlal v Municipal Council, Tonk and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2355; (1996) 6 SCC 100; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 734; 1996 (3) CCC 382; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 499; 1996(6) SCALE 370; 1996 (5) SLR 674; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 227; 1996 (6) Supreme 717

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Petitioner was denied salary - Writ petition for arrears of pay - High Court held that appropriate remedy is before Trial Court - Whether time taken by HC and Supreme Court can be counted in calculating period of limitation? - Held, since HC expressly declined to grant relief relegating petitioner to a suit in Civil Court, petitioner cannot be left remediless - Accordingly, time taken in prosecuting proceedings before HC and Supreme Court, obviously pursued diligently and bona fide, needs to be excluded - Petition disposed of.
