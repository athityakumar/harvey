Periasami and Another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2490; (1996) 6 SCC 457; (1997) SCC (Cr) 121; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 105; 1996 (4) CCR 150; 1996 (4) Crimes 39; 1997 CRLJ 219; JT 1996 (8) SC 449; 1996(7) SCALE 202; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 757; 1996 (7) Supreme 240

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 162 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part I, 302 and 34 - Murder - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Two eye-witnesses - Appellants acquited by Trial Court but High Court convicted them for murder and grevious hurt - Whether HC rightly reversed acquital order? - Law entitles appellants to have benefit of that reasonable doubt concerning beginning part of occurrence and renders them liable for culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Held, prior enimity between parties - Ocular and medical testimony corraborates each other well - There remains doubt regarding who was aggressor - Even if accused pleads right to self defence, they exceeded it - Conviction altered to s. 304, Part I - Order accordingly.
