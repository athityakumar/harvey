Government of Tamil Nadu v P. A. Manickam

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3983; (1996) 8 SCC 519; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1029; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 373; AIR 1996 SC 2250; 1996 (1) CLR 808; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 381; 1996 LabIC 1841; 1996(2) SCALE 759; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 1137; 1996 (2) SLR 331; 1996 (3) Supreme 246

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(d) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Compulsory retirement - Rule permitting to retire employees after attaining age of fifty years or after completion of twenty five years qualifying service - Non referring of cases of compulsory retirement of employees to review committee six months before attaining age of fifty years or on completion of twenty years service, as required by directions of Govt. - Whether can prohibit Govt. to compulsorily retire employee at any time after attaining age of fifty years or on completion of twenty years service - Held, cannot prohibit Govt. to compulsorily retire employee - Appeal allowed.
