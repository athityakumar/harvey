Union of India and Others v Major General Madan Lal Yadav [Retd.]

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 670; (1996) 4 SCC 127; (1996) SCC (Cr) 592; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 220; AIR 1996 SC 1340; 1996 (2) CCR 43; 1996 (2) Crimes 14; JT 1996 (3) SC 465; 1996(3) SCALE 72; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 785; 1996 (3) Supreme 191

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Military Custody

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Adjournment - Retirement - Military - Dereliction of duty - Limitation - Supplies - Court Martial - Respondent, while working as Major General, Army Ordnance Corps., was in-charge of purchase - Controller General of Defense Accounts in special audit on the local purchases sanctioned by the respondent had found that respondent had derelicted his duty and action under the 1950 Act was initiated against him - Respondent was attached to College of Military Engineering, Pune and was promoted as Major General when action under the 1950 Act was initiated against him - Respondent had filed a W.P. against his retirement proceedings - W.P. was dismissed by the HC - Respondent had been kept under open arrest and was retired from service as Major General - Respondent delayed the proceedings by refusing to cross-examine witness and adjournments - Later, respondent had evaded open arrest when the trial proceedings were to begin - Thereafter, respondent surrendered voluntarily and was placed under closed arrest - The Court-martial had assembled but in the meanwhile the respondent had filed W.P. in the HC challenging the jurisdiction of the Court-martial to try him - Respondent alleged that he had ceased to be a subject of the 1950 Act from his retirement - Thus proceedings against him could not be held by General Court Martial (GCM) as period of six months had lapsed as per s. 123(2) of the 1950 Act - The DB held that trial of the accused had not commenced within six months of his ceasing to be subject to the 1950 Act - Thus the trial by the Court-martial was illegal and accordingly writ was issued in favor of the respondent - Hence, the instant appeal by the GCM authorities - Whether the trial of the respondent had commenced within six months from the date of his retirement - Held, that the trial had commenced the moment GCM had assembled to consider the charge and examine the charges - Preceding preliminary investigation was only part of the process of investigation to find whether a charge could be framed and placed before the competent authority to constitute GCM - Also the DB of the HC had recorded the finding that the respondent had absconded from open military detention - Hence, respondent having escaped from lawful military custody had prevented the trial from being proceeded in accordance with law - Respondent could not be allowed to take advantage of his own wrongdoing and plead bar of limitation to frustrate the lawful trial by a competent GCM - Moreover the trial was not barred by limitation under s. 123(2) of the 1950 Act - The order passed by the DB of the HC was set aside - Appellants were at liberty to secure the presence of the respondent and the respondent was to be kept in detention - The GCM authorities were directed to conduct the trial expeditiously - Appeal allowed.
