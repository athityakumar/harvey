State of Rajasthan v Kishore

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2634; (1996) 8 SCC 217; (1996) SCC (Cr) 646; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 283; AIR 1996 SC 3035; 1996 (20) AllCriR 442; 1996 (2) CCR 1; 1996 (1) Crimes 156; 1996 CRLJ 2003; JT 1996 (2) SC 595; 1996 (4) KarLJ 464; 1996(2) SCALE 462; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 1103; 1996 (2) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Indian Penal code, 1860, s. 302 - Conviction on basis of dying declaration - Declaration found to be coherent, trustworthy and made in fit mental state - Statement therein that husband poured kerosene on wife and burned her - (A) Whether liable to be convicted u/s. 302 IPC? - (B) Whether corroboration necessary? - Held, (A) liable to be convicted - (B) Corroboration not necessary - Appeal allowed.
