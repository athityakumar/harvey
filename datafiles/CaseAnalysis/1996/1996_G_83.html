Government of India v K. V. Swaminathan

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1890; (1997) 10 SCC 190; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 379; JT 1996 (11) SC 612; 1996(8) SCALE 698; 1996 (6) SLR 638; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Respondent claimed benefit as freedom fighter but same remained pending for a long time - Ultimately, granted pension on benefit of doubt - Thereafter, writ petition filed by respondent claiming pension from date of his application - High Court directed to pay pension from date of application - Whether HC rightly directed appellant to pay pension to respondent from date of application? - Held, respondent not entitled to pension as a freedom fighter - But entitled to relief on basis of benefit of doubt - He could be sanctioned pension only from date of order and not from date of application - Appeal allowed.
