New Shorrock Mills v Maheshbhai T. Rao

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1790; (1996) 6 SCC 590; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1484; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 104; AIR 1997 SC 252; 1996 (74) FLR 2749; JT 1996 (9) SC 635; 1997 (1) LLJ 1212; 1997 (1) LLN 69; 1996(7) SCALE 791; 1997 (1) SLJ 125; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 762; 1996 (7) Supreme 734

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 78 - Validity of order of Labour Court setting aside, order of discharge and directing reinstatement - Labour Court coming to conclusion as to department enquiry being legal and proper and order of workman's discharge not being by way of victimisation - He also reached conclusion that workman was guilty of misconduct - Labour Court over - looking fact that workman had misconducted himself prior to incident and was punished and reinstating employee with 40% back wages - Whether order liable to be set aside? - Held, order is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
