Ghantesher Ghosh v Madan Mohan Ghosh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2430; (1996) 11 SCC 446; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 217; AIR 1997 SC 471; 1997 (1) AWC 253; 1997 (1) CalLT(SC) 26; 1996 (4) CLT 98; JT 1996 (8) SC 410; 1996 (2) RentLR 644; 1996(6) SCALE 734; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 448; 1996 (7) Supreme 182

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Partition Act, 1893, s. 4 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 44 and 52 - Benevolent provision - Availability - Whether s. 4 of Act of 1893 available at all relevant stages of litigation between contesting co-owners - Held, s. 4 must be construed as to make it available at all relevant stages of litigation between contesting co-owners, till litigation reaches its terminus by way of full and final discharge and satisfaction of final decree for partition - Available at all relevant stages of litigation - Appeal disposed of.
