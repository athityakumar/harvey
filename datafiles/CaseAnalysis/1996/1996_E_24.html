Messrs Mohan Meakin Limited v Excise and Taxation Commissioner, Himachal Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1978; (1997) 2 SCC 193; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 577; JT 1996 (11) SC 655; 1996(9) SCALE 162; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 258; 1997 (2) Supreme 33

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914,ss. 3, 3(1), 3(6), 3(6-b), 3(12-a), 3(14), 3(16), 3(17), 3(19), 23, 31, 58 and 59 - Punjab Breweries Rules, 1932, r. 10(3 4) - Excise duty - Levy of - Whether levy of excise duty, on beer when it was in process of manufacture is correct ? - Excisable article would mean any alcoholic liquor for human consumption or any intoxicating drug, levy or impost of excise duty would be only on alcoholic liquor for human consumption - Beer would mean fermented liquor from malt, when it is potable or in consumable condition as beverage - Alcoholic or intoxicating liquors must be understood as these are, not what these are capable of or able to become - Held, state of levying excise duty upon alcoholic liquor arises when excisable article is brought to stage of human consumption with requisite alcoholic strength - (B) Whether State Legislature had power to impose excise duty with regard to alcoholic liquor not fit for human consumption? - Power of taxation with regard to alcoholic liquor not fit for human consumption, was within legislative competence of Central legislature - Impost by State was held to be justifiable only if it was a fee thereby impliedly and clearly denying any consideration or price for any privilege - Further Further held, State legislature had no authority to levy duty or tax on alcohol which is not for human consumption as that could only be levied by Centre - Appeal allowed.
