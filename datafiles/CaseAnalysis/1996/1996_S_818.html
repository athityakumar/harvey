Suresh Lohiya v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2332; (1996) 10 SCC 397; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 202; AIR 1996 SCW 4111; 1996 (3) Crimes 149; JT 1996 (8) SC 12; 1996(6) SCALE 117; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 130; 1996 (6) Supreme 273

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Indian Forest Act, 1927, s. 2(4) - Whether order of confiscation of bamboo mat belonging to appellant was in accordance with law? - Where a product from bamboo is commercially different from it and in common parlance taken as a distinct product, same would not be encompassed within expression 'forest-produce' as defined in s. 2(4) of Act, despite it being inclusive in nature - Bamboo mat is taken as a product distinct from bamboo in commercial world - Held, bamboo mat is not a forest-produce in eye of Act - Set aside impugned judgment of High Court - Appeal allowed.
