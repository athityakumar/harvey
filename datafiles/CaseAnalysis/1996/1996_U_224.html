Mediwell Hospital and Health Care Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2044; (1997) 1 SCC 759; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 294; AIR 1997 SC 1623; 1997 (89) E.L.T. 425; JT 1997 (1) SC 270; 1996 (17) RLT 1111; 1996(9) SCALE 368; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 228; 1997 (1) UJ 323

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Levy of customs duty - Notification exempting charitable hospitals to import of hospital equipment - Private individual's diagnostic Centre purely on commercial basis - Several such individual diagnostic centres however, granted exemption by authorities - Denial of exemption to diagnostic centre in question without any justifiable reason - Held, tantamount to discriminatory treatment violative of art. 14 - Order refusing exemption - Liable to be struck down.
