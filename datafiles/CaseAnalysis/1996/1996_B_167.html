Commissioner of Income Tax v Bombay Dyeing and Manufacturing Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3494; (1996) 3 SCC 496; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 418; AIR 1996 SC 3309; 1996 (132) CTR 217; [1996] 219 ITR 521; JT 1996 (6) SC 68; 1996(2) SCALE 812; 1996 (3) Supreme 266; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 396; 1996 (2) TLR 514

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: ThIncome Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 37, 256 - Complimentary business - Scheme of amalgamation - Deduction - Revenue expenditure - Company was amalgamated with assessee/company and expenditure was incurred by assessee company towards professional charges paid to firm of Solicitors and the amount was claimed as revenue expenditure - Assessee's contended that company was engaged in same business as assessee - Scheme of amalgamation was evolved and legal expenses incurred in connection with amalgamation are in nature of revenue expenditure - Income Tax Officer did not agree nor did Appellate Assistant Commissioner - On appeal, Tribunal concluded that "as both companies were carrying on complimentary business and their amalgamation was necessary for smooth and efficient conduct of business for purpose of business of assessee - On further appeal, HC also rejected application u/s.256(2)of 1961 Act - Whether charges paid by assessee company for effecting amalgamation of company was revenue in nature and whether it should be allowed as deduction in computation of its total income - Held, any expenditure laid out for acquiring asset of permanent character would be capital expenditure and acquisition of other company in course of carrying of assessee's business was deductible u/s.10(2)(xv) of Act - Expenditure incurred towards amalgamation was in course of carrying of assessee's business and deductible as revenue expenditure - HC was justified in rejecting application u/s. 256 (2) of 1961 Act - Appeals dismissed.
