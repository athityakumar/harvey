Shangrila Food Products Ltd. And Anr. v Life Insurance Corporation Of Indiaand Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2986; (1996) 5 SCC 54; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 643; AIR 1996 SC 2410; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 404; 1996 (3) CCC 140; JT 1996 (6) SC 522; 1997 (1) RCJ 414; 1996 (2) RCR(Rent) 220; 1996 (2) RentLR 436; 1996(5) SCALE 289; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 279; 1996 (5) Supreme 620; 1996 (2) UJ 599

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act - Powers and exercise of - Estate Officer ordering eviction of tenant and sub-tenant company - Holding it to be unlawful Subtenant - Order for damages - Landlord not challenging reversing Order for damages by writ petition - Sub-tenant challenging order of eviction and damages - High Court directing re-determination of question of damages along with question whether sub-tenant was unlawful - Held, order of HC proper - If there be any unfair advantage gained by any party before invoking jurisdiction of HC, Court can make that party to shed it before granting relief.
