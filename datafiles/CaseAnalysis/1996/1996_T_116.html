Rattan Singh v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4003; (1997) 4 SCC 161; (1997) SCC (Cr) 525; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 628; AIR 1997 SC 768; 1997 (1) CCR 107; 1996 (4) Crimes 282; 1997 CRLJ 833; JT 1996 (11) SC 218; 1996(9) SCALE 258; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 938; 1996 (8) Supreme 785

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 379 and 313 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Appellant intruded into house of deceased and shot her dead - Trial Court acquitted appellant but High Court convicted appellant - Whether HC correct in reversing acquittal order? - Criminal courts should not be fastidious with mere omissions in first information statement, since such statements cannot be expected to be chronicle of every detail of what happened, nor to contain an exhaustive catalogue of the events detail of what happened, nor to contain an exhaustive catalogue of events which took place - It is voluntary narrative of informant without interrogation which usually goes into such statement - So any omission therein has to be considered along with other evidence to determine whether fact so omitted never happened at all - Held, some omissions in FIR does not shake prosecution case - Deceased uttered her cause of death just before she was shot dead and can be termed as her dying declaration - Sessions Court wrongly acquitted appellant - Conviction sustained - Appeal dismissed.
