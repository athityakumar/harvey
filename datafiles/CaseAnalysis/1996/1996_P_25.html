Sohan Lal Passi v P. Sesh Reddy & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Faizan Uddin, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2894; (1996) 5 SCC 21; (1996) SCC (Cr) 871; 1996 (2) ACC 617; AIR 1996 SC 2627; 1996 (3) CCC 130; [1998] 91 Comp Cas 526; JT 1996 (6) SC 728; 1996(5) SCALE 388; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 647; 1996 (5) Supreme 603; 1996 (2) UJ 525

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 96 and 110 - (A) Compensation - Vicarious liability - Owner of vehicles authorising driver having valid licence to drive vehicle - Driver allowing cleaner who was also employee of same owner to drive vehicle though he had no driving licence - Accident - Whether owner can escape liability on the ground that had not authorised particular manner in which act was done - (B) Liability of insurance company - Accident - Owner of vehicle who has insured vehicle, duly appointing licensed driver - Vehicle driven by unauthorised person allowed by licensed driver - Can Insurance company escape liability to third party on ground of contravention of Condition in policy excluding liability in case of vehicle being driven by person not duly licensed - Held, (A) Owner cannot escape liability on that ground - (B) Insurance company cannot escape liability to third party on ground of contravention of Condition in policy - Appeal allowed.
