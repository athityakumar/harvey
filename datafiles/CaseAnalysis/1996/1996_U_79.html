Mahe Beach Trading Company and Etc v Union Territory of Pondicherry and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 702; (1996) 3 SCC 741; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 504; JT 1996 (4) SC 45; 1996(3) SCALE 306; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 1; 1996 (3) Supreme 386

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Pondicherry Administration Act, 1962 - Legislative Powers - Delegated Legislation - Appellants were dealers in petrol and diesel oil in the former French Establishment of Mahe - There was a de facto transfer by the French Govt. of all its French Territories to the Indian Govt. - Municipal Council Mahe decided to levy a Municipal Tax of 5 paisa on each litre of petrol and diesel oil sold at the petrol pump situation Mahe - On a representation by the appellant, the Municipal Council by another Resolution decided to reduce the rate of tax from five paisa to two paisa on each litre of petrol and diesel oil - Appellants filed petitions u/art. 226 of the Constitution challenging the said levy which was allowed - Respondents filed an appeal - During the pendency of the appeal Administrator of Pondicherry passed an ordinance know as validation Act which was given retrospective operation - This was challenged by appellants before the HC by filing a fresh W.P. - By common judgement HC allowed the writ appeals and dismissed the W.P.- Hence these appeals - Whether the levy u/ss. 3 and 4 of the validation Act can said to be repugnant to s. 7 of the 1962 Act - Held, s. 7 of the 1962 Act merely continues the existing taxes - Assuming that there was no valid levy of the tax under the Municipal decree of 1880 a competent Legislature could impose a tax and s.7 of the 1962 Act does not in any way prohibit such imposition - Levy u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Validation Act cannot be said to be repugnant to s. 7 of the 1962 Act - Appeals dismissed.
