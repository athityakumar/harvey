T. P. George v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19764; (2001) 2 SCC 758; AIR 2001 SC 816; 2001 (1) AllCJ 515; 2001 (1) ARBLR 490; 2001 (1) AWC 842; JT 2001 (2) SC 438; 2001 (1) KLT 760; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 40; 2001 (1) RAJ 204; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 749; 2001(1) SCALE 667; 2001 (2) SLT 25; 2001 (1) Supreme 556

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act - Contract for construction - In appeal, before High Court, challenge was restricted to Award of claims u/items 12(i), 12(j) and 12(k) and Award of interest from date of Award - Arbitrator could not overlook terms of contract or agreement - (A) Whether this supplemental Agreement was binding on appellant and/or whether he had been forced to sign Agreement and/or whether appellant was entitled to maintain his claims in view of Supplemental agreement? - Issue have been referred to Arbitrator and view of Arbitrator would be binding if it is one which is possible - Even if High Court had considered these aspects it could not have substituted its views for those of Arbitrator as it could not be said that view taken by Arbitrator is unreasonable or one which cannot be arrived at by a reasonable person - (B) Whether HC was right in setting aside Award of interest from date of Award? - Held, Interest can be awarded at all four stages - Impugned judgment to extent that it disallows interest from date of Award is set aside however, impugned judgment to extent that it upholds Award under Item 12(j) is upheld - Appeal disposed of.
