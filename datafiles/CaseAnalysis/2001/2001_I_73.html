Shri Ram and Another v Ist Additional Distt. Judge and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20299; (2001) 3 SCC 24; AIR 2001 SC 1250; 2001 ALJ 578; 2001 AllCJ 497; 2001 (43) ALR 244; 2001 (1) AWC 862; JT 2001 (2) SC 573; 2001 RD 241; 2001(2) SCALE 45; 2001 (2) SCJ 240; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 984; 2001 (2) SLT 109; 2001 (1) Supreme 706; 2001 (1) UC 464

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 331, Sch. II - Whether a suit laid in Civil Court by a recorded tenure - holder in possession for cancellation of sale deed in respondent's favour executed by some imposters in respect of land is barred u/s. 331 and sch. II? - Where a recorded tenure-holder having a prima facie title and in possession files suit in Civil Court for cancellation of sale deed having been obtained on ground of fraud or impersonation cannot be directed to file a suit for declaration in revenue Court, reason being that in such a case, prima facie, title of recorded tenure-holder is not under cloud, he does not require declaration of his title to land - Position would be different where a person not being a recorded tenure-holder seeks cancellation of sale deed by filing a suit in Civil Court on ground of fraud or impersonation and there, necessarily plaintiff is required to seek a declaration of his title and, therefore, may be directed to approach Revenue Court, as sale deed being void has to be ignored for giving him relief for declaration and possession - Held, appellants are recorded tenure-holders in possession of plot in dispute, therefore, view taken by High Court suffers from serious infirmity - Appeal allowed.
