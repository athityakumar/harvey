Solidaire India Limited v Fairgrowth Financial Services Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Brijesh Kumar, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19970; (2001) 3 SCC 71; AIR 2001 SC 958; 2001 (1) BC 656; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 82; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 569; 2001 (1) CompLJ 400; JT 2001 (2) SC 639; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 198; 2001(2) SCALE 1; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 932; 2001 (2) SLT 81; 2001 (1) Supreme 638

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Land & Property; Securities Exchange Board of India

Summary: Banking & Finance - Securities Exchange Board of India - Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992, ss. 3, 11 and 13 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 22 and 32 - Special Court Trial of Offences Relating To Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act, 1976 - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Loan by appellant - Whether rate of intererst charged by respondent was justified? - Yes, court held, there was no formal agreement which had been entered into between the parties at the time when the loan was advanced also there is no document on record to show that amount of interest claimed was immediately refuted - (B) Whether recovery proceedings under the Special Court Act should be stayed in view of the provisions of the 1985 Act? - Held, there was no intention of the Legislature to permit the 1985 Act to apply notwithstanding the fact that proceedings in respect of a company may be going on before the BIFR and 1992 Act is to have an overriding effect notwithstanding any provision to the contrary in another Act - Appeal dismissed.
