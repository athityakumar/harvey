Ram Gulam Chaudhury and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 228; (2001) 8 SCC 311; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1546; AIR 2001 SC 2842; 2001 (4) CCR 100; 2002 (1) CGLJ 64; 2001 (4) Crimes 16; 2002 (1) CRJ 219; 2001 CRLJ 4632; 2001 (2) JCC 228; JT 2001 (8) SC 110; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 347; 2001(6) SCALE 490; 2001 (3) SCJ 588; 2001 (6) SLT 747; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 279; 2001 (7) Supreme 206; 2001 (2) UC 602

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: When there is direct evidence leading to the inescapable conclusion that the person has died and that the accused are the persons who had committed the murder, then it is not necessary that the corpus delicti be found. Further, when the prosecution has succeeded in proving facts from which a reasonable inference can be drawn regarding death, it is for the Appellants to offer an explanation, which might lead the Court to draw a different inference.
