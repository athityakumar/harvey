Narinderjit Singh Sahni and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19936; (2002) 2 SCC 210; AIR 2001 SC 3810; 2001 (4) CCR 140; 2002 (1) JCC 183; JT 2001 (8) SC 477; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 591; 2001(7) SCALE 189; 2001 (5) SCJ 107; 2001 (7) SLT 292; 2002 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 389; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 114; 2001 (7) Supreme 593

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Prevention of Corruption Act - Numerous cases of cheating against petitioners stated u/s. 138 - Petitioners were favoured with an order of bail in one case but were detained by reason of production warrant in another matter and resultantly petitioners languishing in jails deprived of order of grant of bail - Petitioners invoked art. 32 - Petitioners contended that their release virtually not possible so there has been violation of art. 21 - Petitioners demanded to be release on bail u/s. 438 - High Court stated that bail to petitioner could not be granted - Whether HC right in not granting bail to petitioner? - Persons can be deprived of his personal liberty in accordance with procedure established by law - Virtual impossibility of release in view of pendency of cases in different states - It is in this sphere courts will scrupulously observe as to whether same stands differently and contra as regards procedure established by law and in event it is not so done, it would be a plain exercise of judicial power to grant redress to petitioner - Facts are too insufficient however, to come to a conclusion as regards infraction of art. 21 - Petitioners cannot be said to have been trifled with in absence of due process of law - Deprivation, if any, cannot be claimed to be not in accordance with due process of law - Held, petition u/art. 32 is maintainable but petitioners are not entitled to any relief by reason of insufficiency of available materials on record as regards issue of infraction of art. 21 - Petition dismissed.
