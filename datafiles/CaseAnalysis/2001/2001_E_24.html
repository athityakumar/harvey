Workmen Represented By Akhil Bhartiya Koyla Kamgar Union v Employers In Relation To The Management of Industry Colliery of Bharat Coking Coal Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20227; (2001) 4 SCC 55; (2001) SCC (L&S) 641; AIR 2001 SC 1994; 2001 (2) CLR 1; 2001 (89) FLR 552; JT 2001 (4) SC 327; 2001 (34) LabIC 1397; 2001 (1) LLJ 1400; 2001(2) SCALE 626; 2001 (2) SCJ 543; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 553; 2001 (2) SLR 31; 2001 (3) SLT 257; 2001 (2) Supreme 639; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1380

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-H - Retrenchment - Preference in further employment - Whether appellants are entitled to preference in matter of employment u/s. 25-H of the Act? - Held, no obligation on respondents to give effect to s. 25-H of Act - Govt. or respondent cannot be considered to be successor interest of old business inasmuch as only right, title and interest of coking coal mines have been acquired by Govt. under Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, free from all encumbrances - It is neither liability of owners of said mines nor goodwill thereof has been acquired - As long as both assets and liabilities have not been acquired, it cannot be stated to be a successor-interest in question unless statute provides for same - There is no contract between Govt. and owners providing otherwise and that, therefore, there is no obligation on respondents to give effect to s. 25-H of the Act which merely creates an obligation that a retrenched workman will have preference when fresh appointments are made and an opportunity will have to be given to them to offer themselves for re-employment - Compensation will not be entitled to claim re-employment u/s. 25-H of Act as same would result in double benefit in form of payment of compensation, therefore, fair justice means that such workmen will not be entitled to such conferment of double benefit - Order of High Court deserves to be set aside and award made by Tribunal would have to be restored - Appeal allowed.
