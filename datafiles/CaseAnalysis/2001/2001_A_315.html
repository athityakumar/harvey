Commissioner of Income Tax v Attili N. Rao

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20809; (2003) 9 SCC 658; AIR 2002 SC 388; 2001 (171) CTR 188; [2001] 252 ITR 880; JT 2001 (10) SC 412; 2002 (2) SLT 79; [2001] 119 TAXMAN 1030

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Mortgage - Sale of immovable property - Interest in property - Computation of Capital gain - Assessee carried on Abkari business - In course of financial year 1970-71 assessee mortgaged to State the immovable property belonged to assessee to provide security for amounts of kist due to State - State sold immovable property by public auction - Sum of Rs.5,62,980 was realised at auction - State deducted Rs.1,29,020 due to it towards kist and interest and paid over balance to assessee - Revenue stated that assessee was liable to capital gains tax of Rs.3,70,970 - According to assessee, capital gain was only Rs.85,130 - Neither ITO nor appellate authority agreed with assessee - Tribunal uphold assessee's claim - Question referred before HC that whether Tribunal was correct in holding that amount realised by sale of assessee's interest in property was only Rs.4,33,960 i.e.,Rs. 5, 62, 980 minus Rs.1,29,020 - HC answered against Revenue - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee was vested with full interest in sale of property - Held, interest in property was created in favour of State - When immovable property was sold by public auction, its value had reduced to extent of interest that was created in favour of State by reason of mortgage - Tribunal and HC were erred in its holdings - From out of sale price, State deducted its dues towards 'kist' and interest due from assessee and paid over balance to assessee - Capital gain that assessee made was on immovable property belonged to assessee - It was on full price realised (less admitted deductions) that capital gain and tax thereon had computed - Question answered in negative and in favour of Revenue - Appeal allowed.
