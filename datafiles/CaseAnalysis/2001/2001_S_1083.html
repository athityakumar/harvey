Central Bureau of Investigation Through S. P., Jaipur v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20336; (2001) 3 SCC 333; (2001) SCC (Cr) 524; AIR 2001 SC 668; 2001 (1) CCR 165; 2001 (1) Crimes 191; 2001 CRLJ 968; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 907; 2001 (1) JCC 113; JT 2001 (1) SC 624; 2001 (1) KLT 563; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 574; 2001(1) SCALE 250; 2001 (2) SCJ 79; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 472; 2001 (1) SLT 621; 2001 (1) Supreme 263; 2001 (1) UC 350B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, ss. 5 and 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(o), 36, 155, 156 and 202 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 142(1) and 226 - Whether magistrate has power to direct Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) to conduct investigation into any offence in exercise of his powers u/s. 156(3) of CrPC, 1973? - S. 202 only enables a magistrate to have assistance of an investigation conducted either by police or by any other person, for limited purpose of deciding whether or not there is sufficient ground for proceeding with complaint - What is envisaged in ss. 5 and 6 of Act is not one of conferring power on a magistrate to order CBI to conduct investigation in exercise of s. 156(3) - True, powers of High Court u/art. 226 and of Supreme Court u/arts. 32 or 142(1) of Constitution can be invoked for giving such direction to CBI to investigate in certain cases - Magisterial power cannot be stretched under said sub-section beyond directing officer in charge of a police station to conduct investigation - Impugned orders of magistrates as well as judgments of HC set aside - Appeals disposed of.
