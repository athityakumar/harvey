State of Gujarat and Others v Pratamsingh Narsinh Parmar

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20517; (2001) 9 SCC 713; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 23; 2001 (1) CLR 968; 2001 (2) ESC 294; 2001 (89) FLR 323; JT 2001 (3) SC 326; 2001 (1) LLJ 1118; 2001 (2) LLN 41; 2001 (3) SLT 561; 2001 (4) Supreme 227; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 956

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2j and 25F - Constitution of India, 1950 - Gujarat Non - Secretariat Clerks, Clerks - Typist and Typists (Direct Recruitment Procedure) Rules, 1970 - Whether forest department of a state can be called an industry under Industrial Disputes Act? - If a dispute arises as to whether a particular establishment or part of it wherein an appointment has been made is an industry or not, burden of proof to give positive facts for coming to conclusion that it constitutes 'an industry' is on claimant - In absence of any assertion by petitioner in writ petition indicating nature of duty discharged by petitioner as well as job of establishment where he had been recruited, High Court wholly erred in law - Appeal allowed.
