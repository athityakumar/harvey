State of Tamil Nadu v Kutty Alias Lakshmi Narasimhan

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20742; (2001) 6 SCC 550; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1177; AIR 2001 SC 2778; 2001 (3) CCR 163; 2001 (4) Crimes 114; 2002 (1) CRJ 258; 2001 CRLJ 4168; JT 2001 (6) SC 352; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 812; 2001(5) SCALE 190; 2001 (4) SCJ 369; 2001 (5) SLT 734; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 433; 2001 (6) Supreme 49; 2001 (2) UC 494

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appreciation of evidence - Retracted confession - Whether admissible as evidence? - Held, High Court held the confession is inadmissible on two counts - First, is that confession was retracted by maker thereof and second is that recovery of articles was made prior to confession - It is not law that once a confession was retracted Court should presume that confession is tainted - As a matter of practical knowledge, non-retracted confession is a rarity in criminal cases - To retract from confession is right of confessor and all accused against whom confessions were produced by prosecution have invariably adopted that right - It would be injudicious to jettison a judicial confession on mere premise that its maker has retracted from it - The court has a duty to evaluate evidence concerning confession by looking at all aspects twin test of a confession is to ascertain whether it was voluntary and true - Once those tests are found to be positive next endeavour is to see whether there is any other reason which stands in way of acting on it - Even for that, retraction of confession is not ground to throw confession overboard judicial confession would not become bad by reason of fact that articles belonging to victims were recovered prior to making of confession - That aspect, instead of vitiating confession, could be a factor in favour of voluntariness of confession - When culprit finds that articles concealed by him are all disinterred it is possible that he might feel that there is no use in concealing facts any more - Then culprit may desire to make a clean breast of everything to any person or authorities - HC Order acquitting accused after sidelining judicial confession set aside - Appeal allowed.
