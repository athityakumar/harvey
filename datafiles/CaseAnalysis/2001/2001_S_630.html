SWIL Limited and Others v State of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20032; (2001) 6 SCC 670; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1205; AIR 2001 SC 2747; AIR 2001 SCW 3017; 2001 (3) CCR 177; 2001 (4) Crimes 124; 2002 (1) CRJ 365; 2001 CRLJ 4173; 2001 (2) JCC 101; JT 2001 (6) SC 405; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 826; 2001(5) SCALE 224; 2001 (3) SCJ 537; 2001 (5) SLT 787; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 527; 2001 (6) Supreme 85; 2001 (2) UC 443

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319, 190 and 204 - (A) Applicability of s. 319 CrPC, 1973? - Held, there is no question of referring to provisions of s. 319 CrPC, 1973 - That provision would come into operation in course of any enquiry into or trial of an offence - In present case, neither magistrate was holding enquiry as contemplated u/s. 2(g) CrPC, 1973 nor had trial started - He was exercising his jurisdiction u/s. 190 of taking cognizance of an offence and issuing process - There is no bar u/s. 190 CrPC, 1973 that once process is issued against some accused, on next date, magistrate cannot issue process to some other person against whom there is some material on record, but his name is not included as accused in charge sheet - (B) Applicability of s. 190 CrPC, 1973 - Further Further held, at stage of taking cognizance of offence, provisions of s. 190 CrPC, 1973 would be applicable - As per this provision, magistrate takes cognizance of an offence and not offender - After taking cognizance of offence, magistrate u/s. 204 CrPC, 1973 is empowered to issue process to accused at stage of issuing process and it is for magistrate to decide whether process should be issued against particular person/persons named in charge sheet and also not named therein and for that purpose, he is required to consider FIR and statements recorded by police officer and other documents tendered along with charge - Further, upon receipt of police report u/s. 173(2) CrPC, 1973, magistrate is entitled to take cognizance of an offence u/s. 190(1)(b) even if police report is to effect that no case is made out against accused by ignoring conclusion arrived at by investigating officer and independently applying his mind to facts emerging from investigation by taking into account statement of witnesses examined by police - At this stage, there is no question of application of s. 319 CrPC, 1973 - Appeal allowed.
