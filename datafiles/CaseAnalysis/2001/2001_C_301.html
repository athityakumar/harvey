Aspinwell v Commissioner of Income Tax, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 57; (2001) 7 SCC 525; AIR 2001 SC 3708; AIR 2001 SCW 3444; 2001 (170) CTR 68; 2001 (133) E.L.T. 18; [2001] 251 ITR 323; JT 2001 (7) SC 555; 2001 (3) KLT 410; 2001(6) SCALE 111; 2001 (6) SLT 440; 2002 (125) STC 101; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 559; 2001 (6) Supreme 737

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 32A - Curing coffee - Manufacturing - Investment allowance - A/y 1980-1981 and 1983-1984 - Assessee was engaged in the export of coir products, distribution of insecticides and pesticides, running and managing of estates on service contracts - In addition, the assessee had coffee curing plants and assessee claimed investment allowance u/s. 32A of the Act - Income Tax Officer held that the assessee was not entitled to the investment allowance - On appeal, CIT (Appeals)] held that the assessee was entitled to the investment allowance as provided u/s. 32A of the Act - Tribunal upheld the order of CIT (Appeals) - Appeal was filed before HC - HC held that the assessee was not entitled to the investment allowance u/s. 32A of the Act in respect of the machinery used for curing coffee and its sale - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC was right in coming to the conclusion that the assessee was not involved in any manufacturing or production activity in the process of curing the coffee.

Held, test for determination whether manufacture can be said to have taken place is whether the commodity which is subjected to the process of manufacture can no longer be regarded as the original commodity, but is recognized in the trade as a new and distinct commodity. In the instant case, the assessee after processing the raw berries converts them into coffee beans which is commercially different commodity. The coffee beans have an independent identity distinct from raw material from which it was manufactured. Conversion of the raw berry into coffee beans would be a manufacturing activity. The impugned order/judgment of the HC is set aside and that of the Tribunal is restored. Appeal allowed.
