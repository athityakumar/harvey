Commissioner v Griha Yajamanula Samkhya and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S.P. Bharucha, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20141; (2001) 5 SCC 651; AIR 2001 SC 2046; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 492; 2001(3) SCALE 685; 2001 (3) SCJ 213; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 392; 2001 (3) SLT 858; 2001 (3) Supreme 674

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Government - Andhra Pradesh Municipal Corporations (Second Amendment) Act, 1995, s. 5 - Hyderabad Municipal Act, 1955, s. 3 - Municipal Corporations Rules, rr. 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7 - West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1950, s. 9 - Bombay Rent Act, s. 11 - Andhra Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1955, ss. 104, 110, 199, 212, 213, 214, 220 and 224 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, s. 131 - Madras District Municipalities Act, ss. 82(2) and 127(a) - Calcutta Municipal Corporations Act, 1951, ss. 168(1), 197-289, 207-211, 2(10)(b) and 197(1)(i) - Whether order of High Court holding that determination of fair rent under rent control statute will not be binding on Commr. for purpose of assessment of property tax under Act, is correct? - Held, Act and Rules provide a complete code for assessment of property tax to be levied for buildings and lands within the municipal corporation - There is no provision in statute that fair rent determined under Rent Control Act in respect of a property is binding on Commr. - Legislature has wisely not made such a provision because determination of annual rental value under Act depends on several criteria - Criteria for such determination provided under Act may not be similar to those prescribed under Rent Control Act - Further time when such determination was made is also a relevant factor - If in a particular case Commr. finds that there has been a recent determination of fair rent of property by authority under Rent Control Act he may be persuaded to accept amount as basis of determining annual rental value of property - But that is not to say that Commr. is mandatorily required to follow fair rent fixed by authority under Rent Control Act - HC therefore did not commit any error in holding that determination of fair rent under rent control statute will not be binding on Commr. for purpose of assessment of property tax under Act - State Govt. constituted Committee under an executive order - Committee set up by State Govt. has no statutory existence - Its recommendations are advisory and are not binding on Commissioner - In this regard also HC cannot be said to have committed any illegality in holding that powers of Commr. are not fettered by recommendation of Committee - Appeal partly allowed.
