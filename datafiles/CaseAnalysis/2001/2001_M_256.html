State of Bihar and Another v Md. Khalique and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19920; (2002) SCC (Cr) 228; (2002) 1 SCC 652; 2002 (1) CHN 28; 2002 (1) Crimes 162; 2002 (2) CRJ 595B; 2002 CRLJ 553; JT 2001 (10) SC 67; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 103; 2001(8) SCALE 290; 2001 (8) SLT 201; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 355; 2001 (8) Supreme 418; 2002 (1) UC 166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1955, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of Proceedings - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 419, 420, 467, 120-B, 155(2) and 156(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether it justified on part of High Court to use its inherent powers and quash entire proceedings in matter? - In FIR there were categorical allegations of forgery of assessment order of compensation payable under Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1955 and also allegations of fraud played while verifying interest of Zamindari - Relied on Bhajan Lal case [1990 Indlaw SC 91] in which it was held that power of quashing a criminal proceeding should be exercised sparingly and with circumspection and that too in rarest of rare cases - Present case is not rarest of rare case - Held, in view of settled legal position and as offences have been disclosed in FIR, HC ought not have interfered with investigation and should have permitted police to complete it - HC committed a grave error in quashing entire proceedings - Appeal allowed.
