State of Uttar Pradesh and another v Jhinkoo Nai and another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20744; (2001) 6 SCC 503; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1172; AIR 2001 SC 2815; 2001 (3) CCR 159; 2001 (4) Crimes 110; 2002 (1) CRJ 360; 2001 CRLJ 3965; 2001 (2) JCC 114; JT 2001 (6) SC 290; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 755; 2001(5) SCALE 19; 2001 (3) SCJ 380; 2001 (5) SLT 611; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 266; 2001 (6) Supreme 25; 2001 (2) UC 440

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 307 and 324 - Murder - Respondent acquitted by High Court - Whether justified? - Common intent instantly formed, triggering a plurality of persons into an adventure in criminality, some hitting, some missing, some splitting hostile heads, some spilling drops of blood - Guilt goes with community of intent coupled with participatory, presence or operation - Applying aforesaid principles to facts of present case and finding out what reasonable inference could be drawn by a prudent man - Accused were armed with knives, they entered house of complainant dead at night, may be to take revenge of quarrel which had taken place at day time, on a refusal to submit and satisfy their sexual lust despite resistance by girl lifted her brought her in Osara - At that stage when deceased was crying and resisting, her father P.W. 4 and her mother P.W. 5 got up and started raising shouts - One of accused gave knife blow to deceased and two others gave knife blows to P.W. 4 and P.W. 5 - From these facts, only reasonable inference which could be drawn is that common intention of accused was to ravish young girl and in case of resistance to commit murder by inflicting knife blows - Held, in this view of matter, HC materially erred in altering sentence of accused from offence punishable u/s. 302/34, IPC, 1860 to s. 324, IPC, 1860 - Impugned order of HC cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
