E. S. P. Rajaram and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20411; (2001) 2 SCC 186; (2001) SCC (L&S) 352; AIR 2001 SC 581; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 37; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 430; JT 2001 (1) SC 573; 2001 LabIC 517; 2001 (1) LLN 860; 2001(1) SCALE 146; 2001 (2) SCJ 458; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 203; 2001 (2) SLJ 299; 2001 (1) SLT 565; 2001 (1) Supreme 169; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 730

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Whether observations and directions given by this Court in M Bhaskar case (1996 Indlaw SC 2872) particularly in para 18 thereof, are unsustainable since it was passed without giving any notice to appellants and/or other similarly placed employees who were seriously prejudiced by such directions? - Held, decision in that case has been taken on a detailed analysis of relevant provisions of Indian Railway Establishment Code and Indian Railway Establishment Manual (1968 Edn), and in light of certain general principles of law relating to recruitment, cogent reasons have been given in support of findings and conclusions arrived at in judgment - As noted earlier no contention was advanced before court pointing out any serious error in decision therein - Court satisfied that in facts and circumstances of case placed before their Lordships decision is correct and warrants no interference - Appeal dismissed.
