Lokeman Shah and Another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20874; (2001) 5 SCC 235; (2001) SCC (Cr) 829; AIR 2001 SC 1760; AIR 2001 SCW 1646; 2001 (2) CCR 158; 2002 (1) CRJ 570; 2001 CRLJ 2196; JT 2001 (4) SC 520; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 484; 2001(3) SCALE 297; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1095; 2001 (3) SLT 564; 2001 (3) Supreme 305; 2001 (2) UC 33B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act 1872, s. 157 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 142, 149 and 302 - Communal riot - Murder - Convicted and sentenced to death by Trial Court - On a retrial as ordered by High Court, Sessions Court confined conviction to appellants only and awarded sentence of death to both of them - Division Bench of HC confirmed conviction of both, uphold death penalty for one appellant and altered sentence passed on another appellant from death to life imprisonment - Appeal by special leave - Held, appellant 'N' was one among assailants, witness ascribed a specific serious role to that accused - Evidence secured corroboration to proved that the murder Deputy Commr. of Police - No reason to dissent from findings of Trial Court in so far as involvement of appellant 'N' in murder of Deputy Commr. of Police - Further held, appellant 'L' joined unlawful assembly knowing fully well that it had already become unlawful as its common object was to chase persons whom rioters believed to be responsible for defilement of mosque - In all such broad circumstances it would be insane to presume that common object of those chasers was something less than finishing prey whom they were chasing after - Conviction confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
