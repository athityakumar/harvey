Pankajbhai Nagjibhai Patel v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19974; (2001) SCC (Cr) 369; (2001) 2 SCC 595; AIR 2001 SC 567; 2001 (1) AWC 813; 2005 (3) BC 435; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 31; 2001 (1) CCR 106; 2001 (1) CGLJ 497; 2001 (2) CLC 185; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 418; 2001 (1) Crimes 165; 2001 CRLJ 950; 2001 (1) CTC 368; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1558; 2001 (1) JCC 82; JT 2001 (1) SC 523; 2001 (1) KLT 517; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 343; 2001(1) SCALE 102; 2001 (1) SCJ 612; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 337; 2001 (1) SLT 391; 2001 (1) Supreme 124; 2001 (1) UC 198

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 142 and 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 29, 5, 325 and 357 - Judicial magistrate of first class imposed sentence of fine beyond Rs. 5000 in view of s. 29(2) CrPC, 1973 - Challenged - Non obstante limb provided in s. 142 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 is not intended to expand powers of a magistrate of first class beyond what is fixed in Chap. III of code - S. 29, which falls within Chap. III of code, contains a limit for a magistrate of first class in matter of imposing a sentence i.e.,if sentence is imprisonment it shall not exceed three years and if sentence is fine (even if it is part of sentence) it shall not exceed Rs. 5, 000 - Whenever a magistrate of first class feels that complainant should be compensated he can, after imposing a term of imprisonment, award compensation to complainant for which no limit is prescribed in s. 357 - Held, sentence of six months retained and sentence in terms of fine deleted - Appeal disposed of.
