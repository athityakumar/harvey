Hon'Ble Chief Justice High Court of Bombay and Others v B. S. Nayak and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20423; (2001) 9 SCC 763; 2001 (3) AWC 2321; 2001 (3) CLR 222; 2001 (3) ESC 507; 2001 (90) FLR 864; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 341; JT 2001 (5) SC 561; 2001 (4) LLN 176; 2001 (4) SLR 31; 2001 (5) Supreme 397

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Merit-cum-seniority - Leading Chief Justice fixed the principle for filling up the post of the private secretaries to the Hon'ble Judges of High Court and directed that the criteria for promotion shall be merit-cum-seniority to be adjudged with reference to confidential records and dictation-typing and interview tests - (A) Whether court was not justified in deviating from the said norms of seniority for promotion? - Held, appointments to the posts of private secretaries in the HCs could be made by leading Chief Justice as indicated in art. 229 - In the absence of any rules framed by the court or leading Chief Justice, such direction operates in the fields of appointment - Thus, Chief Justice was well within his jurisdiction in deciding the norms of merit-cum-seniority for filling up the posts of private secretaries - (B) Whether the criteria in question not having been duly notified or wide publicity to the same not having been given? - Held, question of giving publicity to the criteria would not arise as leading Chief Justice has formulated the criteria for filling up the posts of private secretaries which he thought appropriate for efficient administration and for efficient discharge of the duties of the Hon'ble Judges - (C) Whether direction passed by leading Chief Justice of HC is sustainable in law with regard to the appointment of private sceretaries to Hon'ble Judges of HC? - Held, it cannot be disputed that the private secretaries to the Hon'ble Judges play an important role in taking down dictations and writing judgment and, if merit is not given its due consideration and appointments are made on the basis of seniority, then it would be difficult for any judge to discharge his obligations - Therefore, court find the impugned direction of the division bench of HC to be wholly unsustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
