State of Maharashtra v Indian Medical Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19912; (2002) 1 SCC 589; AIR 2002 SC 302; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 91; 2002 (4) Bom.C.R. 351; JT 2001 (10) SC 294; 2001(8) SCALE 399; 2002 (1) SCJ 104; 2002 (1) SLR 552; 2001 (8) SLT 309; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 474; 2001 (8) Supreme 59

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Backward Classes

Summary: Education - Maharashtra University of Health Sciences Act, 1998, s. 64 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 10 A,10 A(3),10 A(4),10 B,10 C and 33 - Establishment of New Medical Colleges Opening of Higher Courses of Study and Increase of Admission Capacity in Medical Colleges Regulation, 1993 - Establishment of Medical College - (A) Whether State Govt. required to submit application to Maharashtra University of Health Sciences u/s. 64 of Act, 1998 for obtaining permission from itself, when it decides to establish a Govt - run medical college within State? - Held, when State Govt. resolves to set up a medical college at a proposed location, it is not required to submit any application to itself for permission to establish a Govt - run medical college through University u/s. 64 - (B) Whether perspective plan prepared by University under Act for educational development for location of higher learning is binding on State Govt. when State Govt. resolves to set up a Govt - run medical college within State - Further held, any perspective plan prepared by University is not strictly binding on State Govt. when it resolves to set up a Govt. medical college at a proposed location in view of fact that State is not 'management' u/s. 64 of Act - Appeal allowed.
