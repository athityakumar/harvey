Assam Company Limited and Another v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 231; (2001) 4 SCC 202; AIR 2001 SC 1485; AIR 2001 SCW 1325; 2001 (166) CTR 489; [2001] 248 ITR 567; JT 2001 (4) SC 75; 2001(2) SCALE 613; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 515; 2001 (3) SLT 328; 2001 (2) Supreme 602

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assam Agricultural Income Tax Rules, 1939, r. 5 - Income Tax Rules, 1962, r. 8 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(1 A) and 2(a)(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246(3) and 366(1) - Assessee-appellants approached High Court to recompute agricultural income already assessed by Central Officers under Central Act and for consequential reliefs - HC rejected claims of HC - Whether HC right to hold that power of recomputation can be exercised by State officers concerned only if he came to conclusion that computation of agricultural income made by Central Officer is contrary to provisions of Central Act and Rules? - It is well established rule of interpretation that while interpreting a particular provision of a statute, courts should bear in mind the object and scheme of the entire Act - A particular provision of the Act cannot be considered or interpreted in isolation so as to give room for conflict inter se between the provisions of the same Act - Courts should also bear in mind that while interpreting a provision of the Act an interpretation leading to the provision becoming ultra vires should be avoided - Held, computation of agricultural income even for purpose of State enactments will have to be that which is made under provisions of IT Act and Rules made thereunder - State legislature intended to adopt computation of agricultural income made under provisions of Central Act - Proviso to r. 5 of State Rules to extent it empowers State officers to recompute agricultural income already computed by Central officers is ultra vires of State Act - Proviso to r. 5 of Assam Agrl IT Rules, 1939, to extent it permits recomputation of agricultural income by State officers is declared as ultra vires - Appeals allowed.
