Commandant, 20Th Battalion, Itb Police v Sanjay Binjola

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20143; (2001) 5 SCC 317; (2001) SCC (Cr) 897; AIR 2001 SC 2058; 2001 (2) CCR 240; 2001 (2) CGLJ 202; 2002 (1) CRJ 740; 2001 CRLJ 2349; 2001 (2) JCC 21; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 400; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 798; 2001(3) SCALE 637; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 367; 2001 (5) SLR 457; 2001 (4) SLT 28; 2001 (3) Supreme 669; 2001 (2) UC 109B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Service - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, ss. 10(n)and 16(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 250 and 386(e) - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 3, 4 and 12 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304-A - Respondent, Constable of Indo - Tibetan Border Police, under influence of liquor entered house of his senior and thus committed a offence punishable u/s. 16(2) of Force Act - Convicted u/s. 10(n) of Force Act - On appeal, Sessions Judge modified sentence and directed that 'this order shall not adversely affect service career of accused - (A) Whether criminal court has jurisdiction to issue directions relating to service career other than conviction and sentence? - Held, no jurisdiction upon court to pass orders which tend to interfere with service career of convict - After passing order of conviction and sentence, criminal court should not have issued any direction relating to service career of respondent which is governed by Act, rules made thereunder and service rules governing his conditions of service - (B) Whether such an order can be passed under Probation of Offenders Act? - Further held, Act has been enacted in view of increasing emphasis on reformation and rehabilitation of offenders as useful and self - reliant members of society without subjecting them to deleterious effects of jail life - Nobody can claim benefit of Act as a matter of right and court has to pass appropriate orders in facts and circumstances of each case having regard to nature of offence, its general effect on society and character of offender - In an offence of a trivial nature as respondent is stated to have committed and keeping in view its peculiar circumstances, court find it to be a fit case where powers under Act can be exercised - Appeal allowed.
