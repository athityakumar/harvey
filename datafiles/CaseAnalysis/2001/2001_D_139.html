Suresh Chandra Poddar v Dhani Ram and others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 182; (2002) SCC (L&S) 214; (2002) 1 SCC 766; AIR 2002 SC 439; 2002 (1) AllCriR 379; 2002 (1) AWC 377; 2002 (1) CHN 84; 2002 (1) CLR 1; 2002 (95) DLT 9; 2002 (100) FJR 113; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 308; JT 2001 (10) SC 365; 2002 (1) LLJ 822; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 97; 2001(8) SCALE 452; 2002 (2) SLJ 418; 2002 (1) SLR 581; 2001 (8) SLT 376; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 141; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 498; 2001 (8) Supreme 615; 2002 (1) UC 363

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971-Sections 12 and 13-Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985-Section 17-Contempt of Court/Tribunal-Duty to exercise contempt power-Courts should not feel unduly touchy-Contempt power to be exercised sparingly and not casually and in very deserving cases-Order of Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) for fixing seniority of employees-No time limit fixed by CAT for implementation of order-Said order challenged in High Court in writ petition-No stay granted by High Court-During pendency of writ petition order of CAT implemented-Whether CAT justified in punishing appellant for contempt despite implementation of order and despite tendering apology?-Held, “no”-Order of CAT set aside.
