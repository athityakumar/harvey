T. T. Antony and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20882; (2001) 6 SCC 181; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1048; AIR 2001 SC 2637; 2001 (2) CGLJ 260; 2001 (3) Crimes 276; 2001 CRLJ 3329; 2001 (2) JCC 282; JT 2001 (5) SC 440; 2001 (3) KLT 1; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 436; 2001(4) SCALE 348; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 942; 2001 (5) SLT 211; 2001 (5) Supreme 131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Antiquities and Art Treasures Act, 1972, P.D.P.P. Act

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 129, 132, 154, 156, 157, 162, 169, 170, 173, 227 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 76, 79, 143, 147, 148, 149, 201, 302, 307, 324, 332, 353 and 427 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 5 - P.D.P.P. Act, s. 3(2)(e) - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952, ss. 3(I) and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 21, 226 and 227 - (A) Whether registration of a fresh case, which is in nature of second FIR is valid and can it form basis of a fresh investigation? - Held, FIR is earliest and first information of a cognizable offence recorded by an officer in charge of a police station - Officer in charge of a police station has to commence investigation on basis of entry of First Information Report, on coming to know of commission of a cognizable offence - On completion of investigation and on basis of evidence collected he has to form opinion and forward his report to concerned Magistrate - However, even after filing such a report if he comes into possession of further information or material, he need not register a fresh FIR, he is empowered to make further investigation, normally with leave of court, and where during further investigation he collects further evidence, oral or documentary, he is obliged to forward same with one or more further reports - Thus, there can be no second FIR and consequently there can be no fresh investigation on receipt of every subsequent information in respect of same cognizable offence or same occurrence or incident giving rise to one or more cognizable offences - It follows that if gravamen of charges in two FIRs, the first and second, is in substance and truth same, registering second FIR and making fresh investigation and forwarding report will be irregular and court cannot take cognizance of same - (B) Whether there is any effect of report of Commission of Inquiry during investigation by police? - Further held, report and findings of Commission of Inquiry are meant for information of Govt. - Acceptance of report of Commission by Govt. would only suggest that being bound by rule of law and having duty to act fairly, it has endorsed to act upon it - Duty of police investigating agency of state is to act in accordance with law of land - Investigating agency may with advantage make use of report of Commission in its onerous task of investigation bearing in mind that it does not preclude investigating agency from forming a different opinion if evidence obtained by it supports such a conclusion - Appeal allowed.
