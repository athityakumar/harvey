Cipla Limited v Maharashtra General Kamgar Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20503; (2001) 3 SCC 101; (2001) SCC (L&S) 520; AIR 2001 SC 1165; 2001 (1) CLR 754; 2001 (89) FLR 163; JT 2001 (3) SC 49; 2001 LabIC 1108; 2001 (1) LLJ 1063; 2001(2) SCALE 152; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1; 2001 (5) SLR 1; 2001 (2) SLT 319; 2001 (2) Supreme 112

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Health & Drug; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946 - Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948 - Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1971, s. 32 - Maharashtra Recognition Of Trade Unions And Prevention Of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, s. 28 - Payment Of Wages Act, 1936 - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Unfair labour practices - Whether labour court or industrial tribunal can examine complaints, only in clear cases or undisputed cases under Act? - Even in respect of claims arising u/s. 33 C(2) appropriate dispute can be raised in terms of s. 10 of Industrial Disputes Act - Dispute can be settled where it is clear that workmen are working under a contract - Held, exercise can be done by industrial tribunal under Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 or under Industrial Disputes Act - Appeal allowed.
