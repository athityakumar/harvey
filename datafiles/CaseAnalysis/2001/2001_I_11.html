Mehsana District Central Co-Operative Bank Limited v Income Tax Officer, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20761; (2009) 17 SCC 621; 2001 (170) CTR 169; [2001] 251 ITR 522; [2001] 119 TAXMAN 785

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Banking and Finance - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80P(2)(a)(i) - Gujarat Co-operative Societies Act, 1961, s. 67(2) - Bank Regulation Act, 1949, s. 6(1)(a) - (A) Whether tribunal was right in law in disallowing claim of assessee-bank for deduction under s. 80P(2)(a)(i) in respect of income earned from utilization of its reserve funds being statutory reserves u/s. 67(2) of Gujarat Co-operative Societies Act, 1961? - Held, question is covered against revenue by judgment of Supreme Court in CIT vs. Karnataka State Co-operative Apex Bank 2001 Indlaw SC 282 - (B) Whether tribunal was right in law in holding that locker rent is not deductible u/s. 80P(2)(a)(i) of Act, 1961? - Held, provision of safe deposit vaults is part of ordinary banking business of a bank; this is shown by s. 6(1)(a) of Bank Regulation Act, 1949 - Therefore, income derived by assessee from hiring out of safe deposit vaults is income from business of banking and, therefore deductible u/s. 80P(2)(a)(i) - (C) Whether assessee bank is entitled to claim deduction u/s. 80 P(2)(a)(i) in respect of income earned from utilization of its voluntary reserves other than statutory reserves? - Held, to be able to answer question, it is necessary to ascertain, as a fact, whether income derived by assessee from investment of its voluntary reserves has been utilised by it in course of its ordinary banking business - In interests of justice that assessee should have opportunity to lead evidence before CIT(A) to establish as a fact what is stated above - Therefore matter stands restored to CIT(A) for being decided afresh - Order accordingly.
