K. P. Madhusudhanan v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Brijesh Kumar, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 281; (2001) 6 SCC 665; AIR 2001 SC 2704; 2001 (169) CTR 489; [2001] 251 ITR 99; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 235; JT 2001 (7) SC 7; 2001(5) SCALE 385; 2001 (6) SLT 27; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 1; 2001 (6) Supreme 274; [2001] 118 TAXMAN 324

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 271(1)(c) - Whether penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) can be levied when assessee had himself disclosed income to assessing officer which was added as concealed income? - Application of Explnation 1(B) of section under reference - Express invocation of Explanation in notice u/s. 271(1)(c) referred is not necessary to apply provisions therein - Held, penalty was validly levied - Appeal dismissed.
