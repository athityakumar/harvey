Jayalakshmi Coelho v Oswald Joseph Coelho

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20250; (2001) 4 SCC 181; AIR 2001 SC 1084; 2001 (43) ALR 168; 2001 (2) CGLJ 129; JT 2001 (3) SC 356; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 33; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 515; 2001(2) SCALE 277; 2001 (2) SCJ 174; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 207; 2001 (2) SLT 351; 2001 (2) Supreme 209; 2001 (1) UC 620

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 152 - Divorce decree by mutual consent granted but order remained silent on other reliefs mentioned in agreement - Family Court, later High Court passed an order to amend decree on application moved by respondent/ husband - Order of HC allowing amendment challenged - There would hardly be any doubt about the proposition that in terms of s. 152 CPC, 1908 any error occurred in the decree on account of arithmetical or clerical error or accidental slip may be rectified by the court - The principle behind the provision is that no party should suffer due to mistake of the court and whatever is intended by the court while passing the order or decree must be properly reflected therein, otherwise it would only be destructive to the principle of advancing the cause of justice - Held, application for rectification of decree was totally misconceived and was only liable to be dismissed rather to incorporate terms and conditions of agreement in respect of which no prayer was made in application for modification nor in original petition for dissolution of marriage, more particularly when no accidental slip on part of Court was indicated in application nor same being substantiated - Appeal allowed.
