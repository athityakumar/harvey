Chandra Kanta Sinha v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20110; (2001) 6 SCC 158; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 351; 2001(4) SCALE 162; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 759; 2001 (4) SLT 423; 2001 (4) Supreme 302

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Code of Civil Procdure, 1908, ss. 96 and 100 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Govt. of India Act - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888 - Trade Marks Act, 1940 - Whether Letters Patent Appeal No. 599 of 1998 filed against order of a learned single judge of Patna High Court passed in MA No. 494 of 1996 D/- 13-04-1998 is maintainable? - Held, for purposes of appeal, u/cl. 10 of letters patent, Patna, judgments of one judge of HC of Patna are classified in two groups - In first group fall judgments from which appeal will lie to said HC - Second group takes in judgments of one judge passed in second appeal where judge who passed judgment declares that case is a fit one for appeal - From a judgment passed by one judge in second appeal, u/s. 100, CPC, 1908 or any other provision of a special Act, no letters patent appeal will lie to HC provided second appeal was against a decree or order of a district judge or a subordinate judge or any other judge subject to superintendence of HC passed in a first appeal u/s. 96, CPC or any other provision of a special Act - Thus, appeal against order of learned single judge in MA No. 494 of 1996 D/- 13-04-1998 would lie before division bench u/cl. 10 of letters patent - Appeal allowed.
