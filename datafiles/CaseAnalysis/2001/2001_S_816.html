Barat Fritz Werner Limited and Others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20512; (2001) 4 SCC 498; (2001) SCC (L&S) 752; AIR 2001 SC 1257; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 69B; 2001 (1) CLR 812; 2001 (89) FLR 1; JT 2001 (2) SC 376; 2001 LabIC 1073; 2001 (1) LLJ 763; 2001 (2) LLN 45; 2001(1) SCALE 614; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 835; 2001 (1) SLT 818; 2001 (2) Supreme 162; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 785

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, ss. 10, 10(2) and 10(2)(a) - Factories Act, 1948, s. 46 - Validity of notifications under Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Notification was issued by Govt. of Karnataka u/s. 10 of Act prohibiting w.e.f date of publication of notifications employment of contract labour in industrial canteens and factories employing 250 workers or above in State of Karnataka - (A) Expression-process, operation or other work - Interpretation of - Held, s. 10 of Act provides for prohibition of employment of contract labour in any process, operation or other work in an establishment - Words-process, operation or other work need not be interpreted to mean only core activity and not peripheral activity - In sub-s. (2) of s. 10 of Act certain guidelines have been provided for Govt. before issue of any notification to find out whether process, operation or other work is incidental or necessary for industry, trade, business, manufacture or occupation that is carried on in establishment - Expression used therein is wide in ambit to cover other activity arising in industry and not merely actual manufacture - Otherwise to understand expression-process, operation or other work, other than meaning given in cl. (a) of sub-s. (2) of s. 10 would be to narrow down meaning thereto - (B) Consultation by Govt. of Karnataka before issue of notification - Whether mandatory? - Held, what is required to be done by Govt. in this regard is to consult Board and it does not mean that Govt. is bound by advice given by Advisory Board - All that is required is that Govt. should consult Advisory Board which consisted of representations from different sections such as Govt. nominees, management, employees and contractor who have establishments apart from effectively ascertaining information from various sources - Based upon such report and all factors available in State as per information furnished to them from different sources, Govt. had reached conclusion it did - (C) Whether petitioner being sick industry, requires a different kind of treatment at hands of Govt.? - Held, when as a matter of policy Govt. adopted that in respect of industry where there is at least 250 workmen and a canteen had been provided in terms of Factories Act and Rules framed thereunder to abolish contract labour pursuant to which action has now been taken, petitioner cannot stand on a different footing merely because it has become sick - Challenge to notification fails - Petition dismissed.
