Centre For Enquiry Into Health and Allied Themes (Cehat) and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20134; (2001) 5 SCC 577; AIR 2001 SC 2007; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 347; 2001(4) SCALE 53; 2001 (4) SCJ 155; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 534; 2001 (4) SLT 186; 2001 (4) Supreme 128B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Prenatal Diagnostic Techniques (Regulation and Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1994, ss. 3, 16(iii), 22 and 17(5) - Practice of female infanticide - Developed medical science is misused to get rid of a girl child before birth - Knowing full well that it is immoral and unethical as well as it may amount to an offence, foetus of a girl child is aborted by qualified and unqualified doctors or compounders - This has affected overall sex ratio in various states where female infanticide is prevailing without any hindrance - For controlling situation, Parliament in its wisdom enacted Prenatal Diagnostic Techniques (Regulation and Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1994 - Neither state Govts. nor Central Govt. has taken appropriate action for its implementation - Directions issued on basis of various provisions for proper implementation of Prenatal Diagnostic Techniques (Regulation and Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1994 - Order accordingly.
