Union of India and Others v M. Aslam and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20535; (2001) SCC (L&S) 302; (2001) 1 SCC 720; AIR 2001 SC 526; 2001 (1) CLR 711; 2001 (88) FLR 759; JT 2001 (1) SC 278; 2001 (34) LabIC 488; 2001 (2) LLN 95; 2001(1) SCALE 48; 2001 (1) SCJ 19; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 62; 2001 (2) SLJ 287; 2001 (3) SLR 414; 2001 (1) SLT 293; 2001 (1) Supreme 11; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 620

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Status of Unit-Run-Canteens employees - Whether employees serving in Unit-Run-Canteens can be held to be Govt. servants? - Held, employees working in Unit-Run-Canteens are Govt. servants, as they are providing canteen facilities to Defence Service personnel which is obligatory on part of Govt. - Unit-Run-Canteens discharge duty of retail outlets after getting their provision from wholesale outlet or depot of Canteen Stores Department - Canteen Stores Department forms a part of Ministry - Employees are recruited, controlled and supervised by Rules and Regulations made by Defence Services although these have been given name of executive instructions - Thus Tribunal would have jurisdiction to entertain applications by Unit-Run-Canteen employees - Further, conclusion that Unit-Run-Canteen employees are Govt. Servants, that by itself ipso facto would not entitle them to get all service benefits as is available to regular Govt. Servant or even their counter-parts serving in CSD Canteens - It would necessarily depend upon nature of duty discharged by them as well as on Rules and Regulations and Administrative Instructions issued by employer - It would be open for employer to frame separate conditions of service of employees or to adopt Fundamental Rules - Appeals disposed of
