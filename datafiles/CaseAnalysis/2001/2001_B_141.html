Rajendra Tiwary v Basudeo Prasad and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20381; (2002) 1 SCC 90; AIR 2002 SC 136; 2002 (46) ALR 222; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2001 (9) SC 510; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 190; 2001(8) SCALE 167; 2001 (8) SLT 24; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 243; 2001 (8) Supreme 243; 2002 (1) UC 142

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 7 - Bihar Building (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1982, s. 11 - Whether High Court is right in law in holding that an equitable decree for eviction of defendant can be passed u/O. 7, r. 7 of CPC, 1908 and remanding case to first appellate court for recording its finding on question of title of parties to suit premises, and for passing an equitable decree of eviction against defendant, if plaintiffs were found to have title thereto? - A court of Rent Controller having limited jurisdiction to try suits on grounds specified in special Act, obviously does not have jurisdiction of ordinary civil court and therefore, cannot pass a decree for eviction of defendant on a ground other than one specified in Act - Held, inquiry into title of plaintiffs is beyond scope of court exercising jurisdiction under Act - Appeal allowed.
