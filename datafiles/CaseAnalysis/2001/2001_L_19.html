Smt. Vinitha Ashok v Lakshmi Hospital and others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 211; (2001) 8 SCC 731; AIR 2001 SC 3914; 2001 (45) ALR 405; 2001 (4) AWC 3093; 2003 CCC 664; 2002 (1) CPJ 4; 2002 (1) CPJ(Sc) 4; 2002 (1) CPR 25; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 333; JT 2001 (8) SC 142; 2001 (3) KLT 606; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 137; 2001(6) SCALE 476; 2001 (3) SCJ 632; 2001 (6) SLT 735; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 12; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 292; 2001 (7) Supreme 225

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Family & Personal; Health & Drug; Women & Children

Summary: A doctor will not be guilty of negligence, if he has acted in accordance with the practice accepted as proper by a responsible body of medical men skilled in that particular art, and if, he has acted in accordance with such practice, merely because there is a body of opinion that takes a contrary view, will not make him liable for negligence.
