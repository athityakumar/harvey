M. S. Grewal and Another v Deep Chand Sood and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19842; (2001) 8 SCC 151; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1426; 2001 (2) ACC 540; 2001 (3) ACJ 1719; AIR 2001 SC 3660; 2001 (45) ALR 192; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 100; JT 2001 (7) SC 159; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 214; 2001(5) SCALE 610; 2001 (4) SCJ 402; 2001 (6) SLT 275; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 156; 2001 (6) Supreme 655

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Course Of Employment, Measure Of Damages, Prohibition Act

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Prohibition Act - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - School concerned has in its activities, a usual picnic for all students in batches - Children were allowed to play in danger zone of water without any caution or any warning being sounded, resultant effect of which was drowning of these unfortunate fourteen children - High Court ordered that chairman and management of school shall pay a compensation of Rs. 5 lakhs to each of parents of fourteen students - (A) Whether assessment of compensation must also have a correlation with ability or capability to pay? - Held, though mathematical nicety is not required but a rough and ready estimate can be had from records claiming damages since award of damages cannot be had without any material evidence - Whereas one party is to be compensated, other party is to compensate and as such there must always be some materials available - It is not a fanciful item of compensation but it is on legitimate expectation of loss of pecuniary benefits - Quantum of assessment stands dependent on fact situation of matter before court, than judicial precedents - School as of date stands out to be one of most affluent schools in country, as such ability to pay cannot be termed to be an issue in matter and in wake thereto we are not inclined to deal with same in any further detail - (B) Whether school cannot be made liable under any stretch of imagination by reason of happening of an event which is not within school premises and has, in fact, happened by reason of neglect of two of teachers? - Further held, to escort children was duty assigned to two teachers and till such time thus period of escorting stands over, one cannot but ascribe it to be in course of employment - Safety of children obviously was of prime concern so far as school authorities are concerned and till such time children returned to school, safe and secure after picnic, course of employment, continued and thus resultantly, liability of school - Liability of school, in contextual facts cannot be shifted for any reason whatsoever by reason of factum of teachers being within course of employment of School at time of tragedy - Appeal disposed of.
