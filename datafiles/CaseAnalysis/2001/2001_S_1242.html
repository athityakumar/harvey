Park Leather Industry Private Limited and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20637; (2001) 3 SCC 135; AIR 2001 SC 931; 2001 ALJ 498; 2001 (1) AWC 868; JT 2001 (2) SC 577; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 138; 2001 RD 252; 2001(2) SCALE 49; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1035; 2001 (2) SLT 116; 2001 (122) STC 82; 2001 (2) Supreme 1; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 677

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Leather, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977, Tanning, Madras General Sales Tax (Special Provisions) Act, 1963, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Excise - Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Agriculture produce - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, s. 2(a) - Whether 'tanned leather' be subjected to U P mandi fee payable under the provisions of the U P Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964? - Relied on Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti 1992 Indlaw SC 948(II) - Held, no illegality or infirmity in the judgment of High Court and finished product, i.e., 'tanned leather' even though it may have changed in physical appearance or chemical combination and even though it may commercially be a different item, still remains a hide or skin and tanned leather is subject to U P mandi fee - Appeal dismissed without any costs.
