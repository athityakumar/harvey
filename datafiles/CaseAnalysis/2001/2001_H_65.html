Union of India and Others v Harjeet Singh Sandhu and another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20172; (2001) 5 SCC 593; (2001) SCC (L&S) 891; AIR 2001 SC 1772; 2001 (3) AWC 1726; JT 2001 (4) SC 597; 2001 LabIC 1707; 2001 (2) LLN 756; 2001(3) SCALE 336; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1127; 2002 (1) SLJ 1; 2001 (3) SLT 650; 2001 (3) Supreme 370; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1397

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Government servant, Defence Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Respondent was captain in Army was tried by General Court martial u/s. 109 of 1950 Act, in connection with using third degree methods resulting in death of defence employee during interrogation - GCM awarded sentence of forfeiture of three years' service for purpose of promotion and severe reprimand to respondent - On revision by confirming authority GCM, enhanced punishment inflicted on respondent to forfeiture of three years of service for purpose of promotion and also for purpose of pay and pension - Chief of Army Staff annulled GCM proceedings on ground that proceedings were unjust - Show cause notice was issued to respondent to show cause why his services should not be terminated - Chief of Army Staff passed order dismissing respondent from service - Respondent filed writ petition before HC - Contention raised was that incident, in which respondent was involved had taken place in 1978 and Court martial proceedings had become barred by time 1981 u/s. 122 of Act, whereafter s. 19 of Act, was not available to be invoked - HC relied on decision of SC in Major Radha Krishan Vs. Union of India, 1996 Indlaw SC 3936, squarely applies to facts of this case and therefore exercise of power u/s. 19 r/w r. 14 was vitiated and order of termination had been quashed - In other SLP Respondent was Captain in Army was found blameworthy for discrepancies in respect of stocks in Fuel Petroleum Depot, - General Court Martial was ordered to be convened - Chief of Army Staff in exercise of power conferred by S. 19 r/w Rule 14 issued notice to show cause why his services be not terminated in view of the fact that court martial proceedings against respondent were impracticable and Chief of the Army Staff was of opinion that further retention of respondent in service was not desirable - Respondent filed writ petition in HC submitting that general court martial proceedings having become barred by time against him on account of lapse of three years from date of the offence, notice issued to him was without jurisdiction - HC relied on Major Radha Krishan Vs. Union of India 1996 Indlaw SC 3936, that once court martial proceedings have become time-barred Chief of the Army Staff could not recourse to S. 19 of the Act r/w Rule 14 of the Rules - HC writ petition has been allowed and show cause notice directed to be quashed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in relying on Major Radha Krishan 1996 Indlaw SC 3936 and quashing order of termination - Held, that Major Radha Krishan 1996 Indlaw SC 3936s case lays down propositions too broad to be acceptable to extent it holds that once period of limitation for trial by court martial was over, authorities could not take action u/r. 14(2) - SC did not agree with proposition that for purpose of r. 14(2), impracticability was concept different from impossibility - SC opinioned that HC was not right in allowing two writ petitions filed by respondents, respectively, by placing reliance on decision of SC in Major Radha Krishan 1996 Indlaw SC 3936 and holding that exercise of power u/s. 19 r/w r. 14 by COAS was vitiated solely on account of bar of limitation created by S. 122 of Act - Further held that before any decision to initiate disciplinary action against any of two respondents was taken, conduct and behaviour of respondents concerned during intervening period shall also be taken into consideration while deciding upon desirability of proceeding further in matter at this belated stage, and keeping in view, of course, requirement of military discipline and high traditions of Indian Army - Appeals allowed.
