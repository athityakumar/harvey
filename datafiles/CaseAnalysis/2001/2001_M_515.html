Munna Devi v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21128; (2001) 9 SCC 631; AIR 2002 SC 107; 2002 CRLJ 225; JT 2001 (9) SC 438; 2001(8) SCALE 88; 2002 (1) UC 96

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 376, 451 and 511 - Framing of charges - Revision power of High Court - HC quashed charges framed against respondent - Whether HC could prevent holding of trial by sitting in appeal against order of framing of charge by sifting and weighing evidence recorded during investigation? - Relied on 2000 Indlaw SC 544 - Held, HC has no authority to appreciate evidence in manner as Trial and Appellate Courts are required to do - Revisional powers could be exercised only when it is shown that there is a legal bar against continuance of criminal proceedings or framing of charge or facts as stated in First Information Report even if they are taken at face value and accepted in their entirety do not constitute offence for which accused has been charged - Premature for HC to say that material placed before Trial Court was insufficient for framing charge - Appeal allowed.
