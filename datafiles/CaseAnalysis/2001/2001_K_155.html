Kartar Singh Bhadana v Hari Singh Nalwa and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20191; (2001) 4 SCC 661; AIR 2001 SC 1556; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 5; JT 2001 (4) SC 180; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 575; 2001(3) SCALE 3; 2001 (2) SCJ 639; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 790; 2001 (3) SLT 695; 2001 (3) Supreme 20

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Obligation, Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968, Ferries Act

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 9A - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968 - Ferries Act - Mines And Minerals (Regulation And Development) Act, 1957 - Election to Haryana Assembly - Appellant was declared elected - Election petition - on ground that appellant held from appropriate Govt., that of State of Haryana, five leases for extraction of major and minor minerals and that, therefore, he was disqualified from contesting election - High Court upheld contention - Whether HC was justified in holding that a mining contract or a mining lease is a contract to execute a Govt. work on behalf of Govt. and in setting aside election of appellant by declaring first respondent duly elected? - It is not in dispute that appellant held mining leases from appropriate Govt. - Held, it is only when appropriate Govt. has undertaken works, such as laying of a road, erection of a building or construction of a dam, and has entered into a contract for execution of such works that contractor is disqualified u/s. 9A - S. 9A does not operate to disqualify lessee of a mining lease such as appellant - There is nothing in leases which can support submission that appellant had entered into a contract for execution of any works undertaken by Govt. - Appeal allowed.
