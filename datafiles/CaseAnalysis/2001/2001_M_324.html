N.S.S. Narayana Sarma and others v Messrs Goldstone Exports P. Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 209; (2002) 1 SCC 662; AIR 2002 SC 251; 2002 (46) ALR 360; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 207; JT 2001 (10) SC 13; 2002 (132) PLR 552; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 752; 2001(8) SCALE 269; 2001 (8) SLT 169; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 51; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 327; 2001 (8) Supreme 424; 2002 (1) UC 149

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: When a decree-holder complains of resistance to the execution of a decree for delivery of possession of immovable property, it is incumbent on the execution court to adjudicate upon it while determining only such question, which is relevant to the adjudication of the complaint and has arisen legally between the parties.
