Ramji Singh and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20720; (2001) 9 SCC 528; (2002) SCC (Cr) 760; AIR 2001 SC 3853; 2001 (4) CCR 136; 2001 (4) Crimes 308; 2001 CRLJ 4740; 2001 (2) JCC 241; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 329; JT 2001 (8) SC 521; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 502; 2001(7) SCALE 148; 2001 (7) SLT 280; 2001 (7) Supreme 751

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302/34 - Murder - Common intention - Appreciation of evidence - Whether in absence of charge of offence u/s. 34, conviction could be maintained u/s. 34? - Held, it is true that two injuries which proved to be fatal were not specifically attributed to either of accused common intention can be formed at spot - At times, it is difficult to get direct evidence of pre-concert of minds common intention can be gathered from circumstances and manner in which assault is carried out manner in which assault was carried out leaves no manner of doubt in our mind that appellants had come with intention to kill deceased - Further held, even in absence of charge u/s. 34, conviction could be maintained by courts below - Continuance of causing of injuries in spite of intervention of a witness and refusal to give water when deceased asked for it, shows predetermined mind of accused that they had come with intention to kill - It is true, that there is no evidence of prior meeting of minds - But sequence of events which unfolded during course of occurrence clearly indicate predetermined minds of accused persons to kill deceased - Appeal dismissed.
