Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut v Hindustan Lever Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 321; 2001 (130) E.L.T. 721; 2001 (44) RLT 819

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Three appeals raised issue of quantity discount - Damage discount - Raised issue of bank charges - Held, in respect of quantity discount learned Counsel for assessee and learned Attorney General agreed that order under challenge should be set aside (insofar as it relates to quantity discount) and issue should stand remanded to Tribunal to be considered afresh in accordance with law, with liberty to parties to produce fresh evidence - Further, so far as bank charges are concerned, issue is covered in favour of assessee by judgment of this Court in Commr. of Central Excise v Vikram Detergent Ltd. (2001 Indlaw SC 460) - Accordingly, civil appeals, so far as they relate to bank charges, will stand dismissed - Insofar as damage discount is concerned having considered facts, Court satisfied that assessee is not entitled to a discount on account of damages - Civil appeals, therefore stand allowed to extent of damage discount and quantity discount.
