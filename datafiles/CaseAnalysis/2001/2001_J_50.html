Dhurandhar Prasad Singh v Jai Prakash University and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20079; (2001) 6 SCC 534; AIR 2001 SC 2552; 2001 (44) ALR 579; 2001 (3) AWC 2331; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 31; JT 2001 (5) SC 578; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 280; 2001(4) SCALE 495; 2001 (4) SCJ 389; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1129; 2001 (5) SLT 386; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 148; 2001 (5) Supreme 278; 2001 (2) UC 290B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 47 and 115, O. 22, r. 10 - (A) Whether in a case of devolution of interest during pendency of a suit as postulated under O. 22, r. 10 of Code, decree passed against predecessor interest without bringing successor - interest on record would make decree a nullity and same can be executed? - Decree was passed against governing body of college which was defendant without seeking leave of court to continue suit against University upon whom interest of original defendant devolved and impleading it - Held, such an omission would not make decree void ab initio so as to invoke application of s. 47 of Code and entail dismissal of execution - (B) Whether application under O. 22, r. 10 CPC, 1908 seeking leave of court is required under law to be filed by that person alone upon whom interest has devolved during pendency of suit and by nobody else? - Plain language of O. 22, r. 10, CPC, 1908 does not suggest that leave can be sought by that person alone upon whom interest has devolved - Further held, prayer for leave can be made not only by person upon whom interest has devolved, but also by plaintiff or any other party or person interested - Appeal allowed.
