S. M. Datta v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20004; (2001) 7 SCC 659; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1201; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1361; AIR 2001 SC 3253; 2001 (3) CCR 212; 2001 (4) Crimes 206; 2002 (1) CRJ 103; 2001 CRLJ 4195; 2001 (91) FLR 853; JT 2001 (6) SC 631; 2002 (1) LLJ 3; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 129; 2001(5) SCALE 457; 2001 (6) SLT 129; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 140; 2001 (6) Supreme 408; 2001 (2) UC 656

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Safety, Adult, Factories Act, 1911, Gujarat Factories Rules, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of complaint - Criminal revisional jurisdiction - Held, complaints ought not to be quashed at initial stages unless they are termed to be an abuse of process of court - If an offence is disclosed court will not interfere with an investigation and will permit investigation into offence alleged to have been committed - Quashing of a complaint should rather be an exception and a rarity than an ordinary rule - Genuineness of averments in FIR cannot possibly be gone into and document shall have to be read as a whole so as to decipher intent of maker thereof - It is not a document which requires decision with exactitude, neither is it a document which requires mathematical accuracy and nicety, but same should be able to communicate or indicative of disclosure of an offence broadly and in event said test stands satisfied, question relating to quashing of a complaint would not arise - Appeal dismissed.
