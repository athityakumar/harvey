Union of India v Atar Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21238; (2003) 12 SCC 434; JT 2001 (10) SC 212; 2003(10) SCALE 1010

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Service - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 389 and 438 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Respondent convicted u/s. 409, IPC, 1860 and s. 13 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 preferred appeal to High Court - HC suspended conviction u/s. 389 of CrPC, 1973, solely on ground that non-suspension of conviction may entail removal of delinquent Govt. servant from service - Whether HC erred in exercising its discretionary power u/s. 389 by suspending conviction? - It cannot be doubted that s. 389 confers a discretion on the Appellate Court to decide the question of suspension of a conviction in a given case - But, if in exercise of that discretion, the court suspends a conviction, it would always be open for Supreme Court to examine the correctness of exercising of that discretion and pass appropriate orders, in the event, Supreme Court comes to a conclusion that the discretion had not been appropriately exercised - Held, HC has mechanically passed order by suspending conviction, and discretion ought not to have been exercised by suspending conviction therefore set aside order of HC - Appeal disposed of.
