Munshi Prasad and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19940; (2002) SCC (Cr) 175; (2002) 1 SCC 351; AIR 2001 SC 3031; 2001 (4) CCR 120; 2002 (1) Crimes 89; 2001 CRLJ 4708; 2001 (2) JCC 247; JT 2001 (8) SC 406; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 415; 2001(7) SCALE 114; 2001 (7) SLT 200; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 25; 2001 (8) Supreme 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Former Statement, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, ss. 154, 155, 157, 159, 161, 162, 313 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused murdered deceased and FIR was lodged by his brother PW5 and charge sheet was filed - After elaborate trial and considering evidences Trial Court convicted accused persons - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal in HC and was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Orders of Trial Court and HC were justified - Held, SC in Arjun Marik and others v. Stale of Bihar, 1994 Indlaw SC 1936 held that it was well-settled principle of law that in appeal against conviction for murder SC could only intervene on concurrent finding of fact suffering from vice and violative of fundamental rules - Scrutiny of evidence in murder trial should be effected with more than ordinary care so as not to affect 'dispassionate judicial scrutiny' - Accused plea of alibi could not be believed as place of occurrence was only 400 -500 yards from place of offence - SC in Dudh Nath Pandey v. State of U.P, 1981 Indlaw SC 107 held that plea of alibi could succeed only if accused was so far away from place of offence and distance would be material factor in matters of alibi - Settled law was that mere delay in lodging FIR could not be said to be fatal in criminal prosecution if delay was properly explained as laid down by SC in Apren Joseph Alias Current Kunjukunju and Others v State of Kerala ,1972 Indlaw SC 359 and Shiv Ram and another v. State of U.P., 1997 Indlaw SC 1607 and Stale of Karnataka v. Moin Patel and others, 1996 Indlaw SC 2614 - Evidence of witnesses with minor discrepancies without affecting core of prosecution case could not be rejected in its entirety as held by SC in State of U.P. v. M.K. Anthony, 1984 Indlaw SC 19 and Leela Ram v. State of Haryana, 1999 Indlaw SC 564 - Courts should consider quality and not quantity of evidence - Prosecution had proved case beyond doubt against accused persons - Delay in submission of FIR to Court was properly explained by PW 8 and investigation was promptly taken up and carried out - Appeals dismissed.
