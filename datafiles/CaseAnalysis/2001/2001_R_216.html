Rajender Singh v Ramdhar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20113; (2001) 6 SCC 213; AIR 2001 SC 2220; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 51; 2002 (48) ALR 142; 2001 (2) CGLJ 384; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 95; 2002 (93) RD 605; 2001(4) SCALE 170; 2001 (4) SCJ 197; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 736; 2001 (4) SLT 477; 2001 (4) Supreme 170; 2001 (2) UC 171

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attachment Of Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115 and 136, O. 38, rr. 5 and 7 - Whether court, which passes an order of attachment in respect of properties situated within jurisdiction of another court, can directly send order of attachment to that court or it should always endorse order of attachment to district court within local limits of whose jurisdiction attached property is situated? - S. 136, CPC, 1908 lays down only a procedure and if owner of property raised an objection to effect that procedure was not complied with, court can issue appropriate direction to cure defect in procedure - Held, failure, if any, on part of court which issued attachment order in sending attachment order and connected papers to district court will not invalidate attachment order as such - Appeal disposed of.
