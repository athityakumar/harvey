Punjab University v V.N. Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 46; (2001) 8 SCC 179; (2002) SCC (L&S) 25; AIR 2001 SC 3672; JT 2001 (7) SC 96; 2001(5) SCALE 535; 2001 (6) SLT 187; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 280; 2001 (6) Supreme 524

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Punjab University Act, 1947 - Competency to file appeal - Determination of - Respondent filed a suit against the Punjab University - Suit was decreed - Appeal was filed thereagainst by the Punjab University - Respondent objected to the same contending that the Registrar of the Punjab University was not authorised or competent to file the appeals, in absence of any decision taken by the Senate of the University - Objections was upheld by the First Appellate Court - Appeals filed thereagainst was also dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Registrar was competent to file the appeals without any decision of the Senate of the University to that effect.

Held, Registrar u/s. 21 of the Act is authorised to represent the University in all legal proceedings, except where there is a decision of the Senate to the contrary. While representing the University, in view of the provisions u/s. 21 of the Act, the Registrar would be taking several steps in prosecution of the legal proceedings. Hence, if the Registrar filed the appeal, against the decision of the Trial Court, which had gone against the Punjab University though strictly speaking exceeded his authority, but his action in having filed the appeals was later on ratified by the competent authority by resolution dt. 29-9-1991. The ratification has the effect of relating back to the time when the action was taken without authority. The judgment passed by the HC and Appellate Courts below set aside and matter remanded to the First Appellate Courts for decision on merits. Appeals allowed.
