Vishwambhar and Others v Laxminarayan (Dead) Through Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19859; (2001) 6 SCC 163; AIR 2001 SC 2607; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 10; 2001 (44) ALR 569; 2001 (5) ALT(SC) 27; 2001 (3) AWC 2315; 2001 (4) Bom.C.R. 308; 2001 (2) CGLJ 210; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 104; JT 2001 (5) SC 539; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 815; 2001(4) SCALE 420; 2001 (5) SLT 324; 2001 (5) Supreme 220; 2001 (2) UC 340B

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 65 - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, s. 8 - Ancestral property - Widow managing properties as guardian of minors - Sale of land - Without legal necessity and without obtaining permission of court - Whether suit is within limitation? - Whether sale of land by mother as guardian without any legal necessity and without sanction of court were void ab initio and not binding on plaintiffs? - Held, s. 8(2) of Hindu Minority, Guardianship Act, 1956 lays down, inter alia, that natural guardian shall not, without previous permission of court, transfer by sale any part of immoveable property of minor - In sub-section (3) of said section, it is specifically provided that any disposal of immoveable property by a natural guardian, in contravention of sub-section (2) is voidable at instance of minor or any person claiming under him - There is, therefore, little scope for doubt that alienations made by widow were voidable at instance of plaintiffs and they were required to get alienations set aside if they wanted to avoid transfers and regain properties from purchasers - Plaint as it stood before amendment prayer for setting aside sale deeds was not there, such a prayer appears to have been introduced by amendment during hearing of suit and trial court considered amended prayer and decided suit on that basis - If in law plaintiffs were required to have sale deeds set aside before making any claim in respect of properties sold, then a suit without such a prayer was of no avail to plaintiffs - In all probability, realising this difficulty plaintiffs filed application for amendment of plaint seeking to introduce prayer for setting aside sale deeds - Unfortunately, realisation came too late - Concededly, Plaintiff 2 attained majority on 05-08-1975 and plaintiff 1 on 20-07- 1978 - Though suit was filed on 30-11-1980 prayer seeking setting aside of sale deeds was made in December 1985 - Art. 60 of Limitation Act prescribes a period of three years for setting aside a transfer of property made by guardian of a ward, by ward who has attained majority and period is to be computed from date when ward attains majority - Since limitation started running from dates when plaintiffs attained majority prescribed period had elapsed by date of presentation of plaint so far as plaintiff 1 is concerned - Therefore, trial court rightly dismissed suit filed by plaintiff 1 - Regarding suit filed by plaintiff 2, it was within limitation but without prayer for setting aside sale deeds - Since claim for recovery of possession of properties alienated could not have been made without setting aside sale deeds suit as initially filed was not maintainable - By date defect was rectified (December 1985) by introducing such a prayer by amendment of plaint prescribed period of limitation for seeking such a relief had elapsed - In circumstances, amendment of plaint could not come to rescue of plaintiff - Appeal dismissed.
