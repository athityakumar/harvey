Dental Council of India v Subharti K. K. B. Charitable Trust and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20153; (2001) 5 SCC 486; AIR 2001 SC 2151; AIR 2001 SCW 1883; 2002 (4) AWC 2916; 2001 (4) ESC 564; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 435; 2001(3) SCALE 492; 2001 (5) SCJ 82; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 149; 2001 (2) SLR 615; 2001 (4) SLT 454; 2001 (3) Supreme 529; 2001 (2) UC 89

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Medical Council Act

Summary: Education - Criteria for approval of college - Dentists Act, 1948 - Medical Council Act - (A) Whether students would be permitted to sit in examinations when teaching in college has been suspended by court order? - Held, students would only be permitted to sit in examinations as per Regulations of Dental Council of India laying down requirement of attendance of minimum classes for each year for four years' duration of BDS course - (B) Whether admissions granted on approval of dental college to increase admissions as per mandamus issued by High Court is to be disturbed? - Further held, order passed by HC of Allahabad issuing a mandamus to accord approval to dental college for admitting annually a batch of 100 students instead of 60 students and fact that this court has not stayed operation of said order and also further orders passed by HC - No interference - Appeal disposed of.
