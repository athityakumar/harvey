A-One Granites v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20282; (2001) 3 SCC 537; AIR 2001 SC 1203; AIR 2001 SCW 848; 2001 (43) ALR 835; 2001 (3) AWC 1956; JT 2001 (5) SC 9; 2001(2) SCALE 85; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1085; 2001 (2) SLT 360; 2001 (2) Supreme 27

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 17-A, 3(e) - Uttar Pradesh Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 1963, rr. 2(7), 21, 23, 23(1), 24, 27, 72, 73, 74, 75, 76, 77, 78, 79, 9 - Mining lease - Approval - Validity - State Govt. sanctioned mining lease of granite in relation to area in question in favour of appellant for period of 15 years without following procedure prescribed u/r. 72 of the Rules - In accordance with aforesaid order of Divisional Commissioner passed, when respondent no. 4 moved District Magistrate for considering his application for grant of mining lease, by order expressed inability to decide application on merit in view of lease granted by State Govt. in favour of appellant - Respondent no. 4 challenged aforesaid order passed by State Govt. and order passed by District Magistrate by filing a writ petition HC - Writ application filed by respondent no. 4 was allowed, order passed by State Govt. and order passed by District Magistrate were quashed and District Magistrate was directed to take decision upon application filed by respondent no. 4 in accordance with law as lease was sanctioned on by State Govt. in favour of appellant without following procedure prescribed u/r. 72 of the Rules - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in giving direction to District Magistrate to consider application filed by respondent no. 4 for grant of mining lease - Held, that it was true that on earlier occasion this Court found that notice was valid, but order for grant of mining lease being contrary to r. 72, was held to be invalid - In operative portion of judgment it was specifically directed that respondents will be at liberty to issue fresh notice for grant of lease in accordance with law and keeping in view observations contained herein, which would obviously mean that fresh notice for grant of lease was required to be issued in accordance with r. 72 of the Rules - If fresh notice was issued, all concerned persons including appellant and respondent no. 4 can apply for grant - Hence, HC was not justified in quashing order of District Magistrate and giving direction to him to consider application filed by respondent no. 4 - Authorities were now required to issue fresh notice in terms of r. 72 of the Rules and consider applications for grant of lease filed pursuant thereto in accordance with law and no application filed earlier either pursuant to previous notices or otherwise shall be considered - Appeal partly allowed.
