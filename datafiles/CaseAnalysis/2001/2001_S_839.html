Kerala Magistrates (Judicial) Assn. and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20249; (2001) 3 SCC 521; (2001) SCC (L&S) 605; AIR 2001 SC 1075; 2001 (1) CLR 951; JT 2001 (3) SC 229; 2001 (1) KLT 920; 2001 (34) LabIC 1119; 2001(2) SCALE 301; 2001 (2) SCJ 338; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 222; 2001 (2) SLR 250; 2001 (2) SLT 556; 2001 (2) Supreme 240

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Bifurcation, Ratio in integrated cadre

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(1), 234, 235, 309 - Kerala Public Services Act, 1968, s. 2(1) - Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, rr. 3, 3(4) - Bifurcation - Ratio in integrated cadre - On basis of order issued by Govt., two separate wings of Judicial Service, one on civil side and other on criminal side was formed - On challenge made to bifurcation, HC was of conclusion that such bifurcation was invalid and discriminatory - SC upheld validity of bifurcation and decision of HC on that point was reversed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the Rules providing ratio in integrated cadre for determination of inter se seniority between incumbents of criminal wing and incumbents of civil wing have been arbitrarily fixed.

Held, before enactment of the Rules, the Governor had full consultations with the Public Service Commission as well as the HC. In fact it is the HC which deliberated on the question of providing a ratio in the integrated cadre, which was ultimately accepted by the State Govt. Having examined the considerations made by the HC and the Govt. in fixing the ratio in the integrated cadre, embodied in Sub-rule (4) of Rule 3 bearing in mind the parameters and the power of a Court for interference with such decisions, it is difficult to hold that the 3(4) of the Rules can be termed to be arbitrary or irrational or that the decision has been taken without taking into consideration the germane materials. On the other hand, the final decision has been taken after elaborate discussions, taking into account all the relevant factors and consequently, the Full Court of the HC was fully justified in coming to the conclusion that the Rule cannot be struck down as being discriminatory or being violative of art.14 of the Constitution. No legal infirmity with the conclusion arrived at by the HC, requiring interference. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In Service Jurisprudence, integration is complicated administrative problem, where in doing broad justice to many, some bruise to few cannot be ruled out.
