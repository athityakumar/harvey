Thakarsibhai Devjibhai and Others v Executive Engineer, Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 4822; (2001) 9 SCC 584; AIR 2001 SC 2424; 2001 (43) ALR 17; 2001 (2) AWC 924; JT 2001 (3) SC 90; 2001 (3) SLT 558; 2001 (4) Supreme 457; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 851

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - (A) Whether reliance on previous award of reference court pertaining to similar lands for determining compensation for land acquired, rightly placed? - Held, claimants had adduced sufficient evidence describing nature and situation of acquired lands to be similar to other acquired land of same village - No evidence has been led on behalf of state to find any difference between two lands - Therefore, reference Court was justified in placing reliance on previous award - (B) Whether quantum of compensation rightly reduced on basis of largeness of area and distance between land under acquisition and land in relied upon award? - Each landowners holdings are of small area and largeness is merely when each landholders land is clubbed together then area becomes large, also distance between two classes of lands by itself cannot derogate claim of claimant unless there are such other materials to show that quality and potentiality of such land is inferior - Further held, difference is not such to lead to reduce rate of compensation - Claimants Appeal Nos. 506-514 of 2001 allowed - States' appeal Nos. 515-523 of 2001 dismissed.
