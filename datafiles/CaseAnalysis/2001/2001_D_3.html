State of Rajasthan And Another v D. P. Metals

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21394; (2002) 1 SCC 279; AIR 2001 SC 3076; JT 2001 (8) SC 328; 2001(7) SCALE 14; 2001 (124) STC 611; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 572

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Trader, Value Of Goods, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, Transporter, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1955, Tripura Sales Tax Act, U.P. Sales Tax Rules, 1948, Haryana Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 301 and 304 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 78(5) - Constitutional validity of s. 78(5) - Respondent's truck was seized by Asst. Commercial Taxes Officer as the same was found not be carrying the declaration Form ST 18A - Show cause notice issued and penalty was levied u/s. 78(5) of 1994 Act - Respondent filed applications before tribunal contending that s. 78 (5) was ultra vires being beyond the legislative competence of the State and also excessive, arbitrary and unreasonable and, therefore, violative of arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 301 and 304 of the Constitution which was dismissed by tribunal - HC held s. 78(5) of the Act to be unconstitutional and ultra vires - Hence, present appeal - Whether s. 78(5) of Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 is unconstitutional and ultra vires? - Held, no - Provisions to check evasion of tax are within the legislative competence of the States under Entry 54 of List II - If there was legislative competence to enact s. 78(2) then the same power contained in Entry 54 of List II could enable the State Legislature to provide for consequence of non- compliance by incorporating sub-section (5) therein - Section 78(5) and s. 78(8) are part of an integral scheme and deal with two separate classes of people referred to in s. 78(2) - Penalty u/s. 78(5) is leviable under two circumstances, firstly if there is non-compliance with s. 78(2)(a) i.e. not carrying the documents mentioned in that sub-clause or, secondly if false or forged documents or declaration is submitted - Despite opportunity having been granted u/s. 78(5) if the requisite documents referred to in sub- clause 2(a) are not produced, even though the same should exist, would clearly prove the guilty intent - Objects to be taxed so long as they happen to be within the legislative competence of the legislature can be taxed by the legislature according to the exigencies of its needs, because there can be no doubt that the State is entitled to raise revenue by taxation - Quantum of tax levied by the taxing statute, the conditions subject to which it is levied, the manner in which it is sought to be recovered, are all matters within the competence of the legislature, and in dealing with the contention raised by a citizen that the taxing statute contravenes Art. 19, courts would naturally be circumspect and cautious as such there cannot, in the present case, be any valid challenge to the rate of penalty provided for in s. 78(5) of the Act - Hence, provisions of s. 78(5) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 are valid and the impugned decision of the HC in this regard is not correct - Appeals disposed of.
