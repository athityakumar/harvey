Chitra Kumari and another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20286; (2001) 3 SCC 208; AIR 2001 SC 1237; 2001 (43) ALR 41; JT 2001 (3) SC 70; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 133; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 445; 2001(2) SCALE 58; 2001 (2) SLT 126; 2001 (2) Supreme 97; 2001 (2) UC 213B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 110, 21, 91 - Land granted on old grant terms - Resumption - Validity - Appellants have land with bungalows in Ambala Cantonment area - Land was granted to predecessors of appellants on 'old-grant terms' - Suit was filed in Trial and order of resumption was challenged - Suit was decreed - Respondents then filed appeal and same was dismissed - Respondents then filed second appeal before HC - As this Court had now finally laid down law and as judgment of court and first appellant court were only on basis that prior opportunity of being heard had not been given, HC by its judgment reversed judgment of trial court as well as first appellate court and dismissed suit - Thus, it was to see that before HC it had not been disputed that land was under an old-grant term and that terms of the old grant had been produced on record and the revision filed was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any infirmity in judgment of HC - Held, when respondents went in second appeal before HC, at this stage also, no cross-objections were filed - Before HC it was not disputed that land was on old-grant terms - HC has so recorded in its judgment - It was settled law that one had to proceed on basis of what has been recorded by court - If any party feels aggrieved by what has been recorded by courts clarification had to be sought from that same court - In this case clarification was sought, by way of review petition, to which as stated above, fresh documents were purported to be attached for first time - HC has rejected review petition - HC has thus confirmed that at time second appeal was argued it was not disputed that land was on old-grant terms - This Court has to go by what has been recorded in judgment - What was recorded in judgment was supported by the conduct of the parties inasmuch as no evidence was led to dispute the fact, no documents were tendered or marked as exhibits and no submissions were made on this aspect - That it was not disputed that the land was on old-grant terms is also supported by what has been recorded in the judgments of the trial court and first appellate court - Admissions were relevant evidence if not explained away - Findings of fact were based on evidence before the trial court and require no interference - Once it was admitted that land was on old-grant terms it was irrelevant to argue that it was not shown that Ambala was under Bengal Army - Same would be position when on evidence court had held that land was on old-grant terms - Hence there was no infirmity in impugned judgments of HC - Appeal dismissed.
