Deokinandan Sharma v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21135; (2001) 5 SCC 340; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1079; AIR 2001 SC 1767; 2001 (2) CLR 308; JT 2001 (4) SC 533; 2001 LabIC 1704; 2001 (1) LLJ 1589; 2001(3) SCALE 294; 2001 (2) SLR 606; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1498

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Appellant was employed in State Bank of India - Charges of misappropriation if amount and being responsible for financial irregularities - Suspended - Enquiry - Departmental proceedings - Dismissed - Challenged - (A) Whether Conducting Officer did not afford reasonable opportunity of hearing to appellant to adduce defence evidence? - Held, charge-sheet, list of documents, list of witnesses was served upon appellant - In spite of full opportunity afforded to defence no witness was examined - In view of aforesaid facts, reasonable opportunity was afforded to appellant to adduce evidence during course of enquiry - (B) Whether Appellate Authority disposed of appeal without recording reasons and considering submission made on behalf of appellant? - Held, Board of Directors of Bank while dismissing appeal has taken into consideration all points raised therein and dismissed appeal after duly considering entire matter - No infirmity in impugned orders - Appeal dismissed.
