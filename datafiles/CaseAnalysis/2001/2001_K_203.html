 Kishan Prakash Sharma and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.B. Pattanaik, D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19879; (2001) 5 SCC 212; (2001) SCC (L&S) 805; AIR 2001 SC 1493; JT 2001 (3) SC 554; 2001(2) SCALE 586; 2001 (2) SLR 441; 2001 (3) SLT 1B; 2001 (2) Supreme 467; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1232

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Corporate; Service

Keywords: Sastry Award, Bharat Petroleum Corporation, General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Corporate - Service - Companies Act,1956 - Industrial Disputes Act,1947 - General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971 - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956,ss. 48(2-C), 48, 48(2)(cc) - General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, ss. 2, 3(a), 3(o), 3 to 6, 8, 10, 16, 16(1), 16(1)(g), 16(2), 16(8), 16(7), 16(6), 17, 17-A, 17-A(7)(2) - Constitution of India,1950, arts. 14, 19, 19(1)(c), 19(1)(g), 21, 39(c), 136 - Amendment - Retrospective effect - Challenged - Prior to 1972 there were nearly 106 general insurance companies with different service rules and regulations - Union Govt. enacted the 1972 Act nationalizing all of them to have uniformity and necessary amendments were made from time to time - In 1984, Act was amended and replaced by the 1985 Act by inserting fresh provision to ss.16 and 17- A with restrospective effect w.e.f. dt.2-1-1973 - Challenged by instant writ petitions - Whether retrospective effect given to the 1985 Amendment Act was arbitrary and violative of Constitution - Held, legislature had powers to make laws prospectively and retrospectively - S. 16 of the 1972 Act was amended by inserting sub-section (8) and s. 17A- By amendment of the Act, now enactment specifically provides that every scheme or notification framed by Central Govt. u/s. 16(1) of the 1985 Act was deemed as valid and effective as made u/s. 17A of the 1972 Act - Retrospective effect given to scheme was only to overcome difficulties pointed out by SC in Ajay Kumar Banerjee Vs Union of India, 1984 Indlaw SC 332 - - Validation of invalid rule by amending main enactment under which it was made was well-known legislative device approved by SC - Vide s. 17-A(7)(2) amendments were made to schemes framed in 1976 and 1977 by specifically excluding 1980 Scheme from retrospective operation given to the 1985 Amendment Act - Central govt. made amendments only to adopt uniformity in rules and regulations in nationalized banks and LIC - Schemes with retrospective effect weresubject to several controls and would be approved by high-powered officers with ultimate control of Parliament - SC in Rajnarain Singh vs. Chairman, 1954 Indlaw SC 133 held that there were sufficient guidelines in the Act and executive authority was empowered by statute to modify either existing or future laws - In matters of legislative nature, consultation was not required unless required by law - No merit in petitions - Petitions dismissed.
