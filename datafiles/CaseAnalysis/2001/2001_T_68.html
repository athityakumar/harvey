District Mining Officer and Others v Tata Iron and Steel Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20066; (2001) 7 SCC 358; AIR 2001 SC 3134; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 158; JT 2001 (6) SC 183; 2001(4) SCALE 680; 2001 (5) SLT 568; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 147; 2001 (5) Supreme 545

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Emergency Provisions

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution - Mineral (Validation) Act, 1992, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 32, 139, 254(1), 265, 300A and 372 - Mines and Minerals (Regulations and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 2 and 9 - Tamil Nadu Panchayat Act, 1958, s. 115 - Minerals (Validation) Ordinance, 1992 - Validation Act, 1969 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Goverment of India Act, 1935, s. 72 - India and Burma (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1940 - Coal Production Fund Ordinance, 1944, s. 3 - City of Bombay (Building Works Restriction) Act, 1944 - Cess Act, 1880 - Whether by Act of 1992, is it only taxes on minerals already realising under invalid law or right apt levy tax, which became due up to 4th of April, 1991? - Held, Validation Act enacted by Parliament did not create any right in favour of State to collect cess even after expiry of act itself - All that sub-s. (3) of s. 2 means, which has been introduced for removal of difficulty, is that notwithstanding the fact that the State law remained in force till April 1991, if an assessee has paid more than what he is legally liable to pay and an application for refund had already been made, then he would have the right to get refund of the excess tax paid, even though the life of the Act expires on 04-04-1991 - Validation Acts do not authorize any fresh levy or collection in respect of liabilities accrued prior to 04-04-1991 - No provisions in Validation Act which authorizes imposition and collection of taxes even after 04-04-1991 - Appeal dismissed.
