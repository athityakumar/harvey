A. I. I. M. S. Students Union v A. I. I. M. S. And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21400; (2002) 1 SCC 428; AIR 2001 SC 3262; 2001 (4) AWC 2886; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 284; JT 2001 (7) SC 12; 2001(5) SCALE 430; 2001 (4) SLR 134; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956

Summary: Education - Constitution - All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1933 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 and 16 - Reservation of seats for institutional candidates - Constitutionality - AIIMS conducted a common entrance examination for post-graduation wherein graduates of AIIMS and graduates from all over the country participate - Prospectus issued declared that 1/3rd of the seats were reserved for in-house candidates of the Institute and also 50% seats discipline-wise, subject to an overall reservation of 33% - Writ petitioners before the HC sought for striking down the policy of reservation in favour of institutional candidates as unconstitutional - HC struck down 33% quota carved out in favour of the AIIMS in-house candidates both at the entry level as also discipline wise - Hence, present appeals - Held, reservation is protective discrimination; provision for sources of entry is aimed at securing equal or proportionate distribution - institutional reservation was not supported by the Constitution or constitutional principles - Having taken a common entrance test, there was no intelligible differentia which distinguishes the institutional candidates from others; and there was no nexus sought to be achieved with the objects of AIIMS by such reservation - Such a reservation based on institutional continuity in the absence of any relevant evidence in justification thereof was unconstitutional and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Institutional reservation for AIIMS candidates declared ultra vires the Constitution and, hence, struck down - Directions issued regarding allocation of seats and reservations to be made - Appeals disposed of.
