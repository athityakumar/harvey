Indu Engineering and Textiles Limited v Delhi Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 292; (2001) 5 SCC 691; AIR 2001 SC 2668; 2001 (44) ALR 476; 2001 (2) ARBLR 486; 2001 (3) CompLJ 325; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 70; JT 2001 (5) SC 404; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 111; 2001 (2) RAJ 469; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 770; 2001(4) SCALE 317; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 916; 2001 (5) SLT 190; 2001 (5) Supreme 111; 2001 (2) UC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30 and 35 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 70 - Duress - Prospective application - Natural justice - Whether appellant was entitled to claim enhanced price of hard coke for quantity supplied by it to respondent? - View taken by Arbitrator, in circumstances of case, was a plausible one and same could not be said to be suffering from any manifest error on face of award or wholly improbable or perverse one - Held, as such it was not open to court to interfere with award within statutory invitations laid down in s. 30 of Act - Division Bench exceeded limits of its jurisdiction in entering into facts of case and in interpreting agreement between parties and correspondence which was a part of said agreement - Appeal allowed.
