T. N. Lakshmaiah v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19933; (2002) SCC (Cr) 103; (2002) 1 SCC 219; AIR 2001 SC 3828; 2001 (4) CCR 173; 2001 (4) Crimes 334; 2001 (2) JCC 347; JT 2001 (9) SC 95; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 508; 2001(7) SCALE 256; 2001 (5) SCJ 322; 2001 (7) SLT 350; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 200; 2001 (7) Supreme 658

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 84, 299 and 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 4 and 105 - (A) Whether circumstances on record justifying benefit of exception as incorporated u/s. 84 of IPC? - Held, appellant was sane and understood implications of act done by him and in no case was having unsound mind - Appellant has not led any evidence in proof of plea of insanity and behaviour at time and subsequent to commission of crime clearly indicates that he knew and was capable of knowing nature of act done by him - No record allegedly showing appellant to be suffering from any mental disease when he is stated to have applied for bail - (B) Whether prosecution failed to establish appellant guilt beyond reasonable doubt? - Further held, proved circumstances which lead to only inference of involvement of accused in commission of crime - Appeal dismissed.
