Mohammad Shahnawaz Akhtar and Another v First ADJ Varanasi and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 1003; (2002) 9 SCC 375

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Whether High Court was justified in re-appreciation of evidence in writ petition? - Held, HC has acted like an Appellate Court and re-appreciated evidence and thereby exercised a jurisdiction which it did not have - HC has nowhere arrived at a finding that there was any error of jurisdiction committed by any of courts below or finding of fact impugned before it suffered from perversity - In exercise of writ jurisdiction, HC ought not to have entered into re-appreciation of evidence and dislodged finding of fact recorded by Trial Court and maintained in revision by learned Addl. District Judge Order set aside - Order of Trial Court upheld - Appeal disposed of.
