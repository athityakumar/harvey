Rajsekhar Gogoi v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20092; (2001) 6 SCC 46; AIR 2001 SC 2313; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 189; 2001(4) SCALE 13; 2001 (3) SCJ 233; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 468; 2001 (4) SLT 268; 2001 (4) Supreme 28

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Assam Excise Rules, 1945, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Assam Excise Rules, 1945, rr. 206 and 223 - Dispute in present case pertains to settlement of a country liquor shop in favor of respondent 4 as a result of decision of division bench of High Court - The division bench came to conclusion that r. 206 was not mandatory and also held that respondent 4 was educated unemployed youth within meaning of that expression in r. 223(2) - Present appeal - It was imperative for a tender to furnish full information as required so that same could be verified by Deputy Commr. or any other authorized person "before settlement of shop to tender" - In present case, such an opportunity was clearly denied to authorities when respondent 4 had not furnished requisite particulars along with her tender - Held, as tender itself of respondent 4 was liable to be rejected because of lack of particulars no further question arises - Also observations of HC that r. 206 is not mandatory is not correct - Language of said rule is clear and unambiguous - It not only says that tenders must be in their required form but also stipulates consequence of non - compliance thereto, consequence being that tenders not containing all particulars "shall be liable to be rejected" - Appeal allowed.
