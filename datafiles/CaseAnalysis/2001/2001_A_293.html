Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax v J. K. Synthetics Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 250; (2003) 10 SCC 623; AIR 2001 SC 1531; 2001 (166) CTR 498; [2001] 251 ITR 200; JT 2001 (3) SC 580; 2004(3) SCALE 532; 2001 (2) SLT 763; 2001 (4) Supreme 334; [2001] 116 TAXMAN 598

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1993

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 156, 142(1), 143(1 A) and 143(1)(a) - Petitioner-assessee had returned net loss - After adjustments had been made by taxing authorities under provision of s. 143(1A) amount of loss stood reduced - Whether loss which is reduced by reason of application of provisions of sub-s. (1)(a) falls within ambit of sub-s. (1 A)? - Held, taxing authorities under provisions of s. 143(1A) sought to levy additional tax upon assessee in this behalf - Even where loss declared by assessee had been reduced by reason of adjustments made under sub-s. (1)(a), provisions of sub-s. (1 A) would apply - Appeal allowed.
