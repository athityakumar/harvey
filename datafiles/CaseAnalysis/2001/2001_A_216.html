Shobha Suresh Jumani v Appellate Tribunal, Forfeited Property, and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Ruma Pal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 235; (2001) 5 SCC 755; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1334; AIR 2001 SC 2288; 2001 (2) CCR 210; 2001 CRLJ 2583; [2001] 249 ITR 405; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 112; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 806; 2001(4) SCALE 4; 2001 (3) SCJ 243; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 525; 2001 (4) SLT 193; 2001 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 672; 2001 (4) Supreme 36; [2001] 117 TAXMAN 349

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI; Fema

Keywords: Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Act, 1921

Summary: Exchange Control & FDI - Criminal - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, ss. 2(2)(c), 6(1), 7 and 12(4) - Whether wife whose husband's property is ordered to be forfeited under Act, 1976, is entitled to file an appeal as 'person aggrieved' u/s. 12(4) of Act? - There is no infringement of her legal right - For purposes of Act husband and wife are different entities - If properties standing in name of a relative are forfeited on ground that smugglers or foreign exchange manipulators were holding said properties in their names or that such properties are illegally acquired, then to that extent, relative or associate can be held to be a person aggrieved by order of competent authority - But, a relative or associate cannot be considered to be aggrieved if properties belonging to smuggler or foreign exchange manipulator are forfeited under Act - Appeal dismissed.
