Indian Petrochemicals Corporation Limited and Another v Shramik Sena and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20325; (2001) SCC (L&S) 461; (2001) 2 SCC 529; AIR 2001 SC 857; AIR 2001 SCW 584; 2001 (2) Bom.C.R. 816; 2001 (1) CLR 981; 2001 (88) FLR 1085; JT 2001 (2) SC 508; 2001 (1) LLJ 1040; 2001 (1) LLN 924; 2001(1) SCALE 465; 2001 (2) SCJ 233; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 659; 2001 (1) SLT 908; 2001 (1) Supreme 523; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1121

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation

Summary: Constitution - Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Factories Act, 1948, s. 46 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-F and 25-N - Whether the conditions imposed by the High Court while ordering absorptions of workmen who since were engaged in a statutory canteen ipso facto became the employees of the principal employer, that (a) for regularisation the maximum age-limit should be taken as the age of superannuation and, (e) the workmen who did not fulfill conditions for regularisation should be retrenched in terms of the provisions of Chap. V-B of the ID Act, 1947 are erroneous? - Under condition (a), the age eligibility for regularising the services of the workmen, the minimum and the maximum age-limits prescribed under the policy of the Corporation (18 years and 25 years respectively) has to be considered as at the time of their initial appointment - High Court not justified in holding that the maximum age referred to in Condition (a) meant the age of superannuation - Its open to the management to relax maximum age-limit in appropriate cases - The workmen who do not satisfy conditions (a) to (d) are not eligible for regularisation - Have to be dealt with under Condition (e) which incorporates benefits for unabsorbed workmen who would be rendered out of service - Once it is held that they are the employees of the management they can be retrenched only in accordance with the provisions of the ID Act, 1947 - The obligation to comply with Condition (e) is contingent upon the retrenchment of the workmen in accordance with law - The management is an industrial establishment to which Chap. V-B complies and if that be so, ss. 25-N and 25-H r/w s. 25-S is attracted - Condition (e) postulates that in the event of the management choosing to retrench the workmen who do not fulfill directions (a) to (d) of the conditions for regularisation, they shall be paid retrenchment compensation u/s. 25-N and their cases for re-employment should be considered u/s. 25-H of the ID Act, 1947 - Appeal allowed.
