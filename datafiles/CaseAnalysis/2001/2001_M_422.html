Collector of Central Excise, Meerut v Modi Rubber Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 430; (2002) 1 SCC 5; AIR 2001 SC 2913; 2001 (133) E.L.T. 515; JT 2001 (8) SC 421; 2001 (47) RLT 131; 2001(7) SCALE 91; 2001 (7) SLT 251; 2001 (7) Supreme 549

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Constitution - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 8(1) and 56A - Whether claim of respondent assessee under notification no. 95/83 is sustainable? - Idea underlying Notification No 95/83 is to check or minimise cascading effect of duties which are otherwise payable at various stages - It could not be underlying intention of notification to grant relief of duty on inputs as well as corresponding outputs - Exemption notification cannot be unduly stretched to produce unintended results in derogation of plain language employed therein - Impugned order of CEGAT is set aside - Appeal allowed.
