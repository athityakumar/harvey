Savani Roadlines v Sundaram Textiles Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 309; (2001) 5 SCC 625; AIR 2001 SC 2630; 2001 (44) ALR 509; 2001 (3) AWC 2161; [2001] 106 Comp Cas 659; 2001 (3) CompLJ 376; 2001 (3) CPJ 8A; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 6; JT 2001 (5) SC 425; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 123; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 830; 2001(4) SCALE 335; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 981; 2001 (5) SLT 189; 2001 (5) Supreme 129; 2001 (2) UC 279

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Consumer Protection - Whether an Insurance Company is a consumer and as such consumer can file a complaint before Consumer Forum? - Since there was no assignment but mere subrogation therefore Insurance Company would merely step into shoes of consumer and would be filing complaint on behalf of consumer - Held, in this case there was no assignment but a mere subrogation due to which complaint is maintainable - Appeal allowed.
