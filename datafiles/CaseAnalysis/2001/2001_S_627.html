Umashanker v State of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20723; (2001) 9 SCC 642; (2002) SCC (Cr) 758; AIR 2001 SC 3074; 2001 (4) Crimes 83; 2001 CRLJ 4696; 2001 (2) JCC 215; JT 2001 (8) SC 322; 2001 (3) KLT 68; 2002 (130) PLR 181; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 444; 2001(7) SCALE 37; 2001 (7) SLT 177; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 646; 2001 (7) Supreme 425

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Counterfeit currency - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 489B and 489C - Whether conviction of appellant for using counterfeit currency notes valid? - Held, no - Required mens rea not present - Without requisite mens rea, selling, buying or receiving from another person or otherwise trafficking in or using as genuine forged or counterfeit currency-notes or bank-notes, is not enough to constitute offence - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
