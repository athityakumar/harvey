Sunil Bajaj v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20721; (2001) 9 SCC 417; (2002) SCC (Cr) 608; AIR 2001 SC 3020; 2001 (4) CCR 131; 2001 (4) Crimes 97; 2001 CRLJ 4700; 2001 (2) JCC 262; JT 2001 (8) SC 465; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 401; 2001(7) SCALE 129; 2001 (7) SLT 220; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 51; 2001 (7) Supreme 456

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Dowry Death - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss .304-B, 306 and 498-A - Deceased was wife of appellant - She committed suicide by setting herself on fire, within 7 years of marriage - It was alleged that appellant used to force her to bring money from her parents - It was further alleged that appellant was having relations with girls of doubtful character - Whether appellant is liable to be convicted u/s. 304-B, IPC, 1860 ? - Held, no - Necessary ingredients of dowry death are not made out - Letters of deceased to her parents indicate that no demand was made for dowry - No evidence that deceased was subjected to cruelty or harassment soon before her death or in connection with demand of dowry not established - Considering gravity of punishment there is need for greater care and caution in scrutinizing available evidence before confirmation of conviction - Appeal allowed.
