Director General Civil Aviation v Manvirsingh (Dead), Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21488; 2001 (5) SLR 715

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Seniority, Ad Hoc

Summary: Service - Promotion as Aerodrome Officer - Inadequacy of requisite experience - Notice to parties -Respondent ‘M’/deceased was represented by his legal heirs - ‘M’ was not considered by appellant Authority for promotion to cadre of Aerodrome Officer on ground that he did not have requisite experience of three years in grade - Said ‘M’ approached Tribunal which held that respondent should be deemed to have been promoted as Aerodrome Officer with effect from 14-1-1977 and directed to work out consequential benefits that would flow there from - Appellant Authority alleged that Tribunal did not consider seniority as well as merit and benefit from 14-1-1977 could not have been given to respondent - Hence instant appeal.

Held, that Tribunal did not consider two elements that were adverted to by Court, namely, seniority as well as merit. Although direction was given by Court that his ad hoc service should be taken into consideration for purpose of seniority, it was also indicated that case should be examined on merit. That exercise had not been done at all and in those circumstances it was pointed out that benefit from 14-1-1977 could not have been given to respondent. Now it is brought to our notice that respondent had already been promoted as Aerodrome Officer with effect from 29-12-1980. Ali that needs to be done is to fix as from which date he was suitable to be appointed on that post and to accord appropriate ranking in seniority list. Exercise could be done only after hearing legal representatives of respondent and others who may be affected by such course of action. Part of order by which the HC directed to reckon his seniority from 14-1-1977 is set aside and it is made clear that appellant shall properly exercise its mind and fix his date of seniority in cadre of Aerodrome Officers after due notice to all parties concerned. As matter is against order on review, appeal becomes unnecessary in view of order passed in Civil Appeal. It is disposed of as unnecessary. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Authority should properly exercise its mind and fix his date of seniority in cadre of Aerodrome Officers after due notice to all parties concerned.
