Kashi Ram and others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 131; (2002) SCC (Cr) 68; (2002) 1 SCC 71; AIR 2001 SC 2902; AIR 2001 SCW 4350; 2002 (44) AllCriC 57; 2001 (4) CCR 221; 2001 (4) Crimes 344; 2002 (2) CRJ 432; JT 2001 (8) SC 650; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 556; 2001(7) SCALE 310; 2001 (5) SCJ 164; 2001 (7) SLT 481; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 263; 2001 (7) Supreme 710; 2002 (1) UC 53

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Under Exception II of Section 300, IPC, culpable homicide is not murder, if a person, in the exercise of the right of private defence, exceeds the power given to him by law and causes the death of the person against whom he has exercised such right, without pre-meditation and without any intention of doing more harm than is necessary, for the purpose of such defence. (Para 31)
