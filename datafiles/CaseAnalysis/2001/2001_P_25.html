Alpic Finance Limited v P. Sadasivan and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20283; (2001) 3 SCC 513; (2001) SCC (Cr) 565; AIR 2001 SC 1226; 2001 (1) CGLJ 503; 2001 (1) Crimes 293; 2001 CRLJ 1246; 2001 (1) JCC 179; JT 2001 (2) SC 588; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 756; 2001(2) SCALE 75; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1059; 2001 (2) SLT 202; 2001 (2) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Prevention of Corruption Act

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of Proceedings - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 93, 156(3), 200 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 406, 420, 423, 424 and 467 - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(2) - Whether High Court was justified in invoking powers u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 and setting aside proceedings pending in Trial Court on basis that dispute was of a civil nature and no criminal prosecution would lie? - Held, HC was perfectly justified in quashing proceedings - Relied upon Madhavrao Jiwajirao Scindia v. Sambhajirao Chandrojirao Angre, 1988 Indlaw SC 599 'If allegations in complaint are both of a civil wrong and a criminal offence, there would be certain situations where it would predominantly be a civil wrong and may or may not amount to a criminal offence' further Nagawwa v Veeranna Shivalingappa Konjalgi, 1976 Indlaw 621 'The Magistrate while issuing process against accused should satisfy himself as to whether allegations in complaint, if proved, would ultimately end in conviction of accused' - Appeal dismissed.
