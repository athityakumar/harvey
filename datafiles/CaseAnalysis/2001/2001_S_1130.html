Shri Bhagwan v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20087; (2001) 6 SCC 296; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1095; AIR 2001 SC 2342; AIR 2001 SCW 2189; 2001 (2) CCR 302; 2002 (1) CRJ 642; 2001 CRLJ 2925; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 14; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 695; 2001(4) SCALE 153; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 656; 2001 (4) SLT 237; 2001 (4) Supreme 180; 2001 (2) UC 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 392 and 397 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27 and 114 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 433-A and 57 - Murder - Five members of a family - Robbery - Recovery u/s. 27 of the Evidence Act, 1872 - Presumptions thereof - Held, appellant could not give an explanation as to how he came into possession of various gold ornaments and other articles belonging to victim and his family members and how he sustained injuries on his body and how his shirt became bloodstained - It is a fit case where presumption under Illustration (a) to s. 114 of Evidence Act could be drawn that appellant committed murders and robbery - (B) Imprisonment of life - Meaning of - Held, imprisonment of life means sentence covering whole of remaining period of convicted persons natural life - For a prisoners release, a Govt. order necessary - Death sentence commuted to sentence of imprisonment for life - Appellant directed not to be released from prison unless he had served out at least 20 years of imprisonment including period already undergone - Appeal disposed of.
