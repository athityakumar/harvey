Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut v Kisan Sahkari Chinni Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20013; (2001) 6 SCC 697; AIR 2001 SC 3379; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 523; [2002] 255 ITR 57; JT 2001 (7) SC 1; 2001 (46) RLT 370; 2001(5) SCALE 382; 2001 (6) SLT 19; 2002 (125) STC 432; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 7; 2001 (6) Supreme 241; [2002] 123 TAXMAN 223

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sheera Niyantran Adhiniyam, 1964

Summary: Excise - Uttar Pradesh Sheera Niyantran Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 8(4) and 8(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366 - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(d)(ii) - Whether tribunal erred in holding that in terms of s. 4(4)(d)(ii), assessable value in relation to excisable goods will not include amount of duty of excise, sales tax and 'other taxes' payable on such goods? - U/s. 4(4)(d)(ii) what is to be excluded from assessable value is amount of duty of excise, sales tax and 'other taxes' - Held, administrative charges would be covered u/s. 4(4)(d)(ii) as 'other taxes' because it is a compulsory exaction made under an enactment and, therefore, a duty or impost and such impost must be held to be in nature of a 'tax' covered by aforesaid provisions - No infirmity in tribunal's order - Appeals dismissed.
