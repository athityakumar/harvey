Takhaji Hiraji v Thakore Kubersing Chamansing and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20140; (2001) 6 SCC 145; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1070; AIR 2001 SC 2328; AIR 2001 SCW 2077; 2001 (2) CCR 201; 2001 CRLJ 2602; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 1; 2001 (2) JCC 82; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 415; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 725; 2001(3) SCALE 589; 2001 (4) SLT 202; 2001 (3) Supreme 690

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 109, 147, 148,149, 302, 304, 307, 324, 325 - Conviction - Challenged - Sustainability - There was clash between two communities with knives and sticks and three persons died of injuries and five persons injured from prosecution side and accused also were injured and were examined medico legally - FIR was lodged against accused 1 to 8 - Trial Court after elaborate trial acquitted 5 accused persons and convicted accused nos. 1,2 and 5 - Aggrieved accused nos. 1, 2 and 5 filed appeal in HC and was allowed by DB setting aside Trial Court order and acquitted accused holding that only interested witnesses and not independent witness were examined and accused persons also sustained injuries and exercised right of self defence - Aggrieved complainant/appellant filed instant appeal - Whether acquittal order of HC was justified - Held, HC had not appreciated evidence and no effort had been made by HC at marshalling evidence and assessing testimony of prosecution witnesses who were undoubtedly present in scene of offence having themselves suffered injuries from accused - SC by 3 judge bench decisions in Rajendra Singh & Ors. Vs. State of Bihar, 2000 Indlaw SC 354 and Ram Sunder Yadav & Ors. Vs. State of Bihar, 1998 Indlaw SC 836 and Vijayee Singh & Ors. Vs. State of U.P., 1990 Indlaw SC 795 held that Courts could not reject testimony of prosecution witnesses merely that they had failed to explain injuries on accused persons - HC was not right in acquitting accused stating that prosecution failed to explain injuries on accused - If witnesses already examined were reliable and testimony was unimpeachable Court could safely act upon it uninfluenced by factum of non-examination of other witnesses - In instant case presence of 5 prosecution witnesses was not doubted as they suffered grievous injuries in same incident and they had no prior enimity with accused and genesis of incident was brought out by these witnesses - Presence of prosecution party and accused party in chowk where incident happened was not disputed and was rightly inferred by Trial Court after appreciation of oral and circumstantial evidence - Trial Court had minutely scrutinised evidence of all eye-witnesses and found them consistent and reliable whereas HC made no effort in scrutinising and analysing ocular testimony of witnesses - Evidence of eye-witnesses was consistent and reliable in their narration of incident - Non-examination of other witnesses did not cast any infirmity in prosecution case - Justice had been made sterile by exaggerated adherence to rule of proof - Benefit of doubt should always be reasonable and not fanciful - Order of HC acquitting accused nos. 1, 2 and 5 was set aside and restored Trial Court order and sentences of accused were modified - Appeal disposed of.
