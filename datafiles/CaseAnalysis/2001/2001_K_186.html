Union Bank of India v Khader International Construction and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19951; (2001) 5 SCC 22; AIR 2001 SC 2277; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 119; 2001 (44) ALR 415; 2001 (3) AWC 2017; 2001 (7) CLC 899; [2001] 105 Comp Cas 856; JT 2001 (5) SC 218; 2001 (2) KLT 732; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 63; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 392; 2001(4) SCALE 56; 2001 (4) SCJ 159; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 580; 2001 (4) SLT 110; 2002 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 21; 2001 (4) Supreme 132

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 33, r. 1 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Whether a limited Co. is entitled to sue as an indigent person under O. 33, r. 1, CPC, 1908? - O. 33, r. 1 being benevolent provision, there is only a provision for deferred payment of court fees - Definition of term 'person' in General Clauses Act includes both natural and artificial persons - Held, word 'person' mentioned in O. 33 includes not only a natural person but other juridical persons also, hence, a public limited Co., which is otherwise entitled to maintain a suit as a legal person, can very well maintain an application under O. 33, r. 1, CPC, 1908 - Appeal dismissed.
