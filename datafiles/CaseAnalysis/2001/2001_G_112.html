Verigamto Naveen v Government of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19; (2001) 8 SCC 344; AIR 2001 SC 3609; 2002 AllCJ 300; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 319; JT 2001 (8) SC 29; 2001(6) SCALE 363; 2001 (3) SCJ 606; 2001 (6) SLT 624; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 112; 2001 (7) Supreme 170

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Legality, Terminate sub-lease

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Notification - Terminate sub-lease - Legality - Instant appeals were filed by appellants against order passed by HC. Held, there are at least three weighty reasons as to why period of sub-lease could not have been extended after expiry of period of original lease and they are in most of present cases, the interruptions in respect of which claim is made is for a period of about 10 months and in one other case an additional period of 6-1/2 months. In some cases lease having expired as early as in year 1995 or in others in 1998, it would not be appropriate to direct extension of lease in year 2001 particularly when sub-leases have expired as a result of which parties have to reestablish their infrastructure and put in great deal of logistical support though for a short period once over again, to work mines which will have a pernicious effect on mines and parties concerned. The claim for renewal of leases has been refused already as policy of Govt. is not to grant lease or sub-lease in favor of private parties. Now to ask to Govt. to enter into fresh contracts will be contrary to its policy. When several malpractices had been pointed out by House Committee, it would not be in public interest to extend period of lease which will perpetuate the same. Therefore, HC ought not to have exercised its discretion for extension of period of sub-lease. It would be appropriate to set aside order made by HC. It is unnecessary for us to decide same inasmuch as it would be appropriate for parties to work out their respective rights by making an appropriate claim in civil suit to be filed by each one of them. SC refused relief of restitution by way of extension of lease period without examining question as to whether there is breach of contract as a consequence of which party aggrieved is entitled to damages. That aspect is left open to be considered or be dealt with in civil suit irrespective of and uninfluenced by observations or findings of HC on this aspect. Appeals Partly allowed.
