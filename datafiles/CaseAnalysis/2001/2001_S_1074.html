Ravinder Kumar and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19840; (2001) 7 SCC 690; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1384; AIR 2001 SC 3570; AIR 2001 SCW 3366; 2001 (4) CCR 24; 2001 (4) Crimes 260; 2002 (1) CRJ 236; 2001 CRLJ 4242; JT 2001 (7) SC 377; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 227; 2001(6) SCALE 119; 2001 (4) SCJ 419; 2001 (6) SLT 471; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 463; 2001 (6) Supreme 549; 2001 (2) UC 631

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Murder - Appellants convicted by Trial Court and conviction confirmed by High Court - Conviction challenged - Whether delay of two days in lodging FIR fatal for prosecution? - Law has not fixed any time for lodging FIR - Hence a delayed FIR is not illegal - Held, no material delay in present case in facts and circumstances of a widow who lost her husband - Motive is necessary part of every criminal offence but if prosecution fails to prove motive but otherwise completely proves guilt of accused, conviction may be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
