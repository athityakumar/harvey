L. L. Sudhakar Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20043; (2001) 6 SCC 634; AIR 2001 SC 3205; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 80; JT 2001 (6) SC 410; 2001(5) SCALE 201; 2001 (3) SCJ 388; 2001 (5) SLT 812; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 383; 2001 (7) Supreme 411

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Andhra Pradesh Land-Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982

Summary: Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Land-Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982, ss. 8 and 17-A - First respondent, filed an application u/s. 8 in Special Court - Alleged that first appellant encroached upon Govt. land and sold them to respondents 2 to 15 - Special Court held that Govt. was owner of disputed land and that respondents were land grabbers and ordered them to be evicted from disputed land - Appellants who were not parties before Special Court, joined first appellant in challenging validity of order of Special Court - Held, appellants could have availed remedies of review u/s. 17-A of Act and suit for declaration of title and right, Single Judge ought not to have expressed any opinion on merits of case because after High Court has put its seal of approval on judgment and order of Special Court, result of review application and suit would become a foregone conclusion - Further held, having regard to provisions of sub-s. (2) of s. 8 r/w s. 15 of Act, no suit for title in respect of disputed land which is alleged to be a land grabbed by first appellant, could be entertained by Civil Court -Suit for declaration of title by appellants would not be maintainable - Appeal allowed.
