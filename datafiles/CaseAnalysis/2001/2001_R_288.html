Damodar Mangalji and Company and Others v Regional Director

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20496; (2001) 3 SCC 321; (2001) SCC (L&S) 582; AIR 2001 SC 1386; 2001 (1) CLR 957; 2001 (89) FLR 161; JT 2001 (3) SC 238; 2001 (34) LabIC 1131; 2001 (1) LLJ 1120; 2001 (2) LLN 629; 2001(2) SCALE 268; 2001 (2) SCJ 333; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 182; 2001 (2) SLR 14; 2001 (2) SLT 545; 2001 (2) Supreme 192

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Corporate - Insurance - Labour & Industrial Law - Mines & Minerals - Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(1) and (12) - Mines Act, 1952, s. 2(a)(i) - Whether the expression 'mine' used in s. 2(1) of the Employees' State Insurance Act has to be read along with the expressions 'in respect of' and would mean not only the area where extraction of ores takes place, but also the other offices and that the enactment itself intends to make a distinction as is clear from s. 2(12) which defines the expression 'factory', and mine which is subject to the operation of the Mines Act, 1952 is excluded from the purview of the Act? - Held, having interpreted the expression 'the appropriate Govt.' in the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 in Serajuddin & Co.'s case, wherein the crucial words that fell for interpretation were 'in relation to an industrial dispute concerning a banking or insurance Co. or mine or an oilfield or a major port' and it was held that the word 'mine' as used in s. 2(a)(i) of the ID Act referred to a mine as defined in the Mines Act and that a dispute with reference to the Head Office of the mine was not a dispute concerning a mine which must mean mine as defined under the Mines Act, which is identical with the expression 'the appropriate Govt.' as defined under the Act, the view taken by the High Court calls for no interference - Appeals dismissed.
