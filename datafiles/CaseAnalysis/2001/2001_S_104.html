Swapan Kumar Pal and Others v Samitabhar Chakraborty and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20122; (2001) 5 SCC 581; (2001) SCC (L&S) 880; AIR 2001 SC 2353; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 449; 2001 LabIC 2076; 2001 (3) LLN 5; 2001(4) SCALE 85; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 641; 2001 (4) SLR 654; 2001 (4) SLT 227; 2001 (4) Supreme 200

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Railway Establishment Manual, paras 213, 214 and 302 - Jammu and Kashmir Rules, rr. 15 and 23 - Whether services rendered by promotees on ad hoc basis in post of senior clerk can be allowed to be counted for purpose of their seniority in cadre of senior clerk? - Held, it is true that they had been continuing from their respective dates of ad hoc promotion till they were regularised, after being selected through due process - But that by itself cannot confer a right on them to claim ad hoc period of service to be tagged on for purpose of their seniority inasmuch as there is no provision which says that an employee on being regularly promoted, such regular promotion would date back to date of original promotion in cadre, which might have been on ad hoc basis - When service conditions are governed by a set of rules, in absence of any rules, it is difficult to hold that regular promotion would date back to date of ad hoc promotion itself - Appeal allowed.
