State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Lalsa Ram

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20262; (2001) 3 SCC 389; (2001) SCC (L&S) 593; AIR 2001 SC 1137; 2001 (2) AWC 1018; 2001 (1) CGLJ 424; 2001 (2) CLR 19; 2001 (89) FLR 998; JT 2001 (3) SC 242; 2001 (34) LabIC 1100; 2001 (2) LLJ 955; 2001(2) SCALE 221; 2001 (1) SCJ 474; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 108; 2001 (2) SLT 564; 2001 (2) Supreme 155; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1044

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Keywords: Efficiency, Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, Punjab Civil Services (Premature Retirement) Rules, 1975, Uttar Pradesh Vigilance Establishment Act, 1965, Punjab State Electricity Board Services (Premature Retirement) Regulations, 1982

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services (Premature Retirement) Rules, 1975, r. 3 - Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, r. 56(c) - Whether order of compulsory retirement of respondent was valid? - Held, r. 56(c) of U. P. Fundamental Rules r/w explanation (2) provides an authority to board with an absolute right to retire an employee on date on which he attains age of 50 years - In event same is not contra to conditions as embodied in r. 56 of fundamental rules, question of violation of any legal right of respondent herein does not and cannot arise - Appointing authority upon consideration of entire service record as required under rules and having formed its opinion that compulsory retirement of respondent being in public interest issued order and in wake of aforesaid, question of any interference of this court does not and cannot arise - Appeal allowed.
