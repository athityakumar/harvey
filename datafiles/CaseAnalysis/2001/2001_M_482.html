Commissioner of Income Tax v Mrs. Grace Collis and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19961; (2001) 3 SCC 430; AIR 2001 SC 1133; 2001 (2) CLC 430; 2001 (2) CompLJ 384; 2001 (166) CTR 201; [2001] 248 ITR 323; JT 2001 (3) SC 199; 2001 (1) KLT 913; 2001(2) SCALE 207; 2001 (1) SCJ 647; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 98; 2001 (2) SLT 409; 2001 (3) Supreme 124; [2001] 115 TAXMAN 326

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391(2),394 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s.45 - Scheme of Arrangement - Amalgamation of company - Sale of shares - Levy of capital gains tax - Assessees were shareholders of S Ltd - HC of Kerala sanctioned a Scheme of Arrangement u/ss.391(2),394 of 1956 Act - S Ltd/amalgamating company amalgamated with C Ltd/amalgamated company - Upon amalgamation, amalgamating company ceased to function and amalgamated company would take over all its business, assets and liabilities and carry on its business - Assessees sold 45318 shares of amalgamated company of face value of Rs.100 each which they acquired under scheme to B for aggregate sum of Rs.48,72,523 - Amalgamated company sold each share for Rs.107.50 - For Assessment Year 1976-77, Income Tax Officer(IO) levied capital gains tax upon assessees in respect of sale - Order of IO was confirmed by CIT(Appeals) - Tribunal uphold appellate order - HC decided that the taxing authorities could consider taxing the assessees on basis of transaction under share of Rs.100 was sold for Rs.107.50 - Hence instant appeal - Whether rights of assessees in shares in amalgamating company was extinguished upon amalgamation - Held, s.45 of 1961 Act stated that any profits or gains arose from transfer of a capital asset should chargeable to income tax of previous year in which transfer took place - No exchange was involved in transaction - Assessee could not said to have transferrd any property to anyone - When assessee was allotted shares of amalgamated company, assessee entitled to such allotment because of his holding 90 shares of amalgamating company - Holding of 90 shares in amalgamating company was a qualified condition entitled assessee to allotment of 45 shares in amalgamated company - Dissolution of amalgamating company deprived holding of 90 shares of that company of all value - Rights of assessees in shares in amalgamating company, stood extinguished upon amalgamation of amalgamating company with amalgamated company - Appeals allowed.
