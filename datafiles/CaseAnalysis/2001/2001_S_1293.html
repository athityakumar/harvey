Anil Ratan Sarkar and Others v State of W. B. and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20157; (2001) 5 SCC 327; (2001) SCC (L&S) 866; AIR 2001 SC 2164; 2001 (2) CLR 407; 2001 (89) FLR 1028B; JT 2001 (5) SC 99; 2001 LabIC 1736; 2001(3) SCALE 475; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 109; 2001 (2) SLR 787; 2001 (3) SLT 683; 2001 (4) Supreme 256B; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1765

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Administrative -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - GO - Difference in pay - Challenged - Validity - Appellants were laboratory assistants in non Govt. colleges and were paid allowances including DA in teaching staff category and earlier GO 21-3-1969 of State Govt. shifting them to non-teaching staff category was challenged by appellants by writ petitions and were allowed by single judge and confirmed by DB and SC by order dt.26-7-1994 - During pendency State Govt./respondent by GO dt. 27-7-1988 conferred teaching status to appellants but without any pay increase - In compliance to SC order State Govt./respondent issued circular dt.26-12-1994 allegedly showing two categories of graduate laboratory instructors - Challenged by appellants by writ petition in HC and was allowed by Single Judge holding that petitioners being Graduate Laboratory Instructors question of further classifying them did not and could not arise and relying annual report published by higher education department showing only one category quashed and set aside circular dt.26-12-1994 - Aggrieved State Govt./respondent filed appeal and was allowed by DB holding that circular was not contrary as appellants were given teaching status - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of DB was justified - Held, respondent/State Govt. failed to produce any documentary proof to show existence of two categories inspite of several opportunities and contrary only one pay scale mentioned in report published by higher education department as rightly relied upon and recorded by Single Judge - By GO dt. 2 -7-1984Physical Instructors had been brought into single scale and that petitioners were entitled to said scale without any distinction between Graduates and Non- Graduates and Physical Instructors - Petitioners/appellants as given teaching status salaries should also be paid as per teaching category - Payment of salaries to teaching staff in non teaching category was bad in law - Teacher could not be paid non teacher salary - Administrative could not infiltrate on to arena which stands covered by judicial orders - Order of single judge was restored and order of DB was set aside - Appeals allowed.
