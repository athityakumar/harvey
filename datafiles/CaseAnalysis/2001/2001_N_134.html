M. D., Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation v Neethivilangan, Kumbakonam

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 397; (2001) 9 SCC 99; (2002) SCC (L&S) 40; AIR 2001 SC 2309; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 135; 2001 (90) FLR 27; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 394; 2001 LabIC 1801; 2001 (1) LLJ 1706; 2001 (3) LLN 34; 2001(3) SCALE 702; 2001 (3) SCJ 250; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 499; 2001 (7) SLR 1; 2001 (4) SLT 56; 2001 (4) Supreme 91

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33, 33(2),33(1)(b) and 33(2)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether the respondent is deemed to have been in service continuously since 5-03-1984 onwards and deemed to be discharging his functions as an employee of the respondent and he is entitled to all arrears of salary with annual increments and all attendant and concomitant benefits? - Employer has the discretion to initiate a departmental inquiry and pass an order of dismissal or discharge against the workman the order remains in an inchoate state till the employer obtains order of approval from the Tribunal - By passing the order of discharge or dismissal de facto relationship of employer and employee may be ended but not the de jure relationship for that could happen only when the Tribunal accords its approval - The relationship of employer and employee is not legally terminated till approval of discharge or dismissal is given by the Tribunal - In a case where the Tribunal refuses to accord approval to the action taken by the employer the employer is bound to treat the employee as continuing in service and give him all the benefits - The employer was duty bound to treat the employee as continuing in service and pay him his wages for the period - High Court committed no illegality in reinstating the respondent and pay him the back wages - The appeal is dismissed .
