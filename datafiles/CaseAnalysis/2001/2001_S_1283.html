 Somaiya Organics v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.S.M. Quadri, M.B. Shah, Ruma Pal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 462; (2001) 5 SCC 519; AIR 2001 SC 1723; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 88; 2001 ALJ 1109; 2001 (2) ARBLR 667; 2001 (2) CompLJ 361; 2001 (170) CTR 81; 2001 (130) E.L.T. 3; [2001] 251 ITR 20; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 319; 2001(3) SCALE 412; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 33; 2001 (3) SLT 799; 2001 (123) STC 623; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 61; 2001 (3) Supreme 458; 2002 (74) TTJ 391

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Bihar Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1981, Madras Panchayats Act, 1958, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Excise (Malt Liquors) Act, 1890, Excise Act

Summary: Excise (Malt Liquors) Act, 1890 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 162 and 265 - U.P. Excise Act, 1910 - Whether vend fee which had been levied by appropriate State enactments, but not collected by reasons of orders of Court or otherwise, can be collected now when said provisions by judgment D/-25-10-1989 have been held to be invalid prospectively? - Prospective overruling, despite terminology, is only a recognition of principle that court moulds reliefs claimed to meet justice of case - Justice not in its logical but in its equitable sense - Held, principle of unjust enrichment cannot be extended to give a right to state to recover or realise vend fee after statute has been struck down and states have been specifically asked to restrained from enforcing said levy any further - Vend fee realized by states is not to be refunded to appellants and, at same time, State cannot collect any vend fee for period prior to 25-10-1989 or thereafter - C.A. No. 4093 of 1991 allowed - Civil Appeal No. 2853 of 2001 dismissed.
