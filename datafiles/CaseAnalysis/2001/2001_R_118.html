Leela Soni and Others v Rajesh Goyal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19837; (2001) 7 SCC 494; AIR 2001 SC 3601; 2002 AllCJ 200; 2002 (1) CGLJ 225; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 25; JT 2001 (7) SC 212; 2001(6) SCALE 60; 2001 (6) SLT 373; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 504; 2001 (6) Supreme 764; 2001 (2) UC 591

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1) and 13, cl. (a), (o) - Whether High Court exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering with findings of fact recorded by first appellate court? - S. 103 authorises HC to determine any issue which is necessary for disposal of second appeal provided evidence on record is sufficient - Both issues relating to cls. (a) and (o) of s. 12(1) of 1961 Act, on account of its erroneous approach first appellate court did not determine relevant issues - Held, HC was well within its jurisdiction in recording findings of fact for which evidence was on record, as s. 103 CPC empowers HC to determine such issues of fact - Appeal dismissed.
