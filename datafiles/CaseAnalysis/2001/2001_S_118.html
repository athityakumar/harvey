Pavanendra Narayan Verma v Sanjay Gandhi P. G. I. of Medical Sciences and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20544; (2002) SCC (L&S) 170; (2002) 1 SCC 520; AIR 2002 SC 23; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 81; 2002 (1) AWC 42; 2002 (1) CLR 17; 2002 (100) FJR 64; 2002 (92) FLR 349; JT 2001 (9) SC 420; 2002 (35) LabIC 113; 2002 (1) LLJ 690; 2002 (1) LLN 45; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 151; 2001(8) SCALE 8; 2002 (1) SLJ 336; 2001 (8) SLR 722; 2001 (7) SLT 725; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 41; 2001 (8) Supreme 409; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 28

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination of temporary employee - Probation period - Whether order of termination was punitive and cast a stigma on appellant? - One of judicially evolved tests to determine whether in substance an order of termination is punitive, is to see whether prior to termination, there was,(a) a full scale formal inquiry (b) into allegations involving moral turpitude or misconduct (c) which culminated in a finding of guilt - If all three factors are present, termination has been held to be punitive, irrespective of form of termination order - Whenever a probationer challenges his termination, court's first task will be to apply test of stigma or 'form' test - If order survives this examination, 'substance' of termination will have to be found out - Generally when a probationer's appointment is terminated, it means that probationer is unfit for job, whether by reason of misconduct or ineptitude, whatever language used in termination order may be - Although strictly speaking, stigma is implicit in termination, a simple termination is not stigmatic - Held, use of word 'unsatisfactory work and conduct' in termination order will not amount to a stigma - An employer is entitled to satisfy itself as to competence of a probationer to be confirmed in service and for this purpose satisfy itself fairly as to truth of any allegation that may have been made about employee - Termination not punitive and upheld - Appeal dismissed.
