National Hydroelectric Power Corporation Limited v Shri Bhagwan and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20578; (2001) 8 SCC 574; (2002) SCC (L&S) 21; AIR 2001 SC 3309; 2001 (4) AWC 3053; 2001 (3) CLR 606; 2001 (91) FLR 259; JT 2001 (7) SC 515; 2001 (34) LabIC 3360; 2001 (2) LLJ 1243; 2001(6) SCALE 228; 2002 (1) SLJ 86; 2001 (4) SLR 272; 2001 (6) SLT 545; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 44; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 18; 2001 (6) Supreme 883

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Transfer - Respondent transferred to project at Itanagar - Whether High Court was justified in interfering with impugned orders of transfer? - Held, no Govt. servant or employee of public undertaking has any legal right to posted forever at anyone particular place since transfer of a particular employee appointed to class of category of transferable posts from one place to other is not only an incident, but a condition of service, necessary to in public interest and efficiency in public administration - Unless an order of transfer is shown to be an outcome of mala fide exercise of power or stated to be in violation of statutory provisions prohibiting any such transfer, courts or tribunals cannot interfere with such orders as a matter of routine, as though they are Appellate Authorities substituting their own decision for that of management, as against such orders passed in interest of administrative exigencies of service concerned - In facts of present cases, at any rate, no such result is bound to occur since project undertaken to which respondents have been transferred is itself a new one and, therefore, there is no rhyme or reason in alleged grievance - Appeal allowed.
