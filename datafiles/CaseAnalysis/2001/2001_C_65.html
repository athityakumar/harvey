Hemant Dhasmana v Central Bureau of Investigation and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20019; (2001) 7 SCC 536; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1280; AIR 2001 SC 2721; AIR 2001 SCW 3064; 2001 ALJ 2045; 2001 (3) CCR 183; 2001 (4) Crimes 183; 2002 (1) CRJ 345; 2001 CRLJ 4190; 2001 (2) JCC 109; JT 2001 (6) SC 473; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 830; 2001(5) SCALE 312; 2001 (5) SLT 855; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 646; 2001 (6) Supreme 217; 2001 (2) UC 407

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Prevention of Corruption Act, Delhi Police Establishment Act

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 401 and Chapter XII- Further investigation - After the submission of final report - Powers of Magistrate - Held, when report is filed u/sub-s. 2 of s. 173, Magistrate has to deal with it by bestowing his judicial consideration - If report is to effect that allegations in original complaint were found true in investigation, or that some other accused and/or some other offences were also detected, court has to decide whether cognizance of offences should be taken or not on strength of that report - But when report is against allegations contained in complaint and concluded that no offence committed by any person, it is open to court to accept report after hearing complainant at whose behest investigation had commenced - If court feels on a perusal of such a report that alleged offences have in fact been committed by some persons court has power to ignore contrary conclusions made by investigating officer in final report - It is open to court to independently apply its mind to facts emerging there from and it can even take cognizance of offences which appear to it to have been committed, in exercise of its power u/s. 190(1)(b) of Code - Special Judge or Magistrate could not direct that a particular police officer or even an officer of a particular rank should conduct such further investigation - It is not within province of Magistrate while exercising power u/s. 173(8) to specify any particular officer to conduct such investigation, not even to suggest rank of officer who should conduct such investigation - (B) Whether word Police in Chapter XII would include CBI? - Further held, word Police in Chapter XII would include CBI established under Delhi Special Police Establishment Act - Appeal allowed.
