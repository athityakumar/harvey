M. L. Prabhakar v Rajiv Singal

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20361; (2001) 2 SCC 355; AIR 2001 SC 522; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 24; 2001 (43) ALR 488; 2001 (89) DLT 231; JT 2001 (1) SC 223; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 91; 2001(1) SCALE 54; 2001 (1) SLT 282; 2001 (1) Supreme 138; 2001 (1) UC 309

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(e) - Bona fide requirement - Other accommodation - Whether other accommodations of landlord can be considered to be reasonably suitable residential accommodation? - If landlord wishes to live with comfort in a house of his own, law does not command or compel him to squeeze himself tightly into lesser premises protecting tenant's occupancy - Suitability has to be seen from convenience of landlord and his family members and on basis of totality of circumstances including their profession, vocation, style of living, habits and background - Held, it cannot at all be said that rooms which are available are reasonably suitable residential accommodation - It could not have been intention behind rent control law to compel landlord in such facts and circumstances to shift to a different house and locality so as to permit tenant to continue to live in tenanted premises - Alternate premises cannot be said to be reasonably suitable alternate accommodation - Appeal dismissed.
