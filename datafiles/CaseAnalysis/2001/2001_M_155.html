Mahendra and Mahendra Paper Mills Limited v Mahindra and Mahindra Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19798; (2002) 2 SCC 147; AIR 2002 SC 117; AIR 2001 SCW 4679; 2002 (46) ALR 228; 2002 (3) Bom.C.R. 686; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 228; JT 2001 (9) SC 525; 2002 (24) PTC 121; 2002 (1) RAJ 56; 2001(8) SCALE 174; 2001 (5) SCJ 329; 2001 (8) SLT 12; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 225; 2001 (8) Supreme 443

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Intellectual Property - Corporate - Companies Act, 1913 - Companies Act, 1956 - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Copyright Act, 1957 - Single Judge injuncting defendant from using the name 'Mahendra and Mahendra' - Whether there is as a result of misrepresentation a real likelihood of confusion or deception of the public and consequent damage to the plaintiff? - Court upheld judgment of Trial Court and Division Bench of High Court that plaintiff has established a prima facie case and irreparable prejudice in its favour which calls for passing an order of interim injunction restraining the defendant-company which is yet to commence its business from utilising the name of 'Mahendra' or 'Mahendra and Mahendra' for the purpose of its trade and business - Appeal dismissed.
