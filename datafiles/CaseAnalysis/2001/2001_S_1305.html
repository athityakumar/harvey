Kamaladevi Agarwal v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20719; (2002) SCC (Cr) 200; (2002) 1 SCC 555; AIR 2001 SC 3846; 2001 (4) CCR 187; 2001 (4) Crimes 323; 2001 CRLJ 4733; 2001 (2) JCC 352; JT 2001 (9) SC 162; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 522; 2001(7) SCALE 289; 2001 (5) SCJ 260; 2001 (7) SLT 377; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 284; 2001 (7) Supreme 627

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 465, 468 and 471 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Inherent power - Appellant filed complaint against respondents - Summons issued - Statement of witnesses recorded by Magistrate - High Court quashed proceedings - Whether HC right in quashing proceedings against respondents? - Appellant contended that merely because civil action is pending between parties can be no ground to quash proceedings - Inherent powers of quashing proceedings at initial stage should be exercised sparingly and only where allegations made in complaint or FIR even if taken it at face value and accepted in entirety do not prima facie disclose commission of an offence - Held, perused record including opinion of expert and his deposition and prima facie found that respondents were guilty for offence for which process was issued against them - HC not justified in quashing proceedings initiated against respondents - Appeal allowed.
