T. Phungzathang v Hangkhanlian and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20003; (2001) 8 SCC 358; AIR 2001 SC 3924; JT 2001 (7) SC 439; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 553; 2001(5) SCALE 599; 2001 (6) SLT 213; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 256; 2001 (6) Supreme 564

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 123, 81(1), 81(3), 83(1)(c), 83(3) and 86(1) - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, r. 94-A - Election Petition challenging declaration of results made in favour of respondent 1 as elected candidate - Respondent 1 alleged in election petition and supplied to him, was not duly verified and attested - High Court dismissed election petition u/s. 86(1) of Act on ground of non-compliance with sub-section (3) of s. 81 r/w s. 83(1)(c) of Act - Challenged - Only lacuna pointed out by contesting respondent in his application was that copy supplied to him did not contain verification or affirmation made by Oath Commissioner or prescribed authority as required in Form 25 and r. 94-A - Whether this omission as pointed out by respondent 1 in his petition, ipso facto entail dismissal of election petition u/s. 86(1) of Act? - Expression 'copy' in s. 81(3) of Act means a copy which is substantially same as original, variation if any from original should not be vital in nature or should not be such that can possibly mislead a reasonable person in meeting allegation (b) if copy differs in material particulars from original same cannot be cured after period of limitation - Held, no because by such omission copy supplied will not cease to be a 'true copy' and there is no possibility of any prudent person being in any manner misled in defending himself or being prejudiced in defence of his case - Further, such omissions are only curable irregularities - Appellant had few days before starting of arguments supplied fresh copies of affidavit containing verification as found in original affidavit in its entirety - Defects pointed out in petition being curable, have been cured - Facts of present appeal are fully covered by Constitution Bench judgments of Supreme Court in Jacob case 1999 Indlaw SC 927 - Appeal allowed.
