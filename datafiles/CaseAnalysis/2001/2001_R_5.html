H. F. Sangati v R. G. High Court of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20497; (2001) 3 SCC 117; (2001) SCC (L&S) 534; AIR 2001 SC 1148; 2001 (1) CLR 1033; 2001 (89) FLR 231; JT 2001 (3) SC 145; 2001 LabIC 1104; 2001(2) SCALE 195; 2001 (1) SCJ 492; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 83; 2001 (2) SLT 477; 2001 (3) Supreme 174

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Karnataka Civil Services (Probation) Rules, 1977, r. 6 - Karnataka Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1957 - Appellants discharged from service during period of probation - No opportunity granted before discharging - Whether there was non-compliance with the principles of natural justice? - Held, r. 6 contemplates a probationer being discharged from service on one or more of following grounds : (i) in terms of a condition imposed by rules, (ii) in terms of order of appointment, or (iii) on account of unsuitability of appointee for service or post - R. 6(2) requires an order discharging probationer to indicate grounds for discharge - It also provides that such indication of grounds for discharge in order would not require any formal proceeding under Rules, 1957 - Impugned order of discharge has been passed in strict compliance with requirements of r. 6 - It does not cast any stigma on appellants nor is it punitive - Thus, no requirement to comply with principles of natural justice much less to be preceded by any formal proceedings of enquiry before making order - Appeal dismissed.
