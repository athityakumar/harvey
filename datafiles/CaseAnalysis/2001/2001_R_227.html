Sugarbai M. Siddiq and Others v Ramesh S. Hankare (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19826; (2001) 8 SCC 477; AIR 2001 SCW 4072; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 146; JT 2001 (8) SC 239; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 483; 2001(6) SCALE 593; 2001 (7) SLT 31; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 390; 2001 (7) Supreme 337

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Constitution - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Appellants are landlords of premises and respondents are LRs. of original tenant - Appellants filed a suit for eviction on ground of non-payment of rent - Respondents stated that they did not receive notice; that rent was sent by money order but appellants refused to accept same - On consideration of evidence on record, oral and documentary, trial court found that amount was not paid within permissible period and decreed suit for eviction - Appeal was dismissed by appellate court - High Court set aside order of appellate court confirming order of trial court - Whether HC in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 227 ought not to have disturbed finding of fact? - Held, in an application u/art. 227, HC has to see whether lower court/tribunal has jurisdiction to deal with matter and if so, whether impugned order is vitiated by procedural irregularity - Adverse inference drawn by trial court and appellate court remains unrebutted - There was no other material to justify interference by HC - Therefore, finding recorded by HC cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
