Syed Rahimudin v Director General, C. S. I. R. and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20438; (2001) 9 SCC 575; AIR 2001 SC 2418; 2001 (2) AWC 1247; 2001 (2) CLR 36; 2001 (89) FLR 427; JT 2001 (3) SC 609; 2001 LabIC 2367; 2001 (2) LLJ 1246; 2002 (4) SLR 165; 2001 (5) SLT 342; 2001 (4) Supreme 396; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1541

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules - Delinquent prayed for production of certain documents - Enquiring Officer directed departmental authorities to give copies of those documents to delinquent - Some of those documents were not produced - On basis of materials produced Enquiring Officer passed order - Whether tribunal was justified in holding that there has been no invalidity in inquiry proceeding nor there has been a violation of principles of natural justice? - It is well settled that a conclusion or a finding of fact arrived at in a disciplinary inquiry can be interfered with by court only when there is no materials for said conclusion or that on materials, conclusion cannot be that of a reasonable man - Non-production of certain documents cannot be held to be a denial of reasonable opportunity to delinquent in making his defence - Enquiring Officer has dealt with articles of charge chronologically and relevant materials on basis of which ultimate conclusion is arrived at - Held, no legal infirmity with impugned order of tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
