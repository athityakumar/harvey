K. Duraisamy and Another v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20330; (2001) 2 SCC 538; AIR 2001 SC 717; 2001 (42) ALR 584; 2001 (1) ESC 164; JT 2001 (2) SC 48; 2001(1) SCALE 345; 2001 (2) SCJ 89; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 490; 2001 (2) SCW 48; 2001 (1) SLT 696; 2001 (1) Supreme 434; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 817

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4) - Admission - selection of candidates - Validity - Govt. issued order laying down procedure for selection of candidates for admission to Post- graduate Diploma, and Higher Speciality courses - After conduct of written Entrance Examination appellants were not selected due to a particular understanding of Orders of Govt. and stipulations contained in Prospectus relating to allocation of seats for in-service candidates and non-service candidates in manner by which claims of in-service candidates based on merit on basis of marks came to be ignored in respect of 50% of seats allocated as Open Quota by confining them exclusively to non-service candidates and considering claims of service candidates like appellants only in respect of 50% allocated to and reserved for service candidates - Appellants and others filed writ petitions before HC - Single Judge, while allowing writ petitions held that reservation of 50% of seats for non-service candidates had to be given effect to or worked out by selecting candidates from in-service and non-service, on basis of merit in first instance and thereafter 50% seats reserved for in-service candidates should be filled up by in-service candidates who could not gain selection on basis of merit as against the other 50% earmarked as open - Appeals filed by State came up subsequently before another DB and finding themselves unable to agree with order of dismissal of the earlier appeals, matters were referred to a larger Bench - DB was of the view that the decision of Single Judge was not correct - Full Bench reversed judgment of learned Single Judge and dismissed Writ Petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Full Bench was justified in dismissing Writ Petitions - Held, Scheme was not by way of a mere reservation but was one of classification of sources from which admissions have to be accorded, fixation of respective quota for such classified groups, principles at times applied in construing provisions relating to reservation simpliciter would have no relevance or application - Fixation of quota in given case could not be said to be same as mere reservation and whenever quota was fixed or provided for one or more of classified group or category, candidates falling in or answering description of different classified groups in whose favour respective quota was fixed have to confine their respective claims against quota fixed for each of such category, with no one in one category having any right to stake claim against quota earmarked for other class or category - Hence, Full Bench had correctly come to conclusion that scheme adopted for selection of candidates for admissions in question provided for definite and fixed quota for respective classified sources of admission and reasons assigned therefor do not suffer from any infirmity - Appeals dismissed.
