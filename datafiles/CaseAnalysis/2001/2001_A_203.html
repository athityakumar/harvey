Antonysami v Arulanandam Pillai (D) by Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 137; (2001) 9 SCC 658; AIR 2001 SC 2967; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 370; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 826; 2001(7) SCALE 526; 2001 (7) SLT 525; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 1; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 534; 2001 (7) Supreme 851

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: If, under Article 136 of Limitation Act, 1963 a decree-holder executes a decree after the expiry of the period of limitation prescribed under the Act then, the execution petition becomes time barred unless, in the decree itself, the right of the decree-holder to execute a decree depends on happening of a certain event, or on fulfillment of certain other conditions by the parties in the case or, by an external agency.
