Ashok Yeshwant Badev v Surendra Madhavrao Nighojakar and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20687; (2001) 3 SCC 726; AIR 2001 SC 1315; 2001 (2) AWC 1283; 2001 (2) BC 1; 2001 (1) CCR 358B; 2001 (2) CLC 439; [2001] 105 Comp Cas 167; 2001 (2) Crimes 7; 2001 CRLJ 1674; 2001 (1) JCC 298; JT 2001 (3) SC 508; 2001 (2) KLT 28; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 165; 2001(2) SCALE 472; 2001 (2) SCJ 385; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 426; 2001 (2) SLT 669; 2001 (2) Supreme 340; 2001 (1) UC 538

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Banking Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments Laws (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 138 - Notaries Act, 1952 - Banking Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments Laws (Amendment) Act, 1988 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Sale-purchase of land part payment through post-dated cheque - Cheque presented by bearer to bank for encashment after expiry of six months from date it was made over by accused to complainant, though within a period of six months from date mentioned on it - Dishonour of cheque - Complaint filed - Challenged by accused - Whether period of six months should be reckoned from date mentioned on cheque or date when it was made over by drawer to drawee? - Held, six months period shall be reckoned from date mentioned on face of cheque and not any earlier date on which cheque was made over by drawer to drawee - Appeal dismissed.
