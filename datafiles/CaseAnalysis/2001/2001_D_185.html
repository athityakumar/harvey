Hindustan Lever Limited v Director General (Investigation and Registration) and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20355; (2001) 2 SCC 474; AIR 2001 SC 661; 2001 (2) CLC 135; 2001 (1) CompLJ 381; 2001 (1) CPJ 12A; 2001 (1) CPJ(Sc) 12; JT 2001 (3) SC 37; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 718; 2001(1) SCALE 219; 2001 (1) SCJ 693; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 318; 2001 (1) SLT 529; 2001 (1) Supreme 301

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; MRTP; Trade

Keywords: Trade Practice, Dealers, Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Commission Regulations, 1974

Summary: MRTP - Monopolies And Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 2, 10, 37 and 38 - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Commission Regulations, 1974, regn. 58 - Commission - Appellant indulging in restrictive trade practices - Whether order of Commission was justified and any question of law really arises for consideration? - Court held, it was complaint by Jain General Stores which had been filed with Commission and appellant should have been put to notice thereof but this was not done, therefore, no order could be passed against the appellant in relation to the termination of its agreement with Jain General Stores, hence order of Commission is contrary to law - Appeal allowed.
