Management of Som Vihar Apartment Owners Housing Maintenance Society Limited v Workmen C/O. Indian Engineering and General Mazdoor

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20500; (2002) 9 SCC 652; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1099; AIR 2002 SC 2530; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 67; 2002 (35) LabIC 2468; 2001 (1) LLJ 1413; 2001 (4) SLR 649; 2001 (3) SLT 219; 2001 (4) Supreme 559

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - Whether workmen are entitled to dearness allowance, house rent allowance, conveyance allowance and uniforms and if so, to what extent and from which date directions are necessary in this respect? - Held, service rendered by a domestic servant purely in a personal or domestic matter or even in a casual way would fall outside definition s. 2(j) - Whole purpose of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 is to focus on resolution of industrial disputes and regulation will not meddle with every little carpenter or a blacksmith, a cobbler or a cycle repairer who come outside idea of industry and industrial dispute even when they carry on work in respect of one or many masters - When personal services are rendered to members of a society and that society is constituted only for purposes of those members to engage services of such employees, its activity should not be treated as an industry nor are they workmen - Appeal allowed.
