M. Abbas v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21090; (2001) 10 SCC 103; JT 2001 (4) SC 92; 2001 (1) UC 638

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4(1), 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Appellant convicted u/s. 5(1)(d) r/w s. 5(2) - (A) Conviction challenged - Where an accused sets up a defence or offers an explanation, it is well settled that he is not required to prove his defence beyond a reasonable doubt but only by preponderance of probabilities - Allegation that appellant accepted amount as bribe to process his refund application is true but court cannot convict an accused only on such probability or suspicion, howsoever strong it may be - 'Between may be true and must be true, there is a long distance to travel' and in this case prosecution failed to travel that distance through any unimpeachable evidence - Case of prosecution has not been established beyond a reasonable doubt - (B) Presumption u/s. 4(1) - Offence u/s. 161, IPC, 1860 - Held, presumption u/s. 4(1) in reference to an offence u/s. 161, IPC, 1860 is a rebuttable presumption - Onus can be said to have been duly discharged by appellant, more particularly, when prosecution did not lead any evidence to show as to who made payment - Appeal allowed.
