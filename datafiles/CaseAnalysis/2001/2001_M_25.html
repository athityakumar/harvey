Collector of Customs, Bombay v M. J. Exports Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20031; (2001) 6 SCC 756; AIR 2001 SC 2889; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 514; JT 2001 (6) SC 495; 2001 (46) RLT 130; 2001(5) SCALE 307; 2001 (5) SLT 875; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 564; 2001 (6) Supreme 184

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Trade

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Customs - Prohibition Act - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28(1), 111(o), 112(a) and 113(d) - Whether showcause notice which was issued by appellant to respondent beyond period of one year of import of items in question is covered by provisions of s. 28(1) proviso of Customs Act, 1962 and, therefore, within time? - Any item which was imported under open general licence which fell in category of life saving drugs or medicines or equipment clearly implied that import was for India and not for being exported to another country - There was an obligation on respondent not to claim exemption on import, if respondent intended to export life - saving equipment to Russia - Held, notice is covered by s. 28(1) proviso and therefore within time - Appeal allowed.
