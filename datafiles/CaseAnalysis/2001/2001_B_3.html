N. R. Narayan Swamy v B. Francis Jagan

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19850; (2001) 6 SCC 473; AIR 2001 SC 2469; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 44; 2001 (44) ALR 762; JT 2001 (6) SC 77; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 9; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 169; 2001(4) SCALE 621; 2001 (3) SCJ 67; 2001 (5) SLT 532; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 109; 2001 (5) Supreme 594

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 23, r. 1 - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, ss. 45 and 62 - (A) Whether second suit is is not maintainable when respondent contended that previous suit is withdrawn and is also barred by s. 45 of 1961 Act, when previous proceedings for taking possession of premises were not pressed and stood disposed of without deciding any issue? - S. 45 of Rent Act makes clear that that fresh application under Rent Act could be summarily rejected only if (i) proceedings are between same parties or under whom they or any of them claim, and (ii) substantially same issues as have been finally decided in a former proceeding under Act are raised - Thus section as such, incorporates principles of res judicata - This section would have no application as previous proceedings for taking possession of premises were not pressed and stood disposed of without deciding any issue - (B) Whether O. 23, r. 1 sub-r. (4) CPC is applicable to facts of present case? - Held, aforesaid Rule would have no application in a proceeding initiated for recovering suit premises on ground of bona fide requirement which is a recurring cause - O. 23, r. 1(4)(b) precludes plaintiff from instituting any fresh suit in respect of such subject-matter or such part of claim which plaintiff has withdrawn - Further held, in a suit for eviction of a tenant under Rent Act on ground of bona fide requirement even though premises remain same, subject-matter which is cause of action may be different - Ground for eviction in subsequent proceedings is based upon requirement on date of said suit even though it relates to same property - Appeal allowed.
