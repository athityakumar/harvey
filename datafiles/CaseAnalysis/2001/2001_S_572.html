 Arnit Das v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K.T. Thomas, R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19841; (2001) 7 SCC 657; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1393; AIR 2001 SC 3575; 2001 (45) ALR 188; 2001 (3) CCR 230; 2001 (4) Crimes 257; 2001 CRLJ 4238; JT 2001 (7) SC 157; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 188; 2001(5) SCALE 500; 2001 (6) SLT 198; 2001 (6) Supreme 461; 2001 (2) UC 360

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, s. 32 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Whether date of commission of offence or date on which accused first appears in enquiry proceedings is relevant for purposes of determining whether or not an accused was a juvenile under 1986 Act? - Held, in view of findings recorded in an enquiry conducted u/s. 32 of 1986 Act, that on date of offence accused-petitioner was not a juvenile for purposes of 1986 Act, which finding has been affirmed right up to this court, it is of no consequence, insofar as this petition is concerned, as to whether crucial date for purposes of 1986 Act is date of commission of offence or date when accused first appears in court in enquiry proceedings - Insofar as this petition is concerned, reference is only of an academic interest - Decline to answer an academic question only - Review Petition dismissed.
