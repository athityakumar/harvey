Harshad S. Mehta v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.P. Bharucha, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 60; (2001) 8 SCC 257; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1447; AIR 2001 SC 3774; 2001 (4) CCR 1; 2001 (2) CLC 1422; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 365; 2001 (4) Crimes 229; 2001 CRLJ 4259; JT 2001 (7) SC 390; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 109; 2001(6) SCALE 182; 2001 (3) SCJ 439; 2001 (6) SLT 389; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 577; 2001 (6) Supreme 694

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: The Special Courts Act, 1979, Bengal Suppression of Terrorist Outrages (Supplementary) Act,1932

Summary: The Special Court established under the Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 is a Court of exclusive jurisdiction. Sections 6 and 7 confer on that court wide powers. It is a court of original criminal jurisdiction and has also the powers of such a Court under the Code including those of Sections 306 to 308.
