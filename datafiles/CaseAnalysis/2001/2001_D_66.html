Shibu Soren v Dayanand Sahay and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Shivaraj V. Patil, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20082; (2001) 7 SCC 425; AIR 2001 SC 2583; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 107; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 107; JT 2001 (5) SC 504; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 124; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 493; 2001(4) SCALE 395; 2001 (4) SCJ 338; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1020; 2001 (5) SLT 295; 2001 (5) Supreme 193

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, Durgah Khawaja Saheb Act, 1955, Civil Services Rules, Mysore Town Municipalities Act, 1951, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, Jharkhand Area Autonomous Council Act, 1994, Bihar and Orissa Mining Settlement Act, 1920

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 102(1)(a) and 191(1)(a) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80 and 81 - Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Whether appellant in present case held an office of profit under State Government at relevant time, date of scrutiny of nomination papers, and whether that office has not been declared by Parliament by law not to disqualify its holder? - In order to attract disqualification contained in art. 102(1)(a), a person must not only be holding an office but that office must be an office of profit and should be under Govt. and should be an office other than an office declared by competent legislature by law, not to disqualify its holder - In common parlance expression profit connotes an idea of some pecuniary gain - Held, appellant, as Chairman of Interim JAA Council was in receipt of pecuniary gain in form of honorarium and he, thus, held an office of profit - Appellant was nominated appointed (nominated) by State Govt. and it was State Govt. which had right to remove or dismiss holder of that office besides controlling manner of functioning of Interim Council and providing funds for Interim JAA Council out of which honorarium of Rs. 1750 per month was paid to appellant - Appellant was holding an office of profit under State Govt. - Further held, office of profit under Govt. of India or under Govt. of any State would be a disqualification only if that office is not declared by Parliament by law not to disqualify its holder - Before holding a person disqualified, it will have to be seen whether that office is not exempted by competent legislature from operation of disqualification clause - Arts. 102 and 191 both, by explanation, have clarified that a person shall not be deemed to hold an office of profit under Govt. of India or Govt. of any State specified in First Schedule by reason only that he is a Minister - either for Union or any State - Thus, disqualification, in case of appellant, could only be removed by Parliament, since membership of Rajya Sabha was in issue, within meaning of art. 102(1)(a) - No such disqualification was removed by Parliament as JAAC Act has not been included in Schedule to 1959 Act - State Legislature is not competent to remove any disqualification in respect of a Member of Parliament - Appeal dismissed.
