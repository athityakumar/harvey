Bongalgaon Refinery and Petrochemicals Limited v Samijuddin Ahmed

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20930; (2001) 9 SCC 557; AIR 2001 SC 3507A; 2001 (4) AWC 3004; 2001 (3) CLR 565; 2001 (90) FLR 1167; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 350; JT 2001 (7) SC 252; 2002 (35) LabIC 546; 2001 (2) LLJ 1149; 2001 (4) LLN 44; 2001(6) SCALE 106; 2001 (8) SLR 193; 2001 (6) SLT 430; 2001 (6) Supreme 806

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 2(k) - Whether action of management of Bongalgaon Refinery and Petro Chemicals Ltd Dhaligaon, P O Bongalgaon, Dist Goalpara is justified in removing from service Shri Samijuddin Ahmed, workman on ground that workman has given false information to seek employment against land oustee quota? - If not, to what relief concerned workman is entitled to? - Documentary evidence filed on behalf of appellant clearly goes to show that respondent had never entered into employment of appellant - He was offered an employment under a special scheme whereunder employment was available only subject to satisfying certain eligibility conditions - Respondent made a material concealment of facts and tried to secure an employment to which he was not entitled under scheme - Such material concealment was detected timely and therefore his joining report was not accepted by competent authority of appellant company and same was turned down - This averment made on affidavit and supported by documentary evidence has not been rebutted by respondent - Held, respondent had not entered employment of appellant, referring a dispute u/s. 10 of Industrial Disputes Act based on assumption that respondent had entered service of appellant and was then removed from service, suffered from material infirmity and was therefore vitiated - Appeal allowed.
