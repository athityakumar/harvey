State of West Bengal v Mondal and another

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19836; (2001) 8 SCC 443; AIR 2001 SC 3471; 2001 ARBLR 683; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 189; JT 2001 (7) SC 200; 2001(6) SCALE 82; 2001 (6) SLT 496; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 531; 2001 (6) Supreme 673

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Franchise

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - National Highways Act, 1956 - National Highways Authority of India Act, 1988 - National Highways (Fees for Use of National Highway Section and Permanent Bridge - Public Funded Project) Rules, 1997 - Whether order of a division bench of Calcutta High Court wherein action initiated by superintending engineer to rebid right to collect toll in question was not only stayed but continuance of receiver in respect of possession of expressway and collection of toll charges, etc was also ordered is maintainable? - Held, relevant and vital facts apparent on record and stark realities of case go to show that plaintiff has been entrusted with contract for collecting toll in question only for a period of one year from 13-12-1997 and that as per provisions of law, such collection by executing agency departmentally or through franchisee as in this case, is to be for and on behalf of Central Govt. and amounts have to be remitted to Central Govt., which only is beneficiary of such collection - Without concurrence of Central Govt., which also has to be necessarily in conformity with statutory rules governing same, there cannot be any extension granted in favor of plaintiff and HC could not have directed doing of a thing not permissible in or contrary to statutory rules - Court unable to appreciate as to how division bench, in teeth of concrete and clinching material that from inception of contract first respondent-plaintiff had been playing foul and has been devising methods and ways to avoid his responsibilities and has been shown to be guilty of serious lapses in depositing regularly amounts due, could have been given any protection by issuing directions of nature under challenge in these appeals - Judgments under challenge have not only effect of conferring undue, undeserved and unjustified benefits and rights on plaintiff, first respondent herein, but also have consequence of doing grave injustice to public interest and great loss to public revenue and is liable to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
