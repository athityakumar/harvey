M. P. Electricity Board and Another v Dinesh Kumar Agrawal

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 378; 2001 (3) CLR 216; JT 2001 (5) SC 550; 2001 (2) LLJ 423; 2001 (4) SLR 25

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(bb) and 2(oo) - Whether an order by High Court D/-27-08-1999 held that there is no clear term of termination provided in letter of appointment or in agreement of appointment on failure of passing necessary tests is correct? - There is no warrant for conclusion reached by HC that there is no clause for termination of candidate who does not pass appropriate test - Case falls within exception of sub-cl. (bb) of s. 2 (oo) of Industrial Disputes Act and termination is as stipulated in contract - View taken by HC cannot be justified at all - Held, even though this is position in law, court should not interfere with order made by labour court as confirmed by HC in view of fact that respondent had been appointed as early as 1984 and his services were sought to be terminated by an order made in 1986 while labour court made an order on 17-10-1990 pursuant to which he is reinstated in service and thereafter HC upheld that order and it is admitted that respondent is in service of appellant-board even now - It would not be appropriate to upset that state of affair by interfering with same - Appeal dismissed.
