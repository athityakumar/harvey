Satya Narayan Sharma v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20728; (2001) 8 SCC 607; (2002) SCC (Cr) 39; AIR 2001 SC 2856; AIR 2001 SCW 3818; 2001 (4) CCR 64; 2001 (4) Crimes 35; 2002 (1) CRJ 198; 2001 CRLJ 4640; 2001 (2) JCC 218; JT 2001 (8) SC 157; 2001 (3) KLT 599; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 377; 2001(6) SCALE 442; 2001 (3) SCJ 624; 2001 (6) SLT 725; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 268; 2001 (7) Supreme 246; 2001 (2) UC 636

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 397 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(2), 19(3) and 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 476, 468 and 471 - Appeal for quashing an order under Act, 1988 was dismissed by High Court - Appellant obtained stay of trial and was got adjourned from time to time and delayed trial for 7 years - Whether trials under Act, 1988 could be stayed? - S. 19 of Act, 1988 would not apply to inherent jurisdiction of HC - In cases under Prevention of Corruption Act there can be no stay of trials - If an enactment contains specific bar then inherent jurisdiction cannot be exercised to get over that bar - Held, power of revision and/or inherent jurisdiction can only be exercised by HC - Merely because concerned court is not in a position to take up petition for hearing would be no ground for staying trial even temporarily in cases under Act, 1988 - Appeal dismissed.
