Union of India and Others v Tarun K. Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20480; (2003) 11 SCC 768; (2004) SCC (L&S) 316; AIR 2001 SC 2196; 2001 (34) LabIC 1768; 2001 (3) LLN 118; 2002 (2) SLR 195; 2001 (4) SLT 321

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Selection for post of constable in Railway Protection Force - Complaints alleging malpractice in process of recruitment - Cancellation of entire selection - Order fresh selection - Writ petitions challenging order of cancellation - Allahabad High Court directed appropriate authority to publish result and complete process of selection - Hence, present appeal - Held, subsequent to order of cancellation, in view of allegation of malpractice, departmental authorities has held an inquiry into matter and inquiry revealed gross irregularities and illegalities - Consequently process of selection which stands vitiated by adoption of large scale malpractice to a public office, cannot be permitted to be sustained by Court of Law - Further held, that apart, an individual applicant for any particular post does not get a right to be enforced by a mandamus unless and until he is selected in process of selection and gets letter of appointment much before so called list of selection was approved by Railway Board - Thus, Learned Single Judge of High Court committed an error in issuing direction in question - Appeal disposed of.
