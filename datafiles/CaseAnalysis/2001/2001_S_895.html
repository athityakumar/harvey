Dilip and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 74; (2001) 9 SCC 452; (2002) SCC (Cr) 592; AIR 2001 SC 3049; 2001 (4) CCR 109; 2001 (4) Crimes 105; 2002 (2) CRJ 405; 2001 CRLJ 4721; 2001 (2) JCC 236; JT 2001 (8) SC 390; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 383; 2001(7) SCALE 51; 2001 (7) SLT 165; 2001 (7) Supreme 468; 2002 (1) UC 118

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were held guilty of an offence punishable u/s. 376(2)(g) of the IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by the HC maintaining the conviction - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, prosecution case rests solely on the testimony of prosecutrix. The medical evidence does not lend any positive corroboration to the testimony of the prosecutrix. Hence, it is difficult to accept the truthfulness of the version of the prosecutrix that any sexual assault as alleged was committed on her in view of the fact that her narration of the incident becomes infirm on account of being contradicted by the statement of her own aunt and medical evidence and the report of forensic science laboratory. The conviction of appellants set aside. Appeals allowed.
