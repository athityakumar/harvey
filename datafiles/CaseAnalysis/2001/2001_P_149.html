 Pradip Chandra Parija and Others v Pramod Chandra Patnaik and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, U.C. Banerjee, S.N. Variava, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21248; (2002) 1 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 296; AIR 2001 SCW 5016; 2002 (48) ALR 518; 2002 (1) CHN 117; [2002] 111 Comp Cas 299; 2002 (1) CompLJ 201; 2002 (174) CTR 580; 2003 (90) ECC 878; 2002 (144) E.L.T. 7; [2002] 254 ITR 99; JT 2001 (10) SC 347; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 551; 2001(8) SCALE 384; 2001 (8) SLT 282; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 139; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 460; 2001 (8) Supreme 548

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 145(2) and 145(3) - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 7, r. 1, O. 7, r. 2 - Whether two learned judges of this court can disagree with a judgment of three learned judges of this court and whether, for that reason, they can refer matter before them directly to a bench of five judges? - Held, no - Judicial discipline and propriety demands that a bench of two learned judges should follow a decision of a bench of three learned judges - But if a bench of two learned judges concludes that an earlier judgment of three learned judges is so very incorrect that in no circumstances can it be followed, proper course for it to adopt is to refer matter before it to a bench of three learned judges setting out reasons why it could not agree with earlier judgment - If, then, bench of three learned judges also comes to conclusion that earlier judgment of a bench of three learned judges is incorrect, reference to a bench of five learned judges is justified - Appeal dismissed.
