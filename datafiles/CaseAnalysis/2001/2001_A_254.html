New India Assurance Company Limited and Others v Asha Rani and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19791; (2001) 4 SCC 24; (2001) 6 SCC 724; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1223; 2001 (2) ACC 479; 2001 ACJ 187; AIR 2001 SCW 3295; 2002 AllCJ 10; 2001 (2) AllCriC 479; 2001 (45) ALR 469; 2001 (4) AWC 2855; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 182; JT 2001 (6) SC 665; 2001 (3) KLT 235; 2001 (129) PLR 637B; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 294; 2001(5) SCALE 374; 2001 (6) SLT 43; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 686; 2001 (6) Supreme 359; 2001 (2) UC 470B

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Travelling, Deletion, Gratuitous

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Held, the significant difference is, under the old Act the "goods vehicle" could be used for the carriage of goods "or in addition to passengers" while in the definition of "goods carriage" the words "or in addition to passengers" stand deleted - Now goods carriage cannot carry any passenger - The other striking feature is with reference to s. 149(2) of the new Act - Under the new Act, it would be a breach of condition in case vehicle is used for a purpose other than for which permit has been issued - In case a permit is issued for a "goods carriage" it would not include any passengers and in case they travel it would be contrary to the mandate of the statute and thus in view of s. 149(2) no liability could be passed on to the insurance company - The effect of the deletion of sub-cl. (ii) of the proviso to s. 95(1)(b) in the new Act also requires reconsideration - Let this matter be placed before Hon'ble the Chief justice for constituting a larger Bench.
