M/s Dalmia Cement (Bharat) Limited v M/s Galaxy Traders and Agencies Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20703; (2001) 6 SCC 463; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1163; AIR 2001 SC 676; 2001 (1) BC 684; 2001 (2) CLC 139; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 472; 2001 CRLJ 972; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1770; JT 2001 (2) SC 1; 2001 (1) KLT 528; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 646; 2001(1) SCALE 243; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 461; 2001 (1) UC 359

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 139, 140, 141 and 142 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Dishonour of cheque - Notice - Dismissal of complaint as barred by time - Whether cause of action arises from date of 'service' of notice by complainant or from its 'receipt' by appellant to file complaint within statutory period? - Held, a liberal interpretation should be adopted for person who has statutory obligation to give notice - Limitation starts from date of receipt of notice - But, if it is refused or returned as unclaimed - Limitation starts from date of service of notice as it is conceded to have been served - Appeal allowed.
