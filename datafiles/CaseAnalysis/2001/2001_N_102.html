Ramesh Kumar and Others v National Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19793; (2001) 6 SCC 713; (2003) SCC (Cr) 762; 2001 (2) ACC 492; 2001 ACJ 1565; AIR 2001 SC 3363; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 130; 2002 AllCJ 8; 2001 (2) AllCriC 492; 2001 (45) ALR 131; 2001 (4) AWC 2853; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 177; JT 2001 (6) SC 657; 2003 (2) KLT 47; 2001 (129) PLR 893; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 680; 2001(5) SCALE 370; 2001 (4) SCJ 508; 2001 (6) SLT 36; 2003 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 79; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 679; 2001 (6) Supreme 353; 2001 (2) UC 435

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Travelling

Summary: Corporate - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether insurance co. is liable to pay compensation on account of death or bodily injury of gratuitous passengers including owner or his representative of goods, travelling in a goods vehicle? - Compensation awarded in cases where deceased or injured persons were travelling in a goods carriage were owner or his authorised representative, insurance co. is liable to pay compensation - Only in cases where it is recorded by Tribunal that they were not owners then only insurance co. could succeed that they are not liable to pay - Held, instant case belong to category wherein insurance co. is liable to pay compensation for deceased or injured persons travelling in a goods carriage, who were either owner or his representatives - Appeals disposed of.
