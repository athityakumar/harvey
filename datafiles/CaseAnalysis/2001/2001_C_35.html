Cadila Health Care Limited v Cadila Pharmaceuticals Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Brijesh Kumar, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19957; (2001) 5 SCC 73; AIR 2001 SC 1952; 2001 (2) CLC 564; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1419; JT 2001 (4) SC 243; 2001 (21) PTC 300; 2001 (1) RAJ 504; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 583; 2001(3) SCALE 98; 2001 (2) SCJ 553; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 743; 2001 (3) SLT 225; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 103; 2001 (3) Supreme 1; 2001 (1) UJ 669

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Health & Drug; Intellectual Property; Trade

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade & Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 27 and 28 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, s. 17B - Deceptively similar trademarks - Held, while dealing with cases relating to passing off, one of important tests which has to be applied in each case is whether misrepresentation made by defendant is of such a nature as is likely to cause an ordinary consumer to confuse one product for another due to similarity of marks and other surrounding factors - While determining question of deceptive similarity factors like degree of resemblance between marks, nature of goods in respect of which they are used, similarity in nature, character and performance of goods should be considered - Appeal diposed of.
