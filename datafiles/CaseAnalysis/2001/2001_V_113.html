Jasbir Singh v Vipin Kumar Jaggi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20027; (2001) 8 SCC 289; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1525; AIR 2001 SC 2734; 2001 (3) CCR 169; 2002 (1) CGLJ 105; 2001 (4) Crimes 127; 2002 (1) CRJ 278; 2001 CRLJ 3993; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 529; 2001 (2) JCC 118; JT 2001 (6) SC 419; 2001 (3) KLT 346; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 818; 2001(5) SCALE 258; 2001 (4) SCJ 465; 2001 (5) SLT 769; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 598; 2001 (6) Supreme 135; 2001 (2) UC 506; 2001 (2) UC 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver, Opium Act, 1857, Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 23, 29, 64 and 64(1) - Criminal Procedure Code 1973, ss. 306, 307, 311, 321, 337, 338 and 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 215 and 226 - Scope of power of s. 64 of NDPS, 1985 - U/s. 64(1) of Act, Central Govt. or State Govt. may tender immunity from prosecution for any offence under Act or under Indian Penal Code or under any Central or State Act with a view to obtaining evidence of any person appearing to have been directly or indirectly concerned in or privy to contravention of any of provisions of Act - Underlying rationale of s. 64 is that Govt./ Narcotics Control Bureau which is vitally interested in getting hold of culprits, must be allowed to assess strength of evidence available to it and if necessary, bolster its case with evidence of an accomplice - Therefore, section serves same purpose as grant of pardon to approvers u/s. 307, CrPC, 1973 - Object of s. 64 being same as s. 307, should logically follow that it may be exercised at any time during course of trial - No conflict between powers exercised by court u/s. 307 and by Govt. u/s. 64 - All that s. 64 does is to bring expressly to fore role which executive already has to play u/s. 307 - Only difference is in authority which orders pardon or grant of immunity - Held, assuming there is conflict between powers of Court u/s. 307, CrPC, 1973 and power of Central Govt. u/s. 64 of Act, then it must be held that s. 64 would prevail both on ground that Act being a special Act overrides CrPC, 1973 which is a general Act and also because later enactment must prevail over earlier one - Decision of High Court to grant immunity u/s. 64 after Sessions Court rejected an application for pardon u/s. 307 CrPC, 1973 upheld - Appeal dismissed.
