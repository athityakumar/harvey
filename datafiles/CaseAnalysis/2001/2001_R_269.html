Plasto Pack, Mumbai and Another v Ratnakar Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20040; (2001) 6 SCC 683; AIR 2001 SC 3651; 2002 AllCJ 3; 2001 (45) ALR 169; 2001 (2) BC 611; 2002 (1) Bom.C.R. 173; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 220; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 147; JT 2001 (6) SC 598; 2002 (1) LCD 431; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 250; 2001(5) SCALE 145; 2001 (5) SLT 723; 2001 (5) Supreme 741; 2001 (2) UC 412

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8, r. 10 - Whether High Court was justified in substituting almost a new decree in place of old one by granting such reliefs as were not granted earlier and that too without noticing appellants/ defendant, on notice of motion by respondent/ plaintiff? - Prayers as were not granted by earlier decree would be deemed to have been refused and to that extent suit shall be deemed to have been dismissed - Power to amend a decree cannot be exercised so as to add to or subtract from any relief granted earlier - K Rajamouli v A V K N Swamy 2001 Indlaw SC 291 followed - Held, earlier decree and order amending decree both suffer from serious legal infirmities and error of jurisdiction and as such same deserve to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
