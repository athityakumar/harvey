Murudeshwara Ceramics Limited and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19938; (2002) 1 SCC 116; AIR 2001 SC 3017; JT 2001 (8) SC 595; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 130; 2001(7) SCALE 228; 2001 (7) SLT 470; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 151; 2001 (7) Supreme 798

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Forests Act, Forests Conservation Act

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land & Property - Karnataka Preservation of Trees Act,1976 - Forests Conservation Act,1980 - Karnataka Forests Act,1963,s.17 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss.63, 82, 83,79-A, 79-B, 80, 109 - Setting up of industry - Challenged as against environment and ecology - Validity - Appellants for setting up of clay processing unit purchased land and obtained statutory permissions licences and clearances from concerned authorities - Challenging same respondent nos. 13 to 22 filed writ petition in HC to restrain appellants from setting up industry as it would be coming in notified deep forest area and against provisions of the 1961 Act - HC allowed writ petitions holding that licences and clearances obtained by appellants were not in accordance to law and acquisition of land was in contravention of the Act and directed scientific study as regards sustenance of ecology and environment and ordered to initiate proceedings u/ss. 82 and 83 of the Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, State Govt. had ample powers to grant exemption and by order dt. 3-7-1997 granted exemption from applicability of ss. 63, 79A, 79B and 80 of the 1961 Act on certain terms and conditions and HC erred in concluding that exemption was of no use as granted after purchase of lands and initiating proceedings u/ss. 82 and 83 of the 1961 Act as contravention u/ss. 79 A and 80 of the 1961 Act - State Govt. had supreme powers and even in absence of such purposes could grant exemption in public interest - As per Act detailed procedure had to be followed if found contravention to the Act and land would not immediately vest in Govt. and no such proceedings were initiated in instant case - Examination of HC regarding contravention of provisions was totally uncalled for and ought to have held that those provisions were not applicable as exemptions were granted - Appellant submitted that it was not carrying out any activity and respondents/Govt. submitted that all statutory permissions and licenses issued by them were cancelled as disputed site was within notified area of deep forest with several restrictions - Order of HC was set aside - Appellant had right to apply for fresh clearances and respondents had every right to oppose considering various enactments protecting environment and ecology - Respondent nos. 1 to 12 were directed to make fresh assessment if appellants would approach them for clearances - Appeals disposed of.
