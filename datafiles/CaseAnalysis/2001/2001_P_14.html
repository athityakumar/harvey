Ramalingam Chettiar v P. K. Pattabiraman and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20270; (2001) 4 SCC 96; AIR 2001 SC 1185; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 93; 2001 (43) ALR 742; 2001 (2) CGLJ 77; JT 2001 (3) SC 179; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 48; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 187; 2001(2) SCALE 237; 2001 (1) SCJ 667; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 23; 2001 (2) SLT 509; 2001 (2) Supreme 133

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Revenue Recovery Act, 1864, ss. 38 and 59 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 21 - Auction Sale - Cause of action arose when application of respondent was rejected and sale was confirmed, that is on 11-01-1977 - Suit was laid in Civil Court within six months from time at which cause of action arose - But in said suit was incompetent as State of Tamil Nadu was not impleaded as a defendant - Application for impleadment allowed on 01-10-1979 - Whether said suit was time barred - In view of s. 21 so far as State of Tamil Nadu was concerned, suit filed by respondent has to be treated as instituted when application for impleadment of State of Tamil Nadu was allowed and by that time suit stood barred by time - Under proviso, if Court is satisfied, it can direct that suit, as regards newly - added or substituted plaintiff or defendant, shall be deemed to have been instituted on an earlier date - Held, no order having been passed under proviso to s. 21 that impleadment of State of Tamil Nadu would take effect from date of institution of suit - In absence of such an order by Trial Court, suit filed by respondent was barred by limitation as contemplated u/s. 59 - Appeal allowed.
