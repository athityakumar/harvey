State of Kerala v M. M. Manikantan Nair

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20155; (2001) 4 SCC 752; (2001) SCC (Cr) 808; AIR 2001 SC 2145; 2001 (2) CCR 168; 2001 CRLJ 2346; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 245; 2001 (3) KLT 80; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 657; 2001(3) SCALE 513; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 203; 2001 (3) SLT 680; 2001 (3) Supreme 526

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Kerala Panchayat Act 1960

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 409, 468, 471 and 477 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 19 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Kerala Panchayat Act, 1960, ss. 122 and 122(1) - Respondent 1 was Secretary of Melukavu Gram Panchayat - Misappropriated funds - Whether proper sanction from competent authority was required before cognizance could have been taken against respondent by Court? - Held, as per s. 122 of Kerala Panchayats Act, sanction is required under said sub-s. (1) of s. 122 only if a person holds office of President, executive authority or any member and not otherwise - If a person ceases to hold above office on retirement or otherwise no sanction for prosecution is necessary - As respondent retired from service no previous sanction for prosecution under this section is required because he ceased to hold post under Panchayat - Appeal allowed.
