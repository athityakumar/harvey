Municipal Council, Kota, Rajasthan v Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 261; (2001) 3 SCC 654; AIR 2001 SC 1060; [2001] 249 ITR 560; JT 2001 (3) SC 275; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 711; 2001(2) SCALE 379; 2001 (2) SCJ 346; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 287; 2001 (2) SLT 570; 2001 (123) STC 49; 2001 (2) Supreme 246

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1956, Rajasthan Municipalities Act, 1959, Rubber Act, 1947, Kota State Chungi Act, 1929

Summary: Customs Act, 1962 - Kota State Chungi Act, 1929 - Rajasthan Municipalities Act, 1959, ss. 104 and 105 - What is nature and character of levy of 'dharmada', as it is called in form of an octroi by Municipal Council, Kota, in Rajasthan State? - It is not name of tax but its real nature, its 'pith and substance' as it has sometimes been said, which must determine into what category it falls - Held, levy sought to be imposed and recovered as 'dharmada' being only on goods brought within municipal limits of Kota for consumption, use or sale therein same in truth, reality and substance is only an 'octroi' for purpose of carrying out several public charitable objects statutorily enjoined upon municipal board - Appeals allowed.
