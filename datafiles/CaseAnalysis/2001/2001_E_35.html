Smita Conductors Limited v Euro Alloys Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19996; (2001) 7 SCC 728; AIR 2001 SC 3730; 2001 (44) ARBLR 275; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 715; JT 2001 (7) SC 358; 2001 (3) RAJ 188; 2001(6) SCALE 128; 2001 (6) SLT 361; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 477; 2001 (6) Supreme 743

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937 - Interest Act, 1978 - Convention on Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards - New York Convention - Convention requires arbitration agreement to be in writing - Res judicata - No written contract either in contract bearing no. S-142 or contract bearing no. S-336 because contracts were signed by respondent - But not signed by appellant and thus resulting in only an oral agreement between parties for supply of goods - Whether such an agreement cannot be termed to be one made in writing, to attract paras. (1) and (2) of art. II of schedule to Act and that there has been no exchange of letters or telegrams between parties so as to include arbitral clause? - Held, when appellant and respondent agreed to deal in certain goods, certain terms had to be agreed between them - Those terms were set out in contracts referred to as S-142 and S-336 - If those are two contracts pursuant to which appellant is trading with respondent, conclusion is obvious that those terms are reduced to writing and acknowledged by reason of opening of letters of credit of which reference is made in these two contracts - It would be illogical to contend that those letters of credit though not addressed to respondent would indicate that they were not acting in pursuance of contracts - Appeal dismissed.
