Prakash Dhawal Khairnar (Patil) and another v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19908; (2002) 2 SCC 35; (2002) SCC (Cr) 281; AIR 2002 SC 340; 2002 (1) Crimes 18; 2002 (2) CRJ 225; 2002 CRLJ 928; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 243; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 212; 2001(8) SCALE 482; 2001 (8) SLT 389; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 612; 2001 (8) Supreme 709; 2002 (1) UC 228

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 201, 302 and 397 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25(1)(b)(a) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 164 and 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27 and 30 - (A) Whether benefit of doubt given with regard to conspiracy for commission of murders to A-2 and acquitting him of main offence will make his confessional statement inadmissible? - To what extent can it be used against A1? - Held, confessional statement of A-2 recorded u/s. 164 CrPC, 1973 by Magistrate would be admissible in evidence as accused 1 and 2 were jointly tried - Circumstantial evidence which gets corroborated from confessional statement of A-2 for connecting A-1 with crime could be relied upon - (B) Whether case comes in category of rarest of rare cases, so as to impose death penalty? - No doubt, it is heinous and brutal crime but at same time it will be difficult to hold that it is rarest of rare case - Held, Appeals Nos. 238-39 of 2001 filed by A-1 is allowed - Criminal Appeal No. 489 of 2001 filed by State of Maharashtra against A-2 is dismissed.
