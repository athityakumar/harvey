Surendra Singh Rautela @ Surendra Singh v State of Bihar (Now State of Jharkhand)and

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 174; (2002) SCC (Cr) 165; (2002) 1 SCC 266; AIR 2002 SC 260; 2001 (4) CCR 335; 2001 (4) Crimes 271; 2002 (2) CRJ 588; 2002 CRLJ 555; JT 2001 (10) SC 152; 2001(8) SCALE 298; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 340; 2001 (8) Supreme 524; 2002 (1) UC 207

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: The High Court can exercise suo motu powers of enhancement of sentence under revisional jurisdiction only after giving opportunity of hearing to the accused. Further, under Section 220 (3) of Code of Criminal Procedure, if act or acts of an accused in the same transaction constitute more than one offence under different laws, the person accused of them should be charged with and tried at one trial for each of such offences.
