State of Uttar Pradesh v Harendra Arora and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20093; (2001) 6 SCC 392; (2001) SCC (L&S) 959; AIR 2001 SC 2319; 2001 ALJ 1185; 2002 (4) AWC 2979; 2001 (2) CLR 905; 2002 (95) FLR 451; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 70; 2001 (34) LabIC 1805; 2002 (3) LLJ 1124; 2001 (3) LLN 42; 2001(3) SCALE 659; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 375; 2001 (4) SLR 558; 2001 (4) SLT 80; 2001 (4) Supreme 314

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Government of India Act, 1919, Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1930, Jammu and Kashmir (Government Servants) Prevention of Corruption Act, 1962, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850, Uttar Pradesh State Public Services Tribunal Act, 1976, Civil Services Classification Rules, 1920, Bank Regulations

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Criminal - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 99-A and 115, O. 21, r. 90 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 311(2) - Government of India Act, 1919, s. 96-B(2) - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 465 - Jammu and Kashmir (Government Servants) Prevention of Corruption Act, 1962, s. 17(5) - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1930, r. 55-A and 55 - Civil Services Classification Rules, 1920, r. 14 - Whether an order awarding punishment shall be set aside ipso facto on ground of non-furnishing of a copy of enquiry report to the delinquent? - Held, no - If an enquiry report is not furnished, the same ipso facto would not invalidate the order of punishment unless the delinquent officer has been prejudiced thereby - The law on the subject can be classified in two compartments - One is requirement to furnish the enquiry report under the statute and another will be according to the principles of natural justice - The test would be, whether the delinquent officer had or did not have a fair hearing - Where merely because there is defect, error or irregularity in the order, the same would not be liable to be set aside unless it has prejudicially affected the decision - Appeal allowed.
