Gurbax Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20304; (2001) 3 SCC 28; (2001) SCC (Cr) 426; AIR 2001 SC 1002; AIR 2001 SCW 670; 2001 (1) CCR 211; 2001 (1) Crimes 235; 2001 CRLJ 1166; 2001 (1) JCC 144; JT 2001 (2) SC 330; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 702; 2001(1) SCALE 662; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 882; 2001 (1) SLT 904; 2001 (1) Supreme 625; 2001 (1) UC 508

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Poppy, Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 100(3) - Appellant convicted u/s. 15 of NDPS Act, 1985, for offence of possessing poppy husk - Whether conviction of appellant sustainable? - Appellant contended that investigating officer has not followed procedure prescribed u/s. 50 of NDPS Act, 1985, of informing accused whether search should be carried out in presence of gazetted officer or magistrate - Officer empowered acting on prior information to search a person is under obligation u/s. 50 of NDPS, 1985, to inform the person concerned of his right u/s. 50(1) of being taken to nearest gazetted officer or magistrate for making search - Such information may not necessarily be in writing - This right is extension of right conferred u/s. 100(3) of CrPC, 1973 - Prosecution has not led any evidence whether chemical analyser received sample with proper intact seals - Conduct of panch witness is unusual as he offered himself to be witness for search and seizure despite being not asked by IO - Faulty investigation by prosecution - Held, it would not be safe to convict appellant for serious offence of possessing poppy husk - Appeal allowed.
