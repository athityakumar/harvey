Messrs Ambala Sarabhai Enterprises Limited v Messrs Amrit Lal and Company

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 148; (2001) 8 SCC 397; AIR 2001 SC 3580; 2002 AllCJ 279; 2001 (45) ALR 476; 2001 (93) DLT 164; JT 2001 (7) SC 477; 2001 (2) RCJ 507; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 328; 2001(5) SCALE 509; 2001 (6) SLT 106; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 195; 2001 (6) Supreme 505

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Eviction petition

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1988, ss. 14(b), 6(c) - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(b) - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 6, 6(c) - Sub-letting - Eviction petition - Jurisdiction - Instant appeals were filed by appellant tenant against order passed by HC wherein HC allowed landlords second appeal but dismissed revision of tenant.

Since proceeding for eviction of tenant was pending when repealing Act came into operation, s. 6 of 1897 Act would be applicable in present case. As it is Landlords accrued right in terms of s. 6(c) of the Act refers to any right which may not be limited as a vested right but is limited to be an accrued right. Words any right accrued in s. 6(c) of the Act is wide enough to include landlord's right to evict a tenant in case proceeding was pending when repeal came in. A landlord or tenant are relegated to seek their rights and remedies under the common law once protection given to a tenant under the 1958 Act is withdrawn, except in cases where s. 6 of 1897 of the Act is applicable. A ground of eviction based on illegal subletting u/s. 14(b) of 1958 Act would not constitute to be a vested right of a landlord, but it would be a right and privilege accrued within meaning of s. 6(c) of 1897 Act in a matter if proceeding for eviction is pending. When tenant has no vested right under the Act having only protective right, withdrawal of such protection would not confer on a landlord a vested right to evict a tenant under the Act except where s. 6(c) of 1897 Act is applicable. In view of these findings SC hold landlord has a right under repealed the Act by virtue of s. 6(c) of 1897 Act, which would save pending proceedings before Rent Controller, which may continue to be proceeded with as if repealed Act is still in force. Since Rent Controller has jurisdiction over subject-matter, it will not be right for landlord to continue with two parallel proceedings, one under General Law and other before Rent Controller. Hence SC further order that respondent landlord to withdraw one of the two proceedings within time framed. Appeals dismissed.
