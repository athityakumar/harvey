 Executive Engineer, Dhenkanal Minor Irrigation v N. C. Budharaj (Dead) By Lrs. Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19768; (2001) 2 SCC 721; AIR 2001 SC 626; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 148; 2001 (1) ARBLR 346; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; 2001 (1) CGLJ 261; JT 2001 (1) SC 486; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 128; 2001 (1) RAJ 1; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 613; 2001(1) SCALE 109; 2001 (1) SCJ 617; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 264; 2001 (1) SLT 396; 2001 (1) Supreme 205

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Statutory Arbitration

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Interest Act, 1978 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Award - Interest granted for pre-reference period - Challenged - Disputes between parties were referred to arbitrator - Award passed granting interest pendentilite and pre reference period also - Award pertaining to granting interest for pre reference period was challenged by appellants by instant appeals - Whether Arbitrator was competent to grant interest for pre reference period - Held , this being question of law 3 judge bench referred instant case to larger bench for consideration - SC in Executive Engineer (Irrigation), Balimela and Others v Abhaduta Jena and Others, 1987 Indlaw SC 28416 held that Arbitrator was not competent to grant interest for pre reference period - When proceedings were pending for adjudication parties would be aware of claims made by opposite party - But during pre-reference period neither claims were crystallized nor had opposite party any notice that it might be required to pay certain amount to claimant depending on adjudication of dispute by Arbitrator - Settled law was that arbitrator would resolve disputes between parties arising out of agreements by adjudication in accordance with law - Though arbitrator was nominated as judge by parties he might not have requisite experience in field of law which presiding officer of a Court possesses - Arbitrator would not follow strict rules or procedures as followed by Court - Arbitrator had no jurisdiction to award interest for re-reference period unless provided in agreement or by usage of trade or by any other substantive provisions of law -Appeals allowed.
