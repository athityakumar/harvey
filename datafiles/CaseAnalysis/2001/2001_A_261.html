State of Haryana v Ashok Kumar @ Billu

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21292; (2002) 9 SCC 658; 2001 CRLJ 3298; JT 2001 (4) SC 114

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 and 498A - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Deceased was married to accused-respondent - Deceased died within one year of marriage - Respondent alongwith six other members of his family were sent up for trial for offences u/s. 302/34, IPC, 1860 and 498-A IPC, 1860 - Respondent convicted u/s. 302 IPC, 1860 - On appeal, High Court acquitted respondent - Whether order of acquittal passed by HC justified? - Respondent in his statement u/s. 313, CrPC, 1973 categorically stated that dead body of deceased was inside house and was not lying in front of house, site plan and sketch Ext. PF also show that dead body was found to be inside house - Such factors create doubt about truthfulness of prosecution case as also about fairness of investigating agency - In state of evidence, HC committed no error in giving benefit of doubt to respondent and acquitting him - Held, view taken by HC is undoubtedly a possible view in face of evidence on record and cannot be said to be either perverse or even unreasonable - No reason to interfere with order of acquittal - Appeal dismissed.
