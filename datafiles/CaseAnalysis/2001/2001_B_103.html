U. P. State Electricity Board v Banaras Electric Light and Power Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20022; (2001) 7 SCC 117; AIR 2001 SC 2727; JT 2001 (6) SC 501; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 97; 2001(5) SCALE 325; 2001 (5) SLT 840; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 662; 2001 (6) Supreme 190; 2001 (2) UC 487; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2315

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Date Of Vesting, Uttar Pradesh Public Monies (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972, Indian Electricity (Amendment) Act, 1959, Indian Electricity (Uttar Pradesh Amendment and Validation) Act, 1975

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 6, 7 and 7A (7, 7 A as substituted by Indian Electricity (Uttar Pradesh Amendment and Validation) Act, 1975 - Companies Act, 1956 - S. 468 r/w ss. 518(1)(b) and 518(4) - Taking over of undertaking by Board of respondent company - Collection of uncollected dues from consumers of respondent/company by Board upto date of vesting - Contention by respondent/company as to that amount was collected by Board as agent of Company and Board held amount as a trustee, therefore Board was required in fact and in law to make over amount collected by it and any further sum which it may collect from consumers towards such arrear dues of Company to liquidators - Company being in liquidation process - Whether book-debts of Company are part of undertaking which vested in Board, and whether Board having admittedly so realised amount has right to retain same? - Provisions perused especially ss. 6, 7 and 7 A of 1910 Act - Held, no book-debts of Company were not a part of undertaking which vested in Board and, therefore, Board having admittedly realised arrears of charges which were due from consumers to Company (in liquidation) prior to date of taking over had no right to retain same and court in exercise of jurisdiction vested u/s. 468 r/w ss. 518(1)(b) and 518(4) of Companies Act could adjudicate matter and direct that amount collected by Board as trustee or agent of Company (in liquidation) should be paid to liquidators - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
