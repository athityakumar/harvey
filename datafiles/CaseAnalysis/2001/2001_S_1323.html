Om Wati Smt. and Another v State, Through Delhi Admn. and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20232; (2001) 4 SCC 333; (2001) SCC (Cr) 685; AIR 2001 SC 1507; 2001 (2) CCR 43; 2001 (2) Crimes 59; 2001 CRLJ 1723; JT 2001 (3) SC 585; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 255; 2001(2) SCALE 505; 2001 (2) SCJ 528; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 482; 2001 (2) SLT 796; 2001 (2) Supreme 423; 2001 (1) UC 550

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149 and 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 107, 151, 161, 227, 228 and 245 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Deceased was beaten to death by accused persons - Trial Court framed charges against all accused persons u/ss. 302, 147, 148 r/w s. 149 - Petition was filed in High Court - HC quashed charges framed and direct trial court to reframe charge in accordance with law based upon material on record - Appeal - Even on basis of a strong suspicion founded on materials before it, Court can form a presumptive opinion regarding existence of factual ingredients constituting offence alleged and in that event be justified in framing charges against accused in respect of commission of offence alleged to have been committed by them - Held, Trial Court, in instant case, rightly held that merely on account of observations and opinion incorporated in post - mortem report, prosecution could not be deprived of its right to prove that accused were guilty of offence for which final report had been filed against them - There was no ground for HC to interfere with well - reasoned order of Trial Court by passing a cryptic and telegraphic order which is impugned in this appeal - It is not safe, at this stage, to deprive prosecution in proving its case on basis of direct evidence, statement of deceased claimed to be admissible u/s. 32 of Evidence Act, 1872 and other documents including inquest report allegedly disclosing infliction of injuries on person of deceased which resulted in his death - Order of HC set aside - Upholding order of Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
