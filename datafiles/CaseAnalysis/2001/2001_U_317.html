 Danial Latifi and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju, G.B. Pattanaik, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20886; (2001) 7 SCC 740; AIR 2001 SC 3958; AIR 2001 SCW 3932; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 63; 2002 AllCJ 490; 2001 (45) ALR 426; 2001 (4) CCR 81; 2002 (1) CGLJ 184; 2002 (1) CRC 175; 2002 (1) CRJ 46; 2001 CRLJ 4660; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 531; JT 2001 (8) SC 218; 2001 (3) KLT 651; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 40; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 468; 2001(6) SCALE 537; 2001 (4) SCJ 1; 2001 (6) SLT 785; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 419; 2001 (7) Supreme 297; 2002 (1) UC 3

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Women & Children - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 127(3)(b) and 128 - Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986, ss. 1(d), 2(a),(b), 3(1)(a),(b),(c),(d), 3(2), (3), 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 and 21 - Indian Divorce Act, 1969 - Indian Special Marriage Act, 1954 - (A) Whether a Muslim husband is liable to make reasonable and fair provision extending beyond iddat period in terms of s. 3(1)(a) of Act? - Held, the husband is bound to make and pay a maintenance to wife and if he fails to do so then wife is entitled to recover it by filing an application before Magistrate - That divorced woman shall be entitled to a reasonable and fair provision and maintenance to be made and paid to her within iddat period by her former husband - (B) Whether Muslim Women (Protection Of Rights On Divorce) Act, 1986 is unconstitutional in light of arts. 14, 15 and 21 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, Magistrate has been conferred with power to make appropriate provision for maintenance and, therefore, what could be earlier granted by a Magistrate u/s. 125, CrPC, 1973 would now be granted under very Act itself - This being position, Act cannot be held to be unconstitutional - That Act actually and in reality codifies what was stated in Shah Bano's case - (C) Whether a husband under Muslim law is exempted from any responsibility towards her his divorced wife beyond payment of any mahr due to her and an amount to cover maintenance during iddat period and s. 127(3)(b)? - If divorced wife is able to maintain herself, husband's liability ceases with expiration of period of iddat but if she is unable to maintain herself after period of iddat, she is entitled to have recourse to s. 125 of CrPC, 1973 - Appeal dismissed.
