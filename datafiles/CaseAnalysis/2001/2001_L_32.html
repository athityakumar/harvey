Union of India and Another v Lalita S. Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20176; (2001) 5 SCC 384; (2001) SCC (L&S) 829; AIR 2001 SC 1792; 2001 (89) FLR 765; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 245; JT 2001 (4) SC 551; 2001 (34) LabIC 1353; 2001(3) SCALE 306; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1059; 2001 (4) SLT 494; 2001 (3) Supreme 325; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1481

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointment

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Indian Railway Medical Department (Assistant Medical Officers Class II) Recruitment Rules, 1967, rr. 4 and 8 - Whether ad hoc period could be counted for seniority on regularization? - Held, doctors who had been appointed by railway administration on ad hoc basis or on temporary basis and had got themselves regularized prior to 01-10-1984, by appearing in selection test held by Union Public Service Commission then in their case period prior to their regularization could be counted for determining their seniority - Ad hoc appointees subsequent to 01-10-1984, even if got themselves regularized by appearing in selection test conducted by Union Public Service Commission in accordance with rules, it will not be in interest of justice to apply principle 'B' to their case as statutory recruitment rules do not provide for any other mode of recruitment other than by process of selection by Union Public Service Commission - Appeal disposed of.
