P. C. Joshi v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20618; (2001) 6 SCC 491; (2001) SCC (L&S) 984; AIR 2001 SC 2788; 2001 (3) AWC 2402; 2001 (3) CLR 259; 2001 (91) FLR 105; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2642; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 130; JT 2001 (6) SC 239; 2001 (34) LabIC 2861; 2001 (2) LLJ 1249; 2001(5) SCALE 119; 2001 (3) SCJ 111; 2002 (1) SLJ 73; 2001 (3) SLR 726; 2001 (5) SLT 694; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 369; 2001 (5) Supreme 609; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2473

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Terminated from service - Challenged - Elaborate inquiry has been conducted by Inquiry Officer - Possibility on a given set of facts to arrive at a different conclusion is no ground to indict a judicial officer for taking one view and that too for alleged misconduct for that reason alone - Inquiry Officer has not found any other material, which would reflect on his reputation or integrity or good faith or devotion to duty or that he has been actuated by any corrupt motive - At best he may say that view taken by appellant is not proper or correct and not attribute any motive to him which is for extraneous consideration that he had acted in that manner - If in every case where an order of a Subordinate Court is found to be faulty a disciplinary action were to be initiated, confidence of subordinate judiciary will be shaken and officers will be in constant fear of writing a judgment so as not to face a disciplinary inquiry and thus judicial officers cannot act independently or fearlessly - Held, findings recorded by Inquiry Officer are totally vitiated for want of any legally acceptable or relevant evidence to support charges of misconduct - Appeal allowed.
