Biman Krishna Bose v United India Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19969; (2001) 6 SCC 477; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 14; 2001 (4) CompLJ 11; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 101; JT 2001 (6) SC 125; 2001 (129) PLR 690; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 179; 2001(4) SCALE 675; 2001 (3) SCJ 71; 2001 (5) SLT 558; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 26; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 255; 2001 (5) Supreme 449; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2470

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Insurance

Keywords: General Insurance Business

Summary: Insurance - General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court after setting aside order refusing to renew the policy, was justified in directing the appellant to take a fresh mediclaim policy? - No, Court held, once it is found that act of an insurance company was arbitrary in refusing to renew the policy, policy is required to be renewed with effect from the date when it fell due for its renewal - Appeal allowed.
