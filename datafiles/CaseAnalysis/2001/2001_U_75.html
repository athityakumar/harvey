Chandrika Prasad (Dead) Through LRs and Others v Umesh Kumar Verma and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20383; (2002) 1 SCC 531; AIR 2002 SC 108; JT 2001 (9) SC 493; 2001(8) SCALE 128; 2001 (8) SLT 90; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 173; 2001 (8) Supreme 275

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bihar Building (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1982

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 25-B and 115 - Bihar building (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1982, ss. 11(1)(c) and 14(8) - Appellant-landlords filed a suit for eviction of respondent-tenants from suit premises u/s. 11(1)(c) of Act i.e., on ground that suit premises were reasonably and in good faith required by landlords for use and occupation - Trial Court ordered suit for eviction - High Court held that eviction was not sustainable - Whether HC transgressed in exercising powers u/s. 14(8) of Act? - Trial Court recorded a categorical finding that son-in-law who has obtained MBBS degree is a registered medical practitioner and has started his medical practice two days in a week in one room of part of suit premises, which was vacated by one of tenants - This finding of Trial Court was based on evidence on record - Held, yes - HC not only overlooked findings of Trial Court which were based on evidence but treated revision petition as an appeal - Appeal allowed.
