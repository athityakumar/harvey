Madanlal v Shyamlal

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20379; (2002) 1 SCC 535; AIR 2002 SC 100; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 59; 2002 (1) CHN 38; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 389; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 89; 2001(8) SCALE 172; 2001 (8) SLT 1; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 252; 2001 (8) Supreme 200; 2002 (1) UC 174

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115, O. 13, r. 2 - Whether documents were not produced at relevant time before settlement of issues, whether he should be permitted to produce same before his evidence is recorded? - Held, cause shown by appellant was not considered to be a 'good cause' as provided u/O. 13, r. 2 CPC, 1908 - Further held, it is open to appellant to raise this contention at appellate stage, if decree is passed against him - Appeal dismissed.
