Dr. A. K. Doshi v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21130; (2001) 4 SCC 43; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1072; AIR 2001 SC 1369; 2001 (2) CLC 403; 2001 (2) CompLJ 221; JT 2001 (3) SC 367; 2001 (34) LabIC 1126; 2001(2) SCALE 405; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 320; 2001 (2) SLR 292; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1535

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Corporate; Service

Keywords: Defence Service

Summary: Service - Companies Act, 1956 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14 - CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965, r. 14 - Company Law Board (Qualifications, Experience and Other Conditions of Service of Members) Rules, 1993, r. 4 - Whether appointment of appellant was valid in lieu of violation of procedure? - Held, after Selection Committee completes exercise and recommends one or more names for appointment recommendation along with materials considered by Selection Committee should be placed before Appointments Committee without any further addition or alteration - Notings made by Secy. of Appointments Committee in file, was an attempt to interfere with process of selection, which was neither permissible under rules nor desirable otherwise - Appeal dismissed.
