Puran and another v Rambilas and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20673; (2001) 6 SCC 338; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1124; AIR 2001 SC 2023; 2001 (2) CCR 255; 2002 (1) CRJ 761; 2001 CRLJ 2566; 2001 (2) JCC 31; JT 2001 (5) SC 226; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 801; 2001(3) SCALE 695; 2001 (3) SCJ 237; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 432; 2001 (3) SLT 869; 2001 (3) Supreme 685; 2001 (2) UC 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304- A and 498A - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963 - Petitioner charged with offences u/s. 498-A and 304-B of IPC, 1860 - Petitioner released on bail which was further canceled by High Court - Whether HC right in canceling bail granted by Trial Court? - S. 439(2) grants to HC power to cancel bail - Such powers can be exercised also in respect of orders passed by Court of Session - HC inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482 is not affected by provisions of s. 397(3) of CrPC, 1973 - HC may refuse to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 482 on basis of self-imposed restriction is different aspect - Held, HC can interfere with order which causes miscarriage of justice or is palpably illegal or is unjustified - Appeal dismissed.
