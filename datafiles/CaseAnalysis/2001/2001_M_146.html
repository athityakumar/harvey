Mahendra Lal Das v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 87; (2002) SCC (Cr) 110; (2002) 1 SCC 149; AIR 2001 SC 2989; 2001 CRLJ 4718; 2001 (2) JCC 344; JT 2001 (8) SC 473; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2001(7) SCALE 232; 2001 (7) SLT 372; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 157; 2001 (7) Supreme 748; 2002 (1) UC 23B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Quashing of FIR - Appellant, Executive Engineer, Public Engineering Department, prayed for quashing of the FIR registered against him u/s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(e) of the Act wherein it was alleged that the appellant was in possession of disproportionate assets to the extent of Rs. 50,600 - FIR was sought to be quashed mainly on the ground that despite expiry of over 12 years, the respondent-State had not granted the sanction which amounted to the violation of his right of life and liberty as enshrined in art. 21 of the Constitution - Petition was dismissed on the ground that mere delay in granting the sanction had not prejudiced the appellant in any manner particularly when he was already on anticipatory bail - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, right to speedy trial encompasses all the stages, namely, stage of investigation, enquiry, trial, appeal, revision and re-trial. In the instant case, prosecution has failed to explain the delay of more than 13 years by now, in granting the sanction for prosecution of the appellant-accused of possessing disproportionate wealth of about Rs. 50,600. Hence, proceedings against the appellant quashed as permitting further prosecution would be the travesty of justice and a mere ritual or formality so for as the prosecution agency is concerned, and unnecessary burden as regards the courts. Appeal allowed.
