Dena Bank v Ghanshyam

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20131; (2001) 5 SCC 169; (2001) SCC (L&S) 786; AIR 2001 SC 2270; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 29; 2001 ALJ 1361; 2002 (1) Bom.C.R. 374; 2001 (90) FLR 365; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 229; 2001 (34) LabIC 2069; 2001 (2) LLJ 252; 2001(4) SCALE 62; 2001 (4) SCJ 125; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 591; 2001 (4) SLR 580; 2001 (4) SLT 392; 2001 (4) Supreme 267

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 11-A , 17-B - Termination - Reinstatement order - Challenged - Respondent was driver of Regional Manager(RM) of appellant - As tenure of RM came to end services of respondent were terminated - Respondent claimed to be appellants workman raised industrial dispute with regard to his retrenchment - Labour Court passed award dt. 8-5-1996 holding that respondent was driver of appellant and ordered his reinstatement with back wages - Order of Tribunal was challenged by appellant in HC - HC by interim order dt.4-5-2000 stayed Labour Court Order subject to reinstatement of respondent and to pay full salary according to law - Hence, instant appeal - Whether directions of HC to pay regular pay scale was justified - Held, relationship of master and servant comes to end when workman was retrenched and hence thereafter workman was not entitled to receive any emoluments from his employer - U/s. 17 B of the 1982 Act if employer prefers appeal on reinstatement order he should pay last drawn full wages to workman provided workman was not employed anywhere - Question whether workman was entitled to full wages last drawn or full salary which he would be entitled to in reinstatement while award was under challenge in HC or SC depends upon terms of order passed by Court which had to be determined on interpretation of order granting relief - HC stayed award of Labour Court on condition that respondent should be reinstated into service and to pay salary regularly in accordance with law - Appellant was paying Rs. 900/- pm to respondent full wages last drawn - S. 17B did not preclude HC or SC from granting better benefits on facts of case - HC neither granted relief u/s. 17 B nor there was mention of this section in its order - While passing interlocutory order interests of employer should not be lost sight of - Even though amount paid by employer u/s. 17 B to workman could not be directed to be refunded in the event employer loses case but any amount paid over and above had to be refunded by workman to employer - Pursuant to Orders of HC appellant had paid amount to respondent - It was clarified that if appellant succeeds in writ petition he would be entitled to recover difference of amount i.e. amount paid under impugned order less amount payable u/s. 17-B from respondent in accordance to law - HC order was modified - Appeal allowed.
