Kewal Chand Mimani (D) By Lrs v S. K. Sen and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20080; (2001) 6 SCC 512; AIR 2001 SC 2569; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 14; 2002 (46) ALR 406; JT 2001 (6) SC 264; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 17; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 746; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 158; 2001(4) SCALE 559; 2001 (4) SCJ 292; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1056; 2001 (5) SLT 469; 2001 (5) Supreme 371

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Occupiers, Tenancy At Sufferance, Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47 - Possession of Land - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Vacant land belonging to Daws as owners was leased out to Mimanis for a period of 50 years - An order of requisition u/s. 3(1) of West Bengal Land (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1948 was issued - Both writ petition and appeal were filed by Mimanis and not by owners - During pendency of suits, lease expired by efflux of time - Owners filed an application for being transposed as appellants - High Court concluded its first judgment by recording that order of requisition could not continue any longer by reason of lapse of Act - Collector informed advocate for Mimanis to remain present at site to receive back possession of land - Service of letter, however, evoked a stiff opposition from advocates of owners who informed Collector about non - compliance with directions of court since Mimanis were otherwise not entitled to receive possession as they were neither owners nor occupiers of property - Appellate bench passed an order directing making over of possession to owners - Mimanis, however, being aggrieved by this order moved this court and maintenance of status quo was directed without creation of any third - party interest - (A) Whether conclusiveness of first judgment stands obliterated without recording of any reason whatsoever and HC has thus fallen into a clear error? - Held, strictly speaking in contextual facts, second judgment of HC is not warranted by reason of specific direction as contained in first judgment - (B) What were rights of Mimanis? - Can Mimanis be ascribed to be trespassers on facts of matter under consideration? - Further held, right of lessee stands obliterated by reason of efflux of time coupled with issuance of requisition order which temporarily suspended lessee's right to occupy though that does not put an end to lessor's right to own premises - (C) Whether can Mimanis be termed to be a tenant holding over? - Further held, holding over stands equivalent to retention of possession after determination of lease, but with consent of landlord - Mimanis stand, admittedly, dispossessed from lease premises and hence cannot be a tenant holding over - Appeal dismissed.
