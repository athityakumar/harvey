Chhotu Ram v Urvashi Gulati and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20739; (2001) 7 SCC 530; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1196; AIR 2001 SC 3468; 2001 CRLJ 4204; 2001 (91) FLR 954; JT 2001 (7) SC 45; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 179; 2001(5) SCALE 479; 2001 (6) SLT 146; 2001 (6) Supreme 401; 2001 (2) UC 374

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Haryana Service of Engineers Class II (Public Works Department, Irrigation Branch) Rules r. 9 - Promotion - Cut off date - Validity -Petitioner's grievance was based on factum of non-consideration of petitioner's case or if considered not properly so considered on basis that petitioner was qualified by the cut-off date - Be it noted however, that this Court as noticed above directed in event petitioner was fit for promotion he should be given necessary promotion with all consequential benefits - Hence instant Contempt petition - Whether the Petitioner had proved the case beyond reasonable doubt - Held, proceeding in the Act being quasi-criminal in nature and the burden being in nature of criminal prosecution, namely to prove beyond reasonable doubt as noticed above, requirements of the statute thus has a pivotal role to play - There was no mandate as such to offer promotion to petitioner - Incidentally, petitioner's case was duly considered but since latter was not found eligible and fit for promotion for reasons noticed as below, no promotion could be offered to petitioner - Promotion was to be offered only however, upon compliance with certain eligibility criteria - This Court by reason of the order did not issue mandate but issued a direction for consideration only - No Merits in petition - Petition dismissed.
