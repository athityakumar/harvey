Shaji Kuriakose and Another v Indian Oil Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20030; (2001) 7 SCC 650; AIR 2001 SC 3341; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 48; 2001 (45) ALR 190; 2001 (4) AWC 2884; JT 2001 (7) SC 4; 2002 (1) KLT 381; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 609; 2001(5) SCALE 367; 2001 (6) SLT 25; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 573; 2001 (6) Supreme 243; 2001 (2) UC 596

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Market Value - Whether, while fixing compensation for acquired land on basis of comparable sales method of valuation of land, rate of compensation can be less than market value of land covered by sale? - Comparable sales method is not always conclusive and there are certain factors which are required to be fulfilled before compensation can be awarded according to value of land reflected in sales; factors are : (1) sale must be genuine transaction, (2) sale deed must have been executed at time proximate to date of issue of notification u/s. 4(3) land covered by sale must be in vicinity of acquired land, (4) land covered by sales must be similar to acquired land, and (5) size of plot of land covered by sales be comparable to land acquired - If there is a dissimilarity in regard to locality, shape, site or nature of land between land covered by sales and land acquired, it is open to court to proportionately reduce compensation for acquired land - Held, since there is a difference in similarity in land acquired and land covered by Exhibit A-4, High Court was fully justified in lowering rate of compensation than what was market value of land covered by Exhibit A-4 - Appeal dismissed.
