Charanjit Singh Chadha and Others v Sudhir Mehra

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21174; (2001) 7 SCC 417; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1557; AIR 2001 SC 3721; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 41; 2001 CRLJ 4255; JT 2001 (7) SC 226; 2002 (1) MPLJ 321; 2001 (129) PLR 833; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 192; 2001(6) SCALE 29; 2001 (2) UC 582

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Contract & Commercial - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 120-B, 406 and 420 - Hire-Purchase Act, 1972 - Hire purchase agreement between appellant and respondents - Respondent default in paying installments and possession of vehicle taken by appellants - Respondents filed complaint against appellants u/ss. 406/420/120-B IPC, 1860 - Magistrate issued summons to appellant - High Court declined to quash proceedings against appellant - Whether HC right in not quashing proceedings against appellant? - Hire-purchase agreement in law is executory contract of sale and confers no right in rem on hirer until conditions for transfer of property to him have been fulfilled - Possession of goods as per term of agreement may not amount to any criminal offence - Agreement specifically gave authority to appellants to repossess vehicle and their agents have been given right to enter any property or building wherein motor vehicle was likely to be kept - Appellants have continued to be owners of vehicle and even if entire allegations against them - Held, no offence was made out against appellant - Complaint and any other proceedings initiated pursuant to such complaint are quashed - Appeal allowed.
