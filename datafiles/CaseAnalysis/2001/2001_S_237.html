Vedabai @ Vaijayantabai Baburao Patil v Shantaram Baburao Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 3664; (2001) 9 SCC 106; AIR 2001 SC 2582; 2001 (44) ALR 577; 2001 (3) AWC 2353; 2002 (173) CTR 300; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 15; [2002] 253 ITR 798; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 82; JT 2001 (5) SC 608; 2001(4) SCALE 506; 2001 (4) SCJ 286; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1053; 2001 (5) SLT 366; 2002 (125) STC 375; 2001 (5) Supreme 266; [2002] 122 TAXMAN 114; 2001 (2) UC 362

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5-Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Whether delay of 7 days in filing appeal on ground of illness constitutes 'sufficient cause' u/s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963? - In exercising discretion u/s. 5 of Limitation Act courts should adopt a pragmatic approach - A distinction must be made between a case where delay is inordinate and a case where delay is of a few - In former case consideration of prejudice to other side will be a relevant factor so case called for a more cautious approach but in latter case no such consideration may arise and such a case deserves a liberal approach - No hard and fast rule can be laid down in this regard - Court has to exercise discretion on facts of each case keeping in mind that in construing expression 'sufficient cause', principle of advancing substantial justice is of prime importance - Held, expression 'sufficient cause' should receive liberal construction, delay condoned - Appeal allowed.
