Ritona Consultancy Private Limited and Others v Lohia Jute Press and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19901; (2001) 3 SCC 68; 2001 (42) ALR 834; JT 2001 (2) SC 616; 2001(1) SCALE 647; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 855; 2001 (2) SLT 214; 2001 (1) Supreme 604; 2001 (1) UC 454

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Nonperformance of contract - Relief - Maintainability - Two suits arose out of contract awarded to Jute Press for preparation and supply of 49, 26,465 photo identity cards to be completed by 3-9-1995 subject to several conditions - Jute Press entered into contracts with the three defendants in a suit to which the State of Assam was not a party - In those suits applications were filed for interim reliefs and the HC, on its original side, made an order appointing court receiver in respect of video cassettes and directions were given to him to take steps for preparation of the voter identity cards and deliver the same on preparation thereof - In furtherance of the order, another order was made framing a scheme for preparation of the identity cards by the court receiver - Appeals were preferred before the DB of the HC against the order of Single Judge framing the scheme - DB directed the joint receivers to complete the work of preparation of photo identity cards within a time frame by taking necessary assistance of outside agency for preparation of the same by an order - Hence instant appeal - Whether SC should refer back the case to HC for pronounce effective orders - Held, contract was awarded by the Govt. of Assam on 17-7-1995 to be completed by 3-9-1995 which was not still complete, though over five years had elapsed - (B) In the disputes between the main contractor and sub-contractors arising out of certain contract to which the State Govt. was not a party, various reliefs were sought for even affecting the rights and interests of the Govt. of Assam; thus it was a case of playing Hamlet without the Prince of Denmark - (C) In the event the contract with the Govt. of Assam and Jute Press was cancelled (for which step there was no impediment) many of the reliefs sought for in the suits filed by either of the parties would become infructous and different kinds of reliefs would have to be worked out - (D) Suits were filed in the HC forum found to be convenient to the parties, and hot where the contract had to be executed or the Govt. upon which it was binding was located - In these circumstances, no useful purpose would be served in keeping these proceedings in SC pending and the orders made by the HC as modified by SC should be effective until further orders were made by the HC either on the trial side or in the appeal side - Appeal disposed of.
