State of Kerala and Another v Puthenkavu N. S. S. Karayogam and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21107; (2001) 10 SCC 191; 2001 (44) ALR 598; JT 2001 (5) SC 559; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 75

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - Respondent filed suit for declaration of title and injunction - Trial Court dismissed suit and first appellate court appeal, but High Court u/s. 100, CPC, 1908 interfered with concurrent findings on facts and granted a perpetual injunction - Whether any substantial question was formed by HC? - Held, Single Judge has not formulated any question of law, much less any substantial question of law, which alone would have clothed the HC with jurisdiction u/s. 100, CPC, 1908 to deal with a second appeal - Unless HC is satisfied that there is a substantial question of law, jurisdiction for second appeal cannot be exercised - Appeal disposed of.
