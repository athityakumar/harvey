Kalpana Kothari and another v Sudha Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19755; (2002) 1 SCC 203; AIR 2002 SC 404; 2002 (46) ALR 11; 2002 (1) ALT(SC) 30; 2001 (3) ARBLR 487; 2002 (1) AWC 115B; 2002 (3) CLC 425; 2002 (1) ESC 14; JT 2001 (9) SC 337; 2001 (3) RAJ 452; 2001(7) SCALE 560; 2001 (7) SLT 633; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 598; 2001 (8) Supreme 402; 2002 (1) UC 167

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 34 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 8 - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 44(g) - (A) Scope and object of Act 1940 and 1996 and applicability of estoppel - Held, in this regard plea of estoppel can have no application to deprive appellants of legitimate right to invoke an all comprehensive provision of mandatory character like s. 8 of 1996 Act to have matter relating to disputes referred to arbitration, in terms of arbitration agreement - (B) Need for desirability of appointment of receiver and granting injunction - Balance of convenience - Further held, High Court does not seem to have taken into account overall necessity to balance interests of both parties - Appeal allowed.
