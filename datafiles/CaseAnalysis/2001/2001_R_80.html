State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Raj Pal Singh

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21338; (2010) 5 SCC 783; 2002 (4) AWC 2946; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 44; 2001 (4) SLR 637

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Respondent was an Asst. Warder and allegation against him was that he along with four other Asst. Warders beat one 'X', and even though senior officers disuaded them, they never listened to that - These allegations were proved in a departmental proceedings and disciplinary authority passed order of dismissal - Tribunal refused to interfere but High Court came to conclusion that charges and delinquency being same and identical, so order of dismissal cannot be sustained and also directed that delinquent respondent would be paid only 50% of back wages - Whether conclusion drawn by HC is justified or not? - Held, reasonings given by HC cannot be faulted with since State is not able to indicate as to any difference in delinquency of these employees - It is undoubtedly open for disciplinary authority to deal with delinquency and once charges are established to award appropriate punishment - But when charges are same and identical in relation to one and same incident, then to deal with delinquents differently in award of punishment, would be discriminatory - In this view of matter, court see no infirmity with impugned order requiring interference u/art. 136 - Further court observed that HC by impugned judgment has directed that delinquent would be paid 50% of back wages, but having regard to nature of charges against respondent, court is not inclined to allow any back wage from period of dismissal till date of reinstatement - Appeal disposed of.
