N. R. Nair and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, U.C. Banerjee, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20094; (2001) 6 SCC 84; AIR 2001 SC 2337; 2001 (1) JCC 325; JT 2001 (1) SC 593; 2001 (3) KLT 257; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 333; 2001(4) SCALE 20; 2001 (3) SCJ 209; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 353; 2001 (4) SLT 47; 2001 (4) Supreme 25; 2001 (2) UC 168

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960, ss. 11, 21, 22 and 24 - Prohibition to training and exhibition of animals - Whether power contained in u/s. 22 of Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act is unguided? - Reading of Act as a whole clearly shows that implicit in s. 22 is necessity for Govt. to come to conclusion that if a notification under said section is not issued there would be unnecessary pain or suffering in training or exhibition of animals - Held, power contained in s. 22 of Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act is not unguided - (B) Whether in exercise of judicial review courts can go into correctness of decision of Govt. in issuing a notification? - Further held, agree with decision of High Court that in exercise of judicial review - Neither HC nor Supreme Court can go into correctness of decision of Govt. in issuing impugned notification - Appeal dismissed.
