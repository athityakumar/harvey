Haji Abdul Shakoor and Company v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21039; (2002) 9 SCC 760; AIR 2002 SC 2423; JT 2001 (10) SC 438; 2002 (3) RAJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Appellant a class 'B' contractor for supplying meat group items to respondent 2 - Appellant became class 'A' contractor vide letter issued on that behalf, enhancing his contract carrying capacity - On request of appellant his contract carrying capacity to tune of Rs. 6.5 crores was transferred by respondent No. 2 to respondent No. 3 - Respondent 3 accepted such transfer vide its letter - Respondent 3 by its further order curtailed appellant's contract carrying capacity to half - Whether such order is justified and whether court can interfered with such order? - Civil right is involved in case which cannot be taken away or even curtailed except in accordance with law - Admitted fact, before passing impugned order no opportunity of being heard was afforded to appellant by respondent 3, thus impugned order suffers from vice of violation of principles of natural justice and such an order cannot but be an arbitrary order - Impugned order quashed - HC's order set aside - Appeal allowed.
