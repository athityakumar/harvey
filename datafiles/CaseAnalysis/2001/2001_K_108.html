New India Assurance Co., Shimla v Kamla And Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19818; (2001) 4 SCC 342; (2001) SCC (Cr) 701; 2002 (1) ACC 346; AIR 2001 SC 1419; AIR 2001 SCW 1340; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 24; 2001 (43) ALR 378; 2001 (2) AWC 1405; [2001] 105 Comp Cas 398; 2011 (1) CPJ(Sc) 10; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 916; JT 2001 (4) SC 235; 2001 (127) PLR 830; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 716; 2001(3) SCALE 18; 2001 (2) SCJ 1; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 797; 2001 (3) SLT 150; 2001 (3) Supreme 84; 2001 (1) UC 491; 2001 (1) UC 559

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Miscllaneous - Motor Vehicles Act 1988, ss. 15, 146 and 147 - Can a fake driving license be renewed by authorities and if not, would insurance co. be liable in respect of a motor accident when a fake license is in possession of driver? - A fake licence cannot get its forgery outfit stripped off merely on account of some officer renewing same with or without knowing it to be forged - Any counterfeit document showing that it contains a purported order of a statutory authority would ever remain counterfeit albeit fact that other persons including some statutory authorities would have acted on document unwittingly on assumption that it is genuine - Held, insurer who is made statutorily liable to pay compensation to third parties on account of certificate of insurance issued shall be entitled to recover from insured amount paid to third parties, if there was any breach of policy conditions on account of vehicle being driven without a valid driving licence - Fact that whether there was a breach of policy conditions is to be determined by claims tribunal - Appeals disposed of.
