Anand Prasad Agarwalla v Tarkeshwar Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20124; (2001) 5 SCC 568; AIR 2001 SC 2367; 2002 (48) ALR 295; 2002 (5) AWC 4137; 2001 (2) CGLJ 179; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 139; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 271; 2002 (93) RD 675; 2001(4) SCALE 149; 2001 (4) SLT 288; 2001 (4) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 31 - Grant of temporary injunction - Division bench held that there are two documents which indicated that there was prima facie case to be investigated - Held, unless sale certificate is set aside or declared to be a nullity, same has legal validity and force - It cannot be said that no right could be derived from such certificate - Secondly, when contesting respondents were in possession as evidenced by record of rights, it cannot be said that such possession is by a trespasser - Claim of contesting respondents is in their own right - Serious questions raised in suit - Prima facie case for consideration - Temporary injunction granted by High Court subject to conditions - On facts, course of HC, is correct - Appeals dismissed.
