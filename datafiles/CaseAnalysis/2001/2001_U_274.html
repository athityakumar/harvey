Society of St. Joseph's College v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 205; (2002) 1 SCC 273; AIR 2002 SC 195; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 60; 2002 (46) ALR 240; 2002 (5) ESC 227; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 290; JT 2001 (9) SC 538; 2002 (1) KLT 438; 2001(8) SCALE 206; 2001 (5) SCJ 303; 2001 (8) SLT 119; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 47; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 273; 2001 (8) Supreme 211

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Madras Requisition and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1956

Summary: The safeguard provision in Article 31 and 30 (1A) of the Constitution has been introduced by the Parliament in order to ensure that acquisition of the property of minority educational institutions is such as does not restrict or abrogate the right guaranteed under Article 30, by paying them inadequate amounts in exchange of their property.
