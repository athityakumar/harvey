Deena Nath v Pooran Lal

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20105; (2001) 5 SCC 705; AIR 2001 SC 2655; 2002 (48) ALR 259; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 8; JT 2001 (5) SC 380; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 130; 2001(4) SCALE 312; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 925; 2001 (5) SLT 195; 2001 (5) Supreme 100

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1)(a), 12(1)(f) and 13 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Appellant-landlord filed suit seeking eviction of the respondent tenant on the grounds of default and bona fide requirement u/s. 12(1)(a) and (f) - Trial Court decreed suit for eviction - High Court dismissed suit for eviction - Hence, present appeal - Bona fide requirement must be in present and must be manifested in actual need which would evidence court that it is not a mere fanciful or whimsical desire - Held, judgment of HC clearly bears out position that Lower Courts had failed to consider requirement of s. 12 regarding availability of reasonable accommodation in occupation of the appellant-landlord - Further held, requirement of appellant-landlord cannot be termed to be a bona fide requirement within meaning of statute therefore HC was justified in interfering with concurrent judgments in contextual facts - Appeal dismissed.
