V. M. Kurian v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19887; (2001) 4 SCC 215; AIR 2001 SC 1409; JT 2001 (4) SC 220; 2001 (2) KLT 70; 2001(3) SCALE 115; 2001 (2) SCJ 675; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 818; 2001 (3) SLT 471; 2001 (3) Supreme 112

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Kerala Municipalities Act, 1960, Kerala Municipal Corporation Act, 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Municipalities Act, 1960, s. 222 - Kerala Municipal Corporation Act, 1961, ss. 367 and 238 - Kerela Land Building Rules, r.5, 15, 21 - Construction - Exemption granted by state Govt. for construction of eight - storeyed building under Kerela land building rules challenged - Under rules, there is restriction with regard to maximum height of building - Observance and compliance with rules is for public safety and convenience - There cannot be relaxation of rules which are mandatory in nature and cannot be dispensed with especially in case of a high-rise building - In present case, deviations are of high magnitude, which are contrary to public safety and convenience - Held, order passed by state Govt. exempting provisions of rules for constructing an eight storeyed building was contrary to mandatory provisions of rules and therefore, is not sustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
