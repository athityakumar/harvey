Kari Choudhary v Sita Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21251; (2002) SCC (Cr) 269; (2002) 1 SCC 714; AIR 2002 SC 441; 2002 (1) CCR 53; 2002 (1) CHN 108; 2002 (1) Crimes 11; 2002 (2) CRJ 139; 2002 CRLJ 923; 2002 (3) CTC 385; 2002 (1) JCC 151; JT 2001 (10) SC 361; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 86; 2001(8) SCALE 445; 2001 (8) SLT 373; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 588; 2001 (8) Supreme 706; 2002 (1) UC 478

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 202 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Respondent No. 1 lodged FIR for murder of her sister-in-law - While investigation it was that respondents are real culprits - Second FIR was lodged by police against respondents - Respondents moved to High Court for quashing criminal proceedings - HC quashed proceedings and absolved respondents from charge of murder - Whether HC right in quashing criminal proceedings even without conducting any trial into case registered against respondents? - Police to investigate into murder in regard to first FIR lodged by respondents - Held, legal position is that there cannot be two FIRs against same accused in respect of same case - But when there is rival versions in respect of same episode, then there would be two different FIRs and investigation can be carried on under both of them by same investigating agency - HC not right to quash criminal proceedings merely on ground that final report had been laid in first FIR too technical - Ultimate object of every investigation is to find out whether offences alleged have been committed and, if so, who have committed it - Appeal allowed.
