Union of India v Rajendra Prabhu

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 442; (2001) 4 SCC 472; (2001) SCC (Cr) 745; AIR 2001 SC 1672; 2001 (96) ECR 221; 2001 (129) E.L.T. 286; 2001 (1) JCC 281; JT 2001 (4) SC 97; 2001(2) SCALE 608; 2001 (3) SLT 313B; 2001 (2) Supreme 545

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 11(a) and 123 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether High Court had erred in concluding that finding recorded by statutory authorities is perverse? - Even though HC rightly noticed its limited jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution, it still proceeded to reappreciate evidence on record and substitute its subjective opinion in place of concurrent findings given by statutory authorities - Held, finding of HC that conclusions arrived at by statutory authority were not based on any evidence or could be regarded as perverse, is erroneous and contrary to facts - Appeal allowed.
