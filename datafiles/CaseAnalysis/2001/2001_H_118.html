Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others v Hindustan Petroleum Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20006; (2001) 8 SCC 143; AIR 2001 SC 3630; JT 2001 (7) SC 370; 2001(6) SCALE 23; 2001 (6) SLT 329; 2002 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 700; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 50; 2001 (6) Supreme 785

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Public Health Act, Improvement Trust Act, Lunacy Act, 1890

Summary: Land & Property - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 170, 220 - A and 231 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11 and 16 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Public Health Act, s. 149 - Lunacy Act, 1890, s. 134 - Improvement Trust Act, ss. 45 to 49, 54 and 54-A - As a result of acquisition proceedings, land, drain and all easement right to discharge water in drain came to be vested in State free from all encumbrances - State Govt. leased out certain portions of acquired land to respondents in these two appeals for setting up their industries - Whether drain wherein respondents are discharging their effluents vest in corporation? - Whether corporation has no power to levy or recover any sewerage charges? - Held, water channel or drain belonging to State Govt. after complying with procedure provided u/s. 220-A of Mumbai municipal corporation Act, either can vest in corporation for management or same can vest in corporation after State Govt. transfers its right or possession in water channel or drain to corporation - As and when State Govt. vests or transfers said drains in favour of corporation as contemplated u/s. 220-A of Act, appellant would be entitled to levy and realise sewerage charges from respondents - Appeals dismissed.
