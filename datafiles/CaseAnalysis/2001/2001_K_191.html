Khoday Eswarsa and Sons v Commissioner of Gift Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Brijesh Kumar, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20752; (2001) 9 SCC 457; AIR 2001 SC 4038; 2001 (170) CTR 553; [2001] 251 ITR 883; JT 2001 (9) SC 13; 2001(7) SCALE 237; 2001 (7) SLT 334; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 178; 2001 (7) Supreme 654; [2001] 119 TAXMAN 346

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958, ss. 26(1), 4(1)(a), 5(1)(xiv), 6(1), 6(2), 6(3) - Gift Tax Rules, 1958, r.11(1) - Deemed Gift - Taxability - Appellant/company as consideration for agreement, agreed to pay minimum fee or compensation of Rs. 50,000 per month and maximum of Rs. 60,000 per month - Gift-Tax Officer treated transfer of business by firm to private limited company as gift under the Gift Tax Act made for inadequate consideration and levied gift tax on value of deemed gift which was computed at Rs. 1,10,25000 - On appeal Appellate Authority held that deemed gift made by assessee under agreement was exempt u/s. 5(1)(xiv) of the Act on ground that gift made was for purpose of business of donor - On appeal, Tribunal reversed order of CIT(Appeals) - Reference was made to HC - HC answered questions against assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in holding that there was deemed gift taxable under the Act - Held, Tribunal as well as HC clearly fell into error in upholding levy of gift tax on capitalized value of gift taking gift as for five years and treating as irrelevant clause providing for termination at 6 months notice and also taking into consideration fact that agreement was not cancelled within period of 5 years - U/r. 11(1) what was of relevance was number of complete years for which gift was not revocable - Thus, it was not revocable for specified period which was six months - Capitalized value had to be fixed u/r. 11(1) which if it was revocable for period less than one year, value could not be worked out and, thus, it had to be 'nil' - Hence, Tribunal as well as HC were in error in rejecting assessee's contention as there was no other provision under which capitalized value could be fixed - Appeal allowed.
