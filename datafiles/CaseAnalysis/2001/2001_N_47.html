State of Maharashtra and Another v Najakat Alias Mubarak Ali

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20671; (2001) 6 SCC 311; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1106; AIR 2001 SC 2255; 2001 (2) CCR 244; 2001 CRLJ 2588; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 279; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 778; 2001(4) SCALE 71; 2001 (4) SCJ 220; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 600; 2001 (4) SLT 1; 2001 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 676; 2001 (4) Supreme 45

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 428 - Applicability and scope - Majority - The purpose of s. 428 of Code is for advancing amelioration to prisoner - The section does not contain any indication that if prisoner was in jail as an under trial prisoner in a second case benefit envisaged in section would be denied to him in respect of second case - The purpose is that convicted person is given right to reckon period of his sentence of imprisonment from date he was in jail as an under trial prisoner - In other words, period of his being in jail as an under trial prisoner would be added as a part of period of imprisonment to which he is sentenced - Two requisites postulated in s. 428 of Code: 1. During stage of investigation, enquiry or trial of a particular case prisoner should have been in jail at least for a certain period, 2. He should have been sentenced to a term of imprisonment in that case - If above two conditions are satisfied then operative part of provision comes into play - If sentence of imprisonment awarded is longer than period of detention undergone by him during stages of investigation, enquiry or trial, convicted person need undergo only balance period of imprisonment after deducting earlier period from total period of imprisonment awarded - The words if any in section amplify that if there is no balance period left after such deduction convict will be entitled to be set free from jail, unless he is required in any other case - In other words, if convict was in prison, for whatever reason, during stages of investigation, enquiry or trial of a particular case and was later convicted and sentenced to any term of imprisonment in that case earlier period of detention undergone by him should be counted as part of sentence imposed on him - Reading s. 428 of Code in above perspective, words of same case are not to be understood as suggesting that set-off is allowable only if earlier jail life was undergone by him exclusively for case in which sentence is imposed - The period during which accused was in prison subsequent to inception of a particular case, should be credited towards period of imprisonment awarded as sentence in that particular case - It is immaterial that prisoner was undergoing sentence of imprisonment in another case also during said period - Words of same case were used to refer to pre-sentence period of detention undergone by him - Nothing more can be made out of collocation of those words - S. 428 of Code permits accused to have period undergone by him in jail as an under trial prisoner set off against period of sentence imposed on him irrespective of whether he was in jail in connection with same case during that period - Contra - Sethi, J (dissenting) - Held, a plain reading of s. 428 of Code makes it clear that period of detention which section permits to be set off against term of imprisonment imposed on accused upon conviction, must be during investigation, enquiry or trial in connection with same case in which he has been convicted - Accused tried for various offences in one trial can be held to be entitled to benefit of s. 428 of Code being tried for same case - Words same case appearing in section are ejusdem generis to preceding words investigation, enquiry or trial - If period of detention relating to investigation, enquiry or trial is in a different case, that would not ipso facto entitle accused to claim benefit of s. 428 but that may permit him to persuade court to pass appropriate orders in terms of s. 427, keeping in view period of his under trial detention in other cases as well - It is need of time that court convicting accused should develop a healthy practice of specifying in order total period of pre-conviction detention that he has undergone in that case or in some other case for purposes of awarding sentence upon conviction - Appeal allowed.
