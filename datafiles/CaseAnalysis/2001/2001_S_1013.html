M. S. Chawla and Others v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20160; (2001) 5 SCC 358; (2001) SCC (L&S) 825; AIR 2001 SC 1706; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 281; 2001 (3) LLN 710; 2001(3) SCALE 435; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 81; 2001 (3) SLT 498; 2001 (3) Supreme 446; 2001 (1) UC 648

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Advocate & Judges - Consumer Protection Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Punjab Civil Services Rules, rr. 21, 7 and 18 - Consumer Protection Rules, 1987, r. 3(1) - Punjab Civil Services (Pension) Rules - Whether pension amount received by appellants in respect of their previous services as district judges, can be deducted from salary of President of district consumer forum, fixed under provisions of Consumer Protection Act? - Appointment of a district judge, after his superannuation as President of district consumer forum cannot but be held to be a case of re-employment of a pensioner in as much as said district judge is in receipt of a pension for services rendered as a district judge in accordance with provisions contained in Punjab Civil Services - Conclusion is irresistible that appropriate authority will have to decide pay and allowances, which retired district judge is entitled to receive on being appointed as President of district forum notwithstanding fixation of such pay under rules framed under Consumer Protection Act and while fixing same, principle underlined in Note 3(a)(i) has to be followed - Appeal dismissed.
