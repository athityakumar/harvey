P. Janardhana Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19853; (2001) 6 SCC 50; AIR 2001 SC 2631; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 129; 2001 (44) ALR 498; JT 2001 (5) SC 416; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 678; 2001(4) SCALE 327; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 969; 2001 (5) Supreme 151

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Land & Property; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - High Court Rules - In November 1996, an inquiry was made into alleged irregularities in matter of payment of compensation for acquired lands - Whether on facts and circumstances of case HC was right in quashing notification appointing commission of inquiry on sole ground that state Govt. had not formed an opinion as required u/s. 3 of Act before issuing notification? - Held, appointment of a commission is a matter at discretion of appropriate Govt. - Such an appointment is meant primarily for purpose of information of Govt. - There is no prescribed form or particular manner for appropriate Govt. to express its opinion that it is necessary to appoint a commission of inquiry - When Chief Minister of state expressed on floor of state legislature that he has no objection to appointment of a commission of inquiry under Act to inquire into serious allegations regarding irregularities in payment of compensation for acquired land, it is reasonable to presume that he had given necessary thought to matter and on being satisfied that it is necessary so to do expressed his agreement for appointment of a commission of inquiry under Act - HC omitted to look to main purpose of appointment of commission of inquiry under Act and interfered with notification issued by state Govt. without firm basis in law and without justification on facts - Appeals allowed.
