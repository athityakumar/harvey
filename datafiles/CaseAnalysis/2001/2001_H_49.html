Dental Council of India and Another v Hari Prakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20000; (2001) 8 SCC 61; AIR 2001 SC 3303; 2001 (93) DLT 291; JT 2001 (7) SC 342; 2001(5) SCALE 573; 2001 (4) SCJ 489; 2001 (4) SLR 227; 2001 (6) SLT 291; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 310; 2001 (6) Supreme 556

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug

Summary: Service - Health and Drug - Dentists Act, 1948, ss. 2(j) and 3(d) - All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956, s. 23 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 3, 4 and 10 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Valid Qualification - Election - Whether cancellation of respondent's election to dental council is valid? - It is for Parliament to amend law to give representation appropriately in council to AIIMS - Other modes of interpretation not to be considered when language of provision itself is absolutely clear - Qualification of respondent not from recognized university - Held, cancellation of respondent's election to dental council is valid - Appeal allowed.
