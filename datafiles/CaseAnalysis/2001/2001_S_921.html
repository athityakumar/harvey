Sudhir and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19902; (2001) SCC (Cr) 387; (2001) 2 SCC 688; AIR 2001 SC 826; 2001 (1) CGLJ 401; 2001 (1) Crimes 226; 2001 CRLJ 1072; 2001 (1) JCC 129; JT 2001 (2) SC 274; 2001 (1) KLT 682; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 743; 2001(1) SCALE 600; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 813; 2001 (1) SLT 859; 2001 (1) Supreme 569; 2001 (1) UC 437

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 26, 209, 227, 228 and 323 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 127, 147, 149 and 307 - (A) Framing of charge for offence u/s. 307, IPC - Held, accused cannot claim, on ground of parity, that such charge should also be framed against respondents, especially when materials placed in present case do not warrant framing of charge u/s. 307, IPC against respondents - It is settled law that charge is to be framed on basis of material available in that particular case and Judge or Magistrate should not be influenced by any other consideration - (B) Whether Sessions judge was right in transferring one of two counter cases to Chief Judicial Magistrate, as one was exclusively triable by him? - Further held, when a case involving offence not exclusively triable by such court is committed, Sessions Judge has to exercise a discretion regarding case which he has to continue for trial in his court and case which he has to transfer to Chief Judicial Magistrate - In such situations it is advisable for him to try such offence in his court there is no legal obligation to transfer case to Chief Judicial Magistrate - Sessions Judge ought not to have transferred second case to Chief Judicial Magistrate as he did, but he himself should have tried it in manner indicated in Nathi Lal (1988 Indlaw SC 519) - Appeal allowed.
