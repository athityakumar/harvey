Paradip Port Trust and Others v Unique Builders

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19790; (2001) 2 SCC 680; AIR 2001 SC 846; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 49; 2001 (1) ARBLR 505; 2001 (1) CompLJ 353; JT 2001 (2) SC 477; 2001 (1) RAJ 184; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 699; 2001(1) SCALE 504; 2001 (2) SCJ 166; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 668; 2001 (1) SLT 758; 2001 (1) Supreme 584

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Corporate - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30 and 33 - Dispute arose - Refer to arbitrator - No oral evidence before arbitrator was led - Both parties relied only on correspondence between them - Non speaking award - Held, it is clear that generally an award passed by arbitrator is considered binding between parties for reason that parties select arbitrator and powers of court to set aside award are restricted to cases set out in s. 30 - Award clearly indicated that fair and sufficient opportunity was given to parties - No violation of principle of natural justice in passing award - It is not open to courts to guess or speculate reasons for award when it is non-reasoned - Courts cannot attempt to investigate mental process by which arbitrator arrived at conclusion where it is not visible from award - Jurisdiction of courts not independent of statute - Arbitrator's award is final both on facts as well as law - There is no appeal from arbitrator's verdict - Appeal No. 3683 of 1996 dismissed and Appeal No 4144 of 1996 partly allowed.
