R. V. Srinath Prasad v Nandamuri Jayakrishna and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20240; (2001) 4 SCC 71; AIR 2001 SC 1056; JT 2001 (3) SC 303; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 709; 2001(2) SCALE 347; 2001 (2) SLT 464B; 2001 (2) Supreme 412; 2001 (1) UC 513B

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Custody of minor children - Appellant filed appeal against interim custody of his two sons should be given to maternal grandparents - High Court also granted liberty to move for interim custody and held that arrangement made in order is only an interim arrangement and will not create any right or prejudice to case of either side - Held, custody of minor children is a sensitive issue - It is also a matter involving sentimental attachment - Such a matter is to be approached and tackled carefully - A balance has to be struck between the attachment and sentiments of the parties towards the minor children and the welfare of the minors which is of paramount importance - Further, HC appears to have overlooked settled principle that custody orders by their nature can never be final - Before a change is made it must be proved to be in paramount interest of children - Merits in case is not pleaded since application for custody is pending for adjudication before Family Court - Appellant allowed to take custody of children - Appeals allowed.
