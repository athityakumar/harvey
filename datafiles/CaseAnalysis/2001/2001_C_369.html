South Malabar Gramin Bank v Coordination Committee of South Malabar Gramin Bank Employee's Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20316; (2001) 4 SCC 101; (2001) SCC (L&S) 669; AIR 2001 SC 1028; JT 2001 (2) SC 175; 2001 LabIC 881; 2001 (1) LLJ 743; 2001 (2) LLN 60; 2001(1) SCALE 540; 2001 (1) SCJ 566; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 742; 2001 (1) SLT 882; 2001 (1) Supreme 528

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Parity, Living Wage, Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(1), 32, 38, 39(d) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 18 - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, ss. 17, 17(1) - Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, ss. 9, 9(1) - Employees of Rural Banks had filed writ petitions before SC that they were entitled to get same scale of pay, as was available to employees of rural banks of different nationalised commercial banks and Govt. did not meet their demand to revise pay scale and that s. 17 of Act, be struck down, as being ultra vires of arts. 14 and 16(1) of Constitution and writ in nature of mandamus be issued directing Union of India to fix emoluments of Regional Rural Bank Employees in conformity with judicial maxim of Equal pay for equal work and industry-cum-region formula and bring about parity in emoluments between employees of Regional Rural Banks inter se and employees of Nationalised Commercial Banks - SC directed Central Govt. to refer dispute to Tribunal - Tribunal, passed award and directed that officers and employees of Regional Rural Banks would be entitled to claim parity with officers and other employees of sponsor banks in matter of pay scale, allowances and other benefits - Further held that it was matter which had to be decided by Central Govt. in consultation with such authorities as it may consider necessary - When pay structure of employees of Nationalised Commercial Banks were further revised in 1992 and 1997 by means of 6th and 7th Bipartite Settlements, as there was no corresponding revision of pay structure of employees of Regional Rural Banks - Union of India and South Malabar Gramin Bank Management filed petition before HC - Bank took stand that it was Govt., who had to determine salary of employees of Regional Rural Banks in terms of s. 17 of 1976, Act, and on basis of revised pay structure of Nationalised Commercial Bank Employees, pursuant to 6th and 7th Bipartite Settlements, employees of Regional Rural Banks ipso facto could not get their pay structure changed - It was also stated that in fact RBI had appointed Committee to examine pay structure of employees of Regional Rural Banks and to suggest changes therein - Single Judge came to conclusion that by decision of Govt. implementing award of Justice and thereby extended benefits of 4th and 5th Bipartite Settlements and on principle having accepted same, further consideration by Govt. or any order by Govt. was not necessary for extending benefits of subsequent Bipartite Settlements to employees and officers of Regional Rural Banks and accordingly, impugned direction was issued, which was upheld in appeal by DB - Hence instant appeals - Whether award given by Justice Obul Reddi and accepted by State Govt. and given effect to, can be construed to mean that pay scales and other emoluments of Regional Rural Bank employees would stand automatically altered, as and when pay structure of the employees of commercial banks get revised, on basis of bipartite settlement between employer and employees of those commercial banks - Held, on construction of award of Justice and provisions of second proviso to s. 17(1) of Act, revision of pay structure of employees of Regional Rural Banks could be made, only after Central Govt. exercises its power under provisions of Act and determines same - If however, Central Govt. fails to discharge its obligation as in case in hand, which would result in gross disparity between pay-scales of commercial banks and Regional Rural Banks, then mandamus could be issued to Central Govt. for performance of its duties and Central Govt. would be bound to perform its duties, taking into account all germane factors, including factor of subsequent pay revision of employees of the Nationalised Commercial Banks - Further it held that tribunal had devoted considerable time and considered all aspects of dispute between parties and notification itself of Union Govt. in appointing Tribunal, indicated that decision of tribunal shall be final and binding - Hence, conclusions arrived at on different questions raised by tribunal, could not be assailed indirectly by taking recourse to a procedure either by Union Government or by Bank - Hence, Union Govt. possesses power to determine pay structure in accordance with second proviso to s. 17(1) and, therefore, that power had to be exercised soon after any pay revision of employees of Nationalised Commercial Banks was effected and while exercising that power, Union Govt. should try to maintain parity between pay structure of employees of Regional Rural Banks and employees of Nationalsied Commercial Banks - Impugned judgment of HC, stand set aside - Appeals disposed of.
