Union of India and others v P. Chandra Mouli and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21433; (2003) 10 SCC 196

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules,1965, r.19(i) -Illegal demonstration - Court martialled and convicted - Challenged - Sustainability - Respondents were arrested as they led demonstration for considerable period and were Court martialled in Court martial proceedings - Respondents filed appeal in HC and SC but were dismissed - Respondents applied for remission and obtained a remission by order dt.16-7-1987 by which sentence awarded in Court martial had become final - Competent authority issued show cause notice u/r.19(i) of the Rules - SC had granted stay order restraining employer to proceed with disciplinary action and later modified that enquiry proceedings could be taken but punishment should not be awarded until further orders of SC - No indication as to disciplinary action when appeal of respondents was dismissed - Hence, appropriate authority u/r.19(i) of the Rules ordered compulsory retirement of these respondents - Aggrieved respondents filed writ petition in HC - Single Judge set aside order of compulsory retirement holding that there had been no compliance of r.19 of the Rules and to consider lesser punishment - On appeal DB confirmed order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether orders of Single Judge confirmed by DB were justified - Held, appropriate notice was issued in terms of proviso to r.19 of the Rules as respondents were convicted and sentenced on a criminal charge was not disputed - Settled law was that once charge against delinquent was established quantum of punishment should be decided by employer and Court would not ordinarily interfere with order on quantum of punishment - Orders of Single Judge and DB were set aside - Appeals allowed.
