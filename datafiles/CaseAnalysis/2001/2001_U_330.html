Rathi Menon v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20238; (2001) 3 SCC 714; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1311; 2001 (1) ACC 453; AIR 2001 SC 1333; AIR 2001 SCW 1074; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 52; 2001 (1) CGLJ 518; JT 2001 (3) SC 457; 2001 (2) KLT 12; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 1; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 224; 2001(2) SCALE 432; 2001 (2) SCJ 498; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 365; 2001 (2) SLT 624; 2001 (2) Supreme 314

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Railway Accidents (Compensation) Rules, 1990, Railway (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Railways Act, 1989 - Railway (Amendment) Act, 1994 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, ss. 124 - A and 126 - Railway Accidents (Compensation) Rules, 1990 - As a result of falling off a train, and coming under it, appellant became a one-handed and one-legged paraplegic within span of less than an hour - Claims tribunal awarded a total amount of six lakhs of rupees with regret that it could not award more due to ceiling imposed by statutory rules - A division bench of High Court had chosen to slash down substantially compensation amount awarded to her - Whether HC was so helpless that learned judges could not confirm amount awarded to her by claims tribunal? - Held, provision for payment of interim relief indicated in s. 126 of Act has no utility for deciding as to what should be total amount of compensation payable to injured or other claimant - Right of injured to claim compensation as well as liability of railway administration are both reposed in s. 124-A of Act - Right is to maintain an action and recover damages - Liability is to 'pay compensation to such extent as may be prescribed' - Relevance of date of untoward incident is that right to claim compensation from railway administration would be acquired by injured on that date - If a person files a suit amount of compensation will depend upon what court considers just and reasonable on date of determination - Hence when he goes before claims tribunal claiming compensation determination of amount should be as on date of such determination - Court direct railway administration to pay amount awarded by claims tribunal to appellant - Appeals allowed.
