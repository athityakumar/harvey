Union of India and Others v Sunil Kumar Sarkar

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20251; (2001) 3 SCC 414; (2001) SCC (L&S) 600; AIR 2001 SC 1092; 2002 (4) AWC 2928; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 264; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 193; 2001 LabIC 1114; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 40; 2001(2) SCALE 286; 2001 (2) SLR 271; 2001 (2) SLT 505; 2001 (2) Supreme 199; 2001 (1) UC 518

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India 1950, art. 20 - Army Act, 1950, ss. 4(1) and 4(4) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10 - Whether there was need to keep respondent under suspension even when he was undergoing a sentence of imprisonment? - Respondent was due to be released on 27-1-1977 after serving six months' RI imposed on him - After his release in normal course, he was entitled to claim reinstatement in service unless departmental proceedings were initiated against him for misconduct for which he was convicted - Authority thought it necessary to keep respondent under suspension, hence, orders u/ r.10 of Central Rules were issued keeping respondent under suspension - Held, authority concerned was well within its statutory power to keep respondent under suspension - (B) Whether punished for same misconduct both under Army Act as also under Central Rules would tantamount to 'double jeopardy' and will result in violation of art. 20 of Constitution? - These two proceedings operate in two different fields though crime or misconduct might arise out of same act - Court - martial proceedings deal with penal aspect of misconduct while proceedings under Central Rules deal with disciplinary aspect of misconduct - Further held, two proceedings do not overlap - Issued under Central Rules and u/ss. 4(1) and 4(4) of Army Act makes this position clear - Appeal allowed.
