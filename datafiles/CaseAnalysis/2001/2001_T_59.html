State of Himachal Pradesh v Tarsem Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21140; (2001) 8 SCC 104; AIR 2001 SC 3431; JT 2001 (7) SC 460; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 809; 2001(6) SCALE 92; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966 - Punjab Restitution of Mortgaged Lands Act, 1938, ss. 3, 4, 8, - Vested interest - Easementary interest - Land in a village was used for grazing purposes by village community for grazing their cattles - Subsequently, land came to be vested in Gram Panchayat - Village community continued to exercise their right of grazing and other such right over said pasture land - Thereafter, State passed an Act of 1974 and u/ s. 3 all rights, title and interests in land in any estate vested in Panchayat, u/s. 4 of the Punjab Act vested in State free from all encumbrances - Respondent-plaintiff, brought representative suit for declaration that land in dispute was being used for grazing cattle, cutting fuel wood and for other common purposes and thus it was their easementary right and appellant- defendant be restrained from interfering in their rights and enjoyment of said land - Trial Court decreed suit - Appeal preferred by appellant was substantially dismissed - HC also dismissed second appeal preferred by appellant - HC held that u/s. 3 of the 1938 Act, only interest and right in land would vest in State and easementary right of grazing being over land same has not vested in State u/s. 3 of the 1938 Act - In that view of the matter, State has no authority to interfere with easementary right of village community - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in holding that easementary right had not vested in State - Held, u/s. 3 of the 1938 Act, all rights, title and interests including easementary rights stood extinguished and all such rights, title and interests vested in State free from all encumbrances - Further, it was not disputed that land in dispute was pasture land and was being used for grazing - s. 8 of the 1938 Act provides purpose for which land vested in State Government can be utilized - If purpose of vesting was to provide land to village community for grazing, there was no reason why land be not be used as a pasture land for grazing - Therefore, Courts below fell in error in holding that the easementary right had not vested in the State - Appeal allowed.
