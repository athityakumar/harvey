State of Gujarat v Umedbhai M. Patel

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 367; (2001) 3 SCC 314; (2001) SCC (L&S) 576; AIR 2001 SC 1109; 2001 (2) AWC 1024; 2001 (2) CLR 225; 2001 (89) FLR 173; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2461; JT 2001 (3) SC 223; 2001 (34) LabIC 1096; 2001 (2) LLJ 1140; 2001(2) SCALE 261; 2001 (2) SCJ 273; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 170; 2001 (2) SLT 537; 2001 (2) Supreme 193

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 310 and 136 - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, r. 161(1), cl. (aa)(i)(1) - Service - Compulsory retirement - Whether High Court in dismissing compulsory retirement order of respondent by govt. of Gujarat without reasonable opportunity of hearing is valid? - Relied on State of Orissa & Ors v. Ram Chandra Das [1996 5 SCC 3311996 (5) SCC 3311996 Indlaw SC 3784, UOI v. J. N. Sinha 1970 Indlaw SC 17 - Held, state govt. failed to complete enquiry within reasonable time and review committee also did not recommend compulsory retirement of respondent - No merit found and impugned order set aside - Appeal dismissed
