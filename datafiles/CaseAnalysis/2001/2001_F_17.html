Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai v Fisher Rosemount (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 418; (2002) 9 SCC 555; AIR 2002 SC 96; 2002 (1) CLC 152; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 321; JT 2001 (9) SC 446; 2001 (47) RLT 261; 2001(8) SCALE 89; 2001 (8) SLT 57; 2001 (8) Supreme 190

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 14(1)(b) - Customs Valuation Rules, 1963 - Whether Custom Authorities were correct in doing valuation of goods imported by respondent from M/s Rosemount Inc USA u/s. 14(1)(b) of Customs Act, 1962 r/w Customs Valuation Rules, 1963 due to relationship between respondent and M/s Rosemount Inc? - Held, mere fact that M/s Rosemount Inc., USA, had 40 per cent equity share in respondent company and had provided technical data base for manufacture of electronic pressure transmitters ipso facto did not make two companies related persons - Tribunal was justified in setting aside orders of original as well as appellate authority - Appeal dismissed.
