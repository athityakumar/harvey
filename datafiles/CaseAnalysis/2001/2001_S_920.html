Smt. Akhtari Bi v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20684; (2001) 4 SCC 355; (2001) SCC (Cr) 714; AIR 2001 SC 1528; AIR 2001 SCW 1236; 2001 (2) CCR 41; 2001 (2) Crimes 65; 2001 CRLJ 1727; 2001 (1) JCC 176; JT 2001 (4) SC 40; 2001 (2) KLT 598; 2001(3) M.P.H.T. 187; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 302; 2001(2) SCALE 525; 2001 (2) SCJ 576; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 626; 2001 (2) SLT 794; 2001 (2) Supreme 448; 2001 (1) UC 531

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Bail - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - For the murder of her daughter-in-law, appellant was tried with others and after the conclusion of trial, sentenced to imprisonment for life - Bail application filed by her on the grounds of infirmity and old age has been rejected by High Court - Held, without commenting on the merits of the case but keeping in view the allegations made against the appellant coupled with the fact that she is old and infirm, it is directed her release on bail by keeping the sentence awarded to her in suspension - Thus, order impugned set aside with a direction that the order of conviction and sentence passed against her shall be kept in abeyance and she be released on bail on furnishing personal bond - Appeal allowed.
