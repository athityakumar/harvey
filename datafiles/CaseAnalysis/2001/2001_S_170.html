Balkrishan v Satyaprakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20335; (2001) 2 SCC 498; AIR 2001 SC 700; AIR 2001 SCW 344; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 37; 2001 (1) CGLJ 315; JT 2001 (2) SC 357; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 4; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 95; 2001 (92) RD 598; 2001(1) SCALE 336; 2001 (2) SCJ 84; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 480; 2001 (1) SLT 663; 2001 (1) Supreme 336; 2001 (1) UC 319

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, ss. 38 and 250 - Whether appellant had not perfected his title by adverse possession in present case in view of Tahsildar's order against appellant to deliver possession of suit land to auction-purchasers? - A person claiming title by adverse possession has to prove three nec - nec vi, nec clam and nec precario, in other words, he must show that his possession is adequate in continuity, in publicity and in extent, mere passing of an order of ejectment against a person claiming to be in adverse possession neither causes his dispossession nor discontinuation of his possession which alone breaks continuity of possession - Held, in spite of Tahsildar's order against appellant which was not acted upon, nor executed, appellant continued in possession of suit land and, therefore, continuity of his possession of suit land was neither interrupted nor lost - Appeal allowed.
