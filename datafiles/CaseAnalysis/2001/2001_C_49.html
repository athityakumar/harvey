B. D. Shetty and Others v Ceat Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20551; (2002) SCC (L&S) 131; (2002) 1 SCC 193; AIR 2001 SC 2953; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 11; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 601; 2002 (1) CLR 69; 2002 (93) FLR 785; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 10; 2001 LabIC 4000; 2001 (2) LLJ 1552; 2002 (1) LLN 610; 2001(7) SCALE 513; 2001 (7) SLT 517; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 511; 2001 (8) Supreme 56

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice and Procedure - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, ss. 15 and 10-A(1)(b) - Whether delay in completion of disciplinary proceedings directly attributable to conduct of a workman u/s. 10-A(1)(b) also covers delay occasioned on account of such workman succeeding in getting stay of disciplinary proceedings at hands of competent judicial authority pending trial of a criminal case in a bona fide effort to protect him from prejudice that may be caused by simultaneous proceedings? - Held, if a workman in order to protect himself from prejudice that may be caused by simultaneous proceedings, approaches a competent judicial authority and that authority, on being satisfied, taking into consideration facts and circumstances of case, stays further proceedings in a domestic inquiry pending a criminal trial, delay caused on that account in completion of domestic inquiry cannot be directly attributable to conduct of such workman because granting stay of further proceedings in a domestic inquiry does not depend on pleasure or mere wish of a workman himself - Further held, a plain reading and clear understanding of s. 10-A(1)(b) as excludes delay in completion of disciplinary proceedings caused on account of order granted by a competent Court from mischief of said provision - It is only delay that is directly attributable to workman is covered by said provision - Appellants entitled to subsistence allowance at rate of 75% - Appeal allowed.
