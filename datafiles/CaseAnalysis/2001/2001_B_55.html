Hanuman Prasad Bagri and Others v Bagress Cereals Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19956; (2001) 4 SCC 420; AIR 2001 SC 1416; AIR 2001 SCW 1359; 2001 (2) CLC 385; [2001] 105 Comp Cas 493; 2001 (2) CompLJ 392; JT 2001 (4) SC 424; 2001(3) SCALE 86; 2001 (2) SCJ 644; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 811; 2001 (3) SLT 675; 2001 (3) Supreme 80

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Trade

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397 and 398 - Winding up petition - Oppression and mismanagement - Legality - Whether facts and circumstances of case warranted entertaing a petition u/s. 397 or 398? - If facts fall short of a case upon which company court feels that company should be wound up on just and equitable grounds in that event no relief can be granted to petitioners in regard to s. 397 of Act - Held, petitioner no. 1 ceased to be a director is disputed and that termination of directorship would not entitle to ask for winding up on just and equitable grounds inasmuch as there is an appropriate remedy by way of company suit - A director even if illegally terminated cannot bring his grievance as to winding up company for that single and isolated act - Petition dismissed.
