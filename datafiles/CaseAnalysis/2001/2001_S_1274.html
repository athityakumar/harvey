Arvind Kumar Kankane v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 34; (2001) 8 SCC 355; AIR 2001 SC 2800; 2001 (45) ALR 81; 2001 (3) AWC 2406; JT 2001 (6) SC 260; 2001(5) SCALE 23; 2001 (3) SCJ 93; 2001 (3) SLR 730; 2001 (5) SLT 628; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 262; 2001 (5) Supreme 589; 2001 (2) UC 257C; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2477

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education-Medical education- Admission to post-graduate medical courses-Rules therefor-Validity- Whether if at any subsequent stage of counselling a seat falls vacant same should be allotted on basis of option exercised by those who are in waiting list?-Held, "yes"-Whether such seat available to candidates allotted seats earlier?-Held, "no"-No merit in appeals.

It is clear that once an option is exercised by a candidate on the basis of which he is allotted the subject and thereafter that candidate is allowed to participate in subsequent counselling and his seat becomes vacant, the process of counselling will be endless and, as apprehended by the High Court, it may not be possible to complete the academic course within the stipulated period. The grievance made is that if a choice subject like surgery and medicine is given up by a candidate and that seat becomes vacant, it may go to a candidate who is lower in rank in the merit list. This is only a fortuitous circumstance dependent on so many contingencies like the student, who has been allotted a seat in medicine, giving up the said seat and that seat falling vacant and thereafter, the same is allotted to a candidate who is lower in rank in the merit list. Such freak circumstances cannot be the test of reasonableness of the Rule.
