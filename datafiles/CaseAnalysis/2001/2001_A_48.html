Buddhi Nath Chaudhary and Others v Abahi Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20274; (2001) 3 SCC 328; (2001) SCC (L&S) 589; AIR 2001 SC 1176; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 199; 2001 (34) LabIC 1124; 2001(2) SCALE 145; 2001 (2) SCJ 471; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 18; 2001 (2) SLR 38; 2001 (2) SLT 306; 2001 (2) Supreme 138; 2001 (1) UC 430

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Factories Act, 1948 - Appellants were selected by Bihar Public Service Commission for appointment as Motor Vehicle Inspectors - When process of selection was pending, a new rule was introduced in recruitment rules requiring person to be selected as a Motor Vehicle Inspector to possess a driving licence - Appellants did not possess the requisite qualification - Appointments challenged - Whether High Court was justified in not dealing with contentions of candidates not selected, against appointments of other candidates, and directing Transport Commr. to do the necessary exercise? - Matter of selection and appointment to a post should not have been entrusted to Transport Commr. when Commission had been specifically entrusted with job - Held, however, in facts and circumstances of this case it can be said that if selection of these candidates was improper same should have been set aside with appropriate directions to re-do process of selection - All selected candidates, possess necessary qualification - Selected candidates are now in employment as Motor Vehicle Inspectors for over a decade and thus have acquired requisite experience - Lack of experience, if any, at time of recruitment is made good now - New exercise ordered by HC will only lead to anomalous results - Appointments made long back pursuant to a selection need not be disturbed - Appeals allowed.
