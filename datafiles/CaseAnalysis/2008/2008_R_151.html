Ashok Kumar Shrivastava and Others v Ram Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 19; (2008) 3 SCC 148; 2008(1) SCALE 76; 2008(1) SCALE 176; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Date Of Regularisation, Consideration Of Seniority, Appeals Disposed Of, Ad Hoc Appointee, Executive Instructions, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Officers (Grade II) Service Rules 1983, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Officers Rules, 1977, Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission (Limitation of Function) Regulations, 1954

Summary: Was the State Government entitled to withdraw from the purview of the U.P. Public Service Commission posts which were to be filled up by direct recruitment?; Would the 97 posts which were taken out from the purview of the U.P. Public Service Commission continue to be governed by the recruitment process for direct recruits as contained in the Uttar Pradesh (Sales Tax Officers Grade II) Service Rules, 1983?; Whether having regard to the condition contained in their appointment letters that their appointments were being made on purely ad hoc basis the appointees to the said posts could claim seniority on the basis of the service rendered by them from 1987 to 25.9.1997 when pursuant to an amendment of the Service Rules their services were regularized? - Held, an employee's service is to be counted for seniority only after he is born in the cadre, in the facts of this case the 1987 appointees will have to be treated differently - In the absence of Rules for such a contingency, the High Court took an erroneous view in holding that even though the posts in question had been taken out of the purview of the Public Service Commission the appointments made therein would be subject to the 1983 Service Rules relating to recruitments, as they were of a purely ad hoc nature - Proviso to clause (3) of art. 320 of the Constitution empowers the Governor of a State to withdraw from the purview of the Public Service Commission services and posts in connection with the affairs of a State - Appeals disposed of.
