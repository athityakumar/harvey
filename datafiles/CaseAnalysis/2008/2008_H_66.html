Haryana Financial Corporation and Another v Kailash Chandra Ahuja

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1130; (2008) 9 SCC 31; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 909; 2008 (6) AWC 6188; 2008 (118) FLR 700; JT 2008 (8) SC 70; 2008 (3) LLJ 897; 2008(10) SCALE 101; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 222; 2008 (6) SLR 654; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2416

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Automatic Termination, Punjab Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1961

Summary: Punjab Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1961 - Order of dismissal - Whether the High Court was right in setting aside the order of punishment merely on the ground of non-supply of report of the Inquiry Officer to the delinquent? - Held, though supply of report of Inquiry Officer is part and parcel of natural justice and must be furnished to the delinquent- employee, failure to do so would not automatically result in quashing or setting aside of the order or the order being declared null and void - Unless the delinquent employee is able to show that non-supply of report of the Inquiry Officer has resulted in prejudice or miscarriage of justice, an order of punishment cannot be held to be vitiated - Remitted the matter to the High Court.
