Sita Ram and Others v Moti Lal Nehru Farmers Training Institute

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 344; (2008) 5 SCC 75; AIR 2008 SC 1955; 2008 (2) CLR 763; 2008 (2) ESC 198; 2008 (117) FLR 1191; JT 2008 (3) SC 622; 2008 (2) LLJ 688; 2008 (2) LLN 654; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 105; 2008(4) SCALE 77; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 471; 2008 (3) SLR 769

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Labour Court directed respondents to reinstate the appellants - High Court set aside the award of the Labour Court opining that the burden of proof had wrongly been placed on the respondent - Appeal against - Held, keeping in view the period during which the services were rendered by the respondent; the fact that the respondent had stopped its operation of bee-farming, and the services of the appellants were terminated in December, 1996, it is not a fit case where the appellants could have been directed to be re-instated in service - Payment of a sum of Rs. 1, 00, 000/- to each of the appellant, would meet the ends of justice - Appeal partly allowed.
