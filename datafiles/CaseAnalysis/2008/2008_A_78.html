State (Nct Of Delhi) v Ahmed Jaan

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1311; (2008) 14 SCC 582; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 864; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 695; 2008 CRLJ 4355; 2010 (167) DLT 658; JT 2008 (10) SC 179; 2008 (152) PLR 735; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 126; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 119; 2008 (2) RCR(Rent) 234; 2008(11) SCALE 455; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Party

Summary: IPC, ss. 121, 121A, 122, 124-A, 120-B - Addl. Sessions Judge discharged the accused at the threshold, holding that prima facie there was no legal evidence to show that the respondent has committed any of the alleged acts - Revision petition dismissed on the ground of delay - Appeal against - Held, proof by sufficient cause is a condition precedent for exercise of the extraordinary discretion vested in the court - What counts is not the length of the delay but the sufficiency of the cause and shortness of the delay is one of the circumstances to be taken into account in using the discretion - Explanations offered were plausible and deserved to be accepted - Remitted the matter to High Court.
