Ishwar Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1654; (2008) 15 SCC 667; AIR 2009 SC 675; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 560; 2009 (1) JCC 593; JT 2008 (12) SC 104; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2008(14) SCALE 177; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Old Age

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 307 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Prayer to dispose of appeal on the basis of compromise between the parties - Held, it would not be appropriate to order compounding of an offence not compoundable under the Code ignoring and keeping aside statutory provisions - While imposing substantive sentence, the factum of compromise between the parties is indeed a relevant circumstance which, the Court may keep in mind - Reduced the sentence already undergone by the appellant - Appeal partly allowed.
