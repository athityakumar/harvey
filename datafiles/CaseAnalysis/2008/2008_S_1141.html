State of Punjab v Navraj Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1075; (2008) 11 SCC 71; AIR 2008 SC 2962; 2008 CRLJ 3864; 2008 (3) JCC 1854; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 614; 2008(10) SCALE 162; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 924

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d) r/w 13(2) - High Court stayed the conviction - Appeal against - Held, it is only in very exceptional cases that the court should exercise such power of stay in matters arising out of the Act - High Court nowhere pointed out what is the exceptional fact which in its opinion required it to stay the conviction and failed to note the direction of Court that it has a duty to look at all aspects including ramification of keeping such conviction in abeyance - Single Judge while directing suspension of conviction indicated no reasons - Appeal allowed.
