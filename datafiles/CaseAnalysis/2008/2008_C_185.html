Commissioner Of Income Tax Central, Kanpur v J. K. Charitable Trust Kamal Tower, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1743; (2009) 1 SCC 196; 2008 (232) E.L.T. 769; [2009] 308 ITR 161; JT 2008 (13) SC 643; 2008(14) SCALE 671; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 958; 2010 (18) S.T.R. 225; [2008] 175 TAXMAN 251

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Separate Assessment

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Dispute relates to the question whether the respondent, assessee's trust was hit by the provisions of ss. 13(1)(c) and 13(2)(a)(f) & (h) and therefore cannot be given the benefit of exemption provided under s. 11 - Whether the revenue can be precluded from filing an appeal even though in respect of some other years involving identical dispute no appeal is filed? - Held, if the assessee takes the stand that the Revenue acted mala fide in not preferring appeal in one case and filing the appeal in other case, it has to establish malafides - Fact situation in all the assessment years is same - Revenue cannot file the appeal in one case while deciding not to file an appeal in another case - Appeals dismissed.
