H.V. Nirmala v Karnataka State Financial Corporation & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2118; (2008) 7 SCC 639; AIR 2008 SC 2440; 2009(3) ALL MR 465; 2010 (2) KarLJ 33; 2009 (1) LLJ 208; 2008 (3) LLN 709; 2008(8) SCALE 315; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1157

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, Principle Of Estoppel, Karnataka State Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1965

Summary: (A) Service - Karnataka State Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1965, regn. 41(3) - Framing of charges - Guilty - Dismissal from service - Appointment - Legal advisor as enquiry officer - Disciplinary proceeding was initiated against appellant when she was working as Trainee Assistant Manager in Corporation - Imputation of charges against appellant pertained to sanction and disbursal of amount of loan in four cases - Finding of guilt was arrived at by Enquiry Officer/Legal Advisor and penalty of dismissal from services was imposed upon appellant - Appellant preferred appeal thereagainst which was dismissed by Board - Aggrieved therewith, appellant filed writ petition before HC which resulted in same - Thereafter, intra Court appeal was preferred which had been dismissed by DB of HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether in regard to regn. 41(3) of the Regulation, Legal Advisor could have been appointed as Enquiry Officer - Held, appellant participated in enquiry proceeding without any demur whatsoever - A large number of witnesses were examined before Enquiry Officer - However, therein all consequential benefits were not given - In that case Enquiry Officer had no jurisdiction at all - Even defecto doctrine could not be applied as Enquiry Officer was not holder of office but merely ex-employee, who could not have been appointed as Enquiry Officer - Therefore, on basis that regn. 41(3) of the Regulation remained unchanged and in terms of the Regulation which was prevalent at relevant point of time, an outsider could have been appointed as Enquiry Officer - It was notice where appointment of Enquiry Officer could have something to do only for carrying out procedural aspect of mater, strict adherence to Rules could not be insisted upon - Hence, contention that Legal Advisor could not have been appointed as Enquiry Officer had no merit - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Service - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka State Financial Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1965 - Absence of provision - Transfer of proceeding - Power - Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 13 - Whether in absence of any provision in the Regulations unlike r. 13 of the Rules, Managing Director of Corporation could not have transferred proceeding to Board of Directors - Held, appointing authority of class 'A' Officers was Board and Managing Director was disciplinary authority only in respect of minor punishments - When major punishment was proposed to be imposed, Board of Directors alone would have jurisdiction to consider gravity of alleged misconduct so as to enable it to pass appropriate order - Therefore, Managing Director of Corporation initiated proceeding but he could not impose major penalty and in that view of matter he would have incidental power to place findings of Enquiry Officer before Board - Such incidental power should be held to be existing with all statutory authorities - Hence, absence of any Rule as was obtaining in r. 13 of the Rules would not vitiate proceeding - Appeal dismissed.
