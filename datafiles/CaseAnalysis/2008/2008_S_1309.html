K. D. Sharma v Steel Authority Of India Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2544; (2008) 12 SCC 481; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1309; 2008 (4) BC 557; JT 2008 (8) SC 57; 2008(10) SCALE 227; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1973

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Carriers & Transportation - Purchase/Contract Procedure, 2000 - Contract for carriage - Selection of bidder - Violation of principles of natural justice - 1st respondent/Company invited tenders for raising, transporting and loading of iron ore lump and fines into railway wagons - 2nd respondent was found to be qualified, however, tender process was cancelled owing to no other participant in the tender process - 1st respondent, thereafter, invited tenders four times but cancelled the same in all the four time due to no participants - Appellant was found qualified in the fifth time and his bid was selected - 2nd respondent challenged the selection before HC - HC dismissed 2nd respondent's writ petition - 2nd respondent filed review before HC - HC allowed the review directing 1st respondent to open fourth tender and consider the cases of 2nd respondent and appellant - Appellant and respondents filed various petitions before HC in the meantime - HC, by impugned order, disposed all the applications on the basis of a settlement arrived at between the parties - Appellant filed application before HC to recall the impugned order on the ground of fraud - HC dismissed appellant's application - Appellant contended that he had withdrawn his Power of Attorney holder (PoA) representing him before the 1st respondent, yet 1st respondent allowed the said PoA holder to represent him to arrived at a compromise against the interest of the appellant - Held, a notice was issued by 1st respondent to the appellant before finalizing the tender and appellant informed the 1st respondent that he had authorized the PoA holder to appear on his behalf - PoA holder appeared at the time of consideration of bids for and behalf of appellant and as a result, bid of 2nd respondent was finalized - However, appellant did not place all those facts before the SC but he had chosen to state facts in the manner suited to him by giving an impression to the SC that 1st respondent, an instrumentality of State, has not followed doctrine of natural justice and fundamental principles of fair procedure - Therefore, appellant cannot claim any relief on the ground of equity - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 - Relief from writ-courts - Approach of petitioner - Held, jurisdiction of the SC u/art. 32 and of the HC u/art. 226 of the Constitution is extraordinary, equitable and discretionary and prerogative writs mentioned therein are issued for doing substantial justice - It is, therefore, of utmost necessity that the petitioner approaching the writ-court must come with clean hands, put forward all the facts before the court without concealing or suppressing anything and seek an appropriate relief - If there is no candid disclosure of relevant and material facts or the petitioner is guilty of misleading the court, his petition may be dismissed at the threshold without considering the merits of the claim - Appeal dismissed.
