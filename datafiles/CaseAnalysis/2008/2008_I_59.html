E. Micheal Raj v Intelligence Officer, Narcotic Control Bureau

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 393; (2008) 5 SCC 161; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 558; AIR 2008 SC 1720; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1318; 2008 CRLJ 2250; 2008 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 78; JT 2008 (4) SC 523; 2008 (2) KLT 36; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 597; 2008(4) SCALE 592; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 644

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Personal Consumption, Poppy, Manufactured Drugs, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Amendment) Act, 2001, Morphine, Coca Derivative, Medicinal Opium, Prepared Opium

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c) r/w 21 and 29 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, possession of offending substance would be considered an offence punishable under the NDPS Act, as heroin is an opium derivative as per s. 2(xvi)(e) which says that 'all preparations containing more than 0.2 percent of morphine or containing any diacetylmorphine' is an opium derivative - Narcotic drug which was found in possession of the appellant as per the Analyst's report is 60 gms. which is more than 5 gms., i.e. small quantity, but less than 250 gms., i.e. commercial quantity - Appellant would be punishable under s. 21(b) - Appellant is merely a carrier and is not a kingpin - Reduced the sentence of the accused-appellant to 6 years' rigorous imprisonment with fine of Rs.20, 000/- - Appeal disposed of.
