Life Insurance Corporation Of India And Others v Retired L.I.C. Officers Association And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 237; (2008) 3 SCC 321; AIR 2008 SC 1485; 2008 (117) FLR 1; JT 2008 (2) SC 337; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1240; 2008 (2) LLN 103; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 932; 2008(2) SCALE 484; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 823; 2008 (2) SLR 360; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1442

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: City Compensatory Allowance, Compensatory Allowance, Superannuation Fund, Terms And Conditions Of Service, Instructions, Fiction, Employees Of The Corporation

Summary: Life Insurance Corporation of India Class-I Officers (Revision of Terms and Conditions of Service) Instructions, 1996 - Jurisdiction of Chairman of the Life Insurance Corporation of India (Corporation) to issue instructions in terms of reg. 51 - Held, Clause (2) of the Regulation confers jurisdiction on the Chairman of the Corporation to regulate the same as also other matters connected therewith or incidental thereof by issuance of instructions - Method of fixation, eligibility for the benefit of revision and the date from which the revisions shall apply are thus, the only areas within which the Chairman can exercise jurisdiction - Effect of revision of pay scales on other spheres and which are otherwise governed by another statute or other provisions of the said Regulations would not come within the purview - Appeal dismissed.
