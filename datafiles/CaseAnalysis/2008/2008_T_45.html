Faqruddin (Dead), Through L.Rs. v Tajuddin (Dead), Through L.Rs.

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2596; (2008) 8 SCC 12; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 478; 2008 (4) AWC 3503; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 147; JT 2008 (9) SC 37; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 1402; 2009 (106) RD 440; 2008(8) SCALE 557; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 377

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Family & Personal

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, Religious Endowments Act, 1863, Rajasthan Land Reforms and Resumption of Jagirs Act, 1952, Rajasthan Jagir Decisions and Proceedings (Validation) Act, 1955, Jaipur General Clauses Act, 1944, Jaipur Matmi Rules, 1945, Jaipur Matmi Rules (Validation) Act, 1961, Jaipur Laws (Validating) Act, 1952

Summary: (A) Family & Personal - Mohammedan law - Law of inheritance - Held, law of inheritance amongst the Mohammedans is governed by their personal laws - If the properties are wakf properties, the offices of Sajjadanashin and Mutawalli are to be filled up in accordance with the law or the custom - If the properties are heritable, those who are the 'Quranic Heirs' would be entitled to hold the said posts - Law of primogeniture has no application amongst the Mohammedans - Appeal allowed.

(B) Family & Personal - Office of Sajjadanashin - Appointment of stranger - Held, appointment should be open to a stranger if it be found that no suitable descendant of incumbent Sajjadanashin is available, however, preference would be given to descendants of the founder and among them to those 'nearest in propinquity' to the last incumbent, provided that such persons are duly qualified - Appeal allowed.

(C) Family & Personal - Rejection of suit for recovery of Wakf property - Claim in the capacity of Sajjadanashin - Sustainability - Held, if a suit for recovery of Wakf property was dismissed, plaintiff cannot claim the said property in his capacity as Sajjadanashin or Mutawalli, though the right of plaintiff as a validly elected Sajjadanashin and Mutawalli was not determined in that suit - Appeal allowed.

(D) Administrative - Conferment of title - Jurisdiction of Board of Revenue - Held, Board of Revenue has no jurisdiction to confer the title upon any party - Title in or over a land will depend upon the statutory provisions and a title does not remain in vacuum - Title has to be determined keeping in view the law operating in the field, viz., religious law or statutory law or customary law - Appeal allowed.
