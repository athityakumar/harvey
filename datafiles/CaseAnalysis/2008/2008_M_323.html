Kancherla Lakshminarayana v Mattaparthi Syamala and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 528; (2008) 14 SCC 258; AIR 2008 SC 2069; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 55; 2008 (2) AWC 1798; 2008 (2) CTC 774; 2008 (3) KLT 932; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 252; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 767; 2008(3) SCALE 641; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attachment Of Property, Summary Decision, Adjudication Of Claims, Sale Of Movable Property

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, O. 21 r. 58 - Execution Application was filed whereby the appellant sought to make a prayer for raising the attachment on the suit property or in the alternative to declare the sale being subject to the claim in Original Suit for specific performance of the Sale Agreement and is dismissed - Appeal against - Held, whether the Agreement of Sale was a good Agreement of Sale entitling the appellant for specific performance on the basis of that agreement is essentially a question to be decided subsequently in the suit - Under such circumstances there was a cloud on the property and a person like appellant who had the obligation qua the property in the shape of an Agreement of Sale could not be held to be an utter outsider having no locus standi to take the objections - High Court went on to suggest that a merely Agreement holder could not prevent the right of the auction-purchaser to get the sale confirmed - High Court and the Trial Court were in utter error in relying on proviso to Clause (a) to r. 58 of O. 21 - Appeal allowed.
