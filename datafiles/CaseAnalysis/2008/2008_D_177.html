Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board v Dr. Bhupendra Kumar Modi and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1943; (2009) 2 SCC 147; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 679; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1550; 2009 CRLJ 1148; 2009 (1) CTC 84; 2009 (1) FAC 70; 2009 (1) JCC 599; JT 2008 (13) SC 174; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 280; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 733; 2008(16) SCALE 17; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Water Pollution, Personal Presence, Non- Compliance, Pollution

Summary: Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 - Whether complaint of U.P. Pollution Control Board discloses any material against the first respondent, Joint Managing Director, particularly, his control over the decision making process of the Company; Whether the High Court was justified in quashing the same in a petition under s. 482 Cr.P.C? - Held, at the stage of issuing process, the Magistrate is mainly concerned with the allegations made in the complaint or the evidence led in support of the same and he is only to be prima facie satisfied whether there are sufficient grounds for proceeding against the accused - In a matter of this nature, particularly, when it affects public health if it is ultimately proved, courts cannot afford to deal lightly with cases involving pollution of air and water - Special Judicial Magistrate (Pollution) is directed to proceed with the complaint - Appeal allowed.
