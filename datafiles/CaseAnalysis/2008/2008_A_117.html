American Hotel and Lodging Association v Central Board of Direct Taxes and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1088; (2008) 10 SCC 509; [2008] 301 ITR 86; JT 2008 (6) SC 379; 2008(7) SCALE 588; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Voluntary Contributions

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(22), 10(23C)(vi), 10(20A), 10(22B) and 11 - Income Tax Rules, 1962, r. 2CA - Issues arise - (A) Scope of enquiry by prescribed authority u/s. 10(23C)(vi) r/w third proviso thereto inserted by Finance (No. 2) Act, 1998, with effect from 01-04-1999? - S. 10(23C)(vi) is analogous to s. 10(22) - To that extent, judgments of Supreme Court as applicable to s. 10(22) would equally apply to s. 10(23C)(vi) - With insertion of provisos to s. 10(23C)(vi) applicant who seeks approval has not only to show that it is an institution existing solely for educational purposes [which was also requirement u/s. 10(22)] but it has now to obtain initial approval from prescribed authority, in terms of s. 10(23 C)(vi) by making an application in standardised form as mentioned in first proviso to that section - Under thirteenth proviso, circumstances are given under which prescribed authority is empowered to withdraw approval earlier granted - On an analysis of provisos to s. 10(23 C)(vi) one finds that there is a difference between stipulation of conditions and compliance therewith - Threshold conditions are actual existence of an educational institution and approval of prescribed authority for which every applicant has to move an application in standardized form in terms of first proviso - It is only if pre-requisite condition of actual existence of educational institution is fulfilled that question of compliance with requirements in provisos would arise - To make section with proviso workable monitoring conditions in third proviso like application/utilisation of income, pattern of investments to be made, etc, could be stipulated as conditions by prescribed authority subject to which approval could be granted - View that prescribed authority can stipulate conditions subject to which approval may be granted finds support from sub-cl. (ii)(B) in thirteenth proviso - (B) Whether words 'in India' should be read into s. 10(23C)(vi) and/or in third proviso thereto? - Held, s. 10(23 C)(vi) is analogous to s. 10(22) of 1961 Act - Even after Finance Act, 1998, w.e.f. 01-04-1999, third proviso to s. 10(23C)(vi), which refers to monitoring conditions, confines words 'application of income' to objects for which institution is established - Third proviso does not use words 'in India' in matter of application or accumulation of income though in several other sections like ss. 10(20A), 10(22B) and 11(1)(a), etc, Parliament has used words 'in India' - Therefore, for this one more reason, cannot read in words 'in India' into third proviso - Appeal allowed.
