Balbir Kaur & Anr. v Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Board, Allahabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 959; (2008) 12 SCC 1; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1183; 2008 (6) AWC 6143; JT 2008 (8) SC 381; 2008(8) SCALE 538; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 130; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2376

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Clubbing, U.P. Secondary Education Selection Board Act, 1982, U.P. Public Service (Reservation for Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, U.P. Secondary Education Services Selection Board Rules, 1998

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Selection Board Act, 1982; Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Board Rules, 1998; Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921 - Whether 'Note' appended to sub-r. (5) of r. 12 of the UPSESSB Rules, 1998 has the effect of modifying the conditions of qualifying experience mentioned in Appendix A of the Regulations under the UPIEA, 1921? - Held, expression 'teaching experience' as contemplated in the 'Note' would apply both to the required experience and the experience more than that, thus'Note' appended to sub-r. (5) of r. 12 of the UPSESSB Rules, 1998 has the effect of modifying the conditions of qualifying experience mentioned in Appendix A of the Regulations under the UPIEA, 1921 -'Note' to sub-r. (5) of r. 12 of UPSESSB Rules, 1998 prescribes the requirement of experience for the post, which is different from what is prescribed in the said Appendix A and, therefore, there being a conflict between the two provisions, in the teeth of s. 32, the said 'Note' shall have an overriding effect over Appendix A insofar as the question of experience is concerned - Neither s. 10 nor 11 of the UPSESB Act, 1982 nor the UPSESSB Rules, 1998 as such mandate that selection or determination of vacancies must be yearwise and, therefore, all the vacancies which are "existing or which are likely to fall vacant during the year of recruitment" can be clubbed irrespective of the year of occurrence of the vacancy - State is not obliged to regularize all ad-hoc appointments merely on the strength of their continuance on the post for a long period, particularly when their original appointments were not made by following a due process of selection as envisaged in the relevant rules - Appeals allowed.
