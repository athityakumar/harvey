Rukmini Narvekar v Vijaya Satardekar and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2491; (2008) 14 SCC 1; AIR 2009 SC 1013; 2009 CRLJ 822; 2008 (4) JCC 2879; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 139; JT 2008 (11) SC 32; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 70; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 924; 2008(13) SCALE 523; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 409, 420 and 423 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Quashing of criminal case - FIR was filed against respondents under various provisions of the IPC - It was alleged that respondent no. 1 (advocate by profession) fraudulently and dishonestly induced the complainant and her deceased husband to place their signatures and thumb impression on some papers - Property of the complainant was purported to have been sold away by respondent no. 1 by deceit and misrepresentation - Cognizance was taken of the offence alleged in the chargesheet and process was issued by the Judicial Magistrate u/ss. 468/471/420/120-B r/w s. 34, IPC - HC quashed the criminal proceedings - Hence, present appeal - (A) Prima facie case - Held, allegations in the FIR if treated to be correct prima facie make out an offence against the respondent no. 1 - This was not a fit case for quashing of the criminal proceedings respondent no. 1 in exercise of the powers u/s.482 CrPC or art. 226 of the Constitution - (B) It was submitted at the time of the framing of the charges material produced by the defence cannot be looked into - Reliance was placed on State of Orissa vs. Debendra Nath Padhi 2004 INDLAW SC 1025 - Held, while it is true that ordinarily defence material cannot be looked into by the Court while framing of the charge in view of D.N. Padhi's case, but it cannot be said as an absolute proposition that under no circumstances can the Court look into the material produced by the defence at the time of framing of the charges, though this should be done in very rare cases, i.e. where the defence produces material which convincingly demonstrates that the whole prosecution case is totally absurd or totally concocted - However, in the present case it cannot be said that the evidence in the civil suit which was produced by the defence before the Trial Court established convincingly that the prosecution case is totally absurd or preposterous - Complaint made does make out a prima facie case against respondent no. 1 - However, even prima facie, none of the offences referred to in the charge-sheet can be made out against respondent no. 2 - Judgment of HC upheld in so far as respondent no. 2 is concerned and is set aside in case of respondent no. 1 - Appeal disposed of.
