State of Rajasthan v Udai Lal

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 822; (2008) 11 SCC 408; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1810; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 956; 2008(7) SCALE 317; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - High Court acquitted the accused - Appeal against - Held, High Court failed to take note of the relevant aspect, namely, the quantity of recovery articles is quite huge (115 bags) which could not be produced in the court but on behalf of the prosecution 5 bags have been produced in the Court - Considering the huge quantity merely because the prosecution has not produced all the 119 bags in the Court, an inference cannot be drawn against them - When the view taken either by Session Judge or Special Judge was found by the High Court to be manifestly wrong and that it had led to mis-carriage of justice, the High Court is entitled to interfere and set aside the same - Such recourse has not been adopted by the High Court in this case - Remitted the matter to High Court.
