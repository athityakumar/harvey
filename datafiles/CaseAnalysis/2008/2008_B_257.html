Gauri Shankar Prasad And Ors v Brahma Nand Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1035; (2008) 8 SCC 287; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 917; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 33; 2009 (74) ALR 40; 2008 (6) AWC 6243; 2009 (106) RD 299; 2008(10) SCALE 306; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 839; 2008 (3) UC 1429

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Common Law, Time Limit, Specific Performance Of Contract

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Plaintiffs executed a registered deed of sale in favour of respondent and executed a registered deed of agreement and agreed to re-convey the purchased land to the plaintiffs on payment of consideration money to them - Held, if sale and agreement to repurchase are embodied in separate documents, it cannot be a case of the mortgage and in such cases relating to re-conveyance time is always the essence of the contract - There was an agreement for re-conveyance and there was specific stipulation for re-conveyance of the land within a period of 3 years which was admittedly not complied by the plaintiffs- appellants - Appeal dismissed.
