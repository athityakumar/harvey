Shaukat Hussain Guru v State (Nct) Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 993; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 137; (2008) 6 SCC 776; AIR 2008 SC 2419; 2008 CRLJ 3016; 2008(7) SCALE 525; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Court's Jurisdiction, Re- Examination

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 121, 121A, 122, 120B r/w 302 and 307 r/w 120B; Explosive Substances Act, ss. 3(2), 3(3), 3(5), 4 - Writ petition against conviction on a charge not framed - Held, s. 222 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 authorizes and gives jurisdiction to the court to convict an accused of the charge which has not been framed, if he is found guilty of a minor offence - Court need not frame a separate charge before the conviction is rendered on a minor offence - Prosecution having been successful in proving the necessary ingredients of s. 123, IPC, it would constitute a minor offence of a major offence and, therefore, the petitioner was convicted under s. 123, IPC which is a minor offence of the offences he faced trial - Petition dismissed.
