A. Satyanarayana and Others v S. Purushotham and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 760; (2008) 5 SCC 416; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1944; 2008 (3) ESC 351; JT 2008 (5) SC 508; 2008 (2) LLN 798; 2008(6) SCALE 563; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 1055

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Criteria For Promotion, Feeder Post, State Administrative Tribunal, Other Criteria

Summary: Interpretation of GOMs. No.368 dated 18.8.1999 - Held, High Court was not wholly correct when it opined that a policy decision cannot be a subject matter of judicial review - If the State has the power to fix a quota, the Rule underlying the legislative policy must stop at that and the necessary consequences thereof must ensue - Although the State was entitled to provide for quota as also a guideline as to how the roster should work out itself, but thereby it cannot be permitted to put a cap on promotion for the entire service period - Impugned Government Orders cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
