Baljinder Singh v Rattan Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2419; (2008) 16 SCC 785; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 681B; 2008 (6) AWC 5520; JT 2008 (10) SC 98; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2949; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 903; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 921; 2008(11) SCALE 198; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice and Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 65, 109 - Suit for declaration - Predecessor-in-interest executed a sale deed in favor of his son and a gift deed in favor of his wife - Defendants devolved the suit property by virtue of a Will executed by the wife of the predecessor-in-interest - Plaintiff sought to declare the Will to be a void document - Trial Court dismissed the suit holding that suit was barred by limitation u/art. 109 - First Appellate Court reversed the findings of Trial Court - Defendant filed second appeal - HC held that the suit was not barred u/art. 109 of the Act, however, there was an implication of art. 65 in the given set of facts - Further, HC held that the genuineness of the Will surrounded by suspicious circumstances - Whether impugned HC order is valid in law - Held, findings relating to the genuineness of the Will is essentially factual - Although the courts below have analyzed the factual position in great detail, it would be proper for the HC to re-hear the appeal relating to applicability of art. 109 of the Act - Therefore, matter was remitted back to HC - Appeals disposed of.
