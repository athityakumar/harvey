Sohan Raj Sharma v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 591; (2008) 11 SCC 215; AIR 2008 SC 2108; 2008 CRLJ 2569; 2008 (2) JCC 1166; 2008 (2) KLT 287; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1294; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 810; 2008(6) SCALE 192; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1200

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distinct Offence, Specific Offence, Co- Habitation

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, abetment involves a mental process of instigating a person or intentionally aiding that person in doing of a thing - In cases of conspiracy also it would involve that mental process of entering into conspiracy for the doing of that thing - More active role which can be described as instigating or aiding the doing of a thing it required before a person can be said to be abetting the commission of offence under s. 306 - In cases of alleged abetment of suicide there must be proof of direct or indirect acts of incitement to the commission of suicide - Mere fact that the husband treated the deceased-wife with cruelty is not enough - Accused has been described as a sexual pervert and that he had behaved like an animal and the deceased had tolerated the insulting manner in which he behaved - It cannot be said that ingredients of s. 306 have been established - Appeal allowed.
