Rajoo and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1906; (2008) 15 SCC 133; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 751; AIR 2009 SC 858; JT 2008 (12) SC 457; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 310; 2008(15) SCALE 375; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 1078

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self-Inflicted

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 366, 376 - Appeal against conviction - Held, in so far as allegations of rape are concerned, the evidence of a prosecutrix must be examined as that of an injured witness whose presence at the spot is probable but it can never be presumed that her statement should, without exception, be taken as the gospel truth - Merely because the accused have been acquitted for the offence punishable under s. 366 is ipso-facto no reason to disbelieve the entire prosecution story on this solitary ground - It would be difficult to conclusively show the involvement of each of the accused beyond reasonable doubt - Appeals allowed.
