Commissioner of Central Excise, Bangalore v Srikumar Agencies Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1866; (2009) 1 SCC 469; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 136; 2008 (232) E.L.T. 577; 2008(15) SCALE 333; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 720; 2009 (13) S.T.R. 3

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeal disposed of

Summary: Excise - Practice and Procedure - (A) Division Bench of Supreme Court referred the question that whether the printing on the package is merely incidental or primary to three-judge Bench - Because there are two streams of judgments of this Court - (B) Earlier CEGAT also clubbing all the cases together and without analyzing the special features of each case disposing of the appeals - Held, since the factual position has not been analysed in detail, disposal of appeals by mere reference to decisions, was not the proper way to deal with the appeal - Further held that CEGAT also does not appear to have dealt with the relevance and applicability of ITC's case 1997 INDLAW SC 241 on which strong reliance has been placed and clubbing was not proper so matter remitted to CEGAT without answering the reference made - Appeal disposed of.
