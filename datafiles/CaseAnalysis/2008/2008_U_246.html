Usha Breco Mazdoor Sangh v Management of Usha Breco Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 836; (2008) 5 SCC 554; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 994; 2008 (118) FLR 400; JT 2008 (6) SC 427; 2008 (2) LLJ 945; 2008 (3) LLN 84; 2008(7) SCALE 447; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 13; 2008 (5) SLR 214

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quasi- Judicial

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Interpretation of s. 11A of ID Act, 1947 - Held, in the event, fresh evidence is adduced before Labour Court by the Management, the Labour Court will have the jurisdiction to appreciate the evidence, but, in a case where the materials brought on record by the Enquiry Officer fall for re-appreciation by the Labour Court, it should be slow to interfere therewith, it must come to a conclusion that the case was a "proper" one therefore - Even assuming that, for all intent and purport, the Labour Court acts as an appellate authority over the judgment of the Enquiry Officer, it would exercise appropriate restraint. It must bear in mind that the Enquiry Officer also acts as a quasi-judicial body - Decision of the Labour Court should not be based on mere hypothesis, it cannot overturn a decision of the Management on ipse dixit - Its jurisdiction under Section 11-A of the Act although is a wide one, must be judiciously exercised - Appeal dismissed.
