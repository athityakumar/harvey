State of Uttaranchal and Others v Kharak Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2183; (2008) 8 SCC 236; 2008 (118) FLR 1112; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 20; JT 2008 (9) SC 205; 2009 (1) LLJ 235; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 530; 2008(11) SCALE 321; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 54; 2008 (5) SLR 585

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Non-Supply Of Documents, Supply Of Documents

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Illicit felling of trees - On basis of inquiry report, Divisional Forest Officer dismissed respondent - Respondent filed appeal before Conservator of Forest which was dismissed - Respondent preferred writ petition before HC against said order - HC quashed orders passed by Divisional Forest Officer as well as order passed by Conservator of Forest and directed appellants to reinstate respondent in service with all consequential benefits - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC is justified in quashing orders passed by disciplinary authority as well as appellate authority dismissing respondent from service? - Held, where enquiry officer is other than disciplinary authority, disciplinary proceedings break into two stages - First stage ends when disciplinary authority arrives at its conclusions on basis of evidence, enquiry officer's report and delinquent employee's reply to it and second stage begins when disciplinary authority decides to impose penalty on basis of its conclusions - No enquiry in terms of above-mentioned principles was ever conducted in present case - Perusal of report shows that enquiry officer himself inspected areas in forest and after taking note of certain alleged deficiencies secured some answers from delinquent by putting some questions - Enquiry Officer himself has acted on Investigator, Prosecutor and Judge - Such a procedure is opposed to principles of natural justice and has been frowned upon by SC - Moreover, respondent was not furnished with required documents - Hence, HC has rightly interfered and quashed orders passed by Divisional Forest Officer as well as Conservator of Forest - Appeal dismissed
