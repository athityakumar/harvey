Pareena Swarup v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1629; (2008) 14 SCC 107; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 38; 2009 (1) CalLT(SC) 14; 2008 (231) E.L.T. 210; JT 2008 (11) SC 343; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 750; 2008(13) SCALE 84; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 1217; 2010 (18) S.T.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solicitor General, Qualified To Be a District Judge, Prevention of Money Laundering (Appointment and Conditions of Service of Chairperson and Members of Adjudicating Authorities) Rules, 2007, Adjudicating Authority Rules, 2007, Appellate Tribunal Rules, 2007

Summary: Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 - Petition seeking to declare ss. 6, 25, 27, 28, 32, 40 as ultra vires of Arts. 14, 19 (1) (g), 21, 50, 323B of the Constitution of India - Held, provisions of Prevention of the Money Laundering Act are so provided that there may not be independent judiciary to decide the cases under the Act but the Members and the Chairperson to be selected by the Selection Committee headed by Revenue Secretary - Amendment suggested.
