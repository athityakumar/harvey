Union Of India And Others v Chitra Lekha Chakraborty

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2528

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Central Administrative Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1987, r. 303 - Selection - Claim of seniority - Entitlement of - Selection for post of non-technical popular clerical category of railways was held in year 1984 and several candidates were selected - Respondents were appointed in year 1994 and had claimed seniority over candidates who was already appointed in year 1985 - Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) had directed railway authorities to fix specific position of respondents in panel for purpose of determination in inter se seniority - Order of CAT was challenged before HC contending that respondents were appointed only in year 1994 and that they could not claim seniority over candidates who was already appointed in year 1985 and HC confirmed order of CAT - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether respondents were liable to claim seniority over candidates who were already appointed - Held, it was found that neither respondents nor Railways were in position to give aggregate marks obtained by respondents nor marks obtained by candidates who was already appointed - There was no common panel vis-a-vis candidates from year 1985 and candidates who was subsequently appointed - Only if there was common panel, seniority could be determined on basis of r. 303 of the Rules which regulated seniority of non-gazetted railway employees - There was no common seniority list or any merit list prepared by including all candidates and there were no criteria available to determine inter se merit of candidates - Therefore, seniority of candidates who was appointed in year1985 could not be disturbed without hearing that candidates - Relative merit of candidates could not be considered as no marks were placed - It could also be noticed that respondents were appointed after period of six years, not due to any fault committed by Railway Recruitment Board - There was no negligence or latches on part of appellant in making appointments of respondents belatedly - Respondents were entitled to seniority only from date when they joined their service - Hence, order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.

Service - Selection - Seniority - Entitlement of - Respondent had participated in selection in year 1984 and her name was not found in selection list and she filed representation to railway authorities in year1991 and her representation was not replied by railway authorities - Respondent filed original application before CAT which was allowed - Chairman of Railway Recruitment Board issued direction to appoint respondent and she was consequently appointed - Appellant UOI (Ministry of Railways) had filed review petition before CAT and same was rejected - Appellant had challenged same before HC and HC confirmed order passed by CAT - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether respondents were liable to claim seniority over candidates who were already appointed - Held, direction of CAT was that respondent should be given specific position in panel for purpose of determination of her inter se seniority against that appointed from said panel and relative position of respondent in such panel should be ascribed keeping in view aggregate marks obtained by applicants who were already appointed from said panel - Respondent was not successful in producing any document to show as to what was aggregate marks obtained by her vis-a-vis aggregate marks obtained by other candidates who were already appointed - There was no common panel prepared conjoining selection made in year 1984 and persons appointed subsequently so that seniority could be determined by appellant - Even though direction issued by Tribunal had become final in view of peculiar position, Railways was directed to give seniority to respondent from date on which she joined service - Appeal disposed of.
