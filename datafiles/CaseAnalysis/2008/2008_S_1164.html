Hanuman Ram v State of Rajasthan and Othres

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1628; (2008) 15 SCC 652; AIR 2009 SC 69; 2008 (4) JCC 2829; JT 2008 (11) SC 154; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 823; 2008(13) SCALE 155; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 452, 364, 302/149 and 201/149; Cr.P.C, s. 311 - Application with the prayer that witness may be re-summoned for cross- examination with reference to their statements before the Children's Court allowed by high Court - Appeal against - Held, if a witness called by Court gives evidence against the complainant he should be allowed an opportunity to cross-examine - Right to cross-examine a witness who is called by a Court arises not under the provision of s. 311, but under the Evidence Act which gives a party the right to cross- examine a witness who is not his own witness - Since a witness summoned by the Court could not be termed a witness of any particular party, the Court should give the right of cross-examination to the complainant - High Court ought not to have accepted the prayer made by the accused persons in terms of s. 311 - Appeal allowed.
