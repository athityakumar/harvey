Raj Kumar v State Of H.P.

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1052; (2008) 11 SCC 76; 2008 (3) JCC 2147; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 611; 2008(10) SCALE 37; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 900

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Appellate Jurisdiction, Negligent, Revisional Power, CARRIERS&TRANSPORTATION

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 154, 279 and 304A - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Tort - Appeal against dismissal of criminal revision by High Court - Conviction u/ss.279 and 304A of IPC, 1860 to S.I. for one year and fine of Rs.1, 000/- For rash and negligent driving by bus driver resulting in accident causing fatal injuries to the children of complainant (passenger) - Magistrate found the evidence to be cogent - Ordered conviction but extended benefit of the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Sessions Judge set aside the order - High Court analysed the factual position to conclude that the findings recorded by the Court below were not erroneous and found limited scope for interference - Validity - Plea that basic requirements to attract ss. 279 and 304A not established - And sentence is harsh as accident took place about two decades ago - Supreme Court held, there is no manifest error in the conclusions or in analyzing the evidence - And HC was justified in law in not exercising revisional jurisdiction.
