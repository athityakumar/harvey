Mumtaz Yunus Mulani v State Of Maharashtra & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 451; (2008) 11 SCC 384; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 305; 2008 (2) CLR 52; 2008 (117) FLR 565; JT 2008 (4) SC 512; 2008(4) SCALE 637; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 241; 2008 (3) SLR 782; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1494; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 47

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Service

Keywords: Benefits Of Service

Summary: Service - Whether compassionate appointment of the appellant is warranted in the facts and circumstances of this case? - Held, appointment on compassionate ground is not a source of recruitment - Reason for making such a benevolent scheme by the State or the Public Sector Undertaking is to see that the dependents of the deceased are not deprived of the means of livelihood - It only enables the family of the deceased to get over the sudden financial crisis - Appeal dismissed.
