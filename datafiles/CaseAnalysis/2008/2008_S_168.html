Union of India v Satrohan

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1065; (2008) 8 SCC 313; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 620; 2008 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 182; JT 2008 (8) SC 148; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 803; 2008(10) SCALE 138; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 888

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Third Degree Method, Non- Compliance, Poppy, Non Examination, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Amendment) Act, 2001

Summary: Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 8(c), 15 - Trial Court convicted the respondent and sentenced to undergo RI for 10 years and fine of rupees one lakh with default stipulation - High Court directed acquittal holding that there was non- compliance of Sections 42(2) and 50 - Appeal against - Held, since authorization was obtained s. 57 comes into play - It is pointed out that there is licence of the father also - High Court's order does not suffer from any infirmity - Appeal partly allowed.
