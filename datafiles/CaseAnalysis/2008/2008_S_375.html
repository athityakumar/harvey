Government Of Andhra Pradesh & Ors v Smt. P. Laxmi Devi

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 245; (2008) 4 SCC 720; AIR 2008 SC 1640; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 56; 2008 (73) ALR 324; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2008 (2) SC 639; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 561; 2008 (105) RD 619; 2008(3) SCALE 45; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Movement, Presumption In Favour Of Constitutionality, Constitutionality, Constitutionality Of a Law, Indian Stamps (A.P. Amendment) Act, 1971, Indian Stamps (A.P. Amendment) Act, 1986, Bombay Municipal Act, Women's Right to Property Act, 1937

Summary: Indian Stamp Act, s. 47A - S. 47A which requires a party to deposit 50% deficit stamp duty as a condition precedent for a reference to the Collector under s. 47A is declared unconstitutional - Appeal against - Held, stamp duty is a tax, and hardship is not relevant in construing taxing statutes which are to be construed strictly - If the words used in a taxing statute are clear, one cannot try to find out the intention and the object of the statute - High Court fell in error in trying to go by the supposed object and intendment of the Stamp Act, and by seeking to find out the hardship which will be caused to a party by the impugned amendment of 1998 - Court should, ordinarily defer to the wisdom of the legislature unless it enacts a law about which there can be no manner of doubt about its unconstitutionality - Amendment was obviously made to plug a loophole in the Stamp Act so as to prevent evasion of stamp duty, and for quick collection of the duty - There are other statutes in which there are provisions for deduction at source, advance tax, etc. which aim at quick collection of tax, and the constitutional validity of these provisions have always been upheld - Constitutional validity of amended s. 47A is upheld - Appeal allowed.
