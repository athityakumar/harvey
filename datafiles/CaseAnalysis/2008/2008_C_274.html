C.K. Gangadharan and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Cochin

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1138; (2008) 8 SCC 739; 2008 (228) E.L.T. 497; [2008] 304 ITR 61; 2008 (3) KLT 472; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 1355; 2008(10) SCALE 426; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 52; 2009 (16) S.T.R. 659; [2008] 172 TAXMAN 87

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Whether revenue can be precluded from defending itself by relying upon the contrary decisions - Held, if the assessee takes the stand that the revenue acted mala fide in not preferring appeal in one case and filing the appeal in other case, it has to establish mala fides - Merely because in some cases the revenue has not preferred appeal that does not operate as a bar for the revenue to prefer an appeal in another case where there is just cause for doing so or it is in public interest to do so or for a pronouncement by the higher Court when divergent views are expressed by the Tribunals or the High Courts - Order accordingly.
