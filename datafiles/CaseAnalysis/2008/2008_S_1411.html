Babulal Badriprasad Varma v Surat Municipal Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2627; (2008) 12 SCC 401; AIR 2008 SC 2919; 2009 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2008 (6) SC 504; 2008(8) SCALE 206; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 564

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Town Planning Rules, 1955, Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Rules, 1979

Summary: Land & Property - Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Act, 1976, ss. 52, 67, 68, 81 and 82 - Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Rules, 1979, r. 26 - Plot 'A' and 'B' were owned by respondent no. 4 and appellant was a tenant under the respondent no. 4 in respect of Plot 'B' - A road widening project was proposed in terms of the Town Planning Scheme - Notices therefore were issued both to the appellant as also the respondent no. 4 - Appellant did not pursue his case in regard to the proposal for widening of the road - Subsequently, respondent's plot 'A' and 'B' were acquired by the Government under said scheme and another plots were allotted to respondent no. 4 as per scheme - After final allotment of plots appellant filed writ petition claiming that he was not served with notice of final allotments of the plots - Single Judge dismissed said writ petition and held that the interest of landlord and tenant being common and in absence of any inter se dispute between them even if any portion of the land which remained in possession of the tenant was included in the Scheme, the proper remedy would be to claim compensation to that extent - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Whether order of the Single Judge as affirmed by the DB could be upheld? - Held, appellant did not oppose the draft scheme - The draft scheme was approved and land had been acquired for public purpose - While the proceedings relating to allotment of final plot were in progress, appellant even did not file any objection thereto - If he intended to claim any interest for impugned plot either for the purpose of obtaining compensation for acquisition of said plot or to continue to have possession over said land, it was obligatory on his part to take part in the proceedings - A person interested in continuing to keep possession over a property and/or a part of the amount of compensation must lay his claim before the appropriate authority at the appropriate stage - If in absence of any such claim filed by the appellant, the authorities have proceeded to finalise allotment of final plot in favour of the respondent No. 4, it is too late in the day to contend that the entire scheme should be re-opened - Hence, appellant, through his conduct, has waived his right to an equitable remedy - Such conduct precludes and operates as estoppel against him with respect to asserting a right over a portion of the acquired land in a situation where the scheme in question has attained finality following as a result of the appellant's inaction - Appeal dismissed.
