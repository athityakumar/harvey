C. Balachandran and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2005; (2009) 3 SCC 179; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 610; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 131; 2009 (1) ESC 62; JT 2009 (1) SC 260; 2009 (3) LLJ 1; 2009(1) SCALE 195; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 960; 2009 (3) SLR 398

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Kerala Water Supply And Sewerage Act, 1986

Summary: Kerala Water Supply and Sewerage Act, 1986; Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules - Service - Right of the employees of the Authority for regularisation in service - Prayer for writ in the nature of mandamus directing the Kerala Water Authority to reinstate and regularise them in service purported to be in terms of a judgment and order of this Court in the case of Jacob M. Puthuparambil & Ors. etc. v. Kerala Water Authority & Ors. etc. (1991) 1 SCC 28 - Held, services of the appellants were terminated as far back as in 1987 and they did not question the legality or validity of the said order - A case of regularisation which attained finality and was not sub judice would not come within the purview of exception to the rule - Neither in law nor in equity were entitled to be reinstated in service - Appeal dismissed.
