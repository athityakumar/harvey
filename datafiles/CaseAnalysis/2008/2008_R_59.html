H. U. D. A v Raj Singh Rana

Supreme Court of India

16 June 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1607; (2009) 17 SCC 199; 2008 (1) CCC 324

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1997, Punjab Urban Estate (Development and Regulation) Act, 1964

Summary: Land & Property - Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1997(HUDA) - Interest Act, 1978,s.(3) 3 - Excess Amount of Interest - Sustainability - Appellant/HUDA allotted plot to respondent on payment of entire amount as initially computed as tentative sale price was fully paid by respondent, together with further amounts on account of enhanced compensation paid for plot, on basis of demand notices issued to respondent from time to time - Appellant by Memo dt. 15-6-2001 raised additional demand of Rs.71,800/- by imposing simple interest @ 10 per cent per annum up to 31-3-1987, 15 per cent per annum up to 15-1-1988, compound interest @ 15 per cent up to 31-8-2000 and thereafter again simple interest @ 15 percent per annum up to 31-8-2001 - According to respondent, rate of interest as indicated in allotment letter being 7 per cent simple interest per annum, appellant had acted illegally in demanding interest at higher rates, indicated hereinabove and such demand being arbitrary could not be sustained - Aggrieved, respondent filed complaint before District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum praying for refund of Rs.35,200/- - which allowed and directed appellant to calculate interest @ 7 per cent on 3rd and 4th enhancements and to refund extra amount charged to complainant/respondent with interest at rate of 7 per cent from date of complaint till its refund - Aggrieved appellant approached State Commission which affirmed District Forum's order - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before National Commission while confirming view took by District Forum and State Commission as to rate of interest which could have been charged by appellant, considered another aspect relating to charging of compound interest @ 15 per cent per annum from 16-1-1988 to 31-8-2000 and held that appellant was not entitled to charge such compound interest - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by National Commission was sustainable - Held, even though rate of interest indicated in allotment letter dt. 22-3-1974 might not had application as far as payment of additional price was concerned, District Forum had erred on site of reason and had allowed interest at rate of 7 per cent per annum upon holding that demand made by appellant at higher rate was contrary to mutual agreement contained in allotment letter - Further, even though policy might had been adopted by appellant for imposing deterrent rate of interest on defaults committed by allottees in payment of their dues, such imposition had to be in keeping with provisions of s. 3 of the 1978, Act and not in unreasonable manner - It might perhaps be even more pragmatic if condition regarding charging of interest at prevailing banking rates were included in allotment letters, having regard to provisions of s. 3(3) of the ?? Act - Hence, set aside orders passed by lower Forum and Commission and quashed additional demand of Rs. 71,800 and directed appellant would be entitled to impose simple interest on basis of prevailing current rate of interest and informed at Bar that entire amount by way of additional demand had been deposited upon protest, any amount which was in excess of amount to be computed on basis of said order, should be refunded to respondent within two weeks of such computation - Appeal allowed.
