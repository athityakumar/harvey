V. Y. Jose and Another v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2496; (2009) 3 SCC 78; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 996; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 59; 2009 (4) G.L.R. 3120; 2009 (1) JCC 410; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 711; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 869; 2008(16) SCALE 167; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 588

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 417 and 420 - Quashing of criminal case - Parties entered into a contract in terms whereof the firm was to manufacture a machine - Specifications for the machine were changed - Criminal complaint was filed against the appellants u/ss. 417, 420 r/w s. 114, IPC - Appellants filed an application for quashing of the said proceedings before HC which was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly refused to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 482, CrPC? - Appellant contended that breach of contract simpliciter does not constitute an offence u/s. 420, IPC - Held, there exists a distinction between pure contractual dispute of civil nature and an offence of cheating - Exercise of inherent power u/s. 482, CrPC is permissible where allegations set out in the complaint do not constitute the offence for which cognizance has been taken by the Magistrate - Present case is evidently a case of that nature - Judgment of the HC set aside - Criminal proceedings against the appellants quashed - Appeal allowed.
