State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Jagdish Saran Agrawal and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1856; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 817; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 123; 2008 (73) ALR 912; 2009 (1) AWC 1010; JT 2008 (13) SC 270; 2009 (2) MPLJ 31; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 370; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 373; 2008(15) SCALE 565; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 629

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Resjudicata, Land Acquisition Court, Default Of Plaintiff, Uttar Pradesh Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971, Uttar Pradesh Public Land (Eviction and Recovery of Rent and Damages) Act, 1959, Uttar Pradesh Roads and Lands (Control) Act, 1943

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971; Uttar Pradesh Public Land (Eviction and Recovery of Rent and Damages) Act, 1959 - Whether dismissal for default operate as resjudicata? - Held, dismissal of the suit for non-prosecution was not a decision on merit - Consequently, the said order cannot operate as Resjudicata - Appeal allowed.
