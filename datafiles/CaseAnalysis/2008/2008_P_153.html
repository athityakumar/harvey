Prabha Mathur & Anr. v Pramod Aggarwal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1561; (2008) 9 SCC 469; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 787; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 631; 2008 (4) JCC 2788; JT 2008 (10) SC 501; 2008 (4) KLT 385; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 96; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 636; 2008(13) SCALE 71; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 1086; 2008 (3) ShimLC 284

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952

Summary: IPC, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471 r/w 34 and 120B - Trial Court held that the entire case was of a civil nature - High Court remanded the matter to the learned Magistrate with a direction to make further inquiry in the matter and to pass an appropriate order - Appeal against - Held, High Court ought to have issued notice and afforded hearing before passing the impugned order in writ-petitions - Appeal allowed.
