Tarsem Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1962; (2008) 16 SCC 155; AIR 2009 SC 1454; 2009 (1) JCC 372; JT 2008 (13) SC 185; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 19; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 263; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 850; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 573; 2008(16) SCALE 148; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distinct Offence

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 304-B - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, nothing on record to show that any demand of dowry was made soon before her death - No evidence was brought on record to show that the cruelty or harassment was meted out to her for bringing insufficient dowry, in absence whereof the ingredients of s. 304B cannot be said to have been proved - It is difficult to come to the conclusion that it was the appellant and the appellant alone who was responsible for her death - Appeal allowed.
