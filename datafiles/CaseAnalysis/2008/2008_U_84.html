P.Venugopal v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 825; (2008) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 969; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 696; 2008(7) SCALE 255; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1; 2009 (3) SLR 680

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Corporate Body, Tenure Post, Constitutionality, Motor Spirit and Lubricants Taxation Act, 1938, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, All India Institute of Medical Sciences (Amendment) Act, 2007, Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research (Amendment) Act, 2007, AIIMS Regulation

Summary: All India Institute of Medical Sciences (Amendment) Act, 2007 - Constitutional validity of the proviso to s. 11(1A) - Held, curtailment of the term of five years can only be made for justifiable reasons and compliance with principles of natural justice for premature termination of the term of a Director of AIIMS - squarely applied also to the case of the writ petitioner as well and will also apply to any future Director of AIIMS - There was never any permissibility for any artificial and impermissible classification between the writ petitioner on the one hand and any future Director of AIIMS on the other when it relates to the premature termination of the term of office of the Director - Such an impermissible over classification through a one man legislation clearly falls foul of art. 14 of the Constitution being an apparent case of "naked discrimination" in our democratic civilized society governed by Rule of Law and renders the impugned proviso as void, ab initio and unconstitutional - Petition allowed.
