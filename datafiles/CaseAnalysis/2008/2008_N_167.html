National Insurance Company Limited v Nitin Khandelwal

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 848; (2008) 11 SCC 259; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1166; 2008 (4) ALD(SC) 21; 2008(3) ALL MR 875; 2008 (71) ALR 813; 2008 (3) AWC 2179; 2008 (1) CCC 204; 2008 (2) CompLJ 475; 2008 (4) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2008 (5) CTC 184; 2008 (3) KLT 332; 2008 (151) PLR 754B; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 193; 2008(7) SCALE 351; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1047

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Comprehensive Policy, Third Party Risk, Cause Of Accident, Intervening Cause

Summary: Insurance claim - According to the appellant, respondent had violated the terms of the insurance policy and, therefore, rejected the claim - Held, in the case of theft of vehicle breach of condition is not germane - Appellant Insurance Company is liable to indemnify the owner of the vehicle when the insurer has obtained comprehensive policy for the loss caused to the insurer - In case of theft of vehicle, nature of use of the vehicle cannot be looked into and the Insurance Company cannot repudiate the claim on that basis - Appeal disposed of.
