Ghurey Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1197; (2008) 10 SCC 450; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1318; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2873; JT 2008 (10) SC 324; 2008(10) SCALE 616; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Self Defence, Power Of The Court, Faecal Matter, Right Of Self Defence, Semi Digested Food

Summary: Murder - Trial court acquitted the accused - High Court reversed the trial court's decision, finding the accused guilty - Appeal against conviction - Held, presumption of innocence of accused is reinforced by an order of the acquittal - Appellate court could have interfered only for very substantial and compelling reasons - Trial court carefully scrutinized the entire evidence and documents on record and arrived at the correct conclusion - Reasoning given by the High Court for overturning the judgment of the trial court is wholly unsustainable - If the trial court's view is possible and plausible, the High Court should not substitute the same by its own possible views - Appeal allowed.
