Kanhaiyalal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1155; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 474; (2008) 4 SCC 668; AIR 2008 SC 1044; 2008 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 23; JT 2008 (1) SC 286; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 610; 2008(1) SCALE 165; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specific Plea, TADA Act, Narcotics Commissioner

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8, 18 - TrialCourt acquitted the appellant - High Court convicted appellant on the basis of the statement made by him under s. 67 - Appeal against - Up to what extent can a statement under s. 67 of the NDPS Act be relied upon for convicting a person accused of having committed an offence - Held, what is important is whether the statement made by the person concerned is made during inquiry prior to his arrest or after he had been formally charged with the offence and made an accused in respect thereof - As long as such statement was made by the accused at a time when he was not under arrest, the bar under ss. 24 to 27 of the Evidence Act would not operate nor would the provisions of art. 20(3) of the Constitution be attracted - It is only after a person is placed in the position of an accused that the bar imposed under the aforesaid provision will come into play - Appeal dismissed.
