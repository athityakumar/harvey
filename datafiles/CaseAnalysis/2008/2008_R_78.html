Progressive Education Society & Anr v Rajendra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 196; (2008) 3 SCC 310; AIR 2008 SC 1442; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 655; 2008 (2) ESC 187; 2008 (116) FLR 1021; JT 2008 (2) SC 500; 2008 (2) LLN 729; 2008 (2) MahLJ 715; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 65; 2008(2) SCALE 537; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 1005; 2008 (8) SLR 624; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 648

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Power To Terminate, Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977, Reviewing Authority

Summary: Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977 - Termination on the ground that work was found to be unsatisfactory during the period of probation - Whether the provisions of rr. 14 and 15, and, in particular r. 15(6) of the MEPS Rules, 1981, would control the powers vested in the Management of the School under s. 5 (3) of the MEPS Act? - Held, such a power lies with the Appointing Authority which is at liberty to terminate the services of a Probationer if it finds the performance of the Probationer to be unsatisfactory during the period of probation - While rr. 14 and 15 of the MEPS Rules, 1981 cannot override the provisions of s. 5(3) of the MEPS Act, it has to be said that the requirements of r. 15(6) would be a factor which the School Management has to take into consideration while exercising the powers which it undoubtedly has and is recognised under s. 5 (3) of the Act - No grounds to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
