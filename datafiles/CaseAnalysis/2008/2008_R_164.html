Dharma Naika v Rama Naika & Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 151; (2008) 14 SCC 517; AIR 2008 SC 1276; 2008 (3) KarLJ 188; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 279; 2008(2) SCALE 236; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1978 - Whether sale deed, which was executed and registered after the commencement of the Act but in respect of which, the agreement for sale was executed before the commencement of the Act, would be hit by the provisions of s. 4? - Held, transfer was effected after the commencement of the Act by a deed of sale dated 13th of October, 1986 without the previous permission of the Government - That being the position, such transfer must be held to be null and void and no right, title or interest in such land shall be conveyed or be deemed ever to have conveyed by such transfer - Appeal dismissed.
