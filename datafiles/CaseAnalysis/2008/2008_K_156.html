Kusumam Hotels Private Limited v Kerala State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1392; (2008) 13 SCC 213; AIR 2008 SC 2796; JT 2008 (9) SC 161; 2008 (3) KLT 276; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 592; 2008(9) SCALE 448; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Kerala Buildings Tax Act 1975

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 56(2), 61 and 185(5) - Customs Act, 1962 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Whether Govt. Order D/- 26-09-2000 is reasonable having been given retrospective effect and retroactive operation? - Tourism was declared to be an industry wide range of concessions, inter alia, covered electricity and water charges - It is not a case where some exemptions or concessions were to be given for a specific period or as a one time measure - No time limit was fixed for applicability in respect of policy decisions - It is also beyond any doubt that concessions granted can be withdrawn in public interest - Indisputably, state is also entitled to change or alter economic policies - What is binding on board is policy of state - Direction of state was to apply a particular category of tariff to appellants - Mere issuance of an exemption notification under a provision in a fiscal statute such as s. 25 of Customs Act, 1962, could not create any promissory estoppel because such an exemption by its very nature is susceptible to being revoked or modified or subjected to other conditions - In other words, there is no unequivocal representation - Seeds of equivocation are inherent in power to grant exemption - Appeal dismissed.
