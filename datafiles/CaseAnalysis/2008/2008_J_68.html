Oriental Insurance Company Ltd. v Jashuben & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 210; (2008) 4 SCC 162; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 752; 2009 (3) ACC 699; AIR 2008 SC 1734; 2008 (2) AWC 1144; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1705; JT 2008 (2) SC 415; 2008 (4) MahLJ 569; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 33; 2008 (4) MPLJ 1; 2008 (151) PLR 465; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 91; 2008(2) SCALE 474; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Assessment Of Damages, Original Claim, Life Expectancy

Summary: Motor Accidents Claim - Adopting a multiplier of 16, amount of compensation was determined at Rs.13, 27, 872/- - High Court adopted multiplier of 13 instead of 16 held that reasonable interest payable would be 8% per annum - Appeal against - Held, what would have been the income of the deceased on the date of retirement was not a relevant factor in the light of peculiar facts of this case and, thus, the approach of the Tribunal and the High Court must be held to be incorrect - After deduction, amount of income comes to Rs.5, 738/- per month [Rs.8609/- ' Rs.2871/-] and the amount of compensation should be determined by adopting the multiplier of 13 - Appeal partly allowed.
