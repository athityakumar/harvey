(1) Harendra Sarkar; (2) Kailash Gour and Others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 755; (2008) 9 SCC 204; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 740; AIR 2008 SC 2467; JT 2008 (6) SC 330; 2008(7) SCALE 135; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability, Matter referred to Larger Bench

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 326, 34, 448 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused appellants allegedly murdered deceased - Trial Court relied upon evidence and passed conviction order - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC passed impugned order - Hence instant appeal.

Held, fact that victims were a young woman, and two children, and grevious injuries to two other young boys supports view that murders had not been committed on account of any enmity, but were a fall out of communal tension prevailing in that area. It bears reiteration that victims could hardly have been dealt with on account of any animosity, but assailants attempted to do away with anyone who came along. As already mentioned, Trial Court and HC have already done sifting that is required and ultimately maintained conviction of only a few of accused. It is statements of eye witnesses which are of utmost importance and unless very good reasons can be given for disbelieving them, they must be accepted, and arguments with regard to delay in FIR or some minor contradictions in statements, vis-'-vis statements in Court or a flaw in recording of post mortem or inquest reports or non-recovery of murder weapons etc. are a matter of little concern as these issues would be relevant and in normal circumstances and to situation where civil administration was functioning effectively, but in a case of a complete break-down of civil administration, these broad arguments are wholly inapplicable. Appeal dismissed.
