State of Haryana and Others v Shakuntla Devi

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2592; (2008) 15 SCC 380; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 387; AIR 2009 SC 869; 2008 (119) FLR 1169; JT 2008 (12) SC 48; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 454; 2008(13) SCALE 621; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 62; 2009 (2) SLR 633

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: M.P. Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Act, 1961, Family Pension Scheme, 1964

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services Rules - Family Pension Scheme, 1964 - Family pension - Entitlement - Respondent's husband expired while he was in service - Respondent/legal heir sought family pension - Appellant rejected respondent's request on the ground that deceased employee was an ad hoc employee and not a permanent employee - Respondent challenged appellants order of rejection before the HC on the ground that appellant had allowed deceased employee to continue his employment even after the expiry of his ad hoc appointment period - HC allowed respondent writ petitions holding that any employee who had completed more than one year's service would become eligible for grant of family pension irrespective of the fact that whether he was appointed on a permanent or a temporary post - Whether respondent is entitled to the family pension under the 1964 Scheme - Held, 1964 Scheme is subject to Part II of the Rules and Part II are subject to Part I, which in turn would be subject to the proviso appended to art. 309 of the Constitution - Thus, before a person can be said to have been acquired a right to obtain the benefits of 1964 Family Pension Scheme must satisfy the eligibility as envisaged under the Rules - Family pension can be granted to the dependent of the deceased Government employee under the Family Pension Scheme only by way of a further relief and not by independent of the main Pension Rules - Offers of appointment clearly showed that the nature of appointment of the deceased employee was ad hoc one - Merely allowing the deceased employee to continue even after the expiry of ad hoc period would not, in absence of any statutory interdict, mean that the ad hoc employee ceased to be so and acquired the status of a permanent or temporary employee - Therefore, husband of the respondent was not a regular government employee till his death and, thus, the Family Pension Scheme was not applicable in his case - Appeal allowed.
