Union of India and Others v Dineshan K.K

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 26; (2008) 1 SCC 586; AIR 2008 SC 1026; 2008(1) ALL MR 936; 2008 (1) AWC 763; 2008 (118) FLR 238; JT 2008 (1) SC 231; 2008 (2) LLN 111; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 725; 2008(1) SCALE 74; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 100; 2008 (2) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Warrant Officer, Doctrine Of Equal Pay For Equal Work

Summary: Directions issued to re-designate the rank of Havildar (Radio Mechanic) as Warrant Officer as recommended by the Ministry of Home Affairs and the pay scale as admissible to their counterparts in the Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) and the Border Security Force (BSF) shall be granted from the same date - appeal against - Held, in view of the total absence of any plea on the part of the Union of India that Radio Mechanics in other paramilitary forces were performing different or more onerous duties as compared to the Radio Mechanics in Assam Rifles, the impugned decision of the Government was clearly irrational and arbitrary and thus, violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Not find any infirmity in the impugned directions given by the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
