New India Assurance Company Limited v Sadanand Mukhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2016; (2009) 2 SCC 417; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 815; 2009 (2) ACC 432; AIR 2009 SC 1788; 2009 (2) ALD(SC) 13; 2009(1) ALL MR 978; 2009 (1) AWC 808; 2008 (1) CCC 906; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 94; JT 2008 (13) SC 536; 2009 (3) MPLJ 133; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 817; 2009(1) SCALE 252; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1313

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Amount Of Premium, Public Service Vehicle, Third Party Risk, Claim Tribunal, Gratuitous Passenger, Use Of Vehicle, Use Of The Vehicle In a Public Place, Additional Premium, Insurance Against Third Party Risk, Death Or Injury

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Whether the insurer is liable to pay the amount of compensation in relation to the accident occurred by use of the vehicle which was being driven by the son of the insured? - Held, in view of the authoritative pronouncements, held that the insurance company was not liable - Appeal allowed.
