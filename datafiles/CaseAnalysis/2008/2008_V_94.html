Ramesh Chandra Sankla Etc and Others v Vikram Cement Etc and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2601; (2008) 14 SCC 58; AIR 2008 SC 713; 2008 (4) ESC 614; JT 2008 (8) SC 1; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 801; 2008(10) SCALE 112; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian High Courts Act, 1861, Madhya Pradesh Uchcha Nyayalaya (Khand Nyayapeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005, Madhya Pradesh Uchcha Nyayalaya (Letters Patent Appeals Samapthi) Adhiniyam, 1981

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960, s. 31 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Preliminary issue - Adjudication of - Appellant/workmen opted for voluntary retirement under a Scheme framed by the respondent/management - Appellants retired and received the benefits under the Scheme - Appellants raised an industrial dispute alleging that they were forced to opt for the Scheme under coercion and they were not compensated adequately - Labour Court admitted the matter - Respondents, as preliminary issue, raised the question of maintainability of appellants claim petitions on the ground that the appellants had received the amount towards full and final settlement under the Scheme and hence petition was not maintainable - Labour Court dismissed the preliminary issue of maintainability - Respondents challenged the Labour Court order before Industrial Court - Industrial Court upheld the Labour Court order - Respondents challenged Industrial Court order before HC - Single Judge dismissed respondents writ petition - Respondents filed intra-court appeal before DB - DB dismissed the appeal as non-maintainable, however, direction issued to the appellants to refund the amount received under the Scheme - Held, since Single Judge was exercising power of superintendence over a Court/Tribunal subordinate to it u/art. 227 of the Constitution, a remedy of intra-court appeal was not available to respondents - Therefore, DB was right in coming to the conclusion that intra court appeals filed by the respondents were not maintainable - Since appellants contended that they had not accepted the Scheme with their free consent, Labour Court felt that the controversy raised by the appellants can only be decided in the light of the evidence before it - Therefore, courts below did not commit any error of jurisdiction in not deciding the issue as to the maintainability of claim-petitions as preliminary issue - Further, appellants cannot retain the benefit if they want to prosecute claim petitions instituted by them with the Labour Court, therefore, direction passed by the DB to refund of amount cannot be termed as unjust, inequitable or improper, hence impugned order of DB is confirmed - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Uchcha Nyayalaya (Khand Nyayapeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Single Judge order - Intra-court appeal before DB u/s. 2 of the Act - Maintainability - Held, Single Judge of HC in exercise of original jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution, an intra court appeal would lay, however, Single Judge exercises power of superintendence u/art. 227 of the Constitution, intra court appeal would not be competent - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Withdrawal of writ petition - Filing of fresh petition - Applicability of res judicata - Held, petitioner is open to withdraw a petition filed by him and a Court of Law would not prevent him from withdrawing his petition - However, if such withdrawal is without the leave of the Court, it would mean that the petitioner is not interested in prosecuting or continuing the proceedings and he abandons his claim - In such cases, public policy requires that petitioner should not start afresh second round of litigation and doctrine of res judicata barred him to re-agitate his earlier claims - Appeals disposed of.
