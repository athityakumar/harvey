Subodh S. Salaskar v Jayprakash M. Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1422; (2008) 13 SCC 689; AIR 2008 SC 3086; 2008 (6) Bom.C.R. 139; [2008] 145 Comp Cas 121; 2008 (4) CompLJ 278; 2008 CRLJ 3953; 2009 (1) G.L.R. 484; 2008 (3) JCC (NI) 330; JT 2008 (8) SC 637; 2008 (3) KLT 616; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 904; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 875; 2008(11) SCALE 42; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 681

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice, Power Of The Court, Cheques, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1988, Post Dated Cheque, Post Dated Cheques

Summary: Whether the proviso appended to s. 142 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 inserted by the Negotiable Instruments (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 2002, is retrospective? - Held, clause (b) of s. 142 confers a jurisdiction upon the court to condone the delay - It is a substantive provision and not a procedural one - If the proviso appended to Clause (b) of s. 142 contained a substantive provision and not a procedural one, it could not have been given a retrospective effect - A substantive law, as it is well-settled, in absence of an express provision, cannot be given a retrospective effect or retroactive operation - If the complaint petition was barred by limitation, the learned Magistrate had no jurisdiction to take cognizance under s. 138 - Direction to issue summons on the appellant, therefore, being illegal and without jurisdiction was a nullity - Appeal allowed.
