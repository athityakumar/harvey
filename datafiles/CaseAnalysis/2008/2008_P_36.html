Union of India v Padam Narain Aggarwal Etc

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1593; (2008) 13 SCC 305; AIR 2009 SC 254; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1835; 2008 (231) E.L.T. 397; 2008 (4) JCC 2733; JT 2008 (11) SC 6; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 665; 2008(13) SCALE 171; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 179

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Case, Accused Of An Offence, Power To Arrest, Criminal Procedure, 1898, Power To Search Premises

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Anticipatory bail applications were disposed of by the High Court directing the Customs Authorities not to arrest the respondents of any non-bailable offence without ten days prior notice to them - Appeal against - Held, power to arrest a person by a Custom Officer is statutory in character and cannot be interfered with - Such power of arrest can be exercised only in those cases where the Custom Officer has 'reason to believe' that a person has been guilty of an offence punishable under ss. 132, 133, 135, 135A or 136 of the Act - Order passed by the High Court illegally obstructs, interferes and curtails the authority of Custom Officers from exercising statutory power of arrest a person said to have committed a non-bailable offence by imposing a condition of giving ten days prior notice, a condition not warranted by law - Appeal partly allowed.
