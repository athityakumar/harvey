UCO Bank & Anr v Rajinder Lal Capoor

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2418; (2008) 5 SCC 257; AIR 2008 SC 1831; 2008 (2) AWC 2030; 2008 (5) ESC 793; 2008 (3) LLJ 299; 2008 (3) LLN 122; 2008(6) SCALE 1; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 775; 2008 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: UCO Bank Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976, UCO Bank (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979, UCO Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976

Summary: (A) Service - UCO Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976 - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings - Held, 1976 Regulations provide for the mode and manner in which a disciplinary proceeding is initiated - It expressly provides for service of charge sheet - In terms of the 1976 Regulations drawing up of a charge sheet by the disciplinary authority is the first step for initiation of a disciplinary authority. Unless and until, therefore, a charge sheet is drawn up, a disciplinary proceedings for the purpose of the 1976 Regulations cannot be initiated - When a proceeding is initiated for the purpose of taking any disciplinary action on the ground of any misconduct which might have been committed by the officer concerned indisputably the procedures laid down in the 1976 Regulations are required to be resorted to - Petition dismissed.

(B) Service - UCO Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976 - UCO Bank (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 - Applicability of 1979 Regulations and 1976 Regulations - Held, if it is found that a disciplinary proceeding can be and should be initiated, recourse to the 1976 Regulations would have to be taken, if not, the 1979 Regulations may be resorted to if the conditions precedent therefor are satisfied - 1979 Regulations would be attracted only for the purpose of termination of service - Had the intention of the regulation making authority been that the legal fiction created under Clause (ii) of Sub-regulation (3) of Regulation 20 would cover both Clauses (i) and (iii), the same should have been placed only after Clause (iii) - Petition dismissed.

(C) Service - UCO Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976 - UCO Bank (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings - Whether in absence of any charge-sheet having been issued, the disciplinary proceedings could be said to have been initiated? - Held, drawing up of a charge sheet, is the condition precedent for initiation of a disciplinary proceedings - Ordinarily no disciplinary proceedings can be continued in absence of any rule after an employee reaches his age of superannuation - A rule which would enable the disciplinary authority to continue a disciplinary proceedings despite the officers reaching the age of superannuation must be a statutory rule - In absence of the statutory rules operating in the field, resorting to a preliminary enquiry would not by itself be enough to hold that a departmental proceeding has been initiated - Petition dismissed.
