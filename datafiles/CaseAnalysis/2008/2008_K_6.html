Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v K. Brahmanandam and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 838; (2008) 5 SCC 241; AIR 2008 SC 3170; 2008 (2) ESC 313; 2008 (117) FLR 1086; 2008(4) SCALE 685; 2008(7) SCALE 685; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 140; 2008 (5) SLR 247

Case Digest

Subject: Service; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Quasi-Contract, Temporary Or Ad Hoc, Irregular Appointment, Andhra Pradesh Educational Institutions (Establishment, Recognition, Administration and Control of schools under Private Managements) Rules, 1993

Summary: Whether the State or the Educational Institution was liable to bear the financial burden for payment of wages to the respondents who had been appointed as Secondary Grade Teachers which appointment had not followed the provisions of Andhra Pradesh Educational Institutions (Establishment, Recognition, Administration and Control Of Schools Under Private Managements) Rules, 1993? - Held, if the State in terms of the statute is not liable to pay the salary to the teachers, no legal right accrues in favour of those who had been appointed in violation of mandatory provisions of the statute or statutory rules - Appointments made in violation of the mandatory provisions of a statute would be illegal and, thus, void; illegality cannot be ratified - Respondents however, are entitled to salary from the school authorities as they have worked even if no valid contract had come into being; salary amount would be payable in terms of Section 70 of the Indian Contract Act - Principles of quasi-contract, however, must apply keeping in view the relationship between the parties; doctrine of quasi-contract cannot be applied in such situation against the State - Appeal allowed.
