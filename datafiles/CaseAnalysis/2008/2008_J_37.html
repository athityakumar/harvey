Director, Horticulture Punjab and Others v Jagjivan Parshad

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 562; (2008) 5 SCC 539; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 327; 2008 (117) FLR 777; JT 2008 (4) SC 571B; 2008(6) SCALE 103; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 851; 2008 (3) SLR 745; 2008 (3) SLR 746; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1385

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quasi- Judicial

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Labour Court held that the termination was illegal and that the workman was entitled to reinstatement with 50% back wages, continuity of service and other service benefits - High Court confirmed the award - Appeal against - Held, writ petition involved disputed issues regarding eligibility - Manner in which the High Court has disposed of the writ petition shows that the basic requirement of indicating reasons was not kept in view and is a classic case of non-application of mind - Remitted the matter to High Court.
