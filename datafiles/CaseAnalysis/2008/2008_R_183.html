CSIR and Others v Ramesh Chandra Agrawal and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2606; (2009) 3 SCC 35; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 547; JT 2009 (1) SC 562; 2009(1) SCALE 551; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1378; 2009 (3) SLR 424

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964, Senior Research Associateship (Scientists Pool) Scheme, Fellowship and Associateship Schemes, CSIR Service Rules, 1994

Summary: Service - Regularization - Entitlement - Appellant formulated a Scheme for absorption and regularization of Researchers working with them - Respondent/Research Fellows sought regularization under the Scheme - Appellants rejected respondents request on the ground that respondents did not complete 15 years of service within the fixed cut-off date - Respondents filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal rejected the application - Respondents challenged the Tribunal order before HC - HC set aside the Tribunal order and directed absorption and regularization of respondents - Hence the present appeal - Respondents contended that they could work as an associate with the appellant for a maximum period of 13 years and therefore, fixation of cut-off date for completing 15 years of service was arbitrary - Held, a 'State' is entitled to fix a cut off date and such a decision can be struck down only when it is arbitrary - HC proceeded on the basis that the cut off date should have been the date of issuance of the notification for vacancies, however, employer has discretion in that behalf as a choice - HC cannot fix another date as cut-off date only with a view to show sympathy to some of the candidates as another date was more suitable - Court's power of judicial review is limited in the sense that the impugned action can be struck down only when it is found to be arbitrary - Cut-off date cannot be struck down only on the reason that respondents are provided only two chances for consideration of their candidature - Cut-off date is not required to be fixed by keeping in view of the principles of legitimate expectation - HC committed a serious error insofar as it failed to take into consideration that the respondents did not have any legal right for regularization, therefore, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeals allowed.
