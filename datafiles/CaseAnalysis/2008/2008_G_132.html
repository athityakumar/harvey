Manjul Srivastava v Government of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1378; (2008) 8 SCC 652; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 562; 2008 (1) CCC 332; 2008 (5) CompLJ 66; 2008 (3) CPJ(Sc) 84; 2008 (4) CPR 157; 2008 (3) CPR 352; JT 2008 (9) SC 584; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 612B; 2008(12) SCALE 110; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Dispute pertains to allotment of a certain plot of land by the GDA in its Govindpuram Housing Scheme - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Commission held that the Ghaziabad Development Authority had not resorted to any "unfair trade practice" inasmuch as the appellant was unsuccessful in the draw for allotment of a plot - Appeal against - Held, since the appellant was not allotted any plot and only a plot was reserved subject to holding of a lottery for the specific plots for allotment, the appellant would not acquire any legal right to such plot, only she would be entitled to get refund of her amount deposited with the GDA - Directed the respondent to refund the money already deposited with the GDA with interest at the rate of 18 per cent and not at the rate of 5% - Appeals partly allowed.
