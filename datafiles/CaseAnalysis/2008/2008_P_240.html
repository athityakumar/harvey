Bhagwan Dass & Anr. v Punjab State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 29; (2008) 1 SCC 579; AIR 2008 SC 990; 2008 (70) ALR 47; 2008 (1) AWC 827; 2008 (1) ESC 147; 2008 (116) FLR 627; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 50; JT 2008 (1) SC 240; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 637; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 717; 2008 (1) LLJ 696; 2008 (2) LLN 1; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 78; 2008(1) SCALE 64; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 32; 2008 (2) SLR 32

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, Punjab State Electricity Board Employees Punishment & Appeal Regulation 1971

Summary: Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, s. 47 - Held, only material on the basis of which the officers of the Board took the stand that the appellant had himself made a request for retirement on medical grounds was his letter dated July 17, 1996 - It is impossible to read that letter as a voluntary offer for retirement - It was the duty of the superior officers to explain to appellant the correct legal position and to tell him about his legal rights - Action of the Board in terminating the service of the disabled employee with effect from March 21, 1997 must be held to be bad and illegal - Appeal allowed.
