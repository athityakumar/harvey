Mallavarapu Kasivisweswara Rao v Thadikonda Ramulu Firm and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 875; (2008) 7 SCC 655; AIR 2008 SC 2898; 2008 (4) ALD(SC) 15; 2008 (72) ALR 508; 2008 (3) BC 416; 2008 (6) Bom.C.R. 826; JT 2008 (11) SC 43; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 336; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 205; 2008 (3) RLW 2717; 2008(8) SCALE 680; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1210

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Commercial Transaction

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 118 - Banking and Finance - Promissory Notes - Suit for recovery of its amounts with interest - Fact that the promissory note was for consideration, not denied - Appellant's evidence inconsistent with presumption u/s. 118 of the Act - Courts below refused to pass decree on basis of presumption - Burden of proof - Held, if defendant have discharged the initial onus of proof showing that the existence of consideration was improbable or doubtful or the same was illegal, the onus would shift to the plaintiff who would be obliged to prove it - Mere denial would not have been sufficient to prove the non-existence of consideration - Appeal Allowed.
