Ludhiana Improvement Trust and Another v Today Homes and Infrastructure Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1640; (2008) 10 SCC 715; AIR 2009 SC 246; 2008 (4) AWC 4025; 2009 (1) CompLJ 14; JT 2008 (11) SC 313; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3160; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 1073; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 675; 2008(13) SCALE 222; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 397

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Punjab Town Improvement Trust Rules, 1939

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Chief Justice took the view that having regard to s. 16, it was for the Arbitrator and not the Chief Justice to decide the question of jurisdiction - Appeal against - Held, relying on the earlier Constitution Bench decision in Konkan Railway Corporation Limited and Anr. Vs. Rani Construction Private Limited, [2002 (2) SCC 388], the High Court left it to the learned Arbitrator appointed by it to decide the said issues under s. 16, which was contrary to the directions given by the seven-Judge Bench of this Court in Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd. vs. Pinkcity Midway Petroleum [2003 (6) SCC 503], which categorically overruled the decision of the Constitution Bench in the Konkan Railway case - Remitted the matter.
