Sunil Poddar and Others v Union Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 52; (2008) 2 SCC 326; 2008 (2) ADJ 323; AIR 2008 SC 1006; 2008 (71) ALR 318; 2008 (1) AWC 386; 2011 (1) BC 155; 2009 (1) CGLJ 190; [2008] 141 Comp Cas 597; 2008 (2) CTC 686; 2008 (1) DRTC 153; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 23; JT 2008 (1) SC 308; 2008 (3) MahLJ 84; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1193; 2008 (149) PLR 767; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 555; 2008(1) SCALE 153; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 261

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908; Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Ex parte judgment and order holding that the Bank was entitled to recover 1, 07, 17, 177/- with interest and cost from the defendant Nos. 1-9 jointly and severely - Held, in view of the fact that the appellants were appearing before the Civil Court, it was not necessary for the Bank to get summonses published in a newspaper after the matter was transferred - If the Court is convinced that the defendant had otherwise knowledge of the proceedings and he could have appeared and answered the plaintiff's claim, he cannot put forward a ground of non service of summons for setting aside ex parte decree passed against him by invoking r. 13 of O. 9 of the Code - Appeal dismissed.
