Sudhir Kumar Rana v Surinder Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 768; (2008) 12 SCC 436; 2008 (4) ACC 770; AIR 2008 SC 2405; 2008(3) ALL MR 907; 2008 (3) AWC 2483; 2008 (1) CCC 164; 2008 (3) CTC 605; JT 2008 (7) SC 131; 2008 (3) KLT 322; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 1386; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 265; 2008(8) SCALE 594; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Wrong Doers

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Tribunal opined that as the appellant did not possess a driving licence, he must be held to have contributed to the accident found to be entitled to a sum of Rs. 12, 000/- only - Whether the appellant can be said to have guilty of contributory negligence? - Held, appellant was not possessing any licence but no finding of fact has been arrived at that he was driving the two-wheeler rashly and negligently - If he was not driving rashly and negligently which contributed to the accident, fail to see as to how, only because he was not having a licence, he would be held to be guilty of contributory negligence - Appeal allowed.
