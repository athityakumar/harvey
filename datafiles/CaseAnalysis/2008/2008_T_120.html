Kabushiki Kaisha Toshiba v Tosiba Appliances Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1395; (2008) 10 SCC 766; AIR 2009 SC 892; 2008 (6) AWC 5679; 2008 (37) PTC 394; 2008(8) SCALE 354; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 670

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Trade Marks Rules, 1959

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 46, 47, 56 and 109 - Non-use of trade mark - Removal of the registered mark from the register - Jurisdiction of the Registrar - Whether the order of the DB of High Court dismissing an appeal from judgment of Single Judge of HC affirming final order passed by Deputy Registrar, Trade Marks, is correct in law? - Held, interpretation of and distinction between ss. 46(1)(a) and (b) of Act in issue - If period specified in cl.(b) has elapsed and there has been no bona fide use of trade mark, bona fide intention of registered proprietor to use trade mark, at date of application for registration, becomes immaterial and trade mark is liable to be removed from register unless case falls u/s. 46(3) - U/cl.(a), where there had been a bona fide intention to use trade mark in respect of which registration was sought, merely because trade mark had not been used for a period shorter than five years from date of its registration will not entitle any person to have that trade mark taken off register - Cls. (a) and (b) operate in different fields - S. 46(3) covers a case falling within cl. (b) and not cl. (a) - Intention to use a trade mark sought to be registered must be genuine and real - When a trade mark is registered, it confers a valuable right seeking to prevent trafficking in trademarks - It seeks to distinguish goods made by one person from those made by another - Person who does not have any bona fide intention to use trade mark, is not expected to get his product registered so as to prevent any other person from using same - Appellate jurisdiction of HC u/s. 109(6) is not restricted - HC has power to make any order which Registrar could make under Act - HC having a plenary jurisdiction was not only entitled to take into consideration materials placed on record as also finding of Registrar, but could also arrive at its own finding - Appellant whether from date of registration, or from date of order of rectification or even a post rectification period did not use registered trade mark - In 1985 it issued an advertisement before any controversy arose - Registration was renewed every seven years and stands extended upto 2016 A D - Appeal allowed.
