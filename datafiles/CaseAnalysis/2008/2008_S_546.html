Ravishwar Manjhi & Ors. v State Of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2706; (2008) 16 SCC 561; AIR 2009 SC 1262; 2009 (1) JCC 546; JT 2008 (13) SC 292; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 802; 2008(16) SCALE 45; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 326, 324, 323 and 326 r/w. 149 - Cross FIRs were filed, one by the complainants against the appellants and other by the appellants against the complainant - The Complainant alleged that the appellants assaulted complainant's father (deceased) and his uncle (PW-1) - The appellants alleged that the deceased trespassed their house and tried to outrage the modesty of a woman of the house - Prosecution was initiated against the appellants u/ss. 302, 307, 326, 148 and 324 of IPC - Prosecution was also initiated against the complainant u/ss. 147, 323 and 342 of IPC - The appellants took the plea of self-defence - The case against the complainant was still pending before trial court - The Trial court convicted all the appellants and awarded different quantum of sentences - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, the trial court as also the HC appeared to have proceeded on the premise that as the appellants had not been able to prove their defence, therefore, the prosecution version should be accepted - The approach of the courts below was, thus, not correct - The investigation was carried out in a slipshod manner - The FIR clearly showed that even before lodging of the FIR, investigation had started - Moreover, there was no examination of investigation officer - The case was required to be considered in the light of the defence case - The appellants were entitled to right of private defence - Hence, appellants were acquitted - Appeal allowed.
