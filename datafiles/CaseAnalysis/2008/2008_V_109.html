Vinod Solanki v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2011; (2008) 16 SCC 537; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 23; 2009(2) ALL MR 462; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 157; JT 2009 (1) SC 1; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 911; 2008(16) SCALE 31; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1070; 2009 (13) S.T.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Fema; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act

Summary: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - What would be the effect of a retracted confession for the purpose of levy of penalty? - Held, evidences brought on record by way of confession which stood retracted must be substantially corroborated by other independent and cogent evidences, which would lend adequate assurance to the court that it may seek to rely thereupon - Burden is on the prosecution to show that the confession is voluntary in nature and not obtained as an outcome of threat, etc. if the same is to be relied upon solely for the purpose of securing a conviction - Law does not say that the accused has to prove that retraction of confession made by him was because of threat, coercion, etc. but the requirement is that it may appear to the court as such - Appeal allowed.
