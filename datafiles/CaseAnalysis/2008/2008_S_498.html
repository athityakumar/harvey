Bachan Singh and Another v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1470; (2008) 12 SCC 23; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 904; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 3267; 2008 (4) JCC 2889; JT 2008 (10) SC 135; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 343; 2008(12) SCALE 461B; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence, Appeals Disposed Of, Gun Shot Injury, Under Trial, Non- Examination, Non- Appealing

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 325; Arms Act, s. 25(a) - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, statements in a case diary cannot be utilized as evidence to corroborate the statement of the prosecution witness - Parties appeared to be itching for a fight and had collected their relatives and supporters from far and between to augment their strength - Both groups must share equal responsibility for this incident - Appeals disposed of.
