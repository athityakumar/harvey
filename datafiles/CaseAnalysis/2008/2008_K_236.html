State of Punjab v Kulwant Singh @ Kanta

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1069; (2008) 16 SCC 290; AIR 2008 SC 3279; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2577; 2008 CRLJ 4322; 2008 (3) JCC 2099; JT 2008 (8) SC 128; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2081; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2776; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 630; 2008 (3) RLW 2683; 2008(10) SCALE 215; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Non-Explanation

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302 and 307- CrPc, 1973, ss. 366, 378 and 417 - Appeal against order of acquittal passed by High Court in reference u/s. 366 of CrPC, 1973 reversing the order of death sentence - Accused was charged for murders of three persons by burning in marriage palace and u/s. 307 of 1860 for causing injuries to other - Evidence of doctor that injuries were on account of dragging him on surface littered with broken glasses - Accusations established - High Court interfering with the well-reasoned and elaborate judgment of the trial Court - Validity of reversal of acquittal by HC when different views possible - Held, acquittal as directed by the High Court cannot be faulted - Even though it may be possible hypothetically to take a different view on the evidence, we are not inclined to interfere with the view of HC - Appeal dismissed.
