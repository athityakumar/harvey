Commissioner of Income Tax, Dehradun and Another v Enron Oil and Gas India Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1415; (2008) 15 SCC 33; [2008] 305 ITR 75; JT 2008 (10) SC 154; 2008(12) SCALE 225; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 1168; [2008] 173 TAXMAN 346

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Currency Fluctuation

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether the assessee was entitled to claim deduction for foreign exchange losses on account of foreign currency translation? - Held, an assessee is entitled to allowances which are mentioned in the PSC - In PSC, the foreign company provides the capital investment and cost and the first proportion of oil extracted is generally allocated to the company which uses oil sales to recoup its costs and capital investment - Oil used for that purpose is termed as "cost oil" - Often a company obtains profit not just from the "profit oil", but also from "cost oil" - Such profits cannot be ascertained without taking into account translation losses. Moreover, as stated above, taxes are embedded in the profit oil - If these concepts are kept in mind then it cannot be said that "translation losses" under the PSC are illusory losses - Appeal dismissed.
