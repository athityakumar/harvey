M/S. Deepak Agro Foods v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1056; (2008) 7 SCC 748; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 923; 2008 (228) E.L.T. 510; 2008(10) SCALE 263; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 877; 2009 (16) S.T.R. 518; [2008] 16 VST 454

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 - Division Bench set aside the assessment orders in respect of assessment years 1995-96 and 1996-97 and has remanded the cases for fresh assessments by a new Assessing Officer, to be nominated by the Commissioner of Commercial Taxes - Appeal against - Held, if an assessment order is set aside by an Appellate Authority, fresh assessment has to be completed within a period of two years from the date of communication of the order in appeal to the Assessing Authority and not from the date of order in appeal - All irregular or erroneous or even illegal orders cannot be held to be null and void as there is a fine distinction between the orders which are null and void and orders which are irregular, wrong or illegal - Appeals dismissed.
