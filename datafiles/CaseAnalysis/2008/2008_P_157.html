Pramod Kumar v Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Commission and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2617; (2008) 7 SCC 153; AIR 2008 SC 1817; JT 2008 (13) SC 635; 2008(4) SCALE 580; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 559; 2008 (4) SLR 692

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Board Act, 1982, Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Board (Amendment) Act, 2001, Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Commission Rules, 1993

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Boards Act, 1982, s. 16 - Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Commission Rules, 1993 - Appointment - Appellant, possessing a B.Ed. degree from Maithili Vishwa Vidyapeeth, Sankat Mochan Dham Darbhanga, Bihar, was appointed as an Assistant Teacher in C.T. Grade in an Intermediate College - When it was found by the Management that B.Ed. degree obtained by the appellant was not from an Institution recognized under the University Grants Commission (UGC) Act - Management asked him to obtain a B.Ed. degree from recognized university within a period of two years - Thereafter, appellant obtained a requisite degree - On change of Management, department proceedings were initiated against the appellant on the charge that he had obtained his appointment on the basis of fabricated B.Ed degree - Disciplinary Authority terminated his services - Appellant filed writ petition against said order which was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant was rightly terminated on the charge that he had obtained his appointment on the basis of fabricated B.Ed degree? - Held, if the essential educational qualification for recruitment to a post is not satisfied, ordinarily the same cannot be condoned - Such an act cannot be ratified - An appointment which is contrary to the statute/statutory rules would be void in law - An illegality cannot be regularized, particularly, when the statute in no unmistakable term says so - Management of the school, when it came to learn that the appellant did not possess a degree of B.Ed. from a recognized University, should have terminated his services forthwith - It did not do so for reasons best known to it - HC rightly upheld the termination order of the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
