Kumar Gonsusab & Ors. v Sri Mohammed Miyan Urf Baban & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1307; (2008) 10 SCC 153; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1254; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 72; 2008 (4) AWC 3634; JT 2008 (9) SC 334; 2009 (1) KarLJ 1; 2008 (4) KLT 265; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 73; 2009 (106) RD 660; 2008(11) SCALE 373; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 226; 2008 (3) UC 1618

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Family Customs

Summary: Constitution - Transfer of Property Act, s. 54 - Family and Personal - Right of pre-emption - Appeal - (A) Whether the suit for pre-emption on the ground of vicinage was maintainable in law in view of the admitted fact that only an agreement for sale of the suit property was entered into by the appellants - (B) Constitutionality of the right of pre-emption on the ground of vicinage - High Court hold the right, under the Mohammedan Law cannot be said to be unconstitutional and void in view of the Constitutional amendment - (A) Held, right of pre-emption is a weak right and is not looked upon with favour by courts and therefore the courts cannot go out of their way to help the pre-emptor; and it was not available to the respondents - (B) Supreme Court declined to go into this issue - Appeal Allowed.
