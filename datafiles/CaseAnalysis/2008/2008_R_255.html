Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi v Realest Builders and Services Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 879; (2008) 15 SCC 112; [2008] 307 ITR 202; [2008] 170 TAXMAN 218

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Income Tax Act, 1961, Appeals Dismissed, Accrual Of Income

Summary: Income Tax Act - Whether income accrued to the assessee on registration of the sale deed in favour of the third party (plot purchaser) or whether it accrued at the time of execution of the tripartite agreement? - Held, it is always open to the Department to insist on the change in the method of accounting followed by the assessee over the years (which is the case herein) if the impugned method of accounting results in under estimation of profits/net income - Since the Department has not gone into the vital aspect regarding method of accounting under s. 145 no reason to interfere with the impugned judgment - Appeals dismissed.
