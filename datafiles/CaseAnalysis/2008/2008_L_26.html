Lalita Kumari v Government of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2640; (2008) 14 SCC 337

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154 - Cognizable offence - Registration of case - Discretion of police - Whether upon receipt of information by an officer in-charge of police station disclosing cognizable offence, it is imperative for him/her to register case u/s. 154 of CrPC, or a discretion lies with him/her to make some sort of preliminary enquiry before registering same.

Held, counsel appearing on behalf of State of submitted that an officer in-charge of police station is not obliged under law, upon receipt of information disclosing cognizable offence, to register a case but a discretion lies with him/her in appropriate cases to hold some sort of preliminary enquiry in relation to the veracity or otherwise of the accusations made in report. In view of conflicting decisions of this Court, Court feel that it is necessary to refer matter to a larger Bench. Let this petition be placed before Chief Justice of India for passing appropriate orders to list case before a larger Bench. In view of interim order passed by this Court, Court feel that it would be expedient to hear this petition at an early date. Order accordingly.
