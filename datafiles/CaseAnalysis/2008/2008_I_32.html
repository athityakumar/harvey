C. C. T., Orissa and Others v Indian Explosives Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1370; (2008) 3 SCC 688; AIR 2008 SC 1631; 2008(3) SCALE 307; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 621; [2008] 14 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947 - Whether High Court ought not to have entertained writ petition more particularly when for assessment year 1997-98 and 1998-99 another Division Bench in Writ Petition Nos. O J C Nos. 16928 of 1998 and 1500 of 2000 had rejected stand of assessee? - Held, HC seems to have completely lost sight of parameters highlighted by this court in a large number of cases relating to exhaustion of alternative remedy - Additionally HC did not even refer to judgment of another Division Bench for assessment year 1997-98 and assessment year 1998-99 in respect of ICI India Ltd. - In any event HC ought to have referred to ratio of decision in said case - That judicial discipline has not been adhered to - Looked at from any angle, HC's judgment is indefensible and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
