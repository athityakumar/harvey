Moni Shankar v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 403; (2008) 3 SCC 484; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1657; 2008 (2) Bom.C.R. 774; 2008 (118) FLR 1092; JT 2008 (3) SC 484; 2008 (2) LLN 753; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 270; 2008(3) SCALE 455; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 871; 2008 (2) SLR 818; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 2000

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Vigilance Manual, Railway Servant Discipline and Appeal Rules

Summary: Railway Servant Discipline and Appeal Rules - Service - Penalty of reduction to the lowest scale of pay fixing his pay at the lowest level at Rs.3, 200/- for a period of five years was imposed on appellant - Central Administrative tribunal set aside the order - High Court held that Central Administrative Tribunal in its original order having entered into the realm of evidence and re-appreciated the same, exceeded its jurisdiction - Appeal against - Held, it is permissible in law to look to the evidence for the purpose of ascertaining as to whether the statutory requirement had been complied with or not - High Court even without any material on record held that some excess amount was found from the appellant which itself was sufficient to raise a presumption that it had been recovered from the decoy passenger - Appeal allowed.
