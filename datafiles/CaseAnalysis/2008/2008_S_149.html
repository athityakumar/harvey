Mg. Dir., Bangalore Metropolitan Tpt. Corporation v Sarojamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 710; (2008) 5 SCC 142; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 545; 2009 (2) ACC 850; 2008 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 2008 (71) ALR 495; 2008 (3) AWC 2175; 2008 (1) CCC 154; JT 2008 (6) SC 63; 2009 (1) KarLJ 346; 2008 (4) MPLJ 265; 2009 (1) RLW 294; 2008(7) SCALE 242; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 812; 2008 (2) UC 1262

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 163-A - Son of Respondent no. 1 was travelling in a bus belonging to the appellant met with an accident and he was aged about 18 years - Tribunal calculated the loss of dependency at Rs.3, 84, 000/-, wherefor the multiplier of 16 was applied - High Court while allowing the multiplier of 15 instead of 16 increased the rate of interest from 7% to 10% and respondent No. 1 was held to be entitled to a total sum of Rs. 3, 64, 500/- - Appeal against - Held, for determining the amount of compensation, the most relevant factor, is the income of the deceased - Why the multiplier of 16 had been applied by the Tribunal was not stated - High Court has also not laid down the legal premise upon which it had applied the multiplier of 15 - Rate of interest payable on the awarded amount is brought down to 7%, as was directed by the Tribunal - Appeal partly allowed.
