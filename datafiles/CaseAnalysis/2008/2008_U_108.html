Satyawati Sharma (Dead) By Lrs v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 616; (2008) 5 SCC 287; AIR 2008 SC 3148; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 147; 2008 (71) ALR 499; 2008 (148) DLT 705; JT 2008 (5) SC 376; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 805; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 457; 2008(6) SCALE 325; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 566

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1941, Delhi and Ajmer-Merwara Rent Control Act, Constitution (Seventy-Fifth Amendment) Act, 1994, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging Houses, Rates Control Act, 1947, Madras Hindu Religious Endowment Act, 1951, A.P. Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control, Act, 1960

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Whether s. 14(1)(e) is ultra vires the doctrine of equality enshrined in art. 14 of Constitution of India? - Held, s. 14(1)(e) of the 1958 Act is violative of the doctrine of equality embodied in art. 14 of the Constitution of India insofar as it discriminates between the premises let for residential and non-residential purposes when the same are required bona fide by the landlord for occupation for himself or for any member of his family dependent on him and restricts the latter's right to seek eviction of the tenant from the premises let for residential purposes only - Appeal allowed.
