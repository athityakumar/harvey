Vinay Kumar Rai And Anr v The State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2035; (2008) 12 SCC 202; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2973; 2008 CRLJ 4319; 2008 (4) JCC 2304; 2008(11) SCALE 353; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Gunshot

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 -- Murder -- Conviction - Sustainability - Accused caught hold of deceased and shot at right temple - Accused were brought before Trial Court for prosecution which passed sentence ??? - Aggrieved by same appeal was made before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of conviction was sustainable - Held, SC relied upon Dalip Singh's v. State of Punjab case 1953 Indlaw SC 16 in which it was said that witness being a close relative and consequently being a partisan witness, should not be relied upon had no substance - Further, about alleged discrepancy between ocular evidence and medical evidence as rightly noted by HC there was no discrepancy and medical evidence was clearly in line with what was stated by eye-witnesses - Furthermore, HC had noted that expression used by witnesses could not be analysed in hypothetical manner - Therefore, according to eye witnesses gunshot injury was caused on right temple but injury was found on upper eyelid and averted wound on right oricle margin - Thus, it could never be said that medical evidence was contrary to ocular evidence - Appeal dismissed.
