Elizabeth Jacob v District Collector, Idukki And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2166; (2008) 15 SCC 166; AIR 2009 SC 1140; 2008 (73) ALR 318; 2008 (4) BC 350; 2008 (2) DRTC 537; JT 2008 (10) SC 265; 2008 (3) KLT 957; 2008(12) SCALE 308; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 637

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Kerala Revenue Recovery Act, 1968, ss.36,49 54,56 and 58 - Possession of Land - Entitlement - Appellant was successful bidder in auction held on 5-6-1998, sale was confirmed in favour of her after entire sale consideration on 28-7-1998 u/s.54 of the Act and sale certificate was issued u/s.56 of the Act - Said property was mutated in revenue records on appellant's name and paid tax due - Appellant applied u/s.58 of the Act for delivery of Possession before respondent/Collector/Authorised Officer - There was no response and got evasive reply from Thesildar requesting appellant to contact forest authorities - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC which directed respondents to deliver possession of said land within 1 month to appellant - Respondents duly delivered possession of land to appellant under mahazar - After long time respondents filed appeal before DB in HC which set aside Single Judge's order and dismissed writ petition and directed appellant to approach Civil Court and establish her title and seek possession of property on strength of such title - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB's order was justifiable - Held, Single Judge ought to had directed respondent/ Collector to initiate action u/s. 58 of the Act , instead of straightaway directing possession - But there were special circumstances -Firstly, obstruction to possession was not by private party - Secondly, land was attached and sold by public auction, at instance of State Govt. - Thirdly, without any proof, State Govt. contended that it wanted to verify whether land was forest land, without explaining why then it was sold by public action -Hence, order of DB was set aside and restored Single Judge order and liberty, of course, was reserved to State Govt. to take action in accordance in law if it found that land was forest land - Appeal allowed.
