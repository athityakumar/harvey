State of Karnataka and Others v Sri Chamundeswari Sugar Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1111; (2008) 7 SCC 469; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 494; JT 2008 (6) SC 69; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 1549; 2008(7) SCALE 62; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 16; [2008] 14 VST 355

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Khandsari Sugar, Mysore Sales Tax Act, U. P. Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Bihar Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1981, M. P. Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether State advised price cannot be subjected to purchase tax on purchase of sugarcane? - There being compulsory purchase - Repugnancy between Central and State laws - Purchase price being not defined - Held, having regard to advantages derived by sugar factories, they are fully bound by agreement wherein State advised price may be mentioned and it is not open to them to assail clause relating to price of sugarcane on ground that their consent was not voluntary or was obtained under some kind of duress - Further held, inconsistency or repugnancy will arise if State Govt. fixed a price which is lower than that fixed by Central Govt. - Price fixed by State Govt. is higher than that fixed by Central Govt., there will be no occasion for any inconsistency or repugnancy as it is possible for both orders to operate simultaneously and to comply with both of them - Further held, it is accepted that SAP has been paid - Claim of respondent is that determination is tentative and certain adjustments can be made later - Appellants were justified in demanding purchase tax on amount paid as SAP and HC's view is clearly unsustainable and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
