Aqeel Ahmad v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1980; (2008) 16 SCC 372; AIR 2009 SC 1271; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 516; JT 2009 (1) SC 136; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 521; 2008(16) SCALE 251; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Non-Bailable Warrants, Non Examination

Summary: Murder - Appeal against upholding the conviction of appellant but altering the sentence of death as awarded by Additional District and Sessions Judge, to life sentence - Held, there was no evidence that the special report was sent to the concerned Magistrate with reference to s. 157 Cr.P.C - forwarding of the report is indispensable and absolute and it has to be forwarded with earliest dispatch which intention is implicit with the use of the word 'forthwith' occurring in s. 157 which means promptly and without any undue delay - Even if there has been lapse in the investigation as contended by learned counsel for the appellants that cannot affect the credibility of the witnesses - Number of deaths in a case would not be the determinative factor for awarding the death sentence - Even in the case of single victim death sentence can be awarded taking into consideration the circumstances of the case - It cannot be said that the High Court by altering the sentence from death to life has acted inappropriately - Appeals dismissed.
