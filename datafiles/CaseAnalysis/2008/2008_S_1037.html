Siriya @ Shri Lal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 832; (2008) 8 SCC 72; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 422; AIR 2008 SC 2314; 2008 (3) JCC 1617; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 144; 2008(7) SCALE 712; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mitigating Factor

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 376 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, evidence on records clinchingly nails the appellant as the offender - Undue sympathy to impose inadequate sentence would do more harm to the justice system to undermine the public confidence in the efficacy of law and society could not long endure under such serious threats - It is the duty of every court to award proper sentence having regard to the nature of the offence and the manner in which it was executed or committed etc. - Appeal dismissed.
