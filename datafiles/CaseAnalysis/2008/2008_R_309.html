State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Roshan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 55; (2008) 2 SCC 488; 2008 (2) ADJ 379; AIR 2008 SC 1190; 2008 (71) ALR 1; 2008 (5) AWC 4379; 2008 (105) CutLT 467; JT 2008 (1) SC 537; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 655; 2008 (104) RD 210; 2008(1) SCALE 487; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 787; 2008 (1) UC 204; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consolidation Act, U.P. Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1954, ULC Act

Summary: U.P. Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1954 - High Court held that as the reduction of area made during the consolidation operation is made for public purposes, the petitioner is entitled to the benefit of said reduction - Appeals against - Held, s. 151 CPC cannot be invoked when there is express provision even under which the relief can be claimed by the aggrieved party - Power can only be invoked to supplement the provisions of the Code and not to override or evade other express provisions - Long after the period provided for preferring an appeal under s. 12, application under s. 151 CPC was filed - Appeals allowed.
