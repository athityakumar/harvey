Karnataka State Industrial Investment and Development Corporation Limited v S. K. K. Kulkarni and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1971; (2009) 2 SCC 236; AIR 2009 SC 1713; 2009 (74) ALR 504; 2009 (3) AWC 2484; 2009 (1) DRTC 153; JT 2009 (1) SC 267; 2009 (3) KarLJ 211; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 737; 2009(1) SCALE 180; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 - Whether the High Court erred in law in holding, that the Bangalore court has no territorial jurisdiction to hear the matter in view of the provisions of s. 31(1)? - Held, once the SFC invokes s. 31(1), it has to proceed in accordance with the procedure prescribed in s. 32 - Under s. 31(1) an application to obtain quicker remedy has to be made to the District Judge within whose jurisdiction the industrial concern is located - This is the mandate of s. 31(1) - It is so mandated because wide powers are given to the District Judge under ss. 31 and 32 to attach, sell and recover outstanding dues of SFCs in the shortest possible time - High Court was right in coming to the conclusion that the VI Additional City Civil Judge, Bangalore, had no territorial jurisdiction to hear the matter - Appeal dismissed.
