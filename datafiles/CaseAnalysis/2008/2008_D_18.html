Machindranath Kernath Kasar v D.S. Mylarappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 840; (2008) 13 SCC 198; AIR 2008 SC 2545; 2008 (72) ALR 198; 2008 (3) AWC 2461; 2008 (1) CCC 193; 2008 (5) KarLJ 142; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 1155; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 790; 2008(7) SCALE 496; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Tortfeasors, Wrongdoers, Wrongful, Grant Of Compensation, Necessary Parties, Course Of Employment, In The Course Of Employment, Passengers, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Third Party Risks, Material Particular, Insurance Against Third Party Risk, Negligent, Joint Tortfeasor, Offending Vehicle, Joint Tortfeasors, Tort Feaser, Karnataka Motor Vehicle Rules, 1989

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether in the claims cases before the Motor Vehicles Accident Claims Tribunal, the driver of a vehicle who has been accused of negligence is a necessary party to the proceedings or whether the owner alone can be impleaded? - Held, employer and employee, the former being vicariously liable while the latter being primarily liable are joint tortfeasors and are therefore jointly and severally liable, however, by virtue of the fact that the cause of action is the same and that the same evidence would support an action against either, it follows that this evidence must necessarily include an examination of the driver who is primarily liable - To make a finding on negligence without involving the driver as at least a witness would vitiate the proceedings not only on the basis of the fact that the driver has not been given an opportunity to make a representation, but also because the evidence to make a finding regarding negligence would necessarily be inadequate therefore a driver should be made a 'party' to the proceedings - Without contrary evidence led by the appellant or Corporation, the finding of negligence on the part of the appellant cannot be interfered with; without a deposition on the part of the truck driver and without his involvement at least as a witness, an adverse finding on negligence cannot be made against him - Appellant alone was rash and negligent in driving of the vehicle; no case had been made out to differ with the said finding - Appeal dismissed.
