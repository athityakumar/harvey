Basic Education Board, U.P. v Upendra Rai And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 170; (2008) 3 SCC 432; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1321; JT 2008 (2) SC 479; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1084; 2008(2) SCALE 407; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 707; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 641

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Policy Decisions, Administrative Decision, U.P. Basic Education (Teachers) Service Rules, 1981, U.P. Basic Education Act, 1972

Summary: U.P. Basic Education (Teachers) Service Rules, 1981, r. 8; U.P. Basic Education Act, 1972 - Question about qualification of respondent for being appointed as Assistant Master in Junior Basic Schools in U.P. - Held, Court cannot interfere with policy decisions of the Government unless it is in violation of some statutory or constitutional provision - Respondent was not entitled to be appointed as Assistant Master of a Junior Basic School in U.P - Respondent does not possess qualification mentioned in the Circular - He does not either possess BTC, Hindustani Teaching Certificate, JCT or Certificate of Teaching - No violation of art. 14 by the impugned circular or the advertisement - Appeal dismissed.
