Babulal Khandelwal and Others v Balkrishan D. Sanghvi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1645; (2008) 10 SCC 485; AIR 2009 SC 67; 2009 (75) ALR 521; 2009 (1) AWC 93; JT 2008 (11) SC 303; 2008 (4) KLT 477; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 501; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 789; 2008(13) SCALE 216; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 525; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Administration Suit, Forms Of Decree

Summary: Administration suit - Impleadment of appellants parties to the suit - Held, in an administration suit, the Court, while considering the grant of authority to an individual having an interest in the Estate of the deceased to administer the Estate, has also to determine the extent of the estate of the deceased at the time of his death to facilitate the distribution of the estate to all the heirs of the deceased - Single Judge of the High Court has correctly indicated that in an administration suit, the dispute between the parties relating to the title of the deceased in respect of his properties, can be gone into in an administration suit and that there is no bar to a Court determining the validity of transactions allegedly entered into by or on behalf of the deceased, whose Estate is to be administered - Appeal dismissed.
