National Insurance Company Limited v Geeta Bhat and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 563; (2008) 12 SCC 426; 2008 (2) ACC 341; AIR 2008 SC 1837; 2008 (2) AWC 2039; JT 2008 (4) SC 425; 2008 (2) KLT 1001; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 316; 2008 (150) PLR 784; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 44; 2008 (3) RLW 1947; 2008(4) SCALE 757; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 734

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Third Party Risk, Owner Of Vehicle, f Ob, Driver Had No Licence

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Held, only because the license was fake, the same, having regard to the settled legal position, would not absolve the insurer to reimburse the owner of a vehicle in respect of the amount awarded in favour of a third party by the Tribunal in exercise of its jurisdiction under s. 166 - Appeal dismissed.
