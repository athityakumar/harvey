Kunju @ Balachandran v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 40; (2008) 2 SCC 151; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 331; AIR 2008 SC 1381; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1175; 2008 CRLJ 1804; 2008 (105) CutLT 513; JT 2008 (1) SC 450; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 732; 2008(1) SCALE 479; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, even though PW 1 did not support prosecution version in toto, yet his evidence lent corroboration to the evidence of PW2 - Evidence has to be weighed and not counted - Test is whether the evidence has a ring of truth, is cogent, credible and trustworthy, or otherwise - Appeal dismissed.
