Shamshad Begum v B. Mohammed

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1722; (2008) 13 SCC 77; AIR 2009 SC 1355; 2008 (6) Bom.C.R. 722; 2009 (2) CGLJ 261; 2009 CRLJ 1304; JT 2008 (11) SC 428; 2009 (5) KarLJ 75; 2009 (1) KLT 886; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 557; 2009 (153) PLR 642; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 359; 2008(13) SCALE 669; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 188; 2008 (3) UC 1713

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Appeal against the order dismissing the petition to quash the proceedings - Held, offence under s. 138 can be completed only with the concatenation of a number of acts - It is not necessary that the five acts should have been perpetrated at the same locality - It is possible that each of those five acts could be done at five different localities - But concatenation of all the five is sine qua non for the completion of the offence under s. 138 - Judgment of the High Court does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
