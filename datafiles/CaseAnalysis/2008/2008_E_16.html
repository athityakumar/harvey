Eicher Tractor Limited And Others v Harihar Singh And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1744; (2008) 16 SCC 763; 2009 (1) JCC 260; 2008(14) SCALE 1; 2008(14) SCALE 7; 2008(15) SCALE 60; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 7

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 420, 468, 471 - Petition to quash the proceedings - Held, in exercise of the powers, court would be justified to quash any proceeding if it finds that initiation/continuance of it amounts to abuse of the process of court or quashing of these proceedings would otherwise serve the ends of justice - When no offence is disclosed by the complaint, the court may examine the question of fact - When a complaint is sought to be quashed, it is permissible to look into the materials to assess what the complainant has alleged and whether any offence is made out even if the allegations are accepted in toto - Appeal allowed.
