Meera Sahni v Lt. Governor of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1166; (2008) 9 SCC 177; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 760; 2008 (5) AWC 5129; JT 2008 (8) SC 284; 2008(10) SCALE 414; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Defective Title, Delhi Land (Restrictions on Transfer) Act, 1972

Summary: Whether in view of the provisions of Delhi Lands (Restrictions and Transfer) Act, 1972, read with the provisions of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 transfer of land made by original owner by registering a sale deed on the basis of which mutation was also granted would and could be accepted as legal and valid transfer despite the fact that such land was acquired by the State Government under the provisions of the Land Acquisition Act for the public purpose - Held, under the Land Acquisition Act the subsequent purchaser cannot challenge the acquisition proceedings and that he would be only entitled to get the compensation - Permission for transfer of land by way of sale, mortgage, lease, gift or otherwise of the aforesaid nature could be so registered by the registering authority only when permission is granted by the competent authority in terms of the requirement of the statute - Permission to transfer or refusing to grant transfer any such land is to be given by the competent authority and such permission is to be obtained prior to the transaction entered into, which must be in writing - Neither any proper application was made either by the predecessors in-interest of the appellants or by the appellants themselves, as envisaged under ss. 4 and 5 of the Delhi Lands Act, nor any valid and legal permission was granted to the appellants by the competent authority - Appeals dismissed.
