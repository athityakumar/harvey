National Insurance Company Limited v Kaushalaya Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 876; (2008) 8 SCC 246; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 467; 2008 (4) ACC 796; 2009 (1) ACC 52; AIR 2008 SC 2252; 2008 (1) CCC 363; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 953; 2009 (1) MPLJ 288; 2008 (152) PLR 375; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 902; 2008(8) SCALE 500; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 500; 2008 (3) ShimLC 238

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Gratuitous Passenger, Use Of Vehicle, Third Party Risks, Prescribed Form, Liability Of Insurer

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs.1, 18, 000/- by way of compensation - High Court directed to release the amount in favour of the claimant with liberty to the insurance company to recover the said amount along with interest from the owner - Appeal against - Held, owner alone was liable to pay compensation to the first respondent herein for causing death of her son by rash and negligent driving on the part of the driver of the truck - Appeals disposed of.
