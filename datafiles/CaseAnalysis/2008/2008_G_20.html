G.M. Ongc, Shilchar v Ongc Contractual Workers Union

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 888; (2008) 12 SCC 275; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1994; 2008 (6) AWC 6157; 2008 (3) ESC 456; 2008 (118) FLR 942; JT 2008 (7) SC 309; 2008 (2) LLJ 1071; 2008 (3) LLN 490; 2008(8) SCALE 665; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1223

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Whether the demand of the ONGC 'Contractual Workers' Union on the management of ONGC for regularization of the services of the contractual workers was justified? - Held, argument that the workmen being on a contractual, were not entitled to any relief, cannot be accepted - Workmen were the employees of the ONGC they would ipso-facto be entitled to all benefits available in that capacity, and the issue of regularization would, therefore, pale into insignificance - Industrial Tribunal and the Division Bench of the High Court were justified in lifting the veil in order to determine as to the nature of employment - Appeal dismissed.
