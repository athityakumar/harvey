Sita Ram Gupta v Punjab National Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1011; (2008) 5 SCC 711; AIR 2008 SC 2416; 2008(4) ALL MR 905; 2008 (3) AWC 2281; 2008 (2) BC 691; [2008] 142 Comp Cas 946; 2012 (6) CompLJ 82; 2008 (3) KLT 518; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 457; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 679; 2008(4) SCALE 181; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 636; 2008 (1) UC 606

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Continuing Guarantee, Continuing

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 130 - Whether High Court was justified in holding that appellant, a guarantor of loan advanced to defendants was liable to pay decretal amount u/s. 130 of Contract Act, on ground that appellant had revoked guarantee before such loan was actually paid? - Appellant entered into a binding contract and he can contract himself out unless such agreement was contrary to public policy - Held, appellant cannot claim benefit u/s. 130 of Act because he has waived benefit by entering into agreement of guarantee with bank - Appellant cannot deny his liability on ground that guarantee was revoked before loan was advanced to defendants - Appeal dismissed.
