Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Rajni Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 680; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 67; (2008) 5 SCC 736; 2009 (1) ACC 297; 2008 (4) ALD(SC) 34; 2008 (72) ALR 187; 2008 (3) AWC 2471; 2008 (3) CTC 38; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 56; 2008 (4) KLT 145; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 626; 2008 (152) PLR 381; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 905; 2008 (3) RLW 2285; 2008(6) SCALE 638; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1985

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 163-A - Held, in a case where third party is involved, liability of insurance company would be unlimited - Where compensation is claimed for the death of the owner or another passenger of the vehicle, contract of insurance being governed by the contract qua contract, the claim of the insurance company would depend upon terms thereof - Tribunal was not correct in taking the view that while determining the amount of compensation, the only factor which would be relevant would be merely the use of the motor vehicle - Liability under s.163-A is on the owner of the vehicle as a person cannot be both, a claimant as also a receipient - For the said purpose only the terms of the contract of insurance could be taken recourse to - Liability of insurance company was confined to Rs.1, 00, 000/- - Appeal partly allowed.
