Cable Corporation of India Limited v Additional Commnr. of Labour and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1625; (2008) 7 SCC 680; AIR 2008 SC 2386; 2008(4) ALL MR 939; 2008 (6) AWC 6140; 2008 (3) ESC 452; 2008 (118) FLR 748; JT 2008 (7) SC 327; 2008 (2) LLJ 1057; 2008 (3) LLN 468; 2008(9) SCALE 305; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1182; 2008 (6) SLR 77; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Review and Reference - Scope of - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-N(6), 25-N(b) and 19(1)(g) - (A) Disposal of review application - Scope for further reference u/s. 25-N(6) - Held, Legislature did not intend the reference could be made only after the Govt. or the specified authority had reviewed the matter - If it was so, it would have said so specifically by specific words - Parameters of review are different from a reference - Two courses are open - Either Govt. can review or refer the matter to tribunal by its own motion or on an application made - Whether one or the other of the courses could be adopted depends on the fact of each case, the surrounding circumstances and several other relevant factors - U/s. 25-N(6), it is open to the appropriate Govt. or specified authority to review its order or made a reference - (B) Interpretation of statutes - Held, if the words of a statute are clear, plain or unambiguous and reasonably susceptible to only one meaning, Courts bound to give effect to that meaning irrespective of consequences - In such cases, no question of construction of statutes arises - It would open to the Courts to adopt any other hypothetical construction consistent with the alleged object and policy of the Act - The spirit of the law may well be an elusive and unsafe guide and the supposed spirit can certainly be not given effect to in opposition to the plain language of the sections of the Act - Appeal allowed.
