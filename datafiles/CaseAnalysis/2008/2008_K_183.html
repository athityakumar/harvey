Kunvarjeet Singh Khandpur v Kirandeep Kaur and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 600; (2008) 8 SCC 463; AIR 2008 SC 2058; 2008 (71) ALR 774; 2008 (2) AWC 2024; 2008 (2) CTC 850; 2008 (2) KLT 296; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 239; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 806; 2008 (105) RD 262; 2008(6) SCALE 112; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1057; 2008 (2) UC 865

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Additional District Judge held that the petition for grant of Letters of Administration of Will dated 9.9.1991 was not barred by limitation and was maintainable - Appeal against - Whether art. 137 of Limitation Act is applicable?; Even if it is applicable whether the petition was within time? - Held, art. 137 is clearly applicable to the petition for grant of Letters of Administration - As rightly observed by the High Court in such proceedings the application merely seeks recognition from the Court to perform a duty because of the nature of the proceedings it is a continuing right - Right to apply actually arose on 9.8.1999 when the proceedings were withdrawn - Since the petition was filed within three years, the same was within time - Appeal dismissed.
