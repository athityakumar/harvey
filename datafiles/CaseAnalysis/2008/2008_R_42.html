Ramesh Dass v Raghu Nath And Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 242; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 470; (2008) 4 SCC 588; AIR 2008 SC 1298; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1159; 2008 CRLJ 1945; 2008 (1) JCC 707; JT 2008 (2) SC 348; 2008 (3) KLT 250; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 791; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 885; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 141; 2008(2) SCALE 444; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Probation of Offenders Act, 1958; Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 326 r/w s. 149, 325 r/w 149, 324 r/w 149, 323 r/ 149 - Sentence and fine imposed - High Court modified judgment of courts below and directed release of the accused persons on probation and enhanced the amount of fine to Rs.15, 000/- each and directed 50% shall be payable to the injured - Appeal against - Held, intention to retain the provisions of s. 360 of the Code and the provisions of the Probation Act as applicable at the same time in a given area cannot be gathered from the provisions of s. 360 or any other provision of the Code - By virtue of s. 8(1) of General Clauses Act, where the provisions of the Act have been brought into force, the provisions of s. 360 of the Code are wholly inapplicable - Matter is remitted to the High Court to consider about the non-applicability of s. 360 of the Code both on the ground that the Probation Act has application and s. 326 carries life imprisonment - Appeal allowed.
