Nikhil Merchant v Central Bureau of Investigation and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1321; (2008) 9 SCC 677; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 858; AIR 2009 SC 428; 2008 (4) JCC 2311; JT 2008 (9) SC 192; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 239; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 102; 2009 (1) RLW 185; 2008(11) SCALE 379; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Civil Action

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471A; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2) and 5(1)(d); Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) - Whether the power which independently lies with this Court to quash the criminal proceedings pursuant to the compromise arrived at, should at all be exercised? - Held, technicality should not be allowed to stand in the way in the quashing of the criminal proceedings, since, continuance of the same after the compromise arrived at between the parties would be a futile exercise - Appeal allowed.
