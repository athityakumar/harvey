Mauri Yeast India Private Limited and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1110; (2008) 5 SCC 680; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1904; 2008 (225) E.L.T. 321; JT 2008 (13) SC 657; 2008(6) SCALE 241; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 131; [2008] 14 VST 259

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Interpretative Rules

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948 - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Interpretative Rules - Whether sale of 'yeast' can be classified under head 'chemical' or under 'unclassified item' in entires under Uttar Pradesh Act? - Kerala HC in A M Jose 2003 Indlaw KER 474 having held 'yeast not be a chemical' - Held, entry in question is of wide import and takes within its purview chemicals of all kinds - No distinction is made between an inorganic chemical and an organic chemical - 'Yeast' may answer both 'chemical' as also 'fungi' - Fungi no longer considered to be a plant - 'Yeast', admittedly, has a chemical composition and a chemical formula - Further held, when two views are possible, one which favours assessee should be adopted - A different construction to an entry cannot be resorted to only because rate of tax has been lowered - Onus would be on it to show as to why a different interpretation thereof should be resorted to particularly when no change in statutory provision has taken place - Further held, Circular by Central Govt. has considered yeast to be a chemical - Doctrine of 'executive construction' - If there is a conflict between two entries one leading to an opinion that it comes within purview of tariff entry and another residuary entry, former should be preferred - Hence, 'yeast' is a chemical within meaning of entry in question - Appeal allowed.
