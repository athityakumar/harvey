Bharpur Singh and Others v Shamsher Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1960; (2009) 3 SCC 687; AIR 2009 SC 1766; 2009 (1) AWC 873; 2008 (5) G.L.R. 4543; JT 2009 (1) SC 590; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 535; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 577; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 826; 2009 (107) RD 620; 2009(1) SCALE 481; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 517

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mental Capacity, Testamentary Disposition, Mortgagee In Possession

Summary: Will - Held, a will must be proved having regard to the provisions contained in clause (c) of s. 63 of the Indian Succession Act, 1925 and s. 68 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, in terms whereof the propounder of a will must prove its execution by examining one or more attesting witnesses - Where validity of the Will is challenged on the ground of fraud, coercion or undue influence, the burden of proof would be on the caveator - Subject to offer of reasonable explanation, existence thereof must be taken into consideration for the purpose of arriving at a finding as to whether the execution of the Will had duly been proved or not - Matter be directed to be considered afresh - Appeal allowed.
