Anil Kumar Shahi & Ors v Prof. Ram Sevak Yadav & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2185; (2008) 14 SCC 115; 2008 (5) AWC 5145; JT 2008 (8) SC 345; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 854; 2008(10) SCALE 696; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 211; 2008 (6) SLR 375

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U. P. Educational (General Educational Cadre) Service Rules, 1992

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Service - Contempt Petition - SC laid down the law relating to the interpretation of eligibility criteria for the posts of Principals in Mohammed Altaf & Ors. v. Public Service Commission & Anr. in Civil Appeal Nos. 961-962/1999 - Petitioner-candidates filed application before the SC to allow their appeals in terms of the said order - Respondents opposed the application, contending that petitioners had failed to qualify the written examination, however, petitioners denied the stand of respondents - SC directed the respondents to communicate to the petitioners the reasons for not selecting them - Respondents informed the petitioners that the petitioners were found ineligible, therefore, they were not called for interview and directions of the SC would applicable only to those candidates who had appeared in the interview - Petitioners, hence, filed the present contempt petition - Respondents contended that there were no vacancies available against which the petitioners could be appointed - Held, explanation given by the respondents are satisfactory - Details of year-wise vacancies position for the posts in question stated in the Chart submitted by the respondents showed that there was no deliberate circumvention from the part of respondents - No dubious method adopted by the respondents to avoid implementation of the judgments/orders of SC nor the facts and circumstances mentioned therein would establish that the respondents have willfully or deliberately disobeyed the judgments/orders of SC - Respondents passed an appropriate order which was communicated to the petitioners in terms of SC order - No merit in contempt petition - Petition disposed of.
