State of Himachal Pradesh v Paras Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 906; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 117; (2008) 3 SCC 655; AIR 2008 SC 930; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 555; 2008 CRLJ 1026; 2008 (105) CutLT 392; 2008 (1) JCC 274; JT 2008 (1) SC 184; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 891; 2008 (3) RLW 2595; 2008(1) SCALE 6; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 6

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 378(3) and 154 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 436, 447, 427, 147 and 506 - Whether refusal of High Court to grant leave to file appeal against acquittal without assigning any reason to do so valid? - Reasons introduce clarity in an order - Held, Trial Court was required to carefully appraise entire evidence and then come to a conclusion - If Trial Court was at lapse in this regard HC obliged to undertake such an exercise by entertaining appeal - Trial Court on facts of this case did not perform its duties, as was enjoined on it by law - HC ought to have in such circumstances granted leave and thereafter as a first Court of Appeal, re-appreciated entire evidence on record independently and returned its findings objectively as regards guilt or otherwise of accused - It failed to do so - Further held, HC not given any reasons for refusing to grant and seems to have been completely oblivious to fact that by such refusal, a close scrutiny of order of acquittal, by appellate forum, has been lost once and for all - On plainest consideration of justice, HC ought to have set forth its reasons, howsoever brief, in its order indicative of an application of its mind, all more when its order is amenable to further avenue of challenge - Impugned judgment of HC unsustainable and set aside - Grant leave to State to file appeal - Appeal allowed.
