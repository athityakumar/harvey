Wada Aran Asbestos Private Limited v Gujarat Water Supply and Sewerage Board

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2133; (2009) 2 SCC 432; AIR 2009 SC 1027; 2009 (75) ALR 677; 2009 (1) AWC 431; 2009 (2) G.L.R. 905; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 667; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 425; 2008(15) SCALE 592; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37 r. 3(6)(b), ss. 105, 34, 96, O.37 r.3(6)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993 - Summary Suit - Leave to defend - Sustainability - - Appellants supplied AC pressure pipes to respondents and sum of Rs.1,57,488/- became due - Appellant requested respondent to release said payment - Respondent having not released outstanding payment, appellant became entitled to interest at rate of 23% per annum - Appellant filed Summary suit and respondent filed application for grant of leave to defend suit - Conditional leave was granted, with condition that defendant might give security of 50% amount of suit claim - Amount was not deposited and, thus, said condition was not complied - Decree was passed in favour of appellant as prayed for in plaint and defendants were directed to pay sum of Rs.21,18,335.65 ps along with interest @ 23% per annum - On appeal, HC by its order directed cheque for amount of Rs.15,50,000/- deposited by respondents may be en-cashed by Trial Court but amount should not be permitted to be withdrawn by decree holder till further orders and decree granted in favour of appellant u/ O. 37 r. 3(6)(b) CPC was set aside - Matter was remitted to Trial Court with direction that amount already deposited by defendant under directions of Court should be deemed to be amount deposited under leave granted by that Court and respondent would be allowed proper opportunity to submit his written statement - Hence, instant appeal- Whether HC was justified in setting aside decree passed in favour of appellant underO. 37 r. 3(6)(b) CPC and remitting suit to Trial Court - Held, on basis that order imposing a conditional leave to defend suit was jurisdictional question and, thus, revision application would be maintainable as had been held by various HC's - But if right of appeal from decree was conceded to defendant, in Court's opinion, he could not be denied right to challenge order which was subject to revision in his memorandum of appeal filed from decree ultimately passed - Further, statutory right conferred on litigant could not ordinarily be taken away - Civil revision application might have been maintainable as against order dated granting conditional leave - Said remedy was also available where leave to defend a suit was refused - Leave to defend suit, as noticed hereinbefore, should ordinarily be granted - It was, therefore, permissible for respondent to raise said contention in appeal although it had asked for time to comply with conditions - Appeal dismissed.
