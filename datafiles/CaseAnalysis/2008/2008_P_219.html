Arjan Singh v Punit Ahluwalia and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 870; (2008) 8 SCC 348; AIR 2008 SC 2718; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 78; 2008 (4) AWC 3352; JT 2008 (6) SC 618; 2009 (1) MPLJ 495; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 294; 2008(9) SCALE 378; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 684

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Supplemental Proceeding

Summary: Suits for specific performance of contract - Consent decree was passed - Whether the first part or second part of O. 23 r. 3 of the Code of Civil Procedure would apply? - Held, Trial Judge, has rightly held that it was a case where the first part of O. 23 r. 3 would apply - As the appellant was not a party to the settlement, the same was not binding on him - Compromise was unlawful - If the order of injunction was operative upto a particular date, technically the order of injunction shall not remain operative - Appeal partly allowed.
