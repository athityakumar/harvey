Asraf Ali v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1079; (2008) 16 SCC 328; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 654; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2565; 2008 CRLJ 4338; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 156; JT 2008 (13) SC 598; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 835; 2008(10) SCALE 278; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1115

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 304 Part I - Appeal against judgment of Single Judge altered the conviction to s. 304 Part II and sentenced RI for 5 years - (A) Assault with a piece of wood - Deceased succumbed to head injuries - Some quarrel - Plea of absence of statement as to head injuries by accuse - Trial Court not accepted the plea of alibi - Questions put to the accused in the examination u/s. 313 of the Code did not focus on the evidence on record and the accused was therefore - Contention that circumstances which were relied upon by the trial Court to find the accused guilty were not specifically brought to the notice of the accused - (B) Object of s. 313 of Code - (C) Principles of natural Justice - Maxim audi alteram partem - Intend of s. 313 of the Code - In the light of word 'may' in 313(1)(a) of the Code - (A) Held, so examination u/s. 313 was rendered an empty formality - Impugned judgment of the High Court cannot be sustained and is set aside - Conviction set aside - (B) Object is to establish a direct dialogue between the Court and the accused personally to explain any circumstances appearing in the evidence against him - (C) Held, provision is not intended to nail him to any position, but to comply with the most salutary principle of natural justice enshrined in the maxim audi alteram partem - Word indicates that even if the court does not put any question under that clause the accused cannot raise any grievance for it - Appeal Allowed.
