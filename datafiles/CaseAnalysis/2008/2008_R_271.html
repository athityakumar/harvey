Rekha Patel v Pankaj Verma and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 320; (2008) 4 SCC 17; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 161; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1575; JT 2008 (3) SC 471; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 205; 2008(3) SCALE 497; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 323, 504 and 506; Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - High Court held that in case of petitioner Nos.1 to 5, if the learned Magistrate does not find fit case to release them on bail, they shall be released on personal bond of Rs.30, 000/- each and they shall remain on the same personal bonds till the final disposal of their bail application - Appeal against - Held, s. 438 Cr.P.C. has no application to the State of U.P - After surrender of accused and rejection of his bail application, the protection of the nature granted by the High Court cannot be given - Appeal disposed of.
