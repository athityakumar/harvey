Dinesh Singh v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1229; (2008) 15 SCC 745; 2008 (7) ADJ 151; AIR 2008 SC 3259; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 711; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 3250; JT 2008 (8) SC 684; 2008(11) SCALE 113; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 801

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self-Defence, Civil Case, Gun Shot Injury, Duty Of Court, Right Of Self-Defence, Co- Accused, Gunshot

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302, 325, 323 and 149 - Trial court acquitted appellants - High Court directed conviction for offence punishable under s. 304 Part II - Appeal against - Alleged exercise of right of private defence - Held, accused need not prove the existence of the right of private defence beyond reasonable doubt - It is enough for him to show as in a civil case that the preponderance of probabilities is in favour of his plea - A plea of right of private defence cannot be based on surmises and speculation - While considering whether the right of private defence is available to an accused, it is not relevant whether he may have a chance to inflict severe and mortal injury on the aggressor - A right to defend does not include a right to launch an offensive, particularly when the need to defend no longer survived - No infirmity in the conclusions arrived at by the High Court to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
