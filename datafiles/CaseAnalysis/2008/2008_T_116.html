Shamshad Ahmad and Others v Tilak Raj Bajaj (Deceased), Through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1469; (2008) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 526; 2008 (73) ALR 141; 2008 (152) DLT 301; JT 2008 (10) SC 56; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 801; 2008 (2) RCR(Rent) 346; 2009 (1) RLW 172; 2008(12) SCALE 532; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 352; 2009 (1) UC 523

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Regulation Of Letting, U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Application of the landlords for eviction of respondent-tenant came to be dismissed - Appeal against - Held, authority can undoubtedly decide whether the need or requirement of landlords was or was not bona fide - It can record a finding against the landlords if such requirement is not proved - Authority cannot decline the prayer of the landlords on the ground that they belonged to upper class society having facilities of car, etc - Appellate authority was right in observing that there was no evidence to show that no shop was available to the tenant - It is quite possible, that the tenant might have to pay more rent - But that would not preclude the landlords from getting possession of the suit- shop once they had proved genuine need of the property - High Court was not right in interfering with the order passed by the appellate authority and in dismissing the application of the landlords - Appeal allowed.
