Bank of Baroda and Others v Ganpat Singh Deora

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1981; (2009) 3 SCC 217; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 622; AIR 2009 SC 1745; 2009 (1) ESC 6; 2009 (4) G.L.R. 3540; 2009 (2) LLJ 603; 2009(1) SCALE 168; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1151; 2009 (3) SLR 15; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 52

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Bank of Baroda (Employees Pension) Regulations, 1995

Summary: Bank of Baroda (Employees Pension) Regulations, 1995 - Bank of Baroda Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme-2001 - Whether reg. 29 of the Pension Regulations, 1995, could have been applied in the case of the respondent or whether reg. 14 has been rightly applied both by the Tribunal and the High Court - Held, conditions relating to completing 15 years of service for being eligible to apply for BOBEVRS-2001 are special to the Scheme as also to the case of those employees who wished to apply for voluntary retirement under the aforesaid Scheme, if they had completed or would be completing 40 years of age - Not having completed the required length of qualifying service as provided under reg. 28 of the 1995 Regulations, the respondent was not eligible for pension under the Pension Regulations, 1995, of the appellant Bank - Appeal allowed.
