Mon v Nasimuddin

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1376; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 29; (2008) 6 SCC 721; AIR 2008 SC 2576; 2008 CRLJ 3491; 2008 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 170; 2008 (2) KLT 1022; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 460; 2008(9) SCALE 334; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1159

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973 s. 37 - Respondent prosecuted u/ss. 20, 29 and 60 of NDPS Act, 1985 - Respondent released on bail by Learned Special Judge which was confirmed by High Court - Whether HC right in confirming bail of respondent? - S. 37 of Code imposes limitations on granting of bail in addition to those provided under Code - Opportunity to be given to Public Prosecutor to oppose bail - Court should be satisfied that there is reasonable grounds for believing that accused not guilty of offence and accused not likely to commit any offence while on bail - Conditions are cumulative and not alternative - 'Reasonable grounds' means something more than prima facie grounds - Held, HC seems to have completely overlooked underlying object of s. 37 - Order granting of bail is unsustainable and set aside - Appeal allowed.
