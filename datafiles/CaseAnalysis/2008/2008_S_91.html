Samadhan Dhudaka Koli v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2025; (2008) 16 SCC 705; AIR 2009 SC 1059; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 229; 2009 (1) JCC 399; JT 2009 (1) SC 122; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 704; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 897; 2008(16) SCALE 66; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Oral Declaration, Mother- In-Law

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, a dying declaration made before a Judicial Magistrate has a higher evidentiary value - State cannot suppress any vital document from the court only because the same would support the case of the accused - Sessions Judge as also the High Court committed a serious illegality in refusing to consider the said question in its proper perspective - Court while appreciating evidence on the basis of such dying declarations is required to take into consideration inconsistencies between two statements - Consistency in the dying declaration, therefore, is a very relevant factor. Such a relevant factor cannot be ignored - When a contradictory and inconsistent stand is taken by the deceased herself in different dying declarations, they should not be accepted on their face value - Appeal allowed.
