Dinesh Kuma v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2034; (2008) 8 SCC 270; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 472; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2900; 2008 CRLJ 4311; JT 2008 (9) SC 148; 2008(11) SCALE 108; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross Case, Self Inflicted

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 300, 302, 307, 323, 324, 452 - Murder - Conviction - Maintainability - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 313, 315 - Accused armed with deadly weapons entered into informant house and inflicted injuries on deceased and his family members - Report was lodged against accused who were presented before Trial Court and were convicted and sentenced - Appeal was preferred before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction orders passed by lower Court was maintainable - Held, law was fairly well settled that even if acquittal was recorded in respect of co-accused on ground that there were exaggerations and embellishments, yet conviction could be recorded if evidence was found cogent, credible and truthful in respect of another accused - Further, it was revealed that appellant had inflicted first sword blow to deceased in his abdomen and he fell on ground - However, HC found that role ascribed to others was not fully satisfied - Thus, sword used in offence was recovered at instance of appellant and same was found to be stained with same group of human blood, as that of deceased, as per FSL report, Exh.P-28. PW7 stated that when he tried to save his father, deceased also inflicted blows on him and he sustained injury by sharp edged weapon - Appeal dismissed.
