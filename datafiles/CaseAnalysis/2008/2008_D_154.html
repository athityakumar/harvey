Klm Royal Dutch Airlines v Director-General of Investigation and Registration

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2065; (2009) 1 SCC 230; AIR 2009 SC 938; 2008 (4) AWC 4028; [2008] 146 Comp Cas 421; 2008 (4) CompLJ 478; 2008 (4) CPJ(Sc) 18; JT 2008 (11) SC 317; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 1077; 2008(13) SCALE 514; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 245

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP

Summary: MRTP - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 2(r), 2(u), 36A, 36A(1)((iv), 36A(1)(ii), 36A(1)(vi), 36B, 48C, 55 - Carriage By Air Act, 1972 - Complaint - Restrictive trade practices - Maintainability - Complainant filed complaint against appellant before respondent stating it had lost goodwill due to delay in delivery of goods to buyer - Notice was issued to appellant - Appellant furnished reply to respondent/commission where it came to conclusion that appellant failed to render services which were expected from it and trade practice carried on by appellant constitutes an unfair trade practice u/ss. 36A (1)(ii), 36A(1)(iv), 36A(1)(vii) of the 1969 Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by Commission was maintainable - Held, in findings recorded by Commission there was not even single finding that there was any such false representation or misstatement by appellant regarding their services - Further, the 1969 Act, also contains provisions regarding penalties relating to unfair trade practices s. 48C of the 1969 Act provides that if any person contravenes any order made by Commission u/s. 36D of the 1969 Act, he should be punishable with imprisonment for a term which should not be less than 6 months but which might extend to 3 years and with fine which might extend to ten lakh rupees - Aforesaid provision was a stringent one which provides punishment upto 3 years and also fine upto ten lakh rupees - Therefore, Commission was required to examine allegations with due care and caution and also essential ingredients as contained in provisions of s. 36A of the 1969 Act and while passing order u/s. 36 D of the 1969 Act it should be satisfied that there was in fact contravention of any of clauses of s. 36A of the 1969 Act - Appeals allowed.
