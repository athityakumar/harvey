State of Maharashtra v Bharat Shanti Lal Shah and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1418; (2008) 13 SCC 5; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1135; JT 2008 (10) SC 77; 2008(12) SCALE 167; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 1083

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Competence Of The Legislature, Air Force, New Offence, Distribution Of Legislative Powers, Doctrine Of Pith And Substance, Constitutionality, Legislative Lists, British North America Act, 1867, Tape- Record, Duty Of Police, Central Act, 1885

Summary: Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act, 1999 - Constitutional validity of ss. 13 to 16 on the ground that the State Legislature did not have the legislative competence to enact such a law and also that the aforesaid law is unreasonable and is violative of the provisions of art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, it is necessary for the courts to go into and examine the true character of the enactment, its object, its scope and effect to find out whether the enactment in question is genuinely referable to the field of legislation allotted to the respective Legislature under the constitutional scheme - Object of the MCOCA is to prevent the organised crime and a perusal of the provisions of Act under challenge would indicate that the said law authorizes the interception of wire, electronic or oral communication only if it is intended to prevent the commission of an organised crime or if it is intended to collect the evidence to the commission of such an organized crime - A person who is on bail after being arrested for violation of law unconnected with MCOCA, should not be denied his right to seek bail if he is arrested under the MCOCA, for it cannot be said that he is a habitual offender - Upheld the constitutional validity - Appeals allowed.
