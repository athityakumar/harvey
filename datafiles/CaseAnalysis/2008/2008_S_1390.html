Sunitadevi Singhania Hospital Trust And Anr. v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2123; (2008) 16 SCC 365; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 215; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 295; JT 2008 (13) SC 360; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 121; 2008(15) SCALE 464; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Redemption Fine And Penalty

Summary: Customs - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, s. 129B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Investigation - Compliance of notification - Penalty - Application for rectification - Bar of limitation - Use of power - Appellant No.1/Trust imported certain equipments invoking Notification issued by Govt. in terms whereof exemption from payment of custom duty was granted in respect thereof subject to certain obligation - However, investigation was carried out regarding fulfillment of conditions matter went before Customs Excise and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal in which Tribunal held that appellants had not complied with condition of notification and imposed fine and penalty imposed - Appellant filed application for rectification of mistake before Tribunal in regard to quantum of redemption fine and penalty and that was allowed - Appellant made another application in which Tribunal dismissed application holding that same was barred by limitation on premise - Petition was filed before HC which resulted into dismissal on ground that it was not fit case to exercise Court's extraordinary jurisdiction - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC had rightly dismissed appellant's petition - Held, Tribunal did not considered matter on merit and failed to take into consideration that, ipso-facto, in case of instant nature provisions of s. 129B of the Act as such had no effect - However, label of application was not decisive for consideration by Tribunal as to whether case had been made out to hear application on merit - Thus, in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution impugned judgment of HC was set aside with direction to Tribunal to hear out appellants afresh on merit on said application - Appeal allowed.
