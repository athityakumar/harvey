State of Punjab and Another v Jalour Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 108; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 524; (2008) 2 SCC 660; 2010 (2) ACC 279; AIR 2008 SC 1209; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 145; JT 2008 (2) SC 83; 2008 (2) KLT 236; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1755; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2113; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 311; 2008 (150) PLR 261; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 857; 2008(2) SCALE 52; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 922

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act; Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 - Lok Adalat increased compensation to an extent it considered just and reasonable, by a reasoned order which is adjudicatory in nature - Held, no Lok Adalat has the power to "hear" parties to adjudicate cases as a court does - It discusses the subject matter with the parties and persuades them to arrive at a just settlement - Lok Adalat exercised a power/jurisdiction not vested in it - Appeal allowed.
