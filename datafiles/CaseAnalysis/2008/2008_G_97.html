Golla Yelugu Govindu v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 508; (2008) 16 SCC 769; AIR 2008 SC 1842; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1325; 2008 CRLJ 2607; 2008 (2) JCC 1123; JT 2008 (4) SC 397; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 183; 2008(4) SCALE 569; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Old Age, Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction - Held, though it is an established principle that child witnesses are dangerous witnesses as they are pliable and liable to be influenced easily, shaped and moulded, but it is also an accepted norm that if after careful scrutiny of their evidence the Court comes to the conclusion that there is an impress of truth in it, there is no obstacle in the way of accepting the evidence of a child witness - Appropriate conviction would be under s. 304 Part I - Appeal partly allowed.
