Dinesh Seth v State Of N.C.T. Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2671; (2008) 14 SCC 94; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 400; 2008 (56) BLJR 2791; 2008 CRLJ 4345; 2008 (153) DLT 535; 2008 (4) JCC 2317; JT 2008 (9) SC 67; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 81; 2008(11) SCALE 470; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 221,222 and 464 - Dowry death - Cruelty - Appellant's wife died an unnatural death in her matrimonial home within 2 years of her marriage - There were allegations of harassment and torture against the appellant and his family members - Trial court convicted all the accused u/s. 304B r/w. s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to 7 years rigorous imprisonment - On appeal, HC held that charge u/s. 304B of IPC is not made out and acquitted all the accused - However, the appellant was found guilty u/s. 498A of IPC and sentenced to 3 years rigorous imprisonment - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that his conviction u/s. 498A of IPC is liable to be set aside because he was tried for an offence u/s. 304B r/w. 34 of IPC and not u/s. 498A of IPC - Whether HC committed an illegality by convicting the appellant u/s. 498A of IPC? - Held, SC in State of West Bengal vs. Orilal Jaiswal and Anr. 1993 INDLAW SC 192, held that an offence u/s. 498A of IPC is made out if the woman is subjected to physical assault, humiliation, harassment and mental torture - Even though the prosecution evidence was not sufficient to establish charge u/ss. 304 or 306 of IPC, conviction u/s. 498A of IPC can be upheld because the deceased was treated with cruelty by the appellant - In the present case, the specific allegation levelled against the appellant before the trial court was that they had subjected the deceased to cruelty for or in connection with demand for dowry and she had died an unnatural death within 7 years of her marriage - Thus, the appellant was not only aware of the charge of cruelty but he got and availed the opportunity to defend himself with reference to that charge - Therefore, it cannot be said that omission of the trial court to frame specific charge u/s. 498A of IPC had prejudiced the cause of the appellant or that failure of justice had been occasioned on that count - Hence, HC has not committed any illegality by convicting the appellant u/s. 498A of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
