K. Raghunandan and Others v Ali Hussain Sabir and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 871; (2008) 13 SCC 102; AIR 2008 SC 2337; 2008 (3) AWC 3117; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 766; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 699; 2008(9) SCALE 215; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Registration Act, 1877, Indian Registration Act, 1871, Indian Registration Act, 1866

Summary: Registration Act, 1908 - Application of s. 17(2)(vi) - What would be the effect of admitted case of the parties that the suit passage was not the subject matter thereof? - Held, if a right is created by a compromise decree or is extinguished, it must compulsorily be registered. Clause (vi) is an exception to the exception - If the latter part of Clause (vi) of Sub-section (2) of s. 17 applies, the first part thereof shall not appl - As in this case not only there exists a dispute with regard to the title of the parties over the passage and the passage, itself, having not found the part of the compromise, do not find any infirmity in the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
