Union of India and Another v Pushpa Rani and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2607; (2008) 9 SCC 242; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 851; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1337; 2008 (6) AWC 6543; JT 2008 (8) SC 474; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 64; 2008(10) SCALE 567; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 440; 2008 (7) SLR 362

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Service

Keywords: Indian Railway Medical Services (CMO, Addl. CMO, MS and Div./Sr. MO) Recruitment Rules, 1978, Railway Board's Secretariat Clerical Service (Re-organisation) Scheme, Central Engineering Service, Class I, Recruitment Rules, 1954

Summary: (A) Service - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1940 - Restructuring of cadre - Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe (SC/ST) reservation policy - Applicability of - Appellant/Railway Board implemented the policy of reservation of posts for SC/ST at the stage of restructuring of Group C and D cadres pursuant to letter No.PC-III/2003/CRC/6 dated 9-10-2003 - Respondents-Group C and D employees, filed application before the Tribunal to quash para 14 of the letter which provides reservation to SC/ST - Tribunal quashed para 14 of the letter - Appellants challenged the Tribunal order before HC - HC upheld the Tribunal order - Whether the policy reservation of SC/ST can be applied at the stage of restructuring of Group C and D cadres in the railways and whether para 14 of letter dated 9-10-2003 is violative of doctrine of equality enshrined in the Constitution - Held, restructuring exercise envisaged in letter dated 9-10-2003 resulted in creation of additional posts in some cadres with duties and responsibilities of greater importance and which could be filled by promotion from amongst the persons fulfilling the conditions of eligibility and satisfying the criteria of suitability and/or merit. - Once it is recognized that the additional posts becoming available as a result of restructuring of different cadres are required to be filled by promotion from amongst the employees who satisfy the conditions of eligibility and are adjudged suitable, there can be no rational justification to exclude the applicability of the policy of reservation while effecting promotions, more so because it has not been shown that the procedure for making appointment by promotion against such additional posts is different than the one prescribed for normal promotion. - Tribunal and HC were not justified in treating the present case as a case of up-gradation of posts simplicitor and consequent decision of the Tribunal to quash para 14 of letter dated 9-10-2003 and direction given for making appointments de hors the policy of reservation are legally unsustainable - Therefore, the Railway Board did not commit any illegality by directing that the existing instructions with regard to the policy of reservation of posts for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes would apply at the stage of restructuring the cadres and the Tribunal committed serious illegality by striking down para 14 of letter dated 9-10-2003 - Appeal allowed.

(B) Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1940, r. 157 - Legislation regulating Rules framed u/art. 306 of the Constitution - Effect of - Held, Indian Railway Establishment Code has been issued, by the President of India, in the exercise of his powers, under the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution - President has directed the Railway Board u/r. 157, to make rules, of general application to non-gazetted railway servants, under their control - Thus, rules framed by the Board are within the powers, conferred u/r. 157 and, in the absence of any Act, having been passed by the 'appropriate' Legislature, on the said matter, the rules, framed by the Railway Board, will have full effect and, if so indicated, retrospectively also - Appeals allowed.

(C) Service - Railway Establishment Manual (1985th edition) - Circular R.B.E. No.113/97 - Cadre - Meaning of - Held, term 'cadre' generally denotes the strength of a service or a part of a service sanctioned as a separate unit, however, for the purpose of roster, a wider meaning has been given to the said term so as to take within its fold the posts sanctioned in different grades - Reason for giving enlarged meaning to the term 'cadre' is that posts in the railway establishment are sanctioned with reference to grades and hence even temporary, work charged, supernumerary and shadow posts created in different grades can constitute part of the cadre - Appeals allowed.

(D) Service - Up-gradation of post - Scope of - Held, up-gradation of a post involves the transfer of a post from the lower to the higher grade and placement of the incumbent of that post in the higher grade and ordinarily, such placement does not involve selection but in some of the service rules and/or policy framed by the employer for up-gradation of posts, provision has been made for denial of higher grade to an employee whose service record may contain adverse entries or who may have suffered punishment - Appeals allowed.

(E) Service - Promotion - Meaning of - Held, word 'promotion' means 'advancement or preferment in honour, dignity, rank, or grade' - 'Promotion' thus not only covers advancement to higher position or rank but also implies advancement to a higher grade - In service jurisprudence the expression 'promotion' has been understood in the wider sense and it can be either to a higher pay scale or to a higher post' - Appeals allowed.

(F) Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 - Methodology of Recruitment - Decision of employer - Judicial review - Scope of - Held, power of judicial review can be exercised in such matters only if it is shown that the action of the employer is contrary to any constitutional or statutory provision or is patently arbitrary or is vitiated due to mala fides - Court cannot sit in appeal over the judgment of the employer and ordain that a particular post be filled by direct recruitment or promotion or by transfer - Court has no role in determining the methodology of recruitment or laying down the criteria of selection - It is also not open the Court to make comparative evaluation of the merit of the candidates - Court cannot suggest the manner in which the employer should structure or restructure the cadres for the purpose of improving efficiency of administration - Appeals allowed.
