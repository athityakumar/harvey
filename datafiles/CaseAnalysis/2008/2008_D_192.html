Eastern Coalfields Ltd. v Dugai Kumar

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2417; (2008) 14 SCC 295; AIR 2008 SC 3000; 2008 (5) AWC 5161; 2008 (4) CalLT(SC) 70; JT 2008 (8) SC 276; 2008(10) SCALE 449; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 369; 2008 (3) UC 1382

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appellant a 'Government Company' purchased land of the respondent - Respondent was paid consideration for land - Respondent was offered 1008 MTs of coal as per the policy in force and quantity was also released and same was accepted by the respondent - Petition was filed after a decade by respondent contending that he was entitled to additional quantity of 1008 MTs of coal - Single Judge directed company to allot 'balance quantity of 1008 MTs' of coal to respondent - (A) Delay and laches - Held, where the petitioner invokes extra-ordinary remedy u/art. 226 of the Constitution, that he should come to the Court at the earliest reasonably possible opportunity - Inordinate delay in making the motion for a writ is indeed an adequate ground for refusing to exercise discretion in favour of the applicant - In the present case, Single Judge should not have entertained a writ petition in 1999 and in directing the Company to release balance quantity of 1008 MTs of coal to the writ petitioner - (B) Whether order passed by Single Judge justified? - Held, respondent was entitled to the price for the land sold by him to the appellant which was already been paid to him - He was also entitled to 1008 MTs of coal which was given to him as per the Policy - He was further entitled to 1008 MTs which was ordered to be given to him towards 'balance quantity' on the basis of statement made by the Counsel for the appellant and in terms of 'usual order' passed by the Single Judge - Respondent not entitled to anything more - Appeal partly allowed.
