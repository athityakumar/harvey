H.U.D.A v Raj Singh Rana

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2753; 2008 (3) AWC 3020; 2008 (3) CPJ(Sc) 71; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 49; JT 2008 (8) SC 403; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 182; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 193; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 129; 2008(10) SCALE 458; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1034

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1997, Punjab Urban Estate (Development and Regulation) Act, 1964

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986- Sections 2(1)(g), 12 and 14- Interest Act, 1978-Section 3- Deficiency in service - Housing allotment of plot-Interest on additional price resulting from acquisition cost-Even though rate of interest indicated in allotment letter-May not apply to payment of additional price-District Forum erred in allowing interest at rate of 7%-Imposition of interest for default in payment-Has to be in keeping with Section 3 of Interest Act-And not in unreasonable manner- Impugned orders of Consumer Fora set aside - Additional demand quashed - Appellant entitled to impose simple interest at prevailing current rate of interest- Compensation to be made by appellant within one month.Order dated. 19.11.2004 of National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, New Delhi in R. P. No. 2217 of 2004, reversed.
