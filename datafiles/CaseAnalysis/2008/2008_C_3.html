(1) Severn Trent Water Purification, Inc.; (2) v (1) Chloro Controls (India) Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 263; (2008) 4 SCC 380; AIR 2009 SC 1290; 2008 (4) Bom.C.R. 439; [2008] 142 Comp Cas 81; 2010 (2) CompLJ 31; JT 2008 (2) SC 618; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1140; 2008(3) SCALE 24; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 1127

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Instrument Of Transfer, Appeals Disposed Of, Debenture Stock, Companies Act, 1948, Insolvency Act, Companies Act, 1867

Summary: Companies Act, 1956 - petition for winding up of the Company - Whether a winding up petition filed by Severn Trent is maintainable in the capacity as a contributory?; Whether a winding up petition filed by Severn Trent is maintainable in the capacity as a creditor?; Whether a winding up petition filed by Severn Trent is liable to be dismissed at the threshold on the ground of premature advertisement by Severn Trent without an order of the Court as required by law? - held, if a person intends to present a petition for winding up of a company as a contributory, he/it has to satisfy the Company Court that his/its case is covered by one of the eventualities contemplated by s. 439(4)(b) - If there is omission, default or illegal action on the part of the Company in not registering the name of the contributory even though he/it can be said to be a contributory by holding the shares as required by s. 439(4)(b), the law provides a remedy - No such course has been adopted by Severn Trent - Appeals disposed of.
