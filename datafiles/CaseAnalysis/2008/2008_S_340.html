Union Of India & Ors. v Sicom Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1932; (2009) 2 SCC 121; 2009 (1) AWC 847; [2009] 147 Comp Cas 531; 2009 (2) CompLJ 23; 2009 (1) DRTC 297; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 433; JT 2009 (1) SC 87; 2009(1) SCALE 10; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 120; 2010 (18) S.T.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Cash Credit, Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954

Summary: Whether realization of the duty under the Central Excise Act will have priority over the secured debts in terms of the State Financial Corporation Act, 1951? - Held, right to recover must start with the sale of excisable goods - It is only when the dues of the Central Excise Department are not satisfied by sale of such excisable goods, proceedings may be initiated to recover the dues as land revenue - High Court proceeded on a wrong premise that only in terms of s. 29(4), proceeds of the sale will be held in trust by the Financial Corporation and appropriated towards the discharge of the debt due to it after first applying the proceeds in payment of cost charges and expenses incurred and the balance to be paid to the person entitled and having regard to the doctrine of Crown debt, the auction purchaser must satisfy it - Appeals allowed.
