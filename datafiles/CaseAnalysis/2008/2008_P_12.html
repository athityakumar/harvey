C. Cheriathan v P. Narayanan Embranthiri and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2017; (2009) 2 SCC 673; AIR 2009 SC 1502; 2009 (2) AWC 1283; JT 2008 (13) SC 542; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 720; 2009 (3) MPLJ 568; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 708; 2009(1) SCALE 430; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1239

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Mortgage By Conditional Sale, Mortgagee By Conditional Sale, Sale With a Condition To Repurchase

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Section 58 (c)-Document-Whether it is mortgage by conditional sale or sale with condition of repurchase?-Is vexed question-Condition of repurchase should be embodied in document effecting or purporting to effect sale-Said condition satisfied in instant case-Time for repurchase, namely, three years specified-No evidence to show that any relationship of creditor and borrower came into being-Appellant was already owner in respect of half of property-As parties related to each other-Difficult to conceive that other half of property would be subject to mortgage and not sale-Intention of appellant that by reason of transaction in question-He would become owner of entire property-Was obvious-Impugned judgment unsustainable-And set aside.
