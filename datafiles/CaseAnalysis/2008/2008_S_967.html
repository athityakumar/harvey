Rangaiah v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1956; (2008) 16 SCC 737; AIR 2009 SC 1411; 2009 (3) KarLJ 529; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 789; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 923; 2008(16) SCALE 1; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Old Age, Eye- Witness

Summary: Murder - Trial Court acquitted the accused - High Court reversed acquittal and recorded conviction - Appeal against - Held, a judgment of acquittal passed should not be interfered with when two possible views are possible - No explanation has also been offered as to why the dying declaration could not be recorded by a judicial officer - Prosecution version is totally different from the dying declaration - High Court did not apply the right test for reversing a judgment of acquittal - Appeal allowed.
