Mathania Fabrics v Commissioner of Central Excise, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 6; (2008) 4 SCC 461; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1336; 2008 (221) E.L.T. 481; JT 2008 (1) SC 637; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 6; 2008(1) SCALE 135; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - Benefit of exemption granted to 'cotton fabrics processed without the aid of power' - CESTAT held that appellant's were not entitled to benefit of Notification No.28/94-CE dated 1.3.1994 the Notification No.8/96-CE dated 23.7.1996 and its successor Notifications relying on a larger Bench's decision in the case of M/s Mathania Fabrics v. Commissioner of Central Excise, Jaipur [2002 (142) ELT 49 LB] - Appeal against - Held, stand that there was no use of power is unsustainable - Coming to the period of limitation five years period has to be reckoned backward from 8.2.1989 when the show cause notice was issued - Commissioner shall work out the liability and the penalty amount has to be equivalent to the amount of tax demand - Appeal partly allowed.
