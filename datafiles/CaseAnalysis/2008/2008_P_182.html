State of Himachal Pradesh v Prem Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2139; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 351; (2009) 1 SCC 420; AIR 2009 SC 1010; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1855; 2009 (2) CGLJ 51; 2009 CRLJ 786; 2008(15) SCALE 132; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 1058; 2010 (2) ShimLC 55

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 376 and 506 - Delay in lodging the FIR - Acquittal - Challenged -.Respondent convicted u/ss. 376, 354 and 506 IPC - In appeal, HC set aside the judgment of conviction and directed acquittal of the respondent - Present appeal against acquittal - Held, delay in a case of sexual assault, cannot be equated with the case involving other offences - There are several factors which weigh in the mind of the prosecutrix and her family members before coming to the police station to lodge a complaint - HC lost sight of this vital distinction - Prosecution established commission of offence punishable u/ss. 354 and 506, IPC - However, case of rape not established so far as the respondent is concerned - Appeal allowed to the extent that the respondent is convicted for offences punishable u/ss. 354 and 506 - Order accordingly.
