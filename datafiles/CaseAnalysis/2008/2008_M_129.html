M/S. Malwa Strips Pvt. Ltd v M/S. Jyoti Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2009; (2009) 2 SCC 426; AIR 2009 SC 1581; 2009 (76) ALR 428; 2009 (2) AWC 1280; JT 2008 (13) SC 515; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1352; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1874; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 211; 2009(1) SCALE 438; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1297

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Stay Of Proceedings

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 37 - Trial Court decreed the money suit - High Court stayed the operation and execution of the decree in its entirety - Appeal against - Held, while granting stay of the execution of the decree, it must take into consideration the facts and circumstances of the case before it - It is not to act arbitrarily either way - If a stay is granted, sufficient cause must be shown, which means that the materials on record were required to be perused and reasons are to be assigned - Such reasons should be cogent and adequate - An exceptional case has to be made out for stay of execution of a money decree - Appeals allowed.
