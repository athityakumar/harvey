Ex-Constable Ramvir Sing v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2026; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 33; 2009 (1) JCC 533; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 844; 2008(16) SCALE 261; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1112; 2009 (1) SLR 691

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Attorney-General, Armed Forces, Superior Officer, Court-Martial, Absence Without Leave, Insubordination And Obstruction

Summary: Border Security Force Act, 1968 - Dismissal from service - Held, charge of misconduct that he refused to eat his food was found to be strikingly disproportionate - A punishment of simplicitor dismissal from service, in a situation of this nature, cannot, however, be held to be disproportionate to the gravity of misconduct - Except the cases where the punishment is shockingly disproportionate, the Superior Courts would not ordinarily interfere with the quantum of punishment - Appeal dismissed.
