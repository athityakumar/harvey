Union of India v Dattatray

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, C.K. Thakker, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 628; (2008) 4 SCC 612; AIR 2008 SC 1678; 2008(3) ALL MR 441; 2008 (2) AWC 1600; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 433; 2008 (5) ESC 757; JT 2008 (3) SC 194; 2009 (1) LLJ 316; 2008 (3) LLN 50; 2008 (2) MahLJ 720; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 938; 2008 (2) MPLJ 52; 2008(3) SCALE 235; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 1096; 2008 (2) SLR 764; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1827

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: High Court upheld the order of the Scrutiny Committee invalidating the first respondent's claim that he belonged to 'Halba' tribe, and directed that the first respondent will not be entitled to any of the benefits but directed to continue the first respondent in service - Held, having held that the first respondent had falsely claimed that he belonged to a Schedule Tribe, it wrongly extended him the benefit of continuing in employment - Appeal allowed.
