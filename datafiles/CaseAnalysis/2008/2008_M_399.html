Rajasthan SRTC and Others v Mohar Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 746; (2008) 5 SCC 542; AIR 2008 SC 2553; 2008(4) ALL MR 899; 2008 (3) AWC 2706; 2008 (3) ESC 337; 2008 (117) FLR 1032; 2008 (3) KLT 371; 2009 (1) LLJ 149; 2008 (3) LLN 446; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 869; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 695; 2008(7) SCALE 339; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Order of reinstatement - Jurisdiction of civil court - Held, Courts ordinarily do not adopt an interpretation which takes away the jurisdiction of the Court - If a right is claimed under the Industrial Disputes Act or the sister laws, the jurisdiction of the Civil Court would be barred, but if no such right is claimed, civil court will have jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
