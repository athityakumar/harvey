Dipitimayee Parida v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2616; (2008) 10 SCC 687; AIR 2009 SC 935; JT 2008 (11) SC 430; 2008(13) SCALE 677; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 814; 2009 (1) SLR 181

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Selection of Anganwadi Workers - State of Orissa in terms of the Integrated Child Development Scheme of the Central Government issued an advertisement for appointment of Anganwadi Workers - A Circular Letter dated 7.10.1998 was also issued for selection of Anganwadi Workers laying down minimum educational qualifications and as also other criteria therefor - Clause 8(d) of the said Circular postulates that three marks are to be granted if the candidate is a married woman and additional three marks are to be granted if she is a widow or a divorcee - Appellant was not married at the time of filing of application for recruitment - However, she was awarded three more marks as she got married after filing of the application - Contending that the Selection Committee had no jurisdiction to award three marks to appellant, respondent filed a writ petition, which was allowed - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Held, matter relating to recruitment of Anganwadi Workers is not governed by any statute - Recruitments are made pursuant to a Scheme framed by the Central Government - State, therefore, while making recruitments in such projects in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 162 of the Constitution of India, may issue such guidelines and/or circulars as it may seem fit and proper - Said guidelines are ordinarily binding on all the functionaries working in terms of the 'scheme' including the Selection Committees constituted for recruitment of Anganwadi worker - Further, qualification or extra-qualification laid down for the recruitment should be considered as on the last date for filing of the application - Thus, Selection Committee could not have awarded her three additional marks on the premise that she was married - No legal infirmity in the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
