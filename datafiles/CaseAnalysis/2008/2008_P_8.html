Thukral Mechanical Works v P. M. Diesels Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2014; (2009) 2 SCC 768; AIR 2009 SC 1443; JT 2009 (2) SC 59; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 568; 2009 (39) PTC 193; 2009(1) SCALE 497; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1161

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Registered User, Registrar Of Trade Mark

Summary: Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Interpretation of the provisions of s. 46(1)(b) - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, the application under s. 46(1)(b) was maintainable? - Held, person in whose name the trade mark has been registered may take action against any person for passing off the goods as that of the registered owner - It confers an exclusive right of use of the trade mark in relation to the goods in which the trade mark is registered - The same is an assignable right in terms of s. 36, whereas an unregistered trade mark is not - Non-user of the said mark, therefore, entails the consequences laid down under s. 46 (1)(b) - Such an application can be filed only by a person aggrieved - When the applicant before the Board establishes the fact in regard to non-user of the mark for a period of more than five years on the part of the registered owner, the latter may show that his case falls within the purview of s. 46(3) - Appeal allowed.
