Madan Mohan Abbot v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 507; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 464; (2008) 4 SCC 582; 2008 (4) ADJ 199; AIR 2008 SC 1969; 2008 CRLJ 2243; 2008 (2) JCC 1036; 2008 (3) KLT 19; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 429; 2008(4) SCALE 159; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 379, 406, 409, 418, 506/34 - Application for quashing of FIR on account of the compromise entered into between the complainant and the accused dismissed on the ground that s. 406 was not compoundable as the amount involved was more than Rs.250/- - Appeal against - Held, dispute was purely a personal one between two contesting parties and that it arose out of extensive business dealings between them and that there was absolutely no public policy involved in the nature of the allegations made against the accused - No useful purpose would be served in continuing with the proceedings in the light of the compromise - Appeal allowed.
