State of Madhya Pradesh v Imrat and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1060; (2008) 11 SCC 523; AIR 2008 SC 2967; 2008 CRLJ 3869; 2008(10) SCALE 143; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307 r/w 34 - Trial Court sentenced respondents to undergo seven years RI with fine of Rs.1, 000/- - High Court held that the proper conviction would be under s. 326 r/w s. 34 IPC and the sentences were to be reduced to the period already undergone - Appeal against - Held, it is sufficient to justify a conviction under s. 307 if there is present an intent coupled with some overt act in execution thereof - It is not essential that bodily injury capable of causing death should have been inflicted - An accused charged under s. 307 cannot be acquitted merely because the injuries inflicted on the victim were in the nature of a simple hurt - Order of the High Court is set aside and that of the trial Court is restored - Appeal allowed.
