Anand Sharadchandra Oka v University Of Mumbai & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 139; (2008) 5 SCC 217; AIR 2008 SC 1289; 2008(2) ALL MR 809; 2008 (4) Bom.C.R. 68; JT 2008 (2) SC 204; 2008(2) SCALE 175; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 297; 2008 (2) SLR 348; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 930

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Election

Keywords: Bombay Universities Act, 1974

Summary: Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994 - Writ-petitioner who holds LL.M. degree of the University applied for registering his name in electoral roll for electing ten members in the Senate of the University - Writ petition dismissed on the ground that the petitioner was not aggrieved person - Appeal against - Held, writ-petitioner obtained B.A. Degree from Bombay University - Writ-petitioner was graduated from the respondent-University - His name, therefore, can be registered in the electoral roll for electing members of Senate - S. 99(1) declares that only those persons who are 'Graduates of the University' are entitled to have their names entered in the register of registered graduates - University means any university mentioned in the Schedule - It is not even the case of the writ-petitioner that name of any person who has graduated from the University as defined in s. 2(36) has not been included in the register - Appeal dismissed.
