State of Rajasthan v Jaggu Ram

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 24; (2008) 12 SCC 51; AIR 2008 SC 982; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 852; 2009 (1) CGLJ 158; 2008 CRLJ 1039; JT 2008 (1) SC 199; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 659; 2008 (3) RLW 2584; 2008(1) SCALE 22

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Police Record, Blood Stained Clothes, Destroying Evidence

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 304-B, 201 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, in order to prove the charge of dowry death, prosecution has to establish that the victim died within 7 years of marriage and she was subjected to cruelty or harassment soon before her death and such cruelty or harassment was for dowry - This was a fit case for invoking s. 106 of the Evidence Act, which lays down that when any fact is especially within the knowledge of the any person, the burden of proving that fact is upon him - If Single Judge of the High Court had adverted to s. 106 of the Evidence Act and correctly applied the principles of law, he would not have committed the grave error of acquitting the respondent - Conviction of respondent under ss. 304-B r/w 201, is restored - Appeal allowed.
