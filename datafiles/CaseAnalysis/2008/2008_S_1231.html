Behari Kunj Sahkari Avas Samiti v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2594; (2008) 12 SCC 306; 2009 (2) ADJ 29; 2008 (5) AWC 5138; JT 2008 (8) SC 295; 2008(10) SCALE 551; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949, Displaced Persons (Compensation & Rehabilitation) Act, 1955, Displaced Persons (Compensation & Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation & Rehabilitation) Act 1955 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Displaced Persons (Compensation & Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955 - Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - Custodian of Evacuee Properties - Transfer of property - A evacuee property was given in tenancy of 'H' and 'G' - Portion which was given in tenancy of 'G' was later on given to Addl. Asstt. Custodian - After his death, entire property was auctioned under the provisions of Displaced Persons Act and it was given to the displaced person 'T' - Sale certificate was made in favour of 'T' - Against this auction, 'H' made an application u/r. 92 of the Displaced Persons Rules claiming himself to be a tenant in respect of a part of land of the entire area - Application was rejected - After, a series of proceedings Asstt. Custodian, accepted the claim of the tenancy of 'H' over a part of land deeming it to be a separate part out of the total land - After this order, Asstt. Custodian transferred Zamindari rights in his favour - Thereafter, on an appeal filed 'H', auction and sale certificate in favour of 'T' were cancelled - 'T' application and revision application thereagainst was rejected - State of Uttar Pradesh made a representation before the Central Government u/s. 54 of the Act against the order of the Custodian by which Zamindari rights were sold to 'H' and against the order by which the revision application of 'T' was rejected - Thereafter, on 11.11.82, Authorised Custodian proposed the transfer of land which was the part of disputed subject matter to 'H' and a sale certificate dated 18.11.1982 in compliance thereof - Writ petitions filed by the State of Uttar Pradesh was allowed and the order transferring land and issuance of sale certificate dated 18.11.1982 in compliance thereof were directed to be cancelled - Present appeal - Held, property vests in the Custodian of the particular State - Job of the Custodian is to preserve and maintain the property - Property can be transferred only in case it cannot be preserved - No such action can be taken without prior approval of the Custodian General - Post facto approval is not sufficient - In the present case, this procedure was not followed - Tender process was not adopted which would have ensured that the best price was available - No opportunity was granted to 'T' before passing of the order on 11.11.1982 - Further, there was some suppression of factual position - HC was justified in its view and no interference is therefore called for - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Civil Procedure - "Fraud" - Meaning of - Held, "fraud" is a conduct either by letter or words, which induces the other person or authority to take a definite determinative stand as a response to the conduct of the former either by words or letter - Fraud arises from a deception committed by disclosure of incorrect facts knowingly and deliberately to invoke exercise of power and procure an order from an authority or tribunal - It must result in exercise of jurisdiction which otherwise would not have been exercised - Misrepresentation must be in relation to the conditions provided in a section on existence or non-existence of which the power can be exercised - Suppression of a material document would also amount to a fraud on the court - But non-disclosure of a fact not required by a statute to be disclosed may not amount to fraud - Even in commercial transactions non-disclosure of every fact does not vitiate the agreement - Appeal dismissed.
