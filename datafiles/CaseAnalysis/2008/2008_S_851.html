Naresh Kavarchand Khatri v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 824; (2008) 8 SCC 300; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 614; AIR 2008 SC 2180; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2928; 2008 (2) JCC 1344; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 144; 2008 (2) KLT 747; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 912; 2008(7) SCALE 274; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - Whether the High Court has the requisite jurisdiction to transfer an investigation from one Police Station to another? - Held, first information report prima facie shows that a part of cause of jurisdiction arose within the territorial jurisdiction of Vadodara Police Station - Fail to understand as to how at such an early stage, the investigation should have been directed to be transferred, having regard to the fact that Waghodia Police Station where the 'institution' in question is situated is within the jurisdiction of Vadodara (District) and is, therefore, not a case where the accused would have been even otherwise gravely prejudiced in joining investigation - Appeals allowed.
