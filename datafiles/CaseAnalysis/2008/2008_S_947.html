Puttaswamy v State of Karnataka and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1939; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 607; (2009) 1 SCC 711; 2009 (3) ACC 935; JT 2008 (12) SC 561; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 502; 2008(15) SCALE 483; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 272

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 279 and 304-A - Compounding of offence - Whether the offence u/s. 304-A could at all be compounded since the same is not covered by the provisions of s. 320 of CrPC,1973 - If an offence is not compoundable within the scope of s. 320 of CrPC the Court may, in view of the compromise between the parties, reduce the sentence imposed while maintaining the conviction - Appeal by special leave against conviction u/ss. 279 and 304-A - For causing death of a seven year old girl on account of his rash and negligent driving of tractor - In revision, High Court confirmed conviction, but set aside the sentence u/s. 279 but maintained the conviction and sentence in respect of the offence u/s. 304-A, whereby sentenced only to six months simple imprisonment along with a fine - Held, instead of confining the appellant in prison, the interest of justice will be better served if he is made to compensate the family of the deceased on account of the loss suffered by them - Hence, while maintaining conviction u/ss. 279 and 304-A IPC, notwithstanding the agreement arrived at between the parties, amount of fine increased from Rs.2,000/- to Rs.20,000/- to be paid by the appellant to the parents and reduce the sentence to the period already undergone, subject to payment of the fine - Appeal disposed of.
