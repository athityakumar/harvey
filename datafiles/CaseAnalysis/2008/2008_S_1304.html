State, represented By Inspector of Police v Saravanan and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1638; (2008) 17 SCC 587; AIR 2009 SC 152; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 580; 2008 (4) JCC 2792; JT 2008 (11) SC 290; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 227; 2008(13) SCALE 253; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 427, 448 - Trial Court convicted the accused - Appeal against order of High Court setting aside the order of conviction - Held, relationship is not a factor to affect the credibility of a witness, for the mere reason that an eyewitness can be said to be an interested witness, his/her testimony need not be rejected - Difference in some minor detail, which does not otherwise affect the core of the prosecution case, even if present, that itself would not prompt the court to reject the evidence on minor variations and discrepancies - Injuries were caused by the accused persons not only on the person of eye witnesses but also on the person of deceased which resulted in his death and the prosecution has been able to prove its case beyond any reasonable doubt - Present case cannot be said to be a case falling under the provisions of s. 302 - Appeal partly allowed.
