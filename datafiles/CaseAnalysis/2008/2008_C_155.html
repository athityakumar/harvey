Commissioner Of Central Excise, Chandigarh-I v IPF Vikram India Ltd

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1490; 2008 (226) E.L.T. 4

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Sale Price, Place Of Removal

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, ss. 4, 4A and 11A - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173C and 173Q - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, r. 6(b) - Whether CEGAT erred in holding that if an independent job worker processes goods and returns it back to supplier of goods, prescription of other 'place of removal' in budget will not make any difference in such cases? - Circular No. 251/85/96-CS clarified that if an independent job worker processes goods and returns it back to supplier of goods, prescription of other 'place of removal' in budget will not make any difference in such cases - For products under consideration here, valuation can be done on basis of price of comparable goods u/r. 6(b)(i) or failing that u/r. 6(b)(ii) of Valuation Rules on basis of cost of manufacture plus notional profit, in order to arrive at nearest ascertainable equivalent of price - Held, not inclined to interfere with order passed by tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
