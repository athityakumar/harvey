Union Of India & Ors v Surinder Singh Rathore

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 398; (2008) 5 SCC 747; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1925; JT 2008 (4) SC 435; 2008(4) SCALE 468; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 909; 2008 (4) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Health & Drug; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Armed Forces, Active Service, Medical Service of Armed Force Regulation, 1983

Summary: Army Rules, 1954; Pension Regulations for the Army; Medical Service of Armed Force Regulation 1983 - Disability pension - High Court directed appellants to grant the respondent disability pension on the ground that the controversy was squarely covered by an earlier decision rendered by the High Court in SB Civil Writ No. 1083 of 2001 - Appeal against - Held, Medical Board's opinion was clearly to the effect that the ailment suffered by the appellant was not attributable to the military service and also not aggravated due to it - Single Judge and the Division Bench were not justified in holding that the same was attributable to Military service and/or was aggravated because of service - Respondent is not entitled to disability pension - Appeal allowed.
