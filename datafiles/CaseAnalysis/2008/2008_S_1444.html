State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Swadeshi Polytex Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 958; (2008) 12 SCC 596; 2008 (7) ADJ 377; AIR 2008 SC 2854; 2008 (4) AWC 3556; 2009 (107) RD 22; 2008(9) SCALE 191; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Personal Service, Oil Mills, U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition & Land Reforms Rules, 1952

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition & Land Reforms Rules, 1952; Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Appeals to challenge order passing strictures against the officers of the State Government who had been instrumental in arranging auction - Whether the procedure envisaged for recovery of arrears under U.P.Z. & L. A. Act, 1950 and the U.P.Z. & L. A. Rules, 1950 had been observed? - Held, sale proclamation had been issued on 1st April 2005 without any valuation of the properties and only the area of the vacant land had been specified therein and it was this notice that had been served on the Chowkidar on the 21st April 2005 and publication had been made in the newspaper "Amar Ujala" on the 22nd April 2005, there has, thus, been a clear violation of the rr. 283 and 285 - Wide publication and notice of the proposed sale should be given as per r. 285-A which postulates a notice of 30 days between the date of issuance of the sale proclamation and the date of auction - It can hardly be over emphasized that the proper valuation of the property and wide publicity of the proposed auction is intimately linked with the price that the auction fetches - High Court's direction that action should be initiated against the concerned officers, is not justified - Appeals dismissed.
