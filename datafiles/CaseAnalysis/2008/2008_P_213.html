Animireddy Venkata Ramana and Others v Public Prosecutor, H.C. of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 354; (2008) 5 SCC 368; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 600; AIR 2008 SC 1603; 2008 CRLJ 2038; JT 2008 (3) SC 384; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2008(3) SCALE 429; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1078

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Blood Stained Clothes

Summary: Murder - Trial Court acquitted all the accused - Division Bench of High Court held that the appellants as also Accused No. 10 guilty of commission of murder of the deceased and acquitted Accused Nos. 11 to 24 of all charges - Appeal against conviction - Held, Trial Judge committed a serious error in opining that investigation had already started before the lodging of the First Information Report - Without taking recourse to the right methodology of appreciation of evidence, no court of law should jump to the conclusion that a prosecution witness is wholly untrustworthy only because his evidence has not been corroborated by other witnesses - Non-production of the general diary by itself cannot be a ground for disbelieving the entire prosecution case particularly when apart from a solitary statement made by PW-3 in his note, no other evidence has been brought on records to show that statement of any witness had been recorded under s. 161 of the Code - If the High court has given benefit of doubt to Accused Nos. 11 to 24, the same by itself may not be sufficient to extend the same benefit to the main accused - Appeal dismissed.
