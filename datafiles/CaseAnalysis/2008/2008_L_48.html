Shivanand Gaurishankar Baswanti v Laxmi Vishnu Textile Mills and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1340; (2008) 13 SCC 323; AIR 2009 SC 825; 2008 (4) BC 578; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 602; [2008] 144 Comp Cas 516; 2008 (5) CompLJ 232; 2008 (2) DRTC 545; JT 2008 (8) SC 81; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 665; 2008(10) SCALE 192; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 782; 2008 (8) SLR 426

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Indian Companies Act, 1873

Summary: Corporate - Constitution of India, 1950 - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Indian Companies Act, 1873 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether proceedings under SICA were illegal and arbitrary and whether workers have been paid less benefits than what they are entitled to? - Held, union considered matter in its entirety, financial condition of company, claims of creditors, decrees and orders passed by various authorities under different laws and properties of company - In larger interest, Union decided to accept amount of Rs. 22.21 crores for workers towards full and final settlement - By no stretch of imagination, such action could be held improper, illegal or mala fide - If, at this stage,the Court set aside sale in favour of respondent No. 7, serious prejudice will be caused to vendor and vendee, respectively, but also to others like banks, financial institutions, other creditors and also to workers for whose benefit and welfare appellant is fighting - It is pertinent to note that no secured or unsecured creditor has come forward making grievance that though he was entitled to more amount, he has not been paid such amount - Appeal dismissed.
