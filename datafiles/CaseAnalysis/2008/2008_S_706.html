S.K. Alagh v State Of U.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 207; (2008) 5 SCC 662; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 686; AIR 2008 SC 1731; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1339; 2008 (4) BC 622; [2008] 142 Comp Cas 228; 2008 CRLJ 2256; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 125; JT 2008 (2) SC 540; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 79; 2008(2) SCALE 523; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 1088

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code - Whether complaint petition, even if given face value and taken to be correct in its entirety, disclosed an offence as against the appellant under s. 406? - Held, drafts were drawn in the name of the company, even if appellant was its Managing Director, he cannot be said to have committed an offence under s. 406 - If and when a statute contemplates creation of such a legal fiction, it provides specifically therefore - In absence of any provision laid down under the statute, a Director of a company or an employee cannot be held to be vicariously liable for any offence committed by company itself - High Court committed a manifest error in passing the impugned judgment - Appeal allowed.
