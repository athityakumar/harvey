Dr. Rajbir Singh Dalal v Chaudhari Devi Lal University, Sirsa and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2251; (2008) 9 SCC 284; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 768; 2008 (6) AWC 6578; 2008 (3) ESC 546; JT 2008 (8) SC 621; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 239; 2008(11) SCALE 211; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 992; 2009 (8) SLR 640

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala Agriculturists Debt Relief Act, 1970, U.P. Muslims Waqfs Act, 1936, UGC Regulations, Mimansa Rules

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 2(f) - Mimansa Rules - UGC Regulations, regn. 2 - University Grants Commission Rules - Qualification - Respondent-university issued advertisement for direct recruitment for various posts, including post of Reader in Public Administration - Appellant claiming to be fully eligible and qualified for post of Reader in Public Administration, applied for post on prescribed format - Selection Committee interviewed appellant as per call letter - Appellant was selected as Reader and he joined as such on 4-4-2005 - Respondent No. 2 who was Lecturer in Public Administration had also applied for post of Reader, but he was not selected and instead appellant was selected - Hence, respondent No. 2 filed writ petition in HC, alleging that appellant did not possess requisite qualification for post of Reader in Public Administration - It was alleged that appellant was M.A. and Ph.D. in Political Science and not in Administration - Hence, appellant was not eligible for being selected and appointed as Reader in Public Administration - Counter affidavit filed by respondent No. 1 -University, it was stated that Public Administration was one of branches of Political Science, and hence appellant was rightly selected by Selection Committee consisting of eminent experts after evaluating his academic qualifications - HC by impugned judgment allowed writ petition and set aside selection and appointment of appellant - HC relied on decision of SC in Dr. Bhanu Prasad Panda vs. Chancellor, Sambalpur University (2001) 8 SCC 532 2001 Indlaw SC 19828 in which it was observed that subjects of Public Administration and Political Science were distinct and separate and a person possessing academic qualification in discipline of Political Science could not be appointed in discipline of Public Administration - HC also relied on regn. 2 of the UGC Regulations - HC was of view that person was not qualified for appointment as Reader unless he has qualification in appropriate subject - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant fulfills requisite academic qualification for appointment to post of Reader in Public Administration in Chaudhary Devi Lal University, Sirsa - Held, decision of SC in Dr. Bhanu Prasad Panda's case 2001 Indlaw SC 19828 could not be read as a Enclid's formula or treated as precedent, since it had not given any reason for holding that Political Science and Public Administration were distinct and separate subjects, and since aforesaid decision was given on concession - Recruitment Rules followed by University clearly indicates that order to be appointed as Lecturer in particular discipline candidate should have a post-graduate degree in relevant subject - On other hand, for appointment to post of Reader such condition had not been specified - In fact, in regn. 2 it had been generally indicated that no person should be appointed to teaching post in University or in any institution, in a subject, if he/she did not fulfil requirement as to qualifications for appropriate subject - Expression 'appropriate subject' was intended to cover post of Reader and once expert bodies had indicated that appellant who held post-graduate degree in Political Science was eligible to be appointed to post of Reader in Public Administration and had been rightly appointed to such post, it was normally not for Courts to question such opinion, unless it had specialised knowledge of subject- Appeal allowed.
