Puran Singh v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 155; (2008) 3 SCC 795; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1386; 2008 CRLJ 1058; 2008 (1) JCC 253; JT 2008 (1) SC 302; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 929; 2008(1) SCALE 306; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 491; 2008 (1) UC 238

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Firearm Injuries, Eye- Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence of imprisonment for life - Held, as per Ballistic Expert's opinion, cartridge E.C.1 was not fired from the single barrel 12 bore No. 1319 said to have been used by the accused - Appellant accused is entitled to benefit of doubt - Order of conviction and sentence recorded by the High Court is set aside and the appellant is given benefit of doubt and is ordered to be acquitted - Appeal allowed.
