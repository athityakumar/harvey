P. Venugopal v Madan P. Sarathi

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2176; (2009) 1 SCC 492; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 554; AIR 2009 SC 568; 2009 (1) JCC (NI) 27; 2009 (1) KarLJ 569; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 243; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 25

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheques

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Discharge of debt - Dishonour of cheque - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent gave loan to appellant and in discharge of said debt appellant was said to have issued two cheques and said cheques were presented before bank and were returned dishonoured on ground that sufficient fund was not available - Upon service of notice upon respondent criminal complaint was filed and Additional Metropolitan Magistrate ordered conviction - Appeal was preferred before Fast Track Court wherein appellant filed application for adduction of additional evidence against respondent but appeal was dismissed - Revision application filed by appellant before HC was also dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by Trial Judge, Appellate Court and Revisional Court was justified - Held, initial burden was on complainant and presumption raised in favour of holder of cheque should be kept confined to matters covered thereby - Presumption raised did not extend to extent that cheque was issued for discharge of any debt or liability which was required to be proved by complainant however, it was essentially question of fact - All three Courts below had arrived at concurrent finding that complainant was able to prove his case of grant of loan and burden of proof shifted to appellant - Appellant had failed to discharge his burden and finding of fact arrived at by Courts below could not be said to be such which warrants interference - Question of service of notice in terms of proviso appended to s.138 of the Act was concerned again was essentially question of fact - Evidence of postal peon if was believed by Trial Judge as also by Appellate Court and revisional Court, SC in exercise of jurisdiction should not interfere - Perusal of memo of appeal filed by appellant himself before Appellate Court, it appeared that therein also same address was given - Therefore, no case was made out for interference with impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
