Premkumari & Ors v Prahlad Dev & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 71; (2008) 3 SCC 193; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 694; 2008 (2) ACC 477; AIR 2008 SC 1073; 2008 (71) ALR 312; 2008 (5) AWC 4529; 2008 (1) CCC 77; 2008 (3) CGLJ 238; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 28; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2744; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 568; 2008 (150) PLR 241; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 835; 2008(1) SCALE 531; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 874

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance

Keywords: Arrears Of Land Revenue, Social Welfare Legislation, Breach Of Condition, Exercise Of Jurisdiction, Damage To Property, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Use Of Vehicle, Welfare Legislation, Insurance Of Vehicle, Licensed Driver, Policy Conditions, Duplicate Licence

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether the Tribunal was right in holding that the insurer was not liable as the driver had a fake licence? - Held, when the owner after verification satisfied himself that the driver has a valid licence and driving the vehicle in question competently at the time of the accident there would be no breach of s. 149(2)(a)(ii), in that event, the Insurance Company would not then be absolved of liability - Even in the case that the licence was fake, the Insurance Company would continue to remain liable unless they prove that the owner was aware or noticed that the licence was fake and still permitted him to drive - Permitted third respondent-Insurance Company to recover the said amount from the owner of the vehicle - Appeal disposed of.
