Mavji C Lakum v Central Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 583; (2008) 12 SCC 726; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1817; 2008 (3) CLR 73; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2138; JT 2008 (5) SC 546; 2008 (3) LLJ 1; 2008 (3) LLN 1; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 854; 2008(7) SCALE 32; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1001; 2008 (4) SLR 711

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Back-Wages

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act - Tribunal set aside punishment of discharge as also the other punishments and restricted the said punishment to stoppage of one year's increment - Single Judge set aside the order passed by the Industrial Tribunal - Appeal against - Held, even if the inquiry is found to be fair, that would be only a finding certifying that all possible opportunities were given to the delinquent and the principles of natural justice and fair play were observed - If the Industrial Tribunal comes to the conclusion that the findings could not be supported on the basis of the evidence given or further comes to the conclusion that the punishment given is shockingly disproportionate, the Industrial Tribunal would still be justified in re-appreciating the evidence and/or interfering with the quantum of punishment - Tribunal has correctly appreciated the evidence and has also correctly substituted the punishment - Appeal allowed.
