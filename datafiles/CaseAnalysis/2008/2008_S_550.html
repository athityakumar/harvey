M/S. Girias Investment Pvt.Ltd. & Anr v State Of Karnataka & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 417; (2008) 7 SCC 53; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 860; JT 2008 (4) SC 242; 2008(4) SCALE 498; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 948; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1083

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Acquiring Authority, Non-Compliance, Person Interested, Revenue Official, Right Of Way, Highway, Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board Act, 1966

Summary: Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board Act, 1966 - Challenging acquisition proceedings - Held, it is open to the court to go into the question of malafides raised by a litigant but in order to succeed, much more than a mere allegation is required - Inference of malafide based on the ground that the change in the location of the trumpet interchange and the access road had been suddenly made without proper application of mind to help certain unidentified individuals resulting in the acquisition of the land belonging to the appellants is, thus, without any factual basis - Appeal dismissed.
