Indu Jain v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2138; (2008) 15 SCC 341; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 996; AIR 2009 SC 976; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1847; 2009 CRLJ 951; 2009 (1) JCC 246; JT 2008 (12) SC 524; 2008(15) SCALE 168; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Custodial Death

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 299, 302, 304, 323 and 330 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 228 and 482 - Framing of charges - Officers of Special Police Establishment arrested alleged offender - Alleged offender died while in custody of officers - Sessions Judge framed charges against them u/ss. 304, Part-II, IPC, but dropped charges u/s. 330, IPC - Criminal revision filed before HC - HC set aside charge framed by Sessions Judge and directed that charge could only be framed u/s. 323/34, IPC - Hence, present petition - (A) Whether HC erred in quashing charge framed against accused persons u/s. 304, Part-II? - Held, it has been sufficiently established that deceased was a patient of asthma which could cause asphyxia which was ultimately said to be cause of death - Deceased had been kept in a room which was highly unsuitable for a person suffering from respiratory problems - On a prima facie view of matter, there is ground to proceed against accused persons - (B) Whether Trial court as well as High Court was justified in dropping charge under Section 330, IPC? - Held, since cause of death has been shown to be asphyxia on account of detention of deceased in unhygienic conditions despite his respiratory problems and injuries to ribs and mouth of deceased could possibly have been caused by attempts made by doctor at Hospital to resuscitate deceased, who had been brought to Hospital in a comatose condition, with body showing no signs of pulse, respiration or blood pressure, prima facie a case is made out for framing of charge u/s. 330, IPC - HC order set aside - Appeals disposed of.
