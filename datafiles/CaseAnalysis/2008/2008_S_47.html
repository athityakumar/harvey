M. Viswanathan v S.K. Tiles and Potteries Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1861; (2008) 16 SCC 390; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 761; [2009] 152 Comp Cas 335; JT 2008 (13) SC 412; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 290; 2008(15) SCALE 578; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: IPC, 1860, 379, 468, 471, 420, 506 (ii) - Proceedings quashed - Appeal against - Held, allegations are substantially made out - This is not a case where the jurisdiction under s. 482 Cr.P.C. was to be exercised - Court must be careful to see that its decision in exercise of this power is based on sound principles - The inherent power should not be exercised to stifle a legitimate prosecution - It would not be proper for the High Court to analyse the case of the complainant in the light of all probabilities in order to determine whether a conviction would be sustainable and on such premises arrive at a conclusion that the proceedings are to be quashed - Appeal allowed.
