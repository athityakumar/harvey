Supreme Court of India

04 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2180; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 119; (2009) 1 SCC 768; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 824; 2009 (2) CalLT(SC) 13; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 62; JT 2008 (12) SC 587; 2008(15) SCALE 232; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 194; 2009 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Selection process - Written examination for selection of Medical Technologists was taken in 1995 and list of candidates was published in 1996 - Non-selected candidates challenged said list - Tribunal set aside merit list and a direction was issued to prepare fresh merit list of candidates - Writ petitions filed thereagainst were disposed of and matter was remanded to Tribunal - Tribunal again considered matter and found that selection process was bona fide - Order challenged before HC and by impugned order, HC allowed petitions - HC directed that remaining vacancies should be filled up on basis of seniority position - Contempt petition was filed alleging that authorities had not implemented directions issued by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether relief can be granted in favour of appellants who agitated against selection of candidates? - Held, Tribunal and HC were right in not setting aside their appointments - Relief can be granted in favour of appellants who had challenged the selection of candidates before Tribunal, HC and SC - (B) Whether same relief can be granted to those candidates who had not approached Tribunal, HC or SC and have now filed interim applications? - If petitioner wants to invoke jurisdiction of a Writ Court, he should come to Court at earliest reasonably possible opportunity - Inordinate delay in making motion for a writ will indeed be a good ground for refusing to exercise such discretionary jurisdiction - Held, candidates who never challenged selection before any Court qua them, therefore, matter can be said to have been 'settled' - Initiation of proceedings at instance of those candidates now will 'unsettle settled position' - Relief could not be extended to those candidates - However, in larger interest and keeping in view vacancies in cadre, equitable relief in favour of eligible and qualified applicants granted - Directions would not apply to those candidates who have approached Court for first time by filing interim applications - Applications, therefore, stand dismissed - Appeals partly allowed.
