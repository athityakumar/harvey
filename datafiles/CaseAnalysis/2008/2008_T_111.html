Thiruvengada Pillai v Navaneethammal & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 222; (2008) 4 SCC 530; AIR 2008 SC 1541; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 112; 2008 (2) AWC 1374B; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1115; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 262; 2009 (106) RD 178; 2008(2) SCALE 630; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 23

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Execution Of Document, Opinion Of An Expert, Indian Stamp Rules, 1925

Summary: Suit for specific performance - Whether agreement of sale executed on two stamp papers purchased on different dates and more than six months prior to date of execution is not valid?; Whether the first appellate court was justified in comparing the disputed thumb impression with the admitted thumb impression and recording a finding about the authenticity of the thumb impression, without the benefit of any opinion of an expert?; whether the High Court erred in reversing the judgment of the first appellate court in second appeal? - Held, fact that very old stamp papers of different dates have been used, may certainly be a circumstance that can be used as a piece of evidence to cast doubt on the authenticity of the agreement - That cannot be a clinching evidence - Finding by the first appellant court, recorded without the benefit of any expert opinion, merely on a casual perusal, that there appeared to be no marked differences between the two thumb impressions, and therefore sale agreement must have been executed by first defendant, was unsound - Decision of the High Court, reversing the decision of the first appellate court, does not call for interference - Appeal dismissed.
