Uttar Pradesh State Sugar Corporation Limited and Others v Kamal Swaroop Tondon

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 74; (2008) 2 SCC 41; 2008 (1) ADJ 137; AIR 2008 SC 1235; 2008 (118) FLR 834; 2008 (2) LLJ 854; 2008 (2) LLN 596; 2008 (3) RLW 2194; 2008(1) SCALE 615; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 887; 2008 (8) SLR 658

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Re Employment, Meritorious, Uttar Pradesh State Sugar Corporation Ltd. General Service Rules, 1988, provisions of Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972

Summary: Show cause notice was issued to the respondent for the losses caused to the Corporation due to negligence and carelessness on the part of the respondent - High Court held that disciplinary inquiry against the writ petitioner was illegal as he had retired on that day and no proceedings, hence, could have been initiated against him - Appeal against - Held, a regular show cause notice as to departmental inquiry was also served upon the respondent- employee on the last day of his service - It could not be said that the proceedings had been initiated against the respondent-employee after he retired from service - Proceedings could have been continued after the retirement of the respondent-employee as far as the financial loss caused to the Corporation because of negligence on the part of employee and the benefit claimed by the respondent-workman on his terminal benefits - High Court was wrong in quashing the proceedings and setting aside orders - Appeal allowed.
