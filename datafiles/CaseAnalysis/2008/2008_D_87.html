K.B.Saha and Sons Private Limited v Development Consultant Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 818; (2008) 8 SCC 564; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 850; 2008 (6) ALD(SC) 92; 2008(5) ALL MR 485; 2008 (72) ALR 638; 2008 (5) CTC 260; JT 2008 (9) SC 12; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 526; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 327; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 660; 2008 (105) RD 369; 2008(9) SCALE 490; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Agreement For Lease, Residential Purpose, Change Of User, Unregistered Lease Deed

Summary: West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956 - Suit for declaration and permanent injunction that as per the terms of the Memorandum of Agreement dismissed - Appeals against - Held, it is the case of the appellant that the suit premises was let out only for the particular named officer of the respondent and accordingly, after the same was vacated by the said officer, the respondent was not entitled to allot it to any other employee and was therefore, liable to be evicted which, was an important term forming part of the lease agreement - Such a Clause, namely, Clause 9 of the Lease Agreement in this case, cannot be looked into even for collateral purposes to come to a conclusion that the respondent was liable to be evicted because of violation of Clause 9 of the Lease Agreement - Unable to hold that Clause 9 of the Lease Agreement, which is admittedly unregistered, can be looked into for the purpose of evicting the respondent from the suit premises only because the respondent was not entitled to induct any other person other than the named officer in the same - Appeals dismissed.
