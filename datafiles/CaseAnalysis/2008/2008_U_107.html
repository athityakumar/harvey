Satna Stone and Lime Company Limited, Madhya Pradesh v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1396; (2008) 14 SCC 785; AIR 2008 SC 2928; 2008(3) ALL MR 877; 2008 (3) AWC 2769; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 845; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 226; 2008(7) SCALE 699

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Reasoned Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 16, 20 and 30 - Interference with award passed by arbitrator - Scope of interference by Court is limited and Court would not be justified in reappreciating material on record and substituting its own view in place of arbitrator's view - This exercise is not permissible by court in view of settled legal position - Error apparent on face of award contemplated by s. 16(1)(c) as well as s. 30(c) is an error of law apparent on face of award and not an error of fact - Held, in view of clear legal position which has been correctly appreciated by High Court in impugned judgment, no interference is called for - Appeals dismissed.
