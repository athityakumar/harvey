S.K. Sinha, Chief Enforcement Officer v Videocon International Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 111; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 471; (2008) 2 SCC 492; AIR 2008 SC 1213; [2009] 148 Comp Cas 261; 2008 CRLJ 1636; 2008 (1) JCC 420; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 56; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 38; 2008 (3) RLW 2147; 2008(2) SCALE 23; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Whether issuance of process in a criminal case is one and the same thing or can be equated with taking cognizance by a Criminal Court?; If the period of initiation of criminal proceedings has elapsed at the time of issue of process by a Court, the proceedings should be quashed as barred by limitation? - Held, cognizance of an offence had already been taken by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, Mumbai on May 24, 2002, well within the period prescribed by s. 49(3) of FEMA within two years of coming into force of the Act from June 1, 2000 - High Court has not at all touched the merits of the case and proceedings were not quashed on the ground that the provisions of FERA do not apply to the case before the Court - Remitted the matter to the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
