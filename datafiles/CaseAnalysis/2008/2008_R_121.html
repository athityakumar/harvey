Union Of India And Others v Rajpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1738; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 92; (2009) 1 SCC 216; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 657; 2008 (5) ESC 718; JT 2008 (12) SC 476; 2008(14) SCALE 591; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 879; 2009 (3) SLR 117

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Commissioned Officer, Superior Officer, Junior Commissioned Officer, Tenure Of Service, Medical Unfitness, Warrant Officers, Armed Forces Medical Services Act, 1983

Summary: Army Rules, 1954 - Service - Whether the holding of an "Invalidating Board" is a condition precedent for discharge of a Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) on account of low medical category? - Held, if a JCO is to be discharged from the service on the ground of "medically unfit for further service", irrespective of the fact whether he is or was in a low medical category, his order of discharge can be made only on the recommendation of an Invalidating Board - Rule prescribes a particular procedure for discharge of a JCO on account of medical unfitness, which must be followed and, therefore, any order of discharge passed without subjecting him to Invalidating Board would fall foul of the said statutory rule - Appeal dismissed.
