State Of M.P. And Others v Hazarilal

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 239; (2008) 3 SCC 273; AIR 2008 SC 1300; 2008 (2) CLR 182; 2008 (1) CLR 987; 2008 (2) ESC 238; JT 2008 (2) SC 390; 2008 (2) LLJ 715; 2008 (2) LLN 7; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1061; 2008(2) SCALE 361; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 787; 2008 (2) SLR 366

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: State Administrative Tribunal, Madhya Pradesh State Services Act

Summary: Madhya Pradesh State Services Act - Service - Punishment under s. 323 - Termination - Tribunal held that punishment of removal from service is grossly excessive - Appeal against - Held, an authority which is conferred with a statutory discretionary power is bound to take into consideration all the attending facts and circumstances of the case before imposing an order of punishment - Respondent occupied the lowest rank of the cadre - He was merely a contingency peon - Continuation of his service in the department would not bring a bad name to the State - He was not convicted for any act involving moral turpitude - He was not punished for any heinous offence - Appeal dismissed.
