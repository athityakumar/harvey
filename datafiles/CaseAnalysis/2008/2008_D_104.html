Union of India v Dharamendra Textile Processors

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1837; (2008) 13 SCC 369; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 668; 2008 (231) E.L.T. 3; [2008] 306 ITR 277; JT 2008 (11) SC 255; 2008(13) SCALE 233; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 13; [2008] 174 TAXMAN 571; [2008] 18 VST 180

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Adjudicating Officer, Mis-Statement, Independent Processor, Hot Air Stenter Independent Textile Processors Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1998, Securities And Exchange Board Of India (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996, FERA, 1947

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11AC - Finance Act, 1996 - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 271(1)(c), 276(1)(c) and 276C - Mens rea - Penalty - Maintainability - Assessee/respondent was assessed and penalty was imposed by Assessing officer(AO) - Aggrieved assessee/respondent filed appeal before Appellate Authority which was dismissed - Respondent/Assessee preferred appeal before HC which was allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeals ???(DB of SC )- Which referred appeals to larger Bench - Hence, instant reference - Whether interpretation made by lower Court was maintainable - Held, SC relied upon Chairman, Sebi v Shriram Mutual Fund and Another 2006 Indlaw SC 237 and said legal position was analyzed in correct perspective - Further, in Union Budget of 1996-97, s. 11AC of the 1944 Act was introduced which made position clear that there was no scope for any discretion - Furthermore, SC stated that in Dilip N. Shroof's case2007 Indlaw SC 896 had not considered effect and relevance of s. 276C of the 1961 Act and object behind enactment of s. 271 (1)(e) of the 1961 Act r/w explanations indicate that said section had been enacted to provide for remedy for loss of revenue were penalty under that provision was civil liability - Further, willful concealment was not essential ingredient for attracting civil liability as was case in matter of prosecution u/s. 276C of the 1961 Act - Therefore, matter should be placed before DB to deal with - Hence, orders of HC and Tribunal were quashed and appeals should be placed before DB - Appeals disposed of.
