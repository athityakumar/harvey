Krantikari Suraksha Rakshak Sanghatana v Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1358; (2008) 10 SCC 166; AIR 2009 SC 1118; 2009(2) ALL MR 432; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 748; JT 2008 (10) SC 123; 2009 (1) LLJ 532; 2008(11) SCALE 723; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 649; 2008 (8) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Re Employment, Maternity Benefit, Maharashtra Private Security Guards (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1981, Maharashtra Private Security Guards (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1951

Summary: Maharashtra Private Security Guards (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1981 - Held, as rightly noted by the High Court the provisions of the Act and the statute make it clear that the Board's power of allotment carries with it the implicit and inherent power to recall, re-allot and transfer a guard from one principal employer to another - Appeal dismissed.
