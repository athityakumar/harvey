Ksl and Industries Limited v Arihant Threads Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2042; (2008) 9 SCC 763; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 151; 2008 (4) BC 421; 2008 (5) CompLJ 1; 2008 (153) DLT 27; 2009 (1) DRTC 1; JT 2008 (9) SC 381; 2008(12) SCALE 42; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Small Industries Development Bank Of India Act, 1989, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 19, 20, 30, 34, 34(1), 34(2) - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 22, 22(1), 32 - Financed project - Recovery of debt - Maintainability - Lessor/Corporation allotted Plot on lease to respondent no.1/company with condition that lessee would not transfer interest in property for first fifteen years without prior permission of lessor - bank financed project by way of foreign currency loan - Company suffered losses due to overall recession in Textile Industry and could not repay amount of loan - Successor of lessor cancelled lease agreement on nonpayment of lease money - Bank filed application before Tribunal for recovery under the 1993 Act which passed ex-parte final order in favour Bank - Recovery Officer issued demand notice against Company demanding payment - Appellant was declared highest bidder for auction sale - Appellant made application to Tribunal for acceptance of bank guarantee in lieu of payment which was dismissed - Company moved application asking to set aside auction sale which was allowed - Company and appellant filed appeals before Appellate Tribunal which were disposed of - Company filed writ petitions in HC against order of Tribunal which were allowed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of recovery was maintainable - Held, conduct of respondent no.1 leaves should be desired, without challenging final order passed by Tribunal and allowing Bank's claim - Respondent no.1 questioned order of Recovery Officer, fixing reserve price of Company's assets for purposes of auction sale, u/s. 30 of the 1993 Act having full knowledge of fact that final order of Tribunal could not be challenged in such appeal - Further, steps taken by respondent no.1 were far from bona fide and were only aimed at stalling auction sale -Thus, even at time of auction of company's assets, no attempt was made by respondent no.1 to secure a bid higher than that of appellant - Appeal allowed.
