Uttarakhand Power Corporation Ltd. And Another v Asp Sealing Products Ltd

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2791; (2009) 9 SCC 701; 2010 (3) AWC 2333; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 325; 2009(12) SCALE 326

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Minimum Charge, Default, Supply Of Energy, Sub-Station, Commencement Of Supply, Electricity Supply (Consumers) Regulations, 1984, U.P. Government Electricity Undertakings (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1958

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Supply (Consumers) Regulations, 1984, cl.17(ii) ("Regulations") -Disconnection of electricity - Minimum charges payable - Supply of electricity - Respondent entering into agreement with appellant Board - Respondent approaching Board for reduction of load - Respondent's request allowed - Fresh agreement dated September 1998 ("agreement") executed - May 1999, supply of electricity to respondent's factory discontinued at respondent's request - Demand for minimum charges by appellant as per cl. 17(ii) of Regulations - Writ Petition challenging demand - HC quashed demand - Whether respondent, liable to pay minimum charges - Contention by appellants that HC committed an error by entertaining writ petition ignoring alternative remedy of arbitration in terms of agreement - Further with execution of fresh agreement respondent lost its status as an old consumer and therefore liable to pay minimum charges - Held, respondent cannot be non-suited on the ground of availability of alternative remedy of arbitration as no such argument made before HC - As per conjoint reading of agreement and Regulations declaration/agreement required by customer for taking electrical energy for minimum period of two years - HC failed to consider the fact that respondent executed fresh agreement for reduced load therefore period of 2 years commenced from date of fresh agreement - Per regulations if supply of electricity is disconnected at instance of consumer, latter liable to pay minimum charges for remaining period of agreement or six months, whichever is less - Therefore, in terms of agreement and Regulations, respondent liable to pay minimum charges - Reasons assigned by HC for quashing demand was ex facie untenable, hence impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
