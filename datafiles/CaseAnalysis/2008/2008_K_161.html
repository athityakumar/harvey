Telecom District Manager and Others v Keshab Deb

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 809; (2008) 8 SCC 402; 2008 (4) AWC 3365; 2008 (118) FLR 376; JT 2008 (7) SC 257; 2009 (1) LLJ 733; 2008 (3) LLN 676; 2008(9) SCALE 466; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Controlled, Police Act

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Held, where an employee maintains a writ petition not only on the ground of violation of equality clause enshrines under art. 14 of the Constitution of India but also on the ground of violation of the provisions of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, he has an option to choose his own forum - Tribunal and consequently the High Court were correct that the termination of the services of the respondent was illegal - Grant of compensation in stead of a direction of reinstatement with back wages would meet the ends of justice - Respondent is directed to be paid a compensation of Rs.1, 50, 000/- - Appeal disposed of.
