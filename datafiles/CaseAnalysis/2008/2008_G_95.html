Godrej Pacific Technology Limited v Computer Joint India Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1194; (2008) 11 SCC 108; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 591; 2008 (228) E.L.T. 507; 2008 (3) JCC 2010; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2238; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 897; 2008(10) SCALE 648; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 571; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 4

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Re-Examination

Summary: CrPC, 1973, s. 311 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 60, 64 and 91 - Witness - Re-examination - (A) Appeal challenging order of High Court declining to interfere with the rejection of application seeking re-examination of the witnesses already examined in terms of s. 311 of the Code - Examination in chief of the witness was objected for misleading questions - Statement as to some documents some of which were already proved by other witness - Law states that 'court shall summon and examine or recall and re- examine any such person if his evidence appears to it to be essential to the just decision of the case' - (B) Nature and object of s. 311 of the Code - Held, rejection of the prayer by trial court was not proper and HC should not have declined to interfere - Matter decide in the light of Zahira Sheikh's case - Whereas the word used in the first part is 'may', the second part uses 'shall' so first part gives purely discretionary authority while second part is mandatory ; object is that there may not be failure of justice on account of mistake of either party in bringing the valuable evidence on record or leaving ambiguity in the statements of the witnesses - Appeal Allowed.
