(1) United India Insurance Company Limited; (2) v (1) Manubhai Dharmasinhbhai Gajera and Others; (2)

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 892; (2008) 10 SCC 404; 2008 (2) ACC 718; AIR 2009 SC 446; 2008(5) ALL MR 928; 2008 (6) AWC 6169; 2008 (1) CCC 300; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 43; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2356; JT 2008 (5) SC 457; 2008 (2) KLT 774; 2008(7) SCALE 377; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 778

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Foreign Law, Rate Of Premium, Amount Of Premium, Payment Of Premium, Policy Holder, Third Party Risk, General Insurance Business, Insured Person, Standard Form, Refund Of Premium, Insurance Rules, 1939, Policyholder, Indian Insurance Companies, General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Amendment Act, 2002

Summary: Insurance Act, 1938 - Whether renewal of a mediclaim policy on payment of the amount of premium would be automatic? - Held, if the contract of insurance itself provides for renewal of an insurance policy the same may not mean that the assured has a legal right of automatic renewal - Where a renewal is based on mutual consent, there may be no automatic renewal - Mediclaim policy where a senior citizen will depend upon the contract entered into between the parties and the statutes operating in the field as also constitutional scheme - Private player in the field is only bound by the statutory regulations operating in the field, the public sector insurance companies are also bound by the directions issued by the General Insurance Corporation as also the Central Government - Renewal of a medi-claim policy subject to just exceptions should ordinarily be made, but the same does not mean that the renewal is automatic - Keeping in view the terms and conditions of the prospectus and the insurance policy, the parties are not required to go into all the formalities; very fact that the policy contemplates terms for renewal, subject of course to payment of requisite premium, the same cannot be placed at par with a case of first contract - Appeals dismissed.
