Imagic Creative Private Limited v Commissioner of Commercial Taxes

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 739; (2008) 2 SCC 614; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1387; 2008 (1) CTC 843; JT 2008 (1) SC 496; 2008(1) SCALE 356; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 457; 2008 (9) S.T.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: VAT

Keywords: Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957, Kerala Sales Tax Act, Parliamentary and a Legislative Act

Summary: VAT - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 12 - Finance Act, 1994 - Karnataka Value Added Tax Act, 2003 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957, r. 3 - Kerala Sales Tax Act, s. 24(1) - Whether charges collected towards services for evolution of prototype conceptual design (i.e. creation of concept), on which service tax had been paid under Finance Act, 1994 as amended from time to time is liable to tax under Karnataka Value Added Tax Act, 2003? - Held, test for deciding whether a contract falls into one category or other is to as what is 'the substance of contract' - Payments of service tax as also VAT are mutually exclusive - Therefore, they should be held to be applicable having regard to respective parameters of service tax and sales tax as envisaged in a composite contract as distinguished from an indivisible contract - It may consist of different elements providing for attracting different nature of levy - It is difficult to hold that in a case of this nature, sales tax would be payable on value of entire contract, irrespective of element of service provided approach of assessing authority, thus, appears to be correct - Appeal allowed.
