Kanpur Electricity Supply Company Limited v Shamim Mirza

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1739; (2009) 1 SCC 20; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 70; AIR 2009 SC 638; JT 2008 (12) SC 264; 2008(14) SCALE 604; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 850; 2009 (3) SLR 361; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 563

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Appeal against order bywhich appellant had been directed to reinstate the respective respondents, the two workmen, with full back wages and continuity in service - Held, burden to prove that a claimant was in the employment of a particular management, primarily lies on the person who claims to be so but the degree of proof, so required, varies from case to case - It is essentially a question of fact to be determined by having regard to the cumulative effect of the entire material placed before the adjudicatory forum by the claimant and the management - Courts below were justified in holding that both the workmen have established their claim of having worked with the appellant for more than 240 days as their employees - Orders of the High Court to the extent they affirm the directions of the adjudicatory authority with regard to the payment of back wages are set aside - Appeals partly allowed.
