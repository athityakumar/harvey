Dhariwal Tobaco Products Limited and Others v Sate of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2003; (2009) 2 SCC 370; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 806; AIR 2009 SC 1032; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 234; 2009 (2) CGLJ 416; 2009 CRLJ 974; 2009 (1) FAC 1; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 710; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 677; 2008(16) SCALE 246; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 844; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 109

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Duty Of High Court

Summary: Cr.P.C - Whether an application under s. 482 can be dismissed only on the premise that an alternative remedy of filing a revision application under s. 397 is available? - Held, only because a revision petition is maintainable, the same by itself, would not constitute a bar for entertaining an application under s. 482 - Inherent power of the High Court is not conferred by statute but has merely been saved thereunder - It is, thus, difficult to conceive that the jurisdiction of the High Court would be held to be barred only because the revisional jurisdiction could also be availed of - Appeal allowed.
