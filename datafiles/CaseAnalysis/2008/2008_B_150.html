G. Jayashree & Ors. v Bhagwandas S. Patel & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2023; (2009) 3 SCC 141; AIR 2009 SC 1749; JT 2009 (2) SC 71; 2009 (108) RD 29; 2009(1) SCALE 239; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1454

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Promisee, Residential Purpose, Time-Limit, Novation Of Contract, Joint Promisees, Right To Sue For Specific Performance

Summary: Suit for grant of decree for specific performance of contract - Appeal against dismissal of suit - Held, Plaintiff did not choose to file a suit for specific performance of contract at the first instance speaks volumes about his conduct - Civil courts, in the matter of enforcement of an agreement to sell, exercise a discretionary jurisdiction - Discretionary jurisdiction albeit must be exercised judiciously and not arbitrarily or capriciously - Refused to interfere with the discretionary jurisdiction exercised by the High Court particularly when the trial court had not adverted to this aspect of the matter at all - Appeals dismissed.
