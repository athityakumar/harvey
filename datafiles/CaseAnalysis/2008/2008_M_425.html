Vaniyankandy Bhaskaran v Mooliyil Padinhjarekandy Sheela

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1623; (2008) 17 SCC 1; (2008) 10 SCC 491; AIR 2009 SC 250; 2008 (73) ALR 902; 2008 (4) AWC 4022; JT 2008 (11) SC 325; 2008 (4) KLT 557; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1373; 2009 (106) RD 715; 2008(13) SCALE 531; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 21 rr. 104 and 101 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 103 - Execution proceedings - Pendency of another Suit - Respondent filed suit for recovery of the suit property on the strength of the conveyance executed in her favour - Said suit was decreed in her favour - Respondent filed execution petition seeking possession of suit property - Appellant filed suit and prayed for injunction to prevent eviction in Execution Proceedings - Injunction granted in favour of Appellant - Respondent challenged said order before HC contending that her title to property and eviction order had already been upheld - HC set aside the injunction granted by the trial court and held that appellant filed suit for specific performance to restrain respondent from enjoying benefits of decree obtained by her - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that even though execution was filed prior to suit for specific performance but order u/rr. 97 and 98 were passed subsequently and the Executing Court ought to have waited for the outcome of the said suit - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, suit filed by the appellant for specific performance of contract was considerably later in point of time than the commencement of the execution proceedings - The language of r. 104 of CPC was clear and unambiguous that any order made u/rr. 101 or 103 would be subject to the result of a suit pending on the date of commencement of the proceeding in which orders were made u/rr. 101 or 103 of CPC - Since the appellant's suit was filed long after the commencement of the execution proceedings, the provisions of r. 104 of O. 21 of CPC would not apply to the instant case - Appeal dismissed.
