Barkat Ali and Another v Badri Narain (D) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 145; (2008) 4 SCC 615; AIR 2008 SC 1272; 2008(2) ALL MR 780; 2008 (2) AWC 1939; 2009 (2) CGLJ 489; JT 2008 (2) SC 201; 2008 (1) KLT 1022; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1835; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 117; 2008 (3) RLW 2708; 2008(2) SCALE 255; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attachment Of Property, Process Server

Summary: Decree in a mortgage suit in which an amount of Rs.11, 194.25/- was determined as payable from the date of final decree - Appellants are the legal representatives of the judgment-debtor - Objections to execution application - Single Judge found that objections filed, after warrant of attachment was issued, could not be entertained by the executing Court as the same was barred by principles of constructive res judicata - Appeal against - Held, principles of res judicata not only apply in respect of separate proceedings but the general principles also apply at the subsequent stage of the same proceedings also and the same Court is precluded to go into that question again which has been decided or deemed to have been decided by it at an early stage - Appeal dismissed.
