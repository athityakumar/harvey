State Of Jharkhand & Ors v Atibir Hi-Tech Pvt. Ltd., Giridh And Anr

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2516

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Bihar Electricity Duty Act, 1948, Bihar Electricity Duty Rules, 1949

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Electricity Duty Act, 1948 - Electricity Act, 2003 - Bihar Electricity Duty Rules, 1949, rr. 3, 4 - Refund of electricity duty and surcharge - Maintainability - Respondent-company purchased electricity from corporation for purpose of its industry under agreement executed between them - Respondent sought refund of electricity duty and surcharge paid on footing that it had applied for cancellation of registration which was wrongly rejected by Authority - Respondent pleaded before HC that it applied for registration as assessee u/r. 3 of the Rules and consequently stood registered u/r. 4 of the Rules - Registration Certificate stood issued making it liable to pay duty for distribution and/or consumption of energy as assessee under the 1948 Act - HC concluded that respondent was not liable to pay duty and surcharge and consequently - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether HC judgment should or should not operate prospectively, particularly, when after enactment of the 2003 Act the Regulatory Regime had come into force - Held, with enactment of the 2003 Act, nature of dispute had undergone structural change particularly in view of fact that under the 2003 Act, entire scheme brought in new concepts like deemed licensee - In view point needed to be considered afresh by HC keeping in mind change in law in view of the 2003 Act - It was made clear that till HC decided the matter respondent should continue to pay duty/surcharge under the 1948 Act - It might be clarified that respondent was directed to pay the taxes under the said 1948 Act as primary question involved was not on quantum of liability - Hence it was made clear that HC should consider whether its judgment should or should not operate prospectively, particularly, when after the enactment of the 2003 Act Regulatory Regime had come into force - Appeal disposed of.
