V.K. Jain v High Court Of Delhi Through R.G. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2687; (2008) 17 SCC 538; 2009(12) SCALE 192; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 907

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Respectable, Strictures

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, and 468 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w. 13(1)(d) - Expunging of disparaging remarks - The Respondent no. 3 (NRI) charge-sheeted by the CBI u/s. 120-B r/w. ss. 420, 467, and 468 of IPC and u/ss. 13(2) r/w. 13(1)(d) of 1988 Act - The Respondent no. 3 was granted bail and permission given to go to Hong Kong after depositing the passports of respondent nos. 4 and 5 (mother and wife of respondent no. 3) - Respondent nos. 4 and 5 filed application for releasing their passports - Said application was rejected by the trial court - HC allowed petition filed against said order after giving unjustified and unnecessary remarks to the judgment of the appellant (Judicial Officer of the Trial Court) - The Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that the HC was not justified in passing totally unmerited and undeserved strictures and remarks against him and prayed for expunging the remarks so made - Whether HC was justified in passing certain remarks against the appellant? - Held, the appellant had followed the previous orders passed by different Benches of the HC - As a Subordinate Judge, he was duty bound to follow the orders of the HC - There was no justification in passing any imputations, remarks or strictures against the appellant for passing an order in terms of earlier orders of the HC - Assuming that the order passed by the appellant was wrong or erroneous, even then the HC ought to have either modified or set aside the order, but the HC was not justified in passing totally unmerited, derogatory, harsh and castigating remarks against the appellant - Criticism by superior courts must be in a language of utmost restraint - Judicial decorum to be maintained at all times and even where criticism was justified - The superior courts should always keep in mind that disparaging and derogatory remarks against the judicial officer would cause incalculable harm of a permanent character having the potentiality of spoiling the judicial career of the concerned officer - Even if those remarks are expunged, it would not completely restore the harmed judge from the loss of dignity and honour suffered by him - The impugned order of the HC was set aside to the extent of expunging the remarks made against the appellant in the order - Appeal allowed.
