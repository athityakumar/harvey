Ram Avadh and Others v Ram Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 995; (2008) 8 SCC 58; 2008 (6) ADJ 218; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 275; 2008 (3) AWC 3112; 2009 (106) RD 625; 2008(8) SCALE 249; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953

Summary: U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, s. 9(2) - Order holding that sale deed in favour of appellants is illegal - Appeal against - Held, s. 20 provides that where the person is recorded as an occupant of any land in Khasra Khatauni for 1356 Fasli, which has been taken as the base year, he shall be entitled to retain possession thereof - If the entry was not challenged, it could not be doubted and have to be deemed to be correct in view of explanation III to s. 20 which provide that the entries in the year 1356 Fasli is final and confers all rights on occupant - It was not open to the Assistant Director Consolidation, whose order was affirmed by the High Court in the impugned judgment, to interfere with the concurrent findings of fact arrived at by the Consolidation Officer as also the Settlement Officer, Consolidation - Appeal allowed.
