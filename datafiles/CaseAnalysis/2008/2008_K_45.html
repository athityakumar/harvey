Central Bureau ofInvestigation v K.M. Sharan

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 944; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 430; (2008) 4 SCC 471; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1503; 2008 CRLJ 2027; 2008 (2) CTC 319; JT 2008 (2) SC 595; 2008(3) SCALE 1; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Sale Price, Power Of The Court, Deem, Ante-Dated, Cash Memo, Post Dated Cheque

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B r/w s. 193 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of FIR and charge sheet by High Court - Whether ingredients of offence u/s. 120B r/w s. 193 made out, so as to sustain FIR and chargesheet? - The powers possessed by the HC u/s. 482 of the Code are very wide and the very plenitude of the power requires great caution in its exercise - The Court must be careful to see that its decision in exercise of this power is based on sound principles - The inherent power should not be exercised to stifle a legitimate prosecution - The HC should normally refrain from giving a prima facie decision in a case where all the facts are incomplete and hazy, more so, when the evidence has not been collected and produced before the Court and the issues involved, whether factual or legal, are of such magnitude that they cannot be seen in their true perspective without sufficient material - Of course, no hard and fast rule can be laid down in regard to cases in which the HC ought to exercise its extraordinary jurisdiction of quashing the proceedings at any stage - Held, Court perused prima facie material on record, relevant paras of charge sheet for purpose of adjudging and evaluating as to whether HC was justified in quashing FIR and charge sheet - Ingredients of ss. 193 and 120-B, IPC clearly made out setting out a cognizable offence justifying registration of a case and investigation thereon - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
