Sethi Auto Service Station and Another v Delhi Development Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1648; (2009) 1 SCC 180; AIR 2009 SC 904; JT 2008 (11) SC 520; 2008(13) SCALE 783; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 598

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Resitement of petrol outlets and gas godowns - Whether the recommendation of the Technical Committee vide minutes dated 17-05-2002 for resitement of appellants petrol pumps constitutes an order/decision binding on the Delhi Development Authority? - Held, notings in a departmental file do not have the sanction of law to be an effective order - A noting by an officer is an expression of his viewpoint on the subject - It is no more than an opinion by an officer for internal use and consideration of the other officials of the department and for the benefit of the final decision-making authority - Notings in the file culminate into an executable order, affecting the rights of the parties, only when it reaches the final decision-making authority in the department; gets his approval and the final order is communicated to the person concerned - Under the new policy resitement of a petrol pump etc. is possible only when the existing petrol pump is utilized for a planned project/scheme, which directly necessitates the closing down of the petrol pump - Appeal dismissed.
