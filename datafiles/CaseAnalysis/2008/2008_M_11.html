Commissioner Of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Gurdaspur Distillery

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2147; 2008 (224) E.L.T. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11 A and 35 L(b) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether Methane gas classifiable under Chap. Heading 2711 29 specified in sch. to Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 is liable to excise duty when consumed captively and not marketed? - An article does not become liable to excise duty merely because of its specification in schedule to Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 unless it is salable and known to market - Held, Revenue failed to prove that goods in question are marketable - In absence of same, it cannot be held that Methane gas was marketable and consequently excise duty was exigible - Appeal dismissed.
