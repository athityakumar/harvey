(1) Deputy Commissioner of Income Tax, Ahmedabad; (2) Commissioner of Income Tax, Baroda v Core Health Care Ltd

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 167; (2008) 2 SCC 465; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1338; 2008 (105) CutLT 433; [2008] 298 ITR 194; JT 2008 (2) SC 367; 2008(2) SCALE 327; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 662; [2008] 167 TAXMAN 206

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Borrowed Capital, Depreciation Allowance, Development Rebate, Investment Allowance, Income Tax Act, 1961, Appeals Disposed Of, Revised Return, Actual Cost, Profits And Gains, Profits And Gains Of Business, Substantial Question, Enduring Benefit, New Machinery, Borrowing, Deduction Of Interest, Balancing Charge, Finance Act, 1986

Summary: Income-tax Act, 1961 - Assessee claimed deduction towards expenses aggregating to Rs.2, 12, 05, 459/- which included interest on borrowings of Rs.1, 56, 76, 000/- - Whether interest paid in respect of borrowings on capital assets not put to use in the concerned financial year can be permitted as allowable deduction under s. 36(1)(iii)? - Held, an assessee is entitled to claim interest paid on borrowed capital provided that capital is used for business purpose irrespective of what may be the result of using the capital which the assessee has borrowed - A.O. was not justified in making disallowance of Rs.1, 56, 76, 000/- in respect of borrowings utilized for purchase of machines - Appeals disposed of.
