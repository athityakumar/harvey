Leelabai Gajanan Pansare and Others v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1350; (2008) 9 SCC 720; AIR 2009 SC 523; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 774; JT 2008 (9) SC 551; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 262; 2008 (2) RCR(Rent) 299; 2008(11) SCALE 391; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 248

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Tenancy Rights, Chamber Of Commerce, Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (As Amended up to 2000), Government Companies, Paid Up Capital, Companies (Amendment) Act, 2000, Indian Companies Act, 1882, Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1976, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners' Accounts) Act, 1949, Central Laws (Extension to Jammu and Kashmir) Act, 1968, Dilapidated Building, Indian Companies Act, 1866, Jammu and Kashmir (Extension of Laws) Act, 1956, Bombay Housing Board Act, 1948, Development Authority Act, 1999

Summary: Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Whether a Government Company falls within the compendious expression "any public sector undertakings or corporation established by or under any Central or State Act" in s. 3(1)(b)? - Held, statutory corporations, public sector companies and Government companies are merely corporate forms - India's PSUs are in the form of statutory corporations, public sector companies, Government companies and companies in which the public are substantially interested - When the word PSU is mentioned in s. 3(1)(b), the State Legislature is presumed to know the recommendations of the various Parliamentary Committees on PSUs - These entities are basically cash-rich entities - They have positive net asset value - They have positive net worths - S.. 3(1)(b) clearly applies to different categories of tenants all of whom are capable of paying rent at the market rates - OIC, UIC and BPCL and such other Government companies as defined under s. 617 of the Companies Act are not entitled to protection of the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 in view of the provisions of s. 3(1)(b) - Appeals allowed.
