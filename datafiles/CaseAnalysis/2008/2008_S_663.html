State Of Punjab Rep. Through Secretary v Raj Kumar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1303; (2008) 8 SCC 543; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 566; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 699; 2008 CRLJ 4360; 2008 (3) JCC 2134; JT 2008 (9) SC 53; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3136; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 984; 2008(11) SCALE 270; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 1169

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Trial Court convicted the accused based on dying declaration - High Court directed acquittal - Appeal against - Held, once the Court is satisfied that the declaration was true and voluntary, undoubtedly, it can base its conviction without any further corroboration - It cannot be laid down as an absolute rule of law that the dying declaration cannot form the sole basis of conviction unless it is corroborated - Rule requiring corroboration is merely a rule of prudence - Prosecution has been able to establish the accusations - Case would be covered by s. 304 Part II - Appeal partly allowed.
