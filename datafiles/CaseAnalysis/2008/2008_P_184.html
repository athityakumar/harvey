National Insurance Company Limited v Prema Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 294; (2008) 5 SCC 403; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 627; 2008 (2) ACC 1; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1631; 2008 (71) ALR 297; 2008 (2) AWC 1114; 2008 (1) CCC 126; 2008 (2) CTC 785; JT 2008 (3) SC 320; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 710; 2008(3) SCALE 393; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Legislative Intent, Public Service Vehicle, Gratuitous Passenger, Owner Of Goods, Third Party Risks, Carried For Hire Or Reward, Individual Passenger, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Motor Accident - Stand of appellant was that owner of the goods carriage had not taken any policy for a gratuitous passenger and there was no requirement under law for obtaining a policy for passenger - Held, provisions of the Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on the owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger travelling in a goods carriage and the insurer would have no liability therefore - Impugned order of the High Court is not sustainable and is set aside - It is open to the claimant to recover the amount awarded from the owners of the offending vehicles - Appeal allowed.
