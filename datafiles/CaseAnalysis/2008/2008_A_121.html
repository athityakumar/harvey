Amol Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 862; (2008) 5 SCC 468; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 637; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2323; 2008 (2) JCC 1412; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 478; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 602; 2008(8) SCALE 63; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 956

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Matchbox

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Conviction and sentence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC, 1860 - Hence appeal - Variance in dying declarations - Held, it is not the plurality of the dying declarations but the reliability thereof that adds weight to the prosecution case - If a dying declaration is found to be voluntary, reliable and made in fit mental condition, it can be relied upon without any corroboration - Statement should be consistent throughout - Dying declaration scribed by the Executive Officer, at about 0435 hours in the same night was not in conformity with the FIR and the earlier dying declaration scribed by ASI in so far as different motives have been described; other discrepancy was with regard to the manner in which she is supposed to have been sprinkled with kerosene and thereafter set on fire, therefore, the discrepancies, make the last declaration doubtful; nature of the inconsistencies is such that there are certainly material - Appeal allowed.
