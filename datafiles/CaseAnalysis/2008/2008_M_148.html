H.P. State Forest Company Ltd. v M/S. United India Insurance Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1978; 2009 (1) ACC 137; AIR 2009 SC 1407; 2009 (2) ALD(SC) 33; 2009 (74) ALR 509; 2009 (1) AWC 818; 2009 (1) CompLJ 231; 2009 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2008 (13) SC 66; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 703B; 2009(1) SCALE 216; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cover Note

Summary: Insurance - Complaint dismissed holding that complaint could not be entertained as it was time barred having been brought before the Commission after the expiry of the period fixed by Clause 6(ii) of the Insurance Policy - Held, as on the date of the flood, there was no insurance policy in existence or any commitment on behalf of the respondent to make the payment under the policy - Even accepting the case of the appellant at its very best that the period of limitation would be 3 years under s. 44 of the Limitation Act, the complaint would, even then, be beyond time, having been filed in April 1994 - No case for interference is made out - Appeal dismissed.
