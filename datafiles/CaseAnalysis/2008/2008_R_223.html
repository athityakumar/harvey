Gyatri and Others v Ranjit Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1179; (2008) 4 SCC 241; 2008 (2) CLR 713; 2008 (118) FLR 411; 2008 (2) LLJ 629; 2008 (2) LLN 627; 2008(4) SCALE 105; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1115

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preliminary Order, U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, Tenure-Holder

Summary: High Court after considering writ petition held that the Additional Sessions Judge has gone wrong in quashing the proceedings under s. 145 as well as the order of attachment and supurdaginama - Appeal against - Held, when the proceedings have already been taken under the U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953 and the order has already been passed by the Assistant Consolidation Officer which was brought to the notice of S.D.M - Two parallel proceedings cannot be continued - Appeal allowed.
