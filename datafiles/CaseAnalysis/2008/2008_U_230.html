Ram Babu Tiwari v United India Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2038; (2008) 8 SCC 165; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 442; 2008 (3) ACC 776; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1264; 2008(5) ALL MR 906; 2008 (72) ALR 909; 2008 (4) AWC 3201; 2008 (106) CutLT 700; 2009 (1) G.L.R. 545; JT 2008 (8) SC 454; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 1056; 2009 (1) MPLJ 292; 2008 (152) PLR 138; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 912; 2008(11) SCALE 85; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 725

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Use Of Vehicle, Licensed Driver, Learners Licence

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss.15, 147, 149(2), 166, 173 - Accident - Renewal of license - Compensation - Maintainability - Deceased along with others were travelling by truck met with an accident - Claim for compensation was raised by dependents of deceased before Tribunal which was dismissed - Aggrieved by same appeals were preferred by respondents before HC which were allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether insurer could avoid liability by envisaging defence u/ss. 96(2), 149(2) of the Act as ordered by HC was maintainable - Held, if accident took place within aforementioned period, driver could be held to be possessing a valid license - However, s. 15(1) of the Act, clearly stated that driving license should be renewed w.e.f date of its renewal in event application for renewal of a license was made more than 30 days after date of its expiry - Therefore, it was evident that on renewal of license on such terms, driver of vehicle could not be said to be holding a valid license, insurer would not be liable to indemnify insured - Appeal dismissed.
