State Of Maharashtra & Ors v Reshma Ramesh Meher & Anr

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1055; (2008) 8 SCC 664; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 474; 2008 (6) AWC 6237; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 258; JT 2008 (8) SC 258; 2009 (1) LLJ 553; 2008(10) SCALE 268; 2008 (6) SLR 417

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Post of Clerk under the reserved category of Scheduled Tribes - Termination 0f respondents because of cancellation of caste certificates - Whether the protection granted under Government Resolution dated 15th June, 1995 declaring "Mangela Koli" caste to be a "special backward category", entitled to all special concessions, with effect from 7th December, 1994, which were being enjoyed by other notified castes and tribes, would be available to the respondents? - Held, High Court was justified in holding that as on 7th December, 1994, the respondents were deemed to be in service and, therefore Government Resolution dated 15th June, 1995 was clearly applicable in their cases - Entitlement of an employee to get reinstated does not necessarily result in payment of full or partial back- wages, which is independent of reinstatement - Non-disclosure of the Caste Scrutiny Committee's Report by respondents before the Tribunal is tantamount to suppression of material and vital information from the Court, bordering fraud - Set aside the order of the High Court to the extent it directs treatment of respondents' absence from the date of termination to the date of reinstatement as extra-ordinary leave - Appeal partly allowed.
