P. S. Gopinathan v State Of Kerala And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2543; (2008) 7 SCC 70; AIR 2008 SC 2768; 2008 (5) ESC 729; 2008 (3) LLN 665; 2008(7) SCALE 564; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, Kerala State Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1961

Summary: Service - Advocate & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 233(b) - Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, r. 6 - Re-fixing of seniority - Date of appointment - Appellant was appointed on the post of District and Sessions Judge by the Governor in exercise of the power conferred by clause (1) of art. 233 of the Constitution - A posting order was issued by HC which specified that the appellant was being posted as Additional District Judge on temporary basis without probationary rights in the category of District Judges and his seniority in the category of District Judges will be determined on a later date - Appellant took charge of the post without any demur or objection - Later on, a fresh panel for the transfer/promotion was prepared by the HC which was approved by the Governor and a fresh appointment order issued to appellant - Further, on the basis of the office memorandum of HC, seniority of the appellant was shown below the respondent no. 6 - Writ petition filed challenging the same was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether order passed by HC justified? - Held, for the purposes of ascertaining the seniority of an officer, the date of the order of his first appointment will have a relevant consideration under the rules - Thus, apparently when the order was issued by the Governor, it would be the first appointment for the determination of the seniority of the appellant - However, in the present case, in view of the fact that the Governor issued an order even when previous order was in existence, it is apparent that the appointing authority treated the first order as an order of appointment on temporary basis - Act and action of the appellant in accepting his appointment as temporary one amounts to his assent to the temporary appointment - Facts of present case, clearly make out an acquiescence of the appellant of accepting first order being treated as temporary appointment order on the post of District and Sessions Judge and he cannot now be permitted to change his position and claim the permanent appointment from first order to claim seniority on the post - HC rightly held that in the absence of the challenge to the second appointment order from the fresh panel, that order will stand, though later in time, and has to be given effect to as an order of appointing the appellant on permanent basis u/r. 6 of the Rules - Appeal dismissed.
