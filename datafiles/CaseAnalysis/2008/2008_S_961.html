B. Nagabhushanam v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 988; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 61; (2008) 5 SCC 730; 2008 (4) ACC 728; AIR 2008 SC 2557; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1994; 2008 (4) CalLT(SC) 64; 2008 (2) JCC 1423; JT 2008 (6) SC 568; 2008 (5) KarLJ 157; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 591; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 50; 2008 (3) RLW 2281; 2008(7) SCALE 716; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 279, 304A - Appeal against sentence - Held, reliance by the appellant on the deposition of one of the prosecution witnesses that the bus was being driven at a speed of 15-20 kilometers per hour, has rightly not been accepted - Concurrent finding of fact that the bus was being driven rashly and negligently - Appeal dismissed.
