Rajendran And Others v Shankar Sundaram And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 127; (2008) 2 SCC 724; AIR 2008 SC 1170; 2008(2) ALL MR 797; 2008 (71) ALR 147; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 152; JT 2008 (1) SC 666; 2008 (3) MahLJ 547; 2008 (2) MPLJ 495; 2008 (150) PLR 267; 2008 (151) PLR 637; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 891; 2008 (104) RD 554; 2008(2) SCALE 119; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 209; 2008 (1) UC 385

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, O. 38 r. 5 - Suit for realisation of a sum of Rs.70, 30, 000/- with interest @ 20% per annum inter alia alleging that all the defendants were jointly and severally liable - Interim order calling upon the defendants to execute a security - Appeal against - Held, amount of loan was advanced by a cheque - Cheque was drawn in the name of the partnership firm - Appellants were the partners thereof at the relevant time - Prima facie the liability of the appellants could not have been ignored - As the appellants are not seriously prejudiced if they furnish the security, this is not a fit case where this Court should exercise its jurisdiction under art. 136 - Appeal dismissed.
