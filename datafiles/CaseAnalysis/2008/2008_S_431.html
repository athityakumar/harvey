National Small Industries Corporation Limited v State (Nct Of Delhi) And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1754; (2009) 1 SCC 407; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 513; AIR 2009 SC 1284; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 463; [2009] 147 Comp Cas 11; 2009 CRLJ 1299; 2008 (154) DLT 669; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2109; 2009 (1) JCC (NI) 1; JT 2008 (13) SC 428; 2008 (4) KLT 798; 2009 (3) MPLJ 600; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 10; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 181; 2008(14) SCALE 433; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Negotiable instruments Act, s. 138; Cr.P.C, s. 200 - Where a complaint in regard to dishonour of a cheque is made by a Government company, represented by its officer who is a public servant, whether the exemption made under clause (a) of the proviso to s. 200 of Code of Criminal Procedure, is available? - Held, where an incorporeal body is the payee and the employee who represents such incorporeal body in the complaint is a public servant, he being the de facto complainant, clause (a) of the proviso to s. 200 of the Code will be attracted and consequently, the Magistrate need not examine the complainant and the witnesses - Appeals allowed.
