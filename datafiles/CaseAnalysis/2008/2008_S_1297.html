Arumugam v State, Represented By Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1620; (2008) 15 SCC 590; AIR 2009 SC 331; JT 2008 (11) SC 331; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 48; 2008(13) SCALE 487; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 309

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 Part I and II, 300 Exception 4 - (A) Availability of benefit of Exception 4 to s. 300 - Where the offender takes undue advantage or has acted in a cruel or unusual manner - Accused along with other two co-accuses dragged and inflicted blows on his brother resulting in instantaneous death - Held, benefit cannot be given - (B) Conviction u/s. 302 - Appropriateness of conviction - Considering the factual background on the touchstone of the legal position held that appropriate conviction would be u/s. 304 Part I IPC instead of s. 302 IPC and custodial sentence of 10 years would meet the ends of justice - Appeal Allowed.
