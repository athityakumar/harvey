Parents Association Of Students v M. A. Khan And Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2588; (2009) 2 SCC 641; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1138; 2009 (2) AWC 1354; JT 2009 (1) SC 128; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 922; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 512; 2008(16) SCALE 417; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Education - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 19 - Appellant is an association of Parents of the Wards who have taken admission in different professional colleges including medical colleges, filed writ petition for a direction upon the State of U.P. to regulate professional education so far as it, inter alia, relates to fixation of fee - Single Judge having regard to the SC decision in T.M.A. Pai Foundation vs. State of Karnataka 2002 INDLAW SC 1375 while disposing of the writ petition directed the authorities to ensure and stick on the fair statement made on behalf of the respondents - Clarification as regards interpretation of various directions made in T.M.A. Pai Foundation fell for consideration in Islamic Academy of Education wherein directions given in TM.A Pai Foundation was reiterated - Subsequently, State Government issued a Government order dt. 7-09-2006 permitting the Medical College run by Respondent No.1 to collect certain amount of fees for admission - Appellant filed contempt petition against respondents contending that by issuance of the said order dt. 7-09-2006 state had committed contempt of court - Single Judge passed an interim order and stayed operation of the disputed government order - Special appeal under Letters Patent of HC - Dispute arose over maintainability of the special appeal - DB held the special appeal maintainable - Hence, present appeal - Whether a Special Appeal from an interim order passed by the court in exercise of its contempt jurisdiction is maintainable? - Held, matter pending before three judge bench - Interim order passed by single judge disallowed to continue - DB's order also quashed - Single judge directed to consider the merit of contempt matter only after disposal of the writ petition - Appeal disposed of.
