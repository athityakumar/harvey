Yasha Overseas and Others v Commissioner of Sales Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1243; (2008) 8 SCC 681; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 7; 2008(7) SCALE 117; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 919; [2008] 17 VST 182

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, Imports (Control) Order, 1955, Sales Tax (Forty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947 - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992 - Delhi Sales Tax Act, 1975, s. 2(g) - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366(29A) - Imports (Control) Order, 1955 - (A) Whether decision in Vikas Sales Corporation v. Commr. of Commercial Taxes, can be said to be impliedly overruled by Constitution Bench decision in Sunrise Associates v. Govt. of NCT of Delhi? - Held, in Vikas it was noticed at outset that High Courts' judgments were influenced mainly by decision in Anraj - Ratio of said decision fully supports contention of States herein. As rightly pointed out by Karnataka HC, content of REP Licence/Exim Scrip is far more substantial and real than that of a lottery ticket - If lottery tickets are goods, there is no reason why these licences/scrips are not goods - Court also rejected submission that REP licence was in nature of an actionable claim and was, therefore, expressly excluded from definition of 'goods' in Sales Tax Acts - Decision in Vikas was rendered by a three-judge Bench appeal was referred to a Constitution Bench. Here it is important to bear in mind that decision in Vikas dealing with question of taxability of REP licences was not subject of reference and Vikas came under reference only to extent it approved Anraj - Having thus laid out statutory framework decision in sunrise examined dealers' contention that a lottery ticket was only a slip of paper or memoranda evidencing right of holder to share in prize or distributable funds and was merely a convenient mode for ascertaining identity of winner - (B) REP license/Exim Scrip - Taxability - Relied on the decision of Ansraj - If lottery tickets are goods subject to Sales Tax, REP License/Exim Scrip is far more substantial and real, are goods with taxability - (C) REP Licences/Exim Scrips - Whether they are in the nature of actionable claims? - REP Licences/Exim Scrips are being bought and sold freely in the market as goods - They have value their own unrelated to the goods imported - Therefore, REP Licences/Exim Scrips are not in the nature of actionable claims - Appeal dismissed.
