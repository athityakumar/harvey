Narayanamurthy v State of Karnataka and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 992; (2008) 16 SCC 512; AIR 2008 SC 2377; 2008 (2) JCC 1372; JT 2008 (6) SC 466; 2008 (6) KarLJ 177; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 969; 2008(7) SCALE 673; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 403

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ill- Treatment

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 498A, 304B - Appeal against conviction and sentence imposed by High Court - Held, there is practically no evidence to show that there was any cruelty or harassment for or in connection with the demand of dowry - Trial Judge has appreciated the evidence of PWs.-1, 2 and 3 in its right perspective and concluded that the evidence of these star witnesses has not established that the deceased was being ever harassed or ill-treated by the accused for bringing inadequate and insufficient dowry at the time of her marriage with A-1 or that the accused ever demanded dowry articles from the parents of the deceased before she committed suicide - Appeal allowed.
