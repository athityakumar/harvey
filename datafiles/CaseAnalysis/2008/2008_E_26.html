M/S. Goetze (India) Ltd v Employees State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1036; (2008) 8 SCC 705; AIR 2008 SC 3122; 2008 (118) FLR 654; 2008 (3) LLJ 356; 2008 (3) LLN 649; 2009 (1) MPLJ 55; 2008(9) SCALE 799; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Delay In Payment, Liability To Pay Interest, Who Is Liable To Pay

Summary: Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - Delay in making the payment of the contribution - Quantum payable by the payment was worked out by ESI Court - Dispute relating to interest - Held, liability to pay interest is statutory - There is no power of waiver - Question of any compromise or settlement does not really arise - Reference to "no further due" is obviously relatable to the contribution payable and nothing beyond that - Appeal dismissed.
