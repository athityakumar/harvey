Pradip J. Mehta v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1105; (2008) 14 SCC 283; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1788; [2008] 300 ITR 231; JT 2008 (5) SC 614; 2008(6) SCALE 675; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 383; [2008] 169 TAXMAN 454

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Foreign Income, Central Excise, Appeal allowed, Income Tax Act, 1961, Advance Ruling, Non-Resident, Strict Interpretation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Central Excise Tax - Income Tax Act, 1961 ss. 5(1)(c) ,6(6)(a) and 256(1) - Foreign Income - Assessment - Non resident - Challenged - Appellant/assessee during course of his employment, he was posted to work on high seas and paid abroad for many years - Appellant/assessee while filing his return for assessment year 1982-83 claimed status of 'not ordinarily resident in India' as defined in s. 6(6)(a) of the Act and to exclude income accruing outside India u/s. 5(1)(c) of the Act - Respondent/Assessing Officer(AO) by his Order dt.3-9- 1984 refused to grant assessee status of 'not ordinarily resident' for relevant year - Aggrieved, appellant/assessee filed appeal before Respondent/CIT which dismissed appeal - Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal which was dismissed - Appellant filed application before HC u/s. 256(1) of the Act HC refused to grant status of 'not ordinarily resident ' as contemplated u/s. 6(6)(a) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower Court's order was justifiable - Held, relied on K.P. Varghese v. ITO 1981 Indlaw SC 334 , UCO Bank v. CIT, W.B. 1999 Indlaw SC 107 and Collector of Central Excise Vadodra v. Dhiren Chemical Industries 2002 Indlaw SC 1351 relied on - However, in instant case, Circular issued by Board in which opinion of Central Govt. and Ministry of Finance (Department of Revenue) letter No. 4/22/61-IT(AT), dt.29-11- 1961 had been noted, interpretation similar toe one put by various HC on s. 4B had been accepted to be correct position - In these circumstances, person would become ordinarily resident only if (a) he had been residing in nine out of 10 preceding years and (b) he had been in India for at least 730 days in previous seven years - Hence, order passed by HC and Authorities below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
