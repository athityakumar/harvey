Johar And Others v Mangal Prasad And Another

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 123; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 89; (2008) 3 SCC 423; AIR 2008 SC 1165; 2008 CRLJ 1627; JT 2008 (2) SC 112; 2008(2) SCALE 122; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Party, Private Complainant, Re-Examination, By-Standers

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 323 r/w 34 - Trial Court held that accused Nos. 1 to 4 had only caused simple injuries to the deceased provisions of ss. 148 and 149 were not attracted - Revision petition against by complainant allowed by setting judgment of acquittal and the case is remanded to the trial court to pass the judgment on the basis of evidence on record - Appeal against - Held, High Court's jurisdiction to entertain a revision application, although is not barred, but severally restricted, particularly when it arises from a judgment of acquittal - While hearing the matter afresh in terms of the direction of the High Court, Trial Judge would be bound by the observations made therein and thus, would have no option but to convict the appellants - High Court exceeded its jurisdiction in view of the fact that the judgment of the learned Trial Judge could not be termed to be a perverse one - Appeal allowed.
