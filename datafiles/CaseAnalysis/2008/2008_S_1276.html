Dhananjay Malik and Others v State of Uttaranchal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 343; (2008) 4 SCC 171; AIR 2008 SC 1913; 2008 (2) ESC 206; JT 2008 (3) SC 611; 2008 (2) KLT 969; 2008 (3) LLN 57; 2008(3) SCALE 425; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1035; 2008 (3) SLR 792

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, U.P. Subordinate Educational (Trained Graduates Grade) Service Rules, 1983

Summary: U.P. Subordinate Educational (Trained Graduates Grade) Service Rules, 1983 - Service - High Court by the impugned order set aside the entire selection and appointments of Assistant Teachers (Physical Education) in Garhwal Mandal holding that selection and appointments were made in violation of the Rules - Held, having unsuccessfully participated in the process of selection without any demur they are estopped from challenging the selection criterion inter alia that the advertisement and selection with regard to requisite educational qualifications were contrary to the Rules - Government cannot amend or supersede statutory Rules by administrative instructions, but if the rules are silent on any particular point Government can fill up the gaps and supplement the rules and issue instructions not inconsistent with the rules already framed - Appeals disposed of.
