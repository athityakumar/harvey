Hinsa Virodhak Sangh v Mirzapur Moti Kuresh Jamat & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 413; (2008) 5 SCC 33; AIR 2008 SC 1892; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 345; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2501; JT 2008 (3) SC 421; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 751; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 67; 2008(3) SCALE 582; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1020

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constituent Assembly, Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949

Summary: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Closure of municipal slaughter - Division Bench of High Court held that writ petitioners' right to freedom to carry on the trade of slaughtering and selling meat cannot be curtailed or abridged merely at the asking of a particular section of society, or organizations belonging to a particular community merely because the members of that particular community feel that according to their religion people should not eat non-vegetarian food during a particular festival - Appeal against - Held, closure of the slaughter house is only for 9 days and not for a considerable period of time - This decision indicates that the restriction is reasonable - A period of 9 days is a very short time and surely the non-vegetarians can become vegetarians during those 9 days out of respect for the feeling of the Jain community - Impugned resolutions of the Municipal Corporation of Ahmedabad are held to be valid - Appeal allowed.
