Malayala Manorama Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1106; (2008) 12 SCC 612; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1794; 2010 (5) CompLJ 12; [2008] 300 ITR 251; JT 2008 (5) SC 529; 2008(6) SCALE 659; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1990, Net Profits, Widely Held Company

Summary: Banking & Finance - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 35 and 80WA - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 227 and 154 - Finance Act, 1987, ss. 115-J, 80 WA, 211(2) and 205(1)(b) - Whether in respect of a company consistently charging depreciation in its books of account at the rates prescribed in the Income Tax Rules, the ITO has jurisdiction, to rework net profits by substituting the rates prescribed in the Companies Act, 1956? - An assessing authority has to determine the income of the company under the provisions of the Income Tax, the book profit is to be worked out in accordance to s. 115J(1) - While computing the income, the Assessing Officer has only power to examine whether the books of account were certified by the authorities under the Companies Act as having been properly maintained in accordance with the Companies Act - Assessing Officer has limited powers of making increases and reductions - Assessing Officer does not have the jurisdiction to go beyond the net profits shown in the profit and loss account, except to the extent provided in the accounts maintained by the assessee are certified by the auditors - Held, the book adjustment made by the Assessing Officer being contrary to the decision of the Apex Court, it is answered in the negative and in favour of the assessee - Held, impugned order of the High Court is set aside - Appeals are allowed.
