Bachahan Devi & Anr v Nagar Nigam, Gorakhpur & Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 152; (2008) 12 SCC 372; AIR 2008 SC 1282; 2008 (2) ALD(SC) 118; 2008(2) ALL MR 299; 2008 (71) ALR 166; 2008 (2) AWC 1931; 2008 (2) CTC 790; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 303; 2008 (152) PLR 5; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 367; 2008 (104) RD 609; 2008(2) SCALE 224; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 424; 2008 (2) UC 760

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Nuisance, Public Officer

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for declaration that the land in dispute belongs to plaintiff-respondent and defendants have no concern with the property decreed by trial court - Appellate Court remitted the matter to trial court in view of amendment of written statement - High Court held that if the Appellate Court is of the opinion that if the evidence is insufficient to decide the issue, only then the matter may be remitted to the trial Court - Appeal against - Held, First Appellate Court which has the power to analyse the factual position can decide the issue and the additional issues - Use of word 'may' in a statutory provision would not by itself show that the provision is directory in nature - Where the legislature uses two words may and shall in two different parts of the same provision prima facie it would appear that the legislature manifested its intent on to make one part directory and another mandatory - Power of court to find out whether the provision is directory or mandatory remains unimpaired - Appeals dismissed.
