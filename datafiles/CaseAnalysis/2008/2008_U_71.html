Abhijit Ghosh Dastidar v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2549; (2009) 16 SCC 146

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Retrospective promotion – Annual Confidential Report - Re-fixation of pension - Department Promotion Committee (DPC) considered appellant’s claim for promotion and found that appellant/Post Master General was not eligible for promotion to Higher Administrative Grade Group-A - On application filed by appellant, Tribunal directed authority not to take note of ‘order of caution dt.22-9-1997’ and ‘order of adverse remarks dt.9-6-1998’ for period 1-4-1997 to 13-10-1997 while considering appellant for promotion - Appellant in March, 2003, was promoted to Higher Administrative Grade Group-A and later retired from service - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, though benchmark ‘very good’ was required for being considered for promotion, entry of ‘good’ was not communicated to appellant. Entry of 'good' should have communicated to appellant as he was having ‘very good’ in previous year. Non-communication of entries in ACR to appellant would be arbitrary and as such violative of art.14 of Constitution. Respondent had no case that appellant had ever been informed of nature of grading given to him. Officer who was immediately junior in service to appellant was given promotion on 28-8-2000. Appellant was deemed to have been given promotion from 28-8-2000. Since appellant had retired from service, he was not entitled to any pay or allowances for period for which he had not worked in Higher Administrative Grade Group-A, but his retrospective promotion from 28-8-2000 should be considered for benefit of re-fixation of his pension and other retrial benefits as per rules. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Non-communication of entries in ACR of public servant either he is in civil, judicial, police or any other service other than armed forces has civil consequences, because it may affect his chances for promotion or get other benefits.
