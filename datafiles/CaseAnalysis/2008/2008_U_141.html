Bachan Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1115; (2008) 9 SCC 161; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 731; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1362; 2008(5) ALL MR 992; JT 2008 (13) SC 605; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 684; 2008(10) SCALE 184; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 668; 2009 (1) SLR 409

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Court-Martial, Violation Of Good Order And Discipline, Confirmation And Revision

Summary: Army Act, 1950 - Charge that appellant went across the International Border to Post 'DERA' in Pakistan was sentenced to suffer two years imprisonment and also dismissed from service by GCM - Single Bench set aside the order and petitioner is relegated back to the position he had on the date of passing of the order - Held, Division Bench was justified in setting aside order of Single Judge who was not justified in setting aside the well-reasoned order of the GCM which was based upon proper and fair appreciation of the evidence of the material witnesses, statement made voluntarily by the appellant before it, other material and subsequent order of the confirming authority - Appeal dismissed.
