Kailash v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 310; (2008) 14 SCC 51; AIR 2008 SC 1564; 2008 CRLJ 1914; JT 2008 (3) SC 279; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 200; 2008(3) SCALE 338; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Parole Act

Summary: Indisan Penal ode, ss. 147, 148, 341, 452, 24/149, 323, 324/149, 308/149 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 319 - High Court directed Trial Court to re-hear the application filed under s. 319 in revision - Appeal against - Held, merely because Sessions Judge commented upon the present appellant not being a party accused, that by itself did not justify the interference that there was evidence against him and the evidence was of such nature as would justify his being added as an accused much less under s. 319 - Trial Court had properly considered the evidence had found that none of the witnesses had stated that appellant was having an axe in his hand and that he caused any injury with the axe - Appeal allowed.
