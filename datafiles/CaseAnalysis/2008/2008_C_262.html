B.M. Malani v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1570; (2008) 10 SCC 617; [2008] 306 ITR 196; JT 2008 (11) SC 390; 2008(13) SCALE 327; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 63; [2008] 174 TAXMAN 363

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Tax Demand

Summary: Income Tax Act - Application under s. 220 (2-A) dismissed - Appeal against - Waiver of interest - Held, levy of interest although is statutory in nature, inter alia for re-compensating the revenue from loss suffered by non-deposit of tax by the assessee within the time specified therefore - The said principle should also be applied for the purpose of determining as to whether any hardship had been caused or not - A genuine hardship would, inter alia, mean a genuine difficulty - That per se would not lead to a conclusion that a person having large assets would never be in difficulty as he can sell those assets and pay the amount of interest levied - Matter has not been considered by the Commissioner and the High Court in its proper perspective - Matter is remitted to the Commissioner.
