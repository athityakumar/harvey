General Manager, State Bank of India and Others v Anju Jain

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1357; (2008) 8 SCC 475; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 449; 2008(6) ALL MR 442; 2008 (3) CLR 493; 2008 (4) ESC 606; JT 2008 (9) SC 272; 2009 (1) LLJ 319; 2008 (3) LLN 687; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 895; 2009 (2) MPLJ 1; 2008(11) SCALE 647; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 576; 2009 (1) SLR 463

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Appointment on compassionate ground - Held, dependent of an employee who had died or retired on medical ground but whose service record was blemished on account of disciplinary action having been taken against him will not be considered eligible for compassionate appointment in the Bank - Appointment on compassionate ground is never considered a right of a person - When any appointment is to be made in Government or semi-Government or in public office, cases of all eligible candidates must be considered alike - State Bank was right in refusing appointment on compassionate ground to the widow of deceased employee of the Bank - Appeal allowed.
