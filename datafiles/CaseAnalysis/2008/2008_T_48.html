Tamil Nadu Electricity Board & Anr. v Status Spinning Mills Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2619; (2008) 7 SCC 353; AIR 2008 SC 2838; JT 2008 (7) SC 216; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 1267; 2008(8) SCALE 161; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 870

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Revision of Tariff Rates on Supply of Electrical Energy Act, 1978, Tamil Nadu Essential Articles Control and Requisitioning (Temporary Powers) Act, 1949

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Tamil Nadu Revision of Tariff Rates on Supply of Electrical Energy Act, 1978 - Tariff concession - Eligibility to continue - Appellant/State, by its G.O.Ms No. 29 dated 31-1-1995, provided tariff concession for High Tension (HT) industries by amending the Schedule to the 1978 Act - G.O.Ms. No. 29 amended and placed a condition that new HT industries 'set up' on or after 15-2-1997 should not be eligible for concession - Secretary to the Government, by its letter dated 1-8-1997, clarified that words 'set up' would mean 'obtained service connection' from 1st appellant/Board - Respondent/HT industries challenged the amended G.O. and the clarification letter before the HC - Respondents contended that the letter was barred by promissory estoppel and respondents who had 'established' their industry before 15-2-1997 continued to be eligible for HT tariff concession - Single Judge dismissed respondents writ petition - Respondents preferred the appeal before DB - DB allowed the appeal - Whether by reason of the G.O. Ms. No.29 dated 31-1-1995, the respondent/entrepreneurs who had set up new high tension industries after the 15-2-1997 have acquired any right pursuant thereto - Held, amendment was done not pursuant to any representation made by the State to 1st appellant/Board - Concession in tariff was granted by reason of a statutory provision and therefore, such concessions could also be withdrawn - If the appellants have not altered their position pursuant to any promise, the doctrine of promissory estoppel would not apply - Further, the reasonableness of the 1978 Act was not the subject matter of the writ petition before the HC and the related provisions were not sought to be declared ultra vires - When a statute is amended keeping in view the public interest even the concession can be withdrawn with retrospective effect - Matter remitted back to the appropriate authority of 1st appellant/Board - Appeal allowed.
