Atma Linga Reddy & Ors v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2125; (2008) 7 SCC 788; AIR 2009 SC 436; 2008 (106) CutLT 612; JT 2008 (7) SC 601; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 152; 2008(9) SCALE 745; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 741

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Parliament & Legislature; Water

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Water - Electricity & Energy - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 131, 143, 21, 262, 262(2), 32 - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 11 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - PIL - Maintainability - Petitioners filed instant writ petition, restraining respondent No. 2-State and respondent No. 4 from constructing Mini Hydro Power Project at RDS by quashing and cancelling Power Project - Prayer was also made to direct State to regulate water at RDS anicut and to ensure smooth flow of water in RDS canal to extent of full allocated water of 15.9 TMC to State of A.P - By way of interim relief, prayer was made to grant stay against construction of Power Project at RDS - Petitioners contended that respondent No. 2 acted illegally and unlawfully in sanctioning and approving Power Project in favour of respondent No. 4 - Due to said project, sufficient water for drinking purpose and for irrigation facilities was not available to residents and farmers of District - Petitioners in larger interest of public were, therefore, constrained to approach this Hon'ble Court u/art. 32 of Constitution - Respondents raised certain preliminary objections to maintainability of writ petition - Whether petition filed by Petitioners was maintainable - Held, such a dispute was covered by art. 262 of Constitution and should be dealt with in accordance with provisions of the 1956 Act and it could not be challenged in any Court including HC - S. 3 provides for constitution of Tribunal for adjudication by the Central Govt. on a dispute raised or complaint made by any State that interest of State or any of inhabitants thereof had been prejudicially affected or likely to be affected - Therefore, present petition under art. 32 was not maintainable - From relevant provisions of Constitution, Act of 1956 and decisions referred to, there was no doubt in mind that present writ petition u/ art. 32 of Constitution was not maintainable - Further, held that question of construction of Mini Hydel Project, challenged in present proceedings, was very much before Tribunal constituted under Act and matter was sub judice - It also appears that State of AP prayed for interim relief in respect of above issue before Tribunal - One of prayers in injunction was sought restraining State of Karnataka from constructing or proceeding with said project - Tribunal, however, did not grant interim relief - State of A.P. filed another application being Interim Application u/s. 5(3) of the Act before Tribunal for clarification of earlier order but even that application was dismissed observing that no clarification was required - Thus, Matter was very much before Tribunal and it considered various applications and passed orders from time to time - Hence, present petition was liable to be dismissed - Petition dismissed.
