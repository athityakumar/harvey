H.P. State Forest Corporation v Regional Provident Fund Commissioner

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 586; (2008) 5 SCC 756; 2008 (119) FLR 50; JT 2008 (6) SC 203; 2008 (3) LLJ 581; 2009 (1) LLJ 141; 2008 (3) LLN 43; 2008(6) SCALE 124; 2008 (2) UC 1054

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Act, 1964

Summary: Employees Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, s. 7-A - Proceedings for the deposit of the provident fund were initiated by the issuance of a notice - Order of Presiding Officer to remand the case for the re-determination of the contribution with respect to the liability of the appellant Corporation has been maintained - Appeal against - Held, inaction on the part of the Commissioner to initiate proceedings within a reasonable time, has to be deplored - As the Corporation itself submitted that it was covered under the Act and in view of the limited relief granted by the authorities below and by the High Court, disinclined to interfere with the matter - amounts due from the Corporation will be determined only with respect to those employees who are identifiable and whose entitlement can be proved on the evidence and that in the event the record is not available with the Corporation, it would not be obliged to explain its loss, or that any adverse inference be drawn on this score - Appeals dismissed.
