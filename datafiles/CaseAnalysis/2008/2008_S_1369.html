Oriental Bank of Commerce v Sunder Lal Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 22; (2008) 2 SCC 280; AIR 2008 SC 1339; 2008 (1) BC 582; 2008 (1) DRTC 557; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1181; 2008 (151) PLR 136; 2008(1) SCALE 298; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: A direction was issued to the appellant Oriental Bank of Commerce to declare the respondents' account as Non-Performing Asset (NPA) from 31st March, 2000 and to apply the Reserve Bank of India Guidelines to their case and communicate the outstandings which shall be recoverable by quarterly instalments over a period of two years - Appeal against - Held, respondents have not been able to show that there is any statute or rule having the force of law which casts a duty on the appellant bank to declare their account as NPA from 31st March, 2000 and apply R.B.I. guidelines to their case - High Court, erred in issuing a writ of mandamus directing the appellant bank to declare the respondents' account as NPA - Appeal allowed.
