Management, Essorpe Mills Limited v Presiding Officer, Labour Court and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 605; (2008) 7 SCC 594; AIR 2008 SC 2504; 2008 (2) CLR 904; 2008 (118) FLR 1087; JT 2008 (5) SC 452; 2008 (3) LLJ 614; 2008 (2) LLN 761; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 863; 2008(6) SCALE 485; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1149; 2008 (6) SLR 764

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Lock Out, Failure Report, Tamil Nadu Industrial Disputes Rules, 1958, Chennai Industrial Disputes Rules, 1958

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Appeal to challenge order dismissing writ appeals filed to challenge order dismissing petition filed against alteration of punishment - Appellant submitted that High Court failed to appreciate that in the absence of a valid notice of strike in terms of s. 22(1) there can be no commencement of conciliation proceedings in terms of s. 20(1) of the Act and strike notice issued on 14-3-1991 stating that the strike will commence on or after 24-3-1991 i.e. (just 10 days notice) does not satisfy the requirement of advance notice stipulated u/s. 22 (1) - Held, date of notice is 14.3.1991 and the proposed strike was on 24.3.1991, therefore, on the face of it, it cannot be treated to be a notice as contemplated u/s. 22(1)(a) - Expression "giving such notice" as appearing in s. 22(1)(b) refers to the notice u/s. 22(1)(a), therefore, the workmen cannot go on strike within six weeks notice in terms of s. 22(1)(a) and 14 days thereafter in terms of s. 22(1)(b) - Earlier illegal strike is not remedied by a subsequent strike as provided in s. 22 - Appeal disposed of.
