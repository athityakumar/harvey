Awadesh Chandra Agarwal v Divisional Manager, L.I.C. of India

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2300; (2008) 14 SCC 802; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 372; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 849; 2009 (120) FLR 152; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 126; 2009 (2) LLJ 19; 2008(14) SCALE 663

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Award passed, Computation of monetary benefits

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Computation of monetary benefits - Award passed - Sustainability - Labour Court passed an order for computation of monetary benefits amounting to Rs. 9559.88 towards wages - On appeal before HC, HC allowed petition and set aside the order of Labour Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the HC was entitled to interfere with the findings of fact arrived at by the Labour Court while computing the period of work of 240 days of the appellant.

Held, Court directions to respondent to pay 40,000/- to the appellant in lieu of reinstatement and other benefits. In default of payment of the aforesaid sum of Rs.40,000/- to the appellant within the time specified, the order of the HC shall stand set aside and the award passed by the Labour Court shall stand restored. Appeals disposed of.
