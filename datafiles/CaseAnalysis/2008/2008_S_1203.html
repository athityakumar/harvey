Ponusamy v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 611; (2008) 5 SCC 587; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 656; AIR 2008 SC 2110; 2008 CRLJ 2563; 2008 (2) JCC 1221; JT 2008 (5) SC 316; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 101; 2008(6) SCALE 198; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Extra Judicial Confession, Illiterate Lady

Summary: Appeal to challenge order dismissing appeal filed to challenge conviction and sentence for offence u/s. 302 of IPC and also u/s. 201 of IPC - Circumstantial evidence - Held, if his wife was found missing, ordinarily, the husband would search for her; if she has died in an unnatural situation when she was in his company, he is expected to offer an explanation therefore; lack of such explanation on the part of the appellant itself would be a circumstantial evidence against him - Extra judicial confession is evidence of weak nature - Not only an extra judicial confession was made by the appellant before PW-10, the same was also made before PW-11; jewellery which had been put on by the deceased was produced by the appellant; only upon the disclosure made by the appellant that the dead body had been thrown in the canal, a search was made and it was found; dead body was also identified to be that of the deceased - Extra judicial confession was voluntary or truthful; no case has been made out for interference with the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
