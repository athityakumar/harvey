J. C. Budhraja v Chairman, Orissa Mining Corporation Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2610; (2008) 2 SCC 444; AIR 2008 SC 1363; JT 2008 (1) SC 514; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 179; 2008 (2) MPLJ 261; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 934; 2008(1) SCALE 597; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Orissa Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 18 - Arbitral award - Jurisdiction of arbitrator - Respondent entered into an agreement with the contractor - After completion of work contractor signed the final bill on 14.4.1977 under protest - A Departmental Committee considered the claims in 1979 by respondent to scrutinize the pending claims and the payment was made - Thereafter, contractor invoked the Arbitration Agreement in respect of pending claims - Arbitrator was appointed - Arbitrator made a reasoned award holding that the appellant (legal heir of contractor) was entitled to a sum of Rs.1,02,66,901.36 with interest as against the claim of Rs.95,96,616 made by the appellant - Award of the arbitrator was made the rule of the court - On appeal, HC found final bill was signed by the contractor on 14.4.1977 and therefore held that the cause of action for the contractor to make a claim arose on 14.4.1977 and as the notice invoking arbitration was issued on 4.6.1980 and the petition u/s. 8(2) of the Act was filed thereafter, beyond three years from 14.4.1977, the entire claim was barred by limitation - HC further held that the fact that the Departmental Committee considered the claims in 1979, subsequent to the signing of the final bill under protest, did not have the effect of saving/extending limitation in the absence of any acknowledgement in writing - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the claim made before the arbitrator or any part thereof was barred by limitation? - Held, s. 18 of the Limitation Act deals with effect of acknowledgement in writing - What can be acknowledged is a present subsisting liability - An acknowledgment made with reference to a liability, cannot extend limitation for a time barred liability or a claim that was not made at the time of acknowledgment or some other liability relating to other transactions - Any admission of jural relationship in regard to the ascertained sum due or a pending claim, cannot be an acknowledgement for a new additional claim for damages - In the present case, in regard to the pending claims of the contractor, the limitation stood extended by three years from 4.3.1980 - Therefore, claims made before the Arbitrator was within time, having been made within three years from 28.10.1978 and 4.6.1980 - However, remaining claims of the appellant being fresh claims, were not 'pending claims' therefore the said fresh claims made for the first time in the claims statement made on 27.6.1986 were clearly barred by limitation - (B) Whether the award liable to be set aside on the ground of legal misconduct and the error apparent on the face of the award? - Held, arbitrator committed an error apparent on the face of the record and a legal misconduct in holding that the entire claim was within time - Only that part of the claim before the Arbitrator which was part of the claim made by the contractor, that was existing or pending as on 28.10.1978 and 4.3.1980 could have been considered by the Arbitrator - (C) Whether the award liable to be set aside on the ground that the arbitrator exceeded his jurisdiction? - Held, arbitrator by awarding more than what was claimed in the claim statement exceeded his jurisdiction - Arbitrator thus committed a legal misconduct and the award to that extent, is liable to be set aside - Part of the award which is valid and separable upheld - Appeals partly allowed.
