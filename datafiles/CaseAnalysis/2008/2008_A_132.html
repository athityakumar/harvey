Andhra Bank v Andhra Bank Officers and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 828; (2008) 7 SCC 203; AIR 2008 SC 2936; 2009 (1) ALD(SC) 8; JT 2008 (7) SC 331; 2008 (3) LLN 703; 2008(8) SCALE 614; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1125

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; RBI; Service

Keywords: Public Sector Banks, Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (As Amended up to 2000), Andhra Bank (Officers) Service Regulations, 1982

Summary: Interpretation of reg. 26 of the Andhra Bank (Officers) Service Regulations, 1982 framed under s. 19 of the Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1980 - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, it was necessary to consult the Reserve Bank of India by the Central Government before issuing the impugned guidelines? - Held, there cannot be any doubt whatsoever where one authority is required to consult the another, such consultation must be meaningful - It must mean conscious and effective consultation but the same would apply where the consultation is necessary - As for the purpose of issuance of guidelines, no consultation was necessary to be made with the Reserve Bank of India by the Central Government - Appeal allowed.
