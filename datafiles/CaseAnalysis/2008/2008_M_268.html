Mandali Ranganna and Others Etc v T. Ramachandra and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 889; (2008) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC 2291; 2008(4) ALL MR 932; 2008 (71) ALR 826; 2008 (3) AWC 2711; 2008 (4) CTC 360; 2008 (5) KarLJ 613; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 1331; 2008(8) SCALE 277; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 264

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Tribunal had allowed private respondents to make constructions on the lands in suit, subject to the final decision - Hence, appeal - Whether the suit properties were the subject matter of partition purported to have taken place in 1924 or subsequently or not? - Held, respondents had been in possession of the properties in suit for a long time; appellants never exercised any act of possession; documents produced by the respondents substantiate that the property was in exclusive enjoyment of owner being the adopted father of predecessor in interest from 1924 to 1936; various lease deeds were executed and property was developed and the nature and character was changed from time to time - A person who had kept quiet for a long time and allowed another to deal with the properties exclusively, ordinarily would not be entitled to an order of injunction - No interference only because the property is a very valuable one - Appeals dismissed.
