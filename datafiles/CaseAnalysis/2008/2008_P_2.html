(1) Consolidated Engineering Enterprises; (2) Hatti Gold Mines Company Limited v (1) Principal Secretary Irrigation Department and Others; (2) Vinay Heavy Equipments

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 668; (2008) 7 SCC 169; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 396; 2008(6) ALL MR 423; 2008 (3) AWC 2908; 2009 (3) CompLJ 203; 2008 (5) CTC 741; JT 2008 (6) SC 22; 2008 (5) KarLJ 193; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 431; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 897; 2008(6) SCALE 748; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1108; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 111

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Interim Measures, U.P. Sales Tax Rules

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Whether Limitation Act, 1963 is inapplicable to a proceeding in a court, under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996?; Even if Limitation Act, 1963 is applicable, whether applicability of s. 14 of the said Act is excluded to proceedings under s. 34(1) of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996? - Held, s. 5 of the Limitation Act would not be applicable because the applicability of s. 5 of the Limitation Act stands excluded because of the provisions of s. 29(2) of the Limitation Act - Effect of s. 14 is that in order to ascertain what is the date of expiration of the 'prescribed period', the days excluded from operating by way of limitation, have to be added to what is primarily the period of limitation prescribed - Appeals disposed of.
