Mukul Saikia & Ors v State Of Assam & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1791; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 186; (2009) 1 SCC 386; AIR 2009 SC 747; JT 2008 (12) SC 414; 2008(14) SCALE 622; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Assam Public Service Commission (Limitation of Function) Regulation, 1951

Summary: Assam Public Service Commission (Limitation of Function) Regulation, 1951 - High Court held that no posts beyond 27 advertised vacancies could be filled up from the select list - Appeal against - Held, select list prepared by APSC could be used to fill the notified vacancies and not future vacancies - If the requisition and advertisement was only for 27 posts, the State cannot appoint more than the number of posts advertised, even though APSC had prepared a select list of 64 candidates - Selection list got exhausted when all the 27 posts were filled - Candidates below the 27 appointed candidates have no right to claim appointment to any vacancy in regard to which selection was not held - High Court has rightly held that the appellants do not have any enforceable right of being appointed to the post of CDPOs against the quota meant for promotees and more particularly against the decision of the State Government regularizing the services of the private respondents - Appeal dismissed.
