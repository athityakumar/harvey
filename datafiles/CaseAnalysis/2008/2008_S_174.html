Venture Global Engineering v Satyam Computer Services Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 61; (2008) 4 SCC 190; AIR 2008 SC 1061; 2008 (2) ALD(SC) 46; 2008(3) ALL MR 416; 2008 (1) CompLJ 224; 2008 (1) CTC 348; JT 2008 (1) SC 468; 2008 (4) MahLJ 579; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 289; 2008 (3) MPLJ 329; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 12; 2008(1) SCALE 214; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 501; 2008 (2) WLN(SC) 23

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: International Commercial Arbitrations

Summary: Arbitration award directing appellant VGE to transfer the shares to respondent No.1 - High Court dismissed the appeal holding that the award cannot be challenged even if it is against the public policy and in contravention of statutory provisions - Appeal against - Held, provisions of Part I of the Act would apply to all arbitrations including international commercial arbitrations and to all proceedings relating thereto - Where such arbitration is held in India, the provisions of Part-I would compulsorily apply and parties are free to deviate to the extent permitted by the provisions of Part-I - Even in the case of international commercial arbitrations held out of India provisions of Part-I would apply unless the parties by agreement, express or implied, exclude all or any of its provisions - Such an interpretation does not lead to any conflict between any of the provisions of the Act and there is no lacuna as such - S. 11.5 (c) of the SHA would exclude respondent No.1- Satyam Computer Services Ltd. approaching the US Courts in regard to the enforcement of the Award - Part I of the Act is applicable to the Award in question even though it is a foreign Award - Appeal partly allowed.
