Alka Bose v Parmatma Devi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2117; (2009) 2 SCC 582; AIR 2009 SC 1527; 2009(3) ALL MR 447; 2009 (2) AWC 1370; 2008 (6) CTC 509; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 733; 2009 (3) MPLJ 128; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 450; 2008(16) SCALE 298; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Unilateral Contract, Free Consent

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 10 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 15, 16(c), 20 - Suit for specific performance - Challenged - By virtue of written agreement of sale, 'K' had agreed to sell to respondent no. 1 southern portion of house for consideration of Rs.34,500/- - Respondent no. 1 paid sum of Rs.2001/- as earnest money and part payment and further sum of Rs.2000/- to 'K' on a condition that the sale deed would be executed within three months and balance consideration money would be paid at time of execution of sale deed - As 'K' did not execute sale deed, respondent no. 1 instituted suit for specific performance - Trial Court decreed suit against defendant - On appeal, DB allowed appeal and dismissed suit - Hence, instant appeal - Whether an agreement of sale executed only by vendor, and not by purchaser, was valid - Held, agreement of sale signed by vendor alone and delivered to purchaser, and accepted by purchaser, had always been considered to be valid contract - In event of breach by vendor, it could be specifically enforced by purchaser -However, no practice of purchaser alone signing an agreement of sale - Trial Court as well as DB on analysis of materials in form of oral and documentary evidence concluded that vendee had performed her part by paying earnest money and sent notice conveying her willingness and readiness to pay balance of sale consideration - Said notice was acknowledged by defendant - Clauses in agreement clearly show that vendor had to perform and fulfill the terms of agreement by executing sale deed on receipt of the consideration - Trial Court and DB concluded that plaintiff had fulfilled conditions as stated in s. 16(c) of the Act and in that event plaintiff was entitled decree for specific performance which was rightly granted by Trial Court - Thus, agreement of sale was enforceable and Trial Court had rightly granted decree which was affirmed by DB - Appeal dismissed.
