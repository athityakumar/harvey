Kuldeep Singh v Commissioner of Police and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 192; (1999) 2 SCC 10; (1999) SCC (L&S) 429; AIR 1999 SC 677; 1999 (1) CLR 49; 1999 (80) ECR 265; 1999 (1) ESC 339; 1999 (81) FLR 630; JT 1998 (8) SC 603; 1999 LabIC 437; 1999 (1) LLJ 604; 1999 (2) LLN 74; 1998(6) SCALE 588; 1998 (3) SLJ 111; 1999 (1) SLR 283; 1999 (1) SLT 10; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 594; 1998 (9) Supreme 452

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 32 and 311(2) - Delhi Police Act, 1978, s. 21 - Delhi Police (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1980, r. 16(3) - Judicial review in disciplinary proceedings - Scope of - No evidence in support of charge framed against delinquent officer - No attempt made by Enquiry Officer to produce any of complainants at departmental enquiry - Held, previous statements of complainant cannot be brought on record in view of rules - Findings recorded by Enquiry Officer not supported by evidence and wholly perverse - Order of dismissal liable to be set aside.
