Andhra Pradesh S.R.T.C v tate Transport Appellate Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, S.C. Agrawal, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1287; (1998) 7 SCC 353; 1998 (2) ACC 173; AIR 1998 SC 2621; JT 1998 (5) SC 417; 1998(4) SCALE 445; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 1106; 1998 (6) SLT 608; 1998 (6) Supreme 316

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 71, 95 and 104 - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, r. 258 - (A) Town service route - Connotation - Whether expression 'town service route' would mean a route within a town to enable passengers to go from one place to another in town - (B) Stage carriage permit - On notified route - Application for - Whether to be filed after obtaining permission of Transport Commr. u/r. 258(2)(ii)? - (C) State carriage permit - Grant of permit and issue of permit - Not same - Issue of permit is ministerial act - Whether can be equated with grant? - (D) Town service route - Extension - Whether power of Transport Commr. to extend route is unlimited? - Held, (A) In normal connotation means route within a town - (B) To be filed only after obtaining permission of Transport Commr. - (C) Cannot be equated with grant - (D) Power of Transport Commr. - Not unlimited - Appeal allowed.
