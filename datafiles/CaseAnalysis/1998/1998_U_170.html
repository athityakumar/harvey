Union of India and another v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.T. Thomas, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2269; (2000) 10 SCC 482

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; Telecom

Summary: Sales Tax - Telecom - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366 - Levy of sales tax - Appellants were providing telecommunication facilities by providing telephone connections to the subscribers - Respondents, States of Haryana, Orissa, Uttar Pradesh and Andhra Pradesh in their respective sales tax statutes, made amendments so as to redefine the words "purchase" and "sale" in order to bring those in conformity with the definitions given in art. 366 of the Constitution - Respective assessing authorities under those laws assessed sales tax on the rentals being charged for supply of telephones - Aggrieved UOI filed writ petitions in the respective HCs challenging the levy - Writ petitions were dismissed on ground that an alternative remedy lay in the form of a statutory appeal - Hence, present appeal - Held, question raised was purely legal which required determination as to whether provision of telephone connections and instruments amounted to sale and even so why was the Union of India not exempt from payment of sales tax under the respective statutes - Question raised was fundamental in character and need not have been put through the mill of statutory appeals in the hierarchy - Matters which should not have been dismissed by the respective HCs by suggesting an alternative remedy - Impugned orders are set aside - Matter remitted to HC - Recovery of tax stayed till the disposal of the writ petitions - Order accordingly.
