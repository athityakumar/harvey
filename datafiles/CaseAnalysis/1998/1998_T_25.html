Ram Sunder Yadav and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, G.T. Nanavati, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 836; (1998) 7 SCC 365; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1630; AIR 1998 SC 3117; 1998 (37) AllCriC 519; 1998 CRLJ 4558; JT 1998 (5) SC 620

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Explanation of injury on accused - Whether the prosecution is obliged to explain the injuries sustained by the accused in the same occurrence and whether failure of the prosecution to so explain would mean that prosecution has suppressed the truth and also the origin and genesis of the occurrence.

Held, a three judge Bench of SC had considered the questions in Bhaba Nanda Sarma & Ors. vs. State of Assam 1977 Indlaw SC 130 and held that the prosecution is not obliged to explain the injuries on the person of accused in all cases and in all circumstance sand, according to the learned, Judges, it is not the law. Since the question raised have already been answered by a larger Bench, record send back to the Bench, hearing the connected appeal. Appeal allowed.
