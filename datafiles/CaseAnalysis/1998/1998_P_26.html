Union of India and Others v Parasmal Rampuria

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 642; (1998) 8 SCC 402; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1537; AIR 1998 SCW 2292; 1998 (2) CCR 93; 1998 (2) Crimes 23; 1999 (1) JCC 95; JT 1998 (2) SC 531; 1998(2) SCALE 367; 1998 (4) SLT 7; 1998 (3) Supreme 139

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - FEMA - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - COFEPOSA Act, 1974, s.3(1) - Detention order u/s. 3(1), COFEPOSA Act was passed by authorities against respondent - Respondent before surrendering filed a writ petition in HC and obtained ad interim stay of proposed order which had remained unserved - Writ Petition still not disposed of - Whether writ petition is maintainable? - When writ petition was filed, respondent had not surrendered - Under these circumstances, proper order which was required to be passed was to call upon respondent first to surrender pursuant to detention order and then to have all his grievances examined on merits after he had an opportunity to study grounds of detention and to make his representation against said grounds as required by art. 22(5) of Constitution of India - Last hearing was over on 4-05-1997 and thereafter, Bench has not reassembled - Obvious that for same neither respondent nor appellant is at fault - However, detention order D/- 4-05-1997 has still not been executed and respondent has not surrendered - Under these circumstances, it will be appropriate to direct that ad interim relief which is extended from time to time by HC and which was continued all throughout, shall stand vacated - After surrendering it will be open to respondent to amend his WP and to take all permissible legal grounds to challenge detention order and these grounds will have to be considered by HC on their own merits - All extension orders of interim relief are also set aside - Appeals allowed.
