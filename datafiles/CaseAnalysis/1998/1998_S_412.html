Makharia Brothers v State of Nagaland and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 7; (2000) 10 SCC 503; AIR 1999 SC 3466; 1999 ARBLR 518; 2000 (3) RAJ 151

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Enforcement of Bank Guarantee - Contractor entered into a works contract with first respondent - Contract stipulated that contractor should furnish to State security for due performance of contract - Contractor furnished a bank guarantee -Extension of term of bank guarantee was declined by Contractor - Suit filed - Trial Court decreed suit against contractor and bank - Decision was confirmed by HC - Appeal - Terms of bank guarantee are explicit - Bank guarantee was to remain in force and effect only up to date stated therein or any extended date and it was to be enforceable for a period of 6 months thereafter - No way in law in which it could be declared that bank guarantee would remain effective and enforceable for so long as work contract was successfully commissioned or amounts due under bank guarantee were paid or an injunction could issue to bank to so renew period of bank guarantee - No bank can be compelled to furnish a bank guarantee without adequate funds to fall back upon should bank guarantee have to be honoured - Held, when contractor declined to extend terms of bank guarantee, proper course for State was to terminate contract on ground of breach of terms thereof as aforestated, make a claim for damages and recover on bank guarantee, if necessary by filing a suit - Suit that was filed by State was misconceived, both against bank and contractor - Appeals allowed.
