Hasanali Walimchand (Dead) By L.Rs v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1984; (1998) 2 SCC 388; AIR 1998 SC 700; 1998 (32) ALR 350; 1998 (3) Bom.C.R. 615; 1998 (1) CLT 58; JT 1998 (1) SC 84; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 102; 1998(1) SCALE 93; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 1; 1998 (2) SLT 227; 1998 (1) Supreme 229

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 18 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Determination of - Notification u/s. 4 of the Act was issued for acquisition of land - Land Acquisition Collector, determined the market value of the land at Rs.3,000/ per acre and Rs. 3200/ per acre Compensation amounting to Rs. 49,301/ was awarded - Aggrieved by the Award of the Land Acquisition Collector, the claimants sought a reference u/s. 18 of the Act - Reference court awarded a total compensation of Rs.6,16,118.60 ps. - HC set aside the award of the reference court made u/s. 18 of the Act and restored that of the Land Acquisition Collector - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC fell in error in ignoring the future potential of the land in question and instead resting its finding as realized potential only. The evidence on the record clearly establishes that the acquired land did have future potential on account of its location. The finding recorded by the HC to the effect that there was no demand of any urban character in respect of the land in question is belied by the evidence on record. The impugned judgment and order of the HC is set aside. Appeal disposed of.
