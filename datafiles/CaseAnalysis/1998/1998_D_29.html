Union of India and Others v Dinanath Shantaram Karekar and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2009; (1998) 7 SCC 569; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1837; AIR 1998 SC 2722; 1998 (4) AWC 772; 1998 (2) CLR 849; JT 1998 (6) SC 1; 1998 (2) LLJ 748; 1998 (4) LLN 14; 1998 LLR 1097; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 933; 1999 (1) SLJ 180

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 (A) - Termination of service - Charge - sheet - Registered cover containing charge - sheet returned with endorsement 'not found' - (A)Whether document can legally be treated to have been served on employee? - (i) Whether theory of communication can be invoked? - (2)Whether actual service is essential since delinquent has to submit his reply? - (B)Termination of service - Show cause notice - Service of - Same sought to be effected by publication in newspaper without earlier effort to serve him personally by tendering show cause notice either through office peon or by registered post - Nothing on record indicating that newspaper in which show cause notice was published was popular newspaper which was expected to be read by public in general or had wide circulation in area in which delinquent lived - (1)Whether show cause notice can be considered to have been served on delinquent? - (2)Whether issuance of show cause notice would be bad? - Held, (A) Document not served on employee - Authorities should have made further effort to serve it - (1)Theory of communication cannot be invoked - (2)Actual service is essential -

(B) (1)Show cause notice cannot be considered to have been served - (2)Since very initiation of disciplinary proceeding was bad as charge - sheet was not served, all subsequent steps including issuance of show cause notice would be bad - Appeal dismissed.
