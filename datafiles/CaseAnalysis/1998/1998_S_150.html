Union of India and Others v Shivbachan Rai

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2249; (2001) 9 SCC 356; 2001 (7) SLR 595

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 309 - Respondent, holding post of hatchery officer in central poultry breeding farms under Ministry of Agriculture - UPSC advertised for direct recruitment to post of Asst. Director in central poultry breeding farms under Ministry of Agriculture - As per advertisement, prescribed age for candidates was not exceeding 35 years, relaxed up to 5 years for Govt. servants - Rules were in accordance with proviso to art. 309 - Respondent was overage even after relaxation of 5 years granted under rules - He was not called for an interview - Respondent approached tribunal - Tribunal, accepted plea of respondent, holding that maximum period prescribed for relaxation, in Rules of 1985 is arbitrary and violative of art. 14 - Whether rules framed under proviso to art. 309 whereby age relaxation up to 5 years is permitted in case of Govt. servants can be considered as arbitrary or unreasonable? - Prescribing of any age limit for a given post, as also deciding extent to which any relaxation can be given if an age limit is prescribed, are essentially matters of policy - It is, therefore, open to Govt. while framing rules under proviso to art. 309 to prescribe such age limits or to prescribe extent to which any relaxation can be given - Prescription of such limit or extent of relaxation to be given, cannot be termed as arbitrary or unreasonable - If at all any charge of arbitrariness can be levied in such cases, not prescribing any basis for granting relaxation when no limit is placed on extent of relaxation, might lead to arbitrariness in exercise of power of relaxation - Further held, in any case, Rules of 1976 have been replaced by Rules of 1985 which govern direct recruitment to post of Asst. Director in present case - Therefore, Court does not agree with finding of tribunal that Rules of 1985 insofar as they prescribe extent of relaxation of age limit, are arbitrary or unreasonable - Thus, impugned order of tribunal is set aside - Appeal allowed.
