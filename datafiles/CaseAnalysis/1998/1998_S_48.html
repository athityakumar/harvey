World Tanker Carrier Corporation v SNP Shipping Services Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.P. Kurdukar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 693; (1998) 5 SCC 310; AIR 1998 SC 2330; 1999 (1) Bom.C.R. 196; 1998 (2) CLT 139; JT 1998 (3) SC 468; 1998(3) SCALE 165; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1032; 1998 (4) SLT 708; 1998 (4) Supreme 309

Case Digest

Subject: Water

Keywords: Territorial Waters, Limitation Of Liability, Brussels Convention, Colonial Courts of Admiralty (India) Act, 1891

Summary: Water - Practice and Procedure - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958, ss.352B and 352F - Limitation Act, 1908 - Letters Patent (Bom), cl.12 - Maintainability of limitation action by ship owner in Admiralty jurisdiction - When none of the claimants in respect of whose claims limitation fund is sought to be set up, lies within jurisdiction of the High Court - All claims filed in Foreign Country, USA - And likelihood of any claim being filed in HC, ruled out - So it has no jurisdiction to entertain the suit by company registered in India claiming to be managers of ship involved in collision - Whether the fact that part of cause of action arising within HC, confer jurisdiction - Held, no jurisdiction is conferred thereby - Appeal allowed.
