Samir Chanda v Managing Director, Assam State Tpt. Corpn.

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 608; (1998) 6 SCC 605; (1999) SCC (Cr) 179; 1998 (2) ACC 333; AIR 1999 SC 136; 1998 (34) ALR 294; 1998 (3) AWC 2385; JT 1998 (6) SC 40; 1998(5) SCALE 25; 1998 (7) SLT 230; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 219; 1998 (7) Supreme 66

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s.168 - Negligence of owner and driver - Bomb explosion in bus of State Transport Corporation - When reaching to last stoppage and passengers were alighting - Atmosphere during period of accident was polluted requiring care on part of conductor and driver of bus - Absence of usual police escort - Entitlement of compensation - Held, accident would be out of use of motor vehicle justifying claim of compensation - Entitled to compensation.
