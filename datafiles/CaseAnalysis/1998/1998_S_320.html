Pawan Kumar and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1737; (1998) 3 SCC 309; (1998) SCC (Cr) 740; AIR 1998 SC 958; 1998 (1) CCR 265; 1998 (1) Crimes 164; 1998 CRLJ 1144; 1998 (1) DMC 165; JT 1998 (1) SC 565; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 758; 1998(1) SCALE 486; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 746; 1998 (2) SLT 525; 1998 (1) Supreme 505

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306 and 498A - (A) Dowry demand - Scope - (1) Whether agreement for dowry is always necessary? - Persistent demands for dowry articles - (2) Whether such demands can be said to be in connection with marriage? - (3) Whether such demands would constitute a case falling within definition of 'dowry' u/ss. 2 and 304B of Code? - (B) s. 498A of Code - Dowry death - Cruelty or harassment - Whether need to be physical? - Whether mental torture in given case would be sufficient? - Bride was repeatedly taunted, maltreated and mentally tortured right from next day of marriage - There was quarrel between wife and husband only a day before her death - Leading deceased to commit suicide - Presumption as to dowry death u/s. 113B, Evidence Act - Not rebutted by accused husband - Conviction of accused - No interference - (C) s. 306 of Code - Abetment of suicide - Repeated demands of dowry articles - Quarrel between husband and deceased wife - No evidence led by accused husband to dislodge said inference - Whether any benefit of doubt could be extended to him? - Held, (A) Agreement for dowry is not always necessary - Such demand should be in connection of marriage - Would constitute a case of demand of dowry - Further held, (B) Case of cruelty or harassment - Need not be physical - Mental torture - Sufficient to constitute cruelty and harassment in connection with marriage covered u/ss. 304B and 498A, IPC, 1860 - Further held, (C) No benefit of doubt could be extended to him - Appeal partly allowed.
