Shankarayya and Another v United India Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1383; (1998) 3 SCC 140; (1999) SCC (Cr) 152; 1999 (1) ACC 497; 1998 ACJ 513; AIR 1998 SC 2968; 1998 (33) ALR 639; JT 1998 (4) SC 300; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 124; 1999 (4) SLT 245; 1998 (8) Supreme 579

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 170 - Whether respondent, i.e., Insurance Company could have filed an appeal in High Court against award of Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal and get quantum of compensation reduced when insured had not filed such appeal and when respondent had not moved Tribunal u/s. 170 of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 for getting right to contest proceedings on merits? - Held, no - Insurance Company can be permitted to contest proceedings on merits only if conditions precedent mentioned in section are found to be satisfied and for that purpose Insurance Company has to obtain order in a writing from Tribunal and which should be a reasoned order by Tribunal - Unless that procedure is followed, Insurance Company cannot have a wider defence on merits than what is available to it by way of statutory defence - It is true that claimants themselves had joined respondent in claim petition but that was done with a view to thrust statutory liability on Insurance Company on account of contract of insurance - Consequently, the Insurance Company was not entitled to file an appeal on merits of claim which was awarded by Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
