Central Board Of Direct Taxes & Ors. New Delhi v Oberoi Hotels (India) Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 667; (1998) 4 SCC 552; AIR 1998 SC 1666; 1998 (146) CTR 222; [1998] 231 ITR 148; JT 1998 (2) SC 717; 1998(2) SCALE 516; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 501; 1998 (4) SLT 161; 1998 (3) Supreme 512; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 453; 1998 (2) TLR 520

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 ; Income-tax Act 1961, s.80 O ; Income-tax Act 1961, s.261 - Royalty/Commission - Deduction - Scope of technical services - Agreement by assessee, Indian company with foreign enterprise for rendering of not only technical services for operating the hotel of foreign enterprise but also providing for professional and other services for operating hotel - Remand of Order of CBDT not granting approval to the agreement under s.80-O - Held, non granting approval to the agreement under s.80-O by CBDT was not proper - The agreement has to be seen as a whole and it is quite apparent that it provided for rendering of not only technical services for operating the hotel of the foreign enterprise but also providing for professional and other services in connection with operating of the hotel - Remand of Order found to be illegal - However Supreme Court declined to remand the matter to CBDT for fresh appraisal as the matter was 28 years old.
