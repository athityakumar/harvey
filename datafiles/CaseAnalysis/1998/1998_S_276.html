Surendra Narain Singh and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 702; (1998) 5 SCC 246; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1317; AIR 1998 SC 1841; JT 1998 (3) SC 489; 1998 LabIC 1864; 1998 (2) LLJ 342; 1998 (2) LLN 948; 1998(3) SCALE 242; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1165; 1998 (3) SLJ 129; 1998 (4) SLR 97; 1998 (4) SLT 669; 1998 (4) Supreme 221

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Bihar Judicial Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1955, rr. 2 and 20 - Bihar Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Ad hoc Recruitment Rules, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 234 - (A) Appointment - Candidates nominated by Public Service Commission - Not containing sufficient SC/ST candidates - Supplementary list - Whether list to be confined to SC/ST candidates only? - Whether words 'such candidates' mean SC/ST candidates only? - (B) Bihar Judicial Service Rules, r. 1 - Appointment - Reserved vacancies - Whether can be carried forward? - Provision in this regard absent in 1955 Rules - (C) Constitution, art. 16 - Seniority - Munsifs appointed under 1955 Rules and 1974 Rules - Some posts under 1955 Rules filled through supplementary list after appointments were made under 1974 Rules - Delay in forwarding supplementary list - Whether appointees under 1955 Rules as per Supplementary list are senior to appointees under 1974 Rules? - Held, (A) Need not be confined to SC/ST candidates only - Words 'such candidates' - Do not mean SC/ST candidates only - (B) Cannot be carried forward - (C) For delay in forwarding supplementary list appointees cannot be made to suffer - Appointees under 1955 Rules as per Supplementary list - Senior to appointees under 1974 Rules - Appeal dismissed.
