Mohd. Hadi Raja v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 169; (1998) 5 SCC 91; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1265; AIR 1998 SC 1945; [1998] 93 Comp Cas 362; 1998 CRLJ 2826; JT 1998 (3) SC 507; 1999 (1) LLJ 701; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - Sanction to prosecute - Protection by way of sanction not available to - Officers of Govt. companies or public undertakings - Even when such public undertakings are 'State' within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution on account of deep pervasive control of Govt. - Applicability of s. 197 CrPC, 1973 - Held, ex facie, such instrumentality or agency being a juridical person has an independent status and action taken by them, however important same may be in interest of state cannot be held to be an action taken by or on behalf of Govt. as such within meaning of s. 197, CrPC, 1973 - Therefore, s. 197 of Code of is not applicable - Appeals disposed of.
