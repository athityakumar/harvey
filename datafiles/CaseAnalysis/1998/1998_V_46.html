Virendra Kashinath Ravat and Another v Vinayak N. Joshi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1291; (1999) 1 SCC 47; AIR 1999 SC 162; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 126; JT 1998 (7) SC 596; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 594; 1998(6) SCALE 115; 1998 (9) SLT 180; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 7; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 643; 1998 (8) Supreme 412

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13(1)(e) - Eviction suit - Rejection of - Subletting - Death of original tenant - Landlord doubtful and unable to spell out heirs of deceased tenant - Party as defendant claiming to be daughter of tenant and leaving it to court to decide who is real heir of original tenant - Held, ground for rejection that since landlord has not recognized daughter of original tenant as his tenant there could not be subtenant - Is improper.
