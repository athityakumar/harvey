Ferro Alloys Corporation Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2119; (1999) 2 SCC 198; 1999 (105) E.L.T. 4; JT 1998 (8) SC 544; 1998 (10) SLT 276; 1999 (112) STC 570; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5 - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - Appellants entered into an agreement, called Off-take Agreement, with 'Richco', a corporation having its registered office at Zug, Switzerland - Agreement recited that appellants intended to construct and were in course of constructing a new charge chrome plant in State of Orissa, utilizing chrome ore from mines in that State, as a 100 per cent export oriented unit offering for export its entire production - Richco was an international marketing organization that was specialized and experienced in distribution and handling of ferro alloys, ferrous and non-ferrous ores and concentrate and steel related commodities worldwide with associated companies - Pursuant to Off-take Agreement, charge chrome agreements were entered into from time to time - Such agreements placed on record states that, in terms of Off-Take Agreement, appellants, described as 'sellers', had agreed to sell charge chrome to Richco, described as 'buyers', on terms and conditions therein stated - For assessment years 1990-91 and 1991-92 respondents found that sales made by appellants were intra-State sales subject to levy of tax under Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 and not in course of export, exempt from tax - Whether sales effected by appellants to Richco were sales in course of export to Richco under Charge Chrome Agreements and exempt from sales tax or sale to Richco was under Off-take Agreement and that Charge Chrome Agreements were only delivery orders there under? - Held, Richco was not an intermediary in sense that it was not contract of sale of charge chrome by Richco to ultimate buyer which occasioned export - Charge chrome having been exported by appellants to Richco abroad, Richco resold it to ultimate buyers - High Court and authorities below were in error in concluding that sales made by appellants were not sales in course of export and therefore not exempt from levy of sales tax - Letters state that since appellants' charge chrome plant was customs bonded, it was not possible for appellants to make any domestic sale thereof without approval of competent authority and Customs and Central Excise authorities had certified that appellants had not sold any quantity of charge chrome in India - All export sales were exempt from payment of State and Central sales tax - Appeals allowed.
