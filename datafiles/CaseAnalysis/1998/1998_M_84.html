Indian Bank v Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 963; (1998) 5 SCC 69; AIR 1998 SC 1952; 1998 (3) AWC 1804; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 524; 1998 (3) CLT 15; [1998] 93 Comp Cas 120; 1998 (79) FLR 615; JT 1998 (3) SC 733; 1998(3) SCALE 441; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 187; 1998 (5) SLT 232; 1998 (4) Supreme 521

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Summary suit - Applicability of s. 10 of CPC - Whether the bar to proceed with the trial of subsequently instituted suit, contained in s. 10 of the CPC is applicable to summary suit filed under O. 37 of CPC.

Held, the word 'trial' in s. 10 of CPC, in the context of a summary suit, cannot be interpreted to mean the entire proceedings starting with institution of the suit by lodging a plaint. In a summary suit the 'trial' really begins after the Court or the Judge grants leave to the defendant to contest the suit. Therefore, the Court or the Judge dealing with the summary suit can proceed up to the stage of hearing the summons for judgment and passing the judgment in favour of the plaintiff if (a) the defendant has not applied for leave to defend or if such application has been made and refused or if(b) the defendant who is permitted to defend fails to comply with the conditions on which leave to defend is granted. Appeals allowed.
