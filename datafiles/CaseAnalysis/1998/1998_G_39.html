Pammi @ Brijendra Singh v Government Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 601; (1998) SCC (Cr) 673; (1998) 2 SCC 700; AIR 1998 SC 1185; 1998 (1) CCR 318; 1998 (1) Crimes 187; 1998 CRLJ 1617; JT 1998 (1) SC 686; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 801; 1998(1) SCALE 577; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 842; 1998 (3) SLT 145; 1998 (2) Supreme 65

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s.482; Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.96 and 300; Evidence Act, 1872, ss.9 and 61 - Murder - Evidence - FIR - Injured eye-witness failing to name other eye-witnesses - Testimony of such eye-witnesses - Credibility of - Accused admitting that he went in night to the house of one of the prosecution witness with fire-arms to settle scores - Held, such conduct of accused is act of aggression - Plea of private defence of accused therefore not acceptable - Court wrongly relies on copy of deposition of a person in another criminal case to discredit eye-witness - Failure to hold Test Identification Parade would not vitiate evidence of eye-witnesses - Evidence of eye-witnesses not named by injured eye-witness cannot be discarded if nothing produced to doubt their presence.
