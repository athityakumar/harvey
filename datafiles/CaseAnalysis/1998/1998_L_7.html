Bhagavathi Textiles Limited v Lakshmi Textile Brokers and others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2320; (2000) 10 SCC 457

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Suit for recovery - Case remitted - Legality - Appellant had filed a suit for recovery of money against respondents - Trial Court decreed the suit for a sum of Rs. 1,03,061.22 plus interest thereon - On appeal HC allowed the appeal and the case was remitted to the Trial Court for fresh disposal according to law - Hence instant appeal.

Held, SC fail to understand the course adopted by the High Court. Even if respondent No. 1 was not a partnership firm but was a business owned by respondent No. 2, there was no reason why the HC could not have proceeded with the hearing of the appeal in order to find out whether respondent No. 2 was liable to pay any money to the appellant. It was not the case of the defendants in the suit that anyone other than respondents No. 2 and 3 had any interest in Respondent 1. If respondent No. 1 was a duly constituted partnership firm, as was the case of the appellant herein, then respondents No. 2 and 3 were stated to be its partners. Assuming appellant was wrong, the case of defendants themselves was that it is respondent No. 2 who was the sole proprietor of Respondent 1. Even if this be the case, Respondent 2 was a defendant in the suit which had been filed by the appellant and if the appellant had succeeded, as it did before the trial court, a decree was liable to be passed against defendant No. 2. To put it differently, if the appellant had made out his case on merits, then defendant No. 2 was liable whether as a partner or as the sole proprietor of defendant No. 1. There was, therefore, no need for the HC to set aside the trial court's judgment and remit the case. The HC, therefore, fell in error. The HC did not consider the other contentions which were raised by the respondents herein. Judgment of the HC is set aside and directed it to hear the appeal along with cross objections de novo. Appeal allowed.
