Khudeswar Dutta v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1453; (1998) 4 SCC 492; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1062; AIR 1998 SC 1736; 1998 (2) CCR 132; 1998 CRLJ 2274; 1998 (2) JCC 26; JT 1998 (3) SC 162; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 328; 1998(2) SCALE 682; 1998 (4) SLT 343; 1998 (3) Supreme 594

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 5 - Basis for conviction under - Validity - Allegation, appellants intending to use recovered fire arms for terrorist activities - Reply of appellants to allegation, recovery was not from their house but from another person's at distance of 11/2 kms - Held, only inference that could be drawn from said statement is that he knew that said fire arms were kept at that place - Mere knowledge not amounts to conscious possession of those things - Conviction of appellant on basis of said statement - Not proper.
