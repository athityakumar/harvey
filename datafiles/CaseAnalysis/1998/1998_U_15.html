Mahavir Singh v U.P. State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2065; (1999) 9 SCC 178; (1999) SCC (L&S) 945; 1999 (82) FLR 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute - Services of Chowkidar terminated - Reference - Whether High Court correct in taking a view that as dispute was raised belatedly, reference itself was incompetent though agreeing with Labour Court that on merits termination order could not be sustained and it was illegal? - Held, once termination is held to be illegal, then entire reference could not have been rejected - Dispute lingered on for a number of years - That would not mean that dispute had ceased to exist - It is, of course, true that belatedly dispute was raised but that has been taken care of by Labour Court by not awarding full back wages but only 50% of back wages all throughout from date of termination till reinstatement - Such order as passed by Labour Court could not be said to be in any way uncalled for and illegal - Appeal allowed.
