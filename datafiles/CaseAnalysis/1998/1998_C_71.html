Collector Of Customs, Bombay v Sneha Sales Corporation

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2223; 2000 (121) E.L.T. 577

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d), 111(c), 111(m), 111(p), 17, 47 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Import and Export Order, 1955 - Consignments of good - Contravention of provisions - Justifiability of order - On basis of licenses respondent no. 1 imported two consignments and goods were assessed u/s. 17 of 1962 Act and were allowed clearance for home consumption u/s. 47 of 1962 Act - Director of Revenue intelligence made certain inquiries and goods were detained by officers of Directorate of Revenue Intelligence - Therefore, Collector of Customs ordered for absolute confiscation of goods in question u/s. 111(d) of 1962 Act and imposed penalty - As against appeal filed by respondents was disposed of by Appellate and order of Collector be set aside - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by Appellate Tribunal was justified - Held, where license was obtained by misrepresentation or fraud it was not rendered on estas result of its cancellation so as to result in goods that were imported on basis of said licenses and being treated as goods imported without license in contravention of order passed u/s. 3 of 1947 Act that fraud or misrepresentation only renders license voidable and it becomes inoperative before it cancelled - Though, licenses were cancelled by order after goods was imported and cleared - Therefore, Tribunal was right in holding that import of goods was not in contravention of provisions of the Order and 1947 Act and goods were not liable to be confiscated on that basis u/s.111(d) of 1962 Act - Hence, matter required to be remitted to Tribunal for consideration of question whether goods were liable to be confiscated u/s. 111(m) of 1962 Act on ground of misdeclaration of value of bobbins and u/s. 111(p) of 1962 Act on ground of contravention of provisions of s. 111(C) of 1962 Act and impugned judgment of Tribunal was liable to be set aside - Appeal dismissed.
