Radhey Shyam v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1265; (1999) SCC (Cr) 72; (1999) 1 SCC 168; AIR 1999 SC 1461; 1999 ALJ 235; 1999 (1) AllCriR 255; 1998 (4) CCR 76; 1998 (4) Crimes 74; 1999 CRLJ 590; JT 1998 (7) SC 197; 1999 (1) RCR(Criminal) 765; 1998(5) SCALE 617; 1998 (8) SLT 332; 1998 (8) Supreme 118

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 and 325 - High Court held that accused had acted in exercise of their right of private defence but had exceeded same and therefore, their conviction u/s. 302 was not proper - Conviction u/s. 304 Part I - Appeal against conviction - No evidence to show that appellant had given fatal blow or any blow which was likely to cause death - Held, in absence of evidence, appellant could have been convicted only u/s. 325 - Sentence reduced - Appeal partly allowed.
