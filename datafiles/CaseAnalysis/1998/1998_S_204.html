Municipal Corporation of Bkihanmumbai and Another v State Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, B.N. Kirpal, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 886; (1999) 1 SCC 123; AIR 1999 SC 380; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 449; 1999 (1) CLT 33; JT 1998 (8) SC 387; 1998(6) SCALE 449; 1999 (1) SCJ 44; 1998 (10) SLT 132; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 288; 1998 (9) Supreme 189

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Governments - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 A - Bombay Municipal Act, 1888, s. 217 - Respondent herein had preferred an appeal before Addl. Chief Judge of Small Cause Court u/s. 217 of Act and that was dismissed - Aggrieved by order passed by Judge, respondent filed a second appeal in HC u/s. 218-D(1) of Act - High Court allowed that appeal partly - Appellant questioned order of Judge through a Letters Patent Appeal (LPA) - An objection was raised before Division Bench of HC regarding maintainability of LPA on ground that against an order of dismissal of second appeal by HC, no further appeal could lie either under letters patent or any other law - Reliance was also placed on s. 100-A of CPC, 1908 - Objection found favour with Division Bench and without going into merits of order of Single Judge, LPA was dismissed as not maintainable - Whether it is valid in law? - Held, according to s. 218-D, an appeal shall also lie to HC from any decision of Chief Judge of Small Cause Court in an appeal u/s. 217, 'upon a question of law or usage having force of law or construction of a document' - That respondent had taken recourse to s. 218-D(1) in filing an appeal against appellate order of Addl. Chief Judge of Small Cause Court is not in dispute - Appellant has not questioned maintainability of appeal filed by respondent u/s. 218-D of Act before Single Judge of HC before the Supreme Court - Thus, it is obvious that appeal filed by respondent u/s. 218-D of Act was a second appeal against appellate order made by Additional Chief Judge, Small Cause Court - Under Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, no further appeal has been provided against judgment of a Single Judge of HC deciding second appeal u/s. 218-D of Act against an appellate order of Chief Judge of Small Cause Court passed u/s. 217(1) of Act - S. 100-A of CPC, 1908, which was introduced by Amendment Act, 1976, specifically bars any further appeal in such cases - In instant case, since an appeal from appellate order was heard and decided by a Single Judge of HC, no further appeal was maintainable from judgment and order of Single Judge passed in that appeal - View taken by Division Bench of HC under circumstances suffers from no error - Appeal dismissed.
