State of Maharashtra v Labour Law Practitioners' Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1745; (1998) SCC (L&S) 657; (1998) 2 SCC 688; AIR 1998 SC 1233; 1998 (3) Bom.C.R. 617; 1998 (1) CLR 850; 1998 (1) CLT 146; 1999 (82) FLR 380; JT 1998 (1) SC 604; 1998 (1) LLJ 868; 1998 (1) LLN 961; 1998(1) SCALE 565; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 793; 1998 (2) SLJ 72; 1998 (1) SLR 696; 1998 (2) SLT 561; 1998 (2) Supreme 73

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Bombay General Act, 1904

Summary: Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1947, s. 9 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 7 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 236 and 234 - 'Judicial service' - Recruitment to - Presiding Officers of labour courts constitute judicial service - Appointment u/art. 234 which prescribes qualification of age - Amended s. 9 of Bombay Industrial Relations Act and s. 7 of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 deleting requirement of age - Validity - Held, is ultra vires of Constitution - 'District Judge' - Term includes hierarchy of specialised civil courts viz labour courts and industrial courts.
