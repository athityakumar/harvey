Durga Oil Company and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1087; (1998) 6 SCC 299; AIR 1998 SC 2587; 1998 (3) AWC 2240; JT 1998 (5) SC 185; 1998(4) SCALE 315; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 922; 1998 (6) SLT 417; 1998 (6) Supreme 147

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Essential commodities

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Petroleum Act, 1934, s. 4 - Petroleum Rules, 1976, rr. 2(xv), 2(xxii) and 141 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Storage - (A) More than 1000 litres of petroleum is receptacle, if it is petroleum in bulk u/r. 2(xv) of Rules - (B) Petroleum Class B - If licensee under Form XI can store High Speed Diesel in an underground 'tank' and sale same by using a pump unit - Held, to be petroleum irrespective of quantity contained therein - Cannot store, as licence under Form XII is required - Appeal dismissed.
