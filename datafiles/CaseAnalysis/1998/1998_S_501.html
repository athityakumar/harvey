Panchhi and Others, National Commission For Women v State of Up and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 827; (1998) 7 SCC 177; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1561; AIR 1998 SC 2726; AIR 1998 SCW 2777; 1998 ALJ 2018; 1998 (3) CCR 213; 1998 (3) Crimes 181; 1998 CRLJ 4044; JT 1998 (5) SC 565; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 74; 1998(4) SCALE 603; 1998 (7) SLT 41; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 40; 1998 (6) Supreme 420

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s.374, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.3 ,Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.300 and 302 - Whether the conviction of the accused on the basis of the child witness is correct - Evidence of child witness cannot be rejected if it is reliable- Held, no interference in conviction of the accused on the basis of the child witness- The present case does not deserve to be put in the category of the rarest of rare case, therefore,death sentence converted to sentence of life imprisonment.
