State of Rajasthan v M. C. Saxena

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 372; (1998) 3 SCC 385; (1998) SCC (L&S) 875; AIR 1998 SC 1150; 1998 (2) AWC 1168; 1998 (2) CLR 1076; 1998 (79) FLR 140; JT 1998 (2) SC 103; 1998 LabIC 1038; 1998 (1) LLJ 1244; 2000 (1) LLN 35; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 6; 1998(2) SCALE 26; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 1090; 1998 (3) SLJ 10; 1998 (1) SLR 787; 1998 (3) SLT 216; 1998 (2) Supreme 174; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 987

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Promotion - Departmental proceedings started in 1979 against employee - Provisional promotion of employee - Proceedings culminating in order of punishment in year 1985 - Direction to reconsider case of Promotion of employee w.e.f. 1980 - Held, unsustainable - View that punishment lapses on expiry of 7 years from day of delinquency - Unjustified - Order of disciplinary authority cannot be faulted on ground that before imposing penalty further opportunity of hearing should be given to delinquent.
