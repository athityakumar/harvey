Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Dr. V. Nagaraju and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1032; (1998) 8 SCC 66; AIR 1998 SC 3237; 1998 (80) FLR 704; JT 1998 (6) SC 368; 1998(5) SCALE 289; 1999 (2) SLJ 274; 1998 (5) SLR 24; 1998 (7) SLT 554; 1998 (7) Supreme 346

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Rules for Admission to Post Graduate Courses in the Medical Colleges in the State of Andhra Pradesh, rr. 3(2) and 19 - Whether the in-service candidates who are selected outside the quota reserved for in-service candidates could also be entitled to the same benefits as candidates selected against such reserved quota? - Held, in Govt. service there are two kinds of candidates those who are selected against reserved quota and those who are selected otherwise than such quota - Those candidates who are covered by r. 3(2) are those who have been selected against the quota reserved for the in-service candidates while others who are in Govt. service are selected are those who fall outside such category - In-service candidate is one who has put in a minimum of two years service in respective fields in the rural areas and is selected against the reserved quota and not all candidates who have put in 2 years rural service and are selected to Post Graduate studies - Intention of the Govt. is clear that it is only those candidates who have been selected against the reserved quota who will be entitled to be deputed and not others that is why sub-r. (2) of r. 19 provided that if any candidate in Govt. service other than the in-service candidates is selected for any post graduation courses should not be entitled for any kind of leave including extra-ordinary leave without allowances for prosecuting Post-Graduate Course, unless he has put in a minimum of two years of service on duty in the respective service - Appeal allowed.
