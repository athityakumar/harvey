J. K. Cotton Spinning and Weaving Mills Company Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1631; (1998) 3 SCC 540; AIR 1998 SC 1270; 1998 (75) ECR 473; 1998 (99) E.L.T. 8; JT 1998 (2) SC 293; 1998 (25) RLT 661; 1998(2) SCALE 204; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 102; 1998 (3) SLT 635; 1998 (3) Supreme 31

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 35L and 11A - Recovery of duty - Limitation - Extension - Ground, service of show cause notice is stayed by Court - Availability - Suspension by Court of directives issued by Board intended to be observed by Collector while exercising powers under r. 9 and while making delegation - Held, cannot have effect of preventing issuance of show cause notice under s. 11-A - Since even if directive had not been issued Collector could still have issued a notice - Suspension of Board directives would not extend limitation - No relation either to rate of duty of excise or to value of goods for purpose of assessment.
