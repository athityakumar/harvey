K. K. Modi v K. N. Modi and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1613; (1998) 3 SCC 573; AIR 1998 SC 1297; 1998 (1) ARBLR 296; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 30; JT 1998 (1) SC 407; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 601; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 91

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151, O. 6, r. 16 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 7 - 'Abuse of process of Court' - Arbitration petition challenging decision of Chairman of Financial Corporation as an award - Civil suit instituted on alternative plea that if said decision of Chairman was held not to be an arbitration award then it should be set aside in High Court on same day - Validity - Held, not liable to be dismissed on ground of abuse of process of court - Memorandum of understanding between two groups of family - Is not arbitration agreement nor decision by said Chairman in such reference is an award.
