Vinay Balachandra Joshi v Registrar General Supreme Court to India and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1990; (1998) 7 SCC 461; AIR 1999 SC 107; 1998 (4) CLT 123; JT 1998 (6) SC 203; 1998 (2) KLT 49; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 72; 1998(5) SCALE 70; 1998 (7) SLT 284; 1998 (7) Supreme 97

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.19 (1)(g) 14 and 145; Advocates Act, 1961, ss.29 and 30; Lawyers' Chambers (Allotment and Occupancy) Rules, r.4 - Right of advocate - Allotment of chamber in court premises is right, whether - Held, right to get a chamber in court does not follow from right to practice profession - It is merely a facility provided by the Court - Allotment of chamber to Advocates-on-Record, Non-Advocates on Record and Senior Advocates in the ratio of 7: 2: 1 - Is neither unreasonable nor arbitrary - Though Advocates-on-Record are to be given first priority, relative needs of all three categories of advocates is to be considered - And their interests are required to be balanced.
