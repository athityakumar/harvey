George and Others v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 647; (1998) 4 SCC 605; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1232; AIR 1998 SC 1376; 1998 (2) CCR 44; 1998 (2) Crimes 27; 1998 CRLJ 2034; 1998 (2) JCC 127; JT 1998 (2) SC 496; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 199; 1998(2) SCALE 411; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 303; 1998 (3) SLT 609; 1998 (3) Supreme 192

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ,ss.449, 302, r/w.34 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act ,1970, s.2 - Assailants - Dying declaration - Convictions - Acquittal - Appellants in prosecution trespassed into his house - Appellant Nos.1,2,3 assaulted with different weapons - Deceased died owing to injuries sustained at the hands of assailants - Trial ended in acquittal of all of them - HC set aside acquittal of three appellants and convicted u/ss. 449, 302, r/w.34 of IPC affirming acquittal of others - HC was under challenge in appeals preferred by appellants u/s.2 of 1970 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecution had proved dying declaration of deceased and PW.3 could identify assailants - Held, identity of appellant No.1 as one of assailants, in dying declaration could not be doubted - Trial Court ignored a fundamental rule of criminal jurisprudence that a statement of a witness recorded u/s.164 CrPC could not be used as substantive evidence - Trial Court committed patent errors - Prosecution had prove beyond all reasonable doubts that appellant No.1 was among assailants, as testified by PW.3 and fully corroborated by dying declaration made by deceased before PWs.3, 4 - They were entitled to benefit of reasonable doubt - Convictions and sentences of Appellant No.1 as recorded by HC but set aside convictions of Appellant Nos.2, 3 - Appeals disposed of.
