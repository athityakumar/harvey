S. P. Shivprasad Pipal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1639; (1998) 4 SCC 598; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1263; AIR 1998 SC 1882; 1998 (1) CLR 1153; 1998 (79) FLR 384; JT 1998 (3) SC 216; 1998 LabIC 1873; 1998 (2) LLJ 483; 1998 (3) LLN 41; 1998(2) SCALE 736; 1998 (3) SLJ 108; 1998 (2) SLR 781; 1998 (4) SLT 336; 1998 (4) Supreme 104

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970

Summary: - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Contract, Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Factories Act, 1948 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Payment Of Bonus Act, 1965 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Central Labour Service Rules, 1987 - Arts. 14 and 16? - Whether the merging of three cadres of Central Labour Service by the notification of 03-02-1987 were comparable carrying similar qualifications and comparable duties and salaries, was a justified decision or was it a violation of the Constitution? - This is a matter of policy, the scope of review by the court is limited - A decision to merge such cadres is essentially a matter of internal policy - Three cadres carried the same pay scale at the relevant time, merging of the three cadres cannot be said to have caused any prejudice to the members of any of the cadres - The total number of posts were also increased proportionately when the merger took place so that the percentage of posts available on promotion was not in any manner adversely affected by the merger of the cadres - By reason of the merger, the appellant has not, in fact, suffered any prejudice and he has also received promotions - Held, a mere chance of promotion was not a condition of service and the fact that there was a reduction in the chance of promotion would not amount to a change in the conditions of service - No ground to set aside the Central Labour Service Rules, 1987 - The appeal is dismissed.
