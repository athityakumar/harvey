Income Tax Appellate Tribunal Through President v V. K. Agarwal and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1302; (1999) 1 SCC 16; (1999) 1 SCC 116; AIR 1999 SC 452; 1999 (1) CompLJ 193; 1999 CRLJ 441; 1998 (150) CTR 513; [1999] 235 ITR 175; JT 1998 (7) SC 638; 1999 (1) SLR 65; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 707; [1998] 101 TAXMAN 382

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Corporate - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c), 2(c)(i), 2(c)(iii), 3 and 15 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.129 - Supreme Court Rules, rr. 3(a), 34 and 35 - Publications against tribunal - Without verifying facts -(A) Whether apology is to be accepted in cases of publication against a judicial body without verification of facts? - Held, this kind of conduct, on part of Law Secy., who is expected to maintain independence of ITAT and not interfere with its judicial functioning, amounts to gross contempt of court - Apology cannot be accepted - It is a deliberate attempt on his part to question judicial functioning of tribunal coming as it does from a person of his rank - Respondent guilty of contempt of court - Looking to all circumstances of present case, apology offered cannot be accepted - He has travelled far beyond exercising administrative control over tribunal - He has tried to influence or question decision-making process of tribunal - Further held, therefore, respondent held guilty of contempt of court - Appeal allowed.
