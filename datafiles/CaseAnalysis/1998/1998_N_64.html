Orma Impex Private Limited v Nissai Asb Pte. Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1148; (1999) 2 SCC 541; AIR 1999 SC 2871; 1999 (3) RAJ 406

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 45 and 50 - Delhi High Court Act, 1966, s. 10 - High Court took view that no further appeal lies u/s. 50, against an order u/s. 45 of the Arbitration Act, 1996 - Whether appeal lies against an order u/s. 45 refusing to refer parties to arbitration by learned single judge? - Held, when a question is stated to be referred to an established court, ordinary incidents of procedure of that court will get attached including right of appeal from its decision - Cl. 15 of Letters Patent was held applicable to decisions rendered by learned Single Judges of HC - Therefore, it is, appropriate to direct the special leave petition to be placed before a Bench of three Judges of the Court - Matter referred to Larger Bench.
