Radhey Shyam Gupta v U. P. State Agro Industries Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 270; (1999) 2 SCC 21; (1999) SCC (L&S) 439; AIR 1999 SC 609; 1999 ALJ 315; 1998 (4) AWC 201; 1999 (1) ESC 313; JT 1998 (8) SC 585; 1999 (1) LLJ 432; 1998 (3) SLJ 100; 1999 (1) SLR 44; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 130; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 558; 1999 (9) Supreme 504; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 265

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether High Court was justified in quashing order of tribunal and allowing petition of Corporation by holding that temporary services of appellant have not been terminated by way of punishment founded on any misconduct but Competent Authority found that he was not fit to be continued in service on account of unsatisfactory work and conduct? - In cases where termination is preceded by an inquiry and evidence is received and findings as to misconduct of a definitive nature are arrived at behind back of officer and where on basis of such a report, termination order is issued, such an order will be violative of principles of natural justice inasmuch as purpose of inquiry is to find out truth of allegations with a view to punish him and not merely to gather evidence for a future regular departmental inquiry - In such cases, termination is to be treated as based or founded upon misconduct and will be punitive - These are obviously not cases where employer feels that there is a mere cloud against employees' conduct but are cases where employer has virtually accepted definitive and clear findings of Inquiry Officer, which are all arrived at behind back of employee even though such acceptance of findings is not recorded in order of termination - That is why misconduct is foundation and not merely motive, in such cases - Held, it is a clear case where Inquiry Officer examined witnesses, recorded their statements and gave a clear finding of appellant accepting a bribe and even recommended his termination - All these were done behind back of appellant - Managing Director passed termination order very next day - It is not a preliminary report where some facts are gathered and a recommendation is made for a regular departmental inquiry - This is an obvious case where report and its findings are foundation of termination order and not merely motive - Held, HC in grave error in treating such a report as a preliminary report - Appeal allowed.
