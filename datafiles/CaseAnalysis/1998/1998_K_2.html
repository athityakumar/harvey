B. V. Sivaiah and Others v K. Addanki Babu and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1047; (1998) 6 SCC 720; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1656; AIR 1998 SC 2565; 1999 (1) Bom.C.R. 174; 1998 (2) CLR 492; JT 1998 (5) SC 96; 1998 LabIC 2749; 1999 (1) LLJ 75; 1999 (1) LLJ 754; 1998 (3) LLN 951; 1998(4) SCALE 182; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 782; 1999 (1) SLJ 257; 1998 (5) SLR 59; 1998 (6) SLT 435; 1998 (6) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Mode Of Selection, Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Regional Rural Banks (Amendment) Act, 1987

Summary: Service - Regional Rural Banks (Amendment) Act, 1987, Ss.17, 24, 29, 29(2) - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 - Promotion Policy - Eligibility - Criteria - Seniority-cum-Merit - Circular issued by National Bank to all Regional Rural Banks, regarding promotion of Branch Mangers to posts of Area/Senior Managers - Principle of Criterion of 'seniority-cum-merit' was laid with greater emphasis on seniority u/r. 493)(b) of 1957 Rules and selection of eligible candidates should be based on seniority - Appellants/Officers were aggrieved by this notification and appealed to HC, which hold that performance appraisal could not be taken into consideration, while considering officers for promotion to higher rank and had set aside promotion and junior to them were provided promotions - On appeal filed by appellant/promoted officers against judgment of HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent possesses minimum required merit for holding higher post and, his immediate junior might be considered for purpose of promotion - Held, promotion was based on seniority- cum-merit and appellant/officer could not claim promotion as a matter of right by virtue of his seniority alone - HC was not justified in holding that performance appraisal could not be taken into consideration, while considering officers for promotion to higher rank - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and promotion of appellant for post of Area/Senior Manager was affirmed - Candidates who fulfilled minimum standard were selected for promotion on basis of seniority and it could not be said that selection had not been made in accordance with principle of seniority-cum-merit - Criterion of promotion policy could not be regarded as in consonance with principle of 'seniority-cum-merit' as prescribed under Rules - As per promotion policy, selection was made on basis of total number of marks obtained by eligible candidates, hence Petition was liable to be dismissed - Petition dismissed.
