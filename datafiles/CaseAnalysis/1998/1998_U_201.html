Bhawarlal Bhandari v Universal Heavy Mechanical Lifting Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 6; (1999) 1 SCC 558; AIR 1999 SC 246; 1999 (35) ALR 248; 1999 (1) ARBLR 355; 1999 (1) CalLT(SC) 92; JT 1998 (8) SC 393; 2000 (Supp) RD 635; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act 1940, s. 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Filing of Award was in dispute - Award decree not shown to be passed without jurisdiction - No objections were filed by respondent, judgment-debtor - Award decree becoming final - Execution of award decree in arbitration proceedings - Argued by judgment-debtor that it was mock award and fraud was committed on him and on Court - Held, plea not tenable as objections should have been filed earlier - No equity in his favour as fraud was not proved against him or Court - Relief granted.
