Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Urmila Ramesh

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.C. Agrawal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1419; (1998) 3 SCC 6; AIR 1998 SC 2640; 1998 (146) CTR 81; [1998] 230 ITR 422; JT 1998 (1) SC 208; 1998(1) SCALE 206; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 323; 1998 (2) SLT 485; 1998 (1) Supreme 323; [1998] 96 TAXMAN 533

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(22) and 41(2) - Whether the profits assessed u/s. 41(2) in the preceding years cannot form part of the accumulated profits for the purpose of s. 2(22)(c) of the Income Tax Act, 1961? - Held, in any event as this amount has already been assessed in the hands of the company obviously the same amount cannot also be regarded as capital gain. In other words both s. 41(2) and s. 50 of the 1961 Act cannot apply to the same amount - The amount received by the company, which was taxed u/s. 41(2) of the Act did not represent accumulated profits" within the meaning of that expression in s. 2(22) of the Act - The High Court was right in answering the questions of law referred to it in the affirmative and in favour of the assessee - Appeal dismissed.
