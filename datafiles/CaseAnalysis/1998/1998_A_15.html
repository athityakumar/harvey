A.P. Rice Bran Solvent Extractors' Association and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1481; (1998) 8 SCC 384; 1998 (103) E.L.T. 481; JT 1998 (9) SC 193; 1998 (9) SLT 219

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Essential commodities, Permissibility, Definition of ‘Vegetable oil’

Summary: Indirect Tax- Agriculture & Agri. Products - National Oilseeds and Vegetable Oils Development Board Act, 1983 - Vegetable Oils Cess Act, 1983 - Definition of 'Vegetable oil' - Permissibility - HC held that since rice bran is an oil-bearing material of plant origin, rice bran oil is vegetable oil - Hence instant appeal - Whether rice bran oil is 'vegetable oil' for the purpose of the 1983 Act.

Held, object underlying the enactment of the 1983 Act, is to make available funds to the National Oilseeds and Vegetable Oil Development Board so as to enable the said Board to promote the development and growth of oilseeds industry and vegetable oils industry. Keeping in view that rice bran is in the nature of a waste or a by-product obtained during the course of processing of rice and rice bran oil is obtained by the solvent extraction process and is not fit for human consumption. Rice bran oil does not fall within the ambit of the definition of 'Vegetable oil' contained in s. 3(h) of 1983 Act. Appeal allowed
