National Building Construction Corporation v S. Raghunathan and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M. Srinivasan, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1178; (1998) 7 SCC 66; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1770; AIR 1998 SC 2779; JT 1998 (6) SC 21; 1998 LabIC 3496; 1998 (4) LLN 991; 1998(4) SCALE 694; 1999 (1) SLJ 246; 1998 (7) SLT 169; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 156; 1998 (6) Supreme 570

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Fundamental Rules, r. 51(2) - Employee deputed with Govt. company in their foreign projects - (A) Foreign allowance not stipulated in contract of service - Once pay structure revised but policy decision and order by company allowing foreign allowance only on their original basic salary - Claim on basis of revised pay scale - Observation by High Court that employees are entitled to increased allowance on ground of 'Legitimate Expectation' - (B) Question of 'Legitimate Expectation' not raised in pleadings - Allowed by HC at stage of argument and allowing petitions only on this ground, without least realising that there was hardly any legitimacy in claim of employees, if proper - Held, wholly speculative, besides being erroneous and policy decision have effect of displacing doctrine of 'Legitimate Expectation' - Not proper - Appeal allowed.
