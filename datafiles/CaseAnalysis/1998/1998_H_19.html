State of Uttar Pradesh v Harish Chandra and Company

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, K. Venkataswami, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 14; (1999) 1 SCC 63; 1999 (1) AllCJ 553; 1998 (2) ARBLR 716; 1998 (4) CLT 268; JT 1998 (8) SC 74; 1998 (3) RAJ 543; 1998(6) SCALE 173; 1998 (9) SLT 310; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 11; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 660; 1998 (8) Supreme 458

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Interest Act, 1978 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether award of interest prior to date of reference was within power and jurisdiction of Arbitrator? - Held, mere look at clause shows that claim for interest by way of damages was not to be entertained against Govt. - (B) Whether claims regarding cutting of hard rock were wrongly granted, cannot be made subject matter of an objection u/s. 30 of Arbitration Act, which could have been agitated for getting any reduction of amount as awarded by arbitrator? - Further held, it was a question purely on merits of award which could not be agitated in objections as they were not in nature of an appeal against award before court below - (C) Whether reduction of interest from 15 5 per cent to 6 per cent from date of decree till satisfaction of decree as ordered by HC was justify? - Further held, in our view, reasoning cannot be sustained that when arbitrator has found interest at rate of 6 per cent per annum to be reasonable, trial court ought to have adopted same rate of interest for being awarded to contractor - So far as court is concerned, it is in its discretion to award 15.5 per cent interest on decretal amount from date of decree till satisfaction of decree - Civil Appeal No. 7643 of 1995 dismissed - Petition No. 6307 of 1995 allowed.
