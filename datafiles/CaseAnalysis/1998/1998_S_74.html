Sangappa Kalyanappa Bangi (Dead) Through Lrs v Land Tribunal, Jamkhandi and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1928; (1998) 7 SCC 294; AIR 1998 SC 3229; 1999 ILR(Kar) 863; JT 1998 (6) SC 363; 1998(5) SCALE 265; 1998 (7) SLT 539; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 600; 1998 (7) Supreme 320

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 - Occupancy rights - Tenants - X claiming to be a tenant in respect of land in dispute filed application to the Land Tribunal for grant of occupancy rights in respect of the said land - During the pendency of the proceedings he made a Will bequeathing his tenancy rights in respect of the land in favour of one A, she in turn executed another Will under which H is making a claim to the land through the said X - Respondent No. (wife of X) and respondents No. 3 to 5 (children of X) - Appellate Authority and the Land Tribunal held that respondents No. 2 to 5 are entitled for grant of occupancy rights found no reasons to interfere with the order made by them - HC affirmed said order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a devise under a Will would amount to an assignment of interest in the lands and, therefore, invalid under the provisions of s. 21 of the Act.

Held, what is prohibited u/s. 21 of the Act is that there cannot be any sub-division or sub-letting of the land held by a tenant or assignment of any interest thereunder. The object and purpose of s. 21 of the Act being to confine the rights of tenancy only to those known under law as heirs and therefore, assignment to strangers is barred. Thus it can be seen that; a broad definition of an heir would not be of much help. Appeal dismissed.
