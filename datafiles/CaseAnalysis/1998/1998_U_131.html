Major M. R. Penghal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1912; (1998) 5 SCC 454; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1373; AIR 1999 SC 543; 1998 (80) FLR 300; JT 1998 (5) SC 624; 1999 LabIC 222; 1999 (1) LLJ 1211; 1998 (6) SLR 355; 1998 (9) SLT 279

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14(1)(a) - Vacancies of clerks - Appointment - Service on deputation - Jurisdiction - Appeal arises out of judgment and order passed by Central Administrative Tribunal rejecting application of appellant - Appellant in army service had no jurisdiction to decide such an application appears - Posts and Telegraphs Service Selection Board issued an advertisement inviting applications to fill in 1200 vacancies of clerks - Appellant in response to advertisement, submitted an application to appear in competitive examination - Appellant accepted offer of appointment and consequently posted as a civilian in army service on deputation - While working on deputation in Army, appellant was promoted to various higher ranks, including rank of a Major - Appellant alleged that he was entitled to further promotion, which he was denied - HC found that appellant was not a member of Armed Forces and his status was that of a civilian in army service on deputation and had no jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was an army personnel or a civilian posted on deputation in army service - Held, appellant was offered an appointment to work as a clerk in Army Postal Service on condition that he would remain a civilian employee on deputation in Army - Appellant accepted offer and agreed to conditions - Appellant had a lien with Posts and Telegraphs Department working on deputation in Indian Army Postal Service and at no point of time appellant became a full-fledged army personnel - Appellant was not a member of Armed Forces and continued to work as a civilian on deputation to Army Postal Service was covered u/s.14(1)(a) of 1985 Act - HC was right in rejecting petition filed by appellant, whereas Central Administrative Tribunal erroneously accepted claim of appellant that he was an army personnel - Appeal disposed of.
