East India Commercial Company Private Limited and another v Corporation of Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1591; (1998) 4 SCC 368; AIR 1998 SC 1789; JT 1998 (2) SC 738; 1998(2) SCALE 539; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 543; 1998 (4) SLT 233; 1998 (3) Supreme 425

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, s. 168 - W B Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 8 - Assessment of property tax - Determination of annual value of building - Rent Restriction Act providing for determination of fair rent or standard rent - S. 168 of 1951 Act does not contain any non obstante clause so as to make Tenancy Act inapplicable -Nor does Act itself provide method or basis for determining annual value - Whether fair rent determinable u/s. 8(1)(d) can be annual value for purpose of property tax? - Whether rateable value can be more than fair or standard rent? - Held, (A) Reading two Acts together rateable value cannot be more than fair or standard rent which can be fixed under Rent Control Act - Except when Municipal Act itself provides mode of determination or contains a non obstante clause - Appeal allowed.
