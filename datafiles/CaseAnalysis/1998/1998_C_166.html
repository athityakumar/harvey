Harshad Shantilal Mehta v Custodian and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.P. Kurdukar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 732; (1998) 5 SCC 1; AIR 1998 SC 2291; AIR 1998 SCW 2192; 1998 (3) CLT 19; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 936; [1998] 231 ITR 871; JT 1998 (4) SC 23; 1998(3) SCALE 556; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 389; 1998 (5) SLT 243; 1998 (4) Supreme 537; [1998] 99 TAXMAN 216

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Special Court (Trial Of Offences Relating To Transactions In Securities) Amendment Act, 1994

Summary: Civil - Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992, s. 11 - Whether the assessed tax liability for the statutory period requires to be discharged in full under s. 11(2)(a) or whether the Special Court has any discretion as regards extent of payment to be made under s. 11(2)(a) - Held, no - No question of reopening of tax assessments before the Special Court - Liability of person to pay the tax will be determined under the machinery under relevant tax law - Special Court can decide how much of that liability will be discharged out of the funds in the hands of the Custodian - Special Court can examine if there is any fraud, collusion or miscarriage of justice in assessment proceedings - Taxing authorities can realise the remaining liability from the assessee, but it will not be paid in priority over the claims of other persons - Disposed accordingly.
