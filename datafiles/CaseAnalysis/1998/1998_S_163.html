Vijayabai and Others v Shriram Tukaram and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1308; (1999) 1 SCC 693; AIR 1999 SC 431; 1998 (5) Bom.C.R. 913; JT 1998 (8) SC 105; 1998(6) SCALE 211; 1998 (9) SLT 431; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 54; 1998 (8) Supreme 492

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Administrative - Bombay Tenancy and Agriculture Lands (Vidarbha Region) Act, 1958, ss. 8, 49B - Suit land - Cultivation - Tenant - Compromise - Deletion of name - Allurement - Legality - Appellants, who owned and possessed suit land, filed application before Tenancy Tahsildar for deleting name of respondent no. 1 from list of tenants to correct record prepared u/s. 8(1) 1958 Act - During pendency of proceedings, compromise was arrived at between appellants and respondent no. 1 on 21-12-1960 wherein respondent no. 1 admitted that he was never tenant of appellants over disputed land - In pursuance of the same, Tahsildar on 30-12-1960 ordered deletion of name of respondent no. 1 - As none filed appeal against the order it became final - Respondent no. 1 later purchased part of suit land from appellants - Tahsildar suo motu by order dt. 2-2-1971 held that respondent No. 1 had not cultivated suit land as tenant during 1958-59 and hence he was not entitled for restoration of suit land and dropped proceedings u/s. 49B - Aggrieved by said Order, respondent no. 1 preferred appeal before Special Deputy Collector, who allowed appeal by holding that compromise was brought upon under pressure and allurement, hence could not be acted upon thus held that respondent no. 1 to be tenant of suit land on basis of sole entry recorded u/s. 8 of the Act published on 1-4-1959 - Appellant's revision was dismissed by Revenue Tribunal - Writ petition and Letters Patent Appeal filed against said Order were subsequently dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Held, Tahsildar while exercising his suo motu power u/s. 49B should initiate on basis of materials before him not arbitrarily - On the records of Tahsildar when he initiated proceeding u/s. 49B, admittedly only documentary evidence on record was the sole entry of 1958-59 which stood deleted by order of competent authority, Tahsildar himself in accordance with law under this very same statute - Orders passed u/s. 8 of the Act was not subject to s. 49 B - Finally adjudication had already been made u/s. 8, as to who was tenant in instant case - Tahsildar reopened very question which finally stood concluded that was whether respondent No. 1 was or was not tenant of suit land - So now at relevant date there did not exist any record for the Tahsildar to initiate proceedings suo motu except the record of 1958-59 entry which stood deleted - On the facts of this case and further when respondent did not raise any such issue for 11 years, it was found, exercise of power by Tahsildar suo motu u/s. 49B to be without jurisdiction and unsustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
