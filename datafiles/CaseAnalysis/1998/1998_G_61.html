Vaijanath and Others v Guramma and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1304; (1999) 1 SCC 292; AIR 1999 SC 555; AIR 1998 SCW 3887; 1999 (2) Bom.C.R. 504; JT 1998 (8) SC 125; 1998(6) SCALE 248; 1998 (9) SLT 441; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 36; 1998 (8) Supreme 533

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Joint family - Agricultural land - Partition - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Hyderabad (Application of Central Acts) Act, 1952, s. 3 - Whether under Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act as applicable to erstwhile Hyderabad where lands were situated, respondent widow of deceased R was not entitled to a share in joint family agricultural lands? - Held, s. 3 of Hyderabad (Application of Central Acts.) Act, 1952, stated that Acts specified therein shall, with effect from appointed day, extend to, be in force in whole of State of Hyderabad, subject to modification mentioned in schedule, shall accordingly be in force in said state with effect from said date - In Schedule to said Act, modification to Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Only modification is, 'for sub-section (2) of s. 1, following sub-section shall be substituted, 'it extends to whole of State of Hyderabad' - Annexure 'C' to said Hyderabad Act of 1952 sets out text of Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 as modified by aforesaid Schedule and applicable in State of Hyderabad under sub-section (3) of said Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, when a Hindu governed by any school other than Dayabhaga school of Hindu law or a customary law, dies intestate having at time of his death an interest in a Hindu joint family property, his widow shall have in property same interest as he himself had, subject to sub-section (3) - Sub section (3) interest devolving on a Hindu widow shall be limited interest known as a Hindu woman's estate - There is no definition of property under Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Therefore, term "property" has to be given its ordinary meaning which would include agricultural land also - Language of Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 as enacted in State of Hyderabad is as general as original Act - Words "property" as well as "interest" in joint family property are wide enough to cover agricultural lands - Therefore, on an interpretation of Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 as enacted by State of Hyderabad, Act covers agricultural lands - Appeal dismissed.
