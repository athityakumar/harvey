In Re:Ajay Kumar Pandey, v Na

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1239; (1998) 7 SCC 248; AIR 1998 SC 3299; 1998 (4) CCR 25; 1998 CRLJ 4606; JT 1998 (6) SC 571; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 501; 1998(5) SCALE 393; 1998 (8) SLT 138; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 87; 1998 (7) Supreme 473

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(c) - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 - Advocate using intemperate language and casting unwarranted aspersions on various judicial officers and attributing motives to them while discharging their judicial functions - Making continuous attempts to subvert course of justice in whichever court his case was contemner - Held, guilty of gross contempt of Court sentenced to four months simple imprisonment and fine of Rs. 1000 - Order accordingly.
