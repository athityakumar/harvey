Rajinder Krishan Khanna and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1343; (1998) 7 SCC 129; AIR 1999 SC 463; 1998 (4) AWC 783; 1998 (4) CLT 197; JT 1998 (7) SC 128; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 117; 1998 (3) RAJ 465; 1998(5) SCALE 589; 1999 (1) SCJ 177; 1998 (8) SLT 284; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 302; 1998 (8) Supreme 98

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Land & Property - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34(1), 34(2) and 34(3) - Appellants filed a writ petition in High Court against respondents - Grievance in writ petition was that overflow of effluents and slurry from a pond in second respondent's premises due to breaches in earthen bund thereof had damaged writ petitioners' lands, crops, mango crops, houses, etc - Writ petition dismissed - Appeal - Parties informed Supreme Court to settle dispute through arbitration - Arbitration agreement - Arbitrators made an award - Second respondent filed objections to making award a rule of court - Whether award fell outside ambit of reference to arbitration made by Supreme Court and far beyond terms of arbitration agreement? - S. 34(1) of Act states that recourse to a court against an arbitral award may be made only by an application for setting aside such award in accordance with sub-sections (2) and (3) - There was no claim for damages for alleged loss of potential of lands and no averments or particulars in that behalf - Perusal of award suggests that learned arbitrators did not think that they could award compensation for alleged lost potential of land only if they found that land could not be reclaimed; there is, therefore, no such evidence or discussion or finding in award - Relief that was sought was a direction to second respondent to reclaim appellants' land; awarding compensation for alleged lost potential of land was not moulding relief that was sought - Held, award of Rs. 77,19,800 for loss of potential of land and interest thereon falls outside scope of reference to arbitration and is not in relation to a dispute contemplated thereby - Award set aside - Application to set aside award allowed - Order accordingly.
