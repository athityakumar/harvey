Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi (Central-I) v Continental Construction Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1518; (1998) 2 SCC 632; AIR 1998 SC 1586; 1998 (145) CTR 1; [1998] 230 ITR 485; JT 1998 (1) SC 398; 1998(1) SCALE 361; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 519; 1998 (2) SLT 633; 1998 (2) Supreme 26; [1998] 96 TAXMAN 625; 1998 (2) TLR 514

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 40 A(5)(c)(a)(b) and 40(c) - Whether remuneration paid to directors in respect of their employment outside India has to be excluded from 72000 limit u/ss. 40 - A(5)(a) and 40(c) of Act or not? - Held, purpose of prescribing a ceiling on expenditure in connection with Directors and employees u/ss. 40(c) and 40-A (5), is to discourage a company or an organisation from paying excessive salaries, remuneration, perquisites to its employees and directors - From this ceiling limit, certain kinds of expenditure on employees have been excluded - Includes expenditure on an employee posted outside India - Done so because of certain exigencies might arise - Appeal dismissed.
