Union of India and Others v P. Thayagarajan

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1313; (1999) SCC (L&S) 384; (1999) 1 SCC 733; AIR 1999 SC 449; 1999 (1) CLR 251; 1999 (1) ESC 196; 1999 (81) FLR 76; JT 1998 (8) SC 179; 1999 LabIC 169; 1999 (1) LLJ 507; 1998(6) SCALE 271; 1999 (2) SLJ 72; 1998 (5) SLR 734; 1998 (9) SLT 455; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 114; 1998 (9) Supreme 30; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 399

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Conducting The Enquiry, Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, r. 27(c) - Whether r. 27(c) enables disciplinary authority to record his findings on report and to pass an appropriate order including ordering a de novo enquiry in a case of present nature? - Where there has been no proper enquiry because of some serious defect having crept into enquiry or some important witnesses were not available at time of enquiry or were not examined, disciplinary authority may ask enquiry officer to record further evidence but that provision would not enable disciplinary authority to set aside previous enquiries on ground that report of enquiry officer does not appeal to disciplinary authority - Basis upon which disciplinary authority set aside enquiry is that procedure adopted by enquiry officer was contrary to relevant rules and affects rights of parties and not that report does not appeal to him - Held when important evidence, either to be relied upon by department or by delinquent official, is shut out, this would result in miscarriage of justice and therefore r. 27(c) enables disciplinary authority to record his findings on report and to pass a de novo enquiry - Appeal allowed.
