The Commissioner Of Income Tax, Lucknow v U.P. Forest Corporation

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 466; (1998) 3 SCC 530; AIR 1998 SC 1125; 1998 ALJ 712; 1998 (145) CTR 402; [1998] 230 ITR 945; JT 1998 (2) SC 205; 1998(2) SCALE 138; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 22; 1998 (3) SLT 403; 1998 (2) Supreme 290; 1998 TAXLR 338; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 259; 1998 (2) TLR 338

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Forest Corporation Act, 1974

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 10(20) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(31) - Local Authority - Exemption from Tax - Respondent claimed its status to be that of local authority during course of assessment proceedings and claimed its income liable to be exempted from levy of tax - Assessing officer rejected the claim and taxed it in status of artificial jurisdictional person and HC rejected decision of appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent was a local authority whose income exempted from tax u/s. 10(20) of the 1961 Act - Held, no - Other authority referred to in Section 3(31) of the 1897 Act must be similar or akin to municipal committee district board or body a Port Commissioners - Members of respondent Corporation not wholly or partly directly or indirectly elected by inhabitants of area - Respondent not a local authority whose income was not exempted from tax u/s.10(20) of the 1961 Act - Appeal allowed.
