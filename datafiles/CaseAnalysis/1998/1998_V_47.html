Modi Spinning and Weaving Mills v Virendra and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1601; (1998) 5 SCC 718; 1999 (1) AWC 181; JT 1998 (6) SC 623; 1998 (9) SLT 291

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984, s. 23(2) - Uttar Pradesh Act, 1954 - (A) Whether company, for whose benefit land may have been acquired under provisions of Land Acquisition Act, is competent to file appeal? - Question of law stands answered by a Constitution Bench of Supreme Court in U P Awas Evam Vikas Parishad v Gyan Devi [1994 Indlaw SC 2157] in that Conclusion 7 provided that in event of enhancement of amount of compensation by reference court if Govt. does not file appeal, local authority can file appeal against award in High Court after obtaining leave of court - Held, no specific leave in terms of Conclusion 7 has been taken from Supreme Court focussing issue; Company should not be permitted to challenge impugned order of HC - (B) What is impact of Land Acquistion (Amendment) Act, 1984 whereunder enhanced rate of solatium and enhanced rate of interest was grantable? - Further held, by means of U P Act, 1954, s. 23(2) of Act stood omitted with effect from 19-11-1954 as a result of which State was not obliged to pay any solatium to land owners; seeing to injustice on account of non-payment of solatium and higher rate of interest, U P State Legislature had itself stepped in on 31-07-1972 to reinsert s. 23(2) of Act as it originally stood - Appeal dismissed.
