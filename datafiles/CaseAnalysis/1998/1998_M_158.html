Commissioner of Income Tax, Karnataka, Bangalore v Messrs Shaan Finance Private Limited, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 651; (1998) 3 SCC 605; AIR 1998 SC 1372; 1998 (146) CTR 110; [1998] 231 ITR 308; JT 1998 (2) SC 564; 1998(2) SCALE 395; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 367; 1998 (3) SLT 539; 1998 (3) Supreme 213; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 435

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 32A - Investment allowance - Beneficial Statute - Taxing Statute - Assessee companies/respondent were financial companies which purchased machinery and hire out machinery to manufacturers under agreements of hire - Investment allowance u/s. 32A of the Act claimed by assessee - Respondent/revenue refused investment allowance and HC allowed investment allowance - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessees entitlement to investment allowance u/s. 32A of the Act - Held, yes - Agreement not treated as transfer or disallowing grant of investment allowance as assessee complies with requirements of s. 32A of the Act which was a beneficial provision in a taxing statute - Interpreting a fiscal statute Court cannot proceed to make good deficiencies and if there be any Court must interpret statute as it stands and in case of doubt in a manner favorable to tax-payer - Hence, s. 32A of the Act covered leasing or finance companies which gave machinery on hire - Appeal dismissed.
