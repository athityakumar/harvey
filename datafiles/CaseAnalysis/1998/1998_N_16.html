State Uttar Pradesh v Nahar Singh (Dead) and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 478; (1998) 3 SCC 561; (1998) SCC (Cr) 850; AIR 1998 SC 1328; 1998 (2) CCR 24; 1998 (1) Crimes 197; 1998 CRLJ 2006; JT 1998 (2) SC 41; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 867; 1998(1) SCALE 699; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 948; 1998 (3) SLT 510; 1998 (2) Supreme 139

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ,ss. 148,302,449 and 201 - Conviction and sentence - Evidence - Acquittal - Immaterial - Accused Nos. 1 to 8 were tried by Additional District and Session Judge for various offences punishable under different provisions of IPC - Accused Nos.1,2,6 quality of offences punishable u/s.148, 302,449 and 201 of IPC - Other accused persons held not quality of offences punishable u/s.147, 148, 392/149, 449 and 201 IPC and they were accordingly acquitted of all charges leveled against them - Trial Court had correctly assessed evidence on record and after elaborate discussion found accused Nos.2 and 6 quality of offences charted - HC was not justified in acquitting Accused Nos.2 and 6 for reasons which are trivial and contrary to evidence - HC set aside conviction and sentence and allowed their appeals and dismissed appeals filed by State against acquittal of seven accused - Hence, instant appeal -Whether HC interfering with conviction and sentence of accused Nos.2 and 6 was justified - Held, appeal was against acquittal would be immaterial - Basic principle that presumption of innocence of accused gets strengthened by fact of acquittal by court - Evidence taken by HC was erroneous and that it misled itself in coming to conclusion that quilt of accused Nos.2 and A-6 was not established - Prosecution had proved quilt of accused beyond any reasonable doubt - Trial court was right in convicting them and that HC was not justified in interfering with conviction and sentence of accused Nos.2 and 6 on grounds which were hardly sustainable in law - Appeals disposed of.
