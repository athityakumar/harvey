Commissioner of Rural Development and Others v A. S. Jagannathan

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1917; (1999) 2 SCC 313; AIR 1999 SC 3368; 1999 LabIC 2617; 1999 (1) LLJ 1083; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 127

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 15 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Tamil Nadu Civil Services Rules, 1953, r. 8, 8(v)(a) - Tamil Nadu Pension Rules 1978, r. 9(1)(b) - Imposition of three punishments under r. 8 of 1953 Rules etc for same charge - Looking to serious nature of charges proved but fact that he is about to retire, punishment of stoppage of two increments without cumulative effect imposed - Whether impugned order of Tribunal interfering with punishment imposed by disciplinary authority is proper? - Held, High Court/tribunal, while exercising power of judicial review, cannot normally substitute its own conclusion on penalty, unless punishment is such as would shock conscience of HC - Punishment imposed cannot be interfered with by Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
