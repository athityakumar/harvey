Nain Singh Bhakuni and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1729; (1998) 3 SCC 348; (1998) SCC (L&S) 850; AIR 1998 SC 622; JT 1998 (1) SC 43; 1998 LabIC 843; 1998 (2) LLJ 633; 1998(1) SCALE 43; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 41; 1998 (2) SLJ 168; 1998 (1) SLR 482; 1998 (2) SLT 255; 1998 (1) Supreme 163

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appellants are draftsmen in Central Water Commission - Claim of revised pay scale, notional and actual at par with revised scale of draftsman in CPWD - Entitlement of - (A) Whether Tribunal was in error in not granting revised pay scales to draftsmen of CWC notionally and actually by way of arrears of revised pay scales? - Held, qualification wise draftsmen in CWC at base level or in higher echelons of service were not identically situated as compared to their counterpart draftsmen Grades III, II and I in CPWD - Decision of Tribunal is sustained - (B) Whether OM of 1984 was diluted by respondent authorities in cases of similarly situated draftsmen working in other Govt. departments and departments of Central Govt. similarly situated draftsmen? - Held, it could not be said that appellants have been discriminated by respondents in not giving them further benefit of revised pay scales both notionally and actually than that which was given to them by Tribunal pursuant to OM of 1984 - (C) Whether relief could be denied to appellants on ground that they had not gone to Board of Arbitration? - Held, merely because appellants had not gone to Board of Arbitration, if relief given to CPWD draftsmen by said Board of Arbitration which was accepted by authorities became available to appellants who were identically situated and were doing same type of work as draftsmen in other departments of Govt., question whether they had gone for similar arbitration or not would pale into insignificance - (D) Whether appellants are entitled to similar relief of retrospective grant of benefits as similarly situated draftsmen working in other Govt. departments? - Held, so far as retrospective application was concerned appellants remained unequal as compared to draftsmen in CPWD between 1982 and 1987 and it is only because of application of OM of 1984 that they were given benefit by Tribunal which could not be further enhanced in their favour - Appeal dismissed.
