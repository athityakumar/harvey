State of Andhra Pradesh v V. C. Subbarayudu and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1415; (1998) 2 SCC 516; (1998) SCC (L&S) 605; AIR 1998 SC 848; JT 1998 (1) SC 198; 1998 LabIC 618; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 98; 1998(1) SCALE 170; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 299; 1998 (3) SLJ 5; 1998 (1) SLR 476; 1998 (2) SLT 586; 1998 (1) Supreme 297; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 570

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 226 - (A) Creation of cadre - Interference by Court - Cadre of divisional accountants under state Govt. - Limited by GOM 304 only to divisional accountants working on deputation in state - (A) Whether Court can direct Govt. to consider absorption even of SAS accountants on deputation? - (B) Whether policy of Govt. to take over cadre of divisional accountants but not SAS accountants discriminatory? - (C) Division bench disagreeing with earlier division bench ruling - Whether it should refer matter to larger bench - Held, (A) Court cannot direct Govt. to consider absorption even on deputation - Policy of Govt. to take over cadre of divisional accountants but not SAS accountants - (B) Not discriminatory - (C) As a matter of propriety refer matter to larger bench - Appeal allowed.
