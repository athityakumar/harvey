Mirah Exports Pvt. Ltd v Collector Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 522; (1998) 3 SCC 292; AIR 1998 SC 928; 1998 (75) ECR 1; 1998 (98) E.L.T. 3; JT 1998 (1) SC 489; 1998 (25) RLT 1; 1998(1) SCALE 391; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 580; 1998 (2) SLT 571; 1998 (1) Supreme 465

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Price List

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s.14 - Undervaluation of goods - Proof - Assessees, importers of ball bearings - Invoice price of Assessees were the same at which other importers had imported - No special price given to assessee - No specific allegation nor any evidence shown that the importers and foreign suppliers had any interest in the business of each other - Held, said invoice price therefore could be treated as price at which the goods are ordinarily Sold in course of international trade - No undervaluation of goods.
