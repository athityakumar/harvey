Benny T.D. and Others v Registrar of Co-Operative Societies and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 677; (1998) 5 SCC 269; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1338; AIR 1998 SC 2012; JT 1998 (3) SC 663; 1998 (1) KarLJ 602; 1998 (1) KLT 858; 1998 LabIC 1887; 1999 (1) LLJ 527; 1998 (3) LLN 10; 1998(3) SCALE 392; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 147; 1998 (3) SLJ 279; 1998 (5) SLT 4; 1998 (4) Supreme 490

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Kerala Co-operative Societies Act, 1969, s.80(4) - Co-operative Societies Rules, 1969, rr. 176, 183, 186, 187 - (A) Whether conclusion of HC that there has been an infraction of s. 80(4) of Act is at all correct? - Held, there is neither any allegation nor any finding that quota meant for SC/ST candidates has been usurped either by General category candidates or by candidates belonging to Primary Societies - No violation of s. 80(4) - Conclusion of HC on aforesaid basis wholly erroneous - (B) Whether HC was justified in coming to conclusion that both Banks violated provisions of r.187? - Held, it would be logical to hold no person would be eligible for appointment to 50% of vacancies arising in apex society unless he possesses necessary additional qualification as provided in r. 187 - Construction put to r. 187 by HC is wholly erroneous and same cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
