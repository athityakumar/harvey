Basudeo Tiwary v Sido Kanhu University and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1209; (1998) 8 SCC 194; AIR 1998 SC 3261; JT 1998 (6) SC 464; 1998 LabIC 3539; 1999 (1) LLJ 200; 1998 (4) LLN 617; 1998(5) SCALE 300; 1999 (2) SLJ 269; 1998 (3) SLR 333; 1998 (7) SLT 559; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 633; 1998 (7) Supreme 361

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination - Validity of - Bihar University Act, s. 4(14) - (A) Whether appellant had been given an opportunity of being heard before terminating his services? - Held, no - In sphere of public employment, it is well settled that any action taken by employer against an employee must be fair, just and reasonable which are components of fair treatment - Conferment of absolute power to terminate services of an employee is an antithesis to fair, just and reasonable treatment - Omission to impose hearing requirement in statute under which impugned action is being taken does not exclude hearing - It may be implied from nature of power, particularly when right of a party is affected adversely - (B) Whether in absence of opportunity of being heard, such termination is valid? - Held, no - In order to arrive at a conclusion that an appointment is contrary to provisions of Act, statutes, rules or regulations, etc. a finding has to be recorded and unless such a finding is recorded, termination cannot be made, but to arrive at such a conclusion necessarily an enquiry will have to be made as to whether such appointment was contrary to provisions of Act etc. - If in a given case such exercise is absent, condition precedent stands unfulfilled - Person whose appointment is under enquiry will have to be issued a notice - Appeal allowed.
