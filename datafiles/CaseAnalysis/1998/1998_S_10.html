Babu Ram Alias Durga Prasad v Indra Pal Singh (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1136; (1998) 6 SCC 358; AIR 1998 SC 3021; 1998 (4) AWC 864; 1998 (3) CLT 154; JT 1998 (5) SC 464; 1998(4) SCALE 565; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 1145; 1998 (7) SLT 71; 1998 (6) Supreme 439

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 62 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 54 and 55 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, ss. 37 and 43 - (A) Second appeal - Powers of court - Whether giving finding as to a new contract on issue which was not pressed in trial court permissible? - There was no issue or evidence - Evidence was to contrary - Whether findings would be liable to be set aside? - (B) New plea - Suit on basis of agreement - Whether plea as to novation of contract can be raised for first time in second appeal? - (C) Sale deed - Agreement preceding sale - deed containing clauses of reconveyance sale - deed executed subsequently - Absence of reference to agreement of reconveyance in sale - Whether can lead to inference that said right was given up by plaintiff? - (D) Undischarged insolvent - Exercising option of repurchase of property - Filed suit for specific performance of agreement of reconveyance - Both of his actions get retrospectively validated upon unconditional annulment of adjudication with effect from date of filing of insolvency petition - (E) Agreement of reconveyance - Time is an essence of contract in such agreements - Plaintiff, an undischarged insolvent exercising option within period stipulated in sale - Whether defendant-vendee was bound to reconvey property by receiving sum from plaintiff as stipulated in contract? - (F) Contract of reconveyance - Suit for specific performance - Limitation - Period of 5 years was fixed for exercising option to repurchase - It is not specified in agreement that vendee shall execute deed of repurchase within a particular period from date of exercise of option - Whether second part of third column of art 54 of 1963 Act would be applicable or first part? - Time would start to run only from date when defendant refused to execute deed of reconveyance - Suit filed within 3 years from that date - Whether barred? - Held, (A) In Second Appeal u/s. 100 of CPC High Code has no jurisdiction to give a finding on an issue which was not present in trial court - Accordingly findings would be liable to be set aside - (B) Cannot be raised for first time in second appeal - (C) Such reference/recital is customary - But its absence cannot lead to inference that said right was given up by plaintiff - (D) Both of his actions get retrospectively validated - (E) Defendant-vendee was bound to re convey property - (F) Second part of third column of art. 54 of 1963 Act would be applicable and not first part - Suit not barred - Appeal allowed.
