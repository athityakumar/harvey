Employees' State Insurance Corporation v S. K. Aggarwal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1103; (1998) 6 SCC 288; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1480; AIR 1998 SC 2676; 1998 (2) CLR 518; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 75; 1998 CRLJ 4027; JT 1998 (5) SC 233; 1998 (2) LLJ 794; 1998 (3) LLN 920; 1999 (2) SLJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Service

Keywords: Employees State Insurance (Amendment) Act, 1975

Summary: Indian Penal Code,1860, s. 405, Explanation 2 - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2(17) - Whether Director against whom criminal proceedings for default in payment of contribution to Employees State Insurance, is employer within meaning of Act? - Held, it is Co. and not Director which is employer and therefore criminal breach of trust cannot be imposed on it.
