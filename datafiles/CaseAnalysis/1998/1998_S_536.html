Dr. L.P. Misra v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 842; (1998) 7 SCC 379; AIR 1998 SC 3337; 1998 (4) AWC 892; 1998 (3) CCR 235; 1998 (3) Crimes 204; 1998 CRLJ 4603; JT 1998 (6) SC 6; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 120; 1998(4) SCALE 662; 1998 (7) SLT 149; 1998 (6) Supreme 583

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, art.215; Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s.19; Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952, Chap.XXXV-E - Contempt - Scandalising the Court - Allegation, appellant advocate using abusive language against High Court Judge and entering into court room raising slogans - Order of High Court holding guilty of Contempt - Procedure prescribed by Law not followed, yet - Held, is not proper - Matter remitted - Jurisdiction under art.215 has to be followed in accordance with the procedure prescribed by law.
