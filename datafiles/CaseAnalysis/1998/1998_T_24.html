Raj Deo Sharma v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1131; (1998) 7 SCC 507; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1692; AIR 1998 SC 3281; AIR 1999 SCW 3208; 1998 (4) CCR 11; 1998 (4) Crimes 53; 1998 CRLJ 4596; 1998 (2) JCC 256; JT 1998 (7) SC 1; 1999 (1) KLT 173; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 396; 1998(5) SCALE 477; 1999 (1) SCJ 140; 1998 (8) SLT 194; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 130; 1998 (7) Supreme 556

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 - Speedy trial - Accused charged for offences under Prevention of Corruption Act - Delay in trial - More than 13 years had elapsed since institution of FIR - Application for quashing prosecution - Rejection - Ground, delay was due to fact that there was only one Special Court of CBI functioning and large number of cases were pending before it - Held, it was not proper - Court issued guidelines in addition to propositions laid down in pre decided case in view of large pendency of cases before Special Court, for more than two decades - Appeal allowed.
