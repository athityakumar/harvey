Arul Nadar v Authorised Officer, Land Reforms

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.M. Punchhi, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1221; (1998) 7 SCC 157; AIR 1998 SC 3288; JT 1998 (6) SC 499; 1998(5) SCALE 369; 1998 (8) SLT 65; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 1; 1998 (7) Supreme 438

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Land) Act, 1961- Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Reduction of Ceiling of Land) Act, 1970, s. 21-A - Whether proceedings, having once initiated under principal Act, same have to be continued and concluded thereunder and s. 21-A which was brought into statue by Act, 1970 will have no application? - Held, s. 21-A does apply to a proceeding which was pending on date aforesaid provision was inserted in principal Act even though proceeding might have been initiated under principal Act itself - While legislature intended to reduce ceiling area from 30 standard acres to 15 standard acres, they themselves provided for a transfer holiday by inserting s. 21-A and there would be no justification to give any restrictive meaning to said provision of s. 21-A where legislature indicated that aforesaid provision is notwithstanding anything contained in s. 22 or in any other provision of Act or in any other law for time being in force - Further held, no inconsistency between decision in AG Varadarajulu v State of T.N (1998 Indlaw SC 2169) and Gopal Reddiar v. State of T.N (1995 Indlaw SC 728), two operate in different fields - Appeal allowed.
