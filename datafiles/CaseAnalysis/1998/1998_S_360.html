K. Chandrasekhar, Mariam Rasheeda, S.K. Sharma v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 715; (1998) 5 SCC 223; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1291; 1998 (37) ACC 136; AIR 1998 SC 2001; AIR 1998 SCW 1852; 1998 (3) CCR 11; 1998 (2) Crimes 300; 1998 CRLJ 2897; 1998 (2) JCC 5; JT 1998 (3) SC 612; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 719; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 720; 1998(3) SCALE 294; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 72; 1998 (5) SLT 130; 1998 (4) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Official Secrets Act, 1923, Foreigners Order, 1948

Summary: Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 ; Delhi Special Police Establishment Act 1946, s.6 ; Criminal Procedure Code 1974, s.173 ; CPC 1974, s.156 - Investigation - Entrusted to CBI with consent of State Govt. concerned - CBI completed Investigation and submitted final report - Subsequent withdrawal of consent by State Govt. for further Investigation of case by State Police - Fairness of Investigation was also in question - Held, subsequent withdrawal of consent by State Govt. was impermissible - If an opinion has already been formed regarding the guilt of the accused and, that too, at a stage when the commission of the offence itself is yet to be proved, it is obvious that the investigation cannot and will not be fair.
