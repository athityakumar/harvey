State of Gujarat and Another v Hon'BLE High Court of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1233; (1998) 7 SCC 392; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1640; AIR 1998 SC 3164; 1998 (4) CCR 39; 1998 (4) Crimes 12; 1998 CRLJ 4561; JT 1998 (6) SC 530; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 350; 1998(5) SCALE 410; 1998 (8) SLT 70; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 31; 1998 (7) Supreme 511

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Victimology, Convention, Research, Rehabilitation, Hazardous, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Prison Act, 1894, Essential Services Maintenance Ordinance, 1960

Summary: Criminal - Labour & Industrial Law - Prison Act, 1894 - Universal Declaration of Human Rights - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 357 - Whether prisoners who are required to do labour as part of their punishment, should necessarily be paid wages for such work at rates prescribed under minimum wages law? - Putting prisoner to hard labour while he is undergoing sentence of rigorous imprisonment awarded to him cannot be equated with "begar" or "other similar forms of forced labour" and there is no violation of cl. (1) of art. 23 of Constitution of India, 1950 - Cl. (2) of art. 23 has no application in such case - Held, Constitution however does not bar State by appropriate legislation from granting wages to prisoners subject to hard labour under courts' orders, for their beneficial purpose or otherwise.
