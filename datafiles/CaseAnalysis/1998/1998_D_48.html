Saleem v District Judge, Muzaffarnagar and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1203; (1998) 7 SCC 242; 1998 (2) ARC 617; 1999 (1) AWC 183; JT 1998 (6) SC 503; 1998 (2) RCJ 627; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 372; 1998(5) SCALE 360; 1998 (8) SLT 60; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 625; 1998 (7) Supreme 456

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 2(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Construction - Completion - Tenant - Rent Act - Protection - Respondent no. 3 filed suit for eviction of appellant/tenant, who was carrying on his profession as barber, from suit shop for default of rent - Trial Court decreed suit after coming to conclusion that tenant was in arrears of rent and protection under provisions of 1972 Act would not be applicable to him - Appellant preferred revision before District Court which dismissed revision - Aggrieved by said Order, appellant preferred petition before HC which dismissed the same - Hence, instant appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution - Appellant contended that courts below including HC were in error in taking the view that Rent Act was not applicable to suit shop - Whether appellant was protected under provisions of the 1972 Act - Held, the 1972 Act would provide that relevant provisions would not apply to buildings for period of 10 years from date of completion of their construction - Perusal of Explanation I to s. 2(2) of the Act would make it clear that date of occupation would be taken to be date of completion of the construction only when there was no record of completion of the construction or no assessment thereof - If there was assessment, it would be date of first assessment which would be deemed to be date of completion of the construction - Extract of sanction of water connection by Municipality dt. 1-11-1973 and relied on by appellant itself would not show that construction of suit shop had come into existence on that date and on the contrary, Order of assessment dt. 20-3-1982 relied upon by respondent would clearly indicate that the premises continued to be open plot till 31-3-1982 - Therefore, it was obvious that the suit premises had come into existence somewhere in the beginning of 1982 and provisions of the 1972 Act would not be applicable - Om Prakash Gupta vs. DIG Vijendrapal Gupta, 1982 Indlaw SC 200, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
