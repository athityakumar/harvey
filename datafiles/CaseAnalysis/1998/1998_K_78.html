Vimal Singh v Khuman Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1127; (1998) 7 SCC 223; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1574; AIR 1998 SC 3380; 1998 (4) CCR 19; 1998 (4) Crimes 63; 1999 CRLJ 16; 1998 (2) JCC 243; JT 1998 (7) SC 98; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 423; 1998(5) SCALE 495; 1999 (1) SCJ 130; 1998 (8) SLT 182; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 170; 1998 (8) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Murder - Threaten - Quarrel - Conviction - Order against acquittal - Reappraisal of evidence - Deceased was coming from Khalihan when accused 'V'/appellant, quarreled with him, gave knife blow - Next morning while deceased, (PW-3) and (PW-4) were going to in a bus for lodging FIR , appellant stopped bus and threatened deceased not to lodge any report - This led to a quarrel in bus - It was alleged that appellant gave a knife blow to deceased, who died later on -Trial court declared, prosecution failed to prove the charge beyond reasonable doubt and acquitted the appellant - State did not file any appeal against acquittal - PW-3 sent a letter to High Court against acquittal of the accused/appellant, which was treated as a Revision against order of acquittal - HC, reappraised evidence and set aside order of acquittal - HC opined that, appellant was guilty of the offence u/s. 304 Part-I IPC - Hence instant appeal by appellant challenging the aforesaid conviction - Whether the judgment and order of HC in reversing Trial Court's order was deemed to be proper and sustainable in law - Held, HC entered into domain of reappraisal of evidence which it was not authorized to do in exercise of its reversional power - Order under appeal not sustainable in law - Judgment and order of High Court set aside - Trial court's order restored - Order deserves to be quashed - Appeal allowed.
