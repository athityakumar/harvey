Om Prakash and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1036; (1998) 6 SCC 1; AIR 1998 SC 2504; JT 1998 (4) SC 601; 1998(4) SCALE 73; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 643; 1998 (6) SLT 262; 1998 (5) Supreme 513

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Emergency Provisions

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(4) and 5 A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Acquisition of land - Dispensing with inquiry - Invoking urgency clause - Ground not pleaded in proceedings by concerned authorities - Held, court cannot justify their action - S. 5-A should be treated to be almost otiose or inoperative in cases where purpose of acquisition is development of city or town.
