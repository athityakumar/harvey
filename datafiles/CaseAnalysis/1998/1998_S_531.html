State of Uttar Pradesh v Shatrughan Lal and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2010; (1998) 6 SCC 651; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1635; AIR 1998 SC 3038; 1998 (3) AWC 2373; 1998 (80) FLR 389; JT 1998 (6) SC 55; 1998 (2) LLJ 799; 1998 (4) LLN 639; 1998(5) SCALE 1; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 939; 1999 (1) SLJ 213; 1998 (5) SLR 43; 1998 (7) SLT 196; 1998 (6) Supreme 587

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Termination of services of Govt. servant - Effective opportunity of hearing - Preliminary inquiry - Copies of statements of witnesses examined therein, not supplied even though asked for - Copies of documents indicated to be relied upon in charge-sheet not supplied - Nor was it indicated to delinquent in writing that he may inspect record - (A) Whether merely saying that he could have inspected documents at any time is enough? - (B) Whether effective opportunity was afforded to delinquent? - (C) Whether order setting aside termination proper? - Held, (A) Not enough - Access to record must be assured to him - (B) Effective opportunity was not afforded - (C) Order setting aside termination, proper - Appeal dismissed.
