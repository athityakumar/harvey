P.V. Narasimha Rao v State(Cbi/SPE)

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 690; (1998) 4 SCC 626; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1108; AIR 1998 SC 2120; 1998 (2) CCR 138; 1998 (2) Crimes 124; 1998 CRLJ 2930; JT 1998 (3) SC 318; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 260; 1998(3) SCALE 53; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 870; 1998 (4) SLT 400; 1998 (4) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Criminal Statute, Salary, Allowances And Pension Of Members Of Parliament Act, 1954

Summary: Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 ; Constitution of India, arts.105(2) and 105(3) - Member of Parliament - Privileges - Meaning and scope of expression "in respect of" in Art. 105(2) was in question - No-confidence motion in Parliament - Members accepting bribe and voting - Held, entitled to protection under art. 105(2) - Bribe givers can be proceeded against.
