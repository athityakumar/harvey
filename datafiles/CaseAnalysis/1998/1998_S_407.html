Ronny Alias Ronald James Alwaris and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1683; (1998) 3 SCC 625; (1998) SCC (Cr) 859; AIR 1998 SC 1251; 1998 (2) CCR 72; 1998 (2) Crimes 65; 1998 CRLJ 1638; JT 1998 (2) SC 375; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 270; 1998(2) SCALE 261; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 162; 1998 (3) SLT 712; 1998 (3) Supreme 378

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 9 and 114, illustration (a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1973, ss. 100(4), 166(3) and 166(4)- Indian Penal Code, ss. 300, 302, 392 and 376 - Triple murder, robbery and rape - Stolen articles recovered from possession of accused - Unexplained by accused - Held, robbery and murder part of same transaction - Presumption u/s. 114, illustration (a) that accused and none else committed murders and robbery - Witness of search cannot be rejected merely because he was not from locality of search - Witnesses, friends of deceased can be relied upon even without corroboration of identification parade - 'Rarest of rare' case - Accused not too young or too old - Possibility of reform and rehabilitation, cannot be ruled out.
