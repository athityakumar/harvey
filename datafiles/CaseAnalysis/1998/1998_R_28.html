Tukaram Ramchandra Mane (Dead) By LRs v Rajaram Bapu Lakule (Dead) By LRs

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1644; (1998) 4 SCC 317; AIR 1998 SC 1822; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 628; JT 1998 (3) SC 171; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 90; 1998(2) SCALE 719; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 784; 1998 (4) SLT 385; 1998 (4) Supreme 127

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, Maharashtra Debt Relief Act, 1975

Summary: Banking & Finance - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, s. 37(1) - Effect of order of annulment of insolvency - Words 'all acts, therefore, done by court or receiver, shall be valid', occurring in s. 37(1) - Whether effect is wide enough to save order passed by insolvency court declaring sale deed as null and void and that it was result of collusion between debtor and creditor? - Whether by annulment of insolvency and wiping out its effect retrospectively, in law, order passed in suit becomes non est or ineffective? - Held, order within purview of 'all acts' - Effect of order of annulment, is not to wipe out altogether insolvency and its effect including adjudication made during pendency of insolvency proceedings - The suit and decree thereon is not wiped out, or rendered void or a nullity, automatically - Appeal dismissed.
