Brijlala Pd. Sinha v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 765; (1998) 5 SCC 699; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1382; AIR 1998 SC 2443; 1998 (3) Crimes 4; 1998 CRLJ 3611; 1998 (2) JCC 213; JT 1998 (4) SC 534; 1998 (3) RCR(Criminal) 495; 1998(4) SCALE 25; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 529; 1998 (5) Supreme 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Close Range

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.366 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34, 201 - Conviction - Death penalty - Safety of citizens - Acquitted - All accused were convicted u/s.302/34 IPC and were directed to be hanged till they were dead by accused - Appellant was convicted u/s.201 IPC but no separate sentence was awarded - HC on appeal preferred by accused persons for confirmation of death sentence u/s.366 of CrPC affirmed conviction of all accused u/ss.302/34 - HC affirmed death sentence awarded against appellant - Main ground for giving sentence of imprisonment for life as against three appellants that case would not come within test laid down by Court to bring it as a rarest of rare case - Whether award of death penalty conviction u/ss.302,34 of IPC was sustained - Held, it was a correct appreciation of law on subject dealing with award of death penalty conviction u/ss.302/34 of IPC was sustained - Rarest of rare cases as police officials on whose shoulders safety of citizens lie and without any provocation and resistance - Conviction of accused u/ss.302/34 of IPC was set aside and he was acquitted of charge - Conviction of appellant u/ss. 302/34 of IPC was upheld but award of death sentence against them was commuted to imprisonment for life - Appeal disposed of.
