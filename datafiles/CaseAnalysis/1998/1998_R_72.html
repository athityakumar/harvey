Chandrabhagabai and Others v Ramakrishna and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1092; (1998) 6 SCC 207; AIR 1998 SC 2549; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 703; JT 1998 (5) SC 197; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 133; 1998(4) SCALE 309; 1998 (6) SLT 452; 1998 (6) Supreme 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land of Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 64 - (A) Appeal - Finding of fact - Interference suit for possession - Plaintiffs alleged to have purchased suit houses in Court auction - Only evidence relied was auction sale certificate - Auction certificate did not clearly connect property covered by said certificate with suit rooms - Sch. attached to plaint describing suit property, not traceable - Whether finding of fact that plaintiff failed to establish title of suit rooms illegal? (B) Limitation Act, art. 64 - Adverse possession - Plaintiff purchased suit house in court auction - Rented portion of house to defendants - Facts showed that defendant remained in possession as owner of property since last more than 30 years prior to filing of suit - Statement of defendant admitting himself as tenant and plaintiff as owner in municipal records - Whether can be proved? - Whether defendant's title is established? - Held, (A) finding - Not illegal - No interference - (B) Cannot be proved since deceased not available for examination - Defendant's title established on plea of adverse possession - Appeal dismissed.
