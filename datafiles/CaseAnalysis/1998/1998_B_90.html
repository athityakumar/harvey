Smt. Satya Gupta Alias Madhu Gupta v Brijesh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 537; (1998) 6 SCC 423; 1998 (4) AWC 876; 1998 (3) CLT 137; JT 1998 (5) SC 427; 1998(4) SCALE 532; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 1183; 1998 (6) SLT 645; 1998 (6) Supreme 414

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - One Battu Mal, husband of the defendant-appellant purchased the suit property in the joint names of himself and the plaintiff, who was then a minor aged 16 years - Said Battu Mal was murdered in June, 1956 - Plaintiff was prosecuted for the murder of said Battu Mal and was convicted and sentenced to imprisonment and factually when the suit was filed he was in prison - Plaintiff filed a suit on the ground that Battu Mal had purchased half of the property for him - On reappreciation of evidence, the HC allowed the Second Appeal, set aside the judgment of the Lower Appellate Court and decreed the suit of the plaintiff - Whether the plaintiff was entitled to half share of the property? - Held, the Court noticed earlier the vagueness in the plaint about non-disclosure of how and on what basis the plaintiff claimed partition in the suit property - Lower Appellate Court also found that the plaintiff's evidence was extremely meagre and unsatisfactory - Taking all factors into consideration, the Lower Appellate Court found that Battu Mal did not intend to purchase the house for the plaintiff's benefit - This finding has been upset by the HC illegally by reappreciating the evidence, which is not permissible - Appeal allowed.
