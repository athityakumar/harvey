C. N. Rudramurthy v K. Barkathulla Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1129; (1998) 8 SCC 275; JT 1998 (7) SC 110; 1998 (2) RCJ 481; 1999 (1) RCR(Rent) 257; 1998(5) SCALE 485; 1999 (1) SCJ 135; 1998 (8) SLT 176; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 196; 1998 (8) Supreme 5

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, ss. 21, 21(1)f, 23, 31 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20 r. 10 - Tenancy - Suit for eviction - Decree - Jurisdiction - Appellant filed suit for eviction of respondent no. 1 - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, HC found that respondent no. 1 was liable to be evicted but held that Trial Court had no jurisdiction as 1961 Act in specific ousted jurisdiction of civil court and directed parties to work out their remedies under 1961 Act - Hence instant appeals by both the parties - Appellant contended that s. 31 of 1961 Act enable filing of suit as rent in respect of suit premises was above Rs. 500/- per month but HC held that s. 31 of 1961 was declared invalid in Padmanabha Rao's case - Whether Order of HC could be sustained - Held, SC's pronouncement in Shobha Surendar's case was that D.C. Bhatia's case should be applicable with reference to s. 31 of 1961 Act and, therefore, in view of that decision, HC decision was upset in another matter where HC had followed Padmanabha Rao's case - In effect, Padmanabha Rao's case stood impliedly overruled - Law declared by SC was clear that D.C Bhatia's case was applicable to provisions of 1961 Act - So it was not open to HC to take any other view in the matter - Indeed it was matter of judicial discipline that required that when SC stated as to what the law on the matter was, the same should be binding on all Courts within territory of India - This mandate of art. 141 of Constitution was not based on any doctrine of precedents, but would be imprimatur to all courts - D.C Bhatia and Others v. Union of India and Another, 1994 Indlaw SC 1121, relied on - Thus direction issued by HC to parties to work out their remedies under 1961 Act was not at all correct and accordingly appeal allowed - In view of the same, appeal by respondent no. 1 was dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
