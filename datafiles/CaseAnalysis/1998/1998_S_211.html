Ram Bihari Yadav v State Of Bihar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 696; (1998) 4 SCC 517; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1085; AIR 1998 SC 1850; 1998 (2) CCR 234; 1998 (2) Crimes 254; 1998 CRLJ 2515; 1998 (2) JCC 199; JT 1998 (3) SC 290; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 403; 1998(3) SCALE 200; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1097; 1998 (4) SLT 631; 1998 (4) Supreme 178

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 32 - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - (A) Evidence Act, s.3 - Probative value of evidence - Is the weight to be given to it - Expression relevancy and admissibility - Legal implications of - How distinct stated - (B) Dying declaration - Relevancy and admissibility of stated - Whether a dying declaration is relevant u/s. 32 of 1872 Act - Dying declaration and its admissibility in evidence - Whether dying declaration is substantive evidence - How much weight can be attached to a dying declaration - (C) Validity of dying declaration - Recording of dying declaration by police official - Whether admissible - Whether entry in case diary as to said original dying declaration could be taken as evidence - (D) Recording of dying declaration - Whether it must be recorded in question answer form - Consists of few sentences in actual words of maker - Whether mere fact that it is not in question-answer form can be a ground against its acceptability or reliability - Non-availability of a doctor - (E) Reliability of dying declaration - Dying declaration recorded by Magistrate - Signature of trainee nurse was got on statement due to non-availability of doctor - (F) Murder - Proof - Wife set afire by accused husband by pouring kerosene - Dying declaration found reliable - Same also corroborated by circumstantial evidence - Conduct of accused in delaying the opening of lock of house - Further plea that she died of accident while igniting oven and he poured water to save her - Belied, as there was no sign of water in kitchen - Further investigating officers deliberately omitting to do certain acts to bail out accused - Whether conviction liable to be upheld - Held, (A) More often than not facts which are relevant may not be admissible - So also facts which are admissible may not be relevant - (B) Dying declaration is indirect evidence being a specie of hearsay, yet it is an exception to the rule against admissibility of hearsay evidence -substantive evidence - No corroboration required - It is a question of fact and has to be determined on the facts of each case - (C) Not admissible - Entry in case diary could not be taken as evidence - (D) Must be generally in question answer form - The mere fact that it is not would not be a ground against its reliability - If there is such evidence there is no reason as to why the dying declaration should not be accepted - (E) Concurrent findings as to admissibility of such dying declaration upheld - (F) Offence against accused proved - His conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
