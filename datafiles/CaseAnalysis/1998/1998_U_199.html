Sarla Ahuja v United India Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1519; (1998) 8 SCC 119; AIR 1999 SC 100; AIR 1998 SCW 3451; JT 1998 (7) SC 297; 1999 (1) RCJ 158; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 533; 1998(5) SCALE 674; 1999 (1) SCJ 61; 1998 (8) SLT 374; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 390; 1998 (8) Supreme 133

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 25 B(8) and 14(1)(e) - Revision of eviction petition was in question - Finding of fact as to bona fide need by Rent Controller was revised after reappraisal and reappreciation of evidence by High Court so as to reach different finding - Argued that landlady was in possession of another flat in another city - Held, reappreciation of evidence by HC not permissible - Further, having another flat in some other city, not a ground to disentitle her to seek recovery of possession of tenanted premises.
