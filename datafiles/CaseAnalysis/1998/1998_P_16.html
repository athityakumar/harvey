Canara Bank v P.R.N.Upadhyaya and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M. Srinivasan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 840; (1998) 6 SCC 526; AIR 1998 SC 3000; 1998 (4) AWC 758; 2001 BC 28; 1998 (3) CLT 147; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 569; JT 1998 (5) SC 655; 1998(4) SCALE 651; 1998 (7) SLT 100; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 93; 1998 (6) Supreme 539

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss.35 and 21; Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o.1, r.10 - Loans granted by Banks to landlord - For construction/renovation of premises taken on lease or rent by the Bank - Whether under 'Term loans' - To charging interest as per circulars of R.B.I. - Held, irrespective of purpose for which is granted - Therefore, can be termed as 'loans' - R.B.I. issues circulars - Necessary party.
