Hindustan Times Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1718; (1998) 2 SCC 242; (1998) SCC (L&S) 481; AIR 1998 SC 688; JT 1998 (1) SC 18; 1998 (1) LLJ 682; 1998 (2) LLN 37; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 4; 1998 (1) SLR 495; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 345

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Coal Mines Provident Fund and Bonus Schemes Act, 1948 - Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, s. 14-B - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Recovery of damages - Payments of period - Delay - Show cause notice - Period of limitation - Appellant/employer was consequent to delay in remittal of PF contributions, damages and administrative charges should not be recovered for period from July 1965 to September, 1972 - Payments of period from 1965 to 1968 was arbitrary and unreasonable - On account of delay in payment of provident fund contributions by Department in remitting provident fund amounts for period that amounts were credited in accounts of department only after 20th of 'following' months - Petition filed order of Regional Provident Fund Commissioner passed u/s.14-B of 1952 Act levying damages in a sum and amount for delay in remitting employees provident fund contribution within period stipulated of Employees Provident Scheme - Petition dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether show cause notice issued after lapse of time could be issued beyond reasonable time - Held, no doubt there was delay of 14 years in initiating action and damages were levied because of delay in realization of amounts paid by cheque where amounts were credited into accounts of department beyond grace period of 5 days - No period of limitation prescribed by legislature for initiating action for recovery of damages u/s.14B of 1952 Act - Plea of strike even assuming it to be relevant was not proved - Plea of appellant that department deemed to have dropped proceedings in 1971 - No plea of any irretrievable prejudice - Defence of irretrievable prejudice on account of delay, was to be pleaded and proved in reply to show cause notice - Appeal dismissed.
