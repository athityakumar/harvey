Rafat Ali v Sugni Bai and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1767; (1999) 1 SCC 133; AIR 1999 SC 283; AIR 1998 SCW 3802; JT 1998 (8) SC 157; 1999 (1) RCJ 1; 1998(6) SCALE 221; 1998 (9) SLT 402; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 20; 1998 (8) Supreme 555

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 20 and 22 - Building leased to appellant - Respondents/landlords filed petition for eviction - Respondents failed in the Rent Control Court - High Court granted order of eviction - Appeal - When a construction is alleged to materially impair the value or utility of a building, the construction should be of such a nature as to substantially diminish the value of the building either from the commercial and monetary point of view or from the utilitarian aspect of the building - Burden of proof of such material impairment is on the landlord - Held, Rent Control Court considered the evidence on record regarding that dispute in detail and appellate authority felt that they were concocted for the purpose of evicting the tenants - Appellate authority pointed out that there was no complaint prior to the filing of the eviction petition at any time against the tenant that he caused damage to the building - Both the fact finding Courts found that the above items of damage are only trivial and will not affect the building - Learned Single Judge committed a jurisdictional error in upsetting the concurrent finding in such a manner as it has been done - Impugned judgment of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
