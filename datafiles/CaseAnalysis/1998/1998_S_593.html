Sushma Suri v Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1130; (1999) SCC (L&S) 208; (1999) 1 SCC 330; 1998 (2) JCC 249; JT 1998 (7) SC 121; 1999 (1) LLN 1035; 1998(5) SCALE 490; 1999 (1) SCJ 75; 1999 (3) SLJ 34; 1998 (5) SLR 528; 1998 (8) SLT 188; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 187; 1998 (8) Supreme 10

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Advocates Act, 1961 - Delhi Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1970, rr. 5, 7 and 49 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 233(2) - Whether view of High Court that Public Prosecutor/Govt. Counsel/Standing Counsel of any corporation or authority would cease to be an advocate for purpose of art. 233(2) of Constitution so as not to belong to Bar, is correct? - Held, advocate employed by Govt. or a body corporate as its Law Officer even on terms of payment of salary would not cease to be an advocate in terms of r. 49 if condition is that such advocate is required to act or plead in courts on behalf of employer - For purposes of Advocates Act and Rules framed thereunder Law Officer (Public Prosecutor or Govt. Counsel) will continue to be an advocate - Intention of relevant Rules is that a candidate eligible for appointment to Higher Judicial Service should be a person who regularly practices before court or Tribunal appearing for a client - Whenever any recruitment is conducted to fill up any post, area of recruitment must be as broad-based as Rules permit - Expression 'members of Bar' in relevant Rule would only mean that particular class of persons who are actually practicing in courts of law as pleaders or advocates - Bar Council of India has understood expression 'advocate' as one who is actually practicing before courts which expression would include even those who are law officers appointed as such by Govt. or body corporate - Expression used 'from Bar' would only mean from class or group of advocates practicing in courts of law - It does not have any other attribute - View taken by HC cannot be upheld - Appeals disposed of.
