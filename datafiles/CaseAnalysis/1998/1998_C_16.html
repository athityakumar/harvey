N. M. Veerappa v Canara Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2330; (1998) 2 SCC 317; AIR 1998 SC 1101; 1998 (1) CLT 88; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 467; 1998 (1) CTC 219; JT 1998 (1) SC 221; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 20; 1998 (2) MPLJ 84; 1998(1) SCALE 217; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 342; 1998 (2) SLT 361; 1998 (1) Supreme 363

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Pendente Lite Interest, Usurious Loans Act, 1918

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, s. 21A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 34, O. 34, r. 11 - (A) Applicability of s. 34, O. 34, r. 11 of CPC - Mortgage suits - Grant of interest - Whether special provision in O. 34, r. 11 is applicable or plaintiff entitled to future interest also at contractual rate of interest from date of suit till date of realisation with costs because of s. 34 CPC? - (B) S. 21A of 1949 Act, - Scope - Whether provisions u/s. 21A to override CPC or O. 34, r. 11 of CPC - (C) s. 21A of 1949 Act - CPC, O. 34, r. 11 - Rate of interest - Grant of, for period during pendency of mortgage suits in Court - Discretion of Court - Whether to be continued or to be fettered and whether there should be distinction between different debtor? - (D) CPC, O. 34, r. 11 - Interest - Mortgage suits for recovery of bank loan - Grant of interest at rate of 6% from date of suit - Whether proper? - Held, (A) Special provision in O. 34, r. 11 alone is applicable and not s. 34, CPC - (B) Provisions u/s. 21 A not intended to override Central Legislation like CPC or O. 34, r. 11 of CPC - (C) Are all matters of policy for legislature? - Parliament to bring forward legislation in that regard - (D) Discretion to Court so far as pendente lite interest and subsequent interest is concerned - Such rate as Court deems reasonable - Rate of 6% fixed by Trial Court is restored - Appeal allowed.
