State of Punjab v Dr. R. N. Bhatnagar and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2088; (1999) 2 SCC 330; (1999) SCC (L&S) 513; AIR 1999 SC 647; 1999 (1) ESC 348; JT 1998 (9) SC 7; 1998 LabIC 928; 1998(6) SCALE 642; 1999 (3) SLJ 57; 1999 (2) SLR 552; 1999 (1) SLT 73; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 693; 1998 (9) Supreme 481; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 57

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16(1) and 16(4) - Punjab Medical College Education Service (Class I) Rules, 1978, rr. 3, 4, 6, 9, 9(1), 9(i)(d) and 10 - Recruitment - Determination - Whether vacancy in post of professor of concerned department was to be filled in by direct recruit or by promotee? - Held, Appellant contended that as per relevant quota rule and roster cycles which follows 3 recruitment of promotees and thereafter 1 direct recruitment, concerned 16th vacancy would be available to a direct recruit and hence advertised to be filled up by direct recruitment - Respondent claim that when concerned vacancy arose there was already on direct recruit holding post of Professor, vacancy in question had to go to departmental promotees, was not upheld - Quota rule prescribed for recruitment to a cadre should be correlated to vacancies which are to be filled in - Respondent also contended that in working of cycle concerned 16th vacancy if reckoned from a roster commencing from date when statutory rules came into force would go to a promotee and not to a direct recruit - Irrespective of identity of person retiring, post is to be filled by onward application of prescribed quota rule - Any vacancies arising after statutory rules coming into force would have to be subjected to a new cycle of rotation as per r. 9(i)(d) - Respondent contention was sustained - Appeal dismissed.
