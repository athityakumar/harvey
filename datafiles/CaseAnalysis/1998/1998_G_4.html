Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v G. Narasimhan (Dead) By Heirs Kantha, Narasimhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2073; (1999) 1 SCC 510; AIR 1999 SC 408; 1999 (151) CTR 94; [1999] 236 ITR 327; JT 1998 (9) SC 182; 1998(6) SCALE 546; 1998 (10) SLT 215; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 532; 1998 (9) Supreme 324; [1999] 102 TAXMAN 66; 1999 (2) TLR 147

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income-Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(22)(e) and 45 - Dividend income - Advances by Company to shareholders - Held, such payment treated as deemed dividend - May be treated as coming out of accumulated profits - It would therefore go to reduce accumulated profits of Company - Whether capitalised or not? - Part relating to accumulated profits would be taxable as dividend - Balance amount alone can be treated as capital receipt - Further after considering cost of acquisition of that portion of share diminished, capital gains would be determined.
