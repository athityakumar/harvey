V. Kasturi v Managing Director, State Bank of India, Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 348; (1998) 8 SCC 30; (1999) SCC (L&S) 78; AIR 1999 SC 81; AIR 1998 SCW 3431; 1998 (2) CLR 1041; JT 1998 (7) SC 147; 1999 (1) LLJ 238; 1998 (3) SLJ 17; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - State Bank of India Employees Pension Fund Rules, r. 22(1)(c) - Benefit of pension scheme under the amended Pension Fund Rules - Entitlement - Whether petitioner is entitled to get benefit of pension scheme available to State Bank employees under State Bank of India Employees Pension Fund Rules? - Held, at time of the resignation of petitioner which was treated as voluntary retirement, he was not entitled to get pension under the Rules as eligibility requirement for earning pension as per r. 22(1)(c) of the Rules was to effect that employee should have retired from Bank service after 25 years of pensionable service - If an employee at time of his retirement is not eligible for earning pension and stands outside class of pensioners, if subsequently by amendment of relevant Pension Rules any beneficial umbrella of pension scheme is extended to cover a new class of pensioners and when such a subsequent scheme comes into force erstwhile non-pensioner might have survived, then only if such extension of pension scheme to erstwhile non-pensioners is expressly made retrospective by authorities promulgating such scheme - Erstwhile non-pensioner who has retired prior to advent of such extended pension scheme can claim benefit of such a new extended pension scheme - If scheme is prospective only, old retirees non-pensioners cannot get benefit of such a scheme even if they survive such new scheme - Appeal dismissed.
