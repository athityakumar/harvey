Dinkar Anna Patil and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1190; (1999) SCC (L&S) 216; (1999) 1 SCC 354; AIR 1999 SC 152; 1999 (1) CLR 176; JT 1998 (7) SC 513; 1998 LabIC 3603; 1998 (4) LLN 974; 1998(6) SCALE 54; 1999 (2) SLJ 17; 1998 (9) SLT 114; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 564; 1998 (8) Supreme 392

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Sales Tax Officers Class I (Recruitment) Rules, 1982, Civil Services (Regulation Of Seniority) Rules, 1982

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 309 - Maharashtra Sales Tax Officers Class 1 (Recruitment) Rules, 1982, r. 4-A - Maharashtra Civil Services (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1982, r. 4 - Seniority - Post of Sales Tax Officer Class 1 comprises of promotees and direct recruits - R. 4-A providing for promotion in exigencies of service by relaxing quota - Consultation with Service Commission by Govt. to dispense with - Held, not permissible - 'May' under r. 4-A has to be read as 'shall' - Fortuitous service of promotee - Cannot be reckoned for purposes of determining their seniority over direct recruits.
