Selvaraj v Lt. Governor of Island, Port Blair and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1534; (1998) 4 SCC 291; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1127; AIR 1999 SC 838; 1998 (2) CLR 639; 1998 (80) FLR 24; JT 1998 (4) SC 500; 1999 LabIC 598; 1998 (2) LLJ 1191; 1998 (3) LLN 567; 1998 (3) SLR 770; 1998 (9) SLT 60; 1998 (8) Supreme 532

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Salary - Whether employee ordered to look upon duties of a higher post is entitled to salary attached to higher post? - Employee temporarily worked on higher post in an officiating capacity - Is entitled to salary attached to higher post - Payment of higher salary - Does not amount to promotion - Held, employee ordered to look upon duties of higher post, though temporarily and in an officiating capacity is entitled to salary attached to higher post, however, payment of higher salary shall not amount to promotion.
