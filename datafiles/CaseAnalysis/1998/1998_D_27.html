State of Gujarat and Others v Dilipbhai Nathjibhai Patel and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1526; (1998) 3 SCC 234; (1998) SCC (Cr) 737; AIR 1998 SC 1429; 1998 (1) CCR 380; 1998 (1) Crimes 213; 1998 (2) JCC 42; JT 1998 (2) SC 253; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 107; 1998(2) SCALE 145; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 56; 1998 (3) SLT 400; 1998 (2) Supreme 313

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Criminal Gujarat Co - operative Societies' Act, 1961, s. 71 - Co-operative Societies Act, 1962, ss. 147(1)(c), 147(1)(d) and 149( 3) - (A) Whether obtaining sanction of registrar for proscecution of an offence requires prior hearing or not? - Held, sanction by registrar does not require prior hearing - When words 'such sanction shall not be given' appearing at beginning of second part is read in juxtaposition with words 'by an officer authorised in this behalf by State Govt. by a general or special order' at end, it is also manifest that hearing is to be given only if a sanction for prosecution under s. 147(1)(c) is contemplated and not otherwise - S. 149(3) cannot be interpreted to mean that in respect of offences for which Registrar is sanctioning authority a prior hearing is required to be given - It is not duty of court either to enlarge scope of legislation or intention of legislature when language of provision is plain and unambiguous - Appeal allowed.
