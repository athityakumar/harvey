Raptakos Brett and Company Limited v Ganesh Property

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1187; (1998) 7 SCC 184; AIR 1998 SC 3085; 1999 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; 1998 (4) CLT 11; JT 1998 (6) SC 289; 1998 (2) RCR(Rent) 353; 1998(5) SCALE 195; 1998 (7) SLT 472; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 485; 1998 (7) Supreme 276

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Fixtures, Tenancy At Sufferance, Railway Act 1890, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69(2) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Unregistered firm - Bar to suit arising out of contract - Term lease - Stipulating obligation to handover possession in part of lessee after expiry of lease period - Continuing in possession after expiry of lease period - Held, his status is of tenant at sufferance - Cannot be said to be not arising out of contract of lease on ground that covenant would not remain effective and pending between parties after lease gets determined by efflux of time - S. 69(2) is penal provision - Has to be strictly construed.
