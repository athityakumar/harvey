Raghubar Singh and Others v Gulab Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1033; (1998) 6 SCC 314; AIR 1998 SC 2401; AIR 1998 SCW 2393; 1998 (34) ALR 45; 1999 (1) Bom.C.R. 119; 1998 (3) CLT 47; JT 1998 (4) SC 579; 1998 (2) KLT 21; 1998(4) SCALE 62; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 555; 1998 (6) SLT 175; 1998 (5) Supreme 399

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Rights Of Inheritance, Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce, Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1) and(2), Hindu Married Women's Right to Separate Maintenance and Residence Act, 1946 - Enlargement of widow's estate - Right to maintenance of Hindu female - Is a pre-existing right that existed even under Shastric Hindu Law - Will made by husband of widow bequeathing property to grand child - Stipulation in Will that till testator or his wife (widow) is alive both shall have full control over property - Held, declared that widow was owner of property - Compromise decree passed in suit challenging Will also recognising such right of widow.
