Northern Plastic Limited v Collector of Customs and Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1034; (1998) 6 SCC 443; AIR 1998 SC 2371; 1998 (77) ECR 666; 1998 (101) E.L.T. 549; JT 1998 (4) SC 565; 1998 (27) RLT 556; 1998(4) SCALE 105; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 611; 1998 (6) SLT 197; 1998 (5) Supreme 447

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Importation, Exemption Notifications, Misdeclaration

Summary: Customs Act 1962, s. 111 - Customs Tariff Act 1975, heading 37.02, heading 3702. 41 - Confiscation of goods on ground of misdeclaration - Goods which were imported were described in bill of entry as cinematographic colour films (unexposed) positive - Argued it was found that they did not undergo process of slitting, cutting and perforation - Held, no misdeclaration as cinematographic films and photographic films in rolls fell in same tariff heading.
