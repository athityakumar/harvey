 Presidential Reference(In Re) v Na

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal, G.B. Pattanaik, G.T. Nanavati, K. Venkataswami, M.K. Mukherjee, S.B. Majmudar, S. Saghir Ahmad, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2089; (1998) 7 SCC 739; AIR 1999 SC 1; 1998 (5) ALT 234; 1998 (4) CLT 241; JT 1998 (7) SC 304; 1998 (4) LLN 596; 1998(5) SCALE 629; 2000 (1) SLJ 1; 1998 (5) SLR 601; 1998 (8) SLT 409; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 400; 1998 (8) Supreme 140

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 217(1) - Appointment of SC judges - Consultation between Chief Justice of India (CJI) and his brother Judges - Requirement of - Held, opinion of the CJI which has primacy in the matter of recommendations for appointment to the SC has to be formed in consultation with a collegium of Judges - Collegium consists of the two senior most puisne Judges of the SC - In making a decision as to whom that collegium should recommend, it takes into account the views that are elicited by the CJI from the senior most Judge of the SC who comes from the same HC as the person proposed to be recommended and the views of other Judges of the SC or the CJs or Judges of the HC or, indeed, members of the Bar who may also have been asked by the CJI or on his behalf - Principal objective of the collegium is to ensure that the best available talent is brought to the SC Bench - CJI and the senior most puisne Judges, by reason of their long tenures on the SC, are best fitted to achieve the objective of proper appointment and they can assess the comparative worth of possible appointees by reason of the fact that their judgments would have been the subject matter of petitions for special leave to appeal and appeals - Even where the person under consideration is a member of the Bar, he would have frequently appeared before CJI and other Judges of the SC - Further, collegium would have the benefit of the inputs provided by those whose views have been sought - Therefore, expression 'consultation with the CJI' in arts. 217(1) and 222(1) of the Constitution requires consultation with a plurality of Judges in the formation of the opinion of the CJI and the sole, individual opinion of the CJI does not constitute 'consultation' within the meaning of the said articles - Order accordingly.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 124(2) - Collegium for appointment of SC Judge - Size of - Held, collegium should consist of the CJI and the four seniormost puisne Judges of the SC - Ordinarily, one of the four seniormost puisne Judges of the SC would succeed the CJI, but if the situation should be such that the successor CJ is not one of the four seniormost puisne Judges, he must invariably be made part of the collegiums - Judges to be appointed will function during his term and he should have a hand in their selection - Order accordingly.

(C) Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Appointment of SC Judge - Merit - Consideration of - Held, outstanding meritorious candidate deserves to be appointed regardless of the fact that he may not stand high in the all India seniority list or in his own HC and all that then needs to be recorded when recommending him for appointment is that he has outstanding merit - When the contenders for appointment to the SC do not possess such outstanding merit but have, nevertheless, the required merit in more or less equal degree, there may be reason to recommend one among them because the particular region of the country in which his parent HC is situated is not represented on the SC Bench - It is not unusual that a Judge who has once been passed over for appointment to the SC might still find favor on the occasion of another selection and there is no reason to blot his copybook by recording what might be construed to be an adverse comment about him - It is only when, for very strong reasons, a collegium finds that, whatever his seniority, some HC Judge should never be appointed to the SC that it should so record - Order accordingly.

(D) Advocates & Judges - Appointment of SC Judge - Non-appointment of recommended candidate on the ground of unsuitability - Held, if one or more members of the collegium that made a particular recommendation have retired or are otherwise unavailable when reasons are disclosed to the CJI for the non-appointment of a person, the reasons must be placed before the remaining members of the original collegium plus another Judge or Judges who have reached the required seniority and become one of the first four puisne Judges - Said re-constituted collegium would consider whether the recommendation should be withdrawn or reiterated - Having regard to the objective of securing the best available men for the SC, it is imperative that the number of Judges of the SC who consider the reasons for non-appointment should be as large as the number that had made the particular recommendation - However, CJI may, in his discretion, bring to the knowledge of the person recommended the reasons disclosed by the Government of India for his non-appointment and ask for his response thereto and such response should he considered by the collegium before it withdraws or reiterates the recommendation - Order accordingly.

(E) Advocates & Judges - Appointment of SC and HC Judges - Judicial review - Scope of - Held, judicial review in the case of an appointment, or a recommended appointment, to the SC or a HC is, therefore, available if the recommendation concerned is not a decision of the CJI and his senior most colleagues, which is constitutionally required - Judicial review is also available if, in making the decision, the views of the senior most SC Judge who comes from the HC of the proposed appointee to the SC have not been taken into account - If, in connection with an appointment, or a recommended appointment, to a HC, the views of the CJ and senior Judges of the HC, and of SC Judges knowledgeable about that HC have not been sought or considered by the CJI and his two senior most puisne Judges, judicial review is available - Transfer of puisne Judges is judicially reviewable only to this extent : that the recommendation that has been made by the CJI in this behalf has not been made in consultation with the four seniormost puisne Judges of the SC and/or that the views of the CJ of the HC from which the transfer is to be effected and of the CJ of the HC to which the transfer is to be effected have not been obtained - Order accordingly.

(F) Advocates & Judges - Transfer of HC Judges - Mode of - Held, CJI should obtain the views of the CJ of the HC from which the proposed transfer is to be effected as also the CJ of the HC to which the transfer is to be effected - CJI should also take into account the views of one or more SC Judges who are in a position to provide material which would assist in the process of deciding whether or not a proposed transfer should take place - Said views should be expressed in writing and should be considered by the CJI and the four senior most puisne Judges of the SC - Further, the said views and views of those of each of the four senior most puisne Judges should be conveyed to the Government of India along with the proposal of transfer - Unless the decision to transfer has been taken in the manner as such, it is not decisive and does not bind the Government of India - Order accordingly.
