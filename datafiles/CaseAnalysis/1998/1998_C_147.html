Food Corporation of India v Commissioner of Sales Tax, M.P., Indore

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1368; (1998) 2 SCC 363; AIR 1999 SC 545; 1998 (109) STC 131

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, ss. 38(5) and 43 - Penalty - Enhancement of - Power of authority - (A) Whether power to confirm, reduce, enhance or annul penalty in appeal as provided u/s. 38(5) of State Act includes power to impose penalty? - Held, no - Power u/s. 38(5) is to confirm, reduce, enhance or annul a penalty - It can apply only if a penalty has already been imposed by assessing authority - (B) Whether imposition of proceedings u/s. 43 of act can be regarded as fresh proceedings? - Held, no - Power u/s. 43 can be invoked only in proceedings in appeal from an assessment order or otherwise in proceedings under said act - Imposition of penalty is a part of such proceedings and cannot be regarded as a fresh assessment - Appeal dismissed.
