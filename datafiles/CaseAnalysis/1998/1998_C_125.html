Bharat Commerce and Industries Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central-Ii

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1687; (1998) 3 SCC 510; AIR 1998 SC 1795; 1998 (145) CTR 340; [1998] 230 ITR 733; JT 1998 (2) SC 283; 1998(2) SCALE 240; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 150; 1998 (3) SLT 546; 1998 (3) Supreme 67; [1998] 98 TAXMAN 151

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Voluntary Disclosure of Income and Wealth Act, 1976 - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 37 and 80V - Business expenditure - Interest paid for delayed payment of advance tax - Not allowable as business expenditure - Held, if income-tax itself is not a permissible deduction u/s. 37, any interest payable for default committed by assessee in discharging his statutory obligation under Income-tax Act, which is calculated with reference to tax on income cannot be allowed as a deduction - Appeal dismissed.
