State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Dharam Bir

Supreme Court of India

08 June 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1909; (1998) 6 SCC 165; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1459; 1998 (2) CLR 389; 1998 (79) FLR 833; JT 1998 (4) SC 363; 1998 (2) LLJ 666; 1998 (4) LLN 29; 1998(4) SCALE 4; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 511; 1998 (6) SLT 29; 1998 (5) Supreme 155

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Training (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules 1995, rr. 13 and 14 - Reversion - Validity - Whether a promotee is entitled to hold a post of direct recruitment without specified qualification for post but on basis of long experience? - No - Qualifications applicable not only to direct recruitment but also to promotions - Post of Principal whether it is of Class II or Class I is a post of higher responsibilities - Administrative qualities are mixed and blended with academic achievements of candidates and, therefore, it has been specifically provided in Rules, specially Schedule appended thereto, that candidates - Whether they are to be appointed by direct recruitment or by promotion, must possess a Degree or Diploma in Engineering? - Relied on Roshan Lal Tandon Vs UOI case and Union of India Vs Tulsiram Patel - It is not open to any Govt. employee to claim automatic alteration of status unless that result is specifically envisaged by some provision in statutory rules - Held, 'experience' gained by respondent on account of his working on post in question for over a decade cannot be equated with Educational Qualifications required to be possessed by a candidate as a condition of eligibility for promotion to higher posts - Reversion by appellant is valid - Appeal allowed.
