Kamlesh Kumar Sharma v Yogesh Kumar Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1740; (1998) 3 SCC 45; (1998) SCC (L&S) 720; AIR 1998 SC 1021; 1998 ALJ 683; 1998 (32) ALR 588; JT 1998 (1) SC 642; 1998 (2) LLJ 1074; 1998(1) SCALE 513; 1998 (1) SLR 720; 1998 (2) SLT 675; 1998 (2) Supreme 389; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 743

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointment

Summary: Education - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 13(4) and 12(2) - (A) Appointment from existing list can only be made for unforeseen circumstances - Retirement is not an unforeseen circumstance - Vacancy u/s. 13(4) refers to 'occurring due to death, resignation or otherwise' - Whether it will include foreseen vacancies occurring u/s. 12(2) for succeeding academic years? - (B) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Locus standi - Writ petition challenging order of Director under which appellant claims appointment as Principal - Plea raised that respondent has no locus standi to challenge order because statute contemplates that appointment of Principal should be of senior most teacher - Senior most teacher declined said offer - Respondent, next senior most came to be appointed - Whether respondent has locus standi to challenge said order - Held, (A) Word 'otherwise' occurring u/s. 13(4) has to be read as ejusdem generis - Covering unforeseen vacancies similar to death, resignation - It will not include foreseen - vacancies - (B) Respondent officiating Principal appointed under statute has locus standi - Appeal dismissed.
