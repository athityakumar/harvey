Damodarprasad Chandrikaprasad and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 492; (1972) 1 SCC 107; (1972) SCC (Cr) 110; AIR 1972 SC 622; 1972 CRLJ 451; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 622; 1972 UJ 322

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.143, 147,149, 307 r/w s.149 -Assault - First Information Report - Evidence -Conviction - Appellant No.1 shouted to assault - Appellant Nos. 1 and 4 hit 'C' - Appellant Nos. 2 and 3 hit 'N' -Appellants and another accused were charged u/ss.143, 147, 307, r r/w s.149, 324 r/w.34 of IPC -Four appellants were charged u/s.148 of IPC -Trial Court acquitted all five accused - HC set aside order of acquittal of appellants and convicting them u/s.325 r/w s.34 of IPC for having assaulted and injured 'C' -HC interfered with acquittal without giving any reasons -- FIR about cognizance of offence was wrongly admitted in evidence -Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in convicting appellants on evidence -Held, HC was justified that acquittal of appellant Nos. 2 and 3 by trial Court was to be set aside - Appellant Nos. 1 and 4 were concerned HC was correct in holding that they were wrongly acquitted by trial Court - Oral evidence was rightly accepted by HC that all appellants were guilty of assaulting 'C','N' and 'R' -HC was correct in setting aside order of acquittal and convicting appellants -Appeal dismissed.
