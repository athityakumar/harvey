Col. His Highness Raja Sir Harinder Singh Brar v Commssioner of Income Tax, Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 312; (1972) 4 SCC 509; AIR 1972 SC 202; [1972] 83 ITR 416; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 16

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income-tax Act 1922, ss. 23, 34 and 66(1) - Whether as an erstwhile ruling prince, assessee can at all be entitled to immunity from taxation? - Historical origin of immunities - Immunity from legal-proceedings which is recognised in common law has been subject matter of legislation under which ruling princes of India, notwithstanding that they were not recognised as international personalities, were, however, accorded this immunity - Any exemptions which they may be given, must, be under relevant taxing Acts - Held, in so far as Income Tax Act is concerned exemption of income of rulers derived from Central Govt., securities was specifically given u/s. 60 of Act which implies that Rulers were not exempt from other provisions of law, including those relating to their private income - Appeal dismissed.
