State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Lavu Narendranath and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 261; (1971) 1 SCC 607; AIR 1971 SC 2560; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Andhra University Act 1926

Summary: Education - Andhra University Act, 1926 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 162 and 21 - Admission to Medical colleges - Selection through Entrance Test - Contention of contravention of provisions of Act - Held, selection of candidates to Medical College among successful candidates at public examination neither contravenes provisions of Andhra University Act, 1926 - In this case everybody subject to minimum qualification prescribed was at liberty to apply for admission - Govt. objective in selecting a number of them was certainly not improper in circumstances of case - Even if personal liberty extends to such conduct there has not been any deprivation thereof in violation of any procedure established by law - Appeal allowed.
