C.I.T., U.P. v Bankey Lal Vaidya (Dead) By L.R.S.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 114; (1971) 1 SCC 355; AIR 1971 SC 2270; [1971] 79 ITR 594; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s.12B(1) - Dissolution of partnership - Assets of the firm are valued and a partner is paid a certain amount in lieu of his share - Taxability of amount received by the partner - If capital gains - Held, the transaction is not sale, exchange or transfer of assets of the firm and the amount received by the partner cannot be taxed as capital gains - Appeal Dismissed.
