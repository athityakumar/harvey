Messrs Madhusudan Gordhandas and Company v Madhu Woollen Industries Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 572; (1971) 3 SCC 632; AIR 1971 SC 2600; [1972] 42 Comp Cas 125; 1972 (1) CTR 193; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Substratum, Indebtedness

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 433(e) and (f) - Co. - Non-payment of disputed debts - Petition for winding up u/s. 433 (e) and (f) - Whether court can refuse a petition for winding up when - (A) disputed debt is bona fide - (B) Creditors oppose to winding up - (C) Disappearance of substratum of Co. - (D) Petition out of improper motive - Held, court can refuse - Court can consider their wishes and decline to make order - An allegation that substratum of company is gone is to be proved as a fact - Improper motive can be spelt out when petition is presented to coerce Co. in satisfying some groundless claims - Appeal dismissed.
