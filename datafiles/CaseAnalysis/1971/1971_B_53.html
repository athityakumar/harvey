Bishwanath Rai v Sachhidanand Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 629; (1972) 4 SCC 707; AIR 1971 SC 1949; 1971 UJ 726

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s.123(3) - Assembly election - Challenged by loosing candidate - Allegation of using caste factor to get votes and restraining complainant and his supporters from canvassing for votes - One swamiji appealing to vote as candidate is of same caste - Written letters to many persons of same caste - Whether election can set aside if wining candidate is indulged in using caste factor to gather votes? - Held, finding of HC that Swami made appeals to various persons in several villages to vote for appellant on ground of being members of Bhumidhar Brahmin caste in presence of appellant is correct so that appellant is guilty of commission of corrupt practice falling under provisions of s. 123 (3) of Act and, on this ground election of appellant has been rightly set aside - Appeal dismissed.
