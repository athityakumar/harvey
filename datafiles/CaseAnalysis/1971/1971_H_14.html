Hardwari Lal v Kanwal Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 279; (1972) 1 SCC 214; AIR 1972 SC 515; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 91; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election

Keywords: Procure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 86, 87 & 123(7) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11 - (A) Corrupt practice - Seeking assistance of Govt. servants - For furthering election prospects of a candidate - Election petition must contain material facts and full necessary particulars - Absence of - Maintainability of petition - (B) Dismissal of election petition - Legality - Held, petition is not maintainable - Election petition which does not set out material facts and particulars of corrupt practice so as to furnish a cause of action can be dismissed by virtue of s. 87, though not u/s. 86 - Appeal allowed.
