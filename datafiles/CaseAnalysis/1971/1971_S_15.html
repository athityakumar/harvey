P. C. Purushothama Reddiar v S. Perumal

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 553; (1972) 1 SCC 9; AIR 1972 SC 608; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 83; 1972 (1) SCJ 469; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 646; 1972 UJ 332

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(6) - Assembly election - Challenged by appellant - Allegation of canvassing votes - Incurred expenditure more than prescribed limit - Whether election can be set aside if wining candidate has incurred more expenditure than prescribed limit? - Elected member admitted attending 7 meetings - Other candidate alleged that member attended four more meetings - Corroboration by police records - Estimated expenditure of four meetings as per winning candidate expenditure submitted in return is more than prescribed limit - Held, election set aside - Appeal allowed.
