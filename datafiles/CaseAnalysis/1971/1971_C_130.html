Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii v Coal Shipments Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 443; (1971) 3 SCC 736; AIR 1972 SC 541; 1972 CTR 151; [1971] 82 ITR 902; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 1089

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2) (xv) - Coal shipping business - Mutual arrangement - Payments for assistance - Revenue expenditure - Respondent company exported coal from India to Burma before Second World War - Respondent company and M arrived at a mutual arrangement that M would not export coal during subsistence of agreement - M would assist respondent in procuring coal for shipment to Burma - Respondent carried on coal shipping business and paid M Rs.151 per ton of coal shipped to Burma - Last shipment of coal under arrangement made in 1954 after which arrangement came to end automatically and Govt. Burma made some other arrangement for its coal requirement - Assessee respondent claimed payments to M as admissible business expenditure - Income Tax Officer(IO) decided that expenditures claimed could not allowed - Order uphold by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal observed that expenditures were attributable to revenue hold to be permissible expenditures u/s.10(2) (xv) of Act - On application filed by Revenue, question was referred to HC that whether payments made by assessee to M were of a capital nature and as such not allowable u/s. 10(2) (xv) of Act - HC found that respondent entitled to claim deduction of expenditures u/s.10(2)(xv) of Act - Question was answered in negative and in favour of assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether payments made to M were constituted as a revenue expenditure or not - Held, payments made to M for assistance to respondent in procuring coal for shipment was admittedly an item of revenue expenditure - Arrangement between respondent and M was not for any fixed term but could be terminated at any time at volition of any of parties - Payments made to M were related to actual shipment of coal in course of trading activities of respondent and had no relation to capital value of assets - Payments were not related to or tied up in any way to any fixed sum agreed between parties - Monopoly value payments were imposed for term of licence on grant or renewal though fact that permission was given to pay by yearly instalments gave a false appearance of periodicity - Appeals dismissed.
