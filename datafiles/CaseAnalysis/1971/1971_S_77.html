Shyam Deo Pandey and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 811; (1971) SCC (Cr) 353; (1971) 1 SCC 855; AIR 1972 SC 1606; 1972 (1) ALT(SC) 22; 1971 CRLJ 1177; [1971] S.C.R. 133; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 367, 410, 418, 419, 423 and 424 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.363 - Kidnapping - High Court dismissed appeal of appellants summarily as no one was present in Court - Whether HC order in confirmity with s. 423 CrPC? - Held, improper for HC to dismiss appeal without giving its view on points raised regardless parties present or absent - In passing impugned order HC has not considered material on record before coming to conclusion that there was no case for interference - As order is not in conformity with s. 423; hence it has to be set aside - Case remanded back to HC for disposal according to law - Appeal allowed.
