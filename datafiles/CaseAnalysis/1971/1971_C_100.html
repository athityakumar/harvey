Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat v S. C. Kothari

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 354; (1972) 4 SCC 402; AIR 1972 SC 391; 1974 (3) CTR 137; [1971] 82 ITR 794; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952, s. 15(4) - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(1), 24 - (A) Contract with non-member - A contract entered into in contravention of the provisions of s. 15 (4) - Legality - (B) Income Tax - Illegal transaction - Computation of profit - Deduction of loss and set off u/s. 24(1) first proviso - Permissibility - Held, would fall directly within the ambit of the first part of s. 23 of the Contract Act and would be illegal - Deduction of loss can be made u/s.10(1) but no set off can be allowed u/s. 24(1) first proviso - Appeal dismissed.
