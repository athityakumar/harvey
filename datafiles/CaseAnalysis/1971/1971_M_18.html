S. B. Jain, I.T.O. Nagpur v Mahandera

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 211; (1972) 4 SCC 114; 1972 (1) CTR 292; [1972] 83 ITR 104; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 614; 1972 TAXLR 1040; 1972 UJ 134

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1) (a) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 148 - Issuance of notice - Pending proceedings - Commencement of new Act - Effect - Income-tax Officer issued notice to respondent u/s. 34(1) (a) of the 1922 Act seeking to reopen his assessment for the A/y 1946-47 - Respondent challenged the validity of that notice - HC quashed the impugned notice by its order dt. 6-3-1963 - On 1-4-1962 the 1961 Act came into force - Thereafter the Income-tax Officer again issued a notice u/s. 148 of the new 1961 Act in respect of the very assessment which he earlier unsuccessfully sought to reopen - Respondent again challenged the validity of the notice issued to him - HC quashed that notice on the ground that the Income-tax Officer was not competent to issue that notice - Hence instant appeals - Whether proceedings initiated by notice u/s. 34(1) (a) of 1922 Act were pending at the time when the new Act came into force - Held, it was not denied that such proceedings were factually pending - What s. 297 (2) (a) (ii) required was the factual pendency of a proceeding u/s. 34 of the repealed Act - Question whether that proceeding was barred by limitation or not was irrelevant - It was not denied that those proceedings were initiated by a competent authority - Those proceedings were quashed for the reason that notice u/s. 34 of 1922 Act was issued beyond the time prescribed by law - Hence it could not be said that no proceeding u/s. 34 of 1922 Act either factually or legally was pending at the time when the new Act came into force - Proceedings pending before a competent authority could not be said to be not pending merely because that no relief could be granted in that proceedings because of the bar of limitation - Proceedings initiated u/s. 34(1) (a) of the 1922 Act were pending at the time 1961 Act came into force and that being so the Income-tax Officer was not competent to issue any fresh notice u/s. 148 of the 1961 Act - Appeal dismissed.
