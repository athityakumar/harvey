Pullangode Rubber and Produce Company Limited v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 429; (1972) 4 SCC 683; 1972 CTR 235; [1973] 91 ITR 18; 1972 UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Questions referred - Rejection - Challenged - Assessee filed an application before Tribunal with request to refer questions to HC but said request was rejected - Aggrieved assessee filed petition before HC and the same was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, only question that SC had to considered was whether, from order of Tribunal, this Court could come to conclusion that assessee was given proper opportunity to establish its plea about that SC were not satisfied - Hence, SC was of opinion that questions set out in application of assessee did arise for consideration and, therefore, HC should had directed Tribunal to refer those questions to HC for its opinion - Thus, in result, this Court allowed appeal and directed Tribunal to refer those questions set out in assessee's application to HC for its opinion - Appeal allowed.
