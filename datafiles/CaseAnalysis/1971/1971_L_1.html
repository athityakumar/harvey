R. B. Bansilal Abirchand Milis Company Limited v Labour Court Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 293; (1972) 1 SCC 154; AIR 1972 SC 451; 1972 (24) FLR 169; 1972 LabIC 285; 1972 (1) LLJ 231; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 580; 1972 UJ 291

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Coal Mines Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1948, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Coal Mines Provident Fund and Bonus Schemes Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 133(1)(a) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 6 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(kkk), 7, 7 A, 10(1)(d), 25C, 25F, 25J and 33-C(2) - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 5(1) and 5(2) - Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6-H(2) - Whether Labour Court had jurisdiction to entertain application for lay-off compensation u/s. 33-C(2) of Industrial Disputes Act? - Held, claim u/s. 33-C(2) clearly postulates that determination of question about computing benefit in terms of money may, in some cases, have to be preceded by an enquiry into existence of right and such an enquiry might be held to be incidental to main determination which has been assigned to Labour Court by sub-s. (2) - Labour Court must go into matter and come to a decision as to whether there was really a closure or a lay-off, if it took view that there was a lay-off without any closure of business it would be acting within its jurisdiction' if it awarded compensation - Appeal dismissed.
