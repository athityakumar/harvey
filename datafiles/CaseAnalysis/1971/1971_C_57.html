Delhi Cloth And General Mills Co. Ltd. Etc. v Commissioner Of Sales Tax, Indore

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 217; (1971) 2 SCC 559; AIR 1971 SC 2216; [1971] S.C.R. 945; 1971 (28) STC 331; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 945; 1971 TAXLR 1510

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Collected, Madhya Pradehs General Salestax Act, 1958

Summary: Indirect Tax - Madhya Pradesh General Sales tax Act, 1958 s. 44(1) - Sales - Tax collected - Not included in turnover - Assessed to tax - Challenged - Appellant/petitioner was dealer in vanaspati and recovered sales tax as part of sale price defined u/s.2 cl.(o) of the Act on sales made - Assessee in his turnover return sales tax collected was not included - Concerned authorities and HC held sales tax collected by it from its buyers was part of price of goods sold and taken into consideration in computing its turnover - Hence, instant appeal - Whether collection of sales tax by dealer from its purchasers could be considered as valuable consideration received by him for sale of goods.

Held, if a seller offers goods for sale, it is for him to quote a price which includes the tax if he desires to pass it on to the buyer. If the buyer agrees to the price, it is not for him to consider how it is made up or whether the seller has included tax or not - Therefore, when the seller passed on his tax liability to the buyer, the amount recovered by dealer is really part of the entire consideration paid by the buyer and the distinction between the two amounts, tax and price looses all significance. Appeals dismissed.
