State of West Bengal and Others v North Adjai Coal Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 85; (1971) 1 SCC 309; 1971 (27) STC 268

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 5(2)(a)(v) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Respondent-company released certain quantities of coal for consumption in East Pakistan - Under an arrangement between Govt. of India and Govt. of Pakistan, price of coal so supplied was to be realized by Govt. of India from Govt. of Pakistan - HighCourt declared that respondent was exempt from liability to pay sales tax in respect of coal supplied to Govt. of Pakistan - Whether in facts and circumstances of case, respondents were entitled to sales tax exemption claimed by them? - Held, sale if any was by virtue of s. 5(2)(a)(v) exempt from liability to sales tax under Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, for it was a sale in course of export - Appeal dismissed.
