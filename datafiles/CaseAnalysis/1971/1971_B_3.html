B. Sundar Rao and Others v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 856; (1972) 3 SCC 11; (1972) SCC (Cr) 138; AIR 1972 SC 739; 1972 CRLJ 520; 1971 CutLT 116

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25(f) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 151 and 107 - Orissa Preventive Detention Act, s. 3(2) - Whether detention of petitioners justified? - Grounds of detention must have direct relevance to maintenance of public order - If they have no such relevance they would be extraneous to purpose of Act in which case, order of detention will have to be struck down - Position is similar in cases where some only of many grounds have no relevance to maintenance of public order - By expression 'maintenance of public order' what is intended is prevention of grave public disorder - Held, grounds out of 22 which have been served upon petitioners are either vague or irrelevant and since they have influenced mind of authority in passing detention order, would be difficult to uphold it - Since Govt. had not considered representation as soon as it was received nor even at time of confirmation and continuation of detention, Govt. failed in one of its obligatory duties with regard to detention of petitioners - Therefore, for that reason also detention becomes illegal - Order accordingly.
