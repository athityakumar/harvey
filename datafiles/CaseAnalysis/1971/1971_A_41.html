Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa v Ashoka Marketing Company

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 374; (1972) 4 SCC 426; 1973 (2) CTR 505; [1972] 83 ITR 439; 1972 TAXLR 1115; 1971 UJ 895

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66(1) - Assessment - Shares - Trading losses - Assessee was a limited company acting as selling agents of various companies and also carrying on business in shares in jute as also in speculation - In respect of loss in first and second year of the assessment, the assessee claimed a loss of sum amount in respect of sale of certain shares - It was not disputed that the assessee did incur losses but, according to the revenue, those losses could not be considered as trading losses as the shares were purchased at a very high rate - Tribunal concluded that shares were purchased in course of business and that they could not be purchased so as to enable to control management of certain companies - Purchasing these shares was a misconception - Hence, the present appeal - Whether some of the losses incurred by the assessee were trading losses - Held, Tribunal had carefully considered the evidence on record and after applying the tests laid down had come to the conclusion, that the losses in question were trading losses and its conclusions had been accepted by the HC - HC had elaborately considered the evidence on record and had come to the conclusion that the business dealings and the losses were incurred in the course of business - It was not the case of the revenue that in arriving at its findings the Tribunal had either ignored the relevant considerations or taken into consideration irrelevant matters - Appeal dismissed.
