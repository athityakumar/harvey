N.B. Sanjana, Assistant Collector of Central v Elphinstone Spinning and Weaving Mills Company

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 123; (1971) 1 SCC 337; AIR 1971 SC 2039; 1990 (28) ECR 582; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 399; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Leather, Finance Act, 1951, Khadi and Other Handloom Industries Development (Additional Excise Duty on Cloth) Act, 1953

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 37 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10, 9(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 37 - Quashing of demand notices - Challenged - Respondents, owner of textile mill at Bombay, manufacture grey cloth - Respondents also have a factory for processing grey cloth into various other goods like leather cloth, book binding cloth and other coated fabrics - Central Government issued notification dt. 5.01.1957 exempting cotton fabrics for excise duty - Said notification was withdrawn on 30.07.1960 - Between 4.07.1958 and 30.07.1960, respondent used to manufacture grey cloth and used to store them in a bonded go down - Respondents, had, however, not obtained the requisite licence and so they paid excise duty on grey cloth manufactured in their mill - Respondents later on obtained the necessary licence with the result that they became entitled to remove the grey-cloth manufactured at their textile mill to their factory without paying excise duty on the grey-cloth at the time when the goods were removed - The grey-cloth removed after 30.09.1959 and before 30.07.1960 was removed after filling up the necessary forms and obtaining the permission of the Excise Inspector Incharge of the factory - The grey-cloth after it was processed and made into leather cloth or limitation leather cloth was again removed by the respondents as finished goods after filling in form A.R.I. prescribed by the Rules - Subsequently, excise authorities appear to have entertained some doubt whether the goods covered by these A.R.I, forms were of the description exempted under item No. 2 of the notification dt. 5.01.1957 - Department issued two notices u/rr. 9 and 10(A) of Rules requiring respondents to pay a sum of Rs. 1,07,146,39/- and Rs. 1,502,24/- - Demand notices were also issued against respondents - Respondents filed writ petition against said demand notices submitting that the neither r. 9 nor r. 10A of Rules gave power to the appellants to issue the demand notices and if at all it was r. 10 of Rules that applied and as the demands have been made long after the period of 3 months prescribed in the said Rules, the notices were illegal and void - The Single Judge quashed the said notices and held that r. 10 of Rules to be applied and as the demand notices had been issued long after the expiry of 3 months, the notices, were illegal and void - On appeal, DB confirmed the order of the Single Judge - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether Single Judge rightly quashed the demand notices issued by the appellants? - Held, there was no controversy that whenever goods were cleared by the respondents, necessary applications had been made to the officer concerned and the latter had passed orders of assessment to nil duty - To attract Sub-Rule 2 of r. 9 of Rules, the goods should have been removed in contravention of Sub-rule (1) of r. 9 of Rules - As it was not the case of the appellants that the respondents have not complied with the provisions of Sub-Rule 1 of r. 9 of Rules, demand notices issued u/r. 9A was illegal - As far as demand notice issued u/r. 10A of Rules, goods were removed with express permission of excise authorities duty payable under assessment order was nil there was no clandestine removal without assessment nothing on record to justify notice issued u/r. 10-A of the Rules - The proper provision under which action should have been taken if at all was r. 10 of Rules - The demands having admittedly been made long after the expiry of the period of 3 months, referred to in the said rule, it follows that the demands were not valid - Hence, HC was justified in striking down the notices - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
