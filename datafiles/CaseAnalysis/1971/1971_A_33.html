Mohan Lal v Anandibai And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 276; (1971) 1 SCC 813; AIR 1971 SC 2177; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 6, rr. 4, 17 , ss. 107 - (A) Practice and Procedure - (1) No pleading in respect of fraud or antedating of document and no evidence is led to meet such pleas - (2) Appellate Court remands a case with direction that the findings of the lower Courts are set aside - Directions, meaning of - (3) Appellate Court orders remand with direction to lower Court to consider particular plea and giving liberty to plaintiff to amend the plaint - Construction of the order - (B) Evidence - Presumption that a document is antedated - if can be raised against person producing it - Held, Court cannot on the basis of the plea raised for the first time during arguments record any finding - Direction refers to the findings considered by it and on which it differed from lower Court - plaint the order cannot be construed as allowing the plaintiff to raise any new plea - Cannot be raised against person producing it on ground of failure of the petition-writer to produce register required to be maintained by him as it can only affect the value of petition writer's evidence - Appeal Dismissed.
