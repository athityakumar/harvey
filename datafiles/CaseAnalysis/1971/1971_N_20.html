Sheikhupura Transport Co. Ltd. v Northern India Transport Insurance Co.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 145; (1971) 1 SCC 785; 1971 ACJ 206; AIR 1971 SC 1624; 1971 (73) PLR 722; [1971] S.C.R. 20; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 20; 1971 UJ 489

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Computation Of Compensation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 110B, 95(2)(b) - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Fixing compensation - Determination of the pecuniary loss - (B) Assessment made by the High Court, in awarding compensation to LRs of the deceased persons u/s.110-B of the Act - Not unreasonable - Interference by Supreme Court, if - (C) Statutory liability of the insurer to indemnify the insured in the case of a vehicle allowed to carry more than six passengers - Extent - Held, can be -ascertained only by balancing on the one hand the loss to the claimants of the future pecuniary benefit and on the other any pecuniary advantage which from whatever sources come to them by reason of the death - Cannot be considered to be unreasonable, SC in appeal by special leave will not interfere with the same - In absence of any contract to the contrary, the statutory liability extends only up to Rs. 2000/- each, though the total liability may go up to Rs. 20,000/- - Appeal Dismissed.
