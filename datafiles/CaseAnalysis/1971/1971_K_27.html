Keshavsinh Dwarkadas Kapadia, Etc v M/S. Indian Engineering Company

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 483; (1971) 2 SCC 706; AIR 1972 SC 1538; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 695; 1972 UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 3, 8, 8(1), 33 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 510 - English Arbitration Act, 1950, s. 27 - Appointment of umpire by arbitrators - (A) Whether appointment of an umpire without obtaining his consent is valid? - Where arbitrators were required to appoint an umpire before entering on reference and appointment was not conditional upon acceptance of appointment by umpire, appointment of umpire without obtaining consent of appointee was valid - (B) Manner of appointment - Disagreement between arbitrators - What constitutes disagreement - Held, constitution of arbitral body and manner in which appointments are made are primarily dealt with in arbitration agreement or else Act would apply - Where one of arbitrators declines to act and other is left alone,in such case it amounts to disagreement between two arbitrators - Appeals dismissed.
