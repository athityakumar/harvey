Ram Gobinda Dawan And Others v Smt.Bhaktabala

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 224; (1971) 1 SCC 387; AIR 1971 SC 664; 1971 (1) ALT(SC) 250; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 s. - Acquisition - Compensation - Claim - Title disputed - Resjudicata - Notification was given u/s.4 of the act for acquisition and declaration was published in gazette u/s. 6 of the Act for acquisition of land and compensation was awarded along with interest - Before land acquisition Collector appellant claimed title and entire compensation amount and contended that he purchased said land in court auction and respondents were not entitled to claim compensation - Land Acquisition Collector referred said matter to Trial Court, which held that appellant was not entitled to claim compensation - Appellant filed appeal before HC, which modified decree of Trial court and held appellant and respondents were entitled to half share each and also entitled to compensation amount - Hence, instant appeal - Whether previous decision on title in land acquisition proceedings operated as resjudicata in a subsequent suit between the same parties when the question of title was again raised.

Held, previous decision does not operate as res- judicata even against the claim of respondents inasmuch as the matter was not heard and finally decided on merits after contest by the Land Acquisition Court. Thus, plea of res-judicata is not accepted the decision of the two, courts, regarding respondents having in plot No. 9202 half share will have also to be sustained. Appeals dismissed.
