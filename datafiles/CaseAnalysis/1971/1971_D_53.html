 Smt. Damyanti Naranga and another v The Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 865; (1971) 1 SCC 678; AIR 1971 SC 966; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 840

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules 1955, r. 4B - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(c), 19(4), 32, 33 and 226, sch.7, List I(Entry 63) - Hindi Sahitya Sammelan Act 1962, ss. 2, 4(4), 7(2), 8 and 12(1)(a) - Societies Registration Act 1860, s. 4(1) - Question on validity of the Hindi Sahitya Sammelan Act 1962 and whether it violates right to form association? - The right to form an association includes the right to its continuance and any law altering the composition of the association compulsorily will be a breach of the right to form the association - Held, if law is passed not merely for ensuring proper management and administration of the property, but for totally depriving the persons, in whom the property vested, of their right to hold the property, the law cannot be justified as a reasonable restriction u/art. 19(5) - The Act is declared to be invalid, there will be restraint on the concerned bodies, including the Union Govt., from taking or continuing any action under the Act - Petition allowed.
