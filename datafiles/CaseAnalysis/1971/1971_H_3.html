 Union Of India v H. S. Dhillon

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, Subimal Chandra Roy, D.G. Palekar, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 446; (1971) 2 SCC 779; AIR 1972 SC 1061; [1972] 83 ITR 582; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 33; 1972 TAXLR 449

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Finance Act, 1969, s. 24 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246 and 248 - High Court held that s. 24 of the Finance Act, 1969, amending the provisions of the Wealth Tax Act, 1957 was beyond the legislative competence of Parliament - Amendment had the effect of including the capital value of agricultural land for the purposes of computing net wealth - Whether the amendment of s. 24 of the Finance Act, 1969 having effect of amending the provisions of the Wealth Tax Act, 1957 was legally valid - Held, yes - Powers of Parliament were not restricted - Entry 97 provided for words any other matter - The import of these words was other than those included in Entries 1-96 of List I - Parliament cannot include agricultural lands in capital value of assets under Entry 86 - Parliament can exercise powers under Entry 97 as agricultural land was not included in Entries 1-96 of List I - Article 248 provides Parliament exclusive power to make law on matter not enumerated in Lists II and III - Amendment valid - Appeal allowed.
