Shambhu Prasad Singh v Mst. Phool Kumari & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 867; (1971) 2 SCC 28; AIR 1971 SC 1337; 1972 (1) MLJ 12; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 12; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Bihar and Orissa Municipal Survey Act, 1920 - (A) Whether transaction evidenced by Ex 1, D/- 20-03-1915 was a family arrangement so as to confer on appellant and his father, since deceased, title to a half share in house in dispute? - Taken as a whole and in light of recitals and statements in operative part of document indicating conflicts amongst members of family, document represented an arrangement bona fide entered into, for settling existing or at any rate apprehended disputes, and therefore, satisfied tests of a family arrangement - Held, father must be said to have acquired a half share in house in dispute under Ex 1 - (B) Whether such title became extinguished as a result of adverse possession for statutory period by deceased husband of respondent No. 1? - Disputed house and land sold under Ex. C being different land High Court was not right in relying on that sale deed to prove adverse possession on ground that father of appellant never took objection to the said sale - Incidents described were irrelevant on question of adverse possession as they took place long time after title by adverse possession would have been completed - Held, Division Bench not justified in interfering with finding of fact that adverse possession by 'B' was sufficiently interrupted by acts of possession by father of appellant therefore, his title was not extinguished by adverse possession - Appeal allowed.
