 Parshottam Jadavji Jani v State Of Gujarat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 666; (1971) 1 SCC 843; AIR 1971 SC 1188; [1971] S.C.R. 294; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Gujarat Industrial Development Act, 1962, s. 1 - Constitution, 1950, art. 246 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 55 - (A) Validity of 1962 Act - (B) Land Acquisition Act - (1) The establishment of an industrial area by a corporation - Whether it is for public pourpose - (2) Enquiry u/s. 5A - Held in accordance with the rules framed u/s. 55 - Whether objector can cross examine the officers of the corporation - Act held, is within the Legislative competence of the State Legislature and is valid - Is a public purpose - In an enquiry u/s. 5A held in accordance with the rules framed u/s. 55, the objector is not entitled to cross examine the officers of the corporation, especially when they have given no evidence. It is enough if an opportunity of being heard personally is given to him

Appeal Dismissed.
