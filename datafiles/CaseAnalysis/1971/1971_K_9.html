State of Meghalaya and Others v Ka Brhyien Kurkalang and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 561; (1972) SCC (Cr) 106; (1972) 1 SCC 148; AIR 1972 SC 223; 1972 CRLJ 47; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Summary: Constitution - United Khasi-Jaintia Hills Districts (Application of Laws) Regulation, V of 1952 - Transitional provision, sch.6, Para 19(b) - Validity of the United Khasi-Jaintia Hills Districts (Application of Laws) Regulation, V of 1952 promulgated by the Governor of Assam under Paragraph 19(1)(b) of the Sixth Schedule to the Constitution, the notification thereunder extending thereby the Eastern Bengal and Assam Excise Act, 1910 to the United Khasi-Jaintia Hills District and the order of the Deputy Commissioner refusing renewal of a permit authorising the respondents to distill liquor from millet on the ground that there could not be a renewal of the original permit as that permits was issued by one who had no authority to issue it -

Governor's power to promulgate regulation - Held, regulation promulgated by the Governor does not automatically cease to have effect on the District Council being constituted - Appeal allowed.
