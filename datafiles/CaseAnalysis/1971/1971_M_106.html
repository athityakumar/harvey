Balraj Khanna and Others v Moti Ram

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 651; (1971) 3 SCC 399; (1971) SCC (Cr) 647; AIR 1971 SC 1389; 1971 CRLJ 1110; [1971] S.C.R. 447; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 447; 1971 UJ 605

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Exact Words

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 202 and 203 - IPC, 1860, ss. 500 and 499, Exception - (A) Magistrate is satisfied on basis of the material placed before him by a complainant that the prima facie case made out - (B) Defamation - Form of complaint - Actual words used - (C) Question as to applicability of Exception to s. 499 of IPC, 1860- When arise - Held, he must commit the accused for trial - It is not necessary that the complaint for defamation must set out the actual words used by the accused which are alleged to be defamatory - Question can arise only alter commencement of trial and at the stage of enquiry whether prima facie case is made out on basis of the complaint - Appeal Dismissed.
