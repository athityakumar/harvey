Cricket Association of Bengal and Others v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 810; (1971) 3 SCC 239; (1971) SCC (Cr) 446; AIR 1971 SC 1925; 1971 CRLJ 1432; [1971] S.C.R. 200; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 200; 1971 UJ 535

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss.204, 253 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.114, 337 and 338 - Private complaint against appellant association - Appellant association sold tickets for cricket match more then capacity of stadium - Single judge bench of High Court dismissed revision petition of appellant association challenging order of magistrate issuing summons, but also observed that offence not made out from facts - Magistrate terminated proceedings and discharged accused persons - HC issued suo-moto rule to appellants and complainant stating that discharge order not proper - Held, in proper cases HC can take action suo-moto but this is not a proper case - DB was not justified in interfering with orders passed by Chief Presidency Magistrate, in circumstances of this case - Appeal allowed.
