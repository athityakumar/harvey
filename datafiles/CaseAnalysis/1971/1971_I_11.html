 Indian Mica Micanite Industries v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 665; (1971) 2 SCC 236; AIR 1971 SC 1182; [1971] S.C.R. 319; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Constitution, art. 265, 226 , sch.7, List 2, entry 65 - Board Of Revenue Act, 1913 -

Bihar and Orissa Excise Act, 1915, s. 90 - Rules frame under s. 90 , r. 111 - (A) Vires of the levy made u/r.111 of Rules framed u/s. 90 of 1955 Act - For a licence to possess denatured spirit used by a manufacturer in his process - Whether fee - (B) State through negligence failing to place before High Court sufficient materials to support an impugned rule - Supreme Court in appeal finding rule void on the materials on record - Decision would cause considerable financial loss - Held, cannot be upheld as a fee as there is no sufficient quid pro quo for the levy - Remand of case to enable party to place sufficient materials before HC - Appeal Allowed.
