Karam Chand Thapar & Bros. (P) Ltd v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, (Central)Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 337; (1972) 4 SCC 124; AIR 1971 SC 1590; [1971] 80 ITR 167

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66 and 66A - (A) lTAT is a final fact finding authority - Permissibility to re-appreciate the material on record to find out whether the facts found by the Tribunal are correct or not - (B) Capital or income - Compensation received by assessee for termination of one of its managing agencies, which resulted in the dew truction of a source of income - (C) Question whether a receipt is capital or income is a mixed question of law and fact - Held, neither High Court in reference nor the Supreme Court in appeal can re-appreciate the material on record to find out whether the facts found by the Tribunal are correct or not - Held, is a capital receipt - Question may depend on the particular facts of each case,but the conclusion to be drawn from those facts is one of law - Appeal Allowed.
