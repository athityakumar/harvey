Union of India v Sardar Bahadur

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 45; (1972) 4 SCC 618; AIR 1972 SC 230; 1972 LabIC 627; 1972 (1) LLJ 1; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether the punishment of compulsory retirement imposed by the President can be sustained even though the first two charges have not been proved? - Held, that that if the order of a punishing authority can be supported on any finding as to substantial misdemeanour for which the punishment can be imposed, Court shall not consider whether the charge proved alone would have weighed with the authority in imposing the punishment - The Court is not concerned to decide whether the punishment imposed, provided it is justified by the rules, is appropriate having regard to the misdemeanour established - Appeal is allowed.
