Bankipur Club Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 386; (1972) 4 SCC 386; 1972 CTR 245; [1971] 82 ITR 831; 1972 TAXLR 981; 1972 UJ 139

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s. 34(1)(b) - Validity of reassessment proceedings for the assessment years under reference - Information referred to in s. 34(1)(b) must be what the Income-tax Officer receives after he makes the original order of assessment - It must come to his knowledge subsequent to the assessment sought to be reopened - Held, ITO could not show that he received any material information subsequent to passing of original assessment orders - No such material was placed before the Tribunal - HC had not given any reason to conclude that ITO had subsequent information to reassess the assessee u/ s. 34(1)(b) - ITO was incompetent to initiate proceedings u/ s. 34(1)(b) - Order of HC in respect of reassessment was discharged - Order accordingly.
