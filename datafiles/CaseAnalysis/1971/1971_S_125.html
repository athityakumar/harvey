C. Sankaranarayanan Etc. v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 162; (1971) 2 SCC 361; AIR 1971 SC 1997; 1971 LabIC 1178; [1971] S.C.R. 654; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Kerala Education Rules, 1959, chap. 27B, r. 4 and chap. 14C, r. 2 - Constitution, arts. 14, 309, 311(2) - Education Act, 1959, s. 12 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.115 - Service matter - (A) Age of superannuation of School teachers - Any aided school teacher opting to be governed by rules under chap. 27-B and 14-C is relegated to the position of Govt. school teacher whose age of superannuation is 55 - (B) Conditions of service of Govt. employee - Power of Govt. u/art. 309 to make rules regulating the conditions of service of Govt. employees or of teachers in aided schools u/s. 12 of Kerala Education Act - Effect of agreement upon - (C) Compulsory retirement - Change in the rule relating to retirement - Applicability of arts. 311(2) or 14 of Constitution - Held, cannot get the benefit of note to r. 8 of chap. 27 which provides 60 years as age of superannuation for aided school teachers - Cannot in any way be affected by any agreement - Rule of estoppel against Govt. cannot be invoked in such cases - Change can be validly made and it does not attract either arts. 311(2) or 14 - Petition Dismissed.
