State of Madras v Lateef Hamid and Company

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 839; (1971) 3 SCC 560; AIR 1972 SC 1781; 1972 (1) CTR 341; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 73; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 577; 1971 (28) STC 690

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 31 and 61 - TN General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 12 - (A) Appeal to Supreme Court - Interference with finding of fact - (B) Povision of s. 31 of 1959 Act - Powers of Appellate Asst. Commr. - While disposing an appeal u/s. 31 filed by an assessee against assessment made under repealed Act of 1939 - Held, Supreme Court will not interfere with a finding of fact recorded by Sales Tax Tribunal and more so when High Court declined to interfere - Further held, is competent to enhance assessment - Appeal allowed.
