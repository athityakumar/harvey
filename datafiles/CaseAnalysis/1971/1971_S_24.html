Sadiq Ali and Another v Election Commission of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 321; (1972) 4 SCC 664; AIR 1972 SC 187; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 318

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 324 and 327 - Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 169 - Elections - Allotment of symbol - Entitled to - Appellant filed instant appeal against the order of the respondent/Election Commission of India/ Commission under paragraph 15 of Order - Whereby Commission held that for purpose of allotment of symbol in elections political party presided over by J was Indian National Congress and was entitled to symbol of 'Two Bullocks with Yoke on', reserved for said Congress - Appellant contended that Commission had no jurisdiction to hold enquiry - Whether Commission had jurisdiction within meaning of paragraph 15 of the Order, to decide whether anyone or none of rival sections or groups of Indian National Congress, a national party, was the said Indian National Congress - Held, paragraph 15 designed to ensure that because of a dispute had arose in a political party between two or more groups, entire scheme of the Symbols Order relating to allotment of a symbol reserved for political party was not set at naught - Fact that power for settlement of such a dispute was vested in Commission should not constitute a valid ground for assailing vires of and striking down paragraph 15 - Commission was an authority created by Constitution and according to art. 324 of Constitution, superintendence, direction and control of electoral rolls for and conduct of elections to Parliament and to Legislature of every State and of elections to office of President and Vice-President should be vested in Commission - Fact that power of resolving a dispute between two rival groups for allotment of symbol of a political party was vested in such a high authority should arose a presumption, though rebuttable, and provide a guarantee, though not absolute but to a considerable extent, that power should not be misused but should be exercised in a fair and reasonable manner - There was also no substance in contention that as power to make provisions in respect to elections was given to Parliament by art. 327 of Constitution, power could not be further delegated to Commission - However, art. 324 provided that superintendence, direction and control of elections should be vested in Election Commission - It, therefore, could not be said that when Commission issues direction, it did so not on its own behalf but as delegate of some other authority - It might also be mentioned in context that when Central Govt. issued the Rules in exercise of its powers u/s. 169 of the Act, it did so as required by that section after consultation with Commission - There was no substance in contention that paragraph 15 of Symbols Order was ultra vires powers of Commission - Appeals dismissed.
