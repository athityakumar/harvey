Girdhari Lal Gupta v D. H. Mehta and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 870; (1971) 3 SCC 189; (1971) SCC (Cr) 279; AIR 1971 SC 2162; 1971 CRLJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Corporate - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 23C(1)(2) - Offence by company/firm - Conviction and sentence - Awarded to appellant being one of partner of accused/firm - Review petition against such conviction - Ground s. 23C(1)(2) of Act never brought to notice of court - (A) Expression 'a person incharge and responsible for conduct of affairs of a company', meaning thereof - Term 'in-charge' - Held, person should be in over all control of day to day business of company - Provision further provides for that a director, who may be a party to policy being followed by a company and yet not be incharge of business of company - (B) Whether appellant was in charge of business of firm, if yes, than whether he was in charge of conduct of business of firm at time contravention under Act was committed? - Appellant's own submission that he was one who alone looked after affairs of firm - Thus he was in charge of business - It is true that onus of proving that appellant was in charge of conduct of business of company at time of contravention, lies on prosecution, but when a director/partner in charge of a business proceeds abroad it does not mean that he ceases to be in charge, unless there is evidence that he gave up charge in favour of another person - Held, appellant was in charge of business of firm - On sentencing part court said that it might take notice of fact that a person is being vicariously punished for an offence and if he shows that it is possible that contravention of Act took place without his knowledge or neglect, a sentence of imprisonment may not be imposed - Sentence of fine awarded - Petition partly allowed.
