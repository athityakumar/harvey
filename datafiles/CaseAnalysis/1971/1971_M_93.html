Bhanu Kumar Shastri v Mohan Lal Sukhadia and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 748; (1971) 1 SCC 370; AIR 1971 SC 2025; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 522

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 116C and 123 - Rajasthan Urban Improvement Act, 1959, ss.72, 2(1)(i) and 2(1)(vi) - Public Works Department Financial and Accounts Rules, r. 375 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 101 - CPC, 1908, O. 41, r. 22 - (A) Election - (1) A case neither based on pleadings nor raised before High Court - Raised at before Supreme Court - (2) Respondent in appeal u/s. 116 - Permissibility to support judgment under appeal - On ground decided against him - (3) Allegation of corrupt practice - Ouns of proof - On whom lies - (B) Allegations of corrupt practice against Ministers on eve of election - Abuse of opportunities of power and resources - Scope - (B) Rajasthan Urban Improvement Act, 1959, ss. 72, 2(1)(i) and 2(1)(vi) - (C) Public Works Department Financial and Accounts Rules, r. 375 - Officer required to carry out a work for which no appropriation exists - Duty - Held, cannot be allowed to be raised at appellate stage before Supreme Court - In appeal u/s. 116A, SC may in appropriate case permit respondent to support judgment under appeal on any ground even though decided against him - Being one of serious nature onus of its proof lies on election petitioner - Their normal bona fide acts should be kept apart from abuse of opportunities of power and resources which are not available to their opponents - A road cannot be an improvement so as to attract s. 72 - If an officer is required to carry out a work for which no appropriation exists, he should take immediate steps to obtain orders either to stop work or regularise its execution from appropriate competent authority - Appeal Dismissed.
