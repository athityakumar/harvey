Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company Limited v Ganesh Dutt and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 60; (1972) 4 SCC 834; 1972 (24) FLR 147; 1972 LabIC 594; 1972 (1) LLJ 172

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Enquiry - Validity of - Held, Tribunal's finding that domestic enquiry is vitiated by very serious infirmities is erroneous in law - The Tribunal erred in law in rejecting application filed by management for permission to produce evidence to justify action taken against workmen - Workman against whom an enquiry for misconduct is being conducted, cannot be cross-examined - Where a proper enquiry has been held by management, Tribunal has to accept finding arrived at in that enquiry unless it is perverse or unreasonable and should give permission asked for unless it has reason to believe that management is guilty of victimisation or has been guilty of unfair labour practice or is acting mala fide - If master has held proper enquiry and come to conclusion that servant should be dismissed and in consequence suspends him pending permission required u/s 33, he has power to order such suspension, thus suspending contract of employment temporarily, so that there is no obligation on him to pay wages and no obligation on servant to work - If Tribunal grants permission, suspended contract would come to an end and there will be no further obligation to pay any wages after date of suspension - If permission is refused, suspension would be wrong and workman would be entitled to all his wages from date of suspension - Order of tribunal set aside - Appeal allowed.
