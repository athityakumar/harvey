 Union of India v Jyoti Prakash Mitter

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 120; (1971) 1 SCC 396; AIR 1971 SC 1093; 1971 (1) LLJ 256; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 483; 1971 (2) SLR 203

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132 and 217 - Dispute regarding actual date of birth of High Court Judge - HC held, that case involved a substantial question of law as to interpretation of art. 217(3) of Constitution, 1950 - Whether decision was in truth rendered by Chief Justice of India and not by President? - Held, Court will not sit in appeal over judgment of President nor will Courts determine weight which should be attached to evidence - Appreciation of evidence is entirely left to President and it is not for Courts to hold that on evidence placed before President on which conclusion is founded - If they were called upon to decide case, they would have reached some other conclusion - Appeal allowed.
