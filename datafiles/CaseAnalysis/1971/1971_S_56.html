Hamidullah (Dead) By His Legal Representatives v Sheikh Abdullah and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 534; (1972) 4 SCC 800; AIR 1972 SC 410; 1972 UJ 272

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Permanent tenancy - Plaintiff filed suit for partition and recovery of the plaintiff' share in plaint property - Defendants contended in suit that apart from being co-sharers, also contended that they were permanent lessees of portion marked in report - Trial Court even though passed preliminary decree for partition, yet decided permanent tenancy right against defendant Nos. 1 to 3 - On appeal, Single Judge of HC upheld findings of Trial Court on permanent tenancy rights but on special appeal, DB of HC reversed the said findings - Hence, instant appeal by plaintiffs - Whether defendant Nos. 1 to 3 established their permanent tenancy over portion marked in Commissioner's plan.

Held, as per agreement dt.29-1-1918, firstly, rent would continue to be the same, secondly that the lessee was liable to be evicted without payment of any compensation for the constructions if the mortgagee become the owner of the property and required it for his own purposes, and thirdly that in the event of the mortgagor redeeming the property, then FaquirBux would enter into a fresh agreement with the mortgagor. This document would demolish case of permanent tenancies set up by defendant Nos. 1 to 3. Hence, DB of HC was wrong in reversing judgment and decree of Single Judge and so, decree of Single Judge is restored. Appeal allowed.
