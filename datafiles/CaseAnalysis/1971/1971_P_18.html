Patel Laljibhai Somabhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 511; (1971) 2 SCC 376; (1971) SCC (Cr) 548; AIR 1971 SC 1935; 1971 CRLJ 1437; [1971] S.C.R. 834; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Party

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 , s.195(1)(c) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, u/ss. 467 and 471 - Forged document - Evidence - Certificate - Appellant instituted civil suit and his brother for recovery of Rs 2,000 on basis of cheque under signature of complainant - Question raised related to scope and effect of s.195(1)(c) of CrPC and its applicability to cases where forged document had been produced as evidence in judicial proceeding by party - Defence in suit was that cheque in question and certain coupons which were produced and relied upon in that suit were forged and suit was false - Accused persons for offences punishable u/ss. 467 and 471 of IPC in respect of that document - Appeal with certificate u/art. 134(1)(c) of Constitution against judgment and order - Whether section 195(1)(c) of code applicable for cases were forged document produced as evidence in judicial proceeding by party there to and prosecution of that party sought for offences under ss. 467 and 471, IPC in respect of that document - -- Held, s.195(1)(c) was inapplicable - Purpose and object of Legislature in creating bar against cognizance of private complaints in regard to offences mentioned in s.195(1)(b) and (c) - Forged documents were produced or false evidence was led and conclusions of criminal courts dealing with private complaint - Document were genuine - Mere fact that u/ss., 467 and 420 of IPC were also tacked on to offence u/s.471 of IPC would not serve to take case out of scope and ambit of s.195(1)(c) - Offence u/s.471 of IPC was clearly covered by prohibition contained in s.195(1)(c) - Documents were forged by party to earlier proceeding in his character as such party after suit had been instituted - Appeal allowed.
