Calcutta Electric Supply Corporation v Commissioner Of Wealth Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 269; (1972) 3 SCC 222; AIR 1971 SC 2447; 1973 (2) CTR 475; [1971] 82 ITR 154; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 159; 1971 UJ 794

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 7(2) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Asset - Determination of value - Balance sheet of electricity supply company showed service connections as the assets - No other evidence to show that those were the assets of consumers on relevant valuation date - WTO refused to grant deduction by holding them as company's assets - Validity - WTO held, was justified though he accepted the value of assets as shown in the balance sheet - Mode of determining value of assets indicated - Appeal Dismissed.
