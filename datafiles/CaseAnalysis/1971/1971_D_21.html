Deb Sadhan Roy v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 549; (1972) SCC (Cr) 45; (1972) 1 SCC 308; AIR 1972 SC 1924; 1973 CRLJ 446; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22 - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, s. 2(c) - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 30-A(8) - Preventive detention - Legality of - Whether State Govt. is under duty to confirm Board's report within three months and to communicate such confirmation to detenu for continuation of detention? - Held, confirmation of opinion of Advisory Board to continue detention beyond three months must be within three months from date of detention in conformity with mandate in art. 22(4) of Constitution - (B) Whether confirmation of detention should be communicated to detenu within period of three months from date of detention? - Further held, though u/r. 30-A(8) of there is nothing to indicate that appropriate authority should communicate to detenu decision to extend period beyond three months, 'it is desirable and it would be fair and just that such a decision should in every case be communicated to detenu' - (C) Whether grounds of detention are irrelevant or vague? - Held, the act causing insult referred to in s. 2(c) of Act of 1970 must be such as would be capable of arousing feelings of indignation in someone and was accordingly restricted - Further held, in this case what is said to have been defiled by petitioner and his associates is statue of Rabindra Nath Tagore, a Poet and sage venerated by all in this country and affords a sufficient ground for detention other ground also directly connects act with disturbance of public order - Grounds of detention are not vague - Petition dismissed.
