M/S. Voltas Ltd. v J. M. Demello & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 212; (1971) 2 SCC 479; AIR 1971 SC 1902; 1971 (23) FLR 109; 1971 LabIC 1171; 1971 (2) LLJ 307; [1971] S.C.R. 865; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 865

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cost Of Living

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 s. 33(C)(2) - Merger - Alteration of scheme of DA - Benefit - Denied - Challenged -Respondent no.1 joined service of 'X' company and after merger with appellant company became employee of appellant company - Appellant company altered scheme of dearness allowance(DA), in which there was increase in amount of DA applied only to officers and not to workmen - Respondent no.1 filed application before Tribunal u/s.33(C)(2) for computation of benefit due to him payable under Meher award in accordance with recommendations of Lakdawala committee, which was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent no.1 filed petition before HC, which set aside Tribunals order and petition was allowed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Tribunal came to the conclusion that ceiling existed in the scheme of DA prevailing in the company at all the various stages and that deletion of such a ceiling was not the subject-matter of either demand No. 9 or of the reference before the Meher Tribunal, and that its award was confined to the revision only of the existing scheme in the three matters referred - Further, it is not possible that the decision of Tribunal suffered from an error apparent on the face of it and there was no question of any estoppel against the company against its raising the question of the ceiling in view of the finding by the Tribunal - Therefore, question of the ceiling was not the subject-matter of the reference before the Meher Tribunal and such conclusion of the Tribunal could not be interfered with by the HC on any one of the well-known grounds on which only such interference is permissible - Hence, HC was not justified in interfering with Tribunal's order - Appeal allowed.
