 Hussain Bhai And Others v Hussain Bhai And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 166; (1971) 2 SCC 17; AIR 1971 SC 1256; [1971] 80 ITR 477; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; [1971] S.C.R. 390; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 390

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1959 - (A) Whether provisions of s. 34(4) of 1922 act as inserted by amendment Act of 1959 can be invoked to validate a notice issued prior to it - (B) Provision of s. 4 amending Act 1959 includes all notices issued u/s. 34(1)(a) before the commencement of the Act - Ground on which the validity of notice cannot be attacked - Held, contemplates notice issued after coming into force of 1959 Act and cannot be invoked to validatee a notice issued prior to it - Though s. 4 includes all notices issued u/s. 34(1)(a) before the commencement of the Act, the only ground on which the validity of notice cannot be attacked is that at the time of issue of the notice the period prescribed u/s. 34(1)(a) prior to its amendment by s. 18, Finance Act, 1956 had expired - Appeal Allowed.
