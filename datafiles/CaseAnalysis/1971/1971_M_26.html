Commissioner Of Income-Tax Bombay v Maharashtra Sugar Mills Ltd. Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 389; (1971) 3 SCC 543; AIR 1971 SC 2434; 1973 CTR 489; [1971] 82 ITR 452; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 230; 1971 UJ 806

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act,1922, s.10(2)(xv) - Allowance - Deduction of remuneration - Expenditure - Agricultural income - Assessee/limited Company owned extensive lands in which sugar cane was grown and cane was used by assessee for manufacture of sugar in factory - Cultivation of sugar cane and manufacture of sugar by assessee constituted one single and indivisible business - Assessee claimed deduction of remuneration paid to managing agents u/s.10 (2)(xv) of 1922 Act as an item of expenditure laid out or expended wholly or exclusively for purpose of business - Income-tax Officer and Appellate Assistant Commissioner disallowed a part of remuneration on grounds that part of assessee's business namely cultivation of sugar cane, being an agricultural operation, income was not eligible to tax - Any expenditure incurred in respect of that activity was not deductible - Tribunal and HC on reference upheld plea of assessee that entire sum was deductible - Whether part of commission relating to such income not deductible from assessee's gross profits - Held, part of assessee's income not eligible to tax-Commission to managing agent - Equitable considerations are wholly out of place in construing provisions of taxing statute - If the allowance claimed permissible under Act then it has to be deducted from grow profits, and if it was not so permissible it has to be rejected - Allowance claimed was undoubtedly laid out or expended for the purpose of the business carried on by assessee - Taxable income of business the agricultural income as defined in s. 2 of the Act should be deducted from the total - Income for arriving at the taxable income - No further deduction shall be made of any expenditure incurred by assessee as cultivator or receiver of rent in kind - Reference to expenditure incurred by assessee as a cultivator only applies to process ordinarily employed by cultivator in raising crops and all other incidental and supplementary activities up to stage of sale of produce and disbursements such as payment of managing agency commission - Appeal dismissed.
