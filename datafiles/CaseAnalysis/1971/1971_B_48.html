Bihar State Board Religious Trust, Patna v Mahant Sri Biseshwar Das

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 719; (1971) 1 SCC 574; AIR 1971 SC 2057; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 680

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - CPC, 1908, s. 92 - Hindu Law - Religious and Charitable Endowments - (A) Burden of proof - To prove that properties of the temple are held by the reigning mahant on trust for public purposes of a religious or charitable character - Burden lies on the Board of Religious Trust - Adverse inference for failure of mahant to produce certain document whereunder the properties had been gifted to the founding mahant - (B) Feeding of Sadhu and giving hospitality to wayfarers - If indicate of temple being public temple or its properties being subject to a public trust - (C) Dedication to public - Inference as to - From fact that members of public are freely admitted to the temple for worship and/or for attending festivals celebrated by mahants - (D) Question whether property is given to head of a mutt for his personal benefit (E) Installation of idol permanently on a pedestal and the fact that temple is constructed on grounds separate from residential quarters of mahant - Whether conclusive proof of dedication to public -

Court held, cannot draw an adverse inference for failure of mahant to produce certain document - Is not by itself indicative - Dedication to public cannot be readily inferred from the mere fact - Question only has to be decided either from the terms of grant itself or from circumstances of case - are not conclusive proof of dedication to public as such factors are also found in private temples and mutts and unless the asthal (Religious Mutt) itself is a public trust for religious or charitable purpose properties appertaining thereto would not be properties of public trust for religious or charitable purposes

Appeal Dismissed.
