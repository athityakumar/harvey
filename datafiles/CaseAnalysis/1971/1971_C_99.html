Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat v Bhanji Lavji

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 333; (1972) 4 SCC 88; AIR 1971 SC 717; [1971] 79 ITR 583

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Duty To Disclose Facts

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(a) - A/ys 1947-1948, 1948-1949 and 1949-1950 - Sale proceed out of taxable territories - Disclosed facts - Sustainability - Income-tax Officer initiated proceedings against assessee for A/ys 1947-1948, 1948-1949 and 1949-1950, amongst others and assessee disclosed all facts to Income-tax Officer and contended that since he had no business in taxable territories nor any income taxable in taxable territories and assessment proceedings initiated by Income-tax Officer be discharged - Income-tax Officer upheld assessee's contention for A/ys 1947-1948, 1948-1949 and 1949-50, and proceedings be discharged - Further, in 1956, Income-tax Officer, reopened assessment for A/ys 1947-1948, 1948-1949 and 1949-1950, and assessee challenged jurisdiction of Income-tax Officer and Income-tax Officer rejected plea raised by assessee and brought income which in his view had escaped assessment to tax -Aggrieved assessee filed appeals before Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Income-tax Appellate Tribunal which were dismissed - Thereafter, four questions were referred to HC out of order of Tribunal which were answered in favour of assessee, with certificate granted - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, appellants (Commissioner) counsel contended that in any event assessee had in assessment of income for year 1949-1950, did not disclosed all primary facts and also contended that this statement before Income-tax Officer was bare statement about receipt of interest from X and there was no reference to account with Bank of India Ltd but there was no substance in that contention - Assessee had invited attention of Income-tax Officer to previous assessment proceeding and also had invited his attention to fact that on interest received, tax at maximum rate was charged of bank account there was no express reference - But this Court agreed with HC that since factual position had remained unaltered in A/ys 1949-1950, there was no non-disclosure of material facts necessary for assessment of income - Income-tax Officer was fully aware of assessment proceedings for years 1947-1948 and 1948-1949, and in his order he expressly referred to those proceedings being aware of earlier proceedings and reasons for passing previous order, if Income-tax Officer passed an order in effect holding that there was no income of assessee chargeable to tax, Income-tax Officer could not sought to reassess income on ground of failure to disclosed fully and truly facts necessary for assessment - Therefore, SC did not think that any ground was made out for disagreeing with HC in respect of validity of order of reassessment for year 1949-1950 - Appeals disposed of.
