Ahmedabad Rana Caste Association v Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 246; (1971) 3 SCC 475; AIR 1972 SC 273; [1971] 82 ITR 704; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1922 s. 4(3)(i) - Trust - Beneficiaries - Particular community - Exemption claimed - Denied - Challenged - Assessee/association of persons held properties and among its objects and purpose include management of movable and immovable properties of one particular section of community in Ahmedabad and doing acts to improve education, medical help etc. - Assessee claimed exemption u/s. 4(3)(i) of the Act for A/Y's 1960 - 61, 1961 - 62 and 1962 -63, which was denied - Assessee appealed before Appellate Assistant Commissioner which was denied - Aggrieved assessee filed appeal before Tribunal which allowed exemption - Reference was made before HC which was answered in negative against assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the beneficiaries could be said to constitute a section of the community.

Held, predominant content and requirement of the clause defining "beneficiaries" in the constitution of the assessee is the factum of their belonging to one particular section of community of Ahmedabad - Therefore, uniting the potential beneficiaries into the class consists of being members of the particular caste or community of Ahmedabad whether as natives or as being admitted to that caste or community under custom or usage - Thus, mere fact that a person of that particular section of community who is not an original native of Ahmedabad has to prove his credentials according to the custom and usage of that community to get admitted into that community cannot introduce a personal element - Further, it cannot possibly discover a similar element of personal nature in the members of the one particular section of community who settle in Ahmedabad and have been accepted by the particular section of community of that place as members of that community - Thus, it cannot be regarded as an element which would detract from the impersonal nature of the common quality - Hence, appeals are allowed and matters are remitted to the High Court for returning the answer to the question referred after determining the other points which were left undecided. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - A group of persons relationship which takes a group nexus between them is their personal relationship to a single propositus or to several propositi, they are neither the community nor a section of the community for charitable purposes
