Hazari Lal Gupta v Rameshwar Prashad and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 274; (1972) SCC (Cr) 208; (1972) 1 SCC 452; AIR 1972 SC 484; 1972 CRLJ 298; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 97; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 666; 1972 UJ 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Cheating - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 420 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 561A - Appellant concern was in trade of carpets which they imported from respondents, who used to manufacture carpets - Appellant withheld payment of bills of carpets sent by respondents as a result of which respondent lodged an FIR - Appellants were arrested - Bail was granted to appellants and High Court further reduced surety amount but directed appellants to surrender passport - Appellant contended that there was no proper sanction and no certificate u/s. 188 that charges against appellant ought to be inquired in country - Whether appellant can seek relief u/s. 561A? - Held, no - Where investigation into the circumstances of an alleged recognizable offence is carried on under the provisions of the CrPC, 1973, HC does not interfere with such investigation because it would then be impeding investigation and jurisdiction of statutory authorities to exercise power in accordance with the provisions of the CrPC, 1973 - Appellant cannot seek remedy again u/s. 561A - HC does not as a matter of routine interfere in investigation and HC justified in dismissing application u/s. 561A - Appeal dismissed.
