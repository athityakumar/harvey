Jer and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 323; (1972) 4 SCC 77; [1971] 79 ITR 546; 1972 TAXLR 2436; 1971 UJ 183

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s. 26 A - Uttar Pradesh Excise Rules, 1910 - Partnership to carry on wholesaling of foreign liquor - Licence was renewed for many years, and contained no prohibition against entering into partnership for carrying on business - Partnership was registered under Act, and assessed as such - However, on application for renewal, CIT set aside order allowing renewal - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, firm was entitled to registration u/s. 26 A of Act? - Held, licence does not prohibit holder from entering into partnership by holder of licence it merely provides that licence shall not be sub-let or transferred - No prohibition against entry by holder of licence to a partnership - Firm was entitled on that account to registration - Appeals allowed.
