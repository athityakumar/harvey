Kochan Kani Kunjuraman Kani v Mathevan Kani Sankaran Kani

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 204; (1971) 2 SCC 345; AIR 1971 SC 1398; [1971] S.C.R. 786; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 13 and 101 - Family and Personal - Custom - Family custom (Kerala) - (A) Pleading and Proof of - (B) Kanikkara tribe - Not governed by any uniform customary law in matters of inheritance - Person claiming to inherit a deceased fails to prove the family custom under which he claims that right - Held, a party has to plead in specific terms as to what is the custom that he is relying on and he must prove the custom pleaded by him - Held, son of the deceased would inherit the property of his deceased father under the general law of inheritance - Appeal Allowed.
