Kanu Ambu Vish v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 735; (1971) SCC (Cr) 211; (1971) 1 SCC 503; AIR 1971 SC 2256; 1971 CRLJ 1547

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - CrPC, 1898, s. 417 - (A) Evidence - (1) Statement in Panchnama - Use of , in evidence - For contradicting witness whose statement is contained in Panchanama - (2) Witness is confronted by his previous statement and is given opportunity to explain - (C) Criminal Procedure - In reversing an order of acquittal on review of evidence - Matters to be considered by High Court - Statement held, can be used in evidence only for contradicting witness whose statement is contained in Panchanama , but for so contradicting him, his attention must, before the writing can be so used be called to parts sought to be used for contradicting him - That part of statement which is put to him does not constitute substantive evidence - In reversing an order of acquittal on review of evidence HC should consider all matters on record including Trial Court's reasons for acquittal and particularly aspects favourable to accused and should not act on conjectures or inferences not arising on evidence - Appeal Allowed.
