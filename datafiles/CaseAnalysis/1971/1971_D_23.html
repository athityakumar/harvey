Debu Ghose and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 578; (1972) 3 SCC 294; (1972) SCC (Cr) 507; AIR 1972 SC 530; 1972 CRLJ 317

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, ss. 3, 8, 9, 10 and 11 - Habeas Corpus - Three petitioners detained under Act with orders of District Magistrate - Challenged - State Govt. did not pass confirmation order within stipulated time period of three months - Grounds disclosed were not vague and would be considered vague only if they did not contain sufficient particulars - Two grounds read together go to show that petitioner and his associates had indulged in manufacturing bombs with a view to facilitate breaking of wagons and terrorizing public, should resistance be offered - Held, public held at bay by petitioner and his associates with bombs, pipe guns, pistols, iron rods, etc and their acts prejudical to public safety - No substance in petitions - Petitions dismissed.
