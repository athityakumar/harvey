M. K. Balakrishnan Menon v Assistant Controller Of Estate Duty-Cum-Income Tax Officer, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 303; (1971) 2 SCC 909; AIR 1971 SC 2392; [1972] 83 ITR 162; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 961; 1972 UJ 470

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance, Coparcenary Interest

Summary: Estate Duty Act, 1953, ss. 5 and 7 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 7(3) - Land and Property - (A) Sthanam property - Death of sthanamdar - Property that attracts estate duty is the whole of the property - Effect of s. 7(3) of 1956 Act - (B) Constitution, Pre. - Unconstitutionality of legislation - Presumption as to constitutionally - Held, position is not affected by s. 7 (3) - The Court ought not to interpret statutory provisions, unless compelled by their language, in such a manner as would involve its constitutionality because the legislature is presumed to enact a law which does not contravene or violate the constitutional provisions - Appeal Dismissed.
