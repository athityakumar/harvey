 K. R. Deb v Collector Of Central Excise, Shillong

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 200; (1971) 2 SCC 102; AIR 1971 SC 1447; 1971 LabIC 945; 1971 (1) LLJ 427; [1971] S.C.R. 375; 1971 (1) SLR 29; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 375

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules 1957

Summary: Central Civil Services (Classification Control and Appeal) Rules, 1957 - Services Rules The r.15 is does not contemplate successive inquiries - Defect in inquiry - Whether fresh inquiry by other Officer can be directed - Held, if there is some defect in the inquiry conducted by the Inquiry Officer, the Disciplinary Authority can direct the inquiry Officer to conduct farther inquiries in respect of that matter but it cannot direct a fresh inquiry to he conducted by some other Officer - Appeal Allowed.
