State Of West Bengal v Administrator, Howrah Municipality & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 490; (1972) 1 SCC 366; AIR 1972 SC 749; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 874; 1972 UJ 449

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963 - (A) Construction of expression 'Sufficient cause' u/s. 5 of the Act - (B) 'Sufficient cause' for excusing delay in filing appeal - Perusal of different remedy than appeal on wrong advice by counsel - Held, expression cannot be construed too liberally merely because party in default is Govt. but it should receive a liberal construction so as to advance substantial justice when no negligence or inaction or want of bona fide is imputable to a party - Constitutes sufficient cause within s. 5 - Appeals allowed.
