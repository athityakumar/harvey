K. D. Kamath and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 453; (1971) 2 SCC 873; 1972 (1) CTR 124; [1971] 82 ITR 680; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 1034

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26A - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - A/y 1959-1960 - Partnership deed - Claim for grant of registration - Rejected - Challenged - Assessee/appellant (firm) had partners and partnership was constituted under document dt. 20-3-1959, for A/y 1959-1960, assessee filed an application to Income-tax Officer, for registration of partnership in name of X, who declined claim of assessee on ground that firm had been constituted was not genuine - Aggrieved, assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner, who confirmed order of Income-tax Officer - Assessee further appeal before Income-tax Appellate Tribunal, who held that deed does create relationship of partners inter-se between parties thereto and directed Income-tax Officer to register firm u/s. 26A of the Act - On respondent (Commissioner of Income-tax) request Tribunal referred question to HC which was answered against assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, respondents counsel placed considerable reliance as HC had also done, on earlier decision of Bombay HC in Umarbhai Chandbhai v. Commissioner of Income-tax, Bombay City, 1952 Indlaw MUM 6, but in SC opinion, reliance placed on that decision by both of them was misplaced and clause in present case were totally different - Hence, SC had already indicated that there was an agreement for sharing profits and losses and that even though vast powers of control and management had been given to managing partner, and business was being carried on by said managing partner, on behalf of all partners and those conditions fully satisfied requirements of definition of 'partnership' u/s. 4 of the 1932, Act - Therefore, to conclude this Court were of opinion that all ingredients of partnership were satisfied under partnership deed dt. 20-3-1959, and that view of HC that assessee could not be granted registration u/s. 26A of the Act for A/y 1959-1960, could not be sustained - Thus, in, result, SC answered question of law in affirmative in favour of assessee and this answer given by, SC to question referred to HC by Income-tax Appellate Tribunal would be substituted in place of that given by HC - SC reversed order of HC and allowed appeal - Appeal allowed.
