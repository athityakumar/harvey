Commissioner of Income Tax v Burlop Dealers Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 336; (1971) 1 SCC 462; AIR 1971 SC 1635; [1971] 79 ITR 609; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.34(1)(a) - Assessment - Notice - Reassessment - Evidence - Respondent company submitted profit and loss account of Rs. 1,75,875/ as profit in joint venture from H - Respondent claimed that Rs.87,937 earned was paid to R under partnership agreement - Assessee filed return in relevant year was not accepted and brought entire amount to tax - Income Tax Officer(IO) issued notice for assessment - IO re assessed income u/s.34(1)(a) and added Rs. 87,937 to income returned by assessee - Appeal made to Income Tax Appellate Tribunal favored respondent - Application u/s. 66(1) and petition u/s. 66(2) by appellant in HC was rejected - Hence instant appeal - Whether IO entitled to take action to re open assessment - Held, evidence and materials produced to Income tax Officer had reached conclusion that proceeding u/s. 34(1) (a) of Act will not lie on ground that Income tax Officer had raised inference - Assessee had disclosed his books of account and evidence from which material facts could be discovered - No obligation to inform IO about possible inferences which may be raised against him.
