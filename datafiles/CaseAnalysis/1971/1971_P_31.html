Premier Automobiles Limited and Another Etc v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 291; AIR 1972 SC 1690; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 526; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Supply and Prices of Goods Act, 1950, Tariff Commission Act, 1951

Summary: Trade - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Fixation of fair price - Escalation of cost - validity - Petitioner manufacturers of Fiat, Ambassador and Standard motor cars filed instant writ petition challenging fixation of fair price of said three passenger cars by respondents/UOI by the Order promulgated u/s. 18G of the Act - Held, production capacity of three car manufacturers per annum for purpose of working out ex-works cost was worked out by Court - However, cost and expenses on account of warranty and bonus were rightly included in return and could not be included in ex-works cost - In fixing cost for September 1969 which would be relevant only in case of Premier Automobiles same basis should have been adopted as for July 1970 - Thus, actual cost and not historical cost should have been taken into account but it was unnecessary to take projected and estimated cost for future - A provision should be made for an escalation clause and return which was allowed was adequate on facts proved before Commission - Depreciation on account of plant and machinery was allowed on correct basis but for purpose of allocation capacities indicated should be taken into account - Thus, amount payable on account of royalty per car in case of Standard Motors pursuant to collaboration agreement renewal of which was approved by Govt. would be included in ex-works cost for July 1970 - Further, conclusion of Commission relating to percentage of local steel sheets by Hindustan Motors was correct - Hence, dealers were entitled only to mark-up in terms of recommendation of Commission - Order accordingly.
