Babulal Hargovindas v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 814; (1971) SCC (Cr) 337; (1971) 1 SCC 767; (1977) SCC (Cr) 337; AIR 1971 SC 1277; 1971 CRLJ 1075; [1971] S.C.R. 53; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 53; 1971 UJ 500

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 10(7), 13(2), 13(1), 20(1), and 23(1)(e) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, r. 7(2) - Constitution, art. 134 - Evidence Act, 1872 , ss. 114 and 133 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, ss. 67 and 68 - (A) Constitution - Findings based on appreciation of evidence that the milk was bottled and sealed, signed and attested by a panch witness in the presence of accused - Challenged before Supreme Court - (B) Provisions of s. 10(7) of 1954 Act - Provison being statutary should be complied with by the Food Inspector - Evidence of the Food Inspector who is not an accomplice, that he had complied with the requirements of law by calling a panch witness and taking his signature - Admissibility - (C) Defence that the accused has been deprived of his right u/s. 13(2) - Rigt to send the sample in his custody to the Director, Central Food Laboratory - Due to delay in launching the prosecution - (D) A complaint filed by the Food Inspector with the written consent of the Medical Officer of Health who is authorised by a resolution of Corporation and not by the Municipal Commissioner - Compliance of s. 20(1), if - (E) Vires of provisions of r. 7 of 1955 Rules as regards to the Act - And r. 7(2) authorising the Public Analyst to get the sample analysed by his subordinate - Held, are findings of fact and cannot be challenged before SC - This however does not mean that the evidence cannot be accepted without corroboration especially when the panch has admitted his signature - Defence held, is not open when the accused has not filed an application u/s. 13 (2) during the trial and there is no evidence to show that no preservative was added to the sample - Is in accordance with s. 20 (1) - There is no inconsistency between r. 7 and s.13(1) of the Act, nor is the rule ultra vires the rule-making power u/s. 13(1)(e) , so r.7(2) authorising the Public Analyst to get the sample analysed by his subordinate is not, therefore, ultra vires the Act - Appeal Dismissed.
