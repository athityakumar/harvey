Member, Board of Revenue, West Bengal v Messrs Phelps and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 838; (1972) 4 SCC 121; 1972 (1) CTR 243; 1972 (29) STC 101; 1972 UJ 141

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 5(2), 5(2)(a)(ii) and 8(b) - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 21(3) - Assessee carrying business of tailors, drapers, out-fitters and industrial 'gloves' manufacturers - Claim for exemption u/s. 5(2)(a)(ii) of Act - Whether assessee company is entitled to exemption asked for by it? - Meaning of expression 'for use by him in manufacture of goods for sale' in s. 5(2) - Identical words are used in s. 8(b) of Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Expression in manufacture of goods' would normally encompass entire process carried on by dealer of converting raw materials into finished goods - Where any particular process is so integrally connected with ultimate production of goods but for that process, manufacture or processing of goods would be commercially inexpedient, goods required in that process would, fall within expression 'in manufacture of goods' - Held, in present case, assessee company has sold goods in question to certain manufacturers who were manufacturing iron steel materials - Those gloves were to be used by workmen who were engaged in hot jobs or in handling corrosive substance in course of manufacture - That being so it cannot be denied that those gloves had to be used in course of manufacture - High Court decision is correct - Appeal dismissed.
