Baljeet Singh and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1445; (2004) 3 SCC 122; (2004) SCC (Cr) 692; AIR 2004 SC 1714; JT 2004 (3) SC 93; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 52; 2004(2) SCALE 632

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 306, 498A and 201 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Can courts draw a presumption u/s. 113B of Evidence Act and shift onus of proof on accused without prosecution having proved basic requirements under said section? - Held, no - Courts below have not found guilt of appellant on oral evidence produced by prosecution but same is based primarily on a presumption drawn u/s. 113-B of Evidence Act which Court has held to be impermissible in law in view of prosecution's failure to prove basic facts which was a condition precedent to drawing of such a presumption - Ramesh Kumar v State of Chhattisgarh (2001 Indlaw SC 115) wherein this court held that before presumption may be raised, foundation thereof must exist - A bare reading of s. 113-A shows that to attract applicability of s. 113-A, it must be shown that (i) woman has committed suicide, (ii) such suicide has been committed within a period of seven years from date of her marriage, (iii) husband or his relatives, who are charged had subjected her to cruelty - On existence and availability of above said circumstances, court may presume that such suicide had been abetted by her husband or by such relatives of her husband, relied on - Above case, deals with s. 113-A of Evidence Act - However, principle laid down therein squarely applies to cases involving s. 113-B of Act also in so far as they relate to proof of facts enumerated in section before a presumption is drawn - Appeal allowed.
