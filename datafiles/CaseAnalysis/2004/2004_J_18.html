Chandi Prasad and Others v Jagdish Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 790; (2004) 8 SCC 724; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 75; 2004 (58) ALR 129; 2004 (4) AWC 3208; 2010 (6) CompLJ 452; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 141; JT 2004 (8) SC 255; 2004 (3) KLT 654; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 141; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 737; 2005 (98) RD 267; 2004(8) SCALE 446; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 942; 2005 (1) UC 271; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 972

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 136 - What would be date from which a decree becomes enforceable for execution thereof within meaning of art. 136? - Held, when an Appellate Court passes a decree, decree of trial court merges with decree of Appellate Court decree of Appellate Court supersedes decree of trial court - When an appeal is dismissed on ground of delay no decree is passed and doctrine of merger shall not apply - No interference in judgment - Appeal dismissed.
