Delhi Transport Corporation v Sardar Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 590; (2004) 7 SCC 574; (2004) SCC (L&S) 946; AIR 2004 SC 4161; 2004 (3) AWC 2685; 2004 (20) CLR 289; 2004 (113) DLT 258; 2004 (102) FLR 1031; JT 2004 (6) SC 342; 2004 (3) LLJ 543; 2004 (4) LLN 1; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 40; 2004(6) SCALE 613; 2004 (7) SLR 2; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 494; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2568

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, 1952

Summary: Service - Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, 1952 - Service - Departmental proceedings - Ground of misconduct due to unauthorized long absence from duty; negligence of duties and lack of interest - Removal from service - Tribunal not approved order of dismissal - Whether absence without obtaining leave in advance can be treated as misconduct? - Held, conduct of employees is nothing but irresponsible in extreme - Burden is on employee that there was no negligence and/or lack of interest to establish it by placing relevant materials - Tribunal not justified in refusing to accord approval to order of dismissal - Appeals Disposed Of.
