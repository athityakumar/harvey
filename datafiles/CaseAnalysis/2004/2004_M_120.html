M/s. British Motor Car Co. v Madan Lal Saggi (D) and another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1005; (2005) 1 SCC 8; AIR 2005 SC 240; 2005 (58) ALR 354; JT 2005 (6) SC 470; 2005 (139) PLR 293; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 13; 2004 (2) RCR(Rent) 693; 2004(9) SCALE 568; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 203

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 13, 13(2)(iii) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Eviction order against petitioner - Whether there is any error in eviction order? - Held, appellate court and HC have re-appreciated evidence on record and affirmed findings of trial court - There is no scope for further re-appreciation of evidence as that is not function of this Court under art.136 of Constitution - Three courts correctly applied true test and on facts found that sheds constructed by petitioner-tenant amounted to an act materially impairing value and utility of premises and land leased out to him so as to amount to a ground of eviction u/s. 13(2)(iii).
