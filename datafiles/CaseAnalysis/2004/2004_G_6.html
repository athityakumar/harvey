Pukhraj D. Jain & Ors. v G. Gopalakrishna

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 290; (2004) 7 SCC 251; AIR 2004 SC 3504; 2004 (56) ALR 275; 2004 (3) AWC 2214; 2005 (1) CGLJ 304; JT 2004 (5) SC 329; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 183; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 171; 2004(4) SCALE 688; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 10 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(C) - Whether application u/s. 10 should consider at first instance before deciding issues - Mere filing of an application u/s. 10 CPC does not in any manner put an embargo on power of court to examine merits of matter - Court can decide what will be best course to be adopted for expeditious disposal of case - Respondent no 1 was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract and in view of mandate of section 16 of Specific Relief Act no decree for specific performance could be passed in his favour - Decree dismissing suit passed by trial court affirmed.
