P. Suryanarayana (Dead) By Lrs v K. S. Muddugowramma

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1144; (2004) 3 SCC 589; AIR 2004 SC 1930; 2004 (56) ALR 153; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 15; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 395; 2004(3) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, ss. 21(1)(h) and 21(4) - Karnataka Rent Act, 1999, ss. 70(2)(b) and 31 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Bonafide requirement - Comparative hardship - Landlady has sought for eviction of tenant on ground of bona fide requirement of suit premises for her own occupation u/s. 21(1)(h) of 1961 Act - Landlady is a widow - Rent Controller and Appellate Court found requirement of landlady not made out - Revision petition in High Court u/s. 115 of CPC - During pendency of civil revision, Karnataka Rent Act, 1999 came into force - HC applied 1999 Act and allowed revision - Tenant appealed - (A) Question regarding applicability of 1999 Act - Held, s. 70 of 1999 Act repeals 1961 Act - s. 17(2)(b) provides that all cases, proceedings, other than those referred to in cl. (a), pending at commencement of 1999 Act in respect of premises to which 1999 Act is applicable, shall be continued, disposed of in accordance with provisions of 1999 Act - Scheduled premises are those to which 1999 Act is applicable - Applicability of s. 70(2)(b) of 1999 Act is clearly attracted - (B) Question relating to comparative hardship - Further held, concept of comparative hardship has been given up and instead s. 27(3) now provides that court may, on a ground for eviction u/s. 27(2)(r) having been made out, allow eviction from only a part of premises if landlord is agreeable to same - Also s. 31 of 1999 Act provides that if landlady be a widow and tenanted premises are required for use by her she may apply to court for recovery of immediate possession of such premises - No fault can be found with view taken by HC - Appeal dismissed.
