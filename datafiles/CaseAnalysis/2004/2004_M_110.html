Collector of Central Excise, Patna v M/s. Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1593; (2004) 9 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 3202; 2004 (165) E.L.T. 386; JT 2004 (3) SC 66; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 52; 2004 (61) RLT 239; 2004(2) SCALE 760; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Finance Act, 1981

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9(2) and 174 - Penalty - Assessee declared zinc dross and flux skimming as by-product arises during galvanisation of steel sheets and sold them to various customers without making any declaration in the classification list without paying any duty on clearance of the said product and without maintaining any records prescribed under the Central Excise Rules, 1944 - A show-cause notice was issued to the assessee to show cause why a penalty should not be imposed on them under the provisions of the Rules, 1944 and why the duty be not demanded u/r. 9(2) of the Rules, 1944 - Assessee contended that that they do not manufacture zinc and article thereof but they do galvanize sheets falling under chapter 72 of Central Excise Tariff - Assessee also contended that since zinc dross and flux skimming have already been held to be non- excisable items, the issue of gate passes for removal of products and submission of quarterly returns, etc. and filing of classification list does not arise - Whether zinc dross and flux skimming arising during galvanisation of steel sheets were goods within the meaning of the Central Excise Act, 1944 and were liable to Central excise duty? - Held, dross and skimming were merely the refuse, scum or rubbish throughout in the process of manufacture of aluminium sheets and, therefore, cannot be said to be the result of treatment, labour or manipulation whereby a new and different article emerges with a distinctive name, character or use which can ordinarily come to the market to be bought and sold - Merely because such refuse or scum may fetch some price in the market does not justify it being called a by-product, much less an end product or a finished product - Moreover, onus to show that particular goods on which excise duty was sought to be levied have gone through the process of manufacture in India was on the Revenue and that the Revenue have done nothing to discharge this onus - Hence, penalty could not be imposed on the assessee - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
