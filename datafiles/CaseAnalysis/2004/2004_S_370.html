Mehboob Dawood Shaikh v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 49; (2004) 2 SCC 362; (2004) SCC (Cr) 551; AIR 2004 SC 2890; AIR 2004 SCW 527; 2004 (1) CCR 319; 2004 (1) Crimes 344; 2004 CRLJ 1359; 2004 (1) JCC 286; 2004 (2) KLT 812; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 343; 2004(1) SCALE 418; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 554; 2004 (2) SLT 223; 2004 (1) Supreme 526; 2004 (1) UC 602

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Cancellation of bail - Person to whom bail has been granted either tries to interfere with course of justice or attempts to tamper with evidence or witnesses or threatens witnesses or indulges in similar activities which would hamper smooth investigation or trial - Bail granted can be cancelled - Rejection and cancellation - Not on same footing - Learned Single Judge has given cogent reasons for passing order of cancellation of bail - Appeal dismissed - Any fresh application for bail - To be dealt with in accordance with law.
