T. Shankar Prasad v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 9; (2004) 3 SCC 753; (2004) SCC (Cr) 870; AIR 2004 SC 1242; 2004 CRLJ 884; 2004 (164) E.L.T. 143; JT 2004 (1) SC 188; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 784B; 2004(1) SCALE 318; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss.7, 11, 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Way bills - Bribe - Convicted by Trial Court and upheld by HC - Held, in case of an offence of demanding and accepting illegal gratification, depending on the circumstances of the case, the Court may feel safe in accepting the prosecution version on the basis of the oral evidence of the complainant and the official witnesses even if the trap witnesses turn hostile or are found not to be independent - Further held, when there is circumstantial evidence which is consistent with the guilt of the accused and not consistent with his innocence, there should be no difficulty in upholding the conviction - Appeals Dismissed.
