Godfrey Philips India Limited v Girnar Food and Beverages Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1479; (2004) 5 SCC 257; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 334; 2005 (30) PTC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Appellant alleging passing on of its trade mark 'SUPER CUP' - Respondent, also in tea business, had a wrongfully used words 'SUPER CUP' in connection with sale of its tea - Whether conclusion arrived at by Division Bench that trade mark is descriptive is correct or not? - Held, that enunciation of principle of law with regard to protection available even in respect of descriptive trade mark was wrong - A descriptive trade mark may be entitled to protection if it has assumed a secondary meaning which identifies it with a particular product or as being from a particular source - Matter remanded back to Division Bench of High Court - Appeal disposed of.
