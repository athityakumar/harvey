Bal Thackrey v Harish Pimpalkhute and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1024; (2005) 1 SCC 254; (2005) SCC (Cr) 307; AIR 2005 SC 396; 2005 CRLJ 659; 2005 (3) G.L.R. 2447; JT 2004 (10) SC 244; 2005 (1) KLT 67; 2005 (1) MahLJ 322; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 288; 2004(10) SCALE 37; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 477; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 707

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 129, 215 - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971, s. 15 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952 - Contempt Proceedings - Whether contempt proceedings were initiated against appellant suo motu by court or by respondents - Held, requirement of obtaining consent in writing of Advocate-General for making motion by any person is mandatory - A motion u/s. 15 not in conformity with requirements is not maintainable - Perusal of record including notices issued to appellant shows that Court had not taken suo motu action against appellant - In absence of compliance of mandatory requirement of s.15, petitions were not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
