Zahira Habibullah Sheikh And Anr. Etc. v State Of Gujarat And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 436; (2004) 5 SCC 353; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1613; AIR 2004 SC 3467; 2004 (3) G.L.R. 2058; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 143; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 347; 2004(5) SCALE 397; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 571; 2004 (2) UC 963B

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 311, 391, 406 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 137, 142, 32 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, o. 40 r. 3 - Whether direction for fresh trial outside State is permissible in law without specific prayer in that regard and without petition filed u/s. 406 of CrPC - Held, every prayer need not always be by a separate application, unless such prayer is only relief sought or that proceedings filed had no other claim, by way of relief - If basis of grievance has been sufficiently disclosed openly and relief sought is one among others specified as incidental or ancillary to main relief and Court had power to grant it - When primary consideration was justifiability of rejecting applications in terms of ss 311 and 391 of Code, question of considering evidence on record did not arise.
