Mohd. Ahmed v Nizam Sugar Factory and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1527; (2004) 11 SCC 210; 2005(5) SCALE 299

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Promotion - Arrears of salary - Payment of difference - Legality - Promotion to respondent No. 2 was challenged by appellant - Petition filed by appellant was allowed by Single Judge and directed respondent No. 1 to consider the claim of appellant - Aggrieved respondent No. 1 filed appeal which DB in passing the impugned order, totally ignored to take note of the claim of the appellant so much so, in the operative part of the order, no such observation has been made allowing or refusing any benefit to the appellant or upsetting the direction given by the Single Judge for payment of arrears of salary - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, DB while disposing of the appeal, nothing was said about the appellant, though the same principle was equally rather more applicable to him as appellant was even senior to respondent No. 2 but she alone was given the benefit - Appellant was very much in service when the promotion was given to LR on 25-6-1992 - Appellant continued in service and retired only on 31-5-1995 - Single Judge had rendered the judgment on 27-10-1995 giving the benefit of arrears of difference of pay to appellant which, SC's view, could not be faulted with - Direction was issued by Single Judge and on the reasoning adopted by DB for making the benefit available to respondent No. 2, there would be no reason to deny the said benefit to appellant - At this stage, it would also be pertinent to mention that respondent sugar factory had filed the SLP against the judgment of the DB which was dismissed - As per the direction given by Single Judge, appellant should be paid difference of salary by respondent No. 1 at the earliest - Impugned judgment and order passed by DB stands modified - Appeal allowed.
