Ram Phal Kundu v Kamal Sharma

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 56; (2004) 2 SCC 759; AIR 2004 SC 1657; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 336; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 785; 2004(2) SCALE 49; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 977; 2004 (2) SLT 301; 2004 (1) Supreme 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Election

Summary: Election - Conduct of Election Rules - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - s. 116A - Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 - Candidature - Candidate of a political party - Candidate set up by a policital party - Who shall be deemed to have been so set up by a particular political party? - It has to be determined strictly in accordance with paras 13 and 13A of the Symbols Order - Extrinsic evidence cannot be looked into unless it is pleaded that the signature of the authorised person on Form B had been obtained from him under threat or by playing fraud upon him - Public interest demands that there should be no vagueness or uncertainty regarding the candidature of a person seeking to contest the election as a candidate of a recognised political party - Exercise should be done strictly in accordance with rules - Held on facts - Election petitioner has to fully establish his case - Sufficient evidence not produced - Petition will fail - No fresh election can be ordered - Appeal allowed.
