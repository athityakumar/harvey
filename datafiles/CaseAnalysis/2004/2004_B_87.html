India Umbrella Manufacturing Company and Others v Bhagabandei Agarwalla (Dead) By Lrs. Smt. Savitri Agarwalla

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1433; (2004) 3 SCC 178; AIR 2004 SC 1321; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 12; 2004 (55) ALR 98; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 936; 2004 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; 2004 (2) CHN 96; JT 2004 (1) SC 200; 2004 (137) PLR 251; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 686; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 154; 2004(1) SCALE 53; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 10; 2004 (1) UC 440

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Eviction proceedings - One of co-owners files suit for eviction of tenants in property generally owned by co-owners - Doctrine of agency - Held, one co-owner filing a suit for eviction against tenant does so on his own behalf in his own right and as an agent of other co-owners - Consent of other co-owners is assumed as taken unless it is shown that other co-owners were not agreeable to eject tenant and suit was filed in spite of their disagreement - Suit once filed, rights of parties stand crystallized on date of suit and entitlement of co-owners to seek ejectment must be adjudged by reference to date of institution of suit - Purchasers buying only a part of suit property - Doctrine of merger is not applicable - Appeals dismissed.
