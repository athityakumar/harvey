S. Mariyappa (Dead) By Lrs. and others v Siddappa and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1776; (2005) 10 SCC 235

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 90(1), O.21 r.64, O.21 r.17(4), s. 47 - Decretal debt - Sufficiency - Objections filed by the appellants, u/s. 47 of CPC were dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sale of only part of property would be sufficient to meet decretal debt of approximately Rs. 8000 - Held, u/s. 47 all questions relating to execution, discharge or satisfaction of decree should be determined by executing court alone - Post-sale illegalities or irregularities causing substantial injury to judgment-debtor were covered under O. 21 r. 90 - O. 21 r. 90(1) thereof covered field of material irregularities or fraud in publicity or conducting sale - O. 21 r. 90(2) enjoined proof thereof and court should find that by reason thereof applicant sustained substantial injury - Total absence of drawing up of proclamation of sale and settlement of its term by judicial application of mind rendered sale nullity being void - Sale of 550 sq yards for recovery of paltry sum of Rs 7780.33, without selling portion thereof, caused substantial injury to the appellant - As executing court did not observe its statutory duty, sale was set aside - Purchaser should be returned his monies and property be handed back to appellants - Appeal disposed of.
