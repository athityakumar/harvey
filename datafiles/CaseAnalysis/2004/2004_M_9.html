Principal Secretary, Government of Andhra Pradesh v M. Adinarayana

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 836; (2004) 12 SCC 579; AIR 2004 SC 4870; 2005 (1) CLR 134; JT 2004 (8) SC 562; 2004 (4) LLN 731; 2004(8) SCALE 511; 2004 (6) SLR 432; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 115; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 725

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Act, 1960, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) (Amendment) Act, 1993, Tribunal for Disciplinary Proceedings Rules, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Rules, 1989, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Rules, 1991, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services Rules, 1960, Andhra Pradesh Civil Service (Classification, Conduct) and Appeal) Rules, 1991

Summary: Service - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(e) - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services Rules, 1960 - Possession of disproportionate assets - Disciplinary Proceedings - Whether Tribunal for disciplinary proceedings has jurisdiction to enquire into charges with regard to the disproportionate assets possessed by respondent under s. 13(2) r/w s 13(1)(e) of Act? - Held, a grave misconduct does not cease to be a misconduct because it is grave - Tribunal for disciplinary proceedings is competent to examine the case - Order of Administrative Tribunal interfering with well-considered order of TDP is unwarranted - Appeal allowed.
