Commissioner of Central Excise, Jaipur v J.K. Udaipur Udyog Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 741; (2004) 7 SCC 344; AIR 2004 SC 4437; 2004 (96) ECC 73; 2004 (171) E.L.T. 289; JT 2004 (7) SC 152; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 306; 2004 (64) RLT 219; 2004(7) SCALE 414; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 39

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11-A - Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57 AA, 57 AB, 57 AC, 57 AH, 57 J and 173(q)(i)(bb) - Whether explosives used in mines for blasting purpose can be held to be 'inputs' so as to qualify for taking CENVAT Credit u/r. 57 AB? - Provision shows that a manufacturer or producer shall be allowed to take CENVAT credit paid on any inputs used in manufacture of intermediate products by a job-worker availing benefit of exemption contained in notification No 214/86, D/- 25-03-1986 and received by manufacturer for use in or in relation to manufacture of final products - A manufacturer or producer himself cannot be a job worker - Here mines are operated by assessee itself - Therefore, in terms of amended r. 57 AB also, assessee cannot take CENVAT Credit for explosives used for blasting purposes in mining area - Appeal allowed.
