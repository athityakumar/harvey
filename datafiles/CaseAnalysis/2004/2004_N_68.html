Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs, Vadodara v Narmada Chematur Pharmaceuticals Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1425; (2005) 10 SCC 123; 2005 (179) E.L.T. 276; 2005 (66) RLT 342

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Whether appellant had wrongly availed of Modvat credit and was liable to pay sum amounting to Rs. 6,10,580/-? - Held, it is stated by learned counsel appearing on behalf of assessee that excise duty paid and Modvat credit availed of were identical - Therefore consequences of payment of excise duty after availing of Modvat credit was revenue neutral - Appeals are accordingly dismissed - If upon verification submission of respondent is found to be incorrect/liberty is granted to appellant to mention these matters before this court - Further held, In C A nos. 2593-2597/2001, in view of fact that admittedly according to department there was no revenue implication, appeals are dismissed leaving question of law open - Further held, In C A no., 4003/2002 - It is stated by learned counsel that excise duty paid by not availing of exemption was exact amount and that therefore action of assessee was revenue neutral appeal is accordingly dismissed - Further held, In C A nos. 5485-86/2003 :it is stated by learned counsel that excise duty paid by not availing of exemption was exact amount and that therefore action of assess was revenue neutral - Appeals dismissed.
