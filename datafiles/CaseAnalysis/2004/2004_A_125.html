Reema Aggarwal v Anupam And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 39; (2004) 3 SCC 199; (2004) SCC (Cr) 699; AIR 2004 SC 1418; 2004 (3) CalLT(SC) 16; 2004 (1) CCR 163; 2004 (1) Crimes 276; 2004 (2) CRJ 432; 2004 CRLJ 892; 2004 (1) JCC 209; JT 2004 (1) SC 177; 2004 (2) KLT 822; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 776; 2004(1) SCALE 264; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 378; 2004 (1) Supreme 355; 2004 (1) UC 489

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Family & Personal - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 307, 494, 498A - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 125 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2, 4, 5 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry Prohibition Act - Socio-economic legislation - To curb menace of dowry - Demands even during second marriage - Constitute offence - Ingredients of offences of matrimonial cruelty and bigamy - Distinguished - Matrimonial cruelty - Does not require a strictly and formally valid legal marriage - Mischief rule applied - Matter remanded to HC.
