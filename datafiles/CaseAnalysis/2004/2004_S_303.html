Hasanbhai Valibhai Qureshi v State Of Gujarat And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 429; (2004) 5 SCC 347; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1603; AIR 2004 SC 2078; 2004 (2) Crimes 145; 2004 CRLJ 2018; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1634; 2004 (2) JCC 803; JT 2004 (4) SC 305; 2004 (3) MahLJ 1074; 2004 (3) MPLJ 593; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 463; 2004(4) SCALE 174; 2004 (3) SLT 33; 2004 (3) Supreme 71; 2004 (2) UC 926

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 173(8), 216, 217, 228, 240, 395 - Bombay Police Act, s. 135 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Issuance of appropriate writ for re-investigation by an independent agency - Initially, in FIR offences including s.395, 120 B of IPC, s.135 of Bombay Police Act were mentioned by police officials, after a few hours of registration of FIR ss 395, 120 B deleted by prosecuting agency - Report submitted by Director General of Police, it has been accepted that deletion of s 120 B IPC does not appear to be proper - There is necessity for further investigation same can certainly be done.
