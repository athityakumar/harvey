Kirti Kumar Nirula v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 820; (2005) SCC (Cr) 17; (2005) 9 SCC 65; AIR 2004 SC 4923; JT 2004 (8) SC 48; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 353; 2004(7) SCALE 727

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - COFEPOSA, 1974, s. 3(1) - Detention order - Challenged - Ground, that order of detention being based on a single incident, detention order of the co-detenu being quashed by HC and there was inordinate and unexplained delay in considering representation - Held, material placed before detaining authority coupled with fact of arrest and seizure of foreign currency which is handed over by detenu to one TRS are sufficient to form a reasonable conclusion that detenu is likely to indulge in similar activities in future also - Undated and unsigned representation cannot be treated as a representation within meaning of art. 22(5) of Constitution requiring immediate attention of authorities concerned - Appeal dismissed.
