R. Vishwanatha Pillai, Vimal Ghosh V. v State Of Kerala & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 45; (2004) 2 SCC 105; (2004) SCC (L&S) 350; AIR 2004 SC 1469; 2004 (5) AWC 4025; 2004 (2) CalLT(SC) 17; 2004 (1) ESC 152; 2004 (100) FLR 673; JT 2004 (1) SC 88; 2004 (1) KLT 708; 2004 (1) LLN 323; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 19; 2004(1) SCALE 285; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 360; 2004 (1) SLR 744; 2004 (2) SLT 51; 2004 (1) Supreme 436; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 507

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Education; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950 - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3(1) - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1969, rr. 6, 7(1) - Fraudulent Caste Certificates - Appointment - Person who entered service by producing a false caste certificate and obtained appointment for post meant for Scheduled Caste - Cannot claim to be in a post and claim protection of a 311 - Equity jurisdiction cannot be exercised - No purpose would be served in withholding declaration of result - In terms of orders passed by Constitution Bench of this Court in State of Maharashtra v Milind And Others - Result be declared - Not be treated as a Scheduled Caste candidate in future either in obtaining service or for any other benefits - Caste certificate ordered cancelled - Henceforth to be treated as general category for all purposes.
