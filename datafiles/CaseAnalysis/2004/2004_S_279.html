Tata Consultancy Services v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 943; (2005) 1 SCC 308; AIR 2005 SC 371; 2005 (1) CompLJ 25; 2004 (192) CTR 257; 2004 (178) E.L.T. 22; [2004] 271 ITR 401; JT 2004 (10) SC 64; 2006 (33) PTC 652; 2004 (65) RLT 327; 2004(9) SCALE 349; 2004 (137) STC 620; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Telephone, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Finance Act, 1975, Finance Act, 1976, Processing, Sale of Goods Act, 1979, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1959, Sale of Goods, Act, 1930, Customs Tariff Act, 1997, Sale of Goods Act, 1923, Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax, 1957, ss. 2(h), 2(n) and 5 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Whether canned software sold by appellants can be termed to be 'goods' and as such assessable to sales tax? - Held, no error in HC holding that branded software is goods - Software is capable of being abstracted, consumed and use - Software can be transmitted, transferred, delivered, stored, possessed etc. - No infirmity in Judgment of authorities below or in impugned Judgment - Appeal dismissed.
