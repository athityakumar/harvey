Pramod Kumar Srivastava v Chairman, Bihar Public Service Commission Patna

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 580; (2004) 6 SCC 714; (2004) SCC (L&S) 883; AIR 2004 SC 4116; 2004 (3) AWC 2703; 2004 (20) CLR 300; JT 2004 (6) SC 380; 2004 (5) SLR 457; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 372; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2581

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Employment-Competitive examination-Evaluation of answer books-Appellant appearing in Judicial Services (Competitive) Examination conducted by Bihar Public Service Commission-Securing 35 marks in General Science paper-Commission finding no mistake on scrutiny of marks-Single Judge of High Court getting answer book examined by two teachers of Science College who awarded 63 marks-And directing Commission to reconsider case of appellant after treating his marks in General Science paper as 63-Division Bench setting aside judgment of single Judge-Whether justified?-Held, “yes”-No provision in Commission Rules for re-evaluation of answer books-Manner in which single Judge got answer book re-evaluated not justified-No merit in appeal.
