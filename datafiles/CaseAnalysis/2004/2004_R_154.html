State Through S.P., New Delhi v Ratan Lal Arora

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 336; (2004) 4 SCC 590; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1353; AIR 2004 SC 2364; 2005 (1) CalLT(SC) 24; 2004 CRLJ 2105; 2004 (111) DLT 250; JT 2004 (5) SC 352; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2004(5) SCALE 67; 2004 (3) SLT 244; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 631; 2004 (3) Supreme 439

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Old Act, Defence of India Act, 1962, Reformatory Schools Act, 1897, Sea Customs Act 1878

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 40, 7 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 18, 360, 562 - Probation Of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 18 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 8 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Imposition of a lesser than minimum sentence - Held, HC committed grave error of law in extending benefit of probation even under CrPC - Though reasons assigned by HC to extend benefits of probation may not be relevant, proper or special reasons for going below minimum sentence prescribed is wholly impermissible - Confined sentence of imprisonment to minimum of six months u/s. 7 and minimum of one year u/s. 13(2) of Act.
