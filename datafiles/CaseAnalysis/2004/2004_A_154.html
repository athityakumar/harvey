State of West Bengal v Ashish Kumar Roy and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1029; (2005) 10 SCC 110; AIR 2005 SC 254; JT 2004 (10) SC 217; 2004(10) SCALE 202; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 665

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 1981

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 141, 226, 227, 323A, 323B, 323B(2)(d) - West Bengal Land Reforms and Tenancy Tribunal Act, 1997, ss. 28, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 56 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Acquisition and Regulation) Act, 1981 - West Bengal Land Reforms Act, 1955 - Tribunal constituted under Act - Jurisdiction power and authority of Tribunal - Whether Act ultra vires Constitution of India? - Held, as long as it is a law with respect to 'land reforms', it is sufficient to fall within ambit of sub- Cl (d) of cl (2) of art.323 B of Constitution - Tribunal constituted to deal with disputes arising under specified Acts was very much a tribunal within meaning of art.323 B - Judgment of HC is set aside.
