Godawat Pan Masala Products I.P. Limited and v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 547; (2004) 7 SCC 68; AIR 2004 SC 4057; 2005 (1) Bom.C.R. 194; JT 2004 (6) SC 179; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 67; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 930; 2004(6) SCALE 388; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 239; 2004 (2) UC 1148

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug

Keywords: Manufacture For Sale, Tobacco Board Act, 1975, Injurious To Health, Cigarettes And Other Tobacco Products (Prohibition Of Advertisement And Regulation Of Trade And Commerce, Production, Supply And Distribution) Act, 2003, Central Sales Tax. Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957, Food and Drugs Act, 1955, Goa, Daman and Diu Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1982, Tamil Nadu Scheduled Articles (Prescription of Standards) Order, 1977

Summary: Health and Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Validity of notifications issued by the Food (Health) Authority u/s.7(iv) of Act by which manufacture, sale, storage and distribution of pan masala and gutka (pan masala containing tobacco) were banned for different periods - Notification whether valid? Held, scheme of Act suggests that a decision to ban an article injurious to health, when used as food or as an ingredient in manufacture of any article of food, can only be result of broader policy - This larger power appears to have been located only in Central Govt.under s 23(1A)(f) and not in State Food (Health) Authority - Impugned notification is certainly an administrative act and not a legislative act - Notification is illegal and unconstitutional - Natural justice also violated - Quash notifications - Appeals allowed.
