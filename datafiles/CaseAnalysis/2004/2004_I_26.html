Janki Vashdeo Bhojwani and Another v Indusind Bank Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1030; (2005) 2 SCC 217; AIR 2005 SC 439; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 43; 2005 (58) ALR 440; 2005 (1) AWC 138; 2005 (1) BC 399; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 846; 2004 (2) CLR 151; [2004] 123 Comp Cas 154; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 154; 2005 (1) DRTC 281; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 175; JT 2004 (10) SC 264; 2005 (2) KLT 265; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 532; 2004 (2) LCD 968; 2005 (1) MahLJ 1170; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 109; 2005 (140) PLR 1; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 240; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 570; 2005 (98) RD 371; 2004(10) SCALE 244; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 681; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 421

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 3, rr. 1, 2 - Attachment of property - Matter remitted to Tribunal with some directions - Whether Tribunal acts in accordance with directions? - Held, question whether appellants have any independent source of income and have contributed towards purchase of property from their own independent income can be only answered by appellants themselves and not by a mere holder of power of attorney from them - Having regard to directions in order of remand by which this Court placed burden of proving on appellants that they have a share in property, it was obligatory on part of appellants to have entered box and discharged burden - Appellants failed to establish that on date decrees were passed, appellants were co-owners of property.
