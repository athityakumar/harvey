Ramrao and others v All India Backward Class Bank Employees Welfare

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 38; (2004) 2 SCC 76; (2004) SCC (L&S) 337; AIR 2004 SC 1459; 2004 (2) AWC 1580; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 162; 2004 (100) FLR 807; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 156; JT 2004 (1) SC 331; 2004 (1) LLJ 1061; 2004 (2) LLN 799; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 66; 2004(1) SCALE 149; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 19; 2004 (3) SLR 338; 2004 (2) SLT 566; 2004 (1) Supreme 207; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 945

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Nabard, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment & Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1981

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment & Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1981, r. 10(1)(b) - Promotees not impleaded - Necessary parties - HC could not have issued impugned directions - No directions in present appeals can be issued to Bank to change its policy decision to accommodate Scheduled Tribe candidates - Bank, Sponsor Bank as also NABARD to take appropriate decision in matter.
