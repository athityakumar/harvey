State Of Andhra Pradesh v Golconda Linga Swamy And Anr

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 512; (2004) 6 SCC 522; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1805; AIR 2004 SC 3967; 2004 CRLJ 3845; JT 2004 (6) SC 34; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 831; 2004(6) SCALE 281; 2004(6) SCALE 289; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 147; 2004 (2) UC 1087

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Excise - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968 - Andhra Pradesh Prohibition Act, 1995 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 155(2), 156(1), 173, 482 - HC quashed FIR filed by Prohibition and Excise officers on ground that there was no material to show that seized articles were intended to be used for manufacturing of illicit distilled liquor - Held, there was either statements of witnesses or seizure of illicit distilled liquor which factors cannot be said to be without relevance - Whether material already in existence or to be collected during investigation would be sufficient for holding concerned accused persons guilty has to be considered at time of trial - In some cases FIR did not disclose commission of an offence without anything being added or subtracted from recitals therein - Judgment of HC confirmed in some cases and set aside in some other cases.
