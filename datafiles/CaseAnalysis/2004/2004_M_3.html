Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai-Ii v Allied Photographics India Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 303; (2004) 4 SCC 34; AIR 2004 SC 2953; 2004 (92) ECC 777; 2004 (113) ECR 573; 2004 (166) E.L.T. 3; JT 2004 (4) SC 105; 2004 (61) RLT 602; 2004(3) SCALE 447

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Refund Of Duty, Central Excises And Customs Laws (Amendment) Act, 1991

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 , ss. 3, 4(a) and 11B - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9B - Whether a claim for refund after final assessment is governed by s. 11B of the Central Excise Act 1944? - There is a basic difference between duty paid under protest and duty paid u/r. 9B - The duty paid under protest falls u/s. 11B whereas duty paid under provisional assessment falls u/r. 9B - It is highly improbable for a distributor to incur cost of purchase which included 20% element of duty in addition to the purchase price without passing on the burden to its dealers - Held, no merit in the contention of the respondent that this Court should not interfere u/art. 136 of the Constitution, 1950 in view of the concurrent findings of fact - Appeal allowed.
