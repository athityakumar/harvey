National Insurance Company Limited v Chella Bharathamma and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 755; (2004) 8 SCC 517; 2004 (3) ACC 292; AIR 2004 SC 4882; 2004 (57) ALR 284; 2004 (4) AWC 3369; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 327; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 41; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 81; JT 2004 (7) SC 519; 2004 (3) KLT 454; 2005 (139) PLR 102; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 399; 2004(8) SCALE 90; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 587; 2004 (2) UC 1369

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 66, 166 and 149 - Whether insurer to be liable for indemnifying award of compensation? - Held, a person without permit to ply a vehicle cannot be placed at a better pedestal vis-a-vis one who has a permit, but has violated any condition thereof - Plying of a vehicle without a permit is an infraction - Therefore, defence is available to insurer on that aspect - HC not justified in holding insurer liable - Appeals Disposed Of.
