State of Punjab and Others v Balbir Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 887; (2004) 11 SCC 743; AIR 2004 SC 4655; 2004 (3) CLR 748; 2004 (103) FLR 343; JT 2004 (7) SC 383; 2004(7) SCALE 616; 2004 (6) SLR 1; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 368; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2868

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Punjab Police Rules, 1934, r. 12.21 - Discharge from service - Ground, inefficiency and unfitness - R. 12.21 provides for that a constable who is found unlikely to prove an efficient police officer may be discharged by the Senior Superintendent of Police at any time within three years of enrolment and there shall be no appeal against the order of discharge under this Rule - Whether the order of discharge was punitive and, therefore, illegal having been passed without conducting any disciplinary inquiry? - Precedents referred - Held, Order of termination cannot be held to be punitive in nature - Misconduct on behalf of the respondent was not the inducing factor for the termination of the respondent - The preliminary enquiry was not done with the object of finding out any misconduct on the part of the respondent, it was done only with a view to determine the suitability of the respondent within the meaning of r. 12.21 - The termination was not founded on the misconduct but the misbehaviour with a lady Constable and consumption of liquor in office were considered to determine the suitability of the respondent for the job, in the light of the standards of discipline expected from police personnel - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
