Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan v Damodar Prasad Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 822; (2004) 12 SCC 299; AIR 2004 SC 4850; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 52; 2004 (4) AWC 3385; JT 2004 (9) SC 185; 2004 (1) LLR 1103; 2004(8) SCALE 188; 2004 (6) SLR 189; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 578; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 64

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Teacher in Kendriya Vidyalaya - Transfer - Order thereof - Whether malafide and punitive? - HC held there was no illegality in order of transfer, but after coming to such a conclusion, gave a direction that respondent No. 1 shall be given a posting in State of MP - Whether such an order sustainable? - Held, unless order of transfer is vitiated by malafide or is made in violation of statutory provisions prohibiting any such transfer, the courts or the tribunals normally cannot interfere with such orders as a matter of routine, as though they were the appellate authorities substituting their own decision for that of the employer/management, as against such orders passed in the interest of administrative exigencies of the service concerned - HC did not disturbed finding as to malafides given by Tribunal but issued direction as to posting of respondent no. 1 to State of MP, which is uncalled for, and thus is clearly unsustainable - Appeal disposed of.
