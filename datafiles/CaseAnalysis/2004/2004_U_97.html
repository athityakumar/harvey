 Saurabh Choudhary and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan, B.N. Agrawal, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1127; (2004) 5 SCC 618; AIR 2004 SC 2212; 2004 (55) ALR 769; 2004 (3) AWC 2637; 2004 (3) ESC 407; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 199; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 578; 2004(5) SCALE 432; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 611; 2004 (4) Supreme 292; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1655

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Whether any reservation, be it based on residence or on institutional preference, is constitutionally permissible in PG courses of study? - Held, it is not disputed at the Bar that the process of admission commenced with the release of prospectus and public advertisement in September 2003 and at that point of time the seats available under the all-India quota were only 25% and this is how the examination was planned and obviously the medical graduates also must have made applications seeking admissions against 25% seats - Law has been settled by the Constitution Bench of SC through its judgment in Saurabh Chaudri v. UOI, (2003) 11 SCC 1462003 (11) SCC 1462003 Indlaw SC 959) - However, Court has nowhere in its judgment made the declaration of law applicable to the process of admission which had already commenced - There is no direction made to the contrary either i.e., as to the prospective applicability of the judgment and prospective overruling of the decision of this Court in Dr Dinesh Kumar case (Dinesh Kumar (Dr) (II) v. Motilal Nehru Medical College, (1986) 3 SCC 7271986 (3) SCC 7271986 Indlaw SC 178) - This has prompted the several applications being filed and the position, therefore, needs to be clarified so as to clear the doubts - It is to be directed that the allotment of seats under all-India quota, the process as to which had commenced pursuant to the advertisement D/- 16-09-2003 shall remain confined to 25% only - Applications allowed.
