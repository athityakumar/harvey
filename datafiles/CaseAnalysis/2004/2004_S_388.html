State Of Punjab v Kasturi Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 607; (2004) 12 SCC 195; AIR 2004 SC 4087; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 147; 2004 CRLJ 3866; JT 2004 (6) SC 137; 2004(6) SCALE 303; 2004(6) SCALE 308; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 158; 2004 (2) UC 1074

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - s. 3(1) and 7 (1) (a) (ii) - Vegetable Oil Products Control Order, 1947 - Violation of provison of Order - Proceedings - Quashing thereof - Inherent power of Court as to quashing proceedings initiated, when and it what circumstances to be exercised? - (i) where it manifestly appears that there is a legal bar against the institution or continuance e.g. want of sanction; (ii) where the allegations in the first information report or complaint taken at its face value and accepted in their entirety do not constitute the offence alleged; (iii) where the allegations constitute an offence, but there is no legal evidence adduced or the evidence adduced clearly or manifestly fails to prove the charge - Challenges order of HC that quashing charge framed against respondents - At the stage of summoning when evidence was yet to be led by the parties, the High Court could not on an assumption of facts come to a finding of fact that the respondent was not responsible for the conduct of the - Held, High Court was not justified in quashing the charge framed so far as the present respondents are concerned - Appeal allowed.
