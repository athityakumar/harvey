Jespar I. Slong v State of Meghalaya and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 380; (2004) 11 SCC 485; AIR 2004 SC 3533; 2004 (3) AWC 2383; 2004 (3) BC 311; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 130; JT 2004 (5) SC 107; 2004(5) SCALE 724

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Contract of leasing out a weigh bridge - Whether appeal filed before Appellate Bench of Guwahati HC was maintainable or not - whether value of contract fixed at Rs. 40, 29, 600/- By HC as per its order dated 15/3/2002 was a final value or whether it was open to State to have re-fixed that value of contract on basis of fresh material available before it - Does principle of predatory pricing apply to contract of like involved in this appeal - Tender Acceptance Committee had necessary authority to re-assess value of tender which it did by fixing value at Rs. 2 crores - HC was in error in coming to conclusion that because it felt fair value of contract is Rs. 40, 29, 600/- Any bid over and above that would be predatory - Rejection of bid of appellant or for that matter other bids which were more than 4 th respondent's bid, is unsustainable.
