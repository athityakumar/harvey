Karnataka Rare Earth and Another v The Senior Geologist, Department of Mines and

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 83; (2004) 2 SCC 783; AIR 2004 SC 2915; 2004 (54) ALR 731; JT 2004 (2) SC 472; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 779; 2004(2) SCALE 1; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 965; 2004 (2) SLT 217; 2004 (1) Supreme 1036

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Granite, Karnataka Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1957, Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1969

Summary: Mines and Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Development & Regulation) Act 1957, s. 21(5) - Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules 1969, rr. 3 and 3A - Grant of 203 leases for quarrying granites in Govt. land - Public Interest Litigation - Ground grant of leases in contravention of s. 3A wherein prohibition is provided - Quashing of all the grants by High Court and confirmed in writ appeal - Appeal to present Court was also dismissed and Court held that grants of lease were made against the prohibition contained in r. 3A and were rightly held by HC to be invalid - Recovery of price of mineral by Govt. - What is the nature of such recovery, is it compensatory or penal? - Recovery of price of the mineral is intended to compensate the State for the loss of the mineral owned by it and caused by a person who has been held to be not entitled in law to raise the same - No element of penalty involved - Compensatory - South Eastern Coalfields Ltd Vs State of M P & Ors (2003) 8 SCC 648, applied - Quashing of leases by HC, upheld - Appeal dismissed.
