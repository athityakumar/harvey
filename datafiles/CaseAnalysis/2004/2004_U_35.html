Dr. B. Singh v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 196; (2004) 3 SCC 363; AIR 2004 SC 1923; 2004 (55) ALR 153; 2004 (2) AWC 1352; 2004 (3) CHN 101; 2004 (101) FLR 575; JT 2004 (3) SC 127; 2004 (2) LLN 114; 2004(3) SCALE 224; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1061; 2004 (2) SLT 558; 2004 (2) Supreme 387

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 217, 32 - Public Interest Litigation - Increasing number of petitions filed in garb of PIL by busybodies with oblique motives, especially to interfere with judicial appointments - Serious concern expressed and strongly deprecated - PILs filed on basis of newspaper, magazine or journal reports - Cannot be automatically accepted as true - PILs filed with no public interest but for 'private', 'political', 'publicity' or 'paisa' interest - Should be dismissed at threshold with heavy costs - Guidelines stated - Petition dismissed with costs of Rs. 10, 000.
