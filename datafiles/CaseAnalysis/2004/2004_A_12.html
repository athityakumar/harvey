A. P. Gas Power Corporation Limited v A. P. State Regulatory Commission and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1466; (2004) 10 SCC 511; AIR 2004 SC 3090; JT 2004 (3) SC 600; 2004(3) SCALE 616

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 3 and 28 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 26-A and 43A(1)(c) - Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reform Act, 1998, ss. 13, 14, 15, 16, 21(4) and 56(3)(vi) - Electricity Act, 2003, s. 2(8) - Licence - Requirement thereof - Shortage of power - Production of electricity for captive consumption by industrial undertakings, in practice due to shortage conditions - Idea mooted by State Govt. and Andhra Pradesh Electricity Board to set up Gas Based Combined Cycle Power Station with private participation with a view to attract heavy industries, as setting up such project involved huge investment - Two Memorandums of Understanding entered into (MOU-1 and MOU-2), first in 1988 and other one in 1997 - MOUs provided for share holdings by Board (26%) and rest of participants, and power so generated by company was to be shared proportionately amongst share holding participating industries and their sister concerns - Thus new corporation was born by name A P Gas Power Corporation Ltd. (APGPCL) - And it started working till time Reforms Act, 1998 was enacted - Establishment of Andhra Pradesh Electricity Regulatory Commission(APERC) vide s. 3 of Reforms Act - Transmission Corporation of Andhra Pradesh Limited ('APTRANSCO')established vide s. 13 of Reforms Act which was in effect replacement of A P Electricity Board - APERC sought licence from APGPCL/appellant - Challenged by appellant - Contentions raised - As to that it has been generating power and sharing same amongst its shareholders participating industries as per terms and conditions agreed upon and thus it is a collective captive power generation company generating power for captive consumption of participating/shareholding industries and since power generating company did not require any licence either under Act, 1910 or under Act of 1948 therefore, no licence was ever taken - Whether APGPCL is required to take a licence under law for utilization/sale or supply or power generated by it to participating industries, their sister concern and companies to whom shares of APGPCL have been transferred by participating industries? - Contentions heard - MOU 1 and 2, and Articles of Association looked into by Court, provisions as to licence and exemptions therefrom under three Acts perused - Court consider question separately in respect of three categories of users of electricity generated by APGPCL, namely, (i) participating industries, (ii) their sister concerns and (iii) companies which are transferees of shares by participating industries of APGPCL - Phrase 'captive consumption', 'captive generating plant' as defined in s. 2(8) of Electricity Act, 2008 - Meaning, discussed - 'Generation of electricity' concept, what is? - Held, a generating company is not required to have a licence under Act of 1910 for carrying on any of its activities - 'Sister concern' meaning thereof - It would be a concern or unit different from participating industry and not a part of it - Relevant clause of MOU which provides as to that a participating industry has a right to transfer its share of energy and power to its sister concern, would not do away with requirement of having a licence for supply of electricity generated by APGPCL to such concerns which may be under same group as participating industries but not participating industries themselves - Transfer of shares - Relevant provisions of MOU - Participating industry entitled to transfer its share subject to condition inter alia that transferee should be HT consumers of APSEB - Further,transferee undertakings at par with participating industries - Further held, participating industries and industries to whom participating industries have transferred their shares, consumption of electricity by them within limits of value of their share capital in APGPCL would only amount to captive consumption and for such utilization or consumption of self-generated electricity no licence would be required under any provision of law - Further held, sister concern or concerns would require to have a licence if electricity is made available or provided to them for consumption as it shall fall within ambit of distribution, sale or supply of electricity and not captive consumption of power -Impugned judgment stands modified - Appeal partly allowed.
