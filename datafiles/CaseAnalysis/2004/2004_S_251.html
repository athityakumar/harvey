Thiagarajan & Ors. v Sri Venugopalaswamy B. Koil & Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 201; (2004) 5 SCC 762; AIR 2004 SC 1913; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 8; 2004 (3) AWC 1988; 2004 (3) CHN 161; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 296A; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 296B; JT 2004 (5) SC 54; 2004 (2) KLT 358; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 188; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 444; 2004(3) SCALE 345; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1229; 2004 (2) SLT 764; 2004 (3) Supreme 474

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100, 100(5) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 30 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Existence of a substantial question of law sine qua non for exercise of jurisdiction under amended provisions of Section 100 CPC - Approach of HC was not proper It is obligation of Courts of law to further clear intentment of legislature and not frustrate it by excluding same - Where findings of fact by lower appellate Court are based on evidence, HC in second appeal cannot substitute its own findings on reappreciation of evidence merely on ground that another view was possible -HC exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering with findings of final court of fact - Appeal allowed.
