Mangilal v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 44; (2004) 2 SCC 447; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1085; AIR 2004 SC 1280; 2004 (1) CCR 1; 2004 (1) Crimes 177; 2004 (2) CRJ 484; 2004 CRLJ 880; 2004 (1) JCC 138; JT 2004 (1) SC 81; 2004 (1) KLT 1038; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 791; 2004(1) SCALE 42; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2004 (1) SLT 222; 2004 (1) Supreme 9

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 366 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 357- Indian Penal Code, 1860 - scope and ambit of s.357 of the Code - Power of Court to award compensation to victims of offence is not ancillary to power to award other sentences rather it is in addition thereto - Power of appellate/revisional Court to award compensation can be exercised only after giving accused an opportunity of hearing.
