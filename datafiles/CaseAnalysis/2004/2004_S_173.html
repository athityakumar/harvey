Shri S.K. Zutshi & Another v Shri Bimal Debnath & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 594; (2004) 8 SCC 31; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2096; AIR 2004 SC 4174; JT 2004 (6) SC 323; 2004 (3) KLT 505; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 813; 2004(6) SCALE 550; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s 197 - Sanction for prosecution - Acts done in question as to ransacking goods in a shop taking aways goods and certain documents, and taking signs of certian persons on seizure memo - Challenging legality of cognizance taken and issuance of process on basis of a complaint - Whether acts of accused sanctioned by law? - Held, an accused facing prosecution for offences under Old Act or New Act cannot claim any immunity on ground of want of sanction, if he ceased to be a public servant on date when court took cognizance of offences - No case for interference - Appeal dismissed.
