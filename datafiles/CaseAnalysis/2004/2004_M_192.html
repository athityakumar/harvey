Shail v Manoj Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1469; (2004) 4 SCC 785; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1401; AIR 2004 SC 3210; 2004 ALJ 1876; 2004 (55) ALR 719; JT 2004 (4) SC 391; 2004 (3) MahLJ 503; 2004 (3) MPLJ 336; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 384; 2004(4) SCALE 199

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.- Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 376, 328 and 125 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Petitioner victim of an offence u/ss. 376 and 328 of IPC, 1860, at the hands of the respondent - To save himself from conviction, the respondent agreed to enter into a marriage with the petitioner - Petitioner was deserted by the respondent - Petitioner filed an application u/s. 125 CrPC, 1973 - On application by petitioner High Court directed the Family Court to expeditiously conclude the proceedings - In the absence of relief the petitioner invoked the contempt jurisdiction of the HC complaining of non-compliance with the orders of the HC - HC summoned Presiding Judge of Family Court - Petitioner seeks leave to file appeal against the order of the HC - In exercise of power under art. 227 the HC does have power to guide the inferior Court as to the manner in which it would proceed - HC has jurisdiction to direct suitable amount of maintenance being awarded and to secure compliance with its directions - But the petitioner has approached probably impelled by impatience - Held, it is not necessary to grant leave to appeal the order of the HC as there is nothing in the order by which the Petitioner may feel aggrieved - SLP disposed of.
