Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Ltd. v Chairman Central Board Direct Taxes And Anr

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 415; (2004) 6 SCC 431; AIR 2004 SC 2434; 2004 (189) CTR 97; 2004 (168) E.L.T. 147; [2004] 267 ITR 647; 2004(5) SCALE 705; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 593; 181 TAXATION 394; [2004] 137 TAXMAN 242

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 131 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - A dispute between Govt. Departments and/or between a Govt. Department and a Public Sector Undertaking - Jurisdiction - Parties first attempt conciliation before High Powered Committee - Litigation being of a frivolous nature must not be brought to Court - High Powered Committee will prevent frivolous litigation - If dispute is not resolved Committee would undoubtedly give clearance - As clearance not been given to Appellants these proceedings cannot be proceeded with.
