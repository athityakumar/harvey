Engineering Kamgar Union v M/S. Electro Steels Castings Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 354; (2004) 6 SCC 36; (2004) SCC (L&S) 782; AIR 2004 SC 2401; 2004 ALJ 1859; 2004 (3) AWC 2478; 2004 (2) CLR 794; 2004 (4) ESC 476; 2004 (101) FLR 1086; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 78; 2004 (2) LLJ 815; 2004 (2) LLN 850; 2004(4) SCALE 705; 2004 (3) SLR 606; 2004 (3) SLT 597; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 301; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2400

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Tobacco Board Act, 1975, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Indian Trade Unions Act, 1926, Union Evidence Act

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 254, 254(1), 254(2) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Trade Unions Act, 1926 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Tobacco Board Act, 1975 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(13) - Indian Evidence Act, ss. 114(e), 114(f) - If any provision of a law made by Legislature of a State is repugnant to any provision made by Parliament which Parliament is competent to enact, or to any provision of an existing law with respect to one of matters enumerated in Concurrent List then subject to provisions of cl (2), law made by Parliament shall prevail to extent of repugnancy required - Central Act and State Act had been enacted in terms of Entry 22 of List III of Seventh Schedule of Constitution of India - In case of any conflict therefor constitutional Scheme contained in art.254 will have to be applied - Even if Section 255 of State Act is read to have an overriding effect - Undoubtedly provisions of Constitution shall prevail over a statute.
