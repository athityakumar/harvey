Hindustan Paper Corporation Limited v Ananta Bhattacharjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1120; (2004) 6 SCC 213; 2004(9) SCALE 46

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 226 - Scheme floated by Govt. of India for equitable distribution of white paper for purpose of inter alia printing of school textbooks for states/UTs - White paper was to be supplied by appellant-corporation - Appellant did not supplied in a particular instance and advances made by respondents claimed to be refunded - High Court awarded interest as compensation - Whether u/art. 226 of Constitution such direction was permissible in law? - Held, no - Public law remedy for purpose of grant of compensation can be resorted to only when fundamental right of a citizen u/art. 21 of Constitution is violated and not otherwise - Instant case is not one which would attract invocation of said rule - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
