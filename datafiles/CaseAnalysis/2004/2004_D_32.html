Ram Dass v Davinder

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 219; (2004) 3 SCC 684; AIR 2004 SC 2162; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 140; 2004 (55) ALR 426; JT 2004 (4) SC 9; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 530; 2004(3) SCALE 659; 2004 (2) Supreme 682

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 13(2)(v), s. 15(6) - Eviction - No fault can be found with manner in which evidence has been dealt with and marshalled by Controller - Appellate authority has made an independent evaluation of evidence and confirmed findings of Controller - HC in its revisional jurisdiction, entered into re-appreciation of evidence - Not open to HC - Misreading of evidence by HC - Landlord has been able to show that tenancy premises were not being used for purpose for which they were let out and tenant has discontinued such activities in tenancy premises as would have required tenant's actually being in premises, ground for eviction is made out - Order of HC set aside.
