Supreme Court of India

27 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 723; (2004) 7 SCC 659; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2003; AIR 2004 SC 4412; 2004 CRLJ 4254; 2004 (172) E.L.T. 19; JT 2004 (7) SC 495; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 290; 2004(7) SCALE 201

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 397 - IPC, 1860, ss. 324 and 452 r/w 34 - Convictions and sentencing thereunder - HC in revision reversing convictions awarded by lower Court - Exercise of reversionary power, whether justified? - Held, No - Single Judge exceeded his revisional jurisdiction by embarking upon in-depth re-examination of the oral and medical evidence - Restraint should have been exercised by the HC acting under s. 397 - Order set aside - Appeals allowed.
