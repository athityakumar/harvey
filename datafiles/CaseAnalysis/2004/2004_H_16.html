M/s. Sangham Tape Company v Hans Raj

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 825; (2005) SCC (L&S) 65; (2005) 9 SCC 331; AIR 2004 SC 4776; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 54; 2004 (4) AWC 3215; 2004 (3) CLR 776; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 45; JT 2004 (8) SC 109; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; 2004(8) SCALE 267; 2004 (6) SLR 78; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 728

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17A, 11 - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, rr. 22 and 24 - Setting aside of exparte award by Labour Court - Whether Labour Court has jurisdiction to set aside an ex parte award? - Held, an award made by an industrial court becomes enforceable under s. 17A on expiry of 30 days from date of its publication - Once award becomes enforceable, Industrial Tribunal and/or Labour Court becomes functus officio - Such jurisdiction could be exercised by Labour Court within thirty days from date of publication of award - HC's order confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
