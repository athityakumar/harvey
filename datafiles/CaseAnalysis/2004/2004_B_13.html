B.S. Adityan & Ors. v B. Ramachandran Adityan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 294; (2004) 9 SCC 720; AIR 2004 SC 3448; 2004 (56) ALR 422; 2004 (3) AWC 1947; 2004 (4) CHN 1; JT 2004 (5) SC 370; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 54; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 216; 2004 (2) RD 215; 2004(5) SCALE 43; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 216; 2004 (3) Supreme 423

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 11 r. 21, O. 23 r. 1, O. 7 r. 11, ss. 104(1)(ffa), 141, 151, 92 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Right of appeal - Whether court is required to give an opportunity of being heard to proposed defendants before granting leave to institute a suit u/s.92 CPC - As a rule of caution, court should normally give notice to defendants before granting leave to institute a suit, court is not bound to do so - It is always open to file an application for revocation of leave which can be considered on merits and according to law or even in course of suit which may be established that suit does not fall within scope of s.92 CPC - Appeal dismissed.
