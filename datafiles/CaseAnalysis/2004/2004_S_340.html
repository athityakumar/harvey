Krishnappa v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 688; (2004) 7 SCC 792; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2093; AIR 2004 SC 4298; 2004 CRLJ 4185; JT 2004 (7) SC 509; 2004 (3) KLT 460; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 678; 2004(7) SCALE 282; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 894; 2004 (2) UC 1231

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s 319 - Application thereunder - Dismissal by Trial Magistrate - Discretion thus exercised whether proper? - Held, exercise of discretion by the magistrate did not call for interference by the HC - Incident was of the year 1993 - Role attributed to the appellant, as per the impugned judgment of the HC, was of instigation - Could not be held that the discretion was illegally exercised by the trial Magistrate so as to call for interference in exercise of revisional jurisdiction by the HC - Appeal allowed.
