Deepal Girishbhai Soni And Ors. v United India Insurance Co. Ltd., Baroda

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 234; (2004) 5 SCC 385; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1623; 2004 (1) ACC 728; AIR 2004 SC 2107; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 81; 2004 (55) ALR 462; 2004 (3) AWC 2011; 2004 (5) Bom.C.R. 129; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 292; 2004 (110) DLT 523; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1597; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2004 (4) SC 83; 2004 (2) KLT 395; 2004 (137) PLR 271; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 466; 2004(3) SCALE 546; 2004 (2) SLT 827; 2004 (3) Supreme 602

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Death Or Permanent Disablement, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1982, Motor Vehicles Act, 1914, Gujarat Motor Vehicle Rules

Summary: Constitution - Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1980, ss. 140, 163-A, 165, 166, 167 and 168 - Distinction between s. 163-A and s. 166 - Held, Motor Vehicles Act contains different expressions which goes to show that Parliament intended to insert a non-obstante clause of wide nature which would mean that provisions of s. 163-A would apply despite contrary provisions existing in said Act or any other law for time being in force - S. 163-A of Act covers cases where even negligence is on part of victim - It is by way of an exception to s. 166 and concept of social justice has been duly taken care of - Further, proceeding u/s. 163-A being a social security provision, providing for a distinct scheme, only those whose annual income is upto Rs. 40,000/- Can take benefit thereof - All other claims are required to be determined in terms of Chap. XII of Act - So far as Civil Appeal is concerned, in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 142, it is directed that claim applications of appellants u/s. 163-A of Act be treated to be applications u/s. 140 thereof - Amount invested by tribunal may be allowed to be withdrawn by respondent - Insurance Co. - Appellants shall refund excess amount withdrawn by them after adjusting amount payable in terms of s. 140 of Act and interest which would have accrued thereon shall be adjusted towards compensation received by claimant, where after, Motor Vehicles Accident Claims Tribunal shall proceed to determine their claim petitions filed u/s. 166 of Act in accordance with law - Appeals dismissed.
