Chandravathi P.K. And Others v C. K. Saji And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1685; (2004) SCC (L&S) 544; (2004) 3 SCC 734; AIR 2004 SC 2717; 2004 (101) FLR 1; JT 2004 (3) SC 150; 2004 (2) KLT 320; 2004 (2) LLN 52; 2004(2) SCALE 487; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 330; 2004 (2) SLR 763

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, Kerala Engineering Service (General Branch) Service Rules, Kerala Public Health Engineering Service Rules

Summary: Service - Kerala Engineering Service (General Branch) Rules, rr. 4, 5 - Degree and diploma holders - Seniority - Determination of - Respondent-diploma holder Assistant Engineers sought promotion to the next higher post of Assistant Executive Engineer as they had acquired their degree while they were in service - Respondent/Authority placed appellants, who were the degree-holders at the time of joining itself, over and above the diploma-holders in seniority list in accordance with the stipulation of rr. 4 and 5 of the Rules - Respondent-diploma-holders challenged the seniority of appellant-degree holders before the HC - HC allowed the writ petition of respondents and directed the respondent/Authority to promote the respondent-diploma holders over and above the appellant-degree holders - Whether in terms of the scheme of the Rules, diploma-holders are entitled to claim any weightage in the service rendered by them prior to their acquisition of degree qualification in the matter of promotion or transfer to higher posts when specific quota is fixed for graduates and diploma-holders in the matter of promotion - Held, a diploma-holder Assistant Engineer who subsequently acquired a degree qualification would be eligible for promotion as Assistant Executive Engineer, only in the event he fulfils the conditions precedent thereof and not otherwise and in terms thereof namely his case could be considered only after the cases of promotion of those who had been holding such degree qualification have been considered - By reason of acquisition of higher qualification only the diploma-holders would not become entitled to be considered for promotion as they in terms of the rules were to be regarded junior most in the seniority list of the Graduate Assistant Engineers - Further, separate seniority lists were being maintained in respect of the degree-holders, diploma-holders and certificate-holders - Once a diploma-holder acquiring a qualification of a degree in engineering opts for being included in the stream of the degree-holders, he would have to be placed at the bottom of the relevant seniority list - Therefore, the impugned judgment of HC cannot be sustained and hence set aside - Direction issued to the respondent/Department to consider the cases of all concerned employees strictly in terms of the extant rules - Appeal disposed of.
