Eagle Flask Industries Ltd. v Commissioner Of Central Excise, Pune

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 716; (2004) 7 SCC 377; 2004 (96) ECC 118; 2004 (171) E.L.T. 296; JT 2004 (7) SC 391; 2004 (64) RLT 363; 2004(7) SCALE 453; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 35

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Excisability of goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 174-A Proviso - Central Excise Licensing Control - Exemption therefrom of certain articles - Appellants, they are engaged in the manufacture of articles of plastic i.e. plastic insulated wares and vacuum flasks - Whether exemption from taking licence available to appellants? - Show cause notice issued interalia requiring appellants to show cause as to why duty shall not be levied and penalty imposed for failure to take out licence required for the manufacture of excisable goods falling under relevant Chapter falling during the relevant period and for the failure to file the list of excisable goods - Relevant notification perused - Manufacturer is required to make a declaration and give an undertaking, as specified in the Form annexed while claiming exemption for the first time under the Notification and thereafter before the 15th day of April of each financial year - Requirement not fulfilled - Consequence are denial of benefits under the Notification - Held, exemption from operation of Rule 174, was not available to the appellants - Appeal dismissed.
