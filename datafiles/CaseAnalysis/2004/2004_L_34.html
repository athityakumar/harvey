Captain Sube Singh & Ors. v Lt. Governor Of Delhi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 349; (2004) 6 SCC 440; 2004 (2) ACC 60; AIR 2004 SC 3821; 2004 (56) ALR 591; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 144; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 413; 2004(5) SCALE 371; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g), 226, 38 - Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950, s. 3 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 66, 67(1), 69, 70, 71, 72, 96(2) - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s. 43 - Delhi Motor Vehicles Rules, 1993, rr. 75, 75(2), 76 - u/s 67(1) of Act, State Govt. issued a notification, fares chargeable by operators of stage carriages/city buses plying in National Capital Territory of Delhi - HC uphold legality and validity of said notification - Condition imposed in Paragraph 3(b) of impugned notification that 'All DTC passes shall be applicable to all private Stage Carriages' is ultra vires of powers of State Govt. and is, therefore, liable to be quashed and set aside - Some locus poenitentia should be given to respondents to make alternative arrangements by formulating an appropriate scheme in accordance with law which should take care of student concessional passes - Appeal allowed.
