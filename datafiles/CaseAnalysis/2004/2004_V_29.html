Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur v Vicco Laboratories

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1429; (2005) 4 SCC 17; 2005 (179) E.L.T. 17; 2005 (66) RLT 231

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, 1985 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Whether respondent's products, namely, turmeric skin cream and vajradanti toothpaste and tooth powder are classifiable under Chap. 30 or under Chap. 33 of Central Excise Tariff Act - Burden of proof that a product is classifiable under a particular tariff head is on revenue and must be discharged by proving that it is so understood by consumers of product or in common parlance - Held, show cause notices having proceeded on a misapprehension of tests laid down in Shree Baidyanath's case, 1995 Indlaw SC 982 same cannot be sustained - Appeals dismissed.
