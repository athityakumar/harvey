Gajraj Jain v State Of Bihar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 387; (2004) 7 SCC 151; AIR 2004 SC 3392; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 35; 2004 (57) ALR 311; 2004 (56) ALR 742; 2004 (4) AWC 2907; 2004 (3) BC 514; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 112; 2004 DRTC 545; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 47; 2005 (98) RD 22; 2004(5) SCALE 693; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 677

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Corporate - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, ss. 29, 29(1), 29(4), 30 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 100, 69 - Whether Bihar State Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation Limited acted malafide and in breach of s 29 of State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 by transferring assets of debtor company and executing agreement with M/s Stichworth Exports Pvt Ltd - Conduct of corporation which was required to act in accordance with section 29 of 1951 Act and not unreasonably - Under public notice inviting tenders, corporation was obliged to call for matching offers from directors/promoters/guarantors - Corporation did not call for such offers as its object was to keep out all counter-offers - Corporation misused its authority and power in breach of law by taking into account extraneous matters and by ignoring relevant matters which has rendered all its acts ultra-vires - Set aside impugned judgment.
