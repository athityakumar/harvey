National Mineral Development Corpn. Ltd. v State Of M.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 392; (2004) 6 SCC 281; AIR 2004 SC 2456; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 30; JT 2004 (5) SC 172; 2004(5) SCALE 345; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: [A.] Mines & Minerals - Mines And Minerals (Regulation And Development) Act, 1957, ss. 13, 9, 9(1) - Mines And Minerals (Regulation And Development) Amendment Act, 1972 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 64-B, 64-C - Royalty on slimes - What is 'slime' or 'slimes' as understood in mining industry and trade; Whether 'slimes' is included in 'fines' or 'concentrates', within meaning of Entry 23 of Second Schedule, for purpose of charging royalty - Slime, including its ferrous contents, is just a waste with no commercial value as it can neither be used nor consumed and there are no takers of same in business, commerce and industry - In mining circles, fines and slimes both have different meanings - Both terms are well understood as two different objects - Slimes cannot be included in 'fines' or 'concentrates' - As no rate of royalty has been prescribed in Second Schedule to be charged on slimes and also no rate of royalty has been prescribed on iron ore as run-of-mine, royalty cannot be charged on wastage.
