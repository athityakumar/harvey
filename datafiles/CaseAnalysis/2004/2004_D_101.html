Raj Kumar v Dipender Kaur Sethi

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1023; (2005) 9 SCC 304; AIR 2005 SC 1592; 2004 (58) ALR 155; 2005 (1) AWC 311; 2005 (1) RD 272; 2004(9) SCALE 565

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 6 r. 17, o. 7 r. 11, ss. 115, 151 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 165(c) - Suit for permanent injunction - Application for converting suit for injunction into a suit for specific performance - Whether such application can be allowed? - Held, amendment can be allowed for reason that suit had already been converted into a suit under Specific Relief Act within period of limitation and, thereafter, it is only missing averment which was introduced by para 5 A - There was no question of not complying with law of limitation, as far as 2 nd amended plaint was concerned.
