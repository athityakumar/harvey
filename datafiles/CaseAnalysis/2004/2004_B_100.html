Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd. v P. Kesavan & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 418; (2004) 9 SCC 772; AIR 2004 SC 2206; 2004 (5) AWC 4223; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 163; JT 2004 (4) SC 151; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 565; 2004(4) SCALE 249; 2004 (3) SLT 618; 2004 (3) Supreme 74

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Land & Property; Petroleum & Gas

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Burmah Shell (Acquisition of Undertakings in India) Act, 1976 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 107 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 142 - Whether appellant entitled to a renewal of lease - If Govt. company expresses its desire to renew lease, same would stand renewed on same terms and conditions - By virtue of Acquisition Act right, title and interest inclusive of leasehold rights of Burmah Shell vested in Central Govt. and consequently upon appellant-company - s. 5 of Acquisition Act contemplates automatic renewal of lease - The Act being special statute vis-a-vis Transfer of Property Act which is general statute - Maxim "generalia specialibus non derogant" applies - Impugned judgments set aside.
