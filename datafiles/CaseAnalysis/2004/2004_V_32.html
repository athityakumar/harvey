Allahabad District Co-Operative Bank Ltd., Allahabad v Vidhya Varidh Mishra

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 593; (2004) 6 SCC 482; 2004 (3) AWC 2718; 2004 (20) CLR 285; 2004 (4) ESC 544; 2004 (102) FLR 1043; JT 2004 (6) SC 343; 2004 (3) LLJ 526; 2004(6) SCALE 585; 2004 (7) SLR 1; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 479; 2004 (6) Supreme 127; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2574

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Employment - Termination-Participation of respondent in embezzlement proved in disciplinary enquiry-Proposal for minor punishment of appellant bank not approved by Registrar-Proper punishment was termination of service - Termination order not challenged in writ petition-But rejection of application to reconsider order of termination pursuant to exoneration by appellate court in criminal case against respondent only challenged-Hence, his reinstatement could not have been directed-Termination was pursuant to disciplinary enquiry and not on basis of conviction-Exoneration by appellate court-Of no consequence -Impugned order set aside.
