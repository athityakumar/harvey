Uma Shankar Gopalika v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1763; (2005) 10 SCC 336

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420 - Cheating - Quashing of FIR - Declined - Challenged - FIR was registered against appellant/accused and his brother u/ss. 420/120-B of IPC, petition to quash the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether any criminal offence was made out much less offences u/ss. 420/120-B of IPC - Held, every breach of contract would not give rise to offence of cheating and only in those cases breach of contract would amount to cheating where there was any deception played at very inception - If intention to cheat developed later on, the same could not amount to cheating - In instant case, it was nowhere stated that at very inception there was any intention on behalf of accused persons to cheat - Petition of complaint did not disclose any criminal offence at all much less any offence either u/ss. 420 or 120-B of IPC - It was a case of purely civil dispute between parties for which remedy lied before civil court by filing a properly constituted suit - Hence, impugned order was set aside and police investigation and consequent prosecution of appellant were hereby quashed - Appeal allowed.
