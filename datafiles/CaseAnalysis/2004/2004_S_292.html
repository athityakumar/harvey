Sanjay K. Sinha-Ii And Ors. v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 467; (2004) 10 SCC 734; AIR 2004 SC 3460; 2004 (2) CLR 882; 2004 (102) FLR 215; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 228; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 592; 2004 (3) LLN 828; 2004(6) SCALE 66; 2004 (4) SLR 667; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Bihar Forest Service Rules, rr. 3, 35 - Challenging seniority list Assistant Conservators of Forests (ACFS) to Bihar Forest Service - Appellants who are direct recruits to Bihar Forest Service shown junior to private respondents who are promotees in service - Sanctioned number of posts were not available in year 1987 when respondents promoted as ACFs - Promotions made against non-existing posts - Posts to which substantive appointments were to be made were not available, there could be no appointment to service - When there is no appointment to service, much less substantive appointment to service, promotees could not be given seniority with effect from purported date of their promotion - Seniority list quashed.
