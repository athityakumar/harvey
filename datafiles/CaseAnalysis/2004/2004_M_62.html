State Of Punjab v M/S Nestle India Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 397; (2004) 6 SCC 465; AIR 2004 SC 4559; 2004 (189) CTR 501; [2004] 269 ITR 97; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 283; 2004(5) SCALE 529; 2004 (136) STC 35; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Exemption Notification, Purchase Tax, Requirement, Punjab General Sales Tax Rules, 1949, Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876, Andhra Pradesh Non Agricultural Lands Assessment Tax Act, 1963

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Punjab General Sales Tax Rules, 1949 - Punjab General Sales Tax Rules, 1949, rr. 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 8(1), 8(2) - Principle of promissory estoppel - Respondents not paid purchase tax for period 1 4 96 to 4 6 97 because Govt. decided to abolish purchase tax on milk for period in question - State raised demands for purchase tax on milk on respondents for period 1996 -97 - Overall benefit to state's economy and public would be greater if exemption were allowed - Respondents passed on benefit of that exemption by providing various facilities and concessions for upliftment of milk producers - State Govt's refusal to exercise its discretion to issue necessary notification 'abolishing' or exempting tax on milk was not reasonably exercised - Upheld plea of promissory estoppel raised by respondents - Appeal dismissed.
