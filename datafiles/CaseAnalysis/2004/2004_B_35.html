Chadat Singh v Bahadur Ram And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 564; (2004) 6 SCC 359; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 26; 2004 (57) ALR 275; 2004 (56) ALR 735; 2004 (3) AWC 2583; 2005 (2) Bom.C.R. 300; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 58; JT 2004 (6) SC 296; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 781; 2004 (97) RD 424; 2004(6) SCALE 423; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 298; 2004 (2) UC 1182

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s 100 - Substantial question of law - Whether HC justified in disposing of Second Appeal without formulating substantial question of law? - Held, where HC is satisfied that in any case any substantial question of law is involved it shall formulate that question and second appeal has to be heard on question so formulated - Since no substancial question of law formulated, the judgment cannot be maintained - Appeals Disposed Of.
