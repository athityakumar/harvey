State of Uttranchal Through Collector, Dehradun v (1) Ajit Singh Bhola and Another; (2)

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 388; (2004) 6 SCC 800; 2004 (56) ALR 272; 2004 (2) ARC 270; 2004 (3) AWC 2380; JT 2004 (5) SC 513; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 250; 2004(5) SCALE 811; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 627; 2004 (2) UC 1008

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Uttar Pradesh Accommodation Requisition Act 1947, s. 3(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Possession of premises taken by State under orders of District Magistrate - State has neither been able to produce order passed by District Magistrate in this regard nor has he been able to point out any law or rule which authorizes District Magistrate to take possession of any premises in manner it has been done - This Court will not exercise its discretion and quash an order which appears to be illegal, if its effect is to revive another illegal order.
