Raichurmatham Prabhakar & Anr v Rawatmal Dugar

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 256; (2004) 4 SCC 766; AIR 2004 SC 3625; 2004 (56) ALR 105; JT 2004 (4) SC 495; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 588; 2004(4) SCALE 452

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 12, 12(1), 12(1)(b) - Hyderabad Houses (Rent, Eviction and Lease) Control Act, 1954 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106, 108, 111 - Rent Restriction Act - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Rules, 1961, r. 23 - If law permits a revision of rent or fixation of standard rent afresh, landlord would be at liberty to invoke that provision and revise rent consistently with such provisions - Revision of rent cannot be insisted on by landlord as a condition precedent to re-entry by tenant - Application filed by tenant for enforcing right of re-entry pursuant to undertaking given by landlord not barred by limitation - HC has rightly allowed revision petitions holding application filed by teant to be within limitation and rightly held that it was open to landlord to take necessary steps for claiming fair rent - Appeals dismissed.
