Divisional Controller, KSRTC (NWKRTC) v A.T. Mane

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 826; (2005) 3 SCC 254; (2005) SCC (L&S) 407; AIR 2004 SC 4761; 2004 (3) CLR 772; 2004 (103) FLR 428; JT 2004 (8) SC 103; 2004 (4) LLN 796; 2004(8) SCALE 308; 2004 (6) SLR 30

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Respondent a bus conductor of Transport Corporation - Dismissal - Misconduct - Res ipsa loquitur - Appellant-corporation was directed to reinstate respondent in service with fill back wages and continuity of service and other consequential benefits - Held, fact respondent was carrying Rs 93/- In excess of amount which is supposed to be carried by him as per rules, is a fact proved - Fact thus proved is itself a misconduct, and there was no need for the Courts below to have insisted upon examination of passengers - Courts below erred in interfering with finding of fact on an erroneous basis - Appeal allowed.
