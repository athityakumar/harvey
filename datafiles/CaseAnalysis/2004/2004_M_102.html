M/s. Chitivalasa Jute Mills v M/s. Jaypee Rewa Cement

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 93; (2004) 3 SCC 85; AIR 2004 SC 1687; 2004 (54) ALR 706; 2004 (1) AWC 796; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 924; 2004 (3) CHN 56; JT 2004 (2) SC 535; 2004 (137) PLR 290; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 108; 2004(2) SCALE 213; 2004 (2) SLT 157; 2004 (1) Supreme 824

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, ss. 10 and 151 - Inherent powers - Consolidation of suits - Two plaints filed by the two parties before two different courts - Parties are substantially the same - Possibility that the two courts may record finding inconsistent with each other and conflicting decrees may come to be passed cannot be ruled out -- The District Judge, shall see that the two suits are placed before one Court and on one date of hearing, whereafter the two suits shall be heard and decided consistently - Held civil courts have inherent power under s 151 to consolidate suits - Petition allowed.
