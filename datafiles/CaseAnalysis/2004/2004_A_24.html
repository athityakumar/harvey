A.S. Krishnan And Anr v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 207; (2004) 11 SCC 576; AIR 2004 SC 3229; 2004 (2) Crimes 317; 2004 CRLJ 2833; JT 2004 (3) SC 461; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 312; 2004(3) SCALE 362

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Education; Health & Drug

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss.466, 468, 471- Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Accusation of a doctor having doctored documents for his son to get admission to medical college - HC found that conviction was in order so far as offence relatable to Sections 471, 420 r/w Section 34 were concerned - Convictions u/s. 120 B set aside - Sentence was reduced to three months each for offences punishable u/s. 471 and 420 r/w Section 34 IPC - HC has already taken a liberal view so far as A-2 is concerned - Also held that in a case when students use forged mark sheets to obtain admission thereby depriving eligible candidates to get seats and that too to a medical course and a doctor is involved in whole operation, uncalled for leniency or undue sympathy will be misplaced and actually result in miscarriage of justice - Appeal dismissed.
