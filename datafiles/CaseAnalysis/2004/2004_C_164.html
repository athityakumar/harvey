M/s. Kores India Limited, Chennai v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chennai

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 992; (2005) 1 SCC 385; 2004 (174) E.L.T. 7; JT 2004 (10) SC 55; 2004 (65) RLT 656; 2004(9) SCALE 633; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Typewriter/Telex ribbons - Whether finding of CEGAT that cutting of ribbons in smaller size and spooling them into on automatic spooling machines amounts to manufacture and, therefore, duty is payable on these ribbons is sustainable? - Held, it was categorically observed that assessee produced ribbons in spools out of Jumbo rolls and resultant product is a distinct, identifiable article having distinct name, function and use - Resultant product is also commercially distinct as understood in commercial parlance and has a separate market - Their function and use are also completely different and both products are not inter-changeable - Order of Collector as affirmed on point involved by CEGAT does not warrant any interference being based factual conclusions which cannot be termed as perverse - Appeals Disposed Of.
