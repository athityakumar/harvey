Babulal Bhagwan Khandare and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1033; (2005) 10 SCC 404; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1553; AIR 2005 SC 1460; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 137; 2004(10) SCALE 188; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 633; 2005 (1) UC 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Examination Of Accused

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 100, 101, 300, 302, 307, 34, 38 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Conviction and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life - Appeal against conviction - Whether there is right of private defence and applicability of Exception 4 to s 300? - Held, there is no material even to suggest that accused persons apprehended danger of any kind, much less a threat to life - Claim of right of private defence has therefore been rightly discarded - Courts below have rightly found accused appellants guilty.
