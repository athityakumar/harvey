Fakeerappa and Another v Karnataka Cement Pipe Factory and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 119; (2004) 2 SCC 473; (2004) SCC (Cr) 577; 2004 (1) ACC 494; 2004 (54) ALR 702; 2004 (2) AWC 1837; 2004 (2) CLR 132; JT 2004 (2) SC 432; 2004 (137) PLR 210; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 619; 2004(2) SCALE 428; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 369; 2004 (2) SLT 99; 2004 (1) Supreme 1059

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Claim petition - Tribunal allowing fifty percent for personal expenses - On facts reduced to one-third Grant of interest of six percent - Not questioned before appellate for a - No interference - Pleadings - SC and HC impleaded before tribunal - No sensible reason - Gross abuse of process of law - Claim Petition was filed without any application of mind by counsel concerned - Great sense of responsibility is expected to be exhibited by those concerned - Proper attention ought to be devoted which sadly was not done.
