State Bank Of Patiala v Romesh Chander Kanoji & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 171; (2004) SCC (L&S) 428; (2004) 2 SCC 651; AIR 2004 SC 2016; 2004 (2) AWC 1884; 2004 (2) ESC 305; 2004 (100) FLR 1078; JT 2004 (3) SC 30; 2004 (2) LLJ 1; 2004 (2) LLN 1; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 185; 2004(2) SCALE 676; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 615; 2004 (2) Supreme 229; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1881

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: whether an employee having once opted for voluntary retirement pursuant to a voluntary retirement scheme is precluded from withdrawing his offer/opting out from the scheme - voluntary retirement scheme framed by State Bank of India - opportunity of 15-days was given to the employee-applicant to withdraw from the scheme whereas under the voluntary retirement scheme of nationalized banks no such opportunity was given - held, reading clauses 5, 8 and 9(i), it is clear that employees are precluded from withdrawing after the closure of the scheme on 1.3.2001 - State Bank of Patiala is a subsidiary of State Bank of India - SBPVRS is similar to SBIVRS - Bank of India v. O.P. Swarnakar AIR 2003 SC 858 : (2003) 2SCC 721 applies - each scheme for the purposes of enforceability is required to be read as a whole - appeals allowed.
