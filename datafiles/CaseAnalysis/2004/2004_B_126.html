Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti Sahaswan District v Bipin Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 8; (2004) 2 SCC 283; AIR 2004 SC 2895; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 45; 2004 ALJ 665; 2004 (55) ALR 83; 2004 (2) AWC 1176; 2004 (3) CHN 1; 2004 (109) DLT 356; 2004 (1) ESC 114; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 217; JT 2004 (1) SC 344; 2004 (137) PLR 70; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 720; 2004(1) SCALE 332; 2004 (1) SLT 422; 2004 (1) Supreme 448; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 956

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Compensation - Fixation thereof - Order of Reference Court and High Court fixing quantum of compensation, whether justified? - Potentiality of land - Facts of case - Evidence existent as to high potentiality of land - Increase by HC to the tune of 15% thus is justified - This 15% should be on Rs. 15.40 which is the figure shown in the sale deed - It cannot be on Rs.120 as wrongly taken by the High Court - The HC also erred in considering only three years increase whereas in fact there is four years difference between the respondent's sale deed and the acquisition proceedings - Thus taking an increase of 60% over the price of Rs.15.40 per sq. yard the value comes to Rs.24.64 per sq. yard - Order of HC and Reference Court set aside - The respondent will also to be entitled to solatium and other statutory benefits under the Act - Directions issued - Appeal disposed of.
