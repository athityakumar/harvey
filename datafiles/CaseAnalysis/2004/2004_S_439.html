Satyajit Banerjee And Others v State Of West Bengal And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 994; (2005) 1 SCC 115; (2005) SCC (Cr) 276; 2005 (1) CalLT(SC) 30; 2005 CRLJ 648; JT 2004 (10) SC 27; 2004 (3) KLT 1098; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 723; 2004(9) SCALE 617; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860 ss. 498, 306 - CrPC, 1973, s. 311 - Trial Court acquitted accused but HC set aside acquittal and directed a de novo trial - Whether HC right in directing examination of additional witnesses under s. 311 in revision?; whether direction of HC to trial court to record further evidence and take a 'fresh decision from stage one' is without jurisdiction? - Held, direction for retrial should not be made in all or every case where acquittal of accused is for want of adequate or reliable evidence - Even if a retrial is directed in exercise of revisional powers by HC, evidence already recorded at initial trial cannot be erased or wiped out from record of case - Trial Judge has to decide case on basis of evidence already on record and additional evidence which would be recorded on retrial - Clarified and reiterate that trial Judge, after retrial, shall take a decision on basis of entire evidence on record and strictly in accordance with law - Order accordingly.
