Ashirvad Enterprises and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 223; (2004) 3 SCC 624; AIR 2004 SC 4586; 2004 (2) Crimes 335; 2004 (187) CTR 609; 2004 (168) E.L.T. 433; [2004] 266 ITR 578; 2004 (2) JCC 703; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 213; JT 2004 (4) SC 6; 2004(3) SCALE 515; 2004 (2) SLT 815; 2004 (2) Supreme 554; [2004] 137 TAXMAN 455

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 245 (H) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Finance Act, 1987 - Immunity from proceedings - Whether grant of immunity is called for in a given case is to be decided by Settlement Commission on facts of each case and no straight-jacket formula for any universal application can be laid down? - Held on facts, commission satisfied that grant of immunity is called for - Proceedings in Special Court quashed - Appeal allowed.
