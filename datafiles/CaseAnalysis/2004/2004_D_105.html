Smt. Savita Garg v Director, National Heart Institute

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 845; (2004) 8 SCC 56; AIR 2004 SC 5088; 2004 (4) AWC 3699; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 430; 2004 (4) CompLJ 255; 2004 (4) CPJ 40; 2004 (4) CPJ(Sc) 40; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 22; JT 2004 (8) SC 607; 2004 (3) KLT 919; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 512; 2004(8) SCALE 694; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 359; 2004 (2) UC 1338

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer Protection Rules, 1987, Hospitals, Medical Profession, Public Health Act, 1936

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(o) and 22 - Medical negligence - Contract of service and contract for service - Whether non-impleading the treating doctor as party could result in dismissal of original petition for non-joinder of necessary party? - Held, summary dismissal of original petition by Commission on question of non-joinder of necessary parties was not proper - Once an allegation is made that patient was admitted in a particular hospital and evidence is produced to satisfy that he died because of lack of proper care and negligence, then burden lies on hospital to justify that there was no negligence on part of treating doctor/ or hospital - Set aside order - Remit back original petition to National Commission - Appeal allowed.
