Madhya Pradesh Electricity Board v Hariram etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 829; (2004) 8 SCC 246; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1092; AIR 2004 SC 4791; 2004 (4) AWC 3229; 2004 (3) CLR 768; 2004 (103) FLR 420; JT 2004 (8) SC 98; 2004(8) SCALE 320; 2004 (6) SLR 144; 2005 (1) UC 284

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - M.P. Industrial Relations Act (M.P. Act), ss. 31 r/w 61- Daily wagers - Employment as per need of appellant electricity Board - Grievance when no further employment given - Industrial Court directed to re-instate respondents with 50% back wages - Respondents were engaged by appellant-Board on daily wages - HC after drawing an adverse inference based on grounds of non-production of all Muster Rolls allowed re-instatement - Held, drawing of an adverse inference for non-production of Muster Rolls for the years 1991-92, is wholly erroneous on part of Industrial Court and HC - Industrial Court or HC could not have based an order of re-instatement solely on basis of an adverse inference - Appeals allowed.
