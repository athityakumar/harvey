Prakash Nath Khanna and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 502; (2004) 9 SCC 686; AIR 2004 SC 4552; 2004 CRLJ 3362; 2004 (187) CTR 97; [2004] 266 ITR 1; JT 2004 (2) SC 510; 2004(2) SCALE 512; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 434; 180 TAXATION 18; [2004] 135 TAXMAN 327

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,1961,ss. 139(1), 139(4)(a), 143(3), 192, 271(l)(a), 276CC, 278E - A/y 1988-89 - Delay in filing return - Assessment -Challenged - Appellants filed return of income on dt.20-3-1991 instead of dt.31-7-1988 for a/y 1988-89 - Assessment was completed and proceedings for late submission of return were initiated by respondents after imposing penalty on appellants - Proceedings u/s.276-CC of the Act were initiated by filing complaint in Trial Court - Cognizance was taken and process was issued - Challenged by appellants by writ petitions in HC and were dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Orders of HC were justified - Held, settled principle of law was that Court could not read anything into statutory provision which was plain and unambiguous - Statute was edict of legislature and Court could only interpretive law and could not legislate it - SC in Rishabh Agro Industries Ltd. v. P.N.B. Capital Services Ltd, 2000 Indlaw SC 444 held that if provision of law was misused and subjected to abuse of process of law it was for legislature to amend modify or repeal it if deemed necessary - Legislative casus omissus could not be supplied by judicial interpretative process - SC in Board of Muslim Wakfs, Rajasthan v. Radha Kishan and Ors, 1978 Indlaw SC 99 and Kalawatibai v. Soiryabai and Ors, 1991 Indlaw SC 449 held that marginal notes in an Indian Statute as in Act of Parliament could not be referred to for purpose of construing statute - SC in Smt. Nandini Satpathy v. P.L. Dani and Anr,1978 Indlaw SC 318 held that marginal note certainly could not control meaning of body of section if language employed there was clear - In instant case provisions of s.276-CC were in clear terms and term used 'in due time' indicated s.139(1) and not s.139(4) - Factual aspects highlighted by appellants were to be dealt by trial Court as per statutory provision u/s. 278 E of the Act and hence Order of HC was justified - Appellants could plead absence of culpable mental state in Trial Court - Appeals dismissed.
