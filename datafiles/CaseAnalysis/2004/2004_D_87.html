State of Himachal Pradesh v Dhanwant Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 118; (2004) 13 SCC 331; (2005) SCC (Cr) 248; AIR 2004 SC 1636; 2004 (2) Crimes 177; 2004 CRLJ 1431; JT 2004 (2) SC 367; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 627; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 457; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 698; 2004(2) SCALE 423; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 297; 2004 (1) ShimLC 462; 2004 (2) SLT 637; 2004 (2) Supreme 189

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: [A.] Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227, 32 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 59(2), 69 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 397, 401, 482 - Himachal Pradesh (Second Amendment) Act, 1991, ss. 52 (A), 52 (B) - HC held that petition filed by State u/s. ss.397, 401, 482 not maintainable - Also held, that whether powers u/s. 482 of Code and art.227 of Constitution of India, 1950 (in short 'the Constitution'), could be exercised need not be gone into as this is not a fit case - Where power required to be so exercised - 252 tins of Resins along with truck seized - HC did not examine various submissions made - Whether presumption u/s. 69 of Forest Act was to be pressed into service - In view of statutory presumptions and there is no need for making any separate claim or claims petition separately - Not a frivolous question and HC was required to consider issue in proper perspective - HC was not justified in its conclusion that no case for examination was made out - HC shall entertain proceedings as a petition under art.227 of Constitution of India - About applicability of Section 69 of Act and record its findings objectively on merits and in accordance with law.
