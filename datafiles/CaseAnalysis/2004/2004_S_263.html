Ganesh Santa Ram Sirur v State Bank of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 977; (2005) 1 SCC 13; AIR 2005 SC 314; 2005 (5) AWC 4168; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 888; 2005 (1) CLR 293; JT 2004 (9) SC 620; 2005 (1) LLJ 188; 2005 (1) LLN 13; 2004(9) SCALE 449; 2005 (1) SLR 386; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 101; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 354

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - State Bank of India Officer's Services Rules 1992, r. 49(e), 69(2) - Removal from service - Irregularities committed - Appeal against removal - Earlier penalty of reduction in basic pay by one stage imposed by Appointing Authority enhanced by appellate authority as removal from service - Whether such enhancement is possible with out personal hearing? - Held, rule does not provide for a personal hearing or a personal interview - Appellate authority shall give a show cause notice to officer as to why enhanced penalty should not be imposed upon him and shall pass final order after taking into account representation - Appellate authority considered detailed submissions made by appellant and reached its conclusion on facts and circumstances of case and modified proposed penalty of dismissal to that of penalty of removal - Appeal dismissed.
