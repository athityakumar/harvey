Draupadi Devi & Ors. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 717; (2004) 11 SCC 425; AIR 2004 SC 4684; 2004 (113) DLT 616; JT 2004 (9) SC 35; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 402; 2004(7) SCALE 456; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, Delhi Premises (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1947

Summary: Constitution - Land and Property - Constitution, art. 363 - Suit for declaration of property being land and building situated at 3, Mansingh Road, New Delhi - Plaintiff claimed title to the suit property on the ground that the plaintiff had purchased the suit property by a registered sale deed dated 10.1.1950 for a consideration of Rs. 1.50 lacs from Maharaja Paramjit Singh, son of the Maharaja Jagatjit Singh, erstwhile Ruler of Kapurthala State, who was the rightful owner thereof and in whose name the property stood mutated in the official records of the Government at the material time - Whether title to property made out? - Govt.of India had never recognized the suit property as the private property of Maharaja of Kapurthala - Any attempt to take it away otherwise then by a Constitutionally valid legislative enactment - Merely because the decision not to recognize was conveyed to the plaintiff in the year 1951, the act of the Union of India did not cease to be an act of State, nor does it fall outside the protective umbrella of art.363 of the constitution - Finding that the suit was bared by art.363 thus was liable to fail - No reason to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
