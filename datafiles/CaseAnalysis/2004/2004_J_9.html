State of Rajasthan and Another v J.K. Udaipur Udyog Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1160; (2004) 7 SCC 673; JT 2004 (8) SC 137; 2004(8) SCALE 288; 2004 (137) STC 438; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 812

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Evasion Of Tax, Exemption From Payment Of Tax, Eligibility Certificate, Sales Tax Authorites, Deferment Of Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 15 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(5) - Constitution of India, 1950 - A scheme was framed by first appellant granting exemption to industrial units from payment of sales tax on intra-state and inter-state sale of goods and by-products manufactured within State of Rajasthan - Whether respondent-companies were entitled to benefits of scheme on par with new units under serial no. 1 with effect from date of date of certification of their application u/cl. 4(h)? - Held, in this case scheme being notified under power in state Govt. to grant exemptions both u/s. 15 of RST and s. 8(5) of CST in public interest, state Govt. was competent to modify or revoke grant for same reason - Thus what is granted can be withdrawn unless Govt. is precluded from doing so on ground of promissory estoppel, which principle is itself subject to considerations of equity and public interest - High Court erred in fact in holding that M/s J K Synthetics had a vested right to benefits of scheme - Cl. 2(k)(ii) therefore indicates that highest benefit that a sick industrial unit can claim under scheme, is to be treated at par with new industries - Cl. 9 of scheme makes it clear that right under scheme was temporary in sense that scheme could be modified or reviewed - Therefore, even if one were to assume that respondent-companies were entitled to benefits of scheme on par with new units under serial no. 1 with effect from date of certification of their application u/cl. 4(h), right could be modified with effect from date on which scheme was modified - Appeals allowed.
