Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation and v Tilla Ram

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 657; (2004) 11 SCC 355; 2004 (4) AWC 3015; 2004 (103) FLR 151; JT 2004 (7) SC 51; 2004 (3) LLJ 552; 2004 (4) LLN 728; 2004(7) SCALE 224; 2004 (5) SLR 748; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 792; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2645

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Civil Procedure - Respondent employee were terminated - Employees filed declaration suit before Trial court and the same was dismissed - Corporation filed appeal before Single Judge and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is to be noted that before Appellate Court Corporation was respondent. After referring to some judgments referred to by Corporation, it inappropriately came to conclusions that judgment and decree was liable to be appealed and appeal of plaintiff was liable to be accepted on basis of above mentioned illustrations. Unfortunately, it has not been indicated as to how decisions relied upon by Corporation supported case of plaintiff employee. What was ratio in those cases and how they were applicable and helpful to employee's case has also not been indicated. Such unreasoned and palpably wrong conclusions cannot be supported in law. Unfortunately, HC did not consider these aspects. It confused between conclusions of Trial Court and Appellate Court. Conclusions of Appellate Court were treated to be that of Trial Court. This was certainly a very highly improver way of dealing with the matter. Appeal allowed.
