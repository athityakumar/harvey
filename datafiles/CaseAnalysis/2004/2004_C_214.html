M/S. Tega India Ltd. v Commissioner, Central Excise, Calcutta-Ii

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 149; (2004) 2 SCC 727; AIR 2004 SC 2785; 2004 (92) ECC 1; 112 ECR 741; 2004 (164) E.L.T. 390; JT 2004 (2) SC 266; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 162; 2004 (60) RLT 839; 2004(2) SCALE 391; 2004 (2) SLT 97; 2004 (135) STC 219; 2004 (2) Supreme 135

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Collector (Appeals)

Summary: Manufacture - Coating and other extra processes when does not amount to manufacture - Mere coating would not amount to manufacture of a new commodity when tariff item does not make any difference between coated and uncoated goods - Mere application of some extra process on the product would not by itself make it a new dutiable item - Section 2(f) of Central Excise Act, 1944. [para 9]Manufacture - Fabricated steel pipes (rubber lined) - Mere fixing, rubberizing and painting pipe etc., supplied by customer, which on occasions are also cut and welded with flanks in order to restore pipes to their original length, does not amount to manufacture and no new commodity come into existence - Headings 7303 and 7304 of Central Excise Tariff makes no difference between coated and uncoated pipes - Board's Circulars dated June, 1988 and dated 9-2-1994 relied upon - Sections 2(f) and 3 of Central Excise Act, 1944. [paras 9, 10]Rubber lining, painting, cutting and welding of iron and steel pipes, tanks and other similar articles does not amount to manufacture as Headings 7303 and 7304 of Central Excise Tariff does not make any difference between coated and uncoated tubes/pipes - Thus, such process does not result into manufacture of a new commodity - Sections 2(f) and 3 of Central Excise Act, 1944. [paras 9, 10]Coating of goods - Mere process of coating, painting would not amount to manufacture of new commodity when tariff entry does not make difference between coated and uncoated goods - Merely because of some extra process carried on the product, it would not by itself mean that new commodity has come into existence - Section 2(f) of Central Excise Act, 1944. [para 9]Departmental clarification - Circulars issued by Central Board of Excise & Customs are binding - Section 37B of Central Excise Act, 1944. [para 9]
