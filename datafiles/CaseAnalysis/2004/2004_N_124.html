Dhanraj v New India Assurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 769; (2004) 8 SCC 553; 2004 (3) ACC 300; AIR 2004 SC 4767; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 51; 2004 (57) ALR 720; 2004 (4) AWC 3213; JT 2004 (8) SC 604; 2005 (139) PLR 594; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 786; 2004(8) SCALE 229; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 711

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Whether a comprehensive Policy would cover risk of injury to owner of vehicle also? - Held, an insurance policy covers liability incurred by insured in respect of death of or bodily injury to any person (including an owner of goods or his authorized representative) carried in vehicle or damage to any property of a third party caused by or arising out of use of vehicle - s. 147 does not require an Insurance Company to assume risk for death or bodily injury to owner of vehicle - Appeals Dismissed.
