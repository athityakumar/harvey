Dhanaj Singh @ Shera and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1100; (2004) 3 SCC 654; (2004) SCC (Cr) 851; AIR 2004 SC 1920; 2004 CRLJ 1807; JT 2004 (3) SC 380; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 428; 2004(3) SCALE 93; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 938

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fire Arms, Footprints, Tainted Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code , 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Whether faulty investigation can be a ground to affect credibility of eye-witnesses? - Held, no - Amar Singh v. Balwinder Singh and Ors (2003 Indlaw SC 95) relied on - When direct testimony of eye-witnesses corroborated by medical evidence fully establishes prosecution version failure or omission or negligence on part of IO cannot affect credibility of prosecution version - Stand of appellants relate essentially to acceptability of evidence - Even if investigation is defective, in view of legal principles, that pales into insignificance when ocular testimony is found credible and cogent - Further effect of non-examination of weapons of assault or pellets etc in background of defective investigation have been considered in Amar Singh's case - In case at hand, no crack in evidence of vital witnesses can be noticed - Both Trial Court and High Court have analysed evidence of Ps 2 and 3 with due care and caution keeping in view correct legal principles and have found accused persons guilty - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
