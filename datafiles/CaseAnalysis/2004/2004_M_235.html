Commissioner of Customs, Kandla v Messrs Essar Oil Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 853; (2004) 11 SCC 364; 2004 (172) E.L.T. 433; JT 2004 (9) SC 12; 2004 (65) RLT 1; 2004(8) SCALE 570; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Customs - Excise - Customs Act, 1967 - Imposition of duty, redemption fine, interest and penalty - CEGAT set aside - Whether s 15(1)(b) or s 15(1)(c) is applicable? - Held, CEGAT did not consider aberrations highlighted by Commissioner and in a very cryptic manner dealt with issues - No plausible reason indicated as to why allegations which are quite serious in nature and conclusions in relation thereto recorded by Commissioner were not to be maintained - Manipulative roles of respondents 2 to 7 clearly established - They were clearly active participants in the well-planned deception and fraudulent acts leading to evasion of duty - Levy of penalty is clearly warranted - HC's order set aside - Appeals allowed.
