Main Pal And Anr. v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 420; (2004) 10 SCC 692; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1882; AIR 2004 SC 2158; 2004 (2) Crimes 140; 2004 CRLJ 2036; JT 2004 (4) SC 273; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 466; 2004(4) SCALE 178; 2004 (2) SLT 1042; 2004 (3) Supreme 59

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Appeal against acquittal - HC as court of first appeal is obligated to go into greater detail of evidence to see whether any miscarriage has resulted from order of acquittal, though has to act with great circumspection and utmost care before ordering reversal of an acquittal - Judgment of trial Court practically unreasoned without any attempt to critically and objectively analyse evidence - HC was justified in undertaking a re-appreciation of evidence and HC has taken into account all relevant aspects of case to hold accused persons guilty.
