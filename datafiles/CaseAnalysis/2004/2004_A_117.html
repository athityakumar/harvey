Ntc (Wbab and O) Limited and Another v Anjan K. Saha

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1406; (2004) 7 SCC 581; (2004) SCC (L&S) 961; AIR 2004 SC 4255; 2004 (4) AWC 3018; 2004 (3) CLR 965; JT 2004 (9) SC 109; 2004 (3) LLJ 757; 2004 (3) LLN 1238; 2004(7) SCALE 128; 2004 (6) SLR 844

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Central Rules, 1946 - Whether respondent can be granted relief of reinstatement with full back wages in case there is procedural infirmity of non supply of enquiry report and also non compliance with cl. 14(4)(c) of model standing orders? - Non compliance of cl. 14(4)(c) cannot be held to be a more vitiating factor than non-supply of enquiry report - After fresh enquiry is over from stage of supply of enquiry report, employee can be granted opportunity against proposed penalty in terms of cl. 14(4)(c) of Model Standing Orders - Held, if employee is cleared of charges and is reinstated, disciplinary authority would be at liberty to decide according to law how it will treat period from date of dismissal till period of reinstatement and consequential benefits - Appeal partly allowed.
