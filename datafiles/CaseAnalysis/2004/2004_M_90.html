Food Corporation of India and others v M/S.Babulal Agrawal

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 61; (2004) 2 SCC 712; AIR 2004 SC 2926; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 66; 2004 (2) AWC 1571; JT 2004 (1) SC 220; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; 2004 (137) PLR 72; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 773; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 244; 2004(1) SCALE 158; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 129; 2004 (1) SLT 475; 2004 (1) Supreme 197

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Agreement For Lease

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Food Corporation of India - Breach of contract - Suit for damages - Invitation of tenders for hiring plinths for storing foodgrains - Contract was for three years - FCI vacated plinths before the expiry of three years - Alleging breach of contract suit for damages filed - Suit decreed and 6% of amount payable as monthly rent of plinths, was to be deducted as damages - Quantum of damages whether proper? - Once the measure of damages has been accepted as the amount of monthly rent of the plinths, unless there was some logical and cogent reason to reduce the same, it could not be done - Quantum of damages upheld - Appeals disposed of.
