Sajan Abraham v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 167; (2004) 4 SCC 441; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1299; AIR 2004 SC 2796; 2004 (2) Crimes 299; 2004 CRLJ 1757; 2004 (92) ECC 609; 2004 (113) ECR 594; 2004 (1) JCC 581; JT 2004 (3) SC 40; 2004 (2) KLT 122; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 137; 2004 (61) RLT 253; 2004(2) SCALE 715; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 653; 2004 (2) SLT 668; 2004 (2) Supreme 339; 2004 (2) UC 809

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Appeal is allowed, Personal Consumption

Summary: NDPS Act and Rules - s. 9 of the Act under which the Rules have been framed - if the possession of psychotropic substance is justified under a rule - no separate permit is required to be issued to the person possessing such psychotropic substance - rule itself permits possession of such psychotropic substance to the extent mentioned in the rule and subject to the conditions laid down therein - Hussain vs. State of Kerala (1999 Indlaw SC 1575) followed - no offence under Section 21 of the NDPS Act is made out - appeal allowed.
