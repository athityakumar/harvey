Parkash Chand v State Of H.P.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 582; (2004) 11 SCC 381; AIR 2004 SC 4496; JT 2004 (6) SC 302; 2004(6) SCALE 495; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s 302 - Operation of Exception 4 to s 300 - For application of Exception 4, it is not sufficient to show that there was a sudden quarrel and there was no premeditation - It must further be shown that offender has not taken undue advantage or acted in cruel or unusual manner - It must be proved that there was an intention to inflict that particular bodily injury which in ordinary course of nature, was sufficient to cause death - Held, conviction will be under s 304 Part I and not s 302 - Appeal disposed of.
