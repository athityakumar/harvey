Pawan Kumar Jain v Pradeshiya Industrial and Investment Corporation

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 586; (2004) 6 SCC 758; 2004 (57) ALR 190; 2004 (56) ALR 716; 2004 (3) AWC 2720; 2004 (4) BC 141; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 758; 2007 (3) CompLJ 533; 2004 (2) DRTC 815; JT 2004 (6) SC 305; 2004(6) SCALE 560; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 447; 2004 (2) UC 1129; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2366

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking and Finance - Uttar Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972 - Guarantor - Recovery Notice issued against guarantor - Notification specifying 1st Respondent-Corporation as a Financial Institution within meaning of term as defined in s 2(h) of Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Whether a Financial Institution within meaning of that term in Debt Recovery Act can proceed under U P Act; whether action against guarantor can be taken? - Held, as action was initiated prior to Notification being issued by Central Govt, action would not be barred - Action against guarantor cannot be taken until property of principal-debtor is first sold off - Recovery notice set aside - Appeals Disposed Of.
