National Insurance Co. Ltd. v Keshav Bahadur And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 51; (2004) 2 SCC 370; (2004) SCC (Cr) 558; 2004 (1) ACC 581; AIR 2004 SC 1581; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 24; 2004 (55) ALR 256; 2004 (2) AWC 945; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 940; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 500; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 68; JT 2004 (2) SC 282; 2004 (137) PLR 47; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 99; 2004(2) SCALE 126; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 861; 2004 (2) SLT 69; 2004 (1) Supreme 749

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fixed Liability

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 110, 110A, 110CC, 95, 95(2)(b)(i) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166, 171 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Award of Interest under Motor Vehicles Act - Higher rate - Discretionary - Once exercised - No scope for retrospective enhancement for default in payment of compensation - Retrospective enhancement - Amounts to levy of penalty - Not statutorily envisaged - Held rate of interest as awarded by HC shall alone be applicable till payment, without stipulation for higher rate of interest being enforced as directed by Tribunal.
