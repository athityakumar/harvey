Narayanan v Kumaran & Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 186; (2004) 4 SCC 26; 2004 (55) ALR 131; 2004 (2) AWC 1711; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 198; JT 2004 (5) SC 358; 2004 (2) KLT 312; 2004 (2) LCD 1555; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 60; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 548; 2004(3) SCALE 322; 2004 (2) SLT 951; 2004 (2) Supreme 486

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 23, O. 43 r. (1)(u), ss. 100, 104 - An appeal will lie from an order of remand only in those cases in which an appeal would lie against decree if Appellate Court instead of making an order of remand had passed a decree on strength of adjudication on which order of remand was passed - Whether an appeal would lie if order of remand where it is to be treated as a decree and not a mere order - Safe to adopt that appeal under order 43 Rule (1) cl (u) should be heard only on ground enumerated in Section 100 - Appellant under an appeal under order 43 Rule (1) cl (u) is not entitled to agitate questions of facts - In an appeal against an order of remand under this cl, HC can and should confine itself to such facts, conclusions and decisions which have a bearing on order of remand and cannot canvass all findings of facts arrived at by Lower Appellate Court - Petitions allowed.
