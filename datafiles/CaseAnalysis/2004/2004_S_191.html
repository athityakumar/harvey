Satyam Infoway Limited v Sifynet Solutions Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1061; (2004) 6 SCC 145; AIR 2004 SC 3540; 2004 (3) AWC 2366; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 729; JT 2004 (5) SC 541; 2004 (6) KarLJ 513; 2004 (28) PTC 566; 2004(5) SCALE 635; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Dishonest Adoption

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999 - Internet domain name - Trademark - Infringement - Respondent company was conducting internet based business using similar domain names as that of appellant - Appellant served notice on respondent to desist from carrying on business under said name and to transfer domain names to appellant, respondent refused - Appellant contended that respondent was passing off their business using appellant's domain names - Appellant filed suit against respondent - Civil court allowed temporary injunction as appellant was prior user of domain name and had created goodwill - HC allowed appeal - Whether respondent could be held liable for infringement of trademark under 1999 Act while using similar internet domain name as that of appellant - Held, respondent's assertion that its line of business was different from appellant's was incorrect and legally untenable - Deceptively similar domain names would lead to confusion and receipt of unsought for services - Court said only one name should remain - Respondent was allowed to carry on business and inform clients about change of names - Appellant was entitled to relief even if disruption of respondent's business could occur - Explanation of similar sounding names given by respondent was held doubtful - Respondent sought to cash in on appellant's reputation - Decision of HC set aside and that of civil court was affirmed - Appellant was granted relief - Appeal allowed.
