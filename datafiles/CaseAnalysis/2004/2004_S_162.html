Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board v Shiv Mohan Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1074; (2004) 8 SCC 402; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1141; AIR 2004 SC 5009; JT 2004 (8) SC 272; 2004 (3) KLT 686; 2004(8) SCALE 475; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 953; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 175

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Boilers Act, 1923, Air Force Act, 1950, Navy (Discipline) Act, 1934, Apprenticeship Rules 1992

Summary: [A.] Service - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(z), 6(N) - Apprenticeship Rules 1992 - Scope of - Whether registration of contract u/s. 4(4) of Apprentices Act is mandatory or directory? - If it is a mandatory, what is effect, if it is directory, then what is effect - Held, character of incumbent as an apprentice trainee cannot be changed as he owes his existence under Apprentices Act and after tenure of one year his services were bound to come to an end and he cannot convert this character of a trainee to an employee of Management - Services were terminated way back March 1987 and dispute was raised in 1994 - Appeals disposed of.
