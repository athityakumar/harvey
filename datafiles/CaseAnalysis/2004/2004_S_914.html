National Insurance Company Limited v Swaran Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, D.M. Dharmadhikari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Health & Drug; Insurance

Keywords: Arrears Of Land Revenue, Social Welfare Legislation

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s. 11, s. 96(2), s. 96(2)(b) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s.149(2)(a)(ii), s. 149(4) - Road Traffic Act, 1930 - Third Parties (Rights against Insurance) Act, 1930 - Motor Vehicles Rules, 1939, r. 16 - Interpretation - S.149(2)(a)(ii) vis-à-vis proviso appended to sub-ss (4) and (5) - Held, provisions of sub-ss (4) and (5) of s.149, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 may be considered as liability of Insurer to satisfy decree at first instance - Further held, Tribunal and court must exercise their jurisdiction to issue such a direction upon consideration of facts and circumstances of each case and in event such a direction has been issued despite arriving at a finding of fact to effect that insurer has been able to establish that insured has committed a breach of contract of insurance as envisaged u/s. 149(2)(a)(ii) of Act, insurance company shall be entitled to realise awarded amount from owner or driver of vehicle, as case may be, in execution of same award having regard to provisions of ss.165, 168 of Act - However, in event, having regard to limited scope of inquiry in proceedings before Tribunal it has not been able to do so, insurance company may initiate a separate action therefor against owner or driver of vehicle or both, as case may be - Those exceptional cases may arise when evidence becomes available to or comes to notice of insurer at a subsequent stage or for one reason or other, insurer was not given opportunity to defend at all - Such a course of action may also be resorted when a fraud or collusion between victim and owner of vehicle is detected or comes to knowledge of insurer at a later stage - Findings summarised.
