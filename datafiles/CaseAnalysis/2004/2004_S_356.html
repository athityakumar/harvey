Moti Lal v State Of M.P. (Now Chhattisgarh)

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 21; (2004) 2 SCC 469; (2004) SCC (Cr) 573; AIR 2004 SC 2909; 2004 (3) CalLT(SC) 30; 2004 (1) CCR 171; 2004 (1) Crimes 341; 2004 (2) CRJ 501; 2004 CRLJ 907; 2004 (1) JCC 278; JT 2004 (1) SC 353; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2004(1) SCALE 611; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 854; 2004 (1) SLT 549; 2004 (1) Supreme 518

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 34, 498-A - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 235(2), 309(2) - Harassment for dowry - Suicide - Held, evidence on record sufficiently makes out case of persistent and unabated harassment and acts of cruelty meted out to deceased by not only pestering her and her relatives to give more and more by way of additional dowry from time to time, but that she has been ill-treated physically and consequently challenge made to concurrent findings is not only bereft of substance but does not merit countenance quantum of sentence, keeping in view serious nature of offences, also cannot be said to be on higher side, for showing any further leniency - Further held, appellant cannot make any legitimate grievance at any rate on alleged non-compliance with s 235(2), CrPC, 1973.
