Mahadeorao Sukaji Shivankar v Ramaratan Bapu and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 611; (2004) 7 SCC 181; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 22; 2004 (3) AWC 2675; 2005 (1) Bom.C.R. 155; JT 2004 (6) SC 393; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 228; 2004(6) SCALE 631; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Election petition - High Court granted permission to furnish material particulars of corrupt practices as alleged in petition - Whether petitioner had set out material facts in the election petition? - Held, petitioner stated the details as to corrupt practices adopted by returned candidate - it was incumbent on petitioner to serve a copy of application to returned candidate or to his counsel - It was also duty of Court to grant time to returned candidate and contesting respondents to submit reply, if any, to pass an appropriate order in accordance with law - Since that has not been adopted by High Court, order deserved to be quashed and set aside - Remitted matter back to High Court.
