Dr. Suresh Gupta v Govt. Of N.C.T. Of Delhi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 563; (2004) 6 SCC 422; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1785; AIR 2004 SC 4091; 2004 CCC 128; 2004 (2) CGLJ 273; 2004 (3) CompLJ 271; 2004 CRLJ 3870; 2004 (112) DLT 866; JT 2004 (6) SC 238; 2004 (3) KLT 14; 2004 (138) PLR 375; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 925; 2004(6) SCALE 432; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 323; 2004 (2) UC 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, s 304A - Medical - Criminal proceedings - Liability of Doctor for causing death of his patient - Whether HC was right in holding that criminal liability prima facie has arisen against surgeon and he must face trial? - Held, when a patient agrees to go for medical treatment or surgical operation, every careless act of medical man cannot be termed as 'criminal' - It can be termed 'criminal' only when medical man exhibits a gross lack of competence or inaction and wanton indifference to his patient's safety and which is found to have arisen from gross ignorance or gross negligence - Mere inadvertence or some degree of want of adequate care and caution might create civil liability but would not suffice to hold him criminally liable - No case of recklessness or gross negligence made out against doctor - Quashed criminal proceedings pending - Appeal allowed.
