Exphar Sa & Anr v Eupharma Laboratories Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 160; (2004) 3 SCC 688; AIR 2004 SC 1682; 2004 (2) AWC 1515; 2004 (73) DRJ 663; JT 2004 (3) SC 1; 2004 (2) MLJ 156; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 156; 2004 (28) PTC 251; 2004 (1) RAJ 600; 2004(2) SCALE 589; 2004 (2) SLT 164; 2004 (2) Supreme 146

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: [A.] Intellectual Property - Copyright Act, 1957, ss. 62, 62(2) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 10, O. 7 r. 11, s. 20 - Word 'include' in s.62(2) shows that jurisdiction for purposes of Section 62 is wider than that of Court as prescribed under CPC, 1908 - Object and reason was not to restrict owners of copyright to exercise their rights but to remove any impediment from their doing so - It prescribes an additional ground for attracting jurisdiction of a Court over and above 'normal' grounds as laid down in Section 20 of CPC - Cease and desist notice means that recipient is alleged to have infringed rights of sender of notice to copyright and as a result of such alleged infringement recipient is liable to institution of civil and/ or criminal proceedings - Was received within jurisdiction of HC sufficient to invoke jurisdiction of that Court - There was neither an application for rejection of plaint nor for return of plaint u/o. 7, rr. 10 and 11 - Order of DB directing return of plaint was wholly outside scope of pending appeals - Ex-facie contradictions in judgment - Matter remanded.
