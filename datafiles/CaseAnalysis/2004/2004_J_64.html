Tarak Singh and another Appellants with Dipak K v Jyoti Basu and others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1003; (2005) 1 SCC 201; AIR 2005 SC 338; 2005 (1) AWC 530; JT 2004 (10) SC 606; 2004 (3) KLT 1060; 2004(9) SCALE 493; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Allotment of Govt. Land from discretionary quota of Chief Minister - Allotting a plot of land in favour of Justice B P Banerjee from his discretionary quota in Salt Lake City - Whether allotment is unconstitutional? - Held, Chief Minister passed an order on 24/7/1987 allotting a plot in favour of Justice B P Banerjee, on which date also matter was pending before him - By no stretch of imagination it can be termed as coincidence - There is undoubtedly an unholy nexus in between passing of judicial order and granting order of allotment - Conduct of Judge is beyond condonable limits - Allotment quashed.
