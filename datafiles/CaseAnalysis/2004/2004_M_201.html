Collector of Central Excise, Meerut v Maruti Foam Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 150; (2004) 6 SCC 722; 2004 (164) E.L.T. 394; 2004 (60) RLT 600; 2006 (144) STC 161

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Trade

Keywords: Ad Valorem

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57A, 8(1) - Departmental Clarification vis-a-vis SC decision - Interpretation of phrases - Regardless of interpretation place on such phrases by Court, if during relevant time there were circulars issued by C B E C which place a different interpretation, that interpretation would be binding on Department till it is withdrawn - Waste, parings and scrap arise during manufacture of Polyurethane blocks entitled to benefit of Notification 54/88-C E, even after its amendment in 1989 - Though duty paid in respect of manufactured product is nil Department bound to extend benefit of notification in light of Circulars issued by C B E C which were in operation for relevant period and construing phrase 'duty already paid' mentioned in Condition 1 against Serial No 3 of notification to include 'nil rate of duty' - Circular though at variance with SC decision still binding on department so long as it is not withdrawn.
