Dani Singh And Ors. v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 197; (2004) 13 SCC 203; (2005) SCC (Cr) 127; AIR 2004 SC 4570; 2004 CRLJ 3328; JT 2004 (3) SC 367; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 538; 2004(3) SCALE 245; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1138; 2004 (2) UC 773

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 141, 147, 148, 149, 201, 302, 34, 380, 436 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 319 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 5 - Common intention - Act committed in furtherance of - It is of no avail to argue that no overt act was committed by some of accused - Held on facts that each of accused had a role to play - Objective findings of IO in line with evidence - Lack of independent witness not fatal in this case where village was faction ridden - No reason to interfere - Appeals dismissed.
