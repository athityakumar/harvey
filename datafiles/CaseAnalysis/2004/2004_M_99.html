United India Insurance Co; Ltd. v M/s. Harchand Rai Chandan Lal

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1480; (2004) 8 SCC 644; AIR 2004 SC 4794; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 112; 2004 (57) ALR 713; 2004 (4) AWC 3389; 2004 (15) CCC 451; 2004 (4) CompLJ 443; 2004 (4) CPJ 15; 2004 (4) CPJ(Sc) 15; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 101; JT 2004 (8) SC 8; 2004 (3) KLT 560; 2004 (138) PLR 865; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 217; 2004(8) SCALE 161B; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 662

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Criminal

Summary: Insurance - Consumer Protection - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 380 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether in terms of policy, repudiation of claim of respondent by appellant company is justified or not? - It is settled law that terms of contract has to be strictly read and natural meaning be given to it - No outside aid should be sought unless meaning is ambiguous - Held, it is possible that an insurer may sustain loss in technical terms of criminal law, but no relief can be given to him unless his case is covered by terms of policy - It is not open to interpret expression appearing in policy in terms of common law; but it has to give meaning to expression as defined in policy - Therefore, when definition of word 'burglary' has been defined in policy then cause should fall within that definition - Once a party has agreed to a particular definition, he is bound by it and definition of criminal law will be of no avail - Further held, theft should have preceded with force or violence as per terms of insurance policy - In order to substantiate a claim an insurer has to establish that theft or burglary took place preceding with force or violence and if it is not, then insurance company will be well within their right to repudiate claim of insurer - Appeal allowed.
