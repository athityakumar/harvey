Ipca Laboratory Ltd. v Deputy Commissioner Of Income Tax, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 195; (2004) 12 SCC 742; AIR 2004 SC 3046; 2004 (187) CTR 513; [2004] 266 ITR 521; JT 2004 (3) SC 295; 2004(3) SCALE 214; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trade

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.80 HHC - Has been incorporated with a view to providing incentive to export houses - Though a liberal interpretation has to be given to such a provision interpretation has to be as per wordings of this Section - Plain reading of sub-section (c) shows that 'profits from such exports' has to be profits of exports of self manufactured goods plus profits of exports of trading goods - Profit is to be calculated in manner laid down in 3(c)(i) and (ii) - Deduction can be permitted only if there is a positive profit in exports of both self manufactured goods as well as trading goods - Also held s.80 AB has been given an overriding effect over all other Sections in Chapter VIA - S 80 HHC does not provide that its provisions are to prevail over Section 80 AB or over any other provision of Act - Section 80 HHC would thus be governed by Section 80 AB.
