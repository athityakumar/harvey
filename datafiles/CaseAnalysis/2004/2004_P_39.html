State of Karnataka v Papanaika and others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 871; (2004) 13 SCC 180; (2005) SCC (Cr) 104; AIR 2004 SC 4967; 2004 CRLJ 4667; JT 2004 (9) SC 161; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 992; 2004(8) SCALE 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Eye-Witness, Question Of Fact And Law, Charge - Sheet

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149, 341 - Conviction by Trial Court - HC acquitted all accused - Appeal against acquittal - Held, HC not examined statement of witnesses and just on a bald statement that when prosecution version has been accepted in full and witnesses have tendency to over implicate, then what is guarantee that other part is also true - Division Bench disbelieved entire prosecution evidence - Approach of HC, is not correct - There should be proper appreciation of evidence and finding has to be recorded against each witness as to why the said witness is not being believed when he was believed by trial court - Testimony of witnesses fully substantiate prosecution case - Appeal allowed.
