Vidhya Devi & Anr. v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 17; (2004) 9 SCC 476; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1473; AIR 2004 SC 1757; 2004 (1) CCR 178; 2004 (1) Crimes 320; 2004 (2) CRJ 624; 2004 (1) JCC 282; JT 2004 (1) SC 609; 2004(1) SCALE 616; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 846; 2004 (1) SLT 629; 2004 (1) Supreme 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304-B, 34, 498-A - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act, 1983 - Dowry death - HC affirmed conviction imposed by Trial Court - Death took place within 7 years of marriage - Direct and substantial evidence - Held, before her unnatural death, deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment both for and in connection with a demand for dowry - Further held, facts brought on record further prove existence of a proximate and live link between effect of cruelty related to dowry demand and concerned death expression 'soon before' is a relative term which requires to be construed in context of specific circumstances of each case and no hard and fast rules of any universal application can be laid down by fixing any time limit - Sentence confimred.
