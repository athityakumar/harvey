State of Andhra Pradesh v V. Venkateswara Rao (Dead) By Lr

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1152; (2004) 4 SCC 220; AIR 2004 SC 2900; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 99; 2004 (54) ALR 480; 2004 (2) AWC 1190; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 315; 2004(1) SCALE 409; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 538

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 6, 8 and 20(1) - Whether or not, condition that after lease period of 33 years, land would vest in state Govt. contained in para 4(d) of GOMs no. 7, could be sustained? - GOMs no. 7 exempted excess vacant land in question from provisions of Chap. III of Act by imposing conditions including a condition that after lease period expires, land so exempted would vest in Govt. along with structures; since excess land was not acquired and no notification was published in official gazette declaring excess land in question be deemed to have been acquired by State Govt., it could not be deemed to have vested absolutely in state Govt. free from all encumbrances u/s. 10(3) of Act as on date exemption order was issued - After expiry of lease period, in normal course, land would revert back to LR of respondent in absence of vesting land in State absolutely, subject to provisions of Chap. III to extent they are applicable and if on account of reverting land to respondents, their holding exceeded ceiling limit, s. 15 get attracted and as per s. 15(2), provisions of ss. 6 to 14 shall, so far as may be, apply to statement filed under said section and person concerned could avail rights and protections available to him u/ss. 8 to 10 including exercise of option or choice in matter of retaining land within ceiling limit - If condition contained in cl. (d) of para 4 of Govt. order is sustained, it has effect of taking away rights and protections available u/ss. 6 to 14 as far as they apply - Held, condition contained in para 4(d) of Govt. order cannot be sustained as such conditions cannot run contrary to or defeat provisions of Act - Also, condition contained in para 4(d) is separable and even after setting aside said condition, Govt. order can be validly sustained - Appeals dismissed.
