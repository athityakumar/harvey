Ajay Gandhi And Anr. v B. Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 28; (2004) 2 SCC 120; AIR 2004 SC 1391; 2004 (114) ECR 774; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 257; [2004] 265 ITR 451; JT 2004 (1) SC 211; 2004(1) SCALE 84; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 164; 2004 (1) SLT 483; 2004 (1) Supreme 319; 179 TAXATION 433; [2004] 134 TAXMAN 537

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delegation of Financial Powers Rules, 1958

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 252, 254, 255 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 5A - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Delegation of Financial Powers Rules, 1958, r. 10 - Powers of president of ITAT - Central Govt. had acted sub silentio - Allowed President to delegate his power to constitute benches to various Senior Vice-Presidents over a number of years - Central Govt. was also of opinion that power of transfer and posting is a part of administrative function of President - An ancillary power of constitution of benches - Independence of Tribunal is essential - For maintaining its independence - Any power which may be conferred upon executive authority must be proved to be in interest of imparting justice - However President cannot be said to have an unguided, unfettered and unlimited jurisdiction as same may be flawed with great consequences - Guidelines issued.
