Commissioner of Income Tax v Sesa Goa Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1051; (2004) 13 SCC 548; [2004] 271 ITR 331; [2005] 142 TAXMAN 16; 2005 (192) TTJ 577

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 32A, 80-I, 33(1)(b), 32A and 32A(2)(b)(iii) - Whether Income Tax Appellate Tribunal was justified in holding that assessee is entitled to deduction of investment allowance u/s. 32A of Act in respect of machinery used in mining activity ignoring fact that assessee is engaged in extraction and processing of iron ore, not amounting to manufacture or production of any article or thing? - Held, from wide definition of word 'production', it has to follow that mining activity for purpose of production of mineral ores would come within ambit of word 'production' since ore is 'a thing', which is result of human activity or effort - Other provisions of Act, particularly s. 33(1)(b)(B) r/w Item No. 3 of Fifth Schedule to Act, would show that mining of ore is treated as 'production' - Language of these sections is similar to language of s. 32 A(2) - There is no reason to assume that word 'production' was used in a different sense in s. 32A - Extraction and processing of iron ore amounts to 'production' within meaning of word in s. 32A(2)(b)(iii) of Act and, consequently, assessee is entitled to benefit of s. 32A(1) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
