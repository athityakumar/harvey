Cholan Roadways Limited v G. Thirugnanasambandam

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1069; (2005) 3 SCC 241; (2005) SCC (L&S) 395; 2005 (1) ACC 143; AIR 2005 SC 570; 2005 (1) CLR 524; 2005 (104) FLR 440; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 196; JT 2005 (1) SC 116; 2005 (1) LLN 633; 2004(10) SCALE 578; 2005 (1) SLR 612; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1123; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 817

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11, 33(2)(b) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 19 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173-Q - Dismissal - Rash and negligent driving - Tribunal rejected grant of approval sought for as regard order of dismissal on ground that domestic enquiry conducted cannot be considered as fair and proper and is vitiated by not examining passengers - Whether stand taken by Tribunal right in law? - Held, Tribunal did not apply principle of Res ipsa loquitur which was relevant for purpose of this case and, thus, failed to take into consideration a relevant factor - Industrial Tribunal should have granted approval of order of punishment.
