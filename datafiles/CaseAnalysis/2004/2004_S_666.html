Tata Motors Limited v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 376; (2004) 5 SCC 783; AIR 2004 SC 3618; 2005 (1) Bom.C.R. 444; JT 2004 (5) SC 532; 2004(5) SCALE 649; 2004 (136) STC 1; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 42 - Maharashtra Sales Tax Laws [Levy, Amendment and Repeal] Act, 1989, ss. 26, 27 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Bombay Sales Tax Rules, 1959, rr. 41D, 41E, 44D - Bombay Sales Tax (Amendment) Rules, 1992, r. 41-E - Constitutional validity of retrospective amendment of r.41-E of Bombay Sales Tax Rules - No material has been placed before Court as to why amendments were confined only to a period of eight years and not either before or subsequently and, therefore, impugned provision, deserves to be quashed by striking down words 'not being waste goods or scrap goods or by products' - Requirement under Rule 41-D that assessee concerned has to register in place to which goods are 'exported' under Central Sales Tax Act - It is open to appellant to carry on his business elsewhere and claim benefit in a place where Central Sales Tax Act is applicable.
