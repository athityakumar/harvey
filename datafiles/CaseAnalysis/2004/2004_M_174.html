Manda Jaganath v K.S.Rathnam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 299; (2004) 7 SCC 492; AIR 2004 SC 3600; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 67; JT 2004 (5) SC 8; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 810; 2004(4) SCALE 600; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute

Summary: [A.] Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 329, 329(b) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 100(1)(d)(iv), 36, 81 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 4 - Jurisdiction of HC to entertain a WP after election process had started - Whether Returning Officer justified in rejecting Form B submitted by first respondent or not, is not a matter for HC to decide in exercise of its writ jurisdiction - This issue should be agitated by an aggrieved party in an election petition only - Set aside impugned order of HC.
