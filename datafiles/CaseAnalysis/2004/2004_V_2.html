Inspector of Police, Vigilance and v V. Jayapaul

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 211; (2004) 5 SCC 223; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1607; AIR 2004 SC 2684; 2004 CRLJ 1819; JT 2004 (3) SC 633; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 317; 2004(3) SCALE 507

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1950, TADA Act, Arms Act, Prevention of Corruption Act 1988

Summary: Quashing of criminal proceedings - Investigation by the same officer who 'lodged' FIR on basis of information received - did not have personal knowledge of the suspected offences nor did he participate in any operations connected with the offences - No illegality in the process of investigation - Jurisdiction to investigate offence under Prevention of Corruption Act not the ground which was urged before High Court or even in SLP or in arguments advanced at the time of hearing - Held, this Court not inclined to deal with that question - appeal allowed.
