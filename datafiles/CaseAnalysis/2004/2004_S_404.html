Ashok Leland Ltd v State Of Tamil Nadu And Anr

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2; (2004) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 2836; JT 2004 (1) SC 289; 2004(1) SCALE 224; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 306; 2004 (134) STC 473

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Unregistered Dealer, Stock Transfer, Liability To Tax, Resale, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Subsequent Sale, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 2001, Kerala Sales Tax Act, 1957, Andhra Pradesh High Court (Original Side), Rules, Citizenship Rules, 1956 (As amended upto 20-4-2000)

Summary: [A] Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - ss. 6A, 9(2) - Interpretation of - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 14, 19(1)(g), Chapter XIII - Constitutional validity - Assessment - Penalty - Held, the order of an authority under s. 6A is conclusive for all practical purposes - Further held, the appellants would be entitled to move the High Court for ventilating their grievances. However, if the Central Government creates a new forum, it would be open to them to approach the same. [B] Legal fiction - Whether a legal fiction can be applied to determine whether a particular interstate transaction amounted to an interstate sale or a mere transfer of stock - Held, whenever a legal fiction is created by a statute, the same shall be given full effect.
