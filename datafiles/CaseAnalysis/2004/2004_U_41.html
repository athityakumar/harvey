Vijay Shekhar & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 431; (2004) 4 SCC 666; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1403; AIR 2004 SC 3976; 2004 (4) CHN 48; 2004 CRLJ 3857; 2004 (3) G.L.R. 1932; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 523; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 320; 2004(5) SCALE 118; 2004 (2) UC 1186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non-bailable Warrant

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - Abuse of process of Court - Validity of complaint filed in Court of Metropolitan Magistrate, Court No 10, Ahmedabad - Complaint in question is a product of fraud and a total abuse of process of court - There is also serious doubt whether procedure required under CrPC was really followed by Magistrate at all while taking cognizance of offence alleged - Inherent falsehood that could be ex facie noticed from contents of complaint and coupled with fact admitted by parties to this petition, it is evident that complaint is a fraudulent one and liable to be quashed based on legal principle that an act in fraud is ab initio void.
