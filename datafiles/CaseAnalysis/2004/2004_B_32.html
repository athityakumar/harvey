State Of West Bengal And Ors v Babu Chakraborty

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 709; (2004) 12 SCC 201; AIR 2004 SC 4324; JT 2004 (7) SC 216; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 252; 2004 (64) RLT 365; 2004(7) SCALE 387; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Expunge

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 42(1)(2), 55, 57, 69 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 102 - Search - Heroin - Seizure - Conviction - - Sustainability - On obtaining of secret information raid was conducted at house of respondent/accused under supervision of appellant no. 2 accompanied by appellant no. 3 and seized 3 gms, 25 mgs of Heroin and accused was arrested - Trial Court convicted accused - Accused filed appeal before HC - HC allowed appeal and acquitted accused and - Appellants aggrieved by this judgment, Special Leave Petition was filed and leave was granted and interim stay of direction regarding payment of Rs. 1 lakh as compensation was also made - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that HC was not justified in setting aside Trial Court's judgment and directing State to pay compensation of Rs. 1 lakh to respondent reserving liberty to State Govt. to realize same from appellant no. 2 - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, impugned judgment directing State Govt. to pay Rs. 1 lakh as compensation to accused was great prejudice to State and HC had omitted to take note of fact that action taken under the Act in good faith was protected u/s. 69 of the Act - Hence, HC by pronouncing impugned judgment had not followed parameters laid down by SC in as much as when appellant before HC, accused had not taken plea attributing motives to appellants nos. 2 and 3 - However HC was right in giving finding that non-compliance of mandatory statutory provisions vitiates prosecution - Hence, set aside part of impugned judgment ordering compensation to accused and launch prosecution against appellant no. 2 u/s. 58 of Act - Appeal partly allowed.
