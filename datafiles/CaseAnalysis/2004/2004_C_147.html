Shri Bhagwan Lal Arya v Commissioner Of Police Delhi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 208; (2004) 4 SCC 560; (2004) SCC (L&S) 661; AIR 2004 SC 2131; 2004 (2) CLR 6; 2004 (110) DLT 360; 2004 (101) FLR 193; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 107; JT 2004 (3) SC 384; 2004 (2) LLN 7; 2004(3) SCALE 356; 2004 (3) SLR 70; 2004 (2) SLT 601; 2004 (2) Supreme 677; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1294

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Delhi Police (Punishment & Appeal Rules, 1980), rr. 10, 8, 8(a) - Domestic enquiry has been conducted properly and principles of natural justice has been strictly followed - No denial of reasonable opportunity - No interference warranted - Disciplinary action initiated against appellant is in accordance with rules and regulations and not vitiated by any mala fides - However punishment is totally disproportionate to proved misconduct of appellant - Absence of appellant on medical grounds with application for leave as well as sanction of leave can under no circumstances be termed as grave misconduct or continued misconduct rendering him unfit for police service - Punishment set aside - Mitigating circumstances which warrant that punishment / order of disciplinary authority is to be set aside - Appellant reinstated.
