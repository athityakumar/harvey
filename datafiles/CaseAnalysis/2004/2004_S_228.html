Vasantiben Prahladji Nayak and Others v Somnath Muijibhai Nayak and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1448; (2004) 3 SCC 376; AIR 2004 SC 1893; 2004 (55) ALR 145; 2004 (2) AWC 1784; 2004 (3) CHN 60; 2004 (2) CLR 324; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1335; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 272; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 511; 2004 (137) PLR 303; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 276; 2004(3) SCALE 172; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 100 - (A) Whether appellants became owners of suit land as reversioner under registered deed of partition D/- 29-11-1965 and consequently suit filed by appellants was neither barred by limitation nor by adverse possession? - Held, starting point of limitation for adverse possession cannot be taken as 29-11-1965 and one has to take date when respondents' possession became adverse - (B) Whether respondents have failed to prove ouster along with other three circumstances, namely, hostile intention, long and uninterrupted possession and exercise of right of exclusive ownership openly and to knowledge of owner? - Held, concurrent findings of fact recorded by courts below on issue of adverse possession, there is no reason to interfere in matter - Appeal dismissed.
