State Represented By Inspector of Police and v N.M.T. Joy Immaculate

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 398; (2004) 5 SCC 729; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1722; AIR 2004 SC 2282; 2004 CRLJ 2515; 2004 (3) G.L.R. 2174; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 431; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 322; 2004(5) SCALE 330; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 363 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 160, 161, 167, 173(2), 197, 209, 309, 313, 397, 397(1), 397(2) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20(3) - Whether revision petition was maintainable against an interlocutory order of Metropolitan Magistrate granting police custody of accused for one day - HC held that consequent confession and alleged recovery have no evidentiary value and awarded a compensation of Rs. 1 lakh - HC at that stage could not have gone into merits of prosecution case as if hearing an appeal against an order of conviction or acquittal as trial of accused is yet to begin - Set aside judgment of HC.
