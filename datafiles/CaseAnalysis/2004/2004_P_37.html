Shyam Sundar Sarma v Pannalal Jaiswal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 903; (2005) 1 SCC 436; AIR 2005 SC 226; 2005 (58) ALR 670; 2005 (1) ARC 1; 2005 (1) AWC 410; 2005 (4) Bom.C.R. 839; 2005 (181) E.L.T. 163; JT 2004 (9) SC 436; 2005 (1) KLT 198; 2005 (2) LCD 1425; 2005 (1) MahLJ 340; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 165; 2005 (1) MPLJ 6; 2005 (141) PLR 92; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 756; 2004(9) SCALE 270; 2005 (Supp) RD 163; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 931; 2005 (1) UC 297

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. XLIII r. 1 and O. IX r. 13 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 3 and 5 - Whether an appeal accompanied by an application for condoning the delay in filing the appeal is an appeal in the eye of law, when the application for condoning the delay in filing the appeal is dismissed and consequently the appeal is dismissed as being time barred by limitation, in view of s.3 of the Limitation Act? - Held, appellate court and HC have rightly held that petition under O.IX r. 13 would not lie in view of filing of an appeal against decree by appellant and dismissal of appeal though for default, since a dismissal for default or on ground of it being barred by limitation cannot be equated with a withdrawal of appeal - Appeal dismissed.
