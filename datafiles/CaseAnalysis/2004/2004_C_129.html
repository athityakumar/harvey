Bannari Amman Sugars Limited v Commercial Tax Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1013; (2005) 1 SCC 625; 2005 (58) ALR 357; 2004 (192) CTR 492; JT 2004 (10) SC 500; 2005 (1) KLT 601; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 129; 2004(9) SCALE 604; 2005 (139) STC 86; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 264

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 166, 166(1), 299 - Purchase tax exemption - Discontinuance of purchase tax exemption in case of mills which exceeded ceiling of Rs. 300 lakhs during period of five years - Doctrine of promissory estoppel - Whether withdrawal of benefits extended to appellants as subsidy was in order? - Held, no specific grounds or reasons were indicated to justify withdrawal in affidavits filed before Tribunal or HC - As correctness of factual basis justifying withdrawal is in issue, fair play certainly warranted grant of opportunity to appellants to present its side of picture - HC not dealt with issue that there was no scope for retrospective withdrawal of benefit by an executive order - Matter remitted to HC for fresh consideration.
