M/S. T.T.G. Industries Ltd., Madras v Collector Of Central Excise, Raipur

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 404; (2004) 4 SCC 751; AIR 2004 SC 3422; 2004 (94) ECC 65; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 501; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 97; 2004 (62) RLT 475; 2004(5) SCALE 585; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 659

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 3 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 3 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(25) - Whether, demand of duty having been raised beyond period of six months is barred by limitation; whether processes undertaken by appellant at Bhilai for erection of mudguns and drilling machines resulted in emergence of goods leviable to excise duty or whether it resulted in erection of immovable property and not 'goods' - On manner in which these machines are erected and installed upon concrete structures, they do not answer description of 'goods' within meaning of term in Excise Act - Appellant not liable to pay excise duty.
