Samir K. Shah and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 711; (2005) 10 SCC 134; 2004 (2) DRTC 1091; 2004(7) SCALE 491

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Recovery Officer, Debt Recovery of Tribunals, Maharashtra & Goa, Regulations of Practice, 2003

Summary: Banking and Finance - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, s 29 - Income Tax Act, 1961, rr 52, 53 (II Schedule); Debt Recovery of Tribunals, Maharashtra & Goa, Regulations of Practice, 2003, reg. 60 - Reg. 60 whether contrary to s. 29 of Recovery Act r/w rr. 52 and 53 of sch. II to IT Act? - Rules do not require the grant of any opportunity to the debtor of being heard before the valuation is made and the reserve price fixed - Debtor is entitled to notice only for the drawing up of the proclamation sale - Intention is to keep the debtor informed of the steps taken by the creditor to realize a fair value of the debtor's property - No requirement for the creditor to consider any alternative valuation filed at the instance of the debtor - No corresponding provision in Rules 52 or 53 of the Schedule to the Income Tax Act, 1961 or in any other provision which have been incorporated into the Act by s 29 - Held, reg. 60 is not violative of s. 29 of the Act - Petition disposed of.
