Bhanu Kumar Jain v Archana Kumar & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1072; (2005) 1 SCC 787; AIR 2005 SC 626; 2005 (58) ALR 483; 2005 (1) AWC 609; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 114; JT 2005 (1) SC 220; 2005 (1) KLT 456; 2005 (2) MahLJ 839; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; 2005 (3) MPLJ 3; 2005 (140) PLR 549; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 781B; 2005 (1) RD 466; 2004(10) SCALE 610; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1104; 2005 (1) UC 402

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 43 r. 1(d), O. 9 r. 13, O. 9 r. 7, s. 96(2) - Remedies available to a defendant in event of an ex-parte decree being passed against him in terms of O 9 r.13 and extent and limitation thereof - Whether First Appeal was maintainable despite fact that an application under O 9, r.13 of Code was dismissed? - Held, when an application under O 9, r.13 is dismissed, defendant can only avail a remedy available there against, to prefer an appeal in terms of O 43, r 1 - Once such an appeal is dismissed, Appellant cannot raise same contention in First Appeal - Case remitted to HC for consideration of case of parties.
