C. M. Beena and Another v P. N. Ramachandra Rao

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1462; (2004) 3 SCC 595; AIR 2004 SC 2103; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 108; 2004 (55) ALR 714; 2004 (2) ARC 1; 2004 (3) AWC 2226; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 107; JT 2004 (4) SC 288; 2004 (2) KLT 336; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 494; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 520; 2004(3) SCALE 497

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882-Section 105-Indian Easements Act, 1882 - Section 52 - Lease and licence-Difference between them to be determined by finding out real intention of parties decipherable from document, if any, executed between parties and surrounding circumstances-In licence only right to use property in particular way given to occupant while owner retains control or possession over premises-Nature of suit premises was shop located in busy commercial market-Appellant having exclusive possession over premises-Owner neither can nor does interfere-Business carried on by appellant in premises since 1972-Appellant in possession for about 20 years before deed of licence in 1981-Inspite of such deed, appellant continued to possess, use and enjoy occupation of premises as before-On expiry of so-called licence in 1982, respondent did not insist on appellant to give possession-Possession or control not returned by respondent owner - Hence, relationship between parties was that of landlord and tenant-And possession of appellant was that of tenant-Suit on plea that appellant was licensee was liable to be dismissed.
