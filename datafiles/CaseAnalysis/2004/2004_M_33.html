M. Subba Reddy and Another v A.P. State Road Transport Corporation & Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 407; (2004) 6 SCC 729; (2004) SCC (L&S) 887; AIR 2004 SC 3517; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 71; 2004 (102) FLR 235; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 250; 2004 (2) LLN 829; 2004(4) SCALE 824; 2004 (3) SLR 583; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 7; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2300

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Service

Summary: Service - Road Transport Corporation Act,1950, s.45 - AndhraPradesh. State Road Transport Corporation Employees (Recruitment) Regulations, 1966 - Direct and temporary recruitment - Seniority list - Challenged - Validity - There was ban on recruitment by respondent no.1/ APSRTC - Due to ban recruitments were done on temporary /adhoc basis and later would promote them and regularize them based on requirement - Appellant was appointed as traffic apprentice in respondent no.1 and was promoted temporarily in 1983 as Assistant Traffic Manager (ATM) and was regularized in 1986 and confirmed in 1987 - Ban was lifted in 1988 and direct recruitments were made for posts of ATM and AME - Respondent no.1 integrated seniority list dt.10-11-1994 showed appellant as junior to direct recruits who joined duties in 1988 and 1990 - Aggrieved appellant had challenged seniority list in HC and was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants were justified in challenging seniority list published by respondent no.1 - Held, service regn.3 r/w regns.30 and 34 of Regulations indicates that seniority was reckonable from date of appointment to service or grade - Appellants were promoted originally subject to conditions envisaged in regn. 34 and so could not claim seniority by ignoring said regulations based on their officiating services - Appellants were promoted on temporary basis u/regn.30 that period of officiation would not give any right over direct recruits in future - Regn.34 protects quota prescribed for direct recruits - Neither date of promotion nor date of selection was criteria for fixation of seniority and fixation of seniority depends on number of vacancies falling in particular category - Regulations prescribe quota of 1:1 which leads to rota for confirmation and fixation of seniority under above regulations would depend upon number of vacancies against which promotees became due for promotion - SC in S.G. Jaisinghani v. Union of India & Ors,1967 Indlaw SC 280 held that when fixed quota exists between two sources of recruitment it was not open to govt. to alter or deviate from quota - SC in Union of India & Ors. v. S.D. Gupta & Ors, 1996 Indlaw SC 3820 held that order of seniority had to be determined with reference to rota and quota prescribed under rules and there would be no illegality even when promotees were pushed downwards in order of seniority - Regns.30 and 34 mention that temporary promotees were liable to be reverted as and when direct recruits were available and promotees were liable to be replaced by direct recruits - In absence of direct recruitment appellants would not have got seniority over direct recruits - Where there was inaction on part of State Govt. or employer or imposed ban on direct recruitment in filling up posts meant for direct recruits it could not be held that quota had broken down - There was no merit in appellants and seniority list of respondents was justified - Appeals dismissed.
