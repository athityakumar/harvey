Krishna Bahadur v M/S Purna Theatre & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 681; (2004) 8 SCC 229; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1086; AIR 2004 SC 4282; 2004 (4) AWC 2950; JT 2004 (7) SC 13; 2004 (4) LLN 40; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 158; 2004(7) SCALE 235; 2004 (5) SLR 791; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 833; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2657

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.25F (b) and 25G - West Bengal Industrial Disputes Rules, r. 77A - Retrenchment - Within a very short period after workman was ordered to be reinstated with full back wages and incidental benefits by virtue of an award by the Seventh Industrial Tribunal - Retrenchment, whether proper? - Whether r. 77A complied with? - Evident that the question of a bona fide action on the part of the employer or waiver on the part of the appellant herein had not been raised - Question of substantial compliance or bonafide action on the part of the said respondent was not raised - Estoppel is not a cause of action; it is a rule of evidence; waiver is contractual and may constitute a cause of action; it is an agreement between the parties and a party fully knowing of its rights has agreed not to assert a right for a consideration -right can be waived by the party for whose benefit certain requirements or conditions had been provided for by a statute subject to the condition that no public interest is involved therein - Statutory right, however, may also be waived by his conduct, held on facts, division bench was in error - Appeal allowed.
