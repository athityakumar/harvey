Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh-I v Mahaan Dairies

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 306; (2004) 11 SCC 798; 2004 (166) E.L.T. 23; 2004 (61) RLT 382; 2005(9) SCALE 595

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Exemption - Notification no. 8/98-CE, D/- 02-06-1998 - Whether respondents have used brand name or trade name of some other company, and in view of same, whether they are entitled to exemption of said notification? - Even if goods are different, as long as trade name or brand name of some other company is used, benefit of notification not available - Once a trade name or brand name is used, then mere use of additional words would not enable party to claim benefit of notification - Respondents contended that have applied for registration of Mark 'Mahaan Taste Maker' which is different from already registered mark 'mahaan' - Held, when respondents get their mark registered, they shall become entitled to benefit of aforesaid notification - Appeal disposed of.
