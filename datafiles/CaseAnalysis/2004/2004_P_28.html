Commissioner Of Income Tax v P.V.A.L. Kulandagan Chettiar (Dead) Through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 462; (2004) 6 SCC 235; AIR 2004 SC 3411; 2004 (189) CTR 193; [2004] 267 ITR 654; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 526; 2004(6) SCALE 36; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 697; 181 TAXATION 557; [2004] 137 TAXMAN 460

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 90 - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1974 - Investment Incentive Act, 1968, ss. 21, 22, 26 - Income Tax Act, 1967 - Supplementary Income Tax Act, 1967 - Petroleum (Income Tax) Act, 1967 - Avoidance of Double Taxation of Income and Prevention of Fiscal Evasion of Tax - Whether Malaysian income cannot be subjected to tax in India in basis of agreement of avoidance of double taxation entered into between Govt. of India and Govt. of Malaysia; Whether capital gains should be taxable only in country in which assets are situated - Held, business income out of rubber plantations cannot be taxed in India because of closer economic relations between assessee and Malaysia in which property is located and where permanent establishment has been set up will determine fiscal domicile - Capital gains always treated as income arising out of immovable property though subject to different kind of treatment - HC's view confirmed.
