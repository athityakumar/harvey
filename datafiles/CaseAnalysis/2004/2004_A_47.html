Customs, New Delhi v Ahmadalieva Nodira

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 193; (2004) 3 SCC 549; (2004) SCC (Cr) 834; AIR 2004 SC 3022; 2004 (1) CCR 426; 2004 (1) Crimes 421; 2004 CRLJ 1810; 2004 (110) DLT 300; 2004 (92) ECC 585; 2004 (113) ECR 445; 2004 (166) E.L.T. 302; 2004 (1) JCC 662; JT 2004 (3) SC 264; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 192; 2004 (61) RLT 477; 2004(3) SCALE 211; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1092; 2004 (1) ShimLC 431; 2004 (2) SLT 519; 2004 (2) Supreme 419; 2004 (2) UC 840

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: NDPS Act, s.37 - grant of bail - limitations on granting of bail come in only when the question of granting bail arises on merits - three consitions - apart from the grant of opportunity to the public prosecutor, the other twin conditions which really have relevance so far are satisfaction of the Court that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is not guilty of the alleged offence and that he is not likely to commit any offence while on bail - conditions are cumulative and not alternative - High Court seems to have completely overlooked the underlying object of s. 37 - It did not take note of the confessional statement recorded under Section 67 of the Act and description of the substance - report of laboratory also brushed aside - order granting bail set aside.
