Life Insurance Corporation of India v Anuradha

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1596; (2004) 10 SCC 131; 2004 (2) ACC 44; AIR 2004 SC 2070; 2004 (55) ALR 418; 2004 (5) AWC 4201; 2004 (5) Bom.C.R. 109; 2012 (3) CPJ(Sc) 12; JT 2004 (5) SC 604; 2004 (2) KLT 351; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 94; 2004 (137) PLR 803; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 21; 2004 (97) RD 338; 2004(4) SCALE 80

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 107 and 108 - (A) Presumptions when a person is missing for more than seven years - Held, presumed death had synchronized with date when he was reported to be missing or that date and time of death could be correlated to point of time coinciding with commencement of calculation of seven years backwards from date of initiation of legal proceedings - If an issue may arise as to date or time of death same shall have to be determined on evidence direct or circumstantial and not by assumption or presumption - Presumption of death does not oblige court to presume that death occurred at any time during seven years, nor is there any presumption of law that life continued for any part of seven year period - (B) Whether presumption raised u/s. 108 is a limited presumption confined only to presuming factum of death of person who's life or death is in issue? - Further held, a presumption of fact or law which has gained recognition in statute or by successive judicial pronouncements spread over years cannot be stretched beyond limits permitted by statute or beyond contemplation spelled out from logic, reason and sense prevailing with judges, having written opinions valued as precedents, so as to draw such other inferences as are not contemplated - S. 108 is an exception to rule enacted in s. 107 - In ordinary course of human affairs heard of person in question, have not so heard of him for seven years, presumption raised therefore ceases to operate - Appeal allowed.
