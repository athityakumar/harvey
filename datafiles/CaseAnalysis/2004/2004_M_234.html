Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta v Messrs Emkay Investments Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1037; (2005) 1 SCC 526; AIR 2005 SC 261; 2004 (174) E.L.T. 298; JT 2004 (10) SC 377; 2004 (65) RLT 770; 2004(10) SCALE 269; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 761

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173F, 173G(2), 226, 52A - Manufacture of plywood - Benefit of Notification No 175/86-CE - Benefit of small scale exemption - Whether respondents who are manufacturers of plywood under their own brand name 'Pelicon' have made themselves disentitled to benefit of small scale exemption Notification No 175/86- CE by using a logo indicating 'MERINO' on their products along with their brand name? - Company in question manufacturing plywood, a specified good, and using brand/logo of its own and brand logo of a large scale manufacturer - Company in question would come within mischief of Cl. 7 read with Explanation VIII of the Notification - Held, respondents who are manufacturers of plywood under their own brand name M/s Pelican are disentitled to benefit of small scale exemption Notification No 175/86-CE by using logo indicating 'MERINO' on their product along with their brand name.
