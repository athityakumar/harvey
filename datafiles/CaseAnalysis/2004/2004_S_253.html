Sridhar Bhuyan v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 581; (2004) 11 SCC 395; AIR 2004 SC 4100; 2004 CRLJ 3875; JT 2004 (6) SC 299; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 909; 2004(6) SCALE 504; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 395

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s 302 - Conviction - Whether exception 4 to s 300 is applicable? - Held, for bringing in operation of Exception 4 to s 300 , it has to be established that act was committed without premeditation, in a sudden fight in heat of passion upon a sudden quarrel without offender having taken undue advantage and not having acted in a cruel or unusual manner - Held, ingredients necessary to bring in application of Exception 4 to s 300 are present - Conviction altered to s 304 Part I - Appeal disposed of.
