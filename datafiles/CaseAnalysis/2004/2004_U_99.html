Sumatidevi M. Dhanwatay v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 940; (2004) 6 SCC 113; AIR 2004 SC 2368; 2004 ALJ 1912; 2004 (56) ALR 242; 2004 (3) AWC 2136; 2004 CCC 90; 2004 (2) CPJ(Sc) 27; JT 2004 (4) SC 537; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 140; 2004(4) SCALE 607

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Railways Act, 1989, ss. 100, 103 and 124A - Deficiency in railways services - Appellant complaint claiming compensation - State Commission allowed claim of appellant partly - On appeal, National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission set aside order made by State Commission - Appeal - National Commission, without noticing facts of case, without dislodging reasons recorded by State Commission and without giving reasons, upset order of State Commission simply stating that finding of State Commission could not be upheld that there was a deficiency in service on part of Railway administration - Held, u/s. 124A of Railways Act, 1989 Railway administration cannot escape liability having regard to facts and circumstances of case and in light of incident that had taken place - As to deficiency of service on part of Railway administration, no such plea was raised before State Commissions - Railway administration also did not raise any issue as to maintainability of complaint or jurisdiction of State Commission to deal with complaint - U/s. 3 of Consumer Act, complaint could be entertained by State Commission in absence of any such plea taken by Railway administration as to jurisdiction to entertain complaint - Appeal allowed.
