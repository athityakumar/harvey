Sanjeev Gupta and others Petitioners with v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1002; (2005) 1 SCC 45; AIR 2005 SC 300; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2004 (9) SC 579; 2004(9) SCALE 404; 2004 (8) SLR 28; 2005 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 534; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Grant of recognition for medical courses in medical institutions in erstwhile States of USSR - Conditions for doctors to be given permanent registration - Whether amended s. 13(3) is applicable in the case of petitioner?; whether insistence of MCI that petitioners should first clear screening test and only then they can be granted provisional/permanent registration was ultra vires of Act? - Held, candidates who applied to MCI for provisional registration after 15.3.2001 would complete their one year internship after the cut of date of 15.3.2002 - They would become eligible to seek permanent registration after commencement of provisions of Screening Test Regulations, 2002 - In terms of orders of this Court in Medical Council of India, they all fall under provisions of Screening Test Regulations, 2002 and were required to qualify Screening Test Regulations, 2002 and were required to qualify the screening test before they are granted registration to practice medicine in India - Permission to join internship without qualifying screening test cannot be accepted being contrary to statutory regulations and against public interest.
