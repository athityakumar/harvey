Pune Municipal Corporation and Another v Promoters and Builders Association and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 369; (2004) 10 SCC 796; AIR 2004 SC 3502; JT 2004 (5) SC 191; 2004 (3) MahLJ 360; 2004(5) SCALE 361; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 207; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1937B

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 158, 37, 37(1), 37(2) - Development Control Rules - Whether State Govt. can make any changes of its own in modifications submitted by Planning Authority or not - Interpretation of section 37(2) permits State Govt. to make necessary modifications or put conditions while granting sanction - While exercising legislative functions, unless unreasonableness or arbitrariness is pointed out, it is not open for Court to interfere - Delegated legislation cannot be questioned for violating principles of natural justice in its making except when statute itself provides for that requirement.
