Virender Singh Hooda And Ors. v State Of Haryana And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 958; (2004) 12 SCC 588; JT 2004 (9) SC 293; 2004(9) SCALE 120; 2005 (1) SLR 10; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 720; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 680

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Retrospectivity, Industrial Disputes Act, 1974, Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930, Haryana Civil Service (Executive Branch) and Allied Services and Others Services, Common/combined Examination Act, 2002, Payment Of Bonus (Amendment) Act, 1976, Karnataka State Services (Regulation of Promotion, Pay and Pension) Act, 1973, Haryana Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930

Summary: Haryana Civil Service (Executive Branch) and Allied Services and Others Services, Common/combined Examination Act, 2002 - Challenge to extent of its retrospective application - Whether the Act, to the extent of its retrospectivity, is ultra vires as it amounts to usurpation of judicial power by the legislature or it removes the basis of decisions in Hooda and Sandeep Singh's cases (supra)?; whether Act violative of art. 14 and 16 of Constitution of India? - Impugned Act, to extent of its retrospectivity, except to the limited extent does not amount to usurpation of judicial powers by Legislature - It is not ultra vires - It has removed basis of decisions in Hooda and Sandeep Singh's cases - Act is not violative of art. 14, 16 of Constitution of India except to a limited extent.
