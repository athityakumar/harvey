Directorate Of Education & Ors v Educomp Datamatics Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 194; (2004) 4 SCC 19; AIR 2004 SC 1962; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 7; 2004 (56) ALR 157; 2004 (2) AWC 1789; 2004 (2) BC 386; 2004 (110) DLT 311; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 194; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 274; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 502; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 1; 2004 (2) RAJ 43; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 486; 2004(3) SCALE 111; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1010; 2004 (2) SLT 540; 2004 (2) Supreme 646

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; IT & E-Commerce; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Administrative - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Govt calling for tenders - Minimum turnover prescribed - Term providing a turnover of at least Rs. 20 crores has a nexus with increase in number of schools or quality of education to be provided - Total cost of project was more than 100 crores - Matter of policy Govt. took a conscious decision to deal with one firm having financial capacity to take up such a big project instead of dealing with multiple small companies which is a relevant consideration while awarding such a big project - For authority to set terms of tender - Courts would not interfere with terms of tender notice unless it was shown to be either arbitrary or discriminatory or actuated by malice - Appeal allowed.
