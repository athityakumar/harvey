Madhya Pradesh High Court Bar Association v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 798; (2004) 11 SCC 766; 2005 (3) CompLJ 23; 2004 (103) FLR 480; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 77; JT 2004 (7) SC 548; 2004(8) SCALE 43; 2004 (6) SLR 46; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 520; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 3012

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Administrative Tribunal, Delhi Laws Act, 1915, States Laws Act, 1950, Constitution (32nd Amendment) Act, 1983

Summary: Miscellaneous - Abolition of State Administrative Tribunal, State Government terminated services of all officers and employees other than those on deputation with immediate effect - Whether state has power to abolish State Administrative Tribunal established under Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 enacted by Parliament in exercise of power under art. 323-A of Constitution? - Held, there is provision by Parliament authorizing State Govt. to abolish such Tribunal, it can't be held ultra vires Constitution or inconsistent with law - Decision, cannot be regarded as illegal, unlawful or otherwise objectionable - Appeals and petitions disposed of.
