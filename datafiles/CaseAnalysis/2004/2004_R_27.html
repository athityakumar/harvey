 Nathi Devi v Radha Devi Gupta

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1066; (2005) 2 SCC 271; AIR 2005 SC 648; 2005 (58) ALR 457; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 71; JT 2005 (1) SC 1; 2005 (1) KLT 443; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 631; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 218; 2004(10) SCALE 550; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1141

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e), 14B, 14C, 14D, 25B(4) - Delhi Police Act, 1978, s. 3 - Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1988 - Eviction petition on ground of bona fide personal need invoking provisions of s.14 D - Leave to defend disallowed - Whether conditions for applicability of s.14 D were fulfilled? - whether there is mistake in refusing leave to defend to appellant? - Held, HC fell in error in thinking that only two conditions were required to be fulfilled for application of s.14 D namely, landlady is a widow, and premises are required by her for her residence - Premises must be one let out by her or by her husband - A widow or her late husband who acquired a tenanted premises by sale or transfer cannot invoke provisions of s.14 D to evict a pre-existing tenant - Appeal allowed.
