Union Of India & Anr. v Sneha Khemka & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 24; (2004) 2 SCC 570; (2004) SCC (Cr) 579; AIR 2004 SC 2938; 2004 (54) ALR 473; 2004 (1) CCR 303; 2004 (1) Crimes 350; 2004 CRLJ 941; 2004 (91) ECC 473; 2004 (1) JCC 291; JT 2004 (2) SC 43; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 596; 2004(1) SCALE 699; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1074; 2004 (2) SLT 144; 2004 (1) Supreme 536

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI; Fema

Keywords: Central Clauses Act, 1897

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 22, 22(5) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Central Clauses Act, 1897 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 11, 3, 3(1) - Representation made by detenu or on his behalf - Whether required to be considered by all authorities - Held, decisions whereupon reliance has been placed by HC, nowhere state that copy of representation made by detenu to one authority must be placed before all authorities and all such authorities also should consider and pass orders on those representations, though really not made to any one of them.
