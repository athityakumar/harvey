Punjab Dairy Development Board & Anr., Etc. v Cepham Milk Specialities Ltd. & Ors., Etc.

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 646; (2004) 8 SCC 621; AIR 2004 SC 4466; 2004 (175) E.L.T. 3; JT 2004 (7) SC 5; 2005 (139) PLR 1; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 345; 2004(7) SCALE 82; 2004 (137) STC 163

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Punjab Dairy Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2004, Punjab Dairy Development Board Act, 2000

Summary: Indirect Tax - Punjab Dairy Development Board Act, 2000 - Punjab Dairy Development Board (Amendment) Act, 2004 - Constitution, sch. VII list II - Direction issued under Act for certain milk companies to pay cess at 10paise per litre of their licence capacity for a particular period - Challenge to the Act on ground, a) that the substance of the levy was a tax on the licenced capacity of an Industry and that the State Legislature was not competent to levy tax under any Entry in List II of Schedule VII to the Constitution of India; and (b) that the impost on the licenced capacity was arbitrary and discriminatory - S. 12 of the Act has been deleted vide amendment Act - Whether levy sought to be levied is in the nature of tax or fee, for the purpose of determining under which item of list in sch. VII will it come? - Question left open with a finding that In the absence of any specific order or provision the question of repugnancy cannot arise - Finding of HC as to that the levy, even if it be a fee, is arbitrary and discriminatory, upheld - Cess is levied on only those milk plants having a capacity of more than 10, 000 litres, there is no levy on farmers and co-operative societies, who produce milk and/or milk plants whose capacity is less than 10, 000 litres per day - Appeal dismissed.
