Messrs Muller and Phipps (India) Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay-I

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 358; (2004) 4 SCC 787; AIR 2004 SC 4913; 2004 (94) ECC 1; 2004 (114) ECR 503; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 374; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 490; 2004 (62) RLT 232; 2004(5) SCALE 391; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 39; 2004 (4) Supreme 39

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicament, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 37B - Medicinal And Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, s. 2 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945, r. 17 - Whether Johnson's Prickly Heat Powder and Phipps Processed Talc are patent or proprietary medicines classifiable for purposes of excise duty under erstwhile tariff item 14 E (as prior to 1/3/1986) and Heading 30.03 (subsequent to 1/3/1986) or are cosmetics or toilet preparations falling under erstwhile tariff item 14 F (prior to 1/3/1986) and Heading 33.04 (after 1/3/1986) - Meaning given to products in question not only by department itself but also by other departments like Drug Controller and Central Sales Tax authorities is that product in question is a medicinal preparation should be accepted.
