Punjab National Bank v Virender Kumar Goel & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 22; (2004) 2 SCC 193; (2004) SCC (L&S) 393; AIR 2004 SC 3988; 2004 (1) AWC 793; 2004 (3) CalLT(SC) 43; 2004 (2) ESC 201; 2004 (100) FLR 648; JT 2004 (1) SC 566; 2004 (1) LLJ 1057; 2004 (2) LLN 38; 2004(1) SCALE 621; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 920; 2004 (2) SLT 343; 2004 (1) Supreme 805; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 779

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - VRS - Review/clarify/modify - Order passed by Court on 17/2/2002 - Whether applicants have accepted a part of benefit under VRS in terms of direction given by HC - Held, sentence, 'accepted a part of benefit under scheme', would include withdrawal of benefit and utilisation thereof By no stretch of imagination, utilateral deposit of a part of benefit under scheme into bank account, that too after withdrawal of application, would construe as to have accepted part of benefit under scheme, when same was neither withdrawn nor utilised by employee concerned.
