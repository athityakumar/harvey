Tirupati Balaji Developers Private Limited and v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 346; (2004) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 2351; 2004 (4) CompLJ 171; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 72; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 160; 2004(4) SCALE 724; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 494; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2331

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 132, 133, 134, 135, 136, 139A, 141, 144, 226, 227 - Whether this Court exercising appellate jurisdiction under art.136 of Constitution, have directed a communication being addressed to HC calling for information with object of (I) ascertaining facts, (ii) securing compliance with direction contained in order dated 28/10/2002? - Whether DB of HC is justified in law and on considerations of propriety to make all those observations as have been extracted and reproduced hereinabove? - Is it proper for HC to issue a direction to Registrar General of SC of India to place its communication for consideration before a particular Bench of this Court? - Held, High Court, is not subordinate Court , but Supreme Court has superior place in hierarchy - Conferment of appellate jurisdiction, power to transfer any case u/art. 139A and provisions of Arts. 141, 144 make SC elder brother - Writ jurisdiction of HC is wider than that of SC - HC exercises power of Superintendence over lower Courts - SC does not have such power - Exercise of appellate jurisdiction of SC - Corrective steps to be taken carefully with courtesy and respect and not by harsh criticism - Both High Court and Supreme Court have to have mutual respect for each other - SLP against interim order passed by HC, dismissed with directions to party to approach HC - HC directed to expedite hearing of application stated to have been already filed before it - Subsequent application to SC to recall dismissal of SLP as their application for vacation of interim order have not been heard yet by HC despite requests for early hearing - SC seeking response from HC as to allegations made by petitioner on oath, does not amount to seeking explanation from HC - Nor does it reduce HC to status of party litigant - No occasion arises for HC to engage a lawyer.
