Meher Rusi Dalal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 373; (2004) 7 SCC 362; AIR 2004 SC 3491; 2004 (4) AWC 3266; JT 2004 (5) SC 129; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 244; 2004(5) SCALE 442; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 956; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1945

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11, 12(2), 17, 18, 18(1), 18(3), 23(1), 30, 4, 48(1), 50, 6 - Bombay Rent Act - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, ss. 211, 65 - Reference u/s. 30 and claim of apportionment - Person who had notice of acquisition proceedings and who, by virtue of s 50, is debarred from filing a Reference u/s. 18 cannot be allowed to apply for a Reference u/s. 30 - Claim now made was barred on principle of res-judicata or principles analogous thereto - U/s 18 if a party wants to claim a Reference it is to be done within a particular period - Affirmed order of Land Acquisition Officer and hold that Respondents cannot claim a Reference u/s. 30 nor claim apportionment.
