Sudhir Jaggi and Another v Sunil Akash Sinha Choudhury and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1599; (2004) 7 SCC 515; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 93; 2004 (3) AWC 2729; 2004 (2) CLR 671; JT 2004 (6) SC 336; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 19; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 241; 2004(6) SCALE 580; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Two incomplete flats were purchased by P K Chowdhury in terms of the modified agreement with a developer under which P K Chowdhury had agreed to execute the work of permanent nature at his own costs subject to rebate from the developer - On 10-02-1979, the appellants herein wrongfully and illegally broke into the said two flats and obtained wrongful and forcible possession of the two flats claiming to be bonafide purchasers of the flats from the developer - Whether the appellants are entitled to possession of the flats? - Held, both the courts below have concluded that the dominion/control over the suit flats was with P K Chowdhury - Court do not see any reason to disturb these findings of fact - There is no evidence on record to show that P K Chowdhury was allowed to execute the work on behalf of the developer - It is established that P K Chowdhury was allowed to do the work of permanent nature - There is no term in the agreement between the parties under which P K Chowdhury was obliged to return the possession of the flats on completion of the work - There is no evidence of transfer of the suit flats by the developer to alleged bonafide purchasers - Appeals dismissed.
