State of Punjab v Jagir Singh

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 824; (2004) 8 SCC 129; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1109; AIR 2004 SC 4757; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 124; 2004 (3) CLR 968; 2004 (103) FLR 416; JT 2004 (8) SC 409; 2004 (3) LLJ 1148; 2004(8) SCALE 303; 2004 (6) SLR 175; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 734

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeals) Rules, 1970, Punjab Civil Services Rules, 1953

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1)(c) - Termination - Back wages - Termination on ground being absent from duty - HC ordered reinstatement with 60% of back-wages - Appeals by State and workman - Whether workman is entitled to back-wages? - Held, despite several opportunities workman did not join his duties at all, Labour Court and HC committed a manifest error in granting back-wages - Having regard to totality of situation was not entitled to back-wages - Appeals Disposed Of.
