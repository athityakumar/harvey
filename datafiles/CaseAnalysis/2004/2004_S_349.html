Kunhiabdulla And Anr. v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 270; (2004) 4 SCC 13; (2004) SCC (Cr) 907; AIR 2004 SC 1731; 2004 (1) CCR 394; 2004 (2) Crimes 302; 2004 (1) JCC 623; JT 2004 (3) SC 206; 2004 (2) KLT 152; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2004(3) SCALE 57; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 853; 2004 (2) SLT 439; 2004 (2) Supreme 354; 2004 (1) UC 710

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 34 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113B, 114 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Dowry death -Interval should not be much between concerned cruelty and death in question - If incident of cruelty is remote in time and become stale enough not to disturb mental equilibrium of woman concerned, it would be of no consequence - Accusations clearly established so far as husband and upheld conviction - No evidence against mother in law and acquitted - Appeal disposed of.
