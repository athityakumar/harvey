Man Roland Druckimachinen Ag v Multicolour Offset Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 352; (2004) 7 SCC 447; AIR 2004 SC 3345; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 117; 2004 CCC 424; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 169; 2004 (2) CompLJ 435; 2004 (2) CPJ 30; 2004 (2) CPJ(Sc) 30; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 349; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 219; 2004(4) SCALE 872; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP

Summary: [A.] MRTP - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 23, 28 - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, s.12 B - Challenging jurisdiction of Commission to entertain claim for compensation u/s.12 B - First ground that parties had agreed that applicable law in event of any dispute would be German Law - Second ground that appellant neither provided any service nor carried on any trade or trade practice in India for purpose of Act and even machine in question had been sold to respondent No 1 outside India - Jurisdiction based on cl in agreement was in fact in nature of a demurrer and could be decided as such - But second objection to jurisdiction of Commission was not and it would have to be determined an evidence - Merely because effect of an unfair trade practice is felt in India, this would not clothe Commission with jurisdiction unless 'effect' is itself an 'unfair trade practice' within India - Directed Commission to deal with second aspect of preliminary objection on evidence which may be adduced by either party and in light of legal issues determined.
