D.D. Sharma v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 334; (2004) 5 SCC 325; AIR 2004 SC 2631; 2004 (2) ARBLR 119; 2004 (5) AWC 4590; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 135; 2004 (42) MLJ 1061; 2004(5) SCALE 195; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 767; 2004 (4) Supreme 92

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14(2), 17, 30 - Questioning validity of award - An Arbitrator being a judge chosen by parties, his decision would ordinarily be final unless one or other condition contained in s.30 of Arbitration Act is satisfied for purpose of setting aside his award - Award being not a speaking one, averments made therein should be accepted at their face value unless contrary is proved by party questioning validity of award - No material on records to show that Arbitrator while making an award ignored any material documents - Appeals disposed of.
