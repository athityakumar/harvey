State of Madhya Pradesh v Balu

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 921; (2005) 1 SCC 108; (2005) SCC (Cr) 270; AIR 2005 SC 222; 2005 CRLJ 335; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 153; JT 2004 (10) SC 461; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 951; 2004(9) SCALE 378

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, s. 376 - Rape - Sessions Judge convicted accused and sentence of 7 years rigorous imprisonment - HC order while confirming conviction reduced sentence of imprisonment to a period already undergone - Appeal against reduction of sentence - Held, none of reasons mentioned therein can be construed as either adequate or special reasons to reduce minimum mandatory period of sentence for an offence punishable under s. 376 - HC does not seem to have applied its mind to gravity of offence - Set aside impugned order of HC, allow this appeal and restore sentence awarded by trial court - Appeal allowed.
