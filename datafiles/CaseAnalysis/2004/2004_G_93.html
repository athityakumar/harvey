Government of Orissa v Messrs Ashok Transport Agency and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 919; (2005) 1 SCC 536; 2005 (1) AWC 396; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 226; 2005 (3) CompLJ 10; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 281; JT 2004 (10) SC 599; 2004(9) SCALE 384; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 1081

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Corporate - Practice and Procedure - Amalgamation order - Whether it was for plaintiff to have taken steps to bring on record Corporation and State of Orissa as parties to suit before proceeding with it and obtaining a decree, or whether it was for successors-in-interest of defendant, if they wanted it? - Held, on the terms of Amalgamation Order, plaintiff did have right to proceed with application for restoration and suit as against Corporation by taking appropriate steps in that behalf - It was necessary for plaintiff to have brought on record Corporation and StateGovt.before proceeding with its suit and search for a decree in its favour - Appeal allowed - Appeal allowed.
