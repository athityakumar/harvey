H.P. Housing Board v Bharat S. Negi and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 100; (2004) 2 SCC 184; AIR 2004 SC 1800; 2004 (54) ALR 500; 2004 (2) AWC 995; 2004 (2) CLR 350; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 186B; 2004(2) SCALE 99; 2004 (1) SLT 887; 2004 (1) Supreme 770

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land acquisition - Compensation - Entitled to - Land was acquired for purpose of constructing a social housing colony - Land Acquisition Officer passed Award fixing compensation at Rs. 40,000 per bigha - Dissatisfied claimants filed references was filed before Reference Court - Reference Court refused to give any deductions for largeness of the land - On appeal before HC the same was disposed of by impugned order - Hence instant appeals.

Held, HC was absolutely wrong in excluding 2 sale instances cited by appellants. HC omitted to notice that offer of Rs. 80,000 per bigha had not been accepted by claimants. Offer was for Rs. 80,000 per bigha as a consolidated amount. Appellants would not then have to pay solatium and interest amounts. It is not disputed that figure of Rs. 80,000 per bigha would, if solatium and interest is deducted, have gone down to less than Rs. 40,000 per bigha. Approach of Reference Court was correct. As all sale instances were proved, they were all to be taken into consideration. If all sale instances are taken into consideration, average would work out to Rs. 1,11,660 per bigha. But as all sale instances are of small pieces of lands and acquisition is of large piece of land, a deduction of 33-1/3% must be made towards development cost. Compensation would work out to Rs. 74,444 per bigha. Compensation is fixed at Rs. 74,444 per bigha. It is clarified that respondents/claimants would also be entitled to solatium and interest as per the provisions of the Act. Appeals disposed of.
