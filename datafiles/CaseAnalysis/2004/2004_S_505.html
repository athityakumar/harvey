Upendra Kumar v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1811; (2005) 3 SCC 592

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Juvenile - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused was charged u/s. 302 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant - Aggrieved on appeal HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that he was entitled to the benefit of the 2000 Act since he was a juvenile both on the date of the occurrence and the date of his production before the court since both the dates in instant case were the same, namely, 10-3-1995 - Held, Act came into force on 1-4-2001 - Therefore, when the High Court decided the criminal appeal filed by appellant, the Act had already been in force and appellant was entitled to the benefit thereof - In instant case, at this distant time, the question of referring appellant to the Juvenile Board does not arise - Following the decisions, SC sustained the conviction of appellant for the offences for which he was found guilty by the Trial Court, as affirmed by the HC, at the same time, however, the sentence awarded to appellant was quashed - Appeal allowed.
