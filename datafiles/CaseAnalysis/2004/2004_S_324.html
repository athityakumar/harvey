State Of Haryana v State Of Punjab And Anr

Supreme Court of India

04 June 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 457; (2004) 12 SCC 673; JT 2004 (5) SC 72; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 596; 2004(6) SCALE 75; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Punjab Re-organisation Act, 1966, Supreme Court (Decrees and Orders) Enforcement O. 1954

Summary: Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 131, 136, 142, 142(1), 143, 144, 145(3), 246(3),, 262, 262(2), 32, 356 - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, ss. 11, 14, 2(c), 3 - Punjab Re-organisation Act, 1966, ss. 78, 78(1) - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 36, 37, 38, 39, 40, 41, 42 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 1, O. 23 r. 32, ss. 11, 15, 20, 51 - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 23 r. 6, O. 23 r. 6(a), O. 23 r. 6(b), O. 32 r. 1, O. 32 r. 2, O. 47 r. 6 - Decreed suit in favour of State of Haryana and issued a mandatory injunction directing State of Punjab to complete construction of canal and make it functional within one year from date of judgment - Challenging this decree by state of Punjab by reason of 'changed circumstances' and decree as being un- Constitutional - State of Haryana applied for execution - Construction of SYL canal - Principles of res judicata - Whether plaintiff has legal right - Decree granted a final mandatory injunction - Since plaint does not even ex facie fulfil all four conditions subject to which a decree may be modified, there is no legal right to apply for modification of decree - Issues pertaining to Court's jurisdiction under art.145 (3) and 262 have been considered and decided by this Court - Issues have been concluded inter partes and cannot be raised again in proceedings inter partes - Construction of SYL canal is not a water dispute within meaning of 1956 Act r/w art 262 of Decree confirmed.
