Bharati Vidyapeeth (Deemed University) and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1534; (2004) 11 SCC 755; AIR 2004 SC 1943; JT 2004 (3) SC 451; 2004(3) SCALE 488; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 775; 2004 (3) SLR 291; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1090

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Education - Constitution - Deemed university status - State control on affiliation, recognition - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Karnataka Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1984 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956 ss. 3, 12, 25 and 26 - (A) Whether though institution has attained deemed university status it is within control of State in matter of admissions of students to such colleges as before granting of that status? - Held, it would not be appropriate for State to contend that even though institution has now attained deemed university status it is not beyond clutches of State in matter of admissions of students to such colleges as before granting of deemed university status - (B) Whether university or State Govt. can control of recognition, affiliation or making any financial grants when institutions falls within scope of UGC? - Further held, since institution falls within scope of UGC, university or State Govt. has no role to play either in matter of recognition, affiliation or making any financial grants to exercise powers either as condition thereto or in exercise of Entry 25 of List II - Appeal allowed.
