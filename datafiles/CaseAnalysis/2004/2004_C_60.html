Meenakshiammal (Dead) Through Lrs and Others v Chandrasekaran and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 902; (2005) 1 SCC 280; AIR 2005 SC 52; 2005 (58) ALR 677; 2004 (5) AWC 3793; JT 2004 (9) SC 371; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 734; 2005 (1) RD 608; 2004(9) SCALE 256; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Family and Personal - Land and Property - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Will - Authenticity thereof - Suit for declaration and for recovery of possession - Whether Will in question was true and valid?; whether Will in question was acted upon? - In instant case the propounders of the Will have proved that the Will was signed by Siva; that at the time of execution of the Will, he had a sound disposing state of mind; and that he had reasons to exclude the plaintiffs who did not care for him in his old age - Lastly, the onus to prove forgery, undue influence or collusion was on the plaintiffs who have alleged that Will in question was forged - In the absence of such a plea, the lower appellate Court had erred in holding that the will was forged - Held, execution, attestation and genuineness of the Will in question stands proved as also held by impugned judgement - Appeal without merit - Dismissed.
