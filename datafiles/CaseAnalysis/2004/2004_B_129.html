Board of Secondary Education v Pravas Ranjan Panda and another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1917; (2004) 13 SCC 383

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Directions, Re-evaluation rules

Summary: Education - Re-evaluation rules - Directions - Legality - Respondent no. 1 appeared in High School Certificate Examination, 2003 conducted by the Board of Secondary Examination and passed the said examination securing about 90% marks - Respondent no. 1 filed petition for re evaluation of his answer-books - The HC disposed of the petition with a direction to opposite party, the Board to scrutinise and recheck the answer scripts of examinees securing 90% and above marks - Aggrieved Board filed review petition and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - whether in absence of any provision for re evaluation can an examinee is entitled to ask for re-evaluation of his answer-books.

Held, Pramod Kumar Srivastava v. Chairman, Bihar Public Service Commission 2004 Indlaw SC 580 relied on - In absence of clear rules on the subject, a direction for re-evaluation of the answer-books may throw many problems and in the larger public interest such a direction must be avoided. The impugned order of the HC directing for re-evaluation of the answer- books of all the examinees securing 90% or above marks is unsustainable in law. Appeals allowed.

Ratio: In absence of rules providing for re-evaluation of answer-books, no such direction can be issued.
