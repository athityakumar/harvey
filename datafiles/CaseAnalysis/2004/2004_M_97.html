M/s. Atma Ram Properties (P) Limited v M/s. Federal Motors Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1078; (2005) 1 SCC 705; 2005 (58) ALR 650; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 274; 2004 (115) DLT 531; JT 2004 (10) SC 410; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 212; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 1; 2004(10) SCALE 345; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14, 38, 38(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 5- Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Eviction petition - Eviction of respondent stayed subject to condition that respondent shall deposit in Court Rs. 15,000/- Per month by tribunal - HC set aside condition - Whether superior court, passing an order of stay, acts well within its discretionary jurisdiction by putting on terms appellant who seeks an order of stay? - Held, there is every justification for appellate Court to put tenant on terms and direct to compensate landlord by payment of a reasonable amount which is not necessarily same as contractual rate of rent - HC interfered with order of Tribunal on an erroneous assumption that any direction for payment by tenant to landlord of any amount at any rate above contractual rate of rent could not have been made - Appeal allowed.
