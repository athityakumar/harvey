State of Bihar and others v Bihar Rajya M.S.E.S.K.K. Mahasangh and others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 960; (2005) 9 SCC 129; (2005) SCC (L&S) 460; AIR 2005 SC 1605; 2005 (104) FLR 960; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 126; JT 2005 (11) SC 380; 2004(8) SCALE 712; 2005 (1) SLR 328; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Parliament & Legislature; Service

Summary: Service - Bihar State Universities Act, 1976, ss. 2(c) and 2(i) - 'Affiliated college' and 'constituent college' - Dispute concerning absorption of about 4,000 employees working on teaching and non-teaching posts in 40 colleges affiliated to various universities which were taken over as constituent colleges in accordance with provisions of Act, 1976 - Held, state being author of that decision merely because it is formally not expressed in name of Governor in terms of art.166 of constitution - State itself cannot be allowed to resile or go back on that decision - Mere change of elected Govt. does not justify dishonouring decisions of previous elected Govt.- Order accordingly.
