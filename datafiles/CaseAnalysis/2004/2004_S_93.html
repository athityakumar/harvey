Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited and Others v Sarvesh Berry

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1483; (2005) 10 SCC 471; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1605; AIR 2005 SC 1406; 2005 (1) CLR 263; 2005 (104) FLR 305; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 341; JT 2004 (10) SC 392; 2005 (1) LLJ 588; 2005 (1) LLN 655; 2004(10) SCALE 340; 2005 (1) SLR 641; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 834; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 452

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Hindustan Petroleum Management Employees Conduct, Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1976, r. 13 - Whether departmental proceedings should be kept in abeyance during pendency of a criminal case? - What is required to be seen is whether departmental enquiry would seriously prejudice delinquent in his defence at trial in a criminal case - It is always a question of fact to be considered in each case depending on its own facts and circumstances - Held, High Court was not justified in directing stay of departmental proceedings pending conclusion of criminal charge - Appellants are free to continue departmental proceedings - Appeal allowed.
