Dastagir Sab and Another v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 98; (2004) 3 SCC 106; (2004) SCC (Cr) 678; AIR 2004 SC 2884; 2004 (2) Crimes 15; 2004 (1) JCC 336; JT 2004 (1) SC 635; 2004 (3) MahLJ 694; 2004 (3) MPLJ 154; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 601; 2004(2) SCALE 8; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 952; 2004 (2) SLT 659; 2004 (1) Supreme 918

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 376(2)(g) - Offence of rape - Test Identification Parade - Non-holding of - Would not by itself disprove the prosecution case - How much it would adversely affect the prosecution case - Depends upon the facts and circumstances of each case - Held on facts, not fatal to the case of the prosecution - No interference in conviction or sentence - Appeal dismissed.
