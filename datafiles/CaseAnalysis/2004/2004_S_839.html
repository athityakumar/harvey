State of West Bengal and others v Rupa Ice Factory (Private) Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1858; (2004) 10 SCC 635

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 1956, s. 26 - Unmetered consumption - Disconnection of electricity supply - Prior notice - Central Vigilance Squad had found that Respondent no.1 had tapped electric energy directly from transformer to LT distribution board bypassing meter circuit - Officials of appellants disconnected electricity supply to respondent's establishment - Respondents challenged certain demands raised by appellants and for restoration of electrical connection before HC which allowed by holding that there was no power conferred upon appellants to decide suo motu extent of electricity pilferage and raise demand thereof - Hence instant appeal.

Held, regarding authority to disconnect supply on basis of allegations of pilferage without any notice, HC concluded that facts recorded were sufficient to form opinion or prima facie view that there had been theft of electricity justifying disconnection of line u/cl. 25 of Agreement without notice. Finding is in favour of appellant and there is no challenge to this part of order by respondents and Court need not examine this aspect any further. That finding therefore becomes final. Bill that has been raised by appellants covers two periods. Firstly, from December 1992 to November 1993 and secondly from December 1993 to December 1995. It appears that electric meter was found to be defective in month of December 1993 and defect was set right on same day and thereafter, as result, energy meter had only recorded 2/3rds of electrical energy actually consumed. On that basis claim was made by appellants. Appellants does not dispute view taken by HC on this aspect of matter pertaining to period between December 1992 and November 1993 and that aspect also need not detain Court attention any further. With regarding to W.B. SE had authority to determine quantum of unmetered consumption in cases of pilferage and raise bills on basis thereof claim for period from December 1993 to December 1995, finding of HC is that Vigilance Squad had found that Respondent no.1 had tapped electric energy directly from transformer to LT distribution board bypassing meter circuit. If that is so, Court does not know as to HC would go on to advert to s. 26 of Act and direct reference to the Electrical Inspector for decision u/s. 26(6) of Act. Part of order of HC, directing that there should, be reference to Electrical Inspector, shall stand set aside. In other respects order of HC shall remain undisturbed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In cases of tampering or theft or pilferage of electricity, demand rose falls outside scope of s. 26 of 1956 Act. If that is so, neither limitation period mentioned in s. 26 of 1956 Act nor procedure for raising demand for electricity consumed would arise at all.
