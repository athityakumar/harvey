Shakuntala Bai and Others v Narayan Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 384; (2004) 5 SCC 772; AIR 2004 SC 3484; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 88; 2004 (55) ALR 741; 2004 (6) Bom.C.R. 224; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 538; 2004 (137) PLR 845; 2004 (1) RCR(Rent) 580; 2004(5) SCALE 543; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1), 2(b) - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(e) - Whether on account of death of a landlord during pendency of appeal, a suit validly instituted by him for eviction of a tenant on ground of his personal need, is liable to be dismissed - Principle that 'the need of landlord must exist till decree for eviction is passed by last court and attains finality' have no application - There is no restriction on a landlord who may be minor to seek eviction of a tenant if premises is bonafidely required by him - Excecuting court cannot go behind decree - When amendment is allowed, proceedings have to be decided on basis of such amended pleadings - Judgment and decree passed by HC is set aside and that of Additional District Judge decreeing suit for eviction is restored.
