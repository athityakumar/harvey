Central Bureau of Investigation v Akhilesh Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1040; (2005) 1 SCC 478; AIR 2005 SC 268; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 308; 2004(10) SCALE 284; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120-B, 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, s. 482 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 10 - Offences under ss 120-B r/w 302, 109 - HC discharged respondent from criminal case - Appeal against order of discharge - Held, once main accused, who is alleged to have hatched conspiracy and who had motive to kill deceased was discharged, and when matter attained finality, Single Judge was fully justified in holding that no purpose would be served in further proceeding with case against respondent - No interference.
