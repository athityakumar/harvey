Keshav R. Thakur and another v Suchhibai

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1844; (2005) 9 SCC 424

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, ss.7, 10 - Appointment of Guardian - Custody - Welfare of child - Appellant No.1 was grandfather of appellant no.2 - Appellant No.1's son/‘G’ got married with respondent and appellant no.2 was their son - After death of ‘G’ appellant no. 2 who was six years at that time was residing with appellant no.1 - Appellants filed application u/ss.7, 10 of 1890 Act before Trial Court claiming to be appointed guardian in respect of appellant no.2 -Trial Court held welfare of child lay in his residing with respondent/mother - Appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant no. 2 was now about 16 or 17 years old and remained with appellant no.1 all along. Respondent/mother fairly stated that child was old enough to know his own mind and free to decide whether he wishes to remain with his mother or with grandparents. As grandparents have by reason of interim order or otherwise remained in care and control of minor appellant no. 2 for his entire life, it will not be appropriate to grant custody of child to mother at this stage. Appellant No.1 was directed to allow respondent/mother to meet child whenever approaches. Appeal allowed.
