Adalat Prasad v Rooplal Jindal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 666; (2004) 7 SCC 338; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1927; AIR 2004 SC 4674; 2005 ALR 158; 2005 (1) CGLJ 268; 2004 (113) DLT 356; 2005 (1) G.L.R. 546; JT 2004 (7) SC 243; 2004 (2) KarLJ 727; 2004 (3) KLT 382; 2004 (4) MahLJ 274; 2004 (4) MPLJ 1; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2004(7) SCALE 137; 2004 (2) UC 1236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 202, 203, 482 - if a Magistrate takes cognizance of an offence, issues process without there being any allegation against the accused or any material implicating the accused or in contravention of provision of ss. 200 and 202, the order of the Magistrate may be vitiated - relief an aggrieved accused can obtain at that stage is not by invoking s.203 - Code does not contemplate a review of an order or confers inherent power with the subordinate criminal courts. Observation in K M Matthew's case that no specific provision is required for recalling an erroneous order, amounting to one without jurisdiction, does not lay down the correct law - aggrieved accused can only invoke s. 482 of the Code.
