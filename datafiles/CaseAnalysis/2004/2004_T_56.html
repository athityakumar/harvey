Fairgrowth Investments Limited v The Custodian

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 868; (2004) 11 SCC 472; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 683; 2004 (4) CompLJ 233; JT 2004 (9) SC 124; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 737; 2004(8) SCALE 817; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Special Courts (Trial of offences Relating to Transaction in Securities) Act, 1992, s. 4(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 29(2) - Whether Special Courts constituted under the Special Courts Act has power to condone delay in filing a petition under s 4(2) of Act? - Held, s 29(2) of Limitation Act does not apply to proceedings under s. 4(2) of Special Courts Act - Since appellant's petition of objection had been filed much beyond period prescribed under that section, Special Court was right in rejecting petition in limine - Appeal dismissed.
