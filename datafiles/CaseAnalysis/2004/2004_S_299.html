Monaben Ketanbhai Shah & Anr. v State Of Gujarat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 577; (2004) 7 SCC 15; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1857; AIR 2004 SC 4274; 2004 (57) ALR 272; 2004 (56) ALR 713; 2005 (3) BC 391; [2005] 126 Comp Cas 342; 2004 CRLJ 4249; 2005 (1) G.L.R. 21; JT 2004 (6) SC 309; 2004 (3) KLT 428; 2004 (138) PLR 615; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 799; 2004(6) SCALE 507; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bouncing Of Cheques

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Dishonour of cheque - Magistrate discharged accused holding that there are no allegations in complaint, making out an offence - In complaint no averments against appellants except stating in title that they are partners of firm - Held, primary responsibility on complainant to make necessary averments in complaint so as to make accused vicariously liable - Case is of total absence of requisite averments in complaint - Appeal allowed.
