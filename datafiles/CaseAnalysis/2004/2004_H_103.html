Bar Council Of India v High Court Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 340; (2004) 6 SCC 311; AIR 2004 SC 2227; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 115; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 97; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 428; 2004 (2) KLT 485; 2004 (3) LLN 74; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 890B; 2004(5) SCALE 232; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 649; 2004 (3) Supreme 517

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Right To Practise

Summary: [A.] Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 34(1), 35 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 129, 142, 145, 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 215, 14 - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 175, 178, 179, 180, 228 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 345 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, r. 11 - Whether Rule, that no advocate who has been found guilty of contempt of court shall be permitted to appear, act or plead in any court unless he has purged himself of contempt, of Kerala HC is constitutional - When a person is punished by superior court, right of freedom of speech conferred upon a citizen under art.19(1)(a) of Constitution of India cannot stand as a bar as power of this Court u/art.129 and that of HC under art.215 are independent and not subject to art.19(1)(a) - Bar Council of India is not a citizen entitling it to raise question of validity of Rules on touchstone of art.19(1)(a) of Constitution - It has no such fundamental right - No person aggrieved who is a citizen of India is before court - Rule 11 of Rules framed by Kerala HC is not unconstitutional.
