Vareed Jacob v Sosamma Geevarghese and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1474; (2004) 6 SCC 378; AIR 2004 SC 3992; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 35; 2004 (3) AWC 2033; 2005 (1) Bom.C.R. 413; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 165; 2004 (2) KLT 649; 2005 (1) LCD 661; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 708; 2004(5) SCALE 102; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 534; 2004 (3) Supreme 637; 2004 (2) UC 939

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 9, 51, 94 and 151, O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 - Whether there is automatic revival of interlocutory orders with restoration of suit unless circumstances occurring during interregnum or orders passed by court speak to contrary? - Interlocutory orders passed before dismissal would stand revived along with suit when dismissal is set aside and suit is restored unless court expressly or by implication excludes operation of interlocutory orders passed during period between dismissal of suit and restoration - Held, decree holder was entitled to exclude period from date of filing of suit till its dismissal, in computing period of limitation for execution of decree - Appeal dismissed.
