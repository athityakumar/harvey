State of Punjab v Makhan Chand

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1145; (2004) 3 SCC 453; (2004) SCC (Cr) 830; AIR 2004 SC 3061; JT 2004 (3) SC 360; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2004(2) SCALE 778

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 21, 50, 42, 51 and 52-A(1) - Police informed Respondent-accused that they suspected him of committing an offence under NDPS Act and told him that if he so desired, search would be carried out in presence of a gazetted officer or a Magistrate - Respondent declined offer - Respondent charged with contravention of ss. 18 and 21 of NDPS Act - On appeal High Court held a contravention of s. 50 on ground of non-joining of independent witnesses - (A) Whether there is a contravention of s. 50? - If officer, without any prior information as contemplated by s. 42 of NDPS Act makes a search in normal course of investigation and on completion of that search, a contraband under NDPS Act is recovered, requirements of s. 50 are not attracted - S. 50 would not apply to a situation where search undertaken is not of person of accused but of something carried in his hand - Held, s. 50 does not apply at all - (B) Whether there was contravention of s. 52-A(1)? - S. 52-A(1) does not empower Central Govt. to lay down procedure for search of an accused, but only deals with disposal of seized narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances - They are merely intended to guide officers to see that a fair procedure is adopted by officer in charge of investigation - Further held, there was no substance in this contention - Appeal allowed.
