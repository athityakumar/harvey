Jayamma v Maria Bai Dead By Proposed Lrs. And Anr

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 509; (2004) 7 SCC 459; AIR 2004 SC 3957; JT 2004 (6) SC 57; 2004(6) SCALE 332; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, s. 61 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 213, 276, 299 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Interpretation - Whether application for grant of letters of administration with a copy of Will maintainable in view of s.61 - Testament being agricultural land with occupancy right - Held, a transfer of agricultural land with occupancy right is permissible only in favour of one of heirs who would be entitled to claim partition of land and not others having regard to definition of 'family' as contained in s 2(12) and 'joint family' as contained in s 2 (17).
