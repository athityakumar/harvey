Commissioner of Central Excise, Pondicherry v Messrs Acer India Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 811; (2004) 8 SCC 173; AIR 2004 SC 4805; 2004 (172) E.L.T. 289; JT 2004 (8) SC 53; 2004 (64) RLT 539; 2004(8) SCALE 169; 2004 (137) STC 596; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Duty - Whether central excise duty is payable on a software loaded in a hardware, i.e. computer? - Held, computer and operative softwares are different marketable commodities - Rate of excise duty for computer is 16% whereas that of a software is nil - Computers and softwares are different and distinct goods under Act having been classified differently and in that view of matter, no central excise duty would be leviable upon determination of value thereof by taking total value of computer and software - When an exemption granted from levy of any excise duty on software whether it is operating software or application software in terms of heading 85.24, no excise duty can be levied thereupon indirectly as it was impermissible to levy a tax indirectly - No central excise duty is payable on a software loaded in a hardware - Appeals Dismissed.
