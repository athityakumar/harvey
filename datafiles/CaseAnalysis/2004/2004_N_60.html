Narendra Singh & Anr v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 266; (2004) 10 SCC 699; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1893; AIR 2004 SC 3249; 2004 CRLJ 2842; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 29; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 613; 2004(4) SCALE 543; 2004 (2) UC 863

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 402 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 313 - Presumption of innocence is a human right and such presumption gets stronger when a judgment of acquittal is passed - Benefit must go to appellants as in event of there being two possible views, one supporting accused should be upheld - Appeal allowed.
