State of Uttar Pradesh v Pappu Alias Yunus and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1500; (2005) 3 SCC 594; (2005) SCC (Cr) 780; AIR 2005 SC 1248; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 92; 2005 CRLJ 331; JT 2005 (1) SC 153; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 404; 2004(10) SCALE 147; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 585

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Character of victim - Minor - Rape - Unreasoned order - Respondents were charged for alleged commission of offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC - Reliance was placed on evidence of prosecutrix and her mother - Respondents were, found guilty and sentenced to undergo RI for five years and fined Rs. 2,000 - Respondents alleged that a false case was foisted - Trial Court considered evidence held that prosecutrix was minor, thus question of consent was of no consequence - Respondents appealed before HC and they were acquitted - Appellant submitted that HC had disposed of appeal in laconic and casual manner, based on statement of mother - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment rendered by HC that acquitted respondents was correct - Held, HC's judgment was un-reasoned and its approach in dealing with appeal was rather casual and disclosed non-application of mind - Evidence of mother of prosecutrix was mis-read - Prosecutrix was victim of offence of rape, thus was not accomplice after crime - HC's view that prosecutrix was of loose morale and easy virtues, therefore respondents were entitled to acquittal was indefensible - In absence of reasons or basis appellate court should not have interfered with findings recorded by trial court - Appellate Court should have analysed evidence and recorded its own conclusions - Hence, it was fit case where matter needed to be re-heard by HC - Accordingly judgment of HC was set aside - Matter was remitted to HC for fresh hearing - Court had not expressed any opinion on merits of case except indicated as to how character of victim was really of no consequence while adjudicating if rape was committed on her or not - Respondents were to move HC for bail which was to be considered on its own perspective - Appeal disposed of.
