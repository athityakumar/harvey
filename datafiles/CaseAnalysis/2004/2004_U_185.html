Union of India v Bal Ram and another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1707; (2010) 5 SCC 747; AIR 2004 SC 3981

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Acquisition - Compensation - Pursuant to notification issued u/s. 4 of 1894 Act, lands in 13 villages were acquired and ‘S’ was also one such village - HC taking into consideration that certain other lands coming under different villages which had been acquired for same purpose, found that compensation under market value of Rs 47,224 per bigha would be reasonable and appropriate - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, that HC indeed did not rely upon contiguity of lands alone but it found that nature/quality of lands is by and large similar to those lands considered in Satpal case that is finding of HC, there would not be any justification to make any distinction between lands which had been lying in Palam and Shahbad Moharnadpur. View taken by HC cannot be faulted with. HC also found that it would be unfair to discriminate between landowners to pay more to some and less to others when purpose of acquisition is same and lands are identical and similar, though lying in different villages. Judgment of HC is fair and reasonable and no interference is called for. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - it would be unfair to discriminate between landowners to pay more to some and less to others when purpose of acquisition is same and lands are identical and similar, though lying in different villages.
