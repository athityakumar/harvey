Noratanmal Chouraria v M.R. Murli & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 279; (2004) 5 SCC 689; AIR 2004 SC 2440; 2004 (55) ALR 724; 2004 (3) AWC 2167; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 168; JT 2004 (4) SC 552; 2004 (97) RD 84; 2004(4) SCALE 755; 2004 (3) SLR 644; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 266; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2428

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 35, 38 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - Bar Council of India Rules - Misconduct - Whether findings of Bar Council, be said to be so irrational meriting interference - Disciplinary committee of Bar Council of India is a statutory body - Duty to arrive at a finding of facts in respect of complaint made against a member of legal profession is upon it - This court although enjoys extensive and wide jurisdiction u/s.38 of Act, opinion of Bar Council shall carry great weight - Appeal dismissed.
