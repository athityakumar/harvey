Delhi Financial Corporation and Another v Rajiv Anand and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1528; (2004) 11 SCC 625; 2005 (122) DLT 546; 2005(5) SCALE 323

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1965

Summary: Banking & Finance - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 32-G - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1965 - Debt taken from financial corporations - Action initiated u/s. 32-G of State Financial Corporations Act, 1951 - State Govt. appointed respective Managing Directors as authority u/s. 32-G for issue of certificates of recovery - Writ petitions filed in Delhi High Court and Punjab and Haryana HC challenging appointment of Managing Directors as authority - Delhi HC struck down appointment of Managing Director and certificate of recovery - Punjab and Haryana HC upheld appointment of Managing Director - Whether Managing Director of a corporation can be appointed by State Govt. as authority u/s. 32G? - Held, State Govt. or authority will issue a certificate only after following procedure - Principles of natural justice complied with - Provision is not arbitrary - Mere appointment of an officer of corporation does not by itself bring into play doctrine of no man can be a judge in his own cause - Officer concerned has no personal bias or interest - Managing Director of a financial corporation can be appointed as an authority u/s. 32 - Appeal allowed.
