 E.V. Chinnaiah and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 922; (2005) 1 SCC 394; AIR 2005 SC 162; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 102; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 204; JT 2005 (11) SC 482; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 79; 2004(9) SCALE 316; 2005 (3) SLR 363; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 972

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Pith And Substance

Summary: Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Castes (Rationalisation of Reservations) Act, 2000 - Constitutionality - Whether Act is violative of art, 341(2) of Constitution? - Whether impugned enactment is constitutionally invalid for lack of legislation competence? - Whether impugned enactment creates sub-classification or micro classification of Scheduled Castes so as to violate art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, logical corollary thereof would be that State Legislatures are forbidden from doing that - A uniform yardstick must be adopted for giving benefits to members of Scheduled Castes for purpose of Constitution - Impugned legislation being contrary to constitutional scheme cannot, therefore, be sustained - Legislation apart from being beyond legislative competence of State is also violative of art. 14 and hence is liable to declared as ultra vires the Constitution - Appeals allowed.
