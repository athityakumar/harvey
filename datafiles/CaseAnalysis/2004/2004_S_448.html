A. Venkatakrishnan v State Transport Authority, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1535; (2004) 11 SCC 207; 2005 (3) ACC 708; 2005(5) SCALE 311

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 88(4), 88(5) and 88(6) - Transport Permit - Rejection of application for grant of counter signature of permit on inter-State route challenged - Relied on Ashwani Kumar v Regional Transport Authority, Bikaner 1999 Indlaw SC 750 - A purposive and meaningful construction, it is trite, must be given to a statute, so that it is made workable - A statute should not be construed in such a manner, which would create a vacuum - A mutual approval of states concerned, in matter, therefore, must be held to be mandatory - Held, proviso, appended to sub-s. (4) of s. 88 of Act, must be read conjointly with sub-ss. (5) and (6) of s. 88 thereof and, consequently, it must be held that by necessary implication agreements are contemplated for creation of inter-State routes - Further held, State Transport Authority of one State would have no jurisdiction to entertain an application for grant of an inter-State route, particularly when s. 80 of Act will have no application in relation thereof unless an agreement is entered into by State concerned - Appeal dismissed.
