Deb Narayan Shyam & Ors v State Of West Bengal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1694; (2005) 2 SCC 286; AIR 2005 SC 1167; JT 2004 (10) SC 320; 2004(10) SCALE 124; 2004 (8) SLR 1; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 551; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 571

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Career Advancement Scheme, 1990, West Bengal Service (Revision of Pay and Allowance) Rules, 1961, Revision of Pay & Allowance Rules, 1998

Summary: Service - West Bengal Service (Revision of Pay and Allowance) Rules, 1961 - Parity in pay - Entitlement - Employees in the post of Amins (Md. Anwarul Haque and others ) filed Civil Rule petitions before the HC seeking revision of pay at par with the pay scale of Surveyors - Amins contended that they had the same qualification and job responsibility as that of the Surveyors - Single Judge without comparing the qualification and duties, directed the State Government to grant the pay scale of Surveyors to Amins - On the basis of the said HC order, number of employees holding the post of Amins claimed the parity and the State Government granted the same from time to time - Another set of writ petitions, subsequently, were filed by the Amins seeking parity in pay, however, DB, by impugned order, held that the Amins could not be treated at par with the Surveyors, and dismissed the writ petitions accordingly - Appellant Amins, who were not parties to the writ petitions before the DB but the benefits which had accrued in their favour by the Single Judge order (Md. Anwarul Haque and others ) in different cases, filed appeal before the SC - SC remitted the matter back to DB - DB held that qualification, training and expertise and the work performed by Amins and Surveyors were different and hence their posts could not be treated as equal as far as the higher categories of Surveyors were concerned - DB further held that earlier Single Judge order in Md. Anwarul Haque case was need not be treated as precedent in future cases - Hence the instant appeals - (A) Whether the Amins and Surveyors discharge the same duties - Held, Fourth Pay Commission recommended the pay scale for Amins as scale No.6 and after carefully examining the matter they recorded that the demand of the Amins for being equated with the Surveyors could not be accepted as recruitment qualification for appointment as Amin and Surveyor were different - Technical Rules and Instructions relating to survey work indicated that Surveyors used more sophisticated equipment involving superior training and skills than Amins - Surveyors were technical persons and the Amins were non-technical persons - Surveyors were fully qualified in the engineering surveys whereas the Amins were not supposed to be so qualified - Nature of duties which was being discharged and the instructions which had been issued showing that the Amins and Surveyors were not discharging identical duties - Work of Amins started after the work of Surveyors ended - Further, Amins had to undergo related course of a duration of six months or so, as against the Surveyors' two years certificate course - Surveyors, therefore, stood on superior footing than that of the Amins and they could not be equated from the functional point of view as well as qualification point of view - View taken by the DB, therefore, that the Amins could not be equated with the Surveyors was correct - (B) ) Whether the Amins were entitled to the same pay scale as that of Surveyors - Held, if the two categories of posts performed the same duties and function and carried the same qualification, then there should not be any distinction in pay scale between the two categories of posts similarly situated, however, when they were different and performed different duties and qualifications for recruitment being different, then they could not be said to be equated so as to qualify for equal pay for equal work - Post of Amin was different from that of the Surveyor as the Amins did not discharge the same duties as that of the Surveyors and as such they were not entitled to claim equal pay for equal work - (C) What was the effect of various decisions of the HC passed from time to time in favour of the Amins treating them equivalent to that of Surveyors and allowing them pay scale No.9 - Held, in Md. Anwarul Haque and others case, State Government did not file any affidavit before the Single Judge - No material had been placed by the State before the Court and neither the Single Judge examined the detailed functions of the Amins and that of the Surveyors but only relied on an opinion expressed by the Deputy Commissioner on a representation filed by the Amins that they discharged similar functions and duties - On the basis of such records, Single Judge inferred that the Amins discharging the same functions as that of the Surveyors - However, if all the judgments were allowed to remain which were passed without reference to the material which ought to have been taken into consideration, it would go to cause great anomalous situation and would create two groups i.e. one in whose favour orders had been passed by Court and the others without any order and they would be governed by the existing Rules - Further, such position would create an anomalous position in same cadre such as some Amins were getting scale No.9 and some in Scale No.6 or 7 - Two options, therefore, were available i.e. the Amins who were not getting the benefit of scale No.9 should be given same as was being given to Amins under order of Court i.e. Scale No.9 or all should be brought on par without overburdening the State exchequer, and a uniform pay scale be enforced in the whole State - Appeals disposed of.
