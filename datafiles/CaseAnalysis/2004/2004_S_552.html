Aman Kumar and Anr v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1093; (2004) 4 SCC 379; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1266; AIR 2004 SC 1497; 2004 (2) CGLJ 25; 2004 CRLJ 1399; JT 2004 (2) SC 274; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 925; 2004(2) SCALE 356; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Rape - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376(2)(g), 506, 354 and 511 - (A) Rape - Held, offence of rape is an offence affecting the human body - Rape in its simplest term is 'the ravishment of a woman, without her consent, by force, fear or fraud', or as 'the carnal knowledge of a woman by force against her will' - In the crime of rape, 'carnal knowledge' means the penetration to any the slightest degree of the organ alleged to have been carnally known by the male organ of generation - Even the slightest degree of penetration is sufficient to prove sexual intercourse - (B) Penetration - Penetration is the sine qua non for an offence of rape - In order to constitute penetration, there must be clear evidence to prove that some part of the virile member of the accused was within the labia of the pudendum of the woman, no matter how little - Actus reus is complete with penetration - Depth of penetration is immaterial in an offence punishable u/s. 376, IPC, 1860 - (C) Attempt u/s. 511, distinguished from intention to commit it - Further Held, s. 511 is a general provision dealing with attempts to commit offences not made punishable by other specific sections - An attempt to commit a crime is to be distinguished for an intention to commit it from preparation made for its commission - Mere intention to commit an offence, not followed by any act, cannot constitute an offence - To find an accused guilty of an attempt with intent to commit a rape, court has to be satisfied that the accused, when he laid hold of the prosecutrix, not only desired to gratify his passions upon her person, but that he intended to do so at all events, and notwithstanding any resistance on her part - (D) Outraging of modesty, applicability, ingredients, scope of s. 354 - Further Held, essential ingredients of the offence punishable under s. 354 are that the person assaulted must be a woman, and the accused must have used criminal force on her intending thereby to outrage her modesty - Essence of a woman's modesty is her sex - Appeal partly allowed.
