M/S Mahendra Saree Emporium v G.V. Srinivasa Murthy

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 669; (2005) 1 SCC 481; AIR 2004 SC 4289; 2004 (57) ALR 736; JT 2004 (7) SC 20; 2004 (2) RCR(Rent) 286; 2004(7) SCALE 271; 2005 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 550; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Karnataka General Clauses Act, 1899

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961 (since repealed) - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1999, ss 9, 70 - [A] Pending cases at stage of trial under Old Act how to be dealt with after new Act came into force? - Held that the cases pending at the stage of trial, whether before the Controller or the Court, are covered by s 69 of the 1999 Act - Forum competent in a pending case, depending on the averments made in the plaint, shall be determined by reference to the provisions of the 1999 Act - Having been determined so the case may continue to be tried by the forum in which it is pending or be transferred from the Controller to the Court or vice versa, as the case may be [B] Interpretation of Statutes - Held, State Legislature enacting the New Act could have provided for the suit itself which originated under the local law to abate on the date of coming into force of the New Act but that the Legislature has not chosen to do - The Legislature could not have provided, nor has it provided, for the jurisdiction of this Court under art. 136 being taken away or curtailed in any manner whatsoever, and rightly so - Appeal under art. 136 survive unabated for adjudication on merits [C] Sub-letting - Tenant has not parted with possession in favour of any stranger - Other family members entering into partnership with the tenant for the purpose of carrying on the pre-existing business in the suit premises - Tenant has not dissociated himself from the business activity leaving for the partners alone to carry on the business or that the so-called partners are in exclusive possession of the premises having no relationship with the tenant and the partnership is nothing but a camouflage for parting with by the tenant of the possession or right to use the tenancy premises in favour of the persons in possession - Not a case of sub-letting simply because the sole propriety business was converted into a partnership business - Appeal allowed.
