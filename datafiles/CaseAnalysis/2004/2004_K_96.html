Koppula Venkat Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1099; (2004) 3 SCC 602; (2004) SCC (Cr) 840; AIR 2004 SC 1874; 2004 CRLJ 1804; JT 2004 (3) SC 328; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 189; 2004(3) SCALE 96; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 944; 2004 (2) UC 827

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 122, 375, 376, 399 and 511 - Whether accused rightly convicted for rape? - Sine qua non of offence of rape is penetration, and not ejaculation - Ejaculation without penetration constitutes an attempt to commit rape and not actual rape - Held, definition of 'rape' in s. 375, IPC refers to 'sexual intercourse' and Explanation appended to section provides that penetration is sufficient to constitute sexual intercourse necessary to offence of rape - Intercourse means sexual connection - In instant case that commission of actual rape has not been established - Evidence sufficient to prove that attempt to commit rape was made - Conviction altered from s. 376, IPC to s. 376/511, IPC with RI of 3 and 1/2 years - Order accordingly.
