Uco Bank and Others v Sanwar Mal

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1159; (2004) 4 SCC 412; (2004) SCC (L&S) 699; AIR 2004 SC 2135; 2004 (5) AWC 4570; 2004 (2) CLR 872; 2004 (5) ESC 40; 2004 (101) FLR 437; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 487; 2004 (2) LLJ 490; 2004 (2) LLN 775; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 48; 2004(4) SCALE 280; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 1125; 2004 (3) SLR 629

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(p) and 18(1) - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, r. 58 - Whether respondent is entitled to retrial benefits even though he resigned from his services? - Held, an employee can resign at any point of time, but in case of retirement he retires only after attaining age of superannuation or in case of voluntary retirement on completion of qualifying service - Pension Scheme herein is based on actuarial calculation, it is a self-financing scheme, which does not depend upon budgetary support and consequently it constitutes a complete code by itself - Moreover, resignation brings about complete cessation of master-and-servant relationship whereas voluntary retirement maintains relationship for purposes of grant of retrial benefits, in view of past service - Since pension regulations disqualify an employee, who has resigned, from claiming pension, respondent cannot claim membership of fund - Appeal allowed.
