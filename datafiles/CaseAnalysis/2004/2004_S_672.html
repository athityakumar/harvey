Gangadhar Janardan Mhatre v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 874; (2004) 7 SCC 768; AIR 2004 SC 4753; 2004 CRLJ 4623; 2004 (114) DLT 193; JT 2004 (8) SC 208; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 682; 2004(8) SCALE 329; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 772; 2005 (1) UC 47

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, art. 226 - Questions legality of order passed by Division Bench of Bombay HC dismissing Criminal Writ Petition - Whether writ petition is maintainable to protest against investigating officers? - Held, writ application was not proper remedy, and without availing remedy available under Code, appellant could not have approached HC by filing a Writ Application - No infirmity in HC's order - Appeal dismissed.
