Ahmedabad Electricity Co. Ltd v Gujarat Inns. Pvt. Ltd. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 209; (2004) 3 SCC 587; AIR 2004 SC 2171; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 33; 2004 (55) ALR 17; 2004 (2) AWC 1720; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 880; 2004 (2) CLR 347; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 290; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1363; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 86; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 70; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 627; 2004(3) SCALE 483; 2004 (2) SLT 814; 2004 (2) Supreme 580; 2004 (2) UC 887

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Electricity & Energy

Summary: [A.] Electricity & Energy - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Present two cases are cases of fresh connection - Purchasers have stated that they have taken fresh connections and they have no objection if their connections are treated as fresh connections given on dates on which supply of electricity was restored to premises - In case of a fresh connection though premises are same, auction purchasers cannot be held liable to clear arrears incurred by previous owners in respect of power supply to premises in absence of there being a specific statutory provision in that regard -appeals dismissed.
