Madhya Pradesh Electricity Board v Dev Narayan Patel

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1781; (2005) 10 SCC 220

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Promotion - Eligibility - Maintainability - Respondent was selected and appointed as Graduate Trainee by appellant Electricity Board - After completion of said training, respondent was appointed as Assistant Engineer and thereafter confirmed on post of Assistant Engineer - Respondent then represented that he should not be treated as fresh appointee and deputed for training as he had already undergone training in year 1976-77 itself - Said representation did not evoke any response from Electricity Board - Second round of litigation was generated by refusal of Electricity Board to consider representation of respondent, leading to filing of fresh writ petition praying for direction to regularise his services as Assistant Engineer and to grant him all consequential benefits - Single Judge allowed writ petition and DB of HC confirmed same - Electricity Board filed review petition so as to seek order from Court that respondent could not be entitled to benefit of promotion as Superintending Engineer as he did not put in requisite service - DB of HC dismissed review petition - Hence, instant petition - Whether DB of HC was justified in dismissing review petition holding that respondent did not satisfy criteria for promotion to higher post and petition was maintainable - Held, review petition raising issues as to correct date of promotion as Executive Engineer and eligibility for promotion as Superintending Engineer was wholly misconceived - Said issue was not and could not be decided in writ petition or in LPA filed by Electricity Board - HC should not have gone into aspect relating to promotion pursuant to review petition filed by Electricity Board, presumably laboring under misapprehension that HC in effect commanded Board to give promotion - While deciding review petition HC addressed itself to extraneous issue which was neither raised nor decided in main case - Thus, order under appeal was to be set aside - Any claim as to further promotional benefits had to be separately agitated by respondent in appropriate forum, if it was open to him to do so under law - Appeal disposed of.
