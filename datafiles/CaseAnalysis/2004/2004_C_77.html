Sargunam (Dead) By Lr v Chidambaram and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 855; (2005) 1 SCC 162; AIR 2005 SC 1420; 2005 (58) ALR 134; 2004 (4) AWC 3721; 2005 (1) CalLT(SC) 37; JT 2004 (9) SC 5; 2004(8) SCALE 595; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 156; 2005 (1) UC 113

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land and Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Sale - Deed -Suit for specific performance - Contested on ground that plaintiff had terminated the suit agreement as he had failed to complete the sale within the time stipulated in the suit agreement, and notice of termination to the plaintiff which notice came to be returned with the postal remarks 'refused' and, consequently, he had sold the property to the second defendant that is third party - Whether it is proved before trial Court that defendant no 2 was a bona fide purchaser for value without notice? - Whether plaintiff proved that conveyance deed of second sale was sham, bogus and nominal sale entered into to defeat claim of plaintiff? - Whether circumstances in ss. 20(2)(a) or 20(2)(b) are established? - Held, attesting witnesses deposed that signatures of defendant no 1 on second agreement did not tally with signatures on conveyance deed - HC right in coming to conclusion that second agreement was a concocted document - Defendant was in a position to foresee ensuing hardship - Hence, s 20(2)(b) is not applicable - Appeal dismissed.
