State by Police Inspector v Sri. T. Venkatesh Murthy

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 946; (2004) 7 SCC 763; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2140; AIR 2004 SC 5117; JT 2004 (8) SC 362; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 388; 2004(7) SCALE 573; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, s.19, 7, 13 (1) (d) r/w 13 (2) - Requirement of previous sanction - Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code no finding, sentence and order passed by a Special Judge shall be reversed or altered by a Court in appeal, confirmation or revision on the ground of the absence of, or any error, omission or irregularity in the sanction required under sub-s.(1), unless in the opinion of that court a failure of justice has in fact been occasioned thereby - Question of failure of justice not considered by either lower court or the HC - Orders set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
