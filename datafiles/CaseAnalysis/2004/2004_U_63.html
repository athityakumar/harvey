Hira Tikkoo v Union Territory, Chandigarh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 409; (2004) 6 SCC 765; AIR 2004 SC 3648; 2004 (2) CGLJ 114; JT 2004 (5) SC 231; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 130; 2004(4) SCALE 468; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Capital of Punjab [Development and Regulation] Act, 1952 - Chandigarh Lease Hold of Sites and Building Rules, 1973 - Allotment of industrial plots by Administration of UTC - Due to non-availability of sufficient land new industrial policy of 1990 and decision to reduce size of plots taken by Administration of UTC - Alternative plots of smaller sizes - No citizen can be allowed to claim any vested right which would result in violation of a statutory provision of law or Constitution - 'Promissory estoppel' and 'legitimate expectation' - Where public interest is likely to be harmed, neither doctrine of 'legitimate expectation' nor 'estoppel' can be allowed against State authorities - Directed consentees on being allotted a particular plot of smaller size shall be charged same price which prevailing at time of original allotment of plot in their favour - Consentees and non-consentees, on basis of their actions and inactions, constitute two different classes of allottees and a differential treatment to them cannot be held to be unjustified or in violation of art.14 of Constitution.
