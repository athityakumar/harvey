S. N. Narula v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1804; (2011) 4 SCC 591; 2011(3) SCALE 587

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Challenged, No communication before the order passed

Summary: Service - No communication before the order passed - Challenged - Appellant was appointed as Station Master with respondents - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against appellant - Appellant was exonerated - Thereafter, the proceedings were sent for opinion of the Union Public Service Commission and Commission gave an opinion to the effect that his pension shall be reduced to minimum and he shall not be granted any gratuity - Disciplinary authority imposed the said punishment - It was noticed that the advisory opinion of the Commission was not communicated to the appellant before he was heard by disciplinary authority - Appellant filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal quashed the order and remanded the case back to the Authority - Appellant field petition before HC - Petition was partly allowed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, report of the Commission was not communicated to the appellant before the final order was passed. Therefore, appellant was unable to make an effective representation before disciplinary authority as regards the punishment imposed. SC found that the stand taken by Tribunal was correct and HC was not justified in interfering with the order. Therefore, judgment of HC is set aside and directed that the disciplinary proceedings against the appellant be finally disposed of in accordance with the direction given by the Tribunal. Appeal disposed of.
