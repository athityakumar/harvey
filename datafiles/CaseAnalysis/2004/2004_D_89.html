State of Madhya Pradesh v Dharkole @ Govind Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 884; (2004) 13 SCC 308; (2005) SCC (Cr) 225; AIR 2005 SC 44; 2005 CRLJ 108; JT 2004 (9) SC 501; 2004 (3) KLT 776; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 942; 2004(9) SCALE 149; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149 and 148 - Conviction by Trial Court - Acquittal by HC - Whether proper? - Acquittal on ground that prosecution failed to prove their guilt beyond reasonable doubts - Held, where evidence has not been properly analysed or Court has acted on surmises or conjectures, it is duty of appellate Court to set right the wrong - HC ignored relevant aspects and unnecessarily put emphasis on certain aspects which did not have any foundation - Award of Trial Court is restored by reversing judgment of HC - Appeal allowed.
