Prithawi Nath Ram v State Of Jharkhand And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 648; (2004) 7 SCC 261; AIR 2004 SC 4277; 2004 (4) AWC 2955; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2004 (8) SC 163; 2004 (3) KLT 407; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 106; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 126; 2004(7) SCALE 117; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Constitution, art. 215 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 11 and 15 - Application for initiation of contempt proceedings - Whether it is open in contempt proceedings to examine whether order is valid or not, order which alleged to have been disobeyed? - Held, rightness or wrongness of order cannot be urged in contempt proceedings - Court cannot traverse beyond order - Matter remitted back for fresh consideration - Appeal allowed.
