Manoranjan Das v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1017; (2004) 12 SCC 90; AIR 2004 SC 3623; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 8; 2004 CRLJ 3042; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 213; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 951; 2004(5) SCALE 157; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 601; 2004 (2) UC 931

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420/109 - Abetting office of cheating - Evidence - Collusion - Appellant was a businessman - A person (Loknath Acharya) sought appellant's assistance to open a current account with complainant bank in which appellant had a current account - Bank manager found that Loknath Acharya had sought to present bogus drafts before the bank - Complaint registered u/s. 420, IPC against Loknath Acharya and appellant who introduced that person to the bank - Since Loknath Acharya remained absconding, appellant faced the criminal trial - Appellant found guilty and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for a period of three years - On appeal, Sessions Court converted the conviction of the appellant from s. 420, IPC to conviction u/s. 420/109, IPC, but sentence was maintained - Revision petition to HC was dismissed and sentence of imprisonment reduced to a period of six months - Aggrieved, instant appeal was filed - Whether appellant committed any offence of abetting office of cheating punishable under law and acted in collusion with Loknath Acharya to commit any fraud - Held, nothing on record to show that appellant was in any way related to fraud committed by Loknath Acharya on Bank - Evidence of three witnesses did not show any complicity by appellant in committing any act to cheating - There was no evidence to show that appellant instigated Loknath Acharya to present bogus drafts before complainant bank - It was also not correct to say that appellant was in anyway responsible for loss sustained by bank - Trial court as well as appellate court committed serious error in finding appellant guilty - Hence, appellant was acquitted of the charge u/s. 420/109, IPC - Appeal allowed.
