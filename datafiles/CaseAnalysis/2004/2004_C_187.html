I.T.C. Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 882; (2004) 7 SCC 591; 2004 (171) E.L.T. 433; JT 2004 (7) SC 409; 2004 (64) RLT 341; 2004(7) SCALE 540; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Sale Price, Normal Price, Standards Of Weights And Measures Act, 1976, Standards of Weights and Measures (Packaged Commodities) Rules, 1977

Summary: Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Additional Duties Of Excise (Goods Of Special Importance) Act, 1957 - Standards of Weights and Measures Act, 1976 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 8(1), 9(1), 52, 52A, 209 and 210 - Standards of Weights and Measures (Packaged Commodity) Rules, 1977 - Standards of Weights and Measures Act, 1976 - Notification No. 36/83, D/- 1-03-1983 - Notification No. 201/85,D/- 2-09-1985 - Whether the Tribunal had erred in the upholding the demands of the excise authorities? - The notification had introduced a system for levy of excise duty on an experimental basis - If the experiment was a failure for whatever reason, it was open to the respondents to do away with it and replace the system by some other as it did in 1987 - But as long as the notification stood, it had to be given effect to - Held, the impugned demands raised against the appellant are set aside without any order as to costs - Appeal allowed.
