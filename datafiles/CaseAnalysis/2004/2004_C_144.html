Berger Paints India Ltd v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 161; (2004) 12 SCC 42; AIR 2004 SC 1743; 2004 (187) CTR 193; 2004 (165) E.L.T. 488; [2004] 266 ITR 99; JT 2004 (4) SC 252; 2004(2) SCALE 554; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 502; 180 TAXATION 10

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 141A, 256(1), 261, 263, 43B - No 'just cause' as would justify departure from principle that Revenue could not have been allowed to challenge principle laid down in Lakhanpal National Ltd 's case, which was followed by Inspecting Assistant Commissioner in case of assessee in three assessment years in question - Was followed by judgments of Madras HC and Bombay HC and was again followed by decision of Special Bench of Income Tax Appellate Tribunal, none of which have been challenged - If one HC desired to depart from uniform view taken a certificate to appeal u/s. 261 of Act ought to have been granted - Appeals allowed.
