State Of West Bengal v Sadan K. Bormal & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 355; (2004) 6 SCC 59; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1739; AIR 2004 SC 3666; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 367; 2004(5) SCALE 249; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 870

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949, s. 9 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5, 5A - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 419, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952, ss. 10, 26, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special courts) Amending Act, 1953 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Prevention of Corruption (West Bengal Amendment) Act, 1994, s. 2 - Whether an offence committed while Act of 1947 was in force can be tried by Courts empowered to try offences under Act of 1947 after coming into force of Act of 1988 - Held, Prevention of Corruption (West Bengal Amendment) Act, 1994 by amending Act of 1988 inserting Section 26 A therein has vested jurisdiction in Special Courts appointed under West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949 subject to conditions laid down therein, to try offences under Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - All actions taken by them are validated as if West Bengal Amendment Act, 1994 were in force when such action wataken - Set aside judgment and order of HC quashing proceeding before 3 rd Special Judge - Directed to proceed with trial in accordance with law.
