Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan & Ors. v T.Srinivas

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 569; (2004) 7 SCC 442; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1011; AIR 2004 SC 4127; 2004 (3) AWC 2714; 2004 (3) CLR 4; 2004 (4) ESC 492; 2004 (102) FLR 1045; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 225; JT 2004 (6) SC 292; 2004 (3) KLT 719; 2004 (3) LLN 1232; 2004(6) SCALE 467; 2004 (5) SLR 729; 2004 (6) Supreme 4; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2363

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Employment - Disciplinary proceedings - Criminal prosecution on same charges-Whether both departmental and criminal proceedings can go simultaneously?-Held, “yes”-Approach and objective in both proceedings are altogether distinct and different-Only valid ground for staying disciplinary proceedings-Is that “defence of employee in criminal case may not be prejudiced”-Stay cannot and should not be matter of course-No special facts of case considered by two forums below-Both Tribunal and High Court proceeded as if departmental enquiry had to be stayed in every case where criminal trial regarding same misconduct pending-Stay of departmental proceedings granted by them-Is contrary to principles laid down by Supreme Court-Impugned orders set aside.
