Messrs Ahad Brothers v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1019; (2005) 1 SCC 545; AIR 2005 SC 355; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 68; 2005 (58) ALR 503; 2005 (1) AWC 298; 2005 (1) CalLT(SC) 89; 2005 (2) MahLJ 215; 2005 (139) PLR 598; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 844; 2005 (1) RD 474; 2004(9) SCALE 513; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 191; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 300

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 3(b), 3(c), 30, 4(1) - Acquisition proceedings - Whether HC was right in going into question of title over property acquired by State and in recording a finding that appellant had only leasehold interest in land? - whether HC right in interfering with market value of land determined by Reference Court? - Held, HC committed an error in taking a view that question of title could be decided in proceedings arising u/s. 18 - Finding that appellant had only leasehold interest in land cannot be sustained - Judgment is modified awarding compensation to appellant as owner of land acquired @ Rs. 2/- Per sq ft after deducting 30% of market value of land calculated on basis of Rs. 2/- Per sq ft.
