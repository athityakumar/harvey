Mrs. Janki Vashdeo Bhojwani and Another v Indusind Bank Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 966; (2004) 3 SCC 584; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 76; 2004 (2) CHN 1; [2004] 119 Comp Cas 133; 2004 DRTC 1; JT 2004 (2) SC 254; 2004 (137) PLR 53; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 613; 2004(2) SCALE 370

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Banking & Finance - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 44 - Property - Mortgage - Attachment - Sustainability - Appellant no. 1 was wife of respondent no. 5 and appellant no. 2 was wife of respondent no. 2 - Respondent no. 1-Bank had given loan facilities to respondents nos. 6 and 7, which were concerns had run by respondents nos. 2 to 5 - Respondents nos. 2 to 5 were also guarantors of loan facilities and some of properties belonging to respondents nos. 2 to 5 had been mortgaged to respondent no. 1 - Respondent no. 1-Bank filed against respondent no. 7 and respondent no. 2, before Tribunal for recovery of sum of approximately Rs.3, 86, 000, 000/- - Decree was passed in favour of respondent no. 1 by Tribunal - In pursuance of Recovery Certificate, all mortgaged properties were attached - Appellants filed objections before Recovery Officer against attachment of residential property, which was rejected - Appellants filed appeal before Debt Recovery Tribunal challenging attachment and proposed sale of property on ground that appellants were co-owners in property and that appellants were not debtors of respondent no. 1, which was dismissed - Appeal was filed by appellant before Appellate Tribunal, which was also dismissed - Appellants filed writ petition, which had also been dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether claim of appellants that impugned property had never been mortgaged, hence, attachment of property was not valid, was sustainable - Held, appellants were neither debtors nor guarantors and that property in dispute was not mortgaged in favour of respondent no. 1 - Authorities below and HC were wrong in not deciding existence of share in property in dispute - Property in dispute was residential premises, hence, provision of s. 44 of the Act would apply - Respondent no. 1 could not have taken possession without first applying for partition - Hence, matter was remitted back to Tribunal to record findings related to decrees passed and extent of co-ownership of appellants in property in dispute - Order accordingly.
