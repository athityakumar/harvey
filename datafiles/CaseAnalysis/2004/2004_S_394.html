Jagdish Ram v State Of Rajasthan & Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 269; (2004) 4 SCC 432; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1294; AIR 2004 SC 1734; 2004 (1) CCR 391; 2004 (2) CGLJ 19; 2004 (2) Crimes 296; 2004 (1) JCC 611; JT 2004 (3) SC 202; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 194; 2004(3) SCALE 49; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 846; 2004 (2) SLT 424; 2004 (2) Supreme 336

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, s. 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 17 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 156(3), 173, 200, 202, 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings on ground of delay -Whether criminal proceedings deserve to be quashed on ground of delay, question to be looked into is reason for delay as also seriousness of offence - Appellant is responsible for delay Regarding seriousness of offence, untouchability abolished under Constitution and Act under which complaint in question has been filed was enacted nearly half a century ago - Appeal dismissed.
