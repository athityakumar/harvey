Pramod Mandal v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 749; (2004) 13 SCC 150; (2005) SCC (Cr) 75; JT 2004 (7) SC 583; 2004(7) SCALE 718B; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, s 396 - Test Identification Parade - Conviction and sentence to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years - Appeal against conviction - Whether there is irregularity or unfairness in the holding of Test Identification Parade? - Held, found no irregularity or unfairness in holding of Test Identification Parade, it must be held that evidence of PW-4 is amply corroborated by result of test identification proceeding - No reason to doubt either genuineness of Test Identification proceeding or veracity of witness - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
