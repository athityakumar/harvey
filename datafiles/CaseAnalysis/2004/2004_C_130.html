Sarabhai M. Chemicals v Commis.Of Central Excise,Vadodara

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1059; (2005) 2 SCC 168; AIR 2005 SC 1126; 2005 DC 1; 2005 (179) E.L.T. 3; 2004 (65) RLT 879; 2004(10) SCALE 484; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1010; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1031

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 1979

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A(1), 35L(b) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 1979, s. 2(a) - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173B, 173C, 173G, 8(1), 9(2) - Duty - Sorbitol solution - Exemption under notification no 234/86 - Whether sorbitol solution cleared in favour of non- Pharmaceutical concerns comes under bulk drugs to claim exemption? - whether demands raised by department in impugned three show- Cause notices were time-barred? - Held, exemption was not given to a sorbitol solution, which has uses other than in pharmaceuticals - A sorbitol solution could be called a drug for purposes of exemption only when it was actually used as drug or as an ingredient in any formulation - Where facts are known to both parties, omission by one to do what he might have done and not that he must have done does not constitute suppression of fact - No suppression of facts - Impugned show-cause notices-cum-demands were time barred and as no case is made out by department - Appeals allowed.
