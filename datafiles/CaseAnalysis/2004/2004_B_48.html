Maharwal Khewaji Trust (Regd). Faridkot v Baldev Dass

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 949; (2004) 8 SCC 488; 2004 (57) ALR 428; 2004 (4) AWC 3717; 2005 (4) Bom.C.R. 408; JT 2004 (9) SC 216; 2005 (139) PLR 399; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 760; 2004(8) SCALE 862; 2005 (1) UC 122

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O 39 r. 1, 2 - Prayer to restrain respondent from alienating suit property and putting up any construction thereon - Trial court granted temporary injunction - In appeal order that alienation made, if any, will be subject to law of lis pendens and constructions, if any, put by respondent will have to be removed as his own risk and cost in event of suit being decreed - Appeal against this order - Held, in the facts and circumstances of this case, lower appellate court and HC were not justified in permitting respondent to change nature of property by putting up construction as also by permitting alienation of property, whatever may be condition on which the same is done - Orders set aside and order of trial court is restored - Appeal allowed.
