Messrs Elphinstone Metal Rolling Mills v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 368; (2004) 5 SCC 548; AIR 2004 SC 3515; 2004 (94) ECC 197; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 481; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 217; 2004 (62) RLT 486; 2004(5) SCALE 668; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act - Exemption under Notification No 74/65-CE and 119/66-CE - Waste and Scrap was dutiable only when it was a manufactured product and otherwise excisable under provision of Act - Unless scrap and waste are goods that had been used can be demonstrated to have been a duty paid goods, it cannot be assumed that they are so, particularly when it cannot be said with certainty that all scrap and waste material used has been subject to excise duty earlier - Object of exemption being to avoid cascading effect in matter of payment of excise duty - Appeal dismissed.
