State of Punjab v Balkar Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1453; (2004) 3 SCC 582; (2004) SCC (Cr) 838; AIR 2004 SC 4606; 2004 CRLJ 3839; 2004(3) SCALE 479; 2004 (2) UC 837

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Poppy

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Appeal challenging acquittal of two accused person - Tried for offence punishable u/s. 15, NDPS Act, 1985 - High Court by impugned judgment stated that prosecution failed to prove that these respondents were in conscious possession of poppy husk recovered by police - Held, prosecution failed to discharge its obligation to prove possession of poppy husk by respondents - No infirmity in judgment passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
