State of Orissa and others v Bijaya C. Tripathy

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1693; (2004) 7 SCC 139; AIR 2005 SC 1431; AIR 2004 SCW 7327; 2004(8) SCALE 39

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Certificate Of Fitness

Summary: Indirect Tax - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 66 - Orissa Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1975, s. 3 - Levy of tax - HC held that the vehicle owned by the respondent was not liable to pay road tax under 1975 Act because it did not have, during the relevant period, a stage carriage permit - HC further held that under Schedule 1 of the 1975 Act, tax has to be paid in respect of stage carriage vehicles on the basis of the distance they were permitted to run and in the absence of any permit it was not possible to calculate the quantum of tax payable - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld and respondent not liable to pay road tax under 1975 Act? - Held, as per s. 3 of 1975 Act, tax is levied on every motor vehicle which is "used or kept for use" - The explanation of s. 3 of 1975 Act makes it very clear that if a transport vehicle has a valid certificate of fitness and a valid certificate of registration then it will be presumed that the vehicle is kept for use - This presumption arises in respect of all motor vehicles, whether they are light motor vehicles or transport vehicles and would also include vehicles which do not have a stage carriage permit - Hence, respondent was liable to pay road tax under 1975 Act - Impugned judgment of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
