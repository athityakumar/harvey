M/S S.J.S. Business Enterprises (P) Ltd. v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 206; (2004) 7 SCC 166; AIR 2004 SC 2421; 2004 (5) ALD(SC) 84; 2004 (56) ALR 115; 2004 (4) AWC 3560; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 99; 2004 DRTC 396; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 284; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 601; 2005 (1) LCD 114; 2004(3) SCALE 374

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 32 - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Corporation Act - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Sale of hotel - Axiomatic that statutory powers vested in State Financial Corporation under State Financial Corporation Act, must be exercised bona fide - Held on facts, method in which sale was conducted is questionable - Three valuations were obtained between 3 rd July 2001 to February 2002 before property was sold to respondent No 6 - Substantial differences in valuation - Respondent No 6 had submitted his offer on day on which sale notice was published and made payment of entire consideration on same day before last date for submission of tenders was over and even before its offer could been accepted - No satisfactory explanation is forthcoming from authorities to explain these deviations - Sale set aside - Appeals allowed.
